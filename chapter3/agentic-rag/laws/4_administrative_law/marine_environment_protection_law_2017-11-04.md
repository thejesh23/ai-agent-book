# Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Fifth National People's Congress on August 23, 1982

Revised at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 25, 1999

First Amendment on December 28, 2013, according to the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on November 7, 2016, according to the Decision on Amending the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Third Amendment on November 4, 2017, according to the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws Including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated for the purpose of protecting and improving the marine environment, protecting marine resources, preventing pollution damage, maintaining ecological balance, safeguarding human health, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the internal waters, territorial sea, contiguous zone, exclusive economic zone, continental shelf of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

All units and individuals engaged in navigation, exploration, exploitation, production, tourism, scientific research and other activities in the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, or engaged in activities affecting the marine environment in the coastal land areas, must abide by this Law.

This Law shall also apply to pollution of the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China caused beyond those sea areas.

Article 3 The State shall delimit ecological conservation redlines in sea areas such as key marine ecological function zones, ecologically sensitive and fragile areas, and implement strict protection.

The State shall establish and implement a total pollutant discharge control system for key sea areas, determine the total discharge control indicators for major pollutants discharged into the sea, and allocate discharge control quantities to major pollution sources. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 4 All units and individuals have the obligation to protect the marine environment and have the right to supervise and report any units or individuals that cause pollution damage to the marine environment, as well as any illegal or derelict acts by marine environment supervision and management personnel.

Article 5 The competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council, as the department for unified supervision and management of national environmental protection work, shall guide, coordinate, and supervise national marine environmental protection work, and be responsible for the environmental protection work of preventing pollution damage to the marine environment from land-sourced pollutants and coastal construction projects nationwide.

The State oceanic administrative department shall be responsible for the supervision and management of the marine environment, organizing the investigation, monitoring, surveillance, evaluation, and scientific research of the marine environment, and be responsible for the environmental protection work of preventing pollution damage to the marine environment from marine engineering construction projects and ocean dumping of wastes nationwide.

The State maritime administrative department shall be responsible for the supervision and management of pollution of the marine environment by non-military vessels within the waters of its port jurisdiction and by non-fishing, non-military vessels outside the waters of its port jurisdiction, and be responsible for the investigation and handling of pollution accidents; it shall board and inspect foreign vessels causing pollution accidents while navigating, anchoring, or operating in the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China. Where a vessel pollution accident causes damage to fisheries, the fisheries administrative department shall be involved in the investigation and handling.

The State fisheries administrative department shall be responsible for the supervision and management of pollution of the marine environment by non-military vessels within fishing port waters and by fishing vessels outside fishing port waters, be responsible for protecting the ecological environment of fishing waters, and investigate and handle fishery pollution accidents other than those stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

The environmental protection department of the armed forces shall be responsible for the supervision and management of pollution of the marine environment by military vessels and the investigation and handling of pollution accidents.

The responsibilities of the departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management of the local people's governments at or above the county level along the coast shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 6 The competent administrative department of environmental protection, the oceanic administrative department, and other departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management shall, according to their respective responsibilities, publicly disclose information related to the marine environment in accordance with the law; relevant pollutant-discharging units shall publicly disclose their pollutant discharge information in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Supervision and Management of the Marine Environment

Article 7 The State oceanic administrative department shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of coastal provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulate the national marine functional zoning plan based on the national marine main functional area plan, and submit it to the State Council for approval.

Local people's governments at all levels along the coast shall protect and use sea areas scientifically and rationally in accordance with the national and local marine functional zoning plans.

Article 8 The State shall formulate a national marine environmental protection plan and regional marine environmental protection plans for key sea areas based on the marine functional zoning plan.

The relevant coastal people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government adjacent to key sea areas and the departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management may establish regional cooperation organizations for marine environmental protection, responsible for implementing regional marine environmental protection plans for key sea areas, preventing and controlling marine environmental pollution, and protecting the marine ecology.

Article 9 Cross-regional marine environmental protection work shall be resolved through consultation by the relevant local people's governments along the coast, or coordinated by the higher-level people's government.

Major cross-departmental marine environmental protection work shall be coordinated by the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council; if coordination fails, the State Council shall make a decision.

Article 10 The State shall formulate national marine environmental quality standards based on the state of the marine environment and the national economic and technical conditions.

The people's governments of coastal provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate local marine environmental quality standards for items not specified in the national marine environmental quality standards.

Local people's governments at all levels along the coast shall, based on the provisions of national and local marine environmental quality standards and the environmental quality status of the coastal waters within their administrative regions, determine the objectives and tasks for marine environmental protection, incorporate them into the government work plan, and implement management according to the corresponding marine environmental quality standards.

Article 11 When formulating national and local water pollutant discharge standards, national and local marine environmental quality standards shall be taken as one of the important bases. In key sea areas where the State has established and implemented a total pollutant discharge control system, the total discharge control indicators for major pollutants discharged into the sea shall also be taken as an important basis when formulating water pollutant discharge standards.

While implementing national and local water pollutant discharge standards, pollutant-discharging units shall also comply with the total discharge control indicators for major pollutants discharged into the sea that are allocated to them.

For key sea areas that exceed the total discharge control indicators for major pollutants discharged into the sea and sea areas that have not completed the objectives and tasks for marine environmental protection, the competent administrative departments of environmental protection and the oceanic administrative departments of the people's governments at or above the provincial level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, suspend the approval of environmental impact assessment reports (forms) for construction projects that would increase the total discharge of the corresponding types of pollutants.

Article 12 Units and individuals that directly discharge pollutants into the sea must pay a pollutant discharge fee in accordance with state regulations. If an environmental protection tax is paid in accordance with the law, the pollutant discharge fee shall no longer be paid.

Those who dump wastes into the sea must pay a dumping fee in accordance with state regulations.

The pollutant discharge fees and dumping fees collected in accordance with the provisions of this Law must be used for the remediation of marine environmental pollution and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 13 The State shall strengthen the research and development of science and technology for preventing and controlling marine environmental pollution damage, and implement a phase-out system for outdated production processes and equipment that seriously pollute the marine environment.

Enterprises shall give priority to the use of clean energy, adopt clean production processes with high resource utilization rates and low pollutant discharge, to prevent pollution of the marine environment.

Article 14 The State oceanic administrative department shall, in accordance with national environmental monitoring and surveillance norms and standards, manage the national investigation, monitoring, and surveillance of the marine environment, formulate specific implementation measures, organize the national marine environment monitoring and surveillance network together with relevant departments, regularly evaluate the quality of the marine environment, and issue marine cruise surveillance bulletins.

The departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall be responsible for the monitoring and surveillance of the waters under their respective jurisdictions.

Other relevant departments shall, according to the division of labor in the national marine environment monitoring network, be responsible for the monitoring of river estuaries entering the sea and major discharge outlets.

Article 15 The relevant departments of the State Council shall provide the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council with the marine environment monitoring data necessary for compiling the national environmental quality bulletin.

The competent administrative department of environmental protection shall provide relevant departments with data related to marine environment supervision and management.

Article 16 The State oceanic administrative department shall, in accordance with the national environmental monitoring and surveillance information management system established by the State, be responsible for managing the comprehensive marine information system and providing services for marine environmental protection supervision and management.

Article 17 Units and individuals that cause or may cause marine environmental pollution accidents due to accidents or other emergencies must immediately take effective measures, promptly notify those who may be endangered, report to the departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management in accordance with this Law, and accept investigation and handling.

When the environment of the coastal waters within their administrative region is seriously polluted, the local people's governments at or above the county level along the coast must take effective measures to eliminate or mitigate the harm.

Article 18 The State shall, based on the need to prevent marine environmental pollution, formulate national emergency plans for major marine pollution accidents.

The State oceanic administrative department shall be responsible for formulating the national emergency plan for major offshore oil spills from offshore oil exploration and development, and report it to the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council for record.

The State maritime administrative department shall be responsible for formulating the national emergency plan for major oil spill pollution accidents from vessels, and report it to the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council for record.

Units along the coast that may cause major marine environmental pollution accidents shall, in accordance with state regulations, formulate pollution accident emergency plans and report them to the local competent administrative department of environmental protection and the oceanic administrative department for record.

When a major marine pollution accident occurs, the local people's governments at or above the county level along the coast and their relevant departments must act in accordance with the emergency plan to eliminate or mitigate the harm.

Article 19 The departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management in accordance with this Law may conduct joint law enforcement at sea. When discovering marine pollution accidents or acts violating the provisions of this Law during cruise surveillance, they shall stop them, investigate and collect evidence, and when necessary, have the right to take effective measures to prevent the spread of the pollution situation, and report to the relevant competent department for handling.

The departments exercising the power of marine environment supervision and management in accordance with this Law have the right to conduct on-site inspections of units and individuals discharging pollutants within their jurisdiction. The inspected party shall truthfully report the situation and provide necessary information.

The inspecting authority shall keep confidential the technical secrets and business secrets of the inspected party.

## Chapter III Marine Ecological Protection

Article 20 The State Council and local people's governments at all levels along the coast shall take effective measures to protect typical and representative marine ecosystems such as mangroves, coral reefs, coastal wetlands, islands, bays, river estuaries entering the sea, and important fishing waters, natural concentrated distribution areas of rare and endangered marine organisms, living areas of marine organisms with important economic value, and marine natural historical relics and natural landscapes with significant scientific and cultural value.

Marine ecosystems that have been damaged but have important economic and social value shall be remediated and restored.

Article 21 The relevant departments of the State Council and the coastal provincial people's governments shall, according to the needs of marine ecological protection, select, delimit, and establish marine nature reserves.

The establishment of national marine nature reserves must be approved by the State Council.

Article 22 Marine nature reserves shall be established in any of the following conditions:

(1) Typical marine natural geographical areas, representative natural ecological areas, and marine natural ecological areas that have been damaged but can be restored through protection;

(2) Areas with high abundance of marine biological species, or natural concentrated distribution areas of rare and endangered marine biological species;

(3) Sea areas, coasts, islands, coastal wetlands, river estuaries entering the sea, and bays with special protection value;

(4) Areas where marine natural relics with significant scientific and cultural value are located;

(5) Other areas requiring special protection.

Article 23 For areas with special geographical conditions, ecosystems, biological and non-biological resources, and special needs for marine development and utilization, special marine protected areas may be established, and effective protection measures and scientific development methods shall be adopted for special management.

Article 24 The State shall establish and improve the marine ecological protection compensation system.

The development and utilization of marine resources shall be rationally laid out according to the marine functional zoning plan, strictly abide by the ecological conservation redline, and shall not cause damage to the marine ecological environment.

Article 25 The introduction of marine animal and plant species shall be subject to scientific demonstration to avoid causing harm to the marine ecosystem.

Article 26 The development of resources on islands and their surrounding sea areas shall adopt strict ecological protection measures and shall not cause damage to the island's topography, beaches, vegetation, and the ecological environment of the surrounding sea areas.

Article 27 Local people's governments at all levels along the coast shall, based on the characteristics of the local natural environment, construct coastal protection facilities, coastal shelter forests, coastal urban gardens and green spaces, and carry out comprehensive management of areas affected by coastal erosion and seawater intrusion.

It is prohibited to destroy coastal protection facilities, coastal shelter forests, coastal urban gardens and green spaces.

Article 28 The State encourages the development of ecological fishery construction, promotes various ecological fishery production methods, and improves the marine ecological status.

The construction of new, renovation, or expansion of mariculture farms shall undergo environmental impact assessment.

Mariculture shall scientifically determine the stocking density, and shall rationally apply bait and fertilizer, correctly use drugs, to prevent pollution of the marine environment.

## Chapter IV Prevention of Pollution Damage to the Marine Environment from Land-Sourced Pollutants

Article 29 The discharge of land-sourced pollutants into the sea must strictly comply with the standards and relevant provisions stipulated by the State or local authorities.

Article 30 The selection of the location of sea outfalls for land-sourced pollutants shall be based on the marine functional zoning plan, seawater dynamic conditions, and relevant provisions, and after scientific demonstration, shall be reported to the competent administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government at or above the city divided into districts for record.

The competent administrative department of environmental protection shall, within fifteen working days after completing the record, notify the oceanic, maritime, fisheries administrative departments and the environmental protection department of the armed forces of the establishment of the sea outfall.

No new discharge outlets shall be built in marine nature reserves, important fishing waters, coastal scenic spots, and other areas requiring special protection.

In areas where conditions permit, discharge outlets shall be set up in deep sea areas for offshore discharge. The setting of deep-sea offshore discharge outlets for land-sourced pollutants shall be determined based on the marine functional zoning plan, seawater dynamic conditions, and the situation of submarine engineering facilities. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 31 The competent administrative departments of environmental protection and water administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the laws on water pollution prevention and control, strengthen the management of rivers entering the sea, prevent pollution, and ensure that the water quality of the river estuaries entering the sea is in good condition.

Article 32 Units discharging land-sourced pollutants must declare to the competent administrative department of environmental protection the facilities they possess for discharging and treating land-sourced pollutants, the types, quantities, and concentrations of land-sourced pollutants discharged under normal operating conditions, and provide relevant technical information and data for preventing marine environmental pollution.

If there is a significant change in the types, quantities, or concentrations of land-sourced pollutants discharged, a timely declaration must be made.

Article 33 It is prohibited to discharge oils, acid liquids, alkali liquids, highly toxic waste liquids, and high and intermediate level radioactive wastewater into the sea.

The discharge of low-level radioactive wastewater into the sea is strictly restricted; if discharge is truly necessary, the national radiation protection regulations must be strictly complied with.

The discharge of wastewater containing non-degradable organic matter and heavy metals into the sea shall be strictly controlled.

Article 34 Medical wastewater containing pathogens, domestic sewage, and industrial wastewater must be treated to meet the relevant national discharge standards before being discharged into the sea.

Article 35 The discharge of industrial wastewater and domestic sewage containing organic matter and nutrients into bays, semi-enclosed seas, and other sea areas with poor self-purification capacity shall be strictly controlled.

Article 36 When discharging thermal wastewater into the sea, effective measures must be taken to ensure that the water temperature of adjacent fishing waters meets the national marine environmental quality standards, to avoid harm to aquatic resources from thermal pollution.

Article 37 The application of chemical pesticides on coastal farmland and forest farms must comply with the national regulations and standards for pesticide safety use.

Coastal farmland and forest farms shall use fertilizers and plant growth regulators rationally.

Article 38 The disposal, stacking, and treatment of tailings, mineral slag, coal ash slag, garbage, and other solid wastes on the shore and beach shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Environmental Pollution by Solid Wastes.

Article 39 It is prohibited to transfer hazardous wastes through the internal waters and territorial sea of the People's Republic of China.

To transfer hazardous wastes through other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, written consent must be obtained in advance from the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council.

Article 40 The people's governments of coastal cities shall construct and improve urban drainage networks, plan the construction of urban sewage treatment plants or other centralized sewage treatment facilities, and strengthen the comprehensive treatment of urban sewage.

The construction of marine sewage disposal projects must comply with the relevant state regulations.

Article 41 The State shall take necessary measures to prevent, reduce, and control marine environmental pollution damage originating from or through the atmosphere.

## Chapter V Prevention of Pollution Damage to the Marine Environment from Coastal Engineering Construction Projects

Article 42 The construction of new, renovation, or expansion of coastal engineering construction projects must comply with the state regulations on environmental protection management for construction projects, and incorporate the funds required for pollution prevention into the investment plan of the construction project.

In legally designated marine nature reserves, coastal scenic spots, important fishing waters, and other areas requiring special protection, no coastal engineering construction projects or other activities that pollute the environment or damage the landscape shall be undertaken.

Article 43 The unit undertaking a coastal engineering construction project must conduct a scientific investigation of the marine environment, select a reasonable site based on natural and social conditions, and prepare an environmental impact assessment report (form). Before the start of the construction project, the environmental impact assessment report (form) shall be submitted to the competent administrative department of environmental protection for review and approval.

Before approving the environmental impact assessment report (form), the competent administrative department of environmental protection must seek the opinions of the oceanic, maritime, fisheries administrative departments and the environmental protection department of the armed forces.

Article 44 The environmental protection facilities of a coastal engineering construction project must be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project. The environmental protection facilities shall meet the requirements of the approved environmental impact assessment report (form).**Article 45** It is prohibited to construct new industrial production projects within coastal land areas that lack effective treatment measures, such as chemical pulping and papermaking, chemical, printing and dyeing, leather tanning, electroplating, brewing, oil refining, shoreline beaching for ship dismantling, and other projects that cause serious pollution to the marine environment.

**Article 46** When constructing coastal engineering projects, effective measures must be taken to protect wildlife and plants under national or local key protection, their living environments, and marine fishery resources.

Strict restrictions shall be imposed on the excavation of sand and gravel along the coast. Open-pit mining of coastal placer deposits and drilling from shore to exploit seabed mineral resources must adopt effective measures to prevent pollution of the marine environment.

## Chapter VI Prevention of Pollution Damage to the Marine Environment by Marine Engineering Construction Projects

**Article 47** Marine engineering construction projects must comply with the national marine main functional area plan, marine functional zoning, marine environmental protection plan, and relevant national environmental protection standards. The entity undertaking a marine engineering construction project shall conduct a scientific survey of the marine environment, prepare a marine environmental impact report (form), and submit it to the marine administrative department for review and approval before the commencement of the project.

Before approving the marine environmental impact report (form), the marine administrative department must solicit opinions from the maritime administrative department, the fishery administrative department, and the environmental protection department of the military.

**Article 48** The environmental protection facilities of a marine engineering construction project must be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project. If the environmental protection facilities have not been accepted by the marine administrative department, or have been accepted but found unqualified, the construction project shall not be put into production or use.

The dismantling or idling of environmental protection facilities must obtain prior consent from the marine administrative department.

**Article 49** Marine engineering construction projects shall not use materials containing radioactive substances exceeding the standard or readily leachable toxic and hazardous substances.

**Article 50** When blasting operations are required for marine engineering construction projects, effective measures must be taken to protect marine resources.

During offshore oil exploration, development, and oil transportation, effective measures must be taken to prevent oil spill accidents.

**Article 51** Oily wastewater and oily mixtures from offshore oil drilling vessels, drilling platforms, and production platforms must be treated to meet standards before discharge; residual oil and waste oil must be recovered and shall not be discharged into the sea. If discharged after recovery and treatment, the oil content shall not exceed the standards prescribed by the State.

Oil-based muds and other toxic composite muds used in drilling shall not be discharged into the sea. The discharge of water-based muds, non-toxic composite muds, and drill cuttings must comply with relevant national regulations.

**Article 52** Offshore oil drilling vessels, drilling platforms, production platforms, and related offshore facilities shall not dispose of oily industrial waste into the sea. The disposal of other industrial waste shall not cause pollution to the marine environment.

**Article 53** During offshore well testing, it shall be ensured that oil and gas are fully combusted, and oil and oily mixtures shall not be discharged into the sea.

**Article 54** When exploring and developing offshore oil, an oil spill contingency plan must be prepared in accordance with relevant regulations and filed with the regional agency of the national marine administrative department.

## Chapter VII Prevention of Pollution Damage to the Marine Environment by Dumping of Wastes

**Article 55** No unit may dump any waste into the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China without approval from the national marine administrative department.

Units that need to dump wastes must submit a written application to the national marine administrative department. Only after examination and approval by the national marine administrative department and issuance of a permit may dumping be carried out.

It is prohibited to dump wastes from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China into the sea areas under its jurisdiction.

**Article 56** The national marine administrative department shall formulate evaluation procedures and standards for the dumping of wastes into the sea based on the toxicity, content of toxic substances, and the degree of impact on the marine environment.

The dumping of wastes into the sea shall be managed at different levels according to the category and quantity of the wastes.

The list of wastes that may be dumped into the sea shall be drawn up by the national marine administrative department, submitted to the environmental protection administrative department of the State Council for review, and then reported to the State Council for approval.

**Article 57** The national marine administrative department shall select and designate marine dumping areas based on the principles of science, rationality, economy, and safety. After the environmental protection administrative department of the State Council provides its review opinion, the matter shall be reported to the State Council for approval.

Temporary marine dumping areas shall be approved by the national marine administrative department and filed with the environmental protection administrative department of the State Council.

Before selecting and designating marine dumping areas or approving temporary marine dumping areas, the national marine administrative department must solicit opinions from the national maritime administrative department and the fishery administrative department.

**Article 58** The national marine administrative department shall supervise and manage the use of dumping areas and organize environmental monitoring of these areas. For dumping areas confirmed as no longer suitable for continued use, the national marine administrative department shall close them, terminate all dumping activities in those areas, and report this to the State Council for the record.

**Article 59** Units approved to dump wastes must dump them in the designated areas within the time limit and under the conditions specified in the permit. After the wastes are loaded, the approving department shall verify them.

**Article 60** Units approved to dump wastes shall keep detailed records of the dumping operations and submit a written report to the approving department after dumping. Vessels dumping wastes must submit a written report to the maritime administrative department of the port of departure.

**Article 61** It is prohibited to incinerate wastes at sea.

It is prohibited to dispose of radioactive wastes or other radioactive substances at sea. The exemption concentration for radioactive substances in wastes shall be prescribed by the State Council.

## Chapter VIII Prevention of Pollution Damage to the Marine Environment by Vessels and Related Operations

**Article 62** Within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, no vessel or related operation shall discharge pollutants, wastes, ballast water, ship garbage, or other harmful substances into the sea in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Entities engaged in the reception of ship pollutants, wastes, ship garbage, ship tank cleaning, and hold washing operations must possess the corresponding reception and treatment capabilities.

**Article 63** Vessels must hold certificates and documents for the prevention of marine environmental pollution in accordance with relevant regulations, and shall truthfully record operations involving pollutant discharge.

**Article 64** Vessels must be equipped with corresponding anti-pollution equipment and devices.

Vessels carrying goods with pollution hazards must have a structure and equipment capable of preventing or mitigating pollution of the marine environment by the cargo carried.

**Article 65** Vessels shall comply with the provisions of laws and regulations on maritime traffic safety to prevent marine environmental pollution caused by maritime accidents such as collisions, grounding, stranding, fire, or explosion.

**Article 66** The State shall improve and implement the civil liability system for oil pollution damage from vessels; based on the principle that the risk of liability for oil pollution damage from vessels is shared by the shipowner and the cargo owner, the State shall establish a system for vessel oil pollution insurance and an oil pollution damage compensation fund.

Specific measures for implementing the vessel oil pollution insurance and oil pollution damage compensation fund system shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 67** For vessels carrying goods with pollution hazards entering or leaving ports, the carrier, cargo owner, or their agent must declare this to the maritime administrative department in advance. Only after approval may the vessel enter or leave the port, transit, stay, or conduct loading/unloading operations.

**Article 68** Documents, packaging, markings, quantity limits, etc., for goods with pollution hazards delivered to a vessel for shipment must comply with the relevant regulations for the cargo being loaded.

Goods with unknown pollution hazards requiring shipment by vessel shall be evaluated in advance according to relevant regulations.

During operations involving the loading/unloading of oil and toxic/hazardous goods, both the vessel and the shore must comply with safety and anti-pollution operating procedures.

**Article 69** Ports, wharves, loading/unloading stations, and ship repair yards must be equipped with adequate reception facilities for treating ship pollutants and wastes in accordance with relevant regulations, and keep such facilities in good condition.

Ports, wharves, loading/unloading stations, and vessels involved in loading/unloading oil must prepare oil spill pollution contingency plans and be equipped with corresponding oil spill pollution emergency equipment and devices.

**Article 70** Vessels and related operations shall comply with relevant laws, regulations, and standards, and take effective measures to prevent pollution of the marine environment. The maritime administrative department and other relevant departments shall strengthen the supervision and management of vessels and related operations.

Vessels conducting lightering operations of bulk liquid goods with pollution hazards must report to and obtain approval from the maritime administrative department in advance in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 71** When a vessel suffers a maritime accident that causes or is likely to cause major pollution damage to the marine environment, the national maritime administrative department has the authority to take compulsory measures to avoid or mitigate the pollution damage.

For vessels or offshore facilities on the high seas that, due to a maritime accident, cause major pollution damage consequences to or pose a pollution threat to the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, the national maritime administrative department has the authority to take necessary measures commensurate with the actual or potential damage.

**Article 72** All vessels have the duty to monitor marine pollution. Upon discovering a marine pollution accident or any act violating the provisions of this Law, they must immediately report to the nearest department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law.

Upon discovering marine pollution discharge or pollution incidents, civil aircraft must promptly report to the nearest civil aviation air traffic control unit. The unit receiving the report shall immediately notify the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

**Article 73** For any of the following acts in violation of the relevant provisions of this Law, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall order the cessation of the illegal act, correction within a time limit, or take measures such as ordering restricted production or suspension of production for remediation, and impose a fine; if the party refuses to correct, the department that made the penalty decision may, starting from the day following the date of the order for correction, impose a daily consecutive fine based on the original fine amount; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure:

(1) Discharging into the sea pollutants or other substances prohibited from discharge by this Law;
(2) Discharging pollutants into the sea not in accordance with the provisions of this Law, or discharging pollutants exceeding the standards or total volume control targets;
(3) Dumping wastes into the sea without obtaining a marine dumping permit;
(4) Failing to take immediate treatment measures upon the occurrence of an accident or other emergency event causing marine environmental pollution.

For acts under items (1) and (3) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under items (2) and (4) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 74** For any of the following acts in violation of the relevant provisions of this Law, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall issue a warning or impose a fine:

(1) Failing to declare matters related to pollutant discharge in accordance with regulations, or even refusing to declare, or making false declarations when declaring;
(2) Failing to report in accordance with regulations upon the occurrence of an accident or other emergency event;
(3) Failing to record dumping situations in accordance with regulations, or failing to submit dumping reports in accordance with regulations;
(4) Refusing to declare or falsely declaring matters related to the carriage of goods with pollution hazards by vessel.

For acts under items (1) and (3) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under items (2) and (4) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 75** For violating the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 19 of this Law by refusing an on-site inspection, or making false statements during the inspection, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall issue a warning and impose a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan.

**Article 76** For violating the provisions of this Law, causing damage to marine ecosystems such as coral reefs and mangroves, marine fishery resources, or marine protected areas, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall order correction within a time limit and the taking of remedial measures, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated.

**Article 77** For setting up a sea outfall in violation of paragraphs 1 and 3 of Article 30 of this Law, the environmental protection administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order its closure and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

If the marine, maritime, or fishery administrative department or the environmental protection department of the military discovers that the setting up of a sea outfall violates paragraphs 1 and 3 of Article 30 of this Law, it shall notify the environmental protection administrative department to impose a penalty in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

**Article 78** For violating paragraph 2 of Article 39 of this Law by transferring hazardous wastes through the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, the national maritime administrative department shall order the vessel illegally transporting such hazardous wastes to leave the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

**Article 79** If a coastal engineering construction project fails to conduct an environmental impact assessment in accordance with the law, it shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of the Environmental Impact Assessment Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 80** For violating the provisions of Article 44 of this Law, if a coastal engineering construction project is put into production or use without completing its environmental protection facilities, or if the environmental protection facilities do not meet the prescribed requirements, the environmental protection administrative department shall order the cessation of production or use and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 81** For violating the provisions of Article 45 of this Law by constructing a new industrial production project that seriously pollutes the marine environment, the people's government at or above the county level shall order its closure according to the management authority.

**Article 82** For carrying out a marine engineering construction project in violation of paragraph 1 of Article 47 of this Law, the marine administrative department shall order the cessation of construction and, based on the severity of the violation and the harmful consequences, impose a fine of not less than 1% but not more than 5% of the total investment amount of the construction project, and may order the restoration of the original state.

For violating the provisions of Article 48 of this Law, if a marine engineering construction project is put into production or use without completing its environmental protection facilities, or if the environmental protection facilities do not meet the prescribed requirements, the marine administrative department shall order the cessation of production or use and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 83** For violating the provisions of Article 49 of this Law by using materials containing radioactive substances exceeding the standard or readily leachable toxic and hazardous substances, the marine administrative department shall impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan and order the cessation of the operation of the construction project until the pollution hazard is eliminated.

**Article 84** For conducting offshore oil exploration and development activities in violation of the provisions of this Law, causing pollution to the marine environment, the national marine administrative department shall issue a warning and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 85** For violating the provisions of this Law by dumping not in accordance with the permit conditions, or dumping wastes into a dumping area that has been closed, the marine administrative department shall issue a warning and impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the permit may be temporarily seized or revoked.

**Article 86** For violating paragraph 3 of Article 55 of this Law by transporting wastes from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China into the sea areas under its jurisdiction for dumping, the national marine administrative department shall issue a warning and, based on the harmful consequences caused or likely to be caused, impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

**Article 87** For any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall issue a warning or impose a fine:

(1) Ports, wharves, loading/unloading stations, and vessels are not equipped with anti-pollution facilities or equipment;
(2) Vessels do not hold anti-pollution certificates or documents, or do not record pollutant discharge records in accordance with regulations;
(3) Engaging in ship dismantling, old ship conversion, salvage, or other underwater or above-water construction operations in waters and port areas, causing marine environmental pollution damage;
(4) The cargo carried by a vessel does not meet the conditions for anti-pollution suitability for transport.

For acts under items (1) and (4) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under item (2) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under item (3) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 88** For violating the provisions of this Law, if vessels, oil platforms, and ports, wharves, and loading/unloading stations involved in loading/unloading oil fail to prepare oil spill contingency plans, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall issue a warning or order correction within a time limit.

**Article 89** The party responsible for causing marine environmental pollution damage shall eliminate the hazard and compensate for the losses; if the marine environmental pollution damage is caused entirely by the intentional act or negligence of a third party, the third party shall eliminate the hazard and bear the liability for compensation.

If damage to the marine ecology, marine fishery resources, or marine protected areas causes significant losses to the State, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall, on behalf of the State, claim compensation for damages from the responsible party.

**Article 90** For a unit that violates the provisions of this Law and causes a marine environmental pollution accident, in addition to bearing liability for compensation in accordance with the law, the department exercising the power of marine environmental supervision and management in accordance with this Law shall impose a fine in accordance with paragraph 2 of this Article; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel may be fined not more than 50% of their income received from the unit in the previous year; if the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel are state functionaries, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

For causing a general or relatively major marine environmental pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at 20% of the direct losses; for causing a major or extraordinarily serious marine environmental pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at 30% of the direct losses.

If the act seriously pollutes the marine environment or damages the marine ecology, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 91** If pollution damage to the marine environment cannot be avoided despite the timely adoption of reasonable measures, and it falls entirely under one of the following circumstances, the relevant party responsible for causing the pollution damage shall be exempted from liability:

(1) War;
(2) Irresistible natural disasters;
(3) Negligence or other wrongful acts of the department responsible for lighthouses or other navigational aids in the performance of its duties.

**Article 92** Administrative penalties for violating the provisions of Article 12 of this Law concerning the payment of pollutant discharge fees and dumping fees shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 93** Marine environmental supervision and management personnel who abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, causing marine environmental pollution damage, shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter X Supplementary Provisions

**Article 94** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Marine environmental pollution damage" means the direct or indirect introduction of substances or energy into the marine environment, resulting in harmful effects such as damage to marine biological resources, hazards to human health, hindrance to fishing and other legitimate activities at sea, impairment of seawater quality, and degradation of environmental quality.
(2) "Internal waters" means all sea areas on the landward side of the baseline of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China.(3) Coastal wetlands refer to waters with a depth of less than six meters at low tide and their adjacent wet areas, including permanent waters with a depth not exceeding six meters, intertidal zones (or flood-prone areas), and coastal lowlands.

(4) Marine functional zoning refers to the definition of the dominant functions and usage scope of marine utilization based on the natural and social attributes of the sea, as well as the specific conditions of natural resources and the environment.

(5) Fishery waters refer to spawning grounds, feeding grounds, wintering grounds, migration passages for fish and shrimp, and aquaculture grounds for fish, shrimp, shellfish, and algae.

(6) Oil refers to any type of oil and its refined products.

(7) Oily mixtures refer to any mixture containing oil.

(8) Discharge refers to the act of releasing pollutants into the sea, including pumping, spilling, leaking, spraying, and dumping.

(9) Land-based pollution sources (referred to as land sources) refer to sites, facilities, etc., that discharge pollutants from land to sea, causing or potentially causing marine environmental pollution.

(10) Land-based pollutants refer to pollutants discharged from land-based pollution sources.

(11) Dumping refers to the act of disposing of waste and other harmful substances into the sea through ships, aircraft, platforms, or other means of transport, including the abandonment of ships, aircraft, platforms, their auxiliary facilities, and other floating devices.

(12) Coastal land areas refer to areas connected to the coast, or areas that directly or indirectly discharge pollutants into the sea and conduct related activities through pipelines, ditches, or facilities.

(13) Incineration at sea refers to the intentional burning of waste or other substances on incineration facilities at sea for the purpose of thermal destruction, excluding incidental activities during the normal operation of ships, platforms, or other artificial structures.

Article 95 The specific division of powers among relevant departments responsible for the supervision and management of the marine environment, where not stipulated in this Law, shall be determined by the State Council.

Article 96 Where international treaties concerning the protection of the marine environment that the People's Republic of China has concluded or acceded to contain provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaties shall prevail, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 97 This Law shall take effect as of April 1, 2000.
