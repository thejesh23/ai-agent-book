# Narcotics Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on December 29, 2007

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and punishing narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities, protecting the physical and mental health of citizens, and maintaining social order.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "narcotics" refer to opium, heroin, methamphetamine (ice), morphine, cannabis, cocaine, and other narcotic and psychotropic substances that are controlled by the state and can cause addiction.

For medical, teaching, and research needs, narcotic and psychotropic substances may be produced, operated, used, stored, and transported in accordance with the law.

**Article 3** Narcotics control is the common responsibility of the entire society. State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations and citizens shall fulfill their duties or obligations of narcotics control in accordance with this Law and relevant legal provisions.

**Article 4** Narcotics control work shall adhere to the principles of prevention first, comprehensive governance, and the simultaneous implementation of measures to prohibit cultivation, manufacture, trafficking, and use.

Narcotics control work shall adopt a working mechanism of unified leadership by the government, respective responsibilities of relevant departments, and broad participation by society.

**Article 5** The State Council shall establish a National Narcotics Control Commission, responsible for organizing, coordinating, and guiding national narcotics control work.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may establish narcotics control commissions as needed for narcotics control work, responsible for organizing, coordinating, and guiding narcotics control work within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 6** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate narcotics control work into their national economic and social development plans and include narcotics control funds in their fiscal budgets.

**Article 7** The state encourages social donations for narcotics control work and grants tax incentives in accordance with the law.

**Article 8** The state encourages scientific and technological research on narcotics control and promotes advanced drug detection technologies, equipment, and rehabilitation methods.

**Article 9** The state encourages citizens to report narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities. People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall protect whistleblowers, and commend and reward those who have rendered meritorious service in reporting such activities, as well as units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to narcotics control work.

**Article 10** The state encourages volunteers to participate in narcotics control publicity and education and social services for drug rehabilitation. Local people's governments at all levels shall provide guidance, training, and necessary working conditions for volunteers.

## Chapter II: Narcotics Control Publicity and Education

**Article 11** The state shall adopt various forms to carry out nationwide narcotics control publicity and education, popularize knowledge on drug prevention, enhance citizens' awareness of narcotics control, and improve their ability to consciously resist drugs.

The state encourages citizens and organizations to conduct public welfare narcotics control publicity activities.

**Article 12** People's governments at all levels shall regularly organize various forms of narcotics control publicity and education.

Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, and women's federations shall organize narcotics control publicity and education tailored to the characteristics of their respective target groups.

**Article 13** Education administrative departments and schools shall incorporate narcotics control knowledge into educational and teaching content and conduct narcotics control publicity and education for students. Public security organs, judicial administrative departments, and health administrative departments shall provide assistance.

**Article 14** News, publishing, culture, radio, film, television, and other relevant units shall conduct targeted narcotics control publicity and education for the public.

**Article 15** Operators and managers of public places such as airports, railway stations, long-distance bus stations, docks, hotels, and entertainment venues shall be responsible for narcotics control publicity and education in their premises, implement narcotics control preventive measures, and prevent narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities from occurring within their premises.

**Article 16** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall strengthen narcotics control publicity and education for their personnel.

**Article 17** Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall assist people's governments and public security organs in strengthening narcotics control publicity and education and implementing narcotics control preventive measures.

**Article 18** Parents or other guardians of minors shall educate minors about the dangers of drugs, prevent them from using or injecting drugs, or engaging in other narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities.

## Chapter III: Narcotics Control

**Article 19** The state exercises control over the cultivation of narcotic drug raw material plants. The illegal cultivation of opium poppy, coca plants, cannabis plants, and other raw material plants controlled by the state that can be used for refining and processing drugs is prohibited. The smuggling or illegal trading, transportation, carrying, or possession of non-sterilized seeds or seedlings of narcotic drug raw material plants is prohibited.

Upon discovering illegal cultivation of narcotic drug raw material plants, local people's governments at all levels shall immediately take measures to stop and eradicate them. Villagers' committees and residents' committees, upon discovering such illegal cultivation, shall promptly stop and eradicate it and report to the local public security organ.

**Article 20** Enterprises designated by the state for the cultivation of narcotic drug raw material plants must cultivate such plants in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The extraction and processing sites of enterprises designated by the state for the cultivation of narcotic drug raw material plants, as well as state-established narcotic drug storage warehouses, shall be designated as key state security targets.

Persons entering the extraction and processing sites of such enterprises or state-established narcotic drug storage warehouses, or other security zones, without authorization, shall be ordered by security personnel to leave immediately; those who refuse to leave shall be forcibly removed from the scene.

**Article 21** The state exercises control over narcotic and psychotropic substances and implements a licensing and inspection system for their experimental research, production, operation, use, storage, and transportation.

The state implements a licensing system for the production, operation, purchase, and transportation of precursor chemicals.

The illegal production, trading, transportation, storage, provision, possession, or use of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals is prohibited.

**Article 22** The state implements a licensing system for the import and export of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals. Relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, manage the import and export of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals in accordance with the law. The smuggling of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals is prohibited.

**Article 23** In the event of theft, robbery, loss, or other diversion of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, or precursor chemicals into illegal channels, the entity where the incident occurred shall immediately take necessary control measures and promptly report to the public security organ, while also reporting to the relevant competent authorities in accordance with regulations.

Upon receiving a report, or upon having evidence that narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, or precursor chemicals may have been diverted into illegal channels, the public security organ shall promptly conduct an investigation and may take necessary control measures against the relevant entity. Drug regulatory authorities, health administrative departments, and other relevant departments shall cooperate with the public security organ in its work.

**Article 24** The illegal teaching of methods for manufacturing narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals is prohibited. Upon receiving a report or discovering the illegal teaching of such manufacturing methods, the public security organ shall promptly investigate and handle the matter in accordance with the law.

**Article 25** Specific measures for the management of narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, and precursor chemicals shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 26** For the purposes of drug detection and seizure, public security organs may conduct inspections for narcotic drugs and precursor chemicals on persons, articles, goods, and means of transport in border areas, key traffic routes, ports, airports, railway stations, long-distance bus stations, and docks. Civil aviation, railway, and transportation departments shall provide cooperation.

Customs authorities shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen inspections of persons, articles, goods, and means of transport entering or leaving the country to prevent the smuggling of narcotic drugs and precursor chemicals.

Postal enterprises shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen inspections of mail items to prevent the mailing of narcotic drugs and the illegal mailing of precursor chemicals.

**Article 27** Entertainment venues shall establish a patrol system. Upon discovering narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities within the venue, they shall immediately report to the public security organ.

**Article 28** Narcotic drugs, implements for using or injecting drugs, illegal proceeds and income derived from narcotics-related illegal and criminal activities, and tools, equipment, and funds owned by the perpetrator and directly used for committing such activities, which are seized in accordance with the law, shall be confiscated and disposed of in accordance with regulations.

**Article 29** The anti-money laundering administrative authority shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen the monitoring of funds suspected of being related to narcotics crimes. The anti-money laundering administrative authority and other departments and institutions responsible for anti-money laundering supervision and management shall, upon discovering the flow of funds suspected of being related to narcotics crimes, promptly report to the investigative authorities and cooperate with them in conducting investigations.

**Article 30** The state shall establish and improve a narcotics monitoring and narcotics control information system, and carry out the collection, analysis, use, and exchange of narcotics monitoring and narcotics control information.

## Chapter IV: Drug Rehabilitation Measures

**Article 31** The state shall adopt various measures to help drug users overcome their addiction, and to educate and rescue them.

Persons addicted to drugs shall undergo drug rehabilitation treatment.

The criteria for determining drug addiction shall be formulated by the health administrative department, the drug regulatory authority, and the public security department of the State Council.

**Article 32** Public security organs may conduct necessary tests on persons suspected of using drugs; the persons being tested shall cooperate. For those who refuse to undergo testing, compulsory testing may be carried out with the approval of the responsible person of the public security organ at or above the county level or its dispatched agency.

Public security organs shall register drug users.

**Article 33** For persons addicted to drugs, the public security organ may order them to undergo community-based drug rehabilitation and shall simultaneously notify the urban sub-district office or township people's government of the person's registered residence or current residence. The period of community-based drug rehabilitation shall be three years.

Drug rehabilitation persons shall undergo community-based drug rehabilitation at their place of registered residence; those with a fixed abode in a current residence outside their registered residence may undergo community-based drug rehabilitation at their current residence.

**Article 34** Urban sub-district offices and township people's governments shall be responsible for community-based drug rehabilitation work. They may designate relevant grassroots organizations to sign community-based drug rehabilitation agreements with drug rehabilitation persons based on their personal and family circumstances, and implement targeted community-based drug rehabilitation measures. Public security organs, judicial administrative departments, health administrative departments, and civil affairs departments shall provide guidance and assistance for community-based drug rehabilitation work.

Urban sub-district offices, township people's governments, and the labor administrative departments of county-level people's governments shall provide necessary vocational skills training, employment guidance, and employment assistance to drug rehabilitation persons who are unemployed and lack employability.

**Article 35** Drug rehabilitation persons undergoing community-based drug rehabilitation shall abide by laws and regulations, consciously fulfill the community-based drug rehabilitation agreement, and undergo regular testing as required by the public security organ.

For drug rehabilitation persons who violate the community-based drug rehabilitation agreement, staff involved in community-based drug rehabilitation work shall provide criticism and education; for those who seriously violate the agreement or use or inject drugs again during the community-based drug rehabilitation period, the matter shall be promptly reported to the public security organ.

**Article 36** Drug users may voluntarily go to medical institutions qualified for drug rehabilitation treatment to receive treatment.

Establishing a drug rehabilitation medical institution or having a medical institution engage in drug rehabilitation treatment business shall meet the conditions stipulated by the health administrative department of the State Council, be subject to approval by the health administrative department of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and be filed with the public security organ at the same level. Drug rehabilitation treatment shall comply with the drug rehabilitation treatment norms formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council and shall accept supervision and inspection by the health administrative department.

Drug rehabilitation treatment shall not be for profit. No advertisements shall be made for drugs, medical devices, or treatment methods used in drug rehabilitation treatment. Fees charged for drug rehabilitation treatment shall be in accordance with the fee standards formulated by the price administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in conjunction with the health administrative department.

**Article 37** For the needs of drug rehabilitation treatment, medical institutions may conduct physical examinations and inspections of items carried by drug rehabilitation persons undergoing treatment; for those who pose a personal safety risk during treatment, necessary temporary protective restraint measures may be taken.

If a medical institution discovers that a drug rehabilitation person undergoing treatment uses or injects drugs during the treatment period, it shall promptly report to the public security organ.

**Article 38** The public security organ of the people's government at or above the county level shall make a decision on compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation for a person addicted to drugs under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Refusing to undergo community-based drug rehabilitation;
(2) Using or injecting drugs during the community-based drug rehabilitation period;
(3) Seriously violating the community-based drug rehabilitation agreement;
(4) Using or injecting drugs again after undergoing community-based drug rehabilitation or compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation.

For persons with severe drug addiction who cannot overcome their addiction through community-based drug rehabilitation, the public security organ may directly make a decision on compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation.

A person addicted to drugs who voluntarily accepts compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation may, with the consent of the public security organ, enter a compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility for rehabilitation.

**Article 39** Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation shall not apply to pregnant women or women breastfeeding an infant under one year of age who are addicted to drugs. Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation may not apply to minors under the age of sixteen who are addicted to drugs.

For persons addicted to drugs to whom compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation does not apply under the preceding paragraph, community-based drug rehabilitation shall be carried out in accordance with this Law. The urban sub-district office or township people's government responsible for community-based drug rehabilitation work shall strengthen assistance, education, and supervision, and ensure the implementation of community-based drug rehabilitation measures.

**Article 40** When a public security organ decides to impose compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation on a person addicted to drugs, it shall prepare a written decision on compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation, serve it on the person subject to the decision before the execution of the compulsory isolation, and notify the person's family members, employer, and the public security police station of the registered residence within twenty-four hours after service; if the person subject to the decision does not provide their true name and address, and their identity is unknown, the public security organ shall notify them after ascertaining their identity.

If the person subject to the decision disagrees with the decision on compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation made by the public security organ, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

**Article 41** Persons subject to compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation shall be sent by the public security organ that made the decision to a compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility for execution.

The establishment, management system, and funding guarantees for compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 42** When entering a compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility for rehabilitation, a drug rehabilitation person shall undergo a physical examination and inspection of the items they carry.

**Article 43** Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall provide targeted physiological and psychological treatment and physical rehabilitation training for drug rehabilitation persons based on the types of drugs they used or injected and the degree of their addiction.

As needed for drug rehabilitation, compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities may organize drug rehabilitation persons to participate in necessary productive labor and provide them with vocational skills training. If drug rehabilitation persons are organized to participate in productive labor, they shall be paid remuneration.

**Article 44** Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall implement separate management for drug rehabilitation persons based on factors such as gender, age, and illness.

Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall provide necessary care and treatment for drug rehabilitation persons with severe disabilities or illnesses; for those with infectious diseases, necessary isolation and treatment measures shall be taken in accordance with the law; for those who may engage in self-harm or self-mutilation, corresponding protective restraint measures may be taken.

Management personnel of compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall not physically punish, abuse, or insult drug rehabilitation persons.

**Article 45** Compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall be equipped with licensed physicians as needed for drug rehabilitation treatment. Licensed physicians in compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities who have the authority to prescribe narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances may use such substances for drug rehabilitation persons in accordance with relevant technical specifications.

Health administrative departments shall strengthen professional guidance and supervision over licensed physicians in compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities.

**Article 46** Family members of drug rehabilitation persons and staff of their employer or school may visit them in accordance with relevant regulations. With the approval of the compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility, a drug rehabilitation person may go out to visit their spouse or direct relatives.

Management personnel of compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facilities shall inspect items and mail delivered to drug rehabilitation persons by persons outside the facility to prevent the concealment of narcotic drugs. When inspecting mail, they shall protect the communication freedom and confidentiality of drug rehabilitation persons in accordance with the law.

**Article 47** The period of compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation shall be two years.

After one year of compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation, upon diagnostic evaluation, for drug rehabilitation persons with good rehabilitation progress, the compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility may propose an opinion on early release from compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation, subject to approval by the authority that made the decision on compulsory isolation.

Before the expiration of the compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation period, upon diagnostic evaluation, for drug rehabilitation persons who need an extension of the rehabilitation period, the compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation facility shall propose an opinion on extending the rehabilitation period, subject to approval by the authority that made the decision on compulsory isolation. The maximum extension of the compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation period shall be one year.

**Article 48** For persons released from compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation, the authority that made the decision on compulsory isolation may order them to undergo community-based rehabilitation for a period not exceeding three years.

Community-based rehabilitation shall be implemented with reference to the provisions of this Law on community-based drug rehabilitation.

**Article 49** Local people's governments at or above the county level may establish drug rehabilitation and recovery centers as needed for drug rehabilitation work; they shall support public welfare drug rehabilitation and recovery centers established by social forces in accordance with the law, providing necessary conveniences and assistance.

Drug rehabilitation persons may voluntarily live and work in drug rehabilitation and recovery centers. If drug rehabilitation and recovery centers organize drug rehabilitation persons to participate in productive labor, they shall pay remuneration with reference to the provisions of the national labor employment system.

**Article 50** Public security organs and judicial administrative departments shall provide necessary drug rehabilitation treatment to drug users who are lawfully detained, arrested, serving criminal sentences, or subject to compulsory educational measures.

**Article 51** The health administrative department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, in conjunction with the public security organ and the drug regulatory authority, may organize and carry out drug rehabilitation maintenance treatment work in accordance with relevant state regulations, based on the need to consolidate drug rehabilitation results and the prevalence of AIDS in the administrative region.

**Article 52** Drug rehabilitation persons shall not be discriminated against in terms of school enrollment, employment, or access to social security. Relevant departments, organizations, and individuals shall provide necessary guidance and assistance to drug rehabilitation persons in these areas.

## Chapter V: International Cooperation in Narcotics Control

**Article 53** The People's Republic of China shall carry out international cooperation in narcotics control in accordance with international treaties it has concluded or acceded to, or on the basis of the principle of reciprocity.

**Article 54** The National Narcotics Control Commission, authorized by the State Council, shall be responsible for organizing and carrying out international cooperation in narcotics control and fulfilling obligations under international narcotics control conventions.

**Article 55** Judicial assistance involving the prosecution of narcotics crimes shall be handled by judicial authorities in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

**Article 56** Relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, strengthen the exchange of narcotics control intelligence and information with law enforcement agencies of relevant countries or regions and international organizations, and carry out cooperation in narcotics control law enforcement in accordance with the law.

With the approval of the public security department of the State Council, public security organs of people's governments at or above the county level in border areas may carry out law enforcement cooperation with law enforcement agencies of relevant countries or regions.**Article 57** Where drug-related crimes are solved through international cooperation in drug control, the Government of the People's Republic of China may share with the relevant countries the confiscated illegal proceeds, income derived from such proceeds, and property used in drug-related crimes or the proceeds from the sale of such property.

**Article 58** The relevant departments of the State Council, authorized by the State Council, may support relevant countries in implementing alternative development of narcotic crops and developing alternative industries through channels such as foreign aid.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 59** Any of the following acts, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law:

(1) Smuggling, trafficking, transporting, or manufacturing narcotic drugs;
(2) Illegally possessing narcotic drugs;
(3) Illegally cultivating narcotic crops;
(4) Illegally buying, selling, transporting, carrying, or possessing seeds or seedlings of narcotic crops that have not been rendered inactive;
(5) Illegally teaching methods for manufacturing narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, or precursor chemicals;
(6) Forcing, enticing, instigating, or deceiving others into ingesting or injecting narcotic drugs;
(7) Providing narcotic drugs to others.

**Article 60** Any of the following acts, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law:

(1) Harboring criminals involved in smuggling, trafficking, transporting, or manufacturing narcotic drugs, or concealing, transferring, or hiding narcotic drugs or criminal proceeds for such criminals;
(2) Tipping off offenders during public security organs' investigations into drug-related crimes or illegal activities;
(3) Obstructing legally conducted drug inspections;
(4) Concealing, transferring, selling, or damaging property related to drug-related crimes or illegal activities that has been legally seized, impounded, or frozen by judicial or administrative law enforcement organs.

**Article 61** Providing premises for others to ingest or inject narcotic drugs, or introducing others to drug trafficking, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, the public security organ shall impose detention for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 3,000 yuan; if the circumstances are minor, detention for not more than five days or a fine of not more than 500 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 62** Persons who ingest or inject narcotic drugs shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law. Drug users who voluntarily register with public security organs or undergo drug rehabilitation treatment at qualified medical institutions shall not be penalized.

**Article 63** In activities involving the experimental research, production, operation, use, storage, transportation, import, or export of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances, or the cultivation of medicinal narcotic crops, if national regulations are violated, causing narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, or medicinal narcotic crops to flow into illegal channels, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 64** In activities involving the production, operation, purchase, transportation, import, or export of precursor chemicals, if national regulations are violated, causing precursor chemicals to flow into illegal channels, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 65** If entertainment venues or their employees engage in drug-related crimes or illegal activities, or provide conditions for persons entering the venues to engage in such activities, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

If the management personnel of an entertainment venue, knowing that group ingestion or injection of narcotic drugs or drug trafficking is occurring within the venue, fail to report to the public security organ, they shall be penalized in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 66** Persons who engage in drug rehabilitation treatment services without authorization shall be ordered by the health administration department to cease the illegal business activities, and their illegal gains and the drugs, medical instruments, and other items used shall be confiscated; if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 67** If a drug rehabilitation medical institution discovers that a person undergoing drug rehabilitation treatment ingests or injects narcotic drugs during the treatment period and fails to report to the public security organ, the health administration department shall order it to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification.

**Article 68** If compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation facilities, medical institutions, or physicians violate regulations by using narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 69** If staff members of public security organs, judicial administration departments, or other relevant competent authorities commit any of the following acts in drug control work, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law:

(1) Harboring or conniving at drug-related criminals or offenders;
(2) Subjecting persons undergoing drug rehabilitation to corporal punishment, abuse, humiliation, or other such acts;
(3) Misappropriating, intercepting, or withholding drug control funds;
(4) Unauthorized disposal of seized narcotic drugs or property related to drug-related crimes or illegal activities that has been seized, impounded, or frozen.

**Article 70** If relevant units and their staff members discriminate against persons who have undergone drug rehabilitation in matters such as school enrollment, employment, or enjoyment of social security benefits, the education administration department or the labor administration department shall order them to make corrections; if losses are caused to the parties concerned, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 71** This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2008. The "Decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on Drug Control" shall be simultaneously abolished.
