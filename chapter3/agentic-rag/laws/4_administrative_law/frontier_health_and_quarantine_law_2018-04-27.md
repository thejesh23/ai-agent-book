# Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on December 2, 1986

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2007

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Certain Laws" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China" at the Second Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to prevent infectious diseases from being introduced into or spread from the country, to implement frontier health and quarantine, and to protect human health.

Article 2 Frontier health and quarantine authorities shall be established at international seaports, airports, and land borders and river ports (hereinafter referred to as frontier ports) of the People's Republic of China to implement quarantine, surveillance, and sanitary supervision of infectious diseases in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 3 Infectious diseases as referred to in this Law mean quarantinable infectious diseases and infectious diseases under surveillance.

Quarantinable infectious diseases refer to plague, cholera, yellow fever, and other infectious diseases determined and announced by the State Council.

Infectious diseases under surveillance shall be determined and announced by the health administration department under the State Council.

Article 4 Persons, means of transport, transport equipment, and articles such as baggage, cargo, and postal parcels that may transmit quarantinable infectious diseases entering or leaving the country shall be subject to quarantine. They may be allowed to enter or leave the country only with the permission of the frontier health and quarantine authority. Specific measures shall be stipulated in the implementing rules of this Law.

Article 5 When a frontier health and quarantine authority discovers a quarantinable infectious disease or a suspected case thereof, it shall, in addition to taking necessary measures, immediately notify the local health administration department and, at the same time, report to the health administration department under the State Council by the fastest means, no later than twenty-four hours. The postal and telecommunications departments shall give priority to the transmission of epidemic reports.

The exchange of information on infectious disease epidemics between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries shall be handled by the health administration department under the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

Article 6 When a quarantinable infectious disease is prevalent abroad or domestically, the State Council may order the closure of relevant frontiers or take other emergency measures.

## Chapter II Quarantine

Article 7 Means of transport and persons entering the country must undergo quarantine at a designated location at the first frontier port of arrival. Except for pilots, no person shall board or leave the means of transport, or load or unload baggage, cargo, postal parcels, or other articles without the permission of the frontier health and quarantine authority. Specific measures shall be stipulated in the implementing rules of this Law.

Article 8 Means of transport and persons leaving the country must undergo quarantine at the last frontier port of departure.

Article 9 When a foreign vessel or aircraft, for any reason, berths or lands at a location within China that is not a port, the person in charge of the vessel or aircraft shall immediately report to the nearest frontier health and quarantine authority or local health administration department. Except in emergencies, no person shall board or leave the vessel or aircraft, or load or unload baggage, cargo, postal parcels, or other articles without the permission of the frontier health and quarantine authority or the local health administration department.

Article 10 When a quarantinable infectious disease or a suspected case thereof is discovered at a frontier port, or when a person dies from a cause other than accidental injury and the cause of death is unknown, the relevant units at the frontier port and the persons in charge of the means of transport shall immediately report to the frontier health and quarantine authority and apply for provisional quarantine.

Article 11 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall, based on the quarantine results provided by the quarantine physician, issue an entry quarantine certificate or an exit quarantine certificate to means of transport that are not infected with a quarantinable infectious disease or that have undergone sanitary treatment.

Article 12 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall immediately isolate persons infected with a quarantinable infectious disease; the isolation period shall be determined based on the results of medical examination. Persons suspected of being infected with a quarantinable infectious disease shall be detained for observation; the detention period shall be determined based on the incubation period of the infectious disease.

Corpses of persons who have died from a quarantinable infectious disease must be cremated at the nearest location.

Article 13 Means of transport undergoing entry quarantine shall be subject to disinfection, deratting, disinsection, or other sanitary treatment in any of the following circumstances:

(1) They come from an area where a quarantinable infectious disease is prevalent;
(2) They are contaminated by a quarantinable infectious disease;
(3) Rodents or disease-carrying insects harmful to human health are found on them.

If the person in charge of a foreign means of transport refuses to accept sanitary treatment, the means of transport may, except under special circumstances, be permitted to immediately leave the territory of the People's Republic of China under the supervision of the frontier health and quarantine authority.

Article 14 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall conduct sanitary inspection of baggage, cargo, postal parcels, and other articles coming from epidemic areas, contaminated by quarantinable infectious diseases, or likely to serve as vectors for the transmission of quarantinable infectious diseases, and shall implement disinfection, deratting, disinsection, or other sanitary treatment.

The consignor or agent of corpses or human remains entering or leaving the country must declare them to the frontier health and quarantine authority. They may be transported into or out of the country only after passing sanitary inspection.

## Chapter III Surveillance of Infectious Diseases

Article 15 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall implement surveillance of infectious diseases for persons entering or leaving the country and shall take necessary preventive and control measures.

Article 16 The frontier health and quarantine authority has the right to require persons entering or leaving the country to fill out a health declaration card and present a certificate of vaccination against a specific infectious disease, a health certificate, or other relevant documents.

Article 17 For persons suffering from an infectious disease under surveillance, persons coming from an area abroad where an infectious disease under surveillance is prevalent, or persons in close contact with a person suffering from an infectious disease under surveillance, the frontier health and quarantine authority shall, depending on the circumstances, issue a medical convenience card, implement detention for observation, or take other preventive and control measures, and shall promptly notify the local health administration department. Medical units in various localities shall give priority to the diagnosis and treatment of persons holding a medical convenience card.

## Chapter IV Sanitary Supervision

Article 18 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall, in accordance with the health standards prescribed by the State, implement sanitary supervision over the sanitary conditions of frontier ports and the sanitary conditions of means of transport entering or leaving the country that are staying at frontier ports:

(1) Supervise and guide relevant personnel in the prevention and elimination of rodents and disease-carrying insects;
(2) Inspect and test food, drinking water, and their storage, supply, and transportation facilities;
(3) Supervise the health status of personnel engaged in the supply of food and drinking water and inspect their health certificates;
(4) Supervise and inspect the disposal of garbage, waste, sewage, excrement, and ballast water.

Article 19 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall appoint frontier port sanitary supervisors to carry out the tasks assigned by the frontier health and quarantine authority.

When performing their duties, frontier port sanitary supervisors have the right to conduct sanitary supervision and provide technical guidance for frontier ports and means of transport entering or leaving the country, to propose improvements for poor sanitary conditions and factors that may cause the spread of infectious diseases, and to coordinate with relevant departments to take necessary measures for sanitary treatment.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 20 For units or individuals that violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the frontier health and quarantine authority may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine:

(1) Evading quarantine or concealing the true situation from the frontier health and quarantine authority;
(2) Persons entering the country boarding or leaving means of transport, or loading or unloading baggage, cargo, postal parcels, or other articles without the permission of the frontier health and quarantine authority and refusing to heed advice.

All fines shall be turned over to the State treasury.

Article 21 If a party disagrees with the decision to impose a fine made by the frontier health and quarantine authority, it may file a lawsuit with the local people's court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the notice. If no lawsuit is filed within the time limit and the decision is not complied with, the frontier health and quarantine authority may apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 22 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, causing the spread of a quarantinable infectious disease or creating a serious risk of its spread, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 23 Staff members of the frontier health and quarantine authority shall enforce the law impartially, be devoted to their duties, and promptly conduct quarantine of means of transport and persons entering or leaving the country. Those who violate the law or neglect their duties shall be subject to administrative sanctions; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 24 If an international treaty concerning health and quarantine concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 25 For exchanges between the frontier defense authorities of the People's Republic of China and those of neighboring countries in border areas, and for temporary cross-border movements of residents living in adjacent border areas of the two countries within designated border areas, the quarantine of means of transport and persons entering or leaving the country shall be handled in accordance with agreements between the two parties. In the absence of an agreement, it shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese government.

Article 26 The frontier health and quarantine authority shall charge fees for implementing health and quarantine in accordance with State regulations.

Article 27 This Law shall come into force as of May 1, 1987. The "Frontier Health and Quarantine Regulations of the People's Republic of China" promulgated on December 23, 1957, shall be repealed simultaneously.
