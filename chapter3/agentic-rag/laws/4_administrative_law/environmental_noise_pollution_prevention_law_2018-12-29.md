# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Environmental Noise Pollution

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 29, 1996

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution, protecting and improving the living environment, safeguarding human health, and promoting economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "environmental noise" means sounds that are generated in industrial production, construction, transportation, and social activities and that interfere with the surrounding living environment.

For the purposes of this Law, "environmental noise pollution" means that the environmental noise generated exceeds the environmental noise emission standards prescribed by the State and interferes with the normal life, work, and study of others.

Article 3 This Law shall apply to the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

This Law shall not apply to the prevention and control of noise hazards suffered in the course of performing one's own production or business operations.

Article 4 The State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall incorporate the work of preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution into their environmental protection plans and adopt economic and technical policies and measures conducive to the protection of the acoustic environment.

Article 5 When formulating urban and rural construction plans, local people's governments at all levels shall give full consideration to the impact of noise generated by construction projects and regional development and renovation on the surrounding living environment, make overall plans, rationally arrange functional zones and construction layouts, and prevent or mitigate environmental noise pollution.

Article 6 The competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution nationwide.

The competent departments of ecology and environment under local people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise unified supervision and management over the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution within their respective administrative regions.

The competent departments of public security, transportation, railways, civil aviation, and other relevant departments, as well as harbor superintendency agencies, at all levels shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, exercise supervision and management over the prevention and control of noise pollution from transportation and social activities.

Article 7 All units and individuals have the obligation to protect the acoustic environment and have the right to report and accuse any unit or individual that causes environmental noise pollution.

Article 8 The State encourages and supports scientific research and technological development for the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution, promotes advanced prevention and control technologies, and disseminates scientific knowledge on the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution.

Article 9 People's governments shall reward units and individuals that have achieved outstanding results in the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution.

## Chapter II Supervision and Management of the Prevention and Control of Environmental Noise Pollution

Article 10 The competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council shall formulate national acoustic environment quality standards for different functional zones.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the national acoustic environment quality standards, designate the areas within their respective administrative regions where various acoustic environment quality standards apply and manage them.

Article 11 The competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council shall, based on the national acoustic environment quality standards and the national economic and technical conditions, formulate national environmental noise emission standards.

Article 12 When determining the layout of construction projects, the urban planning department shall, in accordance with the national acoustic environment quality standards and the design specifications for sound insulation of civil buildings, reasonably designate the noise-proof distances between buildings and traffic arteries and put forward corresponding planning and design requirements.

Article 13 New construction, renovation, and expansion projects must comply with the relevant State regulations on environmental protection management for construction projects.

If a construction project is likely to cause environmental noise pollution, the construction unit must submit an environmental impact report, specifying the measures for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution, and submit it to the competent department of ecology and environment for approval in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the State.

The environmental impact report shall include the opinions of the units and residents at the location of the construction project.

Article 14 The facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution of a construction project must be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project.

Before a construction project is put into production or use, its facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution must be accepted in accordance with the standards and procedures prescribed by the State; if they fail to meet the requirements prescribed by the State, the construction project shall not be put into production or use.

Article 15 Enterprises and institutions that generate environmental noise pollution must maintain the normal operation of their facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution; if they dismantle or idle such facilities, they must obtain prior approval from the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level.

Article 16 Units that generate environmental noise pollution shall take measures to control it and pay a fee for discharging pollutants exceeding the standards as prescribed by the State.

The fees collected for discharging pollutants exceeding the standards must be used for the prevention and control of pollution and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

Article 17 For enterprises and institutions that cause serious environmental noise pollution in areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated, a deadline shall be set for them to control the pollution.

The units that are given a deadline for control must complete the control task within the specified period. The decision to set a deadline for control shall be made by the people's government at or above the county level within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council.

For small enterprises and institutions, the people's government at or above the county level may, within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council, authorize its competent department of ecology and environment to decide on the deadline for control.

Article 18 The State implements a phase-out system for outdated equipment that causes serious environmental noise pollution.

The competent department for comprehensive economic affairs under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, publish a catalogue of equipment that causes serious environmental noise pollution and is prohibited from being produced, sold, or imported within a specified period.

Producers, sellers, or importers must stop producing, selling, or importing the equipment listed in the catalogue referred to in the preceding paragraph within the period prescribed by the competent department for comprehensive economic affairs under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 19 If it is truly necessary to emit sporadic and intense noise during production activities within urban areas, an application must be submitted in advance to the local public security organ, and the activity may only be carried out after approval. The local public security organ shall make a public announcement to the community.

Article 20 The competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council shall establish an environmental noise monitoring system, formulate monitoring specifications, and organize a monitoring network together with the relevant departments.

Environmental noise monitoring institutions shall submit the results of environmental noise monitoring in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council.

Article 21 The competent departments of ecology and environment under people's governments at or above the county level and other supervisory and management departments and institutions responsible for the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution have the right, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, to conduct on-site inspections of units that discharge environmental noise within their jurisdiction. The units being inspected must truthfully report the situation and provide the necessary information. The inspecting departments or institutions shall keep confidential the technical and business secrets of the units being inspected.

Inspectors conducting on-site inspections shall present their credentials.

## Chapter III Prevention and Control of Industrial Noise Pollution

Article 22 For the purposes of this Law, "industrial noise" means sounds generated by the use of fixed equipment during industrial production activities that interfere with the surrounding living environment.

Article 23 Industrial noise discharged into the surrounding living environment within urban areas shall comply with the national standards for environmental noise emission at the boundaries of industrial enterprises.

Article 24 Industrial enterprises that cause environmental noise pollution due to the use of fixed equipment in industrial production must, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council, declare to the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level the types and quantities of equipment causing environmental noise pollution they possess, the noise values emitted under normal operating conditions, and the status of facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution, and provide technical data on the prevention and control of noise pollution.

If there are significant changes in the types, quantities, noise values of equipment causing environmental noise pollution, or in the prevention and control facilities, a timely declaration must be made, and appropriate prevention and control measures must be taken.

Article 25 Industrial enterprises that generate environmental noise pollution shall take effective measures to mitigate the impact of noise on the surrounding living environment.

Article 26 The relevant competent departments under the State Council shall, for industrial equipment that may cause environmental noise pollution, gradually stipulate noise limits in the national standards or industry standards for products formulated in accordance with the law, based on the requirements of acoustic environment protection and the national economic and technical conditions.

The noise values emitted by the industrial equipment referred to in the preceding paragraph during operation shall be indicated in the relevant technical documents.

## Chapter IV Prevention and Control of Construction Noise Pollution

Article 27 For the purposes of this Law, "construction noise" means sounds generated during the process of building construction that interfere with the surrounding living environment.

Article 28 Construction noise discharged into the surrounding living environment within urban built-up areas shall comply with the national standards for environmental noise emission at the boundaries of construction sites.

Article 29 Within urban built-up areas, if the use of mechanical equipment during construction may cause environmental noise pollution, the construction unit must, fifteen days before the commencement of the project, declare to the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level at the project location the project name, construction site and duration, the potential environmental noise values, and the measures taken to prevent and control environmental noise pollution.

Article 30 Within areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated in urban built-up areas, construction operations that generate environmental noise pollution are prohibited at night, except for emergency repair and rescue operations, and operations that must be continuous due to production process requirements or special needs.

If continuous operations are necessary due to special needs, a certificate must be obtained from the people's government at or above the county level or its relevant competent department.

For night operations as referred to in the preceding paragraph, a public notice must be given to nearby residents.

## Chapter V Prevention and Control of Transportation Noise Pollution

Article 31 For the purposes of this Law, "transportation noise" means sounds generated by the operation of motor vehicles, railway locomotives, motor vessels, aircraft, and other means of transportation that interfere with the surrounding living environment.

Article 32 The manufacture, sale, or import of automobiles that exceed the prescribed noise limits is prohibited.

Article 33 The mufflers and horns of motor vehicles traveling within urban built-up areas must comply with the requirements prescribed by the State. Motor vehicles must be properly maintained and serviced to maintain good technical performance and prevent environmental noise pollution.

Article 34 When motor vehicles travel within urban built-up areas, motor vessels navigate inland waterways within urban built-up areas, and railway locomotives pass through or enter urban built-up areas or sanatorium areas, they must use sound devices in accordance with regulations.

The installation and use of sirens on motor vehicles such as police cars, fire engines, emergency rescue vehicles, and ambulances must comply with the regulations of the public security department under the State Council; the use of sirens is prohibited when performing non-emergency tasks.

Article 35 The public security organs of city people's governments may, based on the needs of acoustic environment protection in their respective urban built-up areas, designate road sections and time periods where motor vehicles are prohibited from driving or using sound devices, and make a public announcement to the community.

Article 36 When constructing expressways, urban elevated roads, or light rail transit lines that pass through existing areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated and may cause environmental noise pollution, sound barriers shall be installed or other effective measures to control environmental noise pollution shall be taken.

Article 37 When constructing noise-sensitive buildings on both sides of existing urban traffic arteries, the construction unit shall maintain a certain distance as prescribed by the State and take measures to mitigate and avoid the impact of traffic noise.

Article 38 When using loudspeakers for directing operations at stations, railway marshalling yards, ports, docks, airports, and other locations, the volume shall be controlled to mitigate the impact of noise on the surrounding living environment.

Article 39 If railways passing through urban residential areas or cultural and educational areas cause environmental noise pollution due to the operation of railway locomotives, the local city people's government shall organize the railway department and other relevant departments to formulate plans for mitigating environmental noise pollution. The railway department and other relevant departments shall take effective measures in accordance with the requirements of the plans to mitigate environmental noise pollution.

Article 40 Except for takeoff, landing, or circumstances prescribed by law, civil aircraft shall not fly over urban built-up areas. City people's governments shall designate areas around the clear zones for aircraft takeoff and landing where the construction of noise-sensitive buildings is restricted; if noise-sensitive buildings are constructed within such areas, the construction unit shall take measures to mitigate and avoid the impact of noise generated by aircraft operations. The civil aviation department shall take effective measures to mitigate environmental noise pollution.

## Chapter VI Prevention and Control of Social Life Noise Pollution

Article 41 For the purposes of this Law, "social life noise" means sounds generated by human activities, other than industrial noise, construction noise, and transportation noise, that interfere with the surrounding living environment.

Article 42 Within areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated in urban built-up areas, commercial enterprises that cause environmental noise pollution due to the use of fixed equipment in their business operations must, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council, declare to the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level the status of the equipment causing environmental noise pollution they possess and the status of the facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution.

Article 43 The boundary noise of newly established commercial cultural and entertainment venues must comply with the national environmental noise emission standards; if they do not comply with the national environmental noise emission standards, the competent cultural administration department shall not issue a cultural business license, and the market supervision and management department shall not issue a business license.

For cultural and entertainment venues in operation, their operators must take effective measures to ensure that their boundary noise does not exceed the national environmental noise emission standards.

Article 44 The use of high-pitched loudspeakers or other methods of emitting high noise to solicit customers in commercial business activities is prohibited.

For equipment and facilities such as air conditioners and cooling towers that may cause environmental noise pollution used in commercial business activities, their operators shall take measures to ensure that their boundary noise does not exceed the national environmental noise emission standards.

Article 45 The use of high-pitched loudspeakers by any unit or individual within areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated in urban built-up areas is prohibited.

When organizing entertainment, gatherings, or other activities in public places such as streets, squares, and parks within urban built-up areas, if the use of audio equipment may generate excessive volume that interferes with the surrounding living environment, the regulations of the local public security organ must be observed.

Article 46 When using household appliances, musical instruments, or engaging in other indoor recreational activities at home, the volume shall be controlled or other effective measures shall be taken to avoid causing environmental noise pollution to surrounding residents.

Article 47 When conducting interior decoration activities in residential buildings that have been completed and delivered for use, the working hours shall be limited, and other effective measures shall be taken to mitigate and avoid causing environmental noise pollution to surrounding residents.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 48 If, in violation of Article 14 of this Law, a construction project is put into production or use without the necessary supporting facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution having been built or meeting the requirements prescribed by the State, the competent department of ecology and environment at or above the county level shall order correction within a specified period and impose a fine on the unit and the individual; if serious environmental pollution or ecological damage is caused, it shall order the suspension of production or use, or report to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order closure.

Article 49 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a unit refuses to declare or falsely declares the required items for environmental noise emission declaration, the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

Article 50 If, in violation of Article 15 of this Law, a unit dismantles or idles facilities for preventing and controlling environmental noise pollution without the approval of the competent department of ecology and environment, resulting in environmental noise emissions exceeding the prescribed standards, the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and impose a fine.

Article 51 If, in violation of Article 16 of this Law, a unit fails to pay the fee for discharging pollutants exceeding the standards as prescribed by the State, the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

Article 52 If, in violation of Article 17 of this Law, an enterprise or institution fails to complete the control task within the specified deadline for control, in addition to being charged an additional fee for discharging pollutants exceeding the standards in accordance with State regulations, a fine may be imposed based on the harmful consequences caused, or the enterprise or institution may be ordered to suspend operations, relocate, or close down.

The fine referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be decided by the competent department of ecology and environment. The order to suspend operations, relocate, or close down shall be decided by the people's government at or above the county level within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 53 If, in violation of Article 18 of this Law, a unit produces, sells, or imports equipment that is prohibited from being produced, sold, or imported, the competent department for comprehensive economic affairs under the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction; if the circumstances are serious, the competent department for comprehensive economic affairs under the people's government at or above the county level shall submit an opinion and report to the people's government at the same level to order the suspension of operations or closure within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 54 If, in violation of Article 19 of this Law, a unit engages in activities that generate sporadic and intense noise without the approval of the local public security organ, the public security organ may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

Article 55 If a unit that discharges environmental noise, in violation of Article 21 of this Law, refuses an on-site inspection by the competent department of ecology and environment or other departments or institutions exercising the power of supervision and management over environmental noise in accordance with this Law, or practices fraud during the inspection, the competent department of ecology and environment or other supervisory and management departments or institutions exercising the power of supervision and management over environmental noise in accordance with this Law may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

Article 56 If a construction unit, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 30 of this Law, carries out construction operations that generate environmental noise pollution at night within areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated in urban built-up areas, where such operations are prohibited, the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level at the project location shall order correction and may concurrently impose a fine.

Article 57 If, in violation of Article 34 of this Law, a motor vehicle fails to use sound devices in accordance with regulations, the local public security organ may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

If a motor vessel commits the illegal act referred to in the preceding paragraph, the harbor superintendency agency may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine.

If a railway locomotive commits the illegal act referred to in the first paragraph, the competent railway department shall impose administrative sanctions on the responsible personnel.

Article 58 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts is committed, the public security organ shall issue a warning and may concurrently impose a fine:

(1) Using high-pitched loudspeakers within areas where noise-sensitive buildings are concentrated in urban built-up areas;
(2) Organizing entertainment, gatherings, or other activities in public places such as streets, squares, and parks within urban built-up areas, using audio equipment, and generating excessive volume that interferes with the surrounding living environment, in violation of the regulations of the local public security organ;
(3) Failing to take measures in accordance with Articles 46 and 47 of this Law, thereby emitting environmental noise from within a household that seriously interferes with the lives of surrounding residents.

Article 59 If, in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 43 or the second paragraph of Article 44 of this Law, environmental noise pollution is caused, the competent department of ecology and environment under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and may concurrently impose a fine.Article 60: Any entity or individual that, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 44 of this Law, causes environmental noise pollution shall be ordered by the public security authority to make corrections and may also be fined.

Where a people's government at or above the provincial level decides, in accordance with the law, that the administrative penalty power provided for in the preceding paragraph shall be exercised by the competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government at or above the county level, such decision shall prevail.

Article 61: Any entity or individual harmed by environmental noise pollution shall have the right to demand that the injuring party eliminate the harm; if losses are caused, the injuring party shall compensate for the losses in accordance with the law.

Disputes over liability for compensation and the amount of compensation may, upon the request of the parties, be mediated by the competent department of ecological environment or other supervisory and administrative departments or agencies responsible for the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution; if mediation fails, the parties may file a lawsuit with the people's court. The parties may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 62: Any supervisory and administrative personnel for the prevention and control of environmental noise pollution who abuses their power, neglects their duties, or engages in malpractice for personal gain shall be given administrative sanctions by their unit or the competent authority at a higher level; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 63: For the purposes of this Law, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Noise emission" means the radiation of noise from a noise source into the surrounding living environment.

(2) "Noise-sensitive buildings" means buildings such as hospitals, schools, government agencies, scientific research institutions, and residences that need to maintain quiet conditions.

(3) "Noise-sensitive building concentration areas" means medical areas, cultural, educational, and scientific research areas, and areas primarily composed of government agencies or residential buildings.

(4) "Nighttime" means the period from 10:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m.

(5) "Motor vehicles" means automobiles and motorcycles.

Article 64: This Law shall come into effect as of March 1, 1997. The "Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Environmental Noise Pollution" promulgated by the State Council on September 26, 1989, shall be simultaneously abolished.
