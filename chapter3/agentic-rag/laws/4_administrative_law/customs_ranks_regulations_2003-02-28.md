# Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Customs Ranks

Adopted at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on February 28, 2003

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the construction of the customs contingent, enhance the sense of responsibility, honor, and organizational discipline of customs staff, and facilitate the lawful performance of their duties.

Article 2 The customs implements a rank system. State civil servants of the General Administration of Customs, its regional offices, commissioner's offices, all directly subordinate customs offices, subordinate customs offices, and offices may be awarded customs ranks.

Customs anti-smuggling police shall implement the people's police rank system.

Article 3 Customs ranks are titles and insignia that distinguish the grades of customs staff, indicate their status, and represent the honor conferred by the state upon customs staff.

Article 4 Customs staff shall be assigned ranks corresponding to their official positions.

Article 5 A customs staff member with a higher rank is considered the superior of one with a lower rank. When a customs staff member with a higher rank is subordinate in position to one with a lower rank, the one with the higher position is the superior.

Article 6 The General Administration of Customs is responsible for the administration of customs ranks.

## Chapter II Establishment of Customs Rank Grades

Article 7 Customs ranks are established in the following five grades and thirteen levels:

(1) Customs Director General and Customs Deputy Director General;

(2) Customs Supervisors: First, Second, and Third Class;

(3) Customs Inspectors: First, Second, and Third Class;

(4) Customs Investigators: First, Second, and Third Class;

(5) Customs Officers: First and Second Class.

Article 8 Customs staff shall be assigned the following ranks corresponding to their official positions:

(1) Director-General level: Customs Director General;

(2) Deputy Director-General level: Customs Deputy Director General;

(3) Bureau Director level: First Class Customs Supervisor to Second Class Customs Supervisor;

(4) Deputy Bureau Director level: Second Class Customs Supervisor to Third Class Customs Supervisor;

(5) Division Director level: Third Class Customs Supervisor to Second Class Customs Inspector;

(6) Deputy Division Director level: First Class Customs Inspector to Third Class Customs Inspector;

(7) Section Chief level: Second Class Customs Inspector to Second Class Customs Investigator;

(8) Deputy Section Chief level: Third Class Customs Inspector to Third Class Customs Investigator;

(9) Staff level: First Class Customs Investigator to First Class Customs Officer;

(10) Clerk level: Second Class Customs Investigator to Second Class Customs Officer.

## Chapter III Awarding of Customs Ranks

Article 9 The awarding of customs ranks shall be based on the staff member's current position, moral character and professional competence, length of service in the position, and total years of work.

Article 10 When customs recruits or transfers in staff from other departments, the corresponding rank shall be awarded after the position is determined.

Article 11 Customs ranks shall be awarded upon approval according to the following authority:

(1) Customs Director General, Customs Deputy Director General, First Class Customs Supervisor, and Second Class Customs Supervisor shall be awarded upon approval by the Premier of the State Council;

(2) Third Class Customs Supervisor to Third Class Customs Inspector shall be awarded upon approval by the Director General of the General Administration of Customs;

(3) Ranks below First Class Customs Investigator for staff of the General Administration of Customs and its dispatched agencies shall be awarded upon approval by the Director of the Political Department of the General Administration of Customs;

(4) Ranks below First Class Customs Investigator for staff of each directly subordinate and subordinate customs office shall be awarded upon approval by the Director of that directly subordinate customs office.

## Chapter IV Promotion of Customs Ranks

Article 12 Customs staff with ranks below Second Class Customs Inspector shall be promoted within the range of ranks corresponding to their position grade according to the following periods:

From Second Class Customs Officer to First Class Customs Investigator, promotion by one level every three years;

From First Class Customs Investigator to First Class Customs Inspector, promotion by one level every four years.

Article 13 Customs staff with ranks below Second Class Customs Inspector, upon expiration of the promotion period and having passed the assessment meeting the conditions for promotion, shall be promoted level by level. Those who do not meet the conditions shall have their promotion deferred. Those with outstanding achievements in their work may be promoted ahead of schedule upon approval.

Article 14 Promotion of customs staff with ranks of First Class Customs Inspector and above shall be conducted through selective promotion within the range of ranks corresponding to their position grade, based on their moral character, professional competence, and work performance.

Article 15 When a customs staff member is promoted to a higher position, if their current rank is lower than the minimum rank of the new position's rank range, they shall be promoted to that minimum rank.

Article 16 Promotion from Customs Officer to Customs Investigator, from Customs Investigator to Customs Inspector, and from Customs Inspector to Customs Supervisor shall only occur after passing the required training.

Article 17 The approval authority for rank promotion shall be governed by the provisions of Article 11 of these Regulations.

## Chapter V Retention, Demotion, and Cancellation of Customs Ranks

Article 18 Upon retirement, a customs staff member's rank shall be retained, but the insignia may not be worn.

If a customs staff member is transferred out, resigns, or is dismissed, their rank shall not be retained.

Article 19 If a customs staff member is transferred to a lower position due to incompetence, and their current rank is higher than the maximum rank of the new position's rank range, their rank shall be demoted to that maximum rank. The approval authority for rank demotion shall be the same as that for the original rank.

Article 20 If a customs staff member receives an administrative sanction of demotion or removal from office, their rank shall be correspondingly reduced. The approval authority for rank demotion shall be the same as that for the original rank. After rank demotion, the period for subsequent promotion shall be recalculated from the demoted rank level.

Rank demotion shall not apply to Second Class Customs Officers.

Article 21 If a customs staff member receives an administrative sanction of expulsion, or is criminally sentenced to deprivation of political rights or fixed-term imprisonment or a heavier penalty, their rank shall be correspondingly cancelled, and no further approval procedures are required.

If a retired customs staff member commits a crime, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 22 The design of customs rank insignia and the method of wearing them shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 23 These Regulations shall come into force on the date of promulgation.
