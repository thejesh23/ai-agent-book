# Compulsory Education Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Session of the Sixth National People's Congress on April 12, 1986

Revised at the 22nd Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on June 29, 2006

First Amendment on April 24, 2015, in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Compulsory Education Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on December 29, 2018, in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China at the Seventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Education Law to ensure the right of school-age children and adolescents to receive compulsory education, guarantee the implementation of compulsory education, and improve the quality of the entire nation.

Article 2 The State implements a system of nine-year compulsory education.

Compulsory education is education uniformly implemented by the State that all school-age children and adolescents must receive, and it is a public welfare undertaking that the State must guarantee.

In implementing compulsory education, no tuition or miscellaneous fees shall be charged.

The State establishes a mechanism to ensure funding for compulsory education, guaranteeing the implementation of the compulsory education system.

Article 3 Compulsory education must implement the State's educational policy, promote quality education, improve the quality of education, enable school-age children and adolescents to develop comprehensively in morality, intelligence, physical fitness, etc., and lay the foundation for cultivating socialist builders and successors who are well-educated, well-disciplined, and have ideals and moral integrity.

Article 4 All school-age children and adolescents with the nationality of the People's Republic of China, regardless of gender, ethnicity, race, family property status, religious belief, etc., shall enjoy the equal right to receive compulsory education in accordance with the law and fulfill the obligation to receive compulsory education.

Article 5 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall fulfill their respective duties as stipulated in this Law to ensure the right of school-age children and adolescents to receive compulsory education.

Parents or other legal guardians of school-age children and adolescents shall ensure that they enroll in school on time to receive and complete compulsory education in accordance with the law.

Schools that implement compulsory education in accordance with the law shall complete educational and teaching tasks according to prescribed standards and ensure the quality of education and teaching.

Social organizations and individuals shall create a favorable environment for school-age children and adolescents to receive compulsory education.

Article 6 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall rationally allocate educational resources, promote the balanced development of compulsory education, improve the conditions of weak schools, and take measures to ensure the implementation of compulsory education in rural areas and ethnic minority areas, and to ensure that school-age children and adolescents from families with financial difficulties and those with disabilities receive compulsory education.

The State organizes and encourages economically developed areas to support economically underdeveloped areas in implementing compulsory education.

Article 7 Compulsory education is implemented under the leadership of the State Council, with overall planning and implementation by people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and with management primarily by people's governments at the county level.

The administrative departments for education of people's governments at or above the county level are specifically responsible for the implementation of compulsory education; other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for the implementation of compulsory education within their respective duties.

Article 8 The educational supervision institutions of people's governments supervise the implementation of laws and regulations related to compulsory education, the quality of education and teaching, and the balanced development of compulsory education, and publish supervision reports to the public.

Article 9 Any social organization or individual has the right to report or file a complaint with relevant State organs against any act violating this Law.

If a major incident violating this Law occurs, hindering the implementation of compulsory education and causing significant social impact, the responsible leader of the people's government or the head of the administrative department for education of the people's government shall take responsibility and resign.

Article 10 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall commend and reward social organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the implementation of compulsory education in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter II Students

Article 11 For any child who has reached the age of six, his or her parents or other legal guardians shall send him or her to school to receive and complete compulsory education; children in areas where conditions are not available may be postponed to the age of seven.

If a school-age child or adolescent needs to postpone enrollment or suspend schooling due to physical condition, his or her parents or other legal guardians shall submit an application, which shall be approved by the local township people's government or the administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level.

Article 12 School-age children and adolescents shall be admitted to school without entrance examinations. Local people's governments at all levels shall ensure that school-age children and adolescents attend school near their registered residence.

For school-age children and adolescents whose parents or other legal guardians work or live in a place other than their registered residence, if they receive compulsory education at the place where their parents or other legal guardians work or live, the local people's government shall provide them with equal conditions for receiving compulsory education. Specific measures shall be formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level shall ensure that children of military personnel within its administrative region receive compulsory education.

Article 13 The administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level and the township people's government shall organize and urge school-age children and adolescents to enroll in school, help solve difficulties for school-age children and adolescents in receiving compulsory education, and take measures to prevent school-age children and adolescents from dropping out.

Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall assist the government in carrying out the work and urge school-age children and adolescents to enroll in school.

Article 14 Employers are prohibited from employing school-age children and adolescents who should receive compulsory education.

Social organizations that recruit school-age children and adolescents for professional training in arts, sports, etc., with approval in accordance with relevant State regulations, shall ensure that the recruited school-age children and adolescents receive compulsory education; if they implement compulsory education themselves, they shall obtain approval from the administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level.

## Chapter III Schools

Article 15 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on factors such as the number and distribution of school-age children and adolescents residing within their administrative regions, formulate and adjust school setting plans in accordance with relevant State regulations. If a new residential area requires the establishment of a school, it shall be carried out simultaneously with the construction of the residential area.

Article 16 School construction shall meet the national standards for running schools and adapt to the needs of education and teaching; it shall comply with the national site selection requirements and construction standards to ensure the safety of students and faculty.

Article 17 People's governments at the county level shall, as needed, set up boarding schools to ensure that school-age children and adolescents living in scattered areas can enroll in school to receive compulsory education.

Article 18 The administrative department for education of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, as needed, set up schools (classes) in economically developed areas to receive school-age children and adolescents from ethnic minorities.

Article 19 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, as needed, set up corresponding special education schools (classes) to implement compulsory education for school-age children and adolescents with visual, hearing, speech, or intellectual disabilities. Special education schools (classes) shall have venues and facilities that adapt to the characteristics of learning, rehabilitation, and life of children and adolescents with disabilities.

Regular schools shall admit school-age children and adolescents with disabilities who have the ability to receive regular education to study in regular classes and provide assistance for their learning and rehabilitation.

Article 20 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, as needed, set up specialized schools for school-age adolescents with serious misbehavior as stipulated in the Law on the Prevention of Juvenile Delinquency to implement compulsory education.

Article 21 Compulsory education shall be provided to juvenile offenders who have not completed compulsory education and to minors subject to compulsory educational measures, with the necessary funds guaranteed by the people's government.

Article 22 People's governments at or above the county level and their administrative departments for education shall promote the balanced development of schools, narrow the gap in school conditions, and shall not divide schools into key schools and non-key schools. Schools shall not set up key classes and non-key classes.

People's governments at or above the county level and their administrative departments for education shall not change or covertly change the nature of public schools under any pretext.

Article 23 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall maintain order around schools in accordance with the law, protect the legitimate rights and interests of students, teachers, and schools, and provide safety guarantees for schools.

Article 24 Schools shall establish and improve safety systems and emergency mechanisms, conduct safety education for students, strengthen management, promptly eliminate hazards, and prevent accidents.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall regularly inspect the safety of school buildings; for those requiring repair or renovation, they shall promptly carry out repairs or renovations.

Schools shall not employ persons who have been deprived of political rights due to intentional crimes or who are otherwise unsuitable for compulsory education work as staff.

Article 25 Schools shall not charge fees in violation of State regulations, nor shall they seek benefits by selling or covertly selling goods or services to students.

Article 26 Schools implement a principal responsibility system. The principal shall meet the qualifications prescribed by the State. The principal shall be legally appointed by the administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level.

Article 27 For students who violate school management regulations, the school shall criticize and educate them, but shall not expel them.

## Chapter IV Teachers

Article 28 Teachers enjoy the rights stipulated by law, fulfill the obligations stipulated by law, and shall be role models and loyal to the cause of people's education.

The whole society shall respect teachers.

Article 29 In education and teaching, teachers shall treat students equally, pay attention to individual differences among students, teach according to their aptitude, and promote the full development of students.

Teachers shall respect the personality of students, shall not discriminate against students, shall not impose corporal punishment, covert corporal punishment, or other acts that insult the dignity of students, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of students.

Article 30 Teachers shall obtain the teacher qualifications prescribed by the State.

The State establishes a unified teacher position system for compulsory education. Teacher positions are divided into junior positions, intermediate positions, and senior positions.

Article 31 People's governments at all levels shall guarantee the salaries, benefits, and social insurance of teachers, and improve their working and living conditions; they shall improve the mechanism for ensuring the salary funds of teachers in rural areas.

The average salary level of teachers shall not be lower than the average salary level of local civil servants.

Teachers in special education shall receive special post subsidy allowances. Teachers working in ethnic minority areas and remote poverty-stricken areas shall receive hardship subsidy allowances.

Article 32 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen teacher training and take measures to develop teacher education.

The administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level shall allocate teacher resources evenly within its administrative region, organize training and rotation of principals and teachers, and strengthen the construction of weak schools.

Article 33 The State Council and local people's governments at all levels encourage and support urban school teachers and college graduates to engage in compulsory education in rural areas and ethnic minority areas.

The State encourages college graduates to teach as volunteers in schools lacking teachers in rural areas and ethnic minority areas. The administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level shall legally recognize their teacher qualifications, and their teaching years shall be counted as length of service.

## Chapter V Education and Teaching

Article 34 Education and teaching work shall conform to the laws of education and the characteristics of students' physical and mental development, face all students, impart knowledge and educate people, organically integrate moral, intellectual, physical, and aesthetic education in education and teaching activities, focus on cultivating students' independent thinking ability, innovation ability, and practical ability, and promote students' comprehensive development.

Article 35 The administrative department for education of the State Council shall, based on the physical and mental development and actual conditions of school-age children and adolescents, determine the teaching system, educational and teaching content, and curriculum, reform the examination system, and improve the enrollment methods for senior secondary schools to promote the implementation of quality education.

Schools and teachers shall carry out education and teaching activities in accordance with the determined educational and teaching content and curriculum, ensuring that the basic quality requirements prescribed by the State are met.

The State encourages schools and teachers to adopt heuristic education and other teaching methods to improve the quality of education and teaching.

Article 36 Schools shall prioritize moral education, integrate moral education into education and teaching, carry out social practice activities appropriate to the age of students, and form an ideological and moral education system that coordinates school, family, and society, promoting the development of good ideological and moral character and behavioral habits in students.

Article 37 Schools shall ensure students' extracurricular activity time and organize cultural and recreational extracurricular activities. Public cultural and sports facilities shall provide convenience for schools to carry out extracurricular activities.

Article 38 Textbooks shall be compiled in accordance with the State's educational policy and curriculum standards, with concise content, selecting essential basic knowledge and basic skills, being economical and practical, and ensuring quality.

State functionaries and textbook review personnel shall not participate or covertly participate in the compilation of textbooks.

Article 39 The State implements a textbook approval system. The approval method for textbooks shall be prescribed by the administrative department for education of the State Council.

Textbooks that have not been approved shall not be published or selected.

Article 40 The price of textbooks shall be determined by the price administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government jointly with the publishing administrative department at the same level based on the principle of low profit.

Article 41 The State encourages the recycling of textbooks.

## Chapter VI Funding Guarantee

Article 42 The State fully includes compulsory education in the scope of financial guarantee, and the funds for compulsory education shall be guaranteed by the State Council and local people's governments at all levels in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

The State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall include compulsory education funds in the financial budget, and allocate compulsory education funds in full and on time in accordance with the staffing standards, salary standards, school construction standards, and per-student public funding standards, ensuring the normal operation of schools and the safety of school buildings, and ensuring that the salaries of faculty are paid in accordance with regulations.

The growth rate of the proportion of financial appropriations for compulsory education by the State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall be higher than the growth rate of regular financial revenue, ensuring that the average compulsory education expenditure per enrolled student gradually increases, and that the salaries of faculty and the per-student public funding gradually increase.

Article 43 The basic standard for per-student public funding in schools shall be formulated by the financial department of the State Council jointly with the administrative department for education, and shall be adjusted in a timely manner based on economic and social development. The formulation and adjustment of the basic standard for per-student public funding shall meet the basic needs of education and teaching.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the actual conditions of their administrative regions, formulate per-student public funding standards for schools that are not lower than the national standards.

The per-student public funding standard for special education schools (classes) shall be higher than that for regular schools.

Article 44 The investment in compulsory education funds shall be shared by the State Council and local people's governments at all levels according to their responsibilities, with the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government responsible for overall implementation. The funds required for compulsory education in rural areas shall be shared by people's governments at all levels according to projects and proportions as prescribed by the State Council.

People's governments at all levels shall provide free textbooks to school-age children and adolescents from families with financial difficulties and subsidize the living expenses of boarding students.

Specific measures for ensuring compulsory education funds shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 45 Local people's governments at all levels shall list compulsory education funds separately in their financial budgets.

When preparing budgets, people's governments at the county level shall allocate compulsory education funds in a balanced manner, in addition to favoring rural schools and weak schools.

Article 46 The State Council and people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall regulate the fiscal transfer payment system, increase the scale of general transfer payments, standardize special transfer payments for compulsory education, and support and guide local people's governments at all levels to increase investment in compulsory education. Local people's governments at all levels shall ensure that the transfer payment funds for compulsory education from higher-level people's governments are used for compulsory education in accordance with regulations.

Article 47 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual needs, establish special funds to support the implementation of compulsory education in rural areas and ethnic minority areas.

Article 48 The State encourages social organizations and individuals to donate to compulsory education and encourages the establishment of compulsory education funds in accordance with the regulations on the management of foundations of the State.

Article 49 Compulsory education funds shall be strictly used for compulsory education in accordance with budget provisions; no organization or individual may embezzle or misappropriate compulsory education funds, nor illegally collect or apportion fees from schools.

Article 50 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve the audit supervision and statistical announcement system for compulsory education funds.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 51 If the relevant departments of the State Council or local people's governments at all levels violate the provisions of Chapter VI of this Law and fail to fulfill their duties of ensuring compulsory education funds, the State Council or the higher-level local people's government shall order them to make corrections within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 52 If a local people's government at or above the county level has any of the following circumstances, the higher-level people's government shall order it to make corrections within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to formulate or adjust school setting plans in accordance with relevant State regulations;

(2) School construction failing to meet the national standards for running schools, site selection requirements, and construction standards;

(3) Failing to regularly inspect the safety of school buildings and promptly carry out repairs or renovations;

(4) Failing to allocate compulsory education funds in a balanced manner in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 53 If a people's government at or above the county level or its administrative department for education has any of the following circumstances, the higher-level people's government or its administrative department for education shall order it to make corrections within a time limit and circulate a notice of criticism; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Dividing schools into key schools and non-key schools;

(2) Changing or covertly changing the nature of public schools.

If the administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level or the township people's government fails to take measures to organize school-age children and adolescents to enroll in school or prevent dropping out, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 54 If any of the following circumstances occur, the higher-level people's government or the administrative department for education, financial department, price administrative department, and audit authority of the higher-level people's government shall, according to their division of responsibilities, order corrections within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Embezzling or misappropriating compulsory education funds;

(2) Illegally collecting or apportioning fees from schools.

Article 55 If a school or teacher violates the provisions of the Education Law or the Teachers Law in compulsory education work, they shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Education Law and the Teachers Law.

Article 56 If a school charges fees in violation of State regulations, the administrative department for education of the people's government at the county level shall order the return of the fees collected; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.If a school seeks profits by promoting or indirectly promoting the sale of goods or services to students, the education administrative department of the people's government at the county level shall issue a notice of criticism; any illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If state functionaries or textbook review personnel participate or indirectly participate in the compilation of textbooks, the people's government at or above the county level or its education administrative department shall, within the scope of their duties and authority, order corrections within a specified period and impose administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Article 57 If a school falls under any of the following circumstances, the education administrative department of the people's government at the county level shall order corrections within a specified period; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Refusing to admit school-age children or adolescents with disabilities who have the ability to receive general education to study in regular classes;

(2) Establishing key classes and non-key classes separately;

(3) Expelling students in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(4) Selecting textbooks that have not been reviewed and approved.

Article 58 If the parents or other legal guardians of school-age children or adolescents, without justifiable reasons, fail to send them to school to receive compulsory education in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the local township people's government or the education administrative department of the people's government at the county level shall give criticism and education, and order corrections within a specified period.

Article 59 In any of the following circumstances, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations:

(1) Coercing or luring school-age children or adolescents who should receive compulsory education into dropping out or discontinuing their studies;

(2) Illegally employing school-age children or adolescents who should receive compulsory education;

(3) Publishing textbooks that have not been reviewed and approved in accordance with the law.

Article 60 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 61 The implementation steps for not charging miscellaneous fees for school-age children and adolescents receiving compulsory education shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 62 If a social organization or individual runs a private school that implements compulsory education in accordance with the law, the relevant provisions of the Private Education Promotion Law shall apply; if the Private Education Promotion Law does not provide for it, this Law shall apply.

Article 63 This Law shall take effect as of September 1, 2006.
