# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on February 21, 1989

Revised at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing, controlling and eliminating the occurrence and epidemic of infectious diseases, and safeguarding human health and public health.

Article 2 The State applies the principle of putting prevention first to the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, combining prevention with treatment, conducting classified management, relying on science and relying on the masses.

Article 3 The infectious diseases covered by this Law are divided into Class A, Class B and Class C.

Class A infectious diseases refer to: plague, cholera.

Class B infectious diseases refer to: severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS), AIDS, viral hepatitis, poliomyelitis, human infection with highly pathogenic avian influenza, measles, epidemic hemorrhagic fever, rabies, Japanese encephalitis, dengue fever, anthrax, bacillary and amebic dysentery, pulmonary tuberculosis, typhoid fever and paratyphoid fever, epidemic cerebrospinal meningitis, pertussis, diphtheria, neonatal tetanus, scarlet fever, brucellosis, gonorrhea, syphilis, leptospirosis, schistosomiasis, malaria.

Class C infectious diseases refer to: influenza, mumps, rubella, acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis, leprosy, epidemic and endemic typhus, kala-azar, hydatid disease, filariasis, infectious diarrhea diseases other than cholera, bacillary and amebic dysentery, typhoid fever and paratyphoid fever.

The health administration department under the State Council may, in light of the outbreak, prevalence and degree of harm of infectious diseases, decide to add, reduce or adjust the categories of Class B and Class C infectious diseases and publish them.

Article 4 For SARS, pulmonary anthrax among anthrax, and human infection with highly pathogenic avian influenza, which are Class B infectious diseases, the prevention and control measures for Class A infectious diseases as provided for in this Law shall be applied. Where other Class B infectious diseases and infectious diseases with unknown causes of outbreak need to adopt the prevention and control measures for Class A infectious diseases as provided for in this Law, the health administration department under the State Council shall promptly report to the State Council for approval and then publish and implement them.

Where it is necessary to lift the prevention and control measures for Class A infectious diseases adopted in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the health administration department under the State Council shall report to the State Council for approval and then publish them.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in light of the circumstances, decide to manage other local infectious diseases that are common and frequently occur within their respective administrative regions as Class B or Class C infectious diseases and publish them, and shall report them to the health administration department under the State Council for the record.

Article 5 The people's governments at all levels shall lead the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

The people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate plans for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and organize their implementation, and establish and improve the disease prevention and control, medical treatment and supervision and management systems for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

Article 6 The health administration department under the State Council shall be in charge of the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and the supervision and management thereof throughout the country. The health administration departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and the supervision and management thereof within their respective administrative regions.

The other relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases within the scope of their respective duties.

The prevention and treatment of infectious diseases in the armed forces shall be handled in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the State, and the health department of the Chinese People's Liberation Army shall exercise supervision and management.

Article 7 Disease prevention and control institutions at all levels shall undertake the monitoring, prediction, epidemiological investigation, epidemic reporting and other prevention and control work of infectious diseases.

Medical institutions shall undertake the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases related to medical treatment and the prevention of infectious diseases within their areas of responsibility. Urban community and rural grassroots medical institutions shall, under the guidance of disease prevention and control institutions, undertake the corresponding work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases in urban communities and rural grassroots areas.

Article 8 The State shall develop modern medicine and traditional Chinese medicine and other traditional medicines, support and encourage scientific research on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, and improve the scientific and technological level of the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

The State supports and encourages international cooperation in the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

Article 9 The State supports and encourages units and individuals to participate in the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases. The people's governments at all levels shall improve relevant systems to facilitate the participation of units and individuals in the publicity and education on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, epidemic reporting, voluntary service and donation activities.

Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall organize residents and villagers to participate in the prevention and control activities of infectious diseases in communities and rural areas.

Article 10 The State shall carry out health education on the prevention of infectious diseases. The news media shall conduct free public service advertising on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and public health education.

Schools of various types and at various levels shall educate students on health knowledge and knowledge of infectious disease prevention.

Medical colleges and universities shall strengthen education and scientific research on preventive medicine, provide education and training on preventive medicine to current students and other personnel related to the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, and provide technical support for the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

Disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions shall regularly train their staff on the knowledge and skills of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.

Article 11 Units and individuals that have made remarkable achievements and contributions in the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases shall be commended and rewarded.

Personnel who become ill, disabled or die due to participation in the work of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases shall be given subsidies and pensions in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 12 All units and individuals within the territory of the People's Republic of China must accept the prevention and control measures such as investigation, inspection, sample collection, isolation and treatment related to infectious diseases carried out by disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions, and truthfully provide relevant information. Disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions shall not disclose information and materials involving personal privacy.

Where the health administration departments and other relevant departments, disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of units and individuals due to illegal implementation of administrative management or prevention and control measures, the relevant units and individuals may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate legal proceedings in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Prevention of Infectious Diseases

Article 13 The people's governments at all levels shall organize mass health activities, carry out health education on the prevention of infectious diseases, advocate civilized and healthy lifestyles, improve the public's awareness of prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and their response capabilities, strengthen environmental sanitation construction, and eliminate the hazards of rodents and vectors such as mosquitoes and flies.

The administrative departments of agriculture, water resources and forestry under the people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with their respective division of responsibilities, be responsible for guiding and organizing the elimination of rodent and schistosomiasis hazards in farmland, lake areas, rivers, pastures and forest areas, as well as the hazards of other animals and vectors that transmit infectious diseases.

The administrative departments of railways, transportation and civil aviation shall be responsible for organizing the elimination of rodent and vector hazards such as mosquitoes and flies in means of transportation and related places.

Article 14 The local people's governments at all levels shall plan to construct and renovate public health facilities, improve the sanitary conditions of drinking water, and carry out harmless treatment of sewage, waste and feces.

Article 15 The State implements a planned vaccination system. The health administration department under the State Council and the health administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in light of the needs of prevention and control of infectious diseases, formulate plans for vaccination against infectious diseases and organize their implementation. Vaccines used for vaccination must meet national quality standards.

The State implements a vaccination certificate system for children. Vaccination under the national immunization program is free of charge. Medical institutions, disease prevention and control institutions and the guardians of children shall cooperate with each other to ensure that children receive vaccination in a timely manner. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 16 The State and society shall care for and help infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers and suspected infectious disease patients, so that they can receive timely treatment. No unit or individual shall discriminate against infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers and suspected infectious disease patients.

Infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers and suspected infectious disease patients shall not, before being cured or before being ruled out of suspicion of infectious diseases, engage in work that is prohibited by laws, administrative regulations and the health administration department under the State Council and is likely to spread the infectious disease.

Article 17 The State establishes a monitoring system for infectious diseases.

The health administration department under the State Council shall formulate national plans and programs for monitoring infectious diseases. The health administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with the national plans and programs for monitoring infectious diseases, formulate monitoring plans and work programs for infectious diseases within their respective administrative regions.

Disease prevention and control institutions at all levels shall monitor the occurrence and prevalence of infectious diseases and the factors affecting their occurrence and prevalence; and monitor infectious diseases that occur abroad but have not yet occurred in China, or infectious diseases that have newly occurred in China.

Article 18 Disease prevention and control institutions at all levels shall perform the following duties in the prevention and control of infectious diseases:

(1) Implementing plans, programs and schemes for the prevention and control of infectious diseases;
(2) Collecting, analyzing and reporting monitoring information on infectious diseases, and predicting the occurrence and epidemic trends of infectious diseases;
(3) Conducting epidemiological investigations, on-site handling and effect evaluation of infectious disease epidemics and public health emergencies;
(4) Conducting laboratory testing, diagnosis and etiological identification of infectious diseases;
(5) Implementing immunization programs and being responsible for the management of the use of preventive biological products;
(6) Carrying out health education and consultation, and popularizing knowledge on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases;
(7) Guiding and training lower-level disease prevention and control institutions and their staff in carrying out infectious disease monitoring work;
(8) Conducting applied research on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and health evaluation, and providing technical consultation.

National and provincial disease prevention and control institutions shall be responsible for monitoring the occurrence, prevalence and distribution of infectious diseases, predicting the epidemic trends of major infectious diseases, proposing prevention and control strategies, participating in and guiding the investigation and handling of outbreaks, conducting etiological identification of infectious diseases, establishing a quality control system for testing, and conducting applied research and health evaluation.

Disease prevention and control institutions in cities divided into districts and at the county level shall be responsible for the implementation of plans and programs for the prevention and control of infectious diseases, organizing the implementation of immunization, disinfection, and control of vector hazards, popularizing knowledge on the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, being responsible for monitoring and reporting local epidemics and public health emergencies, and conducting epidemiological investigations and testing of common pathogenic microorganisms.

Article 19 The State establishes an early warning system for infectious diseases.

The health administration department under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on predictions of the occurrence and epidemic trends of infectious diseases, issue timely early warnings for infectious diseases and publish them according to the circumstances.

Article 20 The local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate plans for the prevention and control of infectious diseases and report them to the people's government at the next higher level for the record.

The plans for the prevention and control of infectious diseases shall mainly include the following contents:

(1) The composition of the command headquarters for the prevention and control of infectious diseases and the responsibilities of relevant departments;
(2) The system for monitoring, information collection, analysis, reporting and notification of infectious diseases;
(3) The tasks and responsibilities of disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions in the event of an infectious disease epidemic;
(4) The classification of the outbreak and prevalence of infectious diseases and the corresponding emergency work plans;
(5) The prevention of infectious diseases, on-site control of epidemic spots and epidemic areas, and the reserve and deployment of emergency facilities, equipment, treatment drugs and medical devices, and other materials and technologies.

After receiving an early warning for infectious diseases issued by the health administration department under the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government, the local people's government and disease prevention and control institutions shall take corresponding prevention and control measures in accordance with the plan for the prevention and control of infectious diseases.

Article 21 Medical institutions must strictly implement the management systems and operating procedures stipulated by the health administration department under the State Council to prevent nosocomial infection and hospital-acquired infection of infectious diseases.

Medical institutions shall designate specific departments or personnel to be responsible for reporting infectious disease epidemics, the prevention and control of infectious diseases within the unit, and the prevention of infectious diseases within the area of responsibility; and to be responsible for monitoring risk factors related to hospital-acquired infection, safety protection, disinfection, isolation and medical waste disposal in medical activities.

Disease prevention and control institutions shall designate specialized personnel to be responsible for guiding and assessing the prevention of infectious diseases in medical institutions and conducting epidemiological investigations.

Article 22 Laboratories of disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions, and units engaged in experiments with pathogenic microorganisms, shall meet the conditions and technical standards stipulated by the State, establish strict supervision and management systems, and strictly supervise and manage samples of infectious disease pathogens in accordance with the prescribed measures, to strictly prevent laboratory infection of infectious disease pathogens and the spread of pathogenic microorganisms.

Article 23 Blood collection and supply institutions and biological product production units must strictly implement the relevant provisions of the State to ensure the quality of blood and blood products. It is prohibited to illegally collect blood or organize others to sell blood.

Disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions must comply with the relevant provisions of the State when using blood and blood products, to prevent the occurrence of blood-borne diseases caused by blood transfusion or the use of blood products.

Article 24 The people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the work of prevention and treatment of AIDS, and take prevention and control measures to prevent the spread of AIDS. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 25 The administrative departments of agriculture, forestry and other relevant departments under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for the prevention and management of animal infectious diseases related to zoonotic infectious diseases.

Wild animals, livestock and poultry related to zoonotic infectious diseases may be sold or transported only after passing quarantine.

Article 26 The State establishes a repository for bacterial strains and virus strains of infectious diseases.

The collection, preservation, carrying, transportation and use of bacterial strains, virus strains and testing samples of infectious diseases shall be subject to classified management, and a strict management system shall be established and improved.

Where it is truly necessary to collect, preserve, carry, transport and use bacterial strains, virus strains and testing samples of infectious diseases that may cause the spread of Class A infectious diseases or those specified by the health administration department under the State Council, approval must be obtained from the health administration department of the people's government at or above the provincial level. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 27 Units and individuals concerned must, under the guidance of disease prevention and control institutions or in accordance with their hygiene requirements, carry out strict disinfection treatment of sewage, waste, places and articles contaminated by infectious disease pathogens; if disinfection treatment is refused, the local health administration department or disease prevention and control institution shall carry out compulsory disinfection treatment.

Article 28 Before planning to construct large-scale construction projects such as water conservancy, transportation, tourism and energy in natural foci confirmed by the State, a health survey of the construction environment shall be conducted in advance by disease prevention and control institutions at or above the provincial level. The construction unit shall, based on the opinions of the disease prevention and control institution, take necessary measures for the prevention and control of infectious diseases. During the construction period, the construction unit shall assign a special person to be responsible for the health and epidemic prevention work on the construction site. After the project is completed, the disease prevention and control institution shall monitor infectious diseases that may occur.

Article 29 Disinfection products used for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, drinking water supplied by drinking water supply units, and products related to the sanitary safety of drinking water shall meet national hygiene standards and hygiene specifications.

Drinking water supply units engaged in production or supply activities shall obtain a hygiene license in accordance with the law.

Units producing disinfection products for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases and the production of disinfection products for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases shall be subject to the approval of the health administration department of the people's government at or above the provincial level. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter III Reporting, Notification and Publication of Epidemics

Article 30 When disease prevention and control institutions, medical institutions, blood collection and supply institutions, and their personnel on duty discover an epidemic of infectious diseases as provided for in this Law, or discover the outbreak or prevalence of other infectious diseases, or infectious diseases with unknown causes of outbreak, they shall follow the principle of territorial management of epidemic reporting and report in accordance with the content, procedures, methods and time limits prescribed by the State Council or the health administration department under the State Council.

When military medical institutions provide medical services to the public and discover an epidemic of infectious diseases as provided for in the preceding paragraph, they shall report in accordance with the provisions of the health administration department under the State Council.

Article 31 Any unit or individual that discovers an infectious disease patient or a suspected infectious disease patient shall promptly report to the nearby disease prevention and control institution or medical institution.

Article 32 When disease prevention and control institutions at ports, airports and railways, and frontier health and quarantine authorities discover Class A infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers, or suspected infectious disease patients, they shall immediately report to the disease prevention and control institution at the port of entry or the health administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, and notify each other.

Article 33 Disease prevention and control institutions shall proactively collect, analyze, investigate and verify information on infectious disease epidemics. Upon receiving a report of a Class A or Class B infectious disease epidemic or discovering an outbreak or prevalence of an infectious disease, they shall immediately report to the local health administration department, which shall immediately report to the local people's government, and at the same time report to the higher-level health administration department and the health administration department under the State Council.

Disease prevention and control institutions shall establish or designate specialized departments and personnel to be responsible for the management of information on infectious disease epidemics, and promptly verify and analyze epidemic reports.

Article 34 The health administration departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly notify the disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions within their respective administrative regions of information on infectious disease epidemics and related monitoring and early warning. Disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions that receive the notification shall promptly inform the relevant personnel of their units.

Article 35 The health administration department under the State Council shall promptly notify other relevant departments under the State Council and the health administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government of the national infectious disease epidemic situation and related monitoring and early warning information.

Adjacent and relevant local people's government health administration departments shall promptly notify each other of the infectious disease epidemic situation and related monitoring and early warning information within their respective administrative regions.

When relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level discover an infectious disease epidemic, they shall promptly notify the health administration department of the people's government at the same level.

When the health department of the Chinese People's Liberation Army discovers an infectious disease epidemic, it shall notify the health administration department under the State Council.

Article 36 Animal epidemic prevention institutions and disease prevention and control institutions shall promptly notify each other of the epidemic situation of zoonotic infectious diseases occurring among animals and humans and related information.

Article 37 The relevant departments of the people's governments, disease prevention and control institutions, medical institutions, blood collection and supply institutions, and their staff who are responsible for reporting infectious disease epidemics in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall not conceal, falsely report, or delay the reporting of infectious disease epidemics.

Article 38 The State establishes a system for the publication of information on infectious disease epidemics.

The health administration department under the State Council shall regularly publish information on the national infectious disease epidemic situation. The health administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall regularly publish information on the infectious disease epidemic situation within their respective administrative regions.During the outbreak and prevalence of an infectious disease, the health administration department under the State Council is responsible for announcing infectious disease epidemic information to the public, and may authorize the health administration departments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government to announce the infectious disease epidemic information within their respective administrative regions.

The announcement of infectious disease epidemic information shall be timely and accurate.

## Chapter IV: Epidemic Control

Article 39 When a medical institution discovers a Class A infectious disease, it shall promptly take the following measures:

(1) Isolate and treat patients and pathogen carriers, with the isolation period determined based on medical examination results;

(2) For suspected patients, provide isolated treatment in a designated place before diagnosis is confirmed;

(3) For close contacts of patients, pathogen carriers, and suspected patients within the medical institution, conduct medical observation in designated places and take other necessary preventive measures.

If a person refuses isolation treatment or leaves isolation without authorization before the isolation period ends, the public security authority may assist the medical institution in taking compulsory isolation treatment measures.

When a medical institution discovers a patient with a Class B or Class C infectious disease, it shall take necessary treatment and transmission control measures based on the condition.

Medical institutions must, in accordance with laws and regulations, carry out disinfection and harmless disposal of places, articles, and medical waste contaminated by infectious disease pathogens within their premises.

Article 40 When a disease prevention and control institution discovers an infectious disease epidemic or receives a report of an infectious disease epidemic, it shall promptly take the following measures:

(1) Conduct epidemiological investigation of the infectious disease epidemic, propose suggestions for delineating epidemic spots and areas based on the investigation, carry out sanitary treatment of contaminated places, conduct medical observation of close contacts in designated places and take other necessary preventive measures, and submit an epidemic control plan to the health administration department;

(2) During the outbreak and prevalence of an infectious disease, carry out sanitary treatment of epidemic spots and areas, submit an epidemic control plan to the health administration department, and take measures as required by the health administration department;

(3) Guide lower-level disease prevention and control institutions in implementing infectious disease prevention and control measures, and organize and guide relevant units in handling the infectious disease epidemic.

Article 41 For places where cases of Class A infectious disease have occurred, or for persons in specific areas within such places, the local people's government at or above the county level may implement isolation measures and simultaneously report to the people's government at the next higher level; the higher-level people's government that receives the report shall promptly decide whether to approve. If the higher-level people's government decides not to approve, the people's government that implemented the isolation measures shall immediately lift them.

During the isolation period, the people's government that implemented the isolation measures shall provide living guarantees for the isolated persons; if the isolated person has a work unit, the unit shall not stop paying their work remuneration during the isolation period.

The lifting of isolation measures shall be decided and announced by the original decision-making authority.

Article 42 During the outbreak and prevalence of an infectious disease, the local people's government at or above the county level shall immediately organize forces to carry out prevention and control according to the prevention and control plan, cut off the transmission routes of the infectious disease, and when necessary, upon reporting to and obtaining the decision of the people's government at the next higher level, may take the following emergency measures and announce them:

(1) Restrict or stop markets, cinema performances, or other crowd-gathering activities;

(2) Suspend work, business, and classes;

(3) Seal off or seal up public drinking water sources, food, and related articles contaminated by infectious disease pathogens;

(4) Control or cull infected wild animals, livestock, and poultry;

(5) Seal off places that may cause the spread of the infectious disease.

When the higher-level people's government receives a report from the lower-level people's government regarding the adoption of the emergency measures listed in the preceding paragraph, it shall promptly make a decision.

The lifting of emergency measures shall be decided and announced by the original decision-making authority.

Article 43 When Class A or Class B infectious diseases break out or become prevalent, the local people's government at or above the county level may, upon reporting to and obtaining the decision of the people's government at the next higher level, declare part or all of its administrative region as an epidemic area; the State Council may decide and declare epidemic areas spanning provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. The local people's government at or above the county level may take the emergency measures stipulated in Article 42 of this Law within the epidemic area, and may implement sanitary quarantine for persons, materials, and means of transportation entering and leaving the epidemic area.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may decide to blockade epidemic areas of Class A infectious diseases within their administrative regions; however, the blockade of epidemic areas in large and medium-sized cities, or the blockade of epidemic areas spanning provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as the blockade of epidemic areas that cause interruption of trunk transportation or blockade of national borders, shall be decided by the State Council.

The lifting of the blockade of an epidemic area shall be decided and announced by the original decision-making authority.

Article 44 When a Class A infectious disease occurs, in order to prevent the spread of the infectious disease through means of transportation and the persons and materials transported thereby, traffic sanitary quarantine may be implemented. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 45 During the outbreak and prevalence of an infectious disease, according to the needs of infectious disease epidemic control, the State Council has the power to urgently mobilize personnel or allocate reserve materials, and temporarily requisition houses, means of transportation, and related facilities and equipment within the national scope or across provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government; the local people's government at or above the county level has the power to do so within its administrative region.

Persons urgently mobilized shall be given reasonable remuneration according to regulations. For temporarily requisitioned houses, means of transportation, and related facilities and equipment, compensation shall be given according to law; items that can be returned shall be returned promptly.

Article 46 If a person dies of a Class A infectious disease or anthrax, the corpse shall be immediately subjected to sanitary treatment and cremated nearby. If a person dies of another infectious disease, when necessary, the corpse shall be subjected to sanitary treatment before cremation or buried deeply according to regulations.

In order to find the cause of an infectious disease, a medical institution may, when necessary and in accordance with the regulations of the health administration department under the State Council, perform an autopsy and examination on the corpse of an infectious disease patient or a suspected infectious disease patient, and shall inform the family members of the deceased.

Article 47 Articles in an epidemic area that are contaminated or may be contaminated by infectious disease pathogens, if they can be used after disinfection, may only be used, sold, and transported after disinfection treatment under the guidance of the local disease prevention and control institution.

Article 48 When an infectious disease epidemic occurs, disease prevention and control institutions and other infectious disease-related professional technical institutions designated by the health administration departments of the people's governments at or above the provincial level may enter the infectious disease epidemic spots and areas for investigation, sample collection, technical analysis, and testing.

Article 49 During the outbreak and prevalence of an infectious disease, units producing and supplying drugs and medical devices shall promptly produce and supply drugs and medical devices for the prevention and treatment of the infectious disease. Railway, transportation, and civil aviation operating units must give priority to transporting personnel handling the infectious disease epidemic, as well as drugs and medical devices for the prevention and treatment of the infectious disease. Relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall do a good job in organization and coordination.

## Chapter V: Medical Treatment

Article 50 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen and improve the construction of the infectious disease medical treatment service network, designate medical institutions with the conditions and capabilities for infectious disease treatment to undertake infectious disease treatment tasks, or set up infectious disease hospitals according to the needs of infectious disease treatment.

Article 51 The basic standards, architectural design, and service procedures of medical institutions shall meet the requirements for preventing nosocomial infection of infectious diseases.

Medical institutions shall disinfect the medical devices used according to regulations; medical instruments that are specified for single use shall be destroyed after use.

Medical institutions shall take corresponding measures according to the diagnostic standards and treatment requirements for infectious diseases stipulated by the health administration department under the State Council to improve their capacity for infectious disease medical treatment.

Article 52 Medical institutions shall provide medical rescue, on-site rescue, and consultation and treatment to infectious disease patients or suspected infectious disease patients, write medical records and other relevant materials, and keep them properly.

Medical institutions shall implement a pre-examination and triage system for infectious diseases; infectious disease patients and suspected infectious disease patients shall be guided to a relatively isolated triage point for initial diagnosis. If a medical institution does not have the corresponding treatment capacity, it shall transfer the patient and a copy of their medical records to a medical institution with the corresponding treatment capacity. Specific measures shall be formulated by the health administration department under the State Council.

## Chapter VI: Supervision and Management

Article 53 The health administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall perform the following supervision and inspection duties for infectious disease prevention and control work:

(1) Supervise and inspect whether the health administration departments of lower-level people's governments perform the infectious disease prevention and control duties stipulated in this Law;

(2) Supervise and inspect the infectious disease prevention and control work of disease prevention and control institutions and medical institutions;

(3) Supervise and inspect the blood collection and supply activities of blood collection and supply institutions;

(4) Supervise and inspect disinfection products used for infectious disease prevention and control and their production units, and supervise and inspect the production or supply activities of drinking water supply units and products related to the sanitary safety of drinking water;

(5) Supervise and inspect the collection, storage, carrying, transportation, and use of infectious disease bacterial strains, virus strains, and infectious disease testing samples;

(6) Supervise and inspect the sanitary conditions and infectious disease prevention and control measures of public places and relevant units.

The health administration departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level are responsible for organizing the handling of major matters concerning infectious disease prevention and control.

Article 54 When performing supervision and inspection duties, the health administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level have the right to enter the unit being inspected and the scene of the infectious disease epidemic to investigate and collect evidence, consult or copy relevant materials, and collect samples. The unit being inspected shall cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct.

Article 55 When performing supervision and inspection duties, if the health administration department of a local people's government at or above the county level discovers that public drinking water sources, food, and related articles contaminated by infectious disease pathogens, if timely control measures are not taken, may lead to the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease, it may take temporary control measures such as sealing off the public drinking water source, sealing up the food and related articles, or suspending sales, and conduct testing or disinfection. If, after testing, the food is found to be contaminated, it shall be destroyed; for uncontaminated food or articles that can be used after disinfection, the control measures shall be lifted.

Article 56 When health administration department staff perform their duties according to law, there shall be no fewer than two persons, and they shall present their law enforcement credentials and fill out health law enforcement documents.

After the health law enforcement documents are verified to be correct, they shall be signed by the health law enforcement personnel and the party concerned. If the party concerned refuses to sign, the health law enforcement personnel shall note the circumstances.

Article 57 Health administration departments shall establish and improve internal supervision systems according to law, and supervise whether their staff perform their duties in accordance with statutory authority and procedures.

If a higher-level health administration department discovers that a lower-level health administration department fails to promptly handle matters within its scope of duties or fails to perform its duties, it shall order correction or directly handle the matter.

Article 58 Health administration departments and their staff shall consciously accept the supervision of society and citizens when performing their duties. Units and individuals have the right to report violations of this Law to the higher-level people's government and its health administration department. The relevant people's government or its health administration department that receives the report shall promptly investigate and handle it.

## Chapter VII: Safeguard Measures

Article 59 The State incorporates infectious disease prevention and control work into the national economic and social development plan, and local people's governments at or above the county level incorporate infectious disease prevention and control work into the national economic and social development plan of their respective administrative regions.

Article 60 Local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for the daily expenses of infectious disease prevention, control, and supervision work within their administrative regions according to their respective government responsibilities.

The health administration department under the State Council, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, determines national projects for infectious disease prevention, control, treatment, surveillance, prediction, early warning, supervision, and inspection based on the epidemic trend of infectious diseases. The central finance provides subsidies to difficult areas for implementing major infectious disease prevention and control projects.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, based on the epidemic trend of infectious diseases within their administrative regions and within the scope of projects determined by the health administration department under the State Council, determine projects for infectious disease prevention, control, and supervision, and guarantee the implementation funds for the projects.

Article 61 The State strengthens the construction of the grassroots infectious disease prevention and control system, and supports infectious disease prevention and control work in poverty-stricken areas and ethnic minority areas.

Local people's governments at all levels shall guarantee the funds for infectious disease prevention work in urban communities and rural grassroots areas.

Article 62 The State provides medical assistance to disadvantaged populations suffering from specific infectious diseases, reducing or exempting medical expenses. Specific measures shall be formulated by the health administration department under the State Council together with the finance department under the State Council and other departments.

Article 63 People's governments at or above the county level are responsible for stockpiling drugs, medical devices, and other materials for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases for deployment.

Article 64 For personnel engaged in infectious disease prevention, medical treatment, scientific research, teaching, and on-site epidemic handling, as well as other personnel who come into contact with infectious disease pathogens during production or work, the relevant units shall, in accordance with national regulations, take effective health protection measures and medical care measures, and provide appropriate allowances.

## Chapter VIII: Legal Liability

Article 65 If a local people's government at any level fails to perform its reporting duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, or conceals, falsely reports, or delays reporting an infectious disease epidemic, or fails to promptly organize treatment or take control measures during the outbreak or prevalence of an infectious disease, the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 66 If the health administration department of a people's government at or above the county level violates the provisions of this Law in any of the following circumstances, the people's government at the same level or the health administration department of the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Failing to perform the duties of notification, reporting, or announcement of the infectious disease epidemic according to law, or concealing, falsely reporting, or delaying reporting the infectious disease epidemic;

(2) Failing to take timely prevention and control measures when the spread of an infectious disease occurs or may occur;

(3) Failing to perform supervision and inspection duties according to law, or failing to promptly investigate and deal with illegal acts upon discovery;

(4) Failing to promptly investigate and handle reports from units and individuals regarding the failure of lower-level health administration departments to perform their infectious disease prevention and control duties;

(5) Other acts of dereliction of duty or malfeasance in violation of this Law.

Article 67 If the relevant departments of a people's government at or above the county level fail to perform their duties of infectious disease prevention, control, and guarantee in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant departments of the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 68 If a disease prevention and control institution violates the provisions of this Law in any of the following circumstances, the health administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a time limit, circulate a notice of criticism, and give a warning; the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions such as demotion, removal from office, or dismissal according to law, and the practice certificate of the relevant responsible personnel may be revoked according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Failing to perform the duties of infectious disease surveillance according to law;

(2) Failing to perform the duties of reporting or notifying the infectious disease epidemic according to law, or concealing, falsely reporting, or delaying reporting the infectious disease epidemic;

(3) Failing to actively collect infectious disease epidemic information, or failing to promptly analyze, investigate, or verify infectious disease epidemic information and epidemic reports;

(4) Failing to promptly take the measures stipulated in this Law according to its duties upon discovering an infectious disease epidemic;

(5) Intentionally disclosing information and materials involving the personal privacy of infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers, suspected infectious disease patients, and close contacts.

Article 69 If a medical institution violates the provisions of this Law in any of the following circumstances, the health administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a time limit, circulate a notice of criticism, and give a warning; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions such as demotion, removal from office, or dismissal according to law, and the practice certificate of the relevant responsible personnel may be revoked according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Failing to undertake the infectious disease prevention and control work of the unit, hospital infection control tasks, and infectious disease prevention work within the responsibility area according to regulations;

(2) Failing to report the infectious disease epidemic according to regulations, or concealing, falsely reporting, or delaying reporting the infectious disease epidemic;

(3) Failing to provide medical rescue, on-site rescue, consultation, or referral to infectious disease patients or suspected infectious disease patients according to regulations upon discovering an infectious disease epidemic, or refusing to accept referrals;

(4) Failing to carry out disinfection or harmless disposal of places, articles, and medical waste contaminated by infectious disease pathogens within the unit according to regulations;

(5) Failing to disinfect medical devices according to regulations, or failing to destroy medical instruments specified for single use and reusing them;

(6) Failing to keep medical records and materials according to regulations during the medical treatment process;

(7) Intentionally disclosing information and materials involving the personal privacy of infectious disease patients, pathogen carriers, suspected infectious disease patients, and close contacts.

Article 70 If a blood collection and supply institution fails to report an infectious disease epidemic according to regulations, or conceals, falsely reports, or delays reporting an infectious disease epidemic, or fails to implement relevant national regulations, resulting in the occurrence of a blood-borne disease due to blood transfusion, the health administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a time limit, circulate a notice of criticism, and give a warning; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions such as demotion, removal from office, or dismissal according to law, and the practice license of the blood collection and supply institution may be revoked according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If blood is illegally collected or others are organized to sell blood, the health administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall ban it, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 71 If a border health quarantine authority or animal epidemic prevention institution fails to perform the duty of notifying the infectious disease epidemic according to law, the relevant departments shall, within their respective scopes of duties, order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; if it causes the spread or prevalence of the infectious disease or other serious consequences, the responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions such as demotion, removal from office, or dismissal according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.Article 72 Where railway, transportation, or civil aviation operating entities fail to prioritize the transport of personnel handling infectious disease outbreaks and the medicines and medical devices for the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the relevant departments shall order them to rectify within a specified time limit and issue a warning; if serious consequences result, the persons in charge with responsibility and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, dismissal, or expulsion in accordance with the law.

Article 73 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, causing or potentially causing the spread or epidemic of an infectious disease, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified time limit, confiscate illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; if a license has been obtained, the original licensing authority may temporarily seize or revoke the license in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) The drinking water supplied by a drinking water supply unit fails to meet national hygiene standards and hygiene specifications;

(2) Products related to the hygiene and safety of drinking water fail to meet national hygiene standards and hygiene specifications;

(3) Disinfection products used for the prevention and control of infectious diseases fail to meet national hygiene standards and hygiene specifications;

(4) Items sold or transported from epidemic areas that are contaminated or potentially contaminated by infectious disease pathogens are not disinfected;

(5) Blood products produced by biological product manufacturing units fail to meet national quality standards.

Article 74 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification, circulate a notice of criticism, and issue a warning; if a license has been obtained, it may temporarily seize or revoke the license in accordance with the law; if the spread or epidemic of an infectious disease or other serious consequences result, the persons in charge with responsibility and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, dismissal, or expulsion in accordance with the law, and the practice certificates of the responsible personnel may be revoked in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Disease prevention and control institutions, medical institutions, and units engaged in pathogenic microorganism experiments fail to meet the conditions and technical standards prescribed by the state, and fail to strictly manage samples of infectious disease pathogens in accordance with regulations, resulting in laboratory infections and the spread of pathogenic microorganisms;

(2) In violation of relevant state regulations, the collection, preservation, carrying, transportation, and use of bacterial strains, virus strains, and infectious disease testing samples are carried out;

(3) Disease prevention and control institutions or medical institutions fail to implement relevant state regulations, leading to the occurrence of blood-borne diseases due to the importation of blood or the use of blood products.

Article 75 Where wild animals, livestock, or poultry related to zoonotic infectious diseases are sold or transported without quarantine, the animal husbandry and veterinary administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act and impose administrative penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 76 Where, in a natural focus area confirmed by the state, a large-scale construction project such as water conservancy, transportation, tourism, or energy is constructed without a health survey, or without taking necessary infectious disease prevention and control measures in accordance with the opinions of the disease prevention and control institution, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified time limit, issue a warning, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; if rectification is not made within the time limit, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the relevant people's government may be requested, within its authority, to order the suspension of construction or closure.

Article 77 Where a unit or individual, in violation of the provisions of this Law, causes the spread or epidemic of an infectious disease, resulting in harm to the person or property of others, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 78 The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) Infectious disease patient, suspected infectious disease patient: refers to a person who meets the diagnostic criteria for infectious disease patients and suspected infectious disease patients as published by the health administrative department of the State Council in the "Diagnostic Standards for Infectious Diseases Managed under the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases."

(2) Pathogen carrier: refers to a person who is infected with a pathogen without clinical symptoms but can shed the pathogen.

(3) Epidemiological investigation: refers to the investigation and study of the distribution and determinants of diseases or health conditions in a population, proposing disease prevention and control measures and health strategies.

(4) Epidemic focus: refers to a relatively small area where the pathogen spreads from the source of infection to the surroundings, or a single source of infection.

(5) Epidemic area: refers to the area that may be affected when an infectious disease breaks out or becomes epidemic in a population and the pathogen spreads to the surroundings.

(6) Zoonotic infectious disease: refers to an infectious disease shared by humans and vertebrates, such as plague, rabies, schistosomiasis, etc.

(7) Natural focus: refers to areas where certain pathogens that can cause human infectious diseases persist and circulate among wild animals in nature over the long term.

(8) Disease vector: refers to organisms that can transmit pathogens from humans or other animals to humans, such as mosquitoes, flies, fleas, etc.

(9) Iatrogenic infection: refers to an infection caused by the transmission of pathogens during medical services.

(10) Nosocomial infection: refers to an infection acquired by a hospitalized patient within the hospital, including infections occurring during hospitalization and those acquired in the hospital but occurring after discharge, but excluding infections that began before admission or were in the incubation period at the time of admission. Infections acquired by hospital staff within the hospital also constitute nosocomial infections.

(11) Laboratory infection: refers to an infection caused by exposure to pathogens during laboratory work.

(12) Bacterial strains, virus strains: refer to bacterial strains and virus strains that may cause the occurrence of infectious diseases specified in this Law.

(13) Disinfection: refers to the use of chemical, physical, or biological methods to kill or eliminate pathogenic microorganisms in the environment.

(14) Disease prevention and control institution: refers to a disease prevention and control center engaged in disease prevention and control activities, as well as units with business activities identical to those of the aforementioned institutions.

(15) Medical institution: refers to an institution that has obtained a medical institution practice license in accordance with the "Regulations on the Administration of Medical Institutions" and is engaged in disease diagnosis and treatment activities.

Article 79 For matters concerning the management of food, drugs, blood, water, medical waste, and pathogenic microorganisms, as well as animal epidemic prevention and border health quarantine, in the prevention and treatment of infectious diseases, where this Law does not provide provisions, the relevant laws and administrative regulations shall apply respectively.

Article 80 This Law shall come into effect on December 1, 2004.
