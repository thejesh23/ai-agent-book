# Sports Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to develop sports, enhance the people's physique, raise the level of sports, and promote the construction of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization.

Article 2 The State develops sports, carries out mass sports activities, and improves the physical fitness of the entire nation. Sports work shall adhere to the principle of taking the nationwide fitness campaign as the foundation, combining popularization with improvement, and promoting the coordinated development of various types of sports.

Article 3 The State insists that sports serve economic construction, national defense construction, and social development. Sports shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plan.

The State promotes the reform of the sports management system. The State encourages enterprises, institutions, social organizations, and citizens to establish and support sports.

Article 4 The sports administrative department under the State Council shall be in charge of sports work nationwide. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall manage sports work within their respective functions and duties.

The sports administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level or the agencies authorized by the people's governments at the corresponding level shall be in charge of sports work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 5 The State provides special protection for the sports activities of young people, adolescents, and children, and promotes their physical and mental health.

Article 6 The State supports the development of sports in minority nationality regions and trains sports personnel from minority nationalities.

Article 7 The State develops sports education and sports scientific research, promotes advanced and practical sports science and technology achievements, and develops sports by relying on science and technology.

Article 8 The State gives awards to organizations and individuals that have made contributions to sports.

Article 9 The State encourages the conduct of foreign sports exchanges. Foreign sports exchanges shall adhere to the principles of independence, equality and mutual benefit, and mutual respect, safeguard national sovereignty and dignity, and comply with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter II Social Sports

Article 10 The State advocates that citizens participate in social sports activities to enhance their physical and mental health.

Social sports activities shall adhere to the principles of being amateur, voluntary, diverse in small forms, and following local conditions and scientific civilization.

Article 11 The State implements the nationwide fitness program, implements physical exercise standards, and conducts physical fitness monitoring.

The State implements a technical grade system for social sports instructors. Social sports instructors provide guidance for social sports activities.

Article 12 Local people's governments at various levels shall create necessary conditions for citizens to participate in social sports activities, and support and assist the development of mass sports activities.

Cities shall give play to the role of community-level organizations such as residents' committees to organize residents to carry out sports activities.

Rural areas shall give play to the role of villagers' committees and grassroots cultural and sports organizations to carry out sports activities suitable for rural characteristics.

Article 13 State organs, enterprises, and institutions shall carry out various forms of sports activities and hold mass sports competitions.

Article 14 Trade unions and other social organizations shall organize sports activities according to their respective characteristics.

Article 15 The State encourages and supports the discovery, collation, and improvement of traditional ethnic and folk sports events.

Article 16 The whole society shall care for and support the participation of the elderly and persons with disabilities in sports activities. People's governments at various levels shall take measures to facilitate the participation of the elderly and persons with disabilities in sports activities.

## Chapter III School Sports

Article 17 Educational administrative departments and schools shall take sports as a component of school education to cultivate talents with all-round development in morality, intelligence, and physique.

Article 18 Schools must offer physical education classes and include them as subjects for assessing students' academic performance.

Schools shall create conditions to organize sports activities suitable for the characteristics of sick and disabled students.

Article 19 Schools must implement the national physical exercise standards and guarantee the time students spend on physical activities each day during school hours.

Article 20 Schools shall organize various forms of extracurricular sports activities, carry out extracurricular training and sports competitions, and, based on conditions, hold a school-wide sports meet once per academic year.

Article 21 Schools shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, be equipped with qualified physical education teachers and ensure that physical education teachers enjoy treatment related to the characteristics of their work.

Article 22 Schools shall be equipped with sports venues, facilities, and equipment according to the standards set by the educational administrative department under the State Council.

School sports venues must be used for sports activities and shall not be used for other purposes.

Article 23 Schools shall establish a system for physical health examinations of students. Educational, sports, and health administrative departments shall strengthen the monitoring of students' physical fitness.

## Chapter IV Competitive Sports

Article 24 The State promotes the development of competitive sports, encourages athletes to improve their sports skills, achieve excellent results in sports competitions, and win honor for the country.

Article 25 The State encourages and supports the conduct of amateur sports training to cultivate outstanding reserve sports talents.

Article 26 Athletes and sports teams participating in major domestic and international sports competitions shall be selected and formed in accordance with the principles of fairness and merit selection. Specific measures shall be formulated by the sports administrative department under the State Council.

Article 27 The training of athletes must implement strict, scientific, and civilized training and management, and provide athletes with education in patriotism, collectivism, and socialism, as well as moral and disciplinary education.

Article 28 The State provides preferential treatment to outstanding athletes in terms of employment or further education.

Article 29 National individual sports associations shall implement registration management for athletes in their respective sports. Registered athletes may, in accordance with the regulations of the sports administrative department under the State Council, participate in relevant sports competitions and personnel movements between sports teams.

Article 30 The State implements a technical grade system for athletes, a technical grade system for referees, and a professional technical position grade system for coaches.

Article 31 The State implements hierarchical and categorical management of sports competitions.

National comprehensive games shall be managed by the sports administrative department under the State Council or by the sports administrative department under the State Council jointly with relevant organizations.

National individual sports competitions shall be managed by the national association for that sport.

Management measures for local comprehensive games and local individual sports competitions shall be formulated by local people's governments.

Article 32 Disputes arising in competitive sports activities shall be mediated and arbitrated by sports arbitration institutions.

The measures for the establishment of sports arbitration institutions and the scope of arbitration shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Article 33 Sports competitions shall adhere to the principle of fair competition. Organizers of sports competitions, athletes, coaches, and referees shall abide by sports ethics and shall not engage in fraud or malpractice.

The use of prohibited drugs and methods is strictly prohibited in sports. Prohibited drug testing institutions shall strictly inspect prohibited drugs and methods.

No organization or individual is allowed to use sports competitions for gambling activities.

Article 34 Major sports competitions held within the territory of China, their names, emblems, flags, mascots, and other symbols shall be protected in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter V Sports Social Organizations

Article 35 The State encourages and supports sports social organizations to organize and carry out sports activities in accordance with their articles of association, and to promote the development of sports.

Article 36 Sports federations at various levels are mass sports organizations that connect and unite athletes and sports workers, and shall play a role in the development of sports.

Article 37 The Chinese Olympic Committee is a sports organization whose main task is to develop and promote the Olympic movement, representing China in international Olympic affairs.

Article 38 Sports science social organizations are academic mass organizations of sports science and technology workers, and shall play a role in the development of sports science and technology.

Article 39 National individual sports associations manage the popularization and improvement of that sport, representing China in the corresponding international individual sports organizations.

## Chapter VI Guarantee Conditions

Article 40 People's governments at or above the county level shall include sports funds and capital construction funds for sports in their fiscal budgets and capital construction investment plans, and gradually increase investment in sports as the national economy develops.

Article 41 The State encourages enterprises, institutions, and social organizations to raise funds for the development of sports, and encourages organizations and individuals to donate and sponsor sports.

Article 42 Relevant state departments shall strengthen the management of sports funds, and no organization or individual may misappropriate or withhold sports funds.

Article 43 The sports administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the management and supervision of business activities involving fitness and competitive sports in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 44 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with state regulations on the quota indicators for urban public sports facility land, incorporate the construction of urban public sports facilities into urban construction plans and overall land use plans, with rational layout and unified arrangement.

When planning enterprises, schools, streets, and residential areas, cities shall include sports facilities in the construction plan.

Townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall gradually build and improve sports facilities as the economy develops.

Article 45 Public sports facilities shall be open to the public to facilitate mass sports activities, with preferential measures for students, the elderly, and persons with disabilities, to improve the utilization rate of sports facilities.

No organization or individual may occupy or damage public sports facilities. If temporary occupation of sports facilities is necessary due to special circumstances, approval must be obtained from the sports administrative department and the construction planning department, and the facilities must be returned promptly; if the use of sports venues is changed according to urban planning, a new site shall be selected and built as compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 46 The State develops professional sports education, establishes various types of sports professional colleges, departments, and specialties, and trains professionals in sports, training, teaching, scientific research, management, and mass sports.

The State encourages enterprises, institutions, social organizations, and citizens to establish professional sports education in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 47 Those who engage in fraud or other acts violating discipline and sports rules in competitive sports shall be punished by sports social organizations in accordance with their articles of association; for directly responsible personnel among state functionaries, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 48 Those who use prohibited drugs and methods in sports shall be punished by sports social organizations in accordance with their articles of association; for directly responsible personnel among state functionaries, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 49 Those who use competitive sports for gambling activities shall be ordered by the sports administrative department to assist the public security organs to stop the illegal activities, and shall be punished by the public security organs in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

In competitive sports activities, those who engage in bribery, fraud, or organize gambling, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

Article 50 Those who occupy or damage public sports facilities shall be ordered by the sports administrative department to make corrections within a time limit, and shall bear civil liability according to law.

If the acts listed in the preceding paragraph violate public security administration, the public security organs shall impose punishments in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 51 In sports activities, those who create disturbances or disrupt public order shall be criticized, educated, and stopped; if they violate public security administration, the public security organs shall impose punishments in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 52 Those who violate the national financial system or financial system, misappropriate or withhold sports funds, shall be ordered by the superior authority to return the misappropriated or withheld funds within a time limit, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 53 Specific measures for the conduct of sports activities by the military shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 54 This Law shall come into force as of October 1, 1995.
