# Administrative License Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on August 27, 2003

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purpose of regulating the establishment and implementation of administrative licenses, protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons and other organizations, safeguarding public interests and social order, and ensuring and supervising the effective exercise of administrative management by administrative organs.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "administrative license" means the act by which an administrative organ, upon the application of a citizen, legal person or other organization, and after examination in accordance with law, permits the applicant to engage in a specific activity.

Article 3 This Law shall apply to the establishment and implementation of administrative licenses.

The approval by an administrative organ of matters concerning the personnel, finance, foreign affairs, etc., of another organ or of a public institution directly under its management shall not be governed by this Law.

Article 4 The establishment and implementation of administrative licenses shall be carried out in accordance with the statutory authority, scope, conditions and procedures.

Article 5 The establishment and implementation of administrative licenses shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality and non-discrimination.

The provisions concerning administrative licenses shall be published; those not published shall not be used as the basis for implementing administrative licenses. The implementation and results of administrative licenses shall be made public, except those involving state secrets, trade secrets or personal privacy. Without the consent of the applicant, the administrative organ, its staff members, and persons participating in expert reviews, etc., shall not disclose the trade secrets, undisclosed information or confidential business information submitted by the applicant, unless otherwise provided by law or involving national security or major public interests; where the administrative organ lawfully discloses the aforementioned information of the applicant, the applicant shall be allowed to raise objections within a reasonable period.

Where the statutory conditions and standards are met, the applicant has the equal right to obtain an administrative license in accordance with law, and the administrative organ shall not discriminate against any person.

Article 6 The implementation of administrative licenses shall follow the principle of convenience for the people, improve work efficiency, and provide quality services.

Article 7 Citizens, legal persons or other organizations shall have the right to make statements and defend themselves regarding the implementation of administrative licenses by administrative organs; they shall have the right to apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate administrative proceedings in accordance with law; if their lawful rights and interests are damaged due to the illegal implementation of an administrative license by an administrative organ, they shall have the right to claim compensation in accordance with law.

Article 8 The administrative license lawfully obtained by a citizen, legal person or other organization shall be protected by law, and the administrative organ shall not arbitrarily change an administrative license that has already taken effect.

If the laws, regulations or rules on which the administrative license is based are amended or repealed, or if the objective circumstances on which the granting of the administrative license is based undergo major changes, the administrative organ may, for the needs of public interest, alter or revoke the administrative license that has already taken effect in accordance with law. If property losses are caused to citizens, legal persons or other organizations as a result, the administrative organ shall provide compensation in accordance with law.

Article 9 An administrative license lawfully obtained shall not be transferred, except where laws and regulations provide that it may be transferred in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures.

Article 10 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve a supervision system over the implementation of administrative licenses by administrative organs, and strengthen supervision and inspection of the implementation of administrative licenses by administrative organs.

Administrative organs shall exercise effective supervision over the activities of citizens, legal persons or other organizations engaged in matters subject to administrative licenses.

## Chapter II Establishment of Administrative Licenses

Article 11 The establishment of administrative licenses shall follow the laws of economic and social development, be conducive to giving play to the initiative and enthusiasm of citizens, legal persons or other organizations, safeguarding public interests and social order, and promoting the coordinated development of the economy, society and the ecological environment.

Article 12 Administrative licenses may be established for the following matters:

(1) Specific activities directly involving national security, public safety, macroeconomic regulation, ecological and environmental protection, and directly related to personal health, life and property safety, which need to be approved in accordance with statutory conditions;

(2) Matters such as the development and utilization of limited natural resources, allocation of public resources, and market access to specific industries directly related to public interests, which need to be granted specific rights;

(3) Professions and industries that provide public services and are directly related to public interests, which need to determine qualifications or credentials such as special reputation, special conditions or special skills;

(4) Important equipment, facilities, products, and articles directly related to public safety, personal health, life and property safety, which need to be verified through inspection, testing, quarantine, etc., in accordance with technical standards and specifications;

(5) Matters such as the establishment of enterprises or other organizations, which need to determine the subject's qualifications;

(6) Other matters for which administrative licenses may be established as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 13 For the matters listed in Article 12 of this Law, if they can be regulated through the following methods, administrative licenses may not be established:

(1) Citizens, legal persons or other organizations are able to make autonomous decisions;

(2) The market competition mechanism can effectively regulate;

(3) Industry organizations or intermediary agencies can self-regulate;

(4) Administrative organs can resolve through other administrative management methods such as ex-post supervision.

Article 14 For the matters listed in Article 12 of this Law, laws may establish administrative licenses. Where no law has been enacted, administrative regulations may establish administrative licenses.

When necessary, the State Council may establish administrative licenses by issuing decisions. After implementation, except for temporary administrative license matters, the State Council shall promptly request the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee to enact laws, or itself enact administrative regulations.

Article 15 For the matters listed in Article 12 of this Law, where no law or administrative regulation has been enacted, local regulations may establish administrative licenses; where no law, administrative regulation or local regulation has been enacted, and due to the needs of administrative management, it is truly necessary to implement an administrative license immediately, the rules of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may establish a temporary administrative license. If a temporary administrative license needs to continue after one year of implementation, the local people's congress or its standing committee shall be requested to enact local regulations.

Local regulations and rules of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall not establish administrative licenses for qualifications or credentials of citizens, legal persons or other organizations that should be uniformly determined by the state; nor shall they establish administrative licenses for the establishment registration of enterprises or other organizations or preconditions thereof. The administrative licenses they establish shall not restrict individuals or enterprises from other regions from engaging in production, business operations and providing services in the region, nor restrict goods from other regions from entering the regional market.

Article 16 Administrative regulations may, within the scope of matters for which administrative licenses are established by laws, make specific provisions for implementing such administrative licenses.

Local regulations may, within the scope of matters for which administrative licenses are established by laws and administrative regulations, make specific provisions for implementing such administrative licenses.

Rules may, within the scope of matters for which administrative licenses are established by higher-level laws, make specific provisions for implementing such administrative licenses.

The specific provisions made by regulations and rules for implementing administrative licenses established by higher-level laws shall not add new administrative licenses; the specific provisions made on conditions for administrative licenses shall not add other conditions that violate the higher-level laws.

Article 17 Except as provided in Articles 14 and 15 of this Law, no other regulatory documents shall establish administrative licenses.

Article 18 When establishing an administrative license, the implementing organ, conditions, procedures and time limits for the administrative license shall be specified.

Article 19 When drafting a draft law, draft regulation, or draft rules of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, if an administrative license is to be established, the drafting unit shall solicit opinions through forms such as hearings and demonstration meetings, and shall explain to the enacting organ the necessity of establishing the administrative license, the possible impact on the economy and society, and the situation of soliciting and adopting opinions.

Article 20 The organ that establishes an administrative license shall periodically evaluate the administrative license it has established; for an established administrative license, if it is considered that it can be resolved through the methods listed in Article 13 of this Law, the provisions establishing the administrative license shall be promptly amended or repealed.

The organ implementing an administrative license may timely evaluate the implementation status and the necessity of the established administrative license, and report its opinions to the organ that established the administrative license.

Citizens, legal persons or other organizations may submit opinions and suggestions to the organ that establishes or implements an administrative license regarding the establishment and implementation of the administrative license.

Article 21 If the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government considers that an administrative license concerning economic affairs established by administrative regulations can be resolved through the methods listed in Article 13 of this Law, based on the economic and social development of its administrative region, it may, after reporting to and obtaining approval from the State Council, cease implementing the administrative license within its administrative region.

## Chapter III Organs Implementing Administrative Licenses

Article 22 Administrative licenses shall be implemented by administrative organs with the power of administrative license within their statutory functions and powers.

Article 23 Organizations authorized by laws and regulations to have the function of managing public affairs may, within the scope of statutory authorization, implement administrative licenses in their own name. The provisions of this Law concerning administrative organs shall apply to authorized organizations.

Article 24 Within their statutory functions and powers, administrative organs may, in accordance with the provisions of laws, regulations and rules, entrust other administrative organs to implement administrative licenses. The entrusting organ shall announce the entrusted administrative organ and the content of the entrusted implementation of the administrative license.

The entrusting administrative organ shall be responsible for supervising the implementation of the administrative license by the entrusted administrative organ and shall bear legal responsibility for the consequences of such implementation.

The entrusted administrative organ shall implement the administrative license in the name of the entrusting administrative organ within the scope of the entrustment; it shall not further entrust any other organization or individual to implement the administrative license.

Article 25 With the approval of the State Council, the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, based on the principles of simplification, unity and efficiency, decide that one administrative organ shall exercise the administrative license powers of relevant administrative organs.

Article 26 Where an administrative license requires processing by multiple internal organs of an administrative organ, the administrative organ shall designate one organ to uniformly accept the application for the administrative license and uniformly deliver the decision on the administrative license.

Where an administrative license is to be implemented separately by two or more departments of a local people's government in accordance with law, the people's government at the same level may designate one department to accept the application for the administrative license and forward it to the relevant departments for their respective opinions before unified processing, or organize the relevant departments for joint processing or centralized processing.

Article 27 When implementing an administrative license, an administrative organ shall not make improper demands on the applicant, such as purchasing designated goods or accepting paid services.

Staff members of an administrative organ handling an administrative license shall not extort or accept property from the applicant, nor seek other benefits.

Article 28 For the inspection, testing and quarantine of equipment, facilities, products and articles directly related to public safety, personal health, life and property safety, except where laws and administrative regulations provide for implementation by administrative organs, they shall gradually be implemented by professional technical organizations that meet statutory conditions. Professional technical organizations and their relevant personnel shall bear legal responsibility for the conclusions of the inspection, testing and quarantine they implement.

## Chapter IV Procedures for Implementing Administrative Licenses

### Section 1 Application and Acceptance

Article 29 A citizen, legal person or other organization engaging in a specific activity that requires an administrative license in accordance with law shall submit an application to the administrative organ. If the application form needs to be in a standard format, the administrative organ shall provide the applicant with the standard format of the application form for the administrative license. The standard format of the application form shall not contain content that is not directly related to the matter for which the administrative license is applied.

The applicant may entrust an agent to submit the application for the administrative license, except where, in accordance with law, the applicant is required to submit the application for the administrative license in person at the office premises of the administrative organ.

Applications for administrative licenses may be submitted by mail, telegram, telex, fax, electronic data interchange, email, etc.

Article 30 An administrative organ shall display at its office premises the matters, basis, conditions, quantity, procedures, time limits, and the complete list of materials to be submitted, as well as model application forms, concerning the administrative license as prescribed by laws, regulations and rules.

If an applicant requests the administrative organ to explain or interpret the displayed content, the administrative organ shall provide explanations and interpretations, and provide accurate and reliable information.

Article 31 When applying for an administrative license, the applicant shall truthfully submit relevant materials and reflect the true situation to the administrative organ, and shall be responsible for the authenticity of the substantive content of the application materials. The administrative organ shall not require the applicant to submit technical data or other materials unrelated to the matter for which the administrative license is applied.

Administrative organs and their staff members shall not make the transfer of technology a condition for obtaining an administrative license; nor shall they, directly or indirectly, require the transfer of technology during the implementation of the administrative license.

Article 32 An administrative organ shall handle an application for an administrative license submitted by an applicant according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the matter for which the application is made does not require an administrative license in accordance with law, the applicant shall be immediately informed that the application will not be accepted;

(2) If the matter for which the application is made does not fall within the functions and powers of the administrative organ in accordance with law, a decision of non-acceptance shall be made immediately, and the applicant shall be informed to apply to the relevant administrative organ;

(3) If the application materials contain errors that can be corrected on the spot, the applicant shall be allowed to correct them on the spot;

(4) If the application materials are incomplete or do not conform to the statutory form, the applicant shall be informed on the spot or within five days of all the content that needs to be supplemented or corrected; if not informed within the time limit, the application shall be deemed accepted from the date of receipt of the application materials;

(5) If the matter for which the application is made falls within the functions and powers of the administrative organ, and the application materials are complete and conform to the statutory form, or the applicant has submitted all the supplemented or corrected application materials as required by the administrative organ, the application for the administrative license shall be accepted.

When an administrative organ accepts or does not accept an application for an administrative license, it shall issue a written certificate bearing the special seal of the administrative organ and the date.

Article 33 Administrative organs shall establish and improve relevant systems, promote e-government, publish matters concerning administrative licenses on their websites to facilitate applicants to submit applications for administrative licenses by means of data messages, etc.; they shall share relevant information on administrative licenses with other administrative organs to improve work efficiency.

### Section 2 Review and Decision

Article 34 An administrative organ shall review the application materials submitted by the applicant.

If the application materials submitted by the applicant are complete and conform to the statutory form, and the administrative organ can make a decision on the spot, it shall make a written decision on the administrative license on the spot.

If, according to statutory conditions and procedures, the substantive content of the application materials needs to be verified, the administrative organ shall assign two or more staff members to conduct the verification.

Article 35 For an administrative license that, in accordance with law, should first be reviewed by a lower-level administrative organ and then reported to a higher-level administrative organ for decision, the lower-level administrative organ shall, within the statutory time limit, directly submit its preliminary review opinion and all application materials to the higher-level administrative organ. The higher-level administrative organ shall not require the applicant to submit duplicate application materials.

Article 36 When reviewing an application for an administrative license, if the administrative organ finds that the matter of the administrative license directly involves the major interests of another person, it shall inform that interested party. The applicant and the interested party shall have the right to make statements and defend themselves. The administrative organ shall hear the opinions of the applicant and the interested party.

Article 37 After reviewing an application for an administrative license, except for making a decision on the administrative license on the spot, the administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license within the statutory time limit and in accordance with the prescribed procedures.

Article 38 If the applicant's application meets the statutory conditions and standards, the administrative organ shall make a written decision to grant the administrative license in accordance with law.

If the administrative organ makes a written decision not to grant the administrative license in accordance with law, it shall state the reasons and inform the applicant of the right to apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate administrative proceedings in accordance with law.

Article 39 If an administrative organ makes a decision to grant an administrative license and needs to issue an administrative license document, it shall issue to the applicant the following administrative license documents bearing the seal of the administrative organ:

(1) Licenses, permits or other certification documents;

(2) Qualification certificates, credential certificates or other certificates of competence;

(3) Approval documents or certification documents of the administrative organ;

(4) Other administrative license documents as prescribed by laws and regulations.

If an administrative organ implements inspection, testing or quarantine, it may affix labels or stamps of inspection, testing or quarantine on the equipment, facilities, products or articles that have passed the inspection, testing or quarantine.

Article 40 The decision of an administrative organ to grant an administrative license shall be made public, and the public shall have the right to access it.

Article 41 For an administrative license established by laws or administrative regulations, if its scope of application has no territorial restrictions, the administrative license obtained by the applicant shall be valid nationwide.

### Section 3 Time Limits

Article 42 Except where a decision on an administrative license can be made on the spot, the administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license within twenty days from the date of accepting the application for the administrative license. If a decision cannot be made within twenty days, with the approval of the head of the administrative organ, the time limit may be extended by ten days, and the reasons for the extension shall be informed to the applicant. However, if otherwise provided by laws or regulations, those provisions shall prevail.

If, in accordance with the provisions of Article 26 of this Law, an administrative license is handled through unified processing or joint processing or centralized processing, the processing time shall not exceed forty-five days; if it cannot be completed within forty-five days, with the approval of the head of the people's government at the same level, the time limit may be extended by fifteen days, and the reasons for the extension shall be informed to the applicant.

Article 43 For an administrative license that, in accordance with law, should first be reviewed by a lower-level administrative organ and then reported to a higher-level administrative organ for decision, the lower-level administrative organ shall complete the review within twenty days from the date of accepting the application for the administrative license. However, if otherwise provided by laws or regulations, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 44 After an administrative organ makes a decision to grant an administrative license, it shall, within ten days from the date of making the decision, issue and deliver the administrative license document to the applicant, or affix labels or stamps of inspection, testing or quarantine.

Article 45 If an administrative organ, in making a decision on an administrative license, needs to conduct hearings, tenders, auctions, inspections, tests, quarantines, appraisals or expert reviews in accordance with law, the time required for such activities shall not be counted within the time limits specified in this Section. The administrative organ shall inform the applicant in writing of the time required.

### Section 4 Hearings

Article 46 For matters for which laws, regulations or rules provide that hearings shall be held for implementing an administrative license, or for other major administrative license matters involving public interests that the administrative organ considers necessary to hold hearings, the administrative organ shall make a public announcement and hold a hearing.Article 47 Where an administrative license directly involves a significant interest between the applicant and another party, the administrative organ shall, before making a decision on the administrative license, inform the applicant and the interested party of their right to request a hearing. If the applicant or the interested party submits a hearing request within five days from the date of being informed of the hearing right, the administrative organ shall organize a hearing within twenty days.

The applicant and the interested party shall not bear the costs of the hearing organized by the administrative organ.

Article 48 A hearing shall be conducted in accordance with the following procedures:

(1) The administrative organ shall notify the applicant and the interested party of the time and place of the hearing seven days before the hearing is held, and shall make a public announcement if necessary;

(2) The hearing shall be held publicly;

(3) The administrative organ shall designate a person other than the staff member reviewing the administrative license application as the hearing moderator. If the applicant or the interested party believes that the moderator has a direct interest in the administrative license matter, they have the right to request the moderator's recusal;

(4) During the hearing, the staff member reviewing the administrative license application shall present the evidence and reasons for the review opinion. The applicant and the interested party may present evidence and engage in defense and cross-examination;

(5) A record of the hearing shall be made, and the hearing record shall be confirmed as correct by the hearing participants before they sign or affix their seals.

The administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license based on the hearing record.

### Section 5 Modification and Renewal

Article 49 If a licensee requests a modification of the administrative license matter, they shall submit an application to the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license. If the statutory conditions and standards are met, the administrative organ shall handle the modification procedures in accordance with the law.

Article 50 If a licensee needs to renew the validity period of an administrative license obtained lawfully, they shall submit an application to the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license at least thirty days before the expiration of the validity period of the administrative license. However, if laws, regulations, or rules provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

The administrative organ shall, based on the licensee's application, make a decision on whether to approve the renewal before the expiration of the validity period of the administrative license. If no decision is made within the time limit, the renewal shall be deemed approved.

### Section 6 Special Provisions

Article 51 For procedures for implementing administrative licenses, if this Section has provisions, those provisions shall apply; if this Section has no provisions, other relevant provisions of this Chapter shall apply.

Article 52 The procedures for implementing administrative licenses by the State Council shall be governed by the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 53 For implementing administrative licenses for matters listed in Item (2) of Article 12 of this Law, the administrative organ shall make a decision through fair competition methods such as bidding or auction. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

The specific procedures for the administrative organ to make decisions on administrative licenses through bidding, auction, or other methods shall be governed by the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

After the administrative organ determines the winning bidder or buyer through bidding or auction procedures, it shall make a decision to grant the administrative license and issue the administrative license document to the winning bidder or buyer in accordance with the law.

If the administrative organ violates the provisions of this Article by not adopting bidding or auction methods, or by violating bidding or auction procedures, thereby harming the lawful rights and interests of the applicant, the applicant may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

Article 54 For implementing administrative licenses for matters listed in Item (3) of Article 12 of this Law, where granting specific qualifications to citizens requires a national examination to be held in accordance with the law, the administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license based on the examination results and other statutory conditions; where granting specific qualifications or credentials to legal persons or other organizations, the administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license based on the assessment results of the applicant's professional personnel composition, technical conditions, business performance, and management level. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Examinations for specific qualifications of citizens shall be organized by administrative organs or industry organizations in accordance with the law and shall be held publicly. The administrative organ or industry organization shall announce in advance the registration conditions, registration methods, examination subjects, and examination syllabus for the qualification examination. However, they shall not organize compulsory pre-examination training or designate textbooks or other exam preparation materials.

Article 55 For implementing administrative licenses for matters listed in Item (4) of Article 12 of this Law, inspections, tests, and quarantines shall be conducted in accordance with technical standards and technical specifications. The administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license based on the results of the inspections, tests, and quarantines.

When conducting inspections, tests, or quarantines, the administrative organ shall, within five days from the date of accepting the application, assign two or more staff members to conduct the inspections, tests, or quarantines in accordance with technical standards and technical specifications. If it can be determined on the spot whether the equipment, facilities, products, or articles meet the technical standards and technical specifications without further technical analysis of the inspection, test, or quarantine results, the administrative organ shall make a decision on the administrative license on the spot.

If the administrative organ decides not to grant the administrative license based on the inspection, test, or quarantine results, it shall provide a written explanation of the technical standards and technical specifications on which the decision not to grant the license is based.

Article 56 For implementing administrative licenses for matters listed in Item (5) of Article 12 of this Law, if the application materials submitted by the applicant are complete and conform to the statutory form, the administrative organ shall register the matter on the spot. If the substantive content of the application materials needs to be verified, the administrative organ shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 3 of Article 34 of this Law.

Article 57 For administrative licenses with quantity limitations, if two or more applicants' applications all meet the statutory conditions and standards, the administrative organ shall make a decision to grant the administrative license based on the order in which the applications for the administrative license were accepted. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

## Chapter 5 Fees for Administrative Licenses

Article 58 Administrative organs shall not charge any fees for implementing administrative licenses or for supervising and inspecting administrative license matters. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Administrative organs shall not charge fees for providing standard forms for administrative license applications.

The funds required by administrative organs for implementing administrative licenses shall be included in the budget of the administrative organ, guaranteed by the finance at the corresponding level, and allocated in accordance with the approved budget.

Article 59 If administrative organs charge fees for implementing administrative licenses in accordance with laws or administrative regulations, they shall charge fees in accordance with the statutory items and standards that have been published. All fees collected must be turned over to the state treasury in full. No organ or individual may, in any form, intercept, misappropriate, privately divide, or covertly divide the fees. Financial departments shall not, in any form, return or covertly return the fees collected for implementing administrative licenses to administrative organs.

## Chapter 6 Supervision and Inspection

Article 60 Higher-level administrative organs shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the implementation of administrative licenses by lower-level administrative organs and promptly correct illegal acts in the implementation of administrative licenses.

Article 61 Administrative organs shall establish and improve a supervision system, and fulfill their supervisory responsibilities by verifying relevant materials that reflect the licensee's activities in the administrative license matter.

When administrative organs conduct supervision and inspection of the licensee's activities in the administrative license matter in accordance with the law, they shall record the supervision and inspection situation and the handling results, and the records shall be signed by the supervision and inspection personnel and filed. The public has the right to access the supervision and inspection records of administrative organs.

Administrative organs shall create conditions to achieve interconnection of computer file systems with licensees and other relevant administrative organs to verify the licensee's activities in the administrative license matter.

Article 62 Administrative organs may conduct random sampling inspections, tests, and inspections of the products produced or operated by licensees in accordance with the law, and conduct on-site inspections of their production or operation premises. During inspections, administrative organs may, in accordance with the law, review or require licensees to submit relevant materials; licensees shall truthfully provide relevant information and materials.

Administrative organs shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, conduct regular inspections of important equipment and facilities that directly relate to public safety, human health, and the safety of life and property. For those that pass the inspection, the administrative organ shall issue the corresponding certification documents.

Article 63 When conducting supervision and inspection, administrative organs shall not interfere with the normal production and operation activities of licensees, shall not demand or accept property from licensees, and shall not seek other benefits.

Article 64 If a licensee engages in illegal activities related to the administrative license matter outside the jurisdiction of the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license, the administrative organ in the place where the illegal act occurred shall, in accordance with the law, copy the licensee's illegal facts and handling results to the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license.

Article 65 Individuals and organizations that discover illegal activities related to administrative license matters have the right to report them to administrative organs, and the administrative organs shall promptly verify and handle the reports.

Article 66 If a licensee fails to fulfill the obligation to develop and utilize natural resources or fails to fulfill the obligation to utilize public resources in accordance with the law, the administrative organ shall order rectification within a specified time limit. If the licensee fails to rectify within the specified time limit, the administrative organ shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 67 A licensee who has obtained market access for a specific industry that directly relates to the public interest shall provide users with safe, convenient, stable, and reasonably priced services in accordance with the service standards, tariff standards prescribed by the state, and the conditions prescribed by the administrative organ in accordance with the law, and shall fulfill the obligation of universal service. Without the approval of the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license, the licensee shall not suspend or cease business.

If the licensee fails to fulfill the obligations specified in the preceding paragraph, the administrative organ shall order rectification within a specified time limit or take effective measures in accordance with the law to urge the licensee to fulfill its obligations.

Article 68 For important equipment and facilities that directly relate to public safety, human health, and the safety of life and property, administrative organs shall urge the design, construction, installation, and use units to establish corresponding self-inspection systems.

When conducting supervision and inspection, if administrative organs discover safety hazards in important equipment and facilities that directly relate to public safety, human health, and the safety of life and property, they shall order the cessation of construction, installation, and use, and order the design, construction, installation, and use units to immediately rectify the situation.

Article 69 Under any of the following circumstances, the administrative organ that made the decision on the administrative license or its higher-level administrative organ may, upon the request of an interested party or ex officio, revoke the administrative license:

(1) The staff member of the administrative organ abused their power or neglected their duty in making the decision to grant the administrative license;

(2) The decision to grant the administrative license was made beyond the statutory authority;

(3) The decision to grant the administrative license was made in violation of statutory procedures;

(4) The administrative license was granted to an applicant who did not meet the application qualifications or did not meet the statutory conditions;

(5) Other circumstances under which the administrative license may be revoked in accordance with the law.

If a licensee obtains an administrative license through improper means such as fraud or bribery, the license shall be revoked.

If revoking the administrative license in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs may cause significant harm to the public interest, the license shall not be revoked.

If the lawful rights and interests of the licensee are harmed due to the revocation of the administrative license in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 1 of this Article, the administrative organ shall provide compensation in accordance with the law. If the administrative license is revoked in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 2 of this Article, the interests obtained by the licensee based on the administrative license shall not be protected.

Article 70 Under any of the following circumstances, the administrative organ shall handle the cancellation procedures for the relevant administrative license in accordance with the law:

(1) The validity period of the administrative license has expired and has not been renewed;

(2) For an administrative license granting specific qualifications to a citizen, the citizen has died or lost capacity for civil conduct;

(3) The legal person or other organization has been terminated in accordance with the law;

(4) The administrative license has been revoked or withdrawn in accordance with the law, or the administrative license document has been revoked in accordance with the law;

(5) The administrative license matter cannot be implemented due to force majeure;

(6) Other circumstances under which the administrative license should be cancelled as provided by laws or regulations.

## Chapter 7 Legal Liability

Article 71 If an administrative license is established in violation of the provisions of Article 17 of this Law, the relevant organ shall order the organ that established the license to correct the matter or revoke the license in accordance with the law.

Article 72 If an administrative organ or its staff member violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the higher-level administrative organ or the supervisory organ shall order correction. If the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to accept an application for an administrative license that meets the statutory conditions;

(2) Failing to display materials that should be displayed in accordance with the law at the office premises;

(3) Failing to fulfill the statutory notification obligations to the applicant or interested party during the process of accepting, reviewing, or deciding on an administrative license;

(4) Failing to inform the applicant at one time of all the content that needs to be supplemented or corrected when the application materials submitted by the applicant are incomplete or do not conform to the statutory form;

(5) Illegally disclosing trade secrets, undisclosed information, or confidential business information submitted by the applicant;

(6) Making the transfer of technology a condition for obtaining the administrative license, or directly or indirectly requiring the transfer of technology during the implementation of the administrative license;

(7) Failing to explain the reasons for not accepting the application for an administrative license or for not granting the administrative license in accordance with the law;

(8) Failing to hold a hearing when a hearing should be held in accordance with the law.

Article 73 If a staff member of an administrative organ, while handling an administrative license or conducting supervision and inspection, demands or accepts property from others or seeks other benefits, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law. If the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

Article 74 If an administrative organ implements an administrative license under any of the following circumstances, the higher-level administrative organ or the supervisory organ shall order correction, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Granting an administrative license to an applicant who does not meet the statutory conditions or making a decision to grant an administrative license beyond the statutory authority;

(2) Not granting an administrative license to an applicant who meets the statutory conditions or failing to make a decision to grant an administrative license within the statutory time limit;

(3) Failing to make a decision to grant an administrative license based on the results of bidding, auction, or examination results when it should be made based on such results in accordance with the law, or making a decision to grant an administrative license without conducting bidding, auction, or examination, or not based on the results of bidding, auction, or examination results.

Article 75 If an administrative organ, when implementing an administrative license, charges fees without authorization or does not charge fees in accordance with statutory items and standards, the higher-level administrative organ or the supervisory organ shall order the return of the illegally collected fees. Administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

If fees collected in accordance with the law for implementing an administrative license are intercepted, misappropriated, privately divided, or covertly divided, they shall be recovered. Administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 76 If an administrative organ illegally implements an administrative license, causing damage to the lawful rights and interests of a party, it shall provide compensation in accordance with the provisions of the State Compensation Law.

Article 77 If an administrative organ fails to perform its supervisory duties in accordance with the law or performs them inadequately, resulting in serious consequences, the higher-level administrative organ or the supervisory organ shall order correction, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 78 If an applicant for an administrative license conceals relevant information or provides false materials when applying for the administrative license, the administrative organ shall not accept the application or shall not grant the administrative license, and shall issue a warning. If the application for the administrative license directly relates to public safety, human health, or the safety of life and property, the applicant shall not apply for the same administrative license again within one year.

Article 79 If a licensee obtains an administrative license through improper means such as fraud or bribery, the administrative organ shall impose an administrative penalty in accordance with the law. If the obtained administrative license directly relates to public safety, human health, or the safety of life and property, the applicant shall not apply for the same administrative license again within three years. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 80 If a licensee commits any of the following acts, the administrative organ shall impose an administrative penalty in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Altering, selling, leasing, lending, or otherwise illegally transferring the administrative license document;

(2) Engaging in activities beyond the scope of the administrative license;

(3) Concealing relevant information, providing false materials, or refusing to provide truthful materials reflecting their activities to the administrative organ responsible for supervision and inspection;

(4) Other illegal acts as provided by laws, regulations, or rules.

Article 81 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization engages in activities that should require an administrative license in accordance with the law without obtaining the administrative license, the administrative organ shall take measures to stop the activities in accordance with the law and impose an administrative penalty in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 82 The time limits for administrative organs to implement administrative licenses as provided in this Law shall be calculated in working days, excluding statutory holidays.

Article 83 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2004.

Provisions on administrative licenses in effect before the implementation of this Law shall be reviewed by the enacting organs in accordance with the provisions of this Law. Those that do not conform to the provisions of this Law shall cease to be implemented from the date of implementation of this Law.
