# Vaccine Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 29, 2019

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to strengthen vaccine administration, ensure vaccine quality and supply, regulate vaccination, promote the development of the vaccine industry, safeguard public health, and maintain public health security.

**Article 2** This Law applies to the research and development, production, distribution, vaccination, and the supervision and administration thereof within the territory of the People's Republic of China. Matters not covered by this Law shall be governed by the Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases, and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

For the purposes of this Law, "vaccine" means a preventive biological product used for human immunization to prevent and control the occurrence and spread of diseases, including vaccines covered by the national immunization program and vaccines not covered by the national immunization program.

**Article 3** The State applies the strictest administration system to vaccines, adhering to the principles of safety first, risk management, whole-process control, science-based regulation, and social co-governance.

**Article 4** The State upholds the strategic and public welfare nature of vaccine products.

The State supports basic research and applied research on vaccines, promotes vaccine research, development, and innovation, and incorporates the research, development, production, and stockpiling of vaccines for the prevention and control of major diseases into the national strategy.

The State formulates development plans and industrial policies for the vaccine industry, supports the development and structural optimization of the vaccine industry, encourages large-scale and intensive vaccine production, and continuously improves vaccine production processes and quality levels.

**Article 5** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall strengthen the quality management of the entire vaccine lifecycle and be responsible for the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of vaccines.

Entities and individuals engaged in vaccine research and development, production, distribution, and vaccination shall comply with laws, regulations, rules, standards, and norms, ensure that information throughout the entire process is true, accurate, complete, and traceable, bear responsibility according to law, and accept social supervision.

**Article 6** The State implements a national immunization program system.

Residents living within the territory of China have the right to receive vaccines covered by the national immunization program according to law and have the obligation to receive such vaccines. The government provides vaccines covered by the national immunization program to residents free of charge.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall ensure that school-age children receive vaccines covered by the national immunization program. Guardians shall ensure according to law that school-age children receive vaccines covered by the national immunization program on time.

**Article 7** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate vaccine safety work and vaccination work into their respective national economic and social development plans, strengthen the capacity building for vaccine supervision and administration, and establish and improve the working mechanism for vaccine supervision and administration.

Local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for vaccine supervision and administration within their respective administrative regions, and shall provide unified leadership, organization, and coordination for vaccine supervision and administration within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 8** The drug regulatory authority of the State Council is responsible for the nationwide supervision and administration of vaccines. The health authority of the State Council is responsible for the nationwide supervision and administration of vaccination. Other relevant departments of the State Council are responsible for vaccine-related supervision and administration within their respective scopes of duties.

The drug regulatory authorities of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government are responsible for vaccine supervision and administration within their respective administrative regions. The departments responsible for drug regulatory duties of the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties (hereinafter referred to as drug regulatory authorities) are responsible for vaccine supervision and administration within their respective administrative regions. The health authorities of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for vaccination supervision and administration within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for vaccine-related supervision and administration within their respective scopes of duties.

**Article 9** The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish departmental coordination mechanisms to coordinate vaccine supervision and administration work, regularly analyze the vaccine safety situation, strengthen vaccine supervision and administration, and ensure vaccine supply.

**Article 10** The State implements a whole-process electronic traceability system for vaccines.

The drug regulatory authority of the State Council, together with the health authority of the State Council, shall formulate unified vaccine traceability standards and norms, establish a national collaborative platform for vaccine electronic traceability, integrate traceability information from the entire process of vaccine production, distribution, and vaccination, and achieve vaccine traceability.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall establish vaccine electronic traceability systems, which shall be linked to the national collaborative platform for vaccine electronic traceability, to achieve traceability and verifiability of vaccines at the smallest packaging unit throughout the entire process of production, distribution, and vaccination.

Disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units shall truthfully record vaccine distribution, vaccination, and other relevant information according to law, and provide traceability information to the national collaborative platform for vaccine electronic traceability in accordance with regulations.

**Article 11** During vaccine research and development, production, inspection, and other processes, a biosafety management system shall be established and improved, biosafety risks shall be strictly controlled, the biosafety management of pathogenic microorganisms such as bacterial and viral strains shall be strengthened, the health of operators and the public shall be protected, and the lawful and legitimate use of pathogenic microorganisms such as bacterial and viral strains shall be ensured.

Bacterial and viral strains and cell lines used in vaccine research and development, production, inspection, etc., shall have clear histories, biological characteristics, and passage numbers, detailed archives shall be established, and their sources shall be lawful, clear, and traceable; those with unknown sources shall not be used.

**Article 12** People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, marketing authorization holders of vaccines, and vaccine industry associations shall regularly carry out publicity and popularization of vaccine safety laws, regulations, and vaccination knowledge through activities such as National Children's Vaccination Day.

News media shall conduct public welfare publicity on vaccine safety laws, regulations, and vaccination knowledge, and exercise public opinion supervision over illegal vaccine activities. Reports and publicity related to vaccines shall be comprehensive, scientific, objective, and fair.

**Article 13** Vaccine industry associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, establish and improve industry norms, promote the construction of the industry integrity system, and guide and urge members to carry out production and operation activities according to law.

## Chapter II Vaccine Research, Development, and Registration

**Article 14** The State, based on factors such as disease prevalence and population immune status, formulates relevant research and development plans, allocates necessary funds, and supports the research and development of new vaccines such as multivalent and combination vaccines.

The State organizes marketing authorization holders of vaccines, scientific research institutions, and medical and health institutions to jointly tackle key problems and develop vaccines urgently needed for disease prevention and control.

**Article 15** The State encourages marketing authorization holders of vaccines to increase investment in research, development, and innovation, optimize production processes, improve quality control levels, and promote technological progress in vaccines.

**Article 16** Conducting vaccine clinical trials shall be approved by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council according to law.

Vaccine clinical trials shall be implemented or organized by tertiary medical institutions or disease prevention and control institutions at or above the provincial level that meet the conditions prescribed by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council and the health authority of the State Council.

The State encourages qualified medical institutions, disease prevention and control institutions, etc., to conduct vaccine clinical trials according to law.

**Article 17** The sponsor of a vaccine clinical trial shall formulate a clinical trial protocol, establish a clinical trial safety monitoring and evaluation system, carefully select subjects, reasonably set subject groups and age ranges, and take effective measures based on the degree of risk to protect the legitimate rights and interests of subjects.

**Article 18** Conducting a vaccine clinical trial shall obtain the written informed consent of the subject; if the subject is a person without capacity for civil conduct, the written informed consent of the guardian shall be obtained; if the subject is a person with limited capacity for civil conduct, the written informed consent of both the subject and the guardian shall be obtained.

**Article 19** Vaccines marketed within the territory of China shall be approved by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council and obtain a drug registration certificate; applying for vaccine registration shall provide true, sufficient, and reliable data, materials, and samples.

For vaccines urgently needed for disease prevention and control and innovative vaccines, the drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall give priority to review and approval.

**Article 20** For vaccines urgently needed to respond to major public health emergencies or other vaccines deemed urgently needed by the health authority of the State Council, if the assessed benefits outweigh the risks, the drug regulatory authority of the State Council may conditionally approve the vaccine registration application.

In the event of an exceptionally major public health emergency or other emergency events seriously threatening public health, the health authority of the State Council may, based on the needs of infectious disease prevention and control, propose the emergency use of a vaccine. After the drug regulatory authority of the State Council organizes evaluation and agrees, the vaccine may be used urgently within a certain scope and period.

**Article 21** When approving a vaccine registration application, the drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall approve the production process, quality control standards, package inserts, and labels of the vaccine.

The drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall promptly publish the contents of vaccine package inserts and labels on its website.

## Chapter III Vaccine Production and Lot Release

**Article 22** The State implements a strict access system for vaccine production.

Engaging in vaccine production activities shall be approved by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the provincial level and a drug manufacturing license shall be obtained.

Engaging in vaccine production activities shall, in addition to meeting the conditions for engaging in drug production activities stipulated in the Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, also meet the following conditions:

(1) Possessing an appropriate scale and sufficient production capacity reserves;

(2) Having systems, facilities, and equipment to ensure biosafety;

(3) Meeting the needs of disease prevention and control.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall possess vaccine production capacity; if it is truly necessary to entrust production beyond their vaccine production capacity, approval from the drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall be obtained. The entrusted manufacturer shall comply with the provisions of this Law and relevant state regulations to ensure vaccine quality.

**Article 23** The legal representative and principal responsible person of a marketing authorization holder of vaccines shall have a good credit record. Key personnel such as the production management responsible person, quality management responsible person, and authorized quality person shall have relevant professional backgrounds and work experience.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall strengthen the training and assessment of the personnel mentioned in the preceding paragraph and promptly report their appointments and changes to the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 24** Vaccines shall be produced and tested in accordance with the approved production process and quality control standards. The entire production process shall comply with the requirements of the Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall review and test the entire vaccine production process and vaccine quality in accordance with regulations.

**Article 25** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall establish a complete production quality management system, continuously strengthen deviation management, use information technology to truthfully record all data generated during the production and testing processes, and ensure that the entire production process continuously complies with legal requirements.

**Article 26** The State implements a vaccine lot release system.

Before each batch of vaccines is sold or imported, it shall be reviewed and tested by a lot release agency designated by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with relevant technical requirements. If it meets the requirements, a lot release certificate shall be issued; if it does not meet the requirements, a notice of non-issuance of lot release certificate shall be issued.

Vaccines for which a lot release certificate is not issued shall not be sold and shall be destroyed under the supervision of the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; imported vaccines for which a lot release certificate is not issued shall be destroyed under the supervision of the drug regulatory authority at the port location or otherwise disposed of according to law.

The drug regulatory authority of the State Council and the lot release agency shall promptly publish the lot release results of marketed vaccines for public inquiry.

**Article 27** When applying for vaccine lot release, the applicant shall provide the lot release agency with materials such as batch production and testing records summaries and samples of the same batch number product in accordance with regulations. For imported vaccines, certificates of origin and lot release certificates shall also be provided; if lot release is exempted in the country of origin, a certificate of exemption from lot release shall be provided.

**Article 28** Vaccines urgently needed for the prevention and control of infectious disease epidemics or for responding to emergencies may be exempted from lot release upon approval by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 29** Vaccine lot release shall involve batch-by-batch document review and sampling testing. The items and frequency of lot release testing shall be dynamically adjusted based on the risk assessment of vaccine quality.

If there are doubts about the authenticity of the application materials or samples for vaccine lot release, or if there are other circumstances requiring further verification, the lot release agency shall conduct verification, and if necessary, organize on-site verification by means such as on-site sampling testing.

**Article 30** If a lot release agency discovers a significant quality risk in a vaccine during the lot release process, it shall promptly report to the drug regulatory authority of the State Council and the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The department receiving the report shall immediately conduct an on-site inspection of the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine. Based on the inspection results, it shall notify the lot release agency to refuse or suspend the lot release of the relevant products or all products of the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine, and order the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine to rectify. The marketing authorization holder of the vaccine shall immediately rectify and promptly report the rectification status to the department that ordered the rectification.

**Article 31** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall truthfully record production process deviations, quality differences, failures and accidents during the production process, and the measures taken, and include them in the documents submitted for the lot release application of the corresponding batch of products; if it may affect vaccine quality, the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine shall immediately take measures and report to the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter IV Vaccine Distribution

**Article 32** Vaccines covered by the national immunization program shall be procured through centralized bidding or unified negotiation organized by the health authority of the State Council together with the finance authority of the State Council, etc., and the winning bid prices or transaction prices shall be published. Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall implement unified procurement.

Other vaccines covered by the national immunization program and vaccines not covered by the national immunization program shall be procured by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government through provincial-level public resource trading platforms.

**Article 33** The prices of vaccines shall be reasonably and independently determined by marketing authorization holders of vaccines according to law. The price levels, price differential ratios, and profit margins of vaccines shall be maintained within a reasonable range.

**Article 34** Provincial-level disease prevention and control institutions shall, based on the national immunization program and the disease prevention and control needs of their respective administrative regions, formulate plans for the use of vaccines covered by the national immunization program within their administrative regions, report to the department organizing vaccine procurement in accordance with relevant state regulations, and simultaneously file with the health authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 35** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall supply vaccines to disease prevention and control institutions in accordance with the procurement contract.

Disease prevention and control institutions shall supply vaccines to vaccination units in accordance with regulations.

Entities and individuals other than disease prevention and control institutions shall not supply vaccines to vaccination units, and vaccination units shall not accept such vaccines.

**Article 36** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall deliver vaccines to disease prevention and control institutions or vaccination units designated by disease prevention and control institutions in accordance with the procurement contract.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines and disease prevention and control institutions that deliver vaccines themselves shall have the conditions for cold chain storage and transportation of vaccines, or may entrust qualified vaccine delivery units to deliver vaccines.

Disease prevention and control institutions may charge storage and transportation fees for delivering vaccines not covered by the national immunization program. The specific measures shall be formulated by the finance authority of the State Council together with the pricing authority of the State Council, and the charging standards shall be formulated by the pricing authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government together with the finance authority.

**Article 37** Disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, marketing authorization holders of vaccines, and vaccine delivery units shall comply with the vaccine storage and transportation management standards to ensure vaccine quality.

Vaccines shall be kept in the specified temperature environment throughout the entire storage and transportation process. Cold chain storage and transportation shall meet requirements, and temperature shall be monitored and recorded regularly.

The vaccine storage and transportation management standards shall be jointly formulated by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council and the health authority of the State Council.

**Article 38** When selling vaccines, marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall provide copies or electronic files of the lot release certificate stamped with their seal; when selling imported vaccines, they shall also provide copies or electronic files of the import drug clearance certificate stamped with their seal.

When receiving or purchasing vaccines, disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units shall request the certification documents specified in the preceding paragraph and retain them for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiration date for future reference.

**Article 39** Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall, in accordance with regulations, establish true, accurate, and complete sales records and retain them for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiration date for future reference.

Disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, and vaccine delivery units shall, in accordance with regulations, establish true, accurate, and complete records of receipt, purchase, storage, delivery, and supply, and retain them for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiration date for future reference.

When receiving or purchasing vaccines, disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units shall request the temperature monitoring records for the entire transportation and storage process of the current batch and retain them for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiration date for future reference; if the temperature monitoring records for the entire transportation and storage process of the current batch cannot be provided or the temperature control does not meet requirements, they shall not receive or purchase the vaccines and shall immediately report to the drug regulatory authority and the health authority of the local people's government at or above the county level.

**Article 40** Disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units shall establish a regular vaccine inspection system. For vaccines with issues such as unidentifiable packaging, storage temperature not meeting requirements, or exceeding the expiration date, measures such as isolated storage and setting up warning signs shall be taken, and they shall be disposed of in accordance with the regulations of the drug regulatory authority, the health authority, and the ecological environment authority of the State Council. Disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units shall truthfully record the disposal situation, and the disposal records shall be retained for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiration date for future reference.

## Chapter V Vaccination

**Article 41** The health authority of the State Council formulates the national immunization program; the types of vaccines covered by the national immunization program shall be drafted by the health authority of the State Council together with the finance authority of the State Council, and published after approval by the State Council.

The health authority of the State Council establishes a national immunization program expert advisory committee and, together with the finance authority of the State Council, establishes a dynamic adjustment mechanism for the types of vaccines covered by the national immunization program.

When implementing the national immunization program, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may add types of vaccines covered by the national immunization program based on the disease prevention and control needs of their respective administrative regions, and shall report to the health authority of the State Council for filing and publish them.

**Article 42** The health authority of the State Council shall formulate and publish vaccination work standards to strengthen the standardized management of vaccination.

The health authority of the State Council shall formulate and publish the immunization schedule for vaccines covered by the national immunization program and the guiding principles for the use of vaccines not covered by the national immunization program.

The health authorities of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate vaccination plans based on the actual situation of their respective administrative regions and report them to the health authority of the State Council for filing.**Article 43** Disease prevention and control institutions at all levels shall, in accordance with their respective duties, carry out work related to vaccination, including publicity, training, technical guidance, monitoring, evaluation, epidemiological investigation, and emergency response.

**Article 44** A vaccination unit shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Hold a medical institution practice license;

(2) Have physicians, nurses, or village doctors who have participated in and passed the professional vaccination training organized by the health administrative department of the people's government at the county level;

(3) Have cold storage facilities, equipment, and cold storage management systems that comply with the regulations on vaccine storage and transportation management.

The health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall designate qualified medical institutions to be responsible for the vaccination work of the immunization program vaccines within their responsible areas. Qualified medical institutions may undertake the vaccination work of non-immunization program vaccines and shall report to the health administrative department that issued their medical institution practice license for the record.

Vaccination units shall strengthen internal management and shall comply with the vaccination work specifications, immunization procedures, vaccine use guidelines, and vaccination plans when carrying out vaccination work.

Disease prevention and control institutions at all levels shall strengthen technical guidance for the vaccination work of vaccination units and the management of vaccine use.

**Article 45** When administering a vaccination, medical personnel shall inform the recipient or their guardian of the type, effects, contraindications, adverse reactions, and precautions such as on-site observation of the vaccine being administered, inquire about the recipient's health status and whether there are any contraindications for vaccination, and truthfully record the information provided and the inquiries made. The recipient or their guardian shall truthfully provide information on the recipient's health status and vaccination contraindications. If there are contraindications preventing vaccination, the medical personnel shall provide medical advice to the recipient or their guardian and truthfully record the provision of such medical advice.

Before administering a vaccination, medical personnel shall, in accordance with the requirements of the vaccination work specifications, check the recipient's health status, verify vaccination contraindications, check the vaccination certificate, inspect the appearance, batch number, and expiry date of the vaccine and syringe, and verify the recipient's name, age, and the vaccine's name, specification, dosage, injection site, and route of administration, ensuring consistency between the recipient, the vaccination certificate, and the vaccine information. Vaccination may only be administered after confirmation that everything is correct.

Medical personnel shall administer vaccinations to recipients who meet the vaccination conditions. If a recipient experiences an adverse reaction during the on-site observation period, the medical personnel shall promptly take measures such as providing treatment in accordance with the requirements of the vaccination work specifications.

**Article 46** Medical personnel shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administrative department of the State Council, truthfully, accurately, and completely record vaccination information such as the vaccine type, marketing authorization holder, identification information of the smallest packaging unit, expiry date, vaccination time, the medical personnel administering the vaccination, and the recipient, ensuring that the vaccination information is traceable and queryable. Vaccination records shall be kept for no less than five years after the vaccine's expiry date for future reference.

**Article 47** The State implements a vaccination certificate system for children. Within one month after a child is born, their guardian shall apply for a vaccination certificate at the vaccination unit responsible for vaccination work in the child's place of residence or at the hospital where the child was born. The vaccination unit or the hospital of birth shall not refuse to issue the certificate. The guardian shall properly keep the vaccination certificate.

Vaccination is managed based on the place of residence. When a child leaves their original place of residence, the vaccination unit responsible for vaccination work in the new place of residence shall be responsible for administering vaccinations to the child.

The format of the vaccination certificate shall be stipulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

**Article 48** When a child enters a nursery, kindergarten, or school, the institution or school shall check the vaccination certificate. If it is found that the child has not been vaccinated with immunization program vaccines as required, the institution or school shall report this to the vaccination unit responsible for vaccination work in the child's place of residence or the location of the institution or school, and cooperate with the vaccination unit to urge the guardian to arrange for catch-up vaccination as required. Disease prevention and control institutions shall provide technical guidance to nurseries, kindergartens, and schools for checking vaccination certificates.

The measures for checking vaccination certificates for children entering nurseries, kindergartens, or schools shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council jointly with the education administrative department of the State Council.

**Article 49** Vaccination units shall not charge any fees for administering immunization program vaccines.

When administering non-immunization program vaccines, vaccination units may charge a vaccination service fee in addition to the vaccine cost. The charging standard for the vaccination service fee shall be formulated by the price administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government jointly with the financial department.

**Article 50** The health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level may, based on infectious disease monitoring and early warning information, and for the purpose of preventing and controlling the outbreak or epidemic of infectious diseases, decide to conduct mass vaccination within its administrative region, subject to the approval of the people's government at the same level and reporting to the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level for the record.

If mass vaccination is to be conducted nationwide or across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the decision shall be made by the health administrative department of the State Council.

The people's government at or above the county level or the health administrative department of the State Council that makes the decision for mass vaccination shall organize relevant departments to carry out work such as personnel training, publicity and education, and material mobilization.

No unit or individual shall conduct mass vaccination without authorization.

**Article 51** When an infectious disease outbreak or epidemic occurs, if the local people's government at or above the county level or its health administrative department needs to take emergency vaccination measures, it shall do so in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter VI Monitoring and Handling of Adverse Events Following Immunization

**Article 52** An adverse event following immunization (AEFI) refers to an adverse drug reaction caused by a qualified vaccine during or after standardized vaccination, resulting in damage to the recipient's body tissues, organs, or functions, where none of the relevant parties are at fault.

The following situations are not considered AEFIs:

(1) Common post-vaccination reactions caused by the inherent characteristics of the vaccine;

(2) Damage caused to the recipient due to vaccine quality issues;

(3) Damage caused to the recipient due to the vaccination unit's violation of vaccination work specifications, immunization procedures, vaccine use guidelines, or vaccination plans;

(4) The recipient happens to be in the incubation period or prodromal stage of a certain disease at the time of vaccination, and the disease occurs coincidentally after vaccination;

(5) The recipient has vaccination contraindications specified in the vaccine's package insert, and the recipient or their guardian fails to truthfully provide the recipient's health status and vaccination contraindications before vaccination, leading to an acute recurrence or worsening of the recipient's pre-existing disease after vaccination;

(6) Individual or group psychogenic reactions caused by psychological factors.

**Article 53** The State strengthens the monitoring of AEFIs. The AEFI monitoring plan shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council jointly with the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

**Article 54** Vaccination units, medical institutions, etc., upon discovering suspected AEFIs, shall report them to the disease prevention and control institution in accordance with regulations.

The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall establish a specialized agency, assign dedicated personnel, actively collect and track analyze suspected AEFIs, promptly take risk control measures, report suspected AEFIs to the disease prevention and control institution, and submit a quality analysis report to the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 55** For suspected AEFIs, the disease prevention and control institution shall promptly report them in accordance with regulations, organize investigations and diagnoses, and inform the recipient or their guardian of the investigation and diagnosis conclusions. If there is a dispute over the investigation or diagnosis conclusions, an appraisal may be applied for according to the appraisal measures formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

For suspected AEFIs that have a significant social impact, such as those resulting in the death or severe disability of the recipient, or mass suspected AEFIs, the health administrative department and the drug regulatory department of the people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall organize investigations and handling according to their respective duties.

**Article 56** The State implements a compensation system for AEFIs. If damage such as death, severe disability, or organ or tissue injury occurs to the recipient during or after the vaccination process, and it is determined to be an AEFI or cannot be ruled out, compensation shall be provided. The scope of compensation is managed through a catalog and is dynamically adjusted based on actual circumstances.

The compensation costs required for administering immunization program vaccines shall be arranged by the financial department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within the vaccination funds; the compensation costs required for administering non-immunization program vaccines shall be borne by the relevant vaccine marketing authorization holder. The State encourages compensation for AEFI recipients through various forms such as commercial insurance.

Compensation for AEFIs shall be timely, convenient, and reasonable. The scope, standards, and procedures for AEFI compensation shall be stipulated by the State Council, and the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate specific implementation measures.

## Chapter VII Post-Market Management of Vaccines

**Article 57** The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall establish and improve a vaccine lifecycle quality management system, formulate and implement a post-market risk management plan for the vaccine, conduct post-market studies on the vaccine, and further confirm the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of the vaccine.

For vaccines for which further research requirements were proposed at the time of approving the vaccine registration application, the vaccine marketing authorization holder shall complete the research within the specified period; if the research is not completed within the time limit or it cannot be proven that the benefits outweigh the risks, the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall handle the matter according to law, up to and including revoking the drug registration certificate for the vaccine.

**Article 58** The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall conduct quality tracking analysis of the vaccine, continuously improve quality control standards, refine production processes, and enhance the stability of the production process.

If changes occur in the production process, production site, key equipment, etc., an assessment and validation shall be conducted, and the matter shall be filed or reported in accordance with the regulations of the drug regulatory department of the State Council on change management; if the changes may affect the safety, efficacy, or quality controllability of the vaccine, approval from the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall be obtained.

**Article 59** The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall continuously update the package insert and label based on post-market studies, AEFI situations, etc., and apply for approval or filing in accordance with regulations.

The drug regulatory department of the State Council shall promptly publish the updated content of the vaccine package insert and label on its website.

**Article 60** The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall establish a system for vaccine quality review analysis and risk reporting, and shall truthfully report to the drug regulatory department of the State Council each year on the vaccine's production and distribution, post-market studies, risk management, etc., in accordance with regulations.

**Article 61** The drug regulatory department of the State Council may, based on actual circumstances, order the vaccine marketing authorization holder to conduct a post-market evaluation or directly organize a post-market evaluation.

For vaccines that cause severe AEFIs or harm human health for other reasons, the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall revoke the drug registration certificate for the vaccine.

**Article 62** The drug regulatory department of the State Council may, based on disease prevention and control needs and the development of the vaccine industry, organize post-market evaluations of vaccine types. If it is found that the product design, production process, safety, efficacy, or quality controllability of a certain vaccine type is significantly inferior to other vaccine types used for preventing or controlling the same disease, the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall revoke the drug registration certificates for all vaccines of that type and abolish the corresponding national drug standards.

## Chapter VIII Safeguard Measures

**Article 63** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the funds required for vaccine safety work, the purchase of immunization program vaccines, vaccination work, and information technology construction into their government budgets to ensure the implementation of the immunization program system.

People's governments at the county level shall provide subsidies to village doctors and other primary-level medical personnel engaged in vaccination work in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The State shall provide support for vaccination work in economically underdeveloped areas as needed. The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the people's governments of cities divided into districts, shall provide necessary financial subsidies to the people's governments at the county level in economically underdeveloped areas for carrying out work related to vaccination.

**Article 64** The people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, based on the epidemic trend of infectious diseases in its administrative region and within the scope of infectious disease prevention and control projects determined by the health administrative department of the State Council, determine projects related to vaccination in its administrative region and ensure the implementation of these projects.

**Article 65** The health administrative department of the State Council shall, based on the national immunization program vaccine usage plans of each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government, provide demand information for national immunization program vaccines to vaccine marketing authorization holders, who shall reasonably arrange production based on the vaccine demand information.

When there is a risk of vaccine supply shortage, the health administrative department of the State Council and the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall make recommendations, and the competent industry and information technology department of the State Council and the financial department of the State Council shall take effective measures to ensure vaccine production and supply.

The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall organize production according to law to ensure vaccine supply; if the vaccine marketing authorization holder ceases vaccine production, it shall promptly report to the drug regulatory department of the State Council or the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 66** The State includes vaccines in strategic material reserves, implementing a two-tier reserve system at the central and provincial levels.

The competent industry and information technology department of the State Council and the financial department of the State Council, together with the health administrative department of the State Council, the public security department, the market supervision and management department, and the drug regulatory department, shall, based on the needs of disease prevention, control, and public health emergency preparedness, strengthen the management of the production capacity and products of reserve vaccines and establish a dynamic adjustment mechanism.

**Article 67** Funds allocated by governments at all levels for vaccination purposes shall be used exclusively for their designated purposes, and no unit or individual may misappropriate or divert them.

Relevant units and individuals using vaccination funds shall accept audit supervision by audit institutions in accordance with the law.

**Article 68** The State implements a compulsory vaccine liability insurance system.

The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall purchase compulsory vaccine liability insurance in accordance with regulations. If damage is caused to a recipient due to a vaccine quality issue, the insurance company shall pay compensation within the liability limit covered by the insurance.

The specific implementation measures for the compulsory vaccine liability insurance system shall be formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council jointly with the health administrative department of the State Council and the insurance regulatory authority.

**Article 69** When an infectious disease outbreak or epidemic occurs, the relevant vaccine marketing authorization holder shall promptly produce and supply vaccines for the prevention and control of the infectious disease. Transportation units shall prioritize the transportation of vaccines for the prevention and control of infectious diseases. The people's government at or above the county level and its relevant departments shall do a good job in organization, coordination, and support.

## Chapter IX Supervision and Administration

**Article 70** The drug regulatory department and the health administrative department shall, in accordance with their respective duties, supervise and manage the entire process of vaccine research and development, production, distribution, and vaccination, and supervise vaccine marketing authorization holders, disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, etc., to fulfill their obligations according to law.

The drug regulatory department shall, according to law, supervise and inspect the quality of vaccines during research and development, production, storage, transportation, and vaccination. The health administrative department shall, according to law, supervise and inspect the implementation of the immunization program system and vaccination activities.

The drug regulatory department shall strengthen on-site inspections of vaccine marketing authorization holders; when necessary, it may conduct extended inspections of units and individuals that provide products or services for vaccine research and development, production, distribution, etc.; relevant units and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or conceal information.

**Article 71** The State builds a professional and specialized team of drug inspectors at the central and provincial levels to strengthen the supervision and inspection of vaccines.

The drug regulatory department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall assign inspectors to reside at vaccine marketing authorization holders. The inspectors are responsible for supervising and inspecting the implementation of Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) for drugs, collecting clues regarding vaccine quality risks and violations of laws and regulations, reporting situations and making recommendations to the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and being responsible for their actions during the period of assignment.

**Article 72** If there is a potential safety hazard in vaccine quality management and the vaccine marketing authorization holder, etc., fails to take timely measures to eliminate it, the drug regulatory department may take measures such as accountability interviews and ordering rectification within a time limit.

If there is a serious violation of relevant drug quality management standards, the drug regulatory department shall order the suspension of vaccine production, sales, and distribution, and immediate rectification; after the rectification is completed and the drug regulatory department inspects and confirms compliance, production, sales, and distribution may be resumed.

The drug regulatory department shall establish a credit record system for vaccine marketing authorization holders and their relevant personnel, incorporate it into the national credit information sharing platform, publicly disclose their serious dishonest information in accordance with regulations, and implement joint punishment.
**Article 73** If a vaccine has or is suspected of having a quality problem, the vaccine marketing authorization holder, disease prevention and control institution, and vaccination unit shall immediately stop sales, distribution, and use, and if necessary, immediately stop production, and report to the drug regulatory department and the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with regulations. The health administrative department shall immediately organize disease prevention and control institutions and vaccination units to take necessary emergency response measures, and simultaneously report to the health administrative department of the higher-level people's government. The drug regulatory department shall take measures such as sealing up and seizing according to law. For vaccines that have already been sold, the vaccine marketing authorization holder shall promptly notify the relevant disease prevention and control institutions, vaccine distribution units, and vaccination units, recall them in accordance with regulations, and truthfully record the recall and notification situation. The disease prevention and control institutions, vaccine distribution units, and vaccination units shall cooperate.

If production, sales, distribution, use, or recall of vaccines is not stopped in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the drug regulatory department and the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective duties, order the cessation of production, sales, distribution, use, or recall of the vaccines.

Vaccine marketing authorization holders, disease prevention and control institutions, and vaccination units, upon discovering vaccines with existing or suspected quality problems, shall not conceal, misreport, delay reporting, omit reporting, or conceal, forge, or destroy relevant evidence.

**Article 74** The vaccine marketing authorization holder shall establish an information disclosure system and promptly disclose on its website, in accordance with regulations, information such as vaccine product information, package inserts and labels, the implementation status of relevant drug quality management standards, batch release status, recall status, inspection and penalty status, and the status of purchasing compulsory vaccine liability insurance.

**Article 75** The drug regulatory department of the State Council, together with the health administrative department of the State Council, shall establish an information sharing mechanism for vaccine quality, vaccination, etc.

The drug regulatory department and the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, in accordance with the principles of science, objectivity, timeliness, and openness, organize vaccine marketing authorization holders, disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, news media, scientific research units, etc., to exchange and communicate information on vaccine quality, vaccination, etc.**Article 76** The state implements a unified system for the disclosure of vaccine safety information.

Vaccine safety risk warnings, information on major vaccine safety incidents and their investigation and handling, and other vaccine safety information that the State Council determines should be disclosed uniformly, shall be disclosed by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments. Reports on the national situation of adverse events following immunization shall be disclosed uniformly by the health administrative department of the State Council in conjunction with the drug regulatory authority of the State Council. The above information shall not be disclosed without authorization. The disclosure of major vaccine safety information shall be timely, accurate, and comprehensive, and shall be subject to scientific assessment and necessary explanations as required.

Where the drug regulatory authority of a local people's government at or above the county level discovers vaccine safety information that may mislead the public or public opinion, it shall immediately verify and analyze the information jointly with the health administrative department, other relevant departments, professional institutions, and relevant marketing authorization holders of vaccines, and promptly disclose the results.

No unit or individual shall fabricate or disseminate false vaccine safety information.

**Article 77** Any unit or individual has the right to obtain vaccine information in accordance with the law and to put forward opinions and suggestions on vaccine supervision and management.

Any unit or individual has the right to report vaccine violations to the health administrative department, the drug regulatory authority, and other relevant departments, and has the right to report to the people's government at the same level or the next higher level, its relevant departments, or supervisory authorities, regarding the failure of the health administrative department, the drug regulatory authority, and other relevant departments and their staff to perform their supervision and management duties according to law. The relevant departments and authorities shall promptly verify and handle the reports; for verified reports, rewards shall be given to the reporting person in accordance with regulations; if the reporting person reports serious violations committed by their own unit and the report is verified, a substantial reward shall be given.

**Article 78** People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate emergency plans for vaccine safety incidents, stipulating the classification of vaccine safety incidents, the command system for handling and its responsibilities, the prevention and early warning mechanism, the handling procedures, and the emergency support measures.

Marketing authorization holders of vaccines shall formulate plans for handling vaccine safety incidents, regularly inspect the implementation of various preventive measures, and promptly eliminate safety hazards.

When a vaccine safety incident occurs, the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine shall immediately report to the drug regulatory authority of the State Council or the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; disease prevention and control institutions, vaccination units, and medical institutions shall immediately report to the health administrative department and the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level. The drug regulatory authority shall, in conjunction with the health administrative department, establish a command body for handling the vaccine safety incident in accordance with the provisions of the emergency plan, carry out medical treatment, risk control, investigation and handling, information release, explanations, and other work, and properly handle follow-up matters such as catch-up vaccination. The cost of catch-up vaccination caused by a vaccine safety incident due to quality issues shall be borne by the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine.

Relevant units and individuals shall not conceal, make false reports, delay reporting, or omit reporting vaccine safety incidents, nor shall they conceal, falsify, or destroy relevant evidence.

## Chapter 10: Legal Liability

**Article 79** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be subject to heavier criminal liability according to law.

**Article 80** Where vaccines produced or sold are counterfeit drugs, the drug regulatory authority at or above the provincial level shall confiscate the illegal income, the illegally produced or sold vaccines, and the raw materials, excipients, packaging materials, equipment, and other items specifically used for the illegal production of vaccines, order the suspension of production or business for rectification, revoke the drug registration certificate, and even revoke the drug manufacturing license, etc., and impose a fine of not less than fifteen times but not more than fifty times the value of the illegally produced or sold vaccines; if the value is less than 500,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 500,000 yuan.

Where vaccines produced or sold are substandard drugs, the drug regulatory authority at or above the provincial level shall confiscate the illegal income, the illegally produced or sold vaccines, and the raw materials, excipients, packaging materials, equipment, and other items specifically used for the illegal production of vaccines, order the suspension of production or business for rectification, and impose a fine of not less than ten times but not more than thirty times the value of the illegally produced or sold vaccines; if the value is less than 500,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the drug registration certificate shall be revoked, and even the drug manufacturing license, etc., shall be revoked.

Where the vaccines produced or sold are counterfeit drugs, or where the vaccines produced or sold are substandard drugs and the circumstances are serious, the drug regulatory authority at or above the provincial level shall confiscate the income obtained by the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, key position personnel, and other responsible persons from their unit during the period when the illegal act occurred, impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the confiscated income, permanently prohibit them from engaging in drug production and business activities, and the public security authority shall impose detention for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

**Article 81** Under any of the following circumstances, the drug regulatory authority at or above the provincial level shall confiscate the illegal income, the illegally produced or sold vaccines, and the raw materials, excipients, packaging materials, equipment, and other items specifically used for the illegal production of vaccines, order the suspension of production or business for rectification, and impose a fine of not less than fifteen times but not more than fifty times the value of the illegally produced or sold vaccines; if the value is less than 500,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant drug approval certificates shall be revoked, and even the drug manufacturing license, etc., shall be revoked; for the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, key position personnel, and other responsible persons, the income obtained from their unit during the period when the illegal act occurred shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 50% but not more than ten times the confiscated income shall be imposed, and they shall be prohibited from engaging in drug production and business activities for a period of ten years up to life; the public security authority shall impose detention for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days:

(1) Providing false data, materials, or samples, or engaging in other fraudulent acts when applying for vaccine clinical trials, registration, or lot release;
(2) Falsifying production or inspection records or changing product batch numbers;
(3) Units or individuals other than disease prevention and control institutions supplying vaccines to vaccination units;
(4) Entrusting the production of vaccines without approval;
(5) Changes in production processes, production sites, key equipment, etc., that require approval according to regulations but are made without approval;
(6) Updating vaccine package inserts or labels that require approval according to regulations but are made without approval.

**Article 82** Unless otherwise provided in this Law, if a marketing authorization holder of a vaccine or another unit violates the relevant drug quality management standards, the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 3 million yuan shall be imposed, and the unit shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and even the relevant drug approval certificates, drug manufacturing license, etc., shall be revoked; for the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, key position personnel, and other responsible persons, the income obtained from their unit during the period when the illegal act occurred shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the confiscated income shall be imposed, and they shall be prohibited from engaging in drug production and business activities for a period of ten years up to life.

**Article 83** If a marketing authorization holder of a vaccine violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the drug regulatory authority at or above the provincial level shall order correction and give a warning; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the unit shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to establish a vaccine electronic traceability system as required;
(2) The legal representative, principal responsible person, production management responsible person, quality management responsible person, qualified person, and other key position personnel do not meet the required conditions, or failing to provide them with training and assessment as required;
(3) Failing to report or file as required;
(4) Failing to conduct post-marketing studies as required, or failing to establish institutions or assign personnel to actively collect and track analyze suspected adverse events following immunization;
(5) Failing to take out compulsory liability insurance for vaccines as required;
(6) Failing to establish an information disclosure system as required.

**Article 84** If a lot release agency violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and give a warning, and impose a warning up to demotion on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law:

(1) Failing to conduct review and testing as required;
(2) Failing to promptly publish the lot release results of marketed vaccines;
(3) Failing to verify as required;
(4) Discovering a major quality risk in a vaccine and failing to report it as required.

If a lot release agency violates the provisions of this Law by failing to issue a lot release certificate or a notice of non-release as required, the drug regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and give a warning, and impose demotion or removal from office on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law; if the circumstances are serious, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law.

**Article 85** If a disease prevention and control institution, vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of a vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit violates the cold chain storage and transportation requirements of the vaccine storage and transportation management standards, the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, give a warning, destroy the illegally stored or transported vaccines, and confiscate the illegal income; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan shall be imposed on the vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of the vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than thirty times the value of the illegally stored or transported vaccines shall be imposed on the vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of the vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit; if the value is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan; the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine or vaccine distribution unit shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and even the relevant drug approval certificates, drug manufacturing license, etc., shall be revoked; the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, key position personnel, and other responsible persons of the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine or vaccine distribution unit shall be punished in accordance with Article 82 of this Law.

If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit commits the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose a warning up to removal from office on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law, and order the responsible medical personnel to suspend their practice for not less than one year but not more than eighteen months; if serious consequences are caused, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law, and the vaccination qualification of the vaccination unit may be revoked, and the practice certificate of the responsible medical personnel shall be revoked by the original issuing department.

**Article 86** If a disease prevention and control institution, vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of a vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit commits acts violating the vaccine storage and transportation management standards other than those specified in Article 85 of this Law, the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, give a warning, and confiscate the illegal income; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed on the vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of the vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than three times but not more than ten times the value of the illegally stored or transported vaccines shall be imposed on the vaccination unit, marketing authorization holder of the vaccine, or vaccine distribution unit; if the value is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan.

If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit commits the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level may impose a warning up to removal from office on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law, and order the responsible medical personnel to suspend their practice for not less than six months but not more than one year; if serious consequences are caused, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law, and the practice certificate of the responsible medical personnel shall be revoked by the original issuing department.

**Article 87** If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, give a warning, and confiscate the illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, a warning up to removal from office shall be imposed on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law, and the responsible medical personnel shall be ordered to suspend their practice for not less than one year but not more than eighteen months; if serious consequences are caused, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law, and the practice certificate of the responsible medical personnel shall be revoked by the original issuing department:

(1) Failing to supply, receive, or procure vaccines as required;
(2) Administering vaccines without complying with the vaccination work standards, immunization schedules, vaccine use guidelines, or vaccination protocols;
(3) Conducting mass vaccination without authorization.

**Article 88** If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; if the circumstances are serious, a warning up to removal from office shall be imposed on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law, and the responsible medical personnel shall be ordered to suspend their practice for not less than six months but not more than one year; if serious consequences are caused, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law, and the practice certificate of the responsible medical personnel shall be revoked by the original issuing department:

(1) Failing to provide traceability information as required;
(2) Failing to request and retain relevant certification documents and temperature monitoring records as required when receiving or purchasing vaccines;
(3) Failing to establish and maintain records of vaccine receipt, procurement, storage, distribution, supply, administration, and disposal as required;
(4) Failing to inform or inquire the recipient or their guardian about relevant circumstances as required.

**Article 89** If a disease prevention and control institution, vaccination unit, or medical institution fails to report suspected adverse events following immunization, vaccine safety incidents, etc., as required, or fails to organize investigations or diagnoses of suspected adverse events following immunization as required, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the vaccination unit or medical institution, and a warning up to removal from office shall be imposed on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons of the disease prevention and control institution, vaccination unit, or medical institution according to law; if serious consequences are caused, the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons shall be dismissed according to law, and the practice certificate of the responsible medical personnel shall be revoked by the original issuing department.

**Article 90** If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit collects fees in violation of the provisions of this Law, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall supervise the return of the illegally collected fees to the original paying unit or individual, and the market regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties according to law.

**Article 91** If a unit, without being designated by the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, engages in the administration of vaccines included in the national immunization program, or engages in the administration of vaccines not included in the national immunization program without meeting the conditions or without filing, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, give a warning, confiscate the illegal income and the illegally held vaccines, order the suspension of business for rectification, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan, and impose sanctions on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law.

If a unit or individual other than a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit conducts mass vaccination without authorization in violation of the provisions of this Law, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, confiscate the illegal income and the illegally held vaccines, and impose a fine of not less than ten times but not more than thirty times the value of the illegally held vaccines; if the value is less than 50,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 50,000 yuan.

**Article 92** If a guardian fails to ensure that a school-age child receives vaccines included in the national immunization program on time according to law, the health administrative department of the people's government at the county level shall criticize and educate the guardian and order correction.

If a nursery, kindergarten, or school fails to check the vaccination certificate as required when a child enters the nursery, kindergarten, or school, or fails to report to the vaccination unit after discovering a child who has not been vaccinated as required, the education administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, give a warning, and impose sanctions on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law.

**Article 93** Fabricating or disseminating false vaccine safety information, or creating disturbances at a vaccination unit, which constitutes a violation of public security administration, shall be subject to public security administration penalties by the public security authority according to law.

If media such as newspapers, periodicals, radio, television, or websites fabricate or disseminate false vaccine safety information, the relevant departments shall impose penalties according to law, and impose sanctions on the principal responsible person, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible persons according to law.

**Article 94** If a local people's government at or above the county level has any of the following circumstances in vaccine supervision and management, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible persons shall be demoted or removed from office according to law; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be dismissed according to law; if serious consequences are caused, its principal responsible person shall resign to assume responsibility:

(1) Failing to perform duties effectively, causing serious adverse effects or significant losses;
(2) Concealing, making false reports, delaying reporting, or omitting reporting of vaccine safety incidents;
(3) Interfering with or obstructing the investigation of vaccine violations or vaccine safety incidents;
(4) The occurrence of an extremely major vaccine safety incident, or consecutive major vaccine safety incidents, within its administrative region.

**Article 95** If the drug regulatory authority, health administrative department, or other relevant departments have any of the following circumstances in vaccine supervision and management, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible persons shall be demoted or removed from office according to law; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be dismissed according to law; if serious consequences are caused, their principal responsible person shall resign to assume responsibility:

(1) Failing to perform supervision and inspection duties, or failing to promptly investigate and deal with discovered illegal acts;
(2) Conducting mass vaccination without authorization;
(3) Concealing, making false reports, delaying reporting, or omitting reporting of vaccine safety incidents;
(4) Interfering with or obstructing the investigation of vaccine violations or vaccine safety incidents;
(5) Disclosing the information of a whistleblower;
(6) Receiving a report related to a suspected adverse event following immunization and failing to organize an investigation or handling as required;
(7) Other acts of failing to perform vaccine supervision and management duties, causing serious adverse effects or significant losses.

**Article 96** If a recipient suffers harm due to a vaccine quality issue, the marketing authorization holder of the vaccine shall bear compensation liability according to law.

If a disease prevention and control institution or vaccination unit causes harm to a recipient due to violating the vaccination work standards, immunization schedules, vaccine use guidelines, or vaccination protocols, it shall bear compensation liability according to law.

## Chapter 11: Supplementary ProvisionsArticle 97 For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

Immunization program vaccines refer to vaccines that residents are required to receive in accordance with government regulations, including vaccines specified in the national immunization program, vaccines added by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government when implementing the national immunization program, and vaccines used for emergency vaccination or mass preventive vaccination organized by the people's governments at or above the county level or their health authorities.

Non-immunization program vaccines refer to other vaccines that residents voluntarily receive.

Vaccine marketing authorization holders refer to enterprises that have lawfully obtained a drug registration certificate for vaccines and a drug manufacturing license.

Article 98 The State encourages vaccine manufacturers to produce and export vaccines in accordance with international procurement requirements.

Exported vaccines shall meet the standards of the importing country (region) or the requirements of the contract.

Article 99 The procurement of vaccines for entry-exit preventive vaccination and the vaccines required therefor shall be separately regulated by the border health and quarantine authorities in consultation with the finance department of the State Council.

Article 100 This Law shall come into effect on December 1, 2019.
