# Civil Air Defense Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 29, 1996

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to effectively organize civil air defense, protect the safety of people's lives and property, and ensure the smooth progress of socialist modernization.

Article 2 Civil air defense is an integral part of national defense. The State, based on the needs of national defense, mobilizes and organizes the masses to take protective measures to prevent and mitigate the hazards of air raids.

Civil air defense implements the policy of long-term preparation, prioritized construction, and peacetime-wartime integration, adhering to the principles of coordinated development with economic construction and integration with urban construction.

Article 3 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate civil air defense construction into national economic and social development plans.

Article 4 Civil air defense funds shall be borne jointly by the State and society.

The funds borne by the central government for civil air defense shall be included in the central budget; the funds borne by local people's governments at or above the county level for civil air defense shall be included in their respective local budgets.

Relevant entities shall bear civil air defense costs in accordance with State regulations.

Article 5 The State grants preferential treatment to the construction of civil air defense facilities in accordance with relevant regulations.

The State encourages and supports enterprises, institutions, social organizations, and individuals to invest in the construction of civil air defense projects through various channels; civil air defense projects shall be used and managed by the investors during peacetime, with the proceeds belonging to the investors.

Article 6 The State Council and the Central Military Commission lead the national civil air defense work.

Military regions, authorized by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, lead civil air defense work within their respective regions.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and the military organs at the same level lead civil air defense work within their respective administrative areas.

Article 7 The national civil air defense authority manages the national civil air defense work.

The civil air defense authorities of military regions manage civil air defense work within their respective regions.

The civil air defense authorities of local people's governments at or above the county level manage civil air defense work within their respective administrative areas.

The civil air defense authority of central State organs manages the civil air defense work of central State organs.

The establishment, functions, and tasks of civil air defense authorities shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

The relevant departments of planning, programming, and construction of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for related civil air defense work within their respective functions.

Article 8 All organizations and individuals have the right to receive civil air defense protection and must fulfill their civil air defense obligations in accordance with the law.

Article 9 The State protects civil air defense facilities from infringement. No organization or individual is allowed to damage or occupy civil air defense facilities.

Article 10 People's governments at or above the county level and military organs shall reward organizations or individuals that have made outstanding achievements in civil air defense work.

## Chapter II: Protection Priorities

Article 11 Cities are the focus of civil air defense. The State implements classified protection for cities.

The protection categories and standards for cities shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 12 City people's governments shall formulate air raid defense plans and implementation plans, and may organize exercises when necessary.

Article 13 City people's governments shall formulate plans for the construction of civil air defense projects and incorporate them into the overall urban plan.

Article 14 The construction of urban underground transportation arteries and other underground projects shall take into account the needs of civil air defense.

Article 15 Projects for storing grain, medicine, oil, and other essential materials for wartime shall be built underground or in other concealed locations.

Article 16 For important economic targets, relevant departments must take effective protective measures and formulate emergency rescue and repair plans.

The important economic targets referred to in the preceding paragraph include important industrial and mining enterprises, scientific research bases, transportation hubs, communication hubs, bridges, reservoirs, warehouses, power stations, etc.

Article 17 Civil air defense authorities shall, in accordance with regulations, supervise and inspect the civil air defense construction of cities and economic targets. The units being inspected shall truthfully provide information and necessary materials.

## Chapter III: Civil Air Defense Projects

Article 18 Civil air defense projects include underground protective buildings constructed separately to ensure the shelter of personnel and materials, civil air defense command, medical rescue, etc., during wartime, as well as basements constructed in conjunction with above-ground buildings that can be used for air defense during wartime.

Article 19 The State implements classified guidance for the construction of civil air defense projects based on different protection requirements.

The State, based on the needs of national defense construction and in conjunction with urban construction and economic development levels, formulates plans for the construction of civil air defense projects.

Article 20 The construction of civil air defense projects shall, while ensuring wartime operational effectiveness, facilitate peacetime economic construction, the production and daily life of the masses, and the development and utilization of the projects.

Article 21 Civil air defense command projects, public personnel shelter projects, and evacuation trunk road projects shall be organized and constructed by civil air defense authorities; specialized projects such as medical rescue and material storage shall be organized and constructed by other relevant departments.

Relevant entities are responsible for constructing personnel and material shelter projects for their own units.

Article 22 New civil buildings in cities shall, in accordance with State regulations, construct basements that can be used for air defense during wartime.

Article 23 The design, construction, and quality of civil air defense project construction must meet the protection and quality standards prescribed by the State.

The finalization and production of specialized equipment for civil air defense projects must meet the standards prescribed by the State.

Article 24 Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall ensure the land needed for civil air defense project construction in accordance with the law; they shall provide necessary conditions for the construction of facilities connecting civil air defense projects to urban roads, power supply, heating, water supply, drainage, communication, and other systems.

Article 25 Civil air defense authorities shall supervise and inspect the maintenance and management of civil air defense projects.

The maintenance and management of public civil air defense projects shall be the responsibility of civil air defense authorities.

Relevant entities shall, in accordance with State regulations, maintain and manage civil air defense projects that have been constructed or are in use, keeping them in good working condition.

Article 26 The State encourages the peacetime use of civil air defense projects to serve economic construction and people's lives. Peacetime use of civil air defense projects shall not affect their air defense effectiveness.

Article 27 No organization or individual may carry out operations that affect the use of civil air defense projects or reduce their protective capability, discharge wastewater or waste gas into civil air defense projects, dump waste into them, or produce or store explosive, highly toxic, flammable, radioactive, or corrosive items within civil air defense projects.

Article 28 No organization or individual may dismantle civil air defense projects as specified in Article 21 of this Law without authorization; if dismantling is truly necessary, approval must be obtained from the civil air defense authority, and the dismantling unit shall be responsible for rebuilding or providing compensation.

## Chapter IV: Communication and Alarms

Article 29 The State ensures the smooth operation of civil air defense communication and alarms to rapidly and accurately transmit and issue air defense alarm signals and effectively organize and command civil air defense.

Article 30 The national civil air defense authority is responsible for formulating the national plan for civil air defense communication and alarm construction and organizing the construction and management of the national civil air defense communication and alarm network.

The civil air defense authorities of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for formulating plans for civil air defense communication and alarm construction within their respective administrative areas and organizing the construction and management of the civil air defense communication and alarm network within their respective administrative areas.

Article 31 The postal and telecommunications departments, military communication departments, and civil air defense authorities shall ensure civil air defense communication in accordance with the tasks prescribed by the State and the civil air defense communication and alarm construction plan.

Article 32 The circuits and frequencies required by civil air defense authorities for constructing communication and alarm networks shall be ensured by the postal and telecommunications departments, military communication departments, and radio regulatory agencies; when installing civil air defense communication and alarm facilities, relevant entities or individuals shall provide convenient conditions and shall not obstruct them.

No organization or individual may occupy or confuse the dedicated frequencies used by the State for civil air defense communication and the audio signals of air defense alarms.

Article 33 Communication, broadcasting, and television systems must give priority to transmitting and issuing air defense alarm signals during wartime.

Article 34 Relevant military departments shall provide air intelligence to civil air defense authorities and assist in training relevant professional personnel.

Article 35 Civil air defense communication and alarm facilities must be maintained in good working condition.

Civil air defense alarm facilities installed in relevant entities shall be maintained and managed by those entities and shall not be dismantled without authorization.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may organize trial air defense alarms as needed; they shall issue a public notice five days before the trial.

Article 36 Civil air defense communication and alarm facilities shall serve emergency rescue and disaster relief during peacetime.

## Chapter V: Evacuation

Article 37 Civil air defense evacuation shall be uniformly organized by people's governments at or above the county level.

Civil air defense evacuation must be carried out in accordance with orders issued by the State; no organization may act without authorization.

Article 38 Urban civil air defense evacuation plans shall be formulated by people's governments at or above the county level, organizing relevant departments as needed.

The predetermined evacuation areas, if within the same administrative area, shall be determined by the people's government at that level; if they cross administrative areas, they shall be determined by the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 39 People's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments and entities to make preparations for the resettlement of evacuated urban populations and the storage, transportation, and supply of materials.

Article 40 When it is necessary to evacuate the rural population, the local people's government shall organize the implementation in accordance with the principle of proximity.

## Chapter VI: Civil Air Defense Organizations of the Masses

Article 41 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of civil air defense, organize relevant departments to establish civil air defense organizations of the masses.

Civil air defense organizations of the masses shall undertake tasks such as emergency rescue and repair, medical rescue, firefighting and fire prevention, epidemic prevention and sterilization, decontamination, ensuring communication links, rescuing personnel and transporting materials, and maintaining social order during wartime; during peacetime, they shall assist flood control, earthquake prevention, and other departments in undertaking emergency rescue and disaster relief tasks.

Article 42 Civil air defense organizations of the masses shall be formed by the following departments:

(1) Urban construction, public utilities, and electric power departments form emergency rescue and repair teams;
(2) Health and pharmaceutical departments form medical rescue teams;
(3) Public security departments form fire brigades and security teams;
(4) Health, chemical industry, and environmental protection departments form chemical defense and epidemic prevention teams;
(5) Postal and telecommunications departments form communication teams;
(6) Transportation departments form transport teams.

Red Cross organizations shall carry out rescue work in accordance with the law.

Article 43 The equipment, materials, and funds required by civil air defense organizations of the masses shall be provided by civil air defense authorities and the forming units.

Article 44 Civil air defense organizations of the masses shall conduct professional training in accordance with the training outlines and plans formulated by civil air defense authorities.

## Chapter VII: Civil Air Defense Education

Article 45 The State carries out civil air defense education to enhance citizens' awareness of national defense and enable them to master the basic knowledge and skills of civil air defense.

Article 46 The national civil air defense authority is responsible for organizing the formulation of civil air defense education plans and prescribing the content of education.

Civil air defense education for students in school shall be organized and implemented by education authorities and civil air defense authorities at all levels.

Civil air defense education for personnel of State organs, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall be organized and implemented by their respective units; civil air defense education for other personnel shall be organized and implemented by urban and rural grassroots people's governments.

Article 47 Relevant departments such as news, publishing, broadcasting, film, television, and culture shall assist in carrying out civil air defense education.

## Chapter VIII: Legal Liability

Article 48 If a new civil building in a city fails to construct a basement that can be used for air defense during wartime in violation of State regulations, the civil air defense authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall issue a warning to the party concerned, order them to construct it within a specified time limit, and may impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 49 Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be warned by the civil air defense authority of the people's government at or above the county level and ordered to correct the illegal act within a specified time limit; an individual may be fined not more than 5,000 yuan, and a unit may be fined between 10,000 and 50,000 yuan; if losses are caused, compensation shall be paid in accordance with the law:

(1) Occupying a civil air defense project;
(2) Constructing a civil air defense project that does not meet the protection and quality standards prescribed by the State;
(3) Changing the main structure of a civil air defense project, dismantling equipment or facilities of a civil air defense project, or using other methods to harm the safety and operational effectiveness of a civil air defense project in violation of State regulations;
(4) Refusing to rebuild a civil air defense project after dismantling it;
(5) Occupying dedicated frequencies for civil air defense communication, using audio signals identical to air defense alarms, or dismantling civil air defense communication or alarm equipment or facilities without authorization;
(6) Obstructing the installation of civil air defense communication or alarm facilities and refusing to correct the behavior;
(7) Discharging wastewater or waste gas into a civil air defense project or dumping waste into it.

Article 50 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, intentionally damages civil air defense facilities or produces or stores explosive, highly toxic, flammable, radioactive, or other dangerous goods within a civil air defense project, if the act does not constitute a crime, shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 51 If staff members of civil air defense authorities, through dereliction of duty, abuse of power, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or other illegal or derelict acts, constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate implementation measures in accordance with this Law.

Article 53 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1997.
