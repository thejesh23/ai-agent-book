# Air Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 5, 1987

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Air Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1995

First Revision at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 29, 2000

Second Revision at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 29, 2015

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting and improving the environment, preventing and controlling air pollution, safeguarding public health, promoting ecological progress, and facilitating the sustainable development of the economy and society.

Article 2 The prevention and control of air pollution shall aim to improve air quality, adhere to source control, prioritize planning, transform the economic development model, optimize industrial structure and layout, and adjust the energy structure.

The prevention and control of air pollution shall strengthen comprehensive measures against air pollution from coal combustion, industry, motor vehicles and vessels, fugitive dust, and agriculture, promote regional joint prevention and control of air pollution, and implement coordinated control of air pollutants such as particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, volatile organic compounds, ammonia, and greenhouse gases.

Article 3 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate air pollution prevention and control work into their national economic and social development plans and increase financial investment in air pollution prevention and control.

Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for the air quality within their respective administrative regions, formulate plans, and adopt measures to control or gradually reduce the discharge of air pollutants, so as to achieve the prescribed air quality standards and gradually improve air quality.

Article 4 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments under the State Council and in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, assess the completion of air quality improvement targets and key tasks for air pollution prevention and control in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate assessment methods to assess the completion of local air quality improvement targets and key tasks for air pollution prevention and control within their administrative regions. The assessment results shall be made public.

Article 5 The competent departments of ecological environment under the people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise unified supervision and management over the prevention and control of air pollution.

Other relevant departments under the people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise supervision and management over the prevention and control of air pollution within their respective functions and duties.

Article 6 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research on air pollution prevention and control, conducts analysis of the sources and changing trends of air pollution, promotes the application of advanced and suitable technologies and equipment for air pollution prevention and control, facilitates the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, and leverages the supporting role of science and technology in air pollution prevention and control.

Article 7 Enterprises, public institutions, and other producers and business operators shall take effective measures to prevent and reduce air pollution and shall bear liability for the damage caused in accordance with the law.

Citizens shall enhance their awareness of air environmental protection, adopt low-carbon and frugal lifestyles, and consciously fulfill their obligations for air environmental protection.

## Chapter II Air Quality Standards and Plans for Achieving Standards Within a Time Limit

Article 8 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate air quality standards with the aim of safeguarding public health and protecting the ecological environment, ensuring they are compatible with economic and social development, and are scientific and reasonable.

Article 9 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate air pollutant emission standards based on the air quality standards and the national economic and technical conditions.

Article 10 When formulating air quality standards and air pollutant emission standards, experts shall be organized for review and demonstration, and opinions shall be solicited from relevant departments, industry associations, enterprises, public institutions, and the public.

Article 11 The competent departments of ecological environment under the people's governments at or above the provincial level shall publish air quality standards and air pollutant emission standards on their websites for the public to consult and download free of charge.

Article 12 The implementation of air quality standards and air pollutant emission standards shall be evaluated periodically, and the standards shall be revised in a timely manner based on the evaluation results.

Article 13 Quality standards for products such as coal, petroleum coke, biomass fuels, coatings and other products containing volatile organic compounds, fireworks and firecrackers, and boilers shall specify air environmental protection requirements.

Quality standards for fuel oil shall meet the national air pollutant control requirements and shall be coordinated with and implemented simultaneously with the national air pollutant emission standards for motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery.

The term "non-road mobile machinery" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to mobile machinery and transportable industrial equipment equipped with engines.

Article 14 The people's government of a city that fails to meet the national air quality standards shall promptly formulate a plan for achieving the air quality standards within a time limit, take measures, and achieve the air quality standards within the time limit prescribed by the State Council or the provincial people's government.

When formulating a plan for achieving urban air quality standards within a time limit, opinions shall be solicited from relevant industry associations, enterprises, public institutions, experts, and the public.

Article 15 The plan for achieving urban air quality standards within a time limit shall be made public. The plan for achieving air quality standards within a time limit for municipalities directly under the Central Government and cities divided into districts shall be reported to the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council for recordation.

Article 16 When reporting on the environmental conditions and the completion of environmental protection targets to the people's congress or its standing committee at the corresponding level each year, the city people's government shall report on the implementation of the plan for achieving air quality standards within a time limit and make it public.

Article 17 The plan for achieving urban air quality standards within a time limit shall be evaluated and revised in a timely manner based on the requirements of air pollution prevention and control and economic and technical conditions.

## Chapter III Supervision and Administration of Air Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 18 Enterprises, public institutions, and other producers and business operators constructing projects that have an impact on the atmospheric environment shall conduct environmental impact assessments in accordance with the law and disclose the environmental impact assessment documents; those discharging air pollutants into the atmosphere shall comply with the air pollutant emission standards and adhere to the requirements for total emission control of key air pollutants.

Article 19 Enterprises and public institutions that discharge industrial waste gas or the toxic and hazardous air pollutants listed in the catalog specified in Article 78 of this Law, production and operation units of coal-fired heat sources for central heating facilities, and other units that are subject to pollutant discharge permit management in accordance with the law shall obtain pollutant discharge permits. The specific methods and implementation steps for pollutant discharge permits shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 20 Enterprises, public institutions, and other producers and business operators that discharge air pollutants into the atmosphere shall set up air pollutant discharge outlets in accordance with laws, regulations, and the provisions of the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

It is prohibited to discharge air pollutants through evasive means such as clandestine discharge, tampering with or falsifying monitoring data, temporary production suspension to evade on-site inspection, opening emergency discharge channels under non-emergency circumstances, or abnormal operation of air pollution prevention and control facilities.

Article 21 The State implements total emission control of key air pollutants.

The total emission control targets for key air pollutants shall be determined by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval and implementation in conjunction with the comprehensive economic department under the State Council.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall control or reduce the total emission of key air pollutants within their administrative regions in accordance with the total emission control targets issued by the State Council.

The specific methods for determining total emission control targets and allocating total emission control indicators shall be prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council. The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the needs of air pollution prevention and control within their administrative regions, implement total emission control for air pollutants other than the national key air pollutants.

The State gradually promotes the trading of emission rights for key air pollutants.

Article 22 For regions that exceed the total emission control targets for national key air pollutants or fail to achieve the air quality improvement targets assigned by the State, the competent department of ecological environment under the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, together with the relevant departments, hold talks with the principal responsible persons of the people's government of that region and suspend the approval of environmental impact assessment documents for construction projects that would increase the total emission of key air pollutants in that region. The situation of the talks shall be made public.

Article 23 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall be responsible for formulating specifications for monitoring and evaluating air quality and air pollution sources, organizing the construction and management of the national monitoring network for air quality and air pollution sources, organizing the monitoring of air quality and air pollution sources, and uniformly releasing national information on air quality conditions.

The competent departments of ecological environment under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing the construction and management of the monitoring network for air quality and air pollution sources within their administrative regions, conducting monitoring of air quality and air pollution sources, and uniformly releasing information on air quality conditions within their administrative regions.

Article 24 Enterprises, public institutions, and other producers and business operators shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State and monitoring specifications, monitor the industrial waste gas they discharge and the toxic and hazardous air pollutants listed in the catalog specified in Article 78 of this Law, and retain the original monitoring records. Among them, key pollutant-discharging units shall install and use automatic monitoring equipment for air pollutant emissions, connect it to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of ecological environment, ensure the normal operation of the monitoring equipment, and publicly disclose emission information in accordance with the law. The specific methods for monitoring and the conditions for key pollutant-discharging units shall be prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

The list of key pollutant-discharging units shall be determined by the competent department of ecological environment under the local people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts, in consultation with relevant departments, based on factors such as the air environmental carrying capacity of the administrative region, the requirements for total emission control indicators of key air pollutants, and the types, quantities, and concentrations of air pollutants discharged by the pollutant-discharging units, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council, and shall be made public.

Article 25 Key pollutant-discharging units shall be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the automatic monitoring data. If the competent department of ecological environment finds that the data transmitted by the automatic monitoring equipment for air pollutant emissions of a key pollutant-discharging unit is abnormal, it shall promptly conduct an investigation.

Article 26 It is prohibited to occupy, damage, or without authorization move or alter air quality monitoring facilities and automatic monitoring equipment for air pollutant emissions.

Article 27 The State implements a phase-out system for processes, equipment, and products that seriously pollute the atmospheric environment.

The comprehensive economic department under the State Council, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, shall determine the phase-out deadlines for processes, equipment, and products that seriously pollute the atmospheric environment and include them in the national comprehensive industrial policy catalog.

Producers, importers, sellers, or users shall cease the production, import, sale, or use of the equipment and products listed in the catalog specified in the preceding paragraph within the prescribed time limit. Adopters of the processes shall cease using the processes listed in the catalog specified in the preceding paragraph within the prescribed time limit.

Phased-out equipment and products shall not be transferred to others for use.

Article 28 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council, together with the relevant departments, shall establish and improve the assessment system for air pollution damage.

Article 29 The competent departments of ecological environment and their environmental law enforcement agencies, as well as other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection, have the right to conduct supervision and inspection of enterprises, public institutions, and other producers and business operators that discharge air pollutants through methods such as on-site inspection and monitoring, automatic monitoring, remote sensing monitoring, and far-infrared imaging. The inspected party shall truthfully reflect the situation and provide necessary information. The departments, agencies, and their staff conducting the inspection shall keep business secrets for the inspected party.

Article 30 If an enterprise, public institution, or other producer or business operator discharges air pollutants in violation of laws and regulations, causing or likely to cause serious air pollution, or if relevant evidence may be destroyed or concealed, the competent department of ecological environment under the people's government at or above the county level and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection may take administrative compulsory measures such as sealing up or seizing the relevant facilities, equipment, and items.

Article 31 The competent departments of ecological environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection shall publish reporting telephone numbers, email addresses, etc., to facilitate public reporting.

Upon receiving a report, the competent department of ecological environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection shall promptly handle it and keep the relevant information of the reporter confidential; for real-name reports, they shall provide feedback on the handling results and other information; if the report is verified, the handling results shall be made public in accordance with the law, and the reporter shall be rewarded.

If a reporter reports the unit where he or she works, the unit shall not retaliate against the reporter by means such as termination or modification of the labor contract or other means.

## Chapter IV Measures for Air Pollution Prevention and Control

### Section 1 Prevention and Control of Pollution from Coal Combustion and Other Energy Sources

Article 32 The relevant departments under the State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to adjust the energy structure, promote the production and use of clean energy; optimize the use of coal, promote clean and efficient utilization of coal, gradually reduce the proportion of coal in primary energy consumption, and reduce air pollutant emissions during coal production, use, and conversion.

Article 33 The State promotes coal washing and processing to reduce the sulfur and ash content of coal and restricts the mining of high-sulfur and high-ash coal. New coal mines shall simultaneously construct supporting coal washing facilities so that the sulfur and ash content of the coal meets the prescribed standards; for existing coal mines, except where the coal mined is of low sulfur and low ash content or where washing is not required according to the requirements of coal-fired power plants that have achieved standard emissions, supporting coal washing facilities shall be constructed within a specified time limit.

It is prohibited to mine coal with radioactive and toxic and hazardous substances such as arsenic exceeding the prescribed standards.

Article 34 The State adopts economic and technical policies and measures conducive to the clean and efficient utilization of coal, and encourages and supports the development and promotion of clean coal technologies.

The State encourages coal mining enterprises and others to adopt reasonable and feasible technical measures to extract and utilize coalbed methane and to comprehensively utilize coal gangue. Those engaged in the extraction and utilization of coalbed methane shall ensure that coalbed methane emissions comply with relevant standards and specifications.

Article 35 The State prohibits the import, sale, and combustion of coal that does not meet quality standards and encourages the combustion of high-quality coal.

Units storing materials such as coal, coal gangue, coal slag, and coal ash shall take measures to prevent spontaneous combustion and air pollution.

Article 36 Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen the management of bulk coal for civilian use, prohibit the sale of coal that does not meet the quality standards for civilian bulk coal, encourage residents to use high-quality coal and clean briquettes, and promote energy-saving and environmentally friendly stoves.

Article 37 Petroleum refining enterprises shall produce fuel oil in accordance with fuel quality standards.

It is prohibited to import, sell, or combust petroleum coke that does not meet quality standards.

Article 38 City people's governments may designate and publish high-pollution fuel prohibition zones and gradually expand the scope of such zones based on the requirements for improving air quality. The catalog of high-pollution fuels shall be determined by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

Within the prohibition zones, it is prohibited to sell or combust high-pollution fuels; it is prohibited to construct or expand facilities that combust high-pollution fuels; existing facilities shall be converted to use natural gas, shale gas, liquefied petroleum gas, electricity, or other clean energy within the time limit prescribed by the city people's government.

Article 39 Urban construction shall be planned comprehensively. In areas with coal-fired heating, combined heat and power generation and central heating shall be promoted. In areas covered by the central heating pipeline network, it is prohibited to construct or expand decentralized coal-fired heating boilers; existing coal-fired heating boilers that cannot meet emission standards shall be dismantled within the time limit prescribed by the city people's government.

Article 40 The market supervision and administration departments under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with the competent departments of ecological environment, supervise and inspect the compliance of boilers with environmental protection standards or requirements during production, import, sale, and use; boilers that do not meet environmental protection standards or requirements shall not be produced, imported, sold, or used.

Article 41 Coal-fired power plants and other coal-fired units shall adopt clean production processes, construct supporting dust removal, desulfurization, and denitrification devices, or adopt other measures such as technological transformation to control air pollutant emissions.

The State encourages coal-fired units to adopt advanced technologies and devices for the coordinated control of air pollutants such as dust, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and mercury to reduce air pollutant emissions.

Article 42 Power dispatching shall prioritize the connection of clean energy power generation to the grid.

### Section 2 Prevention and Control of Industrial Pollution

Article 43 Enterprises in industries such as iron and steel, building materials, non-ferrous metals, petroleum, and chemicals that emit dust, sulfides, and nitrogen oxides during production shall adopt clean production processes, construct supporting dust removal, desulfurization, and denitrification devices, or adopt other measures such as technological transformation to control air pollutant emissions.

Article 44 The volatile organic compound content of raw materials and products containing volatile organic compounds that are produced, imported, sold, or used shall meet quality standards or requirements.

The State encourages the production, import, sale, and use of low-toxicity, low-volatility organic solvents.

Article 45 Production and service activities that generate waste gases containing volatile organic compounds shall be carried out in enclosed spaces or equipment, and pollution prevention and control facilities shall be installed and used in accordance with regulations; if enclosure is not possible, measures shall be taken to reduce waste gas emissions.

Article 46 Industrial coating enterprises shall use coatings with low volatile organic compound content and establish records documenting the usage, waste, disposal, and volatile organic compound content of production raw materials and auxiliary materials. The records shall be kept for at least three years.

Article 47 Enterprises in the petroleum, chemical, and other industries that produce and use organic solvents shall take measures for the daily maintenance and repair of pipelines and equipment to reduce material leakage, and shall promptly collect and treat leaked materials.

Oil and gas storage depots, gas stations, crude oil and product oil terminals, crude oil and product oil transport vessels, and oil tank trucks and gas tank trucks shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, install oil and gas recovery devices and maintain their normal operation.

Article 48 Enterprises in industries such as iron and steel, building materials, non-ferrous metals, petroleum, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and mining shall strengthen refined management, adopt measures such as centralized collection and treatment, and strictly control the emission of dust and gaseous pollutants.

Industrial production enterprises shall take measures such as enclosure, shielding, covering, cleaning, and watering to reduce the emission of dust and gaseous pollutants generated during the storage, transport, loading, and unloading of internal materials.

Article 49 Flammable gases generated from industrial production, landfill, or other activities shall be recovered and utilized; if recovery and utilization conditions are not available, pollution prevention and control treatment shall be carried out.If a flammable gas recovery and utilization device cannot operate normally, it shall be promptly repaired or replaced. If it is necessary to discharge flammable gas during the period when the recovery and utilization device is not operating normally, the discharged flammable gas shall be fully combusted or other measures to control air pollutant emissions shall be taken, and the local competent department of ecological environment shall be notified, with repairs or replacements to be completed within a specified period as required.

### Section 3: Prevention and Control of Pollution from Motor Vehicles and Vessels

Article 50 The State advocates low-carbon and environmentally friendly travel, reasonably controls the number of fuel-powered motor vehicles based on urban planning, vigorously develops urban public transportation, and increases the proportion of public transport trips.

The State adopts fiscal, tax, and government procurement measures to promote the application of energy-saving, environmentally friendly, and new energy motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery, restricts the development of high-fuel-consumption and high-emission motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery, and reduces the consumption of fossil energy.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in areas where conditions permit, implement the emission limits for the corresponding stages of the national motor vehicle air pollutant emission standards in advance, and report this to the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council for the record.

People's governments of cities shall strengthen and improve urban traffic management, optimize road layout, and ensure the continuity and smoothness of sidewalks and non-motor vehicle lanes.

Article 51 Motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery shall not emit air pollutants exceeding the standards.

The production, import, or sale of motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery whose air pollutant emissions exceed the standards is prohibited.

Article 52 Manufacturers of motor vehicles and non-road mobile machinery shall conduct emission inspections on newly produced motor vehicles and non-road mobile machinery. Only those that pass the inspection may be released from the factory for sale. Inspection information shall be disclosed to the public.

The competent departments of ecological environment of people's governments at or above the provincial level may strengthen the supervision and inspection of the air pollutant emission status of newly produced and sold motor vehicles and non-road mobile machinery through on-site inspections, sampling tests, and other means. Relevant departments such as industry and market supervision and administration shall cooperate.

Article 53 In-use motor vehicles shall undergo periodic emission inspections by motor vehicle emission inspection institutions in accordance with national or local regulations. Only those that pass the inspection may be driven on roads. For those that fail the inspection, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall not issue a safety technical inspection qualification mark.

The competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government at or above the county level may conduct supervisory spot checks on the air pollutant emission status of in-use motor vehicles at centralized parking lots and repair sites; without affecting normal traffic, it may conduct supervisory spot checks on the air pollutant emission status of motor vehicles driving on roads through technical means such as remote sensing monitoring, and the traffic management department of the public security organ shall cooperate.

Article 54 Motor vehicle emission inspection institutions shall legally obtain metrological certification, use legally verified and qualified motor vehicle emission inspection equipment, conduct emission inspections on motor vehicles in accordance with the specifications formulated by the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, and connect to the network of the competent department of ecological environment to achieve real-time sharing of inspection data. Motor vehicle emission inspection institutions and their persons in charge shall be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the inspection data.

The competent department of ecological environment and the certification and accreditation supervision and administration department shall supervise and inspect the emission inspection activities of motor vehicle emission inspection institutions.

Article 55 Motor vehicle production and import enterprises shall publicly disclose the emission inspection information, pollution control technology information, and relevant maintenance technical information of the motor vehicle models they produce or import.

Motor vehicle maintenance units shall repair in-use motor vehicles in accordance with the requirements for preventing air pollution and relevant national technical specifications, so that they meet the prescribed emission standards. The competent departments of transport and ecological environment shall strengthen supervision and management according to law.

It is prohibited for motor vehicle owners to pass motor vehicle emission inspections by fraudulent means such as temporarily replacing motor vehicle pollution control devices. It is prohibited for motor vehicle maintenance units to provide such maintenance services. It is prohibited to damage the on-board emission diagnostic system of motor vehicles.

Article 56 The competent department of ecological environment shall, together with relevant departments such as transport, housing and urban-rural construction, agricultural administration, and water administration, supervise and inspect the air pollutant emission status of non-road mobile machinery. Those that fail the emission inspection shall not be used.

Article 57 The State advocates environmentally friendly driving, encouraging fuel-powered motor vehicle drivers to turn off the engine when parking for more than three minutes without affecting road traffic, to reduce air pollutant emissions.

Article 58 The State establishes a recall system for motor vehicles and non-road mobile machinery for environmental protection.

If a production or import enterprise learns that a motor vehicle or non-road mobile machinery emits air pollutants exceeding the standard due to design or production defects or failure to meet the prescribed environmental protection durability requirements, it shall recall them; if it fails to do so, the market supervision and administration department of the State Council, together with the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, shall order the recall.

Article 59 If in-use heavy-duty diesel vehicles and non-road mobile machinery are not equipped with pollution control devices or the pollution control devices do not meet the requirements, resulting in failure to meet emission standards, they shall be retrofitted with or replaced by pollution control devices that meet the requirements.

Article 60 If an in-use motor vehicle emits air pollutants exceeding the standard, it shall be repaired; if, after repair or the adoption of pollution control technologies, the air pollutant emissions still do not meet the national emission standards for in-use motor vehicles, it shall be compulsorily scrapped. The owner shall deliver the motor vehicle to a scrapped motor vehicle recycling and dismantling enterprise, which shall handle registration, dismantling, destruction, etc., in accordance with relevant national regulations.

The State encourages and supports the early scrapping of high-emission motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery.

Article 61 People's governments of cities may, based on the ambient air quality conditions, designate and announce areas where the use of high-emission non-road mobile machinery is prohibited.

Article 62 Ship inspection institutions shall conduct emission inspections on ship engines and related equipment. Only ships that pass the inspection and meet national emission standards may be operated.

Article 63 Inland river vessels and vessels navigating directly between rivers and seas shall use standard ordinary diesel. Ocean-going vessels, after berthing, shall use marine fuel that meets air pollutant control requirements.

Newly built wharves shall plan, design, and construct shore-based power supply facilities; existing wharves shall gradually implement the retrofitting of shore-based power supply facilities. Vessels shall prioritize the use of shore power after berthing.

Article 64 The competent department of transport of the State Council may designate ship air pollutant emission control areas in coastal waters. Ships entering the emission control areas shall comply with relevant ship emission requirements.

Article 65 It is prohibited to produce, import, or sell fuels for motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery that do not meet the standards; it is prohibited to sell ordinary diesel and other non-motor vehicle fuels to automobiles and motorcycles; it is prohibited to sell residual oil and heavy oil to non-road mobile machinery, inland river vessels, and vessels navigating directly between rivers and seas.

Article 66 The content of harmful substances and other atmospheric environmental protection indicators in engine oils, nitrogen oxide reducing agents, fuel and lubricant additives, and other additives shall meet the requirements of relevant standards, shall not impair the effectiveness and durability of pollution control devices for motor vehicles and vessels, and shall not increase new air pollutant emissions.

Article 67 The State actively promotes the prevention and control of air pollution from civil aircraft, encouraging the adoption of effective measures during design, production, and use to reduce air pollutant emissions.

Civil aircraft shall meet the relevant engine emission requirements stipulated in the national airworthiness standards.

### Section 4: Prevention and Control of Fugitive Dust Pollution

Article 68 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management of construction and transportation, keep roads clean, control the storage of material piles and muck, expand the area of green spaces, water bodies, wetlands, and ground paving, and prevent and control fugitive dust pollution.

Relevant departments such as housing and urban-rural construction, municipal sanitation, transport, and land and resources shall, based on the responsibilities determined by the people's government at the corresponding level, carry out the work of preventing and controlling fugitive dust pollution.

Article 69 Construction entities shall include the cost of preventing and controlling fugitive dust pollution in the project cost and clearly define the responsibility of the construction unit for fugitive dust pollution prevention and control in the construction contract. The construction unit shall formulate a specific implementation plan for the prevention and control of construction fugitive dust.

Construction units engaged in housing construction, municipal infrastructure construction, river channel regulation, and building demolition shall file a record with the competent department responsible for supervising and managing fugitive dust pollution prevention and control.

Construction units shall set up hard enclosures at construction sites and adopt effective dust prevention and reduction measures such as covering, phased operations, scheduling construction times, sprinkling water to suppress dust, and washing the ground and vehicles. Construction earthwork, engineering muck, and construction waste shall be promptly removed; if stored on site, they shall be covered with airtight dust-proof nets. Engineering muck and construction waste shall be processed for resource utilization.

Construction units shall display information at the construction site, including fugitive dust pollution prevention and control measures, the person responsible, and the competent department supervising fugitive dust pollution.

For construction land that cannot be started temporarily, the construction entity shall cover the exposed ground; if it exceeds three months, greening, paving, or covering shall be carried out.

Article 70 Vehicles transporting bulk and fluid materials such as coal, garbage, muck, sand, gravel, earth, and mortar shall adopt airtight or other measures to prevent material spillage causing fugitive dust pollution and shall travel along prescribed routes.

When loading and unloading materials, measures such as airtightness or spraying shall be adopted to prevent fugitive dust pollution.

People's governments of cities shall strengthen the management of sweeping and cleaning of roads, squares, parking lots, and other public places, promote low-dust operation methods such as clean-powered mechanized sweeping, and prevent and control fugitive dust pollution.

Article 71 For municipal river channels and exposed ground along riverbanks, public land, and other exposed ground in urban areas, relevant departments shall organize the implementation of greening or permeable paving according to plans.

Article 72 Materials prone to generating fugitive dust, such as coal, coal gangue, coal slag, coal ash, cement, lime, gypsum, and sand, shall be stored in an airtight manner; if airtight storage is not possible, tight enclosures not lower than the height of the stored materials shall be set up, and effective covering measures shall be taken to prevent fugitive dust pollution.

Wharves, mines, landfills, and disposal sites shall implement zoned operations and take effective measures to prevent fugitive dust pollution.

### Section 5: Agricultural and Other Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 73 Local people's governments at all levels shall promote the transformation of agricultural production methods, develop agricultural circular economy, increase support for comprehensive waste treatment, and strengthen the control of air pollutant emissions from agricultural production and operation activities.

Article 74 Agricultural producers and operators shall improve fertilization methods, scientifically and rationally apply chemical fertilizers, and use pesticides in accordance with national regulations to reduce emissions of air pollutants such as ammonia and volatile organic compounds.

It is prohibited to spray highly toxic or acutely toxic pesticides on trees and flowers in densely populated areas.

Article 75 Livestock and poultry farms and farming communities shall promptly collect, store, remove, and harmlessly treat sewage, livestock and poultry manure, and carcasses to prevent the emission of malodorous gases.

Article 76 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments such as agricultural administration shall encourage and support the use of advanced and applicable technologies for the comprehensive utilization of straw, fallen leaves, etc., as fertilizers, feed, energy, industrial raw materials, and edible fungus substrates, and increase fiscal subsidies for agricultural machinery integrating straw returning and collection.

People's governments at the county level shall organize the establishment of a service system for the collection, storage, transportation, and comprehensive utilization of straw, and use measures such as fiscal subsidies to support rural collective economic organizations, specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations, and enterprises in carrying out services for the collection, storage, transportation, and comprehensive utilization of straw.

Article 77 People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall designate areas where the open burning of straw, fallen leaves, and other materials that produce smoke and dust pollution is prohibited.

Article 78 The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council shall, together with the health administration department of the State Council, publish a list of toxic and hazardous air pollutants based on the degree of harm and impact of air pollutants on public health and the ecological environment, and implement risk management.

Enterprises and institutions that discharge the toxic and hazardous air pollutants listed in the preceding paragraph shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, establish an environmental risk early warning system, conduct regular monitoring of discharge outlets and the surrounding environment, assess environmental risks, investigate environmental safety hazards, and take effective measures to prevent environmental risks.

Article 79 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that discharge persistent organic pollutants into the atmosphere, as well as operators of waste incineration facilities, shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, adopt technical methods and processes conducive to reducing emissions of persistent organic pollutants, equip effective purification devices, and achieve standard discharge.

Article 80 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that generate malodorous gases during production and operation activities shall scientifically select sites, set up reasonable protective distances, and install purification devices or take other measures to prevent the emission of malodorous gases.

Article 81 Operators of catering services that emit cooking fumes shall install fume purification facilities and keep them in normal use, or adopt other fume purification measures to ensure that fumes are discharged up to standard and to prevent pollution of the normal living environment of nearby residents.

It is prohibited to newly build, renovate, or expand catering service projects that generate cooking fumes, odors, or waste gases in residential buildings, commercial-residential mixed-use buildings not equipped with dedicated fume ducts, and commercial floors adjacent to residential floors within commercial-residential mixed-use buildings.

No unit or individual may barbecue food in the open air or provide venues for open-air barbecue in areas prohibited by the local people's government.

Article 82 It is prohibited to burn asphalt, asphalt felt, rubber, plastic, leather, garbage, and other substances that produce toxic, hazardous smoke, dust, and malodorous gases in densely populated areas and other areas requiring special protection according to law.

It is prohibited to produce, sell, or set off fireworks and firecrackers that do not meet quality standards. No unit or individual may set off fireworks and firecrackers during periods or in areas prohibited by the people's government of the city.

Article 83 The State encourages and advocates civilized and green sacrificial practices.

Crematoriums shall install pollution prevention facilities such as dust removal equipment and keep them in normal use to prevent affecting the surrounding environment.

Article 84 Operators engaged in service activities such as garment dry cleaning and motor vehicle maintenance shall, in accordance with relevant national standards or requirements, install pollution prevention facilities such as odor and waste gas treatment devices and keep them in normal use to prevent affecting the surrounding environment.

Article 85 The State encourages and supports the production and use of substitutes for ozone-depleting substances, gradually reducing and eventually ceasing the production and use of ozone-depleting substances.

The State implements total quantity control and quota management for the production, use, import, and export of ozone-depleting substances. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter 5: Joint Prevention and Control of Air Pollution in Key Regions

Article 86 The State establishes a mechanism for joint prevention and control of air pollution in key regions, coordinating and integrating efforts for air pollution prevention and control within these regions. The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, based on the main functional zone planning, regional ambient air quality conditions, and the patterns of air pollution transmission and diffusion, designates national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, subject to approval by the State Council.

People's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within key regions shall designate a leading local people's government, hold regular joint meetings, and carry out joint prevention and control of air pollution in accordance with the requirements of unified planning, unified standards, unified monitoring, and unified prevention and control measures, and implement the responsibility targets for air pollution prevention and control. The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council shall strengthen guidance and supervision.

Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, by reference to the provisions of the first paragraph, designate key regions for air pollution prevention and control within their respective administrative areas.

Article 87 The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within the national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, shall formulate joint prevention and control action plans for air pollution in key regions, define control targets, optimize regional economic layout, coordinate traffic management, develop clean energy, propose key prevention and control tasks and measures, and promote the improvement of ambient air quality in key regions.

Article 88 The comprehensive economic department of the State Council, together with the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, shall further raise requirements for environmental protection, energy consumption, safety, quality, etc., in light of the actual industrial development and ambient air quality conditions in the national key regions for air pollution prevention and control.

People's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within key regions shall implement stricter motor vehicle air pollutant emission standards, unify inspection methods and emission limits for in-use motor vehicles, and provide supporting qualified vehicle fuel.

Article 89 When formulating plans for industrial parks, development zones, regional industries, and development that may cause serious air pollution to the atmospheric environment of national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, an environmental impact assessment shall be conducted according to law. The planning formulation authority shall consult with the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within the key region or relevant departments.

When constructing projects within key regions that may have a significant impact on the ambient air quality of neighboring provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall promptly notify relevant information and conduct consultations.

The consultation opinions and their adoption status shall serve as important bases for the review or approval of environmental impact assessment documents.

Article 90 For new construction, renovation, or expansion projects using coal within national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, equivalent or reduced coal substitution shall be implemented.

Article 91 The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council shall organize the establishment of a relevant information sharing mechanism for ambient air quality monitoring and air pollution source monitoring in national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, use new technologies such as monitoring, simulation, satellite, aerial survey, and remote sensing to analyze the sources and changing trends of air pollution in key regions, and disclose the information to the public.

Article 92 The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within national key regions for air pollution prevention and control may organize relevant departments to carry out joint law enforcement, cross-regional law enforcement, and cross-law enforcement.

## Chapter 6: Response to Heavy Pollution Weather

Article 93 The State establishes a monitoring and early warning system for heavy pollution weather.

The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council, together with relevant departments such as the meteorological authority of the State Council and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within national key regions for air pollution prevention and control, establishes a monitoring and early warning mechanism for heavy pollution weather in key regions, with unified early warning classification standards. If regional heavy pollution weather is likely to occur, timely notification shall be given to the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within the key region.

The competent departments of ecological environment of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts, together with relevant departments such as the meteorological authority, establish a monitoring and early warning mechanism for heavy pollution weather within their respective administrative areas.

Article 94 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the response to heavy pollution weather into the emergency management system for emergencies.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and county-level people's governments where heavy pollution weather may occur shall formulate emergency plans for heavy pollution weather, file them with the competent department of ecological environment of the people's government at the next higher level, and make them public.Article 95 The competent departments of ecology and environment of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts shall, in conjunction with the meteorological authorities, establish a consultation mechanism to forecast atmospheric environmental quality. If heavy pollution weather is likely to occur, they shall promptly report to the people's government at the same level. The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts shall, based on the heavy pollution weather forecast information, conduct comprehensive analysis, determine the warning level, and issue a warning in a timely manner. The warning level shall be adjusted promptly according to changes in the situation. No unit or individual may release heavy pollution weather forecast or warning information to the public without authorization.

After the warning information is released, the people's government and its relevant departments shall inform the public through television, radio, the internet, text messages, and other means to take health protection measures, guide public travel, and adjust other related social activities.

Article 96 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the warning level of heavy pollution weather, promptly initiate emergency response plans and may, as needed for emergency response, take emergency measures such as ordering relevant enterprises to suspend or limit production, restricting the operation of certain motor vehicles, prohibiting the setting off of fireworks and firecrackers, stopping earthwork and building demolition operations at construction sites, stopping outdoor barbecues, suspending outdoor activities organized by kindergartens and schools, and organizing artificial weather modification operations.

After the emergency response ends, the people's government shall promptly evaluate the implementation of the emergency response plan and revise and improve the plan as appropriate.

Article 97 In the event of a sudden environmental incident causing air pollution, the people's government and its relevant departments, as well as relevant enterprises and institutions, shall carry out emergency response work in accordance with the provisions of the Emergency Response Law of the People's Republic of China and the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China. The competent department of ecology and environment shall promptly monitor the air pollutants generated by the sudden environmental incident and publish the monitoring information to the public.

## Chapter 7 Legal Liability

Article 98 Any violation of this Law by refusing to enter a site or otherwise refusing to accept supervision and inspection by the competent department of ecology and environment, its environmental law enforcement agency, or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection, or by engaging in fraud during such supervision and inspection, shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection, and a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 99 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections, or to restrict production or suspend production for remediation, by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure:

(1) Discharging air pollutants without obtaining a pollutant discharge permit in accordance with the law;

(2) Discharging air pollutants exceeding the air pollutant discharge standards or exceeding the total discharge control indicators for key air pollutants;

(3) Discharging air pollutants by evading supervision.

Article 100 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend production for remediation:

(1) Encroaching upon, damaging, or without authorization moving or altering air environmental quality monitoring facilities or automatic monitoring equipment for air pollutant discharge;

(2) Failing to monitor industrial waste gas and toxic and hazardous air pollutants discharged in accordance with regulations and failing to preserve original monitoring records;

(3) Failing to install and use automatic monitoring equipment for air pollutant discharge in accordance with regulations, or failing to connect to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of ecology and environment in accordance with regulations, and failing to ensure the normal operation of the monitoring equipment;

(4) Key pollutant discharging units failing to disclose or failing to truthfully disclose automatic monitoring data;

(5) Failing to set up air pollutant discharge outlets in accordance with regulations.

Article 101 Any violation of this Law by producing, importing, selling, or using equipment and products prohibited in the national comprehensive industrial policy catalog, adopting processes prohibited in the national comprehensive industrial policy catalog, or transferring eliminated equipment and products to others for use shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of economic comprehensive management of the people's government at or above the county level or the customs authority according to their respective responsibilities, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure. If the import act constitutes smuggling, the customs authority shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 102 Any violation of this Law by a coal mine failing to construct supporting coal washing facilities in accordance with regulations shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent energy department of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure.

Any violation of this Law by mining coal containing radioactive and arsenic and other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding the prescribed standards shall be ordered to suspend business or closure by the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 103 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the market supervision and administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level, and the raw materials, products, and illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed:

(1) Selling coal or petroleum coke that does not meet quality standards;

(2) Producing or selling raw materials and products whose volatile organic compound content does not meet quality standards or requirements;

(3) Producing or selling fuels for motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery, engine oils, nitrogen oxide reducing agents, fuel and lubricant additives, and other additives that do not meet standards;

(4) Selling high-pollution fuels in prohibited fuel zones.

Article 104 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the customs authority, and the raw materials, products, and illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed; if the act constitutes smuggling, the customs authority shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law:

(1) Importing coal or petroleum coke that does not meet quality standards;

(2) Importing raw materials and products whose volatile organic compound content does not meet quality standards or requirements;

(3) Importing fuels for motor vehicles, vessels, and non-road mobile machinery, engine oils, nitrogen oxide reducing agents, fuel and lubricant additives, and other additives that do not meet standards.

Article 105 Any violation of this Law by a unit burning coal or petroleum coke that does not meet quality standards shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed.

Article 106 Any violation of this Law by using fuel for vessels that does not meet standards or requirements shall be fined not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 by the maritime administration agency or the competent fisheries department according to their respective responsibilities.

Article 107 Any violation of this Law by constructing or expanding facilities burning high-pollution fuels in prohibited fuel zones, or failing to stop burning high-pollution fuels in accordance with regulations, or constructing or expanding decentralized coal-fired heating boilers in areas covered by urban central heating pipe networks, or failing to demolish existing coal-fired heating boilers that cannot meet emission standards in accordance with regulations, shall have the facilities burning high-pollution fuels confiscated by the competent department of ecology and environment of the local people's government at or above the county level, and the coal-fired heating boilers shall be ordered to be demolished, and a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed.

Any violation of this Law by producing, importing, selling, or using boilers that do not meet prescribed standards or requirements shall be ordered to make corrections by the market supervision and administration department or the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed.

Article 108 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend production for remediation:

(1) Production and service activities generating waste gas containing volatile organic compounds are not carried out in enclosed spaces or equipment, pollution prevention facilities are not installed and used in accordance with regulations, or measures to reduce waste gas emissions are not taken;

(2) Industrial coating enterprises fail to use coatings with low volatile organic compound content or fail to establish and maintain records;

(3) Enterprises producing and using organic solvents, such as petroleum and chemical enterprises, fail to take measures for daily maintenance and repair of pipelines and equipment to reduce material leakage, or fail to promptly collect and treat leaked materials;

(4) Oil and gas storage depots, gas stations, and tank trucks, etc., fail to install and properly use oil and gas recovery devices in accordance with relevant national regulations;

(5) Enterprises such as steel, building materials, non-ferrous metals, petroleum, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and mining fail to take measures such as centralized collection and treatment, enclosure, shielding, covering, sweeping, and watering to control and reduce emissions of dust and gaseous pollutants;

(6) Combustible gases generated in industrial production, landfill, or other activities are not recovered and utilized, or when recovery and utilization conditions are not met, pollution prevention treatment is not carried out, or the recovery and utilization devices for combustible gases cannot operate normally and are not promptly repaired or updated.

Article 109 Any violation of this Law by producing motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that exceed pollutant emission standards shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed, and the motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that cannot meet pollutant emission standards shall be confiscated and destroyed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend production for remediation, and the competent department of motor vehicle production of the State Council shall order the cessation of production of that model.

Any violation of this Law by a motor vehicle or non-road mobile machinery manufacturer engaging in fraud or passing off inferior products as quality ones regarding engines or pollution control devices, and selling them as products passing emission inspection, shall be ordered to suspend production for remediation by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed, and the motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that cannot meet pollutant emission standards shall be confiscated and destroyed, and the competent department of motor vehicle production of the State Council shall order the cessation of production of that model.

Article 110 Any violation of this Law by importing or selling motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that exceed pollutant emission standards shall have the illegal gains confiscated by the market supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the county level or the customs authority according to their respective responsibilities, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods shall be imposed, and the motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that cannot meet pollutant emission standards shall be confiscated and destroyed; if the import act constitutes smuggling, the customs authority shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

Any violation of this Law by selling motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery that do not meet pollutant emission standards, the seller shall be responsible for repair, replacement, or return; if losses are caused to the buyer, the seller shall compensate for the losses.

Article 111 Any violation of this Law by a motor vehicle production or import enterprise failing to publicly disclose the emission inspection information or pollution control technology information of the motor vehicle models it produces or imports in accordance with regulations shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and a fine of not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 500,000 shall be imposed.

Any violation of this Law by a motor vehicle production or import enterprise failing to publicly disclose the relevant maintenance technical information of the motor vehicle models it produces or imports in accordance with regulations shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of transportation of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and a fine of not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 500,000 shall be imposed.

Article 112 Any violation of this Law by falsifying the emission inspection results of motor vehicles or non-road mobile machinery or issuing false emission inspection reports shall have the illegal gains confiscated by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the department responsible for qualification recognition shall revoke its inspection qualification.

Any violation of this Law by falsifying the emission inspection results of vessels or issuing false emission inspection reports shall be penalized by the maritime administration agency in accordance with the law.

Any violation of this Law by passing motor vehicle emission inspection through fraudulent means such as temporarily replacing motor vehicle pollution control devices, or damaging the motor vehicle onboard emission diagnostic system, shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of RMB 5,000 shall be imposed on the motor vehicle owner; a fine of RMB 5,000 per motor vehicle shall be imposed on the motor vehicle maintenance unit.

Article 113 Any violation of this Law by a motor vehicle driver driving a motor vehicle that has failed emission inspection on the road shall be penalized by the traffic management department of the public security organ in accordance with the law.

Article 114 Any violation of this Law by using non-road mobile machinery with unqualified emissions, or failing to install or replace pollution control devices on heavy-duty diesel vehicles or non-road mobile machinery in use in accordance with regulations, shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level according to its responsibilities, and a fine of RMB 5,000 shall be imposed.

Any violation of this Law by using high-emission non-road mobile machinery in areas where their use is prohibited shall be penalized by the competent department of ecology and environment of the city people's government in accordance with the law.

Article 115 Any violation of this Law by a construction unit committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of housing and urban-rural construction of the people's government at or above the county level according to its responsibilities, and a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend work for remediation:

(1) The construction site is not equipped with hard enclosures, or effective dust prevention and reduction measures such as covering, phased operation, timely construction, watering to suppress dust, and washing the ground and vehicles are not taken;

(2) Construction earthwork, engineering waste, and construction waste are not promptly removed, or are not covered with airtight dust-proof nets.

Any violation of this Law by a construction unit failing to cover the exposed ground of a construction site that cannot be started temporarily, or failing to green, pave, or cover the exposed ground of a construction site that cannot be started for more than three months, shall be penalized by the competent department of housing and urban-rural construction of the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 116 Any violation of this Law by vehicles transporting bulk or fluid materials such as coal, garbage, waste, sand, gravel, earth, and mortar, failing to take airtight or other measures to prevent material spillage, shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 2,000 but not more than RMB 20,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the vehicles shall not be allowed to drive on the road.

Article 117 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the county level according to its responsibilities, and a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend work or business for remediation:

(1) Failing to enclose materials prone to dust, such as coal, coal gangue, coal slag, coal ash, cement, lime, gypsum, and sand;

(2) For materials prone to dust that cannot be enclosed, failing to set up tight enclosures not lower than the height of the stacked materials, or failing to take effective covering measures to prevent dust pollution;

(3) Failing to take airtight or spraying methods to control dust emissions when loading and unloading materials;

(4) Failing to take anti-ignition measures when storing materials such as coal, coal gangue, coal slag, and coal ash;

(5) Docks, mines, landfills, and disposal sites failing to take effective measures to prevent dust pollution;

(6) Enterprises and institutions listed in the catalog of toxic and hazardous air pollutants that discharge such pollutants failing to establish an environmental risk early warning system in accordance with regulations, or failing to regularly monitor discharge outlets and the surrounding environment, investigate environmental safety hazards, and take effective measures to prevent environmental risks;

(7) Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that discharge persistent organic pollutants into the atmosphere, and operators of waste incineration facilities, failing to adopt technical methods and processes conducive to reducing emissions of persistent organic pollutants and install purification devices in accordance with relevant national regulations;

(8) Failing to take measures to prevent the emission of malodorous gases.

Article 118 Any violation of this Law by operators of catering services that discharge cooking fumes failing to install fume purification facilities, improperly using fume purification facilities, or failing to take other fume purification measures, and discharging cooking fumes exceeding emission standards, shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 5,000 but not more than RMB 50,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend business for remediation.

Any violation of this Law by constructing, expanding, or renovating catering service projects that generate cooking fumes, odors, or waste gas in residential buildings, commercial-residential mixed buildings without dedicated flues, or commercial floors adjacent to residential floors within commercial-residential mixed buildings, shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be closed, and a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed.

Any violation of this Law by barbecuing food outdoors or providing venues for outdoor barbecuing during periods and in areas prohibited by the local people's government shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and the barbecue tools and illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than RMB 500 but not more than RMB 20,000 shall be imposed.

Article 119 Any violation of this Law by spraying highly toxic or acutely toxic pesticides on trees and flowers in densely populated areas, or openly burning straw, fallen leaves, and other substances that produce smoke and dust pollution, shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 500 but not more than RMB 2,000 may be imposed.

Any violation of this Law by burning asphalt, tar felt, rubber, plastic, leather, garbage, and other substances that produce toxic and hazardous smoke and malodorous gases in densely populated areas and other areas requiring special protection in accordance with the law shall be ordered to make corrections by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the people's government at the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed on the unit, and a fine of not less than RMB 500 but not more than RMB 2,000 shall be imposed on the individual.

Any violation of this Law by setting off fireworks and firecrackers during periods and in areas prohibited by the city people's government shall be penalized by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law.

Article 120 Any violation of this Law by engaging in service activities such as dry cleaning of clothing and motor vehicle repair, failing to install pollution prevention facilities such as odor and waste gas treatment devices and maintain their normal operation, thereby affecting the surrounding environment, shall be ordered to make corrections by the competent department of ecology and environment of the local people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not less than RMB 2,000 but not more than RMB 20,000 shall be imposed; if corrections are not made, the violator shall be ordered to suspend business for remediation.

Article 121 Any violation of this Law by releasing heavy pollution weather forecast or warning information to the public without authorization, if constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be penalized by the public security organ in accordance with the law.

Any violation of this Law by refusing to implement heavy pollution weather emergency measures such as stopping earthwork operations or building demolition construction at construction sites shall be fined not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 by the supervisory and administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level. Article 122: In violation of this Law, if an air pollution accident is caused, the ecological environment competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose a fine in accordance with the provisions of the second paragraph of this Article; the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons may be fined up to 50% of the income obtained from the enterprise or institution in the previous year.
For causing a general or relatively major air pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at one to three times the direct losses caused by the pollution accident; for causing a major or extraordinarily serious air pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at three to five times the direct losses caused by the pollution accident.

Article 123: In violation of this Law, if an enterprise, institution, or other producer or operator commits any of the following acts, is subject to a fine penalty, is ordered to make corrections, and refuses to do so, the administrative authority that made the penalty decision may impose a daily continuous fine at the original penalty amount starting from the day after the date of the order for correction:

(1) Discharging air pollutants without obtaining a pollutant discharge permit as required by law;

(2) Discharging air pollutants exceeding the air pollutant emission standards or exceeding the total emission control targets for key air pollutants;

(3) Discharging air pollutants by evading supervision;

(4) Failing to take effective measures to prevent dust pollution during construction or storage of materials prone to dust.

Article 124: In violation of this Law, if a whistleblower is retaliated against by means such as termination or modification of a labor contract, liability shall be borne in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

Article 125: If discharging air pollutants causes damage, tort liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 126: Local people's governments at all levels, competent departments of ecological environment of people's governments at or above the county level, and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of air environmental protection, as well as their staff, who abuse their power, neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or practice fraud, shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.
Article 127: In violation of this Law, if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 128: Air pollution prevention and control for marine engineering projects shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Marine Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 129: This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2016.
