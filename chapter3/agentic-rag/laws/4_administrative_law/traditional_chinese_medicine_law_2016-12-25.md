# Traditional Chinese Medicine Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 25, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to inherit and promote traditional Chinese medicine, ensure and advance the development of the traditional Chinese medicine cause, and protect the health of the people.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "traditional Chinese medicine" refers to the collective term for the medicine of all ethnic groups in China, including the Han and minority ethnic groups. It reflects the Chinese nation's understanding of life, health, and disease, and constitutes a medical system with a long historical tradition, unique theories, and technical methods.

**Article 3** The traditional Chinese medicine cause is an important component of China's medical and health undertakings. The State vigorously develops the traditional Chinese medicine cause, implements a policy of equal emphasis on traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine, establishes a management system suited to the characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine, and fully leverages the role of traditional Chinese medicine in China's medical and health undertakings.

The development of the traditional Chinese medicine cause shall follow the laws of its development, adhere to the combination of inheritance and innovation, maintain and give full play to the characteristics and advantages of traditional Chinese medicine, and utilize modern science and technology to promote the development of traditional Chinese medicine theory and practice.

The State encourages mutual learning, mutual supplementation, and coordinated development between traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine, allowing each to leverage its respective strengths and promoting the integration of traditional Chinese and Western medicine.

**Article 4** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the traditional Chinese medicine cause into national economic and social development plans, establish and improve the management system for traditional Chinese medicine, and coordinate efforts to advance the development of the traditional Chinese medicine cause.

**Article 5** The competent department of traditional Chinese medicine under the State Council shall be responsible for the nationwide management of traditional Chinese medicine. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for work related to the management of traditional Chinese medicine within their respective scopes of duties.

The competent departments of traditional Chinese medicine of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the management of traditional Chinese medicine within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for work related to the management of traditional Chinese medicine within their respective scopes of duties.

**Article 6** The State strengthens the construction of the traditional Chinese medicine service system, rationally plans and allocates traditional Chinese medicine service resources, and provides guarantees for citizens to obtain traditional Chinese medicine services.

The State supports social forces in investing in the traditional Chinese medicine cause and supports organizations and individuals in donating to and funding the traditional Chinese medicine cause.

**Article 7** The State develops traditional Chinese medicine education, establishes an education system for traditional Chinese medicine that meets the needs of its development, with an appropriate scale, reasonable structure, and diverse forms, and cultivates traditional Chinese medicine talents.

**Article 8** The State supports scientific research and technological development in traditional Chinese medicine, encourages innovation in traditional Chinese medicine science and technology, promotes and applies the achievements of traditional Chinese medicine science and technology, protects the intellectual property rights of traditional Chinese medicine, and enhances the level of traditional Chinese medicine science and technology.

**Article 9** The State supports international exchanges and cooperation in traditional Chinese medicine and promotes the international dissemination and application of traditional Chinese medicine.

**Article 10** Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the traditional Chinese medicine cause shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II Traditional Chinese Medicine Services

**Article 11** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the establishment of traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions into the plan for the establishment of medical institutions, establish traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions of an appropriate scale, and support the development of medical institutions with traditional Chinese medicine characteristics and advantages.

The merger or dissolution of a traditional Chinese medicine medical institution established by the government, or a change in its nature as a traditional Chinese medicine medical institution, shall be subject to the opinions of the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the people's government at the next higher level.

**Article 12** General hospitals, maternal and child health care institutions established by the government, and qualified specialized hospitals, community health service centers, and township health centers shall set up traditional Chinese medicine departments.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to enhance the capacity of community health service stations and village clinics to provide traditional Chinese medicine services.

**Article 13** The State supports social forces in establishing traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions.

Traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions established by social forces shall enjoy the same rights as those established by the government in terms of access, practice, basic medical insurance, scientific research and teaching, and professional title evaluation of medical personnel.

**Article 14** The establishment of a traditional Chinese medicine medical institution shall go through the approval procedures in accordance with the relevant State regulations on the management of medical institutions and comply with the relevant provisions on the management of medical institutions.

For the establishment of a traditional Chinese medicine clinic, the name, address, scope of diagnosis and treatment, and staffing of the clinic shall be reported to the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the local people's government at the county level for filing before practice activities can be carried out. A traditional Chinese medicine clinic shall publicly display the scope of its diagnosis and treatment, the names of its traditional Chinese medicine physicians, and their scope of practice in a conspicuous location of the clinic, and shall not carry out medical activities beyond the filed scope. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine under the State Council and submitted to the health administrative department under the State Council for review and issuance.

**Article 15** Persons engaged in traditional Chinese medicine medical activities shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Licensed Physicians, pass the traditional Chinese medicine physician qualification examination to obtain the qualification of a traditional Chinese medicine physician and undergo practice registration. The content of the traditional Chinese medicine physician qualification examination shall reflect the characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine.

Persons who have studied traditional Chinese medicine through a master-apprentice relationship or have many years of practice and possess genuine medical expertise may, upon recommendation by at least two traditional Chinese medicine physicians and after passing the practical skills and effectiveness assessment organized by the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, obtain the qualification of a traditional Chinese medicine physician. After undergoing practice registration based on the assessment content, they may engage in traditional Chinese medicine medical activities within the registered scope of practice, either by opening their own practice or within a medical institution. The competent department of traditional Chinese medicine under the State Council shall, based on the safety risks of traditional Chinese medicine technical methods, formulate a classified assessment method for the persons specified in this paragraph and submit it to the health administrative department under the State Council for review and issuance.

**Article 16** Traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions shall primarily staff professional and technical personnel in traditional Chinese medicine and mainly provide traditional Chinese medicine services. Traditional Chinese medicine physicians who have obtained physician qualifications through examinations may, in accordance with relevant State regulations and after training and passing assessments, adopt modern scientific and technical methods related to their profession in their practice activities. The adoption of modern scientific and technical methods in medical activities shall be conducive to maintaining and giving full play to the characteristics and advantages of traditional Chinese medicine.

Community health service centers, township health centers, community health service stations, and qualified village clinics shall reasonably allocate professional and technical personnel in traditional Chinese medicine and apply and promote appropriate traditional Chinese medicine technical methods.

**Article 17** The provision of traditional Chinese medicine services shall be guided by traditional Chinese medicine theory, utilize traditional Chinese medicine technical methods, and comply with the basic requirements for traditional Chinese medicine services formulated by the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine under the State Council.

**Article 18** People's governments at or above the county level shall develop traditional Chinese medicine prevention and health care services and incorporate them into the basic public health service projects for coordinated implementation in accordance with relevant State regulations.

People's governments at or above the county level shall give full play to the role of traditional Chinese medicine in emergency response to public health emergencies and strengthen the reserve of traditional Chinese medicine emergency materials, equipment, facilities, technology, and human resources.

Medical and health institutions shall actively apply traditional Chinese medicine theory and technical methods in disease prevention and control.

**Article 19** The release of traditional Chinese medicine medical advertisements by medical institutions shall be subject to the review and approval of the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the institution is located; no advertisement shall be released without review and approval. The content of the released traditional Chinese medicine medical advertisement shall be consistent with the approved content and comply with the relevant provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 20** The competent departments of traditional Chinese medicine of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of traditional Chinese medicine services, with the following matters as the focus of supervision and inspection:

(1) Whether traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions and traditional Chinese medicine physicians carry out medical activities beyond the prescribed scope;

(2) Whether the provision of traditional Chinese medicine services complies with the basic requirements for traditional Chinese medicine services formulated by the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine under the State Council;

(3) Whether the release of traditional Chinese medicine medical advertisements complies with the provisions of this Law.

When the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine conducts supervision and inspection in accordance with the law, the relevant units and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct.

## Chapter III Protection and Development of Traditional Chinese Medicine

**Article 21** The State formulates technical specifications and standards for the planting, breeding, collection, storage, and primary processing of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, strengthens the quality supervision and management of the entire production and circulation process of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, and ensures the quality and safety of traditional Chinese medicinal materials.

**Article 22** The State encourages the development of standardized planting and breeding of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, strictly manages the use of agricultural inputs such as pesticides and fertilizers, prohibits the use of highly toxic and highly hazardous pesticides in the planting of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, supports the breeding of improved varieties of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, and improves the quality of traditional Chinese medicinal materials.

**Article 23** The State establishes an evaluation system for authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials, supports the selection and breeding of varieties of authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials, supports the construction of production bases for authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials, strengthens the ecological environment protection of production bases for authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials, and encourages the adoption of measures such as geographical indication product protection to protect authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials.

"Authentic traditional Chinese medicinal materials" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to traditional Chinese medicinal materials that have been selected through long-term clinical application of traditional Chinese medicine, produced in specific regions, and have better quality and efficacy, stable quality, and higher recognition compared to the same type of traditional Chinese medicinal materials produced in other regions.

**Article 24** The drug regulatory department under the State Council shall organize and strengthen the monitoring of the quality of traditional Chinese medicinal materials and regularly publish the monitoring results to the public. Relevant departments under the State Council shall assist in the work related to the quality monitoring of traditional Chinese medicinal materials.

The collection, storage, and primary processing of traditional Chinese medicinal materials shall comply with the relevant national technical specifications, standards, and management regulations.The State encourages the development of a modern distribution system for Chinese medicinal materials, improving the technical standards for packaging, storage, and other aspects, and establishing a traceability system for the circulation of Chinese medicinal materials. Pharmaceutical manufacturers purchasing Chinese medicinal materials shall establish a system for recording incoming goods inspections. Dealers in Chinese medicinal materials shall establish systems for recording incoming goods inspections and purchase and sales, and shall indicate the place of origin of the Chinese medicinal materials.

Article 25 The State protects medicinal wildlife and plant resources, conducts dynamic monitoring and regular surveys of these resources, establishes a germplasm gene bank for medicinal wildlife and plant resources, encourages the development of artificial cultivation and breeding, and supports the protection, breeding, and related research of precious and endangered medicinal wildlife and plants in accordance with the law.

Article 26 Traditional Chinese medicine physicians practicing in village medical institutions and village doctors who possess knowledge of Chinese medicinal materials and the ability to identify them may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, self-cultivate, self-harvest locally produced Chinese medicinal materials and use them in their practice.

Article 27 The State protects traditional processing techniques and technologies for prepared slices of Chinese medicinal materials (decoction pieces), supports the application of traditional techniques in processing decoction pieces, and encourages the use of modern science and technology to conduct research on processing techniques for decoction pieces.

Article 28 For decoction pieces that are not available on the market, medical institutions may process and use them within the institution based on the needs of prescriptions issued by their physicians. Medical institutions shall comply with relevant regulations on the processing of decoction pieces, be responsible for the quality of the decoction pieces they process, and ensure drug safety. Medical institutions processing decoction pieces shall file a record with the drug regulatory department of the people's government at the city level with districts where the institution is located.

Based on clinical medication needs, medical institutions may further process decoction pieces using prescriptions issued by their physicians.

Article 29 The State encourages and supports the research, development, and production of new traditional Chinese medicines.

The State protects traditional Chinese medicine processing technologies and techniques, supports the production of traditional dosage forms of Chinese patent medicines, and encourages the use of modern science and technology to research and develop traditional Chinese patent medicines.

Article 30 For the production of compound preparations of traditional Chinese medicine derived from ancient classical prescriptions that meet the conditions stipulated by the State, only non-clinical safety research materials need to be provided when applying for drug approval numbers. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in conjunction with the competent traditional Chinese medicine department.

The term "ancient classical prescriptions" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to prescriptions recorded in ancient traditional Chinese medicine classics that are still widely used today, have definite efficacy, and possess distinct characteristics and advantages. The specific catalogue shall be formulated by the competent traditional Chinese medicine department of the State Council in conjunction with the drug regulatory department.

Article 31 The State encourages medical institutions to prepare and use traditional Chinese medicine preparations based on their clinical medication needs, supports the use of traditional techniques in preparing traditional Chinese medicine preparations, and supports the development of new traditional Chinese medicines based on traditional Chinese medicine preparations.

Medical institutions preparing traditional Chinese medicine preparations shall obtain a Medical Institution Preparation License in accordance with the provisions of the Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, or entrust a pharmaceutical manufacturer that has obtained a Drug Manufacturing License or another medical institution that has obtained a Medical Institution Preparation License to prepare the preparations. Entrusting the preparation of traditional Chinese medicine preparations shall be filed with the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the entrusting party is located.

Medical institutions are responsible for the quality of the traditional Chinese medicine preparations they prepare; if the preparation is entrusted, the entrusting party and the entrusted party shall bear corresponding responsibilities for the quality of the prepared traditional Chinese medicine preparations respectively.
Article 32 Varieties of traditional Chinese medicine preparations prepared by medical institutions shall obtain preparation approval numbers in accordance with the law. However, for varieties of traditional Chinese medicine preparations prepared solely using traditional techniques, they can be prepared after filing with the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the medical institution is located, without needing to obtain a preparation approval number.

Medical institutions shall strengthen the adverse reaction monitoring of the filed traditional Chinese medicine preparation varieties and report in accordance with relevant state regulations. Drug regulatory departments shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the preparation and use of filed traditional Chinese medicine preparation varieties.

## Chapter 4: Cultivation of Traditional Chinese Medicine Talents

Article 33 Traditional Chinese medicine education shall follow the laws governing the growth of traditional Chinese medicine talents, focus on traditional Chinese medicine content, reflect the cultural characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine, and emphasize the combination of classical traditional Chinese medicine theory with clinical practice, as well as the integration of modern educational methods with traditional educational methods.

Article 34 The State improves the school education system for traditional Chinese medicine, supporting the development of higher education institutions, secondary vocational schools, and other educational institutions dedicated to traditional Chinese medicine education.

The training objectives, duration of study, teaching forms, teaching content, teaching evaluation, and academic level evaluation standards of traditional Chinese medicine school education shall reflect the disciplinary characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine and conform to the development laws of the traditional Chinese medicine discipline.

Article 35 The State develops the master-apprentice education system for traditional Chinese medicine, supporting traditional Chinese medicine physicians and traditional Chinese medicine professional technicians with rich clinical experience and technical expertise to take on apprentices and impart knowledge during their practice and business activities, teaching traditional Chinese medicine theories, techniques, and methods, and cultivating traditional Chinese medicine professional technicians.

Article 36 The State strengthens the cultivation and training of traditional Chinese medicine physicians and traditional Chinese medicine professional technicians in urban and rural grassroots areas.

The State develops integrated Chinese and Western medicine education, cultivating high-level talents in integrated Chinese and Western medicine.

Article 37 The competent traditional Chinese medicine departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize continuing education in traditional Chinese medicine, strengthening the training of medical personnel, especially grassroots medical personnel in urban and rural areas, in basic traditional Chinese medicine knowledge and skills.

Traditional Chinese medicine professional technicians shall participate in continuing education as stipulated, and their employing institutions shall create conditions for them to receive continuing education.

## Chapter 5: Scientific Research in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Article 38 The State encourages scientific research institutions, higher education institutions, medical institutions, and pharmaceutical manufacturers to conduct scientific research in traditional Chinese medicine using modern science and technology and traditional research methods, strengthen research on the integration of Chinese and Western medicine, and promote the inheritance and innovation of traditional Chinese medicine theories, techniques, and methods.

Article 39 The State takes measures to support the collation, research, and utilization of ancient traditional Chinese medicine literature, the academic thoughts and diagnostic and treatment experiences of famous traditional Chinese medicine experts, and folk traditional Chinese medicine techniques and methods.

The State encourages organizations and individuals to donate traditional Chinese medicine literature, secret prescriptions, proven prescriptions, diagnostic and treatment methods, and techniques that have scientific research and clinical application value.

Article 40 The State establishes and improves a scientific and technological innovation system, evaluation system, and management system that conforms to the characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine, promoting the progress and innovation of traditional Chinese medicine science and technology.

Article 41 The State takes measures to strengthen scientific research on the basic theories of traditional Chinese medicine and methods of syndrome differentiation and treatment, the prevention and treatment of common diseases, frequently occurring diseases, chronic diseases, major difficult and complex diseases, and major infectious diseases using traditional Chinese medicine, as well as other projects that significantly promote the development of traditional Chinese medicine theory and practice.

## Chapter 6: Inheritance and Cultural Dissemination of Traditional Chinese Medicine

Article 42 For traditional Chinese medicine theories, techniques, and methods of significant academic value, the competent traditional Chinese medicine departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level shall organize the selection of traditional Chinese medicine academic inheritance projects and inheritors within their administrative regions, and provide necessary conditions for inheritance activities. Inheritors shall carry out inheritance activities, cultivate successor talents, and collect, collate, and properly preserve relevant academic materials. If the project is a representative item of intangible cultural heritage, inheritance activities shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Intangible Cultural Heritage Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 43 The State establishes a protection database, protection list, and protection system for traditional knowledge of traditional Chinese medicine.

Holders of traditional knowledge of traditional Chinese medicine have the right to inherit and use the traditional knowledge they hold, and have the right to informed consent and benefit sharing regarding others' acquisition and use of the traditional knowledge they hold.

The State provides special protection for the composition of traditional Chinese medicine prescriptions and production processes that are legally determined to be state secrets.

Article 44 The State develops traditional Chinese medicine health preservation and healthcare services, supporting social forces in establishing standardized traditional Chinese medicine health preservation and healthcare institutions. The norms and standards for traditional Chinese medicine health preservation and healthcare services shall be formulated by the competent traditional Chinese medicine department of the State Council.

Article 45 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the promotion of traditional Chinese medicine culture, popularize traditional Chinese medicine knowledge, and encourage organizations and individuals to create traditional Chinese medicine cultural and popular science works.

Article 46 Activities for promoting traditional Chinese medicine culture and popularizing knowledge shall comply with relevant state regulations. No organization or individual may make false or exaggerated claims about traditional Chinese medicine, or use the name of traditional Chinese medicine to seek improper benefits.

Media such as radio, television, newspapers, and the internet shall engage traditional Chinese medicine professional technicians when conducting traditional Chinese medicine knowledge promotion.

## Chapter 7: Safeguard Measures

Article 47 People's governments at or above the county level shall provide policy support and conditional guarantees for the development of the traditional Chinese medicine industry, and include funds for the development of the traditional Chinese medicine industry in their fiscal budgets.

When people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments formulate medical and health policies such as basic medical insurance payment policies and drug policies, the competent traditional Chinese medicine department shall participate, focusing on leveraging the advantages of traditional Chinese medicine, and supporting the provision and utilization of traditional Chinese medicine services.

Article 48 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, in accordance with statutory price management authority, reasonably determine the charging items and standards for traditional Chinese medicine medical services, reflecting the cost and professional technical value of traditional Chinese medicine medical services.

Article 49 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with state regulations, include qualified traditional Chinese medicine medical institutions within the scope of designated medical institutions for basic medical insurance, and include qualified traditional Chinese medicine diagnosis and treatment items, decoction pieces, Chinese patent medicines, and traditional Chinese medicine preparations of medical institutions within the scope of payment by the basic medical insurance fund.Article 50 The State shall strengthen the development of a standard system for traditional Chinese medicine, establishing and promptly revising standards for technical requirements that need to be unified based on the characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine.

National and industry standards for traditional Chinese medicine shall be formulated or revised by the relevant departments of the State Council in accordance with their respective responsibilities, and shall be published on their websites for free public access.

The State shall promote the establishment of an international standard system for traditional Chinese medicine.Article 51: In conducting reviews, evaluations, or appraisals related to traditional Chinese medicine as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations, a specialized organization for TCM review, evaluation, and appraisal shall be established, or TCM experts shall be included in the process.

Article 52: The State shall take measures to increase support for the inheritance, innovation, application development, and talent cultivation of ethnic minority medicine, strengthen the construction of ethnic minority medical institutions and physician teams, and promote and regulate the development of ethnic minority medicine.

## Chapter 8: Legal Liability

Article 53: If the competent departments of traditional Chinese medicine of people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments fail to perform their duties as stipulated in this Law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at a higher level shall order them to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 54: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a TCM clinic carries out medical activities beyond its registered scope, the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the people's government at the county level where it is located shall order it to make corrections, confiscate its illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order the suspension of its practice activities.

If a TCM clinic is ordered to suspend its practice activities, the directly responsible supervisor shall not engage in management work in medical institutions within five years from the date of the penalty decision. If a medical institution employs the aforementioned person who is prohibited from engaging in management work to perform such duties, the original issuing authority shall revoke its practice license, or the original filing authority shall order it to suspend its practice activities.

Article 55: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a TCM physician who has obtained physician qualifications through assessment engages in medical activities beyond the scope of their registered practice, the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the suspension of practice activities for a period of not less than six months but not more than one year, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their practice certificate shall be revoked.

Article 56: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if the establishment of a TCM clinic, the processing of TCM decoction pieces, or the entrustment of the preparation of TCM preparations should have been filed but was not, or false materials were provided at the time of filing, the competent department of traditional Chinese medicine and the drug regulatory authority shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections, confiscate illegal gains, impose a fine of not more than 30,000 yuan, and publicly announce the relevant information; if corrections are refused, they shall order the suspension of practice activities or the suspension of the processing of TCM decoction pieces or the entrustment of the preparation of TCM preparations, and the directly responsible personnel shall not engage in TCM-related activities for five years.

If a medical institution prepares TCM preparations using traditional techniques without filing in accordance with this Law, or fails to prepare the preparations in accordance with the requirements specified in the filed materials, it shall be punished as for the production of counterfeit drugs.

Article 57: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if the content of a published TCM medical advertisement does not match the content approved after review, the original review authority shall revoke the approval document for the advertisement and shall not accept the medical institution's application for advertisement review within one year.

In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a published TCM medical advertisement has illegal acts other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 58: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if highly toxic or highly hazardous pesticides are used in the cultivation of TCM medicinal materials, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and regulations; if the circumstances are serious, the public security authority may impose detention for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel.

Article 59: In violation of the provisions of this Law, if personal or property damage is caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9: Supplementary Provisions

Article 60: For matters concerning the management of traditional Chinese medicine not provided for in this Law, the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Licensed Physicians, the Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws and administrative regulations shall apply.

The management of traditional Chinese medicine in the military shall be organized and implemented by the military health authority in accordance with this Law and relevant military regulations.

Article 61: Ethnic autonomous areas may, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Regional Ethnic Autonomy and this Law, and in light of actual conditions, formulate measures to promote and regulate the development of local ethnic minority medicine.

Article 62: Blind persons who have obtained the qualifications of blind medical massage personnel in accordance with relevant state regulations may provide medical massage services by opening their own practices or within medical institutions.

Article 63: This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2017.
