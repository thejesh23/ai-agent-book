# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Military Facilities

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on February 23, 1990

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Military Facilities adopted at the 9th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 27, 2014

Revised at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on June 10, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure the safety of military facilities, guarantee their operational effectiveness and the normal conduct of military activities, strengthen national defense modernization, consolidate national defense, and resist aggression.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "military facilities" refer to the following buildings, sites, and equipment directly used by the State for military purposes:

(1) Command organs, above-ground and underground command works, and combat works;

(2) Military airfields, ports, and docks;

(3) Barracks, training grounds, and test ranges;

(4) Military caves and warehouses;

(5) Military information infrastructure, military reconnaissance, navigation, and observation stations, and military surveying, navigation, and aid-to-navigation markers;

(6) Military roads and railway sidings, military power transmission lines, and military pipelines for oil, water, and gas;

(7) Border and coastal defense control facilities;

(8) Other military facilities as prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

The military facilities specified in the preceding paragraph include temporary facilities necessary for the military to perform tasks.

**Article 3** The protection of military facilities shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China. People's governments at all levels and military organs shall jointly protect military facilities and safeguard national defense interests.

The State Council and the Central Military Commission shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, manage the protection of military facilities nationwide. Local people's governments at all levels, together with relevant military organs, shall manage the protection of military facilities within their respective administrative regions.

Relevant military organs shall, in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures, propose requirements for the protection of military facilities that need to be implemented by local people's governments. Local people's governments shall, together with relevant military organs, formulate specific protection measures and implement them.

In localities where military facilities are located, relevant military organs and local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a coordination mechanism for the protection of military facilities between the military and local authorities, cooperate with each other, supervise and inspect the protection of military facilities, and coordinate to resolve issues arising from the protection of military facilities.

**Article 4** All organizations and citizens of the People's Republic of China have the obligation to protect military facilities.

No organization or individual may damage or endanger military facilities.

Any organization or individual has the right to report or accuse any act that damages or endangers military facilities.

**Article 5** The State shall make overall plans for economic construction, social development, and the protection of military facilities, promoting coordination between economic and social development and the protection of military facilities.

**Article 6** The State adopts a policy of classified protection and ensuring the priority of key military facilities. The classification and protection standards for military facilities shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 7** The State shall adopt corresponding support policies and measures for localities where economic development is significantly affected due to the presence of military facilities. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 8** Organizations and individuals that make outstanding contributions to the protection of military facilities shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter II Delimitation of Military Restricted Zones and Military Management Zones

**Article 9** Military restricted zones and military management zones shall be delimited based on the nature, function, security and confidentiality requirements, and operational effectiveness needs of military facilities. The specific delimitation standards and determination procedures shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

For the purposes of this Law, "military restricted zones" refer to military areas delimited in accordance with legal procedures and standards where important military facilities are located, or where security and confidentiality requirements for military facilities are high and significant risk factors exist, requiring the State to adopt special measures for key protection.

For the purposes of this Law, "military management zones" refer to military areas delimited in accordance with legal procedures and standards where relatively important military facilities are located, or where security and confidentiality requirements for military facilities are relatively high and considerable risk factors exist, requiring the State to adopt special measures for protection.

**Article 10** Military restricted zones and military management zones shall be determined by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, or by relevant military organs in accordance with the provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

The revocation or modification of military restricted zones and military management zones shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 11** The scope of military restricted zones and military management zones on land and in waters shall be jointly delimited by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and relevant military organs at or above the corps level, or jointly delimited by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, relevant departments of the State Council, and relevant military organs at or above the corps level. The scope of air military restricted zones and particularly important land and water military restricted zones shall be delimited by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Adjustments to the scope of military restricted zones and military management zones shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 12** Military restricted zones and military management zones shall have signboards erected by local people's governments at or above the county level in a style uniformly prescribed by the State.

**Article 13** The delimitation or adjustment of the scope of military restricted zones and military management zones shall, on the premise of ensuring the security, confidentiality, and operational effectiveness of military facilities, take into account economic construction, ecological and environmental protection, and the production and daily life of local residents.

Where the delimitation or adjustment of the scope of military restricted zones and military management zones is necessary due to the construction of military facilities, it shall be completed before the commencement of the military facility construction project, unless approved by a military organ at or above the theater level.

**Article 14** The delimitation or adjustment of the scope of military restricted zones and military management zones that requires the expropriation or requisition of immovable property such as land and houses, the submergence of mineral resources, or the use of sea areas or airspace shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

**Article 15** Where temporary military facilities set up by the military for task performance require the delimitation of temporary military restricted zones or temporary military management zones on land or in waters, they shall be jointly delimited by local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant military organs at or above the regiment level, and each shall report to its superior organ for record. Where the functions and powers of relevant maritime administrative agencies are involved, their opinions shall be sought before delimitation. After delimitation, the local people's government at or above the county level or the relevant maritime administrative agency shall make a public announcement.

After the military completes its tasks, the delimited temporary military restricted zones or temporary military management zones on land or in waters shall be promptly revoked in accordance with the procedures specified in the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter III Protection of Military Restricted Zones

**Article 16** The management unit of a military restricted zone shall, based on specific conditions and within the delimited scope, construct walls, set up barbed wire, or other obstacles for land military restricted zones, and set up obstacles or boundary markers for water military restricted zones.

Where it is difficult to set up obstacles or boundary markers in the actual waters of a water military restricted zone, the relevant maritime administrative agency shall publicly announce the location and boundaries of the water military restricted zone. Military restricted zones in sea areas shall be marked on nautical charts.

**Article 17** Personnel, vehicles, vessels, etc., other than those of the management unit of a land or water military restricted zone are prohibited from entering the military restricted zone. Aircraft are prohibited from flying at low altitudes over land or water military restricted zones. Photography, videography, audio recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, and describing of military restricted zones are prohibited, unless approved by the relevant military organ.

Aircraft are prohibited from entering air military restricted zones, unless approved in accordance with relevant State regulations.

The use of materials obtained from photography, videography, audio recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, and describing of military restricted zones shall be approved by the relevant military organ.

**Article 18** Within land military restricted zones, the construction or installation of non-military facilities and the development and utilization of underground space are prohibited, unless approved by a military organ at or above the theater level.

Within water military restricted zones, the construction or installation of non-military facilities is prohibited, and activities such as aquaculture, fishing, and other activities that hinder the movement of military vessels or endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of military facilities are prohibited.

**Article 19** Where protective measures taken within land or water military restricted zones are insufficient to ensure the security, confidentiality, and operational effectiveness of military facilities, or where military facilities within land or water military restricted zones have significant risk factors, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and the relevant military organ, or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the relevant department of the State Council, and the relevant military organ may, while jointly delimiting the scope of the land or water military restricted zone, jointly delimit a safety control zone outside the restricted zone and set up safety warning signs along its outer edge.

Safety warning signs shall be erected by local people's governments at or above the county level in a style uniformly prescribed by the State, and their locations shall be jointly determined by the management unit of the military restricted zone and the local people's government at or above the county level.

Where it is difficult to set up safety warning signs in the actual waters of the safety control zone outside a water military restricted zone, the provisions of Article 16, paragraph 2 of this Law shall apply.

**Article 20** The delimitation of a safety control zone outside a land or water military restricted zone shall not change the ownership of the original land, land attachments, or waters. Within the safety control zone outside a land or water military restricted zone, local residents may continue their normal production and daily life, but shall not engage in blasting, shooting, or other activities that endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of military facilities.

Where the delimitation of a safety control zone outside a military restricted zone affects the exercise of rights by the owner or usufructuary of immovable property, compensation shall be provided in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter IV Protection of Military Management Zones

**Article 21** The management unit of a military management zone shall, based on specific conditions and within the delimited scope, construct walls, set up barbed wire, or boundary markers for the military management zone.

**Article 22** Personnel, vehicles, vessels, etc., other than those of the management unit of a military management zone, entering the military management zone, or engaging in photography, videography, audio recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, and describing of the military management zone, must obtain approval from the management unit of the military management zone.

**Article 23** Within land military management zones, the construction or installation of non-military facilities and the development and utilization of underground space are prohibited, unless approved by a military organ at or above the corps level.

Within water military management zones, aquaculture is prohibited; without the approval of a military organ at or above the corps level, non-military facilities shall not be constructed or installed; fishing or other activities shall not affect the combat readiness, training, duty, or other operations of military vessels.

**Article 24** For waters of military-civilian joint-use ports designated as military management zones, separate management by the military and local authorities shall be implemented; within waters managed by local authorities, the construction of new non-military facilities must obtain prior consent from the management unit of the military facility.

Measures for the management of military-civilian joint-use airports, ports, and docks designated as military management zones shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter V Protection of Military Facilities Not Included in Military Restricted Zones or Military Management Zones

**Article 25** For military facilities not included in military restricted zones or military management zones, the management unit of the military facility shall take measures to protect them; management units at or above the regiment level may also entrust the protection to the local people's government.

**Article 26** Activities such as quarrying, earth extraction, and blasting conducted within a certain distance of military facilities not included in military restricted zones or military management zones shall not endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of the military facilities.

**Article 27** A safety protection zone shall be delimited around combat works not included in military restricted zones or military management zones. The safety protection zone for combat works shall be jointly delimited by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and the relevant military organ, or jointly delimited by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the relevant department of the State Council, and the relevant military organ, based on the nature of the combat works, the terrain, and local economic construction and social development conditions. In areas where combat works are relatively concentrated, the safety protection zones for combat works may be delimited in contiguous areas. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall set up boundary markers for the safety protection zones of combat works in accordance with relevant regulations.

The revocation or adjustment of safety protection zones for combat works shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 28** The delimitation of safety protection zones for combat works shall not change the ownership of the original land and land attachments. Within the safety protection zones for combat works, local residents may continue their normal production and daily life, but shall not engage in mountain quarrying, mining, or blasting; activities such as constructing buildings, structures, roads, carrying out farmland water conservancy infrastructure construction, and felling trees shall not endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of the combat works.

Where the delimitation of safety protection zones for combat works affects the exercise of rights by the owner or usufructuary of immovable property, compensation shall be provided in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

It is prohibited to open sealed combat works without authorization, to damage the camouflage of combat works, or to block access routes to combat works. Without the approval of the superior military organ at or above the division level of the combat works management unit, photography, videography, audio recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, and describing of the combat works are prohibited, and the storage of non-military materials and equipment or the conduct of production activities such as planting and aquaculture within the combat works are prohibited.

Where new projects and construction projects are truly unavoidable and must pass through combat works, an application for demolition, relocation, or reconstruction of the combat works shall be submitted in accordance with relevant State regulations; if the application is not approved, the combat works shall not be demolished, relocated, or reconstructed.

**Article 29** Within the clearance protection area of a military airfield, the construction of buildings, structures, or other facilities exceeding the airfield clearance standards is prohibited, and activities affecting flight safety and the operational effectiveness of airfield aid-to-navigation facilities are prohibited.

The management unit of a military airfield shall regularly inspect the clearance protection situation of the airfield. If it discovers that constructed buildings, structures, or other facilities exceed the clearance protection standards of the military airfield, it shall promptly report to the relevant military organ and the competent department of the local people's government. The relevant military organ and the competent department of the local people's government shall promptly handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 30** Relevant military organs shall inform local people's governments of the relevant situation and requirements concerning the clearance protection of local military airfields.

Local people's governments shall inform relevant military organs of local territorial spatial plans and construction plans for tall buildings that may affect the clearance protection of military airfields.

Local people's governments shall formulate protection measures and urge relevant units to set up flight obstruction markers for tall buildings, structures, or other facilities within the clearance protection area of military airfields.

**Article 31** The clearance protection work for military-civilian joint-use airfields, as well as retained old airfields and helicopter landing pads managed by the military, shall apply the relevant provisions on the clearance protection of military airfields.

The clearance protection work for highway airstrips shall be carried out with reference to the relevant provisions on the clearance protection of military airfields.

**Article 32** Local people's governments at all levels and relevant military organs shall adopt methods such as entrusted guarding and segmented responsibility to implement military-civilian joint defense to protect the safety of military pipelines.

Underground military pipelines shall be marked with route markers or permanent signs, and sections and parts prone to damage shall be equipped with signboards. Submarine military pipelines whose specific locations, boundaries, and routes have been publicly announced shall be marked on nautical charts.

**Article 33** Within the electromagnetic environment protection zone of fixed military radio facilities, the construction or installation of equipment and electromagnetic obstacles affecting the operational effectiveness of fixed military radio facilities is prohibited, and activities affecting the electromagnetic environment of fixed military radio facilities are prohibited.

Protective measures for the electromagnetic environment of fixed military radio facilities shall be jointly determined by the military and civilian radio management agencies in accordance with relevant State regulations and standards on radio management.

The protection of the electromagnetic environment of fixed radio facilities within military restricted zones and military management zones shall be subject to the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs.

Where the protection of the electromagnetic environment of fixed military radio facilities involves radio management matters between military and non-military systems, the relevant State regulations on radio management shall apply.

**Article 34** Without the approval of the State Council and the Central Military Commission or an organ authorized by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, border and coastal defense control facilities shall not be demolished or moved, and civilian facilities shall not be erected or installed on border and coastal defense control facilities. Construction projects arranged around border and coastal defense control facilities shall not endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of these facilities.

**Article 35** No organization or individual may damage or move military surveying markers without authorization. Construction projects arranged around military surveying markers shall not endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of these markers.

The protection of military surveying markers shall be carried out in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter VI Management Responsibilities

**Article 36** When local people's governments at or above the county level formulate national economic and social development plans or arrange construction projects that may affect the protection of military facilities, and when relevant departments of the State Council or local people's governments formulate plans such as territorial spatial plans, they shall take into account the needs of military facility protection and, in accordance with regulations, seek written opinions from relevant military organs. When necessary, local people's governments may, together with relevant departments and relevant military organs, assess the construction projects.

When relevant departments of the State Council or relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level approve the construction projects specified in the preceding paragraph, they shall review the status of seeking opinions from military organs; if opinions from military organs have not been sought as required, they shall require supplementary consultation; if changes occurring during the approval process to the content of the construction project may affect the protection of military facilities, opinions from relevant military organs shall be sought again.

Relevant military organs shall provide a written reply within thirty days from the date of receiving the consultation letter; if it is necessary to seek instructions from a superior military organ or to conduct surveys, measurements, or tests, the reply time may be appropriately extended, but generally shall not exceed ninety days.

**Article 37** When the military formulates military facility construction plans or organizes military facility project construction, it shall consider the needs of local economic construction, ecological and environmental protection, and social development, comply with the overall requirements of plans such as territorial spatial plans, and conduct security and confidentiality environmental assessments and environmental impact assessments. Where territorial spatial plans or other plans are involved, opinions from relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments shall be sought, and efforts shall be made to avoid ecological protection red lines, nature reserves, local economic construction hotspots, and densely populated civilian facility areas. Where avoidance is truly impossible and the demolition or relocation of production and living facilities is necessary, it shall be carried out in accordance with the law.**Article 38** When arranging construction projects or developing tourist attractions, local people's governments at or above the county level shall avoid military facilities. If avoidance is truly impossible and military facilities need to be dismantled, relocated, or converted to civilian use, the matter shall be negotiated between the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or the relevant department of the State Council, and the theater-level military organ, and shall be reported to the State Council and the Central Military Commission, or an organ authorized by them, for approval. If military facilities need to be altered, the relevant military organ shall grant approval.

If military facilities are dismantled, relocated, altered, or converted to civilian use for the reasons specified in the preceding paragraph, the local people's government that proposed the requirement shall provide policy support or financial subsidies to the relevant military organ in accordance with relevant regulations. If the relocation or alteration of military facilities involves the use of land, sea areas, or islands, the local people's government shall handle the relevant procedures in a timely manner in accordance with the law.

**Article 39** If military facilities lose their operational effectiveness due to adjustments in military missions, changes in the surrounding environment, natural damage, or other reasons, and do not need to be restored or rebuilt, the management unit of the military facility shall promptly report to the State Council and the Central Military Commission, or an organ authorized by them, for approval in accordance with prescribed procedures, and shall dismantle them or convert them to civilian use.

After the military mission is completed, temporary military facilities that were set up shall be dismantled promptly.

**Article 40** The joint military-civilian use of military airfields and ports requires approval from the State Council and the Central Military Commission. The joint military-civilian use of military docks requires approval from the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or the relevant department of the State Council, in consultation with the theater-level military organ.

**Article 41** For military restricted zones, military management zones, and military facilities not designated as military restricted zones or military management zones, local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in conjunction with the management units of the military facilities, formulate specific protection measures, which may be promulgated and implemented.

The specific protection measures for military facilities within military restricted zones and military management zones shall be submitted for approval together with the plan for delineating the boundaries of the military restricted zones and military management zones.

**Article 42** Military organs at all levels shall strictly fulfill their duties to protect military facilities, educate military personnel to cherish military facilities, maintain the confidentiality of military facilities, establish and improve rules and regulations for the protection of military facilities, and supervise, inspect, and resolve issues in the protection of military facilities.

Relevant military organs shall support and cooperate with law enforcement and judicial activities related to the protection of military facilities.

**Article 43** Management units of military facilities shall diligently implement the relevant rules and regulations for the protection of military facilities, establish archives for military facilities, and inspect and maintain military facilities.

Management units of military facilities shall implement security monitoring and technical prevention measures for important parts of military facilities, and shall promptly upgrade and improve them based on the needs of military facility protection and technological progress.

Management units of military facilities shall not use military facilities for non-military purposes, except for emergency tasks such as emergency rescue and disaster relief.

**Article 44** Management units of military facilities shall understand and be aware of the construction projects and other situations surrounding the military facilities. If they discover any potential harm to the safety and operational effectiveness of the military facilities, they shall promptly report to the relevant military organs and the competent departments of the local people's government, and cooperate with the relevant departments in handling the matter in accordance with the law.

**Article 45** Management units of military restricted zones and military management zones shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, protect the ecological environment, natural resources, and cultural relics within the military restricted zones and military management zones.

**Article 46** When necessary, management units of military facilities shall provide location information on underground and underwater military pipelines to local people's governments at or above the county level. When local construction is carried out, the local people's government shall protect the underground and underwater military pipelines.

**Article 47** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen education on national defense and the protection of military facilities, so that all citizens enhance their awareness of national defense, protect military facilities, maintain the confidentiality of military facilities, and stop acts that damage or endanger military facilities.

**Article 48** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in conjunction with the relevant military organs, regularly organize inspections and assessments of the protection of military facilities within their administrative regions, urge the rectification of hidden dangers and problems affecting the protection of military facilities within a specified time limit, and improve measures for the protection of military facilities.

**Article 49** The state implements a target responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for the protection of military facilities, incorporating the completion of targets for the protection of military facilities as part of the assessment and evaluation of local people's governments, relevant military organs, management units of military facilities, and their responsible persons.

**Article 50** If military restricted zones or military management zones require the assistance of public security organs in maintaining public security and order, a public security organ may be established within them upon a decision by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, or upon approval by the public security organ of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, as requested by the relevant military organ.

**Article 51** In violation of the provisions of this Law, in any of the following circumstances, the personnel on duty of the management unit of the military facility shall stop the act:

(1) Illegally entering a military restricted zone or military management zone, or flying at low altitude over a military restricted zone on land or water;
(2) Illegally photographing, filming, recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, or describing a military restricted zone or military management zone;
(3) Engaging in activities that damage or endanger military facilities.

**Article 52** If a person commits any of the acts listed in Article 51 of this Law and refuses to stop upon being ordered, the management unit of the military facility may, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, take the following measures:

(1) Forcibly remove and control persons who illegally enter a military restricted zone or military management zone, or who drive or operate aircraft flying at low altitude over a military restricted zone on land or water; detain persons with serious violations and immediately transfer them to the public security, state security, or other competent authorities;
(2) Immediately stop acts such as information transmission, seize equipment, tools, or other items used for the illegal act, and transfer them to the public security, state security, or other competent authorities;
(3) In emergency situations, remove obstacles that seriously endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of military facilities;
(4) Use weapons in accordance with the law in emergency situations such as when the safety of military facilities or the life safety of personnel on duty is endangered.

If military personnel, civilian personnel in the military, or other military personnel commit any of the acts listed in Article 51 of this Law, they shall be dealt with in accordance with the relevant provisions of the military.

## Chapter 7 Legal Liability

**Article 53** In violation of the provisions of Articles 17, 18, and 23 of this Law, entering a water military restricted zone without authorization, engaging in aquaculture or fishing within a water military restricted zone, engaging in aquaculture within a water military management zone, or engaging in fishing or other activities within a water military management zone that affect the movement of military vessels, the competent departments of transportation, fisheries, and others shall issue a warning, order the person to leave, and confiscate the fishing gear and catch.

**Article 54** In violation of the provisions of Articles 18, 23, and 24 of this Law, constructing or installing non-military facilities within a military restricted zone or military management zone on land or water, developing the underground space of a military restricted zone or military management zone on land without authorization, or constructing or installing non-military facilities in the waters managed by the local authorities of a joint military-civilian port designated as a military management zone without obtaining the consent of the management unit of the military facility, the competent departments of housing and urban-rural development, natural resources, transportation, fisheries, and others shall order the cessation of the construction activities and order the demolition of any completed structures within a specified time limit.

**Article 55** In violation of the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 28 of this Law, quarrying, mining, or blasting within the safety protection zone of operational engineering, the competent departments of natural resources, ecological environment, and public security organs shall order the cessation of the illegal act and confiscate the extracted products and illegal gains. If constructing buildings, structures, roads, or carrying out farmland and water conservancy infrastructure construction affects the safety and operational effectiveness of operational engineering, the competent departments of natural resources, ecological environment, transportation, agriculture and rural affairs, housing and urban-rural development, and others shall issue a warning and order rectification within a specified time limit.

**Article 56** In violation of the provisions of Paragraph 3 of Article 28 of this Law, privately opening sealed operational engineering, destroying the camouflage of operational engineering, blocking the passages of operational engineering, or using operational engineering for storing non-military materials and equipment or for production activities such as planting and breeding, the public security organs and the competent departments of natural resources shall order the cessation of the illegal act and the restoration of the original state within a specified time limit.

**Article 57** In violation of the provisions of Paragraph 4 of Article 28 and Article 34 of this Law, dismantling, relocating, or altering operational engineering without authorization, or dismantling or moving border defense or coastal defense control facilities without authorization, the competent departments of housing and urban-rural development, public security organs, and others shall order the cessation of the illegal act and the restoration of the original state within a specified time limit.

**Article 58** In violation of the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 29 of this Law, constructing buildings, structures, or other facilities within the clearance protection zone of a military airfield that exceed the clearance protection standards of the military airfield, the competent departments of housing and urban-rural development and natural resources shall order the demolition of the parts exceeding the height limit within a specified time limit.

**Article 59** In violation of the provisions of Article 33 of this Law, constructing or installing equipment or electromagnetic obstacles within the electromagnetic environment protection zone of fixed military radio facilities that affect the operational effectiveness of the fixed military radio facilities, or engaging in activities that affect the electromagnetic environment of the fixed military radio facilities, the competent departments of natural resources, ecological environment, and the radio regulatory agency shall issue a warning and order rectification within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, the interfering equipment shall be sealed or the obstacles shall be forcibly removed.

**Article 60** Any of the following acts shall be subject to the penalty provisions of Article 23 of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China:

(1) Illegally entering a military restricted zone or military management zone, or driving or operating an aircraft flying at low altitude over a military restricted zone on land or water, and refusing to stop upon being ordered;
(2) Engaging in activities that endanger the safety and operational effectiveness of military facilities within the outer safety control zone of a military restricted zone, or within a certain distance of military facilities not designated as military restricted zones or military management zones, and refusing to stop upon being ordered;
(3) Engaging in activities within the clearance protection zone of a military airfield that affect flight safety or the operational effectiveness of airport navigation aids, and refusing to stop upon being ordered;
(4) Illegally photographing, filming, recording, surveying, measuring, positioning, mapping, or describing a military restricted zone or military management zone, and refusing to stop upon being ordered;
(5) Other acts that disrupt the management order of military restricted zones or military management zones and endanger the safety of military facilities, where the circumstances are minor and do not yet constitute a criminal offense.

**Article 61** Intentionally interfering with the normal operation of military radio facilities in violation of state regulations, or causing harmful interference to military radio facilities and refusing to rectify in accordance with the requirements of the relevant competent authorities, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of Article 28 of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 62** Damaging border defense or coastal defense control facilities, or the walls, barbed wire, boundary markers, or other military facilities of military restricted zones or military management zones, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of Article 33 of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 63** Any of the following acts, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law:

(1) Destroying military facilities;
(2) Negligently damaging military facilities, causing serious consequences;
(3) Stealing, robbing, or forcibly taking the equipment, materials, or apparatus of military facilities;
(4) Disclosing secrets of military facilities, or stealing, spying, buying, or illegally providing secrets of military facilities for foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals;
(5) Destroying the electromagnetic environment of fixed military radio facilities, interfering with military radio communications, where the circumstances are serious;
(6) Other acts that disrupt the management order of military restricted zones or military management zones and endanger the safety of military facilities, where the circumstances are serious.

**Article 64** Military personnel, civilian personnel in the military, and other military personnel who commit any of the following acts shall be given sanctions in accordance with the relevant provisions of the military; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Committing any of the acts specified in Articles 53 to 63 of this Law;
(2) Using military facilities for non-military purposes without authorization, or committing other acts of abuse of power;
(3) Deserting their posts or neglecting their duties.

**Article 65** Public officials who engage in dereliction of duty, abuse of power, or favoritism in the protection of military facilities shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

**Article 66** If acts that damage or endanger military facilities in violation of the provisions of this Law fall within the purview of the Coast Guard, they shall be handled by the Coast Guard in accordance with the law.

If there are other acts that damage or endanger military facilities in violation of the provisions of this Law, they shall be handled by the relevant competent authorities in accordance with the law.

**Article 67** Anyone who causes losses to military facilities in violation of the provisions of this Law shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 68** Anyone who violates this Law during wartime shall be subject to heavier legal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 69** The protection of military facilities belonging to the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be governed by this Law.

**Article 70** The protection of facilities for the scientific research, production, testing, and storage of important weapons and equipment in the defense science, technology, and industry sector shall be implemented with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law. Specific measures and the list of facilities shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 71** The State Council and the Central Military Commission shall formulate implementing regulations in accordance with this Law.

**Article 72** This Law shall come into force on August 1, 2021.
