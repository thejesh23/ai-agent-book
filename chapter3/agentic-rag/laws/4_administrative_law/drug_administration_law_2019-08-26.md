# Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 20, 1984

First Revision at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on February 28, 2001

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 28, 2013

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Drug Administration Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Second Revision at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 26, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen drug administration, ensure drug quality, safeguard public medication safety and lawful rights and interests, and protect and promote public health.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities involving drug research and development, production, distribution, use, and supervision and management within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "drugs" refer to substances used for the prevention, treatment, and diagnosis of human diseases, intended to regulate human physiological functions purposefully, and having specified indications or functions, usage, and dosage, including traditional Chinese medicines, chemical drugs, and biological products.

Article 3 Drug administration shall be people-centered, adhere to the principles of risk management, full-process control, and social co-governance, establish a scientific and rigorous supervision and management system, comprehensively improve drug quality, and ensure the safety, efficacy, and accessibility of drugs.

Article 4 The State develops modern and traditional drugs, giving full play to their roles in prevention, medical treatment, and healthcare.

The State protects wild medicinal material resources and traditional Chinese medicine varieties, and encourages the cultivation of authentic medicinal materials.

Article 5 The State encourages research and innovation of new drugs, and protects the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations in researching and developing new drugs.

Article 6 The State implements a marketing authorization holder system for drug administration. The drug marketing authorization holder shall be responsible for the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of drugs throughout the entire process of research and development, production, distribution, and use.

Article 7 Entities engaged in drug research and development, production, distribution, and use activities shall comply with laws, regulations, rules, standards, and norms, ensuring that information throughout the entire process is truthful, accurate, complete, and traceable.

Article 8 The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall be responsible for nationwide drug supervision and management. Relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for drug-related supervision and management within their respective scopes of duties. The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall cooperate with relevant departments under the State Council in implementing national drug industry development plans and industrial policies.

The drug regulatory authorities of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for drug supervision and management within their respective administrative regions. The departments responsible for drug supervision and management duties of the people's governments at the city with districts and county levels (hereinafter referred to as drug regulatory authorities) shall be responsible for drug supervision and management within their respective administrative regions. Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for drug-related supervision and management within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 9 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for drug supervision and management within their respective administrative regions, uniformly leading, organizing, and coordinating drug supervision and management work and responses to drug safety emergencies within their administrative regions, and establishing and improving drug supervision and management mechanisms and information sharing mechanisms.

Article 10 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate drug safety work into their respective national economic and social development plans, include drug safety work funds in their respective government budgets, strengthen drug supervision and management capacity building, and provide guarantees for drug safety work.

Article 11 Drug professional and technical institutions established or designated by drug regulatory authorities shall undertake the evaluation, inspection, verification, monitoring, and assessment work required for the implementation of drug supervision and management in accordance with the law.

Article 12 The State establishes and improves a drug traceability system. The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall formulate unified drug traceability standards and norms, promote the exchange and sharing of drug traceability information, and achieve drug traceability.

The State establishes a pharmacovigilance system to monitor, identify, assess, and control adverse drug reactions and other harmful reactions related to drug use.

Article 13 People's governments at all levels, their relevant departments, and drug industry associations shall strengthen drug safety publicity and education, and carry out the popularization of knowledge on drug safety laws and regulations.

News media shall conduct public interest publicity on drug safety laws and regulations and other knowledge, and exercise public opinion supervision over illegal drug activities. Drug-related publicity and reports shall be comprehensive, scientific, objective, and fair.

Article 14 Drug industry associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, establish and improve industry norms, promote the construction of an industry integrity system, and guide and urge members to carry out drug production and distribution activities in accordance with the law.

Article 15 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall commend and reward units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions in drug research and development, production, distribution, use, and supervision and management, in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Drug Research and Development and Registration

Article 16 The State supports drug innovation oriented towards clinical value and with clear or special therapeutic effects on human diseases, encourages the research and development of new drugs with new therapeutic mechanisms, for the treatment of serious life-threatening diseases or rare diseases, and with multi-target systemic regulatory intervention functions on the human body, and promotes the advancement of drug technology.

The State encourages the use of modern science and technology and traditional Chinese medicine research methods to carry out scientific and technological research and drug development of traditional Chinese medicine, establish and improve a technical evaluation system that conforms to the characteristics of traditional Chinese medicine, and promote the inheritance and innovation of traditional Chinese medicine.

The State takes effective measures to encourage the research and development and innovation of pediatric drugs, supports the development of new varieties, dosage forms, and specifications of pediatric drugs that conform to the physiological characteristics of children, and grants priority evaluation and approval to pediatric drugs.

Article 17 Entities engaged in drug research and development activities shall comply with good laboratory practice for non-clinical studies and good clinical practice, ensuring that the entire drug research and development process continuously meets legal requirements.

Good laboratory practice for non-clinical studies and good clinical practice shall be formulated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council jointly with relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 18 Conducting non-clinical drug studies shall comply with relevant state regulations, have personnel, sites, equipment, instruments, and management systems appropriate for the research project, and ensure the authenticity of relevant data, materials, and samples.

Article 19 To conduct a clinical trial of a drug, the sponsor shall truthfully submit relevant data, materials, and samples, including research methods, quality indicators, pharmacological and toxicological test results, to the drug regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with its regulations, and obtain approval. The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall decide whether to consent and notify the clinical trial sponsor within sixty working days from the date of accepting the clinical trial application; if no notification is given within the time limit, consent shall be deemed to have been granted. For bioequivalence trials, the sponsor shall file with the drug regulatory authority under the State Council.

Clinical trials of drugs shall be conducted in clinical trial institutions with appropriate conditions. Clinical trial institutions shall be managed through a filing system, and specific measures shall be jointly formulated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council and the health authority under the State Council.

Article 20 Conducting clinical trials of drugs shall comply with ethical principles, formulate a clinical trial protocol, and obtain approval from the ethics committee after review.

The ethics committee shall establish an ethical review work system, ensure that the ethical review process is independent, objective, and fair, supervise the standardized conduct of clinical trials, protect the lawful rights and interests of subjects, and safeguard the public interest.

Article 21 When implementing a clinical trial, the sponsor shall truthfully explain and clarify the purpose and risks of the trial and other detailed information to the subject or their guardian, obtain the informed consent form voluntarily signed by the subject or their guardian, and take effective measures to protect the lawful rights and interests of the subject.

Article 22 During a clinical trial, if safety issues or other risks are discovered, the clinical trial sponsor shall promptly adjust the clinical trial protocol, suspend or terminate the clinical trial, and report to the drug regulatory authority under the State Council. When necessary, the drug regulatory authority under the State Council may order the adjustment of the clinical trial protocol, suspension, or termination of the clinical trial.

Article 23 For drugs undergoing clinical trials for the treatment of serious life-threatening diseases for which there is no effective treatment, if medical observation indicates potential benefit and ethical principles are met, they may, after review and informed consent, be used within the institution conducting the clinical trial for other patients with the same condition.

Article 24 Drugs marketed in China shall be approved by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council and obtain a drug registration certificate; however, this does not apply to Chinese medicinal materials and prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs that are not subject to approval management. The catalogue of varieties of Chinese medicinal materials and prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs subject to approval management shall be formulated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council jointly with the traditional Chinese medicine authority under the State Council.

To apply for drug registration, the applicant shall provide truthful, sufficient, and reliable data, materials, and samples to demonstrate the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of the drug.

Article 25 For drugs applied for registration, the drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall organize pharmaceutical, medical, and other technical personnel to conduct evaluation, and review the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of the drug, as well as the applicant's capabilities in quality management, risk prevention and control, and liability compensation; if conditions are met, a drug registration certificate shall be issued.

When approving a drug, the drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall simultaneously evaluate and approve the active pharmaceutical ingredient, evaluate related excipients, packaging materials and containers in direct contact with the drug, and approve the drug quality standards, manufacturing process, labeling, and package insert.

For the purposes of this Law, "excipients" refer to vehicles and additives used in the production of drugs and the compounding of prescriptions.

Article 26 For drugs for the treatment of serious life-threatening diseases for which there is no effective treatment and drugs urgently needed for public health, if clinical trial data has shown efficacy and their clinical value can be predicted, conditional approval may be granted, and relevant matters shall be stated in the drug registration certificate.

Article 27 The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall improve the drug evaluation and approval work system, strengthen capacity building, establish and improve mechanisms such as communication and expert consultation, optimize the evaluation and approval process, and improve the efficiency of evaluation and approval.

The evaluation conclusions and basis for approved marketed drugs shall be disclosed in accordance with the law and subject to public supervision. Trade secrets learned during evaluation and approval shall be kept confidential.

Article 28 Drugs shall comply with national drug standards. If the drug quality standards approved by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council are higher than the national drug standards, the approved drug quality standards shall prevail; if there are no national drug standards, the approved drug quality standards shall be complied with.

The "Pharmacopoeia of the People's Republic of China" and drug standards promulgated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council are national drug standards.

The drug regulatory authority under the State Council, jointly with the health authority under the State Council, shall organize the Pharmacopoeia Commission, which is responsible for the formulation and revision of national drug standards.

The drug testing institution established or designated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall be responsible for calibrating national drug standard substances and reference substances.

Article 29 The name of a drug included in the national drug standards is the generic name of the drug. Once a name has been used as a generic name for a drug, it shall not be used as a drug trademark.

## Chapter III Drug Marketing Authorization Holder

Article 30 A drug marketing authorization holder refers to an enterprise or a drug research and development institution that has obtained a drug registration certificate.

The drug marketing authorization holder shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, bear responsibility for the non-clinical studies, clinical trials, production and distribution, post-marketing studies, adverse drug reaction monitoring, reporting, and handling of the drug. Other entities and individuals engaged in activities such as drug research and development, production, distribution, storage, transportation, and use shall bear corresponding responsibilities in accordance with the law.

The legal representative and principal responsible person of the drug marketing authorization holder shall be fully responsible for the quality of the drug.

Article 31 The drug marketing authorization holder shall establish a drug quality assurance system and assign dedicated personnel independently responsible for drug quality management.

The drug marketing authorization holder shall conduct regular audits of the quality management systems of entrusted drug manufacturers and drug distributors, and supervise their continuous possession of quality assurance and control capabilities.

Article 32 The drug marketing authorization holder may manufacture drugs itself or entrust a drug manufacturer to manufacture them.

If the drug marketing authorization holder manufactures drugs itself, it shall obtain a drug manufacturing license in accordance with the provisions of this Law; if it entrusts manufacturing, it shall entrust a qualified drug manufacturer. The drug marketing authorization holder and the entrusted manufacturer shall sign an entrustment agreement and a quality agreement, and strictly fulfill the obligations stipulated in the agreements.

The drug regulatory authority under the State Council shall formulate guidelines for quality agreements for entrusted drug manufacturing to guide and supervise the drug marketing authorization holder and the entrusted manufacturer in fulfilling their drug quality assurance obligations.

Blood products, narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, medicinal toxic drugs, and precursor chemicals for drug production shall not be entrusted for manufacturing, unless otherwise provided by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 33 The drug marketing authorization holder shall establish a drug batch release procedure, review the drugs released by the drug manufacturer, and release them only after being signed by the authorized person for quality. Drugs that do not meet national drug standards shall not be released.

Article 34 The drug marketing authorization holder may sell the drugs for which it has obtained drug registration certificates itself, or entrust a drug distributor to sell them. If the drug marketing authorization holder engages in retail drug activities, it shall obtain a drug distribution license.

If the drug marketing authorization holder sells drugs itself, it shall meet the conditions specified in Article 52 of this Law; if it entrusts sales, it shall entrust a qualified drug distributor. The drug marketing authorization holder and the entrusted distributor shall sign an entrustment agreement and strictly fulfill the obligations stipulated in the agreement.

Article 35 If a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, or drug distributor entrusts the storage or transportation of drugs, it shall assess the quality assurance capability and risk management capability of the entrusted party, sign an entrustment agreement with it, stipulating matters such as drug quality responsibilities and operating procedures, and supervise the entrusted party.
Article 36 Drug marketing authorization holders, drug manufacturers, drug distributors, and medical institutions shall establish and implement a drug traceability system, provide traceability information in accordance with regulations, and ensure drug traceability.

Article 37 The drug marketing authorization holder shall establish an annual reporting system, and report annually on drug production and sales, post-marketing studies, risk management, and other matters to the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with regulations.

Article 38 If the drug marketing authorization holder is an overseas enterprise, the enterprise legal person designated by it within China shall fulfill the obligations of the drug marketing authorization holder and bear joint and several liability with the drug marketing authorization holder.

Article 39 Manufacturers of prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs shall fulfill the relevant obligations of the drug marketing authorization holder, implement full-process management over the production and sale of prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs, establish a traceability system for prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs, and ensure the safety, efficacy, and traceability of prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs.

Article 40 With the approval of the drug regulatory authority under the State Council, the drug marketing authorization holder may transfer the drug marketing authorization. The transferee shall possess the capabilities of quality management, risk prevention and control, and liability compensation to ensure the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of the drug, and fulfill the obligations of the drug marketing authorization holder.

## Chapter IV Drug Production

Article 41 Entities engaged in drug production activities shall obtain approval from the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where they are located, and obtain a drug manufacturing license. No drug manufacturing license, no drug production.

The drug manufacturing license shall indicate the validity period and scope of production, and shall be re-examined and reissued upon expiration.

Article 42 Entities engaged in drug production activities shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having legally qualified pharmaceutical technical personnel, engineering technical personnel, and corresponding skilled workers;

(2) Having factory buildings, facilities, and sanitary environments suitable for drug production;

(3) Having institutions, personnel, and necessary instruments and equipment capable of performing quality management and quality inspection of the drugs produced;

(4) Having rules and regulations to ensure drug quality, and complying with the requirements of the Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs formulated by the drug regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Article 43 Entities engaged in drug production activities shall comply with Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs, establish and improve a drug production quality management system, and ensure that the entire drug production process continuously meets legal requirements.

The legal representative and principal responsible person of a drug manufacturer shall be fully responsible for the drug production activities of the enterprise.

Article 44 Drugs shall be produced in accordance with national drug standards and the manufacturing process approved by the drug regulatory authority. Production and inspection records shall be complete and accurate, and shall not be fabricated.

Prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs shall be processed in accordance with national drug standards; if there are no provisions in the national drug standards, they shall be processed in accordance with the processing specifications formulated by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The processing specifications formulated by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be filed with the drug regulatory authority under the State Council. Prepared slices of Chinese crude drugs that do not meet national drug standards or are not processed in accordance with the processing specifications formulated by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall not be released from the factory or sold.

Article 45 The raw materials and excipients required for drug production shall meet medicinal requirements and the relevant requirements of Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs.

When producing drugs, the manufacturer shall audit suppliers of raw materials, excipients, etc., in accordance with regulations, to ensure that the raw materials, excipients, etc., purchased and used meet the requirements of the preceding paragraph.

Article 46 Packaging materials and containers in direct contact with drugs shall meet medicinal requirements and standards for ensuring human health and safety.

For non-conforming packaging materials and containers in direct contact with drugs, the drug regulatory authority shall order their use to be stopped.

Article 47 Drug manufacturers shall conduct quality inspection of drugs. Drugs that do not meet national drug standards shall not be released from the factory.

Drug manufacturers shall establish a drug batch release procedure, specifying the standards and conditions for batch release. Only after meeting the standards and conditions and being signed by the authorized person for quality may the drugs be released.

Article 48 Drug packaging shall be suitable for the quality requirements of the drug, convenient for storage, transportation, and medical use.

Dispatched Chinese medicinal materials shall be packaged. On each package, the product name, place of origin, date, and supplier shall be indicated, and a mark of qualified quality shall be attached.

Article 49 Drug packaging shall be printed or affixed with a label and accompanied by a package insert in accordance with regulations.Labels or package inserts must indicate the generic name, ingredients, specifications, marketing authorization holder and its address, manufacturer and its address, approval number, batch number, production date, expiration date, indications or functional indications, usage, dosage, contraindications, adverse reactions, and precautions. The text on labels and inserts must be clear, and items such as the production date and expiration date must be prominently marked and easily identifiable.

Labels and inserts for narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, toxic drugs for medical use, radioactive drugs, externally used drugs, and non-prescription drugs must bear the prescribed markings.

Article 50 Personnel who directly handle drugs in marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must undergo an annual health check. Those suffering from infectious diseases or other diseases that may contaminate drugs are prohibited from engaging in work that involves direct contact with drugs.

## Chapter V Pharmaceutical Trading

Article 51 Engaging in wholesale pharmaceutical activities requires approval from the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the entity is located, and obtaining a Pharmaceutical Trading License. Engaging in retail pharmaceutical activities requires approval from the drug regulatory department of the people's government at or above the county level where the entity is located, and obtaining a Pharmaceutical Trading License. Without a Pharmaceutical Trading License, pharmaceutical trading is prohibited.

The Pharmaceutical Trading License must indicate the validity period and scope of business, and is subject to review and reissuance upon expiration.

When implementing pharmaceutical trading licensing, drug regulatory departments must, in addition to the conditions stipulated in Article 52 of this Law, adhere to the principle of facilitating public access to medicines.

Article 52 The following conditions must be met for engaging in pharmaceutical trading activities:

(1) Having pharmacists or other pharmaceutical technical personnel who have been qualified according to law;

(2) Having business premises, equipment, storage facilities, and sanitary environments that are compatible with the drugs being traded;

(3) Having a quality management organization or personnel that is compatible with the drugs being traded;

(4) Having rules and regulations that ensure drug quality, and complying with the requirements of the Good Supply Practice for pharmaceutical products formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Article 53 Entities engaged in pharmaceutical trading activities must comply with the Good Supply Practice for pharmaceutical products, establish and improve a pharmaceutical trading quality management system, and ensure that the entire process of pharmaceutical trading continuously meets legal requirements.

The State encourages and guides chain operations in pharmaceutical retail. The headquarters of enterprises engaged in chain retail pharmaceutical activities must establish a unified quality management system and fulfill management responsibilities for the trading activities of their affiliated retail enterprises.

The legal representative and principal responsible person of a pharmaceutical trading enterprise bear overall responsibility for the enterprise's pharmaceutical trading activities.

Article 54 The State implements a classified management system for prescription drugs and non-prescription drugs. Specific measures are formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in conjunction with the health department of the State Council.

Article 55 Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must purchase drugs from marketing authorization holders or enterprises qualified for pharmaceutical production or trading; however, this does not apply to the purchase of Chinese medicinal materials that are not subject to approval management.

Article 56 When purchasing drugs, pharmaceutical trading enterprises must establish and implement a receiving inspection and acceptance system, verifying drug qualification certificates and other markings; drugs that do not meet the specified requirements must not be purchased or sold.

Article 57 Pharmaceutical trading enterprises must maintain truthful and complete purchase and sales records for their drug transactions. These records must include the generic name, dosage form, specifications, batch number, expiration date, marketing authorization holder, manufacturer, purchasing/selling entity, quantity, price, date, and other content as prescribed by the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Article 58 Pharmaceutical trading enterprises must dispense drugs accurately and correctly explain the usage, dosage, and precautions; prescriptions must be checked before dispensing, and the drugs listed on the prescription must not be altered or substituted without authorization. Prescriptions containing incompatibilities or exceeding the dosage must be refused for dispensing; if necessary, dispensing may proceed only after the prescribing physician corrects or re-signs the prescription.

When selling Chinese medicinal materials, pharmaceutical trading enterprises must indicate the place of origin.

Pharmacists or other pharmaceutical technical personnel who have been qualified according to law are responsible for the enterprise's drug management, prescription review and dispensing, and guidance on rational drug use.

Article 59 Pharmaceutical trading enterprises must formulate and implement drug storage systems, taking necessary measures such as refrigeration, freeze protection, moisture protection, pest control, and rodent control to ensure drug quality.

A checking system must be implemented for drug warehousing and outbound operations.

Article 60 Chinese medicinal materials may be sold in urban and rural trade markets, unless otherwise provided by the State Council.

Article 61 Marketing authorization holders and pharmaceutical trading enterprises selling drugs through the internet must comply with the relevant provisions of this Law on pharmaceutical trading. Specific management measures are formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in conjunction with the health department of the State Council and other relevant departments.

Vaccines, blood products, narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, toxic drugs for medical use, radioactive drugs, precursor chemicals for pharmaceuticals, and other drugs subject to special state management must not be sold online.

Article 62 Third-party platform providers for online drug transactions must file with the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where they are located, in accordance with the provisions of the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Third-party platform providers must, according to law, review the qualifications of marketing authorization holders and pharmaceutical trading enterprises applying to operate on the platform, ensuring they meet legal requirements, and manage the pharmaceutical trading activities occurring on the platform.

If a third-party platform provider discovers that a marketing authorization holder or pharmaceutical trading enterprise operating on the platform has violated the provisions of this Law, it must promptly stop the violation and immediately report to the drug regulatory department of the people's government at the county level where it is located; if serious violations are found, it must immediately cease providing online trading platform services.

Article 63 Newly discovered or introduced Chinese medicinal materials from abroad may be sold only after approval by the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Article 64 Drugs must be imported through ports designated for drug importation, and the importing enterprise must file with the drug regulatory department at the port location. Customs shall handle customs clearance procedures based on the Import Drug Clearance Certificate issued by the drug regulatory department. Without the Import Drug Clearance Certificate, customs must not release the goods.

The drug regulatory department at the port location must notify drug testing institutions to conduct random inspections of imported drugs in accordance with the provisions of the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Ports designated for drug importation are proposed by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in conjunction with the General Administration of Customs and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 65 If a medical institution urgently needs to import a small quantity of drugs for clinical use, it may do so with the approval of the drug regulatory department of the State Council or the authorized people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The imported drugs must be used within the designated medical institution for specific medical purposes.

A small quantity of drugs brought into the country for personal use shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 66 The import and export of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances within the scope specified by the State require an Import Permit or Export Permit issued by the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Article 67 The import of drugs with uncertain efficacy, significant adverse reactions, or that are otherwise harmful to human health is prohibited.

Article 68 The drug regulatory department of the State Council must designate drug testing institutions to conduct inspections on the following drugs before sale or import; drugs that have not been inspected or have failed inspection must not be sold or imported:

(1) Drugs being sold for the first time within China;

(2) Biological products specified by the drug regulatory department of the State Council;

(3) Other drugs specified by the State Council.

## Chapter VI Pharmaceutical Affairs Management in Medical Institutions

Article 69 Medical institutions must be equipped with pharmacists or other pharmaceutical technical personnel who have been qualified according to law, responsible for the institution's drug management, prescription review and dispensing, and guidance on rational drug use. Non-pharmaceutical technical personnel must not directly engage in pharmaceutical technical work.

Article 70 When purchasing drugs, medical institutions must establish and implement a receiving inspection and acceptance system, verifying drug qualification certificates and other markings; drugs that do not meet the specified requirements must not be purchased or used.

Article 71 Medical institutions must have premises, equipment, storage facilities, and sanitary environments compatible with the drugs they use, formulate and implement drug storage systems, and take necessary measures such as refrigeration, freeze protection, moisture protection, pest control, and rodent control to ensure drug quality.

Article 72 Medical institutions must adhere to the principles of safe, effective, and economically rational drug use, follow clinical application guidelines, clinical diagnosis and treatment guidelines, and drug package inserts for rational drug use, and review the appropriateness of physicians' prescriptions and medication orders.

Other drug-using entities besides medical institutions must comply with the provisions of this Law concerning drug use in medical institutions.

Article 73 When dispensing prescriptions, pharmacists or other pharmaceutical technical personnel who have been qualified according to law must check the prescription and must not alter or substitute the drugs listed on the prescription without authorization. Prescriptions containing incompatibilities or exceeding the dosage must be refused for dispensing; if necessary, dispensing may proceed only after the prescribing physician corrects or re-signs the prescription.

Article 74 The preparation of pharmaceutical preparations by medical institutions requires approval from the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the institution is located, and obtaining a Medical Institution Preparation License. Without a Medical Institution Preparation License, preparation is prohibited.

The Medical Institution Preparation License must indicate the validity period and is subject to review and reissuance upon expiration.

Article 75 Medical institutions preparing pharmaceutical preparations must have facilities, management systems, testing instruments, and sanitary environments that can ensure the quality of the preparations.

Medical institutions must prepare pharmaceutical preparations according to approved processes, and the required raw materials, excipients, and packaging materials must meet pharmaceutical requirements.

Article 76 Pharmaceutical preparations prepared by medical institutions must be varieties that are clinically needed by the institution but not available on the market, and must be approved by the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the institution is located; however, this does not apply where the law provides otherwise for the preparation of traditional Chinese medicine preparations.

Pharmaceutical preparations prepared by medical institutions must undergo quality testing as required; those that pass may be used within the institution upon a physician's prescription. With the approval of the drug regulatory department of the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, pharmaceutical preparations prepared by medical institutions may be transferred for use between designated medical institutions.

Pharmaceutical preparations prepared by medical institutions must not be sold on the market.

## Chapter VII Post-Marketing Management of Drugs

Article 77 Marketing authorization holders must formulate a post-marketing risk management plan, proactively conduct post-marketing studies, further confirm the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of drugs, and strengthen the ongoing management of marketed drugs.

Article 78 For conditionally approved drugs, marketing authorization holders must take corresponding risk management measures and complete relevant studies as required within a specified period; if the studies are not completed as required within the time limit or the benefits cannot be proven to outweigh the risks, the drug regulatory department of the State Council shall handle the matter according to law, up to and including revoking the drug registration certificate.

Article 79 Changes in the drug production process are subject to classified management based on their risk and degree of impact on drug safety, efficacy, and quality controllability. Major changes require approval from the drug regulatory department of the State Council; other changes must be filed or reported in accordance with the provisions of the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Marketing authorization holders must comprehensively assess and verify the impact of changes on drug safety, efficacy, and quality controllability in accordance with the provisions of the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Article 80 Marketing authorization holders must conduct post-marketing adverse drug reaction monitoring, proactively collect and track analyze information on suspected adverse drug reactions, and promptly take risk control measures for drugs with identified risks.

Article 81 Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must regularly examine the quality, efficacy, and adverse reactions of the drugs they produce, trade, or use. If suspected adverse reactions are discovered, they must promptly report to the drug regulatory department and the health department. Specific measures are formulated by the drug regulatory department of the State Council in conjunction with the health department of the State Council.

For drugs confirmed to have serious adverse reactions, the drug regulatory department of the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall take emergency control measures such as stopping production, sale, or use based on the actual situation, and must organize an appraisal within five days, and make an administrative decision according to law within fifteen days from the date the appraisal conclusion is made.

Article 82 If a drug has quality issues or other safety hazards, the marketing authorization holder must immediately stop sales, notify relevant pharmaceutical trading enterprises and medical institutions to stop sales and use, recall the sold drugs, promptly disclose recall information, and if necessary, immediately stop production, and report the drug recall and handling situation to the drug regulatory department and the health department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. Pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must cooperate.

If a marketing authorization holder fails to recall a drug that should be recalled according to law, the drug regulatory department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order the recall.

Article 83 Marketing authorization holders must periodically conduct post-marketing evaluations of the safety, efficacy, and quality controllability of marketed drugs. When necessary, the drug regulatory department of the State Council may order the marketing authorization holder to conduct a post-marketing evaluation or directly organize and conduct one.

Based on the evaluation, drugs with uncertain efficacy, significant adverse reactions, or that are otherwise harmful to human health shall have their drug registration certificates revoked.

Drugs whose registration certificates have been revoked must not be produced, imported, sold, or used.

Drugs whose registration certificates have been revoked or that have exceeded their expiration date must be supervised for destruction by the drug regulatory department or subjected to other harmless treatment measures according to law.

## Chapter VIII Drug Prices and Advertising

Article 84 The State improves the drug procurement management system, monitors drug prices, conducts cost and price investigations, strengthens drug price supervision and inspection, investigates and deals with drug price violations such as price monopoly and price gouging according to law, and maintains drug price order.

Article 85 For drugs whose prices are regulated by the market according to law, marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must set prices based on the principles of fairness, reasonableness, good faith, and value for money, providing drug users with reasonably priced drugs.

Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must comply with the regulations of the drug pricing authority of the State Council on drug price management, set and clearly mark retail prices, and prohibit profiteering, price monopoly, and price fraud.

Article 86 Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions must, according to law, provide the drug pricing authority with information such as the actual purchase and sales prices and quantities of their drugs.

Article 87 Medical institutions must provide patients with price lists for the drugs used, truthfully disclose the prices of their commonly used drugs as required, and strengthen rational drug use management. Specific measures are formulated by the health department of the State Council.

Article 88 Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, and medical institutions are prohibited from giving or accepting kickbacks or other improper benefits in drug procurement and sales.

Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, or their agents are prohibited from giving money, property, or other improper benefits to responsible persons, drug procurement personnel, physicians, pharmacists, or other relevant personnel of medical institutions that use their drugs in any name. Responsible persons, drug procurement personnel, physicians, pharmacists, or other relevant personnel of medical institutions are prohibited from accepting money, property, or other improper benefits given by marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, pharmaceutical trading enterprises, or their agents in any name.

Article 89 Drug advertisements must be approved by the advertising review authority designated by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the advertiser is located; without approval, they must not be published.

Article 90 The content of drug advertisements must be truthful and lawful, based on the drug package insert approved by the drug regulatory department of the State Council, and must not contain false content.

Drug advertisements must not contain assertions or guarantees of efficacy or safety; must not use the names or images of state organs, research institutions, academic organizations, industry associations, or experts, scholars, physicians, pharmacists, patients, etc., for recommendations or endorsements.

Non-drug advertisements must not involve drug promotion.

Article 91 Matters concerning drug prices and advertising not provided for in this Law shall be governed by the provisions of the Price Law of the People's Republic of China, the Anti-Monopoly Law of the People's Republic of China, the Anti-Unfair Competition Law of the People's Republic of China, the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws.

## Chapter IX Drug Reserves and Supply

Article 92 The State implements a drug reserve system, establishing central and local levels of drug reserves.

In the event of major disasters, epidemics, or other emergencies, drugs may be urgently requisitioned in accordance with the provisions of the Emergency Response Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 93 The State implements an essential medicines system, selecting an appropriate number of essential medicine varieties, strengthening organized production and reserves, improving the supply capacity of essential medicines, and meeting the basic drug needs for disease prevention and treatment.

Article 94 The State establishes a drug supply monitoring system, promptly collecting and aggregating information on the supply and demand of shortage drugs, issuing early warnings for shortage drugs, and taking countermeasures.

Article 95 The State implements a shortage drug list management system. Specific measures are formulated by the health department of the State Council in conjunction with the drug regulatory department of the State Council and other relevant departments.

If a marketing authorization holder stops producing a shortage drug, it must report to the drug regulatory department of the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government as required.

Article 96 The State encourages the research and production of shortage drugs, granting priority review and approval for clinically urgently needed shortage drugs and new drugs for the prevention and treatment of major infectious diseases and rare diseases.

Article 97 For shortage drugs, the State Council may restrict or prohibit exports. When necessary, relevant departments of the State Council may take measures such as organizing production, price intervention, and expanding imports to ensure drug supply.

Marketing authorization holders, pharmaceutical manufacturers, and pharmaceutical trading enterprises must ensure the production and supply of drugs as required.

## Chapter X Supervision and Administration

Article 98 The production (including preparation, hereinafter the same), sale, or use of counterfeit or substandard drugs is prohibited.Any of the following circumstances constitutes a counterfeit drug:

(1) The ingredients contained in the drug do not conform to the ingredients specified in the national drug standards;

(2) A non-drug substance is passed off as a drug, or one type of drug is passed off as another;

(3) The drug has deteriorated;

(4) The indications or functional indications stated on the drug exceed the specified scope.

Any of the following circumstances constitutes an inferior drug:

(1) The content of the drug ingredients does not meet the national drug standards;

(2) The drug is contaminated;

(3) The drug's expiration date is not indicated or has been altered;

(4) The drug's batch number is not indicated or has been altered;

(5) The drug has exceeded its expiration date;

(6) Preservatives or excipients have been added without authorization;

(7) Other drugs that do not meet drug standards.

It is prohibited to produce or import drugs without obtaining a drug approval document; it is prohibited to produce drugs using active pharmaceutical ingredients, packaging materials, and containers that have not been reviewed and approved in accordance with regulations.

Article 99 The drug regulatory authority shall, in accordance with laws and regulations, conduct supervision and inspection of activities such as drug research and development, production, distribution, and the use of drugs by drug use units. When necessary, it may conduct extended inspections of units and individuals that provide products or services for drug research and development, production, distribution, and use. The relevant units and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or conceal information.

The drug regulatory authority shall implement key supervision and inspection of high-risk drugs.

Where there is evidence that potential safety hazards may exist, the drug regulatory authority shall, based on the results of supervision and inspection, take measures such as warnings, interviews, orders for rectification within a time limit, and suspension of production, sale, use, and import, and shall promptly announce the inspection results.

When conducting supervision and inspection, the drug regulatory authority shall present supporting documents and shall keep confidential any trade secrets learned during the inspection.

Article 100 The drug regulatory authority may conduct random sampling and inspection of drug quality as needed for supervision and management. Random sampling and inspection shall be conducted in accordance with regulations, and no fees shall be charged; samples shall be purchased. The necessary expenses shall be allocated in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

For drugs and related materials that have evidence of potentially endangering human health, the drug regulatory authority may seal or seize them and shall make an administrative decision within seven days; if the drug requires testing, the administrative decision shall be made within fifteen days from the date the test report is issued.

Article 101 The drug regulatory authorities of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall regularly announce the results of drug quality random sampling and inspection; if the announcement is inappropriate, it shall be corrected within the original scope of the announcement.

Article 102 If a party disputes the drug test results, it may, within seven days from the date of receiving the drug test results, apply for a re-test to the original drug testing institution or a drug testing institution established or designated by the drug regulatory authority at the next higher level, or may directly apply for a re-test to a drug testing institution established or designated by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council. The drug testing institution that accepts the re-test application shall issue a re-test conclusion within the time limit specified by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 103 The drug regulatory authority shall inspect drug marketing authorization holders, drug manufacturers, drug distributors, institutions for non-clinical safety evaluation of drugs, and institutions for drug clinical trials to ensure their compliance with Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs, Good Supply Practice for Drugs, Good Laboratory Practice for Non-clinical Safety Studies, and Good Clinical Practice, and supervise their continuous compliance with legal requirements.

Article 104 The State shall establish a professional and specialized team of drug inspectors. Inspectors shall be familiar with drug laws and regulations and possess professional drug knowledge.

Article 105 The drug regulatory authority shall establish drug safety credit files for drug marketing authorization holders, drug manufacturers, drug distributors, institutions for non-clinical safety evaluation of drugs, institutions for drug clinical trials, and medical institutions, recording the issuance of licenses, results of routine supervision and inspection, and the investigation and punishment of illegal acts. These files shall be made public to society in accordance with the law and updated in a timely manner; for those with adverse credit records, the frequency of supervision and inspection shall be increased, and joint disciplinary actions may be implemented in accordance with national regulations.

Article 106 The drug regulatory authority shall publish its email address and telephone number to receive inquiries, complaints, and reports, and shall respond, verify, and handle them in a timely manner in accordance with the law. Rewards shall be given to whistleblowers whose reports are verified in accordance with relevant regulations.

The drug regulatory authority shall keep the information of whistleblowers confidential and protect their legitimate rights and interests. If a whistleblower reports their own unit, that unit shall not retaliate against the whistleblower by terminating or changing the labor contract or by other means.

Article 107 The State implements a unified system for the publication of drug safety information. The overall national drug safety situation, drug safety risk warning information, major drug safety incidents and their investigation and handling information, and other information determined by the State Council to require unified publication shall be published uniformly by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council. If the impact of drug safety risk warning information and major drug safety incidents and their investigation and handling information is limited to a specific region, it may also be published by the drug regulatory authority of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The above information shall not be released without authorization.

Drug safety information shall be published in a timely, accurate, and comprehensive manner, with necessary explanations to avoid misleading.

No unit or individual shall fabricate or disseminate false drug safety information.

Article 108 People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate emergency plans for drug safety incidents. Drug marketing authorization holders, drug manufacturers, drug distributors, and medical institutions shall formulate their own plans for handling drug safety incidents and organize training and emergency drills.

When a drug safety incident occurs, the people's government at or above the county level shall immediately organize response work in accordance with the emergency plan; the relevant units shall immediately take effective measures to handle the situation and prevent the harm from escalating.

Article 109 If the drug regulatory authority fails to promptly identify systemic risks in drug safety or fails to promptly eliminate drug safety hazards within its supervision and management area, the people's government at the same level or the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at the next higher level shall interview its principal responsible person.

If a local people's government fails to fulfill its drug safety responsibilities and fails to promptly eliminate major regional drug safety hazards, the people's government at the next higher level or the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at the next higher level shall interview its principal responsible person.

The interviewed department and local people's government shall immediately take measures to rectify the drug supervision and management work.

The interview situation and rectification status shall be included in the evaluation and assessment records of the drug supervision and management work of the relevant departments and local people's governments.

Article 110 Local people's governments and their drug regulatory authorities shall not restrict or exclude drugs produced by drug marketing authorization holders or drug manufacturers from other regions from entering their region by requiring drug testing, approval, or other means.

Article 111 The drug regulatory authority and its established or designated professional drug technical institutions shall not participate in drug production and distribution activities, nor shall they recommend, supervise the production, or supervise the sale of drugs in their name.

Staff members of the drug regulatory authority and its established or designated professional drug technical institutions shall not participate in drug production and distribution activities.

Article 112 Where the State Council has other special management provisions for narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, toxic drugs for medical use, radioactive drugs, precursor chemicals for drugs, etc., those provisions shall apply.

Article 113 If the drug regulatory authority discovers that a drug-related illegal act is suspected of constituting a crime, it shall promptly transfer the case to the public security organ.

If criminal liability is not required or exempted according to law, but administrative liability should be pursued, the public security organ, the people's procuratorate, and the people's court shall promptly transfer the case to the drug regulatory authority.

If the public security organ, the people's procuratorate, or the people's court requests assistance from the drug regulatory authority, the competent department of ecological environment, or other departments in providing test conclusions, expert opinions, or harmless treatment of the drugs involved, the relevant departments shall promptly provide such assistance.

## Chapter 11 Legal Liability

Article 114 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 115 Anyone who produces or sells drugs without obtaining a Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, or Medical Institution Preparation License shall be ordered to close down, and the illegally produced or sold drugs and illegal gains shall be confiscated. A fine of not less than 15 times but not more than 30 times the value of the illegally produced or sold drugs (including both sold and unsold drugs, the same below) shall also be imposed; if the value is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan.

Article 116 Anyone who produces or sells counterfeit drugs shall have the illegally produced or sold drugs and illegal gains confiscated, be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, have the drug approval document revoked, and be fined not less than 15 times but not more than 30 times the value of the illegally produced or sold drugs; if the value is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, or Medical Institution Preparation License shall be revoked, and the corresponding application shall not be accepted for ten years; if the drug marketing authorization holder is an overseas enterprise, the import of its drugs shall be prohibited for ten years.

Article 117 Anyone who produces or sells inferior drugs shall have the illegally produced or sold drugs and illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 10 times but not more than 20 times the value of the illegally produced or sold drugs; if the value of the illegally produced or wholesale drugs is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan; if the value of the illegally retailed drugs is less than 10,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the offender shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification until the drug approval document, Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, or Medical Institution Preparation License is revoked.

If the produced or sold Chinese herbal decoction pieces do not meet drug standards but do not affect safety or efficacy, the offender shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit and given a warning; a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan may also be imposed.

Article 118 If counterfeit drugs are produced or sold, or if inferior drugs are produced or sold under serious circumstances, the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall have their income obtained from the unit during the period of the illegal act confiscated, and be fined not less than 30% but not more than three times that income. They shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution activities, and may also be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

The raw materials, excipients, packaging materials, and production equipment specially used by the producer for producing counterfeit or inferior drugs shall be confiscated.

Article 119 If a drug use unit uses counterfeit or inferior drugs, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions for selling counterfeit drugs or retailing inferior drugs; if the circumstances are serious, and the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons hold a medical or health personnel practice certificate, that certificate shall also be revoked.

Article 120 Anyone who knows or should know that a drug is a counterfeit or inferior drug, or falls under items (1) to (5) of the first paragraph of Article 124 of this Law, and provides storage, transportation, or other convenient conditions for it, shall have all income from storage and transportation confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than five times but not more than fifteen times the illegal income shall also be imposed; if the illegal income is less than 50,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 50,000 yuan.

Article 121 A decision to penalize counterfeit or inferior drugs shall clearly state the quality test conclusions of the drug testing institution in accordance with the law.

Article 122 Anyone who forges, alters, leases, lends, or illegally buys or sells a license or drug approval document shall have the illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than five times but not more than fifteen times the illegal gains shall also be imposed, and the Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, Medical Institution Preparation License, or drug approval document shall be revoked. The legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and be prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution activities for ten years, and may also be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days; if the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan.

Article 123 Anyone who obtains a clinical trial permit, drug manufacturing permit, drug distribution permit, medical institution preparation permit, or drug registration permit by providing false certificates, data, materials, samples, or by other means shall have the relevant permit revoked, and the corresponding application shall not be accepted for ten years. A fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and be prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution activities for ten years, and may also be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

Article 124 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall have the illegally produced, imported, or sold drugs and illegal gains, as well as the raw materials, excipients, packaging materials, and production equipment specially used for illegal production, confiscated, be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and be fined not less than 15 times but not more than 30 times the value of the illegally produced, imported, or sold drugs; if the value is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the drug approval document shall be revoked until the Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, or Medical Institution Preparation License is revoked. The legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall have their income obtained from the unit during the period of the illegal act confiscated, and be fined not less than 30% but not more than three times that income, and be prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution activities for ten years to life, and may also be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days:

(1) Producing or importing drugs without obtaining a drug approval document;

(2) Producing or importing drugs using a drug approval document obtained by fraudulent means;

(3) Producing drugs using active pharmaceutical ingredients that have not been reviewed and approved;

(4) Selling drugs that should have been tested but were not;

(5) Producing or selling drugs prohibited by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council;

(6) Fabricating production or test records;

(7) Making major changes during the drug production process without approval.

Anyone who sells the drugs specified in items (1) to (3) of the preceding paragraph, or a drug use unit that uses the drugs specified in items (1) to (5) of the preceding paragraph, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph; if the circumstances are serious, and the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons of the drug use unit hold a medical or health personnel practice certificate, that certificate shall also be revoked.

If a small quantity of drugs that have been legally marketed abroad is imported without approval, and the circumstances are minor, the penalty may be reduced or exempted in accordance with the law.

Article 125 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall have the illegally produced or sold drugs and illegal gains, as well as packaging materials and containers, confiscated, be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the drug approval document, Drug Manufacturing License, and Drug Distribution License shall be revoked. The legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and be prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution activities for ten years to life:

(1) Conducting drug clinical trials without approval;

(2) Producing drugs using packaging materials or containers that come into direct contact with the drug and have not been reviewed, or selling such drugs;

(3) Using unapproved labels or package inserts.

Article 126 Except as otherwise provided in this Law, if a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug distributor, institution for non-clinical safety evaluation of drugs, or institution for drug clinical trials fails to comply with Good Manufacturing Practice for Drugs, Good Supply Practice for Drugs, Good Laboratory Practice for Non-clinical Safety Studies, or Good Clinical Practice, they shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit and given a warning; if they fail to rectify within the time limit, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and they shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification until the drug approval document, Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, etc., are revoked. Institutions for non-clinical safety evaluation of drugs and institutions for drug clinical trials shall not conduct non-clinical safety evaluation of drugs or drug clinical trials for five years. The legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible persons shall have their income obtained from the unit during the period of the illegal act confiscated, and be fined not less than 10% but not more than 50% of that income, and be prohibited from engaging in drug production and distribution and other activities for ten years to life.

Article 127 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit and given a warning; if they fail to rectify within the time limit, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Conducting bioequivalence tests without filing;

(2) During a drug clinical trial, if safety issues or other risks are discovered, the clinical trial sponsor fails to adjust the clinical trial plan in a timely manner, suspend or terminate the clinical trial, or fails to report to the drug regulatory authority of the State Council;

(3) Failing to establish and implement a drug traceability system in accordance with regulations;

(4) Failing to submit an annual report in accordance with regulations;

(5) Failing to file or report changes in the drug production process in accordance with regulations;

(6) Failing to formulate a post-marketing risk management plan for the drug;

(7) Failing to conduct post-marketing research or post-marketing evaluation in accordance with regulations.

Article 128 Except where penalties are imposed in accordance with the provisions for counterfeit or inferior drugs, if the drug packaging does not have a label or package insert printed or affixed in accordance with regulations, or the label or package insert does not indicate relevant information or print the specified markings in accordance with regulations, the offender shall be ordered to rectify and given a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the drug registration certificate shall be revoked.

Article 129 If a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug distributor, or medical institution, in violation of the provisions of this Law, purchases drugs from a source other than a drug marketing authorization holder or an enterprise with drug manufacturing or distribution qualifications, they shall be ordered to rectify, and the illegally purchased drugs and illegal gains shall be confiscated. A fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the value of the illegally purchased drugs shall also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than thirty times the value shall be imposed, and the drug approval document, Drug Manufacturing License, Drug Distribution License, or Medical Institution Practice License shall be revoked; if the value is less than 50,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 50,000 yuan.

Article 130 If a drug distributor, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to record the purchase and sale of drugs in accordance with regulations, fails to correctly explain the usage, dosage, and other matters when retailing drugs, or fails to dispense prescriptions in accordance with regulations, they shall be ordered to rectify and given a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the Drug Distribution License shall be revoked.Article 131: Where a third-party platform provider for online drug trading, in violation of this Law, fails to fulfill obligations such as qualification review, reporting, or cessation of online trading platform services, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification and be fined not less than RMB 2,000,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000.

Article 132: Where imported drugs that have obtained drug registration certificates are not filed with the drug regulatory authority at the port of entry as required, the party shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and given a warning; if corrections are not made within the specified period, the drug registration certificate shall be revoked.

Article 133: Where a medical institution, in violation of this Law, sells its prepared preparations on the market, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have the illegally sold preparations and illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than two times but not more than five times the value of the illegally sold preparations; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be fined not less than five times but not more than fifteen times the value of the preparations; if the value is less than RMB 50,000, it shall be calculated as RMB 50,000.

Article 134: Where a drug marketing authorization holder fails to conduct adverse drug reaction monitoring or report suspected adverse drug reactions as required, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and given a warning; if corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification and be fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000.

Where a drug trading enterprise fails to report suspected adverse drug reactions as required, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and given a warning; if corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification and be fined not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 500,000.

Where a medical institution fails to report suspected adverse drug reactions as required, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and given a warning; if corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be fined not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 135: Where a drug marketing authorization holder, after being ordered by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to recall drugs, refuses to do so, it shall be fined not less than five times but not more than ten times the value of the drugs to be recalled; if the value is less than RMB 100,000, it shall be calculated as RMB 100,000; if the circumstances are serious, the drug approval document, drug manufacturing license, and drug trading license shall be revoked, and the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 200,000. Where a drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or medical institution refuses to cooperate with the recall, it shall be fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 136: Where the drug marketing authorization holder is an overseas enterprise, and the enterprise legal person designated by it within China fails to fulfill relevant obligations as required by this Law, the provisions of this Law on the legal liability of drug marketing authorization holders shall apply.

Article 137: Where any of the following acts occurs, a heavier penalty shall be imposed within the range of penalties provided for in this Law:

(1) Passing off narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, toxic drugs for medical use, radioactive drugs, or precursor chemicals for drugs as other drugs, or passing off other drugs as the aforementioned drugs;
(2) Producing or selling counterfeit or substandard drugs primarily intended for pregnant women, parturient women, or children;
(3) Produced or sold biological products that are counterfeit or substandard drugs;
(4) Producing or selling counterfeit or substandard drugs, resulting in personal injury;
(5) Producing or selling counterfeit or substandard drugs again after being dealt with;
(6) Refusing or evading supervision and inspection, forging, destroying, or concealing relevant evidentiary materials, or unauthorized use of sealed or seized items.

Article 138: Where a drug testing institution issues a false testing report, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, removal from office, or dismissal according to law, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not more than RMB 50,000; if the circumstances are serious, their testing qualification shall be revoked. Where a drug testing institution issues an inaccurate testing result causing losses, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

Article 139: The administrative penalties provided for in Articles 115 to 138 of this Law shall be decided by the drug regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level according to its division of responsibilities; the revocation of permits or licenses shall be decided by the original approving or issuing authority.

Article 140: Where a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or medical institution employs personnel in violation of this Law, the drug regulatory authority or the health authority shall order the dismissal and impose a fine of not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 200,000.

Article 141: Where a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or medical institution gives or accepts kickbacks or other illegitimate benefits in drug procurement and sales, or where a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or their agent gives property or other illegitimate benefits to the responsible persons, drug procurement personnel, physicians, pharmacists, or other relevant personnel of medical institutions using their drugs, the market regulatory authority shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine of not less than RMB 300,000 but not more than RMB 3,000,000; if the circumstances are serious, the business licenses of the drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, and drug trading enterprise shall be revoked, and the drug regulatory authority shall revoke the drug approval documents, drug manufacturing license, and drug trading license.

Where a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, or drug trading enterprise bribes state functionaries in drug research, production, or trading, the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in drug production and trading activities.

Article 142: Where the responsible persons, procurement personnel, or other relevant personnel of a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, or drug trading enterprise accept property or other illegitimate benefits given by other drug marketing authorization holders, drug manufacturers, drug trading enterprises, or their agents in drug procurement and sales, the illegal gains shall be confiscated and penalties shall be imposed according to law; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be prohibited from engaging in drug production and trading activities for five years.

Where the responsible persons, drug procurement personnel, physicians, pharmacists, or other relevant personnel of a medical institution accept property or other illegitimate benefits given by a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or their agent, the health authority or the unit shall impose sanctions, confiscate illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, their practice certificates shall also be revoked.

Article 143: Where, in violation of this Law, false drug safety information is fabricated or disseminated, constituting a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose public security administration penalties according to law.

Article 144: Where a drug marketing authorization holder, drug manufacturer, drug trading enterprise, or medical institution, in violation of this Law, causes damage to drug users, it shall bear compensation liability according to law.

Where a person suffers damage due to drug quality issues, the victim may claim compensation for losses from the drug marketing authorization holder or drug manufacturer, or may claim compensation from the drug trading enterprise or medical institution. The party receiving the victim's compensation claim shall implement the first-responsibility system and make advance payment; after advance payment, it may seek recourse according to law.

Where counterfeit or substandard drugs are produced, or where such drugs are knowingly sold or used, the victim or their close relatives may, in addition to claiming compensation for losses, claim compensation of ten times the purchase price or three times the losses; if the increased compensation amount is less than RMB 1,000, it shall be RMB 1,000.

Article 145: Where a drug regulatory authority or its established or designated professional drug technical institution participates in drug production or trading activities, its superior competent authority shall order corrections and confiscate illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Where personnel of a drug regulatory authority or its established or designated professional drug technical institution participate in drug production or trading activities, sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 146: Where a drug regulatory authority or its established or designated drug testing institution illegally collects testing fees during drug supervision and testing, the relevant government department shall order the return of the fees, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious, their testing qualification shall be revoked.

Article 147: Where, in violation of this Law, a drug regulatory authority commits any of the following acts, the relevant permit shall be revoked, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Approving drug clinical trials for entities that do not meet the conditions;
(2) Issuing drug registration certificates for drugs that do not meet the conditions;
(3) Issuing drug manufacturing licenses, drug trading licenses, or medical institution preparation licenses to entities that do not meet the conditions.

Article 148: Where, in violation of this Law, a local people's government at or above the county level commits any of the following acts, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a demerit or major demerit; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be given demotion, removal from office, or dismissal:

(1) Concealing, falsely reporting, delaying reporting, or omitting reporting of drug safety incidents;
(2) Failing to promptly eliminate regional major drug safety hazards, resulting in the occurrence of particularly major drug safety incidents within its administrative region, or consecutive major drug safety incidents;
(3) Failing to perform duties effectively, causing serious adverse effects or significant losses.

Article 149: Where, in violation of this Law, a drug regulatory authority or other relevant department commits any of the following acts, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a demerit or major demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, they shall be given demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be given dismissal:

(1) Concealing, falsely reporting, delaying reporting, or omitting reporting of drug safety incidents;
(2) Failing to promptly investigate and deal with discovered illegal drug safety acts;
(3) Failing to promptly detect systemic drug safety risks, or failing to promptly eliminate drug safety hazards within the supervision and management area, causing serious impact;
(4) Other failures to perform drug supervision and management duties, causing serious adverse effects or significant losses.

Article 150: Where drug supervision and management personnel abuse their power, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or neglect their duties, sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Where there is dereliction of duty or malfeasance in investigating and dealing with illegal acts involving counterfeit or substandard drugs, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel of the drug regulatory authority shall be given heavier sanctions according to law.

Article 151: The value of goods referred to in this Chapter shall be calculated based on the marked price of the illegally produced or sold drugs; if there is no marked price, it shall be calculated based on the market price of similar drugs.

## Chapter 12 Supplementary Provisions

Article 152: The management of the cultivation, collection, and breeding of Chinese medicinal materials shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations.

Article 153: The administrative measures for regional folk medicinal materials shall be formulated by the drug regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the traditional Chinese medicine authority of the State Council.

Article 154: The specific measures for the implementation of this Law by the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 155: This Law shall come into force on December 1, 2019.
