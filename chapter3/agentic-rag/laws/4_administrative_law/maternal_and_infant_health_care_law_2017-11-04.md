# Maternal and Infant Health Care Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 27, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure the health of mothers and infants and to improve the quality of the newborn population.

Article 2 The State shall develop maternal and infant health care services, providing necessary conditions and material assistance so that mothers and infants may receive medical and health care services.

The State shall provide support for maternal and infant health care services in remote and impoverished areas.

Article 3 People's governments at all levels shall lead the work of maternal and infant health care.

Maternal and infant health care services shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plan.

Article 4 The health administration department under the State Council shall be responsible for the nationwide work of maternal and infant health care, proposing guiding principles for classification and grading according to the conditions of different regions, and exercising supervision and management over the nationwide work of maternal and infant health care.

Other relevant departments under the State Council shall, within their respective functions and duties, cooperate with the health administration department in carrying out the work of maternal and infant health care.

Article 5 The State encourages and supports education and scientific research in the field of maternal and infant health care, promotes advanced and practical technologies in maternal and infant health care, and disseminates scientific knowledge on maternal and infant health care.

Article 6 Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in the work of maternal and infant health care or have obtained remarkable results in scientific research on maternal and infant health care shall be given awards.

## Chapter II Pre-marital Health Care

Article 7 Medical and health care institutions shall provide pre-marital health care services to citizens.

Pre-marital health care services shall include the following:

(1) Pre-marital health guidance: education on sexual health knowledge, reproductive knowledge, and knowledge of genetic diseases;

(2) Pre-marital health consultation: provision of medical advice on issues such as marriage suitability and reproductive health;

(3) Pre-marital medical examination: medical examination of men and women who intend to marry for diseases that may affect marriage and childbearing.

Article 8 Pre-marital medical examination shall include examination for the following diseases:

(1) Serious genetic diseases;

(2) Specified infectious diseases;

(3) Relevant mental illnesses.

After a pre-marital medical examination, the medical and health care institution shall issue a pre-marital medical examination certificate.

Article 9 If, after a pre-marital medical examination, a person is found to have a specified infectious disease during its infectious period or a relevant mental illness during its active phase, the physician shall provide medical advice; the man and woman who intend to marry shall postpone the marriage.

Article 10 If, after a pre-marital medical examination, a person is diagnosed with a serious genetic disease that is medically considered unsuitable for childbearing, the physician shall explain the situation to both parties and provide medical advice; if both parties agree to adopt long-term contraceptive measures or undergo sterilization surgery and not have children, they may marry. However, this shall not apply to cases where marriage is prohibited under the Marriage Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 11 Persons who have undergone a pre-marital medical examination and have objections to the examination results may apply for a medical technical appraisal and obtain a medical appraisal certificate.

Article 12 When registering for marriage, both the man and the woman shall present a pre-marital medical examination certificate or a medical appraisal certificate.

Article 13 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on local conditions, formulate measures for implementing the pre-marital medical examination system.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall set reasonable fee standards for pre-marital medical examinations and shall provide reductions or exemptions for persons in remote and impoverished areas or those who have genuine difficulty in paying the fees.

## Chapter III Perinatal Health Care

Article 14 Medical and health care institutions shall provide perinatal health care services to women of childbearing age and pregnant and postpartum women.

Perinatal health care services shall include the following:

(1) Maternal and infant health guidance: provision of medical advice on bearing healthy offspring, as well as on the causes, treatment, and prevention methods of serious genetic diseases and endemic diseases such as iodine deficiency disorders;

(2) Health care for pregnant and postpartum women: provision of consultation and guidance on hygiene, nutrition, psychology, and other aspects, as well as regular prenatal check-ups and other medical and health care services;

(3) Fetal health care: monitoring of fetal growth and development, and provision of consultation and medical guidance;

(4) Newborn health care: provision of medical and health care services for the growth, development, breastfeeding, and care of newborns.

Article 15 If a pregnancy may endanger the life of the pregnant woman or may seriously affect her health and the normal development of the fetus due to a serious illness or exposure to teratogenic substances, the medical and health care institution shall provide medical guidance.

Article 16 If a physician discovers or suspects that a couple of childbearing age suffers from a serious genetic disease, the physician shall provide medical advice. The couple of childbearing age shall take corresponding measures based on the physician's medical advice.

Article 17 If, during a prenatal check-up, a physician discovers or suspects that the fetus is abnormal, the physician shall conduct a prenatal diagnosis for the pregnant woman.

Article 18 If, after a prenatal diagnosis, any of the following circumstances exists, the physician shall explain the situation to both spouses and provide medical advice on terminating the pregnancy:

(1) The fetus suffers from a serious genetic disease;

(2) The fetus has a serious defect;

(3) Due to a serious illness, continuing the pregnancy may endanger the life of the pregnant woman or seriously harm her health.

Article 19 The performance of pregnancy termination or sterilization surgery in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall be subject to the consent of the person concerned, who shall sign a statement of consent. If the person concerned lacks capacity for civil conduct, the consent of the guardian shall be obtained, and the guardian shall sign a statement of consent.

Persons who undergo pregnancy termination or sterilization surgery in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall receive free services.

Article 20 Before a woman who has given birth to a child with a serious defect becomes pregnant again, both spouses shall go to a medical and health care institution at or above the county level for a medical examination.

Article 21 Physicians and midwifery personnel shall strictly comply with relevant operating procedures, improve midwifery techniques and service quality, and prevent and reduce birth injuries.

Article 22 Pregnant women who cannot give birth in a hospital shall have their deliveries attended by trained birth attendants with appropriate delivery skills, using sterile delivery methods.

Article 23 Medical and health care institutions and persons engaged in home delivery shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administration department under the State Council, issue uniformly produced newborn birth medical certificates; in cases of maternal or infant death or newborn birth defects, they shall report to the health administration department.

Article 24 Medical and health care institutions shall provide guidance to postpartum women on scientific childcare, rational nutrition, and breastfeeding.

Medical and health care institutions shall conduct physical examinations and vaccinations for infants, and gradually carry out medical and health care services such as newborn disease screening and the prevention and treatment of common and frequently occurring infant diseases.

## Chapter IV Technical Appraisal

Article 25 People's governments at or above the county level may establish medical technical appraisal organizations responsible for conducting medical technical appraisals for those who have objections to the results of pre-marital medical examinations, genetic disease diagnoses, and prenatal diagnoses.

Article 26 Personnel engaged in medical technical appraisal must have clinical experience and knowledge of medical genetics, and must hold a professional technical position of attending physician or above.

The members of medical technical appraisal organizations shall be nominated by the health administration department and appointed by the people's government at the same level.

Article 27 A recusal system shall be implemented for medical technical appraisal. Any person who has an interest in the parties concerned that may affect an impartial appraisal shall recuse themselves.

## Chapter V Administrative Management

Article 28 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen maternal and infant health care work, improve the level of medical and health care services, actively prevent and treat endemic and high-incidence diseases caused by environmental factors that seriously endanger the health of mothers and infants, and promote the development of maternal and infant health care services.

Article 29 The health administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall manage the maternal and infant health care work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 30 Medical and health care institutions designated by the health administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for maternal and infant health care monitoring and technical guidance within their respective administrative regions.

Article 31 Medical and health care institutions shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administration department under the State Council, be responsible for maternal and infant health care work within their scope of duties, establish medical and health care work standards, improve medical technical levels, take various measures to facilitate the people, and provide good maternal and infant health care services.

Article 32 Medical and health care institutions that carry out pre-marital medical examinations, genetic disease diagnoses, prenatal diagnoses, and perform sterilization surgeries and pregnancy termination surgeries in accordance with the provisions of this Law must meet the conditions and technical standards prescribed by the health administration department under the State Council and must obtain permission from the health administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level.

The use of technical means for fetal sex identification is strictly prohibited, except where medically necessary.

Article 33 Personnel engaged in genetic disease diagnosis and prenatal diagnosis as provided for in this Law must pass the examination conducted by the health administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and obtain the corresponding qualification certificate.

Personnel engaged in pre-marital medical examinations, sterilization surgeries, and pregnancy termination surgeries as provided for in this Law must pass the examination conducted by the health administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level and obtain the corresponding qualification certificate.

Article 34 Personnel engaged in maternal and infant health care work shall strictly abide by professional ethics and maintain confidentiality for the parties concerned.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 35 If a person who has not obtained the relevant qualification certificate issued by the State commits any of the following acts, the health administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall stop the act and may, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning or impose a fine:

(1) Engaging in pre-marital medical examinations, genetic disease diagnoses, prenatal diagnoses, or medical technical appraisals;

(2) Performing pregnancy termination surgeries;

(3) Issuing relevant medical certificates as provided for in this Law.

The relevant medical certificates issued under item (3) of the preceding paragraph shall be invalid.

Article 36 If a person who has not obtained the relevant qualification certificate issued by the State performs a pregnancy termination surgery or uses other methods to terminate a pregnancy, causing death, disability, or loss or substantial loss of the ability to work, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 37 If a person engaged in maternal and infant health care work, in violation of the provisions of this Law, issues a false medical certificate or conducts fetal sex identification, the medical and health care institution or the health administration department shall impose administrative sanctions depending on the circumstances; if the circumstances are serious, the person's professional qualification shall be revoked in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 38 For the purposes of this Law, the meanings of the following terms are:

Specified infectious diseases refer to AIDS, gonorrhea, syphilis, leprosy, and other infectious diseases that are medically considered to affect marriage and childbearing, as stipulated in the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases.

Serious genetic diseases refer to genetic diseases that are congenital due to genetic factors, causing the patient to lose all or part of their ability to live independently, with a high risk of recurrence in offspring, and are medically considered unsuitable for childbearing.

Relevant mental illnesses refer to schizophrenia, manic-depressive psychosis, and other severe mental illnesses.

Prenatal diagnosis refers to the diagnosis of congenital defects and genetic diseases in the fetus.

Article 39 This Law shall come into force as of June 1, 1995.
