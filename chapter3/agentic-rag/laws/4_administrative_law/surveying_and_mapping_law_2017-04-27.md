# Surveying and Mapping Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on December 28, 1992

First Revision at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 29, 2002

Second Revision at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 27, 2017

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of strengthening the administration of surveying and mapping, promoting the development of the surveying and mapping industry, ensuring that surveying and mapping serves economic development, national defense construction, social development, and ecological protection, and safeguarding national geographic information security.

Article 2 Surveying and mapping activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall comply with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "surveying and mapping" means the activities of determining, collecting, and representing the shape, size, spatial position, and attributes of natural geographical elements or surface artificial facilities, as well as processing and providing the data, information, and results obtained.

Article 3 The surveying and mapping industry is a fundamental undertaking for economic development, national defense construction, and social development. People's governments at all levels shall strengthen their leadership over surveying and mapping work.

Article 4 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council shall be responsible for the unified supervision and administration of national surveying and mapping work. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for surveying and mapping work related to their respective functions as defined by the State Council.

The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the unified supervision and administration of surveying and mapping work within its administrative region. Other relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for surveying and mapping work related to their respective functions as defined by the people's government at the same level.

The military surveying and mapping department shall be responsible for administering surveying and mapping work within the military sector and, in accordance with the division of responsibilities defined by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, shall be responsible for administering marine basic surveying and mapping work.

Article 5 Those engaged in surveying and mapping activities shall use the surveying and mapping datums and systems prescribed by the State and comply with the technical specifications and standards for surveying and mapping prescribed by the State.

Article 6 The State encourages innovation and progress in surveying and mapping science and technology, adopts advanced technologies and equipment, improves surveying and mapping capabilities, promotes civil-military integration, and facilitates the application of surveying and mapping results. The State strengthens international exchanges and cooperation in surveying and mapping science and technology.

Units and individuals that have made significant contributions to the innovation and progress of surveying and mapping science and technology shall be rewarded in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall strengthen the publicity and education of national territory awareness, enhancing citizens' awareness of the national territory. News media shall carry out publicity on national territory awareness. Education administrative departments and schools shall incorporate education on national territory awareness into the teaching content of primary and secondary schools, strengthening patriotic education.

Article 8 Foreign organizations or individuals conducting surveying and mapping activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall obtain approval from the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council jointly with the military surveying and mapping department, and shall comply with the relevant laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China.

Foreign organizations or individuals conducting surveying and mapping activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall cooperate with relevant departments or units of the People's Republic of China and shall not involve state secrets or endanger national security.

## Chapter II Surveying and Mapping Datums and Systems

Article 9 The State establishes and adopts unified national geodetic datums, vertical datums, depth datums, and gravity datums. Their data shall be reviewed by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, and after consultation with other relevant departments under the State Council and the military surveying and mapping department, shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 10 The State establishes unified national geodetic coordinate systems, plane coordinate systems, vertical systems, geocentric coordinate systems, and gravity measurement systems, and determines the grades and accuracy of national geodetic surveys, as well as the series and basic accuracy of national basic scale maps. Specific specifications and requirements shall be formulated by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council jointly with other relevant departments under the State Council and the military surveying and mapping department.

Article 11 Where, due to the needs of construction, urban planning, and scientific research, major national projects and large cities designated by the State Council truly need to establish relatively independent plane coordinate systems, approval shall be obtained from the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council; where others truly need to establish such systems, approval shall be obtained from the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The establishment of relatively independent plane coordinate systems shall be linked to the national coordinate system.

Article 12 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council and the competent departments of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, jointly with other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, establish a unified satellite navigation and positioning reference service system based on the principles of coordinated construction and resource sharing, and provide public services for navigation and positioning reference information.

Article 13 For the construction of satellite navigation and positioning reference stations, the construction unit shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, report to the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council or the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for archival filing. The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council shall compile the nationwide filing information on the construction of satellite navigation and positioning reference stations and regularly notify the military surveying and mapping department.

For the purposes of this Law, "satellite navigation and positioning reference station" means a fixed ground observation station that conducts long-term continuous observation of satellite navigation signals and transmits the observation data to a data center in real-time or periodically through communication facilities.

Article 14 The construction, operation, and maintenance of satellite navigation and positioning reference stations shall comply with national standards and requirements and shall not endanger national security.

The units responsible for the construction, operation, and maintenance of satellite navigation and positioning reference stations shall establish a data security guarantee system and comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on confidentiality.

The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall, jointly with other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, strengthen the regulation and guidance for the construction, operation, and maintenance of satellite navigation and positioning reference stations.

## Chapter III Basic Surveying and Mapping

Article 15 Basic surveying and mapping is a public welfare undertaking. The State implements tiered management of basic surveying and mapping.

For the purposes of this Law, "basic surveying and mapping" means the activities of establishing unified national surveying and mapping datums and systems, conducting basic aerial photography, acquiring remote sensing data for basic geographic information, producing and updating national basic scale maps, image maps, and digital products, and establishing and updating basic geographic information systems.

Article 16 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, jointly with other relevant departments under the State Council and the military surveying and mapping department, shall organize the formulation of the national basic surveying and mapping plan, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the State Council for approval.

The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the local people's government at or above the county level, jointly with other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, shall, based on the basic surveying and mapping plans of the State and the people's government at the next higher level and the actual conditions of its administrative region, organize the formulation of the basic surveying and mapping plan for its administrative region, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval.

Article 17 The military surveying and mapping department shall be responsible for formulating military surveying and mapping plans, and, in accordance with the division of responsibilities defined by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, shall be responsible for formulating and implementing marine basic surveying and mapping plans.

Article 18 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate basic surveying and mapping into the annual plan for national economic and social development at the same level and include the funds required for basic surveying and mapping work in the budget of the government at the same level.

The development and reform department under the State Council, jointly with the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, shall formulate the annual national basic surveying and mapping plan based on the national basic surveying and mapping plan.

The development and reform department of the local people's government at or above the county level, jointly with the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at the same level, shall formulate the annual basic surveying and mapping plan for its administrative region based on the basic surveying and mapping plan for that region, and shall report it to the department at the next higher level for archival filing.

Article 19 Basic surveying and mapping results shall be updated periodically. Basic surveying and mapping results urgently needed for economic development, national defense construction, social development, and ecological protection shall be updated promptly.

The update cycle for basic surveying and mapping results shall be determined based on the needs of national economic and social development in different regions.

## Chapter IV Boundary Surveying and Mapping and Other Surveying and Mapping

Article 20 The surveying and mapping of the national boundaries of the People's Republic of China shall be carried out in accordance with the boundary treaties or agreements concluded between the People's Republic of China and neighboring countries, and shall be organized and implemented by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The standard sample maps of the national boundaries of the maps of the People's Republic of China shall be drafted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, and shall be published after being submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 21 The surveying and mapping of administrative boundary lines shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council. The standard drawing maps of the administrative boundary lines of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, counties, autonomous counties, and cities shall be drafted by the civil affairs department under the State Council and the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, and shall be published after being submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 22 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall, jointly with the competent department of real estate registration of the people's government at the same level, strengthen the administration of real estate surveying and mapping.

The measurement of the boundary lines of ownership of land, buildings, structures, and other attachments on the ground shall be carried out based on the boundary points and boundary lines of the ownership boundaries determined by the people's government at or above the county level, or the relevant registration data and attached maps provided. Where the ownership boundary lines change, the relevant parties shall promptly conduct change surveying and mapping.

Article 23 Engineering surveying activities in the field of urban and rural construction, and the measurement of building areas related to housing property rights and property registers, shall comply with the surveying technical specifications organized and formulated by the competent department of housing and urban-rural construction under the State Council and the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council.

Engineering surveying activities in the fields of water conservancy, energy, transportation, communications, resource development, and other areas shall comply with the relevant national engineering surveying technical specifications.

Article 24 The establishment of geographic information systems shall use basic geographic information data that conforms to national standards.

Article 25 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of emergency response work, promptly provide surveying and mapping results such as maps and basic geographic information data, and carry out emergency surveying and mapping support work such as remote sensing monitoring and navigation and positioning.

Article 26 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall, jointly with other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, carry out geographic national conditions monitoring in accordance with the law, and strictly manage and standardize the use of geographic national conditions monitoring results in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

People's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to leverage the role of geographic national conditions monitoring results in government decision-making, economic and social development, and public services.

## Chapter V Surveying and Mapping Qualifications and Credentials

Article 27 The State implements a surveying and mapping qualification administration system for units engaged in surveying and mapping activities.

Units engaged in surveying and mapping activities shall meet the following conditions and obtain a surveying and mapping qualification certificate of the corresponding grade in accordance with the law before they may engage in surveying and mapping activities:

(1) Possess legal person status;
(2) Have professional and technical personnel suitable for the surveying and mapping activities to be undertaken;
(3) Have technical equipment and facilities suitable for the surveying and mapping activities to be undertaken;
(4) Have a sound technical and quality assurance system, safety guarantee measures, information security and confidentiality management system, and a management system for surveying and mapping results and data archives.

Article 28 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council and the competent departments of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for reviewing surveying and mapping qualifications and issuing surveying and mapping qualification certificates. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council in consultation with other relevant departments under the State Council.

The military surveying and mapping department shall be responsible for reviewing the surveying and mapping qualifications of military surveying and mapping units.

Article 29 Surveying and mapping units shall not engage in surveying and mapping activities beyond the scope permitted by their qualification grade, shall not engage in surveying and mapping activities in the name of other surveying and mapping units, and shall not allow other units to engage in surveying and mapping activities in their name.

Where surveying and mapping projects are subject to bidding, the tenderee of the surveying and mapping project shall, in accordance with the law, specify the requirement for the qualification grade of the surveying and mapping unit in the tender notice or the invitation to bid, shall not allow a unit that does not have the corresponding surveying and mapping qualification grade to win the bid, and shall not allow a surveying and mapping unit to win the bid at a price lower than the surveying and mapping cost.

The surveying and mapping unit that wins the bid shall not transfer the surveying and mapping project to others.

Article 30 Professional and technical personnel engaged in surveying and mapping activities shall meet the corresponding professional practice qualification requirements. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council jointly with the competent department of human resources and social security under the State Council.

Article 31 When conducting surveying and mapping activities, surveyors and mappers shall hold a surveying and mapping work certificate.

No unit or individual shall obstruct surveyors and mappers from conducting surveying and mapping activities in accordance with the law.

Article 32 The styles of the surveying and mapping qualification certificate for surveying and mapping units, the professional practice certificate for surveying and mapping professional and technical personnel, and the surveying and mapping work certificate for surveyors and mappers shall be uniformly prescribed by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council.

## Chapter VI Surveying and Mapping Results

Article 33 The State implements a system for the submission of surveying and mapping results. The State protects the intellectual property rights of surveying and mapping results in accordance with the law.

After the completion of a surveying and mapping project, the project sponsor or the unit undertaking a state-invested surveying and mapping project shall submit the surveying and mapping results data to the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council or the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. For basic surveying and mapping projects, a copy of the surveying and mapping results shall be submitted; for non-basic surveying and mapping projects, a catalogue of the surveying and mapping results shall be submitted. The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation responsible for receiving copies and catalogues of surveying and mapping results shall issue a receipt for the submission of surveying and mapping results and promptly transfer the copies and catalogues to the custodian unit. Specific measures for the submission of surveying and mapping results shall be formulated by the State Council.

The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council and the competent departments of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall promptly compile catalogues of surveying and mapping results and make them public.

Article 34 The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall actively promote the processing and compilation of public version surveying and mapping results, and promote the social application of surveying and mapping results by providing public version surveying and mapping results and employing confidentiality technical processing methods.

The custodian unit of surveying and mapping results shall take measures to ensure the integrity and security of the surveying and mapping results, and shall make them public and available for use in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Where surveying and mapping results are state secrets, the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on confidentiality shall apply; where they need to be provided to foreign parties, the approval procedures prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission shall be followed.

The scope and classification level of secrets for surveying and mapping results shall be determined, adjusted, and published in a timely manner in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on confidentiality, based on the principles of ensuring the security of state secrets and promoting the sharing and application of geographic information.

Article 35 For surveying and mapping projects using fiscal funds and other projects using fiscal funds that involve surveying and mapping, the relevant departments shall, before approving the project initiation, seek the opinions of the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at the same level; where suitable surveying and mapping results exist, existing surveying and mapping results shall be fully utilized to avoid duplicate surveying and mapping.

Article 36 Basic surveying and mapping results and other surveying and mapping results completed with state investment, when used for government decision-making, national defense construction, and public services, shall be provided free of charge.

Except for the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, surveying and mapping results shall be subject to a paid use system in accordance with the law. However, people's governments at all levels, relevant departments, and the military may use them free of charge for the needs of the public interest, such as disaster prevention and mitigation, emergency response, and safeguarding national security.

Specific measures for the use of surveying and mapping results shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 37 Important geographic information data such as the location, elevation, depth, area, and length of the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be reviewed by the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation under the State Council, and after consultation with other relevant departments under the State Council and the military surveying and mapping department, shall be submitted to the State Council for approval, and shall be published by the State Council or a department authorized by the State Council.

Article 38 The compilation, publication, display, loading, and updating of maps shall comply with the state regulations on map compilation standards, map content representation, and map review.

Internet map service providers shall use maps that have been reviewed and approved in accordance with the law, establish a map data security management system, adopt security measures, strengthen the verification and checking of new content on internet maps, and improve service quality.

People's governments at or above the county level, the competent departments of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation, the cyberspace administration departments, and other relevant departments shall strengthen the supervision and administration of map compilation, publication, display, loading, and internet map services, ensure map quality, and safeguard national sovereignty, security, and interests.

Specific measures for map administration shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 39 Surveying and mapping units shall be responsible for the quality of the surveying and mapping results they produce. The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and administration of the quality of surveying and mapping results.

Article 40 The State encourages the development of the geographic information industry, promotes the structural adjustment and optimization and upgrading of the geographic information industry, supports the development of various types of geographic information products, improves product quality, and promotes the use of secure and trustworthy geographic information technologies and equipment.

People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve the mechanism for the co-construction and sharing of geographic information resources among government departments, guide and support enterprises in providing socialized geographic information services, and promote the widespread application of geographic information.

The competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government at or above the county level shall promptly acquire, process, and update basic geographic information data, provide public geographic information services to society through the geographic information public service platform, and achieve the open sharing of geographic information data.

## Chapter VII Protection of Surveying Markers

Article 41 No unit or individual may damage or move permanent surveying markers or temporary surveying markers in use without authorization, may not encroach upon the land used for permanent surveying markers, and may not engage in activities within the safety control range of permanent surveying markers that endanger the safety and usability of the surveying markers.

For the purposes of this Law, "permanent surveying markers" means the observation targets and stone markers of triangulation points, baseline points, traverse points, military control points, gravity points, astronomical points, leveling points, and satellite positioning points of various grades, as well as fixed markers used for topographic mapping, engineering surveying, and deformation surveying, and seabed geodetic point facilities.

Article 42 The construction unit of permanent surveying markers shall set clear markings on the permanent surveying markers and entrust a local relevant unit to assign a specific person to be responsible for their safekeeping.

Article 43 Engineering construction shall avoid permanent surveying markers; where it is truly impossible to avoid them and the permanent surveying markers need to be relocated or rendered unusable, approval shall be obtained from the competent department of surveying, mapping, and geoinformation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; where military control points are involved, the consent of the military surveying and mapping department shall be obtained. The costs required for relocation shall be borne by the engineering construction unit.

Article 44 Surveyors and mappers using permanent surveying markers shall hold a surveying and mapping work certificate and shall ensure the integrity of the surveying markers.

The person responsible for safekeeping the surveying markers shall inspect their condition after use to ensure they are intact.

Article 45 People's governments at or above the county level shall take effective measures to strengthen the protection of surveying markers.The competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions, inspect and maintain permanent surveying markers.

The people's government at the township level shall do a good job in protecting surveying markers within its administrative area.

## Chapter VIII Supervision and Administration

Article 46 The competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in conjunction with other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, establish a geo-information security management system and a technical prevention and control system, and strengthen the supervision and administration of geo-information security.

Article 47 Entities that produce, store, or use geo-information shall register and permanently keep records of the acquisition, possession, provision, and use of geo-information classified as state secrets, and implement traceability management.

Engaging in surveying and mapping activities involving the acquisition, possession, provision, or use of geo-information classified as state secrets shall comply with the laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions on secrecy.

Where entities that produce or use geo-information and Internet map service providers collect or use users' personal information, they shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on the protection of personal information.

Article 48 The competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level shall implement credit management for surveying and mapping entities and publicly disclose their credit information in accordance with the law.

Article 49 The competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level shall establish and improve a random inspection mechanism, perform supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law, and if it discovers any act suspected of violating the provisions of this Law, it may take the following measures in accordance with the law:

(1) Inspect and copy relevant contracts, bills, account books, registration ledgers, and other relevant documents and materials;

(2) Seal up or seize equipment, tools, raw materials, surveying and mapping results and materials, etc., directly related to the suspected illegal surveying and mapping activities.

The entity or individual being inspected shall cooperate and truthfully provide relevant documents and materials, and shall not conceal, refuse, or obstruct.

Any entity or individual has the right to report to the competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level any act that violates the provisions of this Law. The competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information that receives the report shall handle it promptly in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

Article 50 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, staff members of the competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments take advantage of their positions to accept property or other benefits from others, or neglect their duties, issue surveying and mapping qualification certificates to entities that do not meet the statutory conditions, fail to perform supervision and administration duties in accordance with the law, or fail to investigate and deal with illegal acts discovered, the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 51 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a foreign organization or individual, without approval, or without cooperating with the relevant departments or entities of the People's Republic of China, engages in surveying and mapping activities without authorization, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal gains, surveying and mapping results, and surveying and mapping tools shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the offender shall be ordered to leave the country within a time limit or be deported; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 52 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a relatively independent plane coordinate system is established without authorization, or a geographic information system is established using basic geo-information data that does not conform to national standards, a warning shall be given, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan may also be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the construction entity of a satellite navigation and positioning reference station fails to file for the record, a warning shall be given, and a correction order shall be issued within a time limit; if no correction is made within the time limit, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the construction, operation, and maintenance of a satellite navigation and positioning reference station do not conform to national standards or requirements, a warning shall be given, a correction order shall be issued within a time limit, the illegal gains and surveying and mapping results shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 300,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if no correction is made within the time limit, the relevant equipment shall be confiscated; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 55 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person engages in surveying and mapping activities without obtaining a surveying and mapping qualification certificate, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal gains and surveying and mapping results shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the agreed remuneration for surveying and mapping shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the surveying and mapping tools shall be confiscated.

Where a person obtains a surveying and mapping qualification certificate by fraudulent means and engages in surveying and mapping activities, the surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked, the illegal gains and surveying and mapping results shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the agreed remuneration for surveying and mapping shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the surveying and mapping tools shall be confiscated.

Article 56 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a surveying and mapping entity commits any of the following acts, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal gains and surveying and mapping results shall be confiscated, a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the agreed remuneration for surveying and mapping shall be imposed, and the entity may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its surveying and mapping qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, the surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked:

(1) Engaging in surveying and mapping activities beyond the scope permitted by its qualification grade;

(2) Engaging in surveying and mapping activities in the name of another surveying and mapping entity;

(3) Allowing another entity to engage in surveying and mapping activities in its own name.

Article 57 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the tenderee of a surveying and mapping project allows a surveying and mapping entity that does not have the corresponding qualification grade to win the bid, or allows a surveying and mapping entity to win the bid at a price lower than the surveying and mapping cost, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not more than two times the agreed remuneration for surveying and mapping may be imposed. Where staff members of the tenderee take advantage of their positions to solicit property from others, or illegally accept property from others to seek benefits for others, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 58 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the winning bidder of a surveying and mapping project transfers the surveying and mapping project to another person, a correction order shall be issued, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the agreed remuneration for surveying and mapping shall be imposed, and the entity may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its surveying and mapping qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, the surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked.

Article 59 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person engages in surveying and mapping activities without obtaining a surveying and mapping professional qualification, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal gains and surveying and mapping results shall be confiscated, and the entity to which the person belongs may be fined not more than two times the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, the surveying and mapping tools shall be confiscated; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 60 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, surveying and mapping results and materials are not submitted for centralized management, a submission order shall be issued within a time limit; if the investor of the surveying and mapping project fails to submit within the time limit, a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the cost of re-surveying shall be imposed; if the entity undertaking a state-invested surveying and mapping project fails to submit within the time limit, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be temporarily seized; if the entity still fails to submit within six months from the date of temporary seizure of the surveying and mapping qualification certificate, the surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 61 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, important geographic information data of the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China is released without authorization, a warning shall be given, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan may also be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 62 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the compilation, publication, display, loading, or updating of maps or Internet map services does not comply with the relevant state regulations on map management, administrative penalties or sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 63 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the quality of surveying and mapping results is substandard, the surveying and mapping entity shall be ordered to conduct supplementary surveying or re-surveying; if the circumstances are serious, the entity shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, and its surveying and mapping qualification grade shall be lowered or its surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 64 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts is committed, a warning shall be given, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan may also be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Damaging or moving permanent surveying markers or temporary surveying markers in use without authorization;

(2) Encroaching upon land used for permanent surveying markers;

(3) Engaging in activities within the safety control range of permanent surveying markers that endanger the safety and use effectiveness of the surveying markers;

(4) Demolishing permanent surveying markers without authorization or rendering permanent surveying markers ineffective, or refusing to pay relocation and reconstruction costs;

(5) Using permanent surveying markers in violation of operating procedures, causing damage to permanent surveying markers.

Article 65 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an entity that produces, stores, or uses geo-information fails to register and permanently keep records of the acquisition, possession, provision, and use of geo-information classified as state secrets, a warning shall be given, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan may also be imposed; if state secrets are leaked, the entity shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, and its surveying and mapping qualification grade shall be lowered or its surveying and mapping qualification certificate shall be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person acquires, possesses, provides, or uses geo-information classified as state secrets, a warning shall be given, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than two times the illegal gains may also be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 66 The administrative penalties of lowering the surveying and mapping qualification grade, temporarily seizing the surveying and mapping qualification certificate, and revoking the surveying and mapping qualification certificate as provided for in this Law shall be decided by the department that issued the surveying and mapping qualification certificate; other administrative penalties shall be decided by the competent department of surveying, mapping and geo-information of the people's government at or above the county level.

The orders to leave the country within a time limit and deportation provided for in Article 51 of this Law shall be decided and executed by the public security organs in accordance with the law.

## Chapter X Supplementary Provisions

Article 67 Measures for the administration of military surveying and mapping shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 68 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 2017.
