# Archives Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 5, 1987

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Archives Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 5, 1996

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Revision adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on June 20, 2020

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to strengthen archives management, standardize the collection and arrangement of archives, effectively protect and utilize archives, enhance the level of archives informatization, promote the modernization of the national governance system and governance capacity, and serve the cause of socialism with Chinese characteristics.

**Article 2** This Law applies to activities involving the collection, arrangement, protection, utilization, and supervision and management of archives.

For the purposes of this Law, "archives" refers to historical records in various forms, such as texts, charts, audio-visual materials, etc., directly formed by state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, other organizations, and individuals in the past and present in their economic, political, cultural, social, ecological civilization, military, foreign affairs, scientific and technological, and other activities, which have preservation value to the state and society.

**Article 3** The leadership of the Communist Party of China over archives work shall be upheld. People's governments at all levels shall strengthen archives work, incorporate the archives undertaking into national economic and social development plans, include archives development funds in government budgets, and ensure that the development of the archives undertaking is commensurate with the level of national economic and social development.

**Article 4** Archives work shall adhere to the principles of unified leadership and hierarchical management to maintain the integrity and security of archives and facilitate their utilization by all sectors of society.

**Article 5** All state organs, armed forces, political parties, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and citizens have the obligation to protect archives and the right to utilize archives in accordance with the law.

**Article 6** The state encourages and supports scientific research and technological innovation in archives, promotes the transformation and application of scientific and technological achievements in the collection, arrangement, protection, and utilization of archives, and advances progress in archives science and technology.

The state shall take measures to strengthen archives publicity and education, enhancing the entire society's awareness of archives.

The state encourages and supports international exchanges and cooperation in the field of archives.

**Article 7** The state encourages social forces to participate in and support the development of the archives undertaking.

Units and individuals that make outstanding contributions to the collection, arrangement, protection, and utilization of archives shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Archives Institutions and Their Functions

**Article 8** The national archives administrative department shall be in charge of archives work nationwide, responsible for the overall planning and organization and coordination of the national archives undertaking, establishing a unified system, and exercising supervision and guidance.

The local archives administrative departments at or above the county level shall be in charge of archives work within their respective administrative regions, and exercise supervision and guidance over the archives work of state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations within their administrative regions.

Township people's governments shall designate personnel to be responsible for managing the archives of their own organs, and exercise supervision and guidance over the archives work of their subordinate units and grassroots self-governing organizations.

**Article 9** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall designate archives institutions or archives staff to be responsible for managing the archives of their own units, and exercise supervision and guidance over the archives work of their subordinate units.

Central state organs shall, based on archives management needs, guide the archives business work of their respective systems within the scope of their functions.

**Article 10** Central and local archives at all levels at or above the county level are cultural institutions dedicated to the centralized management of archives, responsible for collecting, arranging, preserving, and providing access to archives within their respective scopes of responsibility.

**Article 11** The state shall strengthen the training of archives professionals and team building, improving the professional quality of archives staff.

Archives staff shall be devoted to their duties, abide by laws and regulations, and possess corresponding professional knowledge and skills. Archives professionals may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, be evaluated for professional technical titles.

## Chapter III Management of Archives

**Article 12** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations that are required by state regulations to form archives shall establish an archives work responsibility system and improve archives management systems in accordance with the law.

**Article 13** The following materials directly formed that have preservation value to the state and society shall be included in the archiving scope:

(1) Materials reflecting the organizational evolution and main functional activities of state organs and social organizations;
(2) Materials reflecting the main research and development, construction, production, operation, and service activities of state-owned enterprises and public institutions, as well as the protection of the rights and interests of state-owned enterprises, public institutions, and their employees;
(3) Materials reflecting the urban and rural community governance and service activities of grassroots self-governing organizations;
(4) Materials reflecting state governance activities, economic and technological development, social and historical features, cultural customs, and ecological environment in various historical periods;
(5) Other materials that laws and administrative regulations stipulate shall be archived.

Non-state-owned enterprises, social service institutions, and other entities shall preserve their own relevant materials in accordance with the scope listed in item (2) of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 14** Materials that should be archived shall be regularly transferred to the archives institution or archives staff of the unit in accordance with state regulations for centralized management. No individual may refuse to archive or take them as personal property.

Materials that state regulations prohibit from being archived shall not be archived without authorization.

**Article 15** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall regularly transfer archives to archives in accordance with state regulations, and archives shall not refuse to accept them.

If archives are transferred to an archives for preservation earlier with the consent of the archives, before the expiration of the statutory transfer period, matters concerning the disclosure of government information related to such archives shall still be handled by the unit that originally created or preserved the government information. After the transfer period expires, archives involving government information disclosure matters shall be handled in accordance with archives utilization regulations.

**Article 16** When state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations undergo organizational changes, dissolution, merger, or other situations, they shall transfer archives to relevant units or archives in accordance with regulations.

**Article 17** In addition to receiving transferred archives in accordance with state regulations, archives may also collect archives through acceptance of donations, purchases, deposits, and other means.

**Article 18** Where cultural relics, documents, and information preserved by museums, libraries, memorial halls, and other units are also archives, they may be managed by the aforementioned units themselves in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Archives and the units listed in the preceding paragraph shall cooperate with each other in the utilization of archives, may exchange duplicates, copies, or catalogs, jointly hold exhibitions, and jointly research, edit, and publish relevant historical materials.

**Article 19** Archives, as well as the archives institutions of state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations, shall establish scientific management systems to facilitate the utilization of archives; configure warehouses suitable for archives preservation and necessary facilities and equipment in accordance with state regulations to ensure the safety of archives; and adopt advanced technologies to achieve the modernization of archives management.

Archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall establish and improve archives security working mechanisms, strengthen archives security risk management, and improve the capacity for emergency response to archives security incidents.

**Article 20** The management and utilization of archives involving state secrets, as well as the change and declassification of their classification levels, shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations on the protection of state secrets.

**Article 21** The principles for appraising the preservation value of archives, the standards for retention periods, and the procedures and methods for destroying archives shall be formulated by the national archives administrative department.

Tampering with, damaging, or forging archives is prohibited. Unauthorized destruction of archives is prohibited.

**Article 22** Archives formed by non-state-owned enterprises, social service institutions, and other entities and individuals that have significant preservation value to the state and society or should be kept confidential shall be properly preserved by the archives owner. If the preservation conditions do not meet the requirements or there are other reasons that may cause serious damage or insecurity to the archives, the archives administrative department at or above the provincial level may provide assistance, or take measures such as designating an archives to preserve them on behalf of the owner after consultation to ensure the integrity and safety of the archives; when necessary, it may purchase or requisition them in accordance with the law.

The archives mentioned in the preceding paragraph may be deposited or transferred by the archives owner to a state archives. It is strictly prohibited to sell or give them to foreigners or foreign organizations.

If important or precious archives are donated to the state, the state archives shall give rewards in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 23** The sale of archives owned by the state is prohibited.

When assets of state-owned enterprises and public institutions are transferred, the specific measures for transferring relevant archives shall be formulated by the national archives administrative department.

The exchange and transfer of copies of archives shall be handled in accordance with state regulations.

**Article 24** When archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations entrust archives arrangement, deposit, development and utilization, digitization, and other services, they shall sign an entrustment agreement with qualified archives service enterprises, stipulating the scope, quality, technical standards, and other contents of the services, and supervise the entrusted party.

The entrusted party shall establish an archives service management system, comply with relevant safety and confidentiality regulations, and ensure the safety of the archives.

**Article 25** Archives owned by the state and the archives specified in Article 22 of this Law, as well as their copies, are prohibited from being transported, mailed, carried out of the country without authorization, or transmitted out of the country via the internet. If it is truly necessary to take them out of the country, approval procedures shall be handled in accordance with state regulations.

**Article 26** The national archives administrative department shall establish and improve a working mechanism for the collection, arrangement, protection, and utilization of archives related to emergency response activities.

Archives shall strengthen the research, arrangement, development, and utilization of archives related to emergency response activities, providing literature reference and decision-making support for emergency response activities.

## Chapter IV Utilization and Publication of Archives

**Article 27** Archives in archives at or above the county level shall be opened to the public twenty-five years after their formation. Archives in categories such as economy, education, science and technology, and culture may be opened to the public in less than twenty-five years; archives involving national security or major interests, or those that are not suitable for opening upon expiration, may be opened to the public after more than twenty-five years. The state encourages and supports other archives in opening archives to the public. The specific measures for opening archives shall be formulated by the national archives administrative department and submitted to the State Council for approval.

**Article 28** Archives shall regularly publish the catalogues of open archives through their websites or other means, continuously improve utilization rules, innovate service forms, strengthen service functions, improve service levels, actively create conditions for the utilization of archives, simplify procedures, and provide convenience.

Units and individuals holding legal certificates may utilize archives that have been opened. If an archives fails to open and provide access in accordance with regulations, units and individuals may file a complaint with the archives administrative department. The archives administrative department that receives the complaint shall promptly investigate and handle the matter and inform the complainant of the result.

The utilization of archives involving intellectual property rights or personal information shall comply with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 29** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, other organizations, and citizens may, in accordance with state regulations, utilize archives not yet opened in archives, as well as archives preserved by relevant state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations, based on the needs of economic construction, national defense construction, teaching and scientific research, and other work.

**Article 30** The review for the opening of archives in the custody of an archives shall be jointly responsible by the archives and the unit that formed or transferred the archives. The review for the opening of archives that have not yet been transferred to an archives shall be responsible by the unit that formed or preserved the archives, and opinions shall be attached at the time of transfer.

**Article 31** Units and individuals that transfer, donate, or deposit archives to an archives may have priority in utilizing those archives and may propose opinions on restricting the use of parts of the archives that are not suitable for public access. The archives shall support this and provide convenience.

**Article 32** Archives owned by the state shall be published by the state-authorized archives or relevant state organs; without the consent of the archives or relevant state organs, no unit or individual has the right to publish them. For archives formed by non-state-owned enterprises, social service institutions, and other entities and individuals, the archives owner has the right to publish them.

The publication of archives shall comply with relevant laws and administrative regulations, shall not harm national security and interests, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of others.

**Article 33** Archives shall, based on their own conditions, provide support and convenience for state organs in formulating laws, regulations, and policies and conducting research on related issues.

Archives shall equip researchers, strengthen the research and arrangement of archives, and plan the editing and publication of archival materials for distribution within different scopes.

Archives researchers studying and arranging archives shall comply with the regulations on archives management.

**Article 34** The state encourages archives to develop and utilize their collections, carry out activities such as thematic exhibitions, public lectures, and media publicity to conduct education on patriotism, collectivism, and socialism with Chinese characteristics, inherit and develop the excellent traditional Chinese culture, inherit revolutionary culture, develop advanced socialist culture, enhance cultural confidence, and promote the core socialist values.

## Chapter V Archives Informatization Construction

**Article 35** People's governments at all levels shall incorporate archives informatization into informatization development plans, ensuring the safe preservation and effective utilization of archives digital resources such as electronic archives and digitized results of traditional carrier archives.

Archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall strengthen archives informatization construction and take measures to ensure the security of archives information.

**Article 36** State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall actively promote the construction of electronic archives management information systems, ensuring interconnection with office automation systems, business systems, etc.

**Article 37** Electronic archives shall have reliable sources, standardized procedures, and compliant elements.

Electronic archives have the same validity as traditional carrier archives and may be used as evidence in electronic form.

The measures for managing electronic archives shall be formulated by the national archives administrative department in conjunction with relevant departments.

**Article 38** The state encourages and supports archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations in promoting the digitization of traditional carrier archives. Once digitized, the original archives shall be properly preserved.

**Article 39** Electronic archives shall be transferred to archives via networks or storage media that meet security management requirements.

Archives shall inspect received electronic archives to ensure their authenticity, integrity, usability, and security.

Archives may implement off-site backup preservation for important electronic archives.

**Article 40** Archives are responsible for the collection, preservation, and provision of access to archives digital resources. Archives with the necessary conditions shall build digital archives.

**Article 41** The state promotes the construction of an archives information resource sharing service platform, facilitating the cross-regional and cross-departmental sharing and utilization of archives digital resources.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

**Article 42** The archives administrative department may, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on archives management, inspect the following situations of archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations:

(1) Implementation of the archives work responsibility system and management system;
(2) Configuration and use of archives warehouses, facilities, and equipment;
(3) Management of archives staff;
(4) Collection, arrangement, preservation, and provision of access to archives;
(5) Archives informatization construction and information security assurance;
(6) Supervision and guidance of the archives work of subordinate units, etc.

**Article 43** When conducting inspections based on clues of violations, the archives administrative department may, under the premise of meeting safety and confidentiality requirements, inspect relevant warehouses, facilities, and equipment, consult relevant materials, question relevant personnel, and record relevant information. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

**Article 44** If archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations discover archives security risks in their own units, they shall promptly take remedial measures to eliminate the archives security risks. In the event of archives damage, information leakage, or other situations, they shall promptly report to the archives administrative department.

**Article 45** If the archives administrative department discovers that archives and state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations have archives security risks, it shall order them to rectify within a time limit to eliminate the archives security risks.

**Article 46** Any unit or individual has the right to report illegal acts related to archives to the archives administrative department and relevant state organs.

The archives administrative department or relevant state organ that receives the report shall promptly handle it in accordance with the law.

**Article 47** The archives administrative department and its staff shall carry out supervision and inspection work in accordance with statutory functions and procedures, ensuring it is scientific, fair, strict, and efficient. They shall not use their functions to seek benefits, nor disclose state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy learned during the performance of their duties.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

**Article 48** If a unit or individual commits any of the following acts, the archives administrative department at or above the county level or the relevant state organ shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law:

(1) Losing archives owned by the state;
(2) Providing, transcribing, copying, or publishing archives owned by the state without authorization;
(3) Selling or illegally transferring archives owned by the state;
(4) Tampering with, damaging, forging archives, or destroying archives without authorization;
(5) Selling or giving archives to foreigners or foreign organizations;
(6) Failing to archive or transfer archives on time as required, and refusing to correct after being ordered to do so;
(7) Failing to open or provide access to archives to the public as required;
(8) Knowing about archives security risks but failing to take remedial measures, resulting in archives damage or loss, or failing to rectify within the time limit after being ordered to do so due to archives security risks;
(9) Failing to take rescue measures after an archives security accident, or concealing, failing to report, or refusing to investigate;
(10) Archives staff neglecting their duties, resulting in archives damage or loss.

**Article 49** If a person utilizing archives in an archives commits any of the illegal acts specified in items (1), (2), or (4) of Article 48, the archives administrative department at or above the county level shall issue a warning and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the unit, and a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan on the individual.

If an archives service enterprise commits any of the illegal acts specified in items (1), (2), or (4) of Article 48 during the service process, the archives administrative department at or above the county level shall issue a warning and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

If a unit or individual commits any of the illegal acts specified in items (3) or (5) of Article 48, the archives administrative department at or above the county level shall issue a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the unit, and a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan on the individual; and may, in accordance with the provisions of Article 22 of this Law, requisition the archives that were sold or given away.Article 50: In violation of this Law, anyone who transports, mails, carries, or transmits via the internet the prohibited archives or their copies out of the country shall have them confiscated or blocked by customs or relevant authorities. The unit shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan, and the individual shall be fined not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan. The confiscated or blocked archives or their copies shall be handed over to the archives administrative department.

Article 51: In violation of this Law, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if property losses or other damages are caused, civil liability shall be borne according to law.

## Chapter 8: Supplementary Provisions

Article 52: The archives work of the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be governed by management measures formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 53: This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2021.
