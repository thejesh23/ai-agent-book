## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the protection of cultural relics, inherit the outstanding historical and cultural heritage of the Chinese nation, promote scientific research, conduct education in patriotism and revolutionary tradition, and build socialist spiritual and material civilization.

Article 2 Within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the following cultural relics shall be protected by the State:

(1) Sites of ancient culture, ancient tombs, ancient buildings, cave temples, stone carvings, and murals of historical, artistic, or scientific value;

(2) Important modern and contemporary historical sites, physical objects, and representative buildings related to major historical events, revolutionary movements, or famous figures, and which have significant commemorative, educational, or documentary value;

(3) Precious works of art and arts and crafts from various historical periods;

(4) Important documentary materials from various historical periods, as well as manuscripts, books, and materials of historical, artistic, or scientific value;

(5) Representative physical objects reflecting the social systems, social production, and social life of various ethnic groups in different historical periods.

The criteria and procedures for the identification of cultural relics shall be formulated by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Fossils of ancient vertebrates and ancient humans of scientific value shall be protected by the State in the same manner as cultural relics.

Article 3 Immovable cultural relics, such as sites of ancient culture, ancient tombs, ancient buildings, cave temples, stone carvings, murals, important modern and contemporary historical sites, and representative buildings, may be designated as major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level, historical and cultural sites protected at the provincial level, or historical and cultural sites protected at the city or county level, based on their historical, artistic, and scientific value.

Movable cultural relics, such as important physical objects, works of art, documents, manuscripts, books, and representative physical objects from various historical periods, are classified into precious cultural relics and ordinary cultural relics; precious cultural relics are further classified into Grade One, Grade Two, and Grade Three cultural relics.

Article 4 The work concerning cultural relics shall follow the principles of giving priority to protection, putting rescue first, making rational use, and strengthening management.

Article 5 All cultural relics remaining underground, in inland waters, and in territorial seas within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be owned by the State.

Sites of ancient culture, ancient tombs, and cave temples shall be owned by the State. Immovable cultural relics designated for protection by the State, such as memorial buildings, ancient buildings, stone carvings, murals, and modern and contemporary representative buildings, shall be owned by the State, unless otherwise provided by the State.

The ownership of State-owned immovable cultural relics shall not change due to changes in the ownership or right to use of the land on which they are located.

The following movable cultural relics shall be owned by the State:

(1) Cultural relics unearthed within the territory of China, unless otherwise provided by the State;

(2) Cultural relics collected or kept by State-owned cultural relic collection entities, as well as other State organs, armed forces, State-owned enterprises, and public institutions;

(3) Cultural relics collected or purchased by the State;

(4) Cultural relics donated to the State by citizens, legal persons, or other organizations;

(5) Other cultural relics that are owned by the State as provided by law.

The ownership of State-owned movable cultural relics shall not change due to the termination or change of the entity keeping or collecting them.

The ownership of State-owned cultural relics is protected by law and shall not be violated.

Article 6 The ownership of memorial buildings, ancient buildings, and ancestral cultural relics owned by collectives or individuals, as well as other cultural relics legally obtained, shall be protected by law. Owners of cultural relics must comply with the provisions of laws and regulations of the State concerning the protection of cultural relics.

Article 7 All organs, organizations, and individuals have the obligation to protect cultural relics in accordance with the law.

Article 8 The administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall be responsible for the work of protecting cultural relics nationwide.

Local people's governments at various levels shall be responsible for the protection of cultural relics within their respective administrative regions. The departments responsible for the protection of cultural relics under local people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage the protection of cultural relics within their respective administrative regions.

The relevant administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the protection of cultural relics within their respective functions and duties.

Article 9 People's governments at various levels shall attach importance to the protection of cultural relics, correctly handle the relationship between economic construction, social development, and the protection of cultural relics, and ensure the safety of cultural relics.

Capital construction and tourism development must comply with the principles of cultural relics protection, and their activities shall not cause damage to cultural relics.

Public security organs, administrative departments for industry and commerce, customs, urban and rural planning departments, and other relevant State organs shall earnestly fulfill their duties in protecting cultural relics in accordance with the law and maintain the order of cultural relics management.

Article 10 The State shall develop the cause of cultural relics protection. People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the cause of cultural relics protection into their national economic and social development plans, and the necessary funds shall be included in their fiscal budgets.

The financial allocations of the State for the protection of cultural relics shall increase with the growth of fiscal revenue.

The operating income of State-owned museums, memorial halls, and cultural relic protection units shall be used exclusively for the protection of cultural relics, and no unit or individual may misappropriate or divert such funds.

The State encourages the establishment of social funds for the protection of cultural relics through donations and other means, which shall be used exclusively for the protection of cultural relics, and no unit or individual may misappropriate or divert such funds.

Article 11 Cultural relics are non-renewable cultural resources. The State shall strengthen publicity and education on the protection of cultural relics, enhance the awareness of the entire people regarding the protection of cultural relics, encourage scientific research on the protection of cultural relics, and improve the scientific and technological level of cultural relics protection.

Article 12 Units or individuals with the following achievements shall be given spiritual encouragement or material rewards by the State:

(1) Those who earnestly implement laws and regulations on the protection of cultural relics and achieve outstanding results in protecting cultural relics;

(2) Those who resolutely fight against illegal and criminal acts to protect cultural relics;

(3) Those who donate important cultural relics in their personal collection to the State or make donations to the cause of cultural relics protection;

(4) Those who promptly report or hand over discovered cultural relics, thereby ensuring their protection;

(5) Those who make significant contributions in archaeological excavation work;

(6) Those who have important inventions or creations in the science and technology of cultural relics protection or other important contributions;

(7) Those who perform meritorious service in rescuing cultural relics when they are in danger of destruction;

(8) Those who have been engaged in cultural relics work for a long time and have achieved remarkable results.

## Chapter II Immovable Cultural Relics

Article 13 The administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall select from among the historical and cultural sites protected at the provincial, city, or county level those with significant historical, artistic, or scientific value and designate them as major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level, or directly designate them as such, and submit them to the State Council for verification and announcement.

Historical and cultural sites protected at the provincial level shall be verified and announced by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and reported to the State Council for the record.

Historical and cultural sites protected at the city and county levels shall be verified and announced by the people's governments of cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, and counties, respectively, and reported to the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government for the record.

Immovable cultural relics that have not yet been verified and announced as historical and cultural sites shall be registered and announced by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at the county level.

Article 14 Cities that are particularly rich in cultural relics and have significant historical value or revolutionary commemorative significance shall be verified and announced by the State Council as famous historical and cultural cities.

Towns, streets, and villages that are particularly rich in cultural relics and have significant historical value or revolutionary commemorative significance shall be verified and announced by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government as famous historical and cultural blocks, towns, and villages, and reported to the State Council for the record.

Local people's governments at or above the county level where famous historical and cultural cities, blocks, towns, and villages are located shall organize the formulation of special protection plans for these areas and incorporate them into the overall urban planning.

Measures for the protection of famous historical and cultural cities, blocks, towns, and villages shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 15 For historical and cultural sites protected at various levels, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government and the people's governments at the city and county levels shall delineate the necessary scope of protection, set up explanatory signs, establish record files, and, depending on the circumstances, set up specialized agencies or assign specialized personnel to be responsible for management. The scope of protection and record files of major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level shall be reported by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record.

The administrative departments for cultural relics of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the protection needs of different cultural relics, formulate specific protection measures for historical and cultural sites and immovable cultural relics not yet verified as such, and announce them for implementation.

Article 16 When formulating urban and rural construction plans, people's governments at various levels shall, based on the needs of cultural relics protection, have the urban and rural planning department, in conjunction with the administrative department for cultural relics, determine in advance the protection measures for historical and cultural sites at various levels within their administrative regions, and incorporate them into the plans.

Article 17 No other construction projects or operations such as blasting, drilling, or excavation shall be carried out within the scope of protection of a historical and cultural site. However, if due to special circumstances it is necessary to carry out other construction projects or operations such as blasting, drilling, or excavation within the scope of protection of a historical and cultural site, the safety of the historical and cultural site must be ensured, and approval must be obtained from the people's government that verified and announced the site; before granting approval, the consent of the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at the next higher level must be obtained. For carrying out other construction projects or operations such as blasting, drilling, or excavation within the scope of protection of a major historical and cultural site protected at the national level, approval must be obtained from the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; before granting approval, the consent of the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council must be obtained.

Article 18 Based on the actual needs of protecting cultural relics, with the approval of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, a certain construction control zone may be delineated around a historical and cultural site and announced.

Construction projects within the construction control zone of a historical and cultural site shall not damage the historical appearance of the site; the engineering design plan shall, based on the level of the site, be submitted to the urban and rural planning department for approval after obtaining the consent of the corresponding administrative department for cultural relics.

Article 19 Within the scope of protection and the construction control zone of a historical and cultural site, no facilities that pollute the site or its environment shall be constructed, and no activities that may affect the safety of the site or its environment shall be carried out. Existing facilities that pollute the site or its environment shall be rectified within a specified time limit.

Article 20 The site selection for construction projects shall, as far as possible, avoid immovable cultural relics; if avoidance is not possible due to special circumstances, protection in situ shall be implemented as far as possible for historical and cultural sites.

If protection in situ is implemented, the construction unit shall determine the protection measures in advance and submit them to the corresponding administrative department for cultural relics for approval based on the level of the site; without approval, construction shall not commence.

If protection in situ is not feasible and relocation or demolition is necessary, approval must be obtained from the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; for the relocation or demolition of a historical and cultural site protected at the provincial level, the consent of the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council must be obtained before approval. Major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level shall not be demolished; if relocation is necessary, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall submit the matter to the State Council for approval.

Murals, sculptures, architectural components, etc., of collection value from State-owned immovable cultural relics demolished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be collected by cultural relic collection entities designated by the administrative department for cultural relics.

The expenses required for protection in situ, relocation, or demolition as provided in this Article shall be included in the construction project budget by the construction unit.

Article 21 State-owned immovable cultural relics shall be repaired and maintained by the user; non-State-owned immovable cultural relics shall be repaired and maintained by the owner. If a non-State-owned immovable cultural relic is in danger of damage and the owner lacks the capacity to repair it, the local people's government shall provide assistance; if the owner has the capacity to repair but refuses to fulfill the repair obligation in accordance with the law, the people's government at or above the county level may carry out rescue repairs, and the expenses shall be borne by the owner.

Repairs to a historical and cultural site shall be submitted to the corresponding administrative department for cultural relics for approval based on the level of the site; repairs to an immovable cultural relic not yet verified as a historical and cultural site shall be submitted to the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at the county level that registered it for approval.

The repair, relocation, or reconstruction of a historical and cultural site shall be undertaken by a unit that has obtained a qualification certificate for cultural relics protection projects.

The repair, maintenance, or relocation of immovable cultural relics must comply with the principle of not altering the original state of the cultural relics.

Article 22 If an immovable cultural relic has been completely destroyed, site protection shall be implemented, and reconstruction on the original site shall not be permitted. However, if reconstruction on the original site is necessary due to special circumstances, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall submit the matter to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval; if reconstruction on the original site is necessary for a major historical and cultural site protected at the national level, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall submit the matter to the State Council for approval.

Article 23 For State-owned memorial buildings or ancient buildings designated as historical and cultural sites, besides being used for establishing museums, storage facilities, or being opened as tourist sites, if they are to be used for other purposes, for sites protected at the city or county level, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government that verified and announced the site shall obtain the consent of the administrative department for cultural relics at the next higher level and then submit the matter to the people's government that verified and announced the site for approval; for sites protected at the provincial level, the administrative department for cultural relics of the provincial people's government that verified and announced the site shall review and consent, and then submit the matter to the provincial people's government for approval; for major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level to be used for other purposes, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall submit the matter to the State Council for approval. If a State-owned immovable cultural relic not yet verified as a historical and cultural site is to be used for other purposes, it shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at the county level.

Article 24 State-owned immovable cultural relics shall not be transferred or mortgaged. State-owned historical and cultural sites used for establishing museums, storage facilities, or being opened as tourist sites shall not be operated as enterprise assets.

Article 25 Non-State-owned immovable cultural relics shall not be transferred or mortgaged to foreigners.

The transfer, mortgage, or change of use of non-State-owned immovable cultural relics shall be reported to the corresponding administrative department for cultural relics for the record based on their level.

Article 26 Users of immovable cultural relics must comply with the principle of not altering the original state of the cultural relics, be responsible for ensuring the safety of the buildings and their ancillary cultural relics, and shall not damage, alter, add to, or demolish the immovable cultural relics.

For buildings or structures that endanger the safety of a historical and cultural site or damage its historical appearance, the local people's government shall promptly investigate and handle the matter, and if necessary, demolish the buildings or structures.

## Chapter III Archaeological Excavations

Article 27 All archaeological excavations must go through the procedures for submitting reports for approval; units engaged in archaeological excavations shall be approved by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council.

No unit or individual may privately excavate cultural relics buried underground.

Article 28 Units engaged in archaeological excavations, for the purpose of scientific research, shall submit an excavation plan to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for approval; excavation plans for major historical and cultural sites protected at the national level shall be submitted to the State Council for approval after review by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council. Before granting approval or conducting the review, the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall seek opinions from social science research institutions, other scientific research institutions, and relevant experts.

Article 29 For large-scale capital construction projects, the construction unit shall, in advance, request the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to organize units engaged in archaeological excavations to conduct archaeological surveys and explorations in areas within the project scope where cultural relics may be buried.

If cultural relics are discovered during archaeological surveys and explorations, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, based on the requirements of cultural relics protection, jointly determine protection measures with the construction unit; if important discoveries are made, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall promptly report to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for handling.

Article 30 Archaeological excavations required to be carried out in conjunction with construction projects shall be proposed by the administrative department for cultural relics of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government based on exploration work, and submitted to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for approval. Before granting approval, the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall seek opinions from social science research institutions, other scientific research institutions, and relevant experts.

If rescue excavations of ancient cultural sites or ancient tombs are urgently needed due to tight construction schedules or the danger of natural destruction, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall organize the excavation and simultaneously go through the approval procedures retroactively.

Article 31 The expenses required for archaeological surveys, explorations, and excavations necessitated by capital construction or production construction shall be included in the construction project budget by the construction unit.

Article 32 During construction projects or agricultural production, if any unit or individual discovers cultural relics, they shall protect the site and immediately report to the local administrative department for cultural relics. Upon receiving the report, the administrative department for cultural relics shall, unless there are special circumstances, arrive at the site within twenty-four hours and issue handling opinions within seven days. The administrative department for cultural relics may request the local people's government to notify the public security organ to assist in protecting the site; if important cultural relics are discovered, it shall immediately report to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council, which shall issue handling opinions within fifteen days of receiving the report.

Cultural relics discovered in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be owned by the State, and no unit or individual may plunder, privately distribute, or conceal them.

Article 33 No foreigner or foreign organization may conduct archaeological surveys, explorations, or excavations within the territory of the People's Republic of China without special permission from the State Council, reported by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council.

Article 34 The results of archaeological surveys, explorations, and excavations shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council and the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.Cultural relics unearthed through archaeological excavations shall be registered, properly preserved, and transferred in accordance with relevant state regulations to state-owned museums, libraries, or other state-owned cultural relic collection units designated by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council. With the approval of the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, units engaged in archaeological excavations may retain a small number of unearthed relics as scientific research specimens.

No unit or individual may appropriate cultural relics unearthed through archaeological excavations.

Article 35 To ensure the safety of cultural relics, facilitate scientific research, and maximize the utility of cultural relics, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, with the approval of the people's government at the same level, transfer unearthed cultural relics within its administrative region; the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council may, with the approval of the State Council, transfer important unearthed cultural relics nationwide.

## Chapter IV: Collection of Cultural Relics

Article 36 Museums, libraries, and other cultural relic collection units must classify collected cultural relics by grade, establish collection files, set up a strict management system, and report to the competent administrative department for cultural relics for the record.

The administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level shall establish files for collected cultural relics within its administrative region; the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall establish files for Grade One cultural relics and files for collected cultural relics in state-owned cultural relic collection units under its supervision.

Article 37 Cultural relic collection units may acquire cultural relics through the following methods:

(1) Purchase;
(2) Acceptance of donations;
(3) Lawful exchange;
(4) Other methods prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

State-owned cultural relic collection units may also acquire cultural relics through designation for preservation or allocation by the administrative department for cultural relics.

Article 38 Cultural relic collection units shall, based on the protection needs of collected cultural relics, establish and improve management systems in accordance with relevant state regulations and report to the competent administrative department for cultural relics for the record. Without approval, no unit or individual may transfer collected cultural relics.

The legal representative of a cultural relic collection unit is responsible for the safety of the collected cultural relics. When the legal representative of a state-owned cultural relic collection unit leaves office, the transfer procedures for collected cultural relics shall be handled in accordance with the collection files.

Article 39 The administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council may allocate state-owned collected cultural relics nationwide. The administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may allocate collected cultural relics from state-owned cultural relic collection units under its supervision within its administrative region; the allocation of Grade One state-owned collected cultural relics shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record.

State-owned cultural relic collection units may apply for the allocation of state-owned collected cultural relics.

Article 40 Cultural relic collection units shall fully utilize collected cultural relics by organizing exhibitions, scientific research, and other activities to strengthen the publicity and education of the excellent historical culture and revolutionary traditions of the Chinese nation.

If state-owned cultural relic collection units need to borrow collected cultural relics for exhibitions, scientific research, etc., they shall report to the competent administrative department for cultural relics for the record; if borrowing Grade One collected cultural relics, they shall also report to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record.

If non-state-owned cultural relic collection units or other units need to borrow state-owned collected cultural relics for exhibitions, they shall obtain approval from the competent administrative department for cultural relics; if borrowing Grade One state-owned collected cultural relics, approval from the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council is required.

The maximum period for borrowing cultural relics between cultural relic collection units shall not exceed three years.

Article 41 State-owned cultural relic collection units that have established files for collected cultural relics may, with the approval of the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and after reporting to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record, exchange their collected cultural relics among state-owned cultural relic collection units.

Article 42 State-owned cultural relic collection units that have not established files for collected cultural relics shall not dispose of their collected cultural relics in accordance with Articles 40 and 41 of this Law.

Article 43 When state-owned collected cultural relics are allocated, exchanged, or borrowed in accordance with the law, the cultural relic collection unit that receives the cultural relics may provide reasonable compensation to the unit that supplied the cultural relics. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council.

Compensation funds obtained by state-owned cultural relic collection units from the allocation, exchange, or lending of cultural relics must be used to improve the conditions for preserving cultural relics and to collect new cultural relics; they shall not be misappropriated for other purposes. No unit or individual may appropriate such funds.

Cultural relics that are allocated, exchanged, or borrowed must be strictly preserved and shall not be lost or damaged.

Article 44 State-owned cultural relic collection units are prohibited from donating, renting, or selling collected cultural relics to other units or individuals.

Article 45 Measures for the disposal of cultural relics no longer collected by state-owned cultural relic collection units shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Article 46 When restoring collected cultural relics, the original state of the collected cultural relics shall not be altered; when reproducing, photographing, or making rubbings of collected cultural relics, no damage shall be caused to the collected cultural relics. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

The restoration, reproduction, photography, and rubbing of individual artifacts from immovable cultural relics shall be governed by the preceding paragraph.

Article 47 Museums, libraries, and other cultural relic collection units shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, equip themselves with facilities for fire prevention, theft prevention, and protection against natural damage to ensure the safety of collected cultural relics.

Article 48 If Grade One collected cultural relics are damaged, it shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for verification and handling. If other collected cultural relics are damaged, it shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for verification and handling; the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall report the verification and handling results to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record.

If collected cultural relics are stolen, robbed, or lost, the cultural relic collection unit shall immediately report to the public security organ and simultaneously report to the competent administrative department for cultural relics.

Article 49 Staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics and state-owned cultural relic collection units shall not borrow state-owned cultural relics or illegally appropriate state-owned cultural relics.

## Chapter V: Privately Collected Cultural Relics

Article 50 Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations other than cultural relic collection units may collect cultural relics obtained through the following methods:

(1) Lawful inheritance or acceptance of gifts;
(2) Purchase from cultural relic shops;
(3) Purchase from auction enterprises engaged in cultural relic auctions;
(4) Exchange or lawful transfer of cultural relics lawfully owned by individual citizens;
(5) Other lawful methods prescribed by the state.

Cultural relics collected by citizens, legal persons, and other organizations other than cultural relic collection units as mentioned in the preceding paragraph may be circulated in accordance with the law.

Article 51 Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations shall not buy or sell the following cultural relics:

(1) State-owned cultural relics, except those permitted by the state;
(2) Non-state-owned precious collected cultural relics;
(3) Murals, sculptures, architectural components, etc., from state-owned immovable cultural relics, except those removed in accordance with the law that are not required to be collected by cultural relic collection units as stipulated in Article 20, paragraph 4 of this Law;
(4) Cultural relics whose sources do not comply with Article 50 of this Law.

Article 52 The state encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations other than cultural relic collection units to donate their collected cultural relics to state-owned cultural relic collection units or lend them to cultural relic collection units for exhibition and research.

State-owned cultural relic collection units shall respect and follow the wishes of donors, properly collecting, preserving, and displaying donated cultural relics.

Cultural relics prohibited by the state from being taken out of the country shall not be transferred, rented, or pledged to foreigners.

Article 53 Cultural relic shops shall be established with the approval of the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and shall be managed in accordance with the law.

Cultural relic shops shall not engage in cultural relic auction activities or establish auction enterprises for cultural relic auctions.

Article 54 Auction enterprises lawfully established that engage in cultural relic auctions shall obtain a cultural relic auction permit issued by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Auction enterprises engaged in cultural relic auctions shall not engage in cultural relic purchase and sale activities or establish cultural relic shops.

Article 55 Staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics shall not establish or participate in the establishment of cultural relic shops or auction enterprises for cultural relic auctions.

Cultural relic collection units shall not establish or participate in the establishment of cultural relic shops or auction enterprises for cultural relic auctions.

The establishment of Sino-foreign joint ventures, Sino-foreign cooperative enterprises, or wholly foreign-owned enterprises as cultural relic shops or auction enterprises for cultural relic auctions is prohibited.

Except for approved cultural relic shops and auction enterprises for cultural relic auctions, no other unit or individual may engage in the commercial operation of cultural relics.

Article 56 Cultural relic shops shall not sell, and auction enterprises shall not auction, the cultural relics specified in Article 51 of this Law.

Cultural relics to be auctioned by auction enterprises shall be examined by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government before the auction and reported to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for the record.

Article 57 The administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall establish a cultural relic purchase, sale, and auction information and credit management system. Cultural relic shops purchasing or selling cultural relics, and auction enterprises auctioning cultural relics, shall make records in accordance with relevant state regulations and report them to the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for the record within thirty days after the sale or auction of the cultural relics.

When auctioning cultural relics, if the consignor or buyer requests confidentiality of their identity, the administrative department for cultural relics shall keep it confidential; however, unless otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 58 When examining cultural relics to be auctioned, the administrative department for cultural relics may designate state-owned cultural relic collection units to have priority in purchasing precious cultural relics among them. The purchase price shall be negotiated between the representative of the cultural relic collection unit and the consignor of the cultural relic.

Article 59 Banks, smelters, paper mills, and waste material recycling units shall, together with the local administrative department for cultural relics, be responsible for selecting cultural relics mixed in gold and silver articles and waste materials. Selected cultural relics, except for historical currencies necessary for the research of the bank's research institute, which may be retained by the People's Bank, shall be handed over to the local administrative department for cultural relics. Reasonable compensation shall be given for the handover of selected cultural relics.

## Chapter VI: Entry and Exit of Cultural Relics

Article 60 State-owned cultural relics, precious cultural relics among non-state-owned cultural relics, and other cultural relics whose export is prohibited by state regulations shall not be taken out of the country, except for export for exhibition in accordance with the provisions of this Law or for special needs approved by the State Council.

Article 61 The export of cultural relics shall be examined by the cultural relic entry-exit examination institution designated by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council. Cultural relics approved for export shall be issued an export permit by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council and shall be exported from the port designated by the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council.

Any unit or individual transporting, mailing, or carrying cultural relics out of the country shall declare to the customs; the customs shall release them upon presentation of the cultural relic export permit.

Article 62 The export of cultural relics for exhibition shall be reported to the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council for approval; if the number of Grade One cultural relics exceeds the quantity prescribed by the State Council, it shall be reported to the State Council for approval.

Unique and fragile items among Grade One cultural relics are prohibited from being exported for exhibition.

Cultural relics exported for exhibition shall be examined and registered by the cultural relic entry-exit examination institution. The customs shall release them upon presentation of the approval document from the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council or the State Council. When cultural relics exported for exhibition are re-imported, they shall be examined and verified by the original cultural relic entry-exit examination institution.

Article 63 Temporary import of cultural relics shall be declared to the customs and reported to the cultural relic entry-exit examination institution for examination and registration.

Temporarily imported cultural relics to be re-exported must be examined and verified by the original cultural relic entry-exit examination institution that examined and registered them; if found correct, the administrative department for cultural relics under the State Council shall issue a cultural relic export permit, and the customs shall release them upon presentation of the permit.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

Article 64 Any violation of the provisions of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law:

(1) Illegally excavating ancient cultural sites or ancient tombs;
(2) Intentionally or negligently damaging precious cultural relics protected by the state;
(3) Unauthorized sale or private transfer of state-owned collected cultural relics to non-state-owned units or individuals;
(4) Private sale or transfer of precious cultural relics prohibited from being taken out of the country to foreigners;
(5) Profiteering from cultural relics prohibited from being traded by the state for profit;
(6) Smuggling cultural relics;
(7) Stealing, looting, privately distributing, or illegally appropriating state-owned cultural relics;
(8) Other acts hindering cultural relic management that should be investigated for criminal liability.

Article 65 Any violation of the provisions of this Law that causes the loss or damage of cultural relics shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Any violation of the provisions of this Law that constitutes a violation of public security administration shall be given a public security administration penalty by the public security organ in accordance with the law.

Any violation of the provisions of this Law that constitutes smuggling, if not constituting a crime, shall be punished by the customs in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 66 Any of the following acts, if not constituting a crime, shall be ordered to make corrections by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke the qualification certificate:

(1) Unauthorized construction, blasting, drilling, excavation, or other operations within the protection zone of a cultural relic protection unit;
(2) Construction within the construction control zone of a cultural relic protection unit, where the engineering design plan has not been approved by the administrative department for cultural relics and submitted to the urban and rural construction planning department for approval, causing damage to the historical appearance of the cultural relic protection unit;
(3) Unauthorized relocation or demolition of immovable cultural relics;
(4) Unauthorized repair of immovable cultural relics, significantly altering their original state;
(5) Unauthorized reconstruction of completely destroyed immovable cultural relics on the original site, causing damage to the cultural relics;
(6) A construction unit engaging in the repair, relocation, or reconstruction of cultural relics without obtaining a cultural relic protection engineering qualification certificate.

If the carving, defacing, or damaging of cultural relics is not serious, or if the sign of a cultural relic protection unit established in accordance with Article 15, paragraph 1 of this Law is damaged, the public security organ or the unit where the cultural relic is located shall give a warning and may also impose a fine.

Article 67 If facilities that pollute a cultural relic protection unit or its environment are constructed within the protection zone or construction control zone of a cultural relic protection unit, or if existing facilities that pollute a cultural relic protection unit or its environment are not treated within the prescribed period, the environmental protection administrative department shall impose penalties in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 68 Any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the illegal gains are 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Transferring or mortgaging state-owned immovable cultural relics, or operating state-owned immovable cultural relics as enterprise assets;
(2) Transferring or mortgaging non-state-owned immovable cultural relics to foreigners;
(3) Unauthorized change of the use of a state-owned cultural relic protection unit.

Article 69 If the layout, environment, historical appearance, etc., of a famous historical and cultural city are severely damaged, the State Council shall revoke its title as a famous historical and cultural city; if the layout, environment, historical appearance, etc., of a famous historical and cultural town, street, or village are severely damaged, the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke its title as a famous historical and cultural district, town, or village; administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

Article 70 Any of the following acts, if not constituting a crime, shall be ordered to make corrections by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated:

(1) A cultural relic collection unit failing to equip itself with facilities for fire prevention, theft prevention, and protection against natural damage in accordance with relevant state regulations;
(2) The legal representative of a state-owned cultural relic collection unit failing to transfer collected cultural relics in accordance with the collection files when leaving office, or the transferred collected cultural relics not matching the collection files;
(3) Donating, renting, or selling state-owned collected cultural relics to other units or individuals;
(4) Disposing of state-owned collected cultural relics in violation of Articles 40, 41, and 45 of this Law;
(5) Misappropriating or appropriating compensation funds obtained from the lawful allocation, exchange, or lending of cultural relics in violation of Article 43 of this Law.

Article 71 If cultural relics prohibited from being traded by the state are bought or sold, or cultural relics prohibited from being taken out of the country are transferred, rented, or pledged to foreigners, if not constituting a crime, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections, confiscate illegal gains, and if the illegal business turnover is 10,000 yuan or more, impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal business turnover; if the illegal business turnover is less than 10,000 yuan, impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

If a cultural relic shop or auction enterprise commits the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph, the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal gains and the illegally traded cultural relics; if the illegal business turnover is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if the illegal business turnover is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke the permit.

Article 72 If a cultural relic shop or an auction enterprise for cultural relic auctions is established without permission, or if commercial activities involving cultural relics are engaged in without authorization, if not constituting a crime, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall stop it in accordance with the law, confiscate illegal gains and illegally traded cultural relics; if the illegal business turnover is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if the illegal business turnover is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 73 Under any of the following circumstances, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall confiscate illegal gains and illegally traded cultural relics; if the illegal business turnover is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if the illegal business turnover is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke the permit:

(1) A cultural relic shop engaging in cultural relic auction activities;
(2) An auction enterprise engaged in cultural relic auctions engaging in cultural relic purchase and sale activities;
(3) Cultural relics auctioned by an auction enterprise without examination;
(4) A cultural relic collection unit engaging in commercial activities involving cultural relics.

Article 74 Any of the following acts, if not constituting a crime, shall be subject to the recovery of cultural relics by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level in conjunction with the public security organ; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Concealing the discovery of cultural relics without reporting or refusing to hand them over;
(2) Failing to hand over selected cultural relics in accordance with regulations.

Article 75 Any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the administrative department for cultural relics of the people's government at or above the county level:

(1) Changing the use of a state-owned immovable cultural relic not designated as a cultural relic protection unit without reporting in accordance with the provisions of this Law;
(2) Transferring or mortgaging a non-state-owned immovable cultural relic or changing its use without filing in accordance with the provisions of this Law;
(3) The user of a state-owned immovable cultural relic refusing to perform repair obligations in accordance with the law;(4) Conducting archaeological excavations without approval or failing to truthfully report the results of such excavations;

(5) Failing to establish archives and management systems for collected cultural relics in accordance with relevant state regulations, or failing to file such archives and management systems for record;

(6) Violating Article 38 of this Law by transferring collected cultural relics without approval;

(7) Failing to report damage to collected cultural relics to the administrative department for cultural relics for verification and handling, or failing to promptly report to the public security organs or the administrative department for cultural relics when collected cultural relics are stolen, robbed, or lost;

(8) Failing to make records of cultural relics sold by cultural relic stores or auctioned by auction enterprises in accordance with relevant state regulations, or failing to submit such records to the administrative department for cultural relics for filing.

Article 76: Where staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics, cultural relic collection entities, cultural relic stores, or auction enterprises engaged in the auction of cultural relics commit any of the following acts, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be dismissed from public employment or have their professional qualifications revoked according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics, in violation of the provisions of this Law, abuse their approval authority, fail to perform their duties, or fail to investigate and deal with illegal acts discovered, resulting in serious consequences;

(2) Staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics or state-owned cultural relic collection entities borrow or illegally occupy state-owned cultural relics;

(3) Staff members of administrative departments for cultural relics establish or participate in the establishment of cultural relic stores or auction enterprises engaged in the auction of cultural relics;

(4) Causing damage or loss of cultural relic protection units or valuable cultural relics due to irresponsibility;

(5) Embezzling or misappropriating funds for cultural relic protection.

Persons who have been dismissed from public employment or have had their professional qualifications revoked as mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall not serve as cultural relic management personnel or engage in cultural relic business activities within ten years from the date of such dismissal or revocation.

Article 77: Where any of the acts listed in Articles 66, 68, 70, 71, 74, and 75 of this Law are committed, if the responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel are state functionaries, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to law.

Article 78: Where public security organs, administrative departments for industry and commerce, customs, urban and rural construction planning departments, or other state organs, in violation of the provisions of this Law, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, causing damage or loss of valuable cultural relics under state protection, the responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 79: Cultural relics lawfully confiscated by the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, public security organs, customs, and administrative departments for industry and commerce shall be registered and properly kept. After the case is concluded, they shall be transferred without compensation to the administrative department for cultural relics, which shall designate a state-owned cultural relic collection entity to collect them.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 80: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
