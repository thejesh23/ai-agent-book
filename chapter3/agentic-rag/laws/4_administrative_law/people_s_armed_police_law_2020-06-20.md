# People's Armed Police Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revised at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 20, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate and ensure the People's Armed Police Force in performing its duties, build a strong and modernized People's Armed Police Force, safeguard national security and social stability, and protect the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 2 The People's Armed Police Force is an important component of the armed forces of the People's Republic of China and is under the centralized and unified leadership of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the Central Military Commission.

Article 3 The People's Armed Police Force adheres to the absolute leadership of the Communist Party of China, implements Xi Jinping's thinking on strengthening the military, implements the military strategic guidelines for the new era, and in accordance with the strategic requirements of multi-capability integration, maintaining stability and safeguarding rights, strengthens military training and combat readiness, adheres to strict governance according to law, accelerates construction and development, and effectively performs its duties.

Article 4 The People's Armed Police Force undertakes tasks including guard duty, handling of sudden social security incidents, preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, maritime rights protection and law enforcement, emergency rescue and disaster relief, defense operations, and other tasks assigned by the Central Military Commission.

Article 5 The People's Armed Police Force shall abide by the Constitution and laws, be devoted to its duties, and perform its duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws.

Acts performed by the People's Armed Police Force in accordance with the law in the course of performing duties shall be protected by law.

Article 6 People's Armed Police personnel who make outstanding contributions in the execution of tasks shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant laws and the provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Individuals and organizations that make outstanding contributions in assisting the People's Armed Police Force in performing tasks shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 7 The People's Armed Police Force implements a rank system, the specific content of which shall be separately provided by law.

Article 8 People's Armed Police personnel shall enjoy the rights and interests of active-duty servicemen as stipulated by laws and regulations.

## Chapter II Organization and Command

Article 9 The People's Armed Police Force consists of internal security forces, mobile forces, coast guard forces, academies, research institutions, etc.

Internal security forces are organized according to administrative divisions, mobile forces are organized according to tasks, and coast guard forces are organized in coastal areas according to administrative divisions and task areas. The specific organization shall be determined by the Central Military Commission.

Article 10 When the People's Armed Police Force performs tasks in peacetime, it shall be organized and commanded by the Central Military Commission or by the People's Armed Police Force authorized by the Central Military Commission.

When the People's Armed Police Force jointly participates with the People's Liberation Army in non-war military operations such as emergency rescue and disaster relief, stability maintenance and emergency response, and joint training exercises in peacetime, it shall be commanded by the theater command authorized by the Central Military Commission.

When the People's Armed Police Force performs tasks in wartime, it shall be organized and commanded by the Central Military Commission or by the theater command authorized by the Central Military Commission.

Specific measures for organization and command shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 11 Central state organs and local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish mechanisms for task requirements and work coordination with the People's Armed Police Force.

Where central state organs and local people's governments at or above the county level require the assistance of the People's Armed Police Force for security protection of major events, handling of sudden social security incidents, preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, emergency rescue and disaster relief, etc., they shall submit requirements in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The target unit of a guard duty may submit requirements to the People's Armed Police Force responsible for the guard duty.

Article 12 The deployment of the People's Armed Police Force to perform tasks shall adhere to the principles of using troops according to law and strict approval, and shall be organized and implemented in accordance with the command relationship, authority limits, and operational mechanisms. The approval authority and procedures shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

In the event of emergencies such as major disasters, dangerous situations, or violent terrorist incidents that seriously threaten public safety or the personal and property safety of citizens, the People's Armed Police Force shall take action in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission and report simultaneously.

Article 13 The People's Armed Police Force, as required for performing tasks, may participate in command organizations established by central state organs and local people's governments at or above the county level, and shall implement organization and command under the leadership of such command organizations in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Article 14 Central state organs and local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide professional guidance to the People's Armed Police Force in guard duty, handling of sudden social security incidents, preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, and emergency rescue and disaster relief.

When the People's Armed Police Force performs tasks such as armed guard, armed security, armed protection, armed cordon, escort, and convoy, the target unit of the guard duty may provide operational guidance to the People's Armed Police Force unit performing guard duty at its location.

## Chapter III Tasks and Powers

Article 15 The People's Armed Police Force mainly undertakes the following guard duties:

(1) Armed guard of guarded persons and important guarded targets;
(2) Security protection of major events;
(3) Armed security of core and critical parts of important public facilities, nuclear facilities, enterprises, warehouses, water sources, water conservancy projects, power facilities, communication hubs, and other targets;
(4) Armed protection of important bridges and tunnels;
(5) External armed cordon of prisons, detention centers, and other places;
(6) Armed patrols in key areas, special periods, and specific inland borders of municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities where provincial people's governments are located, and other important cities (towns);
(7) Assisting public security organs and state security organs in lawfully executing arrest and pursuit tasks, assisting prisons, detention centers, and other target units of guard duties in executing escort and pursuit tasks, and assisting the People's Bank of China, national defense and military industry units, and other target units of guard duties in executing convoy tasks.

The specific scope of the guard duties specified in the preceding paragraph shall be implemented in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 16 When the People's Armed Police Force participates in handling sudden incidents such as riots, rebellions, unrest, illegal gatherings, and mass incidents, it mainly undertakes the following tasks:

(1) Ensuring the safety of important targets;
(2) Blocking and controlling relevant places and roads;
(3) Carrying out actions such as isolation, dispersal, removal, and expulsion to stop illegal and criminal acts;
(4) Rescuing and aiding trapped persons;
(5) Armed patrols, assisting in mass work, and restoring social order.

Article 17 When the People's Armed Police Force participates in preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, it mainly undertakes the following tasks:

(1) Controlling the scene of terrorist incidents, rescue, medical aid, as well as armed patrols and guarding key targets;
(2) Assisting public security organs in arresting and pursuing terrorist activity personnel;
(3) Rescuing hostages and removing explosives;
(4) Participating in handling incidents involving hijacked aircraft and other means of transportation.

Article 18 When the People's Armed Police Force participates in emergency rescue and disaster relief for natural disasters, accident disasters, public health incidents, and other emergencies, it mainly undertakes the following tasks:

(1) Participating in searching for, rescuing, transferring, or evacuating trapped persons;
(2) Participating in the outer cordon of dangerous areas, dangerous places, and warning zones;
(3) Participating in eliminating and controlling disasters and dangerous situations, and preventing secondary and derivative disasters;
(4) Participating in specialized rescue operations such as nuclear, biological, and chemical rescue, medical aid, epidemic prevention and control, and emergency repair and construction of transportation facilities;
(5) Participating in rescuing, transporting, and transferring important materials.

Article 19 When performing tasks, the People's Armed Police Force may lawfully take the following measures:

(1) Inspecting persons, articles, and means of transportation entering or exiting a cordoned area or passing through a guard checkpoint in accordance with regulations; preventing those not permitted to enter or pass; and taking necessary measures to stop those who forcibly enter or pass;
(2) During armed patrols, with the consent of the on-site commander and upon presentation of the People's Armed Police certificate, interrogating persons suspected of illegal or criminal activities on the spot and inspecting their certificates, and inspecting suspicious articles and means of transportation;
(3) Assisting in implementing traffic control or on-site control;
(4) Taking necessary measures to stop, remove, or disperse persons who gather to disrupt public security order, endanger the personal and property safety of citizens, endanger public safety, or threaten the safety of guard targets;
(5) As required for performing tasks, inquiring about relevant information from relevant units and personnel, or conducting necessary reconnaissance at the scene or in places related to the performance of tasks.

Article 20 When performing tasks, if the People's Armed Police Force discovers persons in any of the following circumstances, with the consent of the on-site commander, it shall promptly control them and transfer them to public security organs, state security organs, or other competent authorities for handling:

(1) Persons who are committing crimes;
(2) Persons who are wanted;
(3) Persons who illegally carry articles that endanger public safety;
(4) Persons who are committing acts that endanger the safety of guard targets;
(5) Persons who obstruct the People's Armed Police Force from performing tasks by violence, threats, or other means.

Article 21 When the People's Armed Police Force assists public security organs, state security organs, prisons, etc., in executing arrest and pursuit tasks, it shall, in accordance with the decisions of the assisted organs, assist in searching the persons and residences of criminal suspects, defendants, and criminals, as well as places and means of transportation suspected of harboring criminal suspects, defendants, criminals, or illegal articles.

Article 22 When the People's Armed Police Force performs tasks of guard duty, handling sudden social security incidents, and preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, the use of police implements and weapons shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions on the use of police implements and weapons by the People's Police and other relevant laws and regulations.

Article 23 When the People's Armed Police Force encounters obstruction or interference while performing tasks, it may take necessary measures to remove obstacles and enforce implementation.

When the People's Armed Police Force needs to take measures to perform tasks, it shall strictly control them within the necessary limits. Where multiple measures are available, the measure that is most conducive to maximizing the protection of the rights and interests of individuals and organizations shall be chosen.

Article 24 When urgently needed for performing tasks, upon presentation of the People's Armed Police certificate, People's Armed Police personnel may have priority in taking public transportation; when encountering traffic obstructions, they shall have priority of passage.

Article 25 When urgently needed for performing tasks, in an emergency, upon presentation of the People's Armed Police certificate by the on-site commander, the People's Armed Police Force may have priority in using or lawfully requisitioning equipment, facilities, premises, buildings, means of transportation, and other materials and appliances of individuals and organizations. After the task is completed, they shall promptly return or restore them to their original condition, and pay the costs in accordance with relevant state regulations; if losses are caused, compensation shall be given in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 26 When the People's Armed Police Force goes abroad to perform tasks such as preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, it shall do so in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and the provisions of the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Obligations and Discipline

Article 27 People's Armed Police personnel shall obey orders, follow commands, perform their duties according to law, and resolutely complete tasks.

Article 28 When People's Armed Police personnel encounter situations where the personal or property safety of citizens is violated or they are in other dangerous circumstances, they shall promptly provide assistance.

Article 29 People's Armed Police personnel shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Disobeying decisions and orders of superiors, acting passively, or deserting in the face of battle;
(2) Using police implements or weapons in violation of regulations;
(3) Unlawfully depriving or restricting others' personal freedom, or unlawfully inspecting or searching persons, articles, means of transportation, residences, or places;
(4) Physically punishing, abusing, or beating persons under custody, supervision, or control;
(5) Abusing power, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, deserting their posts, or neglecting their duties;
(6) Harboring or conniving at illegal or criminal activities;
(7) Disclosing state secrets or military secrets;
(8) Other illegal or disciplinary acts.

Article 30 When performing tasks, People's Armed Police personnel shall wear uniforms as required, hold their People's Armed Police certificates, and use recording equipment to record evidence and present their certificates as required.

Article 31 People's Armed Police personnel shall behave civilly, be polite to others, abide by social ethics, and respect citizens' religious beliefs and ethnic customs.

## Chapter V Safeguard Measures

Article 32 To ensure the People's Armed Police Force performs its tasks, central state organs, local people's governments at or above the county level, and their relevant departments shall, in accordance with their duties, promptly notify the People's Armed Police Force of the following intelligence information:

(1) Social security information;
(2) Intelligence information on terrorist incidents and emergencies;
(3) Information on weather, hydrology, marine environment, geospatial, disaster warnings, etc.;
(4) Other intelligence information related to the performance of tasks.

Central state organs and local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish intelligence information sharing mechanisms with the People's Armed Police Force, and may provide intelligence information and data resources related to the performance of tasks by means such as connecting security information networks, intelligence information systems, and databases.

The People's Armed Police Force shall strictly keep confidential and lawfully use the relevant information obtained.

Article 33 The state shall establish a funding guarantee mechanism that is compatible with economic and social development and coordinated with the tasks and construction development of the People's Armed Police Force. The required funds shall be included in the budget in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 34 The target unit of a guard duty and its superior competent department shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, provide necessary guarantees such as guard facilities and living facilities for the People's Armed Police Force unit performing the guard duty.

Article 35 People's Armed Police personnel performing tasks at target units of guard duties in toxic, dusty, radioactive, noisy, or other severely polluted environments, or in high temperature, low temperature, oxygen-deficient, or other harsh environments, shall enjoy the same protection conditions and welfare subsidies as the staff of the target unit of the guard duty, which shall be guaranteed by the target unit of the guard duty or its superior competent department.

Article 36 The special insignia, uniforms, police equipment, certificates, and seals of the People's Armed Police Force shall be manufactured and provided in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Article 37 The People's Armed Police Force shall, based on the needs of performing tasks, strengthen the education and training of its People's Armed Police personnel to improve their ability to perform tasks according to law.

Article 38 If People's Armed Police personnel are killed, injured, or disabled while performing tasks, they shall be given pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant state regulations on military pensions and preferential treatment.

Article 39 When the People's Armed Police Force performs tasks according to law, citizens, legal persons, and other organizations shall provide necessary support and assistance.

Acts of assistance provided by citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to the People's Armed Police Force in performing tasks shall be protected by law.

If citizens, legal persons, and other organizations are killed, injured, disabled, or suffer property losses due to assisting the People's Armed Police Force in performing tasks, they shall be given pensions, preferential treatment, or corresponding compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 40 The People's Armed Police Force shall supervise and inspect its subordinate units and personnel regarding their compliance with laws, regulations, and discipline.

Article 41 People's Armed Police personnel are subject to the supervision of the Central Military Commission Supervisory Committee and the supervisory committees at all levels of the People's Armed Police Force.

When People's Armed Police personnel perform tasks such as guard duty, handling sudden social security incidents, preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, maritime rights protection and law enforcement, and emergency rescue and disaster relief, they shall accept supervision from people's governments and their relevant departments, citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 42 Upon receiving reports or accusations from citizens, legal persons, and other organizations, or upon receiving notifications from people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments regarding illegal or disciplinary acts of People's Armed Police personnel, the Central Military Commission Supervisory Committee and the supervisory committees at all levels of the People's Armed Police Force shall promptly investigate and handle the matter according to law, and provide feedback on the handling results to the informant or accuser, or notify the people's government at or above the county level and its relevant departments, in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 43 If People's Armed Police personnel fail to perform their duties in the execution of tasks, or commit any of the acts listed in Article 29 of this Law, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Article 44 Anyone who obstructs the People's Armed Police Force from performing tasks according to law, by committing any of the following acts, shall be given public security penalties by the public security organs in accordance with the law:

(1) Insulting, threatening, surrounding, intercepting, or attacking People's Armed Police personnel who are performing tasks;
(2) Forcibly breaking through the warning lines or warning zones set up by the People's Armed Police Force;
(3) Refusing or obstructing the People's Armed Police Force from performing tasks such as pursuit, inspection, search, rescue, or cordon;
(4) Obstructing the passage of means of transportation and personnel of the People's Armed Police Force performing tasks;
(5) Other acts that seriously obstruct the People's Armed Police Force from performing tasks.

Article 45 Anyone who illegally manufactures, buys, sells, possesses, or uses the special insignia, police equipment, certificates, or seals of the People's Armed Police Force shall be detained for not more than 15 days or warned by the public security organs, and may also be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income.

Article 46 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 47 The performance of maritime rights protection and law enforcement tasks by the People's Armed Police Force shall be separately provided by law.

Article 48 The performance of defense operations tasks by the People's Armed Police Force shall be carried out in accordance with the orders of the Central Military Commission.

Article 49 The performance of martial law tasks by the People's Armed Police Force shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Martial Law Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 50 When civilian personnel of the People's Armed Police Force perform tasks stipulated in this Law, they shall perform the relevant duties and obligations of the People's Armed Police in accordance with the law and enjoy corresponding rights and interests.

Article 51 This Law shall come into force on June 21, 2020.
