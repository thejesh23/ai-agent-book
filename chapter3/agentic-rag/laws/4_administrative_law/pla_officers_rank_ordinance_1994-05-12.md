# Regulations on the Military Ranks of Officers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army

Adopted at the Second Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on July 1, 1988; amended in accordance with the "Decision on Revising the Regulations on the Military Ranks of Officers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army" adopted at the Seventh Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 12, 1994.

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Constitution of the People's Republic of China and the Military Service Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 2 In order to strengthen the revolutionization, modernization, and regularization of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, facilitate the command and management of the armed forces, and enhance the sense of responsibility and honor among officers, a system of military ranks for officers is implemented.

Article 3 The military rank of an officer is a title and insignia that distinguishes the officer's grade, indicates the officer's status, and represents the honor conferred by the state upon the officer.

Article 4 The military ranks of officers are divided into those for officers on active service and those for officers in the reserve, according to the nature of their service.

Article 5 An officer with a higher military rank is considered the superior of an officer with a lower military rank. When an officer with a higher military rank is subordinate in position to an officer with a lower military rank, the officer with the higher position is considered the superior.

Article 6 When an officer on active service transfers to the reserve, the term "Reserve" is prefixed to the officer's military rank. When an officer on active service retires from service, the officer's military rank is retained, and the term "Retired" is prefixed to the officer's military rank.

## Chapter II Establishment of Military Rank Grades for Officers on Active Service

Article 7 The military ranks of officers are established in the following three categories and ten grades:

(1) General Officers: General, Lieutenant General, Major General;

(2) Field Grade Officers: Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major;

(3) Company Grade Officers: Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant.

Article 8 The military ranks of officers are distinguished according to the following provisions:

(1) Officers in command, political work, and logistics: General, Lieutenant General, Major General, Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant.

For officers of the Navy and the Air Force, the terms "Navy" and "Air Force" are respectively prefixed to their military ranks.

(2) Officers in technical specialties: Lieutenant General, Major General, Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant. The term "Technical" is prefixed to their military ranks.

## Chapter III Authorized Military Ranks for Officer Positions on Active Service

Article 9 The People's Liberation Army implements a system of authorized military ranks for officer positions.

Article 10 The Central Military Commission of the People's Republic of China leads the national armed forces. The Central Military Commission practices a system of chairman responsibility. The Chairman of the Central Military Commission is not awarded a military rank.

The authorized military rank for the position of Vice Chairman of the Central Military Commission is General.

The authorized military rank for the position of Member of the Central Military Commission is General.

Article 11 Officers in command, political work, and logistics implement the following authorized military ranks for their positions:

Chief of the General Staff of the People's Liberation Army, Director of the General Political Department: General;

Principal Military Region level: General, Lieutenant General;

Deputy Military Region level: Lieutenant General, Major General;

Principal Army level: Major General, Lieutenant General;

Deputy Army level: Major General, Senior Colonel;

Principal Division level: Senior Colonel, Major General;

Deputy Division level (Principal Brigade level): Colonel, Senior Colonel;

Principal Regiment level (Deputy Brigade level): Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel;

Deputy Regiment level: Lieutenant Colonel, Major;

Principal Battalion level: Major, Lieutenant Colonel;

Deputy Battalion level: Captain, Major;

Principal Company level: Captain, First Lieutenant;

Deputy Company level: First Lieutenant, Captain;

Platoon level: Second Lieutenant, First Lieutenant.

Article 12 Officers in technical specialties implement the following authorized military ranks for their positions:

Senior technical positions: Lieutenant General to Major General;

Intermediate technical positions: Senior Colonel to Captain;

Junior technical positions: Lieutenant Colonel to Second Lieutenant.

## Chapter IV Initial Conferment of Military Ranks for Officers on Active Service

Article 13 Military ranks of officers are conferred according to the authorized military ranks for their positions.

Article 14 The conferment of a military rank on an officer is based on the officer's position, moral character and ability, work performance, contributions to the revolutionary cause, and length of service in the armed forces.

Article 15 Personnel initially appointed to officer positions are conferred their first military rank according to the following provisions:

(1) Graduates of military secondary specialized schools are conferred the rank of Second Lieutenant;

Graduates of junior college programs are conferred the rank of Second Lieutenant, and may be conferred the rank of First Lieutenant in accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army;

Graduates of undergraduate programs are conferred the rank of First Lieutenant, and may be conferred the rank of Second Lieutenant in accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army;

Those who have obtained a master's degree are conferred the rank of Captain, and may be conferred the rank of First Lieutenant in accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army; graduates of postgraduate programs who have not obtained a master's degree are conferred the rank of First Lieutenant;

Those who have obtained a doctoral degree are conferred the rank of Major, and may be conferred the rank of Captain in accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army.

(2) Enlisted personnel appointed to officer positions during wartime are conferred the corresponding military rank according to the authorized military rank for the officer position.

(3) Civilian cadres of the armed forces and personnel from non-military departments appointed to officer positions are conferred the corresponding military rank according to the authorized military rank for the officer position.

Article 16 The initial conferment of military ranks on officers is approved according to the following authorities:

(1) The ranks of General, Lieutenant General, Major General, Senior Colonel, and Colonel are approved for conferment by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;

(2) The ranks of Lieutenant Colonel and Major are approved for conferment by the principal heads of the various general departments, military regions, services and arms of the People's Liberation Army, or other units equivalent to a military region;

(3) The ranks of Captain, First Lieutenant, and Second Lieutenant are approved for conferment by the principal heads of group armies or other army-level units with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

## Chapter V Promotion of Military Ranks for Officers on Active Service

Article 17 The military ranks of officers are promoted according to the following time limits:

(1) Time limits for the promotion of military ranks during peacetime: For promotion from Second Lieutenant to First Lieutenant, the period is two years for those who have graduated from junior college or above, and three years for others; for promotion from First Lieutenant to Captain, Captain to Major, Major to Lieutenant Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel to Colonel, and Colonel to Senior Colonel, the period is four years each; promotion to the rank of Senior Colonel and above is by selection, based on the officer's position, moral character and ability, and contributions to national defense construction;

(2) During wartime, the time limits for the promotion of military ranks may be shortened, with specific measures to be stipulated by the Central Military Commission based on wartime conditions.

Time spent by an officer studying at an educational institution is counted within the time limit for promotion.

Article 18 Military ranks of officers shall generally be promoted step by step according to the prescribed time limits.

Article 19 If, upon the expiration of the time limit for promotion, an officer does not meet the conditions for promotion due to a violation of military discipline, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission, the promotion shall be postponed, or the officer shall be discharged from active service.

Article 20 If an officer's military rank is lower than the minimum authorized rank for the new position due to a promotion in position, the officer's rank shall be promoted early to the minimum authorized rank for the new position.

Article 21 If an officer makes outstanding contributions in combat or work, the officer's military rank may be promoted early.

Article 22 Officers who are appointed to the positions of Vice Chairman or Member of the Central Military Commission and are promoted to the rank of General shall be awarded the rank of General by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission.

Article 23 The promotion of military ranks for officers other than those specified in Article 22 of these Regulations shall be approved in accordance with the authority for the appointment and removal of officers in their positions. However, the promotion of military ranks for the following officers shall be approved according to the following provisions:

(1) The promotion of Deputy Division level (Principal Brigade level) officers to Senior Colonel, and the promotion of officers in technical specialties to Senior Colonel, Major General, and Lieutenant General, shall be approved by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;

(2) The promotion of officers in technical specialties to Colonel shall be approved by the principal heads of the various general departments, military regions, services and arms of the People's Liberation Army, or other units equivalent to a military region;

(3) The promotion of Deputy Battalion level officers to Major, and the promotion of officers in technical specialties to Major and Lieutenant Colonel, shall be approved by the principal heads of group armies or other army-level units with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

## Chapter VI Demotion, Cancellation, and Deprivation of Military Ranks for Officers on Active Service

Article 24 If an officer is transferred to a lower position due to incompetence in the current position, and the officer's military rank is higher than the maximum authorized rank for the new position, the rank shall be adjusted to the maximum authorized rank for the new position. The authority for approving the adjustment of the military rank shall be the same as the authority for approving the original rank.

Article 25 If an officer violates military discipline, the officer may be subjected to a demotion in military rank as a disciplinary measure, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission. The authority for approving a demotion in military rank shall be the same as the authority for initially approving the conferment of that rank.

Demotion in military rank shall not apply to officers holding the rank of Second Lieutenant.

Article 26 If an officer's military rank is demoted, the time limit for the promotion of the officer's military rank shall be recalculated from the demoted rank.

If an officer who has been subjected to a demotion in military rank has corrected the mistake and has made significant achievements in combat or work, the time limit for the promotion of the officer's military rank may be shortened.

Article 27 For personnel whose officer position is revoked and whose officer status is cancelled, their military rank shall be cancelled. The authority for approving the cancellation of a military rank shall be the same as the authority for initially approving the conferment of that rank.

If an officer is expelled from military service, the officer's military rank shall be cancelled. The authority for approving the cancellation of the military rank shall be the same as the authority for approving the expulsion from military service.

Article 28 If an officer commits a crime and is sentenced by law to deprivation of political rights or to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years, the officer shall be deprived of the military rank by court judgment.

If a retired officer commits a crime, the officer shall be deprived of the military rank in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If an officer who has been deprived of military rank due to a crime needs to serve in the armed forces and be conferred a military rank after serving the sentence, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Article 16 of these Regulations.

## Chapter VII Insignia and Wearing of Military Ranks for Officers on Active Service

Article 29 The patterns of shoulder boards and insignia for military ranks of officers, and the methods for wearing them, shall be promulgated by the Central Military Commission.

Article 30 The shoulder boards and insignia worn by an officer must correspond to the officer's military rank.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 31 The system of military ranks for reserve officers shall be stipulated separately.

Article 32 The system of military ranks for enlisted personnel shall be stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 33 The Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall implement a system of police ranks, with specific measures to be stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 34 The General Staff Department and the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army shall formulate implementing measures based on these Regulations, which shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the Central Military Commission.

Article 35 These Regulations shall come into effect on the date of their promulgation.
