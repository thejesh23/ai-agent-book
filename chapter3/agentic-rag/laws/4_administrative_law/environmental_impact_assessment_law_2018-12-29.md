# Environmental Impact Assessment Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 28, 2002

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of implementing the strategy of sustainable development, preventing adverse environmental impacts that may be caused by the implementation of plans and construction projects, and promoting the coordinated development of the economy, society, and the environment.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "environmental impact assessment" refers to the methods and systems for analyzing, predicting, and assessing the potential environmental impacts of implementing plans and construction projects, proposing countermeasures and measures to prevent or mitigate adverse environmental impacts, and conducting follow-up monitoring.

Article 3 Plans within the scope specified in Article 9 of this Law and projects that may have environmental impacts within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall undergo environmental impact assessment in accordance with this Law.

Article 4 Environmental impact assessment must be objective, open, and impartial, comprehensively considering the potential impacts of the implementation of plans or construction projects on various environmental factors and the ecosystems they constitute, providing a scientific basis for decision-making.

Article 5 The State encourages relevant entities, experts, and the public to participate in environmental impact assessment in appropriate ways.

Article 6 The State strengthens the construction of basic databases and evaluation indicator systems for environmental impact assessment, encourages and supports scientific research on methods and technical specifications for environmental impact assessment, establishes necessary information-sharing systems for environmental impact assessment, and enhances the scientific nature of environmental impact assessment.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, organize the establishment and improvement of basic databases and evaluation indicator systems for environmental impact assessment.

## Chapter II: Environmental Impact Assessment of Plans

Article 7 The relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts and their relevant departments, shall organize environmental impact assessments during the preparation of plans for land use, and for the construction and development of regions, river basins, and sea areas, and shall prepare chapters or statements on the environmental impacts of such plans.

The chapters or statements on the environmental impacts of a plan shall analyze, predict, and assess the potential environmental impacts after the plan is implemented, propose countermeasures and measures to prevent or mitigate adverse environmental impacts, and be submitted as part of the draft plan to the plan approval authority.

If a draft plan does not include chapters or statements on environmental impacts, the approval authority shall not approve it.

Article 8 The relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts and their relevant departments, shall organize environmental impact assessments for specific plans they prepare concerning industry, agriculture, animal husbandry, forestry, energy, water resources, transportation, urban construction, tourism, and natural resource development (hereinafter referred to as specific plans) before submitting the draft specific plan for approval, and shall submit an environmental impact report to the authority approving the specific plan.

For guiding plans among the specific plans listed in the preceding paragraph, environmental impact assessments shall be conducted in accordance with Article 7 of this Law.

Article 9 The specific scope of plans subject to environmental impact assessment in accordance with Articles 7 and 8 of this Law shall be prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 10 The environmental impact report for a specific plan shall include the following:

(1) Analysis, prediction, and assessment of the potential environmental impacts of implementing the plan;
(2) Countermeasures and measures to prevent or mitigate adverse environmental impacts;
(3) Conclusions of the environmental impact assessment.

Article 11 The authority preparing a specific plan that may cause adverse environmental impacts and directly involve the environmental rights and interests of the public shall, before submitting the draft plan for approval, hold demonstration meetings, public hearings, or adopt other forms to solicit opinions from relevant entities, experts, and the public on the draft environmental impact report, except where confidentiality is required by State regulations.

The preparing authority shall carefully consider the opinions of relevant entities, experts, and the public on the draft environmental impact report and shall attach an explanation of whether the opinions have been adopted or not in the environmental impact report submitted for review.

Article 12 When submitting a draft specific plan for approval, the preparing authority shall attach the environmental impact report for review by the approval authority; if the environmental impact report is not attached, the approval authority shall not approve it.

Article 13 Before approving a draft specific plan and making a decision, the local people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall first have the competent department of ecological environment or another department designated by the people's government convene a review panel composed of representatives from relevant departments and experts to review the environmental impact report. The review panel shall issue a written review opinion.

Experts participating in the review panel as specified in the preceding paragraph shall be randomly selected from the list of experts in relevant specialties within the expert database established in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

For specific plans approved by the relevant departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level, the review method for the environmental impact report shall be formulated by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 14 If the review panel proposes revision opinions, the preparing authority of the specific plan shall revise and improve the draft plan based on the conclusions of the environmental impact report and the review opinions, and explain the adoption of the conclusions and review opinions; if not adopted, reasons shall be provided.

When approving a draft specific plan, the local people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts or the relevant department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall take the conclusions of the environmental impact report and the review opinions as important bases for decision-making.

If the conclusions of the environmental impact report and the review opinions are not adopted during approval, an explanation shall be provided and kept on file for future reference.

Article 15 After the implementation of a plan with significant environmental impacts, the preparing authority shall promptly organize a follow-up environmental impact assessment and report the assessment results to the approval authority; if significant adverse environmental impacts are found, improvement measures shall be proposed promptly.

## Chapter III: Environmental Impact Assessment of Construction Projects

Article 16 The State implements classified management for the environmental impact assessment of construction projects based on the degree of their environmental impact.

The construction entity shall organize the preparation of an environmental impact report, an environmental impact statement, or the filing of an environmental impact registration form (hereinafter collectively referred to as environmental impact assessment documents) in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For projects that may cause significant environmental impacts, an environmental impact report shall be prepared to conduct a comprehensive assessment of the environmental impacts;
(2) For projects that may cause minor environmental impacts, an environmental impact statement shall be prepared to analyze or conduct a specific assessment of the environmental impacts;
(3) For projects with minimal environmental impacts that do not require an environmental impact assessment, an environmental impact registration form shall be filed.

The classified management list for environmental impact assessment of construction projects shall be formulated and published by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

Article 17 The environmental impact report for a construction project shall include the following:

(1) Overview of the construction project;
(2) Current environmental status around the construction project;
(3) Analysis, prediction, and assessment of the potential environmental impacts of the construction project;
(4) Environmental protection measures for the construction project and their technical and economic feasibility;
(5) Economic cost-benefit analysis of the environmental impacts of the construction project;
(6) Recommendations for environmental monitoring during the implementation of the construction project;
(7) Conclusions of the environmental impact assessment.

The content and format of the environmental impact statement and the environmental impact registration form shall be formulated by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

Article 18 The environmental impact assessment of a construction project shall avoid duplication with the environmental impact assessment of plans.

If a plan is considered a single construction project, its environmental impact assessment shall be conducted as for a construction project, and no separate plan environmental impact assessment is required.

If a plan that has already undergone environmental impact assessment includes specific construction projects, the conclusions of the plan's environmental impact assessment shall serve as an important basis for the environmental impact assessment of the construction projects, and the content of the construction project's environmental impact assessment shall be simplified based on the review opinions of the plan's environmental impact assessment.

Article 19 A construction entity may entrust a technical unit to conduct the environmental impact assessment of its construction project and prepare the environmental impact report or statement; if the construction entity has the technical capability for environmental impact assessment, it may conduct the assessment and prepare the documents itself.

The preparation of environmental impact reports and statements for construction projects shall comply with the relevant national standards and technical specifications for environmental impact assessment.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall formulate guidelines for capacity building and regulatory measures for the preparation of environmental impact reports and statements for construction projects.

A technical unit entrusted to prepare environmental impact reports or statements for a construction entity shall not have any conflict of interest with the competent department of ecological environment or other relevant approval authorities responsible for approving the documents.

Article 20 The construction entity shall be responsible for the content and conclusions of the environmental impact report or statement of its construction project, and the technical unit entrusted to prepare the documents shall bear corresponding responsibility for the documents it prepares.

The competent departments of ecological environment of local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall strengthen the supervision, management, and quality assessment of units preparing environmental impact reports and statements for construction projects.

The competent department of ecological environment responsible for approving environmental impact reports and statements for construction projects shall record relevant illegal information of the preparing unit, the lead preparer, and the main preparers in the social credit file, and include it in the national credit information sharing platform and the national enterprise credit information publicity system for public disclosure.

No entity or individual may designate a technical unit for a construction entity to prepare environmental impact reports or statements for construction projects.

Article 21 Except where confidentiality is required by State regulations, for construction projects that may cause significant environmental impacts and require the preparation of an environmental impact report, the construction entity shall, before submitting the report for approval, hold demonstration meetings, public hearings, or adopt other forms to solicit opinions from relevant entities, experts, and the public.

The environmental impact report submitted by the construction entity for approval shall attach an explanation of whether the opinions of relevant entities, experts, and the public have been adopted or not.

Article 22 The environmental impact report or statement of a construction project shall be submitted by the construction entity to the competent department of ecological environment with approval authority in accordance with State Council regulations.

The approval of the marine environmental impact report for marine engineering construction projects shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

The approval authority shall make a decision and notify the construction entity in writing within 60 days from the receipt of the environmental impact report and within 30 days from the receipt of the environmental impact statement.

The State implements record-filing management for environmental impact registration forms.

No fees shall be charged for the review, approval, or filing of environmental impact reports, statements, or registration forms for construction projects.

Article 23 The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council is responsible for approving the environmental impact assessment documents of the following construction projects:

(1) Construction projects of a special nature, such as nuclear facilities and top-secret projects;
(2) Construction projects spanning the administrative regions of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government;
(3) Construction projects approved by the State Council or by departments authorized by the State Council.

The approval authority for environmental impact assessment documents of construction projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

If a construction project may cause adverse environmental impacts across administrative regions and the relevant competent departments of ecological environment have disputes over the conclusions of the environmental impact assessment, the environmental impact assessment documents shall be approved by the common higher-level competent department of ecological environment.

Article 24 After the environmental impact assessment documents of a construction project are approved, if there are significant changes in the nature, scale, location, production process, or measures for pollution prevention and ecological damage prevention of the project, the construction entity shall re-submit the documents for approval.

If the construction of a project does not commence within five years from the date of approval of the environmental impact assessment documents, the documents shall be re-submitted to the original approval authority for re-review; the original approval authority shall notify the construction entity in writing of the review opinion within 10 days from the receipt of the documents.

Article 25 If the environmental impact assessment documents of a construction project have not been reviewed by the approval authority in accordance with the law or have been reviewed but not approved, the construction entity shall not commence construction.

Article 26 During the construction of a project, the construction entity shall simultaneously implement the environmental protection countermeasures and measures proposed in the environmental impact report, statement, and the approval opinion of the approval authority.

Article 27 If, during the construction or operation of a project, circumstances arise that are inconsistent with the approved environmental impact assessment documents, the construction entity shall organize a post-environmental impact assessment, take improvement measures, and report to the original approval authority of the environmental impact assessment documents and the project approval authority for record; the original approval authority may also require the construction entity to conduct a post-environmental impact assessment and take improvement measures.

Article 28 The competent department of ecological environment shall conduct follow-up inspections on the environmental impacts after a construction project is put into production or use. If serious environmental pollution or ecological damage is caused, the reasons and responsibilities shall be identified. If the environmental impact report or statement of the construction project has serious quality issues such as obviously false basic data, major defects, omissions, or falsifications in content, or incorrect or unreasonable conclusions, legal liability shall be pursued against the construction entity and its responsible personnel, as well as the entrusted technical unit and its relevant personnel, in accordance with Article 32 of this Law; if the staff of the approval authority are derelict of duty or negligent, approving environmental impact reports or statements that should not have been approved, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with Article 34 of this Law.

## Chapter IV: Legal Liability

Article 29 If a plan preparing authority violates the provisions of this Law by failing to organize an environmental impact assessment, or by engaging in fraud or dereliction of duty during the assessment, resulting in serious inaccuracies in the assessment, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by the higher-level authority or the supervisory authority in accordance with the law.

Article 30 If a plan approval authority illegally approves a draft plan that should have included chapters or statements on environmental impacts but did not, or a draft specific plan that should have attached an environmental impact report but did not, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by the higher-level authority or the supervisory authority in accordance with the law.

Article 31 If a construction entity commences construction without submitting an environmental impact report or statement for approval in accordance with the law, or without re-submitting or re-applying for review of the documents as required by Article 24 of this Law, the competent department of ecological environment at or above the county level shall order a halt to construction, impose a fine of not less than 1% but not more than 5% of the total investment in the project, depending on the severity of the violation and the consequences, and may order restoration to the original state; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel of the construction entity shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

If a construction entity commences construction without the environmental impact report or statement being approved or without re-review and approval by the original approval authority, penalties and sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

If a construction entity fails to file an environmental impact registration form in accordance with the law, the competent department of ecological environment at or above the county level shall order it to file and impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

If a construction entity of a marine engineering construction project commits any of the illegal acts listed in this Article, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 32 If an environmental impact report or statement for a construction project has serious quality issues such as obviously false basic data, major defects, omissions, or falsifications in content, or incorrect or unreasonable conclusions, the competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan on the construction entity, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan on the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisors, and other directly responsible personnel of the construction entity.

If a technical unit entrusted to prepare an environmental impact report or statement violates national standards and technical specifications for environmental impact assessment, resulting in serious quality issues in the documents it prepares, the competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall impose a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the fees collected; if the circumstances are serious, the unit shall be prohibited from engaging in the preparation of environmental impact reports or statements; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If a preparing unit commits the illegal acts specified in paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, the lead preparer and main preparers shall be prohibited from engaging in the preparation of environmental impact reports or statements for five years; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law, and they shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in such work.

Article 33 If a department responsible for reviewing, approving, or filing environmental impact assessment documents for construction projects charges fees during the approval or filing process, its higher-level authority or the supervisory authority shall order the return of the fees; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 34 If staff members of the competent department of ecological environment or other departments engage in favoritism, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or illegally approve environmental impact assessment documents for construction projects, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V: Supplementary ProvisionsArticle 35 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local conditions, require environmental impact assessments for plans formulated by the people's governments at the county level within their respective jurisdictions. Specific measures shall be formulated by the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government with reference to the provisions of Chapter II of this Law.

Article 36 Measures for environmental impact assessments of military facility construction projects shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with the principles of this Law.

Article 37 This Law shall come into effect as of September 1, 2003.
