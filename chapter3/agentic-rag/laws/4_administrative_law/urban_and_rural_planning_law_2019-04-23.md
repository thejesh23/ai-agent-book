# Urban and Rural Planning Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on October 28, 2007

First Amendment on April 24, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 23, 2019, according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of strengthening the administration of urban and rural planning, coordinating the spatial layout of urban and rural areas, improving the human settlement environment, and promoting the comprehensive, coordinated and sustainable development of urban and rural economy and society.

Article 2 This Law must be observed in formulating and implementing urban and rural plans and in carrying out construction activities within planned areas.

For the purpose of this Law, urban and rural plans include the system of plans for cities and towns, city plans, town plans, township plans, and village plans. City plans and town plans are divided into master plans and detailed plans. Detailed plans are divided into regulatory plans and site plans.

For the purpose of this Law, planned areas refer to the built-up areas of cities, towns and villages, as well as areas that must be subject to planning control due to the needs of urban and rural construction and development. The specific scope of planned areas shall be delimited by the relevant people's government in the master plans of cities, master plans of towns, township plans and village plans it organizes, based on the level of urban and rural economic and social development and the need for coordinated urban and rural development.

Article 3 Cities and towns shall formulate city plans and town plans in accordance with this Law. Construction activities within the planned areas of cities and towns shall conform to the planning requirements.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the level of local rural economic and social development and in accordance with the principles of adapting measures to local conditions and being practical and feasible, determine the areas where township plans and village plans should be formulated. Townships and villages within the determined areas shall formulate plans in accordance with this Law, and construction in townships and villages within the planned areas shall conform to the planning requirements.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall encourage and guide townships and villages in areas other than those specified in the preceding paragraph to formulate and implement township plans and village plans.

Article 4 The formulation and implementation of urban and rural plans shall follow the principles of coordinating urban and rural development, rational layout, economical use of land, intensive development, and planning before construction; improving the ecological environment; promoting the conservation and comprehensive utilization of resources and energy; protecting cultivated land and other natural resources, as well as historical and cultural heritage; preserving local characteristics, ethnic features, and traditional styles; preventing pollution and other public hazards; and meeting the needs of regional population development, national defense construction, disaster prevention and mitigation, public health, and public safety.

Construction activities within planned areas shall comply with the provisions of laws and regulations on land administration, natural resources, and environmental protection.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the actual local economic and social development, reasonably determine the development scale, steps, and construction standards of cities and towns in the master plans of cities and master plans of towns.

Article 5 The formulation of master plans of cities, master plans of towns, township plans, and village plans shall be based on the national economic and social development plan and shall be coordinated with the general land use plan.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall include the funds for the formulation and administration of urban and rural plans in their fiscal budgets.

Article 7 Urban and rural plans approved according to law are the basis for urban and rural construction and planning administration and shall not be modified without going through statutory procedures.

Article 8 The authorities responsible for organizing the formulation of urban and rural plans shall promptly publish the urban and rural plans approved according to law, except for contents that shall not be disclosed as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 9 Any unit or individual shall comply with the urban and rural plans approved and published according to law, submit to planning administration, and have the right to inquire with the competent department of urban and rural planning about whether construction activities involving their legitimate rights and interests conform to the planning requirements.

Any unit or individual has the right to report or accuse violations of urban and rural plans to the competent department of urban and rural planning or other relevant departments. The competent department of urban and rural planning or other relevant departments shall promptly accept and organize verification and handling of such reports or accusations.

Article 10 The State encourages the adoption of advanced science and technology to enhance the scientific nature of urban and rural planning and improve the effectiveness of the implementation, supervision, and administration of urban and rural plans.

Article 11 The competent department of urban and rural planning under the State Council shall be responsible for the administration of urban and rural planning nationwide.

The competent departments of urban and rural planning under local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the administration of urban and rural planning within their respective administrative areas.

## Chapter II Formulation of Urban and Rural Plans

Article 12 The competent department of urban and rural planning under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, organize the formulation of the national system of plans for cities and towns, which shall serve as guidance for the formulation of provincial systems of plans for cities and towns and master plans of cities.

The national system of plans for cities and towns shall be submitted by the competent department of urban and rural planning under the State Council to the State Council for approval.

Article 13 The people's governments of provinces and autonomous regions shall organize the formulation of provincial systems of plans for cities and towns and submit them to the State Council for approval.

The contents of a provincial system of plans for cities and towns shall include: the spatial layout and scale control of cities and towns, the layout of major infrastructure, and areas that need strict control for the protection of the ecological environment, resources, etc.

Article 14 The people's government of a city shall organize the formulation of the master plan of the city.

The master plan of a municipality directly under the Central Government shall be submitted by the people's government of the municipality to the State Council for approval. The master plans of cities that are the seats of the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions, and of cities determined by the State Council, shall be submitted to the State Council for approval after examination and consent by the people's government of the relevant province or autonomous region. The master plans of other cities shall be submitted by the people's government of the city to the people's government of the relevant province or autonomous region for approval.

Article 15 The people's government of a county shall organize the formulation of the master plan of the town that is the seat of the county people's government and submit it to the people's government at the next higher level for approval. The master plans of other towns shall be organized by the people's government of the town and submitted to the people's government at the next higher level for approval.

Article 16 Before a provincial system of plans for cities and towns organized by the people's government of a province or autonomous region, or a master plan organized by the people's government of a city or county, is submitted to the people's government at the next higher level for approval, it shall first be deliberated by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level. The deliberation opinions of the members of the standing committee shall be referred to the people's government at the corresponding level for study and handling.

Before a master plan of a town organized by the people's government of a town is submitted to the people's government at the next higher level for approval, it shall first be deliberated by the people's congress of the town. The deliberation opinions of the deputies shall be referred to the people's government of the town for study and handling.

When submitting a provincial system of plans for cities and towns, a master plan of a city, or a master plan of a town for approval, the authority organizing the formulation shall also submit the deliberation opinions of the members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the deputies of the people's congress of the town, along with the situation of modifications made to the plan based on those opinions.

Article 17 The contents of a master plan of a city or a master plan of a town shall include: the development layout of the city or town, functional zoning, land use layout, comprehensive transportation system, areas where construction is prohibited, restricted, or suitable, and various special plans.

The scope of the planned area, the scale of construction land within the planned area, land for infrastructure and public service facilities, water sources and water systems, basic farmland and green space, environmental protection, protection of natural and historical and cultural heritage, and disaster prevention and mitigation shall be mandatory contents of the master plan of a city or a master plan of a town.

The planning period for a master plan of a city or a master plan of a town is generally twenty years. A master plan of a city shall also include predictive arrangements for the longer-term development of the city.

Article 18 Township plans and village plans shall be based on the actual conditions of rural areas, respect the wishes of villagers, and reflect local and rural characteristics.

The contents of a township plan or a village plan shall include: the scope of the planned area; the land use layout and construction requirements for various constructions such as housing, roads, water supply, drainage, power supply, garbage collection, livestock and poultry breeding sites, and other rural production and living service facilities and public welfare undertakings; and specific arrangements for the protection of cultivated land and other natural resources, historical and cultural heritage, and disaster prevention and mitigation. A township plan shall also include the development layout of villages within its administrative area.

Article 19 The competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of a city shall, based on the requirements of the master plan of the city, organize the formulation of regulatory plans for the city. After approval by the people's government at the corresponding level, these plans shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the people's government at the next higher level for recordation.

Article 20 The people's government of a town shall, based on the requirements of the master plan of the town, organize the formulation of regulatory plans for the town and submit them to the people's government at the next higher level for approval. The regulatory plan for the town that is the seat of the county people's government shall be organized by the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the county based on the requirements of the master plan of the town. After approval by the people's government of the county, it shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the people's government at the next higher level for recordation.

Article 21 The competent departments of urban and rural planning under the people's governments of cities and counties and the people's governments of towns may organize the formulation of site plans for important plots. Site plans shall conform to the regulatory plans.

Article 22 The people's governments of townships and towns shall organize the formulation of township plans and village plans and submit them to the people's government at the next higher level for approval. Before a village plan is submitted for approval, it shall be discussed and approved by the villagers' meeting or the villagers' representative meeting.

Article 23 The master plan and detailed plans of the capital shall take into overall consideration the needs for the land use layout and spatial arrangement of the central state organs.

Article 24 The authorities responsible for organizing the formulation of urban and rural plans shall entrust units with corresponding qualification grades to carry out the specific formulation of urban and rural plans.

Units engaged in the formulation of urban and rural plans shall meet the following conditions and, after passing the examination by the competent department of urban and rural planning under the State Council or the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government according to law and obtaining a qualification certificate of the corresponding grade, may engage in the formulation of urban and rural plans within the scope permitted by the qualification grade:

(1) Having the status of a legal person;

(2) Having the prescribed number of planners registered with relevant industry associations;

(3) Having the prescribed number of relevant professional and technical personnel;

(4) Having corresponding technical equipment;

(5) Having sound technical, quality, and financial management systems.

The formulation of urban and rural plans must comply with the relevant national standards.

Article 25 The formulation of urban and rural plans shall be based on the basic data on survey, mapping, meteorology, seismology, hydrology, environment, etc., as prescribed by the State.

The relevant competent departments under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs for formulating urban and rural plans, promptly provide the relevant basic data.

Article 26 Before an urban or rural plan is submitted for approval, the authority organizing its formulation shall publicly announce the draft plan according to law and solicit opinions from experts and the public through feasibility study meetings, hearings, or other means. The period for public announcement shall not be less than thirty days.

The authority organizing the formulation shall fully consider the opinions of experts and the public and shall attach the status of adoption of opinions and the reasons in the materials submitted for approval.

Article 27 Before a provincial system of plans for cities and towns, a master plan of a city, or a master plan of a town is approved, the approving authority shall organize experts and relevant departments to conduct a review.

## Chapter III Implementation of Urban and Rural Plans

Article 28 Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on the local level of economic and social development, act within their capabilities, respect the wishes of the masses, and organize the implementation of urban and rural plans in a planned and step-by-step manner.

Article 29 In the construction and development of a city, priority shall be given to the construction of infrastructure and public service facilities; the relationship between the development of new areas and the redevelopment of old areas shall be properly handled; and the needs of migrant workers for urban life and the economic and social development of surrounding rural areas, as well as the production and living needs of villagers, shall be taken into overall consideration.

In the construction and development of a town, priority shall be given, in conjunction with rural economic and social development and industrial restructuring, to the construction of infrastructure such as water supply, drainage, power supply, gas supply, roads, communications, radio and television, and public service facilities such as schools, health centers, cultural stations, kindergartens, and welfare homes, so as to provide services to surrounding rural areas.

In the construction and development of townships and villages, measures shall be adapted to local conditions, land shall be used economically, the role of villagers' self-governing organizations shall be brought into play, villagers shall be guided to carry out construction rationally, and rural production and living conditions shall be improved.

Article 30 In the development and construction of new urban areas, the construction scale and sequence shall be reasonably determined; existing municipal infrastructure and public service facilities shall be fully utilized; natural resources and the ecological environment shall be strictly protected; and local characteristics shall be reflected.

No development zones or new urban areas of any type shall be established outside the construction land areas determined in the master plan of a city or the master plan of a town.

Article 31 In the redevelopment of old urban areas, historical and cultural heritage and traditional styles shall be protected; the scale of demolition and construction shall be reasonably determined; and areas with concentrated dilapidated houses, backward infrastructure, etc., shall be redeveloped in a planned manner.

The protection of famous historical and cultural cities, towns, and villages, as well as the maintenance and use of protected buildings, shall comply with the relevant laws, administrative regulations, and provisions of the State Council.

Article 32 In urban and rural construction and development, scenic and historical resources shall be protected and rationally utilized according to law, and the construction of scenic and historical areas and surrounding townships, towns, and villages shall be arranged in a coordinated manner.

The planning, construction, and administration of scenic and historical areas shall comply with the relevant laws, administrative regulations, and provisions of the State Council.

Article 33 The development and utilization of urban underground space shall be commensurate with the level of economic and technological development, follow the principles of overall arrangement, comprehensive development, and rational utilization, fully consider the needs for disaster prevention and mitigation, civil air defense, communications, etc., conform to the city plan, and go through the procedures for planning approval.

Article 34 The people's governments of cities, counties, and towns shall, based on the master plan of the city, the master plan of the town, the general land use plan and annual plan, and the national economic and social development plan, formulate short-term construction plans and report them to the authority that approved the master plan for recordation.

Short-term construction plans shall focus on important infrastructure, public service facilities, housing construction for low- and middle-income residents, and ecological and environmental protection, and shall specify the sequence, development direction, and spatial layout of short-term construction. The planning period for a short-term construction plan is five years.

Article 35 The use of land determined by urban and rural plans for railways, highways, ports, airports, roads, green spaces, power transmission and distribution facilities and transmission line corridors, communication facilities, radio and television facilities, pipeline facilities, river courses, reservoirs, water sources, nature reserves, flood control passages, fire fighting passages, nuclear power stations, landfills and incineration plants, sewage treatment plants, and public service facilities, as well as other land that needs to be protected according to law, is prohibited from being changed without authorization.

Article 36 For a construction project that requires approval or verification by relevant departments according to national regulations, where the right to use state-owned land is to be provided by means of allocation, the construction unit shall, before submitting the project to the relevant departments for approval or verification, apply to the competent department of urban and rural planning for the issuance of a site selection opinion letter.

Construction projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph do not need to apply for a site selection opinion letter.

Article 37 For a construction project within the planned area of a city or town where the right to use state-owned land is to be provided by means of allocation, after the project is approved, verified, or recorded by the relevant departments, the construction unit shall apply to the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county for a construction land planning permit. The competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county shall, based on the regulatory plan, determine the location, area, and permissible construction scope of the construction land and issue the construction land planning permit.

Only after obtaining the construction land planning permit may the construction unit apply to the land administration department under the local people's government at or above the county level for land use. After approval by the people's government at or above the county level, the land shall be allocated by the land administration department.

Article 38 For a construction project within the planned area of a city or town where the right to use state-owned land is to be provided by means of transfer, before the transfer of the right to use state-owned land, the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county shall, based on the regulatory plan, propose planning conditions such as the location, use nature, and development intensity of the land parcel to be transferred, which shall be part of the contract for the transfer of the right to use state-owned land. No right to use state-owned land shall be transferred for a land parcel for which planning conditions have not been determined.

For a construction project that obtains the right to use state-owned land by means of transfer, after obtaining the approval, verification, or recordation documents for the project and signing the contract for the transfer of the right to use state-owned land, the construction unit shall obtain the construction land planning permit from the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county.

The competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of a city or county shall not, in the construction land planning permit, arbitrarily change the planning conditions that are part of the contract for the transfer of the right to use state-owned land.

Article 39 If the planning conditions are not included in the contract for the transfer of the right to use state-owned land, the contract for the transfer of the right to use state-owned land shall be invalid; if land is approved for a construction unit that has not obtained a construction land planning permit, the people's government at or above the county level shall revoke the relevant approval documents; if land has been occupied, it shall be returned promptly; if losses are caused to the parties concerned, compensation shall be given according to law.

Article 40 For the construction of buildings, structures, roads, pipelines, and other projects within the planned area of a city or town, the construction unit or individual shall apply to the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county, or the people's government of the town determined by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, for a construction project planning permit.

To apply for a construction project planning permit, the applicant shall submit relevant documents proving the use of land, the design plan of the construction project, and other materials. For a construction project that requires the construction unit to prepare a site plan, the site plan shall also be submitted. If the project conforms to the regulatory plan and planning conditions, the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county, or the people's government of the town determined by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, shall issue the construction project planning permit.

The competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of a city or county, or the people's government of a town determined by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, shall, according to law, publish the approved site plan and the general layout plan of the construction project design.

Article 41 For the construction of township enterprises, rural public facilities, and public welfare undertakings within the planned area of a township or village, the construction unit or individual shall apply to the people's government of the township or town, which shall report to the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county for the issuance of a rural construction planning permit.

The planning administration measures for the construction of rural villagers' houses on existing homesteads within the planned area of a township or village shall be formulated by the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

For the construction of township enterprises, rural public facilities, and public welfare undertakings, as well as the construction of rural villagers' houses within the planned area of a township or village, agricultural land shall not be occupied; if it is truly necessary to occupy agricultural land, after going through the procedures for the conversion of agricultural land for construction use in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, the competent department of urban and rural planning under the people's government of the city or county shall issue the rural construction planning permit.

Only after obtaining the rural construction planning permit may the construction unit or individual go through the procedures for land use approval.

Article 42 The competent department of urban and rural planning shall not make planning permits outside the construction land areas determined in urban and rural plans.**Article 43** A construction unit shall carry out construction in accordance with the planning conditions; if changes are indeed necessary, it must apply to the competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government. If the changes do not conform to the regulatory detailed plan, the competent department of urban and rural planning shall not approve them. The competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government shall promptly notify the land department at the same level of the legally changed planning conditions and make them public.

The construction unit shall promptly report the legally changed planning conditions to the relevant land department of the people's government for recordation.

**Article 44** Temporary construction within the urban or town planning area shall be approved by the competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government. If the temporary construction affects the implementation of a near-term construction plan or regulatory detailed plan, or impacts traffic, city appearance, safety, etc., approval shall not be granted.

Temporary construction shall be demolished by the unit itself within the approved period of use.

Specific measures for the planning management of temporary construction and temporary land use shall be formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

**Article 45** The competent department of urban and rural planning of a local people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, verify whether a construction project complies with the planning conditions. If the verification has not been carried out or the project does not meet the planning conditions upon verification, the construction unit shall not organize the completion acceptance.

The construction unit shall, within six months after the completion acceptance, submit relevant completion acceptance materials to the competent department of urban and rural planning.

## Chapter IV Modification of Urban and Rural Plans

**Article 46** The organization and compilation authority for a provincial urban system plan, a city master plan, or a town master plan shall organize relevant departments and experts to regularly evaluate the implementation of the plan and shall solicit public opinions through methods such as feasibility study meetings, public hearings, or other means. The organization and compilation authority shall submit an evaluation report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, the town people's congress, and the original approval authority, along with a summary of the opinions solicited.

**Article 47** The organization and compilation authority may modify a provincial urban system plan, a city master plan, or a town master plan in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures only under one of the following circumstances:

(1) The urban and rural plan formulated by the higher-level people's government has changed, requiring modification of the plan;
(2) Administrative division adjustments necessitate modification of the plan;
(3) A major construction project approved by the State Council necessitates modification of the plan;
(4) Evaluation indicates that modification of the plan is necessary;
(5) Other circumstances where the approval authority for the urban and rural plan deems modification necessary.

Before modifying a provincial urban system plan, a city master plan, or a town master plan, the organization and compilation authority shall summarize the implementation of the original plan and report to the original approval authority; if the modification involves mandatory content of the city master plan or town master plan, a special report shall first be submitted to the original approval authority, and the modification plan may be prepared only after obtaining consent.

The modified provincial urban system plan, city master plan, or town master plan shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the approval procedures stipulated in Articles 13, 14, 15, and 16 of this Law.

**Article 48** To modify a regulatory detailed plan, the organization and compilation authority shall demonstrate the necessity of the modification, solicit opinions from interested parties within the planning area, and submit a special report to the original approval authority. The modification plan may be prepared only after obtaining consent from the original approval authority. The modified regulatory detailed plan shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the approval procedures stipulated in Articles 19 and 20 of this Law. If the modification of the regulatory detailed plan involves mandatory content of the city master plan or town master plan, the master plan shall be modified first.

Modifications to a township plan or village plan shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the approval procedures stipulated in Article 22 of this Law.

**Article 49** If a city, county, or town people's government modifies a near-term construction plan, it shall report the modified near-term construction plan to the approval authority of the master plan for recordation.

**Article 50** If, after the issuance of a site selection opinion letter, a construction land planning permit, a construction project planning permit, or a rural construction planning permit, the lawful rights and interests of the permit holder are damaged due to the lawful modification of an urban or rural plan, compensation shall be provided in accordance with the law.

The approved detailed construction plan and the general layout plan of the construction project design scheme shall not be modified arbitrarily; if modification is indeed necessary, the competent department of urban and rural planning shall adopt methods such as public hearings to hear the opinions of interested parties; if the modification causes damage to the lawful rights and interests of interested parties, compensation shall be provided in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supervision and Inspection

**Article 51** The people's governments at or above the county level and their competent departments of urban and rural planning shall strengthen supervision and inspection over the compilation, approval, implementation, and modification of urban and rural plans.

**Article 52** Local people's governments at various levels shall report the implementation of urban and rural plans to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the town people's congress and accept supervision.

**Article 53** The competent department of urban and rural planning of a people's government at or above the county level shall conduct supervision and inspection over the implementation of urban and rural plans and has the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Require relevant units and individuals to provide documents and materials related to the supervised matter and make copies thereof;
(2) Require relevant units and individuals to explain and clarify issues involved in the supervised matter, and enter the site for survey as necessary;
(3) Order relevant units and individuals to cease acts that violate laws and regulations concerning urban and rural planning.

Staff members of the competent department of urban and rural planning performing the supervision and inspection duties as stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall present their law enforcement credentials. The units and individuals under supervision and inspection shall cooperate and shall not obstruct or impede the lawful supervision and inspection activities.

**Article 54** The supervision and inspection situation and the results of handling shall be made public in accordance with the law for public access and supervision.

**Article 55** When investigating acts violating the provisions of this Law, if the competent department of urban and rural planning discovers that a state organ staff member should be given administrative sanctions according to law, it shall submit a proposal for sanctions to the appointing authority or the supervisory authority of that staff member.

**Article 56** If administrative penalties should be imposed according to the provisions of this Law, but the relevant competent department of urban and rural planning fails to impose them, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the higher-level people's government has the authority to order it to make a decision on administrative penalties or recommend to the relevant people's government to order it to impose administrative penalties.

**Article 57** If the competent department of urban and rural planning grants an administrative license in violation of the provisions of this Law, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the higher-level people's government has the authority to order its revocation or directly revoke the administrative license. If the revocation of the administrative license causes damage to the lawful rights and interests of the party concerned, compensation shall be provided in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 58** If an urban or rural plan that should be compiled according to law is not organized for compilation, or is compiled, approved, or modified not in accordance with legal procedures, the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; the person in charge of the relevant people's government and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 59** If the organization and compilation authority for an urban or rural plan entrusts a unit without the corresponding qualification grade to compile the plan, the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; the person in charge of the relevant people's government and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 60** If a town people's government or the competent department of urban and rural planning of a people's government at or above the county level commits any of the following acts, the people's government at the same level, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the higher-level people's government, or the supervisory authority shall, within its authority, order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Failing to organize the compilation of a regulatory detailed plan for a city or for the town where the county people's government is located, as required by law;
(2) Issuing a site selection opinion letter, a construction land planning permit, a construction project planning permit, or a rural construction planning permit beyond its authority or to an applicant who does not meet the statutory conditions;
(3) Failing to issue a site selection opinion letter, a construction land planning permit, a construction project planning permit, or a rural construction planning permit to an applicant who meets the statutory conditions within the statutory time limit;
(4) Failing to publish the approved detailed construction plan or the general layout plan of the construction project design scheme as required by law;
(5) Failing to adopt methods such as public hearings to hear the opinions of interested parties before agreeing to modify the detailed construction plan or the general layout plan of the construction project design scheme;
(6) Failing to investigate and deal with construction activities within the planning area that are carried out without obtaining a planning permit according to law or in violation of the provisions of a planning permit, or failing to handle them according to law after receiving a report.

**Article 61** If a relevant department of a people's government at or above the county level commits any of the following acts, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the higher-level people's government shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Issuing an approval document for a construction project for which a site selection opinion letter has not been obtained according to law;
(2) Failing to determine the planning conditions in a state-owned land use right transfer contract according to law, or changing the planning conditions lawfully determined in a state-owned land use right transfer contract;
(3) Allocating state-owned land use rights to a construction unit that has not obtained a construction land planning permit according to law.

**Article 62** If a unit engaged in urban and rural planning compilation commits any of the following acts, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government where it is located shall order correction within a specified time limit and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the planning compilation fee stipulated in the contract; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order a suspension of business for rectification, and the original issuing authority shall downgrade its qualification grade or revoke its qualification certificate; if losses are caused, it shall bear compensation liability according to law:

(1) Undertaking urban and rural planning compilation work beyond the scope permitted by its qualification grade;
(2) Compiling urban and rural plans in violation of relevant national standards.

If a unit undertakes urban and rural planning compilation work without obtaining a qualification certificate according to law, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order it to cease the illegal act and impose a fine according to the provisions of the preceding paragraph; if losses are caused, it shall bear compensation liability according to law.

If a unit obtains a qualification certificate by fraudulent means and undertakes urban and rural planning compilation work, the original issuing authority shall revoke the qualification certificate and impose a fine according to the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article; if losses are caused, it shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 63** If a unit engaged in urban and rural planning compilation, after obtaining a qualification certificate, no longer meets the corresponding qualification conditions, the original issuing authority shall order correction within a specified time limit; if it fails to correct within the time limit, its qualification grade shall be downgraded or its qualification certificate shall be revoked.

**Article 64** If construction is carried out without obtaining a construction project planning permit or not in accordance with the provisions of the construction project planning permit, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order a cessation of construction; if corrective measures can still be taken to eliminate the impact on the implementation of the plan, it shall order correction within a specified time limit and impose a fine of not less than five percent but not more than ten percent of the construction project cost; if the impact cannot be eliminated through corrective measures, it shall order demolition within a specified time limit; if demolition is not possible, the physical objects or illegal income shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than ten percent of the construction project cost may also be imposed.

**Article 65** If, within a township or village planning area, construction is carried out without obtaining a rural construction planning permit according to law or not in accordance with the provisions of the rural construction planning permit, the township or town people's government shall order a cessation of construction and correction within a specified time limit; if correction is not made within the time limit, demolition may be carried out.

**Article 66** If a construction unit or individual commits any of the following acts, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government where it is located shall order demolition within a specified time limit and may impose a fine of not more than one time the cost of the temporary construction project:

(1) Carrying out temporary construction without approval;
(2) Carrying out temporary construction not in accordance with the approved content;
(3) Failing to demolish temporary buildings or structures after the expiration of the approved period.

**Article 67** If a construction unit fails to submit relevant completion acceptance materials to the competent department of urban and rural planning within six months after the completion acceptance of the construction project, the competent department of urban and rural planning of the city or county people's government where it is located shall order a supplementary submission within a specified time limit; if the supplementary submission is not made within the time limit, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 68** After the competent department of urban and rural planning makes a decision ordering a cessation of construction or demolition within a specified time limit, if the party concerned does not cease construction or fails to demolish within the specified time limit, the local people's government at or above the county level where the construction project is located may instruct relevant departments to take measures such as sealing the construction site or carrying out compulsory demolition.

**Article 69** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 70** This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2008. The "Urban Planning Law of the People's Republic of China" shall be repealed simultaneously.
