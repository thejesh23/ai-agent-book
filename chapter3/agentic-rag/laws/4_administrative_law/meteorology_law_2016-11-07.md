# Meteorology Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 1999

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws Including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of developing meteorology, regulating meteorological work, issuing meteorological forecasts accurately and promptly, preventing meteorological disasters, rationally developing, utilizing and protecting climate resources, and providing meteorological services for economic development, national defense, social progress and the people's livelihood.

Article 2 This Law shall be observed in activities such as meteorological observation, forecasting, services, meteorological disaster prevention, climate resource utilization, and meteorological science and technology research conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 Meteorology is a basic public welfare undertaking for economic development, national defense, social progress and the people's livelihood. Meteorological work shall give priority to public welfare meteorological services.

People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen leadership and coordination of meteorological work, and incorporate meteorology into the national economic and social development plans and fiscal budgets at the corresponding central and local levels, so as to ensure that it fully serves the public, government decision-making and economic development.

For local meteorological projects constructed by local people's governments at or above the county level according to the needs of local socio-economic development, the investment shall be mainly borne by the finance at the corresponding level.

On the premise of ensuring free public welfare meteorological services, meteorological stations and observatories may lawfully provide paid meteorological services.

Article 4 Meteorological stations and observatories under the meteorological competent authorities of counties and cities shall primarily serve agricultural production, and provide timely and proactive public welfare meteorological information services necessary to safeguard local agricultural production.

Article 5 The meteorological competent authority under the State Council shall be responsible for meteorological work nationwide. Local meteorological competent authorities at various levels shall, under the leadership of the superior meteorological competent authority and the people's government at the corresponding level, be responsible for meteorological work within their respective administrative regions.

Meteorological stations and observatories under other relevant departments of the State Council and other relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall accept the guidance, supervision and industry management of the meteorological competent authority at the corresponding level regarding their meteorological work.

Article 6 Those engaged in meteorological business activities shall comply with the national meteorological technical standards, specifications and procedures.

Article 7 The State encourages and supports meteorological science and technology research, the popularization of meteorological scientific knowledge, the cultivation of meteorological personnel, the promotion of advanced meteorological science and technology, the protection of meteorological scientific and technological achievements, the strengthening of international meteorological cooperation and exchange, the development of the meteorological information industry, and the improvement of the level of meteorological work.

People's governments at all levels shall care for and support the construction and operation of meteorological stations and observatories in ethnic minority areas, remote and impoverished areas, harsh areas and islands.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions in meteorological work shall be rewarded.

Article 8 Foreign organizations and individuals engaging in meteorological activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China must obtain approval from the meteorological competent authority under the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

## Chapter II Construction and Management of Meteorological Facilities

Article 9 The meteorological competent authority under the State Council shall organize relevant departments to formulate construction plans for important meteorological facilities such as meteorological observation facilities, dedicated meteorological information transmission facilities, and large-scale specialized meteorological technical equipment, and submit them to the State Council for approval before implementation. Adjustments or modifications to the meteorological facility construction plan must be reported to the State Council for approval.

The formulation of meteorological facility construction plans shall follow the principles of rational layout, effective utilization, and balancing current and long-term needs, avoiding redundant construction.

Article 10 Construction projects for important meteorological facilities shall comply with the requirements of the important meteorological facility construction plan, and opinions shall be sought from the meteorological competent authority under the State Council or the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government before the project proposal and feasibility study report are approved.

Article 11 The State protects meteorological facilities according to law. No organization or individual may occupy, damage, or unauthorizedly move meteorological facilities.
When meteorological facilities are damaged due to force majeure, the local people's government shall take emergency measures and organize forces for repair to ensure the normal operation of the meteorological facilities.

Article 12 Without legal approval, no organization or individual may relocate meteorological stations or observatories. If relocation of a national baseline climate station or basic weather station is truly necessary due to the implementation of urban planning or key national construction projects, approval must be obtained from the meteorological competent authority under the State Council. If relocation of other meteorological stations or observatories is necessary, approval must be obtained from the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The relocation costs shall be borne by the construction unit.
Article 13 Specialized meteorological technical equipment shall meet the technical requirements stipulated by the meteorological competent authority under the State Council and pass the review of the said authority. Equipment that has not been reviewed or has failed the review shall not be used in meteorological operations.

Article 14 Meteorological measuring instruments shall be verified by meteorological metrological verification institutions in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China. Meteorological measuring instruments that have not been verified, have failed verification, or have exceeded the verification validity period shall not be used.

The meteorological competent authority under the State Council and the meteorological competent authorities of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may establish meteorological measurement standards as needed. Their highest measurement standards may only be used after passing assessment in accordance with the provisions of the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter III Meteorological Observation

Article 15 Meteorological stations and observatories under meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall conduct meteorological observations and submit meteorological observation data to the relevant meteorological competent authorities in accordance with the provisions of the meteorological competent authority under the State Council. Without approval from the superior meteorological competent authority, meteorological observations shall not be suspended.

The meteorological competent authority under the State Council and relevant local meteorological competent authorities shall promptly release basic meteorological observation data in accordance with national regulations.

Article 16 Meteorological stations and observatories under other relevant departments of the State Council and other relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as other organizations and individuals engaged in meteorological observations, shall submit the meteorological observation data they obtain to the meteorological competent authority under the State Council or the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with relevant national regulations.

Meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall, in accordance with the principle of sharing and common use of meteorological data and based on relevant national regulations, exchange relevant meteorological information and data with other institutions engaged in meteorological work.

Article 17 Offshore drilling platforms in the internal waters, territorial sea, and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, as well as aircraft flying on international routes and ocean-going vessels holding the nationality of the People's Republic of China, shall conduct meteorological observations and report meteorological observation information in accordance with relevant national regulations.

Article 18 If meteorological observation data other than basic meteorological observation data needs to be kept confidential, the determination, alteration, declassification, and use of its classification level shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Guarding State Secrets.

Article 19 The State protects the meteorological observation environment according to law. All organizations and individuals have the obligation to protect the meteorological observation environment.

Article 20 The following acts that endanger the meteorological observation environment are prohibited:

(1) Setting up obstacles, conducting blasting, and quarrying within the protected range of the meteorological observation environment;
(2) Installing high-frequency electromagnetic radiation devices within the protected range of the meteorological observation environment that affect the working efficiency of meteorological observation facilities;
(3) Engaging in other activities within the protected range of the meteorological observation environment that affect meteorological observations.

The standards for delimiting the protected range of the meteorological observation environment shall be stipulated by the meteorological competent authority under the State Council. People's governments at all levels shall delimit the protected range of the meteorological observation environment according to legal standards and incorporate it into urban planning or village and town planning.

Article 21 The construction of new projects, or the expansion or renovation of existing projects, shall avoid endangering the meteorological observation environment. If it is truly unavoidable, the construction unit shall first obtain consent from the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and take corresponding measures before proceeding with the construction.

## Chapter IV Meteorological Forecasts and Severe Weather Warnings

Article 22 The State implements a unified release system for public meteorological forecasts and severe weather warnings.

Meteorological stations and observatories under meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall, in accordance with their responsibilities, release public meteorological forecasts and severe weather warnings to the public, and promptly supplement or correct them based on changes in weather conditions. No other organization or individual may release public meteorological forecasts or severe weather warnings to the public.

Meteorological stations and observatories under other relevant departments of the State Council and other relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may issue specialized meteorological forecasts for use within their own systems.

Meteorological competent authorities at all levels and the meteorological stations and observatories under them shall improve the accuracy, timeliness, and service level of public meteorological forecasts and severe weather warnings.

Article 23 Meteorological stations and observatories under meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall, as needed, issue specialized meteorological forecasts such as agricultural weather forecasts, urban environmental weather forecasts, and fire weather rating forecasts, and cooperate with military meteorological departments in providing meteorological services required for national defense construction.

Article 24 Radio and television stations at all levels and newspapers designated by provincial people's governments shall allocate specific time slots or space to broadcast or publish public meteorological forecasts or severe weather warnings daily.
Meteorological stations and observatories under meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall ensure the quality of the meteorological forecast programs they produce.

If broadcasting units change the broadcast schedule of meteorological forecast programs, they shall first obtain consent from the relevant meteorological station or observatory. Severe weather warnings and supplementary or corrected meteorological forecasts that may have a significant impact on the national economy and the people's livelihood shall be promptly added or inserted.

Article 25 Media such as radio, television, newspapers, and telecommunications, when disseminating meteorological forecasts and severe weather warnings to the public, must use the timely meteorological information provided by meteorological stations and observatories under the meteorological competent authority, and indicate the release time and the name of the meteorological station or observatory. A portion of the revenue obtained from disseminating meteorological information shall be allocated to support the development of meteorology.

Article 26 The information industry department shall closely cooperate with the meteorological competent authority to ensure smooth meteorological communication and the accurate and timely transmission of meteorological information, meteorological forecasts, and severe weather warnings.

Dedicated meteorological radio frequencies and channels are protected by the State. No organization or individual may encroach upon or interfere with them.

## Chapter V Meteorological Disaster Prevention

Article 27 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of meteorological disaster monitoring and early warning systems, organize relevant departments to formulate meteorological disaster prevention plans, and take effective measures to enhance the capacity for preventing meteorological disasters. Relevant organizations and individuals shall obey the command and arrangements of the people's government and carry out meteorological disaster prevention work well.

Article 28 Meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall organize cross-regional and cross-departmental joint monitoring and forecasting of major severe weather, promptly propose meteorological disaster prevention measures, assess major meteorological disasters, and provide decision-making basis for the people's government at the corresponding level in organizing meteorological disaster prevention.

Meteorological stations and observatories under meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall strengthen the monitoring and forecasting of severe weather that may affect the local area, and promptly report to the relevant meteorological competent authority. Meteorological stations and observatories under other relevant departments and units related to severe weather monitoring and forecasting shall promptly provide the meteorological competent authority with meteorological observation information and relevant monitoring information on water conditions, storm surges, etc., needed for monitoring and forecasting meteorological disasters.

Article 29 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of meteorological disaster prevention, formulate meteorological disaster prevention plans, and organize the implementation of these plans based on meteorological information provided by the meteorological competent authority, to avoid or mitigate meteorological disasters.

Article 30 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen leadership over weather modification work, and carry out weather modification work in an organized and planned manner based on actual conditions.

The meteorological competent authority under the State Council shall strengthen the management and guidance of nationwide weather modification work. Local meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall formulate weather modification operation plans, and under the leadership and coordination of the people's government at the corresponding level, manage, guide, and organize the implementation of weather modification operations. Relevant departments shall, according to their division of responsibilities, cooperate with the meteorological competent authority in carrying out relevant weather modification work.

Organizations conducting weather modification operations must meet the conditions stipulated by the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, use operational equipment that meets the technical standards required by the meteorological competent authority under the State Council, and comply with operational specifications.

Article 31 Meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall strengthen the organization and management of lightning disaster prevention work, and, in conjunction with relevant departments, guide the testing of lightning protection devices installed on buildings, structures, and other facilities that may be struck by lightning.

Installed lightning protection devices shall meet the usage requirements stipulated by the meteorological competent authority under the State Council.

## Chapter VI Development, Utilization and Protection of Climate Resources

Article 32 The meteorological competent authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the comprehensive survey and zoning of national climate resources, organize climate monitoring, analysis, and evaluation, monitor atmospheric components that may cause climate deterioration, and regularly publish national climate status bulletins.

Article 33 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the characteristics of local climate resources, make plans for the direction of climate resource development and utilization and the focus of protection.

Local meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall, based on the plans of the people's government at the corresponding level, submit suggestions to the said people's government and relevant departments at the same level regarding the utilization and protection of climate resources and the promotion and application of achievements such as climate resource zoning.

Article 34 Meteorological competent authorities at all levels shall organize climate feasibility demonstrations for urban planning, key national construction projects, major regional economic development projects, and large-scale solar, wind energy, and other climate resource development and utilization projects.

When units qualified for atmospheric environmental impact assessment conduct atmospheric environmental impact assessments for engineering construction projects, they shall use meteorological data that comply with national meteorological technical standards.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 35 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the relevant meteorological competent authority within its authority to cease the illegal act, restore the original state within a time limit, or take other remedial measures, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if losses are caused, they shall bear compensation liability according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Occupying, damaging, or moving meteorological facilities without authorization;
(2) Engaging in activities that endanger the meteorological observation environment within the protected range of the meteorological observation environment.

If, within the protected range of the meteorological observation environment, land occupation is illegally approved, or land is illegally occupied to construct new buildings or other facilities, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Urban and Rural Planning Law of the People's Republic of China or the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 36 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by using specialized meteorological technical equipment that does not meet technical requirements, causing harm, shall be ordered by the relevant meteorological competent authority within its authority to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 37 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by installing lightning protection devices that do not meet usage requirements shall be ordered by the relevant meteorological competent authority to make corrections and given a warning. If the use of lightning protection devices that do not meet usage requirements causes losses to others, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 38 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the relevant meteorological competent authority within its authority to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) Illegally releasing public meteorological forecasts or severe weather warnings to the public;
(2) Media such as radio, television, newspapers, and telecommunications disseminating public meteorological forecasts or severe weather warnings to the public without using the timely meteorological information provided by meteorological stations or observatories under the meteorological competent authority;
(3) Units conducting atmospheric environmental impact assessments for engineering construction projects using meteorological data that do not meet national meteorological technical standards.

Article 39 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by conducting weather modification operations without meeting the conditions stipulated by the meteorological competent authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or by using operational equipment that does not meet the technical standards required by the meteorological competent authority under the State Council for weather modification operations, shall be ordered by the relevant meteorological competent authority within its authority to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 100,000 yuan; if losses are caused to others, compensation liability shall be borne according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 40 If staff members of meteorological competent authorities at all levels and the meteorological stations and observatories under them, due to dereliction of duty, cause major omissions or errors in public meteorological forecasts or severe weather warnings, or cause accidents such as loss or destruction of original meteorological observation data, or falsification of meteorological data, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if significant losses are caused to national interests and people's lives and property, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 41 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Meteorological facilities" refer to meteorological observation facilities, dedicated meteorological information transmission facilities, large-scale specialized meteorological technical equipment, etc.
(2) "Meteorological observation" refers to the systematic observation and measurement of atmospheric physical processes, phenomena, and their chemical properties in the atmosphere and the near-surface layer using scientific and technological means.
(3) "Meteorological observation environment" refers to the environmental space constituted by the minimum distance necessary to avoid various interferences and ensure that meteorological observation facilities accurately obtain meteorological observation information.
(4) "Meteorological disasters" refer to disasters caused by typhoons, heavy rain (snow), cold waves, strong winds (sandstorms), low temperatures, high temperatures, droughts, lightning, hail, frost, and dense fog.
(5) "Weather modification" refers to activities aimed at avoiding or mitigating meteorological disasters and rationally utilizing climate resources, by artificially influencing the physical and chemical processes of the local atmosphere through scientific and technological means under appropriate conditions, to achieve purposes such as increasing rain and snow, preventing hail, dissipating rain, dissipating fog, and preventing frost.

Article 42 Specific management measures for the scope, items, and charges of paid meteorological services provided by meteorological stations, observatories, and other units engaged in paid meteorological services shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Article 43 Measures for the management of meteorological work of the People's Liberation Army shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission.Article 44 If any international treaty concerning meteorological activities concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of such an international treaty shall apply, except those clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 45 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2000. The Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Meteorology promulgated by the State Council on August 18, 1994 shall be repealed simultaneously.
