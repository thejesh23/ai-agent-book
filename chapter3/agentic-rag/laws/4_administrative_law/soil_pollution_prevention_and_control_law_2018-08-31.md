# Soil Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 31, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purposes of protecting and improving the ecological environment, preventing and controlling soil pollution, safeguarding public health, promoting the sustainable use of soil resources, advancing ecological civilization, and facilitating sustainable economic and social development.

**Article 2** This Law applies to activities for the prevention and control of soil pollution and related activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction.

For the purpose of this Law, "soil pollution" means the phenomenon whereby, due to human factors, certain substances enter the surface soil of land, causing changes in the chemical, physical, or biological properties of the soil, affecting soil functions and effective utilization, endangering public health, or damaging the ecological environment.

**Article 3** The prevention and control of soil pollution shall adhere to the principles of giving priority to prevention, prioritizing protection, classified management, risk control, polluter responsibility, and public participation.

**Article 4** All organizations and individuals have the obligation to protect the soil and prevent soil pollution.

Land use right holders engaging in land development and utilization activities, and enterprises, institutions, and other producers and business operators engaging in production and business activities, shall take effective measures to prevent and reduce soil pollution, and shall bear responsibility for the soil pollution they cause in accordance with the law.

**Article 5** Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for the prevention, control, and safe utilization of soil pollution within their respective administrative regions.

The State implements a target responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for soil pollution prevention and control. The achievement of soil pollution prevention and control targets shall be included as part of the assessment and evaluation of local people's governments at all levels and their responsible persons, as well as the departments and their responsible persons of people's governments at or above the county level that are responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control.

**Article 6** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over soil pollution prevention and control work, and organize, coordinate, and supervise relevant departments to perform their duties of supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control in accordance with the law.

**Article 7** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the national soil pollution prevention and control work; the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments under the State Council shall exercise supervision and management over soil pollution prevention and control work within their respective responsibilities.

The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government shall exercise unified supervision and management over soil pollution prevention and control work within its administrative region; the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments of a local people's government shall exercise supervision and management over soil pollution prevention and control work within their respective responsibilities.

**Article 8** The State establishes a soil environmental information sharing mechanism.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, water resources, health, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments under the State Council, establish a basic database of soil environmental information, build a national soil environmental information platform, and implement dynamic data updates and information sharing.

**Article 9** The State supports the research, development, transformation of achievements, and promotion and application of scientific and technological achievements in soil pollution prevention and control, such as risk control, remediation, and monitoring; encourages the development of the soil pollution prevention and control industry; strengthens the cultivation of professional and technical personnel in soil pollution prevention and control; and promotes the progress of science and technology in soil pollution prevention and control.

The State supports international exchanges and cooperation in soil pollution prevention and control.

**Article 10** People's governments at all levels, their relevant departments, grassroots self-governing mass organizations, and news media shall strengthen publicity, education, and scientific popularization on soil pollution prevention and control, enhance public awareness of soil pollution prevention and control, and guide the public to participate in soil pollution prevention and control work in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Planning, Standards, Surveys, and Monitoring

**Article 11** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate soil pollution prevention and control work into their national economic and social development plans and environmental protection plans.

The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall, together with the competent departments of development and reform, agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments, formulate a soil pollution prevention and control plan based on the requirements of the environmental protection plan, land use, and the results of general surveys and monitoring of soil pollution conditions, and submit it to the people's government at the same level for approval before promulgation and implementation.

**Article 12** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, based on the soil pollution conditions, public health risks, ecological risks, and the level of science and technology, and in accordance with land use, formulate national soil pollution risk control standards and strengthen the construction of the soil pollution prevention and control standard system.

Where items are not specified in the national soil pollution risk control standards, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate local soil pollution risk control standards; for items already specified in the national soil pollution risk control standards, they may formulate local soil pollution risk control standards that are stricter than the national standards. Local soil pollution risk control standards shall be reported to the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council for recordation.

Soil pollution risk control standards are mandatory standards.

The State supports research on soil environmental background values and environmental benchmarks.

**Article 13** When formulating soil pollution risk control standards, experts shall be organized for review and demonstration, and opinions shall be solicited from relevant departments, industry associations, enterprises, institutions, and the public.

The implementation of soil pollution risk control standards shall be regularly evaluated, and the standards shall be revised in a timely manner based on the evaluation results.

The competent department of ecological environment of a people's government at or above the provincial level shall publish soil pollution risk control standards on its website for the public to consult and download free of charge.

**Article 14** The State Council shall provide unified leadership for the national general survey of soil pollution conditions. The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments under the State Council, organize a national general survey of soil pollution conditions at least once every ten years.

Relevant departments under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the city divided into districts may organize detailed surveys of soil pollution conditions based on the actual conditions of their respective industries or administrative regions.

**Article 15** The State implements a soil environmental monitoring system.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall formulate specifications for soil environmental monitoring, and together with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, water resources, health, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments under the State Council, organize a monitoring network and uniformly plan the establishment of national soil environmental monitoring stations (points).

**Article 16** The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and forestry and grassland of local people's governments shall, together with the competent departments of ecological environment and natural resources, conduct key monitoring of the following agricultural land plots:

(1) Those where the pollutant content in the agricultural products produced exceeds the standards;
(2) Those that are or have been used as sewage irrigation areas;
(3) Those that are or have been used for large-scale breeding, solid waste stacking, or landfill;
(4) Those that have been used as industrial or mining land or have experienced major or extraordinarily large pollution accidents;
(5) Those surrounding facilities for the production, storage, use, or disposal of toxic and hazardous substances;
(6) Other circumstances specified by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland, ecological environment, and natural resources under the State Council.

**Article 17** The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government shall, together with the competent department of natural resources, conduct key monitoring of the following construction land plots:

(1) Those that have been used for the production, use, storage, recovery, or disposal of toxic and hazardous substances;
(2) Those that have been used for solid waste stacking or landfill;
(3) Those that have experienced major or extraordinarily large pollution accidents;
(4) Other circumstances specified by the competent departments of ecological environment and natural resources under the State Council.

## Chapter III Prevention and Protection

**Article 18** All plans involving land use and construction projects that may cause soil pollution shall undergo environmental impact assessment in accordance with the law. The environmental impact assessment document shall include the potential adverse effects on the soil and the corresponding preventive measures to be taken.

**Article 19** Units and individuals that produce, use, store, transport, recover, dispose of, or discharge toxic and hazardous substances shall take effective measures to prevent the leakage, loss, or scattering of such substances, thereby avoiding soil pollution.

**Article 20** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of health and other relevant departments under the State Council, screen and assess toxic and hazardous substances in the soil based on the degree of harm and impact on public health and the ecological environment, publish a list of key controlled toxic and hazardous substances in the soil, and update it in a timely manner.

**Article 21** The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council and based on the discharge of toxic and hazardous substances and other conditions, formulate a list of key units for soil pollution supervision within its administrative region, make it public, and update it in a timely manner.

Key units for soil pollution supervision shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Strictly control the discharge of toxic and hazardous substances, and report the discharge situation to the competent department of ecological environment on an annual basis;
(2) Establish a system for investigating hidden dangers of soil pollution to ensure the continuous and effective prevention of leakage, loss, or scattering of toxic and hazardous substances;
(3) Formulate and implement a self-monitoring plan, and report the monitoring data to the competent department of ecological environment.

The obligations specified in the preceding paragraph shall be stated in the pollutant discharge permit.

Key units for soil pollution supervision shall be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the monitoring data. Where the competent department of ecological environment finds abnormal monitoring data from a key unit for soil pollution supervision, it shall promptly conduct an investigation.

The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall regularly monitor the soil surrounding key units for soil pollution supervision.

**Article 22** Where an enterprise or institution dismantles facilities, equipment, or buildings or structures, it shall take corresponding soil pollution prevention and control measures.

Where a key unit for soil pollution supervision dismantles facilities, equipment, or buildings or structures, it shall formulate a soil pollution prevention and control work plan including emergency measures, and report it to the competent departments of ecological environment and industry and information technology of the local people's government for recordation and implementation.

**Article 23** The competent departments of ecological environment and natural resources of people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control in mineral resource development areas, and strictly control the discharge of key pollutants that may cause soil pollution in accordance with relevant standards and total quantity control requirements.

The operation and management units of tailings ponds shall, in accordance with regulations, strengthen the safety management of tailings ponds and take measures to prevent soil pollution. The operation and management units of dangerous ponds, risky ponds, defective ponds, and other tailings ponds requiring key supervision shall, in accordance with regulations, conduct soil pollution monitoring and regular assessment.

**Article 24** The State encourages the adoption of new technologies and new materials in construction projects related to information, networks, lightning protection, grounding, etc., in the fields of construction, communications, electric power, transportation, water resources, etc., to prevent soil pollution.

The use of resistance-reducing products with heavy metal content exceeding the standards in the soil is prohibited.

**Article 25** When constructing and operating centralized sewage treatment facilities and solid waste disposal facilities, measures shall be taken to prevent soil pollution in accordance with the requirements of laws, regulations, and relevant standards.

The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government shall regularly monitor the soil surrounding centralized sewage treatment facilities and solid waste disposal facilities; where the facilities do not comply with laws, regulations, and relevant standards, it shall, based on the monitoring results, require the operating units of the centralized sewage treatment facilities and solid waste disposal facilities to take corresponding improvement measures.

Local people's governments at all levels shall make overall plans, construct urban and rural domestic sewage and domestic waste treatment and disposal facilities, and ensure their normal operation to prevent soil pollution.

**Article 26** The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall formulate plans, improve relevant standards and measures, strengthen guidance on the use of pesticides and chemical fertilizers in agricultural land and control their total usage, and strengthen control over the use of agricultural films.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall strengthen the registration of pesticides and fertilizers, and organize safety assessments of the impact of pesticides and fertilizers on the soil environment.

The formulation of standards for agricultural inputs such as pesticides, veterinary drugs, fertilizers, feed, agricultural films, and their packaging, as well as standards for the quality of water used for farmland irrigation, shall meet the requirements of soil pollution prevention and control.

**Article 27** The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and forestry and grassland of local people's governments shall carry out publicity and technical training activities on soil pollution prevention and control in agricultural land, support specialized agricultural production services, guide agricultural producers to rationally use agricultural inputs such as pesticides, veterinary drugs, fertilizers, feed, and agricultural films, and control the usage of pesticides, veterinary drugs, chemical fertilizers, etc.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of a local people's government shall encourage agricultural producers to adopt agricultural cultivation measures such as crop-livestock integration, crop rotation, and fallowing that are conducive to preventing soil pollution; support measures such as soil improvement and soil fertility enhancement that are conducive to soil conservation and cultivation; and support the construction of facilities for the treatment and utilization of livestock and poultry manure.

**Article 28** It is prohibited to discharge sewage, sludge, or dredged sediment, tailings, slag, etc., containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding the standards, or that may cause soil pollution, onto agricultural land.

Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and management of the collection, storage, utilization, and disposal of livestock and poultry manure, biogas residue, biogas slurry, etc., to prevent soil pollution.

Water used for farmland irrigation shall meet the corresponding quality standards to prevent pollution of the soil, groundwater, and agricultural products. The competent department of ecological environment of a local people's government shall, together with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and water resources, strengthen the management of the quality of water used for farmland irrigation, and monitor and supervise the quality of such water.

**Article 29** The State encourages and supports agricultural producers to adopt the following measures:

(1) Using low-toxicity, low-residue pesticides and advanced spraying technologies;
(2) Using organic fertilizers and high-efficiency fertilizers that meet the standards;
(3) Adopting soil testing and formulated fertilization technologies, and green prevention and control technologies for pests and diseases such as biological control;
(4) Using biodegradable agricultural films;
(5) Comprehensively utilizing crop straw and removing crop straw with high concentrations of pollutants;
(6) Improving acidic soils, etc., in accordance with regulations.

**Article 30** It is prohibited to produce, sell, or use agricultural inputs explicitly prohibited by the State.

Producers, sellers, and users of agricultural inputs shall promptly recover the packaging waste of agricultural inputs such as pesticides and fertilizers, as well as agricultural films, and deliver the pesticide packaging waste to specialized institutions or organizations for harmless treatment. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council together with the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council.

The State takes measures to encourage and support units and individuals to recover packaging waste of agricultural inputs and agricultural films.

**Article 31** The State strengthens the protection of unpolluted soil.

Local people's governments at all levels shall focus on protecting unpolluted arable land, forestland, grassland, and drinking water sources.

People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the protection of natural protected areas such as national parks and maintain their ecological functions.

Unused land shall be protected and shall not be polluted or damaged.

**Article 32** Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, in accordance with the overall land use plan and urban-rural plan, strictly implement the requirements for the layout and site selection of relevant industries and enterprises, and prohibit the construction, renovation, or expansion of construction projects that may cause soil pollution near residential areas, schools, hospitals, sanatoriums, nursing homes, and other similar units.

**Article 33** The State strengthens the protection and rational utilization of soil resources. Topsoil stripped during development and construction shall be collected and stored separately, and where conditions permit, it shall be given priority for use in land reclamation, soil improvement, land creation, and greening.

It is prohibited to use industrial solid waste, domestic waste, or contaminated soil containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding the standards for land reclamation.

**Article 34** Where soil needs to be imported for special reasons such as scientific research, the relevant provisions of the State on entry-exit inspection and quarantine shall be complied with.

## Chapter IV Risk Control and Remediation

### Section 1 General Provisions

**Article 35** Soil pollution risk control and remediation includes activities such as soil pollution condition investigation, soil pollution risk assessment, risk control, remediation, risk control effectiveness assessment, remediation effectiveness assessment, and post-management.

**Article 36** When conducting a soil pollution condition investigation, a soil pollution condition investigation report shall be prepared.

The soil pollution condition investigation report shall mainly include basic information about the plot and whether the pollutant content exceeds the soil pollution risk control standards. Where the pollutant content exceeds the soil pollution risk control standards, the soil pollution condition investigation report shall also include the type of pollution, the source of pollution, and whether the groundwater is contaminated.

**Article 37** When conducting a soil pollution risk assessment, a soil pollution risk assessment report shall be prepared.

The soil pollution risk assessment report shall mainly include the following content:

(1) The status of major pollutants;
(2) The scope of soil and groundwater pollution;
(3) Risks to the quality and safety of agricultural products, public health risks, or ecological risks;
(4) The objectives and basic requirements for risk control and remediation.

**Article 38** Risk control and remediation activities shall be tailored to local conditions, scientifically sound, and targeted and effective.

Risk control and remediation activities shall not cause new pollution to the soil or the surrounding environment.

**Article 39** Before conducting risk control and remediation activities, the relevant departments of the local people's government have the right, based on the actual situation, to require the soil pollution liability holder or the land use right holder to take measures such as removing the pollution source or preventing the spread of pollution.

**Article 40** Wastewater, waste gas, and solid waste generated during risk control and remediation activities shall be treated and disposed of in accordance with regulations and shall meet relevant environmental protection standards.

Where solid waste generated during risk control and remediation activities, or dismantled facilities, equipment, or buildings or structures, constitute hazardous waste, they shall be disposed of in accordance with the requirements of laws, regulations, and relevant standards.

During the remediation construction period, a bulletin board shall be set up to disclose relevant information and environmental protection measures.

**Article 41** Where a remediation construction unit transports contaminated soil, it shall formulate a transport plan and report the transport time, method, route, quantity of contaminated soil, destination, and final disposal measures to the competent departments of ecological environment of the location and the receiving location in advance.

Where the transported contaminated soil constitutes hazardous waste, the remediation construction unit shall dispose of it in accordance with the requirements of laws, regulations, and relevant standards.

**Article 42** When conducting risk control effectiveness assessment and remediation effectiveness assessment, an effectiveness assessment report shall be prepared.

The effectiveness assessment report shall mainly include whether the risk control and remediation objectives determined in the soil pollution risk assessment report have been achieved.

After the completion of risk control and remediation activities, where post-management is required, the soil pollution liability holder shall implement post-management in accordance with the requirements.

**Article 43** Units engaged in activities such as soil pollution condition investigation, soil pollution risk assessment, risk control, remediation, risk control effectiveness assessment, remediation effectiveness assessment, and post-management shall possess corresponding professional capabilities.Entities commissioned to carry out the activities mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be responsible for the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the investigation reports, risk assessment reports, risk control effectiveness assessment reports, and remediation effectiveness assessment reports they produce, and shall be responsible for the outcomes of risk control, remediation, and post-management activities as agreed.

**Article 44** Where an emergency incident may cause soil contamination, local people's governments and their relevant departments, as well as relevant enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators, shall immediately take emergency measures to prevent soil contamination and shall, in accordance with this Law, carry out soil contamination monitoring, investigation, soil contamination risk assessment, risk control, remediation, and other tasks.

**Article 45** The soil contamination liability party has the obligation to implement soil contamination risk control and remediation. If the soil contamination liability party cannot be identified, the land user shall implement soil contamination risk control and remediation.

Local people's governments and their relevant departments may, based on actual circumstances, organize the implementation of soil contamination risk control and remediation.

The State encourages and supports relevant parties to voluntarily implement soil contamination risk control and remediation.

**Article 46** Expenses incurred from implementing or organizing the implementation of soil contamination investigation, soil contamination risk assessment, risk control, remediation, risk control effectiveness assessment, remediation effectiveness assessment, post-management, and other activities shall be borne by the soil contamination liability party.

**Article 47** If the soil contamination liability party changes, the unit or individual that succeeds its claims and debts after the change shall fulfill the relevant soil contamination risk control and remediation obligations and bear the relevant costs.

**Article 48** If the soil contamination liability party is unclear or disputed, for agricultural land, the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland shall, together with the departments of ecology and environment and natural resources, determine the liability party; for construction land, the local people's government's department of ecology and environment shall, together with the department of natural resources, determine the liability party. The determination method shall be formulated by the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

### Section 2 Agricultural Land

**Article 49** The State establishes a classification management system for agricultural land. According to the degree of soil contamination and relevant standards, agricultural land is divided into priority protection class, safe utilization class, and strict control class.

**Article 50** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, designate priority protection class farmland that meets the conditions as permanent basic farmland and implement strict protection.

In areas where permanent basic farmland is concentrated, no construction projects that may cause soil contamination shall be newly built; those already built shall be closed and dismantled within a specified period.

**Article 51** For unused land, reclaimed land, etc., intended to be reclaimed as farmland, the local people's government's department of agriculture and rural affairs shall, together with the departments of ecology and environment and natural resources, conduct a soil contamination investigation and carry out classification management in accordance with the law.

**Article 52** For agricultural land plots where soil contamination risk is indicated by general surveys, detailed surveys, monitoring, or on-site inspections, the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland shall, together with the departments of ecology and environment and natural resources, conduct a soil contamination investigation.

For agricultural land plots where the soil contamination investigation shows that pollutant levels exceed the soil contamination risk control standards, the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland shall, together with the departments of ecology and environment and natural resources, organize a soil contamination risk assessment and manage them according to the agricultural land classification management system.

**Article 53** For safe utilization class agricultural land plots, the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland shall, taking into account the main crop varieties and planting practices, formulate and implement a safe utilization plan.

The safe utilization plan shall include the following:

(1) Agronomic regulation, alternative planting;
(2) Regular collaborative monitoring and evaluation of soil and agricultural products;
(3) Technical guidance and training for farmers, farmer specialized cooperatives, and other agricultural production and operation entities;
(4) Other risk control measures.

**Article 54** For strict control class agricultural land plots, the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland shall take the following risk control measures:

(1) Propose designating areas where the production of specific agricultural products is prohibited, and implement after reporting to the people's government at the same level for approval;
(2) Conduct collaborative monitoring and evaluation of soil and agricultural products in accordance with regulations;
(3) Provide technical guidance and training for farmers, farmer specialized cooperatives, and other agricultural production and operation entities;
(4) Other risk control measures.

People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall encourage the adoption of risk control measures for strict control class agricultural land, such as adjusting planting structures, returning farmland to forest or grassland, returning farmland to wetlands, crop rotation and fallowing, and rotational grazing and rest, and provide corresponding policy support.

**Article 55** If soil contamination from safe utilization class or strict control class agricultural land plots affects or may affect the safety of groundwater or drinking water sources, the local people's government's department of ecology and environment shall, together with the departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments, formulate a pollution prevention plan and take corresponding measures.

**Article 56** For safe utilization class and strict control class agricultural land plots, the soil contamination liability party shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations and the requirements of the soil contamination risk assessment report, take corresponding risk control measures and regularly report to the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland.

**Article 57** For agricultural land plots where the pollutant content in produced agricultural products exceeds standards and remediation is required, the soil contamination liability party shall prepare a remediation plan, report it to the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland for filing, and implement it. The remediation plan shall include content on groundwater pollution prevention.

Remediation activities shall prioritize the adoption of bioremediation measures that do not affect agricultural production or reduce soil production functions, to block or reduce the entry of pollutants into the edible parts of crops and ensure the quality and safety of agricultural products.

After the completion of risk control and remediation activities, the soil contamination liability party shall separately entrust a relevant unit to assess the effectiveness of risk control and remediation, and submit the effectiveness assessment report to the local people's government's departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland for filing.

Rural collective economic organizations and their members, farmer specialized cooperatives, and other agricultural production and operation entities have the obligation to assist in implementing soil contamination risk control and remediation.

### Section 3 Construction Land

**Article 58** The State implements a list system for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land.

The list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land shall be formulated by the competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government together with the department of natural resources and other relevant departments, made public to society in accordance with regulations, and updated in a timely manner based on the progress of risk control and remediation.

**Article 59** For construction land plots where soil contamination risk is indicated by general surveys, detailed surveys, monitoring, or on-site inspections, the local people's government's department of ecology and environment shall require the land user to conduct a soil contamination investigation in accordance with regulations.

If the use of land is changed to residential, public administration, or public service use, a soil contamination investigation shall be conducted in accordance with regulations before the change.

The soil contamination investigation reports specified in the preceding two paragraphs shall be submitted to the local people's government's department of ecology and environment, which shall organize a review together with the department of natural resources.

**Article 60** For construction land plots where the review of the soil contamination investigation report shows that pollutant levels exceed the soil contamination risk control standards, the soil contamination liability party and the land user shall conduct a soil contamination risk assessment in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council, and submit the soil contamination risk assessment report to the competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government.

**Article 61** The competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government shall, together with the department of natural resources and other relevant departments, organize a review of the soil contamination risk assessment report in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council, promptly include plots requiring risk control and remediation into the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land, and regularly report to the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council.

Plots included in the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land shall not be used for residential, public administration, or public service purposes.

**Article 62** For plots on the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land, the soil contamination liability party shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations and the requirements of the soil contamination risk assessment report, take corresponding risk control measures and regularly report to the local people's government's department of ecology and environment. Risk control measures shall include content on groundwater pollution prevention.

**Article 63** For plots on the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land, the local people's government's department of ecology and environment may, based on actual circumstances, take the following risk control measures:

(1) Propose designating isolation areas, and implement after reporting to the people's government at the same level for approval;
(2) Monitor soil and groundwater contamination conditions;
(3) Other risk control measures.

**Article 64** For plots on the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land that require remediation, the soil contamination liability party shall, in conjunction with the overall land use plan and urban-rural plan, prepare a remediation plan, report it to the local people's government's department of ecology and environment for filing, and implement it. The remediation plan shall include content on groundwater pollution prevention.

**Article 65** After the completion of risk control and remediation activities, the soil contamination liability party shall separately entrust a relevant unit to assess the effectiveness of risk control and remediation, and submit the effectiveness assessment report to the local people's government's department of ecology and environment for filing.

**Article 66** For construction land plots that have achieved the risk control and remediation targets determined in the soil contamination risk assessment report, the soil contamination liability party or the land user may apply to the competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government to remove the plot from the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land.

The competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government shall, together with the department of natural resources and other relevant departments, organize a review of the risk control effectiveness assessment report and the remediation effectiveness assessment report, promptly remove plots that have achieved the risk control and remediation targets determined in the soil contamination risk assessment report and can be safely used from the list for soil contamination risk control and remediation of construction land, make this public in accordance with regulations, and regularly report to the competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council.

For construction land plots that have not achieved the risk control and remediation targets determined in the soil contamination risk assessment report, construction of any projects unrelated to risk control and remediation is prohibited.

**Article 67** Before the use of land for production and operation by key soil contamination monitoring entities is changed, or before the land use right is recovered or transferred, the land user shall conduct a soil contamination investigation in accordance with regulations. The soil contamination investigation report shall be submitted as real estate registration information to the local people's government's real estate registration authority and reported to the local people's government's department of ecology and environment for filing.

**Article 68** If the land use right has been recovered by the local people's government and the soil contamination liability party is the original land user, the local people's government shall organize the implementation of soil contamination risk control and remediation.

## Chapter V Guarantees and Supervision

**Article 69** The State adopts economic policies and measures, including fiscal, tax, price, and financial policies, that are conducive to soil pollution prevention and control.

**Article 70** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the prevention and control of soil pollution and allocate necessary funds for the following matters:

(1) Scientific and technological research and development, demonstration projects, and programs for soil pollution prevention and control;
(2) Activities such as general surveys, monitoring, and investigations of soil pollution, and the determination of soil contamination liability parties, risk assessments, risk control, and remediation organized and implemented by people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments;
(3) Emergency response by people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments to emergencies involving soil pollution;
(4) Other matters related to soil pollution prevention and control as stipulated by people's governments at all levels.

The use of funds shall strengthen performance management and audit supervision to ensure the effectiveness of fund utilization.

**Article 71** The State increases investment in soil pollution prevention and control funds and establishes a soil pollution prevention and control fund system. A central special fund for soil pollution prevention and control and provincial soil pollution prevention and control funds shall be established, mainly used for soil pollution prevention and control of agricultural land, risk control and remediation of soil contamination where the liability party or land user cannot be identified, and other matters stipulated by the government.

For contaminated plots that were generated before the implementation of this Law and for which the soil contamination liability party cannot be identified, if the land user actually undertakes soil contamination risk control and remediation, it may apply for the soil pollution prevention and control fund, which shall be used centrally for soil contamination risk control and remediation.

Specific management measures for the soil pollution prevention and control fund shall be formulated by the competent financial department of the State Council together with the competent departments of ecology and environment, agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, forestry and grassland, and other relevant departments under the State Council.

**Article 72** The State encourages financial institutions to increase credit supply for soil contamination risk control and remediation projects.
The State encourages financial institutions to conduct soil contamination investigations when handling land right mortgage business.

**Article 73** Units engaged in soil contamination risk control and remediation shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, enjoy tax benefits.

**Article 74** The State encourages and advocates for all sectors of society to donate property for the prevention and control of soil pollution and, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, grants tax benefits.

**Article 75** People's governments at or above the county level shall include the situation of soil pollution prevention and control in the annual report on environmental conditions and the completion of environmental protection targets, and report to the people's congress at the same level or its standing committee.

**Article 76** The competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, together with relevant departments, hold talks with the principal responsible persons of local people's governments at or above the city divided into districts and their relevant departments in areas where soil pollution problems are prominent, prevention and control efforts are weak, and public complaints are strong, requiring them to take measures for timely rectification. The situation of the talks and rectification shall be made public to society.

**Article 77** The competent department of ecology and environment and its environmental enforcement agencies, as well as other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control, have the right to conduct on-site inspections and sampling of enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators engaged in activities that may cause soil pollution, and require the inspected parties to provide relevant materials and explanations on relevant issues.

The inspected parties shall cooperate with the inspection work, truthfully report the situation, and provide necessary materials.

Departments and agencies conducting on-site inspections and their staff shall keep business secrets for the inspected parties.

**Article 78** If an enterprise, institution, or other producer or operator violates laws and regulations by discharging toxic and harmful substances, causing or potentially causing serious soil pollution, or if relevant evidence may be destroyed or concealed, the competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control may seal up or seize relevant facilities, equipment, and items.

**Article 79** The local people's government's department of work safety supervision and management shall supervise the operation and management units of tailings ponds in fulfilling their statutory obligations to prevent soil pollution, preventing accidents that may pollute the soil; the local people's government's department of ecology and environment shall strengthen supervision, inspection, and regular assessment of soil pollution prevention and control in tailings ponds, and upon discovering risks and hazards, promptly urge the operation and management units of tailings ponds to take corresponding measures.

Local people's governments and their relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen supervision and inspection of acts such as illegally discharging toxic and harmful substances onto unused land such as deserts, beaches, saline-alkali land, and swamps.

**Article 80** The competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government at or above the provincial level and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control shall include the practice status of units and individuals engaged in activities such as soil contamination investigation, soil contamination risk assessment, risk control, remediation, risk control effectiveness assessment, remediation effectiveness assessment, and post-management into the credit system, establish credit records, record illegal information in the social credit file, and include it in the national credit information sharing platform and the national enterprise credit information publicity system for public disclosure.

**Article 81** The competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control shall, in accordance with the law, publicly disclose information on soil pollution conditions and prevention and control.

The competent department of ecology and environment under the State Council is responsible for uniformly releasing national soil environmental information; the competent department of ecology and environment of the provincial people's government is responsible for uniformly releasing soil environmental information within its administrative region. The competent department of ecology and environment shall promptly notify the relevant departments of agriculture and rural affairs, health, and food safety at the same level of major soil environmental information involving the production areas of major edible agricultural products.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations have the right to obtain information on soil pollution conditions and prevention and control, and to participate in and supervise soil pollution prevention and control in accordance with the law.

**Article 82** General survey reports, monitoring data, investigation reports, soil contamination risk assessment reports, risk control effectiveness assessment reports, and remediation effectiveness assessment reports on soil pollution shall be promptly uploaded to the national soil environmental information platform.

**Article 83** News media have the right to exercise public opinion supervision over acts that violate laws and regulations on soil pollution prevention and control, and the supervised units and individuals shall not retaliate.

**Article 84** Any organization or individual has the right to report or tip off acts of soil pollution to the competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control.

The competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control shall publicly disclose the methods for reporting soil pollution to facilitate public reporting.

The department receiving the report shall handle it promptly and keep the reporter's relevant information confidential; rewards shall be given for reports made with real names that are verified.

If a reporter reports on their own unit, the unit shall not retaliate against the reporter by terminating or changing the labor contract or other means.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 85** If local people's governments at all levels, the competent department of ecology and environment, or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control fail to perform their duties in accordance with this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If an administrative penalty decision should be made in accordance with this Law but is not made, the superior competent department may directly make the administrative penalty decision.

**Article 86** In violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered to correct by the local people's government's department of ecology and environment or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of soil pollution prevention and control, and a fine shall be imposed; if the correction is refused, production shall be ordered to be suspended for rectification:

(1) A key soil contamination monitoring entity fails to formulate or implement a self-monitoring plan, or fails to report monitoring data to the competent department of ecology and environment;
(2) A key soil contamination monitoring entity falsifies or forges monitoring data;
(3) A key soil contamination monitoring entity fails to report the discharge of toxic and harmful substances on an annual basis, or fails to establish a soil contamination hazard investigation system;(4) Dismantling facilities, equipment, or buildings/structures, where enterprises and institutions fail to take corresponding soil pollution prevention measures, or where key soil pollution monitoring entities fail to formulate or implement soil pollution prevention work plans;

(5) Tailings pond operation or management entities fail to take measures to prevent soil pollution in accordance with regulations;

(6) Tailings pond operation or management entities fail to conduct soil pollution status monitoring in accordance with regulations;

(7) Constructing and operating centralized wastewater treatment facilities or solid waste disposal facilities without taking measures to prevent soil pollution in accordance with laws, regulations, and relevant standards.

For any act specified in the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; for any act specified in items (2), (4), (5), or (7) of the preceding paragraph that causes serious consequences, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 87: In violation of this Law, where sewage or sludge containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding standards, or dredged sediment, tailings, slag, etc., that may cause soil pollution, is discharged into agricultural land, the local people's government's ecological environment authority shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the case may be transferred to the public security authority, with the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel subject to detention of not less than 5 days but not more than 15 days; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Article 88: In violation of this Law, where producers, sellers, or users of agricultural inputs fail to timely recycle packaging waste of fertilizers or agricultural mulch film in accordance with regulations, or fail to timely recycle pesticide packaging waste and hand it over to specialized institutions or organizations for harmless treatment, the local people's government's agriculture and rural affairs authority shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the user of agricultural inputs is an individual, a fine of not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 89: In violation of this Law, where industrial solid waste, household waste, or contaminated soil containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding standards is used for land reclamation, the local people's government's ecological environment authority shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Article 90: In violation of this Law, where an entity commissioned to conduct soil pollution status investigations, soil pollution risk assessments, risk control effectiveness assessments, or remediation effectiveness assessments issues a false investigation report, risk assessment report, risk control effectiveness assessment report, or remediation effectiveness assessment report, the local people's government's ecological environment authority shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the entity shall be prohibited from engaging in the aforementioned business activities and shall be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Where an entity specified in the preceding paragraph issues a false report, the local people's government's ecological environment authority shall impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be prohibited from engaging in the business activities specified in the preceding paragraph for ten years; if a crime is constituted, they shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in the business activities specified in the preceding paragraph.

If the entity specified in the first paragraph of this Article colludes maliciously with the client to issue a false report, causing personal injury or property damage to others, it shall also bear joint liability with the client.

Article 91: In violation of this Law, any of the following acts shall be subject to the local people's government's ecological environment authority ordering correction and imposing a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; any illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to separately collect and store topsoil stripped during development and construction;

(2) Implementing risk control or remediation activities that cause new pollution to the soil or surrounding environment;

(3) Transporting contaminated soil without reporting in advance the transport time, method, route, quantity of contaminated soil, destination, and final disposal measures to the ecological environment authorities of the departure and receiving locations;

(4) Commencing construction of projects unrelated to risk control or remediation on construction land plots that have not yet met the risk control or remediation targets specified in the soil pollution risk assessment report.

Article 92: In violation of this Law, where a soil pollution liable party or land use right holder fails to carry out post-remediation management in accordance with regulations, the local people's government's ecological environment authority or other department responsible for soil pollution prevention supervision and management shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 93: In violation of this Law, where a person being inspected refuses to cooperate with the inspection or practices fraud during the inspection, the local people's government's ecological environment authority or other department responsible for soil pollution prevention supervision and management shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

Article 94: In violation of this Law, where a soil pollution liable party or land use right holder commits any of the following acts, the local people's government's ecological environment authority or other department responsible for soil pollution prevention supervision and management shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the party refuses to correct, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the authority may commission others to perform the required actions, with the costs borne by the soil pollution liable party or land use right holder; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to conduct a soil pollution status investigation in accordance with regulations;

(2) Failing to conduct a soil pollution risk assessment in accordance with regulations;

(3) Failing to take risk control measures in accordance with regulations;

(4) Failing to implement remediation in accordance with regulations;

(5) After completing risk control or remediation activities, failing to separately commission a relevant entity to assess the effectiveness of risk control or remediation.

If the soil pollution liable party or land use right holder commits any act specified in items (3) or (4) of the preceding paragraph, and the circumstances are serious, the local people's government's ecological environment authority or other department responsible for soil pollution prevention supervision and management may transfer the case to the public security authority, with the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel subject to detention of not less than 5 days but not more than 15 days.

Article 95: In violation of this Law, any of the following acts shall be subject to the relevant local people's government department ordering correction; if the party refuses to correct, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) A key soil pollution monitoring entity fails to submit its soil pollution prevention work plan to the local people's government's ecological environment and industry/information technology authorities for record in accordance with regulations;

(2) A soil pollution liable party or land use right holder fails to submit the remediation plan or effectiveness assessment report to the local people's government's ecological environment, agriculture and rural affairs, or forestry and grassland authorities for record in accordance with regulations;

(3) A land use right holder fails to submit the soil pollution status investigation report to the local people's government's ecological environment authority for record in accordance with regulations.

Article 96: Where soil pollution causes personal injury or property damage to others, tort liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

If the soil pollution liable party cannot be identified, and the land use right holder fails to fulfill its obligations for soil pollution risk control and remediation as stipulated in this Law, causing personal injury or property damage to others, tort liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

For civil disputes arising from soil pollution, the parties may apply to the local people's government's ecological environment or other relevant authorities for mediation, or may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 97: Where soil pollution harms national interests or the public interest, relevant authorities and organizations may file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the provisions of the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, and other laws.

Article 98: In violation of this Law, where an act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 99: This Law shall take effect on January 1, 2019.
