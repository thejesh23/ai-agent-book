# National Defense Mobilization Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on February 26, 2010

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen national defense, improve the national defense mobilization system, ensure the smooth progress of national defense mobilization, and safeguard the sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, and security of the State.

Article 2 The preparation and implementation of national defense mobilization, as well as related activities, shall be governed by this Law.

Article 3 The State shall strengthen national defense mobilization, establish and improve a national defense mobilization system that meets the needs of national defense security, coordinates with economic and social development, and links with emergency response mechanisms, thereby enhancing national defense mobilization capabilities.

Article 4 National defense mobilization shall adhere to the principles of combining peacetime with wartime, integrating military with civilian sectors, and embedding military potential within civilian capabilities, following the principles of unified leadership, universal participation, long-term preparation, prioritized construction, overall planning, and orderly efficiency.

Article 5 Citizens and organizations shall complete national defense mobilization preparations in accordance with the law during peacetime; after the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, they shall fulfill the prescribed national defense mobilization tasks.

Article 6 The State shall guarantee the funds required for national defense mobilization. National defense mobilization funds shall be included in the central and local fiscal budgets respectively, according to the principle of division of powers and responsibilities.

Article 7 The State shall commend and reward citizens and organizations that have made outstanding contributions to national defense mobilization work.

## Chapter II Organizational Leadership Institutions and Their Functions and Powers

Article 8 When the sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, or security of the State is threatened, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall, in accordance with the Constitution and relevant laws, decide on a general mobilization or a partial mobilization. The President of the State shall, based on the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, issue a mobilization order.

Article 9 The State Council and the Central Military Commission shall jointly lead national defense mobilization work nationwide, formulate policies, principles, and regulations for national defense mobilization, submit proposals to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress regarding the implementation of a general or partial mobilization, and organize the implementation of national defense mobilization based on the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the mobilization order issued by the President of the State.

When the sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, or security of the State is directly threatened and immediate countermeasures must be taken, the State Council and the Central Military Commission may, as necessary for emergency response, adopt the necessary national defense mobilization measures stipulated in this Law, while simultaneously reporting to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 10 Local people's governments shall implement and execute the policies, principles, laws, and regulations concerning national defense mobilization; after the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, they shall organize the implementation of national defense mobilization within their respective administrative regions based on the national defense mobilization tasks assigned by higher authorities.

People's governments at or above the county level shall manage national defense mobilization work within their respective administrative regions within the limits of their authority as prescribed by law.

Article 11 Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments of the military shall be responsible for related national defense mobilization work within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 12 The National Defense Mobilization Committee of the State shall, under the leadership of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, be responsible for organizing, guiding, and coordinating national defense mobilization work nationwide; matters decided upon in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures shall be implemented by the relevant departments of the State Council and the Central Military Commission according to their respective duties. The defense mobilization committees of military regions and local defense mobilization committees at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing, guiding, and coordinating national defense mobilization work within their respective regions.

Article 13 The working offices of defense mobilization committees shall handle the daily work of the respective committees and perform relevant national defense mobilization duties in accordance with the law.

Article 14 After the threat to the sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, or security of the State is eliminated, the implementation measures for national defense mobilization shall be lifted in accordance with the authority and procedures used to decide on the implementation of national defense mobilization.

## Chapter III National Defense Mobilization Plans, Implementation Plans, and Potential Statistics and Surveys

Article 15 The State shall implement a system of national defense mobilization plans, national defense mobilization implementation plans, and national defense mobilization potential statistics and surveys.

Article 16 National defense mobilization plans and national defense mobilization implementation plans shall be formulated based on the principles and policies of national defense mobilization, the status of national defense mobilization potential, and military requirements. Military requirements shall be proposed by relevant military departments in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures.

National defense mobilization implementation plans and emergency response plans shall be interconnected in terms of command, force deployment, information, and support.

Article 17 The formulation and approval of national defense mobilization plans and national defense mobilization implementation plans at various levels shall be carried out in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 18 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate relevant content of national defense mobilization into national economic and social development plans. Relevant military departments shall incorporate national defense mobilization implementation plans into combat readiness plans.

People's governments at or above the county level, their relevant departments, and relevant military departments shall implement the national defense mobilization plans and national defense mobilization implementation plans according to their respective duties.

Article 19 Statistical agencies and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of national defense mobilization, accurately and promptly provide relevant statistical data to the working offices of the defense mobilization committees at the corresponding level. When the provided statistical data is insufficient to meet the needs, the working office of the defense mobilization committee may, in accordance with the Statistics Law of the People's Republic of China and relevant State regulations, organize special statistical surveys on national defense mobilization potential.

Article 20 The State shall establish a system for evaluating and inspecting the implementation of national defense mobilization plans and national defense mobilization implementation plans.

## Chapter IV Construction Projects and Important Products Closely Related to National Defense

Article 21 Based on the needs of national defense mobilization, construction projects and important products closely related to national defense shall incorporate national defense requirements and possess national defense functions.

Article 22 The catalogue of construction projects and important products closely related to national defense shall be drafted by the comprehensive economic development department of the State Council in conjunction with other relevant departments of the State Council and relevant military departments, and submitted to the State Council and the Central Military Commission for approval.

For construction projects and important products included in the catalogue, their military requirements shall be proposed by relevant military departments; when approving or authorizing construction projects and finalizing the design of important products, the relevant competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with regulations, seek the opinions of relevant military departments.

Article 23 Construction projects and important products included in the catalogue shall be designed, produced, constructed, supervised, and accepted in accordance with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and technical specifications and standards that implement national defense requirements, ensuring the quality of the construction projects and important products.

Article 24 Enterprises and institutions that invest in or participate in the investment in the construction of projects or the research, development, and manufacture of important products included in the catalogue shall, in accordance with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and State regulations, enjoy subsidies or other preferential policies.

Article 25 People's governments at or above the county level shall provide guidance and policy support for the work of implementing national defense requirements in construction projects and important products included in the catalogue, and relevant departments shall perform relevant management work according to their duties.

## Chapter V Reserve of Reservists and Their Call-up

Article 26 The State shall implement a system for the reserve of reservists.

The State shall, based on the needs of national defense mobilization and following the principles of appropriate scale, scientific structure, and rational distribution, reserve the required reservists.

The State Council and the Central Military Commission shall, based on the needs of national defense mobilization, determine the scale, types, and methods of reservist reserves.

Article 27 Reservists shall be reserved by means of pre-assignment to active-duty units, incorporation into reserve units, incorporation into militia organizations, or other forms, following the principles of matching specialties and facilitating mobilization.

The State shall, based on the needs of national defense mobilization, establish reserve areas for reservists with specialized technical skills.

The State shall provide conditions and guarantees for the training and reserve of reservists. Reservists shall participate in training in accordance with the law.

Article 28 The military service agencies of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing and implementing the reserve of reservists within their respective administrative regions. Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, the township (town) people's governments or sub-district offices where reservists are located, or their enterprises and institutions, shall assist the military service agencies in carrying out relevant work related to the reserve of reservists.

Article 29 Reservists pre-assigned to active-duty units or incorporated into reserve units, and other reservists scheduled for call-up, who leave their place of reservist registration for more than one month, shall report to the military service agency where they are registered as reservists.

Article 30 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, the military service agency of the people's government at the county level shall, based on orders from higher authorities, promptly issue call-up notices to the reservists being called up.

Reservists who receive a call-up notice shall report to the designated location as required by the notice.

Article 31 The units where called-up reservists work shall assist the military service agencies in the call-up of reservists.

Units and individuals engaged in transportation shall give priority to transporting called-up reservists.

Article 32 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, reservists scheduled for call-up shall not leave their place of reservist registration without the approval of the military service agency of the people's government at the county level where they are registered; those who have already left their place of registration shall immediately return or report to the designated location upon receiving notification from the military service agency.

## Chapter VI Strategic Material Reserves and Allocation

Article 33 The State shall implement a system for the reserve and allocation of strategic materials adapted to the needs of national defense mobilization.

The reserve of strategic materials shall be organized and implemented by the relevant competent departments of the State Council.

Article 34 Units undertaking the task of reserving strategic materials shall store and maintain the reserved materials in accordance with relevant State regulations and standards, and periodically adjust and replace them to ensure the effectiveness and safety of the reserved materials.

The State shall provide subsidies to units undertaking the task of reserving strategic materials in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 35 Strategic materials shall be allocated in accordance with relevant State regulations. After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, the allocation of strategic materials shall be approved by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 36 The reserve and allocation of other materials required for national defense mobilization shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter VII Research, Production, and Maintenance Support for Military Products

Article 37 The State shall establish a mobilization system for the research, production, and maintenance support of military products, and reserve the capacity for research, production, and maintenance support of military products based on the needs of wartime military procurement and equipment support.

For the purposes of this Law, "military products" refer to equipment, materials, and specialized production equipment and supplies used for military purposes.

Article 38 The types, distribution, and scale of the reserved capacity for research, production, and maintenance support of military products shall be proposed by the relevant competent departments of the State Council in conjunction with relevant military departments, and implemented after approval by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 39 Units undertaking the tasks of converting to or expanding the production of military products and maintenance support shall, based on the national defense mobilization tasks they undertake, reserve the necessary equipment, materials, supporting products, and technologies, establish the necessary specialized technical teams, and formulate and improve plans and measures.

Article 40 People's governments at all levels shall support and assist units undertaking the tasks of converting to or expanding the production of military products in developing and applying advanced dual-use technologies, promoting the use of common technical standards for military and civilian purposes, and enhancing the comprehensive support capability for converting to or expanding the production of military products.

The relevant competent departments of the State Council shall coordinate and provide support for the implementation of major cross-regional and cross-industry tasks of converting to or expanding the production of military products.

Article 41 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, units undertaking the tasks of converting to or expanding the production of military products shall, in accordance with State military procurement contracts and the requirements for conversion or expansion, organize the research and production of military products, ensure the quality of military products, deliver orders on time, and assist the military in completing maintenance support tasks. Units providing energy, materials, equipment, and supporting products for the conversion or expansion of military product production shall give priority to meeting the needs of such conversion or expansion.

The State shall provide compensation to units that suffer direct economic losses due to undertaking the tasks of converting to or expanding the production of military products.

## Chapter VIII Prevention and Relief of War Disasters

Article 42 The State shall implement a system for the prevention and relief of war disasters to protect the lives and property of the people and ensure the potential for national defense mobilization and sustained mobilization capability.

Article 43 The State shall establish a graded protection system for military, economic, and social targets, as well as leading organs. The standards for graded protection shall be stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

The protection work for military, economic, and social targets, as well as leading organs, shall be jointly organized and implemented by people's governments at or above the county level and relevant military organs.

Article 44 Units undertaking the protection tasks for military, economic, and social targets, as well as leading organs, shall formulate protection plans and emergency repair plans, organize protection drills, implement protection measures, and enhance comprehensive protection effectiveness.

Article 45 The State shall establish a medical and health rescue system that combines peacetime and wartime. After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, it shall mobilize medical and health personnel, allocate medicines, equipment, and facilities to ensure wartime medical rescue and epidemic prevention.

Article 46 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, the evacuation and concealment of personnel and materials within an administrative region shall be decided and organized by the people's government at that level; if conducted across administrative regions, it shall be decided and organized by the common higher-level people's government of the relevant administrative regions.

Units undertaking the tasks of evacuating and concealing personnel and materials shall complete the evacuation and concealment tasks within the specified time limit in accordance with the decisions of the relevant people's government.

Article 47 When a war disaster occurs, the local people's government shall promptly activate the emergency rescue mechanism, organize forces to rescue the wounded, resettle victims, protect property, and quickly eliminate the consequences of the war disaster to restore normal production and living order.

Personnel and organizations affected by war disasters shall promptly take self-rescue and mutual rescue measures to reduce the losses caused by the war disaster.

## Chapter IX Defense Service

Article 48 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, people's governments at or above the county level may, based on the needs of national defense mobilization implementation, mobilize citizens and organizations that meet the conditions stipulated in this Law to undertake defense service.

For the purposes of this Law, "defense service" refers to tasks such as supporting and ensuring military operations, undertaking the prevention and relief of war disasters, and assisting in maintaining social order.

Article 49 Male citizens aged 18 to 60 and female citizens aged 18 to 55 shall undertake defense service; however, they are exempted from undertaking defense service under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Citizens engaged in management and service work in social welfare institutions such as nurseries, kindergartens, orphanages, nursing homes, rehabilitation institutions for the disabled, and relief stations;
(2) Citizens engaged in teaching, management, and service work in schools during the compulsory education stage;
(3) Female citizens who are pregnant or within the lactation period;
(4) Citizens who are ill and unable to undertake defense service;
(5) Citizens who have lost the ability to work;
(6) Citizens holding positions in intergovernmental international organizations such as the United Nations;
(7) Other citizens exempted from undertaking defense service by decision of the people's government at or above the county level.

Professional and technical personnel with specialized expertise undertaking specific defense service are not subject to the age restrictions stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

Article 50 Personnel determined to undertake defense service shall obey commands, perform their duties, abide by discipline, and maintain confidentiality. The units where personnel undertaking defense service work shall provide support and assistance.

Article 51 Units engaged in transportation, postal services, telecommunications, medicine and health, food and grain supply, engineering construction, energy and chemicals, large-scale water conservancy facilities, civilian nuclear facilities, news media, national defense research and production, and municipal facility support shall undertake defense service in accordance with the law.

The units specified in the preceding paragraph shall, during peacetime, form specialized support teams following the principles of matching specialties, lean personnel, and effective emergency response, and organize training and drills to enhance their capability to complete defense service.

Article 52 The undertaking of defense service by citizens and organizations shall be organized by people's governments at or above the county level.

Citizens and specialized support teams undertaking the service of preventing and relieving war disasters and assisting in maintaining social order shall be commanded by the local people's government, which shall also provide service and living support; for service performed across administrative regions, the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize and implement the relevant support.

Citizens and specialized support teams undertaking the service of supporting and ensuring military operations shall be commanded by military organs; those accompanying the troops shall be provided with service and living support by the unit they accompany; others shall be provided with service and living support by the local people's government.

Article 53 Personnel undertaking defense service shall, during the period of service, continue to enjoy their wages, allowances, and other welfare benefits from their original work units; those without work units shall receive subsidies from the local people's government at the county level, with reference to the subsidy standards for militia performing combat readiness service; those injured or killed while performing defense service shall receive pensions and preferential treatment from the local people's government at the county level in accordance with the Regulations on Pension and Preferential Treatment for Servicemen and other relevant regulations.

## Chapter X Requisition and Compensation of Civilian Resources

Article 54 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, if reserved materials cannot meet the mobilization needs in a timely manner, people's governments at or above the county level may, in accordance with the law, requisition civilian resources.

For the purposes of this Law, "civilian resources" refer to facilities, equipment, premises, and other materials owned or used by organizations and individuals for social production, services, and daily life.

Article 55 All organizations and individuals have the obligation to accept the lawful requisition of civilian resources.

Active-duty units of the People's Liberation Army, reserve units, the People's Armed Police Force, and militia organizations that need to use civilian resources shall propose requisition requirements, and the requisition shall be uniformly organized by the local people's government at or above the county level. The local people's government at or above the county level shall register the requisitioned civilian resources and issue a certificate to the person whose resources are requisitioned.

Article 56 The following civilian resources are exempt from requisition:

(1) Articles and residential premises essential for the daily life of individuals and families;
(2) Articles and residential premises essential for the daily life of children, the elderly, the disabled, and relief recipients in social welfare institutions such as nurseries, kindergartens, orphanages, nursing homes, rehabilitation institutions for the disabled, and relief stations;
(3) Other civilian resources exempt from requisition as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 57 If requisitioned civilian resources need to be modified according to military requirements, the modification shall be organized and implemented by the local people's government at or above the county level in conjunction with relevant military organs.

Units undertaking the modification tasks shall carry out the modification in accordance with the military requirements and modification plans proposed by the using unit, and ensure delivery for use on schedule. The expenses required for modification shall be borne by the State.

Article 58 After the use of requisitioned civilian resources is completed, the local people's government at or above the county level shall promptly organize their return; if they have been modified, they shall be returned after restoring their original functions; compensation shall be provided in accordance with relevant State regulations for resources that cannot be repaired or are lost, as well as for direct economic losses caused by the requisition.Article 59 When active and reserve forces of the People's Liberation Army, the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, and militia organizations conduct military exercises or training and need to requisition civilian resources or adopt temporary control measures, the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission shall apply.

## Chapter XI Publicity and Education

Article 60 People's governments at all levels shall organize publicity and education on national defense mobilization to enhance citizens' awareness of national defense and their consciousness of fulfilling national defense obligations according to law. Relevant military authorities shall assist in carrying out publicity and education on national defense mobilization.

Article 61 State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and grassroots self-governing organizations shall organize their personnel to learn and master necessary national defense knowledge and skills.

Article 62 People's governments at all levels shall use various media and means of publicity to conduct patriotic and revolutionary heroism publicity and education among citizens, inspire their patriotic fervor, encourage them to actively participate in combat and support the front, carry out activities to support the military and give preferential treatment to families of servicemen and martyrs, and conduct condolence activities in various forms, and provide pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Units in press and publishing, radio, film and television, and online media shall conduct publicity, education, and related work in accordance with the requirements of national defense mobilization.

## Chapter XII Special Measures

Article 63 After the state decides to implement national defense mobilization, it may, as needed, take the following special measures in the areas where national defense mobilization is implemented according to law:

(1) Impose controls on industries such as finance, transportation, postal services, telecommunications, press and publishing, radio, film and television, information networks, energy and water supply, medical and health care, food and grain supply, and commercial trade;

(2) Impose necessary restrictions on the areas, times, and methods of personnel activities, as well as on the entry and exit of materials and means of transport;

(3) Implement special work systems in state organs, social organizations, enterprises, and public institutions;

(4) Give priority to providing various forms of transportation support to the armed forces;

(5) Other special measures that need to be taken.

Article 64 The implementation of special measures nationwide or in some provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be decided by the State Council and the Central Military Commission and organized for implementation. The implementation of special measures in parts of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be decided by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and organized for implementation by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the special measures are implemented and the military authority at the same level.

Article 65 The organs organizing the implementation of special measures shall implement them within the prescribed limits of authority, areas, and time limits. Citizens and organizations within the areas where special measures are implemented shall submit to the management of the organs organizing the implementation of the special measures.

Article 66 When special measures are no longer necessary, they shall be terminated promptly.

Article 67 If litigation, administrative reconsideration, or arbitration proceedings cannot proceed normally due to the state's issuance of a mobilization order, the provisions on the suspension of the statute of limitations and the suspension of proceedings shall apply, unless otherwise provided by law.

## Chapter XIII Legal Liability

Article 68 If a citizen commits any of the following acts, the people's government at the county level shall order rectification within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, the citizen shall be compelled to fulfill the obligation:

(1) A reservist pre-assigned to active forces or incorporated into reserve forces, or other reservists scheduled for call-up, who leaves the place of reserve registration for more than one month without reporting to the military service authority for reserve registration;

(2) After the state decides to implement national defense mobilization, a reservist scheduled for call-up who leaves the place of reserve registration without approval from the military service authority for reserve registration, or fails to return promptly as required by the military service authority, or fails to report to the designated location;

(3) Refusing or evading call-up, or refusing or evading undertaking national defense service duties;

(4) Refusing or delaying the requisition of civilian resources, or obstructing the modification of requisitioned civilian resources;

(5) Interfering with or undermining the order of national defense mobilization work, or obstructing personnel engaged in national defense mobilization work from performing their duties according to law.

Article 69 If an enterprise or public institution commits any of the following acts, the relevant people's government shall order rectification within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, the entity shall be compelled to fulfill the obligation and may also be fined:

(1) In a construction project undertaken that implements national defense requirements, failing to design, construct, or produce in accordance with national defense requirements and technical specifications and standards;

(2) Due to poor management, causing the loss or damage of strategic reserve materials, or failing to comply with the allocation of strategic materials;

(3) Failing to carry out the reserve of military product research, production, and maintenance support capabilities in accordance with the requirements for conversion to production, expanded production of military products, and maintenance support tasks, or failing to establish specialized technical teams as required;

(4) Refusing or delaying the execution of specialized support tasks;

(5) Refusing or intentionally delaying military orders;

(6) Refusing or delaying the requisition of civilian resources, or obstructing the modification of requisitioned civilian resources;

(7) Obstructing citizens from fulfilling call-up or undertaking national defense service duties.

Article 70 If any of the following acts is committed, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Refusing to execute orders for national defense mobilization issued by superiors;

(2) Abusing power or neglecting duties, causing serious losses to national defense mobilization work;

(3) Refusing to register or issue vouchers for requisitioned civilian resources, or using them in violation of regulations causing serious damage, or failing to return or compensate as required;

(4) Disclosing secrets of national defense mobilization;

(5) Embezzling or misappropriating funds or materials for national defense mobilization;

(6) Abusing power, infringing upon or damaging the lawful rights and interests of citizens or organizations.

Article 71 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a violation of public security administration shall be given a public security administration penalty according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter XIV Supplementary Provisions

Article 72 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 2010.
