# Firearms Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 5, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen firearms control, maintain public order, and ensure public safety.

Article 2 This Law applies to the control of firearms within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Where the State Council and the Central Military Commission have otherwise provided for the control of firearms equipped by the Chinese People's Liberation Army, the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, and the militia, such provisions shall apply.

Article 3 The State strictly controls firearms. No unit or individual may, in violation of the provisions of law, possess, manufacture (including alteration and assembly), trade in, transport, lease, or lend firearms.

The State severely punishes illegal and criminal acts that violate firearms control. Any unit or individual has the duty to report any act that violates firearms control. The State protects whistleblowers and rewards those who have rendered meritorious service in reporting criminal activities that violate firearms control.

Article 4 The public security department under the State Council shall be in charge of firearms control nationwide. The public security organs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of firearms control within their respective administrative regions. The public security organs of people's governments at higher levels shall supervise the firearms control work of public security organs of people's governments at lower levels.

## Chapter II Equipping and Allocation of Firearms

Article 5 People's police of public security organs, state security organs, prisons, and re-education-through-labor institutions, judicial police of people's courts, judicial police of people's procuratorates and procuratorial personnel undertaking case investigation tasks, and anti-smuggling personnel of customs, who indeed need to use firearms in the lawful performance of their duties, may be equipped with service firearms.

Full-time guards and escorts of key state military industrial, financial, warehousing, and scientific research units, who indeed need to use firearms in the performance of their guard and escort duties, may be equipped with service firearms.

Specific measures for equipping service firearms shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council jointly with other relevant state organs in accordance with the principle of strict control, and shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

Article 6 The following units may be allocated civilian firearms:

(1) Units approved by the administrative department for sports of the provincial people's government to be exclusively engaged in shooting competitive sports, and commercial shooting ranges approved by the public security organ of the provincial people's government, may be allocated firearms for shooting sports;

(2) Hunting grounds approved by the administrative department for forestry of the people's government at or above the provincial level may be allocated hunting rifles;

(3) Units engaged in wildlife protection, breeding, and scientific research may, due to business needs, be allocated hunting rifles and anesthetic injection guns.

Hunters in hunting areas and herders in pastoral areas may apply to be allocated hunting rifles. The boundaries of hunting areas and pastoral areas shall be delineated by the provincial people's government.

Specific measures for allocating civilian firearms shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council in accordance with the principle of strict control, and shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

Article 7 The equipping of service firearms shall be examined and approved by the public security department under the State Council or the public security organ of the provincial people's government.

When equipping service firearms, the public security department under the State Council or the public security organ of the provincial people's government shall issue a firearm possession certificate for service firearms.

Article 8 The allocation of firearms for shooting sports to units exclusively engaged in shooting competitive sports shall be proposed by the administrative department for sports under the State Council and examined and approved by the public security department under the State Council. The allocation of firearms for shooting sports to commercial shooting ranges shall be reported by the public security organ of the provincial people's government to the public security department under the State Council for approval.

When allocating firearms for shooting sports, the public security organ of the provincial people's government shall issue a civilian firearm possession certificate.

Article 9 The allocation of hunting rifles to hunting grounds shall, based on the approval document of the administrative department for forestry of the people's government at or above the provincial level, be reported to the public security organ of the people's government at or above the provincial level for examination and approval, and the public security organ of the city divided into districts shall issue a civilian firearm purchase certificate.

Article 10 Units engaged in wildlife protection, breeding, and scientific research applying for the allocation of hunting rifles and anesthetic injection guns shall submit their application to the public security organ of the county people's government where they are located, based on the hunting permit or special hunting permit issued by the administrative department for wildlife of the county people's government where they are located and their business license; hunters applying for the allocation of hunting rifles shall submit their application to the public security organ of the county people's government where they are located, based on the hunting permit issued by the administrative department for wildlife of the county people's government where they are located and their personal identification documents; herders applying for the allocation of hunting rifles shall submit their application to the public security organ of the county people's government where they are located, based on their personal identification documents.

After the public security organ receiving the application has examined and approved it, it shall report to the public security organ of the city divided into districts to issue a civilian firearm purchase certificate.

Article 11 Units and individuals that purchase hunting rifles and anesthetic injection guns must, within thirty days after purchasing the firearms, apply to the public security organ that issued the civilian firearm purchase certificate for a civilian firearm possession certificate.

Article 12 Civilian firearms allocated to commercial shooting ranges and hunting grounds may not be taken out of the commercial shooting range or hunting ground.

Hunting rifles allocated to hunters and herders may not be taken out of the hunting area or pastoral area.

## Chapter III Manufacture of Firearms and Distribution of Civilian Firearms

Article 13 The State implements a special licensing system for the manufacture and distribution of firearms. Without a license, no unit or individual may manufacture or trade in firearms.

Article 14 Service firearms shall be manufactured by enterprises designated by the State.

Article 15 Enterprises manufacturing civilian firearms shall be proposed by the relevant competent department under the State Council and determined by the public security department under the State Council.

Enterprises distributing civilian firearms shall be determined by the public security organ of the provincial people's government.

Enterprises manufacturing civilian firearms shall be issued a civilian firearm manufacturing license by the public security department under the State Council. Enterprises distributing civilian firearms shall be issued a civilian firearm distribution license by the public security organ of the provincial people's government. The validity period of the civilian firearm manufacturing license and distribution license is three years; if it is necessary to continue manufacturing or distributing civilian firearms upon expiration, a new license must be applied for.

Article 16 The State implements quota management for the quantity of civilian firearms manufactured and distributed.

The annual quota for manufacturing civilian firearms shall be proposed by the relevant competent departments under the State Council, such as forestry and sports, and the public security organ of the provincial people's government, determined by the public security department under the State Council, which shall also uniformly compile serial numbers for civilian firearms and assign them to civilian firearm manufacturing enterprises.

The annual quota for distributing civilian firearms shall be proposed by the relevant competent departments under the State Council, such as forestry and sports, and the public security organ of the provincial people's government, determined by the public security department under the State Council, and assigned to civilian firearm distribution enterprises.

Article 17 Enterprises manufacturing civilian firearms may not manufacture civilian firearms exceeding the quota, and all manufactured civilian firearms must be handed over to designated civilian firearm distribution enterprises for distribution; they may not sell them on their own. Civilian firearm distribution enterprises shall distribute civilian firearms manufactured by designated enterprises within the distribution quota.

Article 18 Enterprises manufacturing civilian firearms must strictly manufacture civilian firearms in accordance with the technical standards prescribed by the State, and may not alter the performance or structure of civilian firearms; they must stamp the manufacturer's name, firearm type code, and the firearm serial number uniformly compiled by the public security department under the State Council on the designated parts of civilian firearms, and may not manufacture civilian firearms without serial numbers, with duplicate serial numbers, or with false serial numbers.

Enterprises manufacturing civilian firearms must implement closed management and take necessary security measures to prevent the loss of civilian firearms and their parts.

Article 19 When distributing civilian firearms, the purchase certificate must be verified, and the distribution must be strictly in accordance with the type, model, and quantity specified in the purchase certificate; when distributing ammunition, the firearm possession certificate must be verified. Civilian firearm distribution enterprises must establish distribution ledgers in accordance with the regulations of the public security department under the State Council and keep them for long-term reference.

Article 20 Public security organs must conduct regular inspections of the manufacturing, distribution, storage, and ledger registration of enterprises manufacturing and distributing civilian firearms; when necessary, they may send personnel to the manufacturing enterprises for supervision and inspection.

Article 21 The research, development, and type approval of civilian firearms shall be organized and implemented by the relevant competent department under the State Council jointly with the public security department under the State Council.

Article 22 The manufacture and sale of imitation firearms are prohibited.

## Chapter IV Routine Management of Firearms

Article 23 Units and individuals equipped with or allocated firearms must properly keep the firearms to ensure their safety.

Units equipped with or allocated firearms must clarify the responsibility for firearms management, designate a specific person in charge, have secure dedicated storage facilities, and store firearms and ammunition separately. For firearms assigned for personal use, strict management systems for registration, handover, inspection, and maintenance must be established, and the firearms must be promptly retrieved after use.

For firearms equipped or allocated for personal use, effective measures must be taken to prevent theft, robbery, loss, or other accidents.

Article 24 Persons using firearms must master the performance of the firearms, comply with the relevant regulations on the use of firearms, and ensure the lawful and safe use of firearms. Persons using service firearms must undergo specialized training.

Article 25 Units and individuals equipped with or allocated firearms must comply with the following provisions:

(1) When carrying a firearm, the firearm possession certificate must also be carried; if the firearm possession certificate is not carried, the public security organ shall impound the firearm;

(2) Firearms may not be carried in areas or places where carrying firearms is prohibited;

(3) If a firearm is stolen, robbed, or lost, the public security organ must be immediately reported.

Article 26 When a person equipped with a service firearm no longer meets the conditions for possessing a firearm, the unit to which they belong shall retrieve the firearm and the firearm possession certificate.

When a unit or individual allocated a civilian firearm no longer meets the conditions for possessing a firearm, they must promptly surrender the firearm together with the firearm possession certificate to the public security organ that issued the firearm possession certificate; if not surrendered in a timely manner, the public security organ shall confiscate them.

Article 27 Firearms that do not meet national technical standards and cannot be used safely shall be scrapped. Units and individuals equipped with or possessing firearms shall surrender the scrapped firearms together with the firearm possession certificate to the public security organ that issued the firearm possession certificate; if not surrendered in a timely manner, the public security organ shall confiscate them. Scrapped firearms shall be promptly destroyed.

The destruction of firearms shall be organized and implemented by the public security organ of the provincial people's government.

Article 28 The State implements an inspection system for firearms. Units and individuals possessing firearms must undergo inspection at the time and place designated by the public security organ. When conducting inspections, the public security organ must strictly examine whether the unit or individual possessing the firearm meets the conditions stipulated in this Law, and inspect the condition and usage of the firearm; if a firearm is used illegally, the conditions for possession are not met, or the firearm should be scrapped, the firearm and the firearm possession certificate must be confiscated. If inspection is refused, the firearm and the firearm possession certificate shall be confiscated by the public security organ.

Article 29 For special needs to maintain public order, with the approval of the public security department under the State Council, the public security organs of local people's governments at or above the county level may implement special control measures such as centralized storage of lawfully equipped or allocated firearms in a local area.

## Chapter V Transportation of Firearms

Article 30 No unit or individual may transport firearms without a license. If it is necessary to transport firearms, the unit or individual must truthfully declare to the public security organ the type and quantity of firearms to be transported, and the route and method of transportation, and obtain a firearm transportation permit. For transportation within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, an application shall be made to the public security organ of the city divided into districts in the destination for a firearm transportation permit; for transportation across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, an application shall be made to the public security organ of the provincial people's government in the destination for a firearm transportation permit.

Without a firearm transportation permit, no unit or individual may undertake the transportation, and shall immediately report to the local public security organ.

If a public security organ finds that firearms are being transported without a firearm transportation permit or not in accordance with the provisions of the firearm transportation permit, it shall impound the firearms being transported.

Article 31 Firearms must be transported using safe and reliable closed transport equipment, with a dedicated escort; if stopping overnight during transportation, the local public security organ must be reported.

Firearms and ammunition must be transported separately in accordance with regulations.

Article 32 The mailing of firearms, or the inclusion of firearms in mailed items, is strictly prohibited.

## Chapter VI Entry and Exit of Firearms

Article 33 The State strictly controls the entry and exit of firearms. No unit or individual may bring firearms into or out of the country without a license.

Article 34 Personnel of foreign diplomatic missions and consular posts in China who bring firearms into the country must obtain prior approval from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China; those who take firearms out of the country must notify the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China in advance and go through the relevant procedures.

Firearms brought into the country in accordance with the preceding paragraph may not be taken out of the diplomatic mission or consular post in China.

Article 35 If a foreign sports delegation needs to bring firearms for shooting sports into the country to participate in shooting competitive sports activities, or a Chinese sports delegation needs to take firearms for shooting sports out of the country to participate in shooting competitive sports activities, approval must be obtained from the administrative department for sports under the State Council.

Article 36 Personnel other than those specified in Articles 34 and 35 of this Law who bring firearms into or out of the country must obtain prior approval from the public security department under the State Council.

Article 37 For those approved to bring firearms into the country, upon entry, they shall, based on the approval document, register the firearms at the border inspection station at the port of entry, apply for a firearm transport permit, and declare to the customs, which shall release the firearms upon presentation of the firearm transport permit; after arriving at the destination, they shall apply to the public security organ of the city divided into districts for a firearm possession certificate based on the firearm transport permit.

For those approved to take firearms out of the country, upon exit, they shall declare to the customs at the port of exit based on the approval document, and the border inspection station shall release them upon presentation of the approval document.

Article 38 If a foreign means of transport brings firearms into or transits through the country, the person in charge of the means of transport must declare to the border inspection station, which shall seal the firearms, and the seal shall be removed when the means of transport exits the country.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 39 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, manufactures, trades in, or transports firearms without a license shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If a unit commits the act mentioned in the preceding paragraph, the unit shall be fined, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 40 If an enterprise designated or determined by law for firearm manufacturing or sales, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts, the unit shall be fined, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; the public security organ may order it to suspend operations for rectification or revoke its firearm manufacturing license or firearm distribution license:

(1) Manufacturing or distributing firearms exceeding the quota or not in accordance with the prescribed types;

(2) Manufacturing firearms without serial numbers, with duplicate serial numbers, or with false serial numbers;

(3) Selling firearms privately or selling firearms manufactured for export within the country.

Article 41 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, illegally possesses or conceals firearms, or illegally transports or brings firearms into or out of the country, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 42 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, transports firearms without using safe and reliable transport equipment, without a dedicated escort, without transporting firearms and ammunition separately, or fails to report to the public security organ when stopping overnight during transportation, if the circumstances are serious, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the act does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose a detention of not more than fifteen days on the directly responsible personnel.

Article 43 Anyone who, in violation of the firearms control regulations, leases or lends service firearms shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If a unit commits the act mentioned in the preceding paragraph, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If a unit allocated civilian firearms, in violation of the firearms control regulations, leases or lends firearms, causing serious consequences or having other serious circumstances, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If an individual allocated civilian firearms, in violation of the firearms control regulations, leases or lends firearms, causing serious consequences, shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If the act of leasing or lending firearms in violation of the firearms control regulations is minor and does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose a detention of not more than fifteen days on the individual or the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel of the unit, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan; the leased or lent firearms shall be confiscated.

Article 44 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be given a warning or detained for not more than fifteen days by the public security organ if they are an individual or the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel of a unit; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Manufacturing civilian firearms not in accordance with the prescribed technical standards;

(2) Carrying firearms in areas or places where carrying firearms is prohibited;

(3) Failing to surrender scrapped firearms;

(4) Failing to promptly report the theft, robbery, or loss of a firearm;

(5) Manufacturing or selling imitation firearms.

If any of the acts listed in items (1) to (3) of the preceding paragraph is committed, the firearms shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan may be concurrently imposed; if the act listed in item (5) of the preceding paragraph is committed, the public security organ or the administrative department for industry and commerce shall, within their respective scope of duties, confiscate the imitation firearms, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than five times the amount of the manufacturing or sales value; if the circumstances are serious, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke the business license.

Article 45 If a staff member of a public security organ commits any of the following acts, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law:

(1) Equipping or allocating firearms to units or individuals other than those specified in Articles 5 and 6 of this Law;

(2) Illegally issuing firearms control certificates;

(3) Appropriating confiscated firearms;

(4) Failing to perform firearms control duties, resulting in consequences.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 46 For the purposes of this Law, "firearms" means various types of guns that use gunpowder or compressed gas as a propellant and utilize a tubular device to launch metal projectiles or other substances, capable of causing death, injury, or loss of consciousness.

Article 47 Units and individuals may be allocated air rifles with a caliber not exceeding 4.5 mm for recreational activities. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council.

Management measures for prop firearms used in the production of films and television dramas shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council jointly with the competent department for radio, film, and television under the State Council.

Management measures for firearms kept or exhibited in museums, memorial halls, and exhibition halls shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council jointly with the relevant competent department under the State Council.

Article 48 The relevant provisions of this Law shall apply to the manufacture, distribution, and transportation of major parts of firearms and ammunition used for firearms.

Article 49 Firearms control certificates shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council.Article 50 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 1996.
