# Public Cultural Service Guarantee Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 25, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen the public cultural service system, enrich the spiritual and cultural life of the people, carry forward the excellent traditional Chinese culture, promote the core socialist values, enhance cultural confidence, promote the prosperity and development of socialist culture with Chinese characteristics, and improve the cultural quality of the entire nation.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "public cultural services" refer to public cultural facilities, cultural products, cultural activities, and other related services provided primarily to meet the basic cultural needs of citizens, led by the government and with the participation of social forces.

Article 3 Public cultural services shall adhere to the direction of advanced socialist culture, uphold a people-centered approach, and be guided by the core socialist values. They shall follow the principle of "letting a hundred flowers bloom and a hundred schools of thought contend," support the creation and production of excellent public cultural products, and enrich the content of public cultural services.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate public cultural services into their respective national economic and social development plans. In accordance with the requirements of public welfare, basic needs, equal access, and convenience, they shall strengthen the construction of public cultural facilities, improve the public cultural service system, and enhance the efficiency of public cultural services.

Article 5 The State Council shall formulate and adjust the national guiding standards for basic public cultural services based on the basic cultural needs of citizens and the level of economic and social development.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the national guiding standards for basic public cultural services and taking into account local actual needs, financial capacity, and cultural characteristics, formulate and adjust the implementation standards for basic public cultural services within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The State Council shall establish a comprehensive coordination mechanism for public cultural services to guide, coordinate, and promote public cultural service work nationwide. The cultural administrative department of the State Council shall undertake the specific responsibilities for comprehensive coordination.

Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the overall coordination of public cultural services and promote shared construction and utilization.

Article 7 The cultural administrative department and the press, publication, radio, film, and television administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for public cultural service work nationwide in accordance with this Law and the duties prescribed by the State Council. Other relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for related public cultural service work within their respective duties.

The cultural, press, publication, radio, film, and television administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for public cultural service work within their respective administrative regions according to their duties. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for related public cultural service work within their respective duties.

Article 8 The State shall support public cultural services in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas to promote balanced and coordinated development of public cultural services.

Article 9 People's governments at all levels shall provide corresponding public cultural services based on the characteristics and needs of groups such as minors, the elderly, persons with disabilities, and the floating population.

Article 10 The State encourages and supports the integration of public cultural services with school education, fully leveraging the social education function of public cultural services to improve the ideological, moral, scientific, and cultural quality of young people.

Article 11 The State encourages and supports the role of science and technology in public cultural services, promoting the use of modern information technology and communication technology to enhance the scientific literacy of the public and the level of public cultural services.

Article 12 The State encourages and supports international cooperation and exchange in the field of public cultural services.

Article 13 The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in public cultural services.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations that make outstanding contributions to public cultural services shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Construction and Management of Public Cultural Facilities

Article 14 For the purposes of this Law, "public cultural facilities" refer to buildings, venues, and equipment used to provide public cultural services, mainly including libraries, museums, cultural centers (stations), art galleries, science and technology museums, memorial halls, sports venues, workers' cultural palaces, youth palaces, women's and children's activity centers, elderly activity centers, township (subdistrict) and village (community) grassroots comprehensive cultural service centers, farm (worker) bookstores, public newspaper reading boards (screens), radio and television broadcasting transmission coverage facilities, and public digital cultural service points.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall publish the catalog and related information of public cultural facilities within their respective administrative regions.

Article 15 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the construction of public cultural facilities into their respective urban and rural plans. Based on the national guiding standards for basic public cultural services and the provincial implementation standards for basic public cultural services, and taking into account the local level of economic and social development, population status, environmental conditions, and cultural characteristics, they shall reasonably determine the types, quantities, scales, and layout of public cultural facilities, forming a public cultural facility network that combines venue services, mobile services, and digital services.

The site selection of public cultural facilities shall solicit public opinions, conform to the functions and characteristics of public cultural facilities, and be conducive to their role.

Article 16 Land for the construction of public cultural facilities shall comply with the overall land use plan and urban and rural plans, and shall be approved in accordance with legal procedures.

No unit or individual may encroach upon land designated for the construction of public cultural facilities or arbitrarily change its use. If it is necessary to adjust the land for public cultural facility construction due to special circumstances, new construction land shall be determined. The adjusted land for public cultural facility construction shall not be less than the original area.

When constructing, renovating, or expanding residential areas, supporting public cultural facilities shall be planned and constructed in accordance with relevant regulations and standards.

Article 17 The design and construction of public cultural facilities shall meet the requirements of practicality, safety, science, aesthetics, environmental protection, and economy, as well as the standards prescribed by the State, and shall be equipped with barrier-free facilities and equipment.

Article 18 Local people's governments at all levels may adopt various methods such as new construction, renovation, expansion, joint construction, leasing, and utilization of existing public facilities to strengthen the construction of township (subdistrict) and village (community) grassroots comprehensive cultural service centers, promote the unified management and comprehensive utilization of relevant grassroots public facilities, and ensure their normal operation.

Article 19 No unit or individual may dismantle public cultural facilities without authorization, arbitrarily change their functions or uses, or hinder their normal operation. No unit or individual may encroach upon or misappropriate public cultural facilities, or use them for commercial business activities unrelated to public cultural services.

If it is truly necessary to dismantle public cultural facilities or change their functions or uses due to urban or rural construction, reconstruction or renovation shall be carried out in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations, adhering to the principle of constructing first and then dismantling, or carrying out construction and dismantling simultaneously. The facility configuration standards and building area of the reconstructed or renovated public cultural facilities shall not be reduced.

Article 20 The management units of public cultural facilities shall, in accordance with the standards prescribed by the State, configure and update necessary service content and equipment, strengthen the regular maintenance and management of public cultural facilities, and ensure the normal use and operation of public cultural facilities.

Article 21 The management units of public cultural facilities shall establish and improve management systems and service standards, and establish a statistical reporting system for public cultural facility assets and an annual reporting system for the implementation of public cultural services.

Article 22 The management units of public cultural facilities shall establish and improve safety management systems, conduct safety assessments of public cultural facilities and public activities, equip safety protection equipment and personnel in accordance with the law, and ensure the safety of public cultural facilities and public activities.

Article 23 People's governments at all levels shall establish a performance evaluation system for the utilization efficiency of public cultural facilities with public participation. The management units of public cultural facilities shall improve their work and enhance service quality based on the evaluation results.

Article 24 The State promotes the establishment and improvement of corporate governance structures by management units of public cultural facilities such as public libraries, museums, and cultural centers, based on their functional positioning, and encourages the participation of representatives from relevant parties, professionals, and the public in management.

Article 25 The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to construct, donate, or cooperate with government departments in the construction of public cultural facilities, and encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in the operation and management of public cultural facilities in accordance with the law.

Article 26 When using public cultural facilities, the public shall observe public order, take care of public facilities, and shall not damage public facility equipment and items.

## Chapter III Provision of Public Cultural Services

Article 27 People's governments at all levels shall make full use of public cultural facilities to promote the provision and dissemination of excellent public cultural products, and support activities such as nationwide reading, nationwide legal education, nationwide fitness, nationwide science popularization, art popularization, and the inheritance of excellent traditional culture.

Article 28 Local people's governments at the level of cities divided into districts and counties shall, based on the national guiding standards for basic public cultural services and the implementation standards for basic public cultural services of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and taking into account local actual conditions, formulate and publish the catalog of public cultural services within their respective administrative regions and organize its implementation.

Article 29 Public welfare cultural units shall improve service items, enrich service content, create conditions to provide the public with free or discounted performances, exhibitions, film screenings, radio and television program reception, reading services, art training, etc., and provide support and assistance for the public to carry out cultural activities.

The State encourages for-profit cultural units to provide free or discounted public cultural products and cultural activities.

Article 30 Grassroots comprehensive cultural service centers shall strengthen resource integration, establish and improve the public cultural service network, fully leverage their comprehensive service functions, and provide the public with public cultural services such as book and newspaper reading, film and television viewing, opera performances, legal education, art popularization, science popularization, radio broadcasting, internet access, and mass cultural and sports activities. They shall also provide other public services tailored to local conditions based on their functional characteristics.

Article 31 Public cultural facilities shall be open to the public free of charge or at a discount in accordance with relevant State regulations and based on their functions and characteristics.

If a public cultural facility charges a fee for opening, it shall be open free of charge to primary and secondary school students on a regular monthly basis.

If a public cultural facility charges fees for opening or providing training services, it shall be reported to the relevant department of the people's government at or above the county level for approval. The fees collected shall be used for the maintenance, management, and career development of the public cultural facility and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

The management units of public cultural facilities shall publicize service items and opening hours; if temporarily closed, they shall announce it in a timely manner.

Article 32 The State encourages and supports the opening of cultural and sports facilities of government agencies, schools, enterprises, and institutions to the public.

Article 33 The State makes overall plans for the construction of public digital culture, builds a public digital cultural service network with unified standards and interconnection, establishes a public cultural information resource database, and achieves shared construction and utilization of grassroots network services.

The State supports the development of digital cultural products and promotes the provision of public cultural services using broadband internet, mobile internet, radio and television networks, and satellite networks.

Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the digitalization and network construction of grassroots public cultural facilities and improve digital and network service capabilities.

Article 34 Local people's governments at all levels shall adopt various methods to provide mobile cultural services tailored to local conditions.

Article 35 The State focuses on increasing the supply of public cultural products such as books, newspapers, operas, films, radio and television programs, online information content, festival activities, and sports and fitness activities in rural areas, promoting equal access to public cultural services in urban and rural areas.

Public cultural products such as books, newspapers, and films provided to rural areas shall meet rural characteristics and needs, improving relevance and timeliness.

Article 36 Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on local actual conditions, equip necessary facilities in public places with large population flows, areas where migrant workers are concentrated, and rural areas where left-behind women and children are concentrated, and adopt various forms to provide convenient and accessible public cultural services.

Article 37 The State encourages citizens to actively participate in public cultural services and independently carry out healthy and civilized mass cultural and sports activities. Local people's governments at all levels shall provide necessary guidance, support, and assistance.

Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall carry out mass cultural and sports activities based on the needs of residents and assist relevant departments of local people's governments in carrying out work related to public cultural services.

State organs, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall, based on their own characteristics and needs, organize and carry out mass cultural and sports activities to enrich the cultural life of their staff.

Article 38 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen public cultural services for enrolled students, support schools in carrying out cultural and sports activities suitable for the characteristics of enrolled students, and promote education in moral, intellectual, physical, and aesthetic development.
Article 39 Local people's governments at all levels shall support the cultural construction of military grassroots units, enrich cultural and sports activities in military camps, and promote the integration of military and civilian culture.

Article 40 The State strengthens the supply of cultural products in ethnic minority languages, strengthens the translation of excellent public cultural products into ethnic minority languages and their dissemination in ethnic minority areas, encourages and supports the creation and production of ethnic cultural products, and supports the carrying out of mass cultural and sports activities with ethnic characteristics.

Article 41 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate guiding opinions and catalogs for government procurement of public cultural services. Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the guiding opinions and catalogs and taking into account actual conditions, determine the specific items and content for procurement and promptly announce them to the public.

Article 42 The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in the provision of public cultural services by establishing entities, funding projects, sponsoring activities, providing facilities, donating products, and other means.

Article 43 The State advocates and encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in cultural volunteer services.

The management units of public cultural facilities shall establish a cultural volunteer service mechanism and organize cultural volunteer service activities.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide necessary guidance and support for cultural volunteer activities and establish mechanisms for management evaluation, education and training, and incentive guarantees.

Article 44 No organization or individual may use public cultural facilities, cultural products, cultural activities, or other related services to engage in activities that endanger national security, harm public interests, or violate laws and regulations.

## Chapter IV Guarantee Measures

Article 45 The State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall, based on the powers and expenditure responsibilities for public cultural services, incorporate public cultural service funds into their respective budgets and allocate the necessary funds for public cultural services.

Article 46 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall increase investment, through transfer payments and other means, to primarily support the development of public cultural services in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

The State encourages and supports economically developed areas in providing assistance for public cultural services in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 47 Public cultural facilities that are open free of charge or at a discount shall receive subsidies in accordance with State regulations.

Article 48 The State encourages the investment of social capital in public cultural services in accordance with the law to broaden the sources of funding for public cultural services.

Article 49 The State adopts measures such as government procurement of services to support citizens, legal persons, and other organizations in participating in the provision of public cultural services.

Article 50 Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations that donate property for public cultural services through public welfare social organizations or people's governments at or above the county level and their departments shall enjoy tax benefits in accordance with the law.

The State encourages the establishment of public cultural service funds through donations and other means, specifically for public cultural services.

Article 51 Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on the functions, tasks, and service population size of public cultural facilities, reasonably set up public cultural service positions and equip corresponding professional personnel.

Article 52 The State encourages and supports cultural professionals, college graduates, and volunteers to engage in public cultural service work at the grassroots level.

Article 53 The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to establish social organizations in the field of public cultural services in accordance with the law, promoting the socialization and professional development of public cultural services.

Article 54 The State supports theoretical research on public cultural services and strengthens multi-level professional talent education and training.

Article 55 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve a supervision and statistical announcement system for the use of public cultural service funds, strengthen performance evaluation, and ensure that funds are used for public cultural services. No unit or individual may encroach upon or misappropriate public cultural service funds.

Audit authorities shall strengthen audit supervision over public cultural service funds in accordance with the law.

Article 56 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen supervision and inspection of public cultural service work, establish a consultation and feedback system reflecting public cultural needs and a public cultural service assessment and evaluation system with public participation, and use the assessment and evaluation results as the basis for determining subsidies or rewards.

Article 57 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall promptly disclose public cultural service information and actively accept social supervision.

News media shall actively carry out publicity and reporting on public cultural services and strengthen public opinion supervision.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 58 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level fail to fulfill their responsibilities for guaranteeing public cultural services, their superior organs or supervisory authorities shall order them to make corrections within a specified time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 59 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level commit any of the following acts, their superior organs or supervisory authorities shall order them to make corrections within a specified time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Encroaching upon or misappropriating public cultural service funds;

(2) Dismantling, encroaching upon, or misappropriating public cultural facilities without authorization, or changing their functions or uses, or hindering their normal operation;

(3) Failing to reconstruct public cultural facilities in accordance with the provisions of this Law;

(4) Abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 60 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, land designated for the construction of public cultural facilities is encroached upon or its use is arbitrarily changed, the land administrative department and the urban and rural planning administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, based on their respective duties, order corrections within a specified time limit; if corrections are not made within the time limit, the organ that made the decision shall enforce the law in accordance with the law, or apply to the People's Court for enforcement in accordance with the law.

Article 61 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the management unit of a public cultural facility falls under any of the following circumstances, its competent department shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if serious consequences are caused, the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to open to the public in accordance with regulations;(2) Failing to publicly disclose service items, opening hours, or other relevant information;

(3) Failing to establish a safety management system;

(4) Causing losses due to poor management.

**Article 62** Where, in violation of this Law, a public cultural facility management unit commits any of the following acts, the competent authority or the price authority shall order rectification within a specified period, confiscate any illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains if the illegal gains exceed 5,000 yuan; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 5,000 yuan, a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan may be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Conducting service activities inconsistent with the functions and purposes of the public cultural facility;

(2) Charging fees or indirectly charging fees for public cultural facilities that should be open to the public free of charge;

(3) Using collected fees for purposes other than the maintenance, management, and development of the public cultural facility, i.e., misappropriating them.

**Article 63** Where, in violation of this Law, civil rights and interests of others are harmed, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

# Chapter 6 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 64** Foreign natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations engaging in public cultural services within the territory of China shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 65** This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 2017.
