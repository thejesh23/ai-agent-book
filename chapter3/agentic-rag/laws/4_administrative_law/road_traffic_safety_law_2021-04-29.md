# Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on October 28, 2003

First Amendment on December 29, 2007, according to the Decision on Amending the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 22, 2011, according to the Decision on Amending the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress

Third Amendment on April 29, 2021, according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of maintaining road traffic order, preventing and reducing traffic accidents, protecting personal safety, protecting the property safety and other lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons and other organizations, and improving traffic efficiency.

Article 2 All vehicle drivers, pedestrians, passengers, and units and individuals involved in road traffic activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall comply with this Law.

Article 3 Road traffic safety work shall follow the principles of law-based management and convenience for the public, ensuring orderly, safe, and smooth road traffic.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels shall ensure that road traffic safety management work is compatible with economic construction and social development.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall adapt to the needs of road traffic development, formulate road traffic safety management plans based on road traffic safety laws, regulations, and relevant national policies, and organize their implementation.

Article 5 The public security department of the State Council is responsible for road traffic safety management nationwide. The traffic management departments of public security organs of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for road traffic safety management within their respective administrative regions.

The transportation and construction management departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for related road traffic work.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall regularly conduct road traffic safety education to enhance citizens' awareness of road traffic safety.

When performing their duties, traffic management departments of public security organs and their traffic police shall strengthen the promotion of road traffic safety laws and regulations and set an example in complying with them.

State organs, military units, enterprises, institutions, social groups, and other organizations shall conduct road traffic safety education for their personnel.

Education administrative departments and schools shall incorporate road traffic safety education into legal education content.

Units such as news media, publishing houses, radio stations, and television stations have the obligation to conduct road traffic safety education.

Article 7 Scientific research shall be strengthened in road traffic safety management, and advanced management methods, technologies, and equipment shall be promoted and used.

## Chapter II Vehicles and Drivers

### Section 1 Motor Vehicles and Non-Motor Vehicles

Article 8 The State implements a registration system for motor vehicles. A motor vehicle may be driven on the road only after it has been registered with the traffic management department of a public security organ. A motor vehicle that has not yet been registered but needs to be driven temporarily on the road shall obtain a temporary license plate.

Article 9 To apply for motor vehicle registration, the following certificates and documents shall be submitted:

(1) Identity certificate of the motor vehicle owner;

(2) Certificate of origin of the motor vehicle;

(3) Certificate of conformity for the complete motor vehicle or import certificate for imported motor vehicles;

(4) Tax payment certificate or tax exemption certificate for vehicle purchase tax;

(5) Other certificates and documents that laws and administrative regulations require to be submitted at the time of motor vehicle registration.

The traffic management department of a public security organ shall complete the motor vehicle registration review within five working days from the date of accepting the application. If the conditions specified in the preceding paragraph are met, it shall issue a motor vehicle registration certificate, license plate, and driving permit; if the conditions are not met, it shall explain the reasons for refusal to the applicant.

No unit or individual other than the traffic management department of a public security organ may issue motor vehicle license plates or require motor vehicles to display other license plates, except as otherwise provided in this Law.

The styles of motor vehicle registration certificates, license plates, and driving permits shall be prescribed and supervised by the public security department of the State Council.

Article 10 Motor vehicles eligible for registration shall meet national safety technical standards for motor vehicles. When applying for motor vehicle registration, the motor vehicle shall undergo safety technical inspection. However, for models produced by enterprises recognized by the national motor vehicle product authority as meeting national safety technical standards, new vehicles of such models that have passed inspection at the factory and obtained a certificate of conformity are exempt from safety technical inspection.

Article 11 When driving a motor vehicle on the road, the motor vehicle license plate shall be displayed, the inspection certificate and insurance mark shall be placed, and the motor vehicle driving permit shall be carried with the vehicle.

Motor vehicle license plates shall be displayed in accordance with regulations and kept clear and complete, and shall not be intentionally obscured or defaced.

No unit or individual may confiscate or detain motor vehicle license plates.

Article 12 In any of the following circumstances, corresponding registration shall be handled:

(1) Transfer of ownership of a motor vehicle;

(2) Change in the registered content of a motor vehicle;

(3) Use of a motor vehicle as collateral;

(4) Scrapping of a motor vehicle.

Article 13 For registered motor vehicles driven on the road, regular safety technical inspections shall be conducted in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, based on different conditions such as vehicle use, passenger and cargo capacity, and service life. For those providing a motor vehicle driving permit and a compulsory motor vehicle third-party liability insurance policy, the motor vehicle safety technical inspection institution shall conduct the inspection, and no unit may impose additional conditions. If the motor vehicle meets national safety technical standards, the traffic management department of a public security organ shall issue an inspection certificate.

The safety technical inspection of motor vehicles shall be socialized. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

In areas where motor vehicle safety technical inspection is socialized, no unit may require motor vehicles to be inspected at a designated location.

Traffic management departments of public security organs and motor vehicle safety technical inspection institutions shall not require motor vehicles to be repaired or maintained at designated locations.

Fees charged by motor vehicle safety technical inspection institutions for motor vehicle inspection shall strictly follow the fee standards approved by the price authority of the State Council.

Article 14 The State implements a compulsory motor vehicle scrapping system, prescribing different scrapping standards based on the safety technical condition and different uses of motor vehicles.

Motor vehicles that should be scrapped must undergo cancellation of registration in a timely manner.

Motor vehicles that have reached the scrapping standard shall not be driven on the road. Scrapped large passenger vehicles, trucks, and other operating vehicles shall be dismantled under the supervision of the traffic management department of a public security organ.

Article 15 Police cars, fire engines, ambulances, and engineering rescue vehicles shall be painted with standard markings and equipped with sirens and warning lights in accordance with regulations. Other motor vehicles shall not be painted, equipped with, or use markings, sirens, or warning lights that are exclusive to or similar to those of the above vehicles.

Police cars, fire engines, ambulances, and engineering rescue vehicles shall be used strictly in accordance with their prescribed purposes and conditions.

Special vehicles for highway supervision and inspection shall be equipped with uniform markings and warning lights in accordance with the Highway Law.

Article 16 No unit or individual shall engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Assembling a motor vehicle or unauthorized modification of the registered structure, construction, or characteristics of a motor vehicle;

(2) Altering the model, engine number, chassis number, or vehicle identification number of a motor vehicle;

(3) Forging, altering, or using forged or altered motor vehicle registration certificates, license plates, driving permits, inspection certificates, or insurance marks;

(4) Using the registration certificates, license plates, driving permits, inspection certificates, or insurance marks of other motor vehicles.

Article 17 The State implements a compulsory motor vehicle third-party liability insurance system and establishes a road traffic accident social relief fund. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 18 Non-motor vehicles that should be registered according to law may be driven on the road only after being registered with the traffic management department of a public security organ.

The types of non-motor vehicles that should be registered according to law shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government based on local actual conditions.

The external dimensions, mass, brakes, bells, and nighttime reflective devices of non-motor vehicles shall comply with non-motor vehicle safety technical standards.

### Section 2 Motor Vehicle Drivers

Article 19 To drive a motor vehicle, a motor vehicle driving license shall be obtained according to law.

To apply for a motor vehicle driving license, the applicant shall meet the driving permit conditions prescribed by the public security department of the State Council; after passing the examination, the traffic management department of a public security organ shall issue a motor vehicle driving license of the corresponding category.

A person holding a foreign motor vehicle driving license who meets the driving permit conditions prescribed by the public security department of the State Council and passes the assessment by the traffic management department of a public security organ may be issued a Chinese motor vehicle driving license.

A driver shall drive a motor vehicle in accordance with the type of vehicle permitted by the driving license; when driving a motor vehicle, the driver shall carry the motor vehicle driving license.

No unit or individual other than the traffic management department of a public security organ may confiscate or detain a motor vehicle driving license.

Article 20 Driver training for motor vehicles is socialized. The transportation authority shall implement record-filing management for driving training schools and driving training classes and strengthen supervision over driving training activities. Among them, specialized tractor driving training schools and driving training classes shall be supervised and managed by the agricultural (agricultural machinery) authority.

Driving training schools and driving training classes shall strictly follow relevant national regulations to train students in road traffic safety laws, regulations, and driving skills, ensuring training quality.

No state organ or authority in charge of driving training and examination may establish or participate in establishing driving training schools or driving training classes.

Article 21 Before driving a motor vehicle on the road, the driver shall carefully inspect the safety technical performance of the motor vehicle; a motor vehicle with incomplete safety facilities or components that do not meet technical standards, posing safety hazards, shall not be driven.

Article 22 Motor vehicle drivers shall comply with the provisions of road traffic safety laws and regulations, and drive safely and civilly in accordance with operating norms.

A person who has consumed alcohol, taken psychotropic substances or narcotic drugs controlled by the State, suffers from a disease that hinders safe driving of a motor vehicle, or is excessively fatigued affecting safe driving shall not drive a motor vehicle.

No one may force, instruct, or condone a driver to drive a motor vehicle in violation of road traffic safety laws, regulations, and safe driving requirements.

Article 23 The traffic management department of a public security organ shall periodically review motor vehicle driving licenses in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 24 The traffic management department of a public security organ shall, in addition to imposing administrative penalties according to law for violations of road traffic safety laws and regulations by motor vehicle drivers, implement a cumulative points system. For motor vehicle drivers whose cumulative points reach the prescribed value, the traffic management department of a public security organ shall detain their motor vehicle driving licenses, provide them with education on road traffic safety laws and regulations, and require them to retake the examination; if they pass the examination, their motor vehicle driving licenses shall be returned.

For motor vehicle drivers who comply with road traffic safety laws and regulations and have no cumulative points within one year, the review period for their motor vehicle driving licenses may be extended. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the public security department of the State Council.

## Chapter III Road Traffic Conditions

Article 25 Unified road traffic signals are implemented nationwide.

Road traffic signals include traffic signal lights, traffic signs, traffic markings, and traffic police commands.

The installation of traffic signal lights, traffic signs, and traffic markings shall meet the requirements of road traffic safety and smoothness and national standards, and shall be kept clear, conspicuous, accurate, and intact.

Based on traffic needs, road traffic signals shall be added, adjusted, or updated in a timely manner. When adding, adjusting, or updating restrictive road traffic signals, the public shall be notified in advance, and extensive publicity shall be conducted.

Article 26 Traffic signal lights consist of red, green, and yellow lights. Red indicates prohibition of passage, green indicates permission to pass, and yellow indicates warning.

Article 27 At level crossings of railways and roads, warning lights, warning signs, or safety protection facilities shall be installed. At unattended railway crossings, warning signs shall be installed at a certain distance from the crossing.

Article 28 No unit or individual may unauthorizedly set up, move, occupy, or damage traffic signal lights, traffic signs, or traffic markings.

Trees or other plants planted on both sides of the road and on medians, as well as billboards, pipelines, etc., installed, shall maintain a necessary distance from traffic facilities, shall not obstruct streetlights, traffic signal lights, or traffic signs, shall not hinder safe sight distance, and shall not affect passage.

Article 29 The planning, design, and construction of roads, parking lots, and road supporting facilities shall meet the requirements of road traffic safety and smoothness and shall be adjusted in a timely manner based on traffic needs.

If the traffic management department of a public security organ discovers that a road already in use has sections with frequent traffic accidents, or that a parking lot or road supporting facility has serious traffic safety hazards, it shall promptly report to the local people's government and propose suggestions for preventing traffic accidents and eliminating hazards, and the local people's government shall make a decision on handling in a timely manner.

Article 30 If a road collapses, develops potholes, is washed out, heaves, or is otherwise damaged, or if traffic facilities such as traffic signal lights, traffic signs, or traffic markings are damaged or lost, the maintenance department or management department of the road or traffic facilities shall set up warning signs and repair them promptly.

If the traffic management department of a public security organ discovers the situation in the preceding paragraph, which endangers traffic safety and has not yet set up warning signs, it shall promptly take safety measures, direct traffic, and notify the maintenance department or management department of the road or traffic facilities.

Article 31 Without permission, no unit or individual may occupy the road for non-traffic activities.

Article 32 If it is necessary to occupy or excavate a road, or to erect or add pipeline facilities across or over a road due to engineering construction, prior consent shall be obtained from the road authority; if traffic safety is affected, consent shall also be obtained from the traffic management department of a public security organ.

The construction unit shall carry out construction within the approved road section and time period, set up conspicuous safety warning signs at a safe distance from the construction site in the direction of oncoming traffic, and take protective measures; after construction is completed, obstacles on the road shall be promptly removed, safety hazards eliminated, and passage may be resumed only after the road authority and the traffic management department of a public security organ have inspected and accepted the work, confirming that it meets traffic requirements.

For construction roads where traffic is not interrupted, the traffic management department of a public security organ shall strengthen traffic safety supervision and inspection to maintain road traffic order.

Article 33 For newly built, renovated, or expanded public buildings, commercial districts, residential areas, large (medium-sized) buildings, etc., parking lots shall be constructed or added; if parking spaces are insufficient, they shall be promptly renovated or expanded; parking lots that have been put into use shall not be arbitrarily stopped or used for other purposes.

Within urban road areas, government authorities may delineate parking spaces without affecting the passage of pedestrians and vehicles.

Article 34 On roads in front of schools, kindergartens, hospitals, and nursing homes without pedestrian crossing facilities, crosswalks shall be marked and warning signs shall be installed.

On sidewalks of major urban roads, tactile paving for the blind shall be installed according to planning. The installation of tactile paving for the blind shall comply with national standards.

## Chapter IV Provisions on Road Passage

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 35 Motor vehicles and non-motor vehicles shall keep to the right.

Article 36 Where roads are divided into motor vehicle lanes, non-motor vehicle lanes, and sidewalks based on road conditions and traffic needs, motor vehicles, non-motor vehicles, and pedestrians shall use separate lanes. Where roads are not divided into motor vehicle lanes, non-motor vehicle lanes, and sidewalks, motor vehicles shall travel in the middle of the road, and non-motor vehicles and pedestrians shall travel on both sides of the road.

Article 37 Where a road is marked with a dedicated lane, only prescribed vehicles are allowed to travel in the dedicated lane, and other vehicles shall not enter the dedicated lane.

Article 38 Vehicles and pedestrians shall pass in accordance with traffic signals; when a traffic police officer is directing on-site, they shall pass in accordance with the traffic police officer's command; on roads without traffic signals, they shall pass under the principle of ensuring safety and smoothness.

Article 39 The traffic management department of a public security organ may, based on specific road and traffic flow conditions, take measures such as directing, restricting passage, or prohibiting passage for motor vehicles, non-motor vehicles, and pedestrians. In cases such as large-scale public events or extensive construction that require traffic restriction measures or decisions directly related to public road traffic activities, the public shall be notified in advance.

Article 40 In cases of natural disasters, adverse weather conditions, major traffic accidents, or other situations that seriously affect traffic safety, where other measures are difficult to ensure traffic safety, the traffic management department of a public security organ may implement traffic control.

Article 41 Other specific provisions on road passage shall be prescribed by the State Council.

### Section 2 Provisions on Motor Vehicle Passage

Article 42 When driving a motor vehicle on the road, the maximum speed indicated by speed limit signs shall not be exceeded. On road sections without speed limit signs, a safe speed shall be maintained.

When driving at night, on road sections prone to danger, or under weather conditions such as sandstorms, hail, rain, snow, fog, or ice, the speed shall be reduced.

Article 43 When motor vehicles are traveling in the same lane, the following vehicle shall maintain a safe distance from the preceding vehicle sufficient to take emergency braking measures. Overtaking is prohibited in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The preceding vehicle is turning left, making a U-turn, or overtaking;

(2) There is a possibility of meeting oncoming traffic;

(3) The preceding vehicle is a police car, fire engine, ambulance, or engineering rescue vehicle performing emergency duties;

(4) Passing through railway crossings, intersections, narrow bridges, curves, steep slopes, tunnels, crosswalks, or sections with heavy urban traffic flow where overtaking conditions are not available.

Article 44 When passing through an intersection, a motor vehicle shall pass in accordance with traffic signal lights, traffic signs, traffic markings, or traffic police commands; when passing through an intersection without traffic signal lights, traffic signs, traffic markings, or traffic police commands, the vehicle shall slow down and give way to pedestrians and vehicles with priority.

Article 45 When a motor vehicle encounters a queue of vehicles ahead waiting or moving slowly, it shall not use another lane to overtake or occupy the opposite lane, and shall not cut into the queue of waiting vehicles.

On road sections or at intersections with reduced lanes, or at intersections without traffic signal lights, traffic signs, traffic markings, or traffic police commands where vehicles are queuing or moving slowly, motor vehicles shall alternate in passing.Article 46: When crossing a railway crossing, a motor vehicle shall proceed in accordance with traffic signals or the directions of traffic management personnel. Where there are no traffic signals or traffic management personnel, the vehicle shall slow down or stop, and only cross after confirming it is safe.

Article 47: When approaching a pedestrian crosswalk, a motor vehicle shall slow down. If pedestrians are crossing the crosswalk, the vehicle shall stop and yield.

When a motor vehicle travels on a road without traffic signals and encounters pedestrians crossing the road, it shall yield.

Article 48: The load carried by a motor vehicle shall not exceed the approved load capacity; overloading is strictly prohibited. The length, width, and height of the load shall comply with loading requirements, and the load shall not be allowed to leak or scatter.

When a motor vehicle transports indivisible oversized articles that may affect traffic safety, it shall travel at the time, on the route, and at the speed designated by the traffic management department of the public security organ, and display conspicuous markings. When transporting such articles on a highway, the provisions of the Highway Law shall also be followed.

When a motor vehicle transports explosive materials, flammable or explosive chemicals, or dangerous goods such as highly toxic or radioactive substances, it must obtain approval from the public security organ, travel at the designated time, on the designated route, and at the designated speed, display warning signs, and take necessary safety measures.

Article 49: The number of passengers carried by a motor vehicle shall not exceed the approved number. Passenger motor vehicles shall not carry cargo in violation of regulations.

Article 50: Freight motor vehicles are prohibited from carrying passengers.

If a freight motor vehicle needs to carry accompanying workers, safety measures to protect those workers shall be installed.

Article 51: When a motor vehicle is in motion, the driver and passengers shall use seat belts as required. Motorcycle drivers and passengers shall wear safety helmets as required.

Article 52: If a motor vehicle breaks down on the road and needs to stop for repairs, the driver shall immediately turn on the hazard warning lights, move the vehicle to a place that does not obstruct traffic. If it is difficult to move, the driver shall keep the hazard warning lights on continuously, set up warning signs in the direction of oncoming traffic to increase the warning distance, and promptly call for emergency assistance if necessary.

Article 53: When police cars, fire engines, ambulances, or engineering rescue vehicles are on emergency missions, they may use sirens and warning lights. Provided safety is ensured, they are not restricted by travel routes, directions, speeds, or traffic signals. Other vehicles and pedestrians shall yield.

When police cars, fire engines, ambulances, or engineering rescue vehicles are not on emergency missions, they shall not use sirens or warning lights and do not have the right of way as stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

Article 54: When road maintenance vehicles or engineering operation vehicles are working, their travel routes and directions are not restricted by traffic signs or markings, provided they do not hinder the passage of other vehicles. Passing vehicles and personnel shall be cautious and yield.

Sprinkler trucks, street sweepers, and similar motor vehicles shall operate according to safety operation standards. Provided they do not hinder the passage of other vehicles, they may be exempt from lane-specific travel restrictions, but they shall not drive in the wrong direction.

Article 55: Tractors are prohibited from traveling on expressways and roads within the central urban areas of large and medium-sized cities. Other roads where tractors are prohibited shall be designated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government based on local conditions.

On roads where tractors are permitted, they may be used for freight transport but shall not carry passengers.

Article 56: Motor vehicles shall be parked in designated locations. Parking on sidewalks is prohibited, except in parking spaces marked in accordance with Article 33 of this Law.

Temporary parking on the road shall not obstruct the passage of other vehicles or pedestrians.

### Section 3: Rules for Non-Motor Vehicles

Article 57: When driving a non-motor vehicle on the road, the driver shall comply with relevant traffic safety regulations. Non-motor vehicles shall travel in non-motor vehicle lanes. On roads without non-motor vehicle lanes, they shall travel on the right side of the roadway.

Article 58: When motorized wheelchairs for the disabled and electric bicycles travel in non-motor vehicle lanes, their maximum speed shall not exceed 15 kilometers per hour.

Article 59: Non-motor vehicles shall be parked in designated locations. Where no designated parking locations exist, parking shall not obstruct the passage of other vehicles or pedestrians.

Article 60: When driving an animal-drawn cart, tame animals shall be used. When crossing the road with an animal-drawn cart, the driver shall dismount and lead the animal. When leaving the vehicle, the driver shall tether the animal.

### Section 4: Rules for Pedestrians and Passengers

Article 61: Pedestrians shall walk on sidewalks. Where there are no sidewalks, they shall walk along the roadside.

Article 62: When crossing an intersection or a road, pedestrians shall use crosswalks or pedestrian overpasses/underpasses. When crossing a crosswalk with traffic lights, they shall follow the traffic light signals. When crossing an intersection without traffic lights or a crosswalk, or crossing a road section without pedestrian facilities, they shall cross only after confirming it is safe.

Article 63: Pedestrians shall not climb over or sit on road barriers, hitchhike, forcibly stop vehicles, or engage in other acts that obstruct road traffic safety.

Article 64: Preschool children and individuals with mental illness or intellectual disabilities who cannot recognize or control their actions shall be accompanied by their guardians, persons entrusted by their guardians, or persons responsible for their care and protection when on the road.

Blind persons shall use a white cane or other guiding aids when on the road. Vehicles shall yield to blind persons.

Article 65: When crossing a railway crossing, pedestrians shall proceed in accordance with traffic signals or the directions of traffic management personnel. Where there are no traffic signals or traffic management personnel, they shall cross quickly after confirming that no train is approaching.

Article 66: Passengers shall not carry flammable or explosive dangerous goods, throw objects out of the vehicle, or engage in behavior that affects the driver's safe driving.

### Section 5: Special Provisions for Expressways

Article 67: Pedestrians, non-motor vehicles, tractors, wheeled specialized machinery vehicles, articulated buses, full-trailer trucks, and other motor vehicles with a designed maximum speed of less than 70 kilometers per hour are prohibited from entering expressways. The maximum speed indicated on expressway speed limit signs shall not exceed 120 kilometers per hour.

Article 68: If a motor vehicle breaks down on an expressway, the provisions of Article 52 of this Law shall apply. However, warning signs shall be placed at least 150 meters behind the broken-down vehicle in the direction of oncoming traffic. Occupants shall quickly move to the right shoulder or emergency lane and promptly call for assistance.

If a motor vehicle breaks down or is involved in a traffic accident on an expressway and cannot be driven normally, it shall be towed or pulled by a rescue or recovery vehicle.

Article 69: No unit or individual may stop or inspect moving vehicles on an expressway, except when the people's police of the public security organ are performing emergency official duties in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 5: Handling of Traffic Accidents

Article 70: In the event of a traffic accident on the road, the vehicle driver shall immediately stop the vehicle and protect the scene. If personal injury or death occurs, the driver shall immediately rescue the injured and promptly report to the traffic police on duty or the traffic management department of the public security organ. If the scene is altered due to rescuing the injured, the location shall be marked. Passengers, passing vehicle drivers, and passing pedestrians shall provide assistance.

If a traffic accident occurs on the road without causing personal injury or death, and the parties have no dispute over the facts and causes, they may immediately leave the scene, restore traffic, and negotiate compensation for damages on their own. If they do not immediately leave the scene, they shall promptly report to the traffic police on duty or the traffic management department of the public security organ.

If a traffic accident occurs on the road causing only minor property damage and the basic facts are clear, the parties shall first leave the scene and then negotiate the settlement.

Article 71: If a vehicle flees the scene after a traffic accident, eyewitnesses and other knowledgeable persons shall report to the traffic management department of the public security organ or traffic police. If the report is verified, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall give a reward.

Article 72: Upon receiving a report of a traffic accident, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall immediately dispatch traffic police to the scene, first organize the rescue of the injured, and take measures to restore traffic as soon as possible.

Traffic police shall inspect and examine the accident scene and collect evidence. If necessary for evidence collection, they may impound the accident vehicle, but it shall be properly kept for verification.

For professional examinations such as the physiological or mental state of the parties, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall entrust a specialized agency to conduct an appraisal. The appraisal conclusion shall be signed by the appraiser.

Article 73: The traffic management department of the public security organ shall, based on the inspection, examination, investigation, and relevant appraisal conclusions of the traffic accident scene, promptly prepare a traffic accident identification report as evidence for handling the accident. The report shall state the basic facts, causes, and responsibilities of the parties and be served to the parties.

Article 74: For disputes over compensation for damages from a traffic accident, the parties may request mediation by the traffic management department of the public security organ or directly file a civil lawsuit with the people's court.

If the parties fail to reach an agreement through mediation by the traffic management department of the public security organ, or if the mediation agreement is not performed after taking effect, the parties may file a civil lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 75: Medical institutions shall promptly rescue the injured in traffic accidents and shall not delay treatment due to the failure to pay rescue fees in a timely manner. If the accident vehicle is covered by compulsory motor vehicle third-party liability insurance, the insurance company shall pay the rescue fees within the liability limit. If the rescue fees exceed the liability limit, or if the vehicle is not covered by such insurance or the driver flees after the accident, the Road Traffic Accident Social Assistance Fund shall advance part or all of the rescue fees. The management agency of the fund has the right to recover the fees from the person responsible for the accident.

Article 76: If a motor vehicle traffic accident causes personal injury or death or property damage, the insurance company shall compensate within the liability limit of the compulsory motor vehicle third-party liability insurance. For the portion exceeding the limit, liability shall be borne as follows:

(1) In accidents between motor vehicles, the party at fault shall bear liability. If both parties are at fault, liability shall be shared proportionally based on their respective faults.

(2) In accidents between a motor vehicle and a non-motor vehicle driver or pedestrian, if the non-motor vehicle driver or pedestrian is not at fault, the motor vehicle party shall bear liability. If evidence shows the non-motor vehicle driver or pedestrian is at fault, the motor vehicle party's liability shall be appropriately reduced based on the degree of fault. If the motor vehicle party is not at fault, it shall bear no more than 10% of the liability.

If the loss from a traffic accident is caused by the non-motor vehicle driver or pedestrian intentionally colliding with the motor vehicle, the motor vehicle party shall not bear liability.

Article 77: For accidents occurring when a vehicle is traveling off-road, if the traffic management department of the public security organ receives a report, it shall handle the matter with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

## Chapter 6: Law Enforcement Supervision

Article 78: The traffic management department of the public security organ shall strengthen the management of traffic police, improve their quality, and enhance their ability to manage road traffic.

The traffic management department of the public security organ shall provide legal and traffic safety management training and assessments for traffic police. Traffic police who fail the assessment shall not perform their duties.

Article 79: The traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police shall implement road traffic safety management in accordance with statutory powers and procedures, simplify procedures, and ensure fairness, strictness, civility, and efficiency.

Article 80: When performing duties, traffic police shall wear uniforms as required, display people's police insignia, carry people's police identification, maintain a neat appearance, behave with dignity, and give commands in a standardized manner.

Article 81: Fees charged for issuing licenses and plates under this Law shall strictly follow the fee standards approved by the price department of the State Council and shall be fully turned over to the state treasury.

Article 82: When the traffic management department of the public security organ imposes administrative penalties of fines in accordance with the law, it shall implement the separation of fine decisions and fine collection as required by relevant laws and administrative regulations. The fines collected and the illegal gains confiscated in accordance with the law shall be fully turned over to the state treasury.

Article 83: Traffic police investigating and handling road traffic safety violations and traffic accidents shall recuse themselves in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party;

(2) Having a personal interest in the case themselves or through a close relative;

(3) Having another relationship with a party to the case that may affect the fair handling of the case.

Article 84: The administrative law enforcement activities of the traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police shall be subject to supervision by administrative supervision organs in accordance with the law.

The internal supervision department of the public security organ shall supervise the traffic management department and its traffic police in their enforcement of laws and regulations and compliance with discipline in accordance with the law.

The higher-level traffic management department of the public security organ shall supervise the law enforcement activities of the lower-level traffic management department.

Article 85: The traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police shall consciously accept supervision from society and citizens when performing their duties.

Any unit or individual has the right to report or accuse the traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police of lax law enforcement or illegal and disciplinary acts. The organ receiving the report or accusation shall promptly investigate and handle it according to its duties.

Article 86: No unit shall assign or indirectly assign fine quotas to the traffic management department of the public security organ. The traffic management department shall not use the amount of fines as a criterion for assessing traffic police.

The traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police have the right to refuse to execute instructions that exceed the scope of laws and regulations and shall simultaneously report to the higher-level authority.

## Chapter 7: Legal Liability

Article 87: The traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police shall promptly correct road traffic safety violations.

The traffic management department of the public security organ and its traffic police shall impose penalties for road traffic safety violations based on facts and the relevant provisions of this Law. For minor violations that do not affect road traffic, the violation shall be pointed out, a verbal warning given, and the person allowed to proceed.

Article 88: Types of penalties for road traffic safety violations include: warnings, fines, temporary seizure or revocation of motor vehicle driving licenses, and detention.

Article 89: Pedestrians, passengers, or non-motor vehicle drivers who violate road traffic safety laws and regulations regarding road passage shall be given a warning or fined not less than 5 yuan but not more than 50 yuan. If a non-motor vehicle driver refuses to accept the fine, the non-motor vehicle may be impounded.

Article 90: Motor vehicle drivers who violate road traffic safety laws and regulations regarding road passage shall be given a warning or fined not less than 20 yuan but not more than 200 yuan. If other provisions of this Law apply, those provisions shall govern.

Article 91: Anyone who drives a motor vehicle after consuming alcohol shall have their motor vehicle driving license temporarily seized for six months and be fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan. If punished for driving after consuming alcohol and then drives again after consuming alcohol, they shall be detained for not more than 10 days, fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan, and have their motor vehicle driving license revoked.

Anyone who drives a motor vehicle while intoxicated shall be restrained by the traffic management department of the public security organ until sober, have their motor vehicle driving license revoked, and be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law. They shall not reapply for a motor vehicle driving license within five years.

Anyone who drives a commercial motor vehicle after consuming alcohol shall be detained for 15 days, fined 5,000 yuan, have their motor vehicle driving license revoked, and shall not reapply for a motor vehicle driving license within five years.

Anyone who drives a commercial motor vehicle while intoxicated shall be restrained by the traffic management department of the public security organ until sober, have their motor vehicle driving license revoked, and be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law. They shall not reapply for a motor vehicle driving license within ten years. After reapplying, they shall not drive commercial motor vehicles.

If a major traffic accident occurs due to driving after consuming alcohol or while intoxicated, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law, and the traffic management department of the public security organ shall revoke the motor vehicle driving license, with no possibility of reapplying for life.

Article 92: If a highway passenger vehicle carries passengers exceeding the approved number, the driver shall be fined not less than 200 yuan but not more than 500 yuan. If the excess exceeds 20% of the approved number or cargo is carried in violation of regulations, the driver shall be fined not less than 500 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan.

If a freight motor vehicle exceeds the approved load capacity, the driver shall be fined not less than 200 yuan but not more than 500 yuan. If the excess exceeds 30% of the approved load capacity or passengers are carried in violation of regulations, the driver shall be fined not less than 500 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan.

For the acts in the preceding two paragraphs, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall impound the motor vehicle until the violation is rectified.

If a transport unit's vehicle falls under the circumstances in paragraphs 1 or 2 of this Article and is penalized but fails to correct, the directly responsible supervisor shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan.

Article 93: For violations of road traffic safety laws and regulations regarding motor vehicle parking or temporary stopping, the violation may be pointed out, a verbal warning given, and the driver ordered to leave immediately.

If the motor vehicle driver is not present or is present but refuses to leave immediately, obstructing the passage of other vehicles or pedestrians, the driver shall be fined not less than 20 yuan but not more than 200 yuan, and the motor vehicle may be towed to a location that does not obstruct traffic or to a location designated by the traffic management department of the public security organ. The traffic management department shall not charge the party for towing and shall promptly inform the party of the parking location.

If the motor vehicle is damaged due to improper towing methods, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 94: If a motor vehicle safety technical inspection agency charges fees exceeding the standards approved by the price department of the State Council, the excess fees shall be refunded, and the price department shall impose penalties in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Price Law of the People's Republic of China.

If a motor vehicle safety technical inspection agency fails to conduct inspections in accordance with national motor vehicle safety technical standards and issues false inspection results, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall impose a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the inspection fees collected and revoke its inspection qualification in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 95: If a motor vehicle traveling on the road does not display a motor vehicle license plate, inspection eligibility mark, or insurance mark, or does not carry the vehicle registration certificate or driving license, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall impound the motor vehicle, notify the party to provide the corresponding plates, marks, or go through the corresponding procedures, and may impose penalties in accordance with Article 90 of this Law. If the party provides the corresponding plates, marks, or completes the procedures, the motor vehicle shall be promptly returned.
If a motor vehicle license plate is intentionally obscured, defaced, or not installed in accordance with regulations, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with Article 90 of this Law.

Article 96: Anyone who forges, alters, or uses forged or altered motor vehicle registration certificates, license plates, vehicle registration certificates, or driving licenses shall have them confiscated by the traffic management department of the public security organ, the motor vehicle impounded, and be detained for not more than 15 days and fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.Forging, altering, or using forged or altered inspection or insurance marks shall result in confiscation by the traffic management department of the public security organ, detention of the motor vehicle, detention for up to ten days, and a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 3,000 yuan; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Using another vehicle's motor vehicle registration certificate, license plate, driving permit, inspection mark, or insurance mark shall result in confiscation by the traffic management department of the public security organ, detention of the motor vehicle, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan.

If the party provides the corresponding legal proof or completes the relevant procedures, the motor vehicle shall be promptly returned.

Article 97: Unauthorized installation of alarms or signal lamps shall result in forced removal and confiscation by the traffic management department of the public security organ, along with a fine of not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan.

Article 98: If the owner or manager of a motor vehicle fails to purchase compulsory third-party liability insurance in accordance with national regulations, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall detain the vehicle until insurance is purchased as required, and impose a fine equal to twice the minimum liability limit premium payable according to regulations.

All fines paid under the preceding paragraph shall be fully allocated to the road traffic accident social relief fund. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 99: Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be fined not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan by the traffic management department of the public security organ:

(1) Driving a motor vehicle without a motor vehicle driver's license, while the license is revoked, or during a period of suspension;
(2) Allowing a person who does not have a motor vehicle driver's license, or whose license has been revoked or suspended, to drive a motor vehicle;
(3) Fleeing after causing a traffic accident, if the act does not constitute a crime;
(4) Driving a motor vehicle at a speed exceeding the prescribed limit by 50 percent;
(5) Forcing a motor vehicle driver to drive in violation of road traffic safety laws, regulations, and safe driving requirements, causing a traffic accident that does not constitute a crime;
(6) Forcing passage in violation of traffic control regulations despite warnings;
(7) Intentionally damaging, moving, or altering traffic facilities, causing harmful consequences that do not constitute a crime;
(8) Illegally intercepting or detaining motor vehicles despite warnings, causing severe traffic congestion or significant property loss.

If the actor commits any of the acts specified in items (2) or (4) of the preceding paragraph, the motor vehicle driver's license may also be revoked; if the actor commits any of the acts specified in items (1), (3), or (5) through (8), detention for up to 15 days may also be imposed.

Article 100: Driving a motor vehicle that is illegally assembled or has reached the mandatory scrapping standard on the road shall result in confiscation and mandatory scrapping by the traffic management department of the public security organ.

The driver of a motor vehicle listed in the preceding paragraph shall be fined not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan, and the motor vehicle driver's license shall be revoked.

Anyone who sells a motor vehicle that has reached the mandatory scrapping standard shall have the illegal gains confiscated, be fined an amount equal to the sales amount, and the motor vehicle shall be dealt with according to the provisions of paragraph 1 of this article.

Article 101: Violating road traffic safety laws and regulations, causing a major traffic accident that constitutes a crime, shall result in criminal liability being pursued according to law, and the motor vehicle driver's license shall be revoked by the traffic management department of the public security organ.

Anyone who flees after causing a traffic accident shall have their motor vehicle driver's license revoked by the traffic management department of the public security organ, and they shall never be allowed to reapply for a motor vehicle driver's license.

Article 102: For a professional transport unit that bears primary or full responsibility for two or more major traffic accidents within six months, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall order the elimination of safety hazards; motor vehicles with unresolved safety hazards shall be prohibited from driving on the road.

Article 103: If the national motor vehicle product authority fails to strictly review according to national motor vehicle safety technical standards and permits non-conforming motor vehicle models to be put into production, the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions of demotion or removal from office.

If a motor vehicle manufacturer, for a model permitted by the national motor vehicle product authority, fails to implement national motor vehicle safety technical standards or strictly conduct quality inspections on finished motor vehicles, resulting in the sale of substandard motor vehicles, the quality and technical supervision department shall impose penalties in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China.

Unauthorized production or sale of motor vehicle models not permitted by the national motor vehicle product authority shall result in confiscation of illegally produced or sold motor vehicles and parts, and a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the value of the illegal products may also be imposed; if the entity has a business license, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke the license; if there is no license, the entity shall be sealed up.

Production or sale of illegally assembled motor vehicles, or production or sale of unauthorized modified motor vehicles, shall be punished according to the provisions of paragraph 3 of this article.

If the illegal acts listed in paragraphs 2, 3, or 4 of this article involve the production or sale of motor vehicles that do not meet national motor vehicle safety technical standards and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 104: Unauthorized excavation of roads, occupation of roads for construction, or engagement in other activities affecting road traffic safety shall be ordered by the road authority to cease the illegal act and restore the original condition, and a fine may be imposed according to law; if the act causes losses to persons, vehicles, or other property passing through, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

If the act in the preceding paragraph affects road traffic safety activities, the traffic management department of the public security organ may order the cessation of the illegal act and the rapid restoration of traffic.

Article 105: If, during road construction or when road damage occurs, warning signs are not promptly set up, protective measures are not taken, or traffic signals, traffic signs, or traffic markings that should be installed are not installed, or should be promptly changed but are not, causing losses to persons, vehicles, or other property passing through, the unit with relevant responsibilities shall bear compensation liability according to law.

Article 106: Planting trees or other plants, or installing billboards, pipelines, etc., on both sides of the road or on the median strip, that block streetlights, traffic signals, or traffic signs, or obstruct safe sight distance, shall result in an order from the traffic management department of the public security organ for the actor to remove the obstruction; if the order is not complied with, a fine of not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the obstruction shall be forcibly removed, with the costs borne by the actor.

Article 107: For a road traffic violator who is given a warning or a fine of not more than 200 yuan, a traffic police officer may make an administrative penalty decision on the spot and issue a written administrative penalty decision.

The written administrative penalty decision shall state the violator's illegal facts, the basis for the administrative penalty, the penalty content, time, location, and the name of the penalty authority, and shall be signed or sealed by the law enforcement officer.

Article 108: The party shall pay the fine at the designated bank within 15 days from the date of receiving the written administrative penalty decision for the fine.

For fines imposed on pedestrians, passengers, and non-motor vehicle drivers, if the party has no objection, the fine may be collected on the spot.

A fine receipt uniformly printed by the financial department of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be issued; if such a receipt is not issued, the party has the right to refuse to pay the fine.

Article 109: If the party fails to comply with the administrative penalty decision within the time limit, the administrative authority that made the decision may take the following measures:

(1) For overdue non-payment of the fine, an additional fine of 3 percent of the fine amount per day shall be imposed;
(2) Apply to the People's Court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 110: If a traffic police officer on duty believes that a road traffic violator should be given a penalty of suspension or revocation of the motor vehicle driver's license, the officer may first detain the motor vehicle driver's license and transfer the case to the traffic management department of the public security organ within 24 hours for handling.

The road traffic violator shall appear before the traffic management department of the public security organ within 15 days for processing. If the violator fails to appear without justifiable reasons within the time limit, the motor vehicle driver's license shall be revoked.

If the traffic management department of the public security organ suspends or revokes a motor vehicle driver's license, it shall issue a written administrative penalty decision.

Article 111: Administrative penalties of detention imposed for violations of this Law shall be decided by the county or city public security bureau, public security sub-bureau, or an equivalent county-level public security organ.

Article 112: When detaining a motor vehicle or non-motor vehicle, the traffic management department of the public security organ shall issue a receipt on the spot and inform the party to appear before the traffic management department of the public security organ for processing within the specified time limit.

The traffic management department of the public security organ shall properly keep the detained vehicles and shall not use them.

If the party fails to appear for processing within the time limit and, after a public announcement of three months, still fails to appear, the detained vehicle shall be dealt with according to law.

Article 113: The period of suspension of a motor vehicle driver's license shall be calculated from the date the penalty decision takes effect; if the motor vehicle driver's license was detained before the penalty decision takes effect, one day of detention shall offset one day of the suspension period.

The period for reapplying for a motor vehicle driver's license after revocation shall be handled in accordance with the regulations on motor vehicle driver's license management.

Article 114: Based on traffic technical monitoring records, the traffic management department of the public security organ may impose penalties on the owner or manager of a motor vehicle that violates the law. If the driver can be identified, penalties may be imposed according to the provisions of this Law.

Article 115: A traffic police officer who commits any of the following acts shall be given administrative sanctions according to law:

(1) Issuing motor vehicle registration certificates, license plates, driving permits, or inspection marks to motor vehicles that do not meet legal conditions;
(2) Approving the installation or use of alarms, signal lamps, or the painting of markings on police cars, fire trucks, ambulances, or engineering rescue vehicles that do not meet legal conditions;
(3) Issuing motor vehicle driver's licenses to persons who do not meet driving permit conditions, have not taken exams, or have failed exams;
(4) Failing to implement the system of separating fine decisions from fine collection, or failing to fully turn over to the state treasury fees collected according to law, fines collected, and illegal gains confiscated;
(5) Organizing or participating in the operation of driving schools or training classes, motor vehicle repair shops, or paid parking lots;
(6) Using official position to accept property from others or seek other benefits;
(7) Illegally detaining vehicles, motor vehicle driving permits, driver's licenses, or vehicle license plates;
(8) Using vehicles detained according to law;
(9) Collecting fines on the spot without issuing a fine receipt or not truthfully filling in the fine amount;
(10) Engaging in malpractice for personal gain and failing to handle traffic accidents fairly;
(11) Deliberately creating difficulties or delaying the processing of motor vehicle license plates;
(12) Using alarms or signal lamps when not on emergency duty;
(13) Illegally intercepting or inspecting normally moving vehicles;
(14) Intercepting or hitchhiking in motor vehicles when not on emergency official business;
(15) Failing to perform statutory duties.

If the traffic management department of the public security organ commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given corresponding administrative sanctions.

Article 116: If administrative sanctions are to be imposed on a traffic police officer in accordance with Article 115 of this Law, the officer may be suspended from duty before the decision on administrative sanctions is made; if necessary, the officer may be confined.

If a traffic police officer receives administrative sanctions of demotion or removal from office in accordance with Article 115 of this Law, the officer may be dismissed.

If a traffic police officer is expelled or dismissed, the police rank shall be canceled; if a traffic police officer receives administrative sanctions below removal from office, the police rank shall be reduced.

Article 117: If a traffic police officer uses official authority to illegally occupy public property, extort or accept bribes, or abuses power or neglects duty, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 118: If the traffic management department of the public security organ or its traffic police officers commit any of the acts listed in Article 115 of this Law, causing losses to the party, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 119: The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Road" refers to highways, urban roads, and places within the jurisdiction of a unit that allow social motor vehicles to pass, including squares, public parking lots, and other places used for public passage.
(2) "Vehicle" refers to motor vehicles and non-motor vehicles.
(3) "Motor vehicle" refers to wheeled vehicles driven or towed by power devices, used on roads for passenger transport, cargo transport, or special engineering operations.
(4) "Non-motor vehicle" refers to vehicles driven by human or animal power, used on roads, as well as mobility scooters for the disabled, electric bicycles, and other vehicles that, although driven by power devices, have a design maximum speed, curb weight, and external dimensions that comply with relevant national standards.
(5) "Traffic accident" refers to an event involving personal injury or death or property loss caused by fault or accident on the road involving vehicles.

Article 120: The issuance of motor vehicle license plates and driving permits, the inspection of motor vehicles in active service, and the examination of motor vehicle drivers for the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be the responsibility of the relevant departments of the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force.

Article 121: For tractors driven on roads, the agricultural (agricultural machinery) authority shall exercise the management functions of the traffic management department of the public security organ as specified in Articles 8, 9, 13, 19, and 23 of this Law.

When exercising functions in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the agricultural (agricultural machinery) authority shall comply with the relevant provisions of this Law and accept supervision from the traffic management department of the public security organ; violations shall be subject to legal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Motor vehicle license plates and driving permits issued by the agricultural (agricultural machinery) authority before the implementation of this Law shall remain valid after the implementation of this Law.

Article 122: The state implements unified management of road traffic safety for foreign motor vehicles entering the country.

Article 123: The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local conditions, specify specific enforcement standards within the fine range provided for in this Law.

Article 124: This Law shall take effect as of May 1, 2004.
