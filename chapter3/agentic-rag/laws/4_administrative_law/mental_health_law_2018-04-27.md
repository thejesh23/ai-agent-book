# Mental Health Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of developing mental health services, regulating mental health care, and safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of persons with mental disorders.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities carried out within the territory of the People's Republic of China to maintain and enhance the mental health of citizens, prevent and treat mental disorders, and promote the rehabilitation of persons with mental disorders.

Article 3 Mental health work shall implement the policy of prevention first, adhering to the principle of combining prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation.

Article 4 The personal dignity, personal safety, and property safety of persons with mental disorders shall not be violated.

The legitimate rights and interests of persons with mental disorders in terms of education, employment, medical care, and material assistance from the State and society are protected by law.

Relevant entities and individuals shall keep confidential the names, portraits, addresses, work units, medical records, and other information of persons with mental disorders that may identify them; except where disclosure is necessary for the performance of duties in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The whole society shall respect, understand, and care for persons with mental disorders.

No organization or individual may discriminate against, insult, or abuse persons with mental disorders, or illegally restrict their personal freedom.

News reports and literary and artistic works shall not contain content that discriminates against or insults persons with mental disorders.

Article 6 Mental health work shall implement a comprehensive management mechanism featuring government organization and leadership, departments assuming their respective responsibilities, families and work units doing their utmost, and the whole society participating together.

Article 7 People's governments at or above the county level shall lead mental health work, incorporate it into national economic and social development plans, establish and improve the prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation service system for mental disorders, establish and perfect the coordination mechanism and work responsibility system for mental health work, and assess and supervise the mental health work undertaken by relevant departments.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices shall, based on the actual conditions of their respective regions, organize and carry out work such as preventing the occurrence of mental disorders and promoting the rehabilitation of persons with mental disorders.

Article 8 The health administrative department of the State Council shall be in charge of national mental health work. The health administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of mental health work within their respective administrative regions.

The judicial administration, civil affairs, public security, education, medical security, and other departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for relevant mental health work within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 9 Guardians of persons with mental disorders shall fulfill their guardianship duties and safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of persons with mental disorders.

Domestic violence against persons with mental disorders is prohibited, and abandoning persons with mental disorders is prohibited.

Article 10 The China Disabled Persons' Federation and its local organizations shall, in accordance with laws and regulations or upon entrustment by the government, mobilize social forces to carry out mental health work.

Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall carry out mental health work in accordance with the provisions of this Law and assist the local people's government in its mental health work.

The State encourages and supports trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, the Red Cross Society, science and technology associations, and other organizations to carry out mental health work in accordance with the law.

Article 11 The State encourages and supports the training of specialized mental health personnel, safeguards the legitimate rights and interests of mental health workers, and strengthens the construction of the professional mental health workforce.

The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research in mental health, develops modern medicine, traditional Chinese medicine, and psychology, and raises the scientific and technological level of the prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of mental disorders.

The State encourages and supports international exchanges and cooperation in the field of mental health.

Article 12 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to encourage and support organizations and individuals to provide mental health volunteer services, donate to mental health causes, and build public mental health facilities.

Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to mental health work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II Mental Health Promotion and Prevention of Mental Disorders

Article 13 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to strengthen mental health promotion and the prevention of mental disorders, and improve the level of public mental health.

Article 14 Emergency response plans formulated by people's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall include psychological assistance content. When an emergency occurs, the people's government exercising unified leadership responsibility or organizing the handling of the emergency shall, based on the specific circumstances of the emergency and in accordance with the provisions of the emergency response plan, organize and carry out psychological assistance work.

Article 15 Employers shall create a working environment conducive to the physical and mental health of employees and pay attention to their mental health; for employees in specific periods of career development or in special positions, targeted mental health education shall be provided.

Article 16 Schools at all levels and of all types shall provide mental health knowledge education to students; equip or employ mental health education teachers and counselors, and may establish mental health counseling rooms to provide mental health education to students. Preschool education institutions shall provide mental health education suitable for the characteristics of young children.

When events such as natural disasters, accidental injuries, or public security incidents that may affect the mental health of students occur, schools shall promptly organize professionals to provide psychological assistance to students.

Teachers shall study and understand relevant mental health knowledge, pay attention to the mental health status of students, and correctly guide and motivate students. The education administrative departments of local people's governments at all levels and schools shall pay attention to the mental health of teachers.

Schools and teachers shall communicate with the parents or other guardians, and close relatives of students regarding the mental health status of students.

Article 17 When providing disease diagnosis and treatment services, medical personnel shall provide mental health guidance to patients in accordance with the requirements of diagnostic standards and treatment norms; if they find that a patient may have a mental disorder, they shall recommend that the patient seek medical treatment at a medical institution that meets the requirements of this Law.

Article 18 Prisons, detention centers, administrative detention centers, compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation centers, and other such places shall publicize mental health knowledge to persons serving sentences, persons detained, arrested, or undergoing compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation in accordance with the law, pay attention to their mental health status, and provide psychological counseling and psychological guidance when necessary.

Article 19 The human resources and social security, education, health, judicial administration, public security, and other departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective scopes of duties, supervise and guide the entities specified in Articles 15 to 18 of this Law in fulfilling their obligations for the prevention of mental disorders.

Article 20 Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall assist the local people's government and its relevant departments in carrying out community mental health guidance and mental health knowledge publicity and education activities, and create a community environment conducive to the physical and mental health of residents.

Township health centers or community health service institutions shall provide technical guidance to villagers' committees and residents' committees in carrying out community mental health guidance and mental health knowledge publicity and education activities.

Article 21 Family members shall care for each other, create a good and harmonious family environment, and raise awareness of the prevention of mental disorders; if a family member is found to possibly have a mental disorder, they shall help the person seek medical treatment promptly, take care of their daily life, and provide proper care management.

Article 22 The State encourages and supports news media and social organizations to carry out public interest publicity on mental health, popularize mental health knowledge, guide the public to pay attention to mental health, and prevent the occurrence of mental disorders.

Article 23 Psychological counselors shall improve their professional competence, abide by professional norms, and provide professional psychological counseling services to the public.

Psychological counselors shall not engage in psychotherapy or the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders.

If a psychological counselor finds that a person receiving counseling may have a mental disorder, they shall recommend that the person seek medical treatment at a medical institution that meets the requirements of this Law.

Psychological counselors shall respect the privacy of persons receiving counseling and keep their secrets confidential.

Article 24 The health administrative department of the State Council shall establish a mental health monitoring network, implement a reporting system for the onset of severe mental disorders, and organize monitoring and special investigations into the occurrence status and development trends of mental disorders. The administrative measures for mental health monitoring and the reporting of severe mental disorder onset shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

The health administrative department of the State Council shall, together with relevant departments and organizations, establish an information sharing mechanism for mental health work to achieve information interconnection, exchange, and sharing.

## Chapter III Diagnosis and Treatment of Mental Disorders

Article 25 To carry out activities for the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders, the following conditions must be met, and relevant procedures shall be handled in accordance with the administrative regulations for medical institutions:

(1) Having psychiatrists and nurses with practicing qualifications appropriate for the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders to be carried out;
(2) Having facilities and equipment sufficient to meet the needs of the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders to be carried out;
(3) Having a sound management system and quality monitoring system for the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders.

Specialized medical institutions engaged in the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders shall also be equipped with personnel engaged in psychotherapy.

Article 26 The diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders shall follow the principles of safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of patients and respecting their personal dignity, ensuring that patients receive good mental health services under existing conditions.

The classification, diagnostic criteria, and treatment norms for mental disorders shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 27 The diagnosis of a mental disorder shall be based on the mental health status.

Unless otherwise provided by law, medical examinations to determine whether a person has a mental disorder shall not be conducted against the person's will.

Article 28 Except for individuals who go to a medical institution on their own for diagnosis of a mental disorder, close relatives of a person suspected of having a mental disorder may take the person to a medical institution for diagnosis. For vagrants and beggars suspected of having a mental disorder whose close relatives cannot be found, the local civil affairs and other relevant departments shall, according to their respective duties, assist in taking the person to a medical institution for diagnosis.

If a person suspected of having a mental disorder engages in behavior that harms themselves or endangers the safety of others, or is at risk of harming themselves or endangering the safety of others, their close relatives, work unit, and local public security organ shall immediately take measures to stop the behavior and take the person to a medical institution for diagnosis.

A medical institution that receives a person suspected of having a mental disorder brought for diagnosis shall not refuse to make a diagnosis.

Article 29 The diagnosis of a mental disorder shall be made by a psychiatrist with practicing qualifications.

When a medical institution receives a person suspected of having a mental disorder brought for diagnosis in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 28 of this Law, it shall admit the person to the hospital, immediately assign a psychiatrist with practicing qualifications to make a diagnosis, and promptly issue a diagnostic conclusion.

Article 30 Hospitalization for the treatment of mental disorders shall follow the principle of voluntariness.

If the diagnostic conclusion and condition assessment indicate that the patient has a severe mental disorder and falls under one of the following circumstances, the patient shall be hospitalized for treatment:

(1) Having already engaged in behavior that harms themselves, or being at risk of harming themselves;
(2) Having already engaged in behavior that endangers the safety of others, or being at risk of endangering the safety of others.

Article 31 If a person with a mental disorder falls under the circumstance specified in item (1) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law, and with the consent of their guardian, the medical institution shall hospitalize the patient for treatment; if the guardian does not consent, the medical institution shall not hospitalize the patient for treatment. The guardian shall provide proper care management for the patient living at home.

Article 32 If a person with a mental disorder falls under the circumstance specified in item (2) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law, and the patient or their guardian disagrees with the diagnostic conclusion that hospitalization is required and does not consent to the patient being hospitalized for treatment, they may request a re-diagnosis and appraisal.

A request for re-diagnosis in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall be made to the original medical institution or another legally qualified medical institution within three days from the date of receiving the diagnostic conclusion. The medical institution undertaking the re-diagnosis shall, upon receiving the request for re-diagnosis, assign two psychiatrists with practicing qualifications other than the initial diagnosing physicians to conduct the re-diagnosis and promptly issue a re-diagnosis conclusion. The practicing physicians undertaking the re-diagnosis shall meet and interview the patient at the medical institution where the patient is being treated, and that medical institution shall cooperate.

If there is disagreement with the re-diagnosis conclusion, the patient or guardian may independently entrust a legally qualified appraisal institution to conduct a forensic psychiatric appraisal; the medical institution shall publish the list and contact information of the publicly announced appraisal institutions. The entrusted appraisal institution shall designate at least two appraisers with practicing qualifications for the appraisal matter from within the institution to jointly conduct the appraisal and promptly issue an appraisal report.

Article 33 Appraisers shall meet and interview the patient at the medical institution where the person with a mental disorder is being treated, and that medical institution shall cooperate.

If an appraiser or their close relative has an interest in the appraisal matter that may affect the appraiser's independent, objective, and impartial conduct of the appraisal, the appraiser shall withdraw.

Article 34 Appraisal institutions and appraisers shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws, regulations, and rules, respect science, abide by professional ethics, conduct appraisals independently in accordance with the implementation procedures, technical methods, and operational norms for forensic psychiatric appraisal, and issue objective and impartial appraisal reports.

Appraisers shall make real-time records of the appraisal process and sign them. The content of the records shall be truthful, objective, accurate, and complete, and the text or audio-visual media of the records shall be properly preserved.

Article 35 If the re-diagnosis conclusion or appraisal report indicates that the patient cannot be determined to have a severe mental disorder, or that the patient does not require hospitalization, the medical institution shall not hospitalize the patient for treatment.

If the re-diagnosis conclusion or appraisal report indicates that the person with a mental disorder falls under the circumstance specified in item (2) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law, their guardian shall consent to the patient being hospitalized for treatment. If the guardian obstructs the hospitalization or the patient leaves the hospital without authorization, the public security organ may assist the medical institution in taking measures to hospitalize the patient for treatment.

Before the relevant institution issues the re-diagnosis conclusion or appraisal report, the medical institution treating the person with a mental disorder shall hospitalize the patient for treatment in accordance with the requirements of the diagnosis and treatment norms.

Article 36 If a person with a mental disorder whose diagnostic conclusion indicates the need for hospitalization is unable to handle the admission procedures themselves, their guardian shall handle the admission procedures; if the patient is a vagrant or beggar whose guardian cannot be found, the relevant department that brought the patient for diagnosis shall handle the admission procedures.

If a person with a mental disorder falls under the circumstance specified in item (2) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law, and their guardian fails to handle the admission procedures, the patient's work unit, villagers' committee, or residents' committee shall handle the admission procedures, and the medical institution shall record this in the patient's medical records.

Article 37 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall inform patients or their guardians of the rights that persons with mental disorders enjoy during the process of diagnosis and treatment.

Article 38 Medical institutions shall be equipped with appropriate facilities and equipment to protect the personal safety of persons with mental disorders undergoing diagnosis and hospitalization, prevent them from being harmed, and create an environment and conditions for hospitalized patients that are as close to normal life as possible.

Article 39 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall follow the diagnostic criteria and treatment norms for mental disorders, formulate treatment plans, and inform the person with a mental disorder or their guardian of the treatment plan, treatment methods, purpose, and possible consequences.

Article 40 If a person with a mental disorder engages in or is about to engage in behavior within a medical institution that harms themselves, endangers the safety of others, or disrupts medical order, the medical institution and its medical personnel may implement protective medical measures such as restraint and isolation when no other alternative measures are available. The implementation of protective medical measures shall follow diagnostic criteria and treatment norms, and the patient's guardian shall be informed after implementation.

The use of protective medical measures such as restraint and isolation to punish persons with mental disorders is prohibited.

Article 41 The use of medication for persons with mental disorders shall be for the purpose of diagnosis and treatment, using safe and effective drugs, and shall not be used for purposes other than diagnosis or treatment.

Medical institutions shall not force persons with mental disorders to engage in productive labor.

Article 42 The performance of surgical operations aimed at treating mental disorders on persons with mental disorders hospitalized in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law is prohibited.

Article 43 When implementing the following treatment measures on a person with a mental disorder, a medical institution shall inform the patient or their guardian of the medical risks, alternative treatment plans, and other relevant information, and obtain the patient's written consent; if the patient's opinion cannot be obtained, the written consent of their guardian shall be obtained, and the ethics committee of the medical institution shall approve it:

(1) Surgical operations resulting in the loss of function of a human organ;
(2) Experimental clinical medical treatment related to the treatment of the mental disorder.

When implementing the treatment measure specified in item (1) of the preceding paragraph, if the guardian cannot be found due to an emergency situation, the approval of the person in charge of the medical institution and the ethics committee shall be obtained.

Experimental clinical medical treatment unrelated to the treatment of the person's mental disorder is prohibited for persons with mental disorders.

Article 44 A person with a mental disorder hospitalized voluntarily may request discharge at any time, and the medical institution shall agree.

If a person with a mental disorder falling under the circumstance specified in item (1) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law is hospitalized for treatment, the guardian may request the patient's discharge at any time, and the medical institution shall agree.

If the medical institution believes that the patients specified in the preceding two paragraphs should not be discharged, it shall inform them of the reasons for not being suitable for discharge; if the patient or their guardian still requests discharge, the practicing physician shall record the process of informing in detail in the medical records, and at the same time propose medical recommendations after discharge, which the patient or their guardian shall sign to confirm.

If a person with a mental disorder falling under the circumstance specified in item (2) of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law is hospitalized for treatment, and the medical institution believes the patient can be discharged, it shall immediately inform the patient and their guardian.

Medical institutions shall, based on the condition of the person with a mental disorder, promptly organize psychiatrists with practicing qualifications to examine and evaluate patients hospitalized in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 30 of this Law. If the evaluation results indicate that the patient does not need to continue hospitalization, the medical institution shall immediately notify the patient and their guardian.

Article 45 When a person with a mental disorder is discharged from the hospital, if the person is unable to handle the discharge procedures themselves, the guardian shall handle the discharge procedures for them.

Article 46 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall respect the rights of hospitalized persons with mental disorders, such as communication and meeting with visitors. Except for temporary restrictions that may be imposed during an acute episode or to avoid hindering treatment, the rights of patients to communication and meeting with visitors shall not be restricted.**Article 47** Medical institutions and their medical staff shall truthfully record in the medical records the patient's condition, treatment measures, medication status, implementation of restraint and isolation measures, etc., and shall truthfully inform the patient or their guardian. Patients and their guardians may access and copy medical records; however, if accessing or copying medical records may adversely affect the patient's treatment, such access may be restricted. The retention period for medical records shall be no less than thirty years.

**Article 48** Medical institutions shall not refuse or deny treatment to a patient for other diseases within the scope of their diagnosis and treatment solely because the patient is a person with a mental disorder.

**Article 49** The guardian of a person with a mental disorder shall properly care for the patient who is not hospitalized, and shall, in accordance with medical advice, urge the patient to take medication on time and attend follow-up visits or treatment. Village committees, residents' committees, and the patient's employer shall, upon the request of the patient or their guardian, provide necessary assistance to the guardian in caring for the patient.

**Article 50** The health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall regularly inspect medical institutions engaged in the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders within its administrative region regarding the following matters:

(1) Whether relevant personnel, facilities, and equipment comply with the requirements of this Law;
(2) Whether diagnosis and treatment practices comply with the provisions of this Law, as well as diagnostic criteria and treatment standards;
(3) Whether the procedures for implementing inpatient treatment for persons with mental disorders comply with the provisions of this Law;
(4) Whether the legitimate rights and interests of persons with mental disorders are protected in accordance with the law.

When conducting the inspections specified in the preceding paragraph, the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall solicit opinions from persons with mental disorders and their guardians; if any violations of this Law are discovered, it shall immediately stop or order correction of the violation and handle the matter in accordance with the law.

**Article 51** Psychotherapy activities shall be conducted within medical institutions. Personnel specializing in psychotherapy shall not engage in the diagnosis of mental disorders, nor shall they prescribe medication or provide surgical treatment for persons with mental disorders. The technical specifications for psychotherapy shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

**Article 52** Prisons, compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation centers, and other similar places shall take measures to ensure that incarcerated individuals and those under compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation who have mental disorders receive treatment.

**Article 53** If a person with a mental disorder violates the Public Security Administration Punishments Law or commits a crime under the Criminal Law, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the relevant laws.

## Chapter 4: Rehabilitation of Mental Disorders

**Article 54** Community rehabilitation institutions shall provide venues and conditions for persons with mental disorders in need of rehabilitation, and conduct rehabilitation training for patients in areas such as self-care ability and social adaptation ability.

**Article 55** Medical institutions shall provide basic psychotropic drug maintenance treatment for severely mentally ill patients living at home, and provide technical guidance and support to community rehabilitation institutions regarding the rehabilitation of mental disorders.

Community health service institutions, township health centers, and village clinics shall establish health records for severely mentally ill patients, conduct regular follow-up visits for severely mentally ill patients living at home, guide patients in medication and rehabilitation training, and provide training on mental health knowledge and caregiving skills to the patients' guardians. The health administrative department of the county people's government shall provide guidance and training to community health service institutions, township health centers, and village clinics for carrying out the aforementioned work.

**Article 56** Village committees and residents' committees shall provide assistance to families of persons with mental disorders facing financial difficulties, report the situation and needs of the patients and their families to the local township people's government or sub-district office, as well as the relevant departments of the county people's government, help them solve practical difficulties, and create conditions for the patients to integrate into society.

**Article 57** Disabled persons' organizations or rehabilitation institutions for the disabled shall, based on the rehabilitation needs of persons with mental disorders, organize patients to participate in rehabilitation activities.

**Article 58** Employers shall, based on the actual situation of the person with a mental disorder, arrange work that the patient is capable of performing, ensure the patient enjoys equal treatment, arrange for the patient to participate in necessary vocational skills training to enhance their employability, create a suitable working environment for the patient, and encourage the patient for achievements made at work.

**Article 59** The guardian of a person with a mental disorder shall assist the patient in rehabilitation training for self-care ability and social adaptation ability.

If the guardian of a person with a mental disorder requires technical guidance during the care process, the community health service institution, township health center, village clinic, or community rehabilitation institution shall provide it.

## Chapter 5: Safeguard Measures

**Article 60** The health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, formulate mental health work plans based on the requirements of the national economic and social development plan and organize their implementation.

The results of mental health monitoring and special surveys shall serve as the basis for formulating mental health work plans.

**Article 61** The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, make overall plans, integrate resources, establish and improve the mental health service system, and strengthen the capacity building for the prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation of mental disorders.

County people's governments shall, based on the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, make overall plans and establish community rehabilitation institutions for persons with mental disorders.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to encourage and support social forces to establish medical institutions engaged in the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders and rehabilitation institutions for persons with mental disorders.

**Article 62** People's governments at all levels shall, based on the needs of mental health work, increase financial investment, ensure the funds required for mental health work, and include mental health work expenses in their respective fiscal budgets.

**Article 63** The State shall strengthen the construction of the grassroots mental health service system, support mental health work in impoverished and remote areas, and ensure the funds required for mental health work in urban communities and rural grassroots areas.

**Article 64** Medical colleges and universities shall strengthen the teaching and research of psychiatry, cultivate specialized psychiatric talents according to the actual needs of mental health work, and provide talent guarantees for mental health work.

**Article 65** General medical institutions shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administrative department of the State Council, establish psychiatric outpatient clinics or psychotherapy outpatient clinics to enhance their capacity for the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of mental disorders.

**Article 66** Medical institutions shall organize medical staff to study mental health knowledge and relevant laws, regulations, and policies.

Institutions engaged in the diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of mental disorders shall regularly organize on-the-job training for medical staff and workers to update their mental health knowledge.

The health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall organize mental health knowledge training for medical staff to enhance their ability to identify mental disorders.

**Article 67** Normal universities shall offer mental health courses for students; medical colleges and universities shall offer mental health courses for students not majoring in psychiatry.

The education administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall include mental health content in pre-service and in-service training for teachers, and regularly organize professional training for mental health education teachers and counselors.

**Article 68** The health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall organize medical institutions to provide basic public health services free of charge to severely mentally ill patients.

The medical expenses of persons with mental disorders shall be paid by the basic medical insurance fund in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state on social insurance. Medical insurance agencies shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, include persons with mental disorders in the coverage of basic medical insurance for urban workers, basic medical insurance for urban residents, or the new rural cooperative medical system. County people's governments shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, provide subsidies for severely mentally ill patients from families with financial difficulties to participate in basic medical insurance. Medical security, finance, and other departments shall strengthen coordination, simplify procedures, and realize the direct settlement of medical expenses payable by the basic medical insurance fund between medical institutions and medical insurance agencies.

If a person with a mental disorder still has difficulties after paying medical expenses through basic medical insurance, or cannot pay medical expenses through basic medical insurance, the medical security department shall give priority to providing medical assistance.

**Article 69** For severely mentally ill patients who meet the conditions for urban and rural minimum subsistence allowances, the civil affairs department shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, promptly include them in the minimum subsistence allowance.

For severely mentally ill patients who are eligible for the rural "five guarantees" support, as well as severely mentally ill patients in urban areas who have no labor capacity, no source of income, and no legal obligors for support, maintenance, or guardianship, or whose legal obligors for support, maintenance, or guardianship lack the capacity to provide such support, the civil affairs department shall provide support and assistance in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

For severely mentally ill patients not covered by the preceding two paragraphs who are indeed in financial difficulty, the civil affairs department may take measures such as temporary assistance to help them solve their living difficulties.

**Article 70** Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall take effective measures to ensure that school-age children and adolescents with mental disorders receive compulsory education, support persons with mental disorders who have labor capacity to engage in work they are capable of, and provide employment services for those who have recovered.

The State shall grant tax incentives to employers who arrange employment for persons with mental disorders in accordance with the law, and provide support in production, operation, technology, funds, materials, and venues.

**Article 71** The personal dignity and personal safety of mental health workers shall not be violated, and mental health workers who perform their duties in accordance with the law shall be protected by law. The whole society shall respect mental health workers.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments, medical institutions, and rehabilitation institutions shall take measures to strengthen the occupational protection of mental health workers, improve their treatment levels, and provide appropriate allowances in accordance with regulations. If a mental health worker is injured, disabled, or dies due to work, their work-related injury benefits and pensions shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

## Chapter 6: Legal Liability

**Article 72** If the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments fail to perform their mental health work duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, or abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at the next higher level shall order correction, circulate a notice of criticism, and impose warnings, demerits, or major demerits on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if serious consequences are caused, demotion, removal from office, or expulsion shall be imposed.

**Article 73** If a medical institution that does not meet the conditions stipulated in this Law engages in the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders without authorization, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of relevant diagnosis and treatment activities, issue a warning, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given or ordered to be given demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law; the practice certificates of the relevant medical personnel shall be revoked.

**Article 74** If a medical institution or its staff commits any of the following acts, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and issue a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given or ordered to be given demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law, and the relevant medical personnel may be ordered to suspend their practice activities for not less than one month but not more than six months:

(1) Refusing to make a diagnosis for a suspected person with a mental disorder who has been sent for diagnosis;
(2) Failing to promptly conduct examination and evaluation or failing to take action based on the evaluation results for a patient hospitalized for treatment in accordance with the provisions of Article 30, Paragraph 2 of this Law.

**Article 75** If a medical institution or its staff commits any of the following acts, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given or ordered to be given demotion or removal from office in accordance with the law; the practice activities of the relevant medical personnel shall be suspended for not less than six months but not more than one year; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be given or ordered to be given expulsion, and the practice certificates of the relevant medical personnel shall be revoked:

(1) Implementing protective medical measures such as restraint or isolation in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(2) Forcing a person with a mental disorder to work in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(3) Performing surgical procedures or experimental clinical medical treatment on a person with a mental disorder in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(4) Violating the provisions of this Law by infringing upon the rights of a person with a mental disorder, such as communication and meeting with visitors;
(5) Violating the diagnostic criteria for mental disorders by diagnosing a person without a mental disorder as having one.

**Article 76** Under any of the following circumstances, the health administrative department or the administrative department for industry and commerce of the people's government at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, order correction, issue a warning, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if serious consequences are caused, the practice activities shall be suspended for not less than six months but not more than one year, until the practice certificate or business license is revoked:

(1) A psychological counselor engages in psychotherapy or the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders;
(2) A person engaged in psychotherapy conducts psychotherapy activities outside a medical institution;
(3) A person specializing in psychotherapy engages in the diagnosis of mental disorders;
(4) A person specializing in psychotherapy prescribes medication or provides surgical treatment for a person with a mental disorder.

If a psychological counselor or a person specializing in psychotherapy causes personal, property, or other damage to others during psychological counseling or psychotherapy activities, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 77** If a relevant unit or individual violates the provisions of Article 4, Paragraph 3 of this Law and causes damage to a person with a mental disorder, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel of the unit shall also be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 78** If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances causes personal, property, or other damage to a person with a mental disorder or other citizens, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law:

(1) Intentionally sending a person without a mental disorder into a medical institution for treatment as a person with a mental disorder;
(2) The guardian of a person with a mental disorder abandons the patient, or fails to fulfill other guardianship duties;
(3) Discriminating against, insulting, or abusing a person with a mental disorder, infringing upon the patient's personal dignity or personal safety;
(4) Illegally restricting the personal freedom of a person with a mental disorder;
(5) Other circumstances that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of a person with a mental disorder.

**Article 79** If the diagnosis conclusion issued by a medical institution indicates that a person with a mental disorder should be hospitalized for treatment, but the guardian refuses, causing the patient to cause personal or property damage to others, or if the patient causes personal or property damage to others under other circumstances, the guardian shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 80** If, during the diagnosis, treatment, or assessment of a mental disorder, a person creates disturbances, obstructs relevant staff from performing their duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, or disrupts the work order of medical institutions or assessment institutions, they shall be given public security administration punishments in accordance with the law.

If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, other acts constitute violations of public security administration, they shall be given public security administration punishments in accordance with the law.

**Article 81** If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 82** If a person with a mental disorder, their guardian, or close relative believes that an administrative organ, medical institution, or other relevant unit or individual has violated the provisions of this Law and infringed upon the patient's legitimate rights and interests, they may file a lawsuit in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 83** For the purposes of this Law, "mental disorder" refers to a disorder or abnormality in mental activities such as perception, emotion, and thinking caused by various factors, leading to significant psychological distress or functional impairment in social adaptation for the patient.

For the purposes of this Law, "severe mental disorder" refers to a mental disorder with severe disease symptoms, leading to severe functional impairment in social adaptation for the patient, an inability to fully understand their own health status or objective reality, or an inability to manage their own affairs.

For the purposes of this Law, "guardian of a person with a mental disorder" refers to a person who can serve as a guardian in accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Principles of the Civil Law.

**Article 84** Mental health work in the military shall be governed by management measures formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

**Article 85** This Law shall come into effect on May 1, 2013.
