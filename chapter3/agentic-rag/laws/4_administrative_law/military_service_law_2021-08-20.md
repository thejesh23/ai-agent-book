# Military Service Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Sixth National People's Congress on May 31, 1984

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Military Service Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Sixth Session of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 29, 1998

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Military Service Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Twenty-Third Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 29, 2011

Revision adopted at the Thirtieth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 20, 2021

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1: This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate and strengthen national military service work, ensure that citizens perform military service according to law, guarantee the replenishment and reserve of military personnel, and build a solid national defense and a strong military.

Article 2: It is the sacred duty of every citizen of the People's Republic of China to defend the motherland and resist aggression.

Article 3: The People's Republic of China implements a military service system that combines voluntary service as the mainstay with compulsory service.

Article 4: Military service work adheres to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, implements Xi Jinping's thinking on strengthening the military, implements the military strategic guidelines for the new era, adheres to coordination with national economic and social development, adheres to adaptation to national defense and military construction, and follows the principles of subordinating to national defense needs, focusing on combat readiness and war fighting, highlighting the glory of service, and reflecting the unity of rights and obligations.

Article 5: All citizens of the People's Republic of China, regardless of ethnicity, race, occupation, family background, religious belief, or education level, have the obligation to perform military service in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Citizens with serious physical defects or severe disabilities who are unfit for military service are exempted from military service.

Citizens who have been deprived of political rights according to law shall not perform military service.

Article 6: Military service is divided into active service and reserve service. Those who serve on active duty in the People's Liberation Army are called servicemen; those who are pre-assigned to active units or incorporated into reserve units to serve in the reserves are called reservists.

Article 7: Servicemen and reservists must abide by the Constitution and laws, fulfill the obligations of citizens, and at the same time enjoy the rights of citizens; the rights and obligations arising from military service are stipulated by this Law and other relevant laws and regulations.

Article 8: Servicemen must abide by military regulations and discipline, be loyal to their duties, and be ready to fight for the defense of the motherland at any time.

Reservists must participate in military training, undertake combat readiness duties, and perform non-war military operations tasks as required, and be ready to be called up for service to defend the motherland at any time.

Servicemen and reservists shall take an oath of service according to law when entering service.

Article 9: National military service work is under the leadership of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and is the responsibility of the Ministry of National Defense.

The provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison districts), military sub-commands (garrison districts), and the people's armed forces departments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts concurrently serve as the military service organs of the respective people's governments, and are responsible for handling military service work within their administrative regions under the leadership of higher military organs and the people's governments at the same level.

State organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and the people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall complete military service work tasks in accordance with the provisions of this Law. Military service work business, in units with a people's armed forces department, is handled by that department; in units without a people's armed forces department, a department is designated to handle it. Regular institutions of higher education shall have an organization responsible for military service work.

Article 10: The military service organs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, strengthen the organization, coordination, supervision, and inspection of military service work within their administrative regions.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and military organs at the same level shall include the status of military service work as content in the evaluation of supporting the military and giving preferential treatment to families of servicemen and martyrs, supporting the government and cherishing the people, and in the assessment of relevant units and their responsible persons.

Article 11: The state strengthens the informatization construction of military service work, takes effective measures to achieve information sharing among relevant departments, promotes the modernization of technologies for the collection, processing, transmission, and storage of military service information, and provides support for improving the quality and efficiency of military service work.

Relevant departments of military service work and their staff shall strictly keep confidential the personal information collected and shall not disclose or illegally provide it to others.

Article 12: The state takes measures to strengthen military service publicity and education, enhance citizens' awareness of performing military service according to law, and create a good social atmosphere where service is glorious.

Article 13: Servicemen and reservists who render meritorious service shall be commended in accordance with the state and military regulations on honors and commendations.

Organizations and individuals that make outstanding contributions in military service work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state and military regulations.

## Chapter II: Military Service Registration

Article 14: The state implements a military service registration system. Military service registration includes initial military service registration and reserve service registration.

Article 15: Male citizens who have reached the age of 18 before December 31 each year shall undergo initial military service registration in that year according to the arrangements of the military service organs.

State organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and the people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall, based on the arrangements of the military service organs of the people's governments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts, be responsible for organizing the initial military service registration of eligible male citizens within their units and administrative regions.

Initial military service registration may be conducted online or by on-site registration at military service registration stations (points). When registering for military service, personal information shall be truthfully filled in.

Article 16: For citizens who have undergone initial military service registration but have not served on active duty and meet the conditions for reserve service, the military service organs of the people's governments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts may, as needed, register them for reserve service.

Article 17: Soldiers who have retired from active service shall, within 40 days from the date of retirement, and officers who have retired from active service shall, within 30 days from the date of determining their resettlement location, go to the military service organ of the people's government of the county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district where they are resettled to update their military service registration information; among them, those who meet the conditions for reserve service and are determined by the military unit to need to undergo reserve service registration shall also undergo reserve service registration.

Article 18: The military service organs of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for military service registration work within their administrative regions.

The military service organs of the people's governments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall annually organize the verification of military service registration information, and together with relevant departments, inspect the military service registration status of citizens to ensure timely registration and accurate and complete information.

## Chapter III: Peacetime Conscription

Article 19: The number, frequency, time, and requirements of soldiers to be conscripted for active service nationwide each year are stipulated by orders of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize military service organs and relevant departments to form conscription work institutions responsible for organizing and implementing conscription work.

Article 20: Male citizens who have reached the age of 18 shall be conscripted for active service; those not conscripted in the current year may still be conscripted for active service before reaching the age of 22. The conscription age for graduates of regular institutions of higher education may be relaxed to 24, and for graduate students, to 26.

According to military needs, female citizens may be conscripted for active service in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

According to military needs and with personal consent, citizens who have reached the age of 17 but not yet 18 may be conscripted for active service.

Article 21: Citizens who have undergone initial military service registration and have been preliminarily reviewed as meeting the conscription conditions are called conscription candidates.

During the conscription period, conscription candidates shall, in accordance with the notice of the conscription work institution of the county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district, timely participate in physical examinations and other conscription activities.

Conscription candidates who meet the conditions for active service and have been approved by the conscription work institution of the county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district are conscripted for active service.

Article 22: During the conscription period, if a conscription candidate is conscripted for active service and is simultaneously recruited or employed by a state organ, organization, enterprise, or public institution, the obligation to perform military service shall take precedence; the relevant state organs, organizations, enterprises, and public institutions shall comply with the needs of national defense and military construction and support the conscription work.

Article 23: Conscription candidates who are the sole breadwinner of their family may be deferred from conscription.

Article 24: Conscription candidates who are under supervision and investigation, investigation, prosecution, or trial for suspected crimes according to law, or who are serving sentences of fixed-term imprisonment, criminal detention, or public surveillance, shall not be conscripted.

## Chapter IV: Active Service and Reserve Service of Soldiers

Article 25: Active service soldiers include soldiers under the compulsory service system and soldiers under the voluntary service system. Soldiers under the compulsory service system are called conscripts, and soldiers under the voluntary service system are called non-commissioned officers (NCOs).

Article 26: The term of active service for conscripts is two years.

Article 27: Upon expiration of their active service term, conscripts may, based on military needs and personal consent, be selected and converted to NCOs with approval; those who have performed exceptionally well during active service may, with approval, be selected and converted to NCOs ahead of schedule. According to military needs, NCOs may be directly recruited from citizens with professional skills in non-military sectors.

NCOs implement a graded active service system. The term of active service for NCOs generally does not exceed 30 years, and the age does not exceed 55 years.

The methods for graded active service of NCOs and the methods for directly recruiting NCOs from non-military sectors shall be implemented in accordance with relevant state and military regulations.

Article 28: Soldiers whose active service term has expired shall retire from active service.

Soldiers may retire from active service early with approval if they are needed to retire due to national construction or military establishment adjustments, if a military hospital diagnosis certifies that their health condition is unsuitable for continued active service, or if there are other special reasons requiring retirement.

Article 29: The time of a soldier's active service begins from the date of approval of enlistment by the conscription work institution.

The time of a soldier's retirement from active service is the date on which the military unit issues the order for retirement.

Article 30: For soldiers who have retired from active service and undergone reserve service registration in accordance with Article 17 of this Law, the military unit, together with the military service organ, shall, based on military needs, select and determine them to serve in the soldier reserve; those who, after assessment, are suitable to serve as reserve officers shall serve in the officer reserve.

Article 31: For citizens who have undergone reserve service registration in accordance with Article 16 of this Law and meet the conditions for soldier reserve service, the military unit, together with the military service organ, shall, based on military needs, select and determine them to serve in the soldier reserve.

Article 32: The maximum age for reserve soldiers to serve in the reserves shall be implemented in accordance with other relevant laws.

Reserve soldiers who have reached the maximum age for reserve service shall retire from the reserves.

## Chapter V: Active Service and Reserve Service of Officers

Article 33: Active service officers are selected and recruited from the following personnel:

(1) Graduates of military academies;

(2) Fresh graduates of regular institutions of higher education;

(3) Active service soldiers with outstanding performance;

(4) Professional and technical personnel and other personnel needed by the military.

During wartime, as needed, officers may be directly appointed from active service soldiers, students of military academies, called-up reserve officers, and other personnel.

Article 34: Reserve officers include the following personnel:

(1) Officers who have retired from active service and are determined to serve in the officer reserve;

(2) Soldiers who have retired from active service and are determined to serve in the officer reserve;

(3) Professional and technical personnel and other personnel determined to serve in the officer reserve.

Article 35: The maximum age for officers to serve on active duty and in the reserves shall be implemented in accordance with other relevant laws.

Article 36: Active service officers who have served on active duty for the maximum age or the maximum rank tenure according to regulations shall retire from active service; those who need to extend their active service or postpone retirement shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws.

Active service officers who have not served on active duty for the maximum age or the maximum rank tenure according to regulations but need to retire from active service due to special circumstances may retire from active service with approval.

Article 37: For officers who have retired from active service and undergone reserve service registration in accordance with Article 17 of this Law, and citizens who have undergone reserve service registration in accordance with Article 16 of this Law, if they meet the conditions for officer reserve service, the military unit, together with the military service organ, shall, based on military needs, select and determine them to serve in the officer reserve.

Reserve officers who have served in the reserves for the maximum age according to regulations shall retire from the reserves.

## Chapter VI: Students Recruited by Military Academies from Young Students

Article 38: According to the needs of military construction, military academies may recruit students from young students. The age for recruiting students is not subject to the age limit for conscription for active service.

Article 39: Students who complete their studies and meet the military's training objectives shall be issued a graduation certificate by the academy; they shall be appointed as active service officers or NCOs according to regulations.

Article 40: Students who do not meet the military's training objectives or requirements shall be issued corresponding certificates by the academy in accordance with relevant state and military regulations, and shall be diverted through various methods; among them, students who return to their pre-enrollment registered domicile, if their parents have completed the household registration transfer procedures during their study period, may return to their parents' current registered domicile, and shall be received and resettled by the people's government of the county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 41: Students who are expelled from the academy shall return to their pre-enrollment registered domicile; if their parents have completed the household registration transfer procedures during their study period, they may return to their parents' current registered domicile, and the matter shall be handled by the people's government of the county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 42: The provisions of Articles 39, 40, and 41 of this Law apply to students recruited by military academies from active service soldiers.

## Chapter VII: Wartime Military Manpower Mobilization

Article 43: In order to counter threats to national sovereignty, unification, territorial integrity, security, and development interests, and to resist aggression, people's governments at all levels and military organs at all levels must make preparations for wartime military manpower mobilization during peacetime.

Article 44: After the state issues a mobilization order or the State Council and the Central Military Commission take necessary national defense mobilization measures in accordance with the National Defense Mobilization Law of the People's Republic of China, people's governments at all levels and military organs at all levels must quickly implement mobilization according to law, servicemen shall cease retirement from active service, servicemen on leave or home visit shall immediately return to their units, reservists shall be ready to be called up for active service at any time, and citizens who have undergone reserve service registration shall be prepared to be called up for reserve service.

Article 45: During wartime, as needed, the State Council and the Central Military Commission may decide to appropriately relax the upper age limit for conscripting male citizens for active service and may decide to extend the term of citizens' active service.

Article 46: After the end of the war, servicemen who need to be demobilized shall retire from active service in batches according to the demobilization order of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and shall be properly resettled by people's governments at all levels.

## Chapter VIII: Service Benefits and Compensation and Preferential Treatment

Article 47: The state guarantees that servicemen enjoy salaries, allowances, housing, medical care, insurance, leave, recuperation, and other benefits that are commensurate with the characteristics of military occupations and their performance of duties. The treatment of servicemen shall be coordinated with national economic development and compatible with social progress.

The legitimate rights and interests of female servicemen are protected by law. The military shall, based on the characteristics of female servicemen, reasonably arrange their work tasks and rest and leave, and provide special protection for female servicemen in terms of childbirth, health, etc.

Article 48: Reservists who participate in combat, military training, combat readiness duties, or non-war military operations tasks shall enjoy state-specified subsidies for meals, transportation, etc. If a reservist is a staff member of a state organ, organization, enterprise, or public institution, the unit shall maintain their original salary, bonus, and welfare benefits during the period of participation in combat, military training, combat readiness duties, or non-war military operations tasks. Other benefits and guarantees for reservists shall be implemented in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and state regulations.

Article 49: Servicemen, in accordance with relevant state regulations, enjoy preferential treatment in medical care, finance, transportation, visits to scenic spots, legal services, cultural and sports facility services, postal services, etc. Citizens retain their household registration when enlisting.

Servicemen who become disabled due to combat, duty, or illness shall have their disability grade assessed according to state regulations, be issued a disability serviceman certificate, and enjoy state-specified treatment, preferential treatment, and disability pension. Disabled servicemen who need to continue active service due to work shall be paid a disability pension by their unit according to regulations.

If a serviceman dies in combat, dies in the line of duty, or dies of illness, the state shall pay a pension to their survivors in accordance with regulations.

Article 50: The state establishes a system of preferential treatment funds for families of conscripts. The standard for preferential treatment funds for families of conscripts is set by local people's governments, and the central finance provides a fixed subsidy. The specific subsidy method shall be formulated by the competent department of veterans' affairs of the State Council, the financial department, together with the relevant departments of the Central Military Commission.

If a conscript or NCO was a staff member of a state organ, organization, public institution, or state-owned enterprise before enlistment, they may choose to return to their original position or resume work after retirement from active service.

The contractual right to rural land management legally obtained by a conscript or NCO before enlistment shall be retained during their active service period.

Article 51: The education of children of active service officers and NCOs, and the accompanying of family members, employment and entrepreneurship, and job transfers, shall enjoy preferential treatment from the state and society.

Eligible family members of servicemen shall enjoy preferential treatment in housing, medical care, and elderly care in accordance with relevant regulations.

During the period when the spouse of a serviceman accompanies the serviceman but is unemployed, they shall enjoy corresponding security benefits in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 52: If a reservist becomes disabled or dies due to participation in combat, military training, combat readiness duties, or non-war military operations tasks, the local people's government shall provide compensation and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter IX: Resettlement of Retired Servicemen

Article 53: For conscripts who have retired from active service, the state adopts methods such as self-employment, job placement, and support for proper resettlement.

Conscripts who retire from active service and choose self-employment shall be given a one-time severance pay according to state regulations, be received by the local people's government at or above the county level in the resettlement location, and may be given economic subsidies based on local conditions. The state shall timely adjust the standard of severance pay in accordance with economic and social development.

Conscripts who have received honors of second-class merit or above during peacetime or third-class merit or above during wartime, or who are children of martyrs, shall be arranged for jobs by the local people's government at or above the county level in the resettlement location upon retirement from active service; during the period awaiting job placement, the local people's government shall pay living subsidies in accordance with relevant state regulations; they may also choose self-employment based on personal will.

Conscripts who become disabled due to combat, duty, or illness and retire from active service shall be properly resettled through methods such as job placement or support based on the assessed disability grade according to state regulations; those who meet the conditions for job placement may also choose self-employment based on personal will.

Article 54: For NCOs who have retired from active service, the state adopts methods such as monthly pension, self-employment, job placement, retirement, and support for proper resettlement.

NCOs who retire from active service and have served the prescribed number of years shall be properly resettled through the method of monthly pension.Sergeants who are discharged from active service, having served for a full twelve years or meeting other conditions stipulated by the state, shall be assigned jobs by the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of resettlement; during the period awaiting job assignment, the local people's government shall issue living subsidies in accordance with relevant state regulations; they may also choose self-employment based on their own volition.

Sergeants who have served for a full thirty years, or have reached the age of fifty-five, or meet other conditions stipulated by the state, shall be resettled through retirement.

Sergeants discharged from active service who are disabled due to combat, duty-related incidents, or illness shall be properly resettled through job assignment, retirement, or support in accordance with the disability rating determined by state regulations; those who meet the conditions for job assignment may also choose self-employment based on their own volition.

Sergeants discharged from active service who do not meet the conditions specified in paragraphs two through five of this article shall be properly resettled through self-employment as stipulated in Article 53 of this Law.

Article 55: For officers discharged from active service, the state shall provide proper resettlement through retirement, transfer to civilian work, monthly pension payments, or discharge; the applicable conditions for these resettlement methods shall be implemented in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 56: Disabled servicemen or those suffering from chronic diseases, after being discharged from active service, shall be received and resettled by the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of resettlement in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council and the Central Military Commission; among them, those whose chronic diseases recur and require treatment shall be treated by local medical institutions, and if they have financial difficulties, the necessary medical and living expenses shall be subsidized in accordance with state regulations.

## Chapter 10: Legal Liability

Article 57: Citizens who have the obligation to perform military service and commit any of the following acts shall be ordered by the county-level people's government to make corrections within a specified time limit; if they fail to do so, the county-level people's government shall compel them to perform their military service obligations and impose a fine:

(1) Refusing or evading military service registration;

(2) Conscripts refusing or evading enlistment for active service;

(3) Reservists refusing or evading participation in military training, undertaking combat readiness duties, executing non-war military operations, or responding to call-ups.

For those who commit the act specified in item (2) of the preceding paragraph and refuse to make corrections, they shall not be employed as civil servants or staff managed in accordance with the Civil Service Law of the People's Republic of China, nor shall they be recruited or employed as staff of state-owned enterprises or public institutions; they shall be prohibited from leaving the country or enrolling in or resuming studies for two years, and shall be included in the list of serious dishonesty subjects for failing to fulfill national defense obligations for joint disciplinary action.

Article 58: Servicemen who refuse to perform their duties or desert the armed forces with the intent to evade military service shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the regulations of the Central Military Commission.

Servicemen who commit the acts specified in the preceding paragraph and are expelled from the military, have their military status revoked, or are held criminally liable shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 57 of this Law; among them, those expelled from the military shall also be fined.

Anyone who knowingly recruits or employs a serviceman who has deserted the armed forces shall be ordered by the county-level people's government to make corrections and shall be fined.

Article 59: State organs, organizations, enterprises, and public institutions that refuse to complete the military service tasks stipulated in this Law, obstruct citizens from fulfilling their military service obligations, or engage in other acts hindering military service work shall be ordered by the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections and may be fined; the responsible leaders, directly responsible supervisors, and other directly responsible personnel of the unit shall be punished in accordance with the law.

Article 60: Anyone who disrupts the order of military service work or obstructs military service personnel from performing their duties in accordance with the law shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 61: State functionaries and servicemen who commit any of the following acts in military service work shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law:

(1) Embezzlement or bribery;

(2) Abuse of power or dereliction of duty;

(3) Engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or accepting or sending unqualified recruits;

(4) Leaking or illegally providing personal information related to military service to others.

Article 62: Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be held criminally liable in accordance with the law.

Article 63: The penalties specified in Articles 57, 58, and 59 of this Law shall be investigated by the military service organs of the local people's government at or above the county level in conjunction with relevant departments; after the local people's government at the same level makes a penalty decision, the military service organs, development and reform, public security, veterans' affairs, health, education, human resources and social security departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall implement them specifically according to their respective responsibilities.

## Chapter 11: Supplementary Provisions

Article 64: This Law shall apply to the Chinese People's Armed Police Force.

Article 65: This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 2021.
