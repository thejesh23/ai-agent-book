# Education Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 18, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Education Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 18th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 27, 2015

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Education Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 28th Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2021

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1: This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution, with a view to developing educational undertakings, improving the quality of the entire nation, and promoting the construction of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization.

Article 2: This Law applies to education at all levels and of all types conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3: The State upholds the leadership of the Communist Party of China, adheres to the guidance of Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of Three Represents, the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, follows the basic principles established by the Constitution, and develops socialist educational undertakings.

Article 4: Education is the foundation of socialist modernization, is of decisive significance in improving the comprehensive quality of the people, promoting the well-rounded development of individuals, enhancing the innovation and creativity of the Chinese nation, and realizing the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation. The State guarantees the priority development of education.

The whole society should care for and support the development of education.

The whole society should respect teachers.

Article 5: Education must serve socialist modernization and serve the people, must be integrated with productive labor and social practice, and cultivate socialist builders and successors who are well-developed morally, intellectually, physically, aesthetically, and in labor.

Article 6: Education should adhere to fostering virtue through education, strengthen education on core socialist values among educatees, and enhance their sense of social responsibility, innovative spirit, and practical ability.

The State conducts education in patriotism, collectivism, and socialism with Chinese characteristics among educatees, as well as education in ideals, morality, discipline, rule of law, national defense, and national unity.

Article 7: Education should inherit and promote the excellent traditional Chinese culture, revolutionary culture, and advanced socialist culture, and absorb all outstanding achievements of human civilization.

Article 8: Educational activities must conform to the public interest of the State and society.

The State implements the separation of education from religion. No organization or individual may use religion to engage in activities that hinder the State's educational system.

Article 9: Citizens of the People's Republic of China have the right and obligation to receive education.

Citizens, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, property status, religious belief, etc., enjoy equal opportunities for education in accordance with the law.

Article 10: The State assists ethnic minority regions in developing educational undertakings based on the characteristics and needs of each minority group.

The State supports remote and impoverished areas in developing educational undertakings.

The State supports and develops educational undertakings for persons with disabilities.

Article 11: The State adapts to the needs of the development of the socialist market economy and social progress, promotes educational reform, promotes the coordinated development, articulation, and integration of education at all levels and of all types, improves the modern national education system, perfects the lifelong education system, and raises the level of educational modernization.

The State takes measures to promote educational equity and promote the balanced development of education.

The State supports, encourages, and organizes educational scientific research, promotes the results of educational scientific research, and promotes the improvement of educational quality.

Article 12: The standard spoken and written Chinese language is the basic language for education and teaching in schools and other educational institutions. Schools and other educational institutions shall use the standard spoken and written Chinese language for education and teaching.

Schools and other educational institutions in ethnic autonomous areas where the majority of students are from ethnic minorities shall, based on actual conditions, implement bilingual education using the standard spoken and written Chinese language and the language commonly used by the local ethnic group or the local area.

The State takes measures to provide conditions and support for schools and other educational institutions where the majority of students are from ethnic minorities to implement bilingual education.

Article 13: The State awards organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of educational undertakings.

Article 14: The State Council and local people's governments at all levels lead and manage educational work in accordance with the principles of tiered management and division of responsibilities.

Education at the secondary level and below is managed by local people's governments under the leadership of the State Council.

Higher education is managed by the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 15: The administrative department for education under the State Council is in charge of national educational work, and coordinates, plans, and manages the national educational undertakings.

The administrative departments for education of local people's governments at or above the county level are in charge of educational work within their respective administrative areas.

Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for related educational work within their respective functions and duties.

Article 16: The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall report to the people's congress at the corresponding level or its standing committee on educational work and the budget and final accounts of educational funds, and accept supervision.

## Chapter II: Basic Educational System

Article 17: The State implements a school education system covering preschool education, primary education, secondary education, and higher education.

The State establishes a scientific school system. The establishment, educational forms, duration of study, enrollment targets, and training objectives of schools and other educational institutions within the school system shall be stipulated by the State Council or by the administrative department for education authorized by the State Council.

Article 18: The State formulates standards for preschool education, accelerates the universalization of preschool education, and builds a public service system for preschool education covering urban and rural areas, especially rural areas.

People's governments at all levels shall take measures to provide conditions and support for school-age children to receive preschool education.

Article 19: The State implements a system of nine-year compulsory education.

People's governments at all levels take various measures to ensure that school-age children and adolescents attend school.

Parents or other guardians of school-age children and adolescents, as well as relevant social organizations and individuals, have the obligation to ensure that school-age children and adolescents receive and complete the prescribed years of compulsory education.

Article 20: The State implements a vocational education system and a continuing education system.

People's governments at all levels, relevant administrative departments, industry organizations, and enterprises and institutions shall take measures to develop and ensure that citizens receive vocational school education or various forms of vocational training.

The State encourages the development of various forms of continuing education, enabling citizens to receive appropriate forms of education in politics, economics, culture, science, technology, and professional fields, promoting the mutual recognition and articulation of different types of learning outcomes, and promoting lifelong learning for all.

Article 21: The State implements a national education examination system.

The types of national education examinations are determined by the administrative department for education under the State Council, and the examinations are administered by institutions approved by the State to conduct education examinations.

Article 22: The State implements an academic certificate system.

Schools and other educational institutions established or recognized with the approval of the State shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, issue diplomas or other academic certificates.

Article 23: The State implements a degree system.

Degree-conferring units shall, in accordance with the law, confer corresponding degrees and issue degree certificates to persons who have reached a certain academic level or professional technical level.

Article 24: People's governments at all levels, grassroots mass autonomous organizations, and enterprises and institutions shall take various measures to carry out literacy education.

Citizens who, according to State regulations, have the ability to receive literacy education shall receive literacy education.

Article 25: The State implements an educational supervision system and an educational evaluation system for schools and other educational institutions.

## Chapter III: Schools and Other Educational Institutions

Article 26: The State formulates educational development plans and establishes schools and other educational institutions.

The State encourages enterprises and institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individual citizens to establish schools and other educational institutions in accordance with the law.

Schools and other educational institutions established by the State shall adhere to the principle of diligence and frugality.

Schools and other educational institutions established or participated in with fiscal funds or donated assets shall not be established as for-profit organizations.

Article 27: The establishment of a school or other educational institution must meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Having an organizational structure and charter;
(2) Having qualified teachers;
(3) Having teaching premises, facilities, and equipment that meet prescribed standards;
(4) Having the necessary funds for running the school and a stable source of funding.

Article 28: The establishment, modification, and termination of schools and other educational institutions shall go through the procedures of review, approval, registration, or filing in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 29: Schools and other educational institutions exercise the following rights:

(1) To manage autonomously in accordance with their charters;
(2) To organize and implement educational and teaching activities;
(3) To enroll students or other educatees;
(4) To manage the student status of educatees, and to implement rewards or sanctions;
(5) To issue corresponding academic certificates to educatees;
(6) To appoint teachers and other staff, and to implement rewards or sanctions;
(7) To manage and use the institution's facilities and funds;
(8) To refuse any illegal interference by any organization or individual in educational and teaching activities;
(9) Other rights provided by laws and regulations.

The State protects the lawful rights and interests of schools and other educational institutions from infringement.

Article 30: Schools and other educational institutions shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) To abide by laws and regulations;
(2) To implement the State's educational policies, execute the State's educational and teaching standards, and ensure the quality of education and teaching;
(3) To safeguard the lawful rights and interests of educatees, teachers, and other staff;
(4) To provide convenience for educatees and their guardians to understand the academic performance and other relevant information of the educatees in an appropriate manner;
(5) To collect fees in accordance with relevant State regulations and disclose the items for which fees are charged;
(6) To accept supervision in accordance with the law.

Article 31: The sponsors of schools and other educational institutions shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, determine the management system of the school or other educational institution they sponsor.

The principal or chief administrative officer of a school or other educational institution must be a citizen who has the nationality of the People's Republic of China, resides within the territory of China, and meets the qualifications for the position as prescribed by the State. Their appointment and removal shall be handled in accordance with relevant State regulations. The principal is responsible for the teaching and other administrative management of the school.

Schools and other educational institutions shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, ensure the participation of faculty and staff in democratic management and supervision through organizational forms such as the faculty and staff congress with teachers as the main body.

Article 32: Schools and other educational institutions that meet the conditions for a legal person shall obtain legal person status from the date of approval for establishment or registration.

Schools and other educational institutions enjoy civil rights and bear civil liability in civil activities in accordance with the law.

State-owned assets in schools and other educational institutions belong to the State.

School-run enterprises established by schools and other educational institutions independently bear civil liability.

## Chapter IV: Teachers and Other Educators

Article 33: Teachers enjoy the rights provided by law, fulfill the obligations provided by law, and are loyal to the people's educational cause.

Article 34: The State protects the lawful rights and interests of teachers, improves their working and living conditions, and enhances their social status.

The salaries, remuneration, and welfare benefits of teachers shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.

Article 35: The State implements a system of teacher qualifications, positions, and appointment, and improves the quality of teachers and strengthens the construction of the teaching force through assessment, rewards, training, and professional development.

Article 36: The management personnel in schools and other educational institutions shall be subject to an educational staff system.

Teaching assistants and other professional and technical personnel in schools and other educational institutions shall be subject to a system of appointment for professional and technical positions.

## Chapter V: Educatees

Article 37: Educatees enjoy equal rights in enrollment, advancement to higher levels of education, and employment in accordance with the law.

Schools and relevant administrative departments shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, ensure that women enjoy equal rights with men in enrollment, advancement to higher levels of education, employment, conferment of degrees, and being sent abroad for study.

Article 38: The State and society provide various forms of financial assistance to children, adolescents, and young people who meet the admission requirements and whose families have financial difficulties.

Article 39: The State, society, schools, and other educational institutions shall implement education based on the physical and mental characteristics and needs of persons with disabilities, and provide them with help and convenience.

Article 40: The State, society, families, schools, and other educational institutions shall create conditions for minors who have committed illegal or criminal acts to receive education.

Article 41: Employees have the right and obligation to receive vocational training and continuing education in accordance with the law.

State organs, enterprises and institutions, and other social organizations shall provide conditions and convenience for the learning and training of their own employees.

Article 42: The State encourages schools and other educational institutions and social organizations to take measures to create conditions for citizens to receive lifelong education.

Article 43: Educatees enjoy the following rights:

(1) To participate in various activities arranged in the education and teaching plan, and to use educational and teaching facilities, equipment, and library materials;
(2) To obtain scholarships, student loans, and grants in accordance with relevant State regulations;
(3) To receive fair evaluation in academic performance and conduct, and to obtain corresponding academic certificates and degree certificates upon completion of the prescribed studies;
(4) To file complaints with relevant departments if dissatisfied with the sanctions imposed by the school, and to file complaints or initiate legal proceedings in accordance with the law if the school or teachers infringe upon their personal rights, property rights, or other lawful rights and interests;
(5) Other rights provided by laws and regulations.

Article 44: Educatees shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) To abide by laws and regulations;
(2) To comply with student conduct norms, respect teachers, and cultivate good ideological character and behavioral habits;
(3) To study diligently and complete the prescribed learning tasks;
(4) To comply with the management system of the school or other educational institution they attend.

Article 45: The administrative departments for education, sports, and health, as well as schools and other educational institutions, shall improve sports and health care facilities to protect the physical and mental health of students.

## Chapter VI: Education and Society

Article 46: State organs, the armed forces, enterprises and institutions, social organizations, and other social organizations and individuals shall, in accordance with the law, create a good social environment for the healthy physical and mental growth of children, adolescents, and young students.

Article 47: The State encourages enterprises and institutions, social organizations, and other social organizations to conduct various forms of cooperation with institutions of higher learning and secondary vocational schools in teaching, scientific research, technological development, and promotion.

Enterprises and institutions, social organizations, and other social organizations and individuals may support the construction of schools and participate in school management through appropriate forms.

Article 48: State organs, the armed forces, enterprises and institutions, and other social organizations shall provide help and convenience for student internships and social practice activities organized by schools.

Article 49: Schools and other educational institutions shall actively participate in local social welfare activities, provided that normal educational and teaching activities are not affected.

Article 50: Parents or other guardians of minors shall provide the necessary conditions for their minor children or other minors under their guardianship to receive education.

Parents or other guardians of minors shall cooperate with schools and other educational institutions in educating their minor children or other minors under their guardianship.

Schools and teachers may provide guidance on family education to students' parents.

Article 51: Social public cultural and sports facilities such as libraries, museums, science and technology museums, cultural centers, art galleries, and gymnasiums (stadiums), as well as historical and cultural sites and revolutionary memorial halls (sites), shall provide preferential treatment to teachers and students and provide convenience for educatees to receive education.

Radio and television stations (networks) shall broadcast educational programs to promote the improvement of the ideological, moral, cultural, scientific, and technological qualities of educatees.

Article 52: The State and society establish and develop facilities for out-of-school education for minors.

Schools and other educational institutions shall cooperate with grassroots mass autonomous organizations, enterprises and institutions, and social organizations to strengthen out-of-school education for minors.

Article 53: The State encourages social organizations, social cultural institutions, and other social organizations and individuals to carry out social and cultural educational activities that are beneficial to the physical and mental health of educatees.

## Chapter VII: Educational Investment and Condition Guarantees

Article 54: The State establishes a system with fiscal appropriations as the main source and other multiple channels for raising educational funds as supplementary, gradually increasing investment in education, and ensuring a stable source of funding for schools run by the State.

For schools and other educational institutions established by enterprises and institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individuals in accordance with the law, the sponsors are responsible for raising the funds for running the school, and people's governments at all levels may provide appropriate support.

Article 55: The proportion of State fiscal expenditure on education to the gross national product shall gradually increase with the development of the national economy and the growth of fiscal revenue. The specific proportion and implementation steps shall be stipulated by the State Council.

The proportion of educational expenditure in the total fiscal expenditure at all levels nationwide shall gradually increase with the development of the national economy.

Article 56: The educational expenditure of people's governments at all levels shall be listed as a separate item in the fiscal budget in accordance with the principle of unifying administrative authority and financial authority.

The growth of fiscal appropriations for education by people's governments at all levels shall be higher than the growth of regular fiscal revenue, and the average educational expenditure per student shall gradually increase, ensuring the gradual growth of teachers' salaries and per-student public funds.

Article 57: The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish special education funds, focusing on supporting the implementation of compulsory education in remote and impoverished areas and ethnic minority areas.

Article 58: Tax authorities shall collect the surcharge for education in full amount in accordance with the law, which shall be managed comprehensively by the administrative department for education and mainly used for implementing compulsory education.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with relevant regulations of the State Council, decide to levy local surcharges for education, which shall be used for specific purposes.

Article 59: The State adopts preferential measures to encourage and support schools in carrying out work-study programs and social services and establishing school-run enterprises, provided that normal education and teaching are not affected.

Article 60: The State encourages domestic and foreign social organizations and individuals to donate funds to support education.

Article 61: State fiscal funds for education and donations for education from social organizations and individuals must be used for education and shall not be misappropriated or withheld.

Article 62: The State encourages the use of financial and credit means to support the development of educational undertakings.

Article 63: People's governments at all levels and their administrative departments for education shall strengthen the supervision and management of educational funds of schools and other educational institutions to improve the efficiency of educational investment.Article 64 Local people's governments at all levels and their relevant administrative departments must incorporate the basic construction of schools into urban and rural construction plans, make overall arrangements for the basic construction land and necessary materials for schools, and implement priority and preferential policies in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 65 People's governments at all levels shall implement priority and preferential policies in accordance with relevant state regulations for the publication and distribution of textbooks and teaching reference materials, the production and supply of teaching instruments and equipment, and the import of books, materials, teaching instruments, and equipment used for school education, teaching, and scientific research.

Article 66 The state promotes educational informatization, accelerates the construction of educational information infrastructure, uses information technology to promote the widespread sharing of high-quality educational resources, and improves the level of education and teaching as well as education management.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall develop educational information technology and other modern teaching methods, and relevant administrative departments shall give priority arrangements and support.

The state encourages schools and other educational institutions to promote and apply modern teaching methods.

## Chapter VIII Educational Exchange and Cooperation with Foreign Countries

Article 67 The state encourages educational exchange and cooperation with foreign countries, supports schools and other educational institutions in introducing high-quality educational resources, conducting Sino-foreign cooperative education in accordance with the law, developing international education services, and cultivating internationally-oriented talents.

Educational exchange and cooperation with foreign countries adhere to the principles of independence, equality, mutual benefit, and mutual respect, and must not violate Chinese laws or harm national sovereignty, security, and public interests.

Article 68 Chinese citizens who go abroad for study, research, academic exchange, or teaching shall handle the matter in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 69 Individuals outside China who meet the conditions stipulated by the state and have gone through the relevant procedures may enter schools and other educational institutions in China for study, research, academic exchange, or teaching, and their legitimate rights and interests shall be protected by the state.

Article 70 The recognition of degree certificates, academic certificates, and other academic credentials issued by foreign educational institutions shall be handled in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

Article 71 Where education funds are not allocated according to the budget in violation of relevant state regulations, the people's government at the same level shall order the allocation within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Where education funds are misappropriated or embezzled in violation of state financial and fiscal systems, the superior authority shall order the return of the misappropriated or embezzled funds within a time limit, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 72 Those who engage in gang fights, create disturbances, disrupt the educational and teaching order of schools or other educational institutions, or damage school buildings, sites, or other property, shall be subject to public security penalties by the public security organs; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Those who encroach upon school buildings, sites, or other property of schools or other educational institutions shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 73 Where it is known that school buildings or educational and teaching facilities are dangerous but no measures are taken, resulting in casualties or major property losses, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 74 Where fees are collected from schools or other educational institutions in violation of relevant state regulations, the government shall order the return of the fees collected; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 75 Where schools or other educational institutions are established in violation of relevant state regulations, the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments shall revoke them; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 76 Where a school or other educational institution enrolls students in violation of relevant state regulations, the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments shall order the return of the enrolled students and the refund of fees collected; a warning shall be given to the school or other educational institution, and a fine of up to five times the illegal gains may be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant enrollment qualification shall be suspended for one to three years, up to the revocation of the enrollment qualification and the school-running license; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 77 Where abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or favoritism and fraud occurs in the enrollment of students, the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments shall order the return of those who do not meet the admission conditions; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Those who steal or use another person's identity to replace the admission qualification obtained by another person shall be ordered by the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments to revoke the admission qualification and be prohibited from participating in relevant national education examinations for two to five years; if a degree certificate, academic certificate, or other academic credential has been obtained, the issuing institution shall revoke the relevant certificate; if the person has become a public official, they shall be dismissed in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Those who collude with others to allow another person to use their identity to replace the admission qualification they obtained shall be ordered by the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments to be prohibited from participating in relevant national education examinations for one to three years; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the person has become a public official, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Those who organize or instigate the theft or use of another person's identity to replace the admission qualification obtained by another person, if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the person is a public official, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Those whose right to admission qualification has been infringed upon by replacement may request the restoration of their admission qualification.

Article 78 Where a school or other educational institution collects fees from educatees in violation of relevant state regulations, the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments shall order the refund of the fees collected; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 79 Where an examinee commits any of the following acts in a national education examination, the staff of the education examination institution organizing the examination shall take necessary measures at the examination site to stop the act and terminate the examinee's continued participation in the examination; the education examination institution organizing the examination may cancel the examinee's relevant examination qualification or examination results; if the circumstances are serious, the education administrative department shall order the examinee to be prohibited from participating in relevant national education examinations for one to three years; if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Illegally obtaining examination questions or answers;

(2) Carrying or using examination cheating equipment or materials;

(3) Copying another person's answers;

(4) Having another person take the examination on their behalf;

(5) Other cheating acts to obtain examination results by improper means.

Article 80 Where any organization or individual commits any of the following acts in a national education examination, if there are illegal gains, the public security organ shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, detention of not less than five days but not more than fifteen days shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if the person is a state organ staff member, sanctions shall also be imposed in accordance with the law:

(1) Organizing cheating;

(2) Providing help or convenience for cheating by providing examination cheating equipment or other means;

(3) Taking an examination on behalf of another person;

(4) Disclosing or disseminating examination questions or answers before the end of the examination;

(5) Other acts that disrupt the examination order.

Article 81 Where, in organizing national education examinations, the education administrative department or education examination institution neglects management, resulting in chaotic examination room order or serious cheating, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 82 Where a school or other educational institution issues degree certificates, academic certificates, or other academic credentials in violation of the provisions of this Law, the education administrative department or other relevant administrative departments shall declare the certificates invalid and order their recovery or confiscation; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant enrollment qualification shall be suspended for one to three years, up to the revocation of the enrollment qualification and the qualification to issue certificates; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Where any organization or individual other than those specified in the preceding paragraph manufactures, sells, or issues counterfeit degree certificates, academic certificates, or other academic credentials, if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Where degree certificates, academic certificates, or other academic credentials are obtained by fraudulent means such as cheating, plagiarism, or copying, or by other improper means, the issuing institution shall revoke the relevant certificates. Where counterfeit degree certificates, academic certificates, or other academic credentials are purchased or used, if the act constitutes a violation of public security management, the public security organ shall impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 83 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the legitimate rights and interests of teachers, educatees, schools, or other educational institutions are infringed upon, causing losses or damages, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

## Chapter X Supplementary Provisions

Article 84 Education in military schools shall be stipulated by the Central Military Commission based on the principles of this Law.

Education in religious schools shall be separately stipulated by the State Council.

Article 85 Measures for the establishment of schools and cooperative education by foreign organizations and individuals within China shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 86 This Law shall come into force on September 1, 1995.
