# Food Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Session of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on February 28, 2009

Revised at the 14th Session of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

First Amendment on December 29, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Session of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 29, 2021, according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the 28th Session of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to ensure food safety and safeguard public health and life safety.

**Article 2** The following activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall comply with this Law:

(1) Food production and processing (hereinafter referred to as food production), food sales and catering services (hereinafter referred to as food operations);

(2) Production and operation of food additives;

(3) Production and operation of packaging materials, containers, detergents, disinfectants used for food, and tools and equipment used for food production and operation (hereinafter referred to as food-related products);

(4) Use of food additives and food-related products by food producers and operators;

(5) Storage and transportation of food;

(6) Safety management of food, food additives, and food-related products.

The quality and safety management of primary agricultural products for human consumption (hereinafter referred to as edible agricultural products) shall comply with the provisions of the Agricultural Product Quality Safety Law of the People's Republic of China. However, the market sale of edible agricultural products, the formulation of relevant quality and safety standards, the publication of relevant safety information, and the provisions of this Law on agricultural inputs shall comply with this Law.

**Article 3** Food safety work shall implement the principles of prevention first, risk management, whole-process control, and social co-governance, establishing a scientific and strict supervision and management system.

**Article 4** Food producers and operators are responsible for the safety of the food they produce and operate.

Food producers and operators shall engage in production and operation activities in accordance with laws, regulations, and food safety standards, ensure food safety, act with integrity and self-discipline, be responsible to society and the public, accept social supervision, and bear social responsibility.

**Article 5** The State Council shall establish a Food Safety Commission, whose responsibilities shall be determined by the State Council.

The food safety supervision and management department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and the responsibilities prescribed by the State Council, supervise and manage food production and operation activities.

The health administrative department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and the responsibilities prescribed by the State Council, organize food safety risk monitoring and risk assessment, and formulate and publish national food safety standards jointly with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council.

Other relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and the responsibilities prescribed by the State Council, undertake relevant food safety work.

**Article 6** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for food safety supervision and management within their administrative regions, uniformly leading, organizing, and coordinating food safety supervision and management work and response to food safety emergencies within their administrative regions, and establishing and improving a whole-process food safety supervision and management mechanism and an information sharing mechanism.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the State Council, determine the responsibilities of their food safety supervision and management departments, health administrative departments, and other relevant departments. Relevant departments shall be responsible for food safety supervision and management within their respective administrative regions according to their respective responsibilities.

The food safety supervision and management department of a county people's government may establish dispatched agencies in townships or specific areas.

**Article 7** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall implement a food safety supervision and management responsibility system. The people's government at a higher level shall be responsible for evaluating and assessing the food safety supervision and management work of the people's government at the next lower level. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for evaluating and assessing the food safety supervision and management work of their food safety supervision and management departments and other relevant departments.

**Article 8** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate food safety work into their national economic and social development plans, include food safety work funds in their fiscal budgets, strengthen the capacity building for food safety supervision and management, and provide guarantees for food safety work.

The food safety supervision and management departments and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen communication, cooperate closely, exercise their powers according to their respective responsibilities, and assume responsibilities in accordance with the law.

**Article 9** Food industry associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, establish and improve industry norms and reward and punishment mechanisms in accordance with their articles of association, provide food safety information, technology, and other services, guide and urge food producers and operators to operate in accordance with the law, promote industry integrity building, and publicize and popularize food safety knowledge.

Consumer associations and other consumer organizations shall conduct social supervision in accordance with the law over acts that violate the provisions of this Law and harm the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

**Article 10** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen food safety publicity and education, popularize food safety knowledge, encourage social organizations, grassroots mass autonomous organizations, and food producers and operators to carry out the popularization of food safety laws, regulations, standards, and knowledge, advocate healthy dietary habits, and enhance consumers' food safety awareness and self-protection ability.

News media shall conduct public welfare publicity on food safety laws, regulations, standards, and knowledge, and exercise public opinion supervision over food safety violations. Reports and publicity related to food safety shall be truthful and fair.

**Article 11** The State encourages and supports basic research and applied research related to food safety, and encourages and supports food producers and operators to adopt advanced technologies and advanced management practices to improve food safety levels.

The State implements a strict management system for the use of pesticides, accelerates the elimination of highly toxic, highly hazardous, and high-residue pesticides, promotes the research, development, and application of alternative products, and encourages the use of efficient, low-toxicity, and low-residue pesticides.

**Article 12** Any organization or individual has the right to report food safety violations, obtain food safety information from relevant departments in accordance with the law, and put forward opinions and suggestions on food safety supervision and management work.

**Article 13** Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to food safety work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II: Food Safety Risk Monitoring and Assessment

**Article 14** The State establishes a food safety risk monitoring system to monitor foodborne diseases, food contamination, and harmful factors in food.

The health administrative department of the State Council, together with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council and other relevant departments, shall formulate and implement the national food safety risk monitoring plan.

After obtaining information on food safety risks, the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council and other relevant departments shall immediately verify it and report it to the health administrative department of the State Council. Regarding food safety risk information reported by relevant departments and information on diseases such as foodborne diseases reported by medical institutions, the health administrative department of the State Council shall, together with relevant departments of the State Council, analyze and study it, and if deemed necessary, promptly adjust the national food safety risk monitoring plan.

The health administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, together with the food safety supervision and management departments at the same level and other relevant departments, shall, based on the national food safety risk monitoring plan and in light of the specific conditions of their administrative regions, formulate and adjust food safety risk monitoring plans for their administrative regions, report them to the health administrative department of the State Council for record, and implement them.

**Article 15** Technical institutions undertaking food safety risk monitoring work shall carry out monitoring work in accordance with the food safety risk monitoring plan and monitoring scheme, ensure that monitoring data are true and accurate, and submit monitoring data and analysis results in accordance with the requirements of the food safety risk monitoring plan and monitoring scheme.

Food safety risk monitoring personnel have the right to enter relevant edible agricultural product planting and breeding sites and food production and operation premises to collect samples and gather relevant data. The cost of collecting samples shall be paid according to the market price.

**Article 16** If the results of food safety risk monitoring indicate potential food safety hazards, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall promptly notify the food safety supervision and management department and other relevant departments at the same level of the relevant information, and report to the people's government at the same level and the health administrative department of the people's government at the next higher level. The food safety supervision and management department and other relevant departments shall organize further investigations.

**Article 17** The State establishes a food safety risk assessment system, using scientific methods, based on food safety risk monitoring information, scientific data, and relevant information, to conduct risk assessment of biological, chemical, and physical hazard factors in food, food additives, and food-related products.

The health administrative department of the State Council is responsible for organizing food safety risk assessment work, establishing a food safety risk assessment expert committee composed of experts in medicine, agriculture, food, nutrition, biology, environment, and other fields to conduct food safety risk assessment. The results of the food safety risk assessment shall be published by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Experts from the food safety risk assessment expert committee shall participate in the safety assessment of pesticides, fertilizers, veterinary drugs, feed, and feed additives.

Food safety risk assessment shall not charge fees to producers and operators, and the cost of collecting samples shall be paid according to the market price.

**Article 18** Food safety risk assessment shall be conducted under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Potential safety hazards in food, food additives, or food-related products are discovered through food safety risk monitoring or upon receiving reports;

(2) Risk assessment is needed to provide a scientific basis for formulating or revising national food safety standards;

(3) Risk assessment is needed to determine key areas and key varieties for supervision and management;

(4) New factors that may harm food safety are discovered;

(5) It is necessary to determine whether a certain factor constitutes a food safety hazard;

(6) Other circumstances where the health administrative department of the State Council deems risk assessment necessary.

**Article 19** If the food safety supervision and management department, agricultural administrative department, or other relevant departments of the State Council find that food safety risk assessment is needed during supervision and management work, they shall submit a proposal for food safety risk assessment to the health administrative department of the State Council and provide information and materials such as the source of risk, relevant inspection data, and conclusions. In cases falling under the circumstances specified in Article 18 of this Law, the health administrative department of the State Council shall promptly conduct a food safety risk assessment and notify the relevant departments of the State Council of the assessment results.

**Article 20** The health administrative departments and agricultural administrative departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level shall promptly exchange information on food and edible agricultural product safety risk monitoring with each other.

The health administrative department and the agricultural administrative department of the State Council shall promptly exchange information on food and edible agricultural product safety risk assessment results with each other.

**Article 21** The results of food safety risk assessment are the scientific basis for formulating and revising food safety standards and implementing food safety supervision and management.

If, after food safety risk assessment, a conclusion is reached that food, food additives, or food-related products are unsafe, the food safety supervision and management department and other relevant departments of the State Council shall, according to their respective responsibilities, immediately announce it to the public, inform consumers to stop eating or using them, and take corresponding measures to ensure that the production and operation of such food, food additives, or food-related products are stopped; if it is necessary to formulate or revise relevant national food safety standards, the health administrative department of the State Council shall, together with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council, immediately formulate or revise them.

**Article 22** The food safety supervision and management department of the State Council shall, together with relevant departments of the State Council, conduct a comprehensive analysis of the food safety situation based on the results of food safety risk assessment and food safety supervision and management information. For food that, after comprehensive analysis, indicates a potentially high level of safety risk, the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council shall promptly issue a food safety risk warning and announce it to the public.

**Article 23** The food safety supervision and management departments and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, the food safety risk assessment expert committee and its technical institutions shall, in accordance with the principles of science, objectivity, timeliness, and openness, organize food producers and operators, food inspection institutions, certification bodies, food industry associations, consumer associations, news media, etc., to exchange and communicate information on food safety risk assessment and food safety supervision and management.

## Chapter III: Food Safety Standards

**Article 24** The formulation of food safety standards shall aim to protect public health, be scientific, reasonable, safe, and reliable.

**Article 25** Food safety standards are mandatory standards. No other mandatory food standards other than food safety standards may be formulated.

**Article 26** Food safety standards shall include the following content:

(1) Limit provisions for pathogenic microorganisms, pesticide residues, veterinary drug residues, biotoxins, heavy metals, and other pollutants and other substances harmful to human health in food, food additives, and food-related products;

(2) Varieties, scope of use, and dosage of food additives;

(3) Nutritional composition requirements for staple and supplementary foods specially designed for infants, young children, and other specific populations;

(4) Requirements for labels, markings, and instructions related to hygiene, nutrition, and other food safety requirements;

(5) Hygiene requirements for the food production and operation process;

(6) Quality requirements related to food safety;

(7) Food inspection methods and procedures related to food safety;

(8) Other content that needs to be formulated as food safety standards.

**Article 27** National food safety standards shall be formulated and published by the health administrative department of the State Council together with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council, and the standardization administrative department of the State Council shall provide national standard numbers.

Limit provisions for pesticide residues and veterinary drug residues in food, as well as their inspection methods and procedures, shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council and the agricultural administrative department of the State Council together with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council.

Inspection procedures for slaughtered livestock and poultry shall be formulated by the agricultural administrative department of the State Council together with the health administrative department of the State Council.

**Article 28** When formulating national food safety standards, the results of food safety risk assessment shall be taken as a basis, and the results of edible agricultural product safety risk assessment shall be fully considered, relevant international standards and international food safety risk assessment results shall be referred to, and the draft national food safety standards shall be published to the public to widely solicit opinions from food producers and operators, consumers, relevant departments, and other parties.

National food safety standards shall be reviewed and approved by the National Food Safety Standards Review Committee organized by the health administrative department of the State Council. The National Food Safety Standards Review Committee shall be composed of experts in medicine, agriculture, food, nutrition, biology, environment, and other fields, as well as representatives from relevant departments of the State Council, food industry associations, and consumer associations, and shall review the scientific nature and practicality of the draft national food safety standards.

**Article 29** For local specialty foods for which there are no national food safety standards, the health administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate and publish local food safety standards, and report them to the health administrative department of the State Council for record. Once national food safety standards are formulated, such local standards shall be abolished.

**Article 30** The State encourages food production enterprises to formulate enterprise standards that are stricter than national food safety standards or local food safety standards, apply them within the enterprise, and report them to the health administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for record.

**Article 31** The health administrative departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level shall publish the formulated and recorded national food safety standards, local standards, and enterprise standards on their websites for the public to access and download free of charge.

For issues arising during the implementation of food safety standards, the health administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, provide timely guidance and answers.

**Article 32** The health administrative departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level shall, together with the food safety supervision and management departments, agricultural administrative departments, and other relevant departments at the same level, conduct follow-up evaluations of the implementation of national food safety standards and local standards respectively, and promptly revise food safety standards based on the evaluation results.

The food safety supervision and management departments, agricultural administrative departments, and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level shall collect and summarize problems existing in the implementation of food safety standards and promptly report them to the health administrative departments at the same level.

If food producers and operators or food industry associations discover problems in the implementation of food safety standards, they shall immediately report to the health administrative department.

## Chapter IV: Food Production and Operation

### Section 1: General Provisions

**Article 33** Food production and operation shall comply with food safety standards and meet the following requirements:

(1) Having premises for food raw material processing, food processing, packaging, storage, etc., that are commensurate with the variety and quantity of food produced and operated, keeping the environment of such premises clean and maintaining a prescribed distance from toxic, hazardous, and other pollution sources;

(2) Having production and operation equipment or facilities commensurate with the variety and quantity of food produced and operated, with corresponding equipment or facilities for disinfection, changing clothes, washing, lighting, ventilation, anti-corrosion, dust prevention, fly prevention, rodent prevention, insect prevention, washing, and treatment of wastewater, garbage, and waste;

(3) Having full-time or part-time professional food safety technical personnel, food safety management personnel, and rules and regulations to ensure food safety;

(4) Having a reasonable equipment layout and process flow to prevent cross-contamination between food to be processed and ready-to-eat food, and between raw materials and finished products, and to avoid food contact with toxic or unclean substances;

(5) Tableware, drinking vessels, and containers for holding ready-to-eat food shall be cleaned and disinfected before use; cooking utensils and appliances shall be cleaned after use and kept clean;

(6) Containers, tools, and equipment for storing, transporting, and loading food shall be safe, harmless, kept clean, prevent food contamination, and meet special requirements such as temperature and humidity necessary to ensure food safety; food shall not be stored or transported together with toxic or hazardous items;

(7) Ready-to-eat food shall use non-toxic, clean packaging materials, tableware, drinking vessels, and containers;

(8) Food production and operation personnel shall maintain personal hygiene; when producing and operating food, they shall wash their hands and wear clean work clothes, caps, etc.; when selling unpackaged ready-to-eat food, they shall use non-toxic, clean containers, sales tools, and equipment;

(9) Water used shall comply with the national hygiene standards for drinking water;

(10) Detergents and disinfectants used shall be safe and harmless to the human body;

(11) Other requirements prescribed by laws and regulations.

Non-food producers and operators engaged in food storage, transportation, and loading shall comply with the provisions of item (6) of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 34** The production and operation of the following food, food additives, and food-related products are prohibited:

(1) Food produced using non-food raw materials, or food to which chemical substances other than food additives and other substances that may harm human health are added, or food produced using recycled food as raw materials;(2) Foods, food additives, and food-related products where the levels of pathogenic microorganisms, pesticide residues, veterinary drug residues, biotoxins, heavy metals, and other contaminants or substances harmful to human health exceed the limits set by food safety standards;

(3) Foods and food additives produced using raw materials or food additives that have exceeded their shelf life;

(4) Foods where food additives are used beyond the permitted scope or maximum usage levels;

(5) Staple and supplementary foods specifically formulated for infants, young children, and other specific populations that do not meet nutritional requirements under food safety standards;

(6) Foods and food additives that are spoiled, rancid, moldy, infested, contaminated, adulterated, mixed with foreign matter, or abnormal in sensory characteristics;

(7) Meat and meat products from poultry, livestock, aquatic animals, or other animals that died of disease, poisoning, or unknown causes;

(8) Meat that has not undergone required quarantine or has failed quarantine, or meat products that have not been inspected or have failed inspection;

(9) Foods and food additives contaminated by packaging materials, containers, transport vehicles, etc.;

(10) Foods and food additives with false production dates, false shelf-life labels, or that have exceeded their shelf life;

(11) Prepackaged foods and food additives without labels;

(12) Foods explicitly prohibited from production and operation by the state for special needs such as disease prevention;

(13) Other foods, food additives, and food-related products that do not comply with laws, regulations, or food safety standards.

Article 35 The state implements a licensing system for food production and operation. Those engaged in food production, food sales, or catering services must obtain licenses in accordance with the law. However, the sale of edible agricultural products and the sale of only prepackaged foods do not require a license. Those selling only prepackaged foods must file a record with the food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level.

The food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Administrative License Law of the People's Republic of China, review the relevant materials submitted by the applicant as required by items (1) to (4) of the first paragraph of Article 33 of this Law, and, when necessary, conduct on-site inspections of the applicant's production and operation premises; it shall grant a license to those meeting the prescribed conditions and deny a license to those not meeting the conditions, providing a written explanation of the reasons.

Article 36 Small food production workshops and food vendors engaged in food production and operation activities shall comply with the food safety requirements of this Law that are appropriate to their scale and conditions of production and operation, ensuring that the food they produce and operate is hygienic, non-toxic, and harmless. The food safety supervision and administration department shall strengthen supervision and management over them.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall carry out comprehensive management of small food production workshops and food vendors, strengthen services and unified planning, improve their production and operation environment, encourage and support them in improving their production and operation conditions, and enable them to operate in fixed locations such as centralized trading markets and shops, or in designated temporary operating areas and time periods.

Specific management measures for small food production workshops and food vendors shall be formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 37 To produce food using new food raw materials, or to produce new varieties of food additives or new varieties of food-related products, safety assessment materials for the relevant products shall be submitted to the health administrative department of the State Council. The health administrative department of the State Council shall organize a review within 60 days of receiving the application; it shall grant a license and make it public for those meeting food safety requirements, and deny a license and provide a written explanation of the reasons for those not meeting food safety requirements.

Article 38 Drugs shall not be added to food produced or operated, but substances that are traditionally both food and Chinese medicinal materials may be added. The catalog of substances that are traditionally both food and Chinese medicinal materials shall be formulated and published by the health administrative department of the State Council jointly with the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council.

Article 39 The state implements a licensing system for the production of food additives. Those engaged in the production of food additives must have premises, production equipment or facilities, professional and technical personnel, and management systems appropriate to the variety of food additives produced, and must obtain a food additive production license in accordance with the procedures specified in the second paragraph of Article 35 of this Law.

The production of food additives shall comply with laws, regulations, and national food safety standards.

Article 40 Food additives may only be included in the permitted scope if they are technically necessary and proven safe and reliable through risk assessment; relevant national food safety standards shall be revised in a timely manner based on technical necessity and the results of food safety risk assessments.

Food producers and operators shall use food additives in accordance with national food safety standards.

Article 41 The production of food-related products shall comply with laws, regulations, and national food safety standards. For food-related products with higher risks, such as packaging materials that come into direct contact with food, production licensing shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of the state on the management of industrial product production licenses. The food safety supervision and administration department shall strengthen supervision and management over the production activities of food-related products.

Article 42 The state establishes a whole-chain traceability system for food safety.

Food producers and operators shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, establish a food safety traceability system to ensure that food can be traced. The state encourages food producers and operators to adopt information technology to collect and retain production and operation information and establish a food safety traceability system.

The food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council, together with the agricultural administrative department and other relevant departments of the State Council, shall establish a collaborative mechanism for whole-chain traceability of food safety.

Article 43 Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to encourage large-scale food production and chain operation and distribution.

The state encourages food production and operation enterprises to participate in food safety liability insurance.

### Section 2: Control of Production and Operation Processes

Article 44 Food production and operation enterprises shall establish and improve a food safety management system, provide food safety knowledge training to employees, strengthen food inspection work, and engage in production and operation activities in accordance with the law.

The principal responsible persons of food production and operation enterprises shall implement the enterprise's food safety management system and be fully responsible for the food safety work of their enterprises.

Food production and operation enterprises shall appoint food safety management personnel and strengthen their training and assessment. Those who, after assessment, are found to lack food safety management capabilities shall not be allowed to take up their posts. The food safety supervision and administration department shall conduct random supervisory inspections and assessments of the food safety management personnel of enterprises and publish the results. No fees shall be charged for supervisory inspections and assessments.

Article 45 Food producers and operators shall establish and implement a health management system for employees. Persons suffering from diseases that, as stipulated by the health administrative department of the State Council, may hinder food safety shall not engage in work involving direct contact with ready-to-eat food.

Food production and operation personnel engaged in work involving direct contact with ready-to-eat food shall undergo annual health checks and may only take up their posts after obtaining a health certificate.

Article 46 Food production enterprises shall formulate and implement control requirements for the following matters to ensure that the food they produce meets food safety standards:

(1) Raw material control, including raw material procurement, raw material acceptance, and ingredient input;

(2) Control of key production processes, including production procedures, equipment, storage, and packaging;

(3) Inspection control, including raw material inspection, semi-finished product inspection, and finished product inspection before shipment;

(4) Transportation and delivery control.

Article 47 Food producers and operators shall establish a food safety self-inspection system and regularly evaluate their food safety status. If production or operation conditions change and no longer meet food safety requirements, the food producer or operator shall immediately take corrective measures; if there is a potential risk of a food safety incident, they shall immediately cease food production and operation activities and report to the food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at the county level.

Article 48 The state encourages food production and operation enterprises to comply with good manufacturing practice requirements, implement hazard analysis and critical control point systems, and improve their food safety management level.

For food production and operation enterprises that have obtained certification for good manufacturing practice and hazard analysis and critical control point systems, the certification body shall conduct follow-up investigations in accordance with the law; for enterprises that no longer meet the certification requirements, the certification shall be revoked in accordance with the law, and the matter shall be promptly reported to the food safety supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the county level and made public. The certification body shall not charge fees for conducting follow-up investigations.

Article 49 Producers of edible agricultural products shall use agricultural inputs such as pesticides, fertilizers, veterinary drugs, feed, and feed additives in accordance with food safety standards and relevant state regulations, strictly comply with the safety intervals or withdrawal periods for the use of agricultural inputs, and shall not use agricultural inputs explicitly prohibited by the state. The use of highly toxic and acutely toxic pesticides on vegetables, fruits, tea, Chinese medicinal herbs, and other crops specified by the state is prohibited.

Enterprises producing edible agricultural products and specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations shall establish a record-keeping system for the use of agricultural inputs.

The agricultural administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision, management, and guidance over the use of agricultural inputs, and establish and improve a system for the safe use of agricultural inputs.

Article 50 When purchasing food raw materials, food additives, and food-related products, food producers shall inspect the supplier's license and product conformity certificates; for food raw materials for which conformity certificates cannot be provided, they shall conduct inspections in accordance with food safety standards; they shall not purchase or use food raw materials, food additives, or food-related products that do not comply with food safety standards.

Food production enterprises shall establish a record-keeping system for the inspection of purchased food raw materials, food additives, and food-related products, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, purchase date, and the name, address, and contact information of the supplier, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall be no less than six months after the expiration of the product's shelf life; for products without a clear shelf life, the retention period shall be no less than two years.

Article 51 Food production enterprises shall establish a record-keeping system for the inspection of finished products before shipment, inspecting the inspection certificates and safety status of finished products before shipment, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, inspection certificate number, sale date, and the name, address, and contact information of the purchaser, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 50 of this Law.

Article 52 Producers of food, food additives, and food-related products shall inspect the food, food additives, and food-related products they produce in accordance with food safety standards, and may only release them for shipment or sale after passing inspection.

Article 53 When purchasing food, food operators shall inspect the supplier's license and the food's inspection certificate before shipment or other conformity certificates (hereinafter referred to as conformity certificate documents).

Food business enterprises shall establish a record-keeping system for the inspection of purchased food, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, purchase date, and the name, address, and contact information of the supplier, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 50 of this Law.

For food business enterprises adopting a unified distribution operation model, the enterprise headquarters may uniformly inspect the supplier's license and food conformity certificate documents and maintain records of food purchase inspections.

Business enterprises engaged in food wholesale shall establish a record-keeping system for food sales, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, sale date, and the name, address, and contact information of the purchaser, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 50 of this Law.

Article 54 Food operators shall store food in accordance with requirements to ensure food safety, regularly inspect stored food, and promptly dispose of spoiled or expired food.

When storing bulk food, food operators shall indicate on the storage location the name, production date or batch number, shelf life, name and contact information of the producer, and other relevant details.

Article 55 Catering service providers shall formulate and implement raw material control requirements and shall not purchase food raw materials that do not comply with food safety standards. Catering service providers are encouraged to disclose their processing procedures and publish information on food raw materials and their sources.

During processing, catering service providers shall inspect the food and raw materials to be processed; if any of the circumstances specified in item (6) of Article 34 of this Law are found, they shall not process or use them.

Article 56 Catering service providers shall regularly maintain facilities and equipment for food processing, storage, and display; regularly clean and calibrate thermal insulation facilities and refrigeration and freezing facilities.

Catering service providers shall clean and disinfect tableware and drinking vessels as required and shall not use tableware or drinking vessels that have not been cleaned and disinfected; if catering service providers entrust the cleaning and disinfection of tableware and drinking vessels to a third party, they shall entrust a centralized tableware and drinking vessel disinfection service unit that meets the conditions specified in this Law.

Article 57 Canteens of centralized dining units such as schools, childcare institutions, elderly care institutions, and construction sites shall strictly comply with laws, regulations, and food safety standards; if ordering meals from meal supply units, they shall order from enterprises that have obtained food production and operation licenses and inspect the ordered food as required. Meal supply units shall strictly comply with laws, regulations, and food safety standards, prepare meals on the same day, and ensure food safety.

The competent departments of centralized dining units such as schools, childcare institutions, elderly care institutions, and construction sites shall strengthen food safety education and daily management of these units, reduce food safety risks, and promptly eliminate food safety hazards.

Article 58 Centralized tableware and drinking vessel disinfection service units shall have appropriate workplaces, cleaning and disinfection equipment or facilities, and the water and detergents and disinfectants used shall comply with relevant national food safety standards and other national standards and hygiene specifications.

Centralized tableware and drinking vessel disinfection service units shall inspect each batch of disinfected tableware and drinking vessels, and may only release them for shipment after passing inspection, accompanied by a disinfection certificate. After disinfection, the tableware and drinking vessels shall be individually packaged, with the unit name, address, contact information, disinfection date, and use-by date indicated on the packaging.

Article 59 Food additive producers shall establish a record-keeping system for the inspection of finished products before shipment, inspecting the inspection certificates and safety status of finished products before shipment, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, inspection certificate number, sale date, and the name, address, and contact information of the purchaser, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 50 of this Law.

Article 60 When purchasing food additives, food additive operators shall inspect the supplier's license and product conformity certificate documents in accordance with the law, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, production date or batch number, shelf life, purchase date, and the name, address, and contact information of the supplier, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 50 of this Law.

Article 61 Organizers of centralized trading markets, counter lessors, and exhibition fair organizers shall, in accordance with the law, review the licenses of food operators entering the market, clarify their food safety management responsibilities, regularly inspect their operating environment and conditions, and if they discover any violations of this Law, shall promptly stop them and immediately report to the food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at the county level.

Article 62 Third-party platform providers for online food transactions shall conduct real-name registration of food operators on their platforms, clarify their food safety management responsibilities; for those required to obtain a license in accordance with the law, they shall also review their licenses.

If a third-party platform provider for online food transactions discovers that a food operator on its platform has violated the provisions of this Law, it shall promptly stop the violation and immediately report to the food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at the county level; if a serious violation is discovered, it shall immediately cease providing online trading platform services.

Article 63 The state establishes a food recall system. If a food producer discovers that the food it produces does not comply with food safety standards or there is evidence that it may be harmful to human health, it shall immediately cease production, recall the food that has already been placed on the market, notify relevant producers, operators, and consumers, and record the recall and notification details.

If a food operator discovers that the food it operates falls under the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall immediately cease operations, notify relevant producers, operators, and consumers, and record the cessation of operations and notification details. If the food producer believes a recall is necessary, it shall immediately carry out the recall. If the food operator's own actions cause the food it operates to fall under the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the food operator shall carry out the recall.

Food producers and operators shall take measures such as harmless treatment or destruction of recalled food to prevent it from re-entering the market. However, for food recalled due to non-compliance of labels, markings, or instructions with food safety standards, the food producer may continue to sell it after taking remedial measures and ensuring food safety; when selling, the remedial measures shall be clearly communicated to consumers.

Food producers and operators shall report the recall and handling of food to the food safety supervision and administration department of the local people's government at the county level; if harmless treatment or destruction of recalled food is required, the time and location shall be reported in advance. If the food safety supervision and administration department deems it necessary, it may conduct on-site supervision.

If a food producer or operator fails to carry out a recall or cease operations in accordance with this Article, the food safety supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the county level may order the recall or cessation of operations.

Article 64 Wholesale markets for edible agricultural products shall be equipped with inspection equipment and personnel, or entrust a food inspection institution that meets the requirements of this Law, to conduct sample inspections of edible agricultural products entering the wholesale market for sale; if any non-compliance with food safety standards is found, the seller shall be required to immediately cease sales and report to the food safety supervision and administration department.

Article 65 Sellers of edible agricultural products shall establish a record-keeping system for the inspection of purchased edible agricultural products, truthfully recording the name, quantity, purchase date, and the name, address, and contact information of the supplier, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall be no less than six months.

Article 66 The use of food additives such as preservatives and antioxidants, and food-related products such as packaging materials, in the packaging, preservation, storage, and transportation of edible agricultural products sold in the market shall comply with national food safety standards.

### Section 3: Labels, Instructions, and Advertising

Article 67 Prepackaged food shall have a label on its packaging. The label shall indicate the following:

(1) Name, specification, net content, and production date;

(2) List of ingredients or formulation;

(3) Name, address, and contact information of the producer;

(4) Shelf life;

(5) Product standard code;

(6) Storage conditions;

(7) Common name of the food additives used in the national standard;

(8) Production license number;(9) Other matters that shall be indicated as required by laws, regulations, or national food safety standards.

For staple and supplementary foods specially designed for infants and other specific populations, the label shall also indicate the main nutritional components and their contents.

Where national food safety standards have other provisions on labeling matters, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 68 When selling bulk food, food business operators shall indicate on the container or outer packaging of the bulk food the name of the food, the date of production or production batch number, the shelf life, and the name, address, and contact information of the producer or operator.

Article 69 Genetically modified food shall be prominently labeled as such in accordance with regulations.

Article 70 Food additives shall have labels, instructions, and packaging. The labels and instructions shall contain the matters specified in items (1) to (6), (8), and (9) of paragraph 1 of Article 67 of this Law, as well as the scope of use, dosage, and method of use of the food additive, and the words "food additive" shall be indicated on the label.

Article 71 The labels and instructions of food and food additives shall not contain false content, nor shall they involve disease prevention or treatment functions. Producers and operators shall be responsible for the content of the labels and instructions they provide.

The labels and instructions of food and food additives shall be clear and conspicuous. Matters such as the date of production and shelf life shall be prominently marked and easily identifiable.

If the food or food additive does not match the content of its label or instruction, it shall not be marketed or sold.

Article 72 Food business operators shall sell food in accordance with the warning signs, warning instructions, or precautionary requirements indicated on the food label.

Article 73 The content of food advertisements shall be truthful and lawful, shall not contain false content, and shall not involve disease prevention or treatment functions. Food producers and operators shall be responsible for the truthfulness and legality of the content of food advertisements.

Food safety supervision and management departments of people's governments at or above the county level, other relevant departments, food inspection agencies, and food industry associations shall not recommend food to consumers through advertisements or other forms. Consumer organizations shall not recommend food to consumers by charging fees or seeking other benefits.

### Section 4 Special Foods

Article 74 The State implements strict supervision and management over special foods such as health foods, foods for special medical purposes, and infant formula foods.

Article 75 Claims of health functions for health foods shall have a scientific basis and shall not cause acute, subacute, or chronic harm to the human body.

The catalog of raw materials for health foods and the catalog of health functions allowed to be claimed for health foods shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council in conjunction with the health administrative department of the State Council and the national traditional Chinese medicine management department.

The catalog of raw materials for health foods shall include the names of the raw materials, their dosages, and their corresponding effects; raw materials included in the catalog of raw materials for health foods may only be used for the production of health foods and shall not be used for the production of other foods.

Article 76 Health foods using raw materials not included in the catalog of raw materials for health foods and health foods imported for the first time shall be registered with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council. However, for first-time imported health foods that are nutritional supplements such as vitamins and minerals, they shall be filed with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council. Other health foods shall be filed with the food safety supervision and management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Imported health foods shall be products that are permitted to be marketed and sold by the competent authority of the exporting country (region).

Article 77 For health foods that are required to be registered by law, the applicant shall submit, at the time of registration, materials such as the research and development report, product formula, production process, safety and health function evaluation, labels, instructions, and samples, and provide relevant supporting documents. The food safety supervision and management department of the State Council shall, after organizing a technical review, grant registration to those that meet the requirements for safety and function claims; for those that do not meet the requirements, registration shall be denied and reasons shall be provided in writing. Where a decision is made to grant registration to a health food using raw materials not included in the catalog of raw materials for health foods, such raw materials shall be promptly included in the catalog of raw materials for health foods.

For health foods that are required to be filed by law, the applicant shall submit, at the time of filing, materials such as the product formula, production process, labels, instructions, and materials demonstrating the safety and health functions of the product.

Article 78 The labels and instructions of health foods shall not involve disease prevention or treatment functions, the content shall be truthful and consistent with the registered or filed content, and shall indicate the suitable population, unsuitable population, functional or characteristic ingredients and their contents, and include a statement that "this product cannot replace medicine." The functions and ingredients of health foods shall be consistent with their labels and instructions.

Article 79 In addition to complying with the provisions of paragraph 1 of Article 73 of this Law, health food advertisements shall also include a statement that "this product cannot replace medicine"; their content shall be reviewed and approved by the food safety supervision and management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the production enterprise is located, and an approval document for the health food advertisement shall be obtained. The food safety supervision and management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall publish and promptly update the catalog of approved health food advertisements and the approved advertisement content.

Article 80 Foods for special medical purposes shall be registered with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council. At the time of registration, materials such as the product formula, production process, labels, instructions, and materials demonstrating the product's safety, nutritional adequacy, and clinical effects for special medical purposes shall be submitted.

Advertisements for foods for special medical purposes shall be governed by the provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations concerning the management of drug advertisements.

Article 81 Enterprises producing infant formula foods shall implement whole-process quality control from the entry of raw materials to the exit of finished products, conduct batch-by-batch inspection of infant formula foods leaving the factory, and ensure food safety.

The fresh milk, auxiliary materials, and other food raw materials and food additives used in the production of infant formula foods shall comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and national food safety standards, and ensure the nutritional components required for the growth and development of infants.

Enterprises producing infant formula foods shall file matters such as food raw materials, food additives, product formulas, and labels with the food safety supervision and management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The product formula of infant formula milk powder shall be registered with the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council. At the time of registration, a formula research and development report and other materials demonstrating the scientific nature and safety of the formula shall be submitted.

Infant formula milk powder shall not be produced by repackaging, and the same enterprise shall not use the same formula to produce infant formula milk powder under different brands.

Article 82 The registrant or filer of health foods, foods for special medical purposes, and infant formula milk powder shall be responsible for the authenticity of the materials submitted.

The food safety supervision and management department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall promptly publish the catalog of registered or filed health foods, foods for special medical purposes, and infant formula milk powder, and shall keep confidential any business secrets of enterprises obtained during registration or filing.

Enterprises producing health foods, foods for special medical purposes, and infant formula milk powder shall organize production in accordance with the technical requirements of the registered or filed product formulas and production processes.

Article 83 Enterprises producing health foods, foods for special medical purposes, infant formula foods, and other staple and supplementary foods specially designed for specific populations shall establish a production quality management system suitable for the food produced in accordance with the requirements of good manufacturing practices, conduct regular self-inspections on the operation of the system to ensure its effective operation, and submit self-inspection reports to the food safety supervision and management department of the people's government at the county level where they are located.

## Chapter 5 Food Inspection

Article 84 A food inspection agency may engage in food inspection activities only after obtaining qualification accreditation in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State on certification and accreditation, unless otherwise provided by law.

The conditions for qualification accreditation of food inspection agencies and the inspection specifications shall be stipulated by the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council.

Inspection reports issued by food inspection agencies that meet the requirements of this Law shall have equal validity.

People's governments at or above the county level shall integrate food inspection resources to achieve resource sharing.

Article 85 Food inspection shall be conducted independently by an inspector designated by the food inspection agency.

Inspectors shall conduct food inspections in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, and in accordance with food safety standards and inspection specifications, respect science, abide by professional ethics, ensure that the inspection data and conclusions issued are objective and fair, and shall not issue false inspection reports.

Article 86 Food inspection shall implement a responsibility system for the food inspection agency and the inspector. The food inspection report shall be stamped with the official seal of the food inspection agency and signed or sealed by the inspector. The food inspection agency and the inspector shall be responsible for the food inspection report issued.

Article 87 The food safety supervision and management department of the people's government at or above the county level shall conduct regular or irregular sampling inspections of food, and publish the inspection results in accordance with relevant regulations, and no exemption from inspection is allowed. When conducting sampling inspections, the sampled samples shall be purchased, and a food inspection agency that meets the requirements of this Law shall be entrusted to conduct the inspection, and the relevant costs shall be paid; inspection fees or other fees shall not be collected from food producers or operators.

Article 88 If a food producer or operator disagrees with the inspection conclusion made in accordance with the provisions of this Law, they may, within seven working days from the date of receiving the inspection conclusion, apply for a re-inspection to the food safety supervision and management department that conducted the sampling inspection or its superior food safety supervision and management department. The food safety supervision and management department that accepts the re-inspection application shall randomly determine a re-inspection agency from the published list of re-inspection agencies to conduct the re-inspection. The re-inspection conclusion issued by the re-inspection agency shall be the final inspection conclusion. The re-inspection agency and the initial inspection agency shall not be the same agency. The list of re-inspection agencies shall be jointly published by the certification and accreditation supervision and management, food safety supervision and management, health administrative, and agricultural administrative departments of the State Council.

Where rapid testing methods prescribed by the State are used for sampling and testing of edible agricultural products, if the person being tested disagrees with the test result, they may apply for a re-inspection within four hours from the time of receiving the test result. Rapid testing methods shall not be used for re-inspection.

Article 89 Food production enterprises may conduct inspections on the food they produce themselves, or may entrust a food inspection agency that meets the requirements of this Law to conduct inspections.

If organizations such as food industry associations and consumer associations, or consumers need to entrust a food inspection agency to inspect food, they shall entrust a food inspection agency that meets the requirements of this Law.

Article 90 The inspection of food additives shall be governed by the provisions of this Law on food inspection.

## Chapter 6 Import and Export of Food

Article 91 The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall supervise and manage the safety of imported and exported food.

Article 92 Imported food, food additives, and food-related products shall comply with the national food safety standards of China.

Imported food and food additives shall be inspected and qualified by the entry-exit inspection and quarantine agency in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations on the inspection of import and export commodities.

Imported food and food additives shall be accompanied by qualification certification materials as required by the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department.

Article 93 For imported food for which there are no national food safety standards, the overseas exporter, overseas production enterprise, or its entrusted importer shall submit the relevant national (regional) standards or international standards implemented to the health administrative department of the State Council. The health administrative department of the State Council shall review the relevant standards, and if it deems them to meet food safety requirements, decide on their provisional application, and promptly formulate corresponding national food safety standards. The import of food produced using new food raw materials, or the import of new varieties of food additives or new varieties of food-related products, shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Article 37 of this Law.

The entry-exit inspection and quarantine agency shall, in accordance with the requirements of the health administrative department of the State Council, inspect the food, food additives, and food-related products specified in the preceding paragraph. The inspection results shall be made public.

Article 94 Overseas exporters and overseas production enterprises shall ensure that the food, food additives, and food-related products exported to China comply with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations of China, as well as the requirements of national food safety standards, and shall be responsible for the content of labels and instructions.

Importers shall establish an audit system for overseas exporters and overseas production enterprises, focusing on auditing the content specified in the preceding paragraph; if the audit is not passed, the import shall not proceed.

If it is found that imported food does not comply with China's national food safety standards or there is evidence that it may harm human health, the importer shall immediately stop the import and recall the food in accordance with the provisions of Article 63 of this Law.

Article 95 If a food safety incident occurring overseas may have an impact within China, or if a serious food safety problem is found in imported food, food additives, or food-related products, the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall promptly take risk warning or control measures and notify the food safety supervision and management, health administrative, and agricultural administrative departments of the State Council. The departments receiving the notification shall promptly take corresponding measures.

The food safety supervision and management department of the people's government at or above the county level shall supervise and manage imported food and food additives sold in the domestic market. If a serious food safety problem is found, the food safety supervision and management department of the State Council shall promptly notify the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department. The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall promptly take corresponding measures.

Article 96 Overseas exporters or agents exporting food to China, and importers of imported food, shall file with the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department. Overseas food production enterprises exporting food to China shall be registered with the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department. If a registered overseas food production enterprise provides false materials, or if its own actions cause a major food safety incident involving imported food, the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall revoke its registration and make a public announcement.

The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall regularly publish the list of filed overseas exporters, agents, importers, and registered overseas food production enterprises.

Article 97 Imported pre-packaged food and food additives shall have Chinese labels; if instructions are required by law, they shall also have Chinese instructions. The labels and instructions shall comply with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations of China, as well as the requirements of national food safety standards, and shall indicate the origin of the food and the name, address, and contact information of the domestic agent. Pre-packaged food without Chinese labels or Chinese instructions, or whose labels or instructions do not comply with the provisions of this Article, shall not be imported.

Article 98 Importers shall establish a record-keeping system for the import and sale of food and food additives, truthfully recording the name, specification, quantity, date of production, production or import batch number, shelf life, name, address, and contact information of the overseas exporter and purchaser, delivery date, and other relevant information, and retain relevant vouchers. The retention period for records and vouchers shall comply with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 50 of this Law.

Article 99 Export food production enterprises shall ensure that their exported food meets the standards or contractual requirements of the importing country (region).

Export food production enterprises and the planting and breeding farms for export food raw materials shall file with the national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department.

Article 100 The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall collect and summarize the following import and export food safety information, and promptly notify relevant departments, agencies, and enterprises:

(1) Food safety information discovered by entry-exit inspection and quarantine agencies during the inspection and quarantine of imported and exported food;

(2) Imported food safety information reported by organizations such as food industry associations and consumer associations, and by consumers;

(3) Risk warning information and other food safety information released by international organizations and overseas government agencies, as well as food safety information reported by overseas food industry associations and other organizations and consumers;

(4) Other food safety information.

The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department shall implement credit management for importers, exporters, and export food production enterprises of imported and exported food, establish credit records, and make them public to society in accordance with the law. For importers, exporters, and export food production enterprises with poor records, the inspection and quarantine of their imported and exported food shall be strengthened.

Article 101 The national entry-exit inspection and quarantine department may evaluate and review the food safety management system and food safety status of countries (regions) that export food to China, and determine the corresponding inspection and quarantine requirements based on the evaluation and review results.

## Chapter 7 Handling of Food Safety Incidents

Article 102 The State Council shall organize the formulation of the national food safety incident emergency plan.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, the food safety incident emergency plan of the superior people's government, and the actual conditions of their administrative region, formulate food safety incident emergency plans for their administrative region and report them to the superior people's government for filing.

The food safety incident emergency plan shall stipulate the classification of food safety incidents, the organizational command system and responsibilities for incident handling, the prevention and early warning mechanism, the handling procedures, and the emergency support measures.

Food production and business enterprises shall formulate food safety incident handling plans, regularly inspect the implementation of various food safety preventive measures within the enterprise, and promptly eliminate potential accident hazards.

Article 103 The unit where a food safety incident occurs shall immediately take measures to prevent the incident from escalating. The unit where the incident occurred and the unit receiving patients for treatment shall promptly report to the food safety supervision and management department and the health administrative department of the people's government at the county level where the incident occurred.

If the agricultural administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level discovers a food safety incident during routine supervision and management or receives a report of an incident, it shall promptly notify the food safety supervision and management department at the same level.

When a food safety incident occurs, the food safety supervision and management department of the people's government at the county level that receives the report shall report to the people's government at the same level and the food safety supervision and management department of the superior people's government in accordance with the provisions of the emergency plan. The people's government at the county level and the food safety supervision and management department of the superior people's government shall report upwards in accordance with the provisions of the emergency plan.

No unit or individual shall conceal, falsely report, or delay reporting a food safety incident, nor shall they conceal, forge, or destroy relevant evidence.

Article 104 If a medical institution finds that a patient it has received is a foodborne disease patient or a suspected patient, it shall promptly report the relevant information to the health administrative department of the people's government at the county level where it is located in accordance with regulations. If the health administrative department of the people's government at the county level deems it related to food safety, it shall promptly notify the food safety supervision and management department at the same level.

If the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level discovers information related to food safety during the investigation and handling of infectious diseases or other public health emergencies, it shall promptly notify the food safety supervision and management department at the same level.Article 105 Upon receiving a report of a food safety accident, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall immediately, together with the health administration department, agricultural administration department, and other relevant departments at the same level, investigate and handle the matter, and take the following measures to prevent or mitigate social harm:

(1) Carry out emergency rescue work and organize the treatment of persons injured by the food safety accident;

(2) Seal up the food and its raw materials that may have caused the food safety accident, and immediately conduct inspections; for food and its raw materials confirmed to be contaminated, order the food producer or operator to recall or cease operations in accordance with the provisions of Article 63 of this Law;

(3) Seal up contaminated food-related products and order their cleaning and disinfection;

(4) Properly release information, publish information on the food safety accident and its handling in accordance with the law, and explain and clarify the potential harm.

If an emergency plan needs to be activated due to a food safety accident, the people's government at or above the county level shall immediately establish an accident handling command structure, activate the emergency plan, and handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph and the emergency plan.

When a food safety accident occurs, the disease prevention and control institution at or above the county level shall conduct sanitary treatment at the accident site and carry out epidemiological investigations into factors related to the accident, and relevant departments shall provide assistance. The disease prevention and control institution at or above the county level shall submit an epidemiological investigation report to the food safety supervision and administration department and the health administration department at the same level.

Article 106 When a food safety accident occurs, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the city level with districts shall immediately, together with relevant departments, conduct an investigation into the responsibility for the accident, urge relevant departments to fulfill their duties, and submit an investigation and handling report on the accident responsibility to the people's government at the same level and the food safety supervision and administration department of the people's government at the next higher level.

For a major food safety accident involving two or more provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council shall organize the investigation into the accident responsibility in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 107 In investigating a food safety accident, the principles of seeking truth from facts and respecting science shall be adhered to, the nature and cause of the accident shall be ascertained promptly and accurately, the responsibility for the accident shall be determined, and corrective measures shall be proposed.

In investigating a food safety accident, in addition to ascertaining the responsibility of the unit where the accident occurred, the responsibility of the relevant supervision and administration departments, food inspection institutions, certification bodies, and their staff shall also be ascertained.

Article 108 The department investigating a food safety accident has the right to inquire about the circumstances related to the accident from relevant units and individuals and to require the provision of relevant materials and samples. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate and provide the relevant materials and samples as required, and shall not refuse.

No unit or individual may obstruct or interfere with the investigation and handling of a food safety accident.

## Chapter 8 Supervision and Administration

Article 109 The food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall, based on the results of food safety risk monitoring, risk assessment, and the food safety situation, determine the focus, methods, and frequency of supervision and administration, and implement risk-graded management.

The local people's government at or above the county level shall organize the food safety supervision and administration department, the agricultural administration department, and other departments at the same level to formulate an annual food safety supervision and administration plan for its administrative region, publish it to the public, and organize its implementation.

The annual food safety supervision and administration plan shall take the following matters as the focus of supervision and administration:

(1) Staple and supplementary foods specially designed for infants and other specific populations;

(2) The addition behavior in the production process of health foods and the situation of organizing production in accordance with the registered or filed technical requirements, and the situation of functional claims on health food labels, instructions, and promotional materials;

(3) Food producers or operators with a higher risk of food safety accidents;

(4) Matters where the results of food safety risk monitoring indicate potential food safety hazards.

Article 110 When performing its food safety supervision and administration duties, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level has the right to take the following measures to supervise and inspect the compliance of producers or operators with this Law:

(1) Enter the production or operation site to conduct on-site inspections;

(2) Conduct sample inspections of food, food additives, and food-related products being produced or operated;

(3) Review and copy relevant contracts, invoices, account books, and other relevant materials;

(4) Seal up or seize food, food additives, and food-related products that have evidence proving they do not meet food safety standards or have evidence proving potential safety hazards, as well as those used for illegal production or operation;

(5) Seal up premises where illegal production or operation activities are conducted.

Article 111 If the results of a food safety risk assessment prove that food has safety hazards and it is necessary to formulate or revise food safety standards, before formulating or revising the food safety standards, the health administration department of the State Council shall promptly, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, stipulate temporary limit values and temporary inspection methods for harmful substances in food, which shall serve as the basis for production, operation, supervision, and administration.

Article 112 The food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level may, in its food safety supervision and administration work, use rapid testing methods prescribed by the state to conduct random sample testing of food.

For food where the random sample test results indicate that it may not meet food safety standards, an inspection shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of Article 87 of this Law. If the random sample test results confirm that the relevant food does not meet food safety standards, they may serve as the basis for administrative penalties.

Article 113 The food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall establish food safety credit files for food producers or operators, recording the issuance of permits, results of routine supervision and inspection, and the investigation and punishment of illegal acts, publish them to the public in accordance with the law, and update them in real time; for food producers or operators with adverse credit records, increase the frequency of supervision and inspection; for food producers or operators with serious illegal acts, it may notify the investment regulatory authority, securities regulatory authority, and relevant financial institutions.
Article 114 If there are food safety hazards in the food production or operation process and timely measures are not taken to eliminate them, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level may conduct a responsibility interview with the legal representative or principal responsible person of the food producer or operator. The food producer or operator shall immediately take measures to rectify and eliminate the hazards. The situation of the responsibility interview and the rectification shall be included in the food safety credit file of the food producer or operator.

Article 115 The food safety supervision and administration department and other relevant departments of a people's government at or above the county level shall publish their email addresses or telephone numbers to accept consultations, complaints, and reports. Upon receiving a consultation, complaint, or report, if it falls within the scope of their duties, they shall accept it and promptly respond, verify, and handle it within the statutory time limit; if it does not fall within their duties, they shall transfer it to the department with authority to handle it and notify the person making the consultation, complaint, or report in writing. The department with authority to handle it shall handle it promptly within the statutory time limit and shall not shirk responsibility. Rewards shall be given to those who report verified information.

Relevant departments shall keep the information of the reporter confidential and protect the legitimate rights and interests of the reporter. If the reporter reports on the enterprise where they work, the enterprise shall not retaliate against the reporter by terminating or changing the labor contract or by other means.

Article 116 The food safety supervision and administration department and other relevant departments of a people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen the training of law enforcement personnel on food safety laws, regulations, standards, professional knowledge, and law enforcement capabilities, and organize assessments. Those who do not possess the corresponding knowledge and capabilities shall not engage in food safety law enforcement work.

If food producers or operators, food industry associations, consumer associations, etc., discover that food safety law enforcement personnel have violated laws or regulations or engaged in non-standard law enforcement during the law enforcement process, they may file complaints or reports with the food safety supervision and administration department or the supervisory authority at the same level or at a higher level. The department or authority receiving the complaint or report shall verify the situation and notify the department where the food safety law enforcement personnel work of the verified situation; if suspected of violating laws or disciplines, it shall be handled in accordance with this Law and relevant regulations.

Article 117 If the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level fails to promptly detect systemic food safety risks or promptly eliminate food safety hazards within its supervision and administration area, the people's government at the same level may conduct a responsibility interview with its principal responsible person.

If a local people's government fails to fulfill its food safety duties and fails to promptly eliminate regional major food safety hazards, the people's government at the next higher level may conduct a responsibility interview with its principal responsible person.

The food safety supervision and administration department or local people's government that has been interviewed shall immediately take measures to rectify its food safety supervision and administration work.

The situation of the responsibility interview and the rectification shall be included in the evaluation and assessment records of the food safety supervision and administration work of the local people's government and relevant departments.

Article 118 The state establishes a unified food safety information platform and implements a system for the unified publication of food safety information. The overall national food safety situation, food safety risk warning information, major food safety accidents and their investigation and handling information, and other information that the State Council determines needs to be published uniformly shall be published uniformly by the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council. If the impact of food safety risk warning information and major food safety accidents and their investigation and handling information is limited to a specific region, it may also be published by the food safety supervision and administration department of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The above information shall not be published without authorization.

The food safety supervision and administration department and the agricultural administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall publish routine food safety supervision and administration information in accordance with their respective duties.

When publishing food safety information, it shall be accurate and timely, and necessary explanations shall be provided to avoid misleading consumers and public opinion.

Article 119 If the food safety supervision and administration department, the health administration department, or the agricultural administration department of a local people's government at or above the county level learns of information that needs to be published uniformly as stipulated in this Law, it shall report to the competent department at the next higher level, which shall immediately report to the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council; when necessary, it may directly report to the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council.

The food safety supervision and administration department, the health administration department, and the agricultural administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall exchange information on food safety that they have learned.

Article 120 No unit or individual may fabricate or disseminate false food safety information.

If the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level discovers food safety information that may mislead consumers and public opinion, it shall immediately organize relevant departments, professional institutions, relevant food producers or operators, etc., to verify and analyze it, and promptly publish the results.

Article 121 If the food safety supervision and administration department and other relevant departments of a people's government at or above the county level discover suspected food safety crimes, they shall promptly transfer the case to the public security organ in accordance with relevant regulations. The public security organ shall promptly review the transferred case; if it believes that there is a criminal fact requiring criminal liability, it shall file a case for investigation.

If, during the investigation of a food safety criminal case, the public security organ believes that there is no criminal fact, or the criminal fact is obviously minor and does not require criminal liability, but administrative liability should be pursued according to law, it shall promptly transfer the case to the food safety supervision and administration department and the supervisory authority, and the relevant departments shall handle it according to law.

If the public security organ requests assistance from the food safety supervision and administration department, the ecological environment department, and other departments in providing inspection conclusions, identification opinions, and harmless treatment of the items involved, the relevant departments shall promptly provide such assistance.

## Chapter 9 Legal Liability

Article 122 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person engages in food production or operation activities without obtaining a food production or operation permit, or engages in food additive production activities without obtaining a food additive production permit, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal income and the illegally produced or operated food, food additives, and the tools, equipment, raw materials, and other items used for the illegal production or operation; if the value of the illegally produced or operated food or food additives is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the value is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than 10 times but not more than 20 times the value shall be imposed.

If a person knowingly provides a production or operation site or other conditions for the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate the illegal income, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the legitimate rights and interests of consumers are harmed, they shall bear joint and several liability with the food or food additive producer or operator.

Article 123 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, which does not constitute a crime, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal income and the illegally produced or operated food, and may also confiscate the tools, equipment, raw materials, and other items used for the illegal production or operation; if the value of the illegally produced or operated food is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 150,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the value is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than 15 times but not more than 30 times the value shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the permit shall be revoked, and the public security organ may detain the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel for not less than 5 days but not more than 15 days:

(1) Using non-food raw materials to produce food, adding chemical substances other than food additives and other substances that may be harmful to human health to food, or using recycled food as raw materials to produce food, or operating the aforementioned food;

(2) Producing or operating staple and supplementary foods specially designed for infants and other specific populations whose nutritional components do not meet food safety standards;

(3) Operating the meat of poultry, livestock, wild animals, or aquatic animals that have died of illness, poisoning, or unknown causes, or producing or operating their products;

(4) Operating meat that has not been inspected as required or has failed inspection, or producing or operating meat products that have not been inspected or have failed inspection;

(5) Producing or operating food that the state has explicitly prohibited for production or operation due to special needs such as disease prevention;

(6) Producing or operating food to which drugs have been added.

If a person knowingly provides a production or operation site or other conditions for the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate the illegal income, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the legitimate rights and interests of consumers are harmed, they shall bear joint and several liability with the food producer or operator.

If highly toxic or highly hazardous pesticides are used illegally, in addition to being punished in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, the public security organ may impose detention in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph.

Article 124 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, which does not constitute a crime, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal income and the illegally produced or operated food and food additives, and may also confiscate the tools, equipment, raw materials, and other items used for the illegal production or operation; if the value of the illegally produced or operated food or food additives is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the value is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than 10 times but not more than 20 times the value shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the permit shall be revoked:

(1) Producing or operating food or food additives containing pathogenic microorganisms, pesticide residues, veterinary drug residues, biotoxins, heavy metals, and other pollutants, as well as other substances harmful to human health, in amounts exceeding the limits of food safety standards;

(2) Using food raw materials or food additives that have exceeded their shelf life to produce food or food additives, or operating the aforementioned food or food additives;

(3) Producing or operating food that uses food additives beyond the scope or limit;

(4) Producing or operating food or food additives that are spoiled, rancid, moldy, insect-infested, dirty, mixed with foreign matter, adulterated, or abnormal in sensory properties;

(5) Producing or operating food or food additives with false production dates, shelf lives, or that have exceeded their shelf lives;

(6) Producing or operating health foods, foods for special medical purposes, or infant formula milk powder that have not been registered as required, or failing to organize production in accordance with the registered product formula, production process, and other technical requirements;

(7) Producing infant formula milk powder by repackaging, or a single enterprise producing infant formula milk powder of different brands using the same formula;

(8) Using new food raw materials to produce food, or producing new varieties of food additives, without passing a safety assessment;

(9) After being ordered by the food safety supervision and administration department to recall or cease operations, a food producer or operator still refuses to recall or cease operations.

Except for the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph and Articles 123 and 125 of this Law, if food or food additives are produced or operated that do not comply with laws, regulations, or food safety standards, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If new varieties of food-related products are produced without passing a safety assessment, or if food-related products that do not meet food safety standards are produced, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph.

Article 125 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal income and the illegally produced or operated food and food additives, and may also confiscate the tools, equipment, raw materials, and other items used for the illegal production or operation; if the value of the illegally produced or operated food or food additives is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the value is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than 5 times but not more than 10 times the value shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, production or operation shall be ordered to cease until the permit is revoked:

(1) Producing or operating food or food additives contaminated by packaging materials, containers, transport vehicles, etc.;

(2) Producing or operating pre-packaged food or food additives without labels, or food or food additives whose labels or instructions do not comply with the provisions of this Law;

(3) Producing or operating genetically modified food without labeling as required;

(4) A food producer or operator purchasing or using food raw materials, food additives, or food-related products that do not meet food safety standards.

If the labels or instructions of the produced or operated food or food additives have defects but do not affect food safety and will not mislead consumers, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall order correction; if correction is refused, a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 126 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, the food safety supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, production or operation shall be ordered to cease until the permit is revoked:

(1) A food or food additive producer fails to inspect the purchased food raw materials and the produced food or food additives as required;

(2) A food production or operation enterprise fails to establish a food safety management system as required, or fails to equip, train, or assess food safety management personnel as required;  
(3) Food and food additive producers or traders fail to check licenses and relevant documentation upon purchase, or fail to establish and maintain purchase inspection records, factory inspection records, and sales records as required;
(4) Food production and trading enterprises fail to formulate food safety incident response plans;

(5) Utensils, drinking vessels, and containers for ready-to-eat food are not cleaned or disinfected before use, or are inadequately cleaned or disinfected, or catering service facilities and equipment are not regularly maintained, cleaned, or calibrated as required;

(6) Food producers or traders assign personnel without health certificates or suffering from diseases that hinder food safety as stipulated by the health administrative department of the State Council to handle ready-to-eat food;

(7) Food traders fail to sell food in accordance with regulatory requirements;

(8) Health food production enterprises fail to file with the food safety regulatory authority as required, or fail to organize production according to the filed product formulas, production processes, or other technical requirements;

(9) Infant formula food production enterprises fail to file food raw materials, food additives, product formulas, labels, etc., with the food safety regulatory authority;

(10) Special food production enterprises fail to establish and effectively operate a production quality management system as required, or fail to submit self-inspection reports periodically;

(11) Food producers or traders fail to regularly inspect and evaluate food safety conditions, or fail to handle changes in production or trading conditions as required;

(12) Centralized dining units such as schools, childcare institutions, elderly care facilities, and construction sites fail to fulfill food safety management responsibilities as required;

(13) Food production enterprises and catering service providers fail to formulate and implement production and trading process control requirements as required.

If a centralized disinfection service unit for utensils and drinking vessels violates this Law by using water, detergents, or disinfectants improperly, or fails to inspect and provide disinfection certificates for outgoing utensils and drinking vessels, or fails to label relevant content on individual packaging as required, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

If a producer of food-related products fails to inspect the products as required, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the first paragraph.

If a seller of edible agricultural products violates Article 65 of this Law, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the first paragraph.

Article 127 Penalties for violations by small food production and processing workshops, food vendors, etc., shall be implemented in accordance with specific management measures formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 128 If the responsible entity fails to handle or report a food safety incident after its occurrence in violation of this Law, the relevant competent authorities shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order correction and issue a warning; if evidence is concealed, falsified, or destroyed, the entity shall be ordered to suspend production or business, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if serious consequences result, the license shall be revoked.

Article 129 In violation of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the entry-exit inspection and quarantine authority shall impose penalties in accordance with Article 124 of this Law:

(1) Providing false materials to import food, food additives, or food-related products that do not meet China's national food safety standards;

(2) Importing food for which no national food safety standard exists without submitting the applicable standard for review by the health administrative department of the State Council, or importing food produced with new food raw materials, or new varieties of food additives or food-related products without passing safety assessment;

(3) Failing to comply with the provisions of this Law when exporting food;

(4) An importer refuses to recall imported food after being ordered by the relevant competent authority to do so in accordance with this Law.

If an importer fails to establish and maintain records of food and food additive imports and sales, or a system for auditing overseas exporters or production enterprises, in violation of this Law, the entry-exit inspection and quarantine authority shall impose penalties in accordance with Article 126 of this Law.

Article 130 If the organizer of a centralized trading market, the lessor of counters, or the organizer of an exhibition allows food traders without legally obtained licenses to sell food in the market, or fails to fulfill inspection, reporting, or other obligations, in violation of this Law, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if serious consequences result, the entity shall be ordered to suspend business until the license is revoked by the original issuing authority; if consumers' legitimate rights and interests are harmed, joint liability shall be borne with the food trader.

If a wholesale market for edible agricultural products violates Article 64 of this Law, liability shall be assumed in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 131 If a third-party platform provider for online food trading fails to conduct real-name registration, review licenses, or fulfill obligations such as reporting or stopping the provision of online trading platform services for online food traders, in violation of this Law, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if serious consequences result, the entity shall be ordered to suspend business until the license is revoked by the original issuing authority; if consumers' legitimate rights and interests are harmed, joint liability shall be borne with the food trader.

If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when purchasing food through a third-party platform for online food trading, the consumer may seek compensation from the online food trader or food producer. If the third-party platform provider cannot provide the true name, address, and valid contact information of the online food trader, the third-party platform provider shall compensate. After compensation, the third-party platform provider has the right to seek recourse from the online food trader or food producer. If the third-party platform provider makes commitments more favorable to consumers, it shall fulfill those commitments.

Article 132 If food storage, transportation, or loading/unloading is not carried out as required, in violation of this Law, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to its responsibilities, order correction and issue a warning; if correction is refused, the entity shall be ordered to suspend production or business and be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the license shall be revoked.

Article 133 If anyone refuses, obstructs, or interferes with the lawful conduct of food safety inspections, incident investigations, risk monitoring, or risk assessments by relevant departments, agencies, or their staff, in violation of this Law, the relevant competent authorities shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order suspension of production or business and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the license shall be revoked; if the act constitutes a violation of public security, the public security authority shall impose public security penalties in accordance with the law.

If a whistleblower is retaliated against by means such as termination or modification of a labor contract, or other methods, in violation of this Law, liability shall be assumed in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

Article 134 If a food producer or trader accumulates three penalties within one year for violations of this Law other than orders to suspend production or business or license revocation, the food safety regulatory authority shall order suspension of production or business until the license is revoked.

Article 135 A food producer or trader whose license has been revoked, along with its legal representative, directly responsible supervisors, and other directly responsible personnel, shall not apply for a food production or trading license, or engage in food production or trading management, or serve as a food safety manager of a food production or trading enterprise, for five years from the date the penalty decision is made.

Anyone sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or heavier punishment for a food safety crime shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in food production or trading management or serving as a food safety manager of a food production or trading enterprise.

If a food producer or trader employs personnel who violate the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall revoke the license.

Article 136 If a food trader has fulfilled obligations such as purchase inspection under this Law, has sufficient evidence to prove that it did not know the purchased food did not meet food safety standards, and can truthfully explain the source of the purchase, it may be exempted from punishment, but the food that does not meet food safety standards shall be confiscated in accordance with the law; if personal injury, property damage, or other harm is caused, compensation liability shall be assumed in accordance with the law.

Article 137 If a technical institution or technical personnel undertaking food safety risk monitoring or risk assessment work provides false monitoring or assessment information, in violation of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and technical personnel of the technical institution shall be removed from their posts or dismissed in accordance with the law; if they have professional qualifications, the competent authority that granted the qualifications shall revoke their practice certificates.

Article 138 If a food inspection institution or food inspection personnel issues a false inspection report, in violation of this Law, the competent authority or institution that granted the qualification shall revoke the inspection qualification of the food inspection institution, confiscate the inspection fees collected, and impose a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the inspection fees; if the inspection fees are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and food inspection personnel of the food inspection institution shall be removed from their posts or dismissed in accordance with the law; if a major food safety incident is caused, the directly responsible supervisors and food inspection personnel shall be dismissed.

If a food inspection institution personnel who has been dismissed in violation of this Law engages in food inspection work within ten years from the date the dismissal decision is made, or if a food inspection institution personnel who has been criminally punished for food safety violations or dismissed for issuing false inspection reports causing a major food safety incident engages in food inspection work for life, the competent authority or institution that granted the qualification shall revoke the inspection qualification of the food inspection institution.

If a food inspection institution issues a false inspection report, harming consumers' legitimate rights and interests, it shall bear joint liability with the food producer or trader.

Article 139 If a certification body issues a false certification conclusion, in violation of this Law, the certification and accreditation regulatory authority shall confiscate the certification fees collected and impose a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the certification fees; if the certification fees are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the body shall be ordered to suspend business until the certification body's approval document is revoked and publicly announced; the directly responsible supervisors and directly responsible certification personnel shall have their practice qualifications revoked.

If a certification body issues a false certification conclusion, harming consumers' legitimate rights and interests, it shall bear joint liability with the food producer or trader.

Article 140 If false advertising of food is made to deceive consumers, or if health food advertisements are published without approval documents or with content inconsistent with the approval documents, in violation of this Law, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

If an advertising operator or publisher designs, produces, or publishes false food advertisements, harming consumers' legitimate rights and interests, it shall bear joint liability with the food producer or trader.

If a social group, other organization, or individual recommends food to consumers in false advertisements or other false publicity, harming consumers' legitimate rights and interests, it shall bear joint liability with the food producer or trader.

If food safety regulatory authorities, food inspection institutions, or food industry associations recommend food to consumers through advertisements or other forms, or if consumer organizations recommend food to consumers for fees or other benefits, in violation of this Law, the relevant competent authorities shall confiscate illegal gains and impose demerits, demotion, or removal from posts on directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal shall be imposed.

If false publicity about food is made and the circumstances are serious, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall decide to suspend the sale of the food and publicly announce it; if the food is still sold, the food safety regulatory authority of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate illegal gains and illegally sold food, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 141 If false food safety information is fabricated or disseminated, constituting a violation of public security, in violation of this Law, the public security authority shall impose public security penalties in accordance with the law.

If a media outlet fabricates or disseminates false food safety information, the relevant competent authority shall impose penalties in accordance with the law and impose sanctions on directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel; if the legitimate rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations are harmed, civil liability such as eliminating the impact, restoring reputation, compensating for losses, and making apologies shall be assumed in accordance with the law.

Article 142 If a local people's government at or above the county level commits any of the following acts, in violation of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a major demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from posts shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal shall be imposed; if serious consequences result, the principal responsible person shall also resign:

(1) Failing to promptly organize and coordinate relevant departments to effectively handle a food safety incident occurring within its administrative region, causing adverse effects or losses;

(2) Failing to promptly organize rectification of regional food safety issues involving multiple links within its administrative region, causing adverse effects or losses;

(3) Concealing, falsely reporting, or delaying the reporting of a food safety incident;

(4) A particularly major food safety incident occurs within its administrative region, or major food safety incidents occur consecutively.

Article 143 If a local people's government at or above the county level commits any of the following acts, in violation of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, demerit, or major demerit; if serious consequences result, demotion or removal from posts shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to determine the food safety regulatory responsibilities of relevant departments, failing to establish a sound full-process food safety supervision mechanism and information-sharing mechanism, or failing to implement a food safety regulatory responsibility system;

(2) Failing to formulate a food safety incident emergency plan for its administrative region, or failing to immediately establish an incident handling command structure and activate the emergency plan after a food safety incident occurs.

Article 144 If the food safety regulatory, health administrative, or agricultural administrative departments of a people's government at or above the county level commit any of the following acts, in violation of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a major demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from posts shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal shall be imposed; if serious consequences result, the principal responsible person shall also resign:

(1) Concealing, falsely reporting, or delaying the reporting of a food safety incident;

(2) Failing to investigate a food safety incident as required, or failing to handle a food safety incident report in a timely manner, causing the incident to expand or spread;

(3) After a food safety risk assessment concludes that food, food additives, or food-related products are unsafe, failing to take timely corresponding measures, causing a food safety incident or adverse social impact;

(4) Granting a permit to an applicant who does not meet the conditions, or granting a permit beyond statutory authority;

(5) Failing to perform food safety regulatory duties, leading to a food safety incident.

Article 145 If the food safety regulatory, health administrative, or agricultural administrative departments of a people's government at or above the county level commit any of the following acts, causing adverse consequences, in violation of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, demerit, or major demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from posts shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal shall be imposed:

(1) After obtaining relevant food safety information, failing to report to the higher competent authority and the people's government at the same level as required, or failing to notify each other as required;

(2) Failing to publish food safety information as required;

(3) Failing to perform statutory duties, failing to cooperate in investigating food safety violations, or abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 146 If food safety regulatory authorities illegally implement inspection, enforcement, or other measures during the performance of food safety regulatory duties, causing losses to producers or traders, compensation shall be made in accordance with the law, and sanctions shall be imposed on directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 147 If personal injury, property damage, or other harm is caused in violation of this Law, compensation liability shall be assumed in accordance with the law. If a producer's or trader's property is insufficient to simultaneously bear civil compensation liability and pay fines or penalties, civil compensation liability shall be borne first.

Article 148 If a consumer suffers harm from food that does not meet food safety standards, the consumer may claim compensation for losses from the trader or the producer. The producer or trader receiving the consumer's compensation claim shall implement a first-responsibility system, pay compensation first, and shall not shirk responsibility; if the producer is at fault, the trader has the right to seek recourse from the producer after compensation; if the trader is at fault, the producer has the right to seek recourse from the trader after compensation.

If food that does not meet food safety standards is produced, or if food known to not meet food safety standards is sold, the consumer may, in addition to claiming compensation for losses, demand from the producer or trader a payment of ten times the price or three times the loss as punitive damages; if the additional compensation is less than 1,000 yuan, it shall be 1,000 yuan. However, this does not apply to defects in food labels or instructions that do not affect food safety and are not misleading to consumers.

Article 149 If a violation of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 150 Meanings of the following terms used in this Law:

Food refers to any finished product or raw material intended for human consumption or drinking, as well as items that are traditionally both food and Chinese medicinal materials, but does not include items intended for therapeutic purposes.

Food safety means that food is non-toxic, harmless, meets the required nutritional standards, and does not cause any acute, subacute, or chronic harm to human health.

Prepackaged food refers to food that is pre-quantitatively packaged or placed in packaging materials or containers.

Food additives refer to artificially synthesized or natural substances added to food to improve its quality, color, aroma, and taste, as well as for preservation, freshness, and processing needs, including nutritional fortifiers.

Packaging materials and containers for food refer to products made of paper, bamboo, wood, metal, enamel, ceramics, plastic, rubber, natural fibers, chemical fibers, glass, etc., used for packaging or holding food or food additives, as well as coatings that directly contact food or food additives.

Tools and equipment used in food production and trading refer to machinery, pipes, conveyor belts, containers, utensils, tableware, etc., that directly contact food or food additives during the production, sale, or use of food or food additives.

Detergents and disinfectants used for food refer to substances directly used for washing or disinfecting food, tableware, drinking vessels, and tools, equipment, or food packaging materials and containers that directly contact food.

Food shelf life refers to the period during which food maintains its quality under specified storage conditions.Foodborne illness refers to infectious or toxic diseases caused by the entry of pathogenic factors in food into the human body, including food poisoning.

Food safety incidents refer to incidents such as foodborne illnesses and food contamination that originate from food and cause or may cause harm to human health.

Article 151: For the food safety management of genetically modified foods and edible salt, where this Law does not provide provisions, the provisions of other laws and administrative regulations shall apply.

Article 152: The food safety management measures for railway and civil aviation operations shall be formulated by the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Specific management measures for health foods shall be formulated by the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Specific management measures for the production activities of food-related products shall be formulated by the food safety supervision and administration department of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

The supervision and administration of food at national border ports shall be implemented by the entry-exit inspection and quarantine authorities in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The food safety management measures for military-specific food and self-supplied food shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 153: The State Council may make adjustments to the food safety supervision and management system based on actual needs.

Article 154: This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2015.
