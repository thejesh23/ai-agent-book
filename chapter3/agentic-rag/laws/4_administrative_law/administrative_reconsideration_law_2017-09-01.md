# Administrative Reconsideration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 9th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 29, 1999

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purpose of preventing and correcting unlawful or improper specific administrative acts, protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations, and ensuring and supervising the lawful exercise of administrative powers by administrative organs.

Article 2 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization believes that a specific administrative act has infringed upon their lawful rights and interests and submits an application for administrative reconsideration to an administrative organ, and the administrative organ accepts the application and makes a reconsideration decision, this Law shall apply.

Article 3 The administrative organ that performs the duty of administrative reconsideration in accordance with this Law is the administrative reconsideration organ. The institution responsible for legal affairs within the administrative reconsideration organ shall specifically handle administrative reconsideration matters and perform the following duties:

(1) Accepting applications for administrative reconsideration;

(2) Investigating and gathering evidence from relevant organizations and individuals, and reviewing documents and materials;

(3) Reviewing whether the specific administrative act subject to the reconsideration application is lawful and appropriate, and drafting the administrative reconsideration decision;

(4) Handling or forwarding applications for review of the provisions listed in Article 7 of this Law;

(5) Proposing handling suggestions regarding acts of administrative organs that violate the provisions of this Law, within the prescribed authority and procedures;

(6) Handling litigation matters arising from dissatisfaction with administrative reconsideration decisions;
(7) Other duties as prescribed by laws and regulations.

Persons who engage in administrative reconsideration for the first time in an administrative organ shall pass the national unified legal professional qualification examination to obtain legal professional qualifications.

Article 4 When performing the duty of administrative reconsideration, the administrative reconsideration organ shall adhere to the principles of legality, fairness, openness, timeliness, and convenience for the people, insist on correcting any errors, and ensure the correct implementation of laws and regulations.

Article 5 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization is dissatisfied with an administrative reconsideration decision, they may file an administrative lawsuit with a People's Court in accordance with the provisions of the Administrative Procedure Law, except where the law stipulates that the administrative reconsideration decision is final.

## Chapter II: Scope of Administrative Reconsideration

Article 6 Under any of the following circumstances, a citizen, legal person, or other organization may apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with this Law:

(1) Dissatisfied with an administrative penalty decision made by an administrative organ, such as a warning, fine, confiscation of illegal gains, confiscation of illegal property, order to suspend production or business, temporary suspension or revocation of a permit, temporary suspension or revocation of a license, or administrative detention;

(2) Dissatisfied with a decision on administrative coercive measures made by an administrative organ, such as restricting personal freedom or sealing up, seizing, or freezing property;

(3) Dissatisfied with a decision made by an administrative organ to change, suspend, or revoke certificates such as permits, licenses, qualification certificates, or credential certificates;

(4) Dissatisfied with a decision made by an administrative organ regarding the confirmation of ownership or right to use natural resources such as land, mineral deposits, water bodies, forests, mountains, grasslands, wasteland, tidal flats, or sea areas;

(5) Believing that an administrative organ has infringed upon lawful operational autonomy;

(6) Believing that an administrative organ has changed or terminated an agricultural contract, infringing upon lawful rights and interests;

(7) Believing that an administrative organ has illegally raised funds, collected property, apportioned expenses, or unlawfully demanded the performance of other obligations;

(8) Believing that, although the statutory conditions are met, an administrative organ has failed to issue certificates such as permits, licenses, qualification certificates, or credential certificates upon application, or has failed to handle matters requiring approval or registration;

(9) Applying to an administrative organ to perform its statutory duty to protect personal rights, property rights, or the right to education, and the administrative organ has failed to perform such duty;

(10) Applying to an administrative organ for the lawful distribution of pensions, social insurance benefits, or minimum subsistence allowances, and the administrative organ has failed to distribute them;

(11) Believing that other specific administrative acts of an administrative organ have infringed upon lawful rights and interests.

Article 7 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization believes that the following provisions, on which a specific administrative act is based, are unlawful, they may, when applying for administrative reconsideration of the specific administrative act, simultaneously submit to the administrative reconsideration organ an application for review of such provisions:

(1) Provisions of departments under the State Council;

(2) Provisions of local people's governments at or above the county level and their working departments;

(3) Provisions of the people's governments of townships or towns.

The provisions listed in the preceding paragraph do not include rules of ministries or commissions under the State Council or rules of local people's governments. The review of rules shall be handled in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 8 Dissatisfaction with administrative sanctions or other personnel handling decisions made by an administrative organ shall be appealed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Dissatisfaction with mediation or other handling of civil disputes by an administrative organ shall be submitted for arbitration or filed as a lawsuit with a People's Court in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III: Application for Administrative Reconsideration

Article 9 A citizen, legal person, or other organization that believes a specific administrative act has infringed upon their lawful rights and interests may submit an application for administrative reconsideration within 60 days from the date they become aware of the specific administrative act; however, if the law prescribes a longer application period exceeding 60 days, that period shall apply.

If the statutory application period is missed due to force majeure or other legitimate reasons, the application period shall resume from the date the obstacle is removed.
Article 10 The citizen, legal person, or other organization applying for administrative reconsideration in accordance with this Law is the applicant.

If the citizen entitled to apply for administrative reconsideration dies, their close relatives may apply for administrative reconsideration. If the citizen entitled to apply is a person without or with limited capacity for civil conduct, their legal representative may apply on their behalf. If the legal person or other organization entitled to apply terminates, the legal person or other organization succeeding to its rights may apply for administrative reconsideration.

Other citizens, legal persons, or other organizations with an interest in the specific administrative act subject to the reconsideration application may participate in the administrative reconsideration as third parties.

The administrative organ that made the specific administrative act is the respondent when a citizen, legal person, or other organization applies for administrative reconsideration due to dissatisfaction with that act.

The applicant or third party may appoint an agent to participate in the administrative reconsideration.

Article 11 An applicant may submit an application for administrative reconsideration in writing or orally; if submitted orally, the administrative reconsideration organ shall immediately record the applicant's basic information, the reconsideration request, the main facts, reasons, and time of the application.

Article 12 For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of a working department of a local people's government at or above the county level, the applicant may choose to apply for administrative reconsideration either to the people's government at the same level as the department or to the competent authority at the next higher level.

For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of an administrative organ under vertical leadership, such as customs, finance, state taxation, or foreign exchange control, or of a state security organ, the applicant shall apply to the competent authority at the next higher level.

Article 13 For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of a local people's government at any level, the applicant shall apply to the local people's government at the next higher level.

For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of a county-level local people's government under an agency lawfully established by the people's government of a province or autonomous region, the applicant shall apply to that agency.
Article 14 For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of a department under the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the applicant shall apply for administrative reconsideration to that department or people's government. If dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, the applicant may file an administrative lawsuit with a People's Court or apply to the State Council for a ruling, and the State Council shall make a final ruling in accordance with this Law.

Article 15 For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of other administrative organs or organizations not covered by Articles 12, 13, and 14 of this Law, the applicant shall apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of a dispatched agency lawfully established by a local people's government at or above the county level, the applicant shall apply to the people's government that established the dispatched agency;
(2) For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act made in its own name by a dispatched agency lawfully established by a government working department in accordance with laws, regulations, or rules, the applicant shall apply to the department that established the dispatched agency or to the local people's government at the same level as that department;
(3) For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act of an organization authorized by laws or regulations, the applicant shall apply to the local people's government, the working department of the local people's government, or the department under the State Council that directly manages the organization;

(4) For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act made jointly by two or more administrative organs, the applicant shall apply to the administrative organ at the next higher level common to them;

(5) For dissatisfaction with a specific administrative act made by a dissolved administrative organ before its dissolution, the applicant shall apply to the administrative organ at the next higher level of the organ that continues to exercise its functions.

In any of the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph, the applicant may also submit an application for administrative reconsideration to the county-level local people's government of the place where the specific administrative act occurred, and the accepting county-level local people's government shall handle it in accordance with Article 18 of this Law.

Article 16 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization has applied for administrative reconsideration and the administrative reconsideration organ has accepted it, or where laws or regulations require that an application for administrative reconsideration be made first before filing an administrative lawsuit, no administrative lawsuit may be filed with a People's Court during the statutory period for administrative reconsideration.

Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization has filed an administrative lawsuit with a People's Court and the court has accepted it, no application for administrative reconsideration may be made.

## Chapter IV: Acceptance of Administrative Reconsideration

Article 17 Upon receiving an application for administrative reconsideration, the administrative reconsideration organ shall review it within five days. If the application does not meet the requirements of this Law, it shall decide not to accept it and notify the applicant in writing. If the application meets the requirements of this Law but is not within the jurisdiction of this organ, it shall inform the applicant to submit the application to the relevant administrative reconsideration organ.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, the application for administrative reconsideration shall be deemed accepted from the date it is received by the institution responsible for legal affairs within the administrative reconsideration organ.

Article 18 The county-level local people's government that accepts an application for administrative reconsideration in accordance with paragraph 2 of Article 15 of this Law shall, within seven days from the date of receiving the application, forward it to the relevant administrative reconsideration organ if the application falls within the jurisdiction of another administrative reconsideration organ under paragraph 1 of Article 15, and shall inform the applicant. The administrative reconsideration organ that receives the forwarded application shall handle it in accordance with Article 17 of this Law.

Article 19 Where laws or regulations require that an application for administrative reconsideration be made first before filing an administrative lawsuit, and the administrative reconsideration organ decides not to accept the application or fails to respond within the reconsideration period, the citizen, legal person, or other organization may, within 15 days from the date of receiving the decision not to accept or from the expiration of the reconsideration period, file an administrative lawsuit with a People's Court in accordance with the law.

Article 20 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization lawfully submits an application for administrative reconsideration, and the administrative reconsideration organ refuses to accept it without justifiable reasons, the administrative organ at the next higher level shall order it to accept the application; if necessary, the higher-level administrative organ may directly accept the application.
Article 21 During the period of administrative reconsideration, the specific administrative act shall not cease to be executed; however, execution may be suspended under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The respondent considers that execution needs to be suspended;

(2) The administrative reconsideration organ considers that execution needs to be suspended;

(3) The applicant applies for suspension of execution, and the administrative reconsideration organ considers the request reasonable and decides to suspend execution;

(4) The law provides for suspension of execution.

## Chapter V: Administrative Reconsideration Decision

Article 22 Administrative reconsideration shall, in principle, adopt the method of written review. However, if the applicant requests it or the institution responsible for legal affairs within the administrative reconsideration organ deems it necessary, investigations may be conducted with relevant organizations and individuals, and the opinions of the applicant, the respondent, and third parties may be heard.
Article 23 The institution responsible for legal affairs within the administrative reconsideration organ shall, within seven days from the date of accepting the application for administrative reconsideration, send a copy of the application for administrative reconsideration or a copy of the record of the oral application to the respondent. The respondent shall, within ten days from the date of receiving the copy of the application or the record, submit a written reply and provide the evidence, basis, and other relevant materials for the specific administrative act originally made.

The applicant and third parties may review the written reply submitted by the respondent, as well as the evidence, basis, and other relevant materials for the specific administrative act, unless they involve state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy; the administrative reconsideration organ shall not refuse such review.

Article 24 During the administrative reconsideration process, the respondent shall not collect evidence on its own from the applicant or other relevant organizations or individuals.

Article 25 Before the administrative reconsideration decision is made, if the applicant requests to withdraw the application for administrative reconsideration, the application may be withdrawn after explaining the reasons; upon withdrawal, the administrative reconsideration shall terminate.

Article 26 Where the applicant, when applying for administrative reconsideration, simultaneously submits an application for review of the provisions listed in Article 7 of this Law, and the administrative reconsideration organ has the authority to handle such provisions, it shall handle them in accordance with the law within 30 days; if it does not have such authority, it shall, within seven days, forward the application to the administrative organ with authority to handle it in accordance with statutory procedures, and that organ shall handle it within 60 days in accordance with the law. During the handling period, the review of the specific administrative act shall be suspended.

Article 27 Where the administrative reconsideration organ, when reviewing the specific administrative act made by the respondent, finds that the basis for the act is unlawful, and the organ has the authority to handle it, it shall handle it in accordance with the law within 30 days; if it does not have such authority, it shall, within seven days, forward the matter to the state organ with authority to handle it in accordance with statutory procedures. During the handling period, the review of the specific administrative act shall be suspended.

Article 28 The institution responsible for legal affairs within the administrative reconsideration organ shall review the specific administrative act made by the respondent, put forward opinions, and, after obtaining the approval of the person in charge of the administrative reconsideration organ or through collective discussion, make an administrative reconsideration decision in accordance with the following provisions:
(1) If the facts determined in the specific administrative act are clear, the evidence is conclusive, the applicable basis is correct, the procedures are lawful, and the content is appropriate, the decision shall be to uphold the act;

(2) If the respondent fails to perform its statutory duties, the decision shall be to order it to perform such duties within a specified period;

(3) If the specific administrative act falls under any of the following circumstances, the decision shall be to revoke, change, or confirm that the specific administrative act is unlawful; if the decision is to revoke or confirm that the act is unlawful, the respondent may be ordered to make a new specific administrative act within a specified period:

1. The main facts are unclear or the evidence is insufficient;
2. The applicable basis is erroneous;
3. The statutory procedures are violated;
4. The authority is exceeded or abused;
5. The specific administrative act is clearly inappropriate.

(4) If the respondent fails to submit a written reply or provide the evidence, basis, and other relevant materials for the specific administrative act originally made in accordance with Article 23 of this Law, the specific administrative act shall be deemed to have no evidence or basis, and the decision shall be to revoke the specific administrative act.

Where the administrative reconsideration organ orders the respondent to make a new specific administrative act, the respondent shall not make a specific administrative act that is identical or substantially identical to the original one based on the same facts and reasons.

Article 29 The applicant may simultaneously submit a claim for administrative compensation when applying for administrative reconsideration. If the administrative reconsideration organ finds that compensation should be granted in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Compensation Law, it shall, when deciding to revoke or change the specific administrative act or confirm that it is unlawful, simultaneously decide that the respondent shall grant compensation in accordance with the law.

If the applicant does not submit a claim for administrative compensation when applying for administrative reconsideration, and the administrative reconsideration organ decides in accordance with the law to revoke or change a fine, revoke illegal fundraising, confiscation of property, collection of property, apportionment of expenses, or specific administrative acts such as sealing up, seizing, or freezing property, it shall simultaneously order the respondent to return the property, lift the measures of sealing up, seizing, or freezing property, or compensate with the corresponding amount.

Article 30 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization believes that a specific administrative act of an administrative organ has infringed upon their lawfully obtained ownership or right to use natural resources such as land, mineral deposits, water bodies, forests, mountains, grasslands, wasteland, tidal flats, or sea areas, they shall first apply for administrative reconsideration; if dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, they may file an administrative lawsuit with a People's Court in accordance with the law.

An administrative reconsideration decision made by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, confirming the ownership or right to use natural resources such as land, mineral deposits, water bodies, forests, mountains, grasslands, wasteland, tidal flats, or sea areas, based on a decision of the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government regarding the delimitation or adjustment of administrative divisions or the expropriation of land, shall be final.

Article 31 The administrative reconsideration organ shall make an administrative reconsideration decision within 60 days from the date of accepting the application; however, if the law prescribes a shorter period for administrative reconsideration, that period shall apply. If the case is complex and a decision cannot be made within the prescribed period, the period may be appropriately extended with the approval of the person in charge of the administrative reconsideration organ, and the applicant and respondent shall be informed; however, the extension shall not exceed 30 days at most.

When making an administrative reconsideration decision, the administrative reconsideration organ shall prepare a written decision on administrative reconsideration and affix its seal.

The written decision on administrative reconsideration shall take legal effect upon service.

Article 32 The respondent shall implement the administrative reconsideration decision.

If the respondent fails to implement or delays implementation of the administrative reconsideration decision without justifiable reasons, the administrative reconsideration organ or the relevant higher-level administrative organ shall order it to implement within a specified period.
Article 33 If the applicant neither files a lawsuit within the prescribed period nor implements the administrative reconsideration decision, or fails to implement the final administrative reconsideration decision, the matter shall be handled separately in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) If the administrative reconsideration decision upholds the specific administrative act, the administrative organ that made the specific administrative act shall enforce it in accordance with the law, or apply to a People's Court for enforcement;

(2) If the administrative reconsideration decision changes the specific administrative act, the administrative reconsideration organ shall enforce it in accordance with the law, or apply to a People's Court for enforcement.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability**Article 34** Where an administrative reconsideration authority, in violation of this Law, refuses without justifiable grounds to accept an application for administrative reconsideration filed in accordance with the law, or fails to transfer the application as required, or fails to make an administrative reconsideration decision within the statutory time limit, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions of a warning, demerit, or serious demerit in accordance with the law; if, after being ordered to accept the application, they still refuse to accept it or fail to transfer it as required, thereby causing serious consequences, they shall be given administrative sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law.

**Article 35** Where a staff member of an administrative reconsideration authority engages in favoritism or other acts of dereliction or neglect of duty during administrative reconsideration activities, they shall be given administrative sanctions of a warning, demerit, or serious demerit in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be given administrative sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 36** Where the respondent, in violation of this Law, fails to submit a written reply or to provide the evidence, basis, and other relevant materials for the specific administrative act, or obstructs or covertly obstructs a citizen, legal person, or other organization from applying for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions of a warning, demerit, or serious demerit in accordance with the law; if they engage in retaliation or framing, they shall be given administrative sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 37** Where the respondent fails to perform or delays without justifiable grounds the performance of an administrative reconsideration decision, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions of a warning, demerit, or serious demerit in accordance with the law; if, after being ordered to perform, they still refuse to do so, they shall be given administrative sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or expulsion in accordance with the law.

**Article 38** Where the legal affairs institution of an administrative reconsideration authority discovers circumstances such as refusing without justifiable grounds to accept an application for administrative reconsideration, failing to make an administrative reconsideration decision within the prescribed time limit, engaging in favoritism, retaliating against the applicant, or failing to perform an administrative reconsideration decision, it shall make recommendations to the relevant administrative authority, and the relevant administrative authority shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 39** An administrative reconsideration authority shall not charge any fees to the applicant for accepting an application for administrative reconsideration. The funds required for administrative reconsideration activities shall be included in the administrative budget of the authority and guaranteed by the finance at the corresponding level of government.

**Article 40** The calculation of time periods during administrative reconsideration and the service of administrative reconsideration documents shall be executed in accordance with the provisions of the Civil Procedure Law concerning time periods and service.

The provisions of this Law concerning "five days" and "seven days" in relation to administrative reconsideration time periods refer to working days, excluding holidays.

**Article 41** This Law shall apply to applications for administrative reconsideration made within the territory of the People's Republic of China by foreigners, stateless persons, or foreign organizations.

**Article 42** Where provisions on administrative reconsideration in laws promulgated before the implementation of this Law are inconsistent with the provisions of this Law, the provisions of this Law shall prevail.

**Article 43** This Law shall come into force as of October 1, 1999. The "Regulations on Administrative Reconsideration" promulgated by the State Council on December 24, 1990, and revised and promulgated by the State Council on October 9, 1994, shall be abolished simultaneously.
