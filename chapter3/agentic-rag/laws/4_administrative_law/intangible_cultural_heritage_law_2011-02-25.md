# Intangible Cultural Heritage Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on February 25, 2011

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to inherit and promote the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation, promote the building of socialist spiritual civilization, and strengthen the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "intangible cultural heritage" means various forms of traditional cultural expressions that are passed down from generation to generation by people of all ethnic groups and regarded as part of their cultural heritage, as well as the objects and sites related to such traditional cultural expressions. It includes:

(1) Traditional oral literature and the language used as its carrier;

(2) Traditional fine arts, calligraphy, music, dance, drama, folk art, and acrobatics;

(3) Traditional skills, medicine, and calendar systems;

(4) Traditional rituals, festivals, and other folk customs;

(5) Traditional sports and recreational activities; and

(6) Other forms of intangible cultural heritage.

Where objects and sites that are part of intangible cultural heritage constitute cultural relics, the relevant provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics shall apply.

**Article 3** The State adopts measures such as identification, recording, and archiving to preserve intangible cultural heritage, and adopts measures such as transmission and dissemination to protect intangible cultural heritage that embodies the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation and has historical, literary, artistic, or scientific value.

**Article 4** The protection of intangible cultural heritage shall focus on its authenticity, integrity, and transmissibility, and shall be conducive to enhancing the cultural identity of the Chinese nation, maintaining national unity and ethnic solidarity, and promoting social harmony and sustainable development.

**Article 5** The use of intangible cultural heritage shall respect its form and connotation.

It is prohibited to use intangible cultural heritage in a distorted or derogatory manner.

**Article 6** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage into their national economic and social development plans, and include the funds for protection and preservation in their fiscal budgets.

The State supports the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage in ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

**Article 7** The competent department of culture under the State Council shall be responsible for the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage nationwide; the competent departments of culture under local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage within their respective administrative regions.

Other relevant departments under people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage within the scope of their respective duties.

**Article 8** People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the publicity of intangible cultural heritage protection work and raise public awareness of the protection of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 9** The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in the protection of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 10** Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the protection of intangible cultural heritage shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II Survey of Intangible Cultural Heritage

**Article 11** People's governments at or above the county level shall organize surveys of intangible cultural heritage as needed for its protection and preservation. Such surveys shall be conducted by the competent departments of culture.

Other relevant departments under people's governments at or above the county level may conduct surveys of intangible cultural heritage within their respective fields of work.

**Article 12** When conducting surveys of intangible cultural heritage, the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments shall identify, record, and archive the intangible cultural heritage, and establish and improve a mechanism for sharing survey information.

When conducting surveys of intangible cultural heritage, the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments shall collect representative objects that are part of the intangible cultural heritage, organize the materials obtained during the survey, and properly preserve them to prevent damage or loss. Copies of object images and materials obtained by other relevant departments shall be submitted to the competent department of culture at the same level.

**Article 13** The competent departments of culture shall comprehensively understand the relevant information about intangible cultural heritage and establish archives and related databases. Unless confidentiality is required by law, the archives and related data and information on intangible cultural heritage shall be made public for convenient public access.

**Article 14** Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations may conduct surveys of intangible cultural heritage in accordance with the law.

**Article 15** If an overseas organization or individual intends to conduct a survey of intangible cultural heritage within the territory of the People's Republic of China, it shall report to the competent department of culture of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval; if the survey is to be conducted in the administrative regions of two or more provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, it shall report to the competent department of culture under the State Council for approval; upon completion of the survey, it shall submit a survey report and copies of object images and materials obtained during the survey to the competent department of culture that approved the survey.

If an overseas organization conducts a survey of intangible cultural heritage within the territory of the People's Republic of China, it shall do so in cooperation with domestic academic research institutions on intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 16** When conducting a survey of intangible cultural heritage, the consent of the survey subjects shall be obtained, their customs and habits shall be respected, and their lawful rights and interests shall not be infringed upon.

**Article 17** For items of intangible cultural heritage discovered through surveys or other means that are on the verge of extinction, the competent department of culture of the people's government at the county level shall immediately record them and collect relevant objects, or take other emergency preservation measures; for those requiring transmission, effective measures shall be taken to support their transmission.

## Chapter III Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage Items

**Article 18** The State Council shall establish a national representative list of intangible cultural heritage items, listing items of intangible cultural heritage that embody the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation and have significant historical, literary, artistic, or scientific value for protection.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish local representative lists of intangible cultural heritage items, listing items of intangible cultural heritage within their respective administrative regions that embody the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation and have historical, literary, artistic, or scientific value for protection.

**Article 19** People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may recommend items from their local representative lists of intangible cultural heritage items to the competent department of culture under the State Council for inclusion in the national representative list. When making such recommendations, the following materials shall be submitted:

(1) An introduction to the item, including its name, history, current status, and value;

(2) An introduction to the transmission status, including the scope of transmission, transmission lineage, the skill level of the transmitters, and the social impact of transmission activities;

(3) Protection requirements, including the objectives to be achieved through protection and the measures, steps, and management systems to be adopted; and

(4) Audio-visual materials and other materials helpful for illustrating the item.

**Article 20** If citizens, legal persons, or other organizations believe that a certain item of intangible cultural heritage embodies the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation and has significant historical, literary, artistic, or scientific value, they may submit a proposal to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or to the competent department of culture under the State Council, for its inclusion in the national representative list.

**Article 21** If the form and connotation of the same item of intangible cultural heritage are fully preserved in two or more regions, it may be simultaneously included in the national representative list.

**Article 22** The competent department of culture under the State Council shall organize an expert review panel and an expert review committee to conduct preliminary reviews and deliberations on items of intangible cultural heritage recommended or proposed for inclusion in the national representative list.

A preliminary review opinion shall be adopted by a majority vote of the members of the expert review panel. The expert review committee shall deliberate on the preliminary review opinions and put forward deliberation opinions.

The review work shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality.

**Article 23** The competent department of culture under the State Council shall publicize the items proposed for inclusion in the national representative list to solicit public opinions. The publicity period shall be no less than twenty days.

**Article 24** The competent department of culture under the State Council shall, based on the deliberation opinions of the expert review committee and the results of the publicity, draft the national representative list and submit it to the State Council for approval and promulgation.

**Article 25** The competent department of culture under the State Council shall organize the formulation of protection plans for the protection of items on the national representative list.

The competent departments of culture of people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall organize the formulation of protection plans for the protection of items on the local representative lists approved and promulgated by the people's governments at the corresponding level.

When formulating protection plans for representative items of intangible cultural heritage, priority protection shall be given to representative items that are on the verge of extinction.

**Article 26** For specific areas where representative items of intangible cultural heritage are concentrated, have distinctive features, and whose form and connotation are fully preserved, the local competent department of culture may formulate special protection plans and, upon approval by the people's government at the corresponding level, implement regional overall protection. When deciding to implement regional overall protection for intangible cultural heritage, the wishes of local residents shall be respected, and the objects and sites that are part of the intangible cultural heritage shall be protected from damage.

If the implementation of regional overall protection involves the spatial planning of villages, towns, or neighborhoods where intangible cultural heritage is concentrated, the local competent department of urban and rural planning shall formulate special protection plans in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 27** The competent department of culture under the State Council and the competent departments of culture of people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall supervise and inspect the implementation of protection plans for representative items of intangible cultural heritage; if a protection plan is found not to be effectively implemented, corrective actions shall be taken promptly.

## Chapter IV Transmission and Dissemination of Intangible Cultural Heritage

**Article 28** The State encourages and supports the transmission and dissemination of representative items of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 29** The competent department of culture under the State Council and the competent departments of culture of people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may designate representative transmitters for representative items of intangible cultural heritage approved and promulgated by the people's governments at the corresponding level.

A representative transmitter of a representative item of intangible cultural heritage shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a proficient command of the intangible cultural heritage they transmit;

(2) Being representative within a specific field and having significant influence within a certain region; and

(3) Actively engaging in transmission activities.

When designating representative transmitters for representative items of intangible cultural heritage, the provisions of this Law concerning the review of representative items of intangible cultural heritage shall be applied mutatis mutandis, and the list of designated representative transmitters shall be made public.

**Article 30** The competent departments of culture of people's governments at or above the county level shall, as needed, take the following measures to support representative transmitters of representative items of intangible cultural heritage in carrying out transmission and dissemination activities:

(1) Providing necessary venues for transmission;

(2) Providing necessary funds to support their activities such as teaching apprentices, passing on skills, and conducting exchanges;

(3) Supporting their participation in public welfare activities; and

(4) Other measures to support their transmission and dissemination activities.

**Article 31** A representative transmitter of a representative item of intangible cultural heritage shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Carrying out transmission activities and cultivating successors;

(2) Properly preserving relevant objects and materials;

(3) Cooperating with the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments in conducting surveys of intangible cultural heritage; and

(4) Participating in public welfare publicity of intangible cultural heritage.

If a representative transmitter of a representative item of intangible cultural heritage fails to fulfill the obligations specified in the preceding paragraph without justifiable reasons, the competent department of culture may revoke their status as a representative transmitter and designate a new representative transmitter for the item; if the transmitter loses the ability to transmit, the competent department of culture may designate a new representative transmitter for the item.

**Article 32** People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual conditions, take effective measures to organize the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments to publicize and display representative items of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 33** The State encourages scientific and technological research related to intangible cultural heritage and research on methods for the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage, and encourages activities such as the recording of intangible cultural heritage and the organization and publication of representative items of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 34** Schools shall carry out relevant education on intangible cultural heritage in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of education under the State Council.

News media shall conduct publicity on representative items of intangible cultural heritage and popularize knowledge about intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 35** Public cultural institutions such as libraries, cultural centers, museums, and science and technology museums, as well as academic research institutions and protection institutions for intangible cultural heritage, and performing arts troupes and performance venue operators funded by fiscal funds, shall, within their respective business scopes, carry out the organization, research, and academic exchange of intangible cultural heritage, as well as the publicity and display of representative items of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 36** The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to establish venues for the display and transmission of intangible cultural heritage in accordance with the law, to display and transmit representative items of intangible cultural heritage.

**Article 37** The State encourages and supports leveraging the special advantages of intangible cultural heritage resources, and on the basis of effective protection, rationally utilizing representative items of intangible cultural heritage to develop cultural products and services with local and ethnic characteristics and market potential.

Those who develop and utilize representative items of intangible cultural heritage shall support representative transmitters in carrying out transmission activities and protect the objects and sites that are part of the item.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide support to entities that rationally utilize representative items of intangible cultural heritage. Entities that rationally utilize representative items of intangible cultural heritage shall enjoy tax incentives provided by the State in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

**Article 38** If staff members of the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments neglect their duties, abuse their power, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in the protection and preservation of intangible cultural heritage, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 39** If staff members of the competent departments of culture and other relevant departments, when conducting surveys of intangible cultural heritage, infringe upon the customs and habits of the survey subjects, causing serious consequences, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 40** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, damages objects and sites that are part of intangible cultural heritage shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, public security administration penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

**Article 41** If an overseas organization violates the provisions of Article 15 of this Law, the competent department of culture shall order it to make corrections, issue a warning, confiscate the illegal gains and the objects and materials obtained during the survey; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall also be imposed.

If an overseas individual violates the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 15 of this Law, the competent department of culture shall order them to make corrections, issue a warning, confiscate the illegal gains and the objects and materials obtained during the survey; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall also be imposed.

**Article 42** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

**Article 43** Measures for establishing local representative lists of intangible cultural heritage items shall be formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 44** Where the use of intangible cultural heritage involves intellectual property rights, the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations shall apply.

Where other laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise for the protection of traditional medicine, traditional arts and crafts, etc., those provisions shall prevail.

**Article 45** This Law shall come into force as of June 1, 2011.
