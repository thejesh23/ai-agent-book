# Law of the People's Republic of China on Prevention and Control of Desertification

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 31, 2001

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the Sixth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing land desertification, controlling desertified land, maintaining ecological security, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 All activities involving the prevention of land desertification, the control, development, and utilization of desertified land within the territory of the People's Republic of China must comply with this Law.

Land desertification refers to the process of natural desert expansion and the destruction of vegetation on sandy soil, resulting in exposed sandy soil, caused by climate change and human activities.

For the purposes of this Law, land desertification refers to the process of natural desert expansion and the destruction of vegetation and cover on sandy soil, primarily due to unreasonable human activities, leading to the formation of shifting sand and exposed sandy soil.

For the purposes of this Law, desertified land includes land that has already become desertified and land with a clear trend towards desertification. The specific scope shall be determined by the national plan for desertification prevention and control approved by the State Council.

Article 3 The work of desertification prevention and control shall follow the following principles:

(1) Unified planning, adaptation to local conditions, phased implementation, and adherence to the combination of regional prevention and control with key-point prevention and control;
(2) Prevention first, combining prevention with control, and comprehensive management;
(3) Combining the protection and restoration of vegetation with the rational use of natural resources;
(4) Following ecological laws and relying on scientific and technological progress;
(5) Combining the improvement of the ecological environment with helping farmers and herders to lift themselves out of poverty and become prosperous;
(6) Combining state support with local self-reliance, combining government organization with participation from all sectors of society, and encouraging units and individuals to contract for prevention and control;
(7) Safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of those engaged in desertification prevention and control.

Article 4 The State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall incorporate desertification prevention and control into their national economic and social development plans, and guarantee and support the development of desertification prevention and control work.

Local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located shall take effective measures to prevent land desertification, control desertified land, and protect and improve the ecological quality of their administrative regions.

The State shall establish a system of responsibility, assessment, reward, and punishment for government administrative leaders regarding desertification prevention and control during their term of office in areas where desertified land is located. Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall report on their desertification prevention and control work to the people's congresses at the corresponding level and their standing committees.

Article 5 Under the leadership of the State Council, the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland under the State Council shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, and guiding the national desertification prevention and control work.

The competent administrative departments for forestry and grassland, agriculture, water resources, land, ecological environment, and the meteorological authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with the duties stipulated by relevant laws and the division of responsibilities determined by the State Council, each assume their own responsibilities, cooperate closely, and jointly carry out the work of desertification prevention and control.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize and lead their relevant subordinate departments to, according to their respective responsibilities, each assume their own responsibilities, cooperate closely, and jointly carry out the work of desertification prevention and control within their administrative regions.

Article 6 Units and individuals using land have the obligation to prevent the desertification of that land.

Units and individuals using land that has already become desertified have the obligation to control that desertified land.

Article 7 The State supports scientific research and technology promotion for desertification prevention and control, leverages the role of scientific research departments and institutions in desertification prevention and control, cultivates specialized technical personnel for desertification prevention and control, and raises the scientific and technological level of desertification prevention and control.

The State supports international cooperation in desertification prevention and control.

Article 8 Units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in desertification prevention and control shall be commended and rewarded by the people's government; those who have made outstanding contributions to protecting and improving ecological quality shall be given substantial rewards.

Article 9 People's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located shall organize relevant departments to carry out publicity and education on desertification prevention and control knowledge, enhance citizens' awareness of desertification prevention and control, and improve citizens' ability to prevent and control desertification.

## Chapter II: Plans for Desertification Prevention and Control

Article 10 Desertification prevention and control shall be carried out under a unified plan. Activities engaged in desertification prevention and control, as well as development and utilization activities carried out within the scope of desertified land, must comply with the desertification prevention and control plan.

The desertification prevention and control plan shall clearly stipulate the time limits, steps, and measures for curbing the expansion trend of land desertification and gradually reducing desertified land, and shall incorporate specific implementation plans into the five-year plan and annual plan for national economic and social development.

Article 11 The competent administrative department for forestry and grassland under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments such as agriculture, water resources, land, and ecological environment under the State Council, formulate the national plan for desertification prevention and control, which shall be implemented after approval by the State Council.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the national plan for desertification prevention and control, formulate plans for desertification prevention and control for their respective administrative regions, which shall be implemented after approval by the State Council or the relevant department designated by the State Council.

The people's governments of cities and counties in areas where desertified land is located shall, based on the desertification prevention and control plan of the people's government at the next higher level, organize the formulation of plans for desertification prevention and control for their respective administrative regions, which shall be implemented after approval by the people's government at the next higher level.

Any modification to a desertification prevention and control plan must be approved by the original approving authority; without such approval, no unit or individual may alter the desertification prevention and control plan.

Article 12 When formulating a desertification prevention and control plan, classified protection, comprehensive management, and rational utilization of desertified land shall be implemented based on natural conditions such as the geographical location, land type, vegetation status, climate and water resource conditions, degree of desertification, and the ecological and economic functions they perform.

Contiguous desertified land that is not suitable for control within the planning period or that should not be developed or utilized due to the need for ecological protection shall be planned as closed desertified land protection areas and subject to closed protection. The scope of closed desertified land protection areas shall be determined by the national plan for desertification prevention and control and the plans of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 13 The desertification prevention and control plan shall be coordinated with the overall land use plan; the use of desertified land determined in the desertification prevention and control plan shall conform to the overall land use plan of the people's government at the corresponding level.

## Chapter III: Prevention of Land Desertification

Article 14 The competent administrative department for forestry and grassland under the State Council shall organize other relevant competent administrative departments to monitor, analyze, and compile statistics on the national land desertification situation, and regularly publish the monitoring results.

The competent administrative departments for forestry and grassland or other relevant competent administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall monitor desertified land in accordance with the technical regulations for land desertification monitoring, and report the monitoring results to the people's government at the corresponding level and the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland or other relevant competent administrative department at the next higher level.

Article 15 If, during the process of land desertification monitoring, the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland or other relevant competent administrative department of a local people's government at or above the county level discovers that land is becoming desertified or the degree of desertification is worsening, it shall promptly report to the people's government at the corresponding level. The people's government receiving the report shall instruct the relevant competent administrative department to stop the actions causing land desertification and take effective measures for control.

Meteorological authorities at various levels shall organize the monitoring and forecasting of meteorological droughts and sandstorm weather. When signs of meteorological drought or sandstorm weather are detected, they shall promptly report to the local people's government. The people's government receiving the report shall take preventive measures, publish disaster forecasts when necessary, and organize relevant departments such as forestry, grassland, and agriculture (animal husbandry) to take emergency measures to avoid or mitigate wind and sand hazards.

Article 16 Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall, in accordance with the desertification prevention and control plan, designate a certain proportion of land to establish windbreak and sand-fixing forest networks, forest belts, and plant perennial shrubs and herbaceous plants adapted to local conditions. The competent administrative department for forestry and grassland shall be responsible for determining the standards and specific tasks for the survival rate and preservation rate of afforestation, and organize their implementation plot by plot, clarifying responsibilities to ensure completion.

Except for logging for the purpose of tending and regeneration, no logging of windbreak and sand-fixing forest networks and forest belts shall be approved. Before carrying out logging for the purpose of tending and regeneration on windbreak and sand-fixing forest networks and forest belts, replacement forest networks and forest belts must be established nearby in advance.

For existing windbreak and sand-fixing forest networks and forest belts in areas where forest regeneration is difficult, no logging shall be approved.

Article 17 It is prohibited to cut or dig up shrubs, medicinal herbs, and other sand-fixing plants on desertified land.

The people's government at the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall formulate a vegetation management and protection system, strictly protect vegetation, and establish vegetation management and protection organizations in townships (towns) and villages as needed, designating management and protection personnel.

Within the scope of desertified land, all types of land contract agreements shall include provisions on the responsibility for vegetation protection.

Article 18 Local people's governments at various levels in grassland areas shall strengthen the management and construction of grasslands. The competent administrative department for forestry and grassland shall, together with the competent administrative department for animal husbandry, be responsible for guiding and organizing farmers and herders to establish artificial pastures, control livestock carrying capacity, adjust livestock structure, improve livestock breeds, promote livestock penning and rotational grazing, eliminate rodent and insect pests in grasslands, protect grassland vegetation, and prevent grassland degradation and desertification.

A system for determining livestock carrying capacity based on grass yield shall be implemented for grasslands. The competent administrative department for forestry and grassland shall, together with the competent administrative department for animal husbandry, be responsible for formulating standards and relevant regulations for livestock carrying capacity, and organize their implementation level by level, clarifying responsibilities to ensure completion.

Article 19 The competent administrative department for water resources of local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall strengthen the unified allocation and management of water resources in river basins and regions. When formulating plans for the development and utilization of water resources and water supply plans for river basins and regions, the water demand for vegetation protection in the entire river basin and region must be considered to prevent vegetation destruction and land desertification caused by the over-exploitation and utilization of groundwater and upstream water resources. Such plans and schedules, once approved, must be strictly implemented.

Local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located shall conserve water and develop water-saving agriculture, animal husbandry, and other industries.

Article 20 Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall not approve the reclamation of arable land in desert fringe areas, forest lands, and grasslands; if reclamation has already occurred and has had adverse ecological impacts, a planned conversion of farmland back to forest or grassland shall be organized.

Article 21 Units and individuals engaged in development and construction activities within the scope of desertified land must first conduct an environmental impact assessment regarding the potential impact of the project on the ecology of the local and related areas, and submit an environmental impact report in accordance with the law; the environmental impact report shall include content related to desertification prevention and control.

Article 22 Within closed desertified land protection areas, all activities that destroy vegetation are prohibited.

It is prohibited to relocate immigrants into closed desertified land protection areas. For farmers and herders within the scope of closed desertified land protection areas, the local people's government at or above the county level shall organize their relocation in a planned manner and provide proper resettlement. The production and livelihood of farmers and herders who have not yet been relocated from within the closed desertified land protection area shall be properly arranged by the competent department of the closed desertified land protection area.

Without the consent of the State Council or the department designated by the State Council, no construction activities such as building railways or highways shall be carried out within the scope of closed desertified land protection areas.

## Chapter IV: Control of Desertified Land

Article 23 Local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located shall, in accordance with the desertification prevention and control plan, organize relevant departments, units, and individuals to take measures such as artificial afforestation and grass planting, aerial seeding for afforestation and grass planting, closing hillsides for afforestation and grass cultivation, and rationally allocating ecological water use, tailored to local conditions, to restore and increase vegetation and control land that has already become desertified.

Article 24 The State encourages units and individuals, on a voluntary basis, to donate funds or carry out public welfare desertification control activities in other forms.

The competent administrative departments for forestry and grassland or other relevant competent administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide control sites and free technical guidance for public welfare desertification control activities.

Units and individuals engaged in public welfare desertification control shall carry out control in accordance with the technical requirements of the competent administrative departments for forestry and grassland or other relevant competent administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, and may entrust the planted trees and grass to others for management and protection or hand them over to the relevant competent administrative departments of the local people's government for management and protection.

Article 25 The right holders of state-owned desertified land and the contractual operators of collectively owned land must take control measures to improve land quality; if they are truly unable to complete the control task, they may entrust others to carry out the control or cooperate with others in the control. If entrusting or cooperating in control, an agreement shall be signed to clarify the rights and obligations of each party.

Local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located, along with their relevant competent administrative departments and technology promotion units, shall provide technical guidance to land right holders and contractual operators for their desertification control activities.

Land right holders and contractual operators who adopt measures such as converting farmland back to forest or grassland, afforestation and grass planting, or closure for afforestation to control desertification shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, enjoy policy preferences provided by the people's government.

Article 26 Units and individuals who do not have land ownership or use rights and engage in for-profit desertification control activities shall first sign an agreement with the land owner or right holder to obtain land use rights in accordance with the law.

Before starting the control activities, units and individuals engaged in for-profit desertification control activities shall submit a control application to the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland of the local people's government at or above the county level where the control project is located, or to another competent administrative department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and attach the following documents:

(1) Legal certification documents for the ownership of the land to be controlled and the control agreement;
(2) A control plan that conforms to the desertification prevention and control plan;
(3) Proof of funds required for the control.

Article 27 The term "control plan" referred to in Item (2) of Paragraph 2 of Article 26 of this Law shall include the following content:

(1) The boundaries of the control area;
(2) Phased control objectives and control deadlines;
(3) Main control measures;
(4) The water source and water consumption indicators approved by the local competent administrative department for water resources;
(5) The intended land use after control and vegetation management and protection measures;
(6) Other matters that need to be stated.

Article 28 Units and individuals engaged in for-profit desertification control activities must carry out the control in accordance with the control plan.

The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of those who control desertified land. Within the control area for which the controller has obtained legal land ownership or use rights, no other unit or individual may engage in control, development, or utilization activities without the consent of the controller.

Article 29 After completing the control task, the controller shall submit an application for acceptance inspection to the competent administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level that accepted the control application. If the control passes the acceptance inspection, the competent administrative department that accepted the application shall issue a certificate of qualified control; if the control fails the acceptance inspection, the controller shall continue the control.

Article 30 For railways, highways, rivers, and canal sides, and the surroundings of towns, villages, factories, mines, and reservoirs within the scope of desertified land, a unit control responsibility system shall be implemented. The local people's government at or above the county level shall issue a control responsibility letter, and the responsible unit shall be responsible for organizing afforestation, grass planting, or taking other control measures.

Article 31 Local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located may organize local rural collective economic organizations and their members, on a voluntary basis, to carry out centralized control of land that has already become desertified. The funds and labor invested by rural collective economic organizations and their members may be converted into shares or capital of the control project, or compensation may be provided in other forms.

## Chapter V: Safeguard Measures

Article 32 The State Council and local people's governments at various levels in areas where desertified land is located shall allocate funds in their fiscal budgets through project budgets in accordance with the desertification prevention and control plan for the desertification prevention and control projects determined by the people's government at the corresponding level. When arranging projects for poverty alleviation, agriculture, water resources, roads, minerals, energy, comprehensive agricultural development, etc., several sub-projects for desertification prevention and control shall be established based on specific circumstances.

Article 33 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate preferential policies to encourage and support units and individuals in desertification prevention and control.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations and based on the area and difficulty of desertification prevention and control, provide units and individuals engaged in desertification prevention and control activities with financial subsidies, fiscal interest discounts, and tax and fee reductions or exemptions.

Units and individuals investing in desertification prevention and control shall be exempted from various taxes during the investment phase; after obtaining certain income, relevant taxes may be exempted or reduced.

Article 34 Those who use state-owned desertified land for desertification control activities may, upon approval by the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law, enjoy land use rights for a period not exceeding seventy years. The specific term and management measures shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Those who use collectively owned desertified land for desertification control activities shall sign a land contract agreement with the land owner. The specific contract term and other rights and obligations of the parties shall be agreed upon by the contracting parties in the land contract agreement in accordance with the law. The people's government at the county level shall, in accordance with the law, issue a certificate of land use rights to the controller based on the land contract agreement, protecting the land use rights of those controlling collectively owned desertified land.

Article 35 If, due to special requirements for ecological protection, the controlled land is approved and designated as a nature reserve or a closed desertified land protection area, the approving authority shall provide reasonable economic compensation to the controller.

Article 36 The State shall, based on the needs of desertification prevention and control, organize the establishment of key scientific research projects and demonstration and promotion projects for desertification prevention and control, and provide financial subsidies, tax and fee reductions or exemptions, and other policy preferences for scientific research and technology promotion in areas such as desertification prevention and control, energy in sandy areas, sand-tolerant cash crops, water-saving irrigation, prevention of grassland degradation, and dryland farming on sandy land.

Article 37 No unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate funds for desertification prevention and control.

Audit authorities of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, exercise audit supervision over the use of funds for desertification prevention and control.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

Article 38 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 22 of this Law, engages in activities that destroy vegetation within a closed desertified land protection area shall be ordered by the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland of the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 39 If, in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 25 of this Law, the right holder of state-owned land or the contractual operator of collectively owned land fails to take desertification prevention and control measures, causing severe desertification of the land, the competent administrative department for forestry and grassland of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order control within a specified time limit; if severe desertification of state-owned land is caused, the people's government at or above the county level may withdraw the state-owned land use rights.

Article 40 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in for-profit desertification control activities, causing the aggravation of land desertification, shall be ordered by the competent administrative department responsible for accepting for-profit desertification control applications of the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, and may be concurrently fined not less than RMB 5,000 but not more than RMB 50,000 per hectare.Article 41 Where, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 28 of this Law, control is not carried out in accordance with the control plan, or where, in violation of the provisions of Article 29 of this Law, the control fails to pass inspection and is not continued as required, the competent administrative department responsible for accepting applications for profit-making desertification control under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act, set a time limit for correction, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the control cost.

Article 42 Where, in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 28 of this Law, without the consent of the controller, activities of control or development and utilization are engaged in within the control area of another person, the competent administrative department responsible for accepting applications for profit-making desertification control under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act; if losses are caused to the controller, compensation for the losses shall be made.

Article 43 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following circumstances occurs, the person directly in charge and other persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions by their unit, the supervisory authority, or the competent administrative department at a higher level in accordance with the law:

(1) In violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 15 of this Law, failing to promptly report when discovering that land has become desertified or the degree of desertification has worsened, or failing to instruct the relevant administrative department to take measures after receiving a report;

(2) In violation of the provisions of the second and third paragraphs of Article 16 of this Law, approving the felling of windbreak and sand-fixing forest networks and belts;

(3) In violation of the provisions of Article 20 of this Law, approving the reclamation of farmland in desert fringe areas, forest land, or grassland;

(4) In violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 22 of this Law, resettling migrants within the closed protection area of desertified land;

(5) In violation of the provisions of the third paragraph of Article 22 of this Law, engaging in construction activities such as building railways or highways within the closed protection area of desertified land without approval.

Article 44 Where, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 37 of this Law, funds for desertification prevention and control are intercepted or misappropriated, the person directly in charge and other persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions by the supervisory authority or the competent administrative department at a higher level in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Where supervisory and administrative personnel for desertification prevention and control abuse their power, neglect their duty, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, and if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 46 The relevant laws referred to in the second paragraph of Article 5 of this Law refer to the Forest Law of the People's Republic of China, the Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China, the Water and Soil Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China, the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, and the Meteorology Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 47 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2002.
