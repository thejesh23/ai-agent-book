# Vocational Education Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

Revised at the 34th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 20, 2022

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to promote the high-quality development of vocational education, improve the quality of workers and their technical and skill levels, facilitate employment and entrepreneurship, build an educationally strong country, a country strong in human resources, and a skill-based society, and advance socialist modernization.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "vocational education" refers to education implemented to cultivate high-quality technical and skilled talents, enabling educatees to possess the professional ethics, scientific and cultural knowledge, professional knowledge, technical skills, and other comprehensive vocational qualities and action capabilities required to engage in a certain occupation or achieve career development. This includes both vocational school education and vocational training.

Specialized training provided by state organs and public institutions to their staff shall be governed by separate laws and administrative regulations.

Article 3 Vocational education is a type of education with equal importance to general education. It is an integral part of the national education system and human resource development, and an important pathway for cultivating diverse talents, transmitting technical skills, and promoting employment and entrepreneurship.

The State vigorously develops vocational education, advances vocational education reform, improves the quality of vocational education, enhances its adaptability, and establishes and improves a vocational education system and institutions that meet the needs of the socialist market economy and social development, conform to the growth patterns of technical and skilled talents, thereby providing strong talent and skill support for comprehensively building a modern socialist country.

Article 4 Vocational education must adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, uphold the socialist orientation in running schools, implement the national education policy, persist in fostering virtue through education and cultivating both moral integrity and technical competence, insist on the integration of industry and education and school-enterprise cooperation, adhere to market orientation and employment promotion, emphasize practice and capability enhancement, and commit to education for all with individualized instruction.

In implementing vocational education, the core socialist values shall be promoted. Educatees shall receive ideological and political education and professional ethics education. The spirit of model workers, the spirit of labor, and the spirit of craftsmanship shall be cultivated. Scientific, cultural, and professional knowledge shall be imparted, technical skills shall be cultivated, career guidance shall be provided, and the overall quality of educatees shall be comprehensively improved.

Article 5 Citizens have the right to receive vocational education in accordance with the law.

Article 6 Vocational education shall be implemented under the principles of government overall planning, tiered management, local responsibility, industry guidance, school-enterprise cooperation, and social participation.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate the development of vocational education into their national economic and social development plans, and coordinate its implementation with the overall deployment of promoting employment and entrepreneurship, transforming the development model, adjusting the industrial structure, and optimizing and upgrading technology.

Article 8 The State Council shall establish a coordination mechanism for vocational education work to coordinate vocational education work nationwide.

The education administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for the overall planning, comprehensive coordination, and macro-management of vocational education work. The education administrative department, the human resources and social security administrative department, and other relevant departments of the State Council shall, within their respective responsibilities as defined by the State Council, be responsible for relevant vocational education work.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strengthen leadership over vocational education work within their respective administrative regions, clarify the specific responsibilities of the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties for vocational education work, coordinate the development of vocational education, and organize supervision and evaluation.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen communication and cooperation to jointly promote vocational education work.

Article 9 The State encourages the development of vocational education at multiple levels and in various forms, promotes diversified school-running, and supports the broad and equal participation of social forces in vocational education.

The State gives full play to the important role of enterprises as main school-running entities, promotes deep enterprise participation in vocational education, and encourages enterprises to run high-quality vocational education.

Relevant industry competent departments, trade unions, the All-China Vocational Education Society, and other mass organizations, industry organizations, enterprises, and public institutions shall, in accordance with the law, fulfill their obligations to implement vocational education, and participate in, support, or conduct vocational education.

Article 10 The State shall take measures to vigorously develop technician education and comprehensively improve the quality of industrial workers.

The State shall take measures to support the provision of vocational education for rural areas, organize agricultural skills training, entrepreneurship and employment training for returning home, and vocational skills training, to cultivate high-quality talents for rural revitalization.

The State shall take measures to support the development of vocational education in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and underdeveloped areas.

The State shall take measures to organize various types of job transfer, re-employment, unemployed personnel, and special groups to receive various forms of vocational education, and support the development of vocational education for persons with disabilities.

The State guarantees women's equal right to receive vocational education.

Article 11 In implementing vocational education, education standards or training plans shall be formulated based on economic and social development needs, combined with occupational classification, occupational standards, and career development needs. A system of academic certificates and other academic certificates, training certificates, vocational qualification certificates, and vocational skill level certificates shall be implemented.

The State implements a system requiring workers to receive necessary vocational education before employment or taking up a post.

Article 12 The State shall take measures to enhance the social status and treatment of technical and skilled talents, and promote a social trend of honoring labor, valuing skills, and celebrating creation.

The State shall commend and reward units and individuals that have made significant achievements in vocational education work in accordance with relevant regulations.

The second week of May each year is designated as Vocational Education Activity Week.

Article 13 The State encourages international exchanges and cooperation in the field of vocational education, supports the introduction of high-quality overseas resources for the development of vocational education, encourages qualified vocational education institutions to run schools abroad, and supports the mutual recognition of various forms of vocational education learning outcomes.

## Chapter II Vocational Education System

Article 14 The State establishes and improves a modern vocational education system that meets the needs of economic and social development, deeply integrates industry and education, places equal emphasis on vocational school education and vocational training, promotes mutual integration between vocational education and general education, ensures effective articulation between different levels of vocational education, and serves lifelong learning for all.

The State optimizes the educational structure, scientifically allocates educational resources, and promotes the coordinated development of vocational education and general education in a manner tailored to local conditions at different stages after compulsory education.

Article 15 Vocational school education is divided into secondary vocational school education and higher vocational school education.

Secondary vocational school education is implemented by secondary vocational schools (including technical schools) at the upper secondary education level.

Higher vocational school education is implemented by higher vocational schools and general institutions of higher education at the junior college, undergraduate, and higher education levels. In accordance with the regulations on the establishment of higher vocational schools, qualified technician colleges shall be included in the sequence of higher vocational schools.

Other schools, educational institutions, or qualified enterprises and industry organizations may, in accordance with the overall planning of the education administrative department, implement corresponding levels of vocational school education or provide credit courses included in talent training programs.

Article 16 Vocational training includes pre-employment training, on-the-job training, re-employment training, and other vocational training, which may be implemented by level and type according to actual circumstances.

Vocational training may be implemented by corresponding vocational training institutions or vocational schools.

Other schools or educational institutions, as well as enterprises and social organizations, may, based on their school-running capabilities and social needs, lawfully conduct various forms of vocational training for the public.

Article 17 The State establishes and improves mechanisms for the certification, accumulation, and conversion of credits, qualifications, and other learning outcomes from school education at all levels and types and vocational training, promotes the construction of a national vocational education credit bank, and facilitates the integration and mutual recognition of learning outcomes between vocational education and general education.

Military vocational skill levels shall be incorporated into the national vocational qualification certification and vocational skill level evaluation system.

Article 18 In addition to being implemented by educational institutions for persons with disabilities, vocational education for persons with disabilities shall be provided by vocational schools and vocational training institutions at all levels and types, as well as other educational institutions, which shall accept students with disabilities in accordance with relevant state regulations, strengthen the construction of barrier-free environments, and provide necessary assistance and convenience for the study and life of students with disabilities.

The State shall take measures to support educational institutions for persons with disabilities, vocational schools, vocational training institutions, and other educational institutions in conducting or jointly conducting vocational education for persons with disabilities.

Special education teachers engaged in vocational education for persons with disabilities shall enjoy special education allowances in accordance with regulations.

Article 19 The education administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall encourage and support general primary and secondary schools and general institutions of higher education to increase teaching content related to vocational education according to actual needs, conduct vocational enlightenment, vocational cognition, and vocational experience, provide career planning guidance and labor education, and organize and guide vocational schools, vocational training institutions, enterprises, and industry organizations to provide conditions and support.

## Chapter III Implementation of Vocational Education

Article 20 The education administrative department of the State Council, together with relevant departments, shall organize the formulation and revision of vocational education specialty catalogs, improve vocational education teaching and other standards, and macro-manage and guide the construction of teaching materials for vocational schools, based on the needs of economic and social development and the characteristics of vocational education.

Article 21 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish or participate in establishing vocational schools and vocational training institutions that play a backbone and exemplary role, and provide guidance and support to vocational schools and vocational training institutions lawfully established by social forces.

The State, based on industrial layout and industry development needs, shall take measures to vigorously develop emerging specialties needed by industries such as advanced manufacturing, and support the construction of high-level vocational schools and specialties.

The State shall take measures to accelerate the cultivation of technical and skilled talents in areas such as childcare, nursing, health care, and home services.

Article 22 People's governments at the county level may, based on the needs of county economic and social development, establish vocational education center schools to conduct various forms of vocational education and provide practical technical training.

Education administrative departments may entrust vocational education center schools to undertake public management and service tasks for vocational education, such as teaching guidance, education quality evaluation, and teacher training.

Article 23 Industry competent departments shall strengthen guidance for vocational education based on industry and industrial talent needs, and regularly publish talent demand information.

Industry competent departments, trade unions, the All-China Vocational Education Society, and other mass organizations, and industry organizations may, as needed, participate in formulating vocational education specialty catalogs and relevant vocational education standards, conduct talent demand forecasting, career development research, and information consultation, cultivate industry-education integration service organizations that match supply and demand, establish or jointly establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions, and organize, coordinate, and guide relevant enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations to establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

Article 24 Enterprises shall, based on their actual circumstances, systematically implement vocational education for their employees and personnel to be recruited, and may establish full-time or part-time positions for implementing vocational education.

Enterprises shall implement a training-before-employment system in accordance with relevant state regulations. Workers engaged in technical trades recruited by enterprises must receive safety production education and technical training before taking up their posts; workers recruited for specific occupations (trades) involving public safety, personal health, life and property safety, etc., must undergo training and lawfully obtain vocational qualifications or special operation qualifications.

The implementation of vocational education by enterprises shall be included in their corporate social responsibility reports.

Article 25 Enterprises may utilize factors such as capital, technology, knowledge, facilities, equipment, venues, and management to establish or jointly establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

Article 26 The State encourages, guides, and supports enterprises and other social forces to lawfully establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

Local people's governments at all levels shall support vocational schools and vocational training institutions lawfully established by enterprises and other social forces by adopting measures such as purchasing services, providing student loans, scholarships, and grants to students. For non-profit vocational schools and vocational training institutions among them, support measures such as government subsidies, fund rewards, and donation incentives may also be adopted, and appropriate subsidies shall be provided with reference to the per-student funding standards and support policies for similar public schools at the same level.

Article 27 Enterprises that deeply participate in the integration of industry and education and school-enterprise cooperation, and play a significant main role in improving the quality of technical and skilled talent cultivation and promoting employment, shall be rewarded in accordance with regulations. Enterprises that are recognized as industry-education integration enterprises upon meeting the conditions shall be supported in terms of finance, fiscal policy, and land in accordance with regulations, and shall enjoy reductions or exemptions from education surcharges, local education surcharges, and other tax and fee concessions.

Article 28 Where vocational schools or vocational training institutions are jointly established, the sponsors shall sign a joint school-running agreement stipulating the rights and obligations of each party.

Local people's governments at all levels and industry competent departments shall support social forces in lawfully participating in joint school-running and establishing various forms of vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

Where industry competent departments, trade unions and other mass organizations, industry organizations, enterprises, public institutions, etc., entrust schools or vocational training institutions to implement vocational education, they shall sign an entrustment contract.

Article 29 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of internship and training bases for vocational education, organize industry competent departments, trade unions and other mass organizations, industry organizations, enterprises, etc., to build high-level, specialized, open, and shared industry-education integration internship and training bases based on regional or industry vocational education needs, and provide conditions and support for vocational schools and vocational training institutions to carry out internships and training and for enterprises to conduct training.

Article 30 The State promotes the Chinese-style apprenticeship system, guides enterprises to set up apprenticeship positions in a certain proportion of the total number of posts, encourages and supports enterprises with the capacity to cultivate technical and skilled talents, especially industry-education integration enterprises, to cooperate with vocational schools and vocational training institutions to provide apprenticeship training for newly recruited employees, on-the-job employees, and job-transfer employees, or to jointly recruit students with vocational schools for apprenticeship training through a combination of work and study. Relevant enterprises may enjoy subsidies in accordance with regulations.

Where enterprises jointly recruit students with vocational schools for apprenticeship training through a combination of work and study, they shall sign an apprenticeship training agreement.

Article 31 The State encourages industry organizations, enterprises, etc., to participate in the development of professional teaching materials for vocational education, incorporate new technologies, new processes, and new concepts into vocational school textbooks, and allow for dynamic updates through various methods such as loose-leaf textbooks; supports the use of information technology and other modern teaching methods to develop online courses and other learning resources for vocational education, innovate teaching methods and school management methods, and promote the informatization construction and integrated application of vocational education.

Article 32 The State, by organizing activities such as vocational skills competitions, provides a platform for technical and skilled talents to showcase their skills and learn from each other, continuously cultivating more high-quality technical and skilled talents, skilled craftsmen, and master craftsmen of the nation.

## Chapter IV Vocational Schools and Vocational Training Institutions

Article 33 The establishment of a vocational school shall meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Having an organizational structure and articles of association;
(2) Having qualified teachers and management personnel;
(3) Having teaching and internship/training venues, facilities, and equipment that are suitable for the vocational education to be implemented, meet prescribed standards and safety requirements, as well as a curriculum system, educational and teaching resources, etc.;
(4) Having the necessary school-running funds and a stable source of funding commensurate with the scale of the school.

The establishment of a secondary vocational school shall be approved by the local people's government at or above the county level or relevant departments within the prescribed authority; the establishment of a higher vocational school implementing junior college level education shall be approved by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and reported to the education administrative department of the State Council for recordation; the establishment of a higher vocational school implementing undergraduate level or higher education shall be approved by the education administrative department of the State Council.

Some specialties set up by higher vocational schools at the junior college level for cultivating high-end technical and skilled talents, which meet conditions such as deep integration of industry and education, distinctive school-running characteristics, and high training quality, may, upon approval by the education administrative department of the State Council, implement vocational education at the undergraduate level.

Article 34 The establishment of a vocational training institution shall meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Having an organizational structure and management system;
(2) Having a curriculum system, teachers or other instructors, and management personnel suitable for the training tasks;
(3) Having venues, facilities, and equipment suitable for the training tasks and meeting safety requirements;
(4) Having corresponding funds.

The establishment, modification, and termination of vocational training institutions shall be carried out in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 35 Public vocational schools shall implement a principal responsibility system under the leadership of the grassroots organization of the Communist Party of China in the vocational school. The grassroots organization of the Communist Party of China in the vocational school shall, in accordance with the Constitution of the Communist Party of China and relevant regulations, comprehensively lead the school's work and support the principal in independently and responsibly exercising their authority. Private vocational schools shall improve their decision-making mechanisms in accordance with the law, strengthen the political function of the grassroots organization of the Communist Party of China in the school, and ensure its effective role in the decision-making, supervision, and implementation of major school affairs.

The principal shall be fully responsible for the school's teaching, scientific research, and other administrative management work. The principal shall exercise their authority through the principal's office meeting or the school affairs meeting, and shall accept supervision in accordance with the law.

Vocational schools may, through various forms such as consultation and discussion, listen to the opinions of representatives from industry organizations, enterprises, school graduates, etc., and leverage their role in participating in school construction and supporting school development.

Article 36 Vocational schools shall run schools in accordance with the law and manage themselves autonomously based on their articles of association.

In running schools, vocational schools may carry out the following activities:

(1) Autonomously set up specialties based on industrial needs, in accordance with the law;
(2) Formulate talent training plans based on vocational education standards, and autonomously select or compile professional course textbooks in accordance with the law;
(3) Autonomously establish learning systems and arrange teaching processes based on the needs of cultivating technical and skilled talents;
(4) On the basis of the basic length of schooling, appropriately adjust the duration of study and implement a flexible learning system;
(5) Autonomously select and appoint professional course teachers in accordance with the law.

Article 37 The State establishes an examination and enrollment system that conforms to the characteristics of vocational education.

Secondary vocational schools may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, implement integrated enrollment and cultivation with higher vocational school education in relevant specialties.

Higher vocational schools may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, adopt an assessment method combining cultural quality and vocational skills to recruit students; for technical and skilled talents with outstanding contributions, they may be admitted exceptionally upon passing the assessment.

The education administrative departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level, together with relevant departments of the people's governments at the same level, shall establish a unified enrollment platform for vocational education, collect and publish information on schools implementing vocational education, their specialty settings, enrollment situations, etc., and provide services such as inquiry and application.

Article 38 Vocational schools shall strengthen the construction of school spirit and academic atmosphere, as well as teacher ethics and conduct, create a good learning environment, and ensure the quality of education and teaching.

Article 39 Vocational schools shall establish and improve mechanisms for promoting employment and entrepreneurship, and provide students with employment and entrepreneurship services such as career planning, vocational experience, and job-seeking guidance in various forms, to enhance students' employability and entrepreneurial capabilities.

Article 40 Vocational schools and vocational training institutions implementing vocational education shall focus on the integration of industry and education and implement school-enterprise cooperation.Vocational schools and vocational training institutions may cooperate with industry organizations, enterprises, public institutions, etc., by jointly establishing vocational education institutions, forming vocational education groups, conducting order-based training, and other forms.

The State encourages vocational schools to establish cooperation mechanisms with relevant industry organizations, enterprises, public institutions, etc., in areas such as enrollment and employment, formulation of talent training plans, construction of teaching staff, professional planning, curriculum design, textbook development, teaching design, teaching implementation, quality evaluation, scientific research, technical services, transformation of scientific and technological achievements, as well as the construction of technical skill innovation platforms, specialized technology transfer institutions, and internship and training bases. Where cooperation is carried out, an agreement shall be signed to clarify the rights and obligations of both parties.

Article 41: Income obtained by vocational schools and vocational training institutions from school-enterprise cooperation, provision of social services, or from running enterprises or engaging in business activities for the purpose of internship and training shall be used to improve school conditions; a certain proportion of such income may be used to pay labor remuneration for teachers, enterprise experts, external personnel, and educatees, and may also serve as a source of performance-based pay, which, if meeting national regulations, may not be subject to the total limit on performance-based pay.

Where vocational schools and vocational training institutions carry out the activities specified in the preceding paragraph and comply with relevant national regulations, they shall enjoy relevant tax and fee preferential policies.

Article 42: Vocational schools shall charge tuition fees and other necessary fees in accordance with the prescribed charging standards and methods; where conditions stipulated by the State are met, fees shall be reduced or exempted; fees shall not be illegally charged under the pretext of job placement, arranging internships or training, etc.

Where vocational training institutions and vocational schools provide training to the public, they shall charge fees in accordance with relevant national regulations.

Article 43: Vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall establish and improve an education quality evaluation system, involve industry organizations, enterprises, etc., in the evaluation, and promptly disclose relevant information, subject to educational supervision and social oversight.

The education administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments and industry organizations, establish a quality evaluation system that meets the characteristics of vocational education, organize or entrust industry organizations, enterprises, and third-party professional institutions to evaluate the school-running quality of vocational schools, and promptly make the evaluation results public.

The quality evaluation of vocational education shall highlight the employment orientation, taking the professional ethics, technical skill level, and employment quality of educatees as important indicators, guiding vocational schools to cultivate high-quality technical and skilled talents.

Relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective duties, strengthen the supervision and management of vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

## Chapter 5: Teachers and Educatees in Vocational Education

Article 44: The State guarantees the rights of teachers in vocational education and improves their professional quality and social status.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall incorporate the training and development of vocational education teachers into the teacher team construction plan, ensuring that the vocational education teacher team meets the needs of vocational education development.

Article 45: The State establishes and improves a system for the training and development of vocational education teachers.

People's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen the professional training and development of vocational education teachers, encourage the establishment of specialized vocational education normal universities, support higher education institutions in establishing relevant majors to cultivate vocational education teachers; encourage industry organizations and enterprises to jointly participate in the training and development of vocational education teachers.

Industry-education integrated enterprises and enterprises above a designated scale shall allocate a certain proportion of positions to accommodate the practice of teachers from vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

Article 46: The State establishes and improves a system for the setting of positions and the evaluation and appointment of professional titles (job titles) for teachers in vocational schools that conforms to the characteristics and development requirements of vocational education.

Professional course teachers (including internship guidance teachers) in vocational schools shall have a certain number of years of corresponding work experience or practical experience and reach the corresponding technical skill level.

Management and professional technical personnel from qualified enterprises and public institutions, as well as other persons with specialized knowledge or special skills, who have passed the training in education and teaching ability, may serve as full-time or part-time professional course teachers in vocational schools; those who have obtained teacher qualifications may be appointed to corresponding teaching positions based on their technical titles. Obtaining the qualification for professional course teachers in vocational schools may, depending on the circumstances, lower the academic requirements.

Article 47: The State encourages vocational schools to employ highly skilled talents such as skill masters, model workers, skilled craftsmen, and representative inheritors of intangible cultural heritage, to participate in talent cultivation, technology development, skill inheritance, etc., by serving as full-time or part-time professional course teachers or establishing studios.

Article 48: The State formulates basic standards for the staffing of faculty and staff in vocational schools. Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the basic standards, formulate local standards for the staffing of faculty and staff in vocational schools.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the staffing standards for faculty and staff, school scale, etc., determine the size of the faculty and staff for public vocational schools, a certain proportion of which may be used to support vocational schools in openly recruiting professional technical personnel and skilled talents from society to serve as full-time or part-time teachers.

Article 49: Students in vocational schools shall abide by laws, regulations, and student conduct norms, cultivate good professional ethics, professional spirit, and behavioral habits, study diligently, complete the prescribed learning tasks, participate in internships and training as required, and master technical skills.

The legitimate rights and interests of students in vocational schools are protected by law.

Article 50: The State encourages enterprises and public institutions to arrange internship positions and accept students from vocational schools and vocational training institutions for internships. The accepting units shall ensure that students enjoy rights such as rest and leave, access to labor safety and health protection, participation in relevant insurance, and receipt of vocational skill guidance during the internship period in accordance with regulations; for those taking up internships, an internship agreement shall be signed, and appropriate labor remuneration shall be provided.

Vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall strengthen guidance for students in internships and training, strengthen safety production education, negotiate with internship units to arrange positions matching the students' majors, clarify the content and standards of internships and training, and shall not arrange for students to engage in internships or training unrelated to their majors, nor shall they violate relevant regulations by organizing, arranging, or managing student internships and training through human resources service agencies, labor dispatch units, or units or individuals illegally engaged in human resources services or labor dispatch business.

Article 51: Those who receive vocational school education, meet the corresponding academic requirements, and pass the school's assessment shall obtain the corresponding academic certificate; those who receive vocational training and pass the assessment of the vocational training institution or vocational school shall obtain the corresponding training certificate; those who pass the assessment of a specialized institution that meets national regulations shall obtain the corresponding vocational qualification certificate or vocational skill level certificate.

Academic certificates, training certificates, vocational qualification certificates, and vocational skill level certificates shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, serve as credentials for the educatees to engage in employment.

Learning outcomes such as vocational skill level certificates and training certificates obtained from vocational training, upon recognition by the vocational school, may be converted into corresponding academic education credits; those who meet the academic requirements of the corresponding vocational school may obtain the corresponding academic certificate.

Those receiving higher vocational school education whose academic level meets the national degree standards may apply for corresponding degrees in accordance with the law.

Article 52: The State establishes a system of rewards and financial aid for students in vocational schools, rewarding exceptionally outstanding students, providing financial aid to students with economic difficulties, and giving appropriate preference to students in majors related to arduous or special industries. The State adjusts the standards for rewards and financial aid in a timely manner based on economic and social development.

The State supports enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individual citizens in establishing vocational education scholarships and grants in accordance with relevant national regulations to reward outstanding students and provide financial aid to students with economic difficulties.

Vocational schools shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, allocate a certain proportion of funds from their operating income or tuition income for rewarding and providing financial aid to students.

The relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall improve the management system for vocational school financial aid funds and standardize the management and use of such funds.

Article 53: Students in vocational schools enjoy equal opportunities with students in ordinary schools of the same level in terms of further education, employment, and career development.

Higher vocational schools and ordinary higher education institutions implementing vocational education shall determine a corresponding proportion in their enrollment plans or adopt separate examination methods to specifically recruit graduates from vocational schools.

People's governments at all levels shall create a fair employment environment. Employers shall not set application, recruitment, or employment conditions that hinder the equal employment and fair competition of graduates from vocational schools. When recruiting or hiring for technical and skilled positions, state organs, public institutions, and state-owned enterprises shall specify technical skill requirements and take the level of technical skills as an important condition for recruitment and employment. For positions with vocational skill level requirements in the open recruitment of public institutions, academic requirements may be appropriately lowered.

## Chapter 6: Guarantees for Vocational Education

Article 54: The State optimizes the structure of education expenditure, making the investment in vocational education funds commensurate with the development needs of vocational education, and encourages the raising of funds for the development of vocational education through multiple channels in accordance with the law.

Article 55: People's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the principle of matching powers and expenditure responsibilities, allocate funds for vocational education based on the scale of vocational education, training costs, and school-running quality, and strengthen budget performance management to improve the efficiency of fund utilization.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate local standards for per-student funding or public funding for vocational schools. The sponsors of vocational schools shall allocate funds on time and in full according to the per-student funding standards or public funding standards, and continuously improve school conditions. Tuition fees or income from social services shall not be used to offset per-student appropriations.

Sponsors of private vocational schools shall raise funds through multiple channels with reference to the per-student funding standards of vocational schools of the same level.

No organization or individual may misappropriate or withhold funds specifically allocated by financial special arrangements or donated for vocational education.

Article 56: When local people's governments at all levels arrange funds from local education surcharges and other sources, they shall coordinate the use of funds that can be used for vocational education; leverage the role of the unemployment insurance fund to support employees in improving their vocational skills.
Article 57: People's governments at all levels increase investment in vocational education for rural areas and may appropriately use funds for rural science and technology development and technology promotion for rural vocational training.

Article 58: Enterprises shall, in accordance with the standards set by the State Council, withdraw and use employee education funds based on a certain proportion of the total employee wages. Employee education funds may be used for reasonable purposes such as running vocational education institutions, providing vocational education to their own employees and those to be recruited, among which the funds used for vocational education of front-line employees shall reach the proportion stipulated by the State. Where employers arrange for employees to receive vocational education at vocational schools or vocational training institutions, they shall pay wages in accordance with the law during the period of receiving vocational education and guarantee relevant benefits.

The expenses incurred by enterprises in establishing industry-education integrated internship and training bases with both production and teaching functions may enjoy corresponding preferential treatment in terms of land use and public utility fees with reference to vocational schools.

Article 59: The State encourages financial institutions to support the development of vocational education by providing financial services.

Article 60: The State encourages enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individual citizens to donate to vocational education, and encourages organizations and individuals outside the country to provide financial aid and donations for vocational education. The financial aid and donations provided must be used for vocational education.

Article 61: The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research in vocational education, the development of textbooks and teaching resources, and promotes the cross-regional, cross-industry, and cross-departmental co-construction and sharing of vocational education resources.

The State gradually establishes an information statistics and management system that reflects the characteristics and functions of vocational education.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall establish and improve a service and guarantee system for vocational education, organize and guide trade unions and other mass organizations, industry organizations, enterprises, schools, etc., to carry out activities such as vocational education research, publicity and promotion, and talent supply-demand matching.

Article 62: News media and relevant parties in vocational education shall actively carry out public welfare publicity for vocational education, promote the typical deeds of technical and skilled talents in growing and becoming successful, and create a good social atmosphere where everyone strives to become talented, everyone can become talented, and everyone can fully display their talents.

## Chapter 7: Legal Liability

Article 63: Violations of the provisions of the Education Law of the People's Republic of China, the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws in vocational education activities shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the relevant laws.

Article 64: Where an enterprise fails to implement vocational education for its own employees and those to be recruited, or fails to withdraw and use employee education funds in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the relevant department shall order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, the people's government at or above the county level shall collect the employee education funds it should bear for vocational education.

Article 65: Where a vocational school or vocational training institution violates the provisions of this Law in vocational education activities, the education administrative department or other relevant departments shall order it to make corrections; if the quality of education and teaching is low or management is chaotic, causing serious consequences, it shall be ordered to suspend enrollment and rectify within a time limit; if it fails to rectify within the time limit or still fails to meet the requirements after rectification, its school-running license shall be revoked or it shall be ordered to cease operation.

Article 66: Where a unit that accepts students from vocational schools and vocational training institutions for internships violates the provisions of this Law and infringes upon the students' rights to rest and leave, access to labor safety and health protection, participation in relevant insurance, and receipt of vocational skill guidance, it shall bear corresponding legal liability in accordance with the law.

Where a vocational school or vocational training institution violates the provisions of this Law by organizing, arranging, or managing student internships and training through human resources service agencies, labor dispatch units, or units or individuals illegally engaged in human resources services or labor dispatch business, the education administrative department, the human resources and social security administrative department, or other relevant departments shall order it to make corrections, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 10,000 yuan.

For the human resources service agencies, labor dispatch units, or units or individuals illegally engaged in human resources services or labor dispatch business specified in the preceding paragraph, the human resources and social security administrative department or other relevant departments shall order them to make corrections, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 10,000 yuan.

Article 67: Where staff members of the education administrative department, the human resources and social security administrative department, or other relevant departments violate the provisions of this Law by abusing their power, neglecting their duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8: Supplementary Provisions

Article 68: This Law shall apply to the establishment of vocational schools and vocational training institutions within the country by organizations and individuals outside the country; if laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 69: This Law shall come into force on May 1, 2022.
