# Fire Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 29, 1998

Revised at the Fifth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 28, 2008

First Amendment on April 23, 2019 according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 29, 2021 according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the Twenty-Eighth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing fires and reducing fire hazards, strengthening emergency rescue work, protecting personal and property safety, and maintaining public safety.

Article 2 Fire control work shall implement the policy of putting prevention first and combining prevention with extinguishing, follow the principles of unified leadership by the government, supervision according to law by departments, comprehensive responsibility by units, and active participation by citizens, implement the fire safety responsibility system, and establish and improve a socialized fire control work network.

Article 3 The State Council shall lead the national fire control work. Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for the fire control work within their respective administrative regions.

People's governments at all levels shall incorporate fire control work into the national economic and social development plan, ensuring that fire control work is compatible with economic and social development.

Article 4 The emergency management department of the State Council shall supervise and manage the national fire control work. The emergency management departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage the fire control work within their respective administrative regions, and the fire rescue organizations of the people's governments at the same level shall be responsible for implementation. The fire control work of military facilities shall be supervised and managed by their competent units, with the assistance of fire rescue organizations; the fire control work of underground parts of mines, nuclear power plants, and offshore oil and natural gas facilities shall be supervised and managed by their competent units.

Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions and duties, carry out fire control work in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations.

Where laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise for fire control work in forests and grasslands, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 5 All units and individuals have the duty to maintain fire safety, protect fire control facilities, prevent fires, and report fires. All units and adults have the duty to participate in organized fire extinguishing work.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall organize regular fire control publicity and education to raise citizens' awareness of fire safety.

State organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other units shall strengthen fire control publicity and education for their personnel.

Emergency management departments and fire rescue organizations shall strengthen the publicity of fire control laws and regulations, and urge, guide, and assist relevant units in carrying out fire control publicity and education work.

Education and human resources administrative departments, schools, and relevant vocational training institutions shall incorporate fire control knowledge into the content of education, teaching, and training.

News, broadcasting, television, and other relevant units shall conduct targeted fire control publicity and education for the public.

Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, and other organizations shall organize and carry out fire control publicity and education in light of the characteristics of their respective work targets.

Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall assist people's governments, public security organs, emergency management departments, and other departments in strengthening fire control publicity and education.

Article 7 The State encourages and supports scientific research and technological innovation in fire control, and promotes the use of advanced fire control and emergency rescue technologies and equipment; encourages and supports social forces to carry out fire control public welfare activities.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to fire control work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Fire Prevention

Article 8 Local people's governments at all levels shall incorporate fire control plans, including fire safety layout, fire stations, fire water supply, fire communication, fire truck access, and fire control equipment, into urban and rural plans, and be responsible for organizing implementation.

Where the urban and rural fire safety layout does not meet fire safety requirements, adjustments and improvements shall be made; where public fire control facilities and fire control equipment are insufficient or do not meet actual needs, they shall be added, renovated, configured, or subject to technical transformation.

Article 9 The fire control design and construction of construction projects must comply with the national engineering construction fire control technical standards. Construction, design, construction, and engineering supervision units shall be responsible for the quality of fire control design and construction of construction projects according to law.

Article 10 For construction projects that require fire control design according to national engineering construction fire control technical standards, a fire control design review and acceptance system for construction projects shall be implemented.

Article 11 For special construction projects specified by the competent department of housing and urban-rural development of the State Council, the construction unit shall submit the fire control design documents to the competent department of housing and urban-rural development for review, and the competent department of housing and urban-rural development shall be responsible for the review results according to law.

For other construction projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, the construction unit shall provide fire control design drawings and technical materials that meet construction needs when applying for a construction permit or applying for approval of the commencement report.

Article 12 For special construction projects that have not undergone fire control design review or have failed the review, the construction unit and construction unit shall not commence construction; for other construction projects, if the construction unit fails to provide fire control design drawings and technical materials that meet construction needs, the relevant department shall not issue a construction permit or approve the commencement report.

Article 13 Upon completion of a construction project that requires fire control acceptance according to regulations of the competent department of housing and urban-rural development of the State Council, the construction unit shall apply to the competent department of housing and urban-rural development for fire control acceptance.

For other construction projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, the construction unit shall, after acceptance, report to the competent department of housing and urban-rural development for record, and the competent department of housing and urban-rural development shall conduct spot checks.

Construction projects that require fire control acceptance according to law shall not be put into use if they have not undergone fire control acceptance or have failed the acceptance; other construction projects that fail spot checks according to law shall cease use.

Article 14 Specific measures for fire control design review, fire control acceptance, record filing, and spot checks of construction projects shall be prescribed by the competent department of housing and urban-rural development of the State Council.

Article 15 A commitment-based management system shall be implemented for fire safety inspections before public gathering places are put into use or business. Before a public gathering place is put into use or business, the construction unit or user unit shall apply to the fire rescue organization of the local people's government at or above the county level for a fire safety inspection, make a commitment that the place meets fire control technical standards and management regulations, submit the required materials, and be responsible for the authenticity of its commitment and materials.

The fire rescue organization shall review the materials submitted by the applicant; if the application materials are complete and in the statutory form, permission shall be granted. The fire rescue organization shall, in accordance with fire control technical standards and management regulations, promptly verify the public gathering place that has made the commitment.

If the applicant chooses not to use the commitment-based method, the fire rescue organization shall, within ten working days from the date of accepting the application, inspect the place in accordance with fire control technical standards and management regulations. If the inspection shows compliance with fire safety requirements, permission shall be granted.

Public gathering places shall not be put into use or business without permission from the fire rescue organization. Specific measures for fire safety inspections shall be formulated by the emergency management department of the State Council.

Article 16 State organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other units shall perform the following fire safety duties:

(1) Implement the fire safety responsibility system, formulate the unit's fire safety system and fire safety operating procedures, and formulate fire extinguishing and emergency evacuation plans;

(2) Configure fire control facilities and equipment in accordance with national and industry standards, set up fire safety signs, and regularly organize inspection and maintenance to ensure they are intact and effective;

(3) Conduct a comprehensive inspection of building fire control facilities at least once a year to ensure they are intact and effective, and the inspection records shall be complete and accurate and kept on file for reference;

(4) Ensure the smooth passage of evacuation routes, safety exits, and fire truck access, and ensure that fire and smoke compartments and fire separation distances comply with fire control technical standards;

(5) Organize fire prevention inspections and promptly eliminate fire hazards;

(6) Organize targeted fire drills;

(7) Other fire safety duties as prescribed by laws and regulations.

The principal responsible person of a unit shall be the unit's fire safety responsible person.

Article 17 The fire rescue organization of the local people's government at or above the county level shall designate units with a high probability of fire occurrence or that may cause significant personal injury or property loss in the event of a fire as key fire safety units within its administrative region, and the emergency management department shall report to the people's government at the same level for record.

In addition to performing the duties specified in Article 16 of this Law, key fire safety units shall also perform the following fire safety duties:

(1) Designate a fire safety manager to organize and implement the unit's fire safety management work;

(2) Establish fire control files, identify key fire safety areas, set up fire prevention signs, and implement strict management;

(3) Conduct daily fire prevention patrols and establish patrol records;

(4) Provide pre-job fire safety training for employees, and regularly organize fire safety training and fire drills.

Article 18 Where a building is managed or used by two or more units, the fire safety responsibilities of each party shall be clearly defined, and a responsible person shall be designated for the unified management of shared evacuation routes, safety exits, building fire control facilities, and fire truck access.

Property service enterprises in residential areas shall maintain and manage shared fire control facilities within the management area and provide fire safety prevention services.

Article 19 Places where flammable and explosive dangerous goods are produced, stored, or operated shall not be located in the same building as residential premises, and shall maintain a safe distance from residential premises.

Where places where other goods are produced, stored, or operated are located in the same building as residential premises, they shall comply with national engineering construction fire control technical standards.

Article 20 When organizing large-scale public events, the organizer shall apply to the public security organ for a safety permit according to law, formulate fire extinguishing and emergency evacuation plans and organize drills, clarify fire safety responsibility divisions, designate fire safety management personnel, ensure that fire control facilities and equipment are fully configured, intact, and effective, and ensure that evacuation routes, safety exits, evacuation direction signs, emergency lighting, and fire truck access comply with fire control technical standards and management regulations.

Article 21 Smoking and the use of open flames are prohibited in places with fire or explosion hazards. Where open flame operations are necessary due to special circumstances such as construction, approval procedures shall be completed in advance according to regulations, and corresponding fire safety measures shall be taken; operators shall comply with fire safety regulations.

Personnel engaged in fire-hazardous operations such as electric welding and gas welding, and operators of automatic fire control systems, must hold relevant certificates and comply with fire safety operating procedures.

Article 22 The establishment of factories, warehouses, and dedicated stations and docks for producing, storing, and loading flammable and explosive dangerous goods shall comply with fire control technical standards. Filling stations, supply stations, and pressure regulating stations for flammable and explosive gases and liquids shall be located in positions that meet fire safety requirements and comply with fire and explosion prevention requirements.

Where factories, warehouses, and dedicated stations and docks for producing, storing, and loading flammable and explosive dangerous goods, and filling stations, supply stations, and pressure regulating stations for flammable and explosive gases and liquids that have already been established no longer comply with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the local people's government shall organize and coordinate relevant departments and units to resolve the issue within a specified period and eliminate safety hazards.

Article 23 The production, storage, transportation, sale, use, and disposal of flammable and explosive dangerous goods must comply with fire control technical standards and management regulations.

When entering places where flammable and explosive dangerous goods are produced or stored, fire safety regulations must be complied with. It is prohibited to illegally carry flammable and explosive dangerous goods into public places or on public transportation.

The management of warehouses storing combustible materials must comply with fire control technical standards and management regulations.

Article 24 Fire control products must comply with national standards; where there are no national standards, they must comply with industry standards. It is prohibited to produce, sell, or use substandard fire control products or fire control products that have been explicitly eliminated by the State.

Fire control products subject to compulsory product certification according to law may only be produced, sold, or used after being certified by a legally qualified certification body in accordance with the mandatory requirements of national and industry standards. The catalog of fire control products subject to compulsory product certification shall be formulated and published by the product quality supervision department of the State Council in conjunction with the emergency management department of the State Council.

Newly developed fire control products for which national or industry standards have not yet been formulated may only be produced, sold, or used after technical appraisal in accordance with the methods prescribed by the product quality supervision department of the State Council in conjunction with the emergency management department of the State Council confirms that they meet fire safety requirements.

Fire control products that have passed compulsory product certification or technical appraisal in accordance with this Article shall be published by the emergency management department of the State Council.

Article 25 Product quality supervision departments, administrative departments for industry and commerce, and fire rescue organizations shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, strengthen supervision and inspection of the quality of fire control products.

Article 26 The fire resistance performance of building components, building materials, and interior decoration and finishing materials must comply with national standards; where there are no national standards, they must comply with industry standards.

Interior decoration and finishing of densely populated places shall use non-combustible or flame-retardant materials in accordance with the requirements of fire control technical standards.

Article 27 Product standards for electrical appliances and gas appliances shall comply with fire safety requirements.

The installation, use, and design, laying, maintenance, and inspection of circuits and pipelines of electrical appliances and gas appliances must comply with fire control technical standards and management regulations.

Article 28 No unit or individual may damage, misappropriate, or dismantle or deactivate fire control facilities or equipment without authorization, bury, occupy, or block fire hydrants or occupy fire separation distances, or occupy, block, or seal evacuation routes, safety exits, or fire truck access. Doors and windows of densely populated places shall not be equipped with obstacles that affect escape and fire rescue.

Article 29 Units responsible for the maintenance and management of public fire control facilities shall keep public fire control facilities such as fire water supply, fire communication, and fire truck access intact and effective. When road construction, power outages, water outages, or communication line interruptions may affect fire brigade fire rescue, the relevant units must notify the local fire rescue organization in advance.

Article 30 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over rural fire control work, take measures to strengthen the construction of public fire control facilities, organize the establishment and supervise the implementation of the fire safety responsibility system.

Article 31 During the agricultural harvest season, forest and grassland fire prevention periods, major holidays, and fire-prone seasons, local people's governments at all levels shall organize targeted fire control publicity and education, take fire prevention measures, and conduct fire safety inspections.

Article 32 Township people's governments and urban sub-district offices shall guide, support, and assist villagers' committees and residents' committees in carrying out mass fire control work. Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall designate fire safety managers, organize the formulation of fire safety conventions, and conduct fire safety inspections.

Article 33 The State encourages and guides public gathering places and enterprises that produce, store, transport, or sell flammable and explosive dangerous goods to purchase fire public liability insurance; encourages insurance companies to underwrite fire public liability insurance.

Article 34 Fire control technical service institutions such as those for fire control facility maintenance and testing and fire safety assessment shall meet the conditions for practice, and practicing personnel shall obtain corresponding qualifications according to law; they shall accept commissions to provide fire control technical services in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, national standards, industry standards, and practice standards, and be responsible for the quality of services.

## Chapter III Fire Control Organizations

Article 35 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the construction of fire control organizations, establish various forms of fire control organizations according to the needs of economic and social development, strengthen the training of fire control technical personnel, and enhance the capacity for fire prevention, extinguishing, and emergency rescue.

Article 36 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with state regulations, establish national comprehensive fire rescue teams and full-time fire brigades, equip them with fire control equipment in accordance with national standards, and undertake fire extinguishing work.

Township people's governments shall, based on local economic development and the needs of fire control work, establish full-time fire brigades and volunteer fire brigades to undertake fire extinguishing work.

Article 37 National comprehensive fire rescue teams and full-time fire brigades shall, in accordance with state regulations, undertake emergency rescue work for major disasters and accidents and other situations primarily involving the rescue of human life.

Article 38 National comprehensive fire rescue teams and full-time fire brigades shall fully play the backbone role of professional forces in fire extinguishing and emergency rescue; in accordance with state regulations, organize and implement professional skills training, equip and maintain equipment and supplies, and improve the capacity for fire extinguishing and emergency rescue.

Article 39 The following units shall establish unit full-time fire brigades to undertake their own fire extinguishing work:

(1) Large nuclear facility units, large power plants, civil airports, and major ports;

(2) Large enterprises that produce or store flammable and explosive dangerous goods;

(3) Large warehouses and bases storing important combustible materials;

(4) Other large enterprises not specified in items (1), (2), and (3) that have a high fire risk and are far from national comprehensive fire rescue teams;

(5) Management units of ancient building complexes listed as national key cultural relics protection units that are far from national comprehensive fire rescue teams.

Article 40 The establishment of full-time fire brigades shall comply with relevant state regulations and be reported to the local fire rescue organization for acceptance.

Members of full-time fire brigades shall enjoy social insurance and welfare benefits according to law.

Article 41 State organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other units, as well as villagers' committees and residents' committees, may, as needed, establish various forms of fire control organizations such as volunteer fire brigades to carry out mass self-prevention and self-rescue work.

Article 42 Fire rescue organizations shall provide professional guidance to fire control organizations such as full-time fire brigades and volunteer fire brigades; as needed for fire extinguishing, they may mobilize and command full-time fire brigades to participate in fire extinguishing work.

## Chapter IV Fire Extinguishing and Rescue

Article 43 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to formulate emergency plans based on the characteristics of fires within their administrative regions, establish emergency response and handling mechanisms, and provide personnel, equipment, and other support for fire extinguishing and emergency rescue work.

Article 44 Anyone who discovers a fire shall immediately report it. Any unit or individual shall provide convenience for reporting fires without compensation and shall not obstruct the reporting. False reporting of fires is strictly prohibited.

In the event of a fire in a densely populated place, the on-site staff of that place shall immediately organize and guide the evacuation of persons present.

In the event of a fire in any unit, it must immediately organize forces to extinguish the fire. Neighboring units shall provide support.

Upon receiving a fire alarm, the fire brigade must immediately proceed to the fire scene, rescue persons in distress, eliminate hazards, and extinguish the fire.**Article 45** The fire rescue authority shall organize and command firefighting operations at the scene, prioritizing the safety of persons in danger.

The fire scene commander-in-chief, based on the needs of firefighting, has the authority to decide on the following matters:

(1) Using various water sources;
(2) Cutting off the supply of electricity, combustible gases, and combustible liquids, and restricting the use of fire and electricity;
(3) Delineating a warning zone and implementing partial traffic control;
(4) Utilizing adjacent buildings and related facilities;
(5) To rescue persons and important materials and prevent the spread of fire, demolishing or damaging buildings, structures, or facilities adjacent to the fire scene;
(6) Mobilizing units such as water supply, power supply, gas supply, communications, medical rescue, transportation, and environmental protection to assist in firefighting and rescue.

According to the urgent needs of firefighting, the relevant local people's government shall organize personnel and mobilize necessary materials to support firefighting.

**Article 46** The emergency rescue operations of the National Comprehensive Fire Rescue Team and full-time fire brigades for major disasters and accidents other than fires shall be uniformly led by the people's government at or above the county level.

**Article 47** Fire trucks and fire boats, when proceeding to perform firefighting or emergency rescue tasks, are not restricted by speed limits, routes, directions, or command signals, provided safety is ensured. Other vehicles, vessels, and pedestrians shall give way and shall not cut in or overtake. Tolls on toll roads and bridges shall be exempted for these vehicles. Traffic management command personnel shall ensure the rapid passage of fire trucks and fire boats.

When fire personnel, firefighting equipment, and materials dispatched to a fire or emergency rescue scene require railway, waterway, or air transport, the relevant units shall prioritize the transport.

**Article 48** Fire trucks, fire boats, firefighting equipment, apparatus, and facilities shall not be used for matters unrelated to firefighting and emergency rescue work.

**Article 49** The National Comprehensive Fire Rescue Team and full-time fire brigades shall not charge any fees for firefighting and emergency rescue.

The fuel, extinguishing agents, equipment, and apparatus consumed by unit full-time fire brigades or volunteer fire brigades participating in extinguishing fires in other units shall be compensated by the people's government of the place where the fire occurred.

**Article 50** Personnel injured, disabled, or killed due to participation in firefighting or emergency rescue shall receive medical care and pensions in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 51** The fire rescue authority has the authority to seal off the fire scene as needed, investigate the cause of the fire, and calculate fire losses.

After a fire is extinguished, the unit where the fire occurred and relevant personnel shall protect the scene as required by the fire rescue authority, accept the accident investigation, and truthfully provide information related to the fire.

Based on the scene investigation, inquiry results, and relevant inspection and appraisal opinions, the fire rescue authority shall promptly produce a fire accident determination certificate as evidence for handling the fire accident.

## Chapter V Supervision and Inspection

**Article 52** Local people's governments at all levels shall implement the fire safety responsibility system and supervise and inspect the performance of fire safety duties by the relevant departments of their respective governments.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the characteristics of their respective systems, conduct targeted fire safety inspections and promptly urge the rectification of fire hazards.

**Article 53** The fire rescue authority shall supervise and inspect the compliance of state organs, organizations, enterprises, and institutions with fire laws and regulations in accordance with the law. Police stations may be responsible for routine fire safety supervision and inspections and conducting fire safety publicity and education. Specific measures shall be formulated by the public security department of the State Council.

Personnel of the fire rescue authority and police stations shall present their identification when conducting fire safety supervision and inspections.

**Article 54** If the fire rescue authority discovers fire hazards during fire safety supervision and inspections, it shall notify the relevant unit or individual to take immediate measures to eliminate the hazards. If failing to eliminate the hazards in a timely manner may seriously threaten public safety, the fire rescue authority shall, in accordance with regulations, take temporary sealing measures on the dangerous parts or premises.

**Article 55** If the fire rescue authority discovers during fire safety supervision and inspections that the urban or rural fire safety layout or public fire facilities do not meet fire safety requirements, or that there are major fire hazards in the region affecting public safety, the emergency management department shall report this in writing to the people's government at the same level.

The people's government receiving the report shall promptly verify the situation and organize or instruct relevant departments and units to take measures for rectification.

**Article 56** The competent department of housing and urban-rural development, the fire rescue authority, and their staff shall conduct fire protection design review, fire protection acceptance, filing spot checks, and fire safety inspections in accordance with statutory authority and procedures, ensuring fairness, strictness, civility, and efficiency.

The competent department of housing and urban-rural development, the fire rescue authority, and their staff shall not charge fees for conducting fire protection design review, fire protection acceptance, filing spot checks, and fire safety inspections, nor shall they use their positions to seek benefits; they shall not use their positions to designate or implicitly designate for users or construction units the brands or sales units of fire protection products, or fire protection technical service institutions or fire protection facility construction units.

**Article 57** The competent department of housing and urban-rural development, the fire rescue authority, and their staff shall consciously accept supervision from society and citizens when performing their duties.

Any unit or individual has the right to report and accuse the competent department of housing and urban-rural development, the fire rescue authority, and their staff of illegal acts in law enforcement. The authority receiving the report or accusation shall promptly investigate and handle it according to its duties.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 58** Anyone who violates this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent department of housing and urban-rural development or the fire rescue authority, according to their respective functions, to stop construction, stop use, or cease production and business operations, and shall be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan:

(1) For a construction project that should undergo fire protection design review according to law, commencing construction without undergoing review or after failing the review;
(2) For a construction project that should undergo fire protection acceptance according to law, putting it into use without undergoing acceptance or after failing acceptance;
(3) For other construction projects specified in Article 13 of this Law, failing to stop use after failing the post-acceptance random inspection according to law;
(4) For a public gathering place, putting it into use or operation without permission from the fire rescue authority, or if verification reveals that the use or operation of the place is inconsistent with the promised content.

If verification reveals that the use or operation of a public gathering place is inconsistent with the promised content, and after being ordered to rectify within a time limit, the entity fails to rectify or still fails to meet requirements after rectification, the relevant permission shall be revoked according to law.

If a construction unit fails to file with the competent department of housing and urban-rural development after acceptance as required by this Law, the competent department of housing and urban-rural development shall order correction and impose a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan.

**Article 59** Anyone who violates this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent department of housing and urban-rural development to correct or stop construction, and shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan:

(1) The construction unit requiring the architectural design unit or construction enterprise to lower the fire protection technical standards for design or construction;
(2) The architectural design unit failing to carry out fire protection design in accordance with the mandatory requirements of fire protection technical standards;
(3) The construction enterprise failing to construct in accordance with the fire protection design documents and fire protection technical standards, thereby reducing the quality of fire protection construction;
(4) The project supervision unit colluding with the construction unit or construction enterprise to practice fraud and reduce the quality of fire protection construction.

**Article 60** If a unit violates this Law by committing any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to correct and fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) The configuration or installation of fire protection facilities, equipment, or fire safety signs does not meet national or industry standards, or is not maintained in good and effective condition;
(2) Damaging, misappropriating, or dismantling or discontinuing the use of fire protection facilities or equipment without authorization;
(3) Occupying, blocking, or sealing evacuation routes or safety exits, or engaging in other acts that hinder safe evacuation;
(4) Burying, encircling, or obscuring fire hydrants, or encroaching on fire separation distances;
(5) Occupying, blocking, or sealing fire truck access roads, hindering the passage of fire trucks;
(6) Setting up obstacles on doors or windows in crowded places that affect escape and firefighting rescue;
(7) Failing to take timely measures to eliminate fire hazards after being notified by the fire rescue authority.

An individual who commits any of the acts specified in items (2), (3), (4), or (5) of the preceding paragraph shall be given a warning or fined not more than 500 yuan.

If a person commits any of the acts specified in items (3), (4), (5), or (6) of the first paragraph of this Article and refuses to correct after being ordered to do so, compulsory enforcement shall be carried out, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

**Article 61** If a place producing, storing, or operating flammable and explosive dangerous goods is located in the same building as a residential place, or is not kept at a safe distance from a residential place, the violator shall be ordered to cease production and business operations and fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

If a place producing, storing, or operating other goods is located in the same building as a residential place and does not meet fire protection technical standards, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

**Article 62** Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China":

(1) Violating relevant fire protection technical standards and management regulations in producing, storing, transporting, selling, using, or disposing of flammable and explosive dangerous goods;
(2) Illegally carrying flammable and explosive dangerous goods into public places or onto public transportation;
(3) Falsely reporting a fire alarm;
(4) Obstructing fire trucks or fire boats from performing their duties;
(5) Obstructing staff of the fire rescue authority from performing their duties according to law.

**Article 63** Anyone who violates this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be given a warning or fined not more than 500 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not more than five days:

(1) Entering a place producing or storing flammable and explosive dangerous goods in violation of fire safety regulations;
(2) Using open flames in violation of regulations, or smoking or using open flames in places with fire or explosion hazards.

**Article 64** Anyone who violates this Law by committing any of the following acts, which does not constitute a crime, shall be detained for not less than 10 days but not more than 15 days, and may also be fined not more than 500 yuan; if the circumstances are minor, they shall be given a warning or fined not more than 500 yuan:

(1) Instructing or compelling others to engage in risky operations in violation of fire safety regulations;
(2) Negligently causing a fire;
(3) Blocking the alarm after a fire occurs, or failing to promptly report the alarm by a person responsible for reporting;
(4) Disturbing the order of the fire scene, or refusing to obey the command of the fire scene commander, affecting firefighting and rescue;
(5) Intentionally destroying or falsifying the fire scene;
(6) Unauthorizedly unsealing or using a place or part sealed by the fire rescue authority.

**Article 65** Anyone who violates this Law by producing or selling substandard fire protection products or fire protection products explicitly eliminated by the state shall be given a heavier penalty by the product quality supervision department or the administrative department for industry and commerce in accordance with the "Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China".

If a crowded place uses substandard fire protection products or fire protection products explicitly eliminated by the state, it shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit; if it fails to rectify within the time limit, it shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 500 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease production and business operations.

For the circumstances specified in the second paragraph of this Article, the fire rescue authority shall, in addition to punishing the user according to law, notify the product quality supervision department and the administrative department for industry and commerce of the discovery of substandard fire protection products and fire protection products explicitly eliminated by the state. The product quality supervision department and the administrative department for industry and commerce shall promptly investigate and deal with the producers and sellers according to law.

**Article 66** If the installation, use, design, laying, maintenance, and testing of electrical appliances, gas appliances, and their lines and pipelines do not comply with fire protection technical standards and management regulations, the violator shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit; if it fails to rectify within the time limit, it shall be ordered to stop use and may also be fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan.

**Article 67** If state organs, organizations, enterprises, institutions, or other units violate the provisions of Articles 16, 17, 18, or paragraph 2 of Article 21 of this Law, they shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit; if they fail to rectify within the time limit, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions or a warning penalty according to law.

**Article 68** If a fire occurs in a crowded place and the on-site staff of that place fails to fulfill their duty to organize and guide the evacuation of persons present, and the circumstances are serious but do not constitute a crime, they shall be detained for not less than 5 days but not more than 10 days.

**Article 69** If a fire protection technical service institution, such as one for fire protection facility maintenance, testing, or fire safety assessment, engages in fire protection technical service activities without meeting the business conditions or issues false documents, the fire rescue authority shall order correction, impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if it fails to carry out fire protection technical service activities in accordance with national or industry standards, it shall be ordered to correct, fined not more than 50,000 yuan, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not more than 10,000 yuan; if there is illegal income, the illegal income shall be confiscated; if it causes losses to others, it shall bear compensation liability according to law; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease practice or have its relevant qualifications revoked according to law; if it causes major losses, the relevant department shall revoke its business license and impose a lifelong market ban on the relevant responsible personnel.

If the institution specified in the preceding paragraph issues inaccurate documents, causing losses to others, it shall bear compensation liability according to law; if it causes major losses, the fire rescue authority shall order it to cease practice or revoke its relevant qualifications according to law, the relevant department shall revoke its business license, and impose a lifelong market ban on the relevant responsible personnel.

**Article 70** The administrative penalties stipulated in this Law shall be decided by the competent department of housing and urban-rural development or the fire rescue authority according to their respective functions, except for those that should be decided by the public security organ in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China".

A person ordered to stop construction, stop use, or cease production and business operations shall report to the department or authority that made the decision after rectification, and may resume construction, use, production, or business operations only after passing an inspection.

If a party fails to execute the decision to cease production and business operations, stop use, or stop construction within the time limit, the department or authority that made the decision shall enforce it compulsorily.

If an order to cease production and business operations has a significant impact on the economy and social life, the competent department of housing and urban-rural development or the emergency management department shall submit it to the people's government at the same level for a decision according to law.

**Article 71** If staff of the competent department of housing and urban-rural development or the fire rescue authority abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, committing any of the following acts that do not constitute a crime, they shall be given disciplinary sanctions according to law:

(1) Approving fire protection design documents, construction projects, or premises that do not meet fire safety requirements as passing review, acceptance, or safety inspection;
(2) Unreasonably delaying fire protection design review, fire protection acceptance, or fire safety inspection, failing to perform duties within the statutory time limit;
(3) Failing to promptly notify relevant units or individuals to rectify discovered fire hazards;
(4) Using their positions to designate or implicitly designate for users or construction units the brands or sales units of fire protection products, or fire protection technical service institutions or fire protection facility construction units;
(5) Using fire trucks, fire boats, firefighting equipment, apparatus, and facilities for matters unrelated to firefighting and emergency rescue;
(6) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

If staff of other relevant administrative departments, such as product quality supervision and industry and commerce administration, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in fire protection work, and the acts do not constitute a crime, they shall be given disciplinary sanctions according to law.

**Article 72** Anyone who violates this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 73** The meanings of the following terms used in this Law:

(1) "Fire protection facilities" refers to automatic fire alarm systems, automatic fire extinguishing systems, fire hydrant systems, smoke control and exhaust systems, emergency broadcasting and emergency lighting, and safety evacuation facilities.
(2) "Fire protection products" refers to products specifically used for fire prevention, firefighting and rescue, fire protection, refuge, and escape.
(3) "Public gathering places" refers to hotels, restaurants, shopping malls, markets, passenger station waiting rooms, passenger terminal waiting halls, civil airport terminals, stadiums, auditoriums, and public entertainment venues.
(4) "Crowded places" refers to public gathering places, outpatient buildings and ward buildings of hospitals, teaching buildings, libraries, canteens, and dormitories of schools, nursing homes, welfare homes, nurseries, kindergartens, reading rooms of public libraries, exhibition halls of public exhibition halls and museums, production and processing workshops and employee dormitories of labor-intensive enterprises, and tourist and religious activity venues.

**Article 74** This Law shall come into force on May 1, 2009.
