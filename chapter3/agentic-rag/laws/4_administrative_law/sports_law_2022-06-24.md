# Sports Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Revision adopted at the 35th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on June 24, 2022

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to promote sports, carry forward the Chinese sports spirit, cultivate Chinese sports culture, develop physical education and sports, and enhance the people's physical fitness.

Article 2 Sports work shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, remain people-centered, be based on national fitness, combine popularization with improvement, promote balanced and full development of sports, and advance the building of a leading sports nation and a healthy China.

Article 3 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate sports into their plans for national economic and social development.

Article 4 The sports administrative department under the State Council shall be in charge of sports work nationwide. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall manage related sports work within their respective functions and duties.

The sports administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of sports work within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall manage related sports work within their respective functions and duties.

Article 5 The State shall, in accordance with the law, guarantee citizens' right to participate equally in sports activities, and provide special protection for the right of minors, women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities to participate in sports activities.

Article 6 The State shall expand the supply of public sports services of a public welfare and basic nature, promote equal access to basic public sports services, and gradually improve the basic public sports service system that covers the entire population, provides universal benefits, and integrates urban and rural areas.

Article 7 The State shall adopt measures such as financial support and assistance in building sports facilities to support the development of sports in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and economically underdeveloped areas.

Article 8 The State shall encourage and support the discovery, collation, protection, promotion, and innovation of outstanding traditional ethnic, folk, and folk-custom sports events, and regularly hold traditional ethnic minority sports games.

Article 9 Engaging in and participating in sports activities shall follow the principles of legality and compliance, honesty and trustworthiness, respect for science, adaptation to local conditions, thrift and economy, and ensuring safety.

Article 10 The State shall prioritize the development of youth and school sports, adhere to the integration of sports and education, coordinate cultural learning and physical exercise, and emphasize both physical fitness and character development, to promote the all-round development of young people.

Article 11 The State shall support the development of the sports industry, improve the sports industry system, regulate the sports market order, encourage the expansion of sports market supply, broaden investment and financing channels for the sports industry, and promote sports consumption.

Article 12 The State shall support scientific research and technological innovation in sports, cultivate sports science and technology talent, promote and apply sports science and technology achievements, and raise the level of sports science and technology.

Article 13 The State shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of sports, in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 14 The State shall encourage international sports exchanges, promote the Olympic spirit, and support participation in international sports.

International sports exchanges shall adhere to the principles of independence, equality and mutual benefit, and mutual respect, safeguard national sovereignty, security, development interests and dignity, and comply with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

Article 15 The week in which August 8th, National Fitness Day, falls shall be Sports Promotion Week.

## Chapter II National Fitness

Article 16 The State shall implement the national fitness strategy, build a public service system for national fitness, encourage and support citizens to participate in fitness activities, and promote the deep integration of national fitness with national health.

Article 17 The State shall advocate that citizens establish and practice the concept of scientific fitness, actively learn fitness knowledge, and actively participate in fitness activities.

Article 18 The State shall promote the national fitness plan, formulate and implement physical exercise standards, regularly conduct citizen physical fitness monitoring and surveys on the status of national fitness activities, and carry out scientific fitness guidance work.

The State shall establish a coordination mechanism for national fitness work.

People's governments at or above the county level shall regularly organize relevant departments to evaluate the implementation of the national fitness plan and make the evaluation results public.

Article 19 The State shall implement a social sports instructor system. Social sports instructors shall provide guidance for national fitness activities.

The measures for the administration of social sports instructors shall be formulated by the sports administrative department under the State Council.

Article 20 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall provide necessary conditions for national fitness activities and support and guarantee the conduct of national fitness activities.

Article 21 State organs, enterprises, public institutions, and mass organizations such as trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, and federations of persons with disabilities shall, based on their respective characteristics, organize daily physical exercise and various types of sports games and other national fitness activities.

Article 22 Residents' committees, villagers' committees, and other community organizations shall, based on actual conditions, organize national fitness activities.

Article 23 The whole society shall care for and support minors, women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities in participating in national fitness activities. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to provide convenience and guarantees for the safe participation of minors, women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities in national fitness activities.

## Chapter III Youth and School Sports

Article 24 The State shall implement a promotion plan for youth and school sports activities, improve the work system for youth and school sports, cultivate and enhance youth's awareness of sports and fitness, promote the development and popularization of youth and school sports activities, and promote the physical and mental health and physical strength of young people.

Article 25 Education administrative departments and schools shall include sports in the comprehensive quality evaluation of students, incorporate meeting the national student physical fitness and health standards as an important part of education and teaching assessment, cultivate students' habit of physical exercise, and enhance students' sports literacy.

Sports administrative departments shall provide guidance and assistance to schools in imparting sports knowledge and skills, organizing sports training, holding sports events and activities, and managing sports venues and facilities, and shall cooperate with education administrative departments in promoting the construction of school sports teams and high-level sports teams.

Article 26 Schools must offer sufficient physical education classes in accordance with relevant state regulations and ensure that physical education class hours are not occupied.

Schools shall, during physical education classes, organize students with special physical conditions such as illness or disability to participate in sports activities suitable for their characteristics.

Article 27 Schools shall incorporate extracurricular sports activities for students conducted on campus into their teaching plans, linking them with the content of physical education classes, and ensure that students participate in no less than one hour of physical exercise per day during school hours.

Schools are encouraged to form sports training organizations such as sports teams and clubs, carry out various forms of after-school sports training, and where conditions permit, form high-level sports teams to cultivate reserve talents for competitive sports.

Article 28 The State shall regularly hold the National Student (Youth) Games. Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on actual conditions, regularly organize local student (Youth) Games.

Schools shall hold at least one school-wide sports games per academic year.

Public sports venues and facilities are encouraged to be open to schools free of charge, providing service guarantees for schools to hold sports games.

Schools are encouraged to carry out various forms of student sports exchange activities.

Article 29 The State shall include physical education subjects in the scope of the academic proficiency examinations for junior and senior high schools, and establish an assessment mechanism that conforms to the characteristics of the subject.

The assessment of physical education subjects for students with special physical conditions such as illness or disability shall fully consider their physical condition.

Article 30 Schools shall establish a student physical health examination system. Education, sports, and health administrative departments shall strengthen the monitoring and evaluation of student physical fitness.

Article 31 Schools shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, employ sufficient qualified physical education teachers and ensure that physical education teachers enjoy the same treatment as teachers of other subjects.

Schools may establish positions for sports coaches.

Schools shall give priority to employing qualified outstanding retired athletes for school sports teaching and training activities.

Article 32 Schools shall configure sports venues, facilities, and equipment in accordance with relevant national standards, and conduct regular inspections, maintenance, and timely updates.

School sports venues must ensure the needs of sports activities and shall not be arbitrarily occupied or used for other purposes.

Article 33 The State shall establish and improve an insurance mechanism for accidental injuries in student sports activities.

Education administrative departments and schools shall do a good job in safety management of school sports activities and risk prevention and control of sports injuries.

Article 34 Kindergartens shall provide suitable indoor and outdoor activity venues and sports facilities and equipment for preschool children, and carry out sports activities that conform to the characteristics of preschool children.

Article 35 Educational supervision institutions at all levels shall supervise school sports and publish supervision reports to the public.

Article 36 Education administrative departments, sports administrative departments, and schools shall organize and guide young people to participate in sports activities, prevent and control adverse health conditions such as myopia and obesity among young people, and families shall cooperate.

Article 37 The sports administrative department shall, together with relevant departments, guide and regulate enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and sports professionals to provide sports training and other services for young people.

Article 38 Sports schools of various types and levels shall implement compulsory education for school-age students in accordance with the law, and conduct amateur sports training in accordance with the teaching and training syllabus formulated by the sports administrative department under the State Council.

Education administrative departments shall include the cultural education of sports schools within their management scope.

People's governments at all levels shall support sports schools in terms of venues, facilities, funds, and personnel.

## Chapter IV Competitive Sports

Article 39 The State shall promote the development of competitive sports, encourage athletes to improve their competitive level, achieve outstanding results in sports events, and win honor for the country and the people.

Article 40 The State shall promote and regulate the marketization and professionalization of professional sports, and improve the capability and competitive level of professional sports events.

Article 41 The State shall strengthen the construction of sports schools and traditional sports-featured schools, encourage and support the conduct of amateur sports training, and cultivate outstanding reserve talents for competitive sports.

Article 42 The State shall strengthen the cultivation and management of athletes, and conduct education on patriotism, collectivism, and socialism, as well as moral, discipline, and legal education for athletes.

Athletes shall actively participate in training and competitions, unite and cooperate, be dedicated, strive tenaciously, and continuously improve their competitive level.

Article 43 The State shall strengthen the research, development, and application of science and technology for sports training, implement scientific and civilized training for athletes, and safeguard the physical and mental health of athletes.

Article 44 The State shall, in accordance with the law, guarantee athletes' right to receive cultural education.

Sports administrative departments and education administrative departments shall ensure that athletes in the compulsory education stage complete compulsory education.

Article 45 The State shall, in accordance with the law, guarantee athletes' right to choose registration and transfer.

Athletes may register with individual sports associations and transfer in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 46 The State shall provide preferential treatment to outstanding athletes in terms of employment and further education.

Article 47 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen vocational skills training and social security for retired athletes, and provide guidance and services for their employment and entrepreneurship.

Article 48 The State shall implement a system for sports skill levels, coach title levels, and referee technical levels.

Article 49 Athletes and sports teams representing the country and localities in major international and domestic sports events shall be selected and formed in accordance with the principles of openness, fairness, and merit.

The measures for the selection of athletes and the formation of sports teams shall be formulated by the sports administrative department under the State Council.

Article 50 The State shall implement a graded and classified management system for sports events and activities, with specific measures to be formulated by the sports administrative department under the State Council.

Article 51 Sports events shall adhere to the principle of fair competition.

Organizers of sports events and activities, as well as athletes, coaches, and referees, shall abide by sports ethics and the rules of sports events, and shall not engage in fraud or malpractice for personal gain.

No organization or individual is allowed to engage in gambling activities using sports events.

Article 52 The names, emblems, flags, mascots, and other symbols of sports events held within the territory of China shall be protected in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Without the permission of the relevant rights holders such as the organizers of the sports event or activity, no one may collect or disseminate images, audio, or video information from the scene of the sports event for profit-making purposes.

## Chapter V Anti-Doping

Article 53 The State advocates healthy, civilized, and fair competition in sports, and prohibits the use of doping in sports.

No organization or individual may organize, force, deceive, instigate, or induce sports participants to use doping in sports, nor provide or indirectly provide doping to sports participants.

Article 54 The State shall establish and improve the anti-doping system.

The sports administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with departments of health, education, public security, industry and information technology, commerce, drug regulation, transportation, customs, agriculture, and market regulation, implement comprehensive governance of doping issues.

Article 55 The sports administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for formulating anti-doping regulations.

Article 56 The sports administrative department under the State Council shall, together with the drug regulatory, health, commerce, customs, and other departments under the State Council, formulate and publish a list of prohibited substances and methods, and update it dynamically.

Article 57 The State shall establish an anti-doping agency. The anti-doping agency and its inspection personnel shall conduct inspections in accordance with legal procedures. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate, and no unit or individual may interfere.

The anti-doping agency shall disclose anti-doping information in accordance with the law and accept social supervision.

Article 58 The sports administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall organize and carry out anti-doping publicity and education work to enhance the anti-doping awareness of sports activity participants and the public.

Article 59 The State shall encourage scientific and technological research on anti-doping, and promote advanced anti-doping technologies, equipment, and methods.

Article 60 The State shall carry out international cooperation on anti-doping and fulfill international anti-doping obligations in accordance with relevant international treaties concluded or acceded to.

## Chapter VI Sports Organizations

Article 61 The State shall encourage and support sports organizations to carry out sports activities in accordance with laws, regulations, and their articles of association, and promote the development of sports.

The State shall encourage sports organizations to actively participate in international sports exchanges and cooperation, and participate in the formulation of international sports rules.

Article 62 The All-China Sports Federation and local sports federations at all levels are mass sports organizations that unite various sports organizations, sports workers, and sports enthusiasts, and shall play a role in the development of sports.

Article 63 The Chinese Olympic Committee is a sports organization whose main tasks are to develop sports and promote the Olympic movement, and it represents China in international Olympic affairs.

Article 64 Sports science societies are academic sports social organizations of sports science and technology workers, and shall play a role in the development of sports science and technology.

Article 65 National individual sports associations are sports social organizations registered in accordance with the law, representing China in corresponding international individual sports organizations, joining the All-China Sports Federation in accordance with their articles of association, and sending representatives to serve as members of the Chinese Olympic Committee.

National individual sports associations shall be responsible for the popularization and improvement of their respective sports, formulate technical specifications, competition rules, and group standards for their respective sports, and regulate sports events and activities.

Article 66 Individual sports associations shall, in accordance with the law, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of their members, and actively reflect the opinions and suggestions of their members to relevant units.

Article 67 Individual sports associations shall accept the guidance and supervision of sports administrative departments, improve their internal governance mechanisms, formulate industry rules, and strengthen industry self-discipline.

Article 68 The State shall encourage the development of various types of autonomous sports organizations such as youth sports clubs and community fitness organizations.

## Chapter VII Sports Industry

Article 69 The State shall formulate plans for the development of the sports industry, expand the scale of the sports industry, enhance the vitality of the sports industry, promote high-quality development of the sports industry, and meet the diverse sports needs of the people.

People's governments at or above the county level shall establish a coordination mechanism for the development of the sports industry involving cooperation among multiple government departments.

Article 70 The State shall support and regulate the development of sports goods manufacturing, sports services, and other sports industries, and promote the integrated development of sports with health, culture, tourism, elderly care, and science and technology.

Article 71 The State shall support the innovative development of the sports goods manufacturing industry, encourage enterprises to increase R&D investment, adopt new technologies, new processes, and new materials, and promote the transformation and upgrading of the sports goods manufacturing industry.

The State shall cultivate service formats such as fitness and leisure, competition performance, venue services, sports agency, and sports training, and improve the level and quality of the sports service industry.

Qualified sports industries shall enjoy preferential policies in finance, taxation, land, etc., in accordance with the law.

Article 72 The State shall improve the professional sports development system, expand channels for professional sports development, support the professional development of athletes and coaches, and enhance the maturity and standardization of professional sports.

Professional sports clubs shall improve their internal governance mechanisms, perfect their corporate governance structures, and fully leverage their role as market entities.

Article 73 The State shall establish and improve a coordination and interaction mechanism for regional sports industries, promote the exchange and sharing of sports industry resources between regions, and promote the coordinated development of regional sports.

The State shall support localities in leveraging their resource advantages to develop sports industries with regional and ethnic characteristics.

Article 74 The State shall encourage social capital to invest in the sports industry, build sports facilities, develop sports products, and provide sports services.

Article 75 The State shall encourage qualified institutions of higher learning to set up majors related to the sports industry, carry out school-enterprise cooperation, strengthen vocational education and training, cultivate professional talents for the sports industry, and form a talent team that effectively supports the development of the sports industry.

Article 76 The State shall improve the sports industry statistical system, conduct statistical monitoring of the sports industry, and regularly publish sports industry data.

## Chapter VIII Guarantee Conditions

Article 77 People's governments at or above the county level shall include sports expenditure in their budgets and establish an investment mechanism that is compatible with national economic and social development.

Article 78 The State shall encourage social forces to develop sports, encourage donations and sponsorships for sports, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of participating entities.

Those who support the development of sports through donations of property or other means shall enjoy tax incentives and other policies in accordance with the law.Article 79: Relevant state departments shall strengthen the management of sports funds. No unit or individual may embezzle, misappropriate, intercept, withhold, or privately distribute sports funds.

Article 80: The state supports the provision of public sports services through government procurement of services to improve the level of public sports services.

Article 81: Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations and based on the level of economic and social development, population structure, environmental conditions, and the needs of sports development in their administrative regions, make overall plans and optimize the allocation of sports venues and facilities at all levels and types, giving priority to ensuring the construction and allocation of sports venues and facilities for national fitness.

Article 82: Local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the construction of public sports venues and facilities within their administrative regions into national economic and social development plans and territorial spatial plans, and such plans shall not be altered without legal procedures.

The planning, design, and completion acceptance of public sports venues and facilities shall seek the opinions of the sports administrative department of the people's government at the same level.

The design and construction of public sports venues and facilities shall comply with the national requirements for barrier-free environment construction, effectively meeting the barrier-free needs of specific groups such as the elderly, persons with disabilities, etc.

Article 83: When constructing, renovating, or expanding residential communities, supporting sports venues and facilities for residents' daily fitness shall be planned, designed, and constructed simultaneously in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 84: The management units of public sports venues and facilities shall publicly disclose their opening methods to the public and provide preferential treatment to minors, the elderly, persons with disabilities, etc.

Sports venues and facilities that are open for free or at low cost shall enjoy subsidies in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 85: The state promotes the construction of sports parks, encourages local governments to develop characteristic sports parks according to local conditions, and promotes the free opening of sports parks to meet citizens' sports and fitness needs.

Article 86: The state encourages the full and reasonable use of idle resources such as old factories, warehouses, and old commercial facilities to build sports venues and facilities for citizens' daily fitness, and encourages and supports the opening of sports venues and facilities of government agencies, schools, enterprises, and public institutions to the public.

Article 87: No unit or individual may encroach upon public sports venues and facilities or their construction land, may not dismantle public sports venues and facilities without authorization, and may not change the function or purpose of public sports venues and facilities without authorization or hinder their normal use.

If temporary occupation of public sports venues and facilities for more than ten days is necessary due to special needs, the consent of the sports administrative department of the people's government at the same level shall be obtained; if it exceeds three months, approval from the sports administrative department of the people's government at the next higher level shall be obtained.

If the demolition of public sports venues and facilities or the change of their function or purpose is approved, reconstruction shall be carried out on a selected site first in accordance with relevant state laws and administrative regulations.

Article 88: Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a maintenance and management mechanism for public sports venues and facilities for national fitness, clarifying the responsibilities for management and maintenance.

Article 89: The state develops professional sports education, encourages qualified higher education institutions to cultivate various sports professionals such as coaches, referees, and physical education teachers, and encourages social forces to carry out professional sports education in accordance with the law.

Article 90: The state encourages the establishment and improvement of systems for athlete disability insurance, sports accident insurance, and venue liability insurance.

Organizers of large-scale sports events shall negotiate with participants to take out sports accident insurance.

Organizers of high-risk sports events shall take out sports accident insurance.

Operators of high-risk sports events shall take out sports accident insurance and venue liability insurance.

## Chapter 9 Sports Arbitration

Article 91: The state establishes a sports arbitration system to resolve sports disputes promptly and fairly, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of the parties.

Sports arbitration shall be conducted independently in accordance with the law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 92: Parties may apply for sports arbitration for the following disputes based on arbitration agreements, sports organization constitutions, sports event rules, etc.:

(1) Disputes arising from dissatisfaction with decisions made by sports social organizations, athlete management units, or sports event organizers regarding disqualification, annulment of competition results, or suspension according to anti-doping or other management regulations;

(2) Disputes arising from athlete registration or transfer;

(3) Other disputes arising in competitive sports activities.

Disputes that are arbitrable under the Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China and labor disputes under the Labor Dispute Mediation and Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China are not within the scope of sports arbitration.

Article 93: The sports administrative department of the State Council shall establish a sports arbitration commission in accordance with this Law and formulate sports arbitration rules.

The sports arbitration commission shall be composed of representatives from the sports administrative department, sports social organizations, athletes, coaches, referees, and sports and legal experts, and the number of its members shall be an odd number.

The sports arbitration commission shall maintain a roster of arbitrators. The specific qualifications for arbitrators shall be stipulated by the sports arbitration rules.

Article 94: The sports arbitration commission shall adjudicate sports disputes using an arbitration tribunal system. The number of members of the arbitration tribunal shall be an odd number, and the specific composition method shall be stipulated by the sports arbitration rules.

Article 95: Sports organizations are encouraged to establish internal dispute resolution mechanisms to resolve disputes fairly, justly, and efficiently.

If a sports organization has no internal dispute resolution mechanism or the internal dispute resolution mechanism fails to resolve the dispute in a timely manner, the parties may apply for sports arbitration.

Article 96: If a party is dissatisfied with a decision made by a sports social organization, athlete management unit, or sports event organizer, or with the result of an internal dispute resolution mechanism, the party may apply for sports arbitration within twenty-one days from the date of receiving the decision or the dispute resolution result.

Article 97: The sports arbitration award shall have legal effect from the date it is made.

After the award is made, if a party applies for sports arbitration again for the same dispute or files a lawsuit with a people's court, the sports arbitration commission or the people's court shall not accept it.

Article 98: In any of the following circumstances, a party may, within thirty days from the date of receiving the arbitration award, apply to the intermediate people's court in the place where the sports arbitration commission is located for the revocation of the award:

(1) The application of laws or regulations is indeed erroneous;

(2) The matter adjudicated is not within the scope of sports arbitration;

(3) The composition of the arbitration tribunal or the arbitration procedure violates relevant regulations, sufficient to affect a fair award;

(4) The evidence on which the award is based is forged;

(5) The opposing party has concealed evidence sufficient to affect a fair award;

(6) An arbitrator has engaged in soliciting or accepting bribes, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or perverting the law in rendering the award.

If the people's court, after forming a collegial panel to review and verify, finds that the award falls under any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, or determines that the award violates the public interest, it shall rule to revoke the award.

After accepting an application for revocation of an award, if the people's court considers that the arbitration tribunal may re-arbitrate, it shall notify the arbitration tribunal to re-arbitrate within a certain period and rule to suspend the revocation procedure. If the arbitration tribunal refuses to re-arbitrate, the people's court shall rule to resume the revocation procedure.

Article 99: The parties shall perform the sports arbitration award. If one party fails to perform, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 100: For disputes arising from sports events that require immediate handling, the special procedures for sports arbitration shall apply.

The special procedures shall be stipulated by the sports arbitration rules.

## Chapter 10 Supervision and Management

Article 101: The sports administrative departments and relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall actively perform their supervision and inspection duties. Upon discovering any violation of this Law, they shall handle it promptly. For matters not under their jurisdiction, they shall promptly notify in writing and transfer them to the relevant departments for investigation and handling.

Article 102: The sports administrative departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage sports events in accordance with the law, conduct on-site inspections of event venues, inspect and copy relevant contracts, invoices, account books, and examine materials such as event organization plans and safety emergency plans.

The public security, market supervision, emergency management, and other departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage sports events according to their respective responsibilities.

Organizers of sports events shall fulfill their safety guarantee obligations, provide safety conditions that meet requirements, and formulate safeguard measures such as risk prevention and emergency response plans to ensure the safety of sports events.

If a sports event cannot be held due to extreme weather, natural disasters, public health incidents, or other emergencies, the organizer shall promptly suspend the event; if not suspended, the people's government at or above the county level shall order its suspension.

Article 103: The market supervision, sports administrative, and other departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage the sports market according to their respective responsibilities.

Article 104: The state establishes a sports event management system. New sports events shall be recognized by the sports administrative department of the State Council.

The catalog of sports events shall be published every four years.

Article 105: To operate high-risk sports events, the following conditions shall be met, and an application shall be submitted to the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level:

(1) The relevant sports facilities comply with national standards;

(2) There are a specified number of social sports instructors and rescue personnel who have obtained corresponding national vocational qualification certificates or vocational skill level certificates;

(3) There are corresponding safety guarantee, emergency rescue systems, and measures.

The sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall conduct an on-site inspection within thirty days from the date of receiving the application and make a decision to approve or disapprove. If approved, a license shall be issued; if not approved, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons.

The sports administrative department of the State Council, together with relevant departments, shall formulate and adjust the catalog of high-risk sports events and publish it.

Article 106: To hold high-risk sports events, the following conditions shall be met, and an application shall be submitted to the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level:

(1) Equipped with professional and technical personnel with corresponding qualifications or credentials;

(2) Configured with venues, equipment, and facilities that meet relevant standards and requirements;

(3) Formulated safeguard measures such as communication, safety, transportation, health, food, and emergency rescue.

The sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall conduct an on-site inspection within thirty days from the date of receiving the application and make a decision to approve or disapprove.

The sports administrative department of the State Council, together with relevant departments, shall formulate and adjust the catalog of high-risk sports events and publish it.

Article 107: Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a sports law enforcement mechanism and provide necessary guarantees for sports law enforcement. Sports law enforcement situations shall be made public to society and subject to social supervision.

Article 108: Local people's governments at or above the county level shall report on national fitness, youth, and school sports work to the people's congress or its standing committee at the same level at least once during each term of office.

## Chapter 11 Legal Liability

Article 109: If state organs and their staff violate the provisions of this Law and commit any of the following acts, they shall be ordered to correct by their unit, the competent department, or the higher authority; the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to investigate and deal with illegal acts in accordance with the law;

(2) Embezzling, misappropriating, intercepting, withholding, or privately distributing sports funds;

(3) Engaging in acts that violate sports ethics and sports event rules, such as fraud or malpractice for personal gain, when organizing sports events;

(4) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the law.

Article 110: If a sports organization violates the provisions of this Law, the relevant department shall order it to correct, issue a warning, and impose sanctions on the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; it may order a suspension of activities for a specified period and may order the replacement of the directly responsible person in charge; if the circumstances are serious, its registration shall be revoked.

Article 111: If a school violates the relevant provisions of this Law, the relevant competent department shall order it to correct; the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 112: If athletes, coaches, or referees violate the provisions of this Law and engage in acts that violate sports ethics and sports event rules, such as fraud or malpractice for personal gain, the sports organization shall handle the matter in accordance with relevant regulations; if the circumstances are serious and the social impact is adverse, the sports administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall include them in the list of persons restricted or prohibited from participating in competitive sports activities; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Those who engage in gambling activities using sports events shall be investigated and dealt with by the public security organs in accordance with the law.

Article 113: If a sports event organizer commits any of the following acts, the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order it to correct, impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, a penalty of prohibiting the organization of sports events for not less than one year but not more than three years shall be imposed:

(1) Holding high-risk sports events without permission;

(2) Failing to suspend the sports event in a timely manner when it cannot be held due to an emergency;

(3) Safety conditions not meeting requirements;

(4) Engaging in acts that violate sports ethics and sports event rules, such as fraud or malpractice for personal gain;

(5) Failing to take safeguard measures such as risk prevention and emergency response plans as required.

Article 114: If anyone violates the provisions of this Law by encroaching upon or damaging public sports venues and facilities, the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, together with relevant departments, shall stop the act, order correction, and may impose a fine of not more than five times the actual loss.

Article 115: If anyone violates the provisions of this Law by temporarily occupying public sports venues and facilities without approval, the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, together with relevant departments, shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the management unit of the public sports venues and facilities; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Article 116: If anyone operates high-risk sports events without a license, the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, together with relevant departments, shall order closure within a specified period; if closure is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If anyone illegally operates high-risk sports events, the sports administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if serious consequences are caused, the competent department shall order closure, revoke the license, and the person shall not engage in the operation of such events for five years.

Article 117: If an athlete illegally uses doping, the relevant sports social organization, athlete management unit, or sports event organizer shall impose sanctions such as disqualification, annulment of competition results, or suspension.

Article 118: If anyone organizes, forces, deceives, instigates, or entices an athlete to use doping in sports activities, the sports administrative department of the State Council or the sports administrative department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall confiscate the illegally held doping; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be prohibited from engaging in sports management work and athlete support work for four years; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be prohibited from engaging in sports management work and athlete support work for life.

If anyone provides or indirectly provides doping to an athlete, the sports administrative department of the State Council or the sports administrative department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall confiscate the illegally held doping, impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; and impose a penalty of prohibiting the person from engaging in sports management work and athlete support work for a certain period up to life.

Article 119: If anyone violates the provisions of this Law, causing property loss or other damage, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 12 Supplementary Provisions

Article 120: If any country, region, or organization damages the sovereignty, security, development interests, and dignity of the People's Republic of China in international sports activities, the People's Republic of China may take corresponding measures according to the actual situation.

Article 121: The specific measures for the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force to carry out sports activities shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 122: This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2023.
