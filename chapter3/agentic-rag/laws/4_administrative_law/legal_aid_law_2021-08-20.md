# Legal Aid Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 20, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to regulate and promote legal aid work, protect the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and relevant parties, ensure the correct implementation of laws, and uphold social fairness and justice.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, legal aid refers to a system established by the State to provide legal services such as legal consultation, agency, and criminal defense free of charge to citizens in financial difficulty and other parties meeting statutory conditions. It is an integral part of the public legal service system.

**Article 3** Legal aid work adheres to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, upholds a people-centered approach, respects and protects human rights, follows the principles of openness, fairness, and justice, and combines State guarantees with social participation.

**Article 4** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate legal aid work into national economic and social development plans and the basic public service system, ensuring the coordinated development of legal aid undertakings with the economy and society.

People's governments at or above the county level shall improve the legal aid guarantee system, include relevant legal aid funds in their fiscal budgets, establish a dynamic adjustment mechanism, ensure the needs of legal aid work, and promote the balanced development of legal aid.

**Article 5** The judicial administration department of the State Council shall guide and supervise legal aid work nationwide. Judicial administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall guide and supervise legal aid work within their respective administrative regions.

Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions, provide support and guarantees for legal aid work.

**Article 6** People's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall, within their respective functions, ensure that parties obtain legal aid in accordance with the law and provide convenience for legal aid personnel to carry out their work.

**Article 7** Lawyers' associations shall guide and support law firms and lawyers in participating in legal aid work.

**Article 8** The State encourages and supports mass organizations, public institutions, and social organizations to provide legal aid in accordance with the law under the guidance of judicial administration departments.

**Article 9** The State encourages and supports enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individuals to support legal aid undertakings through donations and other means in accordance with the law; tax incentives shall be granted to those who meet the conditions.

**Article 10** Judicial administration departments shall regularly carry out legal aid publicity and education to popularize legal aid knowledge.

News media shall actively conduct public interest publicity on legal aid and strengthen public opinion supervision.

**Article 11** The State shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to legal aid work in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter II Institutions and Personnel

**Article 12** Judicial administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall establish legal aid institutions. Legal aid institutions are responsible for organizing and implementing legal aid work, accepting and reviewing legal aid applications, assigning lawyers, grassroots legal service workers, legal aid volunteers, and other legal aid personnel to provide legal aid, and paying legal aid subsidies.

**Article 13** Legal aid institutions may, as needed, arrange for their own staff members who hold lawyer qualifications or legal professional qualifications to provide legal aid; they may set up legal aid workstations or contact points to accept legal aid applications nearby.

**Article 14** Legal aid institutions may station duty lawyers in places such as people's courts, people's procuratorates, and detention centers to provide legal aid in accordance with the law to criminal suspects and defendants who do not have defenders.

**Article 15** Judicial administration departments may, through government procurement and other means, select law firms and other legal service institutions to provide legal aid to recipients.

**Article 16** Law firms, grassroots legal service offices, lawyers, and grassroots legal service workers have the obligation to provide legal aid in accordance with the law.

Law firms and grassroots legal service offices shall support and ensure that their lawyers and grassroots legal service workers fulfill their legal aid obligations.

**Article 17** The State encourages and regulates legal aid volunteer services; supports eligible individuals as legal aid volunteers to provide legal aid in accordance with the law.

Institutions of higher education and scientific research institutions may organize personnel engaged in legal education and research, as well as law students, as legal aid volunteers to provide legal consultation, draft legal documents, and other legal aid services to parties under the guidance of judicial administration departments.

Specific management measures for legal aid volunteers shall be formulated by the relevant departments of the State Council.

**Article 18** The State establishes and improves a mechanism for the cross-regional flow of legal service resources in accordance with the law, encouraging and supporting law firms, lawyers, and legal aid volunteers to provide legal aid in areas with relatively scarce legal service resources.

**Article 19** Legal aid personnel shall perform their duties in accordance with the law, promptly provide legal aid services that meet standards to recipients, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of recipients.

**Article 20** Legal aid personnel shall abide by professional ethics and practice discipline and shall not accept any property or money from recipients.

**Article 21** Legal aid institutions and legal aid personnel shall keep confidential any State secrets, trade secrets, and personal privacy learned during the provision of legal aid.

## Chapter III Forms and Scope

**Article 22** Legal aid institutions may organize legal aid personnel to provide the following forms of legal aid services in accordance with the law:

(1) Legal consultation;
(2) Drafting legal documents;
(3) Criminal defense and representation;
(4) Litigation representation and non-litigation representation in civil cases, administrative cases, and State compensation cases;
(5) Legal assistance from duty lawyers;
(6) Representation in labor dispute mediation and arbitration;
(7) Other forms prescribed by laws, regulations, and rules.

**Article 23** Legal aid institutions shall provide legal consultation services through various means such as service windows, telephone, and the internet; remind parties of their right to apply for legal aid in accordance with the law and inform them of the conditions and procedures for applying for legal aid.

**Article 24** If a criminal suspect or defendant in a criminal case has not retained a defender due to financial difficulty or other reasons, the person or their close relatives may apply to a legal aid institution for legal aid.

**Article 25** If a criminal suspect or defendant in a criminal case falls into one of the following categories and has not retained a defender, the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ shall notify a legal aid institution to assign a lawyer to act as a defender:

(1) Minors;
(2) Persons with visual, hearing, or speech disabilities;
(3) Adults who cannot fully recognize their own conduct;
(4) Persons who may be sentenced to life imprisonment or death;
(5) Defendants in death penalty review cases who apply for legal aid;
(6) Defendants in trials by default;
(7) Other persons prescribed by laws and regulations.

For other criminal cases tried under ordinary procedures, if the defendant has not retained a defender, the people's court may notify a legal aid institution to assign a lawyer to act as a defender.

**Article 26** For persons who may be sentenced to life imprisonment or death, and defendants in death penalty review cases, upon receiving notification from the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ, the legal aid institution shall assign a lawyer with at least three years of relevant practice experience to act as a defender.

**Article 27** When a people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ notifies a legal aid institution to assign a lawyer as a defender, it shall not restrict or impair the right of the criminal suspect or defendant to retain a defender.

**Article 28** If the respondent or defendant in a compulsory medical treatment case has not retained a litigation representative, the people's court shall notify a legal aid institution to assign a lawyer to provide legal aid.

**Article 29** If the victim of a criminal public prosecution case and their legal representative or close relatives, the private prosecutor in a criminal private prosecution case and their legal representative, or the plaintiff in a criminal incidental civil action case and their legal representative have not retained a litigation representative due to financial difficulty, they may apply to a legal aid institution for legal aid.

**Article 30** Duty lawyers shall provide legal assistance in accordance with the law to criminal suspects and defendants who do not have defenders, including legal consultation, suggestions on procedural choices, applications for changing compulsory measures, and opinions on case handling.

**Article 31** Parties to the following matters who have not retained a representative due to financial difficulty may apply to a legal aid institution for legal aid:

(1) Claiming State compensation in accordance with the law;
(2) Requesting social insurance benefits or social assistance;
(3) Requesting the payment of pensions;
(4) Requesting the payment of alimony, child support, or maintenance;
(5) Requesting confirmation of a labor relationship or payment of labor remuneration;
(6) Requesting a determination that a citizen has no or limited capacity for civil conduct;
(7) Requesting compensation for personal injury from work-related accidents, traffic accidents, food and drug safety accidents, or medical accidents;
(8) Requesting compensation for damages from environmental pollution or ecological destruction;
(9) Other circumstances prescribed by laws, regulations, and rules.

**Article 32** In any of the following circumstances, a party applying for legal aid is not subject to the financial difficulty condition:

(1) Close relatives of heroes or martyrs seeking to protect their personal rights and interests;
(2) Claiming relevant civil rights and interests due to acts of righteousness and courage;
(3) Requesting State compensation after being acquitted upon retrial;
(4) Victims of abuse, abandonment, or domestic violence claiming relevant rights and interests;
(5) Other circumstances prescribed by laws, regulations, and rules.

**Article 33** If a party files a petition or applies for a retrial against an effective judgment or ruling of a judicial organ, and the people's court decides or rules to retry or the people's procuratorate files a protest, and the party has not retained a defender or litigation representative due to financial difficulty, the party or their close relatives may apply to a legal aid institution for legal aid.

**Article 34** The standard for financial difficulty shall be determined by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government based on the economic development level of the administrative region and the needs of legal aid work, and shall be subject to dynamic adjustment.

## Chapter IV Procedures and Implementation

**Article 35** People's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, and relevant departments shall, when handling cases or related matters, promptly inform the relevant parties of their right to apply for legal aid in accordance with the law.

**Article 36** When handling criminal cases and discovering circumstances specified in Article 25, Paragraph 1, or Article 28 of this Law, people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall notify a legal aid institution to assign a lawyer within three days. Upon receiving the notification, the legal aid institution shall assign a lawyer within three days and notify the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ.

**Article 37** People's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall ensure that duty lawyers provide legal assistance in accordance with the law, inform criminal suspects and defendants without defenders of their right to meet with duty lawyers, and provide convenience for duty lawyers to understand case-related information, review files, and meet with clients in accordance with the law.

**Article 38** For legal aid in litigation matters, the applicant shall submit an application to the legal aid institution at the location of the case-handling organ; for legal aid in non-litigation matters, the applicant shall submit an application to the legal aid institution at the location of the dispute resolution organ or where the matter occurred.

**Article 39** If a detained criminal suspect, defendant, prisoner, or person under compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation submits a legal aid application, the case-handling organ or detention facility shall forward the application to the legal aid institution within 24 hours.

If a criminal suspect or defendant submits a legal aid application for representation or criminal defense through a duty lawyer, the duty lawyer shall forward the application to the legal aid institution within 24 hours.

**Article 40** If a person with no or limited capacity for civil conduct requires legal aid, their legal representative may submit an application on their behalf. If the legal representative infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of the person with no or limited capacity for civil conduct, other legal representatives or close relatives may submit a legal aid application on their behalf.

A detained criminal suspect, defendant, prisoner, or person under compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation may have their legal representative or close relatives submit a legal aid application on their behalf.

**Article 41** When applying for legal aid due to financial difficulty, the applicant shall truthfully explain their financial difficulty situation.

A legal aid institution may verify the applicant's financial difficulty situation through information sharing inquiries or by requiring the applicant to make a personal integrity commitment.

When a legal aid institution conducts verification, relevant departments, units, villagers' committees, residents' committees, and individuals shall cooperate.

**Article 42** If a legal aid applicant has materials proving that they fall into one of the following categories, the verification of financial difficulty shall be exempted:

(1) Specific groups such as minors, elderly persons, or persons with disabilities without a fixed source of income;
(2) Recipients of social assistance, judicial aid, or special care;
(3) Migrant workers claiming labor remuneration or compensation for personal injury from work-related accidents;
(4) Other persons prescribed by laws, regulations, and rules.

**Article 43** A legal aid institution shall review a legal aid application within seven days of receipt and decide whether to grant legal aid. If it decides to grant legal aid, it shall assign legal aid personnel to provide legal aid to the recipient within three days of the decision; if it decides not to grant legal aid, it shall notify the applicant in writing and state the reasons.

If the application materials submitted by the applicant are incomplete, the legal aid institution shall inform the applicant at one time of the materials to be supplemented or require the applicant to provide explanations. If the applicant fails to supplement materials or provide explanations as required, the application shall be deemed withdrawn.

**Article 44** After receiving a legal aid application, if a legal aid institution finds any of the following circumstances, it may decide to provide legal aid in advance:

(1) Less than seven days remain before the statutory limitation or deadline, and it is necessary to file a lawsuit or apply for arbitration or administrative reconsideration promptly;
(2) It is necessary to immediately apply for property preservation, evidence preservation, or advance execution;
(3) Other circumstances prescribed by laws, regulations, and rules.

If a legal aid institution provides legal aid in advance, the recipient shall promptly complete the relevant procedures and supplement the relevant materials.

**Article 45** When providing legal aid services to elderly persons or persons with disabilities, legal aid institutions shall provide accessible facilities, equipment, and services based on actual circumstances.

If laws and regulations have other special provisions on providing legal aid to specific groups, those provisions shall prevail.

**Article 46** After accepting an assignment, legal aid personnel shall not refuse, delay, or terminate the provision of legal aid services without justifiable reasons.

Legal aid personnel shall inform recipients of the progress of legal aid matters in accordance with regulations and shall not harm the legitimate rights and interests of recipients.

**Article 47** Recipients shall truthfully state to legal aid personnel the circumstances related to the legal aid matter, promptly provide evidence materials, and assist and cooperate in handling the legal aid matter.

**Article 48** A legal aid institution shall decide to terminate legal aid in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The recipient obtained legal aid through fraud or other improper means;
(2) The recipient intentionally concealed important facts related to the case or provided false evidence;
(3) The recipient used legal aid to engage in illegal activities;
(4) The recipient's financial situation has changed and no longer meets the conditions for legal aid;
(5) The case has been terminated or dismissed;
(6) The recipient has retained a lawyer or other representative on their own;
(7) The recipient has a legitimate reason to request termination of legal aid;
(8) Other circumstances prescribed by laws and regulations.

If legal aid personnel discover any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, they shall promptly report to the legal aid institution.

**Article 49** If an applicant or recipient disagrees with a legal aid institution's decision not to grant legal aid or to terminate legal aid, they may raise an objection with the judicial administration department that established the legal aid institution.

The judicial administration department shall review the objection within five days of receipt and make a decision to uphold the legal aid institution's decision or order the legal aid institution to correct it.

If an applicant or recipient is dissatisfied with the judicial administration department's decision to uphold the legal aid institution's decision, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

**Article 50** After the legal aid matter is concluded, legal aid personnel shall promptly report to the legal aid institution and submit copies or duplicates of relevant legal documents, case handling reports, and other materials.

## Chapter V Guarantees and Supervision

**Article 51** The State strengthens the informatization of legal aid, promoting information sharing and work coordination between judicial administration departments, judicial organs, and other relevant departments.

**Article 52** Legal aid institutions shall promptly pay legal aid subsidies to legal aid personnel in accordance with relevant regulations.

The standards for legal aid subsidies shall be determined by the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, in conjunction with the finance department at the same level, based on the local economic development level, the type of legal aid service, the cost of undertaking, and basic labor expenses, and shall be subject to dynamic adjustment.

Legal aid subsidies are exempt from value-added tax and personal income tax.

**Article 53** People's courts shall, based on the circumstances, defer, reduce, or exempt litigation costs for recipients; fees for legal aid personnel to copy relevant materials shall be exempted or reduced.

Notary institutions and judicial appraisal institutions shall reduce or exempt notarization and appraisal fees for recipients.

**Article 54** Judicial administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall systematically train legal aid personnel to improve their professional quality and service capabilities.

**Article 55** Recipients have the right to inquire with legal aid institutions and legal aid personnel about the progress of legal aid matters; if legal aid institutions or legal aid personnel fail to perform their duties in accordance with the law, recipients may file complaints with judicial administration departments and may request the legal aid institution to replace the legal aid personnel.

**Article 56** Judicial administration departments shall establish a complaint investigation and handling system for legal aid work; upon receiving a complaint, they shall accept, investigate, and handle it in accordance with relevant regulations and promptly inform the complainant of the handling result.

**Article 57** Judicial administration departments shall strengthen supervision over legal aid services, formulate quality standards for legal aid services, and conduct regular quality assessments through third-party evaluations and other means.

**Article 58** Judicial administration departments and legal aid institutions shall establish a legal aid information disclosure system, regularly publicize to the public the use of legal aid funds, case handling, and quality assessment results, and accept social supervision.

**Article 59** Legal aid institutions shall comprehensively use measures such as court hearing observation, case file inspection, soliciting opinions from judicial organs, and follow-up visits with recipients to urge legal aid personnel to improve service quality.**Article 60** Bar associations shall include the performance of legal aid obligations by law firms and lawyers in the annual assessment content. Law firms and lawyers that refuse or fail to fulfill their legal aid obligations shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 61** Where a legal aid institution or its staff member commits any of the following acts, the judicial administrative department that established the legal aid institution shall order corrective action within a specified period; if there are illegal gains, the gains shall be ordered to be returned or confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Refusing to provide legal aid to persons who meet the conditions for legal aid, or intentionally providing legal aid to persons who do not meet the conditions;

(2) Assigning persons who do not meet the requirements of this Law to provide legal aid;

(3) Accepting property from the aided person;

(4) Engaging in paid legal services;

(5) Embezzling, privately dividing, or misappropriating legal aid funds;

(6) Disclosing state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy learned during the legal aid process;

(7) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 62** Where a law firm or a basic-level legal service office commits any of the following acts, the judicial administrative department shall impose penalties according to law:

(1) Refusing, without justifiable reasons, to accept an assignment from a legal aid institution;

(2) After accepting an assignment, failing to promptly arrange for its lawyers or basic-level legal service workers to handle the legal aid matter, or refusing to provide support and guarantees for its lawyers or basic-level legal service workers to handle the legal aid matter;

(3) Conniving at or allowing its lawyers or basic-level legal service workers to neglect their legal aid obligations or unilaterally terminate the provision of legal aid;

(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 63** Where a lawyer or a basic-level legal service worker commits any of the following acts, the judicial administrative department shall impose penalties according to law:

(1) Refusing, without justifiable reasons, to fulfill legal aid obligations, or neglecting their legal aid obligations;

(2) Unilaterally terminating the provision of legal aid;

(3) Accepting property from the aided person;

(4) Disclosing state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy learned during the legal aid process;

(5) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 64** Where an aided person obtains legal aid through fraud or other improper means, the judicial administrative department shall order them to pay the costs of the legal aid already provided and impose a fine of not more than 3,000 yuan.

**Article 65** Where anyone, in violation of the provisions of this Law, provides legal services and seeks benefits under the guise of legal aid, the judicial administrative department shall order corrective action, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the amount of the illegal gains.

**Article 66** Where a state organ or its staff member abuses their power, neglects their duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain in legal aid work, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 67** Where a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 68** Mass organizations such as trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, and disabled persons' federations shall, when carrying out legal aid work, apply the relevant provisions of this Law by reference.

**Article 69** For the provision of legal aid to foreigners and stateless persons, if Chinese law has provisions, those provisions shall apply; if Chinese law has no provisions, the relevant provisions of this Law may be applied by reference, based on international treaties that China has concluded or acceded to, or in accordance with the principle of reciprocity.

**Article 70** Specific measures for providing legal aid to military personnel and their family members shall be formulated by the relevant departments of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 71** This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2022.
