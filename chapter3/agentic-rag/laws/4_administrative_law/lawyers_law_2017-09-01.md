# Lawyers Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Lawyers Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 29, 2001

Revised at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 28, 2007

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Lawyers Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to improve the lawyer system, regulate lawyers' practice, ensure lawyers practice in accordance with the law, and give full play to the role of lawyers in the construction of the socialist legal system.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, a "lawyer" refers to a practitioner who has lawfully obtained a lawyer's practice certificate, and accepts engagement or appointment to provide legal services to clients.

Lawyers shall safeguard the lawful rights and interests of clients, ensure the correct implementation of the law, and uphold social fairness and justice.

Article 3 Lawyers must abide by the Constitution and laws in their practice, and strictly adhere to lawyers' professional ethics and practice discipline.

Lawyers must base their practice on facts and take the law as the criterion.

Lawyers' practice shall be subject to the supervision of the State, society, and clients.

Lawyers' practice in accordance with the law is protected by law, and no organization or individual may infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of lawyers.

Article 4 The judicial administration department shall supervise and guide lawyers, law firms, and bar associations in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter II Licensing for Lawyers' Practice

Article 5 To apply for a lawyer's practice, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Upholding the Constitution of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Obtaining legal professional qualifications by passing the national uniform legal professional qualification examination;

(3) Completing a one-year internship at a law firm;

(4) Having good conduct.

Certificates of passing the national uniform judicial examination or lawyer qualification certificates obtained before the implementation of the national uniform legal professional qualification examination shall have the same effect as the national uniform legal professional qualification certificate.

Article 6 To apply for a lawyer's practice, an application shall be submitted to the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality directly under the Central Government, along with the following materials:

(1) The national uniform legal professional qualification certificate;

(2) Materials issued by the bar association certifying that the applicant has passed the internship assessment;

(3) The applicant's identity certificate;

(4) A certificate issued by a law firm confirming its agreement to accept the applicant.

If applying to practice as a part-time lawyer, a certificate from the applicant's work unit agreeing to the applicant's part-time practice as a lawyer shall also be submitted.

The department receiving the application shall review it within twenty days from the date of receipt, and submit the review opinion and all application materials to the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall examine the submitted materials within ten days from the date of receipt and decide whether to grant the practice license. If the practice license is granted, a lawyer's practice certificate shall be issued to the applicant; if not, the reasons shall be provided in writing to the applicant.

Article 7 A lawyer's practice certificate shall not be issued to an applicant under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having no civil capacity or having limited civil capacity;

(2) Having been subjected to criminal punishment, except for crimes of negligence;

(3) Having been discharged from public employment or having had their lawyer's or notary's practice certificate revoked.

Article 8 Persons who have obtained a bachelor's degree or above from an institution of higher education, have engaged in professional work in areas with a shortage of legal service personnel for at least fifteen years, possess senior professional titles or equivalent professional level, and have corresponding professional legal knowledge, may apply to practice as a full-time lawyer. If they pass the assessment by the judicial administration department of the State Council, they shall be permitted to practice. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 9 Under any of the following circumstances, the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the decision to permit practice and cancel the lawyer's practice certificate of the person permitted to practice:

(1) The applicant obtained the lawyer's practice certificate by fraudulent, bribery, or other improper means;

(2) The practice license was granted to an applicant who did not meet the conditions prescribed in this Law.

Article 10 A lawyer may practice in only one law firm. If a lawyer changes their practice institution, they shall apply for a replacement lawyer's practice certificate.

A lawyer's practice is not restricted by region.

Article 11 Public servants shall not concurrently practice as lawyers.

If a lawyer serves as a member of the standing committee of a people's congress at any level, they shall not engage in litigation representation or defense business during their term of office.

Article 12 Personnel engaged in legal education or research in institutions of higher education or scientific research institutions who meet the conditions prescribed in Article 5 of this Law may, with the consent of their work unit, apply to practice as part-time lawyers in accordance with the procedures prescribed in Article 6 of this Law.

Article 13 Persons who have not obtained a lawyer's practice certificate shall not engage in legal services business in the name of a lawyer; except as otherwise provided by law, they shall not engage in litigation representation or defense business.

## Chapter III Law Firms

Article 14 A law firm is the practice institution of lawyers. The establishment of a law firm shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having its own name, domicile, and articles of association;

(2) Having lawyers who meet the conditions prescribed in this Law;

(3) The founder shall be a lawyer with a certain period of practice experience and who has not been subjected to a penalty of suspension from practice within the preceding three years;

(4) Having assets in an amount prescribed by the judicial administration department of the State Council.

Article 15 To establish a partnership law firm, in addition to meeting the conditions prescribed in Article 14 of this Law, there shall be at least three partners, and the founder shall be a lawyer with at least three years of practice experience.

A partnership law firm may be established in the form of a general partnership or a special general partnership. Partners of a partnership law firm shall bear liability for the debts of the law firm in accordance with the law based on the partnership form.

Article 16 To establish a sole practitioner law firm, in addition to meeting the conditions prescribed in Article 14 of this Law, the founder shall be a lawyer with at least five years of practice experience. The founder shall bear unlimited liability for the debts of the law firm.

Article 17 To apply for the establishment of a law firm, the following materials shall be submitted:

(1) A written application;

(2) The name and articles of association of the law firm;

(3) A list of lawyers, their resumes, identity certificates, and lawyer's practice certificates;

(4) Proof of domicile;

(5) Proof of assets.

To establish a partnership law firm, a partnership agreement shall also be submitted.

Article 18 To establish a law firm, an application shall be submitted to the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality directly under the Central Government. The department receiving the application shall review it within twenty days from the date of receipt, and submit the review opinion and all application materials to the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall examine the submitted materials within ten days from the date of receipt and decide whether to grant the establishment. If the establishment is granted, a law firm practice certificate shall be issued to the applicant; if not, the reasons shall be provided in writing to the applicant.

Article 19 A partnership law firm that has been established for at least three years and has at least twenty practicing lawyers may establish a branch office. The establishment of a branch office shall be subject to examination by the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the branch office is to be located. The application for establishing a branch office shall follow the procedures prescribed in Article 18 of this Law.

A partnership law firm shall bear liability for the debts of its branch office.

Article 20 A law firm established with State funding shall independently conduct lawyer business in accordance with the law and bear liability for its debts with all of its assets.

Article 21 If a law firm changes its name, responsible person, articles of association, or partnership agreement, it shall report to the original examination department for approval.

If a law firm changes its domicile or partners, it shall report to the original examination department for recordation within fifteen days from the date of the change.

Article 22 A law firm shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) It fails to maintain the statutory conditions for establishment and still does not meet the conditions after being ordered to rectify within a specified time limit;

(2) Its law firm practice certificate is lawfully revoked;

(3) It decides to dissolve on its own;

(4) Other circumstances under which termination is required by laws or administrative regulations.

Upon termination of a law firm, the department that issued the practice certificate shall cancel the law firm's practice certificate.

Article 23 A law firm shall establish and improve systems for practice management, conflict of interest review, fee collection and financial management, complaint handling, annual assessment, and file management, and shall supervise lawyers' compliance with professional ethics and practice discipline during their practice activities.

Article 24 A law firm shall, after the annual assessment each year, submit to the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality directly under the Central Government a report on its annual practice situation and the results of the lawyers' practice assessment.

Article 25 When a lawyer undertakes a case, the law firm shall uniformly accept the engagement, sign a written engagement contract with the client, uniformly collect fees in accordance with State regulations, and record them truthfully in the accounts.

Law firms and lawyers shall pay taxes in accordance with the law.

Article 26 Law firms and lawyers shall not solicit business by improper means such as disparaging other law firms or lawyers, or paying referral fees.

Article 27 Law firms shall not engage in business activities other than legal services.

## Chapter IV Business, Rights, and Obligations of Lawyers

Article 28 Lawyers may engage in the following business:

(1) Accepting engagement by natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations to serve as legal counsel;

(2) Accepting engagement by parties in civil or administrative cases to serve as representatives and participate in litigation;

(3) Accepting engagement by criminal suspects or defendants, or accepting assignment by legal aid institutions in accordance with the law, to serve as defenders; accepting engagement by private prosecutors in private prosecution cases, victims in public prosecution cases, or their close relatives, to serve as representatives and participate in litigation;

(4) Accepting engagement to represent clients in appeals of various types of litigation cases;

(5) Accepting engagement to participate in mediation or arbitration activities;

(6) Accepting engagement to provide non-litigation legal services;

(7) Answering inquiries about the law, drafting litigation documents, and other documents related to legal matters.

Article 29 When serving as legal counsel, a lawyer shall, in accordance with the agreement, provide opinions to the client on relevant legal issues, draft and review legal documents, represent the client in litigation, mediation, or arbitration activities, handle other legal matters entrusted by the client, and safeguard the client's lawful rights and interests.

Article 30 When serving as a representative in litigation or non-litigation legal matters, a lawyer shall safeguard the client's lawful rights and interests within the scope of the authority granted.

Article 31 When serving as a defender, a lawyer shall, based on facts and the law, present materials and opinions that the criminal suspect or defendant is innocent, has a lesser crime, or that their criminal responsibility should be mitigated or exempted, and safeguard the litigation rights and other lawful rights and interests of the criminal suspect or defendant.

Article 32 A client may refuse the continued defense or representation by an engaged lawyer and may also engage another lawyer to serve as defender or representative.

After accepting an engagement, a lawyer shall not refuse defense or representation without justifiable reasons. However, if the entrusted matter is illegal, the client uses the lawyer's services to engage in illegal activities, or the client intentionally conceals important facts related to the case, the lawyer has the right to refuse defense or representation.

Article 33 When serving as a defender, a lawyer has the right to meet with a criminal suspect or defendant who is in custody or under residential surveillance, by presenting their lawyer's practice certificate, law firm certificate, and engagement letter or legal aid letter, in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law. Defense lawyers shall not be monitored when meeting with criminal suspects or defendants.

Article 34 When serving as a defender, a lawyer has the right to consult, extract, and copy the case file materials from the date the people's procuratorate begins to review the case for prosecution.

Article 35 An engaged lawyer may, based on the needs of the case, apply to the people's procuratorate or the people's court to collect or obtain evidence, or apply to the people's court to notify witnesses to testify in court.

When a lawyer investigates and collects evidence on their own, they may, by presenting their lawyer's practice certificate and law firm certificate, investigate with relevant units or individuals regarding the circumstances related to the legal matters they are handling.

Article 36 When a lawyer serves as a litigation representative or defender, their right to debate or defend shall be protected in accordance with the law.

Article 37 A lawyer's personal rights shall not be violated during their practice activities.

Opinions expressed by a lawyer in court as a representative or defender shall not be subject to legal investigation. However, this does not apply to statements that endanger national security, maliciously defame others, or seriously disrupt court order.

If a lawyer is suspected of committing a crime while participating in litigation activities, the investigative authority shall promptly notify the law firm or bar association to which the lawyer belongs; if the lawyer is lawfully detained or arrested, the investigative authority shall notify the lawyer's family members in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law.

Article 38 Lawyers shall keep confidential State secrets and commercial secrets learned during their practice activities, and shall not disclose the privacy of clients.

Lawyers shall keep confidential information and materials learned during their practice activities that the client or others are unwilling to disclose. However, this does not apply to criminal facts or information that the client or others are preparing or are in the process of committing that endanger national security, public safety, or seriously endanger the personal safety of others.

Article 39 A lawyer shall not serve as a representative for both parties in the same case, and shall not handle legal matters that have a conflict of interest with themselves or their close relatives.

Article 40 A lawyer shall not engage in any of the following acts during their practice activities:

(1) Accepting engagement or collecting fees privately, or accepting property or other benefits from the client;

(2) Taking advantage of the convenience of providing legal services to seek disputed rights and interests of the parties;

(3) Accepting property or other benefits from the opposing party, or maliciously colluding with the opposing party or a third party to infringe upon the client's rights and interests;

(4) Meeting with judges, prosecutors, arbitrators, or other relevant staff in violation of regulations;

(5) Offering bribes to judges, prosecutors, arbitrators, or other relevant staff, introducing bribes, or instigating or inducing parties to offer bribes, or otherwise improperly influencing judges, prosecutors, arbitrators, or other relevant staff in handling cases according to law;

(6) Intentionally providing false evidence, or threatening or inducing others to provide false evidence, or obstructing the opposing party from lawfully obtaining evidence;

(7) Inciting or instigating parties to use illegal means such as disrupting public order or endangering public safety to resolve disputes;

(8) Disrupting order in court or arbitration tribunal, or interfering with the normal conduct of litigation or arbitration activities.

Article 41 A lawyer who previously served as a judge or prosecutor shall not serve as a litigation representative or defender within two years after leaving the people's court or people's procuratorate.

Article 42 Lawyers and law firms shall fulfill their legal aid obligations in accordance with State regulations, provide legal services that meet the standards to recipients, and safeguard the lawful rights and interests of recipients.

## Chapter V Bar Associations

Article 43 A bar association is a social organization legal person and a self-regulatory organization of lawyers.

The All China Lawyers Association shall be established nationally, local bar associations shall be established in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and local bar associations may be established in cities divided into districts as needed.

Article 44 The articles of association of the national bar association shall be formulated by the National Congress of Members and reported to the judicial administration department of the State Council for recordation.

The articles of association of a local bar association shall be formulated by the local congress of members and reported to the judicial administration department at the same level for recordation. The articles of association of a local bar association shall not conflict with the articles of association of the national bar association.

Article 45 Lawyers and law firms shall join the local bar association in their locality. Lawyers and law firms that join a local bar association are also members of the national bar association.

Members of a bar association shall enjoy the rights and fulfill the obligations prescribed in the articles of association of the bar association.

Article 46 A bar association shall perform the following duties:

(1) Safeguarding lawyers' practice in accordance with the law and protecting the lawful rights and interests of lawyers;

(2) Summarizing and exchanging lawyers' work experience;

(3) Formulating industry standards and disciplinary rules;

(4) Organizing professional training for lawyers and education on professional ethics and practice discipline, and assessing lawyers' practice activities;

(5) Organizing and managing internship activities for persons applying for a lawyer's practice, and assessing interns;

(6) Implementing rewards and disciplinary sanctions for lawyers and law firms;

(7) Handling complaints or reports against lawyers, mediating disputes arising during lawyers' practice activities, and handling lawyers' appeals;

(8) Other duties prescribed by laws, administrative regulations, rules, and the articles of association of the bar association.

Industry standards and disciplinary rules formulated by a bar association shall not conflict with relevant laws, administrative regulations, or rules.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 47 If a lawyer commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality directly under the Central Government shall issue a warning and may impose a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, a penalty of suspension from practice for not more than three months shall be imposed:

(1) Practicing in two or more law firms simultaneously;

(2) Soliciting business by improper means;

(3) Serving as a representative for both parties in the same case, or handling legal matters that have a conflict of interest with themselves or their close relatives;

(4) Serving as a litigation representative or defender within two years after leaving a people's court or people's procuratorate;

(5) Refusing to fulfill legal aid obligations.

Article 48 If a lawyer commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality directly under the Central Government shall issue a warning and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, a penalty of suspension from practice for not less than three months but not more than six months shall be imposed:

(1) Accepting engagement or collecting fees privately, or accepting property or other benefits from the client;

(2) After accepting an engagement, refusing defense or representation without justifiable reasons, or failing to appear in court on time to participate in litigation or arbitration;

(3) Taking advantage of the convenience of providing legal services to seek disputed rights and interests of the parties;

(4) Disclosing commercial secrets or personal privacy.**Article 49** If a lawyer commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality shall impose a penalty of suspension from practice for not less than six months but not more than one year, and may also impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the lawyer's practice certificate; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Violating regulations by meeting with judges, prosecutors, arbitrators, or other relevant staff members, or influencing the handling of cases in other improper ways;

(2) Offering bribes to judges, prosecutors, arbitrators, or other relevant staff members, introducing bribes, or instigating or inducing a party to offer bribes;

(3) Providing false materials to the judicial administration department or engaging in other fraudulent acts;

(4) Intentionally providing false evidence, or threatening or inducing others to provide false evidence, or obstructing the opposing party from lawfully obtaining evidence;

(5) Accepting property or other benefits from the opposing party, or maliciously colluding with the opposing party or a third party to infringe upon the rights and interests of the client;

(6) Disrupting the order of a court or arbitration tribunal, or interfering with the normal progress of litigation or arbitration proceedings;

(7) Inciting or instigating a party to resort to illegal means such as disrupting public order or endangering public security to resolve disputes;

(8) Making statements that endanger national security, maliciously defame others, or seriously disrupt court order;

(9) Disclosing state secrets.

If a lawyer receives criminal punishment for an intentional crime, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the lawyer's practice certificate.

**Article 50** If a law firm commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality shall, depending on the circumstances, impose a warning or an order to suspend business for rectification for not less than one month but not more than six months, and may also impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are particularly serious, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the law firm's practice certificate:

(1) Violating regulations by accepting representation or collecting fees;

(2) Failing to follow statutory procedures when handling major matters such as changes to the name, responsible person, articles of association, partnership agreement, domicile, or partners;

(3) Engaging in business activities other than legal services;

(4) Soliciting business through improper means such as disparaging other law firms or lawyers, or paying referral fees;

(5) Violating regulations by accepting cases with conflicts of interest;

(6) Refusing to fulfill obligations of legal aid;

(7) Providing false materials to the judicial administration department or engaging in other fraudulent acts;

(8) Negligently managing the lawyers of the firm, resulting in serious consequences.

If a law firm is penalized for the illegal acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph, the responsible person shall be given a warning or a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan, depending on the severity of the circumstances.

**Article 51** If a lawyer, after receiving a warning penalty for violating this Law, commits an act within one year that warrants another warning penalty, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a city divided into districts or of a district under a municipality shall impose a penalty of suspension from practice for not less than three months but not more than one year; if, within two years after the expiration of a suspension-from-practice penalty, the lawyer commits an act that warrants another suspension-from-practice penalty, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the lawyer's practice certificate.

If a law firm, after the expiration of a suspension-of-business-for-rectification penalty for violating this Law, commits an act within two years that warrants another suspension-of-business-for-rectification penalty, the judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the law firm's practice certificate.

**Article 52** The judicial administration department of a people's government at the county level shall conduct routine supervision and management of the practice activities of lawyers and law firms, order corrections for problems discovered during inspections, and promptly investigate complaints from parties. If the judicial administration department of a people's government at the county level believes that an administrative penalty should be imposed for an illegal act by a lawyer or law firm, it shall submit a penalty recommendation to the superior judicial administration department.

**Article 53** A lawyer who has received a penalty of suspension from practice for six months or more shall not serve as a partner within three years after the expiration of the penalty period.

A person whose lawyer's practice certificate has been revoked shall not serve as a defender or agent ad litem, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party in criminal, civil, or administrative litigation.

**Article 54** If a lawyer causes losses to a party due to illegal practice or negligence, the law firm where the lawyer practices shall bear the liability for compensation. After compensation, the law firm may recover the amount from the lawyer who acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

**Article 55** If a person who has not obtained a lawyer's practice certificate engages in legal services under the name of a lawyer, the judicial administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of illegal practice, confiscate any illegal income, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income.

**Article 56** If a staff member of a judicial administration department violates the provisions of this Law by abusing power or neglecting duties, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if a crime is not constituted, disciplinary action shall be taken according to law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 57** For military lawyers providing legal services to the armed forces, the provisions of this Law shall apply to the acquisition of lawyer qualifications, rights and obligations, and codes of conduct. Specific measures for the management of military lawyers shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 58** Measures for the administration of foreign law firms establishing offices within the territory of the People's Republic of China to engage in legal services shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 59** Measures for lawyer fee collection shall be formulated by the competent pricing department of the State Council jointly with the judicial administration department of the State Council.

**Article 60** This Law shall come into force as of June 1, 2008.
