# Teachers Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 31, 1993

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws at the Tenth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of teachers, building a contingent of teachers with sound ideological and ethical cultivation and professional competence, and promoting the development of the socialist education cause.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to teachers who are engaged in education and teaching work in schools of various levels and types and other educational institutions.

Article 3 Teachers are professionals who perform the duties of education and teaching, and are charged with the mission of imparting knowledge and educating people, cultivating builders and successors for the socialist cause, and improving the quality of the nation. Teachers shall be loyal to the people's education cause.

Article 4 People's governments at various levels shall take measures to strengthen the ideological and political education and professional training of teachers, improve their working and living conditions, safeguard their legitimate rights and interests, and enhance their social status.

The whole society shall respect teachers.

Article 5 The administrative department for education under the State Council shall be in charge of the work concerning teachers nationwide.

The relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for the work concerning teachers within their respective functions and powers.

Schools and other educational institutions shall, in accordance with state regulations, conduct the management of teachers independently.

Article 6 September 10 of each year is Teachers' Day.

## Chapter II Rights and Obligations

Article 7 Teachers shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) To conduct education and teaching activities, and to carry out education and teaching reforms and experiments;

(2) To engage in scientific research and academic exchanges, to join professional academic groups, and to fully express their opinions in academic activities;

(3) To guide students' learning and development, and to assess students' conduct and academic performance;

(4) To receive wages and remuneration on time, to enjoy the welfare benefits prescribed by the state, and to have paid vacations during winter and summer holidays;

(5) To put forward opinions and suggestions on the education and teaching, management, and the work of the administrative department for education of the school, and to participate in the democratic management of the school through the congress of faculty and staff or other forms;

(6) To participate in further studies or other forms of training.

Article 8 Teachers shall perform the following obligations:

(1) To abide by the Constitution, laws, and professional ethics, and to be a model for others;

(2) To implement the state's educational policies, to observe rules and regulations, to carry out the school's teaching plans, to fulfill the teacher's employment contract, and to complete the tasks of education and teaching;

(3) To provide students with education on the basic principles established by the Constitution, education on patriotism and national unity, legal education, as well as education on ideology and morality, culture, science and technology, and to organize and lead students in beneficial social activities;

(4) To care for and love all students, to respect their personality, and to promote their all-round development in morality, intelligence, and physique;

(5) To stop acts harmful to students or other acts infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of students, and to criticize and resist phenomena harmful to the healthy growth of students;

(6) To continuously improve their ideological and political awareness and their professional level in education and teaching.

Article 9 To ensure that teachers complete their education and teaching tasks, people's governments at various levels, administrative departments for education, relevant departments, schools, and other educational institutions shall perform the following duties:

(1) To provide education and teaching facilities and equipment that meet national safety standards;

(2) To provide necessary books, materials, and other education and teaching supplies;

(3) To encourage and assist teachers in their creative work in education, teaching, and scientific research;

(4) To support teachers in stopping acts harmful to students or other acts infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of students.

## Chapter III Qualifications and Appointment

Article 10 The state shall implement a system of teacher qualifications.

Any Chinese citizen who abides by the Constitution and laws, loves the education cause, has sound ideological and ethical character, possesses the academic qualifications prescribed by this Law or has passed the national teacher qualification examination, has the ability for education and teaching, and is determined to be qualified, may obtain teacher qualifications.

Article 11 The corresponding academic qualifications required for obtaining teacher qualifications are:

(1) To obtain the qualification for kindergarten teachers, one shall have graduated from a preschool normal school or have a higher academic qualification;

(2) To obtain the qualification for primary school teachers, one shall have graduated from a secondary normal school or have a higher academic qualification;

(3) To obtain the qualification for junior secondary school teachers and teachers of cultural and specialized courses in junior vocational schools, one shall have graduated from a junior college of higher normal education or other junior college or have a higher academic qualification;

(4) To obtain the qualification for senior secondary school teachers and teachers of cultural and specialized courses in specialized secondary schools, technical schools, and vocational senior high schools, one shall have graduated from a college of higher normal education or other college or have a higher academic qualification; the academic qualifications required for obtaining the qualification for teachers guiding practice in specialized secondary schools, technical schools, and vocational senior high schools shall be prescribed by the administrative department for education under the State Council;

(5) To obtain the qualification for teachers of institutions of higher education, one shall have the academic qualifications of a graduate student or a college graduate;

(6) To obtain the qualification for teachers of adult education, one shall have the academic qualifications of a graduate of an institution of higher education or a secondary school or higher, depending on the level and type of adult education.

Citizens who do not possess the academic qualifications for teacher qualifications prescribed by this Law must pass the national teacher qualification examination to apply for teacher qualifications. The system of national teacher qualification examination shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 12 For teachers who were already teaching in schools or other educational institutions before the implementation of this Law and do not possess the academic qualifications prescribed by this Law, the administrative department for education under the State Council shall prescribe transitional measures for teacher qualifications.

Article 13 The qualifications for primary and secondary school teachers shall be determined by the administrative departments for education of local people's governments at or above the county level. The qualifications for teachers of specialized secondary schools and technical schools shall be determined by the administrative departments for education of local people's governments at or above the county level in conjunction with the relevant competent departments. The qualifications for teachers of regular institutions of higher education shall be determined by the administrative department for education under the State Council or the administrative departments for education of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, or by institutions of higher education authorized by them.

When a citizen who possesses the academic qualifications prescribed by this Law or has passed the national teacher qualification examination requests the relevant department to determine his or her teacher qualifications, the relevant department shall make the determination in accordance with the conditions prescribed by this Law.

A person who has obtained teacher qualifications shall have a probationary period when teaching for the first time.

Article 14 A person who has been deprived of political rights or has been subjected to criminal punishment of fixed-term imprisonment or heavier for intentional crimes cannot obtain teacher qualifications; if teacher qualifications have already been obtained, they shall be forfeited.

Article 15 Graduates of normal schools at various levels shall engage in education and teaching work in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

The state encourages graduates of non-normal institutions of higher education to teach in primary and secondary schools or vocational schools.

Article 16 The state shall implement a system of teacher professional titles, and the specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 17 Schools and other educational institutions shall gradually implement a system of teacher appointment. The appointment of teachers shall follow the principle of equality between the two parties, and the school and the teacher shall sign an appointment contract, clearly stipulating the rights, obligations, and responsibilities of both parties.

The procedures and measures for implementing the teacher appointment system shall be prescribed by the administrative department for education under the State Council.

## Chapter IV Cultivation and Training

Article 18 People's governments at various levels and relevant departments shall run normal education well and take measures to encourage outstanding young people to study in normal schools at various levels. Teacher training schools at various levels shall undertake the task of training primary and secondary school teachers.

Non-normal schools shall undertake the task of cultivating and training primary and secondary school teachers.

Students of normal schools at various levels shall enjoy professional scholarships.

Article 19 The administrative departments for education of people's governments at various levels, the competent departments of schools, and the schools themselves shall formulate plans for teacher training and provide teachers with various forms of ideological, political, and professional training.

Article 20 State organs, enterprises, institutions, and other social organizations shall provide convenience and assistance for teachers' social surveys and social practices.

Article 21 People's governments at various levels shall take measures to cultivate and train teachers for minority nationality areas and remote and poverty-stricken areas.

## Chapter V Assessment

Article 22 Schools or other educational institutions shall assess teachers' political ideology, professional competence, work attitude, and work performance.

The administrative departments for education shall guide and supervise the assessment of teachers.

Article 23 The assessment shall be objective, fair, and accurate, and shall fully listen to the opinions of the teacher concerned, other teachers, and students.

Article 24 The results of teacher assessment shall be the basis for appointment, salary increase, and the implementation of rewards and punishments.

## Chapter VI Treatment

Article 25 The average salary level of teachers shall not be lower than or shall be higher than the average salary level of state civil servants, and shall be gradually increased. A normal system for promotion and salary increase shall be established, and the specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 26 Primary and secondary school teachers and vocational school teachers shall enjoy seniority allowances and other allowances, and the specific measures shall be formulated by the administrative department for education under the State Council jointly with the relevant departments.

Article 27 Local people's governments at various levels shall provide subsidies to teachers and graduates with secondary specialized school or higher academic qualifications who engage in education and teaching work in minority nationality areas and remote and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 28 Local people's governments at various levels and the relevant departments under the State Council shall give priority and preferential treatment to the construction, leasing, and sale of housing for urban teachers.

People's governments at the county and township levels shall provide convenience for solving the housing problems of rural primary and secondary school teachers.

Article 29 Teachers shall enjoy the same medical treatment as local state civil servants; regular physical examinations shall be conducted for teachers, and arrangements shall be made for teachers to take rest and recuperation in light of local conditions.

Medical institutions shall provide convenience for the medical treatment of local teachers.

Article 30 After retirement or resignation, teachers shall enjoy the retirement or resignation benefits prescribed by the state.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may appropriately increase the pension ratio for retired primary and secondary school teachers who have been engaged in education and teaching for a long time.

Article 31 People's governments at various levels shall take measures to improve the treatment of primary and secondary school teachers whose wages are paid by state subsidies and collectively, and gradually achieve equal pay for equal work with teachers whose wages are paid by the state. The specific measures shall be prescribed by local people's governments at various levels based on the actual conditions of their respective regions.

Article 32 The treatment of teachers in schools run by social forces shall be determined and guaranteed by the sponsors themselves.

## Chapter VII Rewards

Article 33 Teachers who achieve outstanding results in education and teaching, talent cultivation, scientific research, teaching reform, school construction, social services, and work-study programs shall be commended and rewarded by their schools.

The State Council and local people's governments at various levels and their relevant departments shall commend and reward teachers who have made outstanding contributions.

Teachers who have made significant contributions shall be awarded honorary titles in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

Article 34 The state supports and encourages social organizations or individuals to donate funds to legally established fund organizations for rewarding teachers, and to reward teachers.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 35 Anyone who insults or assaults a teacher shall, depending on the circumstances, be given administrative sanctions or administrative penalties; if damage is caused, compensation shall be ordered; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 36 Anyone who retaliates against a teacher who has, according to law, lodged a complaint, accusation, or report shall be ordered by the unit to which the teacher belongs or the superior authority to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions may be imposed depending on the specific circumstances.

If a state functionary retaliates against a teacher and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 37 A teacher who falls under any of the following circumstances shall be given administrative sanctions or be dismissed by the school, other educational institution, or the administrative department for education:

(1) Intentionally failing to complete education and teaching tasks, causing losses to education and teaching work;

(2) Physically punishing a student, and failing to correct after education;

(3) Having bad conduct, insulting a student, and causing a bad influence.

If a teacher falls under the circumstances listed in items (2) or (3) of the preceding paragraph, and the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 38 If a local people's government violates the provisions of this Law by defaulting on teachers' wages or infringing upon other legitimate rights and interests of teachers, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a time limit.

If anyone violates the state financial system and financial system, misappropriates state financial funds allocated for education, seriously hinders education and teaching work, defaults on teachers' wages, and damages the legitimate rights and interests of teachers, the superior authority shall order the return of the misappropriated funds within a time limit and impose administrative sanctions on the persons directly responsible; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 39 If a teacher believes that a school or other educational institution has infringed upon his or her legitimate rights and interests, or is dissatisfied with the handling by the school or other educational institution, the teacher may lodge a complaint with the administrative department for education, which shall make a decision within 30 days of receiving the complaint.

If a teacher believes that the relevant administrative department of the local people's government has infringed upon the rights he or she enjoys under this Law, the teacher may lodge a complaint with the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at the next higher level, and the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at the next higher level shall make a decision.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

Article 40 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Schools of various levels and types" refer to schools that implement preschool education, regular primary education, regular secondary education, vocational education, regular higher education, as well as special education and adult education.

(2) "Other educational institutions" refer to children's palaces, local teaching research offices, audio-visual education institutions, etc.

(3) "Primary and secondary school teachers" refer to teachers in kindergartens, special education institutions, regular primary and secondary schools, adult primary and secondary education institutions, vocational secondary schools, and other educational institutions.

Article 41 The provisions of this Law may be applied mutatis mutandis to education and teaching support staff in schools and other educational institutions, and to teachers and education and teaching support staff in other types of schools, depending on the actual circumstances.

The Central Military Commission shall, in accordance with this Law, formulate relevant provisions for teachers and education and teaching support staff in schools under the armed forces.

Article 42 The measures for the appointment of foreign teachers shall be prescribed by the administrative department for education under the State Council.

Article 43 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1994.
