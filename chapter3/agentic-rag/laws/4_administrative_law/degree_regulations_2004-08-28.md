# Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Academic Degrees

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Fifth National People's Congress on February 12, 1980

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Academic Degrees at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated to promote the growth of scientific professionals in China, enhance the academic level of various disciplines, and advance the development of education and science, so as to meet the needs of socialist modernization.

Article 2 Any citizen who supports the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist system and possesses a certain academic level may apply for the corresponding academic degree in accordance with these Regulations.

Article 3 Academic degrees are divided into three levels: bachelor's, master's, and doctoral.

Article 4 A bachelor's degree shall be conferred on graduates from undergraduate programs in institutions of higher education who have achieved excellent academic performance and attained the following academic standards:

(1) A solid grasp of the fundamental theories, specialized knowledge, and basic skills of the discipline;

(2) Preliminary ability to engage in scientific research or undertake specialized technical work.

Article 5 A master's degree shall be conferred on graduate students from institutions of higher education and scientific research institutes, or individuals with equivalent academic qualifications to graduate students, who have passed the master's degree course examinations and thesis defense, achieved satisfactory results, and attained the following academic standards:

(1) A solid grasp of the foundational theories and systematic specialized knowledge in the discipline;

(2) The ability to engage in scientific research or independently undertake specialized technical work.

Article 6 A doctoral degree shall be conferred on graduate students from institutions of higher education and scientific research institutes, or individuals with equivalent academic qualifications to graduate students, who have passed the doctoral degree course examinations and thesis defense, achieved satisfactory results, and attained the following academic standards:

(1) A solid and broad grasp of the foundational theories and systematic, in-depth specialized knowledge in the discipline;

(2) The ability to independently engage in scientific research work;

(3) Creative achievements in science or specialized technology.

Article 7 The State Council shall establish an Academic Degrees Committee, responsible for leading the national degree-conferring work. The Academic Degrees Committee shall have one chairperson, and several vice-chairpersons and members. The chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members shall be appointed and removed by the State Council.

Article 8 A bachelor's degree shall be conferred by institutions of higher education authorized by the State Council; a master's degree and a doctoral degree shall be conferred by institutions of higher education and scientific research institutes authorized by the State Council.

The list of institutions of higher education and scientific research institutes authorized to confer degrees (hereinafter referred to as degree-conferring units) and the disciplines for which they may confer degrees shall be proposed by the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council, approved by the State Council, and published.

Article 9 Degree-conferring units shall establish an Academic Degree Evaluation Committee and organize thesis defense committees for relevant disciplines.

The thesis defense committee must include relevant experts from outside the unit, and its members shall be selected and decided by the degree-conferring unit. The list of members of the Academic Degree Evaluation Committee shall be determined by the degree-conferring unit and reported to the relevant departments of the State Council and the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council for record.

Article 10 The thesis defense committee is responsible for reviewing master's and doctoral theses, organizing defenses, and making resolutions on whether to confer a master's or doctoral degree. Resolutions shall be adopted by a secret ballot vote with the approval of more than two-thirds of all members and reported to the Academic Degree Evaluation Committee.

The Academic Degree Evaluation Committee is responsible for reviewing and approving the list of bachelor's degree recipients; and for deciding whether to approve the resolutions submitted by the thesis defense committee for conferring a master's or doctoral degree. Decisions shall be made by a secret ballot vote with the approval of more than half of all members. The list of those approved for master's or doctoral degrees shall be reported to the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council for record.

Article 11 After the Academic Degree Evaluation Committee makes a resolution to confer a degree, the degree-conferring unit shall issue the corresponding degree certificate to the degree recipient.

Article 12 Graduate students who are about to graduate from non-degree-conferring units may, upon recommendation by their original unit, apply for a degree from a nearby degree-conferring unit. If the application is reviewed and approved by the degree-conferring unit, and the candidate passes the thesis defense and meets the academic standards stipulated in these Regulations, the corresponding degree shall be conferred.

Article 13 Individuals who have made significant works, inventions, discoveries, or developments in science or specialized technology may, upon recommendation by relevant experts and approval by the degree-conferring unit, be exempted from examinations and directly participate in the doctoral thesis defense. Those who pass the thesis defense shall be awarded a doctoral degree.

Article 14 An honorary doctoral degree may be conferred on outstanding scholars or renowned social activists, both domestic and international, upon nomination by a degree-conferring unit and approval by the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council.

Article 15 Foreign students studying in China and foreign scholars engaged in research work may apply for a degree from a degree-conferring unit. Those who meet the academic standards stipulated in these Regulations shall be conferred the corresponding degree.

Article 16 If non-degree-conferring units or academic organizations hold different opinions regarding resolutions or decisions on degree conferral, they may raise objections to the degree-conferring unit or the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council. The degree-conferring unit and the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council shall study and address the objections raised.

Article 17 If a degree-conferring unit discovers fraud, falsification, or other serious violations of these Regulations in a degree that has already been conferred, it may revoke the degree after a review by the Academic Degree Evaluation Committee.

Article 18 If the State Council confirms that a unit that has been approved to confer degrees can no longer guarantee the academic level of the degrees it confers, it may suspend or revoke the unit's qualification to confer degrees.

Article 19 The implementing measures for these Regulations shall be formulated by the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 20 These Regulations shall come into effect on January 1, 1981.
