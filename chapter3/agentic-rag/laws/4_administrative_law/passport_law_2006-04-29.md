# Passport Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2006

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating the application, issuance and administration of passports of the People's Republic of China, safeguarding the rights and interests of citizens of the People's Republic of China to enter and exit the country, and promoting international exchanges.

Article 2 The passport of the People's Republic of China is a document for citizens of the People's Republic of China to enter and exit the country and to certify their nationality and identity abroad.

No organization or individual may forge, alter, transfer, intentionally damage, or illegally detain a passport.

Article 3 Passports are divided into ordinary passports, diplomatic passports and service passports.

Passports shall be introduced by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to foreign governments through diplomatic channels.

Article 4 Ordinary passports shall be issued by the exit and entry administration authorities of the public security department under the State Council, or by the exit and entry administration authorities of the public security organs of the local people's governments at or above the county level authorized by the public security department under the State Council, as well as by the embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China abroad and other overseas institutions authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Diplomatic passports shall be issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Service passports shall be issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China abroad, or other overseas institutions authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, as well as by the foreign affairs departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 5 A citizen who intends to go abroad for non-official reasons such as settling down, visiting relatives, studying, working, traveling, or engaging in business activities shall apply for an ordinary passport in person to the exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of his/her registered residence.

Article 6 To apply for an ordinary passport, a citizen shall submit his/her resident identity card, household registration booklet, recent bareheaded photos, and relevant materials concerning the reason for the application. Where a state functionary applies for an ordinary passport for the reasons specified in Article 5 of this Law, he/she shall also submit relevant certification documents in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

The exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ shall issue an ordinary passport within fifteen days from the date of receiving the application materials; if it refuses to issue a passport because the application does not conform to the provisions, it shall give reasons in writing and inform the applicant of his/her right to apply for administrative reconsideration or institute administrative proceedings in accordance with the law.

In remote areas or areas with inconvenient transportation, or due to special circumstances, if a passport cannot be issued on schedule, the time for issuance may be extended to thirty days with the approval of the person in charge of the passport issuing authority.

Where a citizen requests expedited processing due to reasonable urgent reasons, the exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ shall handle it promptly.

Article 7 The registration items of an ordinary passport include: the name, sex, date of birth, and place of birth of the passport holder; the date of issuance, validity period, place of issuance, and issuing authority of the passport.

The validity period of an ordinary passport is: five years for passport holders under the age of sixteen, and ten years for those aged sixteen or above.

The specific measures for issuing ordinary passports shall be formulated by the public security department under the State Council.

Article 8 Diplomatic officials, consular officials, their accompanying spouses, minor children, and diplomatic couriers shall hold diplomatic passports.

Staff members dispatched by the Chinese government to work in embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China abroad, or in the United Nations, specialized agencies thereof, and other intergovernmental international organizations, as well as their accompanying spouses and minor children, shall hold service passports.

Where a citizen not covered by the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs goes abroad on official business, his/her work unit shall apply to the foreign affairs department on his/her behalf in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 2 and 3 of Article 4 of this Law, and the foreign affairs department shall issue a diplomatic passport or a service passport as needed.

Article 9 The registration items of diplomatic passports and service passports include: the name, sex, date of birth, and place of birth of the passport holder; the date of issuance, validity period, and issuing authority of the passport.

The scope of issuance, measures for issuance, validity period of diplomatic passports and service passports, as well as the specific categories of service passports, shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 10 Where the registration items in the passport held by a passport holder change, he/she shall apply to the passport issuing authority for an endorsement of the change by presenting relevant certification materials.

Article 11 Under any of the following circumstances, a passport holder may apply for a renewal or reissuance of the passport in accordance with the provisions:

(1) The validity period of the passport is about to expire;

(2) The visa pages of the passport are about to be used up;

(3) The passport is damaged and cannot be used;

(4) The passport is lost or stolen;

(5) Other circumstances where there is a legitimate reason to apply for a renewal or reissuance of the passport.

Where a passport holder applies for a renewal or reissuance of an ordinary passport, if he/she is within the country, he/she shall submit the application in person to the exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of his/her registered residence; if he/she is abroad, he/she shall submit the application in person to the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China abroad or other overseas institution authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Where a Chinese citizen residing abroad applies for a renewal or reissuance of an ordinary passport after returning to China, he/she shall submit the application in person to the exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of his/her temporary residence.

The renewal or reissuance of diplomatic passports and service passports shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 12 Passports shall have both visual reading and machine-readable functions.

The anti-counterfeiting performance of passports shall be formulated with reference to international technical standards.

The passport issuing authorities and their staff members shall keep confidential the personal information of citizens learned during the production and issuance of passports.

Article 13 The passport issuing authority shall not issue a passport to an applicant under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The applicant does not have the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) The applicant cannot prove his/her identity;

(3) The applicant practices fraud during the application process;

(4) The applicant is serving a sentence imposed by a court;

(5) The people's court has notified that there is an unresolved civil case and the applicant cannot leave the country;

(6) The applicant is a criminal defendant or a criminal suspect;

(7) The relevant competent department under the State Council believes that the applicant's departure from the country will cause harm to national security or cause significant losses to national interests.

Article 14 The passport issuing authority shall not issue a passport to an applicant for a period ranging from six months to three years from the date of completion of the execution of the criminal penalty or the date of repatriation, under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The applicant has been subject to criminal punishment for obstructing national (border) border management;

(2) The applicant has been repatriated for illegal exit, illegal residence, or illegal employment.

Article 15 The people's court, the people's procuratorate, the public security organs, the state security organs, and the administrative supervision organs may, as necessary for handling a case, detain the passport of a party to the case in accordance with the law.

If the party to the case refuses to hand over the passport, the state organs specified in the preceding paragraph may request the passport issuing authority to declare the passport of the party to the case invalid.

Article 16 Where a passport holder loses the nationality of the People's Republic of China, or the passport is lost or stolen, the passport issuing authority shall declare the passport invalid.

Passports that are forged, altered, obtained by fraud, or declared invalid by the issuing authority are void.

Article 17 Anyone who obtains a passport by fraud shall have the passport confiscated or declared invalid by the passport issuing authority; shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan by the public security organ; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 18 Anyone who provides forged or altered passports to others, or sells passports, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act is not serious enough for criminal punishment, the public security organ shall confiscate the illegal gains, impose detention for not less than 10 days but not more than 15 days, and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan; the illegal passports and the equipment used for printing them shall be confiscated by the public security organ.

Article 19 Anyone who uses a forged or altered passport, or fraudulently uses another person's passport to enter or exit the country (border), shall be punished by the public security organ in accordance with the provisions of the laws on exit and entry administration; the illegal passport shall be confiscated by the public security organ.

Article 20 Where a staff member of a passport issuing authority commits any of the following acts in the process of handling passports, he/she shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Refusing to accept an application that should be accepted;

(2) Failing to issue a passport within the statutory time limit without justifiable reasons;

(3) Charging fees exceeding the standards prescribed by the State;

(4) Soliciting or accepting bribes from applicants;

(5) Disclosing the personal information of citizens learned during the production or issuance of passports, infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of citizens;

(6) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 21 The format of ordinary passports shall be prescribed and supervised by the public security department under the State Council; the format of diplomatic passports and service passports shall be prescribed and supervised by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 22 The passport issuing authority may collect the cost of the passport and the fee for endorsements. The collected costs and fees shall be turned over to the State Treasury.

The standards for the cost of passports and fees for endorsements shall be prescribed and published by the price administration department under the State Council jointly with the finance department under the State Council.

Article 23 Where a citizen who is abroad for a short period experiences the loss, theft, or damage of his/her passport rendering it unusable, he/she shall apply to the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China abroad or other overseas institution authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for a Travel Permit of the People's Republic of China.

Article 24 Where a citizen engages in border trade, border tourism services, or participates in border tourism, he/she may apply to the exit and entry administration authority of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level authorized by the public security department under the State Council for an Exit-Entry Permit of the People's Republic of China.

Article 25 Where a citizen enters or exits the country as a seafarer or works on board a foreign vessel, he/she shall apply to the maritime administration authority authorized by the Ministry of Communications for a Seafarer's Passport of the People's Republic of China.

Article 26 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2007. Passports issued before the entry into force of this Law shall remain valid within their validity period.
