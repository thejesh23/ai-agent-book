# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Activities of Overseas Non-Governmental Organizations within the Territory of China

Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Activities of Overseas Non-Governmental Organizations

within the Territory of China

Adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 28, 2016

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate and guide the activities of overseas non-governmental organizations within the territory of China, protect their lawful rights and interests, and promote exchanges and cooperation.

Article 2 This Law applies to the activities carried out by overseas non-governmental organizations within the territory of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "overseas non-governmental organizations" (hereinafter referred to as "overseas NGOs") refer to non-profit, non-governmental social organizations, such as foundations, social groups, and think tanks, which are lawfully established outside the territory of China.

Article 3 Overseas NGOs may, in accordance with this Law, carry out activities conducive to the development of public welfare in fields such as economy, education, science and technology, culture, health, sports, environmental protection, and in areas such as poverty alleviation and disaster relief.

Article 4 Overseas NGOs carrying out activities within the territory of China in accordance with the law shall be protected by law.

Article 5 Overseas NGOs shall abide by Chinese laws when carrying out activities within the territory of China, and shall not jeopardize China's national unity, security, or ethnic solidarity, nor harm China's national interests, public interests, or the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Overseas NGOs shall not engage in or fund for-profit activities or political activities within the territory of China, nor shall they illegally engage in or fund religious activities.

Article 6 The public security department of the State Council and the public security organs of provincial-level people's governments shall be the registration and management authorities for overseas NGOs carrying out activities within the territory of China.

The relevant departments and units of the State Council and the relevant departments and units of provincial-level people's governments shall be the corresponding professional supervisory units for overseas NGOs carrying out activities within the territory of China.

Article 7 The public security organs and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions and duties, supervise and manage the activities of overseas NGOs within the territory of China in accordance with the law and provide services.

The State shall establish a coordination mechanism for the supervision and management of overseas NGOs, responsible for studying, coordinating, and resolving major issues concerning the supervision, management, and service facilitation of activities carried out by overseas NGOs within the territory of China.

Article 8 The State shall commend overseas NGOs that have made outstanding contributions to the development of China's public welfare undertakings.

## Chapter II Registration and Filing

Article 9 Overseas NGOs intending to carry out activities within the territory of China shall, in accordance with the law, register and establish representative offices; if they need to carry out temporary activities without registering and establishing a representative office, they shall file in accordance with the law.

Overseas NGOs that have not registered and established a representative office, or whose temporary activities have not been filed, shall not carry out or covertly carry out activities within the territory of China, nor shall they entrust, fund, or covertly entrust or fund any unit or individual within the territory of China to carry out activities within the territory of China.

Article 10 Overseas NGOs that meet the following conditions may apply to register and establish a representative office within the territory of China, based on their business scope, area of activities, and the needs for carrying out activities:

(1) Lawfully established outside the territory of China;
(2) Capable of independently bearing civil liability;
(3) The purposes and business scope stipulated in their articles of association are conducive to the development of public welfare;
(4) Have existed outside the territory of China for more than two years and have substantively carried out activities;
(5) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 11 An overseas NGO applying to register and establish a representative office shall obtain the consent of the professional supervisory unit.

The list of professional supervisory units shall be published by the public security department of the State Council and the public security organs of provincial-level people's governments in conjunction with relevant departments.

Article 12 An overseas NGO shall, within thirty days from the date of obtaining the consent of the professional supervisory unit, apply to the registration and management authority for the registration of the establishment of a representative office. To apply for the registration of the establishment of a representative office, the following documents and materials shall be submitted to the registration and management authority:

(1) A written application;
(2) Certifying documents and materials proving compliance with the conditions stipulated in Article 10 of this Law;
(3) Identity certificate, resume, and certificate or declaration of no criminal record of the proposed chief representative of the representative office;
(4) Certifying materials for the proposed domicile of the representative office;
(5) Certifying materials for the source of funds;
(6) The consent document from the professional supervisory unit;
(7) Other documents and materials stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

The registration and management authority may organize experts to conduct an evaluation when reviewing an application for the establishment of a representative office by an overseas NGO, if necessary.

The registration and management authority shall make a decision on whether to grant registration or deny registration within sixty days from the date of accepting the application.

Article 13 For an overseas NGO representative office that is granted registration, the registration and management authority shall issue a registration certificate and make a public announcement. The registration items shall include:

(1) Name;
(2) Domicile;
(3) Business scope;
(4) Area of activities;
(5) Chief representative;
(6) Professional supervisory unit.

An overseas NGO representative office shall, based on its registration certificate, handle tax registration in accordance with the law, engrave seals, open bank accounts in banks within the territory of China, and file copies of the tax registration certificate, seal impressions, and bank account information with the registration and management authority for the record.

Article 14 If an overseas NGO representative office needs to change its registered items, it shall, within thirty days from the date of obtaining the consent of the professional supervisory unit, apply to the registration and management authority for change registration.

Article 15 Under any of the following circumstances, the registration of an overseas NGO representative office shall be cancelled by the registration and management authority, and a public announcement shall be made:

(1) The overseas NGO cancels the representative office;
(2) The overseas NGO terminates;
(3) The registration of the overseas NGO representative office is revoked or its registration certificate is revoked in accordance with the law;
(4) Termination due to other reasons.

After the cancellation of the registration of an overseas NGO representative office, the overseas NGO that established the representative office shall properly handle the aftermath. An overseas NGO representative office does not have legal personality; if relevant legal liabilities are involved, they shall be borne by the overseas NGO.

Article 16 If an overseas NGO has not established a representative office within the territory of China but needs to carry out temporary activities within the territory of China, it shall do so in cooperation with Chinese state organs, people's organizations, public institutions, or social organizations (hereinafter referred to as "Chinese partner units").

Article 17 For an overseas NGO to carry out temporary activities, the Chinese partner unit shall go through the approval procedures in accordance with state regulations and file with the registration and management authority at its location fifteen days before carrying out the temporary activities. The following documents and materials shall be submitted for filing:

(1) Certifying documents and materials proving the lawful establishment of the overseas NGO;
(2) A written agreement between the overseas NGO and the Chinese partner unit;
(3) Relevant materials such as the name, purpose, area, and duration of the temporary activities;
(4) Certifying materials for project funds, sources of funds, and the bank account of the Chinese partner unit;
(5) The approval document obtained by the Chinese partner unit;
(6) Other documents and materials stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

In emergency situations such as disaster relief and rescue, where temporary activities need to be carried out, the filing time shall not be subject to the restrictions of the preceding paragraph.

The duration of temporary activities shall not exceed one year; if an extension is truly necessary, a new filing shall be made.

If the registration and management authority considers that the filed temporary activities do not comply with the provisions of Article 5 of this Law, it shall promptly notify the Chinese partner unit to stop the temporary activities.

## Chapter III Activity Norms

Article 18 An overseas NGO representative office shall carry out activities using its registered name, within its registered business scope and area of activities.

Overseas NGOs shall not establish branch offices within the territory of China, unless otherwise provided by the State Council.

Article 19 An overseas NGO representative office shall, before December 31 each year, submit its activity plan for the following year, including project implementation and fund usage, to the professional supervisory unit. Within ten days after obtaining the consent of the professional supervisory unit, it shall file the plan with the registration and management authority. If the activity plan needs to be adjusted under special circumstances, it shall promptly file the adjustment with the registration and management authority.

Article 20 When carrying out activities within the territory of China, overseas NGOs shall not impose conditions on Chinese partner units or beneficiaries that violate Chinese laws or regulations.

Article 21 The funds for activities of overseas NGOs within the territory of China shall include:

(1) Funds from lawful sources outside the territory of China;
(2) Interest on bank deposits within the territory of China;
(3) Other funds lawfully obtained within the territory of China.

Overseas NGOs shall not obtain or use funds other than those specified in the preceding paragraph for their activities within the territory of China.

Overseas NGOs and their representative offices shall not conduct fundraising within the territory of China.

Article 22 An overseas NGO that has established a representative office shall manage the funds used within the territory of China through the bank account of the representative office that has been filed with the registration and management authority.

An overseas NGO carrying out temporary activities shall manage the funds used within the territory of China through the bank account of the Chinese partner unit, maintaining separate accounts and ensuring funds are used for designated purposes.

Without the bank accounts specified in the preceding two paragraphs, no overseas NGO, Chinese partner unit, or individual shall receive or disburse project activity funds within the territory of China in any other form.

Article 23 Overseas NGOs shall use funds in accordance with the business scope and area of activities registered for the representative office, or as agreed in the agreement with the Chinese partner unit.

Article 24 An overseas NGO representative office shall implement the unified accounting system of China. Its financial accounting reports shall be audited by an accounting firm within the territory of China.

Article 25 When carrying out activities within the territory of China, overseas NGOs shall handle foreign exchange receipts and payments in accordance with the relevant foreign exchange management regulations of China.

Article 26 An overseas NGO representative office shall handle tax registration, tax declaration, tax payment, and other matters in accordance with the law.

Article 27 When employing staff within the territory of China, an overseas NGO representative office shall comply with laws and administrative regulations and file the information of the employed staff with the professional supervisory unit and the registration and management authority for the record.

Article 28 Overseas NGO representative offices and overseas NGOs carrying out temporary activities shall not recruit members within the territory of China, unless otherwise provided by the State Council.

Article 29 An overseas NGO representative office shall have one chief representative and may have one to three representatives based on business needs.

Persons under any of the following circumstances shall not serve as chief representative or representative:

(1) Persons with no civil capacity or with limited civil capacity;
(2) Persons with a criminal record;
(3) Persons who served as chief representative or representative of a representative office whose registration was revoked or whose registration certificate was revoked in accordance with the law, within five years from the date of revocation;
(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 30 An overseas NGO carrying out temporary activities shall carry out activities using its filed name.

The overseas NGO and the Chinese partner unit shall, within thirty days after the conclusion of the temporary activities, submit a written report on the activities and fund usage to the registration and management authority.

Article 31 An overseas NGO representative office shall, before January 31 each year, submit its work report for the preceding year to the professional supervisory unit. After the professional supervisory unit issues its opinion, the representative office shall, before March 31, submit the report to the registration and management authority for annual inspection.

The annual work report shall include the audited financial accounting report, information on the activities carried out, and changes in personnel and organizational structure.

An overseas NGO representative office shall make its annual work report publicly available on the unified website of the registration and management authority.

Article 32 No unit or individual within the territory of China shall accept entrustment or funding from an overseas NGO that has not registered a representative office or whose temporary activities have not been filed, nor shall they act as an agent or covert agent for such an overseas NGO in carrying out activities within the territory of China.

## Chapter IV Facilitation Measures

Article 33 The State guarantees and supports overseas NGOs in carrying out activities within the territory of China in accordance with the law. The relevant departments of people's governments at all levels shall provide necessary facilitation and services for overseas NGOs to carry out activities within the territory of China in accordance with the law.

Article 34 The public security department of the State Council and the public security organs of provincial-level people's governments shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, formulate a catalogue of activity fields and projects for overseas NGOs, publish a list of professional supervisory units, and provide guidance for overseas NGOs to carry out activities.

Article 35 The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall provide policy consultation and activity guidance services to overseas NGOs in accordance with the law.

The registration and management authority shall, through a unified website, publish the procedures for overseas NGOs to apply for the establishment of representative offices and for filing temporary activities, for the reference of overseas NGOs.

Article 36 Overseas NGO representative offices shall enjoy tax incentives and other policies in accordance with the law.

Article 37 No fees shall be charged for the annual inspection of overseas NGO representative offices.

Article 38 Foreign personnel serving as the chief representative or representatives of an overseas NGO representative office may, based on the registration certificate and representative certification documents, handle employment and other work-related procedures in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supervision and Management

Article 39 Overseas NGOs carrying out activities within the territory of China shall accept the supervision and management of public security organs, relevant departments, and professional supervisory units.

Article 40 The professional supervisory unit shall be responsible for providing opinions on the establishment of representative offices by overseas NGOs, changes in registered items, and annual work reports; guiding and supervising overseas NGOs and their representative offices in carrying out activities in accordance with the law; and assisting public security organs and other departments in investigating and dealing with illegal activities of overseas NGOs and their representative offices.

Article 41 Public security organs shall be responsible for the registration and annual inspection of overseas NGO representative offices, the filing of temporary activities of overseas NGOs, and the investigation and punishment of illegal activities of overseas NGOs and their representative offices.

When performing supervision and management duties and discovering acts suspected of violating the provisions of this Law, public security organs may take the following measures in accordance with the law:

(1) Interviewing the chief representative and other responsible persons of the overseas NGO representative office;
(2) Entering the domicile and activity venues of the overseas NGO within the territory of China for on-site inspection;
(3) Inquiring of units and individuals related to the investigated incident, and requiring them to provide explanations on matters related to the investigated incident;
(4) Reviewing and copying documents and materials related to the investigated incident, and sealing up documents and materials that may be transferred, destroyed, concealed, or altered;
(5) Sealing up or seizing venues, facilities, or property suspected of being involved in illegal activities.

Article 42 Public security organs may inquire into the bank accounts of units and individuals related to the investigated incident; relevant financial institutions and financial regulatory authorities shall cooperate. For funds in bank accounts suspected of being involved in illegal activities, upon approval by the responsible person of the public security organ of a people's government at or above the city divided into districts, a people's court may be requested to freeze them in accordance with the law; for funds in bank accounts suspected of being involved in criminal activities, freezing measures shall be taken in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 43 Departments of state security, foreign affairs, finance, financial supervision and administration, customs, taxation, and foreign experts shall, within their respective functions and duties, supervise and manage overseas NGOs and their representative offices in accordance with the law.

Article 44 The anti-money laundering administrative authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and manage the compliance of overseas NGO representative offices, Chinese partner units, and units and individuals within the territory of China that receive funds from overseas NGOs with the legal provisions on anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing during the process of opening and using bank accounts.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 45 If an overseas NGO representative office, an overseas NGO carrying out temporary activities, or a Chinese partner unit commits any of the following acts, the public security organ of a people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall issue a warning or order a suspension of activities for a specified period; confiscate illegal property and illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, the registration and management authority shall revoke the registration certificate or ban the temporary activities:

(1) Failing to handle change registration or file relevant matters in accordance with regulations;
(2) Carrying out activities not in accordance with the registered or filed name, business scope, or area of activities;
(3) Engaging in or funding for-profit activities, conducting fundraising, or recruiting members in violation of regulations;
(4) Obtaining or using funds in violation of regulations, or failing to open or use bank accounts or conduct accounting in accordance with regulations;
(5) Failing to submit annual activity plans, or failing to submit or make public annual work reports in accordance with regulations;
(6) Refusing to accept or failing to accept supervision and inspection in accordance with regulations.

If an overseas NGO representative office, an overseas NGO carrying out temporary activities, or a Chinese partner unit obtains a representative office registration certificate or files temporary activities through illegal means such as providing false materials, or forges, alters, buys, sells, rents, or lends registration certificates or seals, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 46 Under any of the following circumstances, the public security organ of a people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall ban the activity or order the cessation of the illegal act; confiscate illegal property and illegal gains; issue a warning to the directly responsible persons; if the circumstances are serious, impose detention for up to ten days:

(1) Carrying out activities in the name of an overseas NGO representative office or an overseas NGO without registration or filing;
(2) Carrying out activities in the name of an overseas NGO representative office after its registration has been revoked, its registration certificate has been revoked, or its registration has been cancelled;
(3) Carrying out activities within the territory of China after the expiration of the temporary activity period of an overseas NGO or after the temporary activities have been banned;
(4) An overseas NGO that has not registered a representative office or whose temporary activities have not been filed entrusts or funds units or individuals within the territory of China to carry out activities within the territory of China.

If a unit or individual within the territory of China, knowing that an overseas NGO has not registered a representative office or its temporary activities have not been filed, cooperates with it, or accepts its entrustment or funding, acts as an agent or covert agent for its activities, or handles the receipt and payment of project activity funds, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 47 If an overseas NGO or an overseas NGO representative office commits any of the following acts, the registration and management authority shall revoke the registration certificate or ban the temporary activities; if the act does not constitute a crime, the public security organ of a people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall impose detention for up to fifteen days on the directly responsible persons:

(1) Inciting resistance to the implementation of laws and regulations;
(2) Illegally obtaining state secrets;
(3) Spreading rumors, slandering, or publishing or disseminating other harmful information, endangering national security or harming national interests;
(4) Engaging in or funding political activities, or illegally engaging in or funding religious activities;(5) Other circumstances that endanger national security, harm national interests, or harm public interests.

If an overseas non-governmental organization (NGO) or its representative office commits criminal acts such as splitting the country, undermining national unity, or subverting state power, the registration management authority shall impose penalties in accordance with the preceding paragraph, and the directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 48: If an overseas NGO or its representative office violates the provisions of this Law and is deregistered, has its registration certificate revoked, or its temporary activities are banned, it shall not establish a representative office or conduct temporary activities within China for five years from the date of deregistration, revocation, or ban.

An overseas NGO that conducts activities without a registered representative office or without filing for temporary activities shall not establish a representative office or conduct temporary activities within China for five years from the date its activities are banned.

For an overseas NGO that falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 47 of this Law, the public security department of the State Council may include it on a list of undesirable entities, and it shall not establish a representative office or conduct temporary activities within China.

Article 49: If an overseas NGO's representative office is ordered to suspend activities within a specified period, the registration management authority shall seal its registration certificate, seal, and financial vouchers. If it is deregistered or has its registration certificate revoked, the registration management authority shall confiscate its registration certificate and seal and publicly declare them invalid.

Article 50: If an overseas individual violates the provisions of this Law, the relevant authorities may, in accordance with the law, order them to leave the country within a specified period, deport them, or expel them.

Article 51: If public security organs, relevant departments, and competent business units, as well as their staff, fail to perform their duties, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in the supervision and management of overseas NGOs, they shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 52: If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 53: Overseas schools, hospitals, research institutions or academic organizations in the fields of natural sciences and engineering technology that engage in exchanges and cooperation with domestic schools, hospitals, research institutions or academic organizations in the fields of natural sciences and engineering technology shall handle matters in accordance with relevant state regulations.

If the activities within China of the overseas schools, hospitals, institutions, and organizations specified in the preceding paragraph violate the provisions of Article 5 of this Law, legal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 54: This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2017.
