# Prison Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 29, 1994

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Prison Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure the correct execution of criminal punishments, to punish and reform offenders, and to prevent and reduce crime.

Article 2 A prison is a state organ for the execution of criminal punishments.

In accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Law and the Criminal Procedure Law, offenders sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve, life imprisonment, or fixed-term imprisonment shall have their sentences executed in prison.

Article 3 A prison shall implement the principles of combining punishment with reform and combining education with labor, aiming to reform offenders into law-abiding citizens.

Article 4 A prison shall supervise offenders according to law, organize them to engage in productive labor as required for their reform, and provide them with ideological, cultural, and technical education.

Article 5 The people's police of a prison shall, according to law, administer the prison, execute criminal punishments, and conduct education and reform of offenders. Their lawful acts shall be protected by law.

Article 6 The People's Procuratorate shall, according to law, supervise whether the activities of a prison in executing criminal punishments are lawful.

Article 7 The personal dignity of an offender shall not be insulted. Their personal safety, lawful property, and rights to defense, appeal, complaint, accusation, and reporting, as well as other rights not deprived or restricted according to law, shall not be infringed upon.

An offender must strictly abide by laws, regulations, and prison discipline, submit to administration, receive education, and participate in labor.

Article 8 The State shall guarantee the funds needed by prisons for reforming offenders. The funds for prison people's police, offender reform, offender living expenses, prison administration facilities, and other special funds shall be included in the state budget.

The State shall provide the necessary production facilities and production funds for offender labor.

Article 9 The land, mineral resources, and other natural resources lawfully used by a prison, as well as the property of a prison, shall be protected by law. No organization or individual may encroach upon or damage them.

Article 10 The judicial administration department under the State Council shall be in charge of prison work nationwide.

## Chapter II Prisons

Article 11 The establishment, abolition, and relocation of a prison shall be approved by the judicial administration department under the State Council.

Article 12 A prison shall have one warden and a number of deputy wardens, and shall establish necessary working organs and appoint other prison administrative personnel according to actual needs.

The administrative personnel of a prison are people's police.

Article 13 The people's police of a prison shall strictly abide by the Constitution and laws, be devoted to their duties, enforce the law impartially, strictly observe discipline, and be honest and upright.

Article 14 The people's police of a prison shall not commit any of the following acts:

(1) Soliciting, accepting, or misappropriating property from offenders or their relatives;
(2) Releasing an offender without authorization or neglecting their duty resulting in an offender's escape;
(3) Extorting confessions by torture or subjecting offenders to corporal punishment or maltreatment;
(4) Insulting the personal dignity of an offender;
(5) Beating an offender or instigating others to beat an offender;
(6) Using an offender's labor for personal gain;
(7) Privately delivering letters or articles to an offender in violation of regulations;
(8) Unlawfully delegating the authority to supervise an offender to another person;
(9) Other illegal acts.

If a people's police officer of a prison commits any act listed in the preceding paragraph which constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed.

## Chapter III Execution of Criminal Punishments

### Section 1 Committal to Prison

Article 15 For an offender sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve, life imprisonment, or fixed-term imprisonment, the People's Court shall serve the execution notice and the judgment on the public security organ detaining the offender. The public security organ shall, within one month from the date of receiving the execution notice and the judgment, deliver the offender to a prison for execution of the sentence.

If the remaining term of an offender's sentence is less than three months before being delivered for execution, the detention center shall execute the sentence on behalf of the prison.

Article 16 When an offender is delivered for execution of a sentence, the People's Court delivering the execution shall simultaneously serve on the prison a copy of the bill of prosecution from the People's Procuratorate, the judgment of the People's Court, the execution notice, and the case closure registration form. The prison shall not accept the offender if it has not received the above documents; if the documents are incomplete or contain errors, the People's Court that rendered the effective judgment shall promptly supplement them or make corrections; if the errors could lead to wrongful committal, the offender shall not be accepted.

Article 17 An offender delivered for execution of a sentence who meets the requirements of Article 16 of this Law shall be committed to prison. After committal, the prison shall conduct a physical examination of the offender. If, upon examination, the offender meets the conditions for temporary service of sentence outside prison, the prison may submit a written opinion to the prison administration authority at or above the provincial level for approval.

Article 18 When an offender is committed to prison, a strict inspection shall be conducted of their person and the articles they carry. Non-essential articles of daily use shall be kept by the prison on behalf of the offender or returned to their family with the offender's consent; contraband shall be confiscated.

Female offenders shall be inspected by female people's police.

Article 19 An offender shall not bring their children into prison to serve a sentence.

Article 20 After an offender is committed to prison, the prison shall notify the offender's family. The notice shall be sent within five days from the date of committal.

### Section 2 Handling of Appeals, Complaints, and Accusations Lodged by Offenders

Article 21 An offender who does not accept an effective judgment may lodge an appeal.

The People's Procuratorate or the People's Court shall promptly handle an appeal lodged by an offender.

Article 22 The prison shall promptly handle materials of complaints or accusations lodged by an offender, or forward them to the public security organ or the People's Procuratorate for handling. The public security organ or the People's Procuratorate shall notify the prison of the handling result.

Article 23 The prison shall promptly forward materials of appeals, complaints, or accusations lodged by an offender and shall not withhold them.

Article 24 If, during the execution of a sentence, the prison believes, based on an offender's appeal, that the judgment may be erroneous, it shall refer the matter to the People's Procuratorate or the People's Court for handling. The People's Procuratorate or the People's Court shall, within six months from the date of receiving the prison's written opinion, notify the prison of the handling result.

### Section 3 Service of Sentence Outside Prison

Article 25 An offender sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment and serving the sentence in prison who meets the conditions for service of sentence outside prison as provided by the Criminal Procedure Law may be allowed to temporarily serve the sentence outside prison.

Article 26 Temporary service of sentence outside prison shall be approved by the prison administration authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government upon a written opinion submitted by the prison. The approving authority shall notify the public security organ and the original trial People's Court of the decision approving temporary service of sentence outside prison, and send a copy to the People's Procuratorate.

If the People's Procuratorate considers the application of temporary service of sentence outside prison to an offender to be improper, it shall, within one month from the date of receiving the notice, submit a written opinion to the authority that approved the temporary service of sentence outside prison. Upon receiving the written opinion from the People's Procuratorate, the approving authority shall immediately re-examine the decision.

Article 27 For an offender serving a sentence temporarily outside prison, community correction shall be implemented according to law, and the community correction institution shall be responsible for its execution. The original prison shall promptly notify the community correction institution responsible for execution of the offender's reform situation in prison.

Article 28 If an offender serving a sentence temporarily outside prison meets the circumstances requiring committal to prison as provided by the Criminal Procedure Law, the community correction institution shall promptly notify the prison to take the offender back into custody; if the sentence term expires, the original prison shall handle the release procedures. If an offender dies during the period of temporary service of sentence outside prison, the community correction institution shall promptly notify the original prison.

### Section 4 Commutation of Sentence and Parole

Article 29 An offender sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment who demonstrates genuine repentance or meritorious performance during the service of their sentence may, based on the prison's assessment results, have their sentence commuted. Commutation of sentence shall be granted if any of the following major meritorious performances are demonstrated:

(1) Preventing another person from committing a major criminal activity;
(2) Reporting a major criminal activity inside or outside the prison, which is verified to be true;
(3) Making an invention or major technical innovation;
(4) Sacrificing oneself to save others in daily production or life;
(5) Demonstrating outstanding performance in resisting natural disasters or eliminating major accidents;
(6) Making other significant contributions to the State and society.

Article 30 A proposal for commutation of sentence shall be submitted by the prison to the People's Court. The People's Court shall examine and rule on the proposal within one month from the date of receiving the proposal; if the case is complex or circumstances are special, the period may be extended by one month. A copy of the ruling on commutation of sentence shall be sent to the People's Procuratorate.

Article 31 For an offender sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve who, during the period of reprieve, meets the legal conditions for commutation to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment, the prison where the offender is held shall, upon the expiration of the two-year period, promptly submit a proposal for commutation of sentence. After review by the prison administration authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the proposal shall be submitted to the Higher People's Court for a ruling.

Article 32 For an offender sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment who meets the legal conditions for parole, the prison shall, based on the assessment results, submit a proposal for parole to the People's Court. The People's Court shall examine and rule on the proposal within one month from the date of receiving the proposal; if the case is complex or circumstances are special, the period may be extended by one month. A copy of the ruling on parole shall be sent to the People's Procuratorate.

Article 33 If the People's Court rules to grant parole, the prison shall release the offender on parole on schedule and issue a parole certificate.

For an offender granted parole, community correction shall be implemented according to law, and the community correction institution shall be responsible for its execution. If an offender granted parole, during the parole probation period, commits acts violating laws, administrative regulations, or the regulatory provisions of the relevant department under the State Council on parole supervision, which do not yet constitute a new crime, the community correction institution shall submit a proposal to the People's Court to revoke the parole. The People's Court shall examine and rule on the proposal within one month from the date of receiving the proposal. If the People's Court rules to revoke the parole, the public security organ shall deliver the offender to the prison for committal.

Article 34 For an offender who does not meet the legal conditions for commutation of sentence or parole, commutation of sentence or parole shall not be granted on any grounds.

If the People's Procuratorate considers the People's Court's ruling on commutation of sentence or parole to be improper, it shall submit a written opinion of correction to the People's Court within the time limit provided by the Criminal Procedure Law. For a case in which the People's Procuratorate submits a written opinion of correction, the People's Court shall retry the case.

### Section 5 Release and Resettlement

Article 35 Upon the expiration of an offender's sentence, the prison shall release the offender on schedule and issue a release certificate.

Article 36 After an offender is released, the public security organ shall handle the household registration based on the release certificate.

Article 37 For a person released upon completion of a sentence, the local people's government shall assist in resettling their livelihood.

If a person released upon completion of a sentence has lost the ability to work and has no legal supporter, caregiver, or basic source of livelihood, the local people's government shall provide relief.

Article 38 A person released upon completion of a sentence shall, according to law, enjoy equal rights with other citizens.

## Chapter IV Prison Administration

### Section 1 Separate Custody and Management

Article 39 A prison shall implement separate custody and management for adult male offenders, female offenders, and juvenile offenders. The reform of juvenile and female offenders shall take into account their physiological and psychological characteristics.

The prison shall implement separate custody for offenders and adopt different management methods based on factors such as the type of crime, type of punishment, length of sentence, and reform performance.

Article 40 Female offenders shall be directly managed by female people's police.

### Section 2 Guarding

Article 41 The armed guarding of a prison shall be the responsibility of the People's Armed Police Force. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 42 If a prison discovers that an offender in custody has escaped, it shall immediately recapture the offender. If immediate recapture is not possible, the prison shall immediately notify the public security organ, which shall be responsible for the pursuit, with close cooperation from the prison.

Article 43 A prison shall set up guard facilities as needed for supervision. A guard isolation belt shall be established around the prison. No person may enter without permission.

Article 44 Organs, organizations, enterprises, institutions, and grassroots organizations around the prison area and work area shall assist the prison in maintaining security and guard work.

### Section 3 Use of Restraints and Weapons

Article 45 A prison may use restraints in any of the following circumstances:

(1) An offender has engaged in escape behavior;
(2) An offender has engaged in violent behavior;
(3) An offender is in the process of being escorted;
(4) An offender has other dangerous behaviors requiring preventive measures.

When the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph cease to exist, the use of restraints shall be stopped.

Article 46 People's police and personnel on duty of the People's Armed Police Force may use weapons in accordance with relevant state regulations in any of the following circumstances where the situation cannot be controlled without the use of weapons:

(1) Offenders engage in mass rioting or insurrection;
(2) Offenders escape or resist arrest;
(3) Offenders possess lethal weapons or other dangerous objects and are committing assault or destruction, endangering the life or property of others;
(4) Seizing offenders;
(5) Offenders seize weapons.

Personnel who use weapons shall report the situation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

### Section 4 Correspondence and Visits

Article 47 An offender may correspond with others during the service of their sentence, but incoming and outgoing letters shall be subject to inspection by the prison. The prison may withhold letters containing content detrimental to the offender's reform. Letters written by an offender to the superior organs of the prison or judicial organs shall not be subject to inspection.

Article 48 During the service of their sentence in prison, an offender may, in accordance with regulations, meet with their family members or guardians.

Article 49 The receipt of articles and money by an offender shall be subject to approval and inspection by the prison.

### Section 5 Living and Health

Article 50 The living standards of offenders shall be calculated based on the quantity of goods and prescribed by the State.

Article 51 The bedding and clothing of offenders shall be uniformly issued by the prison.

Article 52 Special living habits of offenders from ethnic minorities shall be taken into consideration.

Article 53 The cells where offenders live shall be sturdy, ventilated, well-lit, clean, and warm.

Article 54 A prison shall establish medical institutions and living and health facilities, and establish living and health systems for offenders. The medical care of offenders shall be included in the health and epidemic prevention plan of the area where the prison is located.

Article 55 If an offender dies during the service of their sentence, the prison shall immediately notify the offender's family, the People's Procuratorate, and the People's Court. If the offender dies of illness, the prison shall make a medical appraisal. If the People's Procuratorate has doubts about the prison's medical appraisal, it may make a new appraisal of the cause of death. If the offender's family has doubts, they may raise the matter with the People's Procuratorate. If the offender dies an unnatural death, the People's Procuratorate shall immediately conduct an examination and make an appraisal of the cause of death.

### Section 6 Rewards and Punishments

Article 56 A prison shall establish a daily assessment system for offenders. The assessment results shall serve as the basis for rewarding or punishing offenders.

Article 57 A prison may grant commendations, material rewards, or record merits for an offender in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Abiding by prison discipline, studying diligently, working actively, and demonstrating an attitude of admitting guilt and accepting punishment;
(2) Preventing illegal or criminal activities;
(3) Overfulfilling production tasks;
(4) Saving raw materials or taking good care of public property with achievements;
(5) Making technical innovations or imparting production techniques with certain results;
(6) Making certain contributions in preventing or eliminating disasters or accidents;
(7) Making other contributions to the State and society.

If an offender sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment has any of the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph, has served more than half of the original sentence, has consistently behaved well during the service of the sentence, and would not pose a danger to society if released from prison, the prison may, based on the circumstances, permit the offender to leave prison to visit family.

Article 58 A prison may impose a warning, record a demerit, or impose solitary confinement on an offender who commits any of the following acts disrupting prison order:

(1) Gathering a crowd to create a disturbance in the prison, disrupting normal order;
(2) Insulting or beating people's police;
(3) Bullying other offenders;
(4) Stealing, gambling, fighting, or picking quarrels and provoking trouble;
(5) Refusing to participate in labor despite having the ability to work, or engaging in passive work, and failing to correct the behavior after education;
(6) Avoiding labor by self-inflicted injury or self-mutilation;
(7) Intentionally violating operating procedures in production labor or intentionally damaging production tools;
(8) Other acts violating prison discipline.

The period of solitary confinement imposed on an offender in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall be seven to fifteen days.

If an offender commits any act listed in the first paragraph during the service of their sentence which constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Handling of Crimes Committed by Offenders During Service of Sentence

Article 59 If an offender intentionally commits a crime during the service of their sentence, a heavier punishment shall be imposed according to law.

Article 60 Cases of crimes committed by offenders within a prison shall be investigated by the prison. After the investigation is concluded, a written opinion on prosecution shall be prepared and transferred, together with the case files and evidence, to the People's Procuratorate.

## Chapter V Education and Reform of Offenders

Article 61 The education and reform of offenders shall implement the principles of individualized education, classified education, and persuasion through reasoning, adopting methods combining collective education with individual education and education within the prison with education from society.

Article 62 A prison shall conduct ideological education for offenders on legal systems, morality, current situation, policies, and future prospects.

Article 63 A prison shall, based on different circumstances, provide literacy education, primary education, and junior secondary education for offenders. Those who pass the examinations shall be issued corresponding academic certificates by the education department.

Article 64 A prison shall provide vocational and technical education for offenders based on the needs of prison production and the employment of offenders after release. Those who pass the examinations shall be issued corresponding technical grade certificates by the labor department.

Article 65 A prison shall encourage offenders to engage in self-study. Those who pass the examinations shall be issued corresponding certificates by the relevant departments.

Article 66 The cultural and vocational and technical education of offenders shall be included in the education plan of the local area. A prison shall establish necessary educational facilities such as classrooms and library reading rooms.

Article 67 A prison shall organize offenders to engage in appropriate sports activities and cultural and recreational activities.

Article 68 State organs, social organizations, military units, enterprises, institutions, people from all walks of life, and the families of offenders shall assist the prison in carrying out the education and reform of offenders.

Article 69 Offenders with the ability to work must participate in labor.

Article 70 A prison shall, based on the individual circumstances of offenders, reasonably organize labor to help them correct bad habits, develop work habits, learn production skills, and create conditions for employment after release.

Article 71 The working hours of offenders in a prison shall be implemented with reference to the relevant state provisions on working hours; under special circumstances such as seasonal production, working hours may be adjusted.

Offenders have the right to rest on statutory holidays and rest days.

Article 72 A prison shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, provide remuneration to offenders participating in labor and implement the relevant state provisions on labor protection.

Article 73 If an offender is injured, disabled, or dies during labor, the prison shall handle the matter with reference to the relevant state provisions on labor insurance.## Chapter 6: Education and Rehabilitation of Juvenile Offenders

Article 74: Juvenile offenders shall serve their sentences in a juvenile correctional facility.

Article 75: The execution of sentences for juvenile offenders shall primarily focus on education and rehabilitation. The labor of juvenile offenders shall be suited to the characteristics of minors, with the main emphasis on learning cultural knowledge and production skills.

Prisons shall cooperate with educational institutions of the state, society, and schools to provide the necessary conditions for juvenile offenders to receive compulsory education.

Article 76: When a juvenile offender reaches the age of eighteen, if the remaining term of the sentence does not exceed two years, they may still remain in the juvenile correctional facility to serve the remainder of the sentence.

Article 77: Matters concerning the management, education, and rehabilitation of juvenile offenders not covered in this chapter shall be governed by the relevant provisions of this Law.

## Chapter 7: Supplementary Provisions

Article 78: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
