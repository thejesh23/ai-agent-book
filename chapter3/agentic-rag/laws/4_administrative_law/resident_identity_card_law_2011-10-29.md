# Law of the People's Republic of China on Resident Identity Cards

Adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on June 28, 2003

Amended in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Resident Identity Cards" at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 29, 2011

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of certifying the identity of citizens residing within the territory of the People's Republic of China, protecting their lawful rights and interests, facilitating their social activities, and maintaining social order.

Article 2 Chinese citizens who have reached the age of sixteen and reside within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, apply for resident identity cards. Chinese citizens under the age of sixteen may, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, apply for resident identity cards.

Article 3 The items registered on a resident identity card include: name, gender, ethnicity, date of birth, address of permanent residence, citizen identity number, the holder's photograph, fingerprint information, the validity period of the card, and the issuing authority.

The citizen identity number is a unique and lifelong identity code for each citizen, compiled by the public security organs in accordance with the national standard for citizen identity numbers.

When applying for, renewing, or reissuing a resident identity card, citizens shall have their fingerprint information registered.

Article 4 Resident identity cards shall be filled in using standard Chinese characters and numerals that comply with national standards.

The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas may, based on local conditions, decide to also use the script of the ethnic group exercising regional autonomy or choose a commonly used local script for the content registered in Chinese characters on resident identity cards.

Article 5 The validity period of resident identity cards for citizens aged sixteen or above is ten years, twenty years, or long-term. For citizens aged sixteen to twenty-five, identity cards with a ten-year validity period shall be issued; for those aged twenty-six to forty-five, identity cards with a twenty-year validity period shall be issued; for those aged forty-six or above, identity cards with a long-term validity period shall be issued.

For citizens under the age of sixteen who voluntarily apply for resident identity cards, identity cards with a five-year validity period shall be issued.

Article 6 The format of resident identity cards shall be prescribed by the public security department of the State Council. Resident identity cards shall be uniformly produced and issued by the public security organs.

Resident identity cards have both visual and machine-readable functions. The content for visual and machine reading is limited to the items specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 3 of this Law.

Public security organs and their people's police shall keep confidential the personal information of citizens that they become aware of during the production, issuance, inspection, or seizure of resident identity cards.

## Chapter II Application and Issuance

Article 7 Citizens shall, within three months from the date they reach the age of sixteen, apply to the public security organ at their place of permanent residence for a resident identity card.

For citizens under the age of sixteen, their guardians shall apply for resident identity cards on their behalf.

Article 8 Resident identity cards shall be issued by the public security organ of the people's government at the county level in the citizen's place of permanent residence.

Article 9 When compatriots from Hong Kong, Macao, or Taiwan move to the mainland for settlement, when overseas Chinese return to settle in China, and when foreigners or stateless persons settle within the territory of the People's Republic of China and are approved to acquire or resume Chinese nationality, they shall, when registering their permanent residence, apply for resident identity cards in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 10 To apply for a resident identity card, an applicant shall fill in the "Resident Identity Card Application Registration Form" and submit their household registration booklet for verification.

Article 11 When the state decides to issue a new generation of resident identity cards, when the validity period of a resident identity card expires, when a citizen's name changes, or when the card is severely damaged and illegible, the citizen shall apply for a new card. If there is an error in the registered items on a resident identity card, the public security organ shall promptly correct it and issue a new card. When receiving a new card, the original card must be returned. If a resident identity card is lost, the citizen shall apply for a replacement.

If a resident identity card of a citizen under the age of sixteen falls under any of the circumstances in the preceding paragraph, an application may be made for renewal, reissuance, or replacement.

When a citizen handles the procedures for moving their permanent residence, the public security organ shall record the change in the citizen's address of permanent residence in the machine-readable items of the resident identity card and inform the citizen.

Article 12 When a citizen applies for, renews, or reissues a resident identity card, the public security organ shall handle the matter promptly in accordance with regulations. The public security organ shall issue the resident identity card within sixty days from the date the citizen submits the "Resident Identity Card Application Registration Form". In areas with inconvenient transportation, the processing time may be appropriately extended, but the extension shall not exceed thirty days.

If a citizen urgently needs to use a resident identity card while applying for, renewing, or reissuing one, they may apply for a temporary resident identity card, and the public security organ shall handle it promptly in accordance with regulations. The specific measures shall be prescribed by the public security department of the State Council.

## Chapter III Use and Inspection

Article 13 When engaging in relevant activities that require identity verification, citizens have the right to use their resident identity cards to prove their identity, and relevant units and their staff shall not refuse.

Relevant units and their staff shall keep confidential the personal information of citizens recorded on resident identity cards that they obtain during the performance of their duties or the provision of services.

Article 14 Under any of the following circumstances, citizens shall present their resident identity cards to prove their identity:

(1) Changes in permanent residence registration items;
(2) Military service registration;
(3) Marriage registration or adoption registration;
(4) Applying for exit procedures;
(5) Other circumstances where laws or administrative regulations require the use of a resident identity card to prove identity.

Citizens who have not obtained a resident identity card in accordance with the provisions of this Law may use other means of identification that comply with national regulations to prove their identity when engaging in the activities specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 15 When performing their duties in accordance with the law, people's police may, after presenting their law enforcement credentials, inspect resident identity cards under any of the following circumstances:

(1) When it is necessary to ascertain the identity of a person suspected of illegal or criminal activities;
(2) When implementing on-site control in accordance with the law and it is necessary to ascertain the identity of relevant persons;
(3) When a sudden incident seriously endangering public security occurs and it is necessary to ascertain the identity of relevant persons at the scene;
(4) When it is necessary to ascertain the identity of relevant persons at railway stations, long-distance bus stations, ports, docks, airports, or at places designated by the people's government of a city with districts during major events;
(5) Other circumstances where the law requires identity verification.

If a person refuses to have their resident identity card inspected by the people's police under any of the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph, measures shall be taken to handle the matter in accordance with relevant laws, depending on the specific circumstances.

No organization or individual may seize a resident identity card, except in cases where the public security organ implements the compulsory measure of residential surveillance in accordance with the "Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China".

## Chapter IV Legal Liability

Article 16 Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be given a warning by the public security organ and fined not more than 200 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated:

(1) Using false supporting materials to fraudulently obtain a resident identity card;(2) Renting, lending, or transferring a resident identity card;
(3) Illegally seizing another person's resident identity card.

Article 17 Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be fined by the public security organ not less than 200 yuan but not more than 1,000 yuan, or detained for not more than ten days; any illegal gains shall be confiscated:

(1) Using another person's resident identity card or using a fraudulently obtained resident identity card;
(2) Purchasing, selling, or using forged or altered resident identity cards.

Forged or altered resident identity cards and fraudulently obtained resident identity cards shall be confiscated by the public security organ.

Article 18 Anyone who forges or alters a resident identity card shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Anyone who commits any of the acts listed in Article 16 or Article 17 of this Law and engages in criminal activities shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 19 If staff members of state organs or units such as financial institutions, telecommunications, transportation, education, or medical institutions disclose personal information of citizens recorded on resident identity cards obtained during the performance of their duties or the provision of services, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose detention for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days and a fine of 5,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If a unit commits the act in the preceding paragraph and it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose detention for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If the acts in the preceding two paragraphs cause harm to others, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 20 If people's police commit any of the following acts, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to the severity of the circumstances; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Taking advantage of the convenience of producing, issuing, or inspecting resident identity cards to accept property or seek other benefits from others;
(2) Illegally changing a citizen identity number, or registering information other than the items specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 3 of this Law on a resident identity card, or intentionally registering false information;
(3) Failing to issue a resident identity card within the statutory period without justifiable reasons;
(4) Inspecting or seizing resident identity cards in violation of regulations, infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of citizens;
(5) Disclosing personal information of citizens learned during the production, issuance, inspection, or seizure of resident identity cards, infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of citizens.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 21 Citizens applying for, renewing, or reissuing resident identity cards shall pay the production cost of the card. The standard for the production cost of resident identity cards shall be determined by the competent price department of the State Council jointly with the finance department of the State Council.

For residents in cities who receive minimum subsistence allowances and residents in rural areas with special living difficulties, the production cost shall be exempted when they apply for or renew their resident identity cards for the first time. For other residents with genuine living difficulties, the production cost may be reduced when they apply for or renew their resident identity cards for the first time. The specific measures for exemption and reduction of the production cost shall be prescribed by the finance department of the State Council jointly with the competent price department of the State Council.

All production costs of resident identity cards collected by the public security organs shall be turned over to the state treasury.

Article 22 The specific measures for active-duty soldiers of the People's Liberation Army and members of the People's Armed Police to apply for and receive resident identity cards shall be separately prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 23 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2004, and the "Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Resident Identity Cards" shall be simultaneously abolished.

Resident identity cards obtained in accordance with the "Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Resident Identity Cards" shall cease to be valid as of January 1, 2013. Resident identity cards obtained before January 1, 2012, in accordance with this Law shall remain valid within their validity period.

After the state decides to issue a new generation of resident identity cards, the date on which the original resident identity cards cease to be valid shall be determined by the State Council.
