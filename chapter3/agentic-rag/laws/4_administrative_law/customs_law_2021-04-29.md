# Customs Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on January 22, 1987

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on July 8, 2000

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics" at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 28, 2013

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Fifth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Eleven Laws including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of safeguarding national sovereignty and interests, strengthening customs supervision and control, promoting foreign economic relations, trade, and scientific, technological and cultural exchanges, and ensuring the smooth progress of socialist modernization.

Article 2 The Customs of the People's Republic of China is the state organ responsible for the supervision and control of all arrivals in and departures from the Customs territory (hereinafter referred to as "entry into and exit from the territory"). The Customs shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations, supervise and control the means of transport, goods, luggage, postal items and other articles entering or leaving the territory (hereinafter referred to as "means of transport, goods and articles entering or leaving the territory"), collect Customs duties and other taxes and fees, prevent and suppress smuggling, compile Customs statistics, and handle other Customs operations.

Article 3 The State Council sets up the General Administration of Customs, which shall exercise unified administration of all Customs offices throughout the country.

The State establishes Customs offices at ports open to foreign countries and at places where concentrated Customs operations are conducted. The subordination of one Customs office to another shall not be restricted by administrative divisions.

Customs offices shall exercise their functions and powers independently in accordance with the law, and shall be responsible to the General Administration of Customs.

Article 4 The State establishes a specialized public security organ within the General Administration of Customs to investigate smuggling crimes, equipped with full-time anti-smuggling police, responsible for the investigation, detention, arrest, and preliminary inquiry of smuggling criminal cases under its jurisdiction.

The Customs public security organ investigating smuggling crimes shall perform its duties of investigation, detention, arrest, and preliminary inquiry in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

The Customs public security organ investigating smuggling crimes may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, establish branch offices. Each branch office, when handling smuggling criminal cases under its jurisdiction, shall, in accordance with the law, transfer the case to the People's Procuratorate with jurisdiction for prosecution.

Local public security organs at all levels shall cooperate with the Customs public security organ investigating smuggling crimes in lawfully performing its duties.

Article 5 The State implements a joint anti-smuggling system featuring joint crackdown, unified handling, and comprehensive management. The Customs is responsible for organizing, coordinating, and managing anti-smuggling work. Relevant regulations shall be formulated separately by the State Council.

Smuggling cases discovered by relevant administrative law enforcement departments shall, if administrative penalties are to be imposed, be transferred to the Customs for handling according to law; if suspected of a crime, they shall be transferred to the Customs public security organ investigating smuggling crimes or local public security organs for handling according to the division of case jurisdiction and legal procedures.

Article 6 The Customs may exercise the following powers:

(1) To inspect means of transport entering or leaving the territory, and examine goods and articles entering or leaving the territory; to detain those that violate this Law or other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

(2) To examine the papers of persons entering or leaving the territory; to interrogate persons suspected of violating this Law or other relevant laws and administrative regulations, and investigate their illegal acts.

(3) To consult and copy contracts, invoices, account books, vouchers, records, documents, business correspondence, audio and video recordings, and other materials relating to means of transport, goods, or articles entering or leaving the territory; to detain those that are connected with means of transport, goods, or articles entering or leaving the territory that violate this Law or other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

(4) Within the Customs surveillance zone and the designated coastal and border areas adjacent to Customs offices, to inspect means of transport suspected of being involved in smuggling and places suspected of concealing smuggled goods or articles, and to search the bodies of persons suspected of smuggling; to detain, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office director, means of transport, goods, or articles suspected of being involved in smuggling, and persons suspected of smuggling crimes; the detention period for a person suspected of smuggling crimes shall not exceed twenty-four hours, and may be extended to forty-eight hours under special circumstances.

Outside the Customs surveillance zone and the designated coastal and border areas adjacent to Customs offices, when investigating smuggling cases, the Customs may, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office director, inspect means of transport suspected of being involved in smuggling and places, other than citizens' residences, suspected of concealing smuggled goods or articles; the relevant parties shall be present; if the parties are not present, the inspection may be conducted directly in the presence of witnesses; means of transport, goods, or articles that are found to have evidence of being involved in smuggling may be detained.

The scope of the designated coastal and border areas adjacent to Customs offices shall be determined by the General Administration of Customs and the public security department of the State Council jointly with the relevant provincial people's governments.

(5) When investigating smuggling cases, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office director, to inquire about the deposits and remittances of the units or individuals suspected of being involved in the case at financial institutions or postal enterprises.

(6) If a means of transport or an individual entering or leaving the territory flees to evade Customs supervision, the Customs may pursue them continuously beyond the Customs surveillance zone and the designated coastal and border areas adjacent to Customs offices, and bring them back for handling.

(7) The Customs may be equipped with weapons for the performance of its duties. The rules for the carrying and use of weapons by Customs officers shall be formulated by the General Administration of Customs jointly with the public security department of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

(8) Other powers exercised by the Customs as provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 7 All localities and departments shall support the Customs in lawfully exercising its functions and powers, and shall not illegally interfere with the Customs' law enforcement activities.

Article 8 All means of transport, goods, and articles entering or leaving the territory must enter or leave through places where Customs offices are established. Under special circumstances, temporary entry or exit through places without Customs offices must be approved by the State Council or an organ authorized by the State Council, and Customs formalities shall be completed in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 9 Unless otherwise provided, import and export goods may be declared and duties paid by the consignee or consignor themselves, or they may entrust a Customs broker to handle the declaration and duty payment procedures.

The owner of articles entering or leaving the territory may handle the declaration and duty payment procedures themselves, or may entrust another person to do so.

Article 10 When a Customs broker accepts the entrustment of an import or export consignee or consignor to handle Customs declaration procedures in the name of the principal, it shall submit to the Customs a power of attorney signed by the principal and comply with all provisions of this Law applicable to the principal.

When a Customs broker accepts the entrustment of an import or export consignee or consignor to handle Customs declaration procedures in its own name, it shall bear the same legal liability as the consignee or consignor.

When a principal entrusts a Customs broker to handle Customs declaration procedures, it shall provide the Customs broker with true information concerning the entrusted Customs declaration matters; when a Customs broker accepts the entrustment of a principal to handle Customs declaration procedures, it shall reasonably verify the truthfulness of the information provided by the principal.

Article 11 Import and export consignees, consignors, and Customs brokers shall register with the Customs in accordance with the law when handling Customs declaration procedures.

Customs brokers and their personnel shall not illegally act as agents for others in Customs declaration.

Article 12 When the Customs lawfully performs its duties, the relevant units and individuals shall truthfully answer inquiries and provide cooperation, and no unit or individual may obstruct them.

When the Customs is violently resisted in the performance of its duties, the public security organs and the People's Armed Police forces performing relevant tasks shall provide assistance.

Article 13 The Customs shall establish a reporting system for acts of evading Customs supervision in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Any unit or individual has the right to report acts of evading Customs supervision in violation of the provisions of this Law.

The Customs shall give moral or material rewards to units or individuals that have rendered meritorious service in reporting or assisting in the detection of cases violating this Law.

The Customs shall keep the identity of the reporter confidential.

## Chapter II Means of Transport Entering or Leaving the Territory

Article 14 When a means of transport arrives at or departs from a place where a Customs office is established, the person in charge of the means of transport shall make a truthful declaration to the Customs, submit the relevant documents for inspection, and accept Customs supervision and inspection.

A means of transport entering or leaving the territory that stays at a place where a Customs office is established shall not depart without the consent of the Customs.

When a means of transport entering or leaving the territory moves from one place with a Customs office to another place with a Customs office, it shall comply with Customs supervision requirements and complete Customs formalities; it shall not change its course to go abroad before completing Customs formalities.

Article 15 After entering the territory but before declaring to the Customs, and after completing Customs formalities but before leaving the territory, a means of transport entering or leaving the territory shall proceed along the route prescribed by the competent transportation authority; if no such route is prescribed by the competent transportation authority, the route shall be designated by the Customs.

Article 16 The person in charge of a means of transport or the relevant transportation department shall notify the Customs in advance of the arrival and departure times of vessels, trains, or aircraft entering or leaving the territory, their places of stay, any changes of location during their stay, and the times for loading and unloading goods or articles.

Article 17 The loading and unloading of goods or articles entering or leaving the territory, and the embarkation and disembarkation of passengers, by a means of transport shall be subject to Customs supervision.

After the loading or unloading of goods or articles is completed, the person in charge of the means of transport shall submit to the Customs documents and records reflecting the actual loading or unloading situation.

Persons carrying articles who embark or disembark from a means of transport entering or leaving the territory shall make a truthful declaration to the Customs and submit to Customs inspection.

Article 18 When the Customs inspects a means of transport entering or leaving the territory, the person in charge of the means of transport shall be present and, as required by the Customs, open the holds, cabins, rooms, or doors; if smuggling is suspected, they shall also open the parts that may conceal smuggled goods or articles and move the goods or materials.

When necessary for its work, the Customs may dispatch officers to perform duties on board the means of transport, and the person in charge of the means of transport shall provide facilities.

Article 19 Foreign means of transport entering the territory and domestic means of transport leaving the territory shall not be transferred or used for other purposes unless they have completed Customs formalities and paid Customs duties.

Article 20 Vessels and aircraft entering or leaving the territory that also engage in domestic passenger or cargo transport shall comply with Customs supervision requirements.

Means of transport entering or leaving the territory that are converted to domestic transport shall complete Customs formalities.

Article 21 Coastal transport vessels, fishing vessels, and special vessels engaged in maritime operations shall not carry, exchange, purchase, or transfer goods or articles entering or leaving the territory without the consent of the Customs.

Article 22 When vessels or aircraft entering or leaving the territory are forced, due to force majeure, to berth, land, or jettison or discharge goods or articles at a place without a Customs office, the person in charge of the means of transport shall immediately report to the nearest Customs office.

## Chapter III Goods Entering or Leaving the Territory

Article 23 Import goods shall be subject to Customs supervision from the time of entry into the territory until the completion of Customs formalities; export goods shall be subject to Customs supervision from the time of declaration to the Customs until departure from the territory; goods in transit, transshipment, and through goods shall be subject to Customs supervision from the time of entry into the territory until departure from the territory.

Article 24 The consignee of import goods and the consignor of export goods shall make a truthful declaration to the Customs, and submit the relevant import or export license documents and relevant certificates. Goods whose import or export is restricted by the State shall not be released without the required import or export license documents; specific handling procedures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

The consignee of import goods shall declare to the Customs within fourteen days from the date of declaration of the arrival of the means of transport; the consignor of export goods, unless specially approved by the Customs, shall declare to the Customs after the goods arrive at the Customs surveillance zone and at least twenty-four hours before loading.

If the consignee of import goods fails to declare to the Customs within the time limit specified in the preceding paragraph, a fee for delayed declaration shall be imposed by the Customs.

Article 25 Customs declaration procedures for import and export goods shall be conducted using paper Customs declaration forms and electronic data Customs declaration forms.

Article 26 After the Customs accepts a declaration, the Customs declaration documents and their contents shall not be modified or withdrawn, except in circumstances conforming to Customs regulations.

Article 27 With the consent of the Customs, the consignee of import goods may inspect the goods or take samples before declaration. Goods requiring quarantine according to law shall have samples taken only after passing quarantine.

Article 28 Import and export goods shall be subject to Customs examination. When the Customs examines the goods, the consignee of import goods or the consignor of export goods shall be present and be responsible for moving the goods, opening and repacking the packages. When the Customs deems it necessary, it may conduct the examination directly, re-examine, or take samples.

Under special circumstances, the Customs may exempt import or export goods from examination; specific measures shall be formulated by the General Administration of Customs.

Article 29 Unless specially approved by the Customs, import and export goods shall be released by the Customs after the consignee or consignor has paid the duties or provided a guarantee, and the Customs shall affix its seal.

Article 30 If the consignee of import goods fails to declare to the Customs within three months from the date of declaration of the arrival of the means of transport, the Customs shall take possession of the import goods and dispose of them by auction according to law. After deducting expenses for transport, storage, and handling, and duties, any remaining proceeds shall be returned to the consignee upon application within one year from the date of the lawful auction; if the goods are subject to import restrictions by the State and the required license documents cannot be provided, the proceeds shall not be returned. If no application is made or the proceeds are not returned within the time limit, they shall be turned over to the State treasury.

For import goods that are definitely mis-discharged or over-discharged, upon verification by the Customs, the person in charge of the original means of transport or the consignee or consignor of the goods shall, within three months from the date of discharge from that means of transport, complete the procedures for return or import; if necessary, with the approval of the Customs, the period may be extended by three months. If the procedures are not completed within the time limit, the Customs shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If the goods listed in the preceding two paragraphs are not suitable for long-term storage, the Customs may dispose of them in advance based on the actual circumstances.

Import goods that the consignee or owner declares to be abandoned shall be taken possession of by the Customs and disposed of by auction according to law; after deducting expenses for transport, storage, and handling, the proceeds shall be turned over to the State treasury.

Article 31 Goods temporarily imported or temporarily exported in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, or regulations of the State Council or the General Administration of Customs shall be re-exported or re-imported within six months; if an extension of the period for re-export or re-import is necessary, the extension procedures shall be handled in accordance with the regulations of the General Administration of Customs.

Article 32 Engaging in the storage, processing, assembly, display, transport, or consignment of bonded goods, or operating duty-free shops, shall comply with Customs supervision requirements, be approved by the Customs, and complete registration procedures.

The transfer or movement of bonded goods, as well as their entry into or exit from bonded premises, shall be reported to the Customs for relevant procedures and be subject to Customs supervision and examination.

Article 33 Enterprises engaged in processing trade shall register with the Customs in accordance with the regulations of the General Administration of Customs. The unit consumption of materials for finished products of processing trade shall be verified by the Customs in accordance with relevant regulations.

Finished products of processing trade shall be re-exported within the prescribed time limit. For imported materials used therein that are permitted to be bonded under state regulations, the Customs shall be applied to for verification and cancellation procedures; for those on which duties have been paid in advance, the Customs shall be applied to for duty drawback procedures in accordance with the law.

If bonded imported materials or finished products of processing trade are sold domestically, the Customs shall levy duties on the bonded imported materials in accordance with the law; if the goods are subject to import restrictions by the State, the relevant import license documents shall also be submitted to the Customs.

Article 34 Special Customs surveillance zones, such as bonded areas, established within the territory of the People's Republic of China with the approval of the State Council, shall be supervised by the Customs in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 35 Import goods shall have their Customs formalities handled by the consignee at the Customs office at the place of import; export goods shall have their Customs formalities handled by the consignor at the Customs office at the place of export.

Upon application by the consignee or consignor and with the consent of the Customs, the consignee of import goods may handle Customs formalities at the Customs office at the place of destination designated for Customs clearance, and the consignor of export goods may handle Customs formalities at the Customs office at the place of departure for Customs clearance. The transit transport of the aforementioned goods shall comply with Customs supervision requirements; when necessary, the Customs may dispatch officers to escort the goods.

For goods entering or leaving the territory conveyed by cable, pipeline, or other special means, the operating unit shall regularly declare to and handle Customs formalities with the designated Customs office.

Article 36 For goods in transit, transshipment, or through goods, the person in charge of the means of transport shall make a truthful declaration to the Customs at the place of entry and shall transport them out of the territory within the prescribed time limit.

When the Customs deems it necessary, it may examine goods in transit, transshipment, or through goods.

Article 37 Goods under Customs supervision shall not be opened, taken, delivered, dispatched, exchanged, repacked, mortgaged, pledged, placed under lien, transferred, have their marks changed, be used for other purposes, or be subject to other disposal without the permission of the Customs.

No one may open or damage the seals affixed by the Customs without authorization.

If a People's Court judgment or ruling, or a decision by a relevant administrative law enforcement department, requires the disposal of goods under Customs supervision, the relevant parties shall be ordered to complete Customs formalities.

Article 38 Enterprises engaged in the storage business of goods under Customs supervision shall register with the Customs and, in accordance with Customs regulations, handle the procedures for receiving, storing, and delivering the goods.

Storing goods under Customs supervision outside the Customs surveillance zone shall be subject to the consent of the Customs and shall be subject to Customs supervision.

If the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs are violated, or if goods under Customs supervision are damaged or lost during the period of custody, the person obliged to take care of the goods under Customs supervision shall, except in cases of force majeure, bear the corresponding duty payment obligations and legal liability.

Article 39 The supervision measures for containers entering or leaving the territory, for salvaged goods entering or leaving the territory and sunken vessels, for goods imported or exported under border small trade, and for other goods entering or leaving the territory not specifically listed in this Law, shall be formulated separately by the General Administration of Customs, or by the General Administration of Customs jointly with the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 40 Where the State has prohibitive or restrictive provisions on goods or articles entering or leaving the territory, the Customs shall implement supervision in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, the provisions of the State Council, or provisions made by relevant departments of the State Council under the authorization of laws or administrative regulations. Specific supervision measures shall be formulated by the General Administration of Customs.

Article 41 The origin of import and export goods shall be determined in accordance with the state regulations on rules of origin.

Article 42 The commodity classification of import and export goods shall be determined in accordance with the state regulations on commodity classification.

The Customs may require the consignee or consignor of import or export goods to provide relevant information necessary for determining the commodity classification; when necessary, the Customs may arrange for testing or inspection, and the results of the testing or inspection recognized by the Customs shall serve as the basis for commodity classification.

Article 43 Upon the written application of a foreign trade operator, the Customs may make an administrative ruling in advance on the commodity classification, etc., of goods intended for import or export.

The same commodity classification administrative ruling shall apply to the import or export of identical goods.

The Customs shall publish the administrative rulings it makes on commodity classification, etc.**Article 44** The Customs shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, protect intellectual property rights related to goods entering or leaving the country.

Where it is necessary to declare the status of intellectual property rights to the Customs, the consignee or consignor of the import or export goods and their agent shall truthfully declare the relevant status of intellectual property rights to the Customs in accordance with state regulations, and submit the certifying documents for the legal use of the relevant intellectual property rights.

**Article 45** Within three years from the date of release of the import or export goods, or within the period of Customs supervision over bonded goods or goods imported with reduced or exempted duties and the subsequent three years, the Customs may conduct audits on the accounting books, accounting vouchers, Customs declaration documents, and other relevant materials and the relevant import or export goods of enterprises or units directly related to the import or export goods. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter IV Articles Entering or Leaving the Country

**Article 46** Baggage articles carried by individuals entering or leaving the country, and articles sent by post entering or leaving the country, shall be limited to personal use and reasonable quantities, and shall be subject to Customs supervision.

**Article 47** The owner of articles entering or leaving the country shall make a truthful declaration to the Customs and submit to Customs inspection.

No person may open or damage the seals affixed by the Customs without authorization.

**Article 48** The loading, unloading, transshipment, and transit of mail bags entering or leaving the country shall be subject to Customs supervision. The postal enterprise shall submit the mail waybill to the Customs.

The postal enterprise shall notify the Customs in advance of the time for opening and sealing international mail bags, and the Customs shall dispatch personnel to the site for supervision and inspection in a timely manner.

**Article 49** For articles sent by post entering or leaving the country, the relevant operating unit may only deliver or hand them over after they have been inspected and released by the Customs.

**Article 50** Articles that have been registered with the Customs and permitted to be temporarily brought into or taken out of the country duty-free shall be taken out or brought back into the country by the same person.

Persons in transit shall not leave their articles within the country without the approval of the Customs.

**Article 51** Articles abandoned by the owner of articles entering or leaving the country, articles for which Customs formalities have not been completed or which are unclaimed within the time limit prescribed by the Customs, and inbound postal articles that cannot be delivered nor returned, shall be disposed of by the Customs in accordance with the provisions of Article 30 of this Law.

**Article 52** The entry or exit of official or personal articles of foreign institutions or personnel enjoying diplomatic privileges and immunities shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter V Customs Duties

**Article 53** Customs duties shall be levied by the Customs according to law on goods permitted to be imported or exported and articles entering or leaving the country.

**Article 54** The consignee of import goods, the consignor of export goods, and the owner of articles entering or leaving the country are the taxpayers of Customs duties.

**Article 55** The dutiable value of import or export goods shall be examined and determined by the Customs based on the transaction value of the goods. Where the transaction value cannot be determined, the dutiable value shall be assessed by the Customs according to law.

The dutiable value of import goods includes the price of the goods, the cost of transport and related expenses, and insurance premiums incurred before the goods are unloaded at the place of entry within the territory of the People's Republic of China; the dutiable value of export goods includes the price of the goods, the cost of transport and related expenses, and insurance premiums incurred before the goods are loaded at the place of exit within the territory of the People's Republic of China, provided that any export Customs duties included therein shall be deducted.

The dutiable value of articles entering or leaving the country shall be determined by the Customs according to law.

**Article 56** Customs duties on the following import or export goods and articles entering or leaving the country shall be reduced or exempted:

(1) Advertising articles and samples of no commercial value;
(2) Materials donated gratuitously by foreign governments or international organizations;
(3) Goods damaged or lost before their release by the Customs;
(4) Articles within a prescribed amount;
(5) Other goods or articles for which the reduction or exemption of Customs duties is provided for by law;
(6) Goods or articles for which the reduction or exemption of Customs duties is provided for in international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

**Article 57** Customs duties on import or export goods destined for specific areas, specific enterprises, or specific purposes may be reduced or exempted. The scope and measures for specific duty reduction or exemption shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Goods imported with reduced or exempted duties in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph may only be used for the specific areas, specific enterprises, or specific purposes, and shall not be diverted to other uses without the approval of the Customs and the payment of the duties that were reduced or exempted.

**Article 58** Temporary reduction or exemption of Customs duties beyond the scope specified in Article 56 and the first paragraph of Article 57 of this Law shall be decided by the State Council.

**Article 59** For goods temporarily imported or temporarily exported, and for bonded goods imported under special approval, the consignee or consignor may be granted temporary exemption from Customs duties upon providing a deposit equivalent to the amount of duties or a guarantee to the Customs.

**Article 60** The taxpayer of import or export goods shall pay the duties within fifteen days from the date the Customs issues the duty payment notice; if payment is overdue, the Customs shall impose a surcharge for overdue payment. If the taxpayer or guarantor still fails to pay after more than three months, the Customs may, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office, take the following compulsory measures:

(1) Issue a written notice to the taxpayer's bank or other financial institution to deduct the duties from its deposits;
(2) Sell the dutiable goods according to law and use the proceeds to offset the duties;
(3) Detain and sell according to law goods or other property of the taxpayer equivalent to the amount of duties payable, and use the proceeds to offset the duties.

When the Customs takes compulsory measures, the surcharge for overdue payment that has not been paid by the taxpayer or guarantor listed in the preceding paragraph shall also be enforced simultaneously.

The taxpayer of articles entering or leaving the country shall pay the duties before the articles are released.

**Article 61** If the taxpayer of import or export goods shows clear signs of transferring or concealing its dutiable goods or other property within the prescribed duty payment period, the Customs may order the taxpayer to provide a guarantee; if the taxpayer cannot provide a guarantee for the payment of duties, the Customs may, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office, take the following measures to preserve the duties:

(1) Issue a written notice to the taxpayer's bank or other financial institution to suspend payment of a deposit of the taxpayer equivalent to the amount of duties payable;
(2) Detain goods or other property of the taxpayer equivalent to the amount of duties payable.

If the taxpayer pays the duties within the prescribed duty payment period, the Customs must immediately lift the measures to preserve the duties; if the duties are still not paid upon the expiry of the period, the Customs may, with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office, issue a written notice to the taxpayer's bank or other financial institution to deduct the duties from the suspended deposit, or sell the detained goods or other property according to law and use the proceeds to offset the duties.

If the measures to preserve the duties are improperly taken, or if the taxpayer has paid the duties within the prescribed period but the Customs fails to lift the measures immediately, thereby causing losses to the legitimate rights and interests of the taxpayer, the Customs shall bear liability for compensation according to law.

**Article 62** If, after the release of import or export goods or articles entering or leaving the country, the Customs discovers that the duties have been under-collected or omitted, it may collect the shortfall from the taxpayer within one year from the date of payment of the duties or the date of release of the goods or articles. If the under-collection or omission is caused by the taxpayer's violation of regulations, the Customs may recover the duties within three years.

**Article 63** If the Customs has over-collected duties, it shall refund the excess immediately upon discovery; the taxpayer may request a refund from the Customs within one year from the date of payment of the duties.

**Article 64** If a taxpayer disputes the amount of duties payable with the Customs, the taxpayer shall pay the duties and may apply for administrative reconsideration according to law; if still dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, the taxpayer may file a lawsuit with the People's Court according to law.

**Article 65** The provisions governing the collection and administration of Customs duties shall apply to the collection and administration of taxes collected by the Customs on behalf of other authorities at the time of importation.

## Chapter VI Customs Guarantees

**Article 66** Before the classification, valuation, or provision of valid Customs declaration documents for goods are determined, or before other Customs formalities are completed, if the consignee or consignor requests the release of the goods, the Customs shall release the goods upon the provision of a guarantee commensurate with the legal obligations it should perform according to law, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations for exemption from guarantee.

Where laws or administrative regulations otherwise provide for guarantees for the performance of Customs obligations, those provisions shall prevail.

If the state has restrictive provisions on the import or export of goods or articles, and the required license or permit cannot be provided, or in other circumstances where guarantees are not permitted by laws or administrative regulations, the Customs shall not handle the release against a guarantee.

**Article 67** A legal person, other organization, or citizen capable of providing a guarantee for Customs matters may act as a guarantor, except where the law prohibits a person from acting as a guarantor.

**Article 68** A guarantor may provide a guarantee using the following property or rights:

(1) Renminbi or freely convertible currencies;
(2) Drafts, promissory notes, checks, bonds, or certificates of deposit;
(3) Letters of guarantee from banks or non-bank financial institutions;
(4) Other property or rights recognized by the Customs according to law.

**Article 69** The guarantor shall bear the guarantee liability within the guarantee period. The performance of the guarantee liability by the guarantor shall not exempt the principal obligor from the obligation to complete the relevant Customs formalities.

**Article 70** Measures for the administration of Customs guarantees shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter VII Supervision over Law Enforcement

**Article 71** In performing their duties, the Customs must abide by the law, safeguard the interests of the state, strictly enforce the law in accordance with statutory functions, powers, and procedures, and accept supervision.

**Article 72** Customs officers must enforce the law impartially, maintain integrity and self-discipline, be devoted to their duties, and provide courteous service. They shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Covering up or conniving at smuggling, or colluding with others to engage in smuggling;
(2) Unlawfully restricting the personal freedom of others, unlawfully inspecting the body, residence, or premises of others, or unlawfully inspecting or detaining means of transport, goods, or articles entering or leaving the country;
(3) Using their position to seek personal gain for themselves or others;
(4) Soliciting or accepting bribes;
(5) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or Customs work secrets;
(6) Abusing their power, deliberately creating difficulties, or delaying supervision or inspection;
(7) Purchasing, privately dividing, or appropriating confiscated smuggled goods or articles;
(8) Participating in or covertly participating in for-profit business activities;
(9) Performing duties in violation of statutory procedures or exceeding their authority;
(10) Other illegal acts.

**Article 73** The Customs shall, based on the needs of performing their duties according to law, strengthen team building to ensure that Customs officers possess good political and professional qualities.

Customs professionals shall possess legal and relevant professional knowledge and meet the requirements for professional positions stipulated by the Customs.

The recruitment of Customs staff shall be conducted through open examinations, strict assessments, and merit-based selection in accordance with state regulations.

The Customs shall systematically provide political and ideological education, legal training, and Customs business training and assessment for its staff. Customs officers must regularly undergo training and assessment; those who fail the assessment shall not continue to perform their duties.

**Article 74** The General Administration of Customs shall implement a system of regular rotation for Customs directors.

Customs directors shall regularly report on their work to the Customs office at the next higher level, truthfully stating their performance of duties. The General Administration of Customs shall regularly assess the directors of directly subordinate Customs offices, and directly subordinate Customs offices shall regularly assess the directors of their subordinate Customs offices.

**Article 75** The administrative law enforcement activities of the Customs and its staff shall be subject to supervision by supervisory authorities according to law; the investigation activities of the anti-smuggling police shall be subject to supervision by the People's Procuratorates according to law.

**Article 76** Audit authorities shall conduct audit supervision over the financial revenues and expenditures of the Customs according to law, and have the authority to conduct special audit investigations into matters related to the state's financial revenues and expenditures handled by the Customs.

**Article 77** A higher-level Customs office shall supervise the law enforcement activities of its lower-level Customs offices according to law. If a higher-level Customs office considers a decision or action taken by a lower-level Customs office to be inappropriate, it may modify or revoke it according to law.

**Article 78** The Customs shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations, establish and improve internal supervision systems, and conduct supervision and inspection of their staff's compliance with laws, administrative regulations, and discipline.

**Article 79** The responsibilities and powers of the main positions within the Customs responsible for document review, inspection, release, audit, and investigation shall be clearly defined, and these positions shall be mutually separate and check each other.

**Article 80** Any unit or individual has the right to report or inform against the illegal or disciplinary acts of the Customs or its staff. The authority receiving the report or information shall, if it has the power to handle the matter, promptly investigate and deal with it according to law and its division of responsibilities. The authority receiving the report or information and the authority responsible for the investigation shall keep the identity of the informant or reporter confidential.

**Article 81** A Customs officer shall withdraw from handling a case if they fall under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party;
(2) Having a personal interest in the case themselves or through a close relative;
(3) Having another relationship with a party to the case that could affect the impartial handling of the case.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

**Article 82** Any of the following acts, which violate this Law and relevant laws or administrative regulations, evade Customs supervision, evade payable duties, or evade the state's prohibitive or restrictive controls on import or export, constitutes smuggling:

(1) Transporting, carrying, or sending by post goods or articles that are prohibited or restricted by the state from import or export, or goods or articles for which duties are payable according to law, into or out of the country;
(2) Without the permission of the Customs and without paying the payable duties or presenting the relevant licenses or permits, selling within the country bonded goods, goods imported with specific duty reduction or exemption, or other goods or articles under Customs supervision, or means of transport from abroad that have entered the country;
(3) Other acts that evade Customs supervision and constitute smuggling.

If an act listed in the preceding paragraph does not constitute a crime, the Customs shall confiscate the smuggled goods, articles, and illegal gains, and may also impose a fine; goods or articles specially or repeatedly used to conceal smuggling, and means of transport specially or repeatedly used for smuggling, shall be confiscated; specially made equipment for concealing smuggled goods or articles shall be ordered to be dismantled or confiscated.

If an act listed in the first paragraph constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 83** Any of the following acts shall be treated as smuggling and punished in accordance with the provisions of Article 82 of this Law:

(1) Directly purchasing smuggled imported goods or articles illegally from smugglers;
(2) Transporting, purchasing, or selling goods or articles prohibited or restricted by the state from import or export, or transporting, purchasing, or selling goods for which duties are payable according to law, on inland seas, territorial seas, boundary rivers, or boundary lakes, by ships and their personnel, without legal documentation.

**Article 84** Forging, altering, or buying or selling Customs documents; conspiring with smugglers to provide loans, funds, accounts, invoices, certificates, or Customs documents to smugglers; conspiring with smugglers to provide transportation, storage, mailing, or other conveniences to smugglers, if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if not constituting a crime, the Customs shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine.

**Article 85** If an individual carries or sends by post personal articles exceeding a reasonable quantity into or out of the country without declaring them to the Customs according to law, the Customs shall order the payment of the duties and may impose a fine.

**Article 86** Any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law may be subject to a fine, and any illegal gains shall be confiscated:

(1) Means of transport entering or leaving the country not through a place where the Customs is established;
(2) Failing to notify the Customs of the time of arrival, place of stay, or place of change of means of transport entering or leaving the country;
(3) Making false declarations to the Customs regarding import or export goods or articles, or goods in transit, transshipment, or through goods;
(4) Failing to submit to Customs inspection or examination of means of transport, goods, or articles entering or leaving the country as required;
(5) Means of transport entering or leaving the country loading or unloading goods or articles entering or leaving the country, or embarking or disembarking passengers entering or leaving the country, without the consent of the Customs;
(6) Means of transport entering or leaving the country staying at a place where the Customs is established departing without the consent of the Customs;
(7) Means of transport entering or leaving the country traveling from one place where the Customs is established to another place where the Customs is established, without completing Customs formalities and without Customs approval, changing course en route to a place outside the country or to a place within the country where the Customs is not established;
(8) Means of transport entering or leaving the country, not meeting Customs supervision requirements or without completing Customs formalities, engaging in or switching to domestic transport without authorization;
(9) Ships or aircraft entering or leaving the country, forced by force majeure to anchor or land at a place where the Customs is not established, or to jettison or discharge goods or articles within the country, failing to report to the nearest Customs office without justifiable reason;
(10) Without the permission of the Customs, opening, removing, delivering, dispatching, exchanging, modifying, mortgaging, pledging, placing under lien, transferring, changing marks, diverting to other uses, or otherwise disposing of goods under Customs supervision;
(11) Opening or damaging Customs seals without authorization;
(12) In the business of transporting, storing, or processing goods under Customs supervision, the relevant goods are lost or the relevant records are untrue, and no justifiable reason can be provided;
(13) Other acts violating Customs supervision regulations.

**Article 87** If an enterprise permitted by the Customs to engage in relevant business violates the relevant provisions of this Law, the Customs shall order it to make corrections, may give a warning, suspend its engagement in the relevant business, or even revoke its registration.

**Article 88** If a party engages in Customs declaration business without filing with the Customs, the Customs may impose a fine.

**Article 89** If a Customs declaration enterprise illegally acts as an agent for others in Customs declaration, the Customs shall order it to make corrections and impose a fine; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be prohibited from engaging in Customs declaration activities.

If a Customs declaration personnel illegally acts as an agent for others in Customs declaration, the Customs shall order them to make corrections and impose a fine.

**Article 90** If a consignee or consignor of import or export goods, or a Customs declaration enterprise, bribes a Customs officer, the Customs shall prohibit it from engaging in Customs declaration activities and impose a fine; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If a Customs declaration personnel bribes a Customs officer, a fine shall be imposed; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 91** If goods are imported or exported in violation of the provisions of this Law, infringing upon intellectual property rights protected by the laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China, the Customs shall confiscate the infringing goods according to law and impose a fine; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 92** Goods, articles, or means of transport detained by the Customs according to law shall not be disposed of before a judgment by the People's Court or a decision on punishment by the Customs is made. However, dangerous goods, or goods or articles that are fresh, perishable, easily deteriorable, or otherwise unsuitable for long-term storage, as well as goods, articles, or means of transport for which the owner applies for advance sale, may be sold in advance according to law with the approval of the director of the directly subordinate Customs office or its authorized subordinate Customs office, and the proceeds from the sale shall be kept by the Customs, and the owner shall be notified.

Smuggled goods, articles, illegal gains, means of transport used for smuggling, and specially made equipment confiscated by judgment of the People's Court or by decision of the Customs shall be uniformly disposed of by the Customs according to law, and the proceeds from such disposal and the fines imposed by the Customs shall all be turned over to the central treasury.

**Article 93** If a party fails to comply with a Customs punishment decision within the time limit and neither applies for reconsideration nor files a lawsuit with the People's Court, the Customs that made the punishment decision may offset the penalty against its deposit or sell the detained goods, articles, or means of transport according to law to offset the penalty, or may apply to the People's Court for compulsory enforcement.**Article 94** If Customs damages goods or articles under inspection while conducting inspections of inbound or outbound goods or articles, it shall compensate for the actual losses incurred.

**Article 95** If Customs unlawfully detains goods, articles, or means of transport, thereby causing damage to the lawful rights and interests of the parties concerned, it shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 96** Customs officers who commit any of the acts listed in Article 72 of this Law shall be subject to administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; any illegal gains shall be confiscated in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 97** If the financial revenues and expenditures of Customs violate the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, the audit authorities and relevant departments shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 98** If confidentiality is not maintained for accusers, informants, or reporters as required by this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to administrative sanctions by their unit or relevant units in accordance with the law.

**Article 99** If Customs officers fail to recuse themselves as required by this Law when investigating and handling illegal cases, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

**Article 100** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them:

A **directly subordinate Customs office** refers to a Customs office directly under the leadership of the General Administration of Customs, responsible for managing Customs operations within a specific region. A **subordinate Customs office** refers to a Customs office under the leadership of a directly subordinate Customs office, responsible for handling specific Customs operations.

**Inbound or outbound means of transport** refers to various vessels, vehicles, aircraft, and pack animals used to carry persons, goods, or articles into or out of the country.

**Goods in transit, transshipment, or through carriage** refers to goods that are shipped from outside the territory, pass through Chinese territory, and continue to be shipped to destinations outside the territory. Among them, goods transported by land through the territory are called **goods in transit**; goods that are transshipped at a place where Customs is established without being transported by land through the territory are called **goods in transshipment**; goods carried into the territory by vessel or aircraft and carried out of the territory by the same means of transport are called **goods in through carriage**.

**Goods under Customs control** refers to the import and export goods listed in Article 23 of this Law, goods in transit, transshipment, or through carriage, goods granted specific duty reductions or exemptions, as well as temporary import or export goods, bonded goods, and other inbound or outbound goods for which Customs formalities have not yet been completed.

**Bonded goods** refers to goods that are brought into the territory with Customs approval without going through the duty payment procedures, and are re-exported out of the territory after being stored, processed, or assembled within the territory.

**Customs surveillance zone** refers to ports, railway stations, airports, border crossings, international mail exchanges (exchange stations) where Customs is established, and other places where Customs control operations are conducted, as well as places for inbound or outbound entry and exit approved by the State Council even if Customs is not established there.

**Article 101** Measures for the control of means of transport, goods, and articles moving between special economic zones and other specific areas and other areas within the territory shall be separately prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 102** This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 1987. The "Provisional Customs Law of the People's Republic of China" promulgated by the Central People's Government on April 18, 1951, shall be repealed simultaneously.
