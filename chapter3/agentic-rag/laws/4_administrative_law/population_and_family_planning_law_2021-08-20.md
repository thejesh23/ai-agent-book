# Population and Family Planning Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 29, 2001

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Population and Family Planning Law of the People's Republic of China at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 27, 2015

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Population and Family Planning Law of the People's Republic of China at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 20, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution with a view to achieving the coordinated development of population with the economy, society, resources and the environment, promoting family planning, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of citizens, and enhancing family happiness, national prosperity and social progress.

Article 2 China is a country with a large population, and implementing family planning is a basic national policy.

The State shall adopt comprehensive measures to regulate the population size, improve the quality of the population, promote the achievement of an appropriate fertility level, optimize the population structure, and facilitate the long-term balanced development of the population.

The State shall carry out population and family planning work by relying on publicity and education, scientific and technological progress, comprehensive services, and the establishment and improvement of reward and social security systems.

Article 3 The work of population and family planning shall be combined with increasing women's opportunities for education and employment, improving women's health, and raising women's status.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels and their staff members shall strictly administer according to law and enforce the law in a civilized manner when carrying out family planning work, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of citizens.

The health authorities and their staff members who perform their duties according to law shall be protected by law.

Article 5 The State Council shall lead the population and family planning work nationwide.

Local people's governments at all levels shall lead the population and family planning work within their respective administrative areas.

Article 6 The health authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the national family planning work and the population work related to family planning.

The health authorities of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the family planning work and the population work related to family planning within their respective administrative areas.

Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the relevant population and family planning work within the scope of their respective duties.

Article 7 Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, family planning associations and other social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and citizens shall assist the people's governments in carrying out population and family planning work.

Article 8 The State shall give awards to organizations and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in population and family planning work.

## Chapter II Formulation and Implementation of Population Development Plans

Article 9 The State Council shall formulate population development plans and incorporate them into the national economic and social development plans.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the national population development plan and the population development plan of the people's government at the next higher level, and in light of the local actual conditions, formulate population development plans for their respective administrative areas and incorporate them into the national economic and social development plans.

Article 10 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the population development plans, formulate plans for the implementation of population and family planning work and organize their implementation.

The health authorities of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the day-to-day work of implementing the plans for the implementation of population and family planning work.

The people's governments of townships, nationality townships, and towns, and the sub-district offices of cities shall be responsible for the population and family planning work within their respective jurisdictions, and shall implement the plans for the implementation of population and family planning work.

Article 11 Plans for the implementation of population and family planning work shall stipulate measures for regulating the population size, improving the quality of the population, promoting the achievement of an appropriate fertility level, optimizing the population structure, strengthening maternal and infant health care and infant and toddler care services, and promoting family development.

Article 12 Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall do a good job in family planning work according to law.

State organs, the armed forces, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall do a good job in family planning work within their own units.

Article 13 The departments of health, education, science and technology, culture, civil affairs, press and publication, radio and television, etc. shall organize and carry out publicity and education on population and family planning.

The mass media have the obligation to carry out social welfare publicity on population and family planning.

Schools shall, in an appropriate manner consistent with the characteristics of the educatees, carry out planned physiological health education, puberty education, or sexual health education among students.

Article 14 The family planning work for the floating population shall be jointly managed by the people's governments of their registered residence places and current places of residence, with the current place of residence as the main responsible party.

Article 15 The State shall gradually increase the overall level of funding for population and family planning work in accordance with the state of national economic and social development. People's governments at all levels shall guarantee the necessary funds for population and family planning work.

People's governments at all levels shall give priority support to underdeveloped areas and ethnic minority areas in carrying out population and family planning work.

The State encourages social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and individuals to make donations for population and family planning work.

No unit or individual may intercept, withhold, or misappropriate funds for population and family planning work.

Article 16 The State encourages scientific research and international exchanges and cooperation in the field of population and family planning.

## Chapter III Fertility Regulation

Article 17 Citizens have the right to bear children, and also have the obligation to practice family planning according to law. Both husband and wife share the joint responsibility in practicing family planning.

Article 18 The State advocates marriage at an appropriate age and eugenics and optimal childbearing. A couple may have three children.

If the conditions stipulated by laws and regulations are met, a couple may request to have additional children. Specific measures shall be formulated by the people's congresses or their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Ethnic minorities shall also practice family planning. Specific measures shall be formulated by the people's congresses or their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

If the provisions of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the husband and wife are registered regarding having additional children are inconsistent, the principle of favoring the parties concerned shall apply.

Article 19 The State shall create conditions to ensure that citizens can make informed choices of safe, effective, and appropriate contraceptive measures. When performing contraceptive surgery, the safety of the recipient shall be guaranteed.

Article 20 Couples of childbearing age shall independently choose contraceptive measures for family planning to prevent and reduce unintended pregnancies.

Article 21 Couples of childbearing age who practice family planning shall enjoy free family planning technical services for basic items stipulated by the State.

The funds required for the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be included in the fiscal budget or guaranteed by social insurance in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 22 Discrimination against and maltreatment of women who give birth to female infants and infertile women are prohibited.

Discrimination against, maltreatment of, and abandonment of female infants are prohibited.

## Chapter IV Rewards and Social Security

Article 23 The State shall give rewards to couples who practice family planning in accordance with regulations.

Article 24 The State shall establish and improve social security systems such as basic old-age insurance, basic medical insurance, maternity insurance, and social welfare to promote family planning.

The State encourages insurance companies to develop insurance projects conducive to family planning.

Article 25 Couples who bear children in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations may receive rewards such as extended maternity leave or other welfare benefits.

The State supports localities with the conditions to establish parental leave.

Article 26 During pregnancy, childbirth, and breastfeeding, women shall enjoy special labor protection and may receive assistance and compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations. The State guarantees the legitimate employment rights and interests of women and provides employment services for women whose employment is affected by childbirth.

Citizens who undergo family planning surgery shall enjoy the leave stipulated by the State.

Article 27 The State shall adopt support measures in finance, taxation, insurance, education, housing, employment, etc., to reduce the burden on families in childbirth, child-rearing, and education.

Article 28 People's governments at or above the county level shall comprehensively adopt measures in planning, land, housing, finance, taxation, talent, etc., to promote the establishment of a universal childcare service system and improve the accessibility and fairness of services for families with infants and toddlers.

The State encourages and guides social forces to establish childcare institutions, and supports kindergartens, state organs, enterprises, institutions, and communities in providing childcare services.

The establishment and services of childcare institutions shall comply with relevant standards and norms for childcare services. Childcare institutions shall file with the health authorities of the people's governments at the county level.

Article 29 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in the construction and renovation of urban and rural communities, build activity venues and supporting service facilities for infants and toddlers that are commensurate with the size of the permanent population.

Public places and employers with a relatively large number of female employees shall be equipped with maternal and infant facilities to provide convenience for infant and toddler care and breastfeeding.

Article 30 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen support and guidance for family infant and toddler care, and enhance the scientific parenting ability of families.

Medical and health institutions shall, in accordance with regulations, provide services such as vaccination and disease prevention and control for families with infants and toddlers, and provide health guidance on diet, nutrition, growth, and development.

Article 31 During the period when the State advocated that a couple have only one child, couples who voluntarily had only one child throughout their lives were issued the "Certificate of Honor for Single-Child Parents" by the State.

Couples who have obtained the "Certificate of Honor for Single-Child Parents" shall enjoy rewards for single-child parents in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State and provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

If the measures for rewarding couples who have obtained the "Certificate of Honor for Single-Child Parents" stipulated by laws, regulations, or rules are to be implemented by their employing units, the relevant units shall implement them.

During the period when the State advocated that a couple have only one child, those who were entitled to rewards and assistance for the elderly in family planning families according to regulations shall continue to enjoy the relevant rewards and assistance, and shall be given necessary priority and care in terms of welfare for the elderly and elderly care services.

Article 32 Couples who have obtained the "Certificate of Honor for Single-Child Parents" and whose only child is accidentally disabled or dies shall receive assistance in accordance with regulations. People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve comprehensive assistance and security systems for the above-mentioned groups in terms of livelihood, elderly care, medical care, and spiritual comfort.

Article 33 Local people's governments at all levels shall provide financial, technical, and training support and preferences to rural families practicing family planning for economic development; and shall give priority and preferences to poor families practicing family planning in terms of poverty alleviation loans, work-for-relief, poverty alleviation projects, and social relief.

Article 34 For the reward and social security measures stipulated in this Chapter, the people's congresses and their standing committees or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, and in light of local actual conditions, formulate specific implementation measures.

## Chapter V Family Planning Services

Article 35 The State shall establish a premarital health care system and a prenatal and postnatal health care system to prevent or reduce birth defects and improve the health level of newborn infants.

Article 36 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to ensure that citizens enjoy family planning services and improve the reproductive health level of citizens.

Article 37 Medical and health institutions shall carry out publicity and education on eugenics and optimal childbearing knowledge for the childbearing-age population, provide periconceptional and prenatal and postnatal health care services for women of childbearing age, undertake consultation, guidance, and technical services for family planning, eugenics and optimal childbearing, and reproductive health care, and standardize the diagnosis and treatment of infertility.

Article 38 Family planning technical service personnel shall guide citizens practicing family planning to choose safe, effective, and appropriate contraceptive measures.

The State encourages the research, application, and promotion of new technologies and new drugs and devices for family planning.

Article 39 It is strictly prohibited to use ultrasound technology and other technical means for non-medical fetal sex identification; it is strictly prohibited to perform non-medical sex-selective termination of pregnancy.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 40 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the health authority to make corrections, given a warning, and have the illegal gains confiscated; if the illegal gains are 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than six times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke the practice certificate; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Illegally performing family planning surgery for others;

(2) Using ultrasound technology and other technical means to perform non-medical fetal sex identification or sex-selective termination of pregnancy for others.

Article 41 If a childcare institution violates the relevant standards and norms for childcare services, the health authority shall order it to make corrections and give a warning; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to stop childcare services, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If a childcare institution has acts of abusing infants or toddlers, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in infant and toddler care services; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 42 If family planning technical service personnel violate operating procedures or delay rescue, diagnosis, or treatment, causing serious consequences, they shall bear corresponding legal liability in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 43 If a staff member of a state organ, in the course of family planning work, commits any of the following acts, which constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated:

(1) Infringing upon the personal rights, property rights, and other legitimate rights and interests of citizens;

(2) Abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain;

(3) Extorting or accepting bribes;

(4) Intercepting, withholding, misappropriating, or embezzling family planning funds;

(5) Falsely reporting, concealing, forging, tampering with, or refusing to report population and family planning statistical data.

Article 44 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to fulfill the obligation to assist in family planning management shall be ordered by the relevant local people's government to make corrections and be given a notice of criticism; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given administrative sanctions according to law.

Article 45 Anyone who refuses or obstructs the health authority and its staff members from performing their duties according to law shall be given criticism and education by the health authority and be stopped; if it constitutes an act violating public security administration, public security administration penalties shall be imposed according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 46 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization believes that an administrative organ has infringed upon its legitimate rights and interests in the process of implementing family planning management, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit according to law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 47 The specific measures for the implementation of this Law by the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 48 This Law shall come into force as of September 1, 2002.
