# Law of the People's Republic of China on Scientific and Technological Progress

Adopted at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 2, 1993

First Revision at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2007

Second Revision at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution, with a view to comprehensively promoting scientific and technological progress, giving full play to the role of science and technology as the primary productive force, innovation as the primary driving force, and talent as the primary resource, promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements into practical productive forces, driving economic and social development through scientific and technological innovation support and leadership, and comprehensively building a modern socialist country.

Article 2 The comprehensive leadership of the Communist Party of China over scientific and technological undertakings shall be upheld.

The State adheres to the new development philosophy, upholds the core position of scientific and technological innovation in the overall situation of national modernization, takes scientific and technological self-reliance and self-strengthening as the strategic support for national development, implements the strategy of rejuvenating the country through science and education, the strategy of strengthening the nation through talent, and the innovation-driven development strategy, follows the path of independent innovation with Chinese characteristics, and builds a leading power in science and technology.

Article 3 Work on scientific and technological progress shall be oriented towards the world's scientific and technological frontiers, the main economic battlefield, the major needs of the country, and the life and health of the people, serving to promote economic and social development, safeguard national security, and advance human sustainable development.

The State encourages scientific and technological research and development, promotes the application of science and technology to transform and upgrade traditional industries, develop high-tech industries and social undertakings, support the achievement of the carbon peak and carbon neutrality goals, generate new drivers of development, and achieve high-quality development.

Article 4 The State shall improve an efficient, collaborative, and open national innovation system, coordinate scientific and technological innovation with institutional innovation, improve the new nationwide system under the conditions of a socialist market economy, give full play to the decisive role of the market in allocating innovation resources, better play the role of the government, optimize the allocation of scientific and technological resources, improve resource utilization efficiency, promote close cooperation among various innovation entities, orderly flow of innovation elements, and continuous optimization of the innovation ecosystem, enhance systemic capabilities and the ability to make key breakthroughs, and increase the overall effectiveness of the innovation system.

The State shall build and strengthen the national strategic scientific and technological force, with national laboratories, national scientific and technological research and development institutions, high-level research universities, and leading science and technology enterprises as important components, to play a strategic supporting and leading role and exert significant original innovation effectiveness in key fields and major directions, serving the major strategic needs of the country.

Article 5 The State shall coordinate development and security, enhance the capability of science and technology security governance, improve the institutional mechanisms for preventing and resolving science and technology security risks, strengthen the safety management of scientific and technological research, development, and application activities, support scientific and technological innovation in the field of national security, and enhance the capability and level of scientific and technological innovation to support national security.

Article 6 The State encourages the integration of scientific and technological research and development with higher education and industrial development, and encourages interdisciplinary integration and mutual promotion.

The State strengthens cross-regional, cross-industry, and cross-field scientific and technological cooperation, and supports the scientific and technological progress of old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and underdeveloped areas.

The State strengthens the coordinated development of military and civilian science and technology, promotes the exchange of military and civilian scientific and technological resources and technology development needs, and the two-way transfer of technology, and develops dual-use technologies.

Article 7 The State follows the principle of combining scientific and technological activities serving national goals with encouraging free exploration, makes forward-looking deployments of major basic research, cutting-edge technology research with significant industrial application prospects, and social welfare technology research, supports the sustained and stable development of basic research, cutting-edge technology research, and social welfare technology research, strengthens original innovation and tackling of key core technologies, and accelerates the achievement of high-level scientific and technological self-reliance and self-strengthening.

Article 8 The State guarantees the freedom to carry out scientific and technological research and development, encourages scientific exploration and technological innovation, and protects the legitimate rights and interests of scientific and technical personnel, including the freedom of exploration.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, enterprises, public institutions, and citizens have the right to independently choose topics, explore unknown scientific fields, and engage in basic research, cutting-edge technology research, and social welfare technology research.

Article 9 Schools and other educational institutions shall adhere to the principle of integrating theory with practice, focus on cultivating the independent thinking ability, practical ability, innovation ability, and critical thinking of the educated, as well as the scientific spirit of pursuing truth, advocating innovation, and seeking truth from facts.

The State gives full play to the important role of higher education institutions in scientific and technological research, encourages higher education institutions to carry out scientific research, technology development, and social services, and cultivate high-level specialized talents with a sense of social responsibility, innovative spirit, and practical ability.

Article 10 Scientific and technical personnel are an important talent force for the cause of socialist modernization and shall be respected by the whole society.

The State adheres to the strategic position of talent leading development, deepens the reform of the talent development system and mechanism, cultivates, introduces, and utilizes talents in an all-round way, creates an environment that conforms to the laws of scientific and technological innovation and talent growth, and gives full play to the role of talent as the primary resource.

Article 11 The State creates a social environment conducive to scientific and technological innovation, and encourages state organs, mass organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and citizens to participate in and support activities for scientific and technological progress.

The whole society shall respect labor, respect knowledge, respect talent, and respect creation, forming a fashion that advocates science.

Article 12 The State develops the cause of science and technology popularization, popularizes scientific and technological knowledge, strengthens the construction of infrastructure and capabilities for science and technology popularization, and improves the scientific and cultural quality of all citizens, especially young people.

Science and technology popularization is the common responsibility of the whole society. The State establishes and improves an incentive mechanism for science and technology popularization, and encourages scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and scientific and technical personnel to actively participate in and support science and technology popularization activities.

Article 13 The State formulates and implements an intellectual property strategy, establishes and improves the intellectual property system, creates a social environment that respects intellectual property, protects intellectual property, and stimulates independent innovation.

Enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and scientific and technical personnel shall enhance their awareness of intellectual property, enhance their independent innovation capability, improve their ability to create, utilize, protect, manage, and serve intellectual property, and improve the quality of intellectual property.

Article 14 The State establishes and improves a scientific and technological evaluation system conducive to innovation.

Scientific and technological evaluation shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality, be oriented towards the quality, contribution, and performance of scientific and technological innovation, and implement classified evaluation according to the characteristics of different scientific and technological activities.

Article 15 The State Council leads the work on scientific and technological progress nationwide, formulates medium- and long-term science and technology development plans and scientific and technological innovation plans, and determines major national science and technology projects and major projects closely related to science and technology. The medium- and long-term science and technology development plans and scientific and technological innovation plans shall clarify guiding principles, play a strategic guiding role, and guide and coordinate the layout of scientific and technological development, resource allocation, and policy formulation.

People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate work on scientific and technological progress into national economic and social development plans, and ensure that scientific and technological progress is coordinated with economic construction and social development.

Local people's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to strengthen the organization and management of work on scientific and technological progress, optimize the environment for scientific and technological development, and promote scientific and technological progress.

Article 16 The administrative department for science and technology under the State Council is responsible for the macro-management, overall coordination, service guarantee, and supervision of implementation of work on scientific and technological progress nationwide; other relevant departments under the State Council are responsible for related work on scientific and technological progress within their respective scopes of duties.

The administrative department for science and technology under the local people's government at or above the county level is responsible for work on scientific and technological progress within its administrative region; other relevant departments under the local people's government at or above the county level are responsible for related work on scientific and technological progress within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 17 The State establishes a coordination mechanism for work on scientific and technological progress, studies major issues in work on scientific and technological progress, coordinates the establishment and mutual connection of national science and technology plan projects, and coordinates major matters such as the allocation of scientific and technological resources, the integration of scientific and technological research and development institutions, and the combination of scientific and technological research and development with higher education and industrial development.

Article 18 May 30th of each year is National Science and Technology Workers' Day.

The State establishes and improves the science and technology award system, establishes awards such as the State Preeminent Science and Technology Award, and gives awards to organizations and individuals that have made important contributions to activities for scientific and technological progress. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

The State encourages domestic and foreign organizations or individuals to establish science and technology awards and give awards to organizations and individuals that have made contributions to activities for scientific and technological progress.

## Chapter II Basic Research

Article 19 The State strengthens the capacity building for basic research, respects the laws of scientific development and talent growth, strengthens the systematic layout of projects, talents, and bases, and provides good material conditions and strong institutional guarantees for the development of basic research.

The State strengthens planning and deployment, promotes the organic combination of free exploration and goal orientation in basic research, focuses on the frontiers of science and technology, economic and social development, major national security needs, and the life and health of the people, focuses on major key technological issues, strengthens basic research in emerging and strategic industries and other fields, and enhances the source supply capacity of science and technology.

The State encourages scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, enterprises, etc., to leverage their own advantages, strengthen basic research, and promote original innovation.

Article 20 The State finance establishes an investment mechanism to stably support basic research.

The State encourages local people's governments with conditions to reasonably determine financial investment in basic research based on the economic and social development needs of their regions, and strengthen support for basic research.

The State guides enterprises to increase investment in basic research, encourages social forces to invest in basic research through multiple channels such as donations and establishing funds, and provides fiscal, financial, tax, and other policy support.

The proportion of basic research funding in the total amount of the whole society's scientific and technological research and development funding shall be gradually increased, commensurate with the requirements of building an innovative country and a leading power in science and technology.

Article 21 The State establishes the Natural Science Foundation to fund basic research and support talent cultivation and team building. The determination of projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation shall adhere to the principles of macro guidance, independent application, equal competition, peer review, and merit-based support.

Local people's governments with conditions may, based on the actual economic and social conditions and development needs of their regions, establish natural science foundations to support basic research.

Article 22 The State improves the discipline layout and knowledge system construction, promotes interdisciplinary integration, and promotes the coordinated development of basic research and applied research.

Article 23 The State intensifies efforts to cultivate basic research talents, strengthens stable support for basic research talents, and improves the quality and level of the basic research talent team.

The State establishes a resource allocation mechanism that meets the needs of basic research, establishes an evaluation system and incentive mechanism compatible with basic research, creates a good environment for dedicated basic research, and encourages and attracts outstanding scientific and technical personnel to devote themselves to basic research.

Article 24 The State strengthens the construction of basic research bases.

The State improves the construction of basic conditions for basic research and promotes open sharing.

Article 25 The State supports higher education institutions in strengthening the construction of basic disciplines and the cultivation of basic research talents, enhancing the independent layout capability of basic research, and promoting the high-quality development of basic research in higher education institutions.

## Chapter III Applied Research and Transformation of Achievements

Article 26 The State encourages applied research to drive basic research, and promotes the integrated development of basic research, applied research, and transformation of achievements.

The State improves the supply system for generic basic technologies, promotes the deep integration of the innovation chain and the industrial chain, and ensures the security of the industrial chain and supply chain.

Article 27 The State establishes and improves a coordination mechanism for scientific research tackling, focuses on economic and social development, major national security needs, and the life and health of the people, strengthens the integrated allocation of projects, talents, bases, and funds in key areas, promotes close cooperation between industry, academia, and research, and promotes the independent controllability of key core technologies.

Article 28 The State improves the nationwide system for tackling key core technologies, organizes and implements major scientific and technological tasks that reflect national strategic needs, systematically lays out forward-looking and strategic major scientific and technological projects, and makes forward-looking deployments for the research and development of key core technologies.

Article 29 The State strengthens the construction of generic technology platforms and scientific and technological research and development institutions oriented towards industrial development needs, and encourages localities to build applied research scientific and technological research and development institutions based on development needs.

The State encourages scientific and technological research and development institutions and higher education institutions to strengthen research on generic basic technologies, and encourages enterprises to take the lead in carrying out research and development activities oriented towards the market and industrial application.

Article 30 The State strengthens the pilot testing, engineering, and industrial development and application of scientific and technological achievements, and accelerates the transformation of scientific and technological achievements into practical productive forces.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions and higher education institutions funded by fiscal funds shall actively promote the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, strengthen the construction of technology transfer institutions and talent teams, and establish and improve the system for promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Article 31 The State encourages enterprises, scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, and other organizations to establish cooperation mechanisms featuring complementary advantages, clear division of labor, shared achievements, and shared risks, jointly establish research and development platforms, technological innovation alliances, innovation consortia, etc., according to market mechanisms, collaboratively promote research and development and the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, and improve the effectiveness of the transfer and transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Article 32 For scientific and technological achievements arising from scientific and technological plan projects funded by fiscal funds, provided that national security, national interests, and major public interests are not harmed, the project undertakers are authorized to obtain relevant intellectual property rights in accordance with the law. The project undertakers may, in accordance with the law, independently invest in the transformation, transfer to others, jointly implement the transformation with others, license others to use, or invest as equity.

The project undertakers shall implement the intellectual property rights specified in the preceding paragraph in accordance with the law, take protective measures at the same time, and submit an annual report to the project management institution on the implementation and protection status; if the intellectual property rights are not implemented within a reasonable period without justifiable reasons, the State may implement them free of charge, or license others to implement them for a fee or free of charge.

For the intellectual property rights specified in the first paragraph of this article legally obtained by the project undertakers, if necessary for national security, national interests, and major public interests, the State may implement them free of charge, or license others to implement them for a fee or free of charge.

The distribution of benefits arising from the implementation of the intellectual property rights specified in the first paragraph of this article by the project undertakers shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations; if there are no provisions in laws and regulations, it shall be carried out according to the agreement.

Article 33 The State implements a distribution policy oriented towards increasing the value of knowledge, promotes the reform of the ownership and benefit distribution mechanism of intellectual property rights in accordance with relevant state regulations, and explores the system of granting scientific and technical personnel ownership of job-related scientific and technological achievements or long-term use rights.

Article 34 The State encourages the intellectual property rights arising from scientific and technological plan projects funded by fiscal funds to be first used within the country.

If the intellectual property rights specified in the preceding paragraph are transferred to foreign organizations or individuals, or licensed exclusively to foreign organizations or individuals, approval from the project management institution shall be obtained; if laws and administrative regulations have other provisions on the approving institution, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 35 The State encourages the application of new technologies, promotes the application experiments of new technologies, new products, new services, and new models in accordance with the principle of inclusive and prudent regulation, and creates conditions for the application of new technologies and new products.

Article 36 The State encourages and supports applied research in agricultural science and technology, disseminates and popularizes agricultural science and technology knowledge, accelerates the transformation and industrialization of agricultural scientific and technological achievements, promotes agricultural scientific and technological progress, and uses agricultural science and technology to lead rural revitalization and agricultural and rural modernization.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to support public welfare agricultural scientific and technological research and development institutions and agricultural technology extension institutions in the research, development, application, and promotion of new agricultural varieties and new technologies.

Local people's governments at all levels shall encourage and guide agricultural science and technology service institutions, science and technology commissioners, and rural mass science and technology organizations to provide science and technology services for the development of crop farming, forestry, animal husbandry, fisheries, etc., and provide science and technology training and guidance for farmers.

Article 37 The State promotes the integration of scientific and technological research and development with the formulation of product and service standards, and the integration of scientific and technological research and development with product design and manufacturing; guides scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, enterprises, and social organizations to jointly promote the research, formulation, and lawful adoption of national major technological innovation product and service standards, and participate in the formulation of international standards.

Article 38 The State cultivates and develops a unified, open, interconnected, and orderly competitive technology market, encourages the establishment of intermediary service institutions engaged in activities such as technology assessment, technology brokerage, and innovation and entrepreneurship services, guides the establishment of a socialized, specialized, networked, information-based, and intelligent technology transaction service system and innovation and entrepreneurship service system, and promotes the application and promotion of scientific and technological achievements.

Technology transaction activities shall follow the principles of voluntary equality, mutual benefit and compensation, and good faith.

## Chapter IV Enterprise Scientific and Technological Innovation

Article 39 The State establishes a technological innovation system with enterprises as the main body, market-oriented, and close cooperation between enterprises and scientific and technological research and development institutions and higher education institutions, guides and supports enterprise technological innovation activities, supports enterprises to lead national scientific and technological research tasks, gives play to the main role of enterprises in technological innovation, promotes enterprises to become the main body of technological innovation decision-making, scientific research investment, organizing scientific research, and achievement transformation, promotes the agglomeration of various innovation elements to enterprises, and improves the technological innovation capability of enterprises.

The State cultivates leading science and technology enterprises with influence and competitiveness, and gives full play to the innovation driving role of leading science and technology enterprises.

Article 40 The State encourages enterprises to carry out the following activities:

(1) Establish internal scientific and technological research and development institutions;

(2) Conduct cooperative research with other enterprises or scientific and technological research and development institutions and higher education institutions, jointly establish scientific and technological research and development institutions and platforms, establish science and technology enterprise incubators and innovation and entrepreneurship platforms, or conduct scientific and technological research and development by means of entrustment, etc.;

(3) Cultivate, attract, and utilize scientific and technical personnel;

(4) Jointly cultivate professional technical talents and highly skilled talents with scientific and technological research and development institutions, higher education institutions, vocational colleges, or training institutions, and attract graduates of higher education institutions to work in enterprises;

(5) Establish postdoctoral workstations or mobile stations;

(6) Combine technological innovation and employee skills training, carry out science and technology popularization activities, and establish science and technology popularization venues or facilities open to the public.

Article 41 The State encourages enterprises to strengthen original innovation, carry out technical cooperation and exchanges, increase investment in research and development and technological innovation, independently determine research and development topics, and carry out technological innovation activities.The State encourages enterprises to digest, absorb, and re-innovate imported technologies.

Research and development expenses incurred by enterprises in developing new technologies, new products, or new processes may, in accordance with relevant State regulations, be deducted before tax and additionally deducted. Instruments and equipment used for scientific and technological research and development by enterprises may be subject to accelerated depreciation.

**Article 42** The State shall improve the multi-tiered capital market, establish and refine mechanisms to promote scientific and technological innovation, and support qualified technology-based enterprises in utilizing the capital market to drive their own development.

The State shall strengthen guidance and policy support, broaden the sources of venture capital funds through multiple channels, and provide support for the entrepreneurial development of enterprises.

The State shall improve the listing and financing system for technology-based enterprises, smooth the channels for domestic listing and financing of such enterprises, and leverage the financing function of the capital market to serve scientific and technological innovation.

**Article 43** The following enterprises shall enjoy tax preferences in accordance with relevant State regulations:

(1) Enterprises engaged in the research, development, and production of high-tech products;
(2) Small and medium-sized technology-based enterprises;
(3) Venture capital enterprises investing in start-up technology-based enterprises;
(4) Other enterprises related to scientific and technological progress as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 44** The State shall provide support for the construction and operation of public research and development platforms, as well as scientific and technological intermediary and innovation and entrepreneurship service institutions.

Public research and development platforms, scientific and technological intermediary institutions, and innovation and entrepreneurship service institutions shall provide services for the technological innovation of small and medium-sized enterprises.

**Article 45** The State shall protect the intellectual property rights obtained by enterprises through research and development. Enterprises shall continuously improve the quality and efficiency of their intellectual property rights, and enhance their independent innovation capabilities and market competitiveness.

**Article 46** State-owned enterprises shall establish and improve research and development investment systems, distribution systems, and assessment and evaluation systems conducive to technological innovation, and improve incentive and constraint mechanisms.

The persons in charge of state-owned enterprises shall be responsible for the technological progress of the enterprise. The performance assessment of the persons in charge of state-owned enterprises shall include the enterprise's innovation investment, innovation capacity building, and innovation effectiveness within the scope of assessment.

**Article 47** Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall create a market environment of fair competition to promote the technological progress of enterprises.

The relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, by formulating policies on industry, finance, taxation, energy, environmental protection, and climate change response, guide and encourage enterprises to research and develop new technologies, new products, and new processes, carry out technological transformation and equipment updates, eliminate technologically backward equipment and processes, and stop producing technologically backward products.

## Chapter V: Scientific and Technological Research and Development Institutions

**Article 48** The State shall make overall plans for the layout of scientific and technological research and development institutions, and establish and improve the scientific and technological research and development system.

The State shall establish national laboratories in major scientific and technological innovation fields that are crucial to national security and overall economic and social development, establish and improve a laboratory system led by national laboratories and supported by national key laboratories, and improve the stable support mechanism.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds shall adhere to the guidance of national strategic needs, provide public scientific and technological supply, and provide emergency scientific and technological support.

**Article 49** Natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations have the right to establish scientific and technological research and development institutions in accordance with the law. Overseas organizations or individuals may independently establish scientific and technological research and development institutions within the territory of China in accordance with the law, or jointly establish them with organizations or individuals within the territory of China.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions engaged in basic research, frontier technology research, and social welfare technology research may be established using fiscal funds. The establishment of scientific and technological research and development institutions using fiscal funds shall be optimized to prevent duplication.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions and institutions of higher education may establish postdoctoral mobile stations or workstations. Scientific and technological research and development institutions may establish branches abroad in accordance with the law.

**Article 50** Scientific and technological research and development institutions shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) To organize or participate in academic activities in accordance with the law;
(2) To independently determine the direction and projects of scientific and technological research and development in accordance with relevant State regulations, and independently decide on internal management matters such as fund utilization, institutional setup, performance assessment and salary distribution, professional title evaluation, transformation of scientific and technological achievements and income distribution, post setting, personnel recruitment, and reasonable mobility;
(3) To jointly carry out scientific and technological research and development, technical consultation, technical services, and other activities with other scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, and enterprises;
(4) To receive social donations and funding;
(5) Other rights stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 51** Scientific and technological research and development institutions shall formulate articles of association in accordance with the law, and carry out scientific and technological research and development activities in accordance with the functional positioning and business scope stipulated in the articles of association; strengthen the construction of scientific research style and academic atmosphere, establish and improve management systems for research integrity and scientific and technological ethics, and comply with the management norms for scientific research activities; and shall not organize, participate in, or support superstitious activities.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds, when carrying out scientific and technological research and development activities, shall serve national goals and the public interest; where conditions permit, they shall open venues or facilities for popularizing science and technology to the public, and organize and carry out science and technology popularization activities.

**Article 52** Scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds shall establish a modern institute system with clear responsibilities, scientific evaluation, openness and orderliness, and standardized management, implement a director or president responsibility system, establish systems such as a scientific and technical committee advisory system and a staff congress supervision system, and absorb external experts to participate in management and accept social supervision; the appointment of the director or president shall introduce a competitive mechanism.

**Article 53** The State shall improve the evaluation system for scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds, and the evaluation results shall serve as the basis for the establishment, support, adjustment, or termination of the institutions.

**Article 54** Scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds shall establish and improve a mechanism for the open sharing of scientific and technological resources, promoting the effective utilization of scientific and technological resources.

The State encourages scientific and technological research and development institutions established with social funds to implement the open sharing of scientific and technological resources within a reasonable scope.

**Article 55** The State encourages enterprises and other social forces to establish their own scientific and technological research and development institutions and protects their legitimate rights and interests.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions established with social funds have the right, in accordance with relevant State regulations, to compete on an equal footing and participate in the implementation of scientific and technological plan projects funded by fiscal funds.

The State shall improve the tax preference system for non-profit scientific and technological research and development institutions established with social funds.

**Article 56** The State supports the development of new types of innovation entities such as new-type research and development institutions, improves the development model featuring diversified investment entities, modernized management systems, market-oriented operation mechanisms, and flexible employment mechanisms, and guides new types of innovation entities to focus on scientific research, technological innovation, and research and development services.

## Chapter VI: Scientific and Technological Personnel

**Article 57** The State shall create a social environment that respects and cherishes talent, an institutional environment of fairness, equality, competition, and merit selection, and a living environment with appropriate treatment and strong guarantees, creating favorable conditions for scientific and technological personnel to concentrate on scientific research.

The State shall take various measures to enhance the social status of scientific and technological personnel, cultivate and bring up specialized scientific and technological talents, ensure that scientific and technological personnel devote themselves to scientific and technological innovation and research and development activities, and give full play to the role of scientific and technological personnel. It is prohibited to treat scientific and technological personnel and their scientific and technological achievements unfairly by any means or methods.

**Article 58** The State shall accelerate the construction of strategic talent forces, optimize the structure of the scientific and technological talent team, improve the mechanisms for cultivating, discovering, introducing, utilizing, and evaluating innovative talents and teams such as strategic scientists and leading scientific and technological talents, and implement supporting policies for talent echelons, scientific research conditions, and management mechanisms.

**Article 59** The State shall improve the education and training mechanism for innovative talents, strengthen the cultivation of scientific interest in basic education, strengthen the training of technical and skilled talents in vocational education, strengthen the integration of higher education resource allocation with the cultivation of innovative talents in the field of science and technology, and strengthen and improve the reserve of strategic scientific and technological talents.

**Article 60** People's governments at all levels, enterprises, institutions, and social organizations shall take measures to improve the income distribution mechanism that reflects the value of innovation factors such as knowledge and technology, optimize the income structure, establish a mechanism for stable wage growth, and increase the wage level of scientific and technological personnel; provide generous treatment and honor incentives to scientific and technological personnel with outstanding contributions.

Scientific and technological personnel of scientific and technological research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established with fiscal funds may, with the consent of their employer, engage in part-time work to obtain legal income, provided that they fulfill their job responsibilities, complete their own work, and do not create conflicts of interest. The extraction of bonus and reward funds for activities such as technology development, technical consultation, and technical services shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions on the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

The State encourages scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, etc., to adopt methods such as equity, options, and dividends to incentivize scientific and technological personnel.

**Article 61** People's governments at all levels, enterprises, and institutions shall guarantee the right of scientific and technological personnel to receive continuing education, and create an environment and conditions for the reasonable, smooth, and orderly flow of scientific and technological personnel, giving full play to their expertise.

**Article 62** Scientific and technological personnel may choose their work units, compete for corresponding positions, and obtain corresponding positions or professional titles based on their academic level and professional ability.

Scientific and technological personnel shall abide by their work commitments, fulfill their job responsibilities, and complete the work corresponding to their positions or professional titles.

**Article 63** The State implements a classified evaluation system for scientific and technological personnel, applying different evaluation standards and methods to personnel engaged in different scientific and technological activities, highlighting the orientation of innovation value, capability, and contribution, reasonably determining salary and treatment, allocating academic resources, and setting evaluation cycles, forming a talent evaluation system conducive to the dedicated research and innovation of scientific and technological personnel, and stimulating the innovation vitality of scientific and technological personnel.

**Article 64** Relevant departments of science and technology administration, enterprises, and institutions shall improve the management system for scientific and technological personnel, enhance service awareness and support capabilities, simplify management processes, avoid duplicate inspections and evaluations, reduce the burden on scientific and technological personnel in project declaration, material submission, and expense reimbursement, and ensure the research time of scientific and technological personnel.

**Article 65** When scientific and technological personnel work in harsh, remote areas or harsh, dangerous environments, their employer shall provide subsidies in accordance with relevant State regulations, provide the occupational health protection and safety guarantees appropriate to their position or workplace, and provide convenient conditions for them to receive continuing education and professional training.

**Article 66** Young scientific and technological personnel, scientific and technological personnel from ethnic minorities, and female scientific and technological personnel shall enjoy equal rights in competing for professional and technical positions, participating in scientific and technological evaluations, undertaking scientific and technological research and development projects, and receiving continuing education. Elderly scientific and technological personnel are encouraged to play an active role in scientific and technological progress.

People's governments at all levels, enterprises, and institutions shall create an environment and conditions for the growth of young scientific and technological personnel, encourage young scientific and technological personnel to explore and try boldly in the field of science and technology, and give full play to the role of young scientific and technological personnel. The situation of discovering, cultivating, and utilizing young scientific and technological personnel shall be an important part of evaluating the work of scientific and technological progress.

People's governments at all levels, enterprises, and institutions shall improve the cultivation, evaluation, and incentive mechanisms for female scientific and technological personnel, care for female scientific and technological personnel during pregnancy and lactation, and encourage and support female scientific and technological personnel to play a greater role in scientific and technological progress.

**Article 67** Scientific and technological personnel shall vigorously promote the spirit of scientists characterized by patriotism, innovation, truth-seeking, dedication, collaboration, and education, adhere to the spirit of craftsmanship, abide by academic and ethical norms in all kinds of scientific and technological activities, uphold professional ethics, and be honest and trustworthy; they shall not engage in fraud in scientific and technological activities, nor participate in or support superstitious activities.

**Article 68** The State encourages scientific and technological personnel to explore freely and dare to take risks, creating a good atmosphere that encourages innovation and tolerates failure. If original records and other evidence can prove that scientific and technological personnel undertaking scientific and technological research and development projects with strong exploratory nature and high risks have fulfilled their diligent and responsible obligations but still cannot complete the project, they shall be exempted from liability.

**Article 69** Records of research integrity shall serve as an important basis for appointing scientific and technological personnel to professional and technical positions or professional titles, approving their applications for scientific and technological research and development projects, and awarding scientific and technological awards.

**Article 70** Scientific and technological personnel have the right to establish or participate in scientific and technological social groups in accordance with the law.

Science and technology associations and scientific and technological social groups shall play a role in promoting academic exchanges, advancing discipline construction, driving scientific and technological innovation, carrying out science and technology popularization activities, cultivating specialized talents, providing consulting services, strengthening the self-discipline of scientific and technological personnel, and safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of scientific and technological personnel, in accordance with their articles of association.

The legitimate rights and interests of science and technology associations and scientific and technological social groups shall be protected by law.

## Chapter VII: Regional Scientific and Technological Innovation

**Article 71** The State shall coordinate the spatial layout of scientific and technological resources, promote the close integration of central scientific and technological resources with local development needs, and support regional scientific and technological innovation through various means.

**Article 72** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall support scientific and technological research and application, create conditions for promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, and provide a favorable innovation environment for promoting regional innovation and development.

**Article 73** Scientific and technological plans formulated by the people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments related to industrial development shall reflect the needs of industrial development.

When determining scientific and technological plan projects, the people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall encourage enterprises to compete on an equal footing and participate in implementation; for projects that meet industrial development needs and have clear market application prospects, enterprises shall be encouraged to jointly implement them with scientific and technological research and development institutions and institutions of higher education.

The implementation of major local scientific and technological plans shall be aligned with the deployment of major national scientific and technological tasks.

**Article 74** The State Council may, as needed, approve the establishment of national high-tech industrial development zones, national independent innovation demonstration zones, and other science and technology parks, and provide guidance and support for the construction and development of these parks, enabling them to form characteristics and advantages and play a role in agglomeration and demonstration-driven effects.

**Article 75** The State encourages qualified local people's governments at or above the county level to build major scientific and technological innovation bases and platforms, cultivate innovation and entrepreneurship carriers, and create regional highlands of scientific and technological innovation, based on national development strategies and local development needs.

The State supports qualified localities in building scientific and technological innovation centers and comprehensive science centers, playing a role in radiation-driven effects, deepening innovation reform, and participating in global scientific and technological cooperation.

**Article 76** The State shall establish a mechanism for regional scientific and technological innovation cooperation and collaborative mutual assistance, encourage local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments to carry out cross-regional innovation cooperation, and promote the rational flow and efficient agglomeration of various innovation factors.

**Article 77** Major national strategic regions may, relying on regional innovation platforms, build benefit-sharing mechanisms, promote the free flow of factors such as talent, technology, and capital, promote the open sharing of scientific instruments and equipment, scientific and technological infrastructure, scientific engineering, and scientific and technological information resources, and improve the efficiency of regional transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 78** The State encourages localities to actively explore regional scientific and technological innovation models, respect the laws of regional scientific and technological innovation agglomeration, and choose scientific and technological innovation development paths with regional characteristics according to local conditions.

## Chapter VIII: International Scientific and Technological Cooperation

**Article 79** The State promotes open, inclusive, mutually beneficial, and shared international scientific and technological cooperation and exchange, supporting the building of a community with a shared future for mankind.

**Article 80** The Government of the People's Republic of China develops scientific and technological cooperation and exchange with foreign governments and international organizations.

The State encourages various innovation entities such as scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, scientific and technological social groups, enterprises, and scientific and technological personnel to carry out international scientific and technological cooperation and exchange, actively participate in scientific research activities, promote the open flow of international scientific and technological resources, form a high-level pattern of open scientific and technological cooperation, and promote world scientific and technological progress.

**Article 81** The State encourages enterprises, institutions, and social organizations to build international scientific and technological innovation cooperation platforms through various channels and provide international scientific and technological innovation cooperation services.

Enterprises, institutions, social organizations, and scientific and technological personnel are encouraged to participate in and initiate international scientific and technological organizations to enhance international scientific and technological cooperation and exchange.

**Article 82** The State supports collaborative research and development by outstanding scientific and technological talents from home and abroad through various means to address the common challenges facing humanity and explore the frontiers of science.

The State supports scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, and scientific and technological personnel in actively participating in and initiating the organization and implementation of international big science programs and big science projects.

The State shall improve the mechanisms for intellectual property protection, scientific and technological ethics, and security review in international scientific and technological research cooperation.

**Article 83** The State shall expand the openness and cooperation of scientific and technological plans, encourage foreign-invested enterprises and foreign scientific and technological personnel in China to undertake and participate in scientific and technological plan projects, and improve the mechanism for overseas scientific and technological personnel to participate in national scientific and technological plan projects.

**Article 84** The State shall improve relevant social services and safeguard measures, encourage scientific and technological personnel working abroad to return to China, and attract foreign scientific and technological personnel to engage in scientific and technological research and development work in China.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions and other scientific and technological organizations may, based on development needs, employ overseas scientific and technological personnel. Scientific and technological research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established with fiscal funds that employ overseas scientific and technological personnel for scientific and technological research and development work shall provide convenience for their work and life.

Outstanding foreign scientific and technological personnel who come to China to engage in scientific and technological research and development work may, in accordance with relevant State regulations, be given priority in obtaining permanent residency in China or acquiring Chinese nationality.

## Chapter IX: Safeguard Measures

**Article 85** The State shall increase fiscal funding and formulate policies on industry, finance, taxation, government procurement, etc., to encourage and guide social capital investment, and promote the sustained and stable growth of research and development funding for the whole society.

**Article 86** The State shall gradually increase the overall level of scientific and technological funding; the growth rate of State fiscal expenditure on science and technology shall be higher than the growth rate of the State's regular fiscal revenue. The research and development funding of the whole society shall account for an appropriate proportion of the gross domestic product and shall be gradually increased.

**Article 87** Fiscal scientific and technological funds shall be mainly used for the following investments:

(1) Construction of basic conditions and facilities for science and technology;
(2) Basic research and frontier interdisciplinary research;
(3) Frontier technology research, social welfare technology research, and research on major common key technologies that have strategic, fundamental, and forward-looking roles in economic construction and social development;(4) Application of major common key technologies and demonstration of high-tech industrialization;

(5) Research and development of science and technology related to the ecological environment and people's life and health, as well as the application and promotion of such achievements;

(6) Research and development of new agricultural varieties and technologies, and the application and promotion of agricultural scientific and technological achievements;

(7) Training, attraction, and utilization of scientific and technical personnel;

(8) Popularization of science and technology.

The state shall provide support in terms of funding and experimental means to scientific and technological research and development institutions established with fiscal funds.

Article 88 The establishment of national science and technology plans shall be based on national needs, focus on major national strategic tasks, and follow the laws of scientific research, technological innovation, and achievement transformation.

The state shall establish a coordination mechanism for science and technology plans and a performance evaluation system, and strengthen specialized management.

Article 89 The state shall establish funds to support small and medium-sized enterprises in carrying out technological innovation and promote the transformation and application of scientific and technological achievements.

When necessary, the state may establish other non-profit funds to support basic research, social welfare technological research, international joint research, and other activities, to fund scientific and technological progress activities.

Article 90 Those engaged in the following activities shall enjoy tax incentives in accordance with relevant state regulations:

(1) Technology development, technology transfer, technology licensing, technology consulting, and technology services;

(2) Importing items for scientific research, technology development, or popularization of science and technology that cannot be produced domestically or whose performance cannot meet domestic needs;

(3) Importing key equipment, raw materials, or parts that cannot be produced domestically for the implementation of major national science and technology projects or major projects under national science and technology plans;

(4) Science and technology popularization venues and bases conducting science and technology popularization activities open to the public;

(5) Donations and funding for carrying out scientific and technological activities;

(6) Other scientific research, technology development, and technology application activities as stipulated by law and relevant state regulations.

Article 91 For innovative products and services of domestic natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations, if their indicators such as function and quality can meet the needs of government procurement, the government procurement shall purchase them; if they are launched on the market for the first time, the government procurement shall take the lead in purchasing them, and shall not restrict them on the grounds of commercial performance.

If the products to be procured by the government are yet to be researched and developed, they shall be procured through ordering. The procurer shall prioritize the use of competitive methods to determine scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, or enterprises for research and development, and purchase according to the agreement after the product research and development is qualified.

Article 92 The state encourages financial institutions to carry out intellectual property pledge financing business, encourages and guides financial institutions to support the application of science and technology and the development of high-tech industries in credit, investment, and other aspects, encourages insurance institutions to develop insurance products according to the needs of high-tech industry development, and promotes the application of new technologies.

Article 93 The state, following the principles of overall planning and optimized allocation, integrates and establishes national scientific and technological research and experimental bases.

The state encourages the establishment of comprehensive scientific and technological experimental service units to provide or entrust others to provide scientific and technological experimental services for scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, and scientific and technical personnel.

Article 94 Based on the needs of scientific and technological progress, the state, following the principles of overall planning, highlighting sharing, optimized allocation, comprehensive integration, government leadership, and multi-party co-construction, coordinates the procurement of large-scale scientific instruments and equipment, and conducts joint review of large-scale scientific instruments and equipment mainly purchased with fiscal funds.

Article 95 The state strengthens the construction of academic journals, improves the exchange mechanism for scientific research papers and scientific and technological information, promotes the development of open science, and facilitates the exchange and dissemination of science and technology.

Article 96 The state encourages domestic and foreign organizations or individuals to donate property, establish science and technology funds, and fund scientific and technological research and development and popularization of science and technology.

Article 97 For scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, and enterprises established with fiscal funds, if the responsible persons, in the process of promoting scientific and technological management reform, carrying out scientific and technological research and development, and implementing the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, are innovative and exploratory, and make decision-making errors or deviations, but have fulfilled reasonable duty of care and supervision and management responsibilities, and have not sought illegal benefits, they shall be exempted from decision-making responsibilities.

## Chapter 10 Supervision and Management

Article 98 The state strengthens the rule of law in science and technology and the construction of research style and academic atmosphere, establishes and improves the scientific research integrity system and the science and technology supervision system, improves the science and technology ethics governance system, and creates a good environment for scientific and technological innovation.

Article 99 The state improves the rules and procedures for scientific and technological decision-making, establishes a standardized consultation and decision-making mechanism, and promotes scientific, democratic, and legal decision-making.

The state reforms and improves the consultation system for major scientific and technological decisions. When formulating scientific and technological development plans and major policies, and determining major scientific and technological projects and major projects closely related to science and technology, the opinions of scientific and technical personnel shall be fully heard, the role of think tanks shall be played, public participation shall be expanded, scientific evaluation shall be carried out, and scientific decision-making shall be implemented.

Article 100 The state strengthens the performance management of fiscal science and technology funds, and improves the efficiency and effectiveness of fund allocation. The management and use of fiscal science and technology funds shall be subject to the supervision and inspection of audit institutions and financial departments.

The science and technology administrative departments and other relevant departments shall strengthen the supervision of the implementation of science and technology plans established with fiscal funds, and strengthen the coordination, evaluation, and supervision of scientific research project funds.

No organization or individual may falsely report, fraudulently obtain, embezzle, misappropriate, or withhold fiscal science and technology funds.

Article 101 The state establishes a classified management mechanism for science and technology plan projects, and strengthens the assessment and evaluation of project effectiveness. Science and technology plan projects established with fiscal funds shall be established based on problem orientation, goal orientation, and demand orientation, and the project undertakers shall be determined through competitive selection in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The state establishes a science and technology management information system, establishes a database of review experts, and improves the expert review system for science and technology plan projects and the selection, avoidance, confidentiality, and accountability system for review experts.

Article 102 The science and technology administrative department of the State Council shall, together with the relevant competent departments of the State Council, establish information systems and resource databases for scientific and technological research bases, scientific instruments and equipment, and other assets, as well as scientific and technological literature, scientific and technological data, scientific and technological natural resources, and scientific and technological popularization resources, and promptly disclose to the public the distribution and use of scientific and technological resources.

The management unit of scientific and technological resources shall disclose to the public the sharing and use system and use of the scientific and technological resources under its management, and arrange for use according to the use system; if laws and administrative regulations stipulate that confidentiality is required, the provisions shall be followed.

The management unit of scientific and technological resources shall not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of users of scientific and technological resources, and shall determine the charging standards in accordance with relevant state regulations. Other rights and obligations between the management unit and the user shall be agreed upon by both parties.

Article 103 The state establishes a science and technology ethics committee, improves the science and technology ethics system and norms, strengthens science and technology ethics education and research, and improves the review, evaluation, and supervision system.

Scientific and technological research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, and institutions shall fulfill their main responsibilities for science and technology ethics management, establish and improve science and technology ethics review mechanisms in accordance with relevant state regulations, and conduct science and technology ethics reviews on scientific and technological activities.

Article 104 The state strengthens the construction of scientific research integrity, establishes a scientific research project integrity file and a scientific research integrity management information system, adheres to the principle of both prevention and punishment, and both self-discipline and supervision, and improves the prevention, investigation, and handling mechanisms for dishonest behavior.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant industry competent departments shall take various measures to strengthen the construction of scientific research integrity, and enterprises, institutions, and social organizations shall fulfill their main responsibilities for scientific research integrity management.

No organization or individual may fabricate or falsify scientific research results, publish or disseminate false scientific research results, or engage in the buying, selling, ghostwriting, or ghost-submission of academic papers, their experimental research data, or materials for the application and acceptance of science and technology plan projects.

Article 105 The state establishes and improves the scientific and technological statistics survey system and the national innovation survey system, grasps the basic situation of national scientific and technological activities, and monitors and evaluates national innovation capabilities.

The state establishes and improves the science and technology report system, and the undertakers of science and technology plan projects funded by fiscal funds shall submit reports in a timely manner in accordance with regulations.

Article 106 The state implements a science and technology confidentiality system, strengthens the capacity building of science and technology confidentiality, and protects science and technology secrets involving national security and interests.

The state implements an exit management system for important biological germplasm resources, genetic resources, data resources, and other scientific and technological resources, as well as key core technologies, in accordance with the law.

Article 107 Scientific and technological research, development, and application activities that endanger national security, harm public interests, endanger human health, or violate scientific research integrity and science and technology ethics are prohibited.

Those engaged in scientific and technological activities shall comply with the management norms for scientific and technological activities. Organizations and individuals that seriously violate the management norms for scientific and technological activities shall be recorded in the database of serious dishonest acts in scientific research integrity by the science and technology administrative departments and other relevant departments.

## Chapter 11 Legal Liability

Article 108 If, in violation of this Law, the science and technology administrative departments and other relevant departments and their staff, as well as other persons performing public duties in accordance with the law, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 109 If, in violation of this Law, anyone abuses their power to obstruct, restrict, or suppress scientific and technological research and development activities, or uses their power to suppress, exclude, or make things difficult for scientific and technical personnel, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 110 If, in violation of this Law, anyone falsely reports, fraudulently obtains, embezzles, misappropriates, or withholds fiscal funds or social donation funds used for scientific and technological progress, the relevant competent department shall order correction, recover the relevant fiscal funds, order the return of donated funds, give a warning or circulate a notice of criticism, and may suspend funding, terminate or revoke the relevant scientific and technological activities; if the circumstances are serious, a fine shall be imposed in accordance with the law, and the person shall be prohibited from undertaking or participating in scientific and technological activities supported by fiscal funds for a certain period; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given administrative penalties and sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 111 If, in violation of this Law, after purchasing large-scale scientific instruments and equipment with fiscal funds and state-owned capital, the obligation to share and use large-scale scientific instruments, equipment, and other scientific and technological resources is not fulfilled, the relevant competent department shall order correction, give a warning or circulate a notice of criticism, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 112 If, in violation of this Law, anyone engages in scientific and technological research, development, and application activities that endanger national security, harm public interests, endanger human health, or violate scientific research integrity and science and technology ethics, the unit where the scientific and technical personnel are located or the relevant competent department shall order correction; if fiscal funds for scientific and technological progress are obtained or there is illegal income, the relevant competent department shall terminate or revoke the relevant scientific and technological activities, recover the fiscal funds, and confiscate the illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant competent department shall disclose the illegal acts to the public, impose administrative penalties and sanctions in accordance with the law, and prohibit the person from undertaking or participating in scientific and technological activities supported by fiscal funds or applying for administrative permits for relevant scientific and technological activities for a certain period; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given administrative penalties and sanctions in accordance with the law.

If, in violation of this Law, anyone fabricates or falsifies scientific research results, publishes or disseminates false scientific research results, or engages in the buying, selling, ghostwriting, or ghost-submission of academic papers, their experimental research data, or materials for the application and acceptance of science and technology plan projects, the relevant competent department shall give a warning or circulate a notice of criticism, and impose a fine; if there is illegal income, the illegal income shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the license shall be revoked.

Article 113 If, in violation of this Law, anyone engaged in scientific and technological activities violates the management norms for scientific and technological activities, the relevant competent department shall order correction within a time limit, and may recover the relevant fiscal funds, give a warning or circulate a notice of criticism, suspend funding, terminate or revoke the relevant scientific and technological activities supported by fiscal funds; if the circumstances are serious, the person shall be prohibited from undertaking or participating in scientific and technological activities supported by fiscal funds for a certain period, and the management qualification for scientific and technological activities supported by fiscal funds shall be revoked for a certain period; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 114 If, in violation of this Law, anyone fraudulently obtains a national science and technology award, the competent department shall revoke the award in accordance with the law, recover the medal, certificate, and bonus, and impose sanctions in accordance with the law.

If, in violation of this Law, a nominating unit or individual provides false data or materials to assist others in fraudulently obtaining a national science and technology award, the competent department shall circulate a notice of criticism; if the circumstances are serious, the nomination qualification shall be suspended or revoked, and sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

Article 115 For acts violating the provisions of this Law, if this Law does not provide for administrative penalties, and other relevant laws and administrative regulations have provisions, those provisions shall apply; if property losses or other damages are caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, public security administration penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 12 Supplementary Provisions

Article 116 Other matters related to scientific and technological progress in national defense shall be stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 117 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2022.
