# Law of the People's Republic of China on Promoting the Transformation of Scientific and Technological Achievements

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Promoting the Transformation of Scientific and Technological Achievements at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 29, 2015

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to promote the transformation of scientific and technological achievements into real productive forces, regulate activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, accelerate scientific and technological progress, and promote economic construction and social development.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "scientific and technological achievements" means achievements with practical value generated through scientific research and technological development. "Service-related scientific and technological achievements" means scientific and technological achievements completed in the course of performing tasks for research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, and other entities, or mainly using the material and technical conditions of the aforementioned entities.

For the purposes of this Law, "transformation of scientific and technological achievements" means activities such as subsequent testing, development, application, and promotion of scientific and technological achievements to improve productivity, until new technologies, new processes, new materials, new products are formed, and new industries are developed.

**Article 3** Activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements shall be conducive to accelerating the implementation of the innovation-driven development strategy, promoting the integration of science and technology with the economy, improving economic and social benefits, protecting the environment, rationally utilizing resources, and promoting economic construction, social development, and safeguarding national security.

Activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements shall respect market rules, give play to the principal role of enterprises, follow the principles of voluntariness, mutual benefit, fairness, and good faith, and enjoy rights and bear risks in accordance with laws, regulations, and contractual agreements. Intellectual property rights in activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements shall be protected by law.

Activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements shall comply with laws and regulations, safeguard national interests, and shall not harm public interests or the legitimate rights and interests of others.

**Article 4** The State shall make reasonable arrangements for financial investment in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, guide social capital investment, and promote the diversification of funding for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 5** The State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen policy coordination in science and technology, finance, investment, taxation, talent, industry, finance, government procurement, and military-civilian integration, to create a favorable environment for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Local people's governments at all levels may, in accordance with the principles stipulated in this Law and in light of local conditions, adopt measures more conducive to promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 6** The State encourages the implementation of scientific and technological achievements first within China. Where a Chinese entity or individual transfers or licenses the implementation of scientific and technological achievements to an organization or individual outside China, it shall comply with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions.

**Article 7** For the needs of national security, national interests, and major public interests, the State may, in accordance with the law, organize the implementation or license others to implement relevant scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 8** The administrative department for science and technology under the State Council, the comprehensive economic management department, and other relevant administrative departments shall, in accordance with the responsibilities stipulated by the State Council, manage, guide, and coordinate the work of transforming scientific and technological achievements.

Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for managing, guiding, and coordinating the work of transforming scientific and technological achievements within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II: Organization and Implementation

**Article 9** The State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall incorporate the transformation of scientific and technological achievements into national economic and social development plans, and organize and coordinate the implementation of the transformation of relevant scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 10** For applied science and technology projects and other related science and technology projects funded by fiscal funds, the relevant administrative departments and management institutions shall improve and innovate the management methods of scientific research organization. When formulating relevant science and technology plans, programs, and compiling project guidelines, they shall solicit opinions from relevant industries and enterprises. When organizing the implementation of applied science and technology projects, they shall clarify the obligations of project undertakers for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, strengthen intellectual property management, and take the transformation of scientific and technological achievements and the creation and application of intellectual property as important content and basis for project establishment and acceptance.

**Article 11** The State shall establish and improve the science and technology reporting system and the scientific and technological achievements information system, disclose to the public information on the implementation of science and technology projects, scientific and technological achievements, and related intellectual property information, and provide public services such as query and screening of scientific and technological achievements information. The disclosure of relevant information shall not divulge state secrets or trade secrets. For information not to be disclosed, the relevant departments shall promptly inform the relevant project undertakers.

Undertakers of science and technology projects funded by fiscal funds shall promptly submit relevant science and technology reports in accordance with regulations, and submit information on scientific and technological achievements and related intellectual property to the scientific and technological achievements information system.

The State encourages undertakers of science and technology projects funded by non-fiscal funds to submit relevant science and technology reports and submit information on scientific and technological achievements and related intellectual property to the scientific and technological achievements information system. The departments responsible for relevant work of the people's governments at or above the county level shall provide convenience for them.

**Article 12** The State shall support the following projects for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements through government procurement, research and development funding, publication of industrial technology guidance catalogs, demonstration and promotion, and other means:

(1) Those that can significantly improve industrial technology level and economic benefits, or can form new industries that promote healthy social and economic development;
(2) Those that can significantly improve national security capabilities and public safety levels;
(3) Those that can rationally develop and utilize resources, save energy, reduce consumption, prevent and control environmental pollution, protect ecology, and improve the ability to respond to climate change and disaster prevention and mitigation;
(4) Those that can improve people's livelihood and public health levels;
(5) Those that can promote modern agriculture or rural economic development;
(6) Those that can accelerate the social and economic development of ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

**Article 13** The State, by formulating policies and measures, advocates and encourages the adoption of advanced technologies, processes, and equipment, and continuously improves, restricts the use of, or eliminates backward technologies, processes, and equipment.

**Article 14** The State shall strengthen standard-setting work, promptly formulate national standards and industry standards for new technologies, new processes, new materials, and new products in accordance with the law, actively participate in the formulation of international standards, and promote the promotion and application of advanced and applicable technologies.

The State shall establish an effective system for the mutual transformation of military and civilian scientific and technological achievements, and improve the collaborative innovation system and mechanism for national defense science and technology. Military product research and production shall, in accordance with the law, give priority to the adoption of advanced and applicable civilian standards, and promote the mutual transfer and transformation of military and civilian technologies.

**Article 15** For key projects for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements organized and implemented by people's governments at all levels, relevant departments may organize the implementation of the transformation through public bidding. The relevant departments shall provide the winning bidders with the funding or other conditions determined at the time of bidding.

**Article 16** Holders of scientific and technological achievements may transform the achievements in the following ways:

(1) Investing and implementing the transformation independently;
(2) Transferring the scientific and technological achievements to others;
(3) Licensing others to use the scientific and technological achievements;
(4) Using the scientific and technological achievements as cooperation conditions to jointly implement the transformation with others;
(5) Investing the scientific and technological achievements as value, converted into shares or capital contribution ratios;
(6) Other methods determined through negotiation.

**Article 17** The State encourages research and development institutions and institutions of higher education to transfer scientific and technological achievements to enterprises or other organizations by means of transfer, licensing, or investment as value.

Research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State shall strengthen the management, organization, and coordination of the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, promote the construction of teams for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, optimize the process of transforming scientific and technological achievements, and carry out technology transfer through their own institutions responsible for technology transfer work or by entrusting independent service institutions for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 18** Research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State may independently decide to transfer, license, or invest the scientific and technological achievements they hold as value, but shall determine the price through negotiated pricing, listing and trading on the technology trading market, auction, or other means. Where negotiated pricing is adopted, the name of the scientific and technological achievement and the proposed transaction price shall be publicly announced within the entity.

**Article 19** For service-related scientific and technological achievements obtained by research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State, the completers and participants may, without changing the ownership of the service-related scientific and technological achievements, carry out the transformation of such scientific and technological achievements in accordance with an agreement with the entity, and enjoy the rights stipulated in the agreement. The entity shall support the above-mentioned activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

The completer of the scientific and technological achievement or the project leader shall not obstruct the transformation of service-related scientific and technological achievements, nor shall they take possession of the service-related scientific and technological achievements and their technical data and information for themselves, infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of the entity.

**Article 20** The competent departments of research and development institutions and institutions of higher education, as well as relevant administrative departments such as finance and science and technology, shall establish a performance appraisal and evaluation system conducive to promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, take the situation of the transformation of scientific and technological achievements as important content and one of the bases for evaluating relevant entities and personnel and providing scientific research funding support, and increase scientific research funding support for relevant entities and personnel with outstanding performance in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State shall establish a professional title evaluation, post management, and assessment and evaluation system that conforms to the characteristics of the work of transforming scientific and technological achievements, and improve the income distribution incentive and constraint mechanism.

**Article 21** Research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State shall submit an annual report on the transformation of scientific and technological achievements to their competent departments, explaining the number of scientific and technological achievements obtained in accordance with the law, the implementation of the transformation, and the related income distribution. The competent department shall, in accordance with regulations, submit the annual report on the transformation of scientific and technological achievements to relevant administrative departments such as finance and science and technology.

**Article 22** To adopt new technologies, new processes, new materials, and produce new products, enterprises may publish information on their own or entrust science and technology intermediary service institutions to solicit the scientific and technological achievements they need, or seek partners for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

The administrative departments for science and technology and other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, provide help and support for enterprises to obtain the required scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 23** Enterprises have the right, in accordance with the law, to independently or jointly implement the transformation of scientific and technological achievements with domestic or foreign enterprises, institutions, and other partners.

Enterprises may, through fair competition, independently or jointly with other entities, undertake science and technology research and development projects and projects for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements organized and implemented by the government.

**Article 24** For science and technology projects funded by fiscal funds that have market application prospects and clear industrial goals, the relevant government departments and management institutions shall give play to the leading role of enterprises in the selection of research and development directions, project implementation, and achievement application, and encourage enterprises, research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, and other organizations to jointly implement them.

**Article 25** The State encourages research and development institutions and institutions of higher education to combine with enterprises to jointly implement the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Research and development institutions and institutions of higher education may participate in bidding activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements organized by relevant government departments or enterprises.

**Article 26** The State encourages enterprises, research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, and other organizations to adopt industry-university-research cooperation methods such as jointly establishing research and development platforms, technology transfer institutions, or technological innovation alliances to jointly carry out research and development, achievement application and promotion, and standard research and formulation.

The cooperating parties shall sign an agreement, stipulating in accordance with the law matters such as the organizational form of cooperation, division of tasks, capital investment, ownership of intellectual property, distribution of rights and interests, risk sharing, and liability for breach of contract.

**Article 27** The State encourages research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, enterprises, and other organizations to conduct exchanges of scientific and technical personnel. According to professional characteristics and the technological development needs of the industry field, they may hire scientific and technical personnel from enterprises and other organizations to engage in part-time teaching and scientific research work, and support their own scientific and technical personnel to go to enterprises and other organizations to engage in activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 28** The State supports enterprises, research and development institutions, institutions of higher education, vocational colleges, and training institutions to jointly establish student internship and practice training bases and graduate student research practice work institutions, and jointly cultivate professional and technical talents and highly skilled talents.

**Article 29** The State encourages agricultural scientific research institutions and agricultural experiment and demonstration units to independently or cooperate with other entities to implement the transformation of agricultural scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 30** The State cultivates and develops the technology market, encourages the establishment of science and technology intermediary service institutions, and provides services such as trading venues, information platforms, and information retrieval, processing and analysis, evaluation, and brokerage for technology transactions.

Science and technology intermediary service institutions providing services shall follow the principles of fairness and objectivity, shall not provide false information and certificates, and shall be obliged to keep confidential the state secrets and the trade secrets of the parties concerned that they become aware of during the service process.

**Article 31** The State supports the construction of public research and development platforms according to industrial and regional development needs, and provides services such as technology integration, generic technology research and development, pilot testing and industrial testing, systematization and engineering development of scientific and technological achievements, and technology promotion and demonstration for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 32** The State supports the development of science and technology enterprise incubators, university science parks, and other science and technology enterprise incubation institutions, and provides incubation space, entrepreneurship guidance, research and development and management consulting services for start-up technology-based small and medium-sized enterprises.

## Chapter III: Safeguard Measures

**Article 33** Fiscal funds for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements are mainly used for guiding funds, loan interest subsidies, subsidy funds, and venture capital for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, as well as other fund uses for promoting the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 34** The State shall implement tax incentives for activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements in accordance with relevant tax laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 35** The State encourages banking financial institutions to innovate in organizational forms, management mechanisms, financial products, and services, encourages the development of loan businesses such as intellectual property pledge loans and equity pledge loans, and provides financial support for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

The State encourages policy-based financial institutions to take measures to increase financial support for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 36** The State encourages insurance institutions to develop insurance types that conform to the characteristics of the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, and provide insurance services for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

**Article 37** The State shall improve the multi-level capital market, and support enterprises to raise funds for projects for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements through direct financing methods such as equity transactions and the issuance of stocks and bonds in accordance with the law.

**Article 38** The State encourages venture capital institutions to invest in projects for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements.

Venture capital guiding funds established by the State shall guide and support venture capital institutions to invest in start-up technology-based small and medium-sized enterprises.

**Article 39** The State encourages the establishment of funds for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements or risk funds, the sources of which are provided by the State, local governments, enterprises, institutions, and other organizations or individuals, to support the transformation of high-input, high-risk, high-output scientific and technological achievements, and accelerate the industrialization of major scientific and technological achievements.

The establishment of funds for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements and risk funds and the use of their funds shall be carried out in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter IV: Technical Rights and Interests

**Article 40** Where an entity that completes scientific and technological achievements cooperates with other entities to transform scientific and technological achievements, the ownership of the relevant rights and interests of the scientific and technological achievements shall be stipulated by contract in accordance with the law. If the contract does not stipulate, the following principles shall apply:

(1) If no new invention or creation is made in the cooperative transformation, the rights and interests of the scientific and technological achievements shall belong to the entity that completed the scientific and technological achievements;
(2) If a new invention or creation is made in the cooperative transformation, the rights and interests of the new invention or creation shall be jointly owned by the cooperating parties;
(3) For the scientific and technological achievements generated in the cooperative transformation, each party has the right to implement such scientific and technological achievements, and the transfer of such scientific and technological achievements shall be subject to the consent of all cooperating parties.

**Article 41** Where an entity that completes scientific and technological achievements cooperates with other entities to transform scientific and technological achievements, the cooperating parties shall reach an agreement on keeping technical secrets; the parties shall not violate the agreement or the requirements of the right holder on keeping technical secrets, disclose or allow others to use the technology.

**Article 42** Enterprises and institutions shall establish and improve a technical secret protection system to protect their own technical secrets. Employees shall abide by the technical secret protection system of their entity.

Enterprises and institutions may sign agreements with relevant personnel participating in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements to keep the entity's technical secrets during their employment or for a certain period after resignation, leaving office, or retirement; the relevant personnel shall not violate the agreement, disclose the entity's technical secrets, or engage in activities for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements identical to those of the original entity.

Employees shall not transfer or covertly transfer service-related scientific and technological achievements without authorization.

**Article 43** All income obtained by research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State from the transformation of scientific and technological achievements shall be retained by the entity. After giving rewards and remuneration to personnel who have made important contributions to the completion and transformation of service-related scientific and technological achievements, the income shall be mainly used for scientific and technological research and development and achievement transformation and related work.

**Article 44** After the transformation of service-related scientific and technological achievements, the entity that completed the scientific and technological achievements shall give rewards and remuneration to personnel who have made important contributions to the completion and transformation of such scientific and technological achievements.

The entity that completed the scientific and technological achievements may stipulate or agree with the scientific and technical personnel on the methods, amounts, and time limits of rewards and remuneration. When formulating relevant regulations, the entity shall fully solicit the opinions of its scientific and technical personnel and make the relevant regulations public within the entity.

**Article 45** If the entity that completed the scientific and technological achievements has not stipulated or agreed with the scientific and technical personnel on the methods and amounts of rewards and remuneration, rewards and remuneration shall be given to personnel who have made important contributions to the completion and transformation of service-related scientific and technological achievements according to the following standards:

(1) If the service-related scientific and technological achievements are transferred or licensed to others for implementation, no less than 50% of the net income from the transfer or licensing of such scientific and technological achievements shall be extracted;
(2) If the service-related scientific and technological achievements are invested as value, no less than 50% of the shares or capital contribution ratios formed by such scientific and technological achievements shall be extracted;
(3) If the service-related scientific and technological achievements are implemented independently or in cooperation with others, no less than 5% of the operating profit from the implementation of such scientific and technological achievements shall be extracted each year for three to five consecutive years after the successful investment in production following the implementation of the transformation.

The methods and amounts of rewards and remuneration stipulated by research and development institutions and institutions of higher education established by the State or agreed upon with scientific and technical personnel shall meet the standards specified in items (1) to (3) of the preceding paragraph.

The expenditure of state-owned enterprises and institutions on giving rewards and remuneration to personnel who have made important contributions to the completion and transformation of service-related scientific and technological achievements in accordance with this Law shall be included in the total wages of the entity for the current year, but shall not be limited by the total wages of the entity for the current year and shall not be included in the base of the total wages of the entity.

## Chapter V: Legal Liability

**Article 46** If an undertaker of a science and technology project funded by fiscal funds fails to submit a science and technology report or submit information on scientific and technological achievements and related intellectual property in accordance with this Law, the government department or management institution organizing the implementation of the project shall order correction; if the circumstances are serious, a notice of criticism shall be given, and the undertaker shall be prohibited from undertaking science and technology projects funded by fiscal funds within a certain period.A research and development institution or institution of higher learning established by the state that fails to submit an annual report on the status of transformation of scientific and technological achievements in accordance with this Law shall be ordered by its competent authority to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be given a notice of criticism.

Article 47 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any party engages in fraudulent activities during the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, using deceptive means to obtain awards and honorary titles, defraud money, or seek illegal profits, the relevant government department shall, in accordance with its management responsibilities, order corrections, revoke the awards and honorary titles, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine. If economic losses are caused to others, civil liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 48 Where a science and technology service institution or its personnel, in violation of the provisions of this Law, intentionally provides false information, experimental results, or evaluation opinions to deceive the parties involved, or colludes with one party to deceive the other party, the relevant government department shall, in accordance with its management responsibilities, order corrections, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine; if the circumstances are serious, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke the business license in accordance with the law. If economic losses are caused to others, civil liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Where a science and technology intermediary service institution or its personnel, in violation of the provisions of this Law, discloses state secrets or the trade secrets of the parties involved, they shall bear corresponding legal liability in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 49 Where the administrative department for science and technology or other relevant departments and their staff members abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain during the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, the appointing authority or supervisory authority shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 50 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any party encroaches upon another party's scientific and technological achievements by means such as instigating theft, inducement, or coercion, thereby infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of others, civil liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law, and a fine may be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 51 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an employee, without the permission of the unit, discloses the unit's technical secrets, or unauthorizedly transfers or transfers in disguised form the job-related scientific and technological achievements, or where relevant personnel involved in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements violate agreements with the unit by engaging in the same transformation activities as the original unit within the agreed period after resignation, retirement, or leaving the post, causing economic losses to the unit, civil liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 6 Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 1996.
