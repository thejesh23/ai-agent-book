# Emergency Response Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on August 30, 2007

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of preventing and reducing the occurrence of emergencies, controlling, mitigating and eliminating the serious social harm caused by emergencies, regulating emergency response activities, protecting the safety of people's lives and property, and safeguarding national security, public security, environmental security and social order.

Article 2 This Law applies to emergency response activities such as the prevention and emergency preparedness, monitoring and early warning, emergency response and rescue, and post-incident recovery and reconstruction of emergencies.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "emergencies" means natural disasters, accident disasters, public health incidents and social security incidents that occur suddenly, cause or may cause serious social harm, and require emergency response measures to be taken.

Based on factors such as the degree of social harm and the scope of impact, natural disasters, accident disasters and public health incidents are classified into four levels: particularly serious, serious, relatively serious and general. Where laws, administrative regulations or the State Council provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

The classification standards for emergencies shall be formulated by the State Council or the departments designated by the State Council.

Article 4 The State establishes an emergency management system featuring unified leadership, comprehensive coordination, classified management, graded responsibility, and territorial management as the main principle.

Article 5 Emergency response work shall adhere to the principle of prevention first, combining prevention with emergency response. The State establishes a major emergency risk assessment system to conduct comprehensive assessments of potential emergencies, reduce the occurrence of major emergencies, and minimize the impact of major emergencies to the greatest extent possible.

Article 6 The State establishes an effective social mobilization mechanism to enhance the public's awareness of public safety and risk prevention, and to improve the whole society's capacity for risk avoidance and rescue.

Article 7 The people's government at the county level shall be responsible for emergency response work within its administrative region; where two or more administrative regions are involved, the common higher-level people's government of the relevant administrative regions shall be responsible, or the higher-level people's governments of the relevant administrative regions shall be jointly responsible.

After an emergency occurs, the people's government at the county level where the incident takes place shall immediately take measures to control the development of the situation, organize emergency rescue and response work, and immediately report to the higher-level people's government; when necessary, it may report directly to a higher level without following the normal chain of command.

If the people's government at the county level where the emergency occurs cannot eliminate or effectively control the serious social harm caused by the emergency, it shall promptly report to the higher-level people's government. The higher-level people's government shall promptly take measures and provide unified leadership for the emergency response work.

Where laws or administrative regulations stipulate that the relevant departments of the State Council are responsible for emergency response work, those provisions shall prevail; local people's governments shall actively cooperate and provide necessary support.

Article 8 The State Council, under the leadership of the Premier, studies, decides and deploys the response work for particularly serious emergencies; based on actual needs, it may establish a national emergency response command organization responsible for emergency response work; when necessary, the State Council may send working groups to guide relevant work.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish emergency response command organizations composed of the principal responsible persons of the people's government at the same level, responsible persons of relevant departments, and responsible persons of the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force stationed in the locality, to provide unified leadership and coordination for the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level and lower-level people's governments in carrying out emergency response work; based on actual needs, they may establish emergency response command organizations for relevant categories of emergencies to organize, coordinate and command emergency response work.

The competent departments of higher-level people's governments shall, within their respective areas of responsibility, guide and assist lower-level people's governments and their corresponding departments in properly carrying out emergency response work.

Article 9 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level are the administrative leading organs for emergency response work, and their working offices and specific responsibilities shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 10 Decisions and orders made by relevant people's governments and their departments concerning emergency response shall be published in a timely manner.

Article 11 Measures taken by relevant people's governments and their departments to respond to emergencies shall be commensurate with the nature, degree and scope of the social harm that the emergency may cause; where multiple measures are available, the measure that is most conducive to protecting the rights and interests of citizens, legal persons and other organizations shall be chosen.

Citizens, legal persons and other organizations have the obligation to participate in emergency response work.

Article 12 Relevant people's governments and their departments may requisition the property of units and individuals for the purpose of responding to emergencies. Requisitioned property shall be promptly returned after it has been used or after the emergency response work has been completed. If the property is damaged, destroyed or lost during or after requisition, compensation shall be provided.

Article 13 Where litigation, administrative reconsideration or arbitration proceedings cannot be conducted normally due to the adoption of emergency response measures, the provisions on the suspension of the statute of limitations and the suspension of proceedings shall apply, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 14 The People's Liberation Army, the People's Armed Police Force and the militia organizations shall participate in the emergency rescue and response work of emergencies in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws, administrative regulations, military regulations, and the orders of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 15 The government of the People's Republic of China shall cooperate and exchange with foreign governments and relevant international organizations in the areas of emergency prevention, monitoring and early warning, emergency response and rescue, and post-incident recovery and reconstruction.

Article 16 Decisions and orders made by people's governments at or above the county level concerning emergency response shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for the record; after the emergency response work is completed, a special work report shall be made to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level.

## Chapter II Prevention and Emergency Preparedness

Article 17 The State establishes and improves the emergency plan system.

The State Council formulates the national overall emergency plan for emergencies and organizes the formulation of national special emergency plans for emergencies; the relevant departments of the State Council, in accordance with their respective responsibilities and the relevant emergency plans of the State Council, formulate departmental emergency plans for national emergencies.

Local people's governments at all levels and the relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, rules, the emergency plans of higher-level people's governments and their relevant departments, and the actual local conditions, formulate corresponding emergency plans.

The authorities that formulate emergency plans shall revise the emergency plans in a timely manner based on actual needs and changes in circumstances. The procedures for formulating and revising emergency plans shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 18 Emergency plans shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and regulations, and in light of the nature, characteristics and possible social harm of the emergency, specifically stipulate the organizational command system and responsibilities for emergency management work, the prevention and early warning mechanisms for emergencies, the response procedures, the emergency support measures, and the post-incident recovery and reconstruction measures.

Article 19 Urban and rural planning shall meet the needs of preventing and responding to emergencies, make overall arrangements for the equipment and infrastructure necessary for responding to emergencies, and reasonably determine emergency shelters.

Article 20 People's governments at the county level shall investigate, register and conduct risk assessments of the hazard sources and hazardous areas within their administrative regions that are prone to causing natural disasters, accident disasters and public health incidents, conduct regular inspections and monitoring, and order relevant units to take safety precautions.

People's governments at the provincial level and at the level of cities divided into districts shall investigate, register and conduct risk assessments of the hazard sources and hazardous areas within their administrative regions that are prone to causing particularly serious or serious emergencies, organize inspections and monitoring, and order relevant units to take safety precautions.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, promptly make public the hazard sources and hazardous areas that have been registered, in accordance with national regulations.

Article 21 People's governments at the county level and their relevant departments, people's governments at the township level, sub-district offices, residents' committees and villagers' committees shall promptly mediate and handle disputes and conflicts that may trigger social security incidents.

Article 22 All units shall establish and improve safety management systems, regularly inspect the implementation of various safety precautions within their units, and promptly eliminate potential accident hazards; they shall be aware of and promptly handle problems within their units that may trigger social security incidents, preventing the escalation of conflicts and the expansion of the situation; they shall promptly report to the local people's government or the relevant departments of the people's government, in accordance with regulations, on the emergencies that may occur within their units and the safety precautions taken.

Article 23 Mining and construction units, and units that produce, operate, store, transport or use dangerous items such as flammable and explosive substances, hazardous chemicals and radioactive substances, shall formulate specific emergency plans, conduct hazard inspections of their production and operation sites, buildings and structures containing dangerous items, and the surrounding environment, and promptly take measures to eliminate hazards and prevent the occurrence of emergencies.

Article 24 Operating units or management units of public transport vehicles, public places and other densely populated places shall formulate specific emergency plans, equip the vehicles and relevant places with alarm devices and necessary emergency rescue equipment and facilities, indicate their methods of use, and clearly mark safe evacuation passages and routes, ensuring that safety passages and exits are unobstructed.

Relevant units shall regularly test and maintain their alarm devices and emergency rescue equipment and facilities to keep them in good condition and ensure normal use.

Article 25 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve the emergency management training system, and regularly train the staff of the people's government and its relevant departments who are responsible for handling emergencies.

Article 26 People's governments at or above the county level shall integrate emergency resources and establish or designate comprehensive emergency rescue teams. The relevant departments of the people's government may, based on actual needs, establish professional emergency rescue teams.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments may establish emergency rescue teams composed of adult volunteers. Units shall establish full-time or part-time emergency rescue teams composed of their own employees.

People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen cooperation between professional emergency rescue teams and non-professional emergency rescue teams, conduct joint training and joint drills, and improve the capacity for integrated and coordinated emergency response.

Article 27 The relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments, and relevant units shall purchase personal accident insurance for professional emergency rescue personnel, equip them with necessary protective equipment and gear, and reduce the personal risks of emergency rescue personnel.

Article 28 The People's Liberation Army, the People's Armed Police Force and the militia organizations shall plan and organize specialized training for emergency rescue.

Article 29 People's governments at the county level and their relevant departments, people's governments at the township level, and sub-district offices shall organize publicity and popularization activities on emergency knowledge and necessary emergency drills.

Residents' committees, villagers' committees, enterprises and institutions shall, in accordance with the requirements of the local people's government and in light of their own actual conditions, carry out publicity and popularization activities on emergency knowledge related to emergencies and necessary emergency drills.

News media shall conduct free public service announcements on the knowledge of emergency prevention and response, and self-rescue and mutual rescue.

Article 30 Schools of all types and at all levels shall incorporate emergency knowledge education into their teaching content, educate students on emergency knowledge, and cultivate students' safety awareness and their ability to rescue themselves and others.

The competent education departments shall provide guidance and supervision for schools in carrying out emergency knowledge education.

Article 31 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall adopt fiscal measures to ensure the funds needed for emergency response work.

Article 32 The State establishes and improves the emergency material reserve guarantee system, and improves the supervision, production, storage, allocation and emergency distribution system for important emergency materials.

People's governments at or above the level of cities divided into districts and people's governments at the county level in areas prone to or with a high incidence of emergencies shall establish a reserve system for emergency rescue materials, daily necessities and emergency response equipment.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the actual local conditions, sign agreements with relevant enterprises to ensure the production and supply of emergency rescue materials, daily necessities and emergency response equipment.

Article 33 The State establishes and improves the emergency communication guarantee system, improves the public communication network, and establishes an emergency communication system that combines wired and wireless communication, and supports the basic telecommunications network with mobile communication systems, to ensure smooth communication during emergency response work.

Article 34 The State encourages citizens, legal persons and other organizations to provide materials, funds, technical support and donations for the people's government's emergency response work.

Article 35 The State develops the insurance industry, establishes a catastrophe risk insurance system supported by state finances, and encourages units and citizens to participate in insurance.

Article 36 The State encourages and supports qualified teaching and research institutions in cultivating specialized talents for emergency management, and encourages and supports teaching and research institutions and relevant enterprises in researching and developing new technologies, new equipment and new tools for the prevention, monitoring, early warning, emergency response and rescue of emergencies.

## Chapter III Monitoring and Early Warning

Article 37 The State Council establishes a unified national emergency information system.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish or designate a unified emergency information system for their respective regions to collect, store, analyze and transmit information on emergencies, and achieve interconnection and interoperability with the emergency information systems of higher-level people's governments and their relevant departments, lower-level people's governments and their relevant departments, professional institutions and monitoring networks, and strengthen cross-departmental and cross-regional information exchange and intelligence cooperation.

Article 38 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments and professional institutions shall collect information on emergencies through multiple channels.

People's governments at the county level shall establish a system of full-time or part-time information reporters in residents' committees, villagers' committees and relevant units.

Citizens, legal persons or other organizations that become aware of information on an emergency shall immediately report to the local people's government, the relevant competent department or the designated professional institution.

Article 39 Local people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, report information on emergencies to higher-level people's governments. The relevant competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall notify the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level of information on emergencies. Professional institutions, monitoring network points and information reporters shall promptly report information on emergencies to the local people's government and its relevant competent departments.

Relevant units and individuals reporting or submitting information on emergencies shall do so in a timely, objective and truthful manner, and shall not delay reporting, make false reports, conceal reports or omit reports.

Article 40 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly aggregate and analyze information on potential hazards and early warnings of emergencies, and when necessary, organize relevant departments, professional technicians, experts and scholars to hold consultations to assess the possibility of an emergency occurring and its potential impact; if they believe that a serious or particularly serious emergency may occur, they shall immediately report to the higher-level people's government, and notify the relevant departments of the higher-level people's government, local military units, and the people's governments of neighboring or related areas that may be affected.

Article 41 The State establishes and improves the emergency monitoring system.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, based on the types and characteristics of natural disasters, accident disasters and public health incidents, establish and improve basic information databases, improve monitoring networks, divide monitoring areas, determine monitoring points, specify monitoring items, provide necessary equipment and facilities, and assign full-time or part-time personnel to monitor potential emergencies.

Article 42 The State establishes and improves the emergency early warning system.

The early warning levels for natural disasters, accident disasters and public health incidents that can be warned of are classified into Level 1, Level 2, Level 3 and Level 4, according to the urgency of the emergency, its development trend and the degree of harm it may cause, and are indicated by red, orange, yellow and blue respectively, with Level 1 being the highest level.

The classification standards for early warning levels shall be formulated by the State Council or the departments designated by the State Council.

Article 43 When a natural disaster, accident disaster or public health incident that can be warned of is imminent or the likelihood of its occurrence increases, local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the relevant laws, administrative regulations and the authority and procedures stipulated by the State Council, issue an alarm of the corresponding level, decide and announce that the relevant area has entered a warning period, and at the same time report to the higher-level people's government; when necessary, they may report directly to a higher level without following the normal chain of command, and notify local military units and the people's governments of neighboring or related areas that may be affected.

Article 44 After issuing a Level 3 or Level 4 alarm and announcing entry into the warning period, local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the characteristics of the impending emergency and the harm it may cause, take the following measures:

(1) Activate the emergency plan;

(2) Order relevant departments, professional institutions, monitoring network points and personnel with specific responsibilities to promptly collect and report relevant information, make public the channels for reporting information on the emergency to the public, and strengthen the monitoring, forecasting and early warning of the occurrence and development of the emergency;

(3) Organize relevant departments and institutions, professional technicians, and relevant experts and scholars to analyze and assess the information on the emergency at any time, and predict the likelihood, scope and intensity of the emergency's occurrence, as well as the level of the potential emergency;

(4) Periodically release to the public forecast information and analysis and assessment results related to the emergency, and manage the reporting of relevant information;

(5) Promptly issue warnings to the public in accordance with relevant regulations about the potential harm of the emergency, publicize common knowledge on avoiding and mitigating harm, and publish consultation telephone numbers.

Article 45 After issuing a Level 1 or Level 2 alarm and announcing entry into the warning period, local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in addition to the measures stipulated in Article 44 of this Law, take one or more of the following measures based on the characteristics of the impending emergency and the harm it may cause:

(1) Order emergency rescue teams and personnel with specific responsibilities to enter a state of readiness, and mobilize reserve personnel to prepare for participation in emergency rescue and response work;

(2) Mobilize the materials, equipment and tools needed for emergency rescue, prepare emergency facilities and shelters, and ensure they are in good condition and ready for normal use at any time;

(3) Strengthen the security of key units, important locations and critical infrastructure, and maintain public security and social order;

(4) Take necessary measures to ensure the safety and normal operation of public facilities such as transportation, communications, water supply, drainage, power supply, gas supply and heating;

(5) Promptly issue to the public suggestions and advice on taking specific measures to avoid or mitigate harm;

(6) Transfer, evacuate or remove persons vulnerable to the emergency and provide them with proper shelter, and transfer important property;(7) Close or restrict access to locations prone to sudden incidents, and control or restrict activities in public places that may exacerbate harm;

(8) Other necessary preventive and protective measures as stipulated by laws, regulations, and rules.

Article 46: For social security incidents that are imminent or have already occurred, local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant competent departments shall report to the people's government at the next higher level and its relevant competent departments in accordance with regulations, and may report directly to higher levels when necessary.

Article 47: The people's government that issued the emergency alert shall, based on the development of the situation, adjust the alert level in a timely manner according to relevant regulations and reissue the alert.

If facts prove that the emergency is impossible to occur or the danger has been lifted, the people's government that issued the alert shall immediately announce the lifting of the alert, terminate the warning period, and rescind the measures that have been taken.

## Chapter 4: Emergency Response and Rescue

Article 48: After an emergency occurs, the people's government responsible for unified leadership or organizing the emergency response shall, based on its nature, characteristics, and degree of harm, immediately organize relevant departments, mobilize emergency rescue teams and social forces, and take emergency response measures in accordance with the provisions of this chapter and relevant laws, regulations, and rules.

Article 49: After a natural disaster, accident disaster, or public health incident occurs, the people's government responsible for unified leadership may take one or more of the following emergency response measures:

(1) Organize the rescue and treatment of affected persons, evacuate, relocate, and properly accommodate threatened persons, and take other relief measures;

(2) Quickly control the source of danger, mark hazardous areas, seal off dangerous locations, designate warning zones, implement traffic control, and take other control measures;
(3) Immediately repair damaged public facilities such as transportation, communication, water supply, drainage, power supply, gas supply, and heating; provide shelters and daily necessities to affected persons; implement medical rescue and health epidemic prevention, and take other safeguard measures;

(4) Prohibit or restrict the use of relevant equipment and facilities, close or restrict access to relevant locations, suspend densely populated activities or production and business operations that may lead to the expansion of harm, and take other protective measures;

(5) Activate the fiscal reserve funds and emergency rescue materials set aside by the people's government at the same level, and when necessary, requisition other urgently needed materials, equipment, facilities, and tools;

(6) Organize citizens to participate in emergency rescue and response work, and require persons with specific expertise to provide services;

(7) Ensure the supply of basic necessities such as food, drinking water, and fuel;

(8) Strictly punish, in accordance with the law, acts that disrupt market order, such as hoarding and profiteering, price gouging, and producing and selling counterfeit goods, to stabilize market prices and maintain market order;

(9) Strictly punish, in accordance with the law, acts that disrupt social order, such as looting property and interfering with or disrupting emergency response work, to maintain public security;

(10) Take necessary measures to prevent the occurrence of secondary or derivative incidents.

Article 50: After a social security incident occurs, the people's government organizing the response shall immediately organize relevant departments and, together with the public security organs, based on the nature and characteristics of the incident, take one or more of the following emergency response measures in accordance with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and other relevant state regulations:

(1) Forcibly isolate parties who use weapons to confront each other or engage in violent acts in conflicts, properly resolve on-site disputes and conflicts, and control the development of the situation;

(2) Control buildings, means of transportation, equipment, facilities, and the supply of fuel, gas, electricity, and water within a specific area;

(3) Seal off relevant locations and roads, inspect the identity documents of on-site personnel, and restrict activities within relevant public places;

(4) Strengthen the guard of core organs and units vulnerable to attack, and set up temporary security lines near state organs, military organs, state news agencies, radio stations, television stations, and foreign embassies and consulates in China;
(5) Other necessary measures as stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the State Council.

When an incident that seriously endangers public security and social order occurs, the public security organs shall immediately dispatch police in accordance with the law and take corresponding compulsory measures based on the on-site situation to restore social order as quickly as possible.

Article 51: When an emergency occurs that seriously affects the normal operation of the national economy, the State Council or the relevant competent department authorized by the State Council may take necessary emergency measures such as safeguards and controls to meet the basic living needs of the people and minimize the impact of the emergency.

Article 52: The people's government responsible for unified leadership or organizing the emergency response may, when necessary, requisition from units and individuals the equipment, facilities, sites, means of transportation, and other materials needed for emergency rescue; request other local people's governments to provide human, material, financial, or technical support; require enterprises that produce and supply daily necessities and emergency rescue materials to organize production and ensure supply; and require organizations providing public services such as medical care and transportation to provide corresponding services.

The people's government responsible for unified leadership or organizing the emergency response shall organize and coordinate transportation operators to give priority to transporting materials, equipment, tools, emergency rescue personnel, and persons affected by the emergency.

Article 53: The people's government responsible for unified leadership or organizing the emergency response shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, release information on the development of the emergency and the emergency response work in a unified, accurate, and timely manner.

Article 54: No unit or individual may fabricate or disseminate false information regarding the development of an emergency or the emergency response work.

Article 55: Residents' committees, villagers' committees, and other organizations in the area where the emergency occurs shall, in accordance with the decisions and orders of the local people's government, conduct publicity and mobilization, organize the masses to carry out self-rescue and mutual rescue, and assist in maintaining social order.

Article 56: Units affected by natural disasters, accident disasters, or public health incidents shall immediately organize their own emergency rescue teams and staff to rescue affected persons, evacuate, relocate, and accommodate threatened persons, control the source of danger, mark dangerous areas, seal off dangerous locations, and take other necessary measures to prevent the expansion of harm, while simultaneously reporting to the local people's government at the county level; for social security incidents caused by the unit's own problems or primarily involving its personnel, the relevant unit shall report the situation in accordance with regulations and quickly send a responsible person to the scene to conduct mediation and guidance work.

Other units in the area where the emergency occurs shall comply with the decisions and orders issued by the people's government, cooperate with the emergency response measures taken by the people's government, carry out their own emergency rescue work, and actively organize personnel to participate in the local emergency rescue and response work.

Article 57: Citizens in the area where the emergency occurs shall obey the command and arrangements of the people's government, residents' committees, villagers' committees, or their affiliated units, cooperate with the emergency response measures taken by the people's government, actively participate in emergency rescue work, and assist in maintaining social order.

## Chapter 5: Post-Event Recovery and Reconstruction

Article 58: After the threat and harm of an emergency are controlled or eliminated, the people's government responsible for unified leadership or organizing the emergency response shall cease implementing the emergency response measures taken in accordance with this law, while simultaneously taking or continuing necessary measures to prevent the occurrence of secondary or derivative incidents from natural disasters, accident disasters, or public health incidents, or the re-triggering of social security incidents.

Article 59: After the emergency response work is completed, the people's government responsible for unified leadership shall immediately organize an assessment of the losses caused by the emergency, organize the affected areas to restore production, daily life, work, and social order as quickly as possible, formulate a recovery and reconstruction plan, and report to the people's government at the next higher level.

The people's government in the area affected by the emergency shall promptly organize and coordinate relevant departments such as public security, transportation, railways, civil aviation, posts and telecommunications, and construction to restore public security order and quickly repair damaged public facilities such as transportation, communication, water supply, drainage, power supply, gas supply, and heating.

Article 60: If the people's government in the area affected by the emergency needs support from the people's government at the next higher level for recovery and reconstruction work, it may submit a request to that government. The people's government at the next higher level shall, based on the losses suffered by the affected area and the actual situation, provide financial and material support and technical guidance, and organize other areas to provide financial, material, and human resources support.

Article 61: The State Council shall, based on the losses suffered by the area affected by the emergency, formulate preferential policies to support the development of relevant industries in that area.

The people's government in the area affected by the emergency shall, based on the losses suffered by the area, formulate and implement plans for relief, compensation, consolation, pension, and resettlement, and properly resolve conflicts and disputes arising from the emergency response.

During the period when citizens participate in emergency rescue work or assist in maintaining social order, their wages and benefits at their unit shall remain unchanged; those who perform outstandingly and achieve significant results shall be commended or rewarded by the people's government at or above the county level.

The people's government at or above the county level shall provide pensions to personnel who are injured or killed during emergency rescue work in accordance with the law.

Article 62: The people's government responsible for unified leadership shall promptly investigate the process and causes of the emergency, summarize the experiences and lessons learned from the emergency response work, formulate improvement measures, and submit a report to the people's government at the next higher level.

## Chapter 6: Legal Liability

Article 63: If local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level violate the provisions of this law by failing to perform their statutory duties, their superior administrative organs or supervisory organs shall order them to make corrections; in any of the following circumstances, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law based on the severity of the circumstances:

(1) Failing to take preventive measures as required, resulting in the occurrence of an emergency, or failing to take necessary preventive measures, resulting in the occurrence of secondary or derivative incidents;

(2) Delaying, falsifying, concealing, or omitting information about an emergency, or reporting, submitting, or publishing false information, resulting in consequences;

(3) Failing to issue an emergency alert or take measures during the warning period in a timely manner as required, resulting in damage;

(4) Failing to take timely measures to respond to an emergency or handling it improperly, resulting in consequences;

(5) Disobeying the unified leadership, command, and coordination of the people's government at the next higher level regarding emergency response work;

(6) Failing to promptly organize and carry out post-event work such as production self-rescue and recovery and reconstruction;

(7) Intercepting, misappropriating, privately distributing, or covertly distributing emergency rescue funds or materials;

(8) Failing to promptly return requisitioned property of units and individuals, or failing to provide compensation to units and individuals whose property has been requisitioned in accordance with regulations.

Article 64: If a relevant unit has any of the following circumstances, the people's government responsible for unified leadership in the location shall order it to suspend production or business, temporarily withhold or revoke its permit or business license, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to take preventive measures as required, resulting in a serious emergency;

(2) Failing to promptly eliminate discovered hidden dangers that may cause an emergency, resulting in a serious emergency;

(3) Failing to properly carry out daily maintenance and inspection of emergency equipment and facilities, resulting in a serious emergency or the expansion of harm from an emergency;

(4) Failing to promptly organize emergency rescue work after an emergency occurs, resulting in serious consequences.

If other laws or administrative regulations stipulate that the relevant departments of the people's government shall decide on penalties for the acts specified in the preceding paragraph, those provisions shall apply.

Article 65: If a person violates the provisions of this law by fabricating and disseminating false information about the development of an emergency or emergency response work, or disseminating false information about the development of an emergency or emergency response work while knowing it to be false, they shall be ordered to make corrections and given a warning; if serious consequences result, their business activities shall be suspended or their practice permit shall be revoked in accordance with the law; if the directly responsible person is a state functionary, they shall also be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 66: If a unit or individual violates the provisions of this law by disobeying the decisions or orders issued by the local people's government and its relevant departments, or failing to cooperate with the measures taken by them in accordance with the law, and this constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 67: If a unit or individual violates the provisions of this law, causing an emergency to occur or harm to expand, and causing damage to the person or property of others, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 68: If a violation of the provisions of this law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7: Supplementary Provisions

Article 69: If an exceptionally serious emergency occurs that poses a major threat to the safety of people's lives and property, national security, public security, environmental security, or social order, and the emergency response measures provided for in this law and other relevant laws, regulations, and rules cannot eliminate or effectively control or mitigate its serious social harm, and it is necessary to enter a state of emergency, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or the State Council shall decide in accordance with the authority and procedures stipulated by the Constitution and other relevant laws.

Extraordinary measures taken during a state of emergency shall be implemented in accordance with relevant legal provisions or separately stipulated by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 70: This law shall come into effect on November 1, 2007.
