# Maritime Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 2, 1983

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China at the Twenty-Fourth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Revised at the Twenty-Eighth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2021

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen maritime traffic management, maintain maritime traffic order, ensure the safety of life and property, and safeguard national rights and interests.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities such as navigation, berthing, operations, and other activities related to maritime traffic safety conducted within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The State guarantees the use of sea areas for traffic in accordance with the law.

Maritime traffic safety work adheres to the principles of safety first, prevention as the priority, facilitation of passage, and management according to law, ensuring that maritime traffic is safe, orderly, and unobstructed.

Article 4 The competent transport department under the State Council is responsible for national maritime traffic safety work.

The national maritime safety administration is uniformly responsible for the supervision and management of maritime traffic safety. Other maritime safety administrations at various levels are specifically responsible for the supervision and management of maritime traffic safety within their respective jurisdictions according to their duties.

Article 5 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall support maritime traffic safety work, strengthen publicity and education on maritime traffic safety, and enhance the maritime traffic safety awareness of the whole society.

Article 6 The State guarantees the labor safety and occupational health of seafarers in accordance with the law and protects their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 7 Units and individuals engaged in the navigation, berthing, and operation of vessels and maritime facilities, as well as other activities related to maritime traffic, shall comply with relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, mandatory standards, and technical specifications concerning maritime traffic safety; they are entitled to receive navigation support and maritime rescue services in accordance with the law, and bear the obligation to maintain maritime traffic safety and protect the marine ecological environment.

Article 8 The State encourages and supports the application of advanced science and technology in maritime traffic safety work, promotes the modernization of maritime traffic safety, and improves the scientific and technological level of maritime traffic safety.

## Chapter II: Vessels, Maritime Facilities, and Seafarers

Article 9 Chinese-flagged vessels, maritime facilities established within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, shipping containers, and important marine equipment, components, and materials determined by the national maritime safety administration to be related to maritime traffic safety shall comply with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, mandatory standards, and technical specifications. They shall be inspected and certified by a vessel inspection authority and obtain corresponding certificates and documents. The list of certificates and documents shall be formulated and published by the national maritime safety administration.

The establishment of a vessel inspection authority shall be licensed by the national maritime safety administration. The conditions, procedures, and management for the establishment of vessel inspection authorities shall be implemented in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on vessel inspection.

Units holding relevant certificates and documents shall use vessels, maritime facilities, shipping containers, and important marine equipment, components, and materials for their specified purposes and shall undergo regular safety technical inspections in accordance with the law.

Article 10 A vessel may only fly the national flag of the People's Republic of China for navigation, berthing, and operations after applying to the maritime safety administration for nationality registration and obtaining a nationality certificate in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on vessel registration.

If a Chinese-flagged vessel is lost or scrapped, the vessel owner shall apply to cancel the nationality registration within the time limit prescribed by the competent transport department under the State Council. If the vessel owner fails to apply for cancellation within the time limit, the maritime safety administration may issue a public notice regarding the proposed compulsory cancellation of the vessel's nationality registration. If the vessel owner raises no objection within sixty days from the date of the public notice, the maritime safety administration may cancel the vessel's nationality registration.

Article 11 The owner, operator, or manager of a Chinese-flagged vessel shall establish and operate a safety management system for safe operation and pollution prevention.

After verifying that the management system referred to in the preceding paragraph meets the requirements, the maritime safety administration shall issue a Document of Compliance and a corresponding Safety Management Certificate for the vessel.

Article 12 The owner, operator, or manager of a Chinese-flagged vessel engaged in international voyages shall establish a vessel security system, formulate a vessel security plan, equip the vessel with security equipment in accordance with the plan, and conduct regular drills in accordance with the regulations of the competent transport department under the State Council.

Article 13 Chinese seafarers and workers on maritime facilities shall receive professional education and training in maritime traffic safety and their respective positions.

Chinese seafarers shall apply to the maritime safety administration for a seafarer's certificate of competency and obtain a medical certificate in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on seafarer management.

The employment of foreign seafarers on Chinese-flagged vessels shall be governed by the laws and administrative regulations on seafarer management.

Seafarers working on a vessel shall meet the requirements of the vessel, navigation area, and position specified in their certificate of competency.

Article 14 The owner, operator, or manager of a Chinese-flagged vessel engaged in international voyages shall apply to the maritime safety administration for a Maritime Labour Certificate for the vessel. The vessel shall meet the following conditions to obtain the certificate:

(1) The owner, operator, or manager has lawfully recruited seafarers, signed labor contracts or employment agreements with them, and equipped the vessel with seafarers meeting the requirements;
(2) The owner, operator, or manager has ensured that the working environment, occupational health protection and safety, working and rest hours, wages and remuneration, living conditions, medical conditions, and social insurance for seafarers on the vessel comply with relevant national regulations;
(3) The owner, operator, or manager has established a compliant seafarer complaint and handling mechanism;
(4) The owner, operator, or manager has provided corresponding financial guarantees or purchased corresponding insurance for seafarer repatriation costs and expenses legally payable for injuries, illnesses, or deaths occurring during employment on the vessel.

The maritime safety administration, in consultation with the human resources and social security administrative department, shall review whether the applicant and its vessel meet the conditions specified in the preceding paragraph according to their respective duties. If the conditions are met upon review, the maritime safety administration shall issue the Maritime Labour Certificate within ten working days from the date of accepting the application; if the conditions are not met, the maritime safety administration shall inform the applicant and provide reasons.

The specific measures for the issuance and supervision of Maritime Labour Certificates shall be formulated and published by the competent transport department under the State Council jointly with the human resources and social security administrative department under the State Council.

Article 15 The maritime safety administration shall manage the training of seafarers for ocean-going vessels by units in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on seafarer management.

Article 16 The competent transport department under the State Council, other relevant departments, and relevant people's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve early warning and emergency response mechanisms for overseas emergencies involving seafarers, and formulate emergency response plans for such emergencies.

The emergency response for overseas emergencies involving seafarers shall be the responsibility of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the seafarer's dispatching unit is located, with the cooperation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the seafarer's household registration is located.

The embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China in foreign countries and relevant maritime safety administrations shall assist in handling overseas emergencies involving seafarers.

Article 17 The scope of vessels to which Articles 9 through 12 and Article 14 of this Chapter apply shall be specifically stipulated by relevant laws and administrative regulations, or proposed by the competent transport department under the State Council and published after approval by the State Council.

## Chapter III: Maritime Traffic Conditions and Navigation Support

Article 18 The competent transport department under the State Council shall coordinate the planning and management of maritime traffic resources, promoting the rational development and effective utilization of maritime traffic resources.

Maritime traffic resource planning shall conform to territorial space planning.

Article 19 The maritime safety administration shall, based on the natural conditions of sea areas, maritime traffic conditions, and the needs of maritime traffic safety management, designate, adjust, and promptly publish maritime traffic functional areas such as vessel traffic separation schemes, vessel reporting systems, traffic control zones, prohibited navigation zones, safety operation zones, and outer anchorages.

When designating or adjusting vessel traffic separation schemes, outer anchorages, or safety operation zones that may affect other marine functional areas or sea-use activities, the maritime safety administration shall seek opinions from relevant departments such as fisheries administration, ecological environment, and natural resources. The designation or adjustment of prohibited navigation zones for military purposes shall be decided by the military authority responsible for such designation or adjustment, and the maritime safety administration shall publish it.

Article 20 The construction of marine engineering and coastal engineering projects that affect maritime traffic safety shall be equipped with facilities and equipment to prevent vessel collisions and set up special navigation marks as appropriate.

Article 21 The State shall establish and improve maritime traffic support service systems such as vessel positioning, navigation, timing, communication, and remote monitoring to provide information services to vessels and maritime facilities.

Article 22 No unit or individual may damage maritime traffic support service systems or hinder their operational effectiveness. If the construction of buildings or structures or the use of facilities or equipment may affect the normal operation of maritime traffic support service systems, the construction unit, owner, or user shall negotiate with the management unit of the relevant maritime traffic support service system to make appropriate arrangements.

Article 23 The competent transport department under the State Council shall take necessary measures to ensure the reasonable layout and effective coverage of maritime traffic safety radio communication facilities, plan the construction layout and site selection of maritime radio stations (including shore stations) within its system (industry), and issue vessel radio station licenses and radio identification codes.

The competent transport department under the State Council shall organize the construction of a maritime radio monitoring system within its system (industry) and monitor its radio signals, and jointly with the national radio regulatory authority maintain the order of maritime radio waves.

Article 24 When a vessel needs to use shore-based radio stations for relay communication within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, it shall do so through lawfully established domestic coastal radio stations or satellite gateway stations.

Seafarers undertaking radio communication tasks and staff of shore-based radio stations shall comply with maritime radio communication rules, maintain watch and ensure the unobstructed operation of maritime traffic safety communication channels, and shall not use maritime traffic safety communication frequencies for content unrelated to maritime traffic safety.

No unit or individual may use radio station identification codes in violation of relevant national regulations, thereby affecting identity recognition for maritime search and rescue.

Article 25 Relevant units such as astronomy, meteorology, and oceanography shall promptly forecast, broadcast, and provide information on nautical astronomy, universal time, marine meteorology, waves, currents, tides, and ice conditions.

Article 26 The competent transport department under the State Council shall uniformly plan, construct, and manage public navigation marks. If the construction unit, owner, or operator of a marine engineering or coastal engineering project needs to install or remove special navigation marks, move the position of special navigation marks, or change the light or power of navigation marks, it shall obtain approval from the maritime safety administration. If temporary navigation marks need to be installed, they shall comply with the installation points determined by the maritime safety administration.

The competent department of natural resources shall ensure land, sea, and island use for navigation mark facilities and devices in accordance with the law and handle relevant procedures for them.

The construction, maintenance, and upkeep of navigation marks shall comply with the requirements of relevant mandatory standards and technical specifications. The navigation mark maintenance unit and the owner of special navigation marks shall inspect, maintain, and upkeep the navigation marks to ensure they are in good working condition. If a navigation mark is displaced, damaged, or lost, the maintenance unit or the owner of the special navigation mark shall promptly restore it.

Article 27 Any unit or individual that discovers any of the following circumstances shall immediately report to the maritime safety administration; if the matter involves the responsibilities of the waterway management authority or special navigation marks, the maritime safety administration shall promptly notify the waterway management authority or the owner of the special navigation marks:

(1) Displacement, damage, or loss of aids to navigation or navigation facilities;
(2) The presence of sunken objects, floating objects, grounded objects, or other obstructions to navigation that hinder maritime traffic safety;
(3) Other abnormal situations that hinder maritime traffic safety.

Article 28 The maritime safety administration shall, based on the needs of maritime traffic safety management, issue navigational warnings for urgent and dangerous situations, and issue navigational notices for other situations affecting maritime traffic safety.

The maritime safety administration shall notify the naval navigation support department of navigational warnings, navigational notices, and the designation or adjustment of vessel traffic separation schemes, and promptly provide relevant data.

Article 29 The maritime safety administration shall promptly broadcast maritime traffic safety information to vessels and maritime facilities.

When vessels or maritime facilities navigate, berth, or operate within traffic separation schemes, traffic control zones, or areas with dense vessel traffic, the maritime safety administration shall provide corresponding safety information services upon their request.

Article 30 The following vessels navigating, berthing, or shifting berths within the pilotage areas designated by the competent transport department under the State Council shall apply to the pilotage authority for pilotage:

(1) Foreign-flagged vessels, except those that the competent transport department under the State Council, upon approval by the State Council, may exempt;
(2) Nuclear-powered vessels, vessels carrying radioactive substances, and ultra-large crude carriers;
(3) Bulk liquefied gas carriers and bulk dangerous chemical carriers that may endanger port safety;
(4) Vessels whose length, width, or height approaches the limits of the navigable conditions of the corresponding waterway.

The specific standards for vessels referred to in items (3) and (4) of the preceding paragraph shall be formulated and published by the relevant maritime safety administration based on the actual conditions of the port.

If a vessel voluntarily applies for pilotage, the pilotage authority shall provide pilotage services.

Article 31 The pilotage authority shall promptly dispatch pilots with corresponding capabilities and experience to provide pilotage services to vessels.

Pilots shall, according to the assignment of the pilotage authority, embark on and disembark from the vessel being piloted at designated waters, and safely and prudently perform the pilotage task. The vessel being piloted shall be equipped with compliant boarding and disembarkation devices and ensure the safety of the pilot during boarding, disembarkation, and pilotage on board.

When a pilot is piloting a vessel, the master's responsibility for commanding and managing the vessel is not relieved.

Article 32 The competent transport department under the State Council shall determine and promptly publish security levels based on the security threat situation faced by vessels, maritime facilities, and ports. Vessels, maritime facilities, and ports shall take corresponding security measures according to the security level.

## Chapter IV: Navigation, Berthing, and Operations

Article 33 When navigating, berthing, or operating, a vessel shall hold a valid vessel nationality certificate and other statutory certificates and documents, be equipped with nautical charts and publications published in accordance with relevant regulations, fly the flags of relevant countries, regions, or organizations, and display the vessel's name, identification number, port of registry, and load line marks.

A vessel shall meet the minimum safe manning requirements and be equipped with seafarers holding valid certificates.

When berthing or operating, a maritime facility shall hold statutory certificates and documents and be equipped with personnel proficient in professional skills such as collision avoidance, signaling, communication, firefighting, and lifesaving as required.

Article 34 The master shall, before the vessel's departure, check and, at the time of departure, confirm that the seafarers are competent, the vessel is seaworthy, the cargo is stowed properly, and shall be aware of meteorological and sea condition information, as well as navigational notices, navigational warnings, and other warning information issued by the maritime safety administration, implement corresponding emergency measures, and shall not risk sailing.

The owner, operator, or manager of a vessel shall not instruct or compel seafarers to operate or work in violation of regulations or at risk.

Article 35 A vessel shall navigate, berth, and operate within the navigation area specified in its vessel inspection certificate.

When navigating, berthing, or operating, a vessel shall comply with relevant navigation rules, display signals and marks in accordance with relevant regulations, and maintain sufficient under-keel clearance.

Article 36 While navigating, a vessel shall activate devices related to navigation safety, security, and pollution prevention, such as automatic identification systems, voyage data recorders, long-range identification and tracking systems, and communication equipment, in accordance with relevant regulations, and shall maintain continuous display and recording.

No unit or individual may unseal, disassemble, initialize, reset, or read recorded information from voyage data recording devices, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 37 A vessel shall be equipped with navigational records such as a deck logbook, engine logbook, and radio logbook, and shall comprehensively, truthfully, and promptly record vessel operations related to maritime traffic safety and important events during navigation, berthing, and operations, and properly keep the relevant logbooks.

Article 38 The master is responsible for managing and commanding the vessel. The master has the right to make independent decisions regarding the safety of life at sea, vessel security, and pollution prevention.

The master shall take necessary measures to protect the safety of the vessel, persons on board, vessel documents, cargo, and other property. Orders issued by the master within the scope of their authority shall be executed by seafarers, passengers, and other persons on board.

Article 39 To ensure the safety of the vessel and persons on board, the master has the right, within the scope of their duties, to take confinement or other necessary restrictive measures against persons suspected of engaging in illegal or criminal activities on board, and to prevent them from concealing, destroying, or fabricating evidence.

When taking the measures specified in the preceding paragraph, the master shall prepare a case report, signed by the master and at least two other persons on board. After a Chinese-flagged vessel arrives at a port in China, the relevant persons shall be promptly handed over to the relevant competent authorities.

Article 40 If a person on board is found to have or is suspected of having an infectious disease that seriously threatens the health of others, the master shall immediately activate the corresponding emergency plan, take necessary isolation measures for the relevant person within the scope of their duties, and promptly report to the relevant competent authorities.

Article 41 If the master dies or is unable to perform their duties during the voyage, the officer with the highest rank among the deck officers shall act as master; before the vessel departs from the next port, its owner, operator, or manager shall appoint a new master to take over.

Article 42 Seafarers shall operate and manage the vessel in accordance with relevant navigation and watchkeeping regulations, operating procedures, and the master's orders, maintain a safe watch, and shall not leave their posts without authorization. Before and during their watchkeeping duties on board, seafarers shall not consume food, medicine, or other items that may affect safe watchkeeping.

Article 43 When a vessel enters or leaves a port, anchorage, or passes through bridge area waters, straits, narrow channels, important fishing grounds, areas with dense vessel traffic, vessel traffic separation schemes, or traffic control zones, it shall maintain a proper lookout, proceed at a safe speed, and comply with the special navigation rules of those areas.

The term "important fishing grounds" referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be designated and published by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council after consulting the competent transport department under the State Council.

A vessel crossing a fairway shall not impede the normal navigation of vessels within the fairway and shall not cross ahead of another vessel. Vessels exceeding the navigable dimensions of a bridge are prohibited from entering the bridge area waters.

Article 44 A vessel shall not enter or cross a prohibited navigation zone in violation of regulations.

When a vessel enters or leaves a vessel reporting system area, it shall report its position and movement information to the maritime safety administration.Within the safe operation zone and the outer anchorage area, engaging in aquaculture, planting, fishing, or other operations or activities that affect maritime traffic safety is prohibited.

Article 45: When a vessel navigates while carrying or towing a ship, maritime facility, or other object that is extra-long, extra-high, extra-wide, or semi-submersible, it shall adopt special safety assurance measures such as reinforcing the towing parts and providing escort. Before departure, it shall report the navigation plan to the maritime administration and display signals and hang marks in accordance with relevant regulations; when towing large maritime facilities such as mobile platforms or floating docks, it shall also submit the towing survey certificate issued by the ship inspection agency for verification in accordance with the law.

Article 46: International voyage vessels entering or leaving a port shall apply to the maritime administration for permission in accordance with the law and accept the supervision and inspection of the maritime administration and other port inspection authorities. The maritime administration shall make a decision to grant or deny permission within five working days from the date of receiving the application.

Foreign vessels temporarily entering non-open waters shall obtain permission in accordance with the State Council's regulations on vessels entering and leaving ports.

Domestic voyage vessels entering or leaving a port or an off-port loading station shall report to the maritime administration information such as the vessel's voyage plan, seaworthiness status, crew manning, and passenger and cargo loading.

Article 47: Vessels shall berth at docks, berths, loading stations, anchorages, and safe operation zones that meet safety conditions. Vessel berthing shall not endanger the safety of other vessels or maritime facilities.

When vessels enter or leave a port or an off-port loading station, they shall meet the berthing conditions and the requirements for navigation conditions such as tides, weather, and sea conditions.

If an extra-long, extra-high, or extra-wide vessel, or a vessel with restricted maneuverability, entering or leaving a port or an off-port loading station may affect maritime traffic safety, the maritime administration shall verify the safety conditions for the vessel's entry and departure and may require the vessel to take corresponding safety measures such as adding tugboats or entering the port on a rising tide.

Article 48: Construction operations conducted within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be permitted by the maritime administration, and the corresponding safe operation zone shall be determined. To obtain a permit for maritime construction operations, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) The units, personnel, vessels, and facilities involved in the construction operations meet the requirements for safe navigation, berthing, and operations;
(2) A construction operation plan is in place;
(3) There are safeguard measures, emergency plans, and responsibility systems that meet the requirements for maritime traffic safety and the prevention of vessel-source pollution of the marine environment.

Vessels engaged in construction operations shall operate within the approved safe operation zone and implement maritime traffic safety management measures. Other unrelated vessels and maritime facilities shall not enter the safe operation zone.

Operations such as dredging and blasting that may endanger port safety within port waters shall be governed by the legal provisions on port management.

Article 49: Engaging in waterborne and underwater activities such as sports, entertainment, drills, sea trials, and scientific observation shall comply with maritime traffic safety management regulations; if such activities may affect maritime traffic safety, the sea area involved in the activity shall be reported to the maritime administration ten working days in advance.

Article 50: After the completion of maritime construction operations or waterborne and underwater activities, the relevant units and individuals shall promptly eliminate any hazards that may impede maritime traffic safety.

Article 51: The owner, operator, or manager of an obstruction to navigation shall promptly set up warning signs in accordance with the requirements of relevant mandatory standards and technical specifications, report the name, shape, dimensions, position, and depth of the obstruction to the maritime administration, and salvage and remove it within the time limit set by the maritime administration. The abandonment of ownership by the owner of the obstruction does not exempt them from the obligation to salvage and remove it.

If the owner, operator, or manager of the obstruction cannot be identified, the maritime administration shall organize the setting of signs, salvage, or take corresponding measures, and the costs incurred shall be included in the departmental budget.

Article 52: Under any of the following circumstances that have a significant impact on maritime traffic safety, the maritime administration shall, based on the specific situation, take corresponding traffic control measures such as suspending navigation, imposing speed limits, or designating traffic control zones, and announce them to the public:

(1) Severe weather or sea conditions;
(2) Occurrence of maritime distress or maritime traffic accidents affecting navigation;
(3) Conducting military training, exercises, or other related activities;
(4) Carrying out large-scale waterborne and underwater activities;
(5) Navigation density in a specific sea area approaching saturation;
(6) Other circumstances that have a significant impact on maritime traffic safety.

Article 53: The competent transport department of the State Council may, in order to maintain maritime traffic safety and protect the marine environment, take necessary measures jointly with relevant competent departments to prevent and stop the non-innocent passage of foreign vessels in the territorial sea.

Article 54: The following foreign vessels entering or leaving the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China shall report to the maritime administration:

(1) Submersibles;
(2) Nuclear-powered vessels;
(3) Vessels carrying radioactive substances or other toxic and harmful substances;
(4) Other vessels specified by laws, administrative regulations, or the State Council that may endanger the maritime traffic safety of the People's Republic of China.

Vessels specified in the preceding paragraph, when passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China, shall hold relevant certificates, adopt special preventive measures in compliance with the laws, administrative regulations, and rules of the People's Republic of China, and accept the instructions and supervision of the maritime administration.

Article 55: Except for obtaining permission to enter a port in accordance with this Law, foreign vessels shall not enter the internal waters of the People's Republic of China; however, they may enter in emergency situations such as serious illness of personnel, mechanical failure, distress, or seeking shelter from bad weather, without having obtained permission in advance.

If a foreign vessel enters the internal waters of the People's Republic of China due to the emergency situations specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall, at the same time as entering, make an emergency report to the maritime administration and accept the instructions and supervision of the maritime administration. The maritime administration shall promptly notify the maritime police agency with jurisdiction over the sea area, the nearest exit-entry border inspection authority, and other competent authorities such as the local public security authority and customs.

Article 56: When military vessels of the People's Republic of China are performing military missions or public service vessels are performing official duties, and in emergency situations, they may be exempt from the rules governing navigation, berthing, and operations, provided that maritime traffic safety is ensured.

## Chapter 5: Safety of Maritime Passenger and Cargo Transport

Article 57: Except for carrying out rescue or life-saving operations, passenger ships shall carry passengers according to the passenger capacity approved in the ship inspection certificate, and cargo ships shall carry cargo in accordance with the load lines and cargo types approved in the ship inspection certificate and shall not carry passengers.

Article 58: Passenger ships shall not carry dangerous goods simultaneously with passengers.

Passengers shall not carry dangerous goods specified by laws, administrative regulations, or the competent transport department of the State Council on their person or in their luggage.

Article 59: Passenger ships shall prominently display safety instructions to passengers, set up safety signs and warnings, and introduce the methods of using life-saving appliances and the emergency measures to be taken in emergency situations. Passengers shall comply with the requirements for safe boarding.

Article 60: The local people's government at or above the county level where the maritime ferry is located shall establish and improve the ferry safety management responsibility system, formulate safety management measures for the maritime ferry, supervise and guide the ferry operator to implement the main safety responsibility, maintain ferry order, and ensure ferry safety.

The ferry route of the maritime ferry shall be designated by the competent transport department of the local people's government at or above the county level where the ferry is located, in conjunction with the maritime administration. The ferry vessel shall operate safely along the designated route.

In the event of severe weather or sea conditions, the local people's government at or above the county level or its designated department shall issue a notice to suspend ferry operations.

Article 61: Vessels carrying cargo shall safely load, stow, segregate, secure, and manage the cargo in accordance with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and mandatory standards and technical specifications.

Article 62: Vessels carrying dangerous goods shall hold a valid dangerous goods suitable transport certificate, and based on the characteristics of the dangerous goods and the requirements of emergency measures, prepare a dangerous goods emergency response plan and equip corresponding fire-fighting, emergency equipment and appliances.

Article 63: The shipper consigning dangerous goods shall notify the carrier of their proper name, dangerous nature, and the protective measures to be taken, and shall properly package them in accordance with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and mandatory standards and technical specifications, and set up obvious dangerous goods marks and labels.

The shipper shall not mix dangerous goods with ordinary goods consigned for transport or falsely declare dangerous goods as ordinary goods for consignment.

If the goods consigned by the shipper are goods not listed in the International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code or the National Dangerous Goods List but have dangerous characteristics, the shipper shall also submit documents issued by relevant professional institutions indicating the dangerous characteristics of the goods and the protective measures to be taken.

The criteria for judging the dangerous characteristics of goods shall be formulated and published by the national maritime administration.

Article 64: Vessels carrying dangerous goods entering or leaving a port shall meet the following conditions, obtain permission from the maritime administration, and report to the maritime administration matters such as the time of entering and leaving the port and the duration of stay:

(1) The dangerous goods carried meet the requirements for safe maritime transport;
(2) The loading of the vessel meets the requirements of the certificates and documents it holds;
(3) The port, dock, or berth intended for berthing or dangerous goods loading/unloading operations has the business qualifications for dangerous goods operations as required by relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The maritime administration shall make a decision to grant or deny permission within twenty-four hours from the time of receiving the application.

Vessels operating on fixed routes, with fixed cargo types, and on fixed schedules may apply for permission to enter and leave the port multiple times within a certain period, not exceeding thirty days. The maritime administration shall make a decision to grant or deny permission within five working days from the date of receiving the application.

If the maritime administration grants permission, it shall notify the port administrative department.

Article 65: Vessels and maritime facilities engaged in the transport, loading/unloading, or ship-to-ship transfer of dangerous goods shall prepare an operation plan, comply with relevant mandatory standards and safe operation procedures, and take necessary preventive measures to prevent safety accidents.

For ship-to-ship transfer operations of bulk liquid dangerous goods conducted outside port waters, the following conditions shall also be met, and permission from the maritime administration and the determination of a safe operation zone are required:

(1) The vessel or maritime facility intended for the ship-to-ship transfer operation meets the requirements for maritime traffic safety and the prevention of vessel-source pollution of the marine environment;
(2) The goods intended for transfer meet the requirements for safe transfer;
(3) Personnel participating in the transfer operation possess the transfer operation capability as required by laws and administrative regulations;
(4) The intended operation waters, their seabed, and the surrounding environment are suitable for the transfer operation;
(5) The transfer operation does not pose a threat to marine resources or nearby military targets or important civilian targets;
(6) There is a safe transfer operation plan, safety assurance measures, and an emergency plan.

For vessels operating on a single voyage, the maritime administration shall make a decision to grant or deny permission within twenty-four hours from the time of receiving the application; for vessels operating on multiple voyages in a specific water area, the maritime administration shall make a decision to grant or deny permission within five working days from the date of receiving the application.

## Chapter 6: Maritime Search and Rescue

Article 66: Persons in distress at sea have the right to receive life-saving assistance in accordance with the law. Saving lives takes precedence over saving the environment and property.

Article 67: Maritime search and rescue work shall adhere to the principles of government leadership, unified command, local responsibility, combination of professional and volunteer forces, and proximity and speed.

Article 68: The State establishes a maritime search and rescue coordination mechanism to coordinate the national maritime search and rescue emergency response work, study and resolve major issues in maritime search and rescue work, and organize and coordinate major maritime search and rescue emergency operations. The coordination mechanism consists of relevant departments and units of the State Council and relevant military organs.

The China Maritime Search and Rescue Center and the maritime search and rescue centers or designated institutions established by relevant local people's governments (hereinafter collectively referred to as maritime search and rescue centers) are responsible for the organization, coordination, and command of maritime search and rescue.

Article 69: Coastal local people's governments at or above the county level shall allocate necessary funds for maritime search and rescue to ensure the normal conduct of search and rescue work.

Article 70: Each member unit of the maritime search and rescue center shall, under the unified organization, coordination, and command of the maritime search and rescue center, undertake work such as maritime search and rescue emergency response, disaster relief, support and guarantee, and post-incident handling according to their respective responsibilities.

Article 71: The State establishes professional maritime search and rescue teams to strengthen the construction of maritime search and rescue forces. Professional maritime search and rescue teams shall be equipped with professional search and rescue equipment, establish a system of regular drills and daily training, and improve search and rescue capabilities.

The State encourages social forces to establish maritime search and rescue teams and participate in maritime search and rescue operations.

Article 72: Vessels, maritime facilities, aircraft, and personnel in distress at sea shall immediately report to the maritime search and rescue center and shall not conceal or falsely report maritime distress.

If vessels, maritime facilities, aircraft, or personnel send a false distress alert signal, they shall, in addition to immediately reporting to the maritime search and rescue center, take necessary measures to eliminate the impact.

Any other unit or individual that discovers or learns of a maritime distress shall immediately report it to the maritime search and rescue center.

Article 73: Vessels and maritime facilities involved in a collision shall exchange names, nationalities, and ports of registry, and, without seriously endangering their own safety, do their utmost to rescue personnel from the other side, and shall not leave the scene of the accident or flee.

Article 74: Vessels and maritime facilities in distress, as well as their owners, operators, or managers, shall take effective measures to prevent and reduce loss of life and property and marine environmental pollution.

When a vessel is in distress, passengers shall obey the captain's command and cooperate in taking relevant emergency measures. Passengers have the right to be informed of necessary distress information.

When the captain decides to abandon ship, they shall organize passengers and crew to leave the ship in order and do their utmost to rescue the statutory navigation documents. The captain shall be the last to leave the ship.

Article 75: Vessels, maritime facilities, and aircraft that receive a distress signal or discover a person in danger of life shall, without seriously endangering their own safety, do their utmost to rescue the persons in distress.

Article 76: After receiving a distress report, the maritime search and rescue center shall immediately verify it, promptly organize, coordinate, and command government departments, professional search and rescue teams, relevant social units, and other forces to participate in the search and rescue, and designate an on-scene commander. Vessels, maritime facilities, aircraft, and personnel participating in the search and rescue shall obey the on-scene commander and promptly report the search and rescue dynamics and results.

Decisions to suspend, resume, or terminate search and rescue operations shall be made by the maritime search and rescue center. Without the consent of the maritime search and rescue center, vessels, maritime facilities, aircraft, and personnel participating in the search and rescue shall not withdraw from the search and rescue operation without authorization.

The participation of the military in maritime search and rescue shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 77: Vessels, maritime facilities, aircraft, or persons in distress shall obey the instructions of the maritime search and rescue center and the on-scene commander and promptly accept assistance.

If a vessel, maritime facility, or aircraft in distress does not cooperate with the rescue, the on-scene commander may take corresponding rescue measures based on the urgency of the distress.

Article 78: After a maritime accident or distress occurs, the relevant local people's government shall promptly organize medical institutions to provide emergency medical assistance to the persons in distress, provide necessary living support for the rescued persons, and organize relevant parties to take post-incident measures.

Article 79: Search and rescue conducted in sea areas where the People's Republic of China has assumed search and rescue obligations under international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

If a Chinese vessel encounters a distress in sea areas other than the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China and the maritime search and rescue responsibility area, the China Maritime Search and Rescue Center shall, after receiving the information, carry out international cooperation in accordance with the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter 7: Investigation and Handling of Maritime Traffic Accidents

Article 80: Vessels and maritime facilities involved in a maritime traffic accident shall promptly report to the maritime administration and accept investigation.

Article 81: Maritime traffic accidents are classified into particularly major accidents, major accidents, relatively major accidents, and general accidents based on the consequences of damage. The standards for personal injury and death in the classification of accident levels shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on production safety; the standards for direct economic losses in the classification of accident levels shall be determined by the competent transport department of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council based on the special circumstances of maritime traffic accidents, and shall be published and implemented after approval by the State Council.

Article 82: Particularly major maritime traffic accidents shall be investigated by an accident investigation team organized by the State Council or a department authorized by the State Council, and the maritime administration shall participate in or cooperate with the investigation work.

Other maritime traffic accidents shall be investigated by an accident investigation team organized by the maritime administration, with the cooperation of relevant departments. If the State Council deems it necessary, it may directly organize or authorize relevant departments to organize an accident investigation team to conduct the investigation.

When the maritime administration conducts an accident investigation, if the accident involves the execution of military transport tasks, it shall conduct the investigation jointly with the relevant military organs; if it involves fishing vessels, the fishery and fishery administration department and the maritime police agency shall participate in the investigation.

Article 83: The investigation of maritime traffic accidents shall be comprehensive, objective, fair, and timely, and shall ascertain the facts and causes of the accident and determine the responsibility for the accident in accordance with the law.

Article 84: The maritime administration may, as necessary for the investigation and handling of the accident, unseal or dismantle the voyage data recording device of the vessel involved or read the information recorded by it, require the vessel to proceed to a designated location or prohibit it from leaving port, and detain the certificates, documents, articles, and materials of the vessel or maritime facility and keep them properly. Relevant personnel shall cooperate with the accident investigation.

Article 85: The maritime traffic accident investigation team shall submit a maritime traffic accident investigation report within ninety days from the date of the accident; under special circumstances, with the approval of the head of the department responsible for organizing the accident investigation team, the deadline for submitting the accident investigation report may be appropriately extended, but the extension period shall not exceed ninety days at most. The time required for technical appraisal of the accident shall not be counted in the accident investigation period.

The maritime administration shall, within fifteen working days from the date of receiving the maritime traffic accident investigation report, issue a letter of accident responsibility determination as evidence for handling the maritime traffic accident.

If the accident losses are small, the facts are clear, and the responsibility is clear, a simplified investigation procedure may be applied in accordance with the provisions of the competent transport department of the State Council.

Maritime traffic accident investigation reports and letters of accident responsibility determination shall be made public to the society in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 86: If a Chinese vessel has a maritime traffic accident outside the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, it shall promptly report the accident situation to the maritime administration and accept investigation.

If a foreign vessel has an accident outside the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, causing serious injury or death to Chinese citizens, the maritime administration shall participate in the investigation in accordance with the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.**Article 87** If a vessel or maritime installation encounters severe weather, sea conditions, or an accident at sea that causes or may cause damage, and it is necessary to explain and record the time, sea area, and specific response measures taken, it may apply to the maritime administrative agency for the endorsement of a sea protest. The maritime administrative agency shall provide the endorsement service in accordance with regulations.

# Chapter VIII: Supervision and Administration

**Article 88** The maritime administrative agency shall, in accordance with the law, carry out supervision and inspection of activities such as navigation, berthing, operations, and other activities related to maritime traffic safety within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

The maritime administrative agency shall, in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China and the international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, carry out port state and coastal state supervision and inspection of foreign vessels.

When performing official duties, staff of the maritime administrative agency shall wear uniforms as required, display rank insignia, present law enforcement credentials, and consciously accept supervision.

When the maritime administrative agency lawfully performs its supervision and inspection duties, relevant entities and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct the lawful supervision and inspection.

**Article 89** The maritime administrative agency may adopt methods such as onboard inspection, certificate verification, on-site inspection, questioning relevant personnel, and electronic monitoring when carrying out supervision and inspection.

If a vessel carrying dangerous goods is suspected of concealing or misrepresenting the dangerous goods, the maritime administrative agency may conduct inspections such as container inspection. The maritime administrative agency shall notify the relevant departments of the results of the container inspection. Port operators and relevant entities and individuals shall provide assistance.

**Article 90** When carrying out supervision and inspection of vessels and maritime installations, the maritime administrative agency shall avoid or minimize the impact on their normal operations.

Unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations, or unless failure to carry out immediate supervision and inspection may cause serious consequences, a vessel underway shall not be intercepted for inspection.

**Article 91** If a vessel or maritime installation poses a threat to port safety, the maritime administrative agency shall order immediate or corrective action within a time limit, restrict operations, order it to proceed to a designated location, prohibit it from entering the port, or expel it from the port.

If a vessel or maritime installation is in an unseaworthy or unfit-for-tow condition, if the crew or relevant personnel on the maritime installation do not hold valid statutory certificates or documents, or if there are other hidden dangers seriously endangering maritime traffic safety or polluting the marine environment, the maritime administrative agency shall, depending on the circumstances, prohibit the relevant vessel or maritime installation from entering or leaving the port, temporarily detain the relevant certificates or documents, or order it to stop sailing, change course, proceed to a designated location, or stop operations. If a vessel is overloaded, the maritime administrative agency may, in accordance with the law, forcibly reduce its load. The costs incurred from the forced load reduction shall be borne by the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel.

If a vessel or maritime installation has been involved in a maritime traffic accident or pollution accident and has not settled the taxes, fees, and late payment penalties stipulated by the state, has not provided a guarantee, or has not fulfilled other legal obligations, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action and may prohibit it from leaving port.

**Article 92** If a foreign vessel may threaten the security of the internal waters or territorial sea of the People's Republic of China, the maritime administrative agency has the right to order it to leave.

If a foreign vessel violates the laws or administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China concerning maritime traffic safety or the prevention of pollution from vessels, the maritime administrative agency may exercise the right of hot pursuit in accordance with the law.

**Article 93** Any entity or individual has the right to report to the maritime administrative agency any act that hinders maritime traffic safety. Upon receiving a report, the maritime administrative agency shall promptly verify and handle it.

**Article 94** During supervision and inspection, if the maritime administrative agency discovers that a vessel or maritime installation has violated other laws or administrative regulations, it shall promptly notify or transfer the matter to the relevant competent authority for handling.

# Chapter IX: Legal Liability

**Article 95** If a vessel or maritime installation does not hold valid certificates or documents, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the master and relevant responsible persons; if the circumstances are serious, the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarers shall be temporarily detained for 18 to 30 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency; any forged or altered certificates or documents held by the vessel shall be confiscated; if the vessel has serious safety hazards, it may be confiscated in accordance with the law.

**Article 96** If a vessel or maritime installation falls under any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the master and relevant responsible persons; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant certificates and documents of the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel shall be revoked, and the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarers shall be temporarily detained for 12 to 24 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency:

(1) The actual condition of the vessel or maritime installation does not match the certificates or documents held;
(2) The vessel fails to fly the national flag in accordance with the law, or illegally flies the flag of another country, region, or organization;
(3) The vessel fails to display its name, ship identification number, port of registry, and load line marks as required;
(4) The manning of the vessel or maritime installation does not meet the minimum safe manning requirements.

**Article 97** If a person working on a vessel does not hold a valid seafarer's certificate of competency or seafarer's medical certificate, or if the seafarer's certificate of competency or medical certificate held does not meet the requirements, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the vessel, and a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the responsible seafarer; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed on the owner, operator, or manager of the vessel, and the seafarer's certificate of competency of the responsible seafarer shall be temporarily detained for 6 to 12 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency.

**Article 98** If a person obtains relevant certificates or documents for a Chinese vessel by improper means such as deception or bribery, the maritime administrative agency shall revoke the relevant permit, confiscate the relevant certificates or documents, and impose a fine of not less than 40,000 yuan but not more than 400,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the vessel.

If a person obtains a seafarer's certificate of competency by improper means such as deception or bribery, the maritime administrative agency shall revoke the relevant permit, confiscate the seafarer's certificate of competency, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the responsible person.

**Article 99** If a seafarer fails to maintain a safe watch, consumes food, medicine, or other items that may affect safe watch duties in violation of regulations, or commits other acts violating the maritime seafarer watchkeeping rules, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer, or temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency for 3 to 12 months; if the circumstances are serious, the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be revoked.

**Article 100** In any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) When constructing marine engineering or coastal engineering projects, failing to equip corresponding facilities and equipment to prevent vessel collisions and set up special navigation marks as required;
(2) Damaging the maritime traffic support service system or hindering its work efficiency;
(3) Setting up, removing special navigation marks, moving the position of special navigation marks, or changing other conditions such as the light or power of navigation marks without the consent of the maritime administrative agency, or setting up temporary navigation marks not in accordance with the navigation mark setting points determined by the maritime administrative agency;
(4) Engaging in aquaculture, planting, fishing, or other operations or activities affecting maritime traffic safety within the safety operation zone or the outer anchorage area.

**Article 101** In any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not more than 30,000 yuan on the relevant responsible person; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the seafarer's certificate of competency of the responsible seafarer shall be temporarily detained for 1 to 3 months:

(1) Seafarers undertaking radio communication tasks and staff of shore-based radio stations (stations) fail to maintain the watch and clear communication of maritime traffic safety communication channels, or use maritime traffic safety communication frequencies to exchange content unrelated to maritime traffic safety;
(2) Using radio station identification codes in violation of relevant state regulations, affecting identity identification for maritime search and rescue;
(3) Other acts violating maritime radio communication rules.

**Article 102** If a vessel fails to apply for pilotage in accordance with this Law, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, and a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan on the master; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant vessel certificates shall be temporarily detained for 3 to 12 months, and the master's seafarer's certificate of competency shall be temporarily detained for 1 to 3 months.

If a pilotage agency dispatches a pilot who is negligent, causing loss to the vessel, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan on the pilotage agency.

If a person provides pilotage services without being assigned by a pilotage agency, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the person piloting the vessel.

**Article 103** If a vessel is navigating, berthing, or operating at sea under any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer, and temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency for 3 to 12 months; if the circumstances are serious, the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be revoked:

(1) When entering or leaving a port, anchorage, or transiting a bridge area, strait, narrow channel, important fishing area, area with dense vessel traffic, vessel traffic separation scheme, or traffic control area, failing to maintain a proper lookout, safe speed, and comply with the special navigation rules of the aforementioned areas;
(2) Failing to display signals, hoist marks, or maintain sufficient under-keel clearance in accordance with relevant regulations;
(3) Sailing recklessly without meeting safe departure conditions, operating or working in violation of regulations and taking risks, or navigating, berthing, or operating outside the navigation area specified in the vessel's inspection certificate;
(4) Failing to activate devices related to navigation safety, security, and pollution prevention, such as automatic identification systems, voyage data recorders, long-range identification and tracking systems, and communication systems, and continuously display and record data in accordance with relevant regulations;
(5) Unauthorizedly breaking seals, disassembling, initializing, resetting voyage data recording devices, or reading recorded information;
(6) A vessel crossing a fairway obstructing the normal navigation of vessels within the fairway, crossing ahead of another vessel's bow, or entering a bridge area exceeding the bridge's navigable dimensions;
(7) A vessel entering or crossing a prohibited area in violation of regulations;
(8) A vessel carrying or towing an excessively long, high, wide, or semi-submersible vessel, maritime installation, or other object without taking special safety measures, failing to report the navigation plan to the maritime administrative agency before departure, failing to display signals or hoist marks as required, or towing a mobile platform, floating dock, or other large maritime installation without submitting the towing survey certificate issued by the vessel inspection agency as required by law;
(9) Berthing at a wharf, berth, loading/unloading station, anchorage, or safety operation zone that does not meet safety conditions, or berthing in a way that endangers the safety of other vessels or maritime installations;
(10) A vessel carrying passengers or cargo in violation of regulations exceeding the passenger capacity, load line, or cargo type specified in the inspection certificate, or a passenger ship carrying passengers while also carrying dangerous goods;
(11) A passenger ship failing to clearly inform passengers of safety instructions, set up safety signs and warnings;
(12) Failing to safely handle, stow, segregate, secure, and manage cargo in accordance with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and mandatory standards and technical specifications;
(13) Other acts violating the rules for navigation, berthing, or operation at sea.

**Article 104** If an international voyage vessel enters or leaves a port without permission, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the master, responsible seafarer, or other responsible person; if the circumstances are serious, the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be revoked.

If a domestic voyage vessel enters or leaves a port or an outport loading/unloading station without reporting to the maritime administrative agency in accordance with the law, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, and a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan on the master, responsible seafarer, or other responsible person.

**Article 105** If a vessel or maritime installation engages in maritime construction operations without permission, or operates not in accordance with the permit requirements or outside the approved safety operation zone, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer, or temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency for 6 to 12 months; if the circumstances are serious, the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be revoked.

If a person engages in underwater or above-water activities that may affect maritime traffic safety without reporting to the maritime administrative agency in advance as required, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer.

**Article 106** If the owner, operator, or manager of an obstruction to navigation falls under any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if corrective action is not taken within the time limit, the maritime administrative agency has the right to carry out substitute performance in accordance with the law, and the costs of substitute performance shall be borne by the owner, operator, or manager of the obstruction:

(1) Failing to set up warning signs promptly in accordance with the requirements of relevant mandatory standards and technical specifications;
(2) Failing to report the name, shape, dimensions, position, and depth of the obstruction to the maritime administrative agency;
(3) Failing to salvage and remove the obstruction within the time limit set by the maritime administrative agency.

**Article 107** If a foreign vessel enters the internal waters or territorial sea of the People's Republic of China in violation of this Law, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the master.

**Article 108** If a vessel carrying dangerous goods falls under any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action, impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, and a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the master, responsible seafarer, or other responsible person; if the circumstances are serious, operations or navigation shall be ordered to stop, and the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be temporarily detained for 6 to 12 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency:

(1) Entering or leaving a port or engaging in the lightering of bulk liquid dangerous goods without permission;
(2) Failing to prepare corresponding emergency response plans and equip corresponding fire-fighting, emergency equipment and appliances as required;
(3) Engaging in the loading, unloading, or lightering of dangerous goods in violation of the requirements of relevant mandatory standards and safe operating procedures.

**Article 109** If a shipper shipping dangerous goods falls under any of the following circumstances, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to notify the carrier of the proper shipping name, dangerous nature, and protective measures to be taken for the dangerous goods being shipped;
(2) Failing to properly package the dangerous goods and set up clear dangerous goods marks and labels in accordance with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and mandatory standards and technical specifications;
(3) Mixing dangerous goods with ordinary goods in a shipment or misrepresenting dangerous goods as ordinary goods for shipment;
(4) Failing to submit documents issued by relevant professional institutions indicating the dangerous characteristics of the goods and the protective measures to be taken, as required by law.

**Article 110** If a vessel or maritime installation, after being in distress or involved in a maritime traffic accident, fails to fulfill the reporting obligation, or conceals or misrepresents the situation, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer, and temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency for 6 to 24 months; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel or maritime installation, and the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer shall be revoked.

**Article 111** If a vessel flees after being involved in a maritime traffic accident, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the illegal vessel, impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the master and responsible seafarer, and revoke their seafarer's certificate of competency; the punished person shall not be allowed to reapply for it for life.

**Article 112** If a vessel or maritime installation fails to fulfill its maritime salvage obligations in accordance with the law or disobeys the command of the maritime search and rescue center, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan on the owner, operator, or manager of the vessel or maritime installation, and temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer for 6 to 12 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency.

**Article 113** If a relevant entity or individual refuses or obstructs the supervision and inspection of the maritime administrative agency, or practices fraud during the supervision and inspection, the maritime administrative agency shall impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan, and temporarily detain the seafarer's certificate of competency of the master and responsible seafarer for 6 to 24 months, up to revocation of the seafarer's certificate of competency.

**Article 114** If staff members of the competent transport department, the maritime administrative agency, or other relevant departments violate the provisions of this Law by abusing their power, neglecting their duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 115** If a civil dispute arises from a maritime traffic accident, the parties may apply for arbitration or file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law.

**Article 116** If a violation of this Law constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if it causes personal injury or property damage, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

# Chapter X: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 117** The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

"Vessel" means any type of watercraft, including non-displacement craft, WIG craft, submersibles, floating craft, and mobile platforms, as well as other mobile devices.

"Maritime installation" means various fixed or floating structures, devices, and fixed platforms above and below water, but does not include port facilities such as docks and breakwaters.

"Internal waters" means the sea areas on the landward side of the baseline of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China up to the coastline.

"Construction operations" means exploration, extraction, blasting, construction, repair, or demolition of underwater or above-water structures or facilities, waterway construction and dredging (excluding waterway maintenance dredging) operations, and salvage of sunken ships and objects.A maritime traffic accident refers to an incident occurring during the navigation, berthing, or operation of a vessel or maritime installation, resulting in casualties or property damage due to causes such as collision, grounding, striking a reef, contact, fire, wind damage, swamping, or sinking.

A maritime hazard refers to any situation that poses a threat to life safety at sea or the marine environment, requiring immediate measures to avoid, control, mitigate, and eliminate.

Dangerous goods refer to cargoes listed in the International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code and the national list of dangerous goods, which are flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, radioactive, or polluting, and which may cause personal injury, property damage, or environmental pollution during carriage by ship, requiring special protective measures.

A maritime ferry refers to a transport infrastructure dedicated to the ferry transport of persons, luggage, and vehicles between sea islands, between sea islands and the mainland, or between mainland areas separated by sea.

Article 118 Specific measures for the inspection and crew manning of public service vessels shall be separately formulated by the competent transport department of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant competent departments.

The registration and inspection measures for sports vessels shall be separately formulated by the competent sports department of the State Council. The maritime traffic safety supervision and management of sports vessels during training and competition periods shall be the responsibility of the competent sports department.

The supervision and management of fishery crew, fishery radio, and fishery navigation aids, the registration management of fishing vessels, the maritime traffic safety management within fishing port waters, and the investigation and handling of traffic accidents between fishing vessels (including foreign fishing vessels) shall be the responsibility of the competent fishery and fishery administration departments of the people's governments at or above the county level. Where laws, administrative regulations, or the State Council have other provisions on the investigation and handling of traffic accidents between fishing vessels, those provisions shall prevail.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, the maritime traffic safety management of fishing vessels shall be the responsibility of the maritime administration agency. The inspection and supervision management of fishing vessels shall be carried out by the maritime administration agency in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The inspection of offshore oil and natural gas production facilities such as floating storage units shall be subject to the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 119 The internal maritime traffic safety management of maritime military control zones and military vessels and maritime installations, the establishment and management of military navigation aids, and the management of operations or underwater and above-water activities for military purposes shall be separately formulated by the Central Military Commission.

Where the delineation or adjustment of maritime traffic functional zones or specific waters within the territorial sea, the delineation of ferry routes for maritime ferries, or the permission of maritime construction operations may affect the combat readiness, training, or duty performance of military vessels, the maritime administration agency shall consult with the relevant military authorities in advance.

Where special needs arise for the execution of military transport missions, the relevant military authorities shall promptly notify the maritime administration agency of relevant information. The maritime administration agency shall provide necessary facilitation.

Where maritime traffic safety management involves national defense transportation or the protection of military installations, the provisions of relevant laws shall apply.

Article 120 Where a foreign public service vessel navigating, berthing, or operating in the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China violates the laws or administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China, it shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The management of foreign military vessels within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be subject to the provisions of relevant laws.

Article 121 Where an international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for those clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 122 This Law shall come into force as of September 1, 2021.
