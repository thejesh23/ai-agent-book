# Counter-Espionage Law of the People's Republic of China

(Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on November 1, 2014)

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1: This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purpose of preventing, stopping, and punishing espionage activities and safeguarding national security.

Article 2: Counter-espionage work shall adhere to the principles of unified leadership by the Central Committee, the combination of open work and covert work, the integration of specialized work with the mass line, active defense, and lawful punishment.

Article 3: The state security organs are the competent authorities for counter-espionage work.

Public security, confidentiality administration, and other relevant departments, as well as the relevant departments of the military, shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, closely cooperate, strengthen coordination, and lawfully carry out relevant work.

Article 4: Citizens of the People's Republic of China have the obligation to safeguard the security, honor, and interests of the state and shall not commit acts that endanger the security, honor, and interests of the state.

All state organs, armed forces, political parties, social organizations, enterprises, and public institutions have the obligation to prevent and stop espionage activities and to safeguard national security.

State security organs must rely on the support of the people in counter-espionage work, mobilizing and organizing the people to prevent and stop espionage activities that endanger national security.

Article 5: Counter-espionage work shall be conducted in accordance with the law, respecting and safeguarding human rights, and protecting the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and organizations.

Article 6: Espionage activities carried out by foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals, or those instigated or funded by them, or those carried out by domestic institutions, organizations, or individuals in collusion with foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals, that endanger the national security of the People's Republic of China, shall all be subject to legal prosecution.

Article 7: The state shall protect organizations and individuals that support or assist counter-espionage work, and shall reward those who make significant contributions.

## Chapter II: Functions and Powers of State Security Organs in Counter-Espionage Work

Article 8: State security organs shall, in accordance with the law, exercise the powers of investigation, detention, preliminary interrogation, execution of arrest, and other functions and powers as provided by law in counter-espionage work.

Article 9: When carrying out tasks in accordance with the law, personnel of state security organs shall, upon presenting the relevant identification documents as prescribed, have the authority to examine the identity documents of Chinese citizens or foreign nationals, and to investigate and inquire about relevant information from relevant organizations and individuals.

Article 10: When carrying out tasks in accordance with the law, personnel of state security organs may, upon presenting the relevant identification documents as prescribed, enter relevant premises and units; in accordance with relevant state regulations, upon approval and presentation of the relevant identification documents, they may enter restricted areas, premises, and units, and inspect or obtain relevant archives, materials, and articles.

Article 11: When carrying out urgent tasks in accordance with the law, personnel of state security organs may, upon presenting the relevant identification documents, have priority in using public transportation, and when encountering traffic obstructions, have the right of way.

Where necessary for counter-espionage work, state security organs may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, have priority in using or lawfully requisition means of transportation, communication tools, premises, and buildings of state organs, organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and individuals. When necessary, they may set up relevant work sites and equipment or facilities. After the tasks are completed, they shall promptly return or restore the items to their original condition and pay the corresponding fees as prescribed; if losses are caused, compensation shall be made.

Article 12: Where necessary for investigating espionage activities, state security organs may, in accordance with relevant state regulations and through strict approval procedures, adopt technical investigation measures.

Article 13: Where necessary for counter-espionage work, state security organs may, in accordance with regulations, inspect electronic communication tools, equipment, and facilities of relevant organizations and individuals. If, during the inspection, circumstances endangering national security are found, the state security organ shall order rectification; if the party refuses to rectify or still fails to meet the requirements after rectification, the equipment or facilities may be sealed up or seized.

With respect to the equipment or facilities sealed up or seized in accordance with the preceding paragraph, once the circumstances endangering national security are eliminated, the state security organ shall promptly lift the sealing or seizure.

Article 14: Where necessary for counter-espionage work, state security organs may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, request customs, border control, and other inspection authorities to exempt relevant personnel, materials, and equipment from inspection. The relevant inspection authorities shall provide assistance.

Article 15: State security organs may, with the approval of the responsible person of a state security organ at or above the level of a city divided into districts, lawfully seal up, seize, or freeze tools and other property used for espionage activities, as well as funds, premises, and materials used to fund espionage activities.

Article 16: Where necessary for counter-espionage work, state security organs may, in conjunction with relevant departments, formulate technical prevention standards for counter-espionage, guide relevant departments in implementing technical prevention measures against espionage, and, with respect to departments with potential risks, may, through strict approval procedures, conduct technical prevention inspections and tests for counter-espionage.

Article 17: State security organs and their personnel shall strictly act in accordance with the law in their work, shall not exceed their authority or abuse their power, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of organizations and individuals.

Information and materials obtained by state security organs and their personnel in the lawful performance of their counter-espionage duties may only be used for counter-espionage work. Information classified as state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy shall be kept confidential.

Article 18: The lawful performance of duties by personnel of state security organs is protected by law.

## Chapter III: Obligations and Rights of Citizens and Organizations

Article 19: State organs, organizations, and other entities shall conduct education on safeguarding national security among their personnel, and mobilize and organize their personnel to prevent and stop espionage activities.

Article 20: Citizens and organizations shall provide convenience or other assistance for counter-espionage work.

If the personal safety of an individual or their close relatives is endangered due to assisting in counter-espionage work, they may request protection from the state security organ. The state security organ shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, take protective measures in accordance with the law.

Article 21: Upon discovering espionage activities, citizens and organizations shall promptly report them to the state security organ; if reported to other state organs or organizations such as public security organs, the relevant state organ or organization shall immediately transfer the matter to the state security organ for handling.

Article 22: When state security organs investigate and understand circumstances related to espionage activities and collect relevant evidence, the relevant organizations and individuals shall truthfully provide information and shall not refuse.

Article 23: All citizens and organizations shall keep confidential the state secrets concerning counter-espionage work that they have come to know.

Article 24: No individual or organization shall illegally hold documents, materials, or other items that are state secrets.

Article 25: No individual or organization shall illegally hold or use specialized espionage equipment specially needed for espionage activities. Specialized espionage equipment shall be confirmed by the competent state security department of the State Council in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 26: Any individual or organization has the right to report or accuse state security organs and their personnel of exceeding their authority, abusing their power, or other illegal acts to the higher-level state security organ or relevant departments. The state security organ or relevant department that receives the report or accusation shall promptly ascertain the facts, handle the matter responsibly, and promptly inform the complainant or accuser of the outcome.

No individual or organization shall suppress or retaliate against individuals or organizations that assist state security organs in their work or lawfully make reports or accusations.

## Chapter IV: Legal Liability

Article 27: If foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals carry out espionage activities, or instigate or fund others to do so, or if domestic institutions, organizations, or individuals carry out espionage activities in collusion with foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals, and such acts constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

If a person who commits espionage activities voluntarily surrenders or performs meritorious service, the punishment may be mitigated or remitted, or the person may be exempted from punishment; if the person performs major meritorious service, a reward shall be given.

Article 28: If a person, while abroad, is coerced or induced to join a hostile organization or espionage organization and engage in activities endangering the national security of the People's Republic of China, but promptly and truthfully explains the situation to the diplomatic mission of the People's Republic of China abroad, or after entering the country, promptly and truthfully explains the situation to the state security organ or public security organ, either directly or through their unit, and shows repentance, the person may not be prosecuted.

Article 29: If a person, knowing that another person has committed espionage crimes, refuses to provide information when the state security organ investigates the relevant circumstances and collects relevant evidence, the person shall be subject to disciplinary action by their unit or the competent higher authority, or be subject to administrative detention of up to 15 days by the state security organ; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 30: Anyone who obstructs state security organs from lawfully carrying out their tasks by means of violence or threats shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Anyone who intentionally obstructs state security organs from lawfully carrying out their tasks without using violence or threats, causing serious consequences, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are relatively minor, the person shall be subject to administrative detention of up to 15 days by the state security organ.

Article 31: Anyone who divulges state secrets concerning counter-espionage work shall be subject to administrative detention of up to 15 days by the state security organ; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 32: With respect to anyone who illegally holds documents, materials, or other items that are state secrets, or illegally holds or uses specialized espionage equipment, the state security organ may lawfully search their person, articles, residence, and other relevant places; the illegally held documents, materials, or other items that are state secrets, and the illegally held or used specialized espionage equipment, shall be confiscated. If the illegal holding of documents, materials, or other items that are state secrets constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, the person shall be given a warning or be subject to administrative detention of up to 15 days by the state security organ.

Article 33: Anyone who conceals, transfers, sells, or damages property lawfully sealed up, seized, or frozen by the state security organ, or who harbors, transfers, purchases, sells on commission, or otherwise conceals or disguises property involved in espionage activities knowing it to be such, shall have the property recovered by the state security organ. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 34: Foreign nationals who violate this Law may be ordered to leave the country within a specified time limit or be deported.

Article 35: If a party is dissatisfied with an administrative penalty decision or a decision on compulsory administrative measures, they may, within 60 days from the date of receiving the decision, apply for reconsideration to the organ at the next higher level that made the decision; if dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, they may, within 15 days from the date of receiving the reconsideration decision, file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

Article 36: State security organs shall properly keep property sealed up, seized, or frozen in accordance with this Law, and shall handle it according to the following circumstances:

(1) If suspected of a crime, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law;

(2) If it does not constitute a crime but involves illegal facts, items that should be confiscated according to law shall be confiscated, and items that should be destroyed according to law shall be destroyed;

(3) If there are no illegal facts, or if the property is unrelated to the case, the sealing, seizure, or freezing shall be lifted, and the relevant property shall be promptly returned; if losses are caused, compensation shall be made according to law.

All property confiscated by state security organs shall be turned over to the state treasury.

Article 37: If personnel of state security organs abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, constituting a crime, or if they engage in acts such as illegal detention, extorting confessions by torture, obtaining evidence by violence, illegally divulging state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy in violation of regulations, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V: Supplementary Provisions

Article 38: For the purposes of this Law, "espionage activities" refer to the following acts:

(1) Activities carried out by espionage organizations or their agents, or instigated or funded by them, or activities carried out by domestic or foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals in collusion with them, that endanger the national security of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Joining an espionage organization or accepting tasks assigned by an espionage organization or its agents;

(3) Activities carried out by foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals other than espionage organizations or their agents, or instigated or funded by them, or activities carried out by domestic institutions, organizations, or individuals in collusion with them, involving stealing, spying, buying, or illegally providing state secrets or intelligence, or instigating, enticing, or buying over state functionaries to defect;

(4) Designating targets for attack for the enemy;

(5) Engaging in other espionage activities.

Article 39: Where state security organs and public security organs perform their duties of preventing, stopping, and punishing other acts endangering national security besides espionage activities in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions, the relevant provisions of this Law shall apply.

Article 40: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation. The "Law of the People's Republic of China on Safeguarding National Security" adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 7th National People's Congress on February 22, 1993, shall be simultaneously abolished.
