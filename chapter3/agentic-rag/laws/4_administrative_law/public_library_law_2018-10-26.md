# Public Library Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of promoting the development of public library undertakings, giving full play to the functions of public libraries, safeguarding the basic cultural rights and interests of citizens, improving the scientific and cultural quality of citizens and the level of social civility, inheriting human civilization, and strengthening cultural confidence.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "public library" means a public cultural facility that is open to the public free of charge, collects, arranges, preserves document and information, provides inquiry, borrowing and related services, and carries out social education.

The document and information mentioned in the preceding paragraph include books, newspapers, periodicals, audio-visual products, microforms, digital resources, etc.

Article 3 Public libraries are an important part of the socialist public cultural service system and shall take promoting, guiding, and serving nationwide reading as an important task.

Public libraries shall adhere to the direction of advanced socialist culture, adhere to a people-centered approach, adhere to the guidance of the core socialist values, inherit and develop the excellent traditional Chinese culture, inherit revolutionary culture, and develop advanced socialist culture.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the public library undertaking into the national economic and social development plan at the corresponding level, incorporate the construction of public libraries into urban and rural planning and the overall plan for land use, increase investment in public libraries established by the government, include the necessary funds in the government budget at the corresponding level, and allocate them in a timely and full manner.

The State encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to raise funds to establish public libraries. People's governments at or above the county level shall actively mobilize social forces to participate in the construction of public libraries and provide policy support in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 5 The competent department of culture under the State Council shall be responsible for the administration of public libraries nationwide. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for work related to the administration of public libraries within their respective functions and duties.

The competent department of culture of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the administration of public libraries within its administrative region. Other relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for work related to the administration of public libraries within their respective functions and duties within their administrative regions.

Article 6 The State encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to make donations to public libraries in accordance with the law and grants tax preferences in accordance with the law.

Foreign natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations may participate in the construction of public libraries within China through donations in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 7 The State supports the development of public library undertakings in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 8 The State encourages and supports the application of science and technology in the construction, management, and service of public libraries, and promotes the use of modern information technology and communication technology to improve the service efficiency of public libraries.

Article 9 The State encourages and supports international exchanges and cooperation in the field of public libraries.

Article 10 Public libraries shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on the protection of intellectual property rights, and protect and use document and information in accordance with the law.

If the collected document and information are cultural relics, archives, or State secrets, the public library shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on the protection of cultural relics, archives management, or the protection of State secrets.

Article 11 Industry organizations of public libraries shall formulate industry norms in accordance with the law, strengthen industry self-discipline, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of their members, and guide and supervise their members to improve service quality.

Article 12 Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of public library undertakings shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II Establishment

Article 13 The State shall establish a public library service network that covers urban and rural areas and is convenient and practical. The construction of the public library service network shall adhere to government leadership and encourage social participation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on factors such as the population size, population distribution, environmental and traffic conditions within their administrative regions, determine the number, scale, structure, and distribution of public libraries according to local conditions, and strengthen the construction of fixed library buildings, mobile service facilities, and self-service facilities.

Article 14 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish public libraries.

Local people's governments shall make full use of the comprehensive service facilities of townships (subdistricts) and villages (communities) to set up reading rooms to serve urban and rural residents.

Article 15 The establishment of a public library shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having articles of association;
(2) Having a fixed library site;
(3) Having a library building area, reading seats, document and information, facilities and equipment commensurate with its functions;
(4) Having staff commensurate with its functions, collection size, etc.;
(5) Having necessary funds for library establishment and a stable source of operating funds;
(6) Having safety guarantee facilities, systems, and emergency plans.

Article 16 The articles of association of a public library shall include matters such as its name, site, purpose, business scope, management system and relevant rules, termination procedures, and the plan for disposing of residual property.

Article 17 The establishment, alteration, and termination of a public library shall go through registration procedures in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 18 The competent department of culture of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall promptly publish on its website information such as the names, sites, contact information, overview of collected document and information, main service contents and methods of public libraries within its administrative region.

Article 19 The director of a public library established by the government shall possess the appropriate educational level, professional knowledge, and organizational management skills.

A public library shall be staffed with personnel commensurate with its functions, collection size, library building area, service scope, and service population. The staff of a public library shall possess the appropriate professional knowledge and skills, and professional and technical personnel may be evaluated for professional and technical titles in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 20 A public library may name a special collection of document and information or a thematic activity after the name of a donor.

A public library established by a citizen, legal person, or other organization may name the public library, its building, or other facilities after the name of a donor.

Naming after a donor's name shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, conform to national interests and social public interests, and follow public order and good customs.

Article 21 Upon the termination of a public library, its residual property shall be disposed of in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 22 The State establishes the National Library, which mainly undertakes functions such as the strategic preservation of national document and information, the compilation of national bibliographies and union catalogs, serving national legislation and decision-making, organizing the national protection of ancient books, conducting research on library development and international exchanges, and providing professional guidance and technical support to other libraries. The National Library also has the functions of a public library as stipulated in this Law.

## Chapter III Operation

Article 23 The State promotes the establishment and improvement of a corporate governance structure in public libraries, absorbing representatives from relevant parties, professionals, and the public to participate in management.

Article 24 A public library shall extensively collect document and information based on its purpose and the needs of its service targets; a public library established by the government shall also systematically collect local document and information to preserve and inherit local culture.

The collection of document and information shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 25 A public library may collect document and information through legal means such as procurement, acceptance of deposits, or donations.

Article 26 Publishing units shall deposit official publications with the National Library and the provincial-level public library in their locality in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 27 A public library shall organize its collected document and information in accordance with the standards and specifications published by the State, establish a catalog of its collected document and information, and make it public to society through its website or other means in accordance with the law.

Article 28 A public library shall properly preserve its collected document and information and shall not dispose of it arbitrarily; if disposal is truly necessary, it shall comply with the regulations of the competent department of culture under the State Council on the disposal of document and information.

A public library shall be equipped with fire prevention, theft prevention, and other facilities, and shall take special protection measures for ancient books and other precious and fragile document and information in accordance with relevant State regulations and standards to ensure safety.

Article 29 A public library shall regularly inspect and maintain its facilities and equipment to ensure normal operation.

The facilities, equipment, and premises of a public library shall not be used for commercial business activities unrelated to its services.

Article 30 Public libraries shall strengthen inter-library exchanges and cooperation. The State supports public libraries in carrying out joint procurement, joint cataloging, and joint services to achieve the co-construction and sharing of document and information and promote the effective use of document and information.

Article 31 The people's government at the county level shall, according to local conditions, establish a central-branch library system suitable for local characteristics, with the county-level public library as the central library and township (subdistrict) comprehensive cultural stations, village (community) reading rooms, etc., as branch libraries or grassroots service points, improve the digital and networked service system and distribution system, realize interlibrary borrowing and returning, and promote the extension of public library services to urban and rural grassroots. The central library shall strengthen professional guidance to branch libraries and grassroots service points.

Article 32 If the document and information collected by a public library are archives or cultural relics, the public library may exchange duplicates, copies, or catalogs with archives, museums, memorial halls, and other units, jointly hold exhibitions, jointly edit and publish relevant historical materials, or conduct research on historical materials.

## Chapter IV Services

Article 33 A public library shall provide services to the public in accordance with the requirements of equality, openness, and sharing.

A public library shall provide the following services to the public free of charge:

(1) Inquiry and borrowing of document and information;
(2) Opening of public spaces and facilities such as reading rooms and study rooms;
(3) Public welfare lectures, reading promotion, training, and exhibitions;
(4) Other free service items stipulated by the State.

Article 34 A public library established by the government shall set up a reading area for children and adolescents, equip it with corresponding professional personnel based on the characteristics of children and adolescents, carry out reading guidance and social education activities for children and adolescents, and support schools in carrying out relevant extracurricular activities. Areas with conditions may establish separate children's libraries.

A public library established by the government shall consider the characteristics of groups such as the elderly and persons with disabilities, actively create conditions, and provide document and information, barrier-free facilities and equipment, and services suitable for their needs.

Article 35 A public library established by the government shall, based on its own conditions, provide document and information and related consulting services for State organs in formulating laws, regulations, and policies and conducting research on related issues.

Article 36 A public library shall promote nationwide reading by carrying out activities such as reading guidance, reading exchanges, speeches and recitations, and book exchange and sharing.

Article 37 When providing document and information to the public, a public library shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations and shall not provide document and information with inappropriate content to minors.

A public library shall not engage in, or allow other organizations or individuals to engage in, activities within the library that endanger national security, harm social public interests, or violate other laws and regulations.

Article 38 A public library shall announce to the public through its website or other means its service contents, opening hours, borrowing rules, etc.; if it is closed for some reason or the opening hours are changed, except in the case of force majeure, it shall announce this in advance.

A public library shall be open on public holidays and shall have opening hours during statutory national holidays.

Article 39 A public library established by the government shall provide convenient services to the public through mobile service facilities, self-service facilities, etc.

Article 40 The State shall build a unified standard, interconnected digital service network for public libraries, support the development of digital reading products and research on digital resource preservation technology, and promote the use of digital and networked technologies by public libraries to provide convenient services to the public.

A public library established by the government shall strengthen the construction of digital resources, equip corresponding facilities and equipment, establish an online and offline integrated document and information sharing platform, and provide high-quality services to the public.

Article 41 A public library established by the government shall strengthen the protection of ancient books within the library, promote the arrangement, publication, research, and utilization of ancient books using digitalization, photocopying, or microform technology based on its own conditions, and strengthen the promotion of ancient books through methods such as touring exhibitions, public welfare lectures, reproduction of rare books, and development of creative products, to inherit and develop the excellent traditional Chinese culture.

Article 42 A public library shall improve service conditions and service levels, regularly announce the status of service provision, listen to readers' opinions, establish complaint channels, improve feedback mechanisms, and accept social supervision.

Article 43 A public library shall properly protect readers' personal information, borrowing information, and other information that may involve readers' privacy, and shall not sell or illegally provide it to others by other means.

Article 44 Readers shall comply with the relevant regulations of the public library, consciously maintain the order of the public library, take care of the document and information, facilities, and equipment of the public library, and legally use document and information; if borrowing document and information, they shall return it within the prescribed time limit.

If a reader damages the document and information, facilities, or equipment of a public library, or disrupts the order of a public library, the staff of the public library have the right to dissuade and stop them; if dissuasion and stopping are ineffective, the public library may stop providing services to them.

Article 45 The State adopts measures such as government procurement of services to support the provision of services by public libraries established by citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 46 The State encourages citizens to participate in volunteer services in public libraries. The competent department of culture of the people's government at or above the county level shall provide necessary guidance and support for volunteer services in public libraries.

Article 47 The competent department of culture under the State Council and the competent departments of culture of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate service standards for public libraries and assess the service quality and level of public libraries. The assessment shall involve public participation. The assessment results shall be made public and serve as the basis for granting subsidies or rewards to public libraries.

Article 48 The State supports public libraries in strengthening exchanges and cooperation with school libraries, scientific research institution libraries, and other types of libraries, and carrying out joint services.

The State supports school libraries, scientific research institution libraries, and other types of libraries in opening to the public.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 49 If a public library engages in, or allows other organizations or individuals to engage in, activities within the library that endanger national security or harm social public interests, the competent department of culture shall order it to make corrections and confiscate the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, it may order it to suspend operations for rectification or close it down; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 50 If a public library or its staff commits any of the following acts, the competent department of culture shall order it to make corrections and confiscate the illegal gains:

(1) Illegally disposing of document and information;
(2) Selling or illegally providing to others by other means readers' personal information, borrowing information, and other information that may involve readers' privacy;
(3) Providing document and information to the public in violation of the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, or providing document and information with inappropriate content to minors;
(4) Using facilities, equipment, and premises for commercial business activities unrelated to public library services;
(5) Other acts of failing to fulfill the public library service requirements stipulated in this Law.

If a public library or its staff charges fees or charges disguised fees for services that should be provided free of charge, the competent price department shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If a public library or its staff commits the acts specified in the preceding two paragraphs, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 51 If a publishing unit fails to deposit official publications in accordance with relevant State regulations, the competent publishing department shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on publishing management.

Article 52 If the competent department of culture or other relevant departments and their staff abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in the administration of public libraries, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Anyone who damages the document and information, facilities, or equipment of a public library or fails to return borrowed document and information within the prescribed time limit, causing property loss or other damage, shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes an act against public security administration shall be given a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 55 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2018.
