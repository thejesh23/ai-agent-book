# Urban Real Estate Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 8th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 5, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Urban Real Estate Administration Law of the People's Republic of China at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 30, 2007

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China and the Urban Real Estate Administration Law of the People's Republic of China at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 26, 2019

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to strengthening the administration of urban real estate, maintaining the order of the real estate market, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of real estate right holders, and promoting the healthy development of the real estate industry.

Article 2 This Law shall be observed in obtaining the right to the use of state-owned land (hereinafter referred to as "state-owned land") within the urban planning areas of the People's Republic of China for real estate development, engaging in real estate development and real estate transactions, and carrying out real estate administration.

For the purposes of this Law, "houses" refer to buildings and structures such as houses on land.

For the purposes of this Law, "real estate development" refers to the act of carrying out infrastructure construction and house building on land for which the right to the use of state-owned land has been obtained in accordance with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "real estate transactions" include the transfer of real estate, the mortgage of real estate, and the lease of houses.

Article 3 The State shall implement a system of compensated and time-limited use of state-owned land according to law, except where the State allocates the right to the use of state-owned land within the scope provided for in this Law.

Article 4 The State shall, in light of the social and economic development level, support and promote the construction of residential housing and gradually improve the housing conditions of residents.

Article 5 Real estate right holders shall abide by laws and administrative regulations and pay taxes according to law. The legitimate rights and interests of real estate right holders shall be protected by law, and no unit or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 6 For the needs of the public interest, the State may expropriate houses owned by units and individuals on state-owned land, and shall provide demolition compensation according to law to protect the legitimate rights and interests of the expropriated persons; where individual residential houses are expropriated, the housing conditions of the expropriated persons shall also be guaranteed. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 7 The competent administrative department of construction and the land administration department of the State Council shall, in accordance with the division of functions and powers prescribed by the State Council, perform their respective duties, closely cooperate, and administer the national real estate work.

The institutional setup and functions of the real estate administration departments and land administration departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter II Land for Real Estate Development

### Section 1 Grant of Land Use Rights

Article 8 The grant of land use rights refers to the act by which the State grants the right to the use of state-owned land (hereinafter referred to as "land use rights") to a land user for a certain number of years, and the land user pays the State a grant premium for the land use rights.

Article 9 The right to the use of collectively owned land within urban planning areas may be granted for compensation only after the land has been expropriated and converted into state-owned land according to law, except as otherwise provided by law.

Article 10 The grant of land use rights must conform to the overall plan for land utilization, the urban plan, and the annual plan for land used for construction.

Article 11 Where a local people's government at or above the county level grants land use rights for real estate development, it shall, based on the control targets issued by the people's government at or above the provincial level, formulate a plan for the total area of land use rights to be granted annually, and submit it for approval to the State Council or the people's government at the provincial level in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Article 12 The grant of land use rights shall be carried out by the people's governments of cities and counties in a planned and step-by-step manner. The land administration department of the people's government of a city or county shall, together with the departments of urban planning, construction, and real estate administration, formulate a plan for each parcel of land to be granted, including its purpose, term, and other conditions, and after approval by the people's government with the authority to approve in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, the land administration department of the people's government of the city or county shall implement it.

The limits of authority of the people's government of a county under a municipality directly under the Central Government and its relevant departments in exercising the powers provided for in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the people's government of the municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 13 The grant of land use rights may be conducted by auction, tender, or agreement between the two parties.

For land used for commercial, tourism, entertainment, and luxury residential purposes, where conditions permit, auction or tender must be adopted; where conditions do not permit the adoption of auction or tender, the method of agreement between the two parties may be adopted.

The grant premium for land use rights granted by the method of agreement between the two parties shall not be lower than the minimum price determined in accordance with State regulations.

Article 14 The maximum term for the grant of land use rights shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 15 A written grant contract shall be signed for the grant of land use rights.

The grant contract for land use rights shall be signed between the land administration department of the people's government of a city or county and the land user.

Article 16 A land user must pay the grant premium for land use rights in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract; if the land user fails to pay the grant premium for land use rights in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract, the land administration department has the right to rescind the contract and may claim compensation for breach of contract.

Article 17 Where a land user has paid the grant premium for land use rights in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract, the land administration department of the people's government of the city or county must provide the granted land in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract; if the granted land is not provided in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract, the land user has the right to rescind the contract, the land administration department shall return the grant premium for land use rights, and the land user may claim compensation for breach of contract.

Article 18 Where a land user needs to change the land use purpose agreed upon in the grant contract for land use rights, the land user must obtain the consent of the grantor and the competent administrative department of urban planning of the people's government of the city or county, and sign an agreement to amend the grant contract for land use rights or sign a new grant contract for land use rights, with a corresponding adjustment to the grant premium for land use rights.

Article 19 The grant premium for land use rights shall be turned over to the finance in full, included in the budget, and used for urban infrastructure construction and land development. Specific measures for the turnover and use of the grant premium for land use rights shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 20 The State shall not recover the land use rights lawfully obtained by a land user before the expiration of the term agreed upon in the grant contract; under special circumstances, for the needs of the public interest, the land use rights may be recovered before the expiration of the term in accordance with legal procedures, and corresponding compensation shall be given based on the actual period of use of the land by the land user and the actual circumstances of the land development.

Article 21 Land use rights shall terminate upon the loss of the land.

Article 22 Where the term agreed upon in the grant contract for land use rights expires and the land user needs to continue using the land, the land user shall apply for an extension no later than one year before the expiration of the term. Except where the land is to be recovered for the needs of the public interest, the application shall be approved. Where an extension is approved, a new grant contract for land use rights shall be signed, and the grant premium for land use rights shall be paid in accordance with the provisions.

Where the term agreed upon in the grant contract for land use rights expires and the land user does not apply for an extension or, although an application is made, it is not approved in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the land use rights shall be recovered by the State without compensation.

### Section 2 Allocation of Land Use Rights

Article 23 The allocation of land use rights refers to the act by which the people's government at or above the county level, upon approval according to law, delivers the land to the land user for use after the land user pays the compensation, resettlement, and other fees, or delivers the land use rights to the land user for use without compensation.

Where land use rights are obtained by allocation in accordance with the provisions of this Law, there is no restriction on the term of use, except as otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 24 The land use rights for the following construction land, if truly necessary, may be allocated upon approval by the people's government at or above the county level according to law:

(1) Land for state organs and military purposes;

(2) Land for urban infrastructure and public welfare undertakings;

(3) Land for projects such as energy, transportation, and water conservancy that are given priority support by the State;

(4) Other land as provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter III Real Estate Development

Article 25 Real estate development must strictly implement the urban plan, adhere to the principle of unifying economic, social, and environmental benefits, and carry out comprehensive planning, rational layout, integrated development, and supporting construction.

Article 26 Where land use rights are obtained by grant for real estate development, the land must be developed in accordance with the land use purpose and the commencement and development period agreed upon in the grant contract for land use rights. If development has not commenced one year after the date for commencement of development agreed upon in the grant contract, a land idle fee of not more than 20% of the grant premium for land use rights may be levied; if development has not commenced two years after that date, the land use rights may be recovered without compensation, except where the delay in commencement of development is caused by force majeure, actions of the government or relevant government departments, or preliminary work necessary for the commencement of development.

Article 27 The design and construction of real estate development projects must comply with the relevant national standards and specifications.

A real estate development project may be delivered for use only after it has been completed and passed inspection.

Article 28 Lawfully obtained land use rights may, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, be contributed as capital contributions, or used for joint venture or cooperative development and operation of real estate.

Article 29 The State shall adopt preferential measures in taxation and other aspects to encourage and support real estate development enterprises in developing and constructing residential housing.

Article 30 A real estate development enterprise is an enterprise that engages in real estate development and operation for profit. The establishment of a real estate development enterprise shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having its own name and organizational structure;

(2) Having a fixed place of business;

(3) Having a registered capital that meets the requirements prescribed by the State Council;

(4) Having sufficient professional and technical personnel;

(5) Other conditions as provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

To establish a real estate development enterprise, an application for registration of establishment shall be filed with the administrative department for industry and commerce. The administrative department for industry and commerce shall register and issue a business license to those that meet the conditions prescribed by this Law; it shall not register those that do not meet the conditions prescribed by this Law.

Where a limited liability company or a company limited by shares is established to engage in real estate development and operation, the relevant provisions of the Company Law shall also be complied with.

Within one month after receiving the business license, a real estate development enterprise shall file a record with the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level at the place where the registration authority is located.

Article 31 The ratio of the registered capital to the total investment of a real estate development enterprise shall comply with the relevant State regulations.

Where a real estate development enterprise develops real estate in phases, the investment amount for each phase shall be commensurate with the scale of the project, and funds shall be invested on schedule in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract for land use rights for project construction.

## Chapter IV Real Estate Transactions

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 32 Upon the transfer or mortgage of real estate, the ownership of the house and the right to the use of the land within the scope of the house shall be transferred or mortgaged simultaneously.

Article 33 The base land price, the standard land price, and the replacement prices of various types of houses shall be determined and published periodically. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 34 The State shall implement a system of real estate price assessment.

Real estate price assessment shall follow the principles of fairness, impartiality, and openness, be based on the technical standards and assessment procedures prescribed by the State, take the base land price, the standard land price, and the replacement prices of various types of houses as the basis, and be carried out with reference to the local market prices.

Article 35 The State shall implement a system of declaration of real estate transaction prices.

When a real estate right holder transfers real estate, the real estate right holder shall truthfully declare the transaction price to the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and shall not conceal or make a false declaration.

Article 36 For the transfer or mortgage of real estate, the parties concerned shall handle the registration of property rights in accordance with the provisions of Chapter V of this Law.

### Section 2 Transfer of Real Estate

Article 37 The transfer of real estate refers to the act by which a real estate right holder transfers the real estate to another person through sale, gift, or other lawful means.

Article 38 The following real estate shall not be transferred:

(1) Where the land use rights are obtained by grant and the conditions prescribed in Article 39 of this Law are not met;

(2) Where the real estate rights are restricted by a ruling or decision of a judicial organ or administrative organ to be sealed up or otherwise restricted according to law;

(3) Where the land use rights have been recovered according to law;

(4) Where the real estate is co-owned and the written consent of the other co-owners has not been obtained;

(5) Where there is a dispute over the property rights;

(6) Where the property rights certificate has not been obtained through registration according to law;

(7) Other circumstances where transfer is prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 39 Where land use rights are obtained by grant, the following conditions shall be met when transferring the real estate:

(1) The full grant premium for land use rights has been paid in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract, and the certificate for the land use rights has been obtained;

(2) Investment and development have been carried out in accordance with the provisions of the grant contract; for house construction projects, the completed development investment accounts for more than 25% of the total investment in development; for land developed in tracts, conditions for industrial land or other construction land have been formed.

Where the house has already been built at the time of the transfer of the real estate, the certificate of ownership of the house shall also be held.

Article 40 Where land use rights are obtained by allocation, the transfer of the real estate shall be submitted for approval to the people's government with the authority to approve in accordance with the provisions of the State Council. Where the people's government with the authority to approve approves the transfer, the transferee shall go through the procedures for the grant of land use rights and pay the grant premium for land use rights in accordance with the relevant State regulations.

Where land use rights are obtained by allocation, and when submitting the transfer of the real estate for approval, if the people's government with the authority to approve decides, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, that the procedures for the grant of land use rights need not be gone through, the transferor shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, turn over to the State the land proceeds from the income obtained from the transfer of the real estate or handle them otherwise.

Article 41 A written transfer contract shall be signed for the transfer of real estate, and the contract shall specify the method by which the land use rights were obtained.

Article 42 Upon the transfer of real estate, the rights and obligations specified in the grant contract for land use rights shall be transferred accordingly.

Article 43 Where land use rights are obtained by grant, after the transfer of the real estate, the term of use of the land use rights shall be the remaining term after deducting the term already used by the original land user from the term agreed upon in the original grant contract for land use rights.

Article 44 Where land use rights are obtained by grant, after the transfer of the real estate, if the transferee changes the land use purpose agreed upon in the original grant contract for land use rights, the transferee must obtain the consent of the original grantor and the competent administrative department of urban planning of the people's government of the city or county, and sign an agreement to amend the grant contract for land use rights or sign a new grant contract for land use rights, with a corresponding adjustment to the grant premium for land use rights.

Article 45 The pre-sale of commercial housing shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The full grant premium for land use rights has been paid, and the certificate for the land use rights has been obtained;

(2) A permit for a construction project planning has been held;

(3) Based on the commercial housing provided for pre-sale, the funds invested in development and construction account for more than 25% of the total investment in the project construction, and the construction schedule and the date of completion and delivery have been determined;

(4) Pre-sale registration has been handled with the real estate administration department of the people's government at or above the county level, and a permit for the pre-sale of commercial housing has been obtained.

The pre-seller of commercial housing shall, in accordance with the relevant State regulations, report the pre-sale contract to the real estate administration department and the land administration department of the people's government at or above the county level for registration and filing.

The proceeds from the pre-sale of commercial housing must be used for the relevant project construction.

Article 46 The issue of the re-transfer by a pre-purchaser of commercial housing of the pre-sold commercial housing that has been purchased but not yet completed shall be prescribed by the State Council.

### Section 3 Mortgage of Real Estate

Article 47 The mortgage of real estate refers to the act by which a mortgagor provides real estate lawfully owned by the mortgagor to a mortgagee as security for the performance of a debt without transferring possession. Where the debtor fails to perform the debt, the mortgagee has the right to be paid in priority from the proceeds from the auction of the mortgaged real estate according to law.

Article 48 The ownership of a house lawfully obtained together with the right to the use of the land within the scope of the house may be mortgaged.

Land use rights obtained by grant may be mortgaged.

Article 49 The mortgage of real estate shall be handled on the basis of the certificate for the land use rights and the certificate of ownership of the house.

Article 50 For the mortgage of real estate, the mortgagor and the mortgagee shall sign a written mortgage contract.

Article 51 Where the land use rights on which a real estate mortgage is established are obtained by allocation, after the auction of the real estate according to law, the mortgagee may be paid in priority only after the amount equivalent to the grant premium for land use rights payable has been paid from the auction proceeds.

Article 52 After the signing of a real estate mortgage contract, newly built houses on the land shall not be part of the mortgaged property. Where the mortgaged real estate needs to be auctioned, the newly built houses on the land may be auctioned together with the mortgaged property according to law, but the mortgagee has no right to be paid in priority from the proceeds from the auction of the newly built houses.

### Section 4 Lease of Houses

Article 53 The lease of a house refers to the act by which the owner of a house, as the lessor, leases the house to a lessee for use, and the lessee pays rent to the lessor.

Article 54 For the lease of a house, the lessor and the lessee shall sign a written lease contract specifying the lease term, the purpose of the lease, the rent, the responsibility for repairs, and other terms, as well as the other rights and obligations of both parties, and shall register and file the contract with the real estate administration department.

Article 55 The lease of residential housing shall comply with the lease policies prescribed by the State and the people's government of the city where the house is located. Where a house is leased for production or business operations, the rent and other lease terms shall be negotiated and agreed upon by the lessor and the lessee.

Article 56 Where the owner of a house, for profit, leases a house built on state-owned land for which the right to use was obtained by allocation, the owner shall turn over to the State the land proceeds contained in the rent. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

### Section 5 Intermediary Service Agencies

Article 57 Real estate intermediary service agencies include real estate consulting agencies, real estate price assessment agencies, real estate brokerage agencies, etc.

Article 58 A real estate intermediary service agency shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having its own name and organizational structure;

(2) Having a fixed place of service;

(3) Having the necessary property and funds;

(4) Having a sufficient number of professionals;

(5) Other conditions as provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

To establish a real estate intermediary service agency, an application for registration of establishment shall be filed with the administrative department for industry and commerce, and the agency may commence business only after obtaining a business license.

Article 59 The State shall implement a system of qualification certification for real estate price assessors.

## Chapter V Registration Administration of Real Estate Property Rights

Article 60 The State shall implement a system of registration and certification for land use rights and house ownership.**Article 61** Where the right to the use of land is obtained by means of grant or allocation, an application for registration shall be submitted to the land administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level. Upon verification by the said department, a certificate of the right to the use of land shall be issued by the people's government at the same level.

Where a building is constructed on lawfully obtained land for real estate development, an application for registration shall be submitted to the real estate administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level on the strength of the certificate of the right to the use of land. Upon verification by the said department, a certificate of the ownership of the building shall be issued.

In the case of transfer or modification of real estate, an application for modification registration of the real estate shall be submitted to the real estate administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level. On the strength of the modified certificate of the ownership of the building, an application for modification registration of the right to the use of land shall be submitted to the land administration department of the people's government at the same level. Upon verification by the said land administration department, the certificate of the right to the use of land shall be replaced or modified by the people's government at the same level.

Where otherwise provided for by law, the provisions of such law shall apply.

**Article 62** Where real estate is mortgaged, registration of the mortgage shall be made with the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level.

Where the right to the use of land and the ownership of the building are obtained through the disposal of the mortgaged real estate, the registration of the transfer shall be made in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

**Article 63** Where, as determined by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, a single department of the local people's government at or above the county level is responsible for both real estate administration and land administration, a unified certificate of real estate rights may be prepared and issued. In accordance with the provisions of Article 61 of this Law, the confirmation and modification of the ownership of the building and the right to the use of land within the area occupied by the building shall be recorded separately in the certificate of real estate rights.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 64** Where, in violation of the provisions of Articles 11 and 12 of this Law, the right to the use of land is approved for grant or granted without authorization for real estate development, the relevant responsible person shall be given administrative sanctions by the superior organ or the unit to which he belongs.

**Article 65** Where, in violation of the provisions of Article 30 of this Law, real estate development business is engaged in without obtaining a business license, the administrative department for industry and commerce of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the real estate development business activities, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine.

**Article 66** Where, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 39 of this Law, the right to the use of land is transferred, the land administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal gains and may concurrently impose a fine.

**Article 67** Where, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 40 of this Law, real estate is transferred, the land administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the payment of the land use right grant fee, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine.

**Article 68** Where, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 45 of this Law, commodity housing is pre-sold, the real estate administration department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the pre-sale activities, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine.

**Article 69** Where, in violation of the provisions of Article 58 of this Law, real estate intermediary service business is engaged in without obtaining a business license, the administrative department for industry and commerce of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the real estate intermediary service business activities, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine.

**Article 70** Where fees are collected from real estate development enterprises without the basis of laws or regulations, the superior organ shall order the return of the collected fees; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person shall be given administrative sanctions by the superior organ or the unit to which he belongs.

**Article 71** Where personnel of the real estate administration department or the land administration department neglect their duties or abuse their power, constituting a crime, they shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, they shall be given administrative sanctions.

Where personnel of the real estate administration department or the land administration department, taking advantage of their positions, extort property from others, or illegally accept property from others to seek benefits for others, constituting a crime, they shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, they shall be given administrative sanctions.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 72** Where the right to the use of land for real estate development is obtained within the scope of state-owned land outside the urban planning area, and real estate development, transaction activities are engaged in, and real estate administration is implemented, this Law shall be referred to for implementation.

**Article 73** This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1995.
