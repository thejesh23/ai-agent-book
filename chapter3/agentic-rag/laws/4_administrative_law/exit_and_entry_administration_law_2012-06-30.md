# Exit and Entry Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on June 30, 2012

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating exit and entry administration, safeguarding the sovereignty, security and social order of the People's Republic of China, and promoting foreign exchanges and opening up.

Article 2 This Law applies to the administration of the exit and entry of Chinese citizens, the entry and exit of foreigners, the stay and residence of foreigners in China, and the border inspection of means of transport exiting and entering China.

Article 3 The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese citizens in exiting and entering the country.

The legitimate rights and interests of foreigners in China shall be protected by law. Foreigners in China shall abide by Chinese laws and shall not endanger China's national security, harm public interests, or disrupt public order.

Article 4 The Ministry of Public Security and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall, within the scope of their respective duties, be responsible for the administration of exit and entry affairs.

The embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China abroad, or other institutions stationed abroad entrusted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (hereinafter referred to as visa-issuing authorities abroad), are responsible for issuing entry visas to foreigners outside China. The exit and entry border inspection authorities are responsible for implementing exit and entry border inspections. The public security organs of local people's governments at or above the county level and their exit and entry administration agencies are responsible for the administration of the stay and residence of foreigners.

The Ministry of Public Security and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs may, within the scope of their respective duties, entrust the exit and entry administration agencies of the public security organs of local people's governments at or above the county level and the foreign affairs departments of local people's governments at or above the county level with the acceptance of applications for entry, stay and residence of foreigners.

In the administration of exit and entry affairs, the Ministry of Public Security and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall strengthen communication and coordination, and closely cooperate with the relevant departments of the State Council, exercising their powers and assuming responsibilities according to law in accordance with their respective division of duties.

Article 5 The State shall establish a unified exit and entry administration information platform to realize information sharing among relevant administrative departments.

Article 6 The State shall set up exit and entry border inspection authorities at ports open to foreign countries.

Chinese citizens, foreigners and means of transport shall exit and enter China through ports open to foreign countries. Under special circumstances, they may exit and enter China through locations approved by the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council. Persons and means of transport exiting or entering China shall be subject to exit and entry border inspection.

The exit and entry border inspection authorities are responsible for the administration of the restricted zones of ports. Where necessary for safeguarding national security and maintaining the order of exit and entry administration, the exit and entry border inspection authorities may conduct border inspections on articles carried by persons exiting or entering China. When necessary, the exit and entry border inspection authorities may conduct border inspections on goods carried by means of transport exiting or entering China, provided that the customs authorities are notified.

Article 7 With the approval of the State Council, the Ministry of Public Security and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs may, based on the needs of exit and entry administration, formulate provisions on the retention of biometric identification information such as fingerprints of persons exiting or entering China.

Where a foreign government has special provisions on the issuance of visas to Chinese citizens or on exit and entry administration, the Chinese government may, depending on the circumstances, take corresponding reciprocal measures.

Article 8 The departments and agencies performing the duties of exit and entry administration shall take effective measures to continuously improve service and management levels, enforce the law impartially, provide convenience and efficiency for the people, and maintain a safe and convenient exit and entry order.

## Chapter II Exit and Entry of Chinese Citizens

Article 9 Chinese citizens exiting or entering China shall, according to law, apply for passports or other travel documents.

Chinese citizens traveling to other countries or regions shall also obtain visas or other entry permits from the destination countries. However, this does not apply where the Chinese government has signed visa exemption agreements with other governments or where the Ministry of Public Security or the Ministry of Foreign Affairs provides otherwise.

Chinese citizens who exit or enter China as seamen or work on board foreign ships shall, according to law, apply for seamen's certificates.

Article 10 Chinese citizens traveling between the mainland and the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the Macao Special Administrative Region, and Chinese citizens traveling between the mainland and the Taiwan region, shall, according to law, apply for travel permits and comply with the relevant provisions of this Law. Specific administrative measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 11 Chinese citizens exiting or entering China shall present their passports or other travel documents and other exit and entry documents to the exit and entry border inspection authorities, fulfill the prescribed formalities, and may exit or enter only after inspection and approval.

At ports where conditions permit, the exit and entry border inspection authorities shall provide Chinese citizens with convenience measures such as dedicated channels for exit and entry.

Article 12 Chinese citizens shall not be allowed to exit the country under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Not holding valid exit or entry documents, or refusing or evading border inspection;
(2) Being sentenced to criminal punishment which has not yet been fully executed, or being a criminal suspect or defendant in a criminal case;
(3) Having an unresolved civil case and the people's court has decided not to allow exit;
(4) Having been subject to criminal punishment for obstructing national (border) administration, or having been repatriated by another country or region for illegal exit, illegal stay, or illegal employment, and the period during which exit is not allowed as prescribed has not yet expired;
(5) Possibly endangering national security and interests, and the relevant competent department of the State Council has decided not to allow exit;
(6) Other circumstances under which exit is not allowed as provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 13 Chinese citizens residing abroad who wish to return to China for permanent residence shall, before entry, submit an application to the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China abroad, or to another institution stationed abroad entrusted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. They may also submit an application, either by themselves or through their relatives in China, to the overseas Chinese affairs department of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of intended residence.

Article 14 Chinese citizens residing abroad who need to provide identity certificates for handling matters such as finance, education, medical care, transportation, telecommunications, social insurance, and property registration in China may use their passports to prove their identity.

## Chapter III Entry and Exit of Foreigners

### Section 1 Visas

Article 15 Foreigners entering China shall apply for visas from visa-issuing authorities abroad, unless otherwise provided by this Law.

Article 16 Visas are divided into diplomatic visas, courtesy visas, service visas, and ordinary visas.

Diplomatic or service visas shall be issued to foreigners entering China for diplomatic or official reasons. Courtesy visas shall be issued to foreigners who are given courtesy due to their special status. The scope and methods for issuing diplomatic visas, courtesy visas, and service visas shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Ordinary visas of corresponding categories shall be issued to foreigners entering China for non-diplomatic or non-official reasons such as work, study, family visit, tourism, business activities, and talent introduction. The categories and methods for issuing ordinary visas shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 17 The registered items of a visa include: visa type, holder's name, gender, date of birth, number of entries allowed, validity period of entry, duration of stay, date and place of issuance, passport or other international travel document number, etc.

Article 18 When applying for a visa, a foreigner shall submit his/her passport or other international travel document, as well as relevant materials concerning the purpose of application, to the visa-issuing authority abroad, and go through the relevant formalities and accept an interview as required by the visa-issuing authority abroad.

Article 19 Where a foreigner applying for a visa is required to provide an invitation letter issued by a unit or individual in China, the applicant shall provide it as required by the visa-issuing authority abroad. The unit or individual issuing the invitation letter shall be responsible for the authenticity of the invitation content.

Article 20 Foreigners who need to enter China urgently for humanitarian reasons, or who are invited to enter China for urgent business, emergency repairs, or other urgent needs and hold supporting documents from the relevant competent authorities agreeing to apply for a visa at the port, may apply for a port visa from the port visa authority entrusted by the Ministry of Public Security (hereinafter referred to as port visa authority) at a port approved by the State Council to handle port visa business.

Where travel agencies organize inbound tourism in accordance with relevant state regulations, they may apply for group tourist visas from the port visa authority.

When applying for a visa from a port visa authority, a foreigner shall submit his/her passport or other international travel document, as well as relevant materials concerning the purpose of application, go through the relevant formalities as required by the port visa authority, and enter China through the port where the visa is applied for.

A visa issued by a port visa authority is valid for a single entry, and the duration of stay indicated on the visa shall not exceed thirty days.

Article 21 A visa shall not be issued to a foreigner under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having been ordered to be deported or having been decided to be repatriated, and the period during which entry is not allowed as prescribed has not yet expired;
(2) Suffering from a serious mental disorder, infectious pulmonary tuberculosis, or other infectious diseases that may cause serious harm to public health;
(3) Possibly endangering China's national security and interests, disrupting public order, or engaging in other illegal or criminal activities;
(4) Engaging in fraud during the visa application process or being unable to guarantee the necessary expenses during his/her stay in China;
(5) Failing to submit relevant materials required by the visa-issuing authority;
(6) Other circumstances under which the visa-issuing authority deems it inappropriate to issue a visa.

The visa-issuing authority may not state the reasons for refusing to issue a visa.

Article 22 Foreigners under any of the following circumstances may be exempted from applying for a visa:

(1) Being a person exempted from visa application under a visa exemption agreement signed between the Chinese government and the government of another country;
(2) Holding a valid foreigner's residence permit;
(3) Holding a connecting ticket and transiting through China to a third country or region by an international aircraft, ship, or train, staying in China for no more than twenty-four hours without leaving the port, or staying within a specific area approved by the State Council for no more than the prescribed time limit;
(4) Other circumstances under which visa exemption is allowed as prescribed by the State Council.

Article 23 Foreigners under any of the following circumstances who need temporary entry shall apply to the exit and entry border inspection authorities for temporary entry formalities:

(1) Foreign crew members and their accompanying family members disembarking in the city where the port is located;
(2) Persons specified in Item (3) of Article 22 of this Law who need to leave the port;
(3) Needing temporary entry due to force majeure or other urgent reasons.

The duration of temporary entry shall not exceed fifteen days.

For foreigners applying for temporary entry formalities, the exit and entry border inspection authorities may require the foreigner himself/herself, the person in charge of the means of transport that brought him/her into the country, or the agency handling the exit and entry business of the means of transport to provide necessary guarantee measures.

### Section 2 Entry and Exit

Article 24 Foreigners entering China shall present their passports or other international travel documents, visas or other entry permits to the exit and entry border inspection authorities, fulfill the prescribed formalities, and may enter only after inspection and approval.

Article 25 Foreigners shall not be allowed to enter China under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Not holding valid exit or entry documents, or refusing or evading border inspection;
(2) Falling under the circumstances specified in Items (1) to (4) of Paragraph 1 of Article 21 of this Law;
(3) Possibly engaging in activities inconsistent with the visa category after entry;
(4) Other circumstances under which entry is not allowed as provided by laws or administrative regulations.

The exit and entry border inspection authorities may not state the reasons for refusing entry.

Article 26 Foreigners who are not allowed to enter China shall be ordered by the exit and entry border inspection authorities to return; if they refuse to return, they shall be compelled to return. While awaiting return, foreigners shall not leave the restricted area.

Article 27 Foreigners exiting China shall present their passports or other international travel documents and other exit or entry documents to the exit and entry border inspection authorities, fulfill the prescribed formalities, and may exit only after inspection and approval.

Article 28 Foreigners shall not be allowed to exit China under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being sentenced to criminal punishment which has not yet been fully executed, or being a criminal suspect or defendant in a criminal case, except where the transfer of sentenced persons is carried out under an agreement signed between China and a foreign country;
(2) Having an unresolved civil case and the people's court has decided not to allow exit;
(3) Being in arrears with the labor remuneration of workers, and the relevant department of the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government has decided not to allow exit;
(4) Other circumstances under which exit is not allowed as provided by laws or administrative regulations.

## Chapter IV Stay and Residence of Foreigners

### Section 1 Stay and Residence

Article 29 Where the duration of stay indicated on a visa held by a foreigner does not exceed one hundred and eighty days, the holder may stay in China with the visa and within the duration of stay indicated on the visa.

If a foreigner needs to extend the duration of stay indicated on the visa, he/she shall, no later than seven days before the expiry of the duration of stay indicated on the visa, submit an application to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of stay, and submit relevant materials concerning the purpose of application as required. Upon examination, if the reasons for extension are reasonable and sufficient, the extension of the duration of stay shall be granted; if the extension is not granted, the foreigner shall leave the country on schedule.

The cumulative extension of the duration of stay indicated on a visa shall not exceed the original duration of stay indicated on the visa.

Article 30 Where a visa held by a foreigner indicates that a residence permit is required after entry, the foreigner shall, within thirty days from the date of entry, apply to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of intended residence for a foreigner's residence permit.

When applying for a foreigner's residence permit, the foreigner shall submit his/her passport or other international travel document, as well as relevant materials concerning the purpose of application, and provide biometric identification information such as fingerprints. The exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ shall, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the application materials, conduct an examination and make a decision, and issue a foreigner's residence permit of the corresponding category and duration based on the purpose of residence.

The validity period of a foreigner's work-type residence permit shall be a minimum of ninety days and a maximum of five years; the validity period of a non-work-type residence permit shall be a minimum of one hundred and eighty days and a maximum of five years.

Article 31 A foreigner's residence permit shall not be issued to a foreigner under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The category of visa held is not one for which a foreigner's residence permit should be issued;
(2) Engaging in fraud during the application process;
(3) Failing to provide relevant supporting materials as required;
(4) Violating relevant Chinese laws or administrative regulations and being unsuitable for residence in China;
(5) Other circumstances under which the issuing authority deems it inappropriate to issue a foreigner's residence permit.

For foreigners who are specialized talents or investors meeting state regulations, or who genuinely need to change from stay to residence for humanitarian or other reasons, a foreigner's residence permit may be issued upon approval by the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the city with districts.

Article 32 Foreigners residing in China who apply for an extension of the duration of residence shall, no later than thirty days before the expiry of the validity period of the residence permit, submit an application to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of residence, and submit relevant materials concerning the purpose of application as required. Upon examination, if the reasons for extension are reasonable and sufficient, the extension of the duration of residence shall be granted; if the extension is not granted, the foreigner shall leave the country on schedule.

Article 33 The registered items of a foreigner's residence permit include: holder's name, gender, date of birth, purpose of residence, duration of residence, date and place of issuance, passport or other international travel document number, etc.

Where the registered items of a foreigner's residence permit change, the holder shall, within ten days from the date of the change, apply to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of residence for the change.

Article 34 Foreigners entering China without a visa who need to stay in China beyond the visa-free period, foreign crew members and their accompanying family members who need to leave the city where the port is located during their stay in China, or foreigners who need to apply for a foreigner's stay permit under other circumstances, shall apply for a foreigner's stay permit in accordance with regulations.

The validity period of a foreigner's stay permit shall be a maximum of one hundred and eighty days.

Article 35 Where an ordinary visa, stay permit, or residence permit held by a foreigner is damaged, lost, or stolen after entry, or where there are reasons in line with state regulations requiring renewal or reissuance, the foreigner shall, in accordance with regulations, submit an application to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of stay or residence.

Article 36 The decision made by the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ not to renew, reissue, or extend an ordinary visa, not to issue a foreigner's stay or residence permit, or not to extend the duration of residence shall be final.

Article 37 Foreigners staying or residing in China shall not engage in activities inconsistent with the purpose of their stay or residence, and shall leave the country before the expiry of the prescribed duration of stay or residence.

Article 38 Foreigners aged sixteen or above who are staying or residing in China shall carry with them their passports or other international travel documents, or their foreigner's stay or residence permits, and submit them for inspection by public security organs.

Foreigners residing in China shall, within the prescribed time limit, present their foreigner's residence permits for inspection to the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of residence.

Article 39 Where a foreigner stays in a hotel in China, the hotel shall register his/her accommodation in accordance with the relevant regulations on the public security administration of the hotel industry, and report the foreigner's accommodation registration information to the local public security organ.

Where a foreigner resides or stays in a dwelling other than a hotel, he/she or the person hosting him/her shall, within twenty-four hours after check-in, register with the public security organ in the place of residence.

Article 40 For a foreign infant born in China, his/her parents or agent shall, within sixty days of the infant's birth, hold the infant's birth certificate and register the infant's stay or residence with the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in the place of stay or residence of the parents.

Where a foreigner dies in China, his/her family members, guardian, or agent shall, in accordance with regulations, hold the foreigner's death certificate and report to the exit and entry administration agency of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level to cancel the foreigner's stay or residence permit.

Article 41 Foreigners working in China shall, in accordance with regulations, obtain work permits and work-type residence permits. No unit or individual may employ a foreigner who has not obtained a work permit and a work-type residence permit.

The administrative measures for foreigners working in China shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 42 The competent department of human resources and social security of the State Council and the competent department of foreign experts, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, shall formulate and periodically adjust the guidance catalogue for foreigners working in China based on the needs of economic and social development and the supply and demand situation of human resources.

The competent education department of the State Council, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, shall establish a management system for foreign students' work-study programs, and stipulate the scope of positions and time limits for foreign students' work-study activities.

Article 43 Any of the following acts by a foreigner shall be considered illegal employment:

(1) Working in China without obtaining a work permit and a work-type residence permit in accordance with regulations;
(2) Working in China beyond the scope of the work permit;
(3) Foreign students working in China in violation of the work-study management regulations, exceeding the prescribed scope of positions or time limits.**Article 44** When necessary for the maintenance of national security or public security, public security organs and state security organs may restrict foreigners and foreign institutions from establishing residences or offices in certain areas; where such residences or offices have already been established, they may be ordered to relocate within a specified time limit.

Foreigners shall not enter areas restricted to foreigners without approval.

**Article 45** Entities that employ foreigners to work or recruit foreign students shall, in accordance with regulations, report relevant information to the local public security organ.

Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations that discover a foreigner illegally entering, illegally residing, or illegally working in China shall promptly report the matter to the local public security organ.

**Article 46** A foreigner applying for refugee status may, during the period of refugee status determination, stay in China with a temporary identity certificate issued by the public security organ. A foreigner recognized as a refugee may stay or reside in China with a refugee identity document issued by the public security organ.

### Section 2: Permanent Residence

**Article 47** A foreigner who has made outstanding contributions to China's economic and social development or meets other conditions for permanent residence in China may, upon personal application and approval by the Ministry of Public Security, obtain permanent residence status.

The measures for the examination and administration of permanent residence in China for foreigners shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Public Security and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs jointly with the relevant departments of the State Council.

**Article 48** A foreigner who has obtained permanent residence status may reside and work in China with a permanent residence certificate, and may exit and enter China with their passport and the permanent residence certificate.

**Article 49** The Ministry of Public Security shall decide to revoke the permanent residence status of a foreigner in China under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Causing harm to China's national security or interests;
(2) Being ordered to be deported;
(3) Obtaining permanent residence status in China by fraud;
(4) Failing to reside in China for the required duration;
(5) Other circumstances where the foreigner is unsuitable for permanent residence in China.

## Chapter V: Border Inspection of Exit/Entry Transport Vehicles

**Article 50** When an exit/entry transport vehicle departs from or arrives at a port, it shall undergo border inspection. Entry border inspection of a transport vehicle shall be conducted at the port where it first arrives; exit border inspection of a transport vehicle shall be conducted at the port from which it finally departs. Under special circumstances, it may be conducted at a location designated by the relevant competent authority.

From the time of exit inspection until departure, and from the time of entry until entry inspection, an exit/entry transport vehicle shall not embark or disembark persons, or load or unload cargo or articles, without permission from the exit/entry border inspection authority in accordance with prescribed procedures.

**Article 51** The person in charge of a transport vehicle or the agency handling its exit/entry business shall, in accordance with regulations, report to the exit/entry border inspection authority in advance the time of arrival and departure of the transport vehicle at the port and its place of stay, and truthfully declare information such as crew members, passengers, cargo, or articles.

**Article 52** The person in charge of a transport vehicle or the agency handling its exit/entry business shall cooperate with exit/entry border inspection. If they discover any act violating this Law, they shall immediately report it and assist in the investigation and handling.

If an inbound transport vehicle carries persons who are not permitted to enter China, the person in charge of the transport vehicle shall be responsible for taking them out of China.

**Article 53** The exit/entry border inspection authority shall, in accordance with regulations, provide surveillance for exit/entry transport vehicles under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Exit transport vehicles from the start of exit border inspection until departure, and entry transport vehicles from entry until the completion of entry border inspection;
(2) Foreign vessels navigating on China's inland waters;
(3) Other circumstances where surveillance is necessary.

**Article 54** Persons who need to board or disembark from a foreign vessel for purposes such as loading/unloading articles, repair operations, visits, or inspections shall apply to the exit/entry border inspection authority for a boarding permit.

If a Chinese vessel and a foreign vessel, or two foreign vessels, need to engage in alongside operations, the captain or the agency handling the transport vehicle's exit/entry business shall apply to the exit/entry border inspection authority for procedures for vessel alongside operations.

**Article 55** Foreign vessels and aircraft within China shall navigate or fly along prescribed routes or flight paths.

Exit/entry vessels and aircraft shall not enter areas other than open ports. If they enter such areas due to unforeseen emergencies or force majeure, they shall immediately report to the nearest exit/entry border inspection authority or local public security organ, and shall accept surveillance and management.

**Article 56** Exit/entry of a transport vehicle shall not be permitted under any of the following circumstances; if it has already departed from the port, it may be ordered to return:

(1) Departing from or arriving at a port without undergoing inspection and obtaining permission;
(2) Changing the exit/entry port without approval;
(3) Being suspected of carrying persons not permitted to exit or enter China, requiring inspection and verification;
(4) Being suspected of carrying articles endangering national security, interests, or public order, requiring inspection and verification;
(5) Other circumstances involving refusal to accept the management of the exit/entry border inspection authority.

Once the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph cease to exist, the exit/entry border inspection authority shall immediately release the relevant transport vehicle.

**Article 57** Entities acting as agents for the exit/entry business of transport vehicles shall register with the exit/entry border inspection authority. Personnel engaged in such agency business shall go through the registration procedures with the exit/entry border inspection authority through their employing entity.

## Chapter VI: Investigation and Repatriation

**Article 58** The measures of on-the-spot interrogation, continued interrogation, detention for investigation, restriction of movement, and repatriation provided for in this Chapter shall be implemented by the public security organ of a local people's government at or above the county level or the exit/entry border inspection authority.

**Article 59** Persons suspected of violating exit/entry administration may be subjected to on-the-spot interrogation. If, after on-the-spot interrogation, any of the following circumstances exist, they may be subjected to continued interrogation according to law:

(1) Being suspected of illegally exiting or entering China;
(2) Being suspected of assisting others in illegally exiting or entering China;
(3) A foreigner being suspected of illegal residence or illegal employment;
(4) Being suspected of endangering national security and interests, disrupting public order, or engaging in other illegal or criminal activities.

On-the-spot interrogation and continued interrogation shall be conducted in accordance with the procedures stipulated in the *People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China*.

If a public security organ of a local people's government at or above the county level or an exit/entry border inspection authority needs to summon a person suspected of violating exit/entry administration, it shall do so in accordance with the relevant provisions of the *Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China*.

**Article 60** If a foreigner falls under any of the circumstances specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 59 of this Law, and suspicion cannot be eliminated after on-the-spot interrogation or continued interrogation, and further investigation is necessary, they may be detained for investigation.

When executing detention for investigation, a written decision for detention for investigation shall be presented, and an interrogation shall be conducted within twenty-four hours. If it is found that detention for investigation is not warranted, the detention shall be immediately terminated.

The period of detention for investigation shall not exceed thirty days; if the case is complex, it may be extended to sixty days with the approval of the public security organ of the next higher level local people's government or the exit/entry border inspection authority. For a foreigner whose nationality or identity is unknown, the period of detention for investigation shall be calculated from the date their nationality or identity is ascertained.

**Article 61** Detention for investigation shall not apply to a foreigner under any of the following circumstances; instead, their movement may be restricted:

(1) Suffering from a serious illness;
(2) Being pregnant or nursing an infant under one year old;
(3) Being under sixteen years of age or over seventy years of age;
(4) Other circumstances where detention for investigation is inappropriate.

A foreigner whose movement is restricted shall undergo investigation as required and shall not leave the restricted area without the approval of the public security organ. The period of restriction of movement shall not exceed sixty days. For a foreigner whose nationality or identity is unknown, the period of restriction of movement shall be calculated from the date their nationality or identity is ascertained.

**Article 62** A foreigner may be repatriated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being ordered to leave China within a specified time limit but failing to do so;
(2) Falling under circumstances where entry is not permitted;
(3) Illegally residing or illegally working;
(4) Violating this Law or other laws or administrative regulations, necessitating repatriation.

Other persons from outside China falling under any of the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph may be repatriated according to law.

Persons who are repatriated shall not be permitted to enter China for a period of one to five years from the date of repatriation.

**Article 63** Persons who are detained for investigation or who have been ordered to be repatriated but cannot be immediately executed shall be held in a detention center or a repatriation facility.

**Article 64** If a foreigner is dissatisfied with the measures of continued interrogation, detention for investigation, restriction of movement, or repatriation imposed on them in accordance with this Law, they may apply for administrative reconsideration according to law, and the decision on such reconsideration shall be final.

If other persons from outside China are dissatisfied with the measure of repatriation imposed on them in accordance with this Law and apply for administrative reconsideration, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

**Article 65** If a decision is made according to law that a person shall not be permitted to exit or enter China, the deciding authority shall promptly notify the exit/entry border inspection authority in accordance with regulations. When the circumstances preventing exit or entry cease to exist, the deciding authority shall promptly revoke the decision and notify the exit/entry border inspection authority.

**Article 66** When necessary for maintaining national security and the order of exit/entry administration, the exit/entry border inspection authority may, when necessary, conduct a body search on a person exiting or entering China. A body search shall be conducted by two border inspection officers of the same gender as the person being searched.

**Article 67** If an exit/entry document such as a visa or a foreigner's stay or residence permit is damaged, lost, stolen, or robbed, or if it is discovered after issuance that the holder does not meet the issuance conditions, the issuing authority shall declare the exit/entry document void.

Forged, altered, fraudulently obtained, or exit/entry documents declared void by the issuing authority are invalid.

The public security organ may cancel or confiscate the exit/entry documents specified in the preceding paragraph or those used fraudulently by another person.

**Article 68** The public security organ may seize transport vehicles used for organizing, transporting, or assisting others in illegally exiting or entering China, as well as articles needed as evidence for handling the case.

The public security organ shall seize confiscated prohibited articles, documents and materials involving state secrets, and tools used for committing acts violating exit/entry administration, and shall handle them in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 69** The authenticity of an exit/entry document shall be determined by the issuing authority, the exit/entry border inspection authority, or the exit/entry administration of the public security organ.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

**Article 70** Administrative penalties provided for in this Chapter shall, unless otherwise specified in this Chapter, be decided by the public security organ of a local people's government at or above the county level or the exit/entry border inspection authority; among these, a warning or a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan may be decided by the exit/entry administration of the public security organ of a local people's government at or above the county level.

**Article 71** Anyone who commits any of the following acts shall be fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than ten days, and may also be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan:

(1) Using forged, altered, or fraudulently obtained exit/entry documents to exit or enter China;
(2) Using another person's exit/entry documents to exit or enter China;
(3) Evading exit/entry border inspection;
(4) Illegally exiting or entering China by other means.

**Article 72** Anyone who assists others in illegally exiting or entering China shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and shall also be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If a unit commits the act specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan, and any illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 73** Anyone who obtains exit/entry documents such as visas, stay or residence permits by fraud shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and shall also be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

If a unit commits the act specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 74** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, issues an invitation letter or other application materials for a foreigner shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated, and they shall be ordered to bear the expenses for the invited foreigner's departure from China.

If a unit commits the act specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated, and it shall be ordered to bear the expenses for the invited foreigner's departure from China; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 75** If a Chinese citizen, after exiting China, illegally goes to another country or region and is repatriated, the exit/entry border inspection authority shall confiscate their exit/entry documents, and the issuing authority for exit/entry documents shall not issue them exit/entry documents for a period of six months to three years from the date of their repatriation.

**Article 76** A warning shall be given, and a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan may also be imposed, under any of the following circumstances:

(1) A foreigner refuses to accept inspection of their exit/entry documents by the public security organ;
(2) A foreigner refuses to present their residence permit for inspection;
(3) Failing to register the birth of a foreigner or report the death of a foreigner in accordance with regulations;
(4) Failing to register changes in the items recorded on a foreigner's residence permit in accordance with regulations;
(5) A foreigner within China using another person's exit/entry documents;
(6) Failing to register in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 39 of this Law.

If a hotel fails to register the accommodation of a foreigner in accordance with regulations, it shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the *Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China*; if it fails to report the accommodation registration information of a foreigner to the public security organ in accordance with regulations, a warning shall be given; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 77** If a foreigner enters an area restricted to foreigners without approval, they shall be ordered to leave immediately; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than ten days. Any written records, audio-visual materials, electronic data, or other articles illegally obtained by the foreigner shall be confiscated or destroyed, and the tools used shall be confiscated.

If a foreigner or foreign institution violates the provisions of this Law and refuses to comply with the decision of the public security organ or state security organ to relocate within a specified time limit, a warning shall be given and they shall be forcibly relocated; if the circumstances are serious, the responsible person shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

**Article 78** If a foreigner illegally resides, a warning shall be given; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of 500 yuan for each day of illegal residence, with a total not exceeding 10,000 yuan, or detention for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days shall be imposed.

If a guardian or other person responsible for guardianship fails to fulfill their guardianship duties, resulting in the illegal residence of a foreigner under sixteen years of age, a warning shall be given to the guardian or other person responsible for guardianship, and a fine of not more than 1,000 yuan may also be imposed.

**Article 79** Anyone who harbors or conceals a foreigner who has illegally entered or is illegally residing, assists a foreigner who has illegally entered or is illegally residing in evading inspection, or illegally provides exit/entry documents for a foreigner who is illegally residing shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and shall also be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If a unit commits the act specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 80** If a foreigner works illegally, they shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and shall also be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

Anyone who introduces a foreigner for illegal employment shall be fined 5,000 yuan for each person illegally introduced, with a total not exceeding 50,000 yuan for an individual; for a unit, a fine of 5,000 yuan for each person illegally introduced, with a total not exceeding 100,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Anyone who illegally employs a foreigner shall be fined 10,000 yuan for each person illegally employed, with a total not exceeding 100,000 yuan; any illegal gains shall be confiscated.

**Article 81** If a foreigner engages in activities inconsistent with the purpose of their stay or residence, or has other violations of Chinese laws or regulations, making them unsuitable for continued stay or residence in China, they may be ordered to leave China within a specified time limit.

If a foreigner violates the provisions of this Law, and the circumstances are serious but do not constitute a crime, the Ministry of Public Security may order their deportation. The penalty decision of the Ministry of Public Security shall be final.

A foreigner who is deported shall not be permitted to enter China for a period of ten years from the date of deportation.

**Article 82** A warning shall be given, and a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan may also be imposed, under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Disrupting the management order of the port restricted area;
(2) Foreign crew members or their accompanying family members disembarking without completing temporary entry procedures;
(3) Boarding or disembarking from a foreign vessel without a boarding permit.

If the circumstances of violating Item (1) of the preceding paragraph are serious, detention for not less than five days but not more than ten days may also be imposed.

**Article 83** The person in charge of a transport vehicle shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Exiting or entering China without undergoing inspection and obtaining permission, or changing the exit/entry port without approval;
(2) Failing to truthfully declare information such as crew members, passengers, cargo, or articles in accordance with regulations, or refusing to assist in exit/entry border inspection;
(3) Embarking or disembarking persons, or loading or unloading cargo or articles, in violation of exit/entry border inspection regulations.

If an exit/entry transport vehicle carries persons who are not permitted to exit or enter China, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed for each person carried. If the person in charge of the transport vehicle proves that they have taken reasonable preventive measures, the penalty may be mitigated or exempted.

**Article 84** The person in charge of a transport vehicle shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan under any of the following circumstances:

(1) A Chinese or foreign vessel engages in alongside operations with a foreign vessel without approval;
(2) A foreign vessel or aircraft fails to navigate or fly along the prescribed routes or flight paths within China;
(3) An exit/entry vessel or aircraft enters an area other than an open port in violation of regulations.

**Article 85** Staff members performing exit/entry administration duties who commit any of the following acts shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Issuing exit/entry documents such as visas or foreigner's stay or residence permits to foreigners who do not meet the prescribed conditions, in violation of laws or administrative regulations;
(2) Examining and releasing persons or transport vehicles that do not meet the prescribed conditions for exit or entry, in violation of laws or administrative regulations;
(3) Disclosing personal information learned during exit/entry administration work, infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of the parties concerned;(4) Failing to turn over, in accordance with regulations, the fees lawfully collected, fines imposed, and illegal gains and property confiscated to the state treasury;

(5) Privately dividing, appropriating, or misappropriating fines, seized property, or collected fees;

(6) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or failing to lawfully perform statutory duties.

Article 86: For violations of exit and entry administration where a fine of not more than 500 yuan is imposed, the exit-entry border inspection authority may make the penalty decision on the spot.

Article 87: Where a fine is imposed for a violation of exit and entry administration, the person penalized shall, within 15 days from the date of receipt of the penalty decision, pay the fine to a designated bank. If the person penalized has no fixed residence at the location, making it difficult to enforce the fine after the fact if not collected on the spot, or if paying the fine to the designated bank at the port is genuinely difficult, the fine may be collected on the spot.

Article 88: Any violation of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 89: For the purposes of this Law, the following terms are defined as:

"Exit" means leaving the Chinese mainland for another country or region, leaving the Chinese mainland for the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the Macao Special Administrative Region, or leaving the Chinese mainland for the Taiwan region.

"Entry" means entering the Chinese mainland from another country or region, entering the Chinese mainland from the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the Macao Special Administrative Region, or entering the Chinese mainland from the Taiwan region.

"Foreigner" means a person who does not hold Chinese nationality.

Article 90: With the approval of the State Council, provinces and autonomous regions bordering neighboring countries may, in accordance with border management agreements signed between China and the relevant countries, formulate local regulations and local government rules to govern the movement of residents in the border areas between the two countries.

Article 91: The management of the entry, exit, stay, and residence of members of foreign diplomatic missions and consular posts in China, as well as other foreigners enjoying privileges and immunities, shall be governed by other laws if such laws provide otherwise.

Article 92: Foreigners applying for exit and entry documents such as visas, stay or residence permits, or applying for extension or alteration of such documents, shall pay visa fees and document fees in accordance with regulations.

Article 93: This Law shall come into force on July 1, 2013. The "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Entry and Exit of Aliens" and the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Entry and Exit of Citizens" shall be repealed simultaneously.
