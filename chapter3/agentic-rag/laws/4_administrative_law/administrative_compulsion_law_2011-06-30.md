# Administrative Compulsory Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on June 30, 2011

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purposes of regulating the establishment and implementation of administrative compulsion, ensuring and supervising administrative organs in performing their duties according to law, safeguarding public interests and social order, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "administrative compulsion" includes administrative compulsory measures and administrative compulsory enforcement.

"Administrative compulsory measures" refer to the acts of an administrative organ, in the course of administrative management, to temporarily restrict the personal freedom of a citizen or temporarily control the property of a citizen, legal person, or other organization according to law, for the purposes of stopping illegal acts, preventing the destruction of evidence, avoiding the occurrence of hazards, or controlling the expansion of dangers.

"Administrative compulsory enforcement" refers to the acts of an administrative organ, or an administrative organ applying to a people's court, to compulsorily enforce the performance of obligations according to law against a citizen, legal person, or other organization that fails to perform an administrative decision.

Article 3 The establishment and implementation of administrative compulsion shall be governed by this Law.

Where an emergency such as a natural disaster, accident disaster, public health incident, or social security incident occurs or is imminent, and an administrative organ takes emergency measures or interim measures, such measures shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Where an administrative organ takes prudential supervision measures in the financial industry or compulsory technical monitoring measures for imported and exported goods, such measures shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 4 The establishment and implementation of administrative compulsion shall be carried out in accordance with the statutory authority, scope, conditions, and procedures.

Article 5 The establishment and implementation of administrative compulsion shall be appropriate. Where the purpose of administrative management can be achieved through non-compulsory means, administrative compulsion shall not be established or implemented.

Article 6 In implementing administrative compulsion, the principle of combining education with compulsion shall be adhered to.

Article 7 Administrative organs and their staff shall not use the power of administrative compulsion to seek benefits for their units or individuals.

Article 8 Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations shall have the right to make statements and defend themselves against the implementation of administrative compulsion by administrative organs; they shall have the right to apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate administrative litigation according to law; and if they suffer damage due to the illegal implementation of administrative compulsion by an administrative organ, they shall have the right to claim compensation according to law.

Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations that suffer damage due to illegal acts or the expansion of the scope of compulsory enforcement by a people's court shall have the right to claim compensation according to law.

## Chapter II Types and Establishment of Administrative Compulsion

Article 9 The types of administrative compulsory measures are:

(1) Restricting the personal freedom of citizens;
(2) Sealing up premises, facilities, or property;
(3) Distraining property;
(4) Freezing deposits or remittances;
(5) Other administrative compulsory measures.

Article 10 Administrative compulsory measures shall be established by law.

Where no law has been enacted and the matter falls within the scope of administrative functions and powers of the State Council, administrative regulations may establish administrative compulsory measures other than those specified in Items (1) and (4) of Article 9 of this Law and those that should be established by law.

Where no law or administrative regulation has been enacted and the matter is a local affair, local regulations may establish the administrative compulsory measures specified in Items (2) and (3) of Article 9 of this Law.

No other regulatory documents other than laws and regulations may establish administrative compulsory measures.

Article 11 Where a law has provided for the objects, conditions, and types of administrative compulsory measures, administrative regulations and local regulations shall not expand such provisions.

Where a law does not establish administrative compulsory measures, administrative regulations and local regulations shall not establish administrative compulsory measures. However, where a law provides that specific matters shall be subject to specific management measures prescribed by administrative regulations, such administrative regulations may establish administrative compulsory measures other than those specified in Items (1) and (4) of Article 9 of this Law and those that should be established by law.

Article 12 The methods of administrative compulsory enforcement are:

(1) Imposing additional fines or late fees;
(2) Transferring deposits or remittances;
(3) Auctioning or lawfully disposing of sealed-up or distrained premises, facilities, or property;
(4) Removing obstructions or restoring the original state;
(5) Substitute performance;
(6) Other methods of compulsory enforcement.

Article 13 Administrative compulsory enforcement shall be established by law.

Where a law does not provide for compulsory enforcement by an administrative organ, the administrative organ that made the administrative decision shall apply to a people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 14 When drafting a bill for a law or regulation that intends to establish administrative compulsion, the drafting unit shall solicit opinions through methods such as hearings and feasibility studies, and shall explain to the enacting organ the necessity of establishing such administrative compulsion, the potential impacts, and the circumstances of soliciting and adopting opinions.

Article 15 The enacting organ of administrative compulsion shall regularly evaluate the administrative compulsion it has established and shall promptly amend or repeal any inappropriate administrative compulsion.

The implementing organ of administrative compulsion may, from time to time, evaluate the implementation status and the continued necessity of established administrative compulsion and report its opinions to the enacting organ of such administrative compulsion.

Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations may submit opinions and suggestions to the enacting organ and the implementing organ of administrative compulsion regarding the establishment and implementation of administrative compulsion. The relevant organs shall seriously study and verify such opinions and provide feedback in an appropriate manner.

## Chapter III Procedures for Implementing Administrative Compulsory Measures

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 16 An administrative organ shall implement administrative compulsory measures in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations when performing its administrative management duties.

Where the illegal act is of a minor nature or has no obvious social harm, administrative compulsory measures may not be taken.

Article 17 Administrative compulsory measures shall be implemented by the administrative organs prescribed by laws and regulations within their statutory functions and powers. The power to take administrative compulsory measures shall not be delegated.

An administrative organ that exercises relatively centralized administrative penalty powers in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Administrative Penalties may implement administrative compulsory measures related to administrative penalty powers as prescribed by laws and regulations.

Administrative compulsory measures shall be implemented by qualified law enforcement personnel of the administrative organ; no other personnel may implement them.

Article 18 When implementing administrative compulsory measures, an administrative organ shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) Report to and obtain approval from the person in charge of the administrative organ before implementation;
(2) Be implemented by two or more law enforcement personnel;
(3) Present law enforcement identification documents;
(4) Notify the party concerned to be present;
(5) Inform the party concerned on the spot of the reasons and basis for taking the administrative compulsory measures, as well as the rights and remedies available to the party concerned according to law;
(6) Hear the statements and defenses of the party concerned;
(7) Make an on-site record;
(8) The on-site record shall be signed or sealed by the party concerned and the law enforcement personnel; if the party concerned refuses, this shall be noted in the record;
(9) If the party concerned is not present, invite a witness to be present, and the witness and the law enforcement personnel shall sign or seal the on-site record;
(10) Other procedures prescribed by laws and regulations.

Article 19 In an emergency where administrative compulsory measures need to be taken on the spot, the law enforcement personnel shall report to the person in charge of the administrative organ within 24 hours and complete the approval procedures retroactively. If the person in charge of the administrative organ believes that the administrative compulsory measures should not have been taken, they shall immediately lift them.

Article 20 When implementing administrative compulsory measures that restrict the personal freedom of citizens in accordance with the provisions of the law, in addition to fulfilling the procedures specified in Article 18 of this Law, the following provisions shall also be complied with:

(1) Inform the family members of the party concerned on the spot or immediately after implementing the administrative compulsory measures of the administrative organ implementing the measures, the location, and the duration;
(2) If administrative compulsory measures are taken on the spot in an emergency, report to the person in charge of the administrative organ immediately upon returning to the administrative organ and complete the approval procedures retroactively;
(3) Other procedures prescribed by law.

The duration of administrative compulsory measures restricting personal freedom shall not exceed the statutory period. Once the purpose of implementing the administrative compulsory measures has been achieved or the conditions for their implementation have ceased to exist, they shall be immediately lifted.

Article 21 Where an illegal act is suspected of constituting a crime and should be transferred to a judicial organ, the administrative organ shall transfer the sealed-up, distrained, or frozen property together with the case and notify the party concerned in writing.

### Section 2 Sealing Up and Distraint

Article 22 Sealing up and distraint shall be implemented by the administrative organs prescribed by laws and regulations; no other administrative organ or organization may implement them.

Article 23 Sealing up and distraint shall be limited to the premises, facilities, or property involved in the case; premises, facilities, or property unrelated to the illegal act shall not be sealed up or distrained; and the daily necessities of the citizen and their dependents shall not be sealed up or distrained.

Where the premises, facilities, or property of a party concerned have already been lawfully sealed up by another state organ, they shall not be sealed up again.

Article 24 When an administrative organ decides to implement sealing up or distraint, it shall fulfill the procedures specified in Article 18 of this Law and produce and deliver on the spot a decision letter and a list for sealing up or distraint.

The decision letter for sealing up or distraint shall specify the following:

(1) The name or title and address of the party concerned;
(2) The reasons, basis, and duration for the sealing up or distraint;
(3) The names and quantities of the premises, facilities, or property sealed up or distrained;
(4) The channels and time limits for applying for administrative reconsideration or initiating administrative litigation;
(5) The name, seal, and date of the administrative organ.

The list for sealing up or distraint shall be made in duplicate, with one copy kept by the party concerned and the other by the administrative organ.

Article 25 The duration of sealing up or distraint shall not exceed 30 days; in complex cases, it may be extended with the approval of the person in charge of the administrative organ, but the extension shall not exceed 30 days, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

The decision to extend the sealing up or distraint shall be promptly notified to the party concerned in writing, along with the reasons.

Where items need to be tested, inspected, quarantined, or technically appraised, the period for sealing up or distraint shall not include the period for testing, inspection, quarantine, or technical appraisal. The period for testing, inspection, quarantine, or technical appraisal shall be clearly specified and notified to the party concerned in writing. The costs of testing, inspection, quarantine, or technical appraisal shall be borne by the administrative organ.

Article 26 The administrative organ shall properly keep the sealed-up or distrained premises, facilities, or property and shall not use or damage them; if losses are caused, it shall bear the liability for compensation.

For sealed-up premises, facilities, or property, the administrative organ may entrust a third party to keep them; the third party shall not damage or arbitrarily transfer or dispose of them. If losses are caused due to the third party's fault, the administrative organ shall compensate first and then have the right to recover from the third party.

The storage costs incurred due to sealing up or distraint shall be borne by the administrative organ.

Article 27 After taking sealing up or distraint measures, the administrative organ shall promptly investigate the facts and make a handling decision within the time limit specified in Article 25 of this Law. If the illegal facts are clear and the illegal property should be confiscated according to law, it shall be confiscated; if laws or administrative regulations provide that it should be destroyed, it shall be destroyed according to law; if the sealing up or distraint should be lifted, a decision to lift the sealing up or distraint shall be made.

Article 28 In any of the following circumstances, the administrative organ shall promptly make a decision to lift the sealing up or distraint:

(1) The party concerned has not committed an illegal act;
(2) The sealed-up or distrained premises, facilities, or property are unrelated to the illegal act;
(3) The administrative organ has made a handling decision on the illegal act and no longer needs to seal up or distrain;
(4) The duration of the sealing up or distraint has expired;
(5) Other circumstances where sealing up or distraint measures are no longer necessary.

Upon lifting the sealing up or distraint, the property shall be immediately returned; if fresh items or other property not easily kept have been auctioned or sold, the proceeds from the auction or sale shall be returned. If the sale price is significantly lower than the market price, causing losses to the party concerned, compensation shall be given.

### Section 3 Freezing

Article 29 Freezing of deposits or remittances shall be implemented by the administrative organs prescribed by law and shall not be delegated to other administrative organs or organizations; no other administrative organ or organization may freeze deposits or remittances.

The amount of deposits or remittances frozen shall be commensurate with the amount involved in the illegal act; if they have already been lawfully frozen by another state organ, they shall not be frozen again.

Article 30 When an administrative organ decides to freeze deposits or remittances in accordance with the provisions of the law, it shall fulfill the procedures specified in Items (1), (2), (3), and (7) of Article 18 of this Law and deliver a freezing notice to the financial institution.

Upon receiving the freezing notice lawfully issued by the administrative organ, the financial institution shall immediately freeze the deposits or remittances without delay and shall not disclose information to the party concerned before the freezing.

If an administrative organ or organization other than those prescribed by law requests a financial institution to freeze the deposits or remittances of a party concerned, the financial institution shall refuse.

Article 31 When freezing deposits or remittances in accordance with the provisions of the law, the administrative organ that made the decision shall deliver a freezing decision letter to the party concerned within three days. The freezing decision letter shall specify the following:

(1) The name or title and address of the party concerned;
(2) The reasons, basis, and duration for the freezing;
(3) The account number and amount frozen;
(4) The channels and time limits for applying for administrative reconsideration or initiating administrative litigation;
(5) The name, seal, and date of the administrative organ.

Article 32 Within 30 days from the date of freezing the deposits or remittances, the administrative organ shall make a handling decision or a decision to lift the freezing; in complex cases, it may be extended with the approval of the person in charge of the administrative organ, but the extension shall not exceed 30 days, unless otherwise provided by law.

The decision to extend the freezing shall be promptly notified to the party concerned in writing, along with the reasons.

Article 33 In any of the following circumstances, the administrative organ shall promptly make a decision to lift the freezing:

(1) The party concerned has not committed an illegal act;
(2) The frozen deposits or remittances are unrelated to the illegal act;
(3) The administrative organ has made a handling decision on the illegal act and no longer needs to freeze;
(4) The freezing period has expired;
(5) Other circumstances where freezing measures are no longer necessary.

When an administrative organ makes a decision to lift the freezing, it shall promptly notify the financial institution and the party concerned. Upon receiving the notice, the financial institution shall immediately lift the freezing.

If the administrative organ fails to make a handling decision or a decision to lift the freezing within the time limit, the financial institution shall lift the freezing from the date the freezing period expires.

## Chapter IV Procedures for Administrative Compulsory Enforcement by Administrative Organs

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 34 After an administrative organ makes an administrative decision according to law, if the party concerned fails to perform the obligations within the time limit specified in the decision, the administrative organ with the power of administrative compulsory enforcement shall enforce it according to the provisions of this Chapter.

Article 35 Before making a decision on compulsory enforcement, the administrative organ shall first urge the party concerned to perform the obligations by issuing a reminder. The reminder shall be made in writing and shall specify the following:

(1) The time limit for performing the obligations;
(2) The method of performing the obligations;
(3) If monetary payment is involved, the specific amount and method of payment;
(4) The right of the party concerned to make statements and defend themselves according to law.

Article 36 After receiving the reminder, the party concerned shall have the right to make statements and defend themselves. The administrative organ shall fully hear the opinions of the party concerned and shall record and review the facts, reasons, and evidence presented by the party concerned. If the facts, reasons, or evidence presented by the party concerned are established, the administrative organ shall adopt them.

Article 37 After the reminder, if the party concerned still fails to perform the administrative decision without justifiable reasons beyond the time limit, the administrative organ may make a decision on compulsory enforcement.

The decision on compulsory enforcement shall be made in writing and shall specify the following:

(1) The name or title and address of the party concerned;
(2) The reasons and basis for the compulsory enforcement;
(3) The method and time of the compulsory enforcement;
(4) The channels and time limits for applying for administrative reconsideration or initiating administrative litigation;
(5) The name, seal, and date of the administrative organ.

During the reminder period, if there is evidence indicating that the party concerned is transferring or concealing property, the administrative organ may make an immediate decision on compulsory enforcement.

Article 38 The reminder and the decision on administrative compulsory enforcement shall be served directly on the party concerned. If the party concerned refuses to accept service or if direct service is not possible, service shall be made in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 39 In any of the following circumstances, enforcement shall be suspended:

(1) The party concerned genuinely has difficulty performing the administrative decision or temporarily lacks the capacity to perform;
(2) A third party claims rights to the subject matter of enforcement with reasonable grounds;
(3) Enforcement may cause irreparable losses, and suspending enforcement does not harm the public interest;
(4) Other circumstances where the administrative organ deems it necessary to suspend enforcement.

Once the circumstances for suspending enforcement cease to exist, the administrative organ shall resume enforcement. If there is no obvious social harm, the party concerned genuinely lacks the capacity to perform, and enforcement has been suspended for three years without resumption, the administrative organ shall no longer enforce.

Article 40 In any of the following circumstances, enforcement shall be terminated:

(1) The citizen dies, leaving no estate for enforcement and no one to assume the obligations;
(2) The legal person or other organization is dissolved, leaving no property for enforcement and no one to assume the obligations;
(3) The subject matter of enforcement is lost;
(4) The administrative decision on which the enforcement is based is revoked;
(5) Other circumstances where the administrative organ deems it necessary to terminate enforcement.

Article 41 If, during or after enforcement, the administrative decision on which the enforcement is based is revoked or modified, or if the enforcement is erroneous, the original state shall be restored or the property shall be returned; if restoration or return is not possible, compensation shall be given according to law.

Article 42 When implementing administrative compulsory enforcement, the administrative organ may, without harming the public interest or the lawful rights and interests of others, enter into an enforcement agreement with the party concerned. The enforcement agreement may provide for phased performance; if the party concerned takes remedial measures, the additional fines or late fees may be reduced or waived.

The enforcement agreement shall be performed. If the party concerned fails to perform the enforcement agreement, the administrative organ shall resume compulsory enforcement.

Article 43 An administrative organ shall not implement administrative compulsory enforcement at night or on statutory holidays, except in emergencies.

An administrative organ shall not force the party concerned to perform relevant administrative decisions by cutting off water, electricity, heat, or gas supply to residents.

Article 44 For illegal buildings, structures, facilities, etc., that need to be forcibly demolished, the administrative organ shall issue a public notice, setting a time limit for the party concerned to demolish them voluntarily. If the party concerned neither applies for administrative reconsideration nor initiates administrative litigation within the statutory time limit, nor demolishes them, the administrative organ may forcibly demolish them according to law.

### Section 2 Enforcement of Monetary Payment Obligations

Article 45 If an administrative organ makes an administrative decision for monetary payment obligations according to law and the party concerned fails to perform within the time limit, the administrative organ may impose additional fines or late fees according to law. The standard for imposing additional fines or late fees shall be notified to the party concerned.The amount of the additional penalty or late fee shall not exceed the amount of the monetary obligation.

**Article 46** If an administrative agency imposes an additional penalty or late fee in accordance with Article 45 of this Law for more than thirty days, and the party still fails to comply after a reminder, the administrative agency with the power of administrative enforcement may enforce the decision.

Before an administrative agency carries out enforcement, if measures such as sealing up, seizing, or freezing are necessary, they shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Chapter III of this Law.

An administrative agency without the power of administrative enforcement shall apply to the People's Court for enforcement. However, if the party fails to apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit within the statutory time limit and still fails to comply after a reminder, the administrative agency that has already taken sealing up or seizing measures during the administration process may auction the sealed up or seized property in accordance with the law to offset the fine.

**Article 47** The transfer of deposits or remittances shall be decided by the administrative agency as prescribed by law, and the financial institution shall be notified in writing. Upon receiving the decision on the transfer of deposits or remittances made by the administrative agency in accordance with the law, the financial institution shall immediately execute the transfer.

If an administrative agency or organization other than those prescribed by law requests the transfer of a party's deposits or remittances, the financial institution shall refuse.

**Article 48** The auction of property in accordance with the law shall be handled by the administrative agency entrusting an auction institution in accordance with the provisions of the Auction Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 49** The transferred deposits, remittances, and proceeds from auctions and lawful disposals shall be turned over to the State Treasury or transferred to a special fiscal account. No administrative agency or individual may, in any form, intercept, privately divide, or covertly divide them.

### Section 3: Substitute Performance

**Article 50** If an administrative agency makes an administrative decision requiring the party to fulfill obligations such as removing obstructions or restoring the original state, and the party fails to comply within the time limit and still fails to comply after a reminder, and the consequences have or will endanger traffic safety, cause environmental pollution, or damage natural resources, the administrative agency may carry out substitute performance or entrust a disinterested third party to carry out substitute performance.

**Article 51** Substitute performance shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) A decision letter shall be served before substitute performance, which shall state the party's name or title, address, the reasons and basis for substitute performance, the method and time, the subject matter, the cost budget, and the person performing the substitute performance;

(2) Three days before substitute performance, the party shall be reminded to perform; if the party performs, the substitute performance shall be stopped;

(3) During substitute performance, the administrative agency that made the decision shall send personnel to supervise on site;

(4) After substitute performance is completed, the supervising personnel of the administrative agency, the person performing the substitute performance, and the party or witness shall sign or affix their seals on the enforcement document.

The cost of substitute performance shall be reasonably determined based on the cost and borne by the party, unless otherwise provided by law.

Substitute performance shall not involve violence, coercion, or other illegal methods.

**Article 52** If it is necessary to immediately remove spilled items, obstructions, or pollutants from roads, rivers, waterways, or public places, and the party cannot remove them, the administrative agency may decide to immediately carry out substitute performance; if the party is not present, the administrative agency shall immediately notify the party afterward and handle the matter in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V: Application to the People's Court for Enforcement

**Article 53** If a party fails to apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit within the statutory time limit and also fails to comply with the administrative decision, the administrative agency without the power of administrative enforcement may, within three months from the date of expiration of the time limit, apply to the People's Court for enforcement in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

**Article 54** Before an administrative agency applies to the People's Court for enforcement, it shall remind the party to fulfill the obligation. If the party still fails to fulfill the obligation ten days after the reminder is served, the administrative agency may apply to the People's Court with jurisdiction at its location for enforcement; if the subject of enforcement is immovable property, the application shall be made to the People's Court with jurisdiction at the location of the immovable property.

**Article 55** When an administrative agency applies to the People's Court for enforcement, it shall provide the following materials:

(1) A written application for enforcement;

(2) The administrative decision and the facts, reasons, and basis for the decision;

(3) The party's opinions and the administrative agency's reminder situation;

(4) The situation of the subject matter of the enforcement application;

(5) Other materials as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

The written application for enforcement shall be signed by the responsible person of the administrative agency, affixed with the seal of the administrative agency, and dated.

**Article 56** Upon receiving an application for enforcement from an administrative agency, the People's Court shall accept it within five days.

If the administrative agency disagrees with the People's Court's ruling of non-acceptance, it may apply for reconsideration to the higher People's Court within fifteen days, and the higher People's Court shall make a ruling on whether to accept the case within fifteen days from the date of receiving the reconsideration application.

**Article 57** The People's Court shall conduct a written review of the administrative agency's application for enforcement. If the application meets the requirements of Article 55 of this Law and the administrative decision has legal enforcement effect, except for the circumstances specified in Article 58 of this Law, the People's Court shall make an enforcement ruling within seven days from the date of acceptance.

**Article 58** If the People's Court finds any of the following circumstances, it may hear the opinions of the person subject to enforcement and the administrative agency before making a ruling:

(1) The decision clearly lacks factual basis;

(2) The decision clearly lacks a basis in laws or regulations;

(3) Other obvious illegalities that harm the lawful rights and interests of the person subject to enforcement.

The People's Court shall make a ruling on whether to enforce within thirty days from the date of acceptance. If the ruling is not to enforce, reasons shall be stated, and the ruling of non-enforcement shall be served to the administrative agency within five days.

If the administrative agency disagrees with the People's Court's ruling of non-enforcement, it may apply for reconsideration to the higher People's Court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the ruling, and the higher People's Court shall make a ruling on whether to enforce within thirty days from the date of receiving the reconsideration application.

**Article 59** In urgent circumstances, to ensure public safety, an administrative agency may apply to the People's Court for immediate enforcement. With the approval of the president of the People's Court, the People's Court shall enforce within five days from the date of making the enforcement ruling.

**Article 60** An administrative agency applying to the People's Court for enforcement shall not pay an application fee. The costs of enforcement shall be borne by the person subject to enforcement.

If the People's Court enforces by transfer or auction, the enforcement costs may be deducted after the transfer or auction.

The auction of property in accordance with the law shall be handled by the People's Court entrusting an auction institution in accordance with the provisions of the Auction Law of the People's Republic of China.

The transferred deposits, remittances, and proceeds from auctions and lawful disposals shall be turned over to the State Treasury or transferred to a special fiscal account, and shall not be intercepted, privately divided, or covertly divided in any form.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 61** If an administrative agency implements administrative compulsion under any of the following circumstances, the higher administrative agency or relevant department shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Without a basis in laws or regulations;

(2) Changing the subject, conditions, or methods of administrative compulsion;

(3) Implementing administrative compulsion in violation of statutory procedures;

(4) Implementing administrative enforcement at night or on statutory holidays in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(5) Forcing the party to comply with relevant administrative decisions by stopping the supply of water, electricity, heat, or gas to residents;

(6) Other circumstances of illegally implementing administrative compulsion.

**Article 62** If an administrative agency violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the higher administrative agency or relevant department shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Expanding the scope of sealing up, seizing, or freezing;

(2) Using or damaging sealed up or seized premises, facilities, or property;

(3) Failing to make a handling decision within the statutory period for sealing up or seizing, or failing to lift the sealing up or seizing in a timely manner in accordance with the law;

(4) Failing to make a handling decision within the statutory period for freezing deposits or remittances, or failing to lift the freezing in a timely manner in accordance with the law.

**Article 63** If an administrative agency intercepts, privately divides, or covertly divides sealed up or seized property, or transferred deposits, remittances, and proceeds from auctions and lawful disposals, the financial department or relevant department shall recover them; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions such as a major demerit, demotion, removal from office, or dismissal in accordance with the law.

If an administrative agency staff member, taking advantage of their position, appropriates sealed up or seized premises, facilities, or property, the higher administrative agency or relevant department shall order correction and impose sanctions such as a major demerit, demotion, removal from office, or dismissal in accordance with the law.

**Article 64** If an administrative agency or its staff member uses the power of administrative compulsion to seek benefits for a unit or individual, the higher administrative agency or relevant department shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 65** If a financial institution violates the provisions of this Law under any of the following circumstances, the financial regulatory authority shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Disclosing information to the party before freezing;

(2) Failing to freeze or transfer deposits or remittances that should be immediately frozen or transferred, resulting in the transfer of deposits or remittances;

(3) Freezing or transferring deposits or remittances that should not be frozen or transferred;

(4) Failing to lift the freezing of deposits or remittances in a timely manner.

**Article 66** If a financial institution violates the provisions of this Law by transferring funds to an account other than the State Treasury or a special fiscal account, the financial regulatory authority shall order correction and impose a fine equal to twice the amount of the illegally transferred funds; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If an administrative agency or the People's Court, in violation of the provisions of this Law, instructs a financial institution to transfer funds to an account other than the State Treasury or a special fiscal account, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 67** If the People's Court or its staff member engages in illegal acts during enforcement or expands the scope of enforcement, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 68** If a violation of the provisions of this Law causes losses to a citizen, legal person, or other organization, compensation shall be given in accordance with the law.

If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 69** The provisions of this Law regarding a time limit of ten days or less refer to working days, excluding statutory holidays.

**Article 70** Organizations authorized by laws or administrative regulations to perform public administration functions may, within the scope of statutory authorization, implement administrative compulsion in their own name, and the provisions of this Law concerning administrative agencies shall apply.

**Article 71** This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2012.
