# Biosecurity Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to safeguard national security, prevent and respond to biosecurity risks, protect people's life and health, conserve biological resources and the ecological environment, promote the healthy development of biotechnology, advance the building of a community with a shared future for mankind, and achieve harmony between humans and nature.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "biosecurity" refers to the ability of the State to effectively prevent and respond to threats posed by dangerous biological agents and related factors, to ensure the stable and healthy development of biotechnology, to keep people's life and health and ecosystems relatively free from danger and threats, and to maintain the capacity to safeguard national security and sustainable development in the biological domain.

This Law applies to the following activities:

(1) Prevention and control of major emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases, and animal and plant epidemics;
(2) Research, development, and application of biotechnology;
(3) Biosafety management of pathogenic microorganism laboratories;
(4) Safety management of human genetic resources and biological resources;
(5) Prevention of invasive alien species and protection of biodiversity;
(6) Response to antimicrobial resistance;
(7) Prevention of bioterrorism attacks and defense against biological weapon threats; and
(8) Other activities related to biosecurity.

**Article 3** Biosecurity is an important component of national security. The maintenance of biosecurity shall implement a holistic approach to national security, coordinate development and security, and adhere to the principles of putting people first, risk prevention, classified management, and coordinated cooperation.

**Article 4** The leadership of the Communist Party of China over national biosecurity work shall be upheld. A national biosecurity leadership system shall be established and improved, the national biosecurity risk prevention and control and governance system shall be strengthened, and the national biosecurity governance capacity shall be enhanced.

**Article 5** The State encourages innovation in biotechnology, strengthens the construction of biosecurity infrastructure and the development of a biotechnology talent team, supports the development of the bio-industry, and drives the improvement of biotechnology levels through innovation to enhance biosecurity assurance capabilities.

**Article 6** The State strengthens international cooperation in the field of biosecurity, fulfills the obligations stipulated in international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, supports participation in biotechnology exchange and cooperation and international rescue for biosecurity incidents, actively participates in the research and formulation of international rules on biosecurity, and promotes the improvement of global biosecurity governance.

**Article 7** People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall strengthen the publicity and popularization of biosecurity laws, regulations, and knowledge, guide grassroots self-governing organizations and social organizations to conduct such publicity, and promote the enhancement of biosecurity awareness across society.

Relevant scientific research institutions, medical institutions, and other enterprises and institutions shall incorporate biosecurity laws, regulations, and knowledge into education and training content, and strengthen the cultivation of biosecurity awareness and ethical awareness among students and practitioners.

News media shall conduct public interest publicity on biosecurity laws, regulations, and knowledge, exercise public opinion supervision over illegal acts against biosecurity, and enhance the public's sense of social responsibility for maintaining biosecurity.

**Article 8** No unit or individual may endanger biosecurity.

Any unit or individual has the right to report acts that endanger biosecurity; the department receiving the report shall handle it promptly in accordance with the law.

**Article 9** Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to biosecurity work shall be commended and rewarded by the people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments in accordance with state regulations.

## Chapter II Biosecurity Risk Prevention and Control System

**Article 10** The central national security leadership body is responsible for decision-making and deliberation and coordination on national biosecurity work, studying and formulating, and guiding the implementation of national biosecurity strategies and major policies, coordinating major biosecurity matters and important work, and establishing a national biosecurity work coordination mechanism.

Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish biosecurity work coordination mechanisms to organize, coordinate, supervise, and promote biosecurity-related work within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 11** The national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall be composed of the competent departments of the State Council for health, agriculture and rural affairs, science and technology, foreign affairs, and relevant military organs. It shall analyze and assess the national biosecurity situation, and organize, coordinate, supervise, and promote national biosecurity-related work. An office shall be established under the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism to handle its daily work.

Member units of the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism and other relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for biosecurity-related work according to their respective functions and duties.

**Article 12** An expert committee shall be established under the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism to provide decision-making consultation for national biosecurity strategy research, policy formulation, and implementation.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall organize the establishment of biosecurity technical advisory expert committees in relevant fields and industries to provide technical support such as consultation, assessment, and evaluation for biosecurity work.

**Article 13** Local people's governments at all levels are responsible for biosecurity work within their respective administrative regions.

The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for biosecurity-related work according to their respective functions and duties.

Grassroots self-governing organizations shall assist local people's governments and relevant departments in carrying out biosecurity risk prevention and control, emergency response, and publicity and education.

Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate in carrying out biosecurity risk prevention and control and emergency response work.

**Article 14** The State establishes a biosecurity risk monitoring and early warning system. The national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall organize the establishment of a national biosecurity risk monitoring and early warning system to improve the ability to identify and analyze biosecurity risks.

**Article 15** The State establishes a biosecurity risk investigation and assessment system. The national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall regularly organize biosecurity risk investigations and assessments based on data, information, and other materials from risk monitoring.

Under any of the following circumstances, the relevant departments shall promptly conduct a biosecurity risk investigation and assessment and take necessary risk prevention and control measures in accordance with the law:

(1) When a possible biosecurity risk is discovered through risk monitoring or reports;
(2) When determining key areas and key projects for supervision and management, or formulating or adjusting relevant biosecurity catalogs or lists;
(3) When an event endangering biosecurity, such as a major emerging or re-emerging infectious disease or animal or plant epidemic, occurs; or
(4) Other circumstances requiring investigation and assessment.

**Article 16** The State establishes a biosecurity information sharing system. The national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall organize the establishment of a unified national biosecurity information platform. Relevant departments shall submit biosecurity data, information, and other materials to the national biosecurity information platform to achieve information sharing.

**Article 17** The State establishes a biosecurity information release system. Major biosecurity information, such as the overall national biosecurity situation, major biosecurity risk warning information, and major biosecurity incidents and their investigation and handling information, shall be released by the member units of the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism according to their respective functions and duties. Other biosecurity information shall be released by the relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments according to their respective functions and powers.

No unit or individual may fabricate or disseminate false biosecurity information.

**Article 18** The State establishes a biosecurity catalog and list system. The State Council and its relevant departments shall, based on the needs of biosecurity work, formulate and publish catalogs or lists of materials, equipment, technologies, activities, important biological resource data, infectious diseases, animal and plant diseases, invasive alien species, etc., related to biosecurity, and dynamically adjust them.

**Article 19** The State establishes a biosecurity standard system. The competent standardization department of the State Council and other relevant departments of the State Council shall, according to their respective functions and duties, formulate and improve relevant standards in the field of biosecurity.

The national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall organize relevant departments to strengthen the coordination and connection of biosecurity standards in different fields, and establish and improve the biosecurity standard system.

**Article 20** The State establishes a biosecurity review system. Major matters and activities in the biological field that affect or may affect national security shall be subject to biosecurity review by the relevant departments of the State Council to effectively prevent and resolve biosecurity risks.

**Article 21** The State establishes a biosecurity emergency system featuring unified leadership, coordinated action, and orderly and efficient response.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall organize the formulation of contingency plans for biosecurity incidents in relevant fields and industries, and conduct emergency drills, emergency response, emergency rescue, and post-incident recovery in accordance with the contingency plans and unified deployment.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall formulate, organize, guide, and supervise relevant enterprises and institutions to formulate contingency plans for biosecurity incidents, strengthen emergency preparedness, personnel training, and emergency drills, and carry out emergency response, emergency rescue, and post-incident recovery for biosecurity incidents.

The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with the orders of the Central Military Commission, participate in the emergency response and rescue work for biosecurity incidents in accordance with the law.

**Article 22** The State establishes a biosecurity incident investigation and traceability system. When a major emerging or re-emerging infectious disease, animal or plant epidemic, or a biosecurity incident of unknown cause occurs, the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism shall organize an investigation and traceability to determine the nature of the incident, comprehensively assess its impact, and provide opinions and suggestions.

**Article 23** The State establishes a national access system for animals, plants, and their products entering the country for the first time or resuming entry after suspension, and for high-risk biological agents.
Personnel, means of transport, containers, goods, articles, packaging materials entering or leaving the country, and the discharge of ballast water from international voyages shall comply with China's biosecurity management requirements.

Customs shall handle biosecurity risks discovered during entry, exit, and transit in accordance with the law. Personnel, means of transport, goods, articles, etc., assessed as high biosecurity risks shall enter the country through designated border ports and be subject to strict risk prevention and control measures.

**Article 24** The State establishes a system for responding to major biosecurity incidents abroad. When a major biosecurity incident occurs abroad, Customs shall take emergency biosecurity prevention and control measures in accordance with the law, strengthen document verification, increase inspection ratios, and suspend the entry of relevant personnel, means of transport, goods, articles, etc. When necessary, with the approval of the State Council, measures such as temporarily closing relevant ports or sealing relevant borders may be taken.

**Article 25** The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall carry out biosecurity supervision and inspection in accordance with the law. The inspected units and individuals shall cooperate, truthfully explain the situation, provide materials, and shall not refuse or obstruct.

For supervision and inspection work involving high technical requirements or difficult law enforcement operations, biosecurity professional and technical personnel shall participate.

**Article 26** When carrying out biosecurity supervision and inspection, the relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level may take the following measures in accordance with the law:

(1) Entering the inspected unit, location, or place suspected of committing biosecurity violations for on-site monitoring, survey, inspection, or verification;
(2) Inquiring about the situation from relevant units and individuals;
(3) Consulting and copying relevant documents, materials, archives, records, vouchers, etc.;
(4) Sealing places and facilities suspected of being used for biosecurity violations;
(5) Detaining tools, equipment, and related articles suspected of being used for biosecurity violations; and
(6) Other measures stipulated by laws and regulations.

Information on biosecurity violations by relevant units and individuals shall be incorporated into the national credit information sharing platform in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III Prevention and Control of Major Emerging and Re-emerging Infectious Diseases and Animal and Plant Epidemics

**Article 27** The competent departments of the State Council for health, agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland, customs, and ecological environment shall establish monitoring networks for emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases, animal and plant epidemics, entry-exit quarantine, and biotechnology environmental safety. They shall organize the layout and construction of monitoring sites, improve the monitoring information reporting system, conduct active monitoring and pathogen detection, and incorporate them into the national biosecurity risk monitoring and early warning system.

**Article 28** Disease prevention and control institutions, animal disease prevention and control institutions, and plant pest prevention and control institutions (hereinafter collectively referred to as "professional institutions") shall conduct active monitoring of infectious diseases, animal and plant diseases, and unexplained diseases included in the monitoring scope, collect, analyze, and report monitoring information, and predict the occurrence and epidemic trends of emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases and animal and plant diseases.

The relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level, and their relevant departments shall issue timely warnings based on predictions and their respective functions and powers, and take corresponding prevention and control measures.

**Article 29** Any unit or individual discovering an infectious disease or animal or plant disease shall promptly report it to a medical institution, relevant professional institution, or department.

Medical institutions, professional institutions, and their staff, upon discovering an infectious disease, animal or plant disease, or an unexplained cluster disease, shall promptly report it and take protective measures.

Where reporting is required by law, no unit or individual may conceal, misreport, delay reporting, or omit reporting, nor may they instigate others to do so, or obstruct others from reporting.

**Article 30** The State establishes a joint prevention and control mechanism for major emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases and animal and plant epidemics.

When a major emerging or re-emerging infectious disease or animal or plant epidemic occurs, control measures shall be taken promptly in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and contingency plans. The competent departments of the State Council for health, agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland shall immediately organize epidemic consultation and assessment, report the conclusions to the central national security leadership body and the State Council, and notify other member units of the national biosecurity work coordination mechanism and other relevant departments of the State Council.

When a major emerging or re-emerging infectious disease or animal or plant epidemic occurs, local people's governments at all levels shall uniformly fulfill their epidemic prevention and control responsibilities within their respective administrative regions, strengthen organizational leadership, carry out mass prevention and control and medical treatment, and mobilize and encourage social forces to participate in epidemic prevention and control work in an orderly manner in accordance with the law.

**Article 31** The State strengthens the capacity for joint prevention and control of infectious diseases and animal and plant epidemics at borders and ports, establishes an international cooperation network for the prevention and control of infectious diseases and animal and plant epidemics, and detects and controls major emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases and animal and plant epidemics as early as possible.

**Article 32** The State protects wildlife, strengthens animal epidemic prevention, and prevents the spread of zoonotic infectious diseases.

**Article 33** The State strengthens the management of the use and residues of antimicrobial drugs such as antibiotics, and supports basic research and technological breakthroughs to address antimicrobial resistance.

The competent health departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen guidance and supervision over the rational use of drugs in medical institutions, and take measures to prevent the irrational use of antimicrobial drugs. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen guidance and supervision over the rational use of drugs in agricultural production, take measures to prevent the irrational use of antimicrobial drugs, and reduce residues in the agricultural production environment.

The competent departments of the State Council for health, agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland, ecological environment, and the drug regulatory authority shall, according to their respective functions and duties, assess the harm of antimicrobial drug residues to human health and the environment, and establish an evaluation index system for antimicrobial drug pollutants.

## Chapter IV Safety of Biotechnology Research, Development, and Application

**Article 34** The State strengthens the safety management of biotechnology research, development, and application activities, and prohibits engaging in biotechnology research, development, and application activities that endanger public health, damage biological resources, destroy ecosystems and biodiversity, or otherwise harm biosecurity.

Activities involving biotechnology research, development, and application shall comply with ethical principles.

**Article 35** Units engaged in biotechnology research, development, and application activities shall be responsible for the safety of their own biotechnology research, development, and application activities, take biosecurity risk prevention and control measures, formulate work systems for biosecurity training, follow-up inspections, and regular reporting, and strengthen process management.

**Article 36** The State implements classified management of biotechnology research and development activities. Based on the degree of risk of harm to public health, industry and agriculture, and the ecological environment, biotechnology research and development activities are classified into three categories: high risk, medium risk, and low risk.

The risk classification standards and catalogs for biotechnology research and development activities shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the competent departments of the State Council for science and technology, health, agriculture and rural affairs, etc., according to their respective functions and duties, in conjunction with other relevant departments of the State Council.

**Article 37** Those engaged in biotechnology research and development activities shall comply with the national safety management specifications for biotechnology research and development.

Those engaged in biotechnology research and development activities shall judge the risk category, closely monitor risk changes, and promptly take countermeasures.

**Article 38** High-risk and medium-risk biotechnology research and development activities shall be conducted by legal entities established in China in accordance with the law, and shall obtain approval or file for the record in accordance with the law.

Those engaged in high-risk and medium-risk biotechnology research and development activities shall conduct risk assessments, formulate risk prevention and control plans and biosecurity incident contingency plans, and reduce the risks of implementing research and development activities.

**Article 39** The State implements traceability management for important equipment and special biological agents related to biosecurity. The purchase or import of important equipment and special biological agents included in the control list shall be registered to ensure traceability, and shall be reported to the relevant departments of the State Council for the record.

Individuals shall not purchase or possess important equipment and special biological agents included in the control list.

**Article 40** Clinical research on new biomedical technologies shall undergo ethical review and be conducted in medical institutions with appropriate conditions. Human clinical research operations shall be performed by qualified health professionals.

**Article 41** The relevant departments of the State Council shall conduct follow-up assessments of biotechnology application activities in accordance with the law. If biosecurity risks are found, effective remedial and control measures shall be taken promptly.

## Chapter V Biosafety of Pathogenic Microorganism Laboratories

**Article 42** The State strengthens the biosafety management of pathogenic microorganism laboratories and formulates unified laboratory biosafety standards. Pathogenic microorganism laboratories shall comply with national biosafety standards and requirements.

Those engaged in experimental activities with pathogenic microorganisms shall strictly comply with relevant national standards, laboratory technical specifications, and operating procedures, and take safety precautions.

**Article 43** The State implements classified management of pathogenic microorganisms based on their infectivity and the degree of harm to individuals or groups of humans and animals after infection.

Activities involving the collection, preservation, and transportation of samples of highly pathogenic or suspected highly pathogenic microorganisms shall meet the corresponding conditions and comply with biosafety management specifications. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent departments of the State Council for health, and agriculture and rural affairs.

**Article 44** The establishment of a pathogenic microorganism laboratory shall obtain approval or file for the record in accordance with the law.

Individuals shall not establish pathogenic microorganism laboratories or engage in experimental activities with pathogenic microorganisms.

**Article 45** The State implements graded management of pathogenic microorganism laboratories based on their level of biosafety protection for pathogenic microorganisms.Experiments involving pathogenic microorganisms must be conducted in laboratories of the corresponding biosafety level. Low-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories are prohibited from conducting experiments with pathogenic microorganisms that, according to the national catalog of pathogenic microorganisms, must be performed in high-level laboratories.

**Article 46** High-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories conducting experiments with highly pathogenic or suspected highly pathogenic microorganisms must obtain approval from the health or agriculture and rural affairs department of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and report the progress of the experiments to the approving department.

Experiments involving pathogenic microorganisms that have not yet been discovered in China or have been declared eradicated are prohibited without approval.

**Article 47** Pathogenic microorganism laboratories shall take measures to strengthen the management of experimental animals, prevent their escape, and conduct harmless treatment of used experimental animals in accordance with national regulations to ensure traceability. It is prohibited to allow used experimental animals to enter the market.

Pathogenic microorganism laboratories shall strengthen the management of waste generated from experimental activities, dispose of wastewater, exhaust gas, and other waste in accordance with the law, and take measures to prevent pollution.

**Article 48** The entity establishing a pathogenic microorganism laboratory is responsible for the biosafety management of the laboratory. It shall formulate scientific and strict management systems, regularly inspect the implementation of relevant biosafety regulations, and inspect, maintain, and update laboratory facilities, equipment, and materials to ensure they meet national standards.

The legal representative of the entity establishing the pathogenic microorganism laboratory and the laboratory director are responsible for the biosafety of the laboratory.

**Article 49** The entity establishing a pathogenic microorganism laboratory shall establish and improve security systems, adopt security measures, and ensure the safety of the laboratory and its pathogenic microorganisms.

The state strengthens the security of high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories. High-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories shall accept supervision and guidance from public security organs and other departments regarding laboratory security work, and strictly prevent the leakage, loss, theft, or robbery of highly pathogenic microorganisms.

The state establishes an entry review system for high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories. Personnel entering high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories must be approved by the laboratory director. Approval shall be denied to those who may affect laboratory biosafety; for those approved to enter, security measures shall be implemented.

**Article 50** The entity establishing a pathogenic microorganism laboratory shall formulate emergency response plans for biosafety incidents, and regularly organize personnel training and emergency drills. In the event of leakage, loss, theft, or robbery of highly pathogenic microorganisms, or other biosafety risks, control measures shall be taken promptly in accordance with the emergency plan, and reports shall be made in accordance with national regulations.

**Article 51** The provincial people's government where the pathogenic microorganism laboratory is located and its health department shall strengthen the allocation of medical resources for infectious diseases in the laboratory's locality, and improve the capacity for medical treatment of infectious diseases.

**Article 52** The biosafety management of production workshops involving the handling of pathogenic microorganisms by enterprises shall be carried out in accordance with the regulations on pathogenic microorganism laboratories and other biosafety management norms.

The construction and management of biosafety laboratories involving the handling of biological toxins, plant pests, and other biological agents shall be implemented with reference to the regulations on pathogenic microorganism laboratories.

## Chapter 6: Human Genetic Resources and Biosafety of Biological Resources

**Article 53** The state strengthens the management and supervision of activities such as the collection, preservation, utilization, and provision to foreign parties of human genetic resources and biological resources in China, ensuring their safety.

The state has sovereignty over human genetic resources and biological resources in China.

**Article 54** The state conducts surveys of human genetic resources and biological resources.

The science and technology department of the State Council organizes surveys of human genetic resources in China and formulates measures for the declaration and registration of important genetic pedigrees and human genetic resources in specific regions.

The science and technology, natural resources, ecological environment, health, agriculture and rural affairs, forestry and grassland, and traditional Chinese medicine departments of the State Council, according to their respective responsibilities, organize surveys of biological resources and formulate measures for the declaration and registration of important biological resources.

**Article 55** The collection, preservation, utilization, and provision to foreign parties of human genetic resources in China shall comply with ethical principles and shall not endanger public health, national security, or social public interests.

**Article 56** The following activities require approval from the science and technology department of the State Council:

(1) Collecting human genetic resources from important genetic pedigrees or specific regions in China, or collecting human genetic resources of types and quantities specified by the science and technology department of the State Council;
(2) Preserving human genetic resources in China;
(3) Conducting international scientific research cooperation using human genetic resources in China;
(4) Transporting, mailing, or carrying human genetic resource materials out of China.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph do not apply to the collection and preservation of human genetic resources and related activities carried out for purposes such as clinical diagnosis and treatment, blood collection and supply services, investigation of illegal and criminal activities, doping testing, and funeral services.

To obtain marketing authorization for relevant drugs and medical devices in China, conducting international cooperative clinical trials using human genetic resources in China at clinical trial institutions, without the export of human genetic resources, does not require approval; however, the types, quantities, and intended use of the human genetic resources to be used shall be reported to the science and technology department of the State Council for record-keeping before the clinical trial begins.

Foreign organizations, individuals, and institutions established or actually controlled by them are prohibited from collecting or preserving human genetic resources in China, and from providing human genetic resources in China to foreign parties.

**Article 57** Providing or making available for use information on human genetic resources in China to foreign organizations, individuals, and institutions established or actually controlled by them shall be reported in advance to the science and technology department of the State Council, along with the submission of an information backup.

**Article 58** The collection, preservation, utilization, and transport out of China of genetic resources such as precious, endangered, and endemic species and their individuals, organs, tissues, cells, and genes that can be used for regeneration or reproduction shall comply with relevant laws and regulations.

Foreign organizations, individuals, and institutions established or actually controlled by them must obtain approval in accordance with the law to acquire and utilize biological resources in China.

**Article 59** Conducting international scientific research cooperation using biological resources in China must obtain approval in accordance with the law.

When conducting international scientific research cooperation using human genetic resources and biological resources in China, it shall be ensured that Chinese entities and their researchers participate fully and substantively throughout the research process and share relevant rights and interests in accordance with the law.

**Article 60** The state strengthens the prevention and response to the invasion of alien species and protects biodiversity. The agriculture and rural affairs department of the State Council, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, formulates a catalog of invasive alien species and management measures.

Relevant departments of the State Council, according to their respective responsibilities, strengthen the investigation, monitoring, early warning, control, assessment, removal, and ecological restoration of invasive alien species.

No unit or individual may introduce, release, or discard alien species without approval.

## Chapter 7: Prevention of Bioterrorism and Biological Weapons Threats

**Article 61** The state takes all necessary measures to prevent bioterrorism and biological weapons threats.

The development, manufacture, or other acquisition, storage, possession, and use of biological weapons are prohibited.

Instigating, funding, or assisting others in developing, manufacturing, or otherwise acquiring biological weapons by any means is prohibited.

**Article 62** Relevant departments of the State Council formulate, revise, and publish lists of organisms, biological toxins, equipment, or technologies that could be used for bioterrorist activities or the manufacture of biological weapons, and strengthen supervision to prevent their use for the manufacture of biological weapons or for terrorist purposes.

**Article 63** Relevant departments of the State Council and relevant military organs, according to their respective responsibilities, strengthen the monitoring and investigation of activities such as the transit, import and export, acquisition, manufacture, transfer, and release of organisms, biological toxins, equipment, or technologies that could be used for bioterrorist activities or the manufacture of biological weapons, and take necessary preventive and disposal measures.

**Article 64** Relevant departments of the State Council, provincial people's governments, and their relevant departments are responsible for organizing the treatment and resettlement of personnel, environmental disinfection, ecological restoration, safety monitoring, and restoration of social order after a bioterrorist attack or biological weapons attack.

Relevant departments of the State Council, provincial people's governments, and their relevant departments shall effectively guide public opinion to report bioterrorist attacks and biological weapons attacks scientifically and accurately, promptly issue information on evacuation, relocation, and emergency shelter, and conduct long-term environmental and health monitoring of areas and personnel contaminated during emergency response and recovery.

**Article 65** The state organizes investigations into biological weapons left over from wars within China's territory, as well as their harmful consequences and potential impacts.

The state organizes the construction of facilities for the storage and disposal of biological weapons left over from wars, ensuring their safe disposal.

## Chapter 8: Biosafety Capacity Building

**Article 66** The state formulates development plans for the biosafety cause, strengthens biosafety capacity building, and improves the capability and level of responding to biosafety incidents.

People's governments at or above the county level shall support the development of the biosafety cause and, in accordance with the division of powers, include relevant expenditures for supporting the following biosafety undertakings in the government budget:

(1) Construction and operation of monitoring networks;
(2) Stockpiling of emergency response and prevention and control materials;
(3) Construction and operation of critical infrastructure;
(4) Research and development of key technologies and products;
(5) Survey and preservation of human genetic resources and biological resources;
(6) Other important biosafety undertakings stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 67** The state takes measures to support scientific and technological research on biosafety, strengthen research on biosafety risk prevention and control technologies, integrate advantageous forces and resources, establish a multi-disciplinary, multi-departmental collaborative innovation mechanism, promote the output and transformation of core key technologies and major defense products for biosafety, and enhance the scientific and technological support capability for biosafety.

**Article 68** The state makes overall plans for the construction of national biosafety infrastructure. Relevant departments of the State Council, according to their respective responsibilities, accelerate the construction of national strategic resource platforms for biosafety in areas such as bioinformatics, preservation of human genetic resources, preservation of bacterial (viral) strains, preservation of animal and plant genetic resources, and high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratories, establish sharing and utilization mechanisms, and provide strategic support for scientific and technological innovation in biosafety.

**Article 69** Relevant departments of the State Council, according to their respective responsibilities, strengthen the training of talents in basic bioscience research and professional technical personnel in the biological field, and promote the construction of basic bioscience disciplines and scientific research.

Practitioners in important positions of national biosafety infrastructure shall possess the required qualifications, and their relevant information shall be reported to the relevant departments of the State Council for record-keeping and undergo job training.

**Article 70** The state strengthens the stockpiling of materials for the prevention and control of biosafety risks such as major emerging infectious diseases and animal and plant epidemics.

The state strengthens the research, development, and technical stockpiling of materials such as emergency drugs and equipment for biosafety. Relevant departments of the State Council, according to their respective responsibilities, implement relevant measures for the research, development, and technical stockpiling of materials such as emergency drugs and equipment for biosafety.

Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall ensure the production, supply, and allocation of materials such as medical rescue equipment, therapeutic drugs, and medical devices required for emergency response to biosafety incidents; transportation authorities shall promptly organize and coordinate transport operators to give priority to transportation.

**Article 71** The state provides effective protective measures and medical security for personnel engaged in high-risk biosafety work, such as experiments with highly pathogenic microorganisms and on-site disposal of biosafety incidents.

## Chapter 9: Legal Liability

**Article 72** Personnel performing biosafety management duties who abuse their power, neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or commit other illegal acts in biosafety work in violation of this Law shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 73** In violation of this Law, if medical institutions, professional institutions, or their staff conceal, falsely report, delay reporting, omit reporting, instigate others to do so, or obstruct others from reporting infectious diseases, animal or plant epidemics, or unexplained cluster diseases, the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law, and may also have their practice activities suspended for a certain period or have their relevant practice certificates revoked in accordance with the law.

In violation of this Law, fabricating or disseminating false biosafety information, constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be subject to public security administration penalties by the public security organ in accordance with the law.

**Article 74** In violation of this Law, engaging in prohibited biotechnology research, development, and application activities, the health, science and technology, and agriculture and rural affairs departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains, technical materials, and tools, equipment, raw materials, and other items used for the illegal act, impose a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than ten million yuan; if the illegal gains exceed one million yuan, impose a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the illegal gains, and may also prohibit engaging in corresponding biotechnology research, development, and application activities for a certain period and revoke relevant permits; the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law, fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, prohibited from engaging in corresponding biotechnology research, development, and application activities for a period of ten years to life, and have their relevant practice certificates revoked in accordance with the law.

**Article 75** In violation of this Law, engaging in biotechnology research and development activities without complying with national biosafety management norms for biotechnology research and development, the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order correction, give a warning, and may also impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if correction is refused or serious consequences are caused, order cessation of research and development activities and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

**Article 76** In violation of this Law, conducting experiments with pathogenic microorganisms not in a laboratory of the corresponding level, or a high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratory conducting experiments with highly pathogenic or suspected highly pathogenic microorganisms without approval, the health and agriculture and rural affairs departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order cessation of the illegal act, supervise the destruction or delivery of the pathogenic microorganisms used in the experiments to a preservation institution, and give a warning; if the spread or epidemic of an infectious disease or other serious consequences are caused, the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions of removal from office or expulsion in accordance with the law.

**Article 77** In violation of this Law, allowing used experimental animals to enter the market, the science and technology department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if the illegal gains exceed 200,000 yuan, impose a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, the issuing department shall revoke the relevant permits.

**Article 78** In violation of this Law, committing any of the following acts, the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order correction, confiscate illegal gains, give a warning, and may also impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan:

(1) Purchasing or introducing important equipment or special biological agents on the control list without registration or reporting to the relevant department of the State Council for record-keeping;
(2) An individual purchasing or possessing important equipment or special biological agents on the control list;
(3) An individual establishing a pathogenic microorganism laboratory or conducting experiments with pathogenic microorganisms;
(4) Entering a high-level pathogenic microorganism laboratory without the approval of the laboratory director.

**Article 79** In violation of this Law, collecting or preserving human genetic resources in China or conducting international scientific research cooperation using human genetic resources in China without approval, the science and technology department of the State Council shall order cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains and the illegally collected or preserved human genetic resources, and impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan; if the illegal gains exceed one million yuan, impose a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if the circumstances are serious, the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law and prohibited from engaging in corresponding activities for five years.

**Article 80** In violation of this Law, foreign organizations, individuals, and institutions established or actually controlled by them collect or preserve human genetic resources in China, or provide human genetic resources in China to foreign parties, the science and technology department of the State Council shall order cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains and the illegally collected or preserved human genetic resources, and impose a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than ten million yuan; if the illegal gains exceed one million yuan, impose a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the illegal gains.

**Article 81** In violation of this Law, introducing alien species without approval, the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, confiscate the introduced alien species and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 250,000 yuan.

In violation of this Law, releasing or discarding alien species without approval, the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the recapture or retrieval of the released or discarded alien species within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

**Article 82** Violations of this Law that constitute a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; causing personal injury, property damage, or other harm shall result in civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 83** For biosafety violations specified in this Law for which legal liability is not provided in this Law, if other relevant laws and administrative regulations have provisions, those provisions shall apply.

**Article 84** Foreign organizations or individuals who transport, mail, or carry dangerous biological agents into China or otherwise harm China's biosafety shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law, and other necessary measures may be taken.

## Chapter 10: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 85** The meanings of the following terms in this Law:

(1) "Biological agent" refers to animals, plants, microorganisms, biological toxins, and other biologically active substances.
(2) "Major emerging infectious disease" refers to an infectious disease that appears for the first time within China's territory, or that has been declared eradicated and reappears, or that occurs suddenly, causing or potentially causing serious harm to public health and life safety, causing social panic, and affecting social stability.
(3) "Major emerging animal epidemic" refers to an animal disease that occurs for the first time within China's territory, or that has been declared eradicated and reappears, or a latent animal disease with a high incidence or mortality rate that occurs suddenly and spreads rapidly, posing a serious threat or harm to the safety of livestock production, and potentially causing harm to public health and life safety.(4) Major emerging or sudden-onset plant epidemics refer to situations where fungi, bacteria, viruses, insects, nematodes, weeds, harmful rodents, mollusks, or other organisms that seriously harm plants occur for the first time within China's territory or re-emerge after having been declared eradicated, or where local pests suddenly break out on a large scale and spread rapidly, causing severe damage to crops, forests, and other plants.

(5) Biotechnology research, development, and application refer to activities such as scientific research, technological development, and application conducted through the understanding, modification, synthesis, and utilization of living organisms based on scientific and engineering principles.

(6) Pathogenic microorganisms refer to microorganisms that can invade humans or animals, causing infection or even infectious diseases, including viruses, bacteria, fungi, rickettsiae, parasites, and others.

(7) Plant pests refer to organisms such as fungi, bacteria, viruses, insects, nematodes, weeds, harmful rodents, mollusks, and others that can cause harm to crops, forests, and other plants.

(8) Human genetic resources include human genetic resource materials and human genetic resource information. Human genetic resource materials refer to genetic materials such as organs, tissues, and cells that contain human genomes, genes, or other genetic substances. Human genetic resource information refers to data and other information generated from the use of human genetic resource materials.

(9) Antimicrobial resistance refers to the resistance of microorganisms to antimicrobial drugs, rendering such drugs unable to effectively control microbial infections.

(10) Biological weapons refer to microbial agents, other biological agents, and biological toxins, regardless of origin or method of production, in types or quantities not justified for prophylactic, protective, or other peaceful purposes; they also include weapons, equipment, or delivery systems designed to use such biological agents or toxins for hostile purposes or in armed conflict.

(11) Bioterrorism refers to the intentional use of pathogenic microorganisms, biological toxins, or other agents to carry out attacks, harming human, animal, or plant health, causing social panic, and attempting to achieve specific political objectives.

Article 86: Where biosafety information constitutes a state secret, confidentiality management shall be conducted in accordance with the Law of the People's Republic of China on Guarding State Secrets and other relevant state confidentiality provisions.

Article 87: Biosafety activities of the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be separately stipulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with the principles set forth in this Law.

Article 88: This Law shall take effect on April 15, 2021.
