# Law of the People's Republic of China on Reserve Officers

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 10, 1995

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Reserve Officers adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 28, 2010

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Military Service Law for the purposes of improving the reserve officer system, perfecting the mobilization system of the national armed forces, and strengthening the building of national defense reserve forces.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "reserve officers" refer to reservists who are designated as holding a position at or above the platoon level or a primary-level or above professional technical position in the People's Liberation Army, who are awarded corresponding reserve officer ranks, and who are registered with the military service authorities.

Article 3 Reserve officers are an important component of the national defense reserve forces and one of the main sources for supplementing active-duty officers in wartime.

Reserve officers shall perform the duties conferred by law and enjoy the status and honors corresponding to their duties. The State shall, in accordance with the law, protect the lawful rights and interests and corresponding treatment of reserve officers.

Article 4 Reserve officers are classified, according to the nature of their positions, into military officers, political officers, logistics officers, equipment officers, and professional technical officers.

Officer reserve service is divided into two categories based on the needs of peacetime management and wartime mobilization: reserve officers serving in reserve units and those pre-assigned to active-duty units constitute the first category of officer reserve service; other reserve officers constitute the second category of officer reserve service.

Article 5 The management of reserve officers nationwide shall be under the leadership of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and shall be directed by the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army.

The political departments of military regions, provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), and military sub-commands (garrison commands) shall be responsible for the management of reserve officers within their respective areas. The political departments of the services and arms shall be responsible for the relevant management of reserve officers in their respective units.

The people's armed forces departments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts (hereinafter collectively referred to as county people's armed forces departments) shall be responsible for the specific management of reserve officers within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The relevant departments of the State Council and the local people's governments at various levels shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties and the provisions of this Law and relevant laws and regulations, properly manage matters concerning reserve officers.

Article 7 The General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army, the political departments of military regions, the political departments of provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), the political departments of military sub-commands (garrison commands), and the county people's armed forces departments shall, together with relevant government departments, establish and improve a joint meeting system for military and local reserve officer work. They shall regularly convene joint meetings attended by relevant military and local departments, local reserve units, and active-duty units to which reserve officers are pre-assigned, to coordinate and resolve issues related to reserve officer work. When special needs arise, meetings may be convened on an ad hoc basis.

Matters decided at the joint meetings shall be handled by the relevant military and local departments in accordance with their respective functions and duties.

Article 8 The work units to which reserve officers belong shall support reserve officers in participating in military training, performing military duties, and fulfilling other military service obligations, and shall assist in the management of reserve officers.

Article 9 Reserve officers shall abide by the Constitution, laws, regulations, and relevant military rules and systems, participate in military training and military duty activities, receive political education, enhance their organizational command capabilities and professional skills, and be ready to be called up for active service at any time.

Article 10 Reserve officers who make outstanding contributions in the process of fulfilling military service obligations shall be commended, awarded merits, or conferred honorary titles in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Units and individuals that achieve outstanding results in reserve officer work shall be commended or rewarded in accordance with relevant provisions.

## Chapter II Conditions, Sources, and Selection of Reserve Officers

Article 11 Reserve officers shall meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Be loyal to the motherland and abide by the Constitution, laws, and regulations;
(2) Obey orders and follow commands;
(3) Meet the age requirements for officer reserve service as stipulated in this Law;
(4) Have been discharged from active service or have received military professional training and passed the assessment, possessing the scientific and cultural knowledge, organizational command capabilities, or professional skills corresponding to their positions;
(5) Be in good health.

Article 12 Reserve officers shall be selected from the following personnel:

(1) Officers and civilian cadres discharged from active service;
(2) Soldiers discharged from active service;
(3) Full-time people's armed forces cadres and militia cadres;
(4) Graduates of regular institutions of higher education;
(5) Professional and technical personnel from non-military departments;
(6) Other citizens who meet the basic conditions for reserve officers.

Article 13 The plan for selecting reserve officers shall be determined by the Central Military Commission, and the General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army shall organize its implementation together with relevant departments.

Article 14 For reserve officers selected from among officers and civilian cadres discharged from active service in accordance with Article 12 of this Law, the political organs of military units at or above the regiment level shall propose opinions on their transfer to officer reserve service. After approval in accordance with the prescribed authority, they shall register as reserve officers with the county people's armed forces department at the place of resettlement.

For reserve officers selected from among personnel other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, the following procedures shall be followed:

(1) The grassroots people's armed forces department or the unit to which they belong shall make recommendations in accordance with the plan and conditions prescribed by the higher authorities;
(2) For those selected for the first category of officer reserve service, the relevant reserve unit or active-duty unit shall, together with the county people's armed forces department, jointly review and confirm the candidates; for those selected for the second category of officer reserve service, the county people's armed forces department shall review and confirm the candidates;
(3) The training unit shall organize the training;
(4) Approval shall be granted in accordance with the prescribed authority;
(5) The county people's armed forces department shall register them as reserve officers.

## Chapter III Position Levels and Positions of Reserve Officers

Article 15 The position levels for reserve military, political, logistics, and equipment officers are set as follows: division commander level, deputy division commander level, regiment commander level, deputy regiment commander level, battalion commander level, deputy battalion commander level, company commander level, deputy company commander level, and platoon leader level.

The position levels for reserve professional technical officers are set as follows: senior professional technical position, intermediate professional technical position, and junior professional technical position.

Article 16 Personnel determined to serve as reserve officers shall have their position levels determined.

For personnel discharged from active service and transferred to officer reserve service, the determination of their position levels shall be handled in accordance with the authority for appointment and removal of corresponding position levels for active-duty officers.

For other personnel serving as reserve officers other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, the determination of their position levels shall be approved in accordance with the following prescribed authority:

(1) The determination of the division-level officer position level and the senior professional technical officer position level shall be approved by the principal commander of a military region-level unit;
(2) The determination of the regiment-level officer position level and the intermediate professional technical officer position level shall be approved by the principal commander of a corps-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers;
(3) The determination of the battalion-level and below officer position levels and the junior professional technical officer position level shall be approved by the principal commander of a division-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

Article 17 For reserve officers serving in reserve units and those pre-assigned to active-duty units, in addition to having their position levels determined in accordance with Article 16 of this Law, their positions shall be appointed and removed in accordance with the following prescribed authority:

(1) The authority for appointing and removing battalion-level and above officer positions and senior, intermediate, and junior professional technical officer positions shall be exercised in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3 of Article 16 of this Law;
(2) The positions of company commander, deputy company commander, and platoon leader shall be appointed and removed by the principal commander of a regiment-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

Article 18 Reserve officers shall be subject to assessment. The assessment work shall be organized and implemented jointly by the unit to which the reserve officer belongs or the military service authority, together with relevant local departments, in accordance with relevant state provisions and based on their respective functions and duties. The assessment results shall serve as the main basis for appointing and removing reserve officer positions.

Article 19 The determination of position levels and the appointment and removal of positions of reserve officers shall be handled in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Ranks of Reserve Officers

Article 20 The State implements a rank system for reserve officers.

The ranks of reserve officers are titles and symbols that distinguish the levels of reserve officers, indicate their status, and represent the honor conferred upon them by the State.

Article 21 The ranks of reserve officers are divided into the following three grades and eight levels:

(1) Reserve general officers: Reserve Major General;
(2) Reserve field officers: Reserve Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major;
(3) Reserve company officers: Reserve Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant.

Article 22 The ranks of reserve officers are divided into:

(1) Reserve military, political, logistics, and equipment officers: Reserve Major General, Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant;
(2) Reserve professional technical officers: Reserve Professional Technical Major General, Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant.

For reserve officers of the Navy and the Air Force, the words "Navy" or "Air Force" shall be prefixed to their ranks.

Article 23 Reserve officers shall have ranks corresponding to their position levels.

Reserve military, political, logistics, and equipment officers shall have the following ranks corresponding to their position levels:

Division commander level: Reserve Senior Colonel, Major General;
Deputy division commander level: Reserve Colonel, Senior Colonel;
Regiment commander level: Reserve Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel;
Deputy regiment commander level: Reserve Lieutenant Colonel, Major;
Battalion commander level: Reserve Major, Lieutenant Colonel;
Deputy battalion commander level: Reserve Captain, Major;
Company commander level: Reserve Captain, First Lieutenant;
Deputy company commander level: Reserve First Lieutenant, Captain;
Platoon leader level: Reserve Second Lieutenant, First Lieutenant.

Reserve professional technical officers shall have the following ranks corresponding to their position levels:

Senior professional technical position: Reserve Professional Technical Major General, Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major;
Intermediate professional technical position: Reserve Professional Technical Senior Colonel, Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain;
Junior professional technical position: Reserve Professional Technical Lieutenant Colonel, Major, Captain, First Lieutenant, Second Lieutenant.

Article 24 During periods when reserve officers participate in military training or perform military duties, a reserve officer with a higher rank shall be the superior of a reserve officer with a lower rank; if a reserve officer with a higher rank is subordinate in position to a reserve officer with a lower rank, the one with the higher position shall be the superior.

Article 25 The assessment and conferment of ranks on reserve officers shall be based on their position levels, length of service (tenure), moral character, abilities, and work performance.

Article 26 The conferment of ranks on reserve officers shall be approved in accordance with the following prescribed authority:

(1) Reserve Major General and Senior Colonel shall be approved and conferred by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;
(2) Reserve Colonel shall be approved and conferred by the principal commander of a military region-level unit;
(3) Reserve Lieutenant Colonel and Major shall be approved and conferred by the principal commander of a corps-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers;
(4) Reserve Captain, First Lieutenant, and Second Lieutenant shall be approved and conferred by the principal commander of a division-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

Article 27 For officers and civilian cadres transferred to officer reserve service, their reserve officer ranks shall be determined according to their original active-duty officer rank level or civilian cadre grade.

Article 28 The ranks of reserve officers shall be promoted in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For officers approved for discharge from active service and transfer to officer reserve service, if their rank has reached the time limit for promotion and they meet the prescribed conditions, their reserve officer rank may be one level higher than their original active-duty officer rank;
(2) If a reserve officer's rank is lower than the minimum rank corresponding to their new position level due to a promotion in position level, their rank shall be promoted early to the minimum rank corresponding to the new position level;
(3) For reserve officers from Second Lieutenant to Colonel, if they meet the prescribed conditions and promotion time limits, their reserve officer ranks may be promoted step by step within the range of ranks corresponding to their position levels;
(4) Promotion from Reserve Senior Colonel to Reserve Major General shall be by selection;
(5) If a reserve officer has made outstanding contributions in the process of fulfilling military service obligations, their reserve officer rank may be promoted early.

The conditions, time limits, and procedures for the promotion of reserve officer ranks shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 29 The promotion of reserve officer ranks shall be approved in accordance with the following prescribed authority:

(1) Promotion from Reserve Senior Colonel to Reserve Major General and from Reserve Colonel to Reserve Senior Colonel shall be approved by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;
(2) Promotion from Reserve Lieutenant Colonel to Reserve Colonel shall be approved by the principal commander of a military region-level unit;
(3) Promotion from Reserve Major to Reserve Lieutenant Colonel and from Reserve Captain to Reserve Major shall be approved by the principal commander of a corps-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers;
(4) Promotion from Reserve First Lieutenant to Reserve Captain and from Reserve Second Lieutenant to Reserve First Lieutenant shall be approved by the principal commander of a division-level unit with the authority to appoint and remove officers.

Article 30 If a reserve officer violates military discipline, they may be subject to demotion in rank in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission. The authority to approve the demotion of a reserve officer's rank shall be the same as the authority to approve the conferment of that rank.

Demotion in rank shall not apply to reserve officers holding the rank of Second Lieutenant.

Article 31 For personnel whose status as reserve officers is revoked, their reserve officer ranks shall be revoked. The authority to approve the revocation of a reserve officer's rank shall be the same as the authority to approve the conferment of that rank.

Article 32 If a reserve officer commits a crime and is lawfully sentenced to deprivation of political rights or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years, their reserve officer rank shall be stripped. The authority to approve the stripping of a reserve officer's rank shall be the same as the authority to approve the conferment of that rank.

Article 33 After a reserve officer withdraws from reserve service, their reserve officer rank shall be retained, with the word "Retired" prefixed to their rank.

Article 34 The designs of shoulder boards, insignia, and methods of wearing for reserve officer ranks shall be promulgated by the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter V Registration of Reserve Officers

Article 35 The registration of reserve officers shall be handled by the county people's armed forces department.

Personnel discharged from active service and determined to be transferred to officer reserve service shall, within thirty days from the date of arrival at the place of resettlement, register as reserve officers with the county people's armed forces department; other personnel shall register as reserve officers at the same time as they are determined to serve as reserve officers.

Personnel determined to serve as reserve officers who work in state organs, social organizations, enterprises, or institutions shall register as reserve officers with the county people's armed forces department at the place of their work unit; other personnel determined to serve as reserve officers shall register as reserve officers with the county people's armed forces department at the place of their household registration.

When handling the registration of reserve officers, the county people's armed forces department shall verify the identity, experience, and other relevant information of the personnel determined to serve as reserve officers in person, explain relevant matters, and notify their work units of the registration status.

Article 36 If a reserve officer needs to change their place of registration due to job transfer or relocation, they shall go through the transfer-out procedures and, within thirty days from the date of arrival at the new work unit or place of residence, go through the transfer-in procedures with the county people's armed forces department at the new location.

When the county people's armed forces department handles the transfer-out and transfer-in procedures for personnel serving in the first category of officer reserve service, it shall notify the unit to which the reserve officer belongs.

Article 37 The county people's armed forces department shall cancel the registration of a reserve officer under any of the following circumstances:

(1) They withdraw from reserve service;
(2) They settle abroad;
(3) They die;
(4) Their status as a reserve officer is revoked.

Article 38 The county people's armed forces department must, in accordance with regulations, conduct an annual check of reserve officers registered within its administrative region and report the statistics level by level.

## Chapter VI Training of Reserve Officers

Article 39 Personnel who have not served on active duty or have not received military professional training and are selected as reserve officers shall receive military professional training before their position levels as reserve officers are determined.

Article 40 During their period of reserve service, reserve officers shall receive military training and political education in accordance with the provisions of the Military Service Law and this Law.

Personnel serving in the first category of officer reserve service shall undergo corresponding training each time they are promoted to a command position, so as to possess the organizational command capabilities required for the position.

Reserve officers holding professional technical positions shall receive relevant professional technical training.

Article 41 The State Council and the Central Military Commission may, when necessary, decide to implement emergency training for reserve officers. Reserve officers must receive emergency training as prescribed.

Article 42 The military training and political education syllabi for reserve officers shall be formulated by the General Staff Department, the General Political Department, the General Logistics Department, and the General Armament Department of the People's Liberation Army.

Units responsible for the training of reserve officers shall, in accordance with the military training and political education syllabi for reserve officers and based on their respective functions and duties, formulate implementation plans.

Article 43 The training of personnel serving in the first category of officer reserve service shall be organized and implemented by the provincial military command (garrison command, garrison command) or the unit to which they belong; the training of personnel serving in the second category of officer reserve service shall be organized and implemented by the provincial military command (garrison command, garrison command), the military sub-command (garrison command), and the county people's armed forces department. The General Staff Department, the General Political Department, the services and arms, and the military regions shall organize the training of reserve officers as needed.

The work units and local people's governments of reserve officers shall support and assist in the training of reserve officers to ensure the completion of training tasks.

Military academies, reserve officer training institutions, active-duty units, reserve units, and relevant regular institutions of higher education shall, in accordance with relevant state provisions, undertake the task of training reserve officers.

## Chapter VII Call-up of Reserve Officers

Article 44 The provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), military sub-commands (garrison commands), and county people's armed forces departments shall, based on the needs of national defense mobilization, formulate call-up plans for reserve officers together with the relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level. Among these, the call-up plans for personnel serving in the first category of officer reserve service shall be formulated together with the relevant reserve units and active-duty units.

The provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), military sub-commands (garrison commands), county people's armed forces departments, as well as reserve units and active-duty units to which reserve officers are pre-assigned, shall organize call-up drills, and the relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall support and assist.

Article 45 After the State decides to implement national defense mobilization, the county people's armed forces department shall, based on orders from higher authorities, promptly issue call-up notices to reserve officers identified as call-up targets; when necessary, reserve units and active-duty units to which reserve officers are pre-assigned may also directly issue call-up notices to their subordinate reserve officers identified as call-up targets, and shall notify the county people's armed forces department at the place of registration of the reserve officers.

Article 46 Upon receiving a call-up notice, a reserve officer must report to the designated location at the prescribed time. If temporarily unable to respond to the call-up due to illness, injury, or other reasons, they may be granted a deferment after verification by the county people's armed forces department and approval by the military service authority at the next higher level.**Article 47** After the state decides to implement national defense mobilization, reserve officers who have not yet been called up shall not leave their reserve registration location without the approval of their unit or the county people's armed forces department at the place of registration; those who have already left shall, upon receiving the call-up notice, immediately return or report to the designated location as required by the notice.

**Article 48** Reserve officers who are called up and transferred to active service shall have their appointment orders issued in accordance with the authority for appointing and removing active-duty officers during wartime, be re-commissioned with active-duty officer ranks, and perform the corresponding duties of active-duty officers; those not transferred to active service shall perform duties in accordance with the tasks assigned by higher authorities.

**Article 49** The unit to which a called-up reserve officer belongs shall assist the military service organs in carrying out the call-up of reserve officers.

**Article 50** After the state lifts the implementation measures for national defense mobilization, reserve officers who were called up and transferred to active service shall be discharged from active service, unless they are required to continue serving based on the needs of the military.

## Chapter VIII: Treatment of Reserve Officers

**Article 51** The work performance of reserve officers in fulfilling their military service obligations shall serve as one of the bases for their unit's assessment, evaluation, promotion, and salary grade adjustments; those who have rendered meritorious service or been awarded honorary titles shall enjoy the same rewards and preferential treatment granted by the state and local authorities to active-duty personnel with equivalent meritorious service or honors.

**Article 52** During participation in military training or execution of military duties, reserve officers shall wear uniforms as prescribed; when participating in major celebrations such as National Day, Army Day, or other significant events, they may wear the standard uniform of reserve officers and wear reserve officer rank epaulettes and insignia.

**Article 53** During participation in military training or execution of military duties, if a reserve officer's work unit is a state organ, social organization, enterprise, or public institution, their unit shall continue to pay their salary and bonuses, and their welfare benefits shall remain unchanged.

For other reserve officers not covered by the preceding paragraph, during participation in military training or execution of military duties, they shall be provided with compensation for lost work time. The specific methods and standards shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Reserve officers participating in military training or execution of military duties shall receive meal subsidies and reimbursement for round-trip travel expenses in accordance with state regulations.

**Article 54** Reserve officers who participate in military training or execute military duties as prescribed shall receive subsidies based on the duration of such training or duties and their rank. The subsidy standards shall be formulated by the Ministry of Finance, the General Staff Department, the General Political Department, and the General Logistics Department of the People's Liberation Army, with the necessary funds guaranteed by the central finance.

**Article 55** If a reserve officer is killed or disabled during military activities such as military training or execution of military duties, the matter shall be handled with reference to the state's regulations on pensions and preferential treatment for military personnel.

## Chapter IX: Retirement of Reserve Officers

**Article 56** When a reserve officer reaches the maximum age for peacetime reserve service, they shall retire from the reserve.

**Article 57** The maximum ages for peacetime reserve service for reserve officers in military, political, logistics, and equipment branches are:

- For those holding division-level positions: 55 years old;
- For those holding regiment-level positions: 50 years old;
- For those holding battalion-level positions: 45 years old;
- For those holding company-level positions: 40 years old;
- For those holding platoon-level positions: 35 years old.

If personnel serving in the first category of officer reserve are genuinely needed for work, the maximum age for peacetime reserve service may be appropriately extended upon approval, but the extension shall not exceed five years.

**Article 58** The maximum ages for peacetime reserve service for reserve technical officers are:

- For those holding senior technical positions: 60 years old;
- For those holding intermediate technical positions: 55 years old;
- For those holding junior technical positions: 50 years old.

**Article 59** Reserve officers who have not reached the maximum age for peacetime reserve service but are unable to continue serving due to injury, illness, disability, or other reasons shall retire from the reserve.

**Article 60** The authority for approving the retirement of reserve officers from the reserve is the same as that stipulated in Paragraph 3 of Article 16 of this Law.

**Article 61** When a reserve officer retires from the reserve, corresponding honorary medals and certificates shall be issued based on their length of service and contributions.

## Legal Liability

**Article 62** If a reserve officer violates discipline during participation in military training or execution of military duties, they shall be punished in accordance with relevant regulations; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 63** If a reserve officer commits any of the following acts, the people's government at the county level shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is not made within the specified period, the local people's government at or above the county level shall compel them to fulfill their military service obligations; if the person is a state functionary, they shall be punished according to law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Refusing or evading reserve registration;
(2) Refusing or evading military training or execution of military duties;
(3) Refusing or evading call-up.

**Article 64** In the management of reserve officers, if anyone accepts bribes, engages in favoritism or malpractice, or neglects their duties, causing serious losses to reserve work, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

If anyone obstructs a reserve officer from participating in military training, executing military duties, or fulfilling other military service obligations, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is refused, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished according to law.

## Chapter XI: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 65** This Law shall apply to personnel who have been discharged from active service in the Chinese People's Armed Police Force and serve in the reserve of the People's Liberation Army as officers.

**Article 66** This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1996.
