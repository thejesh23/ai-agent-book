# Vocational Education Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Education Law and the Labor Law, for the purpose of implementing the strategy of rejuvenating the country through science and education, developing vocational education, improving the quality of workers, and promoting socialist modernization.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to various types of vocational school education at all levels and various forms of vocational training. Specialized training for state functionaries conducted by state organs shall be governed by other laws and administrative regulations.

Article 3 Vocational education is an important component of the national education enterprise and an important avenue for promoting economic and social development and labor employment.

The State develops vocational education, promotes reform in vocational education, improves the quality of vocational education, and establishes and perfects a vocational education system that meets the needs of the socialist market economy and social progress.

Article 4 In implementing vocational education, the State's educational policy must be carried out; ideological and political education and professional ethics education shall be provided to the educated; vocational knowledge shall be imparted, vocational skills shall be cultivated, vocational guidance shall be conducted, and the quality of the educated shall be comprehensively improved.

Article 5 Citizens have the right to receive vocational education in accordance with the law.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate the development of vocational education into their plans for national economic and social development.

Trade organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall fulfill their obligations to implement vocational education in accordance with the law.

Article 7 The State shall take measures to develop vocational education in rural areas and support the development of vocational education in ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

The State shall take measures to help women receive vocational education, organize unemployed persons to receive various forms of vocational training, and support the development of vocational education for persons with disabilities.

Article 8 The implementation of vocational education shall, based on actual needs, be compatible with the national occupational classification and occupational grade standards, and a system of academic certificates, training certificates, and vocational qualification certificates shall be implemented.

The State implements a system requiring workers to receive necessary vocational education before employment or before taking up their posts.

Article 9 The State encourages and organizes scientific research in vocational education.

Article 10 The State shall give awards to units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in vocational education.

Article 11 The administrative department of education under the State Council shall be responsible for the overall planning, comprehensive coordination, and macro-management of vocational education work.

The administrative department of education, the administrative department of labor, and other relevant departments under the State Council shall, within the scope of their duties prescribed by the State Council, be respectively responsible for the relevant vocational education work.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the leadership, overall coordination, supervision, and evaluation of vocational education work within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Vocational Education System

Article 12 The State, based on the level of economic development and the popularity of education in different regions, implements educational diversion at different stages with a focus on the period after junior secondary school, and establishes and perfects a vocational education system that combines vocational school education with vocational training, and that interconnects and develops in coordination with other types of education.

Article 13 Vocational school education is divided into primary, secondary, and higher vocational school education.

Primary and secondary vocational school education shall be implemented by primary and secondary vocational schools respectively; higher vocational school education shall, based on needs and conditions, be implemented by higher vocational schools or by regular institutions of higher learning. Other schools may, in accordance with the overall planning of the administrative department of education, implement vocational school education at the corresponding level.

Article 14 Vocational training includes pre-employment training, job-transfer training, apprenticeship training, on-the-job training, post-transfer training, and other vocational training, which may be divided into primary, intermediate, and advanced levels based on actual circumstances.

Vocational training shall be implemented by corresponding vocational training institutions and vocational schools.

Other schools or educational institutions may, based on their educational capabilities, conduct various forms of vocational training oriented towards society.

Article 15 In addition to being implemented by educational institutions for persons with disabilities, vocational education for persons with disabilities shall be provided by vocational schools and vocational training institutions at all levels and of all types, as well as other educational institutions, which shall admit students with disabilities in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 16 Regular secondary schools may, in light of local conditions, offer courses in vocational education or appropriately increase the teaching content of vocational education based on actual needs.

## Chapter III Implementation of Vocational Education

Article 17 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions that play a backbone and exemplary role, and shall provide guidance and support to vocational schools and vocational training institutions established in accordance with the law by rural areas, enterprises, institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individual citizens.

Article 18 People's governments at the county level shall, in order to meet the needs of the coordinated development of the rural economy, science and technology, and education, conduct various forms of vocational education, carry out training in practical technologies, and promote the development of vocational education in rural areas.

Article 19 Competent government departments and trade organizations shall establish or jointly establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions, and shall organize, coordinate, and guide enterprises and institutions within their respective industries to establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions.

The State encourages the use of modern teaching methods to develop vocational education.

Article 20 Enterprises shall, based on their actual conditions, provide vocational education in a planned manner to their own employees and persons to be recruited.

Enterprises may independently or jointly establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions, or may entrust schools and vocational training institutions to provide vocational education to their own employees and persons to be recruited.

Employees engaged in technical trades must undergo training before taking up their posts; employees engaged in special operations must undergo training and obtain qualifications for special operations.

Article 21 The State encourages institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individual citizens to establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The measures for overseas organizations and individuals to establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions within the territory of China shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 22 Where vocational schools or vocational training institutions are jointly established, the founders shall sign a joint school-running contract.

Where competent government departments, trade organizations, enterprises, or institutions entrust schools or vocational training institutions to implement vocational education, they shall sign an entrustment contract.

Article 23 In implementing vocational education, vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall integrate education with production, serve the local economic construction, maintain close ties with enterprises, and cultivate practical talents and skilled workers.

Vocational schools and vocational training institutions may establish enterprises or practice sites related to vocational education.

Article 24 The establishment of a vocational school must meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Having an organizational structure and articles of association;
(2) Having qualified teachers;
(3) Having teaching premises that meet prescribed standards, and facilities and equipment suitable for vocational education;
(4) Having the necessary funds for running the school and a stable source of funding.

The establishment of a vocational training institution must meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Having an organizational structure and management system;
(2) Having teachers and management personnel suitable for the training tasks;
(3) Having premises, facilities, and equipment suitable for conducting the training;
(4) Having corresponding funds.

The establishment, alteration, and termination of vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall be carried out in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 25 Students who receive vocational school education and pass the school's assessment shall be issued academic certificates in accordance with relevant state regulations. Students who receive vocational training and pass the assessment of the training vocational school or vocational training institution shall be issued training certificates in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Academic certificates and training certificates shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, serve as credentials for graduates and completers of vocational schools and vocational training institutions to engage in occupations.

## Chapter IV Guarantee Conditions for Vocational Education

Article 26 The State encourages the raising of funds for the development of vocational education through various channels in accordance with the law.

Article 27 People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate the average funding standard per student for vocational schools in their respective regions; the relevant departments under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the finance department under the State Council, formulate the average funding standard per student for vocational schools under their respective departments. The founders of vocational schools shall allocate vocational education funds in full according to the average funding standard per student.

The fiscal funds used by people's governments at all levels and the relevant departments under the State Council for establishing vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall be gradually increased.

No organization or individual may misappropriate or withhold funds for vocational education.

Article 28 Enterprises shall bear the costs of providing vocational education to their own employees and persons to be recruited. The specific measures shall be prescribed by the relevant departments under the State Council in conjunction with the finance department under the State Council, or by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the law.

Article 29 If an enterprise fails to implement vocational education in accordance with the provisions of Article 20 of this Law, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, the government may collect the vocational education funds that the enterprise should bear and use them for vocational education in the region.

Article 30 The local surcharges for education decided to be levied by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Education Law may be used exclusively or in a certain proportion for vocational education.

Article 31 People's governments at all levels may appropriately use funds for rural scientific and technological development and technology promotion for rural vocational training.

Article 32 Vocational schools and vocational training institutions may charge appropriate tuition fees to students receiving secondary and higher vocational school education and vocational training, and shall reduce or waive fees as appropriate for students with financial difficulties and students with disabilities. The fee-charging measures shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The State supports enterprises, institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individual citizens in establishing vocational education scholarships and student loans in accordance with relevant state regulations to reward students with outstanding academic performance or to subsidize students with financial difficulties.

Article 33 The income from enterprises run by vocational schools and vocational training institutions and from social services shall be mainly used for the development of vocational education.

Article 34 The State encourages financial institutions to use credit instruments to support the development of vocational education.

Article 35 The State encourages enterprises, institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and individual citizens to donate funds to support vocational education, and encourages overseas organizations and individuals to provide funding and donations for vocational education. The funding and donations provided must be used for vocational education.

Article 36 People's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall incorporate the training and further training of vocational education teachers into the plan for the construction of the teaching staff, ensuring that the vocational education teaching staff meets the needs of the development of vocational education.

Vocational schools and vocational training institutions may employ professional and technical personnel, persons with special skills, and teachers from other educational institutions as part-time teachers. Relevant departments and units shall provide convenience.

Article 37 The relevant departments under the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level, and the organizations and individual citizens that establish vocational schools and vocational training institutions shall strengthen the construction of production practice bases for vocational education.

Enterprises and institutions shall accept students and teachers from vocational schools and vocational training institutions for practice; for those engaged in on-the-job practice, appropriate labor remuneration shall be given.

Article 38 People's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall establish and perfect a service system for vocational education, and strengthen the editing, publication, and distribution of teaching materials for vocational education.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 39 Any violation of the provisions of the Education Law in vocational education activities shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Education Law.

Article 40 This Law shall come into force as of September 1, 1996.
