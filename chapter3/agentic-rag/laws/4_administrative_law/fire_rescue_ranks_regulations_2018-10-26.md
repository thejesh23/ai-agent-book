# Fire and Rescue Service Rank Regulations of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** These Regulations are formulated in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the standardization, specialization, and professionalization of the national comprehensive fire and rescue teams, enhance the sense of responsibility, honor, and organizational discipline of fire and rescue personnel, and facilitate the command, management, and lawful performance of duties by the national comprehensive fire and rescue teams.

**Article 2** The national comprehensive fire and rescue teams shall implement a fire and rescue rank system.

The recipients of fire and rescue ranks are in-service personnel of the comprehensive fire and rescue teams who are included in the national administrative establishment and are under the unified leadership and management of the emergency management department of the State Council.

**Article 3** Fire and rescue ranks are titles and insignia that indicate the identity of fire and rescue personnel and distinguish their levels. They serve as the basis for the honors and corresponding benefits granted by the State to fire and rescue personnel.

**Article 4** Personnel with a higher fire and rescue rank are considered superiors to those with a lower rank. When a person with a higher rank is subordinate in position to a person with a lower rank, the person holding a leadership position or the higher leadership position is considered the superior.

**Article 5** The emergency management department of the State Council is responsible for the work concerning fire and rescue ranks.

## Chapter II Establishment of Fire and Rescue Rank Levels

**Article 6** Fire and rescue ranks shall be established separately for management and command personnel, professional and technical personnel, and firefighters.

**Article 7** The fire and rescue ranks for management and command personnel shall consist of the following three categories and eleven levels:

(1) Chief, Deputy Chief, Assistant Chief;
(2) Commanders: Senior Commander, First-Level Commander, Second-Level Commander, Third-Level Commander;
(3) Command Officers: First-Level Command Officer, Second-Level Command Officer, Third-Level Command Officer, Fourth-Level Command Officer.

**Article 8** The fire and rescue ranks for professional and technical personnel shall consist of the following two categories and eight levels, prefixed with "Professional and Technical":

(1) Commanders: Senior Commander, First-Level Commander, Second-Level Commander, Third-Level Commander;
(2) Command Officers: First-Level Command Officer, Second-Level Command Officer, Third-Level Command Officer, Fourth-Level Command Officer.

**Article 9** The fire and rescue ranks for firefighters shall consist of the following three categories and eight levels:

(1) Senior Firefighters: First-Level Fire Chief, Second-Level Fire Chief, Third-Level Fire Chief;
(2) Intermediate Firefighters: First-Level Fire Sergeant, Second-Level Fire Sergeant;
(3) Junior Firefighters: Third-Level Firefighter, Fourth-Level Firefighter, Probationary Firefighter.

## Chapter III Establishment of Fire and Rescue Rank Grades

**Article 10** Management and command personnel shall be assigned fire and rescue ranks according to the following position grades:

(1) Principal of the emergency management department of the State Council: Chief;
(2) Principal of the fire and rescue team leadership command organization and the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council: Deputy Chief;
(3) Deputy principal of the fire and rescue team leadership command organization and the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council: Assistant Chief;
(4) Principal at the corps level: Senior Commander;
(5) Deputy principal at the corps level: First-Level Commander;
(6) Principal at the detachment level: Second-Level Commander;
(7) Deputy principal at the detachment level: Third-Level Commander;
(8) Principal at the brigade level: First-Level Command Officer;
(9) Deputy principal at the brigade level: Second-Level Command Officer;
(10) Principal at the station (squadron) level: Third-Level Command Officer;
(11) Deputy principal at the station (squadron) level: Fourth-Level Command Officer.

**Article 11** Professional and technical personnel shall be assigned fire and rescue ranks according to the following position grades:

(1) Senior professional and technical positions: Senior Commander to Third-Level Commander;
(2) Intermediate professional and technical positions: First-Level Commander to Second-Level Command Officer;
(3) Junior professional and technical positions: Third-Level Commander to Fourth-Level Command Officer.

**Article 12** Firefighters shall be assigned fire and rescue ranks according to the following years of service:

(1) Twenty-four or more years of service: First-Level Fire Chief;
(2) Twenty or more years of service: Second-Level Fire Chief;
(3) Sixteen or more years of service: Third-Level Fire Chief;
(4) Twelve or more years of service: First-Level Fire Sergeant;
(5) Eight or more years of service: Second-Level Fire Sergeant;
(6) Five or more years of service: Third-Level Firefighter;
(7) Two or more years of service: Fourth-Level Firefighter;
(8) Less than two years of service: Probationary Firefighter.

## Chapter IV Initial Conferment of Fire and Rescue Ranks

**Article 13** The conferment of fire and rescue ranks shall be based on the fire and rescue personnel's current position, moral character and performance, academic degrees, length of service in the position, and total years of service.

**Article 14** For newly appointed management and command personnel and professional and technical personnel, the initial fire and rescue rank shall be conferred according to the following provisions:

(1) For those recruited from graduates of regular institutions of higher education, those with an associate or bachelor's degree shall be conferred the rank of Fourth-Level Command Officer; those with a master's degree shall be conferred the rank of Third-Level Command Officer; those with a doctoral degree shall be conferred the rank of First-Level Command Officer;
(2) For those selected and appointed as management and command personnel or professional and technical personnel from among firefighters, the corresponding fire and rescue rank shall be conferred according to the position grade to which they are appointed;
(3) For those transferred from state organs or other rescue teams, or recruited from qualified social personnel, the corresponding fire and rescue rank shall be conferred according to the position grade to which they are appointed.

**Article 15** For newly appointed firefighters, the initial fire and rescue rank shall be conferred according to the following provisions:

(1) For those recruited from high school graduates, current students or graduates of regular institutions of higher education, the rank of Probationary Firefighter shall be conferred;
(2) For those recruited from retired soldiers, their years of military service shall be counted towards their years of service, and the corresponding fire and rescue rank shall be conferred in accordance with Article 12 of these Regulations;
(3) For those recruited from other rescue teams or social personnel with professional skills, the corresponding fire and rescue rank shall be conferred based on their years of work in the relevant professional field, by comparison with personnel of equivalent conditions in the national comprehensive fire and rescue teams.

**Article 16** The initial conferment of fire and rescue ranks for management and command personnel and professional and technical personnel shall be approved according to the following authorities:

(1) The conferment of Chief, Deputy Chief, and Assistant Chief shall be approved by the Premier of the State Council;
(2) The conferment of Senior Commander, First-Level Commander, and Second-Level Commander shall be approved by the principal leader of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(3) The conferment of Third-Level Commander and First-Level Command Officer shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit after obtaining the consent of the emergency management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; for personnel of the forest fire teams, it shall be approved by the principal leader of the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(4) The conferment of Second-Level Command Officer, Third-Level Command Officer, and Fourth-Level Command Officer shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit.

**Article 17** The initial conferment of fire and rescue ranks for firefighters shall be approved according to the following authorities:

(1) The conferment of First-Level Fire Chief, Second-Level Fire Chief, and Third-Level Fire Chief shall be approved by the principal leader of the fire and rescue team leadership command organization or the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(2) The conferment of First-Level Fire Sergeant, Second-Level Fire Sergeant, Third-Level Firefighter, Fourth-Level Firefighter, and Probationary Firefighter shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit.

## Chapter V Promotion of Fire and Rescue Ranks

**Article 18** Fire and rescue ranks shall generally be promoted step by step based on adjustments to position grades or years of service.

Fire and rescue personnel promoted to the next higher fire and rescue rank shall be competent in their current duties, abide by laws and regulations, be honest and upright, and have a good work style.

Fire and rescue personnel may only be promoted to the next higher fire and rescue rank after passing relevant training.

**Article 19** The promotion of fire and rescue ranks for management and command personnel and professional and technical personnel shall generally be consistent with the promotion of their position grades.

The promotion of fire and rescue ranks for firefighters shall be carried out in accordance with Article 12 of these Regulations. For firefighters who have passed the national unified entrance examination for regular institutions of higher education and obtained a full-time associate degree or higher, the time spent studying in regular institutions of higher education according to the prescribed academic system shall be considered as service time for the purpose of rank promotion, but shall not be counted towards their length of service for seniority.

**Article 20** The promotion of fire and rescue ranks for management and command personnel and professional and technical personnel shall be approved according to the following authorities:

(1) Promotion to Chief, Deputy Chief, and Assistant Chief shall be approved by the Premier of the State Council;
(2) Promotion to Senior Commander and First-Level Commander shall be approved by the principal leader of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(3) Promotion to Second-Level Commander shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit after obtaining the consent of the emergency management department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; for personnel of the forest fire teams, it shall be approved by the principal leader of the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(4) Promotion to Third-Level Commander and First-Level Command Officer shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit;
(5) Promotion to Second-Level Command Officer and Third-Level Command Officer shall be approved by the principal leader of the detachment-level unit.

**Article 21** The promotion of fire and rescue ranks for firefighters shall be approved according to the following authorities:

(1) Promotion to First-Level Fire Chief, Second-Level Fire Chief, and Third-Level Fire Chief shall be approved by the principal leader of the fire and rescue team leadership command organization or the forest fire team leadership command organization of the emergency management department of the State Council;
(2) Promotion to First-Level Fire Sergeant and Second-Level Fire Sergeant shall be approved by the principal leader of the corps-level unit;
(3) Promotion to Third-Level Firefighter and Fourth-Level Firefighter shall be approved by the principal leader of the detachment-level unit.

**Article 22** Fire and rescue personnel who make significant contributions or demonstrate outstanding moral character and performance in fire and rescue work may have their fire and rescue ranks promoted ahead of schedule.

## Chapter VI Retention, Demotion, and Cancellation of Fire and Rescue Ranks

**Article 23** After fire and rescue personnel retire, their fire and rescue ranks shall be retained.

If fire and rescue personnel withdraw from the fire and rescue teams in accordance with state regulations, or are transferred, resign, or are dismissed, their fire and rescue ranks shall not be retained.

**Article 24** If fire and rescue personnel are transferred to a lower position due to incompetence in their current position, their fire and rescue rank shall be adjusted to the corresponding rank level. The authority for approving such adjustment shall be the same as the authority for approving the original rank.

**Article 25** If fire and rescue personnel are subject to disciplinary sanctions of demotion or removal from office, their fire and rescue rank shall be correspondingly demoted. The authority for approving such demotion shall be the same as the authority for approving the original rank.

Demotion of fire and rescue ranks shall not apply to Fourth-Level Command Officers and Probationary Firefighters.

**Article 26** If fire and rescue personnel are subject to the sanction of expulsion, or are legally sentenced to deprivation of political rights or a fixed-term imprisonment or heavier penalty for a crime, their fire and rescue rank shall be correspondingly cancelled.

If fire and rescue personnel commit a crime after retirement, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 27** The styles and wearing methods of fire and rescue rank insignia shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 28** These Regulations shall come into force on October 27, 2018.
