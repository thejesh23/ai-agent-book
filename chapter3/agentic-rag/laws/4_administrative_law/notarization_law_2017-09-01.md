# Notarization Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on August 28, 2005

First Amendment on April 24, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Compulsory Education Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress

Second Amendment on September 1, 2017, according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate notarization activities, ensure that notarial institutions and notaries perform their duties in accordance with the law, prevent disputes, and protect the lawful rights and interests of natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations.

Article 2 Notarization refers to the activity in which a notarial institution, upon the application of a natural person, legal person, or other organization, certifies the authenticity and legality of civil juristic acts, facts of legal significance, and documents in accordance with statutory procedures.

Article 3 When handling notarization, a notarial institution shall abide by the law and adhere to the principles of objectivity and impartiality.

Article 4 The China Notary Association shall be established nationwide, and local notary associations shall be established in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. The China Notary Association and local notary associations are social group legal persons. The articles of association of the China Notary Association shall be formulated by the congress of representatives and reported to the judicial administration department of the State Council for filing.

Notary associations are self-regulatory organizations of the notary profession, carrying out activities in accordance with their articles of association and supervising the professional conduct of notarial institutions and notaries.

Article 5 Judicial administration departments shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, supervise and guide notarial institutions, notaries, and notary associations.

## Chapter II Notarial Institutions

Article 6 A notarial institution is a certification institution established in accordance with the law, not for profit, independently exercising notarial functions and assuming civil liability.

Article 7 Following the principles of overall planning and rational layout, notarial institutions may be established in counties, cities not divided into districts, cities with districts, municipalities directly under the Central Government, or municipal districts; one or several notarial institutions may be established in a city with districts or a municipality directly under the Central Government. Notarial institutions shall not be established at each administrative level.

Article 8 The establishment of a notarial institution shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having its own name;

(2) Having a fixed place of business;

(3) Having two or more notaries;

(4) Having the necessary funds to carry out notarial business.

Article 9 To establish a notarial institution, the judicial administration department of the locality shall submit an application to the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval in accordance with the prescribed procedures, and a notarial institution practice certificate shall be issued.

Article 10 The person in charge of a notarial institution shall be elected from among notaries with more than three years of practice experience, approved by the judicial administration department of the locality, and reported to the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for filing.

Article 11 Upon the application of a natural person, legal person, or other organization, a notarial institution shall handle the following notarization matters:

(1) Contracts;

(2) Inheritance;

(3) Powers of attorney, declarations, gifts, wills;

(4) Property division;

(5) Bidding, auctions;

(6) Marital status, kinship, adoption relationships;

(7) Birth, survival, death, identity, experience, education, degree, position, professional title, and whether there is a record of criminal offenses;

(8) Articles of association of a company;

(9) Preservation of evidence;

(10) Signatures, seals, dates on documents, and the conformity of copies and photocopies with the original;

(11) Other notarization matters voluntarily applied for by natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations.

For matters that laws and administrative regulations require to be notarized, the relevant natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations shall apply to a notarial institution for notarization.

Article 12 Upon the application of a natural person, legal person, or other organization, a notarial institution may handle the following affairs:

(1) Affairs that laws and administrative regulations require to be registered by a notarial institution;

(2) Deposit;

(3) Custody of wills, estates, or other property, articles, or documents related to notarization matters;

(4) Drafting legal documents related to notarization matters;

(5) Providing legal consultation on notarization.

Article 13 A notarial institution shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Issuing notarial certificates for untrue or illegal matters;

(2) Damaging or altering notarial documents or notarial archives;

(3) Soliciting notarial business by improper means such as denigrating other notarial institutions or notaries, or paying kickbacks or commissions;

(4) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy learned during professional activities;

(5) Charging notarial fees in violation of the prescribed fee standards;

(6) Other acts prohibited by laws, regulations, or the judicial administration department of the State Council.

Article 14 A notarial institution shall establish management systems for business, finance, and assets, supervise the professional conduct of notaries, and establish a system of accountability for professional faults.

Article 15 A notarial institution shall participate in professional liability insurance for notarization.

## Chapter III Notaries

Article 16 A notary is a professional who meets the conditions prescribed by this Law and engages in notarial business in a notarial institution.

Article 17 The number of notaries shall be determined based on the needs of notarial business. The judicial administration department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, based on the establishment of notarial institutions and the needs of notarial business, verify the plan for the allocation of notaries and report it to the judicial administration department of the State Council for filing.

Article 18 To serve as a notary, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Having the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Being at least 25 years old and under 65 years old;

(3) Being fair and upright, abiding by the law, and having good conduct;

(4) Having obtained legal professional qualifications through the national unified legal professional qualification examination;

(5) Having interned in a notarial institution for more than two years, or having more than three years of other legal professional experience and having interned in a notarial institution for more than one year, and having passed the assessment.

Article 19 Persons engaged in legal teaching or research with senior professional titles, or civil servants or lawyers with a bachelor's degree or above who have been engaged in adjudication, prosecution, legal work, or legal services for a full ten years, and who have left their original positions, may serve as notaries after passing the assessment.

Article 20 Persons in any of the following circumstances shall not serve as notaries:

(1) Having no civil capacity or limited civil capacity;

(2) Having been subject to criminal punishment for intentional crimes or duty-related negligent crimes;

(3) Having been dismissed from public office;

(4) Having had their notary or lawyer practice certificates revoked.

Article 21 To serve as a notary, a person who meets the conditions for a notary shall submit an application, be recommended by a notarial institution, and after review and approval by the judicial administration department of the locality and the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, be appointed by the judicial administration department of the State Council, and a notary practice certificate shall be issued by the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 22 A notary shall abide by the law, adhere to professional ethics, perform notarial duties in accordance with the law, and maintain professional confidentiality.

A notary has the right to receive remuneration, enjoy insurance and welfare benefits; has the right to resign, appeal, or lodge a complaint; and shall not be removed or punished except for statutory reasons and through statutory procedures.

Article 23 A notary shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Practicing in two or more notarial institutions simultaneously;

(2) Engaging in other remunerative occupations;

(3) Handling notarization for themselves or their close relatives, or handling notarization in which they or their close relatives have an interest;

(4) Issuing notarial certificates privately;

(5) Issuing notarial certificates for untrue or illegal matters;

(6) Embezzling or misappropriating notarial fees, or embezzling or stealing notarial special articles;

(7) Damaging or altering notarial documents or notarial archives;

(8) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy learned during professional activities;

(9) Other acts prohibited by laws, regulations, or the judicial administration department of the State Council.

Article 24 In any of the following circumstances, a notary shall be removed from office by the judicial administration department of the locality, which shall report to the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to request the judicial administration department of the State Council to remove the notary:

(1) Losing the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Reaching the age of 65 or being unable to continue performing duties due to health reasons;

(3) Voluntarily resigning from the position of notary;

(4) Having their notary practice certificate revoked.

## Chapter IV Notarization Procedures

Article 25 A natural person, legal person, or other organization applying for notarization may submit an application to a notarial institution in the place of domicile, habitual residence, place of the act, or place where the fact occurred.

An application for notarization involving immovable property shall be submitted to the notarial institution in the place where the immovable property is located; the provisions of the preceding paragraph may apply to applications for notarization of powers of attorney, declarations, gifts, or wills involving immovable property.

Article 26 A natural person, legal person, or other organization may entrust another person to handle notarization, except for matters such as wills, survival, and adoption relationships, which shall be handled by the person themselves.

Article 27 The party applying for notarization shall truthfully explain to the notarial institution the relevant circumstances of the matter for which notarization is sought and provide truthful, lawful, and sufficient evidentiary materials; if the evidentiary materials provided are insufficient, the notarial institution may request supplementation.

After accepting a notarization application, the notarial institution shall inform the party of the legal significance of the matter for which notarization is sought and the possible legal consequences, and shall record the content of the information for archiving.

Article 28 When handling notarization, a notarial institution shall, in accordance with the rules for handling different notarization matters, examine the following matters separately:

(1) The identity of the party, their qualifications for applying for the notarization, and their corresponding rights;

(2) Whether the content of the documents provided is complete, whether the meaning is clear, and whether signatures and seals are complete;

(3) Whether the evidentiary materials provided are truthful, lawful, and sufficient;

(4) Whether the matter for which notarization is sought is truthful and lawful.

Article 29 If verification is required or there are doubts regarding the matter for which notarization is sought or the evidentiary materials provided by the party, in accordance with the relevant rules for handling notarization, the notarial institution shall conduct verification or entrust another notarial institution in a different location to conduct verification on its behalf, and the relevant unit or individual shall provide assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 30 After examination, if the notarial institution considers that the evidentiary materials provided by the application are truthful, lawful, and sufficient, and that the matter for which notarization is sought is truthful and lawful, it shall issue a notarial certificate to the party within 15 working days from the date of accepting the notarization application. However, the time required due to force majeure, supplementation of evidentiary materials, or the need to verify relevant circumstances shall not be counted within the time limit.

Article 31 In any of the following circumstances, a notarial institution shall not handle the notarization:

(1) A person with no civil capacity or limited civil capacity applies for notarization without a guardian acting as agent;

(2) The party has no interest in the matter for which notarization is sought;

(3) The matter for which notarization is sought falls within the scope of professional technical appraisal or evaluation;

(4) There is a dispute between the parties regarding the matter for which notarization is sought;

(5) The party fabricates or conceals facts, or provides false evidentiary materials;

(6) The evidentiary materials provided by the party are insufficient, or the party refuses to supplement evidentiary materials;

(7) The matter for which notarization is sought is untrue or illegal;

(8) The matter for which notarization is sought violates social morality;

(9) The party refuses to pay the notarial fee in accordance with the regulations.

Article 32 A notarial certificate shall be made in the format prescribed by the judicial administration department of the State Council, signed or sealed with the signature stamp of the notary, and affixed with the seal of the notarial institution. A notarial certificate shall take effect from the date of issuance.

Notarial certificates shall be written in the language commonly used nationwide; in ethnic autonomous areas, texts in the commonly used ethnic language of the locality may be produced at the request of the party.

Article 33 If a notarial certificate needs to be used abroad and the country of use requires prior authentication, it shall be authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China or an institution authorized by the Ministry, and by the embassy or consulate of the relevant country in the People's Republic of China.

Article 34 The party shall pay the notarial fee in accordance with the regulations.

For parties who meet the conditions for legal aid, the notarial institution shall reduce or exempt the notarial fee in accordance with the regulations.

Article 35 A notarial institution shall classify notarial documents into files and preserve them. Important notarial archives, such as those for matters that laws and administrative regulations require to be notarized, shall, after the preservation period at the notarial institution expires, be transferred to the local archives for preservation in accordance with the regulations.

## Chapter V Effect of Notarization

Article 36 A notarized civil juristic act, fact of legal significance, or document shall serve as the basis for determining facts, unless there is sufficient evidence to the contrary to overturn the notarization.

Article 37 For a notarized debt instrument that is for payment and clearly states the debtor's commitment to accept compulsory enforcement, if the debtor fails to perform or performs improperly, the creditor may apply to the competent people's court for enforcement in accordance with the law.

If the debt instrument specified in the preceding paragraph is indeed erroneous, the people's court shall rule not to enforce it and shall serve the ruling on both parties and the notarial institution.

Article 38 Where laws or administrative regulations stipulate that matters not notarized have no legal effect, those provisions shall apply.

Article 39 If a party or an interested party in a notarization matter believes that a notarial certificate is erroneous, they may request a review from the notarial institution that issued the notarial certificate. If the content of the notarial certificate is illegal or inconsistent with the facts, the notarial institution shall revoke the notarial certificate and announce it, and the notarial certificate shall be void from the beginning; if there are other errors in the notarial certificate, the notarial institution shall correct it.

Article 40 If a party or an interested party in a notarization matter disputes the content of a notarial certificate, they may file a civil lawsuit with the people's court regarding the dispute.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 41 If a notarial institution or its notary commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, or city with districts shall issue a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the notarial institution shall be fined between 10,000 and 50,000 yuan, and the notary shall be fined between 1,000 and 5,000 yuan, and may be suspended from practice for between three and six months; any illegal income shall be confiscated:

(1) Soliciting notarial business by improper means such as denigrating other notarial institutions or notaries, or paying kickbacks or commissions;

(2) Charging notarial fees in violation of the prescribed fee standards;

(3) Practicing in two or more notarial institutions simultaneously;

(4) Engaging in other remunerative occupations;

(5) Handling notarization for themselves or their close relatives, or handling notarization in which they or their close relatives have an interest;

(6) Other acts that should be punished in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 42 If a notarial institution or its notary commits any of the following acts, the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, or city with districts shall issue a warning to the notarial institution, impose a fine of between 20,000 and 100,000 yuan, and may impose a penalty of suspension for rectification for between one and three months; issue a warning to the notary, impose a fine of between 2,000 and 10,000 yuan, and may impose a penalty of suspension from practice for between three and twelve months; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the judicial administration department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall revoke the notary's practice certificate; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Issuing notarial certificates privately;

(2) Issuing notarial certificates for untrue or illegal matters;

(3) Embezzling or misappropriating notarial fees, or embezzling or stealing notarial special articles;

(4) Damaging or altering notarial documents or notarial archives;

(5) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy learned during professional activities;

(6) Other acts that should be punished in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

If a person is subject to criminal punishment for an intentional crime or a duty-related negligent crime, their notary practice certificate shall be revoked.

A person whose notary practice certificate has been revoked shall not serve as a defender or agent ad litem, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party in criminal, civil, or administrative proceedings.

Article 43 If a notarial institution or its notary causes losses to a party or an interested party in a notarization matter due to fault, the notarial institution shall bear the corresponding compensation liability; after compensation, the notarial institution may recover from the notary who acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

If a dispute arises between a party or an interested party in a notarization matter and the notarial institution regarding compensation, a civil lawsuit may be filed with the people's court.

Article 44 If a party or any other individual or organization commits any of the following acts, causing losses to others, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if they violate public security administration, they shall be subject to public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Providing false evidentiary materials to obtain a notarial certificate by fraud;

(2) Using a false notarial certificate to engage in fraudulent activities;

(3) Forging, altering, or buying or selling forged or altered notarial certificates or seals of notarial institutions.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 45 The embassies and consulates of the People's Republic of China abroad may handle notarization in accordance with the provisions of this Law or the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

Article 46 The fee standards for notarization shall be formulated by the competent price department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government jointly with the judicial administration department at the same level.

Article 47 This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 2006.
