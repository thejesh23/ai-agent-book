# Law of the People's Republic of China on Foreign Service Personnel

Adopted at the 11th Session of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on October 31, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Civil Servant Law for the purposes of building a high-quality contingent of foreign service personnel, ensuring that foreign service missions perform their duties according to law, regulating the management of foreign service personnel, and safeguarding their lawful rights and interests.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "foreign service personnel" refers to persons who are employed in the foreign service missions of the People's Republic of China, engaged in diplomatic, consular or other work, using the administrative establishment for overseas posts, and holding diplomatic ranks.

For the purposes of this Law, "foreign service missions" refers to the embassies, consulates, and representative offices such as permanent missions to intergovernmental international organizations like the United Nations, of the People's Republic of China in foreign countries.

The obligations, rights, and management of foreign service personnel shall be governed by this Law. Matters not covered by this Law shall be governed by the provisions of the Civil Servant Law.

**Article 3** Foreign service personnel shall perform their duties according to law and be protected by law.

**Article 4** The Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall exercise unified leadership over the work of foreign service missions and, together with other dispatching departments, administer foreign service personnel.

## Chapter II Duties, Qualifications, Obligations and Rights

**Article 5** Foreign service personnel shall, in accordance with their positions and work assignments, perform the following duties:

(1) Safeguard national sovereignty, security, honor and interests;
(2) Implement the state's foreign policy guidelines;
(3) Conduct diplomatic representations on behalf of the state;
(4) Develop relations between China and the host country, participate in activities of international organizations, and promote bilateral and multilateral friendly exchanges and cooperation;
(5) Protect the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese citizens and legal persons abroad;
(6) Report on the situation in the host country and relevant regional and international developments;
(7) Introduce China's situation and domestic and foreign policies to enhance the host country's and the world's understanding of China;
(8) Perform other diplomatic or consular duties.

An Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary is the representative of the People's Republic of China in the host country.

**Article 6** Foreign service personnel shall meet the following qualifications:

(1) Possess the nationality of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Have reached the age of 23;
(3) Uphold the Constitution of the People's Republic of China;
(4) Possess good political quality and conduct;
(5) Possess the professional knowledge, work ability and language proficiency necessary for the job;
(6) Meet the physical conditions, psychological qualities and adaptability required for long-term residence abroad;
(7) Other qualifications as provided by law.

**Article 7** A person shall not be appointed as a foreign service personnel in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Has been subjected to criminal punishment for committing a crime;
(2) Has been publicly expelled from public office;
(3) Has been dismissed by a state organ;
(4) Holds a long-term or permanent residence permit of a foreign country;
(5) The spouse holds foreign nationality or a long-term or permanent residence permit of a foreign country;
(6) Other circumstances under which appointment as foreign service personnel is prohibited.

**Article 8** Foreign service personnel shall perform the following obligations:

(1) Be loyal to the motherland and the people, and safeguard national dignity;
(2) Be loyal to the Constitution and laws of the People's Republic of China, and respect the laws and customs of the host country;
(3) Be devoted to their duties, be diligent and responsible, and complete all work tasks;
(4) Obey the assignments of the dispatching department, and abide by the rules, regulations and work discipline of the foreign service mission;
(5) Strictly guard state secrets and work secrets;
(6) Not resign during the period of overseas service;
(7) Report personal major matters to the foreign service mission and the dispatching department as required;
(8) Other obligations as provided by law.

**Article 9** Foreign service personnel shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) Obtain the working conditions necessary for performing their duties;
(2) Receive salaries, welfare and insurance benefits commensurate with working and living abroad on a long-term basis;
(3) Not be dismissed during the period of overseas service;
(4) Participate in training before dispatch and during the period of overseas service;
(5) Other rights as provided by law.

**Article 10** Foreign service personnel shall, in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, enjoy corresponding privileges and immunities during their overseas service.

Foreign service personnel shall not abuse privileges and immunities, and shall not waive privileges and immunities without approval.

## Chapter III Positions and Ranks

**Article 11** The positions of foreign service personnel are divided into diplomatic positions and consular positions.

Diplomatic positions include: Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary, Representative, Deputy Representative, Minister, Minister-Counselor, Counselor, First Secretary, Second Secretary, Third Secretary, Attaché.

Consular positions include: Consul General, Deputy Consul General, Consul, Vice Consul, Consular Attaché.

**Article 12** A system of diplomatic ranks shall be implemented for foreign service personnel.

Diplomatic ranks are divided into seven levels: Ambassador rank, Minister rank, Counselor rank, First Secretary rank, Second Secretary rank, Third Secretary rank, Attaché rank.

The diplomatic rank of a foreign service personnel shall be determined based on the position they hold in the foreign service mission, their civil service rank level, and the needs of diplomatic work.

**Article 13** The basic correspondence between diplomatic positions and diplomatic ranks is as follows:

(1) Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary: Ambassador rank;
(2) Representative, Deputy Representative: Ambassador rank, Minister rank, Counselor rank;
(3) Minister, Minister-Counselor: Minister rank;
(4) Counselor: Counselor rank;
(5) First Secretary: First Secretary rank;
(6) Second Secretary: Second Secretary rank;
(7) Third Secretary: Third Secretary rank;
(8) Attaché: Attaché rank.

**Article 14** The basic correspondence between consular positions and diplomatic ranks is as follows:

(1) Consul General: Ambassador rank, Minister rank, Counselor rank;
(2) Deputy Consul General: Counselor rank;
(3) Consul: Counselor rank, First Secretary rank, Second Secretary rank;
(4) Vice Consul: Third Secretary rank, Attaché rank;
(5) Consular Attaché: Attaché rank.

**Article 15** The correspondence between the positions and diplomatic ranks of foreign service personnel and civil service rank levels shall be separately prescribed.

**Article 16** The positions of foreign service personnel shall be decided according to the following authority:

(1) The positions of Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary and Representative or Deputy Representative holding the rank of Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress;
(2) The positions of Representative or Deputy Representative other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be decided by the State Council;
(3) The position of Consul General shall be decided by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs;
(4) The positions of Minister, Minister-Counselor, Counselor, Deputy Consul General, and other foreign service personnel shall be decided by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or other dispatching departments; among them, positions at the level of Third Secretary, Vice Consul and below may be decided by the foreign service mission during the period of overseas service.

**Article 17** Diplomatic ranks shall be approved and conferred according to the following authority:

(1) The Ambassador rank shall be approved and conferred by the Premier of the State Council;
(2) The Minister rank and Counselor rank shall be approved by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or other dispatching departments, and conferred by the Minister of Foreign Affairs;
(3) The First Secretary rank and Second Secretary rank shall be approved and conferred by the dispatching department at the time of dispatch; during the period of overseas service, they shall be approved and conferred by the dispatching department based on the opinion of the foreign service mission;
(4) The Third Secretary rank and Attaché rank shall be approved and conferred by the dispatching department at the time of dispatch; during the period of overseas service, they shall be approved and conferred by the foreign service mission.

**Article 18** The promotion of foreign service personnel to the positions of Third Secretary, Vice Consul, and corresponding ranks shall be carried out step by step in accordance with the prescribed promotion conditions and time limits, and within the authority specified in Articles 16 and 17 of this Law respectively; if, upon assessment, the conditions for promotion are not met, the promotion shall be deferred based on the opinion of the foreign service mission and with the approval of the dispatching department.

The promotion of foreign service personnel to the positions of Second Secretary, First Secretary, Counselor, Minister-Counselor, Minister, or Consul, Deputy Consul General, and corresponding ranks shall be carried out selectively step by step based on the promotion conditions, time limits, and the promotion opinion submitted by the foreign service mission, and within the authority specified in Articles 16 and 17 of this Law respectively.

The conditions and time limits for the promotion of foreign service personnel in positions and ranks shall be separately prescribed.

**Article 19** Foreign service personnel shall not be promoted in position or rank during the period they are under disciplinary sanction.

**Article 20** Foreign service personnel who receive a sanction of demotion or removal from office shall have their ranks lowered accordingly. The lowering of rank shall not apply to the Attaché rank.

When foreign service personnel leave their posts and return to China, their ranks shall terminate accordingly. If necessary for engaging in diplomatic work, the ranks may be retained. Specific measures shall be separately formulated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council.

## Chapter IV Heads of Mission

**Article 21** The head of mission is the chief administrative officer of the foreign service mission. The Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary is the head of the embassy. The Representative is the head of the representative office such as the permanent mission to intergovernmental international organizations like the United Nations. The Consul General is the head of the consulate general. The Consul is the head of the consulate.

Foreign service missions shall implement a system of head responsibility. The head of mission shall exercise unified leadership over all work of the foreign service mission.

**Article 22** When the head of mission is absent or unable to perform duties for any reason, a designated person shall act as the head of mission to exercise functions.

**Article 23** The head of mission shall submit written reports to the dispatching department upon arrival at and departure from the post.

The head of mission shall return to China on schedule to report on work.

## Chapter V Dispatch, Recall and Transfer

**Article 24** The Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary and the Representative or Deputy Representative holding the rank of Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary shall be dispatched and recalled by the President of the People's Republic of China based on the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Representatives and Deputy Representatives other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be dispatched and transferred back by the State Council or the dispatching department.

Other foreign service personnel shall be dispatched and transferred back by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or other dispatching departments.

**Article 25** A system of tenure shall be implemented for foreign service personnel. Based on work needs and with the approval of the dispatching department, the term of office of foreign service personnel may be appropriately shortened or extended.

**Article 26** Foreign service personnel shall be transferred back early under any of the following circumstances:

(1) There are other work arrangements;
(2) They are unable to perform their duties;
(3) They have violated the law or committed serious disciplinary violations;
(4) The spouse has acquired foreign nationality or a long-term or permanent residence permit of a foreign country;
(5) Other circumstances where it is inappropriate to continue working in the foreign service mission.

**Article 27** The state may, as needed, urgently recall, transfer back, or withdraw some or all personnel of relevant foreign service missions.

## Chapter VI Assessment, Training and Exchange

**Article 28** Assessment of foreign service personnel shall be divided into routine assessment and periodic assessment, and shall be organized and implemented by the foreign service mission or the dispatching department in accordance with state regulations.

The assessment of foreign service personnel shall be based on their responsibilities and work tasks, and shall comprehensively assess their virtue, ability, diligence, performance, and integrity, with a focus on work performance.

The results of the assessment shall serve as the basis for adjusting the positions, ranks, levels, and salaries of foreign service personnel, and for granting rewards, providing training, etc.

**Article 29** Before dispatch and during the period of overseas service, foreign service personnel shall receive position-specific and professional training based on the requirements of their work responsibilities.

**Article 30** Foreign service personnel shall undergo position exchange among foreign service missions; based on work needs, they may also undergo position exchange between dispatching departments or other organs.

**Article 31** The training status and position exchange experience of foreign service personnel shall be included in the assessment content.

## Chapter VII Rewards and Sanctions

**Article 32** A foreign service mission or foreign service personnel shall be given rewards according to law under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Making significant contributions to safeguarding national sovereignty, security, honor and interests;
(2) Making outstanding contributions to protecting the personal safety, property safety or other legitimate rights and interests of Chinese citizens and legal persons abroad;
(3) Making significant contributions in responding to major emergencies;
(4) Having outstanding deeds in specific harsh environments such as war;
(5) Making outstanding contributions to protecting state secrets;
(6) Demonstrating exemplary conduct in observing discipline, being honest and upright, having a correct work style, and being fair in handling affairs;
(7) Being diligent and responsible, with outstanding work performance;
(8) Having other outstanding performances that warrant rewards.

**Article 33** Foreign service personnel shall be given corresponding sanctions according to law for any of the following acts; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Damaging national sovereignty, security, honor and interests;
(2) Leaving the foreign service mission without authorization;
(3) Disclosing state secrets or work secrets;
(4) Using their position for personal gain or for the benefit of others;
(5) Engaging in or participating in for-profit activities, or holding concurrent positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations;
(6) Neglecting their duties, causing delays or errors in work;
(7) Disobeying assignments, refusing to go to the assigned post;
(8) Having other illegal or disciplinary violations.

**Article 34** The Ministry of Foreign Affairs or other dispatching departments shall, in accordance with legal authority and procedures, grant rewards to foreign service missions and give rewards or impose sanctions on foreign service personnel.

## Chapter VIII Salaries and Welfare

**Article 35** A system of overseas salaries combining positions, ranks and levels shall be implemented for foreign service personnel.

**Article 36** The state shall establish a salary adjustment mechanism for foreign service personnel and adjust their salaries and living conditions in a timely manner.

**Article 37** Foreign service personnel shall enjoy allowances and subsidies in accordance with state regulations.

**Article 38** The state shall provide necessary medical security and safety measures for foreign service personnel, and provide necessary personal accident insurance in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 39** Foreign service personnel shall enjoy paid annual leave and tenure leave as stipulated by the state.

## Chapter IX Spouses and Children

**Article 40** Before going through marriage formalities, foreign service personnel shall truthfully report to the dispatching department the identity of the person they intend to marry and other relevant information as required.

Foreign service personnel who divorce shall promptly report to the dispatching department.

**Article 41** The accompanying spouse of a foreign service personnel shall be approved by the dispatching department.

When the term of office of a foreign service personnel ends or they are transferred back early, the accompanying spouse shall end their accompaniment at the same time.

**Article 42** During the period of accompaniment, the spouse of a foreign service personnel shall enjoy prescribed leave benefits based on the foreign service personnel's position and rank, length of service, and living conditions in the host country.

**Article 43** If the spouse of a foreign service personnel is a staff member of a state organ or public institution, an employee of a wholly state-owned enterprise, a wholly state-owned company, a state-controlled company, or an active-duty serviceman, the work arrangement at the original unit during the accompaniment period and after returning to China upon the end of accompaniment shall be handled in accordance with state regulations; the original unit shall not dismiss, discharge, or charge compensation fees, management fees, or other fees due to the accompaniment.

**Article 44** If the spouse of a foreign service personnel does not accompany them, they may, with the approval of the dispatching department, visit the foreign service mission and enjoy corresponding visiting leave, travel expenses and subsidies in accordance with regulations.

**Article 45** Minor children of foreign service personnel may, with the approval of the dispatching department, reside with or visit their parents at the foreign service mission.

The state shall take various measures to ensure that minor children of foreign service personnel enjoy the right to education according to law.

**Article 46** The accompanying, co-residing, and visiting spouses and children of foreign service personnel shall abide by the rules and regulations of the foreign service mission and respect the laws and customs of the host country.

## Chapter X Supplementary Provisions

**Article 47** Military attachés of foreign service missions shall be managed with reference to this Law, and specific measures shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission.

**Article 48** This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2010.
