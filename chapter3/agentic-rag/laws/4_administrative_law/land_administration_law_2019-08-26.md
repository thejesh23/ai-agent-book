# Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on June 25, 1986

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on December 29, 1988

Revised at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 29, 1998

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China and the Urban Real Estate Administration Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 26, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen land administration, uphold the socialist public ownership of land, protect and develop land resources, make rational use of land, effectively protect cultivated land, and promote the sustainable development of the economy and society.

Article 2 The People's Republic of China practices socialist public ownership of land, namely, ownership by the whole people and collective ownership by the working masses.

Ownership by the whole people, i.e., state ownership of land, shall be exercised by the State Council on behalf of the state.

No unit or individual may encroach upon, buy, sell, or otherwise illegally transfer land. The right to use land may be transferred in accordance with the law.

For the needs of the public interest, the state may lawfully expropriate or requisition land and provide compensation.

The state implements a system of paid use for state-owned land in accordance with the law, except where state-owned land use rights are allocated by the state within the scope prescribed by law.

Article 3 It is a basic national policy of China to treasure and make rational use of land and to effectively protect cultivated land. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to carry out comprehensive planning, exercise strict management, protect and develop land resources, and stop illegal land occupation.

Article 4 The state implements a land use control system.

The state formulates a general plan for land use, defines land uses, and classifies land into agricultural land, construction land, and unused land. The conversion of agricultural land to construction land is strictly restricted, the total amount of construction land is controlled, and special protection is given to cultivated land.

For the purposes of the preceding paragraph, "agricultural land" means land directly used for agricultural production, including cultivated land, forestland, grassland, land for farmland water conservancy, and aquaculture water surfaces; "construction land" means land for constructing buildings and structures, including land for urban and rural residential and public facilities, industrial and mining land, land for transportation and water conservancy facilities, tourist land, and military facilities land; "unused land" means land other than agricultural land and construction land.

Units and individuals using land must use it strictly in accordance with the uses defined in the general plan for land use.

Article 5 The competent department of natural resources under the State Council shall be responsible for the unified administration and supervision of land nationwide.

The establishment and functions of the competent departments of natural resources of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 6 Institutions authorized by the State Council shall supervise the land use and land administration of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as the people's governments of cities designated by the State Council.

Article 7 All units and individuals have the obligation to abide by land administration laws and regulations and have the right to report and accuse any violations of such laws and regulations.

Article 8 Units and individuals that achieve notable success in protecting and developing land resources, making rational use of land, or conducting related scientific research shall be rewarded by the people's government.

## Chapter II Ownership and Use Rights of Land

Article 9 Land in urban areas of cities shall be owned by the state.

Land in rural areas and suburban areas, except where otherwise provided by law to be owned by the state, shall be owned by peasant collectives; land for residential housing, private plots, and private hills shall be owned by peasant collectives.

Article 10 State-owned land and land owned by peasant collectives may be lawfully designated for use by units or individuals. Units and individuals using land have the obligation to protect, manage, and make rational use of the land.

Article 11 Land owned by peasant collectives that is lawfully owned by village peasant collectives shall be operated and managed by the village collective economic organization or the villagers' committee; land that has been separately owned by two or more rural collective economic organizations within a village shall be operated and managed by those respective rural collective economic organizations or villagers' groups; land that is owned by peasant collectives at the township (town) level shall be operated and managed by the township (town) rural collective economic organization.

Article 12 The registration of land ownership and use rights shall be carried out in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations concerning real estate registration.

Land ownership and use rights registered in accordance with the law shall be protected by law, and no unit or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 13 For cultivated land, forestland, and grassland owned by peasant collectives or state-owned but lawfully used by peasant collectives, as well as other land lawfully used for agriculture, a household contract system within the rural collective economic organization shall be adopted. For barren hills, barren gullies, barren hills, and barren beaches where the household contract system is not suitable, methods such as bidding, auction, or open negotiation may be adopted for contracting to engage in crop farming, forestry, animal husbandry, and fishery production. The contract period for household-contracted cultivated land is thirty years, for grassland is thirty to fifty years, and for forestland is thirty to seventy years; upon expiration of the contract period for cultivated land, it shall be extended for another thirty years, and upon expiration of the contract period for grassland and forestland, it shall be extended accordingly in accordance with the law.

State-owned land lawfully used for agriculture may be contracted and operated by units or individuals to engage in crop farming, forestry, animal husbandry, and fishery production.

The party awarding the contract and the contractor shall lawfully enter into a contract agreement, stipulating the rights and obligations of both parties. Units and individuals contracting to operate land have the obligation to protect and make rational use of the land in accordance with the purposes stipulated in the contract.

Article 14 Disputes over land ownership and use rights shall be resolved through negotiation between the parties; if negotiation fails, the matter shall be handled by the people's government.

Disputes between units shall be handled by the people's government at or above the county level; disputes between individuals, or between individuals and units, shall be handled by the people's government at the township level or at or above the county level.

If a party is dissatisfied with the handling decision of the relevant people's government, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court within thirty days from the date of receiving the notice of the handling decision.

Before a dispute over land ownership and use rights is resolved, neither party may change the current status of land use.

## Chapter III General Plans for Land Use

Article 15 People's governments at all levels shall organize the formulation of general plans for land use based on the national economic and social development plan, the requirements for land consolidation and resource and environmental protection, land supply capacity, and the demand for land for various construction projects.

The planning period for general plans for land use shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 16 A lower-level general plan for land use shall be formulated based on the higher-level general plan for land use.

The total amount of construction land in the general plan for land use formulated by local people's governments at all levels shall not exceed the control indicators determined in the higher-level general plan for land use, and the amount of cultivated land retained shall not be lower than the control indicators determined in the higher-level general plan for land use.

The general plan for land use formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall ensure that the total amount of cultivated land within their administrative regions does not decrease.

Article 17 General plans for land use shall be formulated in accordance with the following principles:

(1) Implementing the requirements for territorial space development and protection, and strictly enforcing land use control;
(2) Strictly protecting permanent basic farmland, and strictly controlling the occupation of agricultural land for non-agricultural construction;
(3) Improving the level of economical and intensive land use;
(4) Coordinating the arrangement of production, living, and ecological land in urban and rural areas, meeting the reasonable land needs of rural industries and infrastructure, and promoting integrated urban-rural development;
(5) Protecting and improving the ecological environment, and ensuring the sustainable use of land;
(6) Balancing the quantity and quality of cultivated land occupied with cultivated land developed and reclaimed.

Article 18 The state establishes a territorial space planning system. The formulation of territorial space plans shall adhere to ecological priority, green and sustainable development, scientifically and orderly coordinate the arrangement of functional spaces such as ecological, agricultural, and urban areas, optimize the structure and layout of territorial space, and improve the quality and efficiency of territorial space development and protection.

Territorial space plans approved in accordance with the law are the basic basis for various development, protection, and construction activities. Where territorial space plans have been formulated, general plans for land use and urban and rural plans shall no longer be formulated.

Article 19 County-level general plans for land use shall divide land use zones and specify land uses.

Township (town) general plans for land use shall divide land use zones, determine the use of each piece of land based on land use conditions, and make public announcements.

Article 20 General plans for land use are subject to approval at different levels.

General plans for land use of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

General plans for land use of cities where the people's governments of provinces and autonomous regions are located, cities with a population of one million or more, and cities designated by the State Council shall be submitted to the State Council for approval after review and consent by the people's governments of the provinces or autonomous regions.

General plans for land use other than those specified in paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Article shall be submitted level by level to the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government for approval; among them, general plans for land use of townships (towns) may be approved by the people's governments of cities with districts or autonomous prefectures authorized by the provincial-level people's governments.

Once a general plan for land use is approved, it must be strictly implemented.

Article 21 The scale of urban construction land shall comply with the standards prescribed by the state, make full use of existing construction land, and avoid or minimize the occupation of agricultural land.

Urban master plans, village and town plans shall be coordinated with the general plan for land use. The scale of construction land in urban master plans, village and town plans shall not exceed the scale of construction land for cities, villages, and towns determined in the general plan for land use.

Within urban planning areas, village and town planning areas, urban, village, and town construction land shall comply with urban master plans, village and town plans.

Article 22 Comprehensive plans for the management and development of rivers and lakes shall be coordinated with the general plan for land use. Within the management and protection areas of rivers, lakes, and reservoirs, as well as flood storage and detention areas, land use shall comply with the comprehensive plans for the management and development of rivers and lakes and meet the requirements for river and lake flood discharge, flood storage, and water conveyance.

Article 23 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management of land use plans and implement total control over construction land.

Annual land use plans shall be formulated based on the national economic and social development plan, national industrial policies, the general plan for land use, and the actual conditions of construction land and land use. Annual land use plans shall make reasonable arrangements for the collective commercial construction land specified in Article 63 of this Law. The procedures for formulating and approving annual land use plans are the same as those for formulating and approving general plans for land use. Once approved and issued, they must be strictly implemented.

Article 24 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall include the implementation of annual land use plans as part of the implementation of the national economic and social development plan and report to the people's congress at the same level.

Article 25 Amendments to an approved general plan for land use must be approved by the original approving authority; without approval, the land uses determined in the general plan for land use shall not be changed.

If land for large-scale energy, transportation, water conservancy, and other infrastructure construction projects approved by the State Council requires changes to the general plan for land use, the general plan for land use shall be amended in accordance with the approval document of the State Council.

If land for energy, transportation, water conservancy, and other infrastructure construction projects approved by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government requires changes to the general plan for land use, and such changes fall within the approval authority of the provincial-level people's government for the general plan for land use, the general plan for land use shall be amended in accordance with the approval document of the provincial-level people's government.

Article 26 The state establishes a land survey system.

The competent departments of natural resources of people's governments at or above the county level, together with relevant departments at the same level, shall conduct land surveys. Landowners or users shall cooperate with the surveys and provide relevant materials.

Article 27 The competent departments of natural resources of people's governments at or above the county level, together with relevant departments at the same level, shall assess land grades based on land survey results, planned land uses, and unified standards formulated by the state.

Article 28 The state establishes a land statistics system.

Statistical institutions and competent departments of natural resources of people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct land statistical surveys in accordance with the law and regularly publish land statistical data. Landowners or users shall provide relevant materials and shall not refuse to report, delay reporting, or provide false or incomplete materials.

The land area statistical data jointly published by statistical institutions and competent departments of natural resources shall serve as the basis for people's governments at all levels to formulate general plans for land use.

Article 29 The state establishes a national land administration information system to dynamically monitor land use conditions.

## Chapter IV Protection of Cultivated Land

Article 30 The state protects cultivated land and strictly controls the conversion of cultivated land to non-cultivated land.

The state implements a compensation system for cultivated land occupation. Where non-agricultural construction occupies cultivated land with approval, the unit occupying the cultivated land shall, in accordance with the principle of "occupying as much as is reclaimed," be responsible for reclaiming cultivated land of a quantity and quality equivalent to the cultivated land occupied; if conditions for reclamation are not available or the reclaimed cultivated land does not meet the requirements, the unit shall pay a cultivated land reclamation fee in accordance with the provisions of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, which shall be used exclusively for reclaiming new cultivated land.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate plans for reclaiming cultivated land, supervise the units occupying cultivated land to reclaim cultivated land in accordance with the plans or organize reclamation in accordance with the plans, and conduct acceptance inspections.

Article 31 Local people's governments at or above the county level may require units occupying cultivated land to use the topsoil of the occupied cultivated land for soil improvement of newly reclaimed cultivated land, inferior land, or other cultivated land.

Article 32 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strictly implement the general plan for land use and annual land use plans, and take measures to ensure that the total amount of cultivated land within their administrative regions does not decrease and its quality does not decline. If the total amount of cultivated land decreases, the State Council shall order the organization of reclamation of cultivated land of a quantity and quality equivalent to the decreased cultivated land within a specified period; if the quality of cultivated land declines, the State Council shall order the organization of improvement within a specified period. The newly reclaimed and improved cultivated land shall be accepted by the competent department of natural resources under the State Council together with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs.

If, due to a shortage of land reserve resources, the amount of newly reclaimed cultivated land in an individual province or municipality directly under the Central Government is insufficient to compensate for the amount of cultivated land occupied after new construction land is added, the reduction in the amount of cultivated land to be reclaimed within its administrative region must be reported to the State Council for approval, and cultivated land of equivalent quantity and quality shall be reclaimed in other locations.

Article 33 The state implements a permanent basic farmland protection system. The following cultivated land shall be designated as permanent basic farmland in accordance with the general plan for land use and subject to strict protection:

(1) Cultivated land within important agricultural product production bases for grain, cotton, oil, sugar, etc., approved by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council or local people's governments at or above the county level;
(2) Cultivated land with good water conservancy and soil and water conservation facilities, medium and low-yield fields under improvement plans or capable of improvement, and high-standard farmland already built;
(3) Vegetable production bases;
(4) Agricultural research and teaching experimental fields;
(5) Other cultivated land that the State Council stipulates should be designated as permanent basic farmland.

The permanent basic farmland designated by each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government shall generally account for at least eighty percent of the cultivated land within its administrative region. The specific proportion shall be prescribed by the State Council based on the actual conditions of cultivated land in each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 34 The designation of permanent basic farmland shall be carried out on a township (town) basis, organized and implemented by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at the county level together with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs at the same level. Permanent basic farmland shall be assigned to specific plots and included in the national permanent basic farmland database for strict management.

The people's government at the township (town) level shall publicly announce the locations and scopes of permanent basic farmland to the public and set up protection markers.

Article 35 Once permanent basic farmland is lawfully designated, no unit or individual may occupy it without authorization or change its use. For key national construction projects such as energy, transportation, water conservancy, and military facilities, if the site selection truly cannot avoid permanent basic farmland and involves the conversion of agricultural land or land expropriation, approval by the State Council is required.

It is prohibited to circumvent the approval procedures for the conversion of agricultural land or land expropriation of permanent basic farmland by unauthorized adjustments to county-level or township (town)-level general plans for land use.

Article 36 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to guide crop rotation and fallowing according to local conditions, improve soil, enhance soil fertility, maintain irrigation and drainage engineering facilities, and prevent land desertification, salinization, soil erosion, and soil pollution.

Article 37 Non-agricultural construction must use land economically; where wasteland can be used, cultivated land shall not be occupied; where inferior land can be used, good land shall not be occupied.

It is prohibited to occupy cultivated land for building kilns, graves, or unauthorized construction of houses, sand mining, quarrying, mining, or soil extraction on cultivated land.

It is prohibited to occupy permanent basic farmland for developing forestry, fruit industry, or digging ponds for fish farming.

Article 38 No unit or individual may idle or abandon cultivated land. If non-agricultural construction that has gone through approval procedures occupies cultivated land but does not use it within one year and the land can still be cultivated and harvested, the collective or individual that originally cultivated the land shall resume cultivation, or the land-using unit may organize cultivation; if construction has not started for more than one year, an idle fee shall be paid in accordance with the provisions of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; if the land has not been used for two consecutive years, with the approval of the original approving authority, the people's government at or above the county level shall recover the land use right of the land-using unit without compensation; if the land was originally owned by a peasant collective, it shall be handed over to the original rural collective economic organization for resumption of cultivation.

Within urban planning areas, idle land obtained through transfer of land use rights for real estate development shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Urban Real Estate Administration Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 39 The state encourages units and individuals, in accordance with the general plan for land use and on the premise of protecting and improving the ecological environment and preventing soil erosion and land desertification, to develop unused land; land suitable for development as agricultural land shall be prioritized for development as agricultural land.

The state protects the legitimate rights and interests of developers in accordance with the law.

Article 40 The reclamation of unused land must undergo scientific demonstration and assessment, be carried out within the reclaimable areas designated in the general plan for land use, and be conducted after approval in accordance with the law. It is prohibited to destroy forests or grasslands to reclaim cultivated land, to reclaim land from lakes, or to encroach upon river beaches.

In accordance with the general plan for land use, land reclaimed or enclosed that damages the ecological environment shall be systematically and step by step returned to forests, grasslands, or lakes.**Article 41** Where state-owned barren hills, wasteland, or barren beaches with undetermined use rights are developed for crop farming, forestry, animal husbandry, or fishery production, they may, upon approval by the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law, be allocated to the development unit or individual for long-term use.

**Article 42** The State encourages land consolidation. The people's governments at the county and township (town) levels shall organize rural collective economic organizations to comprehensively improve fields, water systems, roads, forests, and villages in accordance with the overall plan for land utilization, so as to enhance the quality of arable land, increase the effective area of arable land, and improve agricultural production conditions and the ecological environment.

Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to improve medium- and low-yield fields and consolidate idle and abandoned land.

**Article 43** Where land is damaged due to excavation, subsidence, or compaction, the land user or individual shall be responsible for reclamation in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State; if reclamation is not possible or fails to meet the requirements, a land reclamation fee shall be paid, which shall be used exclusively for land reclamation. Reclaimed land shall be prioritized for agricultural use.

## Chapter V Land for Construction

**Article 44** Where land is occupied for construction involving the conversion of agricultural land into construction land, the approval procedures for the conversion of agricultural land shall be completed.

The conversion of permanent basic farmland into construction land shall be approved by the State Council.

Within the scope of the scale of construction land for cities, villages, and market towns determined in the overall plan for land utilization, the conversion of agricultural land other than permanent basic farmland into construction land for the implementation of the plan shall be approved in batches according to the annual plan for land utilization and in accordance with the provisions of the State Council by the authority that originally approved the overall plan for land utilization or its authorized authority. Within the approved scope of converted agricultural land, the land for specific construction projects may be approved by the people's government of the city or county.

Outside the scope of the scale of construction land for cities, villages, and market towns determined in the overall plan for land utilization, the conversion of agricultural land other than permanent basic farmland into construction land shall be approved by the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government authorized by the State Council.

**Article 45** Where it is necessary for the public interest to expropriate land owned by peasant collectives under any of the following circumstances, expropriation may be carried out in accordance with the law:

(1) Land needed for military and diplomatic purposes;
(2) Land needed for infrastructure construction such as energy, transportation, water conservancy, communications, and postal services organized and implemented by the government;
(3) Land needed for public utilities such as science and technology, education, culture, health, sports, ecological environment and resource protection, disaster prevention and mitigation, cultural relic protection, community comprehensive services, social welfare, municipal public facilities, special care and placement, and martyr protection organized and implemented by the government;
(4) Land needed for poverty alleviation relocation and affordable housing construction projects organized and implemented by the government;
(5) Land needed for large-scale development and construction organized and implemented by the local people's government at or above the county level with the approval of the people's government at or above the provincial level within the scope of urban construction land determined in the overall plan for land utilization;
(6) Other circumstances stipulated by law where the expropriation of land owned by peasant collectives is necessary for the public interest.

The construction activities specified in the preceding paragraph shall conform to the national economic and social development plan, the overall plan for land utilization, the urban and rural plan, and special plans; the construction activities specified in items (4) and (5) shall also be included in the annual plan for national economic and social development; the large-scale development and construction specified in item (5) shall also meet the standards stipulated by the competent department of natural resources of the State Council.

**Article 46** The expropriation of the following land shall be approved by the State Council:

(1) Permanent basic farmland;
(2) Arable land other than permanent basic farmland exceeding thirty-five hectares;
(3) Other land exceeding seventy hectares.

The expropriation of land other than that specified in the preceding paragraph shall be approved by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Where agricultural land is expropriated, the approval procedures for the conversion of agricultural land shall be completed first in accordance with Article 44 of this Law. Where the conversion of agricultural land is approved by the State Council, the land expropriation approval procedures shall be completed simultaneously, and no separate land expropriation approval is required; where the conversion of agricultural land is approved by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within its authority to approve land expropriation, the land expropriation approval procedures shall be completed simultaneously, and no separate land expropriation approval is required; if it exceeds the authority to approve land expropriation, separate land expropriation approval procedures shall be completed in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article.

**Article 47** Where the State expropriates land, after approval in accordance with the statutory procedures, the local people's government at or above the county level shall announce it and organize its implementation.

Before applying for land expropriation, the local people's government at or above the county level shall conduct a survey of the current status of the land to be expropriated and a social stability risk assessment, and shall announce the scope of expropriation, current land status, purpose of expropriation, compensation standards, resettlement methods, and social security arrangements within the township (town), village, and villager groups where the land to be expropriated is located for at least thirty days, soliciting opinions from the rural collective economic organization and its members, the villagers' committee, and other interested parties.

If a majority of the members of the rural collective economic organization whose land is to be expropriated believe that the land expropriation compensation and resettlement plan does not comply with the provisions of laws and regulations, the local people's government at or above the county level shall organize a hearing and modify the plan in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations and the results of the hearing.

The owners and users of the land to be expropriated shall, within the time limit specified in the announcement, complete compensation registration by presenting the certificates of real estate rights. The local people's government at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to calculate and implement the relevant expenses, ensuring full payment in place, and sign agreements on compensation and resettlement with the owners and users of the land to be expropriated; if it is genuinely difficult to reach an agreement in individual cases, this shall be truthfully explained when applying for land expropriation.

Only after the relevant preliminary work is completed may the local people's government at or above the county level apply for land expropriation.

**Article 48** Fair and reasonable compensation shall be given for land expropriation to ensure that the original living standards of the farmers whose land is expropriated are not lowered and their long-term livelihoods are guaranteed.

Land compensation fees, resettlement subsidies, and compensation fees for rural villagers' houses, other above-ground attachments, and young crops shall be paid in full and in a timely manner in accordance with the law, and social security expenses for the farmers whose land is expropriated shall be arranged.

The standards for land compensation fees and resettlement subsidies for the expropriation of agricultural land shall be determined by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government by formulating and publishing comprehensive land prices for the area. When formulating comprehensive land prices for the area, factors such as the original use of the land, land resource conditions, land output value, land location, land supply and demand, population, and economic and social development level shall be comprehensively considered, and the prices shall be adjusted or re-published at least every three years.

The compensation standards for the expropriation of land other than agricultural land, above-ground attachments, and young crops shall be formulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. For the rural villagers' houses among them, fair and reasonable compensation shall be given in accordance with the principles of compensation before relocation and improvement of living conditions, respecting the wishes of rural villagers, by means such as re-allocating homestead land for house construction, providing resettlement housing, or monetary compensation, and compensation shall be given for relocation, temporary resettlement, and other expenses caused by the expropriation, to protect the rural villagers' right to housing and their legitimate housing property rights and interests.

The local people's government at or above the county level shall include the farmers whose land is expropriated in the corresponding social security system such as old-age insurance. The social security expenses for the farmers whose land is expropriated shall be mainly used for subsidies for social insurance premiums such as old-age insurance for eligible farmers whose land is expropriated. The methods for raising, managing, and using the social security expenses for farmers whose land is expropriated shall be formulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 49** The rural collective economic organization whose land is expropriated shall disclose to its members the revenue and expenditure of the compensation fees for the expropriated land and accept supervision.

It is prohibited to embezzle or misappropriate the land expropriation compensation fees and other related expenses of the unit whose land is expropriated.

**Article 50** Local people's governments at all levels shall support the rural collective economic organizations and farmers whose land is expropriated in engaging in development and operation and establishing enterprises.

**Article 51** The compensation standards and resettlement measures for land expropriated for the construction of large and medium-sized water conservancy and hydropower projects shall be separately stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 52** When conducting feasibility study demonstrations for construction projects, the competent department of natural resources may, in accordance with the overall plan for land utilization, the annual plan for land utilization, and the standards for construction land, review matters related to construction land and provide opinions.

**Article 53** For an approved construction project that requires the use of state-owned construction land, the construction unit shall submit an application for construction land to the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level with approval authority, holding the relevant documents stipulated by laws and administrative regulations. After review by the competent department of natural resources, the application shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval.

**Article 54** A construction unit shall obtain the right to use state-owned land through paid methods such as transfer; however, the following construction land may be obtained through allocation upon approval by the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law:

(1) Land for state organs and military use;
(2) Land for urban infrastructure and public welfare undertakings;
(3) Land for infrastructure such as energy, transportation, and water conservancy that are key national support projects;
(4) Other land stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 55** A construction unit that obtains the right to use state-owned land through paid methods such as transfer may use the land only after paying the land use right transfer fee and other land use fees and expenses in accordance with the standards and methods stipulated by the State Council.

From the date of implementation of this Law, thirty percent of the land use fees for newly added construction land shall be turned over to the central finance, and seventy percent shall be retained by the relevant local people's government. The specific methods for use and management shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments and submitted to the State Council for approval.

**Article 56** A construction unit using state-owned land shall use the land in accordance with the provisions of the paid use contract such as the land use right transfer or the approval document for the allocation of the land use right; if it is truly necessary to change the construction purpose of the land, it shall obtain the consent of the competent department of natural resources of the relevant people's government and submit it to the people's government that originally approved the land use for approval. Among them, if the land use is changed within the urban planning area, the consent of the relevant competent department of urban planning administration shall be obtained before submitting for approval.

**Article 57** Where temporary use of state-owned land or land owned by peasant collectives is needed for construction project construction and geological exploration, it shall be approved by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level. Among them, for temporary land use within the urban planning area, the consent of the relevant competent department of urban planning administration shall be obtained before submitting for approval. The land user shall sign a temporary land use contract with the relevant competent department of natural resources or the rural collective economic organization or villagers' committee based on the land ownership, and pay the temporary land use compensation fee in accordance with the contract.

The user of the temporarily used land shall use the land in accordance with the purposes agreed upon in the temporary land use contract and shall not construct permanent buildings.

The term of temporary land use generally shall not exceed two years.

**Article 58** Under any of the following circumstances, the competent department of natural resources of the relevant people's government may, upon reporting to the people's government that originally approved the land use or the people's government with approval authority for approval, recover the right to use state-owned land:

(1) Land is truly needed for the implementation of urban planning for the renovation of old urban areas or other public interests;
(2) The term of use agreed upon in the paid use contract such as land transfer expires, and the land user fails to apply for renewal or the application for renewal is not approved;
(3) The use of the originally allocated state-owned land is discontinued due to the dissolution or relocation of the unit;
(4) Highways, railways, airports, mines, etc. are approved for scrapping.

Where the right to use state-owned land is recovered in accordance with item (1) of the preceding paragraph, appropriate compensation shall be given to the holder of the land use right.

**Article 59** Construction in townships (towns) and villages, such as township enterprises, township (town) village public facilities, public welfare undertakings, and rural villagers' houses, shall be carried out in accordance with village and market town plans, with rational layout, comprehensive development, and supporting construction; the construction land shall conform to the overall plan for land utilization at the township (town) level and the annual plan for land utilization, and the approval procedures shall be completed in accordance with Articles 44, 60, 61, and 62 of this Law.

**Article 60** Where a rural collective economic organization uses construction land determined in the overall plan for land utilization at the township (town) level to establish an enterprise or jointly establish an enterprise with other units or individuals through means such as contributing land use rights as shares or joint operation, it shall submit an application to the competent department of natural resources of the local people's government at or above the county level with the relevant approval documents, and shall be approved by the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the approval authority stipulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; among them, if it involves the occupation of agricultural land, the approval procedures shall be completed in accordance with Article 44 of this Law.

The construction land for enterprises established in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph must be strictly controlled. Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may stipulate land use standards for different industries and business scales of township enterprises.

**Article 61** Where land is needed for the construction of public facilities and public welfare undertakings in townships (towns) and villages, after review by the people's government of the township (town), an application shall be submitted to the competent department of natural resources of the local people's government at or above the county level, and shall be approved by the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the approval authority stipulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; among them, if it involves the occupation of agricultural land, the approval procedures shall be completed in accordance with Article 44 of this Law.

**Article 62** Each rural household may own only one homestead, and the area of the homestead shall not exceed the standards stipulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

In areas with scarce per capita land where it is impossible to guarantee one homestead per household, the people's government at the county level may, on the basis of fully respecting the wishes of rural villagers, take measures to ensure that rural villagers have a place to live in accordance with the standards stipulated by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

When building houses, rural villagers shall comply with the overall plan for land utilization at the township (town) level and the village plan, shall not occupy permanent basic farmland, and shall try to use existing homesteads and idle land within the village. When formulating the overall plan for land utilization at the township (town) level and the village plan, the land for homesteads shall be coordinated and reasonably arranged to improve the living environment and conditions of rural villagers.

The land for rural villagers' houses shall be reviewed and approved by the people's government of the township (town); among them, if it involves the occupation of agricultural land, the approval procedures shall be completed in accordance with Article 44 of this Law.

If a rural villager applies for a homestead again after selling, renting, or donating a house, the application shall not be approved.

The State allows rural villagers who have settled in cities to voluntarily withdraw from their homesteads with compensation in accordance with the law, and encourages rural collective economic organizations and their members to revitalize and utilize idle homesteads and idle houses.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council is responsible for the work related to the reform and management of rural homesteads nationwide.

**Article 63** For collective commercial construction land designated for industrial, commercial, and other commercial purposes in the overall plan for land utilization and the urban and rural plan and registered in accordance with the law, the land owner may transfer or lease it to units or individuals for use, and shall sign a written contract specifying the land boundaries, area, commencement date, term of use, land purpose, planning conditions, and other rights and obligations of both parties.

The transfer or lease of collective commercial construction land as specified in the preceding paragraph shall be approved by more than two-thirds of the members of the villagers' meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the villagers' representatives.

The right to use collective commercial construction land obtained through transfer or other means may be transferred, exchanged, contributed as capital, donated, or mortgaged, unless otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations or otherwise agreed upon in the written contract signed by the land owner and the land use right holder.

The lease of collective commercial construction land, the transfer of the right to use collective construction land and its maximum term, transfer, exchange, contribution as capital, donation, mortgage, etc., shall be implemented with reference to state-owned construction land for similar purposes. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 64** Users of collective construction land shall strictly use the land in accordance with the purposes determined in the overall plan for land utilization and the urban and rural plan.

**Article 65** Buildings and structures constructed before the formulation of the overall plan for land utilization that do not conform to the purposes determined in the overall plan for land utilization shall not be rebuilt or expanded.

**Article 66** Under any of the following circumstances, the rural collective economic organization may, upon reporting to the people's government that originally approved the land use for approval, recover the right to use the land:

(1) Land is needed for the construction of public facilities and public welfare undertakings in townships (towns) and villages;
(2) The land is not used for the approved purposes;
(3) The use of the land is discontinued due to dissolution, relocation, or other reasons.

Where land owned by peasant collectives is recovered in accordance with item (1) of the preceding paragraph, appropriate compensation shall be given to the holder of the land use right.

The recovery of the right to use collective commercial construction land shall be handled in accordance with the written contract signed by both parties, unless otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

**Article 67** The competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level shall supervise and inspect violations of land management laws and regulations.

Where the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level supervises and inspects violations of laws and regulations on rural homestead management, the provisions of this Law on the supervision and inspection by the competent department of natural resources shall apply.

Land management supervision and inspection personnel shall be familiar with land management laws and regulations, be loyal to their duties, and enforce the law impartially.

**Article 68** When performing supervision and inspection duties, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level shall have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide documents and materials related to land rights for review or reproduction;
(2) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide explanations on issues related to land rights;
(3) Enter the site of the land illegally occupied by the unit or individual under inspection for survey;
(4) Order the unit or individual illegally occupying land to cease acts violating land management laws and regulations.

**Article 69** When land management supervision and inspection personnel perform their duties and need to enter the site for survey or require relevant units or individuals to provide documents, materials, and explanations, they shall present their land management supervision and inspection credentials.

**Article 70** Relevant units and individuals shall support and cooperate with the supervision and inspection conducted by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level regarding land violations, provide convenience, and shall not refuse or obstruct land management supervision and inspection personnel from performing their duties according to law.

**Article 71** If, during supervision and inspection work, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level discovers illegal acts by state functionaries that should be subject to disciplinary sanctions according to law, it shall handle them according to law; if it has no authority to handle them, it shall transfer the case to the supervisory authority or relevant authority for handling according to law.

**Article 72** If, during supervision and inspection work, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level discovers that a land violation constitutes a crime, it shall transfer the case to the relevant authority for criminal liability according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, it shall impose administrative penalties according to law.

**Article 73** Where administrative penalties should be imposed in accordance with the provisions of this Law, but the relevant competent department of natural resources fails to impose them, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at the higher level shall have the authority to order the relevant competent department of natural resources to make a decision on administrative penalties or directly impose administrative penalties, and impose disciplinary sanctions on the person in charge of the relevant competent department of natural resources.

## Chapter VII Legal LiabilityArticle 74 Any person who buys, sells, or otherwise illegally transfers land shall have his or her illegal gains confiscated by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level. If the land use is changed from agricultural to construction land in violation of the overall land use plan, the buildings and other facilities newly constructed on the illegally transferred land shall be demolished within a specified time limit, and the land shall be restored to its original state. If the land use complies with the overall land use plan, the buildings and other facilities newly constructed on the illegally transferred land shall be confiscated. A fine may also be imposed. The persons directly in charge and other persons directly responsible shall be given disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 75 Any person who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, occupies farmland to build kilns, graves, or houses, or engages in activities such as digging sand, quarrying, mining, or extracting soil on farmland, thereby destroying cultivation conditions, or who causes land desertification or salinization due to land development, shall be ordered by the competent department of natural resources or the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level, within their respective functions, to make corrections or carry out remediation within a specified time limit. A fine may also be imposed. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 76 Any person who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses to fulfill the obligation of land reclamation shall be ordered by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a specified time limit. If no correction is made within the time limit, the person shall be ordered to pay a reclamation fee, which shall be used exclusively for land reclamation, and a fine may be imposed.

Article 77 Any person who illegally occupies land without approval or by obtaining approval through fraudulent means shall be ordered by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level to return the illegally occupied land. If the land use is changed from agricultural to construction land in violation of the overall land use plan, the buildings and other facilities newly constructed on the illegally occupied land shall be demolished within a specified time limit, and the land shall be restored to its original state. If the land use complies with the overall land use plan, the buildings and other facilities newly constructed on the illegally occupied land shall be confiscated. A fine may also be imposed. The persons directly in charge and other persons directly responsible of the unit that illegally occupies land shall be given disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

If the land occupied exceeds the approved quantity, the excess land shall be treated as illegally occupied land.

Article 78 Any rural villager who illegally occupies land to build a house without approval or by obtaining approval through fraudulent means shall be ordered by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level to return the illegally occupied land and to demolish the house newly constructed on the illegally occupied land within a specified time limit.

If the land occupied exceeds the standards set by the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the excess land shall be treated as illegally occupied land.

Article 79 If a unit or individual without the authority to approve the requisition or use of land illegally approves the occupation of land, exceeds the approval authority to illegally approve the occupation of land, approves land use not in accordance with the purposes specified in the overall land use plan, or approves the occupation or requisition of land in violation of the procedures stipulated by law, the approval document shall be invalid. The persons directly in charge and other persons directly responsible for the illegal approval of land requisition or use shall be given disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law. The land illegally approved or used shall be recovered. If the relevant party refuses to return it, the act shall be treated as illegal occupation of land.

If the illegal approval of land requisition or use causes losses to the party concerned, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 80 Any person who embezzles or misappropriates the compensation fees for land requisition and other related fees of the unit whose land is requisitioned shall, if the act constitutes a crime, be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law. If the act does not constitute a crime, disciplinary sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 81 If the party concerned refuses to return the land when the right to use state-owned land is lawfully recovered, fails to return the land upon the expiration of the temporary land use period, or uses state-owned land not in accordance with the approved purposes, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the return of the land and impose a fine.

Article 82 Any person who, without authorization, transfers or leases the right to use land collectively owned by farmers for non-agricultural construction through means such as assignment or lease, or who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, assigns or leases collectively operated construction land to a unit or individual through means such as assignment or lease, shall be ordered by the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a specified time limit, have his or her illegal gains confiscated, and be fined.

Article 83 In accordance with the provisions of this Law, if an order is given to demolish buildings and other facilities newly constructed on illegally occupied land within a specified time limit, the construction unit or individual must immediately stop construction and demolish them on their own. If construction continues, the authority that made the penalty decision has the right to stop it. If the construction unit or individual disagrees with the administrative penalty decision ordering demolition within a specified time limit, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court within 15 days from the date of receiving the decision ordering demolition within a specified time limit. If no lawsuit is filed within the time limit and the demolition is not carried out on their own, the authority that made the penalty decision shall apply to the people's court for enforcement in accordance with the law, and the costs shall be borne by the violator.

Article 84 Any staff member of the competent department of natural resources or the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs who neglects their duties, abuses their power, or engages in malpractice for personal gain shall, if the act constitutes a crime, be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law. If the act does not constitute a crime, disciplinary sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 85 The use of land by foreign-invested enterprises shall be governed by this Law. If other laws provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 86 Before the preparation of the territorial space plan in accordance with the provisions of Article 18 of this Law, the overall land use plan and urban and rural plans that have been lawfully approved shall continue to be implemented.

Article 87 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1999.
