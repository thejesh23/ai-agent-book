# Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on April 27, 2005

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

Revised at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate the management of civil servants, protect their lawful rights and interests, strengthen supervision over them, ensure that they perform their duties correctly and responsibly, and build a contingent of high-caliber and professional civil servants who are firm in conviction, dedicated to serving the people, diligent and pragmatic, courageous in assuming responsibility, and honest and upright.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "civil servants" refer to staff members who perform public duties in accordance with the law, are included in the state administrative staffing, and whose salaries and benefits are borne by the state treasury.

Civil servants are an important part of the cadre contingent, a backbone force in the socialist cause, and public servants of the people.

Article 3 The obligations, rights, and management of civil servants shall be governed by this Law.

Where laws provide otherwise for the selection, appointment, supervision of leading members among civil servants, or for the obligations, rights, and management of supervisory officers, judges, prosecutors, etc., such provisions shall prevail.

Article 4 The civil servant system adheres to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, is guided by Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of Three Represents, the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, implements the basic line of the primary stage of socialism, implements the organizational line of the Communist Party of China in the new era, and upholds the principle of the Party managing cadres.

Article 5 The management of civil servants shall adhere to the principles of openness, equality, competition, and merit selection, and shall be conducted in accordance with the statutory authority, conditions, standards, and procedures.

Article 6 The management of civil servants shall adhere to the principle of balancing supervision and restraint with incentives and guarantees.

Article 7 The appointment of civil servants shall adhere to the principles of having both integrity and ability, with priority given to integrity; recruiting talents from all over the country and appointing people on their merits; prioritizing the cause and being fair and upright; highlighting political standards; and emphasizing work performance.

Article 8 The state implements classified management of civil servants to improve management efficiency and scientific standards.

Article 9 When assuming office, civil servants shall publicly take an oath of allegiance to the Constitution in accordance with legal provisions.

Article 10 The lawful performance of duties by civil servants shall be protected by law.

Article 11 The funds required for civil servants' salaries, benefits, insurance, as well as recruitment, rewards, training, and dismissal, shall be included in the fiscal budget and guaranteed.

Article 12 The central civil servant management department is responsible for the comprehensive management of civil servants nationwide. Local civil servant management departments at or above the county level are responsible for the comprehensive management of civil servants within their respective jurisdictions. Higher-level civil servant management departments guide the civil servant management work of lower-level departments. Civil servant management departments at all levels guide the civil servant management work of the organs at the same level.

## Chapter II Conditions, Obligations, and Rights of Civil Servants

Article 13 A civil servant shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Possessing the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Being at least 18 years old;

(3) Supporting the Constitution of the People's Republic of China, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, and the socialist system;

(4) Having good political quality and moral character;

(5) Having the physical and psychological conditions necessary to perform duties normally;

(6) Having the educational level and work ability required for the position;

(7) Other conditions stipulated by law.

Article 14 Civil servants shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Being loyal to the Constitution, abiding by and upholding the Constitution and laws in an exemplary manner, and consciously accepting the leadership of the Communist Party of China;

(2) Being loyal to the state, safeguarding the security, honor, and interests of the state;

(3) Being loyal to the people, serving the people wholeheartedly, and accepting supervision by the people;

(4) Being loyal to their duties, being diligent and responsible, obeying and executing decisions and orders lawfully made by superiors, performing duties in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures, and striving to improve work quality and efficiency;

(5) Keeping state secrets and work secrets;

(6) Taking the lead in practicing the core socialist values, adhering to the rule of law, observing discipline, abiding by professional ethics, and setting an example in observing social ethics and family virtues;

(7) Being honest and upright, fair and decent;

(8) Other obligations stipulated by law.

Article 15 Civil servants shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) Obtaining the working conditions necessary for performing their duties;

(2) Not being removed from office, demoted, dismissed, or subjected to disciplinary action without statutory reasons or statutory procedures;

(3) Receiving remuneration for work and enjoying benefits and insurance;

(4) Participating in training;

(5) Criticizing and making suggestions regarding the work of the organ and its leaders;

(6) Filing appeals and complaints;

(7) Applying for resignation;

(8) Other rights stipulated by law.

## Chapter III Positions, Ranks, and Grades

Article 16 The state implements a position classification system for civil servants.

Civil servant positions are classified into categories such as comprehensive management, professional and technical, and administrative law enforcement, based on the nature, characteristics, and management needs of the positions. In accordance with this Law, other position categories may be added for positions with special characteristics requiring separate management. The scope of application for each position category shall be separately stipulated by the state.

Article 17 The state implements a parallel system of positions and ranks for civil servants, establishing sequences for leadership positions and ranks based on the position categories and responsibilities of civil servants.

Article 18 Leadership positions of civil servants are established in accordance with the Constitution, relevant laws, and institutional specifications.

The levels of leadership positions are: national-level principal, national-level deputy, provincial/ministerial-level principal, provincial/ministerial-level deputy, department/bureau-level principal, department/bureau-level deputy, county/division-level principal, county/division-level deputy, township/section-level principal, and township/section-level deputy.

Article 19 Civil servant ranks are established at the department/bureau level and below.

The rank sequence for civil servants in the comprehensive management category is: First-level Inspector, Second-level Inspector, First-level Researcher, Second-level Researcher, Third-level Researcher, Fourth-level Researcher, First-level Principal Staff Member, Second-level Principal Staff Member, Third-level Principal Staff Member, Fourth-level Principal Staff Member, First-level Staff Member, and Second-level Staff Member.

The rank sequences for civil servants in position categories other than comprehensive management shall be separately stipulated by the state in accordance with this Law.

Article 20 Each organ shall set up specific positions for its civil servants and determine the job responsibilities and qualification requirements for each position, in accordance with the established functions, specifications, staffing limits, authorized numbers, and structural ratios.

Article 21 The leadership positions and ranks of civil servants shall correspond to corresponding grades. The correspondence between leadership positions, ranks, and grades of civil servants shall be stipulated by the state.

Based on work needs and the correspondence between leadership positions and ranks, civil servants may be transferred between or concurrently hold leadership positions and ranks; those who meet the prescribed qualification requirements may be promoted to leadership positions or ranks.

The grade of a civil servant is determined based on the leadership position or rank held, as well as their moral integrity, ability, work performance, and seniority. Civil servants in the same leadership position or rank may have their grades upgraded in accordance with state regulations.

The leadership positions, ranks, and grades of civil servants serve as the basis for determining their salaries and other benefits.

Article 22 The state establishes corresponding ranks for civil servants such as the people's police, fire and rescue personnel, customs, and diplomatic missions abroad, based on their work characteristics, corresponding to their leadership positions and ranks.

## Chapter IV Recruitment

Article 23 The recruitment of civil servants for positions at the level of First-level Principal Staff Member and below, or other equivalent rank levels, shall adopt the methods of open examinations, strict assessment, equal competition, and merit-based selection.

When recruiting civil servants in ethnic autonomous areas in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, appropriate consideration shall be given to ethnic minority applicants in accordance with the law and relevant regulations.

Article 24 The recruitment of civil servants for central organs and their directly affiliated institutions shall be organized by the central civil servant management department. The recruitment of civil servants for local organs at all levels shall be organized by the provincial civil servant management departments; when necessary, provincial civil servant management departments may authorize civil servant management departments of cities divided into districts to organize the recruitment.

Article 25 In addition to meeting the conditions stipulated in Article 13 of this Law, applicants for civil servant positions shall also meet the qualification requirements for the proposed position as stipulated by civil servant management departments at or above the provincial level.

The state implements a unified legal professional qualification examination system for civil servants who are initially engaged in the review of administrative penalty decisions, administrative reconsideration, administrative adjudication, and legal advisory work in administrative organs, organized by the judicial administrative department of the State Council in consultation with relevant departments.

Article 26 The following persons shall not be recruited as civil servants:

(1) Those who have been subjected to criminal punishment for committing a crime;

(2) Those who have been expelled from the Communist Party of China;

(3) Those who have been dismissed from public office;

(4) Those who have been listed as targets of joint disciplinary action for dishonesty in accordance with the law;

(5) Other circumstances stipulated by law where recruitment as a civil servant is not permitted.

Article 27 The recruitment of civil servants shall be conducted within the prescribed staffing limits and when there are corresponding position vacancies.

Article 28 When recruiting civil servants, a recruitment announcement shall be published. The recruitment announcement shall specify the positions to be filled, the number of vacancies, the qualification requirements for applicants, the application materials to be submitted, and other matters that applicants need to know.

Recruiting organs shall take measures to facilitate citizens' applications.

Article 29 Recruiting organs shall review applications based on the qualification requirements. The application materials submitted by applicants shall be truthful and accurate.

Article 30 Civil servant recruitment examinations shall be conducted through written examinations and interviews, among other methods. The content of the examinations shall be set separately based on the basic abilities required of civil servants and different position categories and levels of organs.

Article 31 Recruiting organs shall determine candidates for assessment based on examination results, and conduct qualification re-examination, assessment, and physical examinations.

The items and standards for physical examinations shall be determined based on the requirements of the positions. Specific measures shall be stipulated by the central civil servant management department jointly with the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 32 Recruiting organs shall, based on examination results, assessment findings, and physical examination results, propose a list of persons to be recruited and make it public. The publicity period shall be no less than five working days.

After the publicity period expires, recruiting organs at the central level shall submit the list of persons to be recruited to the central civil servant management department for recordation; recruiting organs at all local levels shall submit the list of persons to be recruited to the provincial or city-divided-into-districts level civil servant management department for approval.

Article 33 For the recruitment of civil servants for special positions, with the approval of civil servant management departments at or above the provincial level, the procedures may be simplified or other assessment methods may be adopted.

Article 34 Newly recruited civil servants shall have a probationary period of one year. Those who pass the probationary period shall be appointed; those who fail shall have their recruitment canceled.

## Chapter V Assessment

Article 35 The assessment of civil servants shall, in accordance with management authority, comprehensively assess their moral integrity, ability, diligence, performance, and integrity, with a focus on political quality and work performance. Assessment indicators shall be set separately based on different position categories and levels of organs.

Article 36 The assessment of civil servants includes regular assessment, special assessment, and periodic assessment. Periodic assessment shall be based on regular and special assessments.

Article 37 The periodic assessment of civil servants who are not leading members shall take the form of annual assessment. The individual shall first summarize their work based on position responsibilities and relevant requirements. The supervising leader, after soliciting opinions from the public, shall propose a recommended assessment grade, which shall be determined by the head of the organ or an authorized assessment committee.
The assessment of leading members shall be handled by the competent organ in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 38 The results of periodic assessment are divided into four grades: excellent, competent, basically competent, and incompetent.

The results of periodic assessment shall be notified to the civil servant in writing.

Article 39 The results of periodic assessment shall serve as the basis for adjusting a civil servant's position, leadership position, rank, grade, salary, as well as for rewards, training, and dismissal.

## Chapter VI Appointment and Removal of Positions and Ranks

Article 40 Leadership positions of civil servants are filled through election, appointment, or contract. Civil servant ranks are filled through appointment or contract.

The terms of office for leading members shall be implemented in accordance with state regulations.

Article 41 A civil servant elected to a position assumes the elected position upon the effectiveness of the election results; if the term expires and they are not re-elected, or if they resign, are recalled, or are removed from office during the term, their position shall terminate.

Article 42 For civil servants appointed to positions, when the probationary period expires and the assessment is passed, when their position or rank changes, or in other circumstances requiring the appointment or removal of a position or rank, the appointment or removal shall be conducted in accordance with management authority and prescribed procedures.

Article 43 The appointment of civil servants shall be conducted within the prescribed staffing limits and authorized numbers, and when there are corresponding position vacancies.

Article 44 If a civil servant needs to hold a concurrent position outside the organ due to work requirements, it shall be approved by the relevant organ, and they shall not receive any remuneration for the concurrent position.

## Chapter VII Promotion and Demotion of Positions and Ranks

Article 45 To be promoted to a leadership position, a civil servant shall meet the conditions and qualifications required for the proposed position in terms of political quality, work ability, educational level, and service experience.

Promotion to leadership positions for civil servants shall be step by step. For those who are exceptionally outstanding or have special work needs, they may be promoted by breaking the rules or skipping levels in accordance with regulations.

Article 46 The promotion of a civil servant to a leadership position shall be handled in accordance with the following procedures:

(1) Initiation of the motion;

(2) Democratic recommendation;

(3) Determination of assessment targets and organization of assessment;

(4) Discussion and decision in accordance with management authority;

(5) Completion of appointment procedures.

Article 47 When a leadership position below the department/bureau-level principal is vacant and there is no suitable candidate within the organ, candidates may be selected from society through appropriate means.

Article 48 Civil servants promoted to leadership positions shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, undergo a pre-appointment publicity system and a probationary period system for the position.

Article 49 Civil servant ranks shall be promoted step by step. Candidates shall be determined based on their moral integrity, ability, work performance, and service seniority, with reference to democratic recommendation or democratic evaluation results. After publicity, approval shall be obtained in accordance with management authority.

Article 50 The positions and ranks of civil servants are subject to both promotion and demotion. Those who are unsuitable or incompetent for their current position or rank shall be adjusted.

If a civil servant is assessed as incompetent in the annual assessment, they shall be demoted by one level in position or rank in accordance with prescribed procedures.

## Chapter VIII Rewards

Article 51 Rewards shall be given to civil servants or civil servant collectives who have demonstrated outstanding performance, achieved significant results or contributions, or have other outstanding deeds. Rewards shall adhere to the principle of combining regular rewards with timely rewards, and combining spiritual rewards with material rewards, with spiritual rewards as the main focus.

Rewards for civil servant collectives apply to institutions established according to the staffing sequence or work collectives formed to complete special tasks.

Article 52 Rewards shall be given to civil servants or civil servant collectives in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being loyal to their duties, working actively, daring to take responsibility, and achieving remarkable work performance;

(2) Abiding by laws and discipline, being honest and dedicated, having a decent work style, being fair in handling affairs, and playing an exemplary role;

(3) Making inventions and creations or putting forward reasonable suggestions in work, achieving significant economic or social benefits;

(4) Making outstanding contributions to enhancing national unity and maintaining social stability;

(5) Achieving outstanding results in protecting public property and saving state assets;

(6) Making meritorious contributions in preventing or eliminating accidents, thereby avoiding or reducing losses to the interests of the state and the people;

(7) Making outstanding contributions in specific environments such as emergency rescue and disaster relief;

(8) Making meritorious contributions in fighting against violations of discipline and law;

(9) Winning honor and interests for the state in foreign exchanges;

(10) Having other outstanding achievements.

Article 53 Rewards include: commendation, recording of a third-class merit, recording of a second-class merit, recording of a first-class merit, and conferring of a title.

Awarded civil servants or civil servant collectives shall be commended, and awarded individuals shall receive a one-time bonus or other benefits.

Article 54 The decision or approval of rewards for civil servants or civil servant collectives shall be made in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures.

Article 55 In accordance with state regulations, commemorative certificates or medals may be issued to civil servants who have participated in major work in specific periods or specific fields.

Article 56 Rewards shall be revoked for civil servants or civil servant collectives in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Obtaining the reward through fraud or deception;

(2) Concealing serious mistakes or seriously violating prescribed procedures when applying for the reward;

(3) Engaging in serious disciplinary or illegal acts that affect the reputation of the title;

(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations where the reward should be revoked.

## Chapter IX Supervision and Discipline

Article 57 Organs shall supervise civil servants in terms of their ideology and politics, performance of duties, work style, and compliance with laws and discipline, conduct education on diligent and honest governance, and establish daily management and supervision systems.

If problems are found through supervision of civil servants, depending on the circumstances, measures such as conversation reminders, criticism and education, ordering self-inspection, admonishment, organizational adjustment, or disciplinary action shall be taken.
If a civil servant is suspected of duty-related violations or crimes, the case shall be transferred to the supervisory organ for handling in accordance with the law.

Article 58 Civil servants shall consciously accept supervision, report work and personal matters as required, and report personal matters in accordance with regulations.

Article 59 Civil servants shall abide by laws and discipline and shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Spreading言论 that damages the authority of the Constitution, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, or the reputation of the state; organizing or participating in activities such as assemblies, processions, or demonstrations aimed at opposing the Constitution, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, or the state;
(2) Organizing or participating in illegal organizations, organizing or participating in strikes;

(3) Inciting or undermining ethnic relations, participating in ethnic separatist activities, or organizing or using religious activities to undermine ethnic unity and social stability;

(4) Failing to take responsibility, failing to act, neglecting duties, or delaying work;

(5) Refusing to execute decisions and orders lawfully made by superiors;

(6) Suppressing or retaliating against criticism, appeals, complaints, or reports;

(7) Engaging in fraud, misleading or deceiving leaders and the public;

(8) Engaging in bribery or corruption, using their position to seek personal gain for themselves or others;

(9) Violating financial discipline, wasting state assets;

(10) Abusing power, infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations;

(11) Disclosing state secrets or work secrets;

(12) Damaging the honor and interests of the state in foreign exchanges;

(13) Participating in or supporting activities such as pornography, drug use, gambling, or superstition;

(14) Violating professional ethics, social ethics, and family virtues;

(15) Participating in prohibited online dissemination behaviors or online activities in violation of relevant regulations;

(16) Engaging in or participating in for-profit activities in violation of relevant regulations, or holding concurrent positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations;

(17) Being absent from work without leave, or failing to return on time after going on official business or after the expiration of leave without justifiable reasons;(18) Other acts that violate discipline or the law.

Article 60 When executing official duties, if a public servant believes that a decision or order from a superior is erroneous, they may propose to the superior to correct or revoke the decision or order; if the superior does not change the decision or order, or demands immediate execution, the public servant shall execute the decision or order, with the consequences borne by the superior, and the public servant shall not bear responsibility; however, if the public servant executes a clearly illegal decision or order, they shall bear corresponding responsibility according to law.

Article 61 If a public servant should bear disciplinary responsibility for violating discipline or the law, they shall be given a sanction under this Law or be subject to a disciplinary sanction by a supervisory authority according to law; if the violation is minor and corrected after criticism and education, the sanction may be waived.

For the same disciplinary or legal violation, if the supervisory authority has already made a disciplinary sanction decision, the public servant's employing authority shall not impose another sanction.

Article 62 Sanctions include: warning, demerit, serious demerit, demotion, removal from post, and dismissal.

Article 63 Sanctions against public servants shall be based on clear facts, conclusive evidence, accurate characterization, appropriate handling, lawful procedures, and complete formalities.

When a public servant violates discipline or the law, the sanction-imposing authority shall decide to investigate the circumstances of the violation and inform the public servant of the facts determined by the investigation and the basis for the proposed sanction. The public servant has the right to make statements and defenses; the sanction-imposing authority shall not aggravate the sanction due to the public servant's defense.

If the sanction-imposing authority believes that a public servant should be sanctioned, it shall make a sanction decision within the prescribed time limit, in accordance with the management authority and prescribed procedures. The sanction decision shall be notified to the public servant in writing.

Article 64 During the period of a sanction, a public servant shall not be promoted in position, rank, or level; for those receiving demerit, serious demerit, demotion, or removal from post, they shall not be promoted in salary grade.

The duration of sanctions is: warning, six months; demerit, twelve months; serious demerit, eighteen months; demotion or removal from post, twenty-four months.

For those receiving removal from post, their level shall be reduced according to regulations.

Article 65 If a public servant receives a sanction other than dismissal and shows repentance during the sanction period without further disciplinary or legal violations, the sanction shall be automatically lifted upon expiration.

After the sanction is lifted, promotion in salary grade, level, position, and rank shall no longer be affected by the original sanction. However, lifting a demotion or removal from post sanction shall not be deemed as restoration of the original level, position, or rank.

## Chapter 10: Training

Article 66 Authorities shall conduct classified and graded training for public servants based on the requirements of their job duties and the need to improve their quality.

The state establishes specialized training institutions for public servants. Authorities may also entrust other training institutions with the task of training public servants as needed.

Article 67 Authorities shall provide initial training for newly recruited public servants during the probation period; provide pre-appointment or within-one-year post-appointment training for public servants promoted to leadership positions; provide specialized business training for public servants engaged in special tasks; and provide on-the-job training for all public servants to improve political quality, work ability, and update knowledge, with specialized technical training for public servants in technical positions.

The state systematically strengthens training for outstanding young public servants.

Article 68 Training for public servants is subject to registration management.

The time for public servants to participate in training shall be determined by the public servant management authority in accordance with the training requirements specified in Article 67 of this Law.

The training status and academic performance of public servants shall serve as one of the bases for assessment and for appointment and promotion.

## Chapter 11: Exchange and Recusal

Article 69 The state implements a system of exchange for public servants.

Public servants may exchange within the team of public servants and staff managed by reference to this Law, and may also exchange with personnel engaged in public affairs in state-owned enterprises and public institutions not managed by reference to this Law.

Exchange methods include transfer and rotation.

Article 70 Personnel engaged in public affairs in state-owned enterprises, institutions of higher learning, scientific research institutes, and other public institutions not managed by reference to this Law may be transferred to authorities to hold leadership positions or positions at the level of fourth-level investigator or above and other equivalent ranks.

The candidate for transfer must meet the conditions specified in Article 13 of this Law and the qualification requirements for the proposed position, and must not fall under the circumstances listed in Article 26 of this Law. The transferring authority shall strictly examine the candidate based on the above provisions and obtain approval according to management authority, and may conduct examinations for the candidate if necessary.

Article 71 Rotation of public servants between different positions shall meet the qualification requirements for the proposed position and be conducted within the prescribed staffing limits and number of positions.

For leadership members below the level of provincial or ministerial chief, planned and focused cross-regional and cross-department rotation shall be implemented.

For public servants holding leadership positions in internal organs and those with special job characteristics, planned rotation within the same authority shall be implemented.

Higher-level authorities shall focus on openly selecting public servants from grassroots authorities.

Article 72 Based on work needs, authorities may assign public servants to temporary posts to undertake major projects, key tasks, or other special work.

During the temporary post period, the public servant's personnel relationship with the original authority shall not change.

Article 73 Public servants shall comply with exchange decisions made by their authorities.

If a public servant applies for exchange themselves, it shall be approved according to management authority.

Article 74 Public servants with spousal relationships, direct blood relationships, collateral blood relationships within three generations, or close in-law relationships shall not work in the same authority in positions directly subordinate to the same leader or in positions with direct superior-subordinate leadership relationships, nor shall they work in the authority where one party holds a leadership position in organizational, personnel, disciplinary inspection, supervision, audit, or financial roles.

A public servant shall not serve as a leadership member in the industry regulatory or supervisory authority of an enterprise or for-profit organization operated by their spouse, children, or children's spouses.

If special circumstances due to region or job nature require flexible implementation of recusal from office, it shall be stipulated by the public servant management authority at or above the provincial level.

Article 75 Public servants holding major leadership positions in township-level, county-level, or districted city-level authorities and their relevant departments shall implement regional recusal according to relevant regulations.

Article 76 When executing official duties, a public servant shall recuse themselves in the following circumstances:

(1) Involving their own interests;

(2) Involving the interests of persons with whom they have the kinship relationships listed in paragraph 1 of Article 74 of this Law;

(3) Other circumstances that may affect the impartial execution of official duties.

Article 77 If a public servant falls under a recusal circumstance, they shall apply for recusal themselves; interested parties have the right to apply for the public servant's recusal. Other persons may provide the authority with information about the public servant's need for recusal.

The authority shall, based on the application of the public servant or interested party, decide whether to recuse after review, or may directly decide on recusal without an application.

Article 78 If other laws provide otherwise for recusal of public servants, those provisions shall prevail.

## Chapter 12: Salary, Benefits, and Insurance

Article 79 Public servants implement a nationally unified salary system.

The salary system for public servants follows the principle of distribution according to work, reflecting factors such as job responsibilities, work ability, work performance, and seniority, maintaining reasonable salary differences between different leadership positions, ranks, and levels.

The state establishes a normal salary growth mechanism for public servants.

Article 80 Public servant salaries include basic salary, allowances, subsidies, and bonuses.

Public servants enjoy regional additional allowances, hardship and remote area allowances, and post allowances according to state regulations.

Public servants enjoy housing, medical, and other subsidies and allowances according to state regulations.

Public servants assessed as excellent or competent in regular assessments shall receive year-end bonuses according to state regulations.

Public servant salaries shall be paid in full and on time.

Article 81 The salary level of public servants shall be coordinated with national economic development and adapted to social progress.

The state implements a salary survey system, regularly conducting surveys and comparisons of salary levels between public servants and comparable personnel in enterprises, and using the survey and comparison results as a basis for adjusting public servant salary levels.

Article 82 Public servants enjoy welfare benefits according to state regulations. The state improves the welfare benefits of public servants based on economic and social development levels.

Public servants implement the nationally prescribed working hours system and enjoy leave according to state regulations. If public servants work overtime on statutory working days, they shall be given corresponding compensatory leave; if compensatory leave is not possible, they shall be given subsidies according to state regulations.

Article 83 Public servants participate in social insurance according to law and enjoy insurance benefits according to state regulations.

If a public servant dies in the line of duty or due to illness, their family members shall enjoy the pensions and preferential treatment prescribed by the state.

Article 84 No authority shall, in violation of state regulations, unilaterally change the salary, welfare, or insurance policies for public servants, or arbitrarily raise or lower the salary, welfare, or insurance benefits of public servants. No authority shall deduct or delay the salaries of public servants.

## Chapter 13: Resignation and Dismissal

Article 85 A public servant resigning from public office shall submit a written application to the appointing authority. The appointing authority shall review and approve the application within thirty days from receipt; for applications from leadership members, review and approval shall be completed within ninety days from receipt.

Article 86 A public servant shall not resign from public office in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having not completed the minimum service period prescribed by the state;

(2) Holding a special position involving state secrets or having not passed the declassification period prescribed by the state after leaving such a position;

(3) Important official business has not been completed and requires the public servant's continued handling;

(4) Undergoing audit, disciplinary review, or supervisory investigation, or suspected of a crime with judicial proceedings not yet concluded;

(5) Other circumstances where resignation from public office is not permitted under laws or administrative regulations.

Article 87 If a public servant holding a leadership position needs to resign from their current position due to job changes as required by law, they shall go through resignation procedures.

A public servant holding a leadership position may voluntarily resign from their leadership position for personal or other reasons.

If a leadership member causes significant losses or adverse social impact due to serious errors or dereliction of duty, or bears leadership responsibility for a major accident, they shall take the blame and resign from their leadership position.

If a leadership member is no longer suitable for their current leadership position for other reasons, or should take the blame and resign but does not, they shall be ordered to resign from their leadership position.

Article 88 A public servant shall be dismissed in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Assessed as incompetent for two consecutive years in annual assessments;

(2) Unable to perform current duties and unwilling to accept other assignments;

(3) Due to adjustment, abolition, merger, or reduction of staffing in their authority, requiring job adjustment, but the public servant refuses reasonable arrangements;

(4) Failing to fulfill public servant obligations, not complying with laws and public servant discipline, and after education, still not improving, making them unsuitable to continue working in the authority, yet not warranting dismissal;

(5) Absent without leave, or after official travel or leave, failing to return without justifiable reasons for more than fifteen consecutive days, or accumulating more than thirty days within a year.

Article 89 A public servant shall not be dismissed in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Disabled in the line of duty, confirmed as having lost or partially lost work ability;

(2) Ill or injured, within the prescribed medical treatment period;

(3) Female public servants during pregnancy, maternity leave, or breastfeeding period;

(4) Other circumstances where dismissal is not permitted under laws or administrative regulations.

Article 90 Dismissal of a public servant shall be decided according to management authority. The dismissal decision shall be notified in writing to the dismissed public servant, along with the basis and reasons for dismissal.

A dismissed public servant may receive dismissal compensation or enjoy unemployment insurance according to relevant state regulations.

Article 91 Before leaving office, a public servant who resigns or is dismissed shall complete the handover of official business and, if necessary, undergo an audit according to regulations.

## Chapter 14: Retirement

Article 92 A public servant who reaches the retirement age prescribed by the state or completely loses work ability shall retire.

Article 93 A public servant who meets one of the following conditions may, upon voluntary application and approval by the appointing authority, retire early:

(1) Having worked for a full thirty years;

(2) Being less than five years from the retirement age prescribed by the state and having worked for a full twenty years;

(3) Other circumstances where early retirement is permitted under state regulations.

Article 94 After retirement, public servants shall enjoy the pension and other benefits prescribed by the state. The state shall provide necessary services and assistance for their living and health, and encourage them to utilize their expertise and participate in social development.

## Chapter 15: Appeals and Complaints

Article 95 If a public servant is dissatisfied with any of the following personnel decisions concerning themselves, they may apply for review to the original decision-making authority within thirty days from learning of the decision; if dissatisfied with the review result, they may file an appeal with the public servant management authority at the same level or the authority at the next higher level within fifteen days from receiving the review decision, according to regulations; or they may directly file an appeal within thirty days from learning of the decision without going through review:

(1) Sanctions;

(2) Dismissal or cancellation of recruitment;

(3) Demotion;

(4) Assessment as incompetent in regular assessment;

(5) Removal from office;

(6) Denial of application for resignation or early retirement;

(7) Improper determination or reduction of salary, welfare, or insurance benefits;

(8) Other circumstances where an appeal is permitted under laws or regulations.

If dissatisfied with the appeal decision made by an authority below the provincial level, a further appeal may be filed with the authority at the next higher level.

The authority handling public servant appeals shall establish a fair public servant appeal committee responsible for accepting and hearing appeal cases.

If a public servant is dissatisfied with a decision concerning themselves made by a supervisory authority and applies for review or reconsideration to the supervisory authority, it shall be handled according to relevant regulations.

Article 96 The original decision-making authority shall make a review decision within thirty days from receiving the review application and inform the applicant in writing. The authority handling the appeal shall make a decision within sixty days from accepting the appeal; for complex cases, the time may be appropriately extended, but the extension shall not exceed thirty days.

The execution of personnel decisions shall not be suspended during the review or appeal period.

A public servant shall not be subjected to harsher treatment due to applying for review or filing an appeal.

Article 97 If the authority handling the appeal finds that the personnel decision is erroneous after review, the original decision-making authority shall promptly correct it.

Article 98 If a public servant believes that their lawful rights and interests have been infringed upon by an authority or its leaders, they may file a complaint with the higher-level authority or the supervisory authority according to law. The authority receiving the complaint shall handle it promptly according to regulations.

Article 99 When filing an appeal or complaint, a public servant shall respect the facts and shall not fabricate facts, make false accusations, or frame others. Those who fabricate facts, make false accusations, or frame others shall be held legally responsible.

## Chapter 16: Position Appointment

Article 100 Based on work needs and with approval from the public servant management authority at or above the provincial level, authorities may implement an appointment system for highly specialized positions and auxiliary positions.

If the positions listed in the preceding paragraph involve state secrets, the appointment system shall not be implemented.

Article 101 When appointing public servants, authorities may conduct open recruitment by reference to the procedures for public servant examinations, or directly select and appoint from qualified personnel.

Appointment of public servants shall be conducted within the prescribed staffing limits and salary budget limits.

Article 102 When appointing a public servant, the authority shall sign a written appointment contract based on the principles of equality, voluntariness, and mutual agreement, specifying the rights and obligations of both the authority and the appointed public servant. The appointment contract may be modified or terminated upon mutual agreement.

The signing, modification, or termination of the appointment contract shall be reported to the public servant management authority at the same level for record.

Article 103 The appointment contract shall include terms such as contract duration, position and job requirements, salary, welfare, insurance benefits, and liability for breach of contract.

The contract duration shall be one to five years. The contract may stipulate a probation period of one to twelve months.

Appointed public servants shall implement a negotiated salary system, with specific measures stipulated by the central public servant management authority.

Article 104 Authorities shall manage appointed public servants in accordance with this Law and the appointment contract.

Article 105 If a dispute arises between an appointed public servant and their employing authority regarding the performance of the appointment contract, the public servant may apply for arbitration within sixty days from the occurrence of the dispute.

The public servant management authority at or above the provincial level may establish a personnel dispute arbitration committee as needed to accept arbitration applications. The personnel dispute arbitration committee shall consist of representatives from the public servant management authority, the employing authority, appointed public servants, and legal experts.

If a party is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within fifteen days from receiving the arbitration award. If one party fails to comply with the effective arbitration award, the other party may apply to the People's Court for enforcement.

## Chapter 17: Legal Liability

Article 106 For any of the following violations of this Law, the leading authority at or above the county level or the public servant management authority shall, according to management authority and based on the specific circumstances, order correction or declare the action invalid; for responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel, depending on the severity, they shall be given criticism and education, ordered to make checks, admonished, subject to organizational adjustment, or sanctioned; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Conducting recruitment, transfer, rotation, appointment, or promotion of public servants not in accordance with staffing limits, position numbers, or qualification requirements;

(2) Conducting rewards, punishments, recusal, or retirement of public servants not in accordance with prescribed conditions;

(3) Conducting recruitment, transfer, rotation, appointment, promotion, assessment, rewards, or punishments of public servants not in accordance with prescribed procedures;

(4) Changing the standards for public servant salary, welfare, or insurance benefits in violation of state regulations;

(5) Leaking exam questions, violating exam discipline, or engaging in other actions that seriously affect openness and fairness in recruitment, open selection, or similar activities;

(6) Failing to accept or handle public servant appeals or complaints in accordance with regulations;

(7) Other violations of the provisions of this Law.

Article 107 If a public servant resigns from public office or retires, those who were originally leadership members or held leadership positions at or above the county or department level shall not, within three years of leaving office, and other public servants shall not, within two years of leaving office, take up positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations directly related to their former work, or engage in for-profit activities directly related to their former work.If a civil servant, after resigning from public office or retiring, violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the civil service authority at the same level as their original unit shall order them to rectify within a specified period; if they fail to do so, the market regulatory authority at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal income earned by the individual during their period of employment, order the receiving unit to dismiss the individual, and, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine on the receiving unit of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income of the penalized individual.

Article 108 Any staff member of the civil service authority who, in violation of this Law, abuses their power, neglects their duties, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, they shall be given a sanction or subjected to a government disciplinary action by the supervisory authority in accordance with the law.

Article 109 In the recruitment, appointment, or other processes of civil servants, any act such as concealing true information, engaging in fraud, cheating in examinations, or disrupting examination order shall be dealt with by the civil service authority based on the severity of the circumstances, including invalidating examination results, disqualifying candidates, or restricting future applications; if the circumstances are serious, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 110 If a unit causes damage to the reputation of a civil servant due to erroneous personnel handling, it shall apologize, restore the reputation, and eliminate the adverse effects; if economic losses are caused, compensation shall be provided in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 18 Supplementary Provisions

Article 111 For the purposes of this Law, "leading members" refer to the leading personnel of a unit, excluding those holding leadership positions in internal organs of the unit.

Article 112 Staff members, excluding service workers, of public institutions authorized by laws or regulations to perform public affairs management functions may, upon approval, be managed by reference to this Law.

Article 113 This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2019.
