# Earthquake Prevention and Disaster Reduction Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 29, 1997

Revised at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 27, 2008

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and mitigating earthquake disasters, protecting the safety of people's lives and property, and promoting the sustainable development of the economy and society.

Article 2 This Law applies to earthquake prevention and disaster reduction activities, including earthquake monitoring and prediction, earthquake disaster prevention, earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction, carried out within the territory of the People's Republic of China and in other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The work of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction shall follow the policy of giving priority to prevention and combining prevention with rescue.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen their leadership over earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work, incorporate such work into their respective national economic and social development plans, and include the necessary funds in their fiscal budgets.

Article 5 Under the leadership of the State Council, the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and other relevant departments of the State Council, including those in charge of comprehensive economic macro-control, construction, civil affairs, public health, and public security, shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, assume their respective responsibilities, closely cooperate with each other, and jointly do a good job in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction.

The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments shall, under the leadership of the people's governments at the same level, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, assume their respective responsibilities, closely cooperate with each other, and jointly do a good job in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The national earthquake relief command organization shall be responsible for the unified leadership, command, and coordination of earthquake relief work nationwide. The earthquake relief command organizations of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the unified leadership, command, and coordination of earthquake relief work within their respective administrative regions.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall undertake the routine work of the earthquake relief command organizations of the people's governments at the same level.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels shall organize the publicity and education of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction knowledge, enhance citizens' awareness of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction, and improve the earthquake prevention and disaster reduction capability of the whole society.

Article 8 All units and individuals have the obligation to participate in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction activities in accordance with the law.

The State encourages and guides social organizations and individuals to carry out mass earthquake monitoring and prevention activities, and to monitor and prevent earthquakes.

The State encourages and guides volunteers to participate in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction activities.

Article 9 The Chinese People's Liberation Army, the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, and the militia organizations shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws, administrative regulations, and military regulations, as well as the orders of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, carry out earthquake relief tasks and protect the safety of people's lives and property.

Article 10 Those engaged in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction activities shall comply with the relevant national standards for earthquake prevention and disaster reduction.

Article 11 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research on earthquake prevention and disaster reduction, gradually increases investment in scientific and technological research on earthquake prevention and disaster reduction, promotes advanced scientific research results, strengthens international cooperation and exchanges, and improves the level of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Earthquake Prevention and Disaster Reduction Plans

Article 12 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, organize the formulation of the national earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the State Council for approval.

The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments at the same level, organize the formulation of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plans for their respective administrative regions based on the earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan of the next higher level and the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions. Such plans shall be implemented after being submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval and shall be filed with the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 13 The formulation of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plans shall follow the principles of overall planning, highlighting key points, rational layout, and comprehensive prevention. Such plans shall be based on the results of earthquake situation and earthquake disaster prediction, and shall fully consider the needs for the safety of people's lives and property, economic and social development, and the protection of resources and the environment.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly provide relevant information as needed for the formulation of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plans.

Article 14 The contents of an earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan shall include: the earthquake situation and the overall objectives of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction, the layout of the earthquake monitoring network, earthquake disaster prevention measures, earthquake emergency rescue measures, and safeguard measures for earthquake prevention and disaster reduction technology, information, funds, and materials.

When formulating an earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan, specific arrangements shall be made for the construction of the earthquake monitoring network, earthquake situation tracking, earthquake disaster prevention measures, earthquake emergency preparedness, and publicity and education of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction knowledge in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas.

Article 15 Before submitting an earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan for approval, the organizing and formulating authority shall solicit opinions from relevant departments, units, experts, and the public.

The document submitted for approval of an earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan shall include the status of opinion adoption and the reasons thereof.

Article 16 Once an earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plan is approved and published, it shall be strictly implemented; if modifications are truly necessary due to changes in the earthquake situation and the needs of economic and social development, it shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the original approval procedures.

## Chapter III Earthquake Monitoring and Prediction

Article 17 The State strengthens earthquake monitoring and prediction work, establishes a multi-disciplinary earthquake monitoring system, and gradually improves the level of earthquake monitoring and prediction.

Article 18 The State implements unified planning and graded and classified management of earthquake monitoring networks.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council, formulate plans for earthquake monitoring networks.

The national earthquake monitoring network consists of the national-level earthquake monitoring network, provincial-level earthquake monitoring networks, and city- and county-level earthquake monitoring networks. The construction funds and operating expenses for these networks shall be included in the fiscal budgets.

Article 19 The construction units of major construction projects such as reservoirs, oil fields, and nuclear power plants shall, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council, construct dedicated earthquake monitoring networks or strong motion monitoring facilities. The construction funds and operating expenses for these facilities shall be borne by the construction units.

Article 20 The construction of earthquake monitoring networks shall comply with laws, regulations, and relevant national standards to ensure construction quality.

Article 21 Earthquake monitoring networks shall not be suspended or terminated without authorization.

Units that detect, transmit, analyze, process, store, and report earthquake monitoring information shall ensure the quality and security of the earthquake monitoring information.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant units to provide communication, transportation, electricity, and other support conditions for the operation of earthquake monitoring networks.

Article 22 The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under coastal local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the monitoring and prediction of earthquake activities in sea areas. After an earthquake occurs in a sea area, the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the local people's government at or above the county level shall promptly notify the marine administrative department and the local maritime administration agency, among others.

The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where volcanoes are located shall use earthquake monitoring facilities and technical means to strengthen the monitoring and prediction of volcanic activities.

Article 23 The State protects earthquake monitoring facilities and the earthquake observation environment in accordance with the law.

No unit or individual may occupy, damage, dismantle, or move earthquake monitoring facilities without authorization. If earthquake monitoring facilities are damaged, the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the local people's government at or above the county level shall take emergency measures to organize repairs and ensure the normal operation of the earthquake monitoring facilities.

No unit or individual may endanger the earthquake observation environment. The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments at the same level, delimit the protection scope of the earthquake observation environment in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council, and incorporate it into the overall land use plan and urban and rural plans.

Article 24 The construction of new projects, expansion projects, or renovation projects shall avoid causing harm to earthquake monitoring facilities and the earthquake observation environment. For the construction of national key projects, if it is truly impossible to avoid causing harm to earthquake monitoring facilities and the earthquake observation environment, the construction unit shall, in accordance with the requirements of the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the local people's government at or above the county level, construct additional anti-interference facilities; if anti-interference facilities cannot be constructed, new earthquake monitoring facilities shall be constructed.

For construction projects within the protection scope of the earthquake observation environment, the competent department of urban and rural planning shall, when legally issuing the site selection opinion, seek the opinion of the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration; if a site selection opinion is not required to be issued, the competent department of urban and rural planning shall, when legally issuing the construction land planning permit or the rural construction planning permit, seek the opinion of the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration.

Article 25 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall establish and improve a platform for sharing earthquake monitoring information to provide services to the society.

The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly report earthquake monitoring information to the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the people's government at the next higher level.

The management units of dedicated earthquake monitoring networks and strong motion monitoring facilities shall promptly report earthquake monitoring information to the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 26 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the research results of earthquake monitoring information, make predictions regarding the location, time, and magnitude of possible earthquakes.

Other units and individuals who propose earthquake prediction opinions through research shall submit a written report to the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the local people's government at or above the county level in the location or the predicted area, or directly to the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council. The department or agency receiving the written report shall register it and issue a receipt.

Article 27 Units and individuals that observe abnormal phenomena possibly related to earthquakes may report to the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the local people's government at or above the county level, or directly to the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council.

Upon receiving such reports, the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall register them and promptly organize investigations and verifications.

Article 28 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall organize and convene earthquake situation consultation meetings. When necessary, they shall invite relevant departments, experts, and other relevant personnel to participate, conduct comprehensive analysis and research on earthquake prediction opinions and abnormal phenomena possibly related to earthquakes, form earthquake situation consultation opinions, and report them to the people's government at the same level; if an earthquake prediction opinion is formed through earthquake situation consultation, it shall be reviewed before being reported to the people's government at the same level, the review results shall be made, and countermeasures and suggestions shall be proposed.

Article 29 The State implements a unified release system for earthquake prediction opinions.

Long-term and medium-term earthquake prediction opinions for the whole country shall be released by the State Council. Earthquake prediction opinions for the administrative regions of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be released by the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the State Council.

Except for publishing the research results of oneself or one's own unit on long-term and medium-term earthquake activity trends and conducting relevant academic exchanges, no unit or individual may disseminate earthquake prediction opinions to the public. No unit or individual may disseminate earthquake prediction opinions or their review results to the public.

Article 30 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall, based on the earthquake activity trends and earthquake disaster prediction results, propose opinions on determining key earthquake monitoring and defense areas, and submit them to the State Council for approval.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall strengthen earthquake situation tracking in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas, conduct analysis and assessment of earthquake activity trends, propose annual earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work opinions, and submit them to the State Council for approval before implementation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas shall, based on the annual earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work opinions and local earthquake activity trends, organize relevant departments to strengthen earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work.

The departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas shall increase the density of earthquake monitoring networks, organize and carry out earthquake situation tracking, mobile observation, observation of abnormal phenomena possibly related to earthquakes, and mass monitoring and prevention work, and promptly report the relevant situation to the department or agency responsible for earthquake administration under the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 31 The State supports the construction of a national earthquake intensity rapid reporting system.

After an earthquake disaster occurs, the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall, through the national earthquake intensity rapid reporting system, quickly determine the degree of disaster, providing a basis for commanding earthquake relief work.

Article 32 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen earthquake monitoring in areas where earthquake disasters have occurred, set up mobile observation points at the earthquake site, and promptly analyze and determine the trend of earthquake activity based on the development and changes of the earthquake situation, providing a basis for aftershock prevention work.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council, the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level, and the management units of earthquake monitoring networks shall promptly collect and preserve relevant earthquake data and information, and establish complete archives.

Article 33 Foreign organizations or individuals engaged in earthquake monitoring activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China and in other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China must obtain approval from the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council together with relevant departments, and shall conduct such activities in the form of cooperation with relevant departments or units of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter IV Earthquake Disaster Prevention

Article 34 The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council shall be responsible for formulating the national seismic intensity zoning map or the ground motion parameter zoning map.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for reviewing the seismic safety evaluation reports of construction projects and determining the requirements for seismic fortification.

Article 35 New construction projects, expansion projects, and renovation projects shall meet the requirements for seismic fortification.

Major construction projects and construction projects that may cause serious secondary disasters shall undergo seismic safety evaluation in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council, and shall be seismically fortified in accordance with the requirements for seismic fortification determined by the reviewed seismic safety evaluation report. The seismic safety evaluation unit for construction projects shall conduct seismic safety evaluation in accordance with relevant national standards and shall be responsible for the quality of the seismic safety evaluation report.

Construction projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be seismically fortified in accordance with the requirements for seismic fortification determined by the seismic intensity zoning map or the ground motion parameter zoning map; for construction projects in densely populated places such as schools and hospitals, they shall be designed and constructed in accordance with seismic fortification requirements higher than those for local residential buildings, and effective measures shall be taken to enhance seismic fortification capability.

Article 36 The mandatory standards for relevant construction projects shall be aligned with the requirements for seismic fortification.

Article 37 The State encourages city people's governments to organize the formulation of seismic microzoning maps. Seismic microzoning maps shall be reviewed by the competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council.

Article 38 The construction unit shall be responsible for the entire process of seismic design and construction of the construction project.

The design unit shall carry out seismic design in accordance with the requirements for seismic fortification and the mandatory standards for engineering construction, and shall be responsible for the quality of the seismic design and the accuracy of the construction drawing design documents issued.

The construction unit shall carry out construction in accordance with the construction drawing design documents and the mandatory standards for engineering construction, and shall be responsible for the construction quality.

The construction unit and the construction unit shall select materials, components, and equipment that meet the requirements of the construction drawing design documents and the relevant national standards.

The project supervision unit shall implement supervision in accordance with the construction drawing design documents and the mandatory standards for engineering construction, and shall bear the supervision responsibility for the construction quality.

Article 39 For the following completed construction projects that have not adopted seismic fortification measures or whose seismic fortification measures do not meet the requirements for seismic fortification, a seismic performance appraisal shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant state regulations, and necessary seismic strengthening measures shall be taken:

(1) Major construction projects;

(2) Construction projects that may cause serious secondary disasters;

(3) Construction projects of significant historical, scientific, artistic value or important commemorative significance;

(4) Construction projects in densely populated places such as schools and hospitals;

(5) Construction projects within key earthquake monitoring and defense areas.

Article 40 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the management of seismic fortification for rural residential houses and rural public facilities, organize the research and development of practical seismic technologies for rural areas, promote building design and construction technologies that meet the requirements for seismic fortification, are economical and applicable, and have local characteristics, train relevant technical personnel, build demonstration projects, and gradually improve the level of seismic fortification for rural residential houses and rural public facilities.

The State shall provide necessary support for rural residential houses and rural public facilities that require seismic fortification.

Article 41 Urban and rural planning shall, based on the needs for earthquake emergency shelter, reasonably determine emergency evacuation passages and emergency shelter sites, and make overall arrangements for the construction of infrastructure such as transportation, water supply, sewage discharge, and electricity necessary for earthquake emergency shelter.

Article 42 Local people's governments at or above the county level in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas shall, based on actual needs, allocate earthquake relief funds and materials in their fiscal budgets and material reserves.

Article 43 The State encourages and supports the research, development, promotion, and use of new technologies, new processes, and new materials that meet the requirements for seismic fortification and are economical and practical.

Article 44 County people's governments and their relevant departments, as well as grassroots organizations such as township people's governments and urban sub-district offices, shall organize the popularization and publicity of earthquake emergency knowledge and necessary earthquake emergency rescue drills to enhance citizens' ability to self-rescue and rescue each other during earthquake disasters.

State organs, organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall, in accordance with the requirements of the local people's government and in light of their respective actual conditions, strengthen the publicity and education of earthquake emergency knowledge for their personnel and carry out earthquake emergency rescue drills.

Schools shall conduct education on earthquake emergency knowledge, organize necessary earthquake emergency rescue drills, and cultivate students' safety awareness and ability to self-rescue and rescue each other.

News media shall carry out public interest publicity on earthquake disaster prevention, emergency response, and self-rescue and mutual rescue knowledge.

The competent department of earthquake administration under the State Council and the departments or agencies responsible for earthquake administration under local people's governments at or above the county level shall guide, assist, and supervise relevant units in doing a good job in the publicity and education of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction knowledge and earthquake emergency rescue drills.

Article 45 The State develops an earthquake disaster insurance industry with financial support, and encourages units and individuals to participate in earthquake disaster insurance.## Chapter 5: Earthquake Emergency Rescue

Article 46 The earthquake work department of the State Council shall, jointly with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate the national earthquake emergency plan, which shall be submitted to the State Council for approval. The relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with the national earthquake emergency plan, formulate their own departmental earthquake emergency plans and report them to the earthquake work department of the State Council for recordation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level, their relevant departments, and the people's governments of townships and towns shall, in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, rules, the earthquake emergency plans of the people's governments at higher levels and their relevant departments, and the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, formulate earthquake emergency plans for their administrative regions and their departments. The earthquake emergency plans of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and larger cities shall be reported to the earthquake work department of the State Council for recordation.

The management and operation units of infrastructure such as transportation, railways, water conservancy, electric power, and communications, as well as places with dense populations such as schools and hospitals, and production and operation units such as nuclear power plants, mines, and hazardous materials that may cause secondary disasters, shall formulate earthquake emergency plans and report them to the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the people's government at the county level where they are located for recordation.

Article 47 The contents of an earthquake emergency plan shall include: the organizational command system and its responsibilities, prevention and early warning mechanisms, handling procedures, emergency response and emergency support measures, etc.

Earthquake emergency plans shall be revised in a timely manner according to actual conditions.

Article 48 After the release of an earthquake prediction opinion, the relevant people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, based on the predicted earthquake situation, declare the relevant area to enter a period of imminent earthquake emergency; the relevant local people's government shall, in accordance with the earthquake emergency plan, organize relevant departments to carry out emergency precautions and preparations for earthquake resistance and disaster relief.

Article 49 Based on factors such as the degree of social harm and the scope of impact, earthquake disasters are classified into four levels: general, relatively large, major, and extraordinarily major. The specific classification standards shall be implemented in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

After a general or relatively large earthquake disaster occurs, the people's government of the city or county where the earthquake occurred is responsible for organizing relevant departments to activate the earthquake emergency plan; after a major earthquake disaster occurs, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the earthquake occurred is responsible for organizing relevant departments to activate the earthquake emergency plan; after an extraordinarily major earthquake disaster occurs, the State Council is responsible for organizing relevant departments to activate the earthquake emergency plan.

Article 50 After an earthquake disaster occurs, the earthquake resistance and disaster relief command organization shall immediately organize relevant departments and units to quickly ascertain the disaster situation, propose a plan for the deployment of earthquake emergency rescue forces, and take the following emergency measures:

(1) Quickly organize the rescue of buried persons, and organize relevant units and personnel to carry out self-rescue and mutual rescue;
(2) Quickly organize the implementation of emergency medical treatment, coordinate the transfer, reception, and treatment of the injured;
(3) Quickly organize the repair of damaged infrastructure such as transportation, railways, water conservancy, electric power, and communications;
(4) Activate emergency shelters or set up temporary shelters, set up relief supply points, provide relief items, simple shelters, and temporary housing, promptly transfer and resettle affected people, ensure the disinfection of drinking water and water quality safety, actively carry out sanitation and epidemic prevention, and properly arrange the lives of affected people;
(5) Quickly control dangerous sources, seal off dangerous areas, do a good job in the investigation, monitoring, and early warning of secondary disasters, and prevent secondary disasters such as fires, floods, explosions, landslides, collapses, debris flows, ground subsidence, or large-scale leakage of highly toxic, highly corrosive, or radioactive substances that may be triggered by the earthquake, as well as the occurrence of infectious disease epidemics;
(6) Take necessary measures to maintain social order and public security in accordance with the law.

Article 51 After an extraordinarily major earthquake disaster occurs, the earthquake resistance and disaster relief command organization of the State Council shall establish a field command organization in the earthquake-stricken area, and set up corresponding working groups as needed to uniformly organize, lead, command, and coordinate earthquake resistance and disaster relief efforts.

People's governments at all levels, relevant departments and units, the Chinese People's Liberation Army, the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, and the militia organizations shall, in accordance with the unified deployment, divide responsibilities, cooperate closely, and jointly do a good job in earthquake emergency rescue work.

Article 52 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the earthquake-stricken area shall promptly report information on the earthquake situation and disaster situation to the people's government at the next higher level; when necessary, they may bypass levels to report, and shall not delay, falsify, or conceal reports.

Information on the earthquake situation, disaster situation, and earthquake resistance and disaster relief shall be managed by designated departments in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council, and shall be released uniformly, accurately, and promptly.

Article 53 The State encourages and supports the research and development of new technologies and equipment for earthquake emergency rescue, and the dispatch, transportation, and stockpiling of necessary emergency rescue facilities and equipment to improve the level of emergency rescue.

Article 54 The State Council shall establish a national earthquake disaster emergency rescue team.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the people's governments of cities and counties in key earthquake monitoring and defense areas may, based on actual needs, make full use of existing teams such as firefighting, and establish earthquake disaster emergency rescue teams in accordance with the principle of one team with multiple uses and the combination of full-time and part-time personnel.

Earthquake disaster emergency rescue teams shall be equipped with corresponding equipment and gear, conduct training and drills, and improve their earthquake disaster emergency rescue capabilities.

When carrying out rescue operations, earthquake disaster emergency rescue teams shall first provide emergency rescue to persons buried under collapsed buildings and structures.

Article 55 The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, according to their division of responsibilities, coordinate and cooperate, and take effective measures to ensure that earthquake disaster emergency rescue teams and medical treatment teams can carry out earthquake disaster emergency rescue activities quickly and efficiently.

Article 56 Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments may establish volunteer teams for earthquake disaster rescue, and organize the conduct of earthquake emergency rescue knowledge training and drills, so that volunteers master the necessary earthquake emergency rescue skills and enhance the earthquake disaster emergency rescue capability.

Article 57 The earthquake work department of the State Council shall, jointly with relevant departments and units, organize and coordinate foreign rescue teams and medical teams to carry out earthquake disaster emergency rescue activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

The earthquake resistance and disaster relief command organization of the State Council is responsible for the overall dispatch of foreign rescue teams and medical teams, and shall scientifically and reasonably arrange emergency rescue tasks based on their professional expertise.

Local people's governments at all levels in the earthquake-stricken area shall provide support and cooperation to foreign rescue teams and medical teams in carrying out emergency rescue activities.

## Chapter 6: Post-Earthquake Transitional Resettlement and Recovery and Reconstruction

Article 58 The State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the earthquake-stricken area shall promptly organize investigations and assessments of earthquake disaster losses to provide a basis for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction.

The specific work of investigating and assessing earthquake disaster losses shall be undertaken by the earthquake work department of the State Council or the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the earthquake-stricken area, together with relevant departments such as finance, construction, and civil affairs, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 59 If affected people in the earthquake-stricken area need transitional resettlement, flexible and diverse methods of resettlement shall be adopted based on the actual conditions of the earthquake-stricken area, provided that safety is ensured.

Article 60 Transitional resettlement sites shall be located in areas with convenient transportation conditions that facilitate the resumption of production and daily life for affected people, and shall avoid earthquake active faults and areas prone to serious secondary disasters.

The scale of transitional resettlement sites shall be moderate, and corresponding disaster prevention and epidemic prevention measures shall be taken, along with the construction of necessary supporting infrastructure and public service facilities, to ensure the safety and basic living needs of the affected people.

Article 61 The implementation of transitional resettlement shall, as far as possible, protect agricultural land and avoid causing damage to nature reserves, drinking water source protection areas, and ecologically fragile areas.

Land for transitional resettlement shall be arranged as temporary land, which may be used first, with the relevant land use procedures being handled legally afterwards; if it is not converted to permanent land upon expiration, it shall be reclaimed and returned to the original land user.

Article 62 The people's government at the county level where the transitional resettlement site is located shall organize relevant departments to strengthen the monitoring of secondary disasters, drinking water quality, food hygiene, epidemics, etc., conduct epidemiological investigations, rectify environmental sanitation, and avoid pollution of soil, water environment, etc.

The public security organs at the location of the transitional resettlement site shall strengthen public security management, crack down on various illegal and criminal activities in accordance with the law, and maintain normal social order.

Article 63 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the earthquake-stricken area, their relevant departments, and the people's governments of townships and towns shall promptly organize the repair of damaged agricultural production facilities, provide technical guidance for agricultural production, and restore agricultural production as soon as possible; give priority to restoring the production of enterprises such as power supply, water supply, and gas supply, and provide support for the resumption of production of large backbone enterprises, creating conditions for the comprehensive restoration of agricultural, industrial, and service industry production and operation.

Article 64 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the leadership, organization, and coordination of post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction work.

The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, under the leadership of the people's government at the same level, closely cooperate according to their division of responsibilities, take effective measures, and jointly do a good job in post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction.

Article 65 The relevant departments of the State Council shall organize relevant experts to conduct investigations and assessments of the damage mechanisms of related construction projects caused by earthquake activity, providing a scientific basis for revising and improving the mandatory standards for related construction projects and taking seismic fortification measures.

Article 66 After an extraordinarily major earthquake disaster occurs, the comprehensive economic macro-control department of the State Council shall, jointly with the relevant departments of the State Council and the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the earthquake-stricken area, organize the formulation of a post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the State Council for approval; after a major, relatively large, or general earthquake disaster occurs, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the earthquake-stricken area shall organize the formulation of a post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan based on actual needs.

Basic data such as geology, survey, mapping, land, meteorology, hydrology, and environment obtained from the investigation and assessment of earthquake disaster losses, and the seismic ground motion parameter zoning map reviewed by the earthquake work department of the State Council, shall serve as the basis for formulating the post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan.

When formulating a post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan, opinions from relevant departments, units, experts, and the public, especially the affected people in the earthquake-stricken area, shall be solicited; major issues shall be subject to special expert argumentation.

Article 67 The post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan shall, based on geological conditions, the distribution of earthquake active faults, and the carrying capacity of resources and the environment, focus on making arrangements for the layout of towns and villages, the construction of infrastructure and public service facilities, disaster prevention and mitigation, the ecological environment, and the protection of natural resources and historical and cultural heritage.

The site selection for towns and villages that need to be relocated to new sites within the earthquake-stricken area, and the site selection for post-earthquake reconstruction projects, shall comply with the post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan and the requirements for seismic fortification, disaster prevention and mitigation, and shall avoid earthquake active faults or ecologically fragile areas and areas prone to disasters such as floods, landslides, collapses, debris flows, ground subsidence, as well as natural foci of infectious diseases.

Article 68 Local people's governments at all levels in the earthquake-stricken area shall, based on the post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction plan and the local economic and social development level, organize the implementation of post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction in a planned and step-by-step manner.

Article 69 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the earthquake-stricken area shall organize relevant departments and experts, based on the results of the investigation and assessment of earthquake disaster losses, to formulate a cleanup and protection plan, clarifying the protection scope and measures for typical earthquake sites and relics, cultural relic protection units, and buildings and structures with historical value and ethnic characteristics.

The cleanup of the earthquake disaster site shall be carried out in different zones and categories according to the cleanup and protection plan, and in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state regulations, properly clean up, transport, and dispose of relevant radioactive substances, hazardous waste, and toxic chemicals, carry out epidemic prevention work, and prevent the occurrence of infectious diseases and major animal epidemics.

Article 70 Post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction shall make overall arrangements for the construction of infrastructure and municipal public facilities such as transportation, railways, water conservancy, electric power, communications, water supply, and power supply; public service facilities such as schools, hospitals, culture, commercial services, disaster prevention and mitigation, and environmental protection; as well as housing and barrier-free facilities, and reasonably determine the scale and sequence of construction.

Post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction in rural areas shall respect the wishes of villagers, give play to the role of villagers' self-governing organizations, mainly rely on self-construction by the masses, supplemented by government subsidies, social assistance, and paired assistance, adapt measures to local conditions, save and intensively use land, and protect farmland.

Post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction in areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities shall respect the wishes of the local people.

Article 71 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the earthquake-stricken area shall organize relevant departments and units to rescue, protect, collect, and organize relevant archives and materials, and promptly supplement and restore archives and materials lost or damaged due to the earthquake disaster.

Article 72 Post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction shall adhere to the principle of combining government leadership, social participation, and market operation.

Local people's governments at all levels in the earthquake-stricken area shall organize affected people and enterprises to carry out production self-rescue, rely on their own efforts, work hard, practice thrift, and resume production as soon as possible.

The State provides financial support, tax incentives, and financial assistance for post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction, and provides material, technical, and human resources support.

Article 73 Local people's governments at all levels in the earthquake-stricken area shall organize and do a good job in relief, treatment, rehabilitation, compensation, consolation, pension, resettlement, psychological assistance, legal services, and public cultural services.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall do a good job in the employment of affected people, and encourage enterprises and institutions to give priority to hiring eligible affected people.

Article 74 For matters requiring administrative examination and approval procedures in post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction, the people's government with the approval authority and its relevant departments shall handle them promptly and legally in accordance with the principles of convenience for the masses, simplification of procedures, and improvement of efficiency.

## Chapter 7: Supervision and Management

Article 75 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen supervision and inspection of the formulation and implementation of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction plans and earthquake emergency plans, the setting and management of earthquake emergency shelters, the training of earthquake disaster emergency rescue teams, the publicity and education of earthquake prevention and disaster reduction knowledge, and earthquake emergency rescue drills.

The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the quality and safety of materials for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction.

Article 76 The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level, such as construction, transportation, railways, water conservancy, electric power, and earthquake, shall, according to their division of responsibilities, strengthen the supervision and inspection of the implementation of mandatory standards for engineering construction, seismic fortification requirements, and the work of seismic safety evaluation.

Article 77 It is prohibited to embezzle, intercept, or misappropriate funds and materials for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction.

The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen the management and supervision of the use of funds, materials, and social donations for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction, make them public, register the collection, allocation, appropriation, and use of funds and materials, and establish and improve archives.

Article 78 Local people's governments in the earthquake-stricken area shall regularly publish the sources, quantities, distribution, and use of funds, materials, and social donations for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction, and accept social supervision.

Article 79 Audit institutions shall strengthen the audit of the collection, allocation, appropriation, and use of funds and materials for earthquake emergency rescue, post-earthquake transitional resettlement, and recovery and reconstruction, and promptly publish the audit results.

Article 80 Supervisory organs shall strengthen the supervision of state administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws and regulations to manage public affairs, and their staff, who participate in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction work.

Article 81 Any unit or individual has the right to report illegal activities in earthquake prevention and disaster reduction activities.

The people's government or relevant department that receives the report shall investigate, handle it according to law, and maintain confidentiality for the whistleblower.

## Chapter 8: Legal Liability

Article 82 If the earthquake work department of the State Council, the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the local people's government at or above the county level, or other departments exercising supervision and management powers in accordance with this Law, fail to make administrative licenses or handle approval documents according to law, fail to investigate and deal with illegal activities discovered or reported, or have other acts of failing to perform their duties in accordance with this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 83 If the construction of an earthquake monitoring network is not carried out in accordance with laws, regulations, and relevant national standards, the earthquake work department of the State Council or the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections and take corresponding remedial measures; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 84 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the earthquake work department of the State Council or the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the local people's government at or above the county level to stop the illegal act, restore the original state, or take other remedial measures; if losses are caused, they shall bear compensation liability according to law:

(1) Encroaching upon, damaging, dismantling, or moving earthquake monitoring facilities without authorization;(2) Endangering the earthquake observation environment;
(3) Destroying typical earthquake sites or relics.

If a unit commits the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph, and the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if an individual commits the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph, and the circumstances are serious, a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a penalty according to law.

Article 85 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to add anti-interference facilities or build new earthquake monitoring facilities as required shall be ordered by the earthquake work department of the State Council or the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a time limit; if correction is not made within the time limit, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if losses are caused, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

Article 86 If a foreign organization or individual, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in earthquake monitoring activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China or other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China without approval, the earthquake work department of the State Council shall order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate the monitoring results and monitoring facilities, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a concurrent fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If a foreigner commits the acts specified in the preceding paragraph, in addition to being punished according to the preceding paragraph, the period of their stay in the People's Republic of China shall be shortened or their residence qualification in the People's Republic of China shall be revoked in accordance with the laws on the administration of entry and exit of foreigners; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be ordered to leave the country within a time limit or be deported.Article 87: Failure to conduct a seismic safety evaluation in accordance with the law, or failure to implement seismic fortification requirements as determined by the seismic safety evaluation report, shall be ordered by the competent department of earthquake affairs under the State Council or the department or institution responsible for earthquake management under the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a specified time limit; if corrections are not made within the time limit, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 88: Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, disseminates earthquake prediction opinions, earthquake forecast opinions, or their review results to the public, or disrupts social order during transitional resettlement after an earthquake or post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction, thereby constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be punished by the public security organ in accordance with the law.

Article 89: If the local people's government at or above the county level in an earthquake-stricken area delays reporting, falsely reports, or conceals information such as earthquake conditions and disaster situations, the higher-level people's government shall order corrections; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 90: Anyone who embezzles, intercepts, or misappropriates funds or materials for earthquake emergency rescue, transitional resettlement after an earthquake, or post-earthquake recovery and reconstruction shall be ordered by the financial department or audit authority, within their respective scope of duties, to make corrections and recover the embezzled, intercepted, or misappropriated funds or materials; any illegal gains shall be confiscated; the unit shall be given a warning or a notice of criticism; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 91: Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 92: The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Earthquake monitoring facilities" refer to equipment, instruments, and devices used for the detection, transmission, and processing of earthquake information, as well as the supporting monitoring sites.

(2) "Earthquake observation environment" refers to the spatial scope delineated in accordance with relevant national standards to ensure that earthquake monitoring facilities are free from interference and can function normally.

(3) "Major construction projects" refer to projects of significant value or substantial impact to society.

(4) "Construction projects that may cause serious secondary disasters" refer to projects that, after being damaged by an earthquake, may trigger floods, fires, explosions, or large-scale leakage of highly toxic, highly corrosive, or radioactive substances, as well as other serious secondary disasters. These include reservoir dams, oil and gas storage facilities, facilities storing flammable, explosive, highly toxic, highly corrosive, or radioactive substances, and other construction projects that may cause serious secondary disasters.

(5) "Seismic intensity zoning map" refers to a map that divides the country into different areas with varying seismic fortification requirements, using seismic intensity (the degree of earthquake impact expressed in grades) as an indicator.

(6) "Ground motion parameter zoning map" refers to a map that divides the country into different areas with varying seismic fortification requirements, using ground motion parameters (the degree of earthquake action expressed in terms of acceleration) as an indicator.

(7) "Seismic microzonation map" refers to a map that provides a detailed division of seismic fortification requirements for a specific area based on the local site conditions.

Article 93: This Law shall come into effect on May 1, 2009.
