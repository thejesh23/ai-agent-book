# Nuclear Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is formulated for the purposes of ensuring nuclear safety, preventing and responding to nuclear accidents, safely utilizing nuclear energy, protecting the safety and health of the public and practitioners, preserving the ecological environment, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

**Article 2** This Law applies to activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction that involve taking adequate preventive, protective, mitigation, and regulatory safety measures for nuclear facilities, nuclear materials, and related radioactive waste, preventing nuclear accidents caused by technical reasons, human factors, or natural disasters, and minimizing the radioactive consequences in the event of a nuclear accident.

Nuclear facilities refer to:

(1) Nuclear power plants, nuclear heating plants, nuclear steam and heat supply plants, and other nuclear power installations and devices;
(2) Research reactors, experimental reactors, critical assemblies, and other reactors other than nuclear power installations;
(3) Nuclear fuel cycle facilities such as nuclear fuel production, processing, storage, and reprocessing facilities;
(4) Radioactive waste treatment, storage, and disposal facilities.

Nuclear materials refer to:

(1) Uranium-235 materials and products thereof;
(2) Uranium-233 materials and products thereof;
(3) Plutonium-239 materials and products thereof;
(4) Other nuclear materials requiring control as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Radioactive waste refers to waste that contains or is contaminated with radionuclides, with a concentration or specific activity greater than the national clearance level, generated from the operation and decommissioning of nuclear facilities, and which is no longer intended for use.

**Article 3** The State adheres to a rational, coordinated, and progressive nuclear safety philosophy, strengthens nuclear safety capacity building, and ensures the healthy development of the nuclear industry.

**Article 4** Entities engaged in the nuclear industry must follow the policy of ensuring safety.

Nuclear safety work must adhere to the principles of safety first, prevention foremost, clear responsibilities, strict management, defense in depth, independent regulation, and comprehensive safeguards.

**Article 5** Nuclear facility operating entities bear full responsibility for nuclear safety.

Entities providing equipment, engineering, and services to nuclear facility operating entities shall bear corresponding responsibilities.

**Article 6** The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council is responsible for the supervision and administration of nuclear safety.

The competent department of the nuclear industry, the competent department of energy, and other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for the relevant nuclear safety management within their respective scopes of duties.
The State establishes a coordination mechanism for nuclear safety work to coordinate relevant departments in advancing related work.
**Article 7** The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulates the national nuclear safety plan, which is implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

**Article 8** The State adheres to establishing a nuclear safety standard system with high and strict requirements.

The relevant departments of the State Council formulate nuclear safety standards according to their respective responsibilities. Nuclear safety standards are mandatory standards.

Nuclear safety standards shall be revised in a timely manner in accordance with economic and social development and technological progress.

**Article 9** The State formulates nuclear safety policies and strengthens the construction of nuclear safety culture.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority, the competent nuclear industry department, and the competent energy department of the State Council shall establish mechanisms for cultivating a nuclear safety culture.
Nuclear facility operating entities and entities providing them with equipment, engineering, and services shall actively cultivate and build a nuclear safety culture, integrating it into all aspects of production, operation, scientific research, and management.

**Article 10** The State encourages and supports the research, development, and utilization of science and technology related to nuclear safety, strengthens the protection of intellectual property rights, and focuses on the cultivation of nuclear safety personnel.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall arrange special research projects on key technologies related to the safety of nuclear facilities, nuclear materials, and radiation environment monitoring and assessment in relevant scientific research plans, and promote advanced and reliable nuclear safety technologies.

Nuclear facility operating entities, entities providing them with equipment, engineering, and services, and scientific research institutions related to nuclear safety shall continuously develop advanced and reliable nuclear safety technologies, fully utilize advanced scientific and technological achievements, and improve the level of nuclear safety.

The State Council, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and their relevant departments shall commend and reward units and individuals that have made significant contributions to scientific and technological innovation in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 11** No unit or individual may endanger the safety of nuclear facilities or nuclear materials.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations have the right to obtain nuclear safety information in accordance with the law, and those who suffer nuclear damage have the right to obtain compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 12** The State strengthens the security protection of nuclear facilities and nuclear materials.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall establish and improve security protection systems and take security protection measures to prevent sabotage, damage, and theft of nuclear facilities and nuclear materials.

**Article 13** The State organizes international exchanges and cooperation related to nuclear safety, improves the international cooperation mechanism for nuclear safety, prevents and responds to threats of nuclear terrorism, and fulfills the obligations stipulated in international conventions concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter II Safety of Nuclear Facilities

**Article 14** The State conducts overall planning, scientific justification, and rational layout for the siting and construction of nuclear facilities.
The State implements classified management of nuclear facilities based on factors such as their nature and risk level.

**Article 15** Nuclear facility operating entities shall possess the capability to ensure the safe operation of nuclear facilities and meet the following conditions:

(1) Having an organizational management system and systems for quality assurance, safety management, and post responsibility that meet nuclear safety requirements;
(2) Having a specified number of qualified professional technical personnel and management personnel;
(3) Having safety assessment, resource allocation, and financial capabilities commensurate with nuclear facility safety;
(4) Having the necessary technical support for nuclear safety and the capability for continuous improvement;
(5) Having emergency response capabilities and financial guarantee capabilities for nuclear damage compensation;
(6) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 16** Nuclear facility operating entities shall, in accordance with the requirements of laws, administrative regulations, and standards, establish a defense-in-depth system for nuclear facilities to effectively prevent threats caused by technical reasons, human factors, and natural disasters, and ensure the safety of nuclear facilities.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall conduct periodic safety assessments of nuclear facilities and accept review by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 17** Nuclear facility operating entities and entities providing them with equipment, engineering, and services shall establish and implement a quality assurance system to effectively ensure the quality of equipment, engineering, and services, ensuring that equipment performance meets nuclear safety standard requirements and that engineering and services meet relevant nuclear safety requirements.

**Article 18** Nuclear facility operating entities shall strictly control radiation exposure, ensure that relevant personnel are not exposed to radiation exceeding the dose limits prescribed by the State, and ensure that radiation exposure is kept as low as reasonably achievable.

**Article 19** Nuclear facility operating entities shall monitor the types and concentrations of radionuclides in the environment surrounding nuclear facilities and the total amount of radionuclides in effluents from nuclear facilities, and regularly report the monitoring results to the environmental protection competent department of the State Council and the environmental protection competent department of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.
**Article 20** Nuclear facility operating entities shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, formulate training plans, provide nuclear safety education and skill training to practitioners, and conduct assessments.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall provide corresponding labor protection and occupational health examinations for practitioners to ensure their safety and health.

**Article 21** The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall protect the sites of important nuclear facilities such as nuclear power plants determined by the national plan, and shall not change the use of the sites during the planning period.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall delineate planning restricted zones around important nuclear facilities such as nuclear power plants, which shall be implemented after obtaining the consent of the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council.

It is prohibited to construct production and storage facilities for flammable, explosive, or corrosive materials that may threaten the safety of nuclear facilities, as well as densely populated places, within the planning restricted zones.

**Article 22** The State establishes a nuclear facility safety licensing system.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall apply to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for a license for activities such as siting, construction, operation, and decommissioning of nuclear facilities.

If a nuclear facility operating entity requests a change to the conditions specified in the license document, it shall report to and obtain approval from the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 23** Nuclear facility operating entities shall conduct scientific assessments of factors such as geology, earthquakes, meteorology, hydrology, environment, and population distribution, and submit a nuclear facility siting safety analysis report to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, provided that the requirements for nuclear safety technical evaluation are met. After review confirming compliance with nuclear safety requirements, they shall obtain a nuclear facility site selection review opinion.

**Article 24** The design of nuclear facilities shall comply with nuclear safety standards, adopt scientifically reasonable parameters and technical requirements for structures, systems, and components, provide diverse protection and multiple barriers, ensure reliable, stable, and easy operation of nuclear facilities, and meet nuclear safety requirements.

**Article 25** Before the construction of a nuclear facility, the nuclear facility operating entity shall submit a construction application to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, along with the following materials:

(1) A nuclear facility construction application form;
(2) A preliminary safety analysis report;
(3) An environmental impact assessment document;
(4) Quality assurance documents;
(5) Other materials stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 26** After obtaining a nuclear facility construction license, the nuclear facility operating entity shall ensure that the overall performance of the nuclear facility meets the requirements of nuclear safety standards.

The validity period of a nuclear facility construction license shall not exceed ten years. If an extension of the construction period is needed upon expiration, it shall be reported to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for review and approval. However, this does not apply in the following circumstances, provided that no safety risk is assessed to exist:

(1) National policies or actions cause a delay in the construction of the nuclear facility;
(2) Nuclear facilities used for scientific research;
(3) Nuclear facilities used for engineering demonstration;
(4) Nuclear facilities used for spent fuel reprocessing.

After the construction of a nuclear facility is completed, commissioning shall be carried out to verify whether it meets the designed nuclear safety requirements.

**Article 27** Before the initial loading of fuel or materials into a nuclear facility, the nuclear facility operating entity shall submit an operation application to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, along with the following materials:

(1) A nuclear facility operation application form;
(2) A final safety analysis report;
(3) Quality assurance documents;
(4) An emergency plan;
(5) Other materials stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

After obtaining a nuclear facility operation license, the nuclear facility operating entity shall operate in accordance with the provisions of the license.

The validity period of a nuclear facility operation license is the design lifetime. During the validity period, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council may make reasonable adjustments to the matters specified in the license in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and new nuclear safety standard requirements.

If a nuclear facility operating entity adjusts the following matters, it shall report to and obtain approval from the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council:

(1) Important structures, systems, and components that served as the basis for issuing the operation license;
(2) Operating limits and conditions;
(3) Procedures and other documents related to nuclear safety approved by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 28** If a nuclear facility operation license expires and continued operation is required, the nuclear facility operating entity shall submit an application for extension to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council five years before the expiration date, and shall demonstrate and verify its compliance with nuclear safety standards. Continued operation is permitted only after review and approval.

**Article 29** After a nuclear facility ceases operation, the nuclear facility operating entity shall carry out shutdown management in a safe manner, ensure safety during the shutdown period, and guarantee the basic functions, technical personnel, and documents required for decommissioning.

**Article 30** Before the decommissioning of a nuclear facility, the nuclear facility operating entity shall submit a decommissioning application to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, along with the following materials:

(1) A nuclear facility decommissioning application form;
(2) A safety analysis report;
(3) An environmental impact assessment document;
(4) Quality assurance documents;
(5) Other materials stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

During the decommissioning of a nuclear facility, the nuclear facility operating entity shall treat and dispose of radioactive materials at the nuclear facility site in accordance with the principle of as low as reasonably achievable, reducing the radioactivity levels of structures, systems, and components to meet standard requirements.

After the decommissioning of a nuclear facility, the competent department of environmental protection of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located shall organize monitoring of the types and concentrations of radionuclides in the nuclear facility site and its surrounding environment.
**Article 31** The import of nuclear facilities shall meet the requirements of the relevant nuclear safety laws, administrative regulations, and standards of the People's Republic of China, and shall be reported to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for review and approval.

The export of nuclear facilities shall comply with the provisions of the People's Republic of China on the export control of nuclear facilities.

**Article 32** The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures, organize safety technical reviews of nuclear facility safety license applications. If nuclear safety requirements are met, a decision to grant the license shall be made within twenty days from the date of completion of the technical review.

When reviewing applications for nuclear facility construction and operation licenses, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall seek opinions from the relevant departments of the State Council and the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located. The consulted units shall provide a response within three months.

**Article 33** When organizing safety technical reviews, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall entrust a technical support unit that has no interest relationship with the license applicant to conduct the technical assessment. The entrusted technical support unit shall be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of its technical evaluation conclusions.

**Article 34** The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council establishes a nuclear safety expert committee to provide advisory opinions for nuclear safety decision-making.

When formulating nuclear safety plans and standards and making technical decisions on major nuclear facility safety issues, the opinions of the nuclear safety expert committee shall be consulted.

**Article 35** The State establishes a nuclear safety reporting system for nuclear facility operating entities, with specific measures formulated by the relevant departments of the State Council.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall establish a nuclear safety experience feedback system, promptly process nuclear safety report information, and achieve information sharing.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall establish a nuclear safety experience feedback system.

**Article 36** Units providing design, manufacturing, installation, and non-destructive testing services for nuclear safety equipment for nuclear facilities shall apply to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for a license. Overseas institutions providing design, manufacturing, installation, and non-destructive testing services for nuclear safety equipment for domestic nuclear facilities shall apply to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for registration.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall conduct safety inspections on imported nuclear safety equipment in accordance with the law.

**Article 37** Nuclear facility operators and special process personnel such as nuclear safety equipment welders and non-destructive testing personnel shall obtain corresponding qualification certificates in accordance with national regulations.

Nuclear facility operating entities and units engaged in the manufacture, installation, and non-destructive testing of nuclear safety equipment shall employ personnel who have obtained corresponding qualification certificates to engage in work related to the professional technology of nuclear facility safety.

## Chapter III Safety of Nuclear Materials and Radioactive Waste

**Article 38** Nuclear facility operating entities and other relevant units that possess nuclear materials shall obtain a license in accordance with prescribed conditions and the law, and take the following measures to prevent the theft, sabotage, loss, illegal transfer, and use of nuclear materials, and ensure the safety and lawful use of nuclear materials:

(1) Establishing a dedicated organization or designating personnel to keep nuclear materials;
(2) Establishing a nuclear material accounting system to maintain a balance of nuclear materials receipts and issues;
(3) Establishing a physical protection system commensurate with the protection level of nuclear materials;
(4) Establishing an information confidentiality system and taking confidentiality measures;
(5) Other measures stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 39** Units that generate, store, transport, or reprocess spent fuel shall take measures to ensure the safety of spent fuel and bear nuclear safety responsibility for the spent fuel in their possession.

**Article 40** Radioactive waste shall be subject to classified disposal.

Low and intermediate level radioactive waste shall be disposed of in near-surface or intermediate depth facilities at sites designated by the State that meet nuclear safety requirements.

High level radioactive waste shall be disposed of in deep geological repositories, operated exclusively by units designated by the State Council.

**Article 41** Nuclear facility operating entities and radioactive waste treatment and disposal units shall carry out minimization and harmless treatment and disposal of radioactive waste to ensure permanent safety.
**Article 42** The nuclear industry competent department of the State Council, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulates siting plans for low and intermediate level radioactive waste disposal facilities, which are implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.
The competent department of the State Council for the nuclear industry, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulates siting plans for high-level radioactive waste disposal facilities, which are implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.
The construction of radioactive waste disposal facilities shall be compatible with the requirements of nuclear energy development.

**Article 43** The State establishes a radioactive waste management licensing system.

Units specializing in the treatment, storage, and disposal of radioactive waste shall apply to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for a license.

Nuclear facility operating entities that use treatment and storage facilities built in conjunction with nuclear facilities to treat and store radioactive waste generated by themselves do not need to apply for a license.

**Article 44** Nuclear facility operating entities shall treat the radioactive solid waste and radioactive liquid waste that cannot be purified and discharged generated by themselves, converting them into stable, standardized solid waste, and then promptly deliver them to radioactive waste disposal units for disposal.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall treat the radioactive waste gas generated by themselves before discharging it, ensuring it meets the national standards for radioactive pollution prevention and control.

**Article 45** Radioactive waste disposal units shall dispose of the radioactive waste they receive in accordance with the requirements of national radioactive pollution prevention and control standards.

Radioactive waste disposal units shall establish records of radioactive waste disposal, truthfully recording the source, quantity, characteristics, storage location, and other matters related to the disposal activities of the disposed radioactive waste. The records shall be kept permanently.

**Article 46** The State establishes a closure system for radioactive waste disposal facilities.

A radioactive waste disposal facility shall undergo closure procedures in accordance with the law and have permanent markers placed in the delineated area under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The design service life has expired;
(2) The disposed radioactive waste has reached the design capacity;
(3) Significant changes have occurred in the geological structure or hydrogeological conditions of the area, making it unsuitable for continued disposal of radioactive waste;
(4) Other circumstances requiring closure as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.Article 47 Before the closure of a radioactive waste disposal facility, the radioactive waste disposal unit shall prepare a safety monitoring plan for the closure of the facility and submit it to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council for approval.

The safety monitoring plan shall include the following main contents:

(1) The person responsible for safety monitoring and their responsibilities;

(2) The costs of safety monitoring;

(3) The measures for safety monitoring;

(4) The duration of safety monitoring.

After the closure of a radioactive waste disposal facility, the radioactive waste disposal unit shall carry out safety monitoring in accordance with the approved safety monitoring plan; after approval by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, the responsibility for monitoring and management shall be transferred to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 48 Nuclear facility operating entities shall pay fees for the treatment and disposal of spent fuel in accordance with state regulations, and these fees shall be included in production costs.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall make provisions for decommissioning costs of nuclear facilities and disposal costs of radioactive waste, which shall be included in investment estimates and production costs, and used exclusively for the decommissioning of nuclear facilities and the disposal of radioactive waste. Specific measures shall be formulated by the finance department and the competent pricing department of the State Council in conjunction with the nuclear safety regulatory authority, the nuclear industry competent department, and the energy competent department of the State Council.
Article 49 The state implements classified management for the transport of nuclear materials and radioactive waste, and takes effective measures to ensure transport safety.

Article 50 The state ensures the transport of nuclear materials and radioactive waste by road, railway, waterway, etc. The relevant departments of the State Council shall strengthen the management of such transport and formulate specific safeguard measures.

Article 51 The competent department of the State Council for the nuclear industry is responsible for coordinating the management activities of spent fuel transport and supervising relevant confidentiality measures.
Public security organs supervise the physical protection of nuclear materials and radioactive waste during road transport, and handle accidents that may endanger the safe transport of nuclear materials and radioactive waste in accordance with the law. For the road transport of nuclear materials or radioactive waste, approval shall be obtained from the public security organ of the people's government at or above the county level in the place of departure according to the prescribed authority; among these, the transport of spent fuel or high-level radioactive waste shall be approved by the public security department of the State Council.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council is responsible for approving license applications for transport packaging containers for nuclear materials and radioactive waste.

Article 52 The consignor of nuclear materials or radioactive waste shall take effective radiation protection and security measures during transport, and be responsible for nuclear safety during transport.

The consignor of spent fuel or high-level radioactive waste shall submit a relevant nuclear safety analysis report to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, and transport activities may only be carried out after review and approval.

The carrier of nuclear materials or radioactive waste shall obtain the transport qualifications prescribed by the state in accordance with the law.

Article 53 For the transport of nuclear materials or radioactive waste by road, railway, waterway, etc., where this Law does not provide provisions, the provisions of relevant laws, administrative regulations, and rules concerning the transport of radioactive items and dangerous goods shall apply.

## Chapter IV Nuclear Accident Emergency

Article 54 The state establishes a nuclear accident emergency coordination committee to organize and coordinate the national nuclear accident emergency management work.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may establish nuclear accident emergency coordination committees based on actual needs to organize and coordinate the nuclear accident emergency management work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 55 The competent department of nuclear industry under the State Council shall be responsible for the daily work of the National Nuclear Accident Emergency Coordination Committee, take the lead in formulating the national nuclear accident emergency plan, and organize its implementation after approval by the State Council. Member units of the National Nuclear Accident Emergency Coordination Committee shall formulate their own nuclear accident emergency plans based on the deployment of the national nuclear accident emergency plan, and report them to the competent department of nuclear industry under the State Council for record.
The department designated by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government undertakes the daily work of the nuclear accident emergency coordination committee, is responsible for formulating the off-site nuclear accident emergency plan within its administrative region, and organizes its implementation after approval by the national nuclear accident emergency coordination committee.

Nuclear facility operating entities are responsible for formulating their own on-site nuclear accident emergency plans, and report them to the nuclear industry competent department and energy competent department of the State Council, as well as the department designated by the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for record.
The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, formulate their own emergency work plans for supporting local nuclear accident response, and report them to the competent department of nuclear industry under the State Council for record.
The unit formulating the emergency plan shall revise the plan in a timely manner based on actual needs and changes in circumstances.

Article 56 Nuclear facility operating entities shall, in accordance with the emergency plan, equip emergency equipment, conduct training and drills for emergency staff, and make emergency preparations.

The department designated by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located shall carry out activities to popularize knowledge about nuclear accident emergencies, and organize relevant enterprises, institutions, and communities to conduct nuclear accident emergency drills in accordance with the emergency plan.

Article 57 The state establishes a nuclear accident emergency reserve fund system to ensure the funds needed for nuclear accident emergency preparedness and response work. The measures for the management of the nuclear accident emergency reserve fund shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 58 The state implements graded management for nuclear accident emergencies.

When a nuclear accident occurs, the nuclear facility operating entity shall carry out emergency response in accordance with the requirements of the emergency plan to mitigate the consequences of the accident, and immediately report the status of the nuclear facility to the competent nuclear industry department and nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council, as well as the department designated by the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and propose suggestions for off-site emergency response actions as needed.
Article 59 The national nuclear accident emergency coordination committee, in accordance with the deployment of the national nuclear accident emergency plan, organizes and coordinates the relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments, and nuclear facility operating entities to carry out nuclear accident emergency rescue work.

The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, carry out nuclear accident emergency rescue work.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall provide emergency response support in accordance with the requirements of nuclear accident emergency rescue work.

Article 60 The competent department of the nuclear industry under the State Council or the department designated by the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government is responsible for releasing nuclear accident emergency information.
The national nuclear accident emergency coordination committee coordinates international notification and international rescue efforts for nuclear accidents.

Article 61 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, nuclear facility operating entities, etc., shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions and authorizations of the State Council, organize and carry out post-nuclear accident recovery actions, loss assessment, and other work.

The investigation and handling of nuclear accidents shall be carried out by the State Council or its authorized departments.

The investigation and handling of off-site emergency actions for nuclear accidents shall be carried out by the State Council or its designated agency.

Article 62 The emergency response for the transport of nuclear materials and radioactive waste shall be incorporated into the off-site nuclear accident emergency plan or radiation emergency plan of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government through which the transport passes. When a nuclear accident occurs, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the accident occurs shall be responsible for the emergency response.

## Chapter V Information Disclosure and Public Participation

Article 63 The relevant departments of the State Council and the departments designated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where nuclear facilities are located shall, within their respective responsibilities, disclose nuclear safety-related information in accordance with the law.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, disclose information related to nuclear safety administrative licenses, as well as safety inspection and supervision reports on nuclear safety-related activities, overall safety status, radiation environmental quality, and nuclear accidents.

The State Council shall regularly report on the nuclear safety situation to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 64 Nuclear facility operating entities shall disclose information such as their nuclear safety management systems and related documents, the safety status of nuclear facilities, effluent and surrounding environmental radiation monitoring data, and annual nuclear safety reports. Specific measures shall be formulated by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 65 Nuclear safety information that is disclosed in accordance with the law shall be promptly made public through government announcements, websites, and other means convenient for the public to access.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations may, in accordance with the law, apply to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and the department designated by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located to obtain nuclear safety-related information.

Article 66 Nuclear facility operating entities shall, regarding major nuclear safety matters involving public interests, solicit opinions from interested parties through questionnaires, hearings, demonstration meetings, symposiums, or other forms, and provide feedback in an appropriate manner.

The people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located shall, regarding major nuclear safety matters affecting public interests, hold hearings, demonstration meetings, symposiums, or adopt other forms to solicit opinions from interested parties, and provide feedback in an appropriate manner.

Article 67 Nuclear facility operating entities shall take the following measures to carry out nuclear safety publicity activities:

(1) Open nuclear facilities to the public in an orderly manner, provided the safety of the nuclear facilities is ensured;

(2) Cooperate with schools to carry out nuclear safety knowledge education activities for students;

(3) Build nuclear safety publicity venues, and print and distribute nuclear safety publicity materials;

(4) Other measures as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 68 Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations have the right to report to the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments any acts that pose nuclear safety hazards or violate nuclear safety laws and administrative regulations.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations shall not fabricate or disseminate false nuclear safety information.

Article 69 The disclosure of government information involving state secrets, trade secrets, and personal information shall be carried out in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 70 The state establishes a nuclear safety supervision and inspection system.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments shall supervise and inspect the compliance of units engaged in nuclear safety activities with nuclear safety laws, administrative regulations, rules, and standards.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council may establish dispatched agencies in areas where nuclear facilities are concentrated. The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or its dispatched agencies shall dispatch supervision and inspection personnel to sites such as nuclear facility construction, operation, and decommissioning to carry out nuclear safety supervision and inspection.

Article 71 The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments shall strengthen the capacity building for nuclear safety regulation and improve the level of nuclear safety regulation.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall organize and carry out research and development of nuclear safety regulatory technologies, and maintain a technical evaluation capability commensurate with nuclear safety supervision and management.

Article 72 When conducting nuclear safety supervision and inspection, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments have the right to take the following measures:

(1) Enter the site for monitoring, inspection, or verification;

(2) Access relevant documents, materials, and records;

(3) Investigate and understand the situation from relevant personnel;

(4) If problems are found, require rectification on the spot.

The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments shall form reports on the supervision and inspection situation and establish archives.

Article 73 Units engaged in nuclear safety activities shall cooperate with the supervision and inspection conducted by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments in accordance with the law, truthfully explain the situation, provide necessary materials, and shall not refuse or obstruct.

Article 74 Nuclear safety supervision and inspection personnel shall be loyal to their duties, diligent and responsible, and enforce the law impartially.

Nuclear safety supervision and inspection personnel shall possess the professional knowledge and business capabilities corresponding to the supervision and inspection activities, and receive regular training.

When performing supervision and inspection tasks, nuclear safety supervision and inspection personnel shall present valid credentials, and shall keep confidential any state secrets, trade secrets, and personal information they obtain in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 75 In violation of the provisions of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments fail to review and approve license applications in accordance with the law;

(2) The relevant departments of the State Council or the departments designated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government where nuclear facilities are located fail to disclose nuclear safety-related information in accordance with the law;

(3) The people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the nuclear facility is located fails to solicit opinions from interested parties on major nuclear safety matters affecting public interests;

(4) The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments fail to form reports on the supervision and inspection situation, or fail to establish archives;

(5) Nuclear safety supervision and inspection personnel, when performing supervision and inspection tasks, fail to present valid credentials, or fail to keep confidential state secrets, trade secrets, or personal information obtained in accordance with the law;

(6) The nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments, or the relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, engage in other acts of abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or favoritism and fraud.

Article 76 In violation of the provisions of this Law, endangering the safety of nuclear facilities or nuclear materials, or fabricating or disseminating false nuclear safety information, constituting a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 77 In violation of the provisions of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments shall order correction and give a warning; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan shall be imposed; if correction is refused, construction shall be ordered to stop or production shall be suspended for rectification:

(1) The nuclear facility operating entity fails to establish a defense-in-depth system for the nuclear facility;

(2) The nuclear facility operating entity or the unit providing equipment, engineering, and services for it fails to establish or implement a quality assurance system;

(3) The nuclear facility operating entity fails to control radiation exposure doses as required;

(4) The nuclear facility operating entity fails to establish a nuclear safety experience feedback system;

(5) The nuclear facility operating entity fails to solicit opinions from interested parties on major nuclear safety matters involving public interests.

Article 78 In violation of the provisions of this Law, constructing facilities for the production or storage of flammable, explosive, or corrosive items that may threaten the safety of a nuclear facility, or constructing densely populated places within a planned restricted area, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order the removal within a specified period and restoration to the original state, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 79 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a nuclear facility operating entity falls under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 1 million yuan but not more than 5 million yuan; if correction is refused, construction shall be ordered to stop or production shall be suspended for rectification; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if environmental pollution is caused, the entity shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified period to eliminate the pollution; if measures are not taken within the specified period, a capable unit shall be designated to perform the measures on its behalf, with the costs borne by the polluter; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan:

(1) Engaging in activities such as construction, operation, or decommissioning of a nuclear facility without a license;

(2) Changing the conditions specified in the license document without permission;

(3) Continuing to operate a nuclear facility after the validity period of the nuclear facility operating license has expired, without review and approval;

(4) Importing a nuclear facility without review and approval.

Article 80 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a nuclear facility operating entity falls under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and give a warning; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan shall be imposed; if environmental pollution is caused, the entity shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified period to eliminate the pollution; if measures are not taken within the specified period, a capable unit shall be designated to perform the measures on its behalf, with the costs borne by the polluter:

(1) Failing to conduct periodic safety assessments of the nuclear facility, or refusing to accept review by the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council;

(2) After the termination of operation of a nuclear facility, failing to take safe measures for shutdown management, or failing to ensure the basic functions, technical personnel, and documents required for decommissioning;

(3) During the decommissioning of a nuclear facility, failing to reduce the radioactivity levels of structures, systems, or components to meet the required standards;

(4) Failing to convert the generated radioactive solid waste or radioactive liquid waste that cannot be purified and discharged into stable, standardized solid waste, and failing to promptly deliver it to a radioactive waste disposal unit for disposal;

(5) Failing to treat the generated radioactive gas, or discharging it without meeting the national radioactive pollution prevention and control standards.

Article 81 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a nuclear facility operating entity fails to monitor the types and concentrations of radionuclides in the environment surrounding the nuclear facility or the total amount of radionuclides in the effluent of the nuclear facility, or fails to report monitoring results in accordance with regulations, the environmental protection department of the State Council or the environmental protection department of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 82 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if an entrusted technical support unit issues a false technical evaluation conclusion, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 83 In violation of the provisions of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan:

(1) Providing nuclear safety equipment design, manufacturing, installation, or non-destructive testing services for a nuclear facility without a license;

(2) An overseas institution providing nuclear safety equipment design, manufacturing, installation, or non-destructive testing services for a domestic nuclear facility without registration.

Article 84 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a nuclear facility operating entity or a nuclear safety equipment manufacturing, installation, or non-destructive testing unit employs personnel who have not obtained the corresponding qualification certificates to engage in work related to nuclear facility safety professional technology, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if correction is refused, the license shall be suspended or revoked, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 85 In violation of the provisions of this Law, possessing nuclear materials without a license, the competent department of nuclear industry under the State Council shall confiscate the illegally possessed nuclear materials and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated.
Article 86 In violation of the provisions of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan shall be imposed; if environmental pollution is caused, the entity shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified period to eliminate the pollution; if measures are not taken within the specified period, a capable unit shall be designated to perform the measures on its behalf, with the costs borne by the polluter:(1) Engaging in the treatment, storage, or disposal of radioactive waste without a license;

(2) Failing to establish records of radioactive waste disposal, failing to truthfully record matters related to disposal activities, or failing to permanently preserve such records;

(3) Failing to go through the formalities for closure of a radioactive waste disposal facility that should be closed in accordance with the law;

(4) Closing a radioactive waste disposal facility without setting up permanent markers in the designated area;

(5) Failing to prepare a safety monitoring plan for the closure of a radioactive waste disposal facility;

(6) Failing to conduct safety monitoring in accordance with the approved safety monitoring plan after the closure of a radioactive waste disposal facility.

Article 87 Where a nuclear facility operating entity, in violation of this Law, falls under any of the following circumstances, the nuclear safety supervision and administration department of the State Council shall order it to make corrections and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to formulate an on-site nuclear accident emergency plan as required;

(2) Failing to equip emergency equipment in accordance with the emergency plan, or failing to conduct training or drills for emergency response personnel;

(3) Failing to provide emergency response support as required by nuclear accident emergency rescue work.

Article 88 Where a nuclear facility operating entity, in violation of this Law, fails to disclose relevant information as required, the nuclear safety supervision and administration department of the State Council shall order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 89 Where a unit engaged in nuclear safety activities, in violation of this Law, refuses or obstructs the supervision and inspection lawfully conducted by the nuclear safety supervision and administration department of the State Council or other relevant departments, the nuclear safety supervision and administration department of the State Council or other relevant departments shall order it to make corrections and may impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if it refuses to make corrections, its license shall be suspended or revoked; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 90 Where a nuclear accident causes personal injury or death, property loss, or environmental damage to others, the nuclear facility operating entity shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the national nuclear damage liability system, unless it can prove that the damage was caused by war, armed conflict, riot, or similar circumstances.

Units providing equipment, engineering, and services to nuclear facility operating entities shall not bear nuclear damage compensation liability. If the nuclear facility operating entity has an agreement with such units, it may seek recourse in accordance with the agreement after bearing compensation liability.

Nuclear facility operating entities shall make appropriate financial guarantee arrangements, such as purchasing liability insurance or participating in mutual assistance mechanisms, to ensure timely and effective fulfillment of nuclear damage compensation liability.

Article 91 Where a violation of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 92 Nuclear safety for national defense and military purposes shall be separately stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with the principles of this Law.

Article 93 The meanings of the following terms in this Law:

A nuclear accident refers to an accident or series of accidents involving radioactivity, toxicity, explosiveness, or other hazards caused by nuclear fuel, radioactive products, radioactive waste, or nuclear materials transported into or out of a nuclear facility within the facility.

Defense in depth refers to preventing nuclear accidents and mitigating their consequences by establishing a series of progressive and independent protective, mitigation measures, or physical barriers.

A nuclear facility operating entity refers to a unit that applies for or holds a nuclear facility safety license and is authorized to operate and run a nuclear facility within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Nuclear safety equipment refers to equipment used in nuclear facilities to perform nuclear safety functions, including nuclear safety mechanical equipment and nuclear safety electrical equipment.

Spent fuel refers to nuclear fuel that has been irradiated in a reactor core and permanently removed from the core.

Shutdown refers to the state where a nuclear facility has ceased operation and will not be restarted.

Decommissioning refers to measures such as decontamination, dismantling, and removal to ensure that the radiation dose of sites or equipment no longer in use at a nuclear facility meets the requirements of relevant national standards.

Experience feedback refers to the collection, screening, evaluation, analysis, processing, and distribution of information on events, quality issues, and good practices at nuclear facilities, summarizing and promoting good practices to prevent the recurrence of similar events and issues.

A consignor refers to a unit that applies for the transport of consigned goods and obtains approval within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 94 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2018.
