# Law of the People's Republic of China on Prevention and Control of Radioactive Pollution

Adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on June 28, 2003

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and controlling radioactive pollution, protecting the environment, safeguarding human health, and promoting the development and peaceful use of nuclear energy and nuclear technology.

**Article 2** This Law applies to activities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution occurring in the process of site selection, construction, operation, and decommissioning of nuclear facilities, and in the development and utilization of nuclear technology, uranium (thorium) mines, and associated radioactive mines within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction.

**Article 3** The State applies the principles of prevention first, combining prevention with control, strict management, and safety first to the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

**Article 4** The State encourages and supports scientific research and technological development and utilization for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution, and promotes advanced technologies for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

The State supports international exchanges and cooperation in the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

**Article 5** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the work of preventing and controlling radioactive pollution into their environmental protection plans.

People's governments at or above the county level shall organize targeted publicity and education on the prevention and control of radioactive pollution, enabling the public to understand the relevant circumstances and scientific knowledge concerning the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

**Article 6** Any unit or individual has the right to report and accuse any act that causes radioactive pollution.

**Article 7** Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions in the work of preventing and controlling radioactive pollution shall be rewarded by the people's governments at or above the county level.

**Article 8** The administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the work of preventing and controlling radioactive pollution nationwide in accordance with the law.

The administrative department of health under the State Council and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with the responsibilities prescribed by the State Council, exercise supervision and management over the relevant work of preventing and controlling radioactive pollution in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Supervision and Management of the Prevention and Control of Radioactive Pollution

**Article 9** The national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall be formulated by the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council based on environmental safety requirements and the national economic and technological conditions. The national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall be jointly published by the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council and the administrative department of standardization under the State Council.

**Article 10** The State establishes a monitoring system for radioactive pollution. The administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments under the State Council, organize an environmental monitoring network to implement monitoring and management of radioactive pollution.

**Article 11** The administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council shall, according to their respective division of responsibilities, each assume their own responsibilities, exchange information, and cooperate closely to supervise and inspect the prevention and control of radioactive pollution in the development and utilization of nuclear facilities and uranium (thorium) mines.

The administrative departments of environmental protection of local people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments at the same level shall, according to their respective division of responsibilities, each assume their own responsibilities, exchange information, and cooperate closely to supervise and inspect the prevention and control of radioactive pollution in the utilization of nuclear technology and the development and utilization of associated radioactive mines within their respective administrative regions.

When conducting on-site inspections, inspectors shall present their credentials. The unit being inspected must truthfully report the situation and provide necessary information. Inspectors shall keep confidential the technical secrets and business secrets of the unit being inspected. When inspecting units and locations involving State secrets, they shall comply with the State regulations on the protection of State secrets and go through the relevant approval procedures in accordance with the law.

**Article 12** The operating units of nuclear facilities, units utilizing nuclear technology, and units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines shall be responsible for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution in their own units, accept the supervision and management of the administrative department of environmental protection and other relevant departments, and bear responsibility for the radioactive pollution they cause in accordance with the law.

**Article 13** The operating units of nuclear facilities, units utilizing nuclear technology, and units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines must take safety and protection measures to prevent the occurrence of various accidents that may lead to radioactive pollution and to avoid the hazards of radioactive pollution.

The operating units of nuclear facilities, units utilizing nuclear technology, and units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines shall provide their staff with education and training on radiation safety and take effective protective safety measures.

**Article 14** The State implements a qualification management system for professionals engaged in the prevention and control of radioactive pollution; and implements a qualification management system for institutions engaged in the monitoring of radioactive pollution.

**Article 15** When transporting radioactive substances and radiation devices containing radiation sources, effective measures shall be taken to prevent radioactive pollution. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 16** Radioactive substances and radiation devices shall be provided with conspicuous radioactive signs and warning instructions in Chinese. Places where radioactive substances and radiation devices are produced, sold, used, stored, or disposed of, as well as tools for transporting radioactive substances and radiation devices containing radiation sources, shall be provided with conspicuous radioactive signs.

**Article 17** Products containing radioactive substances shall comply with the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution; those that do not meet the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall not be shipped out of the factory or sold.

The use of slag from associated radioactive mines and stone materials containing natural radioactive substances as building and decoration materials shall comply with the national standards for the control of radionuclides in building materials.

## Chapter III Prevention and Control of Radioactive Pollution from Nuclear Facilities

**Article 18** The site selection for nuclear facilities shall undergo scientific demonstration and go through the approval procedures in accordance with the relevant State regulations. Before going through the approval procedures for the site selection of a nuclear facility, an environmental impact report shall be prepared and submitted to the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council for review and approval; without approval, the relevant departments shall not issue the site selection approval documents for the nuclear facility.
**Article 19** Before carrying out activities such as construction, fuel loading, operation, and decommissioning of a nuclear facility, the operating unit of the nuclear facility must, in accordance with the State Council's regulations on the safety supervision and management of nuclear facilities, apply for and obtain a nuclear facility construction or operation license and go through the approval procedures for fuel loading, decommissioning, etc.

The operating unit of a nuclear facility may carry out the corresponding activities of construction, fuel loading, operation, and decommissioning only after obtaining the relevant license or approval document.

**Article 20** The operating unit of a nuclear facility shall prepare an environmental impact report before applying for a nuclear facility construction or operation license and before going through the decommissioning approval procedures, and submit it to the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council for review and approval; without approval, the relevant departments shall not issue the license or approval document.

**Article 21** Facilities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution supporting a nuclear facility shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

The facilities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall be accepted simultaneously with the main project; only after the acceptance inspection is passed can the main project be put into production or use.

**Article 22** Imported nuclear facilities shall comply with the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution; if there are no corresponding national standards, the relevant foreign standards designated by the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council shall be adopted.

**Article 23** Planned restricted zones shall be delineated in the peripheral areas of important nuclear facilities such as nuclear power plants. The measures for the delineation and management of planned restricted zones shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 24** The operating unit of a nuclear facility shall monitor the types and concentrations of radionuclides contained in the environment surrounding the nuclear facility and the total amount of radionuclides in the effluents from the nuclear facility, and shall regularly report the monitoring results to the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council and the administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the facility is located.

The administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council is responsible for implementing supervisory monitoring of important nuclear facilities such as nuclear power plants, and shall, as needed, implement monitoring of the effluents from other nuclear facilities. The costs for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the supervisory monitoring system shall be arranged from the fiscal budget.

**Article 25** The operating unit of a nuclear facility shall establish and improve a security system, strengthen security work, and accept the supervision and guidance of the public security department.

The operating unit of a nuclear facility shall, based on the scale and nature of the nuclear facility, formulate an on-site emergency plan for nuclear accidents and make preparations for emergency response.

When a nuclear accident emergency situation occurs, the operating unit of the nuclear facility must immediately take effective emergency measures to control the accident and report to the competent department of the nuclear facility, the administrative department of environmental protection, the administrative department of health, the public security department, and other relevant departments.

**Article 26** The State establishes and improves the nuclear accident emergency system.

The competent department of the nuclear facility, the administrative department of environmental protection, the administrative department of health, the public security department, and other relevant departments shall, under the organization and leadership of the people's government at the corresponding level, do a good job in nuclear accident emergency response in accordance with their respective responsibilities and the law.

The Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall provide effective support in nuclear accident emergency response in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

**Article 27** The operating unit of a nuclear facility shall formulate a decommissioning plan for the nuclear facility.

The costs for decommissioning the nuclear facility and disposing of radioactive waste shall be set aside in advance and included in the investment estimate or production cost. The measures for the setting aside and management of the costs for decommissioning nuclear facilities and disposing of radioactive waste shall be formulated by the fiscal department and the competent pricing department under the State Council, together with the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council and the competent department of the nuclear facility.

## Chapter IV Prevention and Control of Radioactive Pollution from the Utilization of Nuclear Technology

**Article 28** Units that produce, sell, or use radioisotopes and radiation devices shall, in accordance with the State Council's regulations on radiation protection for radioisotopes and radiation devices, apply for a license and go through registration procedures.

Units that transfer or import radioisotopes and radiation devices, and units equipped with instruments containing radioisotopes, shall go through the relevant procedures in accordance with the State Council's regulations on radiation protection for radioisotopes and radiation devices.

**Article 29** Units that produce, sell, or use radioisotopes, accelerators, neutron generators, and radiation devices containing radiation sources shall prepare an environmental impact assessment document before applying for a license, and submit it to the administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for review and approval; without approval, the relevant departments shall not issue the license.

The State establishes a filing system for radioisotopes. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 30** Radiation protection facilities for newly built, renovated, or expanded radiation workplaces shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

The radiation protection facilities shall be accepted simultaneously with the main project; only after the acceptance inspection is passed can the main project be put into production or use.

**Article 31** Radioisotopes shall be stored separately and shall not be stored together with flammable, explosive, corrosive, or other such items. Their storage places shall adopt effective safety protection measures against fire, theft, and radiation leakage, and a designated person shall be assigned for safekeeping. When storing, collecting, using, or returning radioisotopes, registration and inspection shall be carried out to ensure that the accounts match the actual items.

**Article 32** Units that produce or use radioisotopes and radiation devices shall collect, package, and store the radioactive waste they generate in accordance with the regulations of the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council.

Units that produce radiation sources shall recycle and utilize used radiation sources in accordance with the regulations of the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council; units that use radiation sources shall, in accordance with the regulations of the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council, return used radiation sources to the unit that produced them or deliver them to a unit specialized in the storage and disposal of solid radioactive waste.

**Article 33** Units that produce, sell, use, or store radiation sources shall establish and improve a security system, designate a specific person responsible, implement a safety responsibility system, and formulate necessary accident emergency measures. When a radiation source is lost, stolen, or a radioactive pollution accident occurs, the relevant unit or individual must immediately take emergency measures and report to the public security department, the administrative department of health, and the administrative department of environmental protection.

After receiving a report of a lost or stolen radiation source or a radioactive pollution accident, the public security department, the administrative department of health, and the administrative department of environmental protection shall report to the people's government at the corresponding level and immediately organize the taking of effective measures according to their respective responsibilities to prevent the spread of radioactive pollution and reduce accident losses. The local people's government shall promptly inform the public of the relevant situation and do a good job in the investigation and handling of the accident.

## Chapter V Prevention and Control of Radioactive Pollution from the Development and Utilization of Uranium (Thorium) Mines and Associated Radioactive Mines

**Article 34** Units that develop, utilize, or close uranium (thorium) mines shall prepare an environmental impact report before applying for a mining license or going through the decommissioning approval procedures, and submit it to the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council for review and approval.

Units that develop and utilize associated radioactive mines shall prepare an environmental impact report before applying for a mining license, and submit it to the administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government at or above the provincial level for review and approval.

**Article 35** Facilities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution supporting construction projects for the development and utilization of uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

The facilities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall be accepted simultaneously with the main project; only after the acceptance inspection is passed can the main project be put into production or use.

**Article 36** Units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines shall monitor the effluents from the uranium (thorium) mines and the surrounding environment, and shall regularly report the monitoring results to the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council and the administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the mine is located.

**Article 37** For tailings generated during the development and utilization of uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines, tailings ponds shall be constructed for storage and disposal; the constructed tailings ponds shall meet the requirements for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

**Article 38** Units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines shall formulate decommissioning plans for the uranium (thorium) mines. The costs for decommissioning uranium mines shall be arranged from the State fiscal budget.

## Chapter VI Management of Radioactive Waste

**Article 39** The operating units of nuclear facilities, units utilizing nuclear technology, and units developing and utilizing uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines shall rationally select and utilize raw materials, adopt advanced production processes and equipment, and minimize the generation of radioactive waste.

**Article 40** The discharge of radioactive waste gas and waste liquid into the environment must comply with the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution.

**Article 41** Units that generate radioactive waste gas or waste liquid and discharge it into the environment in compliance with the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution shall apply to the administrative department of environmental protection that approved the environmental impact assessment document for the discharge amount of radionuclides, and shall regularly report the discharge measurement results.

**Article 42** Units that generate radioactive waste liquid must, in accordance with the requirements of the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution, treat or store radioactive waste liquid that is not permitted to be discharged into the environment.

Units that generate radioactive waste liquid and discharge it into the environment in compliance with the national standards for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution must adopt discharge methods that comply with the regulations of the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council.

It is prohibited to discharge radioactive waste liquid using seepage wells, seepage pits, natural fissures, karst caves, or other methods prohibited by the State.

**Article 43** Low and intermediate level solid radioactive waste shall be disposed of in near-surface disposal facilities in areas designated by the State.

High level solid radioactive waste shall be disposed of by centralized deep geological disposal.

Alpha-bearing solid radioactive waste shall be disposed of in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

It is prohibited to dispose of solid radioactive waste in inland waters and the sea.

**Article 44** The competent department of nuclear facilities under the State Council shall, together with the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council, formulate a siting plan for solid radioactive waste disposal facilities based on geological conditions and the needs for solid radioactive waste disposal, and submit it to the State Council for approval before implementation.

The relevant local people's governments shall, based on the siting plan for solid radioactive waste disposal facilities, provide construction land for solid radioactive waste disposal facilities and take effective measures to support the disposal of solid radioactive waste.

**Article 45** Units that generate solid radioactive waste shall, in accordance with the regulations of the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council, treat the solid radioactive waste they generate and then deliver it to a solid radioactive waste disposal unit for disposal, and bear the disposal costs.

The measures for the collection and use management of fees for the disposal of solid radioactive waste shall be formulated by the fiscal department and the competent pricing department under the State Council, together with the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council.

**Article 46** The establishment of a unit specialized in the storage and disposal of solid radioactive waste must be reviewed and approved by the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council, and a license must be obtained. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

It is prohibited to engage in the storage and disposal of solid radioactive waste without a license or not in accordance with the relevant provisions of the license.

It is prohibited to provide or entrust solid radioactive waste to a unit without a license for storage and disposal.

**Article 47** It is prohibited to import radioactive waste and items contaminated by radioactive substances into the territory of the People's Republic of China or to transfer them through the territory of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

**Article 48** If a supervisor or manager of the prevention and control of radioactive pollution, in violation of the provisions of this Law, takes advantage of his position to accept property from others, seek other benefits, or neglects his duties, and commits any of the following acts, he shall be given an administrative sanction in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Issuing licenses and approval documents to units that do not meet the statutory conditions;

(2) Failing to perform supervision and management duties in accordance with the law;

(3) Failing to investigate and deal with illegal acts discovered.

**Article 49** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the administrative department of environmental protection of the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments, within their respective functions, to make corrections within a specified time limit, and may be fined not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to report relevant environmental monitoring results in accordance with regulations;

(2) Refusing on-site inspections by the administrative department of environmental protection and other relevant departments, or failing to truthfully report the situation and provide necessary information when inspected.

**Article 50** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, carries out activities such as construction, operation, production, or use without preparing an environmental impact assessment document, or without the environmental impact assessment document being approved by the administrative department of environmental protection, shall be ordered by the administrative department of environmental protection that approved the environmental impact assessment document to stop the illegal act, go through the formalities retroactively or restore the original state within a specified time limit, and be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 51** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, puts the main project into production or use without constructing facilities for the prevention and control of radioactive pollution or radiation protection facilities, or without the prevention and control facilities having passed the acceptance inspection, shall be ordered by the administrative department of environmental protection that approved the environmental impact assessment document to stop the illegal act, make corrections within a specified time limit, and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 52** If an operating unit of a nuclear facility, in violation of the provisions of this Law, without a license or approval, carries out activities such as construction, fuel loading, operation, or decommissioning of the nuclear facility, it shall be ordered by the administrative department of environmental protection under the State Council to stop the illegal act, make corrections within a specified time limit, and be fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.Article 53 Any violation of this Law by producing, selling, using, transferring, importing, or storing radioisotopes, radiation-emitting devices, or instruments equipped with radioisotopes shall be ordered by the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments, within their respective functions, to cease the illegal act and rectify within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, production or business operations shall be ordered to cease, or the license shall be revoked; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 100,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 54 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, rectify within a specified time limit, and impose a fine; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Failing to construct a tailings pond or constructing a tailings pond not in accordance with the requirements for radioactive pollution prevention, to store or dispose of tailings from uranium (thorium) mines and associated radioactive mines;

(2) Discharging radioactive waste gas or waste liquid into the environment that is prohibited from being discharged;

(3) Discharging radioactive waste liquid not in accordance with the prescribed methods, or using seepage wells, seepage pits, natural fissures, karst caves, or other methods prohibited by the state to discharge radioactive waste liquid;

(4) Failing to treat or store radioactive waste liquid that is prohibited from being discharged into the environment in accordance with regulations;

(5) Providing or entrusting radioactive solid waste to a unit without a license for storage and disposal.

For any act under item (1), (2), (3), or (5) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; for any act under item (4) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 55 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments, within their respective functions, to rectify within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, production or business operations shall be ordered to cease, and a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Failing to set up radioactive identification signs, markings, or Chinese warning instructions in accordance with regulations;

(2) Failing to establish and improve security and protection systems and formulate accident emergency plans or measures in accordance with regulations;

(3) Failing to report the loss or theft of radioactive sources or radioactive pollution accidents in accordance with regulations.

Article 56 If a unit generating radioactive solid waste fails to dispose of its radioactive solid waste in accordance with Article 45 of this Law, the competent administrative department of environmental protection that approved the environmental impact assessment document for the unit's project establishment shall order it to cease the illegal act and rectify within a specified time limit; if rectification is not made within the time limit, a unit with disposal capability shall be designated to dispose of the waste on its behalf, with the costs borne by the unit generating the radioactive solid waste, and a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan may also be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 57 Any violation of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent administrative department of environmental protection under the people's government at or above the provincial level to cease production or business operations or revoke the license; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 100,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Engaging in the storage and disposal of radioactive solid waste without a license;

(2) Engaging in the storage and disposal of radioactive solid waste not in accordance with the relevant provisions of the license.

Article 58 If radioactive waste or items contaminated by radioactivity are imported into the territory of the People's Republic of China, or transferred through the territory of the People's Republic of China, the customs shall order the return of such radioactive waste and contaminated items, and impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 59 If damage is caused to others due to radioactive pollution, civil liability shall be borne according to law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 60 The supervision and management of radioactive pollution prevention for military facilities and equipment shall be implemented by the relevant competent departments of the State Council and the military in accordance with the principles of this Law and the responsibilities stipulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 61 The prevention and treatment of occupational diseases caused by workers' exposure to radioactive substances during occupational activities shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Occupational Diseases.

Article 62 The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) Radioactive pollution refers to the appearance of radioactive substances or radiation exceeding national standards on the surface or inside materials, human bodies, places, or environmental media due to human activities.

(2) Nuclear facilities refer to nuclear power plants (nuclear power plants, nuclear thermal power plants, nuclear steam supply and heating plants, etc.) and other reactors (research reactors, experimental reactors, critical assemblies, etc.); nuclear fuel production, processing, storage, and reprocessing facilities; radioactive waste treatment and disposal facilities, etc.

(3) Nuclear technology utilization refers to the use of sealed radioactive sources, unsealed radioactive sources, and radiation-emitting devices in fields such as medical treatment, industry, agriculture, geological surveys, scientific research, and teaching.

(4) Radioisotopes refer to nuclides of an element undergoing radioactive decay that have the same atomic number but different masses.

(5) Radioactive sources refer to radioactive materials permanently sealed in containers or with a tight cladding and in solid form, excluding materials within the nuclear fuel cycle of research reactors and power reactors.

(6) Radiation-emitting devices refer to X-ray machines, accelerators, neutron generators, and devices containing radioactive sources.

(7) Associated radioactive mines refer to non-uranium mines (such as rare earth mines and phosphate mines) containing high levels of natural radionuclides.

(8) Radioactive waste refers to waste containing radionuclides or contaminated by radionuclides, with a concentration or specific activity greater than the national clearance level, and no longer intended for use.

Article 63 This Law shall come into force as of October 1, 2003.
