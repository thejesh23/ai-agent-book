# Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 27, 1994

Revised at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

First Amendment on October 26, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws Including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 29, 2021, according to the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

Effective as of September 1, 2015

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate advertising activities, protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, promote the healthy development of the advertising industry, and maintain the socio-economic order.

Article 2 This Law applies to commercial advertising activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China, where commodity operators or service providers, through certain media and forms, directly or indirectly introduce the commodities they sell or the services they provide.

For the purposes of this Law, an "advertiser" means a natural person, legal person, or other organization that, for the purpose of selling commodities or providing services, designs, produces, and publishes advertisements by itself or entrusts others to do so.

For the purposes of this Law, an "advertising operator" means a natural person, legal person, or other organization that accepts entrustment to provide advertising design, production, and agency services.

For the purposes of this Law, an "advertising publisher" means a natural person, legal person, or other organization that publishes advertisements for an advertiser or an advertising operator entrusted by an advertiser.

For the purposes of this Law, an "advertising endorser" means a natural person, legal person, or other organization, other than the advertiser, that recommends or certifies commodities or services in advertisements in its own name or image.

Article 3 Advertisements shall be truthful and lawful, and shall express their content in a healthy manner, conforming to the requirements of building a socialist spiritual civilization and promoting the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation.

Article 4 Advertisements shall not contain false or misleading content, and shall not deceive or mislead consumers.

Advertisers shall be responsible for the truthfulness of the advertising content.

Article 5 Advertisers, advertising operators, and advertising publishers shall, in their advertising activities, abide by laws and regulations, act in good faith, and engage in fair competition.

Article 6 The market supervision and administration department of the State Council shall be in charge of the supervision and administration of advertising nationwide. The relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for the relevant advertising administration work within their respective functions and duties.

The local market supervision and administration departments at or above the county level shall be in charge of the supervision and administration of advertising within their respective administrative regions. The relevant departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the relevant advertising administration work within their respective functions and duties.

Article 7 Advertising industry organizations shall, in accordance with laws, regulations, and their articles of association, formulate industry norms, strengthen industry self-discipline, promote industry development, guide members to engage in advertising activities in accordance with the law, and promote the building of integrity in the advertising industry.

## Chapter II: Standards for Advertising Content

Article 8 Where an advertisement makes representations regarding the performance, function, origin, purpose, quality, ingredients, price, producer, shelf life, promises, etc., of a commodity, or regarding the content, provider, form, quality, price, promises, etc., of a service, such representations shall be accurate, clear, and explicit.

Where an advertisement indicates that the commodity or service being promoted is accompanied by a free gift, the variety, specification, quantity, term, and method of the accompanying commodity or service shall be clearly stated.

Where laws or administrative regulations require that certain content be explicitly stated in an advertisement, such content shall be prominently and clearly indicated.

Article 9 Advertisements shall not contain any of the following:

(1) The use or disguised use of the national flag, national anthem, or national emblem of the People's Republic of China, or the military flag, military anthem, or military emblem;
(2) The use or disguised use of the name or image of state organs or state functionaries;
(3) The use of terms such as "national level," "highest level," or "best";
(4) Impairing the dignity or interests of the state, or divulging state secrets;
(5) Jeopardizing social stability or harming the public interest;
(6) Endangering personal or property safety, or disclosing personal privacy;
(7) Jeopardizing public order or violating good social customs;
(8) Containing content that is obscene, pornographic, gambling-related, superstitious, terrifying, or violent;
(9) Containing content that discriminates on the basis of ethnicity, race, religion, or gender;
(10) Jeopardizing the protection of the environment, natural resources, or cultural heritage;
(11) Other circumstances prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 10 Advertisements shall not harm the physical or mental health of minors or persons with disabilities.

Article 11 Where the content of an advertisement involves matters requiring administrative licensing, it shall conform to the licensed content.

Where an advertisement uses data, statistical information, survey results, excerpts, quotations, or other cited content, such content shall be truthful and accurate, and the source shall be indicated. Where the cited content has a scope of application or a period of validity, this shall be clearly stated.

Article 12 Where an advertisement involves a patented product or patented method, the patent number and patent type shall be indicated.

No one shall falsely claim to have obtained a patent right in an advertisement if no patent right has been granted.

The use of patent applications for which no patent right has been granted, or patents that have been terminated, revoked, or invalidated, in advertisements is prohibited.

Article 13 Advertisements shall not disparage the commodities or services of other producers or business operators.

Article 14 Advertisements shall be identifiable as such, enabling consumers to distinguish them as advertisements.

Mass media shall not publish advertisements in a disguised form as news reports. Advertisements published through mass media shall be prominently marked as "advertisement" to distinguish them from other non-advertising information, and shall not cause consumer misunderstanding.

Radio stations and television stations publishing advertisements shall comply with the regulations of the relevant departments of the State Council regarding duration and methods, and shall provide a clear indication of the advertisement duration.

Article 15 Advertisements for special drugs such as narcotic drugs, psychotropic substances, medicinal toxic drugs, and radioactive drugs, as well as pharmaceutical precursor chemicals, and drugs, medical devices, and treatment methods for drug rehabilitation, are prohibited.

Prescription drugs other than those specified in the preceding paragraph may only be advertised in medical and pharmaceutical professional publications jointly designated by the health administrative department of the State Council and the drug regulatory department of the State Council.

Article 16 Advertisements for medical treatment, drugs, and medical devices shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Assertions or guarantees of efficacy or safety;
(2) Statements of cure rates or effectiveness rates;
(3) Comparisons with the efficacy or safety of other drugs or medical devices, or with other medical institutions;
(4) The use of advertising endorsers for recommendations or certifications;
(5) Other content prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

The content of drug advertisements shall not be inconsistent with the instructions approved by the drug regulatory department of the State Council, and contraindications and adverse reactions shall be prominently indicated. Advertisements for prescription drugs shall prominently state "This advertisement is for medical and pharmaceutical professionals only," and advertisements for non-prescription drugs shall prominently state "Please purchase and use according to the drug instructions or under the guidance of a pharmacist."

Advertisements for medical devices recommended for personal use shall prominently state "Please read the product instructions carefully or purchase and use under the guidance of medical personnel." Where the medical device product registration certificate contains contraindications or precautions, the advertisement shall prominently state "See the instructions for contraindications or precautions."

Article 17 Except for advertisements for medical treatment, drugs, and medical devices, no other advertisements shall involve disease treatment functions, nor shall they use medical terminology or terms that are likely to confuse the promoted commodity with drugs or medical devices.

Article 18 Advertisements for health foods shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Assertions or guarantees of efficacy or safety;
(2) Involvement in disease prevention or treatment functions;
(3) Claims or implications that the advertised product is essential for maintaining health;
(4) Comparisons with drugs or other health foods;
(5) The use of advertising endorsers for recommendations or certifications;
(6) Other content prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Advertisements for health foods shall prominently state "This product cannot replace drugs."

Article 19 Radio stations, television stations, newspapers, audio-visual publishing units, and internet information service providers shall not publish advertisements for medical treatment, drugs, medical devices, or health foods in a disguised form, such as in the guise of introducing health or wellness knowledge.

Article 20 It is prohibited to publish advertisements in mass media or public places for infant milk products, beverages, and other foods that claim to completely or partially replace breast milk.

Article 21 Advertisements for pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed, and feed additives shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Assertions or guarantees of efficacy or safety;
(2) The use of the name or image of scientific research units, academic institutions, technology promotion agencies, industry associations, professionals, or users for recommendations or certifications;
(3) Statements of effectiveness rates;
(4) Text, language, or images that violate safe usage procedures;
(5) Other content prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 22 It is prohibited to publish tobacco advertisements in mass media, public places, public transportation, or outdoors. It is prohibited to send any form of tobacco advertisements to minors.

The use of advertisements for other commodities or services, or public service advertisements, to promote the name, trademark, packaging, or decoration of tobacco products, or similar content, is prohibited.

Notices published by tobacco product producers or sellers regarding relocation, name changes, recruitment, etc., shall not contain the name, trademark, packaging, or decoration of tobacco products, or similar content.

Article 23 Advertisements for alcoholic beverages shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Inducing or encouraging drinking, or promoting excessive drinking;
(2) Depicting the act of drinking;
(3) Depicting activities such as driving vehicles, boats, or aircraft;
(4) Expressly or impliedly suggesting that drinking has effects such as relieving tension and anxiety or increasing physical strength.

Article 24 Advertisements for education or training shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Express or implied guaranteed promises regarding advancement to higher levels of education, passing examinations, obtaining degrees, diplomas, or certificates of qualification, or the effectiveness of the education or training;
(2) Express or implied indications that relevant examination bodies or their staff, or examination question setters, are involved in the education or training;
(3) The use of the name or image of scientific research units, academic institutions, educational institutions, industry associations, professionals, or beneficiaries for recommendations or certifications.

Article 25 Advertisements for commodities or services such as investment promotion that involve expectations of investment returns shall contain reasonable reminders or warnings of possible risks and liability for risk, and shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Guaranteed promises regarding future results, returns, or related circumstances, expressly or impliedly indicating guaranteed principal, no risk, or guaranteed returns, except as otherwise provided by the state;
(2) The use of the name or image of academic institutions, industry associations, professionals, or beneficiaries for recommendations or certifications.

Article 26 In real estate advertisements, property information shall be truthful, the area shall be indicated as the construction area or the usable floor area, and the advertisement shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Promises of appreciation or investment returns;
(2) Indicating the location of the project by the time required to reach a specific reference point;
(3) Violating state regulations on price management;
(4) Misleading publicity regarding planned or under-construction transportation, commercial, cultural, educational, or other municipal facilities.

Article 27 Advertisements for crop seeds, forest tree seeds, grass seeds, breeding livestock and poultry, aquatic fry and fingerlings, and for breeding and cultivation, shall present truthful, clear, and explicit statements regarding variety names, production performance, growth amount or yield, quality, resistance, special use value, economic value, suitable scope and conditions for planting or breeding, etc., and shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Assertions that cannot be scientifically verified;
(2) Assertions or guarantees of efficacy;
(3) Analysis, predictions, or guaranteed promises regarding economic benefits;
(4) The use of the name or image of scientific research units, academic institutions, technology promotion agencies, industry associations, professionals, or users for recommendations or certifications.

Article 28 An advertisement that deceives or misleads consumers with false or misleading content constitutes a false advertisement.

An advertisement falls under any of the following circumstances and is a false advertisement:

(1) The commodity or service does not exist;
(2) Information such as the performance, function, origin, purpose, quality, specifications, ingredients, price, producer, shelf life, sales status, or honors received of the commodity, or the content, provider, form, quality, price, sales status, or honors received of the service, or promises related to the commodity or service, is inconsistent with the actual situation and has a material impact on purchasing behavior;
(3) Using fabricated, forged, or unverifiable scientific research results, statistical information, survey results, excerpts, quotations, or other information as supporting materials;
(4) Fabricating the effects of using a commodity or receiving a service;
(5) Other circumstances where consumers are deceived or misled by false or misleading content.

## Chapter III: Standards for Advertising Conduct

Article 29 Radio stations, television stations, and newspaper publishing units engaged in advertising publishing business shall establish specialized advertising business institutions, equip them with necessary personnel, and have premises and equipment suitable for publishing advertisements.

Article 30 Advertisers, advertising operators, and advertising publishers shall enter into written contracts in accordance with the law in their advertising activities.

Article 31 Advertisers, advertising operators, and advertising publishers shall not engage in any form of unfair competition in advertising activities.

Article 32 When an advertiser entrusts the design, production, or publication of an advertisement, it shall entrust an advertising operator or advertising publisher with lawful business qualifications.

Article 33 An advertiser or advertising operator using another person's name or image in an advertisement shall obtain the prior written consent of that person; using the name or image of a person with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct shall require the prior written consent of that person's guardian.

Article 34 Advertising operators and advertising publishers shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, establish and improve systems for the registration, review, and archiving of advertising business.

Advertising operators and advertising publishers shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, examine relevant certification documents and verify the content of advertisements. For advertisements with inconsistent content or incomplete certification documents, advertising operators shall not provide design, production, or agency services, and advertising publishers shall not publish them.

Article 35 Advertising operators and advertising publishers shall make public their fee standards and fee collection methods.

Article 36 Information such as coverage rates, viewership rates, click-through rates, and circulation figures provided by advertising publishers to advertisers and advertising operators shall be truthful.

Article 37 No unit or individual may design, produce, act as an agent for, or publish advertisements for products that are prohibited from being produced or sold, or services that are prohibited from being provided, or commodities or services that are prohibited from being advertised, under laws or administrative regulations.

Article 38 An advertising endorser recommending or certifying commodities or services in an advertisement shall do so based on facts, in compliance with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, and shall not recommend or certify commodities they have not used or services they have not received.

Minors under the age of ten shall not be used as advertising endorsers.

Natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations that have received administrative penalties for making recommendations or certifications in false advertisements within the last three years shall not be used as advertising endorsers.

Article 39 Advertising activities shall not be conducted in primary or secondary schools or kindergartens. The use of textbooks, supplementary teaching materials, exercise books, stationery, teaching aids, school uniforms, school buses, etc., of primary and secondary school students and preschool children to publish or disguisedly publish advertisements is prohibited, except for public service advertisements.

Article 40 Advertisements for medical treatment, drugs, health foods, medical devices, cosmetics, alcoholic beverages, or beauty services, as well as online game advertisements that are detrimental to the physical and mental health of minors, shall not be published in mass media targeting minors.

Advertisements for commodities or services targeting minors under the age of fourteen shall not contain any of the following:

(1) Inducing them to ask their parents to purchase the advertised commodity or service;
(2) Content that may cause them to imitate unsafe behaviors.

Article 41 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to strengthen the supervision and administration of outdoor advertisements published using outdoor spaces, facilities, etc., and formulate plans for the placement of outdoor advertisements and safety requirements.

Measures for the administration of outdoor advertisements shall be stipulated by local regulations or local government rules.

Article 42 Outdoor advertisements shall not be placed under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Using traffic safety facilities or traffic signs;
(2) Affecting the use of municipal public facilities, traffic safety facilities, traffic signs, fire-fighting facilities, or fire safety signs;
(3) Hindering production or people's lives, or damaging the appearance of cities or towns;
(4) Being placed within the building control zones of state organs, protected cultural relics sites, scenic spots, etc., or in areas where the local people's government at or above the county level prohibits the placement of outdoor advertisements.

Article 43 No unit or individual shall send advertisements to another person's residence, means of transport, etc., or send advertisements by electronic means, without the consent or request of that person.

Where advertisements are sent by electronic means, the sender's true identity and contact information shall be clearly stated, and the recipient shall be provided with a method to refuse further receipt.

Article 44 The provisions of this Law apply to advertising activities conducted via the internet.

The publication or sending of advertisements via the internet shall not affect the normal use of the network by users. Advertisements published on internet pages in the form of pop-ups, etc., shall prominently display a close mark to ensure one-click closure.

Article 45 Managers of public places, telecommunications business operators, and internet information service providers shall stop illegal advertisements that they know or should know are being sent or published using their premises or information transmission or publishing platforms.

## Chapter IV: Supervision and Administration

Article 46 Advertisements for medical treatment, drugs, medical devices, pesticides, veterinary drugs, and health foods, as well as other advertisements that require review under laws or administrative regulations, shall be reviewed by the relevant departments (hereinafter referred to as the advertising review authorities) before publication; they shall not be published without review.

Article 47 An advertiser applying for advertising review shall submit relevant certification documents to the advertising review authority in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

The advertising review authority shall make a review decision in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, and shall send a copy of the review approval document to the market supervision and administration department at the same level. The advertising review authority shall promptly make the approved advertisements public.

Article 48 No unit or individual may forge, alter, or transfer advertising review approval documents.**Article 49** The market supervision and administration department, in performing its advertising supervision and administration duties, may exercise the following powers:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of premises suspected of engaging in illegal advertising activities;

(2) Question parties suspected of violating the law, or their legal representatives, principal responsible persons, and other relevant persons, and investigate relevant entities or individuals;

(3) Require parties suspected of violating the law to provide relevant supporting documents within a specified time limit;

(4) Inspect and copy contracts, invoices, account books, advertising works, and other relevant materials related to suspected illegal advertising;

(5) Seal up or seize advertising materials, business tools, equipment, and other property directly related to suspected illegal advertising;

(6) Order the suspension of the publication of suspected illegal advertisements that may cause serious consequences;

(7) Other powers provided by laws and administrative regulations.

The market supervision and administration department shall establish and improve an advertising monitoring system, refine monitoring measures, and promptly detect and legally investigate illegal advertising activities.

**Article 50** The market supervision and administration department of the State Council, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, shall formulate rules for the publication of advertisements by mass media.

**Article 51** When the market supervision and administration department exercises its powers in accordance with this Law, the parties concerned shall assist and cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct.

**Article 52** The market supervision and administration department, relevant departments, and their staff have a duty to maintain the confidentiality of trade secrets learned during advertising supervision and administration activities.

**Article 53** Any entity or individual has the right to complain or report violations of this Law to the market supervision and administration department and relevant departments. The market supervision and administration department and relevant departments shall make public the telephone numbers, mailboxes, or email addresses for receiving complaints and reports. The department receiving a complaint or report shall handle it within seven working days from the date of receipt and inform the complainant or whistleblower.

If the market supervision and administration department or relevant departments fail to perform their duties according to law, any entity or individual has the right to report this to their superior authorities or supervisory authorities. The authority receiving the report shall handle it according to law and promptly inform the whistleblower of the outcome.

Relevant departments shall maintain the confidentiality of complainants and whistleblowers.

**Article 54** Consumer associations and other consumer organizations shall conduct social supervision according to law over acts that violate this Law, such as publishing false advertisements that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, and other acts that harm the public interest.

## Chapter 5: Legal Liability

**Article 55** Anyone who violates this Law by publishing a false advertisement shall be ordered by the market supervision and administration department to stop publishing the advertisement, ordered by the advertiser to eliminate the impact within the corresponding scope, and fined not less than three times but not more than five times the advertising expenses. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed. If there are three or more violations within two years or other serious circumstances, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the advertising expenses shall be imposed. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the business license may be revoked, and the advertising review authority shall revoke the advertising review approval document and not accept the advertiser's advertising review application for one year.

If a medical institution commits the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph and the circumstances are serious, in addition to being punished by the market supervision and administration department according to this Law, the health administrative department may revoke its diagnosis and treatment subjects or its medical institution practice license.

If an advertising operator or advertising publisher knowingly or should have known that an advertisement is false but still designs, produces, acts as an agent for, or publishes it, the market supervision and administration department shall confiscate the advertising expenses and impose a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the advertising expenses. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed. If there are three or more violations within two years or other serious circumstances, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the advertising expenses shall be imposed. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the relevant department may suspend the advertising publication business or revoke the business license.

If an advertiser, advertising operator, or advertising publisher commits the acts specified in paragraphs 1 or 3 of this Article and constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 56** Anyone who violates this Law by publishing a false advertisement, deceiving or misleading consumers, thereby harming the legitimate rights and interests of consumers who purchase goods or receive services, shall bear civil liability according to law by the advertiser. If the advertising operator or advertising publisher cannot provide the true name, address, and effective contact information of the advertiser, the consumer may demand that the advertising operator or advertising publisher provide compensation first.

If a false advertisement for goods or services related to the life and health of consumers causes damage to consumers, the advertising operator, advertising publisher, and advertising endorser shall bear joint and several liability with the advertiser.

For false advertisements for goods or services other than those specified in the preceding paragraph that cause damage to consumers, if the advertising operator, advertising publisher, or advertising endorser knowingly or should have known that the advertisement was false but still designed, produced, acted as an agent for, published, or recommended or certified it, they shall bear joint and several liability with the advertiser.

**Article 57** In any of the following circumstances, the market supervision and administration department shall order the cessation of the publication of the advertisement, impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan on the advertiser. If the circumstances are serious, the business license may also be revoked, and the advertising review authority shall revoke the advertising review approval document and not accept the advertiser's advertising review application for one year. For the advertising operator or advertising publisher, the market supervision and administration department shall confiscate the advertising expenses, impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan. If the circumstances are serious, the business license may also be revoked:

(1) Publishing advertisements containing the prohibited circumstances specified in Articles 9 or 10 of this Law;

(2) Violating Article 15 of this Law by publishing advertisements for prescription drugs, pharmaceutical precursor chemicals, or medical devices and treatment methods for drug rehabilitation;

(3) Violating Article 20 of this Law by publishing advertisements for infant milk products, beverages, or other foods that claim to completely or partially replace breast milk;

(4) Violating Article 22 of this Law by publishing tobacco advertisements;

(5) Violating Article 37 of this Law by using advertisements to promote products or services whose production or sale is prohibited, or goods or services for which advertising is prohibited;

(6) Violating paragraph 1 of Article 40 of this Law by publishing advertisements for medical treatment, pharmaceuticals, health foods, medical devices, cosmetics, alcohol, or beauty services, or online game advertisements detrimental to the physical and mental health of minors, in mass media targeting minors.

**Article 58** In any of the following circumstances, the market supervision and administration department shall order the cessation of the publication of the advertisement, order the advertiser to eliminate the impact within the corresponding scope, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the advertising expenses. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the advertising expenses shall be imposed. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, the business license may be revoked, and the advertising review authority shall revoke the advertising review approval document and not accept the advertiser's advertising review application for one year:

(1) Violating Article 16 of this Law by publishing advertisements for medical treatment, pharmaceuticals, or medical devices;

(2) Violating Article 17 of this Law by involving disease treatment functions in advertisements, or using medical terminology or language that easily confuses the promoted goods with pharmaceuticals or medical devices;

(3) Violating Article 18 of this Law by publishing health food advertisements;

(4) Violating Article 21 of this Law by publishing advertisements for pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed, or feed additives;

(5) Violating Article 23 of this Law by publishing alcohol advertisements;

(6) Violating Article 24 of this Law by publishing education or training advertisements;

(7) Violating Article 25 of this Law by publishing advertisements for goods or services such as investment promotion that have expectations of investment returns;

(8) Violating Article 26 of this Law by publishing real estate advertisements;

(9) Violating Article 27 of this Law by publishing advertisements for crop seeds, forest tree seeds, grass seeds, breeding livestock and poultry, aquatic fry and fingerlings, or breeding and cultivation;

(10) Violating paragraph 2 of Article 38 of this Law by using a minor under the age of ten as an advertising endorser;

(11) Violating paragraph 3 of Article 38 of this Law by using a natural person, legal person, or other organization as an advertising endorser;

(12) Violating Article 39 of this Law by publishing advertisements in primary or secondary schools, kindergartens, or using items related to primary or secondary school students or young children;

(13) Violating paragraph 2 of Article 40 of this Law by publishing advertisements for goods or services targeting minors under the age of fourteen;

(14) Violating Article 46 of this Law by publishing advertisements without review.

If a medical institution commits the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph and the circumstances are serious, in addition to being punished by the market supervision and administration department according to this Law, the health administrative department may revoke its diagnosis and treatment subjects or its medical institution practice license.

If an advertising operator or advertising publisher knowingly or should have known about the illegal acts specified in paragraph 1 of this Article but still designs, produces, acts as an agent for, or publishes them, the market supervision and administration department shall confiscate the advertising expenses and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the advertising expenses. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the advertising expenses shall be imposed. If the advertising expenses cannot be calculated or are obviously low, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the relevant department may suspend the advertising publication business or revoke the business license.

**Article 59** In any of the following circumstances, the market supervision and administration department shall order the cessation of the publication of the advertisement and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the advertiser:

(1) The advertising content violates Article 8 of this Law;

(2) The cited content in the advertisement violates Article 11 of this Law;

(3) An advertisement involving a patent violates Article 12 of this Law;

(4) Violating Article 13 of this Law by disparaging the goods or services of other producers or operators in the advertisement.

If an advertising operator or advertising publisher knowingly or should have known about the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph but still designs, produces, acts as an agent for, or publishes them, the market supervision and administration department shall impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan.

If an advertisement violates Article 14 of this Law by lacking identifiability, or violates Article 19 of this Law by covertly publishing advertisements for medical treatment, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, or health foods, the market supervision and administration department shall order corrections and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the advertising publisher.

**Article 60** If an advertising operator or advertising publisher violates Article 34 of this Law by failing to establish or improve an advertising business management system in accordance with relevant state regulations, or failing to verify advertising content, the market supervision and administration department shall order corrections and may impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

If an advertising operator or advertising publisher violates Article 35 of this Law by failing to publish its charging standards and methods, the price authority shall order corrections and may impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

**Article 61** If an advertising endorser falls under any of the following circumstances, the market supervision and administration department shall confiscate the illegal income and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the illegal income:

(1) Violating item (4) of paragraph 1 of Article 16 of this Law by making recommendations or certifications in advertisements for medical treatment, pharmaceuticals, or medical devices;

(2) Violating item (5) of paragraph 1 of Article 18 of this Law by making recommendations or certifications in health food advertisements;

(3) Violating paragraph 1 of Article 38 of this Law by making recommendations or certifications for goods they have not used or services they have not received;

(4) Knowingly or should have known that an advertisement is false but still making recommendations or certifications for goods or services in the advertisement.

**Article 62** Anyone who violates Article 43 of this Law by sending advertisements shall be ordered by the relevant department to cease the illegal act and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan.

Anyone who violates paragraph 2 of Article 44 of this Law by using the internet to publish advertisements without prominently displaying a close mark to ensure one-click closure shall be ordered by the market supervision and administration department to correct the act and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan.

**Article 63** If a manager of a public place, a telecommunications business operator, or an internet information service provider violates Article 45 of this Law by knowingly or should have known that an advertising activity is illegal but failing to stop it, the market supervision and administration department shall confiscate the illegal income. If the illegal income is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal income shall also be imposed. If the illegal income is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall also be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the relevant department shall suspend the relevant business according to law.

**Article 64** Anyone who violates this Law by concealing the true situation or providing false materials when applying for an advertising review shall have their application rejected or denied by the advertising review authority, receive a warning, and have their advertising review application not accepted for one year. Anyone who obtains advertising review approval through fraudulent means, bribery, or other improper means shall have their approval revoked by the advertising review authority, be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and have their advertising review application not accepted for three years.

**Article 65** Anyone who violates this Law by forging, altering, or transferring an advertising review approval document shall have their illegal income confiscated by the market supervision and administration department and be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 66** Anyone who commits an illegal act specified in this Law shall have the act recorded in their credit file by the market supervision and administration department and shall be publicly disclosed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 67** If a radio station, television station, newspaper, or audio-visual publishing unit publishes illegal advertisements, or covertly publishes advertisements in the form of news reports, or covertly publishes advertisements for medical treatment, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, or health foods in the form of introducing health or wellness knowledge, and the market supervision and administration department imposes a penalty according to this Law, it shall notify the press and publication, radio and television, and other relevant departments. The press and publication, radio and television, and other relevant departments shall impose sanctions on the responsible supervisors and directly responsible personnel according to law. If the circumstances are serious, the media's advertising publication business may also be suspended.

If the press and publication, radio and television, and other relevant departments fail to handle the matter against the radio station, television station, newspaper, or audio-visual publishing unit in accordance with the preceding paragraph, sanctions shall be imposed on the responsible supervisors and directly responsible personnel according to law.

**Article 68** If an advertiser, advertising operator, or advertising publisher violates this Law by committing any of the following infringements, they shall bear civil liability according to law:

(1) Harming the physical or mental health of minors or persons with disabilities in advertisements;

(2) Counterfeiting another person's patent;

(3) Disparaging the goods or services of other producers or operators;

(4) Using another person's name or likeness in an advertisement without consent;

(5) Other acts infringing upon the lawful civil rights and interests of others.

**Article 69** If a company or enterprise has its business license revoked due to publishing false advertisements or other illegal acts specified in this Law, and its legal representative bears personal responsibility for the illegal act, that person shall not serve as a director, supervisor, or senior manager of a company or enterprise for three years from the date the business license was revoked.

**Article 70** Anyone who violates this Law by refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection by the market supervision and administration department, or commits other acts constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be subject to public security administration penalties according to law. If a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 71** If an advertising review authority makes an approval decision on illegal advertising content, the responsible supervisors and directly responsible personnel shall be subject to sanctions by the appointing authority or supervisory authority according to law. If a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 72** If the market supervision and administration department fails to investigate and punish illegal advertising activities discovered during the performance of its advertising monitoring duties or reported through complaints or reports according to law, the responsible supervisors and directly responsible personnel shall be subject to sanctions according to law.

If staff members of the market supervision and administration department or relevant departments responsible for advertising management-related work neglect their duties, abuse their power, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be subject to sanctions according to law.

If the acts in the preceding two paragraphs constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter 6: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 73** The State encourages and supports the conduct of public interest advertising campaigns to spread core socialist values and promote civilized social conduct.

Mass media have the obligation to publish public interest advertisements. Radio stations, television stations, and newspaper publishing units shall publish public interest advertisements in accordance with the prescribed layout, time slots, and duration. The administrative measures for public interest advertisements shall be formulated by the market supervision and administration department of the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

**Article 74** This Law shall come into force on September 1, 2015.
