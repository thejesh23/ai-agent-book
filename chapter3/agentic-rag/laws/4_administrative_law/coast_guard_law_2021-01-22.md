# Coast Guard Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on January 22, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to regulate and safeguard the performance of duties by coast guard agencies, to uphold national sovereignty, security, and maritime rights and interests, and to protect the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

**Article 2** The Coast Guard of the People's Armed Police Force, i.e., the coast guard agencies, shall uniformly perform the duties of maritime rights protection and law enforcement.

Coast guard agencies include the China Coast Guard, its sea area branches and directly subordinate bureaus, provincial-level coast guard bureaus, municipal-level coast guard bureaus, and coast guard stations.

**Article 3** This Law applies to the maritime rights protection and law enforcement activities conducted by coast guard agencies within the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as "sea areas under China's jurisdiction") and the airspace above them.

**Article 4** Maritime rights protection and law enforcement work shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, implement the holistic approach to national security, and follow the principles of law-based management, comprehensive governance, standardized efficiency, and fairness and civility.

**Article 5** The basic tasks of maritime rights protection and law enforcement work are to carry out maritime security protection, maintain maritime public order, combat maritime smuggling and stowaway, supervise and inspect activities such as the development and utilization of marine resources, protection of the marine ecological environment, and marine fishery production within the scope of duties, and prevent, stop, and punish maritime illegal and criminal activities.

**Article 6** Coast guard agencies and their personnel are protected by law when performing their duties according to law. No organization or individual may illegally interfere with, refuse, or obstruct such performance.

**Article 7** Personnel of coast guard agencies shall abide by the Constitution and laws, uphold honor, be loyal to their duties, maintain strict discipline, enforce the law rigorously, and be honest and upright.

**Article 8** The State shall establish a land-sea coordinated, division-of-labor, scientific, and efficient mechanism for collaboration and coordination in maritime rights protection and law enforcement. Relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments in coastal areas, relevant military departments, and coast guard agencies shall strengthen collaboration and coordination with each other to properly carry out maritime rights protection and law enforcement work.

**Article 9** Organizations and individuals that make outstanding contributions to maritime rights protection and law enforcement activities shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter II: Institutions and Functions

**Article 10** The State shall establish sea area branches and directly subordinate bureaus of the China Coast Guard, provincial-level coast guard bureaus, municipal-level coast guard bureaus, and coast guard stations in coastal areas according to administrative divisions and task areas, which shall be respectively responsible for relevant maritime rights protection and law enforcement work within their respective jurisdictions. The China Coast Guard shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, lead the coast guard agencies under it in carrying out maritime rights protection and law enforcement work.

**Article 11** The jurisdictional areas of coast guard agencies shall be reasonably delimited and adjusted according to the needs of maritime rights protection and law enforcement work, and may not be limited by administrative divisions.

The delimitation and adjustment of the jurisdictional areas of coast guard agencies shall be promptly announced to the public and notified to relevant authorities.

**Article 12** Coast guard agencies shall perform the following duties according to law:

(1) Conduct patrols and surveillance within sea areas under China's jurisdiction, guard key islands and reefs, manage and protect maritime boundaries, and prevent, stop, and eliminate acts that endanger national sovereignty, security, and maritime rights and interests;

(2) Provide security protection for important maritime targets and major activities, and take necessary measures to protect key islands and reefs, as well as artificial islands, installations, and structures in the exclusive economic zone and on the continental shelf;

(3) Implement maritime public security management, investigate and deal with acts violating public security management and entry-exit management at sea, prevent and handle maritime terrorist activities, and maintain maritime public order;

(4) Inspect means of transport or goods, articles, and persons suspected of smuggling at sea, and investigate and deal with maritime smuggling violations;

(5) Within the scope of duties, supervise and inspect activities such as the use of sea areas, protection of islands and the development and utilization of uninhabited islands, exploration and exploitation of marine mineral resources, laying and protection of submarine cables (optical cables) and pipelines, marine survey and measurement, basic marine mapping, and foreign-related marine scientific research, and investigate and deal with violations;

(6) Within the scope of duties, supervise and inspect activities such as marine engineering construction projects, pollution damage to the marine environment caused by dumping of wastes, and the protection and utilization of the seaward side of the coastline in nature reserves, investigate and deal with violations, and participate in the emergency response and investigation of marine environmental pollution accidents according to prescribed authority;

(7) Supervise and inspect activities such as fishery production operations in the sea area outside the motorized fishing vessel bottom trawl prohibited fishing line and in specific fishery resource fishing grounds, and the protection of marine wildlife, investigate and deal with violations, and organize or participate in the investigation and handling of maritime fishery production safety accidents and fishery production disputes according to law;

(8) Prevent, stop, and investigate maritime criminal activities;

(9) Handle maritime emergencies according to the relevant division of responsibilities of the State;

(10) Undertake relevant law enforcement tasks in areas beyond China's jurisdictional sea areas in accordance with laws, regulations, and international treaties concluded or acceded to by China;

(11) Other duties as prescribed by laws and regulations.

The division of responsibilities between coast guard agencies and competent authorities such as public security, natural resources, ecological environment, transportation, fisheries and fishery administration, and customs shall be implemented in accordance with relevant State regulations.

**Article 13** Upon receiving emergency requests for assistance due to natural disasters, accidents, or other incidents at sea, coast guard agencies shall promptly notify the relevant competent authorities and actively carry out emergency rescue and relief.

**Article 14** Central state organs shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, provide professional guidance to maritime rights protection and law enforcement work.

**Article 15** The China Coast Guard and its sea area branches shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, coordinate and guide the maritime law enforcement teams of local people's governments in coastal areas in carrying out relevant law enforcement work concerning the use of sea areas, island protection and development, marine ecological environment protection, and marine fisheries management.

When necessary for maritime rights protection and law enforcement work, the China Coast Guard and its sea area branches may uniformly coordinate and organize the vessels and personnel of maritime law enforcement teams of local people's governments in coastal areas to participate in major maritime rights protection and law enforcement operations.

## Chapter III: Maritime Security Protection

**Article 16** To maintain maritime security and order, coast guard agencies have the right to identify and verify foreign vessels navigating, anchoring, or operating within sea areas under China's jurisdiction according to law, and to ascertain the basic information of the vessels and the basic conditions of their navigation and operations. For foreign vessels suspected of violations, coast guard agencies have the right to take measures such as tracking and surveillance.

**Article 17** For foreign vessels illegally entering China's territorial sea or the sea areas within it, coast guard agencies have the right to order them to leave immediately, or take measures such as detention, forced expulsion, or forced towing.

**Article 18** When performing maritime security protection tasks, coast guard agencies may board and inspect vessels navigating, anchoring, or operating within sea areas under China's jurisdiction according to law.

When boarding and inspecting a vessel, coast guard agencies shall, through clear instructions, require the vessel under inspection to stop and submit to inspection. The vessel under inspection shall, in accordance with the instructions, stop and submit to inspection and provide necessary convenience; if it refuses to cooperate with the inspection, the coast guard agency may conduct a forced inspection; if it flees the scene, the coast guard agency has the right to take necessary measures for interception and hot pursuit.

When inspecting a vessel, coast guard agencies have the right to examine, according to law, certificates and materials related to the vessel and production operation permits, as well as personnel identity information, to inspect the vessel and the goods and articles carried on board, and to investigate and collect evidence regarding the relevant violations.

The boarding, inspection, interception, and hot pursuit of foreign vessels shall comply with the relevant provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by China.

**Article 19** When urgently needed for handling maritime emergencies, coast guard agencies may take the following measures:

(1) Order a vessel to stop navigating or operating;

(2) Order a vessel to change its course or proceed to a designated location;

(3) Order persons on board a vessel to disembark, or restrict or prohibit persons from boarding or disembarking;

(4) Order a vessel to unload cargo, or restrict or prohibit the vessel from unloading cargo;

(5) Other measures as prescribed by laws and regulations.

**Article 20** If a foreign organization or individual, without the approval of the competent authority of China, constructs buildings or structures, or deploys various fixed or floating installations on islands, reefs, or within sea areas under China's jurisdiction, the coast guard agency has the right to order the cessation of the above illegal act or the demolition within a specified time limit; if the illegal act is not stopped or the demolition is not completed within the time limit, the coast guard agency has the right to stop the act or forcibly demolish the installations.

**Article 21** For acts by foreign military vessels and foreign government vessels used for non-commercial purposes that violate Chinese laws and regulations within sea areas under China's jurisdiction, coast guard agencies have the right to take necessary warning and control measures to stop such acts and order them to immediately leave the relevant sea areas; if they refuse to leave and cause serious harm or threat, coast guard agencies have the right to take measures such as forced expulsion or forced towing.

**Article 22** When national sovereignty, sovereign rights, or jurisdiction at sea is being unlawfully infringed upon by a foreign organization or individual, or is under imminent danger of such infringement, coast guard agencies have the right, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations, to take all necessary measures, including the use of weapons, to stop the infringement and eliminate the danger.

## Chapter IV: Maritime Administrative Law Enforcement

**Article 23** Coast guard agencies shall, according to law, impose administrative penalties, including restrictions on personal freedom, administrative coercive measures, or other measures prescribed by laws and regulations, on organizations and individuals that violate laws, regulations, and rules concerning maritime public security, customs, the development and utilization of marine resources, the protection of the marine ecological environment, and marine fisheries management.

Coast guard agencies shall, in accordance with laws and regulations on the development and utilization of marine resources, the protection of the marine ecological environment, and marine fisheries management, supervise and inspect the sites of maritime production operations.

When necessary for investigating maritime violations, coast guard agencies have the right to collect and obtain evidence from relevant organizations and individuals. The relevant organizations and individuals shall truthfully provide evidence.

When coast guard agencies conduct on-the-spot interrogation and inspection or continued interrogation of persons suspected of illegal or criminal activities in order to maintain maritime public order, the provisions of the People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

**Article 24** When coast guard agencies need to board, inspect, intercept, or hot pursue relevant vessels for the purpose of carrying out administrative law enforcement, the provisions of Article 18 of this Law shall apply.

**Article 25** Under any of the following circumstances, a coast guard agency at or above the provincial level may designate a temporary maritime warning zone within sea areas under China's jurisdiction, restricting or prohibiting the passage or stay of vessels and persons:

(1) When necessary for performing maritime security protection tasks;

(2) When necessary for combating maritime illegal and criminal activities;

(3) When necessary for handling maritime emergencies;

(4) When necessary for protecting marine resources and the ecological environment;

(5) Other circumstances requiring the designation of a temporary maritime warning zone.

When designating a temporary maritime warning zone, the scope of the zone, the duration of the warning, and the management measures shall be specified and announced. If it may affect maritime traffic safety, the opinion of the maritime administration agency shall be sought before designation, and a navigation notice or warning shall be applied for from the maritime administration agency in accordance with relevant regulations; if it involves military use of the sea or may affect the safety and use of military facilities at sea, the consent of the relevant military department shall be obtained according to law.

If it is no longer necessary to restrict or prohibit the passage or stay of vessels and persons, the coast guard agency shall promptly lift the warning and announce it.

**Article 26** For a vessel suspected of violating the law and under investigation and processing, the coast guard agency may order it to suspend navigation or operations, to anchor at a designated location, or prohibit it from leaving port. When necessary, the coast guard agency may escort the suspected vessel to a designated location for investigation and processing.

**Article 27** When vessels of international organizations, foreign organizations, or individuals engage in activities such as fishery production operations, exploration and exploitation of other natural resources, marine scientific research, or laying of submarine cables (optical cables) and pipelines within sea areas under China's jurisdiction with the approval of the competent authority of China, coast guard agencies shall supervise them according to law and may dispatch law enforcement personnel to supervise on board.

**Article 28** To prevent, stop, and punish acts violating laws and regulations on security, customs, finance, health, or entry-exit management within China's land territory, internal waters, or territorial sea, coast guard agencies have the right to exercise control in the contiguous zone and to implement administrative coercive measures or other measures prescribed by laws and regulations according to law.

**Article 29** Where the facts of a violation are clear and one of the following circumstances exists, law enforcement personnel of a coast guard agency may make a decision on punishment on the spot:

(1) A fine of not more than 500 yuan or a warning for an individual, or a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan or a warning for a unit;

(2) A fine decision that cannot be made on the spot at sea and would be difficult to enforce afterwards.

A decision on punishment made on the spot shall be promptly reported to the coast guard agency to which the personnel belong for record.

**Article 30** For maritime administrative cases that are not suitable for on-the-spot punishment but where the facts are clear, the parties voluntarily admit fault and accept punishment, and have no objection to the facts of the violation and the application of law, the coast guard agency may, after obtaining the written consent of the parties, handle the case quickly by simplifying measures such as evidence collection and review and approval procedures.

For maritime administrative cases that meet the conditions for quick handling, if the parties admit the facts of the violation and accept fault and punishment in self-written materials or interrogation records, and there is mutual corroboration with key evidence such as audio-visual materials, electronic data, and inspection records, the coast guard agency may no longer carry out other investigation and evidence collection work.

If the interrogation process is recorded by audio and video using devices such as law enforcement recorders, written interrogation records may be replaced. When necessary, written explanations shall be provided for the key content and corresponding time periods of the audio-visual materials.

For maritime administrative cases handled quickly, the coast guard agency shall make a decision on handling within 48 hours after the parties arrive at the case.

**Article 31** Maritime administrative cases with any of the following circumstances are not suitable for quick handling:

(1) Cases where a hearing procedure should be applied according to law;

(2) Cases where a penalty of administrative detention for 10 days or more may be imposed;

(3) Cases with significant social impact;

(4) Cases that may involve a crime;

(5) Other cases not suitable for quick handling.

**Article 32** Before implementing administrative coercive measures, law enforcement personnel of a coast guard agency shall report to and obtain approval from the head of the unit. In an emergency situation requiring the implementation of administrative coercive measures on the spot at sea, the personnel shall report to the head of the unit within 24 hours and promptly go through the approval procedures after returning to shore; if it is impossible to report to the head of the unit within 24 hours due to force majeure, the report shall be made within 24 hours after the impact of the force majeure is eliminated. If the head of the coast guard agency considers that the administrative coercive measures should not be taken, they shall be immediately lifted.

**Article 33** If a party fails to comply with a punishment decision within the time limit, the coast guard agency that made the punishment decision may take the following measures according to law:

(1) For a fine not paid when due, an additional fine of 3% of the fine amount shall be imposed for each day of delay;

(2) Auction or sell off the sealed-up or seized property according to law, or transfer the frozen deposits or remittances to offset the fine;

(3) Adopt other methods of administrative enforcement according to legal provisions.

For matters where this Law and other laws do not provide that coast guard agencies may implement administrative enforcement, coast guard agencies shall apply to the people's court for enforcement.

**Article 34** The division of jurisdiction over maritime administrative cases among coast guard agencies at various levels shall be stipulated by the China Coast Guard.

If there is a dispute over the jurisdiction of a maritime administrative case between a coast guard agency and another authority, the coast guard agency and the other authority shall negotiate based on the principle of facilitating the investigation and handling of the case.

**Article 35** When handling a maritime administrative case, if there is evidence proving that a party has intentionally destroyed evidence at sea, such as by pouring articles into the sea, thereby causing difficulties for the coast guard agency in adducing evidence, the coast guard agency may, in conjunction with other evidence, presume that the relevant facts of the violation are established, unless the party has sufficient evidence to rebut the presumption.

**Article 36** When carrying out maritime law enforcement work such as patrols, surveillance, interception, and hot pursuit, the use of law enforcement vessels or aircraft marked with special insignia by coast guard agencies shall be deemed as identification.

When conducting administrative law enforcement investigations or inspections, law enforcement personnel of a coast guard agency shall be no fewer than two persons and shall proactively present their law enforcement credentials to identify themselves. The parties or other relevant persons have the right to request law enforcement personnel to present their law enforcement credentials.

**Article 37** For procedures for maritime administrative law enforcement by coast guard agencies not provided for in this Law, the provisions of relevant laws such as the Administrative Penalty Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Coercion Law of the People's Republic of China, and the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

## Chapter V: Investigation of Maritime Crimes

**Article 38** When handling criminal cases occurring at sea, coast guard agencies shall exercise the power of investigation and take investigative measures and criminal coercive measures in accordance with the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 39** After filing a case, for criminal cases involving crimes endangering national security, terrorist activities, organized crimes of a mafia nature, major drug crimes, or other serious crimes endangering society, coast guard agencies may, in accordance with the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and relevant regulations, and after undergoing strict approval procedures, adopt technical investigation measures and assign them to relevant authorities for execution according to regulations.

For pursuing fugitive criminal suspects or defendants who are wanted or for whom arrest has been approved or decided, technical investigation measures necessary for the pursuit may be adopted after approval.

**Article 40** If a criminal suspect who should be arrested is at large, the coast guard agency may issue a wanted notice according to regulations and take effective measures to bring them to justice.

If a coast guard agency issues a wanted notice for a criminal suspect, it may request the assistance of the public security authority in the pursuit.

**Article 41** When coast guard agencies need to board, inspect, intercept, or hot pursue relevant vessels for the purpose of handling maritime criminal cases, the provisions of Article 18 of this Law shall apply.

**Article 42** If a coast guard agency, a people's procuratorate, or a people's court decides to grant bail pending trial to a criminal suspect or defendant in a maritime criminal case according to law, the execution shall be carried out by the coast guard agency at the place of residence of the person released on bail. If there is no coast guard agency at the place of residence of the person released on bail, the local public security authority shall assist in the execution.

**Article 43** If a coast guard agency, a people's procuratorate, or a people's court decides to place a criminal suspect or defendant in a maritime criminal case under residential surveillance according to law, the execution shall be carried out by the coast guard agency at the residence of the person under residential surveillance; if the person under residential surveillance has no fixed residence in the city or county where the coast guard agency handling the case is located, the surveillance may be executed at a designated residence. For persons suspected of crimes endangering national security or terrorist activities, if execution at the residence may hinder the investigation, the surveillance may also be executed at a designated residence with the approval of the coast guard agency at the next higher level. However, execution shall not be carried out in detention facilities or specialized case-handling premises.

**Article 44** Coast guard stations shall be responsible for investigating maritime criminal cases occurring within their respective jurisdictions.

Coast guard agencies at or above the municipal level shall be responsible for investigating major criminal cases within their jurisdictions involving crimes endangering national security, terrorist activities, foreign-related crimes, economic crimes, organized crimes, and other major criminal cases.

If a higher-level coast guard agency deems it necessary, it may investigate maritime criminal cases within the jurisdiction of a lower-level coast guard agency; if a lower-level coast guard agency considers a case to be major and requires investigation by a higher-level coast guard agency, it may request the higher-level coast guard agency to take jurisdiction.**Article 45** When a Coast Guard agency handles a maritime criminal case and needs to request approval for an arrest or to transfer the case for prosecution, it shall submit the request or transfer to the corresponding People's Procuratorate at the same level.

## Chapter 6: Use of Police Equipment and Weapons

**Article 46** Coast Guard personnel may use police equipment or other on-site equipment and tools under any of the following circumstances:

(1) When lawfully boarding, inspecting, intercepting, or pursuing a vessel, and it is necessary to compel the vessel to stop;
(2) When lawfully forcibly removing or towing a vessel;
(3) When encountering obstruction or interference during the lawful performance of duties;
(4) Other circumstances requiring on-site intervention to stop illegal or criminal activities.

**Article 47** Coast Guard personnel may use handheld weapons under any of the following circumstances, after warnings have been issued and ignored:

(1) When there is evidence that a vessel is carrying criminal suspects or illegally transporting weapons, ammunition, state secrets, drugs, or other such items, and the vessel refuses to comply with orders to stop;
(2) When a foreign vessel illegally engages in production or operational activities within China's jurisdictional waters, refuses to comply with orders to stop, or otherwise refuses to accept boarding or inspection, and other measures are insufficient to stop the illegal act.

**Article 48** Coast Guard personnel may use ship-borne or aircraft-borne weapons, in addition to handheld weapons, under any of the following circumstances:

(1) When carrying out maritime counter-terrorism missions;
(2) When handling serious violent incidents at sea;
(3) When a law enforcement vessel or aircraft is attacked by weapons or other dangerous means.

**Article 49** If there is no time to issue a warning, or if issuing a warning could lead to more serious harmful consequences, Coast Guard personnel may use weapons directly when lawfully authorized to do so.

**Article 50** Coast Guard personnel shall reasonably assess the necessary limits for using weapons based on the nature, degree, and urgency of the danger posed by the illegal or criminal act and the perpetrator, and shall strive to avoid or minimize unnecessary casualties and property damage.

**Article 51** For matters concerning the use of police equipment and weapons by Coast Guard personnel not covered by this Law, the provisions regarding the use of police equipment and weapons by the People's Police, as well as other relevant laws and regulations, shall apply.

## Chapter 7: Guarantees and Cooperation

**Article 52** The State shall establish a funding guarantee mechanism that aligns with the maritime rights protection and law enforcement missions and development needs of the Coast Guard. Necessary funds shall be included in the budget in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 53** When formulating territorial spatial plans and relevant special plans, the relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level in coastal areas, and their relevant departments shall coordinate the needs of maritime rights protection and law enforcement work. They shall guarantee the construction of venues and facilities for law enforcement case handling, duty training, living quarters, etc., for the Coast Guard in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 54** When urgently needed for maritime rights protection and law enforcement, the Coast Guard may, in accordance with laws, regulations, and rules, prioritize the use or requisition of transportation tools, communication tools, and venues belonging to organizations or individuals. These shall be promptly returned after use, and appropriate compensation shall be paid. If losses are caused, compensation shall be provided in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 55** The Coast Guard shall optimize its force structure, strengthen its talent pool, enhance education and training, ensure that its personnel possess the knowledge, skills, and qualities necessary to perform their statutory duties, and improve their professional capabilities in maritime rights protection and law enforcement.

A system of certification and qualification management shall be implemented for maritime rights protection and law enforcement personnel.

**Article 56** The State shall strengthen the construction of a maritime rights protection and law enforcement equipment system, ensuring that the Coast Guard is equipped with vessels, aircraft, weapons, and other equipment commensurate with its duties.

**Article 57** The Coast Guard shall strengthen its information technology capabilities, utilize modern information technology to promote transparency in law enforcement, enhance services for the public, and improve the efficiency of maritime rights protection and law enforcement work.

The Coast Guard shall establish a maritime reporting service platform to promptly receive reports and emergency requests for help from the public.

**Article 58** The Coast Guard shall establish mechanisms for information sharing, work coordination, and cooperation with the corresponding competent departments for foreign affairs (diplomacy), public security, natural resources, ecological environment, transportation, fisheries administration, emergency management, customs, as well as with the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and relevant military departments.

Relevant competent departments shall promptly provide the Coast Guard with information services and technical support, including basic data, administrative licenses, and administrative management policies related to maritime rights protection and law enforcement.

The Coast Guard shall promptly provide feedback on work data and information, such as maritime supervision, inspection, and investigation of illegal activities, to the relevant competent departments, and cooperate with them in maritime administrative management. If the Coast Guard, when lawfully imposing administrative penalties, deems it necessary to revoke a license, it shall transfer the relevant materials to the licensing authority for processing.

**Article 59** When necessary for maritime rights protection and law enforcement work, the Coast Guard may request assistance from relevant competent departments. If the assistance request falls within the scope of duties of the relevant competent department, that department shall cooperate.

**Article 60** The Coast Guard shall send persons subject to administrative detention, foreigners subject to detention for investigation, and criminal suspects subject to criminal detention or arrest to the detention center or detention house located in the area where the Coast Guard agency is situated for execution.

**Article 61** The Coast Guard shall properly keep property and goods seized or detained according to law, and shall not damage or dispose of them without authorization. However, for the following goods or items, with the approval of the person in charge of a Coast Guard agency at or above the municipal level, they may be auctioned or sold off first according to law, with notification to the owner, or to other parties if the owner is unknown:

(1) Dangerous goods such as refined oil products;
(2) Fresh, perishable, easily degradable, or other items not suitable for long-term storage;
(3) Vehicles, vessels, etc., that are prone to performance degradation and value depreciation if not used for a long time;
(4) Items of enormous volume that are difficult to store;
(5) Items for which the owner requests an advance auction or sale.

The proceeds from the auction or sale shall be temporarily held by the Coast Guard and handled in accordance with relevant state regulations after the case is concluded.

**Article 62** For property and goods involved in a case that should be returned to the owner or other parties, the Coast Guard shall notify the owner or other parties to claim them within six months. If the owner is unknown, a public notice shall be issued for the owner to claim the items. If no one claims the items within six months after notification or public notice, they shall be treated as unclaimed property, auctioned or sold off according to law, and the proceeds turned over to the state treasury. Under special circumstances, the processing period may be extended, but the extension shall not exceed three months.

## Chapter 8: International Cooperation

**Article 63** The China Coast Guard shall conduct international cooperation in maritime law enforcement in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or based on the principles of reciprocity and mutual benefit. Within its prescribed authority, it shall organize or participate in the implementation of relevant international treaties on maritime law enforcement and negotiate and sign cooperative documents on maritime law enforcement.

**Article 64** The main tasks of the Coast Guard in international maritime law enforcement cooperation include participating in the handling of foreign-related maritime emergencies, coordinating the resolution of maritime law enforcement disputes, managing maritime crises, cooperating with foreign maritime law enforcement agencies and relevant international organizations to combat maritime illegal and criminal activities, protecting marine resources and the environment, and jointly maintaining international and regional maritime public safety and order.

**Article 65** The Coast Guard may engage in the following types of international maritime law enforcement cooperation with foreign maritime law enforcement agencies and relevant international organizations:

(1) Establishing bilateral or multilateral maritime law enforcement cooperation mechanisms and participating in their activities;
(2) Exchanging and sharing maritime law enforcement intelligence and information;
(3) Conducting joint maritime patrols, inspections, exercises, and training;
(4) Engaging in educational and training exchanges;
(5) Exchanging liaison officers for international maritime law enforcement cooperation;
(6) Other activities related to international maritime law enforcement cooperation.

## Chapter 9: Supervision

**Article 66** The Coast Guard and its personnel shall perform their duties and exercise their powers in accordance with the conditions, authorities, and procedures stipulated by laws and regulations. They shall not abuse their power, neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of organizations or individuals.

**Article 67** The Coast Guard shall respect and legally guarantee the right of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to be informed, to participate, and to supervise the Coast Guard's law enforcement work, enhancing the transparency and credibility of law enforcement.

The Coast Guard shall lawfully disclose information related to its maritime law enforcement work.

**Article 68** Law enforcement activities such as inquiries, interrogations, continued questioning, identification of criminal suspects, and security checks or information collection concerning criminal suspects shall be conducted in case-handling venues by the Coast Guard. Exceptions apply when on-site inquiries or interrogations are urgently necessary, or when other circumstances make it unsuitable to conduct them in case-handling venues.

The Coast Guard shall create a complete record of its maritime rights protection and law enforcement activities in text, audio-visual, or other forms, in accordance with relevant state regulations, and shall archive these records.

**Article 69** The Coast Guard and its personnel shall, when carrying out maritime rights protection and law enforcement work, accept supervision by the Procuratorial Organs and the Military Supervisory Organs according to law.

**Article 70** People's governments and their relevant departments, citizens, legal persons, and other organizations have the right to report, inform against, or lodge complaints with the Procuratorial Organs or Military Supervisory Organs regarding any illegal or disciplinary acts by the Coast Guard or its personnel. For ongoing illegal, disciplinary, or derelict acts by the Coast Guard or its personnel, complaints or reports may be made through the maritime reporting service platform.

No agency or individual shall suppress or retaliate against citizens, legal persons, or other organizations that lawfully report, inform, complain, or provide tips.

**Article 71** Higher-level Coast Guard agencies shall supervise the maritime rights protection and law enforcement work of lower-level Coast Guard agencies. If they find that a lower-level agency has made an erroneous handling measure or decision, they have the authority to revoke or change it, or order the lower-level agency to do so. If they find that a lower-level agency has failed to perform its statutory duties, they have the authority to order it to perform them according to law.

**Article 72** The China Coast Guard shall establish and improve a supervision mechanism for maritime rights protection and law enforcement work and a system for holding personnel accountable for law enforcement errors.

## Chapter 10: Legal Liability

**Article 73** Anyone who commits one of the following acts obstructing the Coast Guard or its personnel from lawfully performing their duties shall be punished by the public security organ or the Coast Guard in accordance with the provisions of the "Law of the People's Republic of China on Public Security Administration Punishments" concerning obstructing the People's Police from lawfully performing their duties:

(1) Insulting, threatening, surrounding, intercepting, or assaulting Coast Guard personnel;
(2) Obstructing investigation and evidence collection;
(3) Forcibly breaking through a maritime temporary warning area;(4) Obstructing the execution of tasks such as pursuit, inspection, search, rescue, or guard duties;
(5) Obstructing the passage of law enforcement vessels, aircraft, vehicles, or personnel;
(6) Using dangerous driving, setting up obstacles, or other methods to flee in a vessel, endangering the safety of law enforcement vessels or personnel;
(7) Other serious acts obstructing the Coast Guard or its personnel from performing their duties.

**Article 74** Coast Guard personnel who commit one of the following acts during the performance of their duties shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Central Military Commission:

(1) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy;
(2) Engaging in fraud, concealing case facts, or harboring or conniving at illegal or criminal activities;
(3) Using torture to extract confessions or physically punishing or mistreating criminal suspects;
(4) Using police equipment or weapons in violation of regulations;
(5) Unlawfully depriving or restricting personal freedom, or unlawfully inspecting or searching persons, goods, items, vehicles, residences, or premises;
(6) Extorting or accepting bribes, or accepting meals, gifts, or entertainment from parties or their agents;
(7) Unlawfully imposing administrative penalties, administrative coercive measures, criminal coercive measures, or collecting fees;
(8) Neglecting duties or failing to perform statutory obligations;
(9) Other illegal or disciplinary acts.

**Article 75** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

**Article 76** Organizations or individuals who are dissatisfied with an administrative act taken by the Coast Guard have the right to apply for administrative reconsideration to the next higher-level Coast Guard agency in accordance with the "Administrative Reconsideration Law of the People's Republic of China," or to file an administrative lawsuit with the competent People's Court in accordance with the "Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China."

**Article 77** If the Coast Guard or its personnel cause damage by unlawfully exercising their powers and infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of organizations or individuals, they shall provide compensation in accordance with the "State Compensation Law of the People's Republic of China" and other relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter 11: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 78** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Provincial-level Coast Guard Bureau" refers to a Coast Guard bureau established in a coastal province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, directly led by the China Coast Guard. "Municipal-level Coast Guard Bureau" refers to a Coast Guard bureau established in a city under the jurisdiction of a coastal province or autonomous region, or a district under the jurisdiction of a municipality directly under the Central Government, led by the provincial-level Coast Guard Bureau. "Coast Guard Station" generally refers to a grassroots Coast Guard agency established in a coastal county-level administrative region, led by a municipal-level Coast Guard Bureau.
(2) "Vessel" refers to various types of mobile devices, whether displacement or non-displacement, including boats, ships, rafts, watercraft, submersibles, etc., but does not include offshore oil, natural gas, or other operational platforms.
**Article 79** If a foreign country adopts discriminatory prohibitions, restrictions, or other special measures against Chinese citizens, legal persons, or other organizations in maritime law enforcement, the Coast Guard may take corresponding reciprocal measures in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 80** The rights protection and law enforcement measures concerning vessels stipulated in this Law shall apply to various fixed or floating structures, installations, and fixed or mobile platforms at sea.

**Article 81** When the Coast Guard performs law enforcement tasks in areas beyond China's jurisdictional waters in accordance with laws, regulations, and international treaties concluded or acceded to by China, the relevant procedures may be implemented by reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 82** The China Coast Guard shall formulate rules and regulations concerning maritime rights protection and law enforcement matters in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and decisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and shall file them for record as stipulated.

**Article 83** The Coast Guard shall perform tasks such as defensive combat in accordance with the "National Defense Law of the People's Republic of China," the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the People's Armed Police Force," other relevant military laws and regulations, and orders of the Central Military Commission.

**Article 84** This Law shall come into force on February 1, 2021.
