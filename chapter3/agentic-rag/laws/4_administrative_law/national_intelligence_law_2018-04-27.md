# National Intelligence Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on June 27, 2017

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen and safeguard national intelligence work, and to protect national security and interests.

Article 2 National intelligence work shall adhere to a holistic approach to national security, provide intelligence references for major national decisions, offer intelligence support for preventing and mitigating risks endangering national security, and safeguard state power, sovereignty, unity and territorial integrity, the well-being of the people, sustainable economic and social development, and other major national interests.

Article 3 The State shall establish and improve a centralized, unified, coordinated, scientific, and efficient national intelligence system.

The central national security leadership body shall exercise unified leadership over national intelligence work, formulate policies and guidelines for national intelligence work, plan the overall development of national intelligence work, establish and improve coordination mechanisms for national intelligence work, coordinate national intelligence work across various fields, and study and decide on major matters in national intelligence work.

The Central Military Commission shall exercise unified leadership and organize military intelligence work.

Article 4 National intelligence work shall adhere to the principles of combining open work with covert work, combining specialized work with the mass line, and combining division of responsibilities with collaboration and coordination.

Article 5 The intelligence agencies of state security organs, public security organs, and military organs (hereinafter collectively referred to as national intelligence work agencies) shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, cooperate with each other to carry out intelligence work and conduct intelligence operations.

All relevant state organs shall, based on their respective functions and task divisions, closely cooperate with national intelligence work agencies.

Article 6 National intelligence work agencies and their personnel shall be loyal to the State and the people, abide by the Constitution and laws, be devoted to their duties, maintain strict discipline, be honest and upright, be selfless and dedicated, and resolutely safeguard national security and interests.

Article 7 All organizations and citizens shall, in accordance with the law, support, assist, and cooperate with national intelligence work, and keep confidential the secrets of national intelligence work that they have come to know.

The State shall provide protection to individuals and organizations that support, assist, and cooperate with national intelligence work.

Article 8 National intelligence work shall be conducted in accordance with the law, respecting and safeguarding human rights, and protecting the legitimate rights and interests of individuals and organizations.

Article 9 The State shall commend and reward individuals and organizations that have made significant contributions to national intelligence work.

## Chapter II Powers of National Intelligence Work Agencies

Article 10 National intelligence work agencies shall, based on work needs and in accordance with the law, use necessary methods, means, and channels to conduct intelligence work both domestically and abroad.

Article 11 National intelligence work agencies shall, in accordance with the law, collect and process intelligence related to acts that endanger the national security and interests of the People's Republic of China, whether committed by foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals, or directed or funded by them, or carried out in collusion with domestic and foreign institutions, organizations, or individuals, and provide intelligence basis or references for preventing, stopping, and punishing such acts.

Article 12 National intelligence work agencies may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, establish cooperative relationships with relevant individuals and organizations, and entrust them to carry out related work.

Article 13 National intelligence work agencies may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, conduct foreign exchanges and cooperation.

Article 14 When conducting intelligence work in accordance with the law, national intelligence work agencies may request relevant organs, organizations, and citizens to provide necessary support, assistance, and cooperation.

Article 15 National intelligence work agencies may, based on work needs and in accordance with relevant state regulations, and after undergoing strict approval procedures, adopt technical reconnaissance measures and identity protection measures.

Article 16 When lawfully performing their duties, personnel of national intelligence work agencies may, in accordance with relevant state regulations and after approval, present corresponding credentials to enter restricted areas and premises, inquire about relevant information from relevant organs, organizations, and individuals, and consult or retrieve relevant archives, materials, and items.

Article 17 Personnel of national intelligence work agencies may, when necessary for urgent tasks and upon presenting corresponding credentials, enjoy passage facilitation.

Personnel of national intelligence work agencies may, based on work needs and in accordance with relevant state regulations, have priority in using or lawfully requisitioning means of transportation, communication tools, venues, and buildings of relevant organs, organizations, and individuals. When necessary, they may set up relevant work sites and equipment and facilities. After the task is completed, they shall promptly return or restore them to their original state, and pay corresponding fees in accordance with regulations; if losses are caused, compensation shall be made.

Article 18 National intelligence work agencies may, based on work needs and in accordance with relevant state regulations, request customs, border entry-exit inspection, and other authorities to provide conveniences such as exemption from inspection.

Article 19 National intelligence work agencies and their personnel shall strictly act in accordance with the law, shall not exceed their authority or abuse their power, shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and organizations, shall not use their positions to seek personal gain for themselves or others, and shall not disclose state secrets, trade secrets, or personal information.

## Chapter III Safeguards for National Intelligence Work

Article 20 National intelligence work agencies and their personnel conducting intelligence work in accordance with the law shall be protected by law.

Article 21 The State shall strengthen the construction of national intelligence work agencies, implement special management over their organizational setup, personnel, staffing, funding, and assets, and provide special safeguards.

The State shall establish management systems for personnel recruitment, selection, assessment, training, treatment, and exit that meet the needs of intelligence work.

Article 22 National intelligence work agencies shall adapt to the needs of intelligence work and enhance their capacity to conduct intelligence work.

National intelligence work agencies shall utilize scientific and technological means to improve the level of identification, screening, synthesis, and analysis of intelligence information.

Article 23 When the personal safety of personnel of national intelligence work agencies performing tasks, or of personnel who have established cooperative relationships with national intelligence work agencies assisting in national intelligence work, or their close relatives, is threatened, relevant state departments shall take necessary measures to protect and rescue them.

Article 24 The State shall provide proper resettlement for personnel who have contributed to national intelligence work and need to be resettled.

Relevant departments such as public security, civil affairs, finance, health, education, human resources and social security, veterans' affairs, and medical security, as well as state-owned enterprises and institutions, shall assist national intelligence work agencies in carrying out resettlement work.

Article 25 Personnel who become disabled, sacrifice their lives, or die as a result of conducting national intelligence work or supporting, assisting, and cooperating with national intelligence work shall receive corresponding pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Individuals and organizations that suffer property losses due to supporting, assisting, and cooperating with national intelligence work shall receive compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 26 National intelligence work agencies shall establish and improve strict supervision and security review systems, supervise their personnel's compliance with laws and discipline, and take necessary measures in accordance with the law to conduct regular or irregular security reviews.

Article 27 Any individual or organization has the right to report and accuse national intelligence work agencies and their personnel of exceeding their authority, abusing their power, and other illegal or disciplinary acts. The relevant organs receiving such reports and accusations shall promptly investigate and handle them, and inform the whistleblowers and accusers of the results.

No individual or organization shall suppress or retaliate against individuals or organizations that lawfully report or accuse national intelligence work agencies and their personnel.

National intelligence work agencies shall provide convenient channels for individuals and organizations to report, accuse, and reflect situations, and shall keep the whistleblowers and accusers confidential.

## Chapter IV Legal Liability

Article 28 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, obstructs national intelligence work agencies and their personnel from lawfully conducting intelligence work shall be subject to disciplinary action recommended by the national intelligence work agency to the relevant unit, or be given a warning or detained for up to 15 days by the state security organ or public security organ; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 29 Anyone who discloses state secrets related to national intelligence work shall be subject to disciplinary action recommended by the national intelligence work agency to the relevant unit, or be given a warning or detained for up to 15 days by the state security organ or public security organ; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 30 Anyone who impersonates personnel of national intelligence work agencies or other relevant persons to engage in fraudulent activities, extortion, or other acts shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 31 If national intelligence work agencies and their personnel commit illegal or disciplinary acts such as exceeding their authority, abusing their power, infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and organizations, using their positions to seek personal gain for themselves or others, or disclosing state secrets, trade secrets, or personal information, they shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 32 This Law shall come into force on June 28, 2017.
