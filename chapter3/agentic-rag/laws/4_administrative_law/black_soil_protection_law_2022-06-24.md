# Black Soil Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 35th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 24, 2022

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect black soil resources, steadily restore and enhance the basic fertility of black soil, promote the sustainable use of resources, maintain ecological balance, and ensure national food security.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities such as the protection, utilization, and related management and restoration of black soil. Matters not covered by this Law shall be governed by the provisions of relevant laws such as the Land Management Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "black soil" refers to cultivated land with a black or dark black humus topsoil layer, good properties, and high fertility within the relevant regions of Heilongjiang Province, Jilin Province, Liaoning Province, and the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region (hereinafter referred to as the four provinces/regions).

Article 3 The State implements scientific and effective policies for the protection of black soil, ensures financial investment in black soil protection, and comprehensively adopts engineering, agronomic, agricultural machinery, and biological measures to protect the excellent production capacity of black soil, ensuring that the total amount of black soil does not decrease, its functions do not degrade, its quality is improved, and its production capacity is sustainable.

Article 4 The protection of black soil shall adhere to the principles of overall planning, adapting measures to local conditions, combining utilization with nurturing, integrating short-term and long-term goals, highlighting key points, and implementing comprehensive measures. A protection mechanism led by the government, implemented by agricultural producers and operators, and involving social participation shall be established and improved.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council, together with relevant departments such as natural resources and water administration, shall, considering the history of black soil reclamation and current utilization status, as well as black soil layer thickness, soil properties, and soil types, scientifically and reasonably determine the scope of black soil protection and make timely adjustments, based on the principle most conducive to comprehensive protection, integrated management, and systematic restoration. Black soil protection work shall be promoted in a planned, step-by-step, and categorized manner. Land historically classified as black soil shall, in principle, be included in the scope of black soil protection for restoration, except where restoration is truly impossible.

Article 5 Black soil shall be used for the production of agricultural products such as grain, oil crops, sugar crops, and vegetables.

Black soil with a thick black soil layer and good soil properties shall be designated as permanent basic farmland according to prescribed standards, focusing on grain production, and subject to strict protection to ensure long-term stability in quantity and quality.

Article 6 The State Council and the people's governments of the four provinces/regions shall strengthen the leadership, organization, coordination, supervision, and management of black soil protection work, and coordinate the formulation of black soil protection policies. The people's governments of the four provinces/regions shall be responsible for the quantity, quality, and ecological environment of black soil within their respective administrative regions.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a coordination mechanism for black soil protection, composed of relevant departments such as agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, water administration, development and reform, finance, and ecological environment, to strengthen coordination and guidance, clarify work responsibilities, and promote the implementation of black soil protection work.

Township people's governments shall assist in organizing the implementation of black soil protection work, promote protection, management, restoration, and utilization measures suitable for the cultivated land operated by agricultural producers and operators, and urge them to fulfill their obligations for black soil protection.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen publicity and education on black soil protection to raise public awareness of black soil protection.

Units and individuals that make outstanding contributions to black soil protection shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 8 The competent department of standardization of the State Council and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, and water administration shall, according to their respective responsibilities, formulate and improve standards for black soil quality and other protection aspects.

Article 9 The State shall establish and improve a black soil survey and monitoring system.

When conducting land surveys, the competent department of natural resources of the people's government at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, simultaneously conduct surveys on the type, distribution, quantity, quality, protection, and utilization status of black soil, and establish black soil archives.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and water administration of the State Council, together with the people's governments of the four provinces/regions, shall establish and improve a black soil quality monitoring network, strengthen routine monitoring of black soil properties, black soil layer thickness, water erosion, wind erosion, etc., establish a dynamic database of black soil quality changes, and ensure information sharing.

Article 10 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate black soil protection work into their national economic and social development plans.

Territorial spatial plans shall fully consider the protection of black soil and its surrounding ecological environment, and rationally allocate land for various purposes to facilitate the prevention and control of water erosion and wind erosion of black soil.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, based on surveys and monitoring and reflecting overall contiguous management, formulate black soil protection plans, specifying the protection scope, objectives and tasks, technical models, and safeguard measures, to curb the degradation trend of black soil, improve black soil quality, and enhance the ecological environment of black soil. County-level black soil protection plans shall be coordinated with territorial spatial plans, implemented on specific black soil plots, and made public.

Article 11 The State shall take measures to strengthen the scientific and technological support capacity for black soil protection, making scientific and technological innovation in black soil protection, management, restoration, and utilization a key support area; it encourages universities, research institutions, and agricultural technology extension agencies to collaboratively tackle key scientific and technological problems. People's governments at or above the county level shall encourage and support scientific research and the promotion and application of research results in areas such as soil and water conservation, windbreak and sand fixation, soil improvement, fertility cultivation, and ecological protection.

Relevant institutions for cultivated land quality monitoring and protection and agricultural technology extension shall provide technical training and guidance services to agricultural producers and operators for black soil protection.

The State encourages enterprises, universities, vocational schools, research institutions, scientific and technological societies, specialized farmer cooperatives, agricultural socialized service organizations, and agricultural science and technology personnel to provide technical services related to black soil protection.
The State supports international cooperation and exchange in black soil protection.

Article 12 People's governments at or above the county level shall take the following measures to strengthen the construction of black soil farmland infrastructure:

(1) Strengthen the construction of farmland water conservancy projects, and improve the irrigation and drainage systems for paddy fields and dry land;
(2) Strengthen field consolidation, repair ditch-damaged farmland, and rationally divide suitable arable field plots;
(3) Strengthen the construction of soil and water conservation projects on sloping farmland and erosion gullies;
(4) Rationally plan and construct machine roads and production roads;
(5) Build farmland shelterbelt networks;
(6) Other black soil protection measures.

Article 13 People's governments at or above the county level shall promote scientific farming systems and take the following measures to improve black soil quality:

(1) Implement crop rotation and other planting systems that combine land use with land nurturing according to local conditions, and promote moderate fallowing in accordance with relevant State regulations;
(2) Promote conservation tillage techniques such as no-tillage (reduced tillage) and subsoiling according to local conditions, and promote suitable agricultural machinery;
(3) Promote straw return methods such as mulching, crushing and deep (plow) burial, and conversion through livestock consumption according to local conditions;
(4) Organize the implementation of soil testing and formulated fertilization, scientifically reduce the application amount of chemical fertilizers, encourage the increased application of organic fertilizers, and promote technologies such as soil biological improvement;
(5) Promote green prevention and control technologies such as biological technology or biological agents for pest and disease control, scientifically reduce the use of chemical pesticides and herbicides, and rationally use agricultural production materials such as agricultural film;
(6) Other measures to improve black soil quality.

Article 14 The State encourages the adoption of comprehensive measures to prevent and control soil erosion, prevent black soil erosion, land desertification, and salinization, and improve and restore the farmland ecological environment.

People's governments at or above the county level shall carry out erosion gully management, implement reinforcement and protection of gully heads, slopes, and bottoms, and organize, according to local conditions, the creation of plant protection belts or the adoption of other measures in areas such as gully slopes and banks of erosion gullies, along rivers adjacent to black soil areas, and around lakes and reservoirs, to prevent erosion gullies from widening, deepening, and lengthening.

People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the principles of establishing defenses according to hazards, rational management and protection, and scientific layout, formulate plans for the construction of farmland shelterbelts, and organize the planting of farmland shelterbelts along farmland roads, ditches, etc., to prevent afforestation that violates natural laws. Farmland shelterbelts may only be harvested for tending and regeneration purposes to ensure that the function of the shelterbelts is not diminished.

People's governments at or above the county level shall organize and carry out desertification prevention and control, strengthen the management of deserts and desertified land around black soil areas, and prevent the desertification of black soil.

Article 15 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the ecological protection of black soil and the protection and restoration of forests, grasslands, and wetlands around black soil areas, promote the management of barren hills and slopes, enhance the ecological functions of natural ecosystems such as water conservation, soil and water retention, windbreak and sand fixation, and biodiversity maintenance, and maintain a natural ecological environment conducive to black soil protection.

Article 16 County people's governments shall, based on black soil survey and monitoring data, combined with actual conditions such as soil type and quality grade, climate characteristics, and environmental conditions, scientifically zone the black soil within their administrative regions, formulate and organize the implementation of black soil quality improvement plans, and adopt refined measures for protection, management, restoration, and utilization according to local conditions.

Article 17 State-owned farms shall strengthen the protection of black soil within their management scope, give full play to their exemplary role, and accept supervision and inspection according to law.

Rural collective economic organizations, villagers' committees, and villagers' groups shall contract out rural land according to law, supervise the contractors' rational use and protection of black soil in accordance with the purposes stipulated in the contract, and stop acts such as damaging black soil by the contractors.

Rural collective economic organizations, agricultural enterprises, specialized farmer cooperatives, and farming households shall cherish and rationally use black soil, strengthen the construction of farmland infrastructure, apply conservation tillage and other techniques according to local conditions, actively adopt maintenance measures to improve black soil quality and the farmland ecological environment, and protect black soil according to law.

Article 18 Producers, operators, and users of agricultural inputs shall, according to law, recycle and resource-utilize or harmlessly treat the packaging and waste of agricultural inputs such as pesticides, fertilizers, and agricultural film, and shall not discard them arbitrarily to prevent black soil pollution.

County people's governments shall take measures to support the recycling and resource utilization or harmless treatment of packaging and waste of agricultural inputs such as pesticides, fertilizers, and agricultural film.

Article 19 Units and individuals engaged in livestock and poultry breeding shall scientifically carry out the harmless treatment and resource utilization of livestock and poultry manure, focusing on the on-site or nearby return of livestock and poultry manure to farmland, to promote the development of a green crop-livestock circular agriculture on black soil.

People's governments at or above the county level shall support the harmless treatment and resource utilization of livestock and poultry manure.

Article 20 No organization or individual may damage black soil resources and the ecological environment. The illegal digging, excessive digging, and illegal trading of black soil are prohibited. The competent department of natural resources of the State Council, together with departments such as agriculture and rural affairs, water administration, public security, transportation, and market supervision and administration, shall establish and improve a supervision and management system for the protection of black soil resources, and enhance the comprehensive management capacity against illegal digging, excessive digging, illegal trading of black soil, and other acts that damage black soil resources and the ecological environment.

Article 21 Construction projects shall not occupy black soil; if occupation is truly necessary, strict approval shall be obtained according to law, and an equivalent quantity and quality of cultivated land shall be supplemented.

When a construction project occupies black soil, the topsoil layer shall be stripped according to prescribed standards. The stripped black soil shall be used locally for the reclamation of new cultivated land, the improvement of low-quality cultivated land, the management of polluted cultivated land, the construction of high-standard farmland, and land reclamation. The entity responsible for the construction project shall formulate a reuse plan for the stripped black soil and report it to the competent department of natural resources for filing. Specific measures shall be formulated separately by the people's governments of the four provinces/regions.

Article 22 The State shall establish and improve a financial investment guarantee system for black soil protection. People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate funds for black soil protection into their budgets.

The State shall increase the level of support for reward and subsidy funds for black soil protection measures, and establish a long-term and stable reward and subsidy mechanism.
Local people's governments at or above the county level shall make black soil protection a key area for the use of land transfer income in agricultural and rural investment, and increase investment efforts.

The State organizes the construction of high-standard farmland, farmland water conservancy, soil and water conservation, desertification prevention and control, farmland shelterbelts, and land reclamation, and actively supports the needs of black soil protection in project fund arrangements. County people's governments may, in accordance with relevant State regulations, coordinate the use of agricultural-related funds for black soil protection to improve the efficiency of financial fund utilization.
Article 23 The State implements incentive policies oriented towards combining utilization with nurturing and focusing on protection effects, providing rewards and subsidies to agricultural producers and operators who adopt measures for black soil protection, management, and restoration in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 24 The State encourages major grain-consuming regions to participate in black soil protection through economic cooperation methods such as financial support and establishing stable grain purchase and sale relationships with the four provinces/regions, and establishes and improves a cross-regional investment protection mechanism for black soil.

Article 25 The State, based on the principles of policy support, social participation, and market-oriented operation, encourages social capital to invest in black soil protection activities and protects the legitimate rights and interests of investors.

The State encourages insurance institutions to carry out insurance business related to black soil protection.

The State supports specialized farmer cooperatives, enterprises, etc., in establishing interest linkage mechanisms and socialized service mechanisms with farming households in various ways, developing moderate-scale operations, promoting the improvement of agricultural product quality, brand building, and standardized production, and increasing the output benefits of black soil.

Article 26 The State Council shall assess the implementation of black soil protection responsibilities by the people's governments of the four provinces/regions, and incorporate the status of black soil protection into the responsibility target for cultivated land protection.

Article 27 The competent departments of natural resources, agriculture and rural affairs, and water administration of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their duties, jointly conduct supervision and inspection of black soil protection and quality construction according to law.

Article 28 People's governments at or above the county level shall report on the status of black soil protection to the people's congress at the corresponding level or its standing committee, and accept supervision according to law.

Article 29 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, natural resources, etc., of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments commit any of the following acts, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, a demerit, or a major demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, they shall be given demotion or dismissal; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be given expulsion:

(1) Intercepting, misappropriating, or failing to use black soil protection funds as prescribed;
(2) Failing to promptly investigate and deal with acts of damaging black soil upon discovery or upon receiving reports;
(3) Other acts of failing to perform black soil protection duties according to law, resulting in damage to black soil resources and the ecological environment.

Article 30 Anyone who illegally occupies or damages black soil farmland infrastructure shall be ordered by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, water administration, etc., of the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, restore the original state within a specified period, and be fined an amount between one and three times the restoration cost.

Article 31 Anyone who illegally uses black soil for non-agricultural construction shall be severely punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations such as the Land Management Law.

Anyone who, in violation of laws and regulations, causes a reduction in the area of black soil, a decline in quality, functional degradation, or damage to the ecological environment shall be subject to management, restoration, and compensation for losses according to law.

If agricultural producers and operators fail to fulfill their obligations for black soil protection and do not correct their behavior after criticism and education, the relevant subsidies for cultivated land protection may be withheld.

Article 32 Anyone who illegally digs or excessively digs black soil in violation of the provisions of Article 20 of this Law shall be severely punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations such as the Land Management Law.

Anyone who illegally sells black soil shall have the illegally sold black soil and illegal gains confiscated by the competent departments of market supervision and administration, agriculture and rural affairs, and natural resources of the local people's government at or above the county level according to their respective responsibilities, and shall be fined not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan per cubic meter; anyone who knowingly purchases illegally sold black soil shall have the illegally purchased black soil confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods.

Article 33 In violation of the provisions of Article 21 of this Law, if a construction project occupies black soil without stripping the topsoil layer, the competent department of natural resources of the local people's government at or above the county level shall impose a fine of not less than 100 yuan but not more than 200 yuan per square meter; if the topsoil layer is not stripped according to the prescribed standards, a fine of not less than 50 yuan but not more than 100 yuan per square meter shall be imposed.

Article 34 Anyone who refuses or obstructs supervision and inspection of black soil protection according to law shall be ordered by the relevant department of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 35 Anyone who causes black soil pollution or soil erosion shall be severely punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations such as those on pollution prevention and control and soil and water conservation.

Article 36 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

Article 37 For the protection of black soil within the scope of forests, grasslands, wetlands, rivers, lakes, etc., the relevant laws such as the Forest Law of the People's Republic of China, the Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China, the Wetland Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, and the Water Law of the People's Republic of China shall apply; if the relevant laws do not provide for the illegal digging, excessive digging, or illegal trading of black soil, penalties shall be imposed by reference to the provisions of Article 32 of this Law.

Article 38 This Law shall come into force on August 1, 2022.
