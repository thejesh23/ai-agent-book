# Higher Education Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 29, 1998

First Amendment on December 27, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending the Higher Education Law of the People's Republic of China at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on December 29, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Four Laws Including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Education Law, for the purpose of developing higher education, implementing the strategy of rejuvenating the country through science and education, and promoting the construction of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to higher education activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purpose of this Law, "higher education" refers to education provided on the basis of completion of upper secondary education.

Article 3 The State adheres to the guidance of Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, and Deng Xiaoping Theory, follows the basic principles established by the Constitution, and develops socialist higher education.

Article 4 Higher education must implement the State's educational policy, serve socialist modernization and the people, integrate with productive labor and social practice, and cultivate the educated to become socialist builders and successors who are well-developed morally, intellectually, physically, and aesthetically.

Article 5 The task of higher education is to cultivate high-level specialized talents with a sense of social responsibility, innovative spirit, and practical ability, to develop science, technology, and culture, and to promote socialist modernization.

Article 6 The State formulates plans for the development of higher education, establishes higher education institutions, and adopts various forms to actively develop higher education in accordance with the needs of economic construction and social development.

The State encourages enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, other social organizations, and citizens to establish higher education institutions in accordance with the law, and to participate in and support the reform and development of higher education.

Article 7 The State promotes the reform of the higher education system and the reform of higher education teaching, optimizes the structure and resource allocation of higher education, and improves the quality and efficiency of higher education, in accordance with the needs of socialist modernization and the development of a socialist market economy, and based on the actual conditions of different types and levels of higher education institutions.

Article 8 The State, based on the characteristics and needs of ethnic minorities, helps and supports the development of higher education in ethnic minority areas, and cultivates high-level specialized talents for ethnic minorities.

Article 9 Citizens enjoy the right to receive higher education in accordance with the law.

The State takes measures to help students from ethnic minorities and students with financial difficulties to receive higher education.

Higher education institutions must admit disabled students who meet the admission standards prescribed by the State, and shall not refuse admission on the grounds of their disability.

Article 10 The State guarantees, in accordance with the law, the freedom of scientific research, literary and artistic creation, and other cultural activities in higher education institutions.

Those engaged in scientific research, literary and artistic creation, and other cultural activities in higher education institutions shall abide by the law.

Article 11 Higher education institutions shall face society, run schools autonomously in accordance with the law, and implement democratic management.

Article 12 The State encourages collaboration between higher education institutions, and between higher education institutions and scientific research institutions, enterprises, and public institutions, to achieve complementary advantages and improve the utilization efficiency of educational resources.

The State encourages and supports international exchanges and cooperation in higher education.

Article 13 The State Council exercises unified leadership and management over the national higher education.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government coordinate and manage higher education within their respective administrative regions, and manage higher education institutions that primarily cultivate talents for local areas and those authorized by the State Council.

Article 14 The administrative department of education under the State Council is in charge of the national higher education work and manages higher education institutions determined by the State Council to primarily cultivate talents for the whole country. Other relevant departments under the State Council are responsible for related higher education work within the scope of their duties prescribed by the State Council.

## Chapter II Basic System of Higher Education

Article 15 Higher education includes degree education and non-degree education.

Higher education adopts full-time and part-time forms.

The State supports the implementation of higher education through radio, television, correspondence, and other distance education methods.

Article 16 Higher degree education is divided into specialized education, undergraduate education, and graduate education.

Higher degree education shall meet the following academic standards:

(1) Specialized education shall enable students to master the basic theories and specialized knowledge necessary for the specialty, and possess the basic skills and preliminary ability to engage in practical work in the specialty;

(2) Undergraduate education shall enable students to systematically master the basic theories and basic knowledge necessary for the discipline and specialty, master the necessary basic skills, methods, and related knowledge of the specialty, and possess the preliminary ability to engage in practical work and research work in the specialty;

(3) Master's degree education shall enable students to master solid basic theories and systematic professional knowledge of the discipline, master corresponding skills, methods, and related knowledge, and possess the ability to engage in practical work and scientific research work in the specialty. Doctoral degree education shall enable students to master solid and broad basic theories, systematic and in-depth professional knowledge, corresponding skills and methods of the discipline, and possess the ability to independently engage in creative scientific research work and practical work in the discipline.

Article 17 The basic duration of study for specialized education is two to three years, for undergraduate education is four to five years, for master's degree education is two to three years, and for doctoral degree education is three to four years. The duration of study for part-time higher degree education shall be appropriately extended. Higher education institutions may adjust their duration of study according to actual needs.

Article 18 Higher education is implemented by higher education institutions and other higher education institutions.

Universities and independently established colleges mainly implement undergraduate and above education. Higher specialized schools implement specialized education. With the approval of the administrative department of education under the State Council, scientific research institutions may undertake the task of graduate education.

Other higher education institutions implement non-degree higher education.

Article 19 Those who have graduated from upper secondary education or have equivalent academic qualifications, and have passed the examination, may be admitted by higher education institutions implementing corresponding degree education to obtain the qualification for specialized or undergraduate students.

Those who have graduated from undergraduate education or have equivalent academic qualifications, and have passed the examination, may be admitted by higher education institutions implementing corresponding degree education or by approved scientific research institutions undertaking graduate education tasks to obtain the qualification for master's degree students.

Those who have graduated from master's degree education or have equivalent academic qualifications, and have passed the examination, may be admitted by higher education institutions implementing corresponding degree education or by approved scientific research institutions undertaking graduate education tasks to obtain the qualification for doctoral degree students.

Graduates of specific disciplines and specialties may directly obtain the qualification for doctoral degree students. The specific measures shall be prescribed by the administrative department of education under the State Council.

Article 20 Students receiving higher degree education shall be issued corresponding degree certificates or other academic certificates by their higher education institutions or approved scientific research institutions undertaking graduate education tasks, based on their duration of study, academic performance, etc., in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Students receiving non-degree higher education shall be issued corresponding completion certificates by their higher education institutions or other higher education institutions. The completion certificate shall indicate the duration of study and academic content.

Article 21 The State implements a system of self-study examinations for higher education. Those who pass the examinations shall be issued corresponding degree certificates or other academic certificates.

Article 22 The State implements a degree system. Degrees are divided into bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees.

Citizens whose academic level meets the degree standards prescribed by the State, through receiving higher education or self-study, may apply to degree-conferring units for the corresponding degree.

Article 23 Higher education institutions and other higher education institutions shall undertake the work of implementing continuing education based on social needs and their own conditions.

## Chapter III Establishment of Higher Education Institutions

Article 24 The establishment of a higher education institution shall conform to the national higher education development plan, and be in the national interest and social public interest.

Article 25 The establishment of a higher education institution shall meet the basic conditions prescribed by the Education Law.

Universities or independently established colleges shall also have relatively strong teaching and scientific research capabilities, relatively high teaching and scientific research levels and corresponding scale, and be able to implement undergraduate and above education. Universities must also have three or more disciplines prescribed by the State as their main disciplines. The specific standards for establishing higher education institutions shall be formulated by the State Council.

The specific standards for establishing other higher education institutions shall be formulated by the relevant departments authorized by the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the principles prescribed by the State Council.

Article 26 The establishment of a higher education institution shall use a corresponding name based on its level, type, categories of disciplines offered, scale, and teaching and scientific research levels.

Article 27 Those applying to establish a higher education institution shall submit the following materials to the approving authority:

(1) An application report;

(2) Feasibility study materials;

(3) The charter;

(4) Other materials required by the approving authority in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 28 The charter of a higher education institution shall stipulate the following matters:

(1) The name and address of the institution;

(2) The purpose of running the institution;

(3) The scale of the institution;

(4) The setting of disciplines;

(5) The forms of education;

(6) The internal management system;

(7) The sources of funds, property, and financial system;

(8) The rights and obligations between the sponsor and the institution;

(9) The procedure for amending the charter;

(10) Other matters that must be stipulated by the charter.

Article 29 The establishment of a higher education institution implementing undergraduate and above education shall be approved by the administrative department of education under the State Council; the establishment of a higher education institution implementing specialized education shall be approved by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and reported to the administrative department of education under the State Council for the record; the establishment of other higher education institutions shall be approved by the administrative department of education of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The approval of the establishment of higher education institutions and other higher education institutions shall comply with relevant State regulations.

The approval of the establishment of a higher education institution shall entrust a review body composed of experts to conduct a review.

The division, merger, termination, change of name, category, and other important matters of higher education institutions and other higher education institutions shall be approved by the approving authority specified in the first paragraph of this Article; the amendment of the charter shall be reported to the administrative department of education under the State Council or the administrative department of education of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval according to the management authority.

## Chapter IV Organization and Activities of Higher Education Institutions

Article 30 A higher education institution acquires the status of a legal person from the date of its approval for establishment. The president of a higher education institution is the legal representative of the higher education institution.

Higher education institutions enjoy civil rights and bear civil liability in civil activities in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Higher education institutions shall focus on cultivating talents, carry out teaching, scientific research, and social services, and ensure that the quality of education and teaching meets the standards prescribed by the State.

Article 32 Higher education institutions formulate enrollment plans and autonomously adjust the enrollment ratio of departments and specialties based on social needs, school conditions, and the school scale approved by the State.

Article 33 Higher education institutions autonomously set up and adjust disciplines and specialties in accordance with the law.

Article 34 Higher education institutions autonomously formulate teaching plans, select and compile teaching materials, and organize and implement teaching activities according to teaching needs.

Article 35 Higher education institutions autonomously carry out scientific research, technological development, and social services based on their own conditions.

The State encourages higher education institutions to engage in various forms of cooperation with enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other social organizations in scientific research, technological development, and promotion.

The State supports qualified higher education institutions to become national scientific research bases.

Article 36 Higher education institutions autonomously carry out scientific, technological, and cultural exchanges and cooperation with overseas higher education institutions in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 37 Higher education institutions autonomously determine the establishment of internal organizational structures such as teaching, scientific research, and administrative functional departments, and the allocation of personnel, based on actual needs and the principles of simplification and efficiency; in accordance with relevant State regulations, they evaluate and appoint the positions of teachers and other professional and technical personnel, and adjust allowances and salary distribution.

Article 38 Higher education institutions autonomously manage and use the property provided by the sponsor, state financial subsidies, and donated property in accordance with the law.

Higher education institutions shall not misappropriate property used for teaching and scientific research activities for other purposes.

Article 39 State-run higher education institutions implement a system of presidential responsibility under the leadership of the basic-level committee of the Communist Party of China in the higher education institution. The basic-level committee of the Communist Party of China in the higher education institution, in accordance with the Constitution of the Communist Party of China and relevant regulations, exercises unified leadership over the work of the institution, supports the president in independently and responsibly exercising their powers, and its leadership responsibilities mainly include: implementing the line, principles, and policies of the Communist Party of China, adhering to the socialist direction of running the school, leading the ideological and political work and moral education work of the institution, discussing and deciding on the establishment of internal organizational structures and the candidates for the heads of internal organizational structures, discussing and deciding on major matters such as the reform, development, and basic management system of the institution, and ensuring the completion of various tasks centered on cultivating talents.

The internal management system of higher education institutions run by social forces shall be determined in accordance with the State's regulations on running schools by social forces.

Article 40 The president of a higher education institution shall be a citizen who meets the qualifications prescribed by the Education Law. The president and vice presidents of a higher education institution shall be appointed and removed in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 41 The president of a higher education institution is fully responsible for the teaching, scientific research, and other administrative work of the institution, and exercises the following powers:

(1) Drafting development plans, formulating specific rules and regulations and annual work plans, and organizing their implementation;

(2) Organizing teaching activities, scientific research, and ideological and moral education;

(3) Drafting plans for the establishment of internal organizational structures, recommending candidates for vice presidents, and appointing and removing the heads of internal organizational structures;

(4) Appointing and dismissing teachers and other internal staff, managing student status, and implementing rewards or sanctions;

(5) Drafting and implementing annual budget plans, protecting and managing school property, and safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of the institution;

(6) Other powers stipulated by the charter.

The president of a higher education institution presides over the president's office meeting or the university affairs meeting to handle the relevant matters specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 42 A higher education institution shall establish an academic committee to perform the following duties:

(1) Reviewing discipline construction, specialty setting, and teaching and scientific research plans;

(2) Evaluating teaching and scientific research achievements;

(3) Investigating and handling academic disputes;

(4) Investigating and identifying academic misconduct;

(5) Reviewing and deciding on other matters related to academic development, academic evaluation, and academic norms in accordance with the charter.

Article 43 Higher education institutions shall, through organizational forms such as the congress of faculty and staff with teachers as the main body, ensure in accordance with the law that faculty and staff participate in democratic management and supervision, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of faculty and staff.

Article 44 Higher education institutions shall establish an evaluation system for their school-running level and education quality, promptly disclose relevant information, and accept social supervision.

The administrative department of education is responsible for organizing experts or entrusting third-party professional institutions to evaluate the school-running level, efficiency, and education quality of higher education institutions. The evaluation results shall be disclosed to the public.

## Chapter V Teachers and Other Educators in Higher Education Institutions

Article 45 Teachers and other educators in higher education institutions enjoy the rights and perform the obligations prescribed by law, and are loyal to the people's educational cause.

Article 46 A teacher qualification system is implemented in higher education institutions. Chinese citizens who abide by the Constitution and the law, love the educational cause, have good ideological and moral character, possess a graduate or undergraduate degree, have corresponding teaching and educational capabilities, and are determined to be qualified, may obtain the qualification for higher education teachers. Citizens who do not possess a graduate or undergraduate degree but have expertise in a field, pass the national teacher qualification examination, and are determined to be qualified, may also obtain the qualification for higher education teachers.

Article 47 A teacher position system is implemented in higher education institutions. Teacher positions in higher education institutions are set according to the needs of the teaching, scientific research, and other tasks undertaken by the institution. Teacher positions are set as assistant, lecturer, associate professor, and professor.

Teachers in higher education institutions who obtain the positions specified in the preceding paragraph shall meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Obtaining the qualification for higher education teachers;

(2) Systematically mastering the basic theories of the discipline;

(3) Possessing the teaching and educational capabilities and scientific research capabilities corresponding to the position;

(4) Undertaking the courses and prescribed teaching hours corresponding to the position.

In addition to meeting the above basic conditions, professors and associate professors shall also have systematic and solid basic theories of the discipline and relatively rich teaching and scientific research experience, with remarkable teaching achievements, and papers or works reaching a high level or having outstanding teaching and scientific research achievements.

The specific conditions for teacher positions in higher education institutions shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 48 A teacher appointment system is implemented in higher education institutions. After a teacher is evaluated as meeting the conditions for a position, the higher education institution shall appoint them according to the duties, conditions, and term of the teacher position.

The appointment of teachers in higher education institutions shall follow the principle of equality and voluntariness between both parties, and the president of the higher education institution shall sign an appointment contract with the appointed teacher.

Article 49 An educational staff system is implemented for management personnel in higher education institutions. A professional and technical position appointment system is implemented for teaching assistants and other professional and technical personnel in higher education institutions.

Article 50 The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of teachers and other educators in higher education institutions, and takes measures to improve the working and living conditions of teachers and other educators in higher education institutions.

Article 51 Higher education institutions shall provide convenient conditions for teachers to participate in training, carry out scientific research, and conduct academic exchanges.

Higher education institutions shall assess the ideological and political performance, professional ethics, professional level, and work performance of teachers, management personnel, teaching assistants, and other professional and technical personnel. The assessment results shall serve as the basis for appointment or dismissal, promotion, reward, or sanction.

Article 52 Teachers, management personnel, teaching assistants, and other professional and technical personnel in higher education institutions shall focus on teaching and cultivating talents and do their own work well.

## Chapter VI Students of Higher Education Institutions

Article 53 Students of higher education institutions shall abide by laws and regulations, comply with student conduct norms and various management systems of the institution, respect teachers, study diligently, strengthen physical fitness, establish patriotism, collectivism, and socialist ideology, study Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, and Deng Xiaoping Theory diligently, possess good ideological and moral character, and master high-level scientific and cultural knowledge and professional skills.

The legitimate rights and interests of students of higher education institutions are protected by law.Article 54 Students of higher education institutions shall pay tuition fees in accordance with state regulations.

Students from families with financial difficulties may apply for subsidies or tuition waivers.

Article 55 The state establishes scholarships and encourages higher education institutions, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other social entities and individuals to establish various forms of scholarships in accordance with relevant state regulations, to reward students with excellent moral character and academic performance, students majoring in state-designated fields, and students who work in state-designated regions.

The state establishes work-study funds and student loans for higher education students, and encourages higher education institutions, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other social entities and individuals to establish various forms of grants to provide assistance to students from families with financial difficulties.

Students who receive student loans or grants shall fulfill corresponding obligations.

Article 56 Students of higher education institutions may participate in social services and work-study activities during their spare time, but such activities shall not hinder the completion of their academic tasks.

Higher education institutions shall encourage and support students' social service and work-study activities, and provide guidance and management.

Article 57 Students of higher education institutions may organize student groups within the institution. Such groups shall operate within the scope prescribed by laws and regulations and shall be subject to the leadership and management of the institution.

Article 58 Students of higher education institutions who meet the requirements of ideological and moral character, complete the prescribed courses within the specified duration of study, achieve passing grades or accumulate the required credits, shall be permitted to graduate.

Article 59 Higher education institutions shall provide employment guidance and services for graduates and students who have completed coursework.

The state encourages graduates of higher education institutions to work in remote and difficult regions.

## Chapter VII Investment in Higher Education and Guarantee of Conditions

Article 60 Higher education operates under a mechanism where the sponsor primarily provides funding, the recipients reasonably share the cost of training, and higher education institutions raise funds through multiple channels.

The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with the provisions of Article 56 of the Education Law, ensure the gradual increase of funding for state-run higher education.

The state encourages enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other social entities and individuals to invest in higher education.

Article 61 Sponsors of higher education institutions shall ensure a stable source of funding for school operations and shall not withdraw the funds they have invested.

Article 62 The administrative department of education under the State Council, in conjunction with other relevant departments under the State Council, shall, based on the average annual educational cost per student, stipulate the annual expenditure standards and basic principles for fundraising for higher education institutions; the administrative departments of education under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, in conjunction with relevant departments, shall formulate the annual expenditure standards and fundraising methods for higher education institutions within their respective administrative regions, serving as the basic basis for sponsors and higher education institutions to raise funds for school operations.

Article 63 The state implements preferential policies for higher education institutions in importing books, materials, teaching and research equipment, and school-run industries. The income derived from industries run by higher education institutions or from the transfer of intellectual property rights and other scientific and technological achievements shall be used for the operation of higher education institutions.

Article 64 Tuition fees collected by higher education institutions shall be managed and used in accordance with relevant state regulations, and no other organization or individual may misappropriate them.

Article 65 Higher education institutions shall establish and improve financial management systems in accordance with the law, use educational funds reasonably, manage them strictly, and enhance the efficiency of educational investment.

The financial activities of higher education institutions shall be subject to supervision in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 66 Violations of the provisions of the Education Law in higher education activities shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Education Law.

Article 67 Individuals from outside China who meet the conditions prescribed by the state and complete the relevant procedures may enter higher education institutions within China for study, research, academic exchanges, or teaching, and their legitimate rights and interests shall be protected by the state.

Article 68 For the purposes of this Law, higher education institutions refer to universities, independently established colleges, and specialized higher education institutions, including higher vocational schools and adult higher education institutions.

For the purposes of this Law, other higher education institutions refer to organizations engaged in higher education activities other than higher education institutions and scientific research institutions approved to undertake graduate education tasks.

The provisions of this Law concerning higher education institutions shall apply to other higher education institutions and scientific research institutions approved to undertake graduate education tasks, except for provisions specifically applicable to higher education institutions.

Article 69 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 1999.
