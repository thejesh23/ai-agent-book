# Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Police Ranks for People's Police

Adopted at the 26th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on July 1, 1992

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the revolutionary, modernized, and standardized development of the People's Police force, enhance the sense of responsibility, honor, and organizational discipline of the People's Police, and facilitate the command, management, and performance of duties by the People's Police.

Article 2 The People's Police shall implement a police rank system.

Article 3 Police ranks are titles and insignia that distinguish the grades of the People's Police, indicate their status, and represent the honor conferred upon them by the State.

Article 4 The People's Police shall implement a system of police ranks corresponding to their official positions.

Article 5 A People's Police officer with a higher police rank shall be the superior of an officer with a lower police rank. When a People's Police officer with a higher police rank is subordinate in official position to an officer with a lower police rank, the officer with the higher official position shall be the superior.

Article 6 The Ministry of Public Security shall be responsible for the work concerning police ranks of the People's Police.

## Chapter II Establishment of Police Rank Grades

Article 7 The police ranks of the People's Police shall be established in the following five grades and thirteen levels:

(1) Commissioner General and Deputy Commissioner General;
(2) Police Supervisor: First Class, Second Class, Third Class;
(3) Police Superintendent: First Class, Second Class, Third Class;
(4) Police Inspector: First Class, Second Class, Third Class;
(5) Police Constable: First Class, Second Class.

For People's Police officers holding technical positions, their police rank shall be prefixed with "Technical".

Article 8 People's Police officers holding administrative positions shall have the following police ranks corresponding to their official positions:

(1) Ministerial-level Principal: Commissioner General;
(2) Ministerial-level Deputy: Deputy Commissioner General;
(3) Department (Bureau)-level Principal: First Class Police Supervisor to Second Class Police Supervisor;
(4) Department (Bureau)-level Deputy: Second Class Police Supervisor to Third Class Police Supervisor;
(5) Division (Bureau)-level Principal: Third Class Police Supervisor to Second Class Police Superintendent;
(6) Division (Bureau)-level Deputy: First Class Police Superintendent to Third Class Police Superintendent;
(7) Section (Bureau)-level Principal: First Class Police Superintendent to First Class Police Inspector;
(8) Section (Bureau)-level Deputy: Second Class Police Superintendent to Second Class Police Inspector;
(9) Section Member (Police Chief) Position: Third Class Police Superintendent to Third Class Police Inspector;
(10) Clerk (Police Constable) Position: First Class Police Inspector to Second Class Police Constable.

Article 9 People's Police officers holding technical positions shall have the following police ranks corresponding to their official positions:

(1) Senior Technical Position: First Class Police Supervisor to Second Class Police Superintendent;
(2) Intermediate Technical Position: First Class Police Superintendent to Second Class Police Inspector;
(3) Junior Technical Position: Third Class Police Superintendent to First Class Police Constable.

## Chapter III Initial Conferment of Police Ranks

Article 10 Police ranks of the People's Police shall be conferred in accordance with the police rank corresponding to their official positions.

Article 11 The conferment of police ranks on People's Police officers shall be based on their current official position, moral character and professional competence, length of service in their current position, and total years of work experience.

Article 12 Persons who graduate from schools, are recruited from society to serve as People's Police officers, or are transferred from other departments to serve as People's Police officers shall be conferred corresponding police ranks based on their designated official positions.

Article 13 The initial conferment of police ranks on People's Police officers shall be approved according to the following authorities:

(1) The ranks of Commissioner General, Deputy Commissioner General, First Class Police Supervisor, and Second Class Police Supervisor shall be approved and conferred by the Premier of the State Council;
(2) The ranks of Third Class Police Supervisor and Police Superintendent shall be approved and conferred by the Minister of Public Security;
(3) The rank of Police Inspector shall be approved and conferred by the Director of the Public Security Department (Bureau) of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government;
(4) The rank of Police Constable shall be approved and conferred by the Director of the Political Department of the Public Security Department (Bureau) of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The ranks of Police Inspector and Police Constable in the organs of the Ministry of Public Security and its directly subordinate units shall be approved and conferred by the Director of the Political Department of the Ministry of Public Security.

## Chapter IV Promotion of Police Ranks

Article 14 People's Police officers below the rank of Second Class Police Superintendent shall be promoted within the range of police ranks corresponding to their official positions, subject to the time limits and conditions stipulated in this Article.

Time limits for promotion: For promotion from Second Class Police Constable to First Class Police Inspector, each promotion shall be three years; for promotion from First Class Police Inspector to First Class Police Superintendent, each promotion shall be four years. The time spent by serving People's Police officers in training at educational institutions shall be counted within the time limit for police rank promotion.

Conditions for promotion: (1) Implement the laws, regulations, and policies of the State, and abide by discipline and the law; (2) Be competent in their duties; (3) Maintain close ties with the masses, be honest and upright in performing official duties, and have a correct work style.

When the time limit for promotion expires, those who, upon assessment, meet the conditions for promotion shall be promoted level by level; those who do not meet the conditions for promotion shall have their promotion deferred. Those who have made outstanding contributions in their work may be promoted ahead of schedule.

Article 15 The promotion of police ranks for People's Police officers at or above the rank of First Class Police Superintendent shall be conducted through selective promotion within the range of police ranks corresponding to their official positions, based on their moral character, professional competence, and work performance.

Article 16 If a People's Police officer is promoted in official position and their current police rank is lower than the minimum police rank corresponding to the new official position, their police rank shall be promoted to the minimum police rank corresponding to the new official position.

Article 17 Promotion from Police Inspector to Police Superintendent, and from Police Superintendent to Police Supervisor, may only occur after the officer has passed training at the corresponding People's Police educational institution.

Article 18 The approval authority for the promotion of police ranks of the People's Police shall be governed by the provisions of Article 13 regarding approval authority. Early promotions of Police Inspectors and Police Constables shall be approved by the Director of the Political Department of the Ministry of Public Security.

## Chapter V Retention, Demotion, and Cancellation of Police Ranks

Article 19 When People's Police officers retire or are honorably discharged, their police ranks shall be retained, but they shall not wear the insignia.

When People's Police officers are transferred from police work or resign or quit, their police ranks shall not be retained.

Article 20 If a People's Police officer is transferred to a lower official position due to incompetence in their current position, and their current police rank is higher than the maximum police rank corresponding to the new official position, their police rank shall be adjusted to the maximum police rank corresponding to the new official position. The approval authority for adjusting the police rank shall be the same as the approval authority for the original police rank.

Article 21 If a People's Police officer violates police discipline, they may be subject to the sanction of demotion in police rank. The approval authority for demotion in police rank shall be the same as the approval authority for the original police rank. After a People's Police officer receives the sanction of demotion in police rank, the time limit for their subsequent promotion in police rank shall be recalculated from the demoted police rank level.

Demotion in police rank shall not apply to Second Class Police Constables.

Article 22 If a People's Police officer is dismissed from public employment, their police rank shall be correspondingly cancelled.

If a People's Police officer commits a crime and is lawfully sentenced to deprivation of political rights or a fixed-term imprisonment or heavier penalty, their police rank shall be correspondingly cancelled.

If a retired or honorably discharged People's Police officer commits a crime, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 23 These Regulations shall apply to the police rank work of the People's Police in the State security departments, the departments for reform through labor and reeducation through labor, and the judicial police of the People's Courts and the People's Procuratorates.

The approval authority for the conferment and promotion of police ranks for the People's Police in the State security departments and the departments for reform through labor and reeducation through labor shall be stipulated by the State Council.

The approval authority for the conferment and promotion of police ranks for judicial police shall be stipulated by the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate with reference to these Regulations.

Persons in the public security departments, State security departments, and departments for reform through labor and reeducation through labor who do not hold the position of People's Police officer shall not be subject to the police rank system.

Article 24 The designs and wearing methods of police rank insignia for the People's Police shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 25 The implementing measures for these Regulations shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 26 These Regulations shall come into force on the date of their promulgation.
