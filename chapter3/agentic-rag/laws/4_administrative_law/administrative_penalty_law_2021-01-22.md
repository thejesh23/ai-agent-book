# Administrative Penalty Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 17, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

Revised at the 25th Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on January 22, 2021

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purposes of regulating the establishment and implementation of administrative penalties, ensuring and supervising the effective exercise of administrative management by administrative organs, safeguarding public interests and social order, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations.

Article 2 An administrative penalty refers to an act by which an administrative organ, in accordance with the law, imposes sanctions on a citizen, legal person, or other organization that has violated the order of administrative management, by way of reducing their rights or increasing their obligations.

Article 3 The establishment and implementation of administrative penalties shall be governed by this Law.

Article 4 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization commits an act that violates the order of administrative management and for which an administrative penalty should be imposed, such penalty shall be prescribed by laws, regulations, or rules in accordance with this Law, and shall be implemented by an administrative organ in accordance with the procedures provided for in this Law.

Article 5 The imposition of administrative penalties shall follow the principles of fairness and openness.

The establishment and implementation of an administrative penalty must be based on facts and be commensurate with the facts, nature, and circumstances of the illegal act, as well as the degree of social harm caused.

The provisions on imposing administrative penalties for illegal acts must be published; those that have not been published shall not be used as the basis for imposing administrative penalties.

Article 6 In implementing administrative penalties and correcting illegal acts, the principle of combining penalties with education shall be adhered to, and citizens, legal persons, or other organizations shall be educated to abide by the law voluntarily.

Article 7 A citizen, legal person, or other organization that is subject to an administrative penalty imposed by an administrative organ has the right to make statements and to defend themselves; if they disagree with the administrative penalty, they have the right to apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

If a citizen, legal person, or other organization suffers damage due to an illegal administrative penalty imposed by an administrative organ, they have the right to claim compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 8 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization is subject to an administrative penalty for an illegal act, and that illegal act causes damage to others, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

If an illegal act constitutes a crime and criminal liability should be pursued in accordance with the law, an administrative penalty shall not be used as a substitute for criminal punishment.

## Chapter II: Types and Establishment of Administrative Penalties

Article 9 The types of administrative penalties are:

(1) Warning, criticism through circulation of a notice;

(2) Fine, confiscation of illegal gains, confiscation of illegal property;

(3) Suspension of permits or licenses, downgrading of qualification grades, revocation of permits or licenses;

(4) Restriction on carrying out production and business activities, order to suspend production or business, order to close down, restriction on practicing a profession;

(5) Administrative detention;

(6) Other administrative penalties prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 10 Laws may establish various types of administrative penalties.

Administrative penalties that restrict personal freedom may only be established by laws.

Article 11 Administrative regulations may establish administrative penalties other than those restricting personal freedom.

Where a law has already provided for an administrative penalty for an illegal act, and an administrative regulation needs to make specific provisions, it must do so within the scope of the acts, types, and ranges of penalties for which an administrative penalty is provided for by the law.

Where a law has not provided for an administrative penalty for an illegal act, an administrative regulation may, for the purpose of implementing the law, supplementarily establish an administrative penalty. Where an administrative penalty is to be supplementarily established, opinions shall be widely solicited through hearings, demonstration meetings, or other forms, and a written explanation shall be provided to the enacting authority. When an administrative regulation is submitted for filing, the circumstances of the supplementary establishment of the administrative penalty shall be explained.

Article 12 Local regulations may establish administrative penalties other than those restricting personal freedom and revoking business licenses.

Where a law or administrative regulation has already provided for an administrative penalty for an illegal act, and a local regulation needs to make specific provisions, it must do so within the scope of the acts, types, and ranges of penalties for which an administrative penalty is provided for by the law or administrative regulation.

Where a law or administrative regulation has not provided for an administrative penalty for an illegal act, a local regulation may, for the purpose of implementing the law or administrative regulation, supplementarily establish an administrative penalty. Where an administrative penalty is to be supplementarily established, opinions shall be widely solicited through hearings, demonstration meetings, or other forms, and a written explanation shall be provided to the enacting authority. When a local regulation is submitted for filing, the circumstances of the supplementary establishment of the administrative penalty shall be explained.

Article 13 Rules formulated by the departments of the State Council may, within the scope of the acts, types, and ranges of penalties for which an administrative penalty is provided for by laws or administrative regulations, make specific provisions.

Where no law or administrative regulation has been formulated, the rules formulated by the departments of the State Council may, for acts that violate the order of administrative management, establish administrative penalties such as warnings, criticism through circulation of a notice, or fines of a certain amount. The limit of fines shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 14 Rules formulated by local governments may, within the scope of the acts, types, and ranges of penalties for which an administrative penalty is provided for by laws or regulations, make specific provisions.

Where no law or regulation has been formulated, the rules formulated by local governments may, for acts that violate the order of administrative management, establish administrative penalties such as warnings, criticism through circulation of a notice, or fines of a certain amount. The limit of fines shall be prescribed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 15 The departments of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as their relevant departments, shall regularly organize assessments of the implementation and necessity of administrative penalties, and shall propose suggestions for modification or abolition of inappropriate administrative penalty matters, types, and amounts of fines.

Article 16 Except for laws, regulations, and rules, no other normative documents may establish administrative penalties.

## Chapter III: Organs Implementing Administrative Penalties

Article 17 Administrative penalties shall be implemented by administrative organs with the power to impose administrative penalties within their statutory functions and powers.

Article 18 The State promotes the establishment of a comprehensive administrative law enforcement system in areas such as urban management, market supervision, ecological environment, cultural market, transportation, emergency management, and agriculture, and relatively centralizes the power to impose administrative penalties.

The State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government may decide that one administrative organ exercises the power to impose administrative penalties of relevant administrative organs.

The power to impose administrative penalties that restrict personal freedom may only be exercised by public security organs and other organs prescribed by law.

Article 19 Organizations authorized by laws or regulations to manage public affairs may implement administrative penalties within the scope of statutory authorization.

Article 20 An administrative organ may, in accordance with the provisions of laws, regulations, or rules, and within its statutory authority, entrust in writing an organization that meets the conditions specified in Article 21 of this Law to implement administrative penalties. An administrative organ shall not entrust other organizations or individuals to implement administrative penalties.

The letter of entrustment shall specify the specific matters, scope of authority, and term of the entrustment. The entrusting administrative organ and the entrusted organization shall make the letter of entrustment public.

The entrusting administrative organ shall supervise the implementation of administrative penalties by the entrusted organization and shall bear legal liability for the consequences of such implementation.

The entrusted organization shall implement administrative penalties in the name of the entrusting administrative organ within the scope of the entrustment; it shall not further entrust other organizations or individuals to implement administrative penalties.

Article 21 An entrusted organization must meet the following conditions:

(1) It is established in accordance with the law and has the function of managing public affairs;

(2) It has staff members who are familiar with relevant laws, regulations, rules, and business and have obtained qualifications for administrative law enforcement;

(3) If technical inspection or technical appraisal is required, it must have the conditions to organize the corresponding technical inspection or technical appraisal.

## Chapter IV: Jurisdiction and Application of Administrative Penalties

Article 22 An administrative penalty shall be under the jurisdiction of the administrative organ of the place where the illegal act occurs, unless otherwise provided by laws, administrative regulations, or departmental rules.

Article 23 An administrative penalty shall be under the jurisdiction of the administrative organ with the power to impose administrative penalties of the local people's government at or above the county level, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 24 Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local actual conditions, decide to delegate the power to impose administrative penalties of the departments of the people's governments at the county level that are urgently needed for grassroots management to township people's governments and sub-district offices that are capable of effectively undertaking them, and shall regularly organize assessments. The decision shall be made public.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices that undertake the power to impose administrative penalties shall strengthen their law enforcement capacity and implement administrative penalties within the prescribed scope and in accordance with statutory procedures.

The relevant local people's governments and their departments shall strengthen organizational coordination, business guidance, and law enforcement supervision, establish and improve mechanisms for coordination and cooperation in administrative penalties, and improve evaluation and assessment systems.

Article 25 Where two or more administrative organs have jurisdiction, the administrative organ that first files the case shall have jurisdiction.

If a dispute over jurisdiction arises, it shall be resolved through consultation; if consultation fails, it shall be submitted to the common superior administrative organ for designation of jurisdiction; it may also be directly designated by the common superior administrative organ.

Article 26 Where necessary for implementing an administrative penalty, an administrative organ may request assistance from a relevant organ. If the matter for which assistance is requested falls within the functions and powers of the requested organ, it shall provide assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 27 Where an illegal act is suspected of constituting a crime, the administrative organ shall promptly transfer the case to a judicial organ for criminal liability to be pursued in accordance with the law. Where criminal liability is not required to be pursued in accordance with the law or criminal punishment is exempted, but an administrative penalty should be imposed, the judicial organ shall promptly transfer the case to the relevant administrative organ.

Administrative penalty enforcement organs and judicial organs shall strengthen coordination and cooperation, establish and improve a case transfer system, strengthen the handover and acceptance of evidence materials, and improve the mechanism for notifying information on case handling.

Article 28 When implementing an administrative penalty, the administrative organ shall order the party concerned to correct or rectify the illegal act within a specified time limit.

Any illegal gains obtained by the party concerned shall be confiscated, except for those that should be refunded or compensated in accordance with the law. Illegal gains refer to the money obtained from committing the illegal act. Where laws, administrative regulations, or departmental rules otherwise provide for the calculation of illegal gains, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 29 For the same illegal act committed by a party, no administrative penalty of a fine shall be imposed more than once. If the same illegal act violates multiple legal norms and a fine should be imposed, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provision with the higher amount of fine.

Article 30 If a minor under the age of 14 commits an illegal act, no administrative penalty shall be imposed, but their guardian shall be ordered to discipline them; if a minor who has reached the age of 14 but not the age of 18 commits an illegal act, a lighter or mitigated administrative penalty shall be imposed.

Article 31 If a mental patient or an intellectually disabled person commits an illegal act when they are unable to recognize or control their own conduct, no administrative penalty shall be imposed, but their guardian shall be ordered to strictly supervise and treat them. If an intermittent mental patient commits an illegal act when they are in a normal mental state, an administrative penalty shall be imposed. If a mental patient or an intellectually disabled person who has not completely lost the ability to recognize or control their own conduct commits an illegal act, a lighter or mitigated administrative penalty may be imposed.

Article 32 A lighter or mitigated administrative penalty shall be imposed on a party under any of the following circumstances:

(1) They voluntarily eliminate or mitigate the harmful consequences of the illegal act;

(2) They commit the illegal act under duress or deception by others;

(3) They voluntarily confess to an illegal act not yet known to the administrative organ;

(4) They have performed meritorious service in cooperating with the administrative organ to investigate and deal with the illegal act;

(5) Other circumstances where a lighter or mitigated administrative penalty should be imposed as provided by laws, regulations, or rules.

Article 33 If an illegal act is minor and promptly corrected, and no harmful consequences have been caused, no administrative penalty shall be imposed. If it is a first-time illegal act, the harmful consequences are minor, and it is promptly corrected, no administrative penalty may be imposed.

If the party has sufficient evidence to prove that they have no subjective fault, no administrative penalty shall be imposed, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Where an administrative penalty is not imposed on a party's illegal act in accordance with the law, the administrative organ shall educate the party.

Article 34 Administrative organs may, in accordance with the law, formulate benchmarks for the discretion of administrative penalties to regulate the exercise of discretion in imposing administrative penalties. The benchmarks for the discretion of administrative penalties shall be made public.

Article 35 Where an illegal act constitutes a crime, and the people's court sentences the person to criminal detention or fixed-term imprisonment, if the administrative organ has already imposed administrative detention on the party, the term of detention shall be offset against the corresponding term of imprisonment in accordance with the law.

Where an illegal act constitutes a crime, and the people's court imposes a fine, if the administrative organ has already imposed a fine on the party, the fine shall be offset against the corresponding fine; if the administrative organ has not yet imposed a fine on the party, no fine shall be imposed.

Article 36 If an illegal act is not discovered within two years, no administrative penalty shall be imposed; if the illegal act involves the safety of citizens' lives and health or financial security and has caused harmful consequences, the aforementioned period shall be extended to five years, unless otherwise provided by law.

The period specified in the preceding paragraph shall be calculated from the date on which the illegal act occurs; if the illegal act is continuous or ongoing, it shall be calculated from the date on which the act ends.

Article 37 The implementation of an administrative penalty shall be governed by the provisions of the laws, regulations, or rules in force at the time the illegal act was committed. However, if, at the time the decision on the administrative penalty is made, the laws, regulations, or rules have been amended or repealed, and the new provisions impose a lighter penalty or do not consider the act illegal, the new provisions shall apply.

Article 38 If an administrative penalty has no legal basis or the implementing entity does not have the status of an administrative subject, the administrative penalty shall be void.

If a serious and obvious violation of statutory procedures constitutes a major and obvious illegality, the administrative penalty shall be void.

## Chapter V: Decision on Administrative Penalties

### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 39 Information such as the implementing organ of the administrative penalty, the basis for filing a case, the implementation procedures, and the channels for relief shall be made public.

Article 40 Where a citizen, legal person, or other organization commits an act that violates the order of administrative management and for which an administrative penalty should be imposed in accordance with the law, the administrative organ must ascertain the facts; if the facts of the illegal act are unclear or the evidence is insufficient, no administrative penalty shall be imposed.

Article 41 Where an administrative organ uses electronic technology monitoring equipment to collect and fix evidence of illegal acts in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, it shall undergo legal and technical review to ensure that the electronic technology monitoring equipment meets standards, is reasonably placed, and has clear signs, and the location of placement shall be made public.

The records of illegal acts made by electronic technology monitoring equipment shall be truthful, clear, complete, and accurate. The administrative organ shall review whether the content of the records meets the requirements; records that have not been reviewed or have been reviewed but do not meet the requirements shall not be used as evidence for administrative penalties.

The administrative organ shall promptly inform the party of the facts of the illegal act and take information-based measures or other measures to facilitate the party's inquiry, statement, and defense. It shall not restrict or covertly restrict the party's right to make statements and to defend themselves.

Article 42 Administrative penalties shall be implemented by law enforcement personnel who have obtained qualifications for administrative law enforcement. The number of law enforcement personnel shall not be less than two, unless otherwise provided by law.

Law enforcement personnel shall enforce the law in a civilized manner, respecting and protecting the lawful rights and interests of the parties.

Article 43 If a law enforcement personnel has a direct interest in the case or has other relationships that may affect impartial law enforcement, they shall recuse themselves.

If a party believes that a law enforcement personnel has a direct interest in the case or has other relationships that may affect impartial law enforcement, they have the right to apply for their recusal.

If a party applies for recusal, the administrative organ shall review it in accordance with the law, and the decision shall be made by the head of the administrative organ. The investigation shall not be suspended before the decision is made.

Article 44 Before making a decision on an administrative penalty, the administrative organ shall inform the party of the content of the proposed administrative penalty, as well as the facts, reasons, and basis for it, and shall inform the party of their rights to make statements, to defend themselves, and to request a hearing in accordance with the law.

Article 45 The party has the right to make statements and to defend themselves. The administrative organ must fully listen to the party's opinions and shall review the facts, reasons, and evidence presented by the party; if the facts, reasons, or evidence presented by the party are established, the administrative organ shall adopt them.

The administrative organ shall not impose a heavier penalty because the party has made statements or defended themselves.

Article 46 Evidence includes:

(1) Documentary evidence;

(2) Physical evidence;

(3) Audio-visual materials;

(4) Electronic data;

(5) Witness testimony;

(6) Statements of the party;

(7) Expert opinions;

(8) Records of inspection and records of the scene.

Evidence must be verified before it can be used as the basis for determining the facts of a case.

Evidence obtained by illegal means shall not be used as the basis for determining the facts of a case.

Article 47 Administrative organs shall, in accordance with the law, record the entire process of initiating an administrative penalty, investigating and collecting evidence, reviewing, deciding, serving, and executing it, in the form of text, audio-visual recordings, etc., and shall archive and preserve such records.

Article 48 Decisions on administrative penalties that have a certain social impact shall be made public in accordance with the law.

If a publicized decision on an administrative penalty is lawfully modified, revoked, confirmed as illegal, or confirmed as void, the administrative organ shall withdraw the information on the administrative penalty decision within three days and publicly explain the reasons.

Article 49 In the event of a major infectious disease epidemic or other emergency, in order to control, mitigate, and eliminate the social harm caused by the emergency, the administrative organ shall, in accordance with the law, impose penalties quickly and severely on acts that violate the emergency response measures.

Article 50 Administrative organs and their staff members shall keep confidential, in accordance with the law, any state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy that they become aware of during the implementation of administrative penalties.

### Section 2: Summary Procedure

Article 51 Where the facts of an illegal act are conclusive and there is a legal basis, and the administrative penalty is a fine of not more than 200 yuan for a citizen, not more than 3,000 yuan for a legal person or other organization, or a warning, the decision on the administrative penalty may be made on the spot, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 52 When a law enforcement personnel makes a decision on an administrative penalty on the spot, they shall present their law enforcement credentials to the party, fill in a pre-formatted, numbered written decision on the administrative penalty, and deliver it to the party on the spot. If the party refuses to sign for receipt, this shall be noted on the written decision on the administrative penalty.

The written decision on the administrative penalty specified in the preceding paragraph shall state the party's illegal act, the type and basis of the administrative penalty, the amount of the fine, the time and place, the channels and time limits for applying for administrative reconsideration or initiating an administrative lawsuit, and the name of the administrative organ, and shall be signed or sealed by the law enforcement personnel.

A decision on an administrative penalty made on the spot by a law enforcement personnel shall be reported to the administrative organ to which they belong for filing.

Article 53 For a decision on an administrative penalty made on the spot, the party shall perform it in accordance with the provisions of Articles 67 to 69 of this Law.

### Section 3: Ordinary Procedure

Article 54 Except for administrative penalties that may be made on the spot as provided for in Article 51 of this Law, when an administrative organ discovers that a citizen, legal person, or other organization has committed an act for which an administrative penalty should be imposed in accordance with the law, it must conduct a comprehensive, objective, and impartial investigation and collect relevant evidence; when necessary, it may conduct an inspection in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.If the case meets the criteria for filing, the administrative agency shall promptly file the case.

Article 55 When conducting investigations or inspections, law enforcement officers shall proactively present their law enforcement credentials to the parties or relevant individuals. The parties or relevant individuals have the right to request that law enforcement officers present their credentials. If law enforcement officers fail to present their credentials, the parties or relevant individuals have the right to refuse to accept the investigation or inspection.

The parties or relevant individuals shall truthfully answer inquiries and assist with investigations or inspections, and shall not refuse or obstruct them. Records shall be made of inquiries or inspections.

Article 56 When collecting evidence, administrative agencies may use sampling methods; if evidence may be lost or become difficult to obtain later, with the approval of the head of the administrative agency, it may be registered and preserved in advance, and a decision on its handling shall be made within seven days. During this period, the parties or relevant individuals shall not destroy or transfer the evidence.

Article 57 Upon conclusion of an investigation, the head of the administrative agency shall review the investigation results and, based on the circumstances, make one of the following decisions:

(1) If there is indeed an illegal act subject to administrative penalty, an administrative penalty decision shall be made based on the severity and specific circumstances of the act;

(2) If the illegal act is minor and may be exempted from administrative penalty according to law, no administrative penalty shall be imposed;

(3) If the facts of the illegal act cannot be established, no administrative penalty shall be imposed;

(4) If the illegal act is suspected of constituting a crime, it shall be transferred to the judicial authorities.

For complex cases or major illegal acts subject to administrative penalty, the head of the administrative agency shall make the decision through collective discussion.

Article 58 In any of the following circumstances, before the head of the administrative agency makes a decision on an administrative penalty, a legal review shall be conducted by personnel responsible for the legal review of administrative penalty decisions; if no legal review is conducted or the review is not passed, no decision shall be made:

(1) Involving major public interests;

(2) Directly affecting the major rights and interests of the parties or third parties, and a hearing procedure has been conducted;

(3) The case is complex and difficult, involving multiple legal relationships;

(4) Other circumstances where legal review is required by laws, regulations, or rules.

Personnel conducting legal review of administrative penalty decisions for the first time in an administrative agency shall have obtained legal professional qualifications through the national unified legal professional qualification examination.

Article 59 When imposing an administrative penalty in accordance with Article 57 of this Law, the administrative agency shall produce a written decision on the administrative penalty. The written decision shall include the following:

(1) The name or title and address of the party;

(2) The facts and evidence of the violation of laws, regulations, or rules;

(3) The type and legal basis of the administrative penalty;

(4) The method and deadline for fulfilling the administrative penalty;

(5) The channels and deadlines for applying for administrative reconsideration or filing an administrative lawsuit;

(6) The name of the administrative agency making the decision and the date of the decision.

The written decision on the administrative penalty must bear the seal of the administrative agency making the decision.

Article 60 The administrative agency shall make a decision on the administrative penalty within 90 days from the date of filing the case. If otherwise provided by laws, regulations, or rules, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 61 The written decision on the administrative penalty shall be delivered to the party on the spot after being announced; if the party is not present, the administrative agency shall serve the written decision on the party within seven days in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

If the party agrees and signs a confirmation letter, the administrative agency may serve the written decision on the administrative penalty by fax, email, or other means.

Article 62 Before making a decision on an administrative penalty, if the administrative agency and its law enforcement officers fail to inform the party of the content, facts, reasons, and legal basis of the proposed administrative penalty in accordance with Articles 44 and 45 of this Law, or refuse to hear the party's statements or defenses, no decision on the administrative penalty shall be made; except where the party explicitly waives the right to make statements or defenses.

### Section 4: Hearing Procedure

Article 63 When an administrative agency intends to make one of the following administrative penalty decisions, it shall inform the party of the right to request a hearing; if the party requests a hearing, the administrative agency shall organize one:

(1) A relatively large fine;

(2) Confiscation of a relatively large amount of illegal gains or a relatively large value of illegal property;

(3) Downgrading of qualification level or revocation of a license;

(4) Ordering suspension of production or business, ordering closure, or restricting engagement in a profession;

(5) Other relatively severe administrative penalties;

(6) Other circumstances specified by laws, regulations, or rules.

The party shall not bear the costs of the hearing organized by the administrative agency.

Article 64 Hearings shall be organized in accordance with the following procedures:

(1) If the party requests a hearing, it shall be made within five days after being informed by the administrative agency;

(2) The administrative agency shall notify the party and relevant individuals of the time and place of the hearing at least seven days before it is held;

(3) The hearing shall be held publicly, except where state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy are involved and are kept confidential according to law;

(4) The hearing shall be presided over by a person designated by the administrative agency who is not an investigator of the case; if the party believes the presiding officer has a direct interest in the case, it has the right to apply for their recusal;

(5) The party may attend the hearing in person or entrust one or two agents;

(6) If the party or its agents, without justifiable reasons, refuse to attend the hearing or withdraw from it without permission, they shall be deemed to have waived the right to a hearing, and the administrative agency shall terminate the hearing;

(7) During the hearing, the investigator shall present the facts, evidence, and proposed administrative penalty for the party's illegal act, and the party shall present its defense and cross-examine;

(8) A record of the hearing shall be made. The record shall be submitted to the party or its agent for verification and signature or seal. If the party or its agent refuses to sign or seal, the presiding officer shall note this in the record.

Article 65 After the hearing, the administrative agency shall make a decision based on the hearing record in accordance with Article 57 of this Law.

## Chapter 6: Enforcement of Administrative Penalties

Article 66 After an administrative penalty decision is lawfully made, the party shall fulfill it within the period specified in the written decision.

If the party genuinely has financial difficulties and needs an extension or installment payment of the fine, upon the party's application and approval by the administrative agency, payment may be deferred or made in installments.

Article 67 The administrative agency that imposes the fine shall be separate from the institution that collects the fine.

Except for fines collected on the spot in accordance with Articles 68 and 69 of this Law, the administrative agency that makes the administrative penalty decision and its law enforcement officers shall not collect fines themselves.

The party shall pay the fine to the designated bank or through an electronic payment system within 15 days from the date of receiving the written decision on the administrative penalty. The bank shall accept the fine and turn it over directly to the state treasury.

Article 68 When an administrative penalty decision is made on the spot in accordance with Article 51 of this Law, and in any of the following circumstances, law enforcement officers may collect the fine on the spot:

(1) A fine of not more than 100 yuan is imposed according to law;

(2) It would be difficult to enforce the fine if not collected on the spot.

Article 69 In remote areas, on water, or in areas with inconvenient transportation, after the administrative agency and its law enforcement officers make a fine decision in accordance with Articles 51 and 57 of this Law, if the party genuinely has difficulty paying the fine to the designated bank or through an electronic payment system, and upon the party's request, the administrative agency and its law enforcement officers may collect the fine on the spot.

Article 70 When an administrative agency and its law enforcement officers collect a fine on the spot, they must issue a special receipt uniformly printed by the finance department of the State Council or the finance department of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; if no such receipt is issued, the party has the right to refuse to pay the fine.

Article 71 Law enforcement officers who collect fines on the spot shall turn them over to the administrative agency within two days from the date of collection; fines collected on the spot on water shall be turned over to the administrative agency within two days from the date of docking; the administrative agency shall pay the fines to the designated bank within two days.

Article 72 If the party fails to fulfill the administrative penalty decision within the time limit, the administrative agency that made the decision may take the following measures:

(1) For fines not paid when due, an additional fine of 3% of the fine amount per day shall be imposed, with the total additional fine not exceeding the original fine amount;

(2) According to legal provisions, auction or lawfully dispose of sealed or seized property, or transfer frozen deposits or remittances to offset the fine;

(3) According to legal provisions, take other administrative enforcement measures;

(4) Apply to the People's Court for enforcement in accordance with the Administrative Compulsion Law of the People's Republic of China.

If the administrative agency approves an extension or installment payment of the fine, the period for applying to the People's Court for enforcement shall be calculated from the end of the period for deferred or installment payment.

Article 73 If the party disagrees with the administrative penalty decision and applies for administrative reconsideration or files an administrative lawsuit, the administrative penalty shall not be suspended, except as otherwise provided by law.

If the party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision restricting personal freedom and applies for administrative reconsideration or files an administrative lawsuit, it may apply to the agency that made the decision for a suspension of enforcement. If the legal conditions are met, enforcement shall be suspended.

If the party applies for administrative reconsideration or files an administrative lawsuit, the additional fine shall not be calculated during the period of administrative reconsideration or administrative lawsuit.

Article 74 Except for items that should be destroyed according to law, lawfully confiscated illegal property must be publicly auctioned or disposed of in accordance with state regulations.

Fines, confiscated illegal gains, or proceeds from the auction of confiscated illegal property must all be turned over to the state treasury; no administrative agency or individual may intercept, privately distribute, or covertly distribute them in any form.

Fines, confiscated illegal gains, or proceeds from the auction of confiscated illegal property shall not be directly or indirectly linked to the assessment or evaluation of the administrative agency that made the administrative penalty decision and its staff. Except for amounts that should be refunded or compensated according to law, the finance department shall not return fines, confiscated illegal gains, or proceeds from the auction of confiscated illegal property to the administrative agency that made the decision in any form.

Article 75 Administrative agencies shall establish and improve supervision systems for administrative penalties. People's governments at or above the county level shall regularly organize evaluations and assessments of administrative law enforcement, strengthen supervision and inspection of administrative penalties, and standardize and ensure the implementation of administrative penalties.

Administrative agencies implementing administrative penalties shall accept social supervision. Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations have the right to appeal or report the conduct of administrative agencies in implementing administrative penalties; administrative agencies shall carefully review such reports and, if errors are found, shall proactively correct them.

## Chapter 7: Legal Liability

Article 76 If an administrative agency implements an administrative penalty in any of the following circumstances, the superior administrative agency or relevant authority shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Without a legal basis for the administrative penalty;

(2) Unauthorized changes to the type or scope of the administrative penalty;

(3) Violation of the legal procedures for administrative penalties;

(4) Violation of the provisions of Article 20 of this Law on delegated penalties;

(5) Law enforcement officers have not obtained law enforcement credentials.

If an administrative agency fails to promptly file a case that meets the filing criteria, it shall be handled in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 77 If an administrative agency, when imposing a penalty on a party, fails to use receipts for fines or confiscated property, or uses receipts printed by non-statutory departments, the party has the right to refuse and to report the matter; the superior administrative agency or relevant authority shall confiscate and destroy the illegal receipts used, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 78 If an administrative agency violates Article 67 of this Law by collecting fines itself, or if the finance department violates Article 74 of this Law by returning fines, confiscated illegal gains, or auction proceeds to the administrative agency, the superior administrative agency or relevant authority shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 79 If an administrative agency intercepts, privately distributes, or covertly distributes fines, confiscated illegal gains, or property, the finance department or relevant authority shall recover them, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If a law enforcement officer, taking advantage of their position, solicits or accepts property from others, or appropriates collected fines for themselves, and this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, sanctions shall be given according to law.

Article 80 If an administrative agency uses or damages sealed or seized property, causing losses to the party, it shall compensate according to law, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 81 If an administrative agency illegally implements inspection or enforcement measures, causing harm to the person or property of citizens, or causing losses to legal persons or other organizations, it shall compensate according to law, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 82 If an administrative agency fails to transfer a case that should be transferred to judicial authorities for criminal liability according to law, substituting an administrative penalty for criminal punishment, the superior administrative agency or relevant authority shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 83 If an administrative agency fails to stop or penalize illegal acts that should be stopped or penalized, causing damage to the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations, public interests, or social order, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if the circumstances are serious enough to constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter 8: Supplementary Provisions

Article 84 If a foreign national, stateless person, or foreign organization commits an illegal act within the territory of the People's Republic of China and should be given an administrative penalty, this Law shall apply, except as otherwise provided by law.

Article 85 The references to "two days," "three days," "five days," and "seven days" in this Law mean working days, excluding legal holidays.

Article 86 This Law shall take effect on July 15, 2021.
