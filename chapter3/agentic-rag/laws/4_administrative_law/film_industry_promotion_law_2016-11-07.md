# Film Industry Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to promote the healthy and prosperous development of the film industry, to carry forward the core socialist values, to regulate the order of the film market, and to enrich the spiritual and cultural life of the people.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities such as film creation, production, distribution, and screening (hereinafter collectively referred to as film activities) conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "film" means a work that is produced using audiovisual technology and artistic means, recorded on film or digital carriers, composed of continuous sound or silent images expressing certain content, conforming to the technical standards prescribed by the State, and publicly screened in fixed venues such as cinemas or using mobile screening equipment.

Where films are disseminated through information networks such as the Internet, telecommunications networks, or radio and television networks, the provisions of laws and administrative regulations governing the management of such information networks shall also be complied with.

Article 3 In conducting film activities, one shall adhere to serving the people and serving socialism, give priority to social benefits, and achieve the unity of social and economic benefits.

Article 4 The State adheres to a people-centered creative orientation, upholds the principle of letting a hundred flowers bloom and a hundred schools of thought contend, respects and guarantees freedom of film creation, advocates that film creation be close to reality, life, and the masses, and encourages the creation of excellent films that integrate ideological content, artistic quality, and watchability.
Article 5 The State Council shall incorporate the development of the film industry into the national economic and social development plan. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on local conditions, incorporate the development of the film industry into their respective national economic and social development plans.

The State formulates policies for the film and related industries to guide the formation of a unified, open, and fair competitive film market, and to promote the prosperity and development of the film market.

Article 6 The State encourages the research, development, and application of film technology, formulates and improves film technical standards, and builds a film technology innovation system with enterprises as the main body, market orientation, and the integration of industry, academia, and research.

Article 7 Intellectual property rights related to films are protected by law, and no organization or individual may infringe upon them.

The departments responsible for intellectual property law enforcement of people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to protect intellectual property rights related to films and investigate and punish acts of infringement upon intellectual property rights related to films according to law.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations engaged in film activities shall enhance their awareness of intellectual property rights and improve their ability to use, protect, and manage intellectual property rights.

The State encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to develop derivative products such as film character products according to law.

Article 8 The film regulatory department of the State Council shall be responsible for the administration of films nationwide; the film regulatory departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the administration of films within their respective administrative regions.
Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for related film work within their respective functions and duties.

Article 9 Film industry organizations shall formulate industry self-discipline norms according to law, conduct professional exchanges, strengthen professional ethics education, and safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of their members.

Film practitioners such as actors and directors shall adhere to the principle of having both moral integrity and artistic excellence, abide by laws and regulations, respect social morality, observe professional ethics, strengthen self-discipline, and establish a good social image.
Article 10 The State supports the establishment of a film evaluation system and encourages film criticism.

Outstanding films and organizations or individuals that have made outstanding contributions to promoting the development of the film industry shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 11 The State encourages equal and mutually beneficial international cooperation and exchange in the film industry and supports participation in overseas film festivals (exhibitions).

## Chapter II Film Creation and Production

Article 12 The State encourages innovation in film script creation, themes, genres, forms, and means, and encourages academic seminars and professional exchanges in the film industry.

The film competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of film creation, provide necessary convenience and assistance for film creators to go to the grassroots, approach the masses, and experience life.

Article 13 Legal persons or other organizations intending to produce a film shall file a synopsis of the film script with the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; where the film involves major themes or themes related to national security, foreign affairs, ethnicity, religion, or military affairs, the film script shall be submitted for review in accordance with relevant State regulations.

If the film script synopsis or film script complies with the provisions of Article 16 of this Law, the film competent department of the State Council shall announce the basic information of the intended film, and the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall issue a filing certificate or an approval document. Specific measures shall be formulated by the film competent department of the State Council.

Article 14 With the approval of the film competent department of the State Council, legal persons or other organizations may co-produce films with overseas organizations; however, they shall not co-produce with overseas organizations engaged in activities that harm the dignity, honor, or interests of the State, endanger social stability, or hurt national sentiments, nor shall they employ individuals who have engaged in such activities to participate in film production.

If a co-produced film meets the requirements regarding the proportion of creation, investment, and profit distribution, the film shall be deemed as a film produced by a domestic legal person or other organization.

Overseas organizations shall not independently engage in film production activities within the territory of China; overseas individuals shall not engage in film production activities within the territory of China.

Article 15 The film competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall coordinate with departments such as public security, cultural relics protection, and scenic area management to provide necessary convenience and assistance for legal persons or other organizations to engage in film production activities in accordance with this Law.

Those engaged in film production activities shall comply with laws and regulations on environmental protection, cultural relics protection, scenic area management, and production safety, and shall take necessary protection and prevention measures during the production process.

Article 16 Films shall not contain any of the following content:

(1) Content that violates the basic principles established by the Constitution, incites resistance to or undermines the implementation of the Constitution, laws, or administrative regulations;
(2) Content that endangers national unity, sovereignty, or territorial integrity, divulges State secrets, endangers national security, harms the dignity, honor, or interests of the State, or promotes terrorism or extremism;
(3) Content that denigrates the excellent cultural traditions of ethnic groups, incites ethnic hatred or discrimination, infringes upon ethnic customs and habits, distorts ethnic history or historical figures, hurts ethnic sentiments, or undermines ethnic unity;
(4) Content that incites the undermining of State religious policies or promotes cults or superstition;
(5) Content that endangers social morality, disrupts social order, undermines social stability, promotes obscenity, gambling, or drug use, glorifies violence or terror, incites crime or teaches criminal methods;
(6) Content that infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of minors or harms their physical or mental health;
(7) Content that insults or slanders others or disseminates others' privacy, infringing upon their legitimate rights and interests;
(8) Other content prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 17 Legal persons or other organizations shall submit their completed films for review to the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall make a review decision within 30 days from the date of accepting the application. If the film complies with the provisions of this Law, it shall be approved for public screening, a film public screening permit shall be issued, and the decision shall be announced; if the film does not comply with the provisions of this Law, it shall not be approved for public screening, and the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons stated.

The film competent department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law, formulate and improve the specific standards and procedures for film review and make them public. When formulating and improving the specific standards for film review, public opinions shall be solicited, and experts shall be organized for deliberation.
Article 18 When conducting film review, no fewer than five experts shall be organized to conduct the review, and the experts shall provide review opinions. If a legal person or other organization has objections to the expert review opinions, the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may organize another group of experts for a second review. The expert review opinions shall serve as an important basis for making the review decision.

The review experts mentioned in the preceding paragraph include experts from the expert database and experts specially invited based on the film's theme. Specific measures for expert selection and review shall be formulated by the film competent department of the State Council.

Article 19 If the content of a film that has obtained a film public screening permit needs to be changed, it shall be resubmitted for review in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 20 Legal persons or other organizations producing films shall place the obtained film public screening permit mark at the beginning of the film; if the screening of the film may cause physical or psychological discomfort to audiences such as minors, a warning shall be given.

Films that have not obtained a film public screening permit shall not be distributed, screened, disseminated through information networks such as the Internet, telecommunications networks, or radio and television networks, or produced as audio-visual products; however, if the State has other provisions, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 21 A completed film may only participate in film festivals (exhibitions) after obtaining a film public screening permit. If it intends to participate in an overseas film festival (exhibition), the legal person or other organization submitting the film shall, before the holding of the overseas film festival (exhibition), report the relevant materials to the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for filing.

Article 22 Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations may undertake businesses such as developing, processing, and post-production of overseas films, and shall report to the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for filing; however, they shall not undertake related businesses for overseas films that contain content harming the dignity, honor, or interests of the State, endangering social stability, or hurting national sentiments.

Article 23 The film archive institution established by the State shall, according to law, receive, collect, organize, preserve, and make film archives available to the public.

The film archive institution established by the State shall equip necessary equipment, adopt advanced technology, and improve the modernization level of film archive management.

Legal persons or other organizations producing films shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Archives Law of the People's Republic of China, properly preserve film archives and transfer, donate, or deposit film archives to the film archive institution established by the State.

## Chapter III Film Distribution and Screening

Article 24 If an enterprise has personnel and funds suitable for the film distribution activities it intends to engage in, it may engage in film distribution activities upon approval by the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where it is located.

If an enterprise or individual industrial and commercial household has personnel, premises, technology, and equipment suitable for the film screening activities it intends to engage in, it may engage in film screening activities in fixed venues such as cinemas upon approval by the film competent department of the local people's government at the county level.

Article 25 The film competent department responsible for approving film distribution and screening activities in accordance with this Law shall make a decision to approve or disapprove within 30 days from the date of accepting the application. If the conditions are met, approval shall be granted, a film distribution business license or a film screening business license shall be issued, and the decision shall be announced; if the conditions are not met, approval shall not be granted, and the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons stated.

Article 26 Enterprises or individuals engaged in mobile film screening activities shall file the enterprise name or operator's name, address, contact information, and screening equipment with the film competent department of the local people's government at the county level in the area of operation.

Article 27 The State increases support for film screening in rural areas, with government funding to establish and improve a public welfare film screening service network in rural areas, actively guide social funds to invest in rural film screening, continuously improve the conditions for watching films in rural areas, and comprehensively ensure the needs of rural residents for watching films.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate public welfare film screening in rural areas into the construction of the rural public cultural service system and provide subsidies for public welfare film screening activities in rural areas in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Those engaged in public welfare film screening activities in rural areas shall not use fraudulent means such as false reporting or fraudulent claims to obtain subsidies for public welfare film screening in rural areas.

Article 28 The competent education and film departments of the State Council may jointly recommend films conducive to the healthy growth of minors and take measures to support free viewing by students receiving compulsory education, organized and arranged by their schools.

The State encourages cinemas and enterprises or individuals engaged in mobile film screening activities to adopt various measures such as ticket price concessions, building screening halls with different conditions, and setting up community screening points to provide convenience for minors, the elderly, the disabled, urban low-income residents, and migrant workers to watch films; the local people's government where the cinema or the enterprise or individual engaged in mobile film screening activities is located may issue incentive subsidies to them.

Article 29 Cinemas shall reasonably arrange the screening sessions and time slots for films produced by domestic legal persons or other organizations, and the total screening duration of such films shall not be less than two-thirds of the total annual film screening duration.

Cinemas and enterprises or individuals engaged in mobile film screening activities shall ensure the quality of film screening.

Article 30 The facilities and equipment of cinemas and equipment used for mobile screening shall comply with the national standards for film screening technology.

Cinemas shall install computerized ticketing systems in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 31 Without the permission of the rights holder, no one may record audio or video of a film being screened. If such recording is discovered, cinema staff have the right to stop it and demand its deletion; if the person refuses to comply, the staff have the right to demand their departure.

Article 32 The State encourages cinemas to screen public service announcements before the announced start time of the film.

Cinemas shall not screen advertisements after the announced start time of the film until the end of the film screening.

Article 33 Cinemas shall comply with laws and administrative regulations on public security, fire protection, and public place hygiene, maintain public order and environmental hygiene in screening venues, and ensure the safety and health of audiences.

No one may bring explosive, flammable, radioactive, toxic, or corrosive items into screening venues such as cinemas, nor illegally bring firearms, ammunition, or controlled instruments into such venues; if the illegal carrying of such items is discovered, relevant staff shall refuse entry and report to the relevant departments.
Article 34 Film distribution enterprises, cinemas, etc., shall truthfully count film sales revenue, provide true and accurate statistical data, and shall not use improper means such as creating false transactions, falsely reporting or concealing sales revenue to deceive or mislead audiences or disrupt the order of the film market.

Article 35 Holding an international film festival (exhibition) within the territory of China requires approval from the film competent department of the State Council or the film competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter IV Film Industry Support and Guarantees

Article 36 The State supports the creation and production of the following films:

(1) Films on major themes that disseminate excellent Chinese culture and carry forward the core socialist values;
(2) Films that promote the healthy growth of minors;
(3) Films that showcase artistic innovation achievements and promote artistic progress;
(4) Films that promote the development of science and education and the popularization of science and technology;
(5) Other films that meet the State's support policies.

Article 37 The State guides relevant cultural industry special funds and funds to increase investment in the film industry, and based on the development situation of the film industry at different stages and periods, combined with financial conditions and economic and social development needs, comprehensively considers and arranges financial funds to support the film industry, and strengthens the audit of the use of relevant funds and funds.

Article 38 The State implements necessary tax preferential policies to promote the development of the film industry. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent finance and taxation departments of the State Council in accordance with tax laws and administrative regulations.

Article 39 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of the people and the development of the film market, incorporate the construction and renovation of cinemas into the national economic and social development plan, the overall land use plan, and the urban and rural plan.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, effectively guarantee the land demand for cinemas, actively revitalize existing cinema land resources, and support the construction and renovation of cinemas.

Article 40 The State encourages financial institutions to provide financing services for film activities and the improvement of film infrastructure, to carry out intellectual property pledge financing businesses related to films according to law, and to support the development of the film industry through credit and other means.

The State encourages insurance institutions to develop insurance products suitable for the development needs of the film industry according to law.

The State encourages financing guarantee institutions to provide financing guarantees to the film industry according to law, and to disperse risks through methods such as re-guarantee, joint guarantee, and the combination of guarantee and insurance.

For the production of films announced by the film competent department of the State Council in accordance with this Law, the loan term and interest rate shall be reasonably determined in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 41 The State encourages legal persons and other organizations to make cross-border investments through methods such as co-producing films with overseas partners, and guarantees their reasonable foreign exchange needs for foreign trade, cross-border financing, and investment according to law.

Article 42 The State implements a film talent support plan.

The State supports qualified institutions of higher learning, secondary vocational schools, and other educational institutions and training institutions to offer film-related majors and courses, and to adopt various methods to cultivate talents suitable for the development needs of the film industry.

The State encourages legal persons and other organizations engaged in film activities to participate in the cultivation of relevant talents in schools.

Article 43 The State takes measures to support film activities in rural areas, border areas, poverty-stricken areas, and ethnic minority areas.

The State encourages and supports the creation of films on ethnic minority themes, strengthens the dubbing and subtitling of films in ethnic minority languages, and comprehensively ensures the needs of residents in ethnic minority areas for watching films.

Article 44 The State supports the foreign language translation and production of excellent films, and comprehensively utilizes diplomatic, cultural, educational, and other foreign exchange resources to carry out overseas promotion activities for films.

The State encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to engage in the overseas promotion of films.

Article 45 The State encourages social forces to support the development of the film industry through donations, funding, and other means, and grants preferential treatment according to law.

Article 46 The film competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen daily supervision and management of film activities, accept complaints and reports of violations of the provisions of this Law, and promptly verify, handle, and respond; record the circumstances where units and individuals engaged in film activities have been subject to administrative penalties for violating the provisions of this Law in their credit files, and make them public.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

**Article 47** Where anyone, in violation of this Law, engages in the production, distribution, or screening of films without authorization, the film department of the people's government at or above the county level shall ban the activity, confiscate the films, illegal gains, and the special tools and equipment used for the illegal activity; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 250,000 RMB may also be imposed.
**Article 48** Under any of the following circumstances, the original issuing authority shall revoke the relevant license or cancel the relevant approval or certification document; the film主管部门 of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 250,000 RMB may also be imposed:
(1) Forging, altering, leasing, lending, or selling the licenses, approvals, or certification documents provided for in this Law, or illegally transferring such licenses, approvals, or certification documents in any other form;

(2) Obtaining the licenses, approvals, or certification documents provided for in this Law by improper means such as deception or bribery.

**Article 49** Under any of the following circumstances, the original issuing authority shall revoke the license; the film主管部门 of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the films and illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 500,000 RMB may also be imposed:
(1) Distributing or screening films that have not obtained a film public screening license;

(2) After obtaining a film public screening license, changing the film content and distributing, screening, or submitting it for exhibition without re-obtaining the film public screening license as required;

(3) Providing films that have not obtained a film public screening license for participation in film festivals (exhibitions).

**Article 50** Where any entity engages in the development, processing, or post-production of foreign films that contain content harming the dignity, honor, and interests of our country, endangering social stability, or hurting national sentiments, the film regulatory department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal activity, confiscate the films and illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 150,000 RMB may also be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the film regulatory department shall notify the administrative department for industry and commerce, which shall revoke the business license.
**Article 51** Where a film distribution enterprise, cinema, or the like engages in acts such as fabricating false transactions, falsely reporting or concealing sales revenue, thereby disrupting the order of the film market, the film主管部门 of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 RMB but not more than 500,000 RMB; if the illegal gains are 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the entity shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification; if the circumstances are especially serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke the license.
Where a cinema screens advertisements after the movie start time indicated to the audience and before the end of the film screening, the film administrative department of the people's government at the county level shall give a warning and order correction; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 RMB but not more than 50,000 RMB shall be imposed.
**Article 52** Where a legal person or other organization holds a foreign-related film festival (exhibition) within the territory without authorization, the film department of the State Council or the film department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order the cessation of the illegal activity, confiscate the films exhibited and the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 250,000 RMB may also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the entity shall be prohibited from holding foreign-related film festivals (exhibitions) for five years from the date of receiving the penalty.
Where an individual holds a foreign-related film festival (exhibition) within the territory without authorization, or provides films that have not obtained a film public screening license for participation in a film festival (exhibition) without authorization, the film department of the State Council or the film department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order the cessation of the illegal activity, confiscate the films exhibited and the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 250,000 RMB may also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the individual shall be prohibited from engaging in related film activities for five years from the date of receiving the penalty.
**Article 53** Where a legal person, other organization, or individual industrial and commercial household has its license revoked for violating this Law, it shall be prohibited from engaging in the relevant business activities for five years from the date of revocation; its legal representative or principal responsible person shall be prohibited from serving as the legal representative or principal responsible person of a legal person or other organization engaged in film activities for five years from the date of revocation.

**Article 54** Under any of the following circumstances, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state regulations:

(1) In violation of relevant state regulations, producing films that have not obtained a film public screening license into audio-visual products without authorization;

(2) In violation of relevant state regulations, disseminating films that have not obtained a film public screening license through information networks such as the internet, telecommunications networks, or radio and television networks without authorization;

(3) Obtaining subsidies for rural film public welfare screening by fraudulent means such as false reporting or impersonation;

(4) Infringing upon intellectual property rights related to films;

(5) Failing to receive, collect, sort, keep, or hand over film archives as required by law.

Where a cinema commits the act specified in item (4) of the preceding paragraph and the circumstances are serious, the original issuing authority shall revoke its license.

**Article 55** Where a staff member of the film administration department of a people's government at or above the county level or any other relevant department commits any of the following acts, which does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law:
(1) Taking advantage of their position to accept property or other benefits from others;

(2) Conducting approval activities in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(3) Failing to perform supervisory duties;

(4) Failing to investigate and deal with illegal acts discovered;

(5) Embezzling, misappropriating, intercepting, or withholding subsidies for rural film public welfare screening or related special funds or funds;

(6) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain in violation of the provisions of this Law.

**Article 56** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, causes personal injury or property damage shall bear civil liability according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Where a person has received two or more administrative penalties within two years for violating this Law and commits another illegal act that should be penalized under this Law, a heavier penalty shall be imposed.

**Article 57** The film regulatory department of a people's government at or above the county level and its staff members shall strictly exercise their administrative penalty power in accordance with the types and ranges of penalties provided for in this Law, based on the nature and specific circumstances of the illegal act. Specific measures shall be formulated by the film regulatory department of the State Council.
When investigating acts that are proven to be in violation of this Law, the film regulatory department of a people's government at or above the county level may, in accordance with the law, seal up the premises or facilities related to the illegal act, or seal up or seize the property used for the illegal act.
**Article 58** Where a party is dissatisfied with an administrative act taken by the film department of a people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments under this Law, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit according to law. Where a party is dissatisfied with a decision made by the film department of the State Council not to grant a film public screening license, it shall first apply for administrative reconsideration according to law; if it is dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, it may file an administrative lawsuit.
## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

**Article 59** Where foreign capital establishes an enterprise engaged in film activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the matter shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 60** This Law shall come into force on March 1, 2017.
