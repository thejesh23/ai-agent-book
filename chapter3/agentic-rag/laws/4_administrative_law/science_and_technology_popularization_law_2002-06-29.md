# Law of the People's Republic of China on Popularization of Science and Technology

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 29, 2002

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and relevant laws, for the purpose of implementing the strategy of rejuvenating the country through science and education and the strategy of sustainable development, strengthening the work of popularizing science and technology, improving the scientific and cultural quality of citizens, and promoting economic development and social progress.

Article 2 This Law applies to the activities of the State and society in popularizing scientific and technological knowledge, advocating scientific methods, disseminating scientific ideas, and promoting the scientific spirit.

The popularization of science and technology (hereinafter referred to as science popularization) shall be conducted in ways that are easy for the public to understand, accept, and participate in.

Article 3 State organs, armed forces, social organizations, enterprises and institutions, rural grassroots organizations, and other organizations shall carry out science popularization work.

Citizens have the right to participate in science popularization activities.

Article 4 Science popularization is a public welfare undertaking and an important component of the construction of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization. Developing science popularization is a long-term task of the State.

The State supports science popularization work in ethnic minority areas, remote and impoverished areas.

Article 5 The State protects the lawful rights and interests of science popularization organizations and science popularization workers, and encourages them to independently carry out science popularization activities and lawfully establish science popularization undertakings.

Article 6 The State supports social forces in establishing science popularization undertakings. Social forces may operate science popularization undertakings in accordance with market mechanisms.

Article 7 Science popularization work shall adhere to the principles of mass participation, social engagement, and regularity, be tailored to local conditions, and adopt various forms.

Article 8 Science popularization work shall uphold the scientific spirit and oppose and resist pseudoscience. No unit or individual may engage in activities that harm the public interest under the guise of science popularization.

Article 9 The State supports and promotes international cooperation and exchange in science popularization work.

## Chapter II Organization and Management

Article 10 People's governments at all levels shall lead science popularization work, incorporate it into national economic and social development plans, and create a favorable environment and conditions for its implementation.

People's governments at or above the county level shall establish a coordination system for science popularization work.

Article 11 The science and technology administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for formulating national plans for science popularization work, providing policy guidance, conducting supervision and inspection, and promoting the development of science popularization work.

Other administrative departments under the State Council shall be responsible for relevant science popularization work within their respective scopes of duties.

The science and technology administrative departments and other administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, under the leadership of the people's government at the same level and within their respective scopes of duties, be responsible for relevant science popularization work in their respective regions.

Article 12 The scientific and technological associations are the main social force in science popularization work. They shall organize and carry out mass, social, and regular science popularization activities, support relevant social organizations and enterprises and institutions in conducting science popularization activities, assist the government in formulating plans for science popularization work, and provide suggestions for government decision-making on science popularization.

## Chapter III Social Responsibilities

Article 13 Science popularization is a common task of the whole society. All sectors of society shall organize and participate in various types of science popularization activities.

Article 14 Schools and other educational institutions at all levels shall incorporate science popularization as an important part of quality education and organize students to participate in various forms of science popularization activities.

Science and technology museums (stations), science and technology activity centers, and other science popularization education bases shall organize extracurricular science popularization education activities for young people.

Article 15 Scientific research and technological development institutions, institutions of higher learning, and social groups in the fields of natural and social sciences shall organize and support scientists, technologists, and teachers in carrying out science popularization activities, and encourage them to combine science popularization with their professional work; where conditions permit, they shall open laboratories, exhibition rooms, and other venues and facilities to the public, hold lectures, and provide consultations.

Scientists, technologists, and teachers shall leverage their strengths and expertise to actively participate in and support science popularization activities.

Article 16 News publishing, radio, film and television, cultural institutions, and groups shall leverage their respective advantages to carry out science popularization publicity work.

Comprehensive newspapers and periodicals shall set up science popularization columns or special editions; radio stations and television stations shall set up science popularization programs or broadcast science popularization programs; film and television production, distribution, and screening institutions shall strengthen the production, distribution, and screening of science popularization films and television works; book and periodical publishing and distribution institutions shall support the publication and distribution of science popularization books and periodicals; comprehensive internet websites shall set up science popularization web pages; cultural venues such as science and technology museums (stations), libraries, museums, and cultural centers shall play a role in science popularization education.

Article 17 State organs and public institutions in the fields of medical and health care, family planning, environmental protection, land and resources, sports, meteorology, earthquakes, cultural relics, and tourism shall carry out science popularization activities in conjunction with their respective work.

Article 18 Social groups such as trade unions, the Communist Youth League, and women's federations shall organize and carry out science popularization activities tailored to the characteristics of their respective target groups.

Article 19 Enterprises shall carry out science popularization activities in conjunction with technological innovation and employee skills training, and where conditions permit, may establish science popularization venues and facilities open to the public.

Article 20 The State shall strengthen science popularization work in rural areas. Rural grassroots organizations shall, based on local economic and social development needs, focus on scientific production and civilized living, leverage the role of township science popularization organizations and rural schools, and carry out science popularization work.

Various types of rural economic organizations, agricultural technology extension institutions, and rural professional technical associations shall popularize scientific and technological knowledge to farmers in conjunction with the promotion of advanced and applicable technologies.

Article 21 Urban grassroots organizations and communities shall utilize local resources in science, technology, education, culture, health, and tourism, and carry out science popularization activities in response to residents' needs for living, learning, health, and entertainment.

Article 22 Management units of public places such as parks, shopping malls, airports, stations, and docks shall strengthen science popularization publicity within their respective jurisdictions.

## Chapter IV Safeguard Measures

Article 23 People's governments at all levels shall include science popularization funds in their fiscal budgets at the same level, gradually increase the level of investment in science popularization, and ensure the smooth implementation of science popularization work.

Relevant departments of people's governments at all levels shall allocate a certain amount of funds for science popularization work.

Article 24 People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and other local people's governments where conditions permit, shall incorporate the construction of science popularization venues and facilities into urban and rural construction plans and capital construction plans; they shall strengthen the utilization, maintenance, and renovation of existing science popularization venues and facilities.

Science popularization venues constructed with government financial investment shall be equipped with necessary full-time staff, be open to the public year-round, offer preferential treatment to young people, and shall not be converted to other uses without authorization; where funds are insufficient, the fiscal department at the same level shall provide subsidies to ensure normal operation.

In areas where conditions for establishing science popularization venues are not yet available, existing facilities in science, technology, education, and culture may be used to carry out science popularization activities, and science popularization galleries and display windows may be set up.

Article 25 The State supports science popularization work and provides tax incentives for science popularization undertakings in accordance with the law.

Science popularization organizations may lawfully obtain funding and donations for carrying out science popularization activities and establishing science popularization undertakings.

Article 26 The State encourages social organizations and individuals, both domestic and foreign, to establish science popularization funds to support science popularization undertakings.

Article 27 The State encourages social organizations and individuals, both domestic and foreign, to donate property to support science popularization undertakings; preferential treatment shall be given in accordance with the law to those who donate property for science popularization undertakings or invest in the construction of science popularization venues and facilities.

Article 28 Science popularization funds and property donated by social organizations and individuals to support science popularization undertakings must be used for science popularization purposes. No unit or individual may embezzle, withhold, or misappropriate such funds or property.

Article 29 People's governments at all levels, scientific and technological associations, and relevant units shall support science popularization workers in carrying out their work, and shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have made significant contributions to science popularization work.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 30 Where activities that harm the public interest, disrupt social order, or defraud property are carried out under the guise of science popularization, the relevant competent departments shall give criticism and education and stop such activities; where public security administration regulations are violated, the public security organs shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Where science popularization fiscal funds are embezzled, withheld, or misappropriated, or donated funds or materials are embezzled or misappropriated in violation of the provisions of this Law, the relevant competent departments shall order the return of such funds or materials within a specified period; administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 32 Where science popularization venues constructed with government financial investment are converted to other uses without authorization, the relevant competent departments shall order corrections within a specified period; where the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

Where the order of science popularization venues is disrupted or science popularization venues or facilities are damaged, the violator shall be ordered to cease the infringement, restore the original condition, or compensate for losses in accordance with the law; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 33 Where State functionaries abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in science popularization work, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 34 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
