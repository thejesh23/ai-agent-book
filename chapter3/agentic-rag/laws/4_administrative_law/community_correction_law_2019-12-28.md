# Community Correction Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 28, 2019

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to promote and regulate community correction work, ensure the correct execution of criminal judgments, criminal rulings, and decisions on temporary service of sentence outside prison, improve the quality of education and correction, facilitate the smooth reintegration of community correction recipients into society, and prevent and reduce crime.

**Article 2** Community correction shall be implemented according to law for criminals sentenced to public surveillance, granted probation, granted parole, or temporarily serving sentences outside prison.

This Law applies to the supervision, management, education, and assistance activities for community correction recipients.

**Article 3** Community correction work shall adhere to the combination of supervision and management with education and assistance, and the combination of specialized agencies with social forces. It shall adopt classified management and individualized correction, purposefully eliminate factors that may lead community correction recipients to re-offend, and help them become law-abiding citizens.

**Article 4** Community correction recipients shall accept community correction according to law and submit to supervision and management.

Community correction work shall be carried out according to law, respecting and safeguarding human rights. The personal rights, property rights, and other rights lawfully enjoyed by community correction recipients shall not be infringed upon, and they shall not be discriminated against in employment, education, or access to social security.

**Article 5** The State supports community correction agencies in improving their level of informatization and using modern information technology to carry out supervision, management, education, and assistance. Relevant departments involved in community correction work shall share information according to law.

**Article 6** People's governments at all levels shall include community correction funds in their respective government budgets.

The funds needed for residents' committees, villagers' committees, and other social organizations to assist community correction agencies in carrying out their work according to law shall be included in the government budget of the community correction agency's level as prescribed.

**Article 7** Organizations and individuals that make outstanding contributions to community correction work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Institutions, Personnel, and Duties

**Article 8** The judicial administrative department of the State Council shall administer community correction work nationwide. The judicial administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall administer community correction work within their respective administrative regions.

People's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective duties, properly carry out community correction work according to law. People's procuratorates shall exercise legal supervision over community correction work according to law.

Local people's governments may establish community correction committees as needed to be responsible for overall coordination and guidance of community correction work within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 9** Local people's governments at or above the county level may establish community correction agencies as needed to be responsible for the specific implementation of community correction work. The establishment and dissolution of community correction agencies shall be proposed by the judicial administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level and approved in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures.

Judicial offices shall, based on the entrustment of community correction agencies, undertake work related to community correction.

**Article 10** Community correction agencies shall be staffed with specialized state personnel with legal and other professional knowledge (hereinafter referred to as community correction agency staff) to perform law enforcement duties such as supervision, management, education, and assistance.

**Article 11** Community correction agencies may, as needed, organize social workers with professional knowledge or practical experience in law, education, psychology, social work, etc., to carry out work related to community correction.

**Article 12** Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall assist community correction agencies in carrying out community correction work according to law.

The guardians, family members, employers, or schools of community correction recipients shall assist community correction agencies in carrying out community correction work.

**Article 13** The State encourages and supports enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, volunteers, and other social forces to participate in community correction work according to law.

**Article 14** Community correction agency staff shall strictly abide by the Constitution and laws, be devoted to their duties, strictly observe discipline, and be honest and clean.

**Article 15** Community correction agency staff and other personnel participating in community correction work who carry out community correction work according to law shall be protected by law.

**Article 16** The State promotes the building of a high-quality community correction workforce. Community correction agencies shall strengthen the management, supervision, training, and occupational protection of community correction staff, and continuously improve the standardization and professionalization of community correction work.

## Chapter III Decisions and Reception

**Article 17** When a community correction decision-making authority sentences someone to public surveillance, grants probation, rules on parole, or decides or approves temporary service of sentence outside prison, it shall determine the place of execution for community correction.

The place of execution for community correction shall be the place of residence of the community correction recipient. If the community correction recipient resides in multiple places, the habitual residence may be determined as the place of execution.

If the place of residence or habitual residence of the community correction recipient cannot be determined or is unsuitable for executing community correction, the community correction decision-making authority shall determine the place of execution based on the principle of facilitating the recipient's acceptance of correction and better integration into society.

The term "community correction decision-making authority" in this Law refers to the people's court that lawfully sentences someone to public surveillance, grants probation, rules on parole, or decides on temporary service of sentence outside prison, and the prison administration authority or public security organ that lawfully approves temporary service of sentence outside prison.

**Article 18** The community correction decision-making authority may, as needed, entrust a community correction agency or relevant social organization to conduct an investigation and assessment of the defendant's or criminal's social dangerousness and impact on the community of residence, and provide opinions for reference when deciding on community correction. Residents' committees, villagers' committees, and other organizations shall provide necessary assistance.

**Article 19** The community correction decision-making authority shall sentence someone to public surveillance, grant probation, rule on parole, or decide or approve temporary service of sentence outside prison in accordance with the conditions and procedures stipulated in the Criminal Law, Criminal Procedure Law, and other laws.

The community correction decision-making authority shall educate the community correction recipient, inform them of the rules to be observed during community correction and the legal consequences of violating them, and order them to report on time.

**Article 20** The community correction decision-making authority shall notify the community correction agency at the place of execution within five days from the date the judgment, ruling, or decision takes effect, and serve the relevant legal documents within ten days, while also sending copies to the people's procuratorate and the public security organ at the place of execution. If the place of decision and the place of execution are not the same, the community correction agency at the place of execution shall forward the legal documents to the people's procuratorate and public security organ at its location.

**Article 21** Community correction recipients sentenced to public surveillance, granted probation, or granted parole by a people's court shall report to the community correction agency at the place of execution within ten days from the date the judgment or ruling takes effect.

For community correction recipients for whom a people's court decides on temporary service of sentence outside prison, the detention center or the public security organ executing the release on bail pending trial or residential surveillance shall transfer the recipient to the community correction agency within ten days from the date of receiving the decision.

For community correction recipients approved for temporary service of sentence outside prison by the prison administration authority or public security organ, the prison or detention center shall transfer the recipient to the community correction agency within ten days from the date of receiving the approval decision.

**Article 22** Community correction agencies shall receive community correction recipients according to law, verify legal documents, confirm identities, complete reception registration, establish files, and announce the criminal facts of the community correction recipient, the term of community correction, and the rules to be observed.

## Chapter IV Supervision and Management

**Article 23** During the period of community correction, community correction recipients shall abide by laws and administrative regulations, fulfill the obligations determined by legal documents such as judgments, rulings, and decisions on temporary service of sentence outside prison, comply with the supervision and management regulations of the judicial administrative department of the State Council regarding reporting, meeting visitors, traveling outside, changing residence, medical parole, etc., and submit to the management of the community correction agency.

**Article 24** Community correction agencies shall, based on the content of the judgment and factors such as the gender, age, psychological characteristics, health status, cause of crime, type of crime, circumstances of the crime, and expression of remorse of the community correction recipient, develop targeted correction plans to achieve classified management and individualized correction. Correction plans shall be adjusted accordingly based on the performance of the community correction recipient.

**Article 25** Community correction agencies shall, based on the circumstances of the community correction recipient, designate a correction team responsible for implementing the corresponding correction plan.

As needed, the correction team may consist of personnel from judicial offices, residents' committees, villagers' committees, guardians and family members of the community correction recipient, personnel from their employer or school, as well as social workers and volunteers. If the community correction recipient is female, the correction team shall include female members.

**Article 26** Community correction agencies shall understand and grasp the activity status and behavioral performance of community correction recipients. Community correction agencies may verify relevant information through communication, information-based checks, on-site visits, etc., and relevant entities and individuals shall cooperate.

When conducting on-site visits and other work, community correction agencies shall protect the identity information and personal privacy of community correction recipients.

**Article 27** Community correction recipients leaving the city or county where they reside or changing their residence shall report to and obtain approval from the community correction agency. The community correction agency shall approve requests with legitimate reasons; for those who frequently travel across cities or counties due to normal work and life needs, the approval procedures and methods may be simplified based on the circumstances.

If the place of execution needs to be changed due to the relocation of the community correction recipient or other reasons, the community correction agency shall make a change decision in accordance with relevant regulations. After making the change decision, the community correction agency shall notify the community correction decision-making authority and the new community correction agency, and send copies of the relevant legal documents to the new community correction agency. The new community correction agency shall forward the legal documents to the people's procuratorate and public security organ at its location.

**Article 28** Community correction agencies shall, based on the performance of community correction recipients, conduct assessments and implement rewards and punishments in accordance with relevant regulations. Community correction recipients who admit guilt and express remorse, abide by laws and regulations, submit to supervision and management, and demonstrate outstanding performance in accepting education shall be commended. Community correction recipients who violate laws, regulations, or supervision and management rules shall, depending on the circumstances, be given reprimands or warnings according to law, have their cases referred to public security organs for public security penalties, or have their probation or parole revoked, or be taken back into custody for temporary service of sentence outside prison, as legally proposed.

The assessment results of community correction recipients may serve as a basis for determining whether they genuinely show remorse or have seriously violated supervision and management rules.

**Article 29** If a community correction recipient falls under any of the following circumstances, with the approval of the head of the county-level judicial administrative department, electronic positioning devices may be used to strengthen supervision and management:

(1) Violating a restraining order issued by a people's court;
(2) Leaving the city or county where they reside without approval and without legitimate reasons;
(3) Refusing to report their activities as required and having been given a warning;
(4) Violating supervision and management rules and having been subjected to public security penalties;
(5) Being proposed for revocation of probation, parole, or being taken back into custody for temporary service of sentence outside prison.

The period for using electronic positioning devices as specified in the preceding paragraph shall not exceed three months. If continued use is no longer necessary, the devices shall be promptly removed; if, after the period expires, continued use is deemed necessary upon assessment, the period may be extended with approval, each extension not exceeding three months.

Community correction agencies shall strictly keep confidential the information obtained through electronic positioning devices, which shall only be used for community correction work and not for other purposes.

**Article 30** If a community correction recipient loses contact, the community correction agency shall immediately organize a search, and public security organs and other relevant entities and personnel shall cooperate and assist. After locating the community correction recipient, appropriate actions shall be taken according to law based on the circumstances.

**Article 31** If a community correction agency discovers that a community correction recipient is engaging in acts violating supervision and management rules or illegal acts such as violating a people's court's restraining order, it shall immediately stop such acts; if stopping is ineffective, it shall immediately notify the public security organ to handle the situation on site.

**Article 32** If a community correction recipient is subject to circumstances restricting personal freedom, such as being lawfully detained, subjected to compulsory isolation for drug rehabilitation, or subjected to criminal compulsory measures, the relevant authority shall promptly notify the community correction agency.

**Article 33** If a community correction recipient meets the conditions for commutation of sentence stipulated in the Criminal Law, the community correction agency shall submit a commutation proposal to the intermediate people's court or higher at the place of execution of community correction, and send a copy of the commutation proposal to the people's procuratorate at the same level.

The people's court shall rule within thirty days of receiving the commutation proposal from the community correction agency, and serve the ruling on the community correction agency, while also sending copies to the people's procuratorate and the public security organ.

**Article 34** When carrying out community correction work, the legitimate rights and interests of community correction recipients shall be protected. The measures and methods of community correction shall avoid causing unnecessary impact on the normal work and life of community correction recipients; unless provided by law, the personal freedom of community correction recipients shall not be restricted or indirectly restricted.

Community correction recipients who believe their legitimate rights and interests have been infringed upon have the right to file complaints, accusations, and reports with the people's procuratorate or relevant authorities. The receiving authority shall handle the matter promptly and inform the complainant, accuser, or reporter of the outcome.

## Chapter V Education and Assistance

**Article 35** Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, through various means, provide necessary venues and conditions for the education and assistance of community correction recipients, and organize and mobilize social forces to participate in education and assistance work.

Relevant people's organizations shall assist community correction agencies in carrying out education and assistance work according to law.

**Article 36** Community correction agencies shall, as needed, provide education on the rule of law, morality, etc., to community correction recipients, enhance their legal awareness, and improve their moral quality and sense of remorse.

Education for community correction recipients shall be tailored to their individual characteristics, daily performance, and other actual circumstances, fully considering their work and life situations, and teaching them according to their aptitudes.

**Article 37** Community correction agencies may coordinate with relevant departments and entities to provide vocational skills training and employment guidance to community correction recipients facing employment difficulties according to law, and assist community correction recipients who are current students in completing their studies.

**Article 38** Residents' committees and villagers' committees may guide volunteers and community members to use community resources and adopt various forms to provide necessary education and assistance to community correction recipients with special difficulties.

**Article 39** The guardians, family members, employers, or schools of community correction recipients shall assist community correction agencies in educating community correction recipients.

**Article 40** Community correction agencies may, through open selection and merit-based procurement, purchase community correction social work services or other social services to provide necessary assistance to community correction recipients in areas such as education, psychological counseling, vocational skills training, and social relationship improvement.

Community correction agencies may also carry out the above assistance activities through project entrustment to social organizations and other means. The State encourages social organizations with experience and resources to conduct cross-regional assistance exchanges and demonstration activities.

**Article 41** The State encourages enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations to provide job opportunities and vocational skills training for community correction recipients. Enterprises that hire eligible community correction recipients shall enjoy national preferential policies as prescribed.

**Article 42** Community correction agencies may, based on the personal strengths of community correction recipients, organize them to participate in public welfare activities to repair social relationships and cultivate a sense of social responsibility.

**Article 43** Community correction recipients may apply for social assistance, participate in social insurance, and obtain legal aid in accordance with relevant state regulations, and community correction agencies shall provide necessary assistance.

## Chapter VI Release and Termination

**Article 44** When the correction period for a community correction recipient expires or they are granted amnesty, the community correction agency shall issue a certificate of release from community correction to the recipient and notify the community correction decision-making authority, the people's procuratorate, and the public security organ at the location.

**Article 45** Community correction terminates when a community correction recipient's probation or parole is revoked by ruling, a decision is made to take them back into custody, or the community correction recipient dies.

**Article 46** If a community correction recipient has circumstances for revocation of probation or parole as stipulated in the Criminal Law, the people's court shall revoke the probation or parole.

If a new crime is committed during the probation period or it is discovered that there was another crime not adjudicated before the judgment was pronounced, the people's court hearing the case shall revoke the probation or parole and notify the original trial court and the community correction agency at the place of execution in writing.

For circumstances other than those specified in the second paragraph that require revocation of probation or parole, the community correction agency shall submit a proposal for revocation of probation or parole to the original trial court or the people's court at the place of execution, and send a copy of the proposal to the people's procuratorate. When submitting a proposal for revocation of probation or parole, the community correction agency shall state the reasons and provide relevant evidence.

**Article 47** If a community correction recipient proposed for revocation of probation or parole may abscond or pose a social danger, the community correction agency may, at the same time as submitting the proposal for revocation of probation or parole, request the people's court to decide on their arrest.

The people's court shall decide whether to arrest within forty-eight hours. If arrest is decided, it shall be executed by the public security organ. The period of detention after arrest shall not exceed thirty days.

**Article 48** The people's court shall rule within thirty days of receiving the proposal for revocation of probation or parole from the community correction agency, serve the ruling on the community correction agency and the public security organ, and send a copy to the people's procuratorate.

If the people's court intends to revoke probation or parole, it shall hear the defense of the community correction recipient and the opinions of their entrusted lawyer.

If the people's court rules to revoke probation or parole, the public security organ shall promptly deliver the community correction recipient to a prison or detention center for execution. If the recipient was arrested before execution, one day of detention shall offset one day of the sentence.

If the people's court rules not to revoke probation or parole, the public security organ shall immediately release the arrested community correction recipient.

**Article 49** If a community correction recipient temporarily serving a sentence outside prison has circumstances that should result in being taken back into custody as stipulated in the Criminal Procedure Law, the community correction agency shall submit a proposal for taking back into custody to the place of execution or the original community correction decision-making authority, and send a copy of the proposal to the people's procuratorate.

The community correction decision-making authority shall decide within thirty days of receiving the proposal, serve the decision on the community correction agency and the public security organ, and send a copy to the people's procuratorate.

If a people's court or public security organ decides to take a community correction recipient temporarily serving a sentence outside prison back into custody, the public security organ shall immediately deliver the recipient to a prison or detention center for execution.

If a prison administration authority decides to take a community correction recipient temporarily serving a sentence outside prison back into custody, the prison shall immediately take the recipient back into custody.

**Article 50** If a community correction recipient whose probation or parole has been revoked by ruling or who has been decided to be taken back into custody absconds, the public security organ shall pursue them, and the community correction agency, relevant entities, and individuals shall provide assistance.Article 51 If a community correction recipient dies during the period of community correction, their guardian or family members shall promptly report to the community correction institution. The community correction institution shall promptly notify the authority that decided on the community correction, the local People's Procuratorate, and the public security organ.

## Chapter VII Special Provisions on Community Correction for Minors

Article 52 Community correction institutions shall adopt targeted correction measures based on the age, psychological characteristics, developmental needs, growth experience, causes of crime, and family guardianship and education conditions of minor community correction recipients.

When forming a correction team for a minor community correction recipient, the community correction institution shall include personnel familiar with the psychological and physical characteristics of minors.

Community correction for minors shall be conducted separately from that for adults.

Article 53 The guardian of a minor community correction recipient shall fulfill guardianship responsibilities, including obligations of upbringing, discipline, and education.

If a guardian neglects their guardianship duties, the community correction institution shall urge and educate them to fulfill these responsibilities. If the guardian refuses to fulfill their guardianship duties, the relevant authorities shall be notified to handle the matter according to law.

Article 54 Staff of community correction institutions and other personnel lawfully involved in community correction work shall keep confidential the identity information of minors obtained during the performance of their duties.

Unless required for judicial case handling or authorized by state regulations for relevant units to inquire, the file information of minor community correction recipients shall not be provided to any unit or individual. Units conducting inquiries according to law shall keep the obtained information confidential.

Article 55 For minor community correction recipients who have not completed compulsory education, the community correction institution shall notify and cooperate with the education department to provide conditions for them to complete compulsory education. The guardian of the minor community correction recipient shall ensure, according to law, that they attend school on time and complete compulsory education.

For community correction recipients aged 16 or above who are willing to seek employment, the community correction institution may coordinate with relevant departments and units to provide vocational skills training, employment guidance, and assistance.

Article 56 The Communist Youth League, Women's Federation, and organizations for the protection of minors shall, according to law, assist community correction institutions in carrying out community correction work for minors.

The state encourages other social organizations related to minors to participate in community correction work for minors and provides policy support according to law.

Article 57 Minor community correction recipients shall enjoy equal rights with other minors in terms of resuming studies, advancing to higher education, and employment, according to law. No unit or individual may discriminate against them. If discriminatory acts occur, the education, human resources, and social security departments shall handle them according to law.

Article 58 If a minor community correction recipient reaches the age of 18 during the period of community correction, the provisions on community correction for minors shall continue to apply.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 59 If a community correction recipient violates supervision and management regulations during the period of community correction, the public security organ shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China"; if there are circumstances warranting revocation of probation, parole, or temporary service of sentence outside prison with re-imprisonment, the matter shall be handled according to law.

Article 60 If a community correction recipient assaults, threatens, insults, harasses, or retaliates against staff of community correction institutions or other personnel lawfully involved in community correction work, or their close relatives, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose public security administrative penalties according to law.

Article 61 If staff of community correction institutions or other state personnel commit any of the following acts, they shall be subject to disciplinary action; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Using their position or work convenience to solicit or accept bribes;
(2) Failing to perform statutory duties;
(3) Physically punishing or abusing community correction recipients, or illegally restricting or indirectly restricting their personal freedom;
(4) Disclosing secrets of community correction work or other information that should be kept confidential according to law;
(5) Retaliating against community correction recipients who lawfully file complaints, accusations, or reports;
(6) Committing other disciplinary or illegal acts.

Article 62 If the People's Procuratorate discovers that community correction work violates legal provisions, it shall propose corrective opinions or procuratorial recommendations according to law. The relevant unit shall provide a written reply to the People's Procuratorate on whether the corrective opinions or procuratorial recommendations have been adopted; if not adopted, reasons shall be provided.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

Article 63 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2020.
