# People's Republic of China Law on Physicians

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 20, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to protect the lawful rights and interests of physicians, regulate their practice, strengthen the development of the physician workforce, safeguard the health of the people, and advance the building of a Healthy China.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "physician" refers to a professional medical practitioner who has lawfully obtained physician qualifications and, after registration, practices in a medical and health institution, including licensed physicians and licensed assistant physicians.

**Article 3** Physicians shall uphold the principles of putting the people and life above all else, promote the spirit of humanitarianism, and carry forward the noble professional spirit of revering life, saving the dying and healing the injured, being willing to contribute, and showing boundless love. They shall abide by professional ethics, comply with practice standards, improve their practice competence, and fulfill the sacred duty of preventing and treating diseases and protecting the health of the people.

Physicians who practice in accordance with the law are protected by law. The personal dignity and personal safety of physicians shall not be violated.

**Article 4** The health authority of the State Council shall be responsible for the administration of physicians nationwide. The education, human resources and social security, traditional Chinese medicine, and other relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for the relevant administration of physicians within their respective duties.

The health authorities of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the administration of physicians within their respective administrative regions. The education, human resources and social security, traditional Chinese medicine, and other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the relevant administration of physicians within their respective duties.

**Article 5** August 19 of each year is designated as Chinese Physicians' Day.

Physicians who have made outstanding contributions to medical and health services shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The whole society shall respect physicians. People's governments at all levels shall care for and protect physicians, promote their exemplary deeds, strengthen professional training, support innovation and pioneering, help solve difficulties, and foster a positive social atmosphere of respecting physicians and valuing healthcare.

**Article 6** The State shall establish and improve the system for setting, evaluating, and appointing medical professional technical titles for physicians, taking professional ethics, practical professional ability, and work performance as important conditions, and scientifically setting relevant evaluation and appointment standards.

**Article 7** Physicians may, in accordance with the law, organize and participate in industry organizations such as medical associations and professional academic groups.

Industry organizations such as medical associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline and physician practice standards, protect the lawful rights and interests of physicians, and assist health authorities and other relevant departments in carrying out related work.

## Chapter II: Examination and Registration

**Article 8** The State implements a physician qualification examination system.

Physician qualification examinations are divided into the licensed physician qualification examination and the licensed assistant physician qualification examination. Physician qualification examinations shall be organized and implemented by health authorities of people's governments at or above the provincial level.

The categories and specific methods of physician qualification examinations shall be formulated by the health authority of the State Council.

**Article 9** Persons meeting one of the following conditions may take the licensed physician qualification examination:

(1) Having a bachelor's degree or above in a relevant medical specialty from a higher education institution, and having participated in medical professional work practice in a medical and health institution under the guidance of a licensed physician for a full year;

(2) Having an associate degree in a relevant medical specialty from a higher education institution, and having practiced in a medical and health institution for a full two years after obtaining the licensed assistant physician practice certificate.

**Article 10** Persons with an associate degree or above in a relevant medical specialty from a higher education institution, who have participated in medical professional work practice in a medical and health institution under the guidance of a licensed physician for a full year, may take the licensed assistant physician qualification examination.

**Article 11** Persons who have studied traditional Chinese medicine under a master-apprentice relationship for a full three years, or who have proven expertise in medical skills through many years of practice, may take the traditional Chinese medicine physician qualification examination after passing an assessment and receiving a recommendation from a traditional Chinese medicine professional organization or medical and health institution authorized by the health authority of a people's government at or above the county level.

Persons who have studied traditional Chinese medicine under a master-apprentice relationship or through many years of practice and have proven expertise in medical skills may obtain traditional Chinese medicine physician qualifications and the corresponding qualification certificate after being recommended by at least two traditional Chinese medicine physicians and passing a practical skill and effectiveness assessment organized by the traditional Chinese medicine authority of a provincial people's government.

The relevant examination and assessment methods stipulated in this Article shall be drafted by the traditional Chinese medicine authority of the State Council, submitted to the health authority of the State Council for review and publication.

**Article 12** Persons who pass the physician qualification examination and obtain licensed physician or licensed assistant physician qualifications shall be issued a physician qualification certificate.

**Article 13** The State implements a physician practice registration system.

Persons who have obtained physician qualifications may apply for registration with the health authority of the local people's government at or above the county level where they are located. Medical and health institutions may handle collective registration procedures for applicants within their institution.

Except for circumstances where registration is not granted as provided in this Law, the health authority shall grant registration within twenty working days from the date of receiving the application, enter the registration information into the national information platform, and issue a physician practice certificate.

No person may engage in physician practice activities without being registered and obtaining a physician practice certificate.

The specific methods for the administration of physician practice registration shall be formulated by the health authority of the State Council.

**Article 14** After registration, a physician may practice in a medical and health institution in accordance with the registered practice location, practice category, and practice scope, and provide corresponding medical and health services.

Physicians of traditional Chinese medicine or integrated traditional Chinese and Western medicine may practice in the traditional Chinese medicine department, integrated traditional Chinese and Western medicine department, or other clinical departments of a medical institution in accordance with the registered practice category and practice scope.

Physicians may add to their practice scope after completing relevant professional training and passing an assessment. Where laws or administrative regulations have provisions on the qualification conditions for physicians to engage in specific practice activities, those provisions shall prevail.

Traditional Chinese medicine physicians who have obtained physician qualifications through examination may, in accordance with relevant state regulations and after completing training and passing an assessment, adopt Western medicine techniques and methods related to their specialty in their practice activities. Western medicine physicians may, in accordance with relevant state regulations and after completing training and passing an assessment, adopt traditional Chinese medicine techniques and methods related to their specialty in their practice activities.

**Article 15** Physicians who regularly practice in two or more medical and health institutions shall designate one institution as their primary practice location and handle relevant procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations. The State encourages physicians to regularly provide medical and health services at designated locations in medical and health institutions below the county level, including township health centers, village clinics, and community health service centers, and the primary practice institution shall provide support and convenience.

Health authorities and medical and health institutions shall strengthen supervision and management of such physicians, regulate their practice behavior, and ensure the quality of medical and health services.

**Article 16** Registration shall not be granted in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having no civil capacity or having limited civil capacity;

(2) Having been subject to criminal punishment, where less than two years have elapsed since the completion of the sentence, or the period of being prohibited from engaging in the medical profession has not expired;

(3) Having had the physician practice certificate revoked, where less than two years have elapsed;

(4) Having had registration cancelled due to failing the periodic physician assessment, where less than one year has elapsed;

(5) Other circumstances stipulated by laws or administrative regulations where engaging in medical and health services is not permitted.

Where the health authority receiving the application decides not to grant registration, it shall notify the applicant and their medical and health institution in writing within twenty working days from the date of receiving the application, stating the reasons.

**Article 17** After a physician is registered, registration shall be cancelled and the physician practice certificate shall be invalidated in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Death;

(2) Being subject to criminal punishment;

(3) Having the physician practice certificate revoked;

(4) Failing the periodic physician assessment, having practice activities suspended for the specified period, and failing the reassessment;

(5) Having suspended physician practice activities for a full two years;

(6) Other circumstances stipulated by laws or administrative regulations where engaging in medical and health services is not permitted or where cancellation procedures should be handled.

In the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the medical and health institution where the physician practices shall report to the health authority that granted the registration within thirty days; if the health authority discovers, in the course of its duties, that a physician is in any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall promptly notify the health authority that granted the registration. The health authority that granted the registration shall promptly cancel the registration and invalidate the physician practice certificate.

**Article 18** Where a physician changes registration matters such as practice location, practice category, or practice scope, they shall go through change-of-registration procedures with the health authority that granted the registration in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Physicians engaging in the following activities may do so without going through the relevant change-of-registration procedures:

(1) Participating in standardized training, advanced studies, paired assistance, consultations, emergency medical rescue for public health incidents, charitable or other public welfare medical care, or free clinics;

(2) Undertaking national tasks or participating in important activities organized by the government;

(3) Practicing in medical institutions within a medical consortium.

**Article 19** Where a physician has suspended practice activities for more than two years, or where the circumstances preventing registration under this Law have ceased to exist, and the physician applies to resume practice, they must pass an assessment conducted by the health authority of a people's government at or above the county level, or by a medical and health institution or industry organization authorized by it, and be re-registered in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 20** Physicians practicing individually shall go through approval or filing procedures in accordance with the law.

A licensed physician may practice individually only after having practiced in a medical and health institution for a full five years following registration; however, persons who have obtained traditional Chinese medicine physician qualifications in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 11 of this Law may practice individually within the scope of their registered practice immediately after completing practice registration based on the assessment content.

The health authority of a local people's government at or above the county level shall supervise and inspect physicians practicing individually in accordance with relevant state regulations. If it discovers any circumstances requiring cancellation of registration under this Law, it shall promptly cancel the registration and invalidate the physician practice certificate.

**Article 21** The health authority of a local people's government at or above the county level shall promptly publish the list of persons who have been granted registration or whose registration has been cancelled. This information shall be compiled by the health authority of the provincial people's government, reported to the health authority of the State Council for filing, and made available for inquiry through a website providing physician registration information, in accordance with regulations.

## Chapter III: Practice Rules

**Article 22** Physicians shall enjoy the following rights in their practice activities:

(1) Within the registered scope of practice, conducting medical examinations, disease investigations, medical treatments, issuing corresponding medical certificates, and selecting reasonable medical, preventive, and health care plans in accordance with relevant standards;

(2) Receiving remuneration for labor, enjoying welfare benefits stipulated by the State, participating in social insurance in accordance with regulations, and enjoying corresponding benefits;

(3) Obtaining basic practice conditions and occupational protective equipment that meet national standards;

(4) Engaging in medical education, research, and academic exchanges;

(5) Participating in professional training and receiving continuing medical education;

(6) Offering opinions and suggestions on the work of the medical and health institution and the health authority, and participating in the democratic management of the institution in accordance with the law;

(7) Other rights stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 23** Physicians shall fulfill the following obligations in their practice activities:

(1) Cultivating a spirit of dedication, abiding by professional ethics, fulfilling physician responsibilities, treating patients with utmost diligence, and implementing public health measures such as epidemic prevention and control;

(2) Following clinical diagnosis and treatment guidelines, complying with clinical technical operation standards and medical ethics norms;

(3) Respecting, caring for, and protecting patients, and protecting patient privacy and personal information in accordance with the law;

(4) Diligently studying professional knowledge, updating knowledge, improving medical professional technical ability and level, and enhancing the quality of medical and health services;

(5) Promoting and disseminating health science knowledge appropriate to their position, and providing health education and guidance to patients and the public;

(6) Other obligations stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 24** When implementing medical, preventive, or health care measures, or signing relevant medical documents, a physician must personally conduct examinations and investigations, and promptly fill in medical records and other medical documents in accordance with regulations. They shall not conceal, forge, alter, or destroy medical records or related materials without authorization.

Physicians shall not issue false medical certificates, nor issue medical certificates unrelated to their practice scope or inconsistent with their practice category.

**Article 25** In the course of diagnosis and treatment, a physician shall explain the patient's condition, medical measures, and other matters requiring notification to the patient. Where surgery, special examinations, or special treatments are needed, the physician shall promptly explain the medical risks, alternative treatment plans, and other relevant information to the patient in detail, and obtain their explicit consent; if it is impossible or inappropriate to explain to the patient, the physician shall explain to the patient's close relatives and obtain their explicit consent.

**Article 26** Physicians conducting clinical trials of drugs and medical devices and other medical clinical research shall comply with relevant state regulations, adhere to medical ethics norms, pass ethical review in accordance with the law, and obtain written informed consent.

**Article 27** For patients requiring emergency treatment, a physician shall take emergency measures for diagnosis and treatment and shall not refuse emergency medical care.

In emergency situations such as rescuing a patient whose life is in critical danger, where it is impossible to obtain the opinion of the patient or their close relatives, the physician may immediately implement corresponding medical measures with the approval of the person in charge of the medical institution or an authorized person.

The State encourages physicians to actively participate in emergency medical services in public places such as public transportation; physicians who cause harm to the recipient due to voluntarily providing emergency care shall not bear civil liability.

**Article 28** Physicians shall use drugs, disinfectants, and medical devices that have been lawfully approved or filed, and adopt lawful, compliant, and scientific diagnostic and treatment methods.

Except for use in diagnosis and treatment in accordance with standards, physicians shall not use narcotic drugs, toxic drugs for medical use, psychotropic substances, or radioactive drugs.

**Article 29** Physicians shall adhere to the principle of safe, effective, economical, and rational drug use, and follow clinical drug application guidelines, clinical diagnosis and treatment guidelines, and drug instructions for rational drug use.

In special circumstances where no effective or better treatment is available, a physician may, after obtaining the patient's explicit informed consent, adopt a drug usage method not specified in the drug instructions but supported by evidence-based medical evidence for treatment. Medical institutions shall establish management systems to review the appropriateness of physicians' prescriptions and medication orders, and strictly regulate physicians' drug use behavior.

**Article 30** Licensed physicians may, with the consent of their medical and health institution and in accordance with relevant state regulations, provide appropriate medical and health services such as follow-up consultations for some common and chronic diseases through information technologies like the Internet. The State supports remote medical cooperation between medical and health institutions using information technologies such as the Internet.

**Article 31** Physicians shall not use their position to solicit or illegally accept property or seek other improper benefits; they shall not perform unnecessary examinations or treatments on patients.

**Article 32** When emergencies such as natural disasters, accidents, public health incidents, and social security incidents seriously threaten people's lives and health, the health authority of a people's government at or above the county level shall, as needed, organize physicians to participate in health emergency response and medical treatment, and physicians shall obey the deployment.

**Article 33** In any of the following circumstances during practice activities, a physician shall promptly report to their medical and health institution or the relevant department or agency in accordance with relevant regulations:

(1) Discovering infectious diseases, sudden diseases of unknown cause, or abnormal health events;

(2) Occurrence or discovery of a medical accident;

(3) Discovering adverse reactions or adverse events potentially related to drugs or medical devices;

(4) Discovering counterfeit or substandard drugs;

(5) Discovering that a patient is suspected of being involved in an injury incident or unnatural death;

(6) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations.

**Article 34** Licensed assistant physicians shall practice in medical and health institutions under the guidance of licensed physicians, in accordance with their registered practice category and practice scope.

Licensed assistant physicians practicing in township, ethnic township, town, and village medical and health institutions, as well as in county-level medical and health institutions in remote and difficult areas, may independently engage in general practice activities based on the medical and health service situation and their own practical experience.

**Article 35** Medical students participating in clinical teaching practice and medical graduates who have not yet obtained a physician practice certificate and are participating in medical professional work practice in medical and health institutions shall participate in clinical diagnosis and treatment activities under the supervision and guidance of licensed physicians. Medical and health institutions shall provide necessary conditions for such medical students and graduates to participate in clinical diagnosis and treatment activities.

**Article 36** Relevant industry organizations, medical and health institutions, and medical schools shall strengthen medical ethics education for physicians.

Medical and health institutions shall establish and improve systems such as physician post responsibility, internal supervision, and complaint handling, and strengthen the management of physicians.

## Chapter IV: Training and Assessment

**Article 37** The State shall formulate physician training plans, establish a physician training and supply-demand balance mechanism that adapts to industry characteristics and social needs, coordinate the demand for various types of medical talents, and strengthen the training of professionals in shortage areas such as general practice, pediatrics, psychiatry, and geriatrics.

The State shall take measures to strengthen the coordination of medical education, and improve the systems of medical school education, postgraduate education, and continuing education.

The State shall strengthen the training and deployment of primary-level medical and health personnel, with a focus on general practitioners, through various channels.

The State shall take measures to improve the education system for mutual learning between traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine, and cultivate high-level integrated traditional Chinese and Western medicine talents and general practitioners capable of providing integrated traditional Chinese and Western medicine services.

**Article 38** The State shall establish and improve the standardized residency training system, improve the clinical teaching incentive mechanism, ensure the treatment of residents during training, and strictly manage the training process and final assessment.

The State shall establish and improve the standardized specialist physician training system, and continuously improve the specialist diagnosis and treatment level of clinical physicians.

**Article 39** The health authorities of people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments shall formulate physician training plans, adopt various forms to provide graded and classified training for physicians, and provide conditions for physicians to receive continuing medical education.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take effective measures to give priority to ensuring that medical and health personnel in primary-level, underdeveloped, and ethnic minority areas receive continuing medical education.

**Article 40** Medical and health institutions shall reasonably allocate human resources, and ensure that physicians in their institutions receive continuing medical education in accordance with regulations and plans.

The health authorities of people's governments at or above the county level shall systematically organize and coordinate medical and health institutions at or above the county level to provide training for medical and health personnel in primary-level medical and health institutions such as township health centers, village clinics, and community health service centers, to improve their medical professional technical ability and level.

Relevant industry organizations shall provide services and create conditions for physicians to receive continuing medical education, and strengthen the organization and management of continuing medical education.

**Article 41** The State shall, in its annual medical specialty enrollment plans and education and training plans, allocate a certain proportion for targeted training and commissioned training, to strengthen the physician workforce in primary-level and remote and difficult areas.Relevant departments and medical and health institutions shall enter into agreements with personnel receiving targeted training or commissioned training, stipulating matters such as relevant benefits, service duration, and liability for breach of contract. The relevant personnel shall fulfill the obligations stipulated in the agreements. The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to strengthen contract compliance management. Any party to an agreement that violates the agreement shall bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 42 The State implements a system of regular assessment for physicians.

The health administrative department of a people's government at or above the county level, or a medical and health institution or industry organization entrusted by it, shall assess physicians' professional competence, work performance, and professional ethics in accordance with the standards for physician practice. The assessment cycle is three years. For physicians with a long history of practice and no record of misconduct, the assessment procedure may be simplified.

The entrusted institution or organization shall report the assessment results of physicians to the health administrative department that granted the registration for record.

For physicians who fail the assessment, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order them to suspend their practice activities for a period of three to six months and undergo relevant professional training. Upon the expiration of the suspension period, a reassessment shall be conducted. Those who pass the reassessment shall be permitted to resume practice.

Article 43 The health administrative department of a people's government at or above the provincial level shall be responsible for guiding, inspecting, and supervising physician assessment work.

## Chapter V: Safeguard Measures

Article 44 The State establishes and improves personnel, salary, professional title, and reward systems that reflect the professional characteristics and technical labor value of physicians.

Physicians engaged in infectious disease prevention and control, radiology, mental health, and other special positions shall receive appropriate allowances in accordance with relevant state regulations. Allowance standards shall be adjusted periodically.

Physicians working at the grassroots level and in remote and difficult areas shall enjoy allowance and subsidy policies in accordance with relevant state regulations, and shall receive preferential treatment in professional title evaluation, career development, education and training, and commendation and rewards.

Article 45 The State strengthens the construction of the disease prevention and control talent team, establishing a physician training and utilization mechanism adapted to the modern disease prevention and control system.

Disease prevention and control institutions, secondary-level and above medical institutions, as well as grassroots medical and health institutions such as township health centers and community health service centers, shall be staffed with a certain number of public health physicians to engage in public health work, including monitoring of population diseases and risk factors, risk assessment and analysis, surveillance and early warning, epidemiological investigation, immunization program management, and occupational health management. Medical institutions shall establish and improve management systems and strictly implement hospital infection prevention and control measures.

The State establishes a talent training mechanism that integrates public health and clinical medicine, providing clinical physicians with professional training in disease prevention and control, response to public health emergencies, and other areas through various channels, and providing public health physicians with clinical medical professional training, thereby improving the institutional mechanisms for the integration of medical care and prevention, and the coordinated prevention and treatment of Chinese and Western medicine.

Article 46 The State takes measures to coordinate urban and rural resources, strengthen the construction of grassroots medical teams and service capabilities, establish a career development mechanism for rural medical and health personnel that connects the county, township, and village levels, and manage rural medical and health personnel within the county medical and health personnel system through methods such as county management with township use and township employment with village use.

Practicing physicians who are promoted to the deputy senior technical professional title shall have accumulated at least one year of experience providing medical and health services in medical and health institutions at or below the county level or in counterpart support institutions. After being promoted to the deputy senior technical professional title, those who have provided medical and health services in medical and health institutions at or below the county level or in counterpart support institutions for a cumulative period of more than one year shall be given priority for promotion to the senior technical professional title under equal conditions.

The State takes measures to encourage persons who have obtained the qualification certificate for practicing physicians or the qualification certificate for assistant practicing physicians to establish village medical and health institutions in accordance with the law, or to provide medical and health services in village medical and health institutions.

Article 47 The State encourages village doctors who provide preventive, health care, and general medical services to villagers in village medical and health institutions to obtain medical professional academic qualifications through medical education; and encourages eligible village doctors to take the physician qualification examination and obtain physician qualifications in accordance with the law.

The State takes measures to help village doctors improve their medical technical capabilities and levels through information technology and intelligent means, and further improve the multi-channel subsidy mechanism for village doctors' service income and policies such as old-age care.

Specific management measures for village doctors shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 48 Physicians who fall under any of the following circumstances shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations:

(1) Demonstrating noble medical ethics and outstanding deeds in their practice activities;
(2) Making pioneering innovations in medical research and education, achieving major breakthroughs in medical professional technology, and making significant contributions;
(3) Performing outstandingly in prevention and early warning, life-saving, and other work during emergencies;
(4) Working diligently for a long time in medical and health institutions at or below the county level in remote and difficult areas;
(5) Making outstanding contributions in disease prevention and control and health promotion;
(6) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 49 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall incorporate the prevention and handling of medical disputes into the comprehensive social security management system, strengthen comprehensive public security management in and around medical and health institutions, maintain a good practice environment in medical and health institutions, effectively prevent and combat criminal acts involving medical institutions in accordance with the law, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of both medical staff and patients.

Medical and health institutions shall improve safety and security measures, maintain good medical order, promptly and proactively resolve medical disputes, and ensure the safety of physicians' practice.

No organization or individual is allowed to obstruct physicians from practicing lawfully, interfere with physicians' normal work and life; or infringe upon physicians' personal dignity and personal safety through insults, slander, threats, beatings, or other means.

Article 50 Medical and health institutions shall provide physicians with occupational safety and health protection supplies, and adopt effective health protection and medical care measures.

Physicians who suffer accidental injuries or contract diseases or die due to exposure to toxic or harmful factors during their professional activities shall enjoy work-related injury insurance benefits in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 51 Medical and health institutions shall reasonably arrange working hours for physicians, implement the paid leave system, and conduct regular health check-ups.

Article 52 The State establishes and improves a medical risk sharing mechanism. Medical institutions shall participate in medical liability insurance or establish or participate in medical risk funds. Patients are encouraged to participate in medical accident insurance.

Article 53 News media shall conduct public interest publicity on medical and health laws, regulations, and medical and health knowledge, promote the advanced deeds of physicians, and guide the public to respect physicians and rationally view medical and health risks.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

Article 54 Anyone who commits acts such as violating examination discipline in the physician qualification examination, if the circumstances are serious, shall be prohibited from taking the physician qualification examination for a period of one to three years.

Anyone who obtains a physician qualification certificate or a physician practice certificate by improper means shall have the certificate revoked by the health administrative department that issued it, and their corresponding application shall not be accepted for three years.

Anyone who forges, alters, buys, sells, rents, or lends a physician practice certificate shall be ordered by the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains. If the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, they shall be calculated as 10,000 yuan. If the circumstances are serious, the physician practice certificate shall be revoked.

Article 55 In violation of this Law, if a physician commits any of the following acts during practice activities, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections and give a warning. If the circumstances are serious, the physician shall be ordered to suspend practice activities for not less than six months but not more than one year, or their physician practice certificate shall be revoked:

(1) Failing to fulfill the obligation of notification or obtain informed consent in accordance with regulations when providing medical and health services or conducting medical clinical research;
(2) Refusing emergency treatment for patients requiring emergency care, or delaying diagnosis and treatment due to irresponsibility;
(3) Failing to obey the dispatch of the health administrative department during emergencies such as natural disasters, accidents, public health incidents, and social security incidents that seriously threaten people's lives and health;
(4) Failing to report relevant situations in accordance with regulations;
(5) Violating laws, regulations, rules, or practice standards, resulting in medical accidents or other serious consequences.

Article 56 In violation of this Law, if a physician commits any of the following acts during practice activities, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections, give a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan. If the circumstances are serious, the physician shall be ordered to suspend practice activities for not less than six months but not more than one year, or their physician practice certificate shall be revoked:

(1) Disclosing patient privacy or personal information;
(2) Issuing false medical certificates, or signing diagnostic, treatment, epidemiological, or other certificates, or certificates concerning birth, death, etc., without personal examination or investigation;
(3) Concealing, forging, tampering with, or unlawfully destroying medical documents and related materials such as medical records;
(4) Failing to use narcotic drugs, toxic drugs for medical use, psychotropic drugs, radioactive drugs, etc., in accordance with regulations;
(5) Using their position to solicit or illegally accept property or seek other improper benefits, or violating diagnosis and treatment standards by performing unnecessary examinations or treatments on patients, causing adverse consequences;
(6) Engaging in the clinical application of prohibited medical technologies.

Article 57 In violation of this Law, if a physician practices outside the registered practice location, practice category, or practice scope, the health administrative department or the traditional Chinese medicine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections, give a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan. If the circumstances are serious, the physician shall be ordered to suspend practice activities for not less than six months but not more than one year, or their physician practice certificate shall be revoked.

Article 58 Anyone who seriously violates physician professional ethics and medical ethics norms, causing adverse social impact, shall have their physician practice certificate revoked or be ordered to cease illegal practice activities by the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and shall be prohibited from engaging in medical and health services or medical clinical research for a period of five years up to life.

Article 59 In violation of this Law, anyone who practices medicine without being a physician shall be ordered by the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease illegal practice activities, have their illegal gains, drugs, and medical devices confiscated, and be fined not less than two times but not more than ten times the illegal gains. If the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, they shall be calculated as 10,000 yuan.

Article 60 In violation of this Law, anyone who obstructs a physician from practicing lawfully, interferes with a physician's normal work and life, or infringes upon a physician's personal dignity and personal safety through insults, slander, threats, beatings, or other means, constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be subject to public security administration penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 61 In violation of this Law, if a medical and health institution fails to fulfill its reporting duties, causing serious consequences, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall give a warning, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law.

Article 62 In violation of this Law, if staff members of the health administrative department or other relevant departments, or staff members of medical and health institutions, engage in fraud, abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or favoritism and malpractice, they shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law.

Article 63 In violation of this Law, if an act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it causes personal injury or property damage, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 64 The State takes measures to encourage persons with a secondary specialized school medical professional academic qualification to improve their medical technical capabilities and levels through participating in higher-level academic education.

Persons who obtained a relevant medical professional academic qualification from a secondary specialized school before the implementation of this Law and within a certain period after its implementation may take the physician qualification examination. Specific measures shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council in conjunction with the education, traditional Chinese medicine, and other relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 65 Specific measures for the implementation of this Law by the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 66 Specific management measures for foreign personnel participating in the physician qualification examination, applying for registration, practicing, or engaging in clinical teaching, clinical research, clinical academic exchanges, and other activities shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 67 This Law shall come into effect on March 1, 2022. The "Law of the People's Republic of China on Licensed Physicians" shall be repealed simultaneously.
