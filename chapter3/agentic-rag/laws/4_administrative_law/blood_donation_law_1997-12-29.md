# Blood Donation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 29, 1997

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure the supply and safety of blood for clinical use in medical treatment, protect the health of blood donors and recipients, promote the spirit of humanitarianism, and advance the development of socialist material and spiritual civilization.

Article 2 The State implements a system of voluntary blood donation without compensation.

The State encourages healthy citizens between the ages of 18 and 55 to donate blood voluntarily.

Article 3 Local people's governments at all levels shall lead the blood donation work within their respective administrative regions, make unified plans, and be responsible for organizing and coordinating relevant departments to jointly carry out blood donation work.

Article 4 The health administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage blood donation work.

Red Cross societies at all levels shall participate in and promote blood donation work in accordance with the law.

Article 5 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to extensively publicize the significance of blood donation, popularize scientific knowledge about blood donation, and carry out education on the prevention and control of diseases transmitted through blood routes.

News media shall conduct public welfare publicity on blood donation.

Article 6 State organs, the armed forces, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, residents' committees, and villagers' committees shall mobilize and organize eligible citizens within their units or residential areas to participate in blood donation.

The measures for mobilizing and organizing active-duty military personnel to donate blood shall be formulated by the health department of the People's Liberation Army.

Blood donors shall be issued a certificate of voluntary blood donation without compensation produced by the health administrative department of the State Council, and relevant units may provide appropriate subsidies.

Article 7 The State encourages state functionaries, active-duty military personnel, and students in institutions of higher learning to take the lead in donating blood, setting an example for new social trends.

Article 8 A blood station is an institution that collects and provides blood for clinical use and is a non-profit public welfare organization. The establishment of a blood station to collect blood from citizens must be approved by the health administrative department of the State Council or the health administrative department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. Blood stations shall provide blood donors with safe, hygienic, and convenient conditions. The conditions for establishing blood stations and their management measures shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 9 Blood stations must conduct necessary health examinations for blood donors free of charge; if a donor's physical condition does not meet the requirements for blood donation, the blood station shall explain the situation to them and shall not collect blood. The physical health conditions for blood donors shall be stipulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

The amount of blood collected from a donor each time by a blood station shall generally be 200 milliliters, with a maximum of no more than 400 milliliters, and the interval between two collections shall be no less than six months.

It is strictly prohibited for blood stations to collect excessive amounts of blood or collect blood too frequently from donors in violation of the preceding paragraph.

Article 10 Blood stations must strictly comply with relevant operating procedures and regulations when collecting blood. Blood collection must be performed by medical personnel qualified to collect blood, and disposable blood collection equipment must be destroyed after use to ensure the health of blood donors.

Blood stations shall ensure the quality of blood in accordance with the standards formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Blood stations must test the collected blood; blood that has not been tested or has failed testing shall not be provided to medical institutions.

Article 11 Blood donated without compensation must be used for clinical purposes and shall not be bought or sold. Blood stations and medical institutions shall not sell blood donated without compensation to plasma collection stations or blood product manufacturers.

Article 12 The packaging, storage, and transportation of blood for clinical use must comply with the hygiene standards and requirements stipulated by the State.

Article 13 Medical institutions must verify blood for clinical use and shall not use blood that does not meet the standards stipulated by the State for clinical purposes.

Article 14 Citizens shall only pay the costs for the collection, storage, separation, and testing of blood when using blood for clinical purposes; the specific fee standards shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council in conjunction with the price department of the State Council.

When blood donors who have donated without compensation need blood for clinical use, they shall be exempted from the fees specified in the preceding paragraph; when the spouse and direct relatives of such blood donors need blood for clinical use, they may be exempted from or have reduced fees as specified in the preceding paragraph in accordance with the regulations of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 15 To ensure the need for emergency clinical blood use for citizens, the State encourages and guides patients undergoing elective surgery to store their own blood, and mobilizes family members, relatives, friends, work units, and the community to donate blood mutually.

To ensure emergency blood use, medical institutions may temporarily collect blood, but must ensure the safety of blood collection and use in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 16 Medical institutions shall formulate blood use plans for clinical purposes, follow the principles of rationality and science, and shall not waste or abuse blood.

Medical institutions shall actively promote blood transfusion based on blood components according to actual medical needs, and specific management measures shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

The State encourages the research and promotion of new technologies for clinical blood use.

Article 17 People's governments at all levels and Red Cross societies shall reward units and individuals that actively participate in blood donation or have made significant achievements in blood donation work.

Article 18 Any of the following acts shall be banned by the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, with illegal gains confiscated and a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Illegally collecting blood;

(2) Blood stations or medical institutions selling blood donated without compensation;

(3) Illegally organizing others to sell blood.

Article 19 If a blood station collects blood in violation of relevant operating procedures and regulations, the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections; if harm is caused to the health of blood donors, compensation shall be made according to law, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 20 If the packaging, storage, or transportation of blood for clinical use does not comply with the hygiene standards and requirements stipulated by the State, the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections, issue a warning, and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 21 If a blood station provides blood that does not meet the standards stipulated by the State to medical institutions in violation of the provisions of this Law, the health administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections; if the circumstances are serious, causing the spread of diseases transmitted through blood routes or posing a serious risk of such spread, the blood station shall be ordered to rectify within a time limit, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 22 If medical personnel of a medical institution use blood that does not meet the standards stipulated by the State for patients in violation of the provisions of this Law, the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order corrections; if harm is caused to the patient's health, compensation shall be made according to law, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 23 If health administrative departments or their staff members neglect their duties in the supervision and management of blood donation and use, causing serious consequences, and if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 24 This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 1998.
