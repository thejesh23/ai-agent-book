# Law of the People's Republic of China on Active Service Officers

Adopted at the Third Session of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 5, 1988

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Regulations on the Service of Active Service Officers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army at the Seventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 12, 1994

Amended for the second time in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Regulations on the Service of Active Service Officers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army at the Nineteenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 28, 2000

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to build a contingent of active service officers who are revolutionary, younger, more knowledgeable, and more professional, so as to facilitate the People's Liberation Army in fulfilling the tasks assigned by the State.

Article 2 Active service officers of the People's Liberation Army (hereinafter referred to as officers) are active servicemen who are appointed to positions at or above the platoon level or to primary-level or higher professional technical positions, and who are awarded corresponding military ranks.

Officers are divided, according to the nature of their duties, into military officers, political officers, logistics officers, equipment officers, and professional technical officers.

Article 3 Officers are part of the State personnel.

Officers perform the sacred duties conferred by the Constitution and laws, and enjoy a status and honor in social life commensurate with their duties.

The State guarantees the lawful rights and interests of officers in accordance with the law.

Article 4 The selection and appointment of officers shall adhere to the principles of appointing people on their merit, combining moral integrity with professional competence, emphasizing actual performance, and ensuring timely exchange, and shall be subject to democratic supervision and respect for public opinion.

Article 5 The State determines the various treatments for officers in accordance with the principle of giving preferential treatment to active servicemen.

Article 6 Officers who meet the conditions for retirement from active service as provided for in this Law shall retire from active service.

Article 7 The General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army is in charge of the management of officers throughout the army, and the political organs at or above the regiment level are in charge of the management of officers in their respective units.

## Chapter II Basic Conditions, Sources, and Training of Officers

Article 8 Officers must meet the following basic conditions:

(1) Be loyal to the motherland and the Communist Party of China, have firm revolutionary ideals and convictions, serve the people wholeheartedly, and consciously devote themselves to the cause of national defense;

(2) Abide by the Constitution, laws, and regulations, implement the State's guidelines, policies, and the military's rules and systems, obey orders, and follow commands;

(3) Possess the theoretical and policy level, modern military, scientific, cultural, and professional knowledge, and organizational and command capabilities necessary to competently perform their duties, have received training in military academies and obtained corresponding academic qualifications, and be in good health;

(4) Care for soldiers, lead by example, be fair and upright, be honest in performing official duties, work hard, and not fear sacrifice.

Article 9 Sources of officers:

(1) Selection of outstanding soldiers and graduates of ordinary secondary schools for study in military academies and subsequent graduation;

(2) Recruitment of graduates from institutions of higher learning;

(3) Transfer from civilian cadres;

(4) Recruitment of professional and technical personnel and other personnel from outside the military.

In wartime, as needed, officers may be directly appointed from among soldiers, mobilized reserve officers, and personnel from non-military departments.

Article 10 The People's Liberation Army implements a system of promoting officers after training in military academies.

Military, political, logistics, and equipment officers, upon each promotion to a command position, shall undergo training in corresponding military academies or other training institutions. Officers holding command positions at or below the battalion level shall undergo training in primary command academies; officers holding command positions at the regiment and division levels shall undergo training in intermediate command academies; officers holding command positions at the corps level and above shall undergo training in advanced command academies.

Officers serving in organs shall undergo training in corresponding military academies.

Professional technical officers, upon each promotion to a professional technical position, shall undergo training in academies corresponding to their specialty; when such training cannot be met by academies, they shall complete the prescribed continuing education tasks through other means.

## Chapter III Assessment of Officers and Appointment and Removal of Positions

Article 11 Leaders at all levels and political organs shall, according to their division of responsibilities, assess the officers under their command.

The assessment of officers shall combine leadership with the masses, be based on the basic conditions for officers and the standards, procedures, and methods for officer assessment prescribed by the Central Military Commission, and focus on actual work performance for a comprehensive assessment. The assessment results shall be classified into three grades: excellent, competent, and incompetent, and shall serve as the main basis for appointing or removing officers from their positions. The assessment results shall be communicated to the officer concerned.

The appointment or removal of an officer from a position shall be preceded by an assessment; no appointment or removal shall be made without an assessment.

Article 12 Authority for appointment and removal of officer positions:

(1) Positions from Chief of the General Staff and Director of the General Political Department down to the regular division level shall be appointed and removed by the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;

(2) Positions at the deputy division level (regular brigade level), regular regiment level (deputy brigade level), and senior professional technical officer positions shall be appointed and removed by the Chief of the General Staff, the Director of the General Political Department, the Minister and Political Commissar of the General Logistics Department, the Minister and Political Commissar of the General Armament Department, the commanders and political commissars of major military regions and services/arms, or units of equivalent major military region level; regular regiment level (deputy brigade level) officer positions in units of deputy major military region level shall be appointed and removed by the principal leaders of such units;

(3) Positions at the deputy regiment level and regular battalion level, and intermediate professional technical officer positions, shall be appointed and removed by the principal leaders of group armies or other corps-level units with appointment and removal authority; regular battalion level officer positions in independent divisions shall be appointed and removed by the principal leaders of such independent divisions;

(4) Positions at the deputy battalion level and below, and primary professional technical officer positions, shall be appointed and removed by the principal leaders of division (brigade) level units or other division (brigade) level units with appointment and removal authority.

The appointment and removal of officer positions listed in the preceding paragraph shall be handled in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 13 When carrying out urgent tasks such as combat operations or disaster relief, superior leaders have the authority to temporarily remove from their positions officers under their command who disobey orders, fail to perform their duties, or are incompetent, and may temporarily assign other servicemen to act in their stead; when officer positions become vacant for other reasons, superior leaders may also temporarily assign servicemen to act in their stead.

Temporary removal or temporary assignment of a serviceman to act in an officer position in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be reported as soon as possible to the superior authority with appointment and removal power for review and decision, and the appointment and removal procedures shall be completed.

Article 14 The maximum ages for holding positions in peacetime for military, political, logistics, and equipment officers in combat units are respectively:

(1) For platoon-level positions: 30 years of age;

(2) For company-level positions: 35 years of age;

(3) For battalion-level positions: 40 years of age;

(4) For regiment-level positions: 45 years of age;

(5) For division-level positions: 50 years of age;

(6) For corps-level positions: 55 years of age;

(7) For major military region-level positions: 63 years of age for deputy positions, 65 years of age for principal positions.

For officers holding battalion-level and regiment-level positions serving on naval vessels, the maximum ages for holding positions are 45 and 50 years of age respectively; for officers holding regiment-level positions engaged in flying, the maximum age for holding the position is 50 years of age.

For a small number of officers holding division-level and corps-level positions in combat units who are needed for work, the maximum age for holding the position may be appropriately extended upon approval according to the appointment and removal authority, provided that the extension for division-level and regular corps-level officers shall not exceed five years, and for deputy corps-level officers shall not exceed three years.

Article 15 For officers at the deputy regiment level and below and at the major military region level in units other than combat units, the maximum ages for holding positions shall be implemented in accordance with the corresponding provisions of Paragraph 1, Article 14 of this Law; for officers at the regular regiment level, the maximum age for holding the position is 50 years of age; for officers at the division level, the maximum age for holding the position is 55 years of age; for officers at the deputy corps and regular corps levels, the maximum ages for holding positions are 58 and 60 years of age respectively.

Article 16 The maximum ages for holding positions in peacetime for professional technical officers are respectively:

(1) For primary professional technical positions: 40 years of age;

(2) For intermediate professional technical positions: 50 years of age;

(3) For senior professional technical positions: 60 years of age.

For a small number of officers holding senior professional technical positions who are needed for work, the maximum age for holding the position may be appropriately extended upon approval according to the appointment and removal authority, provided that the extension shall not exceed five years.

Article 17 The minimum tenure for holding principal positions at the platoon, company, battalion, regiment, division (brigade), and corps levels in peacetime is three years respectively.

Article 18 For officers holding positions such as section chief, division chief, department chief, bureau chief, minister, and equivalent leadership positions in organs and academies, the minimum tenure for holding the position shall be implemented with reference to the provisions of Article 17 of this Law.

For officers such as staff officers, cadres, secretaries, assistants, and instructors in organs and academies, the minimum tenure for each position grade is three years.

Article 19 The minimum tenure for holding positions in peacetime for professional technical officers shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Article 20 Only after an officer has served the minimum tenure in a position may he be promoted step by step based on authorized vacancies and his moral integrity and professional competence.

Officers with outstanding moral integrity and professional competence, remarkable performance, and work needs may be promoted ahead of schedule; those who are exceptionally outstanding may be promoted by skipping ranks.

Article 21 For promotion to a position, an officer must possess the qualifications required for the proposed position, such as required experience in previous positions, educational level, and training in military academies. Specific conditions shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 22 Officer positions shall be appointed in accordance with the authorized strength and authorized position grades.

Article 23 If an officer is assessed as incompetent, he shall be transferred to a lower position or assigned to other work, and his treatment shall be determined according to the new position.

Article 24 For officers holding positions at the division, corps, and major military region levels, the maximum tenure for holding principal and deputy positions in peacetime is ten years respectively. If the maximum tenure is served, the current position shall be removed.

Article 25 In accordance with the needs of national defense construction, the military may dispatch officers to non-military departments to perform tasks assigned by the military.

Article 26 Officers may be transferred to civilian cadre positions in accordance with the provisions of the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Exchange and Recusal of Officers

Article 27 Officers shall be exchanged between different posts or different units. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 28 Officers who have served the following tenures in a single post shall be exchanged:

(1) For those holding principal positions at the division level and below in combat units: four years; for those holding principal positions at the corps level: five years;

(2) For those holding principal positions at the corps level and below in units other than combat units: five years;

(3) For those holding positions such as section chief, division chief, department chief, and equivalent leadership positions in organs: four years; for those holding positions such as bureau chief, minister, and equivalent leadership positions: five years; except for a small number of highly specialized positions or those with special work needs.

Officers holding leadership positions at the division and corps levels who have worked continuously in the same unit for 25 and 30 years respectively shall be exchanged.

Officers holding other positions shall also be exchanged as needed.

Article 29 The exchange of officers working in arduous areas to other areas shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Article 30 Officers who are related by marriage, direct blood relation, collateral blood relation within three generations, or close affinity shall not hold positions with a direct superior-subordinate relationship or a relationship separated by one level of leadership, shall not serve in the same unit in positions both directly subordinate to the same leader, and shall not serve in the organ of the party holding a leadership position.

Article 31 Officers shall not hold principal positions in the military sub-district (division-level garrison command) of their place of origin or in the people's armed forces departments of counties, cities, or municipal districts, except when specially needed for work.

Article 32 When performing duties, an officer shall recuse himself if the matter involves himself or the interests of persons related to him by the kinship relationships listed in Article 30 of this Law, except when performing combat missions or other urgent tasks.

## Chapter V Rewards and Sanctions for Officers

Article 33 Officers who make outstanding contributions or achieve remarkable results in combat or military construction, or who make other significant contributions to the State and the people, shall be rewarded in accordance with the provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Rewards include: commendation, Merit Citation Class III, Merit Citation Class II, Merit Citation Class I, honorary title, and other rewards prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 34 Officers who violate military discipline shall be sanctioned in accordance with the provisions of the Central Military Commission.

Sanctions include: warning, serious warning, demerit, major demerit, demotion (in position or grade) or reduction in rank, removal from post, expulsion from military service, and other sanctions prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 35 For an officer removed from his post, a new position shall be appointed based on the specific circumstances of his offense; if no new position is appointed, the treatment corresponding to his position grade shall be determined.

Article 36 If an officer violates the law and constitutes a crime, he shall be investigated for criminal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Treatment of Officers

Article 37 Officers shall implement a position-military rank grade salary system and a regular salary increase system, enjoy allowances and subsidies in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State and the military, and shall have their salaries adjusted in a timely manner with the development of the national economy. Specific standards and measures shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Officers shall continue to receive their salaries during periods of off-duty training, leave, medical treatment and recuperation, and while awaiting reassignment after removal from their posts.

Article 38 Officers enjoy publicly funded medical treatment. Relevant departments shall do a good job in the medical care of officers and properly arrange for their medical treatment and recuperation.

Officers shall enjoy military insurance benefits in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State and the military.

Article 39 Officers' housing shall implement a support system combining apartment housing with self-owned housing. Officers shall either use apartment housing or purchase self-owned housing in accordance with regulations, and enjoy corresponding housing subsidies and preferential treatment.

Article 40 Officers enjoy leave benefits. Superior leaders shall arrange leave for officers annually in accordance with regulations.

Officers in units performing combat missions shall have their leave suspended.

After the State issues a mobilization order, officers on leave who are required to return to their units according to the mobilization order shall automatically end their leave and immediately return to their units.

Article 41 The family members of officers accompanying them to the military station, their employment, job transfers, and their children's education shall enjoy preferential treatment from the State and society.

If an officer meets the conditions for having his family accompany him to the military station, upon approval by the political organ at or above the division (brigade) level, his spouse, minor children, and children without independent living ability may accompany him; if they have rural household registration, it shall be converted to urban household registration.

When a unit is relocated or an officer is transferred, the accompanying family members may be transferred accordingly.

If an officer is 50 years of age or older and has no children nearby, one of his employed children may be transferred to the officer's location. If the transferred child is married, his or her spouse, minor children, and children without independent living ability may be transferred accordingly.

The employment and job transfers of accompanying family members of officers and transferred children of officers and their spouses shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 42 After an officer dies in the line of duty or from illness, his accompanying family members shall be transferred to the government for placement and management. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter VII Retirement from Active Service

Article 43 The minimum years of active service in peacetime for military, political, logistics, and equipment officers are respectively:

(1) For platoon-level positions: eight years;

(2) For company-level positions: ten years for deputy positions, twelve years for principal positions;

(3) For battalion-level positions: fourteen years for deputy positions, sixteen years for principal positions;

(4) For regiment-level positions: eighteen years for deputy positions, twenty years for principal positions.

Article 44 The minimum years of active service in peacetime for professional technical officers are respectively:

(1) For primary professional technical positions: twelve years;

(2) For intermediate professional technical positions: sixteen years;

(3) For senior professional technical positions: twenty years.

Article 45 Officers who have not reached the minimum years of active service in peacetime shall not retire from active service. However, under any of the following circumstances, they shall retire from active service ahead of schedule:

(1) Being wounded, ill, or disabled and unable to persist in normal work;

(2) Being assessed as incompetent and unsuitable for other arrangements;

(3) Having committed serious errors and being unsuitable to continue active service;

(4) Being transferred out of the military to work in a non-military department;

(5) Needing to retire from active service due to adjustments and reductions in the military's organizational structure and establishment.

If an officer who has not reached the minimum years of active service in peacetime requests early retirement from active service and the request is not approved, and after education he still insists on retiring from active service, he may be dealt with by demotion (in position or grade) or by cancellation of his officer status, and then be processed as retired from active service.

Article 46 Officers who reach the maximum age for active service in peacetime shall retire from active service.

The maximum ages for active service in peacetime for officers are respectively:

(1) For regular regiment-level positions: 50 years of age;

(2) For division-level positions: 55 years of age;

(3) For corps-level positions: 58 years of age for deputy positions, 60 years of age for principal positions;

(4) For other positions, the maximum age for active service is the same as the maximum age for holding the position.

Article 47 If an officer has not reached the maximum age for active service in peacetime but falls under any of the following circumstances, he shall retire from active service:

(1) Needing to retire from active service after serving the maximum tenure in a position;

(2) Being wounded, ill, or disabled and unable to persist in normal work;

(3) Being restricted by the military's authorized strength and unable to be adjusted or used;

(4) Being transferred out of the military to work in a non-military department;

(5) Having other reasons necessitating retirement from active service.

Article 48 The authority for approving retirement from active service is the same as the authority for appointing and removing officers from their positions.

Article 49 After retiring from active service, officers shall be placed by means of transfer to civilian work with government-arranged jobs and positions, or by government assistance in finding employment with the provision of severance pay; some may also be placed by means of demobilization or retirement.

Officers holding positions at the division level and above and senior professional technical positions shall be placed in retirement after retiring from active service; some may also be placed by transfer to civilian work or other means.

Officers holding positions at the regiment level and below and primary and intermediate professional technical positions shall be placed by transfer to civilian work or other means after retiring from active service.

For officers who retire from active service and are placed by government-arranged jobs and positions or by government assistance in finding employment with severance pay, the government shall provide vocational training as needed.

Officers who have not reached the maximum age for active service but have basically lost their ability to work shall be placed in retirement after retiring from active service.

Officers who have served on active duty for 30 years or more, or whose total active service and work experience amount to 30 years or more, or who are 50 years of age or older, and who hold positions at the division level or above, may, upon their own application and with organizational approval, be placed in retirement after retiring from active service; those holding positions at the regiment level who are unsuitable for transfer to civilian work or other placement may, with organizational approval, be placed in retirement after retiring from active service.

Article 50 Officers who have reached the maximum age for active service and meet the conditions for retirement with honors as prescribed by the State may retire with honors. For work needs or other reasons, retirement with honors may be advanced or postponed upon approval.

Article 51 Specific measures for the placement and management of officers after retirement from active service shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

After officers retire with honors and officers at the corps level and above retire, they shall be placed and managed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 The General Political Department of the People's Liberation Army shall formulate implementing measures in accordance with this Law, which shall be implemented after being reported to the State Council and the Central Military Commission for approval.Article 53 This Law applies to active-duty officers of the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, with specific measures to be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 54 This Law (originally titled the "Regulations on the Service of Active-Duty Officers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army") shall come into force as of January 1, 1989. The "Regulations on the Service of Cadres of the Chinese People's Liberation Army," approved by the Standing Committee of the Fifth National People's Congress on August 18, 1978, and promulgated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission on August 19, 1978, shall be simultaneously abolished.
