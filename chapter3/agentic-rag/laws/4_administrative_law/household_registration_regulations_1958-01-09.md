# Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Household Registration

Adopted at the 91st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on January 9, 1958

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated to maintain social order, protect the rights and interests of citizens, and serve socialist construction.

Article 2 All citizens of the People's Republic of China shall register their households in accordance with the provisions of these Regulations.

The household registration of active-duty military personnel shall be handled by military authorities in accordance with the relevant regulations governing active-duty military personnel.

The household registration of foreigners and stateless persons residing within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by these Regulations, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 3 The work of household registration shall be supervised by public security organs at all levels.

In cities and towns where a public security police station is established, the jurisdiction of the police station shall serve as the household registration jurisdiction; in townships and towns without a public security police station, the jurisdiction of the township or town shall serve as the household registration jurisdiction. The township or town people's committee and the public security police station shall be the household registration authorities.

For households residing within the premises of government organs, organizations, schools, enterprises, institutions, and public dormitories, the respective units shall designate personnel to assist the household registration authorities in handling household registration; for households residing in scattered locations, the household registration authorities shall handle the registration directly.

For households of non-active-duty military personnel residing within military organs and military dormitories, the respective units shall designate personnel to assist the household registration authorities in handling household registration.

For households of agricultural, fishery, salt industry, forestry, animal husbandry, handicraft, and other production cooperatives, the cooperatives shall designate personnel to assist the household registration authorities in handling household registration. Households not belonging to cooperatives shall have their registration handled directly by the household registration authorities.

Article 4 Household registration authorities shall establish household registration ledgers.

In cities, on waterways, and in towns where a public security police station is established, a household register booklet shall be issued to each household.

In rural areas, household register booklets shall be issued on a cooperative basis; households not belonging to cooperatives shall not be issued household register booklets.

The entries in the household registration ledger and the household register booklet shall have the effect of certifying the identity of citizens.

Article 5 Household registration shall be based on the household unit. Persons living together with the head of the household in one place shall be registered as one household, with the head of the household as the householder. Persons living alone shall establish an independent household, with themselves as the householder. Households residing within the premises of government organs, organizations, schools, enterprises, institutions, and public dormitories may be registered as one household or as separate households. The householder shall be responsible for declaring household registration in accordance with the provisions of these Regulations.

Article 6 Citizens shall register as permanent residents at their place of habitual residence. A citizen may register as a permanent resident in only one place.

Article 7 Within one month after the birth of an infant, the householder, relatives, guardian, or neighbor shall declare the birth registration to the household registration authority at the infant's place of habitual residence.

For abandoned infants, the adopter or the child welfare institution shall declare the birth registration to the household registration authority.

Article 8 Upon the death of a citizen, in cities before burial, and in rural areas within one month, the householder, relatives, guardian, or neighbor shall declare the death registration to the household registration authority and cancel the household registration. If a citizen dies in a place of temporary residence, the household registration authority at the place of temporary residence shall notify the household registration authority at the place of habitual residence to cancel the household registration.

If a citizen dies due to an accident or the cause of death is unknown, the householder or the person who discovers the body shall immediately report to the local public security police station or the township or town people's committee.

Article 9 If an infant dies after birth but before the birth registration is declared, both the birth and death registrations shall be declared simultaneously.

Article 10 When a citizen moves out of the household registration jurisdiction, the citizen or the householder shall, before moving out, declare the out-migration registration to the household registration authority, obtain a migration certificate, and cancel the household registration.

When a citizen moves from a rural area to a city, the citizen must hold an employment certificate from the city's labor department, an admission certificate from a school, or a certificate of permission for in-migration from the city's household registration authority, and apply for the out-migration procedures at the household registration authority of the place of habitual residence.

When a citizen moves to a border area, the move must be approved by the public security organ of the county, city, or municipal district of the place of habitual residence.

Article 11 For citizens conscripted for active service, before enlistment, the citizen or the householder shall, holding the conscription notice for the conscripted citizen, declare the out-migration registration to the household registration authority of the place of habitual residence, cancel the household registration, and shall not be issued a migration certificate.

Article 12 For an arrested criminal suspect, the arresting authority shall, while notifying the suspect's family, also notify the household registration authority of the suspect's place of habitual residence to cancel the household registration.

Article 13 When a citizen migrates, from the time of arrival at the place of in-migration, within three days in cities and within ten days in rural areas, the citizen or the householder shall, holding the migration certificate, declare the in-migration registration to the household registration authority and surrender the migration certificate.

Citizens without a migration certificate shall declare in-migration registration to the household registration authority at the place of in-migration using the following documents:

1. For demobilized, transferred to reserve, or discharged military personnel, using the certificate issued by the county or city military service authority or a military authority at or above the regimental level;

2. For overseas Chinese and students returning from abroad, using the passport or entry document of the People's Republic of China;

3. For persons released by the People's Court, the People's Procuratorate, or a public security organ, using the certificate issued by the releasing authority.

Article 14 When convicts on parole or probation, persons under surveillance, and other persons deprived of political rights according to law migrate, they must have the household registration authority forward the matter to the county, city, or municipal district People's Court or public security organ for approval before the out-migration registration can be processed; upon arrival at the place of in-migration, they shall immediately declare the in-migration registration to the household registration authority.

Article 15 When a citizen stays temporarily for more than three days in a city outside the scope of the city or county of habitual residence, the householder at the place of temporary residence or the citizen themselves shall, within three days, declare the temporary residence registration to the household registration authority, and declare its cancellation before leaving; when staying temporarily in a hotel, the hotel shall maintain a guest register and register guests promptly.

When a citizen stays temporarily within the scope of the city or county of habitual residence, or in a rural area outside the scope of the city or county of habitual residence, no temporary residence registration is required, except when staying in a hotel, where the hotel shall maintain a guest register and register guests promptly.

Article 16 If a citizen leaves the place of habitual residence for private affairs and the period of temporary stay exceeds three months, the citizen shall apply to the household registration authority for an extension of time or for migration procedures; if there is neither reason to extend the time nor conditions for migration, the citizen shall return to the place of habitual residence.

Article 17 When the content of household registration needs to be changed or corrected, the householder or the citizen themselves shall declare it to the household registration authority; after verification, the household registration authority shall make the change or correction.

When the household registration authority deems it necessary, it may request the applicant to provide proof related to the change or correction.

Article 18 Changes of a citizen's name shall be handled in accordance with the following provisions:

1. When a person under the age of 18 needs to change their name, the person themselves, their parents, or their adopter shall apply for the change registration to the household registration authority;

2. When a person aged 18 or above needs to change their name, the person themselves shall apply for the change registration to the household registration authority.

Article 19 When a citizen's household registration changes due to marriage, divorce, adoption, recognition, household division, household merger, disappearance, recovery from disappearance, or other reasons, the householder or the citizen themselves shall declare the change registration to the household registration authority.

Article 20 In any of the following circumstances, depending on the severity of the case, a public security administrative penalty shall be imposed or criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

1. Failing to declare household registration in accordance with the provisions of these Regulations;

2. Falsely declaring household registration;

3. Forging, altering, transferring, lending, or selling household registration documents;

4. Assuming another person's household registration under a false name;

5. A hotel manager failing to register guests in accordance with the provisions.

Article 21 If, during household registration work, the household registration authority discovers counter-revolutionaries or other criminals, it shall refer the matter to the judicial authorities for the pursuit of criminal liability according to law.

Article 22 The formats of household register booklets, ledgers, forms, and certificates shall be uniformly designed by the Ministry of Public Security of the People's Republic of China, and shall be uniformly printed by the public security organs of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Citizens shall pay the production cost fee for receiving household register booklets and migration certificates.

Article 23 The autonomous organs of national autonomous areas may, in accordance with the spirit of these Regulations and in light of local specific conditions, formulate separate measures.

Article 24 These Regulations shall come into effect on the date of promulgation.
