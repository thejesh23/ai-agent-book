# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Promotion of Private Education

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 28, 2002

First Amendment on June 29, 2013, according to the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics at the Third Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on November 7, 2016, according to the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Promotion of Private Education at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Third Amendment on December 29, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China at the Seventh Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution and the Education Law for the purpose of implementing the strategy of revitalizing the country through science and education, promoting the healthy development of private education, and safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of private schools and their students.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities in which social organizations or individuals other than state organs, using non-state financial funds, establish schools and other educational institutions for the public. Matters not provided for in this Law shall be handled in accordance with the Education Law and other relevant education laws.

Article 3 Private education is a public welfare undertaking and is an integral part of the socialist education cause.

The state adopts the policy of active encouragement, strong support, correct guidance, and lawful management towards private education.

People's governments at all levels shall incorporate private education into their national economic and social development plans.

Article 4 Private schools shall abide by laws and regulations, implement the state's educational policy, ensure educational quality, and be committed to cultivating various types of talent for the socialist construction cause.

Private schools shall adhere to the principle of separation of education from religion. No organization or individual may use religion to engage in activities that hinder the state's educational system.

Article 5 Private schools and public schools have equal legal status. The state guarantees the autonomy of private schools in running their schools.

The state guarantees the legitimate rights and interests of the sponsors, principals, faculty, and students of private schools.

Article 6 The state encourages donations for school establishment.

The state gives awards and commendations to organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of private education.

Article 7 The education administrative department of the State Council is responsible for the overall planning, comprehensive coordination, and macro-management of private education nationwide.

The human resources and social security administrative department of the State Council and other relevant departments are respectively responsible for related private education work within the scope of their duties as prescribed by the State Council.

Article 8 The education administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are in charge of private education work within their respective administrative regions.

The human resources and social security administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments are respectively responsible for related private education work within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 9 Grassroots organizations of the Communist Party of China in private schools shall carry out Party activities and strengthen Party building in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution of the Communist Party of China.

## Chapter II Establishment

Article 10 A social organization that sponsors a private school shall have the status of a legal person.

An individual who sponsors a private school shall have political rights and full capacity for civil conduct.

A private school shall meet the conditions for being a legal person.

Article 11 The establishment of a private school shall meet the needs of local educational development and satisfy the conditions stipulated by the Education Law and other relevant laws and regulations.

The standards for establishing private schools shall refer to the standards for establishing public schools of the same level and type.

Article 12 The establishment of private schools that provide academic education, preschool education, self-study examination assistance, or other cultural education shall be examined and approved by the education administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level according to the authority prescribed by the state. The establishment of private schools that primarily provide vocational qualification training or vocational skills training shall be examined and approved by the human resources and social security administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level according to the authority prescribed by the state, and a copy shall be sent to the education administrative department at the same level for record.

Article 13 To apply for the preparatory establishment of a private school, the sponsor shall submit the following materials to the examination and approval authority:

(1) An application report, which shall mainly include: the sponsor, training objectives, school scale, school level, school form, school conditions, internal management system, and the raising, management, and use of funds;

(2) The name and address of the sponsor, or the name and address of the organization;

(3) The source of assets, the amount of funds, and valid certification documents, specifying the property rights;

(4) For school property of a donation nature, a donation agreement shall be submitted, specifying the donor's name, the amount of donated assets, their purpose and management methods, and relevant valid certification documents.

Article 14 The examination and approval authority shall, within thirty days from the date of receiving the application for the preparatory establishment of a private school, make a decision in writing on whether to approve.

If the preparatory establishment is approved, a preparatory establishment approval certificate shall be issued. If the preparatory establishment is not approved, the reasons shall be stated.

The preparatory establishment period shall not exceed three years. If it exceeds three years, the sponsor shall re-submit the application.

Article 15 To apply for the formal establishment of a private school, the sponsor shall submit the following materials to the examination and approval authority:

(1) The preparatory establishment approval certificate;

(2) A report on the preparatory establishment situation;

(3) The school charter and the list of members of the first school council, board of directors, or other decision-making body;

(4) Valid certification documents for the school's assets;

(5) Qualification certification documents for the principal, teachers, and financial personnel.

Article 16 If the school conditions are met and the establishment standards are reached, an application for formal establishment may be made directly, and the materials specified in Articles 13 and 15 (3), (4), and (5) of this Law shall be submitted.

Article 17 For an application for the formal establishment of a private school, the examination and approval authority shall, within three months from the date of receiving the application, make a decision in writing on whether to approve and deliver it to the applicant. For an application for the formal establishment of a private institution of higher education, the examination and approval authority may also, within six months from the date of receiving the application, make a decision in writing on whether to approve and deliver it to the applicant.

Article 18 The examination and approval authority shall issue a school license to a private school approved for formal establishment.

If the formal establishment is not approved, the examination and approval authority shall state the reasons.

Article 19 The sponsor of a private school may independently choose to establish a non-profit or for-profit private school. However, no for-profit private school may be established to provide compulsory education.

Sponsors of non-profit private schools shall not obtain school-running profits, and all school surpluses shall be used for school operation.

Sponsors of for-profit private schools may obtain school-running profits, and the school surpluses shall be handled in accordance with the Company Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

After obtaining the school license, a private school shall undergo legal person registration, and the registration authority shall handle it according to law.

## Chapter III Organization and Activities of Schools

Article 20 A private school shall establish a school council, board of directors, or other form of decision-making body and establish corresponding supervision mechanisms.

The sponsor of a private school participates in the school's operation and management according to the authority and procedures stipulated in the school charter.

Article 21 The school council or board of directors shall be composed of the sponsor or its representative, the principal, representatives of the faculty, and other personnel. At least one-third of the council members or directors shall have more than five years of teaching and educational experience.

The school council or board of directors shall consist of at least five members and shall have one chairperson. The list of the chairperson, council members, or chairperson and directors shall be submitted to the examination and approval authority for record.

Article 22 The school council or board of directors exercises the following powers:

(1) Appointing and dismissing the principal;

(2) Amending the school charter and formulating school rules and regulations;

(3) Formulating development plans and approving annual work plans;

(4) Raising school funds and reviewing budgets and final accounts;

(5) Deciding on the staffing quota and salary standards for faculty;

(6) Deciding on the division, merger, or termination of the school;

(7) Deciding on other major matters.

The powers of other forms of decision-making bodies shall be exercised by reference to the provisions of this Article.

Article 23 The legal representative of a private school shall be the chairperson of the council, the chairperson of the board of directors, or the principal.

Article 24 A private school shall appoint a principal who meets the conditions for the appointment of principals of public schools of the same level and type, and the age limit may be appropriately relaxed.

Article 25 The principal of a private school is responsible for the school's teaching, education, and administrative management and exercises the following powers:

(1) Implementing the decisions of the school council, board of directors, or other form of decision-making body;

(2) Implementing development plans and drafting annual work plans, financial budgets, and school rules and regulations;

(3) Appointing and dismissing school staff and implementing rewards and punishments;

(4) Organizing teaching, education, and scientific research activities to ensure the quality of education;

(5) Being responsible for the daily management of the school;

(6) Other authorizations from the school council, board of directors, or other form of decision-making body.

Article 26 A private school may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, issue academic certificates, completion certificates, or training qualification certificates to enrolled students based on their category, length of study, and academic performance.

For students receiving vocational skills training, if they pass the assessment of a registered vocational skills assessment institution, they may be issued a national vocational qualification certificate.

Article 27 A private school shall, in accordance with the law, ensure the participation of faculty in democratic management and supervision through forms such as the faculty congress, which is primarily composed of teachers.

Teachers and other staff of private schools have the right to establish trade union organizations in accordance with the Trade Union Law to protect their legitimate rights and interests.

## Chapter IV Teachers and Students

Article 28 Teachers and students of private schools have the same legal status as teachers and students of public schools.

Article 29 Teachers appointed by private schools shall have the teaching qualifications prescribed by the state.

Article 30 Private schools shall provide ideological and moral education and professional training for their teachers.

Article 31 Private schools shall, in accordance with the law, guarantee the wages, benefits, and other legitimate rights and interests of their faculty and staff, and pay social insurance premiums for them.

The state encourages private schools to handle supplementary endowment insurance for their faculty and staff in accordance with state regulations.

Article 32 Faculty and staff of private schools enjoy the same rights as faculty and staff of public schools in terms of professional training, job appointment, calculation of teaching years and length of service, commendation and awards, and social activities, in accordance with the law.

Article 33 Private schools shall, in accordance with the law, protect the legitimate rights and interests of their students.

Private schools shall establish a student status management system in accordance with state regulations and implement rewards or punishments for students.

Article 34 Students of private schools enjoy the same rights as students of public schools of the same level and type in terms of further education, employment, social preferences, and participation in advanced selection.

## Chapter V School Assets and Financial Management

Article 35 Private schools shall, in accordance with the law, establish financial, accounting, and asset management systems, and set up accounting books in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 36 Private schools enjoy legal person property rights over the assets invested by the sponsor, state-owned assets, donated property, and school operating accumulations.

Article 37 During the existence of a private school, all assets shall be managed and used by the private school in accordance with the law, and no organization or individual may encroach upon them.

No organization or individual may charge any fees from private educational institutions in violation of laws and regulations.

Article 38 The items and standards of fees charged by private schools shall be determined based on factors such as school operating costs and market demand, disclosed to the public, and subject to the supervision of relevant competent departments.

The specific methods for fee collection by non-profit private schools shall be formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. The fee standards for for-profit private schools shall be subject to market regulation and independently decided by the school.

Fees collected by private schools shall be mainly used for teaching and educational activities, improving school conditions, and ensuring the treatment of faculty and staff.

Article 39 The use of assets and financial management of private schools shall be subject to the supervision of the examination and approval authority and other relevant departments.

Private schools shall prepare financial accounting reports at the end of each fiscal year, entrust an accounting firm to conduct audits in accordance with the law, and publish the audit results.

## Chapter VI Management and Supervision

Article 40 The education administrative department and relevant departments shall provide guidance on the teaching, education, and teacher training work of private schools.

Article 41 The education administrative department and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, supervise private schools, establish a private school information disclosure and credit file system to promote the improvement of school quality; organize or entrust social intermediary organizations to assess the school-running level and educational quality, and publish the assessment results to the public.

Article 42 The enrollment brochures and advertisements of private schools shall be submitted to the examination and approval authority for record.

Article 43 If a private school infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of a student, the student and their relatives have the right to appeal to the education administrative department and other relevant departments, and the relevant departments shall handle the matter promptly.

Article 44 The state supports and encourages social intermediary organizations to provide services to private schools.

## Chapter VII Support and Rewards

Article 45 People's governments at or above the county level may establish special funds to subsidize the development of private schools and reward and commend collectives and individuals with outstanding contributions.

Article 46 People's governments at or above the county level may support private schools by purchasing services, providing student loans, scholarships and grants, and leasing or transferring idle state-owned assets. For non-profit private schools, support measures such as government subsidies, fund rewards, and donation incentives may also be adopted.

Article 47 Private schools enjoy tax preferential policies prescribed by the state. Among them, non-profit private schools enjoy the same tax preferential policies as public schools.

Article 48 Private schools may, in accordance with relevant state laws and regulations, accept donations from citizens, legal persons, or other organizations.

The state grants tax preferences to citizens, legal persons, or other organizations that donate property to private schools in accordance with relevant regulations and commends them.

Article 49 The state encourages financial institutions to use credit instruments to support the development of private education.

Article 50 If a people's government entrusts a private school to undertake compulsory education tasks, it shall allocate corresponding education funds in accordance with the entrustment agreement.

Article 51 When building or expanding non-profit private schools, the people's government shall provide land use preferences through allocation or other means in accordance with the same principles as for public schools. When building or expanding for-profit private schools, the people's government shall supply land in accordance with state regulations.

Education land shall not be used for other purposes.

Article 52 The state takes measures to support and encourage social organizations and individuals to establish private schools in ethnic minority areas and remote, impoverished areas to develop education.

## Chapter VIII Changes and Termination

Article 53 The division or merger of a private school shall, after financial liquidation, be reported by the school council or board of directors to the examination and approval authority for approval.

For an application for the division or merger of a private school, the examination and approval authority shall respond in writing within three months from the date of receiving the application. For an application for the division or merger of a private institution of higher education, the examination and approval authority may also respond in writing within six months from the date of receiving the application.

Article 54 A change of the sponsor of a private school shall be proposed by the sponsor, and after financial liquidation, with the consent of the school council or board of directors, shall be submitted to the examination and approval authority for verification and approval.

Article 55 A change in the name, level, or category of a private school shall be reported by the school council or board of directors to the examination and approval authority for approval.

For an application to change to another type of private school, the examination and approval authority shall respond in writing within three months from the date of receiving the application. For an application to change to a private institution of higher education, the examination and approval authority may also respond in writing within six months from the date of receiving the application.

Article 56 A private school shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) It requests termination according to the provisions of the school charter and has obtained approval from the examination and approval authority;

(2) Its school license is revoked;

(3) It is unable to continue operation due to insolvency.

Article 57 When a private school terminates, it shall properly arrange for its enrolled students. When a private school providing compulsory education terminates, the examination and approval authority shall assist the school in arranging for students to continue their studies.

Article 58 When a private school terminates, financial liquidation shall be carried out in accordance with the law.

If the private school requests termination on its own, it shall organize the liquidation. If it is legally revoked by the examination and approval authority, the examination and approval authority shall organize the liquidation. If it is terminated due to insolvency and inability to continue operation, the people's court shall organize the liquidation.

Article 59 The property of a private school shall be liquidated in the following order:

(1) Refund of tuition, miscellaneous fees, and other fees to students;

(2) Payment of wages and social insurance premiums due to faculty and staff;

(3) Repayment of other debts.

The remaining property of a non-profit private school after liquidating the above debts shall continue to be used for the operation of other non-profit schools. The remaining property of a for-profit private school after liquidating the above debts shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Company Law.

Article 60 For a terminated private school, the examination and approval authority shall recover the school license, destroy the seal, and cancel the registration.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

Article 61 If a private school violates the provisions of the Education Law or the Teachers Law in its educational activities, it shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Education Law and the Teachers Law.

Article 62 If a private school commits any of the following acts, the education administrative department, the human resources and social security administrative department, or other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections within a time limit and issue a warning; if there are illegal gains, the fees collected shall be refunded and the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to stop enrolling students and its school license shall be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Dividing or merging the private school without authorization;

(2) Unauthorizedly changing the name, level, category, or sponsor of the private school;

(3) Issuing false enrollment brochures or advertisements to defraud money;

(4) Illegally issuing or forging academic certificates, completion certificates, training certificates, or vocational qualification certificates;

(5) Having chaotic management that seriously affects teaching and education, resulting in adverse social impact;

(6) Submitting false certification documents or using other fraudulent means to conceal important facts to obtain a school license;

(7) Forging, altering, buying, selling, leasing, or lending the school license;

(8) Maliciously terminating school operation, withdrawing funds, or misappropriating school funds.

Article 63 If the education administrative department, the human resources and social security administrative department, or other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level commit any of the following acts, the superior authority shall order them to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if economic losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:(1) Failing to respond within the time limit to an application for establishment that has been accepted;

(2) Approving an application that does not meet the conditions prescribed by this Law;

(3) Neglecting management, resulting in serious consequences;

(4) Charging fees in violation of relevant state regulations;

(5) Infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of a private school;

(6) Other acts of abusing power or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 64 Where a private school is established without authorization in violation of relevant state regulations, the education administrative department or the human resources and social security administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, together with the public security, civil affairs, or market supervision and administration departments at the same level, order the cessation of the school's operation, refund all fees collected, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income on the founder; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 65 The term "private schools" as used in this Law includes other privately-run educational institutions established in accordance with the law.

The term "principal" as used in this Law includes the chief administrative officer of other privately-run educational institutions.

Article 66 Measures for the cooperation in running schools within China by foreign organizations and individuals shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 67 This Law shall come into force as of September 1, 2003. The "Regulations on the Running of Schools by Social Forces" promulgated by the State Council on July 31, 1997 shall be simultaneously repealed.
