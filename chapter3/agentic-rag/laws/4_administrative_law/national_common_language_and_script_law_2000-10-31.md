# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Standard Spoken and Written Chinese Language

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 2000

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purpose of promoting the normalization, standardization and healthy development of the standard spoken and written Chinese language, enabling it to better play its role in social life, and facilitating economic and cultural exchanges among all ethnic groups and regions.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, the standard spoken and written Chinese language refers to Putonghua (the common speech with pronunciation based on the Beijing dialect) and the standardized Chinese characters.

Article 3 The State promotes Putonghua and promotes the use of standardized Chinese characters.

Article 4 Citizens shall have the right to learn and use the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

The State shall provide conditions for citizens to learn and use the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

Local people's governments at various levels and the relevant departments shall take measures to promote Putonghua and the use of standardized Chinese characters.

Article 5 The use of the standard spoken and written Chinese language shall be conducive to safeguarding national sovereignty and national dignity, to national unity and ethnic solidarity, and to the building of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization.

Article 6 The State shall promulgate norms and standards for the standard spoken and written Chinese language, administer its social use, support its teaching and scientific research, and promote its normalization, enrichment and development.

Article 7 The State shall reward organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the cause of the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

Article 8 All ethnic groups shall have the freedom to use and develop their own spoken and written languages.

The use of the spoken and written languages of ethnic minorities shall be governed by the provisions of the Constitution, the Law on Regional Ethnic Autonomy and other relevant laws.

## Chapter II Use of the Standard Spoken and Written Chinese Language

Article 9 State organs shall use Putonghua and standardized Chinese characters as their official language and script, except where otherwise provided by law.

Article 10 Schools and other educational institutions shall use Putonghua and standardized Chinese characters as the basic language and script for education and teaching, except where otherwise provided by law.

Schools and other educational institutions shall teach Putonghua and standardized Chinese characters through courses in the Chinese language. The Chinese language textbooks used shall conform to the norms and standards of the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

Article 11 Publications in the Chinese language shall conform to the norms and standards of the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

Where foreign languages or scripts need to be used in Chinese language publications, necessary annotations shall be made in the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

Article 12 Radio stations and television stations shall use Putonghua as the basic language for broadcasting.

Where a foreign language needs to be used as the broadcasting language, approval from the radio and television department of the State Council shall be obtained.

Article 13 The public service industry shall use standardized Chinese characters as the basic script for service. Where foreign scripts are used on signs, advertisements, notices, signboards, etc., together with Chinese characters due to the needs of public services, standardized Chinese characters shall be used.

The public service industry is encouraged to use Putonghua as the service language.

Article 14 In the following circumstances, the standard spoken and written Chinese language shall be used as the basic language and script:

(1) Language and script used in radio, film and television;
(2) Script used on facilities in public places;
(3) Script used on signs and advertisements;
(4) Names of enterprises and institutions;
(5) Packaging and instructions of commodities sold within the territory of China.

Article 15 The standard spoken and written Chinese language used in information processing and information technology products shall conform to the norms and standards of the State.

Article 16 In the relevant provisions of this Chapter, dialects may be used under the following circumstances:

(1) When it is truly necessary for staff members of State organs to use them in the performance of official duties;
(2) When the broadcasting language is approved by the radio and television department of the State Council or the provincial radio and television department;
(3) When they are needed in artistic forms such as operas, films and television;
(4) When they are truly needed in publishing, teaching and research.

Article 17 In the relevant provisions of this Chapter, traditional Chinese characters or variant Chinese characters may be retained or used under the following circumstances:

(1) Cultural relics and historic sites;
(2) Variant characters in surnames;
(3) Artistic works such as calligraphy and seal carving;
(4) Handwritten inscriptions and signboards;
(5) When they are needed in publishing, teaching and research;
(6) Special circumstances approved by the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 18 The standard spoken and written Chinese language shall use the "Scheme for the Chinese Phonetic Alphabet" as the tool for spelling and phonetic notation.

The "Scheme for the Chinese Phonetic Alphabet" is the unified standard for the Roman alphabet spelling of Chinese personal names, place names and Chinese documents, and shall be used in fields where Chinese characters are inconvenient or cannot be used.

Primary education shall include teaching of the Chinese phonetic alphabet.

Article 19 Any position that uses Putonghua as the working language shall require its staff to have the ability to speak Putonghua.

Broadcasters, program hosts, film and television drama actors, teachers, and staff members of State organs who use Putonghua as the working language shall meet the grade standards prescribed by the State for Putonghua proficiency; those who have not yet met the prescribed grade standards shall receive training according to their respective circumstances.

Article 20 Teaching Chinese as a foreign language shall teach Putonghua and standardized Chinese characters.

## Chapter III Administration and Supervision

Article 21 The language and script department of the State Council shall be responsible for the planning, guidance, administration and supervision of the work concerning the standard spoken and written Chinese language.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall administer the use of the standard spoken and written Chinese language within their respective systems.

Article 22 Local language and script departments and other relevant departments shall administer and supervise the use of the standard spoken and written Chinese language within their respective administrative regions.

Article 23 The administrative departments for industry and commerce of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, administer and supervise the language and script used in enterprise names, commodity names and advertisements.

Article 24 The language and script department of the State Council shall promulgate the grade standards for Putonghua proficiency testing.

Article 25 The translation of proper nouns such as foreign personal names and place names, and scientific and technical terms into the standard spoken and written Chinese language shall be examined and approved by the language and script department of the State Council or other relevant departments.

Article 26 Where the use of language and script violates the relevant provisions of Chapter II of this Law and fails to conform to the norms and standards of the standard spoken and written Chinese language, citizens may raise criticisms and suggestions.

Where the language used by personnel specified in Paragraph 2 of Article 19 of this Law violates the relevant provisions of Chapter II of this Law, the relevant unit shall criticize and educate the directly responsible personnel; if they refuse to correct, the relevant unit shall take disciplinary action.

Where the script used on facilities, signs and advertisements in urban public places violates the relevant provisions of Chapter II of this Law, the relevant administrative department shall order correction; if correction is refused, a warning shall be issued, and the party shall be urged to make correction within a specified time limit.

Article 27 Where anyone, in violation of the provisions of this Law, interferes with others' learning and use of the standard spoken and written Chinese language, the relevant administrative department shall order correction within a specified time limit and issue a warning.

## Chapter IV Supplementary Provisions

Article 28 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2001.
