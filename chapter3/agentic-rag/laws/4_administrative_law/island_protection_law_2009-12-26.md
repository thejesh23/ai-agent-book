# Island Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on December 26, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting the ecosystems of islands and their surrounding sea areas, rationally developing and utilizing the natural resources of islands, safeguarding national maritime rights and interests, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law applies to the protection, development, utilization, and related management activities concerning islands belonging to the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "island" means a naturally formed area of land that is surrounded by seawater and is above water at high tide, including inhabited islands and uninhabited islands.

For the purposes of this Law, "island protection" means the protection of the ecosystems of islands and their surrounding sea areas, the protection of natural resources of uninhabited islands, and the protection of islands for special purposes.

Article 3 The State applies the principles of scientific planning, protection priority, rational development, and sustainable utilization to islands.

The State Council and the local people's governments at all levels in coastal areas shall incorporate island protection and rational development and utilization into national economic and social development plans, take effective measures to strengthen the protection and management of islands, and prevent damage to the ecosystems of islands and their surrounding sea areas.

Article 4 Uninhabited islands belong to the State, and the State Council exercises the ownership of uninhabited islands on behalf of the State.

Article 5 The competent oceanic department under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council shall, in accordance with the law and the division of responsibilities prescribed by the State Council, be responsible for the ecological protection of inhabited islands and their surrounding sea areas nationwide. The competent oceanic departments and other relevant departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level in coastal areas shall, within their respective responsibilities, be responsible for the ecological protection of inhabited islands and their surrounding sea areas within their respective administrative regions.

The competent oceanic department under the State Council shall be responsible for the management of the protection and development and utilization of uninhabited islands nationwide. The competent oceanic departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level in coastal areas shall be responsible for the relevant work concerning the protection and development and utilization management of uninhabited islands within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The names of islands shall be determined and published by the national administrative authority for place names and the competent oceanic department under the State Council in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council.

The local people's governments at or above the county level in coastal areas shall, in accordance with the State regulations, set up island name signs on islands where such signs are required.

It is prohibited to damage or move island name signs without authorization.

Article 7 The State Council and the local people's governments at all levels in coastal areas shall strengthen the publicity and education on island protection, enhance citizens' awareness of island protection, and reward units and individuals that have made remarkable achievements in island protection and related scientific research.

Any unit or individual has the obligation to abide by the laws on island protection and has the right to report to the competent oceanic department or other relevant departments any act that violates the laws on island protection or damages the island ecology.

## Chapter II Island Protection Planning

Article 8 The State implements an island protection planning system. Island protection plans are the basis for engaging in island protection and utilization activities.

The formulation of island protection plans shall follow the principle of being conducive to protecting and improving the ecosystems of islands and their surrounding sea areas and promoting the sustainable economic and social development of islands.

Before an island protection plan is submitted for approval, opinions from relevant experts and the public shall be solicited. After approval, the plan shall be promptly published to the public, except for those involving State secrets.

Article 9 The competent oceanic department under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level and military organs, organize the formulation of a national island protection plan based on the national economic and social development plan and the national marine functional zoning, and submit it to the State Council for approval.

The national island protection plan shall, based on the natural attributes such as the location, natural resources, and environment of islands, as well as their protection and utilization status, determine the principles for classified protection of islands, the uninhabited islands available for utilization, and the islands requiring key restoration, etc.

The national island protection plan shall be coordinated with the national urban system plan and the national general land use plan.

Article 10 The competent oceanic departments of the people's governments of coastal provinces and autonomous regions shall, together with the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level and military organs, organize the formulation of provincial island protection plans based on the national island protection plan, the provincial urban system plan, and the provincial and autonomous regional general land use plans, and submit them to the people's governments of the provinces or autonomous regions for approval and to the State Council for recordation.

The overall urban plan formulated by the people's government of a coastal municipality directly under the Central Government shall include a special plan for island protection within its administrative region.

Provincial island protection plans and the special plans for island protection of municipalities directly under the Central Government shall specify specific measures for classified protection of islands.

Article 11 The people's governments of provinces and autonomous regions may, based on actual conditions, require the people's governments of coastal cities, counties, and towns within their administrative regions to formulate special plans for island protection and incorporate them into the overall urban plan or the overall town plan; they may require the people's governments of coastal counties to formulate county island protection plans.

The special plans for island protection of coastal cities and towns and the county island protection plans shall conform to the national island protection plan and the provincial island protection plan.

When formulating special plans for island protection of coastal cities and towns, opinions from the competent oceanic department of the people's government at the next higher level shall be solicited.

County island protection plans shall be submitted to the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions for approval and to the competent oceanic department under the State Council for recordation.

Article 12 The people's government of a coastal county may organize the formulation of protection and utilization plans for the uninhabited islands available for utilization as determined in the national island protection plan.

Article 13 Any modification of an island protection plan shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the approval procedures prescribed in Articles 9, 10, and 11 of this Law.

Article 14 The State establishes and improves the island statistical survey system. The competent oceanic department under the State Council shall, together with relevant departments, draft a comprehensive island statistical survey plan, organize its implementation after approval in accordance with the law, and publish the island statistical survey bulletin.

Article 15 The State establishes an island management information system, conducts survey and assessment of the natural resources of islands, and implements monitoring and surveillance of the protection and utilization status of islands.

## Chapter III Protection of Islands

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 16 The State Council and the local people's governments at all levels in coastal areas shall take measures to protect the natural resources, natural landscapes, and historical and cultural relics of islands.

It is prohibited to alter the coastline of islands within nature reserves. It is prohibited to quarry or destroy corals and coral reefs. It is prohibited to cut down mangroves in the sea areas surrounding islands.

Article 17 The State protects the vegetation of islands and promotes the conservation of freshwater resources on islands; it supports the construction of projects for freshwater storage, seawater desalination, and the introduction of freshwater from outside on inhabited islands.

Article 18 The State supports the use of islands for scientific research activities. Scientific research activities conducted on islands shall not cause damage to the ecosystems of the islands and their surrounding sea areas.

Article 19 The State conducts registration of island species and protects and manages island biological species in accordance with the law.

Article 20 The State supports the establishment of experimental bases for the development and utilization of renewable energy, ecological construction, etc., on islands.

Article 21 The State allocates special funds for island protection, to be used for the protection, ecological restoration, and scientific research activities of islands.

Article 22 The State protects military facilities established on islands and prohibits acts that damage or endanger military facilities.

The State protects public welfare facilities established on islands in accordance with the law, such as aids to navigation, surveying, meteorological observation, marine monitoring, and earthquake monitoring, and prohibits damaging or moving them without authorization, or hindering their normal use.

### Section 2 Protection of Ecosystems of Inhabited Islands

Article 23 The development and construction of inhabited islands shall comply with the provisions of laws and regulations concerning urban and rural planning, environmental protection, land management, sea area use management, water resources and forest protection, etc., and shall protect the ecosystems of the islands and their surrounding sea areas.

Article 24 The development and construction of inhabited islands shall involve survey and assessment of the island's land resources, water resources, and energy conditions, and shall conduct environmental impact assessments in accordance with the law. The development and construction of an island shall not exceed its environmental capacity. New construction, renovation, and expansion projects must meet the control targets for the discharge of major pollutants, construction land, and total water consumption on the island.

The development and construction of inhabited islands shall prioritize the use of renewable energy sources such as wind energy, ocean energy, and solar energy, as well as technologies such as rainwater harvesting, seawater desalination, and wastewater reuse.

Areas on inhabited islands and their surrounding sea areas shall be designated as prohibited development areas or restricted development areas, and measures shall be taken to protect the habitats of island organisms and prevent the degradation of island vegetation and the reduction of biodiversity.

Article 25 When carrying out engineering construction on an inhabited island, the principles of planning before construction and prioritizing the construction of ecological protection facilities or constructing them simultaneously with the project shall be adhered to.

If ecological damage is caused by engineering construction, the responsible party shall be liable for restoration; if it is unable to restore, the people's government at or above the county level shall order the construction to be stopped and may designate relevant departments to organize the restoration, with the restoration costs borne by the unit or individual that caused the ecological damage.

Article 26 The construction of buildings or facilities on the beaches of inhabited islands is strictly restricted; if construction is truly necessary, it shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations on urban and rural planning, land management, environmental protection, etc. Buildings or facilities constructed on the beaches of inhabited islands without legal approval that cause serious damage to the ecosystems of the islands and their surrounding sea areas shall be demolished in accordance with the law.

The extraction of sea sand from the beaches of inhabited islands is strictly restricted; if extraction is truly necessary, it shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations on sea area use management and mineral resources.

Article 27 Acts that alter the coastline of inhabited islands, such as land reclamation from the sea and enclosure of the sea, are strictly restricted, and the construction of projects connecting islands by land reclamation is strictly restricted; if land reclamation or sea enclosure to alter the island coastline, or land reclamation to connect islands, is truly necessary, the project applicant shall submit application documents including a project demonstration report and an approved environmental impact assessment report, and shall submit them for approval in accordance with the provisions of the "Sea Area Use Management Law of the People's Republic of China."

For projects connecting islands by land reclamation constructed on inhabited islands before the implementation of this Law that have caused serious damage to the ecosystems of the islands and their surrounding sea areas, the competent oceanic department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the island is located shall, together with the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, formulate an ecological restoration plan, and organize its implementation after approval by the people's government at the same level.

### Section 3 Protection of Uninhabited Islands

Article 28 Uninhabited islands that have not been approved for utilization shall maintain their current status; activities such as quarrying, extracting sea sand, felling trees, and engaging in production, construction, and tourism are prohibited.

Article 29 The collection of biological and non-biological samples on uninhabited islands is strictly restricted; if collection is truly necessary for teaching or scientific research, it shall be reported to the competent oceanic department of the local people's government at or above the county level where the island is located for approval.

Article 30 Those engaged in the development and utilization activities of uninhabited islands available for utilization as determined in the national island protection plan shall comply with the protection and utilization plans for such uninhabited islands, adopt strict ecological protection measures, and avoid causing damage to the ecosystems of the islands and their surrounding sea areas.

To develop and utilize the uninhabited islands available for utilization as specified in the preceding paragraph, an application shall be submitted to the competent oceanic department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, along with application documents such as a project demonstration report and a specific development and utilization plan. The competent oceanic department shall organize relevant departments and experts to review the application, provide review opinions, and submit it to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval.

If the development and utilization of an uninhabited island involves the use of an island for special purposes, or if land reclamation to connect islands or other actions that seriously alter the natural topography and landform of the island are truly necessary, the matter shall be approved by the State Council.

Specific measures for the review and approval of the development and utilization of uninhabited islands shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 31 Those approved to develop and utilize uninhabited islands shall pay usage fees in accordance with the law, except for the use of uninhabited islands for public welfare purposes such as national defense, official business, teaching, disaster prevention and mitigation, non-profit public infrastructure construction, basic surveying and mapping, and meteorological observation.

The measures for the collection and management of usage fees for uninhabited islands shall be prescribed by the finance department under the State Council together with the competent oceanic department under the State Council.

Article 32 Those approved to construct buildings or facilities on uninhabited islands available for utilization shall, in accordance with the protection and utilization plans for such uninhabited islands, limit the total quantity, height, and distance from the coastline of the buildings and facilities, so that they are coordinated with the surrounding vegetation and landscape.

Article 33 Wastewater generated during the utilization of uninhabited islands shall be treated and discharged in accordance with regulations.

Solid waste generated during the utilization of uninhabited islands shall be treated and disposed of harmlessly in accordance with regulations. It is prohibited to discard solid waste on uninhabited islands or dump it into their surrounding sea areas.

Article 34 For temporary utilization of uninhabited islands, no permanent buildings or facilities shall be constructed on the utilized island.

Article 35 On uninhabited islands available for utilization that have been legally designated for tourism activities and their surrounding sea areas, no residential settlements shall be constructed, and no productive aquaculture activities shall be carried out; if productive aquaculture activities already exist, corresponding pollution prevention measures shall be determined when formulating the protection and utilization plans for such uninhabited islands.

### Section 4 Protection of Islands for Special Purposes

Article 36 The State provides special protection to islands that serve special purposes or have special conservation value, such as islands where baselines for measuring the territorial sea are located, islands used for national defense, and islands within marine nature reserves.

Article 37 For an island where a baseline for measuring the territorial sea is located, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the island is located shall delimit a protection zone and report it to the competent oceanic department under the State Council for recordation. Obvious signs shall be set up around the baseline point and its protection zone.

It is prohibited to carry out engineering construction or other activities that may alter the topography or landform of the area within the protection zone of a baseline point. If engineering construction is truly necessary for the purpose of protecting the baseline point, it shall undergo scientific demonstration and be submitted to the competent oceanic department under the State Council for consent before going through the approval procedures in accordance with the law.

It is prohibited to damage or move the markers of baseline points without authorization.

The competent oceanic departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with State regulations, implement monitoring and surveillance of the ecosystems of the islands where baseline points are located and their surrounding sea areas.

Any unit or individual has the obligation to protect the baseline points of islands. If it is found that the topography or landform of a baseline point or its protection zone has been damaged, the matter shall be promptly reported to the local people's government or the competent oceanic department.

Article 38 It is prohibited to damage the natural topography and landform of uninhabited islands used for national defense and the topography and landform of areas used for national defense on inhabited islands and their surroundings.

It is prohibited to use uninhabited islands used for national defense for purposes unrelated to national defense. When the use for national defense is terminated, with the approval of the military organ, the island and relevant ecological protection data, etc., shall be transferred to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the island is located.

Article 39 The State Council, the relevant departments under the State Council, and the people's governments of coastal provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the need to protect the natural resources, natural landscapes, and historical and cultural relics of islands, approve the establishment of marine nature reserves or marine special protected areas for islands with special conservation value and their surrounding sea areas in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IV Supervision and Inspection

Article 40 The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the protection, development, and construction of inhabited islands.

Article 41 The competent oceanic departments shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the protection and rational utilization of uninhabited islands.

The competent oceanic departments and their marine surveillance agencies shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the protection of the ecosystems of the sea areas surrounding islands.

Article 42 When performing their supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law, the competent oceanic departments have the right to require the inspected units and individuals to provide explanations on issues related to island utilization and to provide relevant documents and materials on island utilization; they have the right to enter the islands utilized by the inspected units and individuals to conduct on-site inspections.

Inspectors shall present valid law enforcement credentials when performing inspection duties. The relevant units and individuals shall cooperate with the inspection work, truthfully report the situation, and provide relevant documents and materials; they shall not refuse or obstruct the inspection work.

Article 43 Inspectors must be loyal to their duties, enforce the law impartially, be honest and upright, provide civilized services, and accept supervision in accordance with the law. When investigating and punishing acts violating the provisions of this Law in accordance with the law, if it is found that a State functionary has committed an illegal act that warrants disciplinary sanction, a proposal for disciplinary sanction shall be submitted to the appointing authority or the supervisory authority of that functionary.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 44 If a competent oceanic department or another department responsible for the supervision and management of island protection fails to investigate and punish illegal acts upon discovery or after receiving reports of illegal acts in accordance with the law, or commits other acts of failing to perform its duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant competent department of the people's government at the next higher level shall order it to make corrections, and shall impose disciplinary sanctions on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, alters the coastline of an island within a nature reserve, carries out land reclamation or sea enclosure to alter the island coastline, or carries out land reclamation to connect islands shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Sea Area Use Management Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 46 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, quarries or destroys corals or coral reefs, or cuts down mangroves in the sea areas surrounding islands shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 47 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, quarries stone, extracts sea sand, fells trees, or collects biological or non-biological samples on an uninhabited island shall be ordered by the competent oceanic department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, have the illegal gains confiscated, and may also be fined not more than 20,000 yuan.

Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in production or construction activities or organizes tourism activities on an uninhabited island shall be ordered by the competent oceanic department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, have the illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 48 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in activities that seriously alter the natural topography and landform of an uninhabited island shall be ordered by the competent oceanic department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 49 Anyone who illegally discharges pollutants on an island or in its surrounding sea areas shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant environmental protection laws.

Article 50 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, carries out engineering construction or other activities that may alter the topography or landform of the area within the protection zone of a baseline point, constructs permanent buildings or facilities on a temporarily utilized uninhabited island, or constructs residential settlements on an uninhabited island available for utilization that has been legally designated for tourism activities shall be ordered by the competent oceanic department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act and be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 51 Anyone who damages or moves the markers of baseline points without authorization shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law.**Article 52** Any act that damages or endangers military facilities located on islands, or that damages or unauthorizedly moves public facilities such as navigation aids, surveying, meteorological observation, marine monitoring, and seismic monitoring facilities installed on islands, shall be penalized in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 53** If approval is granted for the development and utilization of an uninhabited island without the authority to do so, if approval is granted beyond the authorized scope, or if approval is granted in violation of the island protection plan, the approval document shall be invalid; administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

**Article 54** Any violation of the provisions of this Law, such as refusing supervision and inspection by the marine administrative department, practicing fraud during supervision and inspection, or failing to provide relevant documents and materials, shall be ordered to make corrections by the marine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level, and a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed.

**Article 55** Any violation of the provisions of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Whoever causes damage to the ecosystem of an island and its surrounding sea areas shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

**Article 56** The protection and related management activities of low-tide elevations shall be implemented by reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 57** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Ecosystem of an island and its surrounding sea areas" refers to an organic complex composed of biological communities and non-biological environments, including the island body, coastline, beaches, vegetation, freshwater, and surrounding sea areas, which sustain the existence of the island.

(2) "Uninhabited island" refers to an island that is not registered as a residential address under the household registration management system.

(3) "Low-tide elevation" refers to a naturally formed land area that is surrounded by water at low tide and protrudes above the water surface but is submerged at high tide.

(4) "Reclamation connecting islands" refers to the act of connecting an island to the mainland or another island through land reclamation or other means.

(5) "Temporary use of an uninhabited island" refers to the act of briefly landing on or berthing at an uninhabited island for purposes such as official business, teaching, scientific investigation, disaster relief, or shelter.

**Article 58** This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 2010.
