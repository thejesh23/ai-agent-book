# Law of the People's Republic of China on Guarding State Secrets

Adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 5, 1988

Revised at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 29, 2010

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of guarding state secrets, maintaining national security and interests, and ensuring the smooth progress of reform, opening up, and socialist construction.

**Article 2** State secrets are matters that concern national security and interests, are determined in accordance with statutory procedures, and are known only to a limited scope of persons within a certain period of time.

**Article 3** State secrets are protected by law.

All state organs, armed forces, political parties, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and citizens have the obligation to guard state secrets.

Any act that endangers the security of state secrets must be investigated under the law.

**Article 4** The work of guarding state secrets (hereinafter referred to as secrecy work) shall follow the policy of active prevention, highlighting key points, and management according to law, ensuring both the security of state secrets and the rational utilization of information resources.

Matters that are required to be made public by laws and administrative regulations shall be disclosed according to law.

**Article 5** The state administrative department for secrecy shall be in charge of secrecy work nationwide. The local administrative departments for secrecy at or above the county level shall be in charge of secrecy work within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 6** State organs and units involving state secrets (hereinafter referred to as organs and units) shall manage the secrecy work of their own organs and units.

Central state organs shall, within the scope of their functions and powers, manage or guide the secrecy work of their respective systems.

**Article 7** Organs and units shall implement a responsibility system for secrecy work, improve secrecy management systems, perfect secrecy protection measures, carry out secrecy publicity and education, and strengthen secrecy inspections.

**Article 8** The state shall reward units or individuals that have made outstanding achievements in guarding and protecting state secrets, as well as in improving secrecy technologies and measures.

## Chapter II: Scope and Classification of State Secrets

**Article 9** The following matters concerning national security and interests, the disclosure of which may damage national security and interests in the fields of politics, economy, national defense, diplomacy, etc., shall be determined as state secrets:

(1) Secret matters in major decisions of state affairs;
(2) Secret matters in national defense construction and armed forces activities;
(3) Secret matters in diplomatic and foreign affairs activities, as well as secret matters for which a confidentiality obligation is undertaken externally;
(4) Secret matters in national economic and social development;
(5) Secret matters in science and technology;
(6) Secret matters in activities to safeguard national security and in the investigation of criminal offenses; and
(7) Other secret matters determined by the state administrative department for secrecy.

Secret matters of political parties that meet the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be considered state secrets.

**Article 10** The classification of state secrets is divided into three levels: top secret, confidential, and secret.

Top secret state secrets are the most important state secrets, the disclosure of which would cause particularly serious damage to national security and interests; confidential state secrets are important state secrets, the disclosure of which would cause serious damage to national security and interests; secret state secrets are ordinary state secrets, the disclosure of which would cause damage to national security and interests.

**Article 11** The specific scope of state secrets and their classifications shall be stipulated by the state administrative department for secrecy jointly with the relevant central state organs, including foreign affairs, public security, and national security.

The specific scope of military state secrets and their classifications shall be stipulated by the Central Military Commission.

The stipulations on the specific scope of state secrets and their classifications shall be published within the relevant scope and adjusted in a timely manner according to changes in circumstances.

**Article 12** The heads of organs and units and the persons designated by them shall be the persons responsible for determining secrecy classifications, responsible for the determination, change, and declassification of state secrets in their respective organs and units.

For the determination, change, and declassification of state secrets in an organ or unit, specific opinions shall be put forward by the person handling the matter and submitted to the person responsible for determining secrecy classifications for review and approval.

**Article 13** The classification of state secrets shall be determined in compliance with the authority for determining secrecy classifications.

Central state organs, provincial-level organs, and their authorized organs and units may determine top secret, confidential, and secret state secrets; organs at the level of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture, and their authorized organs and units may determine confidential and secret state secrets. The specific authority for determining secrecy classifications and the scope of authorization shall be stipulated by the state administrative department for secrecy.

If an organ or unit needs to determine a secrecy classification for a matter that implements a state secret matter determined by a higher-level organ, the classification shall be determined according to the classification of the state secret matter being implemented. If a lower-level organ or unit believes that a matter requiring secrecy classification arising from its own work falls within the authority of a higher-level organ or unit, it shall first take secrecy protection measures and immediately report to the higher-level organ or unit for determination; if there is no higher-level organ or unit, it shall immediately request determination by the competent business department or the administrative department for secrecy with corresponding authority.

Public security and national security organs shall determine the classification of state secrets within the scope of their work according to the prescribed authority.

**Article 14** For state secret matters arising from their work, organs and units shall determine the classification in accordance with the provisions on the specific scope of state secrets and their classifications, and at the same time determine the period of secrecy and the scope of knowledge.

**Article 15** The period of secrecy for state secrets shall be limited to the necessary period according to the nature and characteristics of the matter and the needs of maintaining national security and interests; if the period cannot be determined, the conditions for declassification shall be determined.

Unless otherwise provided, the period of secrecy for top secret shall not exceed thirty years, for confidential shall not exceed twenty years, and for secret shall not exceed ten years.

Organs and units shall determine the specific period of secrecy, declassification time, or declassification conditions according to work needs.

If an organ or unit decides to make public a matter that was determined to require secrecy during the process of deciding and handling relevant work, it shall be deemed declassified upon official publication.

**Article 16** The scope of knowledge of state secrets shall be limited to the minimum scope according to work needs.

If the scope of knowledge of a state secret can be limited to specific individuals, it shall be so limited; if it cannot be limited to specific individuals, it shall be limited to organs and units, and the organs and units shall further limit it to specific individuals.

Persons outside the scope of knowledge of a state secret who need to know the state secret due to work needs shall obtain approval from the head of the organ or unit.

**Article 17** Organs and units shall mark state secrets on carriers such as paper media, optical media, and electromagnetic media (hereinafter referred to as state secret carriers), as well as on equipment and products that are state secrets.

Matters that are not state secrets shall not be marked as state secrets.

**Article 18** The classification, period of secrecy, and scope of knowledge of state secrets shall be changed in a timely manner according to changes in circumstances. Changes to the classification, period of secrecy, and scope of knowledge of state secrets shall be decided by the original organ or unit that determined the secrecy classification, or by its higher-level organ.

When the classification, period of secrecy, or scope of knowledge of a state secret is changed, the organs, units, or persons within the scope of knowledge shall be promptly notified in writing.

**Article 19** State secrets shall be automatically declassified upon the expiration of their period of secrecy.

Organs and units shall regularly review the state secrets they have determined. For matters that are no longer state secrets due to adjustments in the scope of secret matters within the period of secrecy, or that will not harm national security and interests if disclosed and do not need to remain confidential, they shall be promptly declassified; for matters that need to have their period of secrecy extended, the period of secrecy shall be re-determined before the original period expires. Early declassification or extension of the period of secrecy shall be decided by the original organ or unit that determined the secrecy classification, or by its higher-level organ.

**Article 20** If an organ or unit is unclear or has a dispute over whether a matter is a state secret or what classification it belongs to, it shall be determined by the state administrative department for secrecy or the administrative department for secrecy of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter III: Secrecy System

**Article 21** The production, receipt and dispatch, transmission, use, reproduction, storage, maintenance, and destruction of state secret carriers shall comply with state secrecy regulations.

Top secret state secret carriers shall be stored in facilities and equipment that meet state secrecy standards, and shall be managed by designated persons; they shall not be reproduced or excerpted without the approval of the original organ or unit that determined the secrecy classification or its higher-level organ; for receipt and dispatch, transmission, and carrying outside, designated persons shall be responsible, and necessary security measures shall be taken.

**Article 22** The research and development, production, transportation, use, storage, maintenance, and destruction of equipment and products that are state secrets shall comply with state secrecy regulations.

**Article 23** Computer information systems that store and process state secrets (hereinafter referred to as classified information systems) shall implement graded protection according to the degree of classification.

Classified information systems shall be equipped with secrecy facilities and equipment in accordance with state secrecy standards. Secrecy facilities and equipment shall be planned, constructed, and operated simultaneously with the classified information system.

Classified information systems shall be inspected and approved before being put into use.

**Article 24** Organs and units shall strengthen the management of classified information systems. No organization or individual may engage in the following acts:

(1) Connecting classified computers or classified storage devices to the internet or other public information networks;
(2) Exchanging information between a classified information system and the internet or other public information networks without taking protective measures;
(3) Using non-classified computers or non-classified storage devices to store or process state secret information;
(4) Unauthorized uninstallation or modification of security technology programs or management programs of classified information systems;
(5) Giving away, selling, discarding, or using for other purposes decommissioned classified computers or classified storage devices that have not undergone security technology processing.

**Article 25** Organs and units shall strengthen the management of state secret carriers. No organization or individual may engage in the following acts:

(1) Illegally obtaining or possessing state secret carriers;
(2) Buying, selling, transferring, or privately destroying state secret carriers;
(3) Transmitting state secret carriers through ordinary postal services, courier services, or other channels without secrecy measures;
(4) Mailing or shipping state secret carriers out of the country;
(5) Carrying or transmitting state secret carriers out of the country without the approval of the relevant competent department.

**Article 26** It is prohibited to illegally reproduce, record, or store state secrets.

It is prohibited to transmit state secrets over the internet, other public information networks, or wired and wireless communications without secrecy measures.

It is prohibited to involve state secrets in private communications and correspondence.

**Article 27** The editing, publishing, printing, and distribution of newspapers, books, audio-visual products, and electronic publications; the production and broadcasting of radio programs, television programs, and films; and the editing and publishing of information on the internet, mobile communication networks, other public information networks, and other media shall comply with relevant secrecy regulations.

**Article 28** Internet and other public information network operators and service providers shall cooperate with public security organs, national security organs, and procuratorial organs in investigating cases of leakage of secrets; if they discover that information published on the internet or other public information networks involves the disclosure of state secrets, they shall immediately stop the transmission, preserve relevant records, and report to the public security organs, national security organs, or the administrative department for secrecy; they shall delete information involving the disclosure of state secrets as required by the public security organs, national security organs, or the administrative department for secrecy.

**Article 29** When organs and units publicly release information or procure projects, goods, or services involving state secrets, they shall comply with secrecy regulations.

**Article 30** If an organ or unit needs to provide state secret matters in foreign exchanges and cooperation, or if an employed or hired foreign national needs to know state secrets due to work needs, it shall report to the relevant competent department of the State Council or the relevant competent department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval, and sign a confidentiality agreement with the other party.

**Article 31** When holding meetings or other activities involving state secrets, the sponsoring unit shall take secrecy measures, provide secrecy education to the participants, and put forward specific secrecy requirements.

**Article 32** Organs and units shall designate institutions involving top secret or a relatively large number of confidential or secret state secrets as key secrecy departments, and designate specialized venues for the centralized production, storage, and safekeeping of state secret carriers as key secrecy locations, and equip and use necessary technical protection facilities and equipment in accordance with state secrecy regulations and standards.

**Article 33** Military restricted zones and other places or locations that are state secrets and not open to the public shall take secrecy measures; without the approval of the relevant department, they shall not be opened to the public or have their scope of opening expanded.

**Article 34** Enterprises and institutions engaged in the production, reproduction, maintenance, or destruction of state secret carriers; the integration of classified information systems; or the scientific research and production of weapons and equipment, and other businesses involving state secrets, shall undergo secrecy review. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

When an organ or unit entrusts an enterprise or institution with the business mentioned in the preceding paragraph, it shall sign a confidentiality agreement with it, put forward secrecy requirements, and take secrecy measures.

**Article 35** Personnel working in classified positions (hereinafter referred to as classified personnel) shall be divided into core classified personnel, important classified personnel, and ordinary classified personnel according to the degree of classification, and shall be managed by category.

The employment or hiring of classified personnel shall be subject to review in accordance with relevant regulations.

Classified personnel shall have good political quality and conduct, and possess the work ability required for the classified position.

The legitimate rights and interests of classified personnel are protected by law.

**Article 36** Classified personnel shall undergo secrecy education and training before taking up their posts, master secrecy knowledge and skills, sign a confidentiality commitment letter, strictly abide by secrecy rules and regulations, and shall not disclose state secrets in any way.

**Article 37** Classified personnel shall obtain approval from the relevant department before going abroad. If the relevant organ considers that the departure of a classified person will cause harm to national security or major losses to national interests, approval for departure shall not be granted.

**Article 38** Classified personnel shall be subject to a period of declassification management when leaving their posts or resigning. During the declassification period, classified personnel shall fulfill their confidentiality obligations in accordance with regulations, shall not violate regulations in seeking employment, and shall not disclose state secrets in any way.

**Article 39** Organs and units shall establish and improve the management system for classified personnel, clarify the rights, job responsibilities, and requirements of classified personnel, and conduct regular supervision and inspection of the performance of duties by classified personnel.

**Article 40** When state functionaries or other citizens discover that state secrets have been or may be disclosed, they shall immediately take remedial measures and promptly report to the relevant organ or unit. Upon receiving the report, the organ or unit shall immediately handle the matter and promptly report to the administrative department for secrecy.

## Chapter IV: Supervision and Administration

**Article 41** The state administrative department for secrecy shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, formulate secrecy rules and state secrecy standards.

**Article 42** The administrative departments for secrecy shall, according to law, organize and carry out secrecy publicity and education, secrecy inspections, secrecy technology protection, and the investigation and handling of cases of leakage of secrets, and provide guidance and supervision over the secrecy work of organs and units.

**Article 43** If an administrative department for secrecy finds that the determination, change, or declassification of a state secret is improper, it shall promptly notify the relevant organ or unit to make corrections.

**Article 44** The administrative departments for secrecy shall inspect the compliance of organs and units with the secrecy system, and the relevant organs and units shall cooperate. If an administrative department for secrecy discovers that an organ or unit has hidden dangers of leakage of secrets, it shall require it to take measures and rectify within a time limit; for facilities, equipment, or places with hidden dangers of leakage of secrets, it shall order their use to be stopped; for classified personnel who seriously violate secrecy regulations, it shall recommend that the relevant organ or unit impose sanctions and remove them from classified positions; if it discovers suspected disclosure of state secrets, it shall supervise and guide the relevant organ or unit to investigate and handle the matter. If a crime is suspected, the case shall be transferred to the judicial authorities for handling.

**Article 45** The administrative departments for secrecy shall confiscate state secret carriers that are illegally obtained or possessed during secrecy inspections.

**Article 46** If an organ handling a case involving suspected disclosure of state secrets needs to identify whether a matter is a state secret and what classification it belongs to, the identification shall be made by the state administrative department for secrecy or the administrative department for secrecy of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 47** If an organ or unit fails to impose sanctions according to law on persons who violate secrecy regulations, the administrative department for secrecy shall recommend correction; if the organ or unit refuses to correct, the administrative department for secrecy shall request its higher-level organ or the supervisory organ to handle the matter according to law against the responsible leaders and directly responsible persons of the organ or unit.

## Chapter V: Legal Liability

**Article 48** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be given sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law:

(1) Illegally obtaining or possessing state secret carriers;
(2) Buying, selling, transferring, or privately destroying state secret carriers;
(3) Transmitting state secret carriers through ordinary postal services, courier services, or other channels without secrecy measures;
(4) Mailing or shipping state secret carriers out of the country, or carrying or transmitting state secret carriers out of the country without the approval of the relevant competent department;
(5) Illegally reproducing, recording, or storing state secrets;
(6) Involving state secrets in private communications and correspondence;
(7) Transmitting state secrets over the internet, other public information networks, or wired and wireless communications without secrecy measures;
(8) Connecting classified computers or classified storage devices to the internet or other public information networks;
(9) Exchanging information between a classified information system and the internet or other public information networks without taking protective measures;
(10) Using non-classified computers or non-classified storage devices to store or process state secret information;
(11) Unauthorized uninstallation or modification of security technology programs or management programs of classified information systems;
(12) Giving away, selling, discarding, or using for other purposes decommissioned classified computers or classified storage devices that have not undergone security technology processing.

If the acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph do not constitute a crime and the persons involved are not subject to sanctions, the administrative department for secrecy shall urge their organ or unit to handle the matter.

**Article 49** If an organ or unit violates the provisions of this Law and a major case of leakage of secrets occurs, the relevant organ or unit shall impose sanctions according to law on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons; if the persons involved are not subject to sanctions, the administrative department for secrecy shall urge their competent department to handle the matter.

If an organ or unit violates the provisions of this Law by failing to determine a secrecy classification for a matter that should be classified, or by determining a secrecy classification for a matter that should not be classified, causing serious consequences, the relevant organ or unit shall impose sanctions according to law on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible persons.Article 50: Internet and other public information network operators and service providers that violate the provisions of Article 28 of this Law shall be punished by public security organs, state security organs, or the competent information industry authorities in accordance with their respective duties and responsibilities.

Article 51: Staff members of the secrecy administration departments who abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain while performing their secrecy management duties shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 6: Supplementary Provisions

Article 52: The Central Military Commission shall, in accordance with this Law, formulate the regulations on secrecy for the People's Liberation Army.

Article 53: This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2010.
