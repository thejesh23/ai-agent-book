# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Environmental Pollution by Solid Waste

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 30, 1995

First Revision at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2004

First Amendment by the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Second Amendment by the Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Ports at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Third Amendment by the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Foreign Trade at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Second Revision at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2020

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of protecting and improving the ecological environment, preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste, safeguarding public health, maintaining ecological security, promoting ecological progress, and facilitating sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law applies to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

The prevention and control of environmental pollution of the marine environment by solid waste and the prevention and control of environmental pollution by radioactive solid waste are not governed by this Law.

Article 3 The State promotes green development and encourages cleaner production and the development of a circular economy.

The State advocates a simple, moderate, green, and low-carbon lifestyle and guides the public to actively participate in the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Article 4 The prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste shall adhere to the principles of reduction, resource utilization, and harmlessness.

All entities and individuals shall take measures to reduce the generation of solid waste, promote the comprehensive utilization of solid waste, and reduce the harmfulness of solid waste.

Article 5 The prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste shall adhere to the principle that the polluter bears responsibility.

Entities and individuals that generate, collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of solid waste shall take measures to prevent or reduce environmental pollution by solid waste and shall bear legal responsibility for the environmental pollution caused.

Article 6 The State implements a domestic waste classification system.

Domestic waste classification shall adhere to the principles of government promotion, public participation, urban-rural coordination, local adaptation, and simplicity and ease of implementation.

Article 7 Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste within their respective administrative regions.

The State implements a target responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, incorporating the completion of targets for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste into the assessment and evaluation content.

Article 8 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over the work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste, and organize, coordinate, and supervise relevant departments to perform their duties of supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste in accordance with the law.

Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may negotiate to establish cross-administrative-region joint prevention and control mechanisms for environmental pollution by solid waste, coordinating planning formulation, facility construction, solid waste transfer, and other work.

Article 9 The competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the national work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste. The competent departments of the State Council for development and reform, industry and information technology, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, transport, agriculture and rural affairs, commerce, health, customs, and other departments shall be responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste within their respective functions.

The competent department of ecology and environment of the local people's government shall exercise unified supervision and management over the work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste within its administrative region. The competent departments of the local people's government for development and reform, industry and information technology, natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, transport, agriculture and rural affairs, commerce, health, and other departments shall be responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste within their respective functions.

Article 10 The State encourages and supports scientific research, technological development, promotion of advanced technologies, and scientific popularization for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and strengthens the scientific and technological support for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Article 11 State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, grassroots self-governing organizations, and news media shall strengthen publicity, education, and scientific popularization on the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and enhance public awareness of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Schools shall carry out education on domestic waste classification and the popularization of knowledge about the prevention and control of environmental pollution by other solid waste.

Article 12 People's governments at all levels shall commend and reward, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State, entities and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in the work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste and related comprehensive utilization activities.

## Chapter II Supervision and Management

Article 13 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by solid waste into the national economic and social development plan and the ecological and environmental protection plan, and take effective measures to reduce the generation of solid waste, promote the comprehensive utilization of solid waste, reduce the harmfulness of solid waste, and minimize the amount of solid waste landfilled.

Article 14 The competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate standards for the identification of solid waste, identification procedures, and national technical standards for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, based on the national environmental quality standards and the national economic and technological conditions.

Article 15 The competent department of standardization of the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of the State Council for development and reform, industry and information technology, ecology and environment, agriculture and rural affairs, and other departments, formulate standards for the comprehensive utilization of solid waste.

The comprehensive utilization of solid waste shall comply with ecological and environmental laws and regulations and meet the technical standards for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste. The use of products from the comprehensive utilization of solid waste shall comply with the purposes and standards prescribed by the State.

Article 16 The competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, establish a national information platform for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by hazardous waste and other solid waste, and promote the whole-process monitoring and information traceability of the collection, transfer, and disposal of solid waste.

Article 17 Projects that generate, store, utilize, or dispose of solid waste shall undergo environmental impact assessment in accordance with the law and comply with the State's regulations on the environmental protection management of construction projects.

Article 18 The facilities for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste that are determined to be necessary for supporting a construction project by the environmental impact assessment document shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project. The preliminary design of the construction project shall, in accordance with the requirements of the environmental protection design specifications, incorporate the content of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste into the environmental impact assessment document, implement measures to prevent environmental pollution and ecological damage by solid waste, and include the investment estimate for the facilities for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

The construction entity shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, accept the supporting facilities for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, prepare an acceptance report, and make it public.

Article 19 Entities and other producers and operators that collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of solid waste shall strengthen the management and maintenance of relevant facilities, equipment, and sites to ensure their normal operation and use.

Article 20 Entities and other producers and operators that generate, collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of solid waste shall take measures to prevent scattering, loss, leakage, or other measures to prevent environmental pollution, and shall not dump, pile, discard, or spill solid waste without authorization.

No entity or individual is allowed to dump, pile, or store solid waste in rivers, lakes, canals, channels, reservoirs, and the beaches and banks below their highest water level, as well as other locations prescribed by laws and regulations.

Article 21 Within ecological protection red line areas, permanent basic farmland concentrated areas, and other areas requiring special protection, the construction of facilities and sites for the centralized storage, utilization, and disposal of industrial solid waste and hazardous waste, as well as domestic waste landfills, is prohibited.

Article 22 To transfer solid waste out of the administrative region of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for storage or disposal, an application shall be submitted to the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is transferred from. The competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is transferred from shall promptly consult with the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is received, and after obtaining consent, approve the transfer of the solid waste out of the administrative region of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within the prescribed time limit. Without approval, no transfer shall be made.

To transfer solid waste out of the administrative region of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for utilization, it shall be reported to the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is transferred from for recordation. The competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is transferred from shall notify the competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the solid waste is received of the recordation information.

Article 23 The dumping, piling, and disposal of solid waste from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China into the territory of the People's Republic of China is prohibited.

Article 24 The State shall gradually achieve zero import of solid waste, and the implementation shall be organized by the competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council together with the competent departments of the State Council for commerce, development and reform, customs, and other departments.

Article 25 If the customs discovers that imported goods are suspected of being solid waste, it may entrust a professional institution to conduct attribute identification and manage them according to law based on the identification conclusion.

Article 26 The competent department of ecology and environment and its environmental enforcement agencies, as well as other departments responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, shall have the right, within their respective functions, to conduct on-site inspections of entities and other producers and operators engaged in activities such as generating, collecting, storing, transporting, utilizing, and disposing of solid waste. The inspected party shall truthfully report the situation and provide necessary information.

When conducting on-site inspections, measures such as on-site monitoring, sample collection, and reviewing or copying materials related to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste may be taken. Inspection personnel shall present their credentials when conducting on-site inspections. Trade secrets learned during the on-site inspection shall be kept confidential.

Article 27 Under any of the following circumstances, the competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste may seal up or seize solid waste that is illegally collected, stored, transported, utilized, or disposed of, as well as the facilities, equipment, sites, tools, and articles:

(1) It may cause the destruction, concealment, or illegal transfer of evidence;

(2) It has caused or may cause serious environmental pollution.

Article 28 The competent department of ecology and environment shall, together with relevant departments, establish a credit record system for entities and other producers and operators that generate, collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of solid waste, and incorporate relevant credit records into the national credit information sharing platform.

Article 29 The competent department of ecology and environment of the people's government of a city divided into districts shall, together with the competent departments for housing and urban-rural development, agriculture and rural affairs, health, and other departments, regularly publish to the public information on the types, generation amounts, disposal capacity, and utilization and disposal status of solid waste.

Entities that generate, collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of solid waste shall, in accordance with the law, promptly disclose information on the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste and actively accept social supervision.

Entities that utilize or dispose of solid waste shall, in accordance with the law, open their facilities and sites to the public to enhance public environmental awareness and participation.

Article 30 People's governments at or above the county level shall include the status of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by industrial solid waste, domestic waste, hazardous waste, and other solid waste in the annual report on environmental conditions and the completion of environmental protection targets, and report to the people's congress at the corresponding level or its standing committee.

Article 31 All entities and individuals have the right to report entities and individuals that cause environmental pollution by solid waste.

The competent department of ecology and environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste shall make the reporting methods for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste public to facilitate public reporting.

The department receiving the report shall promptly handle it and keep the relevant information of the reporter confidential; if the report is made with real name and verified, a reward shall be given.

If the reporter reports the entity where he or she works, the entity shall not retaliate against the reporter by terminating or changing the labor contract or by other means.

## Chapter III Industrial Solid Waste

Article 32 The competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of the State Council for development and reform, industry and information technology, and other departments, define the degree of harm and impact of industrial solid waste on public health and the ecological environment, formulate technical policies for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by industrial solid waste, and organize the promotion of advanced production processes and equipment for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by industrial solid waste.

Article 33 The competent department of industry and information technology of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, organize research, development, and promotion of production processes and equipment that reduce the generation of industrial solid waste and reduce the harmfulness of industrial solid waste, and publish a list of outdated production processes and equipment that generate industrial solid waste causing serious environmental pollution and are subject to elimination within a time limit.

Producers, sellers, importers, and users shall, within the time limit prescribed by the competent department of industry and information technology of the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council, stop producing, selling, importing, or using the equipment listed in the directory prescribed in the preceding paragraph. Adopters of production processes shall, within the time limit prescribed by the competent department of industry and information technology of the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council, stop adopting the processes listed in the directory prescribed in the preceding paragraph.

Equipment eliminated and included in the list for elimination within a time limit shall not be transferred to others for use.

Article 34 The competent department of industry and information technology of the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of the State Council for development and reform, ecology and environment, and other departments, regularly publish a guiding catalogue of technologies, processes, equipment, and products for the comprehensive utilization of industrial solid waste, organize the evaluation of the comprehensive utilization of industrial solid waste resources, and promote the comprehensive utilization of industrial solid waste.

Article 35 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate work plans for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by industrial solid waste, organize the construction of centralized disposal facilities for industrial solid waste, and promote the work of preventing and controlling environmental pollution by industrial solid waste.

Article 36 Entities that generate industrial solid waste shall establish and improve a responsibility system for the prevention and control of environmental pollution throughout the entire process of generating, collecting, storing, transporting, utilizing, and disposing of industrial solid waste, establish management ledgers for industrial solid waste, truthfully record information such as the types, quantities, flow directions, storage, utilization, and disposal of industrial solid waste, ensure the traceability and queryability of industrial solid waste, and take measures to prevent environmental pollution by industrial solid waste.

It is prohibited to put industrial solid waste into domestic waste collection facilities.

Article 37 If an entity that generates industrial solid waste entrusts another party to transport, utilize, or dispose of industrial solid waste, it shall verify the principal qualification and technical capability of the entrusted party, sign a written contract in accordance with the law, and stipulate the pollution prevention and control requirements in the contract.

The entrusted party, when transporting, utilizing, or disposing of industrial solid waste, shall fulfill the pollution prevention and control requirements in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations and the contract, and shall inform the entity that generated the industrial solid waste of the transportation, utilization, and disposal status.

If an entity that generates industrial solid waste violates the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, in addition to being punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, it shall also bear joint and several liability with the entrusted party that caused environmental pollution and ecological damage.

Article 38 Entities that generate industrial solid waste shall conduct cleaner production audits in accordance with the law, reasonably select and utilize raw materials, energy, and other resources, adopt advanced production processes and equipment, reduce the generation of industrial solid waste, and reduce the harmfulness of industrial solid waste.

Article 39 Entities that generate industrial solid waste shall obtain a pollutant discharge permit. The specific methods and implementation steps for pollutant discharge permits shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Entities that generate industrial solid waste shall provide the local competent department of ecology and environment with relevant information such as the types, quantities, flow directions, storage, utilization, and disposal of industrial solid waste, as well as specific measures to reduce the generation of industrial solid waste and promote comprehensive utilization, and shall comply with the relevant provisions of the pollutant discharge permit management system.

Article 40 Entities that generate industrial solid waste shall utilize industrial solid waste based on economic and technological conditions; for industrial solid waste that is temporarily not utilized or cannot be utilized, they shall, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of ecology and environment of the State Council and other departments, construct storage facilities and sites, store them safely by category, or take harmless disposal measures. The storage of industrial solid waste shall take protective measures that meet the national environmental protection standards.

The construction of facilities and sites for the storage and disposal of industrial solid waste shall meet the national environmental protection standards.

Article 41 If an entity that generates industrial solid waste terminates, it shall, before termination, take pollution prevention and control measures for the facilities and sites for the storage and disposal of industrial solid waste, and properly dispose of the untreated industrial solid waste to prevent environmental pollution.

If an entity that generates industrial solid waste undergoes a change, the entity after the change shall, in accordance with the relevant national environmental protection regulations, safely dispose of the untreated industrial solid waste and its storage and disposal facilities and sites, or take effective measures to ensure the safe operation of such facilities and sites. If the parties before the change have otherwise agreed on the pollution prevention and control responsibilities for the industrial solid waste and its storage and disposal facilities and sites, such agreement shall prevail; however, the parties' pollution prevention and control obligations shall not be exempted.

The costs for the safe disposal of untreated industrial solid waste and its storage and disposal facilities and sites of entities that terminated before April 1, 2005, shall be borne by the relevant people's government; however, if the land use right enjoyed by such entity has been legally transferred, the transferee of the land use right shall bear the disposal costs. If the parties have otherwise agreed, such agreement shall prevail; however, the parties' pollution prevention and control obligations shall not be exempted.

Article 42 Mining enterprises shall adopt scientific mining methods and mineral processing techniques to reduce the generation and storage of mining solid waste such as tailings, coal gangue, and waste rock.

The State encourages the adoption of advanced processes for the comprehensive utilization of mining solid waste such as tailings, coal gangue, and waste rock.

After the storage facilities for mining solid waste such as tailings, coal gangue, and waste rock are decommissioned, mining enterprises shall, in accordance with the relevant national environmental protection and other regulations, carry out closure to prevent environmental pollution and ecological damage.

## Chapter IV Domestic Waste

Article 43 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall accelerate the establishment of a domestic waste management system for classified disposal, classified collection, classified transportation, and classified treatment, to achieve effective coverage of the domestic waste classification system.
Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a coordination mechanism for domestic waste classification work, and strengthen and coordinate the capacity building for domestic waste classification management.

People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall organize and carry out publicity on domestic waste classification, educate and guide the public to develop the habit of domestic waste classification, and supervise and guide the work of domestic waste classification.Article 44 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall plan to improve fuel structures, develop clean energy, and reduce the generation of solid wastes such as fuel residues.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen management during product production and circulation, avoid excessive packaging, organize the marketing of cleaned vegetables, and reduce the generation of domestic waste.

Article 45 People's governments at or above the county level shall make overall arrangements for the construction of facilities for the collection, transportation, and treatment of urban and rural domestic waste, determine the sites for such facilities, improve the level of comprehensive utilization and harmless disposal of domestic waste, promote the industrialization of domestic waste collection and treatment, and gradually establish and improve a social service system for the prevention and control of domestic waste pollution.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall make overall plans and rationally arrange collection, sorting, and baling points to promote the recycling of domestic waste.

Article 46 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the prevention and control of domestic waste pollution in rural areas, and protect and improve the rural living environment.

The state encourages the reduction of domestic waste at the source in rural areas. In urban-rural fringe areas, densely populated rural areas, and other places where conditions permit, an integrated urban-rural domestic waste management system shall be established; in other rural areas, domestic waste management models shall be actively explored, and domestic waste shall be utilized locally or disposed of properly according to local conditions.

Article 47 The competent department of environmental sanitation of the people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall formulate operational specifications for the construction and operation of facilities and sites for the sweeping, collection, storage, transportation, and treatment of domestic waste, issue a directory for the classification of domestic waste, and strengthen supervision and management.

Article 48 The competent departments of environmental sanitation and other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize the sweeping, collection, transportation, and treatment of urban and rural domestic waste, and may select qualified units through bidding or other means to engage in the sweeping, collection, transportation, and treatment of domestic waste.

Article 49 Units, families, and individuals that generate domestic waste shall, in accordance with the law, fulfill their obligations to reduce domestic waste at the source and sort it for disposal, and bear the responsibility of domestic waste generators.

Any unit or individual shall sort and dispose of domestic waste at designated locations in accordance with the law. It is prohibited to arbitrarily dump, discard, pile up, or burn domestic waste.

Government agencies, public institutions, etc., shall play an exemplary and leading role in the classification of domestic waste.

Domestic waste that has been sorted for disposal shall be collected, transported, and treated separately according to regulations.

Article 50 The sweeping, collection, transportation, and treatment of urban and rural domestic waste shall comply with the state's regulations on environmental protection and environmental sanitation management to prevent environmental pollution.

Hazardous waste that is sorted and collected separately from domestic waste and falls under the category of hazardous waste shall be managed in accordance with hazardous waste regulations.

Article 51 Operating units engaged in public transportation shall promptly sweep and collect domestic waste generated during the transportation process.

Article 52 Farmers' markets, agricultural product wholesale markets, etc., shall strengthen environmental sanitation management, maintain environmental cleanliness, and promptly sweep, sort, collect, and properly dispose of the waste generated.

Article 53 Units engaged in the development of new urban areas, the renovation of old areas, the development and construction of residential communities, and the construction of villages and towns, as well as the management and operation units of public facilities and places such as airports, docks, railway stations, parks, shopping malls, and sports venues, shall, in accordance with the state's regulations on environmental sanitation, construct supporting facilities for the collection of domestic waste.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall coordinate the effective connection between public domestic waste transfer and treatment facilities and the collection facilities specified in the preceding paragraph, and strengthen the integration of the classified domestic waste collection and transportation system and the renewable resource recycling system in terms of planning, construction, and operation.

Article 54 Materials recovered from domestic waste shall be used in accordance with the purposes and standards prescribed by the state, and shall not be used to produce products that may endanger human health.

Article 55 The construction of domestic waste treatment facilities and sites shall meet the environmental protection and environmental sanitation standards prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council and the competent department of housing and urban-rural construction of the State Council.

The construction of domestic waste treatment facilities in adjacent areas is encouraged to be coordinated, and the cross-administrative-regional co-construction and sharing of domestic waste treatment facilities is promoted.

It is prohibited to close, idle, or dismantle domestic waste treatment facilities or sites without authorization; if it is truly necessary to close, idle, or dismantle them, approval shall be obtained from the competent department of environmental sanitation of the people's government at the city or county level after consultation with the competent department of ecological environment at the same level, and measures to prevent environmental pollution shall be taken.

Article 56 Domestic waste treatment units shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, install and use monitoring equipment to monitor the discharge of pollutants in real time, and make the pollutant discharge data publicly available in real time. The monitoring equipment shall be connected to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of ecological environment at the location.

Article 57 The competent department of environmental sanitation of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing the resource utilization and harmless treatment of kitchen waste.

Units and other producers and operators that generate or collect kitchen waste shall hand over the kitchen waste to units with corresponding qualifications and conditions for harmless treatment.

Livestock and poultry farms and farming communities are prohibited from using kitchen waste that has not undergone harmless treatment to feed livestock and poultry.

Article 58 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a domestic waste treatment charging system in accordance with the principle of "the generator pays."

When local people's governments at or above the county level formulate standards for domestic waste treatment charges, they shall, based on local actual conditions and the situation of domestic waste classification, reflect differentiated management such as classified pricing and metered charging, and fully solicit public opinions. The standards for domestic waste treatment charges shall be made public.

Domestic waste treatment fees shall be used exclusively for the collection, transportation, and treatment of domestic waste, and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

Article 59 Provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures may, based on actual conditions, formulate specific local management measures for domestic waste.

## Chapter V Construction Waste, Agricultural Solid Waste, etc.

Article 60 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the prevention and control of construction waste pollution and establish a system for the classified treatment of construction waste.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate work plans for the prevention and control of construction waste pollution, including source reduction, classified treatment, and the layout and construction of disposal facilities and sites.

Article 61 The state encourages the adoption of advanced technologies, processes, equipment, and management measures to promote the source reduction of construction waste and establish a construction waste recycling system.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall promote the application of products from the comprehensive utilization of construction waste.

Article 62 The competent department of environmental sanitation of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the prevention and control of construction waste pollution, establish a whole-process management system for construction waste, regulate the generation, collection, storage, transportation, utilization, and disposal of construction waste, promote comprehensive utilization, strengthen the construction of construction waste disposal facilities and sites, ensure disposal safety, and prevent environmental pollution.

Article 63 Engineering construction units shall prepare a construction waste treatment plan, take pollution prevention measures, and report it to the competent department of environmental sanitation of the local people's government at or above the county level for record.

Engineering construction units shall promptly remove solid wastes such as construction waste generated during the construction process, and utilize or dispose of them in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of environmental sanitation.

Engineering construction units shall not arbitrarily dump, discard, or pile up construction waste generated during the construction process.

Article 64 The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for guiding the construction of a recycling system for agricultural solid waste, encouraging and guiding relevant units and other producers and operators to collect, store, transport, utilize, and dispose of agricultural solid waste in accordance with the law, strengthen supervision and management, and prevent environmental pollution.

Article 65 Units and other producers and operators that generate agricultural solid wastes such as straw, discarded agricultural mulch film, and pesticide packaging waste shall take measures for recycling and other measures to prevent environmental pollution.

Units engaged in large-scale livestock and poultry breeding shall promptly collect, store, utilize, or dispose of solid wastes such as livestock and poultry manure generated during the breeding process to avoid causing environmental pollution.

It is prohibited to openly burn straw in densely populated areas, around airports, near major transportation routes, and in other areas designated by local people's governments.

The state encourages the research, development, production, sale, and use of agricultural mulch films that are degradable and harmless in the environment.

Article 66 The state establishes an extended producer responsibility system for products such as electrical and electronic equipment, lead-acid batteries, and automotive power batteries.

Producers of products such as electrical and electronic equipment, lead-acid batteries, and automotive power batteries shall, in accordance with regulations, establish a waste product recycling system that matches the product sales volume through self-building or entrustment, and make it public, to achieve effective recycling and utilization.

The state encourages producers of products to carry out ecological design to promote resource recycling.

Article 67 The state implements a system of multi-channel recycling and centralized treatment for waste electrical and electronic products, etc.

It is prohibited to hand over waste motor vehicles and vessels, etc., to enterprises or individuals that do not meet the prescribed conditions for recycling and dismantling.

The dismantling, utilization, and disposal of waste electrical and electronic products, waste motor vehicles and vessels, etc., shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations and take measures to prevent environmental pollution.

Article 68 The design and manufacture of products and packaging shall comply with the state's regulations on cleaner production. The competent department of standardization of the State Council shall, based on the national economic and technological conditions, the status of solid waste pollution prevention and control, and the technical requirements of the products, organize the formulation of relevant standards to prevent environmental pollution caused by excessive packaging.

Producers and operators shall comply with mandatory standards restricting excessive packaging of goods and avoid excessive packaging. The market supervision and management department and other relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, strengthen the supervision and management of excessive packaging.

Enterprises that produce, sell, or import products and packaging that are legally included in the mandatory recycling catalog shall recycle such products and packaging in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

Industries such as e-commerce, express delivery, and food delivery shall give priority to the use of reusable and easily recyclable packaging, optimize product packaging, reduce the use of packaging, and actively recycle packaging. The competent departments of commerce, postal services, etc., of the local people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and management.

The state encourages and guides consumers to use green packaging and reduced packaging.

Article 69 The state prohibits and restricts the production, sale, and use of non-degradable plastic bags and other disposable plastic products in accordance with the law.

The operating units of commodity retail venues, e-commerce platform enterprises, express delivery enterprises, and food delivery enterprises shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, report to the competent departments of commerce, postal services, etc., on the use and recycling of plastic bags and other disposable plastic products.

The state encourages and guides the reduction of use and active recycling of plastic bags and other disposable plastic products, and promotes the application of recyclable, easily recyclable, and degradable alternative products.

Article 70 Industries such as tourism and accommodation shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, refrain from actively providing disposable items.

Offices of government agencies, enterprises, public institutions, etc., shall use products, equipment, and facilities that are conducive to environmental protection, and reduce the use of disposable office supplies.

Article 71 The maintenance and operation units of urban sewage treatment facilities or sludge treatment units shall safely treat sludge, ensure that the treated sludge meets the relevant national standards, track and record the flow, purpose, and amount of sludge, and report to the competent department of urban drainage and the competent department of ecological environment.

The competent department of urban drainage of the people's government at or above the county level shall include sludge treatment facilities in the urban drainage and sewage treatment plan, promote the simultaneous construction of sludge treatment facilities and sewage treatment facilities, encourage co-treatment, and the collection standards and compensation scope of sewage treatment fees shall cover the cost of sludge treatment and the normal operation cost of sewage treatment facilities.

Article 72 It is prohibited to arbitrarily dump, pile up, discard, or spill sludge generated from urban sewage treatment facilities and treated sludge.

It is prohibited for sludge with heavy metal or other toxic and hazardous substance content exceeding the standard to enter agricultural land.

Units engaged in water body dredging shall treat the bottom mud generated during the dredging process in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state to prevent environmental pollution.

Article 73 Laboratories at all levels and their establishing units shall strengthen the management of solid waste generated in laboratories, and collect, store, transport, utilize, and dispose of laboratory solid waste in accordance with the law. If laboratory solid waste falls under the category of hazardous waste, it shall be managed in accordance with hazardous waste regulations.

## Chapter VI Hazardous Waste

Article 74 The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to the prevention and control of hazardous waste pollution; where this Chapter does not provide otherwise, other relevant provisions of this Law shall apply.

Article 75 The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council shall, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate a national hazardous waste list, and stipulate unified hazardous waste identification standards, identification methods, identification marks, and management requirements for identification units. The national hazardous waste list shall be dynamically adjusted.

The competent department of ecological environment of the State Council shall, based on the hazardous characteristics and generation quantity of hazardous waste, scientifically assess its environmental risks, implement graded and classified management, establish an information-based supervision system, and manage and share hazardous waste transfer data and information through information-based means.

Article 76 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall organize relevant departments to formulate construction plans for centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites, scientifically assess the demand for hazardous waste disposal, and rationally lay out centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites to ensure that hazardous waste within their administrative regions is properly disposed of.

When formulating construction plans for centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites, opinions from relevant industry associations, enterprises, public institutions, experts, and the public shall be solicited.

Adjacent provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may carry out regional cooperation and make overall arrangements for the construction of regional centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites.

Article 77 Hazardous waste identification marks shall be set up on containers and packaging of hazardous waste, as well as on facilities and sites for collecting, storing, transporting, utilizing, and disposing of hazardous waste, in accordance with regulations.

Article 78 Units that generate hazardous waste shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, formulate a hazardous waste management plan; establish a hazardous waste management ledger, truthfully record relevant information, and report to the competent department of ecological environment at the location through the national hazardous waste information management system the types, generation quantities, flow directions, storage, disposal, and other relevant information of hazardous waste.

The hazardous waste management plan referred to in the preceding paragraph shall include measures to reduce the generation quantity and hazardous characteristics of hazardous waste, as well as measures for the storage, utilization, and disposal of hazardous waste. The hazardous waste management plan shall be filed with the competent department of ecological environment at the location of the unit generating the hazardous waste.

If a unit generating hazardous waste has already obtained a pollutant discharge permit, it shall comply with the provisions of the pollutant discharge permit management system.

Article 79 Units that generate hazardous waste shall store, utilize, and dispose of hazardous waste in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state and environmental protection standards, and shall not arbitrarily dump or pile it up.

Article 80 Units engaged in business activities of collecting, storing, utilizing, and disposing of hazardous waste shall apply for and obtain a permit in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state. The specific management measures for permits shall be formulated by the State Council.

It is prohibited to engage in business activities of collecting, storing, utilizing, and disposing of hazardous waste without a permit or not in accordance with the provisions of the permit.

It is prohibited to provide or entrust hazardous waste to units or other producers and operators without a permit for collection, storage, utilization, or disposal activities.

Article 81 The collection and storage of hazardous waste shall be carried out according to the characteristics of the hazardous waste. It is prohibited to mix the collection, storage, transportation, and disposal of hazardous wastes that are incompatible in nature and have not undergone safety disposal.

The storage of hazardous waste shall adopt protective measures that meet national environmental protection standards. It is prohibited to mix hazardous waste with non-hazardous waste for storage.

Units engaged in business activities of collecting, storing, utilizing, and disposing of hazardous waste shall not store hazardous waste for more than one year; if an extension is truly necessary, approval shall be obtained from the competent department of ecological environment that issued the permit; unless otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 82 When transferring hazardous waste, the electronic or paper transfer manifest for hazardous waste shall be filled out and operated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

For the transfer of hazardous waste across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, an application shall be made to the competent department of ecological environment of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the hazardous waste is transferred out. The competent department of ecological environment of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the hazardous waste is transferred out shall promptly consult with and obtain the consent of the competent department of ecological environment of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the hazardous waste is received, approve the transfer of the hazardous waste within the prescribed time limit, and notify the relevant competent departments of ecological environment and transportation departments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government of the approval information. Without approval, the transfer shall not be carried out.

The management of hazardous waste transfer shall be subject to full-process control and efficiency improvement. The specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council in conjunction with the competent department of transportation of the State Council and the public security department.

Article 83 The transportation of hazardous waste shall take measures to prevent environmental pollution and comply with the relevant provisions of the state on the management of dangerous goods transportation.

It is prohibited to carry hazardous waste and passengers on the same means of transport.

Article 84 When places, facilities, equipment, containers, packaging, and other items used for collecting, storing, transporting, utilizing, and disposing of hazardous waste are converted to other uses, they shall be decontaminated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state before use.

Article 85 Units that generate, collect, store, transport, utilize, and dispose of hazardous waste shall, in accordance with the law, formulate preventive measures and emergency plans for accidents, and file them with the competent department of ecological environment at the location and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of solid waste pollution prevention and control; the competent department of ecological environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of solid waste pollution prevention and control shall conduct inspections.

Article 86 Units that cause serious environmental pollution by hazardous waste due to accidents or other emergencies shall immediately take effective measures to eliminate or mitigate the pollution hazard to the environment, promptly notify units and residents that may be affected by the pollution hazard, and report to the competent department of ecological environment at the location and relevant departments, and accept investigation and handling.

Article 87 When a hazardous waste seriously pollutes the environment or there is evidence that it may seriously pollute the environment and threaten the life and property safety of residents, the competent department of ecological environment or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of solid waste pollution prevention and control shall immediately report to the people's government at the same level and the relevant departments of the people's government at the next higher level, and the people's government shall take effective measures to prevent or mitigate the hazard. The relevant people's government may, as necessary, order the cessation of operations that cause or may cause environmental pollution accidents.Article 88 Before the decommissioning of key centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites, the operating entity shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, take pollution prevention measures for the facilities and sites. Decommissioning costs shall be accrued in advance and included in the investment budget or production costs, to be used exclusively for the decommissioning of key centralized hazardous waste disposal facilities and sites. The specific methods for accrual and management shall be stipulated by the finance department and the pricing authority of the State Council, in conjunction with the ecological environment department of the State Council.

Article 89 The transfer of hazardous waste through the territory of the People's Republic of China is prohibited.

Article 90 Medical waste shall be managed in accordance with the National Hazardous Waste List. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the capacity building for centralized medical waste disposal.

The health, ecological environment, and other relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, strengthen the supervision and management of the collection, storage, transportation, and disposal of medical waste to prevent harm to public health and environmental pollution.

Medical and health institutions shall, in accordance with the law, collect medical waste generated by their units by category and hand it over to centralized medical waste disposal units for disposal. Centralized medical waste disposal units shall promptly collect, transport, and dispose of medical waste.

Medical and health institutions and centralized medical waste disposal units shall take effective measures to prevent the loss, leakage, seepage, and spread of medical waste.

Article 91 When a major infectious disease epidemic or other emergency occurs, the local people's government at or above the county level shall coordinate the collection, storage, transportation, and disposal of hazardous waste such as medical waste, and ensure the necessary vehicles, sites, disposal facilities, and protective materials. The health, ecological environment, environmental sanitation, transportation, and other relevant departments shall cooperate and perform emergency response duties in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Safeguard Measures

Article 92 When formulating territorial spatial plans and relevant special plans, the relevant departments of the State Council, local people's governments at or above the county level, and their relevant departments shall coordinate the construction needs for facilities such as the transfer and centralized disposal of domestic waste, construction waste, and hazardous waste, and ensure land use for transfer and centralized disposal facilities.

Article 93 The state adopts economic and technical policies and measures conducive to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, encourages and supports relevant parties to take measures conducive to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, strengthens the training and guidance of personnel engaged in the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and promotes the professionalization and scaled development of the industry for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Article 94 The state encourages and supports scientific research institutions, solid waste generating units, solid waste utilization units, and solid waste disposal units to conduct joint research, develop new technologies for the comprehensive utilization and centralized disposal of solid waste, and promote technological progress in the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Article 95 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and, in accordance with the principle of division of powers, allocate necessary funds for the following matters:

(1) Scientific research and technological development for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste;
(2) Classification of domestic waste;
(3) Construction of centralized solid waste disposal facilities;
(4) Emergency disposal of hazardous waste such as medical waste generated during major infectious disease epidemics and other emergencies;
(5) Other matters related to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.

Performance management and audit supervision shall be strengthened for the use of funds to ensure the effectiveness of fund utilization.

Article 96 The state encourages and supports social forces to participate in the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and provides policy support in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 97 The state develops green finance and encourages financial institutions to increase credit allocation for projects related to the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste.
Article 98 Entities engaged in the comprehensive utilization of solid waste and other work for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste shall enjoy tax incentives in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

The state encourages and advocates for all sectors of society to donate property for the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste, and grants tax incentives in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 99 Units that collect, store, transport, utilize, or dispose of hazardous waste shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, purchase environmental pollution liability insurance.

Article 100 The state encourages units and individuals to purchase and use comprehensively utilized products and reusable products.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, in the government procurement process, give priority to the purchase of comprehensively utilized products and reusable products.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 101 If the ecological environment department or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at a higher level shall order correction, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to grant administrative permits or handle approval documents in accordance with the law;
(2) Covering up illegal acts;
(3) Failing to seal up or seize in accordance with the law;
(4) Failing to investigate after discovering an illegal act or receiving a report of an illegal act;
(5) Other illegal acts such as abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

If an administrative penalty decision should have been made in accordance with the provisions of this Law but was not, the competent department at a higher level may directly make the administrative penalty decision.

Article 102 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, fined, and have illegal gains confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down:

(1) A unit that generates, collects, stores, transports, utilizes, or disposes of solid waste fails to publicly disclose information on the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste in a timely manner in accordance with the law;
(2) A domestic waste treatment unit fails to install and use monitoring equipment, monitor pollutant emissions in real-time, and publicly disclose pollution emission data in accordance with relevant state regulations;
(3) Transferring equipment that has been phased out and included in the list of equipment to be phased out within a specified period to others for use;
(4) Constructing facilities and sites for the centralized storage, utilization, or disposal of industrial solid waste and hazardous waste, or domestic waste landfills, within ecological protection red line areas, permanent basic farmland concentrated areas, and other areas requiring special protection;(5) Transferring solid waste out of the administrative region of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for storage or disposal without approval;
(6) Transferring solid waste out of the administrative region of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for utilization without filing for the record;
(7) Arbitrarily dumping, piling up, discarding, or scattering industrial solid waste, or failing to take corresponding preventive measures, causing the scattering, loss, leakage, or other environmental pollution of industrial solid waste;
(8) A unit generating industrial solid waste fails to establish a solid waste management ledger and keep truthful records;
(9) A unit generating industrial solid waste violates the provisions of this Law by entrusting others to transport, utilize, or dispose of industrial solid waste;
(10) Failing to take protective measures that meet national environmental protection standards when storing industrial solid waste;
(11) Units and other producers or operators violate other requirements for solid waste management, polluting the environment and damaging ecosystems.

For acts under items (1) and (8) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under items (2), (3), (4), (5), (6), (9), (10), and (11) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; for the act under item (7) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the required disposal cost shall be imposed, and if the required disposal cost is less than 100,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 100,000 yuan. If other laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise for the penalty for the act under item (11) of the preceding paragraph, those provisions shall apply.

Article 103 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection through delaying, besieging, or detaining law enforcement personnel, or by practicing fraud during the acceptance of supervision and inspection, shall be ordered by the ecological environment department or other departments responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of environmental pollution by solid waste to make corrections, and fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 104 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by generating industrial solid waste without obtaining a pollutant discharge permit in accordance with the law shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, or be subject to production restrictions or suspension for remediation, and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down.

Article 105 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by failing to comply with mandatory standards for restricting excessive packaging of commodities shall be ordered by the market supervision and management department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the relevant department to make corrections; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 106 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by failing to comply with the state's regulations on prohibiting or restricting the use of non-degradable plastic bags and other disposable plastic products, or by failing to report the use of plastic bags and other disposable plastic products in accordance with relevant state regulations, shall be ordered by the commerce, postal, or other relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, and fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 107 Anyone engaged in large-scale livestock and poultry breeding who fails to promptly collect, store, utilize, or dispose of solid waste such as livestock and poultry manure generated during the breeding process shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, and may be fined not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down.

Article 108 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by failing to track and record the flow, use, and quantity of sludge, or by failing to meet relevant national standards for treated sludge, shall be ordered by the urban drainage department to make corrections and given a warning; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if correction is refused, the urban drainage department may designate a unit with remediation capabilities to carry out remediation on its behalf, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by arbitrarily dumping, piling up, discarding, or scattering sludge generated by urban sewage treatment facilities and treated sludge shall be ordered by the urban drainage department to make corrections, and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 2,000,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if correction is refused, the urban drainage department may designate a unit with remediation capabilities to carry out remediation on its behalf, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

Article 109 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by producing, selling, importing, or using phased-out equipment, or by adopting phased-out production processes, shall be ordered by the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan, and have illegal gains confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall submit an opinion, and upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down.

Article 110 If a storage facility for mining solid waste such as tailings, coal gangue, or waste rock fails to be closed in accordance with national environmental protection regulations after being decommissioned, the ecological environment department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

Article 111 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the environmental sanitation department of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, fined, and have illegal gains confiscated:

(1) Arbitrarily dumping, scattering, piling up, or burning domestic waste;
(2) Unauthorized closure, idling, or removal of domestic waste treatment facilities or sites;
(3) An engineering construction unit fails to prepare a construction waste treatment plan for filing, or fails to promptly remove solid waste generated during the construction process;
(4) An engineering construction unit arbitrarily dumps, scatters, or piles up construction waste generated during the construction process, or fails to utilize or dispose of solid waste generated during the construction process in accordance with regulations;
(5) Units and other producers or operators that generate or collect kitchen waste fail to hand over kitchen waste to units with corresponding qualifications for harmless treatment;
(6) Livestock and poultry farms or breeding communities use kitchen waste that has not been harmlessly treated to feed livestock and poultry;
(7) Discarding or scattering domestic waste along the way during transportation.

If a unit commits an act under item (1) or (7) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a unit commits an act under item (2), (3), (4), (5), or (6) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if an individual commits an act under item (1), (5), or (7) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100 yuan but not more than 500 yuan shall be imposed.

Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by failing to sort and deposit domestic waste at designated locations shall be ordered by the environmental sanitation department of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the unit, and a fine shall be imposed on the individual in accordance with the law.

Article 112 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, fined, and have illegal gains confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down:

(1) Failing to set up hazardous waste identification signs in accordance with regulations;
(2) Failing to formulate a hazardous waste management plan or declare relevant hazardous waste information in accordance with relevant state regulations;
(3) Arbitrarily dumping or piling up hazardous waste;
(4) Providing or entrusting hazardous waste to units or other producers or operators without a permit for business operations;
(5) Failing to fill out or operate a hazardous waste transfer manifest in accordance with relevant state regulations, or transferring hazardous waste without approval;
(6) Failing to store, utilize, or dispose of hazardous waste in accordance with national environmental protection standards, or mixing hazardous waste with non-hazardous waste for storage;
(7) Mixing, collecting, storing, transporting, or disposing of incompatible hazardous waste without safety treatment;
(8) Transporting hazardous waste together with passengers on the same vehicle;
(9) Converting the use of places, facilities, equipment, containers, packaging, and other items used for collecting, storing, transporting, or disposing of hazardous waste without decontamination treatment;
(10) Failing to take corresponding preventive measures, causing the scattering, loss, leakage, or other environmental pollution of hazardous waste;
(11) Discarding or scattering hazardous waste along the way during transportation;
(12) Failing to formulate preventive measures and emergency plans for hazardous waste accidents;
(13) Failing to establish a hazardous waste management ledger and keep truthful records in accordance with relevant state regulations.

For acts under items (1), (2), (5), (6), (7), (8), (9), (12), and (13) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; for acts under items (3), (4), (10), and (11) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the required disposal cost shall be imposed, and if the required disposal cost is less than 200,000 yuan, it shall be calculated as 200,000 yuan.

Article 113 If a hazardous waste generator violates the provisions of this Law by failing to dispose of the hazardous waste it generates in accordance with regulations and refuses to correct after being ordered to do so, the ecological environment department shall organize disposal on its behalf, and the disposal costs shall be borne by the hazardous waste generator; if the generator refuses to bear the disposal costs, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the disposal costs shall be imposed.

Article 114 Anyone who engages in the business of collecting, storing, utilizing, or disposing of hazardous waste without a permit shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, fined not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan, and upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down; the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

Anyone who engages in the business of collecting, storing, utilizing, or disposing of hazardous waste in violation of the provisions of the permit shall be ordered by the ecological environment department to make corrections, subject to production restrictions or suspension for remediation, and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan; the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve, the business may be ordered to suspend or close down, and the license may also be revoked by the issuing authority.

Article 115 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by importing solid waste from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be ordered by the customs to return the solid waste, and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

The carrier shall bear joint and several liability with the importer for the return and disposal of the solid waste specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 116 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by transferring hazardous waste through the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be ordered by the customs to return the hazardous waste, and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

Article 117 For solid waste that has been illegally imported, the ecological environment department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, in accordance with the law, submit a handling opinion to the customs, and the customs shall make a penalty decision in accordance with the provisions of Article 115 of this Law; if environmental pollution has already been caused, the ecological environment department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall order the importer to eliminate the pollution.

Article 118 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by causing an accident of environmental pollution by solid waste shall, in addition to bearing compensation liability in accordance with the law, be fined by the ecological environment department in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of this Article, and be ordered to take remediation measures within a specified period; if a major or extraordinarily serious accident of environmental pollution by solid waste is caused, the business may also be ordered to close down upon approval by the people's government with the authority to approve.

If a general or relatively serious accident of environmental pollution by solid waste is caused, the fine shall be calculated at not less than one time but not more than three times the direct economic losses caused by the accident; if a major or extraordinarily serious accident of environmental pollution by solid waste is caused, the fine shall be calculated at not less than three times but not more than five times the direct economic losses caused by the accident, and the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible personnel shall be fined not more than 50% of their income from the unit in the previous year.

Article 119 If a unit or other producer or operator violates the provisions of this Law by discharging solid waste, is fined, and is ordered to make corrections, the administrative organ that made the penalty decision shall organize a review; if it is found that the unit or individual continues to commit the illegal act, a daily fine shall be imposed consecutively in accordance with the provisions of the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 120 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, which does not constitute a crime, shall be detained by the public security organ for not less than 10 days but not more than 15 days for the legal representative, principal responsible person, directly responsible supervisor, and other responsible personnel; if the circumstances are relatively minor, detention for not less than 5 days but not more than 10 days shall be imposed:(1) Dumping, piling up, discarding, or scattering solid waste without authorization, causing serious consequences;

(2) Constructing facilities or sites for centralized storage, utilization, or disposal of industrial solid waste or hazardous waste, or landfills for domestic waste, within ecological protection redline areas, permanent basic farmland concentration areas, or other areas requiring special protection;

(3) Providing or entrusting hazardous waste to units or other producers and operators without a license for piling, utilization, or disposal;

(4) Engaging in collection, storage, utilization, or disposal of hazardous waste without a license or in violation of license provisions;

(5) Transferring hazardous waste without authorization;

(6) Failing to take preventive measures, causing hazardous waste to scatter, flow away, leak, or result in other serious consequences.

Article 121 Where solid waste pollution damages the environment, destroys ecosystems, and harms national interests or public interests, relevant authorities and organizations may file lawsuits with the People's Court in accordance with the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, and other laws.

Article 122 Where solid waste pollution damages the environment and destroys ecosystems, causing significant losses to the state, the local people's government at or above the city with districts level, or its designated departments or institutions, shall negotiate with the units and other producers and operators responsible for the environmental pollution and ecological damage, demanding compensation for damages; if negotiation fails, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

For solid waste seized during law enforcement that cannot be attributed to a responsible party or cannot be returned, the local people's government at or above the county level shall organize its disposal.

Article 123 Any violation of this Law that constitutes a violation of public security administration shall be subject to public security administrative penalties by the public security organs according to law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it causes personal injury or property damage, civil liability shall be borne according to law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 124 For the purposes of this Law, the following terms mean:

(1) "Solid waste" refers to items and substances in solid, semi-solid, or gaseous form contained in containers that have lost their original use value or, although not losing their use value, are discarded or abandoned during production, daily life, or other activities, as well as items and substances that are included in the management of solid waste by laws and administrative regulations. This excludes items and substances that have been harmlessly processed and meet mandatory national product quality standards, do not endanger public health or ecological safety, or are determined not to be solid waste according to solid waste identification standards and procedures.

(2) "Industrial solid waste" refers to solid waste generated during industrial production activities.

(3) "Domestic waste" refers to solid waste generated during daily life or activities providing services for daily life, as well as solid waste deemed as domestic waste by laws and administrative regulations.

(4) "Construction waste" refers to waste soil, waste materials, and other solid waste generated during the construction, renovation, expansion, and demolition of various buildings, structures, pipelines, etc., by construction units and construction units, as well as during residential decoration and renovation.

(5) "Agricultural solid waste" refers to solid waste generated during agricultural production activities.

(6) "Hazardous waste" refers to solid waste listed in the national hazardous waste list or identified as having hazardous characteristics according to national hazardous waste identification standards and methods.

(7) "Storage" refers to the activity of temporarily placing solid waste in specific facilities or sites.

(8) "Utilization" refers to the activity of extracting substances from solid waste as raw materials or fuel.

(9) "Disposal" refers to activities such as incineration of solid waste or other methods that alter the physical, chemical, or biological characteristics of solid waste to reduce the quantity of generated solid waste, reduce its volume, reduce or eliminate its hazardous components, or the activity of finally placing solid waste in landfills that meet environmental protection requirements.

Article 125 This Law applies to the prevention and control of pollution from liquid waste; however, the prevention and control of pollution from wastewater discharged into water bodies shall be governed by relevant laws and not this Law.

Article 126 This Law shall take effect on September 1, 2020.
