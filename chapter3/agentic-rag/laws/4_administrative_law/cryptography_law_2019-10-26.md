# Cryptography Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2019

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating the application and management of cryptography, promoting the development of the cryptography industry, ensuring network and information security, safeguarding national security and public interests, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "cryptography" refers to technologies, products, and services that use specific transformation methods to provide encryption protection and security authentication for information.

Article 3 Cryptography work shall adhere to a holistic approach to national security, following the principles of unified leadership and graded responsibility, innovation-driven development serving the overall situation, and law-based management ensuring security.

Article 4 The leadership of the Communist Party of China over cryptography work shall be upheld. The central cryptography leadership body shall exercise unified leadership over national cryptography work, formulate major policies and guidelines for national cryptography, coordinate major cryptography matters and important tasks, and promote the rule of law in national cryptography.
Article 5 The national cryptography administration shall be responsible for managing cryptography work nationwide. Local cryptography administrations at or above the county level shall be responsible for managing cryptography work within their respective administrative regions.

State organs and units involved in cryptography work shall be responsible for cryptography work within their own organs, units, or systems within the scope of their duties.

Article 6 The State implements classified management of cryptography.

Cryptography is classified into core cryptography, ordinary cryptography, and commercial cryptography.

Article 7 Core cryptography and ordinary cryptography are used to protect classified state information. The highest classification level for information protected by core cryptography is "top secret," and the highest classification level for information protected by ordinary cryptography is "secret."

Core cryptography and ordinary cryptography are state secrets. Cryptography administrations shall exercise strict and unified management over core cryptography and ordinary cryptography in accordance with this Law, relevant laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions.

Article 8 Commercial cryptography is used to protect information that does not fall under state secrets.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations may lawfully use commercial cryptography to protect network and information security.

Article 9 The State encourages and supports research and application of cryptographic science and technology, protects intellectual property rights in the field of cryptography in accordance with the law, and promotes progress and innovation in cryptographic science and technology.

The State strengthens the cultivation of cryptographic talent and team building, and provides commendation and rewards to organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to cryptography work in accordance with relevant state provisions.

Article 10 The State strengthens cryptography security education through various forms, incorporates cryptography security education into the national education system and the civil service education and training system, and enhances the cryptography security awareness of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 11 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate cryptography work into their respective national economic and social development plans, and the necessary funds shall be included in their respective fiscal budgets.

Article 12 No organization or individual may steal encrypted information of others or illegally intrude into another's cryptography security system.
No organization or individual may use cryptography to engage in illegal or criminal activities that endanger national security, public interests, or the lawful rights and interests of others.

## Chapter II Core Cryptography and Ordinary Cryptography

Article 13 The State strengthens the scientific planning, management, and use of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography, strengthens institutional development, improves management measures, and enhances cryptography security capabilities.

Article 14 Classified state information transmitted through wired or wireless communications, as well as information systems that store or process classified state information, shall use core cryptography or ordinary cryptography for encryption protection and security authentication in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions.

Article 15 Institutions engaged in scientific research, production, service, testing, equipment, use, and destruction of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography (hereinafter collectively referred to as cryptography work institutions) shall establish and improve security management systems, adopt strict confidentiality measures and confidentiality responsibility systems, and ensure the security of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, relevant state provisions, and the requirements of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography standards.

Article 16 Cryptography administrations shall, in accordance with the law, provide guidance, supervision, and inspection of the core cryptography and ordinary cryptography work of cryptography work institutions, and such institutions shall cooperate.

Article 17 Cryptography administrations shall, as needed, establish collaborative mechanisms with relevant departments for security monitoring and early warning, security risk assessment, information notification, consultation on major matters, and emergency response for core cryptography and ordinary cryptography, ensuring coordinated and efficient security management of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography.

If a cryptography work institution discovers a leak of core cryptography or ordinary cryptography, or major problems or potential risks affecting the security of core cryptography or ordinary cryptography, it shall immediately take countermeasures and promptly report to the confidentiality administration and the cryptography administration. The confidentiality administration and the cryptography administration shall, together with relevant departments, organize investigations and handle the situation, and guide the relevant cryptography work institution to promptly eliminate security risks.
Article 18 The State strengthens the construction of cryptography work institutions to ensure they fulfill their work responsibilities.

The State establishes management systems for personnel recruitment, selection, confidentiality, assessment, training, treatment, rewards and punishments, exchange, and exit that meet the needs of core cryptography and ordinary cryptography work.

Article 19 When necessary for work, cryptography administrations may, in accordance with relevant state provisions, request public security, transportation, customs, and other departments to provide convenience such as exemption from inspection for items and personnel related to core cryptography and ordinary cryptography, and the relevant departments shall provide assistance.

Article 20 Cryptography administrations and cryptography work institutions shall establish and improve strict supervision and security review systems, supervise their staff's compliance with laws and discipline, and take necessary measures to conduct security reviews regularly or irregularly in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III Commercial Cryptography

Article 21 The State encourages research and development, academic exchange, transformation of achievements, and promotion and application of commercial cryptography technology, improves a unified, open, competitive, and orderly commercial cryptography market system, and encourages and promotes the development of the commercial cryptography industry.

People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall adhere to the principle of non-discrimination and treat equally in accordance with the law all units engaged in commercial cryptography scientific research, production, sales, service, import and export, including foreign-invested enterprises (hereinafter collectively referred to as commercial cryptography practitioners). The State encourages technical cooperation in commercial cryptography based on the principle of voluntariness and commercial rules during foreign investment. Administrative organs and their staff shall not use administrative means to force the transfer of commercial cryptography technology.

Scientific research, production, sales, service, and import and export of commercial cryptography shall not harm national security, public interests, or the lawful rights and interests of others.

Article 22 The State establishes and improves the commercial cryptography standards system.

The standardization administrative department of the State Council and the national cryptography administration shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, organize the formulation of national and industry standards for commercial cryptography.

The State supports social groups and enterprises in using independent innovation technologies to formulate commercial cryptography group standards and enterprise standards that are higher than the technical requirements of relevant national and industry standards.

Article 23 The State promotes participation in international standardization activities for commercial cryptography, participates in the formulation of international standards for commercial cryptography, and promotes the transformation and application of Chinese standards for commercial cryptography with foreign standards.

The State encourages enterprises, social groups, and educational and research institutions to participate in international standardization activities for commercial cryptography.

Article 24 Commercial cryptography practitioners conducting commercial cryptography activities shall comply with the technical requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, mandatory national standards for commercial cryptography, and the public standards of the practitioner.

The State encourages commercial cryptography practitioners to adopt recommended national and industry standards for commercial cryptography to enhance the protection capability of commercial cryptography and safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of users.

Article 25 The State promotes the establishment of a commercial cryptography testing and certification system, formulates technical specifications and rules for commercial cryptography testing and certification, and encourages commercial cryptography practitioners to voluntarily undergo commercial cryptography testing and certification to enhance market competitiveness.

Commercial cryptography testing and certification institutions shall obtain relevant qualifications in accordance with the law and conduct commercial cryptography testing and certification in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and technical specifications and rules for commercial cryptography testing and certification.

Commercial cryptography testing and certification institutions shall assume confidentiality obligations for state secrets and trade secrets learned during commercial cryptography testing and certification.

Article 26 Commercial cryptography products that involve national security, the national economy and people's livelihood, or public interests shall be included in the catalog of critical network equipment and specialized network security products in accordance with the law, and may only be sold or provided after being tested and certified as qualified by qualified institutions. The testing and certification of commercial cryptography products shall apply the relevant provisions of the Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China to avoid duplicate testing and certification.
If commercial cryptography services use network key equipment and network security-specific products, they shall be certified qualified by a commercial cryptography certification institution for such commercial cryptography services.
Article 27 Operators of critical information infrastructure that are required by laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions to use commercial cryptography for protection shall use commercial cryptography for protection and conduct security assessments of commercial cryptography applications themselves or entrust commercial cryptography testing institutions to do so. Security assessments of commercial cryptography applications shall be coordinated with critical information infrastructure security testing and assessment and network security classification protection assessment systems to avoid duplicate assessment and evaluation.
If an operator of critical information infrastructure procures network products and services involving commercial cryptography that may affect national security, it shall undergo a national security review organized by the national cyberspace administration together with the national cryptography administration and other relevant departments in accordance with the provisions of the Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 28 The competent commerce department of the State Council and the national cryptography administration shall, in accordance with the law, implement import licensing for commercial cryptography that involves national security and public interests and has encryption protection functions, and implement export controls for commercial cryptography that involves national security, public interests, or China's international obligations. The import licensing list and export control list for commercial cryptography shall be formulated and published by the competent commerce department of the State Council together with the national cryptography administration and the General Administration of Customs.

Commercial cryptography used in mass consumer products shall not be subject to import licensing and export control systems.

Article 29 The national cryptography administration shall certify institutions that use commercial cryptography technology to provide electronic authentication services for e-government, and shall, together with relevant departments, be responsible for the management of the use of electronic signatures and data messages in government affairs activities.

Article 30 Industry associations and other organizations in the field of commercial cryptography shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and their articles of association, provide information, technology, training, and other services to commercial cryptography practitioners, guide and urge commercial cryptography practitioners to conduct commercial cryptography activities in accordance with the law, strengthen industry self-discipline, promote industry integrity construction, and promote healthy industry development.

Article 31 Cryptography administrations and relevant departments shall establish a commercial cryptography supervision system combining routine supervision and random inspections during and after the event, establish a unified commercial cryptography supervision and management information platform, promote the connection of supervision during and after the event with the social credit system, and strengthen the self-discipline of commercial cryptography practitioners and social supervision.

Cryptography administrations and relevant departments and their staff shall not require commercial cryptography practitioners and commercial cryptography testing and certification institutions to disclose source code and other cryptography-related proprietary information, and shall strictly keep confidential the trade secrets and personal privacy learned in the performance of their duties, and shall not disclose or illegally provide them to others.

## Chapter IV Legal Liability

Article 32 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 12 of this Law by stealing encrypted information of others, illegally intruding into another's cryptography security system, or using cryptography to engage in illegal activities that endanger national security, public interests, or the lawful rights and interests of others shall be investigated for legal liability by relevant departments in accordance with the Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.
Article 33 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 14 of this Law by failing to use core cryptography or ordinary cryptography as required shall be ordered by the cryptography administration to rectify or cease the illegal act and given a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the cryptography administration shall recommend that the relevant state organ or unit impose sanctions or handle the matter in accordance with the law against the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons.

Article 34 In the event of a leak of core cryptography or ordinary cryptography in violation of the provisions of this Law, the confidentiality administration or the cryptography administration shall recommend that the relevant state organ or unit impose sanctions or handle the matter in accordance with the law against the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons.

Anyone who violates the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 17 of this Law by failing to immediately take countermeasures or failing to promptly report upon discovering a leak of core cryptography or ordinary cryptography, or major problems or potential risks affecting the security of core cryptography or ordinary cryptography, shall be recommended by the confidentiality administration or the cryptography administration to the relevant state organ or unit to impose sanctions or handle the matter in accordance with the law against the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons.

Article 35 If a commercial cryptography testing or certification institution violates the provisions of the second and third paragraphs of Article 25 of this Law by conducting commercial cryptography testing or certification, the market supervision administration together with the cryptography administration shall order it to rectify or cease the illegal act, give a warning, and confiscate illegal gains; if the illegal gains are more than 300,000 yuan, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal gains may be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 300,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan may be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant qualifications shall be revoked in accordance with the law.

Article 36 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 26 of this Law by selling or providing commercial cryptography products that have not been tested and certified or have failed testing and certification, or providing commercial cryptography services that have not been certified or have failed certification, shall be ordered by the market supervision administration together with the cryptography administration to rectify or cease the illegal act, given a warning, and the illegal products and illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the illegal gains are more than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal gains may be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 37 If an operator of critical information infrastructure violates the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 27 of this Law by failing to use commercial cryptography as required or failing to conduct security assessments of commercial cryptography applications as required, the cryptography administration shall order it to rectify and give a warning; if it refuses to rectify or causes consequences such as endangering network security, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge.

If an operator of critical information infrastructure violates the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 27 of this Law by using products or services that have not undergone security review or have failed security review, the relevant competent department shall order it to cease use and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the procurement amount; a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons.

Article 38 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 28 of this Law on import licensing and export controls by importing or exporting commercial cryptography shall be punished by the competent commerce department of the State Council or the customs in accordance with the law.

Article 39 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 29 of this Law by engaging in electronic authentication services for e-government without certification shall be ordered by the cryptography administration to rectify or cease the illegal act, given a warning, and the illegal products and illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the illegal gains are more than 300,000 yuan, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal gains may be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 300,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 40 Staff members of cryptography administrations, relevant departments, and units who abuse their power, neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or disclose or illegally provide to others trade secrets and personal privacy learned in the performance of their duties in cryptography work shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 41 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if damage is caused to others, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 42 The national cryptography administration shall formulate cryptography management regulations in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 43 The management measures for cryptography work of the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 44 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2020.
