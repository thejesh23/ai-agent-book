# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Noise Pollution

Adopted at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purposes of preventing and controlling noise pollution, protecting public health, preserving and improving the living environment, maintaining social harmony, advancing ecological civilization, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "noise" means sounds generated in industrial production, construction, transportation, and social activities that disturb the surrounding living environment.

For the purposes of this Law, "noise pollution" means the phenomenon where noise exceeds the noise emission standards or is generated without legally required prevention and control measures, thereby interfering with others' normal life, work, and study.

**Article 3** This Law applies to the prevention and control of noise pollution.

The prevention and control of harm caused by noise in the course of performing one's own production and business operations shall be governed by other relevant laws, such as those on labor protection.

**Article 4** The prevention and control of noise pollution shall adhere to the principles of unified planning, source prevention and control, classified management, social co-governance, and liability for damage.

**Article 5** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate noise pollution prevention and control work into the national economic and social development plan and the ecological and environmental protection plan, and shall include the funding for noise pollution prevention and control work in their respective government budgets.

Ecological and environmental protection plans shall specify the objectives, tasks, and safeguard measures for noise pollution prevention and control.

**Article 6** Local people's governments at all levels are responsible for the acoustic environment quality within their respective administrative regions and shall take effective measures to improve the acoustic environment quality.

The State implements a target responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for noise pollution prevention and control, incorporating the completion of noise pollution prevention and control targets into the assessment and evaluation content.

**Article 7** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law and the regulations of the State Council, define the supervision and management responsibilities of relevant departments for noise pollution prevention and control, establish coordination and linkage mechanisms for noise pollution prevention and control work as needed, strengthen inter-departmental coordination, cooperation, and information sharing, and promote noise pollution prevention and control work within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 8** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the national prevention and control of noise pollution.

The competent departments of ecological environment of local people's governments shall exercise unified supervision and management over the prevention and control of noise pollution within their respective administrative regions.

Departments of housing and urban-rural development, public security, transportation, railway supervision and administration, civil aviation, and maritime affairs at all levels shall, within their respective functions and duties, exercise supervision and management over the prevention and control of noise pollution from construction, transportation, and social activities.

Grassroots mass autonomous organizations shall assist local people's governments and their relevant departments in carrying out noise pollution prevention and control work.

**Article 9** Any unit or individual has the obligation to protect the acoustic environment and, at the same time, has the right to obtain information on the acoustic environment and to participate in and supervise the prevention and control of noise pollution in accordance with the law.

Units and individuals that emit noise shall take effective measures to prevent and mitigate noise pollution.

**Article 10** People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall strengthen the publicity, education, and popularization of laws, regulations, and knowledge on noise pollution prevention and control, enhance public awareness of noise pollution prevention and control, and guide the public to participate in noise pollution prevention and control work in accordance with the law.

News media shall conduct public interest publicity on laws, regulations, and knowledge related to noise pollution prevention and control, and exercise public opinion supervision over acts violating laws and regulations on noise pollution prevention and control.

The State encourages grassroots mass autonomous organizations, social organizations, managers of public places, owners' committees, property service providers, volunteers, and others to carry out publicity on laws, regulations, and knowledge related to noise pollution prevention and control.

**Article 11** The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research and development, transformation of achievements, and promotion and application of noise pollution prevention and control, strengthens the training of professional and technical personnel in noise pollution prevention and control, and promotes scientific and technological progress and industrial development in noise pollution prevention and control.

**Article 12** Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions in noise pollution prevention and control work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with State regulations.

## Chapter II: Standards and Planning for Noise Pollution Prevention and Control

**Article 13** The State promotes the establishment of a standard system for noise pollution prevention and control.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council shall, within their respective functions and duties, formulate and improve relevant standards for noise pollution prevention and control, and strengthen the coordination and coherence between standards.

**Article 14** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council formulates the national acoustic environment quality standards.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the national acoustic environment quality standards, territorial spatial planning, and current land use, delineate the applicable areas for various types of acoustic environment quality standards within their respective administrative regions; they shall designate areas dominated by buildings used for residence, scientific research, medical and health care, culture and education, government offices, social welfare, etc., as concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, and strengthen noise pollution prevention and control.

The scope of applicable areas for acoustic environment quality standards and the scope of concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings shall be made public.

**Article 15** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council formulates national noise emission standards and related environmental vibration control standards based on the national acoustic environment quality standards and the national economic and technical conditions.

For provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where no national noise emission standards have been formulated, they may formulate local noise emission standards; where national noise emission standards have been formulated, they may formulate local noise emission standards that are stricter than the national noise emission standards. Local noise emission standards shall be filed with the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

**Article 16** The competent department of standardization under the State Council, together with the departments of development and reform, ecological environment, industry and information technology, housing and urban-rural development, transportation, railway supervision and administration, civil aviation, and maritime affairs under the State Council, shall, for products such as industrial equipment, construction machinery, motor vehicles, railway locomotives and rolling stock, urban rail transit vehicles, civil aircraft, motor vessels, electrical and electronic products, and building ancillary equipment that may generate noise pollution, stipulate noise limits in their technical specifications or product quality standards, based on the requirements for acoustic environment protection and the national economic and technical conditions.

The noise limits for the products specified in the preceding paragraph when in use shall be indicated in the relevant technical documents. The production, import, or sale of products that do not meet the noise limits is prohibited.

Market supervision and administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct supervision and random inspections of products with noise limits that are produced or sold, and conduct supervision and random testing of noise emitted by special equipment such as elevators when in use, with the cooperation of the competent department of ecological environment.

**Article 17** Acoustic environment quality standards, noise emission standards, and other relevant standards for noise pollution prevention and control shall be regularly evaluated and revised in a timely manner based on the evaluation results.

**Article 18** When formulating and revising territorial spatial plans and related plans, people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall conduct environmental impact assessments in accordance with the law, fully consider the impact of noise generated by urban and rural area development, renovation, and construction projects on the surrounding living environment, make unified plans, reasonably arrange land use and construction layout, and prevent and mitigate noise pollution. The relevant environmental impact chapters, descriptions, or reports shall include content on noise pollution prevention and control.

**Article 19** When determining the construction layout, the noise-proof distance between buildings and traffic arteries, etc., shall be reasonably delineated based on the national acoustic environment quality standards and relevant standards for sound insulation design of civil buildings, and corresponding planning and design requirements shall be proposed.

**Article 20** People's governments of cities divided into districts and counties located in areas that have not met the national acoustic environment quality standards shall promptly formulate acoustic environment quality improvement plans and implementation schemes, and take effective measures to improve the acoustic environment quality.

The acoustic environment quality improvement plans and implementation schemes shall be made public.

**Article 21** When formulating acoustic environment quality improvement plans and implementation schemes, and formulating or revising relevant standards for noise pollution prevention and control, opinions from relevant industry associations, enterprises and institutions, experts, and the public shall be solicited.

## Chapter III: Supervision and Management of Noise Pollution Prevention and Control

**Article 22** The emission of noise and generation of vibration shall comply with the noise emission standards, related environmental vibration control standards, and the requirements of relevant laws, regulations, and rules.

Units that emit noise and managers of public places shall establish a responsibility system for noise pollution prevention and control, and clearly define the responsibilities of the responsible persons and relevant personnel.

**Article 23** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council is responsible for formulating specifications for noise monitoring and evaluation, organizing the establishment of an acoustic environment quality monitoring network together with other relevant departments under the State Council, planning the layout of national acoustic environment quality monitoring stations (points), organizing national acoustic environment quality monitoring, promoting monitoring automation, and uniformly publishing national information on the status of acoustic environment quality.

The competent departments of ecological environment of local people's governments, together with relevant departments, shall set up acoustic environment quality monitoring stations (points) within their respective administrative regions in accordance with regulations, organize acoustic environment quality monitoring within their respective administrative regions, and regularly publish information on the status of acoustic environment quality to the public.

Local ecological environment and other departments shall strengthen the investigation and monitoring of noise emissions in key areas such as the vicinity of noise-sensitive buildings.

**Article 24** For new construction, renovation, and expansion projects that may generate noise pollution, an environmental impact assessment shall be conducted in accordance with the law.

**Article 25** Noise pollution prevention and control facilities of a construction project shall be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project.

Before a construction project is put into production or use, the construction entity shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, accept the supporting noise pollution prevention and control facilities, prepare an acceptance report, and make it public. If the facilities have not been accepted or have failed the acceptance inspection, the construction project shall not be put into production or use.

**Article 26** The construction of noise-sensitive buildings shall comply with the requirements of relevant standards for sound insulation design of civil buildings. Buildings that do not meet the standard requirements shall not pass acceptance inspection or be delivered for use. When constructing noise-sensitive buildings in areas such as along both sides of traffic arteries or around industrial enterprises, a certain distance shall be maintained in accordance with regulations, and measures to reduce vibration and noise shall be taken.

**Article 27** The State encourages and supports the research, development, and promotion of low-noise processes and equipment, and implements a phase-out system for outdated processes and equipment that cause serious noise pollution.

The department of development and reform under the State Council, together with other relevant departments under the State Council, determines the phase-out period for processes and equipment that cause serious noise pollution and includes them in the national comprehensive industrial policy catalog.

Producers, importers, sellers, or users shall stop producing, importing, selling, or using the equipment listed in the catalog specified in the preceding paragraph within the specified period. Adopters of processes shall stop using the processes listed in the catalog specified in the preceding paragraph within the specified period.

**Article 28** For areas that have not achieved the targets set in the acoustic environment quality improvement plan, as well as areas with prominent noise pollution problems and strong public complaints, the competent department of ecological environment under the people's government at or above the provincial level, together with other departments responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control, shall summon the principal responsible persons of the people's government of that area and its relevant departments for talks, requiring them to take effective measures for timely rectification. The situation of the talks and rectification shall be made public.

**Article 29** The competent departments of ecological environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control have the right to conduct on-site inspections of units or places that emit noise. The inspected party shall truthfully provide information and necessary materials, and shall not refuse or obstruct the inspection. The departments and personnel conducting the inspection shall keep confidential any trade secrets learned during the on-site inspection.

On-site inspections shall be conducted by no fewer than two inspectors, who shall proactively present their law enforcement credentials.

**Article 30** Where the emission of noise causes serious pollution and the entity ordered to make corrections refuses to do so, the competent department of ecological environment or other department responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control may seal up or seize the premises, facilities, equipment, tools, and articles used for emitting noise.

**Article 31** Any unit or individual has the right to report acts causing noise pollution to the competent department of ecological environment or other department responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control.

The competent departments of ecological environment and other departments responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control shall publish reporting telephone numbers, email addresses, etc., to facilitate public reporting.

The department receiving the report shall handle it promptly and keep the relevant information of the reporter confidential. If the reported matter falls within the functions of another department, the receiving department shall promptly transfer it to the relevant department and inform the reporter. If the reporter requests a reply and provides valid contact information, the department handling the reported matter shall provide feedback on the handling results, etc.

**Article 32** The State encourages the creation of quiet areas such as quiet residential communities and quiet train carriages, to jointly maintain a harmonious and peaceful living environment.

**Article 33** During special events such as secondary school entrance examinations and the national college entrance examination, local people's governments or their designated departments may impose time and area restrictions on activities that may generate noise impacts, and shall announce these restrictions to the public in advance.

## Chapter IV: Prevention and Control of Industrial Noise Pollution

**Article 34** For the purposes of this Law, "industrial noise" means sounds generated in industrial production activities that disturb the surrounding living environment.

**Article 35** The site selection of industrial enterprises shall comply with territorial spatial planning and relevant planning requirements. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall optimize the layout of industrial enterprises in accordance with planning requirements to prevent industrial noise pollution.

In concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, the construction of new industrial enterprises that emit noise is prohibited. For the renovation or expansion of existing industrial enterprises, effective measures shall be taken to prevent industrial noise pollution.

**Article 36** Enterprises, institutions, and other business operators that emit industrial noise shall take effective measures to reduce vibration and noise, and shall legally obtain a pollutant discharge permit or fill out a pollutant discharge registration form.

Units subject to pollutant discharge permit management shall not emit industrial noise without a pollutant discharge permit, and shall carry out noise pollution prevention and control in accordance with the requirements of the pollutant discharge permit.

**Article 37** The competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government at or above the city divided into districts level shall, in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council, formulate a list of key units for noise pollution discharge within its administrative region based on factors such as noise emissions and requirements for improving acoustic environment quality, make it public, and update it in a timely manner.

**Article 38** Units subject to pollutant discharge permit management shall, in accordance with regulations, conduct self-monitoring of industrial noise, retain original monitoring records, make the monitoring results public, and be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the monitoring data.

Key units for noise pollution discharge shall, in accordance with State regulations, install, use, and maintain automatic noise monitoring equipment, and connect it to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of ecological environment.

## Chapter V: Prevention and Control of Construction Noise Pollution

**Article 39** For the purposes of this Law, "construction noise" means sounds generated during the construction process that disturb the surrounding living environment.

**Article 40** The construction entity shall, in accordance with regulations, include the cost of noise pollution prevention and control in the project cost, and clearly define the construction unit's responsibility for noise pollution prevention and control in the construction contract.

The construction unit shall, in accordance with regulations, formulate an implementation plan for noise pollution prevention and control, and take effective measures to reduce vibration and noise. The construction entity shall supervise the construction unit in implementing the noise pollution prevention and control implementation plan.

**Article 41** For construction operations in concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, low-noise construction processes and equipment shall be given priority.

The competent department of industry and information technology under the State Council, together with the competent departments of ecological environment, housing and urban-rural development, and market supervision and administration under the State Council, shall publish a guidance list of low-noise construction equipment and update it in a timely manner.

**Article 42** For construction operations in concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, the construction entity shall, in accordance with State regulations, install an automatic noise monitoring system, connect it to the network of the supervision and management department, retain original monitoring records, and be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the monitoring data.

**Article 43** In concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, construction operations that generate noise are prohibited at night, except for emergency repair and rescue operations, and operations that must be carried out continuously due to production process requirements or other special needs.

For operations that must be carried out continuously due to special needs, a certificate shall be obtained from the competent departments of housing and urban-rural development, ecological environment, or the department designated by the local people's government, and the information shall be posted in a conspicuous place at the construction site or announced to nearby residents by other means.

## Chapter VI: Prevention and Control of Transportation Noise Pollution

**Article 44** For the purposes of this Law, "transportation noise" means sounds generated by the operation of transportation vehicles such as motor vehicles, railway locomotives and rolling stock, urban rail transit vehicles, motor vessels, and aircraft that disturb the surrounding living environment.

**Article 45** When formulating and revising territorial spatial plans and related transportation plans, people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall comprehensively consider the impact of highways, urban roads, railways, urban rail transit lines, waterways, ports, and civil airports and their takeoff and landing flight paths on the surrounding acoustic environment.

The route selection and design of new highways and railway lines shall, as far as possible, avoid concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings.

The distance between the site selection of new civil airports and concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings shall meet standard requirements.

**Article 46** When formulating technical specifications for transportation infrastructure projects, requirements for noise pollution prevention and control shall be clearly defined.

For the construction, renovation, or expansion of highways, urban viaducts, railways, and urban rail transit lines that pass through concentrated areas for noise-sensitive buildings, the construction entity shall install sound barriers or take other measures to reduce vibration and noise on key sections that may cause noise pollution, in compliance with relevant technical specifications and standards for transportation infrastructure projects.

If the construction entity violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the department designated by the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to formulate and implement a treatment plan.

**Article 47** The mufflers and horns of motor vehicles shall comply with State regulations. It is prohibited to drive motor vehicles that have been illegally modified, such as by removing or damaging mufflers or adding exhaust pipes, to cause noise pollution through roaring, speeding, or other means.

When using audio equipment in motor vehicles, the volume shall be controlled to prevent noise pollution.

Motor vehicles shall be properly maintained and serviced to ensure good performance and prevent noise pollution.

**Article 48** When operating, transportation vehicles such as motor vehicles, railway locomotives and rolling stock, urban rail transit vehicles, and motor vessels shall use sound devices such as horns in accordance with regulations.

The installation and use of sirens on motor vehicles such as police cars, fire engines, emergency rescue vehicles, and ambulances shall comply with the regulations of the State Council's public security and other departments; sirens shall not be used when not on emergency missions.

**Article 49** The competent department of ecological environment of the local people's government, together with the public security organ, may, based on the needs of acoustic environment protection, designate road sections and time periods where motor vehicles are prohibited from driving and using sound devices such as horns, announce them to the public, and the traffic management department of the public security organ shall set up relevant signs and markings in accordance with the law.

**Article 50** When using loudspeakers for directing operations at stations, railway depots, ports, etc., the volume shall be controlled to mitigate noise pollution.**Article 51** Highway maintenance and management entities and urban road maintenance and repair entities shall strengthen the maintenance and upkeep of highways and urban roads to ensure the normal operation of facilities for reducing vibration and noise.

Urban rail transit operation entities and railway transport enterprises shall strengthen the maintenance and upkeep of urban rail transit lines, urban rail transit vehicles, railway lines, and railway locomotives and rolling stock to ensure the normal operation of facilities for reducing vibration and noise. They shall also conduct monitoring in accordance with national regulations, retain original monitoring records, and be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the monitoring data.

**Article 52** The local people's government at the location of a civil airport shall, based on the scope and degree of impact of civil aircraft noise on the living environment around the airport as determined by environmental impact assessments and monitoring results, designate areas where the construction of noise-sensitive buildings is prohibited and areas where it is restricted, and implement controls.

The construction of noise-sensitive buildings unrelated to aviation is prohibited in the prohibited construction areas.

If the construction of noise-sensitive buildings is indeed necessary in the restricted construction areas, the construction entity shall design the building's sound insulation in accordance with the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design.

**Article 53** Civil aircraft shall comply with the noise requirements specified in the airworthiness standards stipulated by the civil aviation authority of the State Council.

**Article 54** The management entity of a civil airport shall be responsible for managing the noise of aircraft taking off and landing at the airport. It shall, in conjunction with air transport enterprises, general aviation enterprises, air traffic control units, and other entities, adopt measures such as low-noise flight procedures, optimization of takeoff and landing runways, control of flight sorties and time slots, restrictions on high-noise aircraft operations, or sound insulation and noise reduction for surrounding noise-sensitive buildings, to prevent and mitigate civil aircraft noise pollution.

The management entity of a civil airport shall, in accordance with national regulations, monitor civil aircraft noise around the airport, retain original monitoring records, be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of the monitoring data, and periodically submit the monitoring results to the civil aviation and ecological environment authorities.

**Article 55** Where noise emissions from the operation of highways, urban roads, and urban rail transit cause serious pollution, the people's government of a city divided into districts or a county shall organize relevant departments and other relevant entities to investigate, assess, and determine responsibility for the noise pollution situation, and formulate a comprehensive noise pollution control plan.

The entity responsible for the noise pollution shall take management or engineering measures as required by the comprehensive noise pollution control plan to mitigate the noise pollution.

**Article 56** Where noise emissions from railway operations cause serious pollution, the railway transport enterprise and the people's government of the city divided into districts or county shall investigate the noise pollution situation and formulate a comprehensive noise pollution control plan.

The railway transport enterprise, relevant departments of the people's government of the city divided into districts or county, and other relevant entities shall take effective measures as required by the comprehensive noise pollution control plan to mitigate the noise pollution.

**Article 57** Where noise emissions from the takeoff and landing of civil aircraft cause serious pollution, the local people's government at the location of the civil airport shall organize relevant departments and other relevant entities to investigate the noise pollution situation, comprehensively consider economic, technical, and management measures, and formulate a comprehensive noise pollution control plan.

The management entity of the civil airport, local people's governments at all levels, and other relevant entities shall take effective measures as required by the comprehensive noise pollution control plan to mitigate the noise pollution.

**Article 58** When formulating a comprehensive noise pollution control plan, opinions from relevant experts and the public shall be solicited.

## Chapter VII: Prevention and Control of Social Life Noise Pollution

**Article 59** For the purposes of this Law, "social life noise" refers to sounds generated by human activities that interfere with the surrounding living environment, excluding industrial noise, construction noise, and transportation noise.

**Article 60** The whole society shall enhance awareness of noise pollution prevention and control, consciously reduce social life noise emissions, actively carry out noise pollution prevention and control activities, foster a positive atmosphere where everyone is responsible, everyone participates, and everyone benefits, and jointly maintain a harmonious and peaceful living environment.

**Article 61** Operators and managers of venues such as cultural entertainment, sports, and catering shall take effective measures to prevent and mitigate noise pollution.

**Article 62** Enterprises, institutions, and other operators and managers that use equipment and facilities such as air conditioners, cooling towers, water pumps, oil fume purifiers, fans, generators, transformers, boilers, and loading and unloading equipment that may generate social life noise pollution shall take measures such as optimizing layout and centralized discharge to prevent and mitigate noise pollution.

**Article 63** The use of loudspeakers or other methods of continuously and repeatedly emitting high noise for advertising purposes in commercial business activities is prohibited.

For other noise generated in commercial business activities, operators shall take effective measures to prevent noise pollution.

**Article 64** The use of loudspeakers in areas concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings is prohibited, except in emergencies and special circumstances stipulated by the local people's government.

When organizing or engaging in recreational, fitness, or other activities in public places such as streets, squares, and parks, the regulations of the public place manager regarding activity areas, time periods, and volume shall be observed, and effective measures shall be taken to prevent noise pollution; the use of audio equipment that produces excessive volume in violation of regulations is prohibited.

Managers of public places shall reasonably stipulate the areas, time periods, and volume for recreational, fitness, and other activities, and may take measures such as installing noise automatic monitoring and display facilities to strengthen management.

**Article 65** Families and their members shall cultivate good habits of reducing noise generation, try to avoid causing disturbance to surrounding people through activities such as using public transportation, keeping pets, and other daily activities, resolve noise disputes through mutual understanding and compromise, and jointly maintain the quality of the acoustic environment.

When using household appliances, musical instruments, or engaging in other activities within family premises, the volume shall be controlled, or other effective measures shall be taken to prevent noise pollution.

**Article 66** For interior decoration activities in residential buildings, shops, office buildings, and other buildings that have been completed and delivered for use, the working hours shall be limited in accordance with regulations, and effective measures shall be taken to prevent and mitigate noise pollution.

**Article 67** Real estate development operators of newly built residential housing shall disclose in the sales venue the potential noise impact on the housing and the prevention measures taken or to be taken, and incorporate this into the sales contract.

Real estate development operators of newly built residential housing shall specify in the sales contract the location of shared facilities and equipment and the building's sound insulation conditions.

**Article 68** When installing shared facilities and equipment such as elevators, water pumps, and transformers in residential areas, the construction entity shall set them up reasonably, take measures to reduce vibration and noise, and comply with the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design.

For shared facilities and equipment such as elevators, water pumps, and transformers in existing residential areas that are already in use, professional operating entities shall be responsible for their maintenance and management, ensuring compliance with the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design.

**Article 69** Grassroots mass autonomous organizations shall guide owners' committees, property service providers, and owners to stipulate the requirements for noise pollution prevention and control within their property management area through the formulation of management covenants or other forms, which shall be jointly observed by the owners.

**Article 70** For acts of social life noise disturbance in areas concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings, grassroots mass autonomous organizations, owners' committees, and property service providers shall promptly advise and mediate; if advice and mediation are ineffective, they may report or complain to the department responsible for the supervision and management of social life noise pollution prevention and control or the department designated by the local people's government. The department receiving the report or complaint shall handle it according to law.

## Chapter VIII: Legal Liability

**Article 71** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, refuses or obstructs supervision and inspection, or practices fraud during supervision and inspection, shall be ordered by the ecological environment authority or other department responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 72** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, produces, imports, or sells products exceeding noise limit values shall be ordered by the market supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the county level or the customs, according to their respective functions, to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure.

Anyone who, in violation of this Law, produces, imports, sells, or uses eliminated equipment, or adopts eliminated processes, shall be ordered by the department designated by the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than three times the value of the goods; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure.

**Article 73** If a construction entity, in violation of this Law, constructs noise-sensitive buildings that do not meet the requirements of the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design, it shall be ordered by the housing and urban-rural construction authority of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 2% but not more than 4% of the construction project contract price.

If a construction entity, in violation of this Law, constructs a noise-sensitive building unrelated to aviation in an area where the construction of noise-sensitive buildings is prohibited, it shall be ordered by the department designated by the local people's government to cease the illegal act, fined not less than 2% but not more than 10% of the construction project contract price, and the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order demolition.

**Article 74** If an industrial enterprise that emits noise is newly built in an area concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings, in violation of this Law, the ecological environment authority shall order the cessation of the illegal act, impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and report the matter to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order closure.

If an industrial enterprise is renovated or expanded in an area concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings, in violation of this Law, without taking effective measures to prevent industrial noise pollution, the ecological environment authority shall order corrections and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order closure.

**Article 75** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, emits industrial noise without a pollutant discharge permit or exceeding noise emission standards shall be ordered by the ecological environment authority to make corrections, or to restrict production or suspend production for remediation, and shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business or closure.

**Article 76** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the ecological environment authority to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, an order to restrict production or suspend production for remediation shall be issued:

(1) A unit subject to pollutant discharge permit management fails to conduct self-monitoring of industrial noise in accordance with regulations, fails to retain original monitoring records, or fails to disclose monitoring results to the public;

(2) A key noise pollutant discharging unit fails to install, use, or maintain noise automatic monitoring equipment in accordance with national regulations, or fails to connect it to the monitoring equipment of the ecological environment authority.

**Article 77** If a construction entity or construction contractor, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts, it shall be ordered by the department designated by the people's government at the project location to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, construction may be ordered to be suspended:

(1) Emitting construction noise exceeding noise emission standards;

(2) Carrying out construction operations that generate noise at night in an area concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings without obtaining a certificate in accordance with regulations.

**Article 78** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the department designated by the people's government at the project location to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) The construction entity fails to include noise pollution prevention and control costs in the project cost in accordance with regulations;

(2) The construction contractor fails to formulate an implementation plan for noise pollution prevention and control in accordance with regulations, or fails to take effective measures to reduce vibration and noise;

(3) The construction entity carrying out construction operations in an area concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings fails to install a noise automatic monitoring system in accordance with national regulations, fails to connect it to the supervisory authority, or fails to retain original monitoring records;

(4) The construction entity, when continuous construction operations are necessary due to special needs, fails to notify nearby residents in accordance with regulations.

**Article 79** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, drives a motor vehicle that has been illegally modified by removing or damaging a muffler, adding an exhaust pipe, etc., causing it to roar or speed, fails to use sound devices in accordance with regulations when the motor vehicle is running, or violates regulations on road sections and time periods prohibiting motor vehicle driving and the use of sound devices, shall be punished by the traffic management department of the public security organ of the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with relevant road traffic safety laws and regulations.

If railway locomotives and rolling stock, urban rail transit vehicles, motor vessels, or other means of transportation fail to use sound devices in accordance with regulations when running, in violation of this Law, the transportation, railway supervision and management, maritime, or other departments, or the department designated by the local people's government for urban rail transit, shall, according to their respective functions, order corrections and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan.

**Article 80** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the transportation, railway supervision and management, civil aviation, or other departments, or the department designated by the local people's government for urban roads or urban rail transit, according to their respective functions, to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) The highway maintenance and management entity, urban road maintenance and repair entity, urban rail transit operation entity, or railway transport enterprise fails to fulfill maintenance and upkeep obligations, or fails to ensure the normal operation of facilities for reducing vibration and noise;

(2) The urban rail transit operation entity or railway transport enterprise fails to conduct monitoring in accordance with national regulations, or fails to retain original monitoring records;

(3) The management entity of a civil airport, air transport enterprise, or general aviation enterprise fails to take measures to prevent or mitigate civil aircraft noise pollution;

(4) The management entity of a civil airport fails to monitor civil aircraft noise around the airport in accordance with national regulations, fails to retain original monitoring records, or fails to periodically submit monitoring results.

**Article 81** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the department designated by the local people's government to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the matter may be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order the suspension of business:

(1) Emitting social life noise exceeding noise emission standards;

(2) Using loudspeakers or other methods of continuously and repeatedly emitting high noise for advertising purposes in commercial business activities;

(3) Failing to take effective measures for other noise generated in commercial business activities, causing noise pollution.

**Article 82** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be persuaded and educated by the department designated by the local people's government and ordered to make corrections; if corrections are refused, a warning shall be given, and an individual may be fined not less than 200 yuan but not more than 1,000 yuan, and a unit may be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Using loudspeakers in an area concentrated with noise-sensitive buildings;

(2) Organizing or engaging in recreational, fitness, or other activities in public places without observing the regulations of the public place manager regarding activity areas, time periods, and volume, failing to take effective measures causing noise pollution, or using audio equipment in violation of regulations producing excessive volume;

(3) Carrying out interior decoration activities in buildings that have been completed and delivered for use without limiting the working hours in accordance with regulations, or failing to take effective measures causing noise pollution;

(4) Other acts in violation of legal provisions causing social life noise pollution.

**Article 83** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the real estate management department of the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, sales shall be ordered to be suspended:

(1) The real estate development operator of newly built residential housing fails to disclose in the sales venue the potential noise impact on the housing and the prevention measures taken or to be taken, or fails to incorporate this into the sales contract;

(2) The real estate development operator of newly built residential housing fails to specify in the sales contract the location of shared facilities and equipment or the building's sound insulation conditions.

**Article 84** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the department designated by the local people's government to make corrections and shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) The installation of shared facilities and equipment in a residential area is unreasonable or fails to take measures to reduce vibration and noise, not meeting the requirements of the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design;

(2) The professional operating entity fails to maintain and manage the shared facilities and equipment in an existing residential area already in use, not meeting the requirements of the relevant standards for civil building sound insulation design.

**Article 85** If a supervisor or manager of noise pollution prevention and control abuses their power, neglects their duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be given disciplinary action by the supervisory authority or the appointing authority or unit according to law.

**Article 86** Units and individuals suffering from noise infringement have the right to demand that the infringer bear civil liability according to law.

Disputes over liability for compensation and the amount of compensation may, upon the request of the parties, be mediated by the department responsible for the supervision and management of noise pollution prevention and control or the people's mediation committee.

The State encourages units and individuals that emit noise and managers of public places to amicably negotiate with units and individuals suffering from noise infringement to properly resolve noise disputes by adjusting production and operation hours or construction work hours, taking measures to reduce vibration and noise, paying compensation, relocating, or other means.

**Article 87** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, generates social life noise, and after being advised, mediated, and dealt with, fails to stop, continuously interferes with the normal life, work, and study of others, or commits other acts of disturbing public order or hindering social management that constitute a violation of public security administration, shall be given a public security administration penalty by the public security organ according to law.

Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits a crime shall be investigated for criminal responsibility according to law.

## Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 88** The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Noise emission" refers to the radiation of noise from a noise source into the surrounding living environment;

(2) "Nighttime" refers to the period between 10:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. the following morning. The people's government at or above the level of a city divided into districts may separately stipulate the start and end times of nighttime within its administrative area, provided that the nighttime period is eight hours long;

(3) "Noise-sensitive buildings" refer to buildings used for residential purposes, scientific research, medical and health care, culture and education, government and organizational offices, social welfare, and other purposes that require maintaining quiet;

(4) "Traffic arteries" refer to railways, expressways, Class 1 highways, Class 2 highways, urban expressways, urban main roads, urban secondary roads, urban rail transit lines, and inland high-grade waterways.
**Article 89** Provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, or cities divided into districts or autonomous prefectures shall, based on actual conditions, formulate specific measures for the prevention and control of noise pollution within their respective localities.

**Article 90** This Law shall come into effect on June 5, 2022. The "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Control of Environmental Noise Pollution" shall be repealed simultaneously.
