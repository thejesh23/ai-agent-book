# Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on May 11, 1984

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

Revised at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on February 28, 2008

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 27, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting and improving the environment, preventing and controlling water pollution, protecting water ecology, ensuring the safety of drinking water, safeguarding public health, promoting ecological civilization, and facilitating sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law applies to the prevention and control of pollution in surface water bodies and groundwater bodies within the territory of the People's Republic of China, including rivers, lakes, canals, channels, and reservoirs.

The prevention and control of marine pollution shall be governed by the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The prevention and control of water pollution shall adhere to the principles of prioritizing prevention, combining prevention with control, and implementing comprehensive management. Priority shall be given to protecting drinking water sources, strictly controlling industrial pollution and urban domestic pollution, preventing and controlling agricultural non-point source pollution, actively promoting the construction of ecological treatment projects, and preventing, controlling, and reducing water environmental pollution and ecological damage.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate water environmental protection work into their national economic and social development plans.

Local people's governments at all levels shall be responsible for the water environmental quality within their respective administrative regions and shall take timely measures to prevent and control water pollution.

Article 5 A river chief system shall be established at the provincial, city, county, and township levels, with graded and segmented organization and leadership for the protection of water resources, management of water shorelines, prevention and control of water pollution, and water environment governance in rivers and lakes within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The State implements a target responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for water environmental protection, incorporating the achievement of water environmental protection targets into the assessment and evaluation of local people's governments and their responsible persons.

Article 7 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research on water pollution prevention and control, as well as the promotion and application of advanced and applicable technologies, and strengthens publicity and education on water environmental protection.

Article 8 The State establishes and improves an ecological protection compensation mechanism for water environments in drinking water source protection areas and upstream areas of rivers, lakes, and reservoirs through means such as fiscal transfer payments.

Article 9 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over the prevention and control of water pollution.

The maritime administration agency of the competent department of transportation shall exercise supervision and management over the prevention and control of pollution of waters by ships.

The competent departments of water administration, land and resources, health, construction, agriculture, and fisheries under people's governments at or above the county level, as well as the basin water resources protection agencies for important rivers and lakes, shall exercise supervision and management over relevant water pollution prevention and control within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 10 The discharge of water pollutants shall not exceed the national or local water pollutant discharge standards and the total discharge control indicators for key water pollutants.

Article 11 All entities and individuals have the duty to protect the water environment and have the right to report acts that pollute or damage the water environment.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant competent departments shall commend and reward entities and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in water pollution prevention and control.

## Chapter II Standards and Planning for Water Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 12 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall formulate national water environmental quality standards.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate local standards for items not specified in the national water environmental quality standards and shall report them to the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council for recordation.

Article 13 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council, together with the competent department of water administration under the State Council and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, may, based on the use functions of water bodies in the basins of important rivers and lakes designated by the State and the economic and technological conditions of the relevant regions, determine the water environmental quality standards applicable to the water bodies at provincial boundaries in the basins of such important rivers and lakes, and shall report them to the State Council for approval before implementation.

Article 14 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall, based on the national water environmental quality standards and the national economic and technological conditions, formulate national water pollutant discharge standards.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate local water pollutant discharge standards for items not specified in the national water pollutant discharge standards; for items already specified in the national water pollutant discharge standards, they may formulate local water pollutant discharge standards that are stricter than the national standards. Local water pollutant discharge standards must be reported to the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council for recordation.

Where pollutants are discharged into water bodies for which local water pollutant discharge standards exist, the local standards shall be complied with.

Article 15 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the requirements of water pollution prevention and control and the national or local economic and technological conditions, timely revise the water environmental quality standards and water pollutant discharge standards.

Article 16 The prevention and control of water pollution shall be planned uniformly by basin or by region. The basin water pollution prevention and control plan for important rivers and lakes designated by the State shall be formulated by the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council, together with the competent department for comprehensive economic macro-control, the competent department of water administration, and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

For rivers and lakes other than those specified in the preceding paragraph that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the basin water pollution prevention and control plan shall be formulated by the competent departments of environmental protection of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, together with the competent departments of water administration at the same level and the relevant people's governments of cities and counties, based on the basin water pollution prevention and control plan for important rivers and lakes designated by the State and the local actual conditions. The plan shall be reviewed by the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and submitted to the State Council for approval.

For rivers and lakes that cross counties within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the basin water pollution prevention and control plan shall be formulated by the competent department of environmental protection of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, together with the competent departments of water administration at the same level, based on the basin water pollution prevention and control plan for important rivers and lakes designated by the State and the local actual conditions. The plan shall be submitted to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval and reported to the State Council for recordation.

The approved water pollution prevention and control plan shall be the fundamental basis for preventing and controlling water pollution, and any revision of the plan must be approved by the original approving authority.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the legally approved basin water pollution prevention and control plans for rivers and lakes, organize the formulation of water pollution prevention and control plans for their respective administrative regions.

Article 17 The relevant people's governments of cities and counties shall, in accordance with the requirements for improving water environmental quality determined in the water pollution prevention and control plan, formulate plans for achieving compliance within a specified period and take measures to meet the standards on schedule.

The relevant people's governments of cities and counties shall report their compliance plans to the people's government at the next higher level for recordation and make them public.

Article 18 When reporting on the environmental situation and the achievement of environmental protection targets to the people's congress or its standing committee at the corresponding level each year, the people's governments of cities and counties shall report on the implementation of the compliance plans for water environmental quality within the specified period and make the information public.

## Chapter III Supervision and Management of Water Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 19 For construction projects and other water facilities that directly or indirectly discharge pollutants into water bodies, whether newly built, renovated, or expanded, an environmental impact assessment shall be conducted in accordance with the law.

Where a construction entity intends to newly build, renovate, or expand a sewage outlet in a river or lake, it shall obtain the consent of the competent department of water administration or the basin management agency; if navigation or fishery waters are involved, the competent department of environmental protection shall, when approving the environmental impact assessment document, solicit the opinions of the competent departments of transportation and fisheries.

Water pollution prevention and control facilities for a construction project shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project. Such facilities shall comply with the requirements of the approved or recorded environmental impact assessment document.

Article 20 The State implements a total discharge control system for key water pollutants.

The total discharge control indicators for key water pollutants shall be determined by the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council, after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall be submitted together with the competent department for comprehensive economic macro-control under the State Council to the State Council for approval and issuance for implementation.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall reduce and control the total discharge of key water pollutants within their respective administrative regions in accordance with the regulations of the State Council. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the water environmental quality status of their administrative regions and the needs of water pollution prevention and control, implement total discharge control for water pollutants other than the national key water pollutants.

For regions that exceed the total discharge control indicators for key water pollutants or fail to achieve the targets for improving water environmental quality, the competent departments of environmental protection under the people's governments at or above the provincial level shall, together with the relevant departments, interview the principal responsible persons of the local people's governments in those regions and suspend the approval of environmental impact assessment documents for construction projects that would increase the total discharge of key water pollutants. The interview situation shall be made public.

Article 21 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that directly or indirectly discharge industrial wastewater, medical sewage, and other wastewater or sewage that, according to regulations, requires a pollutant discharge permit before discharge, shall obtain a pollutant discharge permit; the operating units of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities shall also obtain a pollutant discharge permit. The pollutant discharge permit shall specify the types, concentrations, total amounts, and discharge destinations of the water pollutants to be discharged. Specific measures for pollutant discharge permits shall be formulated by the State Council.

Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators are prohibited from discharging the wastewater or sewage specified in the preceding paragraph into water bodies without a pollutant discharge permit or in violation of the provisions of the permit.

Article 22 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that discharge pollutants into water bodies shall set up sewage outlets in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council; where sewage outlets are set up in rivers or lakes, the provisions of the competent department of water administration under the State Council shall also be complied with.

Article 23 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators subject to pollutant discharge permit management shall, in accordance with the relevant national regulations and monitoring standards, conduct self-monitoring of the water pollutants they discharge and retain the original monitoring records. Key pollutant-discharging units shall also install automatic monitoring equipment for water pollutant discharge, connect it to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of environmental protection, and ensure the normal operation of the monitoring equipment. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council.

The list of key pollutant-discharging units that shall install automatic monitoring equipment for water pollutant discharge shall be determined by the competent departments of environmental protection under the local people's governments at or above the city with districts, in consultation with the relevant departments at the same level, based on factors such as the environmental capacity of the administrative region, the requirements of the total discharge control indicators for key water pollutants, and the types, quantities, and concentrations of water pollutants discharged by the pollutant-discharging units.

Article 24 Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators subject to pollutant discharge permit management shall be responsible for the authenticity and accuracy of monitoring data.

Where the competent department of environmental protection discovers abnormal data transmitted by the automatic monitoring equipment for water pollutant discharge of a key pollutant-discharging unit, it shall promptly conduct an investigation.

Article 25 The State establishes a water environmental quality monitoring system and a water pollutant discharge monitoring system. The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall be responsible for formulating water environment monitoring standards, uniformly releasing national water environment status information, organizing a monitoring network together with the competent department of water administration under the State Council, uniformly planning the layout of national water environmental quality monitoring stations (points), establishing a mechanism for sharing monitoring data, and strengthening the management of water environment monitoring.

Article 26 The water resources protection agency for the basin of an important river or lake designated by the State shall be responsible for monitoring the water environmental quality status of the water bodies at provincial boundaries within its basin and shall promptly report the monitoring results to the competent department of environmental protection under the State Council and the competent department of water administration under the State Council; if a basin water resources protection leadership agency has been established with the approval of the State Council, the monitoring results shall be promptly reported to that leadership agency.

Article 27 When developing, utilizing, regulating, and dispatching water resources, the relevant departments under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall take into account all relevant factors, maintain reasonable flow rates in rivers and reasonable water levels in lakes, reservoirs, and groundwater bodies, ensure basic ecological water use, and preserve the ecological functions of water bodies.

Article 28 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of water administration under the State Council and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, establish a joint coordination mechanism for water environmental protection in the basins of important rivers and lakes, implementing unified planning, unified standards, unified monitoring, and unified prevention and control measures.

Article 29 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council and the competent departments of environmental protection under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, together with the relevant departments at the same level, clarify the requirements for basin ecological environmental protection based on the needs of the basin's ecological environment functions, organize the monitoring and evaluation of the carrying capacity of the basin's environmental resources, and implement early warning for the carrying capacity of the basin's environmental resources.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of the basin's ecological environment functions, organize the protection and restoration of rivers, lakes, and wetlands, construct artificial wetlands, water conservation forests, and vegetation buffer zones and isolation belts along rivers and lakes according to local conditions, and carry out other ecological environment treatment and protection projects, remediate black and odorous water bodies, and enhance the carrying capacity of the basin's environmental resources.

Entities engaged in development and construction activities shall take effective measures to maintain the ecological environment functions of the basin and strictly adhere to the ecological protection red line.

Article 30 The competent departments of environmental protection and other departments exercising supervisory and management powers in accordance with this Law shall have the authority to conduct on-site inspections of pollutant-discharging units within their jurisdiction. The units being inspected shall truthfully report the situation and provide necessary information. The inspecting authorities have the obligation to keep confidential any trade secrets obtained during the inspection.

Article 31 Disputes over water pollution across administrative regions shall be resolved through consultation by the relevant local people's governments, or through coordination by their common higher-level people's government.

## Chapter IV Measures for Water Pollution Prevention and Control

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 32 The competent department of environmental protection under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of health under the State Council, publish a list of toxic and hazardous water pollutants based on the degree of harm and impact on public health and the ecological environment, and implement risk management.

Enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators that discharge the toxic and hazardous water pollutants listed in the preceding paragraph shall monitor the sewage outlets and the surrounding environment, assess environmental risks, investigate potential environmental safety hazards, disclose information on toxic and hazardous water pollutants, and take effective measures to prevent environmental risks.

Article 33 It is prohibited to discharge oils, acid liquids, alkali liquids, or highly toxic waste liquids into water bodies.

It is prohibited to wash in water bodies vehicles and containers that have been used for storing oils or toxic pollutants.

Article 34 It is prohibited to discharge or dump radioactive solid waste or wastewater containing high-level or medium-level radioactive substances into water bodies.

The discharge of wastewater containing low-level radioactive substances into water bodies shall comply with the relevant national regulations and standards on radioactive pollution prevention and control.

Article 35 Where wastewater containing heat is discharged into water bodies, measures shall be taken to ensure that the water temperature of the water body meets the water environmental quality standards.

Article 36 Sewage containing pathogens shall be disinfected before discharge; it may only be discharged after meeting the relevant national standards.

Article 37 It is prohibited to discharge or dump industrial solid waste, urban garbage, and other waste into water bodies.

It is prohibited to discharge, dump, or directly bury into the ground soluble highly toxic waste residues containing substances such as mercury, cadmium, arsenic, chromium, lead, cyanide, or yellow phosphorus.

Sites for storing soluble highly toxic waste residues shall take measures for waterproofing, anti-leakage, and anti-loss.

Article 38 It is prohibited to pile up or store solid waste and other pollutants on the beaches and banks below the highest water level line of rivers, lakes, canals, channels, and reservoirs.

Article 39 It is prohibited to discharge water pollutants through means such as using seepage wells, seepage pits, fissures, karst caves, or secretly installed pipes, tampering with or falsifying monitoring data, or operating water pollution prevention and control facilities abnormally to evade supervision.

Article 40 Operators and management units of chemical production enterprises, industrial agglomeration areas, mining areas, tailings ponds, hazardous waste disposal sites, and landfills shall take measures such as anti-leakage measures and construct groundwater quality monitoring wells for monitoring to prevent groundwater pollution.

Underground oil tanks at gas stations, etc., shall use double-layer tanks or take other effective measures such as constructing anti-leakage pools, and conduct anti-leakage monitoring to prevent groundwater pollution.

It is prohibited to use ditches, pits, or ponds without anti-leakage measures to transport or store wastewater containing toxic pollutants, sewage containing pathogens, and other waste.

Article 41 Where the water quality of aquifers in multi-layer groundwater differs significantly, they shall be exploited in separate layers; for already polluted phreatic water and confined water, mixed exploitation is prohibited.

Article 42 When constructing underground engineering facilities or engaging in activities such as underground exploration or mining, protective measures shall be taken to prevent groundwater pollution.

Abandoned mines, wells, or water intake wells, etc., shall be sealed or backfilled.

Article 43 Artificial recharge to replenish groundwater shall not deteriorate the quality of the groundwater.

### Section 2 Industrial Water Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 44 The relevant departments under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall rationally plan the industrial layout, require enterprises that cause water pollution to carry out technological transformation, adopt comprehensive prevention and control measures, increase the rate of water reuse, and reduce the discharge of wastewater and pollutants.

Article 45 Enterprises that discharge industrial wastewater shall take effective measures to collect and treat all the wastewater generated, preventing environmental pollution. Industrial wastewater containing toxic and hazardous water pollutants shall be collected and treated separately and shall not be diluted before discharge.

Industrial agglomeration areas shall be equipped with corresponding centralized wastewater treatment facilities, install automatic monitoring equipment, connect it to the monitoring equipment of the competent department of environmental protection, and ensure the normal operation of the monitoring equipment.

Where industrial wastewater is discharged into centralized wastewater treatment facilities, it shall be pre-treated in accordance with the relevant national regulations to meet the treatment process requirements of the centralized treatment facilities before discharge.

Article 46 The State implements a phase-out system for outdated processes and equipment that seriously pollute the water environment.

The competent department for comprehensive economic macro-control under the State Council, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, shall publish a list of processes that seriously pollute the water environment and are prohibited from being used within a specified period, and a list of equipment that seriously pollutes the water environment and are prohibited from being produced, sold, imported, or used within a specified period.

Producers, sellers, importers, or users shall stop producing, selling, importing, or using the equipment listed in the equipment list specified in the preceding paragraph within the specified period. Users of the processes shall stop using the processes listed in the process list specified in the preceding paragraph within the specified period.

Equipment phased out in accordance with paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Article shall not be transferred to others for use.### Section 2: Prohibition of Certain Production Projects

**Article 47** The state prohibits the construction of small-scale papermaking, tanning, printing and dyeing, dye, coking, sulfur refining, arsenic refining, mercury refining, oil refining, electroplating, pesticide, asbestos, cement, glass, steel, thermal power, and other production projects that do not comply with national industrial policies and seriously pollute the water environment.

**Article 48** Enterprises shall adopt clean production processes with high raw material utilization efficiency and low pollutant emissions, and strengthen management to reduce the generation of water pollutants.

### Section 3: Prevention and Control of Urban Water Pollution

**Article 49** Urban sewage shall be treated in a centralized manner.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall raise funds through fiscal budgets and other channels, and make overall plans for the construction of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities and supporting pipeline networks, so as to increase the collection rate and treatment rate of urban sewage within their administrative regions.

The competent construction department of the State Council shall, in conjunction with the comprehensive economic macro-control and environmental protection departments of the State Council, organize the formulation of a national plan for the construction of urban sewage treatment facilities based on urban and rural planning and water pollution prevention and control plans. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize the construction, comprehensive economic macro-control, environmental protection, water administration, and other departments to formulate plans for the construction of urban sewage treatment facilities within their administrative regions. The competent construction departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the plan for the construction of urban sewage treatment facilities, organize the construction of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities and supporting pipeline networks, and strengthen the supervision and management of the operation of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities.

The operating units of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities shall, in accordance with state regulations, provide paid sewage treatment services to polluters, collect sewage treatment fees, and ensure the normal operation of the centralized sewage treatment facilities. The collected sewage treatment fees shall be used for the construction and operation of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities and the treatment and disposal of sludge, and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

The specific measures for the charging, management, and use of sewage treatment fees for centralized urban sewage treatment facilities shall be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 50** The discharge of water pollutants into centralized urban sewage treatment facilities shall comply with the national or local water pollutant discharge standards.

The operating units of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities shall be responsible for the quality of the effluent from the centralized urban sewage treatment facilities.

The environmental protection department shall supervise and inspect the quality and quantity of the effluent from centralized urban sewage treatment facilities.

**Article 51** The operating units of centralized urban sewage treatment facilities or sludge treatment and disposal units shall safely treat and dispose of sludge, ensure that the treated and disposed sludge meets national standards, and keep records of the destination of the sludge.

### Section 4: Prevention and Control of Agricultural and Rural Water Pollution

**Article 52** The state supports the construction of rural sewage and garbage treatment facilities, and promotes the centralized treatment of rural sewage and garbage.

Local people's governments at all levels shall make overall plans for the construction of rural sewage and garbage treatment facilities and ensure their normal operation.

**Article 53** The formulation of quality standards and use standards for fertilizers, pesticides, and other products shall meet the requirements of water environmental protection.

**Article 54** The use of pesticides shall comply with the relevant national regulations and standards for the safe use of pesticides.

The transportation, storage of pesticides, and the disposal of expired and invalid pesticides shall be managed in a strengthened manner to prevent water pollution.

**Article 55** The competent agricultural departments and other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to guide agricultural producers in the scientific and rational application of fertilizers and pesticides, promote soil testing and formulated fertilization technology and highly effective, low-toxicity, and low-residue pesticides, control the excessive use of fertilizers and pesticides, and prevent water pollution.

**Article 56** The state supports livestock and poultry farms and farming communities in constructing facilities for the comprehensive utilization or harmless treatment of livestock and poultry manure and wastewater.

Livestock and poultry farms and farming communities shall ensure the normal operation of facilities for the comprehensive utilization or harmless treatment of livestock and poultry manure and wastewater, ensure that wastewater is discharged up to standard, and prevent pollution of the water environment.

The people's governments at the county and township levels where densely populated areas of free-range livestock and poultry are located shall organize the household collection and centralized treatment and utilization of livestock and poultry manure and wastewater.

**Article 57** Those engaged in aquaculture shall protect the aquatic ecological environment, scientifically determine stocking density, rationally apply feed and use drugs, and prevent pollution of the water environment.

**Article 58** Water used for farmland irrigation shall meet the corresponding water quality standards to prevent pollution of soil, groundwater, and agricultural products.

The discharge of industrial wastewater or medical sewage into farmland irrigation channels is prohibited. Where urban sewage and livestock and poultry breeding wastewater or agricultural product processing wastewater that has not been comprehensively utilized are discharged into farmland irrigation channels, it shall be ensured that the water quality at the nearest downstream irrigation water intake point meets the water quality standards for farmland irrigation.

### Section 5: Prevention and Control of Water Pollution from Ships

**Article 59** The discharge of oily wastewater and domestic sewage from ships shall comply with the ship pollutant discharge standards. Ships engaged in marine navigation entering inland rivers and ports shall comply with the ship pollutant discharge standards for inland rivers.

Residual oil and waste oil from ships shall be recovered and are prohibited from being discharged into water bodies.

The dumping of ship garbage into water bodies is prohibited.

Ships loading and transporting oil or toxic cargo shall take measures to prevent overflow and leakage, and prevent cargo from falling into the water and causing water pollution.

Ships on international voyages entering the inland waters of the People's Republic of China that discharge ballast water shall use ballast water treatment devices or take other equivalent measures to inactivate the ballast water. The discharge of ship ballast water that does not meet the regulations is prohibited.

**Article 60** Ships shall be equipped with corresponding anti-pollution equipment and devices in accordance with relevant state regulations, and hold legally valid certificates and documents for the prevention of water environment pollution.

Ships conducting operations involving pollutant discharge shall strictly abide by the operating procedures and truthfully record them in the corresponding logbooks.

**Article 61** The people's governments at the city and county levels where ports, wharves, loading and unloading stations, and ship repair yards are located shall make overall plans for the construction of facilities for receiving, transferring, and treating ship pollutants and waste.

Ports, wharves, loading and unloading stations, and ship repair yards shall be equipped with sufficient facilities for receiving ship pollutants and waste. Units engaged in the reception of ship pollutants and waste, or in the cleaning of ship tanks loaded with oil or polluting hazardous cargo, shall have reception and treatment capabilities commensurate with their operational scale.

**Article 62** Ships and relevant operating units engaged in operational activities with pollution risks shall take effective measures in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and standards to prevent water pollution. The maritime administration agency and the competent fishery department shall strengthen the supervision and management of ships and relevant operational activities.

Ships conducting lightering operations of bulk liquid polluting hazardous cargo shall prepare an operation plan, take effective safety and pollution prevention measures, and report to the maritime administration agency at the place of operation for approval.

The practice of beaching ships for dismantling operations is prohibited.

## Chapter V: Protection of Drinking Water Sources and Other Special Water Bodies

**Article 63** The state establishes a drinking water source protection zone system. Drinking water source protection zones are divided into primary protection zones and secondary protection zones; when necessary, a certain area outside the drinking water source protection zone may be designated as a quasi-protection zone.

The delineation of drinking water source protection zones shall be proposed by the relevant city or county people's government and reported to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval; for drinking water source protection zones spanning multiple cities or counties, the relevant city or county people's governments shall negotiate and propose a delineation plan, which shall be reported to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval; if negotiation fails, the environmental protection department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, in conjunction with the water administration, land and resources, health, construction, and other departments at the same level, propose a delineation plan, and after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments at the same level, report it to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval.

For drinking water source protection zones spanning provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the relevant people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall consult with the relevant river basin management agency for delineation; if negotiation fails, the environmental protection department of the State Council shall, in conjunction with the water administration, land and resources, health, construction, and other departments at the same level, propose a delineation plan, and after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments of the State Council, report it to the State Council for approval.

The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the actual needs of protecting drinking water sources, adjust the scope of drinking water source protection zones to ensure the safety of drinking water. The relevant local people's government shall set up clear geographical markers and obvious warning signs at the boundaries of the drinking water source protection zone.

**Article 64** Within drinking water source protection zones, the setting up of sewage outlets is prohibited.

**Article 65** Within the primary protection zone of a drinking water source, the construction, renovation, or expansion of construction projects unrelated to water supply facilities and source protection is prohibited; existing construction projects unrelated to water supply facilities and source protection shall be ordered by the people's government at or above the county level to be demolished or closed.

Activities such as cage culture, tourism, swimming, angling, or other activities that may pollute the drinking water body are prohibited within the primary protection zone of a drinking water source.

**Article 66** Within the secondary protection zone of a drinking water source, the construction, renovation, or expansion of construction projects that discharge pollutants is prohibited; existing construction projects that discharge pollutants shall be ordered by the people's government at or above the county level to be demolished or closed.

Activities such as cage culture and tourism conducted within the secondary protection zone of a drinking water source shall take measures in accordance with regulations to prevent pollution of the drinking water body.

**Article 67** Within the quasi-protection zone of a drinking water source, the construction or expansion of construction projects that cause serious pollution to the water body is prohibited; the renovation of construction projects shall not increase the amount of pollutant discharge.

**Article 68** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the actual needs of protecting drinking water sources, take engineering measures or construct ecological protection measures such as wetlands and water conservation forests within the quasi-protection zone to prevent water pollutants from being directly discharged into the drinking water body, ensuring the safety of drinking water.

**Article 69** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize environmental protection and other departments to investigate and assess the environmental conditions and pollution risks of the drinking water source protection zone, the recharge area of groundwater-type drinking water sources, and the surrounding areas of water supply units, screen for potential pollution risk factors, and take corresponding risk prevention measures.

If a drinking water source is polluted and threatens the safety of water supply, the environmental protection department shall order the relevant enterprises, institutions, and other producers and operators to take measures such as stopping the discharge of water pollutants, and notify the drinking water supply unit and the water supply, health, water administration, and other departments; if it spans administrative regions, it shall also notify the relevant local people's governments.

**Article 70** The people's government of a city with a single water source shall construct emergency water sources or backup water sources; where conditions permit, regional networked water supply may be implemented.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall reasonably arrange and layout rural drinking water sources; where conditions permit, they may adopt methods such as extending the urban water supply pipeline network or constructing cross-village, cross-township joint centralized water supply projects to develop large-scale centralized water supply.

**Article 71** Drinking water supply units shall conduct water quality testing at the water intake and outlet. If it is found that the water quality at the water intake does not meet the drinking water source water quality standards or the water quality at the outlet does not meet the drinking water hygiene standards, they shall promptly take corresponding measures and report to the local city or county people's government's competent water supply department. After receiving the report, the competent water supply department shall notify the environmental protection, health, water administration, and other departments.

Drinking water supply units shall be responsible for the quality of the supplied water, ensure the safe and reliable operation of water supply facilities, and ensure that the quality of the supplied water meets the relevant national standards.

**Article 72** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to monitor and assess the safety status of drinking water, including the water quality of drinking water sources, the water supplied by water supply units, and the water from user taps within their administrative regions.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall disclose information on the safety status of drinking water to the public at least once per quarter.

**Article 73** The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the needs of water environmental protection, stipulate measures such as prohibiting or restricting the use of phosphorus-containing detergents, fertilizers, and pesticides, and restricting planting and breeding within drinking water source protection zones.

**Article 74** People's governments at or above the county level may designate protection zones for water bodies in scenic spots, important fishery waters, and other water bodies with special economic and cultural value, and take measures to ensure that the water quality in the protection zones meets the water environment quality standards for the designated uses.

**Article 75** Within the protection zones of water bodies in scenic spots, important fishery waters, and other water bodies with special economic and cultural value, new sewage outlets shall not be constructed. When constructing new sewage outlets near the protection zones, it shall be ensured that the water bodies in the protection zones are not polluted.

## Chapter VI: Disposal of Water Pollution Accidents

**Article 76** People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, as well as enterprises and institutions that may cause water pollution accidents, shall, in accordance with the provisions of the "Emergency Response Law of the People's Republic of China," carry out emergency preparedness, emergency response, and post-accident recovery for sudden water pollution accidents.

**Article 77** Enterprises and institutions that may cause water pollution accidents shall formulate emergency plans for water pollution accidents, make emergency preparations, and conduct regular drills.

Enterprises and institutions that produce or store hazardous chemicals shall take measures to prevent the direct discharge of firefighting wastewater and waste liquids that may seriously pollute water bodies, generated during the handling of safety production accidents, into water bodies.

**Article 78** If an accident or other sudden event occurs in an enterprise or institution, causing or potentially causing a water pollution accident, it shall immediately activate its own emergency plan, take emergency measures such as isolation to prevent water pollutants from entering the water body, and report to the local people's government at or above the county level or the environmental protection department at the place of the accident. After receiving the report, the environmental protection department shall promptly report to the people's government at the same level and copy the report to the relevant departments.

If a fishery pollution accident occurs or a fishing vessel causes a water pollution accident, it shall be reported to the competent fishery department at the place of the accident for investigation and handling. If other vessels cause a water pollution accident, it shall be reported to the maritime administration agency at the place of the accident for investigation and handling; if damage is caused to fisheries, the maritime administration agency shall notify the competent fishery department to participate in the investigation and handling.

**Article 79** The people's governments at the city and county levels shall organize the formulation of emergency plans for drinking water safety emergencies.

Drinking water supply units shall, based on the local emergency plan for drinking water safety emergencies, formulate corresponding emergency plans, report them to the local city or county people's government for record, and conduct regular drills.

If a water pollution accident occurs in a drinking water source, or if other sudden events that may affect the safety of drinking water occur, the drinking water supply unit shall take emergency measures, report to the local city or county people's government, and make it public. The relevant people's government shall promptly activate the emergency plan based on the situation and take effective measures to ensure the safety of water supply.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

**Article 80** If the environmental protection department or other departments exercising supervisory and management powers in accordance with this Law fail to make administrative licenses or handle approval documents according to law, fail to investigate and deal with illegal acts after discovering them or receiving reports of illegal acts, or have other acts of failing to perform their duties in accordance with this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 81** If a person refuses or obstructs the supervision and inspection of the environmental protection department or other departments exercising supervisory and management powers in accordance with this Law by means of delay, encirclement, detention of law enforcement personnel, or practices fraud during the supervision and inspection, the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level or other departments exercising supervisory and management powers in accordance with this Law shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

**Article 82** In violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a specified period, and a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if correction is not made within the specified period, the enterprise shall be ordered to suspend production for remediation:

(1) Failing to conduct self-monitoring of the discharged water pollutants as required, or failing to keep original monitoring records;

(2) Failing to install automatic monitoring equipment for water pollutant discharge as required, failing to connect it to the monitoring equipment of the environmental protection department as required, or failing to ensure the normal operation of the monitoring equipment;

(3) Failing to monitor the discharge outlets of toxic and hazardous water pollutants and the surrounding environment as required, or failing to disclose information on toxic and hazardous water pollutants.

**Article 83** In violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, or ordered to restrict production or suspend production for remediation, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval, and the enterprise shall be ordered to suspend business or close down:

(1) Discharging water pollutants without obtaining a pollutant discharge permit according to law;

(2) Discharging water pollutants exceeding the water pollutant discharge standards or exceeding the total discharge control indicators for key water pollutants;

(3) Discharging water pollutants by means of escape supervision, such as using seepage wells, seepage pits, fissures, karst caves, secretly setting up pipes, tampering with or falsifying monitoring data, or not operating water pollution prevention facilities normally;

(4) Failing to carry out pretreatment as required, and discharging industrial wastewater that does not meet the treatment process requirements into centralized sewage treatment facilities.

**Article 84** If a sewage outlet is set up within a drinking water source protection zone, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order its removal within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if it is not removed within the specified period, it shall be forcibly removed, with the cost borne by the violator, and a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the enterprise may be ordered to suspend production for remediation.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, if a sewage outlet is set up in violation of laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the environmental protection department of the State Council, the environmental protection department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order its removal within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if it is not removed within the specified period, it shall be forcibly removed, with the cost borne by the violator, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the enterprise may be ordered to suspend production for remediation.

If a sewage outlet is constructed, renovated, or expanded in rivers or lakes without the consent of the water administrative department or the river basin management agency, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, take measures and impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 85** Any of the following acts shall be ordered by the environmental protection department of the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, take remedial measures within a specified period, eliminate pollution, and impose a fine; if remedial measures are not taken within the specified period, the environmental protection department may designate a unit with the capacity to carry out the remediation on behalf of the violator, with the cost borne by the violator:

(1) Discharging oil, acid liquids, or alkaline liquids into water bodies;

(2) Discharging highly toxic waste liquids into water bodies, or discharging or dumping soluble highly toxic waste residues containing mercury, cadmium, arsenic, chromium, lead, cyanide, yellow phosphorus, etc., into water bodies or directly burying them underground;

(3) Washing vehicles or containers that have stored or transported oil or toxic pollutants in water bodies;(4) Discharging or dumping industrial solid waste, urban garbage, or other waste into water bodies, or stacking, storing solid waste or other pollutants on beaches or banks below the highest water level of rivers, lakes, canals, channels, or reservoirs;

(5) Discharging or dumping radioactive solid waste or wastewater containing high- or medium-level radioactive substances into water bodies;

(6) Discharging wastewater containing low-level radioactive substances, thermal wastewater, or wastewater containing pathogens into water bodies in violation of relevant national regulations or standards;

(7) Failing to take anti-leakage measures or failing to construct groundwater quality monitoring wells for monitoring;

(8) Failing to use double-layer tanks or take other effective measures such as constructing anti-leakage pools for underground oil tanks at gas stations, or failing to conduct anti-leakage monitoring;

(9) Failing to take protective measures as required, or using ditches, pits, or ponds without anti-leakage measures to transport or store wastewater containing toxic pollutants, wastewater containing pathogens, or other waste.

For any act specified in items (3), (4), (6), (7), or (8) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. For any act specified in items (1), (2), (5), or (9) of the preceding paragraph, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order suspension of business or closure.

Article 86: Where, in violation of this Law, equipment listed in the catalogue of equipment prohibited from production, sale, import, or use that seriously pollutes the water environment is produced, sold, imported, or used, or a process listed in the catalogue of processes prohibited from use that seriously pollutes the water environment is adopted, the comprehensive economic macro-control department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the comprehensive economic macro-control department of the people's government at or above the county level shall submit an opinion and request the people's government at the same level to order suspension of business or closure.

Article 87: Where, in violation of this Law, a production project that does not conform to national industrial policies, such as small-scale papermaking, leather tanning, printing and dyeing, dye production, coking, sulfur smelting, arsenic smelting, mercury smelting, oil refining, electroplating, pesticide production, asbestos production, cement production, glass production, steel production, thermal power generation, or other projects that seriously pollute the water environment, is constructed, the people's government of the city or county where the project is located shall order its closure.

Article 88: Where the operating unit of a centralized urban sewage treatment facility or a sludge treatment and disposal unit fails to meet national standards for treated and disposed sludge, or fails to record the destination of the sludge, the urban drainage department shall order corrective measures within a specified period and issue a warning; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if corrective measures are not taken within the specified period, the urban drainage department may designate a unit with the capacity to carry out the treatment on its behalf, with the costs borne by the violator.

Article 89: Where a vessel is not equipped with corresponding anti-pollution equipment and devices, or does not hold valid certificates and documents for preventing water environment pollution, the maritime administration agency or the fisheries department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order correction within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; if correction is not made within the specified period, the vessel shall be ordered to temporarily cease navigation.

Where a vessel, during operations involving pollutant discharge, fails to comply with operating procedures or fails to truthfully record in the corresponding logbook, the maritime administration agency or the fisheries department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order correction and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

Article 90: Where, in violation of this Law, any of the following acts is committed, the maritime administration agency or the fisheries department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if water pollution is caused, the violator shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified period to eliminate the pollution and shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if remedial measures are not taken within the specified period, the maritime administration agency or the fisheries department may, according to their respective responsibilities, designate a unit with the capacity to carry out the treatment on its behalf, with the costs borne by the vessel:

(1) Dumping vessel garbage or discharging vessel residual oil or waste oil into water bodies;

(2) Conducting lightering operations of bulk liquid hazardous cargo without approval from the maritime administration agency at the operation site;

(3) Failing to take pollution prevention measures as required during risk-polluting operations by vessels or related operating units;

(4) Conducting vessel dismantling by beaching;

(5) International voyage vessels entering the inland waters of the People's Republic of China discharging vessel ballast water that does not meet the requirements.

Article 91: Where any of the following acts is committed, the environmental protection department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; and shall report to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order removal or closure:

(1) Constructing, renovating, or expanding construction projects unrelated to water supply facilities and water source protection within a Class I drinking water source protection zone;

(2) Constructing, renovating, or expanding construction projects that discharge pollutants within a Class II drinking water source protection zone;

(3) Constructing or expanding construction projects that cause serious water pollution, or renovating construction projects that increase pollutant discharge, within a drinking water source quasi-protection zone.

Where cage aquaculture is conducted or activities such as tourism, angling, or other activities that may pollute drinking water are organized within a Class I drinking water source protection zone, the environmental protection department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan. Where an individual swims, fishes, or engages in other activities that may pollute drinking water within a Class I drinking water source protection zone, the environmental protection department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and may impose a fine of not more than 500 yuan.

Article 92: Where the water quality of drinking water supplied by a drinking water supply unit does not meet the national standards, the water supply department of the local city or county people's government shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval, and suspension of business for rectification may be ordered; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 93: Where an enterprise or institution commits any of the following acts, the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to formulate an emergency plan for water pollution accidents as required;

(2) Failing to promptly activate the emergency plan for a water pollution accident and take relevant emergency measures after the occurrence of a water pollution accident.

Article 94: Where an enterprise or institution, in violation of this Law, causes a water pollution accident, in addition to bearing compensation liability according to law, the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose a fine in accordance with paragraph 2 of this Article, and order the adoption of remedial measures within a specified period to eliminate the pollution; if the required remedial measures are not taken or the violator lacks the capacity to take them, the environmental protection department shall designate a unit with the capacity to carry out the treatment on its behalf, with the costs borne by the violator; if a major or extraordinarily serious water pollution accident is caused, the matter may also be reported to the people's government with approval authority for approval to order closure; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel may be fined not more than 50% of their income from the unit in the previous year; if any of the acts of illegally discharging water pollutants specified in Article 63 of the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China is committed and does not constitute a crime, the public security authority shall detain the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel for not less than 10 days but not more than 15 days; if the circumstances are minor, detention for not less than 5 days but not more than 10 days shall be imposed.

For a general or relatively major water pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at 20% of the direct losses caused by the water pollution accident; for a major or extraordinarily serious water pollution accident, the fine shall be calculated at 30% of the direct losses caused by the water pollution accident.

Where a fishery pollution accident is caused or a water pollution accident is caused by a fishing vessel, the fisheries department shall impose the penalty; where a water pollution accident is caused by other vessels, the maritime administration agency shall impose the penalty.

Article 95: Where an enterprise, institution, or other producer or business operator illegally discharges water pollutants, is fined, and is ordered to make corrections, the administrative authority that made the penalty decision shall conduct a review; if it is found that the violator continues to illegally discharge water pollutants or refuses or obstructs the review, a daily continuous penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 96: A party harmed by water pollution has the right to demand that the polluter eliminate the hazard and compensate for losses.

Where water pollution damage is caused by force majeure, the polluter shall not bear compensation liability, unless otherwise provided by law.

Where water pollution damage is intentionally caused by the victim, the polluter shall not bear compensation liability. Where water pollution damage is caused by gross negligence of the victim, the polluter's compensation liability may be reduced.

Where water pollution damage is caused by a third party, the polluter, after bearing compensation liability, has the right to seek recourse from the third party.

Article 97: Disputes over liability for damages and the amount of compensation arising from water pollution may, at the request of the parties, be mediated by the environmental protection department, the maritime administration agency, or the fisheries department according to their respective responsibilities; if mediation fails, the parties may file a lawsuit with the people's court. The parties may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 98: In a lawsuit for damages arising from water pollution, the polluter shall bear the burden of proof regarding the statutory grounds for exemption and the absence of a causal relationship between its act and the damage result.

Article 99: Where there are a large number of parties harmed by water pollution, they may, according to law, elect representatives to conduct a joint action.

The environmental protection department and relevant social organizations may, according to law, support parties harmed by water pollution in filing lawsuits with the people's court.

The State encourages legal service institutions and lawyers to provide legal aid to victims in water pollution damage lawsuits.

Article 100: In disputes over liability for damages and the amount of compensation arising from water pollution, the parties may entrust an environmental monitoring institution to provide monitoring data. The environmental monitoring institution shall accept the entrustment and truthfully provide relevant monitoring data.

Article 101: Where a violation of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 102: The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Water pollution" refers to the phenomenon where the introduction of a substance into a water body causes changes in its chemical, physical, biological, or radioactive characteristics, thereby affecting the effective use of the water, endangering human health, or destroying the ecological environment, resulting in deterioration of water quality.

(2) "Water pollutant" refers to a substance discharged directly or indirectly into a water body that can cause water pollution.

(3) "Toxic pollutant" refers to a pollutant that, after being directly or indirectly ingested by an organism, may cause disease, abnormal behavior, genetic mutation, physiological dysfunction, physical deformation, or death in that organism or its offspring.

(4) "Sludge" refers to semi-solid or solid substances generated during the sewage treatment process.

(5) "Fishery water body" refers to designated water bodies that serve as spawning grounds, feeding grounds, wintering grounds, migration channels for fish and shrimp, and aquaculture grounds for fish, shrimp, shellfish, and algae.

Article 103: This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2008.
