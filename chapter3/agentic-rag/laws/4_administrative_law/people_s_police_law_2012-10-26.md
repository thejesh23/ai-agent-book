# People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on February 28, 1995

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution with a view to safeguarding national security and social public order, protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens, strengthening the building of the people's police force, exercising strict discipline over the police, improving the quality of the people's police, ensuring that the people's police exercise their functions and powers according to law, and ensuring the smooth progress of reform, opening up, and socialist modernization.

Article 2 The tasks of the people's police are to safeguard national security, maintain social public order, protect the personal safety, personal freedom, and lawful property of citizens, protect public property, and prevent, stop, and punish illegal and criminal activities.

The people's police include the people's police of public security organs, state security organs, prisons, and reeducation-through-labor management organs, as well as the judicial police of the People's Courts and the People's Procuratorates.

Article 3 The people's police must rely on the support of the people, maintain close ties with the people, listen to the opinions and suggestions of the people, accept the supervision of the people, safeguard the interests of the people, and serve the people wholeheartedly.

Article 4 The people's police must take the Constitution and laws as the norms for their activities, be loyal to their duties, be honest and upright, have strict discipline, obey orders, and strictly enforce the law.

Article 5 The people's police are protected by law when performing their duties according to law.

## Chapter II: Functions and Powers

Article 6 The people's police of public security organs shall, according to the division of their duties, perform the following functions according to law:

(1) Preventing, stopping, and investigating illegal and criminal activities;

(2) Maintaining social public order and stopping acts that endanger social public order;

(3) Maintaining traffic safety and traffic order, and handling traffic accidents;

(4) Organizing and implementing fire-fighting work and exercising fire supervision;

(5) Managing firearms and ammunition, controlled knives, and dangerous articles such as flammable, explosive, highly toxic, and radioactive substances;

(6) Managing special trades as stipulated by laws and regulations;

(7) Guarding specific persons designated by the state, and guarding important places and facilities;

(8) Managing assemblies, processions, and demonstrations;

(9) Managing household registration, nationality, entry and exit affairs, and matters related to the residence and travel of foreigners within the territory of China;

(10) Maintaining public order in border (frontier) areas;

(11) Executing criminal punishments for persons sentenced to criminal detention or deprived of political rights;

(12) Supervising and managing the security protection of computer information systems;

(13) Guiding and supervising the security and protection work of state organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and key construction projects, and guiding the security and prevention work of mass organizations such as security committees;

(14) Other functions as stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 7 The people's police of public security organs may, according to law, impose administrative compulsory measures and administrative penalties on individuals or organizations that violate public security management or other public security administrative laws and regulations.

Article 8 The people's police of public security organs may forcibly take away from the scene persons who seriously endanger social public order or threaten public safety, detain them according to law, or take other measures provided by law.

Article 9 To maintain social public order, the people's police of public security organs may, after presenting the relevant credentials, interrogate and inspect on the spot persons suspected of illegal or criminal activities; if, after interrogation and inspection, any of the following circumstances exists, they may take such persons to a public security organ and, with the approval of that public security organ, continue the interrogation:

(1) Being accused of committing a crime;

(2) Being suspected of committing a crime at the scene;

(3) Having unidentified identity and being suspected of committing a crime;

(4) Carrying articles that are likely to be stolen goods.

The period of detention of the interrogated person shall not exceed twenty-four hours from the time he is brought to the public security organ; under special circumstances, with the approval of a public security organ at or above the county level, it may be extended to forty-eight hours, and a record of the interrogation shall be kept. If the continuation of the interrogation is approved, the family members or the unit to which the person belongs shall be notified immediately. If the continuation of the interrogation is not approved, the interrogated person shall be released immediately.

If, after continued interrogation, the public security organ deems it necessary to take detention or other compulsory measures against the interrogated person according to law, it shall make a decision within the period specified in the preceding paragraph; if it cannot make the above decision within the period specified in the preceding paragraph, the interrogated person shall be released immediately.

Article 10 In emergency situations such as resistance to arrest, riot, prison escape, snatching of firearms, or other violent acts, the people's police of public security organs may use weapons in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

Article 11 To meet the needs of stopping serious illegal and criminal activities, the people's police of public security organs may use police implements in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

Article 12 To meet the needs of investigating criminal activities, the people's police of public security organs may, according to law, execute detention, search, arrest, or other compulsory measures.

Article 13 When urgently needed for performing their duties, the people's police of public security organs may, after presenting the relevant credentials, have priority in taking public transport, and when encountering traffic obstructions, have priority in passing.

When necessary for investigating crimes, public security organs may, in accordance with the relevant state regulations, have priority in using the means of transport, communication tools, sites, and buildings of state organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and individuals, and shall return them promptly after use and pay appropriate fees; if losses are caused, compensation shall be made.

Article 14 The people's police of public security organs may take protective restraint measures against mental patients who seriously endanger public safety or the personal safety of others. If it is necessary to send them to designated units or places for guardianship, approval shall be obtained from the public security organ of the people's government at or above the county level, and their guardians shall be notified promptly.

Article 15 Public security organs of the people's governments at or above the county level may, for the purpose of preventing and stopping acts that seriously endanger social public order, restrict the passage or stay of persons and vehicles in a certain area and time, and may implement traffic control when necessary.

The people's police of public security organs may, in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, take corresponding traffic control measures.

Article 16 When necessary for investigating crimes, public security organs may, in accordance with the relevant state regulations and through strict approval procedures, adopt technical investigation measures.

Article 17 Public security organs of the people's governments at or above the county level may, with the approval of the public security organ at the next higher level and the people's government at the same level, implement on-site control over emergencies that seriously endanger social public order according to the circumstances.

The people's police of public security organs may, in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, take necessary means to forcibly disperse persons, and forcibly take away from the scene or immediately detain persons who refuse to obey.

Article 18 The people's police of state security organs, prisons, and reeducation-through-labor management organs, and the judicial police of the People's Courts and the People's Procuratorates shall exercise their functions and powers respectively in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 19 When encountering emergency situations within the scope of their duties during non-working hours, the people's police shall perform their duties.

## Chapter III: Obligations and Discipline

Article 20 The people's police must:

(1) Enforce the law impartially and handle affairs justly;

(2) Exemplarily observe social ethics;

(3) Be polite to others and perform duties in a civilized manner;

(4) Respect the customs and habits of the people.

Article 21 When the personal safety or property of a citizen is infringed upon or the citizen is in other dangerous situations, the people's police shall immediately provide assistance; when a citizen requests help in resolving a dispute, the people's police shall provide help; when a citizen reports a case to the police, the people's police shall promptly investigate and handle it.

The people's police shall actively participate in emergency rescue, disaster relief, and social welfare work.

Article 22 The people's police shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Spreading statements that harm the reputation of the state, participating in illegal organizations, engaging in assemblies, processions, demonstrations, and other activities aimed at opposing the state, or participating in strikes;
(2) Disclosing state secrets or police work secrets;

(3) Resorting to deception, concealing case facts, or harboring or conniving at illegal and criminal activities;

(4) Extorting confessions by torture or physically punishing or maltreating criminal suspects or offenders;

(5) Illegally depriving or restricting the personal freedom of others, or illegally searching the body, articles, residence, or premises of others;

(6) Extorting or demanding or accepting bribes;

(7) Assaulting others or instigating others to assault others;

(8) Illegally imposing penalties or collecting fees;

(9) Accepting invitations for dinner or gifts from parties or their agents;

(10) Engaging in for-profit business activities or being employed by any individual or organization;

(11) Neglecting duties or failing to perform legal obligations;

(12) Other acts that violate laws or discipline.

Article 23 The people's police must wear uniforms as prescribed, wear the insignia of the people's police or hold the certificate of the people's police, maintain a neat and dignified appearance, and behave with decorum.

## Chapter IV: Organization and Management

Article 24 The state shall, in light of the nature, tasks, and characteristics of the work of the people's police, prescribe the organizational structure and the sequence of posts.

Article 25 The people's police shall implement a police rank system according to law.

Article 26 The following conditions shall be met for serving as a people's police officer:

(1) Being a citizen who has reached the age of eighteen;

(2) Upholding the Constitution of the People's Republic of China;

(3) Having good political and professional qualities and good conduct;

(4) Being in good health;

(5) Having an educational level of senior high school graduation or above;

(6) Voluntarily engaging in the work of the people's police.

A person shall not serve as a people's police officer under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having been subjected to criminal punishment for committing a crime;

(2) Having been discharged from public employment.

Article 27 The recruitment of people's police officers must, in accordance with state regulations, be conducted through open examinations, strict assessment, and selection of the best candidates.

Article 28 Persons holding leadership positions in the people's police force shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having professional legal knowledge;

(2) Having experience in political and legal work and certain organizational management and command capabilities;

(3) Having an educational level of junior college or above;

(4) Having been trained at a people's police academy and passed the examination.

Article 29 The state shall develop the education of the people's police and conduct planned education and training in political ideology, legal system, and police professional knowledge for the people's police.

Article 30 The state shall, in light of the nature, tasks, and characteristics of the work of the people's police, prescribe the service years for different posts and the maximum age for holding different positions.

Article 31 People's police officers or collectives that have made outstanding contributions, achieved remarkable results, or made special contributions in their work shall be given awards. Awards include: commendation, third-class merit, second-class merit, first-class merit, and conferment of honorary titles.

For awarded people's police officers, their police ranks may be promoted ahead of schedule in accordance with the relevant state regulations, and certain material rewards shall be given.

## Chapter V: Police Service Guarantees

Article 32 The people's police must execute the decisions and orders of their superiors.

If the people's police believe that a decision or order is erroneous, they may put forward their opinions in accordance with the regulations, but shall not suspend or change the execution of the decision or order; if their opinions are not adopted, they must obey the decision or order; the consequences of executing the decision or order shall be borne by the superior who made the decision or order.

Article 33 The people's police have the right to refuse to execute instructions that exceed the scope of functions and powers of the people's police as stipulated by laws and regulations, and shall simultaneously report to the superior organ.

Article 34 When the people's police perform their duties according to law, citizens and organizations shall provide support and assistance. The acts of citizens and organizations in assisting the people's police in performing their duties according to law are protected by law. Those who have made remarkable achievements in assisting the people's police in performing their duties shall be commended and rewarded.

If citizens or organizations suffer personal injury or property loss due to assisting the people's police in performing their duties, they shall be given pensions or compensation in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

Article 35 Anyone who refuses or obstructs the people's police from performing their duties according to law, and commits any of the following acts, shall be given a public security penalty:

(1) Publicly insulting a people's police officer who is performing his duties;

(2) Obstructing a people's police officer from investigating and collecting evidence;

(3) Refusing or obstructing a people's police officer from entering relevant residences or premises for tasks such as pursuit, search, or rescue;

(4) Intentionally setting up obstacles for police vehicles performing urgent tasks such as rescue, disaster relief, pursuit, or escort;

(5) Other acts of refusing or obstructing the people's police from performing their duties according to law.

If the acts specified in the preceding paragraph are committed by violence or threat and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 36 The police insignia, uniforms, and police implements of the people's police shall be uniformly supervised by the public security department of the State Council, and managed jointly with other relevant state organs; no other individual or organization may illegally manufacture or sell them.

The police insignia, uniforms, police implements, and certificates of the people's police are exclusively for the use of the people's police; no other individual or organization may possess or use them.

Anyone who violates the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall have the illegally manufactured, sold, possessed, or used police insignia, uniforms, police implements, and certificates of the people's police confiscated, and shall be detained for not more than fifteen days or given a warning by the public security organ, and may also be fined not more than five times the illegal income; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 37 The state shall guarantee the funds for the people's police. The funds for the people's police shall, in accordance with the principle of division of powers and responsibilities, be included in the central and local financial budgets respectively.

Article 38 The people's governments at all levels shall include the construction of communication and training facilities, transportation and fire-fighting facilities, and infrastructure such as police stations and detention centers necessary for the work of the people's police in the basic construction plan and the overall urban and rural construction plan.

Article 39 The state shall strengthen the modernization of the equipment of the people's police and strive to promote and apply advanced scientific and technological achievements.

Article 40 The people's police shall implement the salary system for state civil servants and enjoy the police rank allowance and other allowances, subsidies, as well as insurance and welfare benefits stipulated by the state.

Article 41 If a people's police officer becomes disabled in the line of duty, he shall enjoy the same pensions and preferential treatment from the state as active-duty servicemen who become disabled in the line of duty.

If a people's police officer dies in the line of duty or dies of illness, his family members shall enjoy the same pensions and preferential treatment from the state as the family members of active-duty servicemen who die in the line of duty or die of illness.

## Chapter VI: Supervision over Law Enforcement

Article 42 The people's police shall, when performing their duties, accept supervision by the People's Procuratorates and administrative supervisory organs according to law.

Article 43 The superior organs of the people's police shall supervise the law enforcement activities of the subordinate organs, and if they find that the decisions or dispositions made by the subordinate organs are erroneous, they shall revoke or modify them.

Article 44 The people's police must consciously accept supervision by the society and citizens when performing their duties. Regulations made by the people's police organs that are directly related to the public interest shall be made public.

Article 45 When handling public security cases, the people's police shall withdraw from the case under any of the following circumstances, and the parties or their legal representatives also have the right to request their withdrawal:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party;

(2) Having an interest in the case themselves or their close relatives;

(3) Having other relationships with the parties to the case that may affect the impartial handling of the case.

The withdrawal specified in the preceding paragraph shall be decided by the relevant public security organ.

The withdrawal of the people's police in the process of handling criminal cases shall be governed by the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law.

Article 46 Citizens or organizations have the right to report or accuse the people's police of illegal or disciplinary acts to the people's police organs, the People's Procuratorates, or administrative supervisory organs. The organ that receives the report or accusation shall promptly investigate and handle it, and inform the reporter or accuser of the result.

No one may suppress or retaliate against citizens or organizations that report or accuse according to law.

Article 47 Public security organs shall establish a supervision system to supervise the compliance of the people's police of public security organs with laws, regulations, and discipline.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

Article 48 If a people's police officer commits any of the acts listed in Article 22 of this Law, he shall be given administrative sanctions; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Administrative sanctions include: warning, demerit, major demerit, demotion, dismissal from post, and discharge from public employment. For a people's police officer subjected to administrative sanctions, his police rank may be lowered or revoked in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

For a people's police officer who violates discipline, measures such as suspension from duty or confinement may be taken when necessary.

Article 49 If a people's police officer violates regulations by using weapons or police implements, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 50 If a people's police officer, in the performance of his duties, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen or organization and causes damage, compensation shall be made in accordance with the provisions of the State Compensation Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter VIII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 51 The Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall perform the security tasks assigned by the state.

Article 52 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation. The Regulations on the People's Police of the People's Republic of China promulgated on June 25, 1957 shall be repealed simultaneously.
