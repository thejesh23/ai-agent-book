# International Criminal Judicial Assistance Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure the smooth conduct of international criminal judicial assistance, strengthen international cooperation in the criminal justice field, effectively punish crimes, protect the legitimate rights and interests of individuals and organizations, and safeguard national interests and social order.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "international criminal judicial assistance" refers to the mutual assistance provided between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries in activities such as criminal investigation, inquiry, prosecution, trial, and enforcement of judgments, including the service of documents, collection of evidence, arranging for witnesses to testify or assist in investigations, sealing, seizing, and freezing of property involved in a case, confiscation and return of illegal gains and other property involved in a case, transfer of sentenced persons, and other forms of assistance.

Article 3 Criminal judicial assistance between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries shall be conducted in accordance with this Law.

The execution of requests for criminal judicial assistance made by foreign countries shall be governed by this Law, the Criminal Procedure Law, and other relevant laws.

Matters such as the signing authority of the request letter, the language of the request letter and attached materials, relevant processing deadlines, and specific procedures may be handled in accordance with the provisions of criminal judicial assistance treaties or through consultation between the parties, provided that the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China are not violated.

Article 4 The People's Republic of China and foreign countries shall conduct international criminal judicial assistance based on the principle of equality and mutual benefit.

International criminal judicial assistance shall not harm the sovereignty, security, or social public interests of the People's Republic of China, nor violate the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China.

Without the consent of the competent authorities of the People's Republic of China, foreign institutions, organizations, and individuals shall not conduct criminal proceedings as specified in this Law within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and institutions, organizations, and individuals within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall not provide evidence materials or assistance as specified in this Law to foreign parties.

Article 5 Criminal judicial assistance between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries shall be conducted through liaison authorities.

Liaison authorities such as the Ministry of Justice of the People's Republic of China are responsible for submitting, receiving, and forwarding requests for criminal judicial assistance, and handling other matters related to international criminal judicial assistance.

If there is no criminal judicial assistance treaty between the People's Republic of China and a foreign country, contact shall be made through diplomatic channels.

Article 6 The National Supervisory Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the Ministry of Public Security, the Ministry of State Security, and other departments are the competent authorities for conducting international criminal judicial assistance. They are responsible, according to their respective functions and duties, for reviewing requests for criminal judicial assistance to be submitted to foreign countries, examining and handling requests for criminal judicial assistance from foreign countries forwarded by the liaison authorities, and undertaking other work related to international criminal judicial assistance. In cases involving the transfer of sentenced persons, the Ministry of Justice shall, according to its functions and duties, assume the corresponding responsibilities of the competent authority.

The authorities handling cases related to criminal judicial assistance are the case-handling authorities for international criminal judicial assistance. They are responsible for submitting to their respective competent authorities requests for criminal judicial assistance that need to be made to foreign countries, and for executing requests for criminal judicial assistance from foreign countries assigned by their respective competent authorities.

Article 7 The State guarantees the funds necessary for conducting international criminal judicial assistance.

Article 8 Expenses arising from the mutual execution of requests for criminal judicial assistance between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries shall be borne in accordance with treaty provisions if a treaty exists; if there is no treaty or the treaty has no provisions, the expenses shall be resolved through consultation based on the principle of equality and mutual benefit.

## Chapter II Submission, Receipt, and Processing of Requests for Criminal Judicial Assistance

### Section 1 Requesting Criminal Judicial Assistance from Foreign Countries

Article 9 Where a case-handling authority needs to request criminal judicial assistance from a foreign country, it shall prepare a request letter for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After the request is reviewed and approved by its competent authority, the liaison authority shall promptly submit the request to the foreign country.

Article 10 A request letter for criminal judicial assistance to a foreign country shall be submitted in accordance with the provisions of the criminal judicial assistance treaty; if there is no treaty or the treaty has no provisions, it may be submitted by reference to the provisions of Article 13 of this Law; if the requested state has special requirements, the request may be submitted in accordance with those special requirements, provided that the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China are not violated.

The request letter and attached materials shall be prepared in Chinese, accompanied by a translation in the official language of the requested state.

Article 11 If the requested state imposes additional conditions for executing a request for criminal judicial assistance, and these conditions do not harm the sovereignty, security, or social public interests of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs may make a commitment. If the requested state explicitly indicates that a commitment made by the liaison authority is fully effective, the liaison authority may also make a commitment. Commitments regarding limitations on prosecution shall be decided by the Supreme People's Procuratorate; commitments regarding sentencing shall be decided by the Supreme People's Court.

When pursuing criminal liability against the persons involved, the relevant authorities shall be bound by the commitments made.

Article 12 After receiving relevant notifications or execution results from a foreign country, the liaison authority shall promptly forward or inform the relevant competent authority.

If a foreign country requests notification of the litigation outcome of a case for which it provided criminal judicial assistance, the liaison authority shall forward the request to the relevant competent authority for handling.

### Section 2 Requesting Criminal Judicial Assistance from the People's Republic of China

Article 13 A foreign country requesting criminal judicial assistance from the People's Republic of China shall submit a request letter in accordance with the provisions of the criminal judicial assistance treaty. If there is no treaty or the treaty has no provisions, the request letter shall include the following items and be accompanied by relevant materials:

(1) The name of the requesting authority;
(2) The nature of the case, basic information of the persons involved, and the facts of the crime;
(3) The legal provisions applicable to this case;
(4) The matters requested and the purpose;
(5) The relevance between the requested matters and the case;
(6) The expected deadline for execution of the request;
(7) Other necessary information or additional requirements.

In the absence of a criminal judicial assistance treaty, the requesting state shall make a commitment of reciprocity.

The request letter and attached materials shall be accompanied by a Chinese translation.

Article 14 A request for criminal judicial assistance from a foreign country to the People's Republic of China may be refused in any of the following circumstances:

(1) According to the laws of the People's Republic of China, the act for which the request is made does not constitute a crime;
(2) At the time of receiving the request, the crime for which the request is made is under investigation, inquiry, prosecution, or trial within the territory of the People's Republic of China, a final judgment has been rendered, the criminal proceedings have been terminated, or the statute of limitations for prosecution has expired;
(3) The crime for which the request is made is a political offense;
(4) The crime for which the request is made is purely a military offense;
(5) The purpose of the request is to conduct investigation, inquiry, prosecution, trial, or execution of punishment based on race, ethnicity, religion, nationality, gender, political opinion, or status, or the parties may be subjected to unfair treatment due to the above reasons;
(6) There is a lack of substantial connection between the requested matters and the case for which assistance is sought;
(7) Other circumstances under which assistance may be refused.

Article 15 Upon receiving a request for criminal judicial assistance from a foreign country, the liaison authority shall review the request letter and attached materials. If the form and content of the request letter meet the requirements, it shall, according to the division of functions and duties, forward the request letter and attached materials to the relevant competent authority for processing; if the form and content of the request letter do not meet the requirements, it may request the requesting state to supplement materials or resubmit the request.

If the request for criminal judicial assistance clearly harms the sovereignty, security, or social public interests of the People's Republic of China, the liaison authority may directly refuse assistance.

Article 16 After receiving the request letter for criminal judicial assistance and attached materials forwarded by the liaison authority, the competent authority shall review them and take the following actions accordingly:

(1) If it deems that assistance can be executed in accordance with this Law and the criminal judicial assistance treaty, it shall make a decision and arrange for the relevant case-handling authority to execute it;
(2) If it deems that assistance should be wholly or partially refused in accordance with Article 4 or Article 14 of this Law or the criminal judicial assistance treaty, it shall return the request letter and attached materials to the liaison authority and state the reasons;
(3) If there are confidentiality requirements or other additional conditions for executing the request, it shall raise them with the foreign country through the liaison authority, and after the foreign country accepts the conditions and provides a written guarantee, decide to execute the request with conditions;
(4) If supplementary materials are needed, it shall notify the liaison authority in writing to request the requesting state to provide them within a reasonable period.

If executing the request may hinder an ongoing investigation, inquiry, prosecution, trial, or execution by a relevant authority of the People's Republic of China, the competent authority may decide to postpone assistance and shall notify the liaison authority in writing of the decision and reasons for postponement.

If the foreign country has confidentiality requirements or special procedural requirements for executing its request, the competent authority may arrange for execution in accordance with those requirements, provided that the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China are not violated.

Article 17 After receiving a request for criminal judicial assistance from a foreign country assigned by the competent authority, the case-handling authority shall execute it in accordance with the law and promptly report the execution results or any circumstances hindering execution to the competent authority.

During the execution of the request, the case-handling authority shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of the parties and other relevant persons, and protect personal information.

Article 18 If a foreign country requests to use evidence materials obtained through criminal judicial assistance for purposes other than the case for which the request was made, the liaison authority shall forward the request to the competent authority, which shall decide whether to consent.

Article 19 After receiving relevant notifications or execution results from the competent authority, the liaison authority shall promptly forward or inform the requesting state.

For cases in which the People's Republic of China provides criminal judicial assistance, the competent authority may, through the liaison authority, request the foreign country to notify the litigation outcome.

If the foreign country notifies the litigation outcome, the liaison authority, upon receiving the relevant materials, shall promptly forward or inform the competent authority; if it involves the initiation of criminal proceedings against a citizen of the People's Republic of China, it shall also notify the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

## Chapter III Service of Documents

### Section 1 Requesting Service of Documents from Foreign Countries

Article 20 Where a case-handling authority needs a foreign country to assist in serving summonses, notices, indictments, judgments, and other judicial documents, it shall prepare a request letter for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After the request is reviewed and approved by its competent authority, the liaison authority shall promptly submit the request to the foreign country.

Article 21 When requesting a foreign country to serve documents, the request letter shall state the name of the person to be served, the address for service, and the relevant rights and obligations that need to be informed to the person to be served.

### Section 2 Requesting Service of Documents from the People's Republic of China

Article 22 A foreign country may request the People's Republic of China to assist in serving summonses, notices, indictments, judgments, and other judicial documents. The assistance of the People's Republic of China in serving judicial documents does not represent recognition of the legal validity of the foreign judicial documents.

A request for assistance in serving a summons to appear in court shall be submitted within the time limit specified in the relevant treaty. If there is no treaty or the treaty has no provisions, the request shall be submitted no later than three months before the court session.

The People's Republic of China is not obligated to assist in serving summonses requiring a citizen of the People's Republic of China to be interrogated or to appear in court as a defendant.

Article 23 When a foreign country requests the People's Republic of China to serve documents, the request letter shall state the name of the person to be served, the address for service, and the relevant rights and obligations that need to be informed to the person to be served.

Article 24 The people's court or other case-handling authority responsible for executing the service of documents shall promptly inform the liaison authority of the execution results through its competent authority, and the liaison authority shall inform the requesting state. Except in cases where service is impossible, a receipt of service signed by the person served or other supporting documents shall be attached.

## Chapter IV Collection of Evidence

### Section 1 Requesting Collection of Evidence from Foreign Countries

Article 25 Where a case-handling authority needs a foreign country to assist in collecting evidence on the following matters, it shall prepare a request letter for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After the request is reviewed and approved by its competent authority, the liaison authority shall promptly submit the request to the foreign country:

(1) Locating and identifying relevant persons;
(2) Inquiring about and verifying property involved in the case and financial account information;
(3) Obtaining and providing testimony or statements of relevant persons;
(4) Obtaining and providing relevant documents, records, electronic data, and objects;
(5) Obtaining and providing expert opinions;
(6) Inspecting or examining premises, objects, persons, or bodies;
(7) Searching persons, objects, residences, and other relevant premises;
(8) Other matters.

When requesting a foreign country to assist in collecting evidence, the case-handling authority may simultaneously request to send personnel to be present during the execution of the request.

Article 26 When requesting a foreign country to collect evidence, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, include the following items:

(1) The name, gender, address, identity information, contact information of the person to be investigated, and other materials helpful for identifying the person;
(2) Questions to be asked to the person to be investigated;
(3) The name, gender, address, identity information, contact information, appearance and behavioral characteristics of the person to be located and identified, and other materials helpful for location and identification;
(4) Specific information such as ownership, location, characteristics, appearance, and quantity of the property involved in the case to be inquired about and verified, and relevant financial account information to be inquired about and verified;
(5) Specific information such as the holder, location, characteristics, appearance, and quantity of relevant documents, records, electronic data, and objects to be obtained;
(6) Specific information of the object to be appraised;
(7) Specific information of the premises, objects, etc., to be inspected or examined;
(8) Specific information of the object to be searched;
(9) Other materials helpful for executing the request.

Article 27 If the requested state requires the return of evidence materials or objects it provided, the case-handling authority shall promptly return them through the liaison authority.

### Section 2 Requesting Collection of Evidence from the People's Republic of China

Article 28 A foreign country may request the People's Republic of China to assist in collecting evidence on the matters specified in paragraph 1 of Article 25 of this Law.

When a foreign country requests the People's Republic of China to collect evidence, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, include the items specified in Article 26 of this Law.

Article 29 When a foreign country requests the People's Republic of China to collect evidence, it may simultaneously request to send personnel to be present during the execution of the request. Personnel present with consent shall comply with the laws of the People's Republic of China and follow the arrangements of the competent authority and the case-handling authority.

Article 30 If the case-handling authority requires the requesting state to guarantee the return of evidence materials or objects it provides, and the requesting state provides such a guarantee, the materials or objects may be provided.

## Chapter V Arranging for Witnesses to Testify or Assist in Investigations

### Section 1 Requesting Foreign Countries to Arrange for Witnesses to Testify or Assist in Investigations

Article 31 Where a case-handling authority needs a foreign country to assist in arranging for witnesses or experts to come to the People's Republic of China to testify, or to testify via video or audio, or to assist in investigations, it shall prepare a request letter for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After the request is reviewed and approved by its competent authority, the liaison authority shall promptly submit the request to the foreign country.

Article 32 When requesting a foreign country to arrange for witnesses or experts to testify or assist in investigations, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, include the following items:

(1) The name, gender, address, identity information, contact information of the witness or expert, and other materials helpful for identifying the witness or expert;
(2) The purpose, necessity, time, and place of testifying or assisting in the investigation;
(3) The rights and obligations of the witness or expert;
(4) Protective measures for the witness or expert;
(5) Compensation for the witness or expert;
(6) Other materials helpful for executing the request.

Article 33 Witnesses or experts who come to the People's Republic of China to testify or assist in investigations shall not be prosecuted for crimes committed before their entry until they leave the country; their personal freedom shall not be restricted, except where compulsory measures are taken due to illegal or criminal acts committed after entry.

If the witness or expert does not leave the country within the period specified in the treaty or within fifteen days after being notified that their continued stay is unnecessary, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall no longer apply, except where the failure to leave is due to force majeure or other special reasons.

Article 34 Case-handling authorities shall provide compensation to witnesses or experts who come to the People's Republic of China to testify or assist in investigations, in accordance with the law.

Article 35 If a person coming to the People's Republic of China to testify or assist in an investigation is a person in custody, the liaison authority, together with the competent authority, shall reach an agreement in advance with the requested state on matters related to the transfer of the person in custody.

The competent authority and the case-handling authority shall comply with the content of the agreement, detain the transferred person in accordance with the law, and promptly return the person to the requested state after the testimony or assistance in the investigation is completed.

### Section 2 Requesting the People's Republic of China to Arrange for Witnesses to Testify or Assist in Investigations

Article 36 A foreign country may request the People's Republic of China to assist in arranging for witnesses or experts to go to the foreign country to testify, or to testify via video or audio, or to assist in investigations.

When a foreign country requests the People's Republic of China to arrange for witnesses or experts to testify or assist in investigations, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, include the items specified in Article 32 of this Law.

The requesting state shall provide a written guarantee regarding the content specified in paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law.

Article 37 If a witness or expert gives written consent to testify or assist in an investigation, the case-handling authority shall promptly notify the liaison authority of the witness's or expert's wishes, requirements, and conditions through its competent authority, and the liaison authority shall notify the requesting state.

When arranging for a witness or expert to testify via video or audio, the competent authority or the case-handling authority shall send personnel to be present. If any circumstances are found that harm the sovereignty, security, or social public interests of the People's Republic of China or violate the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China, they shall promptly stop the proceedings.

Article 38 If a foreign country requests the transfer of a person in custody to go abroad to testify or assist in an investigation, and guarantees that the person in custody will be promptly returned after the testimony or assistance in the investigation is completed, the liaison authority shall seek the opinions of the competent authority and the person in custody. If both the competent authority and the person in custody consent to going abroad to testify or assist in the investigation, the liaison authority, together with the competent authority, shall reach an agreement in advance with the requesting state on matters related to the transfer of the person in custody.

The period during which the person in custody is detained in the foreign country shall be deducted from the sentence imposed in the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter VI Sealing, Seizing, and Freezing of Property Involved in a Case

### Section 1 Requesting Foreign Countries to Seal, Seize, or Freeze Property Involved in a Case**Article 39** If an investigating authority needs foreign assistance in sealing, seizing, or freezing property involved in a case, it shall prepare a request for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After review and approval by its competent authority, the request shall be promptly submitted to the foreign state through the external liaison authority.

If the foreign state has special requirements for assisting in the execution of a request from the People's Republic of China to seal, seize, or freeze property involved in a case, such requirements may be accepted provided they do not violate the fundamental principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China. If a decision by a judicial authority is required, the People's Court shall make the decision.

**Article 40** When requesting a foreign state to seal, seize, or freeze property involved in a case, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, specify the following:

(1) The ownership documents, name, characteristics, form, and quantity of the property involved in the case that needs to be sealed, seized, or frozen;
(2) The location of the property involved in the case that needs to be sealed, seized, or frozen. If funds or other financial assets are held in a financial institution, the name, address, and account information of the financial institution shall be stated;
(3) Copies of relevant legal documents;
(4) Legal provisions concerning sealing, seizure, freezing, and the protection of the rights of interested parties;
(5) Other materials helpful for executing the request.

**Article 41** If the period for sealing, seizure, or freezing determined by the foreign state expires, and the investigating authority needs the foreign state to continue sealing, seizing, or freezing the relevant property involved in the case, it shall submit a new request to the foreign state.

If the investigating authority decides to lift the sealing, seizure, or freezing, it shall promptly notify the requested state.

### Section 2 Requests to the People's Republic of China for Sealing, Seizing, or Freezing Property Involved in a Case

**Article 42** A foreign state may request the People's Republic of China to assist in sealing, seizing, or freezing property involved in a case located within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

When a foreign state requests the People's Republic of China to seal, seize, or freeze property involved in a case, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, specify the matters stipulated in Article 40 of this Law.

**Article 43** The competent authority, upon review, may agree to the sealing, seizure, or freezing of the property involved in a case if the following conditions are met, and shall arrange for the relevant investigating authority to execute the request:

(1) The sealing, seizure, or freezing complies with the conditions stipulated by the laws of the People's Republic of China;
(2) The sealing, seizure, or freezing of the property involved in the case is related to the investigation, inquiry, prosecution, and trial activities of the ongoing criminal case in the requesting state;
(3) The property involved in the case is subject to sealing, seizure, or freezing;
(4) Executing the request does not affect the lawful rights and interests of interested parties;
(5) Executing the request does not affect the ongoing investigation, inquiry, prosecution, trial, and execution activities of the relevant authorities of the People's Republic of China.

The investigating authority shall promptly notify the external liaison authority through the competent authority, and the external liaison authority shall inform the requesting state of the results of the sealing, seizure, or freezing. When necessary, the investigating authority may take measures to dispose of the sealed, seized, or frozen property involved in the case in accordance with the law.

**Article 44** If the period for sealing, seizure, or freezing expires, and the foreign state needs to continue sealing, seizing, or freezing the relevant property involved in the case, it shall submit a new request to the external liaison authority.

If the foreign state decides to lift the sealing, seizure, or freezing, the external liaison authority shall, through the competent authority, notify the investigating authority to lift them promptly.

**Article 45** If an interested party raises an objection to the sealing, seizure, or freezing, and the investigating authority, upon review, finds that the sealing, seizure, or freezing does not meet the conditions stipulated in paragraph 1 of Article 43 of this Law, it shall report to the competent authority for a decision to lift the sealing, seizure, or freezing and notify the external liaison authority, which shall inform the requesting state. If the objection pertains to the handling of the case, the investigating authority may transmit the objection through its competent authority to the external liaison authority, which shall raise it with the requesting state.

**Article 46** If improper sealing, seizure, or freezing due to reasons attributable to the requesting state causes damage to the lawful rights and interests of an interested party, the investigating authority may, through the external liaison authority, request the requesting state to bear compensation liability.

## Chapter 7 Confiscation and Return of Illegal Gains and Other Property Involved in a Case

### Section 1 Requests to Foreign States for Confiscation and Return of Illegal Gains and Other Property Involved in a Case

**Article 47** If an investigating authority needs foreign assistance in confiscating illegal gains and other property involved in a case, it shall prepare a request for criminal judicial assistance along with relevant materials. After review and approval by its competent authority, the request shall be promptly submitted to the foreign state through the external liaison authority.

A request for the foreign state to return illegal gains and other property involved in a case to the People's Republic of China or to the victim may be made together with the request for confiscation, or separately.

If the foreign state has special requirements for the return of sealed, seized, or frozen illegal gains and other property involved in a case, such requirements may be accepted provided they do not violate the fundamental principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China. If a decision by a judicial authority is required, the People's Court shall make the decision.

**Article 48** When requesting a foreign state to confiscate or return illegal gains and other property involved in a case, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, specify the following:

(1) The name, characteristics, form, and quantity of the illegal gains and other property involved in the case that need to be confiscated or returned;
(2) The location of the illegal gains and other property involved in the case that need to be confiscated or returned. If funds or other financial assets are held in a financial institution, the name, address, and account information of the financial institution shall be stated;
(3) The reasons for confiscation or return and relevant ownership documents;
(4) Copies of relevant legal documents;
(5) Legal provisions concerning confiscation, return, and the protection of the rights of interested parties;
(6) Other materials helpful for executing the request.

**Article 49** When a foreign state assists in the confiscation or return of illegal gains and other property involved in a case, the external liaison authority, in conjunction with the competent authority, shall negotiate with the foreign state regarding the transfer of the property.

If the foreign state proposes a request for sharing regarding the assistance in confiscating or returning illegal gains and other property involved in a case, the amount or proportion of the share shall be determined through negotiation between the external liaison authority, in conjunction with the competent authority, and the foreign state.

### Section 2 Requests to the People's Republic of China for Confiscation and Return of Illegal Gains and Other Property Involved in a Case

**Article 50** A foreign state may request the People's Republic of China to assist in the confiscation or return of illegal gains and other property involved in a case.

When a foreign state requests the People's Republic of China to assist in the confiscation or return of illegal gains and other property involved in a case, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, specify the matters stipulated in Article 48 of this Law.

**Article 51** The competent authority, upon review, may agree to assist in the confiscation of illegal gains and other property involved in a case if the following conditions are met, and shall arrange for the relevant investigating authority to execute the request:

(1) The confiscation of illegal gains and other property involved in the case complies with the conditions stipulated by the laws of the People's Republic of China;
(2) The foreign state has fully protected the relevant rights of interested parties;
(3) There is property available for execution within the People's Republic of China;
(4) The request letter and attached materials provide a detailed description of the ownership, name, characteristics, form, and quantity of the property targeted by the request;
(5) The confiscation cannot be executed or cannot be fully executed in the requesting state;
(6) Other conditions that the competent authority deems necessary.

**Article 52** If a foreign state requests assistance in confiscating illegal gains and other property involved in a case, and any of the following circumstances exists, assistance may be refused, and reasons shall be provided:

(1) A judicial authority of the People's Republic of China or a third state has already rendered a final judgment on the property targeted by the request, and the judgment has been fully executed or is in the process of execution;
(2) The property targeted by the request does not exist, has been damaged, destroyed, sold, or transferred, making execution impossible, except where the request is for the confiscation of the proceeds of the sale or the transferred property;
(3) The person targeted by the request has outstanding debts or pending litigation within the territory of the People's Republic of China;
(4) Other circumstances under which assistance may be refused.

**Article 53** If a foreign state requests the return of illegal gains and other property involved in a case and can provide sufficient and reliable evidence, the competent authority, upon review, may agree if it complies with the conditions stipulated by the laws of the People's Republic of China, and shall arrange for the relevant investigating authority to execute the request. Before the return, the investigating authority may deduct reasonable expenses incurred in executing the request.

**Article 54** Regarding a foreign state's request for assistance in confiscating or returning illegal gains and other property involved in a case, the external liaison authority, in conjunction with the competent authority, may propose a request for sharing. The amount or proportion of the share shall be determined through negotiation between the external liaison authority, in conjunction with the competent authority, and the foreign state.

## Chapter 8 Transfer of Sentenced Persons

### Section 1 Transfer of Sentenced Persons to Foreign States

**Article 55** A foreign state may request the People's Republic of China to transfer a foreign national who has been sentenced, and the People's Republic of China may request a foreign state to transfer a foreign national who has been sentenced.

**Article 56** The transfer of a sentenced person to a foreign state shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The sentenced person is a national of that state;
(2) The conduct for which the sentence was imposed also constitutes a crime under the laws of that state;
(3) The judgment imposing the sentence on the sentenced person has become legally effective;
(4) The sentenced person has given written consent to the transfer, or, if necessary due to the sentenced person's age, physical or mental condition, etc., their agent has given written consent to the transfer;
(5) Both the People's Republic of China and that state agree to the transfer.

The transfer may be refused under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The sentenced person has been sentenced to death with a two-year suspension of execution or life imprisonment, unless the sentence has been commuted to fixed-term imprisonment at the time of the transfer request;
(2) At the time of the transfer request, the remaining sentence of the sentenced person is less than one year;
(3) The sentenced person has pending litigation within the territory of the People's Republic of China;
(4) Other circumstances under which transfer is not appropriate.

**Article 57** When requesting the transfer of a sentenced person to a foreign state, the request letter and attached materials shall, as necessary, specify the following:

(1) The name of the requesting authority;
(2) The name, gender, nationality, identity information, and other details of the sentenced person to be transferred;
(3) The place of detention of the sentenced person;
(4) The basis and reasons for the transfer request;
(5) The written statement of consent to the transfer from the sentenced person or their agent;
(6) Other matters.

**Article 58** The competent authority shall verify the willingness of the sentenced person to be transferred. If the foreign state requests to send personnel to verify the willingness of the sentenced person to be transferred, the competent authority may make arrangements.

**Article 59** If a foreign state submits a request to the People's Republic of China for the transfer of a sentenced person, or if the competent authority deems it necessary to submit a request to a foreign state for the transfer of a sentenced person, the competent authority shall, in conjunction with the relevant competent departments, decide whether to agree to the foreign state's request or to submit a request to the foreign state. After deciding to agree to the foreign state's transfer request, the external liaison authority shall notify the requesting state and the sentenced person in writing.

**Article 60** The transfer of a sentenced person shall be executed by the penal enforcement authority designated by the competent authority. Matters of execution such as the time, place, and method of the transfer of the sentenced person shall be determined through negotiation between the competent authority and the foreign state.

**Article 61** If a sentenced person files an appeal against the original effective judgment after the transfer, it shall be filed with the People's Court of the People's Republic of China that has jurisdiction.

If the People's Court modifies or revokes the original effective judgment, it shall promptly notify the foreign state.

### Section 2 Transfer of Sentenced Persons to the People's Republic of China

**Article 62** The People's Republic of China may request a foreign state to transfer a Chinese national who has been sentenced, and a foreign state may request the People's Republic of China to transfer a Chinese national who has been sentenced. The specific conditions and procedures for the transfer shall be implemented with reference to the relevant provisions of Section 1 of this Chapter.

**Article 63** After a sentenced person is transferred back to China, they shall be temporarily detained by the penal enforcement authority designated by the competent authority.

**Article 64** The People's Procuratorate shall prepare an application for sentence conversion along with relevant materials and submit it to the Intermediate People's Court at the location of the penal enforcement authority for a ruling on sentence conversion.

The People's Court shall render a ruling on sentence conversion based on the facts established by the judgment of the foreign court and in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Law. If the nature and duration of the sentence imposed by the foreign court comply with the laws of the People's Republic of China, the conversion shall follow the imposed sentence and duration. If the nature and duration of the sentence imposed by the foreign court do not comply with the laws of the People's Republic of China, the type and term of the sentence shall be determined according to the following principles:

(1) The converted sentence shall, as far as possible, be consistent with the sentence imposed by the foreign court;
(2) The converted sentence shall not be more severe in nature or longer in term than the sentence imposed by the foreign court, nor shall it exceed the maximum sentence stipulated by the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China for a similar crime;
(3) A sentence of deprivation of liberty shall not be converted into a property penalty;
(4) The converted sentence shall not be bound by the minimum sentence stipulated by the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China for a similar crime.

If the sentenced person was detained before returning to China to serve the sentence, each day of detention shall be counted as one day against the converted sentence.

The ruling on sentence conversion made by the People's Court is a final ruling.

**Article 65** The penal enforcement authority shall, based on the ruling on sentence conversion, take the sentenced person transferred back to China into custody to execute the sentence. The execution of the sentence, as well as commutation, parole, temporary service of sentence outside prison, etc., shall be handled in accordance with the laws of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 66** An appeal against the judgment of the foreign court by a sentenced person transferred back to China shall be filed with the court of the foreign state that has jurisdiction.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 67** Criminal judicial assistance between the People's Republic of China and relevant international organizations shall be implemented with reference to the provisions of this Law.

**Article 68** Requests for criminal judicial assistance submitted to the People's Republic of China, or documents and evidentiary materials provided in response to a request from the People's Republic of China, shall be notarized and authenticated in accordance with the provisions of the treaty. If there is no treaty or the treaty has no provisions, it shall be handled in accordance with the principle of reciprocity.

**Article 69** The term "criminal judicial assistance treaties" as used in this Law refers to treaties on criminal judicial assistance, treaties on the transfer of sentenced persons, or other treaties containing provisions on criminal judicial assistance or the transfer of sentenced persons, concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China with foreign states.

**Article 70** This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
