# People's Mediation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on August 28, 2010

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1: This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to improve the people's mediation system, regulate people's mediation activities, promptly resolve civil disputes, and maintain social harmony and stability.

Article 2: For the purposes of this Law, "people's mediation" refers to the activities in which a people's mediation committee, through methods such as persuasion and guidance, facilitates the parties to voluntarily reach a mediation agreement on the basis of equal consultation, thereby resolving civil disputes.

Article 3: When mediating civil disputes, a people's mediation committee shall follow the following principles:

(1) Conduct mediation on the basis of the parties' voluntariness and equality;

(2) Not violate laws, regulations, and state policies;

(3) Respect the rights of the parties, and not prevent the parties from safeguarding their rights through arbitration, administrative, judicial, or other channels in accordance with the law due to mediation.

Article 4: A people's mediation committee shall not charge any fees for mediating civil disputes.

Article 5: The judicial administrative department of the State Council is responsible for guiding the people's mediation work nationwide, and the judicial administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for guiding the people's mediation work within their respective administrative regions.

Basic people's courts provide professional guidance to people's mediation committees in mediating civil disputes.

Article 6: The State encourages and supports people's mediation work. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide necessary financial support and guarantees for people's mediation work, and shall commend and reward people's mediation committees and people's mediators with outstanding contributions in accordance with state regulations.

## Chapter II: People's Mediation Committees

Article 7: A people's mediation committee is a mass organization established in accordance with the law to mediate civil disputes.

Article 8: Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall establish people's mediation committees. Enterprises and public institutions may establish people's mediation committees as needed.

A people's mediation committee shall consist of three to nine members, with one director, and if necessary, several deputy directors may be appointed.

A people's mediation committee shall include female members, and in areas where multiple ethnic groups reside, it shall include members from the smaller ethnic groups.

Article 9: The members of the people's mediation committee of a villagers' committee or residents' committee shall be elected by the villagers' meeting, the villagers' representative meeting, or the residents' meeting; the members of the people's mediation committee established by an enterprise or public institution shall be elected by the workers' congress, the workers' representative congress, or the trade union organization.

The term of office for members of a people's mediation committee is three years, and they may be re-elected consecutively.

Article 10: The judicial administrative department of a people's government at the county level shall compile statistics on the establishment of people's mediation committees within its administrative region, and shall promptly notify the relevant basic people's court of the establishment, composition, and adjustments of the people's mediation committees.

Article 11: A people's mediation committee shall establish and improve various mediation work systems, listen to public opinions, and accept public supervision.

Article 12: Villagers' committees, residents' committees, and enterprises and public institutions shall provide office conditions and necessary working funds for the people's mediation committee to carry out its work.

## Chapter III: People's Mediators

Article 13: People's mediators shall be composed of members of the people's mediation committee and personnel appointed by the people's mediation committee.

Article 14: People's mediators shall be adult citizens who are fair and upright, enthusiastic about people's mediation work, and possess a certain level of education, policy knowledge, and legal knowledge.

The judicial administrative department of a people's government at the county level shall regularly provide professional training to people's mediators.

Article 15: If a people's mediator commits any of the following acts during mediation work, the people's mediation committee to which they belong shall give them criticism and education, and order them to correct; if the circumstances are serious, the unit that elected or appointed them shall remove or dismiss them:

(1) Showing partiality towards one party;

(2) Insulting a party;

(3) Extorting, accepting property, or seeking other illegitimate benefits;

(4) Disclosing a party's personal privacy or trade secrets.

Article 16: People's mediators engaged in mediation work shall receive appropriate compensation for lost work time; if they are injured or disabled due to mediation work and face living difficulties, the local people's government shall provide necessary medical and living assistance; if a people's mediator dies in the line of duty, their spouse and children shall receive pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with state regulations.

## Chapter IV: Mediation Procedures

Article 17: A party may apply to a people's mediation committee for mediation; a people's mediation committee may also initiate mediation on its own. If one party explicitly refuses mediation, mediation shall not be conducted.

Article 18: For disputes suitable for resolution through people's mediation, basic people's courts and public security organs may, before accepting the case, inform the parties to apply to a people's mediation committee for mediation.

Article 19: Based on the needs of the dispute, a people's mediation committee may designate one or several people's mediators to conduct the mediation, or the parties may choose one or several people's mediators to conduct the mediation.

Article 20: Based on the needs of the dispute, and with the consent of the parties, a people's mediator may invite the parties' relatives, neighbors, colleagues, etc., to participate in the mediation, or may invite persons with specialized knowledge, specific experience, or personnel from relevant social organizations to participate in the mediation.

People's mediation committees support local fair and upright individuals who are enthusiastic about mediation and recognized by the public to participate in mediation.

Article 21: When mediating civil disputes, people's mediators shall adhere to principles, clarify the law and reasoning, and uphold justice.

Mediation of civil disputes shall be conducted promptly and on-site to prevent the escalation of conflicts.

Article 22: Depending on the circumstances of the dispute, people's mediators may adopt various methods to mediate civil disputes, fully listen to the parties' statements, explain relevant laws, regulations, and state policies, patiently guide, propose dispute resolution plans based on equal consultation and mutual understanding and accommodation, and help the parties voluntarily reach a mediation agreement.

Article 23: Parties in people's mediation activities enjoy the following rights:

(1) To choose or accept a people's mediator;

(2) To accept mediation, refuse mediation, or request termination of mediation;

(3) To request that mediation be conducted openly or not openly;

(4) To freely express their will and voluntarily reach a mediation agreement.

Article 24: Parties in people's mediation activities shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Truthfully state the facts of the dispute;

(2) Comply with the order of the mediation site and respect the people's mediator;

(3) Respect the other party's exercise of their rights.

Article 25: If, during the mediation of a dispute, a people's mediator discovers that the dispute may escalate, they shall take targeted preventive measures; if the dispute may lead to a public security case or criminal case, they shall promptly report to the local public security organ or other relevant departments.

Article 26: If a people's mediator fails to mediate a dispute, they shall terminate the mediation and, in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, inform the parties that they may safeguard their rights through arbitration, administrative, judicial, or other channels in accordance with the law.

Article 27: People's mediators shall record the mediation process. People's mediation committees shall establish mediation work files, and file and archive materials such as mediation registration, mediation work records, and mediation agreements.

## Chapter V: Mediation Agreements

Article 28: If a mediation agreement is reached through the mediation of a people's mediation committee, a written mediation agreement may be prepared. If the parties deem it unnecessary to prepare a written mediation agreement, an oral agreement may be adopted, and the people's mediator shall record the content of the agreement.

Article 29: A written mediation agreement may include the following items:

(1) Basic information of the parties;

(2) The main facts of the dispute, the matters in dispute, and the responsibilities of each party;

(3) The content of the mediation agreement reached by the parties, and the method and time limit for performance.

The mediation agreement shall take effect from the date when all parties sign, affix their seals, or affix their fingerprints, and the people's mediator signs and affixes the seal of the people's mediation committee. Each party shall hold one copy of the mediation agreement, and the people's mediation committee shall retain one copy.

Article 30: An oral mediation agreement shall take effect from the date when all parties reach the agreement.

Article 31: A mediation agreement reached through the mediation of a people's mediation committee has legal binding force, and the parties shall perform it in accordance with the agreement.

The people's mediation committee shall supervise the performance of the mediation agreement and urge the parties to fulfill their agreed obligations.

Article 32: After a mediation agreement is reached through the mediation of a people's mediation committee, if a dispute arises between the parties regarding the performance or content of the mediation agreement, one party may file a lawsuit with a people's court.

Article 33: After a mediation agreement is reached through the mediation of a people's mediation committee, if both parties deem it necessary, they may jointly apply to a people's court for judicial confirmation within thirty days from the date the mediation agreement takes effect. The people's court shall promptly review the mediation agreement and confirm its validity in accordance with the law.

If a people's court confirms the validity of the mediation agreement in accordance with the law, and one party refuses to perform or fails to fully perform it, the other party may apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

If a people's court confirms the invalidity of the mediation agreement in accordance with the law, the parties may amend the original mediation agreement or reach a new mediation agreement through people's mediation, or may file a lawsuit with a people's court.

## Chapter VI: Supplementary Provisions

Article 34: Townships, subdistricts, social organizations, or other organizations may, as needed, establish people's mediation committees by reference to the relevant provisions of this Law to mediate civil disputes.

Article 35: This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2011.
