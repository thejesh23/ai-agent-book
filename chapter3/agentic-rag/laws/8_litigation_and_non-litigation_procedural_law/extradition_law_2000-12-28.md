# Extradition Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 28, 2000

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of ensuring the normal progress of extradition, strengthening international cooperation in punishing crime, protecting the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations, and safeguarding national interests and social order.

Article 2 Extradition between the People's Republic of China and foreign states shall be conducted in accordance with this Law.

Article 3 The People's Republic of China shall cooperate with foreign states in extradition on the basis of equality and mutual benefit.

Extradition cooperation shall not impair the sovereignty, security and social public interests of the People's Republic of China.

Article 4 Extradition between the People's Republic of China and foreign states shall be handled through diplomatic channels. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China is designated as the authority for handling extradition matters.

Where an extradition treaty has special provisions on the authority for handling extradition matters, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 5 In handling extradition cases, compulsory measures such as extradition detention, extradition arrest or extradition residential surveillance may be taken against the person sought, depending on the circumstances.

Article 6 For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings assigned to them:

(1) "Person sought" means a person whom the requesting state requests the requested state to extradite;

(2) "Person extradited" means a person who is extradited from the requested state to the requesting state;

(3) "Extradition treaty" means an extradition treaty concluded between the People's Republic of China and a foreign state, or any other treaty to which the People's Republic of China and a foreign state are both parties and which contains provisions on extradition.

## Chapter II Requests for Extradition to the People's Republic of China

### Section 1 Conditions for Extradition

Article 7 A request for extradition made by a foreign state to the People's Republic of China may be granted only if all of the following conditions are met:

(1) The conduct for which extradition is requested constitutes a crime under the laws of both the People's Republic of China and the requesting state;

(2) Where the extradition is requested for the purpose of instituting criminal proceedings, the crime for which extradition is requested is punishable under the laws of both the People's Republic of China and the requesting state by imprisonment for a term of at least one year or by a more severe penalty; where the extradition is requested for the purpose of executing a sentence, the person sought has at least six months of the sentence remaining to be served at the time the request is made.

Where the request for extradition involves multiple crimes that meet the condition specified in subparagraph (1) of the preceding paragraph, extradition may be granted for all such crimes if at least one of them meets the condition specified in subparagraph (2) of the preceding paragraph.

Article 8 A request for extradition made by a foreign state to the People's Republic of China shall be refused in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The person sought has the nationality of the People's Republic of China under the laws of the People's Republic of China;

(2) At the time the request for extradition is received, the judicial organs of the People's Republic of China have already rendered a final judgment or have terminated the criminal proceedings in respect of the crime for which extradition is requested;

(3) The extradition is requested for a political offence, or the People's Republic of China has granted the person sought the right of asylum;

(4) The person sought may be subjected to criminal proceedings or punishment by reason of his or her race, religion, nationality, gender, political opinion or status, or the person sought may be treated unfairly in judicial proceedings for any of the above reasons;

(5) The crime for which extradition is requested is a purely military offence under the laws of the People's Republic of China or the requesting state;

(6) Under the laws of the People's Republic of China or the requesting state, criminal liability of the person sought should not be pursued because the statute of limitations for the crime has expired or the person sought has been pardoned, or for other reasons, at the time the request for extradition is received;

(7) The person sought has been or may be subjected to torture or other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment in the requesting state;

(8) The requesting state makes the request for extradition on the basis of a judgment rendered in absentia, unless the requesting state undertakes to give the person sought an opportunity for a retrial in his or her presence after extradition.

Article 9 A request for extradition made by a foreign state to the People's Republic of China may be refused in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The People's Republic of China has criminal jurisdiction over the crime for which extradition is requested and is conducting criminal proceedings against the person sought or is preparing to institute such proceedings;

(2) Due to the age, health or other reasons of the person sought, extradition is inappropriate on humanitarian grounds.

### Section 2 Submission of Requests for Extradition

Article 10 A request for extradition by a requesting state shall be submitted to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China.

Article 11 A requesting state requesting extradition shall issue a written request, which shall specify:

(1) The name of the requesting authority;

(2) The name, sex, age, nationality, type and number of identity document, occupation, physical characteristics, domicile and residence of the person sought, and other information helpful in identifying and locating the person;

(3) The facts of the crime, including the time, place, conduct and results of the crime;

(4) The legal provisions on the conviction and sentencing of the crime and the statute of limitations for prosecution.

Article 12 A requesting state requesting extradition shall, at the same time as issuing the written request, provide the following materials:

(1) Where the extradition is requested for the purpose of instituting criminal proceedings, a copy of the arrest warrant or other document of equivalent effect shall be attached; where the extradition is requested for the purpose of executing a sentence, a copy of the legally effective judgment or order shall be attached, and if part of the sentence has already been served, a certificate of the period of sentence already served shall also be attached;

(2) Necessary evidence or evidentiary materials of the crime.

If the requesting state has photographs, fingerprints or other materials of the person sought that can be used to confirm his or her identity, such materials shall be provided.

Article 13 The written request for extradition or other relevant documents submitted by a requesting state under this Section shall be duly signed or sealed by the competent authority of the requesting state and shall be accompanied by a translation into Chinese or into another language agreed to by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China.

Article 14 A requesting state requesting extradition shall give the following undertakings:

(1) The requesting state shall not prosecute the person extradited for any other crime committed before extradition that was not approved for extradition, nor shall it re-extradite the person to a third state, unless with the consent of the People's Republic of China, or unless the person extradited fails to leave the requesting state within thirty days after the conclusion of the proceedings for the crime for which extradition was granted, the completion of the sentence, or release on parole, or leaves and voluntarily returns thereafter;

(2) If the requesting state withdraws or abandons the request for extradition after making it, or if the request for extradition is erroneous, the requesting state shall bear the responsibility for any damage caused to the person sought as a result of the request for extradition.

Article 15 In the absence of an extradition treaty, the requesting state shall give an undertaking of reciprocity.

### Section 3 Review of Requests for Extradition

Article 16 After receiving a request for extradition from a requesting state, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall review whether the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials comply with the provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter and the extradition treaty.

The Higher People's Court designated by the Supreme People's Court shall review whether the request for extradition made by the requesting state complies with the provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty regarding the conditions for extradition, and shall render a ruling. The Supreme People's Court shall review the ruling made by the Higher People's Court.

Article 17 Where two or more states request the extradition of the same person for the same or different acts, the priority of accepting the requests for extradition shall be determined by taking into account factors such as the order in which the People's Republic of China received the requests and whether the People's Republic of China has an extradition treaty relationship with the requesting states.

Article 18 If, after reviewing a request for extradition made by a requesting state, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs finds that it does not comply with the provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter and the extradition treaty, it may request the requesting state to provide supplementary materials within thirty days. Upon the request of the requesting state, the above time limit may be extended by fifteen days.

If the requesting state fails to provide supplementary materials within the above time limit, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall terminate the extradition case. The requesting state may again request the extradition of the person for the same crime.

Article 19 If, after reviewing a request for extradition made by a requesting state, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs finds that it complies with the provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter and the extradition treaty, it shall transmit the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials to the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

Article 20 If the person sought has already been placed under extradition detention before the foreign state makes a formal request for extradition, the Supreme People's Court shall, upon receiving the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials, promptly transmit them to the relevant Higher People's Court for review.

If the person sought has not been placed under extradition detention before the foreign state makes a formal request for extradition, the Supreme People's Court shall, upon receiving the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials, notify the Ministry of Public Security to locate the person sought. After the public security organ locates the person sought, it shall place the person sought under extradition detention or extradition residential surveillance, as the case may be, and the Ministry of Public Security shall notify the Supreme People's Court. Upon receiving the notification from the Ministry of Public Security, the Supreme People's Court shall promptly transmit the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials to the relevant Higher People's Court for review.

If, after searching, the public security organ confirms that the person sought is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China or cannot be located, the Ministry of Public Security shall promptly notify the Supreme People's Court. Upon receiving the notification from the Ministry of Public Security, the Supreme People's Court shall promptly notify the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the search results, and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall notify the requesting state.

Article 21 If, after review, the Supreme People's Procuratorate finds that the crime for which extradition is requested or other crimes of the person sought should be prosecuted by the judicial organs of our country but criminal proceedings have not yet been instituted, it shall, within one month from the date of receiving the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials, inform the Supreme People's Court and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs respectively of its intention to prepare to institute criminal proceedings.

Article 22 The Higher People's Court shall review the request for extradition made by the requesting state in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty regarding the conditions for extradition, and shall conduct the review by a collegial panel composed of three judges.

Article 23 When reviewing an extradition case, the Higher People's Court shall hear the statements of the person sought and the opinions of the Chinese lawyer entrusted by him or her. The Higher People's Court shall, within ten days from the date of receiving the written request for extradition transmitted by the Supreme People's Court, send a copy of the written request for extradition to the person sought. The person sought shall submit his or her opinions within thirty days from the date of receipt.

Article 24 After review, the Higher People's Court shall render one of the following rulings:

(1) If it finds that the request for extradition made by the requesting state complies with the provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty, it shall render a ruling that the conditions for extradition are met. If the person sought falls under the circumstances for deferred extradition as provided in Article 42 of this Law, this shall be noted in the ruling;

(2) If it finds that the request for extradition made by the requesting state does not comply with the provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty, it shall render a ruling of non-extradition.

At the request of the requesting state, and provided that it does not affect other ongoing proceedings within the territory of the People's Republic of China or infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of any third party within the territory of the People's Republic of China, a ruling may be made simultaneously with the ruling that the conditions for extradition are met, ordering the transfer of property related to the case.

Article 25 After rendering a ruling that the conditions for extradition are met or a ruling of non-extradition, the Higher People's Court shall read the ruling to the person sought and, within seven days from the date of rendering the ruling, submit the written ruling together with the relevant materials to the Supreme People's Court for review.

If the person sought disagrees with the ruling of the Higher People's Court that the conditions for extradition are met, the person sought and the Chinese lawyer entrusted by him or her may submit opinions to the Supreme People's Court within ten days from the date the People's Court reads the ruling to the person sought.

Article 26 The Supreme People's Court, when reviewing the ruling of the Higher People's Court, shall handle the matter according to the following circumstances:

(1) If it finds that the ruling made by the Higher People's Court complies with the provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty, it shall approve the ruling of the Higher People's Court;

(2) If it finds that the ruling made by the Higher People's Court does not comply with the provisions of this Law and the extradition treaty, it may quash the ruling and remand the case to the original People's Court for a new review, or it may directly render a modified ruling.

Article 27 During the review process, the People's Court may, when necessary, request the requesting state through the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to provide supplementary materials within thirty days.

Article 28 After the Supreme People's Court renders a ruling of approval or modification, it shall, within seven days from the date of rendering the ruling, send the written ruling to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and simultaneously serve it on the person sought.

If the Supreme People's Court approves a ruling of non-extradition or renders such a ruling, it shall immediately notify the public security organ to release the compulsory measures taken against the person sought.

Article 29 Upon receiving a ruling of non-extradition from the Supreme People's Court, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall promptly notify the requesting state.

Upon receiving a ruling from the Supreme People's Court that the conditions for extradition are met, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall submit the matter to the State Council for a decision on whether to extradite.

If the State Council decides not to extradite, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall promptly notify the requesting state. The People's Court shall immediately notify the public security organ to release the compulsory measures taken against the person sought.

### Section 4 Compulsory Measures for Extradition

Article 30 Before a foreign state formally submits a request for extradition, if it applies for the taking of custodial measures against the person to be sought due to urgency, the public security organ may take extradition detention measures based on the application of the foreign state.

The application referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be submitted in writing through diplomatic channels or to the Ministry of Public Security, and shall specify:

(1) The contents specified in Articles 11 and 14 of this Law;

(2) A statement that the materials referred to in subparagraph (1) of Article 12 of this Law are already available;

(3) A statement that a formal request for extradition will be submitted soon.

If the application is submitted through diplomatic channels, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall promptly transmit the application to the Ministry of Public Security. If the application is submitted to the Ministry of Public Security, the Ministry of Public Security shall notify the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the relevant information about the application.

Article 31 Where the public security organ takes extradition detention measures against the person sought in accordance with Article 30 of this Law, if the application was submitted to the Ministry of Public Security, the Ministry of Public Security shall promptly notify the other party of the implementation; if the application was submitted through diplomatic channels, the Ministry of Public Security shall notify the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the implementation, and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall promptly notify the requesting state. When notifying through the above channels, if the person sought has been placed under extradition detention, the time limit for submitting a formal request for extradition shall also be informed.

If the Ministry of Foreign Affairs does not receive a formal request for extradition from the foreign state within thirty days after the public security organ takes extradition detention measures, the extradition detention shall be revoked. Upon the request of the foreign state, the above time limit may be extended by fifteen days.

If extradition detention is revoked in accordance with the second paragraph of this Article, the requesting state may subsequently formally request the extradition of the person for the same crime.

Article 32 After receiving the written request for extradition and the attached documents and materials, the Higher People's Court shall, if the failure to take extradition arrest measures may affect the normal progress of extradition, promptly decide on extradition arrest. If extradition arrest measures are not taken against the person sought, it shall promptly decide on extradition residential surveillance.

Article 33 Extradition detention, extradition arrest, and extradition residential surveillance shall be executed by the public security organ.

Article 34 The authority taking extradition compulsory measures shall interrogate the person subjected to such measures within twenty-four hours after taking the measures.

A person subjected to extradition compulsory measures may, from the date of being subjected to such measures, retain a Chinese lawyer to provide legal assistance. When executing extradition compulsory measures, the public security organ shall inform the person subjected to such measures of the above rights.

Article 35 If the person sought who should be subjected to extradition arrest suffers from a serious illness, or is a pregnant woman or a woman nursing her own infant, extradition residential surveillance measures may be taken instead.

Article 36 After the State Council decides to grant extradition, it shall promptly notify the Supreme People's Court. If the person sought has not yet been placed under extradition arrest, the People's Court shall immediately decide on extradition arrest.

Article 37 If a foreign state withdraws or abandons its request for extradition, the extradition compulsory measures taken against the person sought shall be immediately released.

### Section 5 Execution of Extradition

Article 38 Extradition shall be executed by the public security organ. If the State Council decides to grant extradition, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall promptly notify the Ministry of Public Security and inform the requesting state to agree with the Ministry of Public Security on the time, place, manner and other matters related to the execution of the extradition for the transfer of the person sought.

Article 39 When executing extradition in accordance with Article 38 of this Law, the public security organ shall, based on the ruling of the People's Court, transfer to the requesting state the property related to the case.

If extradition cannot be executed due to the death, escape or other reasons of the person sought, the above property may also be transferred to the requesting state.

Article 40 If the requesting state fails to take custody of the person sought within fifteen days from the agreed date of transfer, it shall be deemed to have automatically abandoned the request for extradition. The public security organ shall immediately release the person sought, and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs may no longer accept a request from that state for the extradition of the same person for the same crime.

If the requesting state is unable to take custody of the person sought within the above time limit due to reasons beyond its control, it may apply for an extension of the time limit, but the extension shall not exceed thirty days at most, or it may renegotiate the transfer matters in accordance with Article 38 of this Law.

Article 41 If the person extradited flees back to the People's Republic of China before the conclusion of the criminal proceedings or the completion of the sentence in the requesting state, re-extradition may be granted based on the same request for extradition made again by the requesting state, without the need for the requesting state to submit the documents and materials specified in Section 2 of this Chapter.

### Section 6 Deferred Extradition and Temporary Extradition

Article 42 When the State Council decides to grant extradition, if the judicial organs of the People's Republic of China are conducting criminal proceedings against the person sought for other crimes or are executing a sentence, it may simultaneously decide to defer extradition.

Article 43 If deferring extradition may cause serious obstacles to the criminal proceedings of the requesting state, and provided that it does not hinder the ongoing criminal proceedings within the territory of the People's Republic of China and the requesting state undertakes to return the person unconditionally immediately after the completion of the relevant proceedings, the person may be temporarily extradited upon the request of the requesting state.

The decision on temporary extradition shall be made by the State Council after obtaining the consent of the Supreme People's Court or the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

### Section 7 Transit in Extradition

Article 44 If extradition between foreign states requires transit through the territory of the People's Republic of China, a request for transit shall be submitted in accordance with Article 4 of this Law and the relevant provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter.

If transit is by air and there is no plan to land within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply; however, if an unscheduled landing occurs, a request for transit shall be submitted in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 45 For a request for transit submitted by a foreign state, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall review it in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law and decide whether to grant or refuse transit.The decision to grant or deny transit shall be notified by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to the requesting state through the same channel through which the request was received.

After the Ministry of Foreign Affairs decides to grant transit, it shall promptly notify the Ministry of Public Security of the decision. The time, place, and manner of transit shall be determined by the Ministry of Public Security.

Article 46: The transit of extradition shall be supervised or assisted by the public security organs of the place of transit.

Public security organs may, upon the request of the requesting state, provide temporary detention facilities.

## Chapter 3: Requesting Extradition from Foreign States

Article 47: Where a request is to be made to a foreign state for extradition or transit for extradition, the judicial, procuratorial, public security, state security, or prison administration authorities of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government responsible for handling the relevant case shall submit their opinions to the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the Ministry of Public Security, the Ministry of State Security, or the Ministry of Justice, respectively, together with relevant documents and materials and their certified translations. After the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the Ministry of Public Security, the Ministry of State Security, or the Ministry of Justice, as the case may be, jointly with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, examine and approve the request, it shall be submitted to the foreign state through the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 48: In urgent cases, before formally submitting an extradition request to a foreign state, a request may be made to the foreign state, through diplomatic channels or other channels agreed to by the requested state, to take compulsory measures against the person concerned in advance.

Article 49: The documents, files, and materials required for a request for extradition, transit for extradition, or the taking of compulsory measures shall be submitted in accordance with the provisions of the extradition treaty; where there is no extradition treaty or the treaty contains no provisions, the provisions of Sections 2, 4, and 7 of Chapter 2 of this Law may be referred to; where the requested state has special requirements, the request may be made in accordance with those special requirements, provided that the fundamental principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China are not violated.

Article 50: Where the requested state attaches conditions to granting extradition, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs may, on behalf of the government of the People's Republic of China, make commitments to the requested state, provided that such conditions do not harm the sovereignty, national interests, or public interests of the People's Republic of China. Commitments restricting prosecution shall be decided by the Supreme People's Procuratorate; commitments regarding sentencing shall be decided by the Supreme People's Court.

When pursuing criminal liability against the extradited person, the judicial authorities shall be bound by the commitments made.

Article 51: Public security organs shall be responsible for receiving the person extradited by the foreign state and the property related to the case.

Where the extradition request was made by another department, the public security organs shall, after receiving the extradited person and the property related to the case, promptly transfer them to the department that made the extradition request; they may also receive the extradited person and the property related to the case jointly with the relevant departments.

## Chapter 4: Supplementary Provisions

Article 52: Where, according to the provisions of this Law, the decision on whether to extradite is to be made by the State Council, the State Council may, when necessary, authorize the relevant departments under the State Council to make the decision.

Article 53: Where the requesting state, after submitting a request, withdraws or abandons the extradition request, or submits an erroneous extradition request, causing damage to the person sought for extradition, and the person sought for extradition claims compensation, such claim shall be made to the requesting state.

Article 54: Expenses incurred in handling extradition cases shall be handled in accordance with the extradition treaty or agreement jointly participated in or signed by the requesting state and the requested state.

Article 55: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
