# Labor Dispute Mediation and Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on December 29, 2007

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of fairly and promptly resolving labor disputes, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of the parties concerned, and promoting harmonious and stable labor relations.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the following labor disputes arising between employers and workers within the territory of the People's Republic of China:

(1) Disputes arising from the confirmation of labor relations;

(2) Disputes arising from the conclusion, performance, modification, rescission and termination of labor contracts;

(3) Disputes arising from removal, dismissal, resignation and separation;

(4) Disputes arising from working hours, rest and vacation, social insurance, welfare, training and labor protection;

(5) Disputes arising from labor remuneration, medical expenses for work-related injuries, economic compensation or damages, etc.; and

(6) Other labor disputes as provided for by laws and regulations.

Article 3 Labor disputes shall be resolved on the basis of facts, in accordance with the principles of legality, fairness, timeliness and emphasis on mediation, and the legitimate rights and interests of the parties shall be protected according to law.

Article 4 In the event of a labor dispute, a worker may negotiate with the employer, or may request the trade union or a third party to jointly negotiate with the employer, to reach a settlement agreement.

Article 5 In the event of a labor dispute, if the parties are unwilling to negotiate, negotiation fails, or the settlement agreement is not performed after it is reached, they may apply to a mediation organization for mediation; if they are unwilling to mediate, mediation fails, or the mediation agreement is not performed after it is reached, they may apply to a labor dispute arbitration commission for arbitration; if they are dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with a people's court, except as otherwise provided by this Law.

Article 6 In the event of a labor dispute, the parties concerned have the responsibility to provide evidence for their own claims. If the evidence related to the disputed matter is under the control and management of the employer, the employer shall provide it; if the employer fails to provide it, it shall bear adverse consequences.

Article 7 If there are ten or more workers on one side of a labor dispute and they have a common claim, they may elect representatives to participate in mediation, arbitration or litigation activities.

Article 8 The labor administrative department of a people's government at or above the county level shall, together with the trade union and representatives of the enterprise side, establish a tripartite mechanism for coordinating labor relations to jointly study and resolve major issues in labor disputes.

Article 9 If an employer violates state regulations by defaulting on or failing to pay full labor remuneration, or defaulting on medical expenses for work-related injuries, economic compensation or damages, the worker may complain to the labor administrative department, which shall handle the matter according to law.

## Chapter II Mediation

Article 10 In the event of a labor dispute, the parties may apply for mediation to the following mediation organizations:

(1) The labor dispute mediation committee of the enterprise;

(2) A grassroots people's mediation organization established according to law; and

(3) An organization established in a township or subdistrict with the function of mediating labor disputes.

The labor dispute mediation committee of an enterprise shall be composed of worker representatives and enterprise representatives. Worker representatives shall be members of the trade union or elected by all workers, and enterprise representatives shall be designated by the person in charge of the enterprise. The director of the labor dispute mediation committee of an enterprise shall be a member of the trade union or a person elected by both parties.

Article 11 Mediators of labor dispute mediation organizations shall be adult citizens who are fair and upright, maintain close ties with the masses, are enthusiastic about mediation work, and possess certain legal knowledge, policy level and cultural level.

Article 12 A party applying for labor dispute mediation may do so in writing or orally. If the application is made orally, the mediation organization shall record on the spot the basic information of the applicant, the matters in dispute for which mediation is requested, the reasons and the time.

Article 13 When mediating a labor dispute, the mediator shall fully listen to the statements of both parties on the facts and reasons, provide patient guidance, and help them reach an agreement.

Article 14 If an agreement is reached through mediation, a mediation agreement shall be prepared.

The mediation agreement shall become effective after being signed or sealed by both parties, signed by the mediator and affixed with the seal of the mediation organization, and shall be binding on both parties, who shall perform it.

If no mediation agreement is reached within fifteen days from the date the labor dispute mediation organization receives the mediation application, the parties may apply for arbitration according to law.

Article 15 After a mediation agreement is reached, if one party fails to perform the mediation agreement within the time limit specified in the agreement, the other party may apply for arbitration according to law.

Article 16 If a mediation agreement is reached on matters concerning the payment of overdue labor remuneration, medical expenses for work-related injuries, economic compensation or damages, and the employer fails to perform it within the time limit specified in the agreement, the worker may, holding the mediation agreement, apply to a people's court for a payment order according to law. The people's court shall issue the payment order according to law.

## Chapter III Arbitration

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 17 Labor dispute arbitration commissions shall be established in accordance with the principles of overall planning, rational layout and adaptation to actual needs. The people's government of a province or autonomous region may decide to establish them in cities or counties; the people's government of a municipality directly under the Central Government may decide to establish them in districts or counties. A municipality directly under the Central Government or a city divided into districts may also establish one or several labor dispute arbitration commissions. Labor dispute arbitration commissions shall not be established layer by layer according to administrative divisions.

Article 18 The labor administrative department of the State Council shall formulate arbitration rules in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law. The labor administrative department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall provide guidance for labor dispute arbitration work within its administrative region.

Article 19 A labor dispute arbitration commission shall be composed of representatives of the labor administrative department, representatives of the trade union and representatives of the enterprise side. The number of members of a labor dispute arbitration commission shall be an odd number.

A labor dispute arbitration commission shall perform the following duties according to law:

(1) Appointing and dismissing full-time or part-time arbitrators;

(2) Accepting labor dispute cases;

(3) Discussing major or difficult labor dispute cases; and

(4) Supervising arbitration activities.

A labor dispute arbitration commission shall have an office under it to handle the daily work of the labor dispute arbitration commission.

Article 20 A labor dispute arbitration commission shall maintain a roster of arbitrators.

Arbitrators shall be fair and upright and meet one of the following conditions:

(1) Having served as a judge;

(2) Engaged in legal research or teaching work and holding an intermediate or higher professional title;

(3) Having legal knowledge and having been engaged in professional work such as human resources management or trade union work for five years or more; or

(4) Having practiced as a lawyer for three years or more.

Article 21 A labor dispute arbitration commission shall have jurisdiction over labor disputes arising within its region.

A labor dispute shall be under the jurisdiction of the labor dispute arbitration commission of the place where the labor contract is performed or the place where the employer is located. If both parties apply for arbitration to the labor dispute arbitration commissions of the place where the labor contract is performed and the place where the employer is located respectively, the labor dispute arbitration commission of the place where the labor contract is performed shall have jurisdiction.

Article 22 The worker and the employer involved in a labor dispute shall be the parties to the labor dispute arbitration case.

If a labor dispatch unit or the employing unit has a labor dispute with a worker, the labor dispatch unit and the employing unit shall be joint parties.

Article 23 A third party that has an interest in the outcome of the handling of a labor dispute case may apply to participate in the arbitration activities, or the labor dispute arbitration commission shall notify it to participate in the arbitration activities.

Article 24 A party may entrust an agent to participate in arbitration activities. If a party entrusts another person to participate in arbitration activities, it shall submit a power of attorney signed or sealed by the principal to the labor dispute arbitration commission, which shall specify the matters and scope of authorization.

Article 25 A worker who has lost or partially lost capacity for civil conduct shall be represented by his or her legal representative in arbitration activities; if there is no legal representative, the labor dispute arbitration commission shall appoint a representative for him or her. If the worker dies, his or her close relatives or agents shall participate in the arbitration activities.

Article 26 Labor dispute arbitration shall be conducted openly, except where the parties agree not to conduct it openly or where state secrets, commercial secrets or personal privacy are involved.

### Section 2 Application and Acceptance

Article 27 The limitation period for applying for labor dispute arbitration is one year. The limitation period for arbitration shall be calculated from the date on which the party knows or should have known that its rights have been infringed.

The limitation period for arbitration as specified in the preceding paragraph shall be interrupted if one party claims its rights against the other party, or requests rights relief from the relevant department, or the other party agrees to perform its obligations. From the time of interruption, the limitation period for arbitration shall be recalculated.

If a party cannot apply for arbitration within the limitation period for arbitration specified in the first paragraph of this Article due to force majeure or other legitimate reasons, the limitation period for arbitration shall be suspended. From the date on which the cause of suspension is eliminated, the limitation period for arbitration shall continue to run.

If a dispute arises due to overdue labor remuneration during the existence of the labor relationship, the worker's application for arbitration shall not be subject to the limitation period for arbitration specified in the first paragraph of this Article; however, if the labor relationship is terminated, the application shall be made within one year from the date of termination.

Article 28 An applicant applying for arbitration shall submit a written arbitration application and submit copies according to the number of respondents.

The arbitration application shall state the following:

(1) The name, gender, age, occupation, work unit and domicile of the worker, and the name, domicile and name and position of the legal representative or principal person in charge of the employer;

(2) The arbitration claims and the facts and reasons on which they are based; and

(3) The evidence and the source of the evidence, and the names and domiciles of witnesses.

If it is truly difficult to write the arbitration application, an oral application may be made, which shall be recorded in the record by the labor dispute arbitration commission and notified to the other party.

Article 29 Within five days from the date of receiving the arbitration application, if the labor dispute arbitration commission considers that the conditions for acceptance are met, it shall accept the case and notify the applicant; if it considers that the conditions for acceptance are not met, it shall notify the applicant in writing of the non-acceptance and state the reasons. If the labor dispute arbitration commission does not accept the case or fails to make a decision within the time limit, the applicant may file a lawsuit with a people's court regarding the labor dispute matter.

Article 30 After accepting an arbitration application, the labor dispute arbitration commission shall serve a copy of the arbitration application on the respondent within five days.

After receiving the copy of the arbitration application, the respondent shall submit a written defense to the labor dispute arbitration commission within ten days. After receiving the defense, the labor dispute arbitration commission shall serve a copy of the defense on the applicant within five days. The failure of the respondent to submit a defense shall not affect the progress of the arbitration proceedings.

### Section 3 Hearing and Award

Article 31 The labor dispute arbitration commission shall adopt the arbitration tribunal system for adjudicating labor dispute cases. An arbitration tribunal shall be composed of three arbitrators, with a chief arbitrator. Simple labor dispute cases may be arbitrated by a single arbitrator alone.

Article 32 The labor dispute arbitration commission shall notify the parties in writing of the composition of the arbitration tribunal within five days from the date of accepting the arbitration application.

Article 33 An arbitrator shall withdraw from the case under any of the following circumstances, and the parties also have the right to apply for withdrawal orally or in writing:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party or agent;

(2) Having an interest in the case;

(3) Having other relationships with a party or agent of the case that may affect the impartiality of the award; or

(4) Privately meeting with a party or agent, or accepting entertainment or gifts from a party or agent.

The labor dispute arbitration commission shall make a timely decision on the application for withdrawal and notify the parties orally or in writing.

Article 34 If an arbitrator falls under the circumstances specified in Item (4) of Article 33 of this Law, or engages in soliciting or accepting bribes, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or perverting the law in making an award, he or she shall bear legal liability according to law. The labor dispute arbitration commission shall dismiss him or her.

Article 35 The arbitration tribunal shall notify both parties in writing of the date and place of the hearing five days before the hearing. If a party has legitimate reasons, it may request a postponement of the hearing three days before the hearing. Whether to postpone the hearing shall be decided by the labor dispute arbitration commission.

Article 36 If the applicant, after receiving written notice, fails to appear in court without legitimate reasons or leaves the court midway without the permission of the arbitration tribunal, the arbitration application may be deemed withdrawn.

If the respondent, after receiving written notice, fails to appear in court without legitimate reasons or leaves the court midway without the permission of the arbitration tribunal, a default award may be made.

Article 37 If the arbitration tribunal considers that an appraisal of a specialized issue is necessary, it may refer the matter to an appraisal institution agreed upon by the parties; if the parties have not agreed or cannot reach an agreement, it shall be referred to an appraisal institution designated by the arbitration tribunal.

At the request of the parties or the requirement of the arbitration tribunal, the appraisal institution shall send an appraiser to attend the hearing. With the permission of the arbitration tribunal, the parties may question the appraiser.

Article 38 During the arbitration process, the parties have the right to cross-examine evidence and debate. At the conclusion of the cross-examination and debate, the chief arbitrator or the sole arbitrator shall solicit the final opinions of the parties.

Article 39 If the evidence provided by the parties is verified to be true, the arbitration tribunal shall take it as the basis for determining the facts.

If the worker cannot provide evidence under the control and management of the employer that is related to the arbitration claim, the arbitration tribunal may require the employer to provide it within a specified period. If the employer fails to provide it within the specified period, it shall bear adverse consequences.

Article 40 The arbitration tribunal shall record the hearing in the record. If the parties and other arbitration participants consider that there are omissions or errors in the record of their statements, they have the right to apply for corrections. If corrections are not made, the application shall be recorded.

The record shall be signed or sealed by the arbitrators, the recorders, the parties and other arbitration participants.

Article 41 After applying for labor dispute arbitration, the parties may settle the matter on their own. If a settlement agreement is reached, the arbitration application may be withdrawn.

Article 42 Before making an award, the arbitration tribunal shall first conduct mediation.

If an agreement is reached through mediation, the arbitration tribunal shall prepare a mediation statement.

The mediation statement shall state the arbitration claims and the results agreed upon by the parties. The mediation statement shall be signed by the arbitrators, affixed with the seal of the labor dispute arbitration commission, and served on both parties. The mediation statement shall become legally effective after being signed and received by both parties.

If mediation fails or one party reneges before the mediation statement is served, the arbitration tribunal shall make an award promptly.

Article 43 The arbitration tribunal shall conclude the adjudication of a labor dispute case within forty-five days from the date the labor dispute arbitration commission accepts the arbitration application. If the case is complex and requires an extension, with the approval of the director of the labor dispute arbitration commission, the extension may be granted and the parties shall be notified in writing, but the extension period shall not exceed fifteen days. If no arbitration award is made within the time limit, the parties may file a lawsuit with a people's court regarding the labor dispute matter.

When the arbitration tribunal adjudicates a labor dispute case, if part of the facts are already clear, it may make an award on that part first.

Article 44 In cases involving claims for labor remuneration, medical expenses for work-related injuries, economic compensation or damages, the arbitration tribunal may, upon the application of the party, make an award for advance execution and transfer it to a people's court for execution.

The arbitration tribunal shall meet the following conditions when making an award for advance execution:

(1) The rights and obligations between the parties are clear; and

(2) Failure to execute in advance will seriously affect the life of the applicant.

If a worker applies for advance execution, he or she may not be required to provide security.

Article 45 An award shall be made in accordance with the opinions of the majority of the arbitrators, and the dissenting opinions of the minority of arbitrators shall be recorded in the record. If the arbitration tribunal cannot form a majority opinion, the award shall be made in accordance with the opinion of the chief arbitrator.

Article 46 The written award shall state the arbitration claims, the facts of the dispute, the reasons for the award, the result of the award and the date of the award. The written award shall be signed by the arbitrators and affixed with the seal of the labor dispute arbitration commission. Arbitrators who hold dissenting opinions on the award may sign or not sign.

Article 47 For the following labor disputes, except as otherwise provided by this Law, the arbitration award shall be a final award, and the written award shall become legally effective from the date it is made:

(1) Disputes concerning claims for labor remuneration, medical expenses for work-related injuries, economic compensation or damages, where the amount does not exceed the equivalent of twelve months of the local monthly minimum wage; and

(2) Disputes arising from the implementation of national labor standards in terms of working hours, rest and vacation, social insurance, etc.

Article 48 If a worker is dissatisfied with the arbitration award specified in Article 47 of this Law, he or she may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the written award.

Article 49 If an employer has evidence that the arbitration award specified in Article 47 of this Law falls under any of the following circumstances, it may apply to the intermediate people's court in the place where the labor dispute arbitration commission is located for revocation of the award within thirty days from the date of receiving the written award:

(1) The application of laws or regulations is indeed erroneous;

(2) The labor dispute arbitration commission has no jurisdiction;

(3) The statutory procedures are violated;

(4) The evidence on which the award is based is forged;

(5) The other party has concealed evidence sufficient to affect the impartiality of the award; or

(6) The arbitrator engaged in soliciting or accepting bribes, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or perverting the law in making the award in the case.

If, after forming a collegial panel to review and verify, the people's court finds that the award falls under any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall rule to revoke the award.

If the arbitration award is revoked by a ruling of the people's court, the parties may file a lawsuit with a people's court regarding the labor dispute matter within fifteen days from the date of receiving the written ruling.

Article 50 If a party is dissatisfied with an arbitration award for a labor dispute case other than those specified in Article 47 of this Law, it may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the written award; if no lawsuit is filed within the time limit, the written award shall become legally effective.

Article 51 The parties shall perform the legally effective mediation statement or written award within the prescribed time limit. If one party fails to perform within the time limit, the other party may apply to a people's court for enforcement in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law. The people's court that accepts the application shall enforce it according to law.

## Chapter IV Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 If a staff member of a public institution under the employment system has a labor dispute with the institution, this Law shall apply; if laws, administrative regulations or the State Council provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 53 Labor dispute arbitration shall be free of charge. The funds of labor dispute arbitration commissions shall be guaranteed by the fiscal authorities.

Article 54 This Law shall come into force as of May 1, 2008.
