# Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on July 1, 1979

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the Fourth Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 17, 1996

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the Fifth Session of the Eleventh National People's Congress on March 14, 2012

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the Sixth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Part I General Provisions

### Chapter I Tasks and Basic Principles

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and for the purposes of ensuring the correct implementation of the Criminal Law, punishing crimes, protecting the people, safeguarding national security and public safety, and maintaining socialist public order.

Article 2 The tasks of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China are: to ensure accurate and timely identification of criminal facts, correct application of law, punishment of criminals, protection of the innocent from criminal prosecution, education of citizens to voluntarily abide by the law and actively fight against criminal acts, safeguard the socialist legal system, respect and protect human rights, protect citizens' personal rights, property rights, democratic rights and other rights, and ensure the smooth progress of socialist construction.

Article 3 The public security organs are responsible for investigation, detention, execution of arrest, and preliminary inquiry of criminal cases. The people's procuratorates are responsible for procuratorial work, approval of arrests, investigation of cases directly accepted by the procuratorial organs, and initiation of public prosecution. The people's courts are responsible for adjudication. Except where otherwise provided by law, no other organs, organizations, or individuals have the authority to exercise these powers.

In conducting criminal proceedings, the people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs must strictly observe the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws.

Article 4 State security organs shall, in accordance with the law, handle criminal cases endangering national security, exercising the same powers as public security organs.

Article 5 The people's courts shall exercise adjudicative power independently in accordance with the law, and the people's procuratorates shall exercise procuratorial power independently in accordance with the law, free from interference by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 6 In conducting criminal proceedings, the people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs must rely on the masses, take facts as the basis, and take law as the criterion. The law applies equally to all citizens, and no privilege is allowed before the law.

Article 7 In conducting criminal proceedings, the people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall divide responsibilities, cooperate with each other, and check each other to ensure the accurate and effective implementation of the law.

Article 8 The people's procuratorates shall exercise legal supervision over criminal proceedings in accordance with the law.

Article 9 Citizens of all ethnic groups have the right to use their own spoken and written languages in litigation. The people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall provide translation for any participant in the proceedings who is not familiar with the spoken or written language commonly used in the locality.

In areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities or where multiple ethnic groups live together, trials shall be conducted in the language commonly used in the locality, and judgments, notices, and other documents shall be issued in the language commonly used in the locality.

Article 10 The people's courts shall adopt the system of final appeal by the second instance in adjudicating cases.

Article 11 Trials by the people's courts shall be conducted in public, except where otherwise provided by this Law. The defendant has the right to defense, and the people's court has the obligation to ensure that the defendant obtains defense.

Article 12 No person shall be found guilty without a judgment rendered by a people's court in accordance with the law.

Article 13 In adjudicating cases, the people's courts shall implement the system of people's assessors participating in trials in accordance with this Law.

Article 14 The people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs shall safeguard the right to defense and other litigation rights enjoyed by criminal suspects, defendants, and other participants in proceedings in accordance with the law.

Participants in proceedings have the right to file complaints against judicial officers, procuratorial officers, and investigative officers who infringe upon citizens' litigation rights or subject them to personal humiliation.

Article 15 If a criminal suspect or defendant voluntarily and truthfully confesses his or her crimes, admits the alleged criminal facts, and is willing to accept punishment, he or she may be dealt with leniently in accordance with the law.

Article 16 In any of the following circumstances, no criminal liability shall be pursued; if already pursued, the case shall be dismissed, or a decision not to prosecute shall be made, or the proceedings shall be terminated, or a verdict of not guilty shall be rendered:

(1) The act is obviously minor, causing no serious harm, and is not deemed a crime;

(2) The statute of limitations for prosecution has expired;

(3) A special amnesty decree has exempted the person from punishment;

(4) The crime is to be handled only upon complaint under the Criminal Law, and no complaint has been made or the complaint has been withdrawn;

(5) The criminal suspect or defendant has died;

(6) Other circumstances provided by law where criminal liability is exempted.

Article 17 The provisions of this Law shall apply to crimes committed by foreigners for which criminal liability is to be pursued.

If a foreigner enjoying diplomatic privileges and immunity commits a crime for which criminal liability is to be pursued, the matter shall be resolved through diplomatic channels.

Article 18 In accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity, Chinese judicial organs and foreign judicial organs may request mutual criminal judicial assistance.

### Chapter II Jurisdiction

Article 19 Investigation of criminal cases shall be conducted by the public security organs, except where otherwise provided by law.

Crimes involving judicial personnel who, in the course of exercising legal supervision over litigation activities, commit illegal detention, extortion of confessions by torture, illegal searches, or other acts infringing upon citizens' rights and impairing judicial justice, may be placed on file for investigation by the people's procuratorates. For major crimes committed by state functionaries under the jurisdiction of public security organs by taking advantage of their duties, if direct acceptance by the people's procuratorates is necessary, the people's procuratorates may place the case on file for investigation upon decision by a people's procuratorate at or above the provincial level.

Cases of private prosecution shall be directly accepted by the people's courts.

Article 20 Basic people's courts have jurisdiction over ordinary criminal cases of first instance, except where otherwise provided by this Law for jurisdiction by higher people's courts.

Article 21 Intermediate people's courts have jurisdiction over the following criminal cases of first instance:

(1) Cases endangering national security and terrorist activities;

(2) Cases where the possible punishment is life imprisonment or the death penalty.

Article 22 Higher people's courts have jurisdiction over criminal cases of first instance that are major and affect the entire province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 23 The Supreme People's Court has jurisdiction over criminal cases of first instance that are major and affect the entire country.

Article 24 When necessary, a higher people's court may adjudicate criminal cases of first instance that are under the jurisdiction of a lower people's court; if a lower people's court considers a case to be major or complex and requiring adjudication by a higher people's court, it may request to transfer the case to the higher people's court for adjudication.

Article 25 Criminal cases shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the place where the crime was committed. If it is more appropriate for the people's court in the place where the defendant resides to adjudicate the case, that court may have jurisdiction.

Article 26 Where several people's courts at the same level have jurisdiction over a case, the people's court that first accepted the case shall adjudicate it. When necessary, the case may be transferred to the people's court in the principal place where the crime was committed for adjudication.

Article 27 A higher people's court may designate a lower people's court to adjudicate a case with unclear jurisdiction, and may also designate a lower people's court to transfer a case to another people's court for adjudication.

Article 28 Jurisdiction over cases of special people's courts shall be separately provided.

### Chapter III Withdrawal

Article 29 Judicial officers, procuratorial officers, and investigative officers shall withdraw themselves from a case under any of the following circumstances, and the parties and their legal representatives have the right to request their withdrawal:

(1) They are a party to the case or a close relative of a party;

(2) They or their close relatives have an interest in the case;

(3) They have served as witnesses, expert evaluators, defenders, or litigation representatives in the case;

(4) They have any other relationship with a party to the case that may affect the impartial handling of the case.

Article 30 Judicial officers, procuratorial officers, and investigative officers shall not accept entertainment or gifts from parties or their entrustees, nor shall they meet with parties or their entrustees in violation of regulations.

If judicial officers, procuratorial officers, or investigative officers violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph, they shall be investigated for legal liability. The parties and their legal representatives have the right to request their withdrawal.

Article 31 The withdrawal of a judicial officer, procuratorial officer, or investigative officer shall be decided respectively by the president of the court, the chief procurator, or the head of the public security organ; the withdrawal of the president of the court shall be decided by the judicial committee of that court; the withdrawal of the chief procurator or the head of the public security organ shall be decided by the procuratorial committee of the people's procuratorate at the same level.

Before a decision is made on the withdrawal of an investigative officer, the investigative officer shall not suspend the investigation of the case.

If a request for withdrawal is rejected, the parties and their legal representatives may apply for reconsideration once.

Article 32 The provisions of this Chapter on withdrawal apply to court clerks, interpreters, and expert evaluators.

Defenders and litigation representatives may request withdrawal or apply for reconsideration in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

### Chapter IV Defense and Representation

Article 33 In addition to exercising the right to defense themselves, a criminal suspect or defendant may entrust one or two persons as defenders. The following persons may be entrusted as defenders:

(1) Lawyers;

(2) Persons recommended by a people's organization or the unit to which the criminal suspect or defendant belongs;

(3) Guardians, relatives, or friends of the criminal suspect or defendant.

Persons who are currently serving a sentence or who have been deprived of or restricted in their personal freedom in accordance with the law shall not serve as defenders.

Persons who have been dismissed from public office or whose lawyer's or notary's practice certificates have been revoked shall not serve as defenders, except where they are guardians or close relatives of the criminal suspect or defendant.

Article 34 A criminal suspect has the right to entrust a defender from the date of the first interrogation by the investigative organ or from the date of the adoption of compulsory measures; during the investigation period, only a lawyer may be entrusted as a defender. A defendant has the right to entrust a defender at any time.

When interrogating a criminal suspect for the first time or adopting compulsory measures against a criminal suspect, the investigative organ shall inform the criminal suspect of the right to entrust a defender. Within three days from the date of receiving the case materials transferred for examination and prosecution, the people's procuratorate shall inform the criminal suspect of the right to entrust a defender. Within three days from the date of accepting a case, the people's court shall inform the defendant of the right to entrust a defender. If a criminal suspect or defendant in custody requests to entrust a defender, the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ shall promptly convey the request.

If a criminal suspect or defendant is in custody, his or her guardian or close relatives may also entrust a defender on his or her behalf.

After accepting the entrustment of a criminal suspect or defendant, the defender shall promptly inform the organ handling the case.

Article 35 If a criminal suspect or defendant has not entrusted a defender due to financial difficulties or other reasons, he or she or his or her close relatives may apply to a legal aid agency. If the conditions for legal aid are met, the legal aid agency shall assign a lawyer to provide defense.

If a criminal suspect or defendant is blind, deaf, or mute, or is a mental patient who has not completely lost the ability to recognize or control his or her behavior, and has not entrusted a defender, the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ shall notify a legal aid agency to assign a lawyer to provide defense.

If a criminal suspect or defendant may be sentenced to life imprisonment or the death penalty and has not entrusted a defender, the people's court, people's procuratorate, or public security organ shall notify a legal aid agency to assign a lawyer to provide defense.

Article 36 Legal aid agencies may station duty lawyers in places such as people's courts and detention centers. If a criminal suspect or defendant has not entrusted a defender and the legal aid agency has not assigned a lawyer to provide defense, the duty lawyer shall provide legal assistance to the criminal suspect or defendant, including legal consultation, suggestions on procedural choices, applications for modification of compulsory measures, and opinions on case handling.

The people's court, people's procuratorate, and detention center shall inform the criminal suspect or defendant of the right to meet with a duty lawyer and shall facilitate such meetings.

Article 37 The responsibility of a defender is to present, based on facts and law, materials and opinions proving that the criminal suspect or defendant is innocent, has a minor crime, or that his or her criminal liability should be reduced or exempted, and to safeguard the litigation rights and other lawful rights and interests of the criminal suspect or defendant.

Article 38 During the investigation period, a defense lawyer may provide legal assistance to the criminal suspect; file appeals and complaints on his or her behalf; apply for modification of compulsory measures; and learn from the investigative organ about the crime suspected of the criminal suspect and the relevant circumstances of the case, and present opinions.

Article 39 A defense lawyer may meet and correspond with the criminal suspect or defendant in custody. With the permission of the people's court or people's procuratorate, other defenders may also meet and correspond with the criminal suspect or defendant in custody.

If a defense lawyer requests to meet with a criminal suspect or defendant in custody by presenting his or her lawyer's practice certificate, law firm certificate, and power of attorney or legal aid letter, the detention center shall arrange the meeting promptly, no later than forty-eight hours.

In cases endangering national security or terrorist activities, during the investigation period, a defense lawyer's meeting with a criminal suspect in custody shall be subject to the permission of the investigative organ. For the above cases, the investigative organ shall notify the detention center in advance.

When meeting with a criminal suspect or defendant in custody, a defense lawyer may learn about the relevant circumstances of the case and provide legal consultation, etc.; from the date of transfer of the case for examination and prosecution, the defense lawyer may verify relevant evidence with the criminal suspect or defendant. Defense lawyers shall not be monitored when meeting with criminal suspects or defendants.

The provisions of the first, third, and fourth paragraphs shall apply to meetings and correspondence between defense lawyers and criminal suspects or defendants under residential surveillance.

Article 40 From the date of examination and prosecution of a case by the people's procuratorate, a defense lawyer may consult, extract, and copy the case file materials. With the permission of the people's court or people's procuratorate, other defenders may also consult, extract, and copy the above materials.

Article 41 If a defender believes that evidence materials proving the innocence or minor crime of the criminal suspect or defendant collected by the public security organ or people's procuratorate during the investigation or examination and prosecution period have not been submitted, the defender has the right to apply to the people's procuratorate or people's court for their retrieval.

Article 42 If a defender collects evidence that the criminal suspect was not at the crime scene, had not reached the age of criminal responsibility, or is a mental patient who is not criminally liable in accordance with the law, the defender shall promptly inform the public security organ or people's procuratorate.

Article 43 With the consent of witnesses or other relevant units or individuals, a defense lawyer may collect materials related to the case from them, and may also apply to the people's procuratorate or people's court for the collection or retrieval of evidence, or apply to the people's court for the witness to testify in court.

With the permission of the people's procuratorate or people's court, and with the consent of the victim or his or her close relatives, or witnesses provided by the victim, a defense lawyer may collect materials related to the case from them.

Article 44 No defender or any other person shall assist a criminal suspect or defendant in concealing, destroying, or fabricating evidence or colluding in confessions, nor shall they threaten or induce witnesses to give false testimony or engage in other acts that interfere with the litigation activities of judicial organs.

If the provisions of the preceding paragraph are violated, legal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law. If a defender is suspected of committing a crime, the case shall be handled by an investigative organ other than the one handling the case undertaken by the defender. If the defender is a lawyer, the law firm to which he or she belongs or the lawyers' association of which he or she is a member shall be promptly notified.

Article 45 During the trial, the defendant may refuse to have the defender continue to defend him or her, and may also entrust another defender to defend him or her.

Article 46 The victim and his or her legal representatives or close relatives in a public prosecution case, and the parties and their legal representatives in an incidental civil action, have the right to entrust litigation representatives from the date of transfer of the case for examination and prosecution. The private prosecutor and his or her legal representatives in a private prosecution case, and the parties and their legal representatives in an incidental civil action, have the right to entrust litigation representatives at any time.

Within three days from the date of receiving the case materials transferred for examination and prosecution, the people's procuratorate shall inform the victim and his or her legal representatives or close relatives, and the parties and their legal representatives in an incidental civil action, of the right to entrust litigation representatives. Within three days from the date of accepting a private prosecution case, the people's court shall inform the private prosecutor and his or her legal representatives, and the parties and their legal representatives in an incidental civil action, of the right to entrust litigation representatives.

Article 47 The entrustment of litigation representatives shall be carried out by reference to the provisions of Article 33 of this Law.

Article 48 A defense lawyer has the right to keep confidential the relevant circumstances and information of the client learned during the practice of his or her profession. However, if a defense lawyer learns during the practice of his or her profession that the client or others are preparing or are committing crimes endangering national security, public safety, or seriously endangering the personal safety of others, the defense lawyer shall promptly inform the judicial organs.

Article 49 If a defender or litigation representative believes that the public security organ, people's procuratorate, people's court, or their staff have obstructed him or her from exercising litigation rights in accordance with the law, he or she has the right to file a complaint or accusation with the people's procuratorate at the same level or at the next higher level. The people's procuratorate shall promptly review the complaint or accusation; if the situation is true, it shall notify the relevant organ to make corrections.

### Chapter V Evidence

Article 50 Any material that can be used to prove the facts of a case constitutes evidence.

Evidence includes:

(1) Physical evidence;

(2) Documentary evidence;

(3) Witness testimony;

(4) Victim statements;

(5) Confessions and exculpations of criminal suspects or defendants;

(6) Expert evaluation opinions;

(7) Records of inspection, examination, identification, investigative experiments, etc.;

(8) Audio-visual materials and electronic data.

Evidence must be verified before it can be used as the basis for deciding a case.

Article 51 The burden of proof for the guilt of the defendant in a public prosecution case rests with the people's procuratorate, and the burden of proof for the guilt of the defendant in a private prosecution case rests with the private prosecutor.

Article 52 Judicial officers, procuratorial officers, and investigative officers must, in accordance with the statutory procedures, collect various kinds of evidence that can prove the guilt or innocence of a criminal suspect or defendant and the severity of the crime. The use of torture to extort confessions and the collection of evidence by threats, inducement, deception, or other illegal methods are strictly prohibited. No one shall be compelled to prove his or her own guilt. It must be ensured that all citizens related to or informed about the case have the conditions to provide evidence objectively and fully; except in special circumstances, they may be involved to assist in the investigation.

Article 53 The written request for approval of arrest submitted by the public security organ, the indictment submitted by the people's procuratorate, and the judgment rendered by the people's court must be faithful to the truth. Anyone who intentionally conceals the truth shall be held liable.

Article 54 The people's courts, people's procuratorates, and public security organs have the authority to collect and retrieve evidence from relevant units and individuals. The relevant units and individuals shall truthfully provide evidence.Evidence materials such as physical evidence, documentary evidence, audio-visual materials, and electronic data collected by administrative agencies in the course of administrative law enforcement and case investigation may be used as evidence in criminal proceedings.

Evidence involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy shall be kept confidential.

Anyone who forges, conceals, or destroys evidence, regardless of which party they belong to, must be subject to legal prosecution.

Article 55: In adjudicating all cases, emphasis shall be placed on evidence, investigation, and research, and confessions shall not be readily trusted. If there is only the defendant's confession and no other evidence, the defendant cannot be found guilty and sentenced; if there is no defendant's confession but the evidence is reliable and sufficient, the defendant may be found guilty and sentenced.

Evidence being reliable and sufficient shall meet the following conditions:

(1) All facts concerning conviction and sentencing are supported by evidence;
(2) All evidence used to decide the case has been verified through legal procedures;
(3) Comprehensive evaluation of all evidence in the case has eliminated reasonable doubt regarding the facts determined.

Article 56: Confessions of criminal suspects or defendants obtained through illegal methods such as extortion of confessions by torture, and witness testimony or victim statements obtained through illegal methods such as violence or threats, shall be excluded. If the collection of physical or documentary evidence does not comply with legal procedures and may seriously affect judicial fairness, it shall be corrected or a reasonable explanation shall be provided; if it cannot be corrected or reasonably explained, such evidence shall be excluded.

If evidence that should be excluded is discovered during investigation, review for prosecution, or trial, it shall be excluded according to law and shall not be used as the basis for prosecution opinions, prosecution decisions, or judgments.

Article 57: When the People's Procuratorate receives reports, accusations, or tips, or discovers that investigators have collected evidence through illegal methods, it shall investigate and verify. If illegal methods of evidence collection are indeed found, corrective opinions shall be issued; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 58: During court proceedings, if the judicial personnel believe there may be circumstances of illegal evidence collection as stipulated in Article 56 of this Law, they shall conduct a court investigation into the legality of the evidence collection.

The parties, their defenders, and legal representatives have the right to apply to the People's Court for the exclusion of evidence collected through illegal methods. An application for exclusion of evidence collected through illegal methods shall provide relevant clues or materials.

Article 59: During the court investigation into the legality of evidence collection, the People's Procuratorate shall prove the legality of the evidence collection.

If the existing evidence materials cannot prove the legality of the evidence collection, the People's Procuratorate may request the People's Court to notify relevant investigators or other personnel to appear in court to explain the situation; the People's Court may notify relevant investigators or other personnel to appear in court to explain the situation. Relevant investigators or other personnel may also request to appear in court to explain the situation. Upon notification by the People's Court, the relevant personnel shall appear in court.

Article 60: If, after court proceedings, it is confirmed or cannot be ruled out that there are circumstances of illegal evidence collection as stipulated in Article 56 of this Law, the relevant evidence shall be excluded.

Article 61: Witness testimony can only be used as the basis for deciding a case after it has been cross-examined in court by the public prosecutor, the victim, the defendant, and the defender, and verified. If the court finds that a witness has intentionally given false testimony or concealed criminal evidence, it shall be dealt with according to law.

Article 62: Anyone who has knowledge of the circumstances of a case has the obligation to testify.

Persons who, due to physical or mental disabilities or being minors, cannot distinguish right from wrong or express themselves correctly, cannot serve as witnesses.

Article 63: The People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs shall ensure the safety of witnesses and their close relatives.

Threatening, insulting, assaulting, or retaliating against witnesses or their close relatives, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability according to law; if not sufficient for criminal punishment, administrative penalties for public security shall be imposed according to law.

Article 64: For cases involving crimes endangering national security, terrorist activities, organized crimes of a mafia nature, drug crimes, etc., if witnesses, expert witnesses, or victims face danger to their personal safety or that of their close relatives due to testifying in litigation, the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs shall take one or more of the following protective measures:

(1) Not disclosing personal information such as real names, addresses, and work units;
(2) Taking measures such as not exposing appearance or real voice when testifying in court;
(3) Prohibiting specific individuals from contacting witnesses, expert witnesses, victims, and their close relatives;
(4) Taking specialized protective measures for persons and residences;
(5) Other necessary protective measures.

If witnesses, expert witnesses, or victims believe that their personal safety or that of their close relatives is endangered due to testifying in litigation, they may request protection from the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, or Public Security Organs.

When the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, or Public Security Organs take protective measures according to law, relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

Article 65: Expenses such as transportation, accommodation, and meals incurred by witnesses in fulfilling their obligation to testify shall be subsidized. The subsidies for witness testimony shall be included in the operational expenses of judicial organs and guaranteed by the government finance at the same level.

For witnesses who have work units, the unit shall not deduct or covertly reduce their wages, bonuses, or other welfare benefits.

### Chapter 6: Compulsory Measures

Article 66: The People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs may, based on the circumstances of the case, summon criminal suspects or defendants for interrogation, release them on bail pending trial, or place them under residential surveillance.

Article 67: The People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs may release on bail pending trial criminal suspects or defendants in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Those who may be sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, or independently applicable supplementary punishments;
(2) Those who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or above, and releasing them on bail pending trial would not pose a social danger;
(3) Those who are seriously ill, unable to care for themselves, or are pregnant or breastfeeding their own infants, and releasing them on bail pending trial would not pose a social danger;
(4) Those whose detention period has expired but the case has not yet been concluded, and bail pending trial is necessary.

Bail pending trial shall be executed by the Public Security Organs.

Article 68: When the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, or Public Security Organs decide to release a criminal suspect or defendant on bail pending trial, they shall order the criminal suspect or defendant to provide a guarantor or pay a deposit.

Article 69: A guarantor must meet the following conditions:

(1) Not involved in the case;
(2) Capable of fulfilling the guarantee obligations;
(3) Enjoying political rights and not having personal freedom restricted;
(4) Having a fixed residence and income.

Article 70: A guarantor shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Supervise the guaranteed person's compliance with the provisions of Article 71 of this Law;
(2) Promptly report to the executing authority if discovering that the guaranteed person may or has violated the provisions of Article 71 of this Law.

If the guaranteed person violates the provisions of Article 71 of this Law and the guarantor fails to fulfill the guarantee obligations, the guarantor shall be fined; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 71: Criminal suspects or defendants released on bail pending trial shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) Not leave the city or county of residence without permission from the executing authority;
(2) Report changes in residence, work unit, and contact information to the executing authority within 24 hours;
(3) Appear in court promptly when summoned;
(4) Not interfere with witness testimony in any form;
(5) Not destroy, forge evidence, or collude with others.

The People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs may, based on the circumstances of the case, order criminal suspects or defendants released on bail pending trial to comply with one or more of the following provisions:

(1) Not enter specific places;
(2) Not meet or communicate with specific individuals;
(3) Not engage in specific activities;
(4) Surrender passports, other exit-entry documents, and driving licenses to the executing authority for safekeeping.

If a criminal suspect or defendant released on bail pending trial violates the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs and has paid a deposit, part or all of the deposit shall be confiscated, and depending on the circumstances, the criminal suspect or defendant may be ordered to make a written statement of repentance, pay a new deposit, provide a new guarantor, or be placed under residential surveillance or arrested.

If arrest is necessary due to violation of bail pending trial provisions, the criminal suspect or defendant may be detained first.

Article 72: The authority deciding on bail pending trial shall comprehensively consider the need to ensure the normal conduct of litigation, the social danger of the person released on bail, the nature and circumstances of the case, the severity of the possible sentence, and the economic situation of the person released on bail to determine the amount of the deposit.

The person providing the deposit shall deposit it into a special account designated by the executing authority at a bank.

Article 73: If a criminal suspect or defendant released on bail pending trial has not violated the provisions of Article 71 of this Law during the bail period, upon the conclusion of the bail, they may retrieve the refunded deposit from the bank using the notice of release on bail or relevant legal documents.

Article 74: The People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs may place under residential surveillance criminal suspects or defendants who meet the conditions for arrest and have any of the following circumstances:

(1) Those who are seriously ill and unable to care for themselves;
(2) Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding their own infants;
(3) Those who are the sole caregivers of persons unable to care for themselves;
(4) Those for whom residential surveillance is more appropriate due to special circumstances of the case or the needs of case handling;
(5) Those whose detention period has expired but the case has not yet been concluded, and residential surveillance is necessary.

For those who meet the conditions for bail pending trial but cannot provide a guarantor or pay a deposit, residential surveillance may be imposed.

Residential surveillance shall be executed by the Public Security Organs.

Article 75: Residential surveillance shall be executed at the residence of the criminal suspect or defendant; if there is no fixed residence, it may be executed at a designated residence. For those suspected of crimes endangering national security or terrorist activities, if execution at the residence may hinder the investigation, with approval from the Public Security Organ at the next higher level, it may also be executed at a designated residence. However, it shall not be executed in detention facilities or specialized case-handling venues.

For residential surveillance at a designated residence, unless it is impossible to notify, the family members of the person under surveillance shall be notified within 24 hours after the execution of the surveillance.

The provisions of Article 34 of this Law shall apply to the entrustment of a defender by a criminal suspect or defendant under residential surveillance.

The People's Procuratorate shall supervise the legality of the decision and execution of residential surveillance at a designated residence.

Article 76: The period of residential surveillance at a designated residence shall be deducted from the sentence. If sentenced to public surveillance, one day of residential surveillance shall be deducted from one day of the sentence; if sentenced to criminal detention or fixed-term imprisonment, two days of residential surveillance shall be deducted from one day of the sentence.

Article 77: Criminal suspects or defendants under residential surveillance shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) Not leave the place of residential surveillance without permission from the executing authority;
(2) Not meet with others or communicate without permission from the executing authority;
(3) Appear in court promptly when summoned;
(4) Not interfere with witness testimony in any form;
(5) Not destroy, forge evidence, or collude with others;
(6) Surrender passports, other exit-entry documents, identity documents, and driving licenses to the executing authority for safekeeping.

If a criminal suspect or defendant under residential surveillance violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and the circumstances are serious, they may be arrested; if arrest is necessary, the criminal suspect or defendant may be detained first.

Article 78: The executing authority may use electronic monitoring, irregular inspections, and other surveillance methods to supervise the compliance of criminal suspects or defendants under residential surveillance with the provisions; during the investigation period, the communications of criminal suspects under residential surveillance may be monitored.

Article 79: The maximum period for bail pending trial by the People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, and Public Security Organs shall not exceed twelve months, and the maximum period for residential surveillance shall not exceed six months.

During the period of bail pending trial or residential surveillance, the investigation, prosecution, and trial of the case shall not be interrupted. If it is discovered that criminal liability should not be pursued or the period of bail pending trial or residential surveillance has expired, the bail or residential surveillance shall be promptly lifted. The lifting of bail or residential surveillance shall be promptly notified to the person concerned and relevant units.

Article 80: The arrest of a criminal suspect or defendant must be approved by the People's Procuratorate or decided by the People's Court, and executed by the Public Security Organs.

Article 81: For criminal suspects or defendants with evidence of criminal facts who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or above, if bail pending trial is insufficient to prevent the following social dangers, they shall be arrested:

(1) Those who may commit new crimes;
(2) Those who pose a real danger to national security, public safety, or social order;
(3) Those who may destroy or forge evidence, interfere with witness testimony, or collude with others;
(4) Those who may retaliate against victims, informants, or accusers;
(5) Those who attempt suicide or escape.

When approving or deciding on arrest, the nature and circumstances of the suspected crime, and whether the suspect or defendant admits guilt and accepts punishment, shall be considered as factors in determining the likelihood of social danger.

For those with evidence of criminal facts who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of ten years or more, or those with evidence of criminal facts who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or above and have previously intentionally committed crimes or whose identity is unknown, they shall be arrested.

If a criminal suspect or defendant released on bail pending trial or under residential surveillance violates the provisions of bail or residential surveillance and the circumstances are serious, they may be arrested.

Article 82: Public Security Organs may detain current offenders or major suspects in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Those who are preparing to commit a crime, are in the act of committing a crime, or are discovered immediately after committing a crime;
(2) Those identified as committing a crime by the victim or eyewitnesses at the scene;
(3) Those found with criminal evidence on their person or at their residence;
(4) Those who attempt suicide, escape, or are at large after committing a crime;
(5) Those who may destroy or forge evidence or collude with others;
(6) Those who do not provide their true name, address, or whose identity is unknown;
(7) Those with significant suspicion of committing crimes in multiple locations, multiple times, or in groups.

Article 83: When Public Security Organs execute detention or arrest in a different location, they shall notify the Public Security Organ at the location of the detained or arrested person, and that Public Security Organ shall cooperate.

Article 84: Any citizen may immediately seize and deliver to the Public Security Organ, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court for handling any of the following persons:

(1) Those who are in the act of committing a crime or are discovered immediately after committing a crime;
(2) Those who are wanted;
(3) Those who have escaped from prison;
(4) Those who are being pursued.

Article 85: When detaining a person, the Public Security Organ must produce a detention warrant.

After detention, the detained person shall be immediately sent to a detention center for custody, no later than 24 hours. Unless it is impossible to notify or notification may hinder the investigation in cases involving crimes endangering national security or terrorist activities, the family members of the detained person shall be notified within 24 hours after detention. Once the circumstances hindering the investigation disappear, the family members of the detained person shall be immediately notified.

Article 86: The Public Security Organ shall interrogate the detained person within 24 hours after detention. If it is discovered that the person should not have been detained, they must be immediately released and issued a release certificate.

Article 87: When the Public Security Organ requests the arrest of a criminal suspect, it shall prepare a written request for approval of arrest, along with the case files and evidence, and submit them to the People's Procuratorate at the same level for review and approval. When necessary, the People's Procuratorate may send personnel to participate in the discussion of major cases by the Public Security Organ.

Article 88: When reviewing and approving an arrest, the People's Procuratorate may interrogate the criminal suspect; in any of the following circumstances, it shall interrogate the criminal suspect:

(1) There is doubt about whether the conditions for arrest are met;
(2) The criminal suspect requests to make a statement in person to the procuratorial personnel;
(3) There may be significant illegal activities in the investigation.

When reviewing and approving an arrest, the People's Procuratorate may question witnesses and other participants in the proceedings and hear the opinions of defense lawyers; if defense lawyers make a request, their opinions shall be heard.

Article 89: The decision to approve the arrest of a criminal suspect by the People's Procuratorate shall be made by the Chief Procurator. Major cases shall be submitted to the Procuratorial Committee for discussion and decision.

Article 90: After reviewing a case submitted by the Public Security Organ for approval of arrest, the People's Procuratorate shall, based on the circumstances, decide to approve or disapprove the arrest. For a decision to approve the arrest, the Public Security Organ shall immediately execute it and promptly notify the People's Procuratorate of the execution. For a decision not to approve the arrest, the People's Procuratorate shall state the reasons; if supplementary investigation is needed, it shall simultaneously notify the Public Security Organ.

Article 91: If the Public Security Organ believes that a detained person needs to be arrested, it shall, within three days after detention, submit a request to the People's Procuratorate for review and approval. Under special circumstances, the time for submitting the request for review and approval may be extended by one to four days.

For major suspects involved in crimes committed in multiple locations, multiple times, or in groups, the time for submitting the request for review and approval may be extended to thirty days.

The People's Procuratorate shall, within seven days after receiving the written request for approval of arrest from the Public Security Organ, decide to approve or disapprove the arrest. If the People's Procuratorate does not approve the arrest, the Public Security Organ shall immediately release the person upon receiving the notice and promptly notify the People's Procuratorate of the execution. If further investigation is needed and the conditions for bail pending trial or residential surveillance are met, the person shall be released on bail pending trial or placed under residential surveillance according to law.

Article 92: If the Public Security Organ believes that the People's Procuratorate's decision not to approve the arrest is erroneous, it may request a review, but must immediately release the detained person. If its opinion is not accepted, it may submit a request for review to the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level. The higher-level People's Procuratorate shall immediately review and decide whether to change the decision, and notify the lower-level People's Procuratorate and the Public Security Organ to execute it.

Article 93: When arresting a person, the Public Security Organ must produce an arrest warrant.

After arrest, the arrested person shall be immediately sent to a detention center for custody. Unless it is impossible to notify, the family members of the arrested person shall be notified within 24 hours after arrest.

Article 94: The People's Court and the People's Procuratorate must interrogate persons they have decided to arrest, and the Public Security Organ must interrogate persons arrested with the approval of the People's Procuratorate, within 24 hours after arrest. If it is discovered that the person should not have been arrested, they must be immediately released and issued a release certificate.Article 95 Once a criminal suspect or defendant has been arrested, the People's Procuratorate shall still review the necessity of detention. If continued detention is unnecessary, it shall recommend release or a change of compulsory measures. The relevant authority shall notify the People's Procuratorate of the handling within ten days.

Article 96 If the People's Court, People's Procuratorate, or public security authority finds that compulsory measures taken against a criminal suspect or defendant are improper, it shall promptly revoke or change them. If a public security authority releases an arrested person or changes an arrest measure, it shall notify the original approving People's Procuratorate.

Article 97 A criminal suspect or defendant, their legal representative, close relative, or defender has the right to apply for a change of compulsory measures. Upon receiving such an application, the People's Court, People's Procuratorate, or public security authority shall make a decision within three days; if the change is not approved, the applicant shall be informed of the reasons for the denial.

Article 98 If a case involving a detained criminal suspect or defendant cannot be concluded within the time limits for investigation, review for prosecution, first instance, or second instance as prescribed by this Law, the criminal suspect or defendant shall be released; if further investigation or trial is necessary, the criminal suspect or defendant may be released on bail pending trial or placed under residential surveillance.

Article 99 When the statutory time limit for compulsory measures expires for a criminal suspect or defendant, the People's Court, People's Procuratorate, or public security authority shall release them, lift the bail pending trial or residential surveillance, or change the compulsory measures according to law. The criminal suspect or defendant, their legal representative, close relative, or defender has the right to request the lifting of compulsory measures when the statutory time limit expires.

Article 100 In reviewing and approving arrests, if the People's Procuratorate discovers illegal activities in the investigation by the public security authority, it shall notify the public security authority to correct them, and the public security authority shall notify the People's Procuratorate of the corrections.

### Chapter VII: Incidental Civil Actions

Article 101 If a victim suffers material losses due to a criminal act of the defendant, they have the right to file an incidental civil action during the criminal proceedings. If the victim dies or loses capacity, their legal representative or close relative has the right to file an incidental civil action.

If state or collective property suffers losses, the People's Procuratorate may file an incidental civil action when initiating a public prosecution.

Article 102 When necessary, the People's Court may take preservation measures, such as sealing, seizing, or freezing the defendant's property. The plaintiff in the incidental civil action or the People's Procuratorate may apply to the People's Court for preservation measures. The People's Court shall apply the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law when taking preservation measures.

Article 103 When trying an incidental civil action, the People's Court may conduct mediation or render a judgment or ruling based on the material losses.

Article 104 An incidental civil action shall be tried together with the criminal case. Only to prevent excessive delay in the criminal trial may the incidental civil action be tried by the same trial panel after the criminal case is concluded.

### Chapter VIII: Time Periods and Service

Article 105 Time periods shall be calculated in hours, days, and months.

The hour and day from which a time period begins shall not be counted within the period.

Statutory time periods shall not include travel time. If an appeal or other document is mailed before the deadline, it shall not be considered overdue.

If the last day of a time period falls on a holiday, the first day after the holiday shall be the deadline; however, the period of detention for a criminal suspect, defendant, or offender shall end on the last day and shall not be extended due to holidays.

Article 106 If a party misses a deadline due to irresistible causes or other legitimate reasons, they may apply within five days after the obstacle is removed to continue the litigation activities that should have been completed before the deadline.

Whether to grant the application in the preceding paragraph shall be decided by the People's Court.

Article 107 Summons, notices, and other litigation documents shall be served on the recipient in person; if the recipient is absent, they may be received by an adult family member or a responsible person from their unit.

If the recipient or the person receiving the documents refuses to accept or sign or seal them, the server may invite neighbors or other witnesses to be present, explain the situation, leave the documents at the recipient's residence, record the reason for refusal and the date of service on the service certificate, and sign it, deeming service completed.

### Chapter IX: Other Provisions

Article 108 The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Investigation" refers to the work and related compulsory measures carried out by public security authorities and the People's Procuratorate in accordance with the law to collect evidence and ascertain the facts of criminal cases;

(2) "Parties" refers to victims, private prosecutors, criminal suspects, defendants, and plaintiffs and defendants in incidental civil actions;

(3) "Legal representative" refers to the parents, adoptive parents, guardians, and representatives of agencies or organizations responsible for the protection of the represented person;

(4) "Participants in proceedings" refers to parties, legal representatives, litigation representatives, defenders, witnesses, expert witnesses, and interpreters;

(5) "Litigation representative" refers to persons entrusted by victims and their legal representatives or close relatives in public prosecution cases, private prosecutors and their legal representatives in private prosecution cases, and parties and their legal representatives in incidental civil actions to participate in the proceedings on their behalf;

(6) "Close relatives" refers to spouses, parents, children, and siblings.

## Part II: Case Filing, Investigation, and Initiation of Public Prosecution

### Chapter I: Case Filing

Article 109 If a public security authority or the People's Procuratorate discovers a criminal fact or a criminal suspect, it shall file a case and investigate within its jurisdiction.

Article 110 Any unit or individual that discovers a criminal fact or a criminal suspect has the right and duty to report or inform the public security authority, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court.

A victim has the right to report or accuse a criminal fact or criminal suspect that infringes on their personal or property rights to the public security authority, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court.

The public security authority, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court shall accept all reports, accusations, and tips. If a matter is not within its jurisdiction, it shall transfer it to the competent authority and notify the reporter, accuser, or tipster; if urgent measures are necessary for a matter not within its jurisdiction, it shall first take urgent measures and then transfer it.

If a criminal voluntarily surrenders to the public security authority, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court, the provisions of paragraph 3 shall apply.

Article 111 Reports, accusations, and tips may be made in writing or orally. Personnel receiving oral reports, accusations, or tips shall make a record, which, after being read back and confirmed as correct, shall be signed or sealed by the reporter, accuser, or tipster.

Personnel receiving accusations or tips shall inform the accuser or tipster of the legal liability for false accusations. However, as long as facts are not fabricated or evidence forged, even if the reported facts are inaccurate or even mistaken, they shall be strictly distinguished from false accusations.

The public security authority, People's Procuratorate, or People's Court shall ensure the safety of reporters, accusers, tipsters, and their close relatives. If a reporter, accuser, or tipster does not wish to disclose their name or the act of reporting, accusing, or tipping, confidentiality shall be maintained.

Article 112 The People's Court, People's Procuratorate, or public security authority shall promptly review materials from reports, accusations, tips, and voluntary surrenders within its jurisdiction. If it believes that a criminal fact exists and criminal liability should be pursued, it shall file a case; if it believes that no criminal fact exists, or the criminal fact is minor and criminal liability is unnecessary, it shall not file a case and shall notify the accuser of the reasons. If the accuser disagrees, they may apply for reconsideration.

Article 113 If the People's Procuratorate believes that a public security authority should file a case for investigation but fails to do so, or if a victim believes that a public security authority should file a case for investigation but fails to do so and raises the issue with the People's Procuratorate, the People's Procuratorate shall require the public security authority to explain the reasons for not filing the case. If the People's Procuratorate finds the reasons invalid, it shall notify the public security authority to file the case, and the public security authority shall do so upon receiving the notice.

Article 114 For private prosecution cases, the victim has the right to directly sue in the People's Court. If the victim dies or loses capacity, their legal representative or close relative has the right to sue in the People's Court. The People's Court shall accept the case according to law.

### Chapter II: Investigation

#### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 115 A public security authority shall investigate a criminal case that has been filed, collecting and obtaining evidence of guilt or innocence, and of the severity of the crime. A flagrant offender or major suspect may be detained in advance according to law, and a criminal suspect meeting the conditions for arrest shall be arrested according to law.

Article 116 After investigation, if there is evidence to prove a criminal fact, the public security authority shall conduct a preliminary hearing to verify the collected evidence.

Article 117 Parties, defenders, litigation representatives, and interested parties have the right to appeal or accuse a judicial authority or its personnel for any of the following acts:

(1) Failing to release, lift, or change compulsory measures after the statutory time limit expires;

(2) Failing to return bail that should be returned;

(3) Sealing, seizing, or freezing property unrelated to the case;

(4) Failing to lift sealing, seizure, or freezing that should be lifted;

(5) Embezzling, misappropriating, privately dividing, swapping, or using sealed, seized, or frozen property in violation of regulations.

The authority receiving the appeal or accusation shall handle it promptly. If dissatisfied with the handling, one may appeal to the People's Procuratorate at the same level; for cases directly accepted by the People's Procuratorate, one may appeal to the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level. The People's Procuratorate shall promptly review the appeal and, if the facts are true, notify the relevant authority to correct them.

#### Section 2: Interrogation of Criminal Suspects

Article 118 Interrogation of a criminal suspect must be conducted by investigators from the People's Procuratorate or public security authority. During interrogation, there shall be no fewer than two investigators.

After a criminal suspect is delivered to a detention center for custody, interrogation by investigators shall be conducted within the detention center.

Article 119 For a criminal suspect who does not need to be arrested or detained, they may be summoned to a designated location within the city or county where they are located or to their residence for interrogation, but the investigator must present a certificate from the People's Procuratorate or public security authority. For a criminal suspect found at the scene, an oral summons may be made after presenting a work ID, but this shall be noted in the interrogation record.

The duration of a summons or compulsory summons shall not exceed twelve hours; for cases of extreme gravity or complexity requiring detention or arrest, the duration shall not exceed twenty-four hours.

Continuous summons or compulsory summons shall not be used to covertly detain a criminal suspect. During summons or compulsory summons, the criminal suspect shall be ensured adequate time for meals and necessary rest.

Article 120 When interrogating a criminal suspect, investigators shall first ask whether the suspect has committed a crime, allowing them to state guilty conduct or make a defense of innocence, and then ask questions. The criminal suspect shall truthfully answer the investigators' questions. However, they have the right to refuse to answer questions unrelated to the case.

When interrogating a criminal suspect, investigators shall inform them of their litigation rights and the legal provisions that truthful confession may lead to lenient treatment and that admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment are considered.

Article 121 When interrogating a deaf or mute criminal suspect, a person proficient in sign language shall participate, and this shall be noted in the record.

Article 122 The interrogation record shall be shown to the criminal suspect for verification; if they cannot read, it shall be read to them. If there are omissions or errors, the criminal suspect may request supplements or corrections. After the criminal suspect confirms the record is correct, they shall sign or seal it. Investigators shall also sign the record. If the criminal suspect requests to write a confession themselves, it shall be permitted. When necessary, investigators may also require the criminal suspect to write a confession in their own hand.

Article 123 When interrogating a criminal suspect, investigators may audio or video record the interrogation; for cases that may result in life imprisonment or the death penalty, or other major criminal cases, the interrogation shall be audio or video recorded.

The recording shall be continuous and complete.

#### Section 3: Questioning of Witnesses

Article 124 Investigators may question witnesses at the scene, at the witness's unit, residence, or a location proposed by the witness; when necessary, the witness may be summoned to the People's Procuratorate or public security authority to provide testimony. When questioning a witness at the scene, a work ID shall be presented; when questioning at the witness's unit, residence, or proposed location, a certificate from the People's Procuratorate or public security authority shall be presented.

Witnesses shall be questioned individually.

Article 125 When questioning a witness, they shall be informed of the obligation to truthfully provide evidence and testimony and the legal liability for intentionally giving false testimony or concealing criminal evidence.

Article 126 The provisions of Article 122 of this Law also apply to the questioning of witnesses.

Article 127 The provisions of this Section apply to the questioning of victims.

#### Section 4: Inspection and Examination

Article 128 Investigators shall inspect or examine places, objects, persons, and corpses related to the crime. When necessary, persons with specialized knowledge may be assigned or engaged to conduct inspections or examinations under the direction of investigators.

Article 129 Any unit or individual has the duty to protect the crime scene and immediately notify the public security authority to send personnel for inspection.

Article 130 When conducting inspections or examinations, investigators must hold a certificate from the People's Procuratorate or public security authority.

Article 131 For corpses with unknown causes of death, the public security authority has the right to order an autopsy and notify the deceased's family to be present.

Article 132 To determine certain characteristics, injuries, or physiological conditions of a victim or criminal suspect, a physical examination may be conducted, and fingerprints may be taken, as well as biological samples such as blood and urine.

If a criminal suspect refuses an examination, investigators may conduct a compulsory examination when deemed necessary.

Examination of a woman's body shall be conducted by female personnel or a physician.

Article 133 The circumstances of inspections and examinations shall be recorded in writing, signed or sealed by the participants and witnesses.

Article 134 When reviewing a case, if the People's Procuratorate believes that a re-inspection or re-examination of the public security authority's inspection or examination is necessary, it may request the public security authority to conduct one and may send procuratorial personnel to participate.

Article 135 To ascertain the facts of a case, when necessary, with the approval of the head of the public security authority, an investigative experiment may be conducted.

The circumstances of the investigative experiment shall be recorded in writing, signed or sealed by the participants.

Any act that may cause danger, humiliate a person, or be indecent is prohibited in investigative experiments.

#### Section 5: Search

Article 136 To collect criminal evidence and apprehend criminals, investigators may search the person, belongings, residence, and other relevant places of a criminal suspect or anyone who may be hiding a criminal or criminal evidence.

Article 137 Any unit or individual has the duty to hand over, as required by the People's Procuratorate or public security authority, material evidence, documentary evidence, audio-visual materials, and other evidence that may prove a criminal suspect's guilt or innocence.

Article 138 When conducting a search, a search warrant must be presented to the person being searched.

In urgent situations during an arrest or detention, a search may be conducted without a separate search warrant.

Article 139 During a search, the person being searched or their family members, neighbors, or other witnesses shall be present.

A search of a woman's body shall be conducted by female personnel.

Article 140 The circumstances of a search shall be recorded in writing, signed or sealed by the investigators and the person being searched or their family members, neighbors, or other witnesses. If the person being searched or their family members are absent or refuse to sign or seal, this shall be noted in the record.

#### Section 6: Sealing, Seizing of Material Evidence and Documentary Evidence

Article 141 Various property and documents discovered during investigation that may prove a criminal suspect's guilt or innocence shall be sealed or seized; property and documents unrelated to the case shall not be sealed or seized.

Sealed or seized property and documents shall be properly kept or stored and shall not be used, swapped, or damaged.

Article 142 Sealed or seized property and documents shall be checked and counted in the presence of witnesses and the holder, and a list in duplicate shall be made on the spot, signed or sealed by the investigators, witnesses, and holder, with one copy given to the holder and the other attached to the file for reference.

Article 143 If investigators deem it necessary to seize a criminal suspect's mail or telegrams, with the approval of the public security authority or the People's Procuratorate, they may notify the postal or telecommunications authority to inspect and hand over the relevant mail or telegrams for seizure.

When seizure is no longer necessary, the postal or telecommunications authority shall be notified immediately.

Article 144 The People's Procuratorate or public security authority may, in accordance with regulations, inquire into and freeze a criminal suspect's deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, fund shares, and other property as needed for the investigation. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

If a criminal suspect's deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, fund shares, or other property have already been frozen, they shall not be frozen again.

Article 145 If sealed, seized property, documents, mail, telegrams, or frozen deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, fund shares, or other property are verified to be unrelated to the case, the sealing, seizure, or freezing shall be lifted within three days, and the property shall be returned.

#### Section 7: Expert Evaluation

Article 146 To ascertain the facts of a case, when specialized issues need to be resolved, persons with specialized knowledge shall be assigned or engaged to conduct expert evaluations.

Article 147 After conducting an expert evaluation, the expert shall produce an evaluation opinion and sign it.

An expert who intentionally makes a false evaluation shall bear legal liability.Article 148: Investigative authorities shall inform criminal suspects and victims of the expert opinions used as evidence. If the criminal suspect or victim applies, supplementary or new expert evaluations may be conducted.

Article 149: The period for conducting a psychiatric evaluation of a criminal suspect shall not be included in the case-handling time limit.

#### Section 8: Technical Investigation Measures

Article 150: After filing a case, public security organs may adopt technical investigation measures for crimes endangering national security, terrorist activities, organized crimes of a mafia nature, major drug crimes, or other serious crimes endangering society, based on the needs of the criminal investigation and subject to strict approval procedures.

After filing a case, people's procuratorates may adopt technical investigation measures for major crimes involving serious infringement of citizens' personal rights committed by abuse of power, based on the needs of the criminal investigation and subject to strict approval procedures, and shall have them executed by the relevant authorities as prescribed.

To pursue fugitive criminal suspects or defendants who are wanted or for whom arrest has been approved or decided, technical investigation measures necessary for the pursuit may be adopted upon approval.

Article 151: The approval decision shall determine the types and targets of technical investigation measures based on the needs of the criminal investigation. The approval decision shall be valid for three months from the date of issuance. If the measures are no longer necessary, they shall be promptly terminated; for complex or difficult cases where continuation is necessary after the period expires, the validity may be extended upon approval, each extension not exceeding three months.

Article 152: Technical investigation measures must be strictly implemented in accordance with the approved types, targets, and duration.

Investigators shall keep confidential any state secrets, commercial secrets, and personal privacy learned during the adoption of technical investigation measures; materials obtained that are irrelevant to the case must be promptly destroyed.

Materials obtained through technical investigation measures may only be used for the investigation, prosecution, and trial of crimes and for no other purposes.

When public security organs lawfully adopt technical investigation measures, relevant entities and individuals shall cooperate and keep the relevant information confidential.

Article 153: To ascertain the facts of a case, when necessary, upon the decision of the head of the public security organ, relevant personnel may conduct investigations under concealed identities. However, they shall not induce others to commit crimes or use methods that may endanger public safety or cause major personal danger.

For criminal activities involving the delivery of prohibited items such as drugs or property, public security organs may, based on the needs of the criminal investigation, conduct controlled deliveries as prescribed.

Article 154: Materials collected through investigation measures adopted in accordance with this Section may be used as evidence in criminal proceedings. If using such evidence may endanger the personal safety of relevant personnel or cause other serious consequences, protective measures such as not disclosing the identities of relevant personnel or technical methods shall be taken; when necessary, the evidence may be verified by the judicial personnel outside the courtroom.

#### Section 9: Wanted Orders

Article 155: If a criminal suspect who should be arrested is at large, the public security organ may issue a wanted order and take effective measures to apprehend them.

Public security organs at all levels may directly issue wanted orders within their jurisdiction; beyond their jurisdiction, they shall report to the higher-level authority with the power to decide for issuance.

#### Section 10: Conclusion of Investigation

Article 156: The period of investigative custody after the arrest of a criminal suspect shall not exceed two months. For cases that are complex and cannot be concluded within the period, an extension of one month may be granted upon approval by the people's procuratorate at the next higher level.

Article 157: For particularly major and complex cases that, due to special reasons, should not be brought to trial for a relatively long period, the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall submit a request to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for approval to postpone the trial.

Article 158: For the following types of cases that cannot be concluded within the period specified in Article 156 of this Law, an extension of two months may be granted upon approval or decision by the people's procuratorate of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government:

(1) Major and complex cases in remote areas with very inconvenient transportation;
(2) Major cases involving criminal groups;
(3) Major and complex cases involving itinerant crimes;
(4) Major and complex cases involving a wide range of crimes and difficulties in obtaining evidence.

Article 159: For criminal suspects who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of ten years or more, if the investigation cannot be concluded after the extension period granted under Article 158 of this Law expires, a further extension of two months may be granted upon approval or decision by the people's procuratorate of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 160: If, during the investigation, it is discovered that the criminal suspect has committed another major crime, the period of investigative custody shall be recalculated from the date of discovery in accordance with Article 156 of this Law.

If the criminal suspect does not provide their true name or address and their identity is unknown, an investigation into their identity shall be conducted. The period of investigative custody shall be calculated from the date their identity is clarified, but the investigation and evidence collection for their criminal acts shall not cease. If the criminal facts are clear, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, and their identity truly cannot be ascertained, they may be prosecuted and tried under the name they have provided.

Article 161: Before the conclusion of the investigation of a case, if the defense lawyer makes a request, the investigative authority shall listen to the defense lawyer's opinions and record them in the case file. If the defense lawyer submits written opinions, they shall be attached to the case file.

Article 162: For cases where the investigation is concluded by the public security organ, the criminal facts shall be clear, the evidence reliable and sufficient, and a written opinion on prosecution shall be prepared, which, together with the case file materials and evidence, shall be transferred to the people's procuratorate at the same level for review and decision; at the same time, the criminal suspect and their defense lawyer shall be informed of the case transfer.

If the criminal suspect voluntarily admits guilt, this shall be recorded in the case file and transferred with the case, and the relevant circumstances shall be stated in the written opinion on prosecution.

Article 163: If, during the investigation, it is discovered that the criminal suspect should not be held criminally responsible, the case shall be dismissed; if the criminal suspect has been arrested, they shall be immediately released, a release certificate shall be issued, and the people's procuratorate that originally approved the arrest shall be notified.

#### Section 11: Investigation of Cases Directly Accepted by People's Procuratorates

Article 164: The investigation of cases directly accepted by people's procuratorates shall be governed by the provisions of this Chapter.

Article 165: For cases directly accepted by people's procuratorates that meet the circumstances specified in Articles 81, 82(4), and 82(5) of this Law and require the arrest or detention of a criminal suspect, the decision shall be made by the people's procuratorate, and the execution shall be carried out by the public security organ.

Article 166: A person detained in a case directly accepted by a people's procuratorate shall be interrogated within 24 hours of detention. If it is discovered that detention should not have been imposed, the person must be immediately released and a release certificate issued.

Article 167: For a person detained in a case directly accepted by a people's procuratorate, if arrest is deemed necessary, a decision shall be made within 14 days. Under special circumstances, the time for deciding on arrest may be extended by one to three days. If arrest is not necessary, the person shall be immediately released; if further investigation is needed and the conditions for bail pending trial or residential surveillance are met, bail pending trial or residential surveillance shall be applied according to law.

Article 168: For cases where the investigation is concluded by a people's procuratorate, a decision shall be made to initiate a public prosecution, not to initiate a prosecution, or to dismiss the case.

### Chapter 3: Initiation of Public Prosecution

Article 169: All cases requiring public prosecution shall be reviewed and decided by the people's procuratorate.

Article 170: The people's procuratorate shall review cases transferred for prosecution by supervisory organs in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the Supervision Law. If, upon review, the people's procuratorate deems that supplementary verification is necessary, it shall return the case to the supervisory organ for supplementary investigation, and may conduct supplementary investigation on its own when necessary.

For cases transferred for prosecution by supervisory organs where compulsory residence measures have been taken, the people's procuratorate shall first detain the criminal suspect, and the compulsory residence measures shall be automatically lifted. The people's procuratorate shall decide whether to arrest, grant bail pending trial, or impose residential surveillance within 10 days of detention. Under special circumstances, the time for making the decision may be extended by one to four days. The period during which the people's procuratorate decides on compulsory measures shall not be included in the time limit for reviewing the prosecution.

Article 171: When reviewing a case, the people's procuratorate must ascertain:

(1) Whether the criminal facts and circumstances are clear, the evidence reliable and sufficient, and the determination of the nature of the crime and the charge correct;
(2) Whether there are any omitted crimes or other persons who should be held criminally responsible;
(3) Whether the case falls under circumstances where criminal responsibility should not be pursued;
(4) Whether there is any incidental civil action;
(5) Whether the investigative activities are lawful.

Article 172: The people's procuratorate shall make a decision within one month for cases transferred for prosecution by supervisory organs or public security organs; for major or complex cases, the period may be extended by 15 days; for criminal suspects who admit guilt and accept punishment and meet the conditions for applying the fast-track sentencing procedure, a decision shall be made within 10 days, and for those who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of more than one year, the period may be extended to 15 days.

For cases under review for prosecution where the jurisdiction is changed, the time limit for reviewing the prosecution shall be calculated from the date the case is received by the people's procuratorate after the change.

Article 173: When reviewing a case, the people's procuratorate shall interrogate the criminal suspect, listen to the opinions of the defense lawyer or duty lawyer, the victim, and their litigation representatives, and record them in the case file. If the defense lawyer or duty lawyer, the victim, or their litigation representatives submit written opinions, they shall be attached to the case file.

If the criminal suspect admits guilt and accepts punishment, the people's procuratorate shall inform them of their litigation rights and the legal provisions on admitting guilt and accepting punishment, and listen to the opinions of the criminal suspect, the defense lawyer or duty lawyer, the victim, and their litigation representatives on the following matters, and record them in the case file:

(1) The alleged criminal facts, charges, and applicable legal provisions;
(2) Recommendations for lenient punishment, such as a lighter, mitigated, or exempted punishment;
(3) The procedure applicable to the trial of the case after admitting guilt and accepting punishment;
(4) Other matters requiring the hearing of opinions.

When the people's procuratorate listens to the opinions of the duty lawyer in accordance with the preceding two paragraphs, it shall provide the duty lawyer with necessary convenience to understand the relevant circumstances of the case in advance.

Article 174: If a criminal suspect voluntarily admits guilt and agrees to the sentencing recommendation and the application of the procedure, they shall sign a written admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment in the presence of the defense lawyer or duty lawyer.

If a criminal suspect admits guilt and accepts punishment under any of the following circumstances, they are not required to sign a written admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment:

(1) The criminal suspect is blind, deaf, mute, or a person with a mental illness who has not completely lost the capacity to recognize or control their behavior;
(2) The legal representative or defense lawyer of a minor criminal suspect objects to the minor's admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment;
(3) Other circumstances where signing a written admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment is not required.

Article 175: When reviewing a case, the people's procuratorate may request the public security organ to provide evidence materials necessary for the court trial; if it believes that there may be circumstances of illegally collecting evidence as specified in Article 56 of this Law, it may request an explanation of the legality of the evidence collection.

When reviewing a case, if supplementary investigation is needed, the people's procuratorate may return the case to the public security organ for supplementary investigation or conduct the supplementary investigation on its own.

Supplementary investigation shall be completed within one month. Supplementary investigation is limited to two times. After the supplementary investigation is completed and the case is transferred to the people's procuratorate, the time limit for reviewing the prosecution shall be recalculated.

For cases that have undergone two supplementary investigations and the people's procuratorate still finds the evidence insufficient and the conditions for prosecution not met, a decision not to prosecute shall be made.

Article 176: If the people's procuratorate finds that the criminal facts of the criminal suspect have been ascertained, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, and criminal responsibility should be pursued according to law, it shall make a decision to prosecute, initiate a public prosecution in the people's court in accordance with the provisions on trial jurisdiction, and transfer the case file materials and evidence to the people's court.

If the criminal suspect admits guilt and accepts punishment, the people's procuratorate shall propose sentencing recommendations regarding the principal punishment, supplementary punishment, and whether to apply probation, and shall transfer the written admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment and other materials with the case.

Article 177: If the criminal suspect has no criminal facts or falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 16 of this Law, the people's procuratorate shall make a decision not to prosecute.

For minor crimes where, according to the Criminal Law, punishment is not required or exemption from punishment is applicable, the people's procuratorate may make a decision not to prosecute.

For cases where the people's procuratorate decides not to prosecute, it shall simultaneously lift the sealing, seizure, or freezing of property involved in the investigation. If the person not prosecuted needs to be given administrative penalties, disciplinary sanctions, or have their illegal gains confiscated, the people's procuratorate shall issue a procuratorial opinion and transfer it to the relevant competent authority for handling. The relevant competent authority shall promptly notify the people's procuratorate of the handling result.

Article 178: The decision not to prosecute shall be publicly announced, and the written decision not to prosecute shall be served on the person not prosecuted and their work unit. If the person not prosecuted is in custody, they shall be immediately released.

Article 179: For cases transferred for prosecution by public security organs, if the people's procuratorate decides not to prosecute, the written decision not to prosecute shall be served on the public security organ. If the public security organ believes the decision not to prosecute is erroneous, it may request a review; if its opinion is not accepted, it may submit a request for reconsideration to the people's procuratorate at the next higher level.

Article 180: For cases with victims, if a decision not to prosecute is made, the people's procuratorate shall serve the written decision not to prosecute on the victim. If the victim disagrees, they may, within seven days of receiving the decision, petition the people's procuratorate at the next higher level to request a public prosecution. The people's procuratorate shall inform the victim of the review decision. If the people's procuratorate upholds the decision not to prosecute, the victim may file a lawsuit with the people's court. The victim may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court without petitioning. After the people's court accepts the case, the people's procuratorate shall transfer the relevant case materials to the people's court.

Article 181: If the person not prosecuted disagrees with the decision not to prosecute made by the people's procuratorate in accordance with Article 177(2) of this Law, they may, within seven days of receiving the decision, petition the people's procuratorate. The people's procuratorate shall make a review decision, notify the person not prosecuted, and simultaneously send a copy to the public security organ.

Article 182: If a criminal suspect voluntarily and truthfully confesses to the alleged criminal facts and has performed major meritorious service, or the case involves major national interests, upon approval by the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the public security organ may dismiss the case, and the people's procuratorate may make a decision not to prosecute or not to prosecute one or more of the alleged multiple crimes.

If a decision not to prosecute or to dismiss the case is made in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the people's procuratorate and the public security organ shall promptly handle the sealed, seized, or frozen property and its fruits.

## Part Three: Trial

### Chapter 1: Trial Organization

Article 183: For trials of first-instance cases, basic people's courts and intermediate people's courts shall form a collegial panel composed of three judges or three or seven judges and people's assessors; however, for cases in basic people's courts applying summary procedures or fast-track sentencing procedures, a single judge may preside over the trial.

For trials of first-instance cases, higher people's courts shall form a collegial panel composed of three to seven judges or three or seven judges and people's assessors.

For trials of first-instance cases, the Supreme People's Court shall form a collegial panel composed of three to seven judges.

For trials of appeals and protests, people's courts shall form a collegial panel composed of three or five judges.

The number of members in a collegial panel shall be odd.

Article 184: When the collegial panel deliberates, if opinions differ, the decision shall be made according to the majority opinion, but the minority opinion shall be recorded in the minutes. The deliberation minutes shall be signed by the members of the collegial panel.

Article 185: After the collegial panel holds a trial and deliberates, it shall render a judgment. For difficult, complex, or major cases where the collegial panel finds it difficult to make a decision, the collegial panel shall request the president to decide to submit the matter to the judicial committee for discussion and decision. The collegial panel shall implement the decision of the judicial committee.

### Chapter 2: First-Instance Procedure

#### Section 1: Public Prosecution Cases

Article 186: After reviewing a case initiated by public prosecution, if the indictment clearly alleges criminal facts, the people's court shall decide to hold a trial.

Article 187: After deciding to hold a trial, the people's court shall determine the composition of the collegial panel and serve a copy of the indictment of the people's procuratorate on the defendant and their defense lawyer no later than 10 days before the trial.

Before the trial, the judicial personnel may convene the public prosecutor, the parties, the defense lawyer, and the litigation representatives to understand the circumstances and hear opinions on matters related to the trial, such as challenges, the list of witnesses to appear in court, and the exclusion of illegally obtained evidence.

After the people's court determines the trial date, it shall notify the people's procuratorate of the time and place of the trial, summon the parties, and notify the defense lawyer, litigation representatives, witnesses, expert witnesses, and interpreters. The summons and notices shall be served no later than three days before the trial. For cases tried in public, the cause of action, the defendant's name, and the time and place of the trial shall be announced in advance no later than three days before the trial.

The above activities shall be recorded in the minutes and signed by the judicial personnel and the court clerk.

Article 188: People's courts shall try first-instance cases in public. However, cases involving state secrets or personal privacy shall not be tried in public; cases involving commercial secrets, if the parties apply for a closed trial, may not be tried in public.

For cases not tried in public, the reasons for not trying in public shall be announced in court.

Article 189: When a people's court tries a public prosecution case, the people's procuratorate shall send personnel to appear in court to support the prosecution.

Article 190: At the opening of the trial, the presiding judge shall ascertain whether the parties are present, announce the cause of action; announce the list of members of the collegial panel, the court clerk, the public prosecutor, the defense lawyer, the litigation representatives, the expert witnesses, and the interpreters; inform the parties of their right to challenge the members of the collegial panel, the court clerk, the public prosecutor, the expert witnesses, and the interpreters; and inform the defendant of their right to defense.

If the defendant admits guilt and accepts punishment, the presiding judge shall inform the defendant of their litigation rights and the legal provisions on admitting guilt and accepting punishment, and review the voluntariness of the admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment and the authenticity and legality of the content of the written admission of guilt and acceptance of punishment.

Article 191: After the public prosecutor reads the indictment in court, the defendant and the victim may make statements regarding the crimes alleged in the indictment, and the public prosecutor may interrogate the defendant.

The victim, the plaintiff in the incidental civil action, the defense lawyer, and the litigation representatives may, with the permission of the presiding judge, question the defendant.

The judicial personnel may interrogate the defendant.Article 192: If a public prosecutor, a party, or the defender or legal representative has objections to a witness's testimony, and such testimony has a significant impact on the conviction and sentencing of the case, and the people's court deems it necessary for the witness to testify in court, the witness shall appear in court to give testimony.

Where a people's police officer appears in court as a witness regarding a crime witnessed during the performance of official duties, the preceding paragraph shall apply.

If a public prosecutor, a party, or the defender or legal representative has objections to an expert opinion, and the people's court deems it necessary for the expert to appear in court, the expert shall appear in court to give testimony. If, after being notified by the people's court, the expert refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, the expert opinion shall not be used as the basis for a verdict.

Article 193: If, after being notified by the people's court, a witness fails to appear in court without justifiable reasons, the people's court may compel their appearance, except for the spouse, parents, or children of the defendant.

If a witness refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or refuses to testify after appearing in court, they shall be reprimanded. If the circumstances are serious, with the approval of the president of the court, they may be detained for not more than ten days. If the person subject to the penalty disagrees with the detention decision, they may apply to the people's court at the next higher level for reconsideration. Enforcement shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

Article 194: When a witness testifies, the judicial personnel shall inform them of the obligation to provide truthful testimony and the legal liability for intentionally giving false testimony or concealing criminal evidence. With the permission of the presiding judge, the public prosecutor, the parties, the defender, and the legal representative may question the witness or expert. If the presiding judge considers the content of the questioning irrelevant to the case, they shall stop it.

Judicial personnel may question witnesses and experts.

Article 195: The public prosecutor and the defender shall present physical evidence to the court for identification by the parties. The written testimony of witnesses not present in court, the expert opinions of experts, inspection records, and other documentary evidence shall be read out in court. The judicial personnel shall hear the opinions of the public prosecutor, the parties, the defender, and the legal representative.

Article 196: During the court trial, if the collegial panel has doubts about the evidence, it may announce an adjournment to investigate and verify the evidence.

When the people's court investigates and verifies evidence, it may conduct inspections, examinations, seizures, impoundments, appraisals, inquiries, and freezes.

Article 197: During the court trial, the parties, the defender, and the legal representative have the right to apply for the court to summon new witnesses, obtain new physical evidence, or apply for a new appraisal or inspection.

The public prosecutor, the parties, the defender, and the legal representative may apply for the court to notify a person with specialized knowledge to appear in court to provide opinions on the expert opinion made by the expert.

The court shall decide whether to grant the above applications.

The provisions concerning experts shall apply to the appearance of persons with specialized knowledge as specified in the second paragraph.

Article 198: During the court trial, all facts and evidence related to conviction and sentencing shall be investigated and debated.

With the permission of the presiding judge, the public prosecutor, the parties, the defender, and the legal representative may express opinions on the evidence and the circumstances of the case and may debate with each other.

After the presiding judge announces the conclusion of the debate, the defendant has the right to make a final statement.

Article 199: During the court trial, if any participant in the proceedings or member of the audience disrupts the court order, the presiding judge shall warn them to stop. If they refuse to stop, they may be forcibly removed from the court; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not more than 1,000 yuan or detention for not more than 15 days may be imposed. The fine or detention must be approved by the president of the court. If the person subject to the penalty disagrees with the decision on the fine or detention, they may apply to the people's court at the next higher level for reconsideration. Enforcement shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

Anyone who gathers a crowd to create a disturbance, storms the court, or insults, slanders, threatens, or assaults judicial personnel or participants in the proceedings, seriously disrupting court order, and if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 200: After the defendant's final statement, the presiding judge announces an adjournment, and the collegial panel conducts deliberations. Based on the facts that have been ascertained, the evidence, and relevant legal provisions, the following judgments shall be made respectively:

(1) If the facts of the case are clear, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, and the defendant is found guilty according to law, a guilty verdict shall be rendered;

(2) If the defendant is found not guilty according to law, a not guilty verdict shall be rendered;

(3) If the evidence is insufficient and the defendant cannot be found guilty, a not guilty verdict shall be rendered on the grounds of insufficient evidence and the failure to establish the alleged crime.

Article 201: For cases where the defendant admits guilt and accepts punishment, when the people's court renders a judgment according to law, it shall generally adopt the charges and sentencing recommendations of the people's procuratorate, except in the following circumstances:

(1) The defendant's conduct does not constitute a crime or should not be subject to criminal liability;

(2) The defendant admitted guilt and accepted punishment against their will;

(3) The defendant denies the alleged criminal facts;

(4) The charges in the indictment are inconsistent with the charges determined during the trial;

(5) Other circumstances that may affect a fair trial.

If, after trial, the people's court considers the sentencing recommendation clearly inappropriate, or if the defendant or defender raises objections to the sentencing recommendation, the people's procuratorate may adjust the sentencing recommendation. If the people's procuratorate does not adjust the sentencing recommendation, or if the adjusted sentencing recommendation remains clearly inappropriate, the people's court shall render a judgment according to law.

Article 202: All judgments shall be pronounced publicly.

If the judgment is pronounced in court, the written judgment shall be served on the parties and the people's procuratorate that initiated the public prosecution within five days; if the judgment is pronounced on a scheduled date, the written judgment shall be served on the parties and the people's procuratorate that initiated the public prosecution immediately after the pronouncement. The written judgment shall also be served on the defender and the legal representative.

Article 203: The written judgment shall be signed by the judicial personnel and the court clerk, and shall specify the time limit for appeal and the court to which the appeal may be made.

Article 204: During the court trial, if any of the following circumstances occur that affect the conduct of the trial, the trial may be postponed:

(1) It is necessary to summon new witnesses, obtain new physical evidence, conduct a new appraisal or inspection;

(2) The procuratorial personnel discover that the case initiated for public prosecution requires supplementary investigation and make a proposal;

(3) The trial cannot proceed due to an application for withdrawal.

Article 205: For a case postponed in accordance with item (2) of Article 204 of this Law, the people's procuratorate shall complete the supplementary investigation within one month.

Article 206: During the trial, if any of the following circumstances occur, making it impossible to continue the trial for a relatively long period, the trial may be suspended:

(1) The defendant suffers from a serious illness and is unable to appear in court;

(2) The defendant escapes;

(3) The private prosecutor suffers from a serious illness and is unable to appear in court, and has not entrusted a legal representative to appear in court;

(4) Due to force majeure.

After the reasons for the suspension disappear, the trial shall be resumed. The period of suspension shall not be counted in the trial period.

Article 207: All activities of the court trial shall be recorded in writing by the court clerk, reviewed by the presiding judge, and signed by the presiding judge and the court clerk.

The part of the court record concerning witness testimony shall be read out in court or given to the witness to read. After the witness acknowledges that there are no errors, they shall sign or affix their seal.

The court record shall be given to the parties to read or read to them. If the parties consider that there are omissions or errors in the record, they may request additions or corrections. After the parties acknowledge that there are no errors, they shall sign or affix their seal.

Article 208: When the people's court tries a public prosecution case, it shall pronounce judgment within two months after accepting the case, and no later than three months. For cases where the death penalty may be imposed or cases with incidental civil actions, as well as cases involving any of the circumstances specified in Article 158 of this Law, the period may be extended by three months with the approval of the people's court at the next higher level; if further extension is necessary due to special circumstances, approval shall be sought from the Supreme People's Court.

For cases where the people's court changes jurisdiction, the trial period shall be calculated from the date the changed people's court receives the case.

For cases where the people's procuratorate conducts supplementary investigation, after the supplementary investigation is completed and the case is transferred to the people's court, the people's court shall recalculate the trial period.

Article 209: If the people's procuratorate discovers that the people's court has violated legal procedures in trying a case, it has the right to submit corrective opinions to the people's court.

#### Section 2: Private Prosecution Cases

Article 210: Private prosecution cases include the following types:

(1) Cases that are handled only upon complaint;

(2) Minor criminal cases for which the victim has evidence to prove;

(3) Cases where the victim has evidence to prove that the defendant's act of infringing upon the victim's personal or property rights should be investigated for criminal liability according to law, but the public security organ or the people's procuratorate does not investigate the defendant's criminal liability.

Article 211: After reviewing a private prosecution case, the people's court shall handle it according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the facts of the crime are clear and there is sufficient evidence, the case shall be tried in open court;

(2) If the private prosecution case lacks criminal evidence, and the private prosecutor cannot provide supplementary evidence, the court shall persuade the private prosecutor to withdraw the prosecution, or rule to dismiss it.

If the private prosecutor, after being summoned twice according to law, fails to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court without permission during the trial, the case shall be treated as withdrawn.

During the court trial, if the judicial personnel have doubts about the evidence and need to investigate and verify it, the provisions of Article 196 of this Law shall apply.

Article 212: The people's court may conduct mediation in private prosecution cases; before the judgment is pronounced, the private prosecutor may reach a settlement with the defendant or withdraw the prosecution. Mediation shall not apply to cases specified in item (3) of Article 210 of this Law.

For the trial period of private prosecution cases tried by the people's court, if the defendant is in custody, the provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 208 of this Law shall apply; if the defendant is not in custody, the judgment shall be pronounced within six months after accepting the case.

Article 213: During the proceedings, the defendant in a private prosecution case may file a counterclaim against the private prosecutor. The provisions on private prosecution shall apply to counterclaims.

#### Section 3: Summary Procedure

Article 214: Cases under the jurisdiction of a basic people's court may be tried using summary procedure if the following conditions are met:

(1) The facts of the case are clear and the evidence is sufficient;

(2) The defendant admits to the crime committed and has no objection to the alleged criminal facts;

(3) The defendant has no objection to the application of summary procedure.

When initiating a public prosecution, the people's procuratorate may recommend that the people's court apply summary procedure.

Article 215: Summary procedure shall not apply in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The defendant is blind, deaf, mute, or a person with a mental illness who has not completely lost the ability to recognize or control their behavior;

(2) The case has significant social impact;

(3) In a joint crime case, some defendants do not admit guilt or object to the application of summary procedure;

(4) Other circumstances where summary procedure is not suitable.

Article 216: When trying a case using summary procedure, if the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, the case may be tried by a collegial panel or by a single judge; if the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment exceeding three years, the case shall be tried by a collegial panel.

When trying a public prosecution case using summary procedure, the people's procuratorate shall send personnel to appear in court.

Article 217: When trying a case using summary procedure, the judicial personnel shall ask the defendant for their opinion on the alleged criminal facts, inform the defendant of the legal provisions regarding the application of summary procedure, and confirm whether the defendant agrees to the application of summary procedure.

Article 218: When trying a case using summary procedure, with the permission of the judicial personnel, the defendant and their defender may debate with the public prosecutor, the private prosecutor, and their legal representatives.

Article 219: When trying a case using summary procedure, the case is not subject to the restrictions of the provisions in Section 1 of this Chapter regarding service periods, interrogation of the defendant, questioning of witnesses and experts, presentation of evidence, and court debate procedures. However, before the judgment is pronounced, the defendant's final statement shall be heard.

Article 220: When trying a case using summary procedure, the people's court shall conclude the trial within twenty days after accepting the case; for cases where the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment exceeding three years, the period may be extended to one and a half months.

Article 221: If, during the trial, the people's court finds that summary procedure is not suitable, it shall retry the case in accordance with the provisions of Section 1 or Section 2 of this Chapter.

#### Section 4: Fast-Track Sentencing Procedure

Article 222: For cases under the jurisdiction of a basic people's court where the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, the facts of the case are clear, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, the defendant admits guilt and accepts punishment, and agrees to the application of the fast-track sentencing procedure, the fast-track sentencing procedure may be applied, and the case shall be tried by a single judge.

When initiating a public prosecution, the people's procuratorate may recommend that the people's court apply the fast-track sentencing procedure.

Article 223: The fast-track sentencing procedure shall not apply in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The defendant is blind, deaf, mute, or a person with a mental illness who has not completely lost the ability to recognize or control their behavior;

(2) The defendant is a minor;

(3) The case has significant social impact;

(4) In a joint crime case, some defendants object to the alleged criminal facts, charges, sentencing recommendations, or the application of the fast-track sentencing procedure;

(5) The defendant and the victim or their legal representative have not reached a mediation or settlement agreement on matters such as compensation in the incidental civil action;

(6) Other circumstances where the fast-track sentencing procedure is not suitable.

Article 224: When trying a case using the fast-track sentencing procedure, the case is not subject to the restrictions on service periods stipulated in Section 1 of this Chapter. Generally, no court investigation or court debate is conducted, but before the judgment is pronounced, the opinions of the defender and the defendant's final statement shall be heard.

When trying a case using the fast-track sentencing procedure, the judgment shall be pronounced in court.

Article 225: When trying a case using the fast-track sentencing procedure, the people's court shall conclude the trial within ten days after accepting the case; for cases where the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment exceeding one year, the period may be extended to fifteen days.

Article 226: If, during the trial, the people's court discovers that the defendant's conduct does not constitute a crime or should not be investigated for criminal liability, that the defendant admitted guilt and accepted punishment against their will, that the defendant denies the alleged criminal facts, or other circumstances where the fast-track sentencing procedure is not suitable, the case shall be retried in accordance with the provisions of Section 1 or Section 3 of this Chapter.

### Chapter 3: Procedure of Second Instance

Article 227: If the defendant, the private prosecutor, and their legal representatives disagree with the judgment or ruling of first instance rendered by a local people's court at any level, they have the right to appeal in writing or orally to the people's court at the next higher level. The defendant's defender and close relatives may file an appeal with the defendant's consent.

The parties to an incidental civil action and their legal representatives may appeal against the part of the judgment or ruling of first instance rendered by a local people's court at any level that concerns the incidental civil action.

The defendant's right to appeal shall not be deprived on any pretext.

Article 228: If a local people's procuratorate at any level considers that there is a definite error in the judgment or ruling of first instance rendered by the people's court at the corresponding level, it shall file a protest with the people's court at the next higher level.

Article 229: If the victim and their legal representative disagree with the judgment of first instance rendered by a local people's court at any level, they have the right, within five days after receiving the written judgment, to request the people's procuratorate to file a protest. The people's procuratorate shall, within five days after receiving the request from the victim and their legal representative, decide whether to file a protest and notify the requester of the decision.

Article 230: The time limit for appeal or protest against a judgment is ten days, and against a ruling is five days, calculated from the day after the written judgment or ruling is received.

Article 231: If the defendant, the private prosecutor, the plaintiff or defendant in an incidental civil action files an appeal through the original trial people's court, the original trial people's court shall, within three days, transfer the appeal petition together with the case file and evidence to the people's court at the next higher level, and simultaneously deliver a copy of the appeal petition to the people's procuratorate at the corresponding level and the opposing party.

If the defendant, the private prosecutor, the plaintiff or defendant in an incidental civil action directly files an appeal with the people's court of second instance, the people's court of second instance shall, within three days, deliver the appeal petition to the original trial people's court for delivery to the people's procuratorate at the corresponding level and the opposing party.

Article 232: If a local people's procuratorate at any level files a protest against a judgment or ruling of first instance rendered by the people's court at the corresponding level, it shall submit a protest petition through the original trial people's court and send a copy of the protest petition to the people's procuratorate at the next higher level. The original trial people's court shall transfer the protest petition together with the case file and evidence to the people's court at the next higher level, and deliver a copy of the protest petition to the parties.

If the people's procuratorate at the next higher level considers the protest inappropriate, it may withdraw the protest from the people's court at the corresponding level and notify the lower-level people's procuratorate.

Article 233: The people's court of second instance shall conduct a comprehensive review of the facts found and the law applied in the first instance judgment, without being limited by the scope of the appeal or protest.

In a joint crime case where only some defendants appeal, the entire case shall be reviewed and handled together.

Article 234: The people's court of second instance shall form a collegial panel and hold a court session to try the following cases:

(1) Appeal cases where the defendant, the private prosecutor, and their legal representatives raise objections to the facts and evidence found in the first instance, which may affect conviction and sentencing;

(2) Appeal cases where the defendant is sentenced to death;

(3) Cases protested by the people's procuratorate;

(4) Other cases that should be tried in court.

If the people's court of second instance decides not to hold a court session, it shall interrogate the defendant and hear the opinions of other parties, the defender, and the legal representative.

When the people's court of second instance holds a court session to try an appeal or protest case, it may conduct the trial at the place where the crime occurred or at the location of the original trial people's court.

Article 235: For cases protested by the people's procuratorate or public prosecution cases tried in court by the people's court of second instance, the people's procuratorate at the corresponding level shall send personnel to appear in court. The people's court of second instance shall promptly notify the people's procuratorate to review the case file after deciding to hold a court session. The people's procuratorate shall complete the review within one month. The time taken by the people's procuratorate to review the case file shall not be counted in the trial period.

Article 236: After trying an appeal or protest case against a first instance judgment, the people's court of second instance shall handle it according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the facts found and the law applied in the original judgment are correct and the sentence is appropriate, the appeal or protest shall be dismissed by ruling, and the original judgment shall be affirmed;

(2) If the facts found in the original judgment are correct, but the law applied is erroneous, or the sentence is inappropriate, the judgment shall be amended;

(3) If the facts in the original judgment are unclear or the evidence is insufficient, the judgment may be amended after the facts are clarified; or the original judgment may be reversed by ruling and the case remanded to the original trial people's court for retrial.

If, after the original trial people's court renders a judgment on a case remanded for retrial in accordance with item (3) of the preceding paragraph, the defendant appeals or the people's procuratorate protests, the people's court of second instance shall render a judgment or ruling according to law and shall not remand the case to the original trial people's court for retrial again.Article 237 When a case is appealed by the defendant, their legal representative, defender, or close relative, the court of second instance shall not increase the defendant's punishment. When a case is remanded by the court of second instance to the original trial court for retrial, the original trial court shall not increase the defendant's punishment, except where new criminal facts are discovered and the People's Procuratorate supplements the prosecution.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply where the People's Procuratorate files a protest or the private prosecutor files an appeal.

Article 238 If the court of second instance finds that the proceedings of the court of first instance involve any of the following violations of legal procedures, it shall rule to vacate the original judgment and remand the case to the original trial court for retrial:

(1) Violation of the provisions of this Law regarding public trial;
(2) Violation of the recusal system;
(3) Deprivation or restriction of the parties' procedural rights, which may affect the fairness of the trial;
(4) Illegal composition of the trial panel;
(5) Other violations of legal procedures that may affect the fairness of the trial.

Article 239 For a case remanded for retrial, the original trial court shall form a new collegial panel and conduct the trial in accordance with the procedures of first instance. The judgment rendered after retrial may be appealed or protested in accordance with Articles 227, 228, and 229 of this Law.

Article 240 After reviewing an appeal or protest against a ruling of first instance, the court of second instance shall, by reference to Articles 236, 238, and 239 of this Law, rule to dismiss the appeal or protest, or to revoke or modify the original ruling, as the case may be.

Article 241 When a case is remanded by the court of second instance to the original trial court for retrial, the time limit for trial shall be recalculated from the date the original trial court receives the remanded case.

Article 242 The procedures for the court of second instance to hear an appeal or protest case shall, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, be governed by the provisions on procedures of first instance.

Article 243 The court of second instance shall conclude an appeal or protest case within two months. For cases where the death penalty may be imposed, or cases with incidental civil actions, or cases involving any of the circumstances specified in Article 158 of this Law, the time limit may be extended by two months with the approval or decision of the Higher People's Court of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; if further extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be submitted to the Supreme People's Court for approval.

The time limit for the Supreme People's Court to hear appeal or protest cases shall be determined by the Supreme People's Court itself.

Article 244 Judgments and rulings of second instance and those of the Supreme People's Court are final.

Article 245 Public security organs, People's Procuratorates, and People's Courts shall properly keep the property and its fruits of criminal suspects or defendants that have been sealed, seized, or frozen for inspection, and shall prepare an inventory to be transferred with the case. No unit or individual may misappropriate or dispose of them without authorization. Lawful property of victims shall be returned promptly. Contraband or items unsuitable for long-term storage shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Physical evidence used as evidence shall be transferred with the case; if it is not suitable for transfer, its inventory, photographs, or other supporting documents shall be transferred with the case.

The judgment rendered by the People's Court shall address the disposition of the sealed, seized, or frozen property and its fruits.

After the judgment of the People's Court takes effect, the relevant authorities shall dispose of the sealed, seized, or frozen property and its fruits in accordance with the judgment. The illicit money and goods and their fruits that have been sealed, seized, or frozen shall, except for those returned to the victim according to law, be turned over to the state treasury.

If judicial officers embezzle, misappropriate, or privately dispose of the sealed, seized, or frozen property and its fruits, they shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, disciplinary action shall be taken.

### Chapter 4: Procedure for Review of Death Sentences

Article 246 The death penalty shall be subject to approval by the Supreme People's Court.

Article 247 For a case of first instance where the Intermediate People's Court imposes the death penalty and the defendant does not appeal, the case shall be reviewed by the Higher People's Court and then submitted to the Supreme People's Court for approval. If the Higher People's Court disagrees with the death sentence, it may either bring the case up for trial itself or remand it for retrial.

If the defendant does not appeal a death sentence imposed by the Higher People's Court in a case of first instance, or in a case of second instance where the death penalty is imposed, the case shall be submitted to the Supreme People's Court for approval.

Article 248 Cases where the Intermediate People's Court imposes a death sentence with a two-year reprieve shall be subject to approval by the Higher People's Court.

Article 249 When the Supreme People's Court reviews a death sentence case, or the Higher People's Court reviews a death sentence with a two-year reprieve, a collegial panel composed of three judges shall be formed.

Article 250 When the Supreme People's Court reviews a death sentence case, it shall render a ruling approving or disapproving the death sentence. If the death sentence is not approved, the Supreme People's Court may remand the case for retrial or modify the sentence.

Article 251 When the Supreme People's Court reviews a death sentence case, it shall interrogate the defendant, and if the defense lawyer so requests, it shall hear the defense lawyer's opinion.

During the review of a death sentence case, the Supreme People's Procuratorate may submit its opinions to the Supreme People's Court. The Supreme People's Court shall notify the Supreme People's Procuratorate of the result of the death sentence review.

### Chapter 5: Procedure for Trial Supervision

Article 252 Parties and their legal representatives or close relatives may file a petition with the People's Court or the People's Procuratorate against a legally effective judgment or ruling, but the execution of the judgment or ruling shall not be suspended.

Article 253 The People's Court shall retry a case if the petition filed by a party or their legal representative or close relative meets any of the following conditions:

(1) New evidence proves that the facts determined in the original judgment or ruling are indeed erroneous, which may affect the conviction and sentencing;
(2) The evidence on which the conviction and sentencing were based is unreliable or insufficient, and should have been excluded according to law, or there are contradictions among the main evidence proving the facts of the case;
(3) The application of law in the original judgment or ruling is indeed erroneous;
(4) There is a violation of legal procedures that may affect the fairness of the trial;
(5) During the trial of the case, the judicial personnel engaged in acts of corruption, bribery, favoritism, or perversion of the law.

Article 254 If the president of a People's Court at any level finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of that court is indeed erroneous in the determination of facts or the application of law, it must be submitted to the judicial committee for handling.

If the Supreme People's Court finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a People's Court at any level is indeed erroneous, or if a People's Court at a higher level finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a People's Court at a lower level is indeed erroneous, it has the authority to bring the case up for trial itself or to direct the lower court to conduct a retrial.

If the Supreme People's Procuratorate finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a People's Court at any level is indeed erroneous, or if a People's Procuratorate at a higher level finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a People's Court at a lower level is indeed erroneous, it has the authority to file a protest with the People's Court at the same level in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision.

For a case protested by the People's Procuratorate, the People's Court that accepts the protest shall form a collegial panel to conduct a retrial. If the facts of the original judgment are unclear or the evidence is insufficient, it may direct a People's Court at a lower level to conduct a retrial.

Article 255 When a People's Court at a higher level directs a People's Court at a lower level to conduct a retrial, it shall direct a People's Court other than the original trial court to hear the case; if it is more appropriate for the original trial court to hear the case, it may also direct the original trial court to conduct the retrial.

Article 256 When a People's Court retries a case in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, if the retrial is conducted by the original trial court, a new collegial panel shall be formed. If the original case was a case of first instance, it shall be tried in accordance with the procedures of first instance, and the resulting judgment or ruling may be appealed or protested; if the original case was a case of second instance, or a case brought up for trial by a People's Court at a higher level, it shall be tried in accordance with the procedures of second instance, and the resulting judgment or ruling is final.

When a People's Court holds a hearing for a retrial case, the People's Procuratorate at the same level shall send personnel to appear in court.

Article 257 If a People's Court decides to retry a case and it is necessary to take compulsory measures against the defendant, the People's Court shall make the decision according to law; if the retrial case is protested by the People's Procuratorate and it is necessary to take compulsory measures against the defendant, the People's Procuratorate shall make the decision according to law.

When a People's Court tries a case in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, it may decide to suspend the execution of the original judgment or ruling.

Article 258 When a People's Court retries a case in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, it shall conclude the case within three months from the date of the decision to bring the case up for trial or to retry it; if an extension is necessary, it shall not exceed six months.

For a protested case tried by the People's Court that accepts the protest in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, the time limit for trial shall be governed by the provisions of the preceding paragraph; if it is necessary to direct a People's Court at a lower level to conduct a retrial, a decision shall be made within one month from the date of accepting the protest, and the time limit for the lower court to try the case shall be governed by the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

## Part Four: Execution

Article 259 Judgments and rulings shall be executed after they become legally effective.

The following judgments and rulings are legally effective:

(1) Judgments and rulings against which no appeal or protest has been filed within the statutory time limit;
(2) Final judgments and rulings;
(3) Death sentences approved by the Supreme People's Court and death sentences with a two-year reprieve approved by the Higher People's Court.

Article 260 If a People's Court of first instance renders a judgment of acquittal or exemption from criminal punishment, and the defendant is in custody, the defendant shall be released immediately after the judgment is pronounced.

Article 261 A death sentence imposed or approved by the Supreme People's Court and to be executed immediately shall be carried out upon an order for execution signed by the President of the Supreme People's Court.

For a criminal sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve, if during the period of reprieve they do not intentionally commit a crime, and upon the expiration of the reprieve period a commutation of sentence is to be granted, the executing organ shall submit a written opinion to the Higher People's Court for a ruling; if they intentionally commit a crime, the circumstances are flagrant, and the facts are verified, and the death penalty is to be executed, the Higher People's Court shall submit the case to the Supreme People's Court for approval; if the death penalty is not executed for an intentional crime, the period of the death sentence with a two-year reprieve shall be recalculated, and the Supreme People's Court shall be notified for the record.

Article 262 After receiving the order for execution of a death sentence from the Supreme People's Court, the lower People's Court shall deliver the criminal for execution within seven days. However, if any of the following circumstances is discovered, execution shall be suspended, and the Supreme People's Court shall be immediately reported for a ruling:

(1) It is found before execution that the judgment may be erroneous;
(2) Before execution, the criminal exposes major criminal facts or has other major meritorious performances, which may necessitate a commutation of sentence;
(3) The criminal is pregnant.

After the reasons for suspension under items (1) and (2) of the preceding paragraph cease to exist, a new order for execution must be signed by the President of the Supreme People's Court before execution can be carried out; if execution is suspended due to the reason under item (3) of the preceding paragraph, the Supreme People's Court shall be reported for a modification of the sentence according to law.

Article 263 Before delivering a criminal for execution of the death penalty, the People's Court shall notify the People's Procuratorate at the same level to send personnel to supervise the execution on site.

The death penalty shall be executed by shooting, injection, or other methods.

The death penalty may be executed at an execution ground or within a designated detention facility.

The judicial officer directing the execution shall verify the identity of the criminal, ask if they have any last words or letters, and then deliver the criminal to the execution personnel for execution. If any possible error is discovered before execution, execution shall be suspended and reported to the Supreme People's Court for a ruling.

The execution of the death penalty shall be announced but not displayed to the public.

After the execution of the death penalty, the court clerk present shall make a written record. The People's Court that delivered the criminal for execution shall report the circumstances of the execution to the Supreme People's Court.

After the execution of the death penalty, the People's Court that delivered the criminal for execution shall notify the criminal's family.

Article 264 When a criminal is delivered for execution of a criminal penalty, the People's Court that delivers the criminal for execution shall, within ten days after the judgment takes effect, send the relevant legal documents to the public security organ, prison, or other executing organ.

For a criminal sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve, life imprisonment, or fixed-term imprisonment, the public security organ shall, according to law, deliver the criminal to a prison for execution of the penalty. For a criminal sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment, if the remaining term is less than three months before delivery for execution, the detention center shall execute the penalty on behalf of the prison. For a criminal sentenced to criminal detention, the public security organ shall execute the penalty.

Juvenile offenders shall serve their sentences in a juvenile correctional facility.

The executing organ shall take the criminal into custody promptly and notify the criminal's family.

For a criminal sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or criminal detention, upon the expiration of the sentence, the executing organ shall issue a certificate of release.

Article 265 For a criminal sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or criminal detention, if any of the following circumstances exists, the sentence may be temporarily served outside prison:

(1) Suffering from a serious illness requiring medical treatment outside prison;
(2) Being pregnant or nursing their own infant;
(3) Being unable to care for themselves, and serving the sentence outside prison would not endanger society.

For a criminal sentenced to life imprisonment, if the circumstance under item (2) of the preceding paragraph exists, the sentence may be temporarily served outside prison.

For a criminal who may pose a social danger if granted medical treatment outside prison, or a criminal who has self-inflicted injuries or disabilities, medical treatment outside prison shall not be granted.

If a criminal is indeed suffering from a serious illness and must receive medical treatment outside prison, a hospital designated by the provincial-level people's government shall diagnose and issue a certificate.

Before delivery for execution, the decision to allow temporary service of sentence outside prison shall be made by the People's Court that delivers the criminal for execution; after delivery for execution, the prison or detention center shall submit a written opinion to the prison administration authority at or above the provincial level or the public security organ at or above the level of a city divided into districts for approval.

Article 266 When a prison or detention center submits a written opinion on temporary service of sentence outside prison, a copy of the written opinion shall be sent to the People's Procuratorate. The People's Procuratorate may submit a written opinion to the deciding or approving authority.

Article 267 The authority that decides or approves temporary service of sentence outside prison shall send a copy of the decision to the People's Procuratorate. If the People's Procuratorate considers the decision inappropriate, it shall, within one month from receipt of the notice, send a written opinion to the authority that decided or approved the temporary service of sentence outside prison. Upon receipt of the written opinion from the People's Procuratorate, the authority shall immediately re-examine the decision.

Article 268 If any of the following circumstances exists for a criminal serving a sentence temporarily outside prison, the criminal shall be promptly taken back into custody:

(1) It is discovered that the conditions for temporary service of sentence outside prison are not met;
(2) Serious violation of the supervision and management regulations for temporary service of sentence outside prison;
(3) After the circumstances for temporary service of sentence outside prison cease to exist, the criminal's sentence has not yet expired.

If a criminal who was allowed to serve a sentence temporarily outside prison by a People's Court decision needs to be taken back into custody, the People's Court shall make the decision and send the relevant legal documents to the public security organ, prison, or other executing organ.

If a criminal who does not meet the conditions for temporary service of sentence outside prison is allowed to serve the sentence outside prison through illegal means such as bribery, the period served outside prison shall not be counted toward the execution of the sentence. If a criminal escapes during the period of temporary service of sentence outside prison, the period of escape shall not be counted toward the execution of the sentence.

If a criminal dies during the period of temporary service of sentence outside prison, the executing organ shall promptly notify the prison or detention center.

Article 269 For a criminal sentenced to public surveillance, granted probation, granted parole, or allowed to serve a sentence temporarily outside prison, community correction shall be implemented according to law, and the community correction institution shall be responsible for execution.

Article 270 For a criminal sentenced to deprivation of political rights, the public security organ shall execute the sentence. Upon the expiration of the sentence, the executing organ shall notify the criminal in writing, as well as their work unit and the grassroots organization of their place of residence.

Article 271 If a criminal sentenced to a fine fails to pay it upon expiration of the time limit, the People's Court shall enforce payment; if payment is genuinely difficult due to irresistible disasters or other reasons, the People's Court may, by ruling, grant an extension, reduce the amount, or waive the fine.

Article 272 A judgment of confiscation of property, whether imposed as a supplementary or independent penalty, shall be executed by the People's Court; when necessary, it may be executed jointly with the public security organ.

Article 273 If a criminal commits another crime while serving a sentence, or if a crime not discovered at the time of judgment is discovered, the executing organ shall transfer the case to the People's Procuratorate for handling.

If a criminal sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, fixed-term imprisonment, or life imprisonment demonstrates genuine repentance or meritorious performance during the execution of the sentence and should be granted a commutation of sentence or parole according to law, the executing organ shall submit a recommendation to the People's Court for review and ruling, and send a copy of the recommendation to the People's Procuratorate. The People's Procuratorate may submit a written opinion to the People's Court.

Article 274 If the People's Procuratorate considers a ruling on commutation of sentence or parole by the People's Court to be inappropriate, it shall, within twenty days from receipt of a copy of the ruling, submit a written opinion of correction to the People's Court. The People's Court shall, within one month from receipt of the opinion of correction, form a new collegial panel to conduct a hearing and render a final ruling.

Article 275 If a prison or other executing organ believes that a judgment is erroneous during the execution of a criminal penalty, or if a criminal files a petition, the matter shall be referred to the People's Procuratorate or the People's Court that rendered the original judgment for handling.

Article 276 The People's Procuratorate shall supervise whether the activities of the executing organ in executing criminal penalties are lawful. If any illegal activities are discovered, the executing organ shall be notified to correct them.

## Part Five: Special Procedures

### Chapter 1: Criminal Procedure for Juvenile Cases

Article 277 The policy of education, persuasion, and redemption shall be applied to juvenile offenders, adhering to the principle of education as the primary approach and punishment as supplementary.

When handling criminal cases involving juveniles, the People's Court, People's Procuratorate, and public security organs shall ensure that juveniles exercise their procedural rights, receive legal assistance, and that the cases are handled by judicial personnel, procuratorial personnel, and investigative personnel who are familiar with the physical and psychological characteristics of juveniles.

Article 278 If a juvenile criminal suspect or defendant has not retained a defender, the People's Court, People's Procuratorate, or public security organ shall notify a legal aid agency to appoint a lawyer to provide defense.

Article 279 When handling criminal cases involving juveniles, the public security organs, People's Procuratorates, and People's Courts may, as appropriate, investigate the juvenile criminal suspect's or defendant's growth experience, causes of the crime, guardianship and education, and other circumstances.

Article 280 The application of arrest measures to juvenile criminal suspects or defendants shall be strictly restricted. When the People's Procuratorate reviews and approves an arrest, or the People's Court decides on an arrest, it shall interrogate the juvenile criminal suspect or defendant and hear the opinion of the defense lawyer.

Juveniles who are detained, arrested, or serving sentences shall be held separately from adults, managed separately, and educated separately.Article 281: In juvenile criminal cases, during interrogation and trial, the legal representative of the juvenile suspect or defendant shall be notified to be present. If notification is impossible, the legal representative cannot be present, or the legal representative is an accomplice, other adult relatives of the juvenile suspect or defendant, or representatives from their school, work unit, residential community organization, or juvenile protection organization may be notified to be present, and the relevant circumstances shall be recorded. The legal representative present may exercise the litigation rights of the juvenile suspect or defendant on their behalf.

The legal representative or other person present who believes that the case-handling personnel have infringed upon the juvenile's lawful rights and interests during interrogation or trial may raise objections. The interrogation record and court record shall be given to the legal representative or other person present for reading or shall be read to them.

When interrogating a female juvenile suspect, a female staff member shall be present.

In the trial of a juvenile criminal case, after the juvenile defendant makes their final statement, their legal representative may make a supplementary statement.

The provisions of paragraphs 1, 2, and 3 apply to the questioning of juvenile victims and witnesses.

Article 282: For a juvenile suspected of committing crimes stipulated in Chapters IV, V, and VI of the Specific Provisions of the Criminal Law, who may be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than one year, meets the conditions for prosecution, but shows signs of repentance, the People's Procuratorate may decide on conditional non-prosecution. Before making a decision on conditional non-prosecution, the People's Procuratorate shall hear the opinions of the public security organ and the victim.

If the public security organ requests a review or files for a reconsideration, or if the victim files a complaint regarding the decision on conditional non-prosecution, the provisions of Articles 179 and 180 of this Law shall apply.

If the juvenile suspect or their legal representative objects to the People's Procuratorate's decision on conditional non-prosecution, the People's Procuratorate shall make a decision to prosecute.

Article 283: During the probation period for conditional non-prosecution, the People's Procuratorate shall supervise and inspect the juvenile suspect subject to conditional non-prosecution. The guardian of the juvenile suspect shall strengthen discipline and guidance over the juvenile suspect and cooperate with the People's Procuratorate in carrying out the supervision and inspection.

The probation period for conditional non-prosecution shall be not less than six months and not more than one year, calculated from the date the People's Procuratorate makes the decision on conditional non-prosecution.

A juvenile suspect subject to conditional non-prosecution shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) Abide by laws and regulations and submit to supervision;

(2) Report their activities in accordance with the requirements of the supervisory authority;

(3) Obtain approval from the supervisory authority before leaving the city or county where they reside or changing their residence;

(4) Undergo correction and education as required by the supervisory authority.

Article 284: If a juvenile suspect subject to conditional non-prosecution falls under any of the following circumstances during the probation period, the People's Procuratorate shall revoke the decision on conditional non-prosecution and initiate a public prosecution:

(1) Commits a new crime, or it is discovered that there are other crimes that need to be prosecuted before the decision on conditional non-prosecution was made;

(2) Violates public security administration regulations or the supervisory authority's rules on supervision and management related to conditional non-prosecution, where the circumstances are serious.

If a juvenile suspect subject to conditional non-prosecution does not fall under the above circumstances during the probation period, upon the expiration of the probation period, the People's Procuratorate shall make a decision not to prosecute.

Article 285: Cases where the defendant is under the age of 18 at the time of trial shall not be heard in public. However, with the consent of the juvenile defendant and their legal representative, representatives from the juvenile defendant's school and juvenile protection organization may be present.

Article 286: If a person was under the age of 18 at the time of committing a crime and was sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than five years, the relevant criminal record shall be sealed.

Sealed criminal records shall not be provided to any unit or individual, except when judicial authorities need them for case handling or when relevant units inquire in accordance with national regulations. Units that inquire in accordance with the law shall keep the information on the sealed criminal records confidential.

Article 287: The handling of juvenile criminal cases shall, in addition to the provisions of this Chapter, be conducted in accordance with other provisions of this Law.

### Chapter 2: Procedures for Public Prosecution Cases with Party Reconciliation

Article 288: For the following public prosecution cases, if the suspect or defendant sincerely repents and obtains the victim's forgiveness by compensating for losses, apologizing, or other means, and the victim voluntarily reconciles, the parties may reconcile:

(1) Cases arising from civil disputes involving crimes stipulated in Chapters IV and V of the Specific Provisions of the Criminal Law, where the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years;

(2) Cases of negligent crimes, excluding crimes of dereliction of duty, where the possible sentence is fixed-term imprisonment of not more than seven years.

If the suspect or defendant has previously committed an intentional crime within the past five years, the procedures stipulated in this Chapter shall not apply.

Article 289: If the parties reconcile, the public security organ, the People's Procuratorate, and the People's Court shall hear the opinions of the parties and other relevant persons, review the voluntariness and legality of the reconciliation, and preside over the drafting of a reconciliation agreement.

Article 290: For cases where a reconciliation agreement has been reached, the public security organ may recommend lenient handling to the People's Procuratorate. The People's Procuratorate may recommend a lenient punishment to the People's Court; if the crime is minor and does not require a criminal penalty, it may decide not to prosecute. The People's Court may impose a lenient punishment on the defendant in accordance with the law.

### Chapter 3: Trial by Default Procedures

Article 291: For cases of embezzlement and bribery, as well as cases of serious crimes endangering national security or terrorist activities that require timely trial and are approved by the Supreme People's Procuratorate, if the suspect or defendant is abroad, and the supervisory organ or public security organ transfers the case for prosecution, and the People's Procuratorate believes that the criminal facts have been clearly ascertained, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, and criminal liability should be pursued according to law, it may initiate a public prosecution with the People's Court. After review, if the indictment contains clear allegations of criminal facts and meets the conditions for applying trial by default procedures, the People's Court shall decide to hold a trial.

Cases mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be tried by a collegial panel composed of an Intermediate People's Court in the place of the crime, the place of the defendant's residence before leaving the country, or as designated by the Supreme People's Court.

Article 292: The People's Court shall serve the summons and a copy of the People's Procuratorate's indictment on the defendant through judicial assistance methods stipulated in relevant international treaties or through diplomatic channels, or through other methods permitted by the law of the defendant's location. After the summons and copy of the indictment are served, if the defendant fails to appear as required, the People's Court shall hold a trial, render a judgment according to law, and dispose of the illegal gains and other property involved in the case.

Article 293: In a trial by default case, the defendant has the right to retain a defender, and the defendant's close relatives may retain a defender on their behalf. If the defendant or their close relatives have not retained a defender, the People's Court shall notify a legal aid agency to appoint a lawyer to provide defense.

Article 294: The People's Court shall serve the judgment on the defendant, their close relatives, and the defender. If the defendant or their close relatives are dissatisfied with the judgment, they have the right to appeal to the People's Court at the next higher level. The defender may file an appeal with the consent of the defendant or their close relatives.

If the People's Procuratorate believes that the People's Court's judgment is erroneous, it shall file a protest with the People's Court at the next higher level.

Article 295: If, during the trial, the defendant voluntarily surrenders or is apprehended, the People's Court shall retry the case.

If the criminal appears after the judgment or ruling becomes legally effective, the People's Court shall deliver the criminal for execution of the sentence. Before delivering the criminal for execution, the People's Court shall inform the criminal of their right to object to the judgment or ruling. If the criminal objects to the judgment or ruling, the People's Court shall retry the case.

If the handling of the criminal's property based on an effective judgment or ruling is erroneous, the property shall be returned or compensation shall be made.

Article 296: If the defendant cannot appear in court due to a serious illness, and the trial has been suspended for more than six months, and the defendant still cannot appear, and the defendant, their legal representative, or close relatives apply for or agree to resume the trial, the People's Court may conduct a trial by default in the defendant's absence and render a judgment according to law.

Article 297: If the defendant dies, the People's Court shall rule to terminate the trial. However, if there is evidence proving the defendant's innocence, and the People's Court confirms the innocence through a trial by default, it shall render a judgment according to law.

In cases retried by the People's Court under trial supervision procedures, if the defendant dies, the People's Court may conduct a trial by default and render a judgment according to law.

### Chapter 4: Procedures for Confiscation of Illegal Gains in Cases Where the Suspect or Defendant Has Fled or Died

Article 298: For major criminal cases such as embezzlement, bribery, and terrorist activities, if the suspect or defendant has fled and cannot be brought to justice after one year of being wanted, or if the suspect or defendant has died, and according to the Criminal Law, their illegal gains and other property involved in the case should be recovered, the People's Procuratorate may apply to the People's Court for the confiscation of illegal gains.

If the public security organ believes that the circumstances described in the preceding paragraph exist, it shall prepare a written opinion on the confiscation of illegal gains and transfer it to the People's Procuratorate.

The application for confiscation of illegal gains shall provide evidence related to the criminal facts and illegal gains, and shall specify the types, quantities, locations, and status of seizure, impoundment, or freezing of the property.

When necessary, the People's Court may seize, impound, or freeze the property subject to the application for confiscation.

Article 299: An application for confiscation of illegal gains shall be tried by a collegial panel composed of an Intermediate People's Court in the place of the crime or the place of residence of the suspect or defendant.

After accepting an application for confiscation of illegal gains, the People's Court shall issue a public notice. The public notice period is six months. Close relatives of the suspect or defendant and other interested parties have the right to apply to participate in the proceedings, and may also entrust litigation representatives to participate.

After the expiration of the public notice period, the People's Court shall hear the application for confiscation of illegal gains. If interested parties participate in the proceedings, the People's Court shall hold a hearing.

Article 300: After trial, if the People's Court verifies that the property constitutes illegal gains or other property involved in the case, except for property to be returned to the victim according to law, it shall rule to confiscate it; if the property is not subject to recovery, it shall rule to dismiss the application and lift the seizure, impoundment, or freezing measures.

Close relatives of the suspect or defendant, other interested parties, or the People's Procuratorate may appeal or protest against the ruling made by the People's Court in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 301: If, during the trial, the fleeing suspect or defendant voluntarily surrenders or is apprehended, the People's Court shall terminate the trial.

If the confiscation of the suspect's or defendant's property is erroneous, the property shall be returned or compensation shall be made.

### Chapter 5: Procedures for Compulsory Medical Treatment of Mentally Ill Persons Who Are Not Criminally Responsible According to Law

Article 302: A mentally ill person who commits violent acts, endangers public safety, or seriously endangers the personal safety of citizens, and is determined through legal procedures to be not criminally responsible according to law, and who may continue to endanger society, may be subjected to compulsory medical treatment.

Article 303: Compulsory medical treatment of mentally ill persons in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter shall be decided by the People's Court.

If a public security organ discovers that a mentally ill person meets the conditions for compulsory medical treatment, it shall prepare a written opinion on compulsory medical treatment and transfer it to the People's Procuratorate. For a mentally ill person transferred by the public security organ or discovered during the review of prosecution to meet the conditions for compulsory medical treatment, the People's Procuratorate shall apply to the People's Court for compulsory medical treatment. If, during the trial of a case, the People's Court discovers that the defendant meets the conditions for compulsory medical treatment, it may decide on compulsory medical treatment.

For a mentally ill person who commits violent acts, before the People's Court decides on compulsory medical treatment, the public security organ may take temporary protective restraint measures.

Article 304: After accepting an application for compulsory medical treatment, the People's Court shall form a collegial panel to hear the case.

When hearing a case of compulsory medical treatment, the People's Court shall notify the legal representative of the person subject to the application or the defendant to be present. If the person subject to the application or the defendant has not retained a litigation representative, the People's Court shall notify a legal aid agency to appoint a lawyer to provide legal assistance.

Article 305: After trial, if the person subject to the application or the defendant meets the conditions for compulsory medical treatment, the People's Court shall make a decision on compulsory medical treatment within one month.

If the person subject to compulsory medical treatment, the victim, or their legal representatives or close relatives are dissatisfied with the decision on compulsory medical treatment, they may apply for review to the People's Court at the next higher level.

Article 306: Compulsory medical treatment institutions shall regularly conduct diagnostic assessments of persons subjected to compulsory medical treatment. For those who no longer pose a danger to others and do not require continued compulsory medical treatment, the institution shall promptly propose an opinion on lifting the treatment and report it to the People's Court that decided on the compulsory medical treatment for approval.

The person subjected to compulsory medical treatment and their close relatives have the right to apply for the lifting of compulsory medical treatment.

Article 307: The People's Procuratorate shall supervise the decisions on and execution of compulsory medical treatment.

Supplementary Provisions

Article 308: The security departments of the military shall exercise the power of investigation over criminal cases occurring within the military.

The China Coast Guard shall perform duties of maritime rights protection and law enforcement, and exercise the power of investigation over criminal cases occurring at sea.

Cases of crimes committed by criminals within prisons shall be investigated by the prison.

The security departments of the military, the China Coast Guard, and prisons shall apply the relevant provisions of this Law when handling criminal cases.
