# Mediation and Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China for Rural Land Contract and Management Disputes

Mediation and Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China

for Rural Land Contract and Management Disputes

Adopted at the Ninth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on June 27, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of fairly and promptly resolving rural land contract and management disputes, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of the parties, and promoting rural economic development and social stability.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the mediation and arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes.

Rural land contract and management disputes include:

(1) Disputes arising from the conclusion, performance, modification, rescission, or termination of rural land contracts;

(2) Disputes arising from the transfer, lease, exchange, assignment, or pooling of rural land contract management rights as shares;

(3) Disputes arising from the withdrawal or adjustment of contracted land;

(4) Disputes arising from the confirmation of rural land contract management rights;

(5) Disputes arising from the infringement of rural land contract management rights;

(6) Other rural land contract and management disputes as stipulated by laws and regulations.

Disputes arising from the expropriation of collectively owned land and related compensation shall not fall within the scope of acceptance of the rural land contract arbitration committee and may be resolved through administrative reconsideration or litigation.

Article 3 In the event of a rural land contract and management dispute, the parties may settle it through negotiation or request mediation by the villagers' committee or the people's government of a township (town).

Article 4 If the parties fail to reach a settlement through negotiation or mediation, or are unwilling to do so, they may apply for arbitration to the rural land contract arbitration committee or directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 5 Mediation and arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes shall be conducted openly, fairly, and impartially, in a convenient and efficient manner, based on facts, in compliance with the law, and with respect for social ethics.

Article 6 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen guidance on the mediation and arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes.

The rural land contract management departments of people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, support relevant mediation organizations and rural land contract arbitration committees in carrying out their work lawfully.

## Chapter II Mediation

Article 7 Villagers' committees and the people's governments of townships (towns) shall strengthen mediation work for rural land contract and management disputes, assisting the parties in reaching agreements to resolve disputes.

Article 8 A party applying for mediation of a rural land contract and management dispute may submit a written application or an oral application. If an oral application is made, the villagers' committee or the people's government of the township (town) shall record on the spot the basic information of the applicant, the matters of the dispute for which mediation is requested, the reasons, and the time.

Article 9 When mediating a rural land contract and management dispute, the villagers' committee or the people's government of the township (town) shall fully listen to the parties' statements of facts and reasons, explain relevant laws and national policies, provide patient guidance, and assist the parties in reaching an agreement.

Article 10 If an agreement is reached through mediation, the villagers' committee or the people's government of the township (town) shall produce a mediation agreement.

The mediation agreement shall become effective after being signed, sealed, or fingerprinted by both parties, and signed by the mediators and sealed by the mediation organization.

Article 11 The arbitration tribunal shall conduct mediation for rural land contract and management disputes. If an agreement is reached through mediation, the arbitration tribunal shall produce a mediation document; if mediation fails, it shall promptly render an award.

The mediation document shall state the arbitration claims and the results agreed upon by the parties. The mediation document shall be signed by the arbitrators, sealed by the rural land contract arbitration committee, and served to both parties.

The mediation document shall become legally effective upon receipt and signature by both parties. If a party retracts before signing the mediation document, the arbitration tribunal shall promptly render an award.

## Chapter III Arbitration

### Section 1 Arbitration Committee and Arbitrators

Article 12 Rural land contract arbitration committees shall be established based on the actual needs for resolving rural land contract and management disputes. Such committees may be established in counties and cities without districts, or in cities with districts or their municipal districts.

Rural land contract arbitration committees shall be established under the guidance of the local people's government. The daily work of an established rural land contract arbitration committee shall be undertaken by the local rural land contract management department.

Article 13 A rural land contract arbitration committee shall be composed of representatives from the local people's government and its relevant departments, representatives from relevant people's organizations, representatives from rural collective economic organizations, representatives of farmers, and relevant professionals in law and economics, serving concurrently. Among them, representatives of farmers and relevant professionals in law and economics shall account for no less than one-half of the members.

A rural land contract arbitration committee shall have one chairperson, one to two vice-chairpersons, and several members. The chairperson and vice-chairpersons shall be elected by all members.

Article 14 A rural land contract arbitration committee shall perform the following duties in accordance with the law:

(1) Appointing and dismissing arbitrators;

(2) Accepting arbitration applications;

(3) Supervising arbitration activities.

A rural land contract arbitration committee shall formulate its charter in accordance with this Law, stipulating the method of selection and term of office of its members, as well as its rules of procedure.

Article 15 A rural land contract arbitration committee shall appoint arbitrators from among persons who are fair and upright.

An arbitrator shall meet one of the following conditions:

(1) Having been engaged in rural land contract management work for at least five years;

(2) Having been engaged in legal work or people's mediation work for at least five years;

(3) Being a resident with high local prestige and familiarity with rural land contract laws and national policies.

Article 16 A rural land contract arbitration committee shall provide training to arbitrators on rural land contract laws and national policies.

The rural land contract management departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate training plans for arbitrators and strengthen the organization and guidance of arbitrator training.

Article 17 Members and arbitrators of a rural land contract arbitration committee shall perform their duties in accordance with the law, abide by the charter and arbitration rules of the committee, and shall not solicit or accept bribes, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of the parties.

If an arbitrator engages in illegal or disciplinary acts such as soliciting or accepting bribes, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, rendering a judgment in violation of the law, or accepting gifts or entertainment from parties, the rural land contract arbitration committee shall remove them; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall accept complaints and reports of illegal or disciplinary acts by members and arbitrators of rural land contract arbitration committees and shall investigate and handle them in accordance with the law.

### Section 2 Application and Acceptance

Article 18 The statute of limitations for applying for arbitration of a rural land contract and management dispute is two years, calculated from the date when the party knows or should have known that their rights have been infringed.

Article 19 The applicant and the respondent in arbitration of a rural land contract and management dispute are the parties. For household contracts, a representative of the farming household may participate in the arbitration. If one party has a large number of persons, representatives may be elected to participate in the arbitration.

Persons with an interest in the outcome of the case may apply to participate in the arbitration as a third party, or be notified by the rural land contract arbitration committee to participate.

Parties and third parties may appoint agents to participate in the arbitration.

Article 20 An application for arbitration of a rural land contract and management dispute shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The applicant has a direct interest in the dispute;

(2) There is a clear respondent;

(3) There are specific arbitration claims, facts, and reasons;

(4) The dispute falls within the scope of acceptance of the rural land contract arbitration committee.

Article 21 A party applying for arbitration shall submit an arbitration application to the rural land contract arbitration committee of the place where the land involved in the dispute is located. The arbitration application may be sent by mail or entrusted to another person for submission. The arbitration application shall state the basic information of the applicant and the respondent, the arbitration claims and the facts and reasons on which they are based, and provide corresponding evidence and the source of the evidence.

If a written application is genuinely difficult, an oral application may be made, which shall be recorded in the minutes by the rural land contract arbitration committee, and after verification by the applicant, signed, sealed, or fingerprinted by them.

Article 22 The rural land contract arbitration committee shall review the arbitration application and, if it deems it meets the conditions stipulated in Article 20 of this Law, shall accept it. In any of the following circumstances, the application shall not be accepted; if already accepted, the arbitration proceedings shall be terminated:

(1) The application does not meet the conditions;

(2) The people's court has already accepted the dispute;

(3) The law stipulates that the dispute should be handled by another institution;

(4) There is already an effective judgment, ruling, arbitration award, or administrative decision regarding the dispute.

Article 23 If the rural land contract arbitration committee decides to accept the application, it shall, within five working days from the date of receiving the arbitration application, serve the notice of acceptance, the arbitration rules, and the list of arbitrators to the applicant; if it decides not to accept the application or to terminate the arbitration proceedings, it shall, within five working days from the date of receiving the arbitration application or discovering the circumstances for termination, notify the applicant in writing and state the reasons.

Article 24 The rural land contract arbitration committee shall, within five working days from the date of accepting the arbitration application, serve the notice of acceptance, a copy of the arbitration application, the arbitration rules, and the list of arbitrators to the respondent.

Article 25 The respondent shall, within ten days from the date of receiving the copy of the arbitration application, submit a statement of defense to the rural land contract arbitration committee; if a written defense is genuinely difficult, an oral defense may be made, which shall be recorded in the minutes by the rural land contract arbitration committee, and after verification by the respondent, signed, sealed, or fingerprinted by them. The rural land contract arbitration committee shall, within five working days from the date of receiving the statement of defense, serve a copy of the statement of defense to the applicant. The failure of the respondent to submit a statement of defense shall not affect the progress of the arbitration proceedings.

Article 26 If, due to the actions of the other party or other reasons, an award may become unenforceable or difficult to enforce, a party may apply for property preservation.

If a party applies for property preservation, the rural land contract arbitration committee shall submit the party's application to the basic people's court of the place where the respondent is domiciled or where the property is located.

If the application is erroneous, the applicant shall compensate the respondent for losses suffered due to the property preservation.

### Section 3 Composition of the Arbitration Tribunal

Article 27 An arbitration tribunal shall be composed of three arbitrators, with the presiding arbitrator selected jointly by the parties and the other two arbitrators selected individually by each party; if the parties cannot select them, they shall be appointed by the chairperson of the rural land contract arbitration committee.

For rural land contract and management disputes where the facts are clear, rights and obligations are明確, and the dispute is minor, with the consent of both parties, the arbitration may be conducted by a single arbitrator. The arbitrator shall be selected jointly by the parties or appointed by the chairperson of the rural land contract arbitration committee.
The rural land contract arbitration committee shall, within two working days from the date of the composition of the arbitration tribunal, notify the parties of the composition.

Article 28 An arbitrator must withdraw from the case in any of the following circumstances, and the parties also have the right to apply for their withdrawal orally or in writing:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party or agent;

(2) Having an interest in the case;

(3) Having another relationship with a party or agent of the case that may affect impartial arbitration;

(4) Privately meeting with a party or agent, or accepting gifts or entertainment from a party or agent.

A party applying for withdrawal shall state the reasons and submit the application before the first hearing. If the grounds for withdrawal become known after the first hearing, the application may be submitted before the conclusion of the last hearing.

Article 29 The rural land contract arbitration committee shall promptly decide on the application for withdrawal and notify the parties orally or in writing, stating the reasons.

Whether an arbitrator should withdraw shall be decided by the chairperson of the rural land contract arbitration committee; if the chairperson of the rural land contract arbitration committee serves as an arbitrator, the decision shall be made collectively by the committee.

If an arbitrator is unable to perform their duties due to withdrawal or other reasons, a new arbitrator shall be selected or appointed in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

### Section 4 Hearing and Award

Article 30 Arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes shall be conducted through a hearing.

The hearing may be held in the township (town) or village where the land involved in the dispute is located, or at the seat of the rural land contract arbitration committee. If both parties request a hearing in the township (town) or village, the hearing shall be held there.

The hearing shall be open, except when state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy are involved, or when the parties agree not to have it open.

Article 31 The arbitration tribunal shall notify the parties and other participants in the arbitration of the time and place of the hearing at least five working days before the hearing.

If a party has a valid reason, they may request the arbitration tribunal to change the time or place of the hearing. The arbitration tribunal shall decide whether to make the change.

Article 32 After applying for arbitration, the parties may settle the dispute through negotiation. If a settlement agreement is reached, they may request the arbitration tribunal to render an award based on the settlement agreement, or they may withdraw the arbitration application.

Article 33 The applicant may waive or modify the arbitration claims. The respondent may admit or refute the arbitration claims and has the right to file counterclaims.

Article 34 Before the arbitration tribunal renders an award, if the applicant withdraws the arbitration application, the arbitration tribunal shall terminate the arbitration, except when the respondent has filed counterclaims.

Article 35 If the applicant, after being notified in writing, fails to appear in court without a valid reason or leaves the hearing midway without the permission of the arbitration tribunal, the application may be deemed withdrawn.

If the respondent, after being notified in writing, fails to appear in court without a valid reason or leaves the hearing midway without the permission of the arbitration tribunal, an award may be made by default.

Article 36 During the hearing, the parties have the right to express opinions, state facts and reasons, provide evidence, conduct cross-examination, and engage in debate. For parties who are not proficient in the local commonly used language, the rural land contract arbitration committee shall provide interpretation services.

Article 37 The parties shall provide evidence to support their claims. If evidence related to the dispute is in the possession or under the management of the party that is the land contractor, that party shall provide it within the time limit specified by the arbitration tribunal; if it is not provided within the time limit, they shall bear adverse consequences.

Article 38 The arbitration tribunal may collect evidence on its own if it deems it necessary.

Article 39 If the arbitration tribunal considers that a specialized issue requires appraisal, it may entrust the appraisal to an appraisal institution agreed upon by the parties; if the parties have not agreed, it shall be entrusted to an appraisal institution designated by the arbitration tribunal.

At the request of the parties or the requirement of the arbitration tribunal, the appraisal institution shall send an appraiser to attend the hearing. With the permission of the arbitration tribunal, the parties may question the appraiser.

Article 40 Evidence shall be presented during the hearing, except that evidence involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy shall not be presented in an open hearing.

The arbitration tribunal shall conduct the hearing in accordance with the arbitration rules, providing both parties with equal opportunities to present statements and debate, and organizing cross-examination among the parties.

Evidence verified by the arbitration tribunal shall serve as the basis for determining the facts.

Article 41 If evidence may be lost or become difficult to obtain later, a party may apply for evidence preservation. If a party applies for evidence preservation, the rural land contract arbitration committee shall submit the party's application to the basic people's court of the place where the evidence is located.

Article 42 For disputes with clear rights and obligations, upon application by a party, the arbitration tribunal may make an interim ruling to maintain the status quo, restore agricultural production, or stop actions such as soil extraction or land occupation.

If one party fails to comply with the interim ruling, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement, but shall provide corresponding security.

Article 43 The arbitration tribunal shall record the proceedings of the hearing in the minutes, which shall be signed, sealed, or fingerprinted by the arbitrators, recorders, parties, and other participants in the arbitration.

If parties or other participants in the arbitration believe that there are omissions or errors in the recording of their statements, they have the right to apply for corrections. If corrections are not made, the application shall be recorded.

Article 44 The arbitration tribunal shall render an award based on the determined facts, law, and national policies, and produce a written award.

The award shall be made according to the opinion of the majority of the arbitrators; the dissenting opinions of the minority may be recorded in the minutes. If the arbitration tribunal cannot form a majority opinion, the award shall be made according to the opinion of the presiding arbitrator.

Article 45 The written award shall state the arbitration claims, the disputed facts, the reasons for the award, the result of the award, the date of the award, and the right and time limit for the parties to file a lawsuit if they are dissatisfied with the arbitration award. It shall be signed by the arbitrators and sealed by the rural land contract arbitration committee.

The rural land contract arbitration committee shall, within three working days from the date of making the award, serve the written award to the parties and inform them of their right and time limit to file a lawsuit if they are dissatisfied with the arbitration award.

Article 46 The arbitration tribunal shall independently perform its duties in accordance with the law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 47 Arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes shall be concluded within sixty days from the date of accepting the arbitration application; if the case is complex and requires an extension, it may be extended with the approval of the chairperson of the rural land contract arbitration committee, and the parties shall be notified in writing, but the extension period shall not exceed thirty days.

Article 48 If a party is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court within thirty days from the date of receiving the written award. If no lawsuit is filed within the time limit, the written award shall become legally effective.

Article 49 The parties shall perform the mediation document or written award that has become legally effective within the specified time limit. If one party fails to perform within the time limit, the other party may apply to the basic people's court of the place where the respondent is domiciled or where the property is located for enforcement. The people's court that accepts the application shall enforce it in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IV Supplementary Provisions

Article 50 For the purposes of this Law, "rural land" refers to cultivated land, forestland, and grassland owned by peasant collectives or owned by the state but lawfully used by peasant collectives, as well as other land lawfully used for agriculture.

Article 51 The arbitration rules for rural land contract and management disputes and the model charter for rural land contract arbitration committees shall be jointly formulated by the administrative departments of agriculture and forestry under the State Council in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 52 No fees shall be charged to the parties for arbitration of rural land contract and management disputes, and the working expenses for arbitration shall be included in the fiscal budget for guarantee.

Article 53 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2010.
