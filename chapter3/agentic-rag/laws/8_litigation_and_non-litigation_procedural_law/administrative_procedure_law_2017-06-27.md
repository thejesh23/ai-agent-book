# Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 4, 1989

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the 11th Session of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on November 1, 2014

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the 28th Session of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on June 27, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure that the people's courts fairly and promptly try administrative cases, resolve administrative disputes, protect the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations, and supervise the lawful exercise of powers by administrative organs.

Article 2 A citizen, legal person, or other organization that believes that an administrative act of an administrative organ or its personnel infringes upon its lawful rights and interests has the right to file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with this Law.

The term "administrative act" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph includes administrative acts performed by organizations authorized by laws, regulations, or rules.

Article 3 The people's courts shall protect the right of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to file lawsuits and shall accept administrative cases that should be accepted in accordance with the law.

Administrative organs and their personnel shall not interfere with or obstruct the people's courts from accepting administrative cases.

The head of the administrative organ being sued shall appear in court to respond to the lawsuit. If unable to appear, the organ shall entrust a relevant staff member to appear in court.

Article 4 The people's courts shall independently exercise their adjudicative power over administrative cases in accordance with the law, free from interference by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

The people's courts shall establish administrative divisions to hear administrative cases.

Article 5 In trying administrative cases, the people's courts shall base their decisions on facts and take the law as the criterion.

Article 6 In trying administrative cases, the people's courts shall review the legality of administrative acts.

Article 7 In trying administrative cases, the people's courts shall implement the systems of collegial panels, withdrawal, public trial, and the system of final appeal from a court of second instance in accordance with the law.

Article 8 The parties in administrative proceedings shall have equal legal status.

Article 9 Citizens of all ethnic groups shall have the right to use their own spoken and written languages in administrative proceedings.

In areas where minority nationalities live in concentrated communities or where multiple nationalities live together, the people's courts shall conduct trials and issue legal documents in the spoken and written languages commonly used by the local nationalities.

The people's courts shall provide translation for participants in proceedings who are not familiar with the spoken or written languages commonly used by the local nationalities.

Article 10 Parties in administrative proceedings shall have the right to debate.

Article 11 The people's procuratorates shall have the right to exercise legal supervision over administrative proceedings.

## Chapter II Scope of Acceptable Cases

Article 12 The people's courts shall accept lawsuits filed by citizens, legal persons, or other organizations against the following matters:

(1) Dissatisfaction with administrative penalties such as administrative detention, suspension or revocation of permits and licenses, orders to suspend production or business, confiscation of illegal gains, confiscation of illegal property, fines, or warnings;

(2) Dissatisfaction with compulsory administrative measures such as restrictions on personal freedom or seizure, impoundment, or freezing of property, and compulsory administrative enforcement;

(3) Application for an administrative permit, where the administrative organ refuses or fails to respond within the statutory period, or dissatisfaction with other decisions made by the administrative organ concerning the administrative permit;

(4) Dissatisfaction with decisions made by administrative organs concerning the confirmation of ownership or right to use natural resources such as land, mineral deposits, water bodies, forests, mountains, grasslands, wasteland, tidal flats, or sea areas;

(5) Dissatisfaction with decisions on expropriation or requisition and the corresponding compensation decisions;

(6) Application to an administrative organ to perform its statutory duty to protect personal rights, property rights, or other lawful rights and interests, where the administrative organ refuses to perform or fails to respond;

(7) Belief that an administrative organ has infringed upon its right to autonomous operation, or the right to contracted management of rural land, or the right to operate rural land;

(8) Belief that an administrative organ has abused its administrative power to exclude or restrict competition;

(9) Belief that an administrative organ has illegally raised funds, apportioned expenses, or illegally demanded the performance of other obligations;

(10) Belief that an administrative organ has failed to pay pensions, minimum subsistence allowances, or social insurance benefits in accordance with the law;

(11) Belief that an administrative organ has failed to perform, failed to perform as agreed, or illegally changed or terminated agreements such as government concession agreements or agreements on compensation for land and house expropriation; and

(12) Belief that an administrative organ has infringed upon other personal rights, property rights, or other lawful rights and interests.

In addition to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the people's courts shall accept other administrative cases that may be filed in accordance with the provisions of laws or regulations.

Article 13 The people's courts shall not accept lawsuits filed by citizens, legal persons, or other organizations against the following matters:

(1) State acts such as national defense and foreign affairs;

(2) Administrative regulations, rules, or decisions and orders with general binding force formulated and issued by administrative organs;

(3) Decisions of administrative organs on rewards, appointments, or dismissals of their personnel; and

(4) Administrative acts for which the law provides that the administrative organ has the final adjudication.

## Chapter III Jurisdiction

Article 14 The basic people's courts shall have jurisdiction over first-instance administrative cases.

Article 15 The intermediate people's courts shall have jurisdiction over the following first-instance administrative cases:

(1) Cases filed against administrative acts performed by departments under the State Council or by local people's governments at or above the county level;

(2) Cases handled by customs;

(3) Major or complex cases within their respective jurisdictions; and

(4) Other cases over which the intermediate people's courts have jurisdiction as provided by law.

Article 16 The high people's courts shall have jurisdiction over major or complex first-instance administrative cases within their respective jurisdictions.

Article 17 The Supreme People's Court shall have jurisdiction over major or complex first-instance administrative cases nationwide.

Article 18 An administrative case shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the locality of the administrative organ that initially performed the administrative act. For cases that have been reconsidered, the case may also be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the locality of the reconsideration organ.

With the approval of the Supreme People's Court, a high people's court may, based on the actual circumstances of trial work, designate certain people's courts to have cross-administrative-region jurisdiction over administrative cases.

Article 19 A lawsuit filed against a compulsory administrative measure restricting personal freedom shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the locality of the defendant or the plaintiff.

Article 20 An administrative lawsuit arising from immovable property shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the locality of the immovable property.

Article 21 Where two or more people's courts have jurisdiction over a case, the plaintiff may choose one of them to file the lawsuit. If the plaintiff files the lawsuit with two or more people's courts that have jurisdiction, the people's court that first dockets the case shall have jurisdiction.

Article 22 If a people's court discovers that a case it has accepted is not within its jurisdiction, it shall transfer the case to the people's court that has jurisdiction. The people's court to which the case is transferred shall accept it. If the people's court to which the case is transferred believes that the transferred case is not within its jurisdiction according to the provisions, it shall report to the superior people's court for designation of jurisdiction and shall not transfer the case again on its own.

Article 23 If a people's court with jurisdiction is unable to exercise its jurisdiction due to special reasons, a superior people's court shall designate another court to have jurisdiction.

If a dispute over jurisdiction arises between people's courts, the disputing parties shall resolve it through consultation. If consultation fails, they shall report to their common superior people's court for designation of jurisdiction.

Article 24 A superior people's court shall have the authority to try first-instance administrative cases under the jurisdiction of a lower people's court.

If a lower people's court believes that a first-instance administrative case under its jurisdiction needs to be tried by a superior people's court or requires designation of jurisdiction, it may report to the superior people's court for a decision.

## Chapter IV Participants in Proceedings

Article 25 The counterparty to an administrative act, as well as other citizens, legal persons, or other organizations with an interest in the administrative act, shall have the right to file a lawsuit.

If a citizen who has the right to file a lawsuit dies, his or her close relatives may file the lawsuit.

If a legal person or other organization that has the right to file a lawsuit terminates, the legal person or other organization that succeeds to its rights may file the lawsuit.

If, in the performance of their duties, the people's procuratorates discover that an administrative organ responsible for supervision and management in areas such as ecological environment and resource protection, food and drug safety, state-owned property protection, or the transfer of state-owned land use rights has illegally exercised its powers or failed to act, thereby harming national interests or the public interest, they shall issue a procuratorial recommendation to the administrative organ, urging it to perform its duties in accordance with the law. If the administrative organ fails to perform its duties in accordance with the law, the people's procuratorate shall file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

Article 26 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization directly files a lawsuit with a people's court, the administrative organ that performed the administrative act shall be the defendant.

For a case that has been reconsidered, if the reconsideration organ decides to uphold the original administrative act, the administrative organ that performed the original administrative act and the reconsideration organ shall be co-defendants; if the reconsideration organ changes the original administrative act, the reconsideration organ shall be the defendant.

If the reconsideration organ fails to make a reconsideration decision within the statutory period, and the citizen, legal person, or other organization sues the original administrative act, the administrative organ that performed the original administrative act shall be the defendant; if they sue the inaction of the reconsideration organ, the reconsideration organ shall be the defendant.

If two or more administrative organs perform the same administrative act, the administrative organs that jointly performed the administrative act shall be co-defendants.

For an administrative act performed by an organization entrusted by an administrative organ, the entrusting administrative organ shall be the defendant.

If an administrative organ is abolished or its functions and powers are changed, the administrative organ that continues to exercise its functions and powers shall be the defendant.

Article 27 Where one or both parties consist of two or more persons, and the administrative case arises from the same administrative act, or arises from similar administrative acts and the people's court deems it appropriate to consolidate the trials with the consent of the parties, it shall be a joint action.

Article 28 In a joint action with a large number of persons on one side, the parties may elect representatives to conduct the litigation. The litigation acts of the representatives shall be binding on the parties they represent, except that changes or abandonment of claims, or admission of the opposing party's claims by the representatives shall require the consent of the represented parties.

Article 29 A citizen, legal person, or other organization that has an interest in the administrative act being sued but has not filed a lawsuit, or has an interest in the outcome of the case, may apply to participate in the proceedings as a third party, or be notified by the people's court to participate.

If the people's court's judgment imposes obligations on a third party or diminishes the rights and interests of a third party, the third party shall have the right to appeal in accordance with the law.

Article 30 For a citizen without capacity for litigation, his or her legal representative shall act on his or her behalf in the litigation. If the legal representatives shirk their responsibility for representation, the people's court shall designate one of them to act on behalf of the citizen.

Article 31 A party or legal representative may entrust one or two persons as litigation representatives.

The following persons may be entrusted as litigation representatives:

(1) Lawyers or basic-level legal service workers;

(2) Close relatives or staff members of the party; and

(3) Citizens recommended by the community, work unit, or relevant social organization to which the party belongs.

Article 32 A lawyer acting as a litigation representative shall have the right to consult and copy materials related to the case in accordance with the provisions, and to investigate and collect evidence related to the case from relevant organizations and citizens. Materials involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy shall be kept confidential in accordance with the law.

Parties and other litigation representatives shall have the right to consult and copy the trial materials of the case in accordance with the provisions, except for content involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy.

## Chapter V Evidence

Article 33 Evidence includes:

(1) Documentary evidence;

(2) Physical evidence;

(3) Audio-visual materials;

(4) Electronic data;

(5) Witness testimony;

(6) Statements of the parties;

(7) Expert opinions; and

(8) Records of inspection and records of the scene.

The above evidence must be verified by the court before it can be used as the basis for determining the facts of the case.

Article 34 The defendant bears the burden of proof for the administrative act it performed and shall provide evidence of the administrative act and the normative documents on which it is based.

If the defendant fails to provide evidence or fails to provide evidence within the prescribed period without justifiable reasons, it shall be deemed that there is no corresponding evidence. However, this does not apply if the administrative act being sued involves the lawful rights and interests of a third party and the third party provides evidence.

Article 35 During the litigation process, the defendant and its litigation representatives shall not collect evidence on their own from the plaintiff, third parties, or witnesses.

Article 36 If the defendant had already collected evidence when performing the administrative act but is unable to provide it due to force majeure or other justifiable reasons, it may, with the permission of the people's court, provide the evidence at a later date.

If the plaintiff or a third party raises reasons or evidence that were not raised during the administrative processing procedure, the defendant may, with the permission of the people's court, supplement the evidence.

Article 37 The plaintiff may provide evidence proving that the administrative act is illegal. If the evidence provided by the plaintiff is not established, it shall not relieve the defendant of its burden of proof.

Article 38 In a case where the defendant is sued for failing to perform its statutory duties, the plaintiff shall provide evidence that it applied to the defendant. However, this does not apply under the following circumstances:

(1) The defendant should have proactively performed its statutory duties ex officio; or

(2) The plaintiff is unable to provide evidence due to justifiable reasons.

In cases of administrative compensation or damages, the plaintiff shall provide evidence of the damage caused by the administrative act. If the plaintiff is unable to provide evidence due to reasons attributable to the defendant, the defendant shall bear the burden of proof.

Article 39 The people's court shall have the authority to require the parties to provide or supplement evidence.

Article 40 The people's court shall have the authority to obtain evidence from relevant administrative organs, other organizations, or citizens. However, it shall not obtain evidence that was not collected by the defendant at the time of performing the administrative act in order to prove the legality of the administrative act.

Article 41 For the following evidence relevant to the case, if the plaintiff or a third party cannot collect it on its own, it may apply to the people's court to obtain it:

(1) Evidence kept by state organs that must be obtained by the people's court;

(2) Evidence involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy; and

(3) Other evidence that genuinely cannot be collected on its own due to objective reasons.

Article 42 Where evidence may be lost or difficult to obtain later, participants in the proceedings may apply to the people's court for preservation of the evidence, and the people's court may also take preservation measures on its own initiative.

Article 43 Evidence shall be presented in court and cross-examined by the parties. Evidence involving state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy shall not be presented in open court.

The people's court shall, in accordance with statutory procedures, comprehensively and objectively examine and verify the evidence. Reasons shall be stated in the judgment document for evidence that is not admitted.

Evidence obtained by illegal means shall not be used as the basis for determining the facts of the case.

## Chapter VI Filing of Lawsuits and Acceptance

Article 44 For an administrative case that falls within the scope of acceptable cases of the people's court, a citizen, legal person, or other organization may first apply to an administrative organ for reconsideration, and if dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, may then file a lawsuit with the people's court; or may directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

If laws or regulations provide that an application for reconsideration must first be made to an administrative organ, and a lawsuit may be filed with the people's court only if dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, the provisions of such laws or regulations shall be followed.

Article 45 A citizen, legal person, or other organization dissatisfied with a reconsideration decision may file a lawsuit with the people's court within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the written reconsideration decision. If the reconsideration organ fails to make a decision within the statutory period, the applicant may file a lawsuit with the people's court within fifteen days from the expiration of the reconsideration period. Unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 46 A citizen, legal person, or other organization that directly files a lawsuit with the people's court shall do so within six months from the date it knows or should know that the administrative act was performed. Unless otherwise provided by law.

If a lawsuit concerning immovable property is filed more than twenty years after the date the administrative act was performed, or other cases are filed more than five years after the date the administrative act was performed, the people's court shall not accept the case.

Article 47 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization applies to an administrative organ to perform its statutory duty to protect personal rights, property rights, or other lawful rights and interests, and the administrative organ fails to perform within two months from the date of receiving the application, the citizen, legal person, or other organization may file a lawsuit with the people's court. If laws or regulations provide otherwise for the time limit for the administrative organ to perform its duties, those provisions shall apply.

If a citizen, legal person, or other organization requests an administrative organ to perform its statutory duty to protect personal rights, property rights, or other lawful rights and interests in an emergency, and the administrative organ fails to perform, the filing of a lawsuit shall not be subject to the time limit specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 48 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization misses the time limit for filing a lawsuit due to force majeure or other reasons not attributable to itself, the time missed shall not be counted within the time limit for filing.

If a citizen, legal person, or other organization misses the time limit for filing a lawsuit due to other special circumstances not covered by the preceding paragraph, it may apply for an extension within ten days after the obstacle is removed, and the people's court shall decide whether to grant the extension.

Article 49 The filing of a lawsuit shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The plaintiff is a citizen, legal person, or other organization that meets the requirements of Article 25 of this Law;

(2) There is a clear defendant;

(3) There are specific claims and factual basis; and

(4) The case falls within the scope of acceptable cases of the people's court and the jurisdiction of the people's court where the lawsuit is filed.

Article 50 A lawsuit shall be filed by submitting a statement of complaint to the people's court, along with copies equal to the number of defendants.

If it is genuinely difficult to write a statement of complaint, an oral complaint may be made, and the people's court shall record it in the transcript, issue a written certificate with the date of receipt, and inform the opposing party.
Article 51 Upon receiving a statement of complaint, if the conditions for filing a lawsuit as provided in this Law are met, the people's court shall register and docket the case.

If it cannot be immediately determined whether the conditions for filing a lawsuit as provided in this Law are met, the court shall accept the statement of complaint, issue a written certificate with the date of receipt, and decide whether to docket the case within seven days. If the conditions for filing are not met, a ruling of non-acceptance shall be made. The ruling shall state the reasons for non-acceptance. If the plaintiff is dissatisfied with the ruling, it may appeal.

If the content of the statement of complaint is incomplete or contains other errors, guidance and clarification shall be provided, and the party shall be informed at one time of the content that needs to be corrected. The statement of complaint shall not be refused for non-compliance with the conditions for filing without providing guidance and clarification.

If the statement of complaint is not accepted, a written certificate is not issued after acceptance, or the party is not informed at one time of the content of the statement of complaint that needs to be corrected, the party may complain to the superior people's court, which shall order rectification and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 52 If the people's court neither dockets the case nor makes a ruling of non-acceptance, the party may file a lawsuit with the next higher people's court. If the next higher people's court considers that the conditions for filing a lawsuit are met, it shall docket and try the case, or may designate another lower people's court to docket and try the case.

Article 53 If a citizen, legal person, or other organization believes that a normative document formulated by a department under the State Council or by a local people's government or its department, on which an administrative act is based, is illegal, it may, when filing a lawsuit against the administrative act, also request a review of that normative document.The normative documents specified in the preceding paragraph do not include rules.

## Chapter VII: Trial and Judgment

### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 54: People's courts shall hear administrative cases in public, except those involving state secrets, personal privacy, or as otherwise provided by law.

Cases involving trade secrets may be heard in closed session upon application by the parties if the court deems it appropriate.

Article 55: If a party believes that a judicial officer has an interest in the case or other relationship that may affect an impartial trial, the party has the right to apply for the judicial officer's withdrawal.

If a judicial officer believes that he or she has an interest in the case or other relationship, the judicial officer shall apply for withdrawal.

The provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall apply to court clerks, interpreters, expert witnesses, and inspectors.

Withdrawal of the court president when serving as the presiding judge shall be decided by the adjudication committee; withdrawal of judicial officers shall be decided by the court president; withdrawal of other personnel shall be decided by the presiding judge. If a party disagrees with the decision, it may apply for reconsideration once.

Article 56: During the litigation period, the execution of an administrative act shall not be suspended. However, execution may be suspended by ruling under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The defendant deems it necessary to suspend execution;
(2) The plaintiff or an interested party applies for suspension of execution, and the people's court finds that execution of the administrative act would cause irreparable harm and that suspension would not harm national or public interests;
(3) The people's court finds that execution of the administrative act would cause significant harm to national or public interests;
(4) Suspension of execution is required by laws or regulations.

If a party disagrees with a ruling on suspension or non-suspension of execution, it may apply for reconsideration once.

Article 57: In cases where a lawsuit is filed against an administrative agency for failing to pay pensions, minimum subsistence allowances, or work-related injury and medical social insurance benefits as required by law, if the rights and obligations are clear and failure to grant advance execution would seriously affect the plaintiff's livelihood, the people's court may, upon the plaintiff's application, rule for advance execution.

If a party disagrees with a ruling on advance execution, it may apply for reconsideration once. Reconsideration shall not suspend execution of the ruling.

Article 58: If the plaintiff, after being summoned by the people's court, refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court midway without the court's permission, the case may be treated as withdrawn. If the defendant refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court midway without the court's permission, a default judgment may be entered.

Article 59: If any litigation participant or other person commits any of the following acts, the people's court may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, issue a reprimand, order a statement of repentance, impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan, or detain the person for not more than 15 days; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) A person obligated to assist in investigation or execution unreasonably delays, refuses, or obstructs investigation or execution despite a court decision or notice to assist;
(2) Forges, conceals, or destroys evidence, or provides false evidentiary materials, hindering the court's trial of the case;
(3) Instigates, bribes, or coerces others to give false testimony, or threatens or prevents witnesses from testifying;
(4) Conceals, transfers, sells, or damages property that has been sealed up, seized, or frozen;
(5) Uses illegal means such as deception or coercion to cause the plaintiff to withdraw the lawsuit;
(6) Uses violence, threats, or other methods to obstruct court personnel from performing their duties, or disrupts court order by making noise, storming the courtroom, or other means;
(7) Intimidates, insults, slanders, frames, assaults, besieges, or retaliates against court judicial officers or other staff, litigation participants, or persons assisting in investigation or execution.

If a unit commits any of the acts specified in the preceding paragraph, the people's court may impose a fine or detention on its principal responsible person or directly responsible personnel in accordance with the preceding paragraph; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Fines and detention must be approved by the court president. If a party disagrees, it may apply to the next higher people's court for reconsideration once. Reconsideration shall not suspend execution.

Article 60: People's courts shall not apply mediation in hearing administrative cases. However, cases involving administrative compensation, administrative indemnity, or the exercise of discretionary power by administrative agencies as provided by laws and regulations may be mediated.

Mediation shall follow the principles of voluntariness and legality, and shall not harm national interests, public interests, or the lawful rights and interests of others.

Article 61: In administrative litigation involving administrative licensing, registration, expropriation, requisition, or an administrative agency's ruling on a civil dispute, if a party applies to have the related civil dispute resolved together, the people's court may hear them jointly.

In administrative litigation, if the people's court finds that the trial of the administrative case must be based on the judgment of a civil lawsuit, it may rule to suspend the administrative litigation.

Article 62: Before the people's court pronounces a judgment or ruling on an administrative case, if the plaintiff applies to withdraw the lawsuit, or if the defendant changes the administrative act it performed and the plaintiff agrees and applies to withdraw the lawsuit, whether to permit the withdrawal shall be decided by the people's court by ruling.

Article 63: People's courts shall base their trial of administrative cases on laws, administrative regulations, and local regulations. Local regulations apply to administrative cases occurring within the corresponding administrative region.

When trying administrative cases in ethnic autonomous areas, people's courts shall also base their decisions on the autonomous regulations and separate regulations of those areas.

People's courts shall refer to rules when trying administrative cases.

Article 64: If, during the trial of an administrative case, the people's court finds, upon review, that a normative document specified in Article 53 of this Law is illegal, it shall not use it as a basis for finding the administrative act lawful and shall submit a recommendation for handling to the issuing authority.

Article 65: People's courts shall make legally effective judgments and rulings publicly available for public inspection, except for content involving state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy.

Article 66: If, during the trial of an administrative case, the people's court finds that the principal responsible person or directly responsible personnel of the administrative agency has violated laws or discipline, it shall transfer the relevant materials to the supervisory agency, the administrative agency itself, or its next higher administrative agency; if it finds that a crime has been committed, it shall transfer the relevant materials to the public security or procuratorial authorities.

If the defendant, after being summoned by the people's court, refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court midway without the court's permission, the people's court may publicly announce the defendant's failure to appear or mid-trial departure, and may submit a judicial recommendation to the supervisory agency or the defendant's next higher administrative agency requesting that disciplinary action be taken against its principal responsible person or directly responsible personnel according to law.

### Section 2: Ordinary Procedure of First Instance

Article 67: The people's court shall, within five days of filing the case, serve a copy of the statement of complaint on the defendant. The defendant shall, within fifteen days of receiving the copy of the statement of complaint, submit to the people's court the evidence supporting the administrative act and the normative documents on which it is based, and file a statement of defense. The people's court shall, within five days of receiving the statement of defense, serve a copy of it on the plaintiff.

The defendant's failure to file a statement of defense shall not affect the trial by the people's court.

Article 68: People's courts trying administrative cases shall form a collegial panel composed of judges, or of judges and people's assessors. The collegial panel shall consist of an odd number of three or more members.

Article 69: If the evidence for an administrative act is conclusive, the application of laws and regulations is correct, and the legal procedure is followed, or if the plaintiff's application for the defendant to perform a statutory duty or payment obligation is unfounded, the people's court shall render a judgment dismissing the plaintiff's claims.

Article 70: If an administrative act falls under any of the following circumstances, the people's court shall render a judgment annulling it in whole or in part, and may also render a judgment requiring the defendant to perform a new administrative act:

(1) Insufficient primary evidence;
(2) Incorrect application of laws or regulations;
(3) Violation of legal procedures;
(4) Exceeding authority;
(5) Abuse of authority;
(6) Manifestly improper.

Article 71: If the people's court renders a judgment requiring the defendant to perform a new administrative act, the defendant shall not perform an administrative act that is substantially the same as the original act based on the same facts and reasons.

Article 72: If, after trial, the people's court finds that the defendant has failed to perform a statutory duty, it shall render a judgment requiring the defendant to perform it within a specified period.

Article 73: If, after trial, the people's court finds that the defendant has a statutory payment obligation, it shall render a judgment requiring the defendant to perform the payment obligation.

Article 74: If an administrative act falls under any of the following circumstances, the people's court shall render a judgment confirming its illegality, but shall not annul the administrative act:

(1) The administrative act should be annulled according to law, but annulment would cause significant harm to national or public interests;
(2) The procedure of the administrative act is slightly illegal but has no actual impact on the plaintiff's rights.

If an administrative act falls under any of the following circumstances and does not require annulment or a judgment of performance, the people's court shall render a judgment confirming its illegality:

(1) The administrative act is illegal but has no content that can be annulled;
(2) The defendant has changed the original illegal administrative act, but the plaintiff still requests confirmation of the original act's illegality;
(3) The defendant fails to perform or delays performing a statutory duty, and a judgment of performance would be meaningless.

Article 75: If an administrative act has a major and obvious illegal circumstance, such as the implementing entity lacking administrative subject qualification or lacking a legal basis, and the plaintiff applies for confirmation of the administrative act's invalidity, the people's court shall render a judgment confirming its invalidity.

Article 76: If the people's court renders a judgment confirming illegality or invalidity, it may also render a judgment ordering the defendant to take remedial measures; if losses are caused to the plaintiff, it shall render a judgment requiring the defendant to bear compensation liability according to law.

Article 77: If an administrative penalty is manifestly improper, or if another administrative act involves an error in the determination or confirmation of an amount, the people's court may render a judgment of modification.

When rendering a judgment of modification, the people's court shall not increase the plaintiff's obligations or diminish the plaintiff's rights, except where an interested party is also a plaintiff and the claims are adverse.

Article 78: If the defendant fails to perform according to law, fails to perform as agreed, or illegally modifies or rescinds a protocol specified in Item 11 of Paragraph 1 of Article 12 of this Law, the people's court shall render a judgment requiring the defendant to bear liability for continued performance, taking remedial measures, or compensating for losses.

If the defendant lawfully modifies or rescinds a protocol specified in Item 11 of Paragraph 1 of Article 12 of this Law but fails to provide compensation according to law, the people's court shall render a judgment requiring compensation.

Article 79: In cases where the reconsideration agency and the administrative agency that performed the original administrative act are co-defendants, the people's court shall render a judgment on both the reconsideration decision and the original administrative act.

Article 80: People's courts shall publicly pronounce judgments in all cases, whether heard in open or closed session.

If the judgment is pronounced in court, the written judgment shall be served within ten days; if the judgment is pronounced on a scheduled date, the written judgment shall be issued immediately after pronouncement.

When pronouncing a judgment, the parties must be informed of their right to appeal, the appeal period, and the appellate people's court.

Article 81: People's courts shall render a first-instance judgment within six months of filing the case. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the higher people's court; if an extension is necessary for a higher people's court trying a first-instance case, it shall be approved by the Supreme People's Court.

### Section 3: Summary Procedure

Article 82: People's courts may apply summary procedure to the following first-instance administrative cases if they find the facts clear, the rights and obligations clear, and the dispute minor:

(1) The challenged administrative act was performed on the spot according to law;
(2) The case involves an amount of not more than 2,000 yuan;
(3) The case is a government information disclosure case.

For first-instance administrative cases other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, summary procedure may be applied if all parties agree.

Summary procedure shall not apply to cases remanded for retrial or retried under trial supervision procedures.

Article 83: Administrative cases tried under summary procedure shall be heard by a single judge and shall be concluded within 45 days of filing.

Article 84: If, during the trial, the people's court finds that a case is not suitable for summary procedure, it shall rule to convert it to ordinary procedure.

### Section 4: Procedure of Second Instance

Article 85: If a party disagrees with a first-instance judgment of a people's court, it has the right to appeal to the next higher people's court within 15 days of service of the written judgment. If a party disagrees with a first-instance ruling of a people's court, it has the right to appeal to the next higher people's court within 10 days of service of the written ruling. If no appeal is filed within the time limit, the first-instance judgment or ruling of the people's court shall become legally effective.

Article 86: People's courts hearing appeal cases shall form a collegial panel and hold a hearing. After reviewing the case files, conducting investigations, and questioning the parties, if the collegial panel finds that no new facts, evidence, or reasons have been presented and that a hearing is unnecessary, it may decide not to hold a hearing.

Article 87: People's courts hearing appeal cases shall conduct a comprehensive review of the original trial court's judgment, ruling, and the challenged administrative act.

Article 88: People's courts hearing appeal cases shall render a final judgment within three months of receiving the statement of appeal. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the higher people's court; if an extension is necessary for a higher people's court hearing an appeal case, it shall be approved by the Supreme People's Court.

Article 89: People's courts hearing appeal cases shall handle them according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the facts found in the original judgment or ruling are clear and the application of laws and regulations is correct, the appeal shall be dismissed by judgment or ruling, and the original judgment or ruling shall be affirmed;
(2) If the facts found in the original judgment or ruling are erroneous or the application of laws or regulations is incorrect, the judgment or ruling shall be modified, annulled, or changed according to law;
(3) If the basic facts found in the original judgment are unclear or the evidence is insufficient, the case shall be remanded to the original trial court for retrial, or the judgment shall be modified after the facts are clarified;
(4) If the original judgment omitted a party or seriously violated legal procedures, such as by entering a default judgment illegally, the original judgment shall be annulled by ruling and the case remanded to the original trial court for retrial.

After the original trial court renders a judgment on a case remanded for retrial, if a party appeals, the second-instance people's court shall not remand the case for retrial again.

If a people's court hearing an appeal case needs to change the original trial judgment, it shall simultaneously render a judgment on the challenged administrative act.

### Section 5: Trial Supervision Procedure

Article 90: If a party believes that a legally effective judgment or ruling is indeed erroneous, it may apply to the next higher people's court for retrial, but execution of the judgment or ruling shall not be suspended.

Article 91: The people's court shall retry a case if the party's application meets any of the following conditions:

(1) The decision not to accept the case or to dismiss the lawsuit is indeed erroneous;
(2) There is new evidence sufficient to overturn the original judgment or ruling;
(3) The primary evidence on which the facts found in the original judgment or ruling are based is insufficient, was not cross-examined, or is forged;
(4) The application of laws or regulations in the original judgment or ruling is indeed erroneous;
(5) The litigation procedure violated legal provisions and may have affected the impartiality of the trial;
(6) The original judgment or ruling omitted a claim;
(7) The legal document on which the original judgment or ruling was based has been annulled or changed;
(8) The judicial officer engaged in corruption, bribery, favoritism, or perversion of the law during the trial of the case.

Article 92: If the president of a people's court finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of that court falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 91 of this Law, or finds that a mediation violated the principle of voluntariness or that the content of the mediation agreement is illegal, and deems a retrial necessary, the president shall submit the matter to the adjudication committee for discussion and decision.

If the Supreme People's Court finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a local people's court at any level falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 91 of this Law, or finds that a mediation violated the principle of voluntariness or that the content of the mediation agreement is illegal, or if a higher people's court finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a lower people's court falls under such circumstances, it has the authority to bring the case up for trial or to direct the lower people's court to retry it.

Article 93: If the Supreme People's Procuratorate finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a people's court at any level falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 91 of this Law, or finds that a mediation agreement harms national or public interests, or if a higher people's procuratorate finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a lower people's court falls under such circumstances, it shall file a protest.

If a local people's procuratorate at any level finds that a legally effective judgment or ruling of a people's court at the same level falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 91 of this Law, or finds that a mediation agreement harms national or public interests, it may submit a procuratorial recommendation to the people's court at the same level and report it to the higher people's procuratorate for record; it may also request the higher people's procuratorate to file a protest with the people's court at the same level.

People's procuratorates at all levels have the authority to submit procuratorial recommendations to people's courts at the same level regarding illegal conduct by judicial officers in trial procedures other than trial supervision procedures.

## Chapter VIII: Execution

Article 94: Parties must comply with legally effective judgments, rulings, and mediation agreements of people's courts.

Article 95: If a citizen, legal person, or other organization refuses to comply with a judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement, the administrative agency or a third party may apply to the first-instance people's court for compulsory execution, or the administrative agency may enforce it according to law.

Article 96: If an administrative agency refuses to comply with a judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement, the first-instance people's court may take the following measures:

(1) Notify the bank to transfer the amount of fines to be refunded or amounts to be paid from the administrative agency's account;
(2) If the agency fails to comply within the specified period, impose a daily fine of 50 to 100 yuan on the head of the administrative agency from the date the period expires;
(3) Publicly announce the administrative agency's refusal to comply;
(4) Submit a judicial recommendation to the supervisory agency or the next higher administrative agency of the administrative agency. The agency receiving the judicial recommendation shall handle the matter according to relevant provisions and inform the people's court of the handling results;
(5) If the refusal to comply with the judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement has a severe social impact, detain the directly responsible principal person in charge and other directly responsible personnel of the administrative agency; if the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, pursue criminal liability according to law.

Article 97: If a citizen, legal person, or other organization neither files a lawsuit nor complies with an administrative act within the statutory period, the administrative agency may apply to the people's court for compulsory execution, or enforce it according to law.

## Chapter IX: Foreign-Related Administrative Litigation

Article 98: This Law shall apply to administrative litigation conducted in the People's Republic of China by foreigners, stateless persons, or foreign organizations, except as otherwise provided by law.

Article 99: Foreigners, stateless persons, and foreign organizations conducting administrative litigation in the People's Republic of China shall have the same litigation rights and obligations as citizens and organizations of the People's Republic of China.

If a foreign court imposes restrictions on the administrative litigation rights of citizens or organizations of the People's Republic of China, the people's court shall apply the principle of reciprocity to the administrative litigation rights of citizens or organizations of that country.

Article 100: If foreigners, stateless persons, or foreign organizations conducting administrative litigation in the People's Republic of China entrust lawyers to act as agents, they shall entrust lawyers from a law firm of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter X: Supplementary ProvisionsArticle 101: In the trial of administrative cases by the People's Court, matters such as time periods, service, property preservation, court hearings, mediation, suspension of proceedings, termination of proceedings, summary procedures, and enforcement, as well as the supervision by the People's Procuratorate over the acceptance, trial, adjudication, and enforcement of administrative cases, where this Law does not provide provisions, the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

Article 102: The People's Court shall collect litigation costs when trying administrative cases. Litigation costs shall be borne by the losing party; if both parties are at fault, the costs shall be shared between them. Specific measures for collecting litigation costs shall be separately prescribed.

Article 103: This Law shall come into force on October 1, 1990.
