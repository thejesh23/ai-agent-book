# Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 9, 1991

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 30th Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 28, 2007

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 28th Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 31, 2012

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 28th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 27, 2017

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 32nd Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

## Part One General Provisions

### Chapter I Tasks, Scope of Application and Basic Principles

Article 1 The Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China is formulated on the basis of the Constitution, in light of the experience and actual circumstances of the civil trial work of our country.

Article 2 The tasks of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China are to protect the exercise of litigation rights by the parties, ensure that the people's courts ascertain the facts, distinguish right from wrong, correctly apply the law, promptly try civil cases, affirm civil rights and obligations, sanction civil violations, protect the lawful rights and interests of the parties, educate citizens to conscientiously abide by the law, maintain social and economic order, and ensure the smooth progress of socialist construction.

Article 3 Civil lawsuits filed between citizens, between legal persons, between other organizations, and between them concerning property relations and personal relations, which are accepted by the people's courts, shall be governed by the provisions of this Law.

Article 4 Anyone who engages in civil litigation within the territory of the People's Republic of China must abide by this Law.

Article 5 Foreigners, stateless persons, foreign enterprises and organizations that sue or defend in the people's courts shall have the same litigation rights and obligations as citizens, legal persons and other organizations of the People's Republic of China.

If the courts of a foreign country impose restrictions on the civil litigation rights of citizens, legal persons and other organizations of the People's Republic of China, the people's courts of the People's Republic of China shall apply the principle of reciprocity to the civil litigation rights of citizens, enterprises and organizations of that country.

Article 6 The judicial power over civil cases shall be exercised by the people's courts.

The people's courts shall independently adjudicate civil cases in accordance with the law and shall not be subject to interference by administrative organs, social organizations or individuals.

Article 7 In trying civil cases, the people's courts must take facts as the basis and the law as the criterion.

Article 8 The parties to a civil lawsuit shall have equal litigation rights. When trying civil cases, the people's courts shall guarantee and facilitate the exercise of litigation rights by the parties, and apply the law equally to all parties.

Article 9 In trying civil cases, the people's courts shall conduct mediation on a voluntary and lawful basis; if mediation fails, a judgment shall be rendered promptly.

Article 10 In trying civil cases, the people's courts shall, in accordance with the law, implement the systems of collegiate bench, withdrawal, public trial, and the system of trial at two instances being final.

Article 11 Citizens of all ethnic groups shall have the right to use their own spoken and written languages in civil litigation proceedings.

In areas where minority ethnic groups live in concentrated communities or where multiple ethnic groups live together, the people's courts shall conduct trials and issue legal documents in the spoken and written languages commonly used by the local ethnic groups.

The people's courts shall provide translation for any participant in the proceedings who is not familiar with the spoken or written language commonly used by the local ethnic groups.

Article 12 When the people's court tries a civil case, the parties shall have the right to engage in debate.

Article 13 Civil litigation shall adhere to the principle of good faith.

The parties shall have the right to dispose of their civil rights and litigation rights within the scope provided by law.

Article 14 The people's procuratorates shall have the right to exercise legal supervision over civil litigation.

Article 15 State organs, social organizations, enterprises and institutions may support the injured unit or individual to bring a lawsuit in a people's court against any act that damages the civil rights and interests of the state, a collective or an individual.

Article 16 With the consent of the parties, civil litigation activities may be conducted online through information network platforms.

Civil litigation activities conducted online through information network platforms shall have the same legal effect as offline litigation activities.

Article 17 The people's congresses of national autonomous areas may, in accordance with the principles of the Constitution and this Law and in light of the specific circumstances of the local ethnic groups, formulate adaptive or supplementary provisions. The provisions of an autonomous region shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for approval. The provisions of an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress of the relevant province or autonomous region for approval and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the record.

### Chapter II Jurisdiction

#### Section 1 Jurisdiction by Level

Article 18 The basic people's courts shall have jurisdiction over first-instance civil cases, unless otherwise provided by this Law.

Article 19 The intermediate people's courts shall have jurisdiction over the following first-instance civil cases:

(1) Major cases involving foreign elements;
(2) Cases that have a major impact within their respective jurisdictions;
(3) Cases designated by the Supreme People's Court as being under the jurisdiction of intermediate people's courts.

Article 20 The high people's courts shall have jurisdiction over first-instance civil cases that have a major impact within their respective jurisdictions.

Article 21 The Supreme People's Court shall have jurisdiction over the following first-instance civil cases:

(1) Cases that have a major impact nationwide;
(2) Cases that it deems should be tried by itself.

#### Section 2 Territorial Jurisdiction

Article 22 A civil lawsuit brought against a citizen shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the defendant has his domicile; if the defendant's domicile is inconsistent with his habitual residence, the lawsuit shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place of his habitual residence.

A civil lawsuit brought against a legal person or other organization shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the defendant has its domicile.

If the domiciles or habitual residences of several defendants in the same lawsuit are within the jurisdictions of two or more people's courts, each of those people's courts shall have jurisdiction.

Article 23 The following civil lawsuits shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the plaintiff has his domicile; if the plaintiff's domicile is inconsistent with his habitual residence, the lawsuit shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place of the plaintiff's habitual residence:

(1) Lawsuits concerning personal status relationships brought against a person who is not residing within the territory of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Lawsuits concerning personal status relationships brought against a person whose whereabouts are unknown or who has been declared missing;
(3) Lawsuits brought against a person who is subject to compulsory educational measures;
(4) Lawsuits brought against a person who is imprisoned.

Article 24 A lawsuit arising from a contract dispute shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the defendant has its domicile or where the contract is performed.

Article 25 A lawsuit arising from an insurance contract dispute shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the defendant has its domicile or where the subject matter of the insurance is located.

Article 26 A lawsuit arising from a negotiable instrument dispute shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the negotiable instrument is payable or where the defendant has its domicile.

Article 27 A lawsuit arising from disputes over company establishment, confirmation of shareholder qualifications, profit distribution, dissolution, etc., shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the company has its domicile.

Article 28 A lawsuit arising from a dispute over a railway, highway, waterway, air transport or multimodal transport contract shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place of departure, destination, or where the defendant has its domicile.

Article 29 A lawsuit arising from a tort shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the tort was committed or where the defendant has its domicile.

Article 30 A lawsuit brought for damages arising from a railway, highway, waterway or aviation accident shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the accident occurred, or where the vehicle or vessel first arrived, or where the aircraft first landed, or where the defendant has its domicile.

Article 31 A lawsuit brought for damages arising from a collision of ships or other maritime casualty shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the collision occurred, where the colliding ship first arrived, where the ship at fault was detained, or where the defendant has its domicile.

Article 32 A lawsuit brought for expenses of salvage at sea shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the salvage took place or where the salvaged ship first arrived.

Article 33 A lawsuit brought for general average shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the ship first arrived, where the general average adjustment was conducted, or where the voyage ended.

Article 34 The following cases shall be under the exclusive jurisdiction of the people's courts specified in this Article:

(1) A lawsuit arising from a real estate dispute shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the real estate is located;
(2) A lawsuit arising from a dispute occurring in port operations shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the port is located;
(3) A lawsuit arising from a dispute over inheritance shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court of the place where the decedent had his domicile at the time of death or where the principal part of the estate is located.

Article 35 The parties to a dispute over a contract or other property rights and interests may, by written agreement, choose the people's court of the place where the defendant has its domicile, where the contract is performed, where the contract is signed, where the plaintiff has its domicile, where the subject matter is located, or any other place that is actually connected with the dispute to have jurisdiction, provided that the provisions of this Law on jurisdiction by level and exclusive jurisdiction are not violated.

Article 36 In a lawsuit where two or more people's courts have jurisdiction, the plaintiff may bring the lawsuit in one of those people's courts; if the plaintiff brings the lawsuit in two or more people's courts that have jurisdiction, the people's court that first dockets the case shall have jurisdiction.

#### Section 3 Transfer of Jurisdiction and Designation of Jurisdiction

Article 37 If a people's court discovers that a case it has accepted does not fall within its jurisdiction, it shall transfer the case to the people's court that has jurisdiction. The people's court to which the case is transferred shall accept it. If the people's court to which the case is transferred considers that, according to the provisions, the transferred case does not fall within its jurisdiction, it shall report to the superior people's court for the designation of jurisdiction and shall not transfer the case again on its own.

Article 38 If a people's court with jurisdiction is unable to exercise its jurisdiction for special reasons, a superior people's court shall designate another court to have jurisdiction.

If a dispute over jurisdiction arises between people's courts, it shall be resolved by the parties to the dispute through consultation; if consultation fails, the dispute shall be reported to their common superior people's court for the designation of jurisdiction.

Article 39 A superior people's court shall have the authority to try first-instance civil cases that are under the jurisdiction of an inferior people's court; if it is truly necessary to transfer a first-instance civil case under its own jurisdiction to an inferior people's court for trial, it shall report to its superior people's court for approval.

If an inferior people's court considers that a first-instance civil case under its jurisdiction needs to be tried by a superior people's court, it may report to the superior people's court for trial.

### Chapter III Trial Organization

Article 40 When trying first-instance civil cases, the people's court shall form a collegiate bench composed of judges and people's assessors or a collegiate bench composed of judges alone. The number of members of a collegiate bench must be odd.

Civil cases tried under summary procedure shall be tried by a single judge alone. For first-instance civil cases tried by a basic people's court where the basic facts are clear and the rights and obligations are definite, a single judge may try the case under ordinary procedure alone.

When performing their duties as assessors, people's assessors shall have equal rights and obligations as judges.

Article 41 When trying second-instance civil cases, the people's court shall form a collegiate bench composed of judges. The number of members of a collegiate bench must be odd.

For second-instance civil cases where the first-instance trial was concluded under summary procedure or appeals against rulings, if the facts are clear and the rights and obligations are definite, with the consent of both parties, the intermediate people's court may have a single judge try the case alone.

For cases remanded for retrial, the original trial people's court shall form a new collegiate bench in accordance with the first-instance procedure.

For cases retried, if the original trial was of first instance, a new collegiate bench shall be formed in accordance with the first-instance procedure; if the original trial was of second instance or the case was brought up for trial by a superior people's court, a new collegiate bench shall be formed in accordance with the second-instance procedure.

Article 42 The people's court shall not have a single judge try the following civil cases alone:

(1) Cases involving national interests or public interests;
(2) Cases involving group disputes that may affect social stability;
(3) Cases that are of widespread public concern or have other significant social impact;
(4) Cases that are of a new type or are difficult and complex;
(5) Cases that the law provides should be tried by a collegiate bench;
(6) Other cases that are not suitable for trial by a single judge alone.

Article 43 If, during the trial, the people's court finds that a case is not suitable for trial by a single judge alone, it shall rule to transfer the case to a collegiate bench for trial.

If a party considers that the trial of a case by a single judge alone violates the provisions of the law, it may raise an objection to the people's court. The people's court shall review the objection raised by the party; if the objection is established, it shall rule to transfer the case to a collegiate bench for trial; if the objection is not established, it shall rule to reject it.

Article 44 The presiding judge of a collegiate bench shall be designated by the president of the court or the chief judge of the division from among the judges; if the president or the chief judge participates in the trial, he or she shall serve as the presiding judge.

Article 45 When the collegiate bench deliberates a case, the principle of the minority being subordinate to the majority shall apply. The deliberation shall be recorded in writing, and the record shall be signed by the members of the collegiate bench. Dissenting opinions during the deliberation must be truthfully recorded in the minutes.

Article 46 Judicial personnel shall handle cases impartially in accordance with the law.

Judicial personnel shall not accept any entertainment or gifts from the parties or their agents ad litem.

Judicial personnel who engage in embezzlement, bribery, favoritism, or perversion of the law in rendering judgments shall be subject to legal liability; if the conduct constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

### Chapter IV Withdrawal

Article 47 A judicial officer shall withdraw himself or herself under any of the following circumstances, and the parties shall have the right to apply orally or in writing for their withdrawal:

(1) Being a party to the case or a close relative of a party or an agent ad litem;
(2) Having an interest in the case;
(3) Having another relationship with a party or an agent ad litem that could affect the impartial trial of the case.

If a judicial officer accepts entertainment or gifts from a party or an agent ad litem, or meets with a party or an agent ad litem in violation of regulations, the parties shall have the right to request their withdrawal.

Judicial officers who engage in the conduct described in the preceding paragraph shall be subject to legal liability in accordance with the law.

The provisions of the preceding three paragraphs shall apply to court clerks, interpreters, expert witnesses, and inspectors.

Article 48 A party applying for the withdrawal of a judicial officer shall state the reasons and make the application at the commencement of the trial; if the reason for withdrawal becomes known after the commencement of the trial, the application may also be made before the conclusion of the court debate.

The person whose withdrawal is applied for shall suspend participation in the work of the case pending a decision by the people's court on whether to grant the withdrawal, except where the case requires the adoption of urgent measures.

Article 49 The withdrawal of the president of the court when serving as the presiding judge or a single judge shall be decided by the judicial committee; the withdrawal of a judicial officer shall be decided by the president of the court; the withdrawal of other personnel shall be decided by the presiding judge or the single judge.

Article 50 The people's court shall make a decision on an application for withdrawal within three days of the application being made, either orally or in writing. If the applicant is dissatisfied with the decision, he or she may apply for reconsideration once at the time of receiving the decision. During the period of reconsideration, the person whose withdrawal is applied for shall not suspend participation in the work of the case. The people's court shall make a reconsideration decision within three days of receiving the reconsideration application and shall notify the reconsideration applicant.

### Chapter V Participants in Proceedings

#### Section 1 Parties

Article 51 Citizens, legal persons and other organizations may be parties to civil litigation.

A legal person shall be represented in litigation by its legal representative. Other organizations shall be represented in litigation by their principal responsible persons.

Article 52 The parties shall have the right to appoint agents, apply for withdrawal, collect and provide evidence, engage in debate, request mediation, file an appeal, and apply for enforcement.

The parties may consult the relevant materials of the case and may copy the relevant materials and legal documents of the case. The scope and methods for consulting and copying the relevant materials of the case shall be prescribed by the Supreme People's Court.

The parties must exercise their litigation rights in accordance with the law, observe the litigation order, and comply with judgments, rulings and mediation statements that have taken legal effect.

Article 53 The parties may reach a settlement between themselves.

Article 54 The plaintiff may waive or modify its claims. The defendant may admit or rebut the claims and has the right to file a counterclaim.

Article 55 Where one or both parties consist of two or more persons, and the subject matter of the action is the same, or the subject matter of the action is of the same category and the people's court considers that the cases can be tried together with the consent of the parties, it shall be a joint action.

If a party to a joint action has common rights and obligations with respect to the subject matter of the action, the litigation acts of one of them, if recognized by the other joint litigants, shall be binding on the other joint litigants; if there are no common rights and obligations with respect to the subject matter of the action, the litigation acts of one of them shall not be binding on the other joint litigants.

Article 56 In a joint action where one party has a large number of persons, the party may elect representatives to conduct the litigation. The litigation acts of the representatives shall be binding on the parties they represent, but any modification or waiver of claims, admission of the claims of the opposing party, or settlement must be consented to by the represented parties.

Article 57 Where the subject matter of the action is of the same category and one party has a large number of persons whose number is uncertain at the time of filing the lawsuit, the people's court may issue a public notice, explaining the circumstances of the case and the claims, and notifying the right holders to register with the people's court within a specified period.

The right holders who register with the people's court may elect representatives to conduct the litigation; if representatives cannot be elected, the people's court may consult with the right holders who have registered to determine the representatives.

The litigation acts of the representatives shall be binding on the parties they represent, but any modification or waiver of claims, admission of the claims of the opposing party, or settlement must be consented to by the represented parties.

The judgments and rulings rendered by the people's court shall be binding on all right holders who have registered. Right holders who have not registered and bring a lawsuit within the statute of limitations shall be subject to such judgments and rulings.

Article 58 For acts that harm the public interest, such as polluting the environment or infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of numerous consumers, the organs and relevant organizations prescribed by law may bring a lawsuit in a people's court.

If, in the performance of their duties, the people's procuratorate discovers acts that harm the public interest, such as damaging the ecological environment and resource protection, or infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of numerous consumers in the fields of food and drug safety, and if there is no organ or organization as prescribed in the preceding paragraph, or if the organ or organization prescribed in the preceding paragraph does not bring a lawsuit, the people's procuratorate may bring a lawsuit in a people's court. If the organ or organization prescribed in the preceding paragraph brings a lawsuit, the people's procuratorate may support the lawsuit.

Article 59 If a third party considers that it has an independent claim to the subject matter of the action of both parties, it shall have the right to bring a lawsuit.

If a third party does not have an independent claim to the subject matter of the action of both parties, but the outcome of the case has a legal interest in it, it may apply to participate in the proceedings, or the people's court may notify it to participate in the proceedings. A third party whom the people's court's judgment holds liable for civil liability shall have the litigation rights and obligations of a party.The third party as stipulated in the preceding two paragraphs who fails to participate in the proceedings for reasons not attributable to themselves, but who has evidence proving that part or all of the content of a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement is erroneous and infringes upon their civil rights and interests, may, within six months from the date they know or should have known that their civil rights and interests were infringed, file a lawsuit with the people's court that rendered the judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement. After trial, if the claim is established, the people's court shall modify or revoke the original judgment, ruling, or mediation agreement; if the claim is not established, the claim shall be dismissed.

#### Section 2: Litigation Representatives

Article 60: A person without capacity to conduct litigation shall have their guardian act as legal representative in the litigation. If legal representatives shirk their agency responsibilities among themselves, the people's court shall designate one of them to act as representative.

Article 61: A party or legal representative may appoint one to two persons as litigation representatives.

The following persons may be appointed as litigation representatives:

(1) Lawyers and basic-level legal service workers;

(2) Close relatives or staff members of the party;

(3) Citizens recommended by the party's community, work unit, or relevant social organizations.

Article 62: To appoint another person to act in litigation, a power of attorney signed or sealed by the principal must be submitted to the people's court.

The power of attorney must clearly state the matters and scope of authority. For a litigation representative to admit, waive, or modify claims, reach a settlement, file a counterclaim or appeal, special authorization from the principal is required.

A power of attorney sent or delivered from abroad by a citizen of the People's Republic of China residing abroad must be certified by the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China in that country; if there is no such embassy or consulate, it must be certified by the embassy or consulate of a third country that has diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China in that country, and then further certified by the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China in that third country, or certified by a local patriotic overseas Chinese organization.

Article 63: If the authority of a litigation representative is changed or revoked, the party shall inform the people's court in writing, and the people's court shall notify the opposing party.

Article 64: Lawyers and other litigation representatives acting in litigation have the right to investigate and collect evidence and may consult materials related to the case. The scope and methods for consulting materials related to the case shall be stipulated by the Supreme People's Court.

Article 65: In divorce cases where there is a litigation representative, the party themselves, unless unable to express their will, must still appear in court; if they are genuinely unable to appear in court due to special circumstances, they must submit a written opinion to the people's court.

### Chapter 6: Evidence

Article 66: Evidence includes:

(1) Statements of the parties;

(2) Documentary evidence;

(3) Physical evidence;

(4) Audio-visual materials;

(5) Electronic data;

(6) Witness testimony;

(7) Expert opinions;

(8) Inspection records.

Evidence must be verified before it can be used as the basis for determining facts.

Article 67: A party has the responsibility to provide evidence for their own claims.

If a party or their litigation representative is objectively unable to collect evidence on their own, or if the people's court considers that evidence is necessary for the trial of the case, the people's court shall investigate and collect it.

The people's court shall, in accordance with statutory procedures, comprehensively and objectively examine and verify evidence.

Article 68: A party shall provide evidence in a timely manner for their own claims.

The people's court shall, based on the party's claims and the circumstances of the case, determine the evidence to be provided by the party and the time limit for doing so. If a party has genuine difficulty in providing evidence within that time limit, they may apply to the people's court for an extension, and the people's court shall appropriately extend the time limit based on the party's application. If a party provides evidence beyond the time limit, the people's court shall order them to explain the reasons; if they refuse to explain or the reasons are not established, the people's court may, depending on the circumstances, refuse to admit the evidence, or admit the evidence but issue a reprimand or impose a fine.

Article 69: Upon receiving evidentiary materials submitted by a party, the people's court shall issue a receipt stating the name, number of pages, number of copies, whether it is an original or a copy, and the time of receipt, and shall have it signed or sealed by the handling personnel.

Article 70: The people's court has the authority to investigate and collect evidence from relevant units or individuals, and such units or individuals shall not refuse.

The people's court shall distinguish the authenticity of evidentiary documents submitted by relevant units or individuals and examine and determine their validity.

Article 71: Evidence shall be presented in court and cross-examined by the parties. Evidence involving state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy shall be kept confidential; if it needs to be presented in court, it shall not be presented during open sessions.

Article 72: Legal facts and documents that have been notarized through statutory procedures shall be used by the people's court as the basis for determining facts, unless there is contrary evidence sufficient to overturn the notarization.

Article 73: Documentary evidence shall be submitted in its original form. Physical evidence shall be submitted in its original form. If it is genuinely difficult to submit the original, copies, photographs, duplicates, or excerpts may be submitted.

If foreign documentary evidence is submitted, a Chinese translation must be attached.

Article 74: The people's court shall distinguish the authenticity of audio-visual materials and, in conjunction with other evidence in the case, examine and determine whether they can be used as the basis for determining facts.

Article 75: Any unit or individual who knows the circumstances of a case has the obligation to testify in court. The responsible person of the relevant unit shall support the witness in testifying.

A person who cannot correctly express their will cannot testify.

Article 76: Upon notification by the people's court, a witness shall testify in court. Under any of the following circumstances, with the permission of the people's court, testimony may be given through written testimony, audio-visual transmission technology, or audio-visual materials:

(1) Unable to appear in court due to health reasons;

(2) Unable to appear in court due to long distances or inconvenient transportation;

(3) Unable to appear in court due to force majeure such as natural disasters;

(4) Other legitimate reasons for being unable to appear in court.

Article 77: Necessary expenses incurred by a witness for fulfilling the obligation to testify in court, such as transportation, accommodation, meals, and loss of work time, shall be borne by the losing party. If a party applies for a witness to testify, that party shall advance the expenses; if no party applies and the people's court notifies a witness to testify, the people's court shall advance the expenses.

Article 78: The people's court shall, in conjunction with other evidence in the case, examine and determine whether a party's statements can be used as the basis for determining facts.

A party's refusal to make statements shall not affect the people's court's determination of the facts of the case based on evidence.

Article 79: A party may apply to the people's court for an expert evaluation on specialized issues to clarify the facts. If a party applies for an expert evaluation, the parties shall negotiate and agree on a qualified expert; if negotiation fails, the people's court shall designate one.

If no party applies for an expert evaluation, but the people's court considers an evaluation necessary for a specialized issue, it shall entrust a qualified expert to conduct the evaluation.

Article 80: An expert has the right to understand the case materials necessary for the evaluation and may, when necessary, question the parties or witnesses.

The expert shall provide a written expert opinion and sign or seal the evaluation report.

Article 81: If a party objects to the expert opinion or the people's court considers it necessary for the expert to appear in court, the expert shall testify in court. If, after notification by the people's court, the expert refuses to testify in court, the expert opinion shall not be used as the basis for determining facts; the party who paid the evaluation fees may request a refund of the evaluation fees.

Article 82: A party may apply to the people's court to notify a person with specialized knowledge to appear in court to provide opinions on the expert opinion or professional issues.

Article 83: When inspecting physical evidence or a scene, the inspector must present the credentials of the people's court and invite the local grassroots organization or a representative from the party's work unit to participate. The party or their adult family members shall be present; if they refuse to be present, it shall not affect the inspection.

Relevant units and individuals, upon notification by the people's court, have the obligation to protect the scene and assist in the inspection work.

The inspector shall make a record of the inspection situation and results, which shall be signed or sealed by the inspector, the parties, and the invited participants.

Article 84: If evidence may be lost or difficult to obtain later, a party may, during the litigation process, apply to the people's court for preservation of evidence, and the people's court may also take preservation measures on its own initiative.

In urgent circumstances, if evidence may be lost or difficult to obtain later, an interested party may, before filing a lawsuit or applying for arbitration, apply to the people's court at the location of the evidence to be preserved, the domicile of the person against whom the application is made, or the people's court with jurisdiction over the case for preservation measures. The applicant shall provide a guarantee; if no guarantee is provided, the application shall be dismissed.

Other procedures for evidence preservation shall be governed by the relevant provisions on preservation in Chapter 9 of this Law.

### Chapter 7: Time Periods and Service

#### Section 1: Time Periods

Article 85: Time periods include statutory time periods and time periods designated by the people's court.

Time periods are calculated in hours, days, months, and years. The hour and day from which a time period begins are not counted within the time period.

If the last day of a time period falls on a legal holiday, the day after the legal holiday shall be deemed the last day of the time period.

Time periods do not include travel time; if litigation documents are mailed before the expiration of the time period, they shall not be considered overdue.

Article 86: If a party misses a time limit due to force majeure or other legitimate reasons, they may apply for an extension within ten days after the obstacle is removed; whether to grant the extension shall be decided by the people's court.

#### Section 2: Service

Article 87: Service of litigation documents must be accompanied by a service receipt, on which the person served shall record the date of receipt and sign or seal it.

The date of signing on the service receipt by the person served shall be the date of service.

Article 88: Litigation documents shall be served directly on the person to be served. If the person to be served is a citizen and is not present, the documents may be served on their adult family member living with them; if the person to be served is a legal person or other organization, the documents shall be served on the legal representative of the legal person, the principal responsible person of the other organization, or the person responsible for receiving mail of that legal person or organization; if the person to be served has a litigation representative, the documents may be served on that representative for signature; if the person to be served has designated a person to receive documents on their behalf to the people's court, the documents shall be served on that designated person for signature.

The date of signing on the service receipt by the adult family member living with the person to be served, the person responsible for receiving mail of the legal person or other organization, the litigation representative, or the designated recipient shall be the date of service.

Article 89: If the person to be served or their adult family member living with them refuses to receive litigation documents, the server may invite representatives from the relevant grassroots organization or the person's work unit to be present, explain the situation, record the reason for refusal and the date on the service receipt, have the server and witnesses sign or seal it, and leave the litigation documents at the residence of the person to be served; alternatively, the litigation documents may be left at the residence of the person to be served, and the service process may be recorded by taking photos or videos, which shall be deemed as service.

Article 90: With the consent of the person to be served, the people's court may serve litigation documents electronically in a manner that can confirm receipt. If judgments, rulings, or mediation agreements served electronically are requested in paper form by the person served, the people's court shall provide them.

When service is made using the method in the preceding paragraph, the date on which the service information reaches the specific system of the person served shall be the date of service.

Article 91: If direct service of litigation documents is difficult, service may be entrusted to another people's court, or service may be made by mail. For service by mail, the date of receipt indicated on the return receipt shall be the date of service.

Article 92: If the person to be served is a military personnel, the documents shall be forwarded through the political organ at or above the regimental level of the unit to which they belong.

Article 93: If the person to be served is imprisoned, the documents shall be forwarded through the prison where they are held.

If the person to be served is subject to compulsory educational measures, the documents shall be forwarded through the institution where they are held.

Article 94: After receiving litigation documents, the organ or unit forwarding them must immediately deliver them to the person to be served for signature, and the date of signing on the service receipt shall be the date of service.

Article 95: If the whereabouts of the person to be served are unknown, or if service cannot be made by other methods provided in this Section, service shall be made by public announcement. After thirty days from the date of the public announcement, service shall be deemed to have been made.

The reasons and process for service by public announcement shall be recorded in the case file.

### Chapter 8: Mediation

Article 96: When trying civil cases, the people's court shall conduct mediation based on the principle of voluntariness of the parties, on the basis of clear facts, distinguishing right from wrong.

Article 97: Mediation by the people's court may be presided over by a single judge or by a collegial panel, and shall be conducted on-site as much as possible.

When conducting mediation, the people's court may notify the parties and witnesses to appear in court by simple means.

Article 98: When conducting mediation, the people's court may invite relevant units and individuals to assist. The invited units and individuals shall assist the people's court in conducting mediation.

Article 99: A mediation agreement must be reached voluntarily by both parties and shall not be coerced. The content of the mediation agreement shall not violate the law.

Article 100: If a mediation agreement is reached, the people's court shall prepare a mediation document. The mediation document shall state the claims, the facts of the case, and the mediation result.

The mediation document shall be signed by the trial personnel and the clerk, sealed with the seal of the people's court, and served on both parties.

The mediation document becomes legally effective upon being signed and received by both parties.

Article 101: For the following types of cases where a mediation agreement is reached, the people's court may not prepare a mediation document:

(1) Divorce cases where reconciliation is achieved through mediation;

(2) Cases where an adoption relationship is maintained through mediation;

(3) Cases that can be performed immediately;

(4) Other cases where a mediation document is not required.

For agreements that do not require a mediation document, they shall be recorded in the trial record, and upon being signed or sealed by both parties, the trial personnel, and the clerk, they become legally effective.

Article 102: If a mediation agreement is not reached, or if one party reneges before the mediation document is served, the people's court shall render a judgment promptly.

### Chapter 9: Preservation and Advance Execution

Article 103: In cases where it may become difficult to enforce a judgment or cause other damage to a party due to the actions of one party or other reasons, the people's court may, upon application by the opposing party, rule to preserve the party's property, order them to perform a specific act, or prohibit them from performing a specific act; if no party has applied, the people's court may also rule to take preservation measures when necessary.

When taking preservation measures, the people's court may order the applicant to provide a guarantee; if the applicant fails to provide a guarantee, the application shall be dismissed.

After accepting an application, if the circumstances are urgent, the people's court must make a ruling within forty-eight hours; if a ruling is made to take preservation measures, execution shall begin immediately.

Article 104: If an interested party is in an urgent situation where failure to apply for preservation immediately will cause irreparable harm to their legitimate rights and interests, they may, before filing a lawsuit or applying for arbitration, apply to the people's court at the location of the property to be preserved, the domicile of the person against whom the application is made, or the people's court with jurisdiction over the case for preservation measures. The applicant shall provide a guarantee; if no guarantee is provided, the application shall be dismissed.

After accepting an application, the people's court must make a ruling within forty-eight hours; if a ruling is made to take preservation measures, execution shall begin immediately.

If the applicant fails to file a lawsuit or apply for arbitration in accordance with the law within thirty days after the people's court takes preservation measures, the people's court shall lift the preservation.

Article 105: Preservation shall be limited to the scope of the claim or to property related to the case.

Article 106: Property preservation shall be carried out by seizure, impoundment, freezing, or other methods provided by law. After preserving property, the people's court shall immediately notify the person whose property is preserved.

Property that has already been seized or frozen shall not be seized or frozen again.

Article 107: In property dispute cases, if the person against whom the application is made provides a guarantee, the people's court shall rule to lift the preservation.

Article 108: If the application is erroneous, the applicant shall compensate the person against whom the application is made for losses suffered due to the preservation.

Article 109: The people's court may, upon application by a party, rule on advance execution in the following types of cases:

(1) Claims for alimony, support, maintenance, pensions, or medical expenses;

(2) Claims for labor remuneration;

(3) Cases requiring advance execution due to urgent circumstances.

Article 110: For a ruling on advance execution by the people's court, the following conditions must be met:

(1) The rights and obligations between the parties are clear, and failure to grant advance execution would seriously affect the applicant's livelihood or production and operation;

(2) The person against whom the application is made has the ability to perform.

The people's court may order the applicant to provide a guarantee; if the applicant fails to provide a guarantee, the application shall be dismissed. If the applicant loses the case, they shall compensate the person against whom the application is made for property losses suffered due to the advance execution.

Article 111: If a party is dissatisfied with a ruling on preservation or advance execution, they may apply for reconsideration once. The execution of the ruling shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

### Chapter 10: Compulsory Measures Against Obstruction of Civil Proceedings

Article 112: If a defendant who must appear in court, after being summoned twice by subpoena, refuses to appear in court without justifiable reasons, the people's court may issue a warrant for their arrest.

Article 113: Litigation participants and other persons shall abide by court rules.

The people's court may reprimand persons who violate court rules, order them to leave the courtroom, or impose a fine or detention.

Persons who create disturbances, storm the court, insult, slander, threaten, or assault trial personnel, or seriously disrupt court order shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are minor, a fine or detention shall be imposed.

Article 114: If a litigation participant or other person commits any of the following acts, the people's court may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine or detention; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Forging or destroying important evidence, hindering the people's court from trying the case;

(2) Using violence, threats, or bribery to prevent a witness from testifying, or instigating, bribing, or coercing others to commit perjury;

(3) Concealing, transferring, selling, or destroying property that has been seized or impounded, or property that has been inventoried and ordered to be kept in custody, or transferring property that has been frozen;

(4) Insulting, slandering, framing, assaulting, or retaliating against judicial personnel, litigation participants, witnesses, interpreters, experts, inspectors, or persons assisting in execution;

(5) Using violence, threats, or other methods to obstruct judicial personnel from performing their duties;

(6) Refusing to comply with legally effective judgments or rulings of the people's court.

If a unit commits any of the acts specified in the preceding paragraph, the people's court may impose a fine or detention on its principal responsible person or the person directly responsible; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 115: If parties maliciously collude to infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of others through litigation, mediation, or other means, the people's court shall dismiss their claims and, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine or detention; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.Article 116 If a judgment debtor colludes with another person maliciously to evade the obligations specified in a legally effective document through litigation, arbitration, mediation, or other means, the people's court shall, based on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine or detention; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 117 If a unit obligated to assist in investigation or enforcement commits any of the following acts, the people's court may, in addition to ordering it to fulfill its assistance obligations, impose a fine:

(1) The unit refuses or obstructs the people's court in investigating and collecting evidence;
(2) After receiving a notice from the people's court to assist in enforcement, the unit refuses to assist in inquiring, seizing, freezing, transferring, or auctioning property;
(3) After receiving a notice from the people's court to assist in enforcement, the unit refuses to assist in withholding the judgment debtor's income, handling the transfer of property rights certificates, or delivering relevant instruments, certificates, or other property;
(4) Other acts of refusing to assist in enforcement.

For a unit that commits any of the acts specified in the preceding paragraph, the people's court may impose a fine on its principal responsible person or the person directly responsible; if the unit still fails to fulfill its assistance obligations, the court may impose detention; and it may also issue a judicial recommendation to the supervisory authority or relevant authority for disciplinary action.

Article 118 The amount of a fine imposed on an individual shall not exceed RMB 100,000. The amount of a fine imposed on a unit shall be not less than RMB 50,000 and not more than RMB 1,000,000.

The term of detention shall not exceed 15 days.

A detained person shall be handed over by the people's court to the public security authority for custody. During the detention period, if the detained person admits and corrects the mistake, the people's court may decide to release the person before the expiration of the term.

Article 119 A summons by force, fine, or detention must be approved by the president of the court.

A summons by force shall be executed using a summons warrant.

A fine or detention shall be executed using a written decision. If a party disagrees with the decision, it may apply for one reconsideration to the people's court at the next higher level. Enforcement shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

Article 120 Compulsory measures against acts obstructing civil litigation must be decided by the people's court. If any unit or individual illegally detains another person or illegally seizes another person's property to recover a debt, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law, or detention or a fine shall be imposed.

### Chapter 11: Litigation Costs

Article 121 A party conducting civil litigation shall pay the case acceptance fee in accordance with the regulations. For property cases, in addition to the case acceptance fee, other litigation costs shall be paid in accordance with the regulations.

If a party has genuine difficulty in paying the litigation costs, it may apply to the people's court for a deferment, reduction, or exemption of payment in accordance with the regulations.

The measures for collecting litigation costs shall be formulated separately.

## Part Two: Trial Procedure

### Chapter 12: Ordinary Procedure of First Instance

#### Section 1: Filing and Acceptance

Article 122 A lawsuit must meet the following conditions:

(1) The plaintiff is a citizen, legal person, or other organization that has a direct interest in the case;
(2) There is a clear defendant;
(3) There are specific claims, facts, and reasons;
(4) The case falls within the scope of civil litigation accepted by the people's court and is under the jurisdiction of the court where the lawsuit is filed.

Article 123 A lawsuit shall be filed by submitting a statement of complaint to the people's court, along with copies equal to the number of defendants.

If it is genuinely difficult to write a statement of complaint, an oral complaint may be made, which shall be recorded in the court records and notified to the opposing party.

Article 124 A statement of complaint shall specify the following:

(1) The plaintiff's name, gender, age, ethnicity, occupation, work unit, domicile, and contact information; for a legal person or other organization, its name, domicile, and the name, position, and contact information of its legal representative or principal responsible person;
(2) The defendant's name, gender, work unit, domicile, and other information; for a legal person or other organization, its name, domicile, and other information;
(3) The claims and the facts and reasons on which they are based;
(4) The evidence and its source, and the names and domiciles of witnesses.

Article 125 For civil disputes filed with the people's court, if mediation is appropriate, mediation shall be conducted first, unless the parties refuse mediation.

Article 126 The people's court shall protect the parties' right to file a lawsuit in accordance with the law. For a lawsuit that meets the conditions of Article 122 of this Law, it must be accepted. If the conditions for filing a lawsuit are met, the court shall file the case within seven days and notify the parties; if the conditions are not met, the court shall issue a written ruling within seven days not to accept the case; if the plaintiff disagrees with the ruling, an appeal may be filed.

Article 127 The people's court shall handle the following lawsuits according to the circumstances:

(1) If the case falls within the scope of administrative litigation under the Administrative Procedure Law, the plaintiff shall be informed to file an administrative lawsuit;
(2) If, in accordance with the law, the parties have reached a written arbitration agreement to apply for arbitration and cannot file a lawsuit with the people's court, the plaintiff shall be informed to apply for arbitration with the arbitration institution;
(3) If, in accordance with the law, the dispute should be handled by another authority, the plaintiff shall be informed to apply to the relevant authority for resolution;
(4) If the case is not under the jurisdiction of this court, the plaintiff shall be informed to file a lawsuit with the people's court that has jurisdiction;
(5) If a judgment, ruling, or mediation statement has already become legally effective and the party files a lawsuit again, the plaintiff shall be informed to apply for retrial, except for a ruling by the people's court allowing withdrawal of the lawsuit;
(6) If, in accordance with the law, a lawsuit cannot be filed within a certain period, and it is filed within that period, it shall not be accepted;
(7) If a judgment has denied divorce or a mediation has reconciled the parties in a divorce case, or a judgment or mediation has maintained an adoption relationship, and the plaintiff files a lawsuit again within six months without new circumstances or new reasons, it shall not be accepted.

#### Section 2: Preparations Before Trial

Article 128 The people's court shall send a copy of the statement of complaint to the defendant within five days from the date of filing the case, and the defendant shall submit a statement of defense within fifteen days from the date of receipt. The statement of defense shall specify the defendant's name, gender, age, ethnicity, occupation, work unit, domicile, and contact information; for a legal person or other organization, its name, domicile, and the name, position, and contact information of its legal representative or principal responsible person. The people's court shall send a copy of the statement of defense to the plaintiff within five days from the date of receipt.

If the defendant fails to submit a statement of defense, it shall not affect the trial by the people's court.

Article 129 For a case that the people's court decides to accept, it shall inform the parties of their relevant litigation rights and obligations in the case acceptance notice and the response notice, or orally inform them.

Article 130 After the people's court accepts a case, if a party raises an objection to jurisdiction, it shall do so during the period for submitting the statement of defense. The people's court shall examine the objection raised by the party. If the objection is established, the court shall rule to transfer the case to the people's court with jurisdiction; if the objection is not established, the court shall rule to reject it.

If a party does not raise a jurisdictional objection and responds to the lawsuit and defends, it shall be deemed that the court where the lawsuit is filed has jurisdiction, except in cases violating hierarchical jurisdiction or exclusive jurisdiction.

Article 131 After the trial personnel are determined, the parties shall be informed within three days.

Article 132 The trial personnel must carefully review the litigation materials and investigate and collect necessary evidence.

Article 133 When personnel dispatched by the people's court conduct an investigation, they shall present their credentials to the person being investigated.

After the investigation record is reviewed by the person being investigated, it shall be signed or sealed by both the person being investigated and the investigator.

Article 134 The people's court may, when necessary, entrust another people's court to conduct an investigation.

For an entrusted investigation, clear items and requirements must be specified. The entrusted people's court may proactively supplement the investigation.

After receiving the letter of entrustment, the entrusted people's court shall complete the investigation within thirty days. If it cannot be completed for some reason, it shall notify the entrusting people's court in writing within the aforementioned period.

Article 135 If a party who must jointly participate in the litigation has not done so, the people's court shall notify that party to participate.

Article 136 The people's court shall handle accepted cases according to the circumstances:

(1) If the parties have no dispute and the conditions for the supervision procedure are met, the case may be transferred to the supervision procedure;
(2) If mediation is possible before the trial, mediation shall be used to resolve the dispute in a timely manner;
(3) Based on the circumstances of the case, determine whether to apply the summary procedure or the ordinary procedure;
(4) If a trial is necessary, clarify the points of dispute by requiring the parties to exchange evidence, among other means.

#### Section 3: Trial in Open Court

Article 137 The people's court shall hear civil cases in open court, except those involving state secrets, personal privacy, or as otherwise provided by law.

For divorce cases or cases involving trade secrets, if the parties apply for a closed hearing, the hearing may be closed.

Article 138 When hearing civil cases, the people's court shall conduct circuit trials and handle cases locally as needed.

Article 139 The people's court shall notify the parties and other participants in the litigation three days before the trial. For open hearings, the names of the parties, the nature of the case, and the time and place of the trial shall be announced.

Article 140 Before the trial begins, the court clerk shall verify whether the parties and other participants in the litigation are present and announce the courtroom rules.

During the trial, the presiding judge or the sole judge shall verify the parties, announce the nature of the case, announce the list of trial personnel and the court clerk, inform the parties of their relevant litigation rights and obligations, and ask the parties whether they wish to apply for withdrawal.

Article 141 The court investigation shall proceed in the following order:

(1) Statements by the parties;
(2) Informing witnesses of their rights and obligations, witnesses testifying, and reading out the testimony of witnesses who are not present;
(3) Presenting documentary evidence, physical evidence, audio-visual materials, and electronic data;
(4) Reading out expert opinions;
(5) Reading out inspection records.

Article 142 The parties may present new evidence in court.

With the court's permission, the parties may question witnesses, experts, and inspectors.

Whether to allow a party's request for a new investigation, expert evaluation, or inspection shall be decided by the people's court.

Article 143 If the plaintiff increases claims, the defendant files a counterclaim, or a third party files claims related to the case, they may be tried together.

Article 144 The court debate shall proceed in the following order:

(1) Statements by the plaintiff and its litigation representative;
(2) Defense by the defendant and its litigation representative;
(3) Statements or defense by the third party and its litigation representative;
(4) Mutual debate.

After the court debate concludes, the presiding judge or the sole judge shall solicit the final opinions of the parties in the order of plaintiff, defendant, and third party.

Article 145 After the court debate concludes, a judgment shall be made in accordance with the law. If mediation is possible before the judgment, mediation may still be conducted; if mediation fails, a judgment shall be made promptly.

Article 146 If the plaintiff, after being summoned by a summons, fails to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court midway without the court's permission, the case may be treated as withdrawn; if the defendant files a counterclaim, a default judgment may be made.

Article 147 If the defendant, after being summoned by a summons, fails to appear in court without justifiable reasons, or leaves the court midway without the court's permission, a default judgment may be made.

Article 148 Before the judgment is pronounced, if the plaintiff applies to withdraw the lawsuit, whether to permit it shall be decided by the people's court by ruling.

If the people's court rules not to permit the withdrawal of the lawsuit, and the plaintiff, after being summoned by a summons, fails to appear in court without justifiable reasons, a default judgment may be made.

Article 149 The trial may be adjourned under any of the following circumstances:

(1) A party or other participant in the litigation who must be present fails to appear for a justifiable reason;
(2) A party temporarily applies for withdrawal;
(3) It is necessary to notify a new witness to appear, obtain new evidence, conduct a new expert evaluation or inspection, or supplement an investigation;
(4) Other circumstances where adjournment is necessary.

Article 150 The court clerk shall record all activities of the court trial in the record, which shall be signed by the trial personnel and the court clerk.

The court record shall be read out in court, or the parties and other participants in the litigation may be informed to read it in court or within five days. If the parties or other participants in the litigation believe that there are omissions or errors in the record of their statements, they have the right to apply for corrections. If the corrections are not made, the application shall be recorded in the case file.

The court record shall be signed or sealed by the parties and other participants in the litigation. If they refuse to sign or seal, the circumstances shall be noted and attached to the case file.

Article 151 The people's court shall publicly pronounce judgments for all cases, whether heard in open or closed court.

If the judgment is pronounced in court, the written judgment shall be issued within ten days; if the judgment is pronounced on a scheduled date, the written judgment shall be issued immediately after the pronouncement.

When pronouncing a judgment, the parties must be informed of their right to appeal, the appeal period, and the court to which the appeal may be made.

When pronouncing a divorce judgment, the parties must be informed that they may not remarry before the judgment becomes legally effective.

Article 152 A case tried by the people's court under the ordinary procedure shall be concluded within six months from the date of filing. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it may be extended by six months with the approval of the president of the court; if a further extension is necessary, approval from the people's court at the next higher level shall be obtained.

#### Section 4: Suspension and Termination of Litigation

Article 153 Litigation shall be suspended under any of the following circumstances:

(1) One party dies, and it is necessary to wait for the heir to indicate whether to participate in the litigation;
(2) One party loses the capacity to conduct litigation, and a legal representative has not yet been determined;
(3) A legal person or other organization that is a party terminates, and the successor of rights and obligations has not yet been determined;
(4) One party is unable to participate in the litigation due to force majeure;
(5) The case must be based on the outcome of another case, which has not yet been concluded;
(6) Other circumstances where suspension is necessary.

After the cause of suspension is eliminated, the litigation shall resume.

Article 154 Litigation shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The plaintiff dies without an heir, or the heir waives the right to litigate;
(2) The defendant dies without an estate and without a person who should assume obligations;
(3) One party in a divorce case dies;
(4) One party in a case for alimony, support, or maintenance, or for the dissolution of an adoption relationship, dies.

#### Section 5: Judgments and Rulings

Article 155 A written judgment shall state the outcome of the judgment and the reasons for it. The content of the written judgment includes:

(1) The nature of the case, the claims, the disputed facts, and the reasons;
(2) The facts and reasons found in the judgment, and the applicable law and reasons;
(3) The outcome of the judgment and the allocation of litigation costs;
(4) The appeal period and the court to which the appeal may be made.

The written judgment shall be signed by the trial personnel and the court clerk, and sealed with the seal of the people's court.

Article 156 When trying a case, if part of the facts are already clear, the people's court may make a judgment on that part first.

Article 157 Rulings shall apply to the following matters:

(1) Non-acceptance;
(2) Objection to jurisdiction;
(3) Dismissal of the lawsuit;
(4) Preservation and advance enforcement;
(5) Permission or non-permission to withdraw the lawsuit;
(6) Suspension or termination of litigation;
(7) Correction of clerical errors in the written judgment;
(8) Suspension or termination of enforcement;
(9) Revocation or non-enforcement of an arbitration award;
(10) Non-enforcement of a creditor's document with compulsory enforcement effect granted by a notary office;
(11) Other matters requiring resolution by ruling.

An appeal may be filed against rulings on items (1) to (3) of the preceding paragraph.

A written ruling shall state the outcome of the ruling and the reasons for it. The written ruling shall be signed by the trial personnel and the court clerk, and sealed with the seal of the people's court. If the ruling is oral, it shall be recorded in the court record.

Article 158 Judgments and rulings of the Supreme People's Court, as well as judgments and rulings that are not subject to appeal by law or that have not been appealed within the appeal period, are legally effective judgments and rulings.

Article 159 The public may inspect legally effective written judgments and rulings, except for content involving state secrets, trade secrets, and personal privacy.

### Chapter 13: Summary Procedure

Article 160 This chapter applies to simple civil cases tried by basic people's courts and their dispatched tribunals where the facts are clear, the rights and obligations are clear, and the dispute is minor.

For civil cases other than those specified in the preceding paragraph tried by basic people's courts and their dispatched tribunals, the parties may also agree to apply the summary procedure.

Article 161 For simple civil cases, the plaintiff may file an oral complaint.

Both parties may simultaneously go to the basic people's court or its dispatched tribunal to request resolution of the dispute. The basic people's court or its dispatched tribunal may hear the case immediately or set another date for the hearing.

Article 162 When trying simple civil cases, basic people's courts and their dispatched tribunals may use simplified methods to summon the parties and witnesses, serve litigation documents, and hear the case, but the parties' right to state their opinions shall be guaranteed.

Article 163 A simple civil case shall be tried by a single judge alone, and is not subject to the restrictions of Articles 139, 141, and 144 of this Law.

Article 164 A case tried by the people's court under the summary procedure shall be concluded within three months from the date of filing. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it may be extended by one month with the approval of the president of the court.

Article 165 For simple civil cases involving monetary payment tried by basic people's courts and their dispatched tribunals, where the facts are clear, the rights and obligations are clear, and the dispute is minor, and the subject matter is not more than 50% of the average annual salary of employed persons in the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the previous year, the small claims procedure shall be applied, and the judgment shall be final.

For civil cases specified in the preceding paragraph tried by basic people's courts and their dispatched tribunals, where the subject matter exceeds 50% but is not more than twice the average annual salary of employed persons in the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in the previous year, the parties may also agree to apply the small claims procedure.

Article 166 The people's court shall not apply the small claims procedure to the following civil cases:

(1) Cases involving personal relationships or property rights confirmation;
(2) Cases involving foreign elements;
(3) Cases requiring evaluation or expert opinion, or where there is an objection to the results of pre-litigation evaluation or expert opinion;
(4) Cases where one party's whereabouts are unknown;
(5) Cases where the party files a counterclaim;
(6) Other cases where it is inappropriate to apply the small claims procedure.

Article 167 When trying a case under the small claims procedure, the people's court may conclude the case in one trial and pronounce the judgment in court.

Article 168 A case tried by the people's court under the small claims procedure shall be concluded within two months from the date of filing. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it may be extended by one month with the approval of the president of the court.Article 169 If, during trial, a people's court finds that a case is not suitable for application of the small claims procedure, it shall apply the other provisions of the summary procedure for trial or rule to convert the case to the ordinary procedure.

If a party believes that the trial of a case under the small claims procedure violates the provisions of the law, it may raise an objection to the people's court. The people's court shall examine the objection raised by the party; if the objection is sustained, it shall apply the other provisions of the summary procedure for trial or rule to convert the case to the ordinary procedure; if the objection is not sustained, it shall rule to reject it.

Article 170 If, during trial, a people's court finds that a case is not suitable for application of the summary procedure, it shall rule to convert the case to the ordinary procedure.

### Chapter 14: Procedure of Second Instance

Article 171 If a party refuses to accept a judgment of first instance rendered by a local people's court, it has the right to appeal to the next higher people's court within fifteen days from the date the written judgment is served.

If a party refuses to accept a ruling of first instance rendered by a local people's court, it has the right to appeal to the next higher people's court within ten days from the date the written ruling is served.

Article 172 An appeal shall be submitted in the form of a written petition for appeal. The content of the petition for appeal shall include the names of the parties, the name of the legal person and the name of its legal representative, or the name of any other organization and the name of its principal responsible person; the name of the people's court of first instance, the case number, and the cause of action; and the requests and grounds for the appeal.

Article 173 A petition for appeal shall be submitted through the people's court of first instance, and copies shall be provided according to the number of opposing parties or their representatives.

If a party appeals directly to the people's court of second instance, that court shall, within five days, transfer the petition for appeal to the people's court of first instance.

Article 174 Upon receiving the petition for appeal, the people's court of first instance shall, within five days, serve a copy of the petition for appeal on the opposing party. The opposing party shall submit a statement of defense within fifteen days from the date of receipt. The people's court shall, within five days from the date of receiving the statement of defense, serve a copy thereof on the appellant. The failure of the opposing party to submit a statement of defense shall not affect the trial by the people's court.

The people's court of first instance shall, within five days, forward the petition for appeal, the statement of defense, together with the entire case file and evidence, to the people's court of second instance.

Article 175 The people's court of second instance shall review the facts and the application of the law relevant to the requests of the appeal.

Article 176 The people's court of second instance shall hold a hearing for cases on appeal. If, after reviewing the case files, conducting investigations, and questioning the parties, no new facts, evidence, or grounds are presented, and the people's court deems a hearing unnecessary, it may decide not to hold a hearing.

The people's court of second instance may hear cases on appeal at its own seat, or at the place where the case arose or where the people's court of first instance is located.

Article 177 After hearing a case on appeal, the people's court of second instance shall handle it according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the facts are clearly found and the law is correctly applied in the original judgment or ruling, the appeal shall be rejected by a judgment or ruling, and the original judgment or ruling shall be affirmed;

(2) If the facts are incorrectly found or the law is incorrectly applied in the original judgment or ruling, the original judgment or ruling shall be amended, reversed, or modified by a judgment or ruling according to law;

(3) If the basic facts are unclear in the original judgment, the original judgment shall be reversed by a ruling and the case shall be remanded to the people's court of first instance for retrial, or the judgment shall be amended after the facts are clarified;

(4) If the original judgment omits a party or is rendered by default in violation of the law, or there are other serious violations of legal procedure, the original judgment shall be reversed by a ruling and the case shall be remanded to the people's court of first instance for retrial.

After the people's court of first instance renders a judgment on a case remanded for retrial, if a party appeals, the people's court of second instance shall not remand the case for retrial again.

Article 178 All rulings made by the people's court of second instance on appeals against rulings of the people's court of first instance shall be in the form of rulings.

Article 179 The people's court of second instance may conduct mediation in hearing a case on appeal. If a mediation agreement is reached, a mediation document shall be prepared, signed by the judges and the court clerk, and sealed with the seal of the people's court. After the mediation document is served, the judgment of the people's court of first instance shall be deemed to be vacated.

Article 180 Before the judgment of the people's court of second instance is announced, if the appellant applies to withdraw the appeal, whether to permit it shall be decided by a ruling of the people's court of second instance.

Article 181 In hearing a case on appeal, the people's court of second instance shall apply the ordinary procedure of first instance, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter.

Article 182 The judgments and rulings of the people's court of second instance are final.

Article 183 A people's court trying a case on appeal against a judgment shall conclude the case within three months from the date of docketing the appeal in the second instance. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the president of the court.

A people's court trying a case on appeal against a ruling shall make a final ruling within thirty days from the date of docketing the appeal in the second instance.

### Chapter 15: Special Procedures

#### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 184 The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to the following cases tried by a people's court: cases concerning the qualifications of voters; cases concerning the declaration of a person as missing or dead; cases concerning the determination of a citizen as having no or limited capacity for civil conduct; cases concerning the determination of property as ownerless; cases concerning the confirmation of mediation agreements; and cases concerning the realization of security interests. Matters not provided for in this Chapter shall be governed by the relevant provisions of this Law and other laws.

Article 185 Cases tried under the procedures of this Chapter shall be subject to a system of final instance at first instance. Cases concerning the qualifications of voters, or major or difficult cases, shall be tried by a collegial panel composed of judges; other cases shall be tried by a single judge alone.

Article 186 If, in the course of trying a case under the procedures of this Chapter, a people's court finds that the case involves a dispute over civil rights and interests, it shall rule to terminate the special procedure and inform the interested parties that they may file a separate lawsuit.

Article 187 A people's court trying a case under the special procedures shall conclude the case within thirty days from the date of docketing the case or within thirty days after the expiration of the public notice period. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the president of the court, except for cases concerning the qualifications of voters.

#### Section 2: Cases Concerning Voter Qualifications

Article 188 A citizen who refuses to accept the decision made by the election committee on a complaint concerning voter qualifications may bring a lawsuit to the basic people's court in the electoral district no later than five days before the election day.

Article 189 After accepting a case concerning voter qualifications, the people's court must conclude the case before the election day.

During the trial, the plaintiff, the representative of the election committee, and the relevant citizen must participate.

The written judgment of the people's court shall be served on the election committee and the plaintiff before the election day, and the relevant citizen shall be notified.

#### Section 3: Cases Concerning Declaration of Missing Persons or Death

Article 190 If a citizen has been missing for two full years, an interested party applying for a declaration of the citizen's missing status shall file the application with the basic people's court in the place of domicile of the missing person.

The written application shall state the facts, time, and request regarding the missing person, and shall be accompanied by a written certification from the public security organ or other relevant organ concerning the citizen's missing status.

Article 191 If a citizen has been missing for four full years, or has been missing for two full years due to an accident, or has been missing due to an accident and it is proved by the relevant organ that the citizen is unlikely to be alive, an interested party applying for a declaration of the citizen's death shall file the application with the basic people's court in the place of domicile of the missing person.

The written application shall state the facts, time, and request regarding the missing person, and shall be accompanied by a written certification from the public security organ or other relevant organ concerning the citizen's missing status.

Article 192 After accepting a case concerning the declaration of a missing person or death, the people's court shall issue a public notice seeking the missing person. The period of the public notice for declaring a person missing is three months, and the period for declaring a person dead is one year. If a person is missing due to an accident and it is proved by the relevant organ that the citizen is unlikely to be alive, the period of the public notice for declaring death is three months.

Upon the expiration of the public notice period, the people's court shall, based on whether the facts of the missing person or death are confirmed, render a judgment declaring the person missing or dead, or a judgment rejecting the application.

Article 193 If a citizen who has been declared missing or dead reappears, upon the application of the citizen or an interested party, the people's court shall render a new judgment to revoke the original judgment.

#### Section 4: Cases Concerning Determination of a Citizen as Having No or Limited Capacity for Civil Conduct

Article 194 An application for determining a citizen as having no or limited capacity for civil conduct shall be filed by an interested party or a relevant organization with the basic people's court in the place of domicile of the citizen.

The written application shall state the facts and grounds for the citizen having no or limited capacity for civil conduct.

Article 195 After accepting an application, the people's court shall, if necessary, have the citizen who is the subject of the application for determination of having no or limited capacity for civil conduct undergo an expert evaluation. If the applicant has already provided an expert opinion, the expert opinion shall be reviewed.

Article 196 In trying a case concerning the determination of a citizen as having no or limited capacity for civil conduct, the people's court shall appoint a close relative of the citizen, excluding the applicant, as the agent. If the close relatives shirk the responsibility, the people's court shall designate one of them as the agent. If the citizen's health condition permits, the citizen's own opinion shall also be sought.

If, after trial, the people's court finds that the application is supported by facts, it shall render a judgment determining the citizen as a person having no or limited capacity for civil conduct; if it finds that the application is not supported by facts, it shall render a judgment rejecting the application.

Article 197 Upon the application of the person determined as having no or limited capacity for civil conduct, an interested party, or a relevant organization, if the people's court confirms that the cause for the citizen having no or limited capacity for civil conduct has been eliminated, it shall render a new judgment to revoke the original judgment.

#### Section 5: Cases Concerning Determination of Property as Ownerless

Article 198 An application for determining property as ownerless shall be filed by a citizen, legal person, or other organization with the basic people's court in the place where the property is located.

The written application shall state the type and quantity of the property and the grounds for requesting the determination of the property as ownerless.

Article 199 After accepting an application, the people's court shall, upon examination and verification, issue a public notice for claiming the property. If no claim is made within one year from the date of the public notice, the property shall be determined as ownerless by judgment and shall be vested in the state or the collective.

Article 200 After a judgment determines property as ownerless, if the original owner or heir of the property appears and makes a claim within the statute of limitations period provided for in the Civil Code, the people's court shall, upon verification, render a new judgment to revoke the original judgment.

#### Section 6: Cases Concerning Confirmation of Mediation Agreements

Article 201 If a mediation agreement is reached through mediation conducted by a lawfully established mediation organization and the parties apply for judicial confirmation, the parties shall jointly file the application with the following people's courts within thirty days from the date the mediation agreement takes effect:

(1) If the people's court invited the mediation organization to conduct pre-litigation mediation, the application shall be filed with the people's court that made the invitation;

(2) If the mediation organization conducted mediation on its own initiative, the application shall be filed with the basic people's court in the place of domicile of the parties, the place where the subject matter is located, or the place where the mediation organization is located; if the dispute involved in the mediation agreement falls under the jurisdiction of an intermediate people's court, the application shall be filed with the corresponding intermediate people's court.

Article 202 After accepting an application, the people's court shall, upon examination, if it complies with the provisions of the law, rule that the mediation agreement is valid; if one party refuses to perform or fails to fully perform, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement. If it does not comply with the provisions of the law, the people's court shall rule to reject the application, and the parties may modify the original mediation agreement or reach a new mediation agreement through mediation, or may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

#### Section 7: Cases Concerning Realization of Security Interests

Article 203 An application for the realization of a security interest shall be filed by the security interest holder or other person entitled to request the realization of the security interest, in accordance with the Civil Code and other laws, with the basic people's court in the place where the security property is located or where the security interest is registered.

Article 204 After accepting an application, the people's court shall, upon examination, if it complies with the provisions of the law, rule to auction or sell the security property; the parties may apply to the people's court for enforcement based on this ruling. If it does not comply with the provisions of the law, the people's court shall rule to reject the application, and the parties may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

### Chapter 16: Procedure for Trial Supervision

Article 205 If the president of a people's court at any level finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by that court indeed contains an error and deems it necessary to retry the case, the president shall submit it to the judicial committee for discussion and decision.

If the Supreme People's Court finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by a local people's court at any level indeed contains an error, or if a people's court at a higher level finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by a people's court at a lower level indeed contains an error, the higher court has the authority to bring the case up for trial or to direct the lower court to retry the case.

Article 206 If a party believes that a legally effective judgment or ruling contains an error, it may apply for a retrial to the next higher people's court; if a case involves a large number of parties on one side or if both parties are citizens, the application may also be made to the original trial people's court. The filing of an application for retrial by a party shall not suspend the enforcement of the judgment or ruling.

Article 207 A people's court shall retry a case if the application of a party meets any of the following circumstances:

(1) There is new evidence sufficient to overturn the original judgment or ruling;

(2) The basic facts found in the original judgment or ruling lack supporting evidence;

(3) The main evidence for the facts found in the original judgment or ruling is forged;

(4) The main evidence for the facts found in the original judgment or ruling was not cross-examined;

(5) For the main evidence needed for the trial of the case, the party was unable to collect it on its own due to objective reasons, and the people's court, upon written application by the party for investigation and collection, failed to investigate and collect it;

(6) The application of the law in the original judgment or ruling is indeed erroneous;

(7) The composition of the trial organization is illegal, or a judge who should have recused themselves according to law failed to do so;

(8) A person without capacity for litigation was not represented by a legal agent in the proceedings, or a party who should have participated in the proceedings failed to do so for reasons not attributable to the party or its litigation agent;

(9) The party's right to debate was deprived in violation of the provisions of the law;

(10) A default judgment was rendered without service of a summons by subpoena;

(11) The original judgment or ruling omitted or exceeded the claims of the litigation;

(12) The legal document on which the original judgment or ruling was based has been revoked or modified;

(13) The judges who tried the case engaged in acts of corruption, bribery, favoritism, or perversion of the law in rendering the judgment.

Article 208 If a party provides evidence proving that a legally effective mediation document was reached in violation of the principle of voluntariness or that the content of the mediation agreement violates the law, the party may apply for a retrial. If the people's court verifies the matter, it shall retry the case.

Article 209 A party may not apply for a retrial of a legally effective judgment or mediation document that dissolves a marriage.

Article 210 A party applying for a retrial shall submit a written application for retrial and other materials. The people's court shall, within five days from the date of receiving the application for retrial, serve a copy of the application for retrial on the opposing party. The opposing party shall submit written opinions within fifteen days from the date of receiving the copy of the application for retrial; failure to submit written opinions shall not affect the review by the people's court. The people's court may require the applicant and the opposing party to supplement relevant materials and inquire about relevant matters.

Article 211 The people's court shall review the application within three months from the date of receiving the application for retrial; if it meets the provisions of this Law, it shall rule to retry the case; if it does not meet the provisions, it shall rule to reject the application. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the president of the court.

Cases in which a retrial is ordered upon the application of a party shall be tried by a people's court at or above the intermediate level, except where the party chooses to apply for a retrial to the basic people's court in accordance with Article 206 of this Law. Cases in which the Supreme People's Court or a higher people's court orders a retrial shall be retried by that court itself, or may be referred to another people's court for retrial, or may also be referred to the original trial people's court for retrial.

Article 212 A party applying for a retrial shall do so within six months after the judgment or ruling takes legal effect; if the circumstances provided for in items (1), (3), (12), or (13) of Article 207 of this Law exist, the application shall be made within six months from the date the party knew or should have known of such circumstances.

Article 213 For a case decided to be retried in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, the enforcement of the original judgment, ruling, or mediation document shall be suspended by a ruling, except for cases involving claims for alimony, support, maintenance, pensions, medical expenses, or labor remuneration, where enforcement may not be suspended.

Article 214 For a case retried by a people's court in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, if the legally effective judgment or ruling was rendered by a court of first instance, the case shall be tried in accordance with the procedure of first instance, and the parties may appeal the judgment or ruling rendered; if the legally effective judgment or ruling was rendered by a court of second instance, the case shall be tried in accordance with the procedure of second instance, and the judgment or ruling rendered shall be legally effective; if a higher people's court brings the case up for trial in accordance with the procedure for trial supervision, the case shall be tried in accordance with the procedure of second instance, and the judgment or ruling rendered shall be legally effective.

When a people's court tries a retrial case, it shall form a new collegial panel.

Article 215 If the Supreme People's Procuratorate finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by a people's court at any level, or if a higher people's procuratorate finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by a lower people's court, falls under any of the circumstances provided for in Article 207 of this Law, or finds that a mediation document harms the national interest or the public interest, it shall file a protest.

If a local people's procuratorate at any level finds that a legally effective judgment, ruling, or mediation document rendered by a people's court at the same level falls under any of the circumstances provided for in Article 207 of this Law, or finds that a mediation document harms the national interest or the public interest, it may issue a procuratorial recommendation to the people's court at the same level and report it to the higher people's procuratorate for the record; it may also request the higher people's procuratorate to file a protest with the people's court at the same level.

People's procuratorates at all levels have the authority to issue procuratorial recommendations to the people's court at the same level concerning illegal acts by judges in trial procedures other than the procedure for trial supervision.

Article 216 Under any of the following circumstances, a party may apply to the people's procuratorate for a procuratorial recommendation or a protest:

(1) The people's court rejects the application for retrial;

(2) The people's court fails to make a ruling on the application for retrial within the prescribed time limit;

(3) The judgment or ruling rendered upon retrial contains a clear error.

The people's procuratorate shall review the party's application within three months and make a decision to issue or not to issue a procuratorial recommendation or to file or not to file a protest. The party may not apply again to the people's procuratorate for a procuratorial recommendation or a protest.

Article 217 If necessary for fulfilling the duty of legal supervision in issuing a procuratorial recommendation or filing a protest, the people's procuratorate may investigate and verify the relevant circumstances with the parties or persons not involved in the case.Article 218 For a case in which a people's procuratorate has filed a protest, the people's court that accepts the protest shall, within 30 days from the date of receiving the protest, render a ruling to retry the case. If the case falls under any of the circumstances specified in Items 1 through 5 of Article 207 of this Law, the court may refer the case to the next lower people's court for retrial, except where the case has already been retried by that lower court.

Article 219 If a people's procuratorate decides to file a protest against a judgment, ruling, or mediation statement of a people's court, it shall prepare a written protest.

Article 220 When a people's court retries a case in which a people's procuratorate has filed a protest, it shall notify the people's procuratorate to send an officer to appear in court.

### Chapter 17: Supervision Procedure

Article 221 A creditor who requests a debtor to pay a sum of money or negotiable securities may, under the following conditions, apply to the basic people's court with jurisdiction for an order for payment:

(1) There is no other debt dispute between the creditor and the debtor; and

(2) The order for payment can be served on the debtor.

The application shall specify the amount of money or negotiable securities requested and the facts and evidence on which the request is based.

Article 222 After a creditor files an application, the people's court shall, within five days, notify the creditor whether the application is accepted.

Article 223 After accepting an application, the people's court shall, upon examining the facts and evidence provided by the creditor, if the creditor-debtor relationship is clear and lawful, issue an order for payment to the debtor within 15 days from the date of acceptance; if the application is not established, the court shall render a ruling to reject it.

The debtor shall, within 15 days from the date of receiving the order for payment, discharge the debt or submit a written objection to the people's court.

If the debtor neither raises an objection nor complies with the order for payment within the period specified in the preceding paragraph, the creditor may apply to the people's court for enforcement.

Article 224 After the people's court receives a written objection from the debtor, upon examination, if the objection is established, the court shall render a ruling to terminate the supervision procedure, and the order for payment shall automatically become invalid.

If the order for payment becomes invalid, the case shall be transferred to litigation proceedings, unless the party that applied for the order for payment does not agree to initiate litigation.

### Chapter 18: Public Summons Procedure

Article 225 A holder of a negotiable instrument that may be transferred by endorsement, if the instrument is stolen, lost, or destroyed, may apply to the basic people's court at the place of payment for a public summons. Other matters for which a public summons may be applied for in accordance with legal provisions shall be governed by this Chapter.

The applicant shall submit a written application to the people's court, specifying the face amount of the instrument, the drawer, the holder, the endorser, and other main contents of the instrument, as well as the reasons and facts for the application.

Article 226 Upon deciding to accept the application, the people's court shall simultaneously notify the payer to stop payment and shall, within three days, issue a public notice urging interested parties to assert their rights. The period for the public summons shall be determined by the people's court based on the circumstances, but shall not be less than 60 days.

Article 227 Upon receiving the notice from the people's court to stop payment, the payer shall stop payment until the conclusion of the public summons procedure.

During the period of the public summons, any act of transferring rights in the instrument shall be invalid.

Article 228 An interested party shall, during the period of the public summons, file a claim with the people's court.

After receiving the claim from the interested party, the people's court shall render a ruling to terminate the public summons procedure and notify the applicant and the payer.

The applicant or the claimant may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 229 If no claim is filed, the people's court shall, based on the applicant's application, render a judgment declaring the instrument invalid. The judgment shall be publicly announced, and the payer shall be notified. From the date of the public announcement of the judgment, the applicant is entitled to request payment from the payer.

Article 230 If an interested party fails to file a claim with the people's court before the judgment due to justifiable reasons, the party may, within one year from the date of knowing or should have known the public announcement of the judgment, file a lawsuit with the people's court that rendered the judgment.

## Part Three: Enforcement Procedure

### Chapter 19: General Provisions

Article 231 Legally effective civil judgments and rulings, as well as the property portions of criminal judgments and rulings, shall be enforced by the court of first instance or by the people's court at the same level as the court of first instance at the location of the property to be enforced.

Other legal documents that are to be enforced by a people's court in accordance with the law shall be enforced by the people's court at the domicile of the person subject to enforcement or at the location of the property to be enforced.

Article 232 If a party or an interested party believes that an enforcement action violates legal provisions, the party may submit a written objection to the people's court responsible for the enforcement. Upon receiving the written objection, the people's court shall review it within 15 days. If the reasons are established, the court shall render a ruling to revoke or correct the action; if the reasons are not established, the court shall render a ruling to reject the objection. If a party or an interested party is dissatisfied with the ruling, the party may apply for reconsideration to the next higher people's court within 10 days from the date of service of the ruling.

Article 233 If a people's court fails to enforce a case within six months from the date of receiving the application for enforcement, the applicant for enforcement may apply to the next higher people's court for enforcement. Upon review, the next higher people's court may order the original court to enforce within a specified period, or may decide to enforce the case itself or instruct another people's court to enforce it.

Article 234 During the enforcement process, if a person not involved in the case raises a written objection to the subject matter of enforcement, the people's court shall review it within 15 days from the date of receiving the written objection. If the reasons are established, the court shall render a ruling to suspend enforcement of that subject matter; if the reasons are not established, the court shall render a ruling to reject the objection. If the person not involved in the case or a party is dissatisfied with the ruling and believes that the original judgment or ruling is erroneous, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the trial supervision procedure; if the matter is unrelated to the original judgment or ruling, the party may file a lawsuit with the people's court within 15 days from the date of service of the ruling.

Article 235 Enforcement work shall be carried out by enforcement officers.

When taking compulsory enforcement measures, the enforcement officer shall present identification. After enforcement is completed, a record of the enforcement shall be made, which shall be signed or sealed by the relevant persons present.

A people's court may establish enforcement agencies as needed.

Article 236 If the person subject to enforcement or the property to be enforced is located in another locality, the court may entrust the local people's court with the enforcement. The entrusted people's court shall, within 15 days from the date of receiving the entrustment letter, commence enforcement and shall not refuse. After enforcement is completed, the entrusted court shall promptly notify the entrusting court of the enforcement results in writing; if enforcement is not completed within 30 days, the entrusted court shall also inform the entrusting court of the enforcement status in writing.

If the entrusted people's court fails to enforce within 15 days from the date of receiving the entrustment letter, the entrusting court may request the next higher court of the entrusted court to instruct the entrusted court to enforce.

Article 237 During enforcement, if the parties reach a settlement agreement voluntarily, the enforcement officer shall record the contents of the agreement in the record, which shall be signed or sealed by both parties.

If the applicant for enforcement was induced by fraud or coercion to reach a settlement agreement with the person subject to enforcement, or if a party fails to perform the settlement agreement, the people's court may, upon the party's application, resume enforcement of the original legally effective legal document.

Article 238 During enforcement, if the person subject to enforcement provides a guarantee to the people's court and the applicant for enforcement agrees, the people's court may decide to suspend enforcement and set a period for the suspension. If the person subject to enforcement still fails to perform after the expiration of the period, the people's court has the right to enforce the guaranteed property of the person subject to enforcement or the property of the guarantor.

Article 239 If the person subject to enforcement, who is a natural person, dies, the debt shall be repaid from the estate. If the person subject to enforcement, who is a legal person or other organization, is terminated, the successor of its rights and obligations shall perform the obligations.

Article 240 If, after enforcement is completed, the judgment, ruling, or other legal document on which the enforcement was based is found to be erroneous and is revoked by the people's court, the court shall render a ruling ordering the person who obtained the property to return it; if the person refuses to return it, compulsory enforcement shall be applied.

Article 241 The enforcement of mediation statements made by a people's court shall be governed by the provisions of this Part.

Article 242 A people's procuratorate has the right to exercise legal supervision over civil enforcement activities.

### Chapter 20: Application for and Transfer of Enforcement

Article 243 The parties must perform legally effective civil judgments and rulings. If one party refuses to perform, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement, or the trial judge may transfer the case to the enforcement officer for enforcement.

The parties must perform mediation statements and other legal documents that are to be enforced by a people's court. If one party refuses to perform, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement.

Article 244 If a party fails to perform an award made by an arbitration institution established in accordance with the law, the other party may apply to the competent people's court for enforcement. The people's court that receives the application shall enforce the award.

If the person against whom enforcement is sought provides evidence that the arbitration award falls under any of the following circumstances, and upon review by a collegial panel of the people's court, the court shall render a ruling not to enforce the award:

(1) The parties did not include an arbitration clause in the contract or did not subsequently reach a written arbitration agreement;

(2) The matters decided in the award are not within the scope of the arbitration agreement or the arbitration institution lacked jurisdiction;

(3) The composition of the arbitral tribunal or the arbitration procedure violated legal procedures;

(4) The evidence on which the award was based was forged;

(5) The other party concealed evidence from the arbitration institution that was sufficient to affect a fair award; or

(6) The arbitrator engaged in corruption, bribery, favoritism, or perversion of the law in arbitrating the case.

If the people's court determines that enforcing the award would violate public interest, it shall render a ruling not to enforce the award.

The ruling shall be served on both parties and the arbitration institution.

If the arbitration award is ruled not to be enforced by the people's court, the parties may reapply for arbitration based on the written arbitration agreement reached between them, or may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 245 If a party fails to perform a notarized document of creditor's rights that has been given compulsory enforcement effect in accordance with the law, the other party may apply to the competent people's court for enforcement. The people's court that receives the application shall enforce the document.

If the notarized document of creditor's rights is indeed erroneous, the people's court shall render a ruling not to enforce it and shall serve the ruling on both parties and the notarial office.

Article 246 The period for applying for enforcement is two years. The suspension or interruption of the statute of limitations for applying for enforcement shall be governed by the legal provisions on the suspension or interruption of the statute of limitations for litigation.

The period specified in the preceding paragraph shall be calculated from the last day of the performance period specified in the legal document; if the legal document specifies performance in installments, it shall be calculated from the last day of the final installment period; if the legal document does not specify a performance period, it shall be calculated from the date the legal document takes effect.

Article 247 Upon receiving an application for enforcement or a transfer of enforcement, the enforcement officer shall issue an enforcement notice to the person subject to enforcement and may immediately take compulsory enforcement measures.

### Chapter 21: Enforcement Measures

Article 248 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in the legal document in accordance with the enforcement notice, the person shall report the current property status and the property status for the year preceding the date of receiving the enforcement notice. If the person subject to enforcement refuses to report or makes a false report, the people's court may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine or detention on the person subject to enforcement, the legal representative, or the principal responsible person or directly responsible person of the relevant entity.

Article 249 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in the legal document in accordance with the enforcement notice, the people's court has the right to inquire with relevant entities about the property status of the person subject to enforcement, including deposits, bonds, stocks, fund shares, etc. The people's court has the right to seize, freeze, transfer, or sell the property of the person subject to enforcement under different circumstances. The property inquired, seized, frozen, transferred, or sold by the people's court shall not exceed the scope of the obligations the person subject to enforcement should perform.

When deciding to seize, freeze, transfer, or sell property, the people's court shall render a ruling and issue a notice of assistance in enforcement, and the relevant entities must comply.

Article 250 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in the legal document in accordance with the enforcement notice, the people's court has the right to withhold or deduct the portion of the income of the person subject to enforcement that should be used to perform the obligations. However, the necessary living expenses for the person subject to enforcement and the dependents supported by the person shall be reserved.

When withholding or deducting income, the people's court shall render a ruling and issue a notice of assistance in enforcement. The entity where the person subject to enforcement works, banks, credit cooperatives, and other entities with savings business must comply.

Article 251 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in the legal document in accordance with the enforcement notice, the people's court has the right to seal up, seize, freeze, auction, or sell the portion of the property of the person subject to enforcement that should be used to perform the obligations. However, the daily necessities of the person subject to enforcement and the dependents supported by the person shall be reserved.

When taking the measures in the preceding paragraph, the people's court shall render a ruling.

Article 252 When a people's court seals up or seizes property, if the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, the person or the person's adult family members shall be notified to be present; if the person subject to enforcement is a legal person or other organization, the legal representative or principal responsible person shall be notified to be present. If they refuse to be present, it shall not affect the enforcement. If the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, the person's work unit or the grassroots organization at the location of the property shall send personnel to participate.

For the sealed-up or seized property, the enforcement officer must prepare an inventory, which shall be signed or sealed by the persons present, and one copy shall be given to the person subject to enforcement. If the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, a copy may also be given to the person's adult family members.

Article 253 The enforcement officer may designate the person subject to enforcement to be responsible for the custody of the sealed-up property. Any loss caused by the fault of the person subject to enforcement shall be borne by that person.

Article 254 After property is sealed up or seized, the enforcement officer shall order the person subject to enforcement to perform the obligations specified in the legal document within a specified period. If the person subject to enforcement fails to perform within the period, the people's court shall auction the sealed-up or seized property; if the property is not suitable for auction or both parties agree not to auction, the people's court may entrust a relevant entity to sell it or sell it itself. Goods that are prohibited from free trading by the state shall be purchased by the relevant entity at the price prescribed by the state.

Article 255 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in the legal document and conceals property, the people's court has the right to issue a search warrant to search the person subject to enforcement, the person's domicile, or the place where the property is concealed.

When taking the measures in the preceding paragraph, the search warrant shall be signed by the president of the court.

Article 256 For property or negotiable instruments designated to be delivered in the legal document, the enforcement officer shall summon both parties to deliver them in person, or the enforcement officer shall transfer them, and the recipient shall sign for them.

If a relevant entity holds such property or negotiable instruments, it shall transfer them in accordance with the people's court's notice of assistance in enforcement, and the recipient shall sign for them.

If a relevant natural person holds such property or negotiable instruments, the people's court shall notify the person to hand them over. If the person refuses to hand them over, compulsory enforcement shall be applied.

Article 257 For compulsory eviction from a house or compulsory withdrawal from land, the president of the court shall issue a public notice ordering the person subject to enforcement to perform within a specified period. If the person subject to enforcement fails to perform within the period, the enforcement officer shall carry out compulsory enforcement.

During compulsory enforcement, if the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, the person or the person's adult family members shall be notified to be present; if the person subject to enforcement is a legal person or other organization, the legal representative or principal responsible person shall be notified to be present. If they refuse to be present, it shall not affect the enforcement. If the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, the person's work unit or the grassroots organization at the location of the house or land shall send personnel to participate. The enforcement officer shall record the circumstances of the compulsory enforcement in the record, which shall be signed or sealed by the persons present.

Property removed during compulsory eviction from a house shall be transported by personnel of the people's court to a designated location and handed over to the person subject to enforcement. If the person subject to enforcement is a natural person, it may also be handed over to the person's adult family members. Any loss caused by refusal to accept the property shall be borne by the person subject to enforcement.

Article 258 During enforcement, if it is necessary to handle the transfer of title certificates for property rights, the people's court may issue a notice of assistance in enforcement to the relevant entity, and the relevant entity must comply.

Article 259 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform an act specified in a judgment, ruling, or other legal document in accordance with the enforcement notice, the people's court may enforce it compulsorily or entrust a relevant entity or other person to complete it, with the costs borne by the person subject to enforcement.

Article 260 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligation to pay money within the period specified in a judgment, ruling, or other legal document, the person shall pay double the interest on the debt for the period of delayed performance. If the person subject to enforcement fails to perform other obligations within the period specified in a judgment, ruling, or other legal document, the person shall pay a surcharge for delayed performance.

Article 261 After the people's court has taken the enforcement measures specified in Articles 249, 250, and 251 of this Law, if the person subject to enforcement is still unable to repay the debt, the person shall continue to perform the obligations. If the creditor discovers that the person subject to enforcement has other property, the creditor may request the people's court to enforce it at any time.

Article 262 If a person subject to enforcement fails to perform the obligations specified in a legal document, the people's court may take measures against the person or notify relevant entities to assist in taking measures such as restricting departure, recording the information in the credit reporting system, publicizing the information on the failure to perform obligations through the media, and other measures provided by law.

### Chapter 22: Suspension and Termination of Enforcement

Article 263 A people's court shall render a ruling to suspend enforcement under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The applicant indicates that enforcement may be postponed;

(2) A person not involved in the case raises a well-founded objection to the subject matter of enforcement;

(3) A natural person who is a party dies, and it is necessary to wait for an heir to inherit the rights or assume the obligations;

(4) A legal person or other organization that is a party is terminated, and the successor of rights and obligations has not yet been determined; or

(5) Other circumstances under which the people's court deems enforcement should be suspended.

When the circumstances for suspension disappear, enforcement shall resume.

Article 264 A people's court shall render a ruling to terminate enforcement under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The applicant withdraws the application;

(2) The legal document on which the enforcement is based is revoked;

(3) The person subject to enforcement, who is a natural person, dies, leaving no estate for enforcement and no one to assume the obligations;

(4) The right holder in a case claiming alimony, support, or maintenance dies;

(5) The person subject to enforcement, who is a natural person, is unable to repay the loan due to financial hardship, has no source of income, and has lost the ability to work; or

(6) Other circumstances under which the people's court deems enforcement should be terminated.

Article 265 A ruling to suspend or terminate enforcement shall take effect immediately upon service on the parties.

## Part Four: Special Provisions for Civil Procedure Involving Foreign Elements

### Chapter 23: General Principles

Article 266 Civil proceedings involving foreign elements conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the provisions of this Part. Matters not covered in this Part shall be governed by other relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 267 If an international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 268 Civil proceedings brought against a foreign national, foreign organization, or international organization that enjoys diplomatic privileges and immunities shall be handled in accordance with the relevant laws of the People's Republic of China and the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China.Article 269: In the trial of foreign-related civil cases, the people's courts shall use the spoken and written languages commonly used in the People's Republic of China. If a party requests translation, it may be provided, and the costs shall be borne by that party.

Article 270: When foreigners, stateless persons, foreign enterprises, or organizations need to appoint a lawyer to represent them in lawsuits filed or defended in a people's court, they must appoint a lawyer from the People's Republic of China.

Article 271: If a foreigner, stateless person, foreign enterprise, or organization without a domicile within the territory of the People's Republic of China appoints a lawyer from the People's Republic of China or another person to represent them in litigation, the power of attorney sent or submitted from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be notarized by a notary public in the country of domicile and certified by the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China in that country, or shall comply with the certification procedures stipulated in the relevant treaties concluded between the People's Republic of China and that country, before it becomes effective.

### Chapter 24: Jurisdiction

Article 272: For a lawsuit brought against a defendant without a domicile within the territory of the People's Republic of China arising from a contract dispute or other disputes over property rights and interests, if the contract is signed or performed within the territory of the People's Republic of China, or the subject matter of the lawsuit is located within the territory of the People's Republic of China, or the defendant has seizable property within the territory of the People's Republic of China, or the defendant has a representative office within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the people's court at the place where the contract is signed, the place where the contract is performed, the location of the subject matter of the lawsuit, the location of the seizable property, the place where the tortious act occurred, or the domicile of the representative office may have jurisdiction.

Article 273: Lawsuits arising from disputes over contracts for Sino-foreign equity joint ventures, Sino-foreign cooperative joint ventures, or Sino-foreign cooperative exploration and development of natural resources performed within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's courts of the People's Republic of China.

### Chapter 25: Service and Time Periods

Article 274: A people's court may serve litigation documents on a party without a domicile within the territory of the People's Republic of China by the following methods:

(1) Service in the manner stipulated in the international treaties concluded or acceded to by the country where the recipient is domiciled and the People's Republic of China;
(2) Service through diplomatic channels;
(3) For a recipient with the nationality of the People's Republic of China, service may be entrusted to the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China in the country where the recipient is domiciled;
(4) Service on the litigation agent authorized by the recipient to accept service;
(5) Service on the representative office established by the recipient within the territory of the People's Republic of China, or on the branch or business agent authorized to accept service;
(6) If the law of the country where the recipient is domiciled permits service by mail, service may be made by mail. If the receipt of service is not returned within three months from the date of mailing, but it is sufficiently established based on the circumstances that the service has been effected, the service shall be deemed to have been effected on the date of expiry of the period;
(7) Service by methods such as fax or email that can confirm receipt by the recipient;
(8) If service cannot be made by the above methods, service by public announcement. Service shall be deemed to have been effected three months after the date of the public announcement.

Article 275: If the defendant does not have a domicile within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the people's court shall serve a copy of the statement of claim on the defendant and notify the defendant to submit a statement of defense within thirty days after receiving the copy of the statement of claim. Whether an extension requested by the defendant is granted shall be decided by the people's court.

Article 276: A party without a domicile within the territory of the People's Republic of China who disagrees with a judgment or ruling of a people's court of first instance has the right to file an appeal within thirty days from the date the judgment or ruling is served. The appellee shall submit a statement of defense within thirty days after receiving a copy of the appeal petition. If a party cannot file an appeal or submit a statement of defense within the statutory period and requests an extension, whether the extension is granted shall be decided by the people's court.

Article 277: The time periods for a people's court to try foreign-related civil cases shall not be subject to the restrictions of Articles 152 and 183 of this Law.

### Chapter 26: Arbitration

Article 278: For disputes arising from foreign-related economic, trade, transportation, and maritime matters, if the parties have included an arbitration clause in the contract or subsequently reached a written arbitration agreement to submit the dispute to a foreign-related arbitration institution of the People's Republic of China or another arbitration institution for arbitration, the parties shall not file a lawsuit in a people's court.

If the parties have not included an arbitration clause in the contract or subsequently reached a written arbitration agreement, they may file a lawsuit in a people's court.

Article 279: When a party applies for preservation measures, the foreign-related arbitration institution of the People's Republic of China shall submit the party's application to the intermediate people's court at the domicile of the respondent or the location of the property for a ruling.

Article 280: Once an award has been made by a foreign-related arbitration institution of the People's Republic of China, the parties shall not file a lawsuit in a people's court. If one party fails to comply with the arbitration award, the other party may apply to the intermediate people's court at the domicile of the respondent or the location of the property for enforcement.

Article 281: If a party against whom an award made by a foreign-related arbitration institution of the People's Republic of China is invoked provides evidence proving that the arbitration award falls under any of the following circumstances, the people's court shall, after review by a collegial panel, rule to refuse enforcement:

(1) The parties did not include an arbitration clause in the contract or subsequently reach a written arbitration agreement;
(2) The party against whom the award is invoked was not given notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or the arbitration proceedings, or was unable to present its case due to reasons not attributable to that party;
(3) The composition of the arbitral tribunal or the arbitration procedure was not in accordance with the arbitration rules;
(4) The matters decided in the award are beyond the scope of the arbitration agreement or the arbitral institution has no authority to arbitrate.

If the people's court determines that the enforcement of the award would violate the public interest of society, it shall rule to refuse enforcement.

Article 282: If an arbitration award is ruled by a people's court to be refused enforcement, the parties may reapply for arbitration based on a written arbitration agreement reached between them, or they may file a lawsuit in a people's court.

### Chapter 27: Judicial Assistance

Article 283: In accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity, the people's courts and foreign courts may request each other to serve documents, investigate and collect evidence, and perform other litigation acts.

If the matters requested by a foreign court for assistance are detrimental to the sovereignty, security, or public interest of the People's Republic of China, the people's court shall not execute them.

Article 284: Requests for and provision of judicial assistance shall be made through the channels stipulated in the international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China; in the absence of treaty relations, they shall be made through diplomatic channels.

The embassy or consulate of a foreign country in the People's Republic of China may serve documents on and investigate and collect evidence from its own nationals, provided that it does not violate the laws of the People's Republic of China and does not take coercive measures.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, no foreign organ or individual may serve documents or investigate and collect evidence within the territory of the People's Republic of China without the permission of the competent authorities of the People's Republic of China.

Article 285: The request letter and its accompanying documents submitted by a foreign court requesting judicial assistance from a people's court shall be accompanied by a Chinese translation or a text in another language as stipulated by international treaties.

The request letter and its accompanying documents submitted by a people's court requesting judicial assistance from a foreign court shall be accompanied by a translation in the language of that country or a text in another language as stipulated by international treaties.

Article 286: The people's court shall provide judicial assistance in accordance with the procedures stipulated by the laws of the People's Republic of China. If a foreign court requests the use of a special method, it may also be carried out in the special method requested, provided that the requested special method does not violate the laws of the People's Republic of China.

Article 287: If a judgment or ruling made by a people's court that has become legally effective requires enforcement, and the person subject to enforcement or its property is not located within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the party requesting enforcement may directly apply to a foreign court with jurisdiction for recognition and enforcement, or the people's court may, in accordance with the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity, request the foreign court to recognize and enforce it.

If an arbitration award made by a foreign-related arbitration institution of the People's Republic of China that has become legally effective requires enforcement, and the person subject to enforcement or its property is not located within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the party requesting enforcement shall directly apply to a foreign court with jurisdiction for recognition and enforcement.

Article 288: If a judgment or ruling made by a foreign court that has become legally effective requires recognition and enforcement by a people's court of the People's Republic of China, the party may directly apply to the intermediate people's court of the People's Republic of China with jurisdiction for recognition and enforcement, or the foreign court may, in accordance with the provisions of international treaties concluded or acceded to by that country and the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity, request the people's court to recognize and enforce it.

Article 289: After reviewing an application or request for recognition and enforcement of a judgment or ruling made by a foreign court that has become legally effective, in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity, if the people's court finds that it does not violate the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China or the national sovereignty, security, and public interest of society, it shall rule to recognize its validity. If enforcement is required, it shall issue an enforcement order and enforce it in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law. If it violates the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China or the national sovereignty, security, and public interest of society, it shall not be recognized or enforced.

Article 290: If an award made by a foreign arbitration institution requires recognition and enforcement by a people's court of the People's Republic of China, the party shall directly apply to the intermediate people's court at the domicile of the person subject to enforcement or the location of its property. The people's court shall handle the matter in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or on the principle of reciprocity.

Article 291: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation, and the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China (for Trial Implementation) shall be simultaneously abolished.
