# Arbitration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Ninth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 31, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Twenty-Ninth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated to ensure fair and timely arbitration of economic disputes, protect the legitimate rights and interests of the parties, and safeguard the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 Contractual disputes and other disputes over property rights and interests between citizens, legal persons, and other organizations with equal status may be submitted to arbitration.

Article 3 The following disputes may not be submitted to arbitration:

(1) disputes over marriage, adoption, guardianship, maintenance, or inheritance;

(2) administrative disputes that shall be handled by administrative authorities in accordance with the law.

Article 4 The parties' voluntary agreement to submit their dispute to arbitration shall be evidenced by an arbitration agreement. If a party applies for arbitration without an arbitration agreement, the arbitration commission shall not accept the application.

Article 5 If the parties have concluded an arbitration agreement and one party institutes an action in a people's court, the people's court shall not accept the case, unless the arbitration agreement is invalid.

Article 6 The arbitration commission shall be selected by the parties by agreement.

Arbitration is not subject to hierarchical or territorial jurisdiction.

Article 7 Disputes shall be resolved fairly and reasonably on the basis of facts and in accordance with the law.

Article 8 Arbitration shall be conducted independently in accordance with the law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 9 Arbitration shall be conducted under the system of a single and final award. After an award is made, if a party applies for arbitration again in respect of the same dispute or institutes an action in a people's court, the arbitration commission or the people's court shall not accept the case.

If an award is set aside or its enforcement is refused by a people's court in accordance with the law, the parties may apply for arbitration again in respect of the same dispute pursuant to a new arbitration agreement reached between them, or may institute an action in a people's court.

## Chapter II Arbitration Commissions and the Arbitration Association

Article 10 Arbitration commissions may be established in municipalities directly under the Central Government, in cities that are the seats of the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions, or in other cities divided into districts as needed. They shall not be established at each level of administrative divisions.

Arbitration commissions shall be organized by the people's governments of the cities specified in the preceding paragraph, in conjunction with the relevant departments and chambers of commerce.

The establishment of an arbitration commission shall be registered with the judicial administrative department of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 11 An arbitration commission shall meet the following conditions:

(1) have its own name, domicile, and charter;

(2) have the necessary property;

(3) have the members that constitute the commission;

(4) have appointed arbitrators.

The charter of an arbitration commission shall be formulated in accordance with this Law.

Article 12 An arbitration commission shall be composed of one chairperson, two to four vice-chairpersons, and seven to eleven members.

The chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members of an arbitration commission shall be persons with expertise in law, economics, and trade, as well as persons with practical work experience. At least two-thirds of the members of an arbitration commission shall be experts in law, economics, or trade.

Article 13 An arbitration commission shall appoint arbitrators from among persons who are impartial and upright.

An arbitrator shall meet one of the following conditions:

(1) having passed the national uniform legal professional qualification examination and obtained legal professional qualifications, and having been engaged in arbitration work for at least eight years;

(2) having been engaged in legal practice as a lawyer for at least eight years;

(3) having served as a judge for at least eight years;

(4) having been engaged in legal research or teaching and holding a senior professional title;

(5) having legal knowledge, being engaged in professional work such as economics or trade, and holding a senior professional title or having an equivalent professional level.

Arbitration commissions shall maintain a register of arbitrators categorized by specialty.

Article 14 Arbitration commissions are independent of administrative organs and have no subordinate relationship with administrative organs. There is no subordinate relationship between arbitration commissions either.

Article 15 The China Arbitration Association is a social organization with legal person status. Arbitration commissions are members of the China Arbitration Association. The charter of the China Arbitration Association shall be formulated by its national congress of members.

The China Arbitration Association is a self-regulatory organization of arbitration commissions and shall, in accordance with its charter, supervise the disciplinary conduct of arbitration commissions and their members and arbitrators.

The China Arbitration Association shall formulate arbitration rules in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law.

## Chapter III Arbitration Agreement

Article 16 An arbitration agreement includes an arbitration clause contained in a contract and any other written agreement to submit a dispute to arbitration, concluded before or after the dispute arises.

An arbitration agreement shall contain the following:

(1) an expression of intention to request arbitration;

(2) matters to be submitted to arbitration;

(3) a designated arbitration commission.

Article 17 An arbitration agreement shall be invalid under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the matters agreed upon for arbitration exceed the scope of arbitration prescribed by law;

(2) the arbitration agreement is concluded by a person without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity;

(3) one party coerces the other party into concluding the arbitration agreement.

Article 18 If the arbitration agreement does not specify or clearly specify the matters for arbitration or the arbitration commission, the parties may reach a supplementary agreement. If they fail to reach a supplementary agreement, the arbitration agreement shall be invalid.

Article 19 An arbitration agreement shall exist independently. The amendment, rescission, termination, or invalidity of the contract shall not affect the validity of the arbitration agreement.

The arbitral tribunal has the power to determine the validity of a contract.

Article 20 If a party challenges the validity of the arbitration agreement, it may request the arbitration commission to make a decision or request the people's court to make a ruling. If one party requests the arbitration commission to make a decision and the other party requests the people's court to make a ruling, the people's court shall make the ruling.

A party's challenge to the validity of the arbitration agreement shall be raised before the first hearing of the arbitral tribunal.

## Chapter IV Arbitration Procedure

### Section 1 Application and Acceptance

Article 21 A party's application for arbitration shall meet the following conditions:

(1) there is an arbitration agreement;

(2) there is a specific arbitration claim and facts and grounds;

(3) the matter falls within the scope of acceptance of the arbitration commission.

Article 22 A party applying for arbitration shall submit the arbitration agreement, the written application for arbitration, and copies thereof to the arbitration commission.

Article 23 The written application for arbitration shall specify the following:

(1) the name, gender, age, occupation, work unit, and domicile of the party, or the name, domicile, and the name and position of the legal representative or principal responsible person of the legal person or other organization;

(2) the arbitration claim and the facts and grounds on which it is based;

(3) the evidence and the source of the evidence, and the names and domiciles of witnesses.

Article 24 Within five days from the date of receiving the written application for arbitration, if the arbitration commission considers that the application meets the conditions for acceptance, it shall accept the application and notify the parties; if it considers that the application does not meet the conditions for acceptance, it shall notify the party in writing of its rejection and state the reasons.

Article 25 After accepting an application for arbitration, the arbitration commission shall deliver the arbitration rules and the register of arbitrators to the applicant within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules, and shall deliver a copy of the written application for arbitration, the arbitration rules, and the register of arbitrators to the respondent.

After receiving the copy of the written application for arbitration, the respondent shall submit a written defense to the arbitration commission within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules. After receiving the written defense, the arbitration commission shall deliver a copy thereof to the applicant within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules. The failure of the respondent to submit a written defense shall not affect the conduct of the arbitration proceedings.

Article 26 If the parties have concluded an arbitration agreement and one party institutes an action in a people's court without stating the existence of the arbitration agreement, and after the people's court accepts the case, the other party submits the arbitration agreement before the first hearing, the people's court shall dismiss the action, unless the arbitration agreement is invalid. If the other party does not raise an objection to the people's court's acceptance of the case before the first hearing, it shall be deemed to have waived the arbitration agreement, and the people's court shall continue the proceedings.

Article 27 The applicant may waive or amend its arbitration claim. The respondent may accept or reject the arbitration claim and has the right to make a counterclaim.

Article 28 If, due to the conduct of the other party or for other reasons, it may become impossible or difficult to enforce an award, a party may apply for property preservation.

If a party applies for property preservation, the arbitration commission shall submit the party's application to the people's court in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law.

If the application is erroneous, the applicant shall compensate the respondent for any losses suffered as a result of the property preservation.

Article 29 A party or its legal representative may entrust a lawyer or other agent to conduct arbitration activities. To entrust a lawyer or other agent to conduct arbitration activities, a power of attorney shall be submitted to the arbitration commission.

### Section 2 Composition of the Arbitral Tribunal

Article 30 An arbitral tribunal may be composed of three arbitrators or a single arbitrator. If it is composed of three arbitrators, a presiding arbitrator shall be appointed.

Article 31 If the parties agree that the arbitral tribunal shall be composed of three arbitrators, each party shall select or entrust the chairperson of the arbitration commission to appoint one arbitrator. The third arbitrator shall be selected jointly by the parties or appointed jointly by the parties through entrusting the chairperson of the arbitration commission. The third arbitrator shall be the presiding arbitrator.

If the parties agree that the arbitral tribunal shall be composed of a single arbitrator, the arbitrator shall be selected jointly by the parties or appointed jointly by the parties through entrusting the chairperson of the arbitration commission.

Article 32 If the parties fail to agree on the method of composition of the arbitral tribunal or to select the arbitrators within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules, the chairperson of the arbitration commission shall make the appointment.

Article 33 After the arbitral tribunal is formed, the arbitration commission shall notify the parties in writing of the composition of the arbitral tribunal.

Article 34 An arbitrator must withdraw from the case under any of the following circumstances, and the parties also have the right to apply for the arbitrator's withdrawal:

(1) being a party to the case or a close relative of a party or an agent;

(2) having a personal interest in the case;

(3) having another relationship with a party or an agent in the case that may affect fair arbitration;

(4) meeting a party or an agent in private, or accepting an invitation for a meal or a gift from a party or an agent.

Article 35 A party applying for the withdrawal of an arbitrator shall state the reasons and submit the application before the first hearing. If the grounds for withdrawal become known after the first hearing, the application may be submitted before the conclusion of the final hearing.

Article 36 The decision on whether an arbitrator should withdraw shall be made by the chairperson of the arbitration commission. If the chairperson of the arbitration commission serves as an arbitrator, the decision shall be made collectively by the arbitration commission.

Article 37 If an arbitrator cannot perform its duties due to withdrawal or for other reasons, a substitute arbitrator shall be selected or appointed in accordance with this Law.

After a substitute arbitrator is selected or appointed due to withdrawal, the parties may request that the arbitration proceedings already conducted be recommenced. Whether to permit such recommencement shall be decided by the arbitral tribunal. The arbitral tribunal may also decide on its own whether the arbitration proceedings already conducted shall be recommenced.

Article 38 If an arbitrator is found to have engaged in conduct specified in Article 34(4) of this Law, and the circumstances are serious, or if an arbitrator is found to have engaged in conduct specified in Article 58(6) of this Law, the arbitrator shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law, and the arbitration commission shall remove the arbitrator from its register.

### Section 3 Hearing and Award

Article 39 Arbitration shall be conducted by means of a hearing. If the parties agree not to hold a hearing, the arbitral tribunal may make an award on the basis of the written application for arbitration, the written defense, and other materials.

Article 40 Arbitration shall not be conducted in public. If the parties agree to conduct arbitration in public, it may be conducted in public, except where state secrets are involved.

Article 41 The arbitration commission shall notify the parties of the date of the hearing within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules. If a party has a legitimate reason, it may request a postponement of the hearing within the time limit prescribed by the arbitration rules. Whether to postpone the hearing shall be decided by the arbitral tribunal.

Article 42 If the applicant fails to appear before the arbitral tribunal without a legitimate reason after being notified in writing, or leaves the hearing without the permission of the arbitral tribunal, the application for arbitration may be deemed to have been withdrawn.

If the respondent fails to appear before the arbitral tribunal without a legitimate reason after being notified in writing, or leaves the hearing without the permission of the arbitral tribunal, an award may be made by default.

Article 43 The parties shall provide evidence in support of their own claims.

The arbitral tribunal may collect evidence on its own if it considers it necessary.

Article 44 If the arbitral tribunal considers that a specialized issue requires appraisal, it may refer the issue to an appraisal institution agreed upon by the parties or to an appraisal institution designated by the arbitral tribunal.

At the request of a party or as required by the arbitral tribunal, the appraisal institution shall send its appraiser to attend the hearing. With the permission of the arbitral tribunal, the parties may question the appraiser.

Article 45 Evidence shall be presented at the hearing, and the parties may examine the evidence.

Article 46 If evidence may be lost or become difficult to obtain later, a party may apply for preservation of the evidence. If a party applies for preservation of evidence, the arbitration commission shall submit the party's application to the basic people's court at the place where the evidence is located.

Article 47 The parties shall have the right to debate during the arbitration proceedings. At the conclusion of the debate, the presiding arbitrator or the sole arbitrator shall solicit the final opinions of the parties.

Article 48 The arbitral tribunal shall record the proceedings of the hearing in writing. If a party or other participant in the arbitration considers that there are omissions or errors in the record of its statements, it has the right to apply for a correction. If the correction is not made, the application shall be recorded.

The record shall be signed or sealed by the arbitrators, the recorder, the parties, and other participants in the arbitration.

Article 49 After applying for arbitration, the parties may settle the dispute on their own. If a settlement agreement is reached, the parties may request the arbitral tribunal to make an award based on the settlement agreement, or may withdraw the application for arbitration.

Article 50 If a party repudiates a settlement agreement after withdrawing the application for arbitration, it may apply for arbitration again pursuant to the arbitration agreement.

Article 51 Before making an award, the arbitral tribunal may attempt mediation. If the parties voluntarily agree to mediation, the arbitral tribunal shall mediate. If mediation is unsuccessful, an award shall be made promptly.

If a mediation agreement is reached, the arbitral tribunal shall prepare a mediation statement or make an award based on the terms of the agreement. The mediation statement and the award shall have equal legal effect.

Article 52 The mediation statement shall specify the arbitration claim and the terms of the agreement reached by the parties. The mediation statement shall be signed by the arbitrators, sealed by the arbitration commission, and served on the parties.

The mediation statement shall become legally effective after it is signed and received by both parties.

If a party repudiates the mediation statement before it is signed and received, the arbitral tribunal shall promptly make an award.

Article 53 An award shall be made in accordance with the opinion of the majority of the arbitrators. The dissenting opinion of a minority of arbitrators may be recorded in the record. If the arbitral tribunal cannot reach a majority opinion, the award shall be made in accordance with the opinion of the presiding arbitrator.

Article 54 The award shall specify the arbitration claim, the facts of the dispute, the reasons for the award, the result of the award, the allocation of arbitration costs, and the date of the award. If the parties agree not to specify the facts of the dispute and the reasons for the award in the award, such specification may be omitted. The award shall be signed by the arbitrators and sealed by the arbitration commission. An arbitrator who holds a dissenting opinion may sign or not sign the award.

Article 55 When arbitrating a dispute, if a part of the facts has already become clear, the arbitral tribunal may make an interim award on that part.

Article 56 If there are typographical or calculation errors in the award, or if matters that have been decided by the arbitral tribunal are omitted from the award, the arbitral tribunal shall make corrections. Within thirty days from the date of receiving the award, a party may request the arbitral tribunal to make corrections.

Article 57 An award shall become legally effective from the date it is made.

## Chapter V Application for Setting Aside an Award

Article 58 If a party provides evidence that an award falls under any of the following circumstances, it may apply to the intermediate people's court at the place where the arbitration commission is located to set aside the award:

(1) there is no arbitration agreement;

(2) the matters decided in the award exceed the scope of the arbitration agreement or are beyond the authority of the arbitration commission;

(3) the composition of the arbitral tribunal or the arbitration procedure was not in accordance with statutory procedures;

(4) the evidence on which the award is based was forged;

(5) the other party concealed evidence sufficient to affect the fair award;

(6) the arbitrator engaged in extortion or bribery, committed malpractice for personal gain, or perverted the law in making the award.

If the people's court, after forming a collegial panel to examine and verify the case, finds that the award falls under any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall rule to set aside the award.

If the people's court determines that the award violates the public interest, it shall rule to set aside the award.

Article 59 A party applying to set aside an award shall submit the application within six months from the date of receiving the award.

Article 60 The people's court shall make a ruling within two months from the date of accepting the application for setting aside the award, either setting aside the award or dismissing the application.

Article 61 After accepting an application for setting aside an award, if the people's court considers that the case may be re-arbitrated by the arbitral tribunal, it shall notify the arbitral tribunal to re-arbitrate within a specified time limit and shall rule to suspend the setting-aside procedure. If the arbitral tribunal refuses to re-arbitrate, the people's court shall rule to resume the setting-aside procedure.

## Chapter VI Enforcement

Article 62 The parties shall perform the award. If one party fails to perform the award, the other party may apply to the people's court for enforcement in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law. The people's court to which the application is made shall enforce the award.

Article 63 If the respondent provides evidence that the award falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 213(2) of the Civil Procedure Law, the people's court, after forming a collegial panel to examine and verify the case, shall rule to refuse enforcement.

Article 64 If one party applies for enforcement of an award and the other party applies for setting aside the award, the people's court shall rule to suspend the enforcement.

If the people's court rules to set aside the award, it shall rule to terminate the enforcement. If the application for setting aside the award is dismissed by a ruling, the people's court shall rule to resume the enforcement.

## Chapter VII Special Provisions on Foreign-Related Arbitration

Article 65 The arbitration of disputes arising from foreign-related economic and trade relations, transportation, and maritime matters shall be governed by the provisions of this Chapter. Matters not covered in this Chapter shall be governed by the other relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 66 A foreign-related arbitration commission may be organized and established by the China Chamber of International Commerce.

A foreign-related arbitration commission shall be composed of one chairperson, a number of vice-chairpersons, and a number of members.

The chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members of a foreign-related arbitration commission may be appointed by the China Chamber of International Commerce.

Article 67 A foreign-related arbitration commission may appoint arbitrators from among foreign nationals with specialized knowledge in law, economics and trade, science and technology, and other fields.

Article 68 If a party to a foreign-related arbitration applies for preservation of evidence, the foreign-related arbitration commission shall submit the party's application to the intermediate people's court at the place where the evidence is located.

Article 69 The arbitral tribunal in a foreign-related arbitration may record the proceedings of the hearing in writing or prepare a summary of the record. The summary of the record may be signed or sealed by the parties and other participants in the arbitration.Article 70 If a party presents evidence that an arbitral award rendered in a foreign-related arbitration falls under any of the circumstances specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 258 of the Civil Procedure Law, the people's court shall, after forming a collegial panel to review and verify the evidence, rule to set aside the award.

Article 71 If the respondent presents evidence that an arbitral award rendered in a foreign-related arbitration falls under any of the circumstances specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 258 of the Civil Procedure Law, the people's court shall, after forming a collegial panel to review and verify the evidence, rule not to enforce the award.

Article 72 For a legally effective arbitral award made by a foreign-related arbitration commission, if the party requests enforcement and the party against whom enforcement is sought or its property is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the party shall directly apply to a competent foreign court for recognition and enforcement of the award.

Article 73 Foreign-related arbitration rules may be formulated by the China International Chamber of Commerce in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 74 Where there are provisions of law on the limitation period for arbitration, such provisions shall apply. Where there are no provisions of law on the limitation period for arbitration, the provisions on the limitation period for litigation shall apply.

Article 75 Before the China Arbitration Association formulates arbitration rules, an arbitration commission may formulate interim arbitration rules in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law.

Article 76 The parties shall pay arbitration fees in accordance with the regulations.

The measures for collecting arbitration fees shall be submitted to the price administration department for approval.

Article 77 Arbitration of labor disputes and disputes over agricultural contract within agricultural collective economic organizations shall be separately provided for.

Article 78 Where any provisions on arbitration formulated before the implementation of this Law conflict with the provisions of this Law, this Law shall prevail.

Article 79 Arbitration institutions established before the implementation of this Law in municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities where the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions are located, and other cities divided into districts shall be reorganized in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law; those that are not reorganized shall terminate one year after the date of implementation of this Law.

Other arbitration institutions established before the implementation of this Law that do not comply with the provisions of this Law shall terminate on the date of implementation of this Law.

Article 80 This Law shall come into force as of September 1, 1995.
