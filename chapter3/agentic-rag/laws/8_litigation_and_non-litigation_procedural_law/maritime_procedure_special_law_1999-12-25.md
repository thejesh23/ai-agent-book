# Special Maritime Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 25, 1999

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to safeguarding the litigation rights of the parties in maritime proceedings, ensuring that the people's courts ascertain the facts, distinguish the responsibilities, correctly apply the law, and promptly try maritime cases.

Article 2 Maritime proceedings conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and this Law. Where this Law provides otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 3 If any international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions differing from those of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and this Law in respect of foreign-related maritime proceedings, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except those on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 4 Maritime courts shall accept actions brought by parties arising from maritime tort disputes, maritime contract disputes, and other maritime disputes as provided for by law.

Article 5 This Law shall apply when maritime courts, the higher people's courts in their localities, and the Supreme People's Court try maritime cases.

## Chapter II Jurisdiction

Article 6 Territorial jurisdiction over maritime proceedings shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Territorial jurisdiction over the following maritime proceedings shall be determined in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) An action arising from a maritime tort may, in addition to being governed by Articles 29 to 31 of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, also be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the ship's port of registry is located;

(2) An action arising from a dispute over a contract for carriage of goods by sea may, in addition to being governed by Article 28 of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, also be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the port of transshipment is located;

(3) An action arising from a dispute over a charter party for a seagoing ship shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the port of delivery, the port of redelivery, the ship's port of registry, or the domicile of the defendant is located;

(4) An action arising from a dispute over a marine protection and indemnity insurance contract shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the subject matter of the insurance is located, the place where the incident occurred, or the domicile of the defendant is located;

(5) An action arising from a dispute over a crew labor service contract for a seagoing ship shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the plaintiff's domicile is located, the place where the contract was concluded, the place where the crew boarded or disembarked from the ship, or the domicile of the defendant is located;

(6) An action arising from a dispute over maritime security shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the security property is located or the domicile of the defendant is located; an action arising from a dispute over a ship mortgage may also be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the ship's port of registry is located;

(7) An action arising from a dispute over the ownership, right of possession, right of use, or maritime lien of a seagoing ship shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the ship is located, the ship's port of registry is located, or the domicile of the defendant is located.

Article 7 The following maritime proceedings shall be under the exclusive jurisdiction of the maritime courts specified in this Article:

(1) An action arising from a dispute over operations in a coastal port shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the port is located;

(2) An action arising from pollution damage to the sea caused by the discharge, spill, or dumping of oil or other harmful substances from a ship, or by marine production, operations, or shipbreaking or ship repair operations, shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the pollution occurred, the place where the damage resulted, or the place where measures were taken to prevent pollution;

(3) An action arising from a dispute over an ocean exploration and development contract performed within the territory of the People's Republic of China or within the sea areas under its jurisdiction shall be under the jurisdiction of the maritime court of the place where the contract is performed.

Article 8 Where the parties to a maritime dispute are all foreigners, stateless persons, foreign enterprises, or organizations, and they have agreed in writing to submit to the jurisdiction of a maritime court of the People's Republic of China, the maritime court of the People's Republic of China shall have jurisdiction over the dispute, even if the place with actual connection to the dispute is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 9 An application by a party for a declaration that maritime property is ownerless shall be filed with the maritime court of the place where the property is located; an application for a declaration of death due to a maritime accident shall be filed with the maritime court of the place where the competent authority handling the maritime accident is located or the maritime court hearing the relevant maritime case.

Article 10 Where a dispute over jurisdiction arises between a maritime court and a local people's court, the disputing courts shall resolve it through consultation; if consultation fails, the dispute shall be reported to their common superior people's court for designation of jurisdiction.

Article 11 An application for the enforcement of a maritime arbitration award, or for the recognition and enforcement of a judgment or ruling of a foreign court or a foreign maritime arbitration award, shall be filed with the maritime court of the place where the property to be enforced is located or where the person subject to enforcement has his domicile. If there is no maritime court in the place where the property to be enforced is located or where the person subject to enforcement has his domicile, the application shall be filed with the intermediate people's court of that place.

## Chapter III Maritime Property Preservation

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 12 Maritime property preservation refers to the compulsory measures taken by a maritime court, upon the application of a maritime claimant, against the property of the person against whom the claim is made, in order to secure the realization of the maritime claim.

Article 13 An application for maritime property preservation before the initiation of an action shall be filed with the maritime court of the place where the property to be preserved is located.

Article 14 Maritime property preservation shall not be bound by any agreement on litigation jurisdiction or arbitration agreement between the parties concerning the maritime claim.

Article 15 A maritime claimant applying for maritime property preservation shall submit a written application to the maritime court. The application shall state the matters of the maritime claim, the reasons for the application, the subject matter of the preservation, and the amount of security required, and shall be accompanied by relevant evidence.

Article 16 Upon accepting an application for maritime property preservation, the maritime court may order the maritime claimant to provide security. If the maritime claimant fails to provide security, the court shall reject the application.

Article 17 After accepting an application, the maritime court shall make a ruling within forty-eight hours. If it rules to adopt maritime property preservation measures, it shall execute them immediately; if the conditions for maritime property preservation are not met, it shall rule to reject the application.

If a party is dissatisfied with the ruling, it may apply for one reconsideration within five days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The maritime court shall make a reconsideration decision within five days from the date of receipt of the application for reconsideration. Execution of the ruling shall not be suspended during the period of reconsideration.

If an interested party raises an objection to the maritime property preservation, and the maritime court, upon examination, finds the reasons to be valid, it shall lift the preservation of the property.

Article 18 If the person against whom the claim is made provides security, or if a party has legitimate reasons to apply for the lifting of the maritime property preservation, the maritime court shall promptly lift the preservation.

If the maritime claimant fails to initiate an action or apply for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration agreement within the period prescribed by this Law, the maritime court shall promptly lift the preservation or return the security.

Article 19 After the execution of maritime property preservation, if the relevant maritime dispute has not entered into litigation or arbitration proceedings, the party may, with respect to the maritime claim, initiate an action in the maritime court that adopted the maritime property preservation or in another maritime court with jurisdiction, unless the parties have concluded an agreement on litigation jurisdiction or an arbitration agreement.

Article 20 If a maritime claimant erroneously applies for maritime property preservation, it shall compensate the person against whom the claim is made or the interested party for the losses incurred thereby.

### Section 2 Arrest and Auction of Ships

Article 21 The following maritime claims may give rise to an application for the arrest of a ship:

(1) loss or damage caused by the operation of the ship;
(2) loss of life or personal injury occurring in direct connection with the operation of the ship;
(3) salvage operations;
(4) damage or threat of damage caused by the ship to the environment, the coastline, or related interests; measures taken to prevent, minimize, or eliminate such damage; compensation for such damage; costs of reasonable measures actually taken or to be taken to restore the environment; loss or damage sustained or likely to be sustained by third parties as a result of such damage; and damage, costs, or loss of a similar nature to those referred to in this subparagraph;
(5) costs relating to the raising, removal, recovery, destruction, or rendering harmless of a ship which is sunk, wrecked, stranded, or abandoned, including costs relating to the raising, removal, recovery, destruction, or rendering harmless of anything which is or has been on board such ship, and costs relating to the maintenance of an abandoned ship and the maintenance of its crew;
(6) any agreement relating to the use or hire of the ship;
(7) any agreement relating to the carriage of goods or passengers;
(8) loss or damage to goods (including luggage) carried on board the ship or relating thereto;
(9) general average;
(10) towage;
(11) pilotage;
(12) supplies of materials or services for the operation, management, maintenance, or repair of the ship;
(13) construction, reconstruction, repair, conversion, or equipping of the ship;
(14) port, canal, dock, harbor, and other waterway dues and charges;
(15) wages and other sums due to the master, officers, and other members of the ship's complement, including costs of repatriation and social insurance contributions payable on their behalf;
(16) disbursements incurred for the ship or on behalf of its owner;
(17) insurance premiums (including mutual insurance calls) in respect of the ship, payable by or on behalf of the shipowner or demise charterer;
(18) any commissions, brokerage, or agency fees relating to the ship payable by or on behalf of the shipowner or demise charterer;
(19) any dispute as to ownership or possession of the ship;
(20) any dispute between co-owners of the ship as to the use or earnings of the ship;
(21) a mortgage or a charge of a similar nature on the ship;
(22) any dispute arising out of a contract for the sale of the ship.

Article 22 No ship may be arrested in respect of any maritime claim other than those specified in Article 21 of this Law, except for the enforcement of a judgment, arbitration award, or other legal instruments.

Article 23 A maritime court may arrest a ship in respect of which a maritime claim is asserted if:

(1) the person who owned the ship at the time of the arrest is liable for the maritime claim and is the owner of the ship at the time of the arrest;
(2) the demise charterer of the ship is liable for the maritime claim and is the demise charterer or owner of the ship at the time of the arrest;
(3) the claim is based upon a mortgage or a charge of a similar nature on the ship;
(4) the claim relates to the ownership or possession of the ship; or
(5) the claim is secured by a maritime lien on the ship.

A maritime court may arrest any other ship owned by the person who is liable for the maritime claim and who is, at the time of the arrest, the owner of, demise charterer of, time charterer of, or voyage charterer of that ship, except for claims relating to the ownership or possession of the ship.

Ships engaged in military or government public service shall not be arrested.

Article 24 A maritime claimant shall not apply for the arrest of a ship which has already been arrested in respect of the same maritime claim, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) the person against whom the claim is made has not provided sufficient security;
(2) the guarantor is likely to be unable to fulfill all or part of the guarantee obligation;
(3) the maritime claimant has, for reasonable cause, consented to the release of the arrested ship or the return of the security provided, or could not prevent the release of the arrested ship or the return of the security provided through reasonable measures.

Article 25 If a maritime claimant applying for the arrest of a ship in respect of which a maritime claim is asserted cannot immediately ascertain the name of the person against whom the claim is made, this shall not affect the filing of the application.

Article 26 At the same time as issuing or lifting an order for the arrest of a ship, the maritime court may issue a notice of assistance in execution to the relevant authorities. The notice shall specify the scope and content of the assistance to be rendered, and the relevant authorities shall be obliged to provide such assistance. If the maritime court deems it necessary, it may directly send personnel to board the ship for supervision.

Article 27 After the maritime court rules to adopt preservation measures against a ship, it may, with the consent of the maritime claimant, allow the ship to continue its operations by adopting methods such as restricting the disposal or mortgage of the ship.

Article 28 The period for the arrest of a ship for maritime property preservation shall be thirty days.

If the maritime claimant initiates an action or applies for arbitration within thirty days, or applies for the arrest of the ship during the litigation or arbitration proceedings, the period for the arrest of the ship shall not be subject to the time limit prescribed in the preceding paragraph.

Article 29 If, upon the expiry of the period of arrest of the ship, the person against whom the claim is made has not provided security, and it is not appropriate to continue the arrest of the ship, the maritime claimant may, after initiating an action or applying for arbitration, apply to the maritime court that arrested the ship for the auction of the ship.

Article 30 Upon receiving an application for the auction of a ship, the maritime court shall examine it and make a ruling approving or disapproving the auction of the ship.

If a party is dissatisfied with the ruling, it may apply for one reconsideration within five days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The maritime court shall make a reconsideration decision within five days from the date of receipt of the application for reconsideration. Execution of the ruling shall be suspended during the period of reconsideration.

Article 31 If a maritime claimant, after submitting an application for the auction of a ship, applies for the termination of the auction, the maritime court shall decide by ruling whether to grant the application. If the maritime court rules to terminate the auction of the ship, the expenses incurred in preparing for the auction shall be borne by the maritime claimant.

Article 32 If a maritime court rules to auction a ship, it shall publish an announcement through newspapers or other news media. If a foreign ship is to be auctioned, the announcement shall be published through newspapers or other news media distributed abroad.

The announcement shall include the following:

(1) the name and nationality of the ship to be auctioned;
(2) the reasons and basis for the auction of the ship;
(3) the composition of the ship auction committee;
(4) the time and place of the auction of the ship;
(5) the time and place for the display of the ship to be auctioned;
(6) the procedures to be completed for participating in the bidding;
(7) matters concerning the registration of claims;
(8) other matters that need to be announced.

The period for the announcement of the auction of a ship shall be no less than thirty days.

Article 33 The maritime court shall, thirty days prior to the auction of the ship, send a notice to the registry of the country of the ship's registration and to known holders of maritime liens, mortgagees, and the shipowner.

The notice shall include the name of the ship to be auctioned, the time and place of the auction, the reasons and basis for the auction, and the registration of claims.

The notice may be given in writing or by other appropriate means capable of confirming receipt.

Article 34 The auction of a ship shall be carried out by a ship auction committee. The ship auction committee shall be composed of three or five persons, including executing officers designated by the maritime court and an auctioneer and a ship surveyor engaged by the court.

The ship auction committee shall organize the appraisal and valuation of the ship; organize and preside over the auction; sign the confirmation of auction sale with the successful bidder; and handle the procedures for the delivery of the ship.

The ship auction committee shall be responsible to and subject to the supervision of the maritime court.

Article 35 Bidders shall register with the ship auction committee within the prescribed time limit. At the time of registration, they shall submit their identity certificates, the identity certificates of the legal representative of the enterprise or the responsible person of other organizations, and the power of attorney of the authorized agent, and shall pay a certain amount of deposit for the purchase of the ship.

Article 36 The ship auction committee shall, prior to the auction of the ship, display the ship to be auctioned and provide conditions and relevant information for inspecting the ship to be auctioned.

Article 37 After signing the confirmation of auction sale, the buyer shall immediately pay no less than twenty percent of the ship's price, and the remaining price shall be paid in full within seven days from the date of the sale, unless otherwise agreed between the ship auction committee and the buyer.

Article 38 After the buyer has paid the full price, the original shipowner shall, within a specified time limit, deliver the ship to the buyer at the ship's berthing place in its current condition. The ship auction committee shall organize and supervise the delivery of the ship and, after the delivery, sign a confirmation of the completion of the delivery of the ship with the buyer.

Upon the completion of the delivery of the ship, the maritime court shall issue an order lifting the arrest of the ship.

Article 39 After the delivery of the ship, the maritime court shall publish an announcement through newspapers or other news media, stating that the ship has been publicly auctioned and delivered to the buyer.

Article 40 After taking delivery of the ship, the buyer shall, holding the confirmation of auction sale and relevant materials, go through the formalities for the registration of the ownership of the ship with the ship registration authority. The original shipowner shall go through the formalities for the cancellation of the registration of the ownership of the ship with the original ship registration authority. The failure of the original shipowner to cancel the registration of the ownership of the ship shall not affect the transfer of the ownership of the ship.

Article 41 If bidders collude maliciously, the auction shall be invalid. The bidders who participated in the malicious collusion shall bear the expenses of the auction of the ship and compensate for the relevant losses. The maritime court may impose a fine on the bidders who participated in the malicious collusion of not less than ten percent and not more than thirty percent of the highest bid.

Article 42 Except as provided in this Section, the auction shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Auction Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 43 The auction of an arrested ship for the satisfaction of claims in execution proceedings may be carried out by reference to the relevant provisions of this Section.

### Section 3 Arrest and Auction of Cargo on Board

Article 44 A maritime claimant may apply for the arrest of cargo on board a ship in order to secure the realization of its maritime claim.

The cargo on board applied for arrest shall belong to the person against whom the claim is made.

Article 45 The value of the cargo on board applied for arrest by a maritime claimant shall be commensurate with the amount of its claim.

Article 46 The period for the arrest of cargo on board for maritime property preservation shall be fifteen days.

If the maritime claimant initiates an action or applies for arbitration within fifteen days, or applies for the arrest of cargo on board during the litigation or arbitration proceedings, the period for the arrest of cargo on board shall not be subject to the time limit prescribed in the preceding paragraph.

Article 47 If, upon the expiry of the period of arrest of the cargo on board, the person against whom the claim is made has not provided security, and it is not appropriate to continue the arrest of the cargo, the maritime claimant may, after initiating an action or applying for arbitration, apply to the maritime court that arrested the cargo for the auction of the cargo.

For items that cannot be stored, are not easy to store, or whose storage costs may exceed their value, the maritime claimant may apply for an early auction.

Article 48 Upon receiving an application for the auction of cargo on board, the maritime court shall examine it and make a ruling approving or disapproving the auction of the cargo within seven days.

If a party is dissatisfied with the ruling, it may apply for one reconsideration within five days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The maritime court shall make a reconsideration decision within five days from the date of receipt of the application for reconsideration. Execution of the ruling shall be suspended during the period of reconsideration.

Article 49 The auction of cargo on board shall be carried out by an auction organization composed of executing officers designated by the maritime court and an auctioneer engaged by the court, or by an institution entrusted by the maritime court.

For matters not provided for in this Section concerning the auction of cargo on board, reference shall be made to the relevant provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter concerning the auction of ships.

Article 50 The provisions of this Section shall apply to an application by a maritime claimant for maritime property preservation in respect of ship's fuel oil and ship's stores related to the maritime claim.

## Chapter IV Maritime Injunctions

Article 51 A maritime injunction refers to a compulsory measure taken by a maritime court, upon the application of a maritime claimant, ordering the person against whom the claim is made to do or refrain from doing a particular act, in order to protect the claimant's lawful rights and interests from infringement.

Article 52 An application for a maritime injunction before the initiation of an action shall be filed with the maritime court of the place where the maritime dispute occurred.

Article 53 A maritime injunction shall not be bound by any agreement on litigation jurisdiction or arbitration agreement between the parties concerning the maritime claim.

Article 54 A maritime claimant applying for a maritime injunction shall submit a written application to the maritime court. The application shall state the reasons for the application and shall be accompanied by relevant evidence.Article 55 When a maritime court accepts an application for a maritime injunction, it may order the maritime claimant to provide security. If the maritime claimant fails to provide security, the application shall be rejected.

Article 56 A maritime injunction shall be granted only if the following conditions are met:

(1) The claimant has a specific maritime claim;

(2) It is necessary to correct the respondent's conduct that violates legal provisions or contractual agreements;

(3) The circumstances are urgent, and failure to issue the maritime injunction immediately would cause damage or exacerbate existing damage.

Article 57 After accepting an application, the maritime court shall render a ruling within forty-eight hours. If the ruling grants the maritime injunction, it shall be executed immediately; if the conditions for a maritime injunction are not met, the application shall be rejected by ruling.

Article 58 If a party disagrees with the ruling, it may apply for a review within five days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The maritime court shall render a review decision within five days from the date of receipt of the review application. The execution of the ruling shall not be suspended during the review period.

If an interested party raises an objection to the maritime injunction, and the maritime court, upon examination, finds the reasons valid, it shall rule to revoke the maritime injunction.

Article 59 If the respondent refuses to comply with the maritime injunction, the maritime court may impose a fine or detention based on the severity of the circumstances; if the conduct constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

The fine for an individual shall be not less than 1,000 yuan and not more than 30,000 yuan. The fine for an entity shall be not less than 30,000 yuan and not more than 100,000 yuan.

The period of detention shall not exceed fifteen days.

Article 60 If a maritime claimant applies for a maritime injunction wrongfully, it shall compensate the respondent or interested party for the losses incurred as a result.

Article 61 After the execution of a maritime injunction, if the relevant maritime dispute has not entered litigation or arbitration proceedings, the party may, regarding the maritime claim, file a lawsuit with the maritime court that issued the maritime injunction or another maritime court with jurisdiction, unless the parties have entered into a litigation jurisdiction agreement or arbitration agreement.

## Chapter 5: Maritime Evidence Preservation

Article 62 Maritime evidence preservation refers to the compulsory measures taken by a maritime court, upon application by a maritime claimant, to extract, preserve, or seal evidence related to a maritime claim.

Article 63 If a party applies for maritime evidence preservation before filing a lawsuit, the application shall be submitted to the maritime court where the evidence to be preserved is located.

Article 64 Maritime evidence preservation is not subject to any litigation jurisdiction agreement or arbitration agreement between the parties concerning the maritime claim.

Article 65 A maritime claimant applying for maritime evidence preservation shall submit a written application to the maritime court. The application shall specify the evidence to be preserved, the connection between the evidence and the maritime claim, and the reasons for the application.

Article 66 When accepting an application for maritime evidence preservation, the maritime court may order the maritime claimant to provide security. If the maritime claimant fails to provide security, the application shall be rejected.

Article 67 Maritime evidence preservation shall be taken only if the following conditions are met:

(1) The applicant is a party to the maritime claim;

(2) The evidence sought to be preserved is probative of the maritime claim;

(3) The respondent is a person related to the evidence sought to be preserved;

(4) The circumstances are urgent, and failure to take evidence preservation immediately would cause the evidence related to the maritime claim to be lost or difficult to obtain.

Article 68 After accepting an application, the maritime court shall render a ruling within forty-eight hours. If the ruling grants the maritime evidence preservation measures, it shall be executed immediately; if the conditions for maritime evidence preservation are not met, the application shall be rejected by ruling.

Article 69 If a party disagrees with the ruling, it may apply for a review within five days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The maritime court shall render a review decision within five days from the date of receipt of the review application. The execution of the ruling shall not be suspended during the review period. If the reasons for the respondent's review application are found valid, the preserved evidence shall be returned to the respondent.

If an interested party raises an objection to the maritime evidence preservation, and the maritime court, upon examination, finds the reasons valid, it shall rule to revoke the maritime evidence preservation; if execution has already been carried out, the evidence related to the interested party shall be returned to the interested party.

Article 70 When carrying out maritime evidence preservation, the maritime court may, depending on the specific circumstances, seal the evidence, or extract copies, duplicates, take photographs, make video recordings, produce excerpts, investigation records, etc. If truly necessary, the original evidence may also be extracted.

Article 71 If a maritime claimant applies for maritime evidence preservation wrongfully, it shall compensate the respondent or interested party for the losses incurred as a result.

Article 72 After maritime evidence preservation, if the relevant maritime dispute has not entered litigation or arbitration proceedings, the party may, regarding the maritime claim, file a lawsuit with the maritime court that took the evidence preservation or another maritime court with jurisdiction, unless the parties have entered into a litigation jurisdiction agreement or arbitration agreement.

## Chapter 6: Maritime Security

Article 73 Maritime security includes the security involved in procedures such as maritime claim preservation, maritime injunction, and maritime evidence preservation as provided for in this Law.

The methods of providing security are cash, guarantee, mortgage, or pledge.

Article 74 The security provided by the maritime claimant shall be submitted to the maritime court; the security provided by the respondent may be submitted to the maritime court or provided to the maritime claimant.

Article 75 The method and amount of security provided by the maritime claimant shall be determined by the maritime court. The method and amount of security provided by the respondent shall be negotiated between the maritime claimant and the respondent; if negotiation fails, it shall be determined by the maritime court.

Article 76 The amount of security requested by the maritime claimant from the respondent for maritime claim preservation shall be commensurate with the amount of the claim, but shall not exceed the value of the preserved property.

The amount of security provided by the maritime claimant shall be equivalent to the losses that may be caused to the respondent by the application. The specific amount shall be determined by the maritime court.

Article 77 After security is provided, if the provider has legitimate reasons, it may apply to the maritime court for a reduction, modification, or cancellation of the security.

Article 78 If the amount of security requested by the maritime claimant is excessively high, causing losses to the respondent, the claimant shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 79 The provisions of this Chapter may be referred to for security involved in procedures such as the establishment of a limitation fund for maritime claims and advance execution.

## Chapter 7: Service

Article 80 The service of maritime litigation legal documents shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, and may also be effected by the following methods:

(1) Service on the litigation agent authorized by the person on whom service is to be made;

(2) Service on the representative office, branch, or business agent established by the person on whom service is to be made within the territory of the People's Republic of China;

(3) Service by other appropriate means that can confirm receipt.

Legal documents concerning the arrest of a ship may also be served on the master of the ship in question.

Article 81 If a person obliged to accept legal documents refuses to sign for them, and the server records the circumstances on the service certificate, and after the server and a witness sign or affix their seals, leaves the legal documents at the person's residence or place of business, it shall be deemed as service.

## Chapter 8: Trial Procedure

### Section 1: Provisions on Trial of Ship Collision Cases

Article 82 When filing a lawsuit, the plaintiff, and when filing a defense, the defendant, shall truthfully complete the "Marine Accident Investigation Form."

Article 83 When serving the statement of claim or statement of defense on the parties, the maritime court shall not attach relevant evidentiary materials.

Article 84 The parties shall complete the presentation of evidence before the court session begins. After completing the presentation of evidence and submitting a certificate of completion of evidence presentation to the maritime court, the parties may apply to inspect the factual evidentiary materials related to the ship collision.

Article 85 A party may not overturn the statements made in the "Marine Accident Investigation Form" and the evidence already presented, unless there is new evidence and sufficient reasons to explain that the evidence could not be submitted within the evidence presentation period.

Article 86 Ship surveys and valuations shall be carried out by institutions or individuals authorized by the state or possessing professional qualifications. The maritime court shall not adopt survey or valuation conclusions made by institutions or individuals not authorized by the state or lacking professional qualifications.

Article 87 The maritime court shall conclude the trial of a ship collision case within one year after filing the case. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the president of the court.

### Section 2: Provisions on Trial of General Average Cases

Article 88 Parties to a general average dispute may agree to entrust an adjustment institution for adjustment, or may directly file a lawsuit with a maritime court. If the maritime court accepts a general average dispute that has not been adjusted, it may entrust an adjustment institution for adjustment.

Article 89 If the parties do not raise objections to the general average adjustment report made by the adjustment institution, it may serve as the basis for apportioning liability; if a party raises an objection, the maritime court shall decide whether to adopt it.

Article 90 A party may file a lawsuit against the liable person for non-general average losses, without being affected by the general average litigation proceedings initiated due to the same maritime accident.

Article 91 If a party files a non-general average lawsuit with the maritime court handling the general average case regarding the same maritime accident, or files a recourse action against the liable person for general average contribution, the maritime court may consolidate the trials.

Article 92 The maritime court shall conclude the trial of a general average case within one year after filing the case. If an extension is necessary due to special circumstances, it shall be approved by the president of the court.

### Section 3: Provisions on the Exercise of the Right of Subrogation by Marine Insurers

Article 93 If an insured accident is caused by a third party, after the insurer pays insurance compensation to the insured, it may exercise the insured's right to claim compensation from the third party by subrogation within the scope of the insurance compensation.

Article 94 When exercising the right of subrogation to claim compensation, if the insured has not filed a lawsuit against the third party who caused the insured accident, the insurer shall file a lawsuit against the third party in its own name.

Article 95 When exercising the right of subrogation to claim compensation, if the insured has already filed a lawsuit against the third party who caused the insured accident, the insurer may request the court handling the case to change the parties and exercise the insured's right to claim compensation from the third party by subrogation.

If the insurance compensation received by the insured cannot cover all losses caused by the third party, the insurer and the insured may jointly act as co-plaintiffs to claim compensation from the third party.

Article 96 When an insurer files a lawsuit or applies to participate in proceedings in accordance with Articles 94 and 95 of this Law, it shall submit to the maritime court handling the case the certificate of payment of insurance compensation, as well as other documents that should be submitted for participation in the proceedings.

Article 97 For a claim for compensation for oil pollution damage caused by a ship, the injured party may file a claim against the owner of the ship causing the oil pollution damage, or directly against the insurer providing liability insurance for the shipowner's oil pollution damage or another person providing financial security.

If the insurer providing oil pollution damage liability insurance or another person providing financial security is sued, they have the right to request the owner of the ship causing the oil pollution damage to participate in the proceedings.

### Section 4: Summary Procedure, Supervision Procedure, and Public Exhortation Procedure

Article 98 When trying simple maritime cases with clear facts, well-defined rights and obligations, and minor disputes, the maritime court may apply the provisions of the summary procedure of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 99 If a creditor requests a debtor to pay a sum of money or negotiable securities based on a maritime matter, and the request meets the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, the creditor may apply to the maritime court with jurisdiction for an order for payment.

If the debtor is a foreign national, stateless person, foreign enterprise, or organization, but has a domicile, representative office, or branch within the territory of the People's Republic of China and the order for payment can be served, the creditor may apply to the maritime court with jurisdiction for an order for payment.

Article 100 If the holder of a bill of lading or other document of title loses control of the document or the document is lost, the holder may apply to the maritime court at the location of the goods for public exhortation.

## Chapter 9: Procedure for Establishing a Limitation Fund for Maritime Claims

Article 101 After a maritime accident occurs, if the shipowner, charterer, operator, salvor, or insurer applies for limitation of liability according to law, it may apply to the maritime court to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims.

If a ship causes oil pollution damage, the shipowner and its liability insurer or other person providing financial security shall establish a limitation fund for maritime claims for oil pollution damage with the maritime court in order to obtain the right to limit liability as provided by law.

The application to establish a limitation fund may be made before filing a lawsuit or during the lawsuit, but shall be made no later than before the first-instance judgment is rendered.

Article 102 If a party applies to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims before filing a lawsuit, the application shall be submitted to the maritime court at the place where the accident occurred, the place of contract performance, or the place where the ship was arrested.

Article 103 The establishment of a limitation fund for maritime claims is not subject to any litigation jurisdiction agreement or arbitration agreement between the parties.

Article 104 An applicant applying to a maritime court to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims shall submit a written application. The application shall state the amount of the limitation fund for maritime claims to be established, the reasons, and the names, addresses, and means of communication of known interested parties, and shall attach relevant evidence.

Article 105 After accepting an application to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims, the maritime court shall, within seven days, notify the known interested parties and simultaneously publish an announcement through newspapers or other news media.

The notification and announcement shall include the following:

(1) The name of the applicant;

(2) The facts and reasons for the application;

(3) The matter of establishing the limitation fund for maritime claims;

(4) The matter of registering claims;

(5) Other matters that need to be communicated.

Article 106 If an interested party objects to the applicant's application to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims, it shall submit a written objection to the maritime court within seven days from the date of receipt of the notification, or within thirty days from the date of the announcement if no notification was received.

After receiving the written objection from the interested party, the maritime court shall examine it and render a ruling within fifteen days. If the objection is valid, the application shall be rejected by ruling; if the objection is not valid, the applicant shall be permitted to establish the limitation fund for maritime claims by ruling.

If a party disagrees with the ruling, it may appeal within seven days from the date of receipt of the written ruling. The appellate court shall render a ruling within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the appeal petition.

Article 107 If no interested party raises an objection within the prescribed period, the maritime court shall rule to permit the applicant to establish the limitation fund for maritime claims.

Article 108 After the ruling permitting the applicant to establish the limitation fund for maritime claims takes effect, the applicant shall establish the limitation fund for maritime claims with the maritime court.

The limitation fund for maritime claims may be established in cash or by providing security acceptable to the maritime court.

The amount of the limitation fund for maritime claims shall be the limitation amount for maritime claims plus interest from the date of the accident to the date of establishment of the fund. If the fund is established by way of security, the amount of security shall be the amount of the fund plus interest during the period of establishment of the fund.

If the fund is established in cash, the date the fund arrives in the account designated by the maritime court shall be the date of establishment of the fund. If the fund is established by way of security, the date the maritime court accepts the security shall be the date of establishment of the fund.

Article 109 After the establishment of the limitation fund for maritime claims, the parties shall file a lawsuit regarding the relevant maritime dispute with the maritime court where the limitation fund for maritime claims was established, unless the parties have entered into a litigation jurisdiction agreement or arbitration agreement.

Article 110 If an applicant applies to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims wrongfully, it shall compensate the interested party for the losses incurred as a result.

## Chapter 10: Procedure for Registration and Satisfaction of Claims

Article 111 After the announcement of the maritime court's ruling on the compulsory auction of a ship is published, creditors shall apply for registration of claims related to the ship being auctioned within the announcement period. Failure to register within the announcement period shall be deemed as waiving the right to be satisfied from the proceeds of the auction of the ship.

Article 112 After the announcement of the maritime court's acceptance of an application to establish a limitation fund for maritime claims is published, creditors shall apply for registration of claims related to the maritime accident occurring on a specific occasion within the announcement period. Failure to register within the announcement period shall be deemed as waiving the claim.

Article 113 A creditor applying to the maritime court for registration of a claim shall submit a written application and provide relevant evidence of the claim.

Evidence of the claim includes legally effective judgments, rulings, mediation statements, arbitration awards, notarized creditor's rights documents, and other evidentiary materials proving the maritime claim.

Article 114 The maritime court shall examine the creditor's application. If evidence of the claim is provided, the court shall rule to permit registration; if no evidence of the claim is provided, the court shall rule to reject the application.

Article 115 If a creditor provides a judgment, ruling, mediation statement, arbitration award, or notarized creditor's rights document proving the claim, and the maritime court, upon examination, finds the documents to be authentic and lawful, it shall rule to confirm them.

Article 116 If a creditor provides other evidence of a maritime claim, it shall, after completing the claim registration, file a confirmation lawsuit with the maritime court handling the claim registration. If there is an arbitration agreement between the parties, arbitration shall be applied for promptly.

The judgments and rulings made by the maritime court in confirmation lawsuits are legally effective, and the parties may not appeal.

Article 117 After the maritime court hears and confirms the claims, it shall issue a notice of creditors' meeting to the creditors and convene the creditors' meeting.

Article 118 The creditors' meeting may negotiate and propose a distribution plan for the proceeds of the ship auction or the limitation fund for maritime claims, and enter into a satisfaction agreement.

The satisfaction agreement, upon confirmation by the maritime court by ruling, has legal effect.

If the creditors' meeting fails to reach an agreement, the maritime court shall, in accordance with the order of satisfaction provided for in the Maritime Code of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws, rule on the distribution plan for the proceeds of the ship auction or the limitation fund for maritime claims.

Article 119 The proceeds from the auction of the ship and the interest thereon, or the limitation fund for maritime claims and the interest thereon, shall be distributed together.

When distributing the proceeds of the ship auction, the litigation costs to be borne by the liable person, the costs incurred for preserving, auctioning the ship, and distributing the proceeds, and other expenses paid for the common benefit of the creditors, shall be deducted from the proceeds of the ship auction first.

The remaining amount after satisfying the debts shall be returned to the original owner of the ship or the person who established the limitation fund for maritime claims.

## Chapter 11: Procedure for Public Exhortation of Maritime Liens

Article 120 When a ship is transferred, the transferee may apply to the maritime court for a public exhortation of maritime liens, urging the holders of maritime liens to assert their rights promptly, thereby extinguishing the maritime liens attached to the ship.Article 121 An applicant applying for a maritime court to issue a notice of maritime lien shall file the application with the maritime court at the place of delivery of the vessel or at the domicile of the transferee.

Article 122 To apply for a notice of maritime lien, the applicant shall submit to the maritime court the application, the vessel transfer contract, and technical documents of the vessel. The application shall specify the name of the vessel, the facts and reasons for applying for the notice of maritime lien.

Article 123 Upon receipt of the application and relevant documents, the maritime court shall review the application and render a ruling within seven days on whether to grant or deny the application.

If the transferee disagrees with the ruling, they may apply for reconsideration once.

Article 124 After the ruling granting the application becomes effective, the maritime court shall publish an announcement through newspapers or other news media, urging maritime lien holders to assert their maritime liens within the notice period.

The notice period for maritime liens shall be sixty days.

Article 125 During the notice period for maritime liens, if a maritime lien holder asserts their right, they shall register with the maritime court; failure to assert the right shall be deemed a waiver of the maritime lien.

Article 126 If, upon expiration of the notice period for maritime liens, no one asserts a maritime lien, the maritime court shall, upon application by the party, render a judgment declaring that the transferred vessel is free of maritime liens. The content of the judgment shall be published.
