# Securities Investment Fund Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 28, 2003

Revised at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 28, 2012

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating securities investment fund activities, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of investors and relevant parties, and promoting the healthy development of securities investment funds and the capital market.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the raising of funds through public or non-public offerings to establish securities investment funds (hereinafter referred to as "funds") within the territory of the People's Republic of China, where such funds are managed by fund managers, custodied by fund custodians, and engage in securities investment activities for the benefit of fund unit holders. Matters not provided for in this Law shall be governed by the Trust Law of the People's Republic of China, the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 3 The rights and obligations of fund managers, fund custodians, and fund unit holders shall be stipulated in the fund contract in accordance with this Law.

Fund managers and fund custodians shall perform their fiduciary duties in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the fund contract.

Fund unit holders of funds established through public offerings (hereinafter referred to as "publicly offered funds") shall enjoy returns and bear risks in proportion to their fund units held. The distribution of returns and assumption of risks for funds established through non-public offerings (hereinafter referred to as "privately offered funds") shall be stipulated in the fund contract.

Article 4 Securities investment fund activities shall adhere to the principles of voluntariness, fairness, good faith, and shall not harm national interests or public social interests.

Article 5 The debts of fund property shall be borne by the fund property itself, and fund unit holders shall be liable for the debts of the fund property to the extent of their capital contributions. However, if the fund contract stipulates otherwise in accordance with this Law, such stipulations shall prevail.

Fund property is independent from the proprietary property of the fund manager and the fund custodian. The fund manager and the fund custodian shall not incorporate fund property into their proprietary property.

Any property and income acquired by the fund manager or fund custodian through the management, utilization, or other circumstances of fund property shall be included in the fund property.

When the fund manager or fund custodian undergoes liquidation due to lawful dissolution, lawful revocation, or lawful declaration of bankruptcy, the fund property shall not be part of their liquidation assets.

Article 6 The claims of fund property shall not be offset against the debts of the proprietary property of the fund manager or fund custodian. The claims and debts of different fund properties shall not be offset against each other.

Article 7 No compulsory execution shall be carried out against fund property for debts not borne by the fund property itself.

Article 8 Relevant taxes arising from investments of fund property shall be borne by fund unit holders. The fund manager or other withholding agents shall withhold and remit such taxes in accordance with the state's tax collection regulations.

Article 9 Fund managers and fund custodians, when managing and utilizing fund property, and fund service institutions, when engaging in fund service activities, shall perform their duties diligently and fulfill their obligations of good faith, prudence, and diligence.

When utilizing fund property for securities investment, fund managers shall adhere to prudent operation rules, formulate scientifically sound investment strategies and risk management systems, and effectively prevent and control risks.

Fund practitioners shall possess fund practice qualifications, abide by laws and administrative regulations, and adhere to professional ethics and codes of conduct.

Article 10 Fund managers, fund custodians, and fund service institutions shall, in accordance with this Law, establish a Securities Investment Fund Industry Association (hereinafter referred to as the "Fund Industry Association") to conduct industry self-regulation, coordinate industry relations, provide industry services, and promote industry development.

Article 11 The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall supervise and administer securities investment fund activities in accordance with the law; its dispatched agencies shall perform their duties as authorized.

## Chapter II Fund Managers

Article 12 A fund manager shall be a company or partnership established in accordance with the law.

The fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall be a fund management company or another institution approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with regulations.

Article 13 The establishment of a fund management company for managing publicly offered funds shall meet the following conditions and be approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) Having articles of association that comply with this Law and the Company Law of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Having a registered capital of not less than RMB 100 million yuan, which must be paid-in monetary capital;
(3) Major shareholders shall have a good track record in operating financial businesses or managing financial institutions, sound financial conditions and social reputation, an asset scale meeting the standards set by the State Council, and no record of illegal activities in the last three years;
(4) Having a statutory number of personnel who have obtained fund practice qualifications;
(5) Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel meeting the corresponding qualifications for their positions;
(6) Having business premises, security facilities, and other facilities related to fund management business that meet the requirements;
(7) Having a sound internal governance structure, comprehensive internal audit and monitoring systems, and risk control systems;
(8) Other conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council with the approval of the State Council.

Article 14 The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall, within six months from the date of accepting an application for establishing a fund management company, conduct a review in accordance with the conditions stipulated in Article 13 of this Law and the principle of prudential supervision, make a decision to approve or disapprove, and notify the applicant; if disapproved, reasons shall be provided.

If a fund management company changes a shareholder holding five percent or more of its equity, changes its actual controller, or changes other major matters, it shall report to and obtain approval from the securities regulatory authority under the State Council. The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall, within sixty days from the date of accepting the application, make a decision to approve or disapprove, and notify the applicant; if disapproved, reasons shall be provided.

Article 15 Persons falling under any of the following circumstances shall not serve as directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or other practitioners of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund:

(1) Having been sentenced to criminal punishment for crimes of embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, property infringement, or disrupting the order of the socialist market economy;
(2) Being a director, supervisor, factory manager, or senior management personnel personally liable for the bankruptcy liquidation of a company or enterprise they served due to poor management, or for the revocation of its business license due to illegal activities, where less than five years have elapsed since the conclusion of the bankruptcy liquidation or the revocation of the business license;
(3) Owing a relatively large amount of personal debt that has fallen due and remains unpaid;
(4) Having been dismissed from a fund manager, fund custodian, stock exchange, securities company, securities depository and clearing institution, futures exchange, futures company, other institution, or state organ due to illegal acts;
(5) Being a lawyer, certified public accountant, or practitioner of an asset appraisal institution, verification institution, or investment consulting institution whose practice certificate has been revoked or qualification cancelled due to illegal acts;
(6) Other persons stipulated by laws and administrative regulations as being prohibited from engaging in fund business.

Article 16 Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall be familiar with laws and administrative regulations related to securities investment and have at least three years of work experience relevant to their positions; senior management personnel shall also possess fund practice qualifications.

Article 17 Directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other practitioners of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund, as well as their spouses and interested persons, shall declare any securities investments they make to the fund manager in advance and shall not create conflicts of interest with fund unit holders.

The fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall establish management systems for the declaration, registration, review, and disposal of securities investments made by the persons specified in the preceding paragraph, and shall report such systems to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for recordation.

Article 18 Directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other practitioners of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall not hold any position in a fund custodian or other fund manager, and shall not engage in securities transactions or other activities that harm the interests of fund property or fund unit holders.

Article 19 A fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall perform the following duties:

(1) Raising funds in accordance with the law, and handling matters related to the offering and registration of fund units;
(2) Handling fund filing procedures;
(3) Managing and accounting for different fund properties under its management separately, and conducting securities investments;
(4) Determining the fund income distribution plan in accordance with the fund contract, and distributing income to fund unit holders in a timely manner;
(5) Conducting fund accounting and preparing fund financial accounting reports;
(6) Preparing interim and annual fund reports;
(7) Calculating and announcing the net asset value of the fund, and determining the subscription and redemption prices of fund units;
(8) Handling information disclosure matters related to fund property management business activities;
(9) Convening meetings of fund unit holders as required;
(10) Preserving records, account books, statements, and other relevant materials of fund property management business activities;
(11) Exercising litigation rights or performing other legal acts in the name of the fund manager on behalf of the interests of fund unit holders;
(12) Other duties stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 20 A fund manager of a publicly offered fund, its directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other practitioners shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Commingling its proprietary property or others' property with fund property for securities investment;
(2) Unfairly treating different fund properties under its management;
(3) Using fund property or position convenience to seek benefits for persons other than fund unit holders;
(4) Promising returns or bearing losses to fund unit holders in violation of regulations;
(5) Embezzling or misappropriating fund property;
(6) Disclosing non-public information obtained by virtue of position, using such information to engage in, or explicitly or implicitly instructing others to engage in, related trading activities;
(7) Neglecting duties and failing to perform duties as required;
(8) Other acts prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 21 A fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall establish a sound internal governance structure, clearly defining the duties and authority of the shareholders' meeting, board of directors, board of supervisors, and senior management personnel, and ensuring the independent operation of the fund manager.

A fund manager of a publicly offered fund may implement a professional shareholding plan to establish a long-term incentive and constraint mechanism.

Shareholders, directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall, when exercising rights or performing duties, adhere to the principle of prioritizing the interests of fund unit holders.

Article 22 A fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall set aside a risk reserve from the management fees of the fund.

If a fund manager of a publicly offered fund causes losses to fund property or the legitimate rights and interests of fund unit holders due to violations of laws, regulations, or the fund contract, and is liable for compensation, the risk reserve may be used preferentially for such compensation.

Article 23 Shareholders and actual controllers of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall promptly fulfill their obligation to report major matters in accordance with the regulations of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, and shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Making false capital contributions or withdrawing capital contributions;
(2) Interfering with the fund management operations of the fund manager without authorization, without a resolution of the shareholders' meeting or board of directors as required by law;
(3) Requiring the fund manager to use fund property to seek benefits for themselves or others, harming the interests of fund unit holders;
(4) Other acts prohibited by the regulations of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

If shareholders or actual controllers of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund engage in the acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph, or if shareholders no longer meet the statutory conditions, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall order them to make corrections within a specified period, and may, depending on the circumstances, order them to transfer the equity they hold or control in the fund manager.

Before the shareholders or actual controllers specified in the preceding paragraph correct their illegal acts and transfer the equity they hold or control in the fund manager as required, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council may restrict the relevant shareholders from exercising their shareholder rights.

Article 24 If a fund manager of a publicly offered fund violates laws or regulations, or if its internal governance structure, audit monitoring, or risk control management does not comply with regulations, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall order it to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, or if its acts seriously endanger the stable operation of the fund manager or harm the legitimate rights and interests of fund unit holders, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council may, depending on the circumstances, take the following measures against it:

(1) Restricting business activities and ordering the suspension of part or all of its business;
(2) Restricting the distribution of dividends, and restricting the payment of remuneration and provision of benefits to directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(3) Restricting the transfer of proprietary property or the creation of other rights over proprietary property;
(4) Ordering the replacement of directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel, or restricting their rights;
(5) Ordering relevant shareholders to transfer their equity or restricting relevant shareholders from exercising their shareholder rights.

After a fund manager of a publicly offered fund has rectified the issues, it shall submit a report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council. If, upon inspection, the authority finds that the fund manager meets the relevant requirements, it shall lift the measures taken against it within three days from the date of completion of the inspection.

Article 25 If directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund fail to perform their duties diligently, resulting in major illegal acts or major risks for the fund manager, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council may order their replacement.

Article 26 If a fund manager of a publicly offered fund operates illegally or encounters major risks, seriously endangering the order of the securities market and harming the interests of fund unit holders, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council may take supervisory measures against the fund manager, such as ordering a suspension of business for rectification, designating another institution for trusteeship, taking over, cancelling its fund management qualification, or revoking its license.

Article 27 During the period when a fund manager of a publicly offered fund is ordered to suspend business for rectification, is placed under designated trusteeship, is taken over, or is in liquidation, or when major risks arise, with the approval of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, the following measures may be taken against the directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons of the fund manager:

(1) Notifying the exit control authority to prevent their exit from the country in accordance with the law;
(2) Applying to judicial authorities to prohibit them from transferring, assigning, or otherwise disposing of property, or creating other rights over property.

Article 28 The duties of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Its fund management qualification is cancelled in accordance with the law;
(2) It is removed by a meeting of fund unit holders;
(3) It is dissolved in accordance with the law, revoked in accordance with the law, or declared bankrupt in accordance with the law;
(4) Other circumstances stipulated in the fund contract.

Article 29 When the duties of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund terminate, a meeting of fund unit holders shall elect a new fund manager within six months; before a new fund manager is appointed, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall designate an interim fund manager.

When the duties of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund terminate, it shall properly preserve the fund management business materials and promptly handle the handover of fund management business. The new fund manager or interim fund manager shall promptly take over.

Article 30 When the duties of a fund manager of a publicly offered fund terminate, it shall, in accordance with regulations, appoint an accounting firm to audit the fund property, announce the audit results, and simultaneously report them to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for recordation.

Article 31 Specific measures for regulating fund managers of privately offered funds shall be formulated by the financial regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with the principles of this Chapter.

## Chapter III Fund Custodians

Article 32 A fund custodian shall be a commercial bank or other financial institution established in accordance with the law.

If a commercial bank acts as a fund custodian, it shall be approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council in conjunction with the banking regulatory authority under the State Council; if another financial institution acts as a fund custodian, it shall be approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 33 To act as a fund custodian, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Net assets and risk control indicators comply with relevant regulations;
(2) Having a dedicated fund custody department;
(3) Having a statutory number of full-time personnel who have obtained fund practice qualifications;
(4) Having the conditions for safely safekeeping fund property;
(5) Having a safe and efficient clearing and settlement system;
(6) Having business premises, security facilities, and other facilities related to fund custody business that meet the requirements;
(7) Having comprehensive internal audit and monitoring systems and risk control systems;
(8) Other conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the banking regulatory authority under the State Council with the approval of the State Council.

Article 34 The provisions of Articles 15, 17, and 18 of this Law shall apply to the senior management personnel and other practitioners of the dedicated fund custody department of a fund custodian.

The provisions of Article 16 of this Law shall apply to the senior management personnel of the dedicated fund custody department of a fund custodian.

Article 35 A fund custodian and a fund manager shall not be the same institution, and shall not make capital contributions to or hold shares in each other.

Article 36 A fund custodian shall perform the following duties:

(1) Safely safekeeping fund property;
(2) Opening fund property capital accounts and securities accounts in accordance with regulations;
(3) Setting up separate accounts for different fund properties under its custody to ensure the integrity and independence of fund property;
(4) Preserving records, account books, statements, and other relevant materials of fund custody business activities;
(5) Promptly handling clearing and settlement matters in accordance with the fund contract and based on the investment instructions of the fund manager;
(6) Handling information disclosure matters related to fund custody business activities;
(7) Providing opinions on fund financial accounting reports, interim and annual fund reports;
(8) Reviewing and verifying the net asset value of the fund and the subscription and redemption prices of fund units calculated by the fund manager;
(9) Convening meetings of fund unit holders as required;
(10) Supervising the investment operations of the fund manager in accordance with regulations;
(11) Other duties stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.**Article 37** If a custodian discovers that an investment instruction from a fund manager violates laws, administrative regulations, or other relevant provisions, or breaches the fund contract, the custodian shall refuse to execute it, immediately notify the fund manager, and promptly report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

If a custodian discovers that an investment instruction from a fund manager, which has already taken effect according to the trading procedures, violates laws, administrative regulations, or other relevant provisions, or breaches the fund contract, the custodian shall immediately notify the fund manager and promptly report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 38** The provisions of Articles 20 and 22 of this Law apply to fund custodians.

**Article 39** If a fund custodian no longer meets the conditions stipulated in this Law, or fails to perform its duties diligently and makes significant errors in fulfilling its responsibilities under this Law, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall order it to rectify; if it fails to rectify within the time limit, or if its conduct seriously affects the stable operation of the fund under its custody and harms the interests of fund share holders, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the banking regulatory authority under the State Council may, depending on the circumstances, take the following measures against it:

(1) Restrict its business activities and order it to suspend undertaking new fund custody business;
(2) Order the replacement of senior management personnel responsible for the specialized fund custody department.

After the fund custodian has rectified the issues, it shall submit a report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the banking regulatory authority under the State Council; upon inspection and compliance with relevant requirements, the measures taken against it shall be lifted within three days from the date of completion of the inspection.

**Article 40** The securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the banking regulatory authority under the State Council may revoke the fund custody qualification of a fund custodian under any of the following circumstances:

(1) It has not conducted fund custody business for three consecutive years;
(2) It violates the provisions of this Law, and the circumstances are serious;
(3) Other circumstances stipulated by laws or administrative regulations.

**Article 41** The duties of a fund custodian shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Its fund custody qualification is revoked according to law;
(2) It is removed by the fund share holders' meeting;
(3) It is dissolved according to law, revoked according to law, or declared bankrupt according to law;
(4) Other circumstances stipulated in the fund contract.

**Article 42** Upon the termination of a fund custodian's duties, the fund share holders' meeting shall elect a new fund custodian within six months; before the new fund custodian is appointed, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall designate an interim fund custodian.

Upon the termination of a fund custodian's duties, it shall properly preserve the fund property and fund custody business records, and promptly handle the transfer procedures for the fund property and fund custody business. The new fund custodian or interim fund custodian shall promptly take over.

**Article 43** Upon the termination of a fund custodian's duties, it shall, in accordance with regulations, engage an accounting firm to audit the fund property, announce the audit results, and simultaneously file a report with the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record.

## Chapter IV: Fund Operation Methods and Organization

**Article 44** The fund contract shall stipulate the fund's operation method.

**Article 45** The fund's operation method may adopt a closed-end, open-end, or other method.

A fund adopting a closed-end operation method (hereinafter referred to as a closed-end fund) refers to a fund where the total number of fund shares remains fixed during the term of the fund contract, and fund share holders cannot apply for redemption. A fund adopting an open-end operation method (hereinafter referred to as an open-end fund) refers to a fund where the total number of fund shares is not fixed, and fund shares can be subscribed or redeemed at the times and places stipulated in the fund contract.

The methods for the offering, trading, subscription, and redemption of fund shares for funds adopting other operation methods shall be separately stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 46** Fund share holders shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) To share in the income from the fund property;
(2) To participate in the distribution of the remaining fund property after liquidation;
(3) To transfer or apply for redemption of the fund shares they hold according to law;
(4) To request the convening of a fund share holders' meeting or to convene a fund share holders' meeting in accordance with regulations;
(5) To exercise voting rights on matters considered by the fund share holders' meeting;
(6) To initiate legal proceedings according to law against fund managers, fund custodians, or fund service providers that harm their legitimate rights and interests;
(7) Other rights stipulated in the fund contract.

Fund share holders of publicly offered funds have the right to access or copy publicly disclosed fund information materials. Fund share holders of privately offered funds have the right to access the fund's financial accounting books and other financial materials regarding matters affecting their own interests.

**Article 47** The fund share holders' meeting shall be composed of all fund share holders and shall exercise the following powers:

(1) To decide on the expansion of the fund or the extension of the fund contract term;
(2) To decide on the modification of important contents of the fund contract or the early termination of the fund contract;
(3) To decide on the replacement of the fund manager or fund custodian;
(4) To decide on the adjustment of the compensation standards for the fund manager and fund custodian;
(5) Other powers stipulated in the fund contract.

**Article 48** In accordance with the fund contract, the fund share holders' meeting may establish a standing body to exercise the following powers:

(1) To convene the fund share holders' meeting;
(2) To propose the replacement of the fund manager or fund custodian;
(3) To supervise the investment operations of the fund manager and the custody activities of the fund custodian;
(4) To propose adjustments to the compensation standards for the fund manager and fund custodian;
(5) Other powers stipulated in the fund contract.

The standing body referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be composed of persons elected by the fund share holders' meeting; its rules of procedure shall be stipulated in the fund contract.

**Article 49** The fund share holders' meeting and its standing body shall not directly participate in or interfere with the fund's investment management activities.

## Chapter V: Public Offering of Funds

**Article 50** The public offering of a fund shall be registered with the securities regulatory authority under the State Council. Without registration, no fund may be publicly or covertly publicly offered.

The term "public offering of a fund" as used in the preceding paragraph includes raising funds from unspecified objects, raising funds from specified objects where the cumulative number exceeds two hundred persons, and other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Publicly offered funds shall be managed by a fund manager and custodied by a fund custodian.

**Article 51** To register a publicly offered fund, the proposed fund manager shall submit the following documents to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) An application report;
(2) A draft fund contract;
(3) A draft fund custody agreement;
(4) A draft prospectus;
(5) A legal opinion issued by a law firm;
(6) Other documents required to be submitted by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 52** The fund contract for a publicly offered fund shall include the following contents:

(1) The purpose of the fund offering and the fund name;
(2) The names and domiciles of the fund manager and fund custodian;
(3) The fund's operation method;
(4) The total number of fund shares and the term of the fund contract for a closed-end fund, or the minimum total number of shares to be offered for an open-end fund;
(5) The principles for determining the offering date, price, and fees of fund shares;
(6) The rights and obligations of fund share holders, the fund manager, and the fund custodian;
(7) The procedures and rules for convening, deliberating, and voting at the fund share holders' meeting;
(8) The procedures, time, place, fee calculation methods for the offering, trading, subscription, and redemption of fund shares, as well as the time and method for paying redemption proceeds;
(9) The principles and implementation methods for fund income distribution;
(10) The method and proportion for the extraction and payment of compensation to the fund manager and fund custodian;
(11) The method for the extraction and payment of other expenses related to the management and use of fund property;
(12) The investment direction and investment restrictions for fund property;
(13) The calculation method and announcement method for the net asset value of the fund;
(14) The handling method if the fund offering fails to meet the statutory requirements;
(15) The causes and procedures for the dissolution and termination of the fund contract, as well as the method for liquidating fund property;
(16) The method for dispute resolution;
(17) Other matters agreed upon by the parties.

**Article 53** The prospectus for a publicly offered fund shall include the following contents:

(1) The name and registration date of the document approving the fund offering application;
(2) The basic information of the fund manager and fund custodian;
(3) A summary of the contents of the fund contract and the fund custody agreement;
(4) The offering date, price, fees, and period for fund shares;
(5) The offering method, offering institutions, and the name of the registration institution for fund shares;
(6) The names and domiciles of the law firm issuing the legal opinion and the accounting firm auditing the fund property;
(7) The method and proportion for the extraction and payment of compensation to the fund manager and fund custodian, and other related fees;
(8) Risk warning content;
(9) Other contents stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 54** The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall, within six months from the date of accepting the registration application for the public offering of a fund, conduct a review in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, make a decision to register or not to register, and notify the applicant; if registration is denied, the reasons shall be stated.

**Article 55** Fund shares may only be offered after the fund offering application has been registered.

The offering of fund shares shall be handled by the fund manager or a fund sales institution entrusted by it.

**Article 56** The fund manager shall publish the prospectus, fund contract, and other relevant documents three days before the offering of fund shares.

The documents specified in the preceding paragraph shall be truthful, accurate, and complete.

Promotional and publicity activities conducted for the fund offering shall comply with relevant laws and administrative regulations and shall not involve any of the acts listed in Article 77 of this Law.

**Article 57** The fund manager shall conduct the fund offering within six months from the date of receiving the registration approval document. If the offering commences more than six months later and the registered matters have not undergone material changes, it shall report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record; if material changes have occurred, it shall resubmit a registration application to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

The fund offering shall not exceed the fund offering period approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council. The fund offering period shall be calculated from the date of the offering of fund shares.

**Article 58** Upon the expiration of the fund offering period, if the total number of fund shares raised for a closed-end fund reaches 80% or more of the approved scale, or if the total number of fund shares raised for an open-end fund exceeds the approved minimum total number of shares to be offered, and the number of fund share holders meets the requirements stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, the fund manager shall, within ten days from the date of expiration of the offering period, engage a statutory capital verification institution to verify the capital. Within ten days from the date of receiving the capital verification report, it shall submit the capital verification report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, handle the fund registration procedures, and make an announcement.

**Article 59** Funds raised during the fund offering period shall be deposited in a dedicated account, and no one may use them before the conclusion of the fund offering activities.

**Article 60** When an investor pays the subscription amount for fund shares, the fund contract is established; when the fund manager handles the fund registration procedures with the securities regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with Article 58 of this Law, the fund contract takes effect.

If, upon the expiration of the fund offering period, the conditions stipulated in Article 58 of this Law cannot be met, the fund manager shall bear the following responsibilities:

(1) To bear the debts and expenses arising from the offering activities using its own property;
(2) To return the amounts paid by investors within thirty days after the expiration of the fund offering period, plus bank deposit interest for the same period.

## Chapter VI: Trading, Subscription, and Redemption of Fund Shares of Publicly Offered Funds

**Article 61** To apply for the listing and trading of fund shares, the fund manager shall submit an application to a stock exchange. If the stock exchange approves the application after review according to law, the parties shall sign a listing agreement.

**Article 62** The listing and trading of fund shares shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The fund offering complies with the provisions of this Law;
(2) The term of the fund contract is five years or more;
(3) The amount raised in the fund offering is not less than 200 million RMB;
(4) The number of fund share holders is not less than one thousand;
(5) Other conditions stipulated in the rules for the listing and trading of fund shares.

**Article 63** The rules for the listing and trading of fund shares shall be formulated by the stock exchange and submitted to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for approval.

**Article 64** After the listing and trading of fund shares, if any of the following circumstances occurs, the stock exchange shall terminate their listing and trading and report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record:

(1) The conditions for listing and trading stipulated in Article 62 of this Law are no longer met;
(2) The term of the fund contract expires;
(3) The fund share holders' meeting decides to terminate the listing and trading early;
(4) Other circumstances for the termination of listing and trading stipulated in the fund contract or the rules for the listing and trading of fund shares.

**Article 65** The subscription, redemption, and registration of fund shares of an open-end fund shall be handled by the fund manager or a fund service institution entrusted by it.

**Article 66** The fund manager shall handle the subscription and redemption of fund shares on each business day; unless otherwise stipulated in the fund contract.

When an investor pays the subscription amount, the subscription is established; when the fund share registration institution confirms the fund shares, the subscription takes effect.

When a fund share holder submits a redemption application, the redemption is established; when the fund share registration institution confirms the redemption, the redemption takes effect.

**Article 67** The fund manager shall pay the redemption proceeds on time, except in the following circumstances:

(1) Force majeure prevents the fund manager from paying the redemption proceeds;
(2) A securities trading venue decides to temporarily suspend trading according to law, preventing the fund manager from calculating the net asset value of the fund for that day;
(3) Other special circumstances stipulated in the fund contract.

If any of the above circumstances occurs, the fund manager shall report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record on the same day.

After the circumstances specified in paragraph 1 of this Article disappear, the fund manager shall promptly pay the redemption proceeds.

**Article 68** An open-end fund shall maintain sufficient cash or government bonds to meet the redemption requests of fund share holders. The specific proportion of cash or government bonds to be maintained in the fund property shall be stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 69** The subscription and redemption prices of fund shares shall be calculated based on the net asset value per share on the subscription or redemption date, plus or minus relevant fees.

**Article 70** If an error occurs in the valuation of the net asset value per share, the fund manager shall immediately correct it and take reasonable measures to prevent further losses. If the valuation error reaches 0.5% of the net asset value per share, the fund manager shall make an announcement and report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record.

If fund share holders suffer losses due to errors in the valuation of the net asset value per share, they have the right to demand compensation from the fund manager and the fund custodian.

## Chapter VII: Investment and Information Disclosure of Publicly Offered Funds

**Article 71** When using fund property for securities investment, the fund manager shall, unless otherwise stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, adopt an asset portfolio approach.

The specific method of the asset portfolio and the investment proportions shall be stipulated in the fund contract in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 72** Fund property shall be used for the following investments:

(1) Listed and traded stocks and bonds;
(2) Other securities and their derivatives stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 73** Fund property shall not be used for the following investments or activities:

(1) Underwriting securities;
(2) Providing loans or guarantees to others in violation of regulations;
(3) Engaging in investments with unlimited liability;
(4) Buying or selling other fund shares, unless otherwise stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council;
(5) Making capital contributions to the fund manager or fund custodian;
(6) Engaging in insider trading, manipulating securities trading prices, or other improper securities trading activities;
(7) Other activities prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

When using fund property to buy or sell securities issued by the fund manager, fund custodian, their controlling shareholders, actual controllers, or companies with other material interests, or securities underwritten during the underwriting period, or engaging in other material related-party transactions, the principle of prioritizing the interests of fund share holders shall be followed, conflicts of interest shall be prevented, the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall be complied with, and information disclosure obligations shall be fulfilled.

**Article 74** Fund managers, fund custodians, and other fund information disclosure obligors shall disclose fund information according to law and ensure the truthfulness, accuracy, and completeness of the disclosed information.

**Article 75** Fund information disclosure obligors shall ensure that fund information subject to disclosure is disclosed within the time limit stipulated by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, and ensure that investors can access or copy the publicly disclosed information materials at the times and in the manner stipulated in the fund contract.

**Article 76** Publicly disclosed fund information includes:

(1) The fund prospectus, fund contract, and fund custody agreement;
(2) The fund offering status;
(3) The announcement on the listing and trading of fund shares;
(4) The net asset value of the fund and the net asset value per share;
(5) The subscription and redemption prices of fund shares;
(6) The quarterly report on the asset portfolio of the fund property, financial accounting reports, and interim and annual fund reports;
(7) Interim reports;
(8) Resolutions of the fund share holders' meeting;
(9) Major personnel changes in the fund manager's or fund custodian's specialized fund custody department;
(10) Litigation or arbitration involving the fund property, fund management business, or fund custody business;
(11) Other information required to be disclosed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 77** When publicly disclosing fund information, the following acts are prohibited:

(1) False records, misleading statements, or material omissions;
(2) Making predictions about securities investment performance;
(3) Illegally promising returns or bearing losses;
(4) Defaming other fund managers, fund custodians, or fund sales institutions;
(5) Other acts prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

## Chapter VIII: Amendment and Termination of Fund Contracts for Publicly Offered Funds and Liquidation of Fund Property**Article 78** A fund may change its operational model or merge with other funds in accordance with the provisions of the fund contract or a resolution of the fund share holders' meeting.

**Article 79** To increase the scale of a closed-end fund or extend the term of a fund contract, the following conditions shall be met, and the matter shall be filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council:

(1) The fund has a good operational performance;
(2) The fund management institution has not been subject to administrative penalties or criminal punishments for violations of laws or regulations within the preceding two years;
(3) A resolution of the fund share holders' meeting has been passed;
(4) Other conditions stipulated in this Law.

**Article 80** A fund contract shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The term of the fund contract expires and is not extended;
(2) The fund share holders' meeting decides to terminate the contract;
(3) The duties of the fund management institution or the fund custodian are terminated, and no new fund management institution or fund custodian takes over within six months;
(4) Other circumstances stipulated in the fund contract.

**Article 81** Upon termination of a fund contract, the fund management institution shall organize a liquidation team to liquidate the fund property.

The liquidation team shall be composed of the fund management institution, the fund custodian, and relevant intermediary service institutions.

The liquidation report prepared by the liquidation team shall be audited by an accounting firm, and after a legal opinion is issued by a law firm, it shall be filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and publicly announced.

**Article 82** The remaining fund property after liquidation shall be distributed in proportion to the fund shares held by the fund share holders.

## Chapter IX Exercise of Rights by Fund Share Holders of Publicly Offered Funds

**Article 83** A fund share holders' meeting shall be convened by the fund management institution. If a standing body of the fund share holders' meeting is established, it shall be convened by that standing body; if the standing body fails to convene it, the fund management institution shall convene it. If the fund management institution fails to convene it as required or is unable to convene it, the fund custodian shall convene it.

If fund share holders representing more than ten percent of the total fund shares request to convene a fund share holders' meeting on the same matter, and the standing body of the fund share holders' meeting, the fund management institution, and the fund custodian all fail to convene it, the fund share holders representing more than ten percent of the total fund shares have the right to convene the meeting themselves and shall file a report with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 84** When convening a fund share holders' meeting, the convener shall announce the time, form, matters for deliberation, meeting procedures, and voting methods of the meeting at least thirty days in advance.
The fund share holders' meeting shall not vote on matters that have not been announced.

**Article 85** A fund share holders' meeting may be held in person or through communication methods such as correspondence.

Each fund share shall have one voting right. Fund share holders may appoint proxies to attend the fund share holders' meeting and exercise voting rights.

**Article 86** A fund share holders' meeting may be convened only if fund share holders representing more than one-half of the total fund shares are present.

If the fund shares held by the fund share holders present at the meeting are less than the proportion stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the convener may reconvene the fund share holders' meeting on the originally scheduled matters within three to six months after the originally announced meeting date. The reconvened fund share holders' meeting may be convened only if fund share holders representing more than one-third of the total fund shares are present.

Decisions on matters for deliberation at the fund share holders' meeting shall be passed by more than one-half of the voting rights held by the fund share holders present at the meeting. However, decisions on changing the fund's operational model, replacing the fund management institution or fund custodian, terminating the fund contract early, or merging with other funds shall be passed by more than two-thirds of the voting rights held by the fund share holders present at the meeting.

Decisions made by the fund share holders' meeting shall be filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with the law and publicly announced.

## Chapter X Privately Offered Funds

**Article 87** Privately offered funds shall be raised only from qualified investors, and the cumulative number of qualified investors shall not exceed two hundred.

For the purposes of the preceding paragraph, "qualified investors" refers to units and individuals that meet the prescribed asset scale or income level, possess the corresponding risk identification and risk-bearing capabilities, and whose subscription amount for fund shares is not less than the prescribed minimum.

The specific standards for qualified investors shall be stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 88** Unless otherwise agreed in the fund contract, a privately offered fund shall be custodied by a fund custodian.

**Article 89** An institution acting as the fund management institution for a privately offered fund shall register with the fund industry association and submit its basic information in accordance with regulations.

**Article 90** Without registration, no unit or individual may use the words "fund" or "fund management" or similar names to engage in securities investment activities, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

**Article 91** Privately offered funds shall not raise funds from units or individuals other than qualified investors, and shall not promote or solicit to unspecified persons through public media such as newspapers, radio, television, the internet, or through lectures, reports, analysis meetings, or other similar means.

**Article 92** For a privately offered fund, a fund contract shall be formulated and signed. The fund contract shall include the following contents:

(1) The rights and obligations of the fund share holders, the fund management institution, and the fund custodian;
(2) The operational model of the fund;
(3) The method, amount, and subscription period for capital contributions to the fund;
(4) The investment scope, investment strategy, and investment restrictions of the fund;
(5) The principles and implementation methods for distributing fund income;
(6) The relevant expenses borne by the fund;
(7) The content and method of providing fund information;
(8) The procedures and methods for subscribing, redeeming, or transferring fund shares;
(9) The causes and procedures for amending, rescinding, or terminating the fund contract;
(10) The method for liquidating fund property;
(11) Other matters agreed upon by the parties.

The transfer of fund shares by a fund share holder shall comply with the provisions of Articles 87 and 91 of this Law.

**Article 93** In accordance with the provisions of the fund contract, for a privately offered fund, some fund share holders may act as the fund management institution responsible for the fund's investment management activities and shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the fund property when the fund property is insufficient to pay off its debts.

For the privately offered fund stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the fund contract shall also specify:

(1) The names or titles and domiciles of the fund share holders bearing unlimited joint and several liability and other fund share holders;
(2) The conditions for the removal and procedures for the replacement of fund share holders bearing unlimited joint and several liability;
(3) The conditions, procedures, and related responsibilities for the addition or withdrawal of fund share holders;
(4) The procedures for the conversion of fund share holders bearing unlimited joint and several liability and other fund share holders.

**Article 94** After the fundraising for a privately offered fund is completed, the fund management institution shall file a report with the fund industry association. For funds whose total amount of raised funds or number of fund share holders reaches the prescribed standards, the fund industry association shall report to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

The securities investment of the property of a privately offered fund includes the purchase and sale of publicly issued shares of joint-stock companies, bonds, fund shares, and other securities and their derivatives stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 95** The fund management institution and the fund custodian shall provide fund information to fund share holders in accordance with the provisions of the fund contract.

**Article 96** A fund management institution specializing in the management of privately offered funds may, upon approval by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, engage in the management of publicly offered funds if its shareholders, senior management, operational period, and managed fund asset scale meet the prescribed conditions.

## Chapter XI Fund Service Institutions

**Article 97** Institutions providing fund services such as sales, sales payment, share registration, valuation, investment advisory, evaluation, and information technology system services for publicly offered funds shall register or file with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with its regulations.

**Article 98** Fund sales institutions shall fully disclose investment risks to investors and sell fund products of different risk levels based on the investors' risk tolerance.

**Article 99** Fund sales payment institutions shall handle the transfer of fund sales settlement funds in accordance with regulations, ensuring the safe and timely transfer of such funds.

**Article 100** Fund sales settlement funds and fund shares are independent of the proprietary property of the fund sales institution, fund sales payment institution, or fund share registration institution. In the event of bankruptcy or liquidation of the fund sales institution, fund sales payment institution, or fund share registration institution, fund sales settlement funds and fund shares shall not be part of their bankruptcy or liquidation property. Unless due to the investor's own debts or other circumstances stipulated by law, fund sales settlement funds and fund shares shall not be sealed up, frozen, deducted, or subject to compulsory enforcement.

Fund sales institutions, fund sales payment institutions, and fund share registration institutions shall ensure the safety and independence of fund sales settlement funds and fund shares, and prohibit any unit or individual from misappropriating fund sales settlement funds or fund shares in any form.

**Article 101** A fund management institution may entrust a fund service institution to handle matters such as share registration, accounting, valuation, and investment advisory on its behalf. A fund custodian may entrust a fund service institution to handle matters such as accounting, valuation, and verification on its behalf. However, the responsibilities that the fund management institution or fund custodian shall bear according to law shall not be exempted due to such entrustment.

**Article 102** Data registered by a fund share registration institution on electronic media serves as the basis for the ownership of rights by fund share holders. If a fund share holder pledges fund shares, the pledge right is established when the fund share registration institution handles the pledge registration.

The fund share registration institution shall properly preserve the registration data and back up data such as the names, identity information, and details of fund shares of fund share holders to an institution recognized by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. The retention period shall be no less than twenty years from the date of account cancellation.

The fund share registration institution shall ensure the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the registration data and shall not conceal, forge, alter, or destroy it.

**Article 103** Fund investment advisory institutions and their personnel providing fund investment advisory services shall have a reasonable basis, truthfully state their service capabilities and business performance, shall not promise or guarantee investment returns in any way, and shall not harm the legitimate rights and interests of the service recipients.

**Article 104** Fund evaluation institutions and their personnel shall be objective and fair, conduct fund evaluation business in accordance with business rules formulated according to law, prohibit misleading investors, and prevent potential conflicts of interest.

**Article 105** The information technology systems of fund management institutions, fund custodians, and fund service institutions shall meet the prescribed requirements. The securities regulatory authority of the State Council may require information technology system service providers to provide relevant materials regarding their information technology systems.

**Article 106** Law firms and accounting firms, entrusted by fund management institutions or fund custodians to issue documents such as legal opinions, audit reports, and internal control evaluation reports for relevant fund business activities, shall be diligent and responsible, and shall verify and validate the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the content of the documents and materials on which they rely. If the documents they produce or issue contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, causing losses to others' property, they shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the client.

**Article 107** Fund service institutions shall be diligent and responsible, perform their duties faithfully, establish risk management systems such as emergency plans and disaster recovery systems, and shall not disclose non-public information related to fund share holders or fund investment operations.

## Chapter XII Fund Industry Association

**Article 108** The fund industry association is a self-regulatory organization for the securities investment fund industry and is a social organization with legal personality.

Fund management institutions and fund custodians shall join the fund industry association. Fund service institutions may join the fund industry association.

**Article 109** The governing body of the fund industry association is the general meeting of members, which consists of all members.

The fund industry association shall have a council. Members of the council shall be elected in accordance with the provisions of the association's charter.

**Article 110** The charter of the fund industry association shall be formulated by the general meeting of members and filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 111** The fund industry association shall perform the following duties:

(1) Educate and organize members to comply with laws and administrative regulations related to securities investment, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors;
(2) Safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of members according to law, and reflect members' suggestions and demands;
(3) Formulate and implement industry self-regulatory rules, supervise and inspect the professional conduct of members and their practitioners, and impose disciplinary sanctions in accordance with regulations for violations of self-regulatory rules and the association's charter;
(4) Formulate industry practice standards and business norms, organize professional examinations, qualification management, and business training for fund practitioners;
(5) Provide member services, organize industry exchanges, promote industry innovation, and conduct industry publicity and investor education activities;
(6) Mediate fund business disputes between members, and between members and clients;
(7) Handle the registration and filing of privately offered funds according to law;
(8) Other duties stipulated in the association's charter.

## Chapter XIII Supervision and Administration

**Article 112** The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall perform the following duties according to law:

(1) Formulate rules and regulations for the supervision and administration of securities investment fund activities, and exercise the power of approval, verification, or registration;
(2) Handle fund filings;
(3) Supervise and administer the engagement of fund management institutions, fund custodians, and other institutions in securities investment fund activities, investigate and punish illegal acts, and publicly announce the results;
(4) Formulate qualification standards and codes of conduct for fund practitioners, and supervise their implementation;
(5) Supervise and inspect the disclosure of fund information;
(6) Guide and supervise the activities of the fund industry association;
(7) Other duties stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 113** When performing its duties according to law, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council has the right to take the following measures:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of fund management institutions, fund custodians, and fund service institutions, and require them to submit relevant business materials;
(2) Enter the premises where suspected illegal acts occur to investigate and collect evidence;
(3) Question the parties involved and the units and individuals related to the investigated incident, and require them to provide explanations on matters related to the investigated incident;
(4) Inspect and copy materials such as property rights registrations and communication records related to the investigated incident;
(5) Inspect and copy securities transaction records, registration and transfer records, financial accounting materials, and other relevant documents and materials of the parties involved and the units and individuals related to the investigated incident; seal up documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed;
(6) Inquire into the capital accounts, securities accounts, and bank accounts of the parties involved and the units and individuals related to the investigated incident; if there is evidence proving that illegal funds, securities, or other property involved in the case has been or may be transferred or concealed, or that important evidence has been concealed, forged, or destroyed, freeze or seal them with the approval of the principal responsible person of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council;(7) When investigating major securities violations such as market manipulation or insider trading, with the approval of the principal responsible person of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, restrict the securities trading of the parties involved in the investigated incident, but the restriction period shall not exceed fifteen trading days; in complex cases, it may be extended by fifteen trading days.

**Article 114** When performing their duties according to law, conducting investigations or inspections, staff members of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall be at least two in number and shall present their legal credentials; they are obligated to keep confidential any trade secrets learned during the investigation or inspection.

**Article 115** Staff members of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall be loyal to their duties, act according to law, be fair and incorruptible, accept supervision, and shall not use their positions for personal gain.

**Article 116** When the securities regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties according to law, the units and individuals being investigated or inspected shall cooperate, truthfully provide relevant documents and materials, and shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal information.

**Article 117** If, while performing its duties according to law, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council discovers that an illegal act is suspected of constituting a crime, it shall transfer the case to the judicial authorities for handling.

**Article 118** Staff members of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall not hold positions in regulated institutions during their term of office or within the period stipulated by the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China after leaving office.

## Chapter XIV Legal Liability

**Article 119** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, establishes a fund management company without approval or engages in the management of publicly offered funds without verification, shall be banned or ordered to rectify by the securities regulatory authority, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, a fine of not less than one hundred thousand yuan but not more than one million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than thirty thousand yuan but not more than three hundred thousand yuan.

If a fund management company, in violation of this Law, changes shareholders holding more than five percent of the equity, actual controllers, or other material matters without authorization, it shall be ordered to rectify, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than five hundred thousand yuan, a fine of not less than fifty thousand yuan but not more than five hundred thousand yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisor shall be given a warning and fined not less than thirty thousand yuan but not more than one hundred thousand yuan.

**Article 120** Directors, supervisors, senior management, and other personnel of a fund management institution, and senior management and other personnel of the dedicated fund custody department of a fund custodian, who fail to make declarations as required by Article 17, Paragraph 1 of this Law, shall be ordered to rectify and fined not less than thirty thousand yuan but not more than one hundred thousand yuan.

If a fund management institution or fund custodian violates Article 17, Paragraph 2 of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify and fined not less than one hundred thousand yuan but not more than one million yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, suspended or revoked from fund practice qualifications, and fined not less than thirty thousand yuan but not more than three hundred thousand yuan.Article 121 Where directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or other employees of a fund management institution, or senior management personnel or other employees of the specialized fund custody department of a fund custodian, violate Article 18 of this Law, they shall be ordered to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, they shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their fund practice qualifications shall be revoked.

Article 122 Where a fund management institution or fund custodian violates the provisions of this Law by failing to separately manage or maintain separate accounts for fund property, it shall be ordered to make corrections and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 123 Where a fund management institution, fund custodian, or their directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or other employees commit any of the acts listed in Article 20 of this Law, they shall be ordered to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, they shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if a fund management institution or fund custodian commits the above acts, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall also be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Any property and income obtained by a fund management institution, fund custodian, or their directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or other employees through embezzlement or misappropriation of fund property shall be included in the fund property. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 124 Where a shareholder or actual controller of a fund management institution violates Article 23 of this Law, they shall be ordered to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, they shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund or securities practice qualifications suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 125 Where a party engages in fund custody business without approval, it shall be ordered to cease operations, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 126 Where a fund management institution or fund custodian violates the provisions of this Law by making capital contributions to or holding shares in each other, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 127 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law by publicly or in a disguised form publicly raising funds, it shall be ordered to cease operations, return the raised funds plus interest calculated at the bank deposit rate for the same period, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one percent but not more than five percent of the amount of raised funds. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 128 Where a party violates the provisions of Article 59 of this Law by using raised funds, it shall be ordered to return the funds, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, it shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 129 Where a fund management institution or fund custodian commits any of the acts listed in items 1 to 5 and item 7 of paragraph 1 of Article 73 of this Law, or violates the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 73 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections and be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

If a fund management institution or fund custodian commits the acts in the preceding paragraph, any property and income obtained through the use of fund property shall be included in the fund property. However, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 130 Where a fund management institution or fund custodian commits the act listed in item 6 of paragraph 1 of Article 73 of this Law, in addition to being punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked.

Article 131 Where a fund information disclosure obligor fails to disclose fund information in accordance with the law, or the disclosed information contains false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 132 Where a fund management institution or fund custodian fails to convene a meeting of fund share holders in accordance with the provisions, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and have their fund practice qualifications suspended or revoked.

Article 133 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law by engaging in securities investment activities without registration, using the words "fund" or "fund management" or similar names, it shall have its illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 134 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law by failing to file a record after the completion of a non-publicly offered fund raising, the fund management institution shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 135 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law by non-publicly raising funds from or transferring fund shares to entities or individuals other than qualified investors, it shall have its illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 136 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law by engaging in fund service business for publicly offered funds without authorization, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 300,000 yuan, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 137 Where a fund sales institution fails to fully disclose investment risks to investors and misleads them into purchasing fund products that are not commensurate with their risk tolerance, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease its fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 138 Where a fund sales payment institution fails to transfer fund sales settlement funds in accordance with the provisions, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease its fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 139 Where a party misappropriates fund sales settlement funds or fund shares, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 140 Where a fund share registration institution fails to properly preserve or back up fund share registration data, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease its fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

If a fund share registration institution conceals, forges, alters, or destroys fund share registration data, it shall be ordered to make corrections, fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan, and ordered to cease its fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 141 Where a fund investment advisory institution, fund evaluation institution, or their personnel violate the provisions of this Law by providing investment advisory or fund evaluation services, they shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be ordered to cease their fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 142 Where an information technology system service institution fails to provide relevant information technology system materials to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with the provisions, or provides false or materially omitted information technology system materials, it shall be ordered to make corrections and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan.

Article 143 Where an accounting firm or law firm fails to exercise due diligence, and the documents it issues contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its business income confiscated, have its relevant business permits suspended or revoked, and be fined not less than one time but not more than five times the business income. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 144 Where a fund service institution fails to establish risk management systems such as contingency plans and disaster backup systems, or discloses non-public information related to fund share holders or fund investment operations, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to cease its fund service business. The directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning, have their fund practice qualifications revoked, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 145 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law and causes damage to fund property, fund share holders, or investors, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

If a fund management institution or fund custodian, in the course of performing their respective duties, violates the provisions of this Law or the fund contract and causes damage to fund property or fund share holders, they shall each bear compensation liability for their respective actions in accordance with the law; if their joint actions cause damage to fund property or fund share holders, they shall bear joint and several compensation liability.

Article 146 Where a staff member of the securities regulatory authority neglects their duties, abuses their power, engages in malpractice for personal gain, or uses their position to solicit or accept property from others, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 147 Where a party refuses or obstructs the securities regulatory authority or its staff members from lawfully exercising their powers of supervision, inspection, or investigation without using violence or threats, they shall be given public security penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 148 Where a party violates the relevant provisions of laws, administrative regulations, or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, and the circumstances are serious, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may impose measures to ban the relevant responsible personnel from the securities market.

Article 149 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 150 Where a party violates the provisions of this Law and should bear civil compensation liability and pay fines or penalties, and its property is insufficient to pay both, civil compensation liability shall be borne first.

Article 151 In accordance with the provisions of this Law, the civil compensation liability and fines or penalties that a fund management institution, fund custodian, or fund service institution should bear shall be borne by the fund management institution, fund custodian, or fund service institution with its own property.

All fines, penalties, and confiscated illegal gains collected in accordance with the law shall be turned over to the state treasury in full.

## Chapter 15 Supplementary Provisions

Article 152 The raising of funds within the territory of the People's Republic of China for investment in overseas securities, as well as the investment in securities within the territory by qualified foreign investors, shall be approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. Specific measures shall be formulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 153 This Law applies to the securities investment activities of companies or partnerships established for the purpose of conducting securities investment activities through public or non-public fund raising, where the assets are managed by a fund management institution or a general partner.

Article 154 This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2013.
