# People's Republic of China Tendering and Bidding Law

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 30, 1999

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the "Tendering and Bidding Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 27, 2017

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating tendering and bidding activities, protecting national interests, public interests, and the legitimate rights and interests of parties involved in tendering and bidding activities, improving economic efficiency, and ensuring project quality.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to tendering and bidding activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The following construction projects, including their survey, design, construction, supervision, and the procurement of key equipment and materials related to the project construction, conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China, must be subject to tender:

(1) Projects involving large-scale infrastructure, public utilities, and other projects that affect public interests and public safety;
(2) Projects wholly or partially invested with state-owned funds or financed by the state;
(3) Projects using loans or aid funds from international organizations or foreign governments.

The specific scope and scale standards for the projects listed in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated by the development planning department of the State Council jointly with relevant departments of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Where laws or the State Council stipulate the scope of other projects that must be subject to tender, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 4 No unit or individual may break up a project that must be subject to tender according to law into smaller parts or evade tender in any other way.

Article 5 Tendering and bidding activities shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, and good faith.

Article 6 For projects that must be subject to tender according to law, the tendering and bidding activities shall not be restricted by region or department. No unit or individual may illegally restrict or exclude legal persons or other organizations from outside their region or system from participating in bidding, nor may they illegally interfere with tendering and bidding activities in any way.

Article 7 Tendering and bidding activities and the parties involved shall accept supervision implemented according to law.

Relevant administrative supervision departments shall supervise tendering and bidding activities according to law and investigate and deal with illegal acts in tendering and bidding activities according to law.

The specific division of powers for administrative supervision of tendering and bidding activities and the relevant departments shall be stipulated by the State Council.

## Chapter II: Tender

Article 8 A tenderer is a legal person or other organization that proposes a tender project and conducts the tender according to the provisions of this Law.

Article 9 Where a tender project requires project approval procedures according to relevant state regulations, the approval procedures shall be completed first, and approval obtained.

The tenderer shall have the corresponding funds for the tender project or have secured the source of funds, and shall truthfully state this in the tender documents.

Article 10 Tenders are divided into open tender and invited tender.

Open tender refers to a tender where the tenderer invites unspecified legal persons or other organizations to bid by means of a tender announcement.

Invited tender refers to a tender where the tenderer invites specific legal persons or other organizations to bid by means of a bid invitation letter.

Article 11 For national key projects determined by the development planning department of the State Council and local key projects determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, if open tender is not suitable, invited tender may be conducted with the approval of the development planning department of the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 12 A tenderer has the right to choose a tender agency on its own and entrust it with the tender matters. No unit or individual may designate a tender agency for the tenderer in any way.

If a tenderer has the capability to prepare tender documents and organize bid evaluation, it may handle the tender matters on its own. No unit or individual may force it to entrust a tender agency with the tender matters.

For projects that must be subject to tender according to law, if the tenderer handles the tender matters on its own, it shall file a record with the relevant administrative supervision department.

Article 13 A tender agency is a social intermediary organization established according to law, engaged in tender agency business, and providing related services.

A tender agency shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a business premises and corresponding funds for conducting tender agency business;
(2) Having the corresponding professional capability to prepare tender documents and organize bid evaluation.

Article 14 A tender agency shall not have a subordinate relationship or other interest relationship with administrative organs or other state organs.

Article 15 A tender agency shall handle tender matters within the scope entrusted by the tenderer and shall comply with the provisions of this Law concerning the tenderer.

Article 16 If a tenderer adopts the open tender method, it shall issue a tender announcement. The tender announcement for a project that must be subject to tender according to law shall be published through state-designated newspapers, information networks, or other media.

The tender announcement shall state the name and address of the tenderer, the nature, quantity, implementation location and time of the tender project, and the method for obtaining the tender documents, among other matters.

Article 17 If a tenderer adopts the invited tender method, it shall send bid invitation letters to three or more specific legal persons or other organizations that have the capability to undertake the tender project and have good credit standing.

The bid invitation letter shall state the matters specified in paragraph 2 of Article 16 of this Law.

Article 18 A tenderer may, based on the requirements of the tender project itself, require potential bidders to provide relevant qualification certification documents and performance records in the tender announcement or bid invitation letter, and conduct qualification review of potential bidders; where the state has provisions on the qualification conditions of bidders, those provisions shall prevail.

A tenderer shall not restrict or exclude potential bidders with unreasonable conditions, nor shall it discriminate against potential bidders.

Article 19 A tenderer shall prepare tender documents based on the characteristics and needs of the tender project. The tender documents shall include all substantive requirements and conditions such as the technical requirements of the tender project, the criteria for bidder qualification review, bid quotation requirements, and bid evaluation standards, as well as the main terms of the contract to be signed.

Where the state has provisions on the technology and standards of the tender project, the tenderer shall propose corresponding requirements in the tender documents according to those provisions.

If the tender project needs to be divided into bid sections and the construction period determined, the tenderer shall reasonably divide the bid sections and determine the construction period, and state this in the tender documents.

Article 20 The tender documents shall not require or specify a particular producer or supplier, nor contain other content that tends to favor or exclude potential bidders.

Article 21 A tenderer may, based on the specific circumstances of the tender project, organize potential bidders to inspect the project site.

Article 22 A tenderer shall not disclose to others the names and numbers of potential bidders who have obtained the tender documents, nor other information related to the tender that may affect fair competition.

If the tenderer sets a base price, the base price must be kept confidential.

Article 23 If a tenderer makes necessary clarifications or modifications to the issued tender documents, it shall notify all recipients of the tender documents in writing at least fifteen days before the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents. The content of such clarifications or modifications shall form part of the tender documents.

Article 24 A tenderer shall determine a reasonable time for bidders to prepare their bid documents; however, for projects that must be subject to tender according to law, the minimum period from the date of issuance of the tender documents to the deadline for submission of bid documents shall not be less than twenty days.

## Chapter III: Bid

Article 25 A bidder is a legal person or other organization that responds to the tender and participates in the bidding competition.

For scientific research projects subject to tender according to law, if individuals are allowed to participate in the bidding, the provisions of this Law concerning bidders shall apply to the bidding individuals.

Article 26 A bidder shall have the capability to undertake the tender project; where relevant state regulations or the tender documents stipulate qualification conditions for bidders, the bidder shall meet the specified qualification conditions.

Article 27 A bidder shall prepare bid documents in accordance with the requirements of the tender documents. The bid documents shall respond to the substantive requirements and conditions set forth in the tender documents.

If the tender project involves construction, the content of the bid documents shall include the resumes and performance records of the proposed project manager and key technical personnel, as well as the machinery and equipment to be used for completing the tender project.

Article 28 A bidder shall deliver the bid documents to the bid submission location before the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents. Upon receiving the bid documents, the tenderer shall sign for and keep them, and shall not open them. If there are fewer than three bidders, the tenderer shall re-tender according to this Law.

Bid documents delivered after the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents shall be rejected by the tenderer.

Article 29 Before the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents, a bidder may supplement, modify, or withdraw the submitted bid documents, and shall notify the tenderer in writing. The content of the supplements or modifications shall form part of the bid documents.

Article 30 If a bidder intends to subcontract part of the non-primary, non-critical work of the winning project after winning the bid, based on the actual project situation stated in the tender documents, it shall state this in the bid documents.

Article 31 Two or more legal persons or other organizations may form a consortium and bid together as one bidder.

Each party to the consortium shall have the corresponding capability to undertake the tender project; where relevant state regulations or the tender documents stipulate qualification conditions for bidders, each party to the consortium shall meet the specified corresponding qualification conditions. For a consortium formed by units of the same profession, the qualification level shall be determined based on the unit with the lower qualification level.

The parties to the consortium shall sign a joint bidding agreement, clearly stipulating the work and responsibilities each party is to undertake, and submit the joint bidding agreement together with the bid documents to the tenderer. If the consortium wins the bid, the parties to the consortium shall jointly sign a contract with the tenderer and bear joint and several liability to the tenderer for the winning project.

A tenderer shall not force bidders to form a consortium to bid together, nor restrict competition among bidders.

Article 32 Bidders shall not collude with each other on bid prices, nor exclude fair competition from other bidders, harming the legitimate rights and interests of the tenderer or other bidders.

Bidders shall not collude with the tenderer in bidding, harming national interests, public interests, or the legitimate rights and interests of others.

Bidders are prohibited from seeking to win the bid by bribing the tenderer or members of the bid evaluation committee.

Article 33 Bidders shall not bid at a price below cost, nor bid in the name of others or use other fraudulent means to obtain the bid.

## Chapter IV: Bid Opening, Bid Evaluation, and Award of Contract

Article 34 Bid opening shall be conducted publicly at the same time as the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents; the bid opening location shall be the place predetermined in the tender documents.

Article 35 Bid opening shall be presided over by the tenderer, and all bidders shall be invited to participate.

Article 36 At the time of bid opening, the bidders or their elected representatives shall check the sealing condition of the bid documents; alternatively, a notary institution entrusted by the tenderer may check and notarize them. After confirmation of correctness, the staff shall open the seals publicly and announce the names of the bidders, bid prices, and other main contents of the bid documents.

All bid documents received by the tenderer before the deadline for submission of bid documents specified in the tender documents shall be opened and announced publicly at the time of bid opening.

The bid opening process shall be recorded and kept on file for future reference.

Article 37 Bid evaluation shall be the responsibility of the bid evaluation committee established by the tenderer according to law.

For projects that must be subject to tender according to law, the bid evaluation committee shall be composed of representatives of the tenderer and experts in relevant technical, economic, and other fields. The number of members shall be an odd number of five or more, of which experts in technical, economic, and other fields shall account for no less than two-thirds of the total membership.

The experts mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall have worked in the relevant field for at least eight years and hold a senior professional title or have an equivalent professional level. They shall be determined by the tenderer from the expert rosters provided by relevant departments of the State Council or relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, or from the expert lists of relevant specialties in the expert database of tender agencies; for general tender projects, a random selection method may be adopted; for special tender projects, the tenderer may directly determine the experts.

Persons with a conflict of interest with a bidder shall not enter the bid evaluation committee for the relevant project; those already on the committee shall be replaced.

The list of members of the bid evaluation committee shall be kept confidential until the winning bidder is determined.

Article 38 The tenderer shall take necessary measures to ensure that bid evaluation is conducted under strict confidentiality.

No unit or individual may illegally interfere with or influence the process and results of bid evaluation.

Article 39 The bid evaluation committee may require bidders to make necessary clarifications or explanations regarding unclear content in the bid documents, but such clarifications or explanations shall not go beyond the scope of the bid documents or change the substantive content of the bid documents.

Article 40 The bid evaluation committee shall evaluate and compare the bid documents in accordance with the bid evaluation standards and methods specified in the tender documents; if a base price is set, it shall be taken as a reference. After completing the bid evaluation, the bid evaluation committee shall submit a written bid evaluation report to the tenderer and recommend qualified candidates for the winning bid.

The tenderer shall determine the winning bidder based on the written bid evaluation report submitted by the bid evaluation committee and the recommended candidates. The tenderer may also authorize the bid evaluation committee to directly determine the winning bidder.

Where the State Council has special provisions on bid evaluation for specific tender projects, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 41 The bid of the winning bidder shall meet one of the following conditions:

(1) It can best meet the comprehensive evaluation standards specified in the tender documents;
(2) It can meet the substantive requirements of the tender documents, and its evaluated bid price is the lowest, except where the bid price is below cost.

Article 42 If the bid evaluation committee, after evaluation, considers that all bids do not meet the requirements of the tender documents, it may reject all bids.

If all bids for a project that must be subject to tender according to law are rejected, the tenderer shall re-tender according to this Law.

Article 43 Before determining the winning bidder, the tenderer shall not negotiate with bidders on substantive content such as bid prices or bid proposals.

Article 44 Members of the bid evaluation committee shall perform their duties objectively and impartially, abide by professional ethics, and bear personal responsibility for the evaluation opinions they provide.

Members of the bid evaluation committee shall not have private contact with bidders, nor accept property or other benefits from bidders.

Members of the bid evaluation committee and relevant staff involved in bid evaluation shall not disclose the evaluation and comparison of bid documents, the recommendation of candidates for the winning bid, or other information related to the bid evaluation.

Article 45 After the winning bidder is determined, the tenderer shall issue a bid-winning notice to the winning bidder and simultaneously notify all unsuccessful bidders of the result.

The bid-winning notice has legal effect on both the tenderer and the winning bidder. After the bid-winning notice is issued, if the tenderer changes the result of the winning bid, or if the winning bidder abandons the winning project, they shall bear legal liability according to law.

Article 46 The tenderer and the winning bidder shall, within thirty days from the date of issuance of the bid-winning notice, enter into a written contract based on the tender documents and the bid documents of the winning bidder. The tenderer and the winning bidder shall not enter into any other agreement that deviates from the substantive content of the contract.

If the tender documents require the winning bidder to submit a performance bond, the winning bidder shall do so.

Article 47 For projects that must be subject to tender according to law, the tenderer shall, within fifteen days from the date of determining the winning bidder, submit a written report on the tendering and bidding situation to the relevant administrative supervision department.

Article 48 The winning bidder shall perform its obligations according to the contract and complete the winning project. The winning bidder shall not transfer the winning project to others, nor break up the winning project and transfer it to others separately.

The winning bidder may, according to the contract or with the consent of the tenderer, subcontract part of the non-primary, non-critical work of the winning project to others for completion. The subcontractor shall have the corresponding qualification conditions and shall not subcontract again.

The winning bidder shall be responsible to the tenderer for the subcontracted project, and the subcontractor shall bear joint and several liability for the subcontracted project.

## Chapter V: Legal Liability

Article 49 In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a project that must be subject to tender is not tendered, or if a project that must be subject to tender is broken up into smaller parts or tender is evaded in any other way, the violator shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified time limit and may be fined not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the project contract amount; for projects wholly or partially using state-owned funds, project execution may be suspended or fund allocation may be suspended; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel of the unit shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 50 If a tender agency, in violation of the provisions of this Law, discloses information and materials related to tendering and bidding activities that should be kept confidential, or colludes with the tenderer or bidders to harm national interests, public interests, or the legitimate rights and interests of others, it shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan and not more than 250,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel of the unit shall be fined not less than five percent and not more than ten percent of the fine imposed on the unit; if there are illegal gains, they shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the agency shall be prohibited from acting as an agent for projects that must be subject to tender according to law for one to two years, and this shall be announced, up to the revocation of its business license by the administrative authority for industry and commerce; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law. If losses are caused to others, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

If the acts listed in the preceding paragraph affect the result of the winning bid, the winning bid shall be invalid.

Article 51 If a tenderer restricts or excludes potential bidders with unreasonable conditions, discriminates against potential bidders, forces bidders to form a consortium to bid together, or restricts competition among bidders, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not less than 10,000 yuan and not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 52 If a tenderer for a project that must be subject to tender according to law discloses to others the names or numbers of potential bidders who have obtained the tender documents, or other information related to the tender that may affect fair competition, or discloses the base price, it shall be given a warning and may also be fined not less than 10,000 yuan and not more than 100,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel of the unit shall be given sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If the acts listed in the preceding paragraph affect the result of the winning bid, the winning bid shall be invalid.**Article 53** Where a bidder colludes with other bidders in submitting bids or colludes with the tenderer in submitting a bid, or where a bidder seeks to win a bid by offering bribes to the tenderer or members of the bid evaluation committee, the bid shall be invalidated, and a fine of not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the value of the bid-winning project shall be imposed. The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons of the entity shall be fined not less than five percent and not more than ten percent of the fine imposed on the entity. Any illegal income shall be confiscated. If the circumstances are serious, the bidder's qualification to participate in bidding for projects that must be subject to tender according to law shall be revoked for a period of one to two years, and a public announcement shall be made, up to and including the revocation of the business license by the administrative authority for industry and commerce. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law. If losses are caused to others, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 54** Where a bidder wins a bid by submitting a bid in the name of another person or by other fraudulent means, the bid shall be invalidated. If losses are caused to the tenderer, compensation liability shall be borne according to law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If a bidder for a project that must be subject to tender according to law commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph but the act does not constitute a crime, a fine of not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the value of the bid-winning project shall be imposed. The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons of the entity shall be fined not less than five percent and not more than ten percent of the fine imposed on the entity. Any illegal income shall be confiscated. If the circumstances are serious, the bidder's qualification to participate in bidding for projects that must be subject to tender according to law shall be revoked for a period of one to three years, and a public announcement shall be made, up to and including the revocation of the business license by the administrative authority for industry and commerce.

**Article 55** For a project that must be subject to tender according to law, if the tenderer, in violation of the provisions of this Law, negotiates with a bidder on substantive content such as the bid price or the bid proposal, a warning shall be given, and the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons of the entity shall be given sanctions according to law.

If the acts listed in the preceding paragraph affect the bid-winning result, the bid shall be invalidated.

**Article 56** Where a member of the bid evaluation committee accepts property or other benefits from a bidder, or where a member of the bid evaluation committee or a relevant staff member participating in the bid evaluation discloses to others the evaluation and comparison of bid documents, the recommendation of bid-winning candidates, or other information related to the bid evaluation, a warning shall be given, the accepted property shall be confiscated, and a concurrent fine of not less than 3,000 yuan and not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed. The member of the bid evaluation committee who has committed any of the listed illegal acts shall be disqualified from serving as a member of the bid evaluation committee and shall not participate in the bid evaluation of any project that must be subject to tender according to law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 57** Where the tenderer determines the winning bidder from among candidates other than those lawfully recommended by the bid evaluation committee, or where, for a project that must be subject to tender according to law, the tenderer determines the winning bidder on its own after all bids have been rejected by the bid evaluation committee, the bid shall be invalidated, a correction order shall be issued, and a fine of not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the value of the bid-winning project may be imposed. The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons of the entity shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 58** Where the winning bidder transfers the bid-winning project to others, dismembers the bid-winning project and transfers the parts to others separately, subcontracts part of the main structure or key work of the bid-winning project to others in violation of the provisions of this Law, or where the subcontractor further subcontracts, the transfer or subcontract shall be invalidated, and a fine of not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the value of the transferred or subcontracted project shall be imposed. Any illegal income shall be confiscated. A suspension of business for rectification may be ordered. If the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked by the administrative authority for industry and commerce.

**Article 59** Where the tenderer and the winning bidder fail to conclude a contract in accordance with the tender documents and the winning bidder's bid documents, or where the tenderer and the winning bidder conclude an agreement that deviates from the substantive content of the contract, a correction order shall be issued. A fine of not less than five per thousand and not more than ten per thousand of the value of the bid-winning project may be imposed.

**Article 60** Where the winning bidder fails to perform the contract concluded with the tenderer, the performance bond shall not be refunded. If the losses caused to the tenderer exceed the amount of the performance bond, compensation for the excess portion shall also be made. If no performance bond was submitted, the winning bidder shall bear compensation liability for the tenderer's losses.

If the winning bidder fails to perform its obligations in accordance with the contract concluded with the tenderer, and the circumstances are serious, its qualification to participate in bidding for projects that must be subject to tender according to law shall be revoked for a period of two to five years, and a public announcement shall be made, up to and including the revocation of the business license by the administrative authority for industry and commerce.

If the contract cannot be performed due to force majeure, the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall not apply.

**Article 61** The administrative penalties stipulated in this Chapter shall be decided by the relevant administrative supervision departments designated by the State Council, except where this Law has already stipulated the organ that imposes the administrative penalty.

**Article 62** Where any entity, in violation of the provisions of this Law, restricts or excludes legal persons or other organizations from outside its region or system from participating in bidding, designates a bidding agency for the tenderer, forces the tenderer to entrust a bidding agency to handle bidding matters, or interferes with bidding and tendering activities by other means, a correction order shall be issued. The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons of the entity shall be given sanctions such as warnings, demerits, or major demerits according to law. If the circumstances are relatively serious, sanctions such as demotion, dismissal, or expulsion shall be given according to law.

If an individual uses his or her position to commit the illegal acts in the preceding paragraph, liability shall be pursued in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 63** Where a state organ staff member who is legally responsible for administrative supervision over bidding and tendering activities engages in graft, abuses power, or neglects duty, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law. If the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be given according to law.

**Article 64** Where a project that must be subject to tender according to law violates the provisions of this Law, resulting in the invalidation of the winning bid, a new winning bidder shall be re-determined from among the remaining bidders in accordance with the bid-winning conditions stipulated in this Law, or a new tender shall be conducted in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter 6 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 65** Where a bidder or other interested party believes that a bidding and tendering activity does not conform to the relevant provisions of this Law, it has the right to raise an objection to the tenderer or to file a complaint with the relevant administrative supervision department according to law.

**Article 66** For projects involving national security, state secrets, emergency rescue and disaster relief, or special circumstances such as projects using poverty alleviation funds for work-relief programs requiring the use of migrant workers, where bidding is not suitable, bidding may be dispensed with in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

**Article 67** For projects using loans or aid funds from international organizations or foreign governments, if the lender or fund provider has different provisions on the specific conditions and procedures for bidding and tendering, such provisions may apply, provided they do not violate the public interests of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 68** This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2000.
