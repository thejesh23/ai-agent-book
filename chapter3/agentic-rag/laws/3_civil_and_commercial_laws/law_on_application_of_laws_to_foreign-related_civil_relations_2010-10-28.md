# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Application of Laws to Foreign-Related Civil Relations

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on October 28, 2010

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to clarifying the application of laws to foreign-related civil relations, reasonably resolving foreign-related civil disputes, and safeguarding the lawful rights and interests of the parties.

Article 2 The laws applicable to foreign-related civil relations shall be determined in accordance with this Law. Where any other law provides otherwise for the application of laws to foreign-related civil relations, such provisions shall prevail.

Where neither this Law nor any other law provides for the application of laws to a foreign-related civil relation, the law most closely connected with the foreign-related civil relation shall apply.

Article 3 The parties may, in accordance with the provisions of the law, expressly choose the law applicable to a foreign-related civil relation.

Article 4 Where a mandatory provision of the law of the People's Republic of China exists concerning a foreign-related civil relation, such mandatory provision shall directly apply.

Article 5 Where the application of a foreign law would harm the social and public interests of the People's Republic of China, the law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

Article 6 Where a foreign law applies to a foreign-related civil relation and different laws are in force in different regions of that country, the law of the region most closely connected with the foreign-related civil relation shall apply.

Article 7 The statute of limitations shall be governed by the law applicable to the relevant foreign-related civil relation.

Article 8 The characterization of a foreign-related civil relation shall be governed by the law of the forum.

Article 9 A foreign law applicable to a foreign-related civil relation shall not include the choice-of-law rules of that country.

Article 10 A foreign law applicable to a foreign-related civil relation shall be ascertained by the people's court, arbitration institution, or administrative organ. Where the parties choose to apply a foreign law, they shall provide the law of that country.

If the foreign law cannot be ascertained or if no provision exists in that country's law, the law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

## Chapter II Civil Subjects

Article 11 The capacity for civil rights of a natural person shall be governed by the law of his/her habitual residence.

Article 12 The capacity for civil conduct of a natural person shall be governed by the law of his/her habitual residence.

Where a natural person engaging in civil activities has no capacity for civil conduct under the law of his/her habitual residence but has such capacity under the law of the place where the act is performed, the law of the place where the act is performed shall apply, except in matters relating to marriage, family, and succession.

Article 13 A declaration of disappearance or a declaration of death shall be governed by the law of the natural person's habitual residence.

Article 14 Matters such as the capacity for civil rights, capacity for civil conduct, organizational structure, and rights and obligations of shareholders of a legal person and its branches shall be governed by the law of the place of registration.

If the principal place of business of a legal person is inconsistent with its place of registration, the law of the principal place of business may apply. The habitual residence of a legal person shall be its principal place of business.

Article 15 The content of personality rights shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the right holder.

Article 16 Agency shall be governed by the law of the place where the agency act is performed, but the civil relationship between the principal and the agent shall be governed by the law of the place where the agency relationship arises.

The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to a mandate agency.

Article 17 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to a trust. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of the place where the trust property is located or the law of the place where the trust relationship arises shall apply.

Article 18 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to an arbitration agreement. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of the place where the arbitration institution is located or the law of the place of arbitration shall apply.

Article 19 Where the law of the country of nationality applies under this Law and a natural person has two or more nationalities, the law of the country of nationality where he/she has his/her habitual residence shall apply; if he/she has no habitual residence in any country of nationality, the law of the country of nationality with which he/she is most closely connected shall apply. If a natural person has no nationality or his/her nationality is unknown, the law of his/her habitual residence shall apply.

Article 20 Where the law of the habitual residence applies under this Law and the habitual residence of a natural person is unknown, the law of his/her current residence shall apply.

## Chapter III Marriage and Family

Article 21 The conditions for marriage shall be governed by the law of the common habitual residence of the parties; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of the common nationality shall apply; in the absence of a common nationality, if the marriage is concluded in the habitual residence or country of nationality of one party, the law of the place of marriage conclusion shall apply.

Article 22 The formalities for marriage shall be valid if they comply with the law of the place of marriage conclusion, the law of the habitual residence of either party, or the law of the country of nationality of either party.

Article 23 Personal relations between spouses shall be governed by the law of their common habitual residence; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of their common nationality shall apply.

Article 24 Property relations between spouses may be governed by the law of the habitual residence of either party, the law of the country of nationality of either party, or the law of the place where the principal property is located, as chosen by the parties by agreement. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of their common habitual residence shall apply; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of their common nationality shall apply.

Article 25 Personal and property relations between parents and children shall be governed by the law of their common habitual residence; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of the habitual residence of either party or the law of the country of nationality of either party that is more favorable for protecting the rights and interests of the weaker party shall apply.

Article 26 For divorce by agreement, the parties may agree to choose the law of the habitual residence of either party or the law of the country of nationality of either party. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of their common habitual residence shall apply; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of their common nationality shall apply; in the absence of a common nationality, the law of the place where the institution handling the divorce formalities is located shall apply.

Article 27 Divorce by litigation shall be governed by the law of the forum.

Article 28 The conditions and formalities for adoption shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the adopter and the adoptee. The validity of adoption shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the adopter at the time of adoption. The dissolution of an adoption relationship shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the adoptee at the time of adoption or the law of the forum.

Article 29 Maintenance shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of either party, the law of the country of nationality of either party, or the law of the place where the principal property is located, whichever is more favorable for protecting the rights and interests of the maintenance recipient.

Article 30 Guardianship shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of either party or the law of the country of nationality of either party, whichever is more favorable for protecting the rights and interests of the ward.

## Chapter IV Succession

Article 31 Intestate succession shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the decedent at the time of death, except that intestate succession to immovable property shall be governed by the law of the place where the immovable property is located.

Article 32 The form of a will shall be valid if it complies with the law of the habitual residence of the testator at the time of making the will or at the time of death, the law of the country of nationality of the testator, or the law of the place where the will-making act is performed.

Article 33 The validity of a will shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the testator at the time of making the will or at the time of death, or the law of the country of nationality of the testator.

Article 34 Matters such as the administration of an estate shall be governed by the law of the place where the estate is located.

Article 35 The ownership of an estate with no successor shall be governed by the law of the place where the estate is located at the time of the decedent's death.

## Chapter V Real Rights

Article 36 Real rights in immovable property shall be governed by the law of the place where the immovable property is located.

Article 37 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to real rights in movable property. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of the place where the movable property is located at the time of the legal fact shall apply.

Article 38 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to changes in real rights in movable property in transit. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of the place of destination shall apply.

Article 39 Negotiable securities shall be governed by the law of the place where the rights under the negotiable securities are realized or other law most closely connected with the negotiable securities.

Article 40 A pledge of rights shall be governed by the law of the place where the pledge is established.

## Chapter VI Obligations

Article 41 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to a contract. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of the habitual residence of the party whose performance best reflects the characteristics of the contract or other law most closely connected with the contract shall apply.

Article 42 A consumer contract shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the consumer; if the consumer chooses the law of the place where the goods or services are provided, or if the business operator has no relevant business activities in the consumer's habitual residence, the law of the place where the goods or services are provided shall apply.

Article 43 A labor contract shall be governed by the law of the place where the worker works; if the worker's place of work is difficult to determine, the law of the principal place of business of the employer shall apply. For labor dispatch, the law of the place where the dispatch occurs may apply.

Article 44 Tort liability shall be governed by the law of the place where the tortious act is committed, but if the parties have a common habitual residence, the law of their common habitual residence shall apply. If the parties agree to choose the applicable law after the occurrence of the tortious act, their agreement shall prevail.

Article 45 Product liability shall be governed by the law of the habitual residence of the injured party; if the injured party chooses the law of the principal place of business of the tortfeasor or the law of the place where the damage occurred, or if the tortfeasor has no relevant business activities in the habitual residence of the injured party, the law of the principal place of business of the tortfeasor or the law of the place where the damage occurred shall apply.

Article 46 Where personality rights such as the right to name, right to portrait, right to reputation, or right to privacy are infringed upon through the internet or by other means, the law of the habitual residence of the injured party shall apply.

Article 47 Unjust enrichment and voluntary service shall be governed by the law chosen by the parties by agreement. If the parties have not so chosen, the law of their common habitual residence shall apply; in the absence of a common habitual residence, the law of the place where the unjust enrichment or voluntary service occurred shall apply.

## Chapter VII Intellectual Property

Article 48 The ownership and content of intellectual property rights shall be governed by the law of the place where protection is sought.

Article 49 The parties may agree to choose the law applicable to the transfer and licensing of intellectual property rights. If the parties have not so chosen, the relevant provisions of this Law on contracts shall apply.

Article 50 Tort liability for infringement of intellectual property rights shall be governed by the law of the place where protection is sought, and the parties may also agree to choose the law of the forum after the occurrence of the tortious act.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 51 Where Articles 146 and 147 of the General Principles of the Civil Law of the People's Republic of China and Article 36 of the Law of Succession of the People's Republic of China are inconsistent with the provisions of this Law, this Law shall prevail.

Article 52 This Law shall come into force as of April 1, 2011.
