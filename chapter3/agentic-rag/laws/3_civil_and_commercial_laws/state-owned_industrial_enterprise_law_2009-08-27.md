# Law of the People's Republic of China on Industrial Enterprises Owned by the Whole People

Adopted at the First Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 13, 1988

Amended according to the Decision on Revising Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China to ensure the consolidation and development of the economy owned by the whole people, define the rights and obligations of industrial enterprises owned by the whole people, protect their lawful rights and interests, enhance their vitality, and promote socialist modernization.

Article 2 An industrial enterprise owned by the whole people (hereinafter referred to as the enterprise) is a socialist commodity production and operation unit that operates independently, assumes sole responsibility for its profits and losses, and conducts independent accounting in accordance with the law.

The property of the enterprise is owned by the whole people. The State, in accordance with the principle of separating ownership from managerial authority, grants the enterprise the right to manage and operate such property. The enterprise enjoys the rights to possess, use, and dispose of the property granted by the State for its management and operation in accordance with the law.

The enterprise obtains the status of a legal person in accordance with the law and bears civil liability with the property granted by the State for its management and operation.

The enterprise may adopt such forms of managerial responsibility systems as contracting and leasing, subject to the decision of the competent government department. (Deleted on August 27, 2009)

Article 3 The fundamental task of the enterprise is to develop commodity production, create wealth, increase accumulation, and meet the growing material and cultural needs of society in accordance with State plans and market demands.

Article 4 The enterprise must adhere to the principle of building socialist material civilization while simultaneously building socialist spiritual civilization, and foster a workforce that is idealistic, moral, cultured, and disciplined.

Article 5 The enterprise must comply with laws and regulations and uphold the socialist orientation.

Article 6 The enterprise must effectively utilize the property granted by the State for its management and operation to achieve asset appreciation; and pay taxes, fees, and profits in accordance with the law.

Article 7 The enterprise implements a system of factory manager responsibility.
The factory director exercises his functions and powers in accordance with the law, which are protected by law.
Article 8 The primary-level organizations of the Communist Party of China in the enterprise shall guarantee and supervise the implementation of the Party's and the State's principles and policies within the enterprise.

Article 9 The State safeguards the status of workers as masters of the enterprise, and the lawful rights and interests of workers are protected by law.

Article 10 The enterprise practices democratic management through the workers' congress and other forms.

Article 11 The enterprise trade union represents and protects the interests of the workers and carries out its work independently and autonomously in accordance with the law. The enterprise trade union organizes workers to participate in democratic management and democratic supervision.

The enterprise shall give full play to the role of young workers, female workers, and scientific and technical personnel.

Article 12 The enterprise must strengthen and improve its management and operation, implement an economic responsibility system, promote scientific and technological progress, practice strict economy, combat waste, improve economic efficiency, and promote the enterprise's transformation and development.

Article 13 The enterprise implements the principle of distribution according to work. Within the scope prescribed by law, the enterprise may adopt other forms of distribution.

Article 14 The property granted by the State to the enterprise for its management and operation is protected by law and shall not be infringed upon.

Article 15 The lawful rights and interests of the enterprise are protected by law and shall not be infringed upon.

## Chapter II Establishment, Modification, and Termination of Enterprises

Article 16 To establish an enterprise, it must be submitted to the government or the competent government department for examination and approval in accordance with the law and the regulations of the State Council. Upon examination, registration, and issuance of a business license by the administrative department for industry and commerce, the enterprise obtains the status of a legal person.

The enterprise shall engage in production and business operations within the scope of business approved and registered.

Article 17 The following conditions must be met for the establishment of an enterprise:

(1) Its products are needed by society.
(2) It has the necessary conditions for energy, raw materials, and transportation.
(3) It has its own name and premises for production and operation.
(4) It has funds that comply with State regulations.
(5) It has its own organizational structure.
(6) It has a clear scope of business.
(7) Other conditions stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 18 The merger or division of an enterprise shall be approved by the government or the competent government department in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 19 An enterprise shall be terminated for any of the following reasons:

(1) It is ordered to be dissolved due to violation of laws or regulations.
(2) The competent government department decides to dissolve it in accordance with the provisions of laws or regulations.
(3) It is declared bankrupt in accordance with the law.
(4) Other reasons.

Article 20 When an enterprise merges, divides, or terminates, its property must be protected, and its claims and debts must be liquidated in accordance with the law.

Article 21 The merger, division, or termination of an enterprise, as well as changes in registration items such as its scope of business, must be examined and registered by the administrative department for industry and commerce.

## Chapter III Rights and Obligations of Enterprises

Article 22 Under the guidance of the State plan, the enterprise has the right to independently arrange for the production of products needed by society or to provide services to society.

Article 23 The enterprise has the right to request adjustments to mandatory plans that lack the necessary planned supply of materials or arrangements for product sales.

The enterprise has the right to accept or reject production tasks arranged by any department or unit outside the mandatory plan. (Deleted on August 27, 2009)

Article 24 The enterprise has the right to sell its own products independently, unless otherwise provided by the State Council.

Enterprises undertaking mandatory plans have the right to sell products produced in excess of the plan and products shared from within the plan.

Article 25 The enterprise has the right to independently choose its suppliers and purchase the materials needed for production.

Article 26 Unless the prices are controlled by the pricing department and relevant competent departments as stipulated by the State Council, the enterprise has the right to determine the prices of its products and services independently.

Article 27 The enterprise has the right to negotiate and sign contracts with foreign businessmen in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

The enterprise has the right to retain and use its share of foreign exchange earnings in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 28 The enterprise has the right to dispose of its retained funds in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 29 The enterprise has the right to lease or transfer for value the fixed assets granted by the State for its management and operation in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, and the proceeds must be used for equipment renewal and technological transformation.

Article 30 The enterprise has the right to determine the wage forms and bonus distribution methods suitable for its own circumstances.

Article 31 The enterprise has the right to recruit and dismiss workers in accordance with the law and the regulations of the State Council.

Article 32 The enterprise has the right to decide on the establishment of its organizational structure and its staffing.

Article 33 The enterprise has the right to reject any apportionment of manpower, material resources, or financial resources by any organ or unit. Unless otherwise provided by laws or regulations, any request by any organ or unit for the enterprise to provide manpower, material resources, or financial resources in any manner constitutes an apportionment.

Article 34 The enterprise has the right to form joint ventures with other enterprises and institutions, invest in other enterprises and institutions, and hold shares in other enterprises in accordance with the law and the regulations of the State Council.

The enterprise has the right to issue bonds in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 35 The enterprise must fulfill the mandatory plans. (Deleted on August 27, 2009)

The enterprise must perform the contracts concluded in accordance with the law.

Article 36 The enterprise must ensure the normal maintenance of fixed assets, and improve and update equipment.

Article 37 The enterprise must comply with State regulations on financial, labor and wage, and price management, and accept supervision from financial, auditing, labor and wage, and pricing authorities.

Article 38 The enterprise must guarantee product quality and service quality, and be responsible to users and consumers.

Article 39 The enterprise must improve labor efficiency, conserve energy and raw materials, and strive to reduce costs.

Article 40 The enterprise must strengthen security work, maintain production order, and protect State property.

Article 41 The enterprise must implement the system of safe production, improve working conditions, do a good job in labor protection and environmental protection, and achieve safe production and civilized production.

Article 42 The enterprise shall strengthen ideological and political education, legal education, national defense education, scientific and cultural education, and technical and vocational training to improve the quality of its workforce.

Article 43 The enterprise shall support and reward workers for engaging in scientific research, inventions and creations, carrying out technological innovation, making rationalization suggestions, and participating in socialist labor emulation activities.

## Chapter IV The Factory Manager
Article 44 The appointment of the factory director, unless otherwise provided by the State Council, shall be decided by the competent government department based on the circumstances of the enterprise, adopting one of the following methods:
(1) Appointment or recruitment by the competent government department.
(2) Election by the enterprise's workers' congress.

The candidate for factory director appointed or recruited by the competent government department must solicit the opinions of worker representatives; the factory director elected by the enterprise's workers' congress must be reported to the competent government department for approval.
The director appointed or recruited by the competent government department may be removed or dismissed by the competent government department, and the opinions of worker representatives must be solicited; the director elected by the enterprise's workers' congress may be removed by the workers' congress, and this must be reported to the competent government department for approval.
Article 45 The factory director is the legal representative of the enterprise.
The enterprise establishes a production and operation management system headed by the factory director. The factory director holds a central position in the enterprise and bears overall responsibility for the enterprise's material civilization and spiritual civilization construction.
The factory director leads the enterprise's production and operation management work and exercises the following functions and powers:
(1) To decide on or submit for examination and approval the various plans of the enterprise in accordance with the law and the regulations of the State Council.
(2) To decide on the establishment of the enterprise's administrative structure.
(3) To propose to the competent government department the appointment or removal, or the engagement or dismissal, of deputy factory-level administrative leading cadres, unless otherwise provided by law or the State Council.(4) To appoint or remove, or engage or dismiss, the enterprise's middle-level administrative leading cadres, unless otherwise provided by law.
(5) To propose wage adjustment plans, fund distribution plans, and important rules and regulations, and submit them to the workers' congress for examination and approval. To propose plans for the use of welfare funds and other major matters concerning the welfare of workers, and submit them to the workers' congress for deliberation and decision.
(6) To reward or punish workers in accordance with the law; to propose to the competent government department the reward or punishment of deputy factory-level administrative leading cadres.
Article 46 The factory director must rely on the workers to fulfill the various obligations of the enterprise stipulated in this Law, support the work of the workers' congress, the trade union, and other mass organizations, and implement the decisions made by the workers' congress in accordance with the law.
Article 47 The enterprise shall establish a management committee or, through other forms, assist the factory director in deciding on major issues of the enterprise. The management committee shall be composed of responsible persons from various aspects of the enterprise and worker representatives. The factory director serves as the director of the management committee.
The term "major issues" referred to in the preceding paragraph includes:

(1) Business policies, long-term plans and annual plans, capital construction plans and major technological transformation plans, worker training plans, wage adjustment plans, plans for the distribution and use of retained funds, and plans for contracting and leasing responsibility systems.
(2) The staffing of enterprise personnel whose wages are included in the enterprise's cost, and the establishment and adjustment of the administrative structure.
(3) Plans for formulating, amending, and abolishing important rules and regulations.

The plans for discussing the above major issues shall all be proposed by the factory director.
Article 48 If the factory director achieves outstanding results in leading the enterprise to fulfill plans, improve product quality and service quality, enhance economic efficiency, and strengthen spiritual civilization construction, the competent government department shall give rewards.
## Chapter V Workers and the Workers' Congress

Article 49 Workers have the right to participate in the democratic management of the enterprise, to offer opinions and suggestions on the production and work of the enterprise, to enjoy labor protection, labor insurance, rest, and leave in accordance with the law, to report the true situation to State organs, and to criticize and accuse the leading cadres of the enterprise. Female workers have the right to enjoy special labor protection and labor insurance in accordance with State regulations.

Article 50 Workers shall work with the attitude of masters of the State, observe labor discipline and rules and regulations, and fulfill production and work tasks.

Article 51 The workers' congress is the basic form of democratic management in the enterprise and the organ through which workers exercise their power of democratic management.

The working body of the workers' congress is the enterprise's trade union committee. The enterprise's trade union committee is responsible for the daily work of the workers' congress.

Article 52 The workers' congress exercises the following functions and powers:

(1) To hear and deliberate reports from the factory director on the enterprise's business policies, long-term plans, annual plans, capital construction plans, major technological transformation plans, worker training plans, plans for the distribution and use of retained funds, and plans for contracting and leasing responsibility systems, and to offer opinions and suggestions.(2) To examine and approve or veto the enterprise's wage adjustment plans, bonus distribution plans, labor protection measures, reward and punishment methods, and other important rules and regulations.
(3) To deliberate and decide on plans for the use of worker welfare funds, plans for the distribution of worker housing, and other major matters concerning the welfare of workers.
(4) To appraise and supervise the enterprise's administrative leading cadres at all levels, and to propose suggestions for rewards, punishments, appointments, and removals.
(5) To elect the factory director based on the decision of the competent government department, subject to approval by the competent government department.
Article 53 Workshops practice democratic management through workers' general meetings, worker representative groups, or other forms; workers directly participate in the democratic management of their work teams.

Article 54 The workers' congress shall support the factory director in exercising his functions and powers in accordance with the law, and educate workers to fulfill the obligations stipulated in this Law.
## Chapter VI Relationship Between the Enterprise and the Government

Article 55 The government or the competent government department shall, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, uniformly issue mandatory plans to the enterprise, ensure the planned supply of materials needed for the enterprise to fulfill the mandatory plans, examine and approve the enterprise's plans for capital construction and major technological transformation, etc.; appoint, remove, reward, and punish the factory director; appoint, remove, reward, and punish deputy factory-level administrative leading cadres based on the factory director's proposals; and assess and train factory-level administrative leading cadres. (Deleted on August 27, 2009)
Article 56 Relevant government departments shall, in accordance with the State's goal of regulating the market and guiding enterprises through the market, provide services to enterprises, and, in accordance with their respective responsibilities and the provisions of laws and regulations, exercise management and supervision over enterprises.

(1) Formulate and adjust industrial policies, and guide enterprises in formulating development plans.
(2) Provide consultation and information for enterprises' business decisions.
(3) Coordinate relationships between enterprises and other units.
(4) Maintain the normal production order of enterprises and protect the State property managed and operated by enterprises from infringement.
(5) Gradually improve public facilities related to enterprises.

Article 57 The local government at or above the county level where the enterprise is located shall provide the materials needed by the enterprise that are subject to local planned management, coordinate the relationship between the enterprise and other local units, and strive to manage the public welfare undertakings related to the enterprise well.

Article 58 No organ or unit shall infringe upon the enterprise's right to independent management and operation enjoyed in accordance with the law; shall not apportion manpower, material resources, or financial resources to the enterprise; and shall not require the enterprise to establish any organizational structure or prescribe the staffing number of such structures.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 59 Anyone who, in violation of Article 16, engages in production and business activities in the name of an enterprise without examination and approval by the government or the competent government department and registration by the administrative department for industry and commerce, shall be ordered to cease business, and the illegal income shall be confiscated.

If an enterprise practices fraud or conceals the true situation from the registration authority, it shall be given a warning or fined; if the circumstances are serious, its business license shall be revoked.

The administrative penalties stipulated in this Article shall be decided by the administrative department for industry and commerce at or above the county level. If a party is dissatisfied with the decision on fines, orders to cease business, confiscation of illegal income, or revocation of business license, it may file a lawsuit with the court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the notice of penalty; if no lawsuit is filed within the time limit and the decision is not complied with, the organ that made the penalty decision shall apply to the court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 60 If an enterprise causes property or personal damage to users and consumers due to the production or sale of substandard products, it shall bear liability for compensation; if the act constitutes a crime, the directly responsible persons shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

If the quality of the product does not meet the conditions stipulated in the economic contract, liability for breach of contract shall be borne.

Article 61 If a decision made by the government or a relevant government department violates the provisions of Article 58 of this Law, the enterprise has the right to apply to the organ that made the decision for its revocation; if the decision is not revoked, the enterprise has the right to appeal to the organ at the next higher level of the organ that made the decision or to the government supervision department. The organ receiving the appeal shall make a ruling within thirty days from the date of receiving the appeal and notify the enterprise.

Article 62 If leading cadres of an enterprise abuse their power, infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of workers, and the circumstances are serious, they shall be given administrative sanctions by the competent government department; if they abuse their power, use public office for private gain, and retaliate against or frame workers, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 63 If leading cadres of an enterprise or relevant government departments cause relatively significant losses to the enterprise or the State due to work negligence, they shall be given administrative sanctions by the competent government department or the relevant higher-level authority.

If leading cadres of an enterprise or relevant government departments neglect their duty, causing significant losses to the enterprise's property, the State, or the interests of the people, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 64 Anyone who obstructs a leading cadre of an enterprise from performing his duties in accordance with the law, without using violence or threats, shall be punished by the public security organ at the enterprise's location in accordance with the provisions of Article 19 of the Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Administrative Penalties for Public Security; anyone who obstructs a leading cadre of an enterprise from performing his duties in accordance with the law by using violence or threats shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the provisions of Article 157 of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China. (Deleted on August 27, 2009)

Anyone who disrupts the order of an enterprise, causing production, business, or work to be unable to proceed normally, without causing serious losses, shall be punished by the public security organ at the enterprise's location in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Administrative Penalties for Public Security.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 65 The principles of this Law shall apply to enterprises owned by the whole people in the fields of transportation, posts and telecommunications, geological exploration, construction and installation, commerce, foreign trade, materials, agriculture, forestry, and water conservancy.

Article 66 For enterprises implementing contracting or leasing responsibility systems, in addition to complying with the provisions of this Law, the rights and obligations of the contracting party and the contractee, and the lessor and the lessee, shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council.

For joint venture enterprises, large-scale conglomerates, and joint-stock enterprises, their leadership system shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council.

Article 67 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 68 The Standing Committee of the Autonomous Region People's Congress may, based on the principles of this Law and the Law of the People's Republic of China on Regional National Autonomy, and in light of local characteristics, formulate implementing measures, which shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the record.

Article 69 This Law shall come into effect as of August 1, 1988.
