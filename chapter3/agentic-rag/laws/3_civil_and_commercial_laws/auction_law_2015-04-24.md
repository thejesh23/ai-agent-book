# Auction Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 5, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Auction Law of the People's Republic of China at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate auction activities, maintain auction order, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of all parties involved in auction activities.

Article 2 This Law applies to auction activities conducted by auction enterprises within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 Auction refers to a method of sale in which specific items or property rights are transferred to the highest bidder through public competitive bidding.

Article 4 Auction activities shall comply with relevant laws and administrative regulations, and adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, justice, honesty, and trustworthiness.

Article 5 The department of the State Council responsible for the administration of the auction industry shall supervise and manage the auction industry nationwide.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities divided into districts, through their departments responsible for the administration of the auction industry, shall supervise and manage the auction industry within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Auction Targets

Article 6 Auction targets shall be items or property rights owned by the client or lawfully disposable by the client.

Article 7 Items or property rights prohibited from sale by laws or administrative regulations shall not be used as auction targets.

Article 8 Items or property rights that require approval for transfer according to law or regulations of the State Council shall undergo approval procedures before auction.

Cultural relics consigned for auction shall, before auction, be appraised and permitted by the cultural relics administrative department at the place of the auctioneer's domicile in accordance with the law.

Article 9 Items confiscated by state administrative organs according to law, items used to offset taxes and fines, and other items that shall be consigned for auction according to regulations of the State Council shall be auctioned by the auctioneer designated by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, or city divided into districts where the property is located.

The auction of items confiscated by a people's court according to law, items used to offset fines and penalties, and recovered items that cannot be returned shall be governed by the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter III Parties to an Auction

### Section 1 Auctioneer

Article 10 An auctioneer refers to an enterprise legal person established in accordance with this Law and the Company Law of the People's Republic of China to engage in auction activities.

Article 11 An enterprise must obtain approval for engaging in auction business from the department responsible for the administration of the auction industry under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where it is located. Auction enterprises may be established in cities divided into districts.

Article 12 An enterprise applying for permission to engage in auction business shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a registered capital of not less than one million RMB;

(2) Having its own name, organizational structure, domicile, and articles of association;

(3) Having auctioneers and other staff suitable for the auction business;

(4) Having auction business rules that comply with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations;

(5) Complying with the regulations of the State Council on the development of the auction industry;

(6) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 13 An auction enterprise engaged in the auction of cultural relics shall have a registered capital of not less than ten million RMB and personnel with professional knowledge of cultural relic auctions.

Article 14 Auction activities shall be presided over by an auctioneer.

Article 15 An auctioneer shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a college degree or above and professional knowledge of auctions;

(2) Having worked in an auction enterprise for more than two years;

(3) Having good conduct.

Persons who have been dismissed from public office or whose auctioneer qualification certificate has been revoked for less than five years, or who have been criminally punished for intentional crimes, shall not serve as auctioneers.

Article 16 The assessment of auctioneer qualifications shall be organized uniformly by the auction industry association. Those who pass the assessment shall be issued an auctioneer qualification certificate by the auction industry association.

Article 17 The auction industry association is a social organization legal person established according to law and a self-regulatory organization of the auction industry. The auction industry association shall supervise auction enterprises and auctioneers in accordance with this Law and its articles of association.

Article 18 An auctioneer has the right to require the client to explain the source and defects of the auction target.

The auctioneer shall explain the defects of the auction target to the bidders.

Article 19 The auctioneer has the obligation to keep custody of the items delivered by the client for auction.

Article 20 After accepting a consignment, the auctioneer shall not entrust another auctioneer to auction without the consent of the client.

Article 21 If the client or the buyer requests confidentiality of their identity, the auctioneer shall keep it confidential.

Article 22 An auctioneer and its staff shall not participate in auction activities organized by themselves as bidders, nor shall they entrust others to bid on their behalf.

Article 23 An auctioneer shall not auction its own items or property rights in auction activities organized by itself.

Article 24 After the auction is concluded, the auctioneer shall, as agreed, deliver the proceeds of the auction target to the client and transfer the auction target to the buyer as agreed.

### Section 2 Client

Article 25 A client refers to a citizen, legal person, or other organization that entrusts an auctioneer to auction items or property rights.

Article 26 A client may handle the consignment procedures themselves or through their agent.

Article 27 The client shall explain the source and defects of the auction target to the auctioneer.

Article 28 The client has the right to determine the reserve price of the auction target and require the auctioneer to keep it confidential.

For the auction of state-owned assets that require appraisal according to law or regulations of the State Council, the appraisal shall be conducted by an appraisal institution established according to law, and the reserve price of the auction target shall be determined based on the appraisal results.

Article 29 The client may withdraw the auction target before the auction begins. If the client withdraws the auction target, they shall pay the agreed fees to the auctioneer; if no agreement is made, they shall pay the reasonable expenses incurred by the auctioneer for the auction.

Article 30 The client shall not participate in bidding, nor shall they entrust others to bid on their behalf.

Article 31 If the client is to transfer the auction target as agreed, after the auction is concluded, the client shall transfer the auction target to the buyer.

### Section 3 Bidder

Article 32 A bidder refers to a citizen, legal person, or other organization that participates in the bidding for an auction target.

Article 33 If laws or administrative regulations stipulate conditions for the purchase of an auction target, bidders shall meet those conditions.

Article 34 Bidders may participate in bidding themselves or through their agents.

Article 35 Bidders have the right to know the defects of the auction target, inspect the auction target, and review relevant auction materials.

Article 36 Once a bidder makes a bid, it shall not be withdrawn. When another bidder makes a higher bid, the previous bid loses its binding effect.

Article 37 Bidders shall not collude maliciously with each other or with the auctioneer to harm the interests of others.

### Section 4 Buyer

Article 38 A buyer refers to the bidder who purchases the auction target with the highest bid.

Article 39 The buyer shall pay the price of the auction target as agreed. If the buyer fails to pay the price as agreed, they shall bear liability for breach of contract, or the auctioneer may, with the consent of the client, re-auction the auction target.

If the auction target is re-auctioned, the original buyer shall pay the commission that they and the client should have paid in the first auction. If the price of the re-auction is lower than the original auction price, the original buyer shall make up the difference.

Article 40 If the buyer fails to obtain the auction target as agreed, they have the right to require the auctioneer or the client to bear liability for breach of contract.

If the buyer fails to take delivery of the auction target as agreed, they shall pay the storage fees incurred as a result.

## Chapter IV Auction Procedures

### Section 1 Auction Consignment

Article 41 When consigning items or property rights for auction, the client shall provide identification documents and, as required by the auctioneer, proof of ownership or proof of lawful disposal of the auction target, as well as other materials.

Article 42 The auctioneer shall verify the relevant documents and materials provided by the client. If the auctioneer accepts the consignment, they shall sign a written consignment auction contract with the client.

Article 43 If the auctioneer deems it necessary to appraise the auction target, they may conduct an appraisal.

If the appraisal conclusion does not match the condition of the auction target as stated in the consignment auction contract, the auctioneer has the right to demand modification or termination of the contract.

Article 44 The consignment auction contract shall include the following items:

(1) The name or title and domicile of the client and the auctioneer;

(2) The name, specifications, quantity, and quality of the auction target;

(3) The reserve price proposed by the client;

(4) The time and place of the auction;

(5) The time and method of delivery or transfer of the auction target;

(6) The commission and the method and time limit for its payment;

(7) The method and time limit for payment of the price;

(8) Liability for breach of contract;

(9) Other matters agreed upon by both parties.

### Section 2 Auction Announcement and Display

Article 45 The auctioneer shall publish an auction announcement seven days before the auction date.

Article 46 The auction announcement shall include the following items:

(1) The time and place of the auction;

(2) The auction target;

(3) The time and place for displaying the auction target;

(4) The procedures to be completed for participating in the bidding;

(5) Other matters that need to be announced.

Article 47 The auction announcement shall be published through newspapers or other news media.

Article 48 The auctioneer shall display the auction target before the auction and provide conditions for inspecting the auction target and relevant materials. The display period for the auction target shall not be less than two days.

### Section 3 Implementation of the Auction

Article 49 The auctioneer shall announce the auction rules and precautions before the auction.

Article 50 If the auction target has no reserve price, the auctioneer shall explain this before the auction.

If the auction target has a reserve price and the highest bid of the bidders does not reach the reserve price, the bid shall have no effect, and the auctioneer shall stop the auction of that target.

Article 51 The auction is concluded when the highest bid of the bidders is confirmed by the auctioneer's hammer fall or other public indication of acceptance.

Article 52 After the auction is concluded, the buyer and the auctioneer shall sign a confirmation of the transaction.

Article 53 When conducting an auction, the auctioneer shall make auction records. The auction records shall be signed by the auctioneer and the recorder; if the auction is concluded, they shall also be signed by the buyer.

Article 54 The auctioneer shall properly keep complete account books, auction records, and other relevant materials related to business operations.

The retention period for the account books, auction records, and other relevant materials specified in the preceding paragraph shall be calculated from the termination date of the consignment auction contract and shall not be less than five years.

Article 55 If the auction target requires procedures for certificate changes or property rights transfer according to law, the client and the buyer shall, holding the transaction certificate issued by the auctioneer and relevant materials, go through the procedures with the relevant administrative authorities.

### Section 4 Commission

Article 56 The client and the buyer may agree with the auctioneer on the commission rate.

If the client, the buyer, and the auctioneer have not agreed on the commission rate and the auction is concluded, the auctioneer may charge each of the client and the buyer a commission not exceeding five percent of the auction price. The commission rate shall be determined in inverse proportion to the auction price.

If the auction is not concluded, the auctioneer may charge the client the agreed fees; if no agreement is made, the auctioneer may charge the client the reasonable expenses incurred for the auction.

Article 57 If the auction of items specified in Article 9 of this Law is concluded, the auctioneer may charge the buyer a commission not exceeding five percent of the auction price. The commission rate shall be determined in inverse proportion to the auction price.

If the auction is not concluded, the provisions of Article 56, paragraph 3 of this Law shall apply.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 58 If a client violates Article 6 of this Law by consigning for auction items or property rights that they do not own or are not lawfully entitled to dispose of, they shall bear liability according to law. If the auctioneer knowingly consigns for auction items or property rights that the client does not own or is not lawfully entitled to dispose of, they shall bear joint liability.

Article 59 If a state organ violates Article 9 of this Law by disposing of items that should be auctioned by the auctioneer designated by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, or city divided into districts where the property is located, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if losses are caused to the state, they shall also bear compensation liability.

Article 60 If a person violates Article 11 of this Law by engaging in auction business without permission, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall ban the activity, confiscate illegal gains, and may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains.

Article 61 If an auctioneer or client violates Article 18, paragraph 2, or Article 27 of this Law by failing to explain the defects of the auction target, causing damage to the buyer, the buyer has the right to claim compensation from the auctioneer; if the liability lies with the client, the auctioneer has the right to seek recourse from the client.

If the auctioneer or client declares before the auction that they cannot guarantee the authenticity or quality of the auction target, they shall not bear liability for defect warranty.

The statute of limitations for claiming compensation due to undisclosed defects in the auction target is one year, calculated from the date the party knew or should have known that their rights were infringed.

The statute of limitations for claiming compensation for personal injury or property damage caused by defects in the auction target shall be governed by the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws.

Article 62 If an auctioneer or its staff violates Article 22 of this Law by participating in bidding or entrusting others to bid on their behalf, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall give the auctioneer a warning, may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the auction commission; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 63 If a person violates Article 23 of this Law by auctioning their own items or property rights in auction activities organized by themselves, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall confiscate the auction proceeds.

Article 64 If a person violates Article 30 of this Law by having the client participate in bidding or entrust others to bid on their behalf, the administrative department for industry and commerce may impose a fine of not more than thirty percent of the auction price on the client.

Article 65 If a person violates Article 37 of this Law by malicious collusion among bidders or between bidders and the auctioneer, causing damage to others, the auction shall be invalid, and they shall bear compensation liability according to law. The administrative department for industry and commerce shall impose a fine of not less than ten percent but not more than thirty percent of the highest bid on bidders involved in malicious collusion; and a fine of not less than ten percent but not more than fifty percent of the highest bid on auctioneers involved in malicious collusion.

Article 66 If a person violates the provisions on commission rates in Section 4 of Chapter IV of this Law by charging commissions, the auctioneer shall return the excess portion to the client and the buyer. The price control department may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the auction commission on the auctioneer.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 67 This Law applies to foreign nationals, foreign enterprises, and foreign organizations that consign for auction or participate in bidding within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 68 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1997.
