# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Consumer Rights and Interests

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 31, 1993

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Consumer Rights and Interests adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on October 25, 2013

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, maintaining the socio-economic order, and promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 The rights and interests of consumers in purchasing or using commodities or receiving services for daily consumption shall be protected by this Law. Matters not covered by this Law shall be protected by other relevant laws and regulations.

Article 3 Business operators providing consumers with commodities produced or sold by them or providing services shall comply with this Law. Matters not covered by this Law shall comply with other relevant laws and regulations.

Article 4 In transactions between business operators and consumers, the principles of voluntariness, equality, fairness, honesty, and good faith shall be followed.

Article 5 The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of consumers from infringement.

The State takes measures to ensure that consumers exercise their rights in accordance with the law and safeguards the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

The State advocates civilized, healthy, resource-conserving, and environmentally friendly consumption patterns, and opposes waste.

Article 6 Protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers is the common responsibility of the entire society.

The State encourages and supports all organizations and individuals to exercise social supervision over acts that harm the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

Mass media shall conduct publicity to safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of consumers and exercise public opinion supervision over acts that harm the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

## Chapter II: Rights of Consumers

Article 7 Consumers have the right to the protection of their personal and property safety when purchasing or using commodities or receiving services.

Consumers have the right to require that the commodities and services provided by business operators meet the requirements for the protection of personal and property safety.

Article 8 Consumers have the right to know the true information about the commodities they purchase or use or the services they receive.

Consumers have the right to require business operators to provide information such as the price, place of origin, manufacturer, purpose, performance, specifications, grade, main ingredients, production date, expiration date, certificate of inspection, instruction manual, after-sales service, or the content, specifications, and fees of the service, depending on the different circumstances of the commodities or services.

Article 9 Consumers have the right to freely choose commodities or services.

Consumers have the right to freely choose the business operator providing commodities or services, freely choose the type of commodity or service, and freely decide whether to purchase or not purchase any commodity or accept or not accept any service.

When freely choosing commodities or services, consumers have the right to compare, differentiate, and select.

Article 10 Consumers have the right to fair trade.

When purchasing commodities or receiving services, consumers have the right to obtain fair trade conditions such as quality assurance, reasonable prices, and accurate measurement, and have the right to refuse forced transactions by business operators.

Article 11 Consumers who suffer personal injury or property damage from purchasing or using commodities or receiving services have the right to receive compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 12 Consumers have the right to form social organizations to safeguard their legitimate rights and interests in accordance with the law.

Article 13 Consumers have the right to acquire knowledge about consumption and the protection of consumer rights and interests.

Consumers shall strive to master the knowledge and usage skills of the commodities or services they need, use commodities correctly, and enhance their self-protection awareness.

Article 14 When purchasing or using commodities or receiving services, consumers have the right to have their personal dignity and ethnic customs respected, and have the right to have their personal information protected in accordance with the law.

Article 15 Consumers have the right to supervise commodities, services, and the work of protecting consumer rights and interests.

Consumers have the right to report and accuse acts that infringe upon consumer rights and interests, as well as illegal and derelict acts by state organs and their staff in the work of protecting consumer rights and interests, and have the right to criticize and make suggestions regarding the work of protecting consumer rights and interests.

## Chapter III: Obligations of Business Operators

Article 16 Business operators providing commodities or services to consumers shall fulfill their obligations in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and regulations.

If there is an agreement between the business operator and the consumer, the obligations shall be fulfilled in accordance with the agreement, provided that the agreement does not violate the provisions of laws and regulations.

Business operators providing commodities or services to consumers shall abide by social ethics, operate with integrity, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers; they shall not set unfair or unreasonable transaction conditions, nor engage in forced transactions.

Article 17 Business operators shall listen to consumers' opinions on the commodities or services they provide and accept consumer supervision.

Article 18 Business operators shall ensure that the commodities or services they provide meet the requirements for the protection of personal and property safety. For commodities and services that may endanger personal or property safety, they shall provide consumers with truthful explanations and clear warnings, and explain and indicate the correct methods of using the commodities or receiving the services, as well as methods to prevent hazards.

Business operators of premises such as hotels, shopping malls, restaurants, banks, airports, stations, ports, and cinemas shall fulfill their safety guarantee obligations towards consumers.

Article 19 If a business operator discovers that the commodities or services it provides have defects that may endanger personal or property safety, it shall immediately report to the relevant administrative department, inform consumers, and take measures such as suspending sales, issuing warnings, recalling, rendering harmless, destroying, or suspending production or service. If a recall measure is taken, the business operator shall bear the necessary expenses incurred by consumers due to the recall of the commodities.

Article 20 Business operators shall provide truthful and comprehensive information about the quality, performance, purpose, and expiration date of the commodities or services they provide, and shall not make false or misleading publicity.

Business operators shall provide truthful and clear answers to consumers' inquiries regarding the quality and usage methods of the commodities or services they provide.

Business operators providing commodities or services shall clearly mark the prices.

Article 21 Business operators shall indicate their true names and marks.

Business operators leasing counters or premises from others shall indicate their true names and marks.

Article 22 Business operators providing commodities or services shall issue invoices or other purchase vouchers or service documents to consumers in accordance with relevant state regulations or commercial practices; if consumers request invoices or other purchase vouchers or service documents, the business operator must issue them.

Article 23 Business operators shall ensure that the commodities or services they provide have the quality, performance, purpose, and expiration date they should have under normal use or service conditions, except where the consumer knew of the defect before purchasing the commodity or receiving the service and the defect does not violate mandatory legal provisions.

If a business operator indicates the quality status of commodities or services through advertisements, product descriptions, physical samples, or other means, it shall ensure that the actual quality of the commodities or services provided matches the indicated quality status.

For durable commodities such as motor vehicles, computers, televisions, refrigerators, air conditioners, and washing machines, or services such as decoration and renovation provided by business operators, if a consumer discovers a defect within six months from the date of receiving the commodity or service and a dispute arises, the business operator shall bear the burden of proof regarding the defect.

Article 24 If the commodities or services provided by a business operator do not meet quality requirements, consumers may return the goods in accordance with state regulations or the agreement of the parties, or require the business operator to fulfill obligations such as replacement or repair. If there are no state regulations or party agreements, consumers may return the goods within seven days from the date of receipt; if after seven days the conditions for legal rescission of the contract are met, consumers may return the goods promptly; if the conditions for legal rescission of the contract are not met, consumers may require the business operator to fulfill obligations such as replacement or repair.

For returns, replacements, or repairs carried out in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the business operator shall bear necessary expenses such as transportation costs.

Article 25 For commodities sold by business operators through the internet, television, telephone, mail order, etc., consumers have the right to return the goods within seven days from the date of receipt without giving any reason, except for the following commodities:

(1) Custom-made by the consumer;
(2) Fresh, perishable, or easily spoiled;
(3) Audio-visual products, computer software, and other digital commodities downloaded online or opened by the consumer;
(4) Newspapers and periodicals delivered.

Except for the commodities listed in the preceding paragraph, other commodities that, by their nature, are confirmed by the consumer at the time of purchase as not suitable for return are not subject to unconditional return.

The commodities returned by the consumer shall be in good condition. The business operator shall refund the price paid by the consumer within seven days from the date of receiving the returned goods. The shipping cost for returning the goods shall be borne by the consumer; if otherwise agreed between the business operator and the consumer, the agreement shall prevail.

Article 26 When using standard clauses in business activities, business operators shall draw consumers' attention in a conspicuous manner to the quantity and quality of the commodities or services, the price or fees, the performance period and method, safety precautions and risk warnings, after-sales service, civil liability, and other content that has a significant bearing on the consumer's interests, and shall provide explanations upon the consumer's request.

Business operators shall not use standard clauses, notices, declarations, in-store announcements, or other means to exclude or limit consumer rights, reduce or exempt business operator responsibilities, or increase consumer responsibilities in a manner that is unfair or unreasonable to consumers, nor shall they use standard clauses with technical means to force transactions.

If standard clauses, notices, declarations, in-store announcements, etc., contain the content listed in the preceding paragraph, such content shall be void.

Article 27 Business operators shall not insult or slander consumers, search the bodies of consumers or the items they carry, or infringe upon the personal freedom of consumers.

Article 28 Business operators providing commodities or services through the internet, television, telephone, mail order, etc., as well as business operators providing financial services such as securities, insurance, and banking, shall provide consumers with information including the business address, contact information, quantity and quality of commodities or services, price or fees, performance period and method, safety precautions and risk warnings, after-sales service, and civil liability.

Article 29 When collecting and using consumers' personal information, business operators shall follow the principles of legality, legitimacy, and necessity, clearly indicate the purpose, method, and scope of collection and use, and obtain the consumer's consent. When collecting and using consumers' personal information, business operators shall disclose their collection and use rules and shall not collect or use information in violation of laws, regulations, or the agreement between the parties.

Business operators and their staff must strictly keep confidential the consumers' personal information collected, and shall not disclose, sell, or illegally provide it to others. Business operators shall take technical measures and other necessary measures to ensure information security and prevent the disclosure or loss of consumers' personal information. In the event of or potential disclosure or loss of information, remedial measures shall be taken immediately.

Business operators shall not send commercial information to consumers without their consent or request, or when consumers have explicitly refused.

## Chapter IV: State Protection of the Legitimate Rights and Interests of Consumers

Article 30 When the State formulates laws, regulations, rules, and mandatory standards concerning consumer rights and interests, it shall listen to the opinions of consumers and organizations such as consumer associations.

Article 31 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership, organize, coordinate, and urge relevant administrative departments to do a good job in protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers and fulfill their responsibilities for protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

People's governments at all levels shall strengthen supervision, prevent acts that endanger the personal or property safety of consumers, and promptly stop acts that endanger the personal or property safety of consumers.

Article 32 The administrative departments for industry and commerce and other relevant administrative departments of people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations, take measures within their respective responsibilities to protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

Relevant administrative departments shall listen to the opinions of consumers and organizations such as consumer associations on the transaction behavior of business operators and the quality of commodities and services, and promptly investigate and handle them.

Article 33 Relevant administrative departments shall, within their respective responsibilities, conduct regular or irregular spot checks and inspections of the commodities and services provided by business operators, and promptly announce the results of the spot checks and inspections to the public.

If a relevant administrative department discovers and determines that the commodities or services provided by a business operator have defects that may endanger personal or property safety, it shall immediately order the business operator to take measures such as suspending sales, issuing warnings, recalling, rendering harmless, destroying, or suspending production or service.

Article 34 Relevant state organs shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations, punish illegal and criminal acts by business operators that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of consumers when providing commodities or services.

Article 35 People's courts shall take measures to facilitate consumers in filing lawsuits. For consumer rights and interest disputes that meet the conditions for filing a lawsuit under the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, they must accept and hear them promptly.

## Chapter V: Consumer Organizations

Article 36 Consumer associations and other consumer organizations are social organizations established in accordance with the law to exercise social supervision over commodities and services and to protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

Article 37 Consumer associations perform the following public welfare duties:

(1) Provide consumers with consumption information and consulting services, enhance consumers' ability to protect their own legitimate rights and interests, and guide civilized, healthy, resource-conserving, and environmentally friendly consumption patterns;
(2) Participate in the formulation of laws, regulations, rules, and mandatory standards concerning consumer rights and interests;
(3) Participate in the supervision and inspection of commodities and services by relevant administrative departments;
(4) Reflect issues concerning the legitimate rights and interests of consumers to relevant departments, make inquiries, and put forward suggestions;
(5) Accept consumer complaints, and investigate and mediate the matters complained about;
(6) For complaint matters involving the quality of commodities or services, may entrust a qualified appraiser to appraise, and the appraiser shall inform the appraisal opinion;
(7) Support consumers who have suffered damage from acts that harm the legitimate rights and interests of consumers in filing lawsuits or file lawsuits in accordance with this Law;
(8) Expose and criticize acts that harm the legitimate rights and interests of consumers through mass media.

People's governments at all levels shall provide necessary financial and other support for consumer associations to perform their duties.

Consumer associations shall conscientiously fulfill their responsibilities for protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, listen to consumers' opinions and suggestions, and accept social supervision.

Other consumer organizations established in accordance with the law shall carry out activities to protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers in accordance with the provisions of laws, regulations, and their articles of association.

Article 38 Consumer organizations shall not engage in commodity business or for-profit services, nor shall they recommend commodities or services to consumers by charging fees or other means of seeking profits.

## Chapter VI: Settlement of Disputes

Article 39 Disputes over consumer rights and interests between consumers and business operators may be resolved through the following channels:

(1) Negotiation and settlement with the business operator;
(2) Requesting mediation by a consumer association or other mediation organization established in accordance with the law;
(3) Filing a complaint with the relevant administrative department;
(4) Submitting to an arbitration institution for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration agreement reached with the business operator;
(5) Filing a lawsuit with a people's court.

Article 40 If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when purchasing or using a commodity, the consumer may claim compensation from the seller. After the seller compensates, if it is the responsibility of the producer or the responsibility of another seller who provided the commodity to the seller, the seller has the right to recover from the producer or the other seller.

If a consumer or other victim suffers personal injury or property damage due to a defect in a commodity, the consumer or victim may claim compensation from the seller or the producer. If it is the responsibility of the producer, the seller, after compensating, has the right to recover from the producer. If it is the responsibility of the seller, the producer, after compensating, has the right to recover from the seller.

If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when receiving a service, the consumer may claim compensation from the service provider.

Article 41 If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when purchasing or using a commodity or receiving a service, and the original enterprise is divided or merged, the consumer may claim compensation from the enterprise that succeeds to its rights and obligations after the change.

Article 42 If a business operator illegally using another person's business license provides commodities or services and harms the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, the consumer may claim compensation from the business operator or from the holder of the business license.

Article 43 If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when purchasing commodities or receiving services at a trade fair or a leased counter, the consumer may claim compensation from the seller or service provider. After the trade fair ends or the counter lease expires, the consumer may also claim compensation from the organizer of the trade fair or the lessor of the counter. After the organizer of the trade fair or the lessor of the counter compensates, they have the right to recover from the seller or service provider.

Article 44 If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed when purchasing commodities or receiving services through an online trading platform, the consumer may claim compensation from the seller or service provider. If the provider of the online trading platform cannot provide the true name, address, and valid contact information of the seller or service provider, the consumer may also claim compensation from the provider of the online trading platform; if the provider of the online trading platform has made commitments more favorable to consumers, it shall fulfill such commitments. After the provider of the online trading platform compensates, it has the right to recover from the seller or service provider.

If the provider of the online trading platform knows or should know that the seller or service provider is using its platform to infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of consumers and fails to take necessary measures, it shall bear joint and several liability with the seller or service provider in accordance with the law.

Article 45 If a consumer's legitimate rights and interests are harmed because a business operator provides commodities or services through false advertising or other false publicity methods, the consumer may claim compensation from the business operator. If an advertising operator or publisher publishes false advertisements, the consumer may request the administrative department to punish them. If the advertising operator or publisher cannot provide the true name, address, and valid contact information of the business operator, it shall bear compensation liability.

If an advertising operator or publisher designs, produces, or publishes false advertisements concerning commodities or services related to the life and health of consumers, causing damage to consumers, it shall bear joint and several liability with the business operator providing the commodities or services.

If a social group or other organization, or an individual, recommends commodities or services to consumers in false advertisements or other false publicity concerning commodities or services related to the life and health of consumers, causing damage to consumers, it shall bear joint and several liability with the business operator providing the commodities or services.Article 46: Where a consumer files a complaint with the relevant administrative department, the department shall handle the matter and notify the consumer within seven working days from the date of receipt of the complaint.

Article 47: For acts that infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of numerous consumers, the China Consumers' Association and consumer associations established in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may initiate legal proceedings in the People's Courts.

## Chapter 7: Legal Liability

Article 48: Where an operator provides goods or services under any of the following circumstances, unless otherwise provided for in this Law, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with the provisions of other relevant laws and regulations:

(1) The goods or services have defects;
(2) The goods do not possess the performance they should have, and no explanation was given at the time of sale;
(3) The goods do not conform to the standards indicated on the goods or their packaging;
(4) The goods do not conform to the quality status indicated by the product description, physical samples, or other means;
(5) Producing goods that have been explicitly eliminated by the state, or selling expired or deteriorated goods;
(6) The quantity of goods sold is insufficient;
(7) The content and cost of services violate the agreement;
(8) Deliberately delaying or unreasonably refusing consumers' requests for repair, rework, replacement, return, replenishment of goods, refund of payment and service fees, or compensation for losses;
(9) Other circumstances that harm consumers' rights and interests as provided for by laws and regulations.

Where an operator fails to fulfill its safety protection obligations towards consumers, causing damage to consumers, it shall bear tort liability.

Article 49: Where an operator provides goods or services, causing personal injury to consumers or other victims, it shall compensate for reasonable expenses such as medical expenses, nursing fees, transportation expenses, and other expenses incurred for treatment and rehabilitation, as well as the reduced income due to lost work time. If disability is caused, it shall also compensate for the cost of assistive devices for daily living and disability compensation. If death is caused, it shall also compensate for funeral expenses and death compensation.

Article 50: Where an operator infringes upon the personal dignity of consumers, violates their personal freedom, or infringes upon their right to have their personal information protected in accordance with the law, it shall cease the infringement, restore the consumer's reputation, eliminate the adverse effects, make a formal apology, and compensate for losses.

Article 51: Where an operator engages in acts such as insult, defamation, body search, or violation of personal freedom that infringe upon the personal rights of consumers or other victims, causing serious mental harm, the victim may claim compensation for mental damages.

Article 52: Where an operator provides goods or services, causing property damage to consumers, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with legal provisions or the agreement of the parties, such as repair, rework, replacement, return, replenishment of goods, refund of payment and service fees, or compensation for losses.

Article 53: Where an operator provides goods or services on a prepayment basis, it shall provide them in accordance with the agreement. If it fails to provide them as agreed, it shall fulfill the agreement or refund the prepayment at the consumer's request, and shall bear the interest on the prepayment and any reasonable expenses necessarily incurred by the consumer.

Article 54: Where goods are determined to be substandard by the relevant administrative department in accordance with the law, and the consumer requests a return, the operator shall be responsible for the return.

Article 55: Where an operator engages in fraudulent conduct in providing goods or services, it shall, at the consumer's request, increase the compensation for the losses suffered. The amount of increased compensation shall be three times the price of the goods purchased or the cost of the services received by the consumer; if the increased compensation is less than 500 yuan, it shall be 500 yuan. Where other laws provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Where an operator knowingly provides goods or services with defects to consumers, causing death or serious health damage to consumers or other victims, the victim has the right to demand that the operator compensate for losses in accordance with the provisions of Articles 49 and 51 of this Law and other legal provisions, and also has the right to demand punitive damages of up to two times the losses suffered.

Article 56: Where an operator is in any of the following circumstances, in addition to bearing corresponding civil liability, if other relevant laws and regulations have provisions on the punishing authority and methods of punishment, those provisions shall be followed; if there are no such provisions in laws and regulations, the administrative department for industry and commerce or other relevant administrative departments shall order correction, and may, depending on the circumstances, impose a warning, confiscate illegal gains, impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains, or, if there are no illegal gains, impose a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, order a suspension of business for rectification or revoke the business license:

(1) The goods or services provided do not meet the requirements for ensuring personal and property safety;
(2) Adulterating or mixing impurities into goods, passing off fake goods as genuine, shoddy goods as high-quality, or passing off substandard goods as standard goods;
(3) Producing goods that have been explicitly eliminated by the state, or selling expired or deteriorated goods;
(4) Forging the place of origin of goods, forging or using the name or address of another manufacturer without authorization, altering the production date, or forging or using quality marks such as certification marks without authorization;
(5) Failing to inspect or quarantine goods that should be inspected or quarantined before sale, or forging inspection or quarantine results;
(6) Making false or misleading publicity about goods or services;
(7) Refusing or delaying the implementation of measures ordered by the relevant administrative department to stop sales, issue warnings, recall, render harmless, destroy, or stop production or services for defective goods or services;
(8) Deliberately delaying or unreasonably refusing consumers' requests for repair, rework, replacement, return, replenishment of goods, refund of payment and service fees, or compensation for losses;
(9) Infringing upon the personal dignity of consumers, violating their personal freedom, or infringing upon their right to have their personal information protected in accordance with the law;
(10) Other circumstances that harm consumers' rights and interests and should be punished as provided for by laws and regulations.

Where an operator is in any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, in addition to being punished in accordance with laws and regulations, the punishing authority shall record this in the credit file and make it public.

Article 57: Where an operator violates the provisions of this Law by providing goods or services, infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of consumers, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 58: Where an operator violates the provisions of this Law and should bear civil compensation liability and pay fines or penalties, and its property is insufficient to pay both, it shall first bear the civil compensation liability.

Article 59: Where an operator disagrees with an administrative penalty decision, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

Article 60: Where a person obstructs staff members of the relevant administrative department from performing their duties in accordance with the law by means of violence or threats, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; where a person refuses or obstructs staff members of the relevant administrative department from performing their duties in accordance with the law without using violence or threats, the public security organ shall impose a penalty in accordance with the provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 61: Where a state functionary neglects their duties or shields operators who infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of consumers, their unit or a higher-level authority shall impose administrative sanctions; if the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8: Supplementary Provisions

Article 62: Where farmers purchase and use means of production directly used for agricultural production, this Law shall be applied by reference.

Article 63: This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 1994.
