# Partnership Enterprise Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on February 23, 1997

Revised at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 27, 2006

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of regulating the behavior of partnership enterprises, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of partnership enterprises and their partners and creditors, maintaining the social and economic order, and promoting the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "partnership enterprise" means a general partnership enterprise or a limited partnership enterprise established within the territory of China by natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations in accordance with this Law.

A general partnership enterprise shall be composed of general partners, who shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership enterprise. Where there are special provisions in this Law regarding the form of liability assumed by general partners, such provisions shall prevail.

A limited partnership enterprise shall be composed of general partners and limited partners. The general partners shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership enterprise, while the limited partners shall bear liability for the debts of the partnership enterprise to the extent of their subscribed capital contributions.

Article 3 Wholly state-owned companies, state-owned enterprises, listed companies, and public welfare institutions or social organizations shall not become general partners.

Article 4 A partnership agreement shall be concluded in writing through consultation among all partners in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The conclusion of a partnership agreement and the establishment of a partnership enterprise shall follow the principles of voluntariness, equality, fairness, and good faith.

Article 6 The income from production and business operations and other income of a partnership enterprise shall be subject to income tax paid separately by the partners in accordance with the relevant tax provisions of the state.

Article 7 A partnership enterprise and its partners must abide by laws and administrative regulations, observe social morality and business ethics, and bear social responsibilities.

Article 8 The lawful property and rights and interests of a partnership enterprise and its partners shall be protected by law.

Article 9 To apply for the establishment of a partnership enterprise, the applicant shall submit to the enterprise registration authority documents such as a registration application, a partnership agreement, and identity certificates of the partners.

Where the business scope of the partnership enterprise includes items that require approval before registration as stipulated by laws or administrative regulations, such business shall be approved in accordance with the law, and the approval document shall be submitted at the time of registration.

Article 10 Where the registration application materials submitted by the applicant are complete and comply with the statutory form, and the enterprise registration authority can register on the spot, it shall register on the spot and issue a business license.

Except for the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the enterprise registration authority shall, within twenty days from the date of accepting the application, decide whether to grant registration. If registration is granted, a business license shall be issued; if registration is not granted, a written reply shall be given with reasons stated.

Article 11 The date of issuance of the business license of a partnership enterprise shall be the date of establishment of the partnership enterprise.

Before a partnership enterprise obtains its business license, partners shall not engage in partnership business in the name of the partnership enterprise.

Article 12 Where a partnership enterprise establishes a branch, it shall apply for registration with the enterprise registration authority at the location of the branch and obtain a business license.

Article 13 Where registered items of a partnership enterprise change, the partner(s) executing partnership affairs shall, within fifteen days from the date of making the decision on the change or the occurrence of the change event, apply to the enterprise registration authority for registration of the change.

## Chapter II General Partnership Enterprises

### Section 1 Establishment of a Partnership Enterprise

Article 14 To establish a partnership enterprise, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) There are two or more partners. If a partner is a natural person, he/she shall have full civil capacity;

(2) There is a written partnership agreement;

(3) There are capital contributions subscribed or actually paid by the partners;

(4) There is a name for the partnership enterprise and a place for production and business operations; and

(5) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 15 The name of a general partnership enterprise shall contain the words "general partnership".

Article 16 A partner may make capital contributions in currency, in-kind, intellectual property rights, land use rights, or other property rights, and may also contribute labor services.

Where a partner makes capital contributions in in-kind, intellectual property rights, land use rights, or other property rights that require valuation, the valuation may be determined through consultation among all partners or by a statutory appraisal institution entrusted by all partners.

Where a partner makes capital contributions in labor services, the valuation method shall be determined through consultation among all partners and shall be stated in the partnership agreement.

Article 17 A partner shall fulfill the obligation to make capital contributions in accordance with the method, amount, and time limit for contribution stipulated in the partnership agreement.

Where capital contributions are made in non-monetary property and, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, the transfer of property rights is required, such transfer shall be handled in accordance with the law.

Article 18 A partnership agreement shall specify the following matters:

(1) The name of the partnership enterprise and the location of its principal place of business;

(2) The purpose of the partnership and the business scope of the partnership;

(3) The name or title and domicile of each partner;

(4) The method, amount, and time limit for capital contributions by each partner;

(5) The method for profit distribution and loss sharing;

(6) The execution of partnership affairs;

(7) Admission and withdrawal of partners;

(8) Methods for dispute resolution;

(9) Dissolution and liquidation of the partnership enterprise; and

(10) Liability for breach of contract.

Article 19 A partnership agreement shall take effect after being signed and sealed by all partners. Partners shall enjoy rights and perform obligations in accordance with the partnership agreement.

Any amendment or supplement to the partnership agreement shall require the unanimous consent of all partners, unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement.

Matters not stipulated or not clearly stipulated in the partnership agreement shall be decided by the partners through consultation; if consultation fails, the matter shall be handled in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

### Section 2 Property of a Partnership Enterprise

Article 20 The capital contributions of partners, the income obtained in the name of the partnership enterprise, and other property obtained in accordance with the law shall all constitute the property of the partnership enterprise.

Article 21 Before the liquidation of a partnership enterprise, a partner shall not request the division of the partnership property, unless otherwise provided for in this Law.

If a partner privately transfers or disposes of partnership property before the liquidation of the partnership enterprise, the partnership enterprise shall not use this to oppose a bona fide third party.

Article 22 Unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement, a partner who transfers all or part of his/her property share in the partnership enterprise to a person other than a partner shall obtain the unanimous consent of the other partners.

Where a partner transfers all or part of his/her property share in the partnership enterprise to another partner, he/she shall notify the other partners.

Article 23 Where a partner transfers his/her property share in the partnership enterprise to a person other than a partner, the other partners shall have a preemptive right to purchase under the same conditions, unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement.

Article 24 Where a person other than a partner lawfully acquires a partner's property share in the partnership enterprise, upon amendment of the partnership agreement, that person shall become a partner of the partnership enterprise and shall enjoy rights and perform obligations in accordance with this Law and the amended partnership agreement.

Article 25 Where a partner pledges his/her property share in the partnership enterprise, he/she shall obtain the unanimous consent of the other partners; without the unanimous consent of the other partners, such act shall be invalid, and if it causes losses to a bona fide third party, the actor shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

### Section 3 Execution of Partnership Affairs

Article 26 Partners shall have equal rights to execute partnership affairs.

In accordance with the stipulations of the partnership agreement or a decision of all partners, one or several partners may be entrusted to represent the partnership enterprise externally and execute partnership affairs.

Where a legal person or other organization that is a partner executes partnership affairs, it shall do so through its appointed representative.

Article 27 Where one or several partners are entrusted to execute partnership affairs in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 26 of this Law, the other partners shall no longer execute partnership affairs.

Partners who do not execute partnership affairs shall have the right to supervise the execution of partnership affairs by the executing partners.

Article 28 Where one or several partners execute partnership affairs, the executing partner(s) shall regularly report to the other partners on the status of the execution of affairs and the business operations and financial condition of the partnership enterprise. The income arising from the execution of partnership affairs shall belong to the partnership enterprise, and the expenses and losses incurred shall be borne by the partnership enterprise.

For the purpose of understanding the business operations and financial condition of the partnership enterprise, partners shall have the right to inspect the partnership enterprise's accounting books and other financial materials.

Article 29 Where partners execute partnership affairs separately, an executing partner may raise objections to the affairs executed by another partner. When an objection is raised, the execution of such affairs shall be suspended. If a dispute arises, a decision shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Article 30 of this Law.

If a partner entrusted to execute partnership affairs fails to execute the affairs in accordance with the partnership agreement or the decision of all partners, the other partners may decide to revoke the entrustment.

Article 30 When partners make resolutions on matters concerning the partnership enterprise, the voting method stipulated in the partnership agreement shall be followed. If the partnership agreement does not stipulate or does not clearly stipulate the voting method, the voting method shall be one vote per partner, with adoption requiring the approval of more than half of all partners.

Where this Law has other provisions on the voting method for partnership enterprises, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 31 Unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement, the following matters of a partnership enterprise shall require the unanimous consent of all partners:

(1) Changing the name of the partnership enterprise;

(2) Changing the business scope or the location of the principal place of business of the partnership enterprise;

(3) Disposing of the immovable property of the partnership enterprise;

(4) Transferring or disposing of the intellectual property rights and other property rights of the partnership enterprise;

(5) Providing guarantees for others in the name of the partnership enterprise;

(6) Appointing a person other than a partner as the business manager of the partnership enterprise.

Article 32 A partner shall not engage, on his/her own or jointly with others, in any business that competes with the partnership enterprise.

Unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement or unanimously consented to by all partners, a partner shall not conduct transactions with the partnership enterprise.

A partner shall not engage in any activities that harm the interests of the partnership enterprise.

Article 33 The distribution of profits and sharing of losses of a partnership enterprise shall be handled in accordance with the stipulations of the partnership agreement; if the partnership agreement does not stipulate or does not clearly stipulate, it shall be decided by the partners through consultation; if consultation fails, it shall be distributed or shared by the partners in proportion to their actual capital contributions; if the proportion of capital contributions cannot be determined, it shall be distributed or shared equally by the partners.

The partnership agreement shall not stipulate that all profits be distributed to some partners or that all losses be borne by some partners.

Article 34 Partners may increase or decrease their capital contributions to the partnership enterprise in accordance with the stipulations of the partnership agreement or a decision of all partners.

Article 35 The business manager appointed by a partnership enterprise shall perform duties within the scope of authority authorized by the partnership enterprise.

If the appointed business manager of a partnership enterprise performs duties beyond the scope of authority authorized by the partnership enterprise, or causes losses to the partnership enterprise due to intentional acts or gross negligence in the course of performing duties, he/she shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 36 A partnership enterprise shall establish financial and accounting systems in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

### Section 4 Relationship Between a Partnership Enterprise and Third Parties

Article 37 Restrictions imposed by a partnership enterprise on a partner's execution of partnership affairs and authority to represent the partnership enterprise externally shall not be used to oppose a bona fide third party.

Article 38 A partnership enterprise shall first use all of its property to pay off its debts.

Article 39 Where a partnership enterprise is unable to pay off its due debts, the partners shall bear unlimited joint and several liability.

Article 40 Where a partner, due to bearing unlimited joint and several liability, pays off an amount exceeding the proportion of losses he/she should bear as stipulated in paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law, he/she shall have the right to seek recourse from the other partners.

Article 41 Where a partner incurs debts unrelated to the partnership enterprise, the relevant creditor shall not offset such debts against the debts owed by the creditor to the partnership enterprise, nor shall the creditor exercise the partner's rights in the partnership enterprise by subrogation.

Article 42 Where a partner's own property is insufficient to pay off his/her debts unrelated to the partnership enterprise, the partner may use the income distributed from the partnership enterprise for payment; the creditor may also request the people's court to enforce the partner's property share in the partnership enterprise for payment in accordance with the law.

When the people's court enforces a partner's property share, it shall notify all partners, and the other partners shall have a preemptive right to purchase; if the other partners do not purchase and do not agree to transfer the property share to another person, the settlement for the partner's withdrawal shall be handled in accordance with Article 51 of this Law, or a settlement for reducing the corresponding property share of the partner shall be handled.

### Section 5 Admission and Withdrawal of Partners

Article 43 Unless otherwise stipulated in the partnership agreement, the admission of a new partner shall require the unanimous consent of all partners, and a written admission agreement shall be concluded in accordance with the law.

When concluding the admission agreement, the existing partners shall truthfully inform the new partner of the business operations and financial condition of the original partnership enterprise.

Article 44 A newly admitted partner shall enjoy the same rights and bear the same responsibilities as the existing partners. If the admission agreement stipulates otherwise, such stipulations shall prevail.

A new partner shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership enterprise incurred before his/her admission.

Article 45 Where the partnership agreement stipulates a term for the partnership, during the existence of the partnership enterprise, a partner may withdraw from the partnership under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The event for withdrawal stipulated in the partnership agreement occurs;

(2) Unanimous consent of all partners is obtained;

(3) An event occurs that makes it difficult for the partner to continue participating in the partnership; or

(4) Another partner seriously violates the obligations stipulated in the partnership agreement.

Article 46 Where the partnership agreement does not stipulate a term for the partnership, a partner may withdraw from the partnership provided that it does not adversely affect the execution of partnership affairs, but shall notify the other partners thirty days in advance.

Article 47 If a partner withdraws in violation of the provisions of Articles 45 and 46 of this Law, he/she shall compensate the losses caused to the partnership enterprise.

Article 48 A partner shall be automatically withdrawn from the partnership under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The natural person partner dies or is legally declared dead;

(2) The partner loses the ability to pay debts;

(3) The legal person or other organization that is a partner is legally revoked of its business license, ordered to close, dissolved, or declared bankrupt;

(4) The partner loses a qualification that is legally required or stipulated in the partnership agreement for a partner to possess; or

(5) All of the partner's property share in the partnership enterprise is enforced by the people's court.

If a partner is legally determined to be a person without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity, with the unanimous consent of the other partners, he/she may be converted into a limited partner in accordance with the law, and the general partnership enterprise shall be converted into a limited partnership enterprise in accordance with the law. If the other partners fail to reach unanimous consent, the partner without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity shall withdraw from the partnership.

The date on which the event causing withdrawal actually occurs shall be the effective date of withdrawal.

Article 49 Under any of the following circumstances, a partner may be expelled by a resolution passed with the unanimous consent of the other partners:

(1) The partner fails to fulfill the obligation to make capital contributions;

(2) The partner causes losses to the partnership enterprise due to intentional acts or gross negligence;

(3) The partner engages in improper conduct when executing partnership affairs; or

(4) An event stipulated in the partnership agreement occurs.

The resolution to expel a partner shall be notified in writing to the expelled person. The expulsion shall take effect on the date the expelled person receives the expulsion notice, and the expelled person shall withdraw from the partnership.

If the expelled person objects to the expulsion resolution, he/she may file a lawsuit with the people's court within thirty days from the date of receiving the expulsion notice.

Article 50 Where a partner dies or is legally declared dead, the heir who has the legal right to inherit the deceased partner's property share in the partnership enterprise may, in accordance with the stipulations of the partnership agreement or with the unanimous consent of all partners, acquire the status of a partner of the partnership enterprise from the date of commencement of the inheritance.

Under any of the following circumstances, the partnership enterprise shall return the property share of the deceased partner to his/her heir:

(1) The heir is unwilling to become a partner;

(2) The law or the partnership agreement stipulates that a partner must possess a certain qualification, and the heir has not obtained such qualification; or

(3) Other circumstances stipulated in the partnership agreement where the heir cannot become a partner.

If the heir of a partner is a person without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity, with the unanimous consent of all partners, he/she may become a limited partner in accordance with the law, and the general partnership enterprise shall be converted into a limited partnership enterprise in accordance with the law. If all partners fail to reach unanimous consent, the partnership enterprise shall return the property share of the deceased partner to the heir.

Article 51 When a partner withdraws from the partnership, the other partners shall settle accounts with the withdrawing partner based on the property status of the partnership enterprise at the time of withdrawal and return the withdrawing partner's property share. If the withdrawing partner is liable for compensation for losses caused to the partnership enterprise, the amount of compensation shall be deducted accordingly.

If there are unfinished partnership affairs at the time of withdrawal, the settlement shall be made after such affairs are concluded.

Article 52 The method for returning the property share of a withdrawing partner in the partnership enterprise shall be stipulated in the partnership agreement or decided by all partners, and may be in the form of currency or in-kind.

Article 53 A withdrawing partner shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership enterprise arising from causes that occurred before his/her withdrawal.

Article 54 When a partner withdraws from the partnership, if the property of the partnership enterprise is less than its debts, the withdrawing partner shall share the losses in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law.

### Section 6 Special General Partnership Enterprises

Article 55 Professional service institutions that provide paid services to clients with professional knowledge and specialized skills may be established as special general partnership enterprises.

A special general partnership enterprise refers to a general partnership enterprise where partners bear liability in accordance with the provisions of Article 57 of this Law.

Special general partnership enterprises shall be governed by the provisions of this Section; matters not covered by this Section shall be governed by the provisions of Sections 1 through 5 of this Chapter.

Article 56 The name of a special general partnership enterprise shall contain the words "special general partnership".

Article 57 Where one or several partners cause debts of the partnership enterprise due to intentional acts or gross negligence in the course of practicing, such partner(s) shall bear unlimited liability or unlimited joint and several liability, while the other partners shall bear liability limited to their property shares in the partnership enterprise.

Debts of the partnership enterprise caused by partners in the course of practicing not due to intentional acts or gross negligence, as well as other debts of the partnership enterprise, shall be borne by all partners with unlimited joint and several liability.

Article 58 Where debts of the partnership enterprise are caused by a partner's intentional acts or gross negligence in the course of practicing, after the partnership property is used to satisfy such debts externally, the partner shall bear compensation liability for the losses caused to the partnership enterprise in accordance with the stipulations of the partnership agreement.

Article 59 A special general partnership enterprise shall establish a practice risk fund and obtain professional liability insurance.The practice risk fund shall be used to pay debts arising from the practice activities of partners. The practice risk fund shall be managed under a separate account. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter III: Limited Liability Partnerships

Article 60: Limited liability partnerships and their partners shall be governed by the provisions of this Chapter; matters not covered in this Chapter shall be governed by the provisions of Sections 1 through 5 of Chapter II of this Law concerning general partnerships and their partners.

Article 61: A limited liability partnership shall be established by two or more but not more than fifty partners; however, unless otherwise provided by law.

A limited liability partnership shall have at least one general partner.

Article 62: The name of a limited liability partnership shall indicate the words "limited liability partnership".

Article 63: In addition to complying with the provisions of Article 18 of this Law, the partnership agreement shall also specify the following matters:

(1) The names or titles and domiciles of the general partners and limited partners;
(2) The qualifications required for the managing partner and the selection procedure;
(3) The authority of the managing partner and the measures for dealing with breaches;
(4) The conditions for removal of the managing partner and the replacement procedure;
(5) The conditions and procedures for the admission and withdrawal of limited partners, as well as related liabilities;
(6) The procedure for the mutual conversion between limited partners and general partners.

Article 64: A limited partner may contribute capital in the form of currency, tangible assets, intellectual property rights, land use rights, or other property rights.

A limited partner shall not contribute labor services as capital.

Article 65: A limited partner shall make capital contributions in full and on time in accordance with the partnership agreement; if failing to do so, the partner shall bear the obligation to make up the contribution and shall be liable for breach of contract to the other partners.

Article 66: The registration items of a limited liability partnership shall include the names or titles of the limited partners and the amount of their subscribed capital contributions.

Article 67: The business affairs of a limited liability partnership shall be managed by the general partners. The managing partner may request that the remuneration for management and the method of drawing such remuneration be specified in the partnership agreement.

Article 68: A limited partner shall not manage the partnership affairs and shall not represent the limited liability partnership externally.

The following actions by a limited partner shall not be deemed as managing partnership affairs:

(1) Participating in deciding on the admission or withdrawal of general partners;
(2) Making suggestions on the operation and management of the enterprise;
(3) Participating in selecting the accounting firm to undertake the audit business of the limited liability partnership;
(4) Obtaining the audited financial and accounting reports of the limited liability partnership;
(5) Inspecting the financial accounting books and other financial materials of the limited liability partnership when the partner's own interests are involved;
(6) Claiming rights against the responsible partner or filing a lawsuit when the partner's interests in the limited liability partnership are harmed;
(7) Urging the managing partner to exercise its rights when it is negligent in doing so, or filing a lawsuit in the partner's own name for the benefit of the enterprise;
(8) Providing guarantees for the enterprise in accordance with the law.

Article 69: A limited liability partnership shall not distribute all profits to some of the partners; however, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership agreement.

Article 70: A limited partner may conduct transactions with the limited liability partnership; however, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership agreement.

Article 71: A limited partner may engage in business competing with the limited liability partnership, either independently or in cooperation with others; however, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership agreement.

Article 72: A limited partner may pledge its property share in the limited liability partnership; however, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership agreement.

Article 73: A limited partner may transfer its property share in the limited liability partnership to persons other than partners in accordance with the partnership agreement, provided that it notifies the other partners thirty days in advance.

Article 74: If a limited partner's own property is insufficient to pay its debts unrelated to the partnership, the partner may use the income distributed from the limited liability partnership for payment; the creditor may also request the people's court to enforce the partner's property share in the limited liability partnership for payment in accordance with the law.

When the people's court enforces the property share of a limited partner, it shall notify all partners. Under the same conditions, other partners have a preemptive right to purchase.

Article 75: If a limited liability partnership has only limited partners remaining, it shall be dissolved; if it has only general partners remaining, it shall be converted into a general partnership.

Article 76: If a third party has reason to believe that a limited partner is a general partner and transacts with it, the limited partner shall bear the same liability for that transaction as a general partner.

If a limited partner, without authorization, conducts transactions with others in the name of the limited liability partnership, causing losses to the limited liability partnership or other partners, the limited partner shall be liable for compensation.

Article 77: A newly admitted limited partner shall be liable for the debts of the limited liability partnership incurred before its admission only to the extent of its subscribed capital contribution.

Article 78: A limited partner shall automatically withdraw from the partnership under any of the circumstances listed in Items (1), (3), (4), or (5) of Paragraph 1 of Article 48 of this Law.

Article 79: If a natural person who is a limited partner loses capacity for civil conduct during the existence of the limited liability partnership, other partners shall not demand its withdrawal on that ground.

Article 80: When a natural person who is a limited partner dies, is declared dead in accordance with the law, or when a legal person or other organization that is a limited partner terminates, its heir or successor in interest may acquire the status of the limited partner in the limited liability partnership in accordance with the law.

Article 81: After a limited partner withdraws, it shall be liable for the debts of the limited liability partnership arising from causes before its withdrawal, to the extent of the property it took back from the limited liability partnership upon withdrawal.

Article 82: Unless otherwise agreed in the partnership agreement, the conversion of a general partner into a limited partner, or a limited partner into a general partner, shall require the unanimous consent of all partners.

Article 83: If a limited partner is converted into a general partner, it shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the limited liability partnership incurred during the period when it was a limited partner.

Article 84: If a general partner is converted into a limited partner, it shall bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership incurred during the period when it was a general partner.

## Chapter IV: Dissolution and Liquidation of Partnerships

Article 85: A partnership shall be dissolved under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The partnership term expires, and the partners decide not to continue operations;
(2) The dissolution events specified in the partnership agreement occur;
(3) All partners decide to dissolve;
(4) The number of partners falls below the statutory minimum for thirty consecutive days;
(5) The purpose of the partnership as specified in the partnership agreement has been achieved or cannot be achieved;
(6) The business license is revoked, the partnership is ordered to close, or it is cancelled in accordance with the law;
(7) Other reasons provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 86: Upon the dissolution of a partnership, a liquidator shall be appointed to carry out the liquidation.

The liquidator shall be all partners; with the consent of more than half of all partners, one or more partners may be appointed, or a third party may be entrusted, to act as the liquidator within fifteen days after the dissolution event occurs.

If no liquidator is determined within fifteen days from the date the dissolution event occurs, the partners or other interested parties may apply to the people's court to appoint a liquidator.

Article 87: The liquidator shall perform the following tasks during the liquidation period:

(1) Take inventory of the partnership's property and prepare a balance sheet and a property inventory;
(2) Handle the unfinished business of the partnership related to the liquidation;
(3) Pay off the taxes owed;
(4) Settle claims and debts;
(5) Dispose of the remaining property of the partnership after paying off its debts;
(6) Represent the partnership in litigation or arbitration activities.

Article 88: The liquidator shall, within ten days from the date of appointment, notify the creditors of the partnership's dissolution and shall publish an announcement in a newspaper within sixty days. Creditors shall, within thirty days from receipt of the notice, or within forty-five days from the date of the announcement if no notice is received, declare their claims to the liquidator.

When declaring a claim, the creditor shall state the relevant matters of the claim and provide supporting evidence. The liquidator shall register the claims.

During the liquidation period, the partnership shall continue to exist but shall not engage in business activities unrelated to the liquidation.

Article 89: After paying the liquidation expenses, employee wages, social insurance premiums, statutory compensation, paying off taxes owed, and settling debts, the remaining property of the partnership shall be distributed in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law.

Article 90: Upon completion of the liquidation, the liquidator shall prepare a liquidation report, which, after being signed and sealed by all partners, shall be submitted to the enterprise registration authority within fifteen days for the cancellation of the partnership's registration.

Article 91: After the cancellation of a partnership, the original general partners shall continue to bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts incurred during the existence of the partnership.

Article 92: If a partnership is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, the creditor may apply to the people's court for bankruptcy liquidation in accordance with the law, or may demand that the general partners pay off the debts.

If a partnership is declared bankrupt in accordance with the law, the general partners shall continue to bear unlimited joint and several liability for the debts of the partnership.

## Chapter V: Legal Liability

Article 93: If, in violation of this Law, a partnership registration is obtained by submitting false documents or by other deceptive means, the enterprise registration authority shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the enterprise registration shall be revoked, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 94: If, in violation of this Law, a partnership fails to indicate the words "general partnership," "special general partnership," or "limited liability partnership" in its name, the enterprise registration authority shall order correction within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 95: If, in violation of this Law, a person engages in partnership business in the name of a partnership or a branch of a partnership without obtaining a business license, the enterprise registration authority shall order cessation and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

If, when the registration items of a partnership change, the partnership fails to go through the change registration procedures in accordance with this Law, the enterprise registration authority shall order registration within a specified period; if registration is not completed within the specified period, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If, when the registration items of a partnership change, the managing partner fails to apply for change registration within the specified period, the managing partner shall compensate for the losses caused to the partnership, other partners, or bona fide third parties.

Article 96: If a partner, in managing partnership affairs, or an employee of the partnership, takes advantage of their position to appropriate for themselves profits that should belong to the partnership, or uses other means to misappropriate partnership property, they shall return such profits and property to the partnership; if losses are caused to the partnership or other partners, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 97: If a partner, without authorization, handles matters that, under this Law or the partnership agreement, require the unanimous consent of all partners, and causes losses to the partnership or other partners, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 98: If a partner who does not have the authority to manage partnership affairs, without authorization, manages partnership affairs and causes losses to the partnership or other partners, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 99: If a partner, in violation of this Law or the partnership agreement, engages in business competing with the partnership or conducts transactions with the partnership, the profits derived therefrom shall belong to the partnership; if losses are caused to the partnership or other partners, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 100: If a liquidator fails to submit a liquidation report to the enterprise registration authority in accordance with this Law, or submits a liquidation report that conceals important facts or contains material omissions, the enterprise registration authority shall order correction. The expenses and losses incurred as a result shall be borne and compensated by the liquidator.

Article 101: If a liquidator, in performing liquidation tasks, seeks illegal income or misappropriates partnership property, they shall return such income and property to the partnership; if losses are caused to the partnership or other partners, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 102: If a liquidator, in violation of this Law, conceals or transfers partnership property, makes false entries in the balance sheet or property inventory, or distributes property before paying off debts, thereby harming the interests of creditors, they shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 103: If a partner violates the partnership agreement, they shall be liable for breach of contract in accordance with the law.

If a dispute arises between partners over the performance of the partnership agreement, the partners may resolve it through negotiation or mediation. If they are unwilling to resolve it through negotiation or mediation, or if negotiation or mediation fails, they may apply to an arbitration institution for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration clause in the partnership agreement or a written arbitration agreement reached afterwards. If no arbitration clause is included in the partnership agreement and no written arbitration agreement is reached afterwards, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 104: If a staff member of the relevant administrative authority, in violation of this Law, abuses power, engages in malpractice for personal gain, accepts bribes, or infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a partnership, they shall be subject to administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 105: If a violation of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 106: If a person who violates this Law is liable for both civil compensation and the payment of fines or penalties, and their property is insufficient to pay both, they shall first bear the civil compensation liability.

## Chapter VI: Supplementary Provisions

Article 107: If a non-enterprise professional service organization adopts a partnership form in accordance with relevant laws, the form of liability of its partners may be governed by the provisions of this Law concerning the liability of partners in a special general partnership.

Article 108: The measures for the administration of the establishment of partnerships in China by foreign enterprises or individuals shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 109: This Law shall come into effect as of June 1, 2007.
