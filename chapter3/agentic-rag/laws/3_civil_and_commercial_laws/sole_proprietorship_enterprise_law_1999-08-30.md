# Individual Sole Proprietorship Enterprise Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 30, 1999

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution with a view to regulating the acts of individual sole proprietorship enterprises, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of investors and creditors of such enterprises, maintaining the social and economic order, and promoting the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, an "individual sole proprietorship enterprise" means an operating entity which is established within the territory of China in accordance with this Law, invested in by a single natural person, whose property is owned by the investor personally, and for whose debts the investor assumes unlimited liability with his personal property.

Article 3 The domicile of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall be the location of its principal office.

Article 4 In conducting business activities, an individual sole proprietorship enterprise must abide by laws and administrative regulations, adhere to the principle of good faith, and shall not harm the public interest.

An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall fulfill its tax obligations in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The State shall protect the property and other legitimate rights and interests of individual sole proprietorship enterprises in accordance with the law.

Article 6 An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall recruit employees in accordance with the law. The legitimate rights and interests of the employees shall be protected by law.

Employees of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall establish a trade union in accordance with the law, and the trade union shall carry out its activities in accordance with the law.

Article 7 Members of the Communist Party of China in an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall conduct their activities in accordance with the Constitution of the Communist Party of China.

## Chapter II Establishment of Individual Sole Proprietorship Enterprises

Article 8 The following conditions shall be met for the establishment of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise:

(1) The investor is a single natural person;

(2) There is a lawful enterprise name;

(3) There is capital contribution subscribed by the investor;

(4) There is a fixed place of production and business operation and the necessary conditions for production and business operation; and

(5) There are necessary employees.

Article 9 To apply for the establishment of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, the investor or his authorized agent shall submit to the registration authority at the place where the enterprise is located documents such as an application for establishment, the investor's identity certificate, and a certificate of use of the place of production and business operation. When an authorized agent applies for establishment registration, a power of attorney from the investor and a legal certificate of the agent shall be submitted.

An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall not engage in businesses prohibited by laws or administrative regulations; for businesses that require approval from relevant departments as stipulated by laws or administrative regulations, the approval documents from such departments shall be submitted when applying for establishment registration.

Article 10 The application for establishment of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall specify the following:

(1) The name and domicile of the enterprise;

(2) The name and residence of the investor;

(3) The amount and method of capital contribution by the investor; and

(4) The scope of business.

Article 11 The name of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall be consistent with its form of liability and the business it engages in.

Article 12 The registration authority shall, within 15 days from the date of receiving the application documents for establishment, register those that meet the conditions stipulated in this Law and issue a business license; for those that do not meet the conditions, it shall not register and shall give a written reply stating the reasons.

Article 13 The date of issuance of the business license of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall be the date of its establishment.

Before obtaining the business license of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, the investor shall not engage in business activities in the name of the enterprise.

Article 14 To establish a branch, an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall have the investor or his authorized agent apply for registration to the registration authority at the place where the branch is located and obtain a business license.

After the branch is approved and registered, the registration status shall be reported to the registration authority of the individual sole proprietorship enterprise to which the branch belongs for record.

The civil liability of the branch shall be borne by the individual sole proprietorship enterprise that established it.

Article 15 If any registered item of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise changes during its existence, the enterprise shall apply to the registration authority for change registration within 15 days from the date of making the change decision.

## Chapter III Investors and Business Management of Individual Sole Proprietorship Enterprises

Article 16 Persons prohibited by laws or administrative regulations from engaging in profit-making activities shall not apply to establish an individual sole proprietorship enterprise as investors.

Article 17 The investor of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall enjoy ownership of the enterprise's property in accordance with the law, and the relevant rights may be transferred or inherited in accordance with the law.

Article 18 If an investor of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise explicitly uses his family's common property as personal capital contribution when applying for enterprise establishment registration, he shall assume unlimited liability for the enterprise's debts with the family's common property in accordance with the law.

Article 19 The investor of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise may manage the enterprise's affairs himself, or may entrust or employ another person with civil capacity to be responsible for the management of the enterprise's affairs.

If the investor entrusts or employs another person to manage the affairs of the individual sole proprietorship enterprise, a written contract shall be signed with the trustee or the employed person, specifying the content of the entrustment and the scope of authority granted.

The trustee or the employed person shall fulfill the duties of good faith and diligence and be responsible for managing the affairs of the individual sole proprietorship enterprise in accordance with the contract signed with the investor.

Restrictions imposed by the investor on the authority of the trustee or the employed person shall not be valid against a bona fide third party.

Article 20 The personnel entrusted or employed by the investor to manage the affairs of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Soliciting or accepting bribes by taking advantage of their position;

(2) Embezzling enterprise property by taking advantage of their position or work;

(3) Misappropriating enterprise funds for personal use or lending them to others;

(4) Without authorization, opening accounts in personal names or in the names of others to deposit enterprise funds;

(5) Without authorization, providing guarantees with enterprise property;

(6) Without the investor's consent, engaging in businesses competing with the enterprise;

(7) Without the investor's consent, entering into contracts or conducting transactions with the enterprise;

(8) Without the investor's consent, transferring the enterprise's trademarks or other intellectual property rights to others for use;

(9) Disclosing the enterprise's trade secrets; or

(10) Other acts prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 21 An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall set up accounting books and conduct accounting in accordance with the law.

Article 22 If an individual sole proprietorship enterprise recruits employees, it shall sign labor contracts with them in accordance with the law, ensure their labor safety, and pay their wages in full and on time.

Article 23 An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall participate in social insurance in accordance with state regulations and pay social insurance premiums for its employees.

Article 24 An individual sole proprietorship enterprise may apply for loans, obtain land use rights, and enjoy other rights stipulated by laws and administrative regulations in accordance with the law.

Article 25 No unit or individual may, in violation of laws or administrative regulations, compel an individual sole proprietorship enterprise to provide financial, material, or human resources in any manner; the enterprise has the right to refuse any illegal compulsion to provide such resources.

## Chapter IV Dissolution and Liquidation of Individual Sole Proprietorship Enterprises

Article 26 An individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall be dissolved under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The investor decides to dissolve;

(2) The investor dies or is declared dead, and there is no heir or the heir decides to renounce the inheritance;

(3) The business license is revoked in accordance with the law; or

(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 27 Upon dissolution of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, the investor shall conduct liquidation on his own, or the creditors may apply to the People's Court to appoint a liquidator for liquidation.

If the investor conducts liquidation on his own, he shall notify creditors in writing within 15 days before liquidation; if notification is impossible, a public announcement shall be made. Creditors shall declare their claims to the investor within 30 days from receipt of the notice, or within 60 days from the date of the public announcement if no notice is received.

Article 28 After the dissolution of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, the original investor shall still bear the responsibility for repaying debts incurred during the enterprise's existence, but this responsibility shall be extinguished if the creditor fails to demand repayment within five years.

Article 29 Upon dissolution of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, the property shall be liquidated in the following order:

(1) Wages and social insurance premiums owed to employees;

(2) Taxes owed; and

(3) Other debts.

Article 30 During the liquidation period, an individual sole proprietorship enterprise shall not engage in business activities unrelated to the purpose of liquidation. Before settling debts in accordance with the preceding article, the investor shall not transfer or conceal property.

Article 31 If the property of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise is insufficient to settle its debts, the investor shall settle them with his other personal property.

Article 32 After the liquidation of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise is completed, the investor or the liquidator appointed by the People's Court shall prepare a liquidation report and go through the cancellation of registration with the registration authority within 15 days.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 33 If, in violation of this Law, false documents are submitted or other deceptive means are used to obtain enterprise registration, the violator shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not more than 5,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked concurrently.

Article 34 If, in violation of this Law, the name used by an individual sole proprietorship enterprise does not match the name registered with the registration authority, the violator shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified time limit and fined not more than 2,000 yuan.

Article 35 If a business license is altered, leased, or transferred, the violator shall be ordered to make corrections, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than 3,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

If a business license is forged, the violator shall be ordered to suspend business, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan shall be imposed. If a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 36 If an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, without justifiable reasons, fails to commence business for more than six months after establishment, or suspends business on its own for six consecutive months or more after commencing business, its business license shall be revoked.

Article 37 If, in violation of this Law, business activities are conducted in the name of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise without obtaining a business license, the violator shall be ordered to cease business activities and fined not more than 3,000 yuan.

If, when registered items of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise change, the relevant change registration is not handled in accordance with this Law, the violator shall be ordered to handle the change registration within a specified time limit; if the order is not complied with within the time limit, a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 38 If the personnel entrusted or employed by the investor to manage the affairs of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise violate the contract signed between the parties and cause damage to the investor, they shall bear civil liability for compensation.

Article 39 If an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, in violation of this Law, infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of employees, fails to ensure their labor safety, or fails to pay social insurance premiums, it shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations, and the responsible persons shall be held accountable.

Article 40 If the personnel entrusted or employed by the investor violate the provisions of Article 20 of this Law and infringe upon the property rights and interests of an individual sole proprietorship enterprise, they shall be ordered to return the embezzled property; if losses are caused to the enterprise, they shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law; if there are illegal gains, they shall be confiscated; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 41 If, in violation of laws or administrative regulations, an individual sole proprietorship enterprise is compelled to provide financial, material, or human resources, the violator shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations, and the responsible persons shall be held accountable.

Article 42 If an individual sole proprietorship enterprise or its investor conceals or transfers property to evade debts before or during liquidation, the property shall be recovered in accordance with the law, and punishment shall be imposed in accordance with relevant regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 43 If an investor, in violation of this Law, should bear civil liability for compensation and pay fines or penalties, and his property is insufficient to pay, or if he is sentenced to confiscation of property, he shall first bear civil liability for compensation.

Article 44 If a registration authority registers an individual sole proprietorship enterprise that does not meet the conditions stipulated in this Law, or fails to register an enterprise that meets the conditions, the directly responsible persons shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 45 If the relevant responsible persons of the superior department of a registration authority compel the registration authority to register an enterprise that does not meet the conditions stipulated in this Law, or fail to register an enterprise that meets the conditions, or cover up the illegal registration acts of the registration authority, the directly responsible persons shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 46 If a registration authority fails to register an application that meets the statutory conditions or fails to respond within the statutory time limit, the party concerned may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 47 This Law shall not apply to wholly foreign-owned enterprises.

Article 48 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2000.
