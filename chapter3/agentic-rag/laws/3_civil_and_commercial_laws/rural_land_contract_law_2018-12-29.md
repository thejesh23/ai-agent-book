# Rural Land Contract Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 29, 2002

Promulgated by Order No. 73 of the President of the People's Republic of China on August 29, 2002

First amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Rural Land Contract Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution with a view to consolidating and improving the two-tier management system that combines centralized and decentralized operations on the basis of household contract management, maintaining the stability and long-term continuity of rural land contractual relationships, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of parties involved in rural land contract and management, and promoting the development of agriculture and the rural economy as well as social harmony and stability in rural areas.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "rural land" means land owned by peasant collectives and land owned by the State but lawfully used by peasant collectives, including cultivated land, forestland, grassland, and other land lawfully used for agriculture.

Article 3 The State applies the system of rural land contract and management.

Rural land shall be contracted by households within rural collective economic organizations. For barren hills, gullies, hills, and beaches, among other rural land, which are unsuitable for household contract, such land may be contracted through bidding, auction, open negotiation, or other means.

Article 4 After rural land is contracted, the nature of land ownership shall remain unchanged. Contracted land may not be bought or sold.

Article 5 Members of a rural collective economic organization have the right to contract rural land offered by the said collective economic organization in accordance with the law.

No organization or individual may deprive or illegally restrict the right of members of a rural collective economic organization to contract land.

Article 6 In rural land contracting, women and men enjoy equal rights. The legitimate rights and interests of women shall be protected during contracting, and no organization or individual may deprive or infringe upon the land contract and management rights that women are entitled to.

Article 7 Rural land contracting shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and justice, and properly handle the interests of the State, the collective, and individuals.

Article 8 The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of owners of collectively owned land and safeguards the land contract and management rights of the contractor. No organization or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 9 After contracting land, the contractor enjoys the land contract and management rights and may operate the land itself, or retain the land contract rights and transfer the land management rights of the contracted land for others to operate.

Article 10 The State protects the contractor's right to transfer land management rights lawfully, voluntarily, and for compensation, and safeguards the legitimate rights and interests of the holder of land management rights. No organization or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 11 Rural land contract and management operations shall comply with laws and regulations, and protect the rational development and sustainable use of land resources. Contracted land may not be used for non-agricultural construction without lawful approval.

The State encourages increased investment in land, improvement of soil fertility, and enhancement of agricultural production capacity.

Article 12 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry, and grassland under the State Council shall, in accordance with their respective duties as prescribed by the State Council, be responsible for guiding rural land contract and management and the management of contract and management agreements nationwide.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry, and grassland under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective duties, be responsible for rural land contract and management and the management of contract and management agreements within their respective administrative regions.

The people's government of a township (town) shall be responsible for rural land contract and management and the management of contract and management agreements within its administrative region.

## Chapter II Household Contracts

### Section 1 Rights and Obligations of the Party Offering the Contract and the Contractor

Article 13 Land owned by peasant collectives that is collectively owned by village peasants shall be offered for contract by the village collective economic organization or the villagers' committee. Land that is already owned by peasant collectives of two or more rural collective economic organizations within a village shall be offered for contract by each such rural collective economic organization or villagers' group within the village. Where the village collective economic organization or villagers' committee offers the contract, it may not alter the ownership of land owned by peasant collectives of each collective economic organization within the village.

Rural land owned by the State but lawfully used by peasant collectives shall be offered for contract by the rural collective economic organization, villagers' committee, or villagers' group using the land.

Article 14 The party offering the contract enjoys the following rights:

(1) To offer for contract rural land owned by the collective or owned by the State but lawfully used by the collective;
(2) To supervise the contractor's rational use and protection of the land in accordance with the purposes stipulated in the contract agreement;
(3) To stop the contractor from damaging the contracted land and agricultural resources;
(4) Other rights provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 15 The party offering the contract assumes the following obligations:

(1) To maintain the contractor's land contract and management rights and may not unlawfully alter or terminate the contract agreement;
(2) To respect the contractor's autonomy in production and operation and may not interfere with the contractor's lawful normal production and operation activities;
(3) To provide the contractor with services such as production, technology, and information in accordance with the contract agreement;
(4) To implement the overall land use plan of the county and township (town) and organize the construction of agricultural infrastructure within the collective economic organization;
(5) Other obligations provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 16 The contractor in a household contract is a household within the collective economic organization.

Family members within a household lawfully and equally enjoy various rights and interests in the contracted land.

Article 17 The contractor enjoys the following rights:

(1) To lawfully enjoy the right to use and receive benefits from the contracted land, and to have the right to independently organize production and operation and dispose of products;
(2) To lawfully exchange or transfer land contract and management rights;
(3) To lawfully transfer land management rights;
(4) To obtain corresponding compensation in accordance with the law when the contracted land is lawfully expropriated, requisitioned, or occupied;
(5) Other rights provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 18 The contractor assumes the following obligations:

(1) To maintain the agricultural use of the land and may not use it for non-agricultural construction without lawful approval;
(2) To lawfully protect and rationally use the land and may not cause permanent damage to the land;
(3) Other obligations provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

### Section 2 Principles and Procedures for Contracting

Article 19 Land contracting shall follow the following principles:

(1) When contracting is organized uniformly in accordance with regulations, members of the collective economic organization shall lawfully and equally exercise the right to contract land, and may also voluntarily waive the right to contract land;
(2) Democratic consultation, fairness, and reasonableness;
(3) The contract plan shall, in accordance with Article 13 of this Law, be lawfully approved by more than two-thirds of the members of the villagers' meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the villagers' representatives;
(4) The contracting procedures shall be lawful.

Article 20 Land contracting shall be carried out in accordance with the following procedures:

(1) The villagers' meeting of the members of the collective economic organization elects a contracting working group;
(2) The contracting working group drafts and publishes the contract plan in accordance with laws and regulations;
(3) A villagers' meeting of the members of the collective economic organization is lawfully convened to discuss and adopt the contract plan;
(4) The contract plan is publicly organized and implemented;
(5) The contract agreement is signed.

### Section 3 Contract Term and Contract Agreement

Article 21 The term for contracting cultivated land is thirty years. The term for contracting grassland is thirty to fifty years. The term for contracting forestland is thirty to seventy years.

Upon expiration of the term for contracting cultivated land as provided in the preceding paragraph, the term shall be extended by another thirty years. Upon expiration of the term for contracting grassland or forestland, the term shall be extended accordingly in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 22 The party offering the contract shall sign a written contract agreement with the contractor.

A contract agreement generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The names of the party offering the contract and the contractor, and the names and domiciles of the responsible person of the party offering the contract and the representative of the contractor;
(2) The name, location, area, and quality grade of the contracted land;
(3) The term of the contract and its start and end dates;
(4) The purpose of the contracted land;
(5) The rights and obligations of the party offering the contract and the contractor;
(6) Liability for breach of contract.

Article 23 A contract agreement takes effect from the date of its conclusion. The contractor acquires land contract and management rights from the time the contract agreement takes effect.

Article 24 The State implements unified registration for cultivated land, forestland, grassland, and other land. The registration authority shall issue certificates such as the land contract and management rights certificate or forestland rights certificate to the contractor, and register and record them to confirm the land contract and management rights.

Certificates such as the land contract and management rights certificate or forestland rights certificate shall list all family members who hold land contract and management rights.

The registration authority may not charge any fees other than the cost of the certificate as prescribed.

Article 25 After a contract agreement takes effect, the party offering the contract may not alter or terminate it due to changes in the person in charge or responsible person, nor may it be altered or terminated due to the division or merger of the collective economic organization.

Article 26 State organs and their staff may not use their authority to interfere with rural land contracting or alter or terminate contract agreements.

### Section 4 Protection, Exchange, and Transfer of Land Contract and Management Rights

Article 27 During the contract term, the party offering the contract may not take back the contracted land.

The State protects the land contract and management rights of rural households that have moved to cities. The surrender of land contract and management rights may not be made a condition for a rural household to settle in a city.

During the contract term, if a contracting rural household settles in a city, it shall be guided and supported to lawfully transfer the land contract and management rights within the collective economic organization or return the contracted land to the party offering the contract on a voluntary and compensated basis, and it may also be encouraged to transfer the land management rights.

When the contractor returns the contracted land during the contract term or the party offering the contract lawfully takes back the contracted land, the contractor is entitled to corresponding compensation for the increased land productivity resulting from its investment in the contracted land.

Article 28 During the contract term, the party offering the contract may not adjust the contracted land.

During the contract term, if adjustments to cultivated land or grassland contracted by individual households are necessary due to special circumstances such as severe damage to the contracted land caused by natural disasters, such adjustments must be approved by more than two-thirds of the members of the villagers' meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the villagers' representatives, and reported to the people's government of the township (town) and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry, and grassland under the people's government at the county level for approval. If the contract agreement stipulates that adjustments are not allowed, the agreement shall prevail.

Article 29 The following land shall be used for adjusting contracted land or contracting to newly added population:

(1) Reserve land lawfully reserved by the collective economic organization;
(2) Land increased through lawful reclamation or other means;
(3) Land lawfully taken back by the party offering the contract and land lawfully and voluntarily returned by the contractor.

Article 30 During the contract term, the contractor may voluntarily return the contracted land to the party offering the contract. If the contractor voluntarily returns the contracted land, it may receive reasonable compensation, but shall notify the party offering the contract in writing at least six months in advance. If the contractor returns the contracted land during the contract term, it may not request to contract land again during that term.

Article 31 During the contract term, if a woman marries and has not obtained contracted land in her new place of residence, the party offering the contract may not take back her original contracted land. If a woman divorces or becomes widowed and continues to live in her original place of residence, or does not live in her original place of residence but has not obtained contracted land in her new place of residence, the party offering the contract may not take back her original contracted land.

Article 32 The contract benefits due to the contractor shall be inherited in accordance with the provisions of the Inheritance Law.

If the contractor of forestland dies, its successor may continue the contract during the contract term.

Article 33 For the convenience of cultivation or for their respective needs, contractors may exchange land contract and management rights of land belonging to the same collective economic organization and report to the party offering the contract for record.

Article 34 With the consent of the party offering the contract, the contractor may transfer all or part of the land contract and management rights to other households within the collective economic organization, and such households shall establish a new contractual relationship with the party offering the contract, whereupon the original contractual relationship between the contractor and the party offering the contract for that land shall terminate.

Article 35 When land contract and management rights are exchanged or transferred, the parties may apply to the registration authority for registration. Without registration, they may not be asserted against a bona fide third party.

### Section 5 Land Management Rights

Article 36 The contractor may independently decide to transfer land management rights to others through leasing (subcontracting), equity contribution, or other means in accordance with the law, and report to the party offering the contract for record.

Article 37 The holder of land management rights has the right to occupy rural land within the term agreed upon in the contract, independently carry out agricultural production and operation, and obtain income.

Article 38 The transfer of land management rights shall follow the following principles:

(1) Lawful, voluntary, and for compensation; no organization or individual may force or hinder the transfer of land management rights;
(2) The nature of land ownership and the agricultural use of the land may not be changed, and the comprehensive agricultural production capacity and agricultural ecological environment may not be damaged;
(3) The term of the transfer may not exceed the remaining term of the contract;
(4) The transferee must have agricultural operation capacity or qualifications;
(5) Under equal conditions, members of the collective economic organization have priority.

Article 39 The price for the transfer of land management rights shall be determined through negotiation between the parties. The income from the transfer belongs to the contractor, and no organization or individual may arbitrarily intercept or withhold it.

Article 40 For the transfer of land management rights, the parties shall sign a written transfer contract.

A land management rights transfer contract generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The names and domiciles of the parties;
(2) The name, location, area, and quality grade of the transferred land;
(3) The term of the transfer and its start and end dates;
(4) The purpose of the transferred land;
(5) The rights and obligations of the parties;
(6) The transfer price and payment method;
(7) The attribution of relevant compensation when the land is lawfully expropriated, requisitioned, or occupied;
(8) Liability for breach of contract.

If the contractor entrusts the land to another person for cultivation for a period not exceeding one year, a written contract may not be required.

Article 41 If the term of the transfer of land management rights is five years or more, the parties may apply to the registration authority for registration of the land management rights. Without registration, they may not be asserted against a bona fide third party.

Article 42 The contractor may not unilaterally terminate the land management rights transfer contract, except in the following circumstances of the transferee:

(1) Unauthorized change of the agricultural use of the land;
(2) Abandonment of cultivation for two consecutive years or more;
(3) Causing serious damage to the land or seriously damaging the land ecological environment;
(4) Other serious breaches of contract.

Article 43 With the consent of the contractor, the transferee may lawfully invest in improving the soil and constructing ancillary and supporting facilities for agricultural production, and receive reasonable compensation for its investment portion in accordance with the contract agreement.

Article 44 If the contractor transfers the land management rights, its contractual relationship with the party offering the contract remains unchanged.

Article 45 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish systems for qualification review, project evaluation, and risk prevention for industrial and commercial enterprises and other social capital to obtain land management rights through transfer.

If industrial and commercial enterprises or other social capital obtain land management rights through transfer, the collective economic organization may charge an appropriate management fee.

Specific measures shall be prescribed by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, forestry, and grassland under the State Council.

Article 46 With the written consent of the contractor and after reporting to the collective economic organization for record, the transferee may further transfer the land management rights.

Article 47 The contractor may use the land management rights of the contracted land as security for financing from financial institutions and report to the party offering the contract for record. The land management rights obtained by the transferee through transfer may, with the written consent of the contractor and after reporting to the party offering the contract for record, be used as security for financing from financial institutions.

The security interest is established from the time the financing security contract takes effect. The parties may apply to the registration authority for registration; without registration, it may not be asserted against a bona fide third party.

When the security interest is realized, the secured party has the right to be paid in priority from the land management rights.

Measures for financing security of land management rights shall be prescribed by the relevant departments under the State Council.

## Chapter III Other Methods of Contracting

Article 48 For barren hills, gullies, hills, and beaches, among other rural land, which are unsuitable for household contract, if contracted through bidding, auction, open negotiation, or other means, the provisions of this Chapter shall apply.

Article 49 Where rural land is contracted by other methods, a contract agreement shall be signed, and the contractor shall obtain land management rights. The rights and obligations of the parties, the contract term, and other matters shall be determined through negotiation between the parties. If contracted through bidding or auction, the contract fee shall be determined through open bidding or auction; if contracted through open negotiation or other means, the contract fee shall be agreed upon by the parties.

Article 50 Barren hills, gullies, hills, and beaches may be directly contracted for operation through bidding, auction, open negotiation, or other means, or the land management rights may be converted into shares and distributed to members of the collective economic organization before contracting for operation or cooperative operation with shares.

When contracting barren hills, gullies, hills, and beaches, relevant laws and administrative regulations shall be complied with to prevent soil erosion and protect the ecological environment.

Article 51 Where rural land is contracted by other methods, under equal conditions, members of the collective economic organization have the right to contract first.

Article 52 If the party offering the contract contracts rural land to a unit or individual outside the collective economic organization, it shall first obtain the approval of more than two-thirds of the members of the villagers' meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the villagers' representatives, and report to the people's government of the township (town) for approval.

If the contract is made with a unit or individual outside the collective economic organization, the creditworthiness and operation capacity of the contractor shall be examined before the contract agreement is signed.

Article 53 Where rural land is contracted through bidding, auction, open negotiation, or other means, and a title certificate is obtained after lawful registration, the land management rights may be transferred through leasing, equity contribution, mortgage, or other means in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Where land management rights are obtained through bidding, auction, open negotiation, or other means in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter, if the contractor dies, the contract benefits due to it shall be inherited in accordance with the provisions of the Inheritance Law; during the contract term, its successor may continue the contract.

## Chapter IV Dispute Resolution and Legal Liability

Article 55 If a dispute arises over land contract and management, the parties may resolve it through negotiation, or request mediation by the villagers' committee, the people's government of the township (town), or other entities.

If the parties are unwilling to negotiate or mediate, or if negotiation or mediation fails, they may apply for arbitration to a rural land contract arbitration institution, or directly file a lawsuit with a people's court.

Article 56 Any organization or individual that infringes upon land contract and management rights or land management rights shall bear civil liability.

Article 57 If the party offering the contract commits any of the following acts, it shall bear civil liability such as cessation of infringement, removal of obstacles, elimination of danger, return of property, restoration of the original state, and compensation for losses:

(1) Interfering with the contractor's lawful autonomy in production and operation;
(2) Taking back or adjusting contracted land in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(3) Forcing or hindering the contractor from exchanging or transferring land contract and management rights or transferring land management rights;(4) Forcing the contractor to waive or alter the land contractual management right by abusing the principle of majority rule;

(5) Withdrawing contracted land for bidding-based contracting under the pretext of dividing land into "ration fields" and "responsibility fields";

(6) Withdrawing contracted land to offset debts;

(7) Depriving or infringing upon women's land contractual management rights lawfully enjoyed;

(8) Other acts infringing upon land contractual management rights.

Article 58 Any provisions in a contract that violate the will of the contractor or contravene mandatory provisions of laws or administrative regulations concerning the prohibition of withdrawing or adjusting contracted land shall be null and void.

Article 59 If a party fails to perform its contractual obligations or performs them in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, it shall bear liability for breach of contract in accordance with the law.

Article 60 If any organization or individual compels the exchange or transfer of land contractual management rights or the circulation of land management rights, such exchange, transfer, or circulation shall be null and void.

Article 61 Any organization or individual that unlawfully withholds or deducts proceeds from the exchange or transfer of land contractual management rights or the circulation of land management rights shall return such proceeds.

Article 62 Anyone who, in violation of land management laws and regulations, unlawfully expropriates, requisitions, or occupies land, or embezzles or misappropriates compensation fees for land expropriation or requisition, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if causing damage to others, shall bear liability for damages and other responsibilities.

Article 63 If the contractor or the land management right holder unlawfully uses contracted land for non-agricultural construction, the relevant competent department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the law.

If the contractor causes permanent damage to the contracted land, the party that issued the contract has the right to stop such acts and demand compensation for the losses incurred.

Article 64 If the land management right holder unilaterally changes the agricultural use of the land, abandons cultivation for two consecutive years or more, causes serious damage to the land, or severely damages the land's ecological environment, and the contractor fails to terminate the land management right circulation contract within a reasonable period, the party that issued the contract has the right to demand termination of the land management right circulation contract. The land management right holder shall compensate for the damage caused to the land and its ecological environment.

Article 65 If state organs and their staff, by abusing their authority, interfere with rural land contractual management, alter or terminate contractual management contracts, interfere with the lawful production and operation autonomy of the parties to contractual management, or compel or obstruct the parties to contractual management from exchanging or transferring land contractual management rights or circulating land management rights, thereby infringing upon land contractual management rights or land management rights and causing losses to the parties to contractual management, they shall bear liability for damages and other responsibilities; if the circumstances are serious, the superior organ or the unit to which they belong shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible personnel; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 66 Contracts concluded before the implementation of this Law in accordance with the state's provisions on rural land contracting, including those with a contract period longer than that stipulated in this Law, shall remain valid after the implementation of this Law, and the land shall not be re-contracted. If certificates such as land contractual management right certificates or forest right certificates have not been issued to the contractor, such certificates shall be issued retroactively.

Article 67 If reserve land was retained before the implementation of this Law, the area of reserve land shall not exceed five percent of the total arable land area of the collective economic organization. If it is less than five percent, no additional reserve land may be added.

If no reserve land was retained before the implementation of this Law, no reserve land may be retained after the implementation of this Law.

Article 68 The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with this Law and in light of the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, formulate implementation measures.

Article 69 The principles and procedures for confirming the membership status of rural collective economic organization members shall be stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 70 This Law shall come into force on March 1, 2003.
