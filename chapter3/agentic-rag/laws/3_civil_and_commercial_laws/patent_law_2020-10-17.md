# Patent Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on March 12, 1984

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Revising the Patent Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 4, 1992

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Revising the Patent Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 25, 2000

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Revising the Patent Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect the legitimate rights and interests of patentees, encourage inventions and creations, promote the application of inventions and creations, enhance innovation capabilities, and advance scientific and technological progress as well as economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "inventions and creations" mean inventions, utility models, and designs.

"Invention" means a new technical solution proposed for a product, a process, or an improvement thereof.

"Utility model" means a new technical solution that is practical and suitable for use, proposed for the shape, structure, or combination thereof of a product.

"Design" means a new design that is aesthetically pleasing and suitable for industrial application, created for the overall or partial shape, pattern, or combination thereof, as well as the combination of color with shape or pattern, of a product.

Article 3 The patent administration department under the State Council is responsible for the administration of patent work nationwide; it uniformly accepts and examines patent applications and grants patent rights in accordance with the law.

The departments in charge of patent work under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government are responsible for the administration of patent work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 4 Where an invention or creation for which a patent is applied for involves national security or major interests and requires confidentiality, it shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 5 No patent right shall be granted for an invention or creation that violates the law, social morality, or harms public interests.

No patent right shall be granted for an invention or creation that is completed based on genetic resources obtained or utilized in violation of the provisions of laws or administrative regulations and that relies on such genetic resources.

Article 6 An invention or creation made in the course of performing the duties of an employee or primarily using the material and technical conditions of the employer is a service invention or creation. The right to apply for a patent for a service invention or creation belongs to the employer. After the application is approved, the employer is the patentee. The employer may lawfully dispose of the right to apply for a patent and the patent right for the service invention or creation, promoting the implementation and application of the relevant invention or creation.

For a non-service invention or creation, the right to apply for a patent belongs to the inventor or designer. After the application is approved, the inventor or designer is the patentee.

Where an invention or creation is made using the material and technical conditions of the employer, and the employer and the inventor or designer have entered into a contract stipulating the ownership of the right to apply for a patent and the patent right, such stipulation shall prevail.

Article 7 No entity or individual shall suppress an application for a patent for a non-service invention or creation made by an inventor or designer.

Article 8 For an invention or creation made jointly by two or more entities or individuals, or made by one entity or individual upon commission by another entity or individual, the right to apply for a patent belongs, unless otherwise agreed, to the entity or individual that made or jointly made the invention or creation. After the application is approved, the entity or individual that filed the application is the patentee.

Article 9 Only one patent right may be granted for the same invention or creation. However, where the same applicant files both a utility model patent application and an invention patent application for the same invention or creation on the same day, if the utility model patent right obtained first has not yet been terminated and the applicant declares the waiver of the utility model patent right, an invention patent right may be granted.

Where two or more applicants separately file patent applications for the same invention or creation, the patent right shall be granted to the applicant who filed first.

Article 10 The right to apply for a patent and the patent right may be assigned.

Where a Chinese entity or individual assigns the right to apply for a patent or the patent right to a foreigner, foreign enterprise, or other foreign organization, it shall go through the formalities in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations.

To assign the right to apply for a patent or the patent right, the parties shall enter into a written contract and register it with the patent administration department under the State Council, which shall announce it. The assignment of the right to apply for a patent or the patent right takes effect from the date of registration.

Article 11 After the grant of a patent right for an invention or utility model, except as otherwise provided in this Law, no entity or individual may, without the authorization of the patentee, exploit the patent, i.e., may not manufacture, use, offer to sell, sell, or import the patented product, or use the patented process, or use, offer to sell, sell, or import the product directly obtained by the patented process, for production or business purposes.

After the grant of a patent right for a design, no entity or individual may, without the authorization of the patentee, exploit the patent, i.e., may not manufacture, offer to sell, sell, or import the patented design product for production or business purposes.

Article 12 Any entity or individual exploiting the patent of another shall enter into an exploitation license contract with the patentee and pay the patentee a royalty for the use of the patent. The licensee has no right to allow any entity or individual other than those stipulated in the contract to exploit the patent.

Article 13 After the publication of an invention patent application, the applicant may require the entity or individual exploiting the invention to pay an appropriate fee.

Article 14 Where co-owners of the right to apply for a patent or the patent right have an agreement on the exercise of the right, such agreement shall prevail. In the absence of an agreement, the co-owners may individually exploit the patent or license others to exploit the patent by way of a non-exclusive license; where the patent is licensed to others, the royalties collected shall be distributed among the co-owners.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, the exercise of a jointly owned right to apply for a patent or patent right shall require the consent of all co-owners.

Article 15 The entity to which a patent right is granted shall reward the inventor or designer of a service invention or creation. After the patented invention or creation is exploited, the entity shall provide a reasonable remuneration to the inventor or designer based on the scope of its promotion and application and the economic benefits obtained.

The State encourages entities to which patent rights are granted to implement property rights incentives, such as equity, options, and profit sharing, so that inventors or designers can reasonably share in the innovation gains.

Article 16 An inventor or designer has the right to be named as such in the patent document.

The patentee has the right to affix a patent marking on the patented product or the packaging of that product.

Article 17 Where a foreigner, foreign enterprise, or other foreign organization without a habitual residence or place of business in China files a patent application in China, the application shall be handled in accordance with this Law, based on an agreement concluded between the country to which the applicant belongs and China, or an international treaty to which both countries are parties, or on the principle of reciprocity.

Article 18 Where a foreigner, foreign enterprise, or other foreign organization without a habitual residence or place of business in China files a patent application or handles other patent matters in China, it shall entrust a patent agency lawfully established to do so.

Chinese entities or individuals may entrust a lawfully established patent agency to file patent applications or handle other patent matters in China.

Patent agencies shall comply with laws and administrative regulations, handle patent applications or other patent matters according to the entrustment of the principal, and keep confidential the content of the principal's invention or creation, except where the patent application has been published or announced. Specific measures for the administration of patent agencies shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 19 Any entity or individual that intends to file a patent application abroad for an invention or utility model made in China shall first submit it to the patent administration department under the State Council for a confidentiality review. The procedures and time limit for the confidentiality review shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Chinese entities or individuals may file international patent applications in accordance with the relevant international treaties to which the People's Republic of China is a party. Applicants filing international patent applications shall comply with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

The patent administration department under the State Council shall handle international patent applications in accordance with the relevant international treaties to which the People's Republic of China is a party, this Law, and the relevant provisions of the State Council.

For an invention or utility model for which a patent application is filed abroad in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, no patent right shall be granted if a patent application is filed in China.

Article 20 The filing of patent applications and the exercise of patent rights shall follow the principle of good faith. Patent rights shall not be abused to harm the public interest or the legitimate rights and interests of others.

Where the abuse of patent rights to exclude or restrict competition constitutes a monopolistic act, it shall be dealt with in accordance with the Anti-Monopoly Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 21 The patent administration department under the State Council shall handle patent applications and requests in accordance with the law, with the requirements of objectivity, fairness, accuracy, and timeliness.

The patent administration department under the State Council shall strengthen the construction of a public service system for patent information, publish patent information completely, accurately, and promptly, provide basic patent data, regularly publish the Patent Gazette, and promote the dissemination and utilization of patent information.

Before the publication or announcement of a patent application, the staff of the patent administration department under the State Council and relevant persons have the obligation to keep its content confidential.

## Chapter II Conditions for Granting Patent Rights

Article 22 An invention or utility model for which a patent right is granted shall possess novelty, inventiveness, and practical applicability.

"Novelty" means that the invention or utility model does not form part of the prior art; nor has any entity or individual filed an application with the patent administration department under the State Council for the same invention or utility model before the date of filing, which is recorded in patent application documents published or patent documents announced after the date of filing.

"Inventiveness" means that, compared with the prior art, the invention has prominent substantive features and represents a notable progress, and the utility model has substantive features and represents progress.

"Practical applicability" means that the invention or utility model can be manufactured or used and can produce positive effects.

For the purposes of this Law, "prior art" means technology that is known to the public domestically and abroad before the date of filing.

Article 23 A design for which a patent right is granted shall not form part of the prior design; nor has any entity or individual filed an application with the patent administration department under the State Council for the same design before the date of filing, which is recorded in patent documents announced after the date of filing.

Compared with the prior design or a combination of features of the prior design, the design for which a patent right is granted shall have a clear distinction.

The design for which a patent right is granted shall not conflict with the lawful rights obtained by others before the date of filing.

For the purposes of this Law, "prior design" means a design that is known to the public domestically and abroad before the date of filing.

Article 24 An invention or creation for which a patent is applied for shall not lose its novelty if, within six months before the date of filing, any of the following events occurs:

(1) It is first disclosed for the purpose of public interest in the event of a state of emergency or extraordinary circumstances in the country;

(2) It is first exhibited at an international exhibition sponsored or recognized by the Chinese Government;

(3) It is first published at a prescribed academic or technical conference;

(4) Its content is disclosed by another person without the consent of the applicant.

Article 25 No patent right shall be granted for any of the following:

(1) Scientific discoveries;

(2) Rules and methods of intellectual activities;

(3) Methods for the diagnosis or treatment of diseases;

(4) Animal and plant varieties;

(5) Methods of nuclear transformation and substances obtained by means of nuclear transformation;

(6) Designs that serve primarily as an identifier for patterns, colors, or a combination thereof on planar printed matter.

For the production methods of the products listed in item (4) of the preceding paragraph, patent rights may be granted in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

## Chapter III Patent Applications

Article 26 To apply for a patent for an invention or utility model, documents such as a request, a description, an abstract, and claims shall be submitted.

The request shall state the name of the invention or utility model, the name of the inventor, the name or title and address of the applicant, and other relevant matters.

The description shall clearly and completely describe the invention or utility model in a manner sufficient for a person skilled in the relevant technical field to implement it; where necessary, drawings shall be included. The abstract shall briefly state the technical key points of the invention or utility model.

The claims shall be based on the description and shall clearly and concisely define the scope of patent protection sought.

For an invention or creation completed relying on genetic resources, the applicant shall state in the patent application documents the direct source and original source of the genetic resources; if the applicant cannot state the original source, reasons shall be provided.

Article 27 To apply for a patent for a design, documents such as a request, drawings or photographs of the design, and a brief description of the design shall be submitted.

The relevant drawings or photographs submitted by the applicant shall clearly show the design of the product for which patent protection is sought.

Article 28 The date on which the patent administration department under the State Council receives the patent application documents is the date of filing. If the application documents are sent by mail, the postmark date of mailing is the date of filing.

Article 29 Where, within twelve months from the date on which an applicant first files a patent application for an invention or utility model in a foreign country, or within six months from the date on which an applicant first files a patent application for a design in a foreign country, the applicant files a patent application in China for the same subject matter, the applicant may enjoy a right of priority, in accordance with an agreement concluded between that foreign country and China, or an international treaty to which both countries are parties, or on the principle of mutual recognition of the right of priority.

Where, within twelve months from the date on which an applicant first files a patent application for an invention or utility model in China, or within six months from the date on which an applicant first files a patent application for a design in China, the applicant files a patent application with the patent administration department under the State Council for the same subject matter, the applicant may enjoy a right of priority.

Article 30 An applicant claiming the right of priority for an invention or utility model patent shall make a written declaration at the time of filing and, within sixteen months from the date of the first filing, submit a copy of the patent application documents first filed.

An applicant claiming the right of priority for a design patent shall make a written declaration at the time of filing and, within three months, submit a copy of the patent application documents first filed.

If the applicant fails to make a written declaration or fails to submit a copy of the patent application documents within the time limit, the claim for the right of priority shall be deemed not to have been made.

Article 31 A patent application for an invention or utility model shall be limited to one invention or utility model. Two or more inventions or utility models that belong to a single general inventive concept may be filed as one application.

A patent application for a design shall be limited to one design. Two or more similar designs for the same product, or two or more designs for products that belong to the same class and are sold or used in sets, may be filed as one application.

Article 32 An applicant may withdraw a patent application at any time before the patent right is granted.

Article 33 An applicant may amend the patent application documents, but the amendment to the application documents for an invention or utility model patent shall not go beyond the scope of the original description and claims, and the amendment to the application documents for a design patent shall not go beyond the scope of the original drawings or photographs.

## Chapter IV Examination and Approval of Patent Applications

Article 34 After receiving an invention patent application, the patent administration department under the State Council shall, upon preliminary examination and finding that it meets the requirements of this Law, publish it promptly after the expiration of eighteen months from the date of filing. The patent administration department under the State Council may publish the application earlier upon the request of the applicant.

Article 35 Within three years from the date of filing of an invention patent application, the patent administration department under the State Council may, upon the request made by the applicant at any time, conduct substantive examination of the application. If the applicant fails to request substantive examination within the time limit without justifiable reasons, the application shall be deemed to have been withdrawn.

When the patent administration department under the State Council deems it necessary, it may conduct substantive examination of an invention patent application on its own initiative.

Article 36 When requesting substantive examination, the applicant for an invention patent shall submit reference materials relating to the invention before the date of filing.

If an invention patent application has been filed in a foreign country, the patent administration department under the State Council may require the applicant to submit, within a specified time limit, materials from that country on the search conducted for the examination of the application or on the results of the examination. If the applicant fails to submit such materials within the time limit without justifiable reasons, the application shall be deemed to have been withdrawn.

Article 37 After conducting substantive examination of an invention patent application, if the patent administration department under the State Council finds that the application does not comply with the provisions of this Law, it shall notify the applicant and require the applicant to state opinions within a specified time limit or to amend the application. If the applicant fails to respond within the time limit without justifiable reasons, the application shall be deemed to have been withdrawn.

Article 38 If, after the applicant has stated opinions or made amendments, the patent administration department under the State Council still finds that the invention patent application does not comply with the provisions of this Law, it shall reject the application.

Article 39 If no reason for rejection is found after substantive examination of an invention patent application, the patent administration department under the State Council shall make a decision to grant the invention patent right, issue an invention patent certificate, and simultaneously register and announce it. The invention patent right takes effect from the date of announcement.

Article 40 If no reason for rejection is found after preliminary examination of a utility model or design patent application, the patent administration department under the State Council shall make a decision to grant the utility model patent right or design patent right, issue the corresponding patent certificate, and simultaneously register and announce it. The utility model patent right and design patent right take effect from the date of announcement.

Article 41 If a patent applicant is dissatisfied with the decision of the patent administration department under the State Council to reject the application, the applicant may, within three months from the date of receipt of the notification, request the patent administration department under the State Council to conduct a reexamination. After reexamination, the patent administration department under the State Council shall make a decision and notify the patent applicant.

If a patent applicant is dissatisfied with the reexamination decision of the patent administration department under the State Council, the applicant may, within three months from the date of receipt of the notification, institute legal proceedings in a people's court.

## Chapter V Term, Termination, and Invalidation of Patent Rights

Article 42 The term of an invention patent right is twenty years, the term of a utility model patent right is ten years, and the term of a design patent right is fifteen years, all calculated from the date of filing.

Where an invention patent right is granted after four years from the date of filing of the invention patent application and after three years from the date of the request for substantive examination, the patent administration department under the State Council shall, upon the request of the patentee, compensate for the unreasonable delay in the granting process by extending the term of the patent right, except for unreasonable delays caused by the applicant.To compensate for the time taken for the review and approval of new drug marketing, the patent administrative department of the State Council shall, upon the request of the patentee, grant a patent term extension for a patent related to a new drug that has obtained marketing approval in China. The extension period shall not exceed five years, and the total effective patent term after the new drug is approved for marketing shall not exceed fourteen years.

Article 43 A patentee shall pay an annual fee starting from the year in which the patent right is granted.

Article 44 In any of the following circumstances, the patent right shall terminate before the expiration of its term:

(1) Failure to pay the annual fee as prescribed;
(2) The patentee waives the patent right by a written declaration.

Where a patent right terminates before the expiration of its term, the patent administrative department of the State Council shall register and announce it.

Article 45 As of the date of announcement by the patent administrative department of the State Council of the grant of a patent right, any entity or individual that believes the grant does not comply with the relevant provisions of this Law may request the patent administrative department of the State Council to declare the patent right invalid.

Article 46 The patent administrative department of the State Council shall promptly examine and make a decision on the request for invalidation of a patent right, and notify the requester and the patentee. The decision to declare a patent right invalid shall be registered and announced by the patent administrative department of the State Council.

Any party dissatisfied with the decision of the patent administrative department of the State Council to declare a patent right invalid or to uphold the patent right may, within three months from the date of receipt of the notification, file a lawsuit with a people's court. The people's court shall notify the other party in the invalidation procedure to participate in the proceedings as a third party.

Article 47 A patent right that has been declared invalid shall be deemed to have never existed from the beginning.

The decision to declare a patent right invalid shall have no retroactive effect on any judgment or mediation order on patent infringement that has been made and enforced by a people's court before the declaration of invalidity, on any decision on a patent infringement dispute that has been performed or compulsorily enforced, or on any patent license contract or patent assignment contract that has been performed. However, compensation shall be made for losses caused to others by the patentee's malice.

If, in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the non-repayment of patent infringement damages, patent royalties, or patent assignment fees is clearly contrary to the principle of fairness, all or part of such amounts shall be repaid.

## Chapter VI Special Licensing for Patent Implementation

Article 48 The patent administrative department of the State Council and the departments of local people's governments responsible for patent administration shall, in conjunction with relevant departments at the same level, take measures to strengthen patent public services and promote the implementation and utilization of patents.

Article 49 Where an invention patent owned by a state-owned enterprise or institution is of significant importance to national interests or the public interest, the relevant competent department of the State Council and the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, upon approval by the State Council, decide to promote its application within the approved scope and permit designated entities to implement it, with the implementing entity paying royalties to the patentee in accordance with state regulations.

Article 50 A patentee who voluntarily declares in writing to the patent administrative department of the State Council a willingness to permit any entity or individual to implement the patent, and specifies the method and standard for payment of royalties, shall have the declaration announced by the patent administrative department of the State Council, thereby implementing open licensing. For a declaration of open licensing concerning a utility model or design patent, a patent evaluation report shall be provided.

If a patentee withdraws the declaration of open licensing, it shall be done in writing and announced by the patent administrative department of the State Council. The withdrawal of the open licensing declaration after announcement shall not affect the validity of open licenses previously granted.

Article 51 Any entity or individual wishing to implement a patent under open licensing shall notify the patentee in writing and, upon paying the royalties in accordance with the announced method and standard, shall obtain a patent implementation license.

During the period of open licensing implementation, the annual patent fee payable by the patentee shall be correspondingly reduced or exempted.

A patentee implementing open licensing may grant an ordinary license after negotiating royalties with the licensee, but shall not grant an exclusive or sole license for that patent.

Article 52 Where a dispute arises between parties regarding the implementation of open licensing, the parties shall resolve it through negotiation; if negotiation fails or is not desired, either party may request mediation by the patent administrative department of the State Council or file a lawsuit with a people's court.

Article 53 In any of the following circumstances, the patent administrative department of the State Council may, upon the application of an entity or individual with the conditions for implementation, grant a compulsory license to implement an invention patent or utility model patent:

(1) Where the patentee has failed to implement or fully implement the patent without justifiable reasons within three years from the date of grant of the patent right and within four years from the date of filing the patent application;
(2) Where the patentee's exercise of the patent right has been legally determined to constitute a monopolistic act, and the compulsory license is necessary to eliminate or reduce the adverse effects of such act on competition.

Article 54 In the event of a national emergency or extraordinary circumstances, or for the purposes of the public interest, the patent administrative department of the State Council may grant a compulsory license to implement an invention patent or utility model patent.

Article 55 For the purpose of public health, the patent administrative department of the State Council may grant a compulsory license for the manufacture of patented pharmaceuticals and their export to countries or regions that meet the provisions of relevant international treaties to which the People's Republic of China is a party.

Article 56 Where an invention or utility model for which a patent right has been granted represents a significant technological advancement of considerable economic significance compared to an earlier patented invention or utility model, and its implementation depends on the implementation of the earlier invention or utility model, the patent administrative department of the State Council may, upon the application of the later patentee, grant a compulsory license to implement the earlier invention or utility model.

In the case of granting a compulsory license in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the patent administrative department of the State Council may, upon the application of the earlier patentee, also grant a compulsory license to implement the later invention or utility model.

Article 57 Where the invention-creation involved in a compulsory license relates to semiconductor technology, its implementation shall be limited to purposes of the public interest and the circumstances specified in Article 53(2) of this Law.

Article 58 Except for compulsory licenses granted in accordance with Article 53(2) and Article 55 of this Law, the implementation of a compulsory license shall be primarily for the supply of the domestic market.

Article 59 An entity or individual applying for a compulsory license in accordance with Article 53(1) or Article 56 of this Law shall provide evidence demonstrating that they have requested the patentee for a license to implement the patent on reasonable terms but have been unable to obtain such a license within a reasonable period.

Article 60 The decision of the patent administrative department of the State Council to grant a compulsory license shall be promptly notified to the patentee and shall be registered and announced.

The decision to grant a compulsory license shall specify the scope and duration of implementation based on the reasons for the compulsory license. When the reasons for the compulsory license cease to exist and are unlikely to recur, the patent administrative department of the State Council shall, upon the request of the patentee, make a decision to terminate the compulsory license after review.

Article 61 An entity or individual that has obtained a compulsory license shall not enjoy an exclusive right of implementation and shall have no right to authorize others to implement it.

Article 62 An entity or individual that has obtained a compulsory license shall pay a reasonable royalty to the patentee, or handle the royalty issue in accordance with the provisions of relevant international treaties to which the People's Republic of China is a party. The amount of the royalty shall be negotiated between the parties; if the parties fail to reach an agreement, it shall be determined by the patent administrative department of the State Council.

Article 63 If a patentee is dissatisfied with the decision of the patent administrative department of the State Council regarding the grant of a compulsory license, or if the patentee or the entity or individual that has obtained the compulsory license is dissatisfied with the ruling of the patent administrative department of the State Council on the royalty for the compulsory license, they may file a lawsuit with a people's court within three months from the date of receipt of the notification.

## Chapter VII Protection of Patent Rights

Article 64 The scope of protection of an invention or utility model patent shall be determined by the content of its claims, and the description and drawings may be used to interpret the content of the claims.

The scope of protection of a design patent shall be determined by the design of the product as shown in the drawings or photographs, and the brief description may be used to interpret the design of the product as shown in the drawings or photographs.

Article 65 Where a patent is exploited without the authorization of the patentee, thereby infringing the patent right and causing a dispute, the parties shall resolve it through negotiation; if negotiation fails or is not desired, the patentee or interested party may file a lawsuit with a people's court or request the department responsible for patent administration to handle the matter. When the department responsible for patent administration handles the matter and determines that the infringement is established, it may order the infringer to immediately cease the infringing act. If the party is dissatisfied, it may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the handling notice in accordance with the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China; if the infringer neither files a lawsuit nor ceases the infringing act within the time limit, the department responsible for patent administration may apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement. The department responsible for patent administration handling the matter may, upon the request of the parties, mediate the amount of damages for patent infringement; if mediation fails, the parties may file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 66 Where a patent infringement dispute involves an invention patent for a method of manufacturing a new product, the entity or individual manufacturing the same product shall provide proof that their product manufacturing method is different from the patented method.

Where a patent infringement dispute involves a utility model patent or a design patent, the people's court or the department responsible for patent administration may require the patentee or interested party to produce a patent evaluation report issued by the patent administrative department of the State Council after searching, analyzing, and evaluating the relevant utility model or design, as evidence for hearing or handling the patent infringement dispute; the patentee, interested party, or the alleged infringer may also voluntarily produce a patent evaluation report.

Article 67 In a patent infringement dispute, if the alleged infringer has evidence proving that the technology or design they implemented constitutes prior art or prior design, it shall not constitute patent infringement.

Article 68 Anyone who counterfeits a patent shall, in addition to bearing civil liability in accordance with the law, be ordered by the department responsible for patent enforcement to correct the act and make an announcement, and shall have their illegal gains confiscated and may be fined up to five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of up to 250,000 yuan may be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 69 When investigating and handling suspected patent counterfeiting based on evidence already obtained, the department responsible for patent enforcement shall have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Questioning the relevant parties and investigating the circumstances related to the suspected illegal act;
(2) Conducting on-site inspections of the premises where the suspected illegal act is taking place;
(3) Inspecting and copying contracts, invoices, account books, and other relevant materials related to the suspected illegal act;
(4) Inspecting products related to the suspected illegal act;
(5) Sealing or seizing products that are proven to be counterfeit patents.

When the department responsible for patent administration handles a patent infringement dispute upon the request of the patentee or interested party, it may take the measures listed in items (1), (2), and (4) of the preceding paragraph.

When the department responsible for patent enforcement or the department responsible for patent administration exercises the powers specified in the preceding two paragraphs in accordance with the law, the parties shall provide assistance and cooperation and shall not refuse or obstruct.

Article 70 The patent administrative department of the State Council may, upon the request of the patentee or interested party, handle patent infringement disputes that have a significant national impact.

When the department of a local people's government responsible for patent administration handles a patent infringement dispute upon the request of the patentee or interested party, it may consolidate cases involving infringement of the same patent right within its administrative region; for cases involving infringement of the same patent right across regions, it may request the department of the higher-level local people's government responsible for patent administration to handle them.

Article 71 The amount of damages for patent infringement shall be determined based on the actual losses suffered by the right holder due to the infringement or the profits gained by the infringer from the infringement; if the right holder's losses or the infringer's profits are difficult to determine, the amount shall be reasonably determined by reference to a multiple of the patent license royalties. For willful patent infringement with serious circumstances, the amount of damages may be determined at between one and five times the amount determined by the above methods.

If the right holder's losses, the infringer's profits, and the patent license royalties are all difficult to determine, the people's court may, based on factors such as the type of patent right and the nature and circumstances of the infringement, determine damages of between 30,000 yuan and 5,000,000 yuan.

The amount of damages shall also include the reasonable expenses incurred by the right holder to stop the infringing act.

To determine the amount of damages, where the right holder has made every effort to provide evidence, but the account books and materials related to the infringing act are mainly in the possession of the infringer, the people's court may order the infringer to provide the account books and materials related to the infringing act; if the infringer fails to provide them or provides false account books and materials, the people's court may determine the amount of damages with reference to the right holder's claims and the evidence provided.

Article 72 Where a patentee or interested party has evidence that another person is implementing or is about to implement an act of patent infringement that hinders the realization of their rights, and if not stopped promptly, it will cause irreparable harm to their legitimate rights and interests, they may, before filing a lawsuit, apply to the people's court in accordance with the law for measures such as property preservation, ordering a specific act to be performed, or prohibiting a specific act.

Article 73 To stop patent infringement, where evidence may be lost or become difficult to obtain later, the patentee or interested party may, before filing a lawsuit, apply to the people's court in accordance with the law for evidence preservation.

Article 74 The statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit for patent infringement is three years, calculated from the date on which the patentee or interested party knows or should have known of the infringing act and the infringer.

Where an invention is used between the publication of the patent application and the grant of the patent right without payment of appropriate royalties, the statute of limitations for the patentee to claim royalties is three years, calculated from the date on which the patentee knows or should have known of the use of the invention by others; however, if the patentee knew or should have known before the date of grant of the patent right, it shall be calculated from the date of grant of the patent right.

Article 75 In any of the following circumstances, it shall not be deemed as patent infringement:

(1) Where a patented product or a product directly obtained by a patented method, after being sold by the patentee or an entity or individual authorized by the patentee, is used, offered for sale, sold, or imported;
(2) Where, before the date of patent application, the same product had already been manufactured, the same method had already been used, or necessary preparations for manufacturing or use had already been made, and manufacturing or use continues only within the original scope;
(3) Where foreign means of transport temporarily passing through the territorial land, waters, or airspace of China use the relevant patent in their devices and equipment for the needs of the means of transport itself, in accordance with agreements between their home country and China, or international treaties to which both are parties, or on the principle of reciprocity;
(4) Where the relevant patent is used solely for scientific research and experimentation;
(5) Where, for the purpose of providing information required for administrative approval, a patented pharmaceutical or patented medical device is manufactured, used, or imported, or where such patented pharmaceutical or patented medical device is specifically manufactured or imported for that purpose.

Article 76 During the process of drug marketing review and approval, if a dispute arises between the drug marketing authorization applicant and the relevant patentee or interested party concerning the patent right related to the drug applied for registration, the relevant parties may file a lawsuit with a people's court, requesting a judgment on whether the technical solution related to the drug applied for registration falls within the scope of protection of another person's drug patent right. The drug regulatory department of the State Council may, within the prescribed period, decide whether to suspend the approval of the relevant drug for marketing based on the effective judgment of the people's court.

The drug marketing authorization applicant and the relevant patentee or interested party may also request an administrative ruling from the patent administrative department of the State Council regarding the patent dispute related to the drug applied for registration.

The drug regulatory department of the State Council, in conjunction with the patent administrative department of the State Council, shall formulate specific measures for resolving patent disputes during the drug marketing approval and drug marketing authorization application stages, which shall be implemented after being reported to the State Council for approval.
Article 77 Where, for the purpose of production and operation, a person uses, offers for sale, or sells a patented infringing product that they did not know was manufactured and sold without the authorization of the patentee, and can prove the legitimate source of the product, they shall not be liable for damages.

Article 78 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 19 of this Law by filing a patent application abroad, thereby divulging state secrets, shall be given administrative sanctions by their unit or the competent authority at a higher level; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 79 The departments responsible for patent administration shall not participate in business activities such as recommending patented products to the public.

If a department responsible for patent administration violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, its superior authority or supervisory authority shall order it to correct the act, eliminate the impact, and confiscate any illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 80 State functionaries engaged in patent administration work and other relevant state functionaries who neglect their duties, abuse their power, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, if constituting a crime, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 81 Anyone applying for a patent or handling other procedures with the patent administrative department of the State Council shall pay fees in accordance with the regulations.

Article 82 This Law shall come into force on April 1, 1985.
