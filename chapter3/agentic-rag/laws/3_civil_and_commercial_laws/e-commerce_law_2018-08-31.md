# E-Commerce Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on August 31, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect the legitimate rights and interests of all parties involved in e-commerce, regulate e-commerce activities, maintain market order, and promote the sustained and healthy development of e-commerce.

Article 2 This Law applies to e-commerce activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "e-commerce" means business activities of selling goods or providing services through information networks such as the Internet.

Where laws or administrative regulations provide for the sale of goods or provision of services, those provisions shall apply. This Law does not apply to financial products and services, nor to the provision of news information, audio and video programs, publications, cultural products, and other content services through information networks.

Article 3 The State encourages the development of new e-commerce business forms, innovation in business models, promotion of e-commerce technology research, development, and application, advancement of the e-commerce credit system, creation of a market environment conducive to innovative e-commerce development, and full utilization of e-commerce's important role in promoting high-quality development, meeting the people's ever-growing needs for a better life, and building an open economy.

Article 4 The State treats online and offline business activities equally and promotes their integrated development. People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall not adopt discriminatory policies or measures, nor abuse administrative power to exclude or restrict market competition.

Article 5 E-commerce operators, in conducting business activities, shall follow the principles of voluntariness, equality, fairness, and good faith, comply with laws and business ethics, participate fairly in market competition, fulfill obligations regarding consumer rights protection, environmental protection, intellectual property protection, network security, and personal information protection, assume responsibility for product and service quality, and accept supervision from the government and the public.

Article 6 The relevant departments of the State Council shall, according to their respective functions and duties, be responsible for promoting the development of e-commerce, supervision, and management. Local people's governments at or above the county level may, based on the actual conditions of their administrative regions, determine the division of departmental responsibilities for e-commerce within their administrative regions.

Article 7 The State establishes a collaborative management system suited to the characteristics of e-commerce, promoting the formation of an e-commerce market governance system involving relevant departments, e-commerce industry organizations, e-commerce operators, consumers, and others.

Article 8 E-commerce industry organizations shall, in accordance with their charters, conduct industry self-discipline, establish and improve industry norms, promote industry credit building, and supervise and guide operators within the industry to participate fairly in market competition.

## Chapter II E-Commerce Operators

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 9 For the purposes of this Law, "e-commerce operators" means natural persons, legal persons, or unincorporated organizations that engage in business activities of selling goods or providing services through information networks such as the Internet, including e-commerce platform operators, platform-based operators, and e-commerce operators that sell goods or provide services through self-built websites or other network services.

For the purposes of this Law, "e-commerce platform operator" means a legal person or unincorporated organization that provides services such as online business premises, transaction matching, and information publishing for two or more parties to a transaction in e-commerce, enabling them to independently conduct transaction activities.

For the purposes of this Law, "platform-based operator" means an e-commerce operator that sells goods or provides services through an e-commerce platform.

Article 10 E-commerce operators shall register as market entities in accordance with the law, except for individuals selling self-produced agricultural and sideline products or household handicraft products, individuals using their own skills to engage in convenience services for the public that do not require a license according to law, and sporadic small-value transactions, as well as those that do not require registration under laws or administrative regulations.

Article 11 E-commerce operators shall fulfill their tax obligations in accordance with the law and enjoy tax benefits according to the law.

E-commerce operators who are not required to register as market entities under the preceding article shall, after the occurrence of their first tax obligation, apply for tax registration in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on tax collection and administration, and truthfully declare and pay taxes.

Article 12 Where e-commerce operators need to obtain relevant administrative licenses to engage in business activities, they shall obtain such licenses in accordance with the law.

Article 13 Goods sold or services provided by e-commerce operators shall meet the requirements for the safety of persons and property and environmental protection. They shall not sell or provide goods or services whose transaction is prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 14 E-commerce operators selling goods or providing services shall, in accordance with the law, issue paper invoices or electronic invoices or other purchase vouchers or service receipts. Electronic invoices have the same legal effect as paper invoices.

Article 15 E-commerce operators shall continuously display, in a prominent position on their homepage, their business license information, administrative license information related to their business operations, information indicating that they fall under the circumstances not requiring market entity registration as specified in Article 10 of this Law, or links to such information.

Where the information specified in the preceding paragraph changes, the e-commerce operator shall update the displayed information in a timely manner.

Article 16 Where an e-commerce operator voluntarily terminates its e-commerce activities, it shall continuously display relevant information in a prominent position on its homepage for thirty days in advance.

Article 17 E-commerce operators shall comprehensively, truthfully, accurately, and promptly disclose information about goods or services, ensuring consumers' right to know and choose. E-commerce operators shall not engage in false or misleading commercial publicity, such as by fabricating transactions or fabricating user reviews, to deceive or mislead consumers.

Article 18 Where an e-commerce operator provides consumers with search results for goods or services based on their interests, consumption habits, or other characteristics, it shall simultaneously offer the consumer options that are not targeted to their personal characteristics, respecting and equally protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers.

Where an e-commerce operator sends advertisements to consumers, it shall comply with the relevant provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 19 Where an e-commerce operator bundles goods or services for sale, it shall draw consumers' attention in a prominent manner and shall not make the bundled goods or services a default consent option.

Article 20 E-commerce operators shall deliver goods or services to consumers in the manner and within the time limit promised or agreed upon with consumers, and shall bear the risks and responsibilities during the transportation of goods, unless the consumer chooses a different courier logistics service provider.

Article 21 Where an e-commerce operator collects a deposit from a consumer as agreed, it shall clearly indicate the method and procedure for refunding the deposit and shall not set unreasonable conditions for the refund. Where a consumer applies for a refund of the deposit and meets the conditions for refund, the e-commerce operator shall refund it promptly.

Article 22 Where an e-commerce operator has a dominant market position due to factors such as its technological advantages, number of users, control over related industries, and the dependence of other operators on it in transactions, it shall not abuse its dominant market position to exclude or restrict competition.

Article 23 E-commerce operators collecting and using users' personal information shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on personal information protection.

Article 24 E-commerce operators shall clearly indicate the methods and procedures for users to inquire about, correct, or delete their information, and for users to cancel their accounts, and shall not set unreasonable conditions for such inquiries, corrections, deletions, or account cancellations.

Upon receiving a user's request to inquire about, correct, or delete information, the e-commerce operator shall promptly provide the inquiry or make the correction or deletion after verifying the user's identity. Where a user cancels their account, the e-commerce operator shall immediately delete the user's information; where retention is required by laws, administrative regulations, or agreement between the parties, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 25 Where relevant competent authorities, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, require an e-commerce operator to provide relevant e-commerce data information, the e-commerce operator shall provide it. The relevant competent authorities shall take necessary measures to protect the security of the data information provided by the e-commerce operator, and shall strictly keep confidential any personal information, privacy, and trade secrets contained therein, and shall not disclose, sell, or illegally provide them to others.

Article 26 E-commerce operators engaged in cross-border e-commerce shall comply with the laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions on import and export supervision and management.

### Section 2 E-Commerce Platform Operators

Article 27 An e-commerce platform operator shall require operators applying to enter the platform to sell goods or provide services to submit true information such as their identity, address, contact information, and administrative licenses, verify and register such information, establish registration files, and periodically verify and update them.

Where an e-commerce platform operator provides services to non-business users entering the platform to sell goods or provide services, it shall comply with the relevant provisions of this Section.

Article 28 An e-commerce platform operator shall, in accordance with regulations, submit the identity information of platform-based operators to the market supervision and management department, remind operators who have not registered as market entities to register in accordance with the law, and cooperate with the market supervision and management department to facilitate registration for operators required to register as market entities, taking into account the characteristics of e-commerce.

An e-commerce platform operator shall, in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on tax collection and administration, submit the identity information of platform-based operators and information related to tax payment to the tax authorities, and shall remind e-commerce operators who are not required to register as market entities under Article 10 of this Law to apply for tax registration in accordance with Article 11, paragraph 2 of this Law.

Article 29 Where an e-commerce platform operator discovers that information on goods or services on the platform falls under the circumstances specified in Articles 12 and 13 of this Law, it shall take necessary measures in accordance with the law and report to the relevant competent authorities.

Article 30 An e-commerce platform operator shall adopt technical measures and other necessary measures to ensure the security and stable operation of its network, prevent online criminal activities, effectively respond to network security incidents, and ensure the security of e-commerce transactions.

An e-commerce platform operator shall formulate an emergency response plan for network security incidents. When a network security incident occurs, it shall immediately activate the emergency plan, take corresponding remedial measures, and report to the relevant competent authorities.

Article 31 An e-commerce platform operator shall record and preserve information on goods and services published on the platform and transaction information, and ensure the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of such information. The retention period for goods and services information and transaction information shall be no less than three years from the date of completion of the transaction; where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 32 An e-commerce platform operator shall formulate platform service agreements and transaction rules in accordance with the principles of openness, fairness, and justice, clearly defining the rights and obligations regarding entry to and exit from the platform, quality assurance of goods and services, consumer rights protection, and personal information protection.

Article 33 An e-commerce platform operator shall continuously display, in a prominent position on its homepage, the platform service agreement and transaction rules, or links to such information, and ensure that operators and consumers can conveniently and completely view and download them.

Article 34 Where an e-commerce platform operator modifies the platform service agreement and transaction rules, it shall publicly solicit opinions in a prominent position on its homepage and take reasonable measures to ensure that relevant parties can express their opinions fully and in a timely manner. The modified content shall be publicly displayed at least seven days before implementation.

Where a platform-based operator does not accept the modified content and requests to exit the platform, the e-commerce platform operator shall not prevent it and shall bear relevant responsibilities in accordance with the service agreement and transaction rules before the modification.

Article 35 An e-commerce platform operator shall not, by means of service agreements, transaction rules, technology, or otherwise, impose unreasonable restrictions or additional conditions on platform-based operators' transactions, transaction prices, or transactions with other operators on the platform, nor charge unreasonable fees to platform-based operators.

Article 36 Where an e-commerce platform operator takes measures such as warnings, suspension, or termination of services against a platform-based operator for violating laws or regulations in accordance with the platform service agreement and transaction rules, it shall promptly make a public announcement.

Article 37 Where an e-commerce platform operator conducts its own business on its platform, it shall clearly distinguish and mark its own business from the business conducted by platform-based operators in a prominent manner, and shall not mislead consumers.

An e-commerce platform operator shall bear civil liability as a seller of goods or a service provider for its business marked as its own in accordance with the law.

Article 38 Where an e-commerce platform operator knows or should know that goods sold or services provided by a platform-based operator do not meet the requirements for the safety of persons and property, or that there are other acts infringing upon consumers' legitimate rights and interests, and fails to take necessary measures, it shall bear joint and several liability with the platform-based operator in accordance with the law.

For goods or services related to the life and health of consumers, where an e-commerce platform operator fails to fulfill its obligation to review the qualifications of a platform-based operator, or fails to fulfill its obligation to ensure consumer safety, resulting in damage to consumers, it shall bear corresponding liability in accordance with the law.

Article 39 An e-commerce platform operator shall establish and improve a credit evaluation system, publicly disclose credit evaluation rules, and provide consumers with a means to evaluate goods sold or services provided on the platform.

An e-commerce platform operator shall not delete consumers' evaluations of goods sold or services provided on its platform.

Article 40 An e-commerce platform operator shall display search results for goods or services to consumers in multiple ways based on factors such as price, sales volume, and credit rating; for goods or services ranked by bidding, it shall prominently mark them as "advertisement."

Article 41 An e-commerce platform operator shall establish rules for intellectual property protection, strengthen cooperation with intellectual property rights holders, and protect intellectual property rights in accordance with the law.

Article 42 Where an intellectual property rights holder believes that its intellectual property rights have been infringed, it has the right to notify the e-commerce platform operator to take necessary measures such as deletion, blocking, disconnection of links, and termination of transactions and services. The notice shall include preliminary evidence of the infringement.

Upon receiving the notice, the e-commerce platform operator shall promptly take necessary measures and forward the notice to the platform-based operator; if it fails to take necessary measures promptly, it shall bear joint and several liability with the platform-based operator for the expanded part of the damage.

Where damage is caused to the platform-based operator due to an erroneous notice, the notifier shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law. Where an erroneous notice is made maliciously, causing losses to the platform-based operator, the notifier shall bear double the compensation liability.

Article 43 Upon receiving the forwarded notice, the platform-based operator may submit a declaration of non-infringement to the e-commerce platform operator. The declaration shall include preliminary evidence of non-infringement.

Upon receiving the declaration, the e-commerce platform operator shall forward the declaration to the intellectual property rights holder who issued the notice and inform them that they may file a complaint with the relevant competent authority or file a lawsuit with the people's court. If, within fifteen days after the declaration is forwarded to the intellectual property rights holder, the e-commerce platform operator does not receive notice that the rights holder has filed a complaint or lawsuit, it shall promptly terminate the measures taken.

Article 44 An e-commerce platform operator shall promptly publicly disclose the notices, declarations, and processing results received under Articles 42 and 43 of this Law.

Article 45 Where an e-commerce platform operator knows or should know that a platform-based operator is infringing intellectual property rights, it shall take necessary measures such as deletion, blocking, disconnection of links, and termination of transactions and services; if it fails to take necessary measures, it shall bear joint and several liability with the infringer.

Article 46 In addition to the services specified in Article 9, paragraph 2 of this Law, an e-commerce platform operator may, in accordance with the platform service agreement and transaction rules, provide services such as warehousing, logistics, payment settlement, and delivery for e-commerce transactions between operators. Where an e-commerce platform operator provides services for e-commerce transactions between operators, it shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions, and shall not conduct transactions using centralized trading methods such as centralized bidding or market making, nor engage in standardized contract trading.

## Chapter III Conclusion and Performance of E-Commerce Contracts

Article 47 The conclusion and performance of contracts by e-commerce parties shall be governed by this Chapter and the provisions of laws such as the General Principles of the Civil Law of the People's Republic of China, the Contract Law of the People's Republic of China, and the Electronic Signature Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 48 The conduct of an e-commerce party in concluding or performing a contract using an automated information system shall have legal effect on the party using that system.

In e-commerce, the parties are presumed to have the corresponding capacity for civil conduct, unless there is sufficient evidence to the contrary.

Article 49 Where information on goods or services published by an e-commerce operator meets the conditions for an offer, and the user selects the goods or services and successfully submits the order, the contract is concluded, unless the parties agree otherwise.

An e-commerce operator shall not stipulate in standard terms or otherwise that the contract is not concluded after the consumer pays the price; if standard terms or other provisions contain such content, that content shall be void.

Article 50 An e-commerce operator shall clearly, comprehensively, and explicitly inform users of the steps for concluding a contract, precautions, download methods, and other matters, and ensure that users can conveniently and completely view and download them.

An e-commerce operator shall ensure that users can correct input errors before submitting an order.

Article 51 Where the subject matter of the contract is the delivery of goods and delivery is by courier logistics, the time of delivery is the time when the consignee signs for the goods. Where the subject matter of the contract is the provision of services, the time of delivery is the time specified in the generated electronic or physical voucher; if the voucher does not specify a time or the specified time is inconsistent with the actual time of service provision, the time of delivery is the actual time of service provision.

Where the subject matter of the contract is delivered by online transmission, the time of delivery is the time when the subject matter enters the specific system designated by the other party and can be retrieved and identified.

Where the parties to the contract agree otherwise on the method or time of delivery, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 52 E-commerce parties may agree to deliver goods by courier logistics.

Courier logistics service providers providing courier logistics services for e-commerce shall comply with laws and administrative regulations and shall meet the promised service standards and time limits. When delivering goods, the courier logistics service provider shall remind the consignee to inspect the goods in person; if the goods are to be received by another person on behalf of the consignee, the consignee's consent shall be obtained.

Courier logistics service providers shall use environmentally friendly packaging materials in accordance with regulations to achieve reduction and reuse of packaging materials.

While providing courier logistics services, courier logistics service providers may accept entrustment from e-commerce operators to provide collection-on-delivery services.

Article 53 E-commerce parties may agree to pay the price by electronic payment.Electronic payment service providers offering electronic payment services for e-commerce shall comply with state regulations, inform users of the functions, usage methods, precautions, related risks, and fee standards of the electronic payment service, and shall not impose unreasonable transaction conditions. Electronic payment service providers shall ensure the integrity, consistency, traceability, auditability, and non-tamperability of electronic payment instructions.

Electronic payment service providers shall provide users with free reconciliation services and transaction records for the last three years.

Article 54: If an electronic payment service provider fails to meet national payment security management requirements when providing electronic payment services, causing user losses, it shall bear compensation liability.

Article 55: Before issuing a payment instruction, the user shall verify the complete information contained in the payment instruction, including the amount and payee.

If a payment instruction is erroneous, the electronic payment service provider shall promptly investigate the cause and take relevant measures to correct it. If user losses are caused, the electronic payment service provider shall bear compensation liability, unless it can prove that the payment error was not due to its own fault.

Article 56: After completing an electronic payment, the electronic payment service provider shall promptly and accurately provide the user with confirmation of payment information in the agreed manner.

Article 57: Users shall properly safeguard security tools such as transaction passwords and electronic signature data. If a user discovers that a security tool is lost, stolen, or used for unauthorized payments, they shall promptly notify the electronic payment service provider.

Losses caused by unauthorized payments shall be borne by the electronic payment service provider; if the electronic payment service provider can prove that the unauthorized payment was due to the user's fault, it shall not be liable.

When an electronic payment service provider discovers an unauthorized payment instruction or receives a user's notification of an unauthorized payment instruction, it shall immediately take measures to prevent the loss from expanding. If the electronic payment service provider fails to take timely measures, resulting in expanded losses, it shall be liable for the expanded portion.

## Chapter 4: E-commerce Dispute Resolution

Article 58: The State encourages e-commerce platform operators to establish product and service quality guarantee mechanisms that facilitate e-commerce development and consumer rights protection.

If an e-commerce platform operator and an in-platform operator agree to establish a consumer rights deposit, both parties shall clearly agree on the amount, management, use, and refund methods of the deposit.

When a consumer requests the e-commerce platform operator to bear advance compensation liability, and the platform operator seeks recourse from the in-platform operator after compensation, the relevant provisions of the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Consumer Rights and Interests" shall apply.

Article 59: E-commerce operators shall establish convenient and effective complaint and reporting mechanisms, publicly disclose complaint and reporting methods and other information, and promptly accept and handle complaints and reports.

Article 60: E-commerce disputes may be resolved through negotiation and settlement, requesting mediation by consumer organizations, industry associations, or other legally established mediation organizations, filing complaints with relevant authorities, submitting to arbitration, or initiating litigation.

Article 61: When a consumer purchases goods or receives services on an e-commerce platform and a dispute arises with an in-platform operator, the e-commerce platform operator shall actively assist the consumer in safeguarding their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 62: In the handling of e-commerce disputes, the e-commerce operator shall provide the original contract and transaction records. If the e-commerce operator loses, forges, alters, destroys, conceals, or refuses to provide the aforementioned materials, causing the people's court, arbitration institution, or relevant authority to be unable to ascertain the facts, the e-commerce operator shall bear corresponding legal liability.

Article 63: E-commerce platform operators may establish online dispute resolution mechanisms, formulate and publicly disclose dispute resolution rules, and resolve disputes between parties fairly and impartially on a voluntary basis.

## Chapter 5: E-commerce Promotion

Article 64: The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall incorporate e-commerce development into national economic and social development plans, formulate scientific and reasonable industrial policies, and promote innovative e-commerce development.

Article 65: The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall take measures to support and promote green packaging, warehousing, and transportation, and facilitate the green development of e-commerce.

Article 66: The State promotes the construction of e-commerce infrastructure and logistics networks, improves the e-commerce statistical system, and strengthens the development of the e-commerce standards system.

Article 67: The State promotes the application of e-commerce in various fields of the national economy and supports the integrated development of e-commerce with various industries.

Article 68: The State promotes the application of internet technology in agricultural production, processing, and circulation, encourages cooperation among various social resources, promotes the development of rural e-commerce, and leverages the role of e-commerce in targeted poverty alleviation.

Article 69: The State maintains the security of e-commerce transactions, protects the information of e-commerce users, encourages the development and application of e-commerce data, and ensures the orderly and free flow of e-commerce data in accordance with the law.

The State takes measures to promote the establishment of a public data sharing mechanism and facilitates e-commerce operators' lawful use of public data.

Article 70: The State supports legally established credit evaluation agencies in conducting e-commerce credit evaluations and providing e-commerce credit evaluation services to the public.

Article 71: The State promotes the development of cross-border e-commerce, establishes and improves customs, taxation, import and export inspection and quarantine, payment and settlement, and other management systems adapted to the characteristics of cross-border e-commerce, enhances the facilitation of all aspects of cross-border e-commerce, and supports cross-border e-commerce platform operators and others in providing warehousing, logistics, customs declaration, and inspection services for cross-border e-commerce.

The State supports small and micro enterprises in engaging in cross-border e-commerce.

Article 72: The State's import and export management departments shall promote the construction of comprehensive service and supervision systems for customs declaration, tax payment, inspection and quarantine, and other aspects of cross-border e-commerce, optimize supervision processes, promote information sharing, mutual recognition of supervision, and mutual assistance in law enforcement, and improve the efficiency of cross-border e-commerce services and supervision. Cross-border e-commerce operators may use electronic documents to handle relevant procedures with the State's import and export management departments.

Article 73: The State promotes the establishment of exchange and cooperation in cross-border e-commerce with different countries and regions, participates in the formulation of international e-commerce rules, and promotes the international mutual recognition of electronic signatures and electronic identities.

The State promotes the establishment of cross-border e-commerce dispute resolution mechanisms with different countries and regions.

## Chapter 6: Legal Liability

Article 74: If an e-commerce operator sells goods or provides services, fails to perform contractual obligations or performs them inconsistently with the agreement, or causes harm to others, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 75: If an e-commerce operator violates Articles 12 and 13 of this Law, engages in business activities without obtaining the relevant administrative license, sells or provides goods or services prohibited by laws or administrative regulations, or fails to fulfill the information provision obligations under Article 25 of this Law, or if an e-commerce platform operator violates Article 46 of this Law by conducting transactions through centralized trading methods or standardized contract trading, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 76: If an e-commerce operator violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan; for an e-commerce platform operator, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of Article 81, Paragraph 1 of this Law:

(1) Failing to publicly display business license information, administrative license information, information indicating that it is not required to undergo market entity registration, or links to such information, in a prominent position on the homepage;

(2) Failing to continuously display information on the termination of e-commerce activities in a prominent position on the homepage;

(3) Failing to clearly indicate the methods and procedures for user information inquiry, correction, deletion, and user cancellation, or setting unreasonable conditions for user information inquiry, correction, deletion, and user cancellation.

If an e-commerce platform operator fails to take necessary measures against an in-platform operator that violates the preceding paragraph, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 77: If an e-commerce operator violates Article 18, Paragraph 1 of this Law by providing search results, or violates Article 19 of this Law by tying in the sale of goods or services, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period, confiscate illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a concurrent fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 78: If an e-commerce operator violates Article 21 of this Law by failing to clearly inform consumers of the methods and procedures for deposit refunds, setting unreasonable conditions for deposit refunds, or failing to refund deposits in a timely manner, the relevant competent authority shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 79: If an e-commerce operator violates the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on the protection of personal information, or fails to fulfill the network security protection obligations under Article 30 of this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China" and other laws and administrative regulations.

Article 80: If an e-commerce platform operator commits any of the following acts, the relevant competent authority shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, and a concurrent fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to fulfill the verification and registration obligations under Article 27 of this Law;

(2) Failing to report relevant information to the market supervision and management department and the tax department in accordance with Article 28 of this Law;

(3) Failing to take necessary measures against illegal situations in accordance with Article 29 of this Law, or failing to report to the relevant competent authority;

(4) Failing to fulfill the obligation to preserve product and service information and transaction information under Article 31 of this Law.

If other laws and administrative regulations provide for penalties for the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph, those provisions shall apply.

Article 81: If an e-commerce platform operator violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to continuously display platform service agreements, transaction rules, or links to such information in a prominent position on the homepage;

(2) Modifying transaction rules without publicly soliciting opinions in a prominent position on the homepage, failing to publicly announce the modification content in advance within the prescribed time, or preventing in-platform operators from withdrawing;

(3) Failing to clearly distinguish and mark self-operated business and business conducted by in-platform operators in a prominent manner;

(4) Failing to provide consumers with a means to evaluate goods sold or services provided on the platform, or arbitrarily deleting consumer evaluations.

If an e-commerce platform operator violates Article 40 of this Law by failing to prominently mark "advertisement" for goods or services subject to bidding ranking, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China".

Article 82: If an e-commerce platform operator violates Article 35 of this Law by imposing unreasonable restrictions or additional conditions on in-platform operators' transactions, transaction prices, or transactions with other operators, or by charging unreasonable fees to in-platform operators, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 83: If an e-commerce platform operator violates Article 38 of this Law by failing to take necessary measures against in-platform operators' infringement of consumer legitimate rights and interests, failing to fulfill the obligation to review the qualifications of in-platform operators, or failing to fulfill the obligation to ensure consumer safety, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period and may impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, and a concurrent fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 84: If an e-commerce platform operator violates Articles 42 and 45 of this Law by failing to take necessary measures in accordance with the law against in-platform operators' infringement of intellectual property rights, the relevant intellectual property administrative department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 85: If an e-commerce operator violates the provisions of this Law by selling goods or providing services that do not meet the requirements for the protection of personal and property safety, engaging in unfair competition practices such as false or misleading commercial publicity, abusing a dominant market position, or committing acts such as infringing intellectual property rights or consumer rights, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws.

Article 86: If an e-commerce operator commits illegal acts as specified in this Law, it shall be recorded in the credit file in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations and publicly disclosed.

Article 87: If staff members of departments responsible for e-commerce supervision and management neglect their duties, abuse their power, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or disclose, sell, or illegally provide to others personal information, privacy, or trade secrets learned in the course of performing their duties, they shall be held legally accountable in accordance with the law.

Article 88: If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7: Supplementary Provisions

Article 89: This Law shall take effect on January 1, 2019.
