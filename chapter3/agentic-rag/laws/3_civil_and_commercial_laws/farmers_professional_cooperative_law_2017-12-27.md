# Law of the People's Republic of China on Specialized Farmers Cooperatives

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 31, 2006

Revised at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 27, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating the organization and conduct of specialized farmers cooperatives, encouraging, supporting and guiding the development of specialized farmers cooperatives, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of specialized farmers cooperatives and their members, and promoting the modernization of agriculture and rural areas.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "specialized farmers cooperative" refers to a mutual-aid economic organization voluntarily formed and democratically managed by producers and operators of agricultural products, or providers and users of agricultural production and operation services, on the basis of the rural household contract management system.

Article 3 A specialized farmers cooperative, with its members as its main service targets, may engage in one or more of the following businesses:

(1) Purchase and use of agricultural means of production;

(2) Production, sale, processing, transportation, storage and other related services of agricultural products;

(3) Development and operation of rural folk crafts and products, leisure agriculture, and rural tourism resources;

(4) Services related to agricultural production and operation, such as technology, information, facility construction and operation.

Article 4 A specialized farmers cooperative shall adhere to the following principles:

(1) Members are predominantly farmers;

(2) Serving members is the purpose, seeking the common interests of all members;

(3) Voluntary membership and freedom to withdraw;

(4) Equal status of members and democratic management;

(5) Surplus is mainly returned in proportion to the volume (value) of transactions between members and the cooperative.

Article 5 A specialized farmers cooperative shall obtain the status of a legal person upon registration in accordance with this Law.

A specialized farmers cooperative has the right to possess, use and dispose of property formed by members' capital contributions, accumulation funds, direct state financial subsidies, donations from others, and other lawfully obtained assets, and shall be liable for its debts with the aforesaid property.

Article 6 A member of a specialized farmers cooperative shall be liable for the cooperative's debts to the extent of the capital contribution and accumulation fund shares recorded in the member's account.

Article 7 The State guarantees that specialized farmers cooperatives enjoy equal legal status with other market entities.

The State protects the legitimate rights and interests of specialized farmers cooperatives and their members, and no unit or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 8 When engaging in production and business activities, a specialized farmers cooperative shall abide by the law, observe social morality and business ethics, act in good faith, and shall not engage in activities unrelated to those stipulated in its articles of association.

Article 9 To expand the scale of production and operation and services, develop industrialized operations, and enhance market competitiveness, a specialized farmers cooperative may, in accordance with the law and voluntarily, establish or join a federation of specialized farmers cooperatives.

Article 10 The State promotes the development of specialized farmers cooperatives through financial support, tax preferences, and support in finance, science and technology, and talent, as well as guidance through industrial policies.

The State encourages and supports citizens, legal persons and other organizations to provide assistance and services to specialized farmers cooperatives.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 11 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish a comprehensive coordination mechanism for the work of specialized farmers cooperatives, to provide overall guidance, coordination, and promotion of the construction and development of specialized farmers cooperatives.

The competent agricultural departments and other relevant departments and organizations of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, provide guidance, support and services for the construction and development of specialized farmers cooperatives.

## Chapter II Establishment and Registration

Article 12 The establishment of a specialized farmers cooperative shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having at least five members who meet the requirements of Articles 19 and 20 of this Law;

(2) Having articles of association that comply with the provisions of this Law;

(3) Having an organizational structure that complies with the provisions of this Law;

(4) Having a name that complies with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations and a domicile determined by the articles of association;

(5) Having capital contributions from members that comply with the provisions of the articles of association.

Article 13 Members of a specialized farmers cooperative may make capital contributions in currency, or in kind, intellectual property rights, land management rights, forest rights, and other non-monetary property that can be valued in currency and legally transferred, as well as in other ways stipulated in the articles of association; however, property that laws and administrative regulations stipulate cannot be used as capital contributions is excluded.

A member of a specialized farmers cooperative shall not offset capital contributions with claims against the cooperative or other members, nor offset debts owed to the cooperative or other members with the capital contributions paid.

Article 14 To establish a specialized farmers cooperative, an inaugural meeting attended by all founders shall be held. Those who voluntarily become members of the cooperative at the time of its establishment are the founders.

The inaugural meeting shall exercise the following powers:

(1) Adopting the articles of association of the cooperative, which shall be unanimously adopted by all founders;

(2) Electing the chairperson, directors, executive supervisor or members of the supervisory board;

(3) Deliberating on other major matters.

Article 15 The articles of association of a specialized farmers cooperative shall specify the following:

(1) Name and domicile;

(2) Scope of business;

(3) Membership qualifications, admission, withdrawal, and expulsion;

(4) Rights and obligations of members;

(5) Organizational structure, its formation method, powers, term of office, and rules of procedure;

(6) Methods and amounts of members' capital contributions, and the transfer, inheritance, and guarantee of members' capital contributions;

(7) Financial management, surplus distribution, and loss treatment;

(8) Procedures for amending the articles of association;

(9) Grounds for dissolution and liquidation methods;

(10) Matters to be announced and the method of announcement;

(11) Establishment, exercise method, and scope of additional voting rights;

(12) Other matters that need to be specified.

Article 16 To establish a specialized farmers cooperative, the following documents shall be submitted to the administrative department for industry and commerce for registration:

(1) A registration application;

(2) The minutes of the inaugural meeting signed and sealed by all founders;

(3) The articles of association signed and sealed by all founders;

(4) The appointment documents and identity certificates of the legal representative and directors;

(5) A list of capital contributions signed and sealed by the contributing members;

(6) Proof of use of the domicile;

(7) Other documents required by laws and administrative regulations.

The registration authority shall complete the processing within twenty days from the date of accepting the registration application, and issue a business license to applicants who meet the registration conditions, with the registration type being "specialized farmers cooperative."

If the statutory registration items of a specialized farmers cooperative change, an application for change of registration shall be filed.

The registration authority shall notify the relevant agricultural department and other departments at the same level of the registration information of the specialized farmers cooperative.

The measures for the registration of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be formulated by the State Council. No fees shall be charged for registration.

Article 17 A specialized farmers cooperative shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, submit an annual report to the registration authority and make it public to the society.

Article 18 A specialized farmers cooperative may, in accordance with the law, invest in companies and other enterprises, and shall be liable for the invested enterprise to the extent of its capital contribution.

## Chapter III Members

Article 19 Citizens with capacity for civil conduct, as well as enterprises, public institutions, or social organizations engaged in production and operation activities directly related to the business of the specialized farmers cooperative, who can utilize the services provided by the cooperative, recognize and abide by the cooperative's articles of association, and fulfill the admission procedures stipulated in the articles of association, may become members of the specialized farmers cooperative. However, units with the function of managing public affairs shall not join a specialized farmers cooperative.

A specialized farmers cooperative shall maintain a register of members and report it to the registration authority.

Article 20 Among the members of a specialized farmers cooperative, farmers shall account for at least eighty percent of the total number of members.

If the total number of members is twenty or fewer, there may be one member that is an enterprise, public institution, or social organization; if the total number of members exceeds twenty, the number of members that are enterprises, public institutions, or social organizations shall not exceed five percent of the total number of members.

Article 21 Members of a specialized farmers cooperative shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) To attend the general meeting of members, and to enjoy the rights to vote, to stand for election, and to be elected, and to exercise democratic management over the cooperative in accordance with the articles of association;

(2) To utilize the services and production and operation facilities provided by the cooperative;

(3) To share in the surplus in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association or resolutions of the general meeting of members;

(4) To inspect the cooperative's articles of association, register of members, records of general meetings of members or meetings of representatives of members, resolutions of the board of directors, resolutions of the supervisory board, financial accounting reports, account books, and financial audit reports;

(5) Other rights stipulated in the articles of association.

Article 22 Elections and voting at the general meeting of members of a specialized farmers cooperative shall be conducted on the principle of one member, one vote, with each member having one basic vote.

Members with larger capital contributions or larger transaction volumes (values) with the cooperative may, in accordance with the articles of association, enjoy additional voting rights. The total number of additional voting rights of the cooperative shall not exceed twenty percent of the total number of basic voting rights of the cooperative's members. Members entitled to additional voting rights and the number of additional voting rights they hold shall be notified to all members attending the meeting at each general meeting of members.

Article 23 Members of a specialized farmers cooperative shall assume the following obligations:

(1) To implement the resolutions of the general meeting of members, the meeting of representatives of members, and the board of directors;

(2) To make capital contributions to the cooperative in accordance with the articles of association;

(3) To conduct transactions with the cooperative in accordance with the articles of association;

(4) To bear losses in accordance with the articles of association;

(5) Other obligations stipulated in the articles of association.

Article 24 Citizens, enterprises, public institutions, or social organizations that meet the requirements of Articles 19 and 20 of this Law and wish to join an established specialized farmers cooperative shall submit a written application to the chairperson or the board of directors. Upon approval by a vote of the general meeting of members or the meeting of representatives of members, they become members of the cooperative.

Article 25 A member of a specialized farmers cooperative who wishes to withdraw shall submit a written application to the chairperson or the board of directors at least three months before the end of the fiscal year; if the withdrawing member is an enterprise, public institution, or social organization, the application shall be submitted at least six months before the end of the fiscal year; unless otherwise stipulated in the articles of association. The membership of the withdrawing member terminates at the end of the fiscal year.

Article 26 A member of a specialized farmers cooperative who fails to comply with the cooperative's articles of association or the resolutions of the general meeting of members or the meeting of representatives of members, or who seriously harms the interests of other members or the cooperative, may be expelled.

The expulsion of a member shall be approved by a vote of the general meeting of members or the meeting of representatives of members.

When implementing the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the member concerned shall be given an opportunity to state their views.

The membership of the expelled member terminates at the end of the fiscal year.

Article 27 Contracts concluded between a member and the specialized farmers cooperative before the termination of the member's membership shall continue to be performed, unless otherwise stipulated in the articles of association or otherwise agreed upon with the cooperative.

Article 28 Upon termination of membership, the specialized farmers cooperative shall, in the manner and within the time limit stipulated in the articles of association, refund the capital contribution and accumulation fund shares recorded in the member's account; the distributable surplus accrued before the termination of membership shall be returned to the member in accordance with the provisions of Article 44 of this Law.

The member whose membership is terminated shall, in accordance with the articles of association, share in the losses and debts of the cooperative incurred before the termination of membership.

## Chapter IV Organizational Structure

Article 29 The general meeting of members of a specialized farmers cooperative, composed of all members, is the supreme authority of the cooperative and shall exercise the following powers:

(1) Amending the articles of association;

(2) Electing and removing the chairperson, directors, executive supervisor, or members of the supervisory board;

(3) Deciding on major property disposals, external investments, external guarantees, and other major matters in production and operation activities;

(4) Approving the annual business report, surplus distribution plan, and loss treatment plan;

(5) Making resolutions on merger, division, dissolution, liquidation, and the establishment of or joining a federation;

(6) Deciding on the number, qualifications, and term of office of hired management and professional technical personnel;

(7) Hearing reports from the chairperson or board of directors on changes in membership, and making resolutions on the admission and expulsion of members;

(8) Withdrawal and use of accumulation funds;

(9) Other powers stipulated in the articles of association.

Article 30 When convening a general meeting of members of a specialized farmers cooperative, the number of attendees shall reach at least two-thirds of the total number of members.

Elections or resolutions of the general meeting of members shall be passed by more than half of the total voting rights of the cooperative's members; resolutions on amending the articles of association, merger, division, dissolution, or the establishment of or joining a federation shall be passed by at least two-thirds of the total voting rights of the cooperative's members. If the articles of association stipulate a higher number of voting rights, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 31 The general meeting of members of a specialized farmers cooperative shall be convened at least once a year, and the convening of meetings shall be stipulated in the articles of association. An interim general meeting of members shall be convened within twenty days under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Proposed by more than thirty percent of the members;

(2) Proposed by the executive supervisor or the supervisory board;

(3) Other circumstances stipulated in the articles of association.

Article 32 If a specialized farmers cooperative has more than one hundred and fifty members, a meeting of representatives of members may be established in accordance with the articles of association. The meeting of representatives of members may exercise part or all of the powers of the general meeting of members in accordance with the articles of association.

If a meeting of representatives of members is established in accordance with the law, the number of member representatives shall generally be ten percent of the total number of members, with a minimum of fifty-one representatives.

Article 33 A specialized farmers cooperative shall have one chairperson and may have a board of directors. The chairperson is the legal representative of the cooperative.

A specialized farmers cooperative may have an executive supervisor or a supervisory board. The chairperson, directors, manager, and accounting personnel shall not concurrently serve as supervisors.

The chairperson, directors, executive supervisor, or members of the supervisory board shall be elected by the general meeting of members from among the cooperative's members, exercise their powers in accordance with this Law and the articles of association, and be responsible to the general meeting of members.

Voting at meetings of the board of directors and the supervisory board shall be conducted on the principle of one person, one vote.

Article 34 The general meeting of members, meeting of representatives of members, board of directors, and supervisory board of a specialized farmers cooperative shall make records of the decisions on matters discussed, and the members, representatives, directors, and supervisors present at the meeting shall sign the records.

Article 35 The chairperson or board of directors of a specialized farmers cooperative may, in accordance with the decisions of the general meeting of members, appoint a manager and accounting personnel. The chairperson or a director may concurrently serve as the manager. The manager may appoint other personnel in accordance with the articles of association or the decisions of the board of directors.

The manager shall be responsible for specific production and operation activities in accordance with the articles of association and the authorization of the chairperson or the board of directors.

Article 36 The chairperson, directors, and management personnel of a specialized farmers cooperative shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Embezzling, misappropriating, or privately distributing the cooperative's assets;

(2) Lending the cooperative's funds to others or providing guarantees with the cooperative's assets for others, in violation of the articles of association or without the consent of the general meeting of members;

(3) Accepting commissions from others for transactions with the cooperative for personal gain;

(4) Engaging in other activities that harm the economic interests of the cooperative.

Any income obtained by the chairperson, directors, or management personnel in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall belong to the cooperative; if losses are caused to the cooperative, they shall be liable for compensation.

Article 37 The chairperson, directors, or manager of a specialized farmers cooperative shall not concurrently serve as the chairperson, director, supervisor, or manager of another specialized farmers cooperative engaged in the same type of business.

Article 38 Personnel performing official duties related to the business of a specialized farmers cooperative shall not serve as the chairperson, director, supervisor, manager, or accounting personnel of the cooperative.

## Chapter V Financial Management

Article 39 A specialized farmers cooperative shall conduct financial management and accounting in accordance with the financial accounting system formulated by the financial department of the State Council.

Article 40 The chairperson or board of directors of a specialized farmers cooperative shall, in accordance with the articles of association, organize the preparation of the annual business report, surplus distribution plan, loss treatment plan, and financial accounting report, and make them available at the office premises for members' inspection at least fifteen days before the convening of the general meeting of members.

Article 41 Transactions between a specialized farmers cooperative and its members, and transactions between the cooperative and non-members who utilize its services, shall be accounted for separately.

Article 42 A specialized farmers cooperative may, in accordance with the articles of association or resolutions of the general meeting of members, withdraw accumulation funds from the current year's surplus. Accumulation funds shall be used to make up losses, expand production and operation, or be converted into members' capital contributions.

The accumulation funds withdrawn each year shall be quantified into shares for each member in accordance with the articles of association.

Article 43 A specialized farmers cooperative shall establish a member account for each member, which shall mainly record the following:

(1) The member's capital contribution;

(2) The accumulation fund shares quantified to the member;

(3) The volume (value) of transactions between the member and the cooperative.

Article 44 The surplus of the current year after making up losses and withdrawing accumulation funds is the distributable surplus of the specialized farmers cooperative. The distributable surplus shall mainly be returned in proportion to the volume (value) of transactions between members and the cooperative.

The total amount returned in proportion to the volume (value) of transactions between members and the cooperative shall not be less than sixty percent of the distributable surplus; the remaining portion after the return shall be distributed to the members in proportion to the capital contributions and accumulation fund shares recorded in their member accounts, as well as the shares formed by the cooperative's acceptance of direct state financial subsidies and donations from others, which are quantified equally to the members.

With the consent of a vote of the general meeting of members or the meeting of representatives of members, all or part of the distributable surplus may be converted into capital contributions to the specialized farmers cooperative and recorded in the member accounts.

The specific distribution method shall be determined in accordance with the articles of association or by a resolution of the general meeting of members.

Article 45 For a specialized farmers cooperative that has established an executive supervisor or a supervisory board, the executive supervisor or the supervisory board shall be responsible for conducting internal audits of the cooperative's finances, and the audit results shall be reported to the general meeting of members.

The general meeting of members may also entrust a social intermediary institution to conduct an audit of the cooperative's finances.

## Chapter VI Merger, Division, Dissolution, and Liquidation

Article 46 When a specialized farmers cooperative merges, it shall notify its creditors within ten days from the date the merger resolution is made. The debts and claims of all parties to the merger shall be assumed by the organization that survives or is newly established after the merger.

Article 47 When a specialized farmers cooperative divides, its property shall be divided accordingly, and it shall notify its creditors within ten days from the date the division resolution is made. The organizations resulting from the division shall be jointly and severally liable for the debts incurred before the division, unless otherwise agreed in a written agreement reached with the creditors before the division regarding the settlement of debts.

Article 48 A specialized farmers cooperative shall be dissolved for any of the following reasons:

(1) The occurrence of the grounds for dissolution stipulated in the articles of association;

(2) A resolution of the general meeting of members to dissolve;

(3) The need for dissolution due to merger or division;

(4) The business license being revoked or the cooperative being dissolved in accordance with the law.If the dissolution is due to the reasons specified in items (1), (2), or (4) of the preceding paragraph, a liquidation group shall be formed by members elected at a general meeting within fifteen days from the date the dissolution cause arises to commence dissolution and liquidation. If a liquidation group cannot be formed within the prescribed period, members or creditors may apply to the People's Court to designate members to form a liquidation group for liquidation. The People's Court shall accept such application and promptly designate members to form a liquidation group for liquidation.

Article 49 The liquidation group shall take over the specialized farmers cooperative from the date of its establishment, be responsible for handling unfinished business related to liquidation, liquidating property, claims, and debts, distributing remaining property after debt repayment, representing the specialized farmers cooperative in litigation, arbitration, or other legal proceedings, and handling cancellation of registration upon completion of liquidation.

Article 50 The liquidation group shall notify members and creditors of the specialized farmers cooperative within ten days from the date of its establishment and make a public announcement in a newspaper within sixty days. Creditors shall, within thirty days from receipt of the notice, or within forty-five days from the date of the announcement if no notice is received, declare their claims to the liquidation group. If all members and creditors have received notice within the prescribed period, the liquidation group is exempted from the obligation to make a public announcement.

When declaring claims, creditors shall specify relevant matters concerning the claims and provide supporting materials. The liquidation group shall review and register the claims.

During the period for declaring claims, the liquidation group shall not satisfy any creditor's claims.

Article 51 When a specialized farmers cooperative is dissolved due to the reasons specified in paragraph 1 of Article 48 of this Law, or when the People's Court accepts a bankruptcy application, no member withdrawal procedures may be processed.

Article 52 The liquidation group shall formulate a liquidation plan, including the payment of wages and social insurance premiums of employees of the specialized farmers cooperative, settlement of taxes and other debts, and distribution of remaining property. The plan shall be implemented after being approved by the general meeting or confirmed by the People's Court.

If the liquidation group finds that the property of the specialized farmers cooperative is insufficient to settle its debts, it shall apply to the People's Court for bankruptcy in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Property formed from direct financial subsidies received by the specialized farmers cooperative from the state shall not be distributed as distributable remaining assets to members during dissolution or bankruptcy liquidation. Specific measures shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the financial department of the State Council.

Article 54 Members of the liquidation group shall faithfully perform their duties and fulfill liquidation obligations in accordance with the law. If losses are caused to members or creditors of the specialized farmers cooperative due to intentional acts or gross negligence, they shall bear compensation liability.

Article 55 Bankruptcy of a specialized farmers cooperative shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Enterprise Bankruptcy Law. However, after settling bankruptcy expenses and debts of common interest, the bankruptcy property shall first be used to settle payments for transactions that occurred with farmer members before bankruptcy but have not yet been settled.

## Chapter VII: Federations of Specialized Farmers Cooperatives

Article 56 Three or more specialized farmers cooperatives may, on a voluntary basis, contribute capital to establish a federation of specialized farmers cooperatives.

A federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall have its own name, organizational structure, and domicile, a charter formulated and recognized by all members of the federation, and member contributions that comply with the charter.

Article 57 A federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be registered in accordance with this Law, obtain legal person status, and receive a business license. Its registration type shall be "Federation of Specialized Farmers Cooperatives."

Article 58 A federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall bear liability for its debts with all of its property; members of the federation shall bear liability for the federation's debts to the extent of their capital contributions.

Article 59 A federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall establish a general meeting attended by all members. Its powers include amending the charter of the federation, electing and removing the chairperson, directors, and supervisors of the federation, deciding on the operational plan and surplus distribution of the federation, and deciding on major matters such as external investment and guarantee plans.

A federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall not establish a member representative assembly but may, as needed, establish a board of directors, a board of supervisors, or an executive supervisor. The chairperson and directors shall be appointed by personnel selected by member cooperatives.

Article 60 In elections and voting at the general meeting of a federation of specialized farmers cooperatives, each member cooperative shall have one vote.

Article 61 The method for distributing distributable surplus of a federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be stipulated in the charter of the federation in accordance with the principles set forth in this Law.

Article 62 A member withdrawing from a federation of specialized farmers cooperatives shall submit a written request to the board of directors six months before the end of the fiscal year. The membership of the withdrawing member shall terminate at the end of the fiscal year.

Article 63 Matters not provided for in this Chapter concerning federations of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be governed by the provisions of this Law on specialized farmers cooperatives.

## Chapter VIII: Support Measures

Article 64 The state may entrust and arrange for qualified specialized farmers cooperatives to implement construction projects that support the development of agriculture and the rural economy.

Article 65 Central and local finances shall respectively allocate funds to support specialized farmers cooperatives in providing services such as information, training, agricultural product standards and certification, agricultural production infrastructure construction, marketing, and technology promotion. The state shall give priority assistance to specialized farmers cooperatives in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, strengthen supervision over the use of financial subsidy funds.

Article 66 State policy-based financial institutions shall adopt various forms to provide multi-channel financial support for specialized farmers cooperatives. Specific support policies shall be stipulated by the State Council.

The state encourages commercial financial institutions to adopt various forms to provide financial services to specialized farmers cooperatives and their members.

The state encourages insurance institutions to provide various forms of agricultural insurance services to specialized farmers cooperatives. Specialized farmers cooperatives are encouraged to carry out mutual insurance in accordance with the law.

Article 67 Specialized farmers cooperatives shall enjoy corresponding tax preferences stipulated by the state for agricultural production, processing, circulation, services, and other agriculture-related economic activities.

Article 68 Electricity used by specialized farmers cooperatives for the primary processing of agricultural products shall be charged at the price for electricity used in agricultural production. Land for productive supporting auxiliary facilities of specialized farmers cooperatives shall be managed as agricultural land. Specific measures shall be stipulated by the relevant departments of the State Council.

## Chapter IX: Legal Liability

Article 69 Anyone who embezzles, misappropriates, intercepts, privately distributes, or otherwise infringes upon the lawful property of specialized farmers cooperatives and their members, illegally interferes with the production and business activities of specialized farmers cooperatives and their members, imposes apportionments on specialized farmers cooperatives and their members, or forces specialized farmers cooperatives and their members to accept paid services, causing economic losses to specialized farmers cooperatives, shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 70 If a specialized farmers cooperative provides false registration materials to the registration authority or obtains registration through other fraudulent means, the registration authority shall order it to make corrections and may impose a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the registration shall be revoked or the business license shall be cancelled.

Article 71 If a specialized farmers cooperative has not engaged in business activities for two consecutive years, its business license shall be revoked.

Article 72 If a specialized farmers cooperative makes false records or conceals important facts in financial reports or other materials provided to the relevant competent authorities in accordance with the law, it shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter X: Supplementary Provisions

Article 73 This Law shall apply to specialized farmers cooperatives established by employees of state-owned farms, forest farms, livestock farms, and fisheries who operate under contract or lease arrangements and engage in agricultural production, operation, or services.

Article 74 This Law shall come into force on July 1, 2018.
