# Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on June 30, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 28, 2002

Revised at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on February 28, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws including the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws including the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating insurance activities, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of the parties involved in insurance activities, strengthening the supervision and administration of the insurance industry, maintaining the social economic order and public interests, and promoting the healthy development of the insurance industry.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "insurance" refers to a commercial insurance act whereby the applicant pays insurance premiums to the insurer in accordance with the terms of the contract, and the insurer is liable for compensating for property losses caused by the occurrence of a contingent event specified in the contract, or for paying insurance benefits upon the death, injury, illness, or attainment of a specified age or term by the insured.

Article 3 This Law shall apply to insurance activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 4 Insurance activities must comply with laws and administrative regulations, respect social ethics, and shall not harm the public interest.

Article 5 The parties to an insurance activity shall exercise their rights and perform their obligations in accordance with the principle of good faith.

Article 6 Insurance business shall be operated by insurance companies established in accordance with this Law and other insurance organizations prescribed by laws and administrative regulations. No other entity or individual may operate insurance business.

Article 7 Legal persons and other organizations within the territory of the People's Republic of China that need to obtain insurance coverage within the territory shall apply for insurance from insurance companies established within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 8 The insurance industry, banking industry, securities industry, and trust industry shall operate and be managed separately. Insurance companies and institutions for banking, securities, and trust business shall be established separately, unless otherwise provided by the State.

Article 9 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall supervise and administer the insurance industry in accordance with the law.

The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may establish local offices as needed for the performance of its duties. Such local offices shall perform their supervisory and administrative duties within the scope of authorization granted by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

## Chapter II Insurance Contracts

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 10 An insurance contract is an agreement between the applicant and the insurer defining their rights and obligations in relation to insurance.

An applicant is a person who enters into an insurance contract with an insurer and is obligated to pay insurance premiums in accordance with the contract.

An insurer is an insurance company that enters into an insurance contract with an applicant and is obligated to pay compensation or insurance benefits in accordance with the contract.

Article 11 An insurance contract shall be concluded through negotiation and agreement, and the rights and obligations of each party shall be determined in accordance with the principle of fairness.

Unless insurance is compulsory under laws or administrative regulations, insurance contracts shall be concluded voluntarily.

Article 12 At the time of concluding a life insurance contract, the applicant shall have an insurable interest in the insured.

At the time of the occurrence of an insured event under a property insurance contract, the insured shall have an insurable interest in the subject matter insured.

Life insurance is insurance with the life or body of a person as the subject matter insured.

Property insurance is insurance with property and related interests as the subject matter insured.

An insured is a person whose property or person is protected by the insurance contract and who is entitled to claim insurance benefits. The applicant may be the insured.

An insurable interest refers to an interest recognized by law that the applicant or the insured has in the subject matter insured.

Article 13 An insurance contract is formed when the applicant makes an insurance proposal and the insurer agrees to underwrite it. The insurer shall promptly issue an insurance policy or other insurance certificate to the applicant.

The insurance policy or other insurance certificate shall specify the contract terms agreed upon by the parties. The parties may also agree to specify the contract terms in other written forms.

An insurance contract lawfully formed shall become effective upon its formation. The applicant and the insurer may agree on conditions or a time period for the effectiveness of the contract.

Article 14 After the formation of an insurance contract, the applicant shall pay the insurance premiums as agreed, and the insurer shall begin to assume insurance liability from the agreed time.

Article 15 Unless otherwise provided by this Law or agreed in the insurance contract, after the formation of an insurance contract, the applicant may rescind the contract, but the insurer may not rescind the contract.

Article 16 When concluding an insurance contract, if the insurer inquires about the subject matter insured or the relevant circumstances of the insured, the applicant shall truthfully inform the insurer.

If the applicant intentionally or through gross negligence fails to fulfill the obligation of truthful disclosure as specified in the preceding paragraph, and such failure is sufficient to affect the insurer's decision on whether to underwrite the insurance or to increase the premium rate, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract.

The right to rescind the contract as specified in the preceding paragraph shall lapse if not exercised within thirty days from the date the insurer becomes aware of the grounds for rescission. If more than two years have elapsed from the date of formation of the contract, the insurer may not rescind the contract; if an insured event occurs, the insurer shall be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits.

If the applicant intentionally fails to fulfill the obligation of truthful disclosure, the insurer shall not be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits for insured events occurring before the rescission of the contract, and shall not refund the insurance premiums.

If the applicant fails to fulfill the obligation of truthful disclosure due to gross negligence, and such failure has a material impact on the occurrence of the insured event, the insurer shall not be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits for insured events occurring before the rescission of the contract, but shall refund the insurance premiums.

If the insurer was already aware of the applicant's failure to truthfully disclose at the time of concluding the contract, the insurer may not rescind the contract; if an insured event occurs, the insurer shall be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits.

An insured event refers to an event within the scope of insurance liability specified in the insurance contract.

Article 17 When concluding an insurance contract using standard clauses provided by the insurer, the insurance application form provided by the insurer to the applicant shall be accompanied by the standard clauses, and the insurer shall explain the contents of the contract to the applicant.

For clauses in the insurance contract that exclude the insurer's liability, the insurer shall, at the time of concluding the contract, make a prominent notice on the insurance application form, insurance policy, or other insurance certificate sufficient to draw the applicant's attention, and shall clearly explain the contents of such clauses to the applicant in writing or orally; if no such notice or clear explanation is given, such clauses shall not be effective.

Article 18 An insurance contract shall include the following particulars:

(1) The name and domicile of the insurer;
(2) The name or title and domicile of the applicant and the insured, and, in the case of life insurance, the name or title and domicile of the beneficiary;
(3) The subject matter insured;
(4) Insurance liability and exclusions from liability;
(5) The insurance period and the commencement time of insurance liability;
(6) The sum insured;
(7) The insurance premium and the method of payment;
(8) The method of paying compensation or insurance benefits;
(9) Liability for breach of contract and dispute resolution;
(10) The year, month, and day of the conclusion of the contract.

The applicant and the insurer may agree on other matters related to the insurance.

A beneficiary is a person designated by the insured or the applicant in a life insurance contract who is entitled to claim insurance benefits. The applicant or the insured may be the beneficiary.

The sum insured is the maximum amount for which the insurer is liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits.

Article 19 The following clauses in an insurance contract concluded using standard clauses provided by the insurer shall be null and void:

(1) Clauses that exempt the insurer from obligations it should bear under the law or that increase the liability of the applicant or the insured;
(2) Clauses that exclude rights lawfully enjoyed by the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary.

Article 20 The applicant and the insurer may negotiate and modify the contents of the contract.

If an insurance contract is modified, the insurer shall endorse or attach an endorsement slip to the insurance policy or other insurance certificate, or the applicant and the insurer shall enter into a written agreement on the modification.

Article 21 After becoming aware of the occurrence of an insured event, the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary shall promptly notify the insurer. If, due to intentional delay or gross negligence in failing to notify promptly, the nature, cause, and extent of loss of the insured event become difficult to determine, the insurer shall not be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits for the portion that cannot be determined, unless the insurer has already become aware or should have become aware of the occurrence of the insured event through other channels in a timely manner.

Article 22 After the occurrence of an insured event, when requesting the insurer to pay compensation or insurance benefits in accordance with the insurance contract, the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary shall provide the insurer with such evidence and materials as they can provide relating to the confirmation of the nature, cause, and extent of loss of the insured event.

If the insurer considers, in accordance with the terms of the contract, that the relevant evidence and materials are incomplete, it shall promptly notify the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary in a single notice to provide supplementary materials.

Article 23 After receiving a request for compensation or payment of insurance benefits from the insured or the beneficiary, the insurer shall promptly assess the claim; if the circumstances are complex, the assessment shall be completed within thirty days, unless otherwise agreed in the contract. The insurer shall notify the insured or the beneficiary of the assessment result; if the claim falls within the scope of insurance liability, the insurer shall fulfill its obligation to pay compensation or insurance benefits within ten days after reaching an agreement with the insured or the beneficiary on the amount of compensation or insurance benefits. If the insurance contract specifies a time limit for paying compensation or insurance benefits, the insurer shall fulfill its obligation to pay compensation or insurance benefits in accordance with that time limit.

If the insurer fails to fulfill its obligations as specified in the preceding paragraph in a timely manner, it shall, in addition to paying the insurance benefits, compensate the insured or the beneficiary for any losses incurred as a result.

No entity or individual may unlawfully interfere with the insurer's obligation to pay compensation or insurance benefits, nor may they restrict the right of the insured or the beneficiary to receive insurance benefits.

Article 24 After making an assessment in accordance with Article 23 of this Law, if the claim does not fall within the scope of insurance liability, the insurer shall, within three days from the date of making the assessment, issue a notice of refusal to pay compensation or insurance benefits to the insured or the beneficiary, stating the reasons therefor.

Article 25 If, within sixty days from the date of receiving the request for compensation or payment of insurance benefits and the relevant evidence and materials, the insurer is unable to determine the amount of compensation or insurance benefits payable, it shall make an advance payment based on the amount that can be determined from the available evidence and materials; after the insurer finally determines the amount of compensation or insurance benefits, it shall pay the corresponding difference.

Article 26 The statute of limitations for an insured or beneficiary of insurance other than life insurance to bring a lawsuit against the insurer requesting compensation or payment of insurance benefits is two years, commencing from the date on which the insured or beneficiary knows or should have known of the occurrence of the insured event.

The statute of limitations for an insured or beneficiary of life insurance to bring a lawsuit against the insurer requesting payment of insurance benefits is five years, commencing from the date on which the insured or beneficiary knows or should have known of the occurrence of the insured event.

Article 27 If, without the occurrence of an insured event, the insured or beneficiary falsely claims that an insured event has occurred and submits a request for compensation or payment of insurance benefits to the insurer, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract and shall not refund the insurance premiums.

If the applicant or the insured intentionally causes an insured event, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract and shall not be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits; except as provided in Article 43 of this Law, the insurance premiums shall not be refunded.

If, after the occurrence of an insured event, the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary fabricates a false cause of the event or exaggerates the extent of loss by using forged or altered relevant certificates, materials, or other evidence, the insurer shall not be liable for paying compensation or insurance benefits for the falsely reported portion.

If the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary engages in any of the acts specified in the preceding three paragraphs, thereby causing the insurer to pay insurance benefits or incur expenses, they shall refund or compensate for such amounts.

Article 28 When an insurer transfers part of the insurance business it has underwritten to other insurers in the form of reinsurance, this constitutes reinsurance.

At the request of the reinsurer, the ceding insurer shall inform the reinsurer in writing of its own retention and the relevant circumstances of the original insurance.

Article 29 The reinsurer shall not demand payment of insurance premiums from the applicant of the original insurance.

The insured or beneficiary of the original insurance shall not submit a request for compensation or payment of insurance benefits to the reinsurer.

The ceding insurer shall not refuse or delay fulfilling its obligations under the original insurance on the grounds that the reinsurer has not fulfilled its reinsurance obligations.

Article 30 If a dispute arises between the insurer and the applicant, the insured, or the beneficiary regarding the terms of an insurance contract concluded using standard clauses provided by the insurer, the terms shall be interpreted in accordance with their ordinary meaning. If there are two or more interpretations of the contract terms, the people's court or an arbitration institution shall make an interpretation favorable to the insured and the beneficiary.

### Section 2 Life Insurance Contracts

Article 31 The applicant has an insurable interest in the following persons:

(1) The applicant himself or herself;
(2) The applicant's spouse, children, and parents;
(3) Other family members and close relatives of the applicant, other than those specified in the preceding item, who have a relationship of support, maintenance, or care with the applicant;
(4) Workers who have an employment relationship with the applicant.

In addition to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, if the insured consents to the applicant entering into a contract on his or her behalf, the applicant shall be deemed to have an insurable interest in the insured.

If, at the time of concluding the contract, the applicant does not have an insurable interest in the insured, the contract shall be null and void.

Article 32 If the age of the insured declared by the applicant is untrue, and the true age does not fall within the age limit specified in the contract, the insurer may rescind the contract and refund the cash value of the insurance policy in accordance with the contract. The provisions of Article 16, paragraphs 3 and 6 of this Law shall apply to the insurer's exercise of the right to rescind the contract.

If the age of the insured declared by the applicant is untrue, resulting in the applicant paying less premium than the amount payable, the insurer shall have the right to correct the situation and require the applicant to pay the additional premium, or to pay the insurance benefits in proportion to the actual premium paid relative to the premium payable.

If the age of the insured declared by the applicant is untrue, resulting in the applicant paying more premium than the amount payable, the insurer shall refund the excess premium to the applicant.

Article 33 An applicant shall not apply for life insurance with death as the condition for payment of insurance benefits for a person who lacks civil capacity, and an insurer shall not underwrite such insurance.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply to life insurance taken out by parents for their minor children. However, the total amount of insurance benefits payable upon the death of the insured shall not exceed the limit prescribed by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 34 A contract with death as the condition for payment of insurance benefits shall be null and void if the insured has not consented to and acknowledged the sum insured.

An insurance policy issued under a contract with death as the condition for payment of insurance benefits shall not be transferred or pledged without the written consent of the insured.

The provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article shall not apply to life insurance taken out by parents for their minor children.

Article 35 The applicant may pay the entire insurance premium in a lump sum or in installments in accordance with the terms of the contract.

Article 36 If the contract provides for the payment of insurance premiums in installments, and after the applicant has paid the first installment, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, if the applicant fails to pay the current installment within thirty days from the date of the insurer's reminder, or fails to pay the current installment within sixty days from the agreed payment date, the validity of the contract shall be suspended, or the insurer shall reduce the sum insured in accordance with the conditions specified in the contract.

If an insured event occurs to the insured within the period specified in the preceding paragraph, the insurer shall pay the insurance benefits in accordance with the contract, but may deduct the overdue premiums.

Article 37 If the validity of a contract is suspended in accordance with Article 36 of this Law, the validity of the contract may be reinstated after the insurer and the applicant have negotiated and reached an agreement, and the applicant has paid the overdue premiums. However, if the parties fail to reach an agreement within two years from the date of suspension of the contract's validity, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract.

If the insurer rescinds the contract in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall refund the cash value of the insurance policy in accordance with the contract.

Article 38 The insurer shall not use litigation to demand payment of premiums for life insurance from the applicant.

Article 39 The beneficiary of a life insurance contract shall be designated by the insured or the applicant.

When designating a beneficiary, the applicant must obtain the consent of the insured. If the applicant takes out life insurance for a worker with whom it has an employment relationship, it shall not designate any person other than the insured or the insured's close relatives as the beneficiary.

If the insured is a person without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity, the beneficiary may be designated by the insured's guardian.

Article 40 The insured or the applicant may designate one or more persons as beneficiaries.

If there are multiple beneficiaries, the insured or the applicant may determine the order of entitlement and the share of benefits for each beneficiary; if the share of benefits is not determined, the beneficiaries shall share the benefits in equal proportions.

Article 41 The insured or the applicant may change the beneficiary and notify the insurer in writing. After receiving the written notice of the change of beneficiary, the insurer shall endorse or attach an endorsement slip to the insurance policy or other insurance certificate.

When the applicant changes the beneficiary, the consent of the insured must be obtained.

Article 42 After the death of the insured, in any of the following circumstances, the insurance benefits shall be treated as the estate of the insured, and the insurer shall fulfill its obligation to pay the insurance benefits in accordance with the provisions of the Succession Law of the People's Republic of China:

(1) No beneficiary has been designated, or the designation of the beneficiary is unclear and cannot be determined;
(2) The beneficiary predeceases the insured, and there are no other beneficiaries;
(3) The beneficiary has legally lost or waived the right to benefits, and there are no other beneficiaries.

If the beneficiary and the insured die in the same event and the order of death cannot be determined, it shall be presumed that the beneficiary died first.

Article 43 If the applicant intentionally causes the death, injury, or illness of the insured, the insurer shall not be liable for paying insurance benefits. If the applicant has paid the premiums for two years or more, the insurer shall, in accordance with the contract, refund the cash value of the insurance policy to other entitled persons.

If a beneficiary intentionally causes the death, injury, or illness of the insured, or attempts to kill the insured, that beneficiary shall lose the right to benefits.

Article 44 For a contract with the death of the insured as the condition for payment of insurance benefits, if the insured commits suicide within two years from the date of formation of the contract or the reinstatement of its validity, the insurer shall not be liable for paying insurance benefits, unless the insured was a person without civil capacity at the time of suicide.

If the insurer is not liable for paying insurance benefits in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall refund the cash value of the insurance policy in accordance with the contract.**Article 45** If the insured becomes disabled or dies as a result of intentional crime or resistance to legally-prescribed criminal coercive measures, the insurer shall not be liable for paying the insurance benefit. If the policyholder has paid premiums for two full years or more, the insurer shall refund the cash value of the insurance policy in accordance with the contract terms.

**Article 46** If an insured event such as death, disability, or illness of the insured occurs due to the actions of a third party, the insurer, after paying the insurance benefit to the insured or beneficiary, shall not acquire the right of subrogation against the third party. However, the insured or beneficiary retains the right to claim compensation from the third party.

**Article 47** If the policyholder rescinds the contract, the insurer shall, within thirty days from receipt of the notice of rescission, refund the cash value of the insurance policy in accordance with the contract terms.

### Section 3: Property Insurance Contracts

**Article 48** At the time of the occurrence of an insured event, if the insured has no insurable interest in the subject matter insured, the insured shall not be entitled to claim insurance benefits from the insurer.

**Article 49** Upon the transfer of the subject matter insured, the transferee of the subject matter insured succeeds to the rights and obligations of the insured.

Upon the transfer of the subject matter insured, the insured or the transferee shall promptly notify the insurer, except for cargo transportation insurance contracts and contracts otherwise agreed.

If the transfer of the subject matter insured leads to a material increase in risk, the insurer may, within thirty days from receipt of the notice specified in the preceding paragraph, increase the premium or rescind the contract in accordance with the contract terms. If the insurer rescinds the contract, it shall refund the premium collected, after deducting the portion attributable to the period from the commencement of insurance liability to the date of rescission, in accordance with the contract terms.

If the insured or transferee fails to fulfill the notification obligation specified in paragraph 2 of this Article, the insurer shall not be liable for paying insurance benefits for any insured event occurring due to the material increase in risk caused by the transfer.

**Article 50** For cargo transportation insurance contracts and voyage insurance contracts for means of transport, once the insurance liability has commenced, the parties to the contract shall not rescind the contract.

**Article 51** The insured shall comply with relevant national regulations on fire safety, safety, production operations, and labor protection, and maintain the safety of the subject matter insured.

The insurer may inspect the safety condition of the subject matter insured in accordance with the contract terms and promptly provide written suggestions to the policyholder and insured for eliminating unsafe factors and hidden dangers.

If the policyholder or insured fails to fulfill their obligations regarding the safety of the subject matter insured as agreed, the insurer shall have the right to demand an increase in premium or rescind the contract.

To maintain the safety of the subject matter insured, the insurer may, with the consent of the insured, take safety prevention measures.

**Article 52** During the term of the contract, if the risk level of the subject matter insured materially increases, the insured shall promptly notify the insurer in accordance with the contract terms. The insurer may increase the premium or rescind the contract in accordance with the contract terms. If the insurer rescinds the contract, it shall refund the premium collected, after deducting the portion attributable to the period from the commencement of insurance liability to the date of rescission, in accordance with the contract terms.

If the insured fails to fulfill the notification obligation specified in the preceding paragraph, the insurer shall not be liable for paying insurance benefits for any insured event occurring due to the material increase in risk of the subject matter insured.

**Article 53** Under any of the following circumstances, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insurer shall reduce the premium and refund the corresponding premium calculated on a daily basis:

(1) The relevant circumstances used to determine the premium rate have changed, resulting in a material decrease in the risk level of the subject matter insured;
(2) The insured value of the subject matter insured has materially decreased.

**Article 54** Before the commencement of insurance liability, if the policyholder requests to rescind the contract, they shall pay a handling fee to the insurer in accordance with the contract terms, and the insurer shall refund the premium. After the commencement of insurance liability, if the policyholder requests to rescind the contract, the insurer shall refund the premium collected, after deducting the portion attributable to the period from the commencement of insurance liability to the date of rescission, in accordance with the contract terms.

**Article 55** If the policyholder and the insurer agree on the insured value of the subject matter insured and specify it in the contract, the agreed insured value shall be the standard for calculating compensation when the subject matter insured suffers a loss.

If the policyholder and the insurer have not agreed on the insured value of the subject matter insured, the actual value of the subject matter insured at the time of the occurrence of the insured event shall be the standard for calculating compensation.

The sum insured shall not exceed the insured value. Any portion exceeding the insured value shall be void, and the insurer shall refund the corresponding premium.

If the sum insured is lower than the insured value, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insurer shall be liable for paying insurance benefits in proportion to the ratio of the sum insured to the insured value.

**Article 56** A policyholder effecting double insurance shall notify each insurer of the relevant details of the double insurance.

The total compensation paid by all insurers under double insurance shall not exceed the insured value. Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, each insurer shall be liable for paying insurance benefits in proportion to its sum insured to the total sum insured.

The policyholder effecting double insurance may request each insurer to refund the premium proportionally for the portion by which the total sum insured exceeds the insured value.

Double insurance refers to insurance where the policyholder enters into insurance contracts with two or more insurers separately for the same subject matter insured, the same insurable interest, and the same insured event, and the total sum insured exceeds the insured value.

**Article 57** At the time of the occurrence of an insured event, the insured shall take all necessary measures to prevent or mitigate the loss.

After the occurrence of an insured event, the necessary and reasonable expenses incurred by the insured to prevent or mitigate the loss of the subject matter insured shall be borne by the insurer. The amount of expenses borne by the insurer shall be calculated separately from the compensation for the loss of the subject matter insured, and shall not exceed the sum insured.

**Article 58** If the subject matter insured suffers a partial loss, the policyholder may rescind the contract within thirty days from the date of compensation by the insurer. Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insurer may also rescind the contract, but shall notify the policyholder fifteen days in advance.

If the contract is rescinded, the insurer shall refund the premium for the undamaged portion of the subject matter insured, after deducting the portion attributable to the period from the commencement of insurance liability to the date of rescission, in accordance with the contract terms.

**Article 59** After the occurrence of an insured event, if the insurer has paid the full sum insured and the sum insured equals the insured value, all rights to the damaged subject matter insured shall vest in the insurer. If the sum insured is lower than the insured value, the insurer shall acquire partial rights to the damaged subject matter insured in proportion to the ratio of the sum insured to the insured value.

**Article 60** If an insured event is caused by damage to the subject matter insured by a third party, the insurer, from the date of paying compensation to the insured, shall be subrogated to the insured's right to claim compensation from the third party, within the amount of compensation paid.

After the occurrence of the insured event specified in the preceding paragraph, if the insured has already obtained compensation for damages from the third party, the insurer may deduct the amount of compensation already obtained by the insured from the third party when paying the insurance benefit.

The exercise by the insurer of the right of subrogation pursuant to paragraph 1 of this Article shall not affect the insured's right to claim compensation from the third party for the portion not yet compensated.

**Article 61** If, after the occurrence of an insured event and before the insurer pays the insurance benefit, the insured waives the right to claim compensation from the third party, the insurer shall not be liable for paying the insurance benefit.

If, after the insurer pays the insurance benefit to the insured, the insured waives the right to claim compensation from the third party without the insurer's consent, such waiver shall be void.

If the insured, intentionally or through gross negligence, prevents the insurer from exercising the right of subrogation, the insurer may deduct or demand the return of the corresponding insurance benefit.

**Article 62** The insurer shall not exercise the right of subrogation against a family member or member of the insured's household, unless such person intentionally caused the insured event specified in paragraph 1 of Article 60 of this Law.

**Article 63** When the insurer exercises the right of subrogation against a third party, the insured shall provide the insurer with necessary documents and relevant information within their knowledge.

**Article 64** The necessary and reasonable expenses incurred by the insurer and the insured to investigate and determine the nature, cause, and extent of loss of the subject matter insured in relation to an insured event shall be borne by the insurer.

**Article 65** For damage caused by the insured under a liability insurance policy to a third party, the insurer may directly pay the insurance benefit to that third party in accordance with legal provisions or the contract terms.

If the insured under a liability insurance policy causes damage to a third party, and the insured's liability for compensation to the third party is determined, the insurer shall, upon the request of the insured, directly pay the insurance benefit to that third party. If the insured neglects to make such a request, the third party shall have the right to directly claim the insurance benefit from the insurer for the portion they are entitled to receive.

If the insured under a liability insurance policy causes damage to a third party but has not compensated that third party, the insurer shall not pay the insurance benefit to the insured.

Liability insurance refers to insurance where the subject matter insured is the liability for compensation that the insured bears to a third party according to law.

**Article 66** If the insured under a liability insurance policy is brought to arbitration or litigation due to an insured event causing damage to a third party, the arbitration or litigation costs and other necessary and reasonable expenses paid by the insured shall be borne by the insurer, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

## Chapter 3: Insurance Companies

**Article 67** The establishment of an insurance company shall be subject to approval by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

When reviewing an application for the establishment of an insurance company, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall consider the development needs of the insurance industry and the requirements of fair competition.

**Article 68** The establishment of an insurance company shall meet the following conditions:

(1) The major shareholders have sustained profitability, good reputation, no record of major violations of laws or regulations in the past three years, and net assets of not less than RMB 200 million;
(2) Having articles of association that comply with this Law and the Company Law of the People's Republic of China;
(3) Having a registered capital that complies with the provisions of this Law;
(4) Having directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel with the professional knowledge and business experience required for their positions;
(5) Having a sound organizational structure and management system;
(6) Having business premises that meet requirements and other facilities related to the business operations;
(7) Other conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 69** The minimum registered capital for the establishment of an insurance company is RMB 200 million.

The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may adjust the minimum registered capital requirement based on the business scope and scale of operations of the insurance company, but the adjusted amount shall not be lower than the limit specified in paragraph 1 of this Article.

The registered capital of an insurance company must be paid-in monetary capital.

**Article 70** To apply for the establishment of an insurance company, a written application shall be submitted to the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, along with the following materials:

(1) An application letter stating the proposed name, registered capital, business scope, etc., of the insurance company to be established;
(2) A feasibility study report;
(3) A preparation plan;
(4) The business licenses or other background information of the investors, and the audited financial accounting report for the previous fiscal year;
(5) The list of the person in charge of the preparatory group recognized by the investors and the proposed chairman and manager, along with their letters of acceptance;
(6) Other materials stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 71** The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall review the application for the establishment of an insurance company and shall, within six months from the date of acceptance, make a decision to approve or disapprove the preparation, and notify the applicant in writing. If the decision is to disapprove, the reasons shall be stated in writing.

**Article 72** The applicant shall complete the preparation work within one year from the date of receiving the notice of approval for preparation. During the preparation period, no insurance business activities shall be conducted.

**Article 73** After the completion of the preparation work, if the applicant meets the establishment conditions stipulated in Article 68 of this Law, they may submit an application for business commencement to the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall, within sixty days from the date of accepting the application for business commencement, make a decision to approve or disapprove the commencement of business. If the decision is to approve, an insurance business permit shall be issued. If the decision is to disapprove, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons stated.

**Article 74** The establishment of branches by an insurance company within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be subject to approval by the insurance regulatory authority.

Branches of an insurance company do not have legal personality, and their civil liabilities shall be borne by the insurance company.

**Article 75** To apply for the establishment of a branch, an insurance company shall submit a written application to the insurance regulatory authority, along with the following materials:

(1) An application letter for establishment;
(2) A three-year business development plan and market analysis materials for the proposed branch;
(3) Resumes and relevant supporting documents of the proposed senior management personnel;
(4) Other materials stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 76** The insurance regulatory authority shall review the application for the establishment of a branch by an insurance company and shall, within sixty days from the date of acceptance, make a decision to approve or disapprove. If the decision is to approve, a branch insurance business permit shall be issued. If the decision is to disapprove, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons stated.

**Article 77** An approved insurance company and its branches shall, based on the insurance business permit, register with the administrative authority for industry and commerce and obtain a business license.

**Article 78** If an insurance company or its branch fails to register with the administrative authority for industry and commerce within six months from the date of obtaining the insurance business permit without justifiable reasons, the insurance business permit shall become invalid.

**Article 79** The establishment of subsidiaries or branches by an insurance company outside the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be subject to approval by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 80** The establishment of representative offices by foreign insurance institutions within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be subject to approval by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council. Representative offices shall not engage in insurance business activities.

**Article 81** Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of an insurance company shall have good conduct, be familiar with laws and administrative regulations related to insurance, possess the management capabilities required to perform their duties, and obtain the qualifications approved by the insurance regulatory authority before assuming office.

The scope of senior management personnel of an insurance company shall be stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 82** Persons who fall under the circumstances specified in Article 146 of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China or any of the following circumstances shall not serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of an insurance company:

(1) Directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of financial institutions whose qualifications have been revoked by financial regulatory authorities due to illegal or disciplinary acts, where less than five years have elapsed since the revocation of their qualifications;
(2) Lawyers, certified public accountants, or professionals from asset appraisal institutions, verification institutions, etc., whose practicing qualifications have been revoked due to illegal or disciplinary acts, where less than five years have elapsed since the revocation of their practicing qualifications.

**Article 83** If directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of an insurance company violate laws, administrative regulations, or the company's articles of association while performing their duties, causing losses to the company, they shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 84** An insurance company shall obtain approval from the insurance regulatory authority under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Changing its name;
(2) Changing its registered capital;
(3) Changing the business premises of the company or its branches;
(4) Dissolving a branch;
(5) Splitting or merging the company;
(6) Amending the company's articles of association;
(7) Changing shareholders whose capital contribution accounts for five percent or more of the total capital of a limited liability company, or changing shareholders who hold five percent or more of the shares of a joint stock limited company;
(8) Other circumstances stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 85** An insurance company shall employ professionals and establish an actuarial reporting system and a compliance reporting system.

**Article 86** An insurance company shall submit relevant reports, statements, documents, and materials in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority.

The solvency reports, financial accounting reports, actuarial reports, compliance reports, and other relevant reports, statements, documents, and materials of an insurance company must truthfully record insurance business matters and shall not contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions.

**Article 87** An insurance company shall properly maintain complete books, original vouchers, and relevant materials related to its business operations in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

The retention period for the books, original vouchers, and relevant materials specified in the preceding paragraph shall be calculated from the date of termination of the insurance contract. It shall not be less than five years for insurance periods of one year or less, and not less than ten years for insurance periods exceeding one year.

**Article 88** When an insurance company engages or dismisses an accounting firm, asset appraisal institution, credit rating agency, or other intermediary service organization, it shall report to the insurance regulatory authority. When dismissing an accounting firm, asset appraisal institution, credit rating agency, or other intermediary service organization, it shall state the reasons.

**Article 89** An insurance company may be dissolved due to a split or merger requiring dissolution, a resolution of the shareholders' meeting or general meeting for dissolution, or the occurrence of a dissolution event specified in the company's articles of association, subject to approval by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

An insurance company engaged in life insurance business shall not be dissolved except due to a split, merger, or lawful revocation.

Upon the dissolution of an insurance company, a liquidation group shall be established according to law to conduct liquidation.

**Article 90** If an insurance company falls under the circumstances specified in Article 2 of the Enterprise Bankruptcy Law of the People's Republic of China, with the consent of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, the insurance company or its creditors may apply to the People's Court for reorganization, composition, or bankruptcy liquidation according to law. The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may also apply to the People's Court for reorganization or bankruptcy liquidation of the insurance company according to law.

**Article 91** After the bankruptcy estate has been used to satisfy the bankruptcy expenses and common benefit debts in priority, it shall be distributed in the following order:

(1) Wages owed to employees, medical and disability subsidies, bereavement benefits, basic pension insurance and basic medical insurance premiums owed to be transferred to employees' personal accounts, and compensation payable to employees as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations;
(2) Insurance benefits payable or to be paid;
(3) Social insurance premiums owed by the insurance company other than those specified in item (1) and taxes owed;
(4) Ordinary bankruptcy claims.

If the bankruptcy estate is insufficient to satisfy all claims within the same order, distribution shall be made on a pro rata basis.

The wages of directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of the bankrupt insurance company shall be calculated based on the average wage of the company's employees.Article 92 If an insurance company engaged in life insurance business is legally dissolved or declared bankrupt, its life insurance contracts and liability reserves must be transferred to another insurance company engaged in life insurance business; if no transfer agreement can be reached with another insurance company, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall designate an insurance company engaged in life insurance business to accept the transfer.

When transferring or when the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council designates an acceptance of the transfer of the life insurance contracts and liability reserves as specified in the preceding paragraph, the legitimate rights and interests of the insured and beneficiaries shall be protected.

Article 93 When an insurance company legally terminates its business activities, its insurance business permit shall be cancelled.

Article 94 Unless otherwise provided in this Law, an insurance company shall be governed by the provisions of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter IV Insurance Business Operation Rules

Article 95 The business scope of an insurance company:

(1) Personal insurance business, including life insurance, health insurance, accident injury insurance, and other insurance businesses;
(2) Property insurance business, including property loss insurance, liability insurance, credit insurance, guarantee insurance, and other insurance businesses;
(3) Other insurance-related businesses approved by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

An insurer shall not concurrently engage in both personal insurance business and property insurance business. However, an insurance company engaged in property insurance business may, with the approval of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, engage in short-term health insurance business and accident injury insurance business.

An insurance company shall conduct its insurance business activities within the business scope legally approved by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 96 With the approval of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, an insurance company may engage in the following reinsurance businesses for the insurance businesses specified in Article 95 of this Law:

(1) Ceding insurance;
(2) Accepting insurance.

Article 97 An insurance company shall set aside a guarantee fund equal to twenty percent of its total registered capital, deposit it in a bank designated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, and shall not use it except for paying off debts during the company's liquidation.

Article 98 An insurance company shall set aside various liability reserves based on the principles of protecting the interests of the insured and ensuring solvency.

The specific methods for an insurance company to set aside and carry forward liability reserves shall be formulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 99 An insurance company shall legally set aside a statutory surplus reserve fund.

Article 100 An insurance company shall pay an insurance protection fund.

The insurance protection fund shall be managed centrally and used in a coordinated manner under the following circumstances:

(1) To provide relief to policyholders, the insured, or beneficiaries when an insurance company is dissolved or declared bankrupt;
(2) To provide relief to an insurance company that legally accepts the life insurance contracts of an insurance company when that company is dissolved or declared bankrupt;
(3) Other circumstances stipulated by the State Council.

The specific methods for raising, managing, and using the insurance protection fund shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 101 An insurance company shall have a minimum solvency commensurate with its business scale and risk level. The difference between an insurance company's admissible assets and admissible liabilities shall not be less than the amount stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council; if it is less than the stipulated amount, the insurance company shall take corresponding measures as required by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council to reach the stipulated amount.

Article 102 The retained premiums of an insurance company engaged in property insurance business for the current year shall not exceed four times the sum of its actual paid-in capital plus its statutory surplus reserve fund.

Article 103 The liability undertaken by an insurance company for each risk unit, i.e., the maximum loss scope that may be caused by a single insurance accident, shall not exceed ten percent of the sum of its actual paid-in capital plus its statutory surplus reserve fund; the portion exceeding this limit shall be handled through reinsurance.

The classification of risk units by an insurance company shall comply with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 104 An insurance company's method for classifying risk units and its plan for arranging catastrophe risks shall be reported to the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council for recordation.

Article 105 An insurance company shall handle reinsurance in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council and shall prudently select the reinsurance acceptor.

Article 106 The utilization of funds by an insurance company must be sound and adhere to the principle of safety.

The utilization of funds by an insurance company is limited to the following forms:

(1) Bank deposits;
(2) Buying and selling bonds, stocks, securities investment fund shares, and other marketable securities;
(3) Investing in real estate;
(4) Other forms of fund utilization stipulated by the State Council.

The specific management measures for the utilization of funds by an insurance company shall be formulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs.

Article 107 With the approval of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, in conjunction with the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, an insurance company may establish an insurance asset management company.

An insurance asset management company engaging in securities investment activities shall comply with the provisions of the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The management measures for insurance asset management companies shall be formulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, in conjunction with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 108 An insurance company shall, in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, establish a system for the management and information disclosure of related-party transactions.

Article 109 The controlling shareholders, actual controllers, directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of an insurance company shall not use related-party transactions to harm the company's interests.

Article 110 An insurance company shall, in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, truthfully, accurately, and completely disclose major matters such as financial accounting reports, risk management status, and the operational situation of insurance products.

Article 111 Personnel engaged in insurance sales for an insurance company shall have good conduct and possess the professional competence required for insurance sales. The code of conduct and management measures for insurance sales personnel shall be formulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 112 An insurance company shall establish a registration and management system for insurance agents, strengthen the training and management of insurance agents, and shall not instigate or induce insurance agents to engage in activities that violate their fiduciary duties.

Article 113 An insurance company and its branches shall legally use the insurance business permit and shall not transfer, lease, or lend the insurance business permit.

Article 114 An insurance company shall, in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, fairly and reasonably formulate insurance clauses and premium rates, and shall not harm the legitimate rights and interests of policyholders, the insured, and beneficiaries.

An insurance company shall promptly fulfill its obligation to pay compensation or insurance benefits in accordance with the contract terms and the provisions of this Law.

Article 115 An insurance company shall follow the principle of fair competition when conducting business and shall not engage in unfair competition.

Article 116 An insurance company and its staff shall not engage in the following acts during insurance business activities:

(1) Deceiving policyholders, the insured, or beneficiaries;
(2) Concealing important information related to the insurance contract from policyholders;
(3) Obstructing policyholders from fulfilling the obligation of truthful disclosure as stipulated in this Law, or inducing them not to fulfill this obligation;
(4) Giving or promising to give policyholders, the insured, or beneficiaries premium rebates or other benefits not stipulated in the insurance contract;
(5) Refusing to fulfill the obligation to pay compensation or insurance benefits as stipulated in the insurance contract according to law;
(6) Intentionally fabricating insurance accidents that have not occurred, fabricating insurance contracts, or intentionally exaggerating the loss degree of insurance accidents that have occurred to make false claims, thereby defrauding insurance benefits or seeking other illegitimate benefits;
(7) Misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling insurance premiums;
(8) Entrusting institutions without legal qualifications to engage in insurance sales activities;
(9) Using the conduct of insurance business to seek illegitimate benefits for other institutions or individuals;
(10) Using insurance agents, insurance brokers, or insurance assessment institutions to engage in illegal activities such as fabricating insurance intermediary business or fabricating policy surrenders to extract funds;
(11) Damaging the business reputation of competitors by fabricating and spreading false facts, or disrupting the insurance market order through other acts of unfair competition;
(12) Disclosing the business secrets of policyholders or the insured learned during business activities;
(13) Other acts that violate laws, administrative regulations, and the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council.

## Chapter V Insurance Agents and Insurance Brokers

Article 117 An insurance agent is an institution or individual that, based on the entrustment of an insurer, collects commissions from the insurer and handles insurance business on behalf of the insurer within the scope authorized by the insurer.

Insurance agency institutions include specialized insurance agency institutions that exclusively engage in insurance agency business and concurrent-business insurance agency institutions that concurrently engage in insurance agency business.

Article 118 An insurance broker is an institution that, based on the interests of the policyholder, provides intermediary services for the policyholder and the insurer to enter into an insurance contract, and collects commissions according to law.

Article 119 Insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers shall meet the conditions stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council and obtain the insurance agency business permit or insurance brokerage business permit issued by the insurance regulatory authority.

Article 120 For a specialized insurance agency institution or insurance broker established in the form of a company, the minimum registered capital shall be governed by the provisions of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China.

The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may adjust the minimum registered capital for specialized insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers based on their business scope and operational scale, but it shall not be lower than the minimum limit stipulated in the Company Law of the People's Republic of China.

The registered capital or capital contribution of a specialized insurance agency institution or insurance broker must be paid-in monetary capital.

Article 121 Senior management personnel of specialized insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers shall have good conduct, be familiar with insurance laws and administrative regulations, possess the operational management capabilities required to perform their duties, and obtain the qualifications approved by the insurance regulatory authority before taking office.

Article 122 Individual insurance agents, agency practitioners of insurance agency institutions, and brokerage practitioners of insurance brokers shall have good conduct and possess the professional competence required to engage in insurance agency business or insurance brokerage business.

Article 123 Insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers shall have their own business premises and establish specialized account books to record the income and expenditure of their insurance agency and brokerage business.

Article 124 Insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers shall, in accordance with the regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, deposit a guarantee fund or purchase professional liability insurance.

Article 125 When an individual insurance agent handles life insurance business on behalf of an insurer, they shall not accept entrustment from two or more insurers simultaneously.

Article 126 When an insurer entrusts an insurance agent to handle insurance business on its behalf, it shall sign an entrustment agency agreement with the insurance agent, legally stipulating the rights and obligations of both parties.

Article 127 The insurer shall bear responsibility for the acts of an insurance agent handling insurance business on its behalf within the scope of the insurer's authorization.

If an insurance agent, without authority, exceeds the scope of authority, or after the termination of authority, enters into a contract in the name of the insurer, and the policyholder has reason to believe that the agent has such authority, the agency act shall be valid. The insurer may pursue the liability of the insurance agent who exceeded their authority according to law.

Article 128 If an insurance broker causes losses to a policyholder or the insured due to fault, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

Article 129 Parties to an insurance activity may entrust an independent assessment institution legally established, such as an insurance assessment institution, or personnel with relevant professional knowledge to assess and appraise an insurance accident.

Institutions and personnel entrusted to assess and appraise an insurance accident shall conduct the assessment and appraisal legally, independently, objectively, and impartially, and no unit or individual shall interfere.

If the institutions and personnel specified in the preceding paragraph cause losses to the insurer or the insured due to intent or negligence, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

Article 130 Insurance commissions shall only be paid to insurance agents and insurance brokers, and shall not be paid to any other person.

Article 131 Insurance agents, insurance brokers, and their practitioners shall not engage in the following acts during insurance business activities:

(1) Deceiving the insurer, policyholder, insured, or beneficiary;
(2) Concealing important information related to the insurance contract;
(3) Obstructing the policyholder from fulfilling the obligation of truthful disclosure as stipulated in this Law, or inducing them not to fulfill this obligation;
(4) Giving or promising to give the policyholder, insured, or beneficiary benefits not stipulated in the insurance contract;
(5) Using administrative power, position, or professional convenience, or other improper means to force, induce, or restrict the policyholder from entering into an insurance contract;
(6) Forging or unauthorizedly altering an insurance contract, or providing false certification materials to the parties to an insurance contract;
(7) Misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling insurance premiums or insurance benefits;
(8) Using business convenience to seek illegitimate benefits for other institutions or individuals;
(9) Colluding with the policyholder, insured, or beneficiary to defraud insurance benefits;
(10) Disclosing the business secrets of the insurer, policyholder, or insured learned during business activities.

Article 132 The provisions of Article 86, paragraph 1, and Article 113 of this Law shall apply to insurance agency institutions and insurance brokers.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Administration of the Insurance Industry

Article 133 The insurance regulatory authority shall, in accordance with this Law and the duties stipulated by the State Council, follow the principles of legality, openness, and fairness, implement supervision and administration over the insurance industry, maintain the order of the insurance market, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of policyholders, the insured, and beneficiaries.

Article 134 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, formulate and issue regulations concerning the supervision and administration of the insurance industry.

Article 135 Insurance clauses and premium rates for insurance types that affect the public interest, types subject to compulsory insurance according to law, and newly developed life insurance types shall be submitted to the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council for approval. When reviewing and approving, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall follow the principles of protecting the public interest and preventing unfair competition. Insurance clauses and premium rates for other insurance types shall be submitted to the insurance regulatory authority for recordation.

The specific methods for the approval and recordation of insurance clauses and premium rates shall be formulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 136 If the insurance clauses and premium rates used by an insurance company violate laws, administrative regulations, or the relevant regulations of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, the insurance regulatory authority shall order the cessation of their use and a revision within a specified period; if the circumstances are serious, the company may be prohibited from filing new insurance clauses and premium rates for a certain period.

Article 137 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish and improve a solvency supervision system for insurance companies and monitor the solvency of insurance companies.

Article 138 For an insurance company with insufficient solvency, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall list it as a key supervision target and may take the following measures based on the specific circumstances:

(1) Ordering an increase in capital and handling reinsurance;
(2) Restricting the business scope;
(3) Restricting the distribution of dividends to shareholders;
(4) Restricting the scale of fixed asset purchases or operating expenses;
(5) Restricting the forms and proportions of fund utilization;
(6) Restricting the establishment of new branches;
(7) Ordering the auction of non-performing assets and the transfer of insurance business;
(8) Restricting the compensation levels of directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(9) Restricting commercial advertising;
(10) Ordering the cessation of accepting new business.

Article 139 If an insurance company fails to set aside or carry forward various liability reserves in accordance with this Law, fails to handle reinsurance in accordance with this Law, or seriously violates the provisions of this Law regarding fund utilization, the insurance regulatory authority shall order a correction within a specified period and may order the adjustment of responsible persons and relevant management personnel.

Article 140 After the insurance regulatory authority makes a decision ordering a correction within a specified period in accordance with Article 139 of this Law, if the insurance company fails to correct within the specified period, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may decide to select insurance professionals and designate relevant personnel of the insurance company to form a rectification team to rectify the company.

The rectification decision shall state the name of the company being rectified, the reasons for rectification, the members of the rectification team, and the rectification period, and shall be publicly announced.

Article 141 The rectification team has the authority to supervise the daily business of the insurance company under rectification. The responsible persons and relevant management personnel of the company under rectification shall exercise their powers under the supervision of the rectification team.

Article 142 During the rectification process, the original business of the insurance company under rectification shall continue. However, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may order the company under rectification to cease part of its original business, cease accepting new business, and adjust fund utilization.

Article 143 When the insurance company under rectification has corrected its violations of this Law and restored normal business operations, the rectification team shall submit a report. With the approval of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, the rectification shall be concluded, and the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall make a public announcement.

Article 144 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may place an insurance company under receivership under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The company's solvency is seriously insufficient;
(2) The company violates the provisions of this Law, harms the public interest, and may seriously endanger or has already seriously endangered the company's solvency.

The creditor-debtor relationships of the insurance company under receivership shall not change due to the receivership.

Article 145 The composition of the receivership team and the implementation measures for the receivership shall be decided by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council and publicly announced.

Article 146 Upon the expiration of the receivership period, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may decide to extend the receivership period, but the maximum receivership period shall not exceed two years.

Article 147 Upon the expiration of the receivership period, if the insurance company under receivership has restored normal operational capacity, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall decide to terminate the receivership and make a public announcement.

Article 148 If an insurance company under rectification or receivership falls under the circumstances specified in Article 2 of the Enterprise Bankruptcy Law of the People's Republic of China, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may apply to the People's Court for reorganization or bankruptcy liquidation of the insurance company according to law.

Article 149 If an insurance company's insurance business permit is legally revoked due to illegal operations, or its solvency falls below the standards stipulated by the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, and failure to dissolve it would seriously harm the order of the insurance market and the public interest, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall dissolve it and make a public announcement, and promptly organize a liquidation team to carry out liquidation according to law.Article 150 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council has the right to require shareholders and actual controllers of an insurance company to provide relevant information and materials within a specified period.

Article 151 If a shareholder of an insurance company seriously damages the company's interests through related-party transactions, jeopardizing the company's solvency, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall order rectification. Before rectification is completed as required, the authority may restrict the shareholder's rights; if the shareholder refuses to rectify, the authority may order the transfer of the shareholder's equity in the insurance company.

Article 152 The insurance regulatory authority may, as needed for performing its supervisory duties, hold supervisory discussions with directors, supervisors, and senior management of an insurance company, requiring them to provide explanations on major matters concerning the company's business activities and risk management.

Article 153 During the period of rectification, takeover, or liquidation upon revocation of an insurance company, or when the company faces major risks, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may take the following measures against the directly responsible directors, supervisors, senior management, and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Notify the exit-entry administration authority to prevent their departure from the country in accordance with the law;
(2) Apply to a judicial authority to prohibit them from transferring, assigning, or otherwise disposing of property, or from creating other rights over property.

Article 154 When performing its duties in accordance with the law, the insurance regulatory authority may take the following measures:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of insurance companies, insurance agents, insurance brokers, insurance asset management companies, and representative offices of foreign insurance institutions;
(2) Enter the premises where suspected illegal activities occur to investigate and collect evidence;
(3) Question the parties involved and the entities and individuals related to the investigated incident, requiring them to provide explanations on matters related to the investigation;
(4) Inspect and copy materials such as property rights registrations related to the investigated incident;
(5) Inspect and copy financial accounting materials and other relevant documents and materials of insurance companies, insurance agents, insurance brokers, insurance asset management companies, representative offices of foreign insurance institutions, and entities and individuals related to the investigated incident; seal up documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed;
(6) Inquire into the bank accounts of insurance companies, insurance agents, insurance brokers, insurance asset management companies, representative offices of foreign insurance institutions, and entities and individuals suspected of illegal operations or related to suspected illegal activities;
(7) If there is evidence proving that illegal funds or other involved property has been or may be transferred or concealed, or that important evidence has been concealed, forged, or destroyed, apply to a people's court for freezing or sealing up such property, subject to approval by the principal of the insurance regulatory authority.

When taking measures (1), (2), or (5) of the preceding paragraph, the insurance regulatory authority must obtain approval from its head; when taking measure (6), approval from the head of the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council is required.

When conducting supervision, inspection, or investigation in accordance with the law, the insurance regulatory authority must have at least two personnel present, who shall produce valid credentials and a notice of supervision, inspection, or investigation; if fewer than two personnel are present or they fail to produce valid credentials and the notice, the entity or individual under inspection or investigation has the right to refuse.

Article 155 Entities and individuals under inspection or investigation shall cooperate when the insurance regulatory authority performs its duties in accordance with the law.

Article 156 Personnel of the insurance regulatory authority shall be devoted to their duties, act in accordance with the law, be fair and incorruptible, shall not use their positions to seek improper benefits, and shall not disclose the commercial secrets of entities and individuals they have learned about.

Article 157 The insurance regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish a mechanism for sharing supervisory information with the People's Bank of China and other financial regulatory authorities under the State Council.

When the insurance regulatory authority performs its duties, conducts supervision, inspection, or investigation in accordance with the law, relevant departments shall cooperate.

## Chapter 7 Legal Liability

Article 158 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, establishes an insurance company or insurance asset management company without authorization, or illegally engages in commercial insurance business, shall be banned by the insurance regulatory authority, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined between one and five times the amount of illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of between 200,000 and 1 million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 159 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, establishes a specialized insurance agency or insurance broker without authorization, or engages in insurance agency or brokerage business without obtaining a license for insurance agency or brokerage business, shall be banned by the insurance regulatory authority, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined between one and five times the amount of illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 160 If an insurance company, in violation of this Law, engages in business beyond its approved scope, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification within a specified period, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of between one and five times the amount of illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of between 100,000 and 500,000 yuan shall be imposed. If rectification is not made within the time limit or serious consequences result, the authority shall order suspension of business for rectification or revoke the business license.

Article 161 If an insurance company commits any of the acts specified in Article 116 of this Law, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority may restrict its business scope, order it to stop accepting new business, or revoke its business license.

Article 162 If an insurance company violates the provisions of Article 84 of this Law, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 10,000 and 100,000 yuan.

Article 163 If an insurance company, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan:

(1) Underwriting risks in excess of its capacity, where the circumstances are serious;
(2) Underwriting insurance for a person without civil capacity where the insurance payment is conditional on death.

Article 164 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the insurance regulatory authority to rectify and be fined between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority may restrict its business scope, order it to stop accepting new business, or revoke its business license:

(1) Failing to deposit a guarantee fund as required or using the guarantee fund in violation of regulations;
(2) Failing to set aside or carry forward various liability reserves as required;
(3) Failing to pay the insurance protection fund or set aside a provident fund as required;
(4) Failing to arrange reinsurance as required;
(5) Failing to use insurance company funds as required;
(6) Establishing branches without approval;
(7) Failing to apply for approval of insurance clauses and premium rates as required.

Article 165 If an insurance agency or insurance broker commits any of the acts specified in Article 131 of this Law, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority shall revoke its business license.

Article 166 If an insurance agency or insurance broker, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 20,000 and 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority shall order suspension of business for rectification or revoke its business license:

(1) Failing to deposit a guarantee fund or take out professional liability insurance as required;
(2) Failing to set up a dedicated account to record business income and expenses as required.

Article 167 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, appoints a person who does not meet the qualifications for the position shall be ordered by the insurance regulatory authority to rectify and be fined between 20,000 and 100,000 yuan.

Article 168 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, transfers, lends, or leases a business license shall be fined between 10,000 and 100,000 yuan by the insurance regulatory authority; if the circumstances are serious, the authority shall order suspension of business for rectification or revoke the business license.

Article 169 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the insurance regulatory authority to rectify within a specified period; if rectification is not made within the time limit, a fine of between 10,000 and 100,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to submit or retain reports, statements, documents, or materials as required, or failing to provide relevant information or materials as required;
(2) Failing to file insurance clauses or premium rates for record as required;
(3) Failing to disclose information as required.

Article 170 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the insurance regulatory authority to rectify and be fined between 100,000 and 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority may restrict its business scope, order it to stop accepting new business, or revoke its business license:

(1) Preparing or providing false reports, statements, documents, or materials;
(2) Refusing or obstructing lawful supervision and inspection;
(3) Failing to use approved or filed insurance clauses or premium rates as required.

Article 171 If an insurance company, insurance asset management company, specialized insurance agency, or insurance broker violates this Law, the insurance regulatory authority shall, in addition to imposing penalties on the entity in accordance with Articles 160 to 170 of this Law, issue a warning to the directly responsible managers and other directly responsible personnel and impose a fine of between 10,000 and 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the authority shall revoke their qualifications for holding the position.

Article 172 If an individual insurance agent violates this Law, the insurance regulatory authority shall issue a warning and may impose a fine of up to 20,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of between 20,000 and 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 173 If a foreign insurance institution establishes a representative office within the territory of the People's Republic of China without approval from the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council, the authority shall ban it and impose a fine of between 50,000 and 300,000 yuan.

If a representative office established within the territory of the People's Republic of China by a foreign insurance institution engages in insurance business activities, the insurance regulatory authority shall order rectification, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of between one and five times the amount of illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of between 200,000 and 1 million yuan shall be imposed; the authority may order the replacement of its chief representative; if the circumstances are serious, the authority shall revoke the representative office.

Article 174 If a policyholder, insured, or beneficiary commits any of the following acts to engage in insurance fraud, which does not constitute a crime, an administrative penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law:

(1) The policyholder intentionally fabricates the subject matter of insurance to defraud insurance money;
(2) Fabricating an insurance accident that did not occur, or fabricating a false cause of an accident or exaggerating the extent of loss, to defraud insurance money;
(3) Intentionally causing an insurance accident to defraud insurance money.

If an appraiser, assessor, or witness of an insurance accident intentionally provides false documentation to facilitate insurance fraud by the policyholder, insured, or beneficiary, a penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 175 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, causes harm to others shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 176 Anyone who refuses or obstructs the insurance regulatory authority or its personnel from lawfully exercising their powers of supervision, inspection, or investigation, without using violence or threats, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 177 If the circumstances are serious in violation of laws or administrative regulations, the insurance regulatory authority under the State Council may prohibit the responsible persons from entering the insurance industry for a specified period or permanently.

Article 178 If personnel of the insurance regulatory authority engaged in supervisory work fall under any of the following circumstances, they shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law:

(1) Approving the establishment of an institution in violation of regulations;
(2) Approving insurance clauses or premium rates in violation of regulations;
(3) Conducting on-site inspections in violation of regulations;
(4) Inquiring into accounts or freezing funds in violation of regulations;
(5) Disclosing commercial secrets of entities and individuals they have learned about;
(6) Imposing administrative penalties in violation of regulations;
(7) Other acts of abuse of power or dereliction of duty.

Article 179 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits a crime shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 180 Insurance companies shall join the insurance industry association. Insurance agents, insurance brokers, and insurance appraisal institutions may join the insurance industry association.

The insurance industry association is a self-regulatory organization of the insurance industry and a social organization with legal personality.

Article 181 This Law applies to commercial insurance business operated by other insurance organizations lawfully established other than insurance companies.

Article 182 Marine insurance shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Maritime Law of the People's Republic of China; matters not covered by the Maritime Law shall be governed by the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 183 Sino-foreign joint venture insurance companies, wholly foreign-owned insurance companies, and branches of foreign insurance companies shall be governed by this Law; if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Article 184 The State supports the development of insurance undertakings serving agricultural production. Agricultural insurance shall be separately regulated by laws and administrative regulations.

For compulsory insurance, if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Article 185 This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 2009.
