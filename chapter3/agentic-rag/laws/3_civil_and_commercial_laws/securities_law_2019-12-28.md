# Securities Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 29, 1998

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

First Revision at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 27, 2005

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws including the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

Second Revision at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 28, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating the issuance and trading of securities, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of investors, maintaining the socio-economic order and public interests, and promoting the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 The issuance and trading of stocks, corporate bonds, depositary receipts, and other securities recognized by the State Council within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by this Law; matters not provided for in this Law shall be governed by the Company Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations.

The listing and trading of government bonds and shares of securities investment funds shall be governed by this Law; where other laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

The administrative measures for the issuance and trading of asset-backed securities and asset management products shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the principles of this Law.

Where securities issuance and trading activities outside the territory of the People's Republic of China disrupt the market order within the territory of the People's Republic of China and harm the legitimate rights and interests of domestic investors, they shall be dealt with and legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 3 The issuance and trading of securities must adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and justice.

Article 4 The parties involved in securities issuance and trading activities have equal legal status and shall abide by the principles of voluntariness, compensation, good faith, and creditworthiness.

Article 5 The issuance and trading of securities must comply with laws and administrative regulations; fraudulent activities, insider trading, and market manipulation are prohibited.

Article 6 The securities industry, banking industry, trust industry, and insurance industry shall operate and be managed separately. Securities companies shall be established separately from banking, trust, and insurance business institutions, unless otherwise provided by the State.

Article 7 The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall exercise centralized and unified supervision and management over the national securities market in accordance with the law.

The securities regulatory authority under the State Council may establish dispatched agencies as needed to perform supervisory and management duties under authorization.

Article 8 The State audit authority shall conduct audit supervision over securities exchanges, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, and securities regulatory authorities in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Securities Issuance

Article 9 The public issuance of securities must meet the conditions prescribed by laws and administrative regulations and be registered with the securities regulatory authority under the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council in accordance with the law. No unit or individual may publicly issue securities without lawful registration. The specific scope and implementation steps of the securities issuance registration system shall be prescribed by the State Council.

The following circumstances constitute a public issuance:

(1) Issuing securities to non-specific objects;

(2) Issuing securities to specific objects where the cumulative number exceeds two hundred persons, except that the number of employees participating in an employee stock ownership plan implemented in accordance with the law shall not be counted;

(3) Other issuance activities as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Non-public issuance of securities shall not adopt advertising, public solicitation, or disguised public methods.

Article 10 Where an issuer applies for the public issuance of stocks or corporate bonds convertible into stocks, and adopts an underwriting method in accordance with the law, or publicly issues other securities for which a sponsor system is required by laws or administrative regulations, it shall engage a securities company to act as the sponsor.

The sponsor shall comply with business rules and industry standards, act in good faith and with due diligence, carefully verify the issuer's application documents and information disclosure materials, and supervise the issuer's standardized operations.

The administrative measures for sponsors shall be prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 11 To establish a joint stock limited company and publicly issue stocks, the company shall meet the conditions prescribed by the Company Law of the People's Republic of China and other conditions prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council with the approval of the State Council, and shall submit a share offering application and the following documents to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) The company's articles of association;

(2) The promoter's agreement;

(3) The names or titles of the promoters, the number of shares subscribed by the promoters, the types of capital contributions, and capital verification certificates;

(4) The prospectus;

(5) The name and address of the bank designated to collect share subscription payments;

(6) The name of the underwriting institution and the relevant agreement.

If a sponsor is engaged in accordance with this Law, the issuance sponsor letter issued by the sponsor shall also be submitted.

If the establishment of a company must be approved by the relevant authorities as required by laws or administrative regulations, the corresponding approval documents shall also be submitted.

Article 12 A company making an initial public offering of new shares shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a sound and well-functioning organizational structure;

(2) Having sustainable operational capability;

(3) Having unqualified audit opinions on its financial accounting reports for the most recent three years;

(4) The issuer and its controlling shareholders and actual controllers have not been involved in criminal offenses of embezzlement, bribery, misappropriation of property, or disrupting the socialist market economic order in the most recent three years;

(5) Other conditions prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council with the approval of the State Council.

A listed company issuing new shares shall meet the conditions prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council with the approval of the State Council, and the specific administrative measures shall be prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

The public issuance of depositary receipts shall meet the conditions for an initial public offering of new shares and other conditions prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 13 A company publicly issuing new shares shall submit a share offering application and the following documents:

(1) The company's business license;

(2) The company's articles of association;

(3) The resolution of the shareholders' general meeting;

(4) The prospectus or other public offering documents;

(5) Financial accounting reports;

(6) The name and address of the bank designated to collect share subscription payments.

If a sponsor is engaged in accordance with this Law, the issuance sponsor letter issued by the sponsor shall also be submitted. If underwriting is adopted in accordance with this Law, the name of the underwriting institution and the relevant agreement shall also be submitted.

Article 14 A company must use the funds raised from the public issuance of stocks strictly in accordance with the purposes specified in the prospectus or other public offering documents; any change in the use of funds must be resolved by the shareholders' general meeting. If the use of funds is changed without authorization and not corrected, or not approved by the shareholders' general meeting, the company shall not publicly issue new shares.

Article 15 The public issuance of corporate bonds shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a sound and well-functioning organizational structure;

(2) Having average distributable profits for the most recent three years sufficient to pay one year's interest on the corporate bonds;

(3) Other conditions prescribed by the State Council.

The funds raised from the public issuance of corporate bonds must be used strictly in accordance with the purposes specified in the corporate bond offering prospectus; any change in the use of funds must be resolved by a meeting of bondholders. The funds raised from the public issuance of corporate bonds shall not be used to cover losses or for non-productive expenditures.

A listed company issuing corporate bonds convertible into stocks shall, in addition to meeting the conditions specified in the first paragraph, also comply with the provisions of Article 12, paragraph 2 of this Law. However, this shall not apply where the listed company converts corporate bonds through the repurchase of its own shares in accordance with the corporate bond offering prospectus.

Article 16 To apply for the public issuance of corporate bonds, the following documents shall be submitted to the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) The company's business license;

(2) The company's articles of association;

(3) The corporate bond offering prospectus;

(4) Other documents prescribed by the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

If a sponsor is engaged in accordance with this Law, the issuance sponsor letter issued by the sponsor shall also be submitted.

Article 17 A company shall not make another public issuance of corporate bonds under any of the following circumstances:

(1) There is a fact of default or delayed payment of principal and interest on previously publicly issued corporate bonds or other debts, and such fact continues to exist;

(2) The use of funds raised from the public issuance of corporate bonds has been changed in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Article 18 The format and submission method of the application documents submitted by an issuer for the lawful application for public issuance of securities shall be prescribed by the institution or department responsible for registration in accordance with the law.

Article 19 The securities issuance application documents submitted by an issuer shall fully disclose the information necessary for investors to make value judgments and investment decisions, and the content shall be truthful, accurate, and complete.

Securities service institutions and their personnel who issue relevant documents for securities issuance must strictly perform their statutory duties and ensure the truthfulness, accuracy, and completeness of the documents issued.

Article 20 Where an issuer applies for an initial public offering of stocks, after submitting the application documents, it shall pre-disclose the relevant application documents in accordance with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 21 The securities regulatory authority under the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council shall be responsible for the registration of securities issuance applications in accordance with statutory conditions. The specific measures for the registration of public securities issuance shall be prescribed by the State Council.

In accordance with the provisions of the State Council, stock exchanges, among others, may review applications for public securities issuance, determine whether the issuer meets the issuance conditions and information disclosure requirements, and urge the issuer to improve the content of information disclosure.

Personnel participating in the registration of securities issuance applications in accordance with the preceding two paragraphs shall not have any interest in the applicant, shall not directly or indirectly accept gifts from the applicant, shall not hold the securities of the registered issuance application, and shall not have private contact with the applicant.

Article 22 The securities regulatory authority under the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council shall, within three months from the date of accepting the securities issuance application documents, make a decision on whether to register or not in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures. The time taken by the issuer to supplement or amend the issuance application documents as required shall not be counted. If registration is denied, the reasons shall be stated.

Article 23 After the securities issuance application is registered, the issuer shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, announce the public offering documents before the public issuance of securities and make such documents available at designated places for public inspection.

Before the information on the issuance of securities is lawfully made public, any person who is aware of such information shall not disclose or divulge it.

The issuer shall not issue securities before announcing the public offering documents.

Article 24 If the securities regulatory authority under the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council finds that a decision on the registration of a securities issuance that has already been made does not meet the statutory conditions or procedures, and the securities have not yet been issued, it shall revoke the decision and stop the issuance. If the securities have been issued but not yet listed, the decision on the registration of the issuance shall be revoked, and the issuer shall return the securities to the holders at the issuance price plus bank deposit interest for the same period; the controlling shareholders, actual controllers, and sponsors of the issuer shall bear joint and several liability with the issuer, unless they can prove that they are not at fault.

If the issuer of stocks conceals important facts or fabricates significant false content in the prospectus or other securities issuance documents, and the stocks have been issued and listed, the securities regulatory authority under the State Council may order the issuer to repurchase the securities, or order the responsible controlling shareholders or actual controllers to buy back the securities.

Article 25 After stocks are lawfully issued, the issuer shall be responsible for any changes in its operations and profits; the investment risks arising from such changes shall be borne by the investors.

Article 26 Where securities issued by an issuer to non-specific objects are required by laws or administrative regulations to be underwritten by a securities company, the issuer shall enter into an underwriting agreement with the securities company. Securities underwriting business shall adopt the method of agency underwriting or firm commitment underwriting.

Agency underwriting refers to the underwriting method where a securities company sells securities on behalf of the issuer and, at the end of the underwriting period, returns all unsold securities to the issuer.

Firm commitment underwriting refers to the underwriting method where a securities company purchases all of the issuer's securities in accordance with the agreement or purchases all remaining securities after the underwriting period ends.

Article 27 An issuer of publicly issued securities has the right to independently choose the underwriting securities company in accordance with the law.

Article 28 When a securities company underwrites securities, it shall enter into an agency or firm commitment underwriting agreement with the issuer, specifying the following matters:

(1) The names, domiciles, and names of legal representatives of the parties;

(2) The types, quantities, amounts, and issuance prices of the securities to be underwritten on an agency or firm commitment basis;

(3) The term and start and end dates of the agency or firm commitment underwriting;

(4) The payment method and date for the agency or firm commitment underwriting;

(5) The fees and settlement methods for the agency or firm commitment underwriting;

(6) Liability for breach of contract;

(7) Other matters prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 29 When a securities company underwrites securities, it shall verify the truthfulness, accuracy, and completeness of the public offering documents. If it discovers any false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall not proceed with the sales activities; if sales have already commenced, it must immediately stop the sales activities and take corrective measures.

When a securities company underwrites securities, it shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Conducting false or misleading advertising or other promotional activities;

(2) Soliciting underwriting business through unfair competition means;

(3) Other acts that violate the provisions on securities underwriting business.

If a securities company commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph and causes losses to other securities underwriting institutions or investors, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 30 Where an underwriting syndicate is engaged for the issuance of securities to non-specific objects, the underwriting syndicate shall be composed of a lead underwriter and participating securities companies.

Article 31 The maximum term for agency underwriting or firm commitment underwriting of securities shall not exceed ninety days.

During the period of agency or firm commitment underwriting, a securities company shall ensure that the securities it underwrites on an agency or firm commitment basis are first sold to subscribers. The securities company shall not reserve securities for itself under agency underwriting or pre-purchase and retain securities under firm commitment underwriting.

Article 32 Where stocks are issued at a premium, the issuance price shall be determined through negotiation between the issuer and the underwriting securities company.

Article 33 If the stock issuance adopts the method of agency underwriting, and at the end of the underwriting period, the number of stocks sold to investors is less than seventy percent of the number of stocks intended for public issuance, the issuance shall be deemed a failure. The issuer shall return the stock subscription payments to the subscribers at the issuance price plus bank deposit interest for the same period.

Article 34 Upon the expiration of the agency or firm commitment underwriting period for a public issuance of stocks, the issuer shall report the stock issuance situation to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record within the prescribed period.

## Chapter III Securities Trading

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 35 Securities bought and sold by parties to securities transactions must be securities that have been lawfully issued and delivered.

Securities that have not been lawfully issued shall not be bought or sold.

Article 36 Where the Company Law of the People's Republic of China or other laws impose restrictions on the transfer period of lawfully issued securities, such securities shall not be transferred within the restricted period.

Shareholders holding more than five percent of the shares of a listed company, actual controllers, directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other shareholders holding shares issued before the issuer's initial public offering or shares issued by a listed company to specific objects, when transferring their shares of the company, shall not violate the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the securities regulatory authority under the State Council regarding holding periods, selling times, selling quantities, selling methods, information disclosure, etc., and shall comply with the business rules of the stock exchange.

Article 37 Publicly issued securities shall be listed and traded on a lawfully established stock exchange or traded on other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council.

Non-publicly issued securities may be transferred on a stock exchange, other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council, or regional equity markets established in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Article 38 The listing and trading of securities on a stock exchange shall adopt an open centralized trading method or other methods approved by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 39 Securities bought and sold by parties to securities transactions may be in paper form or other forms prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 40 Personnel of securities trading venues, securities companies, and securities registration and clearing institutions, staff of securities regulatory authorities, and other persons prohibited by laws or administrative regulations from participating in stock trading shall not, during their term of office or statutory period, directly or under an alias or in the name of another person, hold or trade stocks or other equity securities, nor shall they accept stocks or other equity securities given by others.

When any person becomes a person listed in the preceding paragraph, any stocks or other equity securities they originally held must be transferred in accordance with the law.

Personnel of securities companies implementing equity incentive plans or employee stock ownership plans may hold and sell stocks or other equity securities of their company in accordance with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 41 Securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, securities service institutions, and their personnel shall keep investors' information confidential in accordance with the law and shall not illegally buy, sell, provide, or disclose investors' information.

Securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, securities service institutions, and their personnel shall not disclose any trade secrets they have learned.

Article 42 Securities service institutions and their personnel that issue audit reports or legal opinions and other documents for securities issuance shall not buy or sell such securities during the underwriting period and within six months after its expiration.

In addition to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, securities service institutions and their personnel that issue audit reports or legal opinions and other documents for the issuer and its controlling shareholders, actual controllers, or acquirers, or parties to major asset transactions, shall not buy or sell such securities from the date of accepting the engagement until five days after the above documents are made public. If the actual commencement of the relevant work is earlier than the date of accepting the engagement, they shall not buy or sell such securities from the actual commencement date of the relevant work until five days after the above documents are made public.

Article 43 Fees for securities transactions must be reasonable, and the fee items, fee standards, and administrative measures shall be made public.**Article 44** Shareholders holding more than five percent of the shares of a listed company or a company whose shares are traded on other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council, as well as directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel, who sell the company's shares or other equity securities they hold within six months after purchase, or repurchase them within six months after sale, shall have the resulting profits belong to the company. The company's board of directors shall recover such profits. However, this does not apply to securities companies that hold more than five percent of the shares due to purchasing unsold shares from underwriting, or other circumstances specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

The shares or other equity securities held by the directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and natural-person shareholders referred to in the preceding paragraph include those held by their spouses, parents, children, and those held through accounts of others.

If the board of directors fails to act in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph, shareholders have the right to demand that the board of directors execute within thirty days. If the board of directors fails to do so within the specified period, shareholders have the right to file a lawsuit directly with the people's court in their own name for the benefit of the company.

If the board of directors fails to act in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph, the responsible directors shall bear joint and several liability according to law.

**Article 45** Programmatic trading conducted through automatically generated or executed trading instructions by computer programs shall comply with the regulations of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council, be reported to the stock exchange, and shall not affect the system security or normal trading order of the stock exchange.

### Section 2: Listing of Securities

**Article 46** To apply for the listing and trading of securities, an application shall be submitted to the stock exchange, which shall review and approve it according to law, and both parties shall sign a listing agreement.

The stock exchange shall arrange for the listing and trading of government bonds based on the decision of the department authorized by the State Council.

**Article 47** To apply for the listing and trading of securities, the applicant must meet the listing conditions stipulated in the stock exchange's listing rules.

The listing conditions stipulated in the stock exchange's listing rules shall require the issuer's operating history, financial condition, minimum public offering ratio, corporate governance, and integrity record.

**Article 48** For listed and traded securities that meet the delisting conditions stipulated by the stock exchange, the stock exchange shall terminate their listing and trading in accordance with its business rules.

If the stock exchange decides to terminate the listing and trading of securities, it shall promptly announce the decision and report it to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council for record.

**Article 49** If a party disagrees with a stock exchange's decision not to list or to terminate the listing of securities, it may apply for review to the review body established by the stock exchange.

### Section 3: Prohibited Trading Activities

**Article 50** It is prohibited for insiders of inside information concerning securities trading and persons who illegally obtain inside information to use such inside information to engage in securities trading activities.

**Article 51** Insiders of inside information concerning securities trading include:

(1) The issuer and its directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(2) Shareholders holding more than five percent of the company's shares and their directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel; the company's actual controllers and their directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(3) Companies controlled or actually controlled by the issuer and their directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(4) Personnel who can obtain relevant inside information about the company due to their positions in the company or business dealings with the company;
(5) Acquirers of listed companies or parties to major asset transactions, and their controlling shareholders, actual controllers, directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(6) Relevant personnel of securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and settlement institutions, and securities service institutions who can obtain inside information due to their duties or work;
(7) Staff of the securities regulatory authority who can obtain inside information due to their responsibilities or work;
(8) Staff of relevant competent authorities and regulatory agencies who can obtain inside information due to their statutory duties in managing the issuance, trading of securities, or the listed company and its acquisitions and major asset transactions;
(9) Other persons specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council who can obtain inside information.

**Article 52** Inside information refers to undisclosed information that, in securities trading activities, involves the issuer's operations, finances, or may have a significant impact on the market price of the issuer's securities.

The major events listed in the second paragraph of Article 80 and the second paragraph of Article 81 of this Law constitute inside information.

**Article 53** Insiders of inside information concerning securities trading and persons who illegally obtain inside information shall not buy or sell the company's securities, disclose the information, or advise others to buy or sell such securities before the inside information is made public.

If natural persons, legal persons, or unincorporated organizations holding, or jointly holding through agreements or other arrangements, more than five percent of the shares of a listed company acquire shares of the listed company, and this Law provides otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Those who engage in insider trading causing losses to investors shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 54** It is prohibited for employees of securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, securities service institutions, and other financial institutions, as well as staff of relevant regulatory authorities or industry associations, to use other undisclosed information obtained through their positions, other than inside information, to engage in securities trading activities related to such information in violation of regulations, or to explicitly or implicitly suggest others to engage in related trading activities.
Those who trade using undisclosed information causing losses to investors shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 55** It is prohibited for any person to manipulate the securities market by the following means, affecting or intending to affect the trading price or volume of securities:

(1) Concentrating capital advantages, shareholding advantages, or information advantages, alone or through collusion, to jointly or continuously buy or sell;
(2) Colluding with others to conduct securities transactions with each other at prearranged times, prices, and methods;
(3) Conducting securities transactions between accounts under their actual control;
(4) Frequently or substantially placing and canceling orders without the intention of executing trades;
(5) Inducing investors to conduct securities transactions using false or uncertain major information;
(6) Publicly making evaluations, forecasts, or investment recommendations about securities or issuers, and then conducting reverse securities transactions;
(7) Manipulating the securities market through activities in other related markets;
(8) Other means of manipulating the securities market.

Those who manipulate the securities market causing losses to investors shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 56** It is prohibited for any unit or individual to fabricate or disseminate false or misleading information, disrupting the securities market.

It is prohibited for securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, securities service institutions and their employees, the securities industry association, the securities regulatory authority and its staff to make false statements or provide misleading information in securities trading activities.
All media disseminating securities market information must be truthful and objective, and misleading information is prohibited. Media and their staff reporting on securities market information shall not engage in securities trading that conflicts with their professional responsibilities.

Those who fabricate or disseminate false or misleading information, disrupting the securities market and causing losses to investors, shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 57** It is prohibited for securities companies and their employees to engage in the following acts that harm the interests of clients:

(1) Buying or selling securities for clients contrary to their instructions;
(2) Failing to provide clients with trade confirmation documents within the specified time;
(3) Buying or selling securities for clients without authorization, or buying or selling securities in the name of clients without their instructions;
(4) Inducing clients to make unnecessary securities transactions to earn commission income;
(5) Other acts that violate the true intentions of clients and harm their interests.

Those who violate the preceding paragraph causing losses to clients shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 58** No unit or individual may, in violation of regulations, lend their own securities account or borrow another person's securities account to engage in securities trading.

**Article 59** Channels for funds to enter the market shall be expanded according to law, and the illegal inflow of funds into the stock market is prohibited.

Investors are prohibited from illegally using fiscal funds or bank credit funds to buy or sell securities.

**Article 60** Wholly state-owned enterprises, wholly state-owned companies, and state-controlled companies buying or selling listed stocks must comply with relevant state regulations.

**Article 61** Securities trading venues, securities companies, securities registration and clearing institutions, securities service institutions, and their employees shall promptly report prohibited trading activities discovered in securities trading to the securities regulatory authority.
## Chapter 4: Takeovers of Listed Companies

**Article 62** Investors may acquire listed companies through tender offers, negotiated acquisitions, and other lawful methods.

**Article 63** When an investor, through securities trading on a stock exchange, holds, or jointly holds through agreements or other arrangements with others, five percent of the issued voting shares of a listed company, the investor shall, within three days from the date of such fact, submit a written report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the stock exchange, notify the listed company, and make a public announcement. During the above period, the investor shall not buy or sell any more shares of the listed company, except in circumstances specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

After an investor holds, or jointly holds through agreements or other arrangements with others, five percent of the issued voting shares of a listed company, each time the proportion of such voting shares held increases or decreases by five percent, the investor shall report and announce in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph. From the date of such fact until three days after the announcement, the investor shall not buy or sell any more shares of the listed company, except in circumstances specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

After an investor holds, or jointly holds through agreements or other arrangements with others, five percent of the issued voting shares of a listed company, each time the proportion of such voting shares held increases or decreases by one percent, the investor shall notify the listed company and make a public announcement on the day following the date of such fact.

If voting shares of a listed company are purchased in violation of the provisions of the first or second paragraph, the voting rights of the shares exceeding the prescribed proportion shall not be exercised for thirty-six months after the purchase.

**Article 64** The announcement made in accordance with the preceding article shall include the following:

(1) The name and domicile of the shareholder;
(2) The name and quantity of the shares held;
(3) The date on which the shareholding reached the statutory proportion or the change in shareholding reached the statutory proportion, and the source of funds for the increased shares;
(4) The time and method of changes in the voting shares held in the listed company.

**Article 65** When an investor, through securities trading on a stock exchange, holds, or jointly holds through agreements or other arrangements with others, thirty percent of the issued voting shares of a listed company and intends to continue the acquisition, the investor shall make a tender offer to all shareholders of the listed company to acquire all or part of the shares of the listed company according to law.

If a tender offer is made to acquire part of the shares of a listed company, the offer shall stipulate that if the number of shares the shareholders of the acquired company commit to sell exceeds the predetermined number of shares to be acquired, the acquirer shall acquire the shares on a pro-rata basis.

**Article 66** When making a tender offer in accordance with the preceding article, the acquirer must publish a report on the acquisition of the listed company, which shall specify the following:

(1) The name and domicile of the acquirer;
(2) The acquirer's decision regarding the acquisition;
(3) The name of the listed company being acquired;
(4) The purpose of the acquisition;
(5) The detailed name of the shares to be acquired and the predetermined number of shares to be acquired;
(6) The acquisition period and price;
(7) The amount of funds required for the acquisition and the guarantee of funds;
(8) The proportion of shares of the acquired company held by the acquirer at the time of publishing the acquisition report to the total issued shares of the company.

**Article 67** The acquisition period stipulated in a tender offer shall not be less than thirty days and shall not exceed sixty days.

**Article 68** During the commitment period specified in the tender offer, the acquirer shall not revoke the tender offer. If the acquirer needs to modify the tender offer, it shall promptly announce the specific modifications, and the modifications shall not include the following:

(1) Lowering the acquisition price;
(2) Reducing the predetermined number of shares to be acquired;
(3) Shortening the acquisition period;
(4) Other circumstances specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

**Article 69** The various acquisition conditions proposed in a tender offer shall apply to all shareholders of the acquired company.

If a listed company has issued different classes of shares, the acquirer may propose different acquisition conditions for different classes of shares.

**Article 70** When adopting the method of a tender offer, the acquirer shall not sell the shares of the acquired company during the acquisition period, nor shall it purchase the shares of the acquired company in forms other than those stipulated in the offer or under conditions exceeding those of the offer.

**Article 71** When adopting the method of a negotiated acquisition, the acquirer may transfer shares with the shareholders of the acquired company through negotiation in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

When acquiring a listed company through negotiation, after reaching an agreement, the acquirer must submit a written report on the acquisition agreement to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the stock exchange within three days, and make a public announcement.

The acquisition agreement shall not be performed before the announcement.

**Article 72** When adopting the method of a negotiated acquisition, both parties to the agreement may temporarily entrust a securities registration and settlement institution to hold the shares to be transferred under the agreement and deposit the funds in a designated bank.
**Article 73** When adopting the method of a negotiated acquisition, if the acquirer acquires, or jointly acquires through agreements or other arrangements with others, thirty percent of the issued voting shares of a listed company and intends to continue the acquisition, the acquirer shall make a tender offer to all shareholders of the listed company to acquire all or part of the shares of the listed company according to law, unless exempted from making a tender offer according to the regulations of the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.

If an acquirer acquires shares of a listed company by way of a tender offer in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall comply with the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 65 and Articles 66 to 70 of this Law.

**Article 74** Upon the expiration of the acquisition period, if the distribution of shares of the acquired company does not meet the listing requirements stipulated by the stock exchange, the stock exchange shall terminate the listing and trading of the shares of the listed company according to law; the remaining shareholders who still hold shares of the acquired company have the right to sell their shares to the acquirer on the same terms as the tender offer, and the acquirer shall purchase them.

After the completion of the acquisition, if the acquired company no longer meets the conditions of a company limited by shares, it shall change its corporate form according to law.

**Article 75** In the acquisition of a listed company, the shares of the acquired listed company held by the acquirer shall not be transferred within eighteen months after the completion of the acquisition.

**Article 76** After the completion of the acquisition, if the acquirer merges with the acquired company and dissolves the acquired company, the original shares of the dissolved company shall be replaced by the acquirer according to law.

After the completion of the acquisition, the acquirer shall report the acquisition situation to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the stock exchange within fifteen days, and make a public announcement.

**Article 77** The securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall formulate specific measures for the acquisition of listed companies in accordance with this Law.

If a listed company is split or merged by another company, it shall report to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and make a public announcement.

## Chapter 5: Information Disclosure

**Article 78** Issuers and other information disclosure obligors specified by laws, administrative regulations, and the securities regulatory authority under the State Council shall fulfill their information disclosure obligations promptly and according to law.

The information disclosed by information disclosure obligors shall be truthful, accurate, complete, clear, concise, and easy to understand, and shall not contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions.

If securities are publicly offered and traded both domestically and abroad, the information disclosed abroad by the information disclosure obligor shall be disclosed domestically at the same time.

**Article 79** Listed companies, companies whose corporate bonds are listed and traded, and companies whose shares are traded on other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council shall prepare periodic reports in accordance with the content and format prescribed by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the stock exchange, and shall submit and announce them according to the following provisions:

(1) Within four months after the end of each fiscal year, submit and announce the annual report, wherein the annual financial accounting report shall be audited by an accounting firm that meets the requirements of this Law;
(2) Within two months after the end of the first half of each fiscal year, submit and announce the interim report.

**Article 80** When a major event occurs that may have a significant impact on the trading price of the shares of a listed company or a company whose shares are traded on other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council, and investors are not yet aware of it, the company shall immediately submit a temporary report on the circumstances of the major event to the securities regulatory authority under the State Council and the stock exchange, and make a public announcement, explaining the cause of the event, its current status, and the possible legal consequences.

The term "major events" as used in the preceding paragraph includes:

(1) Major changes in the company's business policies and scope;
(2) Major investment activities of the company, such as purchasing or selling major assets exceeding thirty percent of the company's total assets within one year, or mortgaging, pledging, selling, or scrapping major assets used in the company's business operations exceeding thirty percent of such assets at one time;
(3) The company entering into important contracts, providing major guarantees, or engaging in related transactions that may have a significant impact on the company's assets, liabilities, equity, and operating results;
(4) The company incurring major debts or defaulting on major debts due;
(5) The company incurring major losses or significant losses;
(6) Major changes in the external conditions of the company's production and operation;
(7) Changes in the company's directors, more than one-third of its supervisors, or managers, or the chairman of the board or manager being unable to perform their duties;
(8) Significant changes in the shareholding of shareholders holding more than five percent of the company's shares or the actual controller's control over the company, or significant changes in the situation where the actual controller and other enterprises under its control engage in the same or similar business as the company;
(9) Plans for the distribution of dividends or capital increase, important changes in the company's equity structure, decisions on capital reduction, merger, division, dissolution, or application for bankruptcy, or entering bankruptcy proceedings or being ordered to close down according to law;
(10) Major litigation or arbitration involving the company, or resolutions of the shareholders' meeting or board of directors being revoked or declared invalid according to law;
(11) The company being placed on file for investigation due to suspected crimes, or the controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of the company being subjected to compulsory measures due to suspected crimes;
(12) Other matters specified by the securities regulatory authority under the State Council.If the controlling shareholder or actual controller of a company has a significant impact on the occurrence or progress of a major event, they shall promptly inform the company in writing of the relevant information they are aware of and cooperate with the company in fulfilling its information disclosure obligations.

**Article 81** When a major event occurs that may have a significant impact on the trading price of a company's listed bonds, and before investors become aware of it, the company shall immediately submit a temporary report on the circumstances of the major event to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and the securities trading venue, and make an announcement, explaining the cause of the event, its current status, and the possible legal consequences.

The term "major events" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph includes:

(I) Significant changes in the company's equity structure or production and operation conditions;
(II) Changes in the credit rating of the company's bonds;
(III) Major asset mortgages, pledges, sales, transfers, or write-offs of the company;
(IV) The company's failure to repay due debts;
(V) New borrowings or external guarantees provided by the company exceeding 20% of its net assets at the end of the previous year;
(VI) The company's waiver of claims or property exceeding 10% of its net assets at the end of the previous year;
(VII) The company incurring major losses exceeding 10% of its net assets at the end of the previous year;
(VIII) The company distributing dividends, making decisions on capital reduction, merger, division, dissolution, or applying for bankruptcy, or entering bankruptcy proceedings according to law, or being ordered to close;
(IX) Major litigation or arbitration involving the company;
(X) The company being placed on file for investigation due to suspected crimes, or the company's controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, supervisors, or senior management being subjected to compulsory measures according to law due to suspected crimes;
(XI) Other matters stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 82** The directors and senior management of an issuer shall sign written confirmation opinions on the securities issuance documents and periodic reports.

The board of supervisors of an issuer shall review the securities issuance documents and periodic reports prepared by the board of directors and provide written review opinions. Supervisors shall sign written confirmation opinions.

The directors, supervisors, and senior management of an issuer shall ensure that the issuer discloses information in a timely and fair manner, and that the disclosed information is truthful, accurate, and complete.

If directors, supervisors, or senior management cannot guarantee the truthfulness, accuracy, or completeness of the content of the securities issuance documents and periodic reports, or have objections, they shall express their opinions and state the reasons in the written confirmation opinions, and the issuer shall disclose them. If the issuer fails to disclose, the directors, supervisors, or senior management may directly apply for disclosure.

**Article 83** Information disclosed by an information disclosure obligor shall be disclosed to all investors simultaneously and shall not be leaked to any unit or individual in advance, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

No unit or individual may illegally require an information disclosure obligor to provide information that is required to be disclosed by law but has not yet been disclosed. Any unit or individual who obtains the aforementioned information in advance shall keep it confidential until it is disclosed according to law.

**Article 84** In addition to information required to be disclosed by law, an information disclosure obligor may voluntarily disclose information relevant to investors' value judgments and investment decisions, but such disclosure shall not conflict with information disclosed according to law, nor mislead investors.

If an issuer, its controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, supervisors, senior management, or others make public commitments, they shall disclose them. If failure to fulfill commitments causes losses to investors, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 85** If an information disclosure obligor fails to disclose information as required, or if the announced securities issuance documents, periodic reports, temporary reports, or other information disclosure materials contain false records, misleading statements, or major omissions, causing losses to investors in securities transactions, the information disclosure obligor shall bear compensation liability; the issuer's controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, supervisors, senior management, and other directly responsible persons, as well as the sponsor, the underwriting securities company, and their directly responsible persons, shall bear joint and several liability with the issuer, unless they can prove that they are not at fault.

**Article 86** Information disclosed according to law shall be published on the website of the securities trading venue and in media that meet the conditions stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, and shall also be kept at the company's domicile and the securities trading venue for public inspection.

**Article 87** The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall supervise and manage the information disclosure behavior of information disclosure obligors.

Securities trading venues shall supervise the information disclosure behavior of information disclosure obligors for the securities they organize for trading, and urge them to disclose information timely and accurately according to law.

## Chapter VI Investor Protection

**Article 88** When selling securities or providing services to investors, a securities company shall, in accordance with regulations, fully understand the investor's basic information, property status, financial asset status, investment knowledge and experience, professional capabilities, and other relevant information; truthfully explain the important content of the securities and services, and fully disclose investment risks; and sell or provide securities and services that match the aforementioned status of the investor.

When purchasing securities or accepting services, investors shall provide truthful information as listed in the preceding paragraph in accordance with the explicit requirements of the securities company. If an investor refuses to provide or fails to provide information as required, the securities company shall inform them of the consequences and, in accordance with regulations, refuse to sell securities or provide services to them.

If a securities company violates the provisions of the first paragraph and causes losses to investors, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

**Article 89** Based on factors such as property status, financial asset status, investment knowledge and experience, and professional capabilities, investors can be divided into ordinary investors and professional investors. The standards for professional investors shall be stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

If a dispute arises between an ordinary investor and a securities company, the securities company shall prove that its actions comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, and that there are no misleading, fraudulent, or other such circumstances. If the securities company cannot prove this, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

**Article 90** The board of directors of a listed company, independent directors, shareholders holding more than 1% of the voting shares, or investor protection institutions established in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, or the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council (hereinafter referred to as investor protection institutions) may act as solicitors, either on their own or by entrusting a securities company or securities service institution, to publicly request the shareholders of the listed company to entrust them to attend the shareholders' meeting on their behalf and exercise shareholder rights such as the right to propose and vote.

When soliciting shareholder rights in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the solicitor shall disclose the solicitation documents, and the listed company shall cooperate.

It is prohibited to publicly solicit shareholder rights in a compensated or disguised compensated manner.

If publicly soliciting shareholder rights violates relevant provisions of laws, administrative regulations, or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, causing losses to the listed company or its shareholders, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 91** A listed company shall specify in its articles of association the specific arrangements and decision-making procedures for distributing cash dividends, and legally protect the shareholders' right to asset returns.

If the listed company has after-tax profits for the year remaining after covering losses and withdrawing the statutory common reserve fund, it shall distribute cash dividends in accordance with the provisions of its articles of association.

**Article 92** When publicly issuing corporate bonds, a bondholders' meeting shall be established, and the prospectus shall explain the convening procedures, meeting rules, and other important matters of the bondholders' meeting.

When publicly issuing corporate bonds, the issuer shall engage a bond trustee for the bondholders and enter into a bond trust management agreement. The trustee shall be the underwriting institution for this issuance or another institution recognized by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. The bondholders' meeting may resolve to change the bond trustee. The bond trustee shall act diligently and faithfully, perform its fiduciary duties impartially, and shall not harm the interests of the bondholders.

If the bond issuer fails to redeem the principal and interest of the bonds on time, the bond trustee may, on behalf of all or part of the bondholders, initiate or participate in civil lawsuits or liquidation proceedings in its own name.

**Article 93** If an issuer causes losses to investors due to fraudulent issuance, false statements, or other major illegal acts, the issuer's controlling shareholder, actual controller, or relevant securities company may entrust an investor protection institution to reach an agreement with the affected investors on compensation matters and provide advance compensation. After advance compensation, they may seek recourse from the issuer and other jointly liable persons according to law.

**Article 94** If a dispute arises between an investor and an issuer, securities company, etc., both parties may apply to an investor protection institution for mediation. If an ordinary investor and a securities company have a dispute over securities business, and the ordinary investor requests mediation, the securities company shall not refuse.

An investor protection institution may, according to law, support investors in filing lawsuits with the people's court against acts that harm the interests of investors.

If directors, supervisors, or senior management of an issuer violate laws, administrative regulations, or the company's articles of association while performing their duties, causing losses to the company, or if the issuer's controlling shareholder, actual controller, etc., infringe upon the company's legitimate rights and interests, causing losses to the company, and the investor protection institution holds shares in the company, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court in its own name for the benefit of the company, and the shareholding ratio and holding period are not subject to the restrictions stipulated in the Company Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 95** When investors file securities civil compensation lawsuits such as those for false statements, if the subject matter of the lawsuit is of the same type and one party involves a large number of persons, representatives may be elected according to law to conduct the litigation.

For lawsuits filed in accordance with the preceding paragraph, if there may be many other investors with the same claims, the people's court may issue a public notice explaining the circumstances of the case and notifying investors to register with the people's court within a specified period. The judgments and rulings made by the people's court are binding on the investors who have registered.

An investor protection institution, entrusted by more than fifty investors, may act as a representative to participate in the lawsuit and register with the people's court on behalf of the rights holders confirmed by the securities registration and settlement institution in accordance with the preceding paragraph, unless the investors explicitly state that they do not wish to participate in the lawsuit.

## Chapter VII Securities Trading Venues

**Article 96** Stock exchanges and other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council provide venues and facilities for centralized securities trading, organize and supervise securities trading, exercise self-regulation, register according to law, and obtain legal personality.

The establishment, modification, and dissolution of stock exchanges and other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council shall be decided by the State Council.

The organizational structure, management measures, etc., of other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council shall be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 97** Stock exchanges and other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council may establish different market levels based on factors such as securities types, industry characteristics, and company size.

**Article 98** Regional equity markets established in accordance with the provisions of the State Council provide venues and facilities for the issuance and transfer of non-publicly offered securities. Specific management measures shall be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 99** When performing self-regulatory functions, a stock exchange shall adhere to the principle of prioritizing social public interests and maintain a fair, orderly, and transparent market.

A stock exchange must have articles of association for its establishment. The formulation and modification of a stock exchange's articles of association must be approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 100** A stock exchange must include the words "stock exchange" in its name. No other unit or individual may use the name "stock exchange" or a similar name.

**Article 101** The various fee income that a stock exchange can freely allocate shall first be used to ensure the normal operation of its securities trading venue and facilities and to gradually improve them.

The property accumulation of a membership-based stock exchange belongs to its members, and the rights and interests are jointly enjoyed by the members. During its existence, the property accumulation shall not be distributed to the members.

**Article 102** A membership-based stock exchange shall have a board of directors and a board of supervisors.

A stock exchange shall have one general manager, who shall be appointed or removed by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 103** Persons who fall under the circumstances stipulated in Article 146 of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China or any of the following circumstances shall not serve as the responsible persons of a stock exchange:

(I) Persons who have been removed from their positions as responsible persons of a securities trading venue, securities registration and settlement institution, or as directors, supervisors, or senior management of a securities company due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of removal;
(II) Lawyers, certified public accountants, or professionals of other securities service institutions whose practice certificates have been revoked or whose qualifications have been cancelled due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of revocation or cancellation.

**Article 104** Persons who have been dismissed from a securities trading venue, securities company, securities registration and settlement institution, or securities service institution due to illegal or disciplinary acts, as well as dismissed state organ staff, shall not be recruited as employees of a stock exchange.

**Article 105** To participate in centralized trading in a membership-based stock exchange, one must be a member of the stock exchange. A stock exchange shall not allow non-members to directly participate in the centralized trading of stocks.

**Article 106** Investors shall enter into a securities trading entrustment agreement with a securities company, open an account with the securities company under their real name, and entrust the securities company to buy or sell securities on their behalf by means of writing, telephone, self-service terminals, the internet, etc.

**Article 107** When opening an account for an investor, a securities company shall verify the identity information provided by the investor in accordance with regulations.

A securities company shall not provide an investor's account to others for use.

Investors shall use accounts opened under their real names for trading.

**Article 108** Based on the investor's entrustment, a securities company shall submit a trading declaration in accordance with securities trading rules, participate in centralized trading on the stock exchange floor, and bear the corresponding clearing and settlement responsibilities based on the transaction results. The securities registration and settlement institution shall, based on the transaction results and in accordance with clearing and settlement rules, conduct the clearing and settlement of securities and funds with the securities company, and handle the registration and transfer procedures for the securities company's clients.

**Article 109** A stock exchange shall provide guarantees for organizing fair centralized trading, publish real-time securities trading quotes, and prepare and publish a securities market quotation table on each trading day.

The rights to real-time securities trading quotes are legally enjoyed by the stock exchange. Without the permission of the stock exchange, no unit or individual may publish real-time securities trading quotes.

**Article 110** A listed company may apply to the stock exchange for the suspension or resumption of trading of its listed stocks, but shall not abuse suspension or resumption to harm the legitimate rights and interests of investors.

A stock exchange may, in accordance with the provisions of its business rules, decide on the suspension or resumption of trading of listed stocks.

**Article 111** When a sudden event such as force majeure, an accident, a major technical failure, or a major human error affects the normal conduct of securities trading, in order to maintain the normal order of securities trading and market fairness, a stock exchange may take disposal measures such as technical suspension or temporary market closure in accordance with its business rules, and shall promptly report to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

If a sudden event as specified in the preceding paragraph causes a major abnormality in the results of securities trading, and settling according to the trading results would have a significant impact on the normal order of securities trading and market fairness, the stock exchange may, in accordance with its business rules, take measures such as canceling transactions or notifying the securities registration and settlement institution to postpone settlement, and shall promptly report to and announce to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

A stock exchange shall not bear civil compensation liability for losses caused by measures taken in accordance with this article, unless there is gross negligence.

**Article 112** A stock exchange shall conduct real-time monitoring of securities trading and, as required by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, submit reports on abnormal trading situations.

A stock exchange may, as needed and in accordance with its business rules, restrict the trading of investors in securities accounts with major abnormal trading situations, and promptly report to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 113** A stock exchange shall strengthen risk monitoring of securities trading. If major abnormal fluctuations occur, the stock exchange may take disposal measures such as restricting trading or compulsory suspension in accordance with its business rules, and report to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council; if it seriously affects the stability of the securities market, the stock exchange may take disposal measures such as temporary market closure in accordance with its business rules and make an announcement.

A stock exchange shall not bear civil compensation liability for losses caused by measures taken in accordance with this article, unless there is gross negligence.

**Article 114** A stock exchange shall set aside a certain percentage of the transaction fees, membership fees, and seat fees it collects to establish a risk fund. The risk fund shall be managed by the board of directors of the stock exchange.

The specific percentage for setting aside the risk fund and the usage methods shall be stipulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the finance department of the State Council.

A stock exchange shall deposit the collected risk fund into a dedicated account at a designated bank and shall not use it without authorization.

**Article 115** A stock exchange shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, formulate listing rules, trading rules, member management rules, and other relevant business rules, and submit them to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for approval.

Engaging in securities trading on a stock exchange shall comply with the business rules legally formulated by the stock exchange. Violations of business rules shall result in disciplinary sanctions or other self-regulatory measures imposed by the stock exchange.

**Article 116** When the responsible persons and other employees of a stock exchange perform duties related to securities trading, they shall recuse themselves if there is a conflict of interest with themselves or their relatives.

**Article 117** The results of transactions conducted in accordance with legally formulated trading rules shall not be altered, except as provided in Article 111, paragraph 2 of this Law. The civil liability of those who engage in illegal transactions shall not be exempted; the gains obtained from illegal transactions shall be dealt with in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter VIII Securities Companies

**Article 118** To establish a securities company, the following conditions must be met, and approval from the securities regulatory authority of the State Council is required:

(I) Having articles of association that comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations;
(II) The main shareholders and the actual controller of the company have good financial conditions and integrity records, with no major illegal or disciplinary records in the last three years;
(III) Having registered capital that complies with the provisions of this Law;
(IV) The directors, supervisors, senior management, and employees meet the conditions stipulated in this Law;
(V) Having a sound risk management and internal control system;
(VI) Having qualified business premises, business facilities, and information technology systems;
(VII) Other conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the securities regulatory authority of the State Council as approved by the State Council.Without approval from the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, no entity or individual may conduct securities business activities in the name of a securities company.

Article 119 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall, within six months from the date of receiving an application for the establishment of a securities company, review the application in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures and based on the principle of prudential regulation, and make a decision to approve or disapprove the application, and notify the applicant; if the application is disapproved, reasons shall be stated.

If the application for the establishment of a securities company is approved, the applicant shall, within the prescribed period, apply to the company registration authority for establishment registration and obtain a business license.

A securities company shall, within fifteen days from the date of obtaining the business license, apply to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for a license to conduct securities business. Without obtaining a license to conduct securities business, a securities company shall not engage in securities business.

Article 120 Upon approval by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and obtaining a license to conduct securities business, a securities company may engage in some or all of the following securities businesses:

(1) Securities brokerage;
(2) Securities investment consulting;
(3) Financial advisory services related to securities trading and securities investment activities;
(4) Securities underwriting and sponsorship;
(5) Securities margin trading;
(6) Securities market making;
(7) Securities proprietary trading;
(8) Other securities businesses.

The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall, within three months from the date of receiving an application for the matters specified in the preceding paragraph, review the application in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures, and make a decision to approve or disapprove the application, and notify the applicant; if the application is disapproved, reasons shall be stated.

Where a securities company conducts securities asset management business, it shall comply with the provisions of the Securities Investment Fund Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations.

Except for securities companies, no entity or individual may engage in securities underwriting, securities sponsorship, securities brokerage, or securities margin trading.

A securities company engaging in securities margin trading shall take measures to strictly prevent and control risks, and shall not lend funds or securities to clients in violation of regulations.

Article 121 For a securities company engaging in the businesses specified in items (1) to (3) of the first paragraph of Article 120 of this Law, the minimum registered capital shall be RMB 50 million; for engaging in any one of the businesses specified in items (4) to (8), the minimum registered capital shall be RMB 100 million; for engaging in two or more of the businesses specified in items (4) to (8), the minimum registered capital shall be RMB 500 million. The registered capital of a securities company shall be paid-in capital.

The securities regulatory authority of the State Council may adjust the minimum registered capital based on the principle of prudential regulation and the risk level of each business, but shall not reduce it below the limits specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 122 A securities company shall obtain approval from the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for changing its scope of securities business, changing its major shareholders or actual controller, merger, division, suspension of business, dissolution, or bankruptcy.

Article 123 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall prescribe provisions on the net capital and other risk control indicators of securities companies.

Except for providing margin trading to its clients in accordance with regulations, a securities company shall not provide financing or guarantees to its shareholders or their affiliates.

Article 124 The directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a securities company shall be honest, have good conduct, be familiar with securities laws and administrative regulations, and possess the operational management capabilities necessary to perform their duties. The appointment or removal of directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a securities company shall be reported to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for record.

Persons who fall under the circumstances specified in Article 146 of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China or any of the following circumstances shall not serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a securities company:

(1) Persons who have been removed from their positions as responsible persons of a securities trading venue, securities registration and clearing institution, or directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a securities company due to illegal or disciplinary acts, where less than five years have elapsed since the date of removal;
(2) Lawyers, certified public accountants, or other professionals of securities service institutions whose practice certificates have been revoked or whose qualifications have been cancelled due to illegal or disciplinary acts, where less than five years have elapsed since the date of revocation or cancellation.

Article 125 Personnel engaged in securities business of a securities company shall have good conduct and possess the professional competence necessary for engaging in securities business.

Personnel who have been dismissed from a securities trading venue, securities company, securities registration and clearing institution, or securities service institution due to illegal or disciplinary acts, and dismissed state organ personnel, shall not be recruited as employees of a securities company.

State organ personnel and other persons prohibited by laws and administrative regulations from holding concurrent positions in companies shall not hold concurrent positions in a securities company.

Article 126 The state establishes a securities investor protection fund. The securities investor protection fund shall be composed of funds contributed by securities companies and other funds raised in accordance with the law. The scale of the fund and the specific measures for its raising, management, and use shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 127 A securities company shall set aside a transaction risk reserve from its annual business revenue to cover losses from securities operations. The specific proportion for setting aside the reserve shall be prescribed by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the finance department of the State Council.

Article 128 A securities company shall establish a sound internal control system and adopt effective segregation measures to prevent conflicts of interest between the company and its clients, and among different clients.

A securities company must handle its securities brokerage business, securities underwriting business, securities proprietary trading business, securities market making business, and securities asset management business separately, and shall not commingle operations.

Article 129 The proprietary trading business of a securities company must be conducted in its own name, and shall not be conducted in the name of another person or in an individual's name.

The proprietary trading business of a securities company must use its own funds and funds raised in accordance with the law.

A securities company shall not lend its proprietary trading account to others for use.

Article 130 A securities company shall operate prudently in accordance with the law, be diligent and responsible, and act in good faith and with integrity.

The business activities of a securities company shall be commensurate with its governance structure, internal control, compliance management, risk management, risk control indicators, and the composition of its personnel, and shall meet the requirements of prudential regulation and protection of investors' legitimate rights and interests.

A securities company shall enjoy the right to operate independently in accordance with the law, and its lawful operations shall not be interfered with.

Article 131 The transaction settlement funds of a securities company's clients shall be deposited in commercial banks, and managed in separate accounts opened in the name of each client.

A securities company shall not include the transaction settlement funds and securities of its clients into its own property. No entity or individual is allowed to misappropriate the transaction settlement funds and securities of clients in any form. In the event of bankruptcy or liquidation of a securities company, the transaction settlement funds and securities of clients shall not be part of its bankruptcy or liquidation property. Unless due to the client's own debts or other circumstances provided by law, the transaction settlement funds and securities of clients shall not be sealed up, frozen, deducted, or enforced.

Article 132 When handling brokerage business, a securities company shall prepare uniformly formulated securities purchase and sale entrustment forms for use by clients. If other forms of entrustment are adopted, records of the entrustment must be made.

The entrustment records of a client's securities purchase and sale orders, whether executed or not, shall be kept by the securities company for the prescribed period.

Article 133 When accepting an entrustment for securities purchase and sale, a securities company shall, based on the securities name, purchase and sale quantity, pricing method, price range, etc., specified in the entrustment form, execute the purchase and sale on behalf of the client in accordance with trading rules, and make truthful transaction records; after the transaction is executed, a transaction confirmation report shall be prepared and delivered to the client in accordance with regulations.

The statements confirming transaction activities and their results in securities trading must be truthful, ensuring that the book balance of securities is consistent with the securities actually held.

Article 134 When handling brokerage business, a securities company shall not accept a client's discretionary authority to decide on securities purchase and sale, select the type of securities, or determine the quantity or price of purchase and sale.

A securities company shall not allow others to directly participate in centralized securities trading in the name of the securities company.

Article 135 A securities company shall not make promises to clients regarding the profits from securities purchase and sale or compensation for losses from securities purchase and sale.

Article 136 If an employee of a securities company, in the course of securities trading activities, executes instructions of the securities company they belong to or violates trading rules by abusing their position, the securities company they belong to shall bear full responsibility.

Employees of a securities company shall not privately accept client entrustments to purchase or sell securities.

Article 137 A securities company shall establish a client information inquiry system to ensure that clients can inquire about their account information, entrustment records, transaction records, and other important information related to receiving services or purchasing products.

A securities company shall properly keep client account opening materials, entrustment records, transaction records, and various information related to internal management and business operations. No person shall conceal, forge, alter, or destroy such information. The retention period for the aforementioned information shall not be less than twenty years.

Article 138 A securities company shall, in accordance with regulations, submit business, financial, and other operational management information and materials to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. The securities regulatory authority of the State Council has the right to require a securities company and its major shareholders and actual controller to provide relevant information and materials within a specified period.

The information and materials submitted or provided by a securities company and its major shareholders and actual controller to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council must be truthful, accurate, and complete.

Article 139 When the securities regulatory authority of the State Council deems it necessary, it may entrust an accounting firm or an asset appraisal institution to audit or evaluate the financial condition, internal control status, and asset value of a securities company. The specific measures shall be formulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the relevant competent authorities.

Article 140 If the governance structure, compliance management, or risk control indicators of a securities company do not meet the requirements, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall order it to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, or if its conduct seriously endangers the sound operation of the securities company or damages the legitimate rights and interests of clients, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may, depending on the circumstances, take the following measures against it:

(1) Restrict business activities, order the suspension of part of its business, or cease approving new business;
(2) Restrict the distribution of dividends, restrict the payment of remuneration and provision of benefits to directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(3) Restrict the transfer of property or the creation of other rights over property;
(4) Order the replacement of directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel, or restrict their rights;
(5) Revoke relevant business permits;
(6) Designate responsible directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel as unsuitable persons;
(7) Order responsible shareholders to transfer their equity, or restrict responsible shareholders from exercising their shareholder rights.

After a securities company has rectified the issues, it shall submit a report to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. If the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, upon inspection, finds that the governance structure, compliance management, and risk control indicators meet the requirements, it shall lift the relevant restrictive measures specified in the preceding paragraph within three days from the date of completion of the inspection.

Article 141 If a shareholder of a securities company makes false capital contributions or withdraws capital contributions, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall order it to make corrections within a specified period, and may also order it to transfer the equity it holds in the securities company.

Before the shareholder specified in the preceding paragraph corrects the illegal act and transfers the equity it holds in the securities company as required, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may restrict its shareholder rights.

Article 142 If directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a securities company fail to be diligent and responsible, resulting in major illegal or irregular acts or major risks in the securities company, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may order the securities company to replace them.

Article 143 If a securities company operates illegally or encounters major risks, seriously endangering the order of the securities market and harming the interests of investors, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may take regulatory measures against the securities company, such as ordering a suspension of business for rectification, designating another institution for trusteeship, taking over, or revoking its license.

Article 144 During the period when a securities company is ordered to suspend business for rectification, is legally designated for trusteeship, takeover, or liquidation, or when major risks arise, with the approval of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, the following measures may be taken against the directly responsible directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons of the securities company:

(1) Notify the exit and entry administration authority to prevent them from leaving the country in accordance with the law;
(2) Apply to a judicial authority to prohibit them from transferring, assigning, or otherwise disposing of property, or creating other rights over property.

## Chapter 9 Securities Registration and Clearing Institutions

Article 145 A securities registration and clearing institution provides centralized registration, depository, and clearing services for securities trading, operates on a non-profit basis, registers in accordance with the law, and obtains legal person status.

The establishment of a securities registration and clearing institution must be approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 146 To establish a securities registration and clearing institution, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Having own funds of not less than RMB 200 million;
(2) Having the premises and facilities necessary for securities registration, depository, and clearing services;
(3) Other conditions prescribed by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

The name of a securities registration and clearing institution shall include the words "securities registration and clearing."

Article 147 A securities registration and clearing institution performs the following functions:

(1) Establishment of securities accounts and settlement accounts;
(2) Depository and transfer of securities;
(3) Registration of the register of securities holders;
(4) Clearing and settlement of securities transactions;
(5) Distribution of securities rights and interests on behalf of the issuer;
(6) Handling inquiries and information services related to the above businesses;
(7) Other businesses approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 148 The registration and clearing of securities traded on a stock exchange or other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council shall adopt a nationally centralized and unified operation method.

For securities other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, their registration and clearing may be entrusted to a securities registration and clearing institution or other institutions lawfully engaged in securities registration and clearing business.

Article 149 A securities registration and clearing institution shall formulate its articles of association and business rules in accordance with the law, which shall be approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. Participants in securities registration and clearing business shall comply with the business rules formulated by the securities registration and clearing institution.

Article 150 Securities traded on a stock exchange or other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council shall be fully deposited with a securities registration and clearing institution.

A securities registration and clearing institution shall not misappropriate clients' securities.

Article 151 A securities registration and clearing institution shall provide the register of securities holders and relevant materials to the securities issuer.

A securities registration and clearing institution shall, based on the results of securities registration and clearing, confirm the fact that a securities holder holds securities, and provide registration materials of the securities holder.

A securities registration and clearing institution shall ensure that the register of securities holders and registration and transfer records are truthful, accurate, and complete, and shall not conceal, forge, alter, or destroy them.

Article 152 A securities registration and clearing institution shall take the following measures to ensure the normal conduct of its business:

(1) Possess necessary service equipment and comprehensive data security protection measures;
(2) Establish comprehensive management systems for business, finance, and security prevention;
(3) Establish a comprehensive risk management system.

Article 153 A securities registration and clearing institution shall properly keep the original vouchers and relevant documents and materials for registration, depository, and clearing. The retention period shall not be less than twenty years.

Article 154 A securities registration and clearing institution shall establish a securities settlement risk fund to cover or compensate for losses incurred by the securities registration and clearing institution due to default in settlement, technical failures, operational errors, or force majeure.

The securities settlement risk fund shall be set aside from the business revenue and income of the securities registration and clearing institution, and may also be contributed by settlement participants in a certain proportion based on the volume of securities trading business.

The measures for raising and managing the securities settlement risk fund shall be prescribed by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the finance department of the State Council.

Article 155 The securities settlement risk fund shall be deposited in a special account with a designated bank and managed exclusively for its purpose.

After compensating from the securities settlement risk fund, the securities registration and clearing institution shall seek recourse from the responsible parties.

Article 156 The dissolution of a securities registration and clearing institution shall be approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 157 An investor entrusting a securities company for securities trading shall apply through the securities company to open a securities account with a securities registration and clearing institution. The securities registration and clearing institution shall open securities accounts for investors in accordance with regulations.

An investor applying to open an account shall hold legal documents proving the identity of a citizen, legal person, or partnership of the People's Republic of China, unless otherwise provided by the state.

Article 158 When a securities registration and clearing institution acts as a central counterparty to provide securities settlement services, it serves as the common clearing and settlement counterparty for settlement participants, conducts net settlement, and provides centralized performance guarantee for securities transactions.

When a securities registration and clearing institution provides net settlement services for securities transactions, it shall require settlement participants to deliver securities and funds in full based on the principle of delivery versus payment, and provide settlement guarantees.

Before settlement is completed, no person may use the securities, funds, or collateral used for settlement.

If a settlement participant fails to perform its settlement obligations on time, the securities registration and clearing institution has the right to dispose of the property specified in the preceding paragraph in accordance with its business rules.

Article 159 All types of settlement funds and securities collected by a securities registration and clearing institution in accordance with its business rules must be deposited in dedicated clearing and settlement accounts, and may only be used for the clearing and settlement of executed securities transactions in accordance with the business rules, and shall not be subject to enforcement.

## Chapter 10 Securities Service Institutions

Article 160 Accounting firms, law firms, and securities service institutions engaged in securities investment consulting, asset appraisal, credit rating, financial advisory, and information technology system services shall be diligent and responsible, fulfill their duties, and provide services for securities trading and related activities in accordance with relevant business rules.

Engaging in securities investment consulting services shall be subject to approval by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council; without approval, services shall not be provided for securities trading and related activities. Engaging in other securities service businesses shall be reported to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and the relevant competent authority of the State Council for record.

Article 161 Securities investment consulting institutions and their employees shall not engage in the following acts when providing securities service business:

(1) Acting as an agent for clients in securities investment;
(2) Agreeing with clients to share securities investment profits or bear securities investment losses;(3) Buying or selling securities for which this securities investment advisory institution provides services;

(4) Other acts prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

Whoever commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph and causes losses to investors shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 162 Securities service institutions shall properly keep client entrustment documents, verification and validation materials, working papers, and information and materials related to quality control, internal management, and business operations. No person may disclose, conceal, forge, alter, or destroy them. The retention period for the aforesaid information and materials shall be no less than ten years, commencing from the date of the conclusion of the business engagement.

Article 163 When securities service institutions produce or issue documents such as audit reports and other assurance reports, asset appraisal reports, financial advisory reports, credit rating reports, or legal opinions for securities business activities such as the issuance, listing, and trading of securities, they shall act diligently and responsibly, and shall verify and validate the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the content of the documents and materials on which they rely. If the documents they produce or issue contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, causing losses to others, they shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the client, unless they can prove that they are not at fault.

## Chapter XI Securities Industry Association

Article 164 The Securities Industry Association is a self-regulatory organization of the securities industry and a social organization with legal person status.

Securities companies shall join the Securities Industry Association.

The authority of the Securities Industry Association is the general meeting of all its members.

Article 165 The articles of association of the Securities Industry Association shall be formulated by the general meeting of members and filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for record.

Article 166 The Securities Industry Association shall perform the following duties:

(1) Educate and organize its members and their practitioners to comply with securities laws and administrative regulations, organize and carry out the building of integrity in the securities industry, and urge the securities industry to fulfill its social responsibilities;

(2) Protect the legitimate rights and interests of its members in accordance with the law, and reflect the suggestions and demands of its members to the securities regulatory authority;

(3) Urge its members to carry out investor education and protection activities, and safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of investors;

(4) Formulate and implement self-regulatory rules for the securities industry, supervise and inspect the conduct of its members and their practitioners, and impose disciplinary sanctions or implement other self-regulatory management measures in accordance with the provisions for violations of laws, administrative regulations, self-regulatory rules, or the association's articles of association;

(5) Formulate business standards for the securities industry and organize professional training for practitioners;

(6) Organize its members to conduct research on the development, operation, and related content of the securities industry, collect, organize, and publish securities-related information, provide member services, organize industry exchanges, and guide industry innovation and development;

(7) Mediate securities business disputes between members, and between members and their clients;

(8) Other duties as stipulated in the articles of association of the Securities Industry Association.

Article 167 The Securities Industry Association shall have a council. Members of the council shall be elected in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association.

## Chapter XII Securities Regulatory Authority

Article 168 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall exercise supervision and administration over the securities market in accordance with the law, maintain the openness, fairness, and impartiality of the securities market, prevent systemic risks, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of investors, and promote the healthy development of the securities market.

Article 169 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall perform the following duties in exercising supervision and administration over the securities market:

(1) Formulate rules and regulations concerning the supervision and administration of the securities market in accordance with the law, and conduct examination, approval, registration, and filing in accordance with the law;

(2) Supervise and administer the issuance, listing, trading, registration, depository, and settlement of securities in accordance with the law;

(3) Supervise and administer the securities business activities of securities issuers, securities companies, securities service institutions, securities trading venues, and securities registration and settlement institutions in accordance with the law;

(4) Formulate codes of conduct for persons engaged in securities business in accordance with the law, and supervise their implementation;

(5) Supervise and inspect the information disclosure of securities issuance, listing, and trading in accordance with the law;

(6) Guide and supervise the self-regulatory management activities of the Securities Industry Association in accordance with the law;

(7) Monitor, prevent, and handle securities market risks in accordance with the law;

(8) Conduct investor education in accordance with the law;

(9) Investigate and deal with securities violations in accordance with the law;

(10) Other duties as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 170 When performing its duties in accordance with the law, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of securities issuers, securities companies, securities service institutions, securities trading venues, and securities registration and settlement institutions;

(2) Enter the premises where suspected illegal acts have occurred to investigate and collect evidence;

(3) Question the parties involved and the entities and individuals related to the matter under investigation, and require them to provide explanations on matters related to the matter under investigation; or require them to submit documents and materials related to the matter under investigation in a designated manner;

(4) Inspect and copy documents and materials such as property rights registrations and communication records related to the matter under investigation;

(5) Inspect and copy securities transaction records, registration and transfer records, financial accounting materials, and other relevant documents and materials of the parties and the entities and individuals related to the matter under investigation; documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed may be sealed or seized;

(6) Inquire into the information of fund accounts, securities accounts, bank accounts, and other accounts with payment, custody, settlement, and other functions of the parties and the entities and individuals related to the matter under investigation, and may copy relevant documents and materials; if there is evidence proving that illegal funds, securities, or other property involved in the case have been or may be transferred or concealed, or that important evidence has been concealed, forged, or destroyed, with the approval of the principal responsible person of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or another responsible person authorized by them, such accounts may be frozen or sealed for a period of six months; if an extension is necessary due to special reasons, each extension shall not exceed three months, and the total period of freezing or sealing shall not exceed two years;

(7) When investigating major securities violations such as market manipulation and insider trading, with the approval of the principal responsible person of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or another responsible person authorized by them, the securities trading of the parties under investigation may be restricted, but the restriction period shall not exceed three months; if the case is complex, it may be extended by three months;

(8) Notify the exit-entry administration authority to prevent, in accordance with the law, the departure of suspected violators, the persons in charge of suspected violating entities, and other persons directly responsible.

To prevent securities market risks and maintain market order, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may take measures such as ordering corrections, regulatory interviews, and issuing warning letters.

Article 171 During the investigation of a suspected securities violation by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council against an entity or individual, if the party under investigation submits a written application, promising to correct the suspected violation within a period recognized by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, compensate the relevant investors for their losses, and eliminate the harm or adverse effects, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may decide to suspend the investigation. If the party under investigation fulfills its promise, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may decide to terminate the investigation; if the party under investigation fails to fulfill its promise or other circumstances specified by the State Council occur, the investigation shall be resumed. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

If the securities regulatory authority of the State Council decides to suspend or terminate an investigation, it shall publicly disclose relevant information in accordance with the provisions.

Article 172 When performing its duties in accordance with the law and conducting supervision, inspection, or investigation, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall have no fewer than two personnel conducting the supervision, inspection, or investigation, and shall present their legal credentials and the supervision, inspection, or investigation notice or other law enforcement documents. If the number of personnel conducting the supervision, inspection, or investigation is fewer than two, or if they fail to present their legal credentials and the supervision, inspection, or investigation notice or other law enforcement documents, the entity or individual under inspection or investigation has the right to refuse.

Article 173 When the securities regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties in accordance with the law, the entity or individual under inspection or investigation shall cooperate, truthfully provide relevant documents and materials, and shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal.

Article 174 The rules, regulations, and supervisory and administrative work systems formulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall be made public in accordance with the law.

The penalty decisions made by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council on securities violations based on the results of investigations shall be made public.

Article 175 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall establish a mechanism for sharing supervisory and administrative information with other financial regulatory authorities of the State Council.

When the securities regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties in accordance with the law and conducts supervision, inspection, or investigation, relevant departments shall cooperate.

Article 176 Any entity or individual has the right to report suspected securities violations or irregularities to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

If a real-name report of a suspected major violation or irregularity is verified, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall reward the whistleblower in accordance with the provisions.

The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall keep the identity information of the whistleblower confidential.

Article 177 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council may establish a mechanism for supervisory and administrative cooperation with securities regulatory authorities of other countries or regions to implement cross-border supervision and administration.

Overseas securities regulatory authorities shall not directly conduct investigations, evidence collection, or other activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China. Without the consent of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and the relevant competent departments of the State Council, no entity or individual may arbitrarily provide documents and materials related to securities business activities to overseas parties.

Article 178 When performing its duties in accordance with the law and discovering that a securities violation is suspected of constituting a crime, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall transfer the case to the judicial authorities for handling in accordance with the law; if it discovers that a public official is suspected of committing a duty-related violation or crime, it shall transfer the case to the supervisory authorities for handling in accordance with the law.

Article 179 Personnel of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council must be loyal to their duties, act in accordance with the law, be fair and honest, shall not use their positions to seek improper benefits, and shall not disclose the business secrets of relevant entities and individuals that they have come to know.

Personnel of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, during their term of office, or within the period specified in the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China after leaving office, shall not hold positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations directly related to their original work, and shall not engage in for-profit activities directly related to their original work.

## Chapter XIII Legal Liability

Article 180 Whoever, in violation of Article 9 of this Law, publicly or in a disguised form publicly issues securities without authorization shall be ordered to cease the issuance, return the funds raised plus interest calculated at the bank deposit rate for the same period, and be fined not less than 5% but not more than 50% of the illegally raised funds; a company established through the unauthorized public or disguised public issuance of securities shall be banned by the institution or department performing supervisory and administrative duties in accordance with the law, in conjunction with the local people's government at or above the county level. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

Article 181 If an issuer conceals important facts or fabricates major false content in its announced securities issuance documents, if the securities have not yet been issued, it shall be fined not less than 2,000,000 yuan but not more than 20,000,000 yuan; if the securities have been issued, it shall be fined not less than 10% but not more than 100% of the illegally raised funds. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be fined not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan.

If the controlling shareholder or actual controller of the issuer organizes or instigates the commission of the illegal act in the preceding paragraph, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 10% but not more than 100% of the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 20,000,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 2,000,000 yuan but not more than 20,000,000 yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be fined not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan.

Article 182 If a sponsor issues a sponsor report containing false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, or fails to perform other statutory duties, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its business income confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the business income; if there is no business income or the business income is less than 1,000,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the sponsor business license shall be suspended or revoked concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

Article 183 If a securities company underwrites or sells securities that have been publicly issued or publicly issued in a disguised form without authorization, it shall be ordered to cease underwriting or selling, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 1,000,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be suspended or revoked concurrently. If losses are caused to investors, it shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the issuer. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

Article 184 If a securities company underwrites securities in violation of Article 29 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and may be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be suspended or revoked. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and may be fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 185 If an issuer, in violation of Article 14 or Article 15 of this Law, changes the use of funds raised through a public offering of securities without authorization, it shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

If the controlling shareholder or actual controller of the issuer engages in, or organizes or instigates the commission of, the illegal act in the preceding paragraph, they shall be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

Article 186 Whoever, in violation of Article 36 of this Law, transfers securities during a restricted transfer period, or transfers stocks not in compliance with laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined an amount not exceeding the value of the securities traded.

Article 187 If a person prohibited by laws or administrative regulations from participating in stock trading, in violation of Article 40 of this Law, directly or under an alias or in another person's name, holds or buys or sells stocks or other securities with equity nature, they shall be ordered to dispose of the illegally held stocks or other securities with equity nature in accordance with the law, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined an amount not exceeding the value of the securities traded; if they are a state functionary, they shall also be given disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 188 If a securities service institution or its practitioner, in violation of Article 42 of this Law, buys or sells securities, they shall be ordered to dispose of the illegally held securities in accordance with the law, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined an amount not exceeding the value of the securities traded.

Article 189 If a director, supervisor, senior manager, or shareholder holding more than 5% of the shares of a listed company or a company whose stocks are traded on other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council, in violation of Article 44 of this Law, buys or sells the company's stocks or other securities with equity nature, they shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

Article 190 Whoever, in violation of Article 45 of this Law, engages in programmatic trading that affects the security of a securities exchange's system or the normal trading order, shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

Article 191 If a person who knows inside information of securities trading or a person who illegally obtains inside information, in violation of Article 53 of this Law, engages in insider trading, they shall be ordered to dispose of the illegally held securities in accordance with the law, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan shall be imposed. If an entity engages in insider trading, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall also be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan. If a staff member of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council engages in insider trading, they shall be given a heavier penalty.

Whoever, in violation of Article 54 of this Law, uses undisclosed information for trading shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 192 Whoever, in violation of Article 55 of this Law, manipulates the securities market, shall be ordered to dispose of the illegally held securities in accordance with the law, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 1,000,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan shall be imposed. If an entity manipulates the securities market, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall also be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan.

Article 193 Whoever, in violation of the first or third paragraph of Article 56 of this Law, fabricates or disseminates false information or misleading information, disrupting the securities market, shall have their illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Whoever, in violation of the second paragraph of Article 56 of this Law, makes false statements or provides misleading information in securities trading activities, shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan; if they are a state functionary, they shall also be given disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law.

If a media outlet or its staff reporting on securities market information, in violation of the third paragraph of Article 56 of this Law, engages in securities trading that conflicts with their work responsibilities, their illegal gains shall be confiscated, and they shall be fined an amount not exceeding the value of the securities traded.

Article 194 If a securities company or its practitioner, in violation of Article 57 of this Law, engages in conduct that harms the interests of clients, they shall be given a warning, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be suspended or revoked.

Article 195 Whoever, in violation of Article 58 of this Law, lends their own securities account or borrows another person's securities account to engage in securities trading, shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 500,000 yuan.Article 196 Where an acquirer fails to perform the obligations of announcement and making a takeover offer for the acquisition of a listed company as required by this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Where an acquirer and its controlling shareholder or actual controller cause losses to the acquired company and its shareholders by utilizing the acquisition of a listed company, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 197 Where an information disclosure obligor fails to submit relevant reports or perform information disclosure obligations as required by this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000. Where the controlling shareholder or actual controller of an issuer organizes or instigates the commission of the above illegal acts, or conceals relevant matters leading to the occurrence of the above circumstances, it shall be fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Where the report submitted or information disclosed by an information disclosure obligor contains false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000. Where the controlling shareholder or actual controller of an issuer organizes or instigates the commission of the above illegal acts, or conceals relevant matters leading to the occurrence of the above circumstances, it shall be fined not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000.

Article 198 Where a securities company violates the provisions of Article 88 of this Law by failing to perform or failing to perform in accordance with regulations the obligation of investor suitability management, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not more than RMB 200,000.

Article 199 Where a person solicits shareholders' rights in violation of the provisions of Article 90 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 200 Where a person illegally establishes a securities trading venue, the people's government at or above the county level shall ban it, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 1,000,000, a fine of not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000 shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Where a stock exchange, in violation of the provisions of Article 105 of this Law, allows non-members to directly participate in centralized trading of stocks, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be concurrently fined not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 201 Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 107, Paragraph 1 of this Law, fails to verify the identity information provided by an investor when opening an account, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 500,000. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not more than RMB 100,000.

Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 107, Paragraph 2 of this Law, provides an investor's account to another person for use, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not more than RMB 200,000.

Article 202 Where a person, in violation of the provisions of Article 118, Article 120, Paragraphs 1 and 4 of this Law, establishes a securities company without authorization, illegally engages in securities business, or conducts securities business activities in the name of a securities company without approval, it shall be ordered to make corrections, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 1,000,000, a fine of not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000 shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000. The securities company established without authorization shall be banned by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 120, Paragraph 5 of this Law, provides securities margin trading services, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than the equivalent value of the margin trading shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be prohibited from engaging in securities margin trading business for a certain period. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 203 Where a person submits false certification documents or uses other fraudulent means to obtain approval for the establishment of a securities company, business license, or approval of major changes, the relevant approval shall be revoked, and a fine of not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000 shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 204 Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 122 of this Law, changes the scope of securities business, changes the major shareholder or actual controller of the company, merges, divides, suspends business, dissolves, or goes bankrupt without approval, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 205 Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 123, Paragraph 2 of this Law, provides financing or guarantees to its shareholders or related persons of its shareholders, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000. If the shareholder is at fault, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may restrict its shareholder rights before it makes corrections as required; if it refuses to make corrections, it may be ordered to transfer the equity it holds in the securities company.

Article 206 Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 128 of this Law, fails to take effective segregation measures to prevent conflicts of interest, or fails to handle related businesses separately or engages in mixed operations, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 207 Where a securities company engages in proprietary securities business in violation of the provisions of Article 129 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked or the company shall be ordered to close. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 208 Where a person, in violation of the provisions of Article 131 of this Law, includes a client's funds and securities in its own property, or misappropriates a client's funds and securities, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 1,000,000, a fine of not less than RMB 1,000,000 but not more than RMB 10,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked or the company shall be ordered to close. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000.

Article 209 Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 134, Paragraph 1 of this Law, accepts a client's discretionary authorization to buy or sell securities, or in violation of the provisions of Article 135 of this Law, makes commitments regarding a client's profits or compensation for a client's losses, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Where a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 134, Paragraph 2 of this Law, allows another person to directly participate in centralized trading of securities in the name of the securities company, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be concurrently fined not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 210 Where an employee of a securities company, in violation of the provisions of Article 136 of this Law, privately accepts a client's entrustment to buy or sell securities, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than RMB 500,000 shall be imposed.

Article 211 Where a securities company and its major shareholder or actual controller, in violation of the provisions of Article 138 of this Law, fail to submit or provide information and materials, or the submitted or provided information and materials contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not more than RMB 1,000,000; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not more than RMB 500,000.

Article 212 Where a person, in violation of the provisions of Article 145 of this Law, establishes a securities registration and settlement institution without authorization, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall ban it, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 213 Where a securities investment consulting institution, in violation of the provisions of Article 160, Paragraph 2 of this Law, engages in securities service business without authorization, or engages in securities service business with conduct specified in Article 161 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Where an accounting firm, law firm, or an institution engaged in asset appraisal, credit rating, financial advisory, or information technology system services, in violation of the provisions of Article 160, Paragraph 2 of this Law, engages in securities service business without filing for record, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than RMB 200,000.

Where a securities service institution, in violation of the provisions of Article 163 of this Law, fails to exercise due diligence, and the documents it produces or issues contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions, it shall be ordered to make corrections, confiscate the business income, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the business income; if there is no business income or the business income is less than RMB 500,000, a fine of not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000 shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be suspended or prohibited from engaging in securities service business. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000.

Article 214 Where an issuer, securities registration and settlement institution, securities company, or securities service institution fails to preserve relevant documents and materials as required, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000; if it leaks, conceals, forges, alters, or destroys relevant documents and materials, it shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 200,000 but not more than RMB 2,000,000; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be fined not less than RMB 500,000 but not more than RMB 5,000,000, and shall be suspended, have its relevant business license revoked, or be prohibited from engaging in relevant business. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given a warning and fined not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000.
Article 215 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, include the compliance of relevant market entities with this Law in the securities market integrity file.

Article 216 Where the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council has any of the following circumstances, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Approving, registering, or approving applications for issuing securities or establishing securities companies that do not meet the requirements of this Law;

(2) Taking measures such as on-site inspections, investigations, inquiries, freezes, or seizures in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(3) Taking supervisory and administrative measures against relevant institutions and personnel in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(4) Imposing administrative penalties on relevant institutions and personnel in violation of the provisions of this Law;

(5) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the law.

Article 217 Where an employee of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council fails to perform the duties stipulated in this Law, abuses power, neglects duty, uses position convenience to seek improper benefits, or discloses known trade secrets of relevant units and individuals, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.
Article 218 Where a person refuses or obstructs the securities regulatory authority and its staff from lawfully exercising their powers of supervision, inspection, and investigation, the securities regulatory authority shall order corrections, impose a fine of not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 1,000,000, and the public security authority shall impose public security penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 219 Where a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 220 Where a person violates the provisions of this Law and should bear civil compensation liability and pay fines, penalties, and illegal gains, and the violator's property is insufficient to pay, priority shall be given to bearing civil compensation liability.

Article 221 Where a person violates relevant provisions of laws, administrative regulations, or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, and the circumstances are serious, the securities regulatory authority of the State Council may take measures to ban the relevant responsible person from the securities market.

The term "ban from the securities market" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to a system prohibiting a person from engaging in securities business or securities service business, serving as a director, supervisor, or senior manager of a securities issuer for a certain period or even for life, or from trading securities on a stock exchange or other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council for a certain period.

Article 222 All fines and confiscated illegal gains collected in accordance with this Law shall be turned over to the state treasury in full.

Article 223 Where a party disagrees with a penalty decision made by the securities regulatory authority or the department authorized by the State Council, it may apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the law, or directly file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 14 Supplementary Provisions

Article 224 Where a domestic enterprise issues securities overseas or lists its securities for trading overseas, directly or indirectly, it shall comply with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 225 Where shares of a domestic company are subscribed and traded in foreign currency, the specific measures shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Article 226 This Law shall come into force on March 1, 2020.
