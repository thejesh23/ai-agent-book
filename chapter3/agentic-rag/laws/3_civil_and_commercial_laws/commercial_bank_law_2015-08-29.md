# Commercial Banking Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 10, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Commercial Banking Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 27, 2003

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Commercial Banking Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 29, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect the legitimate rights and interests of commercial banks, depositors, and other clients, to regulate the conduct of commercial banks, to improve the quality of credit assets, to strengthen supervision and management, to ensure the sound operation of commercial banks, to maintain financial order, and to promote the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, a commercial bank refers to an enterprise legal person established in accordance with this Law and the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China" that engages in the business of absorbing public deposits, granting loans, handling settlements, and other related activities.

Article 3 A commercial bank may engage in part or all of the following businesses:

(1) Absorbing public deposits;

(2) Granting short-term, medium-term, and long-term loans;

(3) Handling domestic and international settlements;

(4) Handling acceptance and discounting of negotiable instruments;

(5) Issuing financial bonds;

(6) Acting as an agent for the issuance, redemption, and underwriting of government bonds;

(7) Buying and selling government bonds and financial bonds;

(8) Engaging in interbank lending;

(9) Buying, selling, and acting as an agent for the buying and selling of foreign exchange;

(10) Engaging in bank card business;

(11) Providing letter of credit services and guarantees;

(12) Acting as an agent for the receipt and payment of funds and for insurance business;

(13) Providing safe deposit box services;

(14) Other businesses approved by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

The business scope shall be stipulated in the articles of association of the commercial bank and submitted to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council for approval.

With the approval of the People's Bank of China, a commercial bank may engage in the business of foreign exchange settlement and sales.

Article 4 A commercial bank shall operate on the principles of safety, liquidity, and efficiency, exercising autonomous management, assuming its own risks, being responsible for its own profits and losses, and practicing self-discipline.

A commercial bank shall conduct its business in accordance with the law and shall not be interfered with by any unit or individual.

A commercial bank shall independently bear civil liability with all its corporate property.

Article 5 In business transactions with clients, a commercial bank shall adhere to the principles of equality, voluntariness, fairness, honesty, and creditworthiness.

Article 6 A commercial bank shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of depositors from infringement by any unit or individual.

Article 7 When conducting credit business, a commercial bank shall strictly examine the creditworthiness of borrowers, implement guarantees, and ensure the timely recovery of loans.

The lawful recovery of principal and interest on due loans from borrowers by a commercial bank shall be protected by law.

Article 8 When conducting business, a commercial bank shall comply with the relevant provisions of laws and administrative regulations and shall not harm the interests of the state or the public interest.

Article 9 When conducting business, a commercial bank shall adhere to the principle of fair competition and shall not engage in unfair competition.

Article 10 A commercial bank shall accept the supervision and management of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with the law. However, if laws stipulate that its relevant business shall be subject to the supervision and management of other supervisory authorities or bodies, those provisions shall apply.

## Chapter II Establishment and Organizational Structure of Commercial Banks

Article 11 The establishment of a commercial bank shall be subject to examination and approval by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Without the approval of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council, no unit or individual may engage in commercial banking business such as absorbing public deposits, and no unit may use the word "bank" in its name.

Article 12 The establishment of a commercial bank shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having articles of association that comply with the provisions of this Law and the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China";

(2) Having the minimum registered capital as stipulated in this Law;

(3) Having directors and senior management personnel with the requisite professional knowledge and business experience for their positions;

(4) Having a sound organizational structure and management system;

(5) Having business premises, security measures, and other facilities related to the business that meet the requirements.

The establishment of a commercial bank shall also meet other prudential conditions.

Article 13 The minimum registered capital for establishing a national commercial bank is RMB one billion yuan. The minimum registered capital for establishing a city commercial bank is RMB one hundred million yuan. The minimum registered capital for establishing a rural commercial bank is RMB fifty million yuan. The registered capital shall be paid-in capital.

The banking regulatory authority under the State Council may adjust the minimum registered capital requirements based on prudential supervision needs, but the adjusted amount shall not be less than the limits specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 14 To establish a commercial bank, the applicant shall submit the following documents and materials to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) An application letter, which shall state the name, location, registered capital, and business scope of the proposed commercial bank;

(2) A feasibility study report;

(3) Other documents and materials as required by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 15 After the application for establishing a commercial bank has been examined and found to comply with the provisions of Article 14 of this Law, the applicant shall complete a formal application form and submit the following documents and materials:

(1) A draft of the articles of association;

(2) Qualification certificates for the proposed directors and senior management personnel;

(3) A capital verification certificate issued by a legally authorized capital verification institution;

(4) A register of shareholders and their capital contributions and shares;

(5) Creditworthiness certificates and relevant materials for shareholders holding five percent or more of the registered capital;

(6) Business policies and plans;

(7) Materials concerning the business premises, security measures, and other facilities related to the business;

(8) Other documents and materials as required by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 16 A commercial bank established with approval shall be issued a banking permit by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council, and shall, based on this permit, register with the administrative department for industry and commerce to obtain a business license.

Article 17 The organizational form and structure of a commercial bank shall be governed by the provisions of the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China."

For commercial banks established before the implementation of this Law, if their organizational form and structure do not fully comply with the provisions of the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China," the original provisions may continue to apply. The date for applying the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 18 Wholly state-owned commercial banks shall establish a board of supervisors. The method for forming the board of supervisors shall be stipulated by the State Council.

The board of supervisors shall supervise the credit asset quality, asset-liability ratios, preservation and appreciation of state-owned assets, as well as acts by senior management personnel that violate laws, administrative regulations, or the articles of association, and acts that harm the interests of the bank.

Article 19 A commercial bank may establish branches within or outside the territory of the People's Republic of China based on business needs. The establishment of branches must be subject to examination and approval by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council. Branches established within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall not be established according to administrative divisions.

When a commercial bank establishes a branch within the territory of the People's Republic of China, it shall allocate an operating capital amount commensurate with its business scale in accordance with regulations. The total operating capital allocated to all branches shall not exceed sixty percent of the total capital of the head office.

Article 20 To establish a branch of a commercial bank, the applicant shall submit the following documents and materials to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council:

(1) An application letter, which shall state the name, operating capital amount, business scope, and location of the proposed branch, as well as the head office and the branch location;

(2) The applicant's financial accounting reports for the most recent two years;

(3) Qualification certificates for the proposed senior management personnel;

(4) Business policies and plans;

(5) Materials concerning the business premises, security measures, and other facilities related to the business;

(6) Other documents and materials as required by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 21 A branch of a commercial bank established with approval shall be issued a banking permit by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council, and shall, based on this permit, register with the administrative department for industry and commerce to obtain a business license.

Article 22 A commercial bank shall implement a financial system for its branches that involves unified accounting by the head office, unified fund allocation, and hierarchical management.

A branch of a commercial bank does not have the status of a legal person. It shall conduct business within the scope authorized by the head office in accordance with the law, and the head office shall bear its civil liability.

Article 23 The establishment of a commercial bank and its branches, once approved, shall be announced by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

If a commercial bank or its branch fails to commence business within six months from the date of obtaining the business license without justifiable reasons, or suspends business for six consecutive months after commencing operations, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall revoke its banking permit and make a public announcement.

Article 24 A commercial bank shall obtain approval from the banking regulatory authority under the State Council for any of the following changes:

(1) Change of name;

(2) Change of registered capital;

(3) Change of location of the head office or branch;

(4) Adjustment of business scope;

(5) Change of shareholders holding five percent or more of the total capital or total shares;

(6) Amendment of the articles of association;

(7) Other changes as specified by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

When replacing directors or senior management personnel, the matter shall be reported to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council for examination of their qualifications.

Article 25 The division or merger of a commercial bank shall be governed by the provisions of the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China."

The division or merger of a commercial bank shall be subject to examination and approval by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 26 A commercial bank shall use its banking permit in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations. The forgery, alteration, transfer, leasing, or lending of a banking permit is prohibited.

Article 27 Persons in any of the following circumstances shall not serve as directors or senior management personnel of a commercial bank:

(1) Those who have been sentenced to criminal punishment for crimes of embezzlement, bribery, misappropriation of property, or crimes undermining the socialist economic order, or who have been deprived of political rights due to a crime;

(2) Those who have served as directors, factory directors, or managers of a company or enterprise that has gone bankrupt due to poor management and bear personal responsibility for the bankruptcy;

(3) Those who have served as the legal representative of a company or enterprise whose business license has been revoked due to illegal activities and bear personal responsibility;

(4) Those who have significant personal debts that are overdue and unpaid.

Article 28 Any unit or individual that purchases five percent or more of the total shares of a commercial bank shall obtain prior approval from the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

## Chapter III Protection of Depositors

Article 29 When handling personal savings deposit business, a commercial bank shall adhere to the principles of voluntary deposit, freedom of withdrawal, interest on deposits, and confidentiality for depositors.

For personal savings deposits, a commercial bank has the right to refuse any inquiry, freezing, or deduction by any unit or individual, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 30 For deposits of entities, a commercial bank has the right to refuse any inquiry by any unit or individual, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations; and has the right to refuse any freezing or deduction by any unit or individual, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 31 A commercial bank shall determine its deposit interest rates within the upper and lower limits prescribed by the People's Bank of China and shall make a public announcement thereof.

Article 32 A commercial bank shall, in accordance with the regulations of the People's Bank of China, deposit a reserve fund with the People's Bank of China and maintain sufficient provisions.

Article 33 A commercial bank shall ensure the payment of deposit principal and interest and shall not delay or refuse payment of deposit principal and interest.

## Chapter IV Basic Rules for Loans and Other Business

Article 34 A commercial bank shall conduct its loan business under the guidance of the state's industrial policies, based on the needs of national economic and social development.

Article 35 When granting a loan, a commercial bank shall strictly examine the borrower's intended use of the loan, repayment ability, repayment method, and other relevant circumstances.

When granting loans, a commercial bank shall implement a system of separating loan review from loan approval and conducting approval at different levels.

Article 36 When a commercial bank grants a loan, the borrower shall provide a guarantee. The commercial bank shall strictly examine the guarantor's repayment ability, the ownership and value of the collateral or pledge, and the feasibility of realizing the mortgage or pledge rights.

If, after examination and evaluation by the commercial bank, the borrower is confirmed to have good creditworthiness and is indeed capable of repaying the loan, a guarantee may not be required.

Article 37 When granting a loan, a commercial bank shall enter into a written contract with the borrower. The contract shall specify the type of loan, the intended use of the loan, the amount, the interest rate, the repayment term, the repayment method, liability for breach of contract, and other matters deemed necessary by both parties.

Article 38 A commercial bank shall determine its loan interest rates within the upper and lower limits prescribed by the People's Bank of China.

Article 39 When granting loans, a commercial bank shall comply with the following provisions on asset-liability ratio management:

(1) The capital adequacy ratio shall not be less than eight percent;

(2) The ratio of liquid asset balance to liquid liability balance shall not be less than twenty-five percent;

(3) The ratio of the loan balance to a single borrower to the commercial bank's capital balance shall not exceed ten percent;

(4) Other provisions on asset-liability ratio management as stipulated by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

For commercial banks established before the implementation of this Law, if their asset-liability ratios do not comply with the provisions of the preceding paragraph after the implementation of this Law, they shall meet the requirements of the preceding paragraph within a specified period. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 40 A commercial bank shall not grant unsecured loans to related parties; the conditions for granting secured loans to related parties shall not be more favorable than those for similar loans to other borrowers.

For the purposes of the preceding paragraph, "related parties" refer to:

(1) Directors, supervisors, management personnel, credit business personnel of the commercial bank, and their close relatives;

(2) Companies, enterprises, and other economic organizations in which the persons listed in the preceding item have invested or hold senior management positions.

Article 41 No unit or individual may compel a commercial bank to grant a loan or provide a guarantee. A commercial bank has the right to refuse any unit or individual that compels it to grant a loan or provide a guarantee.

Article 42 A borrower shall repay the principal and interest of the loan on time.

If a borrower fails to repay a secured loan on time, the commercial bank has the right, in accordance with the law, to demand that the guarantor repay the loan principal and interest, or to seek priority compensation from the security. Real estate or equity obtained by a commercial bank through the exercise of mortgage or pledge rights shall be disposed of within two years from the date of acquisition.

If a borrower fails to repay an unsecured loan on time, the borrower shall bear liability in accordance with the contract.

Article 43 A commercial bank shall not engage in trust investment or securities operations business within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and shall not invest in non-self-use real estate or invest in non-bank financial institutions or enterprises, unless otherwise provided by the state.

Article 44 When handling settlement business such as acceptance of negotiable instruments, remittances, and entrusted collections, a commercial bank shall honor them and credit the accounts within the prescribed time limits, and shall not delay processing, hold up instruments, or refuse payment in violation of regulations. The provisions on the time limits for honoring and crediting accounts shall be made public.

Article 45 A commercial bank shall report for approval in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations when issuing financial bonds or borrowing from abroad.

Article 46 Interbank lending shall comply with the regulations of the People's Bank of China. The use of borrowed funds for granting fixed asset loans or for investment is prohibited.

Lendable funds are limited to the idle funds remaining after meeting the deposit reserve requirements, maintaining sufficient provisions, and repaying due loans from the People's Bank of China. Borrowed funds shall be used to cover shortfalls in instrument settlement and interbank clearing positions, and to meet temporary working capital needs.

Article 47 A commercial bank shall not absorb deposits or grant loans by illegally raising or lowering interest rates or by using other improper means.

Article 48 Enterprises and institutions may independently choose a business premises of a commercial bank to open one basic account for daily transfer settlements and cash receipts and payments, and shall not open more than one basic account.

No unit or individual may open an account in an individual's name for depositing unit funds.

Article 49 The business hours of a commercial bank shall be convenient for clients and shall be made public. A commercial bank shall operate during the announced business hours and shall not suspend business or shorten business hours without authorization.

Article 50 When conducting business and providing services, a commercial bank may charge service fees in accordance with regulations. The fee items and standards shall be formulated by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council and the People's Bank of China, based on their respective responsibilities, in conjunction with the pricing authority under the State Council.

Article 51 A commercial bank shall preserve financial accounting statements, business contracts, and other materials in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 52 Personnel of a commercial bank shall comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and other business management rules, and shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Using their position to solicit or accept bribes, or to accept kickbacks or handling fees of any kind in violation of state regulations;

(2) Using their position to embezzle, misappropriate, or illegally take possession of funds of the bank or its clients;

(3) Granting loans or providing guarantees to relatives or friends in violation of regulations and for personal gain;

(4) Holding concurrent positions in other economic organizations;

(5) Other acts that violate laws, administrative regulations, and business management rules.

Article 53 Personnel of a commercial bank shall not disclose state secrets or commercial secrets that they become aware of during their tenure.

## Chapter V Financial Accounting

Article 54 A commercial bank shall establish and improve its own financial and accounting systems in accordance with the law, the unified national accounting system, and the relevant provisions of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 55 A commercial bank shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, truthfully record and comprehensively reflect its business activities and financial condition, prepare annual financial accounting reports, and submit them promptly to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council, the People's Bank of China, and the financial department under the State Council. A commercial bank shall not maintain any accounting books outside the statutory accounting books.

Article 56 A commercial bank shall, within three months after the end of each fiscal year, publish its operating performance and audit report for the preceding year in accordance with the regulations of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 57 A commercial bank shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, set aside provisions for bad debts and write off bad debts.

Article 58 The fiscal year of a commercial bank shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31 of the Gregorian calendar.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Management

Article 59 A commercial bank shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, formulate its own business rules and establish and improve its risk management and internal control systems.Article 60 A commercial bank shall establish and improve its internal audit and inspection system for deposits, loans, settlements, bad debts, and other matters.

A commercial bank shall conduct regular audits and inspections of its branches.

Article 61 A commercial bank shall, in accordance with regulations, submit balance sheets, income statements, and other financial accounting and statistical reports and materials to the banking regulatory authority of the State Council and the People's Bank of China.

Article 62 The banking regulatory authority of the State Council has the authority, in accordance with the provisions of Chapters III, IV, and V of this Law, to inspect and supervise the deposits, loans, settlements, bad debts, and other matters of a commercial bank at any time. During inspection and supervision, the inspectors shall present valid identification. The commercial bank shall, as required by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council, provide financial accounting materials, business contracts, and other information related to its operation and management.

The People's Bank of China has the authority to inspect and supervise commercial banks in accordance with Articles 32 and 34 of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the People's Bank of China.

Article 63 A commercial bank shall accept audit supervision by audit authorities in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Receivership and Termination

Article 64 When a commercial bank has or may experience a credit crisis that seriously affects the interests of depositors, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council may place the bank under receivership.

The purpose of receivership is to take necessary measures with respect to the commercial bank under receivership to protect the interests of depositors and restore the normal operational capacity of the commercial bank. The creditor-debtor relationships of the commercial bank under receivership shall not change due to the receivership.

Article 65 Receivership shall be decided and organized by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council. The receivership decision of the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall specify the following:

(1) The name of the commercial bank under receivership;
(2) The reasons for receivership;
(3) The receivership organization;
(4) The term of receivership.

The receivership decision shall be announced by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 66 Receivership shall commence from the date the receivership decision takes effect.

From the date of commencement of receivership, the receivership organization shall exercise the management authority of the commercial bank.

Article 67 Upon expiration of the term of receivership, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council may decide to extend it, but the total term of receivership shall not exceed two years.

Article 68 Receivership shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The term specified in the receivership decision expires, or the extension of the term decided by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council expires;
(2) Before the expiration of the term of receivership, the commercial bank has restored its normal operational capacity;
(3) Before the expiration of the term of receivership, the commercial bank has been merged or declared bankrupt in accordance with the law.

Article 69 If a commercial bank needs to dissolve due to a split, merger, or the occurrence of a cause for dissolution specified in its articles of association, it shall submit an application to the banking regulatory authority of the State Council, accompanied by the reasons for dissolution and a plan for repaying debts, including the principal and interest on deposits. Dissolution shall occur after approval by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council.

When a commercial bank dissolves, it shall establish a liquidation group in accordance with the law to carry out liquidation and promptly repay debts, including the principal and interest on deposits, according to the repayment plan. The banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall supervise the liquidation process.

Article 70 If a commercial bank is revoked due to the cancellation of its operating license, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall promptly organize the establishment of a liquidation group in accordance with the law to carry out liquidation and promptly repay debts, including the principal and interest on deposits, according to the repayment plan.

Article 71 If a commercial bank is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, with the consent of the banking regulatory authority of the State Council, the People's Court shall declare it bankrupt in accordance with the law. When a commercial bank is declared bankrupt, the People's Court shall organize the banking regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments and persons to establish a liquidation group to carry out liquidation.

During the bankruptcy liquidation of a commercial bank, after paying the liquidation expenses, wages and labor insurance premiums owed to employees, the principal and interest on individual savings deposits shall be paid in priority.

Article 72 A commercial bank shall terminate upon dissolution, revocation, or declaration of bankruptcy.

## Chapter VIII: Legal Liability

Article 73 If a commercial bank causes property damage to depositors or other clients under any of the following circumstances, it shall bear liability for paying interest on delayed performance and other civil liability:

(1) Unreasonably delaying or refusing to pay the principal and interest on deposits;
(2) Violating regulations on settlement operations such as bill acceptance, refusing to honor, refusing to credit or debit accounts, delaying documents, delaying bills, or improperly returning bills;
(3) Illegally inquiring into, freezing, or deducting individual savings deposits or unit deposits;
(4) Other acts that cause damage to depositors or other clients in violation of the provisions of this Law.

In the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall order rectification; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of between one and five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of between 50,000 and 500,000 RMB shall be imposed.

Article 74 If a commercial bank engages in any of the following acts, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall order rectification; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of between one and five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of between 500,000 and 2,000,000 RMB shall be imposed; if the circumstances are particularly serious or if rectification is not made within the time limit, the bank may be ordered to suspend business for rectification or its operating license may be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Establishing branches without approval;
(2) Splitting or merging without approval, or failing to report changes as required;
(3) Raising or lowering interest rates in violation of regulations, or using other improper means to absorb deposits or grant loans;
(4) Leasing or lending the operating license;
(5) Buying or selling foreign exchange, or acting as an agent for such transactions, without approval;
(6) Buying or selling government bonds, or issuing or buying or selling financial bonds, without approval;
(7) Engaging in trust investment and securities operations, investing in non-self-use real estate, or investing in non-bank financial institutions and enterprises, in violation of state regulations;
(8) Granting credit loans to related parties, or granting secured loans on terms more favorable than those for similar loans to other borrowers.

Article 75 If a commercial bank engages in any of the following acts, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 200,000 and 500,000 RMB; if the circumstances are particularly serious or if rectification is not made within the time limit, the bank may be ordered to suspend business for rectification or its operating license may be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Refusing or obstructing inspection and supervision by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council;
(2) Providing false financial accounting reports, statements, or statistical reports, or concealing important facts;
(3) Failing to comply with the capital adequacy ratio, asset liquidity ratio, single borrower loan ratio, and other regulations of the banking regulatory authority of the State Council on asset-liability ratio management.

Article 76 If a commercial bank engages in any of the following acts, the People's Bank of China shall order rectification; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of between one and five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of between 500,000 and 2,000,000 RMB shall be imposed; if the circumstances are particularly serious or if rectification is not made within the time limit, the People's Bank of China may recommend that the banking regulatory authority of the State Council order the bank to suspend business for rectification or revoke its operating license; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Handling foreign exchange settlement or sale without approval;
(2) Issuing or buying or selling financial bonds on the interbank bond market, or borrowing from abroad, without approval;
(3) Engaging in interbank lending in violation of regulations.

Article 77 If a commercial bank engages in any of the following acts, the People's Bank of China shall order rectification and impose a fine of between 200,000 and 500,000 RMB; if the circumstances are particularly serious or if rectification is not made within the time limit, the People's Bank of China may recommend that the banking regulatory authority of the State Council order the bank to suspend business for rectification or revoke its operating license; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Refusing or obstructing inspection and supervision by the People's Bank of China;
(2) Providing false financial accounting reports, statements, or statistical reports, or concealing important facts;
(3) Failing to deposit deposit reserves in the proportion prescribed by the People's Bank of China.

Article 78 If a commercial bank has the circumstances specified in Articles 73 to 77 of this Law, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 79 If any of the following acts occurs, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall order rectification; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of between one and five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of between 50,000 and 500,000 RMB shall be imposed:

(1) Using the word "bank" in the name without approval;
(2) Purchasing more than 5% of the total shares of a commercial bank without approval;
(3) Opening accounts in individual names for unit funds.

Article 80 If a commercial bank fails to submit relevant documents and materials to the banking regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with regulations, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall order rectification; if rectification is not made within the time limit, a fine of between 100,000 and 300,000 RMB shall be imposed.

If a commercial bank fails to submit relevant documents and materials to the People's Bank of China in accordance with regulations, the People's Bank of China shall order rectification; if rectification is not made within the time limit, a fine of between 100,000 and 300,000 RMB shall be imposed.

Article 81 If a commercial bank is established without the approval of the banking regulatory authority of the State Council, or if public deposits are illegally absorbed or absorbed in disguised form, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; and the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall ban it.

If a commercial bank's operating license is forged, altered, or transferred, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 82 If a borrower obtains a loan through fraudulent means, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 83 If the acts specified in Articles 81 and 82 of this Law do not constitute a crime, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall confiscate the illegal income; if the illegal income is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of between one and five times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of between 500,000 and 2,000,000 RMB shall be imposed.

Article 84 If a staff member of a commercial bank, taking advantage of their position, solicits or accepts bribes, or accepts kickbacks or handling fees of any kind in violation of state regulations, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed.

If, due to the acts in the preceding paragraph, loans are granted or guarantees are provided, resulting in losses, full or partial compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 85 If a staff member of a commercial bank, taking advantage of their position, embezzles, misappropriates, or unlawfully takes possession of the bank's or a client's funds, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed.

Article 86 If a staff member of a commercial bank, in violation of the provisions of this Law, neglects their duty and causes losses, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

If, in violation of regulations, loans are granted or guarantees are provided to relatives or friends out of favoritism, resulting in losses, full or partial compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 87 If a staff member of a commercial bank discloses state secrets or commercial secrets learned during their tenure, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 88 If a unit or individual compels a commercial bank to grant a loan or provide a guarantee, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons or the individual; if losses are caused, full or partial compensation liability shall be borne.

If a staff member of a commercial bank fails to refuse a loan or guarantee compelled by a unit or individual, disciplinary sanctions shall be imposed; if losses are caused, corresponding compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 89 If a commercial bank violates the provisions of this Law, the banking regulatory authority of the State Council may, depending on the circumstances, disqualify the directly responsible directors and senior management personnel from holding positions for a certain period or for life, and prohibit the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons from working in the banking industry for a certain period or for life.

If the act of the commercial bank does not constitute a crime, a warning shall be given to the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons, and a fine of between 50,000 and 500,000 RMB shall be imposed.

Article 90 If a commercial bank or its staff member disagrees with a penalty decision made by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council or the People's Bank of China, it may file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the provisions of the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

Article 91 Commercial banks established with approval in accordance with State Council regulations before the implementation of this Law shall not need to go through the approval procedures again.

Article 92 This Law shall apply to foreign-funded commercial banks, Sino-foreign joint venture commercial banks, and branches of foreign commercial banks; if laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 93 Urban credit cooperatives and rural credit cooperatives handling deposits, loans, settlements, and other business shall apply the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 94 Postal enterprises handling relevant business of commercial banks shall apply the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 95 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2015.
