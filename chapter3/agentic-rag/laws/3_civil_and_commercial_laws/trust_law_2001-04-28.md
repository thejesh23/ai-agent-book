# Trust Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 28, 2001

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate trust relationships, standardize trust activities, protect the lawful rights and interests of the parties involved in a trust, and promote the healthy development of the trust industry.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "trust" refers to a legal relationship in which the settlor, based on trust in the trustee, entrusts the trustee with the settlor's property rights, and the trustee manages or disposes of the property in its own name, in accordance with the settlor's wishes, for the benefit of the beneficiary or for a specific purpose.

Article 3 This Law shall apply to civil, commercial, and charitable trust activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China by settlors, trustees, and beneficiaries (hereinafter collectively referred to as "parties to a trust").

Article 4 Where a trustee engages in trust activities in the form of a trust institution, specific measures for its organization and management shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 5 Parties to a trust must comply with laws and administrative regulations when conducting trust activities, adhere to the principles of voluntariness, fairness, good faith, and must not harm national interests or the public interest.

## Chapter II: Establishment of a Trust

Article 6 To establish a trust, there must be a lawful trust purpose.

Article 7 To establish a trust, there must be definite trust property, and such trust property must be property lawfully owned by the settlor.

For the purposes of this Law, "property" includes lawful property rights.

Article 8 A trust shall be established in writing.

Written form includes trust contracts, wills, or other written documents as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Where a trust is established in the form of a trust contract, the trust is established upon the signing of the trust contract. Where a trust is established in other written forms, the trust is established when the trustee accepts the trust.

Article 9 The written document for establishing a trust shall specify the following matters:

(1) the trust purpose;
(2) the name (or title) and domicile of the settlor and the trustee;
(3) the beneficiary or the scope of beneficiaries;
(4) the scope, type, and condition of the trust property;
(5) the form and method by which the beneficiary receives trust benefits.

In addition to the matters listed in the preceding paragraph, the document may specify the trust term, the method of managing the trust property, the trustee's remuneration, the method for selecting a new trustee, and the grounds for termination of the trust.

Article 10 Where laws or administrative regulations require registration procedures for trust property when establishing a trust, such trust registration shall be completed in accordance with the law.

If trust registration is not completed in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the registration procedures shall be completed retroactively; if not completed, the trust shall not take effect.

Article 11 A trust shall be void under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the trust purpose violates laws or administrative regulations or harms the public interest;
(2) the trust property cannot be determined;
(3) the settlor establishes a trust with illegal property or property that cannot be used to establish a trust under this Law;
(4) the trust is established specifically for the purpose of litigation or debt collection;
(5) the beneficiary or the scope of beneficiaries cannot be determined;
(6) other circumstances as prescribed by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 12 Where a settlor establishes a trust to the detriment of the interests of the settlor's creditors, the creditors have the right to apply to a People's Court to revoke the trust.

Where a People's Court revokes a trust in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the trust benefits already received by a bona fide beneficiary shall not be affected.

The right to apply under the first paragraph of this Article shall lapse if not exercised within one year from the date the creditor knows or should have known of the grounds for revocation.

Article 13 The establishment of a testamentary trust shall comply with the provisions of the Law of Succession regarding wills.

If the person designated in the will refuses or is unable to act as trustee, the beneficiary shall select another trustee; if the beneficiary is a person without or with limited capacity for civil conduct, the beneficiary's guardian shall make the selection on the beneficiary's behalf in accordance with the law. If the will provides otherwise regarding the selection of the trustee, such provisions shall prevail.

## Chapter III: Trust Property

Article 14 The property acquired by the trustee upon acceptance of the trust constitutes trust property.

Property acquired by the trustee through the management, use, disposition, or other circumstances of the trust property shall also be included in the trust property.

Property whose circulation is prohibited by laws or administrative regulations shall not be used as trust property.

Property whose circulation is restricted by laws or administrative regulations may be used as trust property after approval by the relevant competent authority in accordance with the law.

Article 15 Trust property shall be distinguished from other property of the settlor that is not placed in trust. After the establishment of the trust, if the settlor dies, is dissolved in accordance with the law, is revoked in accordance with the law, or is declared bankrupt, and the settlor is the sole beneficiary, the trust shall terminate, and the trust property shall form part of the settlor's estate or liquidation assets; if the settlor is not the sole beneficiary, the trust shall continue, and the trust property shall not form part of the settlor's estate or liquidation assets. However, if the settlor, who is also a co-beneficiary, dies, is dissolved in accordance with the law, is revoked in accordance with the law, or is declared bankrupt, the settlor's trust beneficial interest shall form part of the settlor's estate or liquidation assets.

Article 16 Trust property shall be distinguished from property owned by the trustee (hereinafter referred to as "own property") and shall not be included in the trustee's own property or become part of the trustee's own property.

Where the trustee dies, is dissolved in accordance with the law, is revoked in accordance with the law, or is declared bankrupt and thus terminates, the trust property shall not form part of the trustee's estate or liquidation assets.

Article 17 Trust property shall not be subject to compulsory enforcement except under any of the following circumstances:

(1) a creditor had a right to be satisfied in priority from the trust property before the establishment of the trust and exercises such right in accordance with the law;
(2) a debt arises from the trustee's handling of trust affairs, and the creditor demands satisfaction of such debt;
(3) taxes payable on the trust property itself;
(4) other circumstances as prescribed by law.

Where trust property is subject to compulsory enforcement in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the settlor, trustee, or beneficiary has the right to raise an objection with the People's Court.

Article 18 Claims arising from the trustee's management, use, or disposition of trust property shall not be offset against debts arising from the trustee's own property.

Claims and debts arising from the trustee's management, use, or disposition of trust property of different settlors shall not be offset against each other.

## Chapter IV: Parties to a Trust

### Section 1: The Settlor

Article 19 A settlor shall be a natural person with full capacity for civil conduct, a legal person, or another organization established in accordance with the law.

Article 20 The settlor has the right to be informed of the management, use, disposition, income, and expenditure of the trust property and has the right to request explanations from the trustee.

The settlor has the right to inspect, transcribe, or copy trust accounts related to the trust property and other documents concerning the handling of trust affairs.

Article 21 Where, due to special circumstances unforeseen at the time of establishing the trust, the method of managing the trust property becomes detrimental to achieving the trust purpose or inconsistent with the interests of the beneficiary, the settlor has the right to request the trustee to adjust the method of managing the trust property.

Article 22 Where the trustee disposes of trust property in violation of the trust purpose, or causes loss to the trust property due to breach of management duties or improper handling of trust affairs, the settlor has the right to apply to a People's Court to revoke such disposition and has the right to request the trustee to restore the trust property to its original state or to provide compensation. If the transferee of the trust property knew that the property was accepted in violation of the trust purpose, the transferee shall return the property or provide compensation.

The right to apply under the preceding paragraph shall lapse if not exercised within one year from the date the settlor knows or should have known of the grounds for revocation.

Article 23 Where the trustee disposes of trust property in violation of the trust purpose, or commits gross negligence in the management, use, or disposition of trust property, the settlor has the right to remove the trustee in accordance with the provisions of the trust document, or to apply to a People's Court to remove the trustee.

### Section 2: The Trustee

Article 24 A trustee shall be a natural person with full capacity for civil conduct or a legal person.

Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise regarding the qualifications of a trustee, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 25 The trustee shall comply with the provisions of the trust document and handle trust affairs for the maximum benefit of the beneficiary.

In managing trust property, the trustee must perform the duties with due diligence, good faith, prudence, and effective management.

Article 26 The trustee shall not use trust property for the trustee's own benefit except to obtain remuneration in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

If the trustee violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and uses trust property for the trustee's own benefit, the benefits obtained shall be included in the trust property.

Article 27 The trustee shall not convert trust property into the trustee's own property. If the trustee converts trust property into the trustee's own property, the trustee must restore the trust property to its original state; if loss is caused to the trust property, the trustee shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 28 The trustee shall not conduct transactions between the trustee's own property and trust property, or between trust properties of different settlors, unless otherwise provided in the trust document or with the consent of the settlor or beneficiary, and the transaction is conducted at a fair market price.

If the trustee violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and causes loss to the trust property, the trustee shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 29 The trustee must manage and account for trust property separately from the trustee's own property, and must manage and account for trust properties of different settlors separately.

Article 30 The trustee shall handle trust affairs personally, but may entrust another person to handle them on the trustee's behalf if otherwise provided in the trust document or if there are unavoidable reasons.

Where the trustee lawfully entrusts another person to handle trust affairs, the trustee shall bear responsibility for the actions of that person in handling the trust affairs.

Article 31 Where there are two or more trustees for the same trust, they are co-trustees.

Co-trustees shall handle trust affairs jointly, unless the trust document provides that certain specific matters shall be handled separately by individual trustees.

Where co-trustees handle trust affairs jointly and cannot reach a consensus, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the trust document; if the trust document does not provide for such a situation, the settlor, beneficiary, or their interested parties shall decide.

Article 32 Co-trustees shall bear joint and several liability for debts owed to third parties arising from the handling of trust affairs. A declaration of intention made by a third party to one co-trustee shall be equally effective against the other co-trustees.

If one co-trustee disposes of trust property in violation of the trust purpose, or causes loss to the trust property due to breach of management duties or improper handling of trust affairs, the other co-trustees shall bear joint and several liability for compensation.

Article 33 The trustee must maintain complete records of the handling of trust affairs.

The trustee shall report annually on the management, use, disposition, income, and expenditure of the trust property to the settlor and the beneficiary.

The trustee has a duty to maintain confidentiality regarding the settlor, the beneficiary, and the circumstances and materials concerning the handling of trust affairs, in accordance with the law.

Article 34 The trustee is obligated to pay trust benefits to the beneficiary to the extent of the trust property.

Article 35 The trustee has the right to obtain remuneration in accordance with the provisions of the trust document. If no prior agreement is made in the trust document, a supplementary agreement may be made with the consent of the parties to the trust through consultation; if no prior or supplementary agreement is made, no remuneration may be charged.

The amount of agreed remuneration may be increased or decreased with the consent of the parties to the trust through consultation.

Article 36 Where the trustee disposes of trust property in violation of the trust purpose, or causes loss to the trust property due to breach of management duties or improper handling of trust affairs, the trustee shall not request payment of remuneration until the trust property is restored to its original state or compensation is provided.

Article 37 Expenses incurred by the trustee in handling trust affairs and debts owed to third parties shall be borne by the trust property. If the trustee advances payment from the trustee's own property, the trustee has a right to be satisfied in priority from the trust property.

Debts owed to third parties or losses suffered by the trustee due to breach of management duties or improper handling of trust affairs shall be borne by the trustee's own property.

Article 38 After the establishment of a trust, the trustee may resign with the consent of the settlor and the beneficiary. If this Law provides otherwise regarding the resignation of a trustee of a charitable trust, such provisions shall prevail.

Where a trustee resigns, the trustee shall continue to perform the duties of managing trust affairs until a new trustee is selected.

Article 39 The duties of a trustee shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) death or being declared dead in accordance with the law;
(2) being declared a person without or with limited capacity for civil conduct in accordance with the law;
(3) being revoked or declared bankrupt in accordance with the law;
(4) dissolution in accordance with the law or loss of legal qualifications;
(5) resignation or removal;
(6) other circumstances as prescribed by laws or administrative regulations.

Upon termination of the trustee's duties, the trustee's heir, administrator of the estate, guardian, or liquidator shall properly preserve the trust property and assist the new trustee in taking over the trust affairs.

Article 40 Where the duties of a trustee terminate, a new trustee shall be selected in accordance with the provisions of the trust document; if the trust document does not provide for such a situation, the settlor shall select the new trustee; if the settlor fails to designate or is unable to designate, the beneficiary shall select the new trustee; if the beneficiary is a person without or with limited capacity for civil conduct, the beneficiary's guardian shall make the selection on the beneficiary's behalf in accordance with the law.

The rights and obligations of the original trustee in handling trust affairs shall be assumed by the new trustee.

Article 41 Where the duties of a trustee terminate under any of the circumstances listed in items (3) through (6) of paragraph 1 of Article 39 of this Law, the trustee shall prepare a report on the handling of trust affairs and complete the transfer of trust property and trust affairs to the new trustee.

Upon approval of the report referred to in the preceding paragraph by the settlor or beneficiary, the original trustee shall be released from liability for the matters listed in the report, unless the original trustee engaged in improper conduct.

Article 42 Where the duties of one co-trustee terminate, the trust property shall be managed and disposed of by the remaining co-trustees.

### Section 3: The Beneficiary

Article 43 A beneficiary is a person who enjoys trust beneficial rights in a trust. A beneficiary may be a natural person, a legal person, or another organization established in accordance with the law.

The settlor may be a beneficiary and may also be the sole beneficiary of the same trust.

The trustee may be a beneficiary but shall not be the sole beneficiary of the same trust.

Article 44 A beneficiary enjoys trust beneficial rights from the date the trust takes effect, unless otherwise provided in the trust document.

Article 45 Co-beneficiaries shall enjoy trust benefits in accordance with the provisions of the trust document. If the trust document does not specify the proportion or method of distributing trust benefits, each beneficiary shall enjoy trust benefits in equal proportions.

Article 46 A beneficiary may waive the trust beneficial rights.

If all beneficiaries waive their trust beneficial rights, the trust shall terminate.

If some beneficiaries waive their trust beneficial rights, the waived trust beneficial rights shall be attributed in the following order:

(1) to the person designated in the trust document;
(2) to the other beneficiaries;
(3) to the settlor or the settlor's heirs.

Article 47 Where a beneficiary is unable to pay due debts, the beneficiary's trust beneficial rights may be used to satisfy such debts, unless otherwise restricted by laws, administrative regulations, or the trust document.

Article 48 A beneficiary's trust beneficial rights may be transferred and inherited in accordance with the law, unless otherwise restricted by the trust document.

Article 49 A beneficiary may exercise the rights enjoyed by the settlor as provided in Articles 20 through 23 of this Law. If the beneficiary's exercise of the above rights conflicts with the settlor's opinion, an application may be made to a People's Court for a ruling.

Where the trustee engages in conduct listed in paragraph 1 of Article 22 of this Law, and one co-beneficiary applies to a People's Court to revoke such disposition, the ruling of revocation made by the People's Court shall be effective against all co-beneficiaries.

## Chapter V: Modification and Termination of a Trust

Article 50 Where the settlor is the sole beneficiary, the settlor or the settlor's heirs may terminate the trust, unless otherwise provided in the trust document.

Article 51 After the establishment of a trust, the settlor may change the beneficiary or dispose of the beneficiary's trust beneficial rights under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the beneficiary commits a major tort against the settlor;
(2) the beneficiary commits a major tort against other co-beneficiaries;
(3) with the consent of the beneficiary;
(4) other circumstances as provided in the trust document.

Under any of the circumstances listed in items (1), (3), or (4) of the preceding paragraph, the settlor may terminate the trust.

Article 52 A trust shall not terminate due to the death, loss of capacity for civil conduct, dissolution in accordance with the law, revocation in accordance with the law, or declaration of bankruptcy of the settlor or trustee, nor shall it terminate due to the resignation of the trustee, unless otherwise provided in this Law or the trust document.

Article 53 A trust shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the grounds for termination specified in the trust document occur;
(2) the continuation of the trust violates the trust purpose;
(3) the trust purpose has been achieved or cannot be achieved;
(4) the parties to the trust agree through consultation;
(5) the trust is revoked;
(6) the trust is terminated.

Article 54 Upon termination of a trust, the trust property shall belong to the person designated in the trust document; if the trust document does not designate a person, the property shall be attributed in the following order:

(1) the beneficiary or the beneficiary's heirs;
(2) the settlor or the settlor's heirs.

Article 55 After the attribution of the trust property is determined in accordance with the preceding article, the trust shall be deemed to continue during the process of transferring the trust property to the person entitled to it, and such person shall be deemed the beneficiary.

Article 56 After the termination of a trust, if a People's Court enforces compulsory execution against the original trust property in accordance with Article 17 of this Law, the person entitled to the property shall be the person against whom enforcement is sought.

Article 57 After the termination of a trust, when the trustee exercises the right to request payment of remuneration or compensation from the trust property in accordance with this Law, the trustee may retain the trust property or make a claim against the person entitled to the trust property.

Article 58 Upon termination of a trust, the trustee shall prepare a liquidation report on the handling of trust affairs. If the beneficiary or the person entitled to the trust property has no objection to the liquidation report, the trustee shall be released from liability for the matters listed in the report, unless the trustee engaged in improper conduct.

## Chapter VI: Charitable Trusts

Article 59 Charitable trusts shall be governed by the provisions of this Chapter. Matters not covered in this Chapter shall be governed by this Law and other relevant laws.

Article 60 A trust established for one of the following public interest purposes shall be a charitable trust:

(1) relief of poverty;
(2) assistance to disaster victims;
(3) support for persons with disabilities;
(4) development of education, science and technology, culture, arts, and sports;
(5) development of medical and health care;
(6) development of environmental protection and maintenance of the ecological environment;
(7) development of other social public welfare undertakings.

Article 61 The State encourages the development of charitable trusts.

Article 62 The establishment of a charitable trust and the appointment of its trustee shall be subject to approval by the administrative authority for the relevant public welfare undertaking (hereinafter referred to as the "public welfare undertaking administrative authority").

No activities shall be conducted in the name of a charitable trust without the approval of the public welfare undertaking administrative authority.The public welfare administration authority shall provide support for charitable trust activities.

Article 63 The trust property and its income of a charitable trust shall not be used for non-charitable purposes.

Article 64 A charitable trust shall have a trust supervisor.

The trust supervisor shall be specified in the trust document. If not specified in the trust document, the public welfare administration authority shall appoint one.

Article 65 The trust supervisor has the right to file a lawsuit or take other legal actions in their own name to protect the interests of the beneficiaries.

Article 66 The trustee of a charitable trust shall not resign without the approval of the public welfare administration authority.

Article 67 The public welfare administration authority shall inspect the trustee's handling of charitable trust affairs and the status of the trust property.

The trustee shall, at least once a year, prepare a report on the handling of trust affairs and the status of the trust property. After being approved by the trust supervisor, the report shall be submitted to the public welfare administration authority for verification and shall be announced by the trustee.

Article 68 If the trustee of a charitable trust violates the trust obligations or is incapable of performing their duties, the public welfare administration authority shall replace the trustee.

Article 69 After the establishment of a charitable trust, if circumstances arise that could not have been foreseen at the time of its creation, the public welfare administration authority may, in accordance with the trust purpose, modify the relevant terms in the trust document.

Article 70 Upon the termination of a charitable trust, the trustee shall, within fifteen days from the date of the occurrence of the termination event, report the termination event and the termination date to the public welfare administration authority.

Article 71 Upon the termination of a charitable trust, the liquidation report prepared by the trustee regarding the handling of trust affairs shall, after being approved by the trust supervisor, be submitted to the public welfare administration authority for verification and shall be announced by the trustee.

Article 72 If a charitable trust terminates and there is no person entitled to the trust property, or the person entitled to the trust property is an unspecified public, the trustee shall, with the approval of the public welfare administration authority, use the trust property for purposes similar to the original charitable purpose, or transfer the trust property to a charitable organization or another charitable trust with a similar purpose.

Article 73 If the public welfare administration authority violates the provisions of this Law, the settlor, trustee, or beneficiary has the right to file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 74 This Law shall come into effect as of October 1, 2001.
