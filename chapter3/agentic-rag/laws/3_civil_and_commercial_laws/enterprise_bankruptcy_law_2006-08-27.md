# Enterprise Bankruptcy Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 27, 2006

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate enterprise bankruptcy procedures, fairly settle claims and debts, protect the legitimate rights and interests of creditors and debtors, and safeguard the order of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 Where a legal person enterprise is unable to repay its debts as they fall due, and its assets are insufficient to cover all debts or it is clearly lacking in solvency, it shall settle its debts in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Where a legal person enterprise falls under the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, or is likely to become clearly insolvent, it may undergo reorganization in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 3 Bankruptcy cases shall be under the jurisdiction of the people's court in the place where the debtor is domiciled.

Article 4 Where there are no provisions in this Law governing the trial procedures for bankruptcy cases, the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law shall apply.

Article 5 The bankruptcy proceedings initiated in accordance with this Law shall have effect on the property of the debtor located outside the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Where a legally effective judgment or ruling on a bankruptcy case rendered by a foreign court involves property of the debtor within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and an application or request is made for recognition and enforcement by a people's court, the people's court shall, in accordance with the international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or under the principle of reciprocity, examine the matter. If it finds that the judgment or ruling does not violate the basic principles of the laws of the People's Republic of China, does not harm national sovereignty, security, or public interests, and does not harm the legitimate rights and interests of creditors within the territory of the People's Republic of China, it shall rule to recognize and enforce it.

Article 6 When trying bankruptcy cases, the people's court shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of the enterprise's employees in accordance with the law, and shall pursue the legal liability of the enterprise's management personnel in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Application and Acceptance

### Section 1 Application

Article 7 Where a debtor falls under the circumstances specified in Article 2 of this Law, it may file an application with the people's court for reorganization, composition, or bankruptcy liquidation.

Where a debtor is unable to repay its debts as they fall due, a creditor may file an application with the people's court for reorganization or bankruptcy liquidation of the debtor.

Where a legal person enterprise has been dissolved but has not been liquidated, or has not completed liquidation, and its assets are insufficient to cover its debts, the person legally responsible for liquidation shall file an application with the people's court for bankruptcy liquidation.

Article 8 To file a bankruptcy application with the people's court, a bankruptcy petition and relevant evidence shall be submitted.

The bankruptcy petition shall specify the following:

(1) Basic information about the applicant and the respondent;
(2) The purpose of the application;
(3) The facts and grounds for the application;
(4) Other matters that the people's court deems should be specified.

Where the debtor files the application, it shall also submit to the people's court a statement of its property status, a schedule of debts, a schedule of claims, relevant financial and accounting reports, a plan for employee placement, and information on the payment of employee wages and social insurance premiums.

Article 9 Before the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application, the applicant may request to withdraw the application.

### Section 2 Acceptance

Article 10 Where a creditor files a bankruptcy application, the people's court shall notify the debtor within five days from the date of receiving the application. If the debtor objects to the application, it shall raise the objection with the people's court within seven days from the date of receiving the notice from the people's court. The people's court shall rule on whether to accept the application within ten days from the date of expiry of the objection period.

Except for the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the people's court shall rule on whether to accept the bankruptcy application within fifteen days from the date of receiving the application.

Where special circumstances require an extension of the time limit for ruling on acceptance as specified in the preceding two paragraphs, the time limit may be extended by fifteen days with the approval of the people's court at the next higher level.

Article 11 Where the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, it shall serve the ruling on the applicant within five days from the date of making the ruling.

Where a creditor files the application, the people's court shall serve the ruling on the debtor within five days from the date of making the ruling. The debtor shall, within fifteen days from the date of service of the ruling, submit to the people's court a statement of its property status, a schedule of debts, a schedule of claims, relevant financial and accounting reports, and information on the payment of employee wages and social insurance premiums.

Article 12 Where the people's court rules not to accept a bankruptcy application, it shall serve the ruling on the applicant within five days from the date of making the ruling and state the reasons. If the applicant disagrees with the ruling, it may file an appeal with the people's court at the next higher level within ten days from the date of service of the ruling.

After accepting a bankruptcy application but before declaring bankruptcy, if upon examination it is found that the debtor does not meet the circumstances specified in Article 2 of this Law, the people's court may rule to dismiss the application. If the applicant disagrees with the ruling, it may file an appeal with the people's court at the next higher level within ten days from the date of service of the ruling.

Article 13 Where the people's court rules to accept a bankruptcy application, it shall simultaneously appoint an administrator.

Article 14 The people's court shall, within twenty-five days from the date of ruling to accept the bankruptcy application, notify known creditors and make a public announcement.

The notice and public announcement shall specify the following:

(1) The name of the applicant and the respondent;
(2) The date on which the people's court accepted the bankruptcy application;
(3) The time limit, place, and precautions for filing claims;
(4) The name of the administrator and the address for handling affairs;
(5) The requirement that the debtor's debtors or property holders shall settle debts or deliver property to the administrator;
(6) The time and place of the first creditors' meeting;
(7) Other matters that the people's court deems should be notified and announced.

Article 15 From the date of service of the ruling accepting the bankruptcy application on the debtor until the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings, the relevant personnel of the debtor shall assume the following obligations:

(1) Properly preserve the property, seals, account books, documents, and other materials in their possession and under their management;
(2) Work in accordance with the requirements of the people's court and the administrator, and truthfully answer inquiries;
(3) Attend creditors' meetings and truthfully answer questions from creditors;
(4) Not leave their place of domicile without the permission of the people's court;
(5) Not assume new positions as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of other enterprises.

The "relevant personnel" referred to in the preceding paragraph means the legal representative of the enterprise; with the decision of the people's court, it may include the financial management personnel and other management personnel of the enterprise.

Article 16 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, any repayment of debts by the debtor to individual creditors shall be void.

Article 17 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, the debtor's debtors or property holders shall settle debts or deliver property to the administrator.

Where the debtor's debtors or property holders intentionally violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph by settling debts or delivering property to the debtor, causing losses to creditors, they shall not be exempted from the obligation to settle debts or deliver property.

Article 18 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, the administrator has the right to decide whether to rescind or continue performing contracts that were established before the acceptance of the bankruptcy application and have not been fully performed by both the debtor and the other party, and shall notify the other party. If the administrator fails to notify the other party within two months from the date of acceptance of the bankruptcy application, or fails to respond within thirty days from the date of receiving a reminder from the other party, the contract shall be deemed rescinded.

If the administrator decides to continue performing the contract, the other party shall perform; however, the other party has the right to require the administrator to provide security. If the administrator fails to provide security, the contract shall be deemed rescinded.

Article 19 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, any preservation measures concerning the debtor's property shall be lifted, and enforcement proceedings shall be suspended.

Article 20 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, any civil litigation or arbitration concerning the debtor that has commenced but not yet concluded shall be suspended; after the administrator takes over the debtor's property, such litigation or arbitration shall continue.

Article 21 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, any civil litigation concerning the debtor may only be brought before the people's court that accepted the bankruptcy application.

## Chapter III Administrator

Article 22 The administrator shall be appointed by the people's court.

If the creditors' meeting believes that the administrator cannot perform duties lawfully and impartially, or is otherwise incompetent for the position, it may apply to the people's court for replacement.

The methods for appointing the administrator and determining the administrator's remuneration shall be prescribed by the Supreme People's Court.

Article 23 The administrator shall perform duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, report work to the people's court, and accept supervision from the creditors' meeting and the creditors' committee.

The administrator shall attend creditors' meetings, report on the performance of duties to the creditors' meeting, and answer questions.

Article 24 The administrator may be a liquidation group composed of personnel from relevant departments and institutions, or a law firm, accounting firm, bankruptcy liquidation firm, or other social intermediary institution established in accordance with the law.

Based on the actual circumstances of the debtor, the people's court may, after consulting the opinions of relevant social intermediary institutions, appoint personnel with relevant professional knowledge and practice qualifications from such institutions to serve as the administrator.

A person shall not serve as an administrator under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Having been subject to criminal punishment for an intentional crime;
(2) Having had their relevant professional practice certificate revoked;
(3) Having an interest in the case;
(4) Other circumstances under which the people's court deems it inappropriate to serve as an administrator.

Where an individual serves as an administrator, they shall take out professional liability insurance.

Article 25 The administrator shall perform the following duties:

(1) Take over the debtor's property, seals, account books, documents, and other materials;
(2) Investigate the debtor's property status and prepare a property status report;
(3) Decide on the debtor's internal management affairs;
(4) Decide on the debtor's daily expenses and other necessary expenses;
(5) Decide whether to continue or cease the debtor's business before the first creditors' meeting is convened;
(6) Manage and dispose of the debtor's property;
(7) Represent the debtor in litigation, arbitration, or other legal proceedings;
(8) Propose convening a creditors' meeting;
(9) Other duties that the people's court deems the administrator should perform.

Where there are other provisions in this Law regarding the duties of the administrator, those provisions shall apply.

Article 26 Before the first creditors' meeting is convened, if the administrator decides to continue or cease the debtor's business, or engages in any of the acts specified in Article 69 of this Law, the administrator shall obtain permission from the people's court.

Article 27 The administrator shall be diligent and conscientious, and faithfully perform their duties.

Article 28 With the permission of the people's court, the administrator may hire necessary staff.

The administrator's remuneration shall be determined by the people's court. If the creditors' meeting objects to the administrator's remuneration, it has the right to raise the objection with the people's court.

Article 29 The administrator shall not resign without justifiable reasons. The administrator's resignation shall be subject to the permission of the people's court.

## Chapter IV Debtor's Property

Article 30 All property belonging to the debtor at the time of acceptance of the bankruptcy application, and property acquired by the debtor after acceptance of the bankruptcy application but before the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings, shall constitute the debtor's property.

Article 31 Within one year before the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application, the administrator has the right to request the people's court to revoke the following acts involving the debtor's property:

(1) Gratuitous transfer of property;
(2) Transactions at a manifestly unreasonable price;
(3) Provision of property security for debts that were previously unsecured;
(4) Early repayment of debts not yet due;
(5) Waiver of claims.

Article 32 Within six months before the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application, if the debtor, under the circumstances specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 2 of this Law, still makes repayments to individual creditors, the administrator has the right to request the people's court to revoke such repayments, except where the individual repayment benefits the debtor's property.

Article 33 The following acts involving the debtor's property shall be void:

(1) Concealing or transferring property to evade debts;
(2) Fabricating debts or acknowledging false debts.

Article 34 The administrator has the right to recover the debtor's property obtained through the acts specified in Articles 31, 32, or 33 of this Law.

Article 35 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, if the debtor's capital contributors have not fully performed their capital contribution obligations, the administrator shall require such capital contributors to pay the subscribed capital contributions, without being subject to the time limit for capital contribution.

Article 36 The administrator shall recover any abnormal income obtained by the debtor's directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel from the enterprise by taking advantage of their positions, as well as any enterprise property they have misappropriated.

Article 37 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, the administrator may retrieve pledged or retained property by settling the secured debt or providing security acceptable to the creditor.
The debt settlement or alternative security referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be limited to the then-current market value of the pledged or retained property if its value is lower than the amount of the secured claim.
Article 38 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, the owner of property in the debtor's possession that does not belong to the debtor may retrieve such property through the administrator, unless otherwise provided in this Law.

Article 39 At the time the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application, if the seller has dispatched the subject matter of the sale to the debtor as the buyer, and the debtor has not yet received it and has not paid the full price, the seller may retrieve the subject matter in transit. However, the administrator may pay the full price and request the seller to deliver the subject matter.

Article 40 A creditor who owes a debt to the debtor before the acceptance of the bankruptcy application may set off the debt against the claim by asserting the set-off with the administrator. However, set-off shall not be allowed under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The debtor's debtor acquires a claim against the debtor from another person after the acceptance of the bankruptcy application;
(2) The creditor, knowing that the debtor is unable to repay debts as they fall due or that a bankruptcy application has been filed, incurs a debt to the debtor; except where the creditor incurs the debt due to a legal provision or a cause arising more than one year before the bankruptcy application;
(3) The debtor's debtor, knowing that the debtor is unable to repay debts as they fall due or that a bankruptcy application has been filed, acquires a claim against the debtor; except where the debtor's debtor acquires the claim due to a legal provision or a cause arising more than one year before the bankruptcy application.

## Chapter V Bankruptcy Expenses and Common Benefit Debts

Article 41 The following expenses incurred after the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application shall be bankruptcy expenses:

(1) Litigation costs of the bankruptcy case;
(2) Expenses for managing, realizing, and distributing the debtor's property;
(3) Expenses for the administrator's performance of duties, remuneration, and costs of hiring staff.

Article 42 The following debts incurred after the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application shall be common benefit debts:

(1) Debts arising from the administrator or debtor requesting the other party to perform contracts that have not been fully performed by both parties;
(2) Debts arising from the management of the debtor's property without mandate;
(3) Debts arising from the debtor's unjust enrichment;
(4) Labor remuneration and social insurance premiums payable for the continued operation of the debtor, and other debts arising therefrom;
(5) Debts arising from damage caused by the administrator or relevant personnel in the performance of their duties;
(6) Debts arising from damage caused by the debtor's property.

Article 43 Bankruptcy expenses and common benefit debts shall be paid from the debtor's property at any time.

If the debtor's property is insufficient to cover all bankruptcy expenses and common benefit debts, the bankruptcy expenses shall be paid first.

If the debtor's property is insufficient to cover all bankruptcy expenses or all common benefit debts, payment shall be made on a pro rata basis.

If the debtor's property is insufficient to cover the bankruptcy expenses, the administrator shall request the people's court to conclude the bankruptcy proceedings. The people's court shall, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the request, rule to conclude the bankruptcy proceedings and make a public announcement.

## Chapter VI Filing of Claims

Article 44 A creditor who holds a claim against the debtor at the time the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application shall exercise their rights in accordance with the procedures provided in this Law.

Article 45 After the people's court accepts a bankruptcy application, it shall determine the time limit for creditors to file their claims. The time limit for filing claims shall be calculated from the date the people's court publishes the announcement of acceptance of the bankruptcy application, and shall be not less than thirty days and not more than three months.

Article 46 Claims not yet due shall be deemed due at the time of acceptance of the bankruptcy application.

Interest on interest-bearing claims shall cease to accrue from the time of acceptance of the bankruptcy application.

Article 47 Creditors may file claims that are conditional or subject to a time limit, as well as claims pending litigation or arbitration.

Article 48 Creditors shall file their claims with the administrator within the time limit for filing claims determined by the people's court.

Wages, medical and disability subsidies, and bereavement expenses owed by the debtor to its employees, as well as basic pension insurance and basic medical insurance premiums owed that should be credited to employees' personal accounts, and compensation payable to employees as provided by laws and administrative regulations, need not be filed. The administrator shall investigate and compile a list for public announcement. If employees object to the contents of the list, they may request the administrator to make corrections; if the administrator refuses to make corrections, employees may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 49 When filing a claim, the creditor shall provide a written statement of the amount of the claim and whether it is secured by property, and submit relevant evidence. If the claim being filed is a joint claim, this shall be stated.

Article 50 Joint creditors may be represented by one of them to file the claim on behalf of all joint creditors, or they may jointly file the claim.

Article 51 Where a guarantor or other joint debtor of the debtor has already satisfied the debt on behalf of the debtor, they may file a claim based on their right of recourse against the debtor.

Where a guarantor or other joint debtor of the debtor has not yet satisfied the debt on behalf of the debtor, they may file a claim based on their future right of recourse against the debtor, unless the creditor has already filed the entire claim with the administrator.

Article 52 Where several joint debtors are subject to the procedures provided in this Law by ruling, their creditors have the right to file their claims separately in each bankruptcy case for the entire claim.

Article 53 Where the administrator or the debtor rescinds a contract in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the other party may file a claim based on the right to claim damages arising from the rescission of the contract.

Article 54 Where the debtor is the principal in a mandate contract and is subject to the procedures provided in this Law by ruling, and the agent, unaware of this fact, continues to handle the mandated affairs, the agent may file a claim based on the claims arising therefrom.

Article 55 Where the debtor is the drawer of a negotiable instrument and is subject to the procedures provided in this Law by ruling, and the payer of the instrument continues to make payment or accept the instrument, the payer may file a claim based on the claims arising therefrom.

Article 56 If a creditor fails to file a claim within the time limit for filing claims determined by the people's court, they may file a supplementary claim before the final distribution of the bankruptcy property; however, no supplementary distribution shall be made for distributions already made. The costs of examining and confirming the supplementary claim shall be borne by the person filing the supplementary claim.

A creditor who fails to file a claim in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall not exercise their rights under the procedures provided in this Law.Article 57 After receiving the materials for declaration of claims, the administrator shall register them, examine the declared claims, and prepare a schedule of claims.

The schedule of claims and the materials for declaration of claims shall be kept by the administrator for inspection by interested parties.

Article 58 The schedule of claims prepared in accordance with Article 57 of this Law shall be submitted to the first creditors' meeting for verification.

If the debtor and the creditors have no objection to the claims recorded in the schedule of claims, the people's court shall confirm them by ruling.

If the debtor or any creditor has an objection to a claim recorded in the schedule of claims, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court that accepted the bankruptcy application.

## Chapter VII Creditors' Meeting

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 59 Creditors who have declared their claims in accordance with this Law are members of the creditors' meeting and have the right to attend the meeting and to vote.

Creditors whose claims have not yet been determined shall not exercise the right to vote, unless the people's court can provisionally determine the amount of the claim for them to exercise the right to vote.

Creditors holding security interests in the specific property of the debtor who have not waived their right to preferential satisfaction shall not have the right to vote on the matters specified in Items (7) and (10) of Paragraph 1 of Article 61 of this Law.

Creditors may appoint agents to attend the creditors' meeting and exercise the right to vote. Agents attending the creditors' meeting shall submit a power of attorney from the creditor to the people's court or the chairperson of the creditors' meeting.

Representatives of the debtor's employees and the trade union shall attend the creditors' meeting and express opinions on relevant matters.

Article 60 The creditors' meeting shall have one chairperson, who shall be designated by the people's court from among the creditors with the right to vote.

The chairperson of the creditors' meeting shall preside over the creditors' meeting.

Article 61 The creditors' meeting shall exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) Verifying claims;
(2) Applying to the people's court for the replacement of the administrator, and examining the administrator's expenses and remuneration;
(3) Supervising the administrator;
(4) Electing and replacing members of the creditors' committee;
(5) Deciding whether to continue or cease the debtor's business operations;
(6) Passing the reorganization plan;
(7) Passing the composition agreement;
(8) Passing the plan for administering the debtor's property;
(9) Passing the plan for the realization of the bankruptcy property;
(10) Passing the plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy property;
(11) Other functions and powers that the people's court considers should be exercised by the creditors' meeting.

The creditors' meeting shall prepare minutes of the resolutions on the matters discussed.

Article 62 The first creditors' meeting shall be convened by the people's court within fifteen days from the date of expiry of the period for declaration of claims.

Subsequent creditors' meetings shall be convened when the people's court deems it necessary, or upon the proposal of the administrator, the creditors' committee, or creditors representing one-fourth or more of the total amount of claims to the chairperson of the creditors' meeting.

Article 63 When convening a creditors' meeting, the administrator shall notify known creditors fifteen days in advance.

Article 64 Resolutions of the creditors' meeting shall be passed by a majority of more than half of the creditors with the right to vote present at the meeting, and the amount of claims they represent shall account for more than half of the total amount of unsecured claims, unless otherwise provided in this Law.

If a creditor believes that a resolution of the creditors' meeting violates the provisions of the law and harms their interests, they may, within fifteen days from the date the resolution was made, request the people's court to rule to revoke the resolution and order the creditors' meeting to make a new resolution in accordance with the law.

Resolutions of the creditors' meeting are binding on all creditors.

Article 65 If the matters listed in Items (8) and (9) of Paragraph 1 of Article 61 of this Law are not passed by the creditors' meeting, the people's court shall make a ruling.

If the matter listed in Item (10) of Paragraph 1 of Article 61 of this Law is not passed after a second vote by the creditors' meeting, the people's court shall make a ruling.

The rulings referred to in the preceding two paragraphs may be announced by the people's court at the creditors' meeting or notified separately to the creditors.

Article 66 If a creditor is dissatisfied with the ruling made by the people's court pursuant to Paragraph 1 of Article 65 of this Law, or if creditors representing more than half of the total amount of unsecured claims are dissatisfied with the ruling made by the people's court pursuant to Paragraph 2 of Article 65 of this Law, they may apply for reconsideration to that people's court within fifteen days from the date the ruling was announced or the notice was received. The execution of the ruling shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

### Section 2 Creditors' Committee

Article 67 The creditors' meeting may decide to establish a creditors' committee. The creditors' committee shall consist of representatives of creditors elected by the creditors' meeting and one representative of the debtor's employees or the trade union. The number of members of the creditors' committee shall not exceed nine.

The members of the creditors' committee shall be confirmed by a written decision of the people's court.

Article 68 The creditors' committee shall exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) Supervising the management and disposal of the debtor's property;
(2) Supervising the distribution of bankruptcy property;
(3) Proposing the convening of a creditors' meeting;
(4) Other functions and powers entrusted by the creditors' meeting.

When performing its duties, the creditors' committee has the right to require the administrator and relevant personnel of the debtor to provide explanations or relevant documents regarding matters within their scope of functions and powers.

If the administrator or relevant personnel of the debtor refuse to accept supervision in violation of the provisions of this Law, the creditors' committee has the right to request the people's court to make a decision on the supervised matters; the people's court shall make a decision within five days.

Article 69 The administrator shall promptly report to the creditors' committee when carrying out the following acts:

(1) Transfer of rights to immovable property such as land and houses;
(2) Transfer of property rights such as prospecting rights, mining rights, and intellectual property rights;
(3) Transfer of all inventory or business operations;
(4) Borrowing money;
(5) Creating property security interests;
(6) Transfer of claims and securities;
(7) Performing contracts that neither the debtor nor the counterparty has fully performed;
(8) Waiving rights;
(9) Retrieving collateral;
(10) Other acts of property disposal that have a significant impact on the interests of creditors.

If no creditors' committee has been established, the administrator shall promptly report the acts specified in the preceding paragraph to the people's court.

## Chapter VIII Reorganization

### Section 1 Reorganization Application and Reorganization Period

Article 70 The debtor or creditors may, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, directly apply to the people's court for reorganization of the debtor.

If creditors apply for bankruptcy liquidation of the debtor, the debtor or investors whose capital contributions account for one-tenth or more of the debtor's registered capital may apply to the people's court for reorganization after the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application but before declaring the debtor bankrupt.

Article 71 If the people's court, upon examination, finds that the reorganization application meets the requirements of this Law, it shall rule to reorganize the debtor and make a public announcement.

Article 72 The period from the date the people's court rules to reorganize the debtor to the termination of the reorganization proceedings shall be the reorganization period.

Article 73 During the reorganization period, upon the debtor's application and with the approval of the people's court, the debtor may manage its own property and business affairs under the supervision of the administrator.

In the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the administrator who has taken over the debtor's property and business affairs in accordance with this Law shall transfer the property and business affairs back to the debtor, and the functions and powers of the administrator provided for in this Law shall be exercised by the debtor.

Article 74 If the administrator is responsible for managing the property and business affairs, the administrator may appoint the debtor's management personnel to be responsible for the business affairs.

Article 75 During the reorganization period, security interests in the specific property of the debtor shall be suspended. However, if the collateral is damaged or its value is likely to decrease significantly, thereby endangering the rights of the secured creditor, the secured creditor may request the people's court to restore the exercise of the security interest.

During the reorganization period, if the debtor or the administrator borrows money to continue business operations, they may create security interests for such loans.

Article 76 If a person whose property is lawfully possessed by the debtor requests the return of such property during the reorganization period, the conditions agreed upon in advance shall be met.

Article 77 During the reorganization period, the debtor's investors shall not request distribution of investment returns.

During the reorganization period, the debtor's directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel shall not transfer the debtor's equity held by them to a third party, unless with the consent of the people's court.

Article 78 During the reorganization period, if any of the following circumstances occur, upon the request of the administrator or an interested party, the people's court shall rule to terminate the reorganization proceedings and declare the debtor bankrupt:

(1) The debtor's business and financial conditions continue to deteriorate, lacking the possibility of rescue;
(2) The debtor engages in fraud, maliciously reduces its property, or other acts significantly detrimental to the interests of creditors;
(3) The debtor's conduct renders the administrator unable to perform its duties.

### Section 2 Formulation and Approval of the Reorganization Plan

Article 79 The debtor or the administrator shall, within six months from the date the people's court rules to reorganize the debtor, submit a draft reorganization plan to the people's court and the creditors' meeting simultaneously.

If the period specified in the preceding paragraph expires and the debtor or the administrator requests an extension for justified reasons, the people's court may rule to extend the period by three months.

If the debtor or the administrator fails to submit a draft reorganization plan within the prescribed period, the people's court shall rule to terminate the reorganization proceedings and declare the debtor bankrupt.

Article 80 If the debtor manages its own property and business affairs, the debtor shall prepare the draft reorganization plan.

If the administrator is responsible for managing the property and business affairs, the administrator shall prepare the draft reorganization plan.

Article 81 The draft reorganization plan shall include the following contents:

(1) The debtor's business plan;
(2) Classification of claims;
(3) Plan for adjustment of claims;
(4) Plan for satisfaction of claims;
(5) Period for execution of the reorganization plan;
(6) Period for supervision of the execution of the reorganization plan;
(7) Other plans beneficial to the debtor's reorganization.

Article 82 Creditors of the following categories of claims who attend the creditors' meeting to discuss the draft reorganization plan shall vote on the draft reorganization plan in groups according to the following classification of claims:

(1) Claims secured by the specific property of the debtor;
(2) Wages, medical and disability subsidies, and pension expenses owed by the debtor to its employees, basic endowment insurance and basic medical insurance premiums owed that should be credited to employees' personal accounts, and compensation payable to employees as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations;
(3) Taxes owed by the debtor;
(4) Ordinary claims.

When necessary, the people's court may decide to establish a small-claim group within the ordinary claims group to vote on the draft reorganization plan.

Article 83 The reorganization plan shall not provide for the reduction or exemption of social insurance premiums other than those specified in Item (2) of Paragraph 1 of Article 82 of this Law that are owed by the debtor; creditors of such expenses shall not participate in the vote on the draft reorganization plan.

Article 84 The people's court shall convene a creditors' meeting within thirty days from the date of receiving the draft reorganization plan to vote on the draft reorganization plan.

If more than half of the creditors present at the meeting in the same voting group agree to the draft reorganization plan, and the amount of claims they represent accounts for two-thirds or more of the total amount of claims in that group, the group shall be deemed to have passed the draft reorganization plan.

The debtor or the administrator shall explain the draft reorganization plan to the creditors' meeting and answer questions.

Article 85 Representatives of the debtor's investors may attend the creditors' meeting discussing the draft reorganization plan as non-voting participants.

If the draft reorganization plan involves adjustments to the rights and interests of investors, an investor group shall be established to vote on the matter.

Article 86 When all voting groups pass the draft reorganization plan, the reorganization plan shall be deemed adopted.

Within ten days from the date the reorganization plan is adopted, the debtor or the administrator shall apply to the people's court for approval of the reorganization plan. If the people's court, upon examination, finds that it meets the requirements of this Law, it shall rule to approve it within thirty days from the date of receiving the application, terminate the reorganization proceedings, and make a public announcement.

Article 87 If some voting groups do not pass the draft reorganization plan, the debtor or the administrator may negotiate with the voting groups that did not pass it. The voting group may vote again after negotiation. The result of the negotiation shall not harm the interests of other voting groups.

If the voting group that did not pass the draft reorganization plan refuses to vote again or fails to pass the draft reorganization plan after a second vote, but the draft reorganization plan meets the following conditions, the debtor or the administrator may apply to the people's court for approval of the draft reorganization plan:

(1) Under the draft reorganization plan, the claims listed in Item (1) of Paragraph 1 of Article 82 of this Law will be fully satisfied from the specific property, the losses suffered due to delayed satisfaction will be fairly compensated, and the security interests have not been substantially impaired, or the voting group has already passed the draft reorganization plan;
(2) Under the draft reorganization plan, the claims listed in Items (2) and (3) of Paragraph 1 of Article 82 of this Law will be fully satisfied, or the corresponding voting group has already passed the draft reorganization plan;
(3) Under the draft reorganization plan, the satisfaction ratio for ordinary claims is not lower than the ratio they would receive under bankruptcy liquidation proceedings at the time the draft reorganization plan is submitted for approval, or the voting group has already passed the draft reorganization plan;
(4) The adjustment of investors' rights and interests in the draft reorganization plan is fair and equitable, or the investor group has already passed the draft reorganization plan;
(5) The draft reorganization plan treats members of the same voting group fairly, and the order of claim satisfaction specified therein does not violate the provisions of Article 113 of this Law;
(6) The debtor's business plan is feasible.

If the people's court, upon examination, finds that the draft reorganization plan meets the conditions specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall rule to approve it within thirty days from the date of receiving the application, terminate the reorganization proceedings, and make a public announcement.

Article 88 If the draft reorganization plan is not adopted and is not approved in accordance with Article 87 of this Law, or if the adopted reorganization plan is not approved, the people's court shall rule to terminate the reorganization proceedings and declare the debtor bankrupt.

### Section 3 Execution of the Reorganization Plan

Article 89 The reorganization plan shall be executed by the debtor.

After the people's court rules to approve the reorganization plan, the administrator who has taken over the property and business affairs shall transfer the property and business affairs back to the debtor.

Article 90 From the date the people's court rules to approve the reorganization plan, during the supervision period specified in the reorganization plan, the administrator shall supervise the execution of the reorganization plan.

During the supervision period, the debtor shall report to the administrator on the execution of the reorganization plan and the debtor's financial condition.

Article 91 Upon the expiry of the supervision period, the administrator shall submit a supervision report to the people's court. From the date the supervision report is submitted, the administrator's supervision duties shall terminate.

Interested parties in the reorganization plan have the right to inspect the supervision report submitted by the administrator to the people's court.

Upon the administrator's application, the people's court may rule to extend the supervision period for the execution of the reorganization plan.

Article 92 A reorganization plan approved by the people's court's ruling is binding on the debtor and all creditors.

Creditors who have not declared their claims in accordance with this Law shall not exercise their rights during the execution period of the reorganization plan; after the execution of the reorganization plan is completed, they may exercise their rights in accordance with the satisfaction conditions for similar claims specified in the reorganization plan.

The rights of creditors against the debtor's guarantors and other joint debtors shall not be affected by the reorganization plan.

Article 93 If the debtor is unable or fails to execute the reorganization plan, the people's court, upon the request of the administrator or an interested party, shall rule to terminate the execution of the reorganization plan and declare the debtor bankrupt.

If the people's court rules to terminate the execution of the reorganization plan, the commitments made by creditors in the reorganization plan regarding the adjustment of claims shall lose effect. The satisfaction received by creditors due to the execution of the reorganization plan shall remain valid, and the unsatisfied portion of the claims shall be treated as bankruptcy claims.

Creditors referred to in the preceding paragraph may continue to receive distributions only when other creditors of the same rank have received the same proportion of satisfaction as they have.

In the circumstances specified in Paragraph 1 of this Article, the guarantees provided for the execution of the reorganization plan shall remain valid.

Article 94 Debts reduced or exempted in accordance with the reorganization plan shall no longer be borne by the debtor from the time the execution of the reorganization plan is completed.

## Chapter IX Composition

Article 95 The debtor may, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, directly apply to the people's court for composition; or may apply to the people's court for composition after the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application but before declaring the debtor bankrupt.

When applying for composition, the debtor shall submit a draft composition agreement.

Article 96 If the people's court, upon examination, finds that the composition application meets the requirements of this Law, it shall rule to approve the composition, make a public announcement, and convene a creditors' meeting to discuss the draft composition agreement.

Holders of security interests in the specific property of the debtor may exercise their rights from the date the people's court rules to approve the composition.

Article 97 A resolution of the creditors' meeting to pass the composition agreement shall be passed by a majority of more than half of the creditors with the right to vote present at the meeting, and the amount of claims they represent shall account for two-thirds or more of the total amount of unsecured claims.

Article 98 If the creditors' meeting passes the composition agreement, the people's court shall confirm it by ruling, terminate the composition proceedings, and make a public announcement. The administrator shall transfer the property and business affairs to the debtor and submit a report on the performance of its duties to the people's court.

Article 99 If the draft composition agreement is not passed by the creditors' meeting, or if the composition agreement passed by the creditors' meeting is not confirmed by the people's court, the people's court shall rule to terminate the composition proceedings and declare the debtor bankrupt.

Article 100 A composition agreement confirmed by the people's court's ruling is binding on the debtor and all composition creditors.

Composition creditors refer to persons who hold unsecured claims against the debtor at the time the people's court accepts the bankruptcy application.

Composition creditors who have not declared their claims in accordance with this Law shall not exercise their rights during the execution period of the composition agreement; after the execution of the composition agreement is completed, they may exercise their rights in accordance with the satisfaction conditions specified in the composition agreement.

Article 101 The rights of composition creditors against the debtor's guarantors and other joint debtors shall not be affected by the composition agreement.

Article 102 The debtor shall satisfy its debts in accordance with the conditions specified in the composition agreement.

Article 103 If a composition agreement is established due to the debtor's fraud or other illegal acts, the people's court shall rule it invalid and declare the debtor bankrupt.

In the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the satisfaction received by composition creditors due to the execution of the composition agreement shall not be returned to the extent that it does not exceed the proportion of satisfaction received by other creditors.

Article 104 If the debtor is unable or fails to execute the composition agreement, the people's court, upon the request of a composition creditor, shall rule to terminate the execution of the composition agreement and declare the debtor bankrupt.

If the people's court rules to terminate the execution of the composition agreement, the commitments made by composition creditors in the composition agreement regarding the adjustment of claims shall lose effect. The satisfaction received by composition creditors due to the execution of the composition agreement shall remain valid, and the unsatisfied portion of the composition claims shall be treated as bankruptcy claims.A creditor as described in the preceding paragraph may only continue to receive distributions after other creditors have received repayment at the same proportion as the creditor itself.

Where the circumstances described in the first paragraph of this Article exist, any security provided for the performance of the composition agreement shall remain valid.

Article 105 After the People's Court accepts an application for bankruptcy, if the debtor and all creditors reach a voluntary agreement on the handling of claims and debts, they may request the People's Court to rule on the recognition of the agreement and conclude the bankruptcy proceedings.

Article 106 Debts reduced in accordance with a composition agreement shall be discharged by the debtor from the time the composition agreement is fully performed.

## Chapter 10: Bankruptcy Liquidation

### Section 1: Bankruptcy Adjudication

Article 107 Where the People's Court declares the debtor bankrupt in accordance with the provisions of this Law, it shall serve the ruling on the debtor and the administrator within five days from the date the ruling is made, notify known creditors within ten days from the date the ruling is made, and make a public announcement.

After the debtor is declared bankrupt, the debtor shall be referred to as the bankrupt, the debtor's property shall be referred to as the bankruptcy estate, and the claims held against the debtor at the time the People's Court accepts the bankruptcy application shall be referred to as bankruptcy claims.

Article 108 Before the bankruptcy adjudication, under any of the following circumstances, the People's Court shall rule to conclude the bankruptcy proceedings and make a public announcement:

(1) A third party provides full security for the debtor or pays off all matured debts of the debtor;
(2) The debtor has paid off all matured debts.

Article 109 A holder of a security interest in specific property of the bankrupt shall have the right to be satisfied from that specific property with priority.

Article 110 Where a creditor entitled to the rights under Article 109 of this Law is not fully satisfied after exercising the right to priority satisfaction, the unsatisfied portion of its claim shall be treated as an ordinary claim; if the creditor waives the right to priority satisfaction, its claim shall be treated as an ordinary claim.

### Section 2: Realization and Distribution

Article 111 The administrator shall promptly formulate a plan for the realization of the bankruptcy estate and submit it to the creditors' meeting for discussion.

The administrator shall realize and sell the bankruptcy estate in a timely manner in accordance with the realization plan adopted by the creditors' meeting or ruled by the People's Court pursuant to the first paragraph of Article 65 of this Law.

Article 112 The realization and sale of the bankruptcy estate shall be conducted by auction, unless the creditors' meeting decides otherwise.

The bankrupt enterprise may be sold in whole or in part. When the enterprise is sold, its intangible assets and other property may be sold separately.

Property that cannot be auctioned or whose transfer is restricted according to state regulations shall be disposed of in accordance with state regulations.

Article 113 After the bankruptcy estate has been used to pay off the bankruptcy expenses and debts of common interest in priority, it shall be distributed in the following order:

(1) Wages, medical expenses, disability subsidies, and bereavement expenses owed by the bankrupt to its employees, basic pension insurance and basic medical insurance premiums that should be credited to the employees' personal accounts, and compensation payable to employees as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations;
(2) Social insurance premiums owed by the bankrupt other than those specified in the preceding item, and taxes owed by the bankrupt;
(3) Ordinary bankruptcy claims.

If the bankruptcy estate is insufficient to satisfy all claims within the same order, distribution shall be made on a pro rata basis.

The wages of directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of the bankrupt enterprise shall be calculated based on the average wage of the enterprise's employees.

Article 114 Distribution of the bankruptcy estate shall be made in monetary form, unless the creditors' meeting decides otherwise.

Article 115 The administrator shall promptly formulate a plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate and submit it to the creditors' meeting for discussion.

The plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate shall specify the following:

(1) The names or titles and addresses of the creditors participating in the distribution of the bankruptcy estate;
(2) The amounts of claims participating in the distribution of the bankruptcy estate;
(3) The amount of the bankruptcy estate available for distribution;
(4) The order, proportions, and amounts of the distribution of the bankruptcy estate;
(5) The method for implementing the distribution of the bankruptcy estate.

After the creditors' meeting adopts the plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate, the administrator shall submit the plan to the People's Court for a ruling on its recognition.

Article 116 After the plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate is recognized by a ruling of the People's Court, it shall be executed by the administrator.

If the administrator makes multiple distributions in accordance with the plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate, the amount of property and the amount of claims distributed in each distribution shall be announced. When the administrator makes the final distribution, it shall be indicated in the announcement, and the matters specified in the second paragraph of Article 117 of this Law shall be stated.

Article 117 For claims subject to a condition precedent or a condition subsequent, the administrator shall deposit the distribution share.

The distribution share deposited by the administrator in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall, if the condition precedent is not fulfilled or the condition subsequent is fulfilled on the date of the final distribution announcement, be distributed to other creditors; if the condition precedent is fulfilled or the condition subsequent is not fulfilled on that date, it shall be delivered to the creditor.

Article 118 If a creditor fails to collect its distribution share from the bankruptcy estate, the administrator shall deposit it. If the creditor fails to collect it within two months from the date of the final distribution announcement, it shall be deemed to have waived the right to receive the distribution, and the administrator or the People's Court shall distribute the deposited share to other creditors.

Article 119 At the time of distribution of the bankruptcy estate, for claims pending litigation or arbitration, the administrator shall deposit the distribution share. If the distribution cannot be collected within two years from the date of conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings, the People's Court shall distribute the deposited share to other creditors.

### Section 3: Conclusion of Bankruptcy Proceedings

Article 120 If the bankrupt has no property available for distribution, the administrator shall request the People's Court to rule on the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings.

After the final distribution is completed, the administrator shall promptly submit a report on the distribution of the bankruptcy estate to the People's Court and request the People's Court to rule on the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings.

The People's Court shall, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the administrator's request to conclude the bankruptcy proceedings, make a ruling on whether to conclude them. If the ruling is to conclude, a public announcement shall be made.

Article 121 The administrator shall, within ten days from the date of conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings, take the ruling of the People's Court on the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings to the original registration authority of the bankrupt to handle the cancellation of registration.

Article 122 The administrator shall terminate its duties on the day following the completion of the cancellation of registration, unless there are pending litigation or arbitration matters.

Article 123 Within two years from the date of conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings in accordance with the fourth paragraph of Article 43 or Article 120 of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, creditors may request the People's Court to make additional distributions in accordance with the plan for the distribution of the bankruptcy estate:

(1) Property is discovered that should be recovered in accordance with Articles 31, 32, 33, and 36 of this Law;
(2) Other property that the bankrupt should have made available for distribution is discovered.

If the circumstances in the preceding paragraph exist, but the amount of property is insufficient to cover the costs of distribution, no additional distribution shall be made, and the People's Court shall turn the property over to the state treasury.

Article 124 The guarantors and other joint and several obligors of the bankrupt shall, after the conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings, continue to bear liability for satisfaction of the claims of creditors that were not satisfied in the bankruptcy liquidation proceedings, in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 11: Legal Liability

Article 125 If a director, supervisor, or senior management personnel of an enterprise violates their duties of loyalty and diligence, causing the enterprise to become bankrupt, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Persons falling under the circumstances described in the preceding paragraph shall not serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of any enterprise for three years from the date of conclusion of the bankruptcy proceedings.

Article 126 If a relevant person of the debtor who is obligated to attend the creditors' meeting, after being summoned by the People's Court, refuses to attend the creditors' meeting without justifiable reasons, the People's Court may issue a compulsory summons and impose a fine in accordance with the law. If a relevant person of the debtor, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses to make statements or answer questions, or makes false statements or answers, the People's Court may impose a fine in accordance with the law.

Article 127 If the debtor, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses to submit to the People's Court, or submits untrue, statements of property status, lists of debts, lists of claims, relevant financial and accounting reports, and information on the payment of employee wages and social insurance premiums, the People's Court may impose a fine on the directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

If the debtor, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses to hand over property, seals, account books, documents, and other materials to the administrator, or forges or destroys relevant property evidence materials, causing the property status to be unclear, the People's Court may impose a fine on the directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

Article 128 If the debtor engages in conduct specified in Articles 31, 32, and 33 of this Law, harming the interests of creditors, the legal representative and other directly responsible persons of the debtor shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 129 If a relevant person of the debtor, in violation of the provisions of this Law, leaves their place of domicile without authorization, the People's Court may issue a reprimand, order detention, and may also impose a fine in accordance with the law.

Article 130 If the administrator fails to diligently and faithfully perform its duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the People's Court may impose a fine in accordance with the law; if losses are caused to creditors, the debtor, or third parties, the administrator shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 131 If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 12: Supplementary Provisions

Article 132 After this Law comes into effect, for the portion of wages, medical expenses, disability subsidies, and bereavement expenses owed by the bankrupt to its employees before the date of promulgation of this Law, and the basic pension insurance and basic medical insurance premiums that should be credited to the employees' personal accounts, and compensation payable to employees as stipulated by laws and administrative regulations, which remains unsatisfied after distribution in accordance with Article 113 of this Law, such portion shall be satisfied from the specific property specified in Article 109 of this Law with priority over the holders of security interests in that specific property.

Article 133 Special matters concerning the bankruptcy of state-owned enterprises within the period and scope prescribed by the State Council before the implementation of this Law shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 134 Where a financial institution such as a commercial bank, securities company, or insurance company falls under the circumstances specified in Article 2 of this Law, the financial regulatory authority of the State Council may file an application with the People's Court for reorganization or bankruptcy liquidation of that financial institution. Where the financial regulatory authority of the State Council takes measures such as receivership or trusteeship over a financial institution that has significant operational risks in accordance with the law, it may apply to the People's Court for a stay of civil proceedings or enforcement proceedings in which the financial institution is the defendant or the person subject to enforcement.

Where a financial institution implements bankruptcy, the State Council may formulate implementation measures in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws.

Article 135 Where the liquidation of an organization other than an enterprise legal person is governed by other laws and constitutes bankruptcy liquidation, the procedures stipulated in this Law shall apply mutatis mutandis.

Article 136 This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2007, and the "Enterprise Bankruptcy Law of the People's Republic of China (Trial Implementation)" shall be simultaneously repealed.
