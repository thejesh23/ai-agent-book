# Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 7, 1990

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 27, 2001

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on February 26, 2010

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China" adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on November 11, 2020

Article 1 This Law is enacted, in accordance with the Constitution, to protect the copyright of authors in their literary, artistic and scientific works and the rights related to copyright, to encourage the creation and dissemination of works that contribute to the building of a socialist society that is advanced materially and culturally, and to promote the development and flourishing of socialist culture and science.

Article 2 Works of Chinese citizens, legal persons or unincorporated organizations, whether published or not, shall enjoy copyright in accordance with this Law.

Copyright enjoyed by foreigners and stateless persons in their works under an agreement concluded between the country to which the author belongs or in which the author has his habitual residence and China, or under an international treaty to which both countries are parties, shall be protected by this Law.

Works of foreigners or stateless persons first published in the territory of China shall enjoy copyright in accordance with this Law.

Works of authors from countries that have not concluded an agreement with China or that are not parties to an international treaty to which China is a party, as well as works of stateless persons, first published in a member country of an international treaty to which China is a party, or simultaneously published in a member country and a non-member country, shall be protected by this Law.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "works" means intellectual achievements that are original and can be expressed in a certain form in the fields of literature, art and science, including:

(1) written works;

(2) oral works;

(3) musical, dramatic, quyi, choreographic and acrobatic works;

(4) works of fine art and architecture;

(5) photographic works;

(6) audiovisual works;

(7) graphic works such as engineering design drawings, product design drawings, maps and sketches, and model works;

(8) computer software;

(9) other intellectual achievements that meet the characteristics of works.

Article 4 Copyright owners and owners of rights related to copyright shall not, when exercising their rights, violate the Constitution or laws, nor harm the public interest. The State shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and administer the publication and dissemination of works.

Article 5 This Law shall not apply to:

(1) laws, regulations, resolutions, decisions and orders of state organs of a legislative, administrative or judicial nature, and their official translations;

(2) mere factual news;

(3) calendars, numerical tables, general tables and formulas.

Article 6 Measures for the protection of copyright in works of folk literature and art shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Article 7 The copyright administrative department of the State shall be responsible for the administration of copyright nationwide; the departments responsible for copyright administration at or above the county level shall be responsible for the administration of copyright within their respective administrative regions.

Article 8 Copyright owners and owners of rights related to copyright may authorize a copyright collective administration organization to exercise their copyright or rights related to copyright. A copyright collective administration organization established in accordance with the law is a non-profit legal person. After being authorized, it may, in its own name, claim rights for copyright owners and owners of rights related to copyright, and may, as a party, participate in litigation, arbitration and mediation activities involving copyright or rights related to copyright.

A copyright collective administration organization shall, based on the authorization, collect royalties from users. The standards for collecting royalties shall be determined through consultation between the copyright collective administration organization and representatives of users. If consultation fails, an application may be made to the copyright administrative department of the State for a ruling. If a party is dissatisfied with the ruling, it may file a lawsuit with a people's court; the parties may also directly file a lawsuit with a people's court.

A copyright collective administration organization shall regularly disclose to the public the overall situation of the collection and distribution of royalties, the deduction and use of management fees, and the undistributed portion of royalties, and shall establish a rights information inquiry system for rights holders and users to inquire. The copyright administrative department of the State shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and administer copyright collective administration organizations.

The methods of establishment, rights and obligations, collection and distribution of royalties, and supervision and administration of copyright collective administration organizations shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter II Copyright

### Section 1 Copyright Owners and Their Rights

Article 9 Copyright owners include:

(1) authors;

(2) other natural persons, legal persons or unincorporated organizations that enjoy copyright in accordance with this Law.

Article 10 Copyright includes the following moral rights and property rights:

(1) the right of publication, that is, the right to decide whether to make the work public;

(2) the right of authorship, that is, the right to claim authorship and to have the author's name mentioned in connection with the work;

(3) the right of alteration, that is, the right to alter or authorize others to alter the work;

(4) the right of integrity, that is, the right to protect the work against distortion and mutilation;

(5) the right of reproduction, that is, the right to produce one or more copies of the work by means of printing, photocopying, rubbing, sound recording, video recording, re-recording, re-photographing, digitization, or the like;

(6) the right of distribution, that is, the right to provide the original or copies of the work to the public by sale or gift;

(7) the right of rental, that is, the right to authorize others to temporarily use the original or copies of an audiovisual work or computer software for a fee, except where the computer software is not the main subject of the rental;

(8) the right of exhibition, that is, the right to publicly display the original or copies of a work of fine art or a photographic work;

(9) the right of performance, that is, the right to publicly perform the work and to publicly communicate the performance of the work by any means;

(10) the right of projection, that is, the right to publicly reproduce works of fine art, photography and audiovisual works through technical equipment such as projectors and slide projectors;

(11) the right of broadcasting, that is, the right to publicly communicate or rebroadcast the work by wire or wireless means, and to communicate the broadcast of the work to the public by loudspeaker or any other similar instrument transmitting signs, sounds or images, excluding the right provided for in subparagraph (12) of this paragraph;

(12) the right of communication through information network, that is, the right to provide the work to the public by wire or wireless means, so that the public may access the work at a time and place individually chosen by them;

(13) the right of cinematography, that is, the right to fix the work on a carrier by means of making an audiovisual work;

(14) the right of adaptation, that is, the right to change the work and create a new, original work;

(15) the right of translation, that is, the right to convert the work from one language into another;

(16) the right of compilation, that is, the right to compile the work or fragments of the work into a new work through selection or arrangement;

(17) other rights that shall be enjoyed by copyright owners.

Copyright owners may authorize others to exercise the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the preceding paragraph and receive remuneration in accordance with the agreement or the relevant provisions of this Law.

Copyright owners may transfer, in whole or in part, the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of this Article and receive remuneration in accordance with the agreement or the relevant provisions of this Law.

### Section 2 Ownership of Copyright

Article 11 Except where otherwise provided in this Law, copyright belongs to the author.

The natural person who creates the work is the author.

Where a work is created under the auspices and according to the will of a legal person or unincorporated organization, which bears responsibility for the work, that legal person or unincorporated organization shall be deemed the author.

Article 12 The natural person, legal person or unincorporated organization whose name is mentioned in connection with the work shall, in the absence of proof to the contrary, be deemed the author of the work, and the corresponding rights shall exist in the work.

Authors and other copyright owners may register their works with a registration institution recognized by the copyright administrative department of the State.

The provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall apply mutatis mutandis to rights related to copyright.

Article 13 The copyright in a work created by adaptation, translation, annotation or arrangement of a pre-existing work shall be enjoyed by the adapter, translator, annotator or arranger, provided that the exercise of such copyright does not infringe the copyright in the original work.

Article 14 The copyright in a work created jointly by two or more persons shall be enjoyed jointly by the co-authors. A person who has not participated in the creation of the work shall not be deemed a co-author.

The copyright in a joint work shall be exercised by the co-authors through consensus. If consensus cannot be reached and there is no justifiable reason, no co-author shall prevent the others from exercising rights other than the transfer, exclusive licensing or pledge of the work, provided that the proceeds obtained shall be reasonably distributed among all co-authors.

Where a joint work can be used separately, each author may enjoy independent copyright in the part he or she created, provided that the exercise of such copyright does not infringe the copyright in the joint work as a whole.

Article 15 A work created by compiling a number of works, fragments of works, or data or other material that does not constitute a work, where the selection or arrangement of the content reflects originality, is a compilation work. The copyright in such a work shall be enjoyed by the compiler, provided that the exercise of such copyright does not infringe the copyright in the pre-existing works.

Article 16 To publish, perform or produce sound recordings or video recordings of a work created by adapting, translating, annotating, arranging or compiling a pre-existing work, permission shall be obtained from both the copyright owner of the derivative work and the copyright owner of the original work, and remuneration shall be paid.

Article 17 The copyright in a cinematographic work or a television drama work within an audiovisual work shall be enjoyed by the producer, provided that the screenwriter, director, cinematographer, lyricist, composer and other authors shall enjoy the right of authorship and have the right to receive remuneration in accordance with the contract signed with the producer.

The ownership of copyright in audiovisual works other than those provided for in the preceding paragraph shall be agreed upon by the parties; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, the copyright shall be enjoyed by the producer, provided that the authors shall enjoy the right of authorship and the right to receive remuneration.

Authors of works such as scripts and music that can be used separately from an audiovisual work shall have the right to independently exercise their copyright in such works.

Article 18 A work created by a natural person in the course of fulfilling his or her duties for a legal person or unincorporated organization is a work made for hire. Except as provided in the second paragraph of this Article, the copyright in such a work shall be enjoyed by the author, provided that the legal person or unincorporated organization has the right to use the work preferentially within its business scope. Within two years after the completion of the work, the author shall not, without the consent of the unit, authorize a third party to use the work in the same manner as the unit uses it.

In the case of any of the following works made for hire, the author shall enjoy the right of authorship, while the other rights of copyright shall be enjoyed by the legal person or unincorporated organization, which may reward the author:

(1) works made for hire such as engineering design drawings, product design drawings, maps, sketches and computer software, which are created mainly using the material and technical resources of the legal person or unincorporated organization and for which the legal person or unincorporated organization bears responsibility;

(2) works made for hire created by employees of newspapers, periodicals, news agencies, radio stations and television stations;

(3) works made for hire for which the copyright is, by law, administrative regulation or contract, enjoyed by the legal person or unincorporated organization.

Article 19 The ownership of copyright in a work created under commission shall be agreed upon by the commissioning party and the commissioned party in a contract. In the absence of a clear agreement or a contract, the copyright shall belong to the commissioned party.

Article 20 The transfer of ownership of the original copy of a work shall not change the ownership of copyright in the work, provided that the right of exhibition in the original copy of a work of fine art or a photographic work shall be enjoyed by the owner of the original copy.

Where an author transfers the ownership of the original copy of an unpublished work of fine art or a photographic work to another person, the display of that original copy by the transferee shall not constitute an infringement of the author's right of publication.

Article 21 Where copyright belongs to a natural person, after the death of that natural person, the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall, within the term of protection provided for in this Law, be transferred in accordance with the law.

Where copyright belongs to a legal person or unincorporated organization, after the change or termination of that legal person or unincorporated organization, the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall, within the term of protection provided for in this Law, be enjoyed by the legal person or unincorporated organization that succeeds to its rights and obligations; in the absence of such a successor, the rights shall be enjoyed by the State.

### Section 3 Term of Protection of Rights

Article 22 The term of protection of the rights of authorship, alteration and integrity of an author shall be unlimited.

Article 23 In the case of a work of a natural person, the term of protection of the right of publication and the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall be the lifetime of the author and fifty years after his or her death, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the author's death. In the case of a joint work, the term shall expire on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the death of the last surviving author.

In the case of a work of a legal person or unincorporated organization, or a work made for hire the copyright in which (excluding the right of authorship) is enjoyed by a legal person or unincorporated organization, the term of protection of the right of publication shall be fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the completion of the work; the term of protection of the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall be fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the first publication of the work, provided that if the work has not been published within fifty years after its completion, this Law shall no longer protect it.

In the case of an audiovisual work, the term of protection of the right of publication shall be fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the completion of the work; the term of protection of the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall be fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the first publication of the work, provided that if the work has not been published within fifty years after its completion, this Law shall no longer protect it.

### Section 4 Limitations on Rights

Article 24 In the following cases, a work may be used without permission from, and without payment of remuneration to, the copyright owner, provided that the name or title of the author and the title of the work shall be indicated, and the use shall not affect the normal use of the work nor unreasonably prejudice the legitimate rights and interests of the copyright owner:

(1) use of another person's published work for personal study, research or appreciation;

(2) appropriate quotation of another person's published work in one's own work for the purpose of introducing or commenting on a certain work, or explaining a certain point;

(3) inevitable reproduction or quotation of a published work in newspapers, periodicals, radio stations, television stations or other media for the purpose of reporting news;

(4) reprinting or broadcasting by newspapers, periodicals, radio stations, television stations or other media of articles on current events concerning politics, economy or religion that have been published by other newspapers, periodicals, radio stations, television stations or other media, except where the copyright owner has declared that such reprinting or broadcasting is not permitted;

(5) reprinting or broadcasting by newspapers, periodicals, radio stations, television stations or other media of speeches delivered at public gatherings, except where the author has declared that such reprinting or broadcasting is not permitted;

(6) translation, adaptation, compilation, broadcasting or reproduction in a limited number of copies of a published work for use by teachers or researchers in classroom teaching or scientific research, provided that the work shall not be published or distributed;

(7) use of a published work by a state organ within a reasonable scope for the purpose of performing official duties;

(8) reproduction of a work in the collections of a library, archive, memorial hall, museum, art gallery or cultural center for the purpose of display or preservation of a copy;

(9) free performance of a published work, where the performance does not charge any fee to the public, nor pay any remuneration to the performers, and is not for profit;

(10) copying, drawing, photographing or video recording of an artistic work located or displayed in a public place;

(11) translation of a published work created by a Chinese citizen, legal person or unincorporated organization in the national common language into a work in a minority nationality language for publication and distribution within the country;

(12) provision of a published work to persons with print disabilities in an accessible format that they can perceive;

(13) other circumstances provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply to limitations on rights related to copyright.

Article 25 For the purpose of implementing compulsory education and the national education plan, textbooks may compile, without permission from the copyright owner, published fragments of works, short written works, musical works, or single works of fine art, photographic works or graphic works, provided that remuneration shall be paid to the copyright owner in accordance with the regulations, the name or title of the author and the title of the work shall be indicated, and the other rights enjoyed by the copyright owner under this Law shall not be infringed.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply to limitations on rights related to copyright.

## Chapter III Contracts on Copyright Licensing and Transfer

Article 26 A person who uses another person's work shall conclude a licensing contract with the copyright owner, except where this Law provides that permission is not required.

A licensing contract shall include the following main contents:

(1) the category of rights licensed;

(2) whether the licensed right is an exclusive or non-exclusive right of use;

(3) the territorial scope and term of the license;

(4) the standards and methods of remuneration;

(5) liability for breach of contract;

(6) other contents that the parties consider necessary to agree upon.

Article 27 A contract for the transfer of the rights provided for in subparagraphs (5) through (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law shall be concluded in writing.

A contract for the transfer of rights shall include the following main contents:

(1) the title of the work;

(2) the category and territorial scope of the rights transferred;

(3) the transfer price;

(4) the date and method of payment of the transfer price;

(5) liability for breach of contract;

(6) other contents that the parties consider necessary to agree upon.

Article 28 Where a property right in copyright is pledged, the pledgor and the pledgee shall register the pledge in accordance with the law.

Article 29 In a licensing or transfer contract, if the copyright owner has not clearly licensed or transferred a right, the other party shall not exercise that right without the consent of the copyright owner.

Article 30 The standards for remuneration for the use of a work may be agreed upon by the parties, or may be paid in accordance with the standards formulated by the copyright administrative department of the State jointly with the relevant departments. If the agreement between the parties is unclear, remuneration shall be paid in accordance with the standards formulated by the copyright administrative department of the State jointly with the relevant departments.

Article 31 Publishers, performers, producers of sound recordings and video recordings, radio stations and television stations, among others, who use another person's work in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law, shall not infringe the author's rights of authorship, alteration, integrity and the right to receive remuneration.

## Chapter IV Rights Related to Copyright

### Section 1 Publication of Books, Newspapers and Periodicals

Article 32 A book publisher shall conclude a publishing contract with the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

Article 33 The exclusive right of publication enjoyed by a book publisher in a work delivered by the copyright owner in accordance with the contract shall be protected by law, and no other person shall publish the work.

Article 34 The copyright owner shall deliver the work within the time limit agreed upon in the contract. The book publisher shall publish the book in accordance with the quality and time limit agreed upon in the contract.If a book publisher fails to publish the work within the time limit agreed upon in the contract, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with Article 61 of this Law.

When a book publisher reprints or republishes a work, it shall notify the copyright owner and pay remuneration. If the book publisher refuses to reprint or republish a work that is out of print, the copyright owner has the right to terminate the contract.

**Article 35** Where a copyright owner submits a contribution to a newspaper or periodical publisher, if no notice of acceptance is received from the newspaper publisher within fifteen days from the date of dispatch, or from the periodical publisher within thirty days from the date of dispatch, the copyright owner may submit the same work to another newspaper or periodical publisher, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

After a work is published, other newspapers or periodicals may reprint it or publish it as an abstract or reference material, unless the copyright owner has declared that reprinting or excerpting is not permitted, provided that remuneration is paid to the copyright owner in accordance with regulations.

**Article 36** A book publisher may modify or abridge a work with the permission of the author.

Newspaper and periodical publishers may make textual modifications or abridgments to a work. Modifications to the content shall be subject to the author's permission.

**Article 37** A publisher has the right to permit or prohibit others from using the typographical design of the books or periodicals it publishes.

The term of protection for the right specified in the preceding paragraph is ten years, expiring on December 31 of the tenth year after the first publication of the book or periodical using that typographical design.

### Section 2: Performance

**Article 38** Where a performer performs a work created by another person, the performer shall obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration. If a performance is organized by a performance organizer, that organizer shall obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

**Article 39** A performer shall enjoy the following rights with respect to their performance:

(1) The right to claim their identity as the performer;
(2) The right to protect the performance image from distortion;
(3) The right to authorize others to broadcast and publicly transmit their live performance, and to receive remuneration therefor;
(4) The right to authorize others to make audio and video recordings, and to receive remuneration therefor;
(5) The right to authorize others to reproduce, distribute, and rent out audio and video recordings containing their performance, and to receive remuneration therefor;
(6) The right to authorize others to communicate their performance to the public via information networks, and to receive remuneration therefor.

A licensee who uses the work in the manners specified in items (3) to (6) of the preceding paragraph shall also obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

**Article 40** A performance carried out by a performer to fulfill the performance tasks of their employing unit is considered a work-made-for-hire performance. The performer shall enjoy the right to claim their identity and the right to protect the performance image from distortion. The ownership of other rights shall be determined by agreement between the parties. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the rights to the work-made-for-hire performance shall be enjoyed by the employing unit.

If the rights to the work-made-for-hire performance are enjoyed by the performer, the employing unit may use the performance free of charge within the scope of its business.

**Article 41** The term of protection for the rights specified in items (1) and (2) of the first paragraph of Article 39 of this Law is not limited.

The term of protection for the rights specified in items (3) to (6) of the first paragraph of Article 39 of this Law is fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the performance occurred.

### Section 3: Audio and Video Recordings

**Article 42** A producer of audio or video recordings who uses a work created by another person to make an audio or video recording shall obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

A producer of audio recordings who uses a musical work that has been lawfully recorded as an audio recording by another person to make a new audio recording may do so without obtaining permission from the copyright owner, but shall pay remuneration in accordance with regulations; however, if the copyright owner has declared that such use is not permitted, it shall not be used.

**Article 43** A producer of audio or video recordings shall enter into a contract with the performer and pay remuneration when making an audio or video recording.

**Article 44** A producer of audio or video recordings shall have the right to authorize others to reproduce, distribute, rent out, and communicate the audio or video recording to the public via information networks, and to receive remuneration therefor. The term of protection for this right is fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the recording was first made.

A licensee who reproduces, distributes, or communicates an audio or video recording to the public via information networks shall also obtain permission from the copyright owner and the performer, and pay remuneration. A licensee who rents out an audio or video recording shall also obtain permission from the performer and pay remuneration.

**Article 45** Where an audio recording is used for public broadcast via wire or wireless means, or is publicly transmitted to the public through technical equipment for transmitting sound, remuneration shall be paid to the producer of the audio recording.

### Section 4: Broadcasting by Radio and Television Stations

**Article 46** A radio or television station that broadcasts an unpublished work of another person shall obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

A radio or television station that broadcasts a published work of another person may do so without obtaining permission from the copyright owner, but shall pay remuneration in accordance with regulations.

**Article 47** A radio or television station has the right to prohibit the following acts without its permission:

(1) Rebroadcasting its broadcast by wire or wireless means;
(2) Recording and reproducing its broadcast;
(3) Communicating its broadcast to the public via information networks.

When exercising the rights specified in the preceding paragraph, a radio or television station shall not affect, restrict, or infringe upon the exercise of copyright or related rights by others.

The term of protection for the rights specified in the first paragraph of this Article is fifty years, expiring on December 31 of the fiftieth year after the first broadcast.

**Article 48** A television station that broadcasts an audiovisual work or video recording of another person shall obtain permission from the copyright owner of the audiovisual work or the producer of the video recording, and pay remuneration. When broadcasting a video recording of another person, it shall also obtain permission from the copyright owner and pay remuneration.

## Chapter V: Protection of Copyright and Related Rights

**Article 49** To protect copyright and related rights, right holders may adopt technological measures.

Without the permission of the right holder, no organization or individual may intentionally circumvent or destroy technological measures, manufacture, import, or provide devices or components to the public for the purpose of circumventing or destroying technological measures, or intentionally provide technical services to others for circumventing or destroying technological measures, except as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

For the purposes of this Law, "technological measures" refer to effective technologies, devices, or components used to prevent or restrict the browsing or appreciation of works, performances, audio or video recordings, or the provision of works, performances, or audio or video recordings to the public via information networks without the permission of the right holder.

**Article 50** Technological measures may be circumvented in the following circumstances, provided that the technology, devices, or components for circumventing the technological measures are not provided to others, and other rights lawfully enjoyed by the right holder are not infringed upon:

(1) Providing a small number of published works for use by teaching or research personnel for classroom teaching or scientific research in schools, where the works cannot be obtained through normal channels;
(2) Providing published works to persons with print disabilities in an accessible format perceptible to them, without profit-making purposes, where the works cannot be obtained through normal channels;
(3) State organs performing official duties in accordance with administrative, supervisory, or judicial procedures;
(4) Testing the security performance of a computer, its system, or network;
(5) Conducting encryption research or reverse engineering research on computer software.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph apply to limitations on rights related to copyright.

**Article 51** Without the permission of the right holder, the following acts shall not be performed:

(1) Intentionally deleting or altering rights management information on works, typographical designs, performances, audio or video recordings, or broadcasts, unless unavoidable due to technical reasons;
(2) Providing to the public works, typographical designs, performances, audio or video recordings, or broadcasts, knowing or having reason to know that the rights management information on them has been deleted or altered without permission.

**Article 52** The following acts of infringement shall, depending on the circumstances, result in civil liability such as cessation of infringement, elimination of adverse effects, apology, and compensation for losses:

(1) Publishing a work without the permission of the copyright owner;
(2) Publishing a work created jointly with others as one's own sole creation without the permission of the co-authors;
(3) Signing one's name on another person's work for personal gain without having participated in the creation;
(4) Distorting or tampering with another person's work;
(5) Plagiarizing another person's work;
(6) Using a work by means of exhibition, making an audiovisual work, or by adaptation, translation, annotation, etc., without the permission of the copyright owner, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(7) Using another person's work without paying the remuneration due;
(8) Renting out the original or copies of a work or audio or video recording without the permission of the copyright owner, performer, or producer of the audiovisual work, computer software, or audio or video recording, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(9) Using the typographical design of a book or periodical published by another person without the permission of the publisher;
(10) Broadcasting live or publicly transmitting a live performance, or recording a performance, without the permission of the performer;
(11) Other acts infringing upon copyright and related rights.

**Article 53** The following acts of infringement shall, depending on the circumstances, result in the civil liability specified in Article 52 of this Law; if the infringement also harms the public interest, the copyright administration department shall order the cessation of the infringement, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains, confiscate and destroy the infringing copies and the materials, tools, equipment, etc., mainly used for making the infringing copies, and if the illegal business turnover is 50,000 RMB or more, may also impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal business turnover; if there is no illegal business turnover, the illegal business turnover is difficult to calculate, or it is less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not more than 250,000 RMB may be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Reproducing, distributing, performing, screening, broadcasting, compiling, or communicating a work to the public via information networks without the permission of the copyright owner, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(2) Publishing a book for which another person has an exclusive right of publication;
(3) Reproducing or distributing audio or video recordings containing a performance, or communicating the performance to the public via information networks, without the permission of the performer, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(4) Reproducing, distributing, or communicating an audio or video recording to the public via information networks without the permission of the producer of the audio or video recording, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(5) Broadcasting, reproducing, or communicating a broadcast to the public via information networks without permission, except as otherwise provided in this Law;
(6) Intentionally circumventing or destroying technological measures, intentionally manufacturing, importing, or providing devices or components mainly used for circumventing or destroying technological measures to others, or intentionally providing technical services to others for circumventing or destroying technological measures, without the permission of the copyright owner or related right holder, except as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations;
(7) Intentionally deleting or altering rights management information on works, typographical designs, performances, audio or video recordings, or broadcasts, or providing to the public works, typographical designs, performances, audio or video recordings, or broadcasts, knowing or having reason to know that the rights management information on them has been deleted or altered without permission, without the permission of the copyright owner or related right holder, except as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations;
(8) Making or selling works bearing the forged signature of another person.

**Article 54** Where copyright or related rights are infringed, the infringer shall compensate based on the actual losses suffered by the right holder or the illegal gains of the infringer; if the actual losses of the right holder or the illegal gains of the infringer are difficult to calculate, compensation may be based on the royalty for the use of the right. For intentional infringement of copyright or related rights, if the circumstances are serious, compensation may be awarded at an amount between one and five times the amount determined by the methods above.

If the actual losses of the right holder, the illegal gains of the infringer, and the royalty for the use of the right are difficult to calculate, the people's court shall, based on the circumstances of the infringement, award compensation of not less than 500 RMB but not more than 5,000,000 RMB.

The amount of compensation shall also include the reasonable expenses incurred by the right holder to stop the infringement.

To determine the amount of compensation, if the right holder has fulfilled the necessary burden of proof, and the books, materials, etc., related to the infringement are mainly in the possession of the infringer, the people's court may order the infringer to provide the books, materials, etc., related to the infringement; if the infringer fails to provide them or provides false books, materials, etc., the people's court may determine the amount of compensation with reference to the right holder's claims and the evidence provided.

When hearing a copyright dispute case, the people's court shall, upon the request of the right holder, order the destruction of infringing copies, except in special circumstances; order the destruction of materials, tools, equipment, etc., mainly used for making the infringing copies, without compensation; or, in special circumstances, order that the aforementioned materials, tools, equipment, etc., be prohibited from entering commercial channels, without compensation.

**Article 55** When investigating suspected acts of infringement of copyright and related rights, the copyright administration department may question the relevant parties, investigate the circumstances related to the suspected illegal acts; conduct on-site inspections of the premises and articles involved in the suspected illegal acts; consult and copy contracts, invoices, books, and other relevant materials related to the suspected illegal acts; and may seal up or seize the premises and articles involved in the suspected illegal acts.

When the copyright administration department lawfully exercises the powers specified in the preceding paragraph, the parties concerned shall provide assistance and cooperation and shall not refuse or obstruct.

**Article 56** If a copyright owner or related right holder has evidence that another person is committing or is about to commit an act that infringes upon their rights or hinders the realization of their rights, and that failure to stop it promptly will cause irreparable damage to their lawful rights and interests, they may, before filing a lawsuit, apply to the people's court for measures such as property preservation, ordering a specific act to be performed, or prohibiting a specific act, in accordance with the law.

**Article 57** To stop an infringement, if evidence is likely to be lost or difficult to obtain later, the copyright owner or related right holder may, before filing a lawsuit, apply to the people's court for evidence preservation in accordance with the law.

**Article 58** When hearing a case, the people's court may confiscate illegal gains, infringing copies, and property used for illegal activities in cases of infringement of copyright or related rights.

**Article 59** If the publisher or producer of a copy cannot prove that its publication or production was legally authorized, or if the distributor of a copy or the renter of a copy of an audiovisual work, computer software, or audio or video recording cannot prove that the copy it distributed or rented has a legal source, it shall bear legal liability.

In litigation proceedings, if the alleged infringer claims that it does not bear infringement liability, it shall provide evidence proving that it has obtained permission from the right holder or that there are circumstances under this Law allowing use without the permission of the right holder.

**Article 60** Copyright disputes may be mediated, or may be submitted to an arbitration institution for arbitration based on a written arbitration agreement reached by the parties or an arbitration clause in the copyright contract.

If the parties have neither a written arbitration agreement nor an arbitration clause in the copyright contract, they may directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

**Article 61** The provisions of relevant laws shall apply to the civil liability of a party for failing to perform contractual obligations or for performing contractual obligations not in conformity with the agreement, as well as to the exercise of litigation rights and the application for preservation by the parties.

## Chapter VI: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 62** For the purposes of this Law, "copyright" is synonymous with "author's right."

**Article 63** "Publication" as referred to in Article 2 of this Law means the reproduction and distribution of a work.

**Article 64** The measures for the protection of computer software and the right of communication through information networks shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

**Article 65** For photographic works, if the term of protection for the right of publication and the rights specified in items (5) to (17) of the first paragraph of Article 10 of this Law had already expired before June 1, 2021, but were still within the term of protection according to the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 23 of this Law, they shall no longer be protected.

**Article 66** The rights of copyright owners, publishers, performers, producers of audio and video recordings, and radio and television stations as provided for in this Law, which had not yet expired under this Law on the date of its entry into force, shall be protected in accordance with this Law.

Acts of infringement or breach of contract that occurred before the entry into force of this Law shall be dealt with in accordance with the relevant provisions in effect at the time of the act.

**Article 67** This Law shall come into force on June 1, 1991.
