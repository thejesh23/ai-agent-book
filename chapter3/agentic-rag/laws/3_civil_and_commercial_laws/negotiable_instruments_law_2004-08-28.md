# Negotiable Instruments Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 13th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 10, 1995

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Negotiable Instruments Law of the People's Republic of China at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating negotiable instrument activities, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of parties involved in such activities, maintaining the social and economic order, and promoting the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 Negotiable instrument activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "negotiable instruments" means drafts, promissory notes, and checks.

Article 3 Negotiable instrument activities shall comply with laws and administrative regulations and shall not harm the public interest.

Article 4 The drawer of a negotiable instrument shall sign or seal the instrument in accordance with statutory conditions and shall bear liability on the instrument according to the matters recorded thereon.

The holder, when exercising rights on the instrument, shall sign or seal the instrument in accordance with statutory procedures and present the instrument.

Other obligors on the instrument who sign or seal the instrument shall bear liability on the instrument according to the matters recorded thereon.

For the purposes of this Law, "rights on a negotiable instrument" means the right of the holder to demand payment of the sum specified in the instrument from the obligor on the instrument, including the right to claim payment and the right of recourse.

For the purposes of this Law, "liability on a negotiable instrument" means the obligation of the obligor on the instrument to pay the sum specified in the instrument to the holder.

Article 5 A party to a negotiable instrument may authorize an agent to sign or seal the instrument on its behalf, and the agency relationship shall be indicated on the instrument.

If a person signs or seals the instrument in the name of an agent without having the authority to do so, the signer shall bear liability on the instrument; if an agent exceeds the scope of authority, the agent shall bear liability on the instrument for the part exceeding the authority.

Article 6 If a person with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct signs or seals a negotiable instrument, the signature or seal is invalid, but this does not affect the validity of other signatures or seals.

Article 7 A signature or seal on a negotiable instrument means a signature, a seal, or a signature plus a seal.

For a legal person or other entity using a negotiable instrument, the signature or seal on the instrument shall be the seal of the legal person or entity plus the signature or seal of its legal representative or authorized agent.

A signature on a negotiable instrument shall be the legal name of the party concerned.

Article 8 The sum of a negotiable instrument shall be recorded both in capital Chinese characters and in numerals, and the two must be consistent; if they are inconsistent, the instrument is invalid.

Article 9 The matters recorded on a negotiable instrument must comply with the provisions of this Law.

The sum, date, and name of the payee on a negotiable instrument shall not be altered; if altered, the instrument is invalid.

Other matters recorded on a negotiable instrument may be altered by the original recorder, and the alteration shall be certified by the signature or seal of the original recorder.

Article 10 The issuance, acquisition, and transfer of a negotiable instrument shall follow the principle of good faith and be based on a genuine transaction relationship and a relationship of creditor's rights and debts.

The acquisition of a negotiable instrument must be for consideration, i.e., the consideration corresponding to the instrument as agreed upon by both parties.

Article 11 If a negotiable instrument is acquired gratuitously in accordance with the law due to taxation, inheritance, or donation, it is not subject to the restriction of providing consideration. However, the rights on the instrument enjoyed shall not be superior to those of the prior party.

"Prior party" means other obligors on the instrument who signed or sealed before the signer or holder.

Article 12 A person who acquires a negotiable instrument by means of fraud, theft, or coercion, or who acquires it maliciously knowing the existence of such circumstances, shall not enjoy rights on the instrument.

A holder who acquires a negotiable instrument not conforming to the provisions of this Law due to gross negligence shall also not enjoy rights on the instrument.

Article 13 An obligor on a negotiable instrument may not set up against the holder a defense based on its relationship with the drawer or with a prior party of the holder, unless the holder acquired the instrument knowing the existence of such defense.

An obligor on a negotiable instrument may set up a defense against a holder who has a direct creditor-debtor relationship with it and who fails to perform an agreed obligation.

For the purposes of this Law, "defense" means the act of an obligor on a negotiable instrument refusing to perform its obligation to the creditor on the instrument in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 14 The matters recorded on a negotiable instrument shall be genuine and shall not be forged or altered. Anyone who forges or alters a signature, seal, or other recorded matter on a negotiable instrument shall bear legal liability.

If a signature or seal on a negotiable instrument is forged or altered, it does not affect the validity of other genuine signatures or seals on the instrument.

If other recorded matters on a negotiable instrument are altered, a person who signed or sealed before the alteration shall be liable for the original recorded matters; a person who signed or sealed after the alteration shall be liable for the altered recorded matters; if it cannot be determined whether the signature or seal was made before or after the alteration, it shall be deemed to have been made before the alteration.

Article 15 If a negotiable instrument is lost, the loser may promptly notify the payer of the instrument to stop payment upon reporting the loss, except for instruments where the payer is not recorded or the payer and its agent for payment cannot be determined.

The payer who receives the notice to stop payment upon reporting the loss shall suspend payment.

The loser shall, within three days after notifying the stop payment upon reporting the loss, or after the loss of the instrument, apply to the people's court for a public notice for exhortation or file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

Article 16 The exercise of rights on a negotiable instrument or the preservation of such rights by the holder against the obligor on the instrument shall be carried out at the business premises and during business hours of the party to the instrument; if the party has no business premises, it shall be carried out at its domicile.

Article 17 Rights on a negotiable instrument shall be extinguished if not exercised within the following periods:

(1) The right of the holder against the drawer and the acceptor of a draft shall be two years from the date of maturity. For a draft or promissory note payable at sight, it shall be two years from the date of issue;

(2) The right of the holder against the drawer of a check shall be six months from the date of issue;

(3) The right of recourse of the holder against a prior party shall be six months from the date of dishonor by non-acceptance or non-payment;

(4) The right of further recourse of the holder against a prior party shall be three months from the date of settlement or the date of being sued.

The date of issue and the date of maturity of a negotiable instrument shall be determined by the parties to the instrument in accordance with the law.

Article 18 If a holder loses rights on a negotiable instrument due to the expiration of the time limit for exercising such rights or due to defects in the recorded matters on the instrument, the holder still enjoys civil rights and may request the drawer or acceptor to return benefits equivalent to the unpaid sum of the instrument.

## Chapter II Drafts

### Section 1 Issuance

Article 19 A draft is a negotiable instrument issued by the drawer, entrusting the payer to pay unconditionally a determined sum to the payee or the holder at sight or on a specified date.

Drafts are divided into bank drafts and commercial drafts.

Article 20 "Issuance" means the act of the drawer making a negotiable instrument and delivering it to the payee.

Article 21 The drawer of a draft must have a genuine entrustment payment relationship with the payer and have a reliable source of funds to pay the sum of the draft.

No draft without consideration shall be issued to defraud funds from a bank or other party to the instrument.

Article 22 A draft must record the following matters:

(1) The words indicating "draft";

(2) An unconditional order to pay;

(3) A determined sum;

(4) The name of the payer;

(5) The name of the payee;

(6) The date of issue;

(7) The signature or seal of the drawer.

If any of the matters specified in the preceding paragraph is not recorded on a draft, the draft is invalid.

Article 23 Matters such as the date of payment, place of payment, and place of issue recorded on a draft shall be clear and definite.

If the date of payment is not recorded on a draft, it shall be payable at sight.

If the place of payment is not recorded on a draft, the business premises, domicile, or habitual residence of the payer shall be the place of payment.

If the place of issue is not recorded on a draft, the business premises, domicile, or habitual residence of the drawer shall be the place of issue.

Article 24 Matters other than those specified in this Law may be recorded on a draft, but such recorded matters shall have no effect on the draft.

Article 25 The date of payment may be recorded in one of the following forms:

(1) Payable at sight;

(2) Payable on a fixed date;

(3) Payable at a fixed period after issue;

(4) Payable at a fixed period after sight.

The date of payment specified in the preceding paragraph is the maturity date of the draft.

Article 26 After issuing a draft, the drawer shall be liable for guaranteeing its acceptance and payment. If the draft is dishonored by non-acceptance or non-payment, the drawer shall pay the holder the sums and expenses specified in Articles 70 and 71 of this Law.

### Section 2 Endorsement

Article 27 The holder may transfer the rights on a draft to another person or authorize another person to exercise certain rights on the draft.

If the drawer records the words "not transferable" on the draft, the draft shall not be transferred.

When exercising the rights specified in the first paragraph, the holder shall endorse the draft and deliver it.

"Endorsement" means the act of recording relevant matters on the back of a negotiable instrument or on an allonge and signing or sealing it.

Article 28 If the instrument form is insufficient for recording the matters required by the endorser, an allonge may be attached to the instrument form.

The first person to record on the allonge shall sign or seal at the junction of the draft and the allonge.

Article 29 An endorsement shall be signed or sealed by the endorser and shall record the date of endorsement.

If the date of endorsement is not recorded, it shall be deemed to have been made before the maturity date of the draft.

Article 30 When a draft is transferred by endorsement or when certain rights on the draft are authorized to another person by endorsement, the name of the endorsee must be recorded.

Article 31 For a draft transferred by endorsement, the endorsements shall be continuous. The holder proves its rights on the draft by the continuity of endorsements; if the draft is acquired by other lawful means without endorsement, the holder shall provide evidence in accordance with the law to prove its rights on the draft.

"Continuity of endorsements" referred to in the preceding paragraph means that in the transfer of a negotiable instrument, the signatures or seals of the endorser transferring the draft and the endorsee receiving the draft on the instrument are successively connected.

Article 32 For a draft transferred by endorsement, a subsequent party shall be responsible for the authenticity of the endorsement of its immediate prior party.

"Subsequent party" means other obligors on the instrument who signed or sealed after the signer.

Article 33 An endorsement shall not be conditional. If conditions are attached to an endorsement, the conditions shall have no effect on the draft.

An endorsement transferring part of the sum of a draft or transferring the sum of a draft to two or more persons separately is invalid.

Article 34 If an endorser records the words "not transferable" on a draft, and a subsequent party endorses and transfers it, the original endorser shall not be liable for guaranteeing payment to the endorsee of the subsequent party.

Article 35 If an endorsement records the words "for collection," the endorsee has the right to exercise the entrusted rights on the draft on behalf of the endorser. However, the endorsee shall not further transfer the rights on the draft by endorsement.

A draft may be pledged; when pledged, the endorsement shall record the word "pledge." When the endorsee realizes its pledge right in accordance with the law, it may exercise the rights on the draft.

Article 36 If a draft is dishonored by non-acceptance, dishonored by non-payment, or the time limit for presentment for payment has expired, it shall not be endorsed and transferred; if it is endorsed and transferred, the endorser shall bear liability on the draft.

Article 37 After endorsing and transferring a draft, an endorser shall be liable for guaranteeing the acceptance and payment of the draft held by its subsequent party. If the draft is dishonored by non-acceptance or non-payment, the endorser shall pay the holder the sums and expenses specified in Articles 70 and 71 of this Law.

### Section 3 Acceptance

Article 38 "Acceptance" means the act of the payer of a draft promising to pay the sum of the draft on its maturity date.

Article 39 For a draft payable on a fixed date or at a fixed period after issue, the holder shall present it to the payer for acceptance before the maturity date.

"Presentment for acceptance" means the act of the holder presenting the draft to the payer and requesting the payer to promise payment.

Article 40 For a draft payable at a fixed period after sight, the holder shall present it to the payer for acceptance within one month from the date of issue.

If a draft is not presented for acceptance within the prescribed time limit, the holder loses its right of recourse against its prior parties.

A draft payable at sight does not require presentment for acceptance.

Article 41 The payer shall, within three days from receiving a draft presented for acceptance, accept or refuse to accept it.

When the payer receives a draft presented for acceptance by the holder, the payer shall issue a receipt for the draft to the holder. The receipt shall record the date of presentment for acceptance and be signed or sealed.

Article 42 When the payer accepts a draft, the payer shall record the word "accepted" and the date of acceptance on the face of the draft and sign or seal it; for a draft payable at a fixed period after sight, the date of payment shall be recorded at the time of acceptance.

If the date of acceptance is not recorded on the draft, the last day of the period specified in the first paragraph of the preceding article shall be the date of acceptance.

Article 43 When accepting a draft, the payer shall not attach conditions; if conditions are attached to the acceptance, it shall be deemed as a refusal to accept.

Article 44 After accepting a draft, the payer shall be liable for payment on the maturity date.

### Section 4 Guarantee

Article 45 The obligation on a draft may be guaranteed by a guarantor.

The guarantor shall be a person other than the obligor on the draft.

Article 46 The guarantor must record the following matters on the draft or allonge:

(1) The words indicating "guarantee";

(2) The name and domicile of the guarantor;

(3) The name of the guaranteed person;

(4) The date of guarantee;

(5) The signature or seal of the guarantor.

Article 47 If the guarantor fails to record the matter specified in item (3) of the preceding article on the draft or allonge, for an accepted draft, the acceptor shall be the guaranteed person; for an unaccepted draft, the drawer shall be the guaranteed person.

If the guarantor fails to record the matter specified in item (4) of the preceding article on the draft or allonge, the date of issue shall be the date of guarantee.

Article 48 A guarantee shall not be conditional; if conditions are attached, they shall not affect the liability of guarantee on the draft.

Article 49 The guarantor shall be liable for guaranteeing the rights on the draft enjoyed by the holder who lawfully acquires the draft, unless the obligation of the guaranteed person is invalid due to defects in the recorded matters on the draft.

Article 50 For a guaranteed draft, the guarantor and the guaranteed person shall be jointly and severally liable to the holder. If the draft is not paid at maturity, the holder has the right to demand payment from the guarantor, and the guarantor shall pay the full amount.

Article 51 If there are two or more guarantors, they shall be jointly and severally liable.

Article 52 After the guarantor discharges the obligation on the draft, the guarantor may exercise the right of recourse of the holder against the guaranteed person and its prior parties.

### Section 5 Payment

Article 53 The holder shall present the draft for payment within the following time limits:

(1) For a draft payable at sight, present it to the payer for payment within one month from the date of issue;

(2) For a draft payable on a fixed date, at a fixed period after issue, or at a fixed period after sight, present it to the acceptor for payment within ten days from the date of maturity.

If the holder fails to present the draft for payment within the time limit specified in the preceding paragraph, the acceptor or payer shall still continue to be liable for payment to the holder after the holder provides an explanation.

Presentment for payment to the payer through a collecting bank or through a clearing system shall be deemed as presentment for payment by the holder.

Article 54 If the holder presents the draft for payment in accordance with the preceding article, the payer must pay the full amount on the same day.

Article 55 After receiving payment, the holder shall sign a receipt on the draft and deliver the draft to the payer. If the holder entrusts a bank to collect payment, the entrusted bank shall transfer the collected sum of the draft to the holder's account, which shall be deemed as signing a receipt.

Article 56 The responsibility of the collecting bank entrusted by the holder is limited to transferring the sum of the draft to the holder's account in accordance with the matters recorded on the draft.

The responsibility of the paying bank entrusted by the payer is limited to paying the sum of the draft from the payer's account in accordance with the matters recorded on the draft.

Article 57 When making payment, the payer and its agent for payment shall examine the continuity of endorsements on the draft and examine the legal identity certificate or valid document of the person presenting the draft for payment.

If the payer or its agent for payment makes payment maliciously or with gross negligence, it shall bear the responsibility itself.

Article 58 For a draft payable on a fixed date, at a fixed period after issue, or at a fixed period after sight, if the payer makes payment before the maturity date, the payer shall bear the resulting responsibility itself.

Article 59 If the sum of a draft is in foreign currency, it shall be paid in RMB at the market exchange rate on the date of payment.

If the parties to the draft have agreed otherwise on the type of currency for payment, their agreement shall prevail.

Article 60 After the payer pays the full amount in accordance with the law, the liability of all obligors on the draft is discharged.

### Section 6 Right of Recourse

Article 61 If a draft is dishonored by non-payment at maturity, the holder may exercise the right of recourse against the endorsers, the drawer, and other obligors on the draft.

Before the maturity date of the draft, the holder may also exercise the right of recourse under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The draft is dishonored by non-acceptance;

(2) The acceptor or payer dies or absconds;

(3) The acceptor or payer is declared bankrupt in accordance with the law or is ordered to terminate its business activities due to illegal acts.

Article 62 When exercising the right of recourse, the holder shall provide relevant evidence of dishonor by non-acceptance or non-payment.

If the holder's presentment for acceptance or presentment for payment is refused, the acceptor or payer must issue a certificate of dishonor or a statement of reasons for dishonor. If it fails to issue a certificate of dishonor or a statement of reasons for dishonor, it shall bear the resulting civil liability.

Article 63 If the holder cannot obtain a certificate of dishonor due to the death, absconding, or other reasons of the acceptor or payer, the holder may obtain other relevant evidence in accordance with the law.

Article 64 If the acceptor or payer is declared bankrupt by a people's court in accordance with the law, the relevant judicial documents of the people's court shall have the effect of a certificate of dishonor.

If the acceptor or payer is ordered to terminate its business activities due to illegal acts, the punishment decision of the relevant administrative department shall have the effect of a certificate of dishonor.

Article 65 If the holder fails to present a certificate of dishonor, a statement of reasons for dishonor, or fails to provide other lawful evidence within the prescribed time limit, the holder loses its right of recourse against its prior parties. However, the acceptor or payer shall still be liable to the holder.

Article 66 The holder shall, within three days from receiving the relevant evidence of dishonor by non-acceptance or non-payment, notify its prior party in writing of the reasons for dishonor; the prior party shall, within three days from receiving the notice, notify its own prior party in writing. The holder may also send written notice to all obligors on the draft simultaneously.

If the holder fails to give notice within the time limit specified in the preceding paragraph, the holder may still exercise the right of recourse. If losses are caused to the prior party or the drawer due to the delay in notice, the party to the draft who failed to give notice within the prescribed time limit shall be liable for compensation for such losses, but the amount of compensation shall be limited to the sum of the draft.

If the notice is mailed within the prescribed time limit to the legal address or the agreed address, it shall be deemed as having been sent.

Article 67 The written notice made in accordance with the first paragraph of the preceding article shall record the main matters of the draft and state that the draft has been dishonored.

Article 68 The drawer, endorsers, acceptor, and guarantor of a draft shall be jointly and severally liable to the holder.

The holder may exercise the right of recourse against any one, several, or all of the obligors on the draft, regardless of their order.If the holder has already exercised the right of recourse against one or several of the parties liable on the instrument, the holder may still exercise the right of recourse against the other parties liable on the instrument. After the party against whom recourse is taken has satisfied the debt, that party shall have the same rights as the holder.

Article 69 Where the holder is the drawer, the holder has no right of recourse against the prior parties. Where the holder is an endorser, the holder has no right of recourse against the subsequent parties.

Article 70 When exercising the right of recourse, the holder may demand payment from the party against whom recourse is taken of the following amounts and expenses:

(1) The amount of the bill that has been dishonored by non-payment;
(2) Interest on the amount of the bill calculated at the rate prescribed by the People's Bank of China from the date of maturity or the date of presentment for payment to the date of satisfaction; and
(3) The expenses incurred in obtaining the relevant certificate of dishonor and in giving notice.

When the party against whom recourse is taken satisfies the debt, the holder shall deliver up the bill and the relevant certificate of dishonor and issue a receipt for the interest and expenses received.

Article 71 After satisfying the debt in accordance with the preceding article, the party against whom recourse is taken may exercise a further right of recourse against other parties liable on the instrument, demanding payment of the following amounts and expenses:

(1) The total amount already satisfied;
(2) Interest on the amount referred to in the preceding subparagraph calculated at the rate prescribed by the People's Bank of China from the date of satisfaction to the date of satisfaction of the further recourse; and
(3) The expenses incurred in giving notice.

When the party exercising the further right of recourse receives satisfaction, that party shall deliver up the bill and the relevant certificate of dishonor and issue a receipt for the interest and expenses received.

Article 72 After satisfying the debt in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two articles, the liability of the party against whom recourse was taken shall be discharged.

## Chapter 3 Promissory Notes

Article 73 A promissory note is an instrument issued by the maker promising to pay unconditionally a fixed sum of money to the payee or the holder upon sight.

The promissory notes referred to in this Law mean bank promissory notes.

Article 74 The maker of a promissory note must have a reliable source of funds to pay the amount of the promissory note and guarantee payment.

Article 75 A promissory note must record the following particulars:

(1) The words "promissory note";
(2) An unconditional promise to pay;
(3) A fixed sum of money;
(4) The name of the payee;
(5) The date of issue; and
(6) The signature or seal of the maker.

If any of the particulars specified in the preceding paragraph is not recorded on the promissory note, the promissory note shall be invalid.

Article 76 Matters such as the place of payment and the place of issue recorded on a promissory note shall be clear and definite.

If the place of payment is not recorded on the promissory note, the business premises of the maker shall be the place of payment.

If the place of issue is not recorded on the promissory note, the business premises of the maker shall be the place of issue.

Article 77 The maker of a promissory note must assume the liability to pay when the holder presents the note for payment upon sight.

Article 78 The term for payment of a promissory note shall not exceed two months from the date of issue.

Article 79 If the holder of a promissory note fails to present the note for payment upon sight within the prescribed time limit, the holder loses the right of recourse against the prior parties other than the maker.

Article 80 The endorsement, guarantee, payment, and exercise of the right of recourse in respect of promissory notes shall, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, be governed by the provisions of Chapter 2 of this Law concerning bills of exchange.

The issue of a promissory note shall, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, be governed by the provisions of Article 24 of this Law concerning bills of exchange.

## Chapter 4 Cheques

Article 81 A cheque is an instrument issued by the drawer authorizing a bank or other financial institution with which the drawer has a cheque account to pay unconditionally a fixed sum of money to the payee or the holder upon sight.

Article 82 To open a cheque account, the applicant must use his or her true name and submit legal documents certifying his or her identity.

To open a cheque account and obtain cheques, the applicant must have reliable financial standing and deposit a certain amount of funds.

To open a cheque account, the applicant shall provide a specimen of his or her true name signature or seal for verification.

Article 83 A cheque may be used to withdraw cash or for transfer. When used for transfer, this shall be indicated on the face of the cheque.

Cheques specifically used for withdrawing cash may be separately made as cash cheques, which can only be used for withdrawing cash.

Cheques specifically used for transfer may be separately made as transfer cheques, which can only be used for transfer and cannot be used to withdraw cash.

Article 84 A cheque must record the following particulars:

(1) The words "cheque";
(2) An unconditional order to pay;
(3) A fixed sum of money;
(4) The name of the drawee;
(5) The date of issue; and
(6) The signature or seal of the drawer.

If any of the particulars specified in the preceding paragraph is not recorded on the cheque, the cheque shall be invalid.

Article 85 The amount on a cheque may be filled in by authorization of the drawer. A cheque whose amount has not been filled in shall not be used.

Article 86 If the name of the payee is not recorded on a cheque, it may be filled in by authorization of the drawer.

If the place of payment is not recorded on a cheque, the business premises of the drawee shall be the place of payment.

If the place of issue is not recorded on a cheque, the business premises, domicile, or habitual residence of the drawer shall be the place of issue.

The drawer may record himself or herself as the payee on the cheque.

Article 87 The amount of a cheque issued by the drawer shall not exceed the actual deposit balance held by the drawer with the drawee at the time of payment.

If the amount of a cheque issued by the drawer exceeds the actual deposit balance held by the drawer with the drawee at the time of payment, the cheque is a rubber cheque. The issuance of rubber cheques is prohibited.

Article 88 The drawer of a cheque shall not issue a cheque whose signature or seal does not match the specimen of the drawer's true name signature or seal provided for verification.

Article 89 The drawer must assume the liability to guarantee payment to the holder according to the amount of the cheque issued.

When the deposit of the drawer with the drawee is sufficient to pay the amount of the cheque, the drawee shall pay the full amount on the same day.

Article 90 A cheque is limited to payment upon sight, and no other date of payment shall be recorded. If another date of payment is recorded, such record shall be void.

Article 91 The holder of a cheque shall present the cheque for payment within ten days from the date of issue. For cheques used in different localities, the time limit for presentment for payment shall be separately prescribed by the People's Bank of China.

If the time limit for presentment for payment is exceeded, the drawee may refuse to pay. If the drawee refuses to pay, the drawer shall still be liable on the instrument to the holder.

Article 92 Where the drawee pays the amount of the cheque in accordance with the law, the drawee shall no longer be liable to the drawer for the authorized payment, nor to the holder for payment. However, this shall not apply if the drawee pays with malice or gross negligence.

Article 93 The endorsement, payment, and exercise of the right of recourse in respect of cheques shall, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, be governed by the provisions of Chapter 2 of this Law concerning bills of exchange.

The issue of a cheque shall, except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, be governed by the provisions of Articles 24 and 26 of this Law concerning bills of exchange.

## Chapter 5 Application of Law to Foreign-Related Instruments

Article 94 The application of law to foreign-related instruments shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

The term "foreign-related instruments" as used in the preceding paragraph refers to instruments in which acts such as drawing, endorsement, acceptance, guarantee, and payment occur both within and outside the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 95 If any international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions differing from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for those provisions on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

If neither this Law nor any international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China has provisions on a matter, international custom may be applied.

Article 96 The capacity for civil conduct of a party liable on an instrument shall be governed by the law of the country of that party's nationality.

If a party liable on an instrument is considered to have no capacity for civil conduct or to have limited capacity for civil conduct under the law of the country of that party's nationality, but is considered to have full capacity for civil conduct under the law of the place where the act is performed, the law of the place where the act is performed shall apply.

Article 97 The particulars recorded at the time of drawing a bill of exchange or promissory note shall be governed by the law of the place of issue.

The particulars recorded at the time of drawing a cheque shall be governed by the law of the place of issue, but by agreement of the parties, the law of the place of payment may also apply.

Article 98 The acts of endorsement, acceptance, payment, and guarantee on an instrument shall be governed by the law of the place where the act is performed.

Article 99 The time limit for exercising the right of recourse on an instrument shall be governed by the law of the place of issue.

Article 100 The time limit for presentment of an instrument, the form of the relevant certificate of dishonor, and the time limit for issuing the certificate of dishonor shall be governed by the law of the place of payment.

Article 101 Where an instrument is lost, the procedure for the person who lost the instrument to apply for preservation of rights on the instrument shall be governed by the law of the place of payment.

## Chapter 6 Legal Liability

Article 102 Whoever commits any of the following acts of fraud involving instruments shall, if the act constitutes a crime, be investigated for criminal liability according to law:

(1) Forging or altering an instrument;
(2) Knowingly using a forged or altered instrument;
(3) Issuing a rubber cheque or knowingly issuing a cheque whose signature or seal does not match the specimen of the drawer's true name signature or seal provided for verification, for the purpose of defrauding property;
(4) Issuing a bill of exchange or promissory note without a reliable source of funds, for the purpose of defrauding funds;
(5) Making false records on a bill of exchange or promissory note at the time of issue, for the purpose of defrauding property;
(6) Using another person's instrument without authorization, or knowingly using an overdue or cancelled instrument, for the purpose of defrauding property; or
(7) A drawee maliciously colluding with the drawer or holder to commit any of the acts listed in the preceding six subparagraphs.

Article 103 Whoever commits any of the acts listed in the preceding article, if the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, shall be subject to administrative sanctions in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 104 If a staff member of a financial institution neglects his or her duty in instrument business and accepts, pays, or guarantees an instrument that violates the provisions of this Law, that staff member shall be subject to disciplinary action. If the act causes significant losses and constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

If a party suffers losses due to the act of a staff member of a financial institution as described in the preceding paragraph, the financial institution and the directly responsible person shall bear liability for compensation according to law.

Article 105 If the drawee of an instrument deliberately delays payment of an instrument payable upon sight or at maturity, the financial administrative department shall impose a fine, and the directly responsible person shall be subject to disciplinary action.

If the drawee of an instrument deliberately delays payment, causing losses to the holder, the drawee shall bear liability for compensation according to law.

Article 106 Any person who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits an act other than those for which liability for compensation is provided for in this Law, and thereby causes losses to others, shall bear civil liability according to law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 107 The calculation of the various time limits provided for in this Law shall be governed by the provisions of the General Principles of the Civil Law concerning the calculation of time periods.

Where a time limit is calculated by months, it shall be calculated according to the corresponding day of the month of expiry. If there is no corresponding day, the last day of the month shall be the day of expiry.

Article 108 The formats of bills of exchange, promissory notes, and cheques shall be uniform.

The formats of instrument vouchers and the measures for their printing and management shall be prescribed by the People's Bank of China.

Article 109 Specific measures for the implementation of instrument administration shall be formulated by the People's Bank of China in accordance with this Law and shall come into effect after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

Article 110 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1996.
