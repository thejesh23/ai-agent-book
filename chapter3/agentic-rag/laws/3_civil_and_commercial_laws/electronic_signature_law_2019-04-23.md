# Electronic Signature Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating electronic signature conduct, establishing the legal validity of electronic signatures, and safeguarding the lawful rights and interests of all relevant parties.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "electronic signature" means data in electronic form contained in or attached to a data message, used to identify the signatory and indicate that the signatory approves the content thereof.

For the purposes of this Law, "data message" means information generated, sent, received, or stored by electronic, optical, magnetic, or similar means.

Article 3 In civil activities, the parties may agree to use or not to use electronic signatures or data messages for contracts, other documents, or instruments.

Documents for which the parties have agreed to use electronic signatures or data messages shall not be denied legal validity solely because they are in the form of electronic signatures or data messages.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply to the following documents:

(1) Those involving personal relationships such as marriage, adoption, and inheritance;

(2) Those involving the cessation of public utility services such as water supply, heat supply, and gas supply;

(3) Other circumstances where electronic documents are not applicable as stipulated by laws or administrative regulations.

## Chapter II: Data Messages

Article 4 A data message that can tangibly represent the content it carries and can be accessed and retrieved at any time shall be deemed to satisfy the written form requirements of laws and regulations.

Article 5 A data message that meets the following conditions shall be deemed to satisfy the original form requirements of laws and regulations:

(1) It can effectively represent the content it carries and can be accessed and retrieved at any time;

(2) It can reliably ensure that the content remains complete and unaltered from the time of its final formation. However, additions of endorsements to the data message and formal changes occurring during data exchange, storage, and display shall not affect the integrity of the data message.

Article 6 A data message that meets the following conditions shall be deemed to satisfy the document preservation requirements of laws and regulations:

(1) It can effectively represent the content it carries and can be accessed and retrieved at any time;

(2) The format of the data message is the same as when it was generated, sent, or received, or if the format is different, it can accurately represent the content originally generated, sent, or received;

(3) It can identify the sender, recipient, and the time of sending and receiving of the data message.

Article 7 A data message shall not be rejected as evidence solely because it was generated, sent, received, or stored by electronic, optical, magnetic, or similar means.

Article 8 When examining the authenticity of a data message as evidence, the following factors shall be considered:

(1) The reliability of the method by which the data message was generated, stored, or transmitted;

(2) The reliability of the method for maintaining the integrity of the content;

(3) The reliability of the method for identifying the sender;

(4) Other relevant factors.

Article 9 A data message shall be deemed to have been sent by the sender under any of the following circumstances:

(1) It was sent with the authorization of the sender;

(2) It was sent automatically by the sender's information system;

(3) The recipient verified the data message using a method recognized by the sender, and the result was consistent.

If the parties have otherwise agreed on the matters specified in the preceding paragraph, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 10 If laws, administrative regulations, or the parties' agreement require confirmation of receipt of a data message, such confirmation shall be given. When the sender receives the recipient's confirmation of receipt, the data message shall be deemed to have been received.

Article 11 The time when a data message enters an information system beyond the sender's control shall be deemed the time of sending of the data message.

If the recipient has designated a specific system for receiving data messages, the time when the data message enters that specific system shall be deemed the time of receipt of the data message; if no specific system is designated, the time when the data message first enters any system of the recipient shall be deemed the time of receipt of the data message.

If the parties have otherwise agreed on the time of sending or receipt of a data message, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 12 The sender's principal place of business shall be the place of sending of the data message, and the recipient's principal place of business shall be the place of receipt of the data message. If there is no principal place of business, the habitual residence shall be the place of sending or receipt.

If the parties have otherwise agreed on the place of sending or receipt of a data message, such agreement shall prevail.

## Chapter III: Electronic Signatures and Certification

Article 13 An electronic signature that simultaneously meets the following conditions shall be deemed a reliable electronic signature:

(1) The electronic signature creation data is unique to the signatory when used for the electronic signature;

(2) At the time of signing, the electronic signature creation data is under the sole control of the signatory;

(3) Any alteration to the electronic signature after signing can be detected;

(4) Any alteration to the content or form of the data message after signing can be detected.

The parties may also choose to use an electronic signature that meets the reliable conditions agreed upon by them.

Article 14 A reliable electronic signature shall have the same legal validity as a handwritten signature or seal.

Article 15 The signatory shall properly safeguard the electronic signature creation data. If the signatory becomes aware that the electronic signature creation data has been compromised or may have been compromised, the signatory shall promptly inform all relevant parties and cease using that electronic signature creation data.

Article 16 If an electronic signature requires third-party certification, the certification service shall be provided by an electronic certification service provider established in accordance with the law.

Article 17 To provide electronic certification services, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Obtaining the status of a legal person enterprise;

(2) Having professional and managerial personnel appropriate for providing electronic certification services;

(3) Having funds and business premises appropriate for providing electronic certification services;

(4) Having technology and equipment that meet national security standards;

(5) Having a certificate of approval from the national cryptography administration for the use of cryptography;

(6) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 18 To engage in electronic certification services, an application shall be submitted to the competent information industry department of the State Council, along with relevant materials demonstrating compliance with the conditions specified in Article 17 of this Law. Upon receiving the application, the competent information industry department of the State Council shall, after legal review and consultation with the competent commerce department of the State Council and other relevant departments, make a decision on whether to grant permission within forty-five days from the date of receiving the application. If permission is granted, an electronic certification license shall be issued; if permission is denied, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons.

An electronic certification service provider that has obtained certification qualifications shall publish its name, license number, and other information on the Internet in accordance with the regulations of the competent information industry department of the State Council.

Article 19 An electronic certification service provider shall formulate and publish electronic certification business rules that comply with relevant national regulations, and file them with the competent information industry department of the State Council.

The electronic certification business rules shall include matters such as the scope of liability, operational procedures, and information security safeguards.

Article 20 When applying for an electronic signature certification certificate from an electronic certification service provider, the signatory shall provide truthful, complete, and accurate information.

Upon receiving an application for an electronic signature certification certificate, the electronic certification service provider shall verify the identity of the applicant and examine the relevant materials.

Article 21 An electronic signature certification certificate issued by an electronic certification service provider shall be accurate and shall contain the following content:

(1) The name of the electronic certification service provider;

(2) The name of the certificate holder;

(3) The serial number of the certificate;

(4) The validity period of the certificate;

(5) The electronic signature verification data of the certificate holder;

(6) The electronic signature of the electronic certification service provider;

(7) Other content stipulated by the competent information industry department of the State Council.

Article 22 An electronic certification service provider shall ensure that the content of the electronic signature certification certificate remains complete and accurate during its validity period, and shall ensure that parties relying on the electronic signature can verify or understand the content of the electronic signature certification certificate and other relevant matters.

Article 23 If an electronic certification service provider intends to suspend or terminate electronic certification services, it shall notify all relevant parties of business succession and other related matters at least ninety days before the suspension or termination.

If an electronic certification service provider intends to suspend or terminate electronic certification services, it shall report to the competent information industry department of the State Council at least sixty days before the suspension or termination, and shall negotiate with other electronic certification service providers regarding business succession to make appropriate arrangements.

If an electronic certification service provider fails to reach an agreement with other electronic certification service providers on business succession, it shall apply to the competent information industry department of the State Council to arrange for another electronic certification service provider to take over its business.

If an electronic certification service provider's electronic certification license is lawfully revoked, the handling of its business succession shall be carried out in accordance with the regulations of the competent information industry department of the State Council.

Article 24 An electronic certification service provider shall properly preserve information related to certification, with a preservation period of at least five years after the expiration of the electronic signature certification certificate.

Article 25 The competent information industry department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law, formulate specific management measures for the electronic certification service industry and exercise supervision and management over electronic certification service providers in accordance with the law.

Article 26 After approval by the competent information industry department of the State Council based on relevant agreements or the principle of reciprocity, electronic signature certification certificates issued outside the People's Republic of China by electronic certification service providers outside the People's Republic of China shall have the same legal validity as electronic signature certification certificates issued by electronic certification service providers established in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter IV: Legal Liability

Article 27 If a signatory, knowing that the electronic signature creation data has been compromised or may have been compromised, fails to promptly inform all relevant parties and cease using the electronic signature creation data, fails to provide truthful, complete, and accurate information to the electronic certification service provider, or is otherwise at fault, causing losses to parties relying on the electronic signature or the electronic certification service provider, the signatory shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 28 If a signatory or a party relying on an electronic signature suffers losses from engaging in civil activities based on electronic certification services provided by an electronic certification service provider, and the electronic certification service provider cannot prove that it is not at fault, the provider shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 29 If electronic certification services are provided without a license, the competent information industry department of the State Council shall order the cessation of the illegal act; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the illegal income is 300,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal income shall be imposed; if there is no illegal income or the illegal income is less than 300,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 30 If an electronic certification service provider suspends or terminates electronic certification services without reporting to the competent information industry department of the State Council at least sixty days before the suspension or termination, the competent information industry department of the State Council shall impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge.

Article 31 If an electronic certification service provider fails to comply with certification business rules, fails to properly preserve information related to certification, or commits other illegal acts, the competent information industry department of the State Council shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is not made within the specified period, the electronic certification license shall be revoked, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall not engage in electronic certification services for ten years. If an electronic certification license is revoked, a public announcement shall be made and the administrative department for industry and commerce shall be notified.

Article 32 Forging, misappropriating, or stealing another person's electronic signature, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law; if causing losses to others, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 33 If staff members of the department responsible for the supervision and management of the electronic certification service industry in accordance with this Law fail to perform their duties of administrative licensing and supervision and management in accordance with the law, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V: Supplementary Provisions

Article 34 For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings specified:

(1) "Signatory" means a person who holds electronic signature creation data and executes an electronic signature in their own name or in the name of the person they represent;

(2) "Party relying on an electronic signature" means a person who engages in relevant activities based on trust in an electronic signature certification certificate or an electronic signature;

(3) "Electronic signature certification certificate" means a data message or other electronic record that can verify the connection between the signatory and the electronic signature creation data;

(4) "Electronic signature creation data" means characters, codes, or other data used in the electronic signature process to reliably link the electronic signature to the signatory;

(5) "Electronic signature verification data" means data used to verify an electronic signature, including codes, passwords, algorithms, or public keys.

Article 35 The State Council or a department designated by the State Council may, in accordance with this Law, formulate specific measures for the use of electronic signatures and data messages in government activities and other social activities.

Article 36 This Law shall come into effect as of April 1, 2005.
