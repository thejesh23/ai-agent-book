# Maritime Code of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on November 7, 1992

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Code is enacted with a view to regulating the relations arising from maritime transport and those pertaining to ships, to safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of the parties concerned, and to promoting the development of maritime transport, economy and trade.

Article 2 "Maritime transport" as referred to in this Code means the carriage of goods and passengers by sea, including the direct transport between sea ports and river ports and between river ports and sea ports.

The provisions contained in Chapter IV of this Code concerning the contract of carriage of goods by sea shall not apply to the carriage of goods by sea between ports of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 "Ship" as referred to in this Code means seagoing ships and other mobile units, with the exception of ships or craft used for military or governmental purposes and small ships of less than 20 tons gross tonnage.

The term "ship" as referred to in the preceding paragraph shall include the ship's apparel.

Article 4 Maritime transport and towage services between ports of the People's Republic of China shall be undertaken by ships flying the flag of the People's Republic of China, except as otherwise provided for by laws or administrative rules and regulations.

No foreign ships may engage in the maritime transport or towage services between ports of the People's Republic of China unless approved by the competent authorities of transport and communications under the State Council.

Article 5 Ships are allowed to sail under the flag of the People's Republic of China after being registered, as required by law, and granted the nationality of the People's Republic of China.

Ships illegally flying the flag of the People's Republic of China shall be prohibited and fined by the relevant authorities.

Article 6 Maritime transport shall be placed under the centralized control of the competent authorities of transport and communications under the State Council. The specific measures therefor shall be formulated by such authorities and implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

## Chapter II Ships

### Section 1 Ownership of Ships

Article 7 The ownership of a ship means the shipowner's right to lawfully possess, utilize, profit from and dispose of the ship.

Article 8 With respect to a ship owned by the State, the legal person operating the ship on behalf of the State and enjoying the status of a legal person shall be deemed the shipowner for the purposes of this Code.

Article 9 The acquisition, transfer or extinction of the ownership of a ship shall be registered with the ship registration authorities; no such acquisition, transfer or extinction may be set up against a third party unless registered.

The transfer of the ownership of a ship shall be effected by a contract in writing.

Article 10 Where a ship is owned jointly by two or more legal persons or individuals, the joint ownership shall be registered with the ship registration authorities; no such joint ownership may be set up against a third party unless registered.

### Section 2 Mortgage of Ships

Article 11 The right of mortgage of a ship means the right of a mortgagee to be compensated in priority from the proceeds of the auction sale of the ship provided as a security for a debt when the mortgagor fails to pay the debt.

Article 12 The shipowner or a person authorized by the shipowner may establish a mortgage of the ship.

The mortgage of a ship shall be established by a contract in writing.

Article 13 The mortgage of a ship shall be registered with the ship registration authorities jointly by the mortgagee and the mortgagor. No mortgage may be set up against a third party unless registered.

The registration of a mortgage of a ship shall include the following main particulars:

(1) Name or designation and address of the mortgagee and the mortgagor;
(2) Name and nationality of the mortgaged ship, the authority that issued the certificate of ownership and the certificate number;
(3) Amount of the debt secured, interest rate and period for repayment.

The registration of a mortgage of a ship shall be open to the public for inspection.

Article 14 A mortgage may be established on a ship under construction.

To register a mortgage of a ship under construction, the shipbuilding contract shall be submitted to the ship registration authorities.

Article 15 Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the mortgagor shall insure the mortgaged ship. If the mortgagor fails to insure the ship, the mortgagee shall have the right to insure the ship, and the mortgagor shall pay the premium therefor.

Article 16 Where a ship is jointly owned, the establishment of a mortgage of the ship shall, unless otherwise agreed among the co-owners, be subject to the agreement of those co-owners who hold more than two-thirds of the shares thereof.

The mortgage established by the co-owners of a ship shall not be affected by the division of the joint ownership.

Article 17 Once a mortgage is established on a ship, the mortgagor shall not transfer the ownership of the ship to another person without the consent of the mortgagee.

Article 18 Where the mortgagee transfers all or part of the debt secured by the mortgaged ship to another person, the mortgage shall be transferred accordingly.

Article 19 Two or more mortgages may be established on the same ship. The ranking of the mortgages shall be determined according to the order of registration.

Where two or more mortgages are established on the same ship, the mortgagees shall be compensated in the order of registration of their respective mortgages. If the mortgages are registered on the same day, they shall rank equally.

Article 20 If the mortgaged ship is lost, the mortgage shall be extinguished. With respect to the insurance compensation obtained from the loss of the ship, the mortgagee shall have the right to be compensated in priority over other creditors.

### Section 3 Maritime Liens

Article 21 A maritime lien is the right of the claimant, subject to the provisions of Article 22 of this Code, to take priority in compensation against the shipowner, bareboat charterer or ship operator with respect to the ship which gave rise to the maritime claim.

Article 22 The following maritime claims shall be secured by maritime liens:

(1) Payment claims for wages, other remuneration, crew repatriation costs and social insurance contributions made by the master, crew members and other members of the complement employed on board the ship, pursuant to relevant labour laws, administrative rules and regulations or the employment contract;
(2) Claims in respect of loss of life or personal injury occurring in the operation of the ship;
(3) Payment claims for ship tonnage dues, pilotage dues, harbour dues and other port charges;
(4) Payment claims for salvage remuneration;
(5) Claims for compensation for property damage caused by tort during the operation of the ship.

Compensation claims for oil pollution damage caused by a ship carrying more than 2,000 tons of oil in bulk as cargo, which has a valid certificate evidencing that the ship has insurance or other financial security for civil liability for oil pollution damage, shall not fall within the scope of sub-paragraph (5) of the preceding paragraph.

Article 23 The maritime claims set out in paragraph 1 of Article 22 of this Code shall be satisfied in the order listed. However, any maritime claim arising later under sub-paragraph (4) shall have priority over those under sub-paragraphs (1) to (3).

In case there are more than one maritime claim under sub-paragraphs (1), (2), (3) and (5) of paragraph 1 of Article 22 of this Code, they shall be satisfied pro rata, regardless of their respective times of occurrence. In case there are more than one maritime claim under sub-paragraph (4), those arising later shall have priority over those arising earlier.

Article 24 Legal costs for enforcing a maritime lien, expenses for preserving and selling the ship, expenses for distributing the proceeds of sale, and other expenses incurred for the common interest of the maritime claimants shall be deducted from the proceeds of the auction sale of the ship in priority.

Article 25 A maritime lien shall have priority over a possessory lien, and a mortgage of a ship shall have priority over a possessory lien.

The term "possessory lien" as referred to in the preceding paragraph means the right of the shipbuilder or ship repairer to retain possession of the ship in his possession when the other party to the contract fails to perform the contract, so as to secure the shipbuilding or repair cost. The possessory lien shall be extinguished when the shipbuilder or ship repairer no longer retains possession of the ship built or repaired.

Article 26 Maritime liens shall not be extinguished by the transfer of the ownership of the ship, except that those which are not exercised within 60 days from the date of the public announcement of the transfer of the ship by the court upon the application of the transferee shall be extinguished.

Article 27 In case of the transfer of the maritime claims set out in Article 22 of this Code, the maritime liens attached thereto shall be transferred accordingly.

Article 28 A maritime lien shall be enforced by the court by arresting the ship that gave rise to the maritime lien.

Article 29 A maritime lien shall, except as provided for in Article 26 of this Code, be extinguished by one of the following causes:

(1) The maritime claim secured by the lien is not exercised within one year from the date of the creation of the lien;
(2) The ship has been the subject of a forced sale by the court;
(3) The ship has been lost.

The one-year period referred to in sub-paragraph (1) of the preceding paragraph shall not be suspended or interrupted.

Article 30 The provisions of this Section shall not affect the implementation of the provisions of Chapter XI of this Code concerning the limitation of liability for maritime claims.

## Chapter III Crew

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 31 The term "crew" means all persons employed on board a ship, including the master.

Article 32 The master, deck officers, chief engineer, engineers, electrical engineer and radio operator shall be held by those who hold appropriate certificates of competency.

Article 33 Chinese crew members engaged in international voyages shall hold seaman's books and relevant certificates issued by the harbour superintendency administration of the People's Republic of China.

Article 34 In the absence of provisions in this Code regarding the employment and the rights and obligations of the crew, the relevant provisions of laws and administrative rules and regulations shall apply.

### Section 2 The Master

Article 35 The master shall be responsible for the management and navigation of the ship.

Orders given by the master within the scope of his functions and powers shall be carried out by the crew members, the passengers and all other persons on board.

The master shall take necessary measures to protect the ship and all persons on board, documents, postal matters, cargo and other property.

Article 36 To ensure the safety of the ship and all persons on board, the master shall have the right to confine or take other necessary measures against those who have committed illegal or criminal acts on board, and to prevent them from concealing, destroying or forging evidence.

The master, having taken the measures mentioned in the preceding paragraph, shall make a written report of the case, which shall be signed by the master himself and at least two other persons on board, and shall be handed over, together with the offender, to the relevant authorities.

Article 37 The master shall make entries in the ship's log of any birth or death occurring on board, and shall issue a certificate to that effect in the presence of two witnesses. The death certificate shall be accompanied by a list of the deceased's personal effects. If the deceased has a will, the master shall certify it. The death certificate and the will shall be kept in the custody of the master and shall be delivered to the family members or the relevant parties concerned.

Article 38 Where a maritime accident has occurred on board and endangers the safety of the ship and the persons on board, the master shall organize the crew and other persons on board to make every effort to rescue. If the sinking or destruction of the ship is inevitable, the master may decide to abandon the ship; however, except in urgent circumstances, the master shall obtain the consent of the shipowner before abandoning the ship.

Upon abandoning the ship, the master must take all measures first to organize the passengers to safely leave the ship, then to arrange for the crew to leave the ship, and the master shall be the last to leave. Before leaving the ship, the master shall direct the crew to make every effort to rescue the ship's log, engine log, oil record book, radio log, charts and documents used during the current voyage, as well as valuables, postal matters and cash.

Article 39 The responsibility of the master for the management and navigation of the ship shall not be relieved by the presence of a pilot.

Article 40 Should the master die or become unable to perform his duties during the voyage, the deck officer with the highest rank shall act as master; before the ship sails from the next port of call, the shipowner shall appoint a new master to take command.

## Chapter IV Contract of Carriage of Goods by Sea

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 41 A contract of carriage of goods by sea is a contract whereby the carrier, against payment of freight, undertakes to carry the goods contracted for shipment by sea from one port to another.

Article 42 For the purposes of this Chapter:

(1) "Carrier" means the person who enters into a contract of carriage of goods by sea with a shipper, either personally or through another person acting on his behalf.
(2) "Actual carrier" means the person to whom the performance of the carriage of goods, or part of the carriage, has been entrusted by the carrier, and includes any other person to whom such performance has been entrusted under a sub-contract.
(3) "Shipper" means:
   1. The person who enters into a contract of carriage of goods by sea with a carrier, either personally or through another person acting on his behalf or for him;
   2. The person who delivers the goods to the carrier in connection with the contract of carriage of goods by sea, either personally or through another person acting on his behalf or for him.
(4) "Consignee" means the person entitled to take delivery of the goods.
(5) "Goods" includes live animals and containers, pallets or similar articles of transport supplied by the shipper for consolidating the goods.

Article 43 The carrier or the shipper may require confirmation of the conclusion of the contract of carriage of goods by sea in writing. However, a voyage charter party shall be concluded in writing. Telegrams, telexes and telefaxes have the effect of writing.

Article 44 Any stipulation in a contract of carriage of goods by sea or a bill of lading or other documents evidencing such contract that derogates from the provisions of this Chapter shall be null and void. The nullity of such a stipulation shall not affect the validity of other provisions of the contract or the bill of lading or other documents. A clause assigning the benefit of insurance of the goods in favour of the carrier or any similar clause shall be null and void.

Article 45 The provisions of Article 44 of this Code shall not prejudice the carrier's right to increase his responsibilities and obligations under the provisions of this Chapter.

### Section 2 Responsibilities of the Carrier

Article 46 The period of responsibility of the carrier with respect to the goods carried in containers covers the entire period during which the carrier is in charge of the goods, starting from the time the carrier receives the goods at the port of loading, until the time the goods are delivered at the port of discharge. The period of responsibility of the carrier with respect to the goods not carried in containers covers the entire period during which the carrier is in charge of the goods, starting from the time the goods are loaded on board the ship, until the time the goods are discharged from the ship. During the period of responsibility of the carrier, the carrier shall be liable for the loss of or damage to the goods, except as otherwise provided for in this Section.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not affect the carrier's right to make any agreement concerning the responsibility for the goods not carried in containers before loading and after discharge.

Article 47 The carrier shall, before and at the beginning of the voyage, exercise due diligence to make the ship seaworthy, properly man, equip and supply the ship, and make the holds, refrigerating and cool chambers and all other parts of the ship in which goods are carried, fit and safe for their reception, carriage and preservation.

Article 48 The carrier shall properly and carefully load, handle, stow, carry, keep, care for and discharge the goods carried.

Article 49 The carrier shall carry the goods to the port of discharge on the agreed, customary or geographical route.

Any deviation in saving or attempting to save life or property at sea or any reasonable deviation shall not be deemed to be an act violating the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 50 Delay in delivery occurs when the goods have not been delivered at the port of discharge agreed upon within the time expressly agreed upon.

The carrier shall be liable for the loss of or damage to the goods caused by delay in delivery due to the fault of the carrier, except those circumstances for which the carrier is not liable as provided for in this Chapter.

The carrier shall be liable for the economic losses caused by delay in delivery due to the fault of the carrier, even if no loss of or damage to the goods has occurred, except those circumstances for which the carrier is not liable as provided for in this Chapter.

Where the carrier has not delivered the goods within 60 days from the expiry of the time limit specified in paragraph 1 of this Article, the person entitled to make a claim for the loss of the goods may deem the goods lost.

Article 51 The carrier shall not be liable for the loss of or damage to the goods occurring during the period of responsibility if such loss or damage is caused by one of the following reasons:

(1) Fault of the master, crew members, pilot or the carrier's other servants in the navigation or management of the ship;
(2) Fire, unless caused by the actual fault of the carrier;
(3) Force majeure, perils, dangers and accidents of the sea or other navigable waters;
(4) War or armed conflict;
(5) Act of the government or competent authorities, quarantine restrictions or judicial seizure;
(6) Strikes, lockouts or stoppage or restraint of labour;
(7) Saving or attempting to save life or property at sea;
(8) Act of the shipper, owner of the goods or their agents;
(9) Natural characteristics or inherent defects of the goods;
(10) Inadequacy of packing or insufficiency or illegibility of marks;
(11) Latent defects of the ship not discoverable by due diligence;
(12) Any other cause arising without the fault of the carrier or his servants or agents.

The carrier who is entitled to exoneration from liability under the preceding paragraph shall, except for the cause specified in sub-paragraph (2), bear the burden of proof.

Article 52 The carrier shall not be liable for the loss of or damage to live animals arising from the special risks inherent in the carriage thereof. However, the carrier shall prove that he has complied with the special instructions of the shipper with respect to the carriage of the live animals and that, in the circumstances of the case, the loss or damage was caused by such special risks.

Article 53 The carrier may stow goods on deck only if such stowage is agreed with the shipper, or is in accordance with the custom of the trade, or is required by relevant laws, administrative rules and regulations.

Where the carrier stows goods on deck in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, he shall not be liable for the loss of or damage to the goods caused by the special risks involved in such stowage.

Where the carrier stows goods on deck in violation of the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article, he shall be liable for the loss of or damage to the goods caused thereby.

Article 54 Where the loss of, damage to or delay in delivery of the goods is attributable partly to a cause for which the carrier is not exonerated from liability and partly to another cause, the carrier shall be liable only to the extent of the cause for which he is not exonerated from liability; however, the carrier shall bear the burden of proof with respect to the loss, damage or delay in delivery caused by the other cause.

Article 55 The amount of compensation for the loss of the goods shall be calculated on the basis of the actual value of the goods; the amount of compensation for the damage to the goods shall be calculated on the basis of the difference between the actual value of the goods before and after the damage, or on the basis of the cost of repair.

The actual value of the goods shall be calculated on the basis of the value of the goods at the time of shipment plus insurance and freight.

From the actual value of the goods as provided for in the preceding paragraph, the relevant expenses that have been saved or reduced as a result of the loss or damage shall be deducted.

Article 56 The limitation of liability of the carrier for loss of or damage to the goods shall be calculated on the basis of the number of packages or other shipping units, at the rate of 666.67 units of account per package or other shipping unit, or on the basis of the gross weight of the goods, at the rate of 2 units of account per kilogram, whichever is higher. However, this limitation shall not apply if the nature and value of the goods have been declared by the shipper before shipment and inserted in the bill of lading, or if a higher amount than the limitation of liability provided for in this Article has been agreed upon between the carrier and the shipper.

Where a container, pallet or similar article of transport is used to consolidate goods, the number of packages or other shipping units enumerated in the bill of lading as packed in such article of transport shall be deemed the number of packages or other shipping units as referred to in the preceding paragraph. If not so enumerated, the article of transport shall be deemed one package or one shipping unit.

Where the article of transport is not owned by or furnished by the carrier, such article of transport shall be deemed one package or one shipping unit.**Article 57** The limit of liability of the carrier for economic losses resulting from delay in delivery of the goods shall be the amount of the freight for the goods so delayed. Where the loss of or damage to the goods has occurred concurrently with the delay in delivery thereof, the limit of liability of the carrier shall be that provided for in paragraph 1 of Article 56 of this Code.

**Article 58** The provisions of this Chapter regarding the grounds for exemption of liability and limitation of liability of the carrier shall apply to any legal action brought against the carrier in respect of loss of or damage to or delay in delivery of the goods covered by the contract of carriage of goods by sea, whether the claimant is a party to the contract or whether the action is founded in contract or in tort.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply if the action referred to in the preceding paragraph is brought against the servant or agent of the carrier, provided that such servant or agent proves that he acted within the scope of his employment or agency.

**Article 59** The carrier shall not be entitled to the benefit of the limitation of liability provided for in Article 56 or 57 of this Code if it is proved that the loss of or damage to or delay in delivery of the goods resulted from an act or omission of the carrier done with the intent to cause such loss, damage or delay or recklessly and with knowledge that such loss, damage or delay would probably result.

The servant or agent of the carrier shall not be entitled to the benefit of the limitation of liability provided for in Article 56 or 57 of this Code if it is proved that the loss of or damage to or delay in delivery of the goods resulted from an act or omission of such servant or agent done with the intent to cause such loss, damage or delay or recklessly and with knowledge that such loss, damage or delay would probably result.

**Article 60** Where the performance of the carriage or part thereof has been entrusted to an actual carrier, the carrier shall nevertheless remain liable for the entire carriage in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter. In respect of the carriage performed by the actual carrier, the carrier shall be liable for the acts or omissions of the actual carrier and of the servants or agents of the actual carrier acting within the scope of their employment or agency.

Notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding paragraph, where a contract of carriage by sea provides explicitly that a specified part of the carriage covered by the contract is to be performed by a named actual carrier other than the carrier, the contract may also provide that the carrier is not liable for loss, damage or delay in delivery arising from an occurrence which takes place while the goods are in the charge of the named actual carrier.

**Article 61** The provisions of this Chapter relating to the responsibility of the carrier shall apply to the actual carrier. Where an action is brought against the servant or agent of the actual carrier, the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 58 and paragraph 2 of Article 59 of this Code shall apply.

**Article 62** Any special agreement under which the carrier assumes obligations not imposed by this Chapter or waives rights conferred by this Chapter shall be binding on the actual carrier only if the actual carrier has expressly agreed thereto in writing. Whether the actual carrier has so agreed or not, such special agreement shall not affect the binding force thereof as between the carrier and the shipper.

**Article 63** Where both the carrier and the actual carrier are liable for compensation, they shall be jointly and severally liable within the scope of such liability.

**Article 64** Where claims for compensation for loss of or damage to the goods are made separately against the carrier, the actual carrier and their servants or agents, the aggregate amount of compensation shall not exceed the limit provided for in Article 56 of this Code.

**Article 65** The provisions of Articles 60 to 64 of this Code shall not affect the right of recourse between the carrier and the actual carrier.

### Section 3: Liability of the Shipper

**Article 66** The shipper shall have the goods properly packed and shall guarantee the accuracy of the description, marks, number of packages or pieces, weight or volume of the goods as furnished by him at the time of loading. If the carrier suffers losses due to improper packing or inaccuracies in the aforesaid information, the shipper shall be liable for compensation.

The carrier's right to compensation as provided for in the preceding paragraph shall not affect his obligations under the contract of carriage of goods towards persons other than the shipper.

**Article 67** The shipper shall promptly handle all necessary formalities with the port, customs, quarantine, inspection and other competent authorities in respect of the carriage of the goods, and shall deliver to the carrier the documents evidencing the completion of such formalities. If the carrier's interests are prejudiced due to the untimely, incomplete or incorrect delivery of the relevant documents, the shipper shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 68** The shipper shall, when shipping dangerous goods, have them properly packed, affix danger marks and labels thereon in accordance with the relevant regulations for the carriage of dangerous goods by sea, and notify the carrier in writing of their proper name, nature and the precautions to be taken. If the shipper fails to notify or notifies incorrectly, the carrier may, at any time and at any place, discharge, destroy or render the goods harmless as the circumstances may require, without liability for compensation. The shipper shall be liable for any damage suffered by the carrier as a result of the carriage of such goods.

If the carrier is aware of the nature of the dangerous goods and has consented to their shipment, he may nevertheless discharge, destroy or render the goods harmless without liability for compensation when they become an actual danger to the ship, persons or other goods. However, the provisions of this paragraph shall not affect the contribution in general average.

**Article 69** The shipper shall pay the freight to the carrier as agreed.

The shipper and the carrier may agree that the freight shall be paid by the consignee; however, such agreement shall be stated in the transport document.

**Article 70** The shipper shall not be liable for losses sustained by the carrier or the actual carrier, or for damage sustained by the ship, unless such loss or damage is caused by the fault of the shipper or his servants or agents.

The servants or agents of the shipper shall not be liable for losses sustained by the carrier or the actual carrier, or for damage sustained by the ship, unless such loss or damage is caused by the fault of such servants or agents.

### Section 4: Transport Documents

**Article 71** A bill of lading is a document which serves as evidence of the contract of carriage of goods by sea and the taking over or loading of the goods by the carrier, and based on which the carrier undertakes to deliver the goods against surrender thereof. A provision in the document stating that the goods are to be delivered to the order of a named person, or to order, or to bearer, constitutes such an undertaking.

**Article 72** When the goods are taken over or loaded by the carrier, the carrier shall, on demand of the shipper, issue a bill of lading.

The bill of lading may be signed by a person authorized by the carrier. A bill of lading signed by the master of the ship carrying the goods is deemed to be signed on behalf of the carrier.

**Article 73** The contents of a bill of lading shall include the following particulars:

(1) Description, marks, number of packages or pieces, weight or volume of the goods, and a statement, if applicable, as to the dangerous nature of the goods;
(2) Name and principal place of business of the carrier;
(3) Name of the ship;
(4) Name of the shipper;
(5) Name of the consignee;
(6) Port of loading and the date on which the goods were taken over by the carrier at the port of loading;
(7) Port of discharge;
(8) Place where the goods were taken over and the place where the goods are to be delivered, if the bill of lading is a multimodal transport document;
(9) Date and place of issue of the bill of lading and the number of originals issued;
(10) Payment of freight;
(11) Signature of the carrier or a person acting on his behalf.

The absence in the bill of lading of one or several of the particulars referred to in the preceding paragraph shall not affect the legal character of the document as a bill of lading, provided that it nevertheless meets the requirements of Article 71 of this Code.

**Article 74** If the carrier has, before loading the goods, issued a received-for-shipment bill of lading or other document at the shipper's request, the shipper may, after the goods have been loaded, surrender such document to the carrier in exchange for a shipped bill of lading. The carrier may also note on the received-for-shipment bill of lading the name of the carrying ship and the date of loading, and when so noted, the received-for-shipment bill of lading shall be deemed to constitute a shipped bill of lading.

**Article 75** If the carrier or the person signing the bill of lading on his behalf knows, or has reasonable grounds to suspect, that the description, marks, number of packages or pieces, weight or volume of the goods as stated in the bill of lading do not accurately represent the goods actually taken over, or, where a shipped bill of lading is issued, do not accurately represent the goods actually loaded, or if he has had no reasonable means of checking the particulars, he may insert in the bill of lading a reservation specifying the inaccuracies, the grounds for suspicion, or the lack of reasonable means of checking.

**Article 76** If the carrier or the person signing the bill of lading on his behalf fails to note on the bill of lading the apparent order and condition of the goods, the goods shall be deemed to be in apparent good order and condition.

**Article 77** Except for reservations made in accordance with Article 75 of this Code, the bill of lading issued by the carrier or the person signing on his behalf shall be *prima facie* evidence that the carrier has received the goods as described therein, or, where a shipped bill of lading is issued, that the goods have been loaded as described therein. Evidence to the contrary by the carrier shall not be admissible if the bill of lading has been transferred to a third party, including a consignee, who has acted in good faith in reliance thereon.

**Article 78** The relationship between the carrier and the consignee or holder of the bill of lading shall be determined by the terms and conditions of the bill of lading.

Neither the consignee nor the holder of the bill of lading shall be liable for demurrage, dead freight and other charges relating to loading incurred at the port of loading, unless the bill of lading expressly states that such charges are to be borne by the consignee or holder of the bill of lading.

**Article 79** The transfer of a bill of lading shall be effected in accordance with the following rules:

(1) A straight bill of lading: not transferable;
(2) An order bill of lading: transferable by endorsement, either special or blank;
(3) A bearer bill of lading: transferable without endorsement.

**Article 80** Where a document other than a bill of lading is issued by the carrier as evidence of the receipt of goods for carriage, such document shall be *prima facie* evidence of the conclusion of the contract of carriage of goods by sea and the taking over of the goods listed therein by the carrier.

Such documents issued by the carrier shall not be transferable.

### Section 5: Delivery of Goods

**Article 81** If the consignee does not, upon delivery of the goods by the carrier, give written notice to the carrier of the loss of or damage to the goods, such delivery shall be deemed *prima facie* evidence that the carrier has delivered the goods as described in the transport document and that the goods were in apparent good order and condition.

If the loss of or damage to the goods is not apparent, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply if the consignee fails to give written notice within seven consecutive days from the day after delivery, or, in the case of containerized goods, within fifteen consecutive days from the day after delivery.

Where the consignee has, jointly with the carrier, inspected or surveyed the goods at the time of delivery, no written notice shall be required in respect of the loss or damage ascertained during such inspection or survey.

**Article 82** The carrier shall not be liable for compensation if no written notice is received from the consignee within sixty consecutive days from the day after delivery of the goods to the consignee, in respect of economic losses caused by delay in delivery.

**Article 83** Before the consignee takes delivery of the goods at the port of destination, or before the carrier delivers the goods at the port of destination, either party may request an inspection or survey of the goods by a competent surveyor. The party requesting the inspection or survey shall bear the cost thereof, but shall be entitled to recover such cost from the party responsible for the loss or damage.

**Article 84** The carrier and the consignee shall afford each other reasonable facilities for the inspections and surveys provided for in Articles 81 and 83 of this Code.

**Article 85** Where the goods are delivered by the actual carrier, a written notice given by the consignee to the actual carrier pursuant to Article 81 of this Code shall have the same effect as a notice given to the carrier; and a written notice given by the consignee to the carrier shall have the same effect as a notice given to the actual carrier.

**Article 86** If the goods are not taken delivery of at the port of discharge, or if the consignee delays or refuses to take delivery thereof, the master may discharge the goods into a warehouse or other appropriate place, and the expenses and risks arising therefrom shall be borne by the consignee.

**Article 87** If the freight, contribution in general average, demurrage to be paid to the carrier, and other necessary expenses paid by the carrier for the goods, as well as other sums due to the carrier, are not paid, and no appropriate security has been furnished, the carrier may exercise a lien on the goods within a reasonable limit.

**Article 88** If the goods placed under lien by the carrier pursuant to Article 87 of this Code remain unclaimed for sixty days from the day after the arrival of the ship at the port of discharge, the carrier may apply to the court for an order to sell the goods by auction. If the goods are perishable or if the cost of storing them is likely to exceed their value, the carrier may apply for an earlier auction.

The proceeds from the auction shall be applied to defray the expenses of storing and auctioning the goods, the freight, and other sums due to the carrier. Any shortfall may be recovered by the carrier from the shipper. Any surplus shall be returned to the shipper. If the surplus cannot be returned and remains unclaimed for one year from the date of the auction, it shall be paid into the State Treasury.

### Section 6: Cancellation of Contract

**Article 89** Before the ship sails from the port of loading, the shipper may request the cancellation of the contract. However, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the shipper shall pay to the carrier half of the agreed freight; if the goods have already been loaded, the shipper shall also bear the expenses for loading, discharging and other related charges.

**Article 90** If, before the ship sails from the port of loading, the contract cannot be performed due to force majeure or other causes not attributable to the fault of the carrier or the shipper, either party may cancel the contract, and neither party shall be liable to the other for compensation. Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, if the freight has already been paid, the carrier shall refund it to the shipper; if the goods have already been loaded, the shipper shall bear the loading and discharging expenses; if a bill of lading has already been issued, the shipper shall return it to the carrier.

**Article 91** If, due to force majeure or other causes not attributable to the fault of the carrier or the shipper, the ship cannot discharge the goods at the port of destination as contracted, the master shall have the right to discharge the goods at a safe port or place near the port of destination, and the contract shall be deemed to have been performed.

When deciding to discharge the goods, the master shall promptly notify the shipper or the consignee and shall take into account the interests of the shipper or the consignee.

### Section 7: Special Provisions for Voyage Charter Parties

**Article 92** A voyage charter party is a contract under which the shipowner provides the charterer with a ship or a part of the ship's space for the carriage of agreed goods from one port to another, and the charterer pays the agreed freight.

**Article 93** The contents of a voyage charter party mainly include the names of the shipowner and the charterer, the name, nationality, deadweight tonnage and cubic capacity of the ship, the description of the goods, the port of loading and the port of destination, the laydays, the laytime, the freight, the demurrage, the dispatch money, and other relevant matters.

**Article 94** The provisions of Articles 47 and 49 of this Code shall apply to the shipowner under a voyage charter party.

The provisions of this Chapter relating to the rights and obligations of the parties to the contract shall apply to the shipowner and the charterer under a voyage charter party only in the absence of any provision or different provision in the voyage charter party.

**Article 95** Where a bill of lading is issued for goods carried under a voyage charter party, and the holder of the bill of lading is not the charterer, the rights and obligations between the carrier and such holder of the bill of lading shall be governed by the terms of the bill of lading. However, if the bill of lading expressly incorporates the terms of the voyage charter party, the terms of that voyage charter party shall apply.

**Article 96** The shipowner shall provide the agreed ship. With the consent of the charterer, the ship may be substituted. However, if the ship provided or substituted does not conform to the contract, the charterer shall have the right to reject it or to cancel the contract.

If the charterer suffers losses due to the shipowner's failure to provide the agreed ship through his fault, the shipowner shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 97** If the shipowner fails to provide the ship within the agreed laydays, the charterer shall have the right to cancel the contract. However, if the shipowner notifies the charterer of the delay and the expected date of the ship's arrival at the port of loading, the charterer shall, within forty-eight hours of receiving such notification, notify the shipowner of his decision whether to cancel the contract.

If the charterer suffers losses due to the shipowner's delay in providing the ship through his fault, the shipowner shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 98** The laytime for loading and discharging under a voyage charter party, the method of calculation thereof, the demurrage for exceeding the laytime, and the dispatch money for completing loading or discharging ahead of time shall be agreed upon by the parties.

**Article 99** The charterer may sublet the chartered ship. Subletting shall not affect the rights and obligations under the original charter party.

**Article 100** The charterer shall provide the agreed goods. With the consent of the shipowner, the goods may be substituted. However, if the substituted goods are disadvantageous to the shipowner, the shipowner shall have the right to reject them or to cancel the contract.

If the shipowner suffers losses due to the charterer's failure to provide the agreed goods, the charterer shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 101** The shipowner shall discharge the goods at the port of discharge agreed in the contract. If the contract contains a clause giving the charterer the option to choose the port of discharge, and the charterer fails to notify the shipowner of the chosen port in time as agreed, the master may choose one of the optional ports for discharge. If the shipowner suffers losses due to the charterer's failure to notify the chosen port in time as agreed, the charterer shall be liable for compensation. If the charterer suffers losses due to the shipowner's choosing a port for discharge without authorization and not in accordance with the contract, the shipowner shall be liable for compensation.

### Section 8: Special Provisions for Multimodal Transport Contracts

**Article 102** A multimodal transport contract as referred to in this Code means a contract under which the multimodal transport operator undertakes to transport the goods from the place of receipt to the place of delivery for delivery to the consignee, using two or more different modes of transport, one of which is sea transport, and to collect the freight for the entire carriage.

The multimodal transport operator referred to in the preceding paragraph means a person who enters into a multimodal transport contract with the shipper, either personally or through another person acting on his behalf.

**Article 103** The period of responsibility of the multimodal transport operator for the goods under multimodal transport shall commence from the time he takes over the goods and shall end at the time of delivery of the goods.

**Article 104** The multimodal transport operator shall be responsible for the performance or the procurement of the performance of the multimodal transport contract and shall be liable for the entire carriage.

The multimodal transport operator and the carriers participating in the various stages of the multimodal transport may, by separate contract, agree upon their mutual responsibilities regarding the carriage of each stage. However, such contract shall not affect the liability of the multimodal transport operator for the entire carriage.

**Article 105** If loss of or damage to the goods occurs during a specific stage of the multimodal transport, the provisions of the relevant laws governing the mode of transport applicable to that stage shall apply to the liability of the multimodal transport operator and the limit thereof.

**Article 106** If the stage of transport in which the loss of or damage to the goods occurred cannot be determined, the multimodal transport operator shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter regarding the liability of the carrier and the limits thereof.

## Chapter 5: Contract of Carriage of Passengers by Sea

**Article 107** A contract of carriage of passengers by sea is a contract under which the carrier undertakes to transport passengers and their luggage by sea from one port to another in a ship suitable for that purpose, and the passenger pays the fare.

**Article 108** For the purposes of this Chapter:

(1) "Carrier" means a person who enters into a contract of carriage of passengers by sea with the passenger, either personally or through another person acting on his behalf.
(2) "Actual carrier" means a person to whom the performance of the carriage of passengers or part thereof has been entrusted by the carrier, and includes any other person to whom such performance has been sub-entrusted.
(3) "Passenger" means a person carried under a contract of carriage of passengers by sea. A person who, with the consent of the carrier, accompanies the goods under a contract of carriage of goods by sea shall be deemed to be a passenger.(4) "Luggage" means any article or vehicle carried by the carrier under a contract of carriage of passengers by sea, excluding live animals.

(5) "Cabin luggage" means luggage which the passenger takes with him, keeps in his own custody or places in the passenger cabin.

Article 109 The provisions of this Chapter regarding the liability of the carrier shall apply to the actual carrier. The provisions of this Chapter regarding the liability of the carrier's servants or agents shall apply to the servants or agents of the actual carrier.

Article 110 The passenger ticket serves as evidence of the conclusion of a contract of carriage of passengers by sea.

Article 111 The period of carriage for the carriage of passengers by sea shall commence from the time the passenger embarks on the ship and shall end at the time the passenger disembarks from the ship. If the ticket price includes the cost of transfer, the period of carriage shall also include the time during which the carrier transports the passenger by water from the shore to the ship and from the ship to the shore, but shall not include the time the passenger spends within a port station, on a wharf, or within other port facilities.

The period of carriage for the passenger's cabin luggage shall be the same as that stipulated in the preceding paragraph. For luggage other than cabin luggage, the period of carriage shall commence from the time the passenger delivers the luggage to the carrier or the carrier's servant or agent and shall end at the time the carrier or the carrier's servant or agent returns the luggage to the passenger.

Article 112 If a passenger travels without a ticket, travels on a higher class than that indicated on the ticket, or travels beyond the destination indicated on the ticket, he shall pay the fare difference in accordance with the relevant provisions. The carrier may collect an additional fare in accordance with the relevant provisions. If the passenger refuses to pay, the master has the right to order him to disembark at a suitable place, and the carrier has the right to recover the amount from him.

Article 113 A passenger shall not carry contraband goods or dangerous goods such as flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, radioactive articles, or other dangerous goods that may endanger the safety of persons or property on board, either on his person or in his luggage.

The carrier may, at any time and at any place, unload, destroy, render harmless, or deliver to the relevant authorities any contraband goods or dangerous goods carried by the passenger on his person or in his luggage in violation of the preceding paragraph, without being liable for compensation.

If a passenger causes damage by violating the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, he shall be liable for compensation.

Article 114 During the period of carriage of the passenger and his luggage as stipulated in Article 111 of this Code, if an accident occurs due to the fault of the carrier or the carrier's servants or agents acting within the scope of their employment or agency, resulting in personal injury or death of the passenger or loss of or damage to the luggage, the carrier shall be liable for compensation.

The claimant shall bear the burden of proving the fault of the carrier or the carrier's servants or agents, except in the circumstances specified in paragraphs 3 and 4 of this Article.

If the personal injury or death of the passenger or the loss of or damage to cabin luggage is caused by the sinking, collision, stranding, explosion, or fire of the ship, or by a defect of the ship, the carrier or the carrier's servants or agents shall be presumed to be at fault unless they prove otherwise.

For the loss of or damage to luggage other than cabin luggage, regardless of the cause of the accident, the carrier or the carrier's servants or agents shall be presumed to be at fault unless they prove otherwise.

Article 115 If the carrier proves that the personal injury or death of the passenger or the loss of or damage to the luggage was caused by the fault of the passenger himself or by the joint fault of the passenger and the carrier, the carrier's liability may be wholly or partially exonerated accordingly.

If the carrier proves that the personal injury or death of the passenger or the loss of or damage to the luggage was caused intentionally by the passenger himself, or that the personal injury or death of the passenger was caused by the passenger's own state of health, the carrier shall not be liable for compensation.

Article 116 The carrier shall not be liable for compensation for the loss of or damage to the passenger's money, gold, silver, jewelry, negotiable securities, or other valuables.

If the passenger and the carrier have agreed that the articles specified in the preceding paragraph shall be kept in the custody of the carrier, the carrier shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the provisions of Article 117 of this Code. If the limit of compensation agreed upon in writing by the parties is higher than that stipulated in Article 117 of this Code, the carrier shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the agreed amount.

Article 117 Except in the circumstances specified in paragraph 4 of this Article, the limits of the carrier's liability for compensation in each carriage of passengers by sea shall be as follows:

(1) For personal injury or death of a passenger, not exceeding 46,666 Units of Account per passenger;

(2) For loss of or damage to cabin luggage, not exceeding 833 Units of Account per passenger;

(3) For loss of or damage to a vehicle, including luggage carried in the vehicle, not exceeding 3,333 Units of Account per vehicle;

(4) For loss of or damage to luggage other than that specified in subparagraphs (2) and (3) of this paragraph, not exceeding 1,200 Units of Account per passenger.

The carrier and the passenger may agree on a deductible for the loss of or damage to the passenger's vehicle and luggage other than the vehicle. However, the deductible for the loss of or damage to each vehicle shall not exceed 117 Units of Account, and the deductible for the loss of or damage to luggage other than the vehicle per passenger shall not exceed 13 Units of Account. When calculating the amount of compensation for the loss of or damage to each vehicle or luggage other than the vehicle per passenger, the agreed deductible shall be deducted.

The carrier and the passenger may agree in writing on a limit of liability higher than that stipulated in paragraph 1 of this Article.

For the carriage of passengers by sea between ports of the People's Republic of China, the limits of the carrier's liability for compensation shall be formulated by the competent department of transportation under the State Council and shall come into effect after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

Article 118 If it is proved that the personal injury or death of the passenger or the loss of or damage to the luggage resulted from an act or omission of the carrier done with the intent to cause such loss or damage, or recklessly and with knowledge that such loss or damage would probably result, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Articles 116 and 117 of this Code which limit his liability.

If it is proved that the personal injury or death of the passenger or the loss of or damage to the luggage resulted from an act or omission of the carrier's servant or agent done with the intent to cause such loss or damage, or recklessly and with knowledge that such loss or damage would probably result, the carrier's servant or agent shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Articles 116 and 117 of this Code which limit his liability.

Article 119 If the luggage is apparently damaged, the passenger shall submit a written notice to the carrier or the carrier's servant or agent in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For cabin luggage, the notice shall be submitted before or at the time of the passenger's disembarkation;

(2) For other luggage, the notice shall be submitted before or at the time of its return.

If the damage to the luggage is not apparent and is difficult to discover at the time of the passenger's disembarkation or the return of the luggage, or if the luggage is lost, the passenger shall submit a written notice to the carrier or the carrier's servant or agent within fifteen days from the date of disembarkation, the return of the luggage, or the date when the luggage should have been returned.

If the passenger fails to submit a written notice in a timely manner in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, he shall be presumed to have received the luggage in good condition, unless he proves otherwise.

If, at the time of the return of the luggage, the passenger has jointly inspected or examined the luggage with the carrier, no written notice is required.

Article 120 If a claim for compensation is made against the carrier's servant or agent, such servant or agent shall be entitled to invoke the defenses and limits of liability provided for in Articles 115, 116, and 117 of this Code if he proves that his act was within the scope of his employment or agency.

Article 121 If the carrier entrusts the performance of the carriage of the passenger or part thereof to an actual carrier, the carrier shall nevertheless remain liable for the entire carriage in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter. If the actual carrier performs the carriage, the carrier shall be liable for the acts of the actual carrier or the acts of the actual carrier's servants or agents acting within the scope of their employment or agency.

Article 122 Any special agreement under which the carrier assumes obligations not imposed by this Chapter or waives rights conferred by this Chapter shall be binding on the actual carrier only if the actual carrier has expressly agreed thereto in writing. Whether or not the actual carrier agrees, such special agreement shall be binding on the carrier.

Article 123 If both the carrier and the actual carrier are liable for compensation, they shall be jointly and severally liable within the limits of such liability.

Article 124 If claims for compensation for personal injury or death of the passenger or loss of or damage to the luggage are made separately against the carrier, the actual carrier, and their servants or agents, the total amount of compensation shall not exceed the limits stipulated in Article 117 of this Code.

Article 125 The provisions of Articles 121 to 124 of this Code shall not affect the right of recourse between the carrier and the actual carrier.

Article 126 Any clause in a contract of carriage of passengers by sea that contains any of the following shall be null and void:

(1) Exempting the carrier from the statutory liability he should bear towards the passenger;

(2) Reducing the limits of the carrier's liability stipulated in this Chapter;

(3) Making provisions contrary to the rules on burden of proof stipulated in this Chapter;

(4) Restricting the passenger's right to claim compensation.

The invalidity of such a clause in the contract shall not affect the validity of the other clauses of the contract.

## Chapter 6 Charter Parties

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 127 The provisions of this Chapter regarding the rights and obligations between the shipowner and the charterer shall apply only when there are no provisions or no different provisions in the charter party.

Article 128 Charter parties, including time charter parties and bareboat charter parties, shall be concluded in writing.

### Section 2 Time Charter Parties

Article 129 A time charter party is a contract under which the shipowner provides a ship, manned by the shipowner's crew, to the charterer for use by the charterer for an agreed period for an agreed purpose, against payment of hire.

Article 130 The contents of a time charter party mainly include the names of the shipowner and the charterer, the name, nationality, class, tonnage, capacity, speed, fuel consumption, trading area, purpose, charter period, time and place of delivery and redelivery and the conditions thereof, hire and its payment, and other relevant matters.

Article 131 The shipowner shall deliver the ship at the time agreed in the contract.

If the shipowner violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the charterer shall have the right to cancel the contract. If the shipowner notifies the charterer of the delay in the delivery of the ship and the expected date of the ship's arrival at the port of delivery, the charterer shall, within forty-eight hours of receiving such notification, notify the shipowner of his decision to cancel the contract or to continue to charter the ship.

If the shipowner causes losses to the charterer due to the delay in providing the ship through his fault, the shipowner shall be liable for compensation.

Article 132 At the time of delivery of the ship, the shipowner shall exercise due diligence to make the ship seaworthy. The ship delivered shall be fit for the agreed purpose.

If the shipowner violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the charterer shall have the right to cancel the contract and claim compensation for any losses suffered thereby.

Article 133 If, during the charter period, the ship is not in the agreed seaworthy condition or other condition, the shipowner shall take all reasonable measures that may be necessary to restore it as soon as possible.

If the ship is unable to operate normally for a continuous period of twenty-four hours due to its failure to meet the agreed seaworthy condition or other condition, the charterer shall not pay hire for the lost operating time, unless such failure was caused by the charterer.

Article 134 The charterer shall guarantee that the ship shall be employed in the agreed maritime transport between safe ports or places within the agreed trading area.

If the charterer violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the shipowner shall have the right to cancel the contract and claim compensation for any losses suffered thereby.

Article 135 The charterer shall guarantee that the ship shall be employed in the carriage of lawful goods as agreed.

If the charterer uses the ship for the carriage of live animals or dangerous goods, he shall obtain the prior consent of the shipowner.

If the charterer violates the provisions of paragraph 1 or 2 of this Article and causes losses to the shipowner, he shall be liable for compensation.

Article 136 The charterer shall have the right to give instructions to the master in respect of the operation of the ship, provided that such instructions shall not be inconsistent with the terms of the time charter party.

Article 137 The charterer may sublet the chartered ship, but he shall notify the shipowner of the subletting in a timely manner. The rights and obligations agreed upon in the original charter party shall not be affected by the subletting of the ship.

Article 138 The transfer of ownership of a ship that has been chartered out shall not affect the rights and obligations of the parties under the time charter party, but the shipowner shall notify the charterer in a timely manner. After the transfer of ownership, the original charter party shall be performed by the transferee and the charterer.

Article 139 During the contract period, if the ship renders salvage services, the charterer shall be entitled to one half of the salvage proceeds after deducting the salvage expenses, compensation for loss or damage, the crew's share, and other expenses.

Article 140 The charterer shall pay the hire as agreed in the contract. If the charterer fails to pay the hire as agreed, the shipowner shall have the right to cancel the contract and claim compensation for any losses suffered thereby.

Article 141 If the charterer fails to pay the hire or other sums payable under the contract to the shipowner, the shipowner shall have a lien over the goods and property belonging to the charterer on board and over the sub-hire.

Article 142 When the charterer redelivers the ship to the shipowner, the ship shall be in the same good order and condition as when delivered to the charterer, fair wear and tear excepted.

If the ship fails to maintain the same good order and condition as when delivered, the charterer shall be responsible for repairing it or compensating for the damage.

Article 143 If, upon reasonable calculation, the date for the completion of the final voyage is approximately the date agreed for redelivery, but may exceed the agreed redelivery date, the charterer shall be entitled to retain the ship for the completion of that voyage beyond the agreed redelivery date. During the period of such overrun, the charterer shall pay hire at the rate agreed in the contract; if the market rate of hire is higher than the rate agreed in the contract, the charterer shall pay hire at the market rate.

### Section 3 Bareboat Charter Parties

Article 144 A bareboat charter party is a contract under which the shipowner provides a ship without crew to the charterer for possession, use, and operation by the charterer for an agreed period, against payment of hire.

Article 145 The contents of a bareboat charter party mainly include the names of the shipowner and the charterer, the name, nationality, class, tonnage, capacity, trading area, purpose, charter period, time and place of delivery and redelivery and the conditions thereof, ship survey, maintenance and repair of the ship, hire and its payment, ship insurance, time and conditions for the cancellation of the contract, and other relevant matters.

Article 146 The shipowner shall deliver the ship and the ship's certificates to the charterer at the port or place and at the time agreed in the contract. At the time of delivery, the shipowner shall exercise due diligence to make the ship seaworthy. The ship delivered shall be fit for the purpose agreed in the contract.

If the shipowner violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the charterer shall have the right to cancel the contract and claim compensation for any losses suffered thereby.

Article 147 During the bareboat charter period, the charterer shall be responsible for the maintenance and repair of the ship.

Article 148 During the bareboat charter period, the charterer shall insure the ship for the value agreed in the contract in a manner of insurance acceptable to the shipowner and shall bear the insurance premium.

Article 149 During the bareboat charter period, if the shipowner's interests are affected or losses are suffered due to the charterer's possession, use, or operation of the ship, the charterer shall be responsible for eliminating the adverse effects or compensating for the losses.

If the ship is arrested due to a dispute over its ownership or debts owed by the shipowner, the shipowner shall guarantee that the charterer's interests are not affected; if the charterer suffers losses as a result, the shipowner shall be liable for compensation.

Article 150 During the bareboat charter period, the charterer shall not assign the rights and obligations under the contract or sublet the ship by way of bareboat charter without the prior written consent of the shipowner.

Article 151 The shipowner shall not create a mortgage on the ship during the bareboat charter period without the prior written consent of the charterer.

If the shipowner violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and causes losses to the charterer, he shall be liable for compensation.

Article 152 The charterer shall pay the hire as agreed in the contract. If the charterer fails to pay the hire at the time agreed in the contract for a continuous period of seven days or more, the shipowner shall have the right to cancel the contract and claim compensation for any losses suffered thereby.

If the ship is lost or missing, the hire shall cease to be payable from the date of the loss or the date when the last news of the ship was received. Any hire paid in advance shall be refunded proportionately.

Article 153 The provisions of Articles 134, paragraph 1 of Article 135, Article 142, and Article 143 of this Code shall apply to bareboat charter parties.

Article 154 In a bareboat charter party containing a hire-purchase clause, the ownership of the ship shall pass to the charterer when the charterer has paid off the hire-purchase price to the shipowner as agreed in the contract.

## Chapter 7 Contracts of Sea Towage

Article 155 A contract of sea towage is a contract under which the tugowner uses a tug to tow an object by sea from one place to another, and the tow party pays the towage fee.

The provisions of this Chapter shall not apply to tug services provided to ships within a port area.

Article 156 A contract of sea towage shall be concluded in writing. The contents of a contract of sea towage mainly include the names and addresses of the tugowner and the tow party, the names and principal dimensions of the tug and the tow, the horsepower of the tug, the place of departure and the destination, the date of departure, the towage fee and its method of payment, and other relevant matters.

Article 157 Before and at the commencement of the towage, the tugowner shall exercise due diligence to make the tug seaworthy and tow-worthy, properly man the tug, equip it with towing gear and supplies, and provide other necessary appliances and equipment for the voyage.

Before and at the commencement of the towage, the tow party shall make the tow ready for towage, exercise due diligence to make the tow in a tow-worthy condition, truthfully inform the tugowner of the condition of the tow, and provide the certificate and relevant documents issued by the relevant inspection authority certifying that the tow is fit for towage.

Article 158 If, before the commencement of the towage, the contract cannot be performed due to force majeure or other causes not attributable to either party, either party may cancel the contract, and neither party shall be liable to the other for compensation. Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, if the towage fee has already been paid, the tugowner shall refund it to the tow party.

Article 159 If, after the commencement of the towage, the contract cannot be further performed due to force majeure or other causes not attributable to either party, either party may cancel the contract, and neither party shall be liable to the other for compensation.

Article 160 If the tow cannot be towed to the destination due to force majeure or other causes not attributable to either party, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the tugowner may deliver the tow to the tow party or his agent at a place near the destination or at a safe port or anchorage selected by the master of the tug, and such delivery shall be deemed to have fulfilled the contract.

Article 161 If the tow party fails to pay the towage fee and other reasonable expenses as agreed, the tugowner shall have a lien over the tow.**Article 162** During the course of towage at sea, if the loss suffered by the tugowner or the tow party is caused by the fault of one of the parties, the party at fault shall be liable for compensation. If the loss is caused by the fault of both parties, each party shall be liable in proportion to the degree of its fault.

Notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding paragraph, if the tugowner proves that the loss of the tow party is caused by any of the following circumstances, the tugowner shall not be liable for compensation:

(1) Fault of the master, crew members, pilots or other employees or agents of the tugowner in the navigation or management of the tug;
(2) Fault of the tug in saving or attempting to save life or property at sea.

The provisions of this Article shall apply only when there is no stipulation or different stipulation in the towage contract at sea.

**Article 163** During the course of towage at sea, if personal injury or death or loss of property of a third party is caused by the fault of the tugowner or the tow party, the tugowner and the tow party shall be jointly and severally liable to the third party. Unless otherwise provided in the contract, a party who has paid compensation in excess of its proportionate share shall have the right of recourse against the other party.

**Article 164** Where the owner of a tug tows a barge owned or operated by it to carry cargo by sea from one port to another, it shall be deemed as maritime cargo transportation.

## Chapter VIII: Collision of Ships

**Article 165** Collision of ships refers to an accident causing damage arising from the contact of ships with each other at sea or in other navigable waters connected therewith.

The term "ships" mentioned in the preceding paragraph includes any other non-military or non-government service vessels colliding with the ships referred to in Article 3 of this Law.

**Article 166** When a collision occurs between ships, the masters of the ships involved shall, provided it does not seriously endanger their own ships and the persons on board, do their utmost to rescue the other ship and the persons on board.

The masters of the ships involved in the collision shall, as far as possible, make known to each other the names of their ships, their ports of registry, ports of departure and ports of destination.

**Article 167** If a collision between ships is caused by force majeure or other causes not attributable to the fault of any party, or if the cause thereof is unascertainable, the parties involved shall not be liable for compensation to each other.

**Article 168** If a collision between ships is caused by the fault of one of the ships, that ship shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 169** If the ships involved in a collision are all at fault, each ship shall be liable in proportion to the degree of its fault. If the degrees of fault are equal or the proportions of fault cannot be determined, the liability shall be apportioned equally.

Ships at fault shall be liable for the loss of or damage to the ships, cargo and other property on board caused by the collision in proportion to the degrees of their fault as stipulated in the preceding paragraph. Where the collision causes loss of or damage to the property of a third party, the liability of each ship at fault shall not exceed the proportion it ought to bear.

Ships at fault shall be jointly and severally liable for personal injury or death caused to a third party by the collision. A ship that has paid compensation in excess of the proportion stipulated in the first paragraph of this Article shall have the right of recourse against the other ship(s) at fault.

**Article 170** If a ship, by improper maneuvering or by failing to comply with navigation regulations, causes loss or damage to another ship and the persons, cargo or other property on board that ship, even though no actual collision has occurred, the provisions of this Chapter shall apply.

## Chapter IX: Salvage at Sea

**Article 171** The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to salvage operations rendered to ships and other property in distress at sea or in any other navigable waters connected therewith.

**Article 172** For the purposes of this Chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings hereby assigned to them:

(1) "Ship" means a ship referred to in Article 3 of this Law and any other non-military or non-government service vessel which has a salvage relationship with such a ship.
(2) "Property" means any property not permanently and intentionally attached to the shoreline, including freight at risk.
(3) "Salvage award" means any reward, remuneration or compensation payable to the salvor by the salved party in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

**Article 173** The provisions of this Chapter shall not apply to fixed or floating platforms or mobile offshore drilling units when such platforms or units are on location engaged in the exploration, exploitation or production of seabed mineral resources.

**Article 174** The master shall be bound, so far as he can do so without serious danger to his ship and persons on board, to render assistance to any person in danger of being lost at sea.

**Article 175** A salvage contract is concluded when an agreement is reached between the salvor and the salved party regarding the salvage operation at sea.

The master of a ship in distress shall have the authority to conclude a salvage contract on behalf of the shipowner. The master of a ship in distress or the shipowner shall have the authority to conclude a salvage contract on behalf of the owner of the property on board.

**Article 176** A contract for salvage operations may be annulled or modified by a court or arbitration institution if:

(1) the contract was concluded under undue influence or the influence of danger and its terms are inequitable; or
(2) the payment provided for in the contract is in an excessive degree too large or too small for the services actually rendered.

**Article 177** During the salvage operation, the salvor shall owe a duty to the salved party to:

(1) carry out the salvage operation with due care;
(2) exercise due care to prevent or minimize damage to the environment;
(3) seek assistance from other salvors when reasonably necessary; and
(4) accept the reasonable request of the salved party to accept the participation of other salvors, provided that if such request is not reasonable, the amount of the salvor's award shall not be affected.

**Article 178** During the salvage operation, the salved party shall owe a duty to the salvor to:

(1) cooperate fully with the salvor;
(2) exercise due care to prevent or minimize damage to the environment; and
(3) when the ship or other property has been brought to a place of safety, promptly accept the salvor's reasonable request for the redelivery of the ship or property.

**Article 179** A salvor who has carried out a salvage operation in respect of a ship or other property in distress shall be entitled to a salvage award if the operation has had a useful result. No payment is due under this Chapter unless the salvage operation has had a useful result, except as otherwise provided in Article 182 of this Law or by other laws or by the contract.

**Article 180** The salvage award shall be fixed with a view to encouraging salvage operations, and shall take into account the following criteria:

(1) the salved value of the ship and other property;
(2) the skill and efforts of the salvors in preventing or minimizing damage to the environment;
(3) the measure of success obtained by the salvor;
(4) the nature and degree of the danger;
(5) the skill and efforts of the salvors in salving the ship, other property and life;
(6) the time used and expenses and losses incurred by the salvors;
(7) the risk of liability and other risks run by the salvors or their equipment;
(8) the promptness of the services rendered;
(9) the availability and use of ships or other equipment intended for salvage operations;
(10) the state of readiness and efficiency of the salvor's equipment and the value thereof.

The salvage award shall not exceed the salved value of the ship and other property.

**Article 181** The salved value of the ship and other property means the estimated value of the ship and other property after salvage or the proceeds from their sale, after deducting the relevant taxes, customs duties, quarantine and inspection fees, and expenses incurred for unloading, storage, appraisal and sale.

The value referred to in the preceding paragraph shall not include the value of the salved personal effects of the crew and the salved baggage of the passengers.

**Article 182** If the salvor has carried out a salvage operation in respect of a ship or cargo on board which by itself or its cargo threatened damage to the environment and has failed to earn a salvage award under Article 180 of this Law at least equivalent to the special compensation assessable in accordance with this Article, he shall be entitled to special compensation from the owner of that ship equivalent to his expenses as herein defined.

If the salvor has carried out the salvage operation mentioned in the preceding paragraph and has prevented or minimized damage to the environment, the special compensation payable by the owner to the salvor under the preceding paragraph may be increased up to a maximum of 30% of the expenses incurred by the salvor. A court or arbitration institution may, if it deems it fair and just and taking into account the provisions of paragraph 1 of Article 180 of this Law, increase such special compensation further, but in no event shall the total increase be more than 100% of the expenses incurred by the salvor.

For the purposes of this Article, "salvor's expenses" means the out-of-pocket expenses reasonably incurred by the salvor in the salvage operation and a fair rate for equipment and personnel actually and reasonably used in the salvage operation, taking into account the criteria set out in paragraphs (8), (9) and (10) of Article 180, paragraph 1 of this Law.

The total special compensation under this Article shall be paid only if and to the extent that such compensation is greater than any salvage award recoverable by the salvor under Article 180 of this Law, and the amount of such payment shall be the difference between the special compensation and the salvage award.

If the salvor has been negligent and has thereby failed to prevent or minimize damage to the environment, he may be deprived of the whole or part of any special compensation due under this Article.

Nothing in this Article shall affect the right of recourse of the shipowner against any other salved party.

**Article 183** The salvage award shall be apportioned between the owners of the salved ship and other property in proportion to the respective salved values of the ship and other property.

**Article 184** The salvage award for the salvors participating in the same salvage operation shall be determined by agreement among them on the basis of the criteria set out in Article 180 of this Law; failing agreement, the matter may be submitted to the court having jurisdiction for a decision, or, upon agreement of the parties, to an arbitration institution for an award.

**Article 185** No remuneration is due from persons whose lives are saved, but nothing in this Article shall affect the right of the salvor of human life to a fair share of the payment awarded to the salvor for salving the ship or other property or for preventing or minimizing damage to the environment.

**Article 186** The following salvage operations shall not be entitled to a salvage award:

(1) salvage operations performed as a normal duty under a towage contract or other service contract, unless the services rendered go beyond the performance of such duty;
(2) salvage operations performed despite the express and reasonable prohibition of the master, the shipowner or the owner of any other property in distress.

**Article 187** A salvor who has carried out a salvage operation rendered necessary or more difficult by his fault or who has been guilty of fraud or other dishonest conduct shall be deprived of the whole or part of the payment due to him.

**Article 188** After the completion of the salvage operation, the salved party shall, at the request of the salvor, provide satisfactory security for the salvage award.

Without prejudice to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the owner of the salved ship shall use his best endeavors to cause the owners of the cargo to provide satisfactory security for the portion of the salvage award attributable to the cargo before the cargo is released.

The salved ship and other property shall not, without the consent of the salvor, be removed from the port or place at which they first arrive after the completion of the salvage operation until satisfactory security has been given for the salvor's claim.

**Article 189** A court or arbitration institution having jurisdiction over the claim for a salvage award may, by interim order, direct the salved party to pay the salvor such amount as seems fair and just, and on such terms, including terms as to security, as may be just and reasonable.

Where the salved party has made an interim payment under the preceding paragraph, the amount of security provided under Article 188 of this Law shall be reduced accordingly.

**Article 190** If the salved party has not paid the salvage award or provided satisfactory security within ninety days after the date of the salvage of the ship or other property, the salvor may apply to the court for an order for the forced sale of the salved ship or other property. If the salved ship or other property cannot be kept or is not easy to keep, or the storage expenses may exceed its value, the salvor may apply for an earlier sale.

The proceeds from the sale shall, after deduction of all expenses incurred for the storage and sale, be applied to the payment of the salvage award in accordance with the provisions of this Law. The remainder, if any, shall be returned to the salved party; if the remainder cannot be returned and remains unclaimed for one year from the date of the sale, it shall be paid into the State Treasury. If there is a deficiency, the salvor shall have the right of recourse against the salved party.

**Article 191** The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to salvage operations performed between ships of the same owner.

**Article 192** Where a salvage operation is carried out by or under the control of the competent authorities of the State, the salvors shall be entitled to the rights and remedies provided for in this Chapter in respect of salvage operations.

## Chapter X: General Average

**Article 193** General average means the extraordinary sacrifice or expenditure intentionally and reasonably made or incurred for the common safety for the purpose of preserving from peril the ship, cargo and other property engaged in a common maritime adventure.

Loss or damage sustained by the ship or cargo through delay, whether on the voyage or subsequently, such as demurrage and loss of market, and any indirect loss whatsoever, shall not be admitted as general average.

**Article 194** When a ship is damaged by accident, sacrifice or other extraordinary circumstances, and for the safe completion of the voyage, it becomes necessary to put into a port or place of refuge or to return to its port or place of loading for necessary repairs, the port charges, wages and maintenance of the crew, fuel and stores consumed, and the loss or damage and expenses incurred in discharging, storing, reloading or moving the cargo, fuel, stores or other property on board for the purpose of such repairs, shall be allowed as general average.

**Article 195** Any extra expense incurred in place of another expense which would have been allowable as general average shall be deemed to be general average and so allowed, but the amount allowable shall not exceed the amount of the general average expense avoided.

**Article 196** The party claiming a general average contribution shall have the burden of proof to show that the loss or expense claimed is properly allowable as general average.

**Article 197** The fact that the event which gave rise to the sacrifice or expenditure may have been due to the fault of one of the parties to the adventure shall not affect the right to contribution in general average, but this shall not prejudice any remedies or defenses which may be open against or to that party in respect of such fault.

**Article 198** The amount of sacrifice of the ship, cargo and freight in general average shall be determined as follows:

(1) The amount of sacrifice of the ship shall be calculated on the basis of the actual cost of repairs, less a reasonable deduction for "new for old". Where the ship has not been repaired, the amount shall be calculated on the basis of the reasonable depreciation resulting from the sacrifice, but not exceeding the estimated cost of repairs.

Where the ship is an actual total loss or the cost of repairs would exceed the value of the ship when repaired, the amount of sacrifice shall be calculated on the basis of the estimated sound value of the ship, less the estimated cost of repairs not allowable as general average and the value of the ship after the damage.

(2) The amount of sacrifice of cargo shall be calculated, where the cargo is lost, on the basis of the value of the cargo at the time of shipment plus insurance and freight, less the freight which would have been payable but for the sacrifice. Where the cargo is damaged and is sold before agreement on the extent of the damage, the amount shall be calculated on the basis of the difference between the value of the cargo at the time of shipment plus insurance and freight and the net proceeds of the sale.

(3) The amount of sacrifice of freight shall be calculated on the basis of the loss of freight resulting from the sacrifice of the cargo, less the operating expenses which would have been incurred to earn such freight but for the sacrifice.

**Article 199** General average shall be borne by the contributing parties in proportion to their respective contributory values.

The contributory values of the ship, cargo and freight shall be determined as follows:

(1) The contributory value of the ship shall be calculated on the basis of the sound value of the ship at the termination of the voyage, less the amount of loss or damage not allowable as general average, or on the basis of the actual value of the ship at the termination of the voyage, plus the amount of sacrifice allowed as general average.

(2) The contributory value of the cargo shall be calculated on the basis of the value of the cargo at the time of shipment plus insurance and freight, less the amount of loss or damage not allowable as general average and the freight at risk of the carrier. Where the cargo is sold before arrival at the port of destination, the contributory value shall be calculated on the basis of the net proceeds of the sale, plus the amount of sacrifice allowed as general average.

Passengers' luggage and personal effects shall not contribute in general average.

(3) The contributory value of freight shall be calculated on the basis of the freight at risk of the carrier and which is earned at the termination of the voyage, less the operating expenses incurred after the general average event to complete the voyage, plus the amount of sacrifice allowed as general average.

**Article 200** Cargo which was not declared or was falsely declared shall contribute to general average, but the special sacrifice suffered by such cargo shall not be allowed as general average.

Where the value of the cargo was improperly declared at a value less than its actual value, contribution to general average shall be made on the basis of its actual value; in the event of a general average sacrifice, the amount of sacrifice shall be calculated on the basis of the declared value.

**Article 201** Interest shall be allowed on expenditure, sacrifices and allowances charged to general average. Commission shall be allowed on advances of general average expenses, except for wages, maintenance of the crew and fuel and stores consumed.

**Article 202** Upon the request of the parties interested, the parties liable to contribute shall provide security for the general average contribution.

Where the security is provided by way of a cash deposit, the deposit shall be paid to the average adjuster who shall hold it in a bank in the name of the depositor.

The provision, use or refund of the deposit shall be without prejudice to the ultimate liability of the contributing parties.

**Article 203** The adjustment of general average shall be governed by the rules agreed upon in the relevant contract. Failing such agreement, the provisions of this Chapter shall apply.

## Chapter XI: Limitation of Liability for Maritime Claims

**Article 204** Shipowners and salvors may limit their liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter for the maritime claims set out in Article 207 of this Law.

The term "shipowner" mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall include the charterer and the operator of a ship.

**Article 205** If the maritime claims set out in Article 207 of this Law are not made against the shipowner or salvor himself but against persons for whose act, neglect or default the shipowner or salvor is responsible, such persons shall be entitled to limit their liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

**Article 206** An insurer of liability for maritime claims shall be entitled to the same limitation of liability as the assured himself, provided that the assured is entitled to limit his liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

**Article 207** Except as provided otherwise in Articles 208 and 209 of this Law, the following claims, whatever the basis of liability may be, shall be subject to limitation of liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter:

(1) Claims in respect of loss of life or personal injury or loss of or damage to property occurring on board or in direct connection with the operation of the ship or with salvage operations, including damage to harbor works, basins and waterways and aids to navigation, and consequential loss resulting therefrom;(2) Claims for compensation for losses arising from delay in delivery in maritime carriage of goods or from delayed arrival in the carriage of passengers and their luggage;

(3) Claims for compensation for other losses arising from infringement of non-contractual rights directly related to the operation of a ship or salvage operations;

(4) Claims for compensation by persons other than the person liable for measures taken to avert or minimize losses for which the person liable may limit liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter, and claims for further losses caused by such measures.

The limitation of liability may be invoked for the claims set out in the preceding paragraph, regardless of the manner in which they are presented. However, with respect to the remuneration payable by the person liable under a contractual agreement as referred to in sub-paragraph (4), the person liable may not invoke the provisions on limitation of liability under this Article.

Article 208 The provisions of this Chapter shall not apply to the following:

(1) Claims for salvage payments or contribution in general average;

(2) Claims for oil pollution damage covered by the International Convention on Civil Liability for Oil Pollution Damage to which the People's Republic of China is a party;

(3) Claims for nuclear damage covered by the International Convention on Limitation of Liability for Nuclear Damage to which the People's Republic of China is a party;

(4) Claims for nuclear damage caused by a nuclear-powered ship;

(5) Claims by the employees of the shipowner or salvor, where under the law governing the contract of employment, the shipowner or salvor is not entitled to limit liability for such claims, or where such law provides for limits of liability higher than those provided for in this Chapter.

Article 209 A person liable shall not be entitled to limit liability in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter if it is proved that the loss resulting from the claim arose from his intentional act or omission, or from his reckless act or omission with knowledge that such loss would probably result.

Article 210 Subject to Article 211 of this Law, the limitation of liability for maritime claims shall be calculated as follows:

(1) Claims for loss of life or personal injury:

a. For a ship with a gross tonnage between 300 and 500 tons, the limit of liability shall be 333,000 Units of Account;

b. For a ship with a gross tonnage exceeding 500 tons, the provisions of sub-paragraph a of this paragraph shall apply to the portion up to 500 tons, and the following additional amounts shall be added for the portion exceeding 500 tons:

For the portion from 501 to 3,000 tons, 500 Units of Account per ton;

For the portion from 3,001 to 30,000 tons, 333 Units of Account per ton;

For the portion from 30,001 to 70,000 tons, 250 Units of Account per ton;

For the portion exceeding 70,000 tons, 167 Units of Account per ton.

(2) Claims other than those for loss of life or personal injury:

a. For a ship with a gross tonnage between 300 and 500 tons, the limit of liability shall be 167,000 Units of Account;

b. For a ship with a gross tonnage exceeding 500 tons, the provisions of sub-paragraph a of this paragraph shall apply to the portion up to 500 tons, and the following additional amounts shall be added for the portion exceeding 500 tons:

For the portion from 501 to 30,000 tons, 167 Units of Account per ton;

For the portion from 30,001 to 70,000 tons, 125 Units of Account per ton;

For the portion exceeding 70,000 tons, 83 Units of Account per ton.

(3) Where the limit of liability provided for in paragraph (1) is insufficient to satisfy all claims for loss of life or personal injury, the unpaid balance shall rank equally with claims other than those for loss of life or personal injury and be satisfied pro rata from the amount provided for in paragraph (2).

(4) Without prejudice to the provisions of paragraph (3) concerning claims for loss of life or personal injury, claims for damage to harbor works, basins, waterways, and aids to navigation shall have priority over other claims under paragraph (2).

(5) For a salvor not operating from any ship or operating solely on the ship being salved, the limit of liability shall be calculated as for a ship with a gross tonnage of 1,500 tons.

For ships with a gross tonnage of less than 300 tons, ships engaged in transport between ports of the People's Republic of China, and ships engaged in coastal operations, the limits of liability shall be prescribed by the competent department of transportation under the State Council and implemented upon approval by the State Council.

Article 211 The limitation of liability for claims for loss of life or personal injury to passengers carried by sea shall be calculated as 46,666 Units of Account multiplied by the number of passengers which the ship is authorized to carry according to the ship's certificate, but the maximum amount shall not exceed 25,000,000 Units of Account.

For claims for loss of life or personal injury to passengers carried by sea between ports of the People's Republic of China, the limits of liability shall be prescribed by the competent department of transportation under the State Council and implemented upon approval by the State Council.

Article 212 The limits of liability provided for in Articles 210 and 211 of this Law shall apply to the aggregate of all claims arising from the same incident against the shipowner, the salvor themselves, and any person for whose act, neglect, or default they are responsible.

Article 213 A person liable who wishes to limit liability in accordance with the provisions of this Law may constitute a limitation fund with a competent court. The amount of the fund shall be the limit provided for in Articles 210 and 211 of this Law, together with interest thereon from the date of the occurrence giving rise to the liability until the date of the constitution of the fund.

Article 214 Where a limitation fund has been constituted by a person liable, any person who has made a claim against the person liable shall not be entitled to exercise any right against any assets of the person liable. Where the ship or other property of the person liable who has constituted the limitation fund has been arrested or attached, or where security has been provided by the person constituting the fund, the court shall order the release of the ship or property or the return of the security without delay.

Article 215 Where a person entitled to limitation of liability under the provisions of this Chapter has a counterclaim against the claimant arising from the same incident, the respective claims shall be set off against each other, and the provisions of this Chapter shall apply only to the balance, if any.

## Chapter 12: Contract of Marine Insurance

### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 216 A contract of marine insurance is a contract whereby the insurer undertakes, as agreed, to indemnify the insured against loss arising from an insured event to the subject matter insured, or against liability incurred, and the insured pays the premium.

The term "insured event" as used in the preceding paragraph means any maritime event agreed upon between the insurer and the insured, including events occurring in inland waters or on land which are incidental to a sea voyage.

Article 217 The contents of a contract of marine insurance mainly include the following:

(1) Name of the insurer;

(2) Name of the insured;

(3) Subject matter insured;

(4) Insured value;

(5) Sum insured;

(6) Insured liability and exclusions;

(7) Period of insurance;

(8) Premium.

Article 218 The following may be the subject matter of marine insurance:

(1) Ships;

(2) Cargo;

(3) Earnings from the operation of a ship, including freight, charter hire, and passenger ticket revenue;

(4) Expected profit on cargo;

(5) Crew wages and other remuneration;

(6) Liability to a third party;

(7) Other property that may suffer loss due to an insured event, and any liability or expenses arising therefrom.

The insurer may reinsure the insurance of the subject matter referred to in the preceding paragraph. Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the original insured shall not be entitled to the benefit of the reinsurance.

Article 219 The insured value of the subject matter insured shall be agreed upon between the insurer and the insured.

Where the insured value is not agreed upon between the insurer and the insured, the insured value shall be calculated as follows:

(1) The insured value of a ship shall be the value of the ship at the commencement of the insurance liability, including the value of the hull, machinery, and equipment, plus the value of fuel, stores, rigging, provisions, fresh water, and the insurance premium;

(2) The insured value of cargo shall be the invoice price of the cargo at the place of shipment, or the actual value of non-commercial goods at the place of shipment, plus freight and insurance premium, at the commencement of the insurance liability;

(3) The insured value of freight shall be the total amount of freight receivable by the carrier plus the insurance premium, at the commencement of the insurance liability;

(4) The insured value of other subject matter insured shall be the actual value of the subject matter insured plus the insurance premium, at the commencement of the insurance liability.

Article 220 The sum insured shall be agreed upon between the insurer and the insured. The sum insured shall not exceed the insured value; any portion exceeding the insured value shall be void.

### Section 2: Conclusion, Rescission, and Transfer of the Contract

Article 221 A contract of marine insurance is concluded when the insured submits an application for insurance, the insurer agrees to underwrite the risk, and an agreement is reached on the terms of the contract. The insurer shall promptly issue an insurance policy or other insurance certificate to the insured, specifying the terms agreed upon by the parties.

Article 222 Before the conclusion of the contract, the insured shall truthfully disclose to the insurer material circumstances which the insured knows or ought to know in the ordinary course of business and which are relevant to the insurer's determination of the premium rate or whether to agree to underwrite the risk.

The insured need not disclose circumstances which the insurer knows or ought to know in the ordinary course of business, and which the insurer has not inquired about.

Article 223 If the insured intentionally fails to truthfully disclose the material circumstances as required by paragraph 1 of Article 222 of this Law, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract without refunding the premium. The insurer shall not be liable for losses caused by an insured event occurring before the rescission of the contract.

If the insured fails to truthfully disclose the material circumstances as required by paragraph 1 of Article 222 of this Law, but not intentionally, the insurer shall have the right to rescind the contract or demand an increase in the premium. If the insurer rescinds the contract, the insurer shall be liable for losses caused by an insured event occurring before the rescission; however, this shall not apply if the non-disclosure or misrepresentation of the material circumstances had no effect on the occurrence of the insured event.

Article 224 If, at the time of concluding the contract, the insured knew or ought to have known that the subject matter insured had already suffered loss due to an insured event, the insurer shall not be liable but shall be entitled to the premium. If the insurer knew or ought to have known that the subject matter insured could not possibly suffer loss due to an insured event, the insured shall be entitled to recover the premium already paid.

Article 225 Where the insured enters into several contracts with several insurers for the same subject matter insured and the same insured event, and the total sum insured exceeds the insured value, the insured may claim compensation from any of the insurers, unless otherwise agreed in the contracts. The total amount of compensation received by the insured shall not exceed the damaged value of the subject matter insured. Each insurer shall be liable for compensation in proportion to the sum insured by it to the total sum insured. Any insurer that has paid compensation in excess of its share of liability shall have the right of recourse against the insurers that have not paid their share of liability.

Article 226 Before the commencement of the insurance liability, the insured may request the rescission of the contract, but shall pay a handling fee to the insurer, and the insurer shall refund the premium.

Article 227 Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, neither the insured nor the insurer may rescind the contract after the commencement of the insurance liability.

Where the contract may be rescinded after the commencement of the insurance liability according to the terms of the contract, if the insured requests rescission, the insurer shall be entitled to the premium from the commencement of the insurance liability to the date of rescission, and shall refund the remaining portion. If the insurer requests rescission, it shall refund to the insured the premium from the date of rescission to the expiry of the insurance period.

Article 228 Notwithstanding the provisions of Article 227 of this Law, for cargo insurance and voyage insurance on a ship, the insured may not request rescission of the contract after the commencement of the insurance liability.

Article 229 A contract of marine cargo insurance may be transferred by the insured by endorsement or other means, and the rights and obligations under the contract shall be transferred accordingly. If the premium has not been paid at the time of the transfer, the insured and the transferee of the contract shall be jointly and severally liable for its payment.

Article 230 The transfer of a ship insurance contract due to the transfer of the ship shall be subject to the consent of the insurer. Without the consent of the insurer, the ship insurance contract shall be rescinded from the time of the transfer of the ship. If the transfer of the ship occurs during a voyage, the ship insurance contract shall be rescinded at the end of that voyage.

After the rescission of the contract, the insurer shall refund to the insured the premium from the date of rescission to the expiry of the insurance period.

Article 231 An insured who ships or receives goods in batches over a period of time may conclude an open cover with the insurer. The open cover shall be confirmed by the insurer issuing an open cover certificate.

Article 232 At the request of the insured, the insurer shall issue separate insurance certificates for the goods shipped in batches under the open cover.

If the contents of the separately issued insurance certificates are inconsistent with those of the open cover certificate, the separately issued insurance certificates shall prevail.

Article 233 When the insured learns that the goods insured under the open cover have been shipped or have arrived, the insured shall immediately notify the insurer. The notification shall include the name of the ship carrying the goods, the voyage, the value of the goods, and the sum insured.

### Section 3: Obligations of the Insured

Article 234 Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insured shall pay the premium immediately after the conclusion of the contract. Before the insured pays the premium, the insurer may refuse to issue the insurance certificate.

Article 235 If the insured breaches a warranty clause agreed upon in the contract, the insured shall immediately notify the insurer in writing. Upon receiving the notice, the insurer may rescind the contract or demand a modification of the underwriting conditions or an increase in the premium.

Article 236 Upon the occurrence of an insured event, the insured shall immediately notify the insurer and take necessary and reasonable measures to prevent or minimize the loss. If the insured receives a special notice from the insurer regarding the taking of reasonable measures to prevent or minimize the loss, the insured shall act in accordance with the insurer's notice.

The insurer shall not be liable for any increased loss resulting from the insured's breach of the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

### Section 4: Liability of the Insurer

Article 237 After a loss caused by an insured event occurs, the insurer shall promptly pay insurance compensation to the insured.

Article 238 The insurer's compensation for loss caused by an insured event shall be limited to the sum insured. If the sum insured is less than the insured value, and the subject matter insured suffers a partial loss, the insurer shall be liable for compensation in proportion to the ratio of the sum insured to the insured value.

Article 239 If the subject matter insured suffers losses from several insured events during the period of insurance, the insurer shall compensate for all such losses, even if the total amount of the losses exceeds the sum insured. However, if a total loss occurs after a partial loss that has not been repaired, the insurer shall compensate for the total loss.

Article 240 The necessary and reasonable expenses incurred by the insured for preventing or minimizing the loss for which compensation is payable under the contract, the reasonable expenses for survey and valuation incurred to ascertain the nature and extent of the insured event, and the expenses incurred for carrying out the special instructions of the insurer shall be paid by the insurer in addition to the compensation for the loss of the subject matter insured.

The payment by the insurer for the expenses specified in the preceding paragraph shall be limited to an amount equal to the sum insured.

If the sum insured is less than the insured value, the insurer shall pay the expenses specified in this Article in proportion to the ratio of the sum insured to the insured value, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

Article 241 If the sum insured is less than the contributory value in general average, the insurer shall indemnify the contribution in general average in proportion to the ratio of the sum insured to the contributory value.

Article 242 The insurer shall not be liable for losses intentionally caused by the insured.

Article 243 Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insurer shall not be liable for loss of or damage to cargo caused by any of the following reasons:

(1) Delay in navigation, delay in delivery, or changes in market prices;

(2) Natural wear and tear, inherent vice, or natural characteristics of the cargo;

(3) Improper packing.

Article 244 Unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the insurer shall not be liable for loss of or damage to an insured ship caused by any of the following reasons:

(1) Unseaworthiness of the ship at the time of sailing, unless the insured was unaware thereof in the case of a time policy;

(2) Natural wear and tear or corrosion of the ship.

The provisions of this Article shall apply mutatis mutandis to freight insurance.

### Section 5: Loss of or Damage to the Subject Matter Insured and Abandonment

Article 245 Where the subject matter insured is lost after the occurrence of an insured event, or is so seriously damaged that it has completely lost its original form and utility, or can no longer be owned by the insured, it constitutes an actual total loss.

Article 246 Where, after the occurrence of an insured event, a ship is deemed to be an actual total loss which is unavoidable, or the cost of avoiding an actual total loss would exceed the insured value, it constitutes a constructive total loss.

Where, after the occurrence of an insured event, cargo is deemed to be an actual total loss which is unavoidable, or the cost of avoiding an actual total loss plus the cost of continuing to carry the cargo to its destination would exceed the insured value, it constitutes a constructive total loss.

Article 247 Any loss other than an actual total loss or a constructive total loss shall be a partial loss.

Article 248 Where a ship fails to arrive at its destination within a reasonable time from the place where it was last heard of, and no news of it has been received after two months have elapsed, unless otherwise agreed in the contract, the ship shall be deemed to be missing. The missing of a ship shall be deemed an actual total loss.

Article 249 Where the subject matter insured suffers a constructive total loss, and the insured requests compensation from the insurer for a total loss, the insured shall abandon the subject matter insured to the insurer. The insurer may accept or reject the abandonment, but shall notify the insured of its decision to accept or reject the abandonment within a reasonable time.

Abandonment shall not be subject to any conditions. Once the abandonment is accepted by the insurer, it shall not be withdrawn.

Article 250 Where the insurer accepts the abandonment, all rights and obligations of the insured in respect of the abandoned property shall be transferred to the insurer.

### Section 6: Payment of Insurance Compensation

Article 251 After the occurrence of an insured event and before paying insurance compensation to the insured, the insurer may require the insured to provide evidence and materials relevant to ascertaining the nature of the insured event and the extent of the loss.

Article 252 Where the loss of or damage to the subject matter insured within the scope of insurance liability is caused by a third party, the right of the insured to claim compensation from the third party shall be transferred to the insurer from the date of payment of compensation by the insurer.

The insured shall provide the insurer with necessary documents and information known to the insured, and shall use its best efforts to assist the insurer in pursuing recourse against the third party.

Article 253 If the insured waives the right to claim compensation from the third party without the consent of the insurer, or if the insurer is unable to exercise the right of recourse due to the fault of the insured, the insurer may deduct the corresponding amount from the insurance compensation.

Article 254 When paying insurance compensation, the insurer may deduct from the amount payable the compensation that the insured has already received from the third party.If the compensation obtained by the insurer from a third party exceeds the insurance indemnity paid by the insurer, the excess portion shall be refunded to the insured.

Article 255 After the occurrence of an insured event, the insurer shall have the right to waive its rights to the subject matter insured and pay the full amount of the insurance indemnity as stipulated in the contract, in order to be released from its obligations regarding the subject matter insured.

Where the insurer exercises the right provided for in the preceding paragraph, it shall notify the insured within seven days from the date of receipt of the insured's notice concerning the claim for loss; any necessary and reasonable expenses incurred by the insured before receiving such notice to avert or minimize the loss shall still be reimbursed by the insurer.

Article 256 Except as provided in Article 255 of this Law, where the subject matter insured suffers a total loss and the insurer pays the full amount of the insurance, the insurer shall acquire all rights to the subject matter insured; however, in the case of under-insurance, the insurer shall acquire a partial right to the subject matter insured in proportion to the ratio of the insured amount to the insured value.

## Chapter 13: Limitation of Time

Article 257 The time limit for claims against the carrier for compensation for loss of or damage to goods under a contract of carriage of goods by sea shall be one year, counting from the date on which the carrier delivered or should have delivered the goods. Where, within the limitation period or after its expiry, a person held liable brings a recourse claim against a third party, the limitation period shall be ninety days, counting from the date on which the person seeking recourse settled the original claim or received a copy of the statement of claim filed in the court where the action against that person was instituted.

The time limit for claims under a voyage charter party shall be two years, counting from the date on which the claimant knew or should have known that its rights were infringed.

Article 258 The time limit for claims against the carrier for compensation under a contract of carriage of passengers by sea shall be two years, calculated respectively according to the following provisions:

(1) Claims for personal injury to passengers shall be counted from the date on which the passenger disembarked or should have disembarked;

(2) Claims for death of passengers occurring during the period of carriage shall be counted from the date on which the passenger should have disembarked; where death results from an injury sustained during the period of carriage and occurs after the passenger's disembarkation, the time limit shall be counted from the date of death, provided that this period shall not exceed three years from the date of disembarkation;

(3) Claims for loss of or damage to luggage shall be counted from the date on which the passenger disembarked or should have disembarked.

Article 259 The time limit for claims under a charter party for the hire of a ship shall be two years, counting from the date on which the claimant knew or should have known that its rights were infringed.

Article 260 The time limit for claims under a contract of sea towage shall be one year, counting from the date on which the claimant knew or should have known that its rights were infringed.

Article 261 The time limit for claims arising from a collision of ships shall be two years, counting from the date on which the collision occurred. The time limit for recourse claims as provided for in paragraph 3 of Article 169 of this Law shall be one year, counting from the date on which the parties jointly paid the damages.

Article 262 The time limit for claims arising from salvage at sea shall be two years, counting from the date on which the salvage operation was terminated.

Article 263 The time limit for claims for contribution in general average shall be one year, counting from the date on which the adjustment was completed.

Article 264 The time limit for claims for insurance indemnity under a contract of marine insurance shall be two years, counting from the date on which the insured event occurred.

Article 265 The time limit for claims arising from oil pollution damage caused by a ship shall be three years, counting from the date on which the damage occurred; however, in no case shall the limitation period exceed six years from the date on which the incident causing the damage occurred.

Article 266 The limitation of time shall be suspended if, during the last six months of the limitation period, the claimant is unable to exercise its claim due to force majeure or other obstacles. The limitation period shall continue to run from the date on which the cause of suspension ceases to exist.

Article 267 The limitation of time shall be interrupted by the claimant's institution of an action, submission of the dispute to arbitration, or the respondent's agreement to perform its obligations. However, the limitation of time shall not be interrupted if the claimant withdraws its action or arbitration, or if the action is dismissed by a ruling.

Where the claimant applies for the arrest of a ship, the limitation of time shall be interrupted from the date of such application.

The limitation period shall run anew from the time of interruption.

## Chapter 14: Application of Law in Foreign-Related Relations

Article 268 Where an international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions differing from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except those on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Where neither the laws of the People's Republic of China nor any international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains relevant provisions, international practice may be applied.

Article 269 The parties to a contract may choose the law applicable to the contract, unless otherwise provided by law. Where the parties have not made such a choice, the law of the country with which the contract is most closely connected shall apply.

Article 270 The acquisition, transfer, and extinction of ownership of a ship shall be governed by the law of the flag state of the ship.

Article 271 A mortgage on a ship shall be governed by the law of the flag state of the ship.

Where a mortgage is established on a ship before or during the period of a bareboat charter, the law of the original country of registry of the ship shall apply.

Article 272 Maritime liens shall be governed by the law of the place where the court accepting the case is located.

Article 273 Compensation for damage arising from a collision of ships shall be governed by the law of the place where the tort occurred.

Compensation for damage arising from a collision of ships on the high seas shall be governed by the law of the place where the court accepting the case is located.

Where the colliding ships are of the same nationality, regardless of where the collision occurred, compensation for damage between the colliding ships shall be governed by the law of the flag state of the ships.

Article 274 General average adjustment shall be governed by the law of the place where the adjustment is conducted.

Article 275 Limitation of liability for maritime claims shall be governed by the law of the place where the court accepting the case is located.

Article 276 The application of foreign laws or international practices in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter shall not violate the social and public interests of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter 15: Supplementary Provisions

Article 277 The term "unit of account" as used in this Law refers to the Special Drawing Right as defined by the International Monetary Fund. The amount in Renminbi shall be calculated on the date of the court judgment, the date of the arbitration award, or the date of the agreement between the parties, in accordance with the conversion method for the Special Drawing Right of the International Monetary Fund into Renminbi as prescribed by the competent state foreign exchange authority.

Article 278 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 1993.
