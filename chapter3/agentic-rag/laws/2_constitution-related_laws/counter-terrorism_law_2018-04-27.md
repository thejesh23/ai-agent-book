# Counter-Terrorism Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 27, 2015

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Six Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Frontier Health and Quarantine adopted at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purposes of preventing and punishing terrorist activities, strengthening counter-terrorism work, and safeguarding national security, public security, and the safety of people's lives and property.

Article 2 The State opposes all forms of terrorism, lawfully bans terrorist activity organizations, and shall investigate legal liability according to law against any person who organizes, plans, prepares for, or carries out terrorist activities; advocates terrorism; incites the commission of terrorist activities; organizes, leads, or participates in a terrorist activity organization; or provides assistance for terrorist activities.

The State shall make no compromise with any terrorist activity organization or individual, and shall not grant asylum or refugee status to any terrorist activity personnel.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "terrorism" refers to propositions and actions that create social panic, endanger public security, infringe upon personal and property rights, or coerce state organs or international organizations through means such as violence, destruction, or intimidation, in order to achieve political, ideological, or other purposes.

For the purposes of this Law, "terrorist activities" refer to the following acts of a terrorist nature:

(1) Organizing, planning, preparing for, or carrying out activities that cause or are intended to cause serious social harm, such as casualties, significant property loss, damage to public facilities, or disruption of social order;

(2) Advocating terrorism, inciting the commission of terrorist activities, or illegally possessing articles that advocate terrorism, or compelling others to wear clothing or symbols that advocate terrorism in public places;

(3) Organizing, leading, or participating in a terrorist activity organization;

(4) Providing information, funds, materials, labor, technology, venues, or other support, assistance, or convenience to a terrorist activity organization, terrorist activity personnel, the commission of terrorist activities, or terrorist activity training;

(5) Other terrorist activities.

For the purposes of this Law, "terrorist activity organization" refers to a criminal organization formed by three or more persons for the purpose of committing terrorist activities.

For the purposes of this Law, "terrorist activity personnel" refers to persons who commit terrorist activities and members of terrorist activity organizations.

For the purposes of this Law, "terrorist incident" refers to terrorist activities that are occurring or have occurred and that cause or may cause serious social harm.

Article 4 The State shall incorporate counter-terrorism into its national security strategy, adopt comprehensive measures to address both symptoms and root causes, strengthen capacity building for counter-terrorism, and carry out counter-terrorism work using political, economic, legal, cultural, educational, diplomatic, and military means.

The State opposes all forms of extremism that incite hatred, discrimination, or violence by distorting religious doctrines or by other means, and shall eliminate the ideological foundation of terrorism.

Article 5 Counter-terrorism work shall adhere to the principles of combining specialized work with the mass line, prioritizing prevention while combining punishment with prevention, and striking first to maintain the initiative.

Article 6 Counter-terrorism work shall be carried out in accordance with the law, respecting and safeguarding human rights, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of citizens and organizations.

In counter-terrorism work, citizens' freedom of religious belief and ethnic customs shall be respected, and any discriminatory practices based on region, ethnicity, religion, or other grounds shall be prohibited.

Article 7 The State shall establish a counter-terrorism work leading body to provide unified leadership and command over national counter-terrorism work.

Local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall establish counter-terrorism work leading bodies. People's governments at the county level shall establish counter-terrorism work leading bodies as needed. These bodies shall be responsible for counter-terrorism work in their respective regions under the leadership and command of the higher-level counter-terrorism work leading body.

Article 8 Public security organs, state security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, judicial administrative organs, and other relevant state organs shall, in accordance with their respective functions, implement a work responsibility system and carry out counter-terrorism work in accordance with the law.

The People's Liberation Army, the People's Armed Police Force, and the militia organizations shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws, administrative regulations, military regulations, and orders of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and in accordance with the deployment of the counter-terrorism work leading body, prevent and respond to terrorist activities.

Relevant departments shall establish a mechanism for coordination and cooperation, and rely on and mobilize villagers' committees, residents' committees, enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations to jointly carry out counter-terrorism work.

Article 9 All units and individuals have the obligation to assist and cooperate with relevant departments in carrying out counter-terrorism work. Upon discovering suspected terrorist activities or suspected terrorist activity personnel, they shall promptly report to the public security organs or relevant departments.

Article 10 Units and individuals that make outstanding contributions in reporting terrorist activities or assisting in the prevention or suppression of terrorist activities, as well as those that make other outstanding contributions in counter-terrorism work, shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 11 The People's Republic of China shall exercise criminal jurisdiction over terrorist activity crimes committed outside the territory of the People's Republic of China against the State, its citizens, or its institutions, or over terrorist activity crimes stipulated in international treaties that the People's Republic of China has concluded or acceded to, and shall investigate criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Determination of Terrorist Activity Organizations and Personnel

Article 12 The national counter-terrorism work leading body shall, in accordance with the provisions of Article 3 of this Law, determine terrorist activity organizations and personnel, and the office of the national counter-terrorism work leading body shall make a public announcement thereof.

Article 13 The public security department, state security department, and foreign affairs department under the State Council, as well as provincial-level counter-terrorism work leading bodies, shall submit applications to the national counter-terrorism work leading body for the determination of terrorist activity organizations and personnel when necessary.

Article 14 Financial institutions and specific non-financial institutions shall immediately freeze the funds or other assets of terrorist activity organizations and personnel publicly announced by the office of the national counter-terrorism work leading body, and shall promptly report to the public security department, state security department under the State Council, and the anti-money laundering administrative authority in accordance with regulations.

Article 15 Determined terrorist activity organizations and personnel that disagree with the determination may apply for review through the office of the national counter-terrorism work leading body. The national counter-terrorism work leading body shall conduct a timely review and make a decision to uphold or revoke the determination. The review decision shall be final.

Where the national counter-terrorism work leading body makes a decision to revoke the determination, the office of the national counter-terrorism work leading body shall make a public announcement thereof; if funds or assets have been frozen, they shall be unfrozen.

Article 16 In accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law, an intermediate people's court with jurisdiction may, in the course of trying a criminal case, determine terrorist activity organizations and personnel in accordance with the law. Where a public announcement by the office of the national counter-terrorism work leading body is required after the judgment takes effect, the relevant provisions of this Chapter shall apply.

## Chapter III Security Prevention

Article 17 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall organize and carry out counter-terrorism publicity and education to enhance citizens' awareness of counter-terrorism.

Education and human resources administrative departments, schools, and relevant vocational training institutions shall incorporate knowledge of terrorist activity prevention and emergency response into the content of education, teaching, and training.

News, radio, television, culture, religion, internet, and other relevant entities shall conduct targeted counter-terrorism publicity and education for the public.

Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall assist people's governments and relevant departments in strengthening counter-terrorism publicity and education.

Article 18 Telecommunications business operators and internet service providers shall provide technical interfaces, decryption, and other technical support and assistance to public security organs and state security organs for the lawful prevention and investigation of terrorist activities.

Article 19 Telecommunications business operators and internet service providers shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, implement network security and information content supervision systems, as well as security technical prevention measures, to prevent the dissemination of information containing terrorist or extremist content; upon discovering information containing terrorist or extremist content, they shall immediately stop transmission, preserve relevant records, delete the relevant information, and report to the public security organs or relevant departments.

The competent authorities for cyberspace affairs, telecommunications, public security, state security, and others shall, in accordance with their respective functions, promptly order relevant entities to stop transmission, delete relevant information, or close relevant websites or suspend relevant services regarding information containing terrorist or extremist content. The relevant entities shall immediately execute such orders, preserve relevant records, and assist in investigations. For information containing terrorist or extremist content transmitted across borders on the internet, the competent telecommunications authority shall take technical measures to block its dissemination.

Article 20 Logistics operation entities for railway, highway, waterway, and air freight, as well as postal and express delivery services, shall implement a security inspection system, verify customer identities, and conduct security checks or open-package inspections on transported or delivered items in accordance with regulations. Items that are prohibited from transport or delivery, pose significant safety hazards, or for which the customer refuses security inspection shall not be transported or delivered.

The logistics operation entities specified in the preceding paragraph shall implement a registration system for the identities of customers and information on items transported or delivered.

Article 21 Business operators and service providers in telecommunications, internet, finance, accommodation, long-distance passenger transport, motor vehicle rental, and other sectors shall verify customer identities. Services shall not be provided to persons whose identities are unclear or who refuse identity verification.

Article 22 Production and import entities shall, in accordance with regulations, affix electronic tracking identifiers to firearms and other weapons, ammunition, controlled instruments, hazardous chemicals, civil explosives, and nuclear and radioactive materials, and add security traceability markers to civil explosives.

Transport entities shall, in accordance with regulations, monitor vehicles transporting hazardous chemicals, civil explosives, and nuclear and radioactive materials during operation through positioning systems.

Relevant entities shall, in accordance with regulations, implement strict supervision and management of substances such as pathogens of infectious diseases, and strictly prevent the spread of such substances or their entry into illegal channels.

For controlled instruments, hazardous chemicals, and civil explosives, the relevant competent department under the State Council or a provincial people's government may, as needed, decide to impose controls on production, import and export, transport, sale, use, and disposal in specific areas or during specific periods, and may prohibit the use of cash or physical objects for transactions or impose other restrictions on transaction activities.

Article 23 In the event of theft, robbery, loss, or other loss of control of firearms and other weapons, ammunition, hazardous chemicals, civil explosives, nuclear and radioactive materials, pathogens of infectious diseases, or other substances, the entity where the incident occurred shall immediately take necessary control measures and promptly report to the public security organs, while also reporting to the relevant competent authorities in accordance with regulations. Upon receiving a report, the public security organs shall promptly conduct an investigation. The relevant competent authorities shall cooperate with the public security organs in their work.

No unit or individual shall illegally manufacture, produce, store, transport, import or export, sell, provide, purchase, use, possess, dispose of, or destroy the items specified in the preceding paragraph. If discovered by public security organs, they shall be seized; if discovered by other competent authorities, they shall be seized and promptly reported to the public security organs; if discovered by other units or individuals, they shall promptly report to the public security organs.

Article 24 The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council and relevant departments and institutions under the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and manage the fulfillment of counter-terrorism financing obligations by financial institutions and specific non-financial institutions.

If the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council discovers suspected terrorist financing, it may conduct an investigation in accordance with the law and take provisional freezing measures.

Article 25 When auditing, financial, taxation, and other departments conduct supervision and inspection of relevant entities in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, if they discover that the inflow or outflow of funds is suspected of terrorist financing, they shall promptly notify the public security organs.

Article 26 When customs authorities supervise cash and bearer negotiable securities carried by persons entering or leaving the country, if they discover suspected terrorist financing, they shall promptly report to the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council and the public security organs with jurisdiction.

Article 27 When formulating and implementing urban and rural plans, local people's governments at all levels shall meet the needs of counter-terrorism work.

Local people's governments at all levels shall, as needed, organize and urge relevant construction entities to equip and install technical and physical prevention equipment and facilities for preventing terrorist attacks, such as public security video image information systems, at key locations on main roads, transportation hubs, and urban public areas.

Article 28 Public security organs and relevant departments shall promptly stop and investigate legal liability according to law against acts of advocating extremism, using extremism to endanger public security, disrupt public order, infringe upon personal and property rights, or hinder social management.

If public security organs discover extremist activities, they shall order an immediate stop, forcibly remove the relevant persons from the scene and register their identity information, confiscate relevant items and materials, and seal up the illegal activity premises.

Any unit or individual that discovers items, materials, or information advocating extremism shall promptly report to the public security organs.

Article 29 For persons who are instigated, coerced, or lured into participating in terrorist or extremist activities, or whose participation in such activities is minor and does not constitute a crime, the public security organs shall organize relevant departments, villagers' committees, residents' committees, the person's employer, school, family, and guardians to provide assistance and education.

Prisons, detention centers, and community correction institutions shall strengthen the management, education, and correction of convicted terrorist and extremist offenders. Prisons and detention centers may, based on the needs of education, reform, and maintaining supervision order, house terrorist and extremist offenders together with ordinary criminal offenders or separately.

Article 30 For terrorist and extremist offenders sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or above, prisons and detention centers shall, before their release upon completion of the sentence, conduct a social danger assessment based on the nature and circumstances of the crime, the degree of social harm, their behavior during incarceration, and the potential impact on the community where they will reside after release. When conducting the social danger assessment, opinions from relevant grassroots organizations and the original case-handling organs shall be solicited. If the assessment indicates social danger, the prison or detention center shall submit a proposal for placement in reeducation to the intermediate people's court in the location of the prison, with a copy of the proposal sent to the people's procuratorate at the same level.

If the intermediate people's court in the location of the prison finds that the offender indeed poses a social danger, it shall, before the offender's release upon completion of the sentence, issue a decision ordering the offender to undergo placement in reeducation after release. A copy of the decision shall be sent to the people's procuratorate at the same level. Persons subject to placement in reeducation who disagree with the decision may apply for reconsideration to the people's court at the next higher level.

Placement in reeducation shall be organized and implemented by the provincial people's government. The reeducation institution shall conduct annual assessments of persons in reeducation. For those who show genuine repentance and are unlikely to pose further harm to society, the institution shall promptly propose the termination of reeducation and submit it to the intermediate people's court that made the decision for a ruling. Persons in reeducation have the right to apply for termination of reeducation.

The people's procuratorate shall supervise the decision and implementation of placement in reeducation.

Article 31 Public security organs shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, identify units, places, activities, and facilities that are more likely to be subject to terrorist attacks or that may cause significant casualties, property loss, or social impact if attacked, as key targets for preventing terrorist attacks, and report them to the counter-terrorism work leading body at the same level for record.

Article 32 The management entities of key targets shall perform the following duties:

(1) Formulate plans and measures for preventing and responding to terrorist activities, and conduct regular training and drills;

(2) Establish a special fund guarantee system for counter-terrorism work, and equip and update prevention and response equipment and facilities;

(3) Designate relevant institutions or assign responsible personnel, and clarify job responsibilities;

(4) Conduct risk assessments, monitor security threats in real time, and improve internal security management;

(5) Regularly report to the public security organs and relevant departments on the implementation of prevention measures.

The management entities of key targets shall, in accordance with urban and rural plans, relevant standards, and actual needs, simultaneously design, construct, and operate technical and physical prevention equipment and facilities that comply with the provisions of Article 27 of this Law for the key targets.

The management entities of key targets shall establish management systems for public security video image information systems, including duty monitoring, information storage and use, and operation and maintenance, to ensure the normal operation of relevant systems. The retention period for collected video image information shall not be less than ninety days.

For other units, places, activities, and facilities involving public security that are not key targets, their competent authorities and management entities shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, establish and improve security management systems and implement security responsibilities.

Article 33 The management entities of key targets shall conduct security background checks on personnel in important positions. Personnel found to be unsuitable shall be transferred to other positions, and the relevant information shall be reported to the public security organs.

Article 34 Organizers of large-scale events and management entities of key targets shall, in accordance with regulations, conduct security checks on persons, items, and vehicles entering large-scale event venues, airports, railway stations, docks, urban rail transit stations, long-distance bus stations, ports, and other key targets. If contraband or controlled items are discovered, they shall be detained and promptly reported to the public security organs; if persons suspected of illegal or criminal activities are discovered, they shall be promptly reported to the public security organs.

Article 35 For public transport vehicles such as aircraft, trains, ships, urban rail vehicles, and public buses and trolleybuses, operating entities shall, in accordance with regulations, equip security personnel and corresponding equipment and facilities, and strengthen security checks and protection work.

Article 36 Public security organs and relevant departments shall master the basic information and important dynamics of key targets, and guide and supervise the management entities of key targets in fulfilling their duties to prevent terrorist attacks.

Public security organs and the People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, conduct guard, patrol, and inspection of key targets.

Article 37 The competent authorities for flight control, civil aviation, public security, and others shall, in accordance with their respective functions, strengthen the management of airspace, aircraft, and flight activities, and strictly prevent terrorist activities targeting aircraft or carried out through flight activities.

Article 38 People's governments at all levels and military organs shall install barrier isolation nets, video image capture, and anti-crossing alarm facilities at key national border (frontier) sections and ports.

Public security organs and the People's Liberation Army shall strictly organize border (frontier) patrols, and in accordance with regulations, conduct inspections of persons, means of transport, and items arriving at or departing from the border (frontier) front, entering or leaving border (frontier) management zones and border (frontier) passages and ports, as well as vessels in coastal and border areas.**Article 39** The authorities issuing entry-exit documents and the border inspection authorities have the right to decide not to allow terrorist suspects and terrorist activity suspects to enter or leave the country, not to issue them entry-exit documents, or to declare their entry-exit documents invalid.

**Article 40** If Customs or border inspection authorities discover individuals suspected of terrorist activities or items suspected of being used in terrorist activities, they shall detain them in accordance with the law and immediately transfer them to the public security organs or state security organs.

**Article 41** The competent departments of the State Council for foreign affairs, public security, state security, development and reform, industry and information technology, commerce, tourism, etc., shall establish a security risk assessment system for overseas investment cooperation, tourism, etc., strengthen security protection for Chinese citizens abroad, as well as overseas institutions, facilities, and property, and prevent and respond to terrorist attacks.

**Article 42** Overseas institutions shall establish and improve security protection systems and response plans, and strengthen the security protection of relevant personnel, facilities, and property.

## Chapter IV: Intelligence Information

**Article 43** The National Counter-Terrorism Leading Group shall establish a National Counter-Terrorism Intelligence Center, implement a cross-departmental and cross-regional intelligence information working mechanism, and coordinate counter-terrorism intelligence information work.

Relevant departments shall strengthen the collection of counter-terrorism intelligence information. Intelligence information collected concerning clues, personnel, and actions shall be promptly and uniformly reported to the National Counter-Terrorism Intelligence Center in accordance with regulations.

Local counter-terrorism leading groups shall establish cross-departmental intelligence information working mechanisms, organize and carry out counter-terrorism intelligence information work, promptly report important intelligence information to the higher-level counter-terrorism leading group, and promptly notify relevant localities of urgent intelligence information involving other places.

**Article 44** Public security organs, state security organs, and relevant departments shall rely on the masses, strengthen grassroots basic work, establish grassroots intelligence information work forces, and improve the capability of counter-terrorism intelligence information work.

**Article 45** Public security organs, state security organs, and military organs may, within their scope of duties, adopt technical reconnaissance measures for the needs of counter-terrorism intelligence information work, in accordance with relevant state regulations and after undergoing strict approval procedures.

Materials obtained in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph may only be used for counter-terrorism response and disposal, and for the investigation, prosecution, and trial of terrorist activity crimes and extremist crimes, and shall not be used for other purposes.

**Article 46** Relevant departments shall promptly provide information obtained during the security prevention work stipulated in Chapter III of this Law, as required by the National Counter-Terrorism Intelligence Center.

**Article 47** The National Counter-Terrorism Intelligence Center, local counter-terrorism leading groups, public security organs, and other relevant departments shall screen, assess, verify, and monitor relevant intelligence information. If they believe there is a danger of a terrorist incident occurring and that corresponding security prevention and response measures need to be taken, they shall promptly notify relevant departments and units, and may issue warnings based on the situation. Relevant departments and units shall carry out security prevention and response work based on the notification.

**Article 48** Counter-terrorism leading groups, relevant departments, units, and individuals shall keep confidential any state secrets, commercial secrets, and personal privacy learned during the performance of counter-terrorism duties and obligations.

Those who violate regulations and disclose state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V: Investigation

**Article 49** Upon receiving a report of suspected terrorist activities or discovering suspected terrorist activities that require investigation and verification, the public security organs shall promptly conduct an investigation.

**Article 50** When investigating suspected terrorist activities, the public security organs may, in accordance with relevant legal provisions, interrogate, inspect, and summon suspects; may extract or collect human biometric identification information such as portraits, fingerprints, and iris images, as well as biological samples such as blood, urine, and shed cells; and retain their signatures.

When investigating suspected terrorist activities, the public security organs may notify persons who are aware of the relevant situation to come to the public security organs or other locations to be questioned.

**Article 51** When investigating suspected terrorist activities, the public security organs have the right to collect and obtain relevant information and materials from relevant units and individuals. Relevant units and individuals shall provide them truthfully.

**Article 52** When investigating suspected terrorist activities, with the approval of the head of the public security organ at or above the county level, the public security organs may inquire into the property of the suspect, such as deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, and fund shares, and may take measures such as sealing up, seizing, or freezing assets. The period for sealing up, seizing, or freezing shall not exceed two months. In complex cases, it may be extended by one month with the approval of the head of the public security organ at the next higher level.

**Article 53** When investigating suspected terrorist activities, with the approval of the head of the public security organ at or above the county level, the public security organs may, based on the degree of danger, order the suspected terrorist to comply with one or more of the following restrictive measures:

(1) Not to leave the city, county, or designated place of residence without the approval of the public security organs;
(2) Not to participate in large-scale public events or engage in specific activities;
(3) Not to take public transportation or enter specific places without the approval of the public security organs;
(4) Not to meet or communicate with specific persons;
(5) Regularly report activities to the public security organs;
(6) Surrender passports and other entry-exit documents, identity documents, and driving licenses to the public security organs for safekeeping.

The public security organs may supervise compliance with the restrictive measures through electronic monitoring, irregular inspections, etc.

The period for taking the restrictive measures stipulated in the preceding two paragraphs shall not exceed three months. If the restrictive measures are no longer necessary, they shall be promptly lifted.

**Article 54** If, after investigation, the public security organs discover criminal facts or criminal suspects, they shall file a case for investigation in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law. If the relevant period stipulated in this Chapter expires and the public security organs have not filed a case for investigation, the relevant measures shall be lifted.

## Chapter VI: Response and Disposal

**Article 55** The state shall establish and improve a system of response plans for terrorist incidents.

The National Counter-Terrorism Leading Group shall formulate national response plans by level and category, based on the patterns, characteristics, and potential social harm of terrorist incidents, specifying the organizational command system for terrorist incident response and disposal, procedures for security prevention and response, and post-incident social order restoration.

Relevant departments and local counter-terrorism leading groups shall formulate corresponding response plans.

**Article 56** For the response and disposal of terrorist incidents, counter-terrorism leading groups at all levels shall establish command bodies composed of relevant departments, implementing a commander-in-chief responsibility system. The head of the counter-terrorism leading group may serve as the commander-in-chief, or may designate the head of the public security organ or the head of another member unit of the counter-terrorism leading group to serve as the commander-in-chief.

The response and disposal of terrorist incidents occurring across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, or particularly major terrorist incidents, shall be commanded by the National Counter-Terrorism Leading Group. The response and disposal of terrorist incidents occurring within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government involving multiple administrative regions, or major terrorist incidents, shall be commanded by the provincial-level counter-terrorism leading group.

**Article 57** After a terrorist incident occurs, the local counter-terrorism leading group shall immediately activate the terrorist incident response plan and designate the commander-in-chief. Relevant departments, the People's Liberation Army, the People's Armed Police Force, and the militia organization shall, under the unified leadership and command of the counter-terrorism leading group and the commander-in-chief, coordinate to carry out on-site response and disposal work such as suppression, control, rescue, and medical aid.

Higher-level counter-terrorism leading groups may guide the response and disposal work and, when necessary, mobilize relevant counter-terrorism forces for support.

If a state of emergency needs to be declared, it shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or the State Council in accordance with the authority and procedures stipulated by the Constitution and other relevant laws.

**Article 58** After discovering a terrorist incident or suspected terrorist incident, the public security organs shall immediately carry out disposal and report to the counter-terrorism leading group. If the People's Liberation Army or the People's Armed Police Force discovers ongoing terrorist activities, they shall immediately control the situation and promptly transfer the case to the public security organs.

If the counter-terrorism leading group has not yet designated a commander-in-chief, the highest-ranking officer of the public security organ present at the scene shall serve as the on-site commander. If the public security organs have not arrived at the scene, the highest-ranking officer of the People's Liberation Army or the People's Armed Police Force present at the scene shall serve as the on-site commander. On-site response and disposal personnel, regardless of whether they belong to the same unit or system, shall obey the command of the on-site commander.

After the commander-in-chief is designated, the on-site commander shall report to and seek instructions from the commander-in-chief regarding work or relevant situations.

**Article 59** If Chinese institutions, personnel, or important facilities abroad suffer or are likely to suffer terrorist attacks, the competent departments of the State Council for foreign affairs, public security, state security, commerce, finance, state-owned assets supervision and administration, tourism, transportation, etc., shall promptly activate response plans. The State Council department for foreign affairs shall coordinate with relevant countries to take corresponding measures.

After Chinese institutions, personnel, or important facilities abroad suffer severe terrorist attacks, with the consent of the relevant country through consultation, the National Counter-Terrorism Leading Group may organize departments such as foreign affairs, public security, and state security to send personnel abroad to carry out response and disposal work.

**Article 60** When responding to and disposing of a terrorist incident, priority shall be given to protecting the personal safety of persons directly endangered or threatened by the terrorist activities.

**Article 61** After a terrorist incident occurs, the counter-terrorism leading group responsible for the response and disposal may decide that relevant departments and units take one or more of the following response and disposal measures:

(1) Organize the rescue and treatment of victims, evacuate, relocate, and properly shelter threatened persons, and take other rescue measures;
(2) Seal off the scene and surrounding roads, check the identity documents of persons at the scene, and set up temporary cordons near relevant locations;
(3) Implement airspace, sea (water) area control within specific areas, and inspect means of transport within specific areas;
(4) Implement internet, radio, and telecommunications control within specific areas;
(5) Implement entry-exit control within specific areas or against specific persons;
(6) Prohibit or restrict the use of relevant equipment and facilities, close or restrict the use of relevant places, suspend densely populated activities or production and business activities that may lead to the expansion of harm;
(7) Rush-repair damaged public facilities such as transportation, telecommunications, internet, radio and television, water supply, drainage, power supply, gas supply, and heating;
(8) Organize volunteers to participate in counter-terrorism rescue work, and require persons with specific expertise to provide services;
(9) Other necessary response and disposal measures.

Taking the response and disposal measures stipulated in items (3) to (5) of the preceding paragraph shall be decided or approved by the counter-terrorism leading group at or above the provincial level. Taking the response and disposal measures stipulated in item (6) of the preceding paragraph shall be decided by the counter-terrorism leading group at or above the city level divided into districts. The response and disposal measures shall clearly define the applicable time and spatial scope and be announced to the public.

**Article 62** People's police, people's armed police, and other response and disposal personnel legally equipped with or carrying weapons may use weapons against persons who are holding firearms, knives, or other lethal weapons or using other dangerous methods at the scene, and are committing or preparing to commit violent acts, if warnings are ineffective. In emergency situations or if issuing a warning may lead to more serious harmful consequences, weapons may be used directly.

**Article 63** Information on the occurrence, development, and response to a terrorist incident shall be uniformly released by the provincial-level counter-terrorism leading group of the place where the terrorist incident occurred. For terrorist incidents occurring across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the information shall be uniformly released by the designated provincial-level counter-terrorism leading group.

No unit or individual may fabricate or disseminate false information about terrorist incidents. They shall not report or disseminate details of terrorist activities that may provoke imitation. They shall not publish cruel or inhumane scenes from terrorist incidents. During the response and disposal of a terrorist incident, except for news media approved by the counter-terrorism leading group responsible for releasing information, no reporting or dissemination of the identities of on-site response and disposal personnel or hostages, or details of the response and disposal actions, is permitted.

**Article 64** After the response and disposal of a terrorist incident concludes, people's governments at all levels shall organize relevant departments to help affected units and individuals resume their lives and production as soon as possible, and stabilize social order and public sentiment in the affected areas.

**Article 65** The local people's government shall promptly provide appropriate assistance to the victims of the terrorist incident and their close relatives, and promptly provide basic living security to victims and their close relatives who have lost basic living conditions. The competent departments for health, medical security, etc., shall provide psychological and medical assistance to the victims of the terrorist incident and their close relatives.

**Article 66** The public security organs shall promptly file a case for investigation into the terrorist incident, ascertain the cause, process, and outcome of the incident, and investigate the criminal responsibility of the terrorist organization and personnel in accordance with the law.

**Article 67** The counter-terrorism leading group shall conduct a comprehensive analysis, summary, and evaluation of the occurrence of the terrorist incident and the response and disposal work, propose improvement measures for prevention and response, and report to the counter-terrorism leading group at the next higher level.

## Chapter VII: International Cooperation

**Article 68** The People's Republic of China shall conduct counter-terrorism cooperation with other countries, regions, and international organizations in accordance with the international treaties it has concluded or acceded to, or on the principle of equality and mutual benefit.

**Article 69** The relevant departments of the State Council, authorized by the State Council, shall represent the Chinese government in conducting counter-terrorism policy dialogue, intelligence information exchange, law enforcement cooperation, and international fund supervision cooperation with foreign governments and relevant international organizations.

Without violating Chinese laws, the local people's governments at or above the county level in border areas and their competent departments may, with the approval of the State Council or relevant central departments, conduct counter-terrorism intelligence information exchange, law enforcement cooperation, and international fund supervision cooperation with neighboring countries or regions.

**Article 70** Mutual legal assistance in criminal matters, extradition, and the transfer of sentenced persons involving terrorist activity crimes shall be executed in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

**Article 71** Upon reaching an agreement with the relevant country and reporting to the State Council for approval, the public security department and the state security department of the State Council may send personnel abroad to carry out counter-terrorism missions.

The sending of personnel from the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force abroad to carry out counter-terrorism missions shall be approved by the Central Military Commission.

**Article 72** Materials obtained through international counter-terrorism cooperation may be used as evidence in administrative penalties and criminal proceedings, except where our side has promised not to use them as evidence.

## Chapter VIII: Safeguard Measures

**Article 73** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the division of powers, include counter-terrorism work funds in their respective fiscal budgets at the same level.

The state shall provide necessary financial support to key counter-terrorism areas and provide financial guarantees for the response and disposal of large-scale terrorist incidents.

**Article 74** Public security organs, state security organs, relevant departments, the People's Liberation Army, and the People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with their legal duties, establish professional counter-terrorism forces, strengthen professional training, and equip necessary professional counter-terrorism equipment and facilities.

County-level and township-level people's governments shall, as needed, guide relevant units, villagers' committees, and residents' committees to establish counter-terrorism work forces and volunteer teams to assist and cooperate with relevant departments in carrying out counter-terrorism work.

**Article 75** Persons who become disabled or die due to performing counter-terrorism duties or assisting or cooperating with relevant departments in carrying out counter-terrorism work shall receive corresponding treatment in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 76** If the personal safety of a person who reports or stops terrorist activities, testifies in a terrorist activity crime case, or engages in counter-terrorism work, or their close relatives, is in danger, upon application by the person or their close relatives, the public security organs and relevant departments shall take one or more of the following protective measures:

(1) Not disclose personal information such as real name, address, and work unit;
(2) Prohibit specific persons from contacting the protected person;
(3) Take special protective measures for the person and their residence;
(4) Change the protected person's name, and rearrange their residence and work unit;
(5) Other necessary protective measures.

Public security organs and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, take measures such as not disclosing the real name and address of the protected unit, prohibiting specific persons from approaching the protected unit, taking special protective measures for the office and business premises of the protected unit, and other necessary protective measures.

**Article 77** The state encourages and supports counter-terrorism scientific research and technological innovation, and the development and promotion of advanced counter-terrorism technologies and equipment.

**Article 78** Public security organs, state security organs, the People's Liberation Army, and the People's Armed Police Force may, due to urgent needs in performing counter-terrorism duties, requisition the property of units and individuals in accordance with relevant state regulations. After the mission is completed, the property shall be promptly returned or restored to its original condition, and corresponding fees shall be paid in accordance with regulations. If losses are caused, compensation shall be made.

If the legitimate rights and interests of relevant units and individuals are damaged due to the conduct of counter-terrorism work, compensation shall be made in accordance with the law. Relevant units and individuals have the right to claim compensation in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX: Legal Liability

**Article 79** Organizing, planning, preparing for, or carrying out terrorist activities; advocating terrorism; inciting the commission of terrorist activities; illegally possessing items advocating terrorism; forcing others to wear clothing or symbols advocating terrorism in public places; organizing, leading, or participating in a terrorist organization; providing assistance to terrorist organizations, terrorist activity personnel, the commission of terrorist activities, or terrorist activity training shall be investigated for criminal responsibility in accordance with the law.

**Article 80** Participating in any of the following activities, if the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, shall result in detention by the public security organs for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and may be concurrently fined not more than 10,000 yuan:

(1) Advocating terrorism or extremism, or inciting the commission of terrorist or extremist activities;
(2) Producing, disseminating, or illegally possessing items advocating terrorism or extremism;
(3) Forcing others to wear clothing or symbols advocating terrorism or extremism in public places;
(4) Providing support, assistance, or convenience such as information, funds, materials, labor, technology, or venues for advocating terrorism or extremism, or for carrying out terrorist or extremist activities.**Article 81** Anyone who, using extremism, commits any of the following acts, where the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, shall be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and may also be fined not more than 10,000 yuan:

(1) Forcing others to participate in religious activities, or forcing others to provide property or labor to religious sites or religious personnel;

(2) Using intimidation, harassment, or other means to drive people of other ethnic groups or other beliefs from their places of residence;

(3) Using intimidation, harassment, or other means to interfere with the interactions or cohabitation of others with people of other ethnic groups or other beliefs;

(4) Using intimidation, harassment, or other means to interfere with the living customs, lifestyles, or production and business operations of others;

(5) Obstructing state functionaries from performing their duties according to law;

(6) Distorting or slandering state policies, laws, or administrative regulations, or inciting or instigating resistance to the lawful administration of the people's government;

(7) Inciting or coercing the masses to damage or intentionally damage state-issued documents such as resident identity cards or household registration booklets, or Renminbi;

(8) Inciting or coercing others to replace marriage or divorce registration with religious ceremonies;

(9) Inciting or coercing minors to not receive compulsory education;

(10) Other acts of using extremism to undermine the implementation of the state's legal system.

**Article 82** Anyone who, knowing that another person has committed a crime of terrorist activity or extremism, harbors or covers for them, where the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, or who refuses to provide information when judicial authorities investigate the relevant circumstances or collect relevant evidence, shall be detained by the public security organ for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and may also be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

**Article 83** If a financial institution or a designated non-financial business or profession fails to immediately freeze funds or other assets of a terrorist organization or terrorist individual announced by the office of the state counter-terrorism leading body, the public security organ shall impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the directly responsible directors, senior managers, and other directly responsible personnel; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible directors, senior managers, and other directly responsible personnel, who may also be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

**Article 84** Where a telecommunications business operator or internet service provider falls under any of the following circumstances, the competent authority shall impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel, who may also be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days:

(1) Failing to provide technical interfaces, decryption, and other technical support and assistance to the public security organs or state security organs for the lawful prevention and investigation of terrorist activities as required;

(2) Failing, as required by the competent authority, to stop the transmission or delete information containing terrorist or extremist content, preserve relevant records, close relevant websites, or shut down relevant services;

(3) Failing to implement network security, information content supervision systems, and security technical protection measures, resulting in the dissemination of information containing terrorist or extremist content, where the circumstances are serious.

**Article 85** Where a logistics operation unit for rail, road, water, or air freight, or for postal or courier services, falls under any of the following circumstances, the competent authority shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to implement a security inspection system, failing to verify the identity of customers, or failing to conduct security checks or open-box inspections on items to be transported or shipped as required;

(2) Transporting or shipping items that are prohibited from transport or shipment, that pose a major safety hazard, or for which the customer refuses a security check;

(3) Failing to implement a registration system for the identity of transport or shipment customers and the information of items.

**Article 86** Where a telecommunications, internet, or financial business operator or service provider fails to verify the identity of a customer as required, or provides services to a customer whose identity is unknown or who refuses identity verification, the competent authority shall order rectification; if rectification is refused, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel.

Where a business operator or service provider for accommodation, long-distance passenger transport, or motor vehicle rental falls under the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the competent authority shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel.

**Article 87** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and falls under any of the following circumstances shall be given a warning by the competent authority and ordered to rectify; if rectification is refused, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to affix electronic tracking identifiers to firearms, ammunition, controlled devices, hazardous chemicals, civil explosives, or nuclear and radioactive materials, or failing to add security traceability markers to civil explosives as required;

(2) Failing to monitor, through a positioning system, the means of transport for hazardous chemicals, civil explosives, or nuclear and radioactive materials in operation as required;

(3) Failing to implement strict supervision and management over substances such as infectious disease pathogens, where the circumstances are serious;

(4) Violating control or transaction restriction measures decided by the relevant department of the State Council or a provincial people's government regarding controlled devices, hazardous chemicals, or civil explosives.

**Article 88** Where the management or operation unit of a key target for preventing terrorist attacks violates the provisions of this Law and falls under any of the following circumstances, the public security organ shall give a warning and order rectification; if rectification is refused, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to formulate plans or measures for preventing and responding to terrorist activities;

(2) Failing to establish a special fund guarantee system for counter-terrorism work, or failing to equip prevention and response equipment or facilities;

(3) Failing to designate a working body or responsible personnel;

(4) Failing to conduct security background checks on personnel in important positions, or failing to transfer personnel who are unsuitable to other positions;

(5) Failing to equip public transport vehicles with security personnel and corresponding equipment or facilities as required;

(6) Failing to establish management systems for the monitoring, information storage and use, and operation and maintenance of public security video image information systems.

Where the organizer of a large-scale event or the management unit of a key target fails to conduct security checks on persons, items, and vehicles entering the large-scale event venue, airport, railway station, port, urban rail transit station, long-distance bus station, port, or other key targets as required, the public security organ shall order rectification; if rectification is refused, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel.

**Article 89** Where a person suspected of terrorist activities violates the restraint measures ordered by the public security organ to be observed, the public security organ shall give a warning and order rectification; if rectification is refused, the person shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days.

**Article 90** Where a news media unit or other entity fabricates or disseminates false information about terrorist incidents, reports or disseminates implementation details of terrorist activities that may provoke imitation, publishes cruel or inhumane scenes from terrorist incidents, or, without approval, reports or disseminates information about the identities of on-site response and handling personnel or hostages, or about the response and handling actions, the public security organ shall impose a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan, and detain the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and may also impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

Where an individual commits an act specified in the preceding paragraph, the public security organ shall detain the individual for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and may also impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan.

**Article 91** Anyone who refuses to cooperate with relevant departments in carrying out counter-terrorism security prevention, intelligence information, investigation, or response and handling work shall be fined not more than 2,000 yuan by the competent authority; if serious consequences are caused, the person shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and may also be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Where a unit commits an act specified in the preceding paragraph, the competent authority shall impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 92** Anyone who obstructs relevant departments from carrying out counter-terrorism work shall be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than fifteen days, and may also be fined not more than 50,000 yuan.

Where a unit commits an act specified in the preceding paragraph, the public security organ shall impose a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Anyone who obstructs a people's policeman, a member of the People's Liberation Army, or a member of the People's Armed Police from performing their duties according to law shall be given a heavier punishment.

**Article 93** Where a unit violates the provisions of this Law and the circumstances are serious, the competent authority shall order it to cease engaging in the relevant business or providing the relevant service, or order it to suspend production or business; if serious consequences are caused, the relevant license or certificate shall be revoked, or the registration shall be cancelled.

**Article 94** Where a staff member of the counter-terrorism leading body or a relevant department abuses power, neglects duty, engages in malpractice for personal gain, or leaks state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy in violation of regulations during counter-terrorism work, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Any unit or individual has the right to report or accuse the counter-terrorism leading body, relevant departments, or their staff members of abusing power, neglecting duty, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or committing other illegal or disciplinary acts during counter-terrorism work. Upon receiving such a report or accusation, the relevant department shall handle it promptly and reply to the person who made the report or accusation.

**Article 95** Where items, funds, etc., that have been sealed up, seized, frozen, detained, or confiscated in accordance with the provisions of this Law are found upon review to be unrelated to terrorism, the relevant measures shall be promptly lifted and the items or funds returned.

**Article 96** Where a relevant unit or individual disagrees with a decision on administrative penalty or administrative compulsory measure made in accordance with this Law, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit according to law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 97** This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2016. The "Decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on Strengthening Counter-Terrorism Work" adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on October 29, 2011, shall be simultaneously repealed.
