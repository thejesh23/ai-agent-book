# National Emblem Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on March 2, 1991

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the National Emblem Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to uphold the dignity of the National Emblem, ensure its correct use, enhance citizens' sense of national identity, promote patriotism, and cultivate and practice core socialist values.

Article 2 The National Emblem of the People's Republic of China features Tiananmen under the light of five stars, surrounded by ears of grain and a gear.

The National Emblem of the People's Republic of China is made in accordance with the Design of the National Emblem of the People's Republic of China adopted by the Central People's Government Council in 1950 and the Instructions for the Production of the National Emblem of the People's Republic of China published by the General Office of the Central People's Government Council.

Article 3 The National Emblem of the People's Republic of China is the symbol and hallmark of the People's Republic of China.

All organizations and citizens shall respect and protect the National Emblem.

Article 4 The following organs shall display the National Emblem:

(1) The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels;

(2) The people's governments at all levels;

(3) The Central Military Commission;

(4) The supervisory commissions at all levels;

(5) The people's courts and special people's courts at all levels;

(6) The people's procuratorates and special people's procuratorates at all levels;

(7) The Ministry of Foreign Affairs;

(8) The embassies, consulates and other diplomatic missions of the state in foreign countries;

(9) The relevant organs of the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the relevant organs of the Central People's Government in the Macao Special Administrative Region.

The National Emblem shall be displayed at the center above the main entrance of the organ.

Article 5 The National Emblem shall be displayed in the following places:

(1) The Tiananmen Rostrum and the Great Hall of the People in Beijing;

(2) The meeting halls of the people's congresses and their standing committees at or above the county level, and the assembly halls of the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns;

(3) The courtrooms of the people's courts and special people's courts at all levels;

(4) Venues for constitutional oath-taking;

(5) Appropriate places at entry and exit ports.

Article 6 The seals of the following organs shall bear the pattern of the National Emblem:

(1) The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

(2) The special committees of the National People's Congress, the General Office and working committees of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the ministries, commissions, directly affiliated organs, and the General Office of the State Council, as well as the administrative organs designated by the State Council to use seals bearing the pattern of the National Emblem, the General Office of the Central Military Commission, and other organs designated by the Central Military Commission to use seals bearing the pattern of the National Emblem;

(3) The standing committees of the local people's congresses, people's governments, supervisory commissions, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at or above the county level, as well as special people's courts and special people's procuratorates;

(4) The embassies, consulates and other diplomatic missions of the state in foreign countries.

Article 7 The organs specified in Article 6 of this Law shall prominently display the pattern of the National Emblem on the homepage of their websites.

The standard version of the National Emblem pattern used on websites shall be published on the websites of the National People's Congress and the State Council.

Article 8 The following documents, publications, etc. shall bear the pattern of the National Emblem:

(1) Certificates of honor, letters of appointment, and diplomatic documents issued by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President of the People's Republic of China, and the State Council;

(2) Envelopes, letterheads, and invitations used externally in an official capacity by the President and Vice President of the People's Republic of China, the Chairman and Vice Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the Premier and Vice Premiers of the State Council, State Councilors, the Chairman and Vice Chairmen of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

(3) The covers of the gazettes of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

(4) The covers of the official versions of laws and regulations published by the state.

Article 9 Boundary markers, boundary tablets indicating national boundaries, marker pillars indicating the base points of territorial waters, and other markers used to display national sovereignty may bear the pattern of the National Emblem.

Legal tender issued by the People's Bank of China may bear the pattern of the National Emblem.

Article 10 The following documents and certificates may bear the pattern of the National Emblem:

(1) Work credentials and law enforcement credentials of state organ personnel;

(2) Business licenses, permits, approval certificates, qualification certificates, and title certificates issued by state organs;

(3) Resident identity cards, passports of the People's Republic of China, and other legal entry and exit documents.

The badges of state organs and the armed forces may use the pattern of the National Emblem as their core design.

Citizens may wear National Emblem badges on solemn occasions to express patriotic sentiment.

Article 11 The procedures for using the pattern of the National Emblem in foreign affairs and by embassies, consulates, and other diplomatic missions of the state in foreign countries shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and implemented after approval by the State Council.

Article 12 The display or use of the National Emblem or its pattern beyond the scope specified in this Law shall be prescribed by the General Office of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or the General Office of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant competent authorities.

Article 13 The National Emblem and its pattern shall not be used on:

(1) Trademarks, designs for which patent rights are granted, or commercial advertisements;

(2) Daily necessities or decorations for daily life;

(3) Private celebrations or mourning activities;

(4) Other occasions where the use of the National Emblem and its pattern is prohibited by the General Office of the State Council.

Article 14 No damaged, defaced, or non-conforming National Emblem shall be displayed.

Article 15 The National Emblem shall be an important part of patriotic education.

Primary and secondary schools shall educate students about the history and spiritual connotation of the National Emblem.

News media shall actively publicize knowledge about the National Emblem and guide citizens and organizations in the correct use of the National Emblem and its pattern.

Article 16 The displayed National Emblem shall be uniformly manufactured by enterprises designated by the state, and the standard diameters shall be the following three types:

(1) 100 centimeters;

(2) 80 centimeters;

(3) 60 centimeters.

If a non-standard size National Emblem needs to be displayed, it shall be appropriately enlarged or reduced in proportion to the standard sizes and shall be compatible with the purpose of use, the building where it is located, and the surrounding environment.

Article 17 The General Office of the State Council shall coordinate and oversee the management of the National Emblem nationwide. Local people's governments at all levels shall coordinate and oversee the management of the National Emblem within their respective administrative regions.

The market supervision and administration departments of the people's governments at all levels shall supervise and manage the manufacture and sale of the National Emblem.

The department designated by the people's government at the county level shall supervise and manage the display, use, and retrieval of the National Emblem within its administrative region.

Article 18 Anyone who intentionally insults the National Emblem of the People's Republic of China in a public place by burning, damaging, defacing, soiling, trampling, or other means shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are minor, the public security organ shall impose a detention of up to 15 days.

Article 19 This Law shall come into force as of October 1, 1991.

Appendix:

Design of the National Emblem of the People's Republic of China

(Adopted at the Eighth Meeting of the Central People's Government Council on June 28, 1950)

Explanation: The content of the National Emblem consists of the national flag, Tiananmen, a gear, and ears of wheat and rice, symbolizing the new democratic revolutionary struggle of the Chinese people since the May Fourth Movement and the birth of the new China under the people's democratic dictatorship led by the working class and based on the alliance of workers and peasants.

Instructions for the Production of the National Emblem of the People's Republic of China

(Published by the General Office of the Central People's Government Council on September 20, 1950)

1. Two bundles of wheat and rice form a circular ring. The gear is placed at the intersection of the stalks of wheat and rice below. A red ribbon is tied at the center of the gear. The ribbon passes around the wheat and rice on both sides and hangs down, dividing the gear into upper and lower parts.

2. A vertical line drawn through the center of the design divides it into two completely symmetrical halves.

3. The positions and dimensions of the various parts of the design may be enlarged or reduced according to the proportions of the grid-line drawing.

4. If a relief is made, the heights of the various parts may be enlarged or reduced according to the proportions of the cross-section drawing.

5. The colors of the National Emblem are gold and red: the wheat and rice, the five stars, Tiananmen, and the gear are gold; the background within the circular ring and the hanging ribbon are red; the red is the same as the red of the national flag, and the gold is a bright, lustrous gold.
