# Legislation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Ninth National People's Congress on March 15, 2000

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Legislation Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Third Session of the Twelfth National People's Congress on March 15, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate legislative activities, improve the national legislative system, enhance legislative quality, perfect the socialist legal system with Chinese characteristics, give full play to the leading and driving role of legislation, safeguard and develop socialist democracy, comprehensively advance the rule of law, and build a socialist country under the rule of law.

**Article 2** This Law applies to the formulation, amendment, and repeal of laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, and separate regulations.

The formulation, amendment, and repeal of rules of departments under the State Council and rules of local governments shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 3** Legislation shall adhere to the fundamental principles of the Constitution, center on economic development, uphold the socialist path, the people's democratic dictatorship, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, and the policy of reform and opening up.

**Article 4** Legislation shall be conducted in accordance with statutory authority and procedures, proceed from the overall interests of the state, and safeguard the unity and dignity of the socialist legal system.

**Article 5** Legislation shall embody the will of the people, promote socialist democracy, adhere to openness in legislation, and ensure the participation of the people in legislative activities through various channels.

**Article 6** Legislation shall proceed from actual conditions, adapt to the requirements of economic and social development and comprehensively deepening reform, and scientifically and reasonably define the rights and obligations of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations, as well as the powers and responsibilities of state organs.

Legal norms shall be clear, specific, targeted, and enforceable.

## Chapter II Laws

### Section 1 Legislative Authority

**Article 7** The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee exercise the legislative power of the state.

The National People's Congress formulates and amends basic laws concerning criminal matters, civil matters, state organs, and other matters.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress formulates and amends laws other than those that should be formulated by the National People's Congress; during the recess of the National People's Congress, it may supplement and amend laws enacted by the National People's Congress, provided that such supplements and amendments do not contravene the basic principles of those laws.

**Article 8** The following matters shall only be governed by laws:

(1) Matters concerning state sovereignty;

(2) The creation, organization, and powers of the people's congresses, people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at various levels;

(3) The system of regional ethnic autonomy, the system of special administrative regions, and the system of grassroots self-governance;

(4) Crimes and penalties;

(5) Deprivation of citizens' political rights, and compulsory measures and penalties restricting personal freedom;

(6) Basic tax systems, including the establishment of tax categories, determination of tax rates, and tax collection and administration;

(7) Expropriation or requisition of non-state-owned property;

(8) Basic civil systems;

(9) Basic economic systems, as well as basic systems for finance, customs, banking, and foreign trade;

(10) Systems for litigation and arbitration;

(11) Other matters that must be governed by laws formulated by the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee.

**Article 9** For matters specified in Article 8 of this Law for which no law has yet been enacted, the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee has the authority to make a decision to authorize the State Council to formulate administrative regulations on some of those matters as needed, except for matters concerning crimes and penalties, deprivation of citizens' political rights, compulsory measures and penalties restricting personal freedom, and the judicial system.

**Article 10** An authorization decision shall specify the purpose, matters, scope, duration, and the principles to be followed by the authorized organ in implementing the authorization decision.

The duration of an authorization shall not exceed five years, unless otherwise provided in the authorization decision.

The authorized organ shall, six months before the expiration of the authorization period, report to the authorizing organ on the implementation of the authorization decision and provide opinions on whether relevant laws need to be formulated; if further authorization is needed, it may submit relevant opinions, and the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee shall make a decision.

**Article 11** When the conditions for formulating a law on an authorized legislative matter become mature through practical testing, the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee shall promptly formulate a law. Once the law is enacted, the authorization for the corresponding legislative matter shall terminate.

**Article 12** The authorized organ shall exercise the delegated power strictly in accordance with the authorization decision.

The authorized organ shall not sub-delegate the delegated power to any other organ.

**Article 13** The National People's Congress or its Standing Committee may, based on the needs of reform and development, decide to authorize the temporary adjustment or temporary non-application of certain provisions of laws in specific areas for a limited period regarding specific matters in administrative management and other fields.

### Section 2 Legislative Procedure of the National People's Congress

**Article 14** The Presidium of the National People's Congress may submit bills to the National People's Congress for deliberation at its sessions.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and the special committees of the National People's Congress may submit bills to the National People's Congress, and the Presidium shall decide whether to place them on the agenda of the session.

**Article 15** A delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies may jointly submit a bill to the National People's Congress. The Presidium shall decide whether to place it on the agenda, or first refer it to the relevant special committee for deliberation and an opinion on whether to place it on the agenda, and then decide whether to place it on the agenda.

When a special committee deliberates, it may invite the proposer to attend the meeting and express opinions.

**Article 16** A bill submitted to the National People's Congress may, during the recess of the National People's Congress, be first submitted to the Standing Committee. After deliberation by the Standing Committee in accordance with the relevant procedures stipulated in Section 3 of Chapter II of this Law, the Standing Committee shall decide to submit it to the National People's Congress for deliberation. The Standing Committee or the proposer shall explain the bill to the plenary session of the National People's Congress.

When deliberating a bill in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the Standing Committee shall solicit opinions from deputies to the National People's Congress through various forms and provide feedback on the relevant situation; when special committees and the working bodies of the Standing Committee conduct legislative research, they may invite relevant deputies to the National People's Congress to participate.

**Article 17** For a bill that the Standing Committee decides to submit to the National People's Congress for deliberation, the draft law shall be distributed to the deputies one month before the session convenes.

**Article 18** After a bill placed on the agenda of a session of the National People's Congress is explained by the proposer at the plenary session, it shall be deliberated by the delegations.

When a delegation deliberates a bill, the proposer shall send personnel to listen to opinions and answer inquiries.

When a delegation deliberates a bill, relevant organs and organizations shall, at the request of the delegation, send personnel to explain the relevant situation.

**Article 19** A bill placed on the agenda of a session of the National People's Congress shall be deliberated by the relevant special committee, which shall submit its deliberation opinions to the Presidium and have them printed and distributed at the session.

**Article 20** A bill placed on the agenda of a session of the National People's Congress shall be uniformly deliberated by the Law Committee based on the deliberation opinions of the delegations and the relevant special committee. The Law Committee shall submit a deliberation result report and a revised draft of the law to the Presidium, and important differing opinions shall be explained in the deliberation result report. After approval by the Presidium, the report and revised draft shall be printed and distributed at the session.

**Article 21** When necessary, the executive chairpersons of the Presidium may convene meetings of the heads of delegations to hear the deliberation opinions of the delegations on major issues in a bill placed on the agenda of a session of the National People's Congress, conduct discussions, and report the discussions and opinions to the Presidium.

The executive chairpersons of the Presidium may also convene relevant deputies selected by the delegations to discuss major specialized issues in a bill and report the discussions and opinions to the Presidium.

**Article 22** If the proposer requests the withdrawal of a bill placed on the agenda of a session of the National People's Congress before it is put to a vote, the proposer shall state the reasons. With the consent of the Presidium and a report to the session, deliberation on the bill shall terminate.

**Article 23** If a bill under deliberation involves major issues requiring further study, the Presidium may propose, and the plenary session of the National People's Congress may decide, to authorize the Standing Committee to further deliberate based on the opinions of the deputies, make a decision, and report the decision to the next session of the National People's Congress; or it may authorize the Standing Committee to further deliberate based on the opinions of the deputies, propose a revision plan, and submit it to the next session of the National People's Congress for deliberation and decision.

**Article 24** After the revised draft of a law is deliberated by the delegations, the Law Committee shall revise it based on the deliberation opinions of the delegations and submit a final draft for a vote. The Presidium shall submit the final draft to the plenary session for a vote, and it shall be adopted by a majority vote of all the deputies.

**Article 25** A law adopted by the National People's Congress shall be promulgated by an order of the President signed by the President of the state.

### Section 3 Legislative Procedure of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress

**Article 26** The Council of Chairpersons may submit bills to the Standing Committee for deliberation at its meetings.

The State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and the special committees of the National People's Congress may submit bills to the Standing Committee. The Council of Chairpersons shall decide whether to place them on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, or first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then decide whether to place them on the agenda. If the Council of Chairpersons considers that a bill involves major issues requiring further study, it may suggest that the proposer revise and improve the bill before submitting it to the Standing Committee.

**Article 27** A group of ten or more members of the Standing Committee may jointly submit a bill to the Standing Committee. The Council of Chairpersons shall decide whether to place it on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, or first refer it to the relevant special committee for deliberation and an opinion on whether to place it on the agenda, and then decide whether to place it on the agenda. If a bill is not placed on the agenda, the Council of Chairpersons shall report this to the Standing Committee meeting or explain it to the proposer.

When a special committee deliberates, it may invite the proposer to attend the meeting and express opinions.

**Article 28** For a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, the draft law shall be distributed to the members of the Standing Committee seven days before the meeting convenes, except under special circumstances.

When the Standing Committee deliberates a bill at a meeting, relevant deputies to the National People's Congress shall be invited to attend the meeting.

**Article 29** A bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting shall generally be put to a vote after deliberation at three Standing Committee meetings.

At the first deliberation of a bill at a Standing Committee meeting, the proposer shall explain the bill at the plenary session, and preliminary deliberation shall be conducted in group meetings.

At the second deliberation of a bill at a Standing Committee meeting, the Law Committee shall report at the plenary session on the revision of the draft law and major issues, and further deliberation shall be conducted in group meetings.

At the third deliberation of a bill at a Standing Committee meeting, the Law Committee shall report at the plenary session on the deliberation result of the draft law, and the revised draft of the law shall be deliberated in group meetings.

When the Standing Committee deliberates a bill, joint group meetings or plenary sessions may be convened as needed to discuss major issues in the draft law.

**Article 30** If opinions on a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting are relatively consistent, it may be put to a vote after deliberation at two Standing Committee meetings; for a bill involving relatively simple adjustments or partial amendments, if opinions are relatively consistent, it may be put to a vote after deliberation at one Standing Committee meeting.

**Article 31** When the Standing Committee deliberates a bill in group meetings, the proposer shall send personnel to listen to opinions and answer inquiries.

When the Standing Committee deliberates a bill in group meetings, relevant organs and organizations shall, at the request of the group, send personnel to explain the relevant situation.

**Article 32** A bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting shall be deliberated by the relevant special committee, which shall submit its deliberation opinions and have them printed and distributed at the Standing Committee meeting.

When the relevant special committee deliberates a bill, it may invite members of other special committees to attend the meeting and express opinions.

**Article 33** A bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting shall be uniformly deliberated by the Law Committee based on the deliberation opinions of the members of the Standing Committee, the relevant special committee, and opinions from various sources. The Law Committee shall submit a report on the revision status or a deliberation result report and a revised draft of the law, and important differing opinions shall be explained in the report or deliberation result report. If opinions of the relevant special committee are not adopted, feedback shall be provided to that special committee.

When the Law Committee deliberates a bill, it shall invite members of the relevant special committee to attend the meeting and express opinions.

**Article 34** When a special committee deliberates a bill, it shall hold a plenary meeting for deliberation. When necessary, it may request relevant organs and organizations to send responsible persons to explain the situation.

**Article 35** If there is disagreement among special committees on important issues in a draft law, the matter shall be reported to the Council of Chairpersons.

**Article 36** For a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, the Law Committee, the relevant special committee, and the working bodies of the Standing Committee shall listen to opinions from various sources. Opinions may be solicited through various forms such as symposiums, evaluation meetings, and hearings.

If a bill involves issues of a highly professional nature requiring feasibility assessment, an evaluation meeting shall be held to hear opinions from relevant experts, departments, and deputies to the National People's Congress. The evaluation results shall be reported to the Standing Committee.

If there are significant differences of opinion on issues in a bill or if the bill involves major adjustments of interests requiring a hearing, a hearing shall be held to hear opinions from representatives of relevant grassroots units and groups, departments, people's organizations, experts, deputies to the National People's Congress, and relevant sectors of society. The hearing results shall be reported to the Standing Committee.

The working bodies of the Standing Committee shall send the draft law to relevant deputies to the National People's Congress, the standing committees of local people's congresses, and relevant departments, organizations, and experts for comments.

**Article 37** For a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, the draft law and explanations of its drafting and revision shall be published for public comment after the Standing Committee meeting, unless the Council of Chairpersons decides not to publish them. The period for public comment shall generally be no less than thirty days. The status of the comment solicitation shall be reported to the public.

**Article 38** For a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting, the working bodies of the Standing Committee shall collect and organize opinions from group deliberations, opinions from various sources, and other relevant materials, distribute them to the Law Committee and the relevant special committee, and, as needed, print and distribute them at the Standing Committee meeting.

**Article 39** For a bill to be submitted to a Standing Committee meeting for adoption, before the Law Committee submits its deliberation result report, the working bodies of the Standing Committee may assess the feasibility of the main institutional provisions in the draft law, the timing of the law's enactment, the social effects of its implementation, and potential issues. The assessment results shall be explained by the Law Committee in its deliberation result report.

**Article 40** If the proposer requests the withdrawal of a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting before it is put to a vote, the proposer shall state the reasons. With the consent of the Council of Chairpersons and a report to the Standing Committee, deliberation on the bill shall terminate.

**Article 41** After the revised draft of a law is deliberated at a Standing Committee meeting, the Law Committee shall revise it based on the deliberation opinions of the members of the Standing Committee and submit a final draft for a vote. The Council of Chairpersons shall submit the final draft to the plenary session of the Standing Committee for a vote, and it shall be adopted by a majority vote of all the members of the Standing Committee.

Before the final draft of a law is submitted to the Standing Committee for a vote, the Council of Chairpersons may, based on the deliberation status at the Standing Committee meeting, decide to submit individual important clauses with significant differences of opinion to the Standing Committee for a separate vote.

After the separately voted clauses are voted on at the Standing Committee meeting, the Council of Chairpersons may, based on the results of the separate vote, decide to submit the final draft of the law for a vote, or decide to temporarily withhold it from a vote and refer it to the Law Committee and the relevant special committee for further deliberation.

**Article 42** If a bill placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting is shelved for two years due to significant differences of opinion on major issues such as the necessity and feasibility of enacting the law, or if it has not been placed on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting for two years after being temporarily withheld from a vote, the Council of Chairpersons shall report this to the Standing Committee, and deliberation on the bill shall terminate.

**Article 43** If individual clauses involving similar matters in multiple laws are amended and submitted as a single bill, the Council of Chairpersons may decide to put the bill to a combined vote or separate votes.

**Article 44** A law adopted by the Standing Committee shall be promulgated by an order of the President signed by the President of the state.

### Section 4 Interpretation of Laws

**Article 45** The power to interpret laws belongs to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

A law shall be interpreted by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The specific meaning of a provision of the law needs to be further clarified;

(2) New circumstances arise after the enactment of the law, requiring clarification of the legal basis for application.

**Article 46** The State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the special committees of the National People's Congress, and the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may request an interpretation of a law from the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

**Article 47** The working bodies of the Standing Committee shall study and draft a draft interpretation of a law, and the Council of Chairpersons shall decide to place it on the agenda of a Standing Committee meeting.

**Article 48** After the draft interpretation of a law is deliberated at a Standing Committee meeting, the Law Committee shall deliberate and revise it based on the deliberation opinions of the members of the Standing Committee and submit a final draft for a vote.

**Article 49** The final draft of an interpretation of a law shall be adopted by a majority vote of all the members of the Standing Committee, and the Standing Committee shall promulgate it by announcement.

**Article 50** An interpretation of a law by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress has the same effect as the law itself.

### Section 5 Other Provisions

**Article 51** The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee shall strengthen the organization and coordination of legislative work and play a leading role in the legislative process.Article 52 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall strengthen the overall planning of legislative work through legislative plans, annual legislative plans, and other means. When formulating legislative plans and annual legislative plans, it shall carefully study deputies' proposals and suggestions, extensively solicit opinions, conduct scientific evaluation and assessment, and determine legislative projects based on the needs of economic and social development and the advancement of democracy and the rule of law, so as to improve the timeliness, pertinence, and systematic nature of legislation. The legislative plan and annual legislative plan shall be adopted by the Council of Chairpersons and published to the public.

The working bodies of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall be responsible for formulating the legislative plan and drafting the annual legislative plan, and shall, in accordance with the requirements of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, supervise the implementation of the legislative plan and the annual legislative plan.

Article 53 The relevant special committees of the National People's Congress and the working bodies of the Standing Committee shall participate in the drafting of draft laws by relevant parties in advance; comprehensive, overall, and fundamental important draft laws may be drafted by the relevant special committees or the working bodies of the Standing Committee.

For draft laws with a strong professional nature, experts in relevant fields may be invited to participate in the drafting work, or the drafting may be entrusted to relevant experts, teaching and research institutions, or social organizations.

Article 54 When submitting a legislative proposal, the text of the draft law and its explanation shall be submitted simultaneously, along with necessary reference materials. If a law is to be amended, a comparative text before and after the amendment shall also be submitted. The explanation of the draft law shall include the necessity, feasibility, and main content of enacting or amending the law, as well as the handling of major divergent opinions during the drafting process.

Article 55 A legislative proposal submitted to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee may be withdrawn by the proposer before it is placed on the agenda of the session.

Article 56 If a legislative proposal submitted to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee for a vote at a plenary session fails to be adopted, and the proposer deems it necessary to enact the law, it may be resubmitted in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law, and the Presidium or the Council of Chairpersons shall decide whether to place it on the agenda of the session; among these, a legislative proposal that fails to be adopted by the National People's Congress shall be submitted to the National People's Congress for deliberation and decision.

Article 57 A law shall clearly stipulate the date of its entry into force.

Article 58 The order of the President signed to promulgate a law shall specify the enacting body, the date of adoption, and the date of entry into force of the law.

After a law is signed and promulgated, it shall be published promptly in the Bulletin of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, on the website of the National People's Congress, and in newspapers distributed nationwide.

The text of the law published in the Bulletin of the Standing Committee shall be the standard text.

Article 59 The procedures for amending and repealing laws shall be governed by the relevant provisions of this Chapter.

If a law is amended, the new text of the law shall be promulgated.

If a law is repealed, unless the repeal is provided for by another law, it shall be promulgated by an order of the President signed by the President of the State.

Article 60 If a draft law is inconsistent with the relevant provisions of other laws, the proposer shall provide an explanation and propose a handling opinion, and if necessary, shall simultaneously submit a proposal to amend or repeal the relevant provisions of other laws.

When the Law Committee and the relevant special committees deliberate on a legislative proposal, if they consider it necessary to amend or repeal the relevant provisions of other laws, they shall propose a handling opinion.

Article 61 A law may, according to the needs of its content, be divided into parts, chapters, sections, articles, paragraphs, subparagraphs, and items.

The serial numbers of parts, chapters, sections, and articles shall be expressed in sequence using Chinese numerals; paragraphs shall not be numbered; the serial numbers of subparagraphs shall be expressed in sequence using Chinese numerals in parentheses; and the serial numbers of items shall be expressed in sequence using Arabic numerals.

The title note of a law shall specify the enacting body and the date of adoption. For an amended law, the amending body and the date of amendment shall be specified in sequence.

Article 62 If a law explicitly requires the relevant state organs to make supporting specific provisions on a special matter, the relevant state organs shall make such provisions within one year from the date of entry into force of the law; if the law has other provisions on the time limit for formulating supporting specific provisions, those provisions shall prevail. If the relevant state organs fail to make supporting specific provisions within the time limit, they shall explain the situation to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 63 The relevant special committees of the National People's Congress and the working bodies of the Standing Committee may organize post-legislative evaluations of relevant laws or relevant provisions of laws. The evaluation results shall be reported to the Standing Committee.

Article 64 The working bodies of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may study and reply to legal inquiries on specific issues, and report them to the Standing Committee for recordation.

## Chapter 3 Administrative Regulations

Article 65 The State Council shall formulate administrative regulations in accordance with the Constitution and laws.

Administrative regulations may provide for the following matters:

(1) Matters requiring the formulation of administrative regulations for the implementation of the provisions of laws;

(2) Matters concerning the administrative functions and powers of the State Council as provided for in Article 89 of the Constitution.

For matters that should be regulated by laws enacted by the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee, if the State Council formulates administrative regulations first based on the authorization decision of the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee, and when the conditions for enacting laws are ripe after practical testing, the State Council shall promptly request the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee to enact laws.

Article 66 The legislative affairs office of the State Council shall, in accordance with the overall work deployment of the State, draft the State Council's annual legislative plan and submit it to the State Council for approval. The legislative projects in the State Council's annual legislative plan shall be aligned with the legislative plan and annual legislative plan of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The legislative affairs office of the State Council shall promptly track and understand the implementation of the legislative plan by various departments of the State Council, and strengthen organization, coordination, supervision, and guidance.

If a relevant department of the State Council deems it necessary to formulate an administrative regulation, it shall submit a request to the State Council for project establishment.

Article 67 Administrative regulations shall be specifically drafted by the relevant departments of the State Council or the legislative affairs office of the State Council; draft laws and administrative regulations on important administrative matters shall be drafted by the legislative affairs office of the State Council. During the drafting process of administrative regulations, opinions from relevant organs, organizations, deputies to the People's Congress, and the public shall be extensively solicited. Opinions may be solicited through various forms such as symposiums, demonstration meetings, and hearings.

Draft administrative regulations shall be published to the public for comments, unless the State Council decides not to publish them.

Article 68 After the drafting of an administrative regulation is completed, the drafting unit shall submit the draft, its explanation, different opinions from various parties on the main issues of the draft, and other relevant materials to the legislative affairs office of the State Council for review.

The legislative affairs office of the State Council shall submit a review report and a revised draft to the State Council, and the review report shall explain the main issues of the draft.

Article 69 The decision-making procedure for administrative regulations shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Organic Law of the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

Article 70 Administrative regulations shall be promulgated by an order of the State Council signed by the Premier.

Administrative regulations concerning national defense construction may be promulgated by an order of the State Council and the Central Military Commission jointly signed by the Premier of the State Council and the Chairman of the Central Military Commission.

Article 71 After an administrative regulation is signed and promulgated, it shall be published promptly in the Bulletin of the State Council, on the website of the Chinese Government Legal Information Network, and in newspapers distributed nationwide.

The text of the administrative regulation published in the Bulletin of the State Council shall be the standard text.

## Chapter 4 Local Regulations, Autonomous Regulations, and Separate Regulations, and Rules

### Section 1 Local Regulations, Autonomous Regulations, and Separate Regulations

Article 72 The People's Congresses and their Standing Committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in light of the specific conditions and actual needs of their respective administrative regions and on the condition that they do not contravene the Constitution, laws, and administrative regulations, formulate local regulations.

The People's Congresses and their Standing Committees of cities divided into districts may, in light of the specific conditions and actual needs of their respective cities and on the condition that they do not contravene the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of their respective provinces or autonomous regions, formulate local regulations on matters such as urban and rural construction and management, environmental protection, and historical and cultural preservation; if the law has other provisions on the matters for which cities divided into districts may formulate local regulations, those provisions shall prevail. Local regulations of cities divided into districts shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region for approval before taking effect. The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region shall review the legality of the local regulations submitted for approval, and if they do not contravene the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of the province or autonomous region, they shall be approved within four months.

When reviewing the local regulations of a city divided into district submitted for approval, if the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region finds that they contravene the rules of the people's government of the province or autonomous region, it shall make a decision on handling the matter.

Except for the cities where the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions are located, the cities where special economic zones are located, and the larger cities already approved by the State Council, the specific steps and timing for other cities divided into districts to begin formulating local regulations shall be determined by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region, taking into comprehensive consideration factors such as the population size, geographical area, economic and social development, legislative needs, and legislative capacity of the cities divided into districts under its jurisdiction, and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

The People's Congresses and their Standing Committees of autonomous prefectures may exercise the power of cities divided into districts to formulate local regulations in accordance with the provisions of the second paragraph of this article. The specific steps and timing for autonomous prefectures to begin formulating local regulations shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Local regulations already formulated by the cities where the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions are located, the cities where special economic zones are located, and the larger cities already approved by the State Council, which involve matters beyond the scope specified in the second paragraph of this article, shall remain valid.

Article 73 Local regulations may provide for the following matters:

(1) Matters requiring specific provisions in light of the actual conditions of the administrative region for the implementation of the provisions of laws and administrative regulations;

(2) Matters of a local nature that require the formulation of local regulations.

For matters other than those specified in Article 8 of this Law, if the State has not yet enacted laws or administrative regulations, provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may, in light of their specific conditions and actual needs, first formulate local regulations. After the laws or administrative regulations enacted by the State take effect, any provisions of local regulations that contravene the laws or administrative regulations shall be invalid, and the enacting body shall promptly amend or repeal them.

When cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures formulate local regulations in accordance with the first and second paragraphs of this article, they shall be limited to the matters specified in the second paragraph of Article 72 of this Law.

When formulating local regulations, repetitive provisions on content already clearly stipulated by higher-level laws shall generally not be made.

Article 74 The People's Congresses and their Standing Committees of the provinces and cities where special economic zones are located shall, in accordance with the authorization decision of the National People's Congress, formulate regulations for implementation within the special economic zones.

Article 75 The People's Congresses of national autonomous areas have the power to formulate autonomous regulations and separate regulations in accordance with the political, economic, and cultural characteristics of the local ethnic groups. The autonomous regulations and separate regulations of autonomous regions shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for approval before taking effect. The autonomous regulations and separate regulations of autonomous prefectures and autonomous counties shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval before taking effect.

Autonomous regulations and separate regulations may, in accordance with the characteristics of the local ethnic groups, make adaptive provisions to the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, but shall not contravene the basic principles of laws or administrative regulations, and shall not make adaptive provisions to the provisions of the Constitution and the Law on Regional Ethnic Autonomy, nor to other relevant laws and administrative regulations specifically made for national autonomous areas.

Article 76 Local regulations stipulating particularly important matters of the administrative region shall be adopted by the People's Congress.

Article 77 The procedures for the introduction, deliberation, and voting of bills for local regulations, autonomous regulations, and separate regulations shall be stipulated by the People's Congress at the corresponding level in accordance with the Organic Law of the Local People's Congresses and Local People's Governments of the People's Republic of China, with reference to the provisions of Section 2, Section 3, and Section 5 of Chapter 2 of this Law.

The body responsible for unified deliberation shall submit a report on the results of deliberation and a revised draft of the local regulation.

Article 78 Local regulations formulated by the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be promulgated by an announcement of the Presidium of the Congress.

Local regulations formulated by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be promulgated by an announcement of the Standing Committee.

Local regulations formulated by the People's Congress or its Standing Committee of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture shall, after being approved, be promulgated by an announcement of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the city divided into districts or the autonomous prefecture.

Autonomous regulations and separate regulations shall, after being approved, be promulgated by announcements of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the autonomous region, autonomous prefecture, or autonomous county, respectively.

Article 79 After local regulations, autonomous regulations of autonomous regions, and separate regulations are promulgated, they shall be published promptly in the Bulletin of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, on the website of the National People's Congress, on the website of the local People's Congress, and in newspapers distributed within the administrative region.

The texts of local regulations, autonomous regulations, and separate regulations published in the Bulletin of the Standing Committee shall be the standard texts.

### Section 2 Rules

Article 80 The ministries and commissions of the State Council, the People's Bank of China, the National Audit Office, and the directly affiliated agencies with administrative functions may, in accordance with laws and the administrative regulations, decisions, and orders of the State Council, formulate rules within the scope of their respective powers.

Matters stipulated by departmental rules shall be matters that fall within the implementation of laws or the administrative regulations, decisions, and orders of the State Council. Without the basis of laws or the administrative regulations, decisions, and orders of the State Council, departmental rules shall not establish norms that diminish the rights or increase the obligations of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations, nor shall they increase the powers of the department or reduce its statutory duties.

Article 81 Matters involving the scope of powers of two or more departments of the State Council shall be submitted to the State Council for the formulation of administrative regulations or jointly formulated as rules by the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 82 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of their respective provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulate rules.

Local government rules may provide for the following matters:

(1) Matters requiring the formulation of rules for the implementation of the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and local regulations;

(2) Specific administrative matters of the administrative region.

When the people's governments of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures formulate local government rules in accordance with the first and second paragraphs of this article, they shall be limited to matters such as urban and rural construction and management, environmental protection, and historical and cultural preservation. Local government rules already formulated that involve matters beyond the above scope shall remain valid.

Except for the cities where the people's governments of provinces or autonomous regions are located, the cities where special economic zones are located, and the larger cities already approved by the State Council, the time for the people's governments of other cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures to begin formulating rules shall be synchronized with the time determined by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of their respective province or autonomous region for the city or autonomous prefecture to begin formulating local regulations.

If a local regulation should be formulated but the conditions are not yet ripe, and there is an urgent need for administrative management, a local government rule may be formulated first. If the administrative measures stipulated by the rule need to continue to be implemented after two years of implementation, a request shall be made to the People's Congress at the corresponding level or its Standing Committee to formulate a local regulation.

Without the basis of laws, administrative regulations, or local regulations, local government rules shall not establish norms that diminish the rights or increase the obligations of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 83 The procedures for formulating rules of the departments of the State Council and local government rules shall be stipulated by the State Council with reference to the provisions of Chapter 3 of this Law.

Article 84 Departmental rules shall be decided by a ministerial meeting or a commission meeting.

Local government rules shall be decided by a standing meeting or a plenary meeting of the government.

Article 85 Departmental rules shall be promulgated by an order signed by the head of the department.

Local government rules shall be promulgated by an order signed by the governor of the province, the chairman of the autonomous region, the mayor of the municipality, or the governor of the autonomous prefecture.

Article 86 After departmental rules are signed and promulgated, they shall be published promptly in the Bulletin of the State Council or the departmental bulletin, on the website of the Chinese Government Legal Information Network, and in newspapers distributed nationwide.

After local government rules are signed and promulgated, they shall be published promptly in the Bulletin of the people's government at the corresponding level, on the website of the Chinese Government Legal Information Network, and in newspapers distributed within the administrative region.

The texts of rules published in the Bulletin of the State Council or departmental bulletins and the Bulletins of local people's governments shall be the standard texts.

## Chapter 5 Application and Filing for Review

Article 87 The Constitution has the highest legal effect; all laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules shall not contravene the Constitution.

Article 88 The effect of laws is higher than that of administrative regulations, local regulations, and rules.

The effect of administrative regulations is higher than that of local regulations and rules.

Article 89 The effect of local regulations is higher than that of local government rules at the corresponding level and lower levels.

The effect of rules formulated by the people's government of a province or autonomous region is higher than that of rules formulated by the people's governments of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures within its administrative region.

Article 90 If autonomous regulations and separate regulations make adaptive provisions to laws, administrative regulations, and local regulations in accordance with the law, the provisions of the autonomous regulations and separate regulations shall apply within the autonomous area.

If special economic zone regulations make adaptive provisions to laws, administrative regulations, and local regulations based on authorization, the provisions of the special economic zone regulations shall apply within the special economic zone.

Article 91 Departmental rules have equal effect among themselves and between departmental rules and local government rules, and shall be implemented within their respective scopes of authority.

Article 92 If special provisions are inconsistent with general provisions in laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules formulated by the same organ, the special provisions shall prevail; if new provisions are inconsistent with old provisions, the new provisions shall prevail.

Article 93 Laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules shall not have retroactive effect, except for special provisions made to better protect the rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations.

Article 94 If new general provisions and old special provisions of laws on the same matter are inconsistent, and it is impossible to determine which to apply, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall make a ruling.

If new general provisions and old special provisions of administrative regulations on the same matter are inconsistent, and it is impossible to determine which to apply, the State Council shall make a ruling.

Article 95 If local regulations and rules are inconsistent with each other, the relevant organ shall make a ruling in accordance with the following limits of authority:

(1) If new general provisions and old special provisions formulated by the same organ are inconsistent, the enacting organ shall make a ruling;(2) Where provisions of local regulations and departmental rules on the same matter are inconsistent, and it is unclear which shall apply, the State Council shall provide an opinion. If the State Council deems that the local regulations should apply, it shall decide that the provisions of the local regulations apply in that locality; if it deems that the departmental rules should apply, it shall submit the matter to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for a ruling.

(3) Where provisions of departmental rules, or between departmental rules and local government rules, on the same matter are inconsistent, the State Council shall make a ruling.

Where regulations formulated under delegated authority are inconsistent with the provisions of laws, and it is unclear which shall apply, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall make a ruling.

Article 96: Where any of the following circumstances exists in laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, or rules, the relevant authority shall, in accordance with the authority prescribed in Article 97 of this Law, modify or annul them:

(1) Exceeding the scope of authority;
(2) Lower-level legislation contravenes higher-level legislation;
(3) Provisions of rules on the same matter are inconsistent, and, after a ruling, one party's provisions should be modified or annulled;
(4) Provisions of rules are deemed inappropriate and should be modified or annulled;
(5) Violating statutory procedures.

Article 97: The authority to modify or annul laws, administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules is as follows:

(1) The National People's Congress has the authority to modify or annul any inappropriate law enacted by its Standing Committee, and to annul any autonomous regulation or separate regulation approved by its Standing Committee that contravenes the Constitution or the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 75 of this Law;
(2) The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress has the authority to annul any administrative regulation that contravenes the Constitution or laws, to annul any local regulation that contravenes the Constitution, laws, or administrative regulations, and to annul any autonomous regulation or separate regulation approved by the Standing Committee of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government that contravenes the Constitution or the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 75 of this Law;
(3) The State Council has the authority to modify or annul any inappropriate departmental rule or local government rule;
(4) The People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government has the authority to modify or annul any inappropriate local regulation enacted or approved by its Standing Committee;
(5) The Standing Committee of a local People's Congress has the authority to annul any inappropriate rule formulated by the people's government at the corresponding level;
(6) The People's Government of a province or autonomous region has the authority to modify or annul any inappropriate rule formulated by the people's government at the next lower level;
(7) The delegating authority has the authority to annul any regulation formulated by the delegated authority that exceeds the scope of delegation or contravenes the purpose of the delegation, and may, when necessary, revoke the delegation.

Article 98: Administrative regulations, local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules shall, within thirty days of their promulgation, be reported to the relevant authorities for recordation in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) Administrative regulations shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation;
(2) Local regulations enacted by the People's Congress or its Standing Committee of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation; local regulations enacted by the People's Congress or its Standing Committee of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture shall be reported by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation;
(3) Autonomous regulations and separate regulations enacted by the People's Congress of an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county shall be reported by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation; when submitting autonomous regulations or separate regulations for recordation, an explanation of any adaptations made to laws, administrative regulations, or local regulations shall be provided;
(4) Departmental rules and local government rules shall be reported to the State Council for recordation; local government rules shall also be reported to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for recordation; rules formulated by the people's government of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture shall also be reported to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress and the people's government of the province or autonomous region for recordation;
(5) Regulations formulated under delegated authority shall be reported to the authority specified in the delegation decision for recordation; when submitting regulations of a special economic zone for recordation, an explanation of any adaptations made to laws, administrative regulations, or local regulations shall be provided.

Article 99: Where the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government consider that an administrative regulation, local regulation, autonomous regulation, or separate regulation contravenes the Constitution or laws, they may submit a written request for review to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The working body of the Standing Committee shall distribute the request to the relevant special committees for review and the provision of opinions.

Where state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, or citizens other than those specified in the preceding paragraph consider that an administrative regulation, local regulation, autonomous regulation, or separate regulation contravenes the Constitution or laws, they may submit a written proposal for review to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The working body of the Standing Committee shall study the proposal and, when necessary, submit it to the relevant special committees for review and the provision of opinions.

The relevant special committees and the working body of the Standing Committee may conduct proactive reviews of normative documents submitted for recordation.

Article 100: Where a special committee of the National People's Congress or the working body of the Standing Committee, during review or study, considers that an administrative regulation, local regulation, autonomous regulation, or separate regulation contravenes the Constitution or laws, it may submit a written review opinion or study opinion to the enacting authority; alternatively, the Law Committee may, together with the relevant special committee and the working body of the Standing Committee, convene a joint review meeting, requiring the enacting authority to attend and explain the situation, and then submit a written review opinion to the enacting authority. The enacting authority shall, within two months, study and provide its opinion on whether to amend, and shall report back to the Law Committee of the National People's Congress and the relevant special committee or the working body of the Standing Committee.

Where the Law Committee of the National People's Congress, the relevant special committee, or the working body of the Standing Committee, in accordance with the preceding paragraph, submits a review opinion or study opinion to the enacting authority, and the enacting authority amends or repeals the administrative regulation, local regulation, autonomous regulation, or separate regulation in accordance with the opinion provided, the review shall be terminated.

Where the Law Committee of the National People's Congress, the relevant special committee, or the working body of the Standing Committee, after review or study, considers that an administrative regulation, local regulation, autonomous regulation, or separate regulation contravenes the Constitution or laws, and the enacting authority refuses to amend it, they shall submit a motion or proposal for annulment to the Council of Chairpersons, which shall decide whether to submit it to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation and decision.

Article 101: The relevant special committees of the National People's Congress and the working body of the Standing Committee shall, in accordance with the prescribed requirements, provide feedback on the review or study results to the state organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and citizens that submitted the review proposals, and may make the information public.

Article 102: The review procedures of other authorities that accept recordation for local regulations, autonomous regulations, separate regulations, and rules shall be prescribed by the accepting authority in accordance with the principle of maintaining the unity of the legal system.

## Chapter 6: Supplementary Provisions

Article 103: The Central Military Commission shall, in accordance with the Constitution and laws, formulate military regulations.

The general departments, branches of services and arms, military regions, and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force under the Central Military Commission may, in accordance with laws and the military regulations, decisions, and orders of the Central Military Commission, formulate military rules within their scope of authority.

Military regulations and military rules shall be implemented within the armed forces.

The methods for formulating, amending, and repealing military regulations and military rules shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission in accordance with the principles set forth in this Law.

Article 104: Interpretations of laws by the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate concerning the specific application of laws in judicial or procuratorial work shall primarily target specific legal provisions and shall conform to the legislative purpose, principles, and original intent. In cases falling under the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 45 of this Law, a request for a legislative interpretation or a motion for formulating or amending relevant laws shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Interpretations of laws by the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate concerning the specific application of laws in judicial or procuratorial work shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation within thirty days of their promulgation.

No judicial or procuratorial organs other than the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate may make interpretations concerning the specific application of laws.

Article 105: This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 2000.
