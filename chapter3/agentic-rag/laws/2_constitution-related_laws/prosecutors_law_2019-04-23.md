# People's Republic of China Public Prosecutors Law

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on February 28, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Public Prosecutors Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 30, 2001

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Public Prosecutors Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

Revision adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to comprehensively advance the development of a high-quality public prosecutor workforce, strengthen the management and supervision of public prosecutors, safeguard the lawful rights and interests of public prosecutors, ensure that the People's Procuratorates exercise their prosecutorial power independently according to law, guarantee that public prosecutors perform their duties according to law, and ensure judicial justice.

Article 2 Public prosecutors are prosecutorial personnel who exercise the state's prosecutorial power according to law, including the Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, local People's Procuratorates at various levels, and special People's Procuratorates such as military procuratorates.

Article 3 Public prosecutors must faithfully implement the Constitution and laws, uphold social fairness and justice, and serve the people wholeheartedly.

Article 4 Public prosecutors shall be diligent and responsible, honest and upright, and abide by professional ethics.

Article 5 When performing their duties, public prosecutors shall base their actions on facts, take law as the criterion, and uphold an objective and impartial stance.

When handling criminal cases, public prosecutors shall strictly adhere to the principle of legality, respect and protect human rights, and while pursuing crimes, also ensure that innocent persons are not subject to criminal prosecution.

Article 6 Public prosecutors performing their duties according to law are protected by law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

## Chapter II Duties, Obligations, and Rights of Public Prosecutors

Article 7 The duties of public prosecutors are:

(1) To conduct investigations into criminal cases directly accepted by the People's Procuratorates as provided by law;

(2) To review arrests and prosecutions in criminal cases, and to represent the state in public prosecutions;

(3) To carry out public interest litigation work;

(4) To conduct supervision over criminal, civil, and administrative litigation activities;

(5) Other duties as provided by law.

Public prosecutors are responsible for the decisions they make on cases within their scope of authority.

Article 8 In addition to performing prosecutorial duties, the Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, and members of the Procuratorial Committee of a People's Procuratorate shall also perform duties commensurate with their positions.

Article 9 Public prosecutors work under the leadership of the Procurator-General, and major case-handling matters are decided by the Procurator-General. The Procurator-General may delegate some of his or her powers to public prosecutors and may authorize public prosecutors to issue legal documents.

Article 10 Public prosecutors shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) To strictly abide by the Constitution and laws;

(2) To handle cases impartially and not to bend the law for personal gain;

(3) To protect the litigation rights of parties and other litigation participants according to law;

(4) To safeguard national interests and public interests, and to protect the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations;

(5) To keep state secrets and prosecutorial work secrets, and to keep confidential any trade secrets and personal privacy learned during the performance of duties;

(6) To accept legal supervision and supervision by the people according to law;

(7) To explain the law through case handling according to law, enhance the rule of law awareness of the whole people, and promote the construction of a law-based society;

(8) Other obligations as provided by law.

Article 11 Public prosecutors enjoy the following rights:

(1) The authority and working conditions necessary for performing their duties as public prosecutors;

(2) Not to be transferred, removed from office, demoted, dismissed, or disciplined without statutory reasons and without going through statutory procedures;

(3) The occupational security and welfare benefits they should enjoy when performing their duties as public prosecutors;

(4) The safety of their person, property, and residence is protected by law;

(5) To file petitions or accusations;

(6) Other rights as provided by law.

## Chapter III Qualifications and Selection of Public Prosecutors

Article 12 To serve as a public prosecutor, one must meet the following conditions:

(1) Possess the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Uphold the Constitution of the People's Republic of China, and support the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist system;

(3) Have good political, professional, and moral character;

(4) Have the physical condition to perform duties normally;

(5) Hold a bachelor's degree in law from a regular institution of higher education and have obtained a bachelor's degree or above; or hold a bachelor's degree or above in a non-law field from a regular institution of higher education and have obtained a master's degree in law or a master's degree in law or above; or hold a bachelor's degree or above in a non-law field from a regular institution of higher education, have obtained other corresponding degrees, and possess legal expertise;

(6) Have been engaged in legal work for at least five years. For those who have obtained a master's degree in law or a doctorate in law, the required years of legal work may be reduced to four years and three years, respectively;

(7) First-time public prosecutors must pass the National Unified Legal Professional Qualification Examination to obtain legal professional qualifications.

In places where it is genuinely difficult to apply the educational requirements specified in item (5) of the preceding paragraph, with the review and confirmation of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the educational requirements for serving as a public prosecutor may be relaxed to a bachelor's degree from an institution of higher education within a certain period.

Article 13 The following persons shall not serve as public prosecutors:

(1) Those who have been subjected to criminal punishment for committing a crime;

(2) Those who have been dismissed from public office;

(3) Those whose lawyer or notary practice certificates have been revoked or who have been expelled from an arbitration commission;

(4) Those who fall under other circumstances as provided by law.

Article 14 First-time public prosecutors shall be selected through examinations and assessments, based on the criteria of both moral integrity and professional competence, and candidates shall be chosen from among those who meet the conditions for public prosecutors.

The Procurator-General of a People's Procuratorate shall have legal expertise and legal professional experience. Deputy Procurators-General and members of the Procuratorial Committee shall be selected from among public prosecutors, judges, or other persons who meet the conditions for public prosecutors.

Article 15 A People's Procuratorate may, based on the needs of prosecutorial work, openly select public prosecutors from among lawyers or legal professionals such as legal educators and researchers.

In addition to meeting the conditions for serving as a public prosecutor, lawyers participating in the open selection must have actually practiced for no less than five years, have rich practical experience, and have a good professional reputation; legal educators and researchers participating in the open selection must have an intermediate or higher professional title, have been engaged in teaching or research for more than five years, and have outstanding research capabilities and corresponding research results.

Article 16 Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish Public Prosecutor Selection Committees, responsible for reviewing the professional competence of candidates for first-time public prosecutors.

The members of a provincial-level Public Prosecutor Selection Committee shall include representatives of public prosecutors from local People's Procuratorates at various levels, other legal professionals, and representatives from relevant sectors, with public prosecutor representatives accounting for no less than one-third.

The daily work of a provincial-level Public Prosecutor Selection Committee shall be undertaken by the internal functional department of the provincial-level People's Procuratorate.

For the selection of public prosecutors for the Supreme People's Procuratorate, a Public Prosecutor Selection Committee of the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be established, responsible for reviewing the professional competence of candidates for public prosecutors.

Article 17 First-time public prosecutors shall generally serve in grassroots People's Procuratorates. Public prosecutors of higher-level People's Procuratorates shall generally be selected level by level; public prosecutors of the Supreme People's Procuratorate and provincial-level People's Procuratorates may be selected from People's Procuratorates two levels below. Public prosecutors participating in the selection of a higher-level People's Procuratorate must have served as public prosecutors in a lower-level People's Procuratorate for a certain number of years and have relevant work experience for the selected position.

## Chapter IV Appointment and Removal of Public Prosecutors

Article 18 The appointment and removal of public prosecutors shall be handled in accordance with the authority and procedures for appointment and removal as provided by the Constitution and laws.

The Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate is elected and removed by the National People's Congress; the Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the Procurator-General.

The Procurator-General of a local People's Procuratorate at any level is elected and removed by the People's Congress at the corresponding level; the Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level upon the recommendation of the Procurator-General.

The appointment and removal of the Procurator-General of a local People's Procuratorate at any level must be reported to the Procurator-General of the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level, who shall request approval from the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

The Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors of a branch of a People's Procuratorate of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government upon the recommendation of the Procurator-General of the People's Procuratorate of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors of a People's Procuratorate established as a dispatched agency by a provincial-level People's Procuratorate or a People's Procuratorate of a city divided into districts according to law are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level upon the recommendation of the Procurator-General of the dispatching People's Procuratorate.

The Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and public prosecutors of the People's Procuratorates at various levels of the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps and special People's Procuratorates are appointed and removed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 19 After being elected or appointed in accordance with statutory procedures, public prosecutors shall publicly take an oath to the Constitution when assuming office.

Article 20 A public prosecutor shall be removed from the position of public prosecutor upon the occurrence of any of the following circumstances:

(1) Loss of nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Transfer out of the People's Procuratorate where he or she serves;

(3) Change of position that does not require retaining the position of public prosecutor, or approval of the public prosecutor's own application for removal from the position;

(4) Being assessed as incompetent for the position of public prosecutor;

(5) Being unable to perform duties for a long period due to health reasons;

(6) Retirement;

(7) Resignation or being subject to dismissal according to law;

(8) Being unsuitable to continue holding the position due to disciplinary or legal violations.

Article 21 If a person who does not meet the conditions provided in this Law or who has been elected as Procurator-General of a People's Procuratorate in violation of statutory procedures, the Procurator-General of the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level has the right to request the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level not to approve the appointment.

Article 22 If it is discovered that a public prosecutor has been appointed in violation of the conditions provided in this Law, the appointing authority shall revoke the appointment; if a higher-level People's Procuratorate discovers that the appointment of a public prosecutor by a lower-level People's Procuratorate violates the conditions provided in this Law, it shall require the lower-level People's Procuratorate to request the appointing authority to revoke the appointment according to law.

Article 23 A public prosecutor shall not concurrently serve as a member of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, hold a position in an administrative organ, supervisory organ, or adjudicatory organ, hold a position in an enterprise or other for-profit organization or public institution, or concurrently serve as a lawyer, arbitrator, or notary.

Article 24 Public prosecutors who are spouses, direct blood relatives, collateral blood relatives within three generations, or close in-laws shall not concurrently hold the following positions:

(1) Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, or members of the Procuratorial Committee of the same People's Procuratorate;

(2) Procurator-General, Deputy Procurators-General, and public prosecutors of the same People's Procuratorate;

(3) Public prosecutors in the same operational department;

(4) Procurator-General or Deputy Procurators-General of two adjacent levels of People's Procuratorates.

Article 25 If any of the following circumstances apply to the spouse, parents, or children of a public prosecutor, the public prosecutor shall implement a conflict of interest avoidance:

(1) Serving as a partner or founder of a law firm within the jurisdiction of the People's Procuratorate where the public prosecutor serves;

(2) Acting as a litigation representative or defender in the capacity of a lawyer within the jurisdiction of the People's Procuratorate where the public prosecutor serves, or providing other paid legal services to parties in litigation cases.

## Chapter V Management of Public Prosecutors

Article 26 Public prosecutors are managed under a post-based quota system. The number of public prosecutor posts is determined based on factors such as the number of cases, economic and social development, population size, and the level of the People's Procuratorate. Within provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, total control and dynamic management are implemented, with priority given to the case-handling needs of grassroots People's Procuratorates and People's Procuratorates with a high volume of cases.

When vacancies in public prosecutor posts arise, they shall be filled promptly in accordance with procedures.

The number of public prosecutor posts in the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be determined by the Supreme People's Procuratorate in consultation with relevant departments.

Article 27 Public prosecutors are managed under a separate rank system.

The ranks of public prosecutors are divided into twelve levels, which are, in descending order: Chief Grand Prosecutor, First-Grade Grand Prosecutor, Second-Grade Grand Prosecutor, First-Grade Senior Prosecutor, Second-Grade Senior Prosecutor, Third-Grade Senior Prosecutor, Fourth-Grade Senior Prosecutor, First-Grade Prosecutor, Second-Grade Prosecutor, Third-Grade Prosecutor, Fourth-Grade Prosecutor, and Fifth-Grade Prosecutor.

Article 28 The Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate is the Chief Grand Prosecutor.

Article 29 The determination of a public prosecutor's rank is based on factors such as the public prosecutor's moral character and professional competence, professional level, actual work performance in prosecutorial work, and years of service.

Promotion of public prosecutor ranks combines regular promotion with selective promotion based on merit. Public prosecutors in frontline case-handling positions who are particularly outstanding or needed for special work may be specially selected for promotion.

Article 30 Specific measures for the establishment, determination, and promotion of public prosecutor ranks shall be separately provided by the state.

Article 31 First-time public prosecutors shall undergo a unified pre-service training system.

Article 32 Public prosecutors shall receive planned political, theoretical, and professional training.

Training for public prosecutors shall integrate theory with practice, be tailored to needs, and emphasize practical results.

Article 33 The training status of a public prosecutor serves as one of the bases for the public prosecutor's appointment and rank promotion.

Article 34 Public prosecutor training institutions shall undertake the task of training public prosecutors in accordance with relevant provisions.

Article 35 A public prosecutor applying for resignation shall submit a written application, and after approval, his or her position shall be removed in accordance with the procedures provided by law.

Article 36 The dismissal of a public prosecutor shall be carried out by removing his or her position in accordance with the procedures provided by law.

The decision to dismiss a public prosecutor shall be made in accordance with the management authority. The dismissal decision shall be notified in writing to the dismissed public prosecutor, stating the reasons and basis for the decision.

Article 37 Within two years after leaving the People's Procuratorate, a former public prosecutor shall not act as a litigation representative or defender in the capacity of a lawyer.

After leaving the People's Procuratorate, a former public prosecutor shall not act as a litigation representative or defender in cases handled by the People's Procuratorate where he or she formerly served, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent or defend the party.

After being expelled, a former public prosecutor shall not act as a litigation representative or defender, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent or defend the party.

Article 38 When necessary for work, and upon selection or approval by the unit, a public prosecutor may assist in practical teaching and research activities at institutions of higher education or research institutes, and shall comply with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VI Assessment, Rewards, and Disciplinary Sanctions for Public Prosecutors

Article 39 People's Procuratorates shall establish Public Prosecutor Assessment Committees, responsible for the assessment of public prosecutors within their respective procuratorates.

Article 40 A Public Prosecutor Assessment Committee shall consist of five to nine members.

The director of the Public Prosecutor Assessment Committee shall be the Procurator-General of the procuratorate.

Article 41 The assessment of public prosecutors shall be comprehensive, objective, and fair, combining regular assessment with annual assessment.

Article 42 The content of the assessment of public prosecutors includes: actual work performance in prosecutorial work, professional ethics, professional competence, work ability, and work style. The focus of the assessment is on actual work performance in prosecutorial work.

Article 43 The results of the annual assessment are divided into four grades: excellent, competent, basically competent, and incompetent.

The assessment results serve as the basis for adjusting the rank and salary of public prosecutors, as well as for rewards, disciplinary sanctions, removal from office, demotion, and dismissal of public prosecutors.

Article 44 The assessment results shall be notified in writing to the public prosecutor concerned. If a public prosecutor has objections to the assessment results, he or she may apply for a review.

Article 45 Public prosecutors who have made significant achievements and contributions in prosecutorial work, or who have other outstanding deeds, shall be given rewards.

Article 46 Public prosecutors who demonstrate any of the following shall be given rewards:

(1) Achieving notable results in impartial administration of justice;

(2) Achieving outstanding results in summarizing practical experience in prosecutorial work, which has a guiding role in prosecutorial work;

(3) Making significant achievements and contributions in handling major cases, dealing with emergencies, and undertaking special important tasks;

(4) Proposing reform suggestions for prosecutorial work that are adopted and produce significant results;

(5) Proposing prosecutorial suggestions that are adopted, or carrying out legal publicity or resolving various disputes, with significant results;

(6) Having other meritorious deeds.

Rewards for public prosecutors shall be handled in accordance with relevant provisions.

Article 47 Public prosecutors who engage in any of the following acts shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Engaging in bribery, bending the law for personal gain, or extorting confessions by torture;

(2) Concealing, falsifying, altering, or intentionally destroying evidence or case materials;

(3) Disclosing state secrets, prosecutorial work secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy;

(4) Intentionally handling cases in violation of laws and regulations;

(5) Causing errors in cases due to gross negligence and resulting in serious consequences;

(6) Delaying case handling, causing delays in work;

(7) Using their position to seek personal gain for themselves or others;

(8) Accepting benefits from parties or their agents, or meeting with parties or their agents in violation of relevant provisions;

(9) Engaging in or participating in for-profit activities in violation of relevant provisions, or concurrently holding positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations;

(10) Having other disciplinary or legal violations.

Disciplinary sanctions for public prosecutors shall be handled in accordance with relevant provisions.

Article 48 If a public prosecutor is suspected of disciplinary or legal violations and has been placed under investigation or investigation, and is unsuitable to continue performing duties, his or her performance of duties shall be temporarily suspended in accordance with the management authority and prescribed procedures.

Article 49 The Supreme People's Procuratorate and provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committees, responsible for reviewing and determining from a professional perspective whether a public prosecutor has engaged in the acts of violating prosecutorial duties as specified in items (4) and (5) of Article 47 of this Law, and issuing review opinions on whether the conduct constitutes intentional violation of duties, gross negligence, ordinary negligence, or no violation of duties. After the Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committee issues its review opinion, the People's Procuratorate shall make a decision on whether to impose disciplinary sanctions in accordance with relevant provisions and take corresponding actions.

The Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committee shall consist of representatives of public prosecutors, other legal professionals, and representatives from relevant sectors, with public prosecutor representatives accounting for no less than half.

The daily work of the Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committee of the Supreme People's Procuratorate and provincial-level Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committees shall be undertaken by the internal functional departments of the relevant People's Procuratorates.

Article 50 When the Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committee deliberates on disciplinary matters, the public prosecutor concerned has the right to apply for the withdrawal of relevant personnel, and has the right to make statements, present evidence, and defend themselves.

Article 51 The review opinion issued by the Public Prosecutor Disciplinary Committee shall be served on the public prosecutor concerned. If the public prosecutor concerned has objections to the review opinion, he or she may raise them with the Disciplinary Committee, which shall review the objections and their reasons and make a decision.**Article 52** The specific procedures for the Disciplinary Committee of Procurators to deliberate on disciplinary matters shall be determined by the Supreme People's Procuratorate in consultation with relevant departments.

## Chapter VII: Professional Guarantees for Procurators

**Article 53** The People's Procuratorate shall establish a Procurators' Rights and Interests Protection Committee to safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of procurators and ensure they perform their duties in accordance with the law.

**Article 54** A procurator shall not be transferred from a procuratorial business post except under the following circumstances:

(1) When avoidance of office is required by regulations;
(2) When job rotation is required by regulations;
(3) When work adjustment is necessary due to institutional restructuring, dissolution, merger, or reduction of staffing quotas;
(4) When the procurator is unsuitable for procuratorial business work due to disciplinary or legal violations;
(5) Other circumstances stipulated by law.

**Article 55** No unit or individual may require a procurator to engage in matters beyond the scope of their statutory duties.

For any act of interference with a procurator's handling of a case, the procurator has the right to refuse and to make a comprehensive and truthful record and report; if disciplinary or legal violations are involved, the relevant authorities shall pursue the responsibility of the responsible persons and the actors according to the severity of the circumstances.

**Article 56** The professional dignity and personal safety of procurators are protected by law.

No unit or individual may retaliate against a procurator or their close relatives.

Any illegal or criminal acts such as retaliation, false accusation, defamation, violent assault, threats, intimidation, or harassment against procurators or their close relatives shall be severely punished in accordance with the law.

**Article 57** If a procurator's reputation is damaged due to false reports, false accusations, or defamation arising from their lawful performance of duties, the People's Procuratorate shall, together with the relevant departments, promptly clarify the facts, eliminate adverse effects, and pursue the responsibility of the relevant unit or individual in accordance with the law.

**Article 58** If a procurator or their close relatives face personal safety risks due to the procurator's lawful performance of duties, the People's Procuratorate and public security organs shall take necessary protective measures, such as personal protection and prohibiting contact by specific individuals.

**Article 59** Procurators shall implement a salary system commensurate with their duties, enjoy the salary benefits stipulated by the state according to their procurator rank, and establish a mechanism for synchronous adjustment with civil servant salaries.

The salary system for procurators shall be separately stipulated by the state based on the characteristics of procuratorial work.

**Article 60** Procurators shall implement a system of regular salary increases.

Those assessed as excellent or competent in the annual evaluation may have their salary grades raised in accordance with regulations.

**Article 61** Procurators shall enjoy the allowances, subsidies, bonuses, insurance, and welfare benefits stipulated by the state.

**Article 62** Procurators who become disabled in the line of duty shall enjoy the disability benefits stipulated by the state. If a procurator dies in the line of duty, dies on duty, or dies of illness, their relatives shall enjoy the pensions and preferential treatment stipulated by the state.

**Article 63** The retirement system for procurators shall be separately stipulated by the state based on the characteristics of procuratorial work.

**Article 64** After retirement, procurators shall enjoy the pension and other benefits stipulated by the state.

**Article 65** If state organs or their staff infringe upon the rights of procurators as stipulated in Article 11 of this Law, the procurator has the right to file a complaint.

**Article 66** If a sanction or personnel action against a procurator is erroneous, it shall be promptly corrected; if it causes damage to reputation, the reputation shall be restored, adverse effects eliminated, and an apology made; if it causes economic losses, compensation shall be paid. The directly responsible persons for retaliation shall be held accountable in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 67** The state implements a unified legal professional qualification examination system for junior procurators, organized and implemented by the judicial administrative department of the State Council in consultation with the Supreme People's Procuratorate and other relevant departments.

**Article 68** Procurator assistants in the People's Procuratorate are responsible, under the guidance of procurators, for procuratorial auxiliary tasks such as reviewing case materials and drafting legal documents.

The People's Procuratorate shall strengthen the construction of the procurator assistant team to reserve talent for the selection of procurators.

**Article 69** For matters concerning the rights, obligations, and management systems of procurators already stipulated in this Law, the provisions of this Law shall apply; for matters not stipulated in this Law, the relevant laws and regulations on civil servant management shall apply.

**Article 70** This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2019.
