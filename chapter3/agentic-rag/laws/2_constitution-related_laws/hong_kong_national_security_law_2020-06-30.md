# The Law of the People's Republic of China on Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region

Adopted at the 20th Session of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 30, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China, the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, and the Decision of the National People's Congress on Establishing and Improving the Legal System and Enforcement Mechanisms for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, for the purpose of resolutely and comprehensively implementing the principles of "One Country, Two Systems," "Hong Kong people administering Hong Kong," and a high degree of autonomy, safeguarding national security, and preventing, stopping, and punishing crimes such as secession, subversion of state power, organizing and carrying out terrorist activities, and collusion with foreign or external forces to endanger national security in relation to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, maintaining the prosperity and stability of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 2 The provisions of Article 1 and Article 12 of the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region regarding the legal status of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region are the fundamental provisions of the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. No institution, organization, or individual in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region may, in exercising their rights and freedoms, contravene the provisions of Article 1 and Article 12 of the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 3 The Central People's Government bears the fundamental responsibility for national security affairs relating to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region has the constitutional responsibility to safeguard national security and shall perform its duties in safeguarding national security.

The executive authorities, legislature, and judiciary of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant legal provisions, effectively prevent, stop, and punish acts and activities that endanger national security.

Article 4 In safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, human rights shall be respected and protected, and the rights and freedoms, including the freedoms of speech, press, and publication; the freedoms of association, assembly, procession, and demonstration, which the residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region enjoy under the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the provisions of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights and the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights as applied to Hong Kong, shall be protected in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The rule of law shall be adhered to in preventing, stopping, and punishing crimes that endanger national security. Acts that are defined as criminal offenses by law shall be convicted and punished in accordance with the law; acts that are not defined as criminal offenses by law shall not be convicted or punished.

No person shall be presumed guilty until convicted by a judicial body. The rights of defense and other litigation rights enjoyed by criminal suspects, defendants, and other participants in proceedings in accordance with the law shall be protected. No person who has been finally convicted or acquitted through judicial proceedings shall be tried or punished again for the same act.

Article 6 Safeguarding the sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity of the country is the common duty of all Chinese people, including the compatriots in Hong Kong.

All institutions, organizations, and individuals in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall abide by this Law and other laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region concerning the safeguarding of national security, and shall not engage in acts and activities that endanger national security.

Residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, when standing for election or assuming public office, shall sign a document to confirm or swear allegiance to the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China and bear allegiance to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 2: Duties and Institutions of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region for Safeguarding National Security

### Section 1: Duties

Article 7 The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall complete, as early as possible, the legislation for safeguarding national security as stipulated by the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and improve relevant laws.

Article 8 The law enforcement and judicial authorities of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall effectively implement the provisions of this Law and the existing laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region concerning the prevention, stopping, and punishment of acts and activities that endanger national security, and effectively safeguard national security.

Article 9 The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall strengthen its work in safeguarding national security and preventing terrorist activities. The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall take necessary measures to strengthen publicity, guidance, supervision, and management over matters relating to national security in schools, social organizations, the media, and the internet.

Article 10 The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall carry out education on national security through schools, social organizations, the media, and the internet, so as to enhance the awareness of national security and the awareness of abiding by the law among residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 11 The Chief Executive of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be accountable to the Central People's Government for the affairs of safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and shall submit an annual report on the performance of the duties of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region in safeguarding national security.

If the Central People's Government so requires, the Chief Executive shall submit a report in a timely manner on specific matters concerning the safeguarding of national security.

### Section 2: Institutions

Article 12 The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish a Committee for Safeguarding National Security, which shall be responsible for the affairs of safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, bear the primary responsibility for safeguarding national security, and be subject to the supervision and accountability of the Central People's Government.

Article 13 The Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be chaired by the Chief Executive, and its members shall include the Chief Secretary for Administration, the Financial Secretary, the Secretary for Justice, the Secretary for Security, the Commissioner of Police, the head of the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force stipulated in Article 16 of this Law, the Director of Immigration, the Commissioner of Customs and Excise, and the Director of the Chief Executive's Office.

The Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall have a Secretariat headed by a Secretary-General. The Secretary-General shall be nominated by the Chief Executive and appointed by the Central People's Government.

Article 14 The duties of the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region are:

(1) To analyze and assess the situation of safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, plan related work, and formulate policies for safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(2) To advance the establishment of the legal system and enforcement mechanisms for safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(3) To coordinate key tasks and major actions for safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The work of the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall not be subject to interference by any other institution, organization, or individual in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and information on its work shall not be disclosed. Decisions made by the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall not be subject to judicial review.

Article 15 The Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall have a National Security Adviser, who shall be designated by the Central People's Government and shall provide advice on matters relating to the performance of duties by the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. The National Security Adviser shall sit in on meetings of the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 16 The Hong Kong Police Force of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish a department for safeguarding national security, equipped with law enforcement capabilities.

The head of the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force shall be appointed by the Chief Executive. Before making the appointment, the Chief Executive shall seek in writing the opinion of the institution stipulated in Article 48 of this Law. The head of the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force shall, upon assuming office, swear to uphold the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, bear allegiance to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, abide by the law, and keep confidential matters.

The department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force may engage qualified specialized personnel and technical personnel from outside the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to assist in carrying out tasks related to safeguarding national security.

Article 17 The duties of the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force are:

(1) To collect and analyze intelligence and information concerning national security;

(2) To deploy, coordinate, and advance measures and actions for safeguarding national security;

(3) To investigate criminal cases that endanger national security;

(4) To conduct counter-intervention investigations and carry out national security reviews;

(5) To undertake work for safeguarding national security assigned by the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(6) To perform other duties required for the implementation of this Law.

Article 18 The Department of Justice of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish a specialized prosecution division for national security crimes, which shall be responsible for the prosecution of criminal cases that endanger national security and other related legal matters. The prosecutors of this division shall be appointed by the Secretary for Justice with the consent of the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The head of the prosecution division for national security crimes of the Department of Justice shall be appointed by the Chief Executive. Before making the appointment, the Chief Executive shall seek in writing the opinion of the institution stipulated in Article 48 of this Law. The head of the prosecution division for national security crimes of the Department of Justice shall, upon assuming office, swear to uphold the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, bear allegiance to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, abide by the law, and keep confidential matters.

Article 19 With the approval of the Chief Executive, the Financial Secretary of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall allocate a specific sum from the general revenue of the government to cover expenditure relating to the safeguarding of national security and approve the staffing involved, without being subject to the restrictions of the existing laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. The Financial Secretary shall submit an annual report to the Legislative Council on the control and management of that sum.

## Chapter 3: Offenses and Penalties

### Section 1: Offense of Secession

Article 20 Any person who organizes, plans, carries out, or participates in carrying out any of the following acts, whether or not by force or the threat of force, with the aim of seceding the country or undermining national unity, commits an offense:

(1) Seceding the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or any other part of the People's Republic of China from the People's Republic of China;

(2) Unlawfully altering the legal status of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or any other part of the People's Republic of China;

(3) Subjugating the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or any other part of the People's Republic of China to foreign rule.

A person who commits the offense in the preceding paragraph shall, if a principal offender or if the offense is of a grave nature, be sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years; if an active participant, be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years but not more than ten years; if a participant of a lesser role, be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, criminal detention, or surveillance.

Article 21 Any person who incites, assists, abets, or provides financial or material support to another person to commit the offense stipulated in Article 20 of this Law commits an offense. If the circumstances are serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than five years but not more than ten years; if the circumstances are less serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than five years, criminal detention, or surveillance.

### Section 2: Offense of Subversion of State Power

Article 22 Any person who organizes, plans, carries out, or participates in carrying out any of the following acts by force, threat of force, or other unlawful means, with the aim of subverting state power, commits an offense:

(1) Overthrowing or undermining the fundamental system of the People's Republic of China established by the Constitution of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Overthrowing the central government of the People's Republic of China or the government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(3) Seriously interfering with, obstructing, or undermining the lawful performance of functions by the central government of the People's Republic of China or the government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(4) Attacking or destroying the premises or facilities where the government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region performs its functions, rendering them unable to perform their functions normally.

A person who commits the offense in the preceding paragraph shall, if a principal offender or if the offense is of a grave nature, be sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years; if an active participant, be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years but not more than ten years; if a participant of a lesser role, be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, criminal detention, or surveillance.

Article 23 Any person who incites, assists, abets, or provides financial or material support to another person to commit the offense stipulated in Article 22 of this Law commits an offense. If the circumstances are serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than five years but not more than ten years; if the circumstances are less serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than five years, criminal detention, or surveillance.

### Section 3: Offense of Terrorist Activities

Article 24 Any person who organizes, plans, carries out, participates in carrying out, or threatens to carry out any of the following terrorist activities, causing or intending to cause serious social harm, with the aim of coercing the Central People's Government, the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, or an international organization, or intimidating the public in order to achieve political objectives, commits an offense:

(1) Serious violence against persons;

(2) Explosion, arson, or dissemination of toxic, radioactive, or infectious disease pathogens or other substances;

(3) Sabotaging means of transport, transport facilities, electrical equipment, gas equipment, or other flammable or explosive equipment;

(4) Seriously interfering with or disrupting the electronic control systems of public services and management, such as water, electricity, gas, transport, communications, and the internet;

(5) Using other dangerous methods to seriously endanger public health or safety.

A person who commits the offense in the preceding paragraph, causing serious injury or death to persons or causing heavy losses to public or private property, shall be sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years; in other cases, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years but not more than ten years.

Article 25 Any person who organizes or leads a terrorist organization commits an offense and shall be sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years, and shall be subject to confiscation of property; an active participant shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years but not more than ten years, and shall be fined; a participant of a lesser role shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, criminal detention, or surveillance, and may also be fined.

For the purposes of this Law, a "terrorist organization" means an organization that carries out or intends to carry out the terrorist activities stipulated in Article 24 of this Law, or participates in or assists in carrying out the terrorist activities stipulated in Article 24 of this Law.

Article 26 Any person who provides support, assistance, or facilitation, such as training, weapons, information, funds, materials, labor, transport, technology, or premises, to a terrorist organization, a terrorist, or the carrying out of terrorist activities, or manufactures or unlawfully possesses explosive, toxic, radioactive, or infectious disease pathogens or other substances, or prepares to carry out terrorist activities in other forms, commits an offense. If the circumstances are serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than five years but not more than ten years, and shall be fined or subject to confiscation of property; in other cases, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than five years, criminal detention, or surveillance, and shall be fined.

If an act in the preceding paragraph also constitutes another offense, the person shall be convicted and punished in accordance with the provisions for the more serious penalty.

Article 27 Any person who advocates terrorism or incites the carrying out of terrorist activities commits an offense. If the circumstances are serious, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than five years but not more than ten years, and shall be fined or subject to confiscation of property; in other cases, the person shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than five years, criminal detention, or surveillance, and shall be fined.

Article 28 The provisions of this Section shall not affect the investigation of criminal liability for other forms of terrorist activities and the adoption of measures such as freezing of property in accordance with the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

### Section 4: Offense of Collusion with Foreign or External Forces to Endanger National Security

Article 29 Any person who steals, spies, buys, or unlawfully provides state secrets or intelligence concerning national security for foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons; or requests foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons to carry out, conspires with foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons to carry out, or directly or indirectly receives instructions, control, funding, or other forms of support from foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons to carry out any of the following acts, commits an offense:

(1) Waging war against the People's Republic of China, or using force or the threat of force to cause serious harm to the sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Seriously obstructing the formulation and implementation of laws and policies by the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the Central People's Government, which may cause serious consequences;

(3) Manipulating or sabotaging elections in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, which may cause serious consequences;

(4) Imposing sanctions, blockades, or other hostile actions against the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the People's Republic of China;

(5) Inciting hatred among residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region towards the Central People's Government or the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region through various unlawful means, which may cause serious consequences.

A person who commits the offense in the preceding paragraph shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than three years but not more than ten years; if the offense is of a grave nature, the person shall be sentenced to life imprisonment or fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years.

Foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons involved in the acts stipulated in paragraph 1 of this Article shall be convicted and punished as joint offenders.

Article 30 Any person who conspires with foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons, or directly or indirectly receives instructions, control, funding, or other forms of support from foreign or external institutions, organizations, or persons, for the purpose of committing the offenses stipulated in Articles 20 and 22 of this Law, shall be punished more severely in accordance with the provisions of Articles 20 and 22 of this Law.

### Section 5: Other Provisions on Penalties

Article 31 If a legal person or unincorporated organization, such as a company or group, commits an offense stipulated in this Law, the organization shall be fined.

If a legal person or unincorporated organization, such as a company or group, is subject to criminal punishment for committing an offense stipulated in this Law, it shall be ordered to suspend operations, or its license or business permit shall be revoked.

Article 32 Any illegal gains, such as funding, proceeds, or remuneration, obtained from committing an offense stipulated in this Law, as well as funds and tools used or intended for use in the offense, shall be recovered and confiscated.

Article 33 In any of the following circumstances, the relevant offender, criminal suspect, or defendant may be given a lighter or mitigated punishment; if the offense is minor, the punishment may be waived:

(1) Voluntarily abandoning the offense or voluntarily and effectively preventing the occurrence of the result of the offense during the course of the offense;

(2) Voluntarily surrendering and truthfully confessing one's own offense;

(3) Reporting the criminal acts of others, which is verified, or providing important clues that lead to the solving of other cases.

If a criminal suspect or defendant who has been subjected to compulsory measures truthfully confesses other offenses stipulated in this Law that are not known to the law enforcement or judicial authorities, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of item (2) of the preceding paragraph.

Article 34 A person who is not a permanent resident of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and commits an offense stipulated in this Law may be subject to deportation, either as an independent penalty or as a supplementary penalty.

A person who is not a permanent resident of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and violates the provisions of this Law, but for any reason is not subject to criminal liability, may also be deported.

Article 35 Any person who is convicted by a court of an offense that endangers national security shall be disqualified from standing as a candidate in the elections for the Legislative Council, the District Councils, or any public office in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, or from serving as a member of the Election Committee of the Chief Executive; any Legislative Council member, government official or public servant, member of the Executive Council, judge or other judicial officer, or District Council member who has previously sworn or declared allegiance to the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China and borne allegiance to the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China shall be immediately disqualified from holding such office and shall be disqualified from standing for election or holding the aforementioned offices.

The disqualification from the qualifications or offices stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be declared by the institution responsible for organizing or managing the relevant elections or appointments to public office.

### Section 6: Scope of Application

Article 36 This Law shall apply to any person who commits an offense stipulated in this Law within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. An offense shall be deemed to have been committed within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region if either the act or the result of the offense occurs within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

This Law shall also apply to any person who commits an offense stipulated in this Law on board a ship or aircraft registered in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 37 This Law shall apply to any permanent resident of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, or any legal person or unincorporated organization, such as a company or group, established in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, who commits an offense stipulated in this Law outside the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.Article 38 This Law applies to persons who are not permanent residents of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region who commit crimes prescribed by this Law against the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region outside the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 39 This Law applies to acts committed after its entry into force for the purpose of conviction and sentencing.

## Chapter 4: Jurisdiction over Cases, Applicable Law, and Procedures

Article 40 The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall exercise jurisdiction over criminal cases prescribed by this Law, except in the circumstances specified in Article 55 of this Law.

Article 41 Matters concerning litigation procedures such as the filing and investigation, prosecution, trial, and execution of penalties for criminal cases endangering national security under the jurisdiction of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be governed by this Law and the local laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

No prosecution shall be instituted for a criminal case endangering national security without the written consent of the Secretary for Justice. However, this provision shall not affect the lawful arrest and detention of a criminal suspect in connection with such an offense, nor shall it affect the application for bail by such a criminal suspect.

Trials of criminal cases endangering national security under the jurisdiction of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be conducted by way of public prosecution.

Trials shall be conducted in public. Where it is not appropriate to hear a case in public due to matters involving State secrets, public order, or other circumstances, the press and the public shall be prohibited from attending all or part of the trial proceedings, but the judgment shall be announced publicly in all cases.

Article 42 When applying the provisions of the current laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region concerning detention, trial periods, and other related matters, the law enforcement and judicial authorities of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall ensure the fair and timely handling of criminal cases endangering national security, and effectively prevent, stop, and punish such offenses.

Bail shall not be granted to a criminal suspect or defendant unless the judge has sufficient grounds to believe that the person will not continue to commit acts endangering national security.

Article 43 When handling criminal cases endangering national security, the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region may take various measures that are permitted under the current laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region for the police and other law enforcement departments when investigating serious criminal cases, and may also take the following measures:

(1) Search premises, vehicles, vessels, aircraft, and other relevant places and electronic devices that may contain evidence of a crime;
(2) Require persons suspected of committing acts endangering national security to surrender their travel documents or restrict them from leaving the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;
(3) Freeze property used or intended for use in a crime, proceeds derived from a crime, and other property related to a crime, and apply for restraint orders, charging orders, confiscation orders, and forfeiture;
(4) Require information publishers or relevant service providers to remove information or provide assistance;
(5) Require foreign and overseas political organizations, and agents of foreign and overseas authorities or political organizations to provide information;
(6) With the approval of the Chief Executive, intercept communications and conduct covert surveillance on persons for whom there are reasonable grounds to suspect involvement in the commission of acts endangering national security;
(7) Require persons who are reasonably suspected of possessing information relevant to an investigation or having custody of relevant materials to answer questions and submit information or materials.

The Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall have supervisory responsibility over law enforcement agencies, including the department for safeguarding national security of the Hong Kong Police Force, in taking the measures specified in paragraph 1 of this Article.

The Chief Executive of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region is authorized, in conjunction with the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, to formulate detailed implementation rules for taking the measures specified in paragraph 1 of this Article.

Article 44 The Chief Executive of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall designate a number of judges from among magistrates, judges of the District Court, judges of the Court of First Instance and the Court of Appeal of the High Court, and judges of the Court of Final Appeal, and may also designate a number of judges from among deputy judges or special judges, to handle criminal cases endangering national security. The Chief Executive may consult the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the Chief Justice of the Court of Final Appeal before designating judges. The term of office for the designated judges shall be one year.

Any person who has engaged in any act or speech endangering national security shall not be designated as a judge to hear criminal cases endangering national security. If a designated judge engages in any act or speech endangering national security during their term of office, their designation as a judge shall be terminated.

Criminal proceedings instituted in the Magistrates' Courts, the District Court, the High Court, and the Court of Final Appeal in respect of criminal cases endangering national security shall be handled by the designated judges of the respective courts.

Article 45 Unless otherwise provided in this Law, the Magistrates' Courts, the District Court, the High Court, and the Court of Final Appeal shall handle criminal proceedings instituted in respect of criminal cases endangering national security in accordance with other laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 46 For criminal proceedings instituted in the Court of First Instance of the High Court in respect of criminal cases endangering national security, the Secretary for Justice may, on grounds such as the protection of State secrets, the involvement of foreign elements in the case, or the protection of the personal safety of jurors and their family members, issue a certificate directing that the relevant proceedings be conducted without a jury. Where the Secretary for Justice issues such a certificate, the Court of First Instance of the High Court shall conduct the trial without a jury, and the trial shall be heard by a panel of three judges.

Where the Secretary for Justice issues a certificate as provided in the preceding paragraph, any provision of the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region applicable to the relevant proceedings concerning "jury" or "verdict of the jury" shall be construed as referring to the judge or the function of the judge as the trier of fact.

Article 47 When hearing a case, if a court of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region encounters a question concerning whether an act involves national security or whether relevant evidence involves State secrets, it shall obtain a certificate from the Chief Executive on such questions, and such certificate shall be binding on the court.

## Chapter 5: Office for Safeguarding National Security of the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region

Article 48 The Central People's Government shall establish an Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. The Office for Safeguarding National Security of the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall perform its duties of safeguarding national security and exercise relevant powers in accordance with the law.

Personnel of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be jointly dispatched by the relevant organs of the Central People's Government responsible for safeguarding national security.

Article 49 The duties of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region are:

(1) Analyzing and assessing the situation of safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and providing opinions and suggestions on major strategies and important policies for safeguarding national security;
(2) Supervising, guiding, coordinating, and supporting the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region in fulfilling its duties of safeguarding national security;
(3) Collecting and analyzing intelligence information on national security;
(4) Handling criminal cases endangering national security in accordance with the law.

Article 50 The Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall strictly perform its duties in accordance with the law, accept supervision in accordance with the law, and shall not infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of any individual or organization.

Personnel of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall, in addition to complying with national laws, also comply with the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Personnel of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall accept supervision by the national supervisory authorities in accordance with the law.

Article 51 The funding of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be guaranteed by the central finances.

Article 52 The Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall strengthen its working contacts and coordination with the Liaison Office of the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, the Office of the Commissioner of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and the Hong Kong Garrison of the Chinese People's Liberation Army.

Article 53 The Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish a coordination mechanism with the Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to supervise and guide the work of safeguarding national security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The working departments of the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish a cooperation mechanism with the relevant organs of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region responsible for safeguarding national security to enhance information sharing and operational coordination.

Article 54 The Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the Office of the Commissioner of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall, in conjunction with the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, take necessary measures to strengthen the management and services for institutions of foreign countries and international organizations in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and for foreign and overseas non-governmental organizations and news organizations in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 55 In any of the following circumstances, upon the proposal of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and with the approval of the Central People's Government, the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall exercise jurisdiction over criminal cases endangering national security prescribed by this Law:

(1) The case involves complex circumstances with the involvement of foreign or overseas forces, making it genuinely difficult for the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to exercise jurisdiction;
(2) A serious situation arises where the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region is unable to effectively enforce this Law;
(3) A situation arises where national security faces a major and real threat.

Article 56 When jurisdiction over a criminal case endangering national security is exercised pursuant to Article 55 of this Law, the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be responsible for filing and investigating the case, the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall designate the relevant procuratorial organ to exercise prosecutorial power, and the Supreme People's Court shall designate the relevant court to exercise judicial power.

Article 57 Matters concerning litigation procedures such as the filing and investigation, review of prosecution, trial, and execution of penalties for cases under jurisdiction pursuant to Article 55 of this Law shall be governed by the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws.

When a case is under jurisdiction pursuant to Article 55 of this Law, the law enforcement and judicial authorities specified in Article 56 of this Law shall exercise relevant powers in accordance with the law, and the legal documents issued by them for deciding to take compulsory measures, investigative measures, and judicial rulings shall have legal effect in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. Relevant institutions, organizations, and individuals must comply with the measures lawfully taken by the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 58 When a case is under jurisdiction pursuant to Article 55 of this Law, a criminal suspect shall, from the date of their first interrogation by the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the date on which compulsory measures are taken against them, have the right to entrust a lawyer as their defender. The defense lawyer may provide legal assistance to the criminal suspect or defendant in accordance with the law.

After being lawfully arrested or detained, a criminal suspect or defendant shall have the right to receive a fair trial by a judicial authority at the earliest opportunity.

Article 59 When a case is under jurisdiction pursuant to Article 55 of this Law, any person who has knowledge of the circumstances of a criminal case endangering national security prescribed by this Law has the obligation to testify truthfully.

Article 60 Acts performed by the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and its personnel in the execution of their duties pursuant to this Law shall not be subject to the jurisdiction of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Persons and vehicles holding credentials or certification documents issued by the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall not be subject to inspection, search, or seizure by law enforcement personnel of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region when performing their duties.

The Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and its personnel shall enjoy other rights and immunities provided for by the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 61 When the Office for Safeguarding National Security in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region performs its duties pursuant to this Law, the relevant departments of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall provide necessary facilities and cooperation, and shall, in accordance with the law, stop and hold accountable any acts that obstruct the performance of such duties.

## Chapter 6: Supplementary Provisions

Article 62 Where the provisions of the local laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region are inconsistent with this Law, the provisions of this Law shall apply.

Article 63 The relevant law enforcement and judicial authorities and their personnel handling criminal cases endangering national security prescribed by this Law, or the law enforcement and judicial authorities and their personnel of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region handling other criminal cases endangering national security, shall keep confidential any State secrets, commercial secrets, and personal privacy learned during the handling of the case.

Lawyers acting as defenders or agents ad litem shall keep confidential any State secrets, commercial secrets, and personal privacy learned during the course of their professional activities.

Relevant institutions, organizations, and individuals cooperating in the handling of a case shall keep confidential the relevant circumstances of the case.

Article 64 When this Law is applied in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, the terms "fixed-term imprisonment," "life imprisonment," "confiscation of property," and "fine" as used in this Law shall respectively refer to "imprisonment," "life imprisonment," "forfeiture of proceeds of crime," and "fine"; "criminal detention" shall be applied with reference to "imprisonment," "detention in a detention center," or "detention in a training center" as provided for in the relevant laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; "public surveillance" shall be applied with reference to "community service orders" or "detention in a reformatory school" as provided for in the relevant laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; and "revocation of license or business permit" shall refer to "cancellation of registration or exemption from registration, or cancellation of license" as provided for in the relevant laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 65 The power of interpretation of this Law belongs to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 66 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
