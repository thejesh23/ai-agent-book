# Organic Law of the State Council of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on December 10, 1982

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Organic Law is enacted in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution of the People's Republic of China concerning the State Council.

Article 2 The State Council is composed of the Premier, Vice-Premiers, State Councilors, Ministers in charge of ministries, Ministers in charge of commissions, the Auditor-General, and the Secretary-General.

The State Council practices the system of Premier responsibility. The Premier directs the work of the State Council. The Vice-Premiers and State Councilors assist the Premier in his work.

Article 3 The State Council exercises the functions and powers prescribed in Article 89 of the Constitution.

Article 4 Meetings of the State Council are divided into plenary meetings and executive meetings. Plenary meetings of the State Council are composed of all members of the State Council. Executive meetings of the State Council are composed of the Premier, Vice-Premiers, State Councilors, and the Secretary-General. The Premier convenes and presides over plenary meetings and executive meetings of the State Council. Major issues in the work of the State Council must be discussed and decided at executive meetings or plenary meetings of the State Council.

Article 5 Decisions, orders, and administrative regulations issued by the State Council, bills submitted to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee, and appointments and removals of personnel shall be signed by the Premier.

Article 6 State Councilors, entrusted by the Premier, are responsible for certain aspects of work or special tasks and may represent the State Council in foreign affairs activities.

Article 7 The Secretary-General of the State Council, under the leadership of the Premier, is responsible for handling the day-to-day work of the State Council.

The State Council shall have several Deputy Secretaries-General to assist the Secretary-General in his work.

The State Council establishes a General Office, which is led by the Secretary-General.

Article 8 The establishment, dissolution, or merger of ministries and commissions of the State Council shall be proposed by the Premier and decided by the National People's Congress; when the National People's Congress is not in session, it shall be decided by its Standing Committee.

Article 9 Each ministry shall have one Minister and two to four Vice-Ministers. Each commission shall have one Minister in charge, two to four Vice-Ministers in charge, and five to ten members.

Each ministry and commission practices the system of Minister or Minister-in-charge responsibility. The Minister of each ministry and the Minister in charge of each commission directs the work of their respective department, convenes and presides over ministry meetings or commission meetings and commission executive meetings, signs important reports and requests submitted to the State Council, and issues orders and directives. Vice-Ministers and Vice-Ministers in charge assist the Minister or Minister in charge in their work.

Article 10 The guiding principles, policies, plans, and major administrative measures in the work of each ministry and commission shall be reported to the State Council for decision. In accordance with laws and decisions of the State Council, the responsible ministry or commission may, within its authority, issue orders, directives, and regulations.

Article 11 The State Council may, in accordance with the needs of work and the principle of simplification, establish several directly subordinate organs to take charge of various specialized businesses, and establish several working offices to assist the Premier in handling specialized matters. Each such organ shall have two to five responsible persons.
