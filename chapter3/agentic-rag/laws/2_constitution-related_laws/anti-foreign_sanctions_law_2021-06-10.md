# Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 10, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to safeguard national sovereignty, security, and development interests, and to protect the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese citizens and organizations.

Article 2 The People's Republic of China adheres to an independent foreign policy of peace, upholds the Five Principles of mutual respect for sovereignty and territorial integrity, mutual non-aggression, mutual non-interference in internal affairs, equality and mutual benefit, and peaceful coexistence, maintains the international system with the United Nations at its core and the international order based on international law, develops friendly cooperation with countries around the world, and promotes the building of a community with a shared future for mankind.

Article 3 The People's Republic of China opposes hegemonism and power politics, and opposes any country interfering in China's internal affairs under any pretext or by any means.

Where a foreign country, in violation of international law and the basic norms of international relations, contains or suppresses China under various pretexts or based on its own laws, adopts discriminatory restrictive measures against Chinese citizens or organizations, or interferes in China's internal affairs, China has the right to take corresponding countermeasures.

Article 4 The relevant departments of the State Council may decide to include individuals and organizations that directly or indirectly participate in the formulation, decision, or implementation of the discriminatory restrictive measures specified in Article 3 of this Law on a countermeasure list.

Article 5 In addition to the individuals and organizations included on the countermeasure list pursuant to Article 4 of this Law, the relevant departments of the State Council may also decide to take countermeasures against the following individuals and organizations:

(1) The spouses and immediate family members of individuals on the countermeasure list;

(2) Senior managers or actual controllers of organizations on the countermeasure list;

(3) Organizations where individuals on the countermeasure list serve as senior managers;

(4) Organizations that are actually controlled by, or established or operated with the participation of, individuals or organizations on the countermeasure list.

Article 6 The relevant departments of the State Council may, in accordance with their respective functions, duties, and task assignments, decide to take one or more of the following measures against the individuals and organizations specified in Articles 4 and 5 of this Law, based on the actual circumstances:

(1) Refusing to issue visas, denying entry, canceling visas, or deporting;

(2) Sealing, seizing, or freezing movable property, immovable property, and other types of property within China;

(3) Prohibiting or restricting organizations and individuals within China from engaging in transactions, cooperation, or other activities with them;

(4) Other necessary measures.

Article 7 Decisions made by the relevant departments of the State Council in accordance with Articles 4 through 6 of this Law are final.

Article 8 If the circumstances on which the countermeasures are based change, the relevant departments of the State Council may suspend, modify, or revoke the relevant countermeasures.

Article 9 The determination, suspension, modification, or revocation of the countermeasure list and countermeasures shall be announced by order issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or other relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 10 The State shall establish a coordination mechanism for anti-foreign sanctions work, responsible for overall coordination of related efforts.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall strengthen coordination, cooperation, and information sharing, and determine and implement relevant countermeasures in accordance with their respective functions, duties, and task assignments.

Article 11 Organizations and individuals within China shall implement the countermeasures taken by the relevant departments of the State Council.

Organizations and individuals that violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be dealt with by the relevant departments of the State Council in accordance with the law, and their engagement in relevant activities shall be restricted or prohibited.

Article 12 No organization or individual shall implement or assist in implementing discriminatory restrictive measures taken by foreign countries against Chinese citizens or organizations.

If an organization or individual violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese citizens or organizations, the Chinese citizens or organizations may file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with the law, requesting cessation of the infringement and compensation for losses.

Article 13 For acts that endanger China's sovereignty, security, or development interests, in addition to the provisions of this Law, relevant laws, administrative regulations, and departmental rules may stipulate the taking of other necessary countermeasures.

Article 14 Any organization or individual that fails to implement or cooperate in the implementation of countermeasures shall be held legally liable in accordance with the law.

Article 15 For acts by foreign countries, organizations, or individuals that implement, assist, or support acts endangering China's sovereignty, security, or development interests, where necessary countermeasures need to be taken, the relevant provisions of this Law shall be applied mutatis mutandis.

Article 16 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
