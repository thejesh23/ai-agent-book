# Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the First Session of the 13th National People's Congress on March 20, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to deepen the reform of the national supervision system, strengthen supervision over all public officials who exercise public power, achieve full coverage of national supervision, further advance the fight against corruption, and modernize the national governance system and governance capacity.

Article 2 The leadership of the Communist Party of China over national supervision work shall be upheld. Guided by Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of Three Represents, the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, a centralized, unified, authoritative, and efficient national supervision system with Chinese characteristics shall be established.

Article 3 Supervision commissions at all levels are the specialized organs exercising the functions of national supervision. In accordance with this Law, they shall supervise all public officials who exercise public power (hereinafter referred to as public officials), investigate duty-related violations and crimes, carry out integrity building and anti-corruption work, and uphold the dignity of the Constitution and laws.

Article 4 Supervision commissions shall independently exercise their power of supervision in accordance with the law, free from interference by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

When handling cases of duty-related violations and crimes, supervisory organs shall cooperate with and be subject to checks by adjudicatory organs, procuratorial organs, and law enforcement departments.

Where a supervisory organ needs assistance in its work, the relevant organ or unit shall provide such assistance in accordance with the law upon the request of the supervisory organ.

Article 5 National supervision work shall strictly follow the Constitution and laws, be based on facts, and take the law as the criterion; apply the law equally to all, and protect the lawful rights and interests of the parties concerned; match powers with responsibilities, and exercise strict supervision; combine punishment with education, and temper justice with mercy.

Article 6 National supervision work shall adhere to addressing both symptoms and root causes, comprehensive governance, strengthening supervision and accountability, and severely punishing corruption; deepen reform, improve the rule of law, and effectively check and supervise power; strengthen legal and moral education, promote the fine traditional culture of China, and establish a long-term mechanism that ensures officials dare not, cannot, and do not want to be corrupt.

## Chapter II Supervisory Organs and Their Duties

Article 7 The National Commission of Supervision of the People's Republic of China is the highest supervisory organ.

Supervision commissions shall be established in provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, counties, autonomous counties, cities, and municipal districts.

Article 8 The National Commission of Supervision is created by the National People's Congress and is responsible for national supervision work.

The National Commission of Supervision is composed of one chairperson, a number of vice-chairpersons, and a number of members. The chairperson is elected by the National People's Congress. The vice-chairpersons and members are appointed or removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the chairperson of the National Commission of Supervision.

The term of office of the chairperson of the National Commission of Supervision shall be the same as the term of office of the National People's Congress. The chairperson shall serve no more than two consecutive terms.

The National Commission of Supervision is responsible to the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee and shall accept their supervision.

Article 9 Local supervision commissions at all levels are created by the people's congresses at the corresponding levels and are responsible for supervision work within their respective administrative regions.

A local supervision commission is composed of one chairperson, a number of vice-chairpersons, and a number of members. The chairperson is elected by the people's congress at the corresponding level. The vice-chairpersons and members are appointed or removed by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level upon the recommendation of the chairperson of the supervision commission.

The term of office of the chairperson of a local supervision commission shall be the same as the term of office of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

A local supervision commission is responsible to the people's congress at the corresponding level and its standing committee, as well as to the supervision commission at the next higher level, and shall accept their supervision.

Article 10 The National Commission of Supervision shall direct the work of local supervision commissions at all levels. A supervision commission at a higher level shall direct the work of supervision commissions at lower levels.

Article 11 A supervision commission shall, in accordance with this Law and relevant laws, perform the duties of supervision, investigation, and disposition:

(1) Conduct integrity education for public officials, and supervise and inspect their performance of duties in accordance with the law, impartial exercise of power, clean and honest conduct in government and professional activities, and moral integrity;

(2) Investigate duty-related violations and crimes such as suspected embezzlement or bribery, abuse of power, dereliction of duty, power rent-seeking, transfer of benefits, favoritism or fraud, and waste of state assets;

(3) Make decisions on administrative sanctions against public officials who have violated the law; hold accountable leading officials who have failed to perform their duties effectively or have been derelict or negligent; where a duty-related crime is suspected, transfer the investigation results to the people's procuratorate for review and public prosecution in accordance with the law; and submit supervisory recommendations to the unit to which the supervisee belongs.

Article 12 Supervision commissions at all levels may station or dispatch supervisory agencies or commissioners to the organs of the Communist Party of China, state organs, organizations and units authorized or entrusted by laws and regulations to manage public affairs, as well as administrative regions under their jurisdiction and state-owned enterprises.

Supervisory agencies or commissioners shall be responsible to the supervision commission that stationed or dispatched them.

Article 13 Stationed or dispatched supervisory agencies or commissioners shall, within the scope of authorization and in accordance with the management authority, supervise public officials, make supervisory recommendations, and investigate and dispose of public officials in accordance with the law.

Article 14 The state shall implement a system of supervisors, and establish systems for determining the ranks, appointment and removal, assessment, and promotion of supervisors in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III Scope of Supervision and Jurisdiction

Article 15 Supervisory organs shall supervise the following public officials and relevant personnel:

(1) Civil servants in the organs of the Communist Party of China, the organs of the people's congresses and their standing committees, the people's governments, the supervision commissions, the people's courts, the people's procuratorates, the organs of the committees of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference at all levels, the organs of democratic parties, and the organs of the federations of industry and commerce, as well as personnel managed by reference to the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Personnel engaged in public affairs in organizations authorized by laws or regulations or entrusted by state organs to manage public affairs in accordance with the law;

(3) Management personnel of state-owned enterprises;

(4) Management personnel in public institutions of education, scientific research, culture, medical and health care, sports, etc.;

(5) Management personnel in grassroots self-governing mass organizations;

(6) Other personnel who perform public duties in accordance with the law.

Article 16 Supervision commissions at all levels shall, in accordance with their management authority, have jurisdiction over supervision matters involving the personnel specified in Article 15 of this Law within their respective jurisdictions.

A supervision commission at a higher level may handle supervision matters within the jurisdiction of a supervision commission at the next lower level, and may, when necessary, also handle supervision matters within the jurisdiction of any supervision commission under its authority.

Where there is a dispute over jurisdiction over a supervision matter between supervision commissions, the supervision commission at the next higher level common to them shall determine the jurisdiction.

Article 17 A supervision commission at a higher level may designate a supervision matter under its jurisdiction to a supervision commission at a lower level for handling, or may designate a supervision matter within the jurisdiction of a supervision commission at a lower level to another supervision commission for handling.

Where a supervision commission considers a supervision matter under its jurisdiction to be major or complex and requires handling by a supervision commission at a higher level, it may request the supervision commission at the higher level to handle it.

## Chapter IV Supervision Authority

Article 18 When exercising the powers of supervision and investigation, a supervisory organ shall have the authority to learn about the situation from relevant units and individuals, and to collect and obtain evidence. The relevant units and individuals shall truthfully provide the information.

Supervisory organs and their staff shall keep confidential any state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy learned during the process of supervision and investigation.

No unit or individual may forge, conceal, or destroy evidence.

Article 19 With respect to supervisees who may commit duty-related violations, a supervisory organ may, in accordance with its management authority, directly or through entrusting the relevant organ or personnel, conduct interviews or request explanations of the situation.

Article 20 During an investigation, a supervisory organ may require a person under investigation suspected of a duty-related violation to make a statement regarding the suspected illegal act, and, when necessary, issue a written notice to the person under investigation.

With respect to a person under investigation suspected of duty-related crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance, a supervisory organ may conduct interrogations and require the person to truthfully confess the suspected criminal circumstances.

Article 21 During an investigation, a supervisory organ may question witnesses and other persons.

Article 22 Where a person under investigation is suspected of serious duty-related violations or duty-related crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance, and the supervisory organ has already obtained some facts and evidence of the violations or crimes, but there are still important issues requiring further investigation, and any of the following circumstances exists, the supervisory organ may, upon approval in accordance with the law, detain the person in a designated place:

(1) The case involves major or complex circumstances;

(2) The person may abscond or commit suicide;

(3) The person may collude to make consistent statements or forge, conceal, or destroy evidence;

(4) There may be other acts obstructing the investigation.

With respect to persons involved in a case who are suspected of bribery crimes or joint duty-related crimes, a supervisory organ may take detention measures in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

The establishment, management, and supervision of detention places shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

Article 23 When investigating serious duty-related violations or duty-related crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance, a supervisory organ may, as required by work, inquire into and freeze the deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, fund shares, and other property of the units and individuals involved in the case in accordance with the provisions. The relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

If the frozen property is found to be unrelated to the case, the freeze shall be lifted and the property returned within three days after the finding.

Article 24 A supervisory organ may search the body, articles, residence, and other relevant places of a person under investigation suspected of a duty-related crime, as well as of persons who may be hiding the person under investigation or criminal evidence. When conducting a search, a search warrant shall be presented, and witnesses such as the person being searched or his or her family members shall be present.

A search of a female's body shall be conducted by female staff.

When conducting a search, a supervisory organ may request the assistance of a public security organ as required by work. The public security organ shall provide assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 25 During an investigation, a supervisory organ may obtain, seal, or seize property, documents, electronic data, and other information used to prove the suspected violations or crimes of the person under investigation. When taking measures to obtain, seal, or seize, the original items shall be collected, and in the presence of the holder or custodian and witnesses, the items shall be photographed, registered, and numbered one by one, a list shall be made, checked and signed by the persons present on the spot, and a copy of the list shall be handed over to the holder or custodian of the property or documents.

With respect to the property and documents obtained, sealed, or seized, a supervisory organ shall set up a special account and a special place, designate special personnel for proper custody, strictly fulfill the procedures for transfer and obtaining, conduct regular account verification, and shall not damage or use them for other purposes. Items of unknown value shall be appraised promptly and sealed and stored separately.

If the sealed or seized property or documents are found to be unrelated to the case, the sealing or seizure shall be lifted and the items returned within three days after the finding.

Article 26 During an investigation, a supervisory organ may directly conduct, or assign or engage persons with specialized knowledge and qualifications to conduct, an inspection or examination under the direction of the investigators. A record of the inspection or examination shall be made, signed or sealed by the persons participating in the inspection or examination and the witnesses.

Article 27 During an investigation, a supervisory organ may, with respect to specialized issues in a case, assign or engage persons with specialized knowledge to conduct an appraisal. After conducting the appraisal, the appraiser shall issue an appraisal opinion and sign it.

Article 28 When investigating suspected major duty-related crimes such as embezzlement or bribery, a supervisory organ may, as necessary and after undergoing strict approval procedures, adopt technical investigation measures, which shall be executed by the relevant organ in accordance with the provisions.

The approval decision shall specify the types of technical investigation measures and the subjects to whom they apply, and shall be valid for a period of three months from the date of issuance; for complex or difficult cases, if it is still necessary to continue the technical investigation measures after the expiration of the period, the validity period may be extended upon approval, each extension not exceeding three months. Where it is no longer necessary to continue the technical investigation measures, they shall be promptly terminated.

Article 29 If a person under investigation who should be detained in accordance with the law is at large, the supervisory organ may decide to issue a wanted notice within its administrative region, and the public security organ shall issue the wanted notice and pursue the person for arrest. If the scope of the wanted notice exceeds the administrative region, the matter shall be reported to the supervisory organ at the next higher level that has the authority to decide.

Article 30 To prevent a person under investigation or related persons from fleeing abroad, a supervisory organ may, upon approval by a supervisory organ at or above the provincial level, take measures to restrict the exit of the person under investigation or related persons, which shall be executed by the public security organ in accordance with the law. Where it is no longer necessary to continue the measures restricting exit, they shall be promptly terminated.

Article 31 Where a person under investigation suspected of a duty-related crime voluntarily admits guilt and accepts punishment, and falls under any of the following circumstances, the supervisory organ may, after collective study by the leading personnel and approval by the supervisory organ at the next higher level, propose a recommendation for lenient punishment when transferring the case to the people's procuratorate:

(1) Voluntarily surrendering and sincerely repenting;

(2) Actively cooperating with the investigation and truthfully confessing violations or crimes not yet known to the supervisory organ;

(3) Actively returning illicit gains and reducing losses;

(4) Having made significant meritorious contributions, or the case involves major national interests, etc.

Article 32 Where a person involved in a duty-related violation or crime exposes the duty-related violation or criminal conduct of the person under investigation, and the exposure is verified, or provides important clues that help in the investigation of other cases, the supervisory organ may, after collective study by the leading personnel and approval by the supervisory organ at the next higher level, propose a recommendation for lenient punishment when transferring the case to the people's procuratorate.

Article 33 Physical evidence, documentary evidence, witness testimony, statements and exculpations of the person under investigation, audio-visual materials, electronic data, and other evidentiary materials collected by a supervisory organ in accordance with the provisions of this Law may be used as evidence in criminal proceedings.

When collecting, fixing, reviewing, and using evidence, a supervisory organ shall be consistent with the requirements and standards for evidence in criminal trials.

Evidence collected by illegal means shall be excluded in accordance with the law and shall not be used as a basis for case disposition.

Article 34 Where state organs such as the people's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, and audit organs discover clues of duty-related violations or crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance by public officials in the course of their work, they shall transfer the clues to the supervisory organ, which shall investigate and dispose of them in accordance with the law.

Where a person under investigation is suspected of both serious duty-related violations or duty-related crimes and other violations or crimes, the supervisory organ shall generally take the lead in the investigation, with other organs providing assistance.

## Chapter V Supervision Procedures

Article 35 A supervisory organ shall accept reports or tip-offs and handle them in accordance with the relevant provisions. If the matter is not within its jurisdiction, it shall transfer it to the competent organ for handling.

Article 36 A supervisory organ shall strictly carry out its work in accordance with procedures, and establish a working mechanism for coordination and mutual checks among the departments handling problem clues, investigation, and adjudication.

A supervisory organ shall strengthen the supervision and management of the entire process of investigation and disposition, and establish corresponding work departments to perform management and coordination functions such as clue management, supervision and inspection, urging handling, and statistical analysis.

Article 37 With respect to problem clues concerning a supervisee, a supervisory organ shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions, propose a disposition opinion, go through the approval procedures, and handle them by category. The handling of clues shall be summarized and reported periodically, and regularly inspected and spot-checked.

Article 38 Where it is necessary to adopt preliminary verification methods to handle problem clues, a supervisory organ shall go through the approval procedures in accordance with the law and establish a verification team. After the preliminary verification work is completed, the verification team shall write a preliminary verification report and propose handling suggestions. The undertaking department shall propose opinions on handling by category. The preliminary verification report and the opinions on handling by category shall be submitted to the principal person in charge of the supervisory organ for approval.

Article 39 After preliminary verification, if a supervisee is suspected of duty-related violations or crimes and legal liability needs to be pursued, the supervisory organ shall go through the case-filing procedures in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures.

After the principal person in charge of the supervisory organ approves the case filing in accordance with the law, the principal person in charge shall preside over a special meeting to study and determine the investigation plan and decide on the investigation measures to be taken.

The decision to file a case for investigation shall be announced to the person under investigation and reported to the relevant organization. If serious duty-related violations or duty-related crimes are suspected, the family members of the person under investigation shall be notified, and the matter shall be publicly announced to the public.

Article 40 A supervisory organ shall investigate cases of duty-related violations and crimes, collect evidence of whether the person under investigation has committed violations or crimes and the severity of the circumstances, ascertain the facts of the violations or crimes, and form a mutually corroborating, complete, and stable chain of evidence.

It is strictly prohibited to collect evidence by threats, inducement, deception, or other illegal means, and it is strictly prohibited to insult, beat, abuse, torture, or subject the person under investigation or persons involved in the case to corporal punishment or disguised corporal punishment.

Article 41 When investigators take investigation measures such as interrogation, questioning, detention, search, obtaining, sealing, seizing, inspection, or examination, they shall present their credentials, issue a written notice, and be conducted by two or more persons, and shall produce written materials such as records and reports, which shall be signed or sealed by the relevant persons.

When investigators conduct important evidence collection work such as interrogation, search, sealing, or seizure, they shall make audio and video recordings of the entire process for retention and future reference.

Article 42 Investigators shall strictly implement the investigation plan and shall not arbitrarily expand the scope of investigation or change the subjects or matters of investigation.

Important matters during the investigation process shall be studied collectively and then reported and requested for instructions in accordance with procedures.

Article 43 The decision to take detention measures by a supervisory organ shall be made after collective study by the leading personnel of the supervisory organ. If a supervisory organ at or below the level of a city divided into districts takes detention measures, it shall report to the supervisory organ at the next higher level for approval. If a supervisory organ at the provincial level takes detention measures, it shall report to the National Commission of Supervision for the record.

The period of detention shall not exceed three months. Under special circumstances, it may be extended once, and the extension shall not exceed three months. If a supervisory organ below the provincial level takes detention measures, the extension of the detention period shall be reported to the supervisory organ at the next higher level for approval. If a supervisory organ finds that the detention measures taken are inappropriate, it shall promptly terminate them.

When taking detention measures, a supervisory organ may request the assistance of a public security organ as required by work. The public security organ shall provide assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 44 After taking detention measures against a person under investigation, the unit to which the detained person belongs and his or her family members shall be notified within 24 hours, except where there are circumstances that may obstruct the investigation, such as the possibility of destroying or forging evidence, interfering with witness testimony, or colluding to make consistent statements. Once the circumstances obstructing the investigation disappear, the unit to which the detained person belongs and his or her family members shall be notified immediately.

A supervisory organ shall ensure the food, rest, and safety of the detained person and provide medical services. Interrogation of the detained person shall be reasonably arranged in terms of time and duration, and the interrogation record shall be signed by the interrogated person after being read.

If the detained person is suspected of a crime and transferred to the judicial authorities, and is subsequently sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, or fixed-term imprisonment in accordance with the law, one day of detention shall be offset against two days of public surveillance or one day of criminal detention or fixed-term imprisonment.

Article 45 A supervisory organ shall, based on the results of supervision and investigation, make the following dispositions in accordance with the law:

(1) With respect to a public official who has committed a duty-related violation but the circumstances are minor, the supervisory organ shall, in accordance with the management authority, directly or through entrusting the relevant organ or personnel, conduct a talk as a reminder, criticize and educate, order a self-examination, or issue a reprimand;(2) Make decisions on disciplinary sanctions such as warnings, demerits, major demerits, demotion, removal from office, and expulsion for public employees who violate the law, in accordance with legal procedures;

(3) For leading officials who are responsible for failing to perform or improperly performing their duties, directly make accountability decisions within their management authority, or submit accountability recommendations to the authority empowered to make such decisions;

(4) For suspected duty-related crimes, if the supervisory authority determines through investigation that the criminal facts are clear, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, it shall prepare a prosecution opinion and transfer it together with the case files and evidence to the People's Procuratorate for legal review and initiation of public prosecution;

(5) Propose supervisory recommendations regarding issues such as integrity building and performance of duties in the unit where the supervisee works.

If, after investigation, the supervisory authority finds no evidence of illegal or criminal conduct by the person under investigation, it shall close the case and notify the person's unit.

Article 46: If, after investigation, the supervisory authority determines that property was obtained illegally, it shall confiscate, recover, or order restitution in accordance with the law; property obtained through suspected criminal activities shall be transferred to the People's Procuratorate along with the case.

Article 47: For cases transferred by the supervisory authority, the People's Procuratorate shall take compulsory measures against the person under investigation in accordance with the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

If, upon review, the People's Procuratorate finds that the criminal facts have been established, the evidence is reliable and sufficient, and criminal liability should be pursued according to law, it shall make a decision to initiate prosecution.

If, upon review, the People's Procuratorate finds that further verification is needed, it shall return the case to the supervisory authority for supplementary investigation, and may conduct supplementary investigation on its own when necessary. Supplementary investigation shall be completed within one month and may be conducted no more than twice.

If the People's Procuratorate finds circumstances under the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China that warrant non-prosecution, it shall, with the approval of the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level, make a decision not to prosecute in accordance with the law. If the supervisory authority believes the decision not to prosecute is erroneous, it may request a review by the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level.

Article 48: During the investigation of duty-related crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance, if the person under investigation absconds or dies and further investigation is necessary, the investigation shall continue and a conclusion shall be reached with the approval of a supervisory authority at or above the provincial level. If the person under investigation absconds and cannot be brought to justice after one year of wanted notice, or dies, the supervisory authority shall request the People's Procuratorate to apply to the People's Court for the confiscation of illegal gains in accordance with legal procedures.

Article 49: If a supervisee disagrees with a decision made by the supervisory authority concerning them, they may apply for review to the supervisory authority that made the decision within one month of receiving the decision. The review authority shall make a review decision within one month. If the supervisee still disagrees with the review decision, they may apply for reconsideration to the supervisory authority at the next higher level within one month of receiving the review decision. The reconsideration authority shall make a reconsideration decision within two months. During the review and reconsideration period, the execution of the original decision shall not be suspended. If the reconsideration authority determines that the original decision is erroneous, the original decision-making authority shall promptly correct it.

## Chapter 6: International Cooperation Against Corruption

Article 50: The National Commission of Supervision shall coordinate international exchanges and cooperation against corruption with other countries, regions, and international organizations, and organize the implementation of international anti-corruption treaties.

Article 51: The National Commission of Supervision shall organize and coordinate relevant parties to strengthen cooperation with relevant countries, regions, and international organizations in areas such as anti-corruption law enforcement, extradition, judicial assistance, transfer of sentenced persons, asset recovery, and information exchange.

Article 52: The National Commission of Supervision shall strengthen the organization and coordination of international fugitive pursuit, asset recovery, and prevention of flight, and urge relevant units to carry out the following work:

(1) For major cases of duty-related crimes such as embezzlement, bribery, dereliction of duty, or malfeasance, if the person under investigation has fled abroad and the evidence is relatively conclusive, pursue and apprehend them through international fugitive pursuit cooperation;

(2) Request the country where the illicit assets and proceeds are located to inquire, freeze, seize, confiscate, recover, and return the assets involved;

(3) Inquire into and monitor the entry and exit of public officials suspected of duty-related crimes and their related persons, as well as cross-border capital flows, and establish flight prevention procedures during case investigation.

## Chapter 7: Supervision of Supervisory Authorities and Personnel

Article 53: Supervisory commissions at all levels shall accept supervision from the People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at the corresponding levels.

People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at all levels shall hear and deliberate special work reports from the supervisory commissions at the corresponding levels and organize law enforcement inspections.

When the People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at or above the county level hold meetings, deputies to the People's Congresses or members of the Standing Committees may, in accordance with legal procedures, raise inquiries or interpellations on relevant issues in supervisory work.

Article 54: Supervisory authorities shall publicly disclose supervisory work information in accordance with the law and accept democratic supervision, social supervision, and media supervision.

Article 55: Supervisory authorities shall strengthen supervision over supervisory personnel's performance of duties and compliance with laws by establishing internal specialized supervisory bodies, and build a supervisory team that is loyal, clean, and responsible.

Article 56: Supervisory personnel must faithfully abide by the Constitution and laws, be loyal to their duties, enforce the law impartially, be honest and clean, and maintain confidentiality; they must possess good political qualities, be familiar with supervisory work, and have the ability to apply laws, regulations, policies, and conduct investigations and evidence collection, and consciously accept supervision.

Article 57: If supervisory personnel inquire about case details, intervene in cases, or make pleas for leniency, the supervisory personnel handling the matter shall promptly report it. The relevant circumstances shall be registered and filed.

If it is discovered that supervisory personnel handling a supervisory matter have contacted the person under investigation, involved persons, or their specific related persons without authorization, or have engaged in interactions, the person aware of the situation shall promptly report it. The relevant circumstances shall be registered and filed.

Article 58: Supervisory personnel handling a supervisory matter shall recuse themselves in any of the following circumstances, and the supervisee, whistleblower, and other relevant persons also have the right to request their recusal:

(1) Being a close relative of the supervisee or whistleblower;

(2) Having served as a witness in the case;

(3) Having a personal interest or a close relative's interest in the supervisory matter being handled;

(4) Other circumstances that may affect the fair handling of the supervisory matter.

Article 59: After leaving their posts or resigning, supervisory personnel with access to classified information shall comply with the management regulations on the declassification period, strictly fulfill confidentiality obligations, and shall not disclose relevant secrets.

Within three years of resignation or retirement, supervisory personnel shall not engage in occupations related to supervisory and judicial work that may create conflicts of interest.

Article 60: If a supervisory authority or its personnel engage in any of the following acts, the person under investigation or their close relatives have the right to file a complaint with the authority:

(1) Failing to lift compulsory detention measures after the legally prescribed period expires;

(2) Seizing, impounding, or freezing property unrelated to the case;

(3) Failing to lift seizure, impoundment, or freezing measures when they should be lifted;

(4) Embezzling, misappropriating, privately dividing, swapping, or improperly using seized, impounded, or frozen property;

(5) Other violations of laws and regulations that infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of the person under investigation.

The supervisory authority receiving the complaint shall make a decision within one month of receiving the complaint. If the complainant disagrees with the decision, they may apply for review to the supervisory authority at the next higher level within one month of receiving the decision. The higher-level supervisory authority shall make a decision within two months of receiving the review application, and if the situation is verified, promptly correct it.

Article 61: If, after the investigation is concluded, it is discovered that the basis for filing the case was insufficient or untrue, there were major errors in case handling, or supervisory personnel committed serious violations, the leading officials and directly responsible personnel shall be held accountable.

## Chapter 8: Legal Liability

Article 62: If a relevant unit refuses to execute a decision made by a supervisory authority or refuses to adopt a supervisory recommendation without justifiable reasons, its competent department or higher-level authority shall order correction and circulate a notice of criticism; the leading officials and directly responsible personnel shall be dealt with according to law.

Article 63: If relevant personnel violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, their unit, competent department, higher-level authority, or the supervisory authority shall order correction and deal with them according to law:

(1) Failing to provide relevant materials as required, refusing or obstructing investigation measures, or otherwise refusing to cooperate with the supervisory authority's investigation;

(2) Providing false information or concealing the truth;

(3) Colluding to provide consistent testimony, or forging, concealing, or destroying evidence;

(4) Preventing others from exposing, reporting, or providing evidence;

(5) Other violations of the provisions of this Law, where the circumstances are serious.

Article 64: If a supervisee retaliates against an accuser, whistleblower, witness, or supervisory personnel; or if an accuser, whistleblower, or witness fabricates facts to falsely accuse a supervisee, they shall be dealt with according to law.

Article 65: If a supervisory authority or its personnel engage in any of the following acts, the leading officials and directly responsible personnel shall be dealt with according to law:

(1) Disposing of problem clues without authorization, concealing major case developments without reporting, or privately retaining or handling case materials;

(2) Using their position or influence to interfere with investigations or seeking personal gain through case handling;

(3) Illegally stealing or leaking investigation information, or leaking reporting matters, report acceptance status, or whistleblower information;

(4) Forcing confessions or inducing confessions from the person under investigation or involved persons, or insulting, beating, abusing, physically punishing, or imposing disguised physical punishment;

(5) Improperly disposing of seized, impounded, or frozen property;

(6) Causing safety incidents during case handling in violation of regulations, or concealing, misreporting, or improperly handling safety incidents after they occur;

(7) Taking compulsory detention measures in violation of regulations;

(8) Unlawfully restricting others from leaving the country, or failing to lift exit restrictions as required;

(9) Other acts of abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 66: Violations of the provisions of this Law that constitute a crime shall be subject to criminal liability according to law.

Article 67: If a supervisory authority or its personnel, in exercising their powers, infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations, causing damage, they shall provide state compensation according to law.

## Chapter 9: Supplementary Provisions

Article 68: The Central Military Commission shall formulate specific regulations based on this Law for the supervisory work of the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force.

Article 69: This Law shall come into effect on the date of its promulgation. The Administrative Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China shall be simultaneously repealed.
