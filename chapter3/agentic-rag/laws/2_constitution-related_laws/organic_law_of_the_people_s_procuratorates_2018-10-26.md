# Organic Law of the People's Procuratorates of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on July 1, 1979

First amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Organic Law of the People's Procuratorates of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Second Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 2, 1983

Second amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Organic Law of the Local People's Congresses and Local People's Governments of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on December 2, 1986

Revision adopted at the Sixth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate the establishment, organization, and functions and powers of the People's Procuratorates, and to ensure that the People's Procuratorates perform their duties according to law.

Article 2 The People's Procuratorates are the state organs for legal supervision.

The People's Procuratorates, by exercising procuratorial power, prosecute crimes, safeguard national security and social order, protect the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations, protect national interests and social public interests, ensure the correct implementation of laws, uphold social fairness and justice, maintain the unity, dignity, and authority of the national legal system, and ensure the smooth progress of the construction of socialism with Chinese characteristics.

Article 3 The People's Procuratorates are established in accordance with the Constitution, laws, and decisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 4 The People's Procuratorates shall independently exercise their procuratorial power according to law, free from interference by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 5 In exercising procuratorial power, the People's Procuratorates shall apply the law equally to all; no organization or individual shall be privileged beyond the law, and any form of discrimination is prohibited.

Article 6 The People's Procuratorates shall uphold judicial fairness, base their actions on facts and take law as the criterion, comply with legal procedures, and respect and protect human rights.

Article 7 The People's Procuratorates shall practice judicial openness, except as otherwise provided by law.

Article 8 The People's Procuratorates shall implement a judicial accountability system, establishing and improving a judicial power operation mechanism that unifies power and responsibility.

Article 9 The Supreme People's Procuratorate is responsible and reports its work to the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee. Local People's Procuratorates at various levels are responsible and report their work to the People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at the corresponding levels.

The People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at various levels shall supervise the work of the People's Procuratorates at the corresponding levels.

Article 10 The Supreme People's Procuratorate is the highest procuratorial organ.

The Supreme People's Procuratorate directs the work of local People's Procuratorates at various levels and special People's Procuratorates; a People's Procuratorate at a higher level directs the work of those at lower levels.

Article 11 The People's Procuratorates shall accept supervision by the people, ensuring that the people enjoy the right to be informed, the right to participate, and the right to supervise the work of the People's Procuratorates according to law.

## Chapter II Establishment and Functions and Powers of the People's Procuratorates

Article 12 The People's Procuratorates are divided into:

(1) the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

(2) local People's Procuratorates at various levels; and

(3) special People's Procuratorates, such as military procuratorates.

Article 13 Local People's Procuratorates at various levels are divided into:

(1) provincial-level People's Procuratorates, including the People's Procuratorates of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government;

(2) People's Procuratorates at the level of a city divided into districts, including the People's Procuratorates of cities under the jurisdiction of provinces or autonomous regions, the People's Procuratorates of autonomous prefectures, and the branch procuratorates of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government; and

(3) primary-level People's Procuratorates, including the People's Procuratorates of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts.

Article 14 The organization, case jurisdiction, and appointment and removal of procurators of the People's Procuratorates established in the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 15 The establishment, organization, functions and powers, and appointment and removal of procurators of special People's Procuratorates shall be prescribed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 16 Provincial-level People's Procuratorates and People's Procuratorates at the level of a city divided into districts may, with the consent of the Supreme People's Procuratorate and relevant provincial-level departments, and upon approval by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, establish People's Procuratorates in specific areas within their jurisdiction as dispatched agencies, as required by procuratorial work.

Article 17 People's Procuratorates may, as required by procuratorial work, establish procuratorial offices in prisons, detention centers, and other places to exercise part of the functions and powers of the People's Procuratorate that dispatched them, and may also conduct circuit procuratorial work in the aforementioned places.

A provincial-level People's Procuratorate shall obtain the consent of the Supreme People's Procuratorate and relevant provincial-level departments before establishing a procuratorial office. A People's Procuratorate at the level of a city divided into districts or a primary-level People's Procuratorate shall obtain the consent of the provincial-level People's Procuratorate and relevant provincial-level departments before establishing a procuratorial office.

Article 18 People's Procuratorates shall establish necessary operational departments as required by procuratorial work. People's Procuratorates at the level of a city divided into districts and primary-level People's Procuratorates with a smaller number of procurator posts may establish comprehensive operational departments.

Article 19 People's Procuratorates may, as required by work, establish necessary procuratorial support departments and administrative management departments.

Article 20 The People's Procuratorates exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) exercising the power of investigation over relevant criminal cases according to law;

(2) reviewing criminal cases, and approving or deciding whether to arrest criminal suspects;

(3) reviewing criminal cases, deciding whether to initiate public prosecution, and supporting public prosecution in cases where a decision to initiate public prosecution has been made;

(4) initiating public interest litigation according to law;

(5) exercising legal supervision over litigation activities;

(6) exercising legal supervision over the execution of effective legal documents such as judgments and rulings;

(7) exercising legal supervision over the law enforcement activities of prisons and detention centers; and

(8) other functions and powers as provided by law.

Article 21 When exercising the functions and powers of legal supervision as provided in Article 20 of this Law, the People's Procuratorates may conduct investigations and verifications, and may, according to law, lodge protests, issue opinions on corrections, or issue procuratorial recommendations. The relevant units shall cooperate and shall, in a timely manner, provide a written reply to the People's Procuratorate regarding the adoption of the opinions on corrections or procuratorial recommendations.

The scope of application and procedures for protests, opinions on corrections, and procuratorial recommendations shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the law.

Article 22 The Supreme People's Procuratorate shall supervise the death penalty review activities of the Supreme People's Court; it shall review cases submitted for approval of prosecution and decide whether to initiate prosecution.

Article 23 The Supreme People's Procuratorate may interpret issues concerning the specific application of laws in procuratorial work.

The Supreme People's Procuratorate may issue guiding cases.

Article 24 A People's Procuratorate at a higher level exercises the following functions and powers over a People's Procuratorate at a lower level:

(1) if it considers a decision of the lower-level People's Procuratorate to be erroneous, it may instruct the lower-level People's Procuratorate to correct it, or revoke or change it according to law;

(2) it may designate the jurisdiction over cases under the jurisdiction of the lower-level People's Procuratorate;

(3) it may handle cases under the jurisdiction of the lower-level People's Procuratorate; and

(4) it may uniformly deploy procuratorial personnel within its jurisdiction to handle cases.

Decisions of a People's Procuratorate at a higher level shall be made in writing.

Article 25 A People's Procuratorate at a lower level shall implement the decisions of the People's Procuratorate at a higher level; if it has different opinions, it may report them to the higher-level People's Procuratorate while implementing the decisions.

Article 26 The Chief Procurator of a People's Procuratorate, or a Deputy Chief Procurator authorized by the Chief Procurator, may attend the meetings of the Judicial Committee of the People's Court at the corresponding level as a non-voting participant.

Article 27 People's Supervisors shall, in accordance with regulations, supervise the case-handling activities of the People's Procuratorates.

## Chapter III Case-Handling Organizations of the People's Procuratorates

Article 28 When handling cases, the People's Procuratorates may have a single procurator handle the case independently, or may form a case-handling group composed of two or more procurators to handle the case.

If a case is handled by a procurator case-handling group, the Chief Procurator shall designate one procurator as the lead procurator to organize and direct the case-handling group in handling the case.

Article 29 Procurators shall carry out their work under the leadership of the Chief Procurator, and major case-handling matters shall be decided by the Chief Procurator. The Chief Procurator may delegate some of his or her functions and powers to procurators and may authorize procurators to issue legal documents.

Article 30 People's Procuratorates at various levels shall establish a Procuratorial Committee. The Procuratorial Committee shall be composed of the Chief Procurator, Deputy Chief Procurators, and a number of senior procurators, and the number of its members shall be an odd number.

Article 31 The Procuratorial Committee performs the following functions:

(1) summarizing experience in procuratorial work;

(2) discussing and deciding on major, difficult, and complex cases; and

(3) discussing and deciding on other major issues concerning procuratorial work.

Interpretations by the Supreme People's Procuratorate on issues concerning the specific application of laws in procuratorial work and the issuance of guiding cases shall be discussed and adopted by the Procuratorial Committee.

Article 32 A meeting of the Procuratorial Committee shall be held only when more than half of its members are present.

Meetings of the Procuratorial Committee shall be presided over by the Chief Procurator or a Deputy Chief Procurator authorized by the Chief Procurator. The Procuratorial Committee shall practice democratic centralism.

If the Chief Procurator of a local People's Procuratorate at any level disagrees with the opinion of the majority of the members of the Procuratorial Committee of that People's Procuratorate, and the matter concerns case handling, the matter may be submitted to the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level for a decision; if the matter concerns a major issue, it may be submitted to the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level or the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for a decision.

Article 33 Procurators may, with respect to major cases and other major issues, request the Chief Procurator to make a decision. The Chief Procurator may, based on the circumstances of the case, submit the matter to the Procuratorial Committee for discussion and decision.

When the Procuratorial Committee discusses a case, the procurator is responsible for the facts reported, and the members of the Procuratorial Committee are responsible for the opinions they express and the votes they cast. Procurators shall implement the decisions of the Procuratorial Committee.

Article 34 The People's Procuratorates shall implement a system of procurator case-handling responsibility. Procurators are responsible for the decisions they make on cases within the scope of their functions and powers. The Chief Procurator and the Procuratorial Committee shall bear corresponding responsibility for decisions made on cases.

## Chapter IV Composition of Personnel of the People's Procuratorates

Article 35 The procuratorial personnel of the People's Procuratorates shall be composed of the Chief Procurator, Deputy Chief Procurators, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and procurators.

Article 36 The Chief Procurator of a People's Procuratorate shall lead the procuratorial work of that People's Procuratorate and manage its administrative affairs. The Deputy Chief Procurators of a People's Procuratorate shall assist the Chief Procurator in his or her work.

Article 37 The Chief Procurator of the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be elected and removed by the National People's Congress, and the Deputy Chief Procurators, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and procurators shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the Chief Procurator.

Article 38 The Chief Procurator of a local People's Procuratorate at any level shall be elected and removed by the People's Congress at the corresponding level, and the Deputy Chief Procurators, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and procurators shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level upon the recommendation of the Chief Procurator.

The appointment and removal of the Chief Procurator of a local People's Procuratorate at any level shall be reported to the Chief Procurator of the People's Procuratorate at the next higher level, who shall submit it to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for approval.

The Chief Procurator, Deputy Chief Procurators, members of the Procuratorial Committee, and procurators of a branch procuratorate of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government upon the recommendation of the Chief Procurator of the People's Procuratorate of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 39 The term of office of the Chief Procurator of a People's Procuratorate shall be the same as the term of office of the People's Congress that elects him or her.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, upon the recommendation of the Chief Procurator of the People's Procuratorate at the corresponding level, remove the Chief Procurator, Deputy Chief Procurators, and members of the Procuratorial Committee of a People's Procuratorate at a lower level.

Article 40 The procurators, procuratorial support personnel, and judicial administrative personnel of the People's Procuratorates shall be subject to classified management.

Article 41 Procurators shall be subject to a post system. The number of procurator posts shall be determined based on factors such as the number of cases, economic and social development, population size, and the level of the People's Procuratorate.

The number of procurator posts in the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be determined by the Supreme People's Procuratorate in consultation with relevant departments. The number of procurator posts in local People's Procuratorates at various levels shall be subject to total control and dynamic management within provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 42 Procurators shall be selected and appointed from among persons who have obtained legal professional qualifications and meet other conditions prescribed by law. The professional competence of first-time procurators shall be reviewed by a procurator selection committee. Procurators of People's Procuratorates at higher levels shall generally be selected and appointed from among procurators of People's Procuratorates at lower levels through a competitive process.

The Chief Procurator shall have professional legal knowledge and legal professional experience. Deputy Chief Procurators and members of the Procuratorial Committee shall be selected from among procurators, judges, or other persons who meet the conditions for being a procurator or judge.

The duties, management, and guarantees of procurators shall be governed by the provisions of the Procurators Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 43 Procurator assistants of the People's Procuratorates shall, under the guidance of procurators, be responsible for procuratorial support tasks such as reviewing case materials and drafting legal documents.

Procurator assistants who meet the conditions for appointment as procurators may, after selection, be appointed as procurators in accordance with the procedures for the appointment and removal of procurators.

Article 44 Clerks of the People's Procuratorates shall be responsible for procuratorial support tasks such as case recording.

Article 45 Judicial police of the People's Procuratorates shall be responsible for police matters such as guarding case-handling premises, escorting and guarding persons.

Judicial police shall be managed in accordance with the People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 46 People's Procuratorates may, as required by procuratorial work, appoint procuratorial technical personnel to be responsible for matters related to procuratorial work.

## Chapter V Guarantees for the Exercise of Functions and Powers by the People's Procuratorates

Article 47 No unit or individual may require a procurator to engage in matters beyond the scope of his or her statutory duties.

If leading cadres or others interfere with judicial activities or meddle in the handling of specific cases, or if internal personnel of the People's Procuratorate inquire about case circumstances, the case-handling personnel shall fully and truthfully record and report such matters; if there are violations of law or discipline, the relevant authorities shall pursue the responsibility of the actor based on the severity of the circumstances.

Article 48 The People's Procuratorates shall take necessary measures to ensure the safety of case handling. Illegal or criminal acts that hinder the People's Procuratorates from exercising their functions and powers according to law shall be subject to legal liability according to law.

Article 49 The People's Procuratorates shall implement a training system, and procurators, procuratorial support personnel, and judicial administrative personnel shall receive theoretical and professional training.

Article 50 The staffing of the People's Procuratorates shall be subject to special management.

Article 51 The funds of the People's Procuratorates shall be included in the fiscal budget in accordance with the principle of division of powers and responsibilities, to ensure the needs of procuratorial work.

Article 52 The People's Procuratorates shall strengthen information technology development, utilizing modern information technology to promote judicial openness and improve work efficiency.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 53 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2019.
