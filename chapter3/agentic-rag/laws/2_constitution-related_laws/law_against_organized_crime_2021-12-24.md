# People's Republic of China Organized Crime Prevention and Control Law

Adopted at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

Effective as of May 1, 2022

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to prevent and punish organized crime, strengthen and regulate the work against organized crime, safeguard national security, social order, and economic order, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and organizations.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "organized crime" refers to the crimes of organizing, leading, or participating in a mafia-type organization as stipulated in Article 294 of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China, as well as crimes committed by mafia-type organizations or criminal groups of an evil nature.

For the purposes of this Law, "criminal group of an evil nature" refers to a criminal organization that is often assembled together, repeatedly committing illegal and criminal activities in a certain region or industry through violence, threats, or other means, acting tyrannically, oppressing the masses, disrupting social order and economic order, causing relatively severe social impact, but which has not yet formed a mafia-type organization.

This Law applies to overseas mafia-type organizations that recruit members or commit crimes within the territory of the People's Republic of China, as well as crimes committed outside the territory against the People's Republic of China or its citizens.

**Article 3** The work against organized crime shall adhere to a holistic approach to national security, comprehensively utilize legal, economic, technological, cultural, and educational means, and establish and improve the working mechanism for combating organized crime and the system for preventing and controlling organized crime.

**Article 4** The work against organized crime shall adhere to the combination of specialized work with the mass line, the combination of special campaigns with systematic governance, the combination with the fight against corruption, and the combination with strengthening the construction of grassroots organizations, taking both punishment and prevention measures and addressing both symptoms and root causes.

**Article 5** The work against organized crime shall be carried out in accordance with the law, respecting and safeguarding human rights, and protecting the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and organizations.

**Article 6** Supervisory organs, people's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, judicial administrative organs, and other relevant state organs shall, according to their respective functions, cooperate with and check each other, and properly carry out the work against organized crime in accordance with the law.

Relevant departments shall mobilize and rely on villagers' committees, residents' committees, enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations to jointly carry out the work against organized crime.

**Article 7** All units and individuals have the obligation to assist and cooperate with relevant departments in carrying out the work against organized crime.

The state shall, in accordance with the law, protect units and individuals that assist and cooperate in the work against organized crime.

**Article 8** The state encourages units and individuals to report organized crime.

Units and individuals that report organized crime or make outstanding contributions in the work against organized crime shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Prevention and Control

**Article 9** People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, organize the prevention and control of organized crime and incorporate such work into the evaluation system.

Villagers' committees and residents' committees shall assist people's governments and relevant departments in carrying out the prevention and control of organized crime.

**Article 10** Departments responsible for the prevention and control of organized crime shall carry out publicity and education on combating organized crime to enhance citizens' awareness and ability to combat organized crime.

Supervisory organs, people's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, and judicial administrative organs shall carry out publicity and education on combating organized crime through legal popularization, case interpretation, and other means.

News, radio, television, culture, internet information services, and other units shall carry out targeted publicity and education on combating organized crime for the public.

**Article 11** Education administrative departments and schools shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, establish a working mechanism to prevent organized crime from infringing on campuses, strengthen publicity and education on combating organized crime, enhance students' awareness of preventing organized crime, educate and guide students to consciously resist organized crime, and prevent infringement by organized crime.

If a school discovers that organized crime infringes on the personal or property safety of students, disrupts campus and surrounding order, that an organized crime organization is recruiting members among students, or that students are participating in organized crime activities, it shall promptly stop it, take preventive measures, and report to the public security organ and the education administrative department.

**Article 12** Civil affairs departments shall, in conjunction with supervisory organs, public security organs, and other relevant departments, review the qualifications of candidates for members of villagers' committees and residents' committees. If it is found that a candidate has been criminally punished for committing organized crime, the matter shall be promptly handled in accordance with relevant regulations; if clues of organized crime are found, they shall be promptly reported to the public security organ.

**Article 13** Market regulation, financial supervision, natural resources, transportation, and other competent industry authorities shall, in conjunction with public security organs, establish a long-term mechanism for the prevention and control of organized crime in their respective industries, monitor and analyze the situation of organized crime in relevant industries and fields, and strengthen supervision and management of industries and fields prone to organized crime.

**Article 14** If supervisory organs, people's courts, people's procuratorates, or public security organs discover problems in the prevention and control of organized crime by competent industry authorities during case handling, they may submit written opinions and suggestions to the relevant competent industry authorities. The relevant competent industry authorities shall promptly handle the matter and provide written feedback.

**Article 15** Public security organs may, in conjunction with relevant departments, determine key areas, industries, or venues for prevention and control based on the local situation of organized crime.

Management units of key areas, industries, or venues shall take effective measures to strengthen management and promptly report their work situation to the public security organ.

**Article 16** Telecommunications business operators and internet service providers shall, in accordance with the law, fulfill their obligations for network information security management, adopt security technical prevention measures to prevent the dissemination of information that promotes or induces organized crime; if they discover information that promotes or induces organized crime, they shall immediately stop transmission, take measures such as removal, preserve relevant records, and report to the public security organ or relevant departments, and provide technical support and assistance to public security organs in investigating organized crime in accordance with the law.

The competent authorities for cyberspace administration, telecommunications, and public security shall, according to their respective functions, promptly order relevant units to stop transmission, take measures such as removal, or remove relevant applications, close relevant websites, or shut down relevant services regarding information that promotes or induces organized crime. Relevant units shall immediately execute such orders, preserve relevant records, and assist in the investigation. For the aforementioned information originating from abroad on the internet, the competent telecommunications authority shall take technical measures to promptly block its dissemination.

**Article 17** The anti-money laundering administrative department of the State Council and other relevant departments and institutions of the State Council shall urge financial institutions and specific non-financial institutions to fulfill their anti-money laundering obligations. If suspicious transaction activities related to organized crime are discovered, the relevant competent authorities may investigate in accordance with the law; if the suspicion of money laundering cannot be ruled out after investigation, they shall promptly report to the public security organ.

**Article 18** Prisons, detention centers, and community correction institutions shall adopt targeted supervision, education, and correction measures for offenders of organized crime.

After offenders of organized crime are released from prison upon completion of their sentence, the judicial administrative organs shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, implement necessary measures such as resettlement and assistance to facilitate their smooth reintegration into society.

**Article 19** For persons sentenced to punishment for organizing or leading a mafia-type organization, the public security organ at or above the level of a city divided into districts may decide that, from the date of completion of the execution of the sentence, they shall report their personal property and daily activities to the public security organ in accordance with relevant state regulations. The reporting period shall not exceed five years.

**Article 20** If an organizer or leader of a mafia-type organization or a principal offender of a criminal group of an evil nature who has been previously sentenced to punishment establishes a business or serves as a senior manager in a business, the relevant competent industry authority shall review the matter in accordance with the law and strengthen supervision and management of their business activities.

**Article 21** Immigration management, customs, coast guard, and other departments shall, in conjunction with public security organs, strictly prevent overseas mafia-type organizations from infiltrating, recruiting members, and committing illegal and criminal activities within the territory.

The authorities issuing entry-exit documents and immigration management agencies have the right to decide not to allow entry, not to issue entry documents, or to declare entry documents invalid for members of overseas mafia-type organizations.

If immigration management, customs, coast guard, and other departments discover that a member of an overseas mafia-type organization has entered the country, they shall promptly notify the public security organ. If they discover that relevant persons are suspected of violating Chinese laws or discover items suspected of being related to organized crime, they shall detain them in accordance with the law and handle them promptly.

## Chapter III Case Handling

**Article 22** Cases of organized crime shall be handled based on facts and in accordance with the law, adhering to the principle of combining leniency with severity.

For organizers, leaders, and core members of organized crime, the conditions for applying bail pending trial, non-prosecution, probation, commutation of sentence, parole, and temporary service of sentence outside prison shall be strictly controlled, and penalties such as deprivation of political rights, confiscation of property, and fines shall be fully applied.

If a criminal suspect or defendant of organized crime voluntarily and truthfully confesses their crimes, admits the criminal facts charged, and is willing to accept punishment, they may be dealt with leniently in accordance with the law.

**Article 23** Crimes committed using the internet that meet the provisions of Article 2 of this Law shall be deemed organized crime.

Acts such as organized harassment, pestering, creating disturbances, and gathering crowds to exert pressure, aimed at seeking illegal benefits or forming an illegal influence, creating psychological coercion on others, sufficient to restrict personal freedom, endanger personal and property safety, and affect normal social order and economic order, may be deemed as criminal means of organized crime.

**Article 24** Public security organs shall, in accordance with the law, utilize modern information technology to establish mechanisms for collecting and assessing clues of organized crime, and handle them by classification and level.

After receiving reports, accusations, or tip-offs regarding organized crime, public security organs shall promptly conduct statistics, analysis, and assessment, organize verification, or transfer the matter to the relevant competent authority for handling in accordance with the law.

**Article 25** If relevant state organs discover clues of organized crime while performing their duties, or receive reports of organized crime, they shall promptly transfer the matter to the public security organ or other competent authority for handling in accordance with the law.

**Article 26** When verifying clues of organized crime, public security organs may adopt investigation measures in accordance with relevant state regulations. When public security organs collect and obtain relevant information and materials from relevant units and individuals, such units and individuals shall truthfully provide them.

**Article 27** When verifying clues of organized crime, public security organs may, with the approval of the person in charge of the public security organ at or above the county level, inquire about the deposit, remittance, bonds, stocks, fund shares, and other property information of the suspected persons.

When verifying clues of mafia-type organization crimes, if there is an urgent risk that the property involved may be destroyed or transferred, the public security organ may, with the approval of the person in charge of the public security organ at or above the level of a city divided into districts, take emergency measures such as emergency payment suspension, temporary freezing, or temporary seizure of the relevant property involved, for a period not exceeding forty-eight hours. Upon expiration of the period or when the circumstances justifying the emergency measures cease to exist, the emergency measures shall be immediately lifted.

**Article 28** If, after verifying clues of organized crime, a public security organ discovers criminal facts or criminal suspects, it shall file a case for investigation in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 29** When handling cases of organized crime, public security organs may, in accordance with the provisions of the Exit and Entry Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, decide to impose exit restrictions on criminal suspects and notify immigration management agencies for execution.

**Article 30** For criminal suspects and defendants in organized crime cases, measures such as detention in a different location, separate detention, or solitary detention may be taken as needed for case handling and maintaining supervision order. If measures of detention in a different location are taken, the family members and defense lawyers of the criminal suspect or defendant shall be notified in accordance with the law.

**Article 31** After filing a case, public security organs may, as needed for investigating the crime and in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, adopt technical investigation measures, implement controlled delivery, or conduct investigations with personnel concealing their identities.

**Article 32** If a criminal suspect or defendant reports or exposes other co-offenders of a major crime or provides important clues or evidence for solving a major case, and joint handling may pose a personal danger to themselves or their close relatives, the cases may be handled separately.

**Article 33** If a criminal suspect or defendant actively cooperates with the investigation, prosecution, and trial of an organized crime case and falls under any of the following circumstances, they may be dealt with leniently in accordance with the law, but this shall be strictly applied to the organizers and leaders of the organized crime:

(1) Providing important clues or evidence for ascertaining the organizational structure of the criminal organization and the status and role of its organizers, leaders, or principal offenders;

(2) Providing important clues or evidence for solving major crimes committed by the criminal organization;

(3) Providing important clues or evidence for investigating state functionaries involved in organized crime;

(4) Assisting in the recovery and confiscation of illicit money and property not yet seized;

(5) Other circumstances of providing important clues or evidence for investigating organized crime cases.

If a criminal suspect or defendant who participated in an organized crime organization is not prosecuted or is exempted from criminal punishment, they may, depending on the circumstances of the case, be subjected to admonishment, ordered to make a statement of repentance, apologize, compensate for losses, or be subjected to administrative penalties or sanctions by the competent authority in accordance with the law.

**Article 34** For organizers and leaders of mafia-type organizations, confiscation of property shall be imposed concurrently in accordance with the law. For other members of the organization, depending on their status and role in the criminal organization, as well as the frequency, nature, amount of illegal gains, and losses caused by their participation in illegal and criminal activities, fines or confiscation of property may be imposed concurrently in accordance with the law.

**Article 35** For offenders of organized crime, the executing organ shall strictly manage them in accordance with the law.

If an organizer or leader of a mafia-type organization or a principal offender of a criminal group of an evil nature is sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years, life imprisonment, or death penalty with a two-year reprieve, the sentence shall be executed in a different province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 36** For commutation of sentence of an organizer or leader of a mafia-type organization or a principal offender of a criminal group of an evil nature sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not less than ten years, life imprisonment, or death penalty with a two-year reprieve, the executing organ shall submit a proposal for commutation in accordance with the law, which shall be submitted to the people's court for a ruling after review by the prison administration authority of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

For parole of an organizer or leader of a mafia-type organization or a principal offender of a criminal group of an evil nature, the procedure stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall apply.

**Article 37** When a people's court hears a case concerning commutation of sentence or parole of an offender of a mafia-type organization crime, it shall notify the people's procuratorate and the executing organ to participate in the hearing, and notify the offender for whom commutation or parole is requested to participate and hear their opinions.

**Article 38** When an executing organ submits a proposal for commutation or parole, and when a people's court hears a case concerning commutation or parole, full consideration shall be given to the offender's fulfillment of property-related judgments in effective judgments and cooperation in the disposal of property involved in the case.

## Chapter IV Identification and Disposal of Property Involved in the Case

**Article 39** Various properties and documents discovered during the handling of organized crime cases that can be used to prove the guilt or innocence of a criminal suspect or defendant shall be sealed up or seized in accordance with the law.

Public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts may, in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, inquire about and freeze the deposits, remittances, bonds, stocks, fund shares, and other property of criminal suspects or defendants. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

**Article 40** Public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts may, as needed for handling organized crime cases, comprehensively investigate the property status of the organization suspected of organized crime and its members.

**Article 41** Sealing up, seizure, freezing, and disposal of property involved in a case shall be carried out strictly in accordance with statutory conditions and procedures, legally protecting the legitimate property rights and interests of citizens and organizations, strictly distinguishing between illegal gains and legitimate property, and between the property of the person concerned and that of their family members, and minimizing adverse effects on the normal business activities of enterprises. Property unrelated to the case shall not be sealed up, seized, or frozen. Property confirmed to be genuinely unrelated to the case shall be released from sealing up, seizure, or freezing and returned within three days. Legitimate property of victims shall be promptly returned.

When sealing up, seizing, or freezing property involved in a case, necessary living expenses and items shall be reserved for the criminal suspect or defendant and their dependents.

**Article 42** Public security organs may inquire with the anti-money laundering administrative department for information and data related to organized crime, and request assistance in investigating suspicious transaction activities related to organized crime. The anti-money laundering administrative department shall cooperate and respond promptly.

**Article 43** With the approval of the principal person in charge of the public security organ, people's procuratorate, or people's court at or above the county level, the following property may be sold, realized, or auctioned in advance in accordance with the law, with the proceeds kept by the seizure or freezing authority, and the criminal suspect, defendant, or their close relatives shall be promptly notified:

(1) Items that are easily damaged, lost, deteriorated, or otherwise unsuitable for long-term storage;

(2) Drafts, promissory notes, checks, etc., whose validity period is about to expire;

(3) Property such as bonds, stocks, and fund shares, where the sale, upon application by the right holder, does not harm the interests of the state or the victim, and does not affect the normal progress of the proceedings.

**Article 44** Public security organs and people's procuratorates shall examine and screen the property involved in the case. When transferring a case for review and prosecution or initiating a public prosecution, they shall propose opinions on the handling of the property involved.

During the trial of an organized crime case, court investigation and debate shall be conducted on the facts and evidence related to the nature and ownership of the property involved. The people's court shall render a judgment in accordance with the law and dispose of the property involved.

**Article 45** All property, fruits, and proceeds obtained illegally by the organized crime organization and its members, as well as contraband and property owned by the person concerned used for the crime, shall be recovered, confiscated, or ordered to be returned or compensated in accordance with the law.

If the property involved that should be recovered or confiscated in accordance with the law cannot be found, has been destroyed, or is mixed with other legitimate property and cannot be separated, other property of equivalent value or the equivalent portion of the mixed property may be recovered or confiscated.

If the facts for conviction and sentencing of a defendant for a mafia-type organization crime have been ascertained, and there is evidence that the property obtained by the defendant during the criminal period is highly likely to be illegal gains, fruits, and proceeds from the mafia-type organization crime, and the defendant cannot explain the legal source of the property, it shall be recovered or confiscated in accordance with the law.

**Article 46** Property involved in a case that meets any of the following circumstances shall be recovered or confiscated in accordance with the law:

(1) Property provided to the organized crime organization and its members to support or fund organized crime activities;

(2) The portion of the family property of a member of the organized crime organization that is actually used to support organized crime activities;

(3) Property obtained through the illegal and criminal activities of the organized crime organization and its members, as well as its fruits and proceeds.**Article 47** If a suspect or defendant in an organized crime case of a mafia-style nature absconds and cannot be apprehended after one year of being wanted, or if the suspect or defendant dies, and according to the provisions of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China, their illegal gains and other involved property should be recovered, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the procedures for the confiscation of illegal gains in cases where the suspect or defendant has absconded or died, as stipulated in the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.
**Article 48** Supervisory organs, public security organs, and people's procuratorates shall, in accordance with the law, investigate and handle money laundering and crimes of concealing or disguising criminal proceeds and the benefits derived therefrom that are related to organized crime.

**Article 49** If an interested party raises an objection to the sealing, seizure, freezing, or disposal of property involved in a case, the public security organ, people's procuratorate, or people's court shall promptly verify the matter, hear their opinion, and handle it according to law.
After the public security organ, people's procuratorate, or people's court has disposed of the property involved in the case, if the interested party is dissatisfied with the handling, they may file a complaint or accusation.
## Chapter V Handling of State Functionaries Involved in Organized Crime

**Article 50** State functionaries who engage in any of the following acts shall be subject to a comprehensive investigation and handled according to law:

(1) Organizing, leading, or participating in organized criminal activities;
(2) Providing assistance to organized crime groups or their criminal activities;
(3) Harboring organized crime groups or conniving at organized criminal activities;
(4) Dereliction of duty or malfeasance in the investigation and handling of organized crime cases;
(5) Using their authority or the influence of their position to interfere with anti-organized crime work;
(6) Other illegal or criminal acts related to organized crime.

State functionaries who organize, lead, or participate in organized crime shall be given a heavier punishment according to law.

**Article 51** Supervisory organs, people's courts, people's procuratorates, public security organs, and judicial administrative organs shall strengthen coordination and cooperation, establish a communication mechanism for handling leads, and upon discovering leads that a state functionary is suspected of the illegal or criminal acts specified in Article 50 of this Law, shall handle them according to law or promptly transfer them to the competent authority for handling.

Any unit or individual that discovers illegal or criminal acts by a state functionary related to organized crime has the right to report, accuse, or inform the supervisory organ, people's procuratorate, public security organ, or other relevant departments. Upon receiving such reports, accusations, or information, the relevant departments shall handle them promptly.

**Article 52** State functionaries who investigate organized crime cases according to law or support or assist in such investigations by virtue of their duties shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Failing to accept reports, accusations, or information; concealing, failing to report truthfully, or without approval or authorization, disposing of or failing to transfer criminal leads or case materials;
(2) Leaking information to offenders to hinder case investigation;
(3) Handling cases contrary to facts and law;
(4) Violating regulations by sealing, seizing, freezing, or disposing of property involved in the case;(5) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duty, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

**Article 53** After receiving a report against law enforcement or judicial personnel engaged in anti-organized crime work, the relevant authorities shall handle it according to law, preventing suspects, defendants, etc., from using the report to interfere with case handling or retaliate.

Those who distort facts, fabricate stories, or make false accusations against law enforcement or judicial personnel engaged in anti-organized crime work through reports or other means shall be held accountable according to law; if it causes adverse effects, the facts shall be clarified promptly, their reputation restored, and the adverse effects eliminated according to regulations.

## Chapter VI International Cooperation

**Article 54** The People's Republic of China shall conduct anti-organized crime cooperation with other countries, regions, and international organizations based on international treaties it has concluded or acceded to, or on the principle of equality and mutual benefit.

**Article 55** Relevant departments of the State Council, authorized by the State Council, shall represent the Chinese government in conducting intelligence information exchange and law enforcement cooperation on anti-organized crime with foreign governments and relevant international organizations.

The public security department of the State Council shall strengthen cross-border police cooperation against organized crime and promote the establishment of police cooperation mechanisms with relevant countries and regions. With the approval of the public security department of the State Council, public security organs in border areas may establish mechanisms for cross-border intelligence information exchange and police cooperation on organized crime with law enforcement agencies of neighboring countries or regions.

**Article 56** Criminal judicial assistance and extradition involving organized crime shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws.

**Article 57** Materials obtained through international cooperation against organized crime may be used as evidence in administrative penalties and criminal proceedings, except where treaty provisions or our commitments stipulate that they shall not be used as evidence.

## Chapter VII Safeguard Measures

**Article 58** The State shall provide necessary organizational, institutional, and material guarantees for anti-organized crime work.

**Article 59** Public security organs and relevant departments shall, according to their duties, establish and improve specialized anti-organized crime forces, strengthen talent team building and professional training, and enhance the capability for anti-organized crime work.

**Article 60** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall, according to the division of powers, include the funding for anti-organized crime work in their respective fiscal budgets.

**Article 61** If the personal safety of a person who reports, accuses, or stops organized criminal activities, or testifies in an organized crime case, or that of their close relatives, is in danger, the public security organ, people's procuratorate, or people's court shall, according to relevant regulations, take one or more of the following protective measures:

(1) Not disclosing personal information such as real name, address, and work unit;
(2) Taking measures such as not revealing appearance or real voice when testifying in court;
(3) Prohibiting specific persons from contacting the protected person;
(4) Taking specialized protective measures for the person and their residence;
(5) Changing the protected person's identity and reassigning their residence and work unit;
(6) Other necessary protective measures.

**Article 62** The protective measures specified in items (3) and (4) of Article 61 of this Law shall be executed by the public security organs. Changing the identity of a protected person under item (5) of Article 61 of this Law shall be approved and organized by the public security department of the State Council.

When public security organs, people's procuratorates, or people's courts take protective measures according to law, relevant units and individuals shall cooperate.

**Article 63** Persons who have committed organized crime and cooperate with investigations, prosecutions, trials, etc., playing a significant role in solving the case or ascertaining the facts of the case, may be treated in accordance with the provisions on witness protection.

**Article 64** Protective measures such as personal protection and prohibiting specific persons from contacting may be taken for law enforcement and judicial personnel handling organized crime cases and their close relatives.

**Article 65** Persons who become disabled or die due to performing duties in anti-organized crime work or assisting or cooperating with relevant departments in such work shall receive corresponding benefits in accordance with national regulations.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

**Article 66** Those who organize, lead, or participate in a mafia-style organization; state organ functionaries who harbor or connive at a mafia-style organization; and those who commit crimes as part of a mafia-style organization or an evil force organization shall be investigated for criminal responsibility according to law.

Personnel from overseas mafia-style organizations who come to the territory of the People's Republic of China to develop members or commit crimes, or who commit crimes abroad against the State or citizens of the People's Republic of China, shall be investigated for criminal responsibility according to law.

**Article 67** Those who recruit minors to participate in a mafia-style organization or an overseas mafia-style organization, instigate or induce minors to commit organized crime, or commit organized crime that infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of minors shall be given a heavier criminal responsibility according to law.

**Article 68** For convicts of organized crime, the people's court may, in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China on occupational prohibitions, prohibit them from engaging in relevant occupations and notify the relevant industry competent authorities.
**Article 69** Anyone who falls under any of the following circumstances, which does not constitute a crime, shall be detained by the public security organ for not less than five days but not more than ten days, and may also be fined not more than 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are relatively serious, they shall be detained for not less than ten days but not more than fifteen days, and fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; any illegal gains, except those that should be returned to the victim according to law, shall be confiscated:

(1) Participating in an overseas mafia-style organization;
(2) Actively participating in an evil force organization;
(3) Instigating or inducing others to join an organized crime group, or preventing others from withdrawing from an organized crime group;
(4) Providing financial support, premises, or other support, assistance, or convenience for organized criminal activities;
(5) Preventing others from reporting or providing evidence of organized crime, or, knowing that another person has committed organized crime, refusing to provide information when judicial authorities investigate the relevant circumstances or collect relevant evidence.

Instigating or inducing minors to join an organized crime group or preventing minors from withdrawing from an organized crime group, which does not constitute a crime, shall be given a heavier punishment according to the preceding paragraph.

**Article 70** Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 19 of this Law by failing to truthfully report their personal property and daily activities as required by the public security organ's decision shall be warned by the public security organ and ordered to correct; if they refuse to correct, they shall be detained for not less than five days but not more than ten days and fined not more than 30,000 yuan.

**Article 71** If financial institutions or other relevant units fail to assist public security organs in taking emergency payment suspension or temporary freezing measures as required by Article 27 of this Law, the public security organ shall order them to correct; if they refuse to correct, the public security organ shall impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel; if the circumstances are serious, the public security organ may recommend that the relevant competent authority impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law.

**Article 72** If a telecommunications business operator or internet service provider falls under any of the following circumstances, the relevant competent authority shall order them to correct; if they refuse to correct or the circumstances are serious, the relevant competent authority shall impose penalties in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China:

(1) Refusing to provide technical support and assistance for the investigation of organized crime;
(2) Failing to stop the transmission of information containing content that promotes or induces organized crime, take measures such as elimination, or preserve relevant records as required by the competent authority.

**Article 73** If a relevant state organ or industry competent authority refuses to perform or delays the performance of statutory anti-organized crime duties, or refuses to cooperate with anti-organized crime investigations and evidence collection, or abuses measures related to anti-organized crime work in other tasks, the superior authority shall order them to correct; if the circumstances are serious, the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal responsibility shall be investigated according to law.

**Article 74** Relevant departments, units, and individuals shall keep confidential any state secrets, trade secrets, and personal privacy learned during the course of anti-organized crime work. Those who violate regulations by disclosing state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy shall be investigated for legal responsibility according to law.

**Article 75** State functionaries who engage in the acts specified in Articles 50 and 52 of this Law, which constitute a crime, shall be investigated for criminal responsibility according to law; if they do not constitute a crime, they shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 76** Relevant units and individuals who are dissatisfied with the decisions on administrative penalties and compulsory administrative measures made in accordance with this Law may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate administrative litigation according to law.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

**Article 77** This Law shall come into force on May 1, 2022.
