# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Procedure of Conclusion of Treaties

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on December 28, 1990

<!-- INFO END -->

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 2** This Law applies to bilateral and multilateral treaties, agreements, and other documents of a treaty or agreement nature concluded between the People's Republic of China and foreign states.

**Article 3** The State Council of the People's Republic of China, i.e., the Central People's Government, concludes treaties and agreements with foreign states.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China decides on the ratification and denunciation of treaties and important agreements concluded with foreign states.

The President of the People's Republic of China, in accordance with the decisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, ratifies and denounces treaties and important agreements concluded with foreign states.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China, under the leadership of the State Council, manages the specific affairs concerning the conclusion of treaties and agreements with foreign states.

**Article 4** The People's Republic of China concludes treaties and agreements with foreign states in the following names:

(1) The People's Republic of China;
(2) The Government of the People's Republic of China;
(3) The government departments of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 5** The decision-making procedures for negotiating and signing treaties and agreements are as follows:

(1) For negotiating and signing treaties and agreements in the name of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall submit proposals and draft the Chinese text of the treaties and agreements, and report to the State Council for examination and decision;
(2) For negotiating and signing treaties and agreements in the name of the Government of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall submit proposals and draft the Chinese text, or the relevant department of the State Council shall submit proposals and draft the Chinese text after consultation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and report to the State Council for examination and decision. For agreements concerning specific operational matters, with the consent of the State Council, the Chinese text of the agreement shall be examined and decided by the relevant department of the State Council, after consultation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs if necessary;
(3) For negotiating and signing agreements in the name of a government department of the People's Republic of China within the scope of its functions and powers, the decision shall be made by the department itself or after consultation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; if the matter involves major issues or falls within the functions and powers of other relevant departments of the State Council, the department shall, after consultation with those other departments, report to the State Council for decision. The Chinese text of the agreement shall be examined and decided by the department itself, after consultation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs if necessary.

If the Chinese text of a treaty or agreement, as examined and decided by the State Council, requires significant changes during negotiations, it shall be re-reported to the State Council for examination and decision.

**Article 6** Representatives for negotiating and signing treaties and agreements shall be appointed in accordance with the following procedures:

(1) For concluding treaties and agreements in the name of the People's Republic of China or the Government of the People's Republic of China, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council shall report to the State Council for the appointment of representatives. The full powers of the representatives shall be signed by the Premier of the State Council, or may be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs;
(2) For concluding agreements in the name of a government department of the People's Republic of China, the head of the department shall appoint the representatives. The authorization certificate of the representatives shall be signed by the head of the department. If the head of the department signs an agreement concluded in the name of the department, and the parties agree that a full powers certificate is required, the full powers certificate shall be signed by the Premier of the State Council, or may be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

The following persons are not required to produce full powers certificates when negotiating and signing treaties and agreements:

(1) The Premier of the State Council and the Minister of Foreign Affairs;
(2) The head of the embassy of the People's Republic of China in the state where he/she is accredited, for negotiating and signing treaties and agreements with that state, unless otherwise agreed by the parties;
(3) The head of a government department of the People's Republic of China, for negotiating and signing agreements concluded in the name of the department, unless otherwise agreed by the parties;
(4) Representatives of the People's Republic of China sent to international conferences or accredited to international organizations, for participating in the negotiation of treaties and agreements within such conferences or organizations, unless otherwise provided by the conference or the charter of the organization.

**Article 7** The ratification of treaties and important agreements shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

The treaties and important agreements referred to in the preceding paragraph are:

(1) Political treaties such as treaties of friendship and cooperation, and peace treaties;
(2) Treaties and agreements concerning territories and the delimitation of boundaries;
(3) Treaties and agreements concerning judicial assistance and extradition;
(4) Treaties and agreements that contain provisions different from those of the laws of the People's Republic of China;
(5) Treaties and agreements that the contracting parties agree shall be subject to ratification;
(6) Other treaties and agreements that require ratification.

After a treaty or important agreement is signed, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall report to the State Council for examination; the State Council shall submit it to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for a decision on ratification; the President of the People's Republic of China shall ratify it in accordance with the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

After a bilateral treaty or important agreement is ratified, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall handle the procedures for exchanging instruments of ratification with the other contracting party; after a multilateral treaty or important agreement is ratified, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall handle the procedures for depositing the instrument of ratification with the depositary state or international organization. The instrument of ratification shall be signed by the President of the People's Republic of China and countersigned by the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

**Article 8** After an agreement or other document of a treaty nature, which is outside the scope of paragraph 2 of Article 7 of this Law and which is required by the State Council to be approved or which the contracting parties agree shall be subject to approval, is signed, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall report to the State Council for approval.

After an agreement or other document of a treaty nature is approved, if it is bilateral, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall handle the procedures for exchanging instruments of approval with the other contracting party or for notifying each other of the approval by diplomatic notes; if it is multilateral, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall handle the procedures for depositing the instrument of approval with the relevant depositary state or international organization. The instrument of approval shall be signed by the Premier of the State Council, or may be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

**Article 9** After an agreement that does not require ratification by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or approval by the State Council is signed, except for agreements concluded in the name of a government department of the People's Republic of China, which shall be submitted by the department to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for registration, other agreements shall be reported by the relevant department of the State Council to the State Council for the record.

**Article 10** If the domestic legal procedures required for the entry into force of the same treaty or agreement differ between the contracting parties, the treaty or agreement shall enter into force after both parties have completed their respective legal procedures and have notified each other by diplomatic notes.

After a treaty or agreement referred to in the preceding paragraph is signed, the procedures for ratification, approval, record-filing, or registration shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Articles 7, 8, and 9 of this Law, as the case may be. The procedures for notification by diplomatic notes shall be handled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

**Article 11** The accession to multilateral treaties and agreements shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or the State Council, respectively.

The procedures for accession to multilateral treaties and agreements are as follows:

(1) For accession to multilateral treaties and important agreements falling within the scope of paragraph 2 of Article 7 of this Law, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall, after examination, submit proposals and report to the State Council for examination; the State Council shall submit them to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for a decision on accession. The instrument of accession shall be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, and the specific procedures shall be handled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs;
(2) For accession to multilateral treaties and agreements not falling within the scope of paragraph 2 of Article 7 of this Law, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall, after examination, submit proposals and report to the State Council for a decision on accession. The instrument of accession shall be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, and the specific procedures shall be handled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

**Article 12** The acceptance of multilateral treaties and agreements shall be decided by the State Council.

For multilateral treaties and agreements containing acceptance clauses, which have been signed by Chinese representatives or do not require signature, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council, together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, shall, after examination, submit proposals and report to the State Council for a decision on acceptance. The instrument of acceptance shall be signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, and the specific procedures shall be handled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

**Article 13** Bilateral treaties and agreements concluded between the People's Republic of China and foreign states shall be written in Chinese and the official language of the other contracting party, both texts being equally authentic; when necessary, a third language agreed upon by both contracting parties may be used additionally, either as a third official text of equal authenticity or as an unofficial text for reference purposes; with the agreement of both contracting parties, it may also be stipulated that in the event of a divergence in the interpretation of the treaty or agreement, the third text shall prevail.

For certain agreements concerning specific operational matters, as well as treaties and agreements concluded with international organizations, with the agreement of both contracting parties or in accordance with the provisions of the relevant international organization's charter, only one widely used international language may be used.

**Article 14** The signed originals of bilateral treaties and agreements concluded in the name of the People's Republic of China or the Government of the People's Republic of China, as well as certified true copies of multilateral treaties and agreements certified by the depositary state or international organization, shall be kept by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; the signed originals of bilateral agreements concluded in the name of a government department of the People's Republic of China shall be kept by that department.

**Article 15** Treaties and important agreements that have been decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress to be ratified or acceded to shall be published in the Gazette of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The methods for publishing other treaties and agreements shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 16** Treaties and agreements concluded by the People's Republic of China shall be compiled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs into the "Collection of Treaties of the People's Republic of China."

**Article 17** Treaties and agreements concluded by the People's Republic of China shall be registered by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs with the Secretariat of the United Nations in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Charter of the United Nations.

If treaties and agreements concluded by the People's Republic of China need to be registered with other international organizations, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the relevant department of the State Council shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of the charters of those international organizations.

**Article 18** The procedures for concluding treaties and agreements between the People's Republic of China and international organizations shall be handled in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the relevant international organization's charter.

**Article 19** The procedures for the amendment, denunciation, or withdrawal from treaties and agreements concluded by the People's Republic of China shall be handled by analogy with the procedures for the conclusion of those treaties and agreements.

**Article 20** The State Council may formulate implementing regulations in accordance with this Law.

**Article 21** This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
