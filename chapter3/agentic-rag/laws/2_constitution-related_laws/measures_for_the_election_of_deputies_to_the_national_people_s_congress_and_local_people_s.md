# Measures for the Election of Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses at and above the County Level by the Chinese People's Liberation Army

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Fifth National People's Congress on June 10, 1981

Revised at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 29, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Measures for the Election of Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses at and above the County Level by the Chinese People's Liberation Army adopted at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on June 30, 2012

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Measures for the Election of Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses at and above the County Level by the Chinese People's Liberation Army adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Measures are formulated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Constitution of the People's Republic of China and the Electoral Law of the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses of the People's Republic of China.

Article 2 Servicemen and women of the People's Liberation Army and other persons participating in military elections shall elect deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level in accordance with these Measures.

Article 3 Election committees shall be established in the People's Liberation Army and in units at the regimental level and above.

The Election Committee of the People's Liberation Army shall lead the election work of the entire army, and election committees at other levels shall preside over the election work of their respective units.

Article 4 The servicemen's committees of companies and other grassroots units shall preside over the election work of their respective units.

Article 5 Servicemen and women of the People's Liberation Army, civilian personnel, military-managed retirees and other persons shall participate in military elections.

If the garrison is located far from the residence of local residents, making it difficult for accompanying family members to participate in local elections, they may, with the approval of the election committee or the servicemen's committee, participate in military elections.

Article 6 Military representatives stationed in local factories, railway, water transport, scientific research and other units, and military personnel studying in local institutions of higher learning may participate in local elections.

Article 7 All persons listed in Article 5 of these Measures who have reached the age of 18, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, family background, religious belief, education level, property status, or length of residence, shall have the qualifications of a voter and enjoy the right to vote and stand for election.

Persons who have been deprived of political rights in accordance with the law shall have no right to vote or stand for election.

Mentally ill persons who are unable to exercise their right to vote shall, upon confirmation by the election committee, not participate in the election.

## Chapter II Election Committees

Article 8 The composition of the Election Committee of the People's Liberation Army shall be approved by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The composition of election committees at other levels shall be approved by the election committee at the next higher level.

Election committees at lower levels shall be under the leadership of election committees at higher levels.

The term of office of an election committee is five years, and it shall exercise its functions and powers until a new election committee is formed. If a member of an election committee is transferred away from the unit, removed from office, or retired, their position on the election committee shall automatically terminate; if, due to a change in position or other reasons, they are no longer suitable to serve on the election committee, they shall be relieved of their position on the election committee. When a vacancy occurs among the members of an election committee, it shall be filled promptly.

Article 9 The Election Committee of the People's Liberation Army shall be composed of 11 to 19 members, with one chairperson, one to three vice-chairpersons, and a number of other members. Election committees at other levels shall be composed of 7 to 17 members, with one chairperson, one to two vice-chairpersons, and a number of other members.

Article 10 Election committees of units at the regimental level and above shall organize and guide the election work of subordinate units and handle the following matters:

(1) Reviewing the qualifications of deputies to the servicemen's congress;
(2) Determining the date of the election;
(3) Publishing the list of candidates for deputies to the people's congress;
(4) Presiding over the voting and election at the servicemen's congress or servicemen's assembly at the corresponding level;
(5) Determining the validity of the election results and publishing the list of elected deputies to the people's congress;
(6) Presiding over the recall and by-election of deputies to the people's congress and accepting the resignation of deputies to the people's congress at the servicemen's congress or servicemen's assembly at the corresponding level.

Article 11 An election committee shall establish an office to specifically handle the day-to-day work related to the election at the corresponding level.

The office shall be located in the political work department, and its staff shall be determined by the election committee at the corresponding level.

## Chapter III Determination and Allocation of Deputy Quotas

Article 12 The number of deputies to be elected to the National People's Congress by the People's Liberation Army shall be determined by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 13 The number of deputies to be elected to the National People's Congress by the departments of the Central Military Commission, theater commands, services and branches, the Academy of Military Sciences, the National Defense University, the National University of Defense Technology, and other units shall be allocated by the Election Committee of the People's Liberation Army. Organs directly under the Central Military Commission shall participate in the election of the department that manages them on behalf of the Commission.

Article 14 The number of deputies to be elected to local people's congresses at and above the county level by local garrisons shall be determined by the standing committees of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels in the localities.

Matters related to the election shall be decided through consultation between the provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), military sub-commands (garrison commands), and people's armed forces departments and the standing committees of the people's congresses in the localities.

## Chapter IV Electoral Districts and Electoral Units

Article 15 For the election of deputies to the people's congress at the county level by garrisons, the servicemen and women and other persons participating in military elections within the administrative area shall directly elect the deputies by electoral districts. Electoral districts shall be delineated based on the distribution of various garrison units within the administrative area.

The size of an electoral district shall be such that one to three deputies are elected from each district.

Article 16 Deputies to be elected by garrisons to the people's congresses of cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be elected by servicemen's congresses convened by units at the regimental level and above.

The servicemen's congresses of the departments of the Central Military Commission, theater commands, services and branches, the Academy of Military Sciences, the National Defense University, the National University of Defense Technology, and other units shall elect deputies to the National People's Congress.

Article 17 Deputies to the servicemen's congress of units at the division level and above in the People's Liberation Army shall be elected by servicemen's congresses at lower levels. If a lower-level unit does not convene a servicemen's congress, the deputies shall be elected by a servicemen's assembly.

Deputies to the servicemen's congress of units at the brigade and regimental level shall be elected by servicemen's assemblies convened by companies and other grassroots units.

A servicemen's congress shall be convened by the election committee, and a servicemen's assembly shall be convened by the election committee or the servicemen's committee.

The term of office of a servicemen's congress is five years. The term of office of a deputy to a servicemen's congress shall begin from the first meeting of the current servicemen's congress and end at the first meeting of the next servicemen's congress.

## Chapter V Nomination of Candidates

Article 18 For the election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level by the People's Liberation Army, candidates shall be nominated by electoral districts or electoral units.

Party organizations at all levels within the military may recommend candidates for deputies. Voters or deputies to the servicemen's congress, jointly recommended by ten or more persons, may also recommend candidates for deputies. Recommenders shall introduce the candidates' information to the election committee or the servicemen's committee. Candidates for deputies who accept the recommendation shall truthfully provide their basic personal information to the election committee or the servicemen's committee. If the basic information provided is untrue, the election committee or the servicemen's committee shall notify the voters or the deputies to the servicemen's congress.

Article 19 The election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level by the People's Liberation Army shall be conducted as a contested election. The number of candidates for deputies shall be greater than the number of deputies to be elected.

For direct election by voters, the number of candidates for deputies shall be one-third to one time greater than the number of deputies to be elected; for election by a servicemen's congress, the number of candidates for deputies shall be one-fifth to one-half greater than the number of deputies to be elected.

Article 20 For direct election by voters, the election committee or the servicemen's committee shall compile the list of candidates for deputies and their basic personal information, publish it fifteen days before the election day, and submit it to the voters of the respective electoral districts for repeated discussion and consultation to determine the list of official candidates for deputies. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies exceeds the maximum margin ratio stipulated in Article 19 of these Measures, the election committee or the servicemen's committee shall submit the list to the voters of the respective electoral districts for discussion and consultation, and determine the list of official candidates for deputies based on the opinions of the majority of voters; if a relatively consistent opinion on the official candidates for deputies cannot be formed, a primary election shall be held, and the list of official candidates for deputies shall be determined in the order of the number of votes received in the primary election. The list of official candidates for deputies and their basic personal information shall be published seven days before the election day.

When a servicemen's congress of a unit at the regimental level and above elects deputies to a people's congress, the time for nominating and deliberating candidates for deputies shall not be less than two days. The election committee at the corresponding level shall print and distribute the list of candidates for deputies legally nominated and their basic personal information to all deputies of the servicemen's congress for deliberation and discussion. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies meets the margin ratio stipulated in Article 19 of these Measures, a direct vote shall be held. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies exceeds the maximum margin ratio stipulated in Article 19 of these Measures, a primary election shall be held, and the list of official candidates for deputies shall be determined in the order of the number of votes received in the primary election, according to the specific margin ratio determined by the servicemen's congress at the corresponding level, and then a vote shall be held.

Article 21 When a servicemen's congress elects deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level, candidates for deputies are not limited to the deputies of the servicemen's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 22 The election committee or the servicemen's committee shall introduce the information of candidates for deputies.

Organizations or individuals that recommend candidates for deputies may introduce the information of the recommended candidates at meetings of voter groups or groups of deputies to the servicemen's congress. In a direct election, the election committee or the servicemen's committee shall, at the request of voters, organize meetings between candidates for deputies and voters, where the candidates for deputies shall introduce their own information and answer questions from voters. However, the introduction of candidates for deputies must cease on the election day.

## Chapter VI Election Procedures

Article 23 In a direct election, each electoral district shall convene a servicemen's assembly for the election, or set up polling stations for the election based on the principle of convenience for voters to vote. Voters who are scattered in location or have difficulty moving may vote in mobile ballot boxes. The voting and election shall be presided over by the servicemen's committee or the election committee.

The voting and election of a servicemen's congress shall be presided over by the election committee.

Article 24 The election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level by the People's Liberation Army shall be conducted by secret ballot. A secret voting booth shall be provided during the election.

Voters who are unable to fill out a ballot due to disability or other reasons may entrust a person they trust to fill it out on their behalf.

Article 25 If a voter is away during the election period, they may, with the consent of the servicemen's committee or the election committee, entrust another voter in writing to vote on their behalf. Each voter may accept no more than three such entrustments and shall vote according to the wishes of the principal.

Article 26 An elector may vote in favor of a candidate for deputy, vote against, vote for any other voter, or abstain from voting.

Article 27 After the voting is concluded, the scrutineers and vote counters elected by the voters or the deputies to the servicemen's congress, together with the personnel of the election committee or the servicemen's committee, shall check and record the number of voters and the number of ballots, and the scrutineers shall sign the record.

Close relatives of a candidate for deputy shall not serve as scrutineers or vote counters.

Article 28 If the number of ballots cast in an election exceeds the number of voters, the election shall be invalid; if it is equal to or less than the number of voters, the election shall be valid.

If the number of persons voted for on a ballot exceeds the prescribed number of deputies to be elected, the ballot shall be void; if it is equal to or less than the prescribed number, the ballot shall be valid.

Article 29 In a direct election, the election shall be valid if the number of voters who cast their votes exceeds half of all voters in the electoral district. A candidate for deputy shall be elected only if they receive more than half of the votes cast by the voters who participated in the voting.

In an election by a servicemen's congress, a candidate for deputy shall be elected only if they receive more than half of the votes of all deputies.

Article 30 If the number of candidates for deputies who receive more than half of the votes exceeds the number of deputies to be elected, those with the highest number of votes shall be elected. If the votes are tied and the elected persons cannot be determined, a second vote shall be held for the tied candidates, and those with the highest number of votes shall be elected.

If the number of elected deputies who receive more than half of the votes is less than the number of deputies to be elected, a by-election shall be held for the remaining seats. In the by-election, the list of candidates shall be determined in the order of the number of votes received in the first vote, according to the margin ratio stipulated in Article 19 of these Measures. If only one person is to be elected, there shall be two candidates.

When a by-election for deputies to the people's congress at the county level is held in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the candidate for deputy with the highest number of votes shall be elected, provided that the number of votes received is not less than one-third of the total ballots cast; when a by-election for deputies to the people's congresses of cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the National People's Congress is held by a servicemen's congress of a unit at the regimental level and above, a candidate for deputy shall be elected only if they receive more than half of the votes of all deputies of the servicemen's congress.

Article 31 The election committee or the servicemen's committee shall determine the validity of the election results in accordance with these Measures and announce them.

## Chapter VII Supervision, Recall, Resignation, and By-election of Deputies

Article 32 Deputies elected by the People's Liberation Army to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level shall be subject to the supervision of the voters and the original electoral units. Voters or electoral units shall have the right to recall the deputies they have elected.

Article 33 For a deputy to the people's congress at the county level, ten or more voters from the original electoral district may jointly submit a written request for recall to the election committee at the brigade or regimental level.

The request for recall shall state the reasons for the recall. The deputy proposed for recall shall have the right to present a defense at a servicemen's assembly or submit a written defense.

The election committee at the brigade or regimental level shall print and distribute the recall request and the written defense of the deputy proposed for recall to the voters of the original electoral district.

The vote on the recall request shall be presided over by the election committee at the brigade or regimental level.

Article 34 When a servicemen's congress is in session, the election committee of a unit at the regimental level and above may submit a recall case against a deputy to the people's congress elected by that servicemen's congress. The recall case shall state the reasons for the recall.

When a servicemen's congress is in session, the deputy proposed for recall shall have the right to present a defense at the meeting or submit a written defense. The recall case shall be voted on after deliberation by the meeting.

Article 35 The recall of a deputy shall be conducted by secret ballot.

Article 36 The recall of a deputy to the people's congress at the county level shall require the approval of more than half of the voters of the original electoral district.

The recall of a deputy to the people's congress elected by a servicemen's congress shall require the approval of more than half of the deputies of that servicemen's congress.

The resolution on the recall shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the election committee at the next higher level within the military for the record.

Article 37 Deputies elected by the People's Liberation Army to the people's congresses of cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the National People's Congress may submit a written resignation to the election committee of the original electoral unit. Deputies elected by the People's Liberation Army to the people's congress at the county level may submit a written resignation to the election committee or the servicemen's committee of the original electoral district. Acceptance of the resignation shall require the approval of more than half of all members of the servicemen's congress or servicemen's assembly, and shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the election committee at the next higher level within the military for the record.

If a servicemen's congress cannot be convened due to reasons such as the execution of tasks, the election committee of a unit at the regimental level and above may accept the resignation of deputies elected by that level to the people's congresses of cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the National People's Congress. After the election committee accepts the resignation of a deputy to the people's congress, it shall promptly notify the deputies of the servicemen's congress that elected the deputy and submit the matter to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the election committee at the next higher level within the military for the record.

Article 38 If a deputy's seat becomes vacant during their term of office for any reason, a by-election shall be held by the original electoral district or the original electoral unit.

If a deputy elected by the People's Liberation Army to a local people's congress at or above the county level is transferred out of the administrative area during their term of office, their deputy qualification shall automatically terminate, and the vacancy shall be filled by a by-election.

In a by-election for a deputy, the number of candidates for deputy may be greater than or equal to the number of deputies to be elected.

If a servicemen's congress cannot be convened due to reasons such as the execution of tasks, the by-election may be conducted by the election committee at the corresponding level.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 39 The election expenses of the People's Liberation Army shall be borne by military funds.

Article 40 These Measures shall apply to the election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at and above the county level by the People's Armed Police Force.
