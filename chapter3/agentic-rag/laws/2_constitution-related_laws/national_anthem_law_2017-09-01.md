# National Anthem Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to uphold the dignity of the national anthem, regulate its performance, broadcast, and use, enhance citizens' national consciousness, promote patriotism, and cultivate and practice core socialist values.

Article 2 The national anthem of the People's Republic of China is "March of the Volunteers."

Article 3 The national anthem of the People's Republic of China is a symbol and emblem of the People's Republic of China.

All citizens and organizations shall respect the national anthem and uphold its dignity.

Article 4 The national anthem shall be performed on the following occasions:

(1) The opening and closing of sessions of the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at all levels;
The opening and closing of sessions of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference and local committees at all levels;

(2) Congresses of political parties and people's organizations at all levels, among others;

(3) Constitutional oath-taking ceremonies;

(4) National flag-raising ceremonies;

(5) Major celebrations, commendations, and commemorative ceremonies held or organized by organs at all levels;

(6) National mourning ceremonies;

(7) Major diplomatic events;

(8) Major sports events;

(9) Other occasions where the national anthem should be performed.

Article 5 The State encourages citizens and organizations to perform the national anthem on appropriate occasions to express patriotic sentiments.

Article 6 When performing the national anthem, the lyrics and musical score as set forth in the annex to this Law shall be used, and no form of performance that impairs the dignity of the national anthem shall be adopted.

Article 7 When the national anthem is performed, persons present shall stand solemnly and behave with dignity, and shall not engage in any act disrespectful to the national anthem.

Article 8 The national anthem shall not be used, directly or indirectly, in trademarks or commercial advertisements, shall not be used on inappropriate occasions such as private funeral activities, and shall not be used as background music in public places.

Article 9 The occasions and etiquette for performing the national anthem in diplomatic activities shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

The occasions and etiquette for performing the national anthem by the armed forces shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 10 When the national anthem is performed on the occasions specified in Article 4 of this Law, the standard performance score or the official recorded version of the national anthem shall be used.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and its diplomatic missions abroad shall provide the standard performance score and the official recorded version of the national anthem to the relevant foreign diplomatic departments and international organizations for use in diplomatic activities.

The sports administrative department of the State Council shall provide the standard performance score and the official recorded version of the national anthem to relevant international sports organizations and event organizers for use in international sports events.

The standard performance score and the official recorded version of the national anthem shall be reviewed and recorded by the department designated by the State Council, and published on the websites of the National People's Congress and the Chinese government.

Article 11 The national anthem shall be incorporated into primary and secondary education.

Primary and secondary schools shall include the national anthem as an important part of patriotic education, organize students to learn to sing the national anthem, educate students about its history and spiritual connotation, and teach them to observe the etiquette of performing the national anthem.

Article 12 News media shall actively promote the national anthem and popularize knowledge of the etiquette for performing the national anthem.

Article 13 On important national statutory holidays and commemorative days such as National Day and International Labor Day, radio and television stations at the central level and in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall broadcast the national anthem at the times prescribed by the competent radio and television department of the State Council.

Article 14 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, within their respective responsibilities, supervise and manage the performance, broadcast, and use of the national anthem.

Article 15 Anyone who, in a public place, intentionally alters the lyrics or musical score of the national anthem, performs it in a distorted or derogatory manner, or insults the national anthem in any other way, shall be warned by the public security organ or detained for up to 15 days; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 16 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2017.

Annex: National Anthem of the People's Republic of China (staff notation version and numbered musical notation version)
