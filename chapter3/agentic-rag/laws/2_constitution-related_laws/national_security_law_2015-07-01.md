# National Security Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on July 1, 2015

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to safeguard national security, protect the people's democratic dictatorship and the socialist system with Chinese characteristics, protect the fundamental interests of the people, ensure the smooth progress of reform, opening up, and socialist modernization, and realize the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.

Article 2 National security refers to the state in which the state's regime, sovereignty, unity and territorial integrity, the well-being of the people, sustainable economic and social development, and other major national interests are relatively free from danger and from internal and external threats, as well as the capacity to ensure a sustained state of security.

Article 3 National security work shall adhere to a holistic approach to national security, with the security of the people as the purpose, political security as the foundation, economic security as the basis, military, cultural, and social security as safeguards, and the promotion of international security as support. It shall safeguard national security in all areas, build a national security system, and follow a path of national security with Chinese characteristics.

Article 4 The leadership of the Communist Party of China over national security work shall be upheld, and a centralized, unified, efficient, and authoritative national security leadership system shall be established.

Article 5 The central national security leadership body is responsible for decision-making, deliberation, and coordination in national security work. It studies, formulates, and guides the implementation of national security strategies and relevant major policies and principles, coordinates major national security matters and important tasks, and promotes the rule of law in national security.

Article 6 The state formulates and continuously improves its national security strategy, comprehensively assesses the international and domestic security situation, and defines the guiding principles, medium- and long-term objectives, national security policies for key areas, work tasks, and measures.

Article 7 In safeguarding national security, the Constitution and laws shall be observed, the principles of socialist rule of law shall be upheld, human rights shall be respected and protected, and the rights and freedoms of citizens shall be protected in accordance with the law.

Article 8 Safeguarding national security shall be coordinated with economic and social development.

National security work shall coordinate internal and external security, homeland and public security, traditional and non-traditional security, and one's own security and common security.

Article 9 In safeguarding national security, the principles of prevention first, addressing both symptoms and root causes, and combining specialized work with the mass line shall be adhered to. The functional roles of specialized agencies and other relevant organs in safeguarding national security shall be fully utilized, and citizens and organizations shall be widely mobilized to prevent, stop, and legally punish acts that endanger national security.

Article 10 In safeguarding national security, the principles of mutual trust, mutual benefit, equality, and collaboration shall be upheld. The state shall actively engage in security exchanges and cooperation with foreign governments and international organizations, fulfill international security obligations, promote common security, and safeguard world peace.

Article 11 All citizens of the People's Republic of China, all state organs and armed forces, all political parties and people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other social organizations have the responsibility and obligation to safeguard national security.

China's sovereignty and territorial integrity are inviolable and indivisible. Safeguarding national sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity is the common duty of all Chinese people, including compatriots in Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan.

Article 12 The state shall commend and reward individuals and organizations that have made outstanding contributions in the work of safeguarding national security.

Article 13 State functionaries who abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in national security work or activities involving national security shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

Any individual or organization that violates this Law or relevant laws, fails to fulfill the obligation to safeguard national security, or engages in activities that endanger national security shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

Article 14 April 15 of each year is designated as National Security Education Day for all citizens.

## Chapter II Tasks of Safeguarding National Security

Article 15 The state upholds the leadership of the Communist Party of China, maintains the socialist system with Chinese characteristics, develops socialist democratic politics, improves the socialist rule of law, strengthens mechanisms for restricting and supervising the exercise of power, and guarantees the rights of the people to be the masters of the country.

The state prevents, stops, and legally punishes any acts of treason, secession, incitement to rebellion, subversion or incitement to subvert the people's democratic dictatorship; prevents, stops, and legally punishes acts that endanger national security, such as stealing or leaking state secrets; and prevents, stops, and legally punishes infiltration, sabotage, subversion, and secession activities by foreign forces.

Article 16 The state safeguards and develops the fundamental interests of the broadest majority of the people, protects the security of the people, creates good conditions for survival and development and a stable working and living environment, and protects the safety of citizens' lives and property and other legitimate rights and interests.

Article 17 The state strengthens border, coastal, and air defense construction, adopts all necessary defense and control measures, safeguards the security of the land territory, internal waters, territorial sea, and territorial airspace, and upholds national territorial sovereignty and maritime rights and interests.

Article 18 The state strengthens the revolutionization, modernization, and regularization of the armed forces, and builds armed forces that meet the needs of safeguarding national security and development interests; implements an active defense military strategy, guards against and resists aggression, and stops armed subversion and secession; conducts international military security cooperation, and carries out military operations such as UN peacekeeping, international rescue, maritime escort, and the protection of national overseas interests, to safeguard national sovereignty, security, territorial integrity, development interests, and world peace.

Article 19 The state maintains the basic economic system of the state and the socialist market economic order, improves institutional mechanisms for preventing and resolving economic security risks, and ensures the security of important industries and key areas related to the lifeline of the national economy, key industries, major infrastructure, major construction projects, and other major economic interests.

Article 20 The state improves mechanisms for macro-prudential management of finance and for the prevention and resolution of financial risks, strengthens the construction of financial infrastructure and basic capabilities, prevents and resolves systemic and regional financial risks, and prevents and resists the impact of external financial risks.

Article 21 The state rationally utilizes and protects resources and energy, effectively controls the development of strategic resources and energy, strengthens the reserve of strategic resources and energy, improves the construction of strategic resource and energy transportation channels and security protection measures, strengthens international cooperation on resources and energy, comprehensively enhances emergency support capabilities, and ensures the sustainable, reliable, and effective supply of resources and energy needed for economic and social development.

Article 22 The state improves the food security guarantee system, protects and enhances the comprehensive grain production capacity, improves the grain reserve system, circulation system, and market regulation mechanism, improves the food security early warning system, and ensures food supply and quality safety.

Article 23 The state adheres to the direction of advanced socialist culture, inherits and promotes the excellent traditional culture of the Chinese nation, cultivates and practices the core socialist values, prevents and resists the influence of undesirable culture, holds the dominant position in the ideological field, and enhances the overall strength and competitiveness of culture.

Article 24 The state strengthens the construction of independent innovation capabilities, accelerates the development of independent and controllable strategic high-tech and core key technologies in important fields, strengthens the application of intellectual property rights, protection, and the construction of scientific and technological confidentiality capabilities, and ensures the security of major technologies and projects.

Article 25 The state builds a network and information security guarantee system, enhances the capability to protect network and information security, strengthens innovative research, development, and application of network and information technology, achieves the security and controllability of core network and information technologies, critical infrastructure, and information systems and data in important fields; strengthens network management, prevents, stops, and legally punishes cyber attacks, cyber intrusions, cyber theft of secrets, dissemination of illegal and harmful information, and other cyber crimes, and safeguards national cyberspace sovereignty, security, and development interests.

Article 26 The state upholds and improves the system of regional ethnic autonomy, and consolidates and develops socialist ethnic relations characterized by equality, unity, mutual assistance, and harmony. It adheres to the equality of all ethnic groups, strengthens ethnic contacts, exchanges, and integration, prevents, stops, and legally punishes ethnic separatist activities, maintains national unity, ethnic solidarity, and social harmony, and achieves the common struggle and common prosperity and development of all ethnic groups.

Article 27 The state protects citizens' freedom of religious belief and normal religious activities in accordance with the law, adheres to the principle of independence and self-management of religious affairs, prevents, stops, and legally punishes illegal and criminal activities that endanger national security under the guise of religion, opposes interference in domestic religious affairs by foreign forces, and maintains the order of normal religious activities.

The state bans cult organizations in accordance with the law, and prevents, stops, and legally punishes illegal and criminal activities of cults.

Article 28 The state opposes all forms of terrorism and extremism, strengthens capacity building for preventing and dealing with terrorism, carries out intelligence, investigation, prevention, handling, and fund supervision work in accordance with the law, bans terrorist activity organizations in accordance with the law, and severely punishes violent terrorist activities.

Article 29 The state improves the institutional mechanisms for effectively preventing and resolving social conflicts, improves the public safety system, actively prevents, reduces, and resolves social conflicts, properly handles public health, social security, and other emergencies that affect national security and social stability, promotes social harmony, and maintains public safety and social stability.

Article 30 The state improves the system for ecological and environmental protection, increases efforts in ecological construction and environmental protection, delimits ecological protection red lines, strengthens early warning and prevention and control of ecological risks, properly handles environmental emergencies, ensures that the natural environment and conditions such as the atmosphere, water, and soil on which the people depend for survival and development are not threatened or damaged, and promotes the harmonious development of man and nature.

Article 31 The state adheres to the peaceful use of nuclear energy and nuclear technology, strengthens international cooperation, prevents nuclear proliferation, improves the non-proliferation mechanism, strengthens the safety management, supervision, and protection of nuclear facilities, nuclear materials, nuclear activities, and nuclear waste disposal, strengthens the construction of nuclear accident emergency systems and emergency response capabilities, prevents, controls, and eliminates the harm of nuclear accidents to citizens' lives, health, and the ecological environment, and continuously enhances the ability to effectively respond to and prevent nuclear threats and nuclear attacks.

Article 32 The state adheres to the peaceful exploration and use of outer space, the international seabed area, and the polar regions, enhances the capabilities for safe access, scientific investigation, development, and utilization, strengthens international cooperation, and safeguards the security of China's activities, assets, and other interests in outer space, the international seabed area, and the polar regions.

Article 33 The state takes necessary measures in accordance with the law to protect the safety and legitimate rights and interests of overseas Chinese citizens, organizations, and institutions, and to protect the country's overseas interests from threats and infringement.

Article 34 The state continuously improves the tasks of safeguarding national security in accordance with the needs of economic and social development and national development interests.

## Chapter III Responsibilities for Safeguarding National Security

Article 35 The National People's Congress, in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution, decides on issues of war and peace, and exercises other powers related to national security as stipulated by the Constitution.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution, decides on the proclamation of a state of war, decides on national or partial mobilization, decides on the entry of the whole country or individual provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government into a state of emergency, and exercises other powers related to national security as stipulated by the Constitution and conferred by the National People's Congress.

Article 36 The President of the People's Republic of China, in accordance with the decisions of the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee, proclaims a state of emergency, proclaims a state of war, issues mobilization orders, and exercises other powers related to national security as stipulated by the Constitution.

Article 37 The State Council, in accordance with the Constitution and laws, formulates administrative regulations related to national security, prescribes relevant administrative measures, issues relevant decisions and orders; implements national security laws, regulations, and policies; decides, in accordance with legal provisions, on the entry of parts of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government into a state of emergency; and exercises other powers related to national security as stipulated by the Constitution and laws and conferred by the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee.

Article 38 The Central Military Commission leads the national armed forces, decides on military strategy and operational guidelines for the armed forces, uniformly commands military operations to safeguard national security, formulates military regulations related to national security, and issues relevant decisions and orders.

Article 39 All departments of the central state organs, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, implement national security policies, principles, laws, and regulations, and manage and guide national security work in their respective systems and fields.

Article 40 Local people's congresses at various levels and the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level shall ensure compliance with and implementation of national security laws and regulations within their respective administrative regions.

Local people's governments at various levels shall manage national security work within their respective administrative regions in accordance with laws and regulations.

The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the Macao Special Administrative Region shall fulfill their responsibilities for safeguarding national security.

Article 41 The people's courts exercise judicial power in accordance with legal provisions, and the people's procuratorates exercise procuratorial power in accordance with legal provisions, to punish crimes that endanger national security.

Article 42 State security organs and public security organs shall, in accordance with the law, collect intelligence information related to national security, and exercise the powers of investigation, detention, preliminary interrogation, execution of arrest, and other powers stipulated by law in national security work.

Relevant military organs shall exercise relevant powers in accordance with the law in national security work.

Article 43 State organs and their functionaries shall implement the principle of safeguarding national security when performing their duties.

State organs and their functionaries shall strictly perform their duties in accordance with the law in national security work and activities involving national security, shall not exceed their authority or abuse their power, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of individuals and organizations.

## Chapter IV National Security System

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 44 The central national security leadership body implements a national security system and working mechanism that combines unified and decentralized functions and is coordinated and efficient.

Article 45 The state establishes a coordination mechanism for work in key areas of national security to coordinate relevant functional departments of the central authorities in advancing related work.

Article 46 The state establishes a mechanism for supervision, inspection, and accountability in national security work to ensure the implementation of national security strategies and major deployments.

Article 47 All departments and regions shall take effective measures to implement the national security strategy.

Article 48 The state establishes an inter-departmental consultation and working mechanism, as needed for safeguarding national security, to conduct consultations and assessments on major matters of safeguarding national security and to provide opinions and suggestions.

Article 49 The state establishes a mechanism for coordination and linkage between the central and local authorities, between departments, between the military and local authorities, and between regions regarding national security.

Article 50 The state establishes a national security decision-making consultation mechanism, organizes experts and relevant parties to conduct analysis and assessment of the national security situation, and promotes scientific decision-making in national security.

### Section 2 Intelligence Information

Article 51 The state improves the system for the collection, assessment, and use of intelligence information that is unified, responsive, accurate, efficient, and smooth, establishes a coordination mechanism for intelligence information work, and achieves the timely collection, accurate assessment, effective use, and sharing of intelligence information.

Article 52 State security organs, public security organs, and relevant military organs shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, collect intelligence information related to national security in accordance with the law.

All departments of state organs shall promptly report information related to national security obtained in the course of performing their duties.

Article 53 In carrying out intelligence information work, modern scientific and technological means shall be fully utilized, and the identification, screening, synthesis, and assessment analysis of intelligence information shall be strengthened.

Article 54 The reporting of intelligence information shall be timely, accurate, and objective, and there shall be no delayed reporting, omission, concealment, or false reporting.

### Section 3 Risk Prevention, Assessment, and Early Warning

Article 55 The state formulates and improves contingency plans for responding to national security risks in various fields.

Article 56 The state establishes a national security risk assessment mechanism, and regularly conducts investigations and assessments of national security risks in various fields.

Relevant departments shall regularly submit national security risk assessment reports to the central national security leadership body.

Article 57 The state improves the national security risk monitoring and early warning system, and issues corresponding risk warnings in a timely manner based on the degree of national security risk.

Article 58 For events that may imminently occur or have already occurred that endanger national security, the local people's government at or above the county level and its relevant competent departments shall immediately report to the people's government at the next higher level and its relevant competent departments in accordance with regulations, and may report directly to higher levels when necessary.

### Section 4 Review and Supervision

Article 59 The state establishes a system and mechanism for national security review and supervision, and conducts national security reviews of foreign investments, specific items and key technologies, network and information technology products and services, construction projects involving national security matters, and other major matters and activities that affect or may affect national security, to effectively prevent and resolve national security risks.

Article 60 All departments of the central state organs exercise national security review functions in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, make national security review decisions or issue security review opinions in accordance with the law, and supervise their implementation.

Article 61 Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government are responsible for relevant national security review and supervision work within their respective administrative regions in accordance with the law.

### Section 5 Crisis Management and Control

Article 62 The state establishes a national security crisis management and control system that is unified in leadership, coordinated and linked, and orderly and efficient.

Article 63 When a major event endangering national security occurs, relevant central departments and relevant localities shall, in accordance with the unified deployment of the central national security leadership body, initiate emergency plans in accordance with the law and take management, control, and handling measures.

Article 64 When an exceptionally major event endangering national security occurs that requires entering a state of emergency, a state of war, or carrying out national or partial mobilization, the National People's Congress, its Standing Committee, or the State Council shall decide in accordance with the authority and procedures stipulated by the Constitution and relevant laws.

Article 65 After the state decides to enter a state of emergency, a state of war, or implement national defense mobilization, relevant organs responsible for national security crisis management and control may, in accordance with legal provisions or regulations of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, take special measures to restrict the rights of citizens and organizations and increase their obligations.

Article 66 The control measures taken by relevant organs responsible for national security crisis management and control to handle national security crises in accordance with the law shall be commensurate with the nature, degree, and scope of the harm that the national security crisis may cause; when multiple measures are available, the measure that is most conducive to protecting the rights and interests of citizens and organizations shall be chosen.

Article 67 The state improves the mechanism for information reporting and release regarding national security crises.

After a national security crisis event occurs, relevant organs responsible for national security crisis management and control shall, in accordance with regulations, report accurately and promptly, and shall uniformly release information to the public regarding the occurrence, development, management, control, handling, and aftermath of the relevant national security crisis event in accordance with the law.

Article 68 After the national security threat and harm are controlled or eliminated, the management, control, and handling measures shall be promptly lifted, and follow-up work shall be properly carried out.

## Chapter V National Security GuaranteesArticle 69: The state shall improve the national security assurance system and enhance its capacity to safeguard national security.

Article 70: The state shall improve the legal system for national security and promote the rule of law in national security.

Article 71: The state shall increase investment in all aspects of national security construction to ensure the funds and equipment required for national security work.

Article 72: Units tasked with stockpiling strategic national security materials shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations and standards, procure, store, maintain, and periodically rotate these materials to ensure their effectiveness and safety.

Article 73: Scientific and technological innovation in the field of national security shall be encouraged to leverage the role of science and technology in safeguarding national security.

Article 74: The state shall take necessary measures to recruit, cultivate, and manage specialized and exceptional personnel for national security work.

In accordance with the needs of national security work, the state shall, as provided by law, protect the identity and lawful rights and interests of personnel specifically engaged in national security work in relevant agencies, and enhance personal protection and resettlement support.

Article 75: National security authorities, public security authorities, and relevant military authorities may, in carrying out specialized national security work, adopt necessary means and methods as provided by law, and relevant departments and local authorities shall provide support and cooperation within their respective responsibilities.

Article 76: The state shall strengthen news dissemination and public opinion guidance on national security, conduct national security publicity and education activities in various forms, incorporate national security education into the national education system and the training system for civil servants, and enhance the national security awareness of all citizens.

## Chapter VI: Obligations and Rights of Citizens and Organizations

Article 77: Citizens and organizations shall fulfill the following obligations to safeguard national security:

(1) Comply with the relevant provisions of the Constitution, laws, and regulations concerning national security;

(2) Promptly report clues about activities that endanger national security;

(3) Truthfully provide evidence known to them concerning activities that endanger national security;

(4) Provide convenient conditions or other assistance for national security work;

(5) Provide necessary support and assistance to national security authorities, public security authorities, and relevant military authorities;

(6) Keep state secrets known to them;

(7) Other obligations stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

No individual or organization may engage in acts that endanger national security, nor provide any funding or assistance to individuals or organizations that endanger national security.

Article 78: State organs, people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other social organizations shall conduct education on safeguarding national security for their personnel, and mobilize and organize their personnel to prevent and stop acts that endanger national security.

Article 79: Enterprises and public institutions shall, in accordance with the requirements of national security work, cooperate with relevant departments in adopting relevant security measures.

Article 80: The actions of citizens and organizations in supporting and assisting national security work shall be protected by law.

If the personal safety of a citizen or their close relatives is endangered due to supporting or assisting national security work, they may request protection from public security authorities or national security authorities. Public security authorities and national security authorities shall, together with relevant departments, take protective measures in accordance with the law.

Article 81: Citizens and organizations suffering property losses due to supporting or assisting national security work shall receive compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations; in the event of personal injury or death, they shall receive pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 82: Citizens and organizations have the right to submit criticisms and suggestions to state organs regarding national security work, and the right to file complaints, accusations, and reports against illegal or derelict acts by state organs and their staff in national security work.

Article 83: When special measures restricting the rights and freedoms of citizens are necessary in national security work, they shall be carried out in accordance with the law and limited to the actual needs of safeguarding national security.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 84: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
