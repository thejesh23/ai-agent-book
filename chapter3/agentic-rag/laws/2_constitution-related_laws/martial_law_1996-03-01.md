# Martial Law Act of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 1, 1996

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 2** When an emergency situation occurs that seriously endangers the unity, security, or public safety of the state, such as turmoil, rebellion, or severe unrest, and extraordinary measures are necessary to maintain social order and protect the lives and property of the people, the state may decide to impose martial law.

**Article 3** Martial law for the whole country or for individual provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the proposal of the State Council; the President of the People's Republic of China shall issue the martial law order based on the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Martial law for parts of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be decided by the State Council, and the Premier of the State Council shall issue the martial law order.

**Article 4** During the period of martial law, to ensure the implementation of martial law and maintain public security and order, the state may, in accordance with this Law, make special provisions within the martial law area regarding the exercise of citizens' rights and freedoms stipulated by the Constitution and laws.

**Article 5** The people's government within the martial law area shall, in accordance with this Law, take necessary measures to restore normal social order as soon as possible, ensure the safety of people's lives and property, and guarantee the supply of basic necessities.

**Article 6** All organizations and individuals within the martial law area must strictly abide by the martial law order and the provisions for its implementation, and actively assist the people's government in restoring normal social order.

**Article 7** The state shall take effective measures to protect the lawful rights and interests of organizations and individuals who comply with the martial law order and the provisions for its implementation from infringement.

**Article 8** Martial law tasks shall be carried out by the People's Police and the People's Armed Police; when necessary, the State Council may propose to the Central Military Commission, which shall decide to dispatch the People's Liberation Army to assist in carrying out martial law tasks.

## Chapter II Implementation of Martial Law

**Article 9** Martial law for the whole country or for individual provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be organized and implemented by the State Council.

Martial law for parts of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be organized and implemented by the people's government of that province, autonomous region, or municipality; when necessary, the State Council may directly organize and implement it.

The organ responsible for organizing and implementing martial law is referred to as the martial law implementation organ.

**Article 10** The martial law implementation organ shall establish a martial law command structure, which shall coordinate the actions of relevant parties carrying out martial law tasks and uniformly deploy and implement martial law measures.

The People's Liberation Army carrying out martial law tasks shall be commanded by the military organ designated by the Central Military Commission, under the unified deployment of the martial law command structure.

**Article 11** The martial law order shall specify the geographical scope, start time, implementation organ, and other relevant matters.

**Article 12** After the emergency situation that necessitated the imposition of martial law under Article 2 of this Law has been eliminated, the martial law shall be lifted promptly.

The procedure for lifting martial law shall be the same as the procedure for deciding to impose it.

## Chapter III Measures for Implementing Martial Law

**Article 13** During the period of martial law, the martial law implementation organ may decide to take the following measures within the martial law area and may formulate specific implementation rules:

(1) Prohibit or restrict assemblies, processions, demonstrations, street speeches, and other crowd activities;
(2) Prohibit strikes, business closures, and class boycotts;
(3) Impose press controls;
(4) Impose controls on communications, postal services, and telecommunications;
(5) Impose controls on exit and entry;
(6) Prohibit any activities opposing martial law.

**Article 14** During the period of martial law, the martial law implementation organ may decide to take traffic control measures within the martial law area, restrict the entry and exit of persons into and out of the traffic control zone, and inspect the identification documents, vehicles, and articles of persons entering or leaving the traffic control zone.

**Article 15** During the period of martial law, the martial law implementation organ may decide to impose a curfew within the martial law area. During the curfew, anyone passing through streets or other public places in the area under curfew must carry their personal identification documents and a special pass issued by the martial law implementation organ.

**Article 16** During the period of martial law, the martial law implementation organ or the martial law command structure may take special control measures over the following articles within the martial law area:

(1) Weapons and ammunition;
(2) Controlled knives;
(3) Flammable and explosive materials;
(4) Hazardous chemicals, radioactive materials, highly toxic substances, etc.

**Article 17** As required for carrying out martial law tasks, the people's government at or above the county level within the martial law area may temporarily requisition houses, premises, facilities, means of transport, engineering machinery, etc., belonging to state organs, enterprises, institutions, social organizations, or individual citizens. In extremely urgent situations, on-site commanders of the People's Police, People's Armed Police, or People's Liberation Army carrying out martial law tasks may directly decide on temporary requisition, and the local people's government shall provide assistance. A requisition receipt shall be issued for any requisition.

The temporarily requisitioned items specified in the preceding paragraph shall be returned promptly after use or upon the lifting of martial law; if damage is caused by the requisition, the people's government at or above the county level shall provide appropriate compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 18** During the period of martial law, measures shall be taken to strengthen the security of the following units and premises within the martial law area:

(1) Leading state organs;
(2) Military organs and important military installations;
(3) Foreign embassies and consulates in China, representative offices of international organizations in China, and residences of state guests;
(4) Important news organizations such as radio stations, television stations, and the state news agency, and their important facilities;
(5) Public utilities and public facilities of vital importance to the national economy and people's livelihood;
(6) Airports, railway stations, and ports;
(7) Prisons, re-education through labor facilities, and detention centers;
(8) Other units and premises requiring enhanced security.

**Article 19** To ensure the supply of basic necessities for the people within the martial law area, the martial law implementation organ may take special control measures over the production, transportation, supply, and pricing of basic necessities.

**Article 20** Measures and methods taken by the martial law implementation organ in accordance with this Law to implement the martial law order that require public compliance shall be made public; during implementation, measures and methods that are no longer necessary shall be promptly announced as discontinued.

## Chapter IV Duties of Martial Law Duty Personnel

**Article 21** The People's Police, People's Armed Police, and People's Liberation Army carrying out martial law tasks are martial law duty personnel.

When carrying out martial law tasks, martial law duty personnel shall wear insignia uniformly prescribed by the martial law implementation organ.

**Article 22** In accordance with the regulations of the martial law implementation organ, martial law duty personnel have the authority to inspect the identification documents, vehicles, and articles of persons on public roads or in other public places within the martial law area.

**Article 23** In accordance with the regulations of the martial law implementation organ, martial law duty personnel have the authority to detain persons violating curfew regulations until the end of the curfew the following morning; and have the authority to search the person of the detainee and inspect the articles they carry.

**Article 24** In accordance with the regulations of the martial law implementation organ, martial law duty personnel have the authority to immediately detain the following persons:

(1) Those who are committing crimes endangering national security or disrupting social order, or are under strong suspicion of doing so;
(2) Those who obstruct or resist martial law duty personnel in carrying out martial law tasks;
(3) Those who resist traffic control or curfew regulations;
(4) Those who engage in other activities resisting the martial law order.

**Article 25** In accordance with the regulations of the martial law implementation organ, martial law duty personnel have the authority to search the person of detained individuals and to search the residences of criminal suspects and places suspected of harboring criminals, criminal suspects, or dangerous articles such as weapons and ammunition.

**Article 26** In the martial law area, under any of the following circumstances of crowd gathering, if persuasion is ineffective, martial law duty personnel may, in accordance with relevant regulations, use police equipment to forcibly stop or disperse the crowd, and forcibly remove the organizers and those who refuse to comply from the scene or immediately detain them:

(1) Illegally holding assemblies, processions, demonstrations, or other crowd activities;
(2) Illegally occupying public places or inciting destructive activities in public places;
(3) Storming state organs or other important units or premises;
(4) Disrupting traffic order or intentionally blocking traffic;
(5) Looting or destroying the property of state organs, organizations, enterprises, institutions, or individual citizens.

**Article 27** Martial law duty personnel shall promptly register and interrogate persons detained in accordance with the provisions of this Law; if it is found that continued detention is unnecessary, they shall be released immediately.

The procedures and time limits for detention and arrest during the period of martial law may not be subject to the relevant provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, but arrest must be approved or decided by the People's Procuratorate.

**Article 28** In the martial law area, under any of the following extremely urgent circumstances where the use of police equipment is insufficient to stop the situation, martial law duty personnel may use firearms and other weapons:

(1) When the life safety of citizens or martial law duty personnel is under violent threat;
(2) When detaining, arresting, or escorting a criminal suspect or offender encounters violent resistance, assault, or escape;
(3) When encountering violent seizure of weapons or ammunition;
(4) When an important guarded object or target is under violent attack, or there is an imminent danger of violent attack;
(5) When carrying out firefighting, rescue, or other major emergency tasks encounters severe violent obstruction;
(6) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations where the use of firearms and other weapons is permitted.

Martial law duty personnel must strictly comply with the regulations on the use of firearms and other weapons.

**Article 29** Martial law duty personnel shall abide by laws, regulations, and duty rules, obey orders, fulfill their duties, respect local ethnic customs and habits, and shall not infringe upon or harm the lawful rights and interests of citizens.

**Article 30** Acts performed by martial law duty personnel in accordance with the law in carrying out their tasks are protected by law.

Martial law duty personnel who violate the provisions of this Law, abuse their power, and infringe upon or harm the lawful rights and interests of citizens shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

**Article 31** If a severe riot suddenly occurs within a local area of an individual county or city, seriously endangering national security, public safety, and the safety of people's lives and property, and the state has not made a decision to impose martial law, the local provincial people's government, upon approval by the State Council, may decide to and organize the People's Police and People's Armed Police to implement traffic control and on-site control, restrict the entry and exit of persons into and out of the controlled area, inspect the identification documents, vehicles, and articles of persons entering or leaving the controlled area, forcibly disperse participants in the riot, forcibly remove them from the scene, conduct searches, and immediately detain the organizers and those who refuse to comply; if the forces of the People's Police and People's Armed Police are insufficient to maintain social order, the matter may be reported to the State Council to propose to the Central Military Commission, which shall decide to dispatch the People's Liberation Army to assist the local people's government in restoring and maintaining normal social order.

**Article 32** This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
