# Regulations of the People's Republic of China Concerning Consular Privileges and Immunities

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on October 30, 1990

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated for the purpose of defining the consular privileges and immunities of foreign consular posts in China and of the members of such consular posts, and of facilitating the efficient performance of their functions on behalf of their states in the consular districts.

Article 2 Consular officers shall be nationals of the sending state. The appointment of any person as a consular officer who is a national of China or a third state, or who is a permanent resident of China, shall be subject to the consent of the competent authorities of China. The competent authorities of China may withdraw such consent at any time.

Article 3 The consular post and its head shall have the right to use the national flag and national emblem of the sending state on the premises of the consular post, the residence of the head of the consular post, and on the means of transport used by the head of the consular post in the performance of his functions.

Article 4 The premises of the consular post shall be inviolable. The competent authorities of China may enter the premises of the consular post only with the consent of the head of the consular post, the head of the diplomatic mission of the sending state, or a person authorized by either of them. In case of fire or other disaster requiring prompt protective action, the consent of the head of the consular post may be presumed. The competent authorities of China shall take appropriate measures to protect the premises of the consular post against any intrusion or damage.

Article 5 The premises of the consular post and the residence of the head of the consular post shall be exempt from all dues and taxes, except such as represent payment for specific services rendered.

The fees and charges levied by the consular post in the course of its official duties shall be exempt from all dues and taxes.

Article 6 The archives and documents of the consular post shall be inviolable.

Article 7 Members of the consular post shall enjoy freedom of movement and travel within Chinese territory, except for areas the entry into which is prohibited or restricted by the regulations of the Chinese Government.

Article 8 The consular post may communicate freely with the Government of the sending state, the diplomatic mission of the sending state, and other consular posts of the sending state. For this purpose, the consular post may employ all appropriate means, including diplomatic or consular couriers, diplomatic or consular bags, and messages in code or cipher.

Article 9 The installation and use of a wireless transmitter-receiver by the consular post shall be subject to the consent of the Chinese Government. The importation of such equipment shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese Government.

Article 10 The consular bag shall not be opened or detained.

The consular bag may contain only official correspondence, documents, and articles for official use. It shall be sealed and bear visible external marks of its character. If the competent authorities of China have serious reason to believe that the bag contains something other than the above-mentioned items, they may request that the bag be opened in their presence by an authorized representative of the sending state. If the request is refused, the bag shall be returned to its place of origin.

Article 11 A consular courier shall be a national of the sending state and shall not be a permanent resident of China. He shall be provided with an official document issued by the competent authorities of the sending state certifying his status. The person of a consular courier shall be inviolable; he shall not be liable to arrest or detention.

An ad hoc consular courier shall be provided with an ad hoc document issued by the competent authorities of the sending state certifying his status, and shall enjoy the same immunities as a consular courier while entrusted with the consular bag.

The captain of a commercial aircraft or a commercial ship may be entrusted to transmit the consular bag, provided that he is in possession of an official document issued by the entrusting state indicating the number of bags. The captain shall not be considered a consular courier. The consular post may send a member of the consular post to receive the consular bag from, or deliver it to, the captain, subject to arrangements with the competent local authorities of the People's Government.

Article 12 The person of a consular officer shall be inviolable. The competent authorities of China shall take appropriate measures to prevent any attack on his personal freedom and dignity.

A consular officer shall not be liable to arrest or detention, except in the case of a grave crime and pursuant to a decision by the competent judicial authority.

A consular officer shall not be committed to prison, except in execution of a judicial decision of final effect.

Article 13 The residence of a consular officer shall be inviolable.

The papers and correspondence of a consular officer shall be inviolable.

The property of a consular officer shall be inviolable, except as provided in Article 14 of these Regulations.

Article 14 Consular officers and members of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post shall enjoy immunity from the judicial and administrative jurisdiction of China in respect of acts performed in the exercise of their functions. Immunity from jurisdiction in respect of acts performed outside the exercise of their functions shall be dealt with in accordance with bilateral treaties or agreements concluded between China and foreign states, or on the basis of reciprocity.

The immunity from judicial jurisdiction enjoyed by consular officers and members of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post shall not apply to the following civil actions:

(1) Actions relating to a contract not concluded expressly as a representative of the sending state;

(2) Actions relating to private immovable property situated in China, except that which is held on behalf of the sending state for the purposes of the consular post;

(3) Actions relating to succession in which they are involved as private persons;

(4) Actions relating to damage arising from an accident caused by a vehicle, vessel, or aircraft in China.

Article 15 Members of the consular post may be called upon to attend as witnesses in judicial or administrative proceedings. They shall not, however, be obliged to give evidence concerning matters connected with the exercise of their functions. They are entitled to decline to give evidence as expert witnesses with regard to the law of the sending state.

No coercive measures or penalty may be applied to a consular officer who declines to give evidence.

Members of the administrative and technical staff and members of the service staff of the consular post shall not decline to give evidence, except in matters connected with the exercise of their functions.

Article 16 The immunity from jurisdiction enjoyed by the persons concerned under these Regulations may be waived expressly by the Government of the sending state.

A person enjoying immunity from jurisdiction under these Regulations who initiates proceedings shall not be entitled to invoke immunity from jurisdiction in respect of any counterclaim directly connected with the principal claim.

Waiver of immunity from civil or administrative jurisdiction shall not be deemed to imply waiver of immunity in respect of the execution of the judgment. A separate express waiver by the Government of the sending state shall be necessary for the waiver of immunity in respect of the execution of the judgment.

Article 17 Consular officers and members of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post shall be exempt from all dues and taxes, except:

(1) Dues and taxes of a kind which are normally incorporated in the price of goods or services;

(2) Dues and taxes on private immovable property situated in China, except that used as premises of the consular post;

(3) Estate, succession, or inheritance duties, except that movable property in China of a deceased consular officer shall be exempt from such duties;

(4) Dues and taxes on private income having its source in China;

(5) Charges levied for specific services rendered.

Members of the service staff of the consular post shall be exempt from dues and taxes on the wages which they receive for their services in the consular post.

Article 18 Members of the consular post shall be exempt from all personal and public services and from military obligations.

Consular officers and members of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post shall be exempt from the obligations under the laws and regulations of China concerning the registration of aliens and residence permits.

Article 19 Articles for the official use of the consular post, articles for the personal use of a consular officer, and articles for the personal use of a member of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post, including articles for the establishment of his household, imported within six months of his first arrival, shall be exempt from customs duties and all other dues and taxes, except charges for storage, cartage, and similar services, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese Government.

The articles for personal use imported by a consular officer or a member of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post as mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall not exceed the quantities necessary for direct use.

The personal baggage of a consular officer shall be exempt from inspection, unless the competent authorities of China have serious reason to believe that it contains articles not falling within the exemptions provided for in the first paragraph of this Article, or articles the import or export of which is prohibited or restricted by the laws and regulations of China. Such inspection shall be conducted in the presence of the consular officer or his authorized representative.

Article 20 The import and export of firearms and ammunition for personal use by the consular post and its members shall be subject to the approval of the Chinese Government and shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese Government.

Article 21 The spouse and minor children of a consular officer, a member of the administrative and technical staff, or a member of the service staff of the consular post, if they form part of his household, shall enjoy the privileges and immunities respectively accorded to consular officers, members of the administrative and technical staff, and members of the service staff under Articles 7, 17, 18, and 19 of these Regulations, except that they are nationals of China or permanent residents of China.

Article 22 A consular officer who is a national of China or a permanent resident of China shall enjoy only the privileges and immunities provided for in these Regulations in respect of acts performed in the exercise of his functions.

A member of the administrative and technical staff or a member of the service staff of the consular post who is a national of China or a permanent resident of China shall not enjoy the privileges and immunities provided for in these Regulations, except that he shall have no obligation to give evidence concerning matters connected with the exercise of his functions.

Private servants shall not enjoy the privileges and immunities provided for in these Regulations.

Article 23 The following persons shall enjoy the necessary immunity and inviolability during their transit or stay in China:

(1) Consular officers of a foreign state accredited to a third state, and their spouses and minor children forming part of their households, who are passing through China;

(2) Foreign consular officers holding diplomatic visas issued by China or holding diplomatic passports of states which have mutual visa exemption agreements with China.

Article 24 Persons enjoying consular privileges and immunities shall:

(1) Respect the laws and regulations of China;

(2) Not interfere in the internal affairs of China;

(3) Not use the premises of the consular post and the residences of members of the consular post for purposes incompatible with the exercise of consular functions.

Article 25 Consular officers shall not carry on any professional or commercial activity for personal profit within Chinese territory outside the scope of their functions.

Article 26 If the consular privileges and immunities granted by a foreign state to the consular post of China in that state, to the members of such consular post, and to Chinese consular officers accredited to a third state who are passing through or temporarily in that state, differ from those granted by China to the consular post of that state in China, to the members of such consular post, and to the consular officers of that state accredited to a third state who are passing through or temporarily in China, the Chinese Government may grant them corresponding consular privileges and immunities on a reciprocal basis.

Article 27 Where an international treaty concluded or acceded to by China contains provisions relating to consular privileges and immunities that differ from those in these Regulations, the provisions of the international treaty shall prevail, except for those provisions on which China has declared reservations.

Where a bilateral treaty or agreement concluded between China and a foreign state contains provisions relating to consular privileges and immunities that differ from those in these Regulations, the provisions of such treaty or agreement shall prevail.

Article 28 For the purposes of these Regulations, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them:

(1) "Consular post" means any consulate-general, consulate, vice-consulate, or consular agency;

(2) "Consular district" means the area assigned to a consular post for the exercise of consular functions;

(3) "Head of consular post" means the consul-general, consul, vice-consul, or consular agent charged by the sending state with the direction of the consular post;

(4) "Consular officer" means any consul-general, deputy consul-general, consul, vice-consul, consular attache, or consular agent;

(5) "Member of the administrative and technical staff of the consular post" means any person employed in the administrative or technical service of the consular post;

(6) "Member of the service staff of the consular post" means any person employed in the domestic service of the consular post;

(7) "Member of the consular post" means consular officers, members of the administrative and technical staff, and members of the service staff of the consular post;

(8) "Private servant" means a person who is employed solely in the domestic service of a member of the consular post;

(9) "Premises of the consular post" means the buildings or parts of buildings and the land ancillary thereto used exclusively for the purposes of the consular post.

Article 29 These Regulations shall come into force as of the date of promulgation.
