# Organic Law of the People's Courts of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on July 1, 1979

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Organic Law of the People's Courts of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Second Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 2, 1983

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Organic Law of the Local People's Congresses and Local People's Governments of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on December 2, 1986

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Organic Law of the People's Courts of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 31, 2006

Revision adopted at the Sixth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate the establishment, organization, and functions and powers of the people's courts, and to ensure that the people's courts perform their duties in accordance with the law.

Article 2 The people's courts are the judicial organs of the state.

The people's courts, through adjudicating criminal, civil, administrative cases and other cases as prescribed by law, punish crimes, protect innocent persons from criminal prosecution, resolve civil and administrative disputes, protect the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations, supervise the lawful exercise of administrative powers by administrative organs, safeguard national security and social order, uphold social fairness and justice, maintain the unity, dignity, and authority of the national legal system, and ensure the smooth progress of socialist construction with Chinese characteristics.

Article 3 The people's courts shall be established in accordance with the Constitution, laws, and decisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 4 The people's courts shall, in accordance with the provisions of the law, independently exercise their adjudicative power, free from interference by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 5 In adjudicating cases, the people's courts shall apply the law equally to all, and no organization or individual shall be permitted to have privileges beyond the law; any form of discrimination is prohibited.

Article 6 The people's courts shall uphold judicial impartiality, base their decisions on facts and take the law as the criterion, comply with statutory procedures, protect the litigation rights and other lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations in accordance with the law, and respect and safeguard human rights.

Article 7 The people's courts shall practice judicial transparency, except as otherwise provided by law.

Article 8 The people's courts shall implement a judicial accountability system, establishing and improving a judicial power operation mechanism with unified powers and responsibilities.

Article 9 The Supreme People's Court shall be responsible and report its work to the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee. Local people's courts at various levels shall be responsible and report their work to the people's congresses and their standing committees at the corresponding levels.

The people's congresses and their standing committees at various levels shall supervise the work of the people's courts at the corresponding levels.

Article 10 The Supreme People's Court is the highest adjudicative organ.

The Supreme People's Court supervises the adjudicative work of local people's courts at various levels and special people's courts; people's courts at higher levels supervise the adjudicative work of people's courts at lower levels.

Article 11 The people's courts shall accept supervision by the people, ensuring that the people have the right to be informed, the right to participate, and the right to supervise the work of the people's courts in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Establishment and Functions and Powers of the People's Courts

Article 12 The people's courts are divided into:

(1) the Supreme People's Court;

(2) local people's courts at various levels; and

(3) special people's courts.

Article 13 Local people's courts at various levels are divided into higher people's courts, intermediate people's courts, and basic people's courts.

Article 14 The organization, case jurisdiction, and appointment and removal of judges of the people's courts established in the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps shall be governed by the relevant provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 15 Special people's courts include military courts, maritime courts, intellectual property courts, financial courts, etc.

The establishment, organization, functions and powers, and appointment and removal of judges of special people's courts shall be prescribed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 16 The Supreme People's Court shall adjudicate the following cases:

(1) cases of first instance over which it has jurisdiction as prescribed by law and those which it deems should be under its own jurisdiction;

(2) appeals and protests against judgments and rulings of higher people's courts;

(3) appeals and protests filed in accordance with the provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress;

(4) retrial cases initiated in accordance with the trial supervision procedure;

(5) death penalty cases submitted by higher people's courts for approval.

Article 17 Except for death sentences imposed by the Supreme People's Court in accordance with the law, all death sentences shall be submitted to the Supreme People's Court for approval.

Article 18 The Supreme People's Court may interpret issues concerning the specific application of law in adjudicative work.

The Supreme People's Court may issue guiding cases.

Article 19 The Supreme People's Court may set up circuit courts to adjudicate cases determined by the Supreme People's Court in accordance with the law.

Circuit courts are constituent parts of the Supreme People's Court. Judgments and rulings of circuit courts are judgments and rulings of the Supreme People's Court.

Article 20 Higher people's courts include:

(1) higher people's courts of provinces;

(2) higher people's courts of autonomous regions; and

(3) higher people's courts of municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 21 Higher people's courts shall adjudicate the following cases:

(1) cases of first instance over which they have jurisdiction as prescribed by law;

(2) cases of first instance submitted by lower people's courts for adjudication;

(3) cases of first instance designated by the Supreme People's Court for jurisdiction;

(4) appeals and protests against judgments and rulings of intermediate people's courts;

(5) retrial cases initiated in accordance with the trial supervision procedure;

(6) death penalty cases submitted by intermediate people's courts for review.

Article 22 Intermediate people's courts include:

(1) intermediate people's courts of cities divided into districts in provinces and autonomous regions;

(2) intermediate people's courts established in municipalities directly under the Central Government;

(3) intermediate people's courts of autonomous prefectures; and

(4) intermediate people's courts established in regions within provinces and autonomous regions.

Article 23 Intermediate people's courts shall adjudicate the following cases:

(1) cases of first instance over which they have jurisdiction as prescribed by law;

(2) cases of first instance submitted by basic people's courts for adjudication;

(3) cases of first instance designated by higher people's courts for jurisdiction;

(4) appeals and protests against judgments and rulings of basic people's courts;

(5) retrial cases initiated in accordance with the trial supervision procedure.

Article 24 Basic people's courts include:

(1) people's courts of counties and autonomous counties;

(2) people's courts of cities not divided into districts; and

(3) people's courts of municipal districts.

Article 25 Basic people's courts shall adjudicate cases of first instance, except as otherwise provided by law.

Basic people's courts shall provide professional guidance to the mediation work of people's mediation committees.

Article 26 Basic people's courts may, based on the region, population, and case circumstances, establish several people's tribunals.

People's tribunals are constituent parts of basic people's courts. Judgments and rulings of people's tribunals are judgments and rulings of basic people's courts.

Article 27 People's courts may, as required by adjudicative work, establish necessary specialized adjudication divisions. Intermediate people's courts and basic people's courts with a smaller number of judge posts may establish comprehensive adjudication divisions or no adjudication divisions.

People's courts may, as required by adjudicative work, establish comprehensive operational units. Intermediate people's courts and basic people's courts with a smaller number of judge posts may not establish comprehensive operational units.

Article 28 People's courts may, as required by work, establish necessary adjudicative support units and administrative management units.

## Chapter III Adjudicative Organizations of the People's Courts

Article 29 People's courts shall adjudicate cases by a collegial panel or by a single judge.

The scope of cases to be adjudicated by a collegial panel or by a single judge shall be prescribed by law.

Article 30 A collegial panel shall be composed of judges, or of judges and people's assessors, with an odd number of members of three or more.

A collegial panel shall have one judge serving as the presiding judge. When the president or a division chief participates in adjudicating a case, he or she shall serve as the presiding judge.

The presiding judge shall preside over the trial and organize the deliberation of the case, and shall have equal rights with other members of the collegial panel during the deliberation of the case.

Article 31 Decisions in collegial panel deliberations shall be made based on the opinion of the majority, and the opinion of the minority shall be recorded in the minutes. The minutes of the case deliberation shall be signed by all members of the collegial panel.

Article 32 Adjudicative documents formed from cases adjudicated by a collegial panel or by a single judge shall, after being signed by the members of the collegial panel or the single judge, be issued by the people's court.

Article 33 When a collegial panel adjudicates a case, the judges shall be responsible for the determination of facts and the application of law; when a single judge adjudicates a case, the single judge shall be responsible for the determination of facts and the application of law.

People's courts shall strengthen internal supervision; if there are illegal circumstances in adjudicative activities, they shall promptly investigate and verify, and handle them according to the law based on the illegal circumstances.

Article 34 People's assessors shall participate in adjudicating cases in collegial panels in accordance with the provisions of the law.

Article 35 Intermediate people's courts and above shall establish compensation committees to adjudicate state compensation cases in accordance with the law.

A compensation committee shall be composed of three or more judges, with an odd number of members, and decisions shall be made based on the opinion of the majority.

Article 36 People's courts at all levels shall establish adjudication committees. An adjudication committee shall be composed of the president, vice presidents, and several senior judges, with an odd number of members.

Meetings of the adjudication committee are divided into plenary meetings and specialized committee meetings.

Intermediate people's courts and above may, based on the specialties and work divisions of the members of the adjudication committee, convene specialized committee meetings for criminal adjudication, civil and administrative adjudication, etc., as required by adjudicative work.

Article 37 The adjudication committee shall perform the following functions:

(1) summarizing experience in adjudicative work;

(2) discussing and deciding on the application of law in major, difficult, and complex cases;

(3) discussing and deciding whether judgments, rulings, and mediation statements that have already taken legal effect by the court should be retried;

(4) discussing and deciding on other major issues concerning adjudicative work.

Interpretations by the Supreme People's Court on issues concerning the specific application of law in adjudicative work shall be discussed and adopted at a plenary meeting of the adjudication committee; the issuance of guiding cases may be discussed and adopted at a specialized committee meeting of the adjudication committee.

Article 38 Plenary meetings and specialized committee meetings of the adjudication committee shall be held with the attendance of more than half of its members.

Meetings of the adjudication committee shall be presided over by the president or a vice president authorized by the president. The adjudication committee shall practice democratic centralism.

When the adjudication committee holds a meeting, the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate at the same level or a deputy chief procurator authorized by the chief procurator may attend as a non-voting participant.

Article 39 If a collegial panel deems that a case needs to be submitted to the adjudication committee for discussion and decision, the presiding judge shall submit an application, which shall be approved by the president.

When the adjudication committee discusses a case, the collegial panel shall be responsible for the facts it reports, and the members of the adjudication committee shall be responsible for the opinions they express and the votes they cast. The collegial panel shall implement the decisions of the adjudication committee.

The decisions of the adjudication committee on the discussion of a case and the reasons therefor shall be disclosed in the adjudicative documents, except as otherwise provided by law for non-disclosure.

## Chapter IV Personnel Composition of the People's Courts

Article 40 The adjudicative personnel of the people's courts shall be composed of the president, vice presidents, members of the adjudication committee, and judges.

Article 41 The president of a people's court shall be responsible for the overall work of the court, supervise the adjudicative work of the court, and manage the administrative affairs of the court. Vice presidents of a people's court shall assist the president in his or her work.

Article 42 The President of the Supreme People's Court shall be elected by the National People's Congress; the vice presidents, members of the adjudication committee, division chiefs, deputy division chiefs, and judges shall be appointed or removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the President of the Supreme People's Court.

The chief judges and deputy chief judges of the circuit courts of the Supreme People's Court shall be appointed or removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the President of the Supreme People's Court.

Article 43 The presidents of local people's courts at various levels shall be elected by the people's congresses at the corresponding levels; the vice presidents, members of the adjudication committee, division chiefs, deputy division chiefs, and judges shall be appointed or removed by the standing committees of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels upon the recommendation of the presidents of the people's courts.

The presidents of intermediate people's courts established in regions within provinces and autonomous regions and in municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be appointed or removed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government upon the nomination of the council of chairs; the vice presidents, members of the adjudication committee, division chiefs, deputy division chiefs, and judges shall be appointed or removed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government upon the recommendation of the presidents of the higher people's courts.

Article 44 The term of office of the president of a people's court shall be the same as the term of office of the people's congress that elects him or her.

People's congresses at various levels have the power to recall the presidents of the people's courts elected by them. During the recess of a local people's congress, if the standing committee of that people's congress deems that the president of the people's court needs to be removed, it shall report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level for approval.

Article 45 Judges, adjudicative support personnel, and judicial administrative personnel of the people's courts shall be managed by category.

Article 46 A post system shall be implemented for judges. The number of judge posts shall be determined based on factors such as the number of cases, economic and social development, population size, and the level of the people's court.

The number of judge posts in the Supreme People's Court shall be determined by the Supreme People's Court in consultation with relevant departments. The number of judge posts in local people's courts at various levels shall be subject to total control and dynamic management within provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 47 Judges shall be selected from among persons who have obtained legal professional qualifications and meet other conditions prescribed by law. Newly appointed judges shall undergo a professional competence review by a judge selection committee. Judges of people's courts at higher levels shall generally be selected on a competitive basis from among judges of people's courts at lower levels.

The president shall possess professional legal knowledge and legal professional experience. Vice presidents and members of the adjudication committee shall be selected from among judges, procurators, or other persons who meet the qualifications for judges or procurators.

The duties, management, and guarantees of judges shall be governed by the provisions of the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 48 Judge assistants of the people's courts shall, under the guidance of judges, be responsible for adjudicative support tasks such as reviewing case materials and drafting legal documents.

Judge assistants who meet the conditions for appointment as judges may, after selection, be appointed as judges in accordance with the procedures for the appointment and removal of judges.

Article 49 Court clerks of the people's courts shall be responsible for adjudicative support tasks such as recording court proceedings.

Article 50 Judicial police of the people's courts shall be responsible for police matters such as court security, escorting and guarding persons.

Judicial police shall be managed in accordance with the People's Police Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 51 People's courts may, as required by adjudicative work, appoint judicial technical personnel to be responsible for matters related to adjudicative work.

## Chapter V Guarantees for the Exercise of Functions and Powers by the People's Courts

Article 52 No unit or individual may require a judge to engage in matters beyond the scope of statutory duties.

Where leading cadres or others interfere in judicial activities or meddle in the handling of specific cases, or where internal personnel of the people's court inquire about a case, the handling personnel shall fully and truthfully record and report the matter; if there are illegal or disciplinary circumstances, the relevant authorities shall pursue the responsibility of the actor based on the severity of the circumstances.

Article 53 The obligor shall perform the effective legal documents such as judgments and rulings made by the people's courts in accordance with the law; if the obligor refuses to perform, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 54 People's courts shall take necessary measures to maintain court order and the authority of adjudication. Illegal or criminal acts that hinder the people's courts from exercising their functions and powers in accordance with the law shall be subject to legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 55 People's courts shall implement a training system; judges, adjudicative support personnel, and judicial administrative personnel shall receive theoretical and professional training.

Article 56 The staffing of the people's courts shall be subject to special management.

Article 57 The funds of the people's courts shall be included in the fiscal budget in accordance with the principle of division of powers, to ensure the needs of adjudicative work.

Article 58 People's courts shall strengthen information technology development, utilize modern information technology to promote judicial transparency and improve work efficiency.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 59 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2019.
