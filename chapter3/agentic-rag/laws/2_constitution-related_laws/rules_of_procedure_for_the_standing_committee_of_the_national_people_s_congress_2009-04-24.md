# Rules of Procedure of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on November 24, 1987

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Rules of Procedure of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 8th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 24, 2009

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 These Rules are formulated in accordance with the Constitution, the Organic Law of the National People's Congress, and the practical experience of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 2 When the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress deliberates bills and decides on issues, it shall fully promote democracy and implement the principle of democratic centralism.

## Chapter II: Convening of Meetings

Article 3 Meetings of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall generally be held once every two months; when special needs arise, meetings may be convened on an ad hoc basis.

Meetings of the Standing Committee shall be convened and presided over by the Chairman. The Chairman may entrust a Vice-Chairman to preside over a meeting.

Article 4 A meeting of the Standing Committee may be held only if more than half of all the members of the Standing Committee are present.

Article 5 The Council of Chairmen shall draft the agenda for a meeting of the Standing Committee and submit it to the plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for decision.

If the agenda needs to be adjusted during a meeting of the Standing Committee, the Council of Chairmen shall make the proposal, subject to the approval of the plenary meeting of the Standing Committee.

Article 6 When a meeting of the Standing Committee is to be held, the date of the meeting and the main items proposed for discussion shall be notified to the members of the Standing Committee and persons attending the meeting as non-voting participants at least seven days before the meeting; for meetings convened on an ad hoc basis, such notice may be given on an ad hoc basis.

Article 7 When a meeting of the Standing Committee is held, the leading persons of the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall attend the meeting as non-voting participants.

The chairmen, vice-chairmen, and members of the special committees of the National People's Congress who are not members of the Standing Committee, the Deputy Secretaries-General of the Standing Committee, the directors and deputy directors of the working committees of the Standing Committee, and the responsible persons of relevant departments shall attend the meeting as non-voting participants.

Article 8 When a meeting of the Standing Committee is held, one chairman or vice-chairman of the standing committee of each province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall attend the meeting as a non-voting participant, and relevant deputies to the National People's Congress may be invited to attend the meeting as non-voting participants.

Article 9 When a meeting of the Standing Committee is held, plenary meetings, group meetings, and joint group meetings shall be convened.

For group meetings of the Standing Committee, the Council of Chairmen shall designate several conveners to preside over the meetings in rotation. The list of group assignments shall be drafted by the administrative office of the Standing Committee, submitted to the Secretary-General for approval, and adjusted periodically.

Joint group meetings of the Standing Committee shall be presided over by the Chairman. The Chairman may entrust a Vice-Chairman to preside over a meeting.

Article 10 When a meeting of the Standing Committee is held, members of the Standing Committee shall attend the meeting unless they have requested leave due to illness or other special reasons.

## Chapter III: Proposal and Deliberation of Bills

Article 11 The Council of Chairmen may submit to the Standing Committee bills that fall within the scope of the Standing Committee's functions and powers, which shall be deliberated at a meeting of the Standing Committee.

The State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and the special committees of the National People's Congress may submit to the Standing Committee bills that fall within the scope of the Standing Committee's functions and powers. The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to submit such bills to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation, or to first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then decide whether to submit them to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation.

Ten or more members of the Standing Committee jointly may submit to the Standing Committee bills that fall within the scope of the Standing Committee's functions and powers. The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to submit such bills to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation, or to first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then decide whether to submit them to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation; if a bill is not to be submitted to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation, the Council of Chairmen shall report this to the meeting of the Standing Committee or explain it to the proposer.

Article 12 The Council of Chairmen may, as needed, entrust a working committee or the General Office of the Standing Committee to draft a bill and provide explanations at a meeting of the Standing Committee.

Article 13 For a bill placed on the agenda of a meeting of the Standing Committee, the organ that proposed the bill, the relevant special committee, and the relevant working department of the Standing Committee shall provide relevant materials.

A bill on appointments and removals shall be accompanied by the basic information of the persons to be appointed or removed and the reasons for the appointment or removal; when necessary, the responsible person concerned shall attend the meeting to answer inquiries.

Article 14 The plenary meeting of the Standing Committee shall hear explanations on a bill.

After hearing the explanation on a bill at the plenary meeting of the Standing Committee, the bill shall be deliberated in group meetings and by the relevant special committee.

Article 15 For a draft law placed on the agenda of a meeting, after the Standing Committee hears the explanation and conducts preliminary deliberation, it shall be referred to the relevant special committee for deliberation and to the Law Committee for unified deliberation. The Law Committee shall submit a report on the results of its deliberation to the next or a subsequent meeting of the Standing Committee, and the deliberation opinions of other relevant special committees shall be distributed to the meeting of the Standing Committee.

For bills on decisions concerning legal matters and bills on amending laws, after deliberation by the Law Committee, a report on the results of deliberation may be submitted to the current meeting of the Standing Committee, or to the next or a subsequent meeting of the Standing Committee.

Article 16 Bills submitted for approval of final accounts and budget adjustment plans shall be referred to the Financial and Economic Committee for deliberation, and may also be referred to other relevant special committees for deliberation at the same time. The Financial and Economic Committee shall submit a report on the results of its examination to the meeting of the Standing Committee.

Bills submitted for approval of treaties and agreements shall be referred to the Foreign Affairs Committee for deliberation, and may also be referred to other relevant special committees for deliberation at the same time. The Foreign Affairs Committee shall submit a report on the results of its review to the meeting of the Standing Committee.

Article 17 Joint group meetings of the Standing Committee may hear and deliberate reports from special committees on their deliberation opinions on bills, and discuss the topics of the meeting.

Article 18 The responsible person of the organ that proposed a bill may provide supplementary explanations on the bill at plenary meetings or joint group meetings of the Standing Committee.

Article 19 If, before a bill placed on the agenda of a meeting of the Standing Committee is put to a vote, the proposer requests its withdrawal, deliberation on that bill shall be terminated with the consent of the Council of Chairmen.

Article 20 If a bill to be submitted to the plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for a vote involves major issues that require further study during deliberation, upon the proposal of the Chairman or the Council of Chairmen and with the consent of the joint group meeting or the plenary meeting, the vote may be postponed, and the bill shall be referred to the relevant special committee for further deliberation and a report.

Article 21 When the Standing Committee deems it necessary, it may organize an investigation committee on a specific issue and, based on the report of the investigation committee, adopt a corresponding resolution.

## Chapter IV: Hearing and Deliberating Work Reports

Article 22 The plenary meeting of the Standing Committee shall hear special work reports from the State Council, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate; hear reports on the implementation of the national economic and social development plan and the budget; hear the final accounts report and the audit work report; hear law enforcement inspection reports submitted by the law enforcement inspection teams of the Standing Committee; and hear other reports.

Article 23 After hearing a work report at the plenary meeting of the Standing Committee, deliberation may be conducted in group meetings and joint group meetings.

The Council of Chairmen may decide to refer the work report to the relevant special committee for deliberation and to provide opinions.

Article 24 When the Standing Committee deems it necessary, it may adopt a resolution on a work report.

## Chapter V: Inquiries and Interpellations

Article 25 When group meetings of the Standing Committee deliberate on a bill or a relevant work report, the relevant department shall be notified to send personnel to attend the meeting to hear opinions and answer inquiries.

When joint group meetings of the Standing Committee deliberate on a bill or a relevant work report, the relevant responsible person shall be notified to attend the meeting to hear opinions and answer inquiries.

Article 26 During a meeting of the Standing Committee, ten or more members of the Standing Committee jointly may submit a written interpellation to the Standing Committee against the State Council and its departments, the Supreme People's Court, or the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

Article 27 An interpellation must clearly state the object of the interpellation, the issues and content of the interpellation.

Article 28 The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to refer an interpellation to the relevant special committee for deliberation or to submit it to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation.

Article 29 The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether the responsible person of the organ being interpellated shall give an oral reply at a meeting of the Standing Committee or a meeting of the relevant special committee, or whether the organ being interpellated shall provide a written reply. If the reply is given at a meeting of a special committee, the special committee shall submit a report to the Standing Committee or the Council of Chairmen.

If the reply to an interpellation is in writing, it shall be signed by the responsible person of the organ being interpellated and distributed to the members of the Standing Committee and the relevant special committee.

When a special committee deliberates an interpellation, members of the Standing Committee who proposed the interpellation may attend the meeting and express their opinions.

## Chapter VI: Speeches and Voting

Article 30 When members of the Standing Committee speak at plenary meetings, joint group meetings, and group meetings, they shall focus on the topics determined for the meeting.

When a plenary meeting or joint group meeting of the Standing Committee schedules deliberation on relevant topics, members of the Standing Committee who wish to speak shall submit a request to the administrative office of the Standing Committee before the meeting, and the presiding officer of the meeting shall arrange for them to speak in order. Those who wish to speak on an ad hoc basis at plenary meetings and joint group meetings may do so only with the consent of the presiding officer. Those who wish to speak at group meetings may do so with the consent of the presiding officer.

The provisions of this Chapter shall apply mutatis mutandis to speeches by persons attending the meeting as non-voting participants.

Article 31 Speeches at plenary meetings shall not exceed ten minutes; at joint group meetings and group meetings, the first speech shall not exceed fifteen minutes, and a second speech on the same issue shall not exceed ten minutes. If a request is made in advance and approved by the presiding officer, the speaking time may be extended.

Speeches at meetings of the Standing Committee shall be recorded by the staff of the administrative office of the Standing Committee. After being checked and signed by the speaker, the records shall be compiled into meeting bulletins and archived.

Article 32 The adoption of a bill shall require the affirmative vote of more than half of all the members of the Standing Committee.

The result of the vote shall be announced on the spot by the presiding officer of the meeting.

Article 33 If a bill put to a vote has amendments, the amendments shall be voted on first.

Article 34 Bills on appointments and removals shall be voted on individually; they may also be voted on collectively depending on the circumstances.

Article 35 The Standing Committee shall vote on bills by secret ballot, a show of hands, or other methods.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 36 These Rules shall come into force on the date of their promulgation.
