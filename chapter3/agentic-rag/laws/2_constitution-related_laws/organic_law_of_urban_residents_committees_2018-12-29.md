# Organization Law of the Urban Residents Committee of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on December 26, 1989

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Organization Law of the Villagers Committee of the People's Republic of China and the Organization Law of the Urban Residents Committee of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the construction of urban residents committees, enable urban residents to handle their own affairs according to law, and promote the development of socialist democracy at the grassroots level in cities as well as the development of socialist material civilization and spiritual civilization in cities.

Article 2 The residents committee is a grassroots mass self-governing organization for residents to practice self-management, self-education, and self-service.

The people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or its agency, shall provide guidance, support, and assistance to the work of the residents committee. The residents committee shall assist the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or its agency, in carrying out its work.

Article 3 The tasks of the residents committee are:

(1) to publicize the Constitution, laws, regulations, and state policies; safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of residents; educate residents to fulfill their obligations as prescribed by law; protect public property; and carry out various forms of activities for the development of socialist spiritual civilization;

(2) to handle public affairs and public welfare undertakings for residents in the residential area;

(3) to mediate civil disputes;

(4) to assist in maintaining public security;

(5) to assist the people's government or its agency in carrying out tasks related to residents' interests, such as public health, family planning, special care and relief, and youth education;

(6) to reflect residents' opinions, demands, and suggestions to the people's government or its agency.

Article 4 The residents committee shall carry out community service activities that are convenient and beneficial to the people, and may establish relevant service undertakings.

The residents committee manages the property of the residents committee. No department or unit may infringe upon the property ownership of the residents committee.

Article 5 In areas where multiple ethnic groups live, the residents committee shall educate residents to help each other, respect each other, and strengthen ethnic unity.

Article 6 Based on the residential conditions and the principle of facilitating residents' self-governance, a residents committee shall generally be established within a range of 100 to 700 households.

The establishment, dissolution, and scale adjustment of a residents committee shall be decided by the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district.

Article 7 A residents committee shall consist of a director, a deputy director, and members, totaling five to nine persons. In areas where multiple ethnic groups live, the residents committee shall include members from minority ethnic groups.

Article 8 The director, deputy director, and members of a residents committee shall be elected by all eligible voters in the residential area or by representatives sent by each household; or, based on residents' opinions, may be elected by two to three representatives elected from each residents group. The term of office of a residents committee is five years, and its members may be re-elected.

All residents of the residential area who have reached the age of 18, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, family background, religious belief, education level, property status, or length of residence, have the right to vote and stand for election, except persons who have been deprived of political rights according to law.

Article 9 The residents meeting shall be composed of residents aged 18 or above.

The residents meeting may be attended by all residents aged 18 or above, or by representatives sent by each household, or by two to three representatives elected from each residents group.

A residents meeting may be held only if more than half of all residents aged 18 or above, household representatives, or representatives elected by residents groups are present. Decisions of the meeting shall be adopted by a majority vote of the attendees.

Article 10 The residents committee is responsible to the residents meeting and reports on its work.

The residents meeting shall be convened and presided over by the residents committee. A residents meeting shall be convened upon the proposal of more than one-fifth of residents aged 18 or above, more than one-fifth of households, or more than one-third of residents groups. On important issues involving the interests of all residents, the residents committee must submit them to the residents meeting for discussion and decision.

The residents meeting has the power to recall and by-elect members of the residents committee.

Article 11 When making decisions, the residents committee shall follow the principle of the minority being subordinate to the majority.

The residents committee shall adopt democratic methods in its work and shall not resort to coercion or command.

Article 12 Members of the residents committee shall abide by the Constitution, laws, regulations, and state policies; be fair in handling affairs; and be enthusiastic in serving residents.

Article 13 The residents committee may, as needed, establish sub-committees for people's mediation, public security, public health, etc. Members of the residents committee may concurrently serve as members of these sub-committees. For residents committees with a small number of residents, sub-committees may not be established, and the members of the residents committee shall be responsible for the relevant work through division of labor.

Article 14 The residents committee may be divided into several residents groups, and the group leaders shall be elected by the residents groups.

Article 15 The residents charter shall be formulated through discussion at the residents meeting, reported to the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or its agency, for the record, and supervised and implemented by the residents committee. Residents shall abide by the resolutions of the residents meeting and the residents charter.

The content of the residents charter shall not contravene the Constitution, laws, regulations, or state policies.

Article 16 Expenses required by the residents committee for handling public welfare undertakings in the residential area shall, after discussion and decision by the residents meeting, be raised from residents on a voluntary basis, or from benefiting units in the residential area with their consent; the income and expenditure accounts shall be published in a timely manner and subject to residents' supervision.

Article 17 The working funds and their sources of the residents committee, as well as the scope, standards, and sources of living allowances for members of the residents committee, shall be stipulated and allocated by the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or by the people's government at a higher level; with the consent of the residents meeting, appropriate subsidies may be provided from the economic income of the residents committee.

The office space for the residents committee shall be arranged by the local people's government through overall planning.

Article 18 Persons who have been deprived of political rights according to law shall be placed in residents groups, and the residents committee shall supervise and educate them.

Article 19 State organs, organizations, military units, enterprises, and institutions shall not participate in the residents committee of the locality where they are located, but shall support the work of the residents committee there. When the residents committee of the locality discusses issues related to these units and needs their participation in the meeting, they shall send representatives to attend and shall abide by the relevant decisions of the residents committee and the residents charter.

Employees and their family members, as well as military personnel and their accompanying family members, of the units mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall participate in the residents committee of the residential area; where their family members live in concentrated communities, a family committee may be established separately to undertake the work of the residents committee, under the guidance of the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or its agency, and the unit to which they belong. The working funds of the family committee, the living allowances for its members, and its office space shall be provided by the unit to which they belong.

Article 20 When relevant departments of the people's government of a city or a municipal district need the assistance of the residents committee or its sub-committees in their work, they shall obtain the consent of the people's government of a city not divided into districts or of a municipal district, or its agency, and make unified arrangements. Relevant departments of the people's government of a city or a municipal district may provide professional guidance to the relevant sub-committees of the residents committee.

Article 21 This Law applies to residents committees established in the seats of the people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns.

Article 22 The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate implementation measures in accordance with this Law.

Article 23 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 1990. The Organic Regulations of Urban Residents Committees adopted by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on December 31, 1954 shall be repealed simultaneously.
