# Several Provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on the Direct Election of Deputies to People's Congresses at or below the County Level

Adopted at the 26th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Fifth National People's Congress on March 5, 1983

<!-- INFO END -->

For the purpose of facilitating the implementation of the Electoral Law of the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses of the People's Republic of China, the following provisions are made on several issues concerning the direct election of deputies to people's congresses at or below the county level:

1. Election committees shall be established in counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, townships, ethnic townships, and towns. The members of the election committees of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall be appointed by the standing committees of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels. The members of the election committees of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be appointed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts.

The election committees shall set up working offices to handle the specific affairs of the election.

2. The functions and powers of the election committees are:

   (1) to preside over the election of deputies to the people's congresses at the corresponding levels;

   (2) to register voters, examine voter qualifications, publish the list of voters; to accept appeals against the list of voters and make decisions thereon;

   (3) to divide electoral districts for the election of deputies to the people's congresses at the corresponding levels and allocate the number of deputies to be elected in each electoral district;

   (4) to determine and publish the list of official candidates for deputies based on the opinions of the majority of voters;

   (5) to set the date of the election;

   (6) to determine whether the election results are valid and publish the list of elected deputies.

The election committees of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall guide the work of the election committees of townships, ethnic townships, and towns.

3. Persons suffering from mental illness who are unable to exercise the right to vote shall, upon confirmation by the election committee, not exercise the right to vote.

4. Persons who are detained and are under investigation, prosecution, or trial for counterrevolutionary cases or other serious criminal offenses shall, upon decision by the people's procuratorate or the people's court, have their right to vote suspended during the period of detention.

5. The following persons are permitted to exercise the right to vote:

   (1) those sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment, criminal detention, or public surveillance without additional deprivation of political rights;

   (2) those who are detained and are under investigation, prosecution, or trial, and for whom the people's procuratorate or the people's court has not decided to suspend the exercise of the right to vote;

   (3) those who are released on bail pending trial or under residential surveillance;

   (4) those who are undergoing reeducation through labor;

   (5) those who are under detention as a penalty.

The participation of the above-listed persons in the election shall be jointly decided by the election committee and the organ executing imprisonment, detention, custody, or reeducation through labor. They may vote in a mobile ballot box or entrust a relative who has the right to vote or another voter to vote on their behalf. Persons sentenced to criminal detention, under detention as a penalty, or undergoing reeducation through labor may also return to their original electoral district to vote on election day.

6. Where the seat of the people's government of a county or autonomous county is located in a municipal district, the staff of its subordinate organs, organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall participate in the election of deputies to the people's congress of the county or autonomous county and shall not participate in the election of deputies to the people's congress of the city or municipal district.

7. The staff of enterprises and institutions stationed in townships, ethnic townships, or towns that are not under the leadership of the people's government at or below the county level may participate only in the election of deputies to the people's congress at the county level and shall not participate in the election of deputies to the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town.

8. The size of an electoral district shall be determined on the basis that each electoral district elects one to three deputies.

9. If a voter is temporarily working, laboring, or residing outside his or her place of origin during the election period and cannot return to the original electoral district to vote, he or she may, with the approval of the election committee of the original place of residence, entrust in writing a relative who has the right to vote or another voter to vote on his or her behalf in the original electoral district.

If a voter has actually moved to another place but has not transferred his or her household registration, he or she may, after obtaining a certificate of voter qualification from the original electoral district, participate in the election in the electoral district of his or her current place of residence.

10. The number of candidates for deputies recommended by each voter (with the endorsement of three or more voters) shall not exceed the number of deputies to be elected in that electoral district.

All candidates for deputies recommended by voters, political parties, and people's organizations shall be included in the list of candidates for deputies, and the election committee shall not replace or add or subtract any of them.

If the list of official candidates for deputies is determined through a preliminary election, the candidates shall be listed in order of the number of votes they receive.
