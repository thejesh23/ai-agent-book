# Law of the People's Republic of China on the National Flag

Adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on June 28, 1990

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Law of the People's Republic of China on the National Flag adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to uphold the dignity of the National Flag, standardize its use, enhance citizens' sense of national identity, promote patriotism, and cultivate and practice the core socialist values.

Article 2 The National Flag of the People's Republic of China is the Five-Starred Red Flag.

The National Flag of the People's Republic of China shall be made in accordance with the Instructions for Making the National Flag promulgated by the Presidium of the First Plenary Session of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference.

Article 3 The standard dimensions of the National Flag shall be the five dimensions specified in the Instructions for Making the National Flag. Where flags of other dimensions are used under special circumstances, they shall be appropriately enlarged or reduced in proportion to the standard dimensions.

The dimensions and proportions of the National Flag and the flagpole shall be appropriate and compatible with the purpose of use, surrounding buildings, and the environment.

Article 4 The National Flag of the People's Republic of China is the symbol and emblem of the People's Republic of China.

Every citizen and organization shall respect and protect the National Flag.

Article 5 The National Flag shall be hoisted daily at the following places or premises of the following institutions:

(1) Tiananmen Square and Xinhua Gate in Beijing;

(2) The Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection of the Communist Party of China, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

The National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference;

(3) The Ministry of Foreign Affairs;

(4) Airports, ports, railway stations, and other border crossings for entry and exit, and border and coastal defense posts.

Article 6 The National Flag shall be hoisted on working days at the premises of the following institutions:

(1) All departments of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and local committees at all levels;

(2) All departments of the State Council;

(3) Standing committees of local people's congresses at all levels;

(4) Local people's governments at all levels;

(5) Local commissions for discipline inspection of the Communist Party of China and local commissions of supervision at all levels;

(6) Local people's courts and special people's courts at all levels;

(7) Local people's procuratorates and special people's procuratorates at all levels;

(8) Local committees of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference at all levels;

(9) Democratic parties and people's organizations;

(10) Relevant institutions of the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and relevant institutions of the Central People's Government in the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Schools shall hoist the National Flag daily, except during winter and summer vacations and on rest days. Kindergartens with the necessary conditions may hoist the National Flag in accordance with the provisions for schools.

Public cultural and sports facilities such as libraries, museums, cultural centers, art galleries, science and technology museums, memorial halls, exhibition halls, gymnasiums, and youth palaces shall hoist or display the National Flag on their opening days.

Article 7 On important festivals and commemorative days such as National Day, International Labor Day, New Year's Day, the Spring Festival, and National Constitution Day, state organs at all levels, people's organizations, and public activity venues such as large squares and parks shall hoist the National Flag; enterprises, public institutions, villagers' committees, residents' committees, and residential courtyards (buildings, communities) with the necessary conditions shall hoist the National Flag.

In ethnic autonomous areas, the National Flag shall be hoisted on the anniversary of the establishment of the autonomous area and on major traditional ethnic festivals.

When a constitutional oath ceremony is held, the National Flag shall be displayed at the oath-taking venue.

Article 8 The National Flag may be hoisted during major celebrations and commemorative events, large-scale cultural and sports activities, and large exhibitions.

Article 9 The State encourages citizens and organizations to use the National Flag and its design on appropriate occasions to express patriotic sentiments.

When citizens and organizations use the design of the National Flag on the internet, they shall comply with relevant internet management regulations and shall not harm the dignity of the National Flag.

The standard version of the design of the National Flag for internet use shall be published on the websites of the National People's Congress and the State Council.

Article 10 The method for hoisting and using the National Flag in diplomatic activities and at embassies, consulates, and other diplomatic missions abroad shall be prescribed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Article 11 The method for hoisting and using the National Flag by the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 12 The method for hoisting the National Flag on civilian vessels and foreign vessels entering China's territorial waters shall be prescribed by the competent transport department of the State Council.

The method for hoisting the National Flag on vessels performing tasks of entry-exit border inspection, border management, and public security shall be prescribed by the public security department of the State Council.

The method for hoisting the National Flag on vessels of the national comprehensive fire rescue team shall be prescribed by the emergency management department of the State Council.

Article 13 Where the National Flag is hoisted in accordance with Articles 5, 6, and 7 of this Law, it shall be raised in the morning and lowered in the evening.

Where the National Flag is required to be hoisted in accordance with this Law, it may not be hoisted in severe weather conditions.

Article 14 When hoisting the National Flag, a flag-raising ceremony may be held.

When a flag-raising ceremony is held, the national anthem shall be played or sung. During the raising of the National Flag, all persons present shall face the National Flag, stand at attention, and salute with their eyes or in the prescribed manner, and shall not engage in any act that harms the dignity of the National Flag.

A flag-raising ceremony shall be held daily at Tiananmen Square in Beijing.

Schools shall hold a flag-raising ceremony once a week, except during holidays.

Article 15 The National Flag shall be flown at half-mast to mourn the following deceased persons:

(1) The President of the People's Republic of China, the Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the Premier of the State Council, and the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;

(2) The Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference;

(3) Persons who have made outstanding contributions to the People's Republic of China;

(4) Persons who have made outstanding contributions to world peace or the cause of human progress.

When a national memorial ceremony is held, or when serious natural disasters, public health emergencies, or other unfortunate events cause particularly heavy casualties, the National Flag may be flown at half-mast nationwide, or in certain areas or specific venues.

The decision to fly the National Flag at half-mast in accordance with items (3) and (4) of the first paragraph and the second paragraph of this Article shall be made by the relevant department of the State Council or the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and reported to the State Council for decision.

The dates and venues for flying the National Flag at half-mast in accordance with this Article shall be decided by the state-established funeral committee or the State Council.

Article 16 When mourning ceremonies are held for the following deceased persons, their remains, coffins, or cinerary caskets may be covered with the National Flag:

(1) Persons specified in items (1) to (3) of the first paragraph of Article 15 of this Law;

(2) Martyrs;

(3) Other persons specified by the State.

When covering with the National Flag, the flag shall not touch the ground, and after the ceremony, the flag shall be retrieved and stored.

Article 17 When hoisting the National Flag, it shall be placed in a prominent position.

When marching in a column carrying the National Flag and other flags, the National Flag shall be placed in front of the other flags.

When the National Flag is hoisted together with other flags, the National Flag shall be placed in the center, at a higher position, or in a prominent position.

When hoisting the national flags of two or more countries simultaneously in foreign affairs activities, they shall be hoisted in accordance with the regulations of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or international practice.

Article 18 When raising or lowering the National Flag on a vertical flagpole, it shall be done slowly. When raising, the flag must be raised to the top of the pole; when lowering, the flag shall not touch the ground.

When flying the flag at half-mast, the flag shall first be raised to the top of the pole, then lowered to a point where the distance between the top of the flag and the top of the pole is one-third of the full length of the pole; when lowering, the flag shall first be raised to the top of the pole, and then lowered.

Article 19 No damaged, defaced, faded, or substandard National Flag shall be hoisted or used, and the National Flag shall not be hoisted or used upside down, inserted upside down, or in any other manner that harms its dignity.

The National Flag shall not be discarded at will. Damaged, defaced, faded, or substandard National Flags shall be retrieved and disposed of in accordance with relevant state regulations. After the conclusion of a large-scale mass event, the organizer shall retrieve or properly dispose of the National Flags used at the event venue.

Article 20 The National Flag and its design shall not be used as trademarks, granted as design patents, or used in commercial advertisements, nor shall they be used in inappropriate circumstances such as private funeral activities.

Article 21 The National Flag shall be an important part of patriotic education.

Primary and secondary schools shall educate students to understand the history and spiritual connotation of the National Flag, and to comply with the norms for hoisting and using the National Flag and the etiquette of flag-raising ceremonies.

News media shall actively publicize knowledge about the National Flag and guide citizens and organizations in the correct use of the National Flag and its design.

Article 22 The General Office of the State Council shall coordinate and oversee the management of the National Flag nationwide. Local people's governments at all levels shall coordinate and oversee the management of the National Flag within their respective administrative regions.

Market supervision and administration departments of people's governments at all levels shall supervise and manage the manufacture and sale of the National Flag.

The department designated by the people's government at the county level shall supervise and manage the hoisting, use, and retrieval of the National Flag within its administrative region.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the competent transport department of the State Council, and the relevant departments of the Central Military Commission shall supervise and manage the hoisting, use, and retrieval of the National Flag within their respective jurisdictions.

Article 23 Anyone who intentionally insults the National Flag of the People's Republic of China in a public place by burning, damaging, defacing, soiling, trampling, or other means shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are minor, the public security organ shall impose a detention of up to 15 days.

Article 24 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 1990.

Appendix:

Instructions for Making the National Flag

(Promulgated by the Presidium of the First Plenary Session of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference on September 28, 1949)

The shape and color of the National Flag are the same on both sides, and the five stars on the flag are opposite each other on both sides. For convenience, this document only takes the side with the flagpole on the left as the standard for explanation. For the side with the flagpole on the right, wherever "left" is mentioned in this document, it should be changed to "right," and wherever "right" is mentioned, it should be changed to "left."

(1) The flag surface is red and rectangular, with a ratio of length to height of three to two. On the upper left of the flag surface are five yellow five-pointed stars. One star is larger, with its circumscribed circle diameter being three-tenths of the flag's height, located on the left; the four stars are smaller, with their circumscribed circle diameters being one-tenth of the flag's height, arranged in an arc to the right of the larger star. The flagpole sleeve is white.

(2) The positions and drawing method of the five stars are as follows:

A. To facilitate determining the positions of the five stars, first divide the flag surface into four equal rectangles, then divide the upper left rectangle into ten equal parts vertically and fifteen equal parts horizontally.

B. The center point of the large five-pointed star is located at the position five parts from the top, five parts from the bottom, five parts from the left, and ten parts from the right within this rectangle. The drawing method is: take this point as the center and a radius of three equal parts to draw a circle. On this circumference, determine five points at equal distances, with one point located directly above the circle. Then connect every two points that are separated by one point among these five points, forming straight lines. The outer contour formed by these five straight lines is the required large five-pointed star. One tip of the five-pointed star points directly upward.

C. The center points of the four small five-pointed stars: the first point is at the position two parts from the top, eight parts from the bottom, ten parts from the left, and five parts from the right within this rectangle; the second point is at the position four parts from the top, six parts from the bottom, twelve parts from the left, and three parts from the right; the third point is at the position seven parts from the top, three parts from the bottom, twelve parts from the left, and three parts from the right; the fourth point is at the position nine parts from the top, one part from the bottom, ten parts from the left, and five parts from the right. The drawing method is: take the above four points as centers and a radius of one equal part each to draw four circles. On each circle, determine five points at equal distances, with one point in each circle located on the connecting line between the center point of the large five-pointed star and the center of the respective small circle. Then, using the same method as for constructing the large five-pointed star, construct the small five-pointed stars. Each of these four small five-pointed stars has one tip pointing directly toward the center point of the large five-pointed star.

(3) The standard dimensions of the National Flag are set as the following five types, which may be selected as appropriate by various sectors:

A. Length 288 cm, height 192 cm.

B. Length 240 cm, height 160 cm.

C. Length 192 cm, height 128 cm.

D. Length 144 cm, height 96 cm.

E. Length 96 cm, height 64 cm.
