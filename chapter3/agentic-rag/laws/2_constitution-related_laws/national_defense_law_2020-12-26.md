# National Defense Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 14, 1997

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revised at the Twenty-Fourth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 26, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to build and consolidate national defense, ensure the smooth progress of reform, opening up, and socialist modernization, and realize the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.

Article 2 This Law applies to military activities conducted by the State to guard against and resist aggression, stop armed subversion and secession, and defend national sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, security, and development interests, as well as activities related to the military in political, economic, diplomatic, scientific, technological, and educational fields.

Article 3 National defense is the safeguard of the nation's survival and development.

The State shall strengthen the building of the armed forces, reinforce border defense, coastal defense, air defense, and defense in other major security domains, develop defense-related scientific research and production, popularize national defense education among all citizens, improve the national defense mobilization system, and achieve the modernization of national defense.

Article 4 National defense activities shall adhere to the guidance of Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of Three Represents, the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, implement Xi Jinping's thinking on strengthening the military, uphold a holistic approach to national security, implement the military strategic guidelines for the new era, and build a consolidated national defense and strong armed forces commensurate with China's international status and adapted to the needs of national security and development.

Article 5 The State exercises unified leadership over national defense activities.

Article 6 The People's Republic of China pursues a defensive national defense policy, independently and self-reliantly builds and consolidates national defense, implements active defense, and adheres to a national defense by the whole people.

The State insists on the coordinated, balanced, and integrated development of economic construction and national defense construction, conducts national defense activities in accordance with the law, accelerates the modernization of national defense and the armed forces, and achieves the unity of a prosperous country and a strong military.

Article 7 It is the sacred duty of every citizen of the People's Republic of China to defend the motherland and resist aggression.

Citizens of the People's Republic of China shall fulfill their national defense obligations in accordance with the law.

All state organs and armed forces, all political parties and people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations shall support and participate in national defense construction in accordance with the law, perform their national defense duties, and accomplish national defense tasks.

Article 8 The State and society respect and give preferential treatment to military personnel, safeguard their status and legitimate rights and interests, carry out various forms of activities to support the military and give preferential treatment to the families of servicemen, and make the military a profession respected by the whole society.

The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall carry out activities to support the government and cherish the people, and consolidate the unity between the military and the government and between the military and the people.

Article 9 The People's Republic of China actively promotes international military exchanges and cooperation, maintains world peace, and opposes acts of aggression and expansion.

Article 10 Organizations and individuals that make contributions to national defense activities shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations.

Article 11 Any organization or individual that violates this Law and relevant laws, refuses to fulfill national defense obligations, or harms national defense interests shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

Public officials who abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in national defense activities shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II National Defense Functions and Powers of State Organs

Article 12 The National People's Congress, in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution, decides on issues of war and peace and exercises other functions and powers relating to national defense as provided for by the Constitution.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution, decides on the proclamation of a state of war, decides on general or partial mobilization, and exercises other functions and powers relating to national defense as provided for by the Constitution.

Article 13 The President of the People's Republic of China, in accordance with the decisions of the National People's Congress and the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, proclaims a state of war, issues mobilization orders, and exercises other functions and powers relating to national defense as provided for by the Constitution.

Article 14 The State Council leads and administers the undertaking of national defense construction and exercises the following functions and powers:

(1) Formulating relevant development plans and programs for national defense construction;

(2) Formulating relevant policies and administrative regulations for national defense construction;

(3) Leading and administering defense-related scientific research and production;

(4) Managing national defense funds and national defense assets;

(5) Leading and administering the work of national economic mobilization, civil air defense, national defense transportation, and related construction and implementation;

(6) Leading and administering the work of supporting the military and giving preferential treatment to the families of servicemen, and the work of guaranteeing the rights and interests of ex-servicemen;

(7) Jointly leading with the Central Military Commission the building of the militia, conscription work, and the management of border defense, coastal defense, air defense, and defense in other major security domains;

(8) Other functions and powers relating to the undertaking of national defense construction as provided for by law.

Article 15 The Central Military Commission leads the armed forces of the whole country and exercises the following functions and powers:

(1) Unifying the command of the armed forces of the whole country;

(2) Deciding on military strategy and operational guidelines for the armed forces;

(3) Leading and administering the building of the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force, formulating plans and programs, and organizing their implementation;

(4) Submitting proposals to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee;

(5) Formulating military regulations, issuing decisions and orders in accordance with the Constitution and laws;

(6) Deciding on the structure and organization of the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force, and stipulating the tasks and responsibilities of the departments of the Central Military Commission organs, theater commands, services and arms, and the People's Armed Police Force;

(7) Appointing, training, assessing, and rewarding or punishing members of the armed forces in accordance with laws and military regulations;

(8) Deciding on the weaponry system of the armed forces, formulating weaponry development plans and programs, and coordinating with the State Council in leading and administering defense-related scientific research and production;

(9) Managing national defense funds and national defense assets jointly with the State Council;

(10) Leading and administering the work of people's armed forces mobilization and reserve service;

(11) Organizing and conducting international military exchanges and cooperation;

(12) Other functions and powers as provided for by law.

Article 16 The Central Military Commission implements the system of the Chairman assuming overall responsibility.

Article 17 The State Council and the Central Military Commission shall establish a coordination mechanism to resolve major issues in national defense affairs.

The relevant departments of the central state organs and the Central Military Commission organs may, as circumstances require, hold meetings to coordinate and resolve issues concerning national defense affairs.

Article 18 Local people's congresses at all levels and the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level shall, within their respective administrative regions, ensure compliance with and implementation of laws and regulations concerning national defense affairs.

Local people's governments at all levels shall, within the limits of their authority as prescribed by law, administer conscription, militia, national economic mobilization, civil air defense, national defense transportation, protection of national defense facilities, and the work of guaranteeing the rights and interests of ex-servicemen and supporting the military and giving preferential treatment to the families of servicemen within their respective administrative regions.

Article 19 Local people's governments at all levels and the military organs stationed in their localities shall, as necessary, convene joint military-civilian meetings to coordinate and resolve issues concerning national defense affairs within their respective administrative regions.

Joint military-civilian meetings shall be jointly convened by the responsible persons of the local people's government and the responsible persons of the stationed military organ. The participants in the joint military-civilian meetings shall be determined by the conveners.

Matters decided at the joint military-civilian meetings shall be handled by the local people's government and the stationed military organ according to their respective duties and task divisions, and major matters shall be reported separately to higher authorities.

## Chapter III The Armed Forces

Article 20 The armed forces of the People's Republic of China belong to the people. Their tasks are to consolidate national defense, resist aggression, defend the motherland, protect the peaceful labor of the people, participate in national construction, and serve the people wholeheartedly.

Article 21 The armed forces of the People's Republic of China are under the leadership of the Communist Party of China. The organizations of the Communist Party of China in the armed forces shall conduct their activities in accordance with the Constitution of the Communist Party of China.

Article 22 The armed forces of the People's Republic of China are composed of the People's Liberation Army, the People's Armed Police Force, and the militia.

The People's Liberation Army is composed of active service units and reserve service units. Its mission in the new era is to provide strategic support for consolidating the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist system, for safeguarding national sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity, for protecting national overseas interests, and for promoting world peace and development. Active service units are the standing forces of the State, mainly responsible for defensive combat tasks and, as prescribed, for carrying out non-war military operations. Reserve service units shall conduct military training, perform defensive combat tasks, and carry out non-war military operations as prescribed; upon the issuance of a mobilization order by the State, they shall be converted into active service units by order of the Central Military Commission.

The People's Armed Police Force is responsible for guard duties, handling sudden social security incidents, preventing and dealing with terrorist activities, maritime rights protection and law enforcement, emergency rescue and disaster relief, defensive combat, and other tasks assigned by the Central Military Commission.

The militia, under the command of military organs, is responsible for combat readiness duties, carrying out non-war military operations, and performing defensive combat tasks.

Article 23 The armed forces of the People's Republic of China must abide by the Constitution and laws.

Article 24 The building of the armed forces of the People's Republic of China shall adhere to the path of strengthening the military with Chinese characteristics, persist in building the military through political work, reform, science and technology, and talent, and governing the military according to law, strengthen military training, carry out political work, improve support levels, comprehensively promote the modernization of military theory, military organizational forms, military personnel, and weaponry and equipment, build a modern combat system with Chinese characteristics, comprehensively enhance combat effectiveness, and strive to achieve the Party's goal of strengthening the military in the new era.

Article 25 The size of the armed forces of the People's Republic of China shall be commensurate with the needs of safeguarding national sovereignty, security, and development interests.

Article 26 Military service in the People's Republic of China is divided into active service and reserve service. The service system for servicemen and reservists shall be prescribed by law.

The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall implement a rank system in accordance with the law.

Article 27 The People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall implement a civilian personnel system for designated positions.

Article 28 The flag and emblem of the People's Liberation Army are the symbols and marks of the People's Liberation Army. The flag and emblem of the People's Armed Police Force are the symbols and marks of the People's Armed Police Force.

Citizens and organizations shall respect the flag and emblem of the People's Liberation Army and the flag and emblem of the People's Armed Police Force.

The designs, styles, and measures for the use and management of the flag and emblem of the People's Liberation Army and the flag and emblem of the People's Armed Police Force shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 29 The State prohibits any organization or individual from illegally establishing armed organizations, engaging in illegal armed activities, or impersonating military personnel or armed forces organizations.

## Chapter IV Border Defense, Coastal Defense, Air Defense, and Defense in Other Major Security Domains

Article 30 The land territory, territorial waters, and territorial airspace of the People's Republic of China are inviolable. The State shall build strong and stable modern border defense, coastal defense, and air defense, adopt effective defense and management measures, safeguard the security of the land territory, territorial waters, and territorial airspace, and protect national maritime rights and interests.

The State shall take necessary measures to safeguard the security of activities, assets, and other interests in outer space, the electromagnetic spectrum, cyberspace, and other major security domains.

Article 31 The Central Military Commission shall exercise unified leadership over defense work in border defense, coastal defense, air defense, and other major security domains.

Central state organs, local people's governments at all levels, and relevant military organs shall, within their prescribed scopes of functions and powers, be responsible for the management and defense work of border defense, coastal defense, air defense, and other major security domains, and jointly safeguard national security and interests.

Article 32 The State shall, based on the needs of defense in border defense, coastal defense, air defense, and other major security domains, strengthen the building of defense forces and construct national defense facilities such as combat, command, communication, measurement and control, navigation, protection, transportation, and support facilities. People's governments at all levels and military organs shall, in accordance with laws and regulations, ensure the construction of national defense facilities and protect the safety of national defense facilities.

## Chapter V Defense-Related Scientific Research and Production and Military Procurement

Article 33 The State shall establish and improve the defense-related science, technology, and industry system, develop defense-related scientific research and production, and provide the armed forces with advanced performance, reliable quality, complete supporting, and easy-to-operate and maintain weaponry and equipment, as well as other applicable military materials, to meet national defense needs.

Article 34 The defense-related science, technology, and industry shall adhere to the policy of combining military and civilian purposes, integrating peacetime and wartime needs, prioritizing military products, driving innovation, and achieving self-reliance and controllability.

The State shall make overall plans for the construction of the defense-related science, technology, and industry, adhere to state leadership, division of labor and cooperation, professional supporting, openness and integration, and maintain a moderate scale and a reasonable layout of defense-related scientific research and production capacity.

Article 35 The State shall fully utilize the superior resources of the whole society, promote the progress of defense-related science and technology, accelerate independent technological research and development, give play to the leading role of high and new technologies in the development of weaponry and equipment, increase technological reserves, improve the defense-related intellectual property system, promote the transformation of defense-related scientific and technological achievements, advance the sharing of scientific and technological resources and collaborative innovation, and enhance the capacity of defense-related scientific research and the technical level of weaponry and equipment.

Article 36 The State shall create a favorable environment and conditions, strengthen the cultivation of defense-related scientific and technological personnel, encourage and attract outstanding talents to enter the field of defense-related scientific research and production, and stimulate the innovation vitality of talents.

Defense-related scientific and technological workers shall be respected by the whole society. The State shall gradually improve the treatment of defense-related scientific and technological workers and protect their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 37 The State shall implement a military procurement system in accordance with the law to ensure the procurement and supply of weaponry, equipment, materials, engineering, and services required by the armed forces.

Article 38 The State shall exercise unified leadership and planned regulation over defense-related scientific research and production; pay attention to leveraging the role of the market mechanism, and promote fair competition in defense-related scientific research and production and military procurement activities.

The State shall, in accordance with the law, provide necessary guarantee conditions and preferential policies for organizations and individuals undertaking defense-related scientific research and production tasks and accepting military procurement. Local people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the law, provide assistance and support to organizations and individuals undertaking defense-related scientific research and production tasks and accepting military procurement.

Organizations and individuals undertaking defense-related scientific research and production tasks and accepting military procurement shall maintain confidentiality, complete tasks promptly and efficiently, ensure quality, and provide corresponding service guarantees.

The State shall, in accordance with the law, implement a quality responsibility system for the weaponry, equipment, materials, engineering, and services supplied to the armed forces.

## Chapter VI National Defense Funds and National Defense Assets

Article 39 The State shall guarantee the necessary funds for the undertaking of national defense. The growth of national defense funds shall be commensurate with national defense needs and the level of national economic development.

National defense funds shall be managed through a budget system in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Funds directly invested by the State for the building of the armed forces, defense-related scientific research and production, and other national defense construction, resources such as land allocated and used, and the weaponry, equipment, facilities, materials, and technological achievements formed thereby for national defense purposes shall constitute national defense assets.

National defense assets belong to the State.

Article 41 The State shall determine the scale, structure, and layout of national defense assets, and adjust and dispose of national defense assets based on the needs of national defense construction and economic construction.

The management institutions and the occupying and using units of national defense assets shall manage national defense assets in accordance with the law and give full play to the effectiveness of national defense assets.

Article 42 The State shall protect national defense assets from infringement and ensure the safety, integrity, and effectiveness of national defense assets.

No organization or individual may destroy, damage, or encroach upon national defense assets. Without the approval of the State Council, the Central Military Commission, or institutions authorized by the State Council or the Central Military Commission, the occupying and using units of national defense assets may not change the purpose of national defense assets for national defense. Technological achievements in national defense assets may, on the premise of adhering to the priority of national defense and ensuring security, be used for other purposes in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The management institutions or the occupying and using units of national defense assets that are no longer used for national defense purposes shall report for approval in accordance with regulations and be converted to other uses or disposed of in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII National Defense Education

Article 43 Through carrying out national defense education, the State shall enable all citizens to enhance their national defense awareness, strengthen their sense of urgency, master national defense knowledge, improve national defense skills, carry forward the spirit of patriotism, and fulfill their national defense obligations in accordance with the law.

Popularizing and strengthening national defense education is the common responsibility of the whole society.

Article 44 National defense education shall implement the policy of participation by the whole people, long-term persistence, and emphasis on practical results, and adhere to the principles of combining regular education with concentrated education, combining universal education with key education, and combining theoretical education with behavioral education.

Article 45 The competent department of national defense education shall strengthen the organization and management of national defense education, and other relevant departments shall do a good job in national defense education in accordance with their prescribed duties.

Military organs shall support relevant organs and organizations in carrying out national defense education and provide relevant convenience conditions in accordance with the law.

All state organs and armed forces, all political parties and people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations shall organize the carrying out of national defense education in their respective regions, departments, and units.

National defense education in schools is the foundation of national defense education for the whole people. Schools at all levels and of all types shall set up appropriate national defense education courses or add content on national defense education to relevant courses. Ordinary institutions of higher learning and senior high schools shall organize military training for students in accordance with regulations.

Public officials shall actively participate in national defense education, enhance their national defense literacy, and play an exemplary and leading role in national defense education for the whole people.

Article 46 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate national defense education into national economic and social development plans and guarantee the funds needed for national defense education.

## Chapter VIII National Defense Mobilization and State of War

Article 47 When the sovereignty, unity, territorial integrity, security, and development interests of the People's Republic of China are threatened, the State shall, in accordance with the Constitution and laws, carry out general or partial mobilization.

Article 48 The State shall incorporate national defense mobilization preparations into the overall national development plans and programs, improve the national defense mobilization system, enhance the potential for national defense mobilization, and increase the capacity for national defense mobilization.

Article 49 The State shall establish a system for strategic material reserves. Strategic material reserves shall be of an appropriate scale, stored safely, convenient for deployment, and replaced regularly to meet wartime needs.

Article 50 The leading body for national defense mobilization of the State, the central state organs, and the relevant departments of the Central Military Commission organs shall, according to their division of duties, organize the preparation and implementation of national defense mobilization.

All state organs and armed forces, all political parties and people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, other organizations, and citizens must complete the preparations for national defense mobilization in accordance with the provisions of the law; after the State issues a mobilization order, they must complete the prescribed national defense mobilization tasks.

Article 51 The State may, based on the needs of national defense mobilization, lawfully requisition or expropriate the equipment, facilities, means of transport, premises, and other property of organizations and individuals.People's governments at or above the county level shall provide fair and reasonable compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations for direct economic losses incurred by those whose property is requisitioned or expropriated.

Article 52 The state shall, in accordance with the Constitution, declare a state of war, take various measures to mobilize human, material, and financial resources, and lead all citizens in defending the motherland and resisting aggression.

## Chapter IX: National Defense Duties and Rights of Citizens and Organizations

Article 53 It is the honorable duty of citizens of the People's Republic of China to perform military service and join the militia in accordance with the law.

Military service agencies at all levels and grassroots people's armed forces organizations shall handle military service matters in accordance with the law, fulfill conscription tasks as ordered by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and ensure the quality of recruits. Relevant state organs, people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations shall, in accordance with the law, complete militia and reserve service tasks and assist in fulfilling conscription tasks.

Article 54 Enterprises, public institutions, and individuals undertaking national defense scientific research and production tasks or accepting military procurement shall provide weapons, equipment, materials, projects, or services that meet quality standards as required.

Enterprises, public institutions, and individuals shall, in accordance with state regulations, implement national defense requirements in construction projects closely related to national defense, and ensure the needs of national defense construction and military operations in accordance with the law. Management and operation units of transportation facilities such as stations, ports, airports, and roads shall provide priority services for the passage of military personnel, military vehicles, and vessels, and grant preferential treatment as stipulated.

Article 55 Citizens shall receive national defense education.

Citizens and organizations shall protect national defense facilities and shall not damage or endanger them.

Citizens and organizations shall observe confidentiality regulations, shall not disclose state secrets concerning national defense, and shall not illegally possess secret documents, materials, or other secret items related to national defense.

Article 56 Citizens and organizations shall support national defense construction and provide convenient conditions or other assistance for activities such as military training, combat readiness duties, defensive operations, and non-war military operations of the armed forces.

The state encourages and supports eligible citizens and enterprises to invest in national defense undertakings, protects the legitimate rights and interests of investors, and grants policy preferences in accordance with the law.

Article 57 Citizens and organizations have the right to make suggestions regarding national defense construction and the right to stop or report acts that harm national defense interests.

Article 58 When militia, reservists, and other citizens participate in military training, undertake combat readiness duties, defensive operations, or non-war military operations in accordance with the law, they shall fulfill their duties and obligations. The state and society shall ensure they receive corresponding treatment and provide pensions and preferential treatment in accordance with relevant regulations.

Citizens and organizations that suffer direct economic losses due to national defense construction and military activities may receive compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter X: Duties and Rights of Military Personnel

Article 59 Military personnel must be loyal to the motherland and the Communist Party of China, fulfill their duties, fight bravely, fear no sacrifice, and safeguard the security, honor, and interests of the motherland.

Article 60 Military personnel must exemplary abide by the Constitution and laws, observe military regulations, execute orders, and strictly maintain discipline.

Article 61 Military personnel shall carry forward the fine traditions of the People's Army, love the people, protect the people, actively participate in socialist modernization, and complete tasks such as disaster relief and rescue.

Article 62 Military personnel shall be respected by the whole society.

The state shall establish a system of honors and commendations for military personnel.

The state shall take effective measures to protect the honor and personal dignity of military personnel, and provide special protection for the marriages of military personnel in accordance with the law.

The lawful performance of duties by military personnel shall be protected by law.

Article 63 The state and society shall give preferential treatment to military personnel.

The state shall establish a system for ensuring the treatment of military personnel that is compatible with the military profession and coordinated with the development of the national economy.

Article 64 The state shall establish a security system for retired military personnel, properly resettle them, and protect their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 65 The state and society shall provide pensions and preferential treatment to disabled military personnel, and grant special guarantees for their living and medical care in accordance with the law.

After disabled military personnel who are disabled or become ill due to war or duty retire from active service, people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly accept and resettle them, and ensure that their living standards are not lower than the local average.

Article 66 The state and society shall give preferential treatment to the family members of military personnel, and provide pensions and preferential treatment to the family members of martyrs and military personnel who have died in the line of duty or from illness.

## Chapter XI: Foreign Military Relations

Article 67 The People's Republic of China adheres to the Five Principles of Mutual Respect for Sovereignty and Territorial Integrity, Mutual Non-Aggression, Non-Interference in Each Other's Internal Affairs, Equality and Mutual Benefit, and Peaceful Coexistence. It upholds the international system centered on the United Nations and the international order based on international law, adheres to the concept of common, comprehensive, cooperative, and sustainable security, promotes the building of a community with a shared future for mankind, and independently handles foreign military relations and conducts military exchanges and cooperation.

Article 68 The People's Republic of China follows the basic norms of international relations based on the purposes and principles of the United Nations Charter, uses armed forces in accordance with relevant national laws to protect the safety of Chinese citizens, organizations, institutions, and facilities overseas, participates in activities such as UN peacekeeping, international rescue, maritime escort, joint exercises and training, and counter-terrorism, fulfills international security obligations, and safeguards national overseas interests.

Article 69 The People's Republic of China supports military-related activities carried out by the international community that are conducive to maintaining world and regional peace, security, and stability. It supports the efforts of the international community to justly and reasonably resolve international disputes and to promote international arms control, disarmament, and non-proliferation. It participates in multilateral security dialogues and negotiations, and promotes the formulation of universally accepted, fair, and reasonable international rules.

Article 70 The People's Republic of China shall comply with relevant treaties and agreements concluded or acceded to with foreign countries and international organizations in its foreign military relations.

## Chapter XII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 71 For the purposes of this Law, "military personnel" refers to officers, non-commissioned officers, and enlisted personnel serving in the People's Liberation Army.

The provisions of this Law concerning military personnel shall apply to the People's Armed Police.

Article 72 The defense of the Special Administrative Regions of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the basic laws of the Special Administrative Regions and relevant legal provisions.

Article 73 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2021.
