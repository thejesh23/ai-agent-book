## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to improve the organization and working systems of local people's congresses and local people's governments, to ensure and regulate the exercise of their functions and powers, to uphold and improve the system of people's congresses, and to ensure that the people are the masters of the country.

Article 2 Local people's congresses are local organs of state power.

The standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level are the permanent organs of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels.

Local people's governments are the executive organs of local organs of state power and are local state administrative organs.

Article 3 Local people's congresses, the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level, and local people's governments adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, are guided by Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of the "Three Represents," the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, and exercise their functions and powers in accordance with the Constitution and laws.

Article 4 Local people's congresses, the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level, and local people's governments adhere to a people-centered approach, uphold and develop whole-process people's democracy, maintain close ties with the people at all times, listen to their opinions and suggestions, serve the people, be accountable to the people, and accept the supervision of the people.

Article 5 Local people's congresses, the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level, and local people's governments follow the principle of giving full play to the initiative and enthusiasm of local authorities under the unified leadership of the central authorities, and ensure the implementation of the Constitution, laws, and administrative regulations within their respective administrative areas.

Article 6 Local people's congresses, the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level, and local people's governments practice the principle of democratic centralism.

Local people's congresses and the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level shall fully promote democracy and exercise their functions and powers collectively.

Local people's governments practice the system of responsibility of the chief executive. Major matters in government work shall be decided after collective discussion.

## Chapter II Local People's Congresses

### Section 1 Composition and Term of Office of Local People's Congresses

Article 7 People's congresses are established in provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, counties, autonomous counties, cities, municipal districts, townships, ethnic townships, and towns.

Article 8 Deputies to the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, and cities divided into districts are elected by the people's congresses at the next lower level; deputies to the people's congresses of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, townships, ethnic townships, and towns are directly elected by the voters.

The number of deputies to local people's congresses and the methods for their election are prescribed by the Electoral Law. There shall be an appropriate number of deputies representing the minority nationalities within each administrative area.

Article 9 The term of office of each local people's congress is five years.

### Section 2 Functions and Powers of Local People's Congresses

Article 10 The people's congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, in light of the specific conditions and actual needs of its administrative area and provided that it does not contravene the Constitution, laws, and administrative regulations, formulate and promulgate local regulations, and report them to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for the record.

The people's congress of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture may, in light of the specific conditions and actual needs of its administrative area and provided that it does not contravene the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of its province or autonomous region, formulate local regulations within the limits of its authority as prescribed by law. Such regulations shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress of the province or autonomous region for approval before implementation, and shall be reported by that standing committee to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for the record.

The people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may engage in coordinated legislation in accordance with the needs of regional coordinated development.

Article 11 The local people's congress at or above the county level exercises the following functions and powers:

(1) Ensuring the observance and implementation of the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the resolutions of the people's congress at the next higher level and its standing committee within its administrative area, and ensuring the implementation of state plans and state budgets;

(2) Examining and approving the outline of the plan for national economic and social development, plans, budgets, and reports on their implementation within its administrative area; examining and supervising government debt; and supervising the management of state-owned assets by the people's government at the corresponding level;

(3) Discussing and deciding on major matters and projects concerning political, economic, educational, scientific, cultural, public health, ecological and environmental protection, natural resources, urban and rural development, civil affairs, social security, and ethnic affairs within its administrative area;

(4) Electing the members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(5) Electing the governor and deputy governors of the province, the chairman and vice-chairmen of the autonomous region, the mayor and deputy mayors of the municipality, the prefect and deputy prefects of the autonomous prefecture, the county head and deputy county heads, and the district head and deputy district heads;

(6) Electing the director of the supervisory commission at the corresponding level, the president of the people's court, and the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate; the election of the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate shall be reported to the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate at the next higher level for submission to the standing committee of the people's congress at that level for approval;

(7) Electing deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level;

(8) Hearing and deliberating the work reports of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(9) Hearing and deliberating the work reports of the people's government, the people's court, and the people's procuratorate at the corresponding level;

(10) Altering or annulling inappropriate resolutions of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(11) Annulling inappropriate decisions and orders of the people's government at the corresponding level;

(12) Protecting socialist property owned by the whole people and property collectively owned by working people, protecting citizens' lawful private property, maintaining social order, and safeguarding citizens' personal rights, democratic rights, and other rights;

(13) Protecting the lawful rights and interests of various economic organizations;

(14) Fostering a strong sense of community for the Chinese nation, promoting extensive exchanges, interactions, and integration among all ethnic groups, and safeguarding the lawful rights and interests of minority nationalities;

(15) Safeguarding the rights of women, including equality between men and women, equal pay for equal work, and freedom of marriage, as granted by the Constitution and laws.

Article 12 The people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town exercises the following functions and powers:

(1) Ensuring the observance and implementation of the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the resolutions of the people's congress at the next higher level and its standing committee within its administrative area;

(2) Adopting and issuing resolutions within the limits of its authority;

(3) Deciding on construction plans and projects for economic, cultural, and public undertakings within its administrative area in accordance with state plans;

(4) Examining and approving the budget and the report on its implementation within its administrative area, supervising the implementation of the budget at the corresponding level, examining and approving the adjusted budget plan, and examining and approving the final accounts at the corresponding level;

(5) Deciding on the implementation plans for civil affairs work within its administrative area;

(6) Electing the chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(7) Electing the township head and deputy township heads, and the town head and deputy town heads;

(8) Hearing and deliberating the work reports of the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town;

(9) Hearing and deliberating the work reports of the presidium of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town;

(10) Annulling inappropriate decisions and orders of the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town;

(11) Protecting socialist property owned by the whole people and property collectively owned by working people, protecting citizens' lawful private property, maintaining social order, and safeguarding citizens' personal rights, democratic rights, and other rights;

(12) Protecting the lawful rights and interests of various economic organizations;

(13) Fostering a strong sense of community for the Chinese nation, promoting extensive exchanges, interactions, and integration among all ethnic groups, and safeguarding the lawful rights and interests of minority nationalities;

(14) Safeguarding the rights of women, including equality between men and women, equal pay for equal work, and freedom of marriage, as granted by the Constitution and laws.

When exercising their functions and powers, the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns where minority nationalities live in concentrated communities may adopt specific measures suited to the characteristics of the nationalities within the limits of their authority as prescribed by law.

Article 13 A local people's congress has the power to remove from office members of the people's government at the corresponding level. A local people's congress at or above the county level has the power to remove from office members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the director of the supervisory commission, the president of the people's court, and the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate elected by it. The removal of the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate shall be reported to the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate at the next higher level for submission to the standing committee of the people's congress at that level for approval.

### Section 3 Convening of Sessions of Local People's Congresses

Article 14 A session of a local people's congress shall be convened at least once a year. A session of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town shall generally be convened twice a year. The date for convening the session shall be decided by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town, and shall be announced.

In special circumstances, the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level or the presidium of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town may decide to convene the session appropriately earlier or later. If the date for convening the session earlier or later cannot be decided at the current session, the standing committee or its authorized council of chairpersons, or the presidium of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town, may decide separately and announce it.

If the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level or the presidium of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town deems it necessary, or if more than one-fifth of the deputies propose it, an interim session of the people's congress at the corresponding level may be convened.

A session of a local people's congress may be held only when more than two-thirds of the deputies are present.

Article 15 Sessions of a local people's congress at or above the county level are convened by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 16 When convening a session, a local people's congress shall reasonably arrange the duration and agenda of the session to improve the quality and efficiency of deliberations.

Article 17 Before each session of a local people's congress at or above the county level, a preparatory meeting shall be held to elect the presidium and a secretary-general of the current session and to adopt the agenda of the current session and other decisions on preparatory matters.

The preparatory meeting shall be presided over by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level. The preparatory meeting for the first session of each people's congress shall be presided over by the standing committee of the preceding people's congress at the corresponding level.

When a session of a local people's congress at or above the county level is held, it shall be presided over by the presidium.

A session of a local people's congress at or above the county level shall have several deputy secretaries-general, whose candidates shall be decided by the presidium.

Article 18 The people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town shall have a chairman and may have one or two vice-chairmen. The chairman and vice-chairmen shall be elected by the people's congress at the corresponding level from among its deputies, and their term of office shall be the same as that of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

The chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town shall not hold any post in a state administrative organ; if they hold such a post, they must resign from the posts of chairman or vice-chairmen to the people's congress at the corresponding level.

The chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town shall, when the people's congress at the corresponding level is not in session, be responsible for maintaining contact with the deputies of the people's congress at the corresponding level, organizing deputies to carry out activities according to the arrangements of the presidium, reflecting the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies and the masses regarding the work of the people's government at the corresponding level, and handling the day-to-day work of the presidium.

Article 19 When a session of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town is held, a presidium shall be elected. The presidium shall preside over the session and be responsible for convening the next session of the people's congress at the corresponding level. The chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town shall be members of the presidium.

When the people's congress at the corresponding level is not in session, the presidium shall, each year, select a number of issues that are of immediate concern to the people in the locality and of general public concern, and shall, in a planned manner, arrange for deputies to hear and discuss special work reports of the people's government at the corresponding level, inspect the implementation of laws and regulations, and conduct observation and research activities; it shall also hear and reflect the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies and the masses regarding the work of the people's government at the corresponding level. The work of the presidium when the congress is not in session shall be reported to the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 20 The first session of each local people's congress shall be convened within two months after the completion of the election of deputies to the current people's congress by the standing committee of the preceding people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the preceding people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town.

Article 21 Members of the people's government at or above the county level, the director of the supervisory commission, the president of the people's court, and the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate at the corresponding level, and leading personnel of the people's government at the township level, shall attend the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level as non-voting participants; responsible persons of other relevant organs and organizations at or above the county level may, upon decision by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, attend the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level as non-voting participants.

Article 22 When a session of a local people's congress is held, the presidium, the standing committee, the special committees, and the people's government at the corresponding level may submit bills to the people's congress at the corresponding level that fall within the scope of its functions and powers. The presidium shall decide whether to submit such bills to the session of the people's congress for deliberation, or to refer them to the relevant special committees for deliberation and the submission of reports, and then the presidium shall deliberate and decide whether to submit them to the session for a vote.

Ten or more deputies to a local people's congress at or above the county level, or five or more deputies to the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town, may jointly submit bills to the people's congress at the corresponding level that fall within the scope of its functions and powers. The presidium shall decide whether to place such bills on the agenda of the session, or first refer them to the relevant special committees for deliberation and the submission of opinions on whether to place them on the agenda, and then the presidium shall decide whether to place them on the agenda.

If a bill that has been placed on the agenda of the session is withdrawn by the proposer before it is put to a vote, the deliberation of the bill by the session shall be terminated with the consent of the presidium.

Article 23 When a local people's congress is deliberating a bill, deputies may address inquiries to the relevant local state organs, and the relevant organs shall send personnel to provide explanations.

Article 24 When a session of a local people's congress is held, ten or more deputies may jointly submit a written question to the people's government at the corresponding level and its constituent departments, as well as to the supervisory commission, the people's court, and the people's procuratorate. The question must specify the organ to which it is addressed, the issue raised, and the content of the question.

The presidium shall decide to refer the question to the organ questioned, which shall give an oral reply at a meeting of the presidium, a plenary meeting of the session, or a meeting of the relevant special committee, or give a written reply. If the reply is given at a meeting of the presidium or a special committee, the deputies who raised the question have the right to attend the meeting as non-voting participants and express their opinions; if the presidium deems it necessary, it may have the report on the reply to the question printed and distributed to the session.

If the question is replied to orally, the responsible person of the organ questioned shall attend the meeting to give the reply; if the question is replied to in writing, the reply shall be signed by the responsible person of the organ questioned and printed and distributed by the presidium to the session or to the deputies who raised the question.

Article 25 Elections and the adoption of resolutions by a local people's congress shall be decided by a majority vote of all the deputies.

### Section 4 Election, Recall, and Resignation of Members of Local State Organs

Article 26 Candidates for members of the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level, the chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town, the governor and deputy governors of a province, the chairman and vice-chairmen of an autonomous region, the mayor and deputy mayors of a municipality, the prefect and deputy prefects of an autonomous prefecture, the county head and deputy county heads, the district head and deputy district heads, the township head and deputy township heads, the town head and deputy town heads, the director of a supervisory commission, the president of a people's court, and the chief procurator of a people's procuratorate shall be nominated jointly by the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level or by deputies in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Thirty or more deputies to the people's congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, twenty or more deputies to the people's congress of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture, and ten or more deputies to the people's congress at the county level may, in writing, jointly nominate candidates for members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, leading personnel of the people's government, the director of the supervisory commission, the president of the people's court, and the chief procurator of the people's procuratorate. Ten or more deputies to the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town may, in writing, jointly nominate candidates for the chairman and vice-chairmen of the people's congress at the corresponding level and leading personnel of the people's government. Deputies elected from different electoral districts or units may deliberate and jointly nominate candidates.

The number of candidates nominated by the presidium and the number of candidates jointly nominated by each deputy and other deputies shall not exceed the number of persons to be elected.

The nominator shall truthfully introduce the circumstances of the nominated candidates.**Article 27** When electing the Chairperson and Secretary-General of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, the Chairperson of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, the principal leading person of a people's government, the Chairperson of a supervisory commission, the President of a people's court, and the Chief Procurator of a people's procuratorate, the number of candidates may be one more than the number to be elected, and a contested election shall be held; if only one candidate is nominated, a single-candidate election may also be held. When electing the Vice-Chairpersons of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, the Vice-Chairpersons of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, and the deputy leading persons of a people's government, the number of candidates shall be one to three more than the number to be elected; when electing members of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, the number of candidates shall be one-tenth to one-fifth more than the number to be elected. The specific number of candidates exceeding the number to be elected shall be stipulated in the election measures by the People's Congress at the corresponding level based on the number to be elected, and a contested election shall be held. If the number of nominated candidates conforms to the number of candidates exceeding the number to be elected as stipulated in the election measures, the Presidium shall submit the list to the deputies for deliberation and discussion before the election. If the number of nominated candidates exceeds the number of candidates exceeding the number to be elected as stipulated in the election measures, the Presidium shall submit the list to the deputies for deliberation and discussion, then conduct a preliminary election. Based on the order of the number of votes obtained in the preliminary election, and in accordance with the number of candidates exceeding the number to be elected stipulated in the election measures, the list of official candidates shall be determined, and the election shall be held.

When a local People's Congress at or above the county level holds a general election to elect the leading personnel of state organs at the corresponding level, the time for nominating and deliberating candidates shall not be less than two days.

**Article 28** Elections shall be conducted by secret ballot. Deputies may vote for, against, or abstain from voting for the determined candidates; they may also vote for any other deputy or voter.

**Article 29** When a local People's Congress elects the leading personnel of state organs at the corresponding level, if the number of candidates who receive more than half of the votes exceeds the number to be elected, those with the highest number of votes shall be elected. If the number of votes is tied and it is impossible to determine the elected persons, a second vote shall be taken for those with tied votes, and those with the highest number of votes shall be elected.

If the number of elected persons who receive more than half of the votes is less than the number to be elected, a separate election shall be held for the remaining vacancies. In the separate election, candidates may be determined based on the order of the number of votes obtained in the first vote, or candidates may be separately nominated and determined in accordance with the procedures stipulated in this Law. With the decision of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, the separate election for the remaining vacancies may be held at the current session of the People's Congress or at the next session.

When conducting a separate election for Vice-Chairpersons and members of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, Vice-Chairpersons of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, and deputy leading persons of a people's government, the number of candidates exceeding the number to be elected shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 27 of this Law, and a contested election shall be held.

**Article 30** When a local People's Congress conducts a by-election for the Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, Secretary-General, and members of its Standing Committee; the Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress; the Governor and Deputy Governors of a province; the Chairman and Vice-Chairmen of an autonomous region; the Mayor and Deputy Mayors of a city; the Prefect and Deputy Prefects of a prefecture; the County Mayor and Deputy County Mayors; the District Mayor and Deputy District Mayors; the Township Mayor and Deputy Township Mayors; the Town Mayor and Deputy Town Mayors; the Chairperson of a supervisory commission; the President of a people's court; and the Chief Procurator of a people's procuratorate, the number of candidates may be more than or equal to the number to be elected. The election measures shall be determined by the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

**Article 31** When a local People's Congress at or above the county level is in session, the Presidium, the Standing Committee, or one-tenth or more of the deputies jointly may submit a proposal for the recall of members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, members of the people's government, the Chairperson of the supervisory commission, the President of the people's court, or the Chief Procurator of the people's procuratorate. The proposal shall be submitted by the Presidium to the congress for deliberation.

When a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress is in session, the Presidium or one-fifth or more of the deputies jointly may submit a proposal for the recall of the Chairperson or Vice-Chairpersons of the People's Congress, the Township Mayor or Deputy Township Mayors, or the Town Mayor or Deputy Town Mayors. The proposal shall be submitted by the Presidium to the congress for deliberation.

The recall proposal shall state the reasons for the recall.

The person proposed for recall has the right to present a defense at a meeting of the Presidium or at a plenary session of the congress, or to submit a written defense. A defense presented at a Presidium meeting or submitted in writing shall be printed and distributed to the congress by the Presidium.

A recall proposal submitted to a local People's Congress at or above the county level shall be referred by the Presidium to the congress for deliberation and then submitted to a plenary session for a vote; or, upon the proposal of the Presidium and a decision by the plenary session, an investigation committee may be organized, and the next session of the People's Congress at the corresponding level shall deliberate and decide based on the investigation committee's report.

**Article 32** Members of the Standing Committee of a local People's Congress at or above the county level, members of special committees, leading personnel of the people's government, the Chairperson of the supervisory commission, the President of the people's court, and the Chief Procurator of the people's procuratorate may submit their resignations to the People's Congress at the corresponding level, and the congress shall decide whether to accept the resignations. When the congress is not in session, they may submit their resignations to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, and the Standing Committee shall decide whether to accept the resignations. After the Standing Committee decides to accept a resignation, it shall report to the People's Congress at the corresponding level for the record. The resignation of the Chief Procurator of a people's procuratorate must be submitted to the Chief Procurator of the people's procuratorate at the next higher level, who shall request approval from the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at that level.

The Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, the Township Mayor and Deputy Township Mayors, and the Town Mayor and Deputy Town Mayors may submit their resignations to the People's Congress at the corresponding level, and the congress shall decide whether to accept the resignations.

### Section 5: Committees of Local People's Congresses

**Article 33** The People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts may, as needed, establish a Legal Affairs Committee, a Financial and Economic Affairs Committee, an Education, Science, Culture and Public Health Committee, an Environment and Resources Protection Committee, a Social Development Committee, and other special committees as needed. The People's Congress of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district may, as needed, establish a Legal Affairs Committee, a Financial and Economic Affairs Committee, and other special committees.

Each special committee is under the leadership of the People's Congress at the corresponding level; when the congress is not in session, it is under the leadership of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

**Article 34** The candidates for the Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, and members of each special committee shall be nominated by the Presidium from among the deputies and approved by the congress. When the congress is not in session, the Standing Committee may appoint or remove individual Vice-Chairpersons and some members of the special committees, upon nomination by the Council of Chairpersons and approval by a meeting of the Standing Committee.

The term of office of each special committee is the same as the term of office of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, and it shall perform its duties until the next session of the People's Congress at the corresponding level elects a new special committee.

**Article 35** Under the leadership of the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee, each special committee shall carry out the following work:

(1) Deliberating on bills submitted by the Presidium of the People's Congress at the corresponding level or by its Standing Committee;
(2) Submitting to the Presidium of the People's Congress at the corresponding level or to its Standing Committee bills that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the People's Congress or its Standing Committee and are related to the committee, and organizing the drafting of draft bills;
(3) Undertaking the specific organization and implementation of work such as hearing and deliberating special work reports, conducting law enforcement inspections, and holding special inquiries as carried out by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level;
(4) In accordance with the work arrangements of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, hearing special reports from the working departments of the people's government at the corresponding level, the supervisory commission, the people's court, and the people's procuratorate, and making suggestions;
(5) Conducting investigations and research on issues within the scope of functions and powers of the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee that are related to the committee, and making suggestions;
(6) Studying and handling suggestions, criticisms, and opinions from deputies, and being responsible for supervising the handling of relevant suggestions, criticisms, and opinions;
(7) Handling other work assigned by the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee.

**Article 36** A local People's Congress at or above the county level may organize an investigation committee on a specific issue.

The Presidium, or one-tenth or more of the deputies jointly in writing, may propose to the People's Congress at the corresponding level the organization of an investigation committee on a specific issue. The proposal shall be submitted by the Presidium to a plenary session for a decision.

The investigation committee shall be composed of a Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, and members, nominated by the Presidium from among the deputies and submitted to a plenary session for approval.

The investigation committee shall submit an investigation report to the People's Congress at the corresponding level. The People's Congress may make corresponding resolutions based on the investigation committee's report. The People's Congress may authorize its Standing Committee to hear the investigation report of the investigation committee, and the Standing Committee may make corresponding resolutions, which shall be reported to the next session of the People's Congress for the record.

**Article 37** The Credentials Committee approved at the first session of each term of a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress shall exercise its functions and powers until the expiration of the term of that People's Congress.

### Section 6: Deputies to Local People's Congresses

**Article 38** The term of office of a deputy to a local People's Congress begins with the first session of that People's Congress for the term and ends with the first session of the next term of that People's Congress.

**Article 39** Deputies to local People's Congresses and members of their Standing Committees shall not be subject to legal liability for their speeches and votes at meetings of the People's Congress and the Standing Committee.

**Article 40** A deputy to a local People's Congress at or above the county level shall not be arrested or subjected to criminal trial without the permission of the Presidium of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, or, when the congress is not in session, without the permission of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level. If a deputy is detained for being caught in flagrante delicto, the public security organ executing the detention shall immediately report to the Presidium or the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

**Article 41** When deputies to local People's Congresses attend sessions of the People's Congress or perform their duties as deputies, the state shall provide them with necessary travel expenses and material conveniences or subsidies.

**Article 42** Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on various aspects of work submitted by deputies to a local People's Congress at or above the county level to the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee shall be referred by the working office of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level to the relevant organs and organizations for study and handling, and they shall be responsible for replying.

Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on various aspects of work submitted by deputies to a township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress to the People's Congress at the corresponding level shall be referred by the Presidium of the People's Congress at the corresponding level to the relevant organs and organizations for study and handling, and they shall be responsible for replying.

The handling status of suggestions, criticisms, and opinions from deputies to local People's Congresses shall be reported by the working office of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at or above the county level, or by the Presidium of the township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level or the township, ethnic township, or town People's Congress, and shall be made public.

**Article 43** Deputies to local People's Congresses shall maintain close ties with the voters of their electoral districts or the units that elected them, as well as with the people, listen to and reflect their opinions and demands, and fully play their role in developing whole-process people's democracy.

Deputies to the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts may attend by right of consultation the sessions of the People's Congress of the unit that elected them.

Deputies to the People's Congress of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, municipal district, township, ethnic township, or town shall maintain contact with voters on a division-of-labor basis. In residential areas or production units where there are three or more deputies, deputy groups may be organized.

Deputies to local People's Congresses shall report to the voters of their electoral districts or the units that elected them on their performance of duties.

**Article 44** Deputies to the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts are subject to the supervision of the units that elected them; deputies to the People's Congress of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, municipal district, township, ethnic township, or town are subject to the supervision of the voters.

The units that elected deputies to local People's Congresses and the voters have the right to recall the deputies they have elected at any time. The recall of a deputy must be approved by a majority vote of all deputies of the original electoral unit or by a majority vote of the voters of the original electoral district.

**Article 45** When a deputy to a local People's Congress is unable to serve as a deputy for any reason, a by-election shall be held by the original electoral unit or by the voters of the original electoral district.

## Chapter 3: Standing Committees of Local People's Congresses at or above the County Level

### Section 1: Composition and Term of Office of the Standing Committee

**Article 46** The People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, county, autonomous county, city, or municipal district shall establish a Standing Committee, which shall be responsible to and report on its work to the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

**Article 47** The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts shall be composed of a Chairperson, several Vice-Chairpersons, a Secretary-General, and several members, elected by the People's Congress at the corresponding level from among its deputies.

The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district shall be composed of a Chairperson, several Vice-Chairpersons, and several members, elected by the People's Congress at the corresponding level from among its deputies.

Members of the Standing Committee shall not hold positions in state administrative organs, supervisory organs, adjudicatory organs, or procuratorial organs; if they hold such positions, they must resign from their positions on the Standing Committee.

The number of members of the Standing Committee:

(1) For provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government: 45 to 75 persons; for provinces with a population exceeding 80 million, no more than 95 persons;
(2) For cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures: 29 to 51 persons; for cities divided into districts with a population exceeding 8 million, no more than 61 persons;
(3) For counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts: 15 to 35 persons; for counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts with a population exceeding 1 million, no more than 45 persons.

The number of members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress for each term of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be determined by the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with the preceding paragraph, based on the size of the population and the needs of the composition of the Standing Committee. The number of members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress for each term of an autonomous prefecture, county, autonomous county, city, or municipal district shall be determined by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with the preceding paragraph, based on the size of the population and the needs of the composition of the Standing Committee. Once the number of members of the Standing Committee for each term is determined, it shall not be changed during the term of that People's Congress.

**Article 48** The term of office of the Standing Committee of a local People's Congress at or above the county level is the same as the term of office of the People's Congress at the corresponding level. It shall exercise its functions and powers until the next session of the People's Congress at the corresponding level elects a new Standing Committee.

### Section 2: Functions and Powers of the Standing Committee

**Article 49** The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, when the People's Congress at the corresponding level is not in session, formulate and promulgate local regulations, provided they do not contravene the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the specific conditions and actual needs of the administrative region, and shall report them to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for the record.

The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture may, when the People's Congress at the corresponding level is not in session, formulate local regulations within the limits of their authority as prescribed by law, provided they do not contravene the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of the province or autonomous region, and the specific conditions and actual needs of the administrative region. Such regulations shall be implemented after approval by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region, and shall be reported by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for the record.

The Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may carry out collaborative legislation based on the needs of coordinated regional development.

**Article 50** The Standing Committee of a local People's Congress at or above the county level shall exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) Ensuring the observance and implementation of the Constitution, laws, administrative regulations, and the resolutions of the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee within its administrative region;
(2) Leading or presiding over the election of deputies to the People's Congress at the corresponding level;
(3) Convening sessions of the People's Congress at the corresponding level;
(4) Discussing and deciding on major issues and projects within its administrative region concerning political, economic, educational, scientific, cultural, public health, ecological and environmental protection, natural resources, urban and rural development, civil affairs, social security, and ethnic affairs;
(5) Based on the proposal of the people's government at the corresponding level, examining and approving adjustments to the plan for national economic and social development, the outline of the plan, and the budget at the corresponding level;
(6) Supervising the implementation of the plan for national economic and social development, the outline of the plan, and the budget within its administrative region; examining and approving the final accounts at the corresponding level; supervising the rectification of problems identified in audits; and examining and supervising government debt;
(7) Supervising the work of the people's government, supervisory commission, people's court, and people's procuratorate at the corresponding level; hearing and deliberating special work reports; organizing law enforcement inspections; conducting special inquiries, etc.; maintaining contact with deputies to the People's Congress at the corresponding level; and receiving complaints and opinions from the people concerning the above-mentioned organs and state personnel;
(8) Supervising the management of state-owned assets by the people's government at the corresponding level; hearing and deliberating reports from the people's government at the corresponding level on the management of state-owned assets;
(9) Hearing and deliberating reports from the people's government at the corresponding level on the annual environmental situation and the achievement of environmental protection targets;
(10) Hearing and deliberating work reports on the review of records and filings;
(11) Annulling inappropriate resolutions of the People's Congress and its Standing Committee at the next lower level;
(12) Annulling inappropriate decisions and orders of the people's government at the corresponding level;
(13) When the People's Congress at the corresponding level is not in session, deciding on the appointment or removal of individual Deputy Governors of a province, Deputy Chairmen of an autonomous region, Deputy Mayors of a city, Deputy Prefects of a prefecture, Deputy County Mayors, or Deputy District Mayors; when the Governor of a province, Chairman of an autonomous region, Mayor of a city, Prefect of a prefecture, County Mayor, District Mayor, Chairperson of a supervisory commission, President of a people's court, or Chief Procurator of a people's procuratorate is unable to hold office for any reason, deciding on an acting person from among the deputy leading persons of the people's government, supervisory commission, people's court, or people's procuratorate at the corresponding level, based on the nomination of the Council of Chairpersons; the decision on an acting Chief Procurator must be reported to the people's procuratorate at the next higher level and its Standing Committee for the record;
(14) Based on the nomination of the Governor of a province, Chairman of an autonomous region, Mayor of a city, Prefect of a prefecture, County Mayor, or District Mayor, deciding on the appointment or removal of the Secretary-General, Directors of Departments, Directors of Bureaus, Chairpersons of Commissions, and Section Chiefs of the people's government at the corresponding level, and reporting to the people's government at the next higher level for the record;
(15) Based on the nomination of the Chairperson of the supervisory commission, appointing or removing the Vice-Chairpersons and members of the supervisory commission;(16) In accordance with the provisions of the Organic Law of the People's Courts and the Organic Law of the People's Procuratorates, appoint and remove vice-presidents, chief judges, deputy chief judges, members of judicial committees, and judges of the People's Courts; appoint and remove deputy procurators-general, members of procuratorial committees, and procurators of the People's Procuratorates; and approve the appointment and removal of chief procurators of the People's Procuratorates at the next lower level. The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, based on the nomination of the Council of Chairmen, decide on the appointment and removal of the presidents of intermediate people's courts established within the prefectures of the province or autonomous region and within the municipality directly under the Central Government; and, based on the nomination of the chief procurator of the People's Procuratorate of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, decide on the appointment and removal of the chief procurators of branch procuratorates.

(17) During the recess of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, decide on the removal of individual vice-governors, vice-chairmen of autonomous regions, deputy mayors, vice-prefects, deputy county heads, and deputy district heads; decide on the removal of other members of the people's government at the corresponding level appointed by it, as well as deputy directors and members of supervisory commissions, vice-presidents, chief judges, deputy chief judges, members of judicial committees, and judges of people's courts, deputy procurators-general, members of procuratorial committees, and procurators of people's procuratorates, presidents of intermediate people's courts, and chief procurators of branch procuratorates.

(18) During the recess of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, hold by-elections for vacant deputies to the People's Congress at the next higher level and recall individual deputies.

When discussing the major matters and projects within its administrative region as specified in item (4) of the preceding paragraph, the Standing Committee may make decisions or resolutions, or may refer relevant opinions and suggestions to the relevant local state organs or units for study and handling. The relevant handling situation shall be reported to the Standing Committee in a timely manner.

### Section 3: Convening of Meetings of the Standing Committee

Article 51: Meetings of the Standing Committee shall be convened and presided over by the Chairperson, and shall be held at least once every two months. When special needs arise, an interim meeting of the Standing Committee may be convened. The Chairperson may entrust a Vice-Chairperson to preside over the meeting.

The responsible persons of the local people's government, supervisory commission, people's court, and people's procuratorate at or above the county level shall attend the meetings of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level as non-voting participants.

A meeting of the Standing Committee may be held only when more than half of all the members of the Standing Committee are present.

Resolutions of the Standing Committee shall be adopted by a majority vote of all the members of the Standing Committee.

Article 52: The Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the Local People's Congress at or above the county level may submit to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level proposals that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the Standing Committee, which shall be deliberated at a meeting of the Standing Committee.

The local people's government at or above the county level and the special committees of the People's Congress may submit to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level proposals that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the Standing Committee. The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to submit such proposals to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation, or to first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then submit them to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation.

Five or more members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts, or three or more members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the county level, may jointly submit to the Standing Committee at the corresponding level proposals that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the Standing Committee. The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to submit such proposals to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation, or to first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then decide whether to submit them to a meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation.

Article 53: During a meeting of the Standing Committee, five or more members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts, or three or more members of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the county level, may jointly submit a written question to the Standing Committee addressed to the people's government at the corresponding level and its working departments, the supervisory commission, the people's court, or the people's procuratorate. The question must specify the organ being questioned, the issue, and the content of the question.

The Council of Chairmen shall decide whether to refer the question to the organ being questioned for an oral reply at a plenary meeting of the Standing Committee or at a meeting of the relevant special committee, or for a written reply from the organ being questioned. When the reply is made at a meeting of a special committee, the members of the Standing Committee who submitted the question have the right to attend the meeting and express their opinions. When the Council of Chairmen deems it necessary, the report on the reply to the question may be printed and distributed to the meeting.

If the reply to the question is oral, the responsible person of the organ being questioned shall attend the meeting to give the reply. If the reply is written, it shall be signed by the responsible person of the organ being questioned and distributed to the meeting or to the members of the Standing Committee who submitted the question by the Council of Chairmen.

Article 54: The Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts shall be composed of the Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, and Secretary-General. The Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district shall be composed of the Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons.

The Council of Chairmen handles the important day-to-day work of the Standing Committee:

(1) Determining the duration of each meeting of the Standing Committee, drafting the proposed agenda for the meeting, and, when necessary, proposing adjustments to the agenda;
(2) Deciding to refer proposals and questions submitted to the Standing Committee to the relevant special committee for deliberation or to submit them to a plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation;
(3) Deciding whether to submit proposals, draft decisions, and draft resolutions to a plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for a vote, and, for those not submitted for a vote temporarily, proposing next steps for handling;
(4) Adopting the annual work plan of the Standing Committee, etc.;
(5) Guiding and coordinating the day-to-day work of the special committees;
(6) Other important day-to-day work.

Article 55: When the Chairperson of the Standing Committee is unable to perform duties due to health reasons or when the position is vacant, the Standing Committee shall elect one of the Vice-Chairpersons to act as the Chairperson until the Chairperson recovers or the People's Congress elects a new Chairperson.

### Section 4: Committees and Working Bodies of the Standing Committee

Article 56: The Standing Committee of the Local People's Congress at or above the county level shall establish a Credentials Committee.

The candidates for the chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members of the Credentials Committee shall be nominated by the Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee from among the members of the Standing Committee, and shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee.

Article 57: The Credentials Committee shall examine whether the election of deputies complies with legal provisions.

Article 58: The Council of Chairmen or one-fifth or more of the members of the Standing Committee jointly in writing may propose to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level the establishment of an investigation committee on a specific issue, which shall be decided by a plenary meeting.

The investigation committee shall be composed of a chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members, nominated by the Council of Chairmen from among the members of the Standing Committee and other deputies, and submitted to a plenary meeting for approval.

The investigation committee shall submit an investigation report to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level. The Standing Committee may make corresponding resolutions based on the report of the investigation committee.

Article 59: The Standing Committee shall, based on work needs, establish administrative offices and working bodies such as the Legislative Affairs Commission, the Budgetary Affairs Commission, and the Deputies Affairs Commission.

The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a province or autonomous region may establish working bodies in prefectures.

The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a municipal district or a city not divided into districts may establish working bodies in subdistricts. The working bodies shall be responsible for maintaining contact with deputies to the People's Congress within the subdistrict jurisdiction, organizing deputies to carry out activities, reflecting suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies and the public, handling supervision, election, and other tasks assigned by the Standing Committee, and reporting their work to the Standing Committee.

The Standing Committee of the People's Congress of a county or autonomous county may, by analogy with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, establish working bodies in subdistricts.

Article 60: The Standing Committee of the Local People's Congress at or above the county level, as well as the special committees and working bodies, shall establish and improve working mechanisms for members of the Standing Committee and the special committees and working bodies to maintain contact with deputies, support and ensure that deputies perform their duties in accordance with the law, expand deputies' participation in various tasks, and give full play to the role of deputies.

The Standing Committee of the Local People's Congress at or above the county level shall, through establishing grassroots contact points, deputies' liaison stations, and other means, maintain close ties with the people and solicit opinions and suggestions on legislation, supervision, and other work.

## Chapter IV: Local People's Governments at Various Levels

### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 61: People's governments shall be established in provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, counties, autonomous counties, cities, municipal districts, townships, ethnic townships, and towns.

Article 62: Local people's governments at various levels shall uphold the authority of the Constitution and laws, adhere to law-based administration, and build a law-based government with scientific functions, statutorily defined powers and responsibilities, strict law enforcement, openness and fairness, intelligence and efficiency, integrity and honesty, and satisfaction of the people.

Article 63: Local people's governments at various levels shall adhere to a people-centered approach, serve the people wholeheartedly, improve administrative efficiency, and build a service-oriented government.

Article 64: Local people's governments at various levels shall strictly implement all regulations on clean governance, strengthen integrity building, and build a clean government.

Article 65: Local people's governments at various levels shall adhere to the principle of good faith, strengthen the building of government integrity, and build a trustworthy government.

Article 66: Local people's governments at various levels shall adhere to open government affairs, comprehensively promote the openness of decision-making, execution, management, service, and results, disclose government information in accordance with the law, in a timely and accurate manner, promote the orderly sharing of government data, and enhance the transparency of government work.

Article 67: Local people's governments at various levels shall adhere to scientific, democratic, and law-based decision-making to improve the quality of decisions.

Article 68: Local people's governments at various levels shall accept supervision in accordance with the law to ensure the lawful and correct exercise of administrative power.

Article 69: Local people's governments at various levels shall be responsible for and report on their work to the People's Congress at the corresponding level and to the state administrative organ at the next higher level. During the recess of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for and report on their work to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

All local people's governments at various levels throughout the country are state administrative organs under the unified leadership of the State Council and are subordinate to the State Council.

Local people's governments at various levels shall implement a system of reporting on major matters.

### Section 2: Composition and Term of Office of Local People's Governments at Various Levels

Article 70: The people's government of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts shall be composed of the governor and deputy governors, the chairman and vice-chairmen of the autonomous region, the mayor and deputy mayors, the prefect and vice-prefects, and the secretary-general, department heads, bureau directors, and commission chairpersons, respectively.

The people's government of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district shall be composed of the county head and deputy county heads, the mayor and deputy mayors, the district head and deputy district heads, and bureau directors and section chiefs, respectively.

The people's government of a township or ethnic township shall have a township head and deputy township heads. The township head of an ethnic township shall be held by a citizen of the ethnic minority that established the ethnic township. The people's government of a town shall have a town head and deputy town heads.

Article 71: After the leading personnel of a new term of the people's government are lawfully elected, they shall, within two months, submit to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level the appointment of the secretary-general, department heads, bureau directors, commission chairpersons, and section chiefs of the people's government.

Article 72: The term of office of a local people's government at any level is five years.

### Section 3: Functions and Powers of Local People's Governments at Various Levels

Article 73: A local people's government at or above the county level shall exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) Implement the resolutions of the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee, as well as the decisions and orders of the state administrative organ at the next higher level, formulate administrative measures, and issue decisions and orders;
(2) Lead the work of its subordinate working departments and the people's governments at lower levels;
(3) Alter or revoke inappropriate orders and directives of its subordinate working departments and inappropriate decisions and orders of the people's governments at lower levels;
(4) Appoint, remove, train, assess, and reward or punish staff of state administrative organs in accordance with the provisions of the law;
(5) Formulate and implement plans and budgets for national economic and social development, manage the economic, educational, scientific, cultural, health, sports, urban and rural development, and other undertakings within its administrative region, as well as administrative work such as ecological and environmental protection, natural resources, finance, civil affairs, social security, public security, ethnic affairs, judicial administration, and population and family planning;
(6) Protect socialist property owned by the whole people and property collectively owned by the working masses, protect citizens' lawful private property, maintain social order, and safeguard citizens' personal rights, democratic rights, and other rights;
(7) Fulfill the responsibilities of state-owned assets management;
(8) Protect the lawful rights and interests of various economic organizations;
(9) Foster a strong sense of community for the Chinese nation, promote extensive exchanges, interactions, and integration among all ethnic groups, safeguard the lawful rights and interests of ethnic minorities, guarantee the freedom of ethnic minorities to maintain or reform their own customs and habits, assist the ethnic autonomous areas within its administrative region in exercising regional autonomy in accordance with the Constitution and laws, and help all ethnic minorities develop their political, economic, and cultural undertakings;
(10) Safeguard the rights conferred by the Constitution and laws on women, such as equality between men and women, equal pay for equal work, and freedom of marriage;
(11) Handle other matters assigned by the state administrative organ at the next higher level.

Article 74: The people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, formulate rules and regulations, and report them to the State Council and the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for the record. The people's government of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture may, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the local regulations of the province or autonomous region, formulate rules and regulations within the limits of authority prescribed by law, and report them to the State Council, the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the province or autonomous region, the people's government, and the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for the record.

The formulation of rules and regulations in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be discussed and decided at an executive meeting or a plenary meeting of the respective government.

Article 75: When a local people's government at or above the county level formulates regulatory documents involving the rights and obligations of individuals and organizations, it shall, in accordance with statutory authority and procedures, conduct evaluation and demonstration, solicit public opinions, carry out legality review, make decisions through collective discussion, and publish and file them for the record.

Article 76: The people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town shall exercise the following functions and powers:

(1) Implement the resolutions of the People's Congress at the corresponding level and the decisions and orders of the state administrative organ at the next higher level, and issue decisions and orders;
(2) Implement the economic and social development plans and budgets within its administrative region, and manage the economic, educational, scientific, cultural, health, sports, and other undertakings within its administrative region, as well as administrative work such as ecological and environmental protection, finance, civil affairs, social security, public security, judicial administration, and population and family planning;
(3) Protect socialist property owned by the whole people and property collectively owned by the working masses, protect citizens' lawful private property, maintain social order, and safeguard citizens' personal rights, democratic rights, and other rights;
(4) Protect the lawful rights and interests of various economic organizations;
(5) Foster a strong sense of community for the Chinese nation, promote extensive exchanges, interactions, and integration among all ethnic groups, safeguard the lawful rights and interests of ethnic minorities, and guarantee the freedom of ethnic minorities to maintain or reform their own customs and habits;
(6) Safeguard the rights conferred by the Constitution and laws on women, such as equality between men and women, equal pay for equal work, and freedom of marriage;
(7) Handle other matters assigned by the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 77: Local people's governments at various levels shall implement the system of responsibility of the governor, chairman of the autonomous region, mayor, prefect, county head, district head, township head, and town head, respectively.

The governor, chairman of the autonomous region, mayor, prefect, county head, district head, township head, and town head shall respectively preside over the work of the local people's government at the corresponding level.

Article 78: Meetings of a local people's government at or above the county level are divided into plenary meetings and executive meetings. Plenary meetings shall be composed of all members of the people's government at the corresponding level. The executive meeting of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts shall be composed of the governor and deputy governors, the chairman and vice-chairmen of the autonomous region, the mayor and deputy mayors, the prefect and vice-prefects, and the secretary-general, respectively. The executive meeting of the people's government of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district shall be composed of the county head and deputy county heads, the mayor and deputy mayors, the district head and deputy district heads, respectively. The governor, chairman of the autonomous region, mayor, prefect, county head, and district head shall convene and preside over the plenary meetings and executive meetings of the people's government at the corresponding level. Major issues in government work must be discussed and decided at an executive meeting or a plenary meeting of the government.

### Section 4: Establishment of Organs of Local People's Governments at Various Levels

Article 79: Local people's governments at various levels shall, based on work needs and the principles of optimization, coordination, efficiency, and leanness, establish necessary working departments.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish audit institutions. Local audit institutions at various levels shall independently exercise the power of audit supervision in accordance with the provisions of the law, and shall be responsible to the people's government at the corresponding level and the audit institution at the next higher level.

The establishment, increase, reduction, or merger of working departments such as departments, bureaus, and commissions of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and of working departments such as bureaus and sections of the people's government of an autonomous prefecture, county, autonomous county, city, or municipal district, shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the prescribed procedures and reported to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for the record.

Article 80: Local people's governments at or above the county level may, in accordance with the national regional development strategy and in light of local actual needs, jointly establish a working mechanism for cross-administrative regional coordinated development to strengthen regional cooperation.

The people's government at a higher level shall guide, coordinate, and supervise the regional cooperation work of the people's government at a lower level.

Article 81: Local people's governments at or above the county level may, based on the need to respond to major emergencies, establish cross-departmental command and coordination mechanisms.

Article 82: Each department, bureau, commission, and section shall have a department head, bureau director, commission chairperson, or section chief, and may have deputies when necessary.

Each general office or office shall have a director, and may have deputy directors when necessary.

The people's government of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, or city divided into districts shall have one secretary-general and several deputy secretaries-general.

Article 83: The working departments of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be under the unified leadership of the people's government and, in accordance with the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, shall be subject to the professional guidance or leadership of the competent department of the State Council.

The working departments of the people's government of an autonomous prefecture, county, autonomous county, city, or municipal district shall be under the unified leadership of the people's government and, in accordance with the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, shall be subject to the professional guidance or leadership of the competent department of the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 84: The people's government of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefecture, county, autonomous county, city, or municipal district shall assist the state organs, enterprises, and institutions located within its administrative region but not under its own management in carrying out their work, and supervise their compliance with and implementation of laws and policies.

Article 85: When necessary, the people's government of a province or autonomous region may, with the approval of the State Council, establish several dispatched agencies.

When necessary, the people's government of a county or autonomous county may, with the approval of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, establish several district offices as its dispatched agencies. The people's government of a municipal district or a city not divided into districts may, with the approval of the people's government at the next higher level, establish several sub-district offices as its dispatched agencies.
Article 86: A sub-district office shall, within its jurisdiction, handle public services, public management, public safety, and other tasks assigned by the people's government that established it, and shall perform duties such as comprehensive management, overall coordination, emergency response, and administrative law enforcement in accordance with the law, and reflect the opinions and demands of residents.

Article 87: The people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town, and the people's government of a municipal district or a city not divided into districts, or a sub-district office, shall provide guidance, support, and assistance to the work of grassroots self-governing mass organizations. Grassroots self-governing mass organizations shall assist the people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town, and the people's government of a municipal district or a city not divided into districts, or a sub-district office, in carrying out their work.

Article 88: The people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town, and a sub-district office may, based on actual circumstances, establish a system for residents to attend relevant meetings as observers.

## Chapter 5 Supplementary Provisions

Article 89: The autonomous organs of autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties shall, in addition to exercising the functions and powers provided for in this Law, exercise their powers of autonomy in accordance with the Constitution, the Law on Regional Ethnic Autonomy, and other laws.

Article 90: The people's congresses and their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on this Law and actual circumstances, formulate specific provisions on issues arising during implementation.
