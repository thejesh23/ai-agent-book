# Organic Law of the Villagers Committees of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on November 4, 1998

Revised at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 28, 2010

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Organic Law of the Villagers Committees of the People's Republic of China and the Organic Law of the Urban Residents Committees of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure self-governance by rural villagers, enable villagers to manage their own affairs according to law, develop grassroots democracy in rural areas, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of villagers, and promote the building of a new socialist countryside.

Article 2 The villagers committee is a grassroots mass self-governance organization for villagers to practice self-management, self-education, and self-service, and shall implement democratic elections, democratic decision-making, democratic management, and democratic supervision.

The villagers committee shall manage the public affairs and public welfare undertakings of the village, mediate civil disputes, assist in maintaining public security, and reflect villagers' opinions, demands, and suggestions to the people's government.

The villagers committee shall be responsible for and report its work to the villagers assembly and the villagers representative assembly.

Article 3 Villagers committees shall be established based on the living conditions and population of the villagers, in accordance with the principles of facilitating self-governance by the masses and being conducive to economic development and social management.

The establishment, dissolution, or adjustment of the scope of a villagers committee shall be proposed by the people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town, discussed and approved by the villagers assembly, and reported to the people's government at the county level for approval.

A villagers committee may, based on the living conditions of the villagers and the ownership of collective land, establish several villagers groups.

Article 4 The grassroots organizations of the Communist Party of China in rural areas shall carry out their work in accordance with the Constitution of the Communist Party of China, play a leading core role, lead and support the villagers committee in exercising its functions and powers; and, in accordance with the Constitution and laws, support and guarantee that villagers carry out self-governance activities and directly exercise their democratic rights.

Article 5 The people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town shall provide guidance, support, and assistance to the work of the villagers committee, but shall not interfere with matters that fall within the scope of villagers' self-governance according to law.

The villagers committee shall assist the people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town in carrying out its work.

## Chapter II Composition and Functions of the Villagers Committee

Article 6 A villagers committee shall be composed of three to seven members, including a director, deputy directors, and members.

Among the members of the villagers committee, there shall be female members; in villages inhabited by multiple ethnic groups, there shall be members from the smaller ethnic groups.

Appropriate subsidies shall be given to members of the villagers committee based on their work performance.

Article 7 A villagers committee may, as needed, establish sub-committees for people's mediation, public security, public health, and family planning. Members of the villagers committee may concurrently serve as members of these sub-committees. In villages with a small population, the villagers committee may not establish sub-committees, and its members shall be responsible for the division of work in people's mediation, public security, public health, and family planning.

Article 8 The villagers committee shall support and organize villagers to develop various forms of cooperative and other economies according to law, undertake the service and coordination work for the village's production, and promote rural production construction and economic development.

The villagers committee shall, in accordance with legal provisions, manage the land and other property owned collectively by the village farmers, guide villagers in the rational use of natural resources, and protect and improve the ecological environment.

The villagers committee shall respect and support the autonomy of collective economic organizations to independently conduct economic activities according to law, uphold the two-tier management system based on household contract management combined with unified and decentralized management, and protect the legitimate property rights and other lawful rights and interests of collective economic organizations, villagers, contract households, joint households, or partnerships.

Article 9 The villagers committee shall publicize the Constitution, laws, regulations, and state policies; educate and urge villagers to fulfill their legal obligations, protect public property, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of villagers; develop culture and education, popularize scientific and technological knowledge; promote gender equality; properly carry out family planning work; promote unity and mutual assistance among villages; and carry out various forms of activities for building socialist spiritual civilization.

The villagers committee shall support service-oriented, public welfare, and mutual-aid social organizations in carrying out activities according to law, and promote the construction of rural communities.

In villages inhabited by multiple ethnic groups, the villagers committee shall educate and guide villagers of all ethnic groups to enhance unity, respect each other, and help each other.

Article 10 The villagers committee and its members shall abide by the Constitution, laws, regulations, and state policies; comply with and organize the implementation of the villagers' self-governance charter and village rules and regulations; implement the decisions and resolutions of the villagers assembly and the villagers representative assembly; be fair and honest in handling affairs; be dedicated to serving the villagers; and accept the supervision of the villagers.

## Chapter III Election of the Villagers Committee

Article 11 The director, deputy directors, and members of the villagers committee shall be directly elected by the villagers. No organization or individual may appoint, assign, or remove members of the villagers committee.

The term of office of a villagers committee is five years. Upon the expiration of the term, a new election shall be held promptly. Members of the villagers committee may be re-elected.

Article 12 The election of the villagers committee shall be presided over by the villagers election committee.

The villagers election committee shall be composed of a director and members, and shall be elected by the villagers assembly, the villagers representative assembly, or the meetings of each villagers group.

If a member of the villagers election committee is nominated as a candidate for the villagers committee, they shall withdraw from the villagers election committee.

If a member of the villagers election committee withdraws or vacates their position for other reasons, a replacement shall be made in order according to the original election results, or a new election may be held.

Article 13 Every villager who has reached the age of 18 shall have the right to vote and stand for election, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, family background, religious belief, education level, property status, or length of residence; except for persons who have been deprived of political rights according to law.

Before the election of the villagers committee, the following persons shall be registered and included in the list of villagers participating in the election:

(1) Villagers whose household registration is in the village and who reside in the village;

(2) Villagers whose household registration is in the village but who do not reside in the village, and who have expressed their intention to participate in the election;

(3) Citizens whose household registration is not in the village but who have resided in the village for more than one year, who have applied to participate in the election, and whose participation has been approved by the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly.

Villagers who have been registered to participate in the election in their village of household registration or residence shall not participate in the election of the villagers committee of any other place.

Article 14 The list of villagers registered to participate in the election shall be published by the villagers election committee at least twenty days before the election date.

Any objection to the list of villagers registered to participate in the election shall be lodged with the villagers election committee within five days from the date of publication. The villagers election committee shall make a decision on the handling of the objection within three days from the receipt of the objection and publish the result.

Article 15 In the election of the villagers committee, candidates shall be directly nominated by the villagers registered to participate in the election. When nominating candidates, villagers shall, in the interests of all villagers, recommend villagers who abide by the law, are of good conduct, are fair and upright, are enthusiastic about public welfare, and have a certain level of education and work ability. The number of candidates shall be greater than the number of positions to be filled. The villagers election committee shall organize meetings between candidates and villagers, where candidates shall introduce their plans for performing their duties and answer questions raised by villagers.

In the election of the villagers committee, the election is valid if more than half of the villagers registered to participate in the election cast their votes; a candidate is elected if they receive more than half of the votes cast by the villagers who participated in the voting. If the number of elected candidates is less than the number of positions to be filled, a by-election shall be held for the vacant positions. In the by-election, the candidates who received the most votes but were not elected in the first round of voting shall be the candidates, and the candidate with the most votes shall be elected, provided that the number of votes received is not less than one-third of the total number of votes cast.

Elections shall be conducted by secret ballot and public counting of votes, and the election results shall be announced on the spot. During the election, a private voting booth shall be set up.

Villagers registered to participate in the election who are away from the village during the election period and cannot vote in person may entrust a close relative in the village who has the right to vote to vote on their behalf by written proxy. The villagers election committee shall publish the list of principals and proxies.

Specific election procedures shall be prescribed by the standing committee of the people's congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 16 A motion to recall a member of the villagers committee may be initiated by more than one-fifth of the villagers with the right to vote in the village, or jointly by more than one-third of the villagers' representatives, stating the reasons for the recall. The member of the villagers committee proposed for recall has the right to present a defense.

The recall of a member of the villagers committee requires the votes of more than half of the villagers registered to participate in the election, and must be approved by more than half of the villagers who cast their votes.

Article 17 Any person elected as a member of the villagers committee through improper means such as violence, threats, deception, bribery, forging ballots, or falsifying vote counts shall be deemed invalidly elected.

Villagers have the right to report to the people's congress and people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town, or to the standing committee of the people's congress and the people's government at the county level and their relevant competent departments, any acts that hinder villagers from exercising their right to vote and stand for election, or disrupt the election of the villagers committee through improper means such as violence, threats, deception, bribery, forging ballots, or falsifying vote counts. The people's government at the township or county level shall be responsible for investigating and handling the matter according to law.

Article 18 If a member of the villagers committee loses capacity for civil conduct or is sentenced to a criminal penalty, their position shall be automatically terminated.

Article 19 If a position on the villagers committee becomes vacant, a by-election may be held by the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly. The by-election procedures shall be handled with reference to the provisions of Article 15 of this Law. The term of office of a member elected in a by-election shall end upon the expiration of the term of the current villagers committee.

Article 20 The villagers committee shall complete the handover of work within ten days from the date the new villagers committee is formed. The handover of work shall be presided over by the villagers election committee and supervised by the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town.

## Chapter IV Villagers Assembly and Villagers Representative Assembly

Article 21 The villagers assembly shall be composed of villagers of the village who have reached the age of 18.

The villagers assembly shall be convened by the villagers committee. The villagers assembly shall be convened upon the proposal of more than one-tenth of the villagers or more than one-third of the villagers' representatives. When convening the villagers assembly, villagers shall be notified ten days in advance.

Article 22 To convene a villagers assembly, more than half of the villagers of the village who have reached the age of 18, or representatives of more than two-thirds of the households in the village, must be present. Decisions made by the villagers assembly shall be approved by more than half of the attendees. Where laws provide otherwise for convening the villagers assembly and making decisions, those provisions shall prevail.

When convening the villagers assembly, representatives of enterprises, institutions, and mass organizations located in the village may be invited to attend as non-voting participants as needed.

Article 23 The villagers assembly shall review the annual work report of the villagers committee and evaluate the work of the members of the villagers committee; it has the power to revoke or modify inappropriate decisions made by the villagers committee; it has the power to revoke or modify inappropriate decisions made by the villagers representative assembly.

The villagers assembly may authorize the villagers representative assembly to review the annual work report of the villagers committee, evaluate the work of the members of the villagers committee, and revoke or modify inappropriate decisions made by the villagers committee.

Article 24 The following matters involving the interests of villagers shall be discussed and decided by the villagers assembly before they can be handled:

(1) Persons in the village who receive work-loss subsidies and the subsidy standards;

(2) The use of income derived from the village collective economy;

(3) The initiation of public welfare undertakings in the village, plans for raising funds and labor, and construction contracting plans;

(4) Plans for land contract management;

(5) The initiation and contracting plans for village collective economic projects;

(6) Plans for the use of residential land;

(7) Plans for the use and distribution of land acquisition compensation fees;

(8) The disposal of village collective property by means of loans, leases, or other methods;

(9) Other matters involving the interests of villagers that the villagers assembly deems should be discussed and decided by the villagers assembly.

The villagers assembly may authorize the villagers representative assembly to discuss and decide on the matters specified in the preceding paragraph.

Where laws provide otherwise for discussing and deciding on matters concerning the property of village collective economic organizations and the rights and interests of their members, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 25 In villages with a large population or scattered residences, a villagers representative assembly may be established to discuss and decide on matters authorized by the villagers assembly. The villagers representative assembly shall be composed of members of the villagers committee and villagers' representatives. Villagers' representatives shall account for at least four-fifths of the members of the villagers representative assembly, and female villagers' representatives shall account for at least one-third of the members of the villagers representative assembly.

Villagers' representatives shall be elected by villagers, with one representative elected for every five to fifteen households, or several representatives elected by each villagers group. The term of office of villagers' representatives shall be the same as that of the villagers committee. Villagers' representatives may be re-elected.

Villagers' representatives shall be responsible to the households or villagers group that elected them and shall accept the supervision of the villagers.

Article 26 The villagers representative assembly shall be convened by the villagers committee. The villagers representative assembly shall meet once every quarter. The villagers representative assembly shall be convened upon the proposal of more than one-fifth of the villagers' representatives.

The villagers representative assembly may be convened only if more than two-thirds of its members are present. Decisions made shall be approved by more than half of the attendees.

Article 27 The villagers assembly may formulate and amend the villagers' self-governance charter and village rules and regulations, and shall report them to the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town for recordation.

The villagers' self-governance charter, village rules and regulations, and decisions of the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly shall not contravene the Constitution, laws, regulations, and state policies, and shall not contain content that infringes upon the personal rights, democratic rights, or legitimate property rights of villagers.

If the villagers' self-governance charter, village rules and regulations, or decisions of the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town shall order corrections.

Article 28 To convene a villagers group meeting, more than two-thirds of the villagers of the group who have reached the age of 18, or representatives of more than two-thirds of the households in the group, must be present. Decisions made shall be approved by more than half of the attendees.

The head of a villagers group shall be elected by the villagers group meeting. The term of office of the head of a villagers group shall be the same as that of the villagers committee, and they may be re-elected.

Matters concerning the management and operation of land, enterprises, and other property collectively owned by the villagers group, as well as the handling of public welfare matters, shall be discussed and decided by the villagers group meeting in accordance with relevant legal provisions. The decisions made and their implementation shall be promptly announced to the villagers of the group.

## Chapter V Democratic Management and Democratic Supervision

Article 29 The villagers committee shall implement a democratic decision-making mechanism based on the principle of minority submitting to the majority and a working principle of openness and transparency, and shall establish and improve various working systems.

Article 30 The villagers committee shall implement a system of village affairs disclosure.

The villagers committee shall promptly announce the following matters to accept the supervision of the villagers:

(1) Matters discussed and decided by the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly as stipulated in Articles 23 and 24 of this Law, and the status of their implementation;

(2) The implementation plan for the national family planning policy;

(3) The management and use of funds and materials allocated by the government and accepted as social donations for disaster relief, assistance, subsidies, and grants;

(4) The status of the villagers committee assisting the people's government in carrying out its work;

(5) Other matters involving the interests of the villagers of the village that are of general concern to the villagers.

For the matters specified in the preceding paragraph, general matters shall be announced at least once per quarter; where there are frequent collective financial transactions, the financial revenue and expenditure shall be announced once per month; major matters involving the interests of villagers shall be announced at any time.

The villagers committee shall ensure the authenticity of the matters announced and shall accept inquiries from villagers.

Article 31 If the villagers committee fails to promptly announce matters that should be announced or announces matters that are untrue, villagers have the right to report to the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town, or to the people's government at the county level and its relevant competent departments. The relevant people's government or competent department shall be responsible for investigating and verifying the matter, and shall order the villagers committee to make the announcement according to law; if illegal acts are verified, the relevant persons shall bear legal responsibility according to law.

Article 32 A village shall establish a village affairs supervision committee or another form of village affairs supervision body, responsible for villagers' democratic financial management and supervising the implementation of systems such as village affairs disclosure. Its members shall be elected from among the villagers by the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly, and shall include persons with knowledge of finance and management. Members of the villagers committee and their close relatives shall not serve as members of the village affairs supervision body. Members of the village affairs supervision body shall be responsible to the villagers assembly and the villagers representative assembly, and may attend meetings of the villagers committee as non-voting participants.

Article 33 Members of the villagers committee and employed persons who receive work-loss subsidies from villagers or the village collective shall undergo democratic evaluation by the villagers assembly or the villagers representative assembly regarding their performance of duties. Democratic evaluation shall be conducted at least once a year, presided over by the village affairs supervision body.

If a member of the villagers committee is evaluated as incompetent in two consecutive evaluations, their position shall be terminated.

Article 34 The villagers committee and the village affairs supervision body shall establish village affairs archives. Village affairs archives shall include: election documents and ballots, meeting minutes, land contracting plans and contracts, economic contracts, collective financial accounts, collective asset registration documents, basic information on public facilities, basic construction materials, residential land use plans, and plans for the use and distribution of land acquisition compensation fees. Village affairs archives shall be truthful, accurate, complete, and standardized.

Article 35 Members of the villagers committee shall undergo economic responsibility audits during their term of office and upon leaving office. The audit shall include the following matters:

(1) The financial revenue and expenditure of the village;

(2) The claims and debts of the village;

(3) The management and use of funds and materials allocated by the government and accepted as social donations;

(4) The contracting management of production, operation, and construction projects in the village, and the bidding for public welfare construction projects;

(5) The management and use of village funds, and the contracting, leasing, guarantee, and transfer of village collective assets and resources, and the use and distribution of land acquisition compensation fees;

(6) Other matters requested to be audited by more than one-fifth of the villagers of the village.

The economic responsibility audits of members of the villagers committee during their term of office and upon leaving office shall be organized by the agricultural department or finance department of the people's government at the county level, or by the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town. The audit results shall be announced, and the results of the audit upon leaving office shall be announced before the election of the next villagers committee.

Article 36 If a decision made by the villagers committee or a member of the villagers committee infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of a villager, the aggrieved villager may apply to the people's court for its revocation, and the responsible person shall bear legal liability according to law.

If the villagers committee fails to perform its legal obligations in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations, the people's government of the township, ethnic township, or town shall order it to make corrections.

If the people's government of a township, ethnic township, or town interferes with matters that fall within the scope of villagers' self-governance according to law, the people's government at the next higher level shall order it to make corrections.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 37 The people's government shall provide necessary conditions for the villagers committee to assist the government in carrying out its work; if a relevant department of the people's government entrusts the villagers committee to carry out work that requires funding, the entrusting department shall bear the costs.The funds needed by the villagers' committee to handle public welfare undertakings in the village shall be raised through contributions and labor as decided by the villagers' meeting; if funds are truly insufficient, the local people's government shall provide appropriate support.

Article 38 State organs, organizations, military units, state-owned and state-controlled enterprises, public institutions, and their personnel stationed in the village shall not participate in the organization of the villagers' committee, but shall participate in the construction of the rural community through various forms and comply with relevant village rules and regulations.

When the villagers' committee, villagers' meeting, or villagers' representative meeting discusses and decides matters related to the units mentioned in the preceding paragraph, it shall consult with them.

Article 39 Local people's congresses at all levels and the standing committees of local people's congresses at or above the county level shall ensure the implementation of this Law within their respective administrative regions and guarantee that villagers exercise their rights of self-governance in accordance with the law.

Article 40 The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with this Law and in light of the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, formulate implementation measures.

Article 41 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
