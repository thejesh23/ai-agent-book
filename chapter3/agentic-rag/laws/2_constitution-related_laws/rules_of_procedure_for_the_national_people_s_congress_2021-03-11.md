# Rules of Procedure for the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 4, 1989

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Rules of Procedure for the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Fourth Session of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on March 11, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 These Rules are formulated in accordance with the Constitution, the Organic Law of the National People's Congress, and the practical experience of the National People's Congress.

## Chapter I Convening of Sessions

Article 2 The session of the National People's Congress shall be held in the first quarter of each year. The date of the session shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and announced.

Under special circumstances, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may decide to convene the session appropriately earlier or later. If the date for convening the session earlier or later cannot be decided at the current session, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may make a separate decision or authorize the Council of Chairmen to make the decision, and announce it.

Article 3 The session of the National People's Congress shall be convened by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The first session of each National People's Congress shall be convened by the Standing Committee of the previous National People's Congress within two months after the election of the deputies to that National People's Congress.

Article 4 A session of the National People's Congress may be held only when more than two-thirds of the deputies are present.

Article 5 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall make the following preparations before the convening of a session of the National People's Congress:

(1) Proposing a draft agenda for the session;
(2) Proposing a draft list of the Presidium and the Secretary-General;
(3) Deciding on the list of persons to attend the session as non-voting participants;
(4) Other preparatory matters for the session.

Article 6 One month before the convening of a session of the National People's Congress, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall notify the deputies of the date of the session and the main items to be discussed, and distribute to the deputies the draft laws to be submitted to the session for deliberation.

Before the convening of a session of the National People's Congress, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may organize deputies to study and discuss relevant draft laws, solicit opinions from the deputies, and inform them of the relevant information concerning the main items to be discussed at the session.

The provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall not apply to sessions of the National People's Congress convened on an ad hoc basis.

Article 7 Before the convening of a session of the National People's Congress, the deputies shall form delegations according to the electoral units. A plenary meeting of a delegation shall elect a leader and deputy leaders. The leader shall convene and preside over the plenary meetings of the delegation. The deputy leaders shall assist the leader in his or her work.

A delegation may be divided into several deputy groups. A meeting of a deputy group shall elect the convener(s) of the group.

Article 8 Before the convening of a session of the National People's Congress, a preparatory meeting shall be held to elect the Presidium and the Secretary-General, and to adopt the agenda and decisions on other preparatory matters for the session.

The preparatory meeting shall be presided over by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The preparatory meeting for the first session of each National People's Congress shall be presided over by the Standing Committee of the previous National People's Congress.

Each delegation shall deliberate the draft list of the Presidium and the Secretary-General, the draft agenda, and other preparatory matters for the session proposed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, and put forward opinions.

Based on the opinions put forward by the delegations, the Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may propose adjustments to the draft list of the Presidium and the Secretary-General, the draft agenda, and other preparatory matters for the session, and submit them to the preparatory meeting for deliberation.

Article 9 The Presidium shall preside over the session of the National People's Congress.

Decisions of the Presidium shall be adopted by a majority vote of all its members.

Article 10 The first meeting of the Presidium shall elect several standing chairpersons of the Presidium, elect several members of the Presidium to serve as the executive chairpersons for each plenary meeting of the session, and decide on the following matters:

(1) The choice of the Deputy Secretaries-General;
(2) The schedule of the session;
(3) The deadline for deputies to submit bills during the session;
(4) Other matters that need to be decided at the first meeting of the Presidium.

Article 11 The standing chairpersons of the Presidium shall convene and preside over the meetings of the Presidium. The first meeting of the Presidium shall be convened and presided over by the Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. After the election of the standing chairpersons of the Presidium at that meeting, the standing chairpersons of the Presidium shall preside over the meeting.

Article 12 Deliberation of bills and relevant reports by delegations shall be conducted at plenary meetings of the delegations and meetings of deputy groups.

Bills, questions of inquiry, and proposals for removal from office submitted in the name of a delegation shall be adopted by a majority vote of all the deputies of that delegation.

Article 13 The standing chairpersons of the Presidium may convene meetings of the leaders of the delegations to hear the opinions of the delegations on major issues concerning bills and relevant reports, conduct discussions, and report the discussions and opinions to the Presidium.

The standing chairpersons of the Presidium may, on major specialized issues, convene relevant deputies elected by the delegations for discussion; responsible persons from the relevant departments of the State Council shall attend the meetings, report on the situation, and answer questions. The discussions and opinions of the meetings shall be reported to the Presidium.

Article 14 The Presidium may convene plenary meetings of the session for speeches to express opinions on bills and relevant reports.

Article 15 Deputies to the National People's Congress shall attend the session; those who are unable to attend due to illness or other special reasons shall ask for leave in writing from the secretariat of the session. The secretariat shall report to the Presidium on the attendance of the deputies and the reasons for their absence.

Deputies shall diligently fulfill their duties, carefully deliberate on various bills and reports, and strictly abide by the discipline of the session.

Article 16 The members of the State Council, the members of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall attend the session of the National People's Congress as non-voting participants; responsible persons of other relevant organs and organizations may, upon decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, attend the session of the National People's Congress as non-voting participants.

Article 17 The session of the National People's Congress shall be held publicly.

The agenda, schedule, and proceedings of the session of the National People's Congress shall be made public.

During the session of the National People's Congress, summaries of the speeches made by deputies at various meetings shall be compiled and distributed to the session, and, at the request of the deputies, the records or summaries of their speeches may be distributed to the session. The summaries, records, or summaries of speeches may be in paper or electronic form.

A gallery for observers shall be set up at the plenary meetings of the session. Measures for observers shall be formulated separately.

Article 18 The session of the National People's Congress shall hold press conferences and briefings.

The session of the National People's Congress shall have a spokesperson, and delegations may have spokespersons as needed.

The secretariat may organize deputies and responsible persons of relevant departments and units to accept interviews by news media. Delegations may organize deputies of their delegation to accept interviews by news media.

The plenary meetings of the session shall be publicly reported through media such as radio, television, and the internet.

Article 19 The National People's Congress may, when necessary, hold closed sessions. The decision to hold a closed session shall be made by a meeting of the Presidium attended by the leaders of all delegations, after the Presidium has solicited the opinions of the delegations.

Article 20 When the National People's Congress is in session, the secretariat and the relevant delegations shall provide necessary translation services for deputies from ethnic minorities.

Article 21 When the National People's Congress is in session, the schedule of the session shall be reasonably arranged to improve the quality and efficiency of deliberations.

Each delegation shall conduct deliberations in accordance with the schedule of the session.

Article 22 The session of the National People's Congress shall utilize modern information technology to promote the digitization of session documents and materials, and use methods such as online video to provide convenience and services for deputies to perform their duties.

## Chapter II Submission and Deliberation of Bills

Article 23 The Presidium, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the special committees of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate may submit bills to the National People's Congress that fall within the scope of its functions and powers. The Presidium shall decide whether to place them on the agenda of the session.

A delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies jointly may submit bills to the National People's Congress that fall within the scope of its functions and powers. The Presidium shall decide whether to place them on the agenda of the session, or first refer them to the relevant special committee for deliberation and an opinion on whether to place them on the agenda, and then decide whether to place them on the agenda. The report of the Presidium on the handling of the bills shall be distributed to the session. When a special committee deliberates, it may invite the proposer(s) to attend the meeting and express opinions.

Bills submitted jointly by deputies or by a delegation may be submitted before the convening of the session of the National People's Congress.

Article 24 For bills placed on the agenda of the session, the proposer(s) and the relevant special committee of the National People's Congress and the relevant working departments of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall provide relevant materials.

Article 25 For bills placed on the agenda of the session, the proposer(s) shall provide an explanation of the bill to the session. The bill shall be deliberated by each delegation. The Presidium may also refer it to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report. The Presidium shall deliberate and decide to submit the bill to the plenary meeting of the session for a vote.

Article 26 For a legislative bill placed on the agenda of the session, after the plenary meeting of the session has heard the explanation of the legislative bill, it shall be deliberated by each delegation and by the Constitution and Law Committee and the relevant special committee.

The Constitution and Law Committee shall conduct a unified deliberation of the legislative bill based on the opinions of the delegations and the relevant special committee, and submit to the Presidium a report on the results of the deliberation and a revised draft of the law or the decision on a relevant legal issue. Important dissenting opinions shall be explained in the report on the results of the deliberation. After the report is deliberated and adopted by the Presidium, it shall be distributed to the session. The revised draft shall be deliberated by each delegation. The Constitution and Law Committee shall make revisions based on the opinions of the delegations and submit a final draft for a vote. The Presidium shall submit the final draft to the plenary meeting of the session for a vote.

The opinions of the relevant special committee shall be distributed to the session in a timely manner.

The procedures for deliberation and voting on legislative bills drafted and submitted by a specific legislative drafting committee established by decision of the National People's Congress shall be formulated separately.

Article 27 A legislative bill submitted to the National People's Congress may, when the National People's Congress is not in session, be first submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. After deliberation by the Standing Committee in accordance with relevant procedures, it shall decide to submit the bill to the National People's Congress for deliberation.

For a legislative bill to be submitted to the National People's Congress for deliberation, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall publish the draft law to the public to solicit extensive opinions, unless the Council of Chairmen decides not to publish it. The period for soliciting opinions from the public shall generally be no less than thirty days.

Article 28 When a special committee deliberates bills and relevant reports involving specialized issues, it may invite relevant deputies and experts to attend the meeting and express opinions.

A special committee may decide to hold closed meetings.

Article 29 If, before a bill placed on the agenda of the session is put to a vote, the proposer(s) requests its withdrawal, the deliberation of the bill by the session shall be terminated upon the consent of the Presidium.

Article 30 If, during the deliberation of a bill placed on the agenda of the session, there are major issues that require further study, the Presidium may propose, and the plenary meeting of the session may decide, to authorize the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress to deliberate and decide on the matter, and report to the next session of the National People's Congress for the record or submit it to the next session of the National People's Congress for deliberation.

Article 31 If a bill submitted by a delegation or jointly by thirty or more deputies is decided by the Presidium not to be placed on the agenda of the current session, it shall be referred to the relevant special committee for deliberation after the conclusion of the session of the National People's Congress. After deliberation, the relevant special committee shall submit a report on the results of the deliberation to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. After the report is deliberated and adopted by the Standing Committee, it shall be distributed to the next session of the National People's Congress.

Article 32 Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on various aspects of work submitted by deputies to the National People's Congress to the National People's Congress shall be handled by the working offices of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, which shall refer them to the relevant organs and organizations for study and handling. These organs and organizations shall be responsible for replying within three months from the date of referral, and no later than six months. If a deputy is not satisfied with the reply, he or she may put forward opinions, and the working offices of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall refer them to the relevant organs, organizations, or their superior organs or organizations for further study and handling, and they shall be responsible for replying.

## Chapter III Deliberation of Work Reports, Examination of the National Plan and the National Budget

Article 33 When the National People's Congress meets each year, the work reports submitted to the session by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be deliberated by each delegation, and the session may make corresponding resolutions.

Article 34 Forty-five days before the convening of the session of the National People's Congress, the relevant competent departments of the State Council shall report to the Financial and Economic Committee and the relevant special committees of the National People's Congress on the main contents of the implementation of the plan for national economic and social development for the previous year and the preliminary plan for the draft plan for national economic and social development for the current year, and on the main contents of the implementation of the central and local budgets for the previous year and the preliminary plan for the draft central and local budgets for the current year. The Financial and Economic Committee shall conduct a preliminary examination. When conducting the preliminary examination, the Financial and Economic Committee shall invite deputies to the National People's Congress to participate.

Article 35 When the National People's Congress meets each year, the State Council shall submit to the session a report on the implementation of the plan for national economic and social development for the previous year and the draft plan for national economic and social development for the current year, the draft plan for national economic and social development, a report on the implementation of the central and local budgets for the previous year and the draft central and local budgets for the current year, and the draft central and local budgets. These shall be examined by each delegation and by the Financial and Economic Committee and the relevant special committees.

Based on the examination opinions of the delegations and the relevant special committees, the Financial and Economic Committee shall examine the matters specified in the preceding paragraph and submit a report on the results of the examination to the Presidium. After the report is deliberated and adopted by the Presidium, it shall be distributed to the session. The Presidium shall submit the draft resolution on the implementation of the plan for national economic and social development for the previous year and the plan for national economic and social development for the current year, and the draft resolution on the implementation of the central and local budgets for the previous year and the central and local budgets for the current year, to the plenary meeting of the session for a vote.

The examination opinions of the relevant special committees shall be distributed to the session in a timely manner.

Article 36 If, after the plan for national economic and social development and the central budget are approved by the National People's Congress, partial adjustments must be made during implementation, the State Council shall submit the adjustment plan to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for examination and approval.

Article 37 The examination, approval, and adjustment of the Five-Year Plan for National Economic and Social Development and the medium- and long-term plan outline shall be carried out with reference to the relevant provisions of this Chapter.

## Chapter IV Election, Recall, Appointment and Removal, and Resignation of Members of State Organs

Article 38 The candidates for the Chairman, Vice-Chairmen, Secretary-General, and members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the candidates for the President and Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, the candidate for the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the candidate for the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the candidates for the President of the Supreme People's Court and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be nominated by the Presidium. After deliberation and consultation by the delegations, the Presidium shall, based on the opinions of the majority of the deputies, determine the list of official candidates.

The candidates for the Premier and other members of the State Council, and the candidates for members of the Central Military Commission other than the Chairman shall be nominated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Constitution.

The candidates for the chairpersons, vice-chairpersons, and members of the special committees shall be nominated by the Presidium from among the deputies.

Article 39 The nominator(s) of a candidate shall introduce the basic information of the candidate to the session and provide necessary explanations on questions raised by the deputies.

Article 40 Elections or decisions on appointments by the session of the National People's Congress shall be conducted by secret ballot. A candidate shall be elected or a decision shall be passed only when the number of votes obtained exceeds half of all the deputies.

When the plenary meeting of the session conducts an election or votes on an appointment proposal, a secret ballot booth shall be set up.

The results of the election or vote shall be announced on the spot by the presiding officer of the meeting. The number of votes obtained by the candidates shall be published.

Article 41 The specific methods for elections and decisions on appointments by the session of the National People's Congress shall be adopted by the plenary meeting of the session.

Article 42 Members of state organs elected or appointed by decision of the National People's Congress shall, after being produced in accordance with legal procedures, publicly take an oath of allegiance to the Constitution. The oath-taking ceremony shall be organized by the Presidium.

Article 43 During the session of the National People's Congress, if a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President or Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, a member of the State Council, a member of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, or a member of a special committee of the National People's Congress submits a resignation, the Presidium shall refer the resignation request to each delegation for deliberation and then submit it to the plenary meeting of the session for a decision; if the resignation is submitted when the National People's Congress is not in session, the Council of Chairmen shall refer the resignation request to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for deliberation and decision.

If the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress accepts the resignation of the Chairman, Vice-Chairmen, or Secretary-General of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President or Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, the Premier, Vice-Premiers, or State Councilors of the State Council, the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, or the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, it shall report to the next session of the National People's Congress for confirmation.

If the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress accepts the resignation of a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, it shall report to the next session of the National People's Congress.

When the National People's Congress is not in session, if the positions of the Premier of the State Council, the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, or the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate become vacant, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may decide on an acting person from among the Vice-Premiers of the State Council, the Vice-Chairmen of the Central Military Commission, the Deputy Directors of the National Commission of Supervision, the Vice-Presidents of the Supreme People's Court, or the Deputy Procurators-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, respectively.**Article 44** The Presidium, three or more delegations, or one-tenth or more of the deputies may propose a motion to remove from office any member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President or Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, any member of the State Council, any member of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, or the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate. The Presidium shall refer the motion to the delegations for deliberation and then submit it to a plenary meeting of the Congress for a vote; or, in accordance with the provisions of Chapter VI of these Rules, the Presidium may propose, subject to a decision by a plenary meeting of the Congress, the formation of an investigation committee, and the next session of the National People's Congress shall deliberate and decide on the matter based on the committee's report.

A motion for removal shall clearly state the reasons for the removal and provide relevant materials.

Before a motion for removal is submitted to a plenary meeting of the Congress for a vote, the person against whom the motion is directed has the right to present a defense at a meeting of the Presidium or at a plenary meeting of the Congress, or to submit a written defense, which shall be distributed to the Congress by the Presidium.

**Article 45** If a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or a member of a special committee is removed from his or her post as a deputy to the National People's Congress by the electoral unit that elected him or her, his or her position as a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or a member of a special committee shall be revoked accordingly, and the Presidium or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall make an announcement to this effect.

**Article 46** If a request by a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or a member of a special committee to resign his or her post as a deputy to the National People's Congress is accepted, his or her position as a member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress or a member of a special committee shall terminate accordingly, and the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall make an announcement to this effect.

## Chapter V: Inquiries and Interpellations

**Article 47** When delegations deliberate on bills and relevant reports, the departments concerned shall send responsible persons to attend the meetings to hear opinions and answer inquiries raised by deputies.

When a plenary meeting of a delegation deliberates on the report on the work of the government, examines the report on the implementation of the plan for national economic and social development for the previous year and the draft plan for national economic and social development for the current year, and the draft plan for national economic and social development, examines the report on the implementation of the central and local budgets for the previous year and the draft central and local budgets for the current year, and the draft central and local budgets, and deliberates on the work reports of the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate, the heads of the State Council and its departments, and the heads of the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate, or persons designated by them, shall attend the respective meetings to hear opinions and answer inquiries.

When the Presidium and special committees deliberate on bills and relevant reports, the heads of the State Council or the relevant organs shall attend the meetings to hear opinions, answer inquiries, and may provide supplementary explanations on the bills or relevant reports.

**Article 48** During a session of the National People's Congress, a delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies may jointly submit a written interpellation to the State Council and its departments, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, or the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

**Article 49** An interpellation must clearly state the organ to which it is addressed, the question, and its content.

**Article 50** An interpellation shall be answered orally by the responsible person of the organ addressed at a meeting of the Presidium, a meeting of the relevant special committee, or a meeting of the relevant delegation, or in writing by the organ addressed, as decided by the Presidium. If the answer is given at a meeting of the Presidium or a special committee, the head of the delegation that submitted the interpellation or the deputy who submitted it has the right to attend the meeting as a non-voting participant and express opinions.

If the deputy or delegation that submitted the interpellation is not satisfied with the answer, they may make a request, and upon decision by the Presidium, the organ addressed shall give another answer.

If the answer is given at a meeting of a special committee or a delegation, the relevant special committee or delegation shall report the circumstances of the answer to the Presidium.

When the Presidium deems it necessary, it may distribute the report on the circumstances of the answer to the Congress.

If the interpellation is answered in writing, the responsible person of the organ addressed shall sign it, and the Presidium shall decide on its distribution to the Congress.

## Chapter VI: Investigation Committees

**Article 51** When the National People's Congress deems it necessary, it may form an investigation committee on a specific issue.

**Article 52** The Presidium, three or more delegations, or one-tenth or more of the deputies jointly may propose the formation of an investigation committee on a specific issue. The Presidium shall submit the proposal to a plenary meeting of the Congress for a decision.

An investigation committee shall be composed of a chairperson, several vice-chairpersons, and several members. The Presidium shall nominate candidates from among the deputies and submit them to a plenary meeting of the Congress for approval. An investigation committee may engage experts to participate in the investigation.

**Article 53** When an investigation committee conducts an investigation, all relevant state organs, social organizations, and citizens have the duty to truthfully provide it with necessary materials. If a citizen providing materials requests the investigation committee to keep the source of the materials confidential, the investigation committee shall do so.

During the investigation, the investigation committee may choose not to disclose the circumstances and materials of the investigation.

**Article 54** An investigation committee shall submit an investigation report to the National People's Congress. The National People's Congress may adopt appropriate resolutions based on the committee's report.

The National People's Congress may authorize its Standing Committee to hear the investigation report of the investigation committee when the Congress is not in session and may adopt appropriate resolutions, which shall be reported to the next session of the National People's Congress for the record.

## Chapter VII: Speeches and Voting

**Article 55** Deputies to the National People's Congress shall not be held legally liable for their speeches or votes at various meetings of the National People's Congress.

**Article 56** When speaking at various meetings of the National People's Congress, deputies shall focus on the topics set for the meeting.

**Article 57** A deputy speaking at a plenary meeting of the Congress may speak twice, with the first speech not exceeding ten minutes and the second not exceeding five minutes.

Deputies wishing to speak at a plenary meeting of the Congress shall register with the Secretariat before the meeting, and the executive chairman of the meeting shall arrange the order of speeches. Those who wish to speak temporarily at a plenary meeting may do so only with the permission of the executive chairman.

**Article 58** A member of the Presidium, the head of a delegation, or a deputy elected by a delegation may speak twice on the same topic at each meeting of the Presidium, with the first speech not exceeding fifteen minutes and the second not exceeding ten minutes. With the permission of the person presiding over the meeting, the speaking time may be appropriately extended.

**Article 59** A bill submitted to a plenary meeting of the Congress for a vote shall be adopted by a majority vote of all deputies.

Amendments to the Constitution shall be adopted by a vote of two-thirds or more of all deputies.

The result of a vote shall be announced on the spot by the person presiding over the meeting.

When voting, a deputy may vote in favor, against, or abstain.

**Article 60** Voting on bills at meetings shall be conducted by secret ballot using an electronic voting system. If the electronic voting system malfunctions during use, voting shall be conducted by a show of hands.

Amendments to the Constitution shall be voted on by secret ballot.

The provisions of the first paragraph of this Article shall apply to the voting methods of preparatory meetings and meetings of the Presidium.

## Chapter VIII: Promulgation

**Article 61** The election of the Chairman, Vice-Chairpersons, Secretary-General, and members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President and Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate by the National People's Congress, the decision on the appointment of the Vice-Chairmen and members of the Central Military Commission, and the approval of the members of the special committees of the National People's Congress shall be announced by an announcement of the National People's Congress.

The Premier, Vice-Premiers, State Councilors, Ministers, Ministers of Commissions, the Governor of the People's Bank of China, the Auditor-General, and the Secretary-General of the State Council, whose appointments are decided by the National People's Congress, shall be appointed by an order signed by the President of the People's Republic of China based on the decision of the National People's Congress, and the appointment shall be announced.

**Article 62** If a member of a state organ resigns or is removed from office during a session of the National People's Congress, the promulgation procedures stipulated in Article 61 of these Rules shall apply.

**Article 63** Amendments to the Constitution adopted by the National People's Congress shall be announced by an announcement of the National People's Congress.

**Article 64** Laws adopted by the National People's Congress shall be promulgated by an order signed by the President of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 65** Laws, resolutions, and decisions adopted by the National People's Congress, announcements issued by it, as well as explanations of draft laws and reports on the results of their deliberation, shall be promptly published in the Bulletin of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and on the website of the National People's Congress.

## Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 66** These Rules shall come into force on the date of their promulgation.
