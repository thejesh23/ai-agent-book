# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Territorial Sea and the Contiguous Zone

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on February 25, 1992

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of exercising the sovereignty of the People's Republic of China over its territorial sea and the control over its contiguous zone, and safeguarding the national security and the maritime rights and interests.

Article 2 The territorial sea of the People's Republic of China is a sea belt adjacent to the land territory and the internal waters of the People's Republic of China.

The land territory of the People's Republic of China includes the mainland of the People's Republic of China and its coastal islands, Taiwan and its affiliated islands including the Diaoyu Islands, the Penghu Islands, the Dongsha Islands, the Xisha Islands, the Zhongsha Islands, the Nansha Islands, and all other islands belonging to the People's Republic of China.

The waters on the landward side of the baseline of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China constitute the internal waters of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The breadth of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China is twelve nautical miles, measured from the baselines of the territorial sea.

The baselines of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China are designated by the method of straight baselines, composed of straight lines connecting the adjacent base points.

The outer limit of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China is a line every point of which is at a distance of twelve nautical miles from the nearest point of the baseline of the territorial sea.

Article 4 The contiguous zone of the People's Republic of China is a sea belt adjacent to and beyond the territorial sea. The breadth of the contiguous zone is twelve nautical miles.

The outer limit of the contiguous zone of the People's Republic of China is a line every point of which is at a distance of twenty-four nautical miles from the nearest point of the baseline of the territorial sea.

Article 5 The sovereignty of the People's Republic of China over its territorial sea extends to the airspace over the territorial sea, as well as to the seabed and subsoil of the territorial sea.

Article 6 Foreign non-military ships enjoy the right of innocent passage through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

Foreign military ships entering the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China must obtain approval from the Government of the People's Republic of China.

Article 7 Foreign submarines and other underwater vehicles, when passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China, must navigate on the surface and show their flags.

Article 8 Foreign ships passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China must comply with the laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China and must not prejudice the peace, security, and good order of the People's Republic of China.

Foreign nuclear-powered ships and ships carrying nuclear substances, toxic substances, or other dangerous substances, when passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China, must hold relevant certificates and take special precautionary measures.

The Government of the People's Republic of China has the right to take all necessary measures to prevent and stop non-innocent passage through the territorial sea.

Foreign ships that violate the laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China shall be dealt with by the relevant organs of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

Article 9 For the maintenance of navigational safety and other special needs, the Government of the People's Republic of China may require foreign ships passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China to use designated sea lanes or to navigate in accordance with prescribed traffic separation schemes. Specific measures shall be promulgated by the Government of the People's Republic of China or its competent authorities.

Article 10 Where foreign military ships or foreign government ships used for non-commercial purposes, when passing through the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China, violate the laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China, the competent authorities of the People's Republic of China have the right to order them to leave the territorial sea immediately. The flag state shall bear international responsibility for any loss or damage caused.

Article 11 Any international organization, foreign organization, or individual conducting scientific research, marine operations, or other activities within the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China must obtain approval from the Government of the People's Republic of China or its competent authorities and comply with the laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China.

Those who violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph by illegally entering the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China to conduct scientific research, marine operations, or other activities shall be dealt with by the relevant organs of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

Article 12 Foreign aircraft may enter the airspace over the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China only in accordance with agreements or protocols concluded between the government of that country and the Government of the People's Republic of China, or with the approval or acceptance of the Government of the People's Republic of China or its authorized organs.

Article 13 The People's Republic of China has the right to exercise control within the contiguous zone to prevent and punish violations of its laws and regulations concerning security, customs, finance, health, or entry and exit management within its land territory, internal waters, or territorial sea.

Article 14 When the competent authorities of the People's Republic of China have good reason to believe that a foreign ship has violated the laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China, they may exercise the right of hot pursuit against such foreign ship.

Such pursuit must be commenced when the foreign ship or one of its small boats or other craft operating as a mother ship for the pursued ship is within the internal waters, the territorial sea, or the contiguous zone of the People's Republic of China.

If the foreign ship is within the contiguous zone of the People's Republic of China, the pursuit may be undertaken only when the rights provided for in the relevant laws and regulations listed in Article 13 of this Law have been violated.

The pursuit may be continued outside the territorial sea or the contiguous zone of the People's Republic of China so long as it has not been interrupted. The pursuit shall cease as soon as the ship pursued enters the territorial sea of its own country or of a third country.

The right of hot pursuit provided for in this Article shall be exercised by military ships, military aircraft, or other ships or aircraft on government service authorized by the Government of the People's Republic of China.

Article 15 The baselines of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China shall be promulgated by the Government of the People's Republic of China.

Article 16 The Government of the People's Republic of China shall formulate relevant regulations in accordance with this Law.

Article 17 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
