# Garrison Law of the People's Republic of China in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 30, 1996

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to ensure that the troops stationed by the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region for defense fulfill their duties according to law, and to safeguard the sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity of the State and the security of Hong Kong.

Article 2 The troops stationed by the Central People's Government in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region for defense shall be composed of units from the Army, Navy, and Air Force of the People's Liberation Army and shall be designated as the People's Liberation Army Hong Kong Garrison (hereinafter referred to as the Hong Kong Garrison).

Article 3 The Hong Kong Garrison shall be under the leadership of the Central Military Commission of the People's Republic of China, and its size shall be determined by the need for defense of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

A system of rotation of personnel shall be instituted in the Hong Kong Garrison.

Article 4 The expenses for the Hong Kong Garrison shall be borne by the Central People's Government.

## Chapter II Functions and Duties of the Hong Kong Garrison

Article 5 The Hong Kong Garrison shall perform the following defense functions and duties:

(1) to prepare against and resist aggression, and to safeguard the security of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(2) to carry out defense duties;

(3) to manage military facilities; and

(4) to handle matters relating to foreign military affairs.

Article 6 When the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress decides to declare a state of war or, by reason of turmoil within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region which endangers national unity or security and is beyond the control of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, decides that the Region is in a state of emergency, the Hong Kong Garrison shall perform its duties in accordance with the national laws that the Central People's Government decides to apply in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 7 The weapons, equipment, and materials such as aircraft and vessels of the Hong Kong Garrison, and the persons and vehicles that hold certificates or documents issued by the Hong Kong Garrison and are on duty, shall not be subject to inspection, search, or seizure by the law enforcement personnel of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The Hong Kong Garrison and its members shall also enjoy other rights and immunities provided for by the laws in force in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 8 Members of the Hong Kong Garrison may take measures to stop acts that obstruct them from performing their duties, in accordance with the provisions of the laws in force in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

## Chapter III Relationship Between the Hong Kong Garrison and the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region

Article 9 The Hong Kong Garrison shall not interfere in the local affairs of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 10 The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall support the Hong Kong Garrison in performing its defense functions and duties, and shall safeguard the lawful rights and interests of the Hong Kong Garrison and its members.

When the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region formulates policies or drafts legislation that involves the Hong Kong Garrison, it shall seek the views of the Hong Kong Garrison.

Article 11 When the Hong Kong Garrison conducts military activities such as training and exercises that involve the public interests of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, it shall notify the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region in advance.

Article 12 The Hong Kong Garrison and the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall jointly protect the military facilities within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The Hong Kong Garrison shall, in conjunction with the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, delimit military restricted zones. The location and scope of the military restricted zones shall be announced by the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall assist the Hong Kong Garrison in maintaining the security of the military restricted zones.

No person, vehicle, vessel, or aircraft other than those of the Hong Kong Garrison may enter a military restricted zone without the approval of the highest commander of the Hong Kong Garrison or an officer authorized by him. The guards of the military restricted zones shall have the right to stop, in accordance with the law, any unauthorized entry into the military restricted zones and any act that damages or endangers military facilities.

The Hong Kong Garrison shall protect the natural resources, cultural relics and historic sites, and non-military rights and interests within the military restricted zones in accordance with the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 13 Where military land of the Hong Kong Garrison, with the approval of the Central People's Government, is no longer needed for defense purposes, it shall be turned over without compensation to the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region for disposal.

If the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region needs to use part of the military land of the Hong Kong Garrison for public purposes, it must obtain the approval of the Central People's Government; upon such approval, the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall, at a location agreed upon by the Central People's Government, provide replacement military land and military facilities for the Hong Kong Garrison and bear all the expenses incurred.

Article 14 When necessary, the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region may, in accordance with the provisions of the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, request the Central People's Government for assistance from the Hong Kong Garrison in maintaining public order or in disaster relief.

Upon approval by the Central People's Government of the request from the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, the Hong Kong Garrison shall, in accordance with an order of the Central Military Commission, dispatch troops to carry out tasks of assisting in maintaining public order or providing disaster relief, and such troops shall return to their barracks upon completion of the tasks.

When the Hong Kong Garrison assists in maintaining public order or providing disaster relief, it shall act under the arrangement of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and be commanded by the highest commander of the Hong Kong Garrison or an officer authorized by him.

When members of the Hong Kong Garrison assist in maintaining public order or providing disaster relief, they shall exercise the powers conferred by the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 15 The Hong Kong Garrison and the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall establish necessary liaison to consult and deal with matters relating to the Garrison.

## Chapter IV Obligations and Discipline of Members of the Hong Kong Garrison

Article 16 Members of the Hong Kong Garrison shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) to be loyal to the motherland, to perform their duties, to safeguard the security, honor, and interests of the motherland, and to safeguard the security of Hong Kong;

(2) to abide by the national laws and the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and to observe military discipline;

(3) to respect the organs of political power of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and to respect the social system and way of life of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region;

(4) to take good care of the public property of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the private property of Hong Kong residents and other persons; and

(5) to observe social ethics and to be civilized and courteous.

Article 17 Members of the Hong Kong Garrison shall not join political organizations, religious organizations, or social organizations in Hong Kong.

Article 18 The Hong Kong Garrison and its members shall not engage in any form of profit-making business activities. Members of the Hong Kong Garrison shall not engage in any other activities incompatible with their duties as military personnel.

Article 19 Members of the Hong Kong Garrison who violate the national laws or the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall be investigated for legal responsibility according to law.

Members of the Hong Kong Garrison who violate military discipline shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions.

## Chapter V Jurisdiction Over Members of the Hong Kong Garrison

Article 20 Cases of crimes committed by members of the Hong Kong Garrison shall be under the jurisdiction of military judicial organs; however, cases of crimes committed by members of the Hong Kong Garrison through acts not in the performance of their duties, which infringe upon the personal or property rights of Hong Kong residents or persons other than members of the Hong Kong Garrison, or which otherwise violate the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and constitute crimes, shall be under the jurisdiction of the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the relevant law enforcement organs.

If the military judicial organs and the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the relevant law enforcement organs consider that a case of a crime committed by a member of the Hong Kong Garrison which is under their respective jurisdiction would be more appropriately handled by the other party, they may, after consultation and agreement between the two sides, transfer the case to the other party for jurisdiction.

In cases of crimes committed by members of the Hong Kong Garrison under the jurisdiction of military judicial organs, if the accused includes Hong Kong residents or persons other than members of the Hong Kong Garrison, such accused shall be tried by the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 21 If a person suspected of committing a crime who is lawfully arrested or detained by law enforcement personnel of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region is found to be a member of the Hong Kong Garrison, he shall be handed over to the Hong Kong Garrison for custody. The jurisdiction over the case involving the person in custody shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of Article 20 of this Law.

Article 22 If a member of the Hong Kong Garrison is sentenced by a court of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to a penalty that deprives or restricts personal freedom, he shall be delivered for execution in accordance with the provisions of the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; however, unless otherwise agreed upon through consultation between the relevant law enforcement organs of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the military judicial organs regarding the place of execution.

Article 23 If a member of the Hong Kong Garrison, in violation of the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, infringes upon the civil rights of Hong Kong residents or persons other than members of the Hong Kong Garrison, the parties concerned may settle the matter through consultation or mediation; if they are unwilling to do so or if consultation or mediation fails, the injured party may bring a lawsuit in a court. Cases of civil torts arising from acts not in the performance of duties by members of the Hong Kong Garrison shall be under the jurisdiction of the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; cases of civil torts arising from acts in the performance of duties shall be under the jurisdiction of the Supreme People's Court of the People's Republic of China, and the law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall apply to the compensation for damages from such torts.

Article 24 If a dispute arises from a contract between an organ or unit of the Hong Kong Garrison and a Hong Kong resident or a person other than a member of the Hong Kong Garrison within the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, the parties concerned may settle the matter through consultation or mediation. If the parties are unwilling to do so or if consultation or mediation fails, they may apply to an arbitration body for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration clause contained in the contract or a written arbitration agreement reached afterwards. If the parties have not included an arbitration clause in the contract and have not reached a written arbitration agreement afterwards, they may bring a lawsuit in a court of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; however, unless otherwise agreed by the parties regarding the court in which the lawsuit is to be brought.

Article 25 In litigation proceedings in the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, certificates issued by the Hong Kong Garrison concerning facts such as the status of a member of the Hong Kong Garrison or acts performed in the course of duty shall be valid evidence, unless the contrary is established.

Article 26 State acts of the Hong Kong Garrison, such as national defense, shall not be subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

Article 27 If a judgment or order rendered by a court of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region involves the enforcement of property of an organ or unit of the Hong Kong Garrison, such organ or unit must comply; however, the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall not enforce compulsory execution against the weapons, equipment, materials, or other property of the Hong Kong Garrison.

Article 28 Military judicial organs may, through consultation, maintain judicial liaison and provide mutual assistance with the courts of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the relevant law enforcement organs.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 29 The power of interpretation of this Law shall be vested in the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 30 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 1997.
