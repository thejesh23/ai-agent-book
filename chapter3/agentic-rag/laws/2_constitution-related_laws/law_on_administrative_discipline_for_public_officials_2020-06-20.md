# Law of the People's Republic of China on Administrative Discipline for Public Officials

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 20, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1: This Law is enacted in accordance with the Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China to standardize administrative discipline, strengthen supervision over all public officials who exercise public power, and promote public officials' lawful performance of duties, impartial exercise of power, clean and honest governance, and adherence to moral integrity.

Article 2: This Law applies to the activities of supervisory organs in imposing administrative discipline on public officials who have violated the law.

Chapters II and III of this Law apply to the imposition of sanctions by the organs or units that appoint and remove public officials on public officials who have violated the law. The procedures for sanctions, appeals, and other matters shall be governed by other laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules of the State Council, and relevant state provisions.

The term "public officials" as used in this Law refers to the personnel specified in Article 15 of the Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3: Supervisory organs shall, in accordance with their management authority, strengthen supervision over public officials and impose administrative discipline on public officials who have violated the law in accordance with the law.

The organs or units that appoint and remove public officials shall, in accordance with their management authority, strengthen education, management, and supervision over public officials, and impose sanctions on public officials who have violated the law in accordance with the law.

If a supervisory organ discovers that an organ or unit that appoints and removes public officials should have imposed a sanction but failed to do so, or that the sanction imposed is illegal or improper, it shall promptly make a supervision recommendation.

Article 4: The imposition of administrative discipline on public officials shall adhere to the principle of the Party managing cadres and be decided through collective discussion; adhere to the principle of equality before the law, be based on facts, and take the law as the criterion, ensuring that the administrative discipline imposed is commensurate with the nature, circumstances, and degree of harm of the illegal act; and adhere to the combination of punishment and education, with leniency and severity being applied appropriately.

Article 5: The imposition of administrative discipline on public officials shall be based on clear facts, conclusive evidence, accurate characterization, appropriate handling, lawful procedures, and complete formalities.

Article 6: Public officials who perform their duties in accordance with the law are protected by law and shall not be subject to administrative discipline except for statutory reasons and through statutory procedures.

## Chapter II: Types and Application of Administrative Discipline

Article 7: The types of administrative discipline are:

(1) Warning;
(2) Demerit;
(3) Serious demerit;
(4) Demotion;
(5) Removal from office;
(6) Dismissal.

Article 8: The duration of administrative discipline is:

(1) Warning: six months;
(2) Demerit: twelve months;
(3) Serious demerit: eighteen months;
(4) Demotion and removal from office: twenty-four months.

The decision on administrative discipline shall take effect from the date it is made, and the period of administrative discipline shall be calculated from the date the decision takes effect.

Article 9: If two or more public officials jointly commit an illegal act, administrative discipline shall be imposed on each of them separately based on their respective roles in the illegal act and the legal responsibility they should bear.

Article 10: If a relevant organ, unit, or organization makes a collective decision that is illegal or commits an illegal act, administrative discipline shall be imposed on the public officials among the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 11: Administrative discipline may be mitigated or reduced for a public official under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Voluntarily confessing to the illegal act for which administrative discipline should be imposed;
(2) Cooperating with the investigation and truthfully explaining the facts of the illegal act;
(3) Reporting the illegal or disciplinary violations of others, which are verified to be true;
(4) Voluntarily taking measures to effectively avoid or recover losses or eliminate adverse effects;
(5) Playing a secondary or auxiliary role in a joint illegal act;
(6) Voluntarily surrendering or returning illegal gains;
(7) Other circumstances provided for by laws or regulations where mitigation or reduction is warranted.

Article 12: If the illegal act of a public official is minor and falls under any of the circumstances specified in Article 11 of this Law, the official may be given a talk as a reminder, criticism and education, ordered to make a check, or given a reprimand, and may be exempted from or not subject to administrative discipline.

If a public official is coerced or forced to participate in illegal activities due to ignorance of the facts and shows genuine repentance after criticism and education, the administrative discipline may be mitigated, or the official may be exempted from or not subject to administrative discipline.

Article 13: Administrative discipline shall be imposed more severely on a public official under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Intentionally committing another illegal act during the period of administrative discipline for which administrative discipline should be imposed;
(2) Preventing others from reporting or providing evidence;
(3) Colluding to fabricate confessions or forging, concealing, or destroying evidence;
(4) Harboring co-defendants;
(5) Coercing or instigating others to commit illegal acts;
(6) Refusing to surrender or return illegal gains;
(7) Other circumstances provided for by laws or regulations where more severe punishment is warranted.

Article 14: A public official who commits a crime shall be dismissed under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, or fixed-term imprisonment or a heavier punishment (including a suspended sentence) for an intentional crime;
(2) Sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment for a term exceeding three years for a negligent crime;
(3) Sentenced to deprivation of political rights as a sole or additional punishment for a crime.

If a public official is sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, or fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years for a negligent crime, the official shall generally be dismissed; if the circumstances of the case are special and removal from office is more appropriate, the official may not be dismissed, but approval from the next higher-level organ shall be obtained.

If a public official is solely fined for a crime, or if the circumstances of the crime are minor and the People's Procuratorate makes a decision not to prosecute or the People's Court exempts the official from criminal punishment in accordance with the law, the official shall be removed from office; if adverse effects are caused, the official shall be dismissed.

Article 15: If a public official commits two or more illegal acts, separate administrative discipline shall be determined for each act. If two or more types of administrative discipline should be imposed, the most severe one shall be executed; if multiple identical administrative disciplines below removal from office should be imposed, the period of administrative discipline may be determined as a period not less than the longest single period and not more than the sum of all periods, but the maximum shall not exceed forty-eight months.

Article 16: For the same illegal act of a public official, the supervisory organ and the organ or unit that appoints and removes the public official shall not impose administrative discipline and sanctions repeatedly.

Article 17: If a public official commits an illegal act and a relevant organ imposes organizational handling in accordance with regulations, the supervisory organ may simultaneously impose administrative discipline.

Article 18: If a public official holding a leadership position commits an illegal act and is removed, dismissed, relieved of, or resigns from the leadership position, the supervisory organ may simultaneously impose administrative discipline.

Article 19: During the period of administrative discipline, civil servants and personnel managed by reference to the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China shall not be promoted in position, rank, grade, or level; among them, those who have received a demerit, serious demerit, demotion, or removal from office shall not be promoted in salary grade. Those who are removed from office shall have their position, rank, grade, and level lowered in accordance with regulations, and their salary and benefits shall also be reduced.

Article 20: Personnel engaged in public affairs in organizations authorized by laws or regulations or entrusted by state organs to manage public affairs in accordance with the law, as well as personnel engaged in management in publicly funded educational, scientific research, cultural, medical and health, and sports institutions, shall not be promoted in position, post, staff grade, or professional title during the period of administrative discipline; among them, those who have received a demerit, serious demerit, demotion, or removal from office shall not be promoted in salary grade. Those who are removed from office shall have their position, post, or staff grade lowered, and their salary and benefits shall also be reduced.

Article 21: Management personnel of state-owned enterprises shall not be promoted in position, post grade, or professional title during the period of administrative discipline; among them, those who have received a demerit, serious demerit, demotion, or removal from office shall not be promoted in salary grade. Those who are removed from office shall have their position or post grade lowered, and their salary and benefits shall also be reduced.

Article 22: If personnel engaged in management in grassroots self-governing organizations commit illegal acts, the supervisory organ may impose a warning, demerit, or serious demerit.

If personnel engaged in management in grassroots self-governing organizations receive administrative discipline, the county or township people's government shall reduce or withhold their subsidies and bonuses based on the specific circumstances.

Article 23: If personnel specified in Item 6 of Article 15 of the Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China commit illegal acts, the supervisory organ may impose a warning, demerit, or serious demerit. If the circumstances are serious, the unit to which they belong shall directly impose, or the supervisory organ shall recommend that the relevant organ or unit impose, handling such as reducing salary and benefits, transferring them from their post, or terminating their personnel or labor relationship.

If personnel specified in Item 2 of Article 15 of the Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China do not hold positions as civil servants, personnel managed by reference to the Civil Servant Law, staff of public institutions, or management personnel of state-owned enterprises, their illegal acts shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 24: Public officials who are dismissed, or who are subject to handling terminating their personnel or labor relationship in accordance with Article 23 of this Law, shall not be employed as civil servants or personnel managed by reference to the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 25: Property illegally obtained by a public official and the official's own property used for the illegal act shall, unless it should be confiscated, recovered, or ordered to be returned by other organs in accordance with the law, be confiscated, recovered, or ordered to be returned by the supervisory organ; if it should be returned to the original owner or holder, it shall be returned in accordance with the law; if it is state property or should not be returned or cannot be returned, it shall be turned over to the state treasury.

If a public official obtains other benefits such as positions, ranks, grades, levels, posts, staff grades, professional titles, treatment, qualifications, academic degrees, honors, or awards due to the illegal act, the supervisory organ shall recommend that the relevant organ, unit, or organization correct this in accordance with regulations.

Article 26: If a public official is dismissed, the personnel or labor relationship between the official and the organ or unit to which the official belongs shall be terminated from the date the decision on administrative discipline takes effect.

If a public official receives administrative discipline other than dismissal and shows repentance during the period of administrative discipline without committing any further illegal act for which administrative discipline should be imposed, the administrative discipline shall be automatically lifted upon expiration of the period, and promotions in position, rank, grade, level, post, staff grade, professional title, and salary and benefits shall no longer be affected by the original administrative discipline. However, if demotion or removal from office is lifted, the original position, rank, grade, level, post, staff grade, professional title, and salary and benefits shall not be restored.

Article 27: If a retired public official commits an illegal act before or after retirement, administrative discipline shall no longer be imposed, but the official may be placed on file for investigation; if demotion, removal from office, or dismissal should be imposed in accordance with the law, the benefits enjoyed by the official shall be adjusted accordingly in accordance with regulations, and the property illegally obtained by the official and the official's own property used for the illegal act shall be handled in accordance with Article 25 of this Law.

If a public official who has resigned or died commits an illegal act during the period of performing duties, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter III: Illegal Acts and Applicable Administrative Discipline

Article 28: Any of the following acts shall result in a demerit or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal:

(1) Disseminating remarks that harm the authority of the Constitution, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, or the reputation of the state;
(2) Participating in assemblies, processions, demonstrations, or other activities aimed at opposing the Constitution, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, or the state;
(3) Refusing to implement or covertly not implementing the lines, policies, and major decisions of the Communist Party of China and the state;
(4) Participating in illegal organizations or illegal activities;
(5) Stoking or undermining ethnic relations, or participating in ethnic separatist activities;
(6) Using religious activities to undermine ethnic unity and social stability;
(7) Damaging national honor and interests in foreign exchanges.

For any of the acts specified in Items 2, 4, 5, and 6 of the preceding paragraph, the planners, organizers, and core members shall be dismissed.

Anyone who publicly publishes articles, speeches, declarations, or statements opposing the guiding ideology established by the Constitution for the state, opposing the leadership of the Communist Party of China, opposing the socialist system, or opposing reform and opening up shall be dismissed.

Article 29: Failing to request instructions or report major matters in accordance with regulations, if the circumstances are relatively serious, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office.

Violating the provisions on reporting personal matters and concealing or failing to report, if the circumstances are relatively serious, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit.

Tampering with or forging one's own personnel file materials shall result in a demerit or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office.

Article 30: Any of the following acts shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office:

(1) Violating the principle of democratic centralism by having an individual or a minority decide major matters, or refusing to implement or arbitrarily changing a major decision made collectively;
(2) Refusing to implement, covertly not implementing, or delaying the implementation of decisions or orders lawfully made by a higher-level organ.

Article 31: Going abroad or applying for a passport for private purposes in violation of regulations shall result in a demerit or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office.

Obtaining foreign nationality or acquiring permanent residence status or long-term residence permits abroad in violation of regulations shall result in removal from office or dismissal.

Article 32: Any of the following acts shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal:

(1) Violating relevant regulations in cadre and personnel work such as selection and appointment, recruitment, employment, assessment, promotion, and evaluation;
(2) Engaging in fraud to obtain positions, ranks, grades, levels, posts, staff grades, professional titles, treatment, qualifications, academic degrees, honors, awards, or other benefits;
(3) Suppressing or retaliating against acts of exercising rights such as criticism, appeals, complaints, and reports in accordance with the law;
(4) Making false accusations with the intent to damage another person's reputation or cause them to be held accountable or suffer other adverse effects;
(5) Disrupting elections through violence, threats, bribery, deception, or other means.

Article 33: Any of the following acts shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal:

(1) Engaging in embezzlement or bribery;
(2) Using one's position or the influence of one's position to seek personal gain for oneself or others;
(3) Conniving at or acquiescing to specific related persons using one's position or the influence of one's position to seek personal gain.

Refusing to correct the improper appointment, concurrent position, or business activities of specific related persons in accordance with regulations and not accepting job adjustments shall result in removal from office.

Article 34: Accepting gifts, cash, negotiable securities, or other property that may affect the impartial exercise of public power shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal.

Giving gifts, cash, negotiable securities, or other property to a public official or their specific related persons that may affect the impartial exercise of public power, or accepting or providing arrangements for banquets, travel, fitness, entertainment, or other activities that may affect the impartial exercise of public power, if the circumstances are relatively serious, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office.

Article 35: Any of the following acts, if the circumstances are relatively serious, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office:

(1) Setting or paying salaries, allowances, subsidies, or bonuses in violation of regulations;
(2) Exceeding standards or scope in official receptions, official transportation, meeting activities, office space, and other work and life support in violation of regulations;
(3) Using public funds for consumption in violation of regulations.

Article 36: Engaging in or participating in for-profit activities in violation of regulations, or holding concurrent positions or receiving remuneration in violation of regulations, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal.

Article 37: Using clan or mafia-like forces to oppress the masses, or conniving at or harboring the activities of mafia-like forces, shall result in removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal.

Article 38: Any of the following acts, if the circumstances are relatively serious, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are serious, demotion or removal from office:

(1) Collecting or apportioning property from management or service targets in violation of regulations;
(2) Deliberately creating difficulties, extorting, or demanding bribes in management or service activities;
(3) Having a bad or rude attitude in management or service activities, causing adverse consequences or effects;
(4) Failing to disclose work information in accordance with regulations, infringing on the right to know of management or service targets, causing adverse consequences or effects;
(5) Other acts infringing on the interests of management or service targets, causing adverse consequences or effects.

For acts specified in Items 1, 2, and 5 of the preceding paragraph, if the circumstances are particularly serious, dismissal shall result.

Article 39: Any of the following acts, causing adverse consequences or effects, shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal:

(1) Abusing power, harming national interests, public interests, or infringing on the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations;
(2) Failing to perform or correctly perform duties, neglecting duties, or delaying work;
(3) Engaging in formalism or bureaucracy in work;
(4) Engaging in fraud, misleading, or deception in work;
(5) Disclosing state secrets, work secrets, or business secrets or personal privacy learned in the course of performing duties.

Article 40: Any of the following acts shall result in a warning, demerit, or serious demerit; if the circumstances are relatively serious, demotion or removal from office; if the circumstances are serious, dismissal:

(1) Violating public order and good customs by engaging in improper conduct in public places, causing adverse effects;
(2) Participating in or supporting superstitious activities, causing adverse effects;
(3) Participating in gambling;
(4) Refusing to fulfill obligations of support, maintenance, or care;
(5) Committing domestic violence, abusing, or abandoning family members;
(6) Other acts seriously violating family virtues or social ethics.

Using or injecting drugs, organizing gambling, or organizing, supporting, or participating in prostitution, solicitation, or obscene and promiscuous activities shall result in removal from office or dismissal.

Article 41: If a public official commits other illegal acts that affect the image of public officials and harm the interests of the state and the people, corresponding administrative discipline may be imposed based on the severity of the circumstances.

## Chapter IV: Procedures for Administrative Discipline

Article 42: When a supervisory organ investigates a public official suspected of violating the law, the investigation shall be conducted by two or more staff members. When conducting an investigation, the supervisory organ has the right to learn about the situation from relevant units and individuals and to collect and obtain evidence in accordance with the law. Relevant units and individuals shall truthfully provide information.

It is strictly prohibited to collect evidence through threats, inducements, deception, or other illegal means. Evidence collected through illegal means shall not be used as the basis for imposing administrative discipline.**Article 43** Before making a decision on a disciplinary sanction, the supervisory organ shall inform the person under investigation of the illegal facts identified through investigation and the basis for the proposed disciplinary sanction, hear the person's statements and defenses, verify the facts, reasons, and evidence presented, and record them in the case file. Facts, reasons, and evidence presented by the person under investigation that are established shall be adopted. The disciplinary sanction shall not be aggravated due to the person's defense.

**Article 44** After the investigation is concluded, the supervisory organ shall, based on the following different circumstances, handle the matter separately:

(1) If there are indeed illegal acts subject to disciplinary sanctions, a decision on the disciplinary sanction shall be made according to the severity of the circumstances, within the authority for deciding disciplinary sanctions, and after completing the prescribed approval procedures;

(2) If the illegal facts cannot be established, the case shall be dismissed;

(3) If the conditions for exemption from or non-imposition of a disciplinary sanction are met, a decision on exemption from or non-imposition of a disciplinary sanction shall be made;

(4) If the person under investigation is suspected of other illegal or criminal acts, the matter shall be transferred to the competent authority for handling according to law.

**Article 45** If a decision is made to impose a disciplinary sanction, a written decision on the disciplinary sanction shall be prepared.

The written decision on the disciplinary sanction shall specify the following:

(1) The name, work unit, and position of the person sanctioned;

(2) The illegal facts and evidence;

(3) The type and basis of the disciplinary sanction;

(4) The channels and time limit for applying for review or reconsideration if dissatisfied with the disciplinary sanction decision;

(5) The name of the organ making the disciplinary sanction decision and the date.

The written decision on the disciplinary sanction shall bear the seal of the supervisory organ that made the decision.

**Article 46** The written decision on the disciplinary sanction shall be promptly served on the person sanctioned and the organ or unit to which the person belongs, and shall be announced within a certain scope.

After making the decision on the disciplinary sanction, the supervisory organ shall, based on the specific identity of the person sanctioned, notify the relevant organs or units in writing.

**Article 47** Personnel involved in the investigation and handling of illegal cases involving public officials shall voluntarily withdraw under any of the following circumstances, and the person under investigation, the informant, and other relevant persons also have the right to request their withdrawal:

(1) Being a close relative of the person under investigation or the informant;

(2) Having served as a witness in this case;

(3) Having a personal interest or a close relative having a personal interest in the case under investigation;

(4) Other circumstances that may affect the impartial investigation and handling of the case.

**Article 48** The withdrawal of the head of a supervisory organ shall be decided by the supervisory organ at the next higher level; the withdrawal of other personnel involved in the investigation and handling of illegal cases shall be decided by the head of the supervisory organ.

If the supervisory organ or the supervisory organ at the next higher level discovers that personnel involved in the investigation and handling of illegal cases have circumstances requiring withdrawal, it may directly decide on the withdrawal of such personnel.

**Article 49** If a public official is subject to criminal liability according to law, the supervisory organ shall, based on the effective judgment, ruling, or decision of the judicial organ and the facts and circumstances determined therein, impose a disciplinary sanction in accordance with this Law.

If a public official is subject to an administrative penalty according to law and should be given a disciplinary sanction, the supervisory organ may, based on the facts and circumstances determined in the administrative penalty decision, after filing a case, investigating, and verifying, impose a disciplinary sanction in accordance with this Law.

If, after the supervisory organ imposes a disciplinary sanction pursuant to paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, the judicial or administrative organ changes the original effective judgment, ruling, decision, etc., according to law, which affects the original disciplinary sanction decision, the supervisory organ shall re-make corresponding handling based on the changed judgment, ruling, decision, etc.

**Article 50** If a supervisory organ imposes a sanction of removal from office or expulsion on a public official who was elected or decided to be appointed by the People's Congresses at various levels or the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses at or above the county level, it shall first have the official's position recalled, removed, or dismissed according to law, and then make a decision on the disciplinary sanction according to law.

If a supervisory organ imposes a sanction of removal from office or expulsion on a public official who was elected or decided to be appointed by the plenary sessions of the committees of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) at various levels or their Standing Committees, it shall first have the official's position removed according to the charter, and then make a decision on the disciplinary sanction according to law.

If a supervisory organ imposes a disciplinary sanction on a deputy to a People's Congress at any level or a member of a CPPCC committee at any level, it shall notify the relevant Standing Committee of the People's Congress, the presidium of the People's Congress of a township, ethnic township, or town, or the Standing Committee of the CPPCC committee.

**Article 51** For a case investigated by a lower-level supervisory organ pursuant to a designation of jurisdiction by a higher-level supervisory organ, after the investigation is concluded, if the subject of supervision is not within the jurisdiction of the lower-level supervisory organ, the case shall be handed over to the supervisory organ with the authority to make a decision on the disciplinary sanction according to law.

**Article 52** If a public official is suspected of illegal acts and has been placed under investigation, and it is inappropriate for them to continue performing their duties, the organ or unit that appointed the public official may decide to suspend them from performing their duties.

During the period when a public official is under investigation, they shall not leave the country or resign from their public office without the consent of the supervisory organ; the organ or unit to which the public official under investigation belongs, as well as the higher-level organ or unit, shall not arrange for their transfer, promotion, reward, punishment, or handle their retirement procedures.

**Article 53** If, during an investigation, a supervisory organ discovers that a public official has been subjected to false reports, accusations, or malicious framing, resulting in adverse effects, it shall promptly clarify the facts, restore their reputation, and eliminate the adverse effects in accordance with regulations.

**Article 54** If a public official receives a disciplinary sanction, the written decision on the disciplinary sanction shall be placed in their personal file. For those receiving a disciplinary sanction of demotion or above, the personnel department shall, within one month after the decision on the disciplinary sanction is made, handle the procedures for changing their position, salary, and other related benefits according to the management authority; under special circumstances, the processing period may be appropriately extended with approval, but the maximum extension shall not exceed six months.

## Chapter V: Review and Reconsideration

**Article 55** If a public official is dissatisfied with a disciplinary sanction decision made by a supervisory organ concerning themselves, they may apply for a review to the supervisory organ that made the decision according to law; if the public official is still dissatisfied with the review decision, they may apply for reconsideration to the supervisory organ at the next higher level.

If a supervisory organ discovers that a disciplinary sanction decision made by itself or a lower-level supervisory organ is indeed erroneous, it shall promptly correct it or order the lower-level supervisory organ to promptly correct it.

**Article 56** During the period of review or reconsideration, the execution of the original disciplinary sanction decision shall not be suspended.

A public official shall not have their disciplinary sanction aggravated because they filed for review or reconsideration.

**Article 57** Under any of the following circumstances, the review or reconsideration organ shall revoke the original disciplinary sanction decision, make a new decision, or order the supervisory organ that made the original decision to make a new decision:

(1) The illegal facts on which the disciplinary sanction was based are unclear or the evidence is insufficient;

(2) The legal procedures were violated, affecting the fair handling of the case;

(3) The decision on the disciplinary sanction was made by exceeding authority or abusing power.

**Article 58** Under any of the following circumstances, the review or reconsideration organ shall modify the original disciplinary sanction decision, or order the supervisory organ that made the original decision to modify it:

(1) The application of laws or regulations is indeed erroneous;

(2) The determination of the circumstances of the illegal act is indeed erroneous;

(3) The disciplinary sanction is inappropriate.

**Article 59** If the review or reconsideration organ finds that the facts determined in the disciplinary sanction decision are clear and the applicable laws are correct, it shall uphold the decision.

**Article 60** If a public official's disciplinary sanction decision is modified, requiring adjustment of the official's position, rank, grade, level, post, staff grade, or salary and benefits, etc., such adjustments shall be made according to regulations. If the disciplinary sanction decision is revoked, the public official's grade and salary and benefits shall be restored, corresponding positions, ranks, grades, posts, and staff grades shall be arranged according to the original position, rank, grade, post, and staff grade, and their reputation shall be restored within the scope where the original disciplinary sanction decision was announced. If property was confiscated or recovered erroneously, it shall be returned or compensated according to law.

If a public official's disciplinary sanction is revoked or mitigated due to the circumstances specified in Articles 57 and 58 of this Law, compensation shall be made for the losses incurred to their salary and benefits.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 61** If a relevant organ or unit refuses to adopt a supervisory suggestion without justifiable reasons, its higher-level organ or competent department shall order it to make corrections, circulate a notice of criticism against the organ or unit, and impose sanctions according to law on the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel.

**Article 62** If a relevant organ, unit, organization, or person falls under any of the following circumstances, its higher-level organ, competent department, appointing organ or unit, or the supervisory organ shall order it to make corrections and impose sanctions according to law:

(1) Refusing to execute a disciplinary sanction decision;

(2) Refusing to cooperate with or obstructing an investigation;

(3) Retaliating against an informant, witness, or investigator;

(4) Maliciously framing a public official;

(5) Other circumstances violating the provisions of this Law.

**Article 63** If a supervisory organ or its staff falls under any of the following circumstances, sanctions shall be imposed according to law on the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel:

(1) Handling problem clues in violation of regulations;

(2) Stealing or disclosing investigation work information, or disclosing information about reported matters, the receipt of reports, or the informant's identity;

(3) Using extortion or inducement to obtain confessions from the person under investigation or persons involved, or insulting, beating, mistreating, physically punishing, or imposing disguised physical punishment on them;

(4) Accepting property or other benefits from the person under investigation or persons involved;

(5) Handling property involved in the case in violation of regulations;

(6) Adopting investigation measures in violation of regulations;

(7) Using authority or the influence of their position to interfere with investigation work or seeking personal gain from cases;

(8) Causing a safety incident during case handling due to violation of regulations, or concealing, misreporting, or improperly handling a safety incident after it occurs;

(9) Violating procedural regulations such as those on withdrawal, causing adverse effects;

(10) Failing to accept and handle public officials' applications for review or reconsideration according to law;

(11) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

**Article 64** Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 65** The State Council and its relevant competent departments shall, based on the principles and spirit of this Law and in light of the actual circumstances of public institutions and state-owned enterprises, formulate specific provisions on disciplinary sanctions for illegal public officials in public institutions and state-owned enterprises.

**Article 66** The Central Military Commission may formulate relevant specific provisions based on this Law.

**Article 67** For cases that have been concluded before the implementation of this Law, if review or reconsideration is needed, the provisions in effect at that time shall apply. For cases that have not yet been concluded, if the provisions in effect at the time of the act did not consider it illegal, the provisions in effect at that time shall apply; if the provisions in effect at the time of the act considered it illegal, it shall be handled according to the provisions in effect at that time; however, if this Law does not consider it illegal or the punishment under this Law is lighter, this Law shall apply.

**Article 68** This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2020.
