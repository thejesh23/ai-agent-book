# Judges Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on February 28, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 30, 2001

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Judges Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

Revision adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to comprehensively advance the development of a high-quality judge team, strengthen the management and supervision of judges, protect the lawful rights and interests of judges, ensure that the people's courts independently exercise their adjudicative power in accordance with the law, guarantee that judges perform their duties lawfully, and ensure judicial fairness.

**Article 2** Judges are judicial personnel who exercise the state's adjudicative power in accordance with the law, including the presidents, vice-presidents, members of the judicial committees, chief judges, deputy chief judges, and judges of the Supreme People's Court, local people's courts at various levels, and special people's courts such as military courts.

**Article 3** Judges must faithfully implement the Constitution and laws, uphold social fairness and justice, and serve the people wholeheartedly.

**Article 4** Judges shall treat parties and other participants in proceedings fairly and apply the law equally to all individuals and organizations.

**Article 5** Judges shall be diligent and responsible, honest and upright, and adhere to professional ethics.

**Article 6** When adjudicating cases, judges shall base their decisions on facts, take the law as the criterion, and maintain an objective and impartial stance.

**Article 7** Judges who perform their duties in accordance with the law are protected by law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

## Chapter II Duties, Obligations, and Rights of Judges

**Article 8** The duties of judges include:

(1) Participating in collegial panel adjudication or sole judge adjudication of criminal, civil, administrative litigation, and state compensation cases in accordance with the law;

(2) Handling cases of extradition, judicial assistance, etc., in accordance with the law;

(3) Other duties as prescribed by law.

Judges are responsible for the cases they handle within the scope of their authority.

**Article 9** In addition to performing their adjudicative duties, the presidents, vice-presidents, members of the judicial committees, chief judges, and deputy chief judges of people's courts shall also perform duties commensurate with their positions.

**Article 10** Judges shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Strictly abide by the Constitution and laws;

(2) Handle cases impartially and not bend the law for personal gain;

(3) Protect the litigation rights of parties and other participants in proceedings in accordance with the law;

(4) Safeguard national interests and public interests, and protect the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations;

(5) Keep state secrets and secrets related to adjudicative work, and maintain the confidentiality of commercial secrets and personal privacy learned during the performance of duties;

(6) Accept legal supervision and supervision by the people in accordance with the law;

(7) Explain the law through the handling of cases, enhance the public's awareness of the rule of law, and promote the construction of a law-based society;

(8) Other obligations as prescribed by law.

**Article 11** Judges enjoy the following rights:

(1) The authority and working conditions necessary for performing their duties as judges;

(2) Not to be transferred, removed from office, demoted, dismissed, or disciplined without statutory reasons and procedures;

(3) The occupational security and welfare benefits commensurate with their duties as judges;

(4) The safety of their person, property, and residence is protected by law;

(5) The right to file petitions or complaints;

(6) Other rights as prescribed by law.

## Chapter III Qualifications and Selection of Judges

**Article 12** To serve as a judge, one must meet the following conditions:

(1) Possess the nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Uphold the Constitution of the People's Republic of China, the leadership of the Communist Party of China, and the socialist system;

(3) Have good political and professional qualities, as well as moral character;

(4) Be physically fit to perform duties normally;

(5) Hold a bachelor's degree in law from a regular institution of higher education and a bachelor's degree or higher; or hold a bachelor's degree or higher in a non-law field from a regular institution of higher education and a master's degree in law or a master's degree or higher in law; or hold a bachelor's degree or higher in a non-law field from a regular institution of higher education, possess another corresponding degree, and have professional legal knowledge;

(6) Have engaged in legal work for at least five years. For those holding a master's degree in law or a master's degree or doctorate in law, the required years of legal work may be reduced to four and three years, respectively;

(7) First-instance judges must pass the national uniform legal professional qualification examination to obtain legal professional qualifications.

In places where it is genuinely difficult to meet the educational requirements specified in item (5) of the preceding paragraph, the Supreme People's Court may, upon review and confirmation, relax the educational requirements for serving as a judge to a bachelor's degree from an institution of higher education for a certain period.

**Article 13** The following persons shall not serve as judges:

(1) Those who have received criminal punishment for committing a crime;

(2) Those who have been dismissed from public office;

(3) Those whose lawyer's or notary's practice certificates have been revoked or who have been expelled from an arbitration commission;

(4) Those who fall under other circumstances as prescribed by law.

**Article 14** First-instance judges shall be selected through examinations and assessments, based on the criteria of both moral integrity and professional competence, from among persons who meet the qualifications for judges.

The president of a people's court shall have professional legal knowledge and legal professional experience. Vice-presidents and members of the judicial committee shall be selected from among judges, prosecutors, or other persons who meet the qualifications for judges.

**Article 15** People's courts may, based on the needs of adjudicative work, openly select judges from among lawyers or legal professionals such as legal educators and researchers.

In addition to meeting the qualifications for judges, lawyers participating in open selection must have practiced law for at least five years, have extensive practical experience, and have a good professional reputation. Legal educators and researchers participating in open selection must hold an intermediate or higher professional title, have engaged in teaching or research for at least five years, and possess outstanding research capabilities and corresponding research achievements.

**Article 16** Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish judge selection committees responsible for reviewing the professional competence of candidates for first-instance judges.

The members of a provincial judge selection committee shall include representatives of judges from local people's courts at various levels, other legal professionals, and relevant parties, with judges representing no less than one-third of the total.

The daily work of a provincial judge selection committee shall be undertaken by the internal functional department of the Higher People's Court.

For the selection of judges for the Supreme People's Court, a Supreme People's Court judge selection committee shall be established to review the professional competence of candidates.

**Article 17** First-instance judges shall generally serve in basic people's courts. Judges of higher people's courts shall generally be selected level by level; judges of the Supreme People's Court and Higher People's Courts may be selected from the two lower levels of people's courts. Judges participating in selection for a higher people's court must have served as judges in a lower people's court for a certain number of years and have relevant work experience for the selected position.

## Chapter IV Appointment and Removal of Judges

**Article 18** The appointment and removal of judges shall be handled in accordance with the authority and procedures for appointment and removal as prescribed by the Constitution and laws.

The President of the Supreme People's Court is elected and removed by the National People's Congress. The vice-presidents, members of the judicial committee, chief judges, deputy chief judges, and judges are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the President.

The chief judges and deputy chief judges of the circuit courts of the Supreme People's Court are appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the President.

The presidents of local people's courts at various levels are elected and removed by the corresponding people's congresses. The vice-presidents, members of the judicial committee, chief judges, deputy chief judges, and judges are appointed and removed by the standing committees of the corresponding people's congresses upon the recommendation of the presidents.

The presidents of intermediate people's courts established within regions in provinces, autonomous regions, and in municipalities directly under the Central Government are appointed and removed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government upon the nomination of the chairperson's council. The vice-presidents, members of the judicial committee, chief judges, deputy chief judges, and judges are appointed and removed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government upon the recommendation of the presidents of the Higher People's Courts.

The presidents, vice-presidents, members of the judicial committee, chief judges, deputy chief judges, and judges of the people's courts at various levels of the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps and special people's courts are appointed and removed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

**Article 19** After being selected in accordance with statutory procedures, judges shall publicly take an oath to the Constitution upon assuming office.

**Article 20** A judge's position shall be removed in accordance with the law under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Loss of nationality of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Transfer out of the people's court where the judge serves;

(3) Change of position where retention of the judge position is unnecessary, or the judge's application for removal of the judge position is approved;

(4) Failure to be competent for the judge position after assessment;

(5) Inability to perform duties for an extended period due to health reasons;

(6) Retirement;

(7) Resignation or dismissal as required by law;

(8) Unfitness to continue serving due to disciplinary or legal violations.

**Article 21** If it is discovered that a judge has been appointed in violation of the conditions stipulated in this Law, the appointing authority shall revoke the appointment. If a higher people's court discovers that the appointment of a judge by a lower people's court violates the conditions stipulated in this Law, it shall recommend that the lower people's court request the appointing authority to revoke the appointment in accordance with the law.

**Article 22** Judges shall not concurrently serve as members of the standing committees of people's congresses, hold positions in administrative organs, supervisory organs, or procuratorial organs, hold positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations or public institutions, or concurrently serve as lawyers, arbitrators, or notaries.

**Article 23** Judges who are spouses, direct blood relatives, collateral blood relatives within three generations, or close in-laws shall not concurrently hold the following positions:

(1) President, vice-president, member of the judicial committee, chief judge, or deputy chief judge of the same people's court;

(2) President, vice-president, and judge of the same people's court;

(3) Chief judge, deputy chief judge, and judge of the same adjudication division;

(4) President or vice-president of two adjacent levels of people's courts (upper and lower).

**Article 24** A judge shall implement a system of recusal from office if the judge's spouse, parents, or children fall under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Serving as a partner or founder of a law firm within the jurisdiction of the people's court where the judge serves;

(2) Acting as a litigation representative or defender in the capacity of a lawyer, or providing other paid legal services to parties in litigation cases within the jurisdiction of the people's court where the judge serves.

## Chapter V Management of Judges

**Article 25** Judges are managed under a post-based quota system. The quota of judge posts is determined based on factors such as the number of cases, economic and social development, population size, and the level of the people's court. Total control and dynamic management are implemented within provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, prioritizing the needs of basic people's courts and people's courts with a high volume of cases.

Vacancies in judge posts shall be filled promptly in accordance with procedures.

The quota of judge posts for the Supreme People's Court is determined by the Supreme People's Court in consultation with relevant departments.

**Article 26** Judges are managed under a separate rank system.

The ranks of judges are divided into twelve levels, which are, in descending order: Chief Justice, Grand Justice First Class, Grand Justice Second Class, Senior Justice First Class, Senior Justice Second Class, Senior Justice Third Class, Senior Justice Fourth Class, Justice First Class, Justice Second Class, Justice Third Class, Justice Fourth Class, and Justice Fifth Class.

**Article 27** The President of the Supreme People's Court is the Chief Justice.

**Article 28** The determination of a judge's rank is based on the judge's moral integrity and professional competence, professional level, actual performance in adjudicative work, and years of service.

Promotion of judge ranks adopts a combination of regular promotion and selective merit-based promotion. Judges in frontline case-handling positions who are exceptionally outstanding or needed for special work may be specially selected for promotion.

**Article 29** Specific measures for the establishment, determination, and promotion of judge ranks shall be separately stipulated by the state.

**Article 30** First-instance judges shall undergo a unified pre-service training system.

**Article 31** Judges shall receive planned political, theoretical, and professional training.

Training for judges shall integrate theory with practice, be tailored to needs, and emphasize practical results.

**Article 32** The training status of a judge serves as one of the bases for the judge's appointment and rank promotion.

**Article 33** Judge training institutions undertake the task of training judges in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 34** A judge applying for resignation shall submit a written application. Upon approval, the judge's position shall be removed in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law.

**Article 35** The dismissal of a judge shall be carried out by removing the judge's position in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law.

The decision to dismiss a judge shall be made in accordance with the management authority. The dismissal decision shall be notified in writing to the dismissed judge, stating the reasons and basis for the decision.

**Article 36** Within two years after leaving a people's court, a former judge shall not act as a litigation representative or defender in the capacity of a lawyer.

After leaving a people's court, a former judge shall not act as a litigation representative or defender in cases handled by the court where the judge previously served, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent the party in litigation or to defend the party.

After being expelled, a former judge shall not act as a litigation representative or defender, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent the party in litigation or to defend the party.

**Article 37** Judges may, as required by work and upon selection or approval by their unit, assist in practical teaching and research activities at institutions of higher education or research institutes, and shall comply with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VI Assessment, Rewards, and Discipline of Judges

**Article 38** People's courts shall establish judge assessment committees responsible for the assessment of judges in their respective courts.

**Article 39** A judge assessment committee shall consist of five to nine members.

The director of the judge assessment committee shall be the president of the court.

**Article 40** The assessment of judges shall be comprehensive, objective, and fair, combining regular assessment with annual assessment.

**Article 41** The content of the assessment of judges includes: actual performance in adjudicative work, professional ethics, professional level, work ability, and adjudicative work style. The focus of the assessment is on actual performance in adjudicative work.

**Article 42** The results of the annual assessment are divided into four grades: excellent, competent, basically competent, and incompetent.

The assessment results serve as the basis for adjusting a judge's rank, salary, rewards, disciplinary actions, removal from office, demotion, and dismissal.

**Article 43** The assessment results shall be notified to the judge in writing. If a judge has objections to the assessment results, the judge may apply for a review.

**Article 44** Judges who achieve significant results or contributions in adjudicative work, or who have other outstanding deeds, shall be given rewards.

**Article 45** Judges shall be given rewards for any of the following performances:

(1) Achieving significant results in impartial judicial work;

(2) Achieving outstanding results in summarizing practical experience in adjudication, providing guidance for adjudicative work;

(3) Making significant achievements and contributions in handling major cases, dealing with emergencies, and undertaking special important tasks;

(4) Proposing reform suggestions for adjudicative work that are adopted and yield significant results;

(5) Proposing judicial suggestions that are adopted, or carrying out legal publicity, guiding mediation organizations in mediating various disputes, with significant results;

(6) Having other meritorious deeds.

Rewards for judges shall be handled in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 46** Judges who engage in any of the following acts shall be subject to disciplinary action; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Accepting bribes, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, or bending the law in adjudication;

(2) Concealing, forging, altering, or intentionally destroying evidence or case materials;

(3) Disclosing state secrets, secrets related to adjudicative work, commercial secrets, or personal privacy;

(4) Intentionally violating laws and regulations in handling cases;

(5) Causing erroneous adjudication results and serious consequences due to gross negligence;

(6) Delaying case handling, causing delays in work;

(7) Using authority to seek personal gain for oneself or others;

(8) Accepting benefits from parties or their representatives, or meeting with parties or their representatives in violation of relevant regulations;

(9) Engaging in or participating in for-profit activities in violation of relevant regulations, or concurrently holding positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations;

(10) Having other disciplinary or illegal acts.

Disciplinary actions for judges shall be handled in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 47** If a judge is suspected of disciplinary or illegal acts and has been placed under investigation or inquiry, and is unfit to continue performing duties, the judge's duties shall be temporarily suspended in accordance with the management authority and prescribed procedures.

**Article 48** The Supreme People's Court and provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish judge disciplinary committees responsible for reviewing and determining, from a professional perspective, whether a judge has engaged in the acts of violating adjudicative duties as specified in items (4) and (5) of Article 46 of this Law, and for issuing review opinions on whether the act constitutes intentional violation of duties, gross negligence, ordinary negligence, or no violation of duties. After the judge disciplinary committee issues its review opinion, the people's court shall make a decision on whether to impose discipline in accordance with relevant regulations and take corresponding action.

A judge disciplinary committee shall consist of representatives of judges, other legal professionals, and relevant parties, with judges representing no less than half of the total.

The daily work of the Supreme People's Court judge disciplinary committee and provincial judge disciplinary committees shall be undertaken by the internal functional departments of the relevant people's courts.

**Article 49** When a judge disciplinary committee deliberates on disciplinary matters, the judge concerned has the right to request the recusal of relevant personnel and the right to make statements, present evidence, and offer defenses.

**Article 50** The review opinion issued by the judge disciplinary committee shall be served on the judge concerned. If the judge concerned has objections to the review opinion, the judge may raise them with the disciplinary committee, which shall review the objections and their reasons and make a decision.

**Article 51** The specific procedures for the judge disciplinary committee to deliberate on disciplinary matters shall be determined by the Supreme People's Court in consultation with relevant departments.

## Chapter VII Occupational Security for Judges

**Article 52** People's courts shall establish judge rights and interests protection committees to safeguard the lawful rights and interests of judges and ensure that judges perform their duties in accordance with the law.

**Article 53** A judge shall not be transferred from an adjudicative post except under the following circumstances:

(1) Recusal from office is required by regulations;

(2) Job rotation is required by regulations;

(3) Job adjustment is necessary due to institutional restructuring, dissolution, merger, or reduction of staffing quotas;

(4) Unfitness to work in an adjudicative post due to disciplinary or legal violations;

(5) Other circumstances as prescribed by law.

**Article 54** No unit or individual may require a judge to engage in matters beyond the scope of statutory duties.

For any act of interference with a judge's handling of a case, the judge has the right to refuse and to comprehensively and truthfully record and report it; if there are disciplinary or illegal circumstances, the relevant authorities shall pursue the responsibility of the responsible persons and the actors according to the severity of the circumstances.

**Article 55** The professional dignity and personal safety of judges are protected by law.

No unit or individual may retaliate against a judge or the judge's close relatives.Acts of retaliation, framing, insult, defamation, violent assault, threats, intimidation, harassment, or other illegal conduct against judges and their close relatives shall be severely punished in accordance with the law.

Article 56: If a judge's reputation is harmed due to false reports, false accusations, framing, insults, or defamation arising from their lawful performance of duties, the people's court shall, in coordination with relevant departments, promptly clarify the facts, eliminate adverse effects, and pursue the legal liability of the relevant unit or individual in accordance with the law.

Article 57: If a judge or their close relatives face personal safety risks due to the judge's lawful performance of duties, the people's court and public security organs shall take necessary protective measures, such as personal protection and prohibiting specific individuals from contact, for the judge and their close relatives.

Article 58: Judges shall have a salary system commensurate with their duties, enjoy the salary benefits prescribed by the state according to their judge rank, and establish a mechanism for synchronized adjustment with civil servant salaries.

The salary system for judges shall be separately prescribed by the state based on the characteristics of judicial work.

Article 59: Judges shall have a system of regular salary increases.

Those assessed as excellent or competent in the annual evaluation may have their salary grades raised in accordance with regulations.

Article 60: Judges shall enjoy the allowances, subsidies, bonuses, insurance, and welfare benefits prescribed by the state.

Article 61: If a judge becomes disabled due to work-related reasons, they shall enjoy the disability benefits prescribed by the state. If a judge dies in the line of duty, dies from work-related causes, or dies of illness, their relatives shall enjoy the pensions and preferential treatment prescribed by the state.

Article 62: The retirement system for judges shall be separately prescribed by the state based on the characteristics of judicial work.

Article 63: After retirement, judges shall enjoy the pensions and other benefits prescribed by the state.

Article 64: If state organs or their staff infringe upon the rights of judges as stipulated in Article 11 of this Law, the judge has the right to file a complaint.

Article 65: If disciplinary actions or personnel decisions against a judge are erroneous, they shall be promptly corrected; if reputation is harmed, the reputation shall be restored, adverse effects eliminated, and an apology made; if economic losses are incurred, compensation shall be provided. The directly responsible persons for retaliation shall be held accountable in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8: Supplementary Provisions

Article 66: The state implements a unified legal professional qualification examination system for newly appointed judges, organized and implemented by the judicial administration department of the State Council in consultation with the Supreme People's Court and other relevant departments.

Article 67: Judge assistants in people's courts shall, under the guidance of judges, be responsible for reviewing case materials, drafting legal documents, and other trial support tasks.

People's courts shall strengthen the construction of the judge assistant team to reserve talent for judge selection.

Article 68: Where this Law provides for the rights, obligations, and management systems of judges, the provisions of this Law shall apply; where this Law does not provide, the relevant laws and regulations on civil servant management shall apply.

Article 69: This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 2019.
