# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of the Status and Rights and Interests of Military Personnel

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 10, 2021

Effective as of August 1, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to protect the status and lawful rights and interests of military personnel, to motivate them to fulfill their duties and missions, to make the military profession one that is respected by the whole society, and to promote the modernization of national defense and the armed forces.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "military personnel" refers to officers, non-commissioned officers, and conscripts who are on active service in the People's Liberation Army.

Article 3 Military personnel shoulder the sacred duty and lofty mission of safeguarding national sovereignty, security, and development interests, and protecting the peaceful labor of the people.

Article 4 The military profession is one that is respected by the whole society. The State and society respect and give preferential treatment to military personnel, ensuring that they enjoy a status and rights and interests commensurate with the characteristics of their profession, the duties and missions they bear, and the contributions they make, and regularly carry out various forms of activities to support the military and give preferential treatment to the families of military personnel.

All State organs and armed forces, political parties and people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations have the responsibility to protect the status and rights and interests of military personnel in accordance with the law, and all citizens shall safeguard the lawful rights and interests of military personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The work of protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, take serving the construction of the combat effectiveness of the armed forces as its fundamental purpose, and follow the principles of unifying rights and obligations, combining material guarantees with spiritual incentives, and ensuring that the level of protection is commensurate with national economic and social development.

Article 6 The Political Work Department of the Central Military Commission, the competent department for the work of ex-servicemen of the State Council, and the relevant organs of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council, as well as the relevant departments of the Central Military Commission, shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, do a good job in protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the work of protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel within their respective administrative regions. The political work departments of military units at or above the regimental level shall be responsible for the work of protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel within their units.

The provincial military commands (garrison commands, garrison commands), military sub-commands (garrison commands), and the people's armed forces departments of counties, autonomous counties, cities, and municipal districts shall be responsible for the liaison and coordination between the people's governments of their respective administrative regions and military units regarding the protection of the status and rights and interests of military personnel, and shall establish working coordination mechanisms as needed.

Township people's governments, sub-district offices, and grassroots self-governing mass organizations shall, in accordance with their duties, do a good job in protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel.

Article 7 The funds required for the protection of the status and rights and interests of military personnel shall be included in the budgets of the central and local governments in accordance with the principle of matching powers and expenditure responsibilities.

Article 8 The relevant organs of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council, the relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, and the organs at all levels of the armed forces shall take the work of protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel as an important part of the evaluation of activities such as supporting the military and giving preferential treatment to the families of military personnel, supporting the government and cherishing the people, and the assessment of the persons in charge of the relevant units and their staff.

Article 9 The State encourages and guides social forces such as people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individuals to provide support for the protection of the rights and interests of military personnel through donations, volunteer services, and other means in accordance with the law, and those that meet the prescribed conditions shall enjoy tax incentives and other policies in accordance with the law.

Article 10 August 1 of each year is the Army Day of the Chinese People's Liberation Army. People's governments at all levels and military units shall organize celebrations, commemorations, and other activities on Army Day.

Article 11 Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the work of protecting the status and rights and interests of military personnel shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

## Chapter II Status of Military Personnel

Article 12 Military personnel are the basic members of the national armed forces under the leadership of the Communist Party of China. They must be loyal to the motherland, loyal to the Communist Party of China, obey the Party's command, resolutely follow orders, and conscientiously fulfill the important duties and missions of consolidating the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist system.

Article 13 Military personnel are the people's soldiers. They shall love the people, serve the people wholeheartedly, protect the lives and property of the people, and when the lives and property of the people are seriously threatened, they shall step forward and actively provide assistance.

Article 14 Military personnel are a strong force for safeguarding national sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity. They shall possess the combat spirit and capabilities required to consolidate national defense, resist aggression, and defend the motherland, maintain a state of readiness in accordance with the requirements of actual combat, diligently practice combat skills, be fearless of sacrifice, be capable of winning battles, and resolutely complete tasks.

Article 15 Military personnel are an important force in the modernization of socialism with Chinese characteristics. They shall actively devote themselves to the cause of comprehensively building a modern socialist country and participate in emergency rescue and disaster relief operations in accordance with the law.

Article 16 Military personnel enjoy the political rights provided for by the Constitution and laws, participate in the election of members of the organs of state power in accordance with the law, and participate in the management of state affairs, economic and cultural undertakings, and social affairs in accordance with the law.

Article 17 The armed forces practice equality between officers and soldiers. Military personnel are equal in political status and personal dignity and shall respect and treat each other equally.

The armed forces shall establish and improve democratic systems such as the congress of military representatives and the military committee to ensure the military personnel's right to know, the right to participate, the right to make suggestions, and the right to supervise.

Article 18 Military personnel must modelly abide by the Constitution and laws, conscientiously fulfill the civic obligations provided for by the Constitution and laws, strictly abide by military regulations and military discipline, maintain excellent conduct, and take the lead in practicing the core socialist values.

Article 19 The State provides guarantees for military personnel to perform their duties, and the acts of military personnel in performing their duties in accordance with the law are protected by law.

If military personnel cause damage to the lawful rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations in the course of performing their tasks, the State shall provide compensation or indemnity in accordance with the relevant provisions.

Citizens, legal persons, and other organizations shall provide the necessary support and assistance for military personnel to perform their duties in accordance with the law.

Article 20 The specific rights and interests enjoyed and the specific obligations undertaken by military personnel due to the performance of their duties shall be provided for by this Law and relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter III Maintenance of Honor

Article 21 The honor of military personnel is the praise and encouragement given by the State and society to military personnel for their dedication to national defense, the construction of the armed forces, and socialist modernization. It is a spiritual force that boosts the morale of military personnel and enhances the combat effectiveness of the armed forces.

The State maintains the honor of military personnel and encourages them to uphold and cherish their honor.

Article 22 The armed forces shall strengthen education in patriotism, collectivism, and revolutionary heroism, enhance the honor consciousness of military personnel, cultivate revolutionary military personnel of a new era with soul, capability, courage, and virtue, and forge a strong military force with faith as firm as iron, conviction as firm as iron, discipline as firm as iron, and responsibility as firm as iron.

Article 23 The State shall adopt various forms of publicity, education, reward, incentive, and guarantee measures to cultivate the sense of professional mission, pride, and honor of military personnel, and stimulate their enthusiasm, initiative, and creativity in making contributions and serving the country.

Article 24 The whole society shall study the glorious history of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, publicize the achievements and the spirit of sacrifice and dedication of military personnel, and create a good atmosphere for maintaining the honor of military personnel.

The national defense education courses offered by schools at all levels and of all types shall include the glorious history of the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the heroic deeds of military personnel.

Article 25 The State shall establish and improve the military honor system, and confer honors and commendations on military personnel who have made outstanding achievements and contributions by awarding medals, honorary titles, recording merits, issuing commendations, presenting citations, and issuing commemorative medals, so as to commend the dedication and sacrifice of military personnel to the country and the people.

Article 26 With the approval of their military unit, military personnel may accept honors conferred by local people's governments, people's organizations, social organizations, etc., as well as honors conferred by international organizations and other countries, armed forces, etc.

Article 27 Military personnel who have received honors and commendations shall enjoy corresponding courtesies and treatment. Military personnel who have received honors and commendations for performing combat missions shall enjoy courtesies and treatment at a level higher than that for peacetime.

The names and achievements of military personnel who have received honors and commendations and who have performed combat missions shall be recorded in the book of merits, the roll of honor, local chronicles, and other historical records in accordance with the provisions.

Article 28 The relevant organs of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council, the relevant organs at the local and military levels, and media such as radio, television, newspapers, and the Internet shall actively publicize the advanced models and heroic deeds of military personnel.

Article 29 The State and society respect and remember military personnel who have sacrificed their lives for the country, the people, and the nation, and respect and extend courtesies to their surviving family members.

The State shall establish memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs for the public to pay their respects, mourn and commemorate heroes and martyrs, and carry out commemorative and educational activities.

The State shall promote the construction of military cemeteries. After the death of a military personnel, if the prescribed conditions are met, they may be buried in a military cemetery.

Article 30 The State shall establish a system of ceremonies and courtesies for military personnel. Appropriate ceremonies shall be held on occasions such as when citizens enlist in the military and when military personnel retire from active service; mourning ceremonies shall be held on occasions such as the burial of martyrs and military personnel who have died in the line of duty.

People's governments at all levels shall organize activities to visit and extend condolences to military units, military families, and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness on major holidays and commemorative days, and shall invite military personnel, family members of military personnel, and representatives of the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness to participate in important celebrations and commemorative activities.

Article 31 Local people's governments shall hang honor plaques for the families of military personnel and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness. When military personnel receive honors and commendations, the relevant departments of the local people's government and military organs shall deliver good news to their families and organize publicity work.

Article 32 The honor and reputation of military personnel are protected by law.

The honor acquired by military personnel shall be enjoyed by them for life and shall not be revoked except for statutory reasons and through statutory procedures.

No organization or individual may, in any way, slander or disparage the honor of military personnel, insult or defame their reputation, or intentionally damage or deface their honor insignia.

## Chapter IV Guarantee of Treatment

Article 33 The State shall establish a system for guaranteeing the treatment of military personnel to ensure that they can fulfill their duties and missions and to guarantee the living standards of military personnel and their families.

For military personnel performing combat missions and major non-war military operations, as well as those working in remote and difficult areas or in special positions, preferential treatment and guarantees shall be provided.

Article 34 The State shall establish a relatively independent, distinctive, and comparatively advantageous salary and treatment system for military personnel. Officers and non-commissioned officers shall be subject to a salary system, while conscripts shall be subject to a supply-based living allowance system. Military personnel shall enjoy preferential policies for individual income tax.

The State shall establish a normal growth mechanism for the salaries and treatment of military personnel.

The structure, standards, and adjustment methods for the salaries and treatment of military personnel shall be prescribed by the Central Military Commission.

Article 35 The State shall guarantee the housing treatment of military personnel by combining military guarantees, government guarantees, and market allocation, and by combining in-kind guarantees with monetary subsidies.

Military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions shall be entitled to military apartment housing or resettlement housing guarantees.

The State shall establish and improve the housing provident fund system and housing subsidy system for military personnel. Military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions for purchasing housing shall be supported by the State with preferential policies.

Article 36 The State shall guarantee that military personnel enjoy free medical care, disease prevention, convalescence, rehabilitation, and other treatment in accordance with the provisions.

The expenses incurred by military personnel for medical treatment in local medical institutions shall, if the prescribed conditions are met, be covered by the military.

Article 37 The State shall implement a military insurance system that reflects the characteristics of the military profession and is linked to the social insurance system, and shall supplement military insurance items in a timely manner to ensure the insurance treatment of military personnel.

The State encourages and supports commercial insurance institutions to provide exclusive insurance products for military personnel and their family members.

Article 38 Military personnel shall have the right to rest and leave, including annual leave and home leave. Economic compensation shall be given to those who are unable to take leave or complete their leave due to work needs.

If the spouse and children of a military personnel live separately from the military personnel, they may visit the military personnel at the unit where the military personnel is stationed. If the spouse of a military personnel visits the military personnel at the unit, the spouse's employer shall arrange leave in accordance with the provisions and guarantee the corresponding salary and treatment, and shall not dismiss, terminate, or dissolve the labor relationship because the spouse enjoys the home leave. The travel expenses for the spouse, minor children, and adult children who cannot live independently of a military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions shall be covered by the unit where the military personnel is stationed.

Article 39 The State shall establish and improve the education and training system for military personnel, guarantee the right to education of military personnel, organize and support military personnel to participate in professional and cultural study and training, and improve their ability to perform their duties and their ability to find employment and start businesses after retirement from active service.

Article 40 The lawful rights and interests of female military personnel are protected by law. The armed forces shall reasonably arrange the work tasks and rest and leave of female military personnel according to their characteristics, and provide special protection for female military personnel in terms of childbirth, health, etc.

Article 41 The State provides special protection for the marriage of military personnel and prohibits any act that undermines the marriage of military personnel.

Article 42 The spouses, minor children, and adult children who cannot live independently of officers and non-commissioned officers who meet the prescribed conditions may apply to have their household registration moved to the military personnel's place of service; the parents of military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions may apply to have their household registration moved to the military personnel's place of service in accordance with the provisions. If both spouses are military personnel, their children may choose to have their household registration moved to the place of service of either parent.

If the place of active service of a military personnel changes, the family members who have already moved with the military personnel may move their household registration with them, or choose to move their household registration to the original place of household registration of the military personnel or the military personnel's spouse, or to the place of household registration of the military personnel's parents or the military personnel's spouse's parents.

The relevant departments of local people's governments and the relevant military units shall handle the relevant procedures for the relocation of household registration of the family members of military personnel in a timely and efficient manner.

Article 43 The State shall guarantee the household registration management and related rights and interests of military personnel and their family members.

Citizens shall retain their household registration when they enlist in the military.

Military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions may enjoy the relevant rights and interests in education, elderly care, medical care, housing security, etc., of the household registration population at the place of active service.

The measures for the household registration management and the protection of related rights and interests of military personnel shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 44 The State shall provide proper resettlement and corresponding preferential treatment and guarantees for military personnel who retire from active service in accordance with the law, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the laws and regulations on the protection of ex-servicemen.

## Chapter V Pension and Preferential Treatment

Article 45 The State and society respect the dedication and sacrifice made by military personnel and their families for national defense and the construction of the armed forces, give preferential treatment to military personnel and their family members, provide pensions and preferential treatment to the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness, and guarantee the lives of disabled military personnel.

The State shall establish a pension and preferential treatment guarantee system, reasonably determine the standards for pensions and preferential treatment, and gradually raise the level of pensions and preferential treatment.

Article 46 Family members of military personnel shall enjoy the preferential treatment guarantees provided for by laws and regulations by presenting the certificates issued by the relevant departments. The specific measures shall be formulated by the relevant departments of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 47 People's governments at all levels shall ensure that the recipients of pensions and preferential treatment enjoy the universal benefits for citizens while also enjoying the corresponding pension and preferential treatment benefits.

Article 48 The State implements a system of pensions for the death of military personnel.

If a military personnel is assessed as a martyr after death, the State shall issue a martyr certificate to the surviving family members of the martyr, and ensure that the surviving family members of the martyr enjoy the prescribed martyr's commendation allowance, pension, and other treatment.

If a military personnel dies in the line of duty or dies of illness, the State shall issue a certificate to the surviving family members, and ensure that the surviving family members enjoy the prescribed pension and other treatment.

Article 49 The State implements a system of pensions for the disability of military personnel.

If a military personnel becomes disabled due to war, in the line of duty, or due to illness, the disability grade shall be assessed and a certificate shall be issued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, and the military personnel shall enjoy a disability pension and other treatment. If the prescribed conditions are met, the military personnel shall be properly resettled by means of job placement, support, retirement, etc.

Article 50 The State provides housing preferential treatment to the family members of military personnel and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness.

If the family members of military personnel and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness meet the prescribed conditions for applying for affordable housing, or if they live in rural areas and have housing difficulties, the local people's government shall give them priority in solving the problem.

If the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness fall under the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the local people's government shall provide them with preferential treatment.

Article 51 Public medical institutions shall provide preferential treatment services for military personnel seeking medical treatment. The family members of military personnel and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness shall enjoy medical preferential treatment when seeking medical treatment in military medical institutions and public medical institutions.

The State encourages private medical institutions to provide preferential treatment services for military personnel, family members of military personnel, and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness.

The State and society shall provide special guarantees for the medical care of disabled military personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 52 The State guarantees the right to employment placement for the spouses of military personnel in accordance with the law. Organs, people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations shall fulfill the obligation to accept the employment placement of the spouses of military personnel in accordance with the law.

If the spouse of a military personnel worked in an organ or public institution before moving with the military personnel, the people's government of the place of resettlement shall arrange for the spouse to work in a corresponding work unit in accordance with the relevant provisions; if the spouse worked in another unit or was unemployed, the people's government of the place of resettlement shall provide employment guidance and employment training, and give priority to assisting in employment. The local people's government shall give priority to arranging employment for the surviving family members of martyrs and military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and for the spouses of military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions.

Article 53 The State encourages employers with labor needs to give priority to arranging employment for the family members who have moved with military personnel. When recruiting new employees, state-owned enterprises shall employ a certain proportion of the family members who have moved with military personnel according to the appropriate proportion of labor needs; qualified private enterprises may employ a certain proportion of the family members who have moved with military personnel according to the appropriate proportion of labor needs when recruiting new employees.

Article 54 The State encourages and supports the spouses of military personnel to find their own employment or start their own businesses. If the spouse of a military personnel engages in self-employment, the State shall provide support in accordance with the relevant preferential policies.

Article 55 The State provides educational preferential treatment for the children of military personnel. Local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall provide local high-quality educational resources for the children of military personnel and create conditions for them to receive a good education.

When enrolling in public compulsory education schools and inclusive kindergartens, the children of military personnel may enroll in the place of household registration of themselves, their parents, grandparents, maternal grandparents, or other legal guardians, or in the place of residence of their parents or the place where the military unit is stationed, and enjoy the local preferential education policies for the children of military personnel.

When applying for admission to regular senior high schools and secondary vocational schools, the children of military personnel shall be given priority admission under equal conditions; the children of martyrs and military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and the children of military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions, shall enjoy preferential treatment in admission and other aspects in accordance with the local preferential education policies for the children of military personnel.

When applying for admission to institutions of higher learning, the children of military personnel who have died in the line of duty and the children of military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions shall be given priority admission in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State; the children of martyrs shall enjoy preferential treatment such as bonus points.

The children of martyrs and the children of military personnel who meet the prescribed conditions shall enjoy student financial aid policies such as scholarships, grants, and exemption from relevant fees in accordance with the provisions.

The State encourages and supports qualified private schools to provide educational preferential treatment for the children of military personnel and the children of martyrs and military personnel who have died in the line of duty.

Article 56 The family members of military personnel and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who have died in the line of duty, and military personnel who have died of illness, who meet the prescribed conditions for applying for centralized support, hospitalization, or short-term convalescence in state-run homes for the honored or preferential treatment hospitals, shall enjoy priority and preferential treatment; those who apply for elderly care in public elderly care institutions shall be given priority placement under equal conditions.**Article 57** Military personnel, their family members, and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who died in the line of duty, and military personnel who died of illness are entitled to priority and discounted services when visiting parks, museums, memorial halls, exhibition halls, places of historical interest, and in cultural and tourism services.

Military personnel may ride on city buses, trams, ferries, and rail transit vehicles free of charge. Military personnel, the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who died in the line of duty, and military personnel who died of illness, as well as their accompanying family members, enjoy priority ticketing and boarding (boarding ships, boarding vehicles) services on domestic trains, ships, long-distance buses, and civil aviation flights. Disabled military personnel enjoy discounted fares.

**Article 58** Local people's governments and military units shall provide assistance and condolences to military families whose basic living conditions have become severely difficult due to natural disasters, accidents, serious illnesses, or other reasons.

**Article 59** Local people's governments and military units shall provide necessary assistance to military families facing difficulties in areas such as the enrollment and childcare of minor children, and the care of the elderly.

The State encourages and supports enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, other organizations, and individuals in providing aid services to military families in difficulty.

**Article 60** If the legitimate rights and interests of military personnel, their family members, and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who died in the line of duty, and military personnel who died of illness are infringed upon, they have the right to file complaints and accusations with the relevant state organs and military units. The state organs and military units responsible for accepting such complaints shall handle them promptly in accordance with the law and shall not shirk responsibility or delay. If a lawsuit is filed with a people's court in accordance with the law, the people's court shall give priority to filing, hearing, and executing the case, and the people's procuratorate may support the prosecution.

**Article 61** If military personnel, their family members, and the surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who died in the line of duty, and military personnel who died of illness encounter difficulties in safeguarding their legitimate rights and interests, legal aid institutions shall provide legal aid on a priority basis in accordance with the law, and judicial organs shall provide judicial relief on a priority basis in accordance with the law.

**Article 62** If the infringement of military personnel's honor, reputation, or other related legitimate rights and interests seriously affects their effective performance of duties and missions, thereby harming the public interest, the people's procuratorate may initiate a public interest lawsuit in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law and the Administrative Procedure Law.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 63** If state organs and their staff, military units and their staff violate the provisions of this Law by abusing their power, neglecting their duties, or engaging in favoritism and malpractice in the work of safeguarding the status and rights of military personnel, they shall be ordered to make corrections by their unit, the competent department, or the higher-level authority; the responsible leaders and directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 64** If mass organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations violate the provisions of this Law by failing to fulfill their preferential treatment obligations, the relevant departments shall order them to make corrections; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 65** If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, through mass media or other means, one slanders or belittles the honor of military personnel, insults or defames the reputation of military personnel, or intentionally damages or defiles the honor insignia of military personnel, the public security, culture and tourism, press and publication, film, radio and television, cyberspace administration, or other relevant competent departments shall, within their respective functions and powers, order corrections and handle the matter in accordance with the law; if mental damage is caused, the victim has the right to claim compensation for mental damages.

**Article 66** If one fraudulently obtains or obtains the relevant honors, benefits, or pensions and preferential treatments stipulated in this Law by means of impersonation, fraud, or forging supporting materials, the relevant departments shall cancel them and impose administrative penalties such as confiscation of illegal gains in accordance with the law.

**Article 67** If one violates the provisions of this Law and infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of military personnel, causing property loss or other damage, one shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

If one violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes an act violating public security administration, one shall be given a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 68** For the purposes of this Law, "family members of military personnel" refers to the spouse, parents (guardians), minor children, and adult children who are unable to live independently of the military personnel.

For the purposes of this Law, "surviving family members of martyrs, military personnel who died in the line of duty, and military personnel who died of illness" refers to the spouse, parents (guardians), children, and siblings whom the martyr, military personnel who died in the line of duty, or military personnel who died of illness had the obligation to support.

**Article 69** This Law applies to officers, non-commissioned officers, and conscripts serving on active duty in the Chinese People's Armed Police Force.

**Article 70** Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local actual conditions, formulate specific measures to safeguard the status and rights of military personnel in accordance with this Law.

**Article 71** This Law shall come into force on August 1, 2021.
