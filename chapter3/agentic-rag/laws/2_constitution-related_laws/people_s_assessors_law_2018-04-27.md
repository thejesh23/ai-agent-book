# People's Assessors Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure citizens' participation in judicial activities in accordance with the law, promote judicial fairness, and enhance judicial credibility.

Article 2 Citizens have the right and obligation to serve as people's assessors in accordance with the law.

People's assessors shall be selected in accordance with this Law and shall participate in the judicial activities of the People's Courts in accordance with the law. Unless otherwise provided by law, they shall have the same rights as judges.

Article 3 People's assessors shall, in accordance with the law, enjoy the rights to participate in judicial activities, express opinions independently, and obtain guarantees for the performance of their duties.

People's assessors shall faithfully perform their judicial duties, maintain judicial confidentiality, observe judicial etiquette, and uphold the judicial image.

Article 4 People's assessors participating in judicial activities in accordance with the law shall be protected by law.

People's Courts shall, in accordance with the law, ensure that people's assessors perform their judicial duties.

The employer, or the grassroots self-governing organization at the place of domicile or habitual residence of a people's assessor, shall, in accordance with the law, ensure the assessor's participation in judicial activities.

Article 5 Citizens serving as people's assessors shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Uphold the Constitution of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Have reached the age of 28;

(3) Abide by the law, be of good moral character, and be fair and upright;

(4) Be in good health to perform their duties normally.

People's assessors shall generally have an education level of senior high school or above.

Article 6 The following persons shall not serve as people's assessors:

(1) Members of the standing committees of the People's Congresses, and staff members of supervisory commissions, People's Courts, People's Procuratorates, public security organs, state security organs, and judicial administrative organs;

(2) Lawyers, notaries, arbitrators, and grassroots legal service workers;

(3) Other persons who are unsuitable to serve as people's assessors due to their positions.

Article 7 Persons in any of the following circumstances shall not serve as people's assessors:

(1) Have been subjected to criminal punishment;

(2) Have been dismissed from public office;

(3) Have had their lawyer's or notary's practicing certificates revoked;

(4) Have been included in the list of dishonest persons subject to enforcement;

(5) Have been removed from the position of people's assessor due to disciplinary sanction;

(6) Have other serious violations of law or discipline that may affect judicial credibility.

Article 8 The number of people's assessors shall be determined by the basic People's Court, based on the needs of trial work, and submitted to the standing committee of the People's Congress at the same level for approval.

The number of people's assessors shall be no less than three times the number of judges in that court.

Article 9 The judicial administrative organ shall, together with the basic People's Court and the public security organ, randomly select from the list of permanent residents within its jurisdiction a number of candidates for people's assessors that is at least five times the number of people's assessors to be appointed, conduct a qualification review of the candidates, and seek their opinions.

Article 10 The judicial administrative organ shall, together with the basic People's Court, randomly select the people's assessors from the list of candidates who have passed the qualification review. The president of the basic People's Court shall submit the selected candidates to the standing committee of the People's Congress at the same level for appointment.

Article 11 Where necessary for judicial activities, candidates for people's assessors may be generated through individual applications or recommendations by the employer, the grassroots self-governing organization at the place of domicile or habitual residence, or people's organizations. After the judicial administrative organ, together with the basic People's Court and the public security organ, conducts a qualification review, the people's assessors shall be determined, and the president of the basic People's Court shall submit them to the standing committee of the People's Congress at the same level for appointment.

The number of people's assessors generated in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall not exceed one-fifth of the total number of people's assessors.

Article 12 After being appointed by the standing committee of the People's Congress, people's assessors shall take a public oath of office. The oath-taking ceremony shall be organized by the basic People's Court together with the judicial administrative organ.

Article 13 The term of office for a people's assessor is five years, and generally, they shall not serve consecutive terms.

Article 14 People's assessors and judges shall form a collegial panel to try cases, with a judge serving as the presiding judge. The collegial panel may consist of three members, or may consist of three judges and four people's assessors to form a seven-member collegial panel.

Article 15 When a People's Court tries a first-instance criminal, civil, or administrative case under any of the following circumstances, a collegial panel composed of people's assessors and judges shall be formed:

(1) Cases involving group interests or public interests;

(2) Cases of widespread public concern or with significant social impact;

(3) Cases with complex facts or other circumstances requiring the participation of people's assessors.

Where the law provides that cases specified in the preceding paragraph shall be tried by a single judge or by a collegial panel composed solely of judges, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 16 A People's Court shall try the following first-instance cases with a seven-member collegial panel composed of people's assessors and judges:

(1) Criminal cases with significant social impact that may result in a sentence of fixed-term imprisonment of ten years or more, life imprisonment, or the death penalty;

(2) Public interest litigation cases filed in accordance with the Civil Procedure Law or the Administrative Procedure Law;

(3) Cases involving land acquisition and demolition, ecological and environmental protection, or food and drug safety, with significant social impact;

(4) Other cases with significant social impact.

Article 17 Where the defendant in a first-instance criminal case, the plaintiff or defendant in a civil case, or the plaintiff in an administrative case applies for the participation of people's assessors in the collegial panel, the People's Court may decide to form a collegial panel composed of people's assessors and judges for the trial.

Article 18 The provisions of the law on the withdrawal of judicial personnel shall apply to the withdrawal of people's assessors.

Article 19 Where a basic People's Court needs people's assessors to participate in a collegial panel for the trial of a case, the assessors shall be randomly selected from the list of people's assessors.

Where an intermediate People's Court or a higher People's Court needs people's assessors to participate in a collegial panel for the trial of a case, the assessors shall be randomly selected from the list of people's assessors of the basic People's Courts within its jurisdiction.

Article 20 The presiding judge shall fulfill the duty of providing guidance and reminders related to the trial of the case, but shall not hinder the independent judgment of the people's assessors on the case.

When the collegial panel deliberates on a case, the presiding judge shall provide necessary explanations and clarifications to the people's assessors regarding matters such as the determination of facts, rules of evidence, and legal provisions involved in the case, as well as issues that require attention.

Article 21 When people's assessors participate in the trial of a case in a three-member collegial panel, they shall independently express their opinions and exercise their voting rights on the determination of facts and the application of law.

Article 22 When people's assessors participate in the trial of a case in a seven-member collegial panel, they shall independently express their opinions on the determination of facts and vote jointly with the judges; on the application of law, they may express their opinions but shall not participate in the vote.

Article 23 When the collegial panel deliberates on a case, the principle of the minority being subordinate to the majority shall apply. If a people's assessor disagrees with other members of the collegial panel, their opinion shall be recorded in the minutes.

If there is a significant disagreement among the members of the collegial panel, a people's assessor or a judge may request that the collegial panel submit the case to the president to decide whether to refer it to the judicial committee for discussion and decision.

Article 24 People's Courts shall, taking into account the actual circumstances of their respective jurisdictions, reasonably determine the maximum number of cases each people's assessor may participate in trying per year and shall make this information public.

Article 25 The routine management of people's assessors, including training, assessment, rewards, and disciplinary actions, shall be the responsibility of the basic People's Court together with the judicial administrative organ.

People's assessors shall receive training in a planned manner. People's assessors shall participate in training as required.

Article 26 People's assessors who have achieved remarkable results or have other outstanding contributions in judicial work shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 27 Where a people's assessor falls under any of the following circumstances, and the facts are verified by the basic People's Court together with the judicial administrative organ, the president shall submit a request to the standing committee of the People's Congress at the same level to remove the assessor from office:

(1) The assessor applies to resign from the position for legitimate reasons;

(2) The assessor falls under any of the circumstances listed in Article 6 or Article 7 of this Law;

(3) The assessor, without legitimate reason, refuses to participate in judicial activities, thereby affecting the normal conduct of judicial work;

(4) The assessor violates laws or relevant regulations related to judicial work, engages in malpractice for personal gain, resulting in erroneous judgments or other serious consequences.

Where a people's assessor engages in conduct listed in items (3) or (4) of the preceding paragraph, measures such as notifying the assessor's employer, the grassroots self-governing organization at the place of domicile or habitual residence, or people's organizations, and issuing a public notice within the jurisdiction may be taken as disciplinary actions; if the conduct constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 28 The personal safety and the safety of the residence of people's assessors shall be protected by law. No unit or individual shall retaliate against a people's assessor or their close relatives.

Anyone who retaliates against, insults, slanders, or commits violent acts against a people's assessor or their close relatives shall be held legally liable in accordance with the law.

Article 29 During the period when a people's assessor participates in judicial activities, the employer shall not deduct or covertly deduct the assessor's wages, bonuses, or other benefits.

If the employer of a people's assessor violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the basic People's Court shall promptly issue a corrective opinion to the employer or the competent authority or higher-level department of the employer.

Article 30 During the period when a people's assessor participates in judicial activities, the People's Court shall provide subsidies based on actual working days in accordance with relevant regulations.

Expenses such as transportation and meals incurred by a people's assessor due to participation in judicial activities shall be subsidized by the People's Court in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 31 The subsidies that people's assessors are entitled to for participating in judicial activities, and the necessary expenses incurred by the People's Courts and judicial administrative organs for implementing the people's assessor system, shall be included in the operational budgets of the People's Courts and judicial administrative organs and shall be guaranteed by the corresponding government finances. Specific measures shall be formulated by the Supreme People's Court and the judicial administrative department of the State Council together with the financial department of the State Council.

Article 32 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation. The "Decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on Improving the People's Assessor System" adopted at the Eleventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004, shall be simultaneously repealed.
