# Law of the People's Republic of China on National Medals and National Honorary Titles

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 27, 2015

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to commend outstanding individuals who have made remarkable contributions to the socialist cause with Chinese characteristics, to promote the national spirit and the spirit of the times, to stimulate the initiative of the people of all ethnic groups across the country in building a modern socialist country that is prosperous, strong, democratic, culturally advanced, and harmonious, and to realize the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.

Article 2 National Medals and National Honorary Titles are the highest honors of the state.

The establishment and conferment of National Medals and National Honorary Titles shall be governed by this Law.

Article 3 The state establishes the "Medal of the Republic," which is conferred upon outstanding individuals who have made tremendous contributions and established extraordinary merits in the socialist cause with Chinese characteristics and in the defense of the state.

The state establishes the "Friendship Medal," which is conferred upon foreigners who have made outstanding contributions to China's socialist modernization, to the promotion of exchanges and cooperation between China and foreign countries, and to the maintenance of world peace.

Article 4 The state establishes National Honorary Titles, which are conferred upon outstanding individuals who have made significant contributions and enjoy high prestige in various fields and sectors, including the economy, society, national defense, diplomacy, education, science and technology, culture, health, and sports.

The names of National Honorary Titles shall be prefixed with "People's," but other names may also be used. The specific names of National Honorary Titles shall be determined by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress when deciding on their conferment.

Article 5 The Council of Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may, based on suggestions from various sources, submit proposals to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the conferment of National Medals and National Honorary Titles.

The State Council and the Central Military Commission may submit proposals to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the conferment of National Medals and National Honorary Titles.

Article 6 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress decides on the conferment of National Medals and National Honorary Titles.

Article 7 The President of the People's Republic of China, in accordance with the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, confers National Medals and National Honorary Titles medals upon the recipients and issues certificates.

Article 8 The President of the People's Republic of China may, during state visits, directly confer the "Friendship Medal" upon foreign dignitaries, international friends, and other individuals.

Article 9 The state holds ceremonies for the conferment of National Medals and National Honorary Titles on National Day or other major festivals or commemorative days; when necessary, such ceremonies may also be held at other times.

Article 10 The state establishes a National Register of Merits to record the recipients of National Medals and National Honorary Titles and their achievements.

Article 11 Recipients of National Medals and National Honorary Titles shall be respected by the state and society, and shall enjoy such high honors as being invited to participate in national celebrations and other major events, as well as the treatment prescribed by the state.

Article 12 The state and society shall, through various forms, publicize the extraordinary achievements and outstanding deeds of the recipients of National Medals and National Honorary Titles.

Article 13 National Medals and National Honorary Titles are held for life by their recipients, except where they are revoked in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 14 Recipients of National Medals and National Honorary Titles shall wear the National Medals and National Honorary Titles medals as prescribed, and shall properly keep the medals, medals, and certificates.

Article 15 Upon the death of a recipient of a National Medal or National Honorary Title, the medals, medals, and certificates obtained shall be kept by their heirs or designated persons; in the absence of heirs or designated persons, they may be kept by the state.

National Medals, National Honorary Titles medals, and certificates shall not be sold, leased, or used for other profit-making activities.

Article 16 For individuals who, before their death, made outstanding contributions and met the conditions for conferment of National Medals or National Honorary Titles as provided in this Law, but who died after the implementation of this Law, National Medals or National Honorary Titles may be conferred posthumously.

Article 17 Recipients of National Medals and National Honorary Titles shall cherish and maintain the honors granted by the state, faithfully abide by the Constitution and laws, strive to serve the people, and consciously uphold the reputation of National Medals and National Honorary Titles.

Article 18 Where a recipient of a National Medal or National Honorary Title is sentenced to a criminal penalty for a crime or commits other serious illegal or disciplinary acts, and continuing to hold the National Medal or National Honorary Title would seriously damage the reputation of the state's highest honors, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall decide to revoke their National Medal or National Honorary Title and make a public announcement.

Article 19 Specific matters concerning National Medals and National Honorary Titles shall be handled by the relevant working bodies for state honors and awards.

Article 20 The State Council and the Central Military Commission may, within their respective scopes of authority, carry out work on honors, awards, and commendations.

Article 21 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2016.
