# Supervision Officers Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 20, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Supervision Law of the People's Republic of China, for the purposes of strengthening the management and supervision of supervision officers, ensuring that supervision officers perform their duties according to law, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of supervision officers, promoting the building of a contingent of highly qualified and professional supervision officers, and advancing the standardization and rule of law in supervision work.

**Article 2** The management and supervision of supervision officers shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, be guided by Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of Three Represents, the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, adhere to the principle of the Party managing officials, enhance the sense of mission, responsibility, and honor of supervision officers, and build a contingent of supervision officers who are loyal, clean, and responsible.

**Article 3** Supervision officers include the following personnel:

(1) Directors, deputy directors, and members of supervisory commissions at all levels;
(2) Supervision personnel within the organs of supervisory commissions at all levels;
(3) Supervision personnel and supervision commissioners stationed or dispatched by supervisory commissions at all levels to Party organs, state organs, organizations and units authorized or entrusted by laws and regulations to manage public affairs, and the administrative regions under their jurisdiction;
(4) Supervision personnel in other supervisory institutions that exercise supervisory power according to law.

The management and supervision of staff and supervision commissioners of supervisory institutions stationed by supervisory commissions at all levels in state-owned enterprises, as well as staff of other supervisory institutions in state-owned enterprises that exercise supervisory power according to law, shall be implemented with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 4** Supervision officers shall be loyal and steadfast, responsible and dedicated, honest and upright, and set an example of strict self-discipline, excellent conduct, and resistance to corruption and prevention of degeneration.

**Article 5** Supervision officers shall uphold the dignity and authority of the Constitution and laws, base their work on facts and take law as the criterion, perform their duties objectively and impartially, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of the parties involved.

**Article 6** Supervision officers shall strictly perform their duties in accordance with the prescribed limits of authority and procedures, adhere to democratic centralism, and conduct collective research on major matters.

**Article 7** Supervisory organs shall establish and improve supervision systems and mechanisms for supervision officers to ensure that power is strictly constrained.

Supervision officers shall consciously accept organizational supervision, democratic supervision, social supervision, and public opinion supervision.

**Article 8** Supervision officers' lawful performance of their duties shall be protected by law and shall not be interfered with by administrative organs, social organizations, or individuals.

## Chapter II Duties, Obligations, and Rights of Supervision Officers

**Article 9** Supervision officers shall perform the following duties according to law:

(1) Conduct integrity education for public officials;
(2) Supervise and inspect public officials' lawful performance of duties, impartial exercise of power, clean and honest conduct in office and business, and moral integrity;
(3) Investigate duty-related violations and crimes under the jurisdiction of supervisory organs as stipulated by law;
(4) Propose disposal opinions on supervisory matters handled based on the results of supervision and investigation;
(5) Carry out work related to international cooperation against corruption;
(6) Other duties as stipulated by law.

Supervision officers shall be responsible for the supervisory matters they handle within the scope of their authority.

**Article 10** Supervision officers shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Consciously adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, and strictly implement the lines, principles, policies, and major decisions and deployments of the Communist Party of China and the state;
(2) Exemplarily abide by the Constitution and laws;
(3) Safeguard the interests of the state and the people, enforce the law impartially, be responsible and courageous, dare to supervise, and resolutely fight against corruption;
(4) Protect the legitimate rights and interests of supervisees and relevant personnel according to law;
(5) Be devoted to their duties, diligent and responsible, and strive to improve work quality and efficiency;
(6) Keep state secrets and secrets of supervision work, and keep confidential any trade secrets, personal privacy, and personal information learned during the performance of duties;
(7) Strictly observe discipline, abide by professional ethics, and exemplarily observe social ethics and family virtues;
(8) Consciously accept supervision;
(9) Other obligations as stipulated by law.

**Article 11** Supervision officers shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) The authority and working conditions necessary for performing the duties of a supervision officer;
(2) The occupational security and welfare benefits entitled to for performing the duties of a supervision officer;
(3) The safety of person, property, and residence shall be protected by law;
(4) The right to file complaints or accusations;
(5) Other rights as stipulated by the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws.

## Chapter III Qualifications and Selection of Supervision Officers

**Article 12** To serve as a supervision officer, one must meet the following conditions:

(1) Possess the nationality of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Be loyal to the Constitution, and adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist system;
(3) Possess good political quality, moral character, and clean conduct;
(4) Be familiar with laws, regulations, and policies, and possess the professional knowledge and ability to perform duties such as supervision, investigation, and disposal;
(5) Have the physical and psychological conditions necessary to perform duties normally;
(6) Possess a bachelor's degree or above from an institution of higher education;
(7) Other conditions as stipulated by law.

If supervision personnel before the implementation of this Law do not meet the educational requirements specified in item (6) of the preceding paragraph, they shall undergo training and assessment. Specific measures shall be formulated by the National Commission of Supervision.

**Article 13** Persons in any of the following circumstances shall not serve as supervision officers:

(1) Having been subjected to criminal punishment for a crime, or having been subject to a decision by a People's Procuratorate not to prosecute due to minor criminal circumstances, or having been exempted from criminal punishment by a People's Court according to law;
(2) Having been removed from a Party post within the Communist Party of China, placed on probation within the Party, or expelled from the Party;
(3) Having been removed from office or expelled from public employment;
(4) Having been listed as a target of joint disciplinary action for dishonesty according to law;
(5) Having a spouse who has emigrated outside the country (territory), or having no spouse but all children have emigrated outside the country (territory);
(6) Other circumstances as stipulated by law.

**Article 14** The selection of supervision officers shall adhere to the principles of selecting both ability and political integrity, with priority given to political integrity; selecting from all over the country and appointing people on their merits; prioritizing the cause and being fair and upright; highlighting political standards; and focusing on work performance.

**Article 15** Supervision officers shall be selected through examinations and assessments, choosing the best candidates from among those who meet the conditions for supervision officers.

**Article 16** The recruitment of supervision officers shall adopt methods of open examinations, strict inspections, equal competition, and selective recruitment based on merit in accordance with the law and relevant state regulations.
**Article 17** Supervisory commissions may, based on the needs of supervision work and in accordance with the law and relevant state regulations, select persons engaged in public affairs from Party organs, state organs, public institutions, state-owned enterprises, and other organs and units who meet the conditions for appointment to serve as supervision officers.

**Article 18** Supervisory commissions may, based on the needs of supervision work and in accordance with the law and relevant state regulations, select or appoint persons engaged in professions or teaching and research related to the functions and duties of supervisory organs who meet the conditions for appointment to serve as supervision officers.

## Chapter IV Appointment and Removal of Supervision Officers

**Article 19** The Director of the National Commission of Supervision shall be elected and removed by the National People's Congress. The Deputy Directors and members shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress upon the recommendation of the Director of the National Commission of Supervision.

The Director of a local supervisory commission at any level shall be elected and removed by the People's Congress at the corresponding level. The Deputy Directors and members shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level upon the recommendation of the Director of the supervisory commission.

The Directors, Deputy Directors, and members of supervisory commissions at all levels in the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps shall be appointed and removed by the Standing Committee of the People's Congress of the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region upon the recommendation of the Director of the Supervisory Commission of the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region.

The appointment and removal of other supervision officers shall be handled in accordance with the management authority and prescribed procedures.

**Article 20** Upon assuming office, supervision officers shall take a constitutional oath in accordance with the law.

**Article 21** A supervision officer shall be removed from their post under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Losing the nationality of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Changing positions such that retaining the post of supervision officer is unnecessary;
(3) Retiring;
(4) Resigning or being subject to dismissal according to law;
(5) Being transferred away or expelled due to disciplinary or legal violations;
(6) Other circumstances as stipulated by law.

**Article 22** Supervision officers shall not concurrently serve as members of the Standing Committee of a People's Congress, hold positions in administrative organs, judicial organs, or procuratorial organs, hold positions in enterprises or other for-profit organizations or public institutions, or serve as people's assessors, people's supervisors, practicing lawyers, arbitrators, or notaries.

If a supervision officer needs to hold a concurrent position due to work requirements, it shall be approved according to the management authority, but no remuneration for the concurrent position shall be accepted.

**Article 23** Supervision officers serving as Directors of supervisory commissions at the county level or at the level of a city divided into districts shall implement geographical avoidance in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 24** Supervision officers who are spouses, direct blood relatives, collateral blood relatives within three generations, or close in-laws shall not concurrently hold the following positions:

(1) Director, Deputy Director, or member of the same supervisory commission, or these persons and other supervision officers;
(2) Supervision officers in the same department of a supervisory commission organ;
(3) Supervision officers in the same stationed agency, dispatched agency, or other supervisory institution;
(4) Directors, Deputy Directors, or members of two adjacent levels of supervisory commissions (upper and lower).

## Chapter V Management of Supervision Officers

**Article 25** The ranks of supervision officers are divided into thirteen levels, which are: Chief Supervision Officer, First Deputy Chief Supervision Officer, Second Deputy Chief Supervision Officer; First Senior Supervision Officer, Second Senior Supervision Officer, Third Senior Supervision Officer, Fourth Senior Supervision Officer; First-Level Supervision Officer, Second-Level Supervision Officer, Third-Level Supervision Officer, Fourth-Level Supervision Officer, Fifth-Level Supervision Officer, Sixth-Level Supervision Officer.

**Article 26** The Director of the National Commission of Supervision shall be the Chief Supervision Officer.

**Article 27** The determination of a supervision officer's rank shall be based on the position and rank held, moral integrity and performance, professional competence, work performance, and length of service.

Promotion in the rank of supervision officers shall combine regular promotion with selective promotion based on merit. Those who are exceptionally outstanding or have made exceptional contributions may be promoted ahead of schedule through selective promotion.

**Article 28** Specific measures for the establishment, determination, and promotion of ranks of supervision officers shall be separately stipulated by the state.

**Article 29** A pre-service training system shall be implemented for newly appointed supervision officers.

**Article 30** Supervision officers shall receive planned political, theoretical, and professional training.

Training shall highlight the characteristics of political organs, adhere to the integration of theory with practice, provide training based on needs, emphasize practical results, and enhance professional competence.

The training status of supervision officers shall serve as part of their assessment and as one of the bases for their appointment and rank promotion.

**Article 31** Training institutions for supervision officers shall undertake the task of training supervision officers in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 32** The state shall strengthen the construction of the discipline of supervision, encourage qualified institutions of higher education to establish supervision majors or offer supervision courses, cultivate high-quality reserve talents for supervision officers with both integrity and ability, and enhance the professional competence of supervision officers.

**Article 33** Supervision officers shall undergo job rotation in accordance with the law and relevant state regulations.

**Article 34** A supervision officer applying for resignation shall submit a written application. After approval according to the management authority, their position shall be removed in accordance with prescribed procedures.

**Article 35** If a supervision officer is subject to dismissal according to law, their position shall be removed in accordance with prescribed procedures.

The decision to dismiss a supervision officer shall be made according to the management authority. The dismissal decision shall be notified in writing to the dismissed supervision officer, stating the reasons and legal basis for the decision.

## Chapter VI Assessment and Rewards for Supervision Officers

**Article 36** The assessment of supervision officers shall be comprehensive, objective, and fair, combining regular assessment, special assessment, and annual assessment.

**Article 37** The assessment of supervision officers shall, according to the management authority, comprehensively assess their moral integrity, ability, diligence, performance, and integrity, with a focus on political quality, work performance, and self-discipline and integrity.

**Article 38** The results of the annual assessment shall be divided into four grades: Excellent, Competent, Basically Competent, and Incompetent.

The assessment results shall serve as the basis for adjusting the rank and salary of supervision officers, as well as for rewards, punishments, removal from office, demotion, and dismissal of supervision officers.

**Article 39** The results of the annual assessment shall be notified in writing to the supervision officer concerned. If a supervision officer has objections to the assessment results, they may apply for a review.

**Article 40** Rewards shall be given to supervision officers or groups of supervision officers who have made significant achievements and contributions in supervision work, or who have other outstanding deeds.

**Article 41** Rewards shall be given to supervision officers who demonstrate any of the following:

(1) Achieving notable results in performing supervision duties;
(2) Making significant achievements and contributions in investigating and disposing of duty-related violations and crimes;
(3) Proposing valuable supervisory suggestions that have a significant effect on preventing and eliminating major risks and hidden dangers;
(4) Achieving outstanding results in researching supervision theory or summarizing practical supervision experience, which has a guiding role in supervision work;
(5) Having other meritorious service.

Rewards for supervision officers shall be handled in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter VII Supervision and Discipline of Supervision Officers

**Article 42** Supervisory organs shall standardize work procedures, strengthen the construction of internal supervision and restraint mechanisms, and enhance the supervision of supervision officers' performance of duties and compliance with laws.

**Article 43** Any unit or individual has the right to report or accuse supervision officers of disciplinary or legal violations. The organ receiving the report or accusation shall promptly investigate and handle the matter, and inform the reporter or accuser of the result.

No one shall suppress or retaliate against any unit or individual that reports or accuses according to law.

**Article 44** For clues of disciplinary or legal violations by supervision officers in the performance of their duties transferred by judicial organs, procuratorial organs, law enforcement departments, etc., the supervisory organ shall promptly investigate and handle them.

**Article 45** Supervisory commissions may, based on work needs and in accordance with regulations, appoint special supervisors and other supervisory personnel from representatives of various sectors to supervise the performance of duties by supervision officers and to provide opinions and suggestions for strengthening and improving supervision work.

**Article 46** Supervision officers shall not inquire about case details, intervene in cases, or intercede on behalf of others. For such acts, the supervision officer handling the supervisory matter shall promptly report to their superiors. The relevant circumstances shall be registered and filed.

A supervision officer handling a supervisory matter shall not, without approval, contact the person under investigation, persons involved in the case, or their specific related persons, or engage in interactions with them. For such acts, any supervision officer who becomes aware of the situation shall promptly report to their superiors. The relevant circumstances shall be registered and filed.

**Article 47** A supervision officer handling a supervisory matter shall voluntarily withdraw under any of the following circumstances. The supervisee, reporter, accuser, and other relevant persons also have the right to request their withdrawal. If the supervision officer does not voluntarily apply for withdrawal, the supervisory organ shall decide on their withdrawal according to law:

(1) Being a close relative of the supervisee, reporter, or accuser;
(2) Having served as a witness in the case;
(3) Having a personal interest or a close relative's interest in the supervisory matter being handled;
(4) Other circumstances that may affect the fair handling of the supervisory matter.

**Article 48** Supervision officers shall strictly implement the confidentiality system, control the scope and duration of knowledge of supervisory matters, shall not privately retain, conceal, inspect, excerpt, copy, or carry problem clues and case-related materials, and are strictly prohibited from disclosing secrets of supervision work.

After leaving their posts or resigning, supervision officers shall comply with the management regulations for the declassification period, strictly fulfill their confidentiality obligations, and shall not disclose relevant secrets.

**Article 49** Within three years after leaving their posts, supervision officers shall not engage in professions related to supervision and judicial work that may create conflicts of interest.

After leaving their posts, supervision officers shall not serve as litigation representatives or defenders in cases handled by the supervisory organ where they previously worked, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent them in litigation or defend them.

After being expelled, supervision officers shall not serve as litigation representatives or defenders, except when acting as a guardian or close relative of a party to represent them in litigation or defend them.

**Article 50** Supervision officers shall comply with the regulations concerning the regulation of leading cadres' spouses, children, and children's spouses engaging in business. Violations shall be dealt with accordingly.

**Article 51** The spouse, parents, children, and children's spouses of a supervision officer shall not act as litigation representatives or defenders in cases handled by the supervisory organ where the supervision officer works, in their capacity as lawyers, or provide other paid legal services.

**Article 52** Supervision officers who commit any of the following acts shall be dealt with according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Engaging in embezzlement or bribery;
(2) Failing to perform or correctly perform supervision duties, failing to discover problems that should have been discovered, or failing to report or handle discovered problems, causing adverse effects;
(3) Disposing of problem clues without authorization or approval, concealing major case information without reporting, or privately retaining or handling case-related materials;
(4) Using their authority or the influence of their position to interfere with investigation work or seek personal gain from cases;
(5) Stealing or disclosing investigation work information, or disclosing reporting matters, reporting acceptance status, or reporter information;
(6) Concealing, falsifying, altering, or intentionally destroying evidence or case materials;
(7) Extorting confessions or inducing confessions from the person under investigation or persons involved in the case, or insulting, beating, abusing, physically punishing, or imposing disguised physical punishment on them;
(8) Taking investigation measures or disposing of case-related property in violation of regulations;
(9) Causing safety accidents in case handling due to violations of regulations, or failing to report, reporting inaccurately, or handling improperly after a safety accident occurs;
(10) Other duty-related illegal or criminal acts.

If a supervision officer commits other disciplinary or legal violations that affect the image of the supervision officer contingent and harm the interests of the state and the people, corresponding responsibility shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 53** If a supervision officer is suspected of disciplinary or legal violations and has been placed on file for review, investigation, or criminal investigation, and is unsuitable to continue performing duties, their performance of duties shall be temporarily suspended according to the management authority and prescribed procedures.

**Article 54** A system of accountability for supervision officers shall be implemented. Those who abuse their power or neglect their duties, causing serious consequences, shall be held accountable for life or subject to accountability inquiries.

If a supervision officer is suspected of serious duty-related violations or crimes, or if there is a major error in the handling of a case, the responsible leading personnel and directly responsible personnel shall be held accountable.

## Chapter VIII Occupational Security for Supervision Officers

**Article 55** A supervision officer shall not be transferred except under the following circumstances:

(1) Required to implement position avoidance according to regulations;
(2) Required to implement job rotation according to regulations;
(3) Requiring job adjustment due to institutional or staffing adjustments;
(4) Unsuitable to continue supervision work due to disciplinary or legal violations;
(5) Other circumstances as stipulated by law.

**Article 56** No unit or individual shall require a supervision officer to engage in matters beyond the scope of their statutory duties.

A supervision officer has the right to refuse any act that interferes with their lawful performance of duties and to fully and truthfully record and report such acts. If there are disciplinary or legal violations, the relevant organs shall pursue the responsibility of the relevant personnel according to the severity of the circumstances.Article 57: The professional dignity and personal safety of supervision officers are protected by law.

No organization or individual shall retaliate against supervision officers or their close relatives.

Any illegal or criminal acts such as retaliation, framing, defamation, violent assault, threats, intimidation, harassment, or disturbance against supervision officers or their close relatives shall be severely punished in accordance with the law.

Article 58: If a supervision officer's reputation is damaged due to false reports, false accusations, defamation, or slander arising from the lawful performance of their duties, the supervisory authority shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, promptly clarify the facts, eliminate adverse effects, and pursue the legal liability of the relevant organization or individual in accordance with the law.

Article 59: If the personal safety of a supervision officer or their close relatives is endangered due to the lawful performance of their duties, the supervisory authority and public security authority shall take necessary protective measures, such as personal protection and prohibiting specific individuals from approaching the supervision officer and their close relatives.

Article 60: Supervision officers shall enjoy the salary system prescribed by the state, as well as rank allowances and other allowances, subsidies, bonuses, insurance, and welfare benefits. The salary and rank allowance system for supervision officers shall be separately prescribed by the state.

Article 61: If a supervision officer becomes disabled in the line of duty, they shall enjoy the disability benefits prescribed by the state. If a supervision officer dies in the line of duty or due to illness, their relatives shall enjoy the pensions and preferential treatment prescribed by the state.

Article 62: After retirement, supervision officers shall enjoy the pension and other benefits prescribed by the state.

Article 63: Supervision officers have the right to file complaints against acts by state organs and their personnel that infringe upon the rights of supervision officers.

The authority that accepts the complaint shall investigate and handle the matter in accordance with the law and promptly inform the complainant of the investigation and handling results.

Article 64: If a supervision officer is dissatisfied with the disciplinary sanction, punishment, or personnel action imposed on them, they may apply for review, reconsideration, or appeal in accordance with the prescribed procedures.

Article 65: If a disciplinary sanction, punishment, or personnel action against a supervision officer is erroneous, it shall be promptly corrected; if it causes damage to their reputation, their reputation shall be restored, the adverse effects shall be eliminated, and an apology shall be made; if it causes economic losses, compensation shall be provided. The directly responsible personnel for retaliation shall be held accountable in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9: Supplementary Provisions

Article 66: Where this Law provides for the rights, obligations, and management systems of supervision officers, the provisions of this Law shall apply; where this Law does not provide for such matters, the provisions of the Civil Servant Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and regulations shall apply.

Article 67: The supervision officer system for the People's Liberation Army and the People's Armed Police Force shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state and the military.

Article 68: This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2022.
