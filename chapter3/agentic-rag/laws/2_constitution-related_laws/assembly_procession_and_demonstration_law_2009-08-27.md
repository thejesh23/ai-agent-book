# Law of the People's Republic of China on Assemblies, Processions and Demonstrations

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on October 31, 1989

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to safeguard citizens' exercise of their rights to assembly, procession and demonstration in accordance with the law and to maintain social stability and public order.

Article 2 This Law applies to assemblies, processions and demonstrations held within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "assembly" means an activity in which people gather in a public place in the open air to express opinions or convey wishes.

For the purposes of this Law, "procession" means an activity in which people march in formation along a public road or in a public place in the open air to express their common wishes.

For the purposes of this Law, "demonstration" means an activity in which people express their common wishes, such as demands, protests, support or solidarity, in the form of assemblies, processions or sit-ins in a public place in the open air or on a public road.

This Law does not apply to cultural, sports or recreational activities, normal religious activities, or traditional folk customs.

Article 3 The people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, guarantee the exercise by citizens of their rights to assembly, procession and demonstration.

Article 4 In exercising their rights to assembly, procession and demonstration, citizens must abide by the Constitution and laws, shall not oppose the basic principles established by the Constitution, and shall not harm the interests of the State, society or the collective, or the lawful freedoms and rights of other citizens.

Article 5 Assemblies, processions and demonstrations shall be conducted peacefully. No weapons, controlled cutting tools or explosives shall be carried, and no violence shall be used or incited.

Article 6 The competent authorities for assemblies, processions and demonstrations are the public security bureaus of the cities or counties, or the public security sub-bureaus of the cities, where the assemblies, processions and demonstrations are held. If the route of a procession or demonstration passes through two or more districts or counties, the competent authority shall be the public security organ at the next higher level common to the public security organs of the districts or counties concerned.

## Chapter II Application and Permission for Assemblies, Processions and Demonstrations

Article 7 To hold an assembly, procession or demonstration, an application must be submitted to the competent authority in accordance with the provisions of this Law and permission must be obtained.

The following activities do not require an application:

(1) Celebrations or commemorations held by the State or in accordance with State decisions;

(2) Assemblies held by State organs, political parties, social organizations, enterprises or institutions in accordance with the law or their organizational bylaws.

Article 8 An assembly, procession or demonstration must have a responsible person.

For assemblies, processions and demonstrations that require an application in accordance with this Law, the responsible person must submit a written application to the competent authority five days before the date of the event. The application shall state the purpose, manner, slogans, chants, number of participants, number of vehicles, types and quantities of sound equipment to be used, start and end times, locations (including assembly and dispersal points), route, and the name, occupation and address of the responsible person.

Article 9 After receiving an application for an assembly, procession or demonstration, the competent authority shall, two days before the date of the proposed event, notify the responsible person in writing of its decision to permit or not permit the event. If permission is not granted, the reasons shall be stated. If no notification is given within the time limit, the event shall be deemed to have been permitted.

If an assembly, procession or demonstration is urgently required due to a sudden incident, the competent authority must be immediately notified. Upon receiving such notification, the competent authority shall immediately examine the matter and decide whether to permit or not permit the event.

Article 10 If an application for an assembly, procession or demonstration is made to demand the resolution of a specific problem, the competent authority may, after receiving the application, notify the relevant organ or unit to negotiate with the responsible person of the assembly, procession or demonstration to resolve the problem, and may postpone the proposed date of the event by five days.

Article 11 If the competent authority considers that holding an assembly, procession or demonstration at the time, place and along the route applied for will seriously affect traffic order and social order, it may, when deciding to grant permission or after granting permission, change the time, place and route of the assembly, procession or demonstration, and shall promptly notify the responsible person thereof.

Article 12 An application for an assembly, procession or demonstration shall not be permitted if any of the following circumstances exists:

(1) It opposes the basic principles established by the Constitution;

(2) It endangers the unity, sovereignty and territorial integrity of the State;

(3) It incites ethnic division;

(4) There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the proposed assembly, procession or demonstration will directly endanger public security or seriously disrupt social order.

Article 13 If the responsible person of an assembly, procession or demonstration disagrees with the decision of the competent authority not to grant permission, he or she may, within three days of receiving the notice of the decision, apply for reconsideration to the people's government at the same level. The people's government shall make a decision within three days of receiving the application for reconsideration.

Article 14 The responsible person of an assembly, procession or demonstration may withdraw the application after submitting it but before receiving notification from the competent authority. If, after receiving notification of permission from the competent authority, the responsible person decides not to hold the assembly, procession or demonstration, he or she shall promptly inform the competent authority. If participants have already assembled, the responsible person shall be responsible for their dispersal.

Article 15 Citizens shall not initiate, organize or participate in assemblies, processions or demonstrations of local citizens in cities other than their place of residence.

Article 16 State functionaries shall not organize or participate in assemblies, processions or demonstrations that violate the duties and obligations of State functionaries as stipulated by relevant laws and regulations.

Article 17 To organize or participate in an assembly, procession or demonstration in the name of a State organ, social organization, enterprise or institution, the approval of the responsible person of that unit must be obtained.

## Chapter III The Conduct of Assemblies, Processions and Demonstrations

Article 18 For assemblies, processions and demonstrations held in accordance with the law, the competent authority shall dispatch people's police to maintain traffic order and social order and to ensure the smooth progress of the assemblies, processions and demonstrations.

Article 19 No one may disrupt, break into or damage by violence, coercion or other illegal means an assembly, procession or demonstration held in accordance with the law.

Article 20 In order to ensure the progress of a procession held in accordance with the law, the people's police responsible for maintaining traffic order may temporarily make flexible adjustments to the relevant provisions of the traffic regulations.

Article 21 If a procession encounters unforeseen circumstances during its march and cannot proceed along the permitted route, the people's police officer in charge at the scene has the authority to change the marching route of the procession.

Article 22 When an assembly, procession or demonstration is held at or passes by the locations of State organs, military organs, radio stations, television stations, or foreign embassies or consulates in China, the competent authority may, in order to maintain order, set up temporary security cordons nearby. No one may cross these cordons without the permission of the people's police.
Article 23 No assemblies, processions or demonstrations shall be held within a distance of ten meters to three hundred meters from the perimeter of the following places, unless approved by the State Council or the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government:

(1) The locations of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate;

(2) The residences of state guests;

(3) Important military installations;

(4) Airports, railway stations and ports.

The specific perimeter distances for the places listed in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 24 The time for holding assemblies, processions and demonstrations shall be limited to between 6:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m., unless otherwise decided or approved by the local people's government.

Article 25 Assemblies, processions and demonstrations shall be conducted in accordance with the permitted purpose, manner, slogans, chants, start and end times, locations, route and other matters.

The responsible person of an assembly, procession or demonstration must be responsible for maintaining order and strictly prevent others from joining.

When necessary, the responsible person of an assembly, procession or demonstration shall designate specific persons to assist the people's police in maintaining order. Persons responsible for maintaining order shall wear identification marks.

Article 26 In holding assemblies, processions and demonstrations, no one may violate public security administration regulations, commit criminal acts, or incite criminal acts.

Article 27 The people's police shall stop an assembly, procession or demonstration if any of the following circumstances exists:

(1) No application has been made in accordance with this Law, or the application has not been granted permission;

(2) It is not conducted in accordance with the purpose, manner, slogans, chants, start and end times, location, or route permitted by the competent authority;

(3) Circumstances arise during the event that endanger public security or seriously disrupt social order.

If any of the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph occurs and the participants refuse to stop, the people's police officer in charge at the scene has the authority to order dispersal. If the participants refuse to disperse, the people's police officer in charge at the scene has the authority, in accordance with relevant State regulations, to decide on necessary measures to forcibly disperse them, and to forcibly remove from the scene or immediately detain those who refuse to obey.

If participants in an assembly, procession or demonstration cross the temporary cordon lines set up in accordance with Article 22 of this Law, enter the specific perimeter areas of the places listed in Article 23 of this Law where assemblies, processions and demonstrations are prohibited, or commit other illegal or criminal acts, the people's police may forcibly remove them from the scene or immediately detain them.
## Chapter IV Legal Liability

Article 28 If an assembly, procession or demonstration involves acts violating public security administration, punishment shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

If an assembly, procession or demonstration involves any of the following circumstances, the public security organ may impose a warning or detention for up to fifteen days on its responsible person and the persons directly responsible:

(1) No application has been made in accordance with this Law, or the application has not been granted permission;

(2) It is not conducted in accordance with the purpose, manner, slogans, chants, start and end times, location, or route permitted by the competent authority, and the participants refuse to stop.

Article 29 If an assembly, procession or demonstration involves a criminal act, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If weapons, controlled cutting tools or explosives are carried, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If no application has been made in accordance with this Law, or the application has not been granted permission, or the event is not conducted in accordance with the permitted start and end times, location, or route, and the participants refuse to obey the order to disperse, thereby seriously disrupting social order, the responsible person and the persons directly responsible of the assembly, procession or demonstration shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If State organs are surrounded or stormed, causing the normal official or state activities of the State organs to be unable to proceed, the responsible person and the persons directly responsible of the assembly, procession or demonstration shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If public places are occupied, vehicles or pedestrians are intercepted, or crowds gather to block traffic, thereby seriously disrupting public order or traffic order, the responsible person and the persons directly responsible of the assembly, procession or demonstration shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 30 If anyone disrupts, breaks into or otherwise damages an assembly, procession or demonstration held in accordance with the law, the public security organ may impose a warning or detention for up to fifteen days. If the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 31 If a party disagrees with the decision on detention punishment imposed by the public security organ in accordance with the provisions of Article 28, paragraph 2, or Article 30 of this Law, he or she may, within five days of receiving the notice of the punishment decision, file a petition with the public security organ at the next higher level. The public security organ at the next higher level shall make a ruling within five days of receiving the petition. If the party disagrees with the ruling of the public security organ at the next higher level, he or she may, within five days of receiving the notice of the ruling, file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 32 If, during the course of an assembly, procession or demonstration, public or private property is damaged or another person's body is injured resulting in casualties, in addition to punishment that may be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law or the Public Security Administration Punishments Law, liability for compensation shall also be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 33 If a citizen initiates or organizes local citizens' assemblies, processions or demonstrations in a city other than his or her place of residence, the public security organ has the authority to detain the citizen or forcibly send him or her back to the original place.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 34 The provisions of this Law apply to assemblies, processions and demonstrations held by foreigners within the territory of China.

Foreigners within the territory of China shall not participate in assemblies, processions or demonstrations held by Chinese citizens without the approval of the competent authority.

Article 35 The public security department under the State Council may, in accordance with this Law, formulate implementing regulations, which shall be submitted to the State Council for approval before implementation.

The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with this Law, formulate implementing measures.

Article 36 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
