Adopted at the Second Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on July 1, 1979

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Election Law for the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses is formulated in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 2** The election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, fully promote democracy, and strictly follow legal procedures.

**Article 3** Deputies to the National People's Congress, and deputies to the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures, shall be elected by the next lower level people's congress.

Deputies to the people's congresses of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, autonomous counties, townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be directly elected by the voters.

**Article 4** All citizens of the People's Republic of China who have reached the age of 18 shall have the right to vote and stand for election, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, family background, religious belief, education level, property status, or length of residence.

Persons who have been deprived of political rights in accordance with the law shall not have the right to vote or stand for election.

**Article 5** Each voter shall have only one vote in an election.

**Article 6** The People's Liberation Army shall conduct elections separately, and the election procedures shall be formulated separately.

**Article 7** Deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses shall have broad representativeness and shall include an appropriate number of grassroots deputies, especially workers, peasants, and intellectuals; and an appropriate number of women deputies, with the proportion of women deputies to be gradually increased.

The National People's Congress and local people's congresses in areas with a relatively large number of returned overseas Chinese shall have an appropriate number of deputies who are returned overseas Chinese.

Citizens of the People's Republic of China residing abroad who are in China during the election of deputies to people's congresses below the county level may participate in the election in their place of origin or the place where they resided before going abroad.

**Article 8** Election funds for the National People's Congress and local people's congresses shall be included in the fiscal budget and disbursed from the state treasury.

## Chapter II: Election Organs

**Article 9** The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall preside over the election of deputies to the National People's Congress. The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures shall preside over the election of deputies to the people's congresses at their corresponding levels.

Election committees shall be established in cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, autonomous counties, townships, ethnic townships, and towns to preside over the election of deputies to the people's congresses at their corresponding levels. The election committees of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, and autonomous counties shall be under the leadership of the standing committees of the people's congresses at their corresponding levels. The election committees of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be under the leadership of the standing committees of the people's congresses of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, and autonomous counties.

The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures shall guide the election of deputies to people's congresses below the county level within their respective administrative areas.

**Article 10** The members of the election committees of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, and autonomous counties shall be appointed by the standing committees of the people's congresses at their corresponding levels. The members of the election committees of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be appointed by the standing committees of the people's congresses of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, and autonomous counties.

If a member of an election committee becomes a candidate for deputy, they shall resign from the election committee.

**Article 11** An election committee shall perform the following duties:

(1) Divide the electoral districts for electing deputies to the people's congress at its level and allocate the number of deputies to be elected in each electoral district;
(2) Conduct voter registration, examine voter qualifications, publish the list of voters; accept and decide on appeals regarding objections to the voter list;
(3) Determine the election date;
(4) Ascertain, verify, and organize the introduction of information about candidates for deputies; determine and publish the list of official candidates for deputies based on the opinions of the majority of voters;
(5) Preside over the voting;
(6) Determine the validity of the election results and publish the list of elected deputies;
(7) Other duties as prescribed by law.

The election committee shall promptly publish election information.

## Chapter III: Number of Deputies to Local People's Congresses

**Article 12** The number of deputies to local people's congresses shall be determined as follows:

(1) The base number of deputies for a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be 350. One additional deputy may be added for every 150,000 people in a province or autonomous region, and for every 25,000 people in a municipality directly under the Central Government; however, the total number of deputies shall not exceed 1,000;
(2) The base number of deputies for a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture shall be 240. One additional deputy may be added for every 25,000 people; if the population exceeds 10 million, the total number of deputies shall not exceed 650;
(3) The base number of deputies for a city not divided into districts, municipal district, county, or autonomous county shall be 140. One additional deputy may be added for every 5,000 people; if the population exceeds 1.55 million, the total number of deputies shall not exceed 450; if the population is less than 50,000, the total number of deputies may be less than 140;
(4) The base number of deputies for a township, ethnic township, or town shall be 45. One additional deputy may be added for every 1,500 people; however, the total number of deputies shall not exceed 160; if the population is less than 2,000, the total number of deputies may be less than 45.

The total number of deputies to a local people's congress shall be the sum of the base number of deputies and the number of deputies added based on population as stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

For autonomous regions and provinces with a high concentration of ethnic minorities, the number of deputies may be increased by an additional 5% upon decision by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. For counties, autonomous counties, townships, and ethnic townships with a high concentration of ethnic minorities or a dispersed population, the number of deputies may be increased by an additional 5% upon decision by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 13** The specific number of deputies to the people's congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be determined by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress in accordance with this Law. The specific number of deputies to the people's congress of a city divided into districts, autonomous prefecture, or county level shall be determined by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with this Law and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record. The specific number of deputies to a township-level people's congress shall be determined by the standing committee of the county-level people's congress in accordance with this Law and reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level for record.

**Article 14** Once the total number of deputies to a local people's congress is determined, it shall not be changed. If a significant change in population occurs due to administrative division adjustments or major construction projects, the total number of deputies to that people's congress shall be re-determined in accordance with this Law.

If the number of deputies is re-determined in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, within 30 days, report the re-determined number of deputies to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

**Article 15** The number of deputies to a local people's congress shall be allocated by the standing committee of that people's congress or the election committee based on the population of each next lower level administrative area or electoral district within its administrative area, in accordance with the principle that each deputy represents an equal number of urban and rural residents, and the requirement that all regions, ethnic groups, and sectors have an appropriate number of deputies. In the people's congress of a county or autonomous county, townships, ethnic townships, and towns with a very small population shall have at least one deputy.

The method for allocating the number of deputies to local people's congresses shall be formulated by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, with reference to the method for allocating the number of deputies to the National People's Congress and in light of local conditions.

## Chapter IV: Number of Deputies to the National People's Congress

**Article 16** Deputies to the National People's Congress shall be elected by the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and by the People's Liberation Army.

The number of deputies to the National People's Congress shall not exceed 3,000.

The number of deputies to be elected to the National People's Congress from the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the Macao Special Administrative Region, and the method for their election, shall be separately prescribed by the National People's Congress.

**Article 17** The number of deputies to the National People's Congress shall be allocated by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress based on the population of each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government, in accordance with the principle that each deputy represents an equal number of urban and rural residents, and the requirement that all regions, ethnic groups, and sectors have an appropriate number of deputies.

The number of deputies to be elected to the National People's Congress from a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall consist of the number determined by population calculation, the same basic number for each region, and other numbers to be elected.

The specific allocation of the number of deputies to the National People's Congress shall be decided by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

**Article 18** The number of deputies to be elected to the National People's Congress from ethnic minorities shall be allocated by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress to the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government for election, with reference to the population and distribution of each ethnic minority. Ethnic minorities with a very small population shall have at least one deputy.

## Chapter V: Election of Ethnic Minorities

**Article 19** In areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities, each such minority shall have deputies participating in the local people's congress.

If the total population of a particular ethnic minority living in a concentrated community accounts for 30% or more of the total population of that area, the number of people represented by each deputy from that minority shall be equivalent to the number of people represented by each deputy in the local people's congress.

If the total population of a particular ethnic minority living in a concentrated community accounts for less than 15% of the total population of that area, the number of people represented by each deputy from that minority may be appropriately less than the number represented by each deputy in the local people's congress, but shall not be less than half; for an autonomous county with a very small population of the ethnic group exercising regional autonomy, upon decision by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province or autonomous region, it may be less than half. Other ethnic minorities with a very small population living in concentrated communities shall have at least one deputy.

If the total population of a particular ethnic minority living in a concentrated community accounts for 15% or more but less than 30% of the total population of that area, the number of people represented by each deputy from that minority may be appropriately less than the number represented by each deputy in the local people's congress, but the number of deputies allocated to that minority shall not exceed 30% of the total number of deputies.

**Article 20** For the election of deputies from other ethnic minorities and the Han nationality living in concentrated communities within the administrative areas of autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, autonomous counties, and townships, ethnic townships, and towns with concentrated ethnic minority communities, the provisions of Article 19 of this Law shall apply.

**Article 21** For deputies to be elected to local people's congresses from ethnic minorities living in scattered communities, the number of people represented by each deputy may be less than the number represented by each deputy in the local people's congress.

For the election of deputies from other ethnic minorities and the Han nationality living in scattered communities within the administrative areas of autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, autonomous counties, and townships, ethnic townships, and towns with concentrated ethnic minority communities, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

**Article 22** For the election of deputies to the people's congresses of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, townships, ethnic townships, and towns where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities, voters from ethnic minorities may vote separately or jointly, based on local ethnic relations and living conditions.

For the election of deputies from other ethnic minorities and the Han nationality living within the administrative areas of autonomous counties and townships, ethnic townships, and towns with concentrated ethnic minority communities, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

**Article 23** Election documents, voter lists, voter cards, lists of candidates for deputies, certificates of election for deputies, and seals of election committees formulated or published by autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties shall simultaneously use the local commonly used ethnic minority languages.

**Article 24** Other matters concerning the election of ethnic minorities shall be handled with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

## Chapter VI: Division of Electoral Districts

**Article 25** The number of deputies to the people's congresses of cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, counties, autonomous counties, townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be allocated to electoral districts, and elections shall be conducted by electoral district. Electoral districts may be divided based on residential areas, or based on production units, public institutions, or work units.

The size of an electoral district shall be such that each district elects one to three deputies.

**Article 26** The number of people represented by each deputy in each electoral district within an administrative area shall be roughly equal.

## Chapter VII: Voter Registration

**Article 27** Voter registration shall be conducted by electoral district. The voter qualification once registered and confirmed shall be valid for a long term. Before each election, voters who have newly reached the age of 18 since the last voter registration, and voters whose political rights have been restored after the expiration of the period of deprivation of political rights, shall be registered. Voters who have moved out of their original electoral district after registration shall be included in the voter list of the newly moved-in electoral district; those who have died or have been deprived of political rights in accordance with the law shall be removed from the voter list.

Mentally ill persons who are unable to exercise their voting rights shall not be included in the voter list upon confirmation by the election committee.

**Article 28** The voter list shall be published at least 20 days before the election day. For elections where voting is conducted by presenting a voter card, voter cards shall be issued.

**Article 29** If a person has an objection to the published voter list, they may file an appeal with the election committee within five days from the date of publication of the voter list. The election committee shall make a decision on the appeal within three days. If the appellant is dissatisfied with the decision, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court at least five days before the election day, and the people's court shall render a judgment before the election day. The judgment of the people's court shall be final.

## Chapter VIII: Nomination of Candidates for Deputies

**Article 30** Candidates for deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses shall be nominated by electoral districts or electoral units.

Political parties and people's organizations may jointly or separately recommend candidates for deputies. Voters or deputies, with a joint recommendation of ten or more persons, may also recommend candidates for deputies. The recommender shall introduce the information about the candidates for deputies to the election committee or the presidium of the congress. The recommended candidates for deputies shall truthfully provide the election committee or the presidium of the congress with their personal identity, resume, and other basic information. If the basic information provided is untrue, the election committee or the presidium of the congress shall inform the voters or deputies.

The number of candidates for deputies recommended jointly or separately by political parties and people's organizations, and the number of candidates for deputies recommended by each voter or deputy through joint recommendation, shall not exceed the number of deputies to be elected in that electoral district or electoral unit.

**Article 31** Competitive elections shall be held for deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses. The number of candidates for deputies shall be greater than the number of deputies to be elected.

For the direct election of deputies to the people's congress by voters, the number of candidates for deputies shall be between one-third and one time greater than the number of deputies to be elected. For the election of deputies to the next higher level people's congress by a local people's congress at or above the county level, the number of candidates for deputies shall be between one-fifth and one-half greater than the number of deputies to be elected.

**Article 32** For the direct election of deputies to the people's congress by voters, candidates for deputies shall be nominated and recommended by voters in each electoral district, and by political parties and people's organizations. The election committee shall compile the list of candidates for deputies and their basic information, publish it at least 15 days before the election day, and submit it to the voter groups of the respective electoral districts for discussion and consultation to determine the list of official candidates for deputies. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies exceeds the maximum differential ratio stipulated in Article 31 of this Law, the election committee shall submit it to the voter groups of the respective electoral districts for discussion and consultation, and determine the list of official candidates for deputies based on the opinions of the majority of voters; if a relatively consistent opinion on the official candidates for deputies cannot be formed, a primary election shall be held, and the list of official candidates for deputies shall be determined in the order of the number of votes obtained in the primary election. The list of official candidates for deputies and their basic information shall be published at least seven days before the election day.

When a local people's congress at or above the county level elects deputies to the next higher level people's congress, the time for nominating and deliberating candidates for deputies shall not be less than two days. The presidium of that people's congress shall print and distribute the list of candidates for deputies nominated in accordance with the law and their basic information to all deputies for deliberation and discussion. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies meets the differential ratio stipulated in Article 31 of this Law, a direct vote shall be conducted. If the number of nominated candidates for deputies exceeds the maximum differential ratio stipulated in Article 31 of this Law, a primary election shall be held, and the list of official candidates for deputies shall be determined in the order of the number of votes obtained in the primary election, according to the specific differential ratio determined by the election method of that people's congress in accordance with this Law, and then a vote shall be conducted.

**Article 33** When a local people's congress at or above the county level elects deputies to the next higher level people's congress, candidates for deputies are not limited to deputies of that people's congress.Article 34 The election committee or the presidium of the people's congress shall introduce the information of candidates for deputies to voters or delegates. Political parties, people's organizations, voters, and delegates that recommend candidates for deputies may introduce the information of the recommended candidates at voter group or delegate group meetings. Upon the request of voters, the election committee shall organize meetings between candidates and voters, where candidates shall introduce their own information and answer voters' questions. However, the introduction of candidates for deputies must cease on election day.

Article 35 When citizens participate in the election of deputies to people's congresses at various levels, they shall not directly or indirectly accept any form of funding related to the election provided by overseas institutions, organizations, or individuals.

Those who violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not be included in the list of candidates for deputies; if already included, they shall be removed from the list; if already elected, their election shall be invalid.

## Chapter IX Election Procedures

Article 36 The election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at various levels shall be strictly conducted in accordance with legal procedures and shall be subject to supervision. No organization or individual may in any way interfere with the free exercise of the right to vote by voters or delegates.

Article 37 When voters directly elect deputies to the people's congress, they shall obtain their ballots with their identity cards or voter registration cards in accordance with the regulations of the election committee.

Article 38 The election committee shall, based on the distribution of voters in each electoral district and in accordance with the principle of facilitating voting, set up polling stations to conduct the election. Where voters are relatively concentrated, an election meeting may be held for voting; voters who have difficulty moving due to illness or other reasons, or who are scattered and have inconvenient transportation, may vote in mobile ballot boxes.

Article 39 When a local people's congress at or above the county level elects deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level, the election shall be presided over by the presidium of that people's congress.

Article 40 The election of deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at various levels shall be conducted by secret ballot. Secret voting booths shall be provided during the election.

If a voter is illiterate or unable to mark the ballot due to disability, they may entrust a person they trust to mark the ballot on their behalf.

Article 41 An elector may cast a vote in favor of a candidate for deputy, cast a vote against, vote for any other voter, or abstain from voting.

Article 42 If a voter is away from the electoral district during the election period, they may, with the approval of the election committee, entrust another voter in writing to vote on their behalf. Each voter may accept no more than three such entrustments and shall vote in accordance with the wishes of the entrusting party.

Article 43 After the voting concludes, the scrutineers and vote counters elected by voters or delegates, along with personnel from the election committee or the presidium of the people's congress, shall verify the number of voters and ballots, make a record, and have it signed by the scrutineers.

Close relatives of a candidate for deputy shall not serve as scrutineers or vote counters.

Article 44 If the number of ballots cast in an election exceeds the number of voters, the election is invalid; if it is equal to or less than the number of voters, it is valid.

If the number of candidates marked on a ballot exceeds the number of deputies to be elected, the ballot is void; if it is equal to or less than the number of deputies to be elected, it is valid.

Article 45 When voters directly elect deputies to the people's congress, the election is valid if more than half of all voters in the electoral district participate in the voting. A candidate for deputy is elected only if they receive more than half of the votes cast by the voters who participated in the voting.

When a local people's congress at or above the county level elects deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level, a candidate for deputy is elected only if they receive more than half of the votes of all delegates.

If the number of candidates for deputy who receive more than half of the votes exceeds the number of deputies to be elected, those with the highest number of votes are elected. If there is a tie in votes and the winner cannot be determined, a second vote shall be held among the tied candidates, and the one with the highest number of votes is elected.

If the number of elected deputies who receive more than half of the votes is less than the number of deputies to be elected, a by-election shall be held for the remaining seats. In the by-election, the list of candidates shall be determined based on the order of votes received in the first vote, in accordance with the proportional margin stipulated in Article 31 of this Law. If only one deputy is to be elected, there shall be two candidates.

When conducting a by-election for deputies to the county or township people's congress in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the candidate with the highest number of votes is elected, provided that the number of votes received is not less than one-third of the total ballots cast; when a local people's congress at or above the county level conducts a by-election for deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level, a candidate for deputy is elected only if they receive more than half of the votes of all delegates.

Article 46 The election committee or the presidium of the people's congress shall determine the validity of the election results in accordance with this Law and announce them.

The list of elected deputies shall be published by the election committee or the presidium of the people's congress.

Article 47 The credentials committee shall, in accordance with the law, review whether the elected deputies meet the basic qualifications for deputies stipulated by the Constitution and laws, whether the election complies with the legal procedures, and whether there are any illegal acts that undermine the election or render the election invalid. It shall provide an opinion on the validity of the deputies' election and report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town.

The standing committee of the people's congress at or above the county level or the presidium of the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town shall, based on the report submitted by the credentials committee, confirm the qualifications of the deputies or determine the invalidity of their election, and publish the list of deputies before the first session of each term of the people's congress.

Article 48 A citizen shall not concurrently serve as a deputy to the people's congress of two or more administrative regions that are not subordinate to each other.

## Chapter X Supervision, Recall, Resignation, and By-election of Deputies

Article 49 Deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at various levels are subject to the supervision of voters and the original electoral units. Voters or electoral units have the right to recall the deputies they have elected.

Article 50 For a deputy to the people's congress at the county level, a joint request for recall signed by fifty or more voters from the original electoral district; for a deputy to the people's congress at the township level, a joint request for recall signed by thirty or more voters from the original electoral district may be submitted in writing to the standing committee of the people's congress at the county level.

The recall request shall state the reasons for the recall. The deputy subject to the recall has the right to present a defense at the voter meeting or submit a written defense.

The standing committee of the people's congress at the county level shall distribute the recall request and the written defense of the deputy subject to the recall to the voters of the original electoral district.

The vote on the recall request shall be presided over by relevant responsible personnel dispatched by the standing committee of the people's congress at the county level.

Article 51 When a local people's congress at or above the county level is in session, the presidium or one-tenth or more of the delegates jointly may propose a recall case against a deputy to the people's congress at the next higher level elected by that people's congress. During the recess of the people's congress, the council of chairpersons of the standing committee of the local people's congress at or above the county level, or one-fifth or more of the members of the standing committee jointly, may submit a recall case against a deputy to the people's congress at the next higher level elected by that people's congress to the standing committee. The recall case shall state the reasons for the recall.

When a local people's congress at or above the county level is in session, the deputy subject to the recall has the right to present a defense at the presidium meeting and the plenary session of the congress, or submit a written defense, which shall be distributed to the session by the presidium. After the recall case is deliberated by the session, the presidium shall submit it to the plenary session for a vote.

When the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level is in session, the deputy subject to the recall has the right to present a defense at the council of chairpersons meeting and the plenary session of the standing committee, or submit a written defense, which shall be distributed to the session by the council of chairpersons. After the recall case is deliberated by the session, the council of chairpersons shall submit it to the plenary session for a vote.

Article 52 The recall of a deputy shall be conducted by secret ballot.

Article 53 The recall of a deputy to the people's congress at the county or township level must be approved by more than half of the voters in the original electoral district.

The recall of a deputy elected by a local people's congress at or above the county level must be approved by more than half of the delegates of that people's congress; during the recess of the congress, it must be approved by more than half of the members of the standing committee. The resolution on the recall shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level for filing and announcement.

Article 54 If the deputy status of a member of the standing committee of a people's congress at or above the county level, or a member of a special committee of a people's congress at or above the county level, is recalled, their position as a member of the standing committee or special committee shall be correspondingly revoked, and the presidium or standing committee shall announce this.

If the deputy status of the chairperson or vice-chairperson of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town is recalled, their position as chairperson or vice-chairperson shall be correspondingly revoked, and the presidium shall announce this.

Article 55 A deputy to the National People's Congress, or a deputy to the people's congress of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, a city divided into districts, or an autonomous prefecture, may submit a written resignation to the standing committee of the people's congress that elected them. The acceptance of the resignation by the standing committee requires approval by more than half of its members. The resolution accepting the resignation shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level for filing and announcement.

A deputy to the people's congress at the county level may submit a written resignation to the standing committee of the people's congress at that level, and a deputy to the people's congress at the township level may submit a written resignation to the people's congress at that level. The acceptance of the resignation by the standing committee of the people's congress at the county level requires approval by more than half of its members. The acceptance of the resignation by the people's congress at the township level requires approval by more than half of the delegates of that people's congress. The acceptance of the resignation shall be announced.

Article 56 If the request to resign as a deputy is accepted for a member of the standing committee of a people's congress at or above the county level, or a member of a special committee of a people's congress at or above the county level, their position as a member of the standing committee or special committee shall correspondingly terminate, and the standing committee shall announce this.

If the request to resign as a deputy is accepted for the chairperson or vice-chairperson of the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town, their position as chairperson or vice-chairperson shall correspondingly terminate, and the presidium shall announce this.

Article 57 If a deputy's seat becomes vacant during their term of office for any reason, a by-election shall be held by the original electoral district or original electoral unit.

If a deputy to a local people's congress at any level is transferred out of or moves out of their administrative region during their term of office, their deputy qualifications shall automatically terminate, and a by-election shall be held for the vacancy.

During the recess of a local people's congress at or above the county level, the standing committee of that people's congress may conduct a by-election for deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level.

When conducting a by-election for a vacant deputy seat, the number of candidates for deputy may be greater than or equal to the number of deputies to be elected. The specific procedures for by-elections shall be stipulated by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Deputies elected through by-elections shall undergo credentials review in accordance with Article 47 of this Law.

## Chapter XI Sanctions Against Election Sabotage

Article 58 To ensure that voters and delegates freely exercise their right to vote and stand for election, anyone who commits any of the following acts, sabotaging the election and violating public security administration regulations, shall be subject to public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Bribing voters or delegates with money or other property, thereby hindering the free exercise of their right to vote and stand for election;

(2) Using violence, threats, deception, or other illegal means to hinder voters or delegates from freely exercising their right to vote and stand for election;

(3) Forging election documents, falsifying election vote counts, or committing other illegal acts;

(4) Suppressing or retaliating against persons who accuse or report illegal acts in the election, or against persons who request the recall of a deputy.

If a state functionary commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph, they shall also be subject to disciplinary sanctions by supervisory authorities or sanctions by their own organ or unit.

If a person is elected through the illegal acts listed in paragraph 1 of this Article, their election shall be invalid.

Article 59 If an election-conducting body discovers acts of election sabotage or receives reports of such acts, it shall promptly investigate and handle them in accordance with the law; if legal liability needs to be pursued, it shall promptly refer the matter to the relevant authorities for handling.

## Chapter XII Supplementary Provisions

Article 60 The people's congresses and their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with this Law, formulate detailed rules for the implementation of elections, which shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for filing.
