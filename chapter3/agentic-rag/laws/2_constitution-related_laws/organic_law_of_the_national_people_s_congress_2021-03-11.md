# Organic Law of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Fifth National People's Congress on December 10, 1982

Promulgated and effective as of the Announcement of the National People's Congress on December 10, 1982

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Organic Law of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Fourth Session of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on March 11, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to improve the organization and working procedures of the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee, to safeguard and regulate the exercise of their functions and powers, to uphold and improve the system of people's congresses, and to ensure that the people are the masters of the country.

Article 2 The National People's Congress is the highest organ of state power. Its permanent body is the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 3 The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, uphold Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of the "Three Represents," the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era as their guide, and exercise their functions and powers in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution and laws.

Article 4 The National People's Congress is composed of deputies elected through democratic elections. It is responsible to the people and subject to their supervision.

The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee adhere to whole-process democracy, maintain close ties with the people at all times, listen to their opinions and suggestions, reflect their will, and safeguard their rights and interests.

Article 5 The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee exercise the legislative power of the state, decide on major issues, supervise the implementation of the Constitution and laws, uphold the unity, dignity, and authority of the socialist legal system, and build a socialist country under the rule of law.

Article 6 The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee practice the principle of democratic centralism, fully promote democracy, and exercise their functions and powers collectively.

Article 7 The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee actively engage in foreign exchanges and strengthen exchanges and cooperation with parliaments of other countries and international and regional parliamentary organizations.

## Chapter II Sessions of the National People's Congress

Article 8 The term of office of the National People's Congress is five years.

The National People's Congress meets in session once a year. It is convened by its Standing Committee. The Standing Committee may convene an interim session of the National People's Congress whenever it deems it necessary, or when more than one-fifth of the deputies to the National People's Congress so propose.

Article 9 After the election of deputies to the National People's Congress, the Credentials Committee of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall examine their credentials.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall, on the basis of the report submitted by the Credentials Committee, confirm the credentials of the deputies or determine that the election of an individual deputy is invalid. It shall publish the list of deputies before the first session of each National People's Congress.

The credentials of by-elected deputies to the National People's Congress shall be examined in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 10 Deputies to the National People's Congress form delegations according to the electoral units that elected them. Each delegation shall elect a leader and deputy leaders.

Before each session of the National People's Congress, the delegations shall discuss the preparatory matters for the session proposed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. During the session, the delegations shall deliberate on the various bills submitted to the National People's Congress. The leader of a delegation or a deputy designated by the delegation may, at a meeting of the Presidium or at a plenary meeting of the Congress, express the views of the delegation on the bills under deliberation.

Article 11 Before each session of the National People's Congress, a preparatory meeting shall be held to elect the Presidium and the Secretary-General of the current session and to adopt the agenda of the session and other decisions on preparatory matters.

The draft list of the Presidium and the Secretary-General shall be proposed by the Council of Chairpersons of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, submitted to the Standing Committee for deliberation and adoption, and then presented to the preparatory meeting.

Article 12 The Presidium presides over the sessions of the National People's Congress.

The Presidium shall elect several standing chairpersons to convene and preside over its meetings.

The Presidium shall elect several of its members to serve as the executive chairpersons for each plenary meeting of the Congress and shall designate one of them to preside over the plenary meeting.

Article 13 A Secretariat shall be established for the session of the National People's Congress. The Secretariat shall be composed of a Secretary-General and several Deputy Secretaries-General. The candidates for Deputy Secretaries-General shall be decided by the Presidium.

Under the leadership of the Secretary-General, the Secretariat shall handle matters assigned by the Presidium and manage the day-to-day affairs of the session. The Deputy Secretaries-General shall assist the Secretary-General in his work.

Article 14 The Presidium shall handle the following matters:

(1) Determine the daily schedule of the session based on its agenda;
(2) Decide on the deadline for the submission of bills by deputies during the session;
(3) Hear and deliberate reports on the handling of bills and decide whether bills submitted during the session shall be placed on the agenda of the session;
(4) Hear and deliberate reports from the Secretariat and relevant special committees on the deliberation and examination of various bills and reports, and decide whether the bills and draft decisions or draft resolutions shall be submitted to the session for a vote;
(5) Hear explanations from the standing chairpersons of the Presidium regarding the list of candidates for members of state organs, nominate candidates for members of state organs to be elected by the session, and determine the list of official candidates in accordance with legal procedures;
(6) Propose draft methods for election and decision on appointment during the session;
(7) Organize the constitutional oath-taking ceremony for members of state organs elected or appointed by the session;
(8) Other matters that should be handled by the Presidium.

Article 15 The standing chairpersons of the Presidium shall hear reports from the Secretariat and relevant special committees on matters to be submitted to the Presidium for deliberation and make recommendations to the Presidium.

The standing chairpersons of the Presidium may make necessary adjustments to the daily schedule of the session.

Article 16 The Presidium of the National People's Congress, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the special committees of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate may submit bills to the National People's Congress that fall within the scope of its functions and powers.

Article 17 A delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies may submit bills to the National People's Congress that fall within the scope of its functions and powers.

Article 18 The candidates for the Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, Secretary-General, and members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the candidates for the President and Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, the candidate for the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the candidate for the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the candidates for the President of the Supreme People's Court and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate shall be nominated by the Presidium. After deliberation and consultation by the delegations, the Presidium shall determine the list of official candidates based on the opinions of the majority of the deputies.

Article 19 The candidates for the Premier of the State Council and other members of the State Council, and the candidates for members of the Central Military Commission other than its Chairman shall be nominated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Constitution.

Article 20 The Presidium of the National People's Congress, three or more delegations, or one-tenth or more of the deputies may submit a proposal for the recall of members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President or Vice-President of the People's Republic of China, members of the State Council or the Central Military Commission, the Director of the National Commission of Supervision, the President of the Supreme People's Court, or the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate. The proposal shall be submitted by the Presidium to the session for deliberation.

Article 21 During a session of the National People's Congress, a delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies may submit in writing a question to the State Council or the ministries and commissions under the State Council, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, or the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

## Chapter III The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress

Article 22 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress is responsible to the National People's Congress and reports on its work to the Congress.

The term of office of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress is the same as that of the National People's Congress. It shall exercise its functions and powers until a new Standing Committee is elected by the succeeding National People's Congress.

Article 23 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress is composed of the following:

The Chairperson,
Several Vice-Chairpersons,
The Secretary-General,
Several members.

The members of the Standing Committee shall be elected by the National People's Congress from among its deputies.

Members of the Standing Committee shall not hold posts in state administrative, supervisory, adjudicatory, or procuratorial organs. If they hold such posts, they shall resign from their posts in the Standing Committee.

Article 24 The Chairperson of the Standing Committee presides over the meetings of the Standing Committee and directs its work. The Vice-Chairpersons and the Secretary-General assist the Chairperson in his work. The Vice-Chairpersons may, upon entrustment by the Chairperson, exercise part of the Chairperson's functions and powers.

If the Chairperson is unable to work due to health reasons or if the post of Chairperson falls vacant, the Standing Committee shall elect one of the Vice-Chairpersons to act as the Chairperson until the Chairperson recovers his health or a new Chairperson is elected by the National People's Congress.

Article 25 The Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, and Secretary-General of the Standing Committee form the Council of Chairpersons, which handles the important day-to-day work of the Standing Committee:

(1) Decide on the dates of each meeting of the Standing Committee, draft the agenda for the meeting, and, when necessary, propose adjustments to the agenda;
(2) Decide whether bills and questions submitted to the Standing Committee shall be referred to relevant special committees for deliberation or submitted to a plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for deliberation;
(3) Decide whether bills and draft decisions or draft resolutions shall be submitted to a plenary meeting of the Standing Committee for a vote, and, for those not submitted for a vote for the time being, propose further handling opinions;
(4) Adopt the annual work priorities, legislative work plan, supervision work plan, work plan for deputies, special work plans, and work normative documents of the Standing Committee;
(5) Guide and coordinate the day-to-day work of the special committees;
(6) Handle other important day-to-day work of the Standing Committee.

Article 26 The Standing Committee shall establish a Credentials Committee.

The candidates for the chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members of the Credentials Committee shall be nominated by the Council of Chairpersons from among the members of the Standing Committee. The Standing Committee shall appoint or remove them.

Article 27 The Standing Committee shall establish a General Office, which works under the leadership of the Secretary-General.

The Standing Committee shall have several Deputy Secretaries-General, who shall be appointed or removed by the Standing Committee upon the proposal of the Chairperson.

Article 28 The Standing Committee shall establish a Legislative Affairs Commission, a Budgetary Affairs Commission, and other working commissions as needed.

The chairpersons, vice-chairpersons, and members of the working commissions shall be appointed or removed by the Standing Committee upon the proposal of the Chairperson.

The establishment, functions, and appointment and removal of members of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Basic Law Committee and the Macao Special Administrative Region Basic Law Committee shall be governed by relevant laws and decisions of the National People's Congress.

Article 29 The Council of Chairpersons, the special committees of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the Central Military Commission, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and a group of ten or more members of the Standing Committee may submit bills to the Standing Committee that fall within the scope of its functions and powers.

Article 30 During a meeting of the Standing Committee, a group of ten or more members of the Standing Committee may submit in writing a question to the State Council or the ministries and commissions under the State Council, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, or the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

Article 31 When the National People's Congress is not in session, the Standing Committee may, upon the nomination of the Premier of the State Council, decide on the appointment or removal of other members of the State Council; and, upon the nomination of the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, decide on the appointment or removal of other members of the Central Military Commission.

Article 32 When the National People's Congress is not in session, the Standing Committee may, upon the proposal of the Council of Chairpersons or the Premier of the State Council, decide on the removal of individual other members of the State Council; and, upon the proposal of the Chairman of the Central Military Commission, decide on the removal of individual other members of the Central Military Commission.

Article 33 The Standing Committee must submit a work report to the National People's Congress at each session of the Congress.

## Chapter IV The Committees of the National People's Congress

Article 34 The National People's Congress establishes the Ethnic Affairs Committee, the Constitution and Law Committee, the Supervisory and Judicial Affairs Committee, the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee, the Education, Science, Culture and Public Health Committee, the Foreign Affairs Committee, the Overseas Chinese Affairs Committee, the Environment and Resources Protection Committee, the Agriculture and Rural Affairs Committee, the Social Development Affairs Committee, and such other special committees as the National People's Congress deems necessary. Each special committee works under the leadership of the National People's Congress; when the Congress is not in session, it works under the leadership of its Standing Committee.

Each special committee is composed of a chairperson, several vice-chairpersons, and several members.

The candidates for the chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members of each special committee shall be nominated by the Presidium from among the deputies and voted on by the National People's Congress. When the Congress is not in session, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may appoint or remove the vice-chairpersons and members of the special committees upon the nomination of the Council of Chairpersons, subject to a vote at a meeting of the Standing Committee.

Article 35 The term of office of each special committee is the same as that of the National People's Congress. It shall perform its functions until a new special committee is formed by the succeeding National People's Congress.

Article 36 The chairperson of each special committee presides over the meetings of the committee and directs its work. The vice-chairpersons assist the chairperson in his work.

Each special committee may, as needed, appoint several experts as advisers. Advisers may attend meetings of the special committee and express their opinions.

Advisers shall be appointed or removed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 37 The work of each special committee is as follows:

(1) Deliberate on bills submitted by the Presidium of the National People's Congress or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress;
(2) Submit to the Presidium of the National People's Congress or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress bills that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee and are related to the committee, and organize the drafting of draft laws and other draft bills;
(3) Undertake specific work related to the Standing Committee's hearing and deliberation of special work reports;
(4) Undertake the specific organization and implementation of the Standing Committee's law enforcement inspections;
(5) Undertake specific work related to the Standing Committee's thematic inquiries;
(6) In accordance with the work arrangements of the Standing Committee, hear special reports from relevant departments of the State Council, the National Commission of Supervision, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and make recommendations;
(7) Conduct investigations and research on issues related to the committee that fall within the scope of functions and powers of the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee, and make recommendations;
(8) Deliberate on administrative regulations, decisions, and orders of the State Council; orders, directives, and rules of the ministries and commissions of the State Council; supervisory regulations of the National Commission of Supervision; local regulations, decisions, and resolutions of the people's congresses and their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures; decisions, orders, and rules of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures; autonomous regulations and separate regulations of autonomous areas; regulations of special economic zones; and interpretations of the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate concerning the specific application of laws, which are submitted by the Standing Committee and are considered to contravene the Constitution or laws, and provide opinions;
(9) Deliberate on questions submitted by the Presidium of the National People's Congress or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, hear the replies from the organs questioned, and, when necessary, submit reports to the Presidium of the National People's Congress or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress;
(10) Study and handle suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies, and be responsible for supervising the handling of relevant suggestions, criticisms, and opinions;
(11) Conduct foreign exchanges in accordance with the arrangements of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress;
(12) Other work assigned by the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee.

Article 38 The Ethnic Affairs Committee may conduct investigations and research on issues concerning the strengthening of ethnic unity, and make recommendations; deliberate on autonomous regulations and separate regulations of autonomous regions submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for approval, and submit reports to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 39 The Constitution and Law Committee is responsible for promoting the implementation of the Constitution, interpreting the Constitution, advancing constitutional review, strengthening constitutional supervision, and cooperating with constitutional publicity work.

The Constitution and Law Committee shall conduct unified deliberation on draft laws and draft decisions on legal issues submitted to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee. Other special committees shall provide opinions to the Constitution and Law Committee on relevant drafts.

Article 40 The Financial and Economic Affairs Committee shall examine the draft national economic and social development plan, draft outline of the plan, draft central and local budgets, draft final central accounts, and related reports and adjustment plans submitted by the State Council, and provide preliminary examination opinions and examination result reports. Other special committees may provide opinions to the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee on relevant drafts and reports.

Article 41 The National People's Congress or its Standing Committee may organize investigation committees on specific issues. The organization and work of such investigation committees shall be decided by the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee.

## Chapter V Deputies to the National People's Congress

Article 42 The term of office of a deputy to the National People's Congress is five years, commencing from the first session of the National People's Congress for which the deputy is elected and ending at the first session of the succeeding National People's Congress.

Article 43 Deputies to the National People's Congress must faithfully observe the Constitution and laws, keep state secrets, and assist in the implementation of the Constitution and laws in their own production, work, and social activities.

Article 44 Deputies to the National People's Congress shall maintain close contact with the electoral units that elected them and the people. They may attend, as non-voting participants, the meetings of the people's congresses of the electoral units that elected them. They shall listen to and reflect the opinions and demands of the people through various means, strive to serve the people, and give full play to their role in whole-process democracy.

Article 45 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the special committees, and the working commissions shall maintain close contact with the deputies, listen to their opinions and suggestions, support and guarantee the deputies' performance of their duties in accordance with the law, expand the deputies' participation in various work, and give full play to the role of the deputies.The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall establish and improve the working mechanism for its members and the special committees and working committees to maintain contact with deputies.

The administrative and working bodies of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall provide service and support for deputies in performing their duties.

Article 46: Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on various aspects of work submitted by deputies to the National People's Congress to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee shall be referred by the administrative body of the Standing Committee to the relevant organs or organizations for study and handling, with a requirement to provide a response.

Regarding the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions submitted by deputies to the National People's Congress, the relevant organs or organizations shall communicate with the deputies, fully listen to their views, introduce relevant circumstances, carefully study and handle the matters, and provide a timely response.

The relevant special committees of the National People's Congress and the administrative body of the Standing Committee shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the handling work. The administrative body of the Standing Committee shall report annually to the Standing Committee on the handling of deputies' suggestions, criticisms, and opinions, and make the report public.

Article 47: When deputies to the National People's Congress attend meetings of the National People's Congress or perform other duties as deputies, the state shall provide appropriate subsidies and material conveniences based on actual needs.

Article 48: Deputies to the National People's Congress and members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall not be subject to legal liability for their speeches and votes at various meetings of the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee.

Article 49: No deputy to the National People's Congress may be arrested or subjected to criminal trial without the permission of the Presidium of the National People's Congress, or, when the Congress is not in session, without the permission of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

If a deputy to the National People's Congress is detained because he or she is caught in the act of committing a crime, the public security organ that carried out the detention shall immediately report to the Presidium of the National People's Congress or the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.
