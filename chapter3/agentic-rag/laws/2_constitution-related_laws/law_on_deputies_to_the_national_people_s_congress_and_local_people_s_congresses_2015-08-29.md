# Law of the People's Republic of China on Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 3, 1992

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses adopted at the Seventeenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 28, 2010

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Organic Law of the Local People's Congresses and Local People's Governments of the People's Republic of China, the Electoral Law of the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses of the People's Republic of China, and the Law of the People's Republic of China on Deputies to the National People's Congress and Local People's Congresses adopted at the Sixteenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 29, 2015

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure that deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses exercise their functions and powers, fulfill their obligations, and play their roles in accordance with the law.

Article 2 Deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses are elected in accordance with the provisions of law.

Deputies to the National People's Congress are members of the highest state authority, and deputies to local people's congresses are members of local state authorities at various levels.

Deputies to the National People's Congress and local people's congresses represent the interests and will of the people, and participate in the exercise of state power in accordance with the functions and powers conferred by the Constitution and laws upon the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 3 Deputies enjoy the following rights:

(1) To attend sessions of the people's congress at the corresponding level, participate in the deliberation of various bills, reports, and other agenda items, and express opinions;

(2) To jointly submit bills, questions of inquiry, and motions for recall, among others, in accordance with the law;

(3) To put forward suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on all aspects of work;

(4) To participate in all elections of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(5) To participate in all votes of the people's congress at the corresponding level;

(6) To obtain the information and various guarantees necessary for the lawful performance of their duties as deputies;

(7) Other rights provided for by law.

Article 4 Deputies shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) To faithfully abide by the Constitution and laws, protect state secrets, and assist in the implementation of the Constitution and laws in their own production, work, and social activities;

(2) To attend sessions of the people's congress at the corresponding level on time, carefully deliberate various bills, reports, and other agenda items, express opinions, and carry out all work during the sessions;

(3) To actively participate in organized activities such as inspections, special investigations, and law enforcement inspections for the performance of their duties;

(4) To strengthen study and research related to the performance of their duties, and continuously improve their ability to perform their duties as deputies;

(5) To maintain close ties with the voters of their electoral districts or the units that elected them, as well as the people, listen to and reflect their opinions and demands, and strive to serve the people;

(6) To consciously observe social ethics, be honest and self-disciplined, fair and upright, diligent and responsible;

(7) Other obligations provided for by law.

Article 5 The work of deputies during sessions of the people's congress at the corresponding level and their activities during the recess of the people's congress at the corresponding level, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, constitute the performance of their duties as deputies.

The state and society provide guarantees for deputies to perform their duties.

Deputies shall not be separated from their respective production and work. When attending sessions of the people's congress at the corresponding level or participating in organized activities for the performance of their duties during recess, deputies shall arrange their own production and work appropriately and give priority to performing their duties as deputies.

Article 6 Deputies are subject to the supervision of the voters of their electoral districts or the units that elected them.

## Chapter II: Work of Deputies During Sessions of the People's Congress at the Corresponding Level

Article 7 Deputies shall attend sessions of the people's congress at the corresponding level on time. If a deputy is unable to attend a session due to health or other special reasons, leave shall be requested in accordance with regulations.

Before attending a session of the people's congress at the corresponding level, deputies shall listen to the opinions and suggestions of the people and prepare for the performance of their duties during the session.

Article 8 Deputies participate in plenary sessions of the congress, plenary sessions of delegations, and group meetings, and deliberate on various bills and reports included in the agenda of the session.

Deputies may be recommended or invited to attend meetings of the presidium or special committees as non-voting participants and express opinions.

Deputies shall express opinions around the agenda items of the session and comply with the rules of procedure.

Article 9 Deputies have the right to submit bills to the people's congress at the corresponding level that fall within the scope of its functions and powers, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law. A bill shall include the reasons, basis, and plan.

A bill submitted by a deputy in accordance with the law shall be decided by the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level whether to include it in the agenda of the session, or it may be first referred to the relevant special committee for deliberation and an opinion on whether to include it in the agenda, before a decision is made.

If, before the bill is submitted to the congress for a vote, the deputy who submitted the bill requests its withdrawal, and the presidium agrees, the deliberation of the bill at the session shall be terminated.

Article 10 Deputies to the National People's Congress have the right to submit bills to the National People's Congress for amending the Constitution in accordance with the procedures provided for by the Constitution.

Article 11 Deputies participate in all elections of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Deputies to the National People's Congress have the right to express opinions on the candidates for members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress nominated by the presidium, the candidates for President and Vice President of the People's Republic of China, the candidate for Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the candidates for President of the Supreme People's Court and Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, and the candidates for the special committees of the National People's Congress.

Deputies to local people's congresses at or above the county level have the right to nominate candidates for members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, leading persons of the people's government, president of the people's court, procurator-general of the people's procuratorate, and deputies to the people's congress at the next higher level, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law, and have the right to express opinions on the candidates for the above-mentioned persons nominated by the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level and by deputies in accordance with the law.

Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns have the right to nominate candidates for chairman and vice-chairman of the people's congress at the corresponding level and leading persons of the people's government, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law, and have the right to express opinions on the candidates for the above-mentioned persons nominated by the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level and by deputies in accordance with the law.

Deputies to people's congresses at all levels have the right to express opinions on the candidates for the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Deputies may vote in favor of, vote against, vote for another person, or abstain from voting on the determined candidates.

Article 12 Deputies to the National People's Congress participate in deciding on the candidates for members of the State Council and for Vice-Chairmen and members of the Central Military Commission.

Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level participate in voting to approve the candidates for members of the special committees of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 13 When deliberating bills and reports, deputies may raise inquiries with the relevant state organs at the corresponding level. The relevant state organs shall send responsible persons or officials to answer the inquiries.

Article 14 During a session of the National People's Congress, a delegation or a group of thirty or more deputies jointly has the right to submit a written question of inquiry to the State Council and its ministries and commissions, the Supreme People's Court, and the Supreme People's Procuratorate.

Deputies to local people's congresses at or above the county level have the right to submit questions of inquiry to the people's government at the corresponding level and its subordinate departments, the people's court, and the people's procuratorate, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns have the right to submit questions of inquiry to the people's government at the corresponding level, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

A question of inquiry shall clearly state the organ inquired of, the issues inquired about, and the content.

The question of inquiry shall be answered by the organ inquired of in accordance with the decision of the presidium. If more than half of the deputies who submitted the question of inquiry are dissatisfied with the answer, they may request the organ inquired of to answer again.

Article 15 Deputies to the National People's Congress have the right to submit motions for the recall of members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the President and Vice President of the People's Republic of China, members of the State Council, members of the Central Military Commission, the President of the Supreme People's Court, and the Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

Deputies to local people's congresses at or above the county level have the right to submit motions for the recall of members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, members of the people's government, the president of the people's court, and the procurator-general of the people's procuratorate, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns have the right to submit motions for the recall of the chairman and vice-chairman of the people's congress at the corresponding level and leading persons of the people's government, in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

A motion for recall shall clearly state the reasons for the recall.

Article 16 Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level have the right to propose the establishment of an investigation committee on a specific issue in accordance with the law.

Article 17 When participating in votes of the people's congress at the corresponding level, deputies may vote in favor, vote against, or abstain.

Article 18 Deputies have the right to put forward suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on all aspects of work to the people's congress at the corresponding level. Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions shall be clear and specific, and focus on reflecting actual situations and problems.

## Chapter III: Activities of Deputies During the Recess of the People's Congress at the Corresponding Level

Article 19 The standing committees of people's congresses at or above the county level organize activities for deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level during the recess.

The standing committees of local people's congresses at or above the county level, entrusted by the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level, organize activities for deputies elected to the people's congress at the next higher level during the recess.

The chairmen and vice-chairmen of the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns, in accordance with the arrangements of the presidium, organize activities for deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level during the recess.

Article 20 Activities of deputies during the recess are mainly collective activities, with deputy group activities as the basic form. Deputies may listen to and reflect the opinions and demands of the voters of their electoral districts or the units that elected them through various means.

Article 21 Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level may, with the assistance of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or at a lower level, form deputy groups based on the principle of facilitating organization and activities.

Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level may participate in the activities of deputy groups of deputies to people's congresses at lower levels.

Article 22 Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level, in accordance with the arrangements of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, conduct inspections of the work of state organs and relevant units at the corresponding level or at lower levels. Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns, in accordance with the arrangements of the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level, conduct inspections of the work of the people's government and relevant units at the corresponding level.

When conducting inspections in accordance with the preceding paragraph, deputies may request to meet with the responsible persons of the relevant state organs at the corresponding level or at lower levels. The responsible persons of the relevant state organs who are requested to meet, or the responsible persons delegated by them, shall listen to the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of the deputies.

Deputies may conduct on-site inspections using their deputy credentials. The standing committees of local people's congresses at or above the county level or the presidiums of the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall, upon the request of the deputies, arrange for deputies at the corresponding level or at a higher level to conduct on-site inspections using their deputy credentials.

When conducting inspections, deputies may put forward suggestions, criticisms, and opinions to the units being inspected, but shall not directly handle problems.

Article 23 Deputies, in accordance with arrangements, conduct special investigations on major issues related to economic and social development and the vital interests of the people, as well as issues of general social concern.

Article 24 Reports formed by deputies from participating in inspections and special investigations shall be forwarded by the working offices of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of townships, ethnic townships, and towns to the relevant organs and organizations. The research and handling status of the opinions and suggestions raised in the reports shall be reported back to the deputies.

Article 25 Deputies have the right to propose the convening of an interim session of the people's congress at the corresponding level in accordance with the procedures provided for by law.

Article 26 Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level may be invited to attend as non-voting participants meetings of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and meetings of the special committees of the people's congress at the corresponding level, and participate in law enforcement inspections and other activities organized by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level. Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns participate in law enforcement inspections and other activities organized by the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 27 Deputies to the National People's Congress and deputies to the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, and cities divided into districts may attend as non-voting participants sessions of the people's congress of the unit that elected them, and may be invited to attend as non-voting participants meetings of the standing committee of the people's congress of the unit that elected them.

Article 28 Deputies to people's congresses at or above the county level, in accordance with the decisions of the people's congress at the corresponding level or its standing committee, participate in investigation committees on specific issues.

Article 29 During the recess of the people's congress at the corresponding level, deputies have the right to put forward suggestions, criticisms, and opinions on all aspects of work to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of townships, ethnic townships, and towns. Suggestions, criticisms, and opinions shall be clear and specific, and focus on reflecting actual situations and problems.

Article 30 Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns, during the recess of the people's congress at the corresponding level, conduct research and other activities in accordance with unified arrangements; form deputy groups, divide responsibilities to maintain contact with voters, and reflect the opinions and demands of the people.

## Chapter IV: Guarantees for Deputies to Perform Their Duties

Article 31 Deputies shall not be subject to legal liability for their speeches and votes at various meetings of the people's congress.

Article 32 No deputy to a people's congress at or above the county level may be arrested or subjected to criminal trial without the permission of the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level, or, during the recess of the people's congress at the corresponding level, without the permission of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level. If a deputy is detained as a flagrante delicto, the organ executing the detention shall immediately report to the presidium or the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

If other measures restricting personal freedom provided for by law are to be taken against a deputy to a people's congress at or above the county level, permission shall be obtained from the presidium or the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

When the presidium or the standing committee of the people's congress receives an application submitted by a relevant organ in accordance with this Article for permission, it shall examine whether there is any situation of legal liability being pursued for the deputy's speeches and votes at various meetings of the people's congress, or retaliation for the deputy's performance of duties such as putting forward suggestions, criticisms, and opinions, and shall make a decision accordingly.

If a deputy to the people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town is arrested, subjected to criminal trial, or has other measures restricting personal freedom provided for by law taken against them, the executing organ shall immediately report to the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town.

Article 33 When deputies participate in activities arranged by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of townships, ethnic townships, and towns during the recess of the people's congress at the corresponding level, the unit to which the deputy belongs must provide time guarantees.

Article 34 When deputies perform their duties in accordance with the provisions of Article 33 of this Law, their unit shall treat it as normal attendance and the deputy shall enjoy the salary and other benefits of the unit.

Deputies without fixed salary income who perform their duties shall receive appropriate subsidies from the finance at the corresponding level based on actual circumstances.

Article 35 The activity funds for deputies shall be included in the fiscal budget at the corresponding level for guarantee and used exclusively for their intended purposes.

Article 36 The standing committees of people's congresses at or above the county level shall maintain contact with deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level through various means and expand the participation of deputies in the activities of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 37 The standing committees of local people's congresses at or above the county level shall provide necessary conditions for deputies within their administrative regions to perform their duties.

Article 38 The standing committees of people's congresses at or above the county level, people's governments at all levels, and the people's courts and people's procuratorates shall promptly report their work situations to deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level, provide information and materials, and guarantee the deputies' right to know.

Article 39 The standing committees of people's congresses at or above the county level shall plan to organize deputies to participate in study for the performance of their duties, assist deputies in fully understanding the system of people's congresses, and mastering the legal knowledge and other professional knowledge necessary for performing their duties.

Deputies to the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns may participate in study for the performance of their duties organized by the standing committee of the people's congress at a higher level.

Article 40 The working offices and working organs of the standing committees of people's congresses at or above the county level are collective service organs for deputies to perform their duties, providing service guarantees for deputies to perform their duties.

Article 41 To facilitate deputies in performing their duties, people's congresses at all levels may issue deputy credentials to deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 42 Relevant organs and organizations shall seriously study and handle the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies, and shall reply within three months from the date of referral. For suggestions, criticisms, and opinions that are broad in scope and difficult to handle, a reply shall be given within six months from the date of referral.

During the process of studying and handling the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies, relevant organs and organizations shall maintain contact and communication with the deputies and fully listen to their opinions.

The handling status of the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the presidium of the people's congress of townships, ethnic townships, and towns, and printed and distributed at the next session of the people's congress. The report on the handling status of the suggestions, criticisms, and opinions of deputies shall be made public.

Article 43 When deputies from ethnic minorities perform their duties, relevant departments shall provide necessary assistance and care in terms of language, script, and living habits.

Article 44 All organizations and individuals must respect the rights of deputies and support deputies in performing their duties.

If a person who has the obligation to assist deputies in performing their duties refuses to fulfill that obligation, the relevant unit shall criticize and educate them, or even impose administrative sanctions.

If a person obstructs a deputy from performing their duties in accordance with the law, depending on the circumstances, the unit to which they belong or a higher authority shall impose administrative sanctions, or apply the penalty provisions of Article 50 of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Public Security Administration Punishments; if a person obstructs a deputy from performing their duties in accordance with the law by means of violence or threat, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.Anyone who retaliates against a representative for lawfully performing their duties shall be ordered to make corrections or be subject to administrative sanctions by the unit to which they belong or the superior authority; if a state functionary engages in such retaliation and it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

## Chapter V: Supervision of Representatives

Article 45: Representatives shall regularly solicit opinions from the people on their performance of duties through various means, respond to inquiries from the original electoral districts or electoral units regarding their work and activities, and accept supervision.

Representatives directly elected by voters shall report their performance of duties to the original electoral districts through various means. The standing committees of county-level people's congresses and the presidiums of people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall regularly organize representatives of the people's congresses at the corresponding level to report their performance of duties to the voters of the original electoral districts.

Article 46: Representatives shall properly handle the relationship between their personal professional activities and the performance of their representative duties, and shall not use the performance of their representative duties to interfere in specific judicial cases or economic activities such as bidding and tendering for personal gain.

Article 47: Voters or electoral units have the right to recall the representatives they have elected in accordance with the law. A representative proposed for recall has the right to attend the meeting to consider the recall and present a defense, or to submit a written defense.

Article 48: The performance of duties by a representative shall be temporarily suspended under any of the following circumstances, and the Credentials Committee shall report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or to the people's congress of the township, ethnic township, or town:

(1) Being detained in a criminal case and undergoing investigation, prosecution, or trial;
(2) Being sentenced to public surveillance, criminal detention, or fixed-term imprisonment without additional deprivation of political rights, and currently serving the sentence.

Once the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph cease to exist during the representative's term, their performance of duties shall be resumed, except in cases where the representative's qualification is terminated.

Article 49: The qualification of a representative shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) A representative of a local people's congress at any level moves out of or is transferred out of the administrative region;
(2) The resignation is accepted;
(3) Failing to attend the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level twice without approval;
(4) Being recalled;
(5) Losing Chinese nationality;
(6) Being deprived of political rights in accordance with the law;
(7) Losing the capacity to act.

Article 50: The termination of the qualification of a representative of a people's congress at or above the county level shall be reported by the Credentials Committee to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, which shall make a public announcement.

The termination of the qualification of a representative of a people's congress of a township, ethnic township, or town shall be reported by the Credentials Committee to the people's congress at the corresponding level, which shall make a public announcement.

## Chapter VI: Supplementary Provisions

Article 51: The people's congresses and their standing committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate implementation measures based on this Law and the actual circumstances of their respective administrative regions.

Article 52: This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
