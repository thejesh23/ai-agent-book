# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Garrison in the Macao Special Administrative Region

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 28, 1999

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution and the Basic Law of the Macao Special Administrative Region, for the purpose of ensuring that the troops stationed by the Central People's Government in the Macao Special Administrative Region for defense perform their functions and duties according to law, and safeguarding the sovereignty, unity, and territorial integrity of the State and the security of Macao.

Article 2 The troops stationed by the Central People's Government in the Macao Special Administrative Region for defense shall be designated as the Macao Garrison of the Chinese People's Liberation Army (hereinafter referred to as the Macao Garrison).

The Macao Garrison shall be under the leadership of the Central Military Commission of the People's Republic of China; its composition and number shall be determined according to the needs of the defense of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

The Macao Garrison shall practice a system of rotation of its personnel.

Article 3 The Macao Garrison shall not interfere in the local affairs of the Macao Special Administrative Region. The Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region may, when necessary, ask the Central People's Government for assistance from the Macao Garrison in the maintenance of public order and in disaster relief.

Article 4 Personnel of the Macao Garrison shall abide by the national laws as well as the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 5 The expenses of the Macao Garrison shall be borne by the Central People's Government.

## Chapter II Functions and Duties of the Macao Garrison

Article 6 The Macao Garrison shall perform the following defense functions and duties:

(1) to guard against and resist aggression, and to safeguard the security of the Macao Special Administrative Region;

(2) to undertake defense duties;

(3) to administer military facilities;

(4) to handle matters relating to foreign military affairs.

Article 7 When the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress decides to declare a state of war or, by reason of turmoil within the Macao Special Administrative Region which endangers national unity or security and is beyond the control of the Government of the Region, decides that the Region is in a state of emergency, the Macao Garrison shall perform its duties in accordance with the provisions of the national laws implemented in the Macao Special Administrative Region as decided by the Central People's Government.

Article 8 The aircraft, vessels, weapons, equipment, and materials of the Macao Garrison, and the personnel and vehicles on duty bearing certificates or documents issued by the Macao Garrison, shall not be subject to inspection, search, or seizure by law enforcement personnel of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

The Macao Garrison and its personnel shall also enjoy other rights and immunities provided for by the laws implemented in the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 9 Personnel of the Macao Garrison may take measures to stop acts that obstruct them from performing their duties, in accordance with the provisions of the laws implemented in the Macao Special Administrative Region.

## Chapter III Relationship Between the Macao Garrison and the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region

Article 10 The Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall support the Macao Garrison in performing its defense functions and duties, and shall protect the lawful rights and interests of the Macao Garrison and its personnel.

The Macao Special Administrative Region shall, by law, guarantee the rights and immunities that the Macao Garrison and its personnel should enjoy when performing their duties.

When formulating policies, introducing bills, or drafting administrative regulations that involve the Macao Garrison, the Macao Special Administrative Region shall seek the views of the Macao Garrison.

Article 11 When the Macao Garrison conducts military activities such as training and exercises that involve the public interests of the Macao Special Administrative Region, it shall notify the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region in advance.

Article 12 The Macao Garrison and the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall jointly protect the military facilities within the Macao Special Administrative Region.

The Macao Garrison shall, in conjunction with the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region, delimit military restricted zones. The location and scope of the military restricted zones shall be announced by the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

The Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall assist the Macao Garrison in maintaining the security of the military restricted zones and shall prohibit any organization or individual from damaging or endangering military facilities.

No person, vehicle, vessel, or aircraft other than those of the Macao Garrison may enter a military restricted zone without the approval of the highest commander of the Macao Garrison or an officer authorized by him. The guards of the military restricted zones shall have the right to stop, according to law, any unauthorized entry into the military restricted zones and any act that damages or endangers military facilities.

The Macao Garrison shall protect the natural resources, cultural relics, and historical sites, as well as non-military rights and interests, within the military restricted zones in accordance with the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 13 The military land for the Macao Garrison shall be provided free of charge by the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

If military land of the Macao Garrison is no longer needed for defense purposes with the approval of the Central People's Government, it shall be transferred to the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region free of charge.

If the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region needs to use part of the military land of the Macao Garrison for public purposes, it must obtain the approval of the Central People's Government; upon approval, the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall provide the Macao Garrison with new military land and military facilities at a location agreed upon by the Central People's Government, and shall bear all costs.

Article 14 After the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region requests the Central People's Government for assistance from the Macao Garrison in the maintenance of public order and in disaster relief and obtains the approval of the Central People's Government, the Macao Garrison shall dispatch troops in accordance with the orders of the Central Military Commission to carry out tasks of assisting in the maintenance of public order and disaster relief, and shall return to their barracks immediately after the tasks are completed.

When the Macao Garrison assists in the maintenance of public order and disaster relief, it shall be under the command of the highest commander of the Macao Garrison or an officer authorized by him, under the arrangement of the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

When personnel of the Macao Garrison assist in the maintenance of public order and disaster relief, they shall exercise the powers of relevant law enforcement personnel provided for by the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region, which are appropriate for the performance of their tasks.

Article 15 The Macao Garrison and the Government of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall establish necessary liaison to consult and handle matters relating to the Garrison.

## Chapter IV Obligations and Discipline of Personnel of the Macao Garrison

Article 16 Personnel of the Macao Garrison shall perform the following obligations:

(1) to be loyal to the motherland, perform their duties, safeguard the security, honor, and interests of the State, and safeguard the security of Macao;

(2) to abide by the national laws and the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region, and to observe military discipline;

(3) to respect the organs of political power of the Macao Special Administrative Region, and to respect the social system and way of life of the Macao Special Administrative Region;

(4) to take good care of the public property of the Macao Special Administrative Region and the private property of Macao residents and other persons;

(5) to observe social morality and to be civilized and courteous.

Article 17 Personnel of the Macao Garrison shall not join political organizations, religious organizations, or social organizations in Macao.

Article 18 The Macao Garrison and its personnel shall not engage in any form of profit-making business activities. Personnel of the Macao Garrison shall not engage in any other activities that are incompatible with their duties as military personnel.

Article 19 If personnel of the Macao Garrison violate the national laws or the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region, they shall be investigated for legal responsibility according to law.

If personnel of the Macao Garrison violate military discipline, they shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions.

## Chapter V Jurisdiction Over Personnel of the Macao Garrison

Article 20 Cases of crimes committed by personnel of the Macao Garrison shall be under the jurisdiction of military judicial organs; however, cases of crimes committed by personnel of the Macao Garrison through acts not in the performance of their duties, which infringe upon the personal or property rights of Macao residents or persons other than those of the Macao Garrison, or which otherwise violate the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region and constitute crimes, shall be under the jurisdiction of the judicial organs of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

If the military judicial organs and the judicial organs of the Macao Special Administrative Region consider that a case of a crime committed by a person of the Macao Garrison under their respective jurisdiction is more appropriately tried by the other party, they may, after consultation and agreement, transfer the case to the other party for jurisdiction.

In cases of crimes committed by personnel of the Macao Garrison under the jurisdiction of the military judicial organs, if the accused includes Macao residents or persons other than those of the Macao Garrison, such accused shall be tried by the courts of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 21 If a person suspected of committing a crime who is lawfully arrested or detained by law enforcement personnel of the Macao Special Administrative Region is found to be a person of the Macao Garrison, he shall be handed over to the Macao Garrison for custody. The jurisdiction over the case involving the person in custody shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of Article 20 of this Law.

Article 22 If a person of the Macao Garrison is sentenced by a court of the Macao Special Administrative Region to a penalty or security measure that deprives or restricts personal freedom, he shall be delivered for execution in accordance with the provisions of the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region; however, this shall not apply if the relevant law enforcement organs of the Macao Special Administrative Region and the military judicial organs have otherwise agreed on the place of execution through consultation.

Article 23 If a person of the Macao Garrison violates the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region and infringes upon the civil rights of Macao residents or persons other than those of the Macao Garrison, the parties concerned may resolve the matter through consultation or mediation; if they are unwilling to do so or if consultation or mediation fails, the infringed party may file a lawsuit in a court. Cases of civil infringement arising from acts not in the performance of duties by personnel of the Macao Garrison shall be under the jurisdiction of the courts of the Macao Special Administrative Region; cases of civil infringement arising from acts in the performance of duties shall be under the jurisdiction of the Supreme People's Court of the People's Republic of China, and the compensation for damages from the infringement shall be governed by the laws of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 24 If a dispute arises from a contract between an organ or unit of the Macao Garrison and a Macao resident or a person other than those of the Macao Garrison in the Macao Special Administrative Region, the parties concerned may resolve it through consultation or mediation. If the parties are unwilling to do so or if consultation or mediation fails, they may apply to an arbitration body for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration clause in the contract or a written arbitration agreement reached afterwards. If the parties have not included an arbitration clause in the contract and have not reached a written arbitration agreement afterwards, they may file a lawsuit in a court of the Macao Special Administrative Region; however, this shall not apply if the parties have otherwise agreed on the court to which the lawsuit is to be filed.

Article 25 In litigation activities in the courts of the Macao Special Administrative Region, the certificates issued by the Macao Garrison regarding facts such as the identity of a person of the Macao Garrison or whether an act was performed in the course of duty shall be valid evidence, unless contrary evidence is established.

Article 26 State acts such as national defense of the Macao Garrison shall not be subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

Article 27 If a judgment or ruling made by a court of the Macao Special Administrative Region involves the enforcement of property of an organ or unit of the Macao Garrison, the organ or unit of the Macao Garrison must comply; however, the courts of the Macao Special Administrative Region shall not enforce compulsory execution against the weapons, equipment, materials, or other property of the Macao Garrison.

Article 28 Military judicial organs may, through consultation, maintain judicial contacts and provide mutual assistance with the judicial organs and relevant law enforcement organs of the Macao Special Administrative Region.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 29 The power of interpretation of this Law shall be vested in the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 30 This Law shall come into force as of December 20, 1999.
