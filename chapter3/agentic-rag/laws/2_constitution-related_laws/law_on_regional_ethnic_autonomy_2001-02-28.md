# Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Sixth National People's Congress on May 31, 1984

Amended in accordance with the Decision on the Revision of the Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on February 28, 2001

<!-- INFO END -->

## Preamble

The People's Republic of China is a unified multi-ethnic country jointly created by the people of all ethnic groups. Regional ethnic autonomy is the basic policy of the Communist Party of China for solving ethnic issues in China by applying Marxism-Leninism, and is a fundamental political system of the state.

Regional ethnic autonomy means that under the unified leadership of the state, regional autonomy is practiced in areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities, and autonomous organs are established to exercise the power of autonomy. The practice of regional ethnic autonomy embodies the spirit of the state fully respecting and guaranteeing the right of ethnic minorities to manage their own internal affairs, and embodies the principle of the state adhering to equality, unity, and common prosperity among all ethnic groups.

The practice of regional ethnic autonomy has played a huge role in giving full play to the initiative of the people of all ethnic groups to be masters of the country, developing socialist ethnic relations of equality, unity, and mutual assistance, consolidating the unity of the state, and promoting the development of socialist construction in ethnic autonomous areas and the whole country. In the future, we will continue to uphold and improve the system of regional ethnic autonomy, so that this system will play a greater role in the process of the country's socialist modernization.

Practice has proven that to uphold the practice of regional ethnic autonomy, it is necessary to effectively ensure that ethnic autonomous areas implement the laws and policies of the state in accordance with their local conditions; it is necessary to train a large number of cadres at all levels, various professional personnel, and skilled workers from among ethnic minorities; ethnic autonomous areas must carry forward the spirit of self-reliance and hard work, strive to develop their own socialist construction, and contribute to national construction; the state, in accordance with the national economic and social development plan, strives to help ethnic autonomous areas accelerate economic and cultural development. In the struggle to maintain ethnic unity, it is necessary to oppose great-ethnic chauvinism, mainly Han chauvinism, and also to oppose local ethnic chauvinism.

The people of all ethnic groups in ethnic autonomous areas, together with the people of the whole country, under the leadership of the Communist Party of China, guided by Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, and Deng Xiaoping Theory, adhere to the people's democratic dictatorship, adhere to reform and opening up, follow the path of building socialism with Chinese characteristics, concentrate on socialist modernization, develop the socialist market economy, strengthen socialist democracy and the legal system, strengthen the construction of socialist spiritual civilization, accelerate the economic and cultural development of ethnic autonomous areas, build united and prosperous ethnic autonomous areas, and strive to build the motherland into a prosperous, strong, democratic, and civilized socialist country for the common prosperity of all ethnic groups.

The Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China is the basic law for implementing the system of regional ethnic autonomy stipulated in the Constitution.

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 The Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China is formulated in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China.

Article 2 Regional autonomy is practiced in areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities.

Ethnic autonomous areas are divided into autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties.

All ethnic autonomous areas are inseparable parts of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 Autonomous organs are established in ethnic autonomous areas. The autonomous organs are local state organs at a specific level.

The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas practice the principle of democratic centralism.

Article 4 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas exercise the functions and powers of local state organs as specified in Section 5 of Chapter III of the Constitution, and at the same time exercise the power of autonomy within the limits prescribed by the Constitution, this Law, and other laws, and implement the laws and policies of the state in light of local conditions.

The autonomous organs of autonomous prefectures exercise the functions and powers of local state organs of cities divided into districts and counties, and at the same time exercise the power of autonomy.

Article 5 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas must safeguard the unity of the state and ensure the observance and implementation of the Constitution and laws in their respective areas.

Article 6 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas lead the people of all ethnic groups in concentrating their efforts on socialist modernization.

The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas have the right, in light of local conditions and without violating the principles of the Constitution and laws, to adopt special policies and flexible measures to accelerate the development of economic and cultural construction in ethnic autonomous areas.

Under the guidance of state plans, the autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas, proceeding from reality, continuously raise labor productivity and economic efficiency, develop social productive forces, and gradually raise the material living standards of all ethnic groups.

The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas inherit and carry forward the fine traditions of ethnic culture, build a socialist spiritual civilization with ethnic characteristics, and continuously raise the socialist consciousness and scientific and cultural level of the people of all ethnic groups.

Article 7 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas shall give top priority to the overall interests of the state and actively complete the tasks assigned by the higher state organs.

Article 8 Higher state organs shall ensure that the autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas exercise their power of autonomy, and, based on the characteristics and needs of ethnic autonomous areas, strive to help ethnic autonomous areas accelerate the development of socialist construction.

Article 9 Higher state organs and the autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas shall safeguard and develop the socialist ethnic relations of equality, unity, and mutual assistance. Discrimination against or oppression of any ethnic group is prohibited; acts that undermine ethnic unity or create ethnic division are prohibited.

Article 10 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas shall ensure that all ethnic groups in their respective areas have the freedom to use and develop their own spoken and written languages, and the freedom to preserve or reform their own customs and habits.

Article 11 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas shall ensure that citizens of all ethnic groups have freedom of religious belief.

No state organ, social organization, or individual may compel citizens to believe in or not believe in religion, nor may they discriminate against citizens who believe in or do not believe in religion.

The state protects normal religious activities.

No one may use religion to engage in activities that disrupt social order, harm the physical health of citizens, or impede the state's educational system.

Religious groups and religious affairs are not subject to the domination of foreign forces.

## Chapter II Establishment of Ethnic Autonomous Areas and Composition of Autonomous Organs

Article 12 In areas where ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities, autonomous areas may be established based on the concentrated communities of one or more ethnic minorities, taking into account local ethnic relations, economic development, and other conditions, as well as historical circumstances.

In areas within an ethnic autonomous area where other ethnic minorities live in concentrated communities, corresponding autonomous areas or ethnic townships shall be established.

An ethnic autonomous area may, based on its actual conditions, include residential areas and towns of Han or other ethnic groups.

Article 13 The name of an ethnic autonomous area shall, except in special circumstances, be composed in the order of the place name, the ethnic name, and the administrative status.

Article 14 The establishment of an ethnic autonomous area, the delimitation of its boundaries, and the composition of its name shall be fully negotiated and proposed by the higher state organ together with the relevant local state organs and representatives of the relevant ethnic groups, and shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law.

Once an ethnic autonomous area is established, it shall not be dissolved or merged without legal procedures; once the boundaries of an ethnic autonomous area are delimited, they shall not be altered without legal procedures; if dissolution, merger, or alteration is truly necessary, it shall be fully negotiated and proposed by the relevant departments of the higher state organ and the autonomous organ of the ethnic autonomous area, and submitted for approval in accordance with legal procedures.

Article 15 The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas are the people's congresses and people's governments of autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties.

The people's government of an ethnic autonomous area is responsible and reports on its work to the people's congress at the corresponding level and to the state administrative organ at the next higher level; when the people's congress at the corresponding level is not in session, it is responsible and reports on its work to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level. The people's governments of all ethnic autonomous areas are state administrative organs under the unified leadership of the State Council and are subordinate to the State Council.

The organization and work of the autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas shall be prescribed by the regulations on the exercise of autonomy or separate regulations of the ethnic autonomous area in accordance with the Constitution and laws.

Article 16 In the people's congress of an ethnic autonomous area, in addition to the representatives of the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy, other ethnic groups residing in the administrative area shall also have an appropriate number of representatives.

The number and proportion of representatives of the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy and other ethnic minorities in the people's congress of an ethnic autonomous area shall be determined by the standing committee of the people's congress of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with the principles prescribed by law, and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the record.

The standing committee of the people's congress of an ethnic autonomous area shall have a citizen of the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy serving as its chairperson or vice-chairperson.

Article 17 The chairperson of an autonomous region, the prefect of an autonomous prefecture, or the head of an autonomous county shall be a citizen of the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy. The other members of the people's government of an autonomous region, autonomous prefecture, or autonomous county shall include an appropriate proportion of personnel from the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy and other ethnic minorities.

The people's government of an ethnic autonomous area implements the system of responsibility of the chairperson of the autonomous region, the prefect of the autonomous prefecture, or the head of the autonomous county. The chairperson of the autonomous region, the prefect of the autonomous prefecture, or the head of the autonomous county shall respectively preside over the work of the people's government at the corresponding level.

Article 18 Among the cadres of the working departments under the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area, an appropriate proportion of personnel from the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy and other ethnic minorities shall be included.

## Chapter III The Power of Autonomy of Autonomous Organs

Article 19 The people's congress of an ethnic autonomous area has the power to formulate regulations on the exercise of autonomy and separate regulations in accordance with the political, economic, and cultural characteristics of the local ethnic group(s). The regulations on the exercise of autonomy and separate regulations of an autonomous region shall come into effect after being approved by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The regulations on the exercise of autonomy and separate regulations of an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county shall come into effect after being approved by the standing committee of the people's congress of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for the record.

Article 20 If resolutions, decisions, orders, or directives of higher state organs are not suitable for the actual conditions of an ethnic autonomous area, the autonomous organ may, subject to the approval of the higher state organ, implement them with modifications or cease their implementation; the higher state organ shall reply within sixty days from the date of receiving the report.

Article 21 When performing their duties, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall, in accordance with the regulations on the exercise of autonomy of the ethnic autonomous area, use one or more of the spoken and written languages commonly used in the locality; when performing duties using several commonly used spoken and written languages, the spoken and written language of the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy may be used as the main language.

Article 22 In accordance with the needs of socialist construction, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall adopt various measures to train a large number of cadres at all levels, scientific and technological personnel, management professionals, and skilled workers from among the local ethnic groups, give full play to their roles, and pay attention to training cadres at all levels and various professional and technical personnel from among ethnic minority women.

When recruiting staff, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall give appropriate consideration to personnel from the ethnic group practicing regional autonomy and other ethnic minorities.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area may adopt special measures to provide preferential treatment and encouragement for various professionals to participate in the construction of the autonomous area.

Article 23 When recruiting personnel in accordance with state regulations, enterprises and institutions in an ethnic autonomous area shall give priority to recruiting personnel from ethnic minorities, and may recruit personnel from among the ethnic minority populations in rural and pastoral areas.

Article 24 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area may, in accordance with the military system of the state and the actual needs of the locality, and with the approval of the State Council, organize local public security forces to maintain public order.

Article 25 Under the guidance of state plans, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall, in light of local characteristics and needs, formulate guidelines, policies, and plans for economic construction, and independently arrange and manage local economic construction.

Article 26 On the premise of adhering to socialist principles, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall, in accordance with legal provisions and the characteristics of local economic development, rationally adjust relations of production and economic structure, and strive to develop the socialist market economy.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area adhere to the basic economic system with public ownership as the mainstay and diverse forms of ownership developing together, and encourage the development of the non-public sector of the economy.

Article 27 In accordance with legal provisions, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall determine the ownership and use rights of grasslands and forests within their locality.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall protect and develop grasslands and forests, and organize and encourage tree planting and grass planting. Any organization or individual is prohibited from using any means to destroy grasslands and forests. It is strictly forbidden to destroy grasslands and forests for land reclamation.

Article 28 In accordance with legal provisions, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall manage and protect the natural resources of their locality.

In accordance with legal provisions and the unified planning of the state, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall give priority to the rational development and utilization of natural resources that can be developed by the locality.

Article 29 Under the guidance of state plans, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall, in light of local financial resources, material resources, and other specific conditions, independently arrange local capital construction projects.

Article 30 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently manage the enterprises and institutions subordinate to the locality.

Article 31 In accordance with state regulations, an ethnic autonomous area may engage in foreign economic and trade activities, and with the approval of the State Council, may open foreign trade ports.

An ethnic autonomous area bordering a foreign country may, with the approval of the State Council, conduct border trade.

An ethnic autonomous area shall enjoy preferential state policies in foreign economic and trade activities.

Article 32 The finance of an ethnic autonomous area constitutes a level of finance and is a component of state finance.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area have the power of autonomy in managing local finance. All fiscal revenues belonging to an ethnic autonomous area under the state fiscal system shall be independently arranged and used by the autonomous organ of the ethnic autonomous area.

Under the unified national fiscal system, an ethnic autonomous area shall enjoy preferential treatment from higher-level finance through the standardized fiscal transfer payment system implemented by the state.

In the fiscal budget expenditure of an ethnic autonomous area, a reserve fund shall be established in accordance with state regulations, and the proportion of the reserve fund in the budget shall be higher than that in general areas.

In the process of implementing the fiscal budget, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently arrange for the use of surplus revenue and savings in expenditure.

Article 33 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area may formulate supplementary provisions and specific measures for the expenditure standards, staffing, and quotas of the locality, in accordance with the principles prescribed by the state and in light of local conditions. The supplementary provisions and specific measures formulated by an autonomous region shall be reported to the State Council for the record; the supplementary provisions and specific measures formulated by an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county shall be submitted to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval.

Article 34 When implementing the state tax laws, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area may, in addition to tax reduction and exemption items that require unified state approval, implement tax reduction or exemption for certain items that belong to local fiscal revenue and need tax support and encouragement. The decision of an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county to implement tax reduction or exemption shall be submitted to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval.

Article 35 An ethnic autonomous area may, in accordance with legal provisions and based on the needs of local economic and social development, establish local commercial banks and urban and rural credit cooperative organizations.

Article 36 In accordance with the state's educational policy and legal provisions, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall decide on local educational plans, the establishment of schools of various types and levels, the length of schooling, forms of school operation, teaching content, language of instruction, and enrollment methods.

Article 37 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently develop ethnic education, eliminate illiteracy, establish various types of schools, popularize nine-year compulsory education, develop regular senior secondary education and secondary vocational and technical education in various forms, and develop higher education according to conditions and needs, cultivating professional personnel from various ethnic minorities.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall establish public ethnic primary schools and ethnic middle schools, mainly boarding schools and mainly providing grants, for ethnic minority pastoral areas and economically disadvantaged, sparsely populated ethnic minority mountainous areas, ensuring that enrolled students complete their compulsory education. School operating expenses and grants shall be covered by local finances; if local finances are difficult, higher-level finances shall provide subsidies.

Schools (classes) and other educational institutions that mainly enroll ethnic minority students shall, where conditions permit, use textbooks in the ethnic minority language and teach in the ethnic minority language; depending on the situation, Chinese language courses shall be offered from the lower or upper grades of primary school, and the nationally common spoken and written Chinese language shall be promoted.

People's governments at all levels shall provide financial support for the compilation, translation, and publication of textbooks and publications in ethnic minority languages.

Article 38 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently develop ethnic cultural undertakings such as literature, art, journalism, publishing, broadcasting, film, and television with ethnic forms and characteristics, increase investment in cultural undertakings, strengthen the construction of cultural facilities, and accelerate the development of various cultural undertakings.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall organize and support relevant units and departments in collecting, sorting, translating, and publishing books on ethnic history and culture, protecting ethnic scenic spots and historical sites, precious cultural relics, and other important historical and cultural heritage, and inheriting and developing excellent ethnic traditional culture.

Article 39 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently decide on the local science and technology development plan and popularize scientific and technological knowledge.

Article 40 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently decide on the local medical and health development plan, and develop modern medicine and ethnic traditional medicine.

The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall strengthen the prevention and control of infectious diseases and endemic diseases, as well as maternal and child health care, and improve medical and health conditions.

Article 41 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall independently develop sports undertakings, carry out ethnic traditional sports activities, and enhance the physical fitness of the people of all ethnic groups.

Article 42 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall actively carry out exchanges and cooperation with other places in education, science and technology, culture and arts, health, and sports.

The autonomous organs of an autonomous region or autonomous prefecture may, in accordance with state regulations, conduct exchanges with foreign countries in education, science and technology, culture and arts, health, and sports.

Article 43 In accordance with legal provisions, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall formulate measures for managing the floating population.

Article 44 An ethnic autonomous area shall implement family planning and promote eugenics and better child rearing to improve the population quality of all ethnic groups.

In accordance with legal provisions and in light of local conditions, the autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall formulate measures for implementing family planning.

Article 45 The autonomous organs of an ethnic autonomous area shall protect and improve the living environment and ecological environment, prevent and control pollution and other public hazards, and achieve coordinated development of population, resources, and the environment.

## Chapter IV People's Courts and People's Procuratorates of Ethnic Autonomous AreasArticle 46 The people's courts and people's procuratorates of national autonomous areas shall be responsible to the people's congresses at the corresponding levels and their standing committees. The people's procuratorates of national autonomous areas shall also be responsible to the people's procuratorates at higher levels.

The judicial work of the people's courts of national autonomous areas shall be supervised by the Supreme People's Court and the people's courts at higher levels. The work of the people's procuratorates of national autonomous areas shall be directed by the Supreme People's Procuratorate and the people's procuratorates at higher levels.

Among the leading members and staff of the people's courts and people's procuratorates of national autonomous areas, there shall be persons from the ethnic group exercising regional autonomy.

Article 47 The people's courts and people's procuratorates of national autonomous areas shall hear and prosecute cases in the languages commonly used in the locality, and shall reasonably allocate personnel proficient in the spoken and written languages of the local minority ethnic groups. For participants in proceedings who are not familiar with the languages commonly used in the locality, interpretation shall be provided. Legal documents shall, based on actual needs, be written in one or more of the languages commonly used in the locality. The right of citizens of all ethnic groups to use their own spoken and written languages in legal proceedings shall be guaranteed.

## Chapter V Ethnic Relations Within National Autonomous Areas

Article 48 The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall guarantee equal rights for all ethnic groups within their respective areas.

The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall unite cadres and the masses of all ethnic groups, fully mobilize their initiative, and jointly build the national autonomous areas.

Article 49 The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall educate and encourage cadres of all ethnic groups to learn each other's spoken and written languages. Han cadres shall learn the spoken and written languages of the local minority ethnic groups, while minority cadres, while learning and using their own spoken and written languages, shall also learn Putonghua (common speech) and standard Chinese characters in common use nationwide.

State functionaries in national autonomous areas who are proficient in using two or more of the languages commonly used in the locality shall be rewarded.

Article 50 The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall assist other minority ethnic groups living in concentrated communities within their areas in establishing corresponding autonomous areas or ethnic townships.

The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall assist all ethnic groups within their areas in developing their economies, education, science and technology, culture, health, and sports undertakings.

The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall attend to the characteristics and needs of ethnic groups living in scattered communities within their areas.

Article 51 When dealing with special issues involving the various ethnic groups within their areas, the organs of self-government of national autonomous areas must fully consult with their representatives and respect their opinions.

Article 52 The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall guarantee that all citizens of all ethnic groups within their areas enjoy the rights of citizens as stipulated by the Constitution, and shall educate them in fulfilling their duties as citizens.

Article 53 The organs of self-government of national autonomous areas shall advocate the public virtues of loving the motherland, the people, labor, science, and socialism, and conduct education among citizens of all ethnic groups within their areas in patriotism, communism, and ethnic policies. They shall educate cadres and the masses of all ethnic groups to trust each other, learn from each other, help each other, respect each other's spoken and written languages, customs, habits, and religious beliefs, and jointly safeguard the unity of the state and the unity of all ethnic groups.

## Chapter VI Duties of State Organs at Higher Levels

Article 54 Resolutions, decisions, orders, and directives of state organs at higher levels concerning national autonomous areas shall be suited to the actual conditions of the national autonomous areas.

Article 55 State organs at higher levels shall assist and guide the research, formulation, and implementation of economic development strategies for national autonomous areas, and assist national autonomous areas in accelerating the development of their economies, education, science and technology, culture, health, and sports undertakings through financial, monetary, material, technological, and human resources.

The state shall formulate preferential policies to guide and encourage domestic and foreign capital to invest in national autonomous areas.

When formulating plans for national economic and social development, state organs at higher levels shall attend to the characteristics and needs of national autonomous areas.

Article 56 The state shall, in accordance with unified planning and market demand, give priority to the rational arrangement of resource development projects and infrastructure construction projects in national autonomous areas. The state shall appropriately increase the proportion of investment and the proportion of policy bank loans in major infrastructure investment projects in national autonomous areas.

When the state arranges infrastructure construction in national autonomous areas that requires matching funds from the national autonomous areas, it shall, depending on the circumstances, reduce or exempt the matching funds.

The state shall assist national autonomous areas in accelerating the development and transformation of practical scientific and technological achievements, vigorously promote practical technologies and high technologies that can be developed under suitable conditions, and actively guide the rational flow of scientific and technological personnel to national autonomous areas. When the state provides transferred construction projects to national autonomous areas, it shall provide advanced and suitable equipment and technology based on local conditions.

Article 57 The state shall, based on the characteristics and needs of the economic development of national autonomous areas, comprehensively utilize the money market and capital market to increase financial support for national autonomous areas. Financial institutions shall provide key support to fixed asset investment projects in national autonomous areas and to enterprises that conform to the state's industrial policies for their reasonable capital needs in developing resources and diversifying the economy.

The state shall encourage commercial banks to increase credit input to national autonomous areas and actively support the reasonable capital needs of local enterprises.

Article 58 State organs at higher levels shall assist enterprises in national autonomous areas in technological innovation and promote the upgrading of their industrial structure from financial, monetary, and human resources perspectives.

State organs at higher levels shall organize and encourage enterprise management personnel and technical personnel from national autonomous areas to study in economically developed areas, and at the same time guide and encourage enterprise management personnel and technical personnel from economically developed areas to work in enterprises in national autonomous areas.

Article 59 The state shall establish various special funds to assist national autonomous areas in developing their economic and cultural undertakings.

No department may deduct, intercept, misappropriate, or use to replace the normal budgetary revenue of national autonomous areas any special funds established by the state or temporary ethnic subsidies.

Article 60 State organs at higher levels shall, in accordance with the state's ethnic trade policies and the needs of national autonomous areas, provide support to commercial, supply and marketing, and pharmaceutical enterprises in national autonomous areas in terms of investment, finance, and taxation.

Article 61 The state shall formulate preferential policies to support national autonomous areas in developing foreign economic trade, expand the autonomy of production enterprises in national autonomous areas in foreign trade operations, encourage the export of local advantageous products, and implement preferential border trade policies.

Article 62 With the development of the national economy and the growth of fiscal revenue, the fiscal authorities at higher levels shall gradually increase the intensity of fiscal transfer payments to national autonomous areas. Through general fiscal transfer payments, special fiscal transfer payments, fiscal transfer payments for ethnic preferential policies, and other methods determined by the state, the state shall increase capital investment in national autonomous areas to accelerate their economic development and social progress, gradually narrowing the gap with developed areas.

Article 63 State organs at higher levels shall support national autonomous areas in improving production conditions in agriculture, animal husbandry, and forestry, as well as infrastructure such as water conservancy, transportation, energy, and communications, through investment, finance, and taxation; and support national autonomous areas in rationally utilizing local resources to develop local industries, township enterprises, small and medium-sized enterprises, and the production of commodities specially needed by minority ethnic groups and traditional handicraft products.

Article 64 State organs at higher levels shall organize, support, and encourage economically developed areas to carry out economic and technological cooperation and multi-level, multi-faceted counterpart support with national autonomous areas, helping and promoting the development of the economy, education, science and technology, culture, health, and sports undertakings in national autonomous areas.

Article 65 When developing resources and carrying out construction in national autonomous areas, the state shall attend to the interests of the national autonomous areas, make arrangements conducive to their economic construction, and attend to the production and livelihood of the local minority ethnic groups. The state shall take measures to provide certain compensation for the benefits of national autonomous areas that export natural resources.

The state shall guide and encourage enterprises in economically developed areas to invest in national autonomous areas and carry out various forms of economic cooperation on the principle of mutual benefit.

Article 66 State organs at higher levels shall incorporate major ecological balance and comprehensive environmental protection and management projects in national autonomous areas into the national economic and social development plan for unified deployment.

If national autonomous areas make contributions to the state's ecological balance and environmental protection, the state shall provide certain compensation.

When any organization or individual develops resources or carries out construction in national autonomous areas, they shall take effective measures to protect and improve the local living and ecological environment, and prevent and control pollution and other public hazards.

Article 67 When enterprises and institutions subordinate to state organs at higher levels in national autonomous areas recruit personnel in accordance with state regulations, they shall give priority to recruiting local minority personnel.

Enterprises and institutions in national autonomous areas shall respect the autonomy of the local organs of self-government, abide by local autonomous regulations, separate regulations, local laws and regulations, and rules, and accept the supervision of the local organs of self-government.

Article 68 State organs at higher levels shall not change the subordinate relationship of enterprises belonging to national autonomous areas without the consent of the organs of self-government of the national autonomous areas.

Article 69 The state and the people's governments at higher levels shall increase support for poverty-stricken areas within national autonomous areas from financial, monetary, material, technological, and human resources perspectives, helping the impoverished population to shake off poverty as soon as possible and achieve a moderately prosperous standard of living.

Article 70 State organs at higher levels shall assist national autonomous areas in training a large number of cadres at all levels, various types of specialized personnel, and skilled workers from among the local ethnic groups; and, according to the needs of the national autonomous areas, adopt various forms to dispatch an appropriate number of teachers, doctors, scientific and technological personnel, and management personnel to participate in the work of the national autonomous areas, giving appropriate consideration to their living conditions.

Article 71 The state shall increase investment in education in national autonomous areas and adopt special measures to help national autonomous areas accelerate the popularization of nine-year compulsory education and develop other educational undertakings, thereby raising the scientific and cultural level of the people of all ethnic groups.

The state shall establish institutions of higher learning for ethnic groups, set up ethnic classes and preparatory courses for ethnic groups in institutions of higher learning, specifically or mainly enroll minority students, and may adopt methods of directional enrollment and directional assignment. When enrolling new students, institutions of higher learning and secondary specialized schools shall appropriately relax admission standards and conditions for minority examinees, and give special consideration to examinees from very small minority groups. People's governments at all levels and schools shall adopt various measures to help minority students from families with financial difficulties to complete their studies.

The state shall establish ethnic middle schools in developed areas or set up ethnic classes in ordinary middle schools to enroll minority students for secondary education.

The state shall assist national autonomous areas in training and cultivating teachers of all ethnic groups. The state shall organize and encourage teachers of all ethnic groups and graduates of all ethnic groups who meet the qualifications for teaching positions to engage in education and teaching work in national autonomous areas, and grant them corresponding preferential treatment.

Article 72 State organs at higher levels shall strengthen education on ethnic policies among cadres and the masses of all ethnic groups, and regularly inspect the observance and implementation of ethnic policies and relevant laws.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 73 The State Council and its relevant departments shall, within their respective functions and powers, formulate administrative regulations, rules, specific measures, and methods for the implementation of this Law.

The people's congresses and their standing committees of autonomous regions, and provinces and municipalities directly under the Central Government that have autonomous prefectures or autonomous counties shall, in light of local actual conditions, formulate specific measures for the implementation of this Law.

Article 74 This Law is adopted by the National People's Congress and shall come into force on October 1, 1984.
