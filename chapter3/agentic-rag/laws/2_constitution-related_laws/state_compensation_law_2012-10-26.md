# State Compensation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 12, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the State Compensation Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 29, 2010

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the State Compensation Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure the right of citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to obtain state compensation in accordance with the law, and to promote the lawful exercise of functions and powers by state organs.

Article 2 Where a state organ or its personnel, in exercising their functions and powers, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization as provided for in this Law, causing damage, the injured party has the right to obtain state compensation in accordance with this Law.

The compensatory obligation organ as provided for in this Law shall fulfill its compensation obligations in a timely manner in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter II: Administrative Compensation

### Section 1: Scope of Compensation

Article 3 Where an administrative organ or its personnel, in exercising administrative functions and powers, commits any of the following acts infringing upon personal rights, the injured party has the right to obtain compensation:

(1) Unlawfully detaining a person or unlawfully taking administrative compulsory measures restricting the personal freedom of a citizen;

(2) Unlawfully imprisoning a person or otherwise unlawfully depriving a citizen of personal freedom;

(3) Using violence such as beating or mistreatment, or instigating or indulging others in such acts, causing bodily injury or death to a citizen;

(4) Unlawfully using weapons or police equipment, causing bodily injury or death to a citizen;

(5) Other unlawful acts causing bodily injury or death to a citizen.

Article 4 Where an administrative organ or its personnel, in exercising administrative functions and powers, commits any of the following acts infringing upon property rights, the injured party has the right to obtain compensation:

(1) Unlawfully imposing fines, revoking permits or licenses, ordering suspension of production or business, confiscating property, or taking other administrative penalties;

(2) Unlawfully taking administrative compulsory measures such as sealing up, seizing, or freezing property;

(3) Unlawfully levying or requisitioning property;

(4) Other unlawful acts causing damage to property.

Article 5 The state shall not bear compensation liability under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Personal acts of administrative organ personnel unrelated to the exercise of their functions and powers;

(2) Damage caused by the acts of the citizen, legal person, or other organization themselves;

(3) Other circumstances as provided for by law.

### Section 2: Claimants for Compensation and Compensatory Obligation Organs

Article 6 The injured citizen, legal person, or other organization has the right to claim compensation.

Where the injured citizen dies, their heirs and other relatives who were supported by them have the right to claim compensation.

Where the injured legal person or other organization is terminated, the successor of its rights has the right to claim compensation.

Article 7 Where an administrative organ or its personnel, in exercising administrative functions and powers, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization, causing damage, that administrative organ shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where two or more administrative organs jointly exercise administrative functions and powers and infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization, causing damage, the administrative organs that jointly exercised the functions and powers shall be the joint compensatory obligation organs.

Where an organization authorized by law or regulation, in exercising the administrative power granted to it, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization, causing damage, the authorized organization shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where an organization or individual entrusted by an administrative organ, in exercising the entrusted administrative power, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization, causing damage, the entrusting administrative organ shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where the compensatory obligation organ is abolished, the administrative organ that continues to exercise its functions and powers shall be the compensatory obligation organ; if there is no administrative organ continuing to exercise its functions and powers, the administrative organ that abolished the compensatory obligation organ shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Article 8 Where a case is reviewed by a reconsideration organ, the administrative organ that initially committed the infringing act shall be the compensatory obligation organ; however, if the reconsideration decision of the reconsideration organ aggravates the damage, the reconsideration organ shall fulfill the compensation obligation for the aggravated part.

### Section 3: Compensation Procedures

Article 9 Where a compensatory obligation organ has any of the circumstances provided for in Article 3 or Article 4 of this Law, it shall grant compensation.

A claimant for compensation shall first submit a claim to the compensatory obligation organ, or may submit it together with an application for administrative reconsideration or an administrative lawsuit.

Article 10 A claimant for compensation may claim compensation from any one of the joint compensatory obligation organs, and that compensatory obligation organ shall first provide compensation.

Article 11 A claimant for compensation may simultaneously make several claims for compensation based on different types of damage suffered.

Article 12 A claim for compensation shall be submitted in writing, and the written application shall specify the following:

(1) The name, gender, age, work unit, and address of the injured party; the name, address, and name and position of the legal representative or principal responsible person of the legal person or other organization;

(2) Specific claims, factual basis, and reasons;

(3) The year, month, and date of the application.

If a claimant for compensation has genuine difficulty in writing the application, they may entrust another person to write it on their behalf, or may make an oral application, which shall be recorded in writing by the compensatory obligation organ.

If the claimant for compensation is not the injured party themselves, they shall explain their relationship with the injured party and provide corresponding evidence.

If the claimant for compensation submits the application in person, the compensatory obligation organ shall immediately issue a written receipt stamped with the special seal of the administrative organ and indicating the date of receipt. If the application materials are incomplete, the compensatory obligation organ shall, on the spot or within five days, inform the claimant for compensation of all the contents that need to be supplemented.

Article 13 The compensatory obligation organ shall, within two months from the date of receiving the application, make a decision on whether to grant compensation. When making a compensation decision, the compensatory obligation organ shall fully listen to the opinions of the claimant for compensation and may negotiate with the claimant regarding the method, items, and amount of compensation in accordance with the provisions of Chapter IV of this Law.

If the compensatory obligation organ decides to grant compensation, it shall prepare a compensation decision document and serve it to the claimant for compensation within ten days from the date of the decision.

If the compensatory obligation organ decides not to grant compensation, it shall notify the claimant for compensation in writing within ten days from the date of the decision and explain the reasons for not granting compensation.

Article 14 If the compensatory obligation organ fails to make a decision on whether to grant compensation within the prescribed time limit, the claimant for compensation may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within three months from the expiry of the time limit.

If the claimant for compensation disagrees with the method, items, or amount of compensation, or if the compensatory obligation organ makes a decision not to grant compensation, the claimant for compensation may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within three months from the date the compensatory obligation organ makes the decision to grant or not grant compensation.

Article 15 When a People's Court hears an administrative compensation case, the claimant for compensation and the compensatory obligation organ shall provide evidence for their respective claims.

If, during the period when the compensatory obligation organ takes administrative detention or compulsory measures restricting personal freedom, the person whose personal freedom is restricted dies or loses capacity for civil conduct, the compensatory obligation organ shall provide evidence on whether there is a causal relationship between its actions and the death or loss of capacity for civil conduct of the person whose personal freedom is restricted.

Article 16 After compensating for losses, the compensatory obligation organ shall order the personnel who acted intentionally or with gross negligence, or the entrusted organization or individual, to bear part or all of the compensation costs.

For responsible persons who acted intentionally or with gross negligence, the relevant organ shall impose sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III: Criminal Compensation

### Section 1: Scope of Compensation

Article 17 Where an organ exercising investigative, prosecutorial, or judicial functions and powers, or a detention center or prison administration organ, or their personnel, in exercising their functions and powers, commits any of the following acts infringing upon personal rights, the injured party has the right to obtain compensation:

(1) Taking detention measures against a citizen in violation of the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law, or taking detention measures against a citizen in accordance with the conditions and procedures stipulated by the Criminal Procedure Law, but the detention period exceeds the time limit stipulated by the Criminal Procedure Law, and subsequently deciding to dismiss the case, not to prosecute, or to acquit the person, thereby terminating the criminal liability;

(2) After taking arrest measures against a citizen, deciding to dismiss the case, not to prosecute, or to acquit the person, thereby terminating the criminal liability;

(3) Reversing a conviction and acquitting the person through retrial in accordance with the trial supervision procedure, where the original sentence has already been executed;

(4) Extorting confessions by torture, or using violence such as beating or mistreatment, or instigating or indulging others in such acts, causing bodily injury or death to a citizen;

(5) Unlawfully using weapons or police equipment, causing bodily injury or death to a citizen.

Article 18 Where an organ exercising investigative, prosecutorial, or judicial functions and powers, or a detention center or prison administration organ, or their personnel, in exercising their functions and powers, commits any of the following acts infringing upon property rights, the injured party has the right to obtain compensation:

(1) Unlawfully taking measures such as sealing up, seizing, freezing, or recovering property;

(2) Reversing a conviction and acquitting the person through retrial in accordance with the trial supervision procedure, where the original fines or confiscation of property have already been executed.

Article 19 The state shall not bear compensation liability under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Where a citizen is detained or sentenced due to their own deliberate false confession or forgery of other incriminating evidence;

(2) Where a person who bears no criminal liability in accordance with Articles 17 and 18 of the Criminal Law is detained;

(3) Where a person who is not subject to criminal liability in accordance with Article 15, Paragraph 2 of Article 173, Paragraph 2 of Article 273, or Article 279 of the Criminal Procedure Law is detained;

(4) Personal acts of personnel of organs exercising investigative, prosecutorial, or judicial functions and powers, or detention centers or prison administration organs, unrelated to the exercise of their functions and powers;

(5) Damage caused by intentional acts of the citizen, such as self-harm or self-mutilation;

(6) Other circumstances as provided for by law.

### Section 2: Claimants for Compensation and Compensatory Obligation Organs

Article 20 The determination of claimants for compensation shall be in accordance with the provisions of Article 6 of this Law.

Article 21 Where an organ exercising investigative, prosecutorial, or judicial functions and powers, or a detention center or prison administration organ, or their personnel, in exercising their functions and powers, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a citizen, legal person, or other organization, causing damage, that organ shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where detention measures are taken against a citizen and state compensation should be granted in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the organ that made the detention decision shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where, after taking arrest measures against a citizen, a decision is made to dismiss the case, not to prosecute, or to acquit the person, the organ that made the arrest decision shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

Where a conviction is reversed and the person is acquitted through retrial, the People's Court that rendered the original effective judgment shall be the compensatory obligation organ. Where a conviction is reversed and the person is acquitted in the second instance, or where the case is remanded for retrial and the person is subsequently found not guilty, the People's Court that rendered the original guilty verdict in the first instance shall be the compensatory obligation organ.

### Section 3: Compensation Procedures

Article 22 Where a compensatory obligation organ has any of the circumstances provided for in Article 17 or Article 18 of this Law, it shall grant compensation.

A claimant for compensation shall first submit a claim to the compensatory obligation organ.

The provisions of Articles 11 and 12 of this Law shall apply to the submission of a compensation claim by a claimant for compensation.

Article 23 The compensatory obligation organ shall, within two months from the date of receiving the application, make a decision on whether to grant compensation. When making a compensation decision, the compensatory obligation organ shall fully listen to the opinions of the claimant for compensation and may negotiate with the claimant regarding the method, items, and amount of compensation in accordance with the provisions of Chapter IV of this Law.

If the compensatory obligation organ decides to grant compensation, it shall prepare a compensation decision document and serve it to the claimant for compensation within ten days from the date of the decision.

If the compensatory obligation organ decides not to grant compensation, it shall notify the claimant for compensation in writing within ten days from the date of the decision and explain the reasons for not granting compensation.

Article 24 If the compensatory obligation organ fails to make a decision on whether to grant compensation within the prescribed time limit, the claimant for compensation may apply for reconsideration to the organ at the next higher level of the compensatory obligation organ within thirty days from the expiry of the time limit.

If the claimant for compensation disagrees with the method, items, or amount of compensation, or if the compensatory obligation organ makes a decision not to grant compensation, the claimant for compensation may apply for reconsideration to the organ at the next higher level of the compensatory obligation organ within thirty days from the date the compensatory obligation organ makes the decision to grant or not grant compensation.

If the compensatory obligation organ is a People's Court, the claimant for compensation may, in accordance with the provisions of this Article, apply to the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the next higher level for a compensation decision.

Article 25 The reconsideration organ shall make a decision within two months from the date of receiving the application.

If the claimant for compensation disagrees with the reconsideration decision, they may apply to the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the same level as the reconsideration organ for a compensation decision within thirty days from the date of receiving the reconsideration decision; if the reconsideration organ fails to make a decision within the time limit, the claimant for compensation may apply to the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the same level as the reconsideration organ for a compensation decision within thirty days from the expiry of the time limit.

Article 26 When the Compensation Committee of a People's Court handles a compensation claim, the claimant for compensation and the compensatory obligation organ shall provide evidence for their respective claims.

If a detained person dies or loses capacity for civil conduct during the period of detention, the compensatory obligation organ shall provide evidence on whether there is a causal relationship between its actions and the death or loss of capacity for civil conduct of the detained person.

Article 27 When the Compensation Committee of a People's Court handles a compensation claim, it shall adopt the method of documentary review. When necessary, it may investigate the circumstances and collect evidence from relevant units and individuals. If there is a dispute between the claimant for compensation and the compensatory obligation organ regarding the facts of damage and the causal relationship, the Compensation Committee may hear the statements and arguments of the claimant for compensation and the compensatory obligation organ, and may conduct cross-examination.

Article 28 The Compensation Committee of a People's Court shall make a decision within three months from the date of receiving the compensation application; for cases that are difficult, complex, or of major importance, the period may be extended by three months with the approval of the president of the court.

Article 29 A Compensation Committee shall be established in Intermediate People's Courts and above, composed of three or more judges of the People's Court, and the number of members shall be an odd number.

The Compensation Committee shall make compensation decisions in accordance with the principle of majority rule.

Compensation decisions made by the Compensation Committee are legally effective and must be executed.

Article 30 If a claimant for compensation or a compensatory obligation organ believes that a decision made by the Compensation Committee is indeed erroneous, they may file a complaint with the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the next higher level.

After a compensation decision made by the Compensation Committee takes effect, if it is found that the compensation decision violates the provisions of this Law, the Compensation Committee shall, upon the decision of the president of the court or upon the instruction of a higher People's Court, re-examine the case within two months and make a decision in accordance with the law; the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the next higher level may also directly review and make a decision.

If the Supreme People's Procuratorate finds that a decision made by the Compensation Committee of a People's Court at any level, or if a higher People's Procuratorate finds that a decision made by the Compensation Committee of a lower People's Court, violates the provisions of this Law, it shall submit its opinion to the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the same level, and the Compensation Committee of the People's Court at the same level shall re-examine the case within two months and make a decision in accordance with the law.

Article 31 After compensating for losses, the compensatory obligation organ shall recover part or all of the compensation costs from personnel who fall under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Those who have the circumstances provided for in Items (4) and (5) of Article 17 of this Law;

(2) Those who have committed acts of embezzlement, bribery, favoritism, or perverting the law in handling cases.

For responsible persons who have the circumstances provided for in the preceding paragraph, the relevant organ shall impose sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IV: Methods of Compensation and Calculation Standards

Article 32 State compensation shall be mainly in the form of monetary compensation.

If property can be returned or restored to its original state, it shall be returned or restored.

Article 33 For infringement upon the personal freedom of a citizen, the daily compensation amount shall be calculated based on the average daily wage of employees in the previous year in the state.

Article 34 For infringement upon the right to life and health of a citizen, the compensation amount shall be calculated in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For causing bodily injury, medical expenses, nursing expenses, and compensation for reduced income due to missed work shall be paid. The daily compensation amount for reduced income shall be calculated based on the average daily wage of employees in the previous year in the state, with the maximum amount not exceeding five times the average annual wage of employees in the previous year in the state;

(2) For causing partial or total loss of labor capacity, medical expenses, nursing expenses, expenses for assistive devices for disability, rehabilitation expenses, and other necessary expenses incurred due to the disability and required for continued treatment, as well as disability compensation, shall be paid. Disability compensation shall be determined based on the degree of loss of labor capacity in accordance with the disability grade stipulated by the state, with the maximum amount not exceeding twenty times the average annual wage of employees in the previous year in the state. For total loss of labor capacity, living expenses shall also be paid to the persons without labor capacity who were supported by the injured party;

(3) For causing death, death compensation and funeral expenses shall be paid, with the total amount being twenty times the average annual wage of employees in the previous year in the state. Living expenses shall also be paid to the persons without labor capacity who were supported by the deceased before their death.

The standard for paying living expenses provided for in Items (2) and (3) of the preceding paragraph shall be determined with reference to the local minimum subsistence guarantee standard. If the supported person is a minor, living expenses shall be paid until they reach the age of eighteen; for other persons without labor capacity, living expenses shall be paid until their death.

Article 35 Where any of the circumstances provided for in Article 3 or Article 17 of this Law causes mental damage, the infringing organ shall, within the scope of the impact of the infringing act, eliminate the adverse effects, restore the reputation, and make an apology to the injured party; if serious consequences are caused, corresponding mental distress damages shall be paid.

Article 36 For infringement upon the property rights of a citizen, legal person, or other organization causing damage, the following provisions shall apply:

(1) For fines, penalties, recoveries, confiscation of property, or unlawful levies or requisitions of property, the property shall be returned;

(2) For property that has been sealed up, seized, or frozen, the sealing up, seizure, or freezing shall be lifted; if the property is damaged or lost, compensation shall be paid in accordance with Items (3) and (4) of this Article;

(3) If property that should be returned is damaged, it shall be restored to its original state if possible; if it cannot be restored, corresponding compensation shall be paid based on the degree of damage;

(4) If property that should be returned is lost, corresponding compensation shall be paid;

(5) If the property has been auctioned or sold, the proceeds from the auction or sale shall be paid; if the proceeds from the sale are significantly lower than the value of the property, corresponding compensation shall be paid;

(6) For revocation of permits or licenses, or orders for suspension of production or business, necessary recurring expenses during the period of suspension of production or business shall be compensated;(7) For the return of fines or penalties that have been executed, or the return of money that has been recovered or confiscated, or the release of frozen deposits or remittances, interest at the bank deposit rate for the same period shall be paid;

(8) For other damages to property rights, compensation shall be given according to direct losses.

Article 37 Compensation expenses shall be included in the fiscal budgets at all levels.

A claimant for compensation shall apply to the compensation obligation organ for payment of compensation based on an effective judgment, reconsideration decision, compensation decision, or mediation document.

The compensation obligation organ shall, within seven days from the date of receiving the application for payment of compensation, submit a payment application to the relevant financial department in accordance with budget management authority, and the financial department shall pay the compensation within fifteen days from the date of receiving the payment application.

Specific measures for the budgeting and payment management of compensation expenses shall be prescribed by the State Council.

## Chapter V Other Provisions

Article 38 If, during civil litigation or administrative litigation, a people's court unlawfully adopts compulsory measures or preservation measures to hinder litigation, or makes errors in executing judgments, rulings, or other effective legal documents, causing damage, the procedure for the claimant for compensation to request compensation shall apply the provisions of the criminal compensation procedure of this Law.

Article 39 The statute of limitations for a claimant for compensation to request state compensation is two years, calculated from the day when the claimant knows or should know that the actions of state organs and their staff in exercising their functions and powers have infringed upon their personal or property rights, but the period during which the claimant is in custody or otherwise restricted in personal freedom shall not be counted. If a request for compensation is made together with an application for administrative reconsideration or the filing of an administrative lawsuit, the provisions of the Administrative Reconsideration Law and the Administrative Procedure Law regarding the statute of limitations shall apply.

If, within the last six months of the statute of limitations for requesting compensation, the claimant is unable to exercise the right to claim due to force majeure or other obstacles, the statute of limitations shall be suspended. From the day when the cause of the suspension is eliminated, the statute of limitations for requesting compensation shall continue to run.

Article 40 This Law shall apply to foreigners, foreign enterprises, and foreign organizations that request state compensation from the People's Republic of China within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

If the country to which a foreigner, foreign enterprise, or foreign organization belongs does not protect or restricts the right of Chinese citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to request compensation from that country, the People's Republic of China shall apply the principle of reciprocity with the country to which the foreigner, foreign enterprise, or foreign organization belongs.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 41 When a claimant for compensation requests state compensation, the compensation obligation organ, the reconsideration organ, and the people's court shall not charge any fees to the claimant for compensation.

No tax shall be levied on the compensation obtained by the claimant for compensation.

Article 42 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1995.
