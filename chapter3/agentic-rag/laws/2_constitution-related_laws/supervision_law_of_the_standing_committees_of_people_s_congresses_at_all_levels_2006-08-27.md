# Supervision Law of the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses at All Levels of the People's Republic of China

Supervision Law of the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses at All Levels of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 27, 2006

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to ensure that the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the standing committees of the local people's congresses at and above the county level exercise their supervisory powers according to law, develop socialist democracy, and advance the rule of law.

Article 2 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall exercise their supervisory powers in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution and relevant laws.

The procedures for the standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels to exercise their supervisory powers shall be governed by this Law; matters not covered by this Law shall be governed by the provisions of relevant laws.

Article 3 In exercising their supervisory powers, the standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall focus on the overall work of the state, take economic development as the central task, uphold the leadership of the Communist Party of China, adhere to Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory and the important thought of the "Three Represents," uphold the people's democratic dictatorship, adhere to the socialist path, and persist in reform and opening up.

Article 4 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall exercise their supervisory powers collectively, in accordance with the principle of democratic centralism.

Article 5 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall supervise the work of the people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at the corresponding level, promoting law-based administration and impartial justice.

Article 6 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall report to the people's congress at the corresponding level on the exercise of their supervisory powers and accept its supervision.

Article 7 The exercise of supervisory powers by the standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall be made public.

## Chapter II Hearing and Deliberating Special Work Reports of the People's Governments, People's Courts, and People's Procuratorates

Article 8 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall, each year, select a number of major issues concerning the overall situation of reform, development, and stability, as well as the immediate interests of the people and issues of general public concern, and plan to hear and deliberate special work reports of the people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at the corresponding level.

The annual plan for the standing committee to hear and deliberate special work reports shall be adopted by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons, distributed to the members of the standing committee, and made public.

Article 9 The topics for the standing committee to hear and deliberate special work reports of the people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at the corresponding level shall be determined based on issues identified through the following channels:

(1) Prominent problems discovered by the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level during law enforcement inspections;
(2) Issues reflected in concentrated suggestions, criticisms, and opinions raised by deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level regarding the work of the people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates;
(3) Relatively concentrated issues raised by members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level;
(4) Prominent problems discovered by the special committees of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the working bodies of the standing committee during investigations and research;
(5) Issues reflected in concentrated petitions and visits from the public;
(6) Other issues of general public concern.

The people's governments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates may request the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level to hear their special work reports.

Article 10 Before the standing committee hears and deliberates a special work report, the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons may organize members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level to conduct inspections or special investigations and research on the relevant work.

The standing committee may arrange for deputies who participated in the inspections or special investigations and research to attend the meetings of the standing committee, hear the special work report, and offer opinions.

Article 11 Before the standing committee hears and deliberates a special work report, the working body of the standing committee shall compile opinions from various quarters on the work in question and submit them to the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate at the corresponding level for study and response in the special work report.

Article 12 The people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate shall, twenty days before the meeting of the standing committee, submit the special work report through its working body to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the relevant working body of the standing committee for comments; after revising the report based on the comments, it shall submit the report to the standing committee ten days before the meeting of the standing committee.

The working body of the standing committee shall distribute the special work report to the members of the standing committee seven days before the meeting of the standing committee.

Article 13 The special work report shall be made to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level by the responsible person of the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate; the people's government may also entrust the responsible person of the relevant department to make the report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 14 The deliberation opinions of the members of the standing committee on the special work report shall be submitted to the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate at the corresponding level for study and handling. The people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate shall, after submitting the study and handling situation through its working body to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the relevant working body of the standing committee for comments, submit a written report to the standing committee. If the standing committee deems it necessary, it may adopt a resolution on the special work report; the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate at the corresponding level shall, within the time limit specified in the resolution, report to the standing committee on the implementation of the resolution.

The special work report heard by the standing committee and the deliberation opinions, as well as the report of the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate on the study and handling of the deliberation opinions or the implementation of the resolution, shall be notified to the deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level and made public.

## Chapter III Examining and Approving Final Accounts, Hearing and Deliberating Reports on the Implementation of the National Economic and Social Development Plan and Budget, and Hearing and Deliberating Audit Work Reports

Article 15 The State Council shall, in June of each year, submit the draft central final accounts for the previous year to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for examination and approval.

Local people's governments at and above the county level shall, between June and September of each year, submit the draft final accounts for the previous year at the corresponding level to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for examination and approval.

The draft final accounts shall be prepared in accordance with the budget categories approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level, listing the budgeted figures, adjusted or changed figures, and actual implementation figures separately, with explanations.

Article 16 The State Council and local people's governments at and above the county level shall, between June and September of each year, report to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level on the implementation of the national economic and social development plan and budget for the preceding period of the current year.

Article 17 After the national economic and social development plan and budget are approved by the people's congress, if partial adjustments are needed during implementation, the State Council and local people's governments at and above the county level shall submit the adjustment plan to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for examination and approval.

Strict control shall be exercised over the transfer of funds between different budget categories. If funds allocated for agriculture, education, science and technology, culture, health, social security, etc., in the budget need to be reduced, the State Council and local people's governments at and above the county level shall submit the matter to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for examination and approval.

The relevant competent departments of the State Council and local people's governments at and above the county level shall, one month before the meeting of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level to examine and approve the budget adjustment plan, submit the preliminary budget adjustment plan to the Financial and Economic Committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary examination, or to the relevant working body of the standing committee for comments.

Article 18 When examining the draft final accounts and the report on budget implementation, the standing committee shall focus on the following:

(1) The balance of budget revenues and expenditures;
(2) The arrangement of key expenditures and the availability of funds;
(3) The arrangement and use of excess budget revenues;
(4) The establishment and implementation of the departmental budget system;
(5) Transfer payments to lower-level governments;
(6) The implementation of the resolution of the people's congress at the corresponding level on approving the budget.

In addition to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress shall also focus on examining the balance of national debt; the standing committees of local people's congresses at and above the county level shall also focus on examining the arrangement and use of financial subsidy funds from higher-level governments.

Article 19 While examining and approving the final accounts each year, the standing committee shall also hear and deliberate the audit work report submitted by the people's government at the corresponding level on the implementation of the budget and other fiscal revenues and expenditures for the previous year, as proposed by the audit authority.

Article 20 The deliberation opinions of the members of the standing committee on the report on the implementation of the national economic and social development plan, the report on budget implementation, and the audit work report shall be submitted to the people's government at the corresponding level for study and handling. The people's government shall submit a written report to the standing committee on the study and handling situation. If the standing committee deems it necessary, it may adopt a resolution on the audit work report; the people's government at the corresponding level shall, within the time limit specified in the resolution, report to the standing committee on the implementation of the resolution.

The reports on the implementation of the national economic and social development plan, budget implementation, and audit work heard by the standing committee, along with the deliberation opinions, and the report of the people's government on the study and handling of the deliberation opinions or the implementation of the resolution, shall be notified to the deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level and made public.

Article 21 After the five-year plan for national economic and social development is approved by the people's congress, during the mid-term stage of its implementation, the people's government shall submit a mid-term evaluation report on the implementation of the plan to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for deliberation. If the plan needs adjustment based on the mid-term evaluation, the people's government shall submit the adjustment plan to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for examination and approval.

## Chapter IV Inspection of the Implementation of Laws and Regulations

Article 22 The standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels shall, with reference to the channels specified in Article 9 of this Law, select a number of major issues concerning the overall situation of reform, development, and stability, as well as the immediate interests of the people and issues of general public concern each year, and plan to organize law enforcement inspections on the implementation of relevant laws and regulations.

Article 23 The annual law enforcement inspection plan of the standing committee shall be adopted by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons, distributed to the members of the standing committee, and made public.

The law enforcement inspection work of the standing committee shall be specifically organized and implemented by the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the relevant working body of the standing committee.

Article 24 The standing committee shall, in accordance with the annual law enforcement inspection plan and based on the principles of compactness and efficiency, organize a law enforcement inspection group.

The members of the law enforcement inspection group shall be determined from among the members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and the members of the relevant special committees of the people's congress at the corresponding level, and deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level may be invited to participate.

Article 25 The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, as needed, entrust the standing committees of the people's congresses at the next lower level to inspect the implementation of relevant laws and regulations within their respective administrative regions. The entrusted standing committee of the people's congress shall submit a written report on the inspection to the standing committee of the people's congress at the next higher level.

Article 26 After the law enforcement inspection is completed, the law enforcement inspection group shall promptly submit a law enforcement inspection report, which shall be decided by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons to be submitted to the standing committee for deliberation.

The law enforcement inspection report shall include the following:

(1) An evaluation of the implementation of the inspected laws and regulations, identifying problems in law enforcement and proposing suggestions for improvement;
(2) Suggestions for amending and improving the relevant laws and regulations.

Article 27 The deliberation opinions of the members of the standing committee on the law enforcement inspection report, together with the law enforcement inspection report, shall be submitted to the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate at the corresponding level for study and handling. The people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate shall, after submitting the study and handling situation through its working body to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the relevant working body of the standing committee for comments, submit a report to the standing committee. If necessary, the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons shall decide to submit it to the standing committee for deliberation, or the standing committee shall organize follow-up inspections; the standing committee may also entrust the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level or the relevant working body of the standing committee to organize follow-up inspections.

The law enforcement inspection report and deliberation opinions of the standing committee, as well as the report of the people's government, people's court, or people's procuratorate on their study and handling, shall be notified to the deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level and made public.

## Chapter VI Inquiries and Interpellations

Article 34 When the standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels deliberate bills and relevant reports, the people's government or relevant departments, people's courts, or people's procuratorates at the corresponding level shall send responsible persons to attend the meetings, listen to opinions, and answer inquiries.

Article 35 Ten or more members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress may jointly submit, five or more members of the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, autonomous prefectures, and cities divided into districts may jointly submit, and three or more members of the standing committees of the people's congresses at the county level may jointly submit, a written interpellation to the standing committee against the people's government and its departments, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at the corresponding level.

The interpellation shall clearly state the object of the interpellation, the questions raised, and their content.

Article 36 The interpellation shall be decided by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons to be answered by the organ subjected to the interpellation.

The Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons may decide that the organ subjected to the interpellation shall answer orally at a meeting of the standing committee or a meeting of the relevant special committee, or answer in writing. If the answer is given at a meeting of a special committee, the members of the standing committee who raised the interpellation have the right to attend the meeting and express opinions. If the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons deems it necessary, it may distribute the report on the answer to the interpellation to the meeting of the standing committee.

Article 37 If a majority of the members of the standing committee who raised the interpellation are dissatisfied with the answer given by the organ subjected to the interpellation, they may make a request, and upon decision by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons, the organ subjected to the interpellation shall answer again.

Article 38 If the interpellation is answered orally, the responsible person of the organ subjected to the interpellation shall attend the meeting to answer. If the interpellation is answered in writing, the answer shall be signed by the responsible person of the organ subjected to the interpellation.

## Chapter VII Investigation into Specific Issues

Article 39 If the standing committees of the people's congresses at all levels need to make a resolution or decision on a matter within their terms of reference but the relevant major facts are unclear, they may organize a committee of inquiry into specific issues.

Article 40 The Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons may propose to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level to organize a committee of inquiry into specific issues, and submit it to the standing committee for deliberation.

One-fifth or more of the members of the standing committee may jointly propose in writing to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level to organize a committee of inquiry into specific issues; the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons shall decide whether to submit it to the standing committee for deliberation, or first refer it to the relevant special committee for deliberation and a report, and then decide whether to submit it to the standing committee for deliberation.

Article 41 A committee of inquiry shall be composed of a chairperson, vice-chairpersons, and members, nominated by the Council of Chairpersons or the Meeting of Chairpersons from among the members of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level and deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level, and submitted to the standing committee for deliberation and approval. The committee of inquiry may engage relevant experts to participate in the investigation.

Members of the standing committee and other persons who have an interest in the matter under investigation shall not serve on the committee of inquiry.

Article 42 When a committee of inquiry conducts an investigation, the relevant state organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and citizens have the obligation to provide necessary materials.If a citizen who provides materials requests that the source of the materials be kept confidential, the investigation committee shall maintain such confidentiality.

During the investigation process, the investigation committee may choose not to disclose the investigation details and materials.

Article 43 The investigation committee shall submit an investigation report to the standing committee that established it. Based on the report, the standing committee may adopt corresponding resolutions or decisions.

# Chapter VIII: Consideration and Decision on Removal Motions

Article 44 During the period when the people's congress is not in session, the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level may decide to remove individual deputy governors, deputy chairpersons of autonomous regions, deputy mayors, deputy prefectural heads, deputy county heads, or deputy district heads from the people's government at the same level; it may also remove other members of the people's government appointed by it, as well as vice presidents, chief judges, deputy chief judges, members of the judicial committee, and judges of the people's court, deputy chief procurators, members of the procuratorial committee, and procurators of the people's procuratorate, the president of the intermediate people's court, and the chief procurator of the branch of the people's procuratorate.

Article 45 The people's government, people's court, and people's procuratorate at or above the county level may submit a removal motion to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level regarding the state functionaries specified in Article 44 of this Law.

The council of chairpersons of the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level may submit a removal motion to the standing committee regarding the state functionaries specified in Article 44 of this Law.

If one-fifth or more of the members of the standing committee of a local people's congress at or above the county level jointly submit a written removal motion regarding the state functionaries specified in Article 44 of this Law, the council of chairpersons shall decide whether to submit the motion to the standing committee for deliberation; alternatively, upon the proposal of the council of chairpersons and a decision by the plenary session, an investigation committee may be established, and the subsequent standing committee meeting shall deliberate and decide based on the investigation committee's report.

Article 46 A removal motion shall clearly state the target and reasons for the removal and provide relevant materials.

Before a removal motion is put to a vote, the person subject to the removal has the right to present a defense at the standing committee meeting or submit a written defense, which shall be distributed to the standing committee meeting as decided by the council of chairpersons.

The vote on a removal motion shall be conducted by secret ballot and shall be passed by a majority of all members of the standing committee.

# Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

Article 47 The standing committee of the people's congress of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with this Law and relevant laws, and considering local actual conditions, formulate implementation measures.

Article 48 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2007.
