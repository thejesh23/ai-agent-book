# Land Border Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 23, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate and strengthen land border work, ensure the security and stability of the land border and frontier areas, promote good-neighborly friendship, exchanges and cooperation with neighboring land countries, and safeguard national sovereignty, security and territorial integrity.

**Article 2** This Law applies to the delimitation and demarcation of the land border of the People's Republic of China, the defense, management and construction of the land border and frontier areas, and international cooperation on land border affairs.

**Article 3** Land border refers to the boundary that divides the land territory and internal waters of the People's Republic of China from those of its neighboring land countries. The land border vertically divides the airspace and subsoil of the People's Republic of China from those of its neighboring land countries.

The area within a certain range on the inner side of the land border of the People's Republic of China constitutes the frontier area.

**Article 4** The sovereignty and territorial integrity of the People's Republic of China are sacred and inviolable.

The State takes effective measures to resolutely safeguard territorial sovereignty and the security of the land border, and to prevent and combat any act that damages territorial sovereignty or undermines the land border.

**Article 5** The State exercises unified leadership over land border work.

**Article 6** The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is responsible for foreign affairs related to the land border, participates in work related to land border management, takes the lead in conducting foreign negotiations, concluding and implementing treaties, and engaging in international cooperation, handles issues that need to be resolved through diplomatic channels, and organizes the maintenance and management of the border line and boundary markers.

The public security department under the State Council is responsible for public security work in frontier areas, guides and supervises frontier public security organs in strengthening social security management, and prevents and combats illegal and criminal activities in the frontier areas.

The General Administration of Customs is responsible for the supervision and management of entry and exit at border ports, etc., and organizes the implementation of customs supervision and quarantine of means of transport, goods, articles and persons entering or leaving the country in accordance with the law.

The national immigration administration department is responsible for immigration management in frontier areas, and organizes the implementation of border entry-exit inspection and control, management of border residents' movement, and border management in frontier areas in accordance with the law.

Other relevant departments under the State Council shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, exercise their powers and carry out relevant work in accordance with the law.

**Article 7** Under the leadership of the Central Military Commission, relevant military organs organize, guide and coordinate the defense and control of the land border and frontier areas, the maintenance of social stability, the handling of emergencies, border cooperation and related work.

The Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall, in accordance with their respective tasks and division of responsibilities, guard and defend the land border, resist armed invasion, handle major emergencies and terrorist activities on the land border and in frontier areas, jointly with or assist relevant local departments in preventing, stopping and combating illegal border crossing, and safeguard the security and stability of the land border and frontier areas.

**Article 8** The people's congresses at various levels and their standing committees in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall, within their respective administrative areas, ensure the observance and implementation of laws and regulations concerning the land border and frontier areas.

The people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall, in accordance with laws and regulations, manage the work related to the land border and frontier areas within their respective administrative areas.

**Article 9** Relevant military and local departments and units shall, relying on relevant overall coordination mechanisms, jointly promote the strengthening of border defenses, organize and carry out border defense and control, construction, maintenance and management of border defense infrastructure, etc., and jointly maintain the security, stability and normal order of the land border and frontier areas.

**Article 10** The State takes effective measures to strengthen border defense construction, support the economic and social development and opening up of frontier areas, promote actions to consolidate the border, revitalize the border and enrich the people, improve the level of public services and infrastructure construction in frontier areas, improve production and living conditions in frontier areas, encourage and support border residents to live and work in frontier areas, and promote the coordinated development of border defense construction and the frontier economy and society.

**Article 11** The State strengthens publicity and education on the land border, forges a strong sense of community for the Chinese nation, promotes the spirit of the Chinese nation in defending the unity of the country and territorial integrity, enhances citizens' national consciousness and awareness of territorial security, and builds a shared spiritual home for the Chinese nation.

People's governments at various levels and relevant educational and scientific research institutions shall strengthen the collection, protection and research of historical materials related to the land border and frontier areas.

Citizens and organizations that discover historical materials, relics and objects related to the land border and frontier areas shall promptly report or hand them over to relevant state departments in accordance with the law.

**Article 12** The State guarantees the funding for land border work.

The State Council and the people's governments at or above the county level in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall incorporate work related to the land border and frontier areas into the national economic and social development plan.

**Article 13** Citizens and organizations shall safeguard the security and stability of the land border and frontier areas, protect boundary markers and border defense infrastructure, and cooperate with and assist in carrying out work related to the land border.

The State encourages and supports citizens and organizations that cooperate with and assist in carrying out work related to the land border, and commends and rewards citizens and organizations that have made outstanding contributions in accordance with relevant regulations.

**Article 14** The People's Republic of China abides by treaties concluded with or jointly acceded to by foreign countries concerning land border affairs.

**Article 15** The State adheres to the principles of equality, mutual trust, and friendly consultation, handles land border and related affairs through negotiations with neighboring land countries, and properly resolves disputes and historical boundary issues.

## Chapter II Delimitation and Demarcation of the Land Border

**Article 16** The State concludes treaties on the delimitation of the land border through negotiations with neighboring land countries, stipulating the alignment and location of the land border.

Treaties on the delimitation of the land border shall, in accordance with legal provisions, be submitted by the State Council to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for decision on ratification, and shall be ratified by the President of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

**Article 17** The State, based on the delimitation treaty, demarcates the land border on the ground with neighboring land countries and concludes a demarcation treaty.

The demarcation treaty shall be approved by the State Council in accordance with legal provisions.

**Article 18** In order to maintain the clarity and stability of the border line, the State conducts joint inspections of the land border with relevant neighboring land countries and concludes joint inspection treaties.

**Article 19** When significant changes occur in the natural geographical environment on which the demarcation of the land border is based and which cannot be restored to their original state, the State may negotiate with neighboring land countries to re-demarcate the land border.

**Article 20** The State sets up boundary markers to indicate the land border on the ground.

The location, type, specification, material and method of installation of boundary markers shall be determined through consultation between the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the relevant departments of neighboring land countries.

**Article 21** The specific work of delimitation, demarcation, joint inspection and setting up of boundary markers of the land border shall be organized and implemented by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council, relevant military organs and relevant border provinces and autonomous regions in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III Defense of the Land Border and Frontier Areas

**Article 22** The Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall conduct border defense duty and control, organize training, exercises and reconnaissance activities in frontier areas, resolutely prevent, stop and combat acts of invasion, encroachment, infiltration, provocation, etc., guard the land border, and maintain border security and stability.

**Article 23** The people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall coordinate the allocation of resources, strengthen the building of mass defense teams to maintain national border security, and support and cooperate with border defense duty and control work.

When constructing infrastructure, the people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall take into account the needs of land border and frontier area defense.

Citizens and organizations shall support border defense duty and control activities and provide convenient conditions or other assistance for them.

**Article 24** The State may, based on the needs of border defense and control, designate specific areas near the land border as border restricted zones and set up warning signs, prohibiting unauthorized persons from entering.

The designation of border restricted zones shall take into account economic and social development, natural resources and ecological environment protection, and the production and living of residents. The plan shall be proposed by the relevant military organs jointly with the people's governments of the border provinces and autonomous regions, and after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments under the State Council, shall be submitted to the State Council and the Central Military Commission for approval.

The modification or cancellation of border restricted zones shall follow the procedures stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

**Article 25** The State may, based on the needs of land border and frontier area defense, construct border defense infrastructure such as barriers, transportation, communication, monitoring, warning, defense and auxiliary facilities on the inner side of the land border, or may, after consultation with neighboring land countries, construct barrier facilities on the land border line.

The construction and maintenance of border defense infrastructure shall, while ensuring national sovereignty, security and territorial integrity, take into account the needs of economic and social development, natural resources and ecological environment protection, wildlife migration, and the production and living of residents, and shall not impair the effective control of the territory between the land border and the border defense infrastructure.

The construction plan for border defense infrastructure shall be approved by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Management of the Land Border and Frontier Areas

### Section 1 General Provisions

**Article 26** The State implements overall coordination, division of responsibilities, and management in accordance with the law for the management and related construction of the land border and frontier areas.

**Article 27** The management of the land border and frontier areas shall ensure the clarity, security and stability of the land border.

**Article 28** The establishment of cross-border cooperation zones for economy, trade, tourism, etc., or the conduct of cross-border cooperation activities in frontier areas in accordance with this Law shall comply with border defense and control requirements and shall not endanger border defense security.

**Article 29** When important situations and major issues arise in the management of the land border and frontier areas, the relevant local people's governments and military organs shall immediately report to higher authorities in accordance with regulations.

**Article 30** Border provinces and autonomous regions may, based on the specific circumstances and actual needs of their respective administrative areas, formulate local regulations and local government rules to stipulate issues in the implementation of land border and frontier area management.

**Article 31** The State may conclude treaties with relevant neighboring land countries to stipulate the management system for the land border and frontier areas. If the treaty has other provisions on the management system for the land border and frontier areas, those provisions shall prevail.

### Section 2 Management of the Land Border

**Article 32** Boundary markers and border defense infrastructure are protected by law.

No organization or individual may move or damage boundary markers or border defense infrastructure without authorization.

If boundary markers are moved, damaged or lost, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs shall organize their restoration, repair or reconstruction after consultation with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries.

**Article 33** In order to keep the land border clearly visible, the State may open and clear sight lines on the inner side of the land border.

**Article 34** The relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall take measures to maintain the stability of the course of boundary rivers (rivers, lakes), and protect and rationally utilize boundary waters in accordance with relevant treaties.

Vessels and personnel that need to enter boundary rivers (rivers, lakes) for activities shall obtain approval or file a record with the relevant competent authorities, report to the public security organs, and submit to inspection.

**Article 35** The State may, after consultation with neighboring land countries, establish border ports at suitable locations near the land border.

The establishment, closure, management, etc., of border ports shall be determined in accordance with laws, regulations and relevant treaties, and the neighboring land countries shall be notified through diplomatic channels.

**Article 36** The relevant departments under the State Council may, after consultation with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries or based on the needs of facilitating the movement of border residents and maintaining border security and stability, establish border resident passages and manage them in a standardized manner.

**Article 37** Persons, means of transport, goods, articles, etc., shall enter or leave the country across the land border in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, and shall submit to inspection, quarantine and supervision by the relevant competent authorities.

Under special circumstances, after concluding a treaty or through diplomatic channels or consultation between competent authorities, the relevant persons, means of transport, goods, articles, etc., shall enter or leave the country from locations approved by the State Council, the Central Military Commission or their authorized departments.

**Article 38** Unauthorized border crossing by any individual is prohibited.

After an unauthorized border crosser is controlled, they shall be dealt with by the public security organs or other competent authorities; if the unauthorized border crosser is a member of the armed forces, they shall be dealt with by the relevant military organs.

If an unauthorized border crosser commits assault, resists arrest or commits other violent acts, endangering the personal safety and property of others, law enforcement and duty personnel may use police equipment and weapons in accordance with the law.

**Article 39** Aircraft flying across the land border shall obtain approval from the relevant competent authorities and comply with the laws and regulations of our country. If an aircraft flies across the land border without approval, the relevant competent authorities shall take necessary measures to deal with it.

No organization or individual may operate unmanned aircraft for flight near the land border without the approval of the relevant competent authorities. The flight activities of model aircraft, delta wings, unmanned free balloons, etc., shall be managed with reference to the management of unmanned aircraft.

**Article 40** No organization or individual may construct permanent buildings near the land border without the approval of the relevant competent authorities.

**Article 41** No organization or individual may engage in activities that endanger national security or affect the friendly relations between our country and neighboring countries near the land border through sound, light, displaying signs, throwing or passing objects, placing drifting objects or airborne objects, etc.

Suspicious items such as drifting objects or airborne objects salvaged or picked up by individuals on or near the land border shall be promptly handed over to the local people's government or relevant military organs, and shall not be disposed of without authorization.

### Section 3 Frontier Area Management

**Article 42** The State may designate frontier management areas based on the needs of border defense management. Special management measures shall be implemented for the passage and residence of persons in frontier management areas in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The designation, modification and cancellation of frontier management areas shall be proposed by the people's governments of the border provinces and autonomous regions, and after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments under the State Council and relevant military organs, shall be submitted to the State Council for approval and public announcement.

**Article 43** The State supports the construction of border towns, improves the system of border towns, enhances the functions of border towns, and strengthens support capacity building.

**Article 44** The relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments of the border provinces and autonomous regions may, in accordance with relevant regulations, construct or approve the establishment of areas such as border resident mutual trade zones (points) and border economic cooperation zones.

**Article 45** The relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall take measures to protect the border ecological environment, prevent ecological damage, and prevent and control air, water, soil and other pollution.

**Article 46** The relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments at various levels in the border provinces and autonomous regions shall take measures to prevent the introduction of infectious diseases, animal and plant epidemics, alien species invasion, and floods, fires, etc., from across the land border or their spread and proliferation in the frontier areas.

**Article 47** The State may seal off and control the border, close ports, and take other emergency measures in accordance with relevant laws and regulations under any of the following circumstances:

(1) War or armed conflict occurs in the surrounding areas that may affect national border security and stability;
(2) A major event occurs that seriously threatens national security or the personal safety and property of border residents;
(3) The frontier area is seriously threatened by natural disasters, accidents, public health incidents, or nuclear, biological or chemical contamination;
(4) Other circumstances that seriously affect the security and stability of the land border and frontier areas.

The measures stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be organized and implemented by the relevant departments under the State Council, relevant military organs and local people's governments in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Chapter V International Cooperation on Land Border Affairs

**Article 48** The State conducts international cooperation with neighboring land countries in accordance with the principle of equality and mutual benefit, handles land border affairs, promotes security cooperation, and deepens mutual benefit and win-win outcomes.

**Article 49** The State may, through consultation with relevant neighboring land countries, establish a joint boundary commission mechanism to guide and coordinate relevant international cooperation, implement relevant treaties, and consult and handle important matters related to land border management.

**Article 50** Relevant military organs may establish border cooperation mechanisms with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries to communicate and consult on major matters and issues in border exchanges and cooperation, establish border talks and meeting mechanisms with relevant border defense agencies of neighboring land countries to negotiate and handle relevant border defense affairs, consolidate and develop good-neighborly and friendly relations, and jointly maintain the security and stability of the land border.

**Article 51** The State may, through consultation with relevant neighboring land countries, establish a boundary (border defense) representative mechanism in the corresponding border sections, composed of representatives, deputy representatives and relevant staff, to handle issues such as border incidents and daily disputes through talks, meetings and joint investigations.

The specific work of the boundary (border defense) representatives shall be organized and implemented by the Chinese People's Liberation Army jointly with relevant departments such as foreign affairs, public security, and immigration under the guidance of the relevant departments under the State Council and the relevant departments of the Central Military Commission.

**Article 52** The public security, customs, immigration and other departments may establish cooperation mechanisms with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries to exchange information, conduct law enforcement cooperation, and jointly prevent and combat cross-border illegal and criminal activities.

**Article 53** The relevant competent authorities of the State and relevant military organs may cooperate with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries to jointly combat terrorism, separatism and extremism.

**Article 54** The State improves the level of facilitation for opening up along the border, optimizes the business environment in frontier areas; after consultation with neighboring land countries, it may establish areas such as cross-border economic cooperation zones, cross-border tourism cooperation zones, and ecological protection zones in the contiguous areas of both sides.

The State jointly constructs and maintains cross-border facilities with neighboring land countries, and may establish temporary enclosed construction zones on the inner side of the land border.

**Article 55** The people's governments at or above the county level in the border provinces and autonomous regions may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, conduct cooperation with local governments of corresponding administrative areas of neighboring land countries in fields such as economy, tourism, culture, sports, emergency rescue and disaster relief, and ecological environment.

**Article 56** The relevant departments under the State Council may cooperate with the relevant departments of neighboring land countries in fields such as port construction and management, natural resource utilization, ecological environment protection, epidemic prevention and control, and emergency management, and establish cooperation mechanisms such as mutual notification, information sharing, and technology and talent exchange.

The people's governments at or above the county level in the border provinces and autonomous regions may, under the guidance of the relevant competent authorities of the State, participate in relevant cooperation mechanisms and undertake specific cooperation work.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 57** Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 32 of this Law shall be punished by the public security organ in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Law of the People's Republic of China on Public Security Administration Punishments" and the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Military Facilities". If boundary markers or border defense infrastructure are damaged, the perpetrator shall be ordered to compensate for the losses.

Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 34 or Article 41 of this Law shall be given a warning or a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan by the public security organ; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be detained for not more than five days or fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan.

**Article 58** Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 37, Paragraph 1 of Article 38, or Article 39 of this Law shall be punished by the relevant departments in accordance with relevant laws and regulations according to their respective functions and duties.

Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 40 of this Law shall be ordered by the relevant competent authorities to stop the illegal act and restore the original state, and be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan. If a unit violates the provisions of this Article, it shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.Whoever violates the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 34, the second paragraph of Article 39, or Article 41 of this Law may also have the tools used to commit acts violating the land border and border management confiscated.

Article 59 Where state organs and their staff members fail to perform their statutory duties, disclose state secrets, or abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in land border work, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 60 Whoever violates the provisions of this Law, constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 61 A boundary marker refers to a marker erected on or on both sides of the land border, indicating the course of the land border on the ground, with its geographic coordinates measured and recorded in the boundary delimitation treaty or joint inspection treaty, including basic boundary markers, auxiliary boundary markers, guide markers, and buoys, among others.

A visibility corridor refers to a passage opened within a certain width on both sides of the land border to maintain visibility of the land border.

Article 62 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2022.
