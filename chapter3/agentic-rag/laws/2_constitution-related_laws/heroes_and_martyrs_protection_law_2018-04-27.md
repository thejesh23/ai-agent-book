# Heroes and Martyrs Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 27, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the protection of heroes and martyrs, safeguard public interests, inherit and promote the spirit of heroes and martyrs and the spirit of patriotism, cultivate and practice the core socialist values, and inspire the powerful spiritual strength to realize the Chinese Dream of the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.

Article 2 The state and the people shall forever respect and remember the sacrifices and contributions made by heroes and martyrs to the country, the people, and the nation.

Since modern times, heroes and martyrs who have fought for national independence and people's liberation, for national prosperity and people's happiness, and for world peace and human progress, with lifelong dedication and heroic sacrifice, have made brilliant achievements recorded in history, and their spirit will be immortal.

Article 3 The deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs constitute the common historical memory of the Chinese nation and an important manifestation of the core socialist values.

The state protects heroes and martyrs, commends and commemorates them, strengthens the publicity and education of their deeds and spirit, and safeguards their dignity and legitimate rights and interests.

The whole society shall uphold, learn from, and defend heroes and martyrs.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the protection of heroes and martyrs, and regard the publicity and promotion of their deeds and spirit as an important part of the construction of socialist spiritual civilization.

The departments responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs under people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments shall perform their duties according to law and do a good job in protecting heroes and martyrs.

The relevant departments of the military shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, do a good job in protecting heroes and martyrs.

People's governments at or above the county level shall include the funds for the protection of heroes and martyrs in their budgets.

Article 5 September 30 of each year is designated as Martyrs' Day. The state holds a memorial ceremony in front of the Monument to the People's Heroes at Tiananmen Square in Beijing, the capital, to commemorate heroes and martyrs.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments of the military shall hold commemorative activities on Martyrs' Day.

When holding commemorative activities for heroes and martyrs, representatives of their surviving family members shall be invited to participate.

Article 6 On Qingming Festival and important anniversaries, state organs, organizations, villages, communities, schools, enterprises, institutions, and relevant military units shall, according to actual conditions, organize and carry out commemorative activities for heroes and martyrs.

Article 7 The state establishes and protects memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs to commemorate and remember them.

The Monument to the People's Heroes, standing at Tiananmen Square in Beijing, the capital, is a symbol of the spirit of the Chinese people and the Chinese nation in their struggle for national independence and liberation, people's freedom and happiness, and national prosperity and strength since modern times. It is a permanent memorial facility for the state and the people to commemorate and remember heroes and martyrs.

The Monument to the People's Heroes, including its name, inscriptions, epitaph, reliefs, graphics, and symbols, is protected by law.

Article 8 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the construction and protection of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs into national economic and social development plans and urban and rural plans, and strengthen the protection and management of these facilities. Memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs of significant commemorative and educational value shall be verified and announced as protected cultural heritage sites in accordance with the provisions of the Cultural Relics Protection Law of the People's Republic of China.

The central finance shall provide subsidies for the repair and protection of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs in old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas, in accordance with state regulations.

Article 9 Memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs shall be open to the public free of charge for people to pay homage and mourn heroes and martyrs, to carry out commemorative and educational activities, and to console the spirits of the martyrs.

If the memorial facilities mentioned in the preceding paragraph are managed by relevant military units, they shall be opened in accordance with the relevant military regulations.

Article 10 The units responsible for the protection of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs shall improve service and management standards, facilitate paying homage and mourning, and maintain a solemn, respectful, and tranquil environment and atmosphere.

No organization or individual may engage in activities that harm the environment and atmosphere of commemorating heroes and martyrs within the protected areas of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs, nor may they encroach upon the land and facilities within the protected areas, or damage or defile the memorial facilities.

Article 11 When burying heroes and martyrs, people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments of the military shall hold solemn, respectful, civilized, and frugal ceremonies for sending off and burying them.

Article 12 The state establishes and improves the system and etiquette norms for paying respects to heroes and martyrs, guiding citizens to carry out such activities in a solemn and orderly manner.

The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall facilitate the surviving family members of heroes and martyrs in paying respects.

Article 13 The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall guide citizens to remember the deeds of heroes and martyrs and inherit and promote their spirit through activities such as visiting memorial facilities, taking collective oaths, and offering online tributes.

Article 14 If heroes and martyrs are buried abroad, the diplomatic and consular missions of the People's Republic of China in those countries shall, taking into account the actual conditions of the host country, organize activities to pay respects.

The state, through cooperation with relevant countries, shall search for and collect the remains, relics, and historical materials of heroes and martyrs, and strengthen the repair and protection of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs located abroad.

Article 15 The state encourages and supports research on the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs, guided by dialectical materialism and historical materialism in understanding and recording history.

Article 16 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments of the military shall strengthen the collection, protection, and display of relics and historical materials of heroes and martyrs, and organize research, compilation, and publicity of historical materials on heroes and martyrs.

The state encourages and supports old revolutionary base areas to leverage their local resource advantages to carry out research, publicity, and education on the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs.

Article 17 The administrative department of education shall, focusing on young students, incorporate the publicity and education of the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs into the national education system.

The administrative department of education and schools of all types and at all levels shall include the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs in educational content, organize commemorative and educational activities, and strengthen education on patriotism, collectivism, and socialism for students.

Article 18 Departments of culture, press and publication, radio, television, film, and cyberspace administration shall encourage and support the creation, production, and promotion of excellent literary and artistic works, radio and television programs, and publications themed on the deeds of heroes and martyrs and promoting their spirit.

Article 19 Radio stations, television stations, newspaper and periodical publishing units, and internet information service providers shall widely publicize the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs by broadcasting or publishing works themed on heroes and martyrs, airing public service advertisements, and setting up special columns.

Article 20 The state encourages and supports natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations to participate in the protection of heroes and martyrs through public welfare activities such as donating property, voluntarily giving lectures on the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs, and assisting the surviving family members of heroes and martyrs.

Natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations that donate property for the protection of heroes and martyrs shall enjoy tax benefits in accordance with the law.

Article 21 The state implements a system of pensions and preferential treatment for heroes and martyrs. The surviving family members of heroes and martyrs shall enjoy preferential treatment in education, employment, elderly care, housing, and medical care in accordance with state regulations. The level of pensions and preferential treatment shall be commensurate with national economic and social development and gradually improved.

The relevant departments of the State Council, the relevant departments of the military, and local people's governments shall care about the living conditions of the surviving family members of heroes and martyrs and visit them regularly each year.

Article 22 It is prohibited to distort, vilify, desecrate, or deny the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs.

The names, portraits, reputations, and honors of heroes and martyrs are protected by law. No organization or individual may, in public places, on the internet, or through radio, television, films, publications, etc., insult, slander, or otherwise infringe upon the names, portraits, reputations, or honors of heroes and martyrs. No organization or individual may use or indirectly use the names or portraits of heroes and martyrs for trademarks or commercial advertisements, thereby damaging their reputations and honors.

The public security, culture, press and publication, radio, television, film, cyberspace administration, market supervision and management departments, and the departments responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs shall, upon discovering acts specified in the preceding paragraph, handle them promptly in accordance with the law.

Article 23 In the course of supervising and managing online information in accordance with the law, the cyberspace administration, telecommunications, public security, and other relevant departments shall, upon discovering information that insults, slanders, or otherwise infringes upon the names, portraits, reputations, or honors of heroes and martyrs, require network operators to stop transmitting the information and take measures such as removal and other necessary measures. For the aforementioned information originating from outside the territory of the People's Republic of China, the relevant departments shall notify relevant agencies to take technical measures and other necessary measures to block its dissemination.

If a network operator discovers that its user has published information specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall immediately stop transmitting the information, take measures such as removal to prevent its spread, retain relevant records, and report to the relevant competent authorities. If a network operator fails to take measures such as stopping transmission or removal, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Cybersecurity Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 24 Any organization or individual has the right to report acts that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of heroes and martyrs or otherwise violate the provisions of this Law to the departments responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs, the cyberspace administration, the public security, and other relevant departments. The department receiving the report shall handle it promptly in accordance with the law.

Article 25 For acts that infringe upon the names, portraits, reputations, or honors of heroes and martyrs, their close relatives may file a lawsuit in a people's court in accordance with the law.

If a hero or martyr has no close relatives, or if the close relatives do not file a lawsuit, the procuratorial authority shall, in accordance with the law, file a lawsuit in a people's court against acts that infringe upon the names, portraits, reputations, or honors of heroes and martyrs and harm the public interest.

If the departments responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs and other relevant departments, in the course of performing their duties, discover acts specified in the first paragraph and deem it necessary for the procuratorial authority to file a lawsuit, they shall report to the procuratorial authority.

If the close relatives of a hero or martyr file a lawsuit in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph, a legal aid institution shall provide legal aid services in accordance with the law.

Article 26 Anyone who insults, slanders, or otherwise infringes upon the names, portraits, reputations, or honors of heroes and martyrs, thereby harming the public interest, shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 27 If anyone engages in activities that harm the environment and atmosphere of commemorating heroes and martyrs within the protected areas of memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs, the unit responsible for protecting the memorial facility shall promptly dissuade them. If the person refuses to be dissuaded, the department responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs under the local people's government at or above the county level or the cultural relics authority shall, within their respective responsibilities, give criticism and education and order correction. If the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law.

If anyone desecrates or denies the deeds and spirit of heroes and martyrs, advocates or glorifies wars of aggression and acts of aggression, creates disturbances, or disrupts public order, and the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 28 If anyone encroaches upon, damages, or defiles memorial facilities for heroes and martyrs, the department responsible for the protection of heroes and martyrs under the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction. If losses are caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law. If the encroached, damaged, or defiled memorial facility is a protected cultural heritage site, punishment shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Cultural Relics Protection Law of the People's Republic of China. If the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authority shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 29 If the relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level and their staff members abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in the protection of heroes and martyrs, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 30 This Law shall come into force on May 1, 2018.
