# Anti-Secession Law

Adopted at the Third Session of the Tenth National People's Congress on March 14, 2005

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is formulated, in accordance with the Constitution, for the purpose of opposing and checking the secession of the country by the "Taiwan independence" secessionist forces, promoting the peaceful reunification of the motherland, maintaining peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait region, safeguarding national sovereignty and territorial integrity, and upholding the fundamental interests of the Chinese nation.

Article 2 There is only one China in the world. Both the mainland and Taiwan belong to one China. China's sovereignty and territorial integrity brook no division. Safeguarding national sovereignty and territorial integrity is the common obligation of all Chinese people, including our compatriots in Taiwan.

Taiwan is part of China. The state shall never allow the "Taiwan independence" secessionist forces to make Taiwan secede from China under any name or by any means.

Article 3 The Taiwan issue is a leftover issue from China's civil war.

Solving the Taiwan issue and achieving the reunification of the motherland is China's internal affair, which is subject to no interference by any foreign forces.

Article 4 Accomplishing the great task of reunifying the motherland is the sacred duty of all Chinese people, including our compatriots in Taiwan.

Article 5 Upholding the principle of one China is the foundation for the peaceful reunification of the motherland.

To reunify the country through peaceful means best serves the fundamental interests of compatriots on both sides of the Taiwan Strait. The state shall make the greatest sincerity and the utmost effort to achieve peaceful reunification.

After the country is reunified peacefully, Taiwan may practice systems different from those of the mainland and enjoy a high degree of autonomy.

Article 6 The state shall take the following measures to maintain peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait region and develop cross-strait relations:

(1) encouraging and facilitating personnel exchanges across the strait to enhance mutual understanding and mutual trust;

(2) encouraging and facilitating economic exchanges and cooperation, realizing direct links of postal, air and shipping services and trade, and bringing closer economic ties between the two sides to their mutual benefit;

(3) encouraging and facilitating exchanges in education, science and technology, culture, health and sports, and jointly promoting the fine traditional culture of the Chinese nation;

(4) encouraging and facilitating joint efforts to combat crime;

(5) encouraging and facilitating other activities that are conducive to maintaining peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait region and developing cross-strait relations.

The state shall protect the rights and interests of our compatriots in Taiwan in accordance with law.

Article 7 The state advocates the achievement of peaceful reunification through equal consultations and negotiations between the two sides of the Taiwan Strait. Consultations and negotiations may be conducted step by step and in a phased manner, and the methods may be flexible and diverse.

The two sides of the Taiwan Strait may consult and negotiate on the following matters:

(1) formally ending the state of hostility between the two sides;

(2) planning for the development of cross-strait relations;

(3) steps and arrangements for peaceful reunification;

(4) the political status of the Taiwan authorities;

(5) the space for international activities that is compatible with the status of the Taiwan region; and

(6) any other issues relating to the achievement of peaceful reunification.

Article 8 In the event that the "Taiwan independence" secessionist forces cause the fact of Taiwan's secession from China under any name or by any means, or that major incidents entailing Taiwan's secession from China occur, or that possibilities for peaceful reunification are completely exhausted, the state shall employ non-peaceful means and other necessary measures to safeguard national sovereignty and territorial integrity.

The decision to employ non-peaceful means and other necessary measures as provided for in the preceding paragraph shall be made and implemented by the State Council and the Central Military Commission, and shall be reported promptly to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 9 When non-peaceful means and other necessary measures are employed and implemented in accordance with this Law, the state shall do its utmost to protect the lives, property and other legitimate rights and interests of Taiwan civilians and foreigners in Taiwan, and to minimize losses; at the same time, the state shall protect the rights and interests of our compatriots in Taiwan in other parts of China in accordance with law.

Article 10 This Law shall go into effect as of the date of its promulgation.
