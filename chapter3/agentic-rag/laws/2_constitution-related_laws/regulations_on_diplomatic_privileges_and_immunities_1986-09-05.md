# Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Diplomatic Privileges and Immunities

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 5, 1986

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated for the purpose of defining the diplomatic privileges and immunities of foreign embassies in China and their personnel, and facilitating the effective performance of their functions by foreign embassies in China on behalf of their states.

Article 2 In principle, diplomatic personnel of an embassy shall be nationals of the sending state. If it is necessary to appoint a national of China or a third state as a diplomatic officer of the embassy, the consent of the competent authorities of China must be obtained, and the said authorities may withdraw such consent at any time.

Article 3 The embassy and its head shall have the right to use the flag or emblem of the sending state on the premises of the embassy and on the means of transport of the head of the embassy.

Article 4 The premises of the embassy shall be inviolable. Entry into the premises of the embassy by Chinese state functionaries shall require the consent of the head of the embassy or an authorized person. The competent authorities of China shall take appropriate measures to protect the premises of the embassy against intrusion or damage.

The premises, equipment, and other property on the premises of the embassy, as well as the means of transport of the embassy, shall be immune from search, requisition, attachment, or execution.

Article 5 The premises of the embassy shall be exempt from all dues and taxes, except such as represent payment for specific services rendered.

The fees and charges levied by the embassy in the course of its official duties shall be exempt from all dues and taxes.

Article 6 The archives and documents of the embassy shall be inviolable.

Article 7 The personnel of the embassy shall enjoy freedom of movement and travel within Chinese territory, except for areas where entry is prohibited or restricted by the regulations of the Chinese government.

Article 8 The embassy may communicate freely with the government of the sending state and with other embassies and consulates of the sending state for official purposes. In such communication, the embassy may employ all appropriate means, including diplomatic couriers, diplomatic bags, and messages in code or cipher.

Article 9 The installation and use of radio transmitters for communication by the embassy shall require the consent of the Chinese government. The importation of the said equipment by the embassy shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese government.

Article 10 The official correspondence of the embassy shall be inviolable.

The diplomatic bag shall not be opened or detained.

The diplomatic bag may contain only diplomatic documents or articles intended for official use, and must be sealed and bear visible external marks of its character.

Article 11 The diplomatic courier shall be provided with a courier certificate issued by the competent authorities of the sending state. The person of a diplomatic courier shall be inviolable; he shall not be liable to arrest or detention.

A temporary diplomatic courier shall be provided with a temporary courier certificate issued by the competent authorities of the sending state, and shall enjoy the same immunities as a diplomatic courier while in charge of the diplomatic bag.

A commercial aircraft pilot entrusted with the transmission of a diplomatic bag must hold an official document issued by the entrusting state indicating the number of packages constituting the diplomatic bag. The pilot shall not be regarded as a diplomatic courier. The embassy shall send its personnel to receive the diplomatic bag from the pilot.

Article 12 The person of a diplomatic agent shall be inviolable; he shall not be liable to arrest or detention. The competent authorities of China shall take appropriate measures to prevent any attack on his person, freedom, or dignity.

Article 13 The residence of a diplomatic agent shall be inviolable and shall be protected.

The papers and correspondence of a diplomatic agent shall be inviolable. The property of a diplomatic agent shall be inviolable, except as provided in Article 14.

Article 14 A diplomatic agent shall enjoy immunity from criminal jurisdiction.

He shall enjoy immunity from civil and administrative jurisdiction, except in the case of:

(1) an action relating to succession in which the diplomatic agent is involved as a private person;

(2) an action relating to any professional or commercial activity engaged in by the diplomatic agent within Chinese territory outside his official functions, in contravention of the provisions of Article 25, paragraph 3.

A diplomatic agent shall not be liable to any form of execution, except in the cases mentioned in the preceding paragraph, where execution does not constitute any violation of his person or residence.

A diplomatic agent shall not be obliged to give evidence as a witness.

Article 15 The immunity from jurisdiction of diplomatic agents and of persons enjoying immunity under Article 20 may be waived by the sending state.

If a diplomatic agent or a person enjoying immunity under Article 20 initiates proceedings, he shall not be entitled to invoke immunity from jurisdiction in respect of any counterclaim directly connected with the principal claim.

Waiver of immunity from civil or administrative jurisdiction shall not be deemed to imply waiver of immunity in respect of the execution of the judgment, for which a separate express waiver shall be necessary.

Article 16 A diplomatic agent shall be exempt from all dues and taxes, except:

(1) dues and taxes of a kind which are normally incorporated in the price of goods or services;

(2) dues and taxes on private immovable property situated in China, subject to the provisions of Article 18;

(3) estate, succession, or inheritance duties levied by China, subject to the provisions of paragraph (2) of this Article;

(4) dues and taxes on private income having its source in China;

(5) charges levied for specific services rendered.

Article 17 A diplomatic agent shall be exempt from all personal and public services and from military obligations.

Article 18 Articles for the official use of the embassy and articles for the personal use of a diplomatic agent shall be exempt from customs duties and all other dues and taxes, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese government.

The personal baggage of a diplomatic agent shall be exempt from inspection, unless there are serious grounds for presuming that it contains articles not covered by the exemptions mentioned in the preceding paragraph, or articles the import or export of which is prohibited by Chinese law or government regulations, or articles subject to quarantine regulations. Such inspection shall be conducted in the presence of the diplomatic agent or his authorized representative.

Article 19 The importation of firearms and ammunition for personal use by the embassy and its personnel shall require the approval of the Chinese government and shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Chinese government.

Article 20 The spouse and minor children of a diplomatic agent, if not nationals of China, shall enjoy the privileges and immunities specified in Articles 12 to 18.

The administrative and technical staff of the embassy, together with their spouses and minor children, if not nationals of China and not permanently resident in China, shall enjoy the privileges and immunities specified in Articles 12 to 17, except that immunity from civil and administrative jurisdiction shall be limited to acts performed in the course of their duties. They shall enjoy the privilege of exemption from customs duties and other dues and taxes, as provided in paragraph 1 of Article 18, in respect of articles imported for the purpose of establishing themselves within six months of their first arrival.

The service staff of the embassy, if not nationals of China and not permanently resident in China, shall enjoy immunity in respect of acts performed in the course of their duties and exemption from income tax on the emoluments they receive by reason of their employment. They shall enjoy the privilege of exemption from customs duties and other dues and taxes, as provided in paragraph 1 of Article 18, in respect of articles imported for the purpose of establishing themselves within six months of their first arrival.

Private servants of members of the embassy, if not nationals of China and not permanently resident in China, shall be exempt from income tax on the emoluments they receive by reason of their employment.

Article 21 If a diplomatic agent is a national of China or a foreign national who has obtained the status of permanent residence in China, he shall enjoy immunity from jurisdiction and inviolability only in respect of acts performed in the course of his official duties.

Article 22 The following persons shall enjoy such immunities and inviolability as are necessary for their transit through or sojourn in China:

(1) a diplomatic agent of a third state accredited to China, together with his spouse and minor children, while passing through China;

(2) a foreign official who holds a Chinese diplomatic visa or a diplomatic passport (only for countries with mutual visa exemption) and is coming to China;

(3) other foreign nationals coming to China on an official visit, to whom the Chinese government has granted the privileges and immunities provided for in this Article.

The provisions of Articles 10 and 11 shall apply, mutatis mutandis, to a diplomatic courier of a third state passing through China and the diplomatic bag entrusted to him.

Article 23 The head of a foreign state, the head of a foreign government, the minister of foreign affairs, and other officials of comparable status, while on an official visit to China, shall enjoy the privileges and immunities provided for in these Regulations.

Article 24 The treatment of foreign representatives coming to China to attend international conferences convened by the United Nations or its specialized agencies, officials and experts of the United Nations or its specialized agencies temporarily present in China, and the representative offices and personnel of the United Nations or its specialized agencies in China, shall be governed by the relevant international conventions to which China has acceded and the agreements concluded between China and the relevant international organizations.

Article 25 Persons enjoying diplomatic privileges and immunities shall:

(1) respect the laws and regulations of China;

(2) not interfere in the internal affairs of China;

(3) not engage in any professional or commercial activity for personal profit within Chinese territory;

(4) not use the premises of the embassy or the residences of the members of the embassy for purposes incompatible with the functions of the embassy.

Article 26 If the diplomatic privileges and immunities granted by a foreign state to the Chinese embassy in that state, its personnel, and relevant persons temporarily present in that state are less extensive than those granted by China to the embassy of that state in China, its personnel, and relevant persons temporarily present in China under these Regulations, the Chinese government may, on a reciprocal basis, grant corresponding diplomatic privileges and immunities to the embassy of that state in China, its personnel, and relevant persons temporarily present in China.

Article 27 Where provisions of international treaties to which China is a party or has acceded differ from those of these Regulations, the provisions of the international treaties shall prevail, except for those clauses on which China has made reservations.

Where provisions of agreements on diplomatic privileges and immunities concluded between China and a foreign state differ from those of these Regulations, the provisions of the agreements shall prevail.

Article 28 For the purposes of these Regulations, the following terms shall have the meanings hereunder assigned to them:

(1) "Head of the embassy" means the ambassador, minister, charge d'affaires, or other person of equivalent rank appointed by the sending state to hold that position;

(2) "Members of the embassy" means the head of the embassy and the members of the staff of the embassy;

(3) "Members of the staff of the embassy" means the diplomatic staff, the administrative and technical staff, and the service staff of the embassy;

(4) "Diplomatic staff of the embassy" means the members of the staff of the embassy having diplomatic rank;

(5) "Diplomatic agent" means the head of the embassy or a member of the diplomatic staff of the embassy;

(6) "Administrative and technical staff of the embassy" means the members of the staff of the embassy engaged in the administrative and technical work of the embassy;

(7) "Service staff of the embassy" means the members of the staff of the embassy engaged in the domestic work of the embassy;

(8) "Private servant" means a person who is in the domestic employment of a member of the embassy;

(9) "Premises of the embassy" means the buildings and the land ancillary thereto used for the purposes of the embassy and the residence of the head of the embassy.

Article 29 These Regulations shall come into force on the date of their promulgation.
