# Civil Code of the People's Republic of China

## General Provisions

Adopted at the Third Session of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on May 28, 2020

Effective as of January 1, 2021

### Chapter I  Basic Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China to protect the lawful rights and interests of civil subjects, regulate civil relations, maintain social and economic order, meet the requirements for developing socialism with Chinese characteristics, and promote the core socialist values.

Article 2 The civil law regulates personal and property relations among natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations that are equal in status.

Article 3 The personal rights, property rights, and other lawful rights and interests of civil subjects are protected by law, and no organization or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 4 All civil subjects are equal in legal status in civil activities.

Article 5 When engaging in civil activities, civil subjects shall follow the principle of voluntariness, establishing, modifying, and terminating civil legal relations according to their own will.

Article 6 When engaging in civil activities, civil subjects shall follow the principle of fairness, reasonably determining the rights and obligations of each party.

Article 7 When engaging in civil activities, civil subjects shall follow the principle of good faith, acting in good faith and honoring their commitments.

Article 8 When engaging in civil activities, civil subjects shall not violate the law or contravene public order and good customs.

Article 9 When engaging in civil activities, civil subjects shall act in a manner conducive to resource conservation and the protection of the ecological environment.

Article 10 Civil disputes shall be resolved in accordance with the law. Where the law does not provide for a matter, customs may be applied, provided that they do not contravene public order and good customs.

Article 11 Where other laws have special provisions on civil relations, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 12 Civil activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the laws of the People's Republic of China, unless otherwise provided by law.

### Chapter II Natural Persons

#### Section 1  Capacity for Civil Rights and Capacity for Civil Conduct

Article 13 A natural person has capacity for civil rights from birth until death, and enjoys civil rights and assumes civil obligations in accordance with the law.

Article 14 All natural persons are equal in their capacity for civil rights.

Article 15 The time of birth and death of a natural person shall be determined by the time recorded on the birth certificate or death certificate. In the absence of a birth certificate or death certificate, the time recorded in the household registration or other valid identity registration shall prevail. Where other evidence is sufficient to overturn the recorded time, the time proven by such evidence shall prevail.

Article 16 Where the protection of the interests of a fetus is involved, such as in matters of inheritance or acceptance of gifts, the fetus shall be deemed to have capacity for civil rights. However, if the fetus is stillborn, its capacity for civil rights shall be deemed never to have existed.

Article 17 A natural person who has reached the age of eighteen is an adult. A natural person under the age of eighteen is a minor.

Article 18 An adult is a person with full capacity for civil conduct and may independently perform civil juristic acts.

A minor who has reached the age of sixteen and whose main source of livelihood is his or her own labor income shall be deemed a person with full capacity for civil conduct.

Article 19 A minor who has reached the age of eight is a person with limited capacity for civil conduct. Such a minor shall perform civil juristic acts through or with the consent or ratification of his or her legal representative; however, he or she may independently perform civil juristic acts that are purely beneficial or that are appropriate to his or her age and intelligence.

Article 20 A minor under the age of eight is a person with no capacity for civil conduct, and his or her civil juristic acts shall be performed by his or her legal representative.

Article 21 An adult who is unable to recognize his or her own conduct is a person with no capacity for civil conduct, and his or her civil juristic acts shall be performed by his or her legal representative.

Where a minor who has reached the age of eight is unable to recognize his or her own conduct, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 22 An adult who is unable to fully recognize his or her own conduct is a person with limited capacity for civil conduct. Such an adult shall perform civil juristic acts through or with the consent or ratification of his or her legal representative; however, he or she may independently perform civil juristic acts that are purely beneficial or that are appropriate to his or her intelligence and mental health condition.

Article 23 The guardian of a person with no capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct is his or her legal representative.

Article 24 Where an adult is unable to recognize or fully recognize his or her own conduct, an interested party or relevant organization may apply to the people's court for a determination that the adult is a person with no capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct.

Where a person has been determined by a people's court to be a person with no capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct, upon application by the person himself or herself, an interested party, or a relevant organization, the people's court may, based on the recovery of his or her intelligence and mental health, determine that the adult has been restored to the status of a person with limited capacity for civil conduct or a person with full capacity for civil conduct.

The relevant organizations referred to in this Article include: residents' committees, villagers' committees, schools, medical institutions, women's federations, disabled persons' federations, lawfully established organizations for the elderly, and civil affairs departments.

Article 25 The domicile of a natural person is the place of residence recorded in the household registration or other valid identity registration. If the habitual residence is inconsistent with the domicile, the habitual residence shall be deemed the domicile.

#### Section 2  Guardianship

Article 26 Parents have the duty to raise, educate, and protect their minor children.

Adult children have the duty to support, assist, and protect their parents.

Article 27 Parents are the guardians of their minor children.

Where the parents of a minor are deceased or lack the capacity to be guardians, the following persons with the capacity to be guardians shall act as guardians in the following order:

(1) paternal and maternal grandparents;

(2) elder brothers and sisters;

(3) other individuals or organizations willing to act as guardians, subject to the consent of the residents' committee, villagers' committee, or civil affairs department in the place of the minor's domicile.

Article 28 For an adult with no capacity for civil conduct or limited capacity for civil conduct, the following persons with the capacity to be guardians shall act as guardians in the following order:

(1) spouse;

(2) parents and children;

(3) other close relatives;

(4) other individuals or organizations willing to act as guardians, subject to the consent of the residents' committee, villagers' committee, or civil affairs department in the place of the ward's domicile.

Article 29 Where the parents of a ward act as guardians, they may designate a guardian by will.

Article 30 Persons who are legally qualified to be guardians may agree among themselves to determine a guardian. The determination of a guardian by agreement shall respect the true wishes of the ward.

Article 31 Where there is a dispute over the determination of a guardian, the residents' committee, villagers' committee, or civil affairs department in the place of the ward's domicile shall designate a guardian. If a party concerned disagrees with the designation, it may apply to the people's court for the designation of a guardian; the party concerned may also directly apply to the people's court for the designation of a guardian.

The residents' committee, villagers' committee, civil affairs department, or people's court shall respect the true wishes of the ward and designate a guardian from among persons legally qualified to be guardians in accordance with the principle of best interests of the ward.

Where, before the designation of a guardian in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, the personal rights, property rights, and other lawful rights and interests of the ward are in a state of being unprotected, the residents' committee, villagers' committee, relevant organization provided by law, or civil affairs department in the place of the ward's domicile shall act as a temporary guardian.

Once a guardian is designated, no change may be made without authorization; any unauthorized change shall not relieve the designated guardian of his or her responsibility.

Article 32 Where there is no person legally qualified to be a guardian, the guardian shall be the civil affairs department, or may be the residents' committee or villagers' committee in the place of the ward's domicile that is capable of performing the duties of guardianship.

Article 33 An adult with full capacity for civil conduct may, through prior consultation with his or her close relatives or other individuals or organizations willing to act as guardians, determine his or her guardian in writing. When the adult loses or partially loses capacity for civil conduct, the guardian shall perform the duties of guardianship.

Article 34 The duties of a guardian are to represent the ward in performing civil juristic acts and to protect the personal rights, property rights, and other lawful rights and interests of the ward.

The rights arising from a guardian's lawful performance of guardianship duties are protected by law.

A guardian who fails to perform guardianship duties or infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of the ward shall bear legal liability.

Where, due to an emergency such as a sudden incident, a guardian is temporarily unable to perform guardianship duties and the ward's life is left unattended, the residents' committee, villagers' committee, or civil affairs department in the place of the ward's domicile shall arrange necessary temporary living care measures for the ward.

Article 35 A guardian shall perform guardianship duties in accordance with the principle of best interests of the ward. A guardian shall not dispose of the ward's property except for the benefit of the ward.

When performing guardianship duties, the guardian of a minor shall, in making decisions related to the ward's interests, respect the true wishes of the ward based on the ward's age and intelligence.

When performing guardianship duties, the guardian of an adult shall respect the true wishes of the ward to the greatest extent possible, and ensure and assist the ward in performing civil juristic acts appropriate to the ward's intelligence and mental health condition. The guardian shall not interfere with matters that the ward is capable of handling independently.

Article 36 Where a guardian falls under any of the following circumstances, the people's court shall, upon application by a relevant individual or organization, disqualify the guardian, arrange necessary temporary guardianship measures, and designate a guardian in accordance with the law based on the principle of best interests of the ward:

(1) committing acts that seriously harm the physical or mental health of the ward;

(2) being negligent in performing guardianship duties, or being unable to perform guardianship duties and refusing to delegate part or all of the guardianship duties to others, thereby leaving the ward in a state of danger or distress;

(3) committing other acts that seriously infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of the ward.

The relevant individuals and organizations referred to in this Article include: other persons legally qualified to be guardians, residents' committees, villagers' committees, schools, medical institutions, women's federations, disabled persons' federations, organizations for the protection of minors, lawfully established organizations for the elderly, and civil affairs departments.

Where the individuals and organizations other than the civil affairs department specified in the preceding paragraph fail to apply to the people's court in a timely manner for disqualification of the guardian, the civil affairs department shall make the application to the people's court.

Article 37 Parents, children, spouses, and others who are legally obligated to pay maintenance, support, or alimony for the ward shall continue to perform such obligations after the guardian is disqualified by the people's court.

Article 38 Where a parent or child of a ward has been disqualified as guardian by the people's court, if the person truly shows repentance, except in cases where the person intentionally committed a crime against the ward, upon the person's application, the people's court may, respecting the true wishes of the ward, restore the person's guardian status as appropriate, and the guardianship relationship between the guardian designated by the people's court and the ward shall terminate simultaneously.

Article 39 Guardianship terminates under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the ward acquires or regains full capacity for civil conduct;

(2) the guardian loses the capacity to be a guardian;

(3) the ward or the guardian dies;

(4) other circumstances under which the people's court determines that guardianship terminates.

After the termination of guardianship, if the ward still needs guardianship, another guardian shall be determined separately in accordance with the law.

#### Section 3  Declaration of Missing Persons and Declaration of Death

Article 40 Where a natural person has been missing for two years, an interested party may apply to the people's court for a declaration that the natural person is a missing person.

Article 41 The period of a natural person's disappearance shall be calculated from the date on which the person's whereabouts became unknown. If the person disappeared during a war, the period of disappearance shall be calculated from the date the war ended or from the date determined by the relevant authority as the date of disappearance.

Article 42 The property of a missing person shall be managed by his or her spouse, adult children, parents, or other persons willing to act as property custodians.

If there is a dispute over the custodianship, or if there is no person specified in the preceding paragraph, or if the person specified in the preceding paragraph lacks the capacity to act as custodian, the property shall be managed by a person designated by the people's court.

Article 43 A property custodian shall properly manage the property of the missing person and protect the property rights and interests of the missing person.

Taxes, debts, and other expenses payable by the missing person shall be paid by the property custodian from the missing person's property.

A property custodian who causes loss to the missing person's property due to intentional act or gross negligence shall be liable for compensation.

Article 44 Where a property custodian fails to perform custodial duties, infringes upon the property rights and interests of the missing person, or loses the capacity to act as custodian, an interested party of the missing person may apply to the people's court for a change of property custodian.

Where a property custodian has a legitimate reason, he or she may apply to the people's court for a change of property custodian.

Where the people's court changes the property custodian, the new property custodian shall have the right to request the former property custodian to promptly transfer the relevant property and report on the status of property management.

Article 45 Where a missing person reappears, upon application by the person himself or herself or an interested party, the people's court shall revoke the declaration of missing person.

Where a missing person reappears, he or she has the right to request the property custodian to promptly transfer the relevant property and report on the status of property management.

Article 46 Where a natural person falls under any of the following circumstances, an interested party may apply to the people's court for a declaration of death of the natural person:

(1) the person's whereabouts have been unknown for four years;

(2) the person's whereabouts have been unknown for two years due to an accident.

Where a person's whereabouts become unknown due to an accident, and the relevant authority proves that the natural person is unlikely to survive, the application for a declaration of death is not subject to the two-year time limit.

Article 47 Where, for the same natural person, some interested parties apply for a declaration of death and others apply for a declaration of missing person, and the conditions for a declaration of death under this Law are met, the people's court shall declare the person dead.

Article 48 For a person declared dead, the date of the people's court's judgment declaring death shall be deemed the date of death. If the person is declared dead due to disappearance in an accident, the date of the accident shall be deemed the date of death.

Article 49 Where a natural person is declared dead but is not actually dead, this does not affect the validity of civil juristic acts performed by the natural person during the period of the declaration of death.

Article 50 Where a person declared dead reappears, upon application by the person himself or herself or an interested party, the people's court shall revoke the declaration of death.

Article 51 The marital relationship of a person declared dead shall be dissolved from the date of the declaration of death. If the declaration of death is revoked, the marital relationship shall be automatically restored from the date of revocation of the declaration of death, unless the spouse has remarried or has declared in writing to the marriage registration authority that he or she is unwilling to restore the relationship.

Article 52 Where a child of a person declared dead was lawfully adopted by another person during the period of the declaration of death, after the declaration of death is revoked, the person may not claim that the adoption is invalid on the ground that it was done without his or her consent.

Article 53 A person whose declaration of death has been revoked has the right to request the civil subject who obtained his or her property in accordance with the provisions of Book VI of this Law to return the property. If the property cannot be returned, appropriate compensation shall be made.

Where an interested party, by concealing the true situation, causes another person to be declared dead and obtains that person's property, the interested party shall, in addition to returning the property, be liable for compensation for the losses caused thereby.

#### Section 4  Individual Businesses and Rural Land Contractual Management Households

Article 54 Where a natural person engages in industrial or commercial operations and is registered in accordance with the law, the natural person is an individual business. An individual business may adopt a trade name.

Article 55 Where a member of a rural collective economic organization lawfully obtains the right to contractual management of rural land and engages in household contractual management, the member is a rural land contractual management household.

Article 56 For the debts of an individual business, if the business is operated by an individual, the debts shall be borne with the individual's property; if the business is operated by a household, the debts shall be borne with the household's property; if it cannot be distinguished, the debts shall be borne with the household's property.

For the debts of a rural land contractual management household, the debts shall be borne with the property of the household engaged in the contractual management of rural land; if the business is actually operated by some members of the household, the debts shall be borne with the property of those members.

### Chapter III  Legal Persons

#### Section 1  General Provisions

Article 57 A legal person is an organization that has capacity for civil rights and capacity for civil conduct, and independently enjoys civil rights and assumes civil obligations in accordance with the law.

Article 58 A legal person shall be established in accordance with the law.

A legal person shall have its own name, organizational structure, domicile, and property or funds. The specific conditions and procedures for the establishment of a legal person shall be in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Where the establishment of a legal person requires approval by a relevant authority under laws or administrative regulations, such provisions shall be followed.

Article 59 The capacity for civil rights and capacity for civil conduct of a legal person arise upon its establishment and cease upon its termination.

Article 60 A legal person shall independently bear civil liability with all of its property.

Article 61 The person who, in accordance with the law or the articles of association of the legal person, represents the legal person in engaging in civil activities is the legal representative of the legal person.

The legal consequences of civil activities conducted by the legal representative in the name of the legal person shall be borne by the legal person.

Any restriction on the authority of the legal representative to represent the legal person, as set forth in the articles of association or by the authoritative body of the legal person, shall not be effective against a bona fide counterparty.

Article 62 Where the legal representative causes harm to another person in the performance of his or her duties, the legal person shall bear civil liability.

After bearing civil liability, the legal person may, in accordance with the law or its articles of association, seek recourse from the legal representative who is at fault.

Article 63 The domicile of a legal person is the location of its principal place of business. Where a legal person is required to be registered in accordance with the law, the location of its principal place of business shall be registered as its domicile.

Article 64 Where registered items of a legal person change during its existence, the legal person shall apply to the registration authority for modification of registration in accordance with the law.

Article 65 Where the actual situation of a legal person is inconsistent with the registered items, this shall not be effective against a bona fide counterparty.

Article 66 The registration authority shall, in accordance with the law, promptly publicize relevant information about the registration of legal persons.

Article 67 Where a legal person is merged, the rights and obligations of the original legal person shall be enjoyed and assumed by the surviving legal person.

Where a legal person is divided, the rights and obligations of the original legal person shall be enjoyed and assumed by the surviving legal persons, who shall have joint claims and bear joint debts, unless otherwise agreed by the creditors and debtors.

Article 68 A legal person terminates upon the occurrence of any of the following reasons and the completion of liquidation and cancellation of registration in accordance with the law:

(1) dissolution of the legal person;

(2) declaration of bankruptcy of the legal person;

(3) other reasons provided by law.

Where the termination of a legal person requires approval by a relevant authority under laws or administrative regulations, such provisions shall be followed.

Article 69 A legal person is dissolved under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the term of existence specified in the articles of association of the legal person expires, or other grounds for dissolution specified in the articles of association arise;

(2) the authoritative body of the legal person resolves to dissolve;

(3) dissolution is required due to the merger or division of the legal person;

(4) the legal person's business license or registration certificate is lawfully revoked, or the legal person is ordered to close or is revoked;

(5) other circumstances provided by law.**Article 70** Where a legal person is dissolved, except in the cases of merger or division, the liquidation obligors shall promptly form a liquidation group to carry out liquidation.

The members of the executive or decision-making body of a legal person, such as its directors or council members, are the liquidation obligors. Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

If a liquidation obligor fails to perform its liquidation obligations in a timely manner, thereby causing damage, it shall bear civil liability. The competent authority or an interested person may apply to the people's court to designate relevant persons to form a liquidation group to carry out liquidation.

**Article 71** The liquidation procedures and the functions and powers of the liquidation group of a legal person shall be governed by relevant laws; in the absence of such provisions, the relevant provisions of the company laws shall apply mutatis mutandis.

**Article 72** During the liquidation period, the legal person continues to exist but shall not engage in any activities unrelated to the liquidation.

The residual property of a legal person after liquidation shall be disposed of in accordance with the provisions of the legal person's articles of association or the resolution of its authority organ. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

Upon the conclusion of liquidation and the completion of the cancellation of registration of the legal person, the legal person is terminated. Where registration is not required by law, the legal person is terminated upon the conclusion of liquidation.

**Article 73** Where a legal person is declared bankrupt, it is terminated upon the completion of bankruptcy liquidation and the cancellation of its registration in accordance with the law.

**Article 74** A legal person may establish branches in accordance with the law. Where laws or administrative regulations require a branch to be registered, such provisions shall prevail.

A branch engages in civil activities in its own name, and the civil liability arising therefrom shall be borne by the legal person. Alternatively, the liability may first be borne with the property managed by the branch, and if such property is insufficient, the remaining liability shall be borne by the legal person.

**Article 75** The legal consequences of civil activities conducted by a founder for the purpose of establishing a legal person shall be assumed by the legal person. If the legal person is not established, the legal consequences shall be assumed by the founder(s). Where there are two or more founders, they shall have joint creditor's rights and bear joint and several debts.

For civil liability arising from civil activities conducted by a founder in their own name for the purpose of establishing a legal person, a third party has the right to choose to claim against either the legal person or the founder.

### Section 2 For-profit Legal Persons

**Article 76** A legal person established for the purpose of obtaining profits and distributing them to its contributors (such as shareholders) is a for-profit legal person.

For-profit legal persons include limited liability companies, joint stock limited companies, and other enterprise legal persons.

**Article 77** A for-profit legal person is established upon registration in accordance with the law.

**Article 78** A for-profit legal person established in accordance with the law shall be issued a business license for a for-profit legal person by the registration authority. The date of issuance of the business license is the date of establishment of the for-profit legal person.

**Article 79** To establish a for-profit legal person, its articles of association shall be formulated in accordance with the law.

**Article 80** A for-profit legal person shall have an authority organ.

The authority organ exercises the powers to amend the articles of association, to elect or replace members of the executive and supervisory organs, and to perform other functions as stipulated in the articles of association.

**Article 81** A for-profit legal person shall have an executive organ.

The executive organ exercises the powers to convene meetings of the authority organ, to decide on the business plans and investment plans of the legal person, to decide on the setup of the internal management structure of the legal person, and to perform other functions as stipulated in the articles of association.

Where the executive organ is a board of directors or an executive director, the chairman of the board, the executive director, or the manager shall serve as the legal representative in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association. Where there is no board of directors or executive director, the principal responsible person as stipulated in the articles of association shall serve as the executive organ and the legal representative.

**Article 82** Where a for-profit legal person has a supervisory organ such as a board of supervisors or a supervisor, the supervisory organ shall, in accordance with the law, exercise the powers to inspect the financial affairs of the legal person, to supervise the conduct of the members of the executive organ and senior management personnel in performing their duties for the legal person, and to perform other functions as stipulated in the articles of association.

**Article 83** Contributors of a for-profit legal person shall not abuse their contributor rights to harm the interests of the legal person or other contributors. If abuse of contributor rights causes losses to the legal person or other contributors, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Contributors of a for-profit legal person shall not abuse the independent status of the legal person and the limited liability of contributors to harm the interests of the legal person's creditors. If abuse of the independent status of the legal person and the limited liability of contributors to evade debts seriously harms the interests of the legal person's creditors, they shall bear joint and several liability for the debts of the legal person.

**Article 84** The controlling contributor, actual controller, directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a for-profit legal person shall not use their affiliated relationships to harm the interests of the legal person. If losses are caused to the legal person through the use of affiliated relationships, they shall be liable for compensation.

**Article 85** If the convening procedures or voting methods of a meeting of the authority organ or executive organ of a for-profit legal person that adopts a resolution violate laws, administrative regulations, or the articles of association, or if the content of the resolution violates the articles of association, the contributors of the for-profit legal person may request the people's court to revoke the resolution. However, the civil legal relationship formed between the for-profit legal person and a bona fide counterparty based on such resolution shall not be affected.

**Article 86** When engaging in business activities, a for-profit legal person shall abide by business ethics, maintain transaction security, accept supervision from the government and the public, and bear social responsibilities.

### Section 3 Non-profit Legal Persons

**Article 87** A legal person established for public welfare purposes or other non-profit purposes, which does not distribute the profits obtained to its contributors, founders, or members, is a non-profit legal person.

Non-profit legal persons include public institutions, social groups, foundations, social service organizations, etc.

**Article 88** A public institution established to meet the needs of economic and social development and to provide public services, which meets the conditions for being a legal person, shall, upon registration in accordance with the law, acquire the status of a public institution legal person. If registration as a legal person is not required by law, it shall have the status of a public institution legal person from the date of its establishment.

**Article 89** Where a public institution legal person has a council, unless otherwise provided by law, the council shall be its decision-making body. The legal representative of a public institution legal person shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, or its articles of association.

**Article 90** A social group established based on the common will of its members for public welfare purposes or for the common interests of its members (non-profit purposes), which meets the conditions for being a legal person, shall, upon registration in accordance with the law, acquire the status of a social group legal person. If registration as a legal person is not required by law, it shall have the status of a social group legal person from the date of its establishment.

**Article 91** To establish a social group legal person, its articles of association shall be formulated in accordance with the law.

A social group legal person shall have an authority organ such as a general assembly of members or a congress of members.

A social group legal person shall have an executive organ such as a council. The responsible person, such as the chairman or president, shall serve as the legal representative in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association.

**Article 92** A foundation, social service organization, etc., established with donated property for public welfare purposes, which meets the conditions for being a legal person, shall, upon registration in accordance with the law, acquire the status of a donated legal person.

A place of religious activity established in accordance with the law, which meets the conditions for being a legal person, may apply for legal person registration and acquire the status of a donated legal person. Where laws or administrative regulations have provisions on places of religious activity, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 93** To establish a donated legal person, its articles of association shall be formulated in accordance with the law.

A donated legal person shall have a decision-making body such as a council or a democratic management organization, and an executive organ. The responsible person, such as the chairman, shall serve as the legal representative in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association.

A donated legal person shall have a supervisory organ such as a board of supervisors.

**Article 94** The donor has the right to inquire about the use and management of the donated property from the donated legal person and to put forward opinions and suggestions. The donated legal person shall provide timely and truthful responses.

If the procedures for a decision made by the decision-making body, executive organ, or legal representative of a donated legal person violate laws, administrative regulations, or the articles of association, or if the content of the decision violates the articles of association, interested parties such as the donor or the competent authority may request the people's court to revoke the decision. However, the civil legal relationship formed between the donated legal person and a bona fide counterparty based on such decision shall not be affected.

**Article 95** When a non-profit legal person established for public welfare purposes is terminated, no residual property shall be distributed to its contributors, founders, or members. The residual property shall be used for public welfare purposes in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association or the resolution of the authority organ. If it cannot be disposed of in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association or the resolution of the authority organ, the competent authority shall transfer it to a legal person with the same or similar purpose and make a public announcement.

### Section 4 Special Legal Persons

**Article 96** The following are special legal persons: government organs, rural collective economic organizations, urban and rural cooperative economic organizations, and grassroots self-governing mass organizations as provided for in this Section.

**Article 97** An organ with independent funds and a statutory body performing administrative functions shall, from the date of its establishment, have the status of a government organ legal person and may engage in civil activities necessary for the performance of its functions.

**Article 98** Where a government organ legal person is abolished, the legal person is terminated, and its civil rights and obligations shall be enjoyed and assumed by the successor government organ legal person. If there is no successor government organ legal person, they shall be enjoyed and assumed by the government organ legal person that made the decision to abolish it.

**Article 99** A rural collective economic organization shall obtain the status of a legal person in accordance with the law.

Where laws or administrative regulations have provisions on rural collective economic organizations, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 100** An urban or rural cooperative economic organization shall obtain the status of a legal person in accordance with the law.

Where laws or administrative regulations have provisions on urban or rural cooperative economic organizations, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 101** A residents' committee or a villagers' committee has the status of a grassroots self-governing mass organization legal person and may engage in civil activities necessary for the performance of its functions.

Where no village collective economic organization has been established, the villagers' committee may, in accordance with the law, perform the functions of the village collective economic organization on its behalf.

### Chapter 4 Unincorporated Organizations

**Article 102** An unincorporated organization is an organization that does not have the status of a legal person but can engage in civil activities in its own name in accordance with the law.

Unincorporated organizations include sole proprietorship enterprises, partnerships, and professional service institutions without legal person status.

**Article 103** An unincorporated organization shall be registered in accordance with the provisions of the law.

Where laws or administrative regulations require approval from relevant authorities for the establishment of an unincorporated organization, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 104** Where the property of an unincorporated organization is insufficient to pay off its debts, its contributors or founders shall bear unlimited liability. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 105** An unincorporated organization may designate one or more persons to represent the organization in engaging in civil activities.

**Article 106** An unincorporated organization shall be dissolved under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The term of existence stipulated in its articles of association has expired, or other grounds for dissolution stipulated in its articles of association have arisen;

(2) The contributors or founders decide to dissolve it;

(3) Other circumstances provided for by law.

**Article 107** Where an unincorporated organization is dissolved, liquidation shall be carried out in accordance with the law.

**Article 108** In addition to the application of the provisions of this Chapter, the relevant provisions of Section 1 of Chapter 3 of this Part shall apply mutatis mutandis to unincorporated organizations.

### Chapter 5 Civil Rights

**Article 109** The personal freedom and personal dignity of a natural person are protected by law.

**Article 110** A natural person enjoys rights to life, bodily integrity, health, name, portrait, reputation, honor, privacy, and marital autonomy.

Legal persons and unincorporated organizations enjoy rights to name, reputation, and honor.

**Article 111** The personal information of a natural person is protected by law. Any organization or individual that needs to obtain another person's personal information shall obtain it in accordance with the law and ensure its security. It shall not illegally collect, use, process, or transmit another person's personal information, nor illegally buy, sell, provide, or disclose another person's personal information.

**Article 112** The personal rights of a natural person arising from marriage, family, and other relationships are protected by law.

**Article 113** The property rights of civil subjects are equally protected by law.

**Article 114** Civil subjects enjoy real rights in accordance with the law.

A real right is the right of a right holder to directly control and exclusively enjoy a specific thing in accordance with the law, including ownership, usufructuary rights, and security interests.

**Article 115** Things include immovable and movable property. Where the law provides that rights can be the object of real rights, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 116** The types and content of real rights shall be provided for by law.

**Article 117** For the needs of the public interest, the expropriation or requisition of immovable or movable property in accordance with the authority and procedures provided for by law shall be subject to fair and reasonable compensation.

**Article 118** Civil subjects enjoy creditor's rights in accordance with the law.

A creditor's right is the right of a right holder to request a specific obligor to perform or refrain from performing a specific act, arising from contracts, torts, negotiorum gestio (management of affairs without mandate), unjust enrichment, and other provisions of the law.

**Article 119** A contract established in accordance with the law is legally binding on the parties.

**Article 120** Where a civil right or interest is infringed upon, the infringed party has the right to request the infringer to bear tort liability.

**Article 121** A person who, without a statutory or contractual obligation, manages the affairs of another person to prevent loss to that person's interests has the right to request the beneficiary to reimburse the necessary expenses incurred thereby.

**Article 122** Where a person obtains unjust benefits without a legal basis, the person who suffers a loss has the right to request the return of the unjust benefits.

**Article 123** Civil subjects enjoy intellectual property rights in accordance with the law.

Intellectual property rights are the exclusive rights enjoyed by right holders in accordance with the law over the following objects:

(1) Works;

(2) Inventions, utility models, and designs;

(3) Trademarks;

(4) Geographical indications;

(5) Trade secrets;

(6) Layout designs of integrated circuits;

(7) New plant varieties;

(8) Other objects provided for by law.

**Article 124** A natural person enjoys the right of inheritance in accordance with the law.

The lawful private property of a natural person may be inherited in accordance with the law.

**Article 125** Civil subjects enjoy equity rights and other investment rights in accordance with the law.

**Article 126** Civil subjects enjoy other civil rights and interests provided for by law.

**Article 127** Where the law has provisions on the protection of data and network virtual property, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 128** Where the law has special provisions on the protection of the civil rights of minors, the elderly, the disabled, women, consumers, etc., such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 129** Civil rights may be acquired through civil juristic acts, factual acts, events provided for by law, or other means provided for by law.

**Article 130** A civil subject shall exercise its civil rights according to its own will in accordance with the law, without interference.

**Article 131** When exercising rights, a civil subject shall perform the obligations provided for by law and agreed upon by the parties.

**Article 132** A civil subject shall not abuse its civil rights to harm the national interest, the public interest, or the lawful rights and interests of others.

### Chapter 6 Civil Juristic Acts

#### Section 1 General Provisions

**Article 133** A civil juristic act is an act of a civil subject to establish, change, or terminate a civil legal relationship through a declaration of will.

**Article 134** A civil juristic act may be established based on the consensus of declarations of will by two or more parties, or based on a unilateral declaration of will.

Where a legal person or an unincorporated organization adopts a resolution in accordance with the deliberation methods and voting procedures stipulated by law or its articles of association, such resolution act is established.

**Article 135** A civil juristic act may be in written form, oral form, or other forms. Where laws or administrative regulations stipulate or the parties agree on a specific form, such specific form shall be adopted.

**Article 136** A civil juristic act takes effect upon its establishment, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

An actor shall not unilaterally alter or rescind a civil juristic act unless in accordance with the provisions of law or with the consent of the other party.

#### Section 2 Declaration of Will

**Article 137** A declaration of will made in a dialogue takes effect when the counterparty becomes aware of its content.

A declaration of will made in a non-dialogue manner takes effect when it reaches the counterparty. Where a declaration of will is made in a non-dialogue manner using electronic data messages, if the counterparty has designated a specific system for receiving such messages, it takes effect when the electronic data message enters that specific system. If no specific system is designated, it takes effect when the counterparty knows or should know that the electronic data message has entered its system. If the parties have otherwise agreed on the effective time of a declaration of will made in the form of electronic data messages, such agreement shall prevail.

**Article 138** A declaration of will made without a counterparty takes effect upon completion of the declaration. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 139** A declaration of will made by public announcement takes effect upon the publication of the announcement.

**Article 140** An actor may make a declaration of will expressly or impliedly.

Silence may only be deemed as a declaration of will when provided for by law, agreed upon by the parties, or consistent with the transaction practices between the parties.

**Article 141** An actor may withdraw a declaration of will. The notice of withdrawal of the declaration of will shall reach the counterparty before or at the same time as the declaration of will reaches the counterparty.

**Article 142** For the interpretation of a declaration of will made to a counterparty, the meaning of the declaration of will shall be determined based on the words and sentences used, taking into account the relevant clauses, the nature and purpose of the act, customs, and the principle of good faith.

For the interpretation of a declaration of will made without a counterparty, one shall not rigidly adhere to the words and sentences used but shall determine the true intention of the actor by taking into account the relevant clauses, the nature and purpose of the act, customs, and the principle of good faith.

#### Section 3 Validity of Civil Juristic Acts

**Article 143** A civil juristic act is valid if it meets the following conditions:

(1) The actor has the corresponding capacity for civil conduct;

(2) The declaration of will is genuine;

(3) It does not violate the mandatory provisions of laws or administrative regulations, nor does it contravene public order and good customs.

**Article 144** A civil juristic act performed by a person without capacity for civil conduct is void.

**Article 145** A civil juristic act performed by a person with limited capacity for civil conduct that is purely beneficial or appropriate to their age, intelligence, or mental health is valid. Other civil juristic acts performed by such person are valid only upon consent or ratification by their legal agent.

The counterparty may urge the legal agent to ratify the act within thirty days from the date of receipt of the notice. If the legal agent fails to respond, it shall be deemed a refusal to ratify. Before the civil juristic act is ratified, a bona fide counterparty has the right to revoke it. Revocation shall be made by notice.

**Article 146** A civil juristic act performed by an actor and a counterparty based on a false declaration of will is void.

The validity of a civil juristic act concealed by a false declaration of will shall be dealt with in accordance with the relevant provisions of law.

**Article 147** Where a civil juristic act is performed based on a material misunderstanding, the actor has the right to request the people's court or an arbitration institution to revoke it.

**Article 148** Where one party induces the other party to perform a civil juristic act against their true will by means of fraud, the defrauded party has the right to request the people's court or an arbitration institution to revoke it.**Article 149** Where a third party commits a fraudulent act, causing one party to perform a civil juristic act contrary to its true intention, and the other party knew or should have known of the fraud, the defrauded party has the right to request a people's court or an arbitration institution to revoke the act.

**Article 150** Where one party or a third party uses duress to cause the other party to perform a civil juristic act contrary to its true intention, the coerced party has the right to request a people's court or an arbitration institution to revoke the act.

**Article 151** Where one party takes advantage of the other party's state of distress or lack of judgment, causing a manifestly unfair situation at the time of the establishment of the civil juristic act, the injured party has the right to request a people's court or an arbitration institution to revoke the act.

**Article 152** The right to revoke is extinguished under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The party fails to exercise the right to revoke within one year from the date it knew or should have known of the grounds for revocation, or, in the case of a material mistake, within ninety days from the date it knew or should have known of the grounds for revocation;
(2) The party, having been coerced, fails to exercise the right to revoke within one year from the date the duress ceased;
(3) The party, after learning of the grounds for revocation, expressly indicates or by its conduct manifests the waiver of the right to revoke.

If the party fails to exercise the right to revoke within five years from the date the civil juristic act occurred, the right to revoke is extinguished.

**Article 153** A civil juristic act that violates a mandatory provision of a law or administrative regulation is void. However, this does not apply if the mandatory provision does not render the civil juristic act void.

A civil juristic act that is contrary to public order and good customs is void.

**Article 154** A civil juristic act performed by the actor and the counterparty through malicious collusion, harming the lawful rights and interests of another person, is void.

**Article 155** A void or revoked civil juristic act has no legal binding force from the outset.

**Article 156** If a part of a civil juristic act is void, and this does not affect the validity of the remaining parts, the remaining parts remain valid.

**Article 157** After a civil juristic act is void, revoked, or determined to have no effect, the property obtained by the actor as a result of the act shall be returned; if return is impossible or unnecessary, compensation shall be made at an appraised price. The party at fault shall compensate the other party for the losses incurred thereby; if all parties are at fault, they shall each bear corresponding liability. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall apply.

#### Section 4: Conditional and Time-Limited Civil Juristic Acts

**Article 158** A civil juristic act may be subject to a condition, unless its nature precludes it. A civil juristic act subject to a condition precedent becomes effective when the condition is fulfilled. A civil juristic act subject to a condition subsequent becomes invalid when the condition is fulfilled.

**Article 159** Where a party to a conditional civil juristic act improperly prevents the fulfillment of the condition for its own benefit, the condition is deemed to have been fulfilled; where a party improperly facilitates the fulfillment of the condition, the condition is deemed not to have been fulfilled.

**Article 160** A civil juristic act may be subject to a time limit, unless its nature precludes it. A civil juristic act subject to a suspensive time limit becomes effective when the time limit arrives. A civil juristic act subject to a resolutive time limit becomes invalid when the time limit expires.

### Chapter VII: Agency

#### Section 1: General Provisions

**Article 161** A civil subject may perform civil juristic acts through an agent.

A civil juristic act that, by law, by agreement of the parties, or by its nature, must be performed personally by the party concerned shall not be performed through an agent.

**Article 162** A civil juristic act performed by an agent within the scope of the agency authority in the name of the principal is binding on the principal.

**Article 163** Agency includes agency by mandate and agency by operation of law.

An agent by mandate exercises the agency authority in accordance with the mandate of the principal. An agent by operation of law exercises the agency authority in accordance with the provisions of the law.

**Article 164** If an agent fails to perform or fails to fully perform its duties, causing damage to the principal, the agent shall bear civil liability.

If the agent and the counterparty maliciously collude, harming the lawful rights and interests of the principal, the agent and the counterparty shall be jointly and severally liable.

#### Section 2: Agency by Mandate

**Article 165** Where the authorization for agency by mandate is made in writing, the power of attorney shall state the name or title of the agent, the matters of agency, the scope of authority, and the term, and shall be signed or sealed by the principal.

**Article 166** If several persons are agents for the same matter of agency, they shall exercise the agency authority jointly, unless the parties agree otherwise.

**Article 167** If the agent, knowing or having reason to know that the matter of agency is illegal, still performs the agency act, or if the principal, knowing or having reason to know that the agent's act is illegal, fails to object, the principal and the agent shall be jointly and severally liable.

**Article 168** An agent shall not perform a civil juristic act in the name of the principal with itself, unless the principal consents or ratifies it.

An agent shall not perform a civil juristic act in the name of the principal with another person whom it simultaneously represents, unless both principals consent or ratify it.

**Article 169** If an agent needs to sub-entrust a third person to act as agent, the agent shall obtain the consent or ratification of the principal.

If the sub-entrusted agency is consented to or ratified by the principal, the principal may directly give instructions to the sub-entrusted third person regarding the agency matters, and the agent shall be liable only for the selection of the third person and the instructions given to the third person.

If the sub-entrusted agency is not consented to or ratified by the principal, the agent shall be liable for the acts of the sub-entrusted third person; however, this does not apply if the agent needs to sub-entrust a third person in an emergency to protect the interests of the principal.

**Article 170** A civil juristic act performed by a person performing tasks for a legal person or an unincorporated organization, within the scope of its functions and powers, in the name of the legal person or unincorporated organization, is binding on the legal person or unincorporated organization.

Restrictions imposed by a legal person or unincorporated organization on the scope of functions and powers of a person performing tasks for it shall not be asserted against a bona fide counterparty.

**Article 171** If an actor performs an agency act without agency authority, beyond the scope of agency authority, or after the termination of agency authority, and the act is not ratified by the principal, it is not binding on the principal.

The counterparty may urge the principal to ratify the act within thirty days from the date of receipt of the notice. If the principal fails to respond, it is deemed a refusal to ratify. Before the act performed by the actor is ratified, a bona fide counterparty has the right to revoke. The revocation shall be made by notice.

If the act performed by the actor is not ratified, a bona fide counterparty has the right to request the actor to perform the obligation or to compensate for the damage suffered. However, the scope of compensation shall not exceed the benefits the counterparty would have obtained had the principal ratified the act.

If the counterparty knew or should have known that the actor had no agency authority, the counterparty and the actor shall bear liability according to their respective faults.

**Article 172** If an actor performs an agency act without agency authority, beyond the scope of agency authority, or after the termination of agency authority, and the counterparty has reason to believe that the actor has agency authority, the agency act is valid.

#### Section 3: Termination of Agency

**Article 173** Agency by mandate is terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The term of agency expires or the agency matters are completed;
(2) The principal revokes the mandate or the agent resigns from the mandate;
(3) The agent loses capacity for civil conduct;
(4) The agent or the principal dies;
(5) The legal person or unincorporated organization acting as the agent or principal is terminated.

**Article 174** After the death of the principal, the agency act performed by the agent by mandate is valid under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The agent did not know and should not have known of the death of the principal;
(2) The heirs of the principal acknowledge the act;
(3) The authorization clearly states that the agency authority terminates upon completion of the agency matters;
(4) The act was performed before the death of the principal and is continued for the benefit of the principal's heirs.

If the legal person or unincorporated organization acting as the principal is terminated, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply mutatis mutandis.

**Article 175** Agency by operation of law is terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The principal acquires or regains full capacity for civil conduct;
(2) The agent loses capacity for civil conduct;
(3) The agent or the principal dies;
(4) Other circumstances provided by law.

### Chapter VIII: Civil Liability

**Article 176** Civil subjects shall perform civil obligations and bear civil liability in accordance with the provisions of law or the agreement of the parties.

**Article 177** Where two or more persons bear liability in shares according to law, if the extent of liability can be determined, each shall bear corresponding liability; if it is difficult to determine the extent of liability, they shall bear liability equally.

**Article 178** Where two or more persons bear joint and several liability according to law, the obligee has the right to request some or all of the jointly and severally liable persons to bear liability.

The share of liability of a jointly and severally liable person is determined according to the extent of each person's liability; if it is difficult to determine the extent of liability, they shall bear liability equally. A jointly and severally liable person who has actually borne liability exceeding its own share has the right to recourse against the other jointly and severally liable persons.

Joint and several liability shall be provided by law or agreed upon by the parties.

**Article 179** The main methods of bearing civil liability include:

(1) Cessation of infringement;
(2) Removal of obstruction;
(3) Elimination of danger;
(4) Return of property;
(5) Restoration to original condition;
(6) Repair, rework, or replacement;
(7) Continued performance;
(8) Compensation for losses;
(9) Payment of liquidated damages;
(10) Elimination of adverse effects and restoration of reputation;
(11) Apology.

Where the law provides for punitive damages, such provisions shall apply.

The methods of bearing civil liability provided in this Article may be applied individually or in combination.

**Article 180** A person who fails to perform a civil obligation due to force majeure shall not bear civil liability, unless otherwise provided by law.

Force majeure is an objective circumstance that is unforeseeable, unavoidable, and insurmountable.

**Article 181** A person who causes damage by acting in justifiable defense shall not bear civil liability.

If justifiable defense exceeds the necessary limit, causing undue damage, the person acting in justifiable defense shall bear appropriate civil liability.

**Article 182** If damage is caused by an act of necessity to avert an imminent danger, the person who caused the danger shall bear civil liability.

If the danger arises from natural causes, the person acting in necessity shall not bear civil liability but may provide appropriate compensation.

If the measures taken in necessity are improper or exceed the necessary limit, causing undue damage, the person acting in necessity shall bear appropriate civil liability.

**Article 183** If a person suffers damage while protecting the civil rights and interests of another, the tortfeasor shall bear civil liability, and the beneficiary may provide appropriate compensation. If there is no tortfeasor, the tortfeasor has absconded, or the tortfeasor is unable to bear civil liability, and the victim requests compensation, the beneficiary shall provide appropriate compensation.

**Article 184** A person who voluntarily renders emergency aid and causes damage to the aided person shall not bear civil liability.

**Article 185** A person who infringes upon the name, likeness, reputation, or honor of heroes and martyrs, etc., harming the public interest, shall bear civil liability.

**Article 186** If a breach of contract by one party damages the personal or property rights and interests of the other party, the injured party has the right to choose to request the breaching party to bear liability for breach of contract or tort liability.

**Article 187** If a civil subject shall bear civil liability, administrative liability, and criminal liability for the same act, bearing administrative or criminal liability does not affect the bearing of civil liability; if the property of the civil subject is insufficient to pay all liabilities, it shall be used first to bear civil liability.

### Chapter IX: Statute of Limitations

**Article 188** The statute of limitations for requesting protection of civil rights from a people's court is three years, unless otherwise provided by law.

The statute of limitations begins to run from the date the obligee knows or should have known that its rights were damaged and who the obligor is, unless otherwise provided by law. However, if more than twenty years have passed since the date the rights were damaged, the people's court shall not grant protection; under special circumstances, the people's court may, upon application by the obligee, decide to extend the period.

**Article 189** If the parties agree to perform the same debt in installments, the statute of limitations begins to run from the date the last installment's performance period expires.

**Article 190** The statute of limitations for a claim by a person with no capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct against its statutory agent begins to run from the date the statutory agency terminates.

**Article 191** The statute of limitations for a claim for damages arising from sexual assault suffered by a minor begins to run from the date the victim reaches the age of eighteen.

**Article 192** After the statute of limitations expires, the obligor may raise a defense of non-performance.

After the statute of limitations expires, if the obligor agrees to perform, it shall not later raise a defense based on the expiration of the statute of limitations; if the obligor has voluntarily performed, it shall not request restitution.

**Article 193** A people's court shall not apply the provisions on the statute of limitations on its own initiative.

**Article 194** Within the last six months of the statute of limitations, if the obligee is unable to exercise its claim due to the following obstacles, the statute of limitations is suspended:

(1) Force majeure;
(2) The person with no capacity for civil conduct or the person with limited capacity for civil conduct has no statutory agent, or the statutory agent dies, loses capacity for civil conduct, or loses agency authority;
(3) After the commencement of inheritance, no heir or estate administrator has been determined;
(4) The obligee is controlled by the obligor or another person;
(5) Other obstacles causing the obligee to be unable to exercise its claim.

The statute of limitations expires six months after the date the cause of suspension is eliminated.

**Article 195** The statute of limitations is interrupted under any of the following circumstances, and the period begins anew from the time of interruption or the conclusion of the relevant proceedings:

(1) The obligee makes a request for performance to the obligor;
(2) The obligor agrees to perform the obligation;
(3) The obligee initiates a lawsuit or applies for arbitration;
(4) Other circumstances having equivalent effect to initiating a lawsuit or applying for arbitration.

**Article 196** The following claims are not subject to the statute of limitations:

(1) Claims for cessation of infringement, removal of obstruction, or elimination of danger;
(2) Claims by the obligee of a real right in immovable property or a registered movable property for return of property;
(3) Claims for payment of child support, spousal support, or parental support;
(4) Other claims not subject to the statute of limitations according to law.

**Article 197** The period of the statute of limitations, the method of calculation, and the grounds for suspension and interruption shall be provided by law; agreements of the parties to the contrary are void.

Any prior waiver of the benefits of the statute of limitations by the parties is void.

**Article 198** Where the law provides for a limitation period for arbitration, such provisions shall apply; where there is no such provision, the provisions on the statute of limitations shall apply.

**Article 199** The duration of rights such as the right to revoke or the right to rescind, as provided by law or agreed upon by the parties, unless otherwise provided by law, begins to run from the date the obligee knows or should have known that the right arises, and the provisions on the suspension, interruption, and extension of the statute of limitations shall not apply. Upon expiration of the duration, the right to revoke, the right to rescind, and other such rights are extinguished.

### Chapter X: Calculation of Time Periods

**Article 200** Time periods referred to in the Civil Code shall be calculated according to the Gregorian calendar in years, months, days, and hours.

**Article 201** When calculating a time period in years, months, or days, the starting day is not counted; the calculation begins from the following day.

When calculating a time period in hours, the calculation begins from the time provided by law or agreed upon by the parties.

**Article 202** When calculating a time period in years or months, the corresponding day of the expiration month is the last day of the period; if there is no corresponding day, the last day of the month is the last day of the period.

**Article 203** If the last day of a time period falls on a legal holiday, the day following the end of the legal holiday is the last day of the period.

The cut-off time on the last day of a time period is 24:00; if there are business hours, the time when business activities cease is the cut-off time.

**Article 204** The method of calculating time periods shall be in accordance with the provisions of this Code, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed upon by the parties.

### Part One: General Principles

#### Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 205** This Book regulates civil relations arising from the ownership and utilization of things.

**Article 206** The State upholds and improves the basic socialist economic systems, including the system under which public ownership is the mainstay and multiple forms of ownership develop together, the system under which distribution according to work is the mainstay and multiple modes of distribution coexist, and the socialist market economy system.

The State consolidates and develops the public sector of the economy, and encourages, supports, and guides the development of the non-public sector of the economy.

The State practices a socialist market economy and guarantees the equal legal status and development rights of all market entities.

**Article 207** The real rights of the State, collectives, individuals, and other obligees are equally protected by law, and no organization or individual may infringe upon them.

**Article 208** The creation, alteration, transfer, and extinguishment of real rights in immovable property shall be registered in accordance with the provisions of law. The creation and transfer of real rights in movable property shall be delivered in accordance with the provisions of law.

#### Chapter II: Creation, Alteration, Transfer, and Extinguishment of Real Rights

##### Section 1: Registration of Immovable Property

**Article 209** The creation, alteration, transfer, and extinguishment of real rights in immovable property take effect upon registration in accordance with law; without registration, they do not take effect, unless otherwise provided by law.

Ownership of natural resources owned by the State according to law may be exempted from registration.

**Article 210** Registration of immovable property shall be handled by the registration authority at the place where the immovable property is located.

The State implements a unified registration system for immovable property. The scope of unified registration, the registration authority, and the registration methods shall be prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 211** A party applying for registration shall provide, according to the different registration matters, the certificate of title and necessary materials such as the boundaries and area of the immovable property.

**Article 212** The registration authority shall perform the following duties:

(1) Examine the certificate of title and other necessary materials provided by the applicant;
(2) Inquire of the applicant regarding the relevant registration matters;
(3) Register the relevant matters truthfully and promptly;
(4) Other duties prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

If the relevant circumstances of the immovable property for which registration is applied require further verification, the registration authority may request the applicant to supplement materials, and may conduct an on-site inspection when necessary.

**Article 213** The registration authority shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Requiring an appraisal of the immovable property;(2) Conducting duplicate registrations under the pretext of annual inspections, etc.;

(3) Other actions beyond the scope of registration duties.

Article 214: The creation, alteration, transfer, or extinguishment of real property rights that require registration by law shall take effect upon being recorded in the real property register.

Article 215: A contract concluded between parties concerning the creation, alteration, transfer, or extinguishment of real property rights shall take effect upon its formation, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties; failure to register the property right shall not affect the validity of the contract.

Article 216: The real property register is the basis for determining the ownership and content of real property rights.

The real property register shall be managed by the registration authority.

Article 217: The certificate of real property ownership is proof that the right holder enjoys the real property right. The particulars recorded in the certificate of real property ownership shall be consistent with the real property register; in case of inconsistency, the real property register shall prevail, unless there is evidence proving that the real property register is indeed erroneous.

Article 218: Right holders and interested parties may apply to inspect or copy real property registration materials, and the registration authority shall provide them.

Article 219: Interested parties shall not disclose or illegally use the real property registration materials of the right holder.

Article 220: If a right holder or interested party believes that the particulars recorded in the real property register are erroneous, they may apply for a corrective registration. If the right holder recorded in the real property register gives written consent to the correction, or if there is evidence proving that the registration is indeed erroneous, the registration authority shall make the correction.

If the right holder recorded in the real property register does not consent to the correction, the interested party may apply for a dissenting registration. The registration authority shall make the dissenting registration; if the applicant does not file a lawsuit within fifteen days from the date of the dissenting registration, the dissenting registration shall lapse. If the dissenting registration is improper and causes damage to the right holder, the right holder may claim damages from the applicant.

Article 221: Where parties enter into an agreement for the sale of a house or other agreements concerning real property rights, in order to secure the future realization of the property right, they may apply to the registration authority for a advance notice registration in accordance with the agreement. After the advance notice registration, any disposition of the real property without the consent of the right holder under the advance notice registration shall not have the effect of a property right.

After the advance notice registration, if the claim is extinguished or if no application for registration is made within ninety days from the date when the real property registration could be made, the advance notice registration shall lapse.

Article 222: If a party submits false materials in applying for registration, causing damage to others, they shall bear liability for compensation.

If damage is caused to others due to a registration error, the registration authority shall bear liability for compensation. After compensating, the registration authority may seek recourse against the person who caused the registration error.

Article 223: Real property registration fees shall be charged per item and shall not be charged based on the area, volume, or price of the real property.

##### Section 2: Delivery of Movable Property

Article 224: The creation and transfer of a right in movable property shall take effect upon delivery, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 225: The creation, alteration, transfer, or extinguishment of rights in ships, aircraft, motor vehicles, etc., shall not be valid against a bona fide third party if not registered.

Article 226: If, before the creation or transfer of a right in movable property, the right holder is already in possession of the movable property, the property right shall take effect upon the effectiveness of the civil juristic act.

Article 227: If, before the creation or transfer of a right in movable property, a third party is in possession of the movable property, the person obligated to deliver may substitute delivery by transferring the right to claim the return of the original property from the third party.

Article 228: When a right in movable property is transferred, if the parties agree that the transferor shall continue to possess the movable property, the property right shall take effect upon the effectiveness of that agreement.

##### Section 3: Other Provisions

Article 229: Where the creation, alteration, transfer, or extinguishment of a property right is caused by a legal document from a people's court or arbitration institution, or by an expropriation decision of the people's government, etc., it shall take effect upon the effectiveness of such legal document or expropriation decision.

Article 230: Where a property right is acquired through inheritance, it shall take effect upon the commencement of the inheritance.

Article 231: Where a property right is created or extinguished by factual acts such as lawful construction or demolition of a house, it shall take effect upon the completion of such factual act.

Article 232: Where the disposition of a real property right enjoyed under the provisions of this Section requires registration by law, it shall not have the effect of a property right if not registered.

#### Chapter 3: Protection of Property Rights

Article 233: Where a property right is infringed upon, the right holder may seek resolution through reconciliation, mediation, arbitration, or litigation.

Article 234: Where there is a dispute over the ownership or content of a property right, interested parties may request confirmation of the right.

Article 235: Where a person is in unauthorized possession of real or movable property, the right holder may request the return of the original property.

Article 236: Where a person obstructs or may obstruct a property right, the right holder may request the removal of the obstruction or the elimination of the danger.

Article 237: Where real or movable property is damaged, the right holder may, in accordance with the law, request repair, re-making, replacement, or restoration to its original state.

Article 238: Where a property right is infringed upon, causing damage to the right holder, the right holder may, in accordance with the law, claim damages or request the assumption of other civil liability.

Article 239: The methods of protecting property rights provided in this Chapter may be applied individually or in combination, depending on the circumstances of the infringement.

### Part Two: Ownership

#### Chapter 4: General Provisions

Article 240: The owner has the right to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of his or her own real or movable property in accordance with the law.

Article 241: The owner has the right to establish a usufructuary right or a security right on his or her own real or movable property. The usufructuary or security right holder, when exercising their rights, shall not harm the rights and interests of the owner.

Article 242: Real and movable property that is exclusively owned by the state as provided by law shall not be acquired as ownership by any organization or individual.

Article 243: For the needs of the public interest, land owned by collectives and houses and other real property owned by organizations and individuals may be expropriated in accordance with the authority and procedures provided by law.

When expropriating land owned by collectives, land compensation fees, resettlement subsidies, and compensation for rural villagers' houses, other above-ground attachments, and young crops shall be paid in full and in a timely manner in accordance with the law, and social security expenses for the farmers whose land is expropriated shall be arranged to ensure their livelihood and protect their legitimate rights and interests.

When expropriating houses and other real property owned by organizations or individuals, expropriation compensation shall be given in accordance with the law to protect the legitimate rights and interests of the expropriated persons; when expropriating individual residential houses, the living conditions of the expropriated persons shall also be guaranteed.

No organization or individual may embezzle, misappropriate, privately distribute, withhold, or default on expropriation compensation fees, etc.

Article 244: The state provides special protection for arable land, strictly restricts the conversion of agricultural land into construction land, and controls the total amount of construction land. Land owned by collectives shall not be expropriated in violation of the authority and procedures provided by law.

Article 245: For urgent needs such as disaster relief, epidemic prevention and control, etc., the real or movable property of organizations or individuals may be requisitioned in accordance with the authority and procedures provided by law. The requisitioned real or movable property shall be returned to the requisitioned person after use. If the real or movable property of an organization or individual is requisitioned or is damaged or lost after requisition, compensation shall be given.

#### Chapter 5: State Ownership, Collective Ownership, and Private Ownership

Article 246: Property owned by the state as provided by law belongs to the state, i.e., the whole people.

State-owned property shall be exercised by the State Council on behalf of the state. If the law provides otherwise, that provision shall prevail.

Article 247: Mineral deposits, waters, and sea areas are owned by the state.

Article 248: Uninhabited islands are owned by the state, and the State Council exercises ownership of uninhabited islands on behalf of the state.

Article 249: Land in cities is owned by the state. Land in rural areas and suburban areas that is owned by the state as provided by law belongs to the state.

Article 250: Natural resources such as forests, mountains, grasslands, wastelands, and tidal flats are owned by the state, except those that are owned by collectives as provided by law.

Article 251: Wildlife resources that are owned by the state as provided by law belong to the state.

Article 252: Radio spectrum resources are owned by the state.

Article 253: Cultural relics that are owned by the state as provided by law belong to the state.

Article 254: National defense assets are owned by the state.

Infrastructure such as railways, highways, power facilities, telecommunications facilities, and oil and gas pipelines, which are owned by the state as provided by law, belongs to the state.

Article 255: State organs have the right to possess, use, and dispose of the real and movable property under their direct control in accordance with the law and the relevant regulations of the State Council.

Article 256: Public institutions established by the state have the right to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of the real and movable property under their direct control in accordance with the law and the relevant regulations of the State Council.

Article 257: For enterprises invested in by the state, the State Council and local people's governments shall, in accordance with the law and administrative regulations, respectively perform the duties of investors on behalf of the state and enjoy the rights and interests of investors.

Article 258: State-owned property is protected by law, and no organization or individual may encroach upon, loot, privately distribute, withhold, or destroy it.

Article 259: Institutions and their staff members responsible for the management and supervision of state-owned property shall strengthen the management and supervision of state-owned property in accordance with the law, promote the preservation and appreciation of state-owned property, and prevent losses of state-owned property; those who abuse their power or neglect their duties, causing losses to state-owned property, shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law.

Those who violate state-owned property management regulations, such as transferring property at low prices, conspiring to privately distribute property, providing unauthorized guarantees, or otherwise causing losses to state-owned property during enterprise restructuring, merger or division, related-party transactions, etc., shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 260: Real and movable property owned by a collective includes:

(1) Land and forests, mountains, grasslands, wastelands, and tidal flats that are owned by the collective as provided by law;

(2) Buildings, production facilities, and farmland water conservancy facilities owned by the collective;

(3) Educational, scientific, cultural, health, and sports facilities owned by the collective;

(4) Other real and movable property owned by the collective.

Article 261: Real and movable property owned by a farmers' collective belongs to the members of that collective collectively.

The following matters shall be decided by the members of the collective in accordance with legal procedures:

(1) The plan for contracting land and the contracting of land to organizations or individuals outside the collective;

(2) The adjustment of contracted land between individual land contract operators;

(3) The use and distribution of land compensation fees, etc.;

(4) Changes in the ownership of enterprises invested in by the collective;

(5) Other matters provided by law.

Article 262: For land and forests, mountains, grasslands, wastelands, and tidal flats owned by a collective, ownership shall be exercised in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) If it belongs to a village farmers' collective, the village collective economic organization or the villagers' committee shall exercise ownership on behalf of the collective in accordance with the law;

(2) If it belongs to two or more farmers' collectives within a village, the respective collective economic organizations or villagers' groups within the village shall exercise ownership on behalf of the collective in accordance with the law;

(3) If it belongs to a township farmers' collective, the township collective economic organization shall exercise ownership on behalf of the collective.

Article 263: For real and movable property owned by an urban collective, the collective shall enjoy the rights to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of it in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 264: Rural collective economic organizations, villagers' committees, or villagers' groups shall disclose the status of collective property to the members of the collective in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, articles of association, and village rules and regulations. Collective members have the right to inspect and copy relevant materials.

Article 265: Property owned by a collective is protected by law, and no organization or individual may encroach upon, loot, privately distribute, or destroy it.

If a decision made by a rural collective economic organization, villagers' committee, or their responsible person infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of a collective member, the infringed collective member may request the people's court to revoke it.

Article 266: An individual has ownership of his or her lawful income, houses, daily necessities, production tools, raw materials, and other real and movable property.

Article 267: The lawful property of an individual is protected by law, and no organization or individual may encroach upon, loot, or destroy it.

Article 268: The state, collectives, and individuals may, in accordance with the law, invest in the establishment of limited liability companies, joint-stock limited companies, or other enterprises. Where real or movable property owned by the state, a collective, or an individual is invested in an enterprise, the investor shall enjoy rights such as asset returns, major decisions, and selection of business managers, and perform obligations in accordance with the agreement or the proportion of investment.

Article 269: A for-profit legal person has the right to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of its real and movable property in accordance with the law, administrative regulations, and its articles of association.

For legal persons other than for-profit legal persons, their rights to real and movable property shall be governed by the relevant laws, administrative regulations, and articles of association.

Article 270: Real and movable property lawfully owned by social organization legal persons and donation legal persons is protected by law.

#### Chapter 6: Condominium Ownership of Buildings

Article 271: An owner has ownership of the exclusive parts of a building, such as residential units and commercial units, and has the right to co-ownership and joint management of the common parts outside the exclusive parts.

Article 272: An owner has the right to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of the exclusive parts of his or her building. When exercising rights, the owner shall not endanger the safety of the building or harm the legitimate rights and interests of other owners.

Article 273: An owner has rights and bears obligations with respect to the common parts of the building outside the exclusive parts; the owner shall not refuse to perform obligations on the ground of waiving rights.

When an owner transfers a residential unit or commercial unit in the building, the rights to co-ownership and joint management of the common parts shall be transferred together.

Article 274: Roads within the building area are owned jointly by the owners, except for urban public roads. Green spaces within the building area are owned jointly by the owners, except for urban public green spaces or those explicitly indicated as belonging to individuals. Other public places, public facilities, and property service premises within the building area are owned jointly by the owners.

Article 275: The ownership of parking spaces and garages planned for parking motor vehicles within the building area shall be agreed upon by the parties through sale, gift, or lease.

Parking spaces used for parking motor vehicles that occupy roads or other sites jointly owned by the owners belong to the owners jointly.

Article 276: Parking spaces and garages planned for parking motor vehicles within the building area shall first meet the needs of the owners.

Article 277: Owners may establish an owners' assembly and elect an owners' committee. The specific conditions and procedures for the establishment of an owners' assembly and an owners' committee shall be in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.

The relevant departments of local people's governments and residents' committees shall provide guidance and assistance for the establishment of an owners' assembly and the election of an owners' committee.

Article 278: The following matters shall be decided jointly by the owners:

(1) Formulating and amending the rules of procedure of the owners' assembly;

(2) Formulating and amending the management regulations;

(3) Electing or replacing members of the owners' committee;

(4) Appointing and dismissing property service enterprises or other managers;

(5) Using maintenance funds for the building and its ancillary facilities;

(6) Raising maintenance funds for the building and its ancillary facilities;

(7) Altering or rebuilding the building and its ancillary facilities;

(8) Changing the use of common parts or using common parts for business activities;

(9) Other major matters concerning co-ownership and joint management rights.

For matters decided jointly by the owners, owners representing at least two-thirds of the total area of exclusive parts and at least two-thirds of the total number of owners shall participate in the vote. For decisions on matters specified in items (6) to (8) of the preceding paragraph, the consent of owners representing at least three-quarters of the area of exclusive parts of the participating owners and at least three-quarters of the number of participating owners shall be obtained. For decisions on other matters specified in the preceding paragraph, the consent of owners representing more than half of the area of exclusive parts of the participating owners and more than half of the number of participating owners shall be obtained.

Article 279: An owner shall not change a residential unit into a commercial unit in violation of laws, regulations, and management regulations. If an owner changes a residential unit into a commercial unit, in addition to complying with laws, regulations, and management regulations, the unanimous consent of the interested owners shall be obtained.

Article 280: Decisions made by the owners' assembly or the owners' committee are legally binding on the owners.

If a decision made by the owners' assembly or the owners' committee infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of an owner, the infringed owner may request the people's court to revoke it.

Article 281: Maintenance funds for the building and its ancillary facilities belong to the owners jointly. With the joint decision of the owners, they may be used for the repair, renewal, and renovation of common parts such as elevators, roofs, exterior walls, and barrier-free facilities. The collection and use of maintenance funds for the building and its ancillary facilities shall be disclosed periodically.

In an emergency requiring the repair of the building and its ancillary facilities, the owners' assembly or the owners' committee may, in accordance with the law, apply for the use of the maintenance funds for the building and its ancillary facilities.

Article 282: Income generated by the construction unit, property service enterprise, or other manager from the use of the common parts of the owners, after deducting reasonable costs, belongs to the owners jointly.

Article 283: Matters such as the apportionment of expenses and the distribution of income for the building and its ancillary facilities shall be in accordance with the agreement; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, it shall be determined based on the proportion of the area of the exclusive parts owned by each owner.

Article 284: Owners may manage the building and its ancillary facilities themselves, or they may entrust a property service enterprise or other manager to manage them.

Owners have the right to replace the property service enterprise or other manager appointed by the construction unit in accordance with the law.

Article 285: A property service enterprise or other manager shall, based on the entrustment of the owners, manage the building and its ancillary facilities within the building area in accordance with the provisions of Part Three of this Code concerning property service contracts, accept the supervision of the owners, and promptly respond to inquiries from the owners regarding property services.

A property service enterprise or other manager shall implement emergency response measures and other management measures implemented by the government in accordance with the law and actively cooperate in carrying out related work.

Article 286: Owners shall comply with laws, regulations, and management regulations, and their relevant actions shall meet the requirements of resource conservation and ecological environment protection. Owners shall cooperate in accordance with the law with property service enterprises or other managers in implementing emergency response measures and other management measures implemented by the government in accordance with the law.The owners' assembly or the owners' committee has the right, in accordance with laws, regulations, and the management covenant, to request that a person who engages in acts harmful to the legitimate rights and interests of others, such as arbitrarily discarding garbage, discharging pollutants or noise, raising animals in violation of regulations, constructing illegally, encroaching on passageways, or refusing to pay property management fees, cease the infringement, remove the obstruction, eliminate the danger, restore the property to its original condition, and compensate for losses.

If the owner or other actor refuses to perform the relevant obligations, the interested party may report or complain to the relevant administrative department, which shall handle the matter in accordance with the law.

**Article 287** Where a developer, property service enterprise, other manager, or other owner infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of an owner, the owner has the right to request that they bear civil liability.

### Chapter VII: Neighboring Relations

**Article 288** Neighboring right holders of immovable property shall properly handle neighboring relations in accordance with the principles of facilitating production, making life convenient, promoting unity and mutual assistance, and ensuring fairness and reasonableness.

**Article 289** Where laws or regulations provide for the handling of neighboring relations, those provisions shall apply; where there are no such provisions, local customs may be followed.

**Article 290** A right holder of immovable property shall provide necessary convenience to neighboring right holders for the use and drainage of water.

The use of natural water flow shall be reasonably allocated among neighboring right holders of immovable property. The drainage of natural water flow shall respect its natural direction.

**Article 291** Where a neighboring right holder must use the land of a right holder of immovable property for passage or other necessary purposes, the right holder shall provide necessary convenience.

**Article 292** Where a right holder of immovable property must use neighboring land or buildings for constructing or repairing buildings, or laying wires, cables, water pipes, heating pipes, or gas pipelines, the right holder of such land or buildings shall provide necessary convenience.

**Article 293** The construction of buildings shall not violate national engineering construction standards, nor shall it obstruct the ventilation, lighting, or sunlight of neighboring buildings.

**Article 294** A right holder of immovable property shall not, in violation of national regulations, discard solid waste, or discharge harmful substances such as atmospheric pollutants, water pollutants, soil pollutants, noise, light radiation, or electromagnetic radiation.

**Article 295** When excavating land, constructing buildings, laying pipelines, or installing equipment, a right holder of immovable property shall not endanger the safety of neighboring immovable property.

**Article 296** When using neighboring immovable property for water use, drainage, passage, or laying pipelines, a right holder of immovable property shall avoid causing damage to the neighboring right holder of immovable property as much as possible.

### Chapter VIII: Co-ownership

**Article 297** Immovable or movable property may be co-owned by two or more organizations or individuals. Co-ownership includes co-ownership by shares and joint co-ownership.

**Article 298** A co-owner by shares enjoys ownership rights over the co-owned immovable or movable property in proportion to their share.

**Article 299** Joint co-owners jointly enjoy ownership rights over the co-owned immovable or movable property.

**Article 300** Co-owners shall manage the co-owned immovable or movable property in accordance with their agreement; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, each co-owner has the right and obligation to manage the property.

**Article 301** The disposal of co-owned immovable or movable property, or major repairs, changes in nature or use of such property, shall require the consent of co-owners by shares holding more than two-thirds of the shares, or all joint co-owners, unless otherwise agreed among the co-owners.

**Article 302** The management expenses and other burdens of the co-owned property shall be borne as agreed among the co-owners; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, co-owners by shares shall bear them in proportion to their shares, and joint co-owners shall bear them jointly.

**Article 303** Where co-owners have agreed not to partition the co-owned immovable or movable property in order to maintain the co-ownership relationship, the agreement shall be followed, unless a co-owner has a major reason requiring partition, in which case partition may be requested; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, a co-owner by shares may request partition at any time, and a joint co-owner may request partition when the basis for joint co-ownership is lost or there is a major reason requiring partition. If partition causes damage to other co-owners, compensation shall be made.

**Article 304** Co-owners may negotiate to determine the method of partition. If an agreement cannot be reached, and the co-owned immovable or movable property can be partitioned without diminishing its value, the property shall be partitioned in kind; if it is difficult to partition or partition would diminish its value, the proceeds from a discounted sale or auction shall be partitioned.

If the immovable or movable property obtained by a co-owner upon partition has defects, the other co-owners shall share the losses.

**Article 305** A co-owner by shares may transfer their share of the co-owned immovable or movable property. Other co-owners have a preemptive right to purchase under the same conditions.

**Article 306** When a co-owner by shares transfers their share of the co-owned immovable or movable property, they shall promptly notify the other co-owners of the transfer conditions. The other co-owners shall exercise their preemptive right within a reasonable period.

If two or more other co-owners claim the preemptive right, they shall negotiate to determine their respective purchase proportions; if negotiation fails, they shall exercise the preemptive right in proportion to their respective co-ownership shares at the time of transfer.

**Article 307** With respect to claims and debts arising from co-owned immovable or movable property, in external relations, the co-owners shall have joint claims and bear joint debts, unless otherwise provided by law or the third party is aware that the co-owners do not have joint claims or debts; in internal relations among the co-owners, unless otherwise agreed, co-owners by shares shall enjoy claims and bear debts in proportion to their shares, and joint co-owners shall jointly enjoy claims and bear debts. A co-owner by shares who has repaid debts exceeding their share has the right to seek recourse from the other co-owners.

**Article 308** Where co-owners have not agreed whether the co-ownership is by shares or joint, or the agreement is unclear, unless the co-owners have a family relationship or similar, the co-ownership shall be deemed to be co-ownership by shares.

**Article 309** Where the share of a co-owner by shares in the co-owned immovable or movable property is not agreed upon or the agreement is unclear, it shall be determined based on the amount of capital contribution; if the capital contribution cannot be determined, the shares shall be deemed equal.

**Article 310** Where two or more organizations or individuals jointly enjoy usufructuary rights or security rights, the relevant provisions of this Chapter shall apply mutatis mutandis.

### Chapter IX: Special Provisions on the Acquisition of Ownership

**Article 311** Where a person without the right of disposition transfers immovable or movable property to a transferee, the owner has the right to recover the property; unless otherwise provided by law, if the following conditions are met, the transferee acquires ownership of the immovable or movable property:

(1) The transferee was in good faith at the time of the transfer of the immovable or movable property;

(2) The transfer was made at a reasonable price;

(3) The transferred immovable or movable property, if required by law to be registered, has been registered, or if not required, has been delivered to the transferee.

Where the transferee acquires ownership of the immovable or movable property in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the original owner has the right to claim damages from the person without the right of disposition.

Where a party acquires other real rights in good faith, the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall apply mutatis mutandis.

**Article 312** The owner or other right holder has the right to recover lost property. If the lost property is possessed by another person through transfer, the right holder has the right to claim damages from the person without the right of disposition, or within two years from the date they knew or should have known of the transferee, request the transferee to return the original property; however, if the transferee purchased the lost property through auction or from a qualified operator, the right holder, when requesting the return of the original property, shall pay the expenses incurred by the transferee. After paying the expenses to the transferee, the right holder has the right to seek recourse from the person without the right of disposition.

**Article 313** After a bona fide transferee acquires movable property, the original rights on the movable property are extinguished, unless the bona fide transferee knew or should have known of such rights at the time of the transfer.

**Article 314** A finder of lost property shall return it to the right holder. The finder shall promptly notify the right holder to claim it, or deliver it to the public security authorities or other relevant departments.

**Article 315** Upon receiving lost property, if the relevant department knows the right holder, it shall promptly notify them to claim it; if it does not know, it shall promptly publish a notice for claiming.

**Article 316** Before the finder delivers the lost property to the relevant department, and before the relevant department releases the lost property for claiming, they shall take proper care of the lost property. If the lost property is damaged, destroyed, or lost due to intentional or gross negligence, civil liability shall be borne.

**Article 317** When the right holder claims the lost property, they shall pay the finder or the relevant department the necessary expenses incurred for the care of the lost property.

If the right holder has offered a reward for finding the lost property, they shall fulfill their promise when claiming the lost property.

If the finder misappropriates the lost property, they have no right to claim expenses for the care of the lost property, nor to request the right holder to fulfill their promise.

**Article 318** If lost property is not claimed within one year from the date of publication of the notice for claiming, it shall become state property.

**Article 319** The provisions on finding lost property shall apply mutatis mutandis to finding drifting objects, discovering buried objects, or hidden objects. If laws provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

**Article 320** When the principal property is transferred, the accessory property shall be transferred together with it, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

**Article 321** Natural fruits shall be acquired by the owner; if there is both an owner and a usufructuary right holder, they shall be acquired by the usufructuary right holder. If the parties have agreed otherwise, that agreement shall prevail.

Legal fruits shall be acquired as agreed by the parties; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, they shall be acquired in accordance with trade practices.

**Article 322** The ownership of property resulting from processing, attachment, or mixing shall be determined as agreed by the parties; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, it shall be determined in accordance with the law; if the law does not provide for it, it shall be determined based on the principle of maximizing the utility of the property and protecting the innocent party. If damage is caused to another party due to the fault of one party or the determination of ownership, compensation or indemnification shall be given.

### Subpart III: Usufructuary Rights

#### Chapter X: General Provisions

**Article 323** A usufructuary right holder has the right to possess, use, and benefit from the immovable or movable property owned by others in accordance with the law.

**Article 324** Organizations and individuals may, in accordance with the law, possess, use, and benefit from natural resources that are owned by the state, or owned by the state but used by collectives, or that are owned by collectives as provided by law.

**Article 325** The state implements a system of paid use of natural resources, unless otherwise provided by law.

**Article 326** When exercising their rights, a usufructuary right holder shall comply with laws and regulations on the protection and rational development and utilization of resources, and on the protection of the ecological environment. The owner shall not interfere with the exercise of rights by the usufructuary right holder.

**Article 327** If the usufructuary right is extinguished or its exercise is affected due to the expropriation or requisition of immovable or movable property, the usufructuary right holder has the right to obtain corresponding compensation in accordance with Articles 243 and 245 of this Code.

**Article 328** The right to use sea areas obtained in accordance with the law is protected by law.

**Article 329** The rights to explore, mine, and extract water, as well as the right to use waters and tidal flats for aquaculture and fishing, obtained in accordance with the law, are protected by law.

#### Chapter XI: Land Contractual Management Rights

**Article 330** Rural collective economic organizations implement a two-tier management system based on household contractual management, combining unified and decentralized management.

For cultivated land, forestland, grassland, and other agricultural land owned by peasant collectives or owned by the state but used by peasant collectives, a land contractual management system shall be implemented in accordance with the law.

**Article 331** A land contractual management right holder has the right to possess, use, and benefit from the cultivated land, forestland, grassland, etc., that they contractually manage in accordance with the law, and has the right to engage in agricultural production such as crop cultivation, forestry, and animal husbandry.

**Article 332** The contract period for cultivated land is thirty years. The contract period for grassland is thirty to fifty years. The contract period for forestland is thirty to seventy years.

Upon the expiration of the contract period specified in the preceding paragraph, the land contractual management right holder shall continue the contract in accordance with the provisions of the law on rural land contracts.

**Article 333** The land contractual management right is established when the land contractual management contract takes effect.

The registration authority shall issue certificates such as the land contractual management right certificate and the forestland right certificate to the land contractual management right holder, and shall register and record them to confirm the land contractual management right.

**Article 334** A land contractual management right holder has the right to exchange or transfer their land contractual management right in accordance with the law. Without approval in accordance with the law, contracted land shall not be used for non-agricultural construction.

**Article 335** When a land contractual management right is exchanged or transferred, the parties may apply to the registration authority for registration; without registration, it shall not be used to oppose a bona fide third party.

**Article 336** During the contract period, the grantor shall not adjust the contracted land.

If special circumstances, such as severe damage to the contracted land due to natural disasters, require appropriate adjustment of the contracted cultivated land and grassland, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the law on rural land contracts.

**Article 337** During the contract period, the grantor shall not take back the contracted land. If the law provides otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

**Article 338** If the contracted land is expropriated, the land contractual management right holder has the right to obtain corresponding compensation in accordance with Article 243 of this Code.

**Article 339** A land contractual management right holder may independently decide to transfer the land management right to others by means such as leasing, shareholding, or other methods in accordance with the law.

**Article 340** A land management right holder has the right to possess rural land within the period agreed in the contract, independently carry out agricultural production and operations, and obtain benefits.

**Article 341** A land management right with a transfer period of five years or more is established when the transfer contract takes effect. The parties may apply to the registration authority for registration of the land management right; without registration, it shall not be used to oppose a bona fide third party.

**Article 342** Where rural land is contracted through methods such as bidding, auction, or public negotiation, and a title certificate is obtained after registration in accordance with the law, the land management right may be transferred by means such as leasing, shareholding, mortgage, or other methods in accordance with the law.

**Article 343** Where agricultural land owned by the state is subject to contractual management, the relevant provisions of this Subpart shall apply mutatis mutandis.

#### Chapter XII: Right to Use Construction Land

**Article 344** A right holder of the right to use construction land has the right to possess, use, and benefit from land owned by the state, and has the right to use the land to construct buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities.

**Article 345** The right to use construction land may be established separately on the surface, above ground, or underground of the land.

**Article 346** The establishment of the right to use construction land shall comply with the requirements of resource conservation and ecological environment protection, abide by the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on land use purposes, and shall not harm existing usufructuary rights.

**Article 347** The right to use construction land may be established through methods such as transfer or allocation.

For commercial land such as industrial, commercial, tourism, entertainment, and commercial residential land, as well as land for which there are two or more intended users, the transfer shall be conducted through public bidding methods such as auction or bidding.

The establishment of the right to use construction land through allocation shall be strictly restricted.

**Article 348** Where the right to use construction land is established through transfer methods such as bidding, auction, or agreement, the parties shall enter into a written contract for the transfer of the right to use construction land.

The contract for the transfer of the right to use construction land generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The names and domiciles of the parties;

(2) The boundaries and area of the land;

(3) The space occupied by buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities;

(4) The land use purpose and planning conditions;

(5) The term of the right to use construction land;

(6) The transfer fee and other fees and their payment methods;

(7) The methods for dispute resolution.

**Article 349** To establish the right to use construction land, an application for registration of the right to use construction land shall be made to the registration authority. The right to use construction land is established upon registration. The registration authority shall issue a title certificate to the right holder of the right to use construction land.

**Article 350** A right holder of the right to use construction land shall use the land rationally and shall not change the land use purpose; if the land use purpose needs to be changed, approval shall be obtained from the relevant administrative department in accordance with the law.

**Article 351** A right holder of the right to use construction land shall pay the transfer fee and other fees in accordance with the provisions of the law and the contract.

**Article 352** The ownership of buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities constructed by the right holder of the right to use construction land belongs to the right holder of the right to use construction land, unless there is evidence to the contrary.

**Article 353** A right holder of the right to use construction land has the right to transfer, exchange, contribute as capital, donate, or mortgage the right to use construction land, unless otherwise provided by law.

**Article 354** When the right to use construction land is transferred, exchanged, contributed as capital, donated, or mortgaged, the parties shall enter into a written contract accordingly. The term of use shall be agreed upon by the parties, but shall not exceed the remaining term of the right to use construction land.

**Article 355** When the right to use construction land is transferred, exchanged, contributed as capital, or donated, an application for change of registration shall be made to the registration authority.

**Article 356** When the right to use construction land is transferred, exchanged, contributed as capital, or donated, the buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities attached to the land shall be disposed of together.

**Article 357** When buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities are transferred, exchanged, contributed as capital, or donated, the right to use construction land within the area occupied by such buildings, structures, and their ancillary facilities shall be disposed of together.

**Article 358** Before the expiration of the term of the right to use construction land, if the land needs to be taken back in advance for the needs of public interest, compensation shall be given for the houses and other immovable property on the land in accordance with Article 243 of this Code, and the corresponding transfer fee shall be refunded.

**Article 359** Upon the expiration of the term of the right to use residential construction land, it shall be automatically renewed. The payment or reduction of the renewal fee shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

The renewal of the right to use non-residential construction land after the expiration of its term shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the law. The ownership of the houses and other immovable property on the land shall be as agreed; in the absence of an agreement or if the agreement is unclear, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 360** When the right to use construction land is extinguished, the grantor shall promptly handle the cancellation of registration. The registration authority shall take back the title certificate.

**Article 361** Where collectively owned land is used as construction land, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the law on land administration.

#### Chapter XIII: Right to Use Residential Land

**Article 362** A right holder of the right to use residential land has the right to possess and use collectively owned land in accordance with the law, and has the right to use the land to construct houses and their ancillary facilities in accordance with the law.

**Article 363** The acquisition, exercise, and transfer of the right to use residential land shall be governed by the law on land administration and relevant state regulations.Article 364 If a house site is lost due to natural disasters or other reasons, the right to the use of the house site shall be extinguished. For villagers who have lost their house sites, new house sites shall be allocated in accordance with the law.

Article 365 Where a registered right to the use of a house site is transferred or extinguished, the registration of modification or cancellation shall be handled in a timely manner.

#### Chapter 14 Right of Habitation

Article 366 A right holder of habitation is entitled to possess and use another person's residential house according to the contract, to satisfy the needs of living and habitation.

Article 367 To establish a right of habitation, the parties shall conclude a contract of habitation in writing.

A contract of habitation generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The name or title and domicile of the parties;

(2) The location of the residential house;

(3) The conditions and requirements for habitation;

(4) The term of the right of habitation;

(5) The method for dispute resolution.

Article 368 A right of habitation is established gratuitously, unless otherwise agreed by the parties. Where a right of habitation is established, an application for registration of the right of habitation shall be made to the registration authority. The right of habitation is established upon registration.

Article 369 A right of habitation shall not be transferred or inherited. The residential house on which a right of habitation is established shall not be leased, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 370 A right of habitation is extinguished upon the expiration of its term or the death of the right holder. Where a right of habitation is extinguished, the cancellation registration shall be handled in a timely manner.

Article 371 Where a right of habitation is established by will, the relevant provisions of this Chapter shall apply mutatis mutandis.

#### Chapter 15 Easement

Article 372 An easement holder is entitled to use another person's immovable property according to the contract, to enhance the utility of his own immovable property.

The immovable property of another person mentioned in the preceding paragraph is the servient tenement, and his own immovable property is the dominant tenement.

Article 373 To establish an easement, the parties shall conclude an easement contract in writing.

An easement contract generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The name or title and domicile of the parties;

(2) The location of the servient tenement and the dominant tenement;

(3) The purpose and method of use;

(4) The term of the easement;

(5) The fees and the method of payment;

(6) The method for dispute resolution.

Article 374 An easement is established upon the entry into effect of the easement contract. If the parties request registration, they may apply for registration of the easement with the registration authority; without registration, it shall not be valid against a bona fide third party.

Article 375 The holder of the servient tenement shall, in accordance with the contract, allow the easement holder to use his immovable property and shall not obstruct the exercise of the right by the easement holder.

Article 376 The easement holder shall use the servient tenement in accordance with the purpose and method agreed in the contract, and shall minimize the restriction on the property rights of the holder of the servient tenement.

Article 377 The term of an easement shall be agreed upon by the parties; however, it shall not exceed the remaining term of the usufructuary right such as the right to land contractual management or the right to the use of construction land.

Article 378 Where the owner of the land enjoys or bears an easement, when establishing usufructuary rights such as the right to land contractual management or the right to the use of a house site, the usufructuary right holder shall continue to enjoy or bear the easement already established.

Article 379 Where usufructuary rights such as the right to land contractual management, the right to the use of construction land, or the right to the use of a house site have been established on the land, the owner of the land shall not establish an easement without the consent of the usufructuary right holder.

Article 380 An easement shall not be transferred separately. When the right to land contractual management or the right to the use of construction land is transferred, the easement shall be transferred together, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

Article 381 An easement shall not be mortgaged separately. When the right to land management or the right to the use of construction land is mortgaged, the easement shall be transferred together upon the realization of the mortgage.

Article 382 Where the dominant tenement and the right to land contractual management or the right to the use of construction land on it are partially transferred, if the transferred part involves the easement, the transferee shall simultaneously enjoy the easement.

Article 383 Where the servient tenement and the right to land contractual management or the right to the use of construction land on it are partially transferred, if the transferred part involves the easement, the easement shall be legally binding on the transferee.

Article 384 Where an easement holder commits any of the following acts, the holder of the servient tenement has the right to rescind the easement contract, and the easement shall be extinguished:

(1) Abusing the easement in violation of legal provisions or the contract;

(2) Where the use of the servient tenement is for compensation, failing to pay the fees within a reasonable period after the expiration of the agreed payment term following two demands.

Article 385 Where a registered easement is modified, transferred, or extinguished, the registration of modification or cancellation shall be handled in a timely manner.

### Subpart 4 Security Interests in Property

#### Chapter 16 General Provisions

Article 386 Where a debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due, or where the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the security interest occur, the security interest holder is entitled to be paid in priority out of the secured property in accordance with the law, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 387 Where a creditor needs security to secure the realization of his claim in civil activities such as lending or sale, he may establish a security interest in property in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other laws.

Where a third party provides security to the creditor for the debtor, it may require the debtor to provide counter-security. The provisions of this Law and other laws shall apply to counter-security.

Article 388 To establish a security interest in property, a security contract shall be concluded in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other laws. Security contracts include mortgage contracts, pledge contracts, and other contracts with a security function. A security contract is an ancillary contract to the principal claim-obligation contract. If the principal claim-obligation contract is void, the security contract shall be void, unless otherwise provided by law.

After a security contract is confirmed to be void, if the debtor, the surety, or the creditor is at fault, they shall bear corresponding civil liability according to their respective fault.

Article 389 The scope of security of a security interest in property includes the principal claim and its interest, liquidated damages, damages for loss, the cost of preserving the secured property, and the cost of realizing the security interest. If the parties have agreed otherwise, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 390 During the period of security, if the secured property is damaged, lost, or expropriated, the security interest holder may be paid in priority out of the insurance proceeds, compensation, or damages received. If the performance period of the secured claim has not yet expired, the insurance proceeds, compensation, or damages may also be deposited.

Article 391 Where a third party provides security, if the creditor allows the debtor to transfer all or part of the debt without the written consent of the surety, the surety shall no longer bear the corresponding security liability.

Article 392 Where a secured claim is secured by both a security interest in property and a personal security, and the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the security interest occur, the creditor shall realize the claim in accordance with the agreement; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and the debtor himself provides the security interest in property, the creditor shall first realize the claim against the security interest in property; if a third party provides the security interest in property, the creditor may realize the claim against the security interest in property or request the surety to bear the suretyship liability. After the third party providing the security bears the security liability, it has the right to recourse against the debtor.

Article 393 A security interest in property is extinguished under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The principal claim is extinguished;

(2) The security interest in property is realized;

(3) The creditor waives the security interest in property;

(4) Other circumstances provided by law for the extinguishment of a security interest in property.

#### Chapter 17 Mortgage

##### Section 1 General Mortgage

Article 394 To secure the performance of an obligation, if the debtor or a third party mortgages his property to the creditor without transferring possession of the property, and the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur, the creditor is entitled to be paid in priority out of that property.

The debtor or third party mentioned in the preceding paragraph is the mortgagor, the creditor is the mortgagee, and the property provided as security is the mortgaged property.

Article 395 The following property, which the debtor or a third party has the right to dispose of, may be mortgaged:

(1) Buildings and other fixtures on the land;

(2) The right to the use of construction land;

(3) The right to the use of sea areas;

(4) Production equipment, raw materials, semi-finished products, and finished products;

(5) Buildings, ships, and aircraft under construction;

(6) Means of transport;

(7) Other property not prohibited from being mortgaged by laws or administrative regulations.

The mortgagor may mortgage all the property listed in the preceding paragraph together.

Article 396 An enterprise, individual industrial or commercial household, or agricultural producer or operator may mortgage its existing and future production equipment, raw materials, semi-finished products, and finished products. If the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur, the creditor is entitled to be paid in priority out of the movable property as determined at the time of the mortgage.

Article 397 Where a building is mortgaged, the right to the use of the construction land within the area occupied by the building shall be mortgaged together. Where the right to the use of construction land is mortgaged, the buildings on the land shall be mortgaged together.

If the mortgagor fails to mortgage the property together in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the unmortgaged property shall be deemed to be mortgaged together.

Article 398 The right to the use of construction land of a township or village enterprise shall not be mortgaged separately. Where a factory building or other building of a township or village enterprise is mortgaged, the right to the use of the construction land within the area occupied by the building shall be mortgaged together.

Article 399 The following property shall not be mortgaged:

(1) Land ownership;

(2) The right to the use of land owned by the collective, such as house sites, private plots, and private hills, except where the law provides that they may be mortgaged;

(3) Educational facilities, medical and health facilities, and other public welfare facilities of non-profit legal persons established for public welfare purposes, such as schools, kindergartens, and medical institutions;

(4) Property whose ownership or right to use is unclear or disputed;

(5) Property that has been sealed up, seized, or placed under supervision in accordance with the law;

(6) Other property that may not be mortgaged as provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 400 To establish a mortgage, the parties shall conclude a mortgage contract in writing.

A mortgage contract generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The type and amount of the secured claim;

(2) The time limit for the debtor to perform the obligation;

(3) The name, quantity, and other details of the mortgaged property;

(4) The scope of the security.

Article 401 If, before the expiration of the time limit for the performance of the obligation, the mortgagee agrees with the mortgagor that the mortgaged property shall belong to the creditor if the debtor fails to perform the obligation that is due, the creditor may only be paid in priority out of the mortgaged property in accordance with the law.

Article 402 Where property specified in items (1) to (3) of paragraph 1 of Article 395 of this Law or buildings under construction specified in item (5) is mortgaged, the mortgage shall be registered. The mortgage is established upon registration.

Article 403 Where movable property is mortgaged, the mortgage is established upon the entry into effect of the mortgage contract; without registration, it shall not be valid against a bona fide third party.

Article 404 Where movable property is mortgaged, it shall not be valid against a buyer who has paid a reasonable price and obtained possession of the mortgaged property in the ordinary course of business.

Article 405 If, before the establishment of the mortgage, the mortgaged property has been leased and possession has been transferred, the original lease relationship shall not be affected by the mortgage.

Article 406 During the period of the mortgage, the mortgagor may transfer the mortgaged property. If the parties have agreed otherwise, such agreement shall prevail. The transfer of the mortgaged property shall not affect the mortgage.

Where the mortgagor transfers the mortgaged property, it shall promptly notify the mortgagee. If the mortgagee can prove that the transfer of the mortgaged property may impair the mortgage, it may request the mortgagor to use the proceeds from the transfer to repay the debt to the mortgagee in advance or to deposit them. The portion of the proceeds from the transfer exceeding the amount of the claim shall belong to the mortgagor, and the shortfall shall be paid by the debtor.

Article 407 A mortgage shall not be transferred separately from the claim or used as security for another claim. Where a claim is transferred, the mortgage securing the claim shall be transferred together, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 408 If the act of the mortgagor is sufficient to reduce the value of the mortgaged property, the mortgagee has the right to request the mortgagor to cease such act; if the value of the mortgaged property is reduced, the mortgagee has the right to request the restoration of the value of the mortgaged property or the provision of security corresponding to the reduced value. If the mortgagor neither restores the value of the mortgaged property nor provides security, the mortgagee has the right to request the debtor to repay the debt in advance.

Article 409 The mortgagee may waive the mortgage or the priority order of the mortgage. The mortgagee and the mortgagor may agree to change the priority order of the mortgage and the amount of the secured claim. However, a change in the mortgage shall not adversely affect other mortgagees without their written consent.

Where the debtor mortgages his own property, and the mortgagee waives the mortgage, the priority order of the mortgage, or changes the mortgage, other sureties shall be exempted from their suretyship liability to the extent of the mortgagee's loss of the right to priority payment, unless the other sureties have promised to continue to provide security.

Article 410 Where the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur, the mortgagee may agree with the mortgagor to convert the mortgaged property into money or to be paid in priority out of the proceeds from the auction or sale of the mortgaged property. If the agreement harms the interests of other creditors, the other creditors may request the people's court to revoke the agreement.

If the mortgagee and the mortgagor fail to reach an agreement on the method of realizing the mortgage, the mortgagee may request the people's court to auction or sell the mortgaged property.

The conversion of the mortgaged property into money or its sale shall be based on the market price.

Article 411 Where a mortgage is established in accordance with Article 396 of this Law, the mortgaged property shall be determined upon the occurrence of any of the following circumstances:

(1) The time limit for the performance of the obligation expires, and the claim is not realized;

(2) The mortgagor is declared bankrupt or dissolved;

(3) The circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur;

(4) Other circumstances that seriously affect the realization of the claim.

Article 412 Where the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur, resulting in the mortgaged property being seized by the people's court in accordance with the law, from the date of seizure, the mortgagee has the right to collect the natural fruits or legal fruits of the mortgaged property, unless the mortgagee fails to notify the obligor who should pay the legal fruits.

The fruits mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall first be used to offset the expenses of collecting the fruits.

Article 413 After the mortgaged property is converted into money, auctioned, or sold, the portion of the proceeds exceeding the amount of the claim shall belong to the mortgagor, and the shortfall shall be paid by the debtor.

Article 414 Where the same property is mortgaged to two or more creditors, the proceeds from the auction or sale of the mortgaged property shall be used for payment in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) Where the mortgages have been registered, the order of payment shall be determined by the time of registration;

(2) A registered mortgage shall be paid before an unregistered mortgage;

(3) Where the mortgages are not registered, payment shall be made in proportion to the amount of the claims.

For other security interests in property that may be registered, the order of payment shall be determined by reference to the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 415 Where both a mortgage and a pledge are established on the same property, the order of payment from the proceeds of the auction or sale of the property shall be determined by the time of registration or delivery.

Article 416 Where the principal claim secured by a mortgage on movable property is the price of the mortgaged property, and the mortgage is registered within ten days after the delivery of the subject matter, the mortgagee shall be paid in priority over other security interest holders of the buyer of the mortgaged property, except for the lien holder.

Article 417 After the right to the use of construction land is mortgaged, the buildings newly added on the land shall not be part of the mortgaged property. When the mortgage on the right to the use of construction land is realized, the newly added buildings on the land shall be disposed of together with the right to the use of construction land. However, the mortgagee has no right to be paid in priority out of the proceeds from the newly added buildings.

Article 418 Where the right to the use of land owned by the collective is mortgaged in accordance with the law, after the mortgage is realized, the nature of the land ownership and the use of the land shall not be changed without legal procedures.

Article 419 The mortgagee shall exercise the mortgage within the limitation period for litigation of the principal claim; if it is not exercised, the people's court shall not provide protection.

##### Section 2 Maximum Amount Mortgage

Article 420 To secure the performance of an obligation, if the debtor or a third party provides secured property for claims that will arise continuously within a certain period, and the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the mortgage occur, the mortgagee is entitled to be paid in priority out of the secured property within the limit of the maximum amount of the claim.

Claims existing before the establishment of the maximum amount mortgage may, with the consent of the parties, be included in the scope of claims secured by the maximum amount mortgage.

Article 421 Before the claims secured by the maximum amount mortgage are determined, if part of the claims is transferred, the maximum amount mortgage shall not be transferred, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 422 Before the claims secured by the maximum amount mortgage are determined, the mortgagee and the mortgagor may, through agreement, change the period for determining the claims, the scope of the claims, and the maximum amount of the claim. However, the content of the change shall not adversely affect other mortgagees.

Article 423 The claims of the mortgagee are determined under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The agreed period for determining the claims expires;

(2) Where there is no agreement on the period for determining the claims or the agreement is unclear, and the mortgagee or the mortgagor requests the determination of the claims two years after the establishment of the maximum amount mortgage;

(3) It is impossible for new claims to arise;

(4) The mortgagee knows or should know that the mortgaged property has been sealed up or seized;

(5) The debtor or the mortgagor is declared bankrupt or dissolved;

(6) Other circumstances provided by law for the determination of claims.

Article 424 In addition to the provisions of this Section, the relevant provisions of Section 1 of this Chapter shall apply to the maximum amount mortgage.

#### Chapter 18 Pledge

##### Section 1 Pledge of Movable Property

Article 425 To secure the performance of an obligation, if the debtor or a third party delivers his movable property to the creditor for possession as a pledge, and the debtor fails to perform an obligation that is due or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the pledge occur, the creditor is entitled to be paid in priority out of that movable property.

The debtor or third party mentioned in the preceding paragraph is the pledgor, the creditor is the pledgee, and the delivered movable property is the pledged property.

Article 426 Movable property whose transfer is prohibited by laws or administrative regulations shall not be pledged.

Article 427 To establish a pledge, the parties shall conclude a pledge contract in writing.

A pledge contract generally includes the following clauses:

(1) The type and amount of the secured claim;

(2) The time limit for the debtor to perform the obligation;

(3) The name, quantity, and other details of the pledged property;

(4) The scope of the security;

(5) The time and method of delivery of the pledged property.

Article 428 If, before the expiration of the time limit for the performance of the obligation, the pledgee agrees with the pledgor that the pledged property shall belong to the creditor if the debtor fails to perform the obligation that is due, the creditor may only be paid in priority out of the pledged property in accordance with the law.Article 429 A pledge is established when the pledgor delivers the pledged property to the pledgee.

Article 430 The pledgee is entitled to collect the fruits of the pledged property, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

The fruits specified in the preceding paragraph shall first be used to offset the expenses for collecting them.

Article 431 If the pledgee, during the existence of the pledge, uses or disposes of the pledged property without the pledgor's consent, thereby causing damage to the pledgor, the pledgee shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 432 The pledgee has the obligation to properly keep the pledged property; if the pledged property is damaged or lost due to improper custody, the pledgee shall bear liability for compensation.

If the pledgee's conduct may cause damage or loss to the pledged property, the pledgor may request the pledgee to deposit the pledged property with a competent authority, or request early repayment of the debt and return of the pledged property.

Article 433 If, due to reasons not attributable to the pledgee, the pledged property is likely to be damaged or its value is obviously decreasing, thereby endangering the pledgee's rights, the pledgee has the right to request the pledgor to provide corresponding security; if the pledgor fails to do so, the pledgee may auction or sell the pledged property and, after consultation with the pledgor, use the proceeds to repay the debt in advance or deposit them with a competent authority.

Article 434 If the pledgee, during the existence of the pledge, repledges the pledged property without the pledgor's consent, thereby causing damage or loss to the pledged property, the pledgee shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 435 The pledgee may waive the pledge. If the debtor pledges his own property and the pledgee waives the pledge, other guarantors are exempted from their guarantee liability to the extent of the priority right to repayment lost by the pledgee, unless the other guarantors have promised to continue to provide the guarantee.

Article 436 When the debtor performs the debt or the pledgor repays the secured claim in advance, the pledgee shall return the pledged property.

If the debtor fails to perform the debt at maturity or the circumstances agreed upon by the parties for the realization of the pledge occur, the pledgee may, after consultation with the pledgor, convert the pledged property into money at a price agreed upon, or have priority to be repaid with the proceeds from the auction or sale of the pledged property.

The conversion of the pledged property into money or its sale shall be based on the market price.

Article 437 The pledgor may request the pledgee to exercise the pledge promptly after the debt performance period expires; if the pledgee fails to do so, the pledgor may request the people's court to auction or sell the pledged property.

If the pledgor requests the pledgee to exercise the pledge promptly, and the pledgee causes damage to the pledgor by delaying the exercise of the right, the pledgee shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 438 After the pledged property is converted into money, auctioned, or sold, the portion of the proceeds exceeding the amount of the claim belongs to the pledgor, and the shortfall shall be paid by the debtor.

Article 439 The pledgor and the pledgee may agree to establish a maximum amount pledge.

The maximum amount pledge shall, in addition to the application of the relevant provisions of this Section, be governed by the relevant provisions of Section 2 of Chapter 17 of this Part by reference.

##### Section 2 Pledge of Rights

Article 440 The following rights that the debtor or a third party has the right to dispose of may be pledged:

(1) Bills of exchange, promissory notes, and checks;
(2) Bonds and certificates of deposit;
(3) Warehouse receipts and bills of lading;
(4) Transferable fund units and equity shares;
(5) Property rights in intellectual property such as transferable exclusive rights to use registered trademarks, patent rights, and copyrights;
(6) Existing and future accounts receivable;
(7) Other property rights that may be pledged as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 441 Where a bill of exchange, promissory note, check, bond, certificate of deposit, warehouse receipt, or bill of lading is pledged, the pledge is established when the document of title is delivered to the pledgee; if there is no document of title, the pledge is established when the pledge registration is completed. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 442 If the date for payment or delivery of goods under a bill of exchange, promissory note, check, bond, certificate of deposit, warehouse receipt, or bill of lading falls before the maturity of the principal claim, the pledgee may cash or take delivery of the goods and, after consultation with the pledgor, use the proceeds or the goods to repay the debt in advance or deposit them with a competent authority.

Article 443 Where fund units or equity shares are pledged, the pledge is established when the pledge registration is completed.

After fund units or equity shares are pledged, they shall not be transferred, unless the pledgor and the pledgee have agreed otherwise. The proceeds obtained by the pledgor from the transfer of the pledged fund units or equity shares shall be used to repay the debt to the pledgee in advance or be deposited with a competent authority.

Article 444 Where property rights in intellectual property such as exclusive rights to use registered trademarks, patent rights, and copyrights are pledged, the pledge is established when the pledge registration is completed.

After property rights in intellectual property are pledged, the pledgor shall not transfer or license others to use them, unless the pledgor and the pledgee have agreed otherwise. The proceeds obtained by the pledgor from the transfer or licensing of the pledged property rights in intellectual property shall be used to repay the debt to the pledgee in advance or be deposited with a competent authority.

Article 445 Where accounts receivable are pledged, the pledge is established when the pledge registration is completed.

After accounts receivable are pledged, they shall not be transferred, unless the pledgor and the pledgee have agreed otherwise. The proceeds obtained by the pledgor from the transfer of the pledged accounts receivable shall be used to repay the debt to the pledgee in advance or be deposited with a competent authority.

Article 446 The pledge of rights shall, in addition to the application of the provisions of this Section, be governed by the relevant provisions of Section 1 of this Chapter.

#### Chapter 19 Lien

Article 447 If the debtor fails to perform the debt at maturity, the creditor may retain the debtor's movable property that is already in its lawful possession and has the right to be repaid in priority from such property.

The creditor specified in the preceding paragraph is the lien holder, and the movable property in its possession is the retained property.

Article 448 The movable property retained by the creditor shall be in the same legal relationship as the claim, unless the retention is between enterprises.

Article 449 Movable property that cannot be retained as provided by law or as agreed by the parties shall not be retained.

Article 450 If the retained property is divisible, the value of the retained property shall be equivalent to the amount of the debt.

Article 451 The lien holder has the obligation to properly keep the retained property; if the retained property is damaged or lost due to improper custody, the lien holder shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 452 The lien holder is entitled to collect the fruits of the retained property.

The fruits specified in the preceding paragraph shall first be used to offset the expenses for collecting them.

Article 453 The lien holder and the debtor shall agree on a period for the performance of the debt after the property is retained; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the lien holder shall give the debtor a period of not less than sixty days for the performance of the debt, except for perishable or difficult-to-store movable property. If the debtor fails to perform within the prescribed period, the lien holder may, after consultation with the debtor, convert the retained property into money at a price agreed upon, or have priority to be repaid with the proceeds from the auction or sale of the retained property.

The conversion of the retained property into money or its sale shall be based on the market price.

Article 454 The debtor may request the lien holder to exercise the lien after the debt performance period expires; if the lien holder fails to do so, the debtor may request the people's court to auction or sell the retained property.

Article 455 After the retained property is converted into money, auctioned, or sold, the portion of the proceeds exceeding the amount of the claim belongs to the debtor, and the shortfall shall be paid by the debtor.

Article 456 If a mortgage or pledge has been established on the same movable property and the property is subsequently retained, the lien holder has priority to be repaid.

Article 457 The lien is extinguished if the lien holder loses possession of the retained property or if the lien holder accepts alternative security provided by the debtor.

### Part 5 Possession

#### Chapter 20 Possession

Article 458 Possession arising from a contractual relationship or the like shall be governed by the contract regarding the use, income, and liability for breach of contract concerning the immovable or movable property; if the contract has no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the relevant legal provisions shall apply.

Article 459 If a possessor causes damage to the immovable or movable property in their possession through its use, a possessor in bad faith shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 460 If immovable or movable property is in the possession of a possessor, the right holder may request the return of the original property and its fruits; however, the right holder shall pay the necessary expenses incurred by a possessor in good faith for the maintenance of the immovable or movable property.

Article 461 If the immovable or movable property in possession is damaged or lost, and the right holder of the property requests compensation, the possessor shall return any insurance proceeds, compensation, or indemnity obtained due to the damage or loss to the right holder; if the right holder's damage is not fully compensated, a possessor in bad faith shall also compensate for the loss.

Article 462 If the immovable or movable property in possession is encroached upon, the possessor has the right to request the return of the original property; if there is an act disturbing the possession, the possessor has the right to request the removal of the disturbance or the elimination of the danger; if damage is caused by the encroachment or disturbance, the possessor has the right to request compensation for the damage in accordance with the law.

The right of the possessor to request the return of the original property is extinguished if not exercised within one year from the date of the encroachment.

### Part 1 General Provisions

#### Chapter 1 General Provisions

Article 463 This Part regulates civil relations arising from contracts.

Article 464 A contract is an agreement between civil subjects to establish, modify, or terminate a civil legal relationship.

Agreements concerning personal status relationships such as marriage, adoption, and guardianship shall be governed by the legal provisions on such personal status relationships; if there are no such provisions, the provisions of this Part may be applied by reference based on their nature.

Article 465 A contract lawfully formed is protected by law.

A lawfully formed contract is legally binding only on the parties thereto, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 466 If the parties dispute the understanding of a contract clause, the meaning of the disputed clause shall be determined in accordance with paragraph 1 of Article 142 of this Law.

If a contract is concluded in two or more languages and it is agreed that all versions are equally authentic, the words and sentences used in each version are presumed to have the same meaning. If the words and sentences used in each version are inconsistent, they shall be interpreted in accordance with the relevant clauses, nature, purpose, and the principle of good faith of the contract.

Article 467 For contracts not expressly provided for by this Law or other laws, the provisions of the General Provisions of this Part shall apply, and the provisions of this Part or other laws on the most similar contracts may be applied by reference.

Contracts for Sino-foreign equity joint ventures, Sino-foreign contractual joint ventures, and Sino-foreign cooperative exploration and development of natural resources to be performed within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the laws of the People's Republic of China.

Article 468 For creditor-debtor relationships not arising from a contract, the legal provisions on such creditor-debtor relationships shall apply; if there are no such provisions, the relevant provisions of the General Provisions of this Part shall apply, unless they cannot be applied due to their nature.

#### Chapter 2 Formation of Contracts

Article 469 The parties may conclude a contract in writing, orally, or in any other form.

A written form is a form such as a contract document, letter, telegram, telex, or facsimile that can tangibly express the content contained therein.

Data messages that can tangibly express the content contained therein and can be accessed and retrieved at any time, such as electronic data interchange and email, are deemed to be in written form.

Article 470 The content of a contract shall be agreed upon by the parties and generally includes the following clauses:

(1) Names or titles and domiciles of the parties;
(2) Subject matter;
(3) Quantity;
(4) Quality;
(5) Price or remuneration;
(6) Time, place, and method of performance;
(7) Liability for breach of contract;
(8) Methods for dispute resolution.

The parties may conclude a contract by reference to model texts of various types of contracts.

Article 471 The parties may conclude a contract by making an offer, acceptance, or any other means.

Article 472 An offer is an expression of intention to conclude a contract with another person, and such expression of intention shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Its content is specific and definite;
(2) It indicates that the offeror will be bound by the expression of intention upon acceptance by the offeree.

Article 473 An invitation to offer is an expression of intention to invite another person to make an offer to oneself. Auction announcements, tender notices, prospectuses, bond offering circulars, fund prospectuses, commercial advertisements and promotions, and sent price lists are invitations to offer.

If the content of a commercial advertisement or promotion meets the conditions of an offer, it constitutes an offer.

Article 474 The time when an offer becomes effective shall be governed by Article 137 of this Law.

Article 475 An offer may be withdrawn. The withdrawal of an offer shall be governed by Article 141 of this Law.

Article 476 An offer may be revoked, except in the following circumstances:

(1) The offeror has indicated that the offer is irrevocable by specifying a fixed time for acceptance or in any other manner;
(2) The offeree has reason to believe that the offer is irrevocable and has made reasonable preparations for performing the contract.

Article 477 If the expression of intention to revoke an offer is made orally, the content of the expression of intention shall be known to the offeree before the offeree makes an acceptance; if the expression of intention to revoke an offer is made in a non-oral manner, it shall reach the offeree before the offeree makes an acceptance.

Article 478 An offer loses its effect in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The offer is rejected;
(2) The offer is lawfully revoked;
(3) The offeree fails to make an acceptance before the expiry of the time limit for acceptance;
(4) The offeree makes a material change to the content of the offer.

Article 479 An acceptance is an expression of intention by the offeree to agree to the offer.

Article 480 An acceptance shall be made by notice, unless, according to trade practices or as indicated by the offer, the acceptance may be made by performing an act.

Article 481 An acceptance shall reach the offeror within the time limit specified in the offer.

If no time limit for acceptance is specified in the offer, the acceptance shall reach the offeror in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) If the offer is made orally, the acceptance shall be made immediately;
(2) If the offer is made in a non-oral manner, the acceptance shall reach the offeror within a reasonable time.

Article 482 If an offer is made by letter or telegram, the time limit for acceptance begins to run from the date stated in the letter or the date of dispatch of the telegram. If no date is stated in the letter, it begins to run from the postmark date on the envelope. If an offer is made by telephone, facsimile, email, or other rapid means of communication, the time limit for acceptance begins to run from the time the offer reaches the offeree.

Article 483 A contract is formed when the acceptance becomes effective, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 484 The time when an acceptance made by notice becomes effective shall be governed by Article 137 of this Law.

If an acceptance does not require notice, it becomes effective when the act of acceptance is performed in accordance with trade practices or the requirements of the offer.

Article 485 An acceptance may be withdrawn. The withdrawal of an acceptance shall be governed by Article 141 of this Law.

Article 486 If the offeree makes an acceptance after the expiry of the time limit for acceptance, or makes an acceptance within the time limit but it cannot reach the offeror in time under normal circumstances, it constitutes a new offer, unless the offeror promptly notifies the offeree that the acceptance is effective.

Article 487 If the offeree makes an acceptance within the time limit for acceptance and it can reach the offeror in time under normal circumstances, but due to other reasons it reaches the offeror after the expiry of the time limit, the acceptance is effective, unless the offeror promptly notifies the offeree that the acceptance is not accepted because it has exceeded the time limit.

Article 488 The content of an acceptance shall be consistent with the content of the offer. If the offeree makes a material change to the content of the offer, it constitutes a new offer. Changes concerning the subject matter, quantity, quality, price or remuneration, time, place and method of performance, liability for breach of contract, and methods for dispute resolution are material changes to the content of the offer.

Article 489 If the acceptance makes a non-material change to the content of the offer, the acceptance is effective, and the content of the contract shall be based on the content of the acceptance, unless the offeror promptly objects or the offer indicates that the acceptance shall not make any change to the content of the offer.

Article 490 If the parties conclude a contract in the form of a contract document, the contract is formed when the parties sign, seal, or affix their fingerprints. If one party has performed the principal obligation before signing, sealing, or affixing fingerprints, and the other party accepts it, the contract is formed.

If a contract is required by law, administrative regulations, or agreement of the parties to be concluded in writing, and the parties have not used a written form, but one party has performed the principal obligation and the other party accepts it, the contract is formed.

Article 491 If the parties conclude a contract by letters, data messages, or other forms and require a confirmation letter, the contract is formed when the confirmation letter is signed.

If the information on goods or services published by one party through the internet or other information networks meets the conditions of an offer, and the other party selects the goods or services and successfully submits the order, the contract is formed, unless the parties agree otherwise.

Article 492 The place where the acceptance becomes effective is the place where the contract is formed.

If a contract is concluded in the form of data messages, the principal place of business of the recipient is the place where the contract is formed; if there is no principal place of business, the domicile of the recipient is the place where the contract is formed. If the parties agree otherwise, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 493 If the parties conclude a contract in the form of a contract document, the place where the last party signs, seals, or affixes fingerprints is the place where the contract is formed, unless the parties agree otherwise.

Article 494 If the state issues a state order or mandatory task for the purpose of emergency rescue and disaster relief, epidemic prevention and control, or other needs, the relevant civil subjects shall conclude a contract in accordance with the rights and obligations provided by relevant laws and administrative regulations.

A party who is obligated to make an offer under laws and administrative regulations shall promptly make a reasonable offer.

A party who is obligated to make an acceptance under laws and administrative regulations shall not refuse the other party's reasonable request to conclude a contract.

Article 495 Subscription letters, order letters, reservation letters, etc., in which the parties agree to conclude a contract within a certain period in the future, constitute a preliminary contract.

If one party fails to perform the obligation to conclude a contract as agreed in the preliminary contract, the other party may request that party to bear the liability for breach of the preliminary contract.

Article 496 A standard term is a clause that is prepared in advance by one party for repeated use and is not negotiated with the other party at the time of concluding the contract.When a contract is concluded using standard terms, the party providing the standard terms shall determine the rights and obligations between the parties in accordance with the principle of fairness, and shall draw the other party's attention in a reasonable manner to the terms that exempt or mitigate the liability of the party providing the standard terms or that otherwise materially affect the other party's interests, and shall explain such terms upon the other party's request. If the party providing the standard terms fails to perform the obligation to draw attention or to explain, causing the other party not to notice or understand the terms that materially affect its interests, the other party may assert that such terms are not part of the contract.

Article 497 A standard term shall be void under any of the following circumstances:

(1) it falls under the circumstances of invalidity provided for in Section 3 of Chapter 6 of Part I and Article 506 of this Code;

(2) the party providing the standard terms unreasonably exempts or mitigates its own liability, increases the other party's liability, or restricts the other party's main rights; or

(3) the party providing the standard terms excludes the other party's main rights.

Article 498 Where a dispute arises over the understanding of a standard term, it shall be interpreted in accordance with the common understanding. Where a standard term is subject to two or more interpretations, it shall be interpreted in a manner unfavorable to the party providing the standard terms. Where a standard term is inconsistent with a non-standard term, the non-standard term shall prevail.

Article 499 Where a person making a reward by a public announcement promises to pay a reward to a person who performs a specified act, the person who has performed the act may request the reward.

Article 500 A party that causes loss to the other party under any of the following circumstances in the course of concluding a contract shall be liable for damages:

(1) negotiating in bad faith under the pretext of concluding a contract;

(2) intentionally concealing material facts relating to the conclusion of the contract or providing false information; or

(3) engaging in any other act that violates the principle of good faith.

Article 501 A party that, in the course of concluding a contract, learns of a trade secret or other information that should be kept confidential shall not disclose or improperly use such trade secret or information, regardless of whether the contract is concluded. If the disclosure or improper use of such trade secret or information causes loss to the other party, the party shall be liable for damages.

#### Chapter 3: Validity of Contracts

Article 502 A contract lawfully formed shall become effective upon its formation, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Where, according to the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, a contract is subject to approval or other formalities, such provisions shall be followed. If the failure to complete the approval or other formalities affects the validity of the contract, it shall not affect the validity of the clauses in the contract concerning the obligation to apply for approval or other formalities and related clauses. If the party that should apply for approval or other formalities fails to perform its obligation, the other party may request it to bear liability for breach of that obligation.

Where, according to the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, the modification, assignment, or termination of a contract is subject to approval or other formalities, the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 503 Where a person without authority or with excess authority concludes a contract in the name of the principal, and the principal has begun to perform the contractual obligations or has accepted the performance of the other party, the contract shall be deemed to have been ratified.

Article 504 Where a contract is concluded by the legal representative of a legal person or the person in charge of an unincorporated organization exceeding their authority, the act of representation shall be effective, and the contract concluded shall be binding on the legal person or unincorporated organization, unless the other party knew or should have known that the authority was exceeded.

Article 505 The validity of a contract concluded by a party exceeding its business scope shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of Section 3 of Chapter 6 of Part I of this Code and the relevant provisions of this Part, and the contract shall not be invalidated solely on the ground of exceeding the business scope.

Article 506 The following exemption clauses in a contract are void:

(1) those causing personal injury to the other party; or

(2) those causing property loss to the other party intentionally or through gross negligence.

Article 507 The invalidity, voidance, revocation, or termination of a contract shall not affect the validity of the clauses in the contract concerning the settlement of disputes.

Article 508 Where this Part does not provide for the validity of a contract, the relevant provisions of Section 3 of Chapter 6 of Part I of this Code shall apply.

#### Chapter 4: Performance of Contracts

Article 509 The parties shall fully perform their obligations as agreed.

The parties shall follow the principle of good faith and perform the obligations of notification, assistance, and confidentiality in accordance with the nature and purpose of the contract and trade usage.

In the course of performing a contract, the parties shall avoid wasting resources, polluting the environment, and damaging the ecosystem.

Article 510 After a contract becomes effective, if the parties have not agreed on the quality, price or remuneration, place of performance, or other contents, or such agreement is unclear, they may supplement it by agreement; if they cannot reach a supplementary agreement, it shall be determined in accordance with the relevant clauses of the contract or trade usage.

Article 511 Where the parties have not agreed on the relevant contents of the contract, or such agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with the provisions of the preceding article, the following provisions shall apply:

(1) If the quality requirement is unclear, performance shall be in accordance with mandatory national standards; in the absence of mandatory national standards, in accordance with recommended national standards; in the absence of recommended national standards, in accordance with industry standards; in the absence of national or industry standards, in accordance with the usual standards or specific standards consistent with the purpose of the contract.

(2) If the price or remuneration is unclear, performance shall be in accordance with the market price at the place of performance at the time of conclusion of the contract; if the government-set price or government-guided price is to be implemented according to law, performance shall be in accordance with such provisions.

(3) If the place of performance is unclear, and the obligation is to pay money, performance shall be at the place where the party receiving the money is located; if the obligation is to deliver immovable property, performance shall be at the place where the immovable property is located; for other subject matters, performance shall be at the place where the party performing the obligation is located.

(4) If the time of performance is unclear, the debtor may perform at any time, and the creditor may request performance at any time, but the other party shall be given the necessary time to prepare.

(5) If the method of performance is unclear, performance shall be in a manner conducive to achieving the purpose of the contract.

(6) If the burden of performance expenses is unclear, it shall be borne by the party performing the obligation; if the increase in performance expenses is due to the creditor, it shall be borne by the creditor.

Article 512 Where the subject matter of an electronic contract concluded through information networks such as the Internet is the delivery of goods and the goods are delivered by courier logistics, the time of receipt by the consignee is the time of delivery. Where the subject matter of an electronic contract is the provision of services, the time indicated in the generated electronic or physical voucher is the time of service provision; if the voucher does not indicate the time or the indicated time is inconsistent with the actual time of service provision, the actual time of service provision shall prevail.

Where the subject matter of an electronic contract is delivered by online transmission, the time when the subject matter enters the specific system designated by the other party and can be retrieved and identified is the time of delivery.

If the parties to an electronic contract have otherwise agreed on the method or time of delivery of goods or provision of services, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 513 Where the government-set price or government-guided price is implemented, if the government price is adjusted within the delivery period agreed in the contract, the price at the time of delivery shall be used for pricing. If the subject matter is delivered late, and the price rises, the original price shall apply; if the price falls, the new price shall apply. If the subject matter is collected late or payment is made late, and the price rises, the new price shall apply; if the price falls, the original price shall apply.

Article 514 For an obligation to pay money, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties, the creditor may request the debtor to perform in the legal tender of the actual place of performance.

Article 515 Where there are multiple subject matters and the debtor only needs to perform one of them, the debtor has the right of choice; however, unless otherwise provided by law, agreed by the parties, or required by trade usage.

If the party entitled to choose fails to make a choice within the agreed period or by the time performance is due, and after being urged, still fails to make a choice within a reasonable period, the right of choice passes to the other party.

Article 516 A party exercising the right of choice shall notify the other party in a timely manner; the subject matter is determined when the notice reaches the other party. Once the subject matter is determined, it may not be changed, unless otherwise agreed by the other party.

If a chosen subject matter becomes impossible to perform, the party entitled to choose may not choose the subject matter that is impossible to perform, unless the impossibility is caused by the other party.

Article 517 Where there are two or more creditors and the subject matter is divisible, and each creditor has a claim in proportion to its share, it is a claim by shares; where there are two or more debtors and the subject matter is divisible, and each debtor bears a debt in proportion to its share, it is a debt by shares.

If the shares of the creditors by shares or the debtors by shares are difficult to determine, the shares shall be deemed equal.

Article 518 Where there are two or more creditors, and all or part of the creditors may request the debtor to perform the obligation, it is a joint claim; where there are two or more debtors, and the creditor may request all or part of the debtors to perform the entire obligation, it is a joint debt.

Joint claims or joint debts shall be provided for by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 519 If the shares among joint debtors are difficult to determine, the shares shall be deemed equal.

A joint debtor that has actually borne a debt exceeding its own share has the right to seek recourse from the other joint debtors within the scope of their unperformed shares, and accordingly enjoys the creditor's rights, but shall not harm the creditor's interests. The defenses that other joint debtors have against the creditor may be asserted against such debtor.

If the joint debtor from whom recourse is sought cannot perform its share, the other joint debtors shall bear it proportionally within the corresponding scope.

Article 520 Where some joint debtors perform, offset the debt, or deposit the subject matter, the debts of the other debtors to the creditor are extinguished within the corresponding scope; such debtor may seek recourse from the other debtors in accordance with the preceding article.

If the debt of some joint debtors is remitted by the creditor, the debts of the other debtors to the creditor are extinguished within the scope of the share that such joint debtors should bear.

If the debt of some joint debtors and the creditor's claim are merged in the same person, the creditor's claim against the other debtors continues to exist after deducting the share that such debtor should bear.

If the creditor delays in accepting performance from some joint debtors, it has effect on the other joint debtors.

Article 521 If the shares among joint creditors are difficult to determine, the shares shall be deemed equal.

A joint creditor that has actually received the claim shall return it to the other joint creditors in proportion.

Joint claims shall be governed by the relevant provisions of this Chapter on joint debts by reference.

Article 522 Where the parties agree that the debtor shall perform the obligation to a third party, and the debtor fails to perform the obligation to the third party or performs it not in conformity with the agreement, the debtor shall bear liability for breach of contract to the creditor.

Where the law provides or the parties agree that the third party may directly request the debtor to perform the obligation to it, and the third party does not expressly refuse within a reasonable period, and the debtor fails to perform the obligation to the third party or performs it not in conformity with the agreement, the third party may request the debtor to bear liability for breach of contract; the defenses that the debtor has against the creditor may be asserted against the third party.

Article 523 Where the parties agree that a third party shall perform the obligation to the creditor, and the third party fails to perform or performs not in conformity with the agreement, the debtor shall bear liability for breach of contract to the creditor.

Article 524 Where the debtor fails to perform the obligation, and a third party has a legitimate interest in performing the obligation, the third party has the right to perform it on behalf of the debtor to the creditor; however, unless the obligation can only be performed by the debtor due to its nature, as agreed by the parties, or as provided by law.

After the creditor accepts the performance by the third party, the creditor's claim against the debtor is transferred to the third party, unless otherwise agreed by the debtor and the third party.

Article 525 Where the parties owe obligations to each other and there is no order of performance, they shall perform simultaneously. One party has the right to refuse the other party's request for performance before the other party performs. One party has the right to refuse the corresponding request for performance when the other party's performance does not conform to the agreement.

Article 526 Where the parties owe obligations to each other and there is an order of performance, and the party that should perform first fails to perform, the party that should perform later has the right to refuse its request for performance. If the party that should perform first performs not in conformity with the agreement, the party that should perform later has the right to refuse the corresponding request for performance.

Article 527 A party that should perform the obligation first may suspend its performance if it has conclusive evidence that the other party is in any of the following circumstances:

(1) its business conditions have seriously deteriorated;

(2) it has transferred property or withdrawn funds to evade debts;

(3) it has lost commercial credibility; or

(4) it is in any other circumstance that has lost or may lose the ability to perform the obligation.

If a party suspends performance without conclusive evidence, it shall bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 528 A party that suspends performance in accordance with the preceding article shall promptly notify the other party. If the other party provides appropriate security, performance shall be resumed. After the suspension of performance, if the other party fails to regain its ability to perform and fails to provide appropriate security within a reasonable period, it shall be deemed to have indicated by its own actions that it will not perform the main obligation, and the party that suspended performance may rescind the contract and may request the other party to bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 529 If the creditor is divided, merged, or changes its domicile without notifying the debtor, making it difficult to perform the obligation, the debtor may suspend performance or deposit the subject matter.

Article 530 The creditor may refuse the debtor's early performance, unless the early performance does not harm the creditor's interests.

Any additional expenses incurred by the creditor due to the debtor's early performance shall be borne by the debtor.

Article 531 The creditor may refuse the debtor's partial performance, unless the partial performance does not harm the creditor's interests.

Any additional expenses incurred by the creditor due to the debtor's partial performance shall be borne by the debtor.

Article 532 After a contract becomes effective, the parties shall not fail to perform their contractual obligations due to a change in name, or a change in legal representative, person in charge, or handling person.

Article 533 After a contract is formed, if a fundamental change in the basic conditions of the contract that was unforeseeable by the parties at the time of conclusion and is not a commercial risk occurs, making the continued performance of the contract manifestly unfair to one party, the adversely affected party may renegotiate with the other party; if negotiation fails within a reasonable period, the party may request the people's court or an arbitration institution to modify or rescind the contract.

The people's court or arbitration institution shall, in light of the actual circumstances of the case, modify or rescind the contract in accordance with the principle of fairness.

Article 534 Where a party uses a contract to harm the national interest or the public interest, the market supervision and administration authority and other relevant administrative departments shall be responsible for supervision and handling in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

#### Chapter 5: Preservation of Contracts

Article 535 Where the debtor's failure to exercise its claim or the accessory rights related to the claim affects the realization of the creditor's due claim, the creditor may request the people's court to subrogatively exercise the debtor's rights against the counterparty in its own name, unless the rights are exclusively personal to the debtor.

The scope of exercising the subrogation right is limited to the creditor's due claim. The necessary expenses incurred by the creditor in exercising the subrogation right shall be borne by the debtor.

The defenses that the counterparty has against the debtor may be asserted against the creditor.

Article 536 Before the creditor's claim becomes due, if the debtor's claim or the accessory rights related to the claim are about to expire by limitation of action, or the debtor fails to declare its bankruptcy claim in a timely manner, or other circumstances affect the realization of the creditor's claim, the creditor may subrogatively request the debtor's counterparty to perform to the debtor, declare to the bankruptcy administrator, or take other necessary actions.

Article 537 Where the people's court determines that the subrogation right is established, the debtor's counterparty shall perform the obligation to the creditor; after the creditor accepts the performance, the corresponding rights and obligations between the creditor and the debtor, and between the debtor and the counterparty, are terminated. If the debtor's claim against the counterparty or the accessory rights related to the claim are subject to preservation or enforcement measures, or the debtor is bankrupt, the relevant legal provisions shall apply.

Article 538 Where the debtor disposes of its property rights without consideration by waiving its claim, waiving the security for the claim, transferring property without consideration, or maliciously extending the performance period of its due claim, affecting the realization of the creditor's claim, the creditor may request the people's court to revoke the debtor's act.

Article 539 Where the debtor transfers property at a manifestly unreasonably low price, acquires property from another person at a manifestly unreasonably high price, or provides security for another person's debt, affecting the realization of the creditor's claim, and the debtor's counterparty knew or should have known of such circumstances, the creditor may request the people's court to revoke the debtor's act.

Article 540 The scope of exercising the revocation right is limited to the creditor's claim. The necessary expenses incurred by the creditor in exercising the revocation right shall be borne by the debtor.

Article 541 The revocation right shall be exercised within one year from the date the creditor knew or should have known of the grounds for revocation. If the revocation right is not exercised within five years from the date of the debtor's act, the revocation right is extinguished.

Article 542 Where an act of the debtor that affects the realization of the creditor's claim is revoked, it shall have no legal binding force from the outset.

#### Chapter 6: Modification and Assignment of Contracts

Article 543 The parties may modify a contract upon mutual agreement.

Article 544 Where the parties' agreement on the content of the modification of a contract is unclear, it shall be presumed not to have been modified.

Article 545 The creditor may assign all or part of its claim to a third party, unless under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the claim cannot be assigned due to its nature;

(2) the claim cannot be assigned as agreed by the parties; or

(3) the claim cannot be assigned as provided by law.

Where the parties agree that a non-monetary claim cannot be assigned, such agreement shall not be asserted against a bona fide third party. Where the parties agree that a monetary claim cannot be assigned, such agreement shall not be asserted against a third party.

Article 546 Where the creditor assigns a claim without notifying the debtor, the assignment shall not be effective against the debtor.

A notice of assignment of a claim may not be revoked, unless the assignee consents.

Article 547 Where the creditor assigns a claim, the assignee acquires the accessory rights related to the claim, unless the accessory rights are exclusively personal to the creditor.

The assignee's acquisition of the accessory rights shall not be affected by the fact that the transfer of the accessory rights has not been registered or the possession has not been transferred.

Article 548 After the debtor receives the notice of assignment of the claim, the defenses that the debtor has against the assignor may be asserted against the assignee.

Article 549 Under any of the following circumstances, the debtor may assert set-off against the assignee:

(1) at the time the debtor receives the notice of assignment of the claim, the debtor has a claim against the assignor, and the debtor's claim becomes due before or at the same time as the assigned claim; or

(2) the debtor's claim and the assigned claim arise from the same contract.

Article 550 Any additional performance expenses incurred due to the assignment of the claim shall be borne by the assignor.

Article 551 Where the debtor transfers all or part of its debt to a third party, it shall obtain the consent of the creditor.

The debtor or the third party may urge the creditor to give its consent within a reasonable period; if the creditor does not respond, it shall be deemed not to have consented.Article 552 Where a third party agrees with the debtor to join in the debt and notifies the creditor, or the third party indicates to the creditor its willingness to join in the debt, and the creditor does not expressly refuse within a reasonable period, the creditor may request the third party to assume joint and several liability with the debtor to the extent of the debt the third party is willing to assume.

Article 553 Where a debtor transfers its debt, the new debtor may assert any defenses that the original debtor had against the creditor; however, the new debtor may not assert set-off against the creditor in respect of any claim that the original debtor had against the creditor.

Article 554 Where a debtor transfers its debt, the new debtor shall assume any accessory obligations related to the principal debt, unless such accessory obligations are personal to the original debtor.

Article 555 With the consent of the other party, a party may transfer its rights and obligations under a contract together to a third party.

Article 556 Where rights and obligations under a contract are transferred together, the relevant provisions on assignment of claims and assumption of debts shall apply.

#### Chapter 7 Termination of Rights and Obligations under a Contract

Article 557 The rights and obligations under a contract shall terminate under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the debt has been performed;
(2) the debts have been set off against each other;
(3) the debtor has deposited the subject matter in accordance with the law;
(4) the creditor has released the debtor from the debt;
(5) the creditor and the debtor have become the same person; or
(6) any other circumstance provided for by law or agreed upon by the parties.

Where a contract is rescinded, the rights and obligations under the contract shall terminate.

Article 558 After the termination of rights and obligations under a contract, the parties shall, in accordance with the principles of good faith and trade practices, perform obligations such as notification, assistance, confidentiality, and recycling of used items.

Article 559 Upon termination of rights and obligations under a contract, the accessory rights to the claim shall also be extinguished, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 560 Where a debtor owes several debts of the same type to the same creditor, and the debtor's performance is insufficient to discharge all the debts, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, the debtor shall specify the debt to be performed at the time of performance.

If the debtor fails to specify, performance shall be applied first to the debt that has fallen due; if several debts have all fallen due, performance shall be applied first to the debt that is least secured or unsecured; if all debts are equally secured or unsecured, performance shall be applied first to the debt that is more burdensome to the debtor; if the burdens are equal, performance shall be applied in the order of the due dates; if the due dates are the same, performance shall be applied in proportion to the amounts of the debts.

Article 561 Where, in addition to the principal debt, the debtor is also liable to pay interest and expenses incurred for enforcing the claim, and the debtor's performance is insufficient to discharge all the debts, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, the performance shall be applied in the following order:

(1) expenses incurred for enforcing the claim;
(2) interest; and
(3) the principal debt.

Article 562 The parties may rescind the contract upon mutual agreement.

The parties may agree on the grounds for a party to rescind the contract. When the agreed grounds for rescission occur, the party entitled to rescind may rescind the contract.

Article 563 A party may rescind the contract under any of the following circumstances:

(1) the purpose of the contract cannot be achieved due to force majeure;
(2) before the expiration of the period for performance, the other party expressly states or indicates by its conduct that it will not perform its principal obligations;
(3) the other party delays in performing its principal obligations and still fails to perform them within a reasonable period after being demanded;
(4) the other party delays in performing its obligations or commits any other breach of contract, thereby making it impossible to achieve the purpose of the contract; or
(5) any other circumstance provided for by law.

For a contract of an indefinite term that requires continuous performance, a party may rescind the contract at any time, provided that it gives the other party reasonable notice in advance.

Article 564 Where a law provides or the parties agree on a period for exercising the right to rescind, and the party fails to exercise it within that period, the right shall be extinguished.

Where no law provides or the parties have not agreed on a period for exercising the right to rescind, the right shall be extinguished if it is not exercised within one year from the date the party entitled to rescind knew or should have known of the grounds for rescission, or if it is not exercised within a reasonable period after being demanded by the other party.

Article 565 Where a party claims to rescind the contract in accordance with the law, it shall notify the other party. The contract shall be rescinded when the notice reaches the other party; if the notice states that the contract will be automatically rescinded if the debtor fails to perform its debt within a specified period, and the debtor fails to perform within that period, the contract shall be rescinded upon the expiration of the period specified in the notice. If the other party objects to the rescission, either party may request a people's court or an arbitration institution to confirm the validity of the rescission.

Where a party claims to rescind the contract by filing a lawsuit or applying for arbitration without notifying the other party, and the people's court or arbitration institution confirms the claim, the contract shall be rescinded when the copy of the statement of claim or the copy of the arbitration application is served on the other party.

Article 566 After a contract is rescinded, if the obligations have not been performed, performance shall be terminated; if the obligations have been performed, the parties may, based on the circumstances of performance and the nature of the contract, request restoration to the original state or take other remedial measures, and have the right to claim damages.

If a contract is rescinded due to a breach of contract, the party entitled to rescind may request the breaching party to bear liability for breach of contract, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

After the principal contract is rescinded, the guarantor shall still bear the guarantee liability for the civil liability that the debtor should bear, unless otherwise agreed in the guarantee contract.

Article 567 The termination of rights and obligations under a contract shall not affect the validity of the settlement and liquidation clauses in the contract.

Article 568 Where the parties owe each other debts, and the subject matter of the debts is of the same type and quality, either party may set off its own debt against the other party's debt that has fallen due, unless set-off is excluded by the nature of the debt, by agreement of the parties, or by law.

A party claiming set-off shall notify the other party. The notice shall become effective when it reaches the other party. Set-off may not be subject to any condition or time limit.

Article 569 Where the parties owe each other debts, and the subject matter of the debts is not of the same type and quality, they may also set off the debts upon mutual agreement.

Article 570 A debtor may deposit the subject matter under any of the following circumstances where it is difficult to perform the debt:

(1) the creditor refuses to accept the performance without justifiable reasons;
(2) the creditor's whereabouts are unknown;
(3) the creditor dies without determining an heir or estate administrator, or loses capacity for civil conduct without determining a guardian; or
(4) any other circumstance provided for by law.

If the subject matter is not suitable for deposit or the cost of deposit is excessively high, the debtor may auction or sell the subject matter in accordance with the law and deposit the proceeds.

Article 571 A deposit is established when the debtor delivers the subject matter, or the proceeds from the auction or sale of the subject matter in accordance with the law, to the deposit authority.

Once the deposit is established, the debtor is deemed to have delivered the subject matter to the extent of the deposit.

Article 572 After the subject matter is deposited, the debtor shall promptly notify the creditor or the creditor's heir, estate administrator, guardian, or property custodian.

Article 573 After the subject matter is deposited, the risk of damage or loss shall be borne by the creditor. During the period of deposit, the fruits of the subject matter shall belong to the creditor. The expenses of deposit shall be borne by the creditor.

Article 574 The creditor may collect the deposited subject matter at any time. However, if the creditor owes a debt to the debtor that has fallen due, the deposit authority shall, at the request of the debtor, refuse to allow the creditor to collect the deposited subject matter until the creditor has performed the debt or provided security.

The right of the creditor to collect the deposited subject matter shall be extinguished if not exercised within five years from the date of deposit, and the deposited subject matter, after deducting the deposit expenses, shall belong to the state. However, if the creditor fails to perform its debt due to the debtor, or if the creditor has made a written statement to the deposit authority waiving its right to collect the deposited subject matter, the debtor shall have the right to retrieve the deposited subject matter after bearing the deposit expenses.

Article 575 Where the creditor releases the debtor from part or all of the debt, the rights and obligations under the contract shall be partially or wholly terminated, unless the debtor refuses within a reasonable period.

Article 576 Where the creditor and the debtor become the same person, the rights and obligations under the contract shall terminate, unless the interests of a third party are prejudiced.

#### Chapter 8 Liability for Breach of Contract

Article 577 Where a party fails to perform its contractual obligations or performs them in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, it shall bear liability for breach of contract, such as continuing to perform, taking remedial measures, or compensating for losses.

Article 578 Where a party expressly states or indicates by its conduct that it will not perform its contractual obligations, the other party may, before the expiration of the period for performance, request it to bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 579 Where a party fails to pay the price, remuneration, rent, or interest, or fails to perform other monetary debts, the other party may request payment.

Article 580 Where a party fails to perform a non-monetary debt or performs it in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, the other party may request performance, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) performance is impossible in law or in fact;
(2) the subject matter of the debt is not suitable for compulsory performance or the cost of performance is excessively high; or
(3) the creditor fails to request performance within a reasonable period.

Where any of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph makes it impossible to achieve the purpose of the contract, the people's court or arbitration institution may, upon the request of a party, terminate the contractual rights and obligations, without affecting the liability for breach of contract.

Article 581 Where a party fails to perform a debt or performs it in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, and the nature of the debt does not permit compulsory performance, the other party may request it to bear the expenses of substitute performance by a third party.

Article 582 Where the performance does not conform to the agreement, the party shall bear liability for breach of contract in accordance with the agreement. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on liability for breach of contract, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the injured party may, based on the nature of the subject matter and the extent of the loss, reasonably choose to request the other party to bear liability for breach of contract, such as repair, rework, replacement, return of goods, or reduction of price or remuneration.

Article 583 Where a party fails to perform its contractual obligations or performs them in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, and the other party suffers other losses after the performance of obligations or the taking of remedial measures, the party shall compensate for the losses.

Article 584 Where a party fails to perform its contractual obligations or performs them in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, causing losses to the other party, the amount of damages shall be equivalent to the losses caused by the breach, including the benefits obtainable after the performance of the contract; however, it shall not exceed the losses that the breaching party foresaw or should have foreseen at the time of entering into the contract as a possible consequence of the breach.

Article 585 The parties may agree that, in the event of a breach of contract, the breaching party shall pay a certain amount of liquidated damages to the other party based on the circumstances of the breach, and may also agree on the method for calculating the amount of damages for losses caused by the breach.

If the agreed liquidated damages are lower than the losses caused, the people's court or arbitration institution may, upon the request of a party, increase them; if the agreed liquidated damages are excessively higher than the losses caused, the people's court or arbitration institution may, upon the request of a party, appropriately reduce them.

Where the parties agree on liquidated damages for delayed performance, the breaching party shall, after paying the liquidated damages, still perform the debt.

Article 586 The parties may agree that one party shall pay a deposit to the other party as security for the claim. The deposit contract shall be established upon the actual delivery of the deposit.

The amount of the deposit shall be agreed upon by the parties; however, it shall not exceed twenty percent of the value of the subject matter of the principal contract, and any excess shall not have the effect of a deposit. If the actual amount of the deposit delivered is more or less than the agreed amount, the agreed amount of the deposit shall be deemed to have been modified.

Article 587 Where the debtor performs the debt, the deposit shall be applied to the price or returned. If the party paying the deposit fails to perform the debt or performs it in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, thereby making it impossible to achieve the purpose of the contract, it shall not be entitled to demand the return of the deposit; if the party receiving the deposit fails to perform the debt or performs it in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, thereby making it impossible to achieve the purpose of the contract, it shall return double the deposit.

Article 588 Where the parties have agreed on both liquidated damages and a deposit, in the event of a breach of contract by one party, the other party may choose to apply either the liquidated damages clause or the deposit clause.

If the deposit is insufficient to compensate for the losses caused by the breach of one party, the other party may request compensation for the losses exceeding the amount of the deposit.

Article 589 Where the debtor performs the debt in accordance with the agreement, and the creditor refuses to accept it without justifiable reasons, the debtor may request the creditor to compensate for the increased expenses.

During the period of the creditor's delay in acceptance, the debtor is not required to pay interest.

Article 590 Where a party is unable to perform a contract due to force majeure, it shall be partially or wholly exempted from liability based on the impact of the force majeure, unless otherwise provided by law. Where a party is unable to perform a contract due to force majeure, it shall promptly notify the other party to mitigate the possible losses to the other party, and shall provide proof within a reasonable period.

If force majeure occurs after a party has delayed performance, its liability for breach of contract shall not be exempted.

Article 591 After a party breaches the contract, the other party shall take appropriate measures to prevent the expansion of losses; if the other party fails to take appropriate measures, resulting in the expansion of losses, it shall not be entitled to claim damages for the expanded losses.

Reasonable expenses incurred by a party in preventing the expansion of losses shall be borne by the breaching party.

Article 592 Where both parties are in breach of contract, they shall each bear their respective liabilities.

Where a party's breach of contract causes losses to the other party, and the other party is at fault for the occurrence of the losses, the amount of damages may be reduced accordingly.

Article 593 Where a party breaches a contract due to the act of a third party, it shall bear liability for breach of contract to the other party in accordance with the law. Disputes between the party and the third party shall be resolved in accordance with the law or the agreement.

Article 594 The statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit or applying for arbitration regarding disputes arising from contracts for the international sale of goods and contracts for technology import and export shall be four years.

### Part 2 Typical Contracts

#### Chapter 9 Sales Contracts

Article 595 A sales contract is a contract whereby the seller transfers the ownership of the subject matter to the buyer, and the buyer pays the price.

Article 596 The contents of a sales contract generally include the name, quantity, quality, and price of the subject matter, the period, place, and method of performance, the method of packaging, the inspection standards and methods, the method of settlement, the language used in the contract and its validity, and other clauses.

Article 597 Where the ownership of the subject matter cannot be transferred due to the seller's lack of the right of disposal, the buyer may rescind the contract and request the seller to bear liability for breach of contract.

Where the transfer of the subject matter is prohibited or restricted by laws or administrative regulations, the relevant provisions shall apply.

Article 598 The seller shall perform the obligation to deliver the subject matter or the documents for taking delivery of the subject matter to the buyer, and to transfer the ownership of the subject matter.

Article 599 The seller shall deliver to the buyer, in accordance with the agreement or trade practices, the relevant documents and materials other than the documents for taking delivery of the subject matter.

Article 600 Where the subject matter sold has intellectual property rights, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties, the intellectual property rights in the subject matter shall not belong to the buyer.

Article 601 The seller shall deliver the subject matter at the agreed time. If a period for delivery is agreed upon, the seller may deliver at any time within that period.

Article 602 Where the parties have not agreed on the time for delivery of the subject matter or the agreement is unclear, the provisions of Articles 510 and 511(4) of this Code shall apply.

Article 603 The seller shall deliver the subject matter at the agreed place.

Where the parties have not agreed on the place of delivery or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the following provisions shall apply:

(1) If the subject matter requires carriage, the seller shall deliver the subject matter to the first carrier for dispatch to the buyer;
(2) If the subject matter does not require carriage, and the seller and the buyer knew the location of the subject matter at the time of entering into the contract, the seller shall deliver the subject matter at that location; if the location of the subject matter is unknown, the seller shall deliver the subject matter at its place of business at the time of entering into the contract.

Article 604 The risk of damage or loss of the subject matter shall be borne by the seller before delivery and by the buyer after delivery, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 605 Where the subject matter is not delivered at the agreed time due to the buyer's reasons, the buyer shall bear the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter from the time of the breach.

Article 606 Where the seller sells a subject matter that is in transit and delivered to a carrier, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, the risk of damage or loss shall be borne by the buyer from the time the contract is formed.

Article 607 After the seller delivers the subject matter to the buyer's designated place and hands it over to the carrier in accordance with the agreement, the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter shall be borne by the buyer.

Where the parties have not agreed on the place of delivery or the agreement is unclear, and the subject matter requires carriage in accordance with Article 603(2)(1) of this Code, the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter shall be borne by the buyer after the seller delivers the subject matter to the first carrier.

Article 608 Where the seller places the subject matter at the place of delivery in accordance with the agreement or Article 603(2)(2) of this Code, and the buyer fails to take delivery in breach of the agreement, the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter shall be borne by the buyer from the time of the breach.

Article 609 The seller's failure to deliver the relevant documents and materials of the subject matter in accordance with the agreement shall not affect the transfer of the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter.

Article 610 Where the subject matter fails to meet the quality requirements, thereby making it impossible to achieve the purpose of the contract, the buyer may refuse to accept the subject matter or rescind the contract. If the buyer refuses to accept the subject matter or rescinds the contract, the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter shall be borne by the seller.

Article 611 Where the risk of damage or loss of the subject matter is borne by the buyer, it shall not affect the buyer's right to request the seller to bear liability for breach of contract due to the seller's failure to perform its obligations in accordance with the agreement.

Article 612 The seller shall have the obligation to ensure that no third party has any rights to the delivered subject matter, unless otherwise provided by law.

Article 613 Where the buyer knew or should have known at the time of entering into the contract that a third party had rights to the subject matter of the sale, the seller shall not bear the obligation specified in the preceding article.

Article 614 Where the buyer has conclusive evidence that a third party has rights to the subject matter, it may suspend payment of the corresponding price, unless the seller provides appropriate security.

Article 615 The seller shall deliver the subject matter in accordance with the agreed quality requirements. If the seller provides a description of the quality of the subject matter, the delivered subject matter shall meet the quality requirements described.

Article 616 Where the parties have not agreed on the quality requirements of the subject matter or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the provisions of Article 511(1) of this Code shall apply.Article 617 Where the subject matter delivered by the seller fails to conform to the quality requirements, the buyer may demand that the seller assume liability for breach of contract in accordance with Articles 582 through 584 of this Code.

Article 618 Where the parties agree to mitigate or exempt the seller's liability for defects in the subject matter, but the seller intentionally or through gross negligence fails to inform the buyer of such defects, the seller shall not be entitled to claim mitigation or exemption of liability.

Article 619 The seller shall deliver the subject matter in the agreed manner of packaging. Where there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on the manner of packaging, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the subject matter shall be packaged in a general manner; if there is no general manner, it shall be packaged in a manner sufficient to protect the subject matter and conducive to resource conservation and ecological environmental protection.

Article 620 The buyer shall inspect the subject matter within the agreed inspection period upon receipt. If no inspection period is agreed upon, the buyer shall inspect it promptly.

Article 621 Where the parties have agreed on an inspection period, the buyer shall notify the seller within that period of any non-conformity in the quantity or quality of the subject matter. If the buyer fails to give such notice, the subject matter shall be deemed to conform to the agreement in quantity and quality.

Where the parties have not agreed on an inspection period, the buyer shall notify the seller within a reasonable period after discovering or ought to have discovered the non-conformity in quantity or quality. If the buyer fails to notify within a reasonable period or fails to notify the seller within two years from the date of receipt of the subject matter, the subject matter shall be deemed to conform to the agreement; however, if there is a quality guarantee period for the subject matter, the quality guarantee period shall apply, and the two-year provision shall not apply.

If the seller knew or ought to have known that the subject matter delivered did not conform to the agreement, the buyer shall not be subject to the time limits for notification specified in the preceding two paragraphs.

Article 622 Where the agreed inspection period is too short for the buyer to complete a comprehensive inspection given the nature of the subject matter and trade practices, that period shall be deemed only as the period for the buyer to raise objections regarding apparent defects of the subject matter.

If the agreed inspection period or quality guarantee period is shorter than the period prescribed by laws or administrative regulations, the period prescribed by laws or administrative regulations shall prevail.

Article 623 Where the parties have not agreed on an inspection period, and the delivery note, confirmation note, or similar document signed by the buyer specifies the quantity, model, or specification of the subject matter, it shall be presumed that the buyer has inspected the quantity and apparent defects, unless sufficient evidence to the contrary exists.

Article 624 Where the seller delivers the subject matter to a third party in accordance with the buyer's instructions, and the inspection standards agreed between the seller and the buyer differ from those agreed between the buyer and the third party, the inspection standards agreed between the seller and the buyer shall prevail.

Article 625 Where, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, or the agreement of the parties, the subject matter is to be recycled after the expiration of its useful life, the seller shall be obligated to recycle the subject matter itself or entrust a third party to do so.

Article 626 The buyer shall pay the price in the agreed amount and manner. If the amount and manner of payment are not agreed upon or are unclear, the provisions of Article 510 and Items 2 and 5 of Article 511 of this Code shall apply.

Article 627 The buyer shall pay the price at the agreed place. If the place of payment is not agreed upon or is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the buyer shall pay at the seller's place of business; however, if payment of the price is conditional upon delivery of the subject matter or the documents for taking delivery of the subject matter, payment shall be made at the place where the subject matter or such documents are delivered.

Article 628 The buyer shall pay the price at the agreed time. If the time of payment is not agreed upon or is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the buyer shall pay at the same time as receiving the subject matter or the documents for taking delivery of the subject matter.

Article 629 If the seller delivers an excess quantity of the subject matter, the buyer may accept or reject the excess part. If the buyer accepts the excess part, it shall pay the price at the agreed rate; if the buyer rejects the excess part, it shall promptly notify the seller.

Article 630 Fruits generated from the subject matter before delivery belong to the seller; those generated after delivery belong to the buyer, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 631 If the contract is rescinded due to non-conformity of the principal subject matter, the effect of rescission extends to the accessory subject matter. If the contract is rescinded due to non-conformity of the accessory subject matter, the effect of rescission does not extend to the principal subject matter.

Article 632 Where the subject matter consists of several items, and one of them does not conform to the agreement, the buyer may rescind the contract with respect to that item. However, if separation of that item from the others significantly impairs the value of the subject matter, the buyer may rescind the contract with respect to all items.

Article 633 Where the seller delivers the subject matter in batches, and the seller fails to deliver a batch or delivers it in a non-conforming manner, thereby preventing the purpose of the contract from being achieved for that batch, the buyer may rescind the contract with respect to that batch.

If the seller fails to deliver a batch or delivers it in a non-conforming manner, thereby preventing the purpose of the contract from being achieved for subsequent batches, the buyer may rescind the contract with respect to that batch and all subsequent batches.

If the buyer rescinds the contract with respect to one batch, and that batch is interdependent with other batches, the buyer may rescind the contract with respect to all batches, whether delivered or not.

Article 634 Where a buyer under an installment payment plan fails to pay an amount of the due price reaching one-fifth of the total price, and after being urged, still fails to pay the due price within a reasonable period, the seller may demand that the buyer pay the full price or rescind the contract.

If the seller rescinds the contract, it may demand that the buyer pay a usage fee for the subject matter.

Article 635 In a sale by sample, the parties shall seal the sample and may provide a description of its quality. The subject matter delivered by the seller shall conform to the quality of the sample and its description.

Article 636 In a sale by sample, if the buyer is unaware of latent defects in the sample, even if the delivered subject matter is identical to the sample, the quality of the subject matter delivered by the seller shall still conform to the ordinary standards for the same type of subject matter.

Article 637 In a sale on approval, the parties may agree on a trial period for the subject matter. If the trial period is not agreed upon or is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the seller shall determine it.

Article 638 In a sale on approval, the buyer may purchase or refuse to purchase the subject matter during the trial period. If the buyer does not indicate whether to purchase the subject matter upon expiration of the trial period, it shall be deemed a purchase.

If the buyer has paid part of the price or has engaged in acts such as selling, leasing, or creating a security interest in the subject matter during the trial period, it shall be deemed to have agreed to purchase.

Article 639 In a sale on approval, if the parties have not agreed or have not clearly agreed on a usage fee for the subject matter, the seller shall not be entitled to demand payment from the buyer.

Article 640 The risk of damage or loss of the subject matter during the trial period shall be borne by the seller.

Article 641 The parties may agree in a sales contract that if the buyer fails to pay the price or perform other obligations, the ownership of the subject matter shall remain with the seller.

The seller's retained ownership of the subject matter shall not be effective against a bona fide third party unless registered.

Article 642 Where the parties agree that the seller retains ownership of the subject matter of the contract, and before the transfer of ownership, the buyer commits any of the following acts causing damage to the seller, the seller shall have the right to repossess the subject matter, unless otherwise agreed by the parties:

(1) Failing to pay the price as agreed, and after being urged, still failing to pay within a reasonable period;
(2) Failing to fulfill specific conditions as agreed;
(3) Selling, pledging, or otherwise improperly disposing of the subject matter.

The seller may negotiate with the buyer for repossession of the subject matter; if negotiation fails, the procedures for realizing a security interest may be applied by reference.

Article 643 After the seller repossesses the subject matter in accordance with paragraph 1 of the preceding article, if the buyer eliminates the cause for repossession within a reasonable redemption period agreed by the parties or specified by the seller, the buyer may request redemption of the subject matter.

If the buyer fails to redeem the subject matter within the redemption period, the seller may sell the subject matter to a third party at a reasonable price. If the proceeds from the sale, after deducting the unpaid price and necessary expenses, have a surplus, it shall be returned to the buyer; if there is a deficiency, the buyer shall make up the difference.

Article 644 The rights and obligations of the parties in a sale by tender or bid, as well as the tender or bid procedures, shall be governed by relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 645 The rights and obligations of the parties in an auction, as well as the auction procedures, shall be governed by relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 646 Where laws provide for other onerous contracts, those provisions shall apply; in the absence of such provisions, the relevant provisions on sales contracts shall apply by reference.

Article 647 Where the parties agree to a barter transaction involving the transfer of ownership of the subject matter, the relevant provisions on sales contracts shall apply by reference.

#### Chapter 10: Contracts for Supply of Electricity, Water, Gas, and Heat

Article 648 A contract for the supply of electricity is a contract under which the supplier supplies electricity to the consumer, and the consumer pays the electricity fee.

A supplier that supplies electricity to the public shall not refuse a consumer's reasonable request to enter into a contract.

Article 649 The content of a contract for the supply of electricity generally includes clauses on the method, quality, and time of supply; the capacity, location, and nature of use; the metering method; the electricity price and settlement method; and the maintenance responsibilities for the supply facilities.

Article 650 The place of performance of a contract for the supply of electricity shall be as agreed by the parties; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the boundary point of ownership of the power supply facilities shall be the place of performance.

Article 651 The supplier shall supply electricity safely in accordance with the national quality standards and the agreement. If the supplier fails to do so, causing losses to the consumer, it shall be liable for compensation.

Article 652 If the supplier needs to interrupt the power supply due to planned maintenance, temporary maintenance, lawful load shedding, or the consumer's illegal use of electricity, it shall notify the consumer in advance in accordance with relevant national regulations; if it fails to do so, causing losses to the consumer, it shall be liable for compensation.

Article 653 If a power outage occurs due to natural disasters or other reasons, the supplier shall promptly carry out emergency repairs in accordance with relevant national regulations; if it fails to do so promptly, causing losses to the consumer, it shall be liable for compensation.

Article 654 The consumer shall pay the electricity fee promptly in accordance with relevant national regulations and the agreement. If the consumer fails to pay the electricity fee on time, it shall pay liquidated damages as agreed. If, after being urged, the consumer still fails to pay the electricity fee and liquidated damages within a reasonable period, the supplier may suspend the power supply in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the state.

If the supplier suspends the power supply in accordance with the preceding paragraph, it shall notify the consumer in advance.

Article 655 The consumer shall use electricity safely, economically, and in a planned manner in accordance with relevant national regulations and the agreement. If the consumer fails to do so, causing losses to the supplier, it shall be liable for compensation.

Article 656 Contracts for the supply of water, gas, and heat shall be governed by reference to the relevant provisions on contracts for the supply of electricity.

#### Chapter 11: Gift Contracts

Article 657 A gift contract is a contract under which the donor gives its own property to the donee gratuitously, and the donee indicates acceptance of the gift.

Article 658 The donor may revoke the gift before the transfer of rights in the gifted property.

The preceding paragraph shall not apply to notarized gift contracts or gift contracts of a charitable, poverty-alleviating, disability-assistance, or other public welfare or moral obligation nature that are irrevocable by law.

Article 659 If the gifted property requires registration or other formalities by law, such formalities shall be completed.

Article 660 If the donor fails to deliver the gifted property under a notarized gift contract or a gift contract of a charitable, poverty-alleviating, disability-assistance, or other public welfare or moral obligation nature that is irrevocable by law, the donee may demand delivery.

If the gifted property that should be delivered in accordance with the preceding paragraph is damaged or lost due to the donor's intentional act or gross negligence, the donor shall be liable for compensation.

Article 661 A gift may be made subject to obligations.

If a gift is made subject to obligations, the donee shall perform the obligations as agreed.

Article 662 The donor shall not be liable for defects in the gifted property. If a gift is made subject to obligations and the gifted property has defects, the donor shall bear the same liability as a seller to the extent of the obligations imposed.

If the donor intentionally fails to disclose defects or warrants that there are no defects, causing losses to the donee, the donor shall be liable for compensation.

Article 663 The donor may revoke the gift if the donee commits any of the following acts:

(1) Seriously infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of the donor or the donor's close relatives;
(2) Failing to perform the obligation to support the donor despite having such an obligation;
(3) Failing to perform the obligations stipulated in the gift contract.

The donor's right of revocation shall be exercised within one year from the date the donor knew or ought to have known of the grounds for revocation.

Article 664 If the donor dies or loses capacity for civil conduct due to the donee's illegal act, the donor's heir or legal representative may revoke the gift.

The right of revocation of the donor's heir or legal representative shall be exercised within six months from the date they knew or ought to have known of the grounds for revocation.

Article 665 If the person entitled to revocation revokes the gift, they may demand the return of the gifted property from the donee.

Article 666 If the donor's financial condition significantly deteriorates, seriously affecting its production, business, or family life, the donor may cease performing the gift obligation.

#### Chapter 12: Loan Contracts

Article 667 A loan contract is a contract under which the borrower borrows a sum of money from the lender and repays the loan with interest upon maturity.

Article 668 A loan contract shall be in writing, unless otherwise agreed between natural persons.

The content of a loan contract generally includes clauses on the type of loan, currency, purpose, amount, interest rate, term, and repayment method.

Article 669 When entering into a loan contract, the borrower shall provide truthful information about its business activities and financial status related to the loan as required by the lender.

Article 670 Interest on a loan shall not be deducted from the principal in advance. If interest is deducted from the principal in advance, the loan shall be repaid and interest calculated based on the actual amount borrowed.

Article 671 If the lender fails to provide the loan on the agreed date and in the agreed amount, causing losses to the borrower, it shall compensate for the losses.

If the borrower fails to draw down the loan on the agreed date and in the agreed amount, it shall pay interest on the agreed date and amount.

Article 672 The lender may, as agreed, inspect and supervise the use of the loan. The borrower shall regularly provide the lender with relevant financial accounting statements or other materials as agreed.

Article 673 If the borrower uses the loan for a purpose other than that agreed upon, the lender may stop disbursing the loan, accelerate the repayment, or rescind the contract.

Article 674 The borrower shall pay interest at the agreed intervals. If the interval for interest payment is not agreed upon or is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, if the loan term is less than one year, interest shall be paid together with the repayment of the principal; if the loan term is one year or more, interest shall be paid at the end of each year, and if the remaining period is less than one year, interest shall be paid together with the repayment of the principal.

Article 675 The borrower shall repay the loan at the agreed time. If the loan term is not agreed upon or is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the borrower may repay at any time; the lender may urge the borrower to repay within a reasonable period.

Article 676 If the borrower fails to repay the loan at the agreed time, it shall pay overdue interest in accordance with the agreement or relevant national regulations.

Article 677 If the borrower repays the loan early, interest shall be calculated based on the actual period of the loan, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 678 The borrower may apply to the lender for an extension of the repayment period before its expiration; if the lender agrees, the period may be extended.

Article 679 A loan contract between natural persons is established when the lender provides the loan.

Article 680 Usurious lending is prohibited, and the interest rate on a loan shall not violate relevant national regulations.

If a loan contract does not provide for the payment of interest, it shall be deemed to have no interest.

If a loan contract provides for the payment of interest but the terms are unclear, and the parties cannot reach a supplementary agreement, interest shall be determined based on factors such as local or the parties' transaction methods, trade practices, and market interest rates; for loans between natural persons, it shall be deemed to have no interest.

#### Chapter 13: Guarantee Contracts

##### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 681 A guarantee contract is a contract under which the guarantor and the creditor agree that, when the debtor fails to perform its due debt or when the circumstances agreed upon by the parties occur, the guarantor shall perform the debt or assume liability to ensure the realization of the creditor's rights.

Article 682 A guarantee contract is an ancillary contract to the principal creditor-debtor contract. If the principal creditor-debtor contract is invalid, the guarantee contract shall also be invalid, unless otherwise provided by law.

After a guarantee contract is confirmed invalid, if the debtor, guarantor, or creditor is at fault, they shall each bear corresponding civil liability according to their fault.

Article 683 An institutional legal person shall not act as a guarantor, except where approved by the State Council for on-lending loans from foreign governments or international economic organizations.

Non-profit legal persons and unincorporated organizations established for public welfare purposes shall not act as guarantors.

Article 684 The content of a guarantee contract generally includes clauses on the type and amount of the principal creditor's right being guaranteed, the time limit for the debtor to perform the debt, and the method, scope, and period of the guarantee.

Article 685 A guarantee contract may be a separate written contract or a guarantee clause in the principal creditor-debtor contract.

If a third party unilaterally makes a guarantee to the creditor in writing, and the creditor accepts it without objection, the guarantee contract is established.

Article 686 Methods of guarantee include general guarantee and joint and several liability guarantee.

If the parties do not agree or the agreement is unclear on the method of guarantee in the guarantee contract, the guarantor shall bear the guarantee liability as a general guarantee.

Article 687 A general guarantee is one where the parties agree in the guarantee contract that the guarantor shall bear the guarantee liability only when the debtor is unable to perform the debt.A surety under a general suretyship has the right to refuse to assume suretyship liability to the creditor before the dispute over the principal contract has been adjudicated or arbitrated and the debtor's property has been enforced in accordance with the law but is insufficient to satisfy the debt, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The debtor's whereabouts are unknown and there is no property available for enforcement;

(2) A people's court has already accepted a bankruptcy case against the debtor;

(3) The creditor has evidence proving that the debtor's property is insufficient to satisfy all debts or that the debtor has lost the ability to perform the debts;

(4) The surety has waived the rights provided for in this paragraph in writing.

Article 688 Where the parties agree in the suretyship contract that the surety and the debtor shall be jointly and severally liable for the debt, this constitutes a joint and several suretyship.

Where a debtor under a joint and several suretyship fails to perform its due debt or a circumstance agreed upon by the parties occurs, the creditor may request the debtor to perform the debt, or may request the surety to assume suretyship liability within the scope of its suretyship.

Article 689 A surety may require the debtor to provide a counter-guarantee.

Article 690 A surety and a creditor may negotiate to enter into a contract for a maximum amount suretyship, agreeing to provide suretyship for claims arising continuously within a certain period up to a maximum amount of claims.

Unless otherwise provided in this Chapter, the provisions of Part Two of this Code regarding maximum amount mortgages shall apply mutatis mutandis to maximum amount suretyships.

##### Section 2 Suretyship Liability

Article 691 The scope of suretyship includes the principal claim and its interest, liquidated damages, damages for loss, and the costs of realizing the claim. If the parties have agreed otherwise, such agreement shall prevail.

Article 692 The suretyship period is the period during which the surety's liability to assume suretyship is determined, and it is not subject to suspension, interruption, or extension.

The creditor and the surety may agree on a suretyship period. However, if the agreed suretyship period expires earlier than or on the same date as the performance period of the principal debt, it shall be deemed as no agreement; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the suretyship period shall be six months from the date the performance period of the principal debt expires.

If the creditor and the debtor have no agreement on the performance period of the principal debt or the agreement is unclear, the suretyship period shall be calculated from the date the grace period for the creditor to request the debtor to perform the debt expires.

Article 693 If a creditor under a general suretyship fails to file a lawsuit or apply for arbitration against the debtor within the suretyship period, the surety shall no longer bear suretyship liability.

If a creditor under a joint and several suretyship fails to request the surety to assume suretyship liability within the suretyship period, the surety shall no longer bear suretyship liability.

Article 694 If a creditor under a general suretyship files a lawsuit or applies for arbitration against the debtor before the expiration of the suretyship period, the statute of limitations for the suretyship debt shall begin to run from the date the surety's right to refuse to assume suretyship liability is extinguished.

If a creditor under a joint and several suretyship requests the surety to assume suretyship liability before the expiration of the suretyship period, the statute of limitations for the suretyship debt shall begin to run from the date the creditor requests the surety to assume suretyship liability.

Article 695 If the creditor and the debtor, without the written consent of the surety, negotiate to modify the content of the principal claim and debt contract, and the modification reduces the debt, the surety shall still bear suretyship liability for the modified debt; if the modification increases the debt, the surety shall not bear suretyship liability for the increased portion.

If the creditor and the debtor modify the performance period of the principal claim and debt contract without the written consent of the surety, the suretyship period shall not be affected.

Article 696 If the creditor transfers all or part of the claim without notifying the surety, such transfer shall not be effective against the surety.

If the surety and the creditor agree to prohibit the transfer of the claim, and the creditor transfers the claim without the written consent of the surety, the surety shall no longer bear suretyship liability to the transferee.

Article 697 If the creditor, without the written consent of the surety, allows the debtor to transfer all or part of the debt, the surety shall no longer bear suretyship liability for the debt transferred without its consent, unless otherwise agreed by the creditor and the surety.

If a third party joins the debt, the surety's suretyship liability shall not be affected.

Article 698 After the performance period of the principal debt expires, if a surety under a general suretyship provides the creditor with true information about the debtor's property available for enforcement, and the creditor waives or neglects to exercise its rights, causing such property to be unenforceable, the surety shall no longer bear suretyship liability to the extent of the value of the property provided as available for enforcement.

Article 699 If there are two or more sureties for the same debt, the sureties shall bear suretyship liability in accordance with the suretyship shares agreed in the suretyship contract; if no suretyship shares are agreed, the creditor may request any surety to assume suretyship liability within the scope of its suretyship.

Article 700 After a surety has assumed suretyship liability, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, it has the right to seek recourse against the debtor within the scope of the suretyship liability it has assumed, and enjoys the creditor's rights against the debtor, but shall not harm the interests of the creditor.

Article 701 A surety may assert against the creditor any defense that the debtor has against the creditor. If the debtor waives its defense, the surety still has the right to assert the defense against the creditor.

Article 702 If the debtor has a right of set-off or a right of revocation against the creditor, the surety may refuse to assume suretyship liability within the corresponding scope.

#### Chapter 14 Lease Contracts

Article 703 A lease contract is a contract whereby the lessor delivers the leased property to the lessee for use and enjoyment, and the lessee pays rent.

Article 704 The content of a lease contract generally includes the name, quantity, and purpose of the leased property, the lease term, the rent and its payment term and method, and the maintenance of the leased property.

Article 705 The lease term shall not exceed twenty years. If it exceeds twenty years, the portion exceeding twenty years shall be void.

Upon the expiration of the lease term, the parties may renew the lease contract; however, the agreed lease term from the date of renewal shall not exceed twenty years.

Article 706 The failure of the parties to register or file a lease contract in accordance with laws or administrative regulations shall not affect the validity of the contract.

Article 707 If the lease term is six months or more, the contract shall be in writing. If the parties fail to adopt a written form and the lease term cannot be determined, the lease shall be deemed a lease for an indefinite term.

Article 708 The lessor shall deliver the leased property to the lessee as agreed and shall, during the lease term, keep the leased property fit for the agreed purpose.

Article 709 The lessee shall use the leased property in the agreed manner. If there is no agreement or the agreement on the method of using the leased property is unclear, and it still cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the lessee shall use the leased property according to its nature.

Article 710 If the lessee uses the leased property in the agreed manner or according to its nature, causing wear and tear to the leased property, the lessee shall not be liable for compensation.

Article 711 If the lessee fails to use the leased property in the agreed manner or according to its nature, causing damage to the leased property, the lessor may terminate the contract and claim damages.

Article 712 The lessor shall perform the obligation to maintain the leased property, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 713 When the leased property requires maintenance, the lessee may request the lessor to carry out the maintenance within a reasonable period. If the lessor fails to perform its maintenance obligation, the lessee may carry out the maintenance itself, and the maintenance costs shall be borne by the lessor. If the maintenance of the leased property affects the lessee's use, the rent shall be reduced accordingly or the lease term shall be extended.

If the leased property requires maintenance due to the lessee's fault, the lessor shall not be liable for the maintenance obligation provided in the preceding paragraph.

Article 714 The lessee shall take good care of the leased property. If the leased property is damaged or lost due to improper care, the lessee shall be liable for compensation.

Article 715 With the lessor's consent, the lessee may make improvements to the leased property or add other items to it.

If the lessee makes improvements to the leased property or adds other items to it without the lessor's consent, the lessor may request the lessee to restore the property to its original state or compensate for the loss.

Article 716 With the lessor's consent, the lessee may sublease the leased property to a third party. If the lessee subleases the property, the lease contract between the lessee and the lessor remains valid; if the third party causes damage to the leased property, the lessee shall compensate for the loss.

If the lessee subleases the property without the lessor's consent, the lessor may terminate the contract.

Article 717 If the lessee, with the lessor's consent, subleases the leased property to a third party, and the sublease term exceeds the remaining term of the lessee's lease, the agreement on the exceeding portion shall not be legally binding on the lessor, unless otherwise agreed by the lessor and the lessee.

Article 718 If the lessor knows or should know that the lessee has subleased the property but fails to raise an objection within six months, the lessor shall be deemed to have consented to the sublease.

Article 719 If the lessee is in arrears with the rent, the sub-lessee may pay the overdue rent and liquidated damages on behalf of the lessee, unless the sublease contract is not legally binding on the lessor.

The rent and liquidated damages paid by the sub-lessee on behalf of the lessee may be offset against the rent payable by the sub-lessee to the lessee; if the amount exceeds the rent payable by the sub-lessee, the sub-lessee may seek recourse against the lessee.

Article 720 During the lease term, the income derived from the possession and use of the leased property shall belong to the lessee, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 721 The lessee shall pay the rent within the agreed period. If there is no agreement or the agreement on the rent payment period is unclear, and it still cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, if the lease term is less than one year, the rent shall be paid upon the expiration of the lease term; if the lease term is one year or more, the rent shall be paid at the end of each year, and if the remaining term is less than one year, the rent shall be paid upon the expiration of the lease term.

Article 722 If the lessee fails to pay or delays payment of the rent without justifiable reasons, the lessor may request the lessee to pay within a reasonable period; if the lessee fails to pay within the extended period, the lessor may terminate the contract.

Article 723 If a third party asserts rights, preventing the lessee from using and enjoying the leased property, the lessee may request a reduction in rent or exemption from paying rent.

If a third party asserts rights, the lessee shall promptly notify the lessor.

Article 724 Under any of the following circumstances, if the leased property cannot be used for reasons not attributable to the lessee, the lessee may terminate the contract:

(1) The leased property is sealed up, seized, or detained by a judicial or administrative authority in accordance with the law;

(2) The ownership of the leased property is disputed;

(3) The leased property violates the mandatory provisions of laws or administrative regulations regarding conditions of use.

Article 725 A change in the ownership of the leased property during the period the lessee is in possession of it under the lease contract shall not affect the validity of the lease contract.

Article 726 If the lessor sells the leased house, it shall notify the lessee within a reasonable period before the sale, and the lessee has the right of first refusal to purchase the house under the same conditions; except where a co-owner of the house by shares exercises the right of first refusal or the lessor sells the house to a close relative.

After the lessor fulfills its notification obligation, if the lessee fails to clearly express its intention to purchase within fifteen days, the lessee shall be deemed to have waived the right of first refusal.

Article 727 If the lessor entrusts an auctioneer to auction the leased house, it shall notify the lessee five days before the auction. If the lessee does not participate in the auction, it shall be deemed to have waived the right of first refusal.

Article 728 If the lessor fails to notify the lessee or otherwise hinders the lessee from exercising the right of first refusal, the lessee may request the lessor to bear liability for compensation. However, the validity of the house sale contract concluded between the lessor and the third party shall not be affected.

Article 729 If the leased property is partially or wholly damaged or lost due to reasons not attributable to the lessee, the lessee may request a reduction in rent or exemption from paying rent; if the purpose of the contract cannot be achieved due to the partial or total damage or loss of the leased property, the lessee may terminate the contract.

Article 730 If the parties have no agreement on the lease term or the agreement is unclear, and it still cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the lease shall be deemed a lease for an indefinite term; the parties may terminate the contract at any time, but shall notify the other party within a reasonable period in advance.

Article 731 If the leased property endangers the lessee's safety or health, the lessee may terminate the contract at any time, even if the lessee knew at the time of concluding the contract that the leased property was defective in quality.

Article 732 If the lessee dies during the lease term of the house, the persons who lived with the lessee or jointly operated with the lessee may lease the house under the original lease contract.

Article 733 Upon the expiration of the lease term, the lessee shall return the leased property. The returned leased property shall be in a condition consistent with its use in the agreed manner or according to its nature.

Article 734 Upon the expiration of the lease term, if the lessee continues to use the leased property and the lessor raises no objection, the original lease contract shall remain valid, but the lease term shall be for an indefinite period.

Upon the expiration of the lease term, the lessee of a house has the right to renew the lease under the same conditions.

#### Chapter 15 Financial Lease Contracts

Article 735 A financial lease contract is a contract whereby the lessor, based on the lessee's choice of the seller and the leased property, purchases the leased property from the seller and provides it to the lessee for use, and the lessee pays rent.

Article 736 The content of a financial lease contract generally includes the name, quantity, specifications, technical performance, and inspection method of the leased property, the lease term, the composition of the rent and its payment term and method, the currency, and the ownership of the leased property upon the expiration of the lease term.

A financial lease contract shall be in writing.

Article 737 A financial lease contract concluded by the parties by fabricating the leased property is void.

Article 738 If, in accordance with laws or administrative regulations, an administrative license is required for the operation or use of the leased property, the lessor's failure to obtain the administrative license shall not affect the validity of the financial lease contract.

Article 739 In a sales contract concluded by the lessor based on the lessee's choice of the seller and the leased property, the seller shall deliver the subject matter to the lessee as agreed, and the lessee enjoys the rights of a buyer related to the acceptance of the subject matter.

Article 740 If the seller violates its obligation to deliver the subject matter to the lessee under any of the following circumstances, the lessee may refuse to accept the subject matter delivered by the seller:

(1) The subject matter seriously fails to conform to the agreement;

(2) The subject matter is not delivered as agreed, and after being urged by the lessee or the lessor, it is still not delivered within a reasonable period.

If the lessee refuses to accept the subject matter, it shall promptly notify the lessor.

Article 741 The lessor, seller, and lessee may agree that if the seller fails to perform its obligations under the sales contract, the lessee shall exercise the right to claim compensation. If the lessee exercises the right to claim compensation, the lessor shall provide assistance.

Article 742 The lessee's exercise of the right to claim compensation against the seller shall not affect its obligation to pay rent. However, if the lessee relied on the lessor's skill to determine the leased property or the lessor interfered with the selection of the leased property, the lessee may request a corresponding reduction or exemption of rent.

Article 743 If the lessor is under any of the following circumstances, causing the lessee's failure to exercise the right to claim compensation against the seller, the lessee has the right to request the lessor to bear corresponding liability:

(1) Knowing of a quality defect in the leased property but failing to inform the lessee;

(2) Failing to provide necessary assistance promptly when the lessee exercises the right to claim compensation.

If the lessor neglects to exercise the right to claim compensation that can only be exercised by the lessor against the seller, causing loss to the lessee, the lessee has the right to request the lessor to bear liability for compensation.

Article 744 In a sales contract concluded by the lessor based on the lessee's choice of the seller and the leased property, the lessor shall not change the contract content related to the lessee without the lessee's consent.

Article 745 The lessor's ownership of the leased property, if not registered, shall not be asserted against a bona fide third party.

Article 746 The rent under a financial lease contract shall, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, be determined based on most or all of the costs of purchasing the leased property and the lessor's reasonable profit.

Article 747 If the leased property does not conform to the agreement or is not fit for the purpose of use, the lessor shall not be liable, except where the lessee relied on the lessor's skill to determine the leased property or the lessor interfered with the selection of the leased property.

Article 748 The lessor shall ensure the lessee's possession and use of the leased property.

If the lessor is under any of the following circumstances, the lessee has the right to request it to compensate for the loss:

(1) Recovering the leased property without justifiable reasons;

(2) Unjustifiably hindering or interfering with the lessee's possession and use of the leased property;

(3) Causing a third party to assert rights over the leased property due to the lessor's reasons;

(4) Other circumstances that improperly affect the lessee's possession and use of the leased property.

Article 749 During the period the lessee is in possession of the leased property, if the leased property causes personal injury or property damage to a third party, the lessor shall not be liable.

Article 750 The lessee shall properly keep and use the leased property.

The lessee shall perform the obligation to maintain the leased property during the period it is in possession of it.

Article 751 If the leased property is damaged or lost during the period the lessee is in possession of it, the lessor has the right to request the lessee to continue paying the rent, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

Article 752 The lessee shall pay the rent as agreed. If the lessee fails to pay the rent within a reasonable period after being urged, the lessor may request payment of all rent; or may terminate the contract and recover the leased property.

Article 753 If the lessee, without the lessor's consent, transfers, mortgages, pledges, contributes as capital, or otherwise disposes of the leased property, the lessor may terminate the financial lease contract.

Article 754 Under any of the following circumstances, the lessor or the lessee may terminate the financial lease contract:

(1) The sales contract concluded between the lessor and the seller is terminated, confirmed void, or revoked, and a new sales contract cannot be concluded;

(2) The leased property is damaged or lost due to reasons not attributable to the parties, and cannot be repaired or a substitute cannot be determined;

(3) The purpose of the financial lease contract cannot be achieved due to the seller's reasons.

Article 755 If a financial lease contract is terminated due to the termination, confirmation of voidness, or revocation of the sales contract, and the seller and the leased property were chosen by the lessee, the lessor has the right to request the lessee to compensate for corresponding losses; except where the termination, confirmation of voidness, or revocation of the sales contract is caused by the lessor's reasons.

If the lessor's losses have already been compensated upon the termination, confirmation of voidness, or revocation of the sales contract, the lessee shall no longer bear corresponding liability for compensation.

Article 756 If a financial lease contract is terminated due to the accidental damage or loss of the leased property after delivery to the lessee, or other reasons not attributable to the parties, the lessor may request the lessee to provide compensation based on the depreciation of the leased property.Article 757 The lessor and the lessee may agree on the ownership of the leased property upon the expiration of the lease term; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on the ownership of the leased property, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Law, the ownership of the leased property shall belong to the lessor.

Article 758 Where the parties agree that the leased property shall belong to the lessee upon the expiration of the lease term, and the lessee has paid most of the rent but is unable to pay the remaining rent, and the lessor therefore rescinds the contract and takes back the leased property, if the value of the taken-back leased property exceeds the rent and other expenses owed by the lessee, the lessee may request a corresponding refund.

Where the parties agree that the leased property shall belong to the lessor upon the expiration of the lease term, and the lessee is unable to return the property due to its destruction, loss, or attachment to or mixing with other property, the lessor has the right to request reasonable compensation from the lessee.

Article 759 Where the parties agree that upon the expiration of the lease term, the lessee only needs to pay a nominal price to the lessor, it shall be deemed that the ownership of the leased property belongs to the lessee after the performance of the agreed rent obligations is completed.

Article 760 If a financial leasing contract is invalid, and the parties have an agreement on the ownership of the leased property under such circumstances, that agreement shall prevail; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the leased property shall be returned to the lessor. However, if the invalidity of the contract is caused by the lessee, and the lessor does not request the return, or returning the property would significantly reduce its utility, the ownership of the leased property shall belong to the lessee, who shall provide reasonable compensation to the lessor.

#### Chapter 16 Factoring Contracts

Article 761 A factoring contract is a contract under which an accounts receivable creditor transfers its existing or future accounts receivable to a factor, and the factor provides services such as financing, management or collection of accounts receivable, or payment guarantee for the debtor of the accounts receivable.

Article 762 The content of a factoring contract generally includes clauses such as the type of business, scope of services, service term, details of the underlying transaction contract, information on the accounts receivable, factoring financing funds or service remuneration, and the payment method thereof.

A factoring contract shall be in writing.

Article 763 If an accounts receivable creditor and debtor fabricate accounts receivable as the subject of transfer and enter into a factoring contract with a factor, the debtor of the accounts receivable shall not defend against the factor on the grounds that the accounts receivable do not exist, unless the factor knew of the fabrication.

Article 764 When a factor sends a notice of assignment of accounts receivable to the debtor of the accounts receivable, the factor shall indicate its identity and attach necessary supporting documents.

Article 765 After the debtor of the accounts receivable receives the notice of assignment, if the creditor and debtor of the accounts receivable, without justifiable reasons, negotiate to change or terminate the underlying transaction contract, causing adverse effects on the factor, such change or termination shall not be effective against the factor.

Article 766 Where the parties agree on factoring with recourse, the factor may claim against the creditor of the accounts receivable for the return of the principal and interest of the factoring financing or the repurchase of the accounts receivable, or may claim against the debtor of the accounts receivable for the accounts receivable. If, after the factor claims against the debtor of the accounts receivable and deducts the principal and interest of the factoring financing and related expenses, there is a surplus, the surplus shall be returned to the creditor of the accounts receivable.

Article 767 Where the parties agree on factoring without recourse, the factor shall claim against the debtor of the accounts receivable for the accounts receivable. The factor does not need to return to the creditor of the accounts receivable any portion exceeding the principal and interest of the factoring financing and related expenses.

Article 768 If a creditor of accounts receivable enters into multiple factoring contracts for the same accounts receivable, causing multiple factors to claim rights, the factor that has registered shall have priority over the unregistered factor in obtaining the accounts receivable; if all have registered, the accounts receivable shall be obtained in the order of registration time; if none have registered, the factor specified in the notice of assignment that first reaches the debtor of the accounts receivable shall obtain the accounts receivable; if neither registration nor notice has occurred, the accounts receivable shall be obtained in proportion to the factoring financing funds or service remuneration.

Article 769 For matters not provided for in this Chapter, the relevant provisions of Chapter 6 of this Part on the assignment of creditor's rights shall apply.

#### Chapter 17 Work Contracts

Article 770 A work contract is a contract under which the contractor completes work and delivers the work results according to the requirements of the client, and the client pays remuneration.

Work includes processing, manufacturing, repairing, reproducing, testing, inspecting, and other such tasks.

Article 771 The content of a work contract generally includes clauses such as the subject matter of the work, quantity, quality, remuneration, method of work, provision of materials, performance period, acceptance standards and methods.

Article 772 The contractor shall complete the main work using its own equipment, technology, and labor, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

If the contractor entrusts the main work to a third party, it shall be liable to the client for the work results completed by that third party; if the client does not consent, the client may also rescind the contract.

Article 773 The contractor may entrust auxiliary work to a third party. If the contractor entrusts auxiliary work to a third party, it shall be liable to the client for the work results completed by that third party.

Article 774 If the contractor provides materials, it shall select materials as agreed and submit to inspection by the client.

Article 775 If the client provides materials, it shall provide them as agreed. The contractor shall promptly inspect the materials provided by the client; if they are found not to conform to the agreement, the contractor shall promptly notify the client to replace, supplement, or take other remedial measures.

The contractor shall not replace the materials provided by the client without authorization, nor replace parts that do not require repair.

Article 776 If the contractor discovers that the drawings or technical requirements provided by the client are unreasonable, it shall promptly notify the client. If the client causes losses to the contractor due to delay in responding or other reasons, it shall compensate for the losses.

Article 777 If the client changes the requirements for the work midway, causing losses to the contractor, it shall compensate for the losses.

Article 778 If the work requires the assistance of the client, the client has the obligation to provide assistance. If the client fails to perform its assistance obligation, causing the work to be unable to be completed, the contractor may urge the client to perform the obligation within a reasonable period and may extend the performance period; if the client fails to perform within the extended period, the contractor may rescind the contract.

Article 779 During the work period, the contractor shall accept necessary supervision and inspection by the client. The client shall not hinder the contractor's normal work due to supervision and inspection.

Article 780 Upon completion of the work, the contractor shall deliver the work results to the client and submit necessary technical materials and relevant quality certificates. The client shall accept the work results.

Article 781 If the work results delivered by the contractor do not meet the quality requirements, the client may reasonably choose to request the contractor to bear liability for breach of contract such as repair, rework, reduction of remuneration, or compensation for losses.

Article 782 The client shall pay remuneration within the agreed period. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on the period for paying remuneration, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Law, the client shall pay when the contractor delivers the work results; if the work results are delivered in part, the client shall pay correspondingly.

Article 783 If the client fails to pay the contractor remuneration or material costs, the contractor has a lien on the completed work results or has the right to refuse delivery, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 784 The contractor shall properly keep the materials provided by the client and the completed work results; if damage or loss occurs due to improper storage, it shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 785 The contractor shall keep secrets as required by the client and shall not retain copies or technical materials without the client's permission.

Article 786 Co-contractors shall be jointly and severally liable to the client, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

Article 787 The client may rescind the contract at any time before the contractor completes the work; if this causes losses to the contractor, it shall compensate for the losses.

#### Chapter 18 Construction Project Contracts

Article 788 A construction project contract is a contract under which the contractor undertakes the construction of a project, and the client pays the price.

Construction project contracts include contracts for survey, design, and construction of the project.

Article 789 A construction project contract shall be in writing.

Article 790 Bidding and tendering activities for construction projects shall be conducted openly, fairly, and impartially in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws.

Article 791 The client may enter into a construction project contract with a general contractor, or may enter into separate survey, design, and construction contracts with the surveyor, designer, and constructor. The client shall not divide a construction project that should be completed by one contractor into several parts and contract them out to multiple contractors.

With the consent of the client, the general contractor or the survey, design, or construction contractor may entrust part of the contracted work to a third party. The third party shall be jointly and severally liable with the general contractor or the survey, design, or construction contractor to the client for the work results completed. The contractor shall not subcontract the entire construction project to a third party or divide the entire contracted project and subcontract it to third parties in the name of subcontracting.

Contractors are prohibited from subcontracting projects to entities without corresponding qualifications. Subcontractors are prohibited from re-subcontracting the projects they have contracted. The construction of the main structure of a construction project must be completed by the contractor itself.

Article 792 Contracts for major national construction projects shall be entered into in accordance with the procedures stipulated by the state and documents such as the state-approved investment plan and feasibility study report.

Article 793 If a construction project construction contract is invalid, but the construction project has passed acceptance inspection, the contractor may be compensated by reference to the agreement on the project price in the contract.

If a construction project construction contract is invalid, and the construction project fails the acceptance inspection, it shall be handled according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the repaired construction project passes the acceptance inspection, the client may request the contractor to bear the repair costs;

(2) If the repaired construction project fails the acceptance inspection, the contractor has no right to request compensation by reference to the agreement on the project price in the contract.

If the client is at fault for the losses caused by the unqualified construction project, it shall bear corresponding responsibility.

Article 794 The content of a survey and design contract generally includes clauses such as the period for submitting relevant basic data and budget/estimate documents, quality requirements, fees, and other cooperation conditions.

Article 795 The content of a construction contract generally includes clauses such as the scope of the project, construction period, start and completion times of intermediate handover projects, project quality, project cost, time for delivery of technical data, responsibility for supply of materials and equipment, allocation and settlement, completion acceptance, scope of quality warranty and warranty period, and mutual cooperation.

Article 796 If supervision is implemented for a construction project, the client shall enter into a written contract of mandate for supervision with the supervisor. The rights, obligations, and legal liabilities of the client and the supervisor shall be in accordance with the provisions of this Part on mandate contracts and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 797 The client may inspect the progress and quality of the work at any time, provided it does not hinder the normal operations of the contractor.

Article 798 Before concealed work is concealed, the contractor shall notify the client for inspection. If the client fails to inspect in a timely manner, the contractor may extend the project period and has the right to claim compensation for losses such as work stoppage and idle labor.

Article 799 After the completion of a construction project, the client shall promptly conduct acceptance inspection in accordance with the construction drawings and specifications, the construction acceptance specifications issued by the state, and quality inspection standards. If the project passes the acceptance inspection, the client shall pay the price as agreed and accept the construction project.

A construction project may only be delivered for use after passing the completion acceptance inspection; it shall not be delivered for use if it has not been inspected or has failed the inspection.

Article 800 If the quality of the survey or design does not meet the requirements, or if the survey or design documents are not submitted within the specified period, causing delays and losses to the client, the surveyor or designer shall continue to improve the survey or design, reduce or waive the survey or design fees, and compensate for losses.

Article 801 If the quality of the construction project does not meet the agreement due to the constructor's reasons, the client has the right to request the constructor to repair, redo, or reconstruct free of charge within a reasonable period. If the project is delivered late after repair, redo, or reconstruction, the constructor shall bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 802 If personal injury or property damage occurs within the reasonable service life of a construction project due to the contractor's reasons, the contractor shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 803 If the client fails to provide raw materials, equipment, site, funds, or technical data according to the agreed time and requirements, the contractor may extend the project period and has the right to claim compensation for losses such as work stoppage and idle labor.

Article 804 If the project is suspended or delayed midway due to the client's reasons, the client shall take measures to remedy or reduce the losses and compensate the contractor for losses and actual expenses incurred, such as work stoppage, idle labor, material handling, machinery and equipment relocation, and accumulation of materials and components.

Article 805 If the client changes the plan, provides inaccurate data, or fails to provide necessary survey or design working conditions within the specified period, causing rework, suspension, or design modification in the survey or design, the client shall pay additional fees based on the actual workload consumed by the surveyor or designer.

Article 806 If the contractor subcontracts or illegally subcontracts the construction project, the client may rescind the contract.

If the client fails to provide main construction materials, building components, and equipment that meet mandatory standards, or fails to perform its assistance obligations, making it impossible for the contractor to proceed with construction, and after being urged, fails to perform the corresponding obligations within a reasonable period, the contractor may rescind the contract.

After the contract is rescinded, if the quality of the completed construction project is qualified, the client shall pay the corresponding project price as agreed; if the quality of the completed construction project is unqualified, the provisions of Article 793 of this Law shall apply.

Article 807 If the client fails to pay the price as agreed, the contractor may urge the client to pay within a reasonable period. If the client fails to pay within the extended period, unless the nature of the construction project makes it unsuitable for conversion into a price or auction, the contractor may agree with the client to convert the project into a price, or may request the People's Court to auction the project according to law. The price of the construction project shall be given priority for compensation from the proceeds of the conversion or auction.

Article 808 For matters not provided for in this Chapter, the relevant provisions on work contracts shall apply.

#### Chapter 19 Transport Contracts

##### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 809 A transport contract is a contract under which the carrier transports passengers or goods from the place of departure to the agreed destination, and the passenger, consignor, or consignee pays the fare or freight.

Article 810 A carrier engaged in public transport shall not refuse the usual and reasonable transport requests of passengers or consignors.

Article 811 The carrier shall transport passengers and goods safely to the agreed destination within the agreed period or a reasonable period.

Article 812 The carrier shall transport passengers and goods to the agreed destination via the agreed or usual transport route.

Article 813 Passengers, consignors, or consignees shall pay the fare or freight. If the carrier increases the fare or freight by not following the agreed or usual route, the passenger, consignor, or consignee may refuse to pay the increased portion.

##### Section 2 Passenger Transport Contracts

Article 814 A passenger transport contract is formed when the carrier issues a ticket to the passenger, unless otherwise agreed by the parties or there is a different trade practice.

Article 815 Passengers shall board according to the time, shift, and seat number indicated on the valid ticket. Passengers who travel without a ticket, beyond the paid distance, in a higher class than paid for, or with a discounted ticket not meeting the discount conditions, shall make up the fare, and the carrier may charge an additional fare according to regulations; if the passenger does not pay the fare, the carrier may refuse transport.

If a passenger under a real-name passenger transport contract loses the ticket, they may request the carrier to report the loss and reissue the ticket, and the carrier shall not charge the fare again or any other unreasonable fees.

Article 816 If a passenger cannot board at the time indicated on the ticket for reasons attributable to themselves, they shall go through the procedures for refund or change within the agreed period; if they fail to do so within the period, the carrier may refuse to refund the fare and shall no longer be obligated to transport.

Article 817 Carry-on luggage of passengers shall comply with the agreed limits on quantity and type; if luggage exceeds the limits or violates the type requirements, it shall be checked in.

Article 818 Passengers shall not carry or include in their luggage dangerous goods or prohibited items that are flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, radioactive, or may endanger the safety of persons or property on the means of transport.

If a passenger violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the carrier may unload, destroy, or hand over the dangerous goods or prohibited items to the relevant authorities. If the passenger insists on carrying or including dangerous goods or prohibited items, the carrier shall refuse transport.

Article 819 The carrier shall strictly perform its duty of safe transport and promptly inform passengers of matters requiring attention for safe transport. Passengers shall actively assist and cooperate with the carrier's reasonable arrangements for safe transport.

Article 820 The carrier shall transport passengers according to the time, shift, and seat number indicated on the valid ticket. If the carrier delays transport or has other abnormal transport situations, it shall promptly inform and remind passengers, take necessary accommodation measures, and arrange for a change to another shift or a refund according to the passenger's request; if this causes losses to the passenger, the carrier shall bear liability for compensation, unless the delay or situation is not attributable to the carrier.

Article 821 If the carrier unilaterally lowers the service standard, it shall refund the fare or reduce the fare at the passenger's request; if it raises the service standard, it shall not charge an additional fare.

Article 822 During transport, the carrier shall do its utmost to rescue passengers who are acutely ill, in childbirth, or in danger.

Article 823 The carrier shall bear liability for compensation for the death or injury of passengers during transport; however, if the death or injury is caused by the passenger's own health condition, or if the carrier proves that the death or injury was caused by the passenger's intentional act or gross negligence, the carrier shall not be liable.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph apply to passengers who ride without a ticket, with a preferential ticket, or with the carrier's permission as stipulated by regulations.

Article 824 If carry-on items of a passenger are damaged or lost during transport, and the carrier is at fault, it shall bear liability for compensation.

For damage or loss of checked luggage, the relevant provisions on goods transport shall apply.

##### Section 3 Goods Transport ContractsArticle 825 When consigning goods for carriage, the consignor shall accurately inform the carrier of the name or title of the consignee, or the consignee to be notified, the name, nature, weight, quantity of the goods, the place of receipt, and other necessary information relating to the carriage of the goods.

If the carrier suffers losses due to the consignor's false declaration or omission of important information, the consignor shall be liable for compensation.

Article 826 Where the carriage of goods requires procedures such as approval or inspection, the consignor shall submit the documents evidencing completion of such procedures to the carrier.

Article 827 The consignor shall package the goods in the agreed manner. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on the packaging method, the provisions of Article 619 of this Code shall apply.

If the consignor violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the carrier may refuse to transport the goods.

Article 828 Where the consignor consigns dangerous goods such as inflammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, or radioactive articles, it shall properly package the dangerous goods in accordance with the state regulations on the transport of dangerous goods, affix danger marks and labels, and submit to the carrier written materials concerning the name, nature, and preventive measures for the dangerous goods.

If the consignor violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the carrier may refuse to transport the goods, or may take corresponding measures to avoid the occurrence of losses, and the expenses incurred thereby shall be borne by the consignor.

Article 829 Before the carrier delivers the goods to the consignee, the consignor may request the carrier to suspend the carriage, return the goods, change the place of destination, or deliver the goods to another consignee, provided that it compensates the carrier for any losses incurred thereby.

Article 830 After the goods arrive at the place of destination, if the carrier knows the consignee, it shall promptly notify the consignee, and the consignee shall promptly take delivery of the goods. If the consignee delays in taking delivery, it shall pay the carrier storage fees and other expenses.

Article 831 When taking delivery of the goods, the consignee shall inspect the goods within the agreed period. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear on the inspection period, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the inspection shall be carried out within a reasonable period. If the consignee raises no objection to the quantity, damage, or other aspects of the goods within the agreed period or a reasonable period, it shall be deemed as prima facie evidence that the carrier has delivered the goods as recorded in the transport document.

Article 832 The carrier shall be liable for compensation for the damage to or loss of the goods during the course of carriage. However, the carrier shall not be liable for compensation if it proves that the damage to or loss of the goods was caused by force majeure, the natural nature of the goods themselves or reasonable wear and tear, or the fault of the consignor or consignee.

Article 833 The amount of compensation for damage to or loss of the goods shall be calculated according to the agreement of the parties; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, it shall be calculated based on the market price at the place of destination at the time of delivery or when delivery should have been made. If laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for the method of calculating the amount of compensation and the limit of compensation, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 834 Where two or more carriers operate a combined transport using the same mode of transport, the carrier that entered into the contract of carriage with the consignor shall be liable for the entire carriage; if the loss occurs in a specific section of the carriage, the carrier that entered into the contract with the consignor and the carrier of that section shall be jointly and severally liable.

Article 835 If the goods are lost during carriage due to force majeure and the freight has not been collected, the carrier shall not be entitled to demand payment of the freight; if the freight has already been collected, the consignor may request its return. If the law provides otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 836 If the consignor or consignee fails to pay the freight, storage fees, or other expenses, the carrier shall have a lien over the corresponding transported goods, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

Article 837 If the consignee is unknown or refuses to take delivery of the goods without justifiable reason, the carrier may deposit the goods in accordance with the law.

##### Section 4   Multimodal Transport Contracts

Article 838 The multimodal transport operator shall be responsible for performing or organizing the performance of the multimodal transport contract and shall enjoy the rights and assume the obligations of a carrier for the entire carriage.

Article 839 The multimodal transport operator may agree with the carriers of each section participating in the multimodal transport on their respective responsibilities for the carriage of each section under the multimodal transport contract; however, such agreement shall not affect the obligations of the multimodal transport operator for the entire carriage.

Article 840 When the multimodal transport operator receives the goods delivered by the consignor, it shall issue a multimodal transport document. At the request of the consignor, the multimodal transport document may be a negotiable document or a non-negotiable document.

Article 841 If the multimodal transport operator suffers losses due to the fault of the consignor when consigning the goods, the consignor shall still be liable for compensation, even if it has already transferred the multimodal transport document.

Article 842 If the damage to or loss of the goods occurs in a specific section of the multimodal transport, the liability for compensation and the limit of liability of the multimodal transport operator shall be governed by the laws applicable to the mode of transport of that section; if the section of transport where the damage to or loss of the goods occurred cannot be determined, the liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

#### Chapter 20   Technology Contracts

##### Section 1   General Provisions

Article 843 A technology contract is a contract concluded between parties to establish rights and obligations regarding the development, transfer, licensing, consultation, or service of technology.

Article 844 The conclusion of a technology contract shall be conducive to the protection of intellectual property rights and the advancement of science and technology, and shall promote the research, development, transformation, application, and dissemination of scientific and technological achievements.

Article 845 The content of a technology contract generally includes clauses such as the name of the project, the subject matter, scope, and requirements of the subject matter, the plan, place, and method of performance, the confidentiality of technical information and materials, the ownership of technological achievements and the method of distributing proceeds, the acceptance criteria and methods, and the interpretation of terms and phrases.

Technical background materials, feasibility studies and technical evaluation reports, project task books and plans, technical standards, technical specifications, original designs and process documents, and other technical documents related to the performance of the contract may, as agreed by the parties, form part of the contract.

If a technology contract involves a patent, it shall indicate the name of the invention-creation, the patent applicant and patentee, the application date, application number, patent number, and the term of validity of the patent right.

Article 846 The method of payment of the price, remuneration, or royalty under a technology contract shall be agreed upon by the parties. It may be a lump sum payment, a lump sum payment in installments, or a royalty payment, or a royalty payment plus an upfront entry fee.

If a royalty payment is agreed upon, it may be calculated as a percentage of the product price, the increased output value, profit, or product sales volume after the implementation of the patent or the use of the technical know-how, or calculated in other agreed manners. The percentage of the royalty may be a fixed percentage, a percentage that increases year by year, or a percentage that decreases year by year.

If a royalty payment is agreed upon, the parties may agree on a method for inspecting the relevant accounting books.

Article 847 Where the right to use and the right to transfer a service technological achievement belong to a legal person or an unincorporated organization, the legal person or unincorporated organization may conclude a technology contract regarding such service technological achievement. When a legal person or unincorporated organization concludes a technology contract to transfer a service technological achievement, the person who completed the service technological achievement shall have the right of first refusal under the same conditions.

A service technological achievement is a technological achievement completed in the performance of a task assigned by a legal person or an unincorporated organization, or mainly by utilizing the material and technical conditions of the legal person or unincorporated organization.

Article 848 The right to use and the right to transfer a non-service technological achievement belong to the individual who completed the technological achievement, and such individual may conclude a technology contract regarding such non-service technological achievement.

Article 849 The individual who completed a technological achievement shall have the right to be named as the person who completed the technological achievement in the relevant documents of the technological achievement and the right to obtain certificates of honor and rewards.

Article 850 A technology contract that illegally monopolizes technology or infringes upon the technological achievements of others is void.

##### Section 2   Technology Development Contracts

Article 851 A technology development contract is a contract concluded between parties for the research and development of new technologies, new products, new processes, new varieties, or new materials and their systems.

Technology development contracts include commissioned development contracts and cooperative development contracts.

Technology development contracts shall be in writing.

Contracts concluded between parties for the transformation of scientific and technological achievements with practical value shall be governed by reference to the relevant provisions on technology development contracts.

Article 852 The commissioning party under a commissioned development contract shall, in accordance with the contract, pay the research and development expenses and remuneration, provide technical materials, put forward research and development requirements, complete the matters of cooperation, and accept the research and development results.

Article 853 The research and development party under a commissioned development contract shall, in accordance with the contract, formulate and implement a research and development plan, use the research and development expenses reasonably, complete the research and development work on schedule, deliver the research and development results, provide relevant technical materials and necessary technical guidance, and assist the commissioning party in mastering the research and development results.

Article 854 If a party to a commissioned development contract violates the contract, causing the research and development work to be stalled, delayed, or fail, it shall bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 855 The parties to a cooperative development contract shall, in accordance with the contract, make investments, including investments in the form of technology, participate in the research and development work with a division of labor, and cooperate and coordinate in the research and development work.

Article 856 If a party to a cooperative development contract violates the contract, causing the research and development work to be stalled, delayed, or fail, it shall bear liability for breach of contract.

Article 857 If the technology that is the subject matter of a technology development contract has already been disclosed by others, rendering the performance of the technology development contract meaningless, the parties may rescind the contract.

Article 858 During the performance of a technology development contract, if the research and development fails or partially fails due to insurmountable technical difficulties, the risk shall be borne as agreed by the parties; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the risk shall be reasonably shared by the parties.

If one party discovers the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph that may cause the research and development to fail or partially fail, it shall promptly notify the other party and take appropriate measures to reduce the losses; if it fails to promptly notify and take appropriate measures, causing the losses to increase, it shall be liable for the increased losses.

Article 859 For an invention-creation completed under a commissioned development, unless otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties, the right to apply for a patent belongs to the research and development party. If the research and development party obtains a patent right, the commissioning party may exploit the patent in accordance with the law.

If the research and development party transfers the right to apply for a patent, the commissioning party shall have the right of first refusal under the same conditions.

Article 860 For an invention-creation completed under a cooperative development, the right to apply for a patent belongs jointly to the parties to the cooperative development; if one party transfers its joint right to apply for a patent, the other parties shall have the right of first refusal under the same conditions, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

If one party to a cooperative development waives its joint right to apply for a patent, unless the parties have agreed otherwise, the other party may apply alone, or the other parties may jointly apply. If the applicant obtains a patent right, the party that waived the right to apply for a patent may exploit the patent free of charge.

If one party to a cooperative development does not agree to apply for a patent, the other party or parties shall not apply for a patent.

Article 861 The right to use, the right to transfer, and the method of distributing proceeds from the technical know-how results completed under a commissioned development or cooperative development shall be agreed upon by the parties; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, before the same technical solution is granted a patent right, each party shall have the right to use and transfer it. However, the research and development party under a commissioned development shall not transfer the research and development results to a third party before delivering them to the commissioning party.

##### Section 3   Technology Transfer Contracts and Technology License Contracts

Article 862 A technology transfer contract is a contract concluded by a right holder who legally owns technology to transfer the relevant rights of an existing specific patent, patent application, or technical know-how to another person.

A technology license contract is a contract concluded by a right holder who legally owns technology to license another person to exploit or use the relevant rights of an existing specific patent or technical know-how.

Agreements in a technology transfer contract or technology license contract concerning the provision of special equipment, raw materials, or related technical consulting or technical services for exploiting the technology shall form part of the contract.

Article 863 Technology transfer contracts include contracts for the transfer of patent rights, contracts for the transfer of the right to apply for a patent, and contracts for the transfer of technical know-how.

Technology license contracts include contracts for the license to exploit a patent and contracts for the license to use technical know-how.

Technology transfer contracts and technology license contracts shall be in writing.

Article 864 Technology transfer contracts and technology license contracts may stipulate the scope of exploiting the patent or using the technical know-how, but shall not restrict technological competition or technological development.

Article 865 A patent exploitation license contract shall only be valid within the duration of the patent right. If the term of validity of the patent right expires or the patent right is declared invalid, the patentee shall not conclude a patent exploitation license contract with another person regarding that patent.

Article 866 The licensor under a patent exploitation license contract shall, in accordance with the contract, license the licensee to exploit the patent, deliver the technical materials related to the exploitation of the patent, and provide necessary technical guidance.

Article 867 The licensee under a patent exploitation license contract shall exploit the patent in accordance with the contract, shall not license a third party other than those agreed upon to exploit the patent, and shall pay the royalty in accordance with the contract.

Article 868 The transferor under a technical know-how transfer contract and the licensor under a technical know-how use license contract shall, in accordance with the contract, provide technical materials, provide technical guidance, ensure the practicality and reliability of the technology, and assume confidentiality obligations.

The confidentiality obligations specified in the preceding paragraph shall not restrict the licensor from applying for a patent, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

Article 869 The transferee under a technical know-how transfer contract and the licensee under a technical know-how use license contract shall use the technology in accordance with the contract, pay the transfer fee or royalty, and assume confidentiality obligations.

Article 870 The transferor under a technology transfer contract and the licensor under a technology license contract shall guarantee that they are the legal owners of the technology provided and that the technology provided is complete, error-free, effective, and capable of achieving the agreed objectives.

Article 871 The transferee under a technology transfer contract and the licensee under a technology license contract shall assume confidentiality obligations regarding the undisclosed confidential parts of the technology provided by the transferor or licensor, within the agreed scope and period.

Article 872 If the licensor fails to license the technology in accordance with the contract, it shall refund part or all of the royalty and shall bear liability for breach of contract; if it exploits the patent or uses the technical know-how beyond the agreed scope, or, in violation of the contract, licenses a third party to exploit the patent or use the technical know-how without authorization, it shall cease the breach and bear liability for breach of contract; if it violates the agreed confidentiality obligations, it shall bear liability for breach of contract.

The liability for breach of contract of the transferor shall be governed by reference to the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 873 If the licensee fails to pay the royalty in accordance with the contract, it shall make up the payment and pay liquidated damages as agreed; if it fails to make up the payment or pay the liquidated damages, it shall cease exploiting the patent or using the technical know-how, return the technical materials, and bear liability for breach of contract; if it exploits the patent or uses the technical know-how beyond the agreed scope, or, without the consent of the licensor, licenses a third party to exploit the patent or use the technical know-how without authorization, it shall cease the breach and bear liability for breach of contract; if it violates the agreed confidentiality obligations, it shall bear liability for breach of contract.

The liability for breach of contract of the transferee shall be governed by reference to the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 874 If the exploitation of a patent or the use of technical know-how by the transferee or licensee in accordance with the contract infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of others, the transferor or licensor shall be liable, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

Article 875 The parties may, in accordance with the principle of mutual benefit, agree in the contract on the method of sharing the technical achievements resulting from subsequent improvements made to the exploited patent or used technical know-how; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the other parties shall have no right to share the technical achievements resulting from subsequent improvements made by one party.

Article 876 The transfer and licensing of other intellectual property rights such as the exclusive right to layout-designs of integrated circuits, the right to new plant varieties, and the copyright in computer software shall be governed by reference to the relevant provisions of this Section.

Article 877 If laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for technology import and export contracts or contracts for patents or patent applications, those provisions shall prevail.

##### Section 4   Technical Consulting Contracts and Technical Service Contracts

Article 878 A technical consulting contract is a contract concluded by one party, using its technical knowledge, to provide the other party with feasibility studies, technical forecasts, special technical investigations, analysis and evaluation reports, etc., for specific technical projects.

A technical service contract is a contract concluded by one party, using its technical knowledge, to solve specific technical problems for the other party, excluding work contracts and construction project contracts.

Article 879 The commissioning party under a technical consulting contract shall, in accordance with the contract, clarify the consulting questions, provide technical background materials and relevant technical materials, accept the work results of the受托人, and pay the remuneration.
Article 880 The trustee under a technical consulting contract shall complete the consulting report or answer the questions within the agreed period, and the consulting report submitted shall meet the agreed requirements.
Article 881 If the commissioning party under a technical consulting contract fails to provide the necessary materials as agreed, affecting the work progress and quality, or fails to accept or delays in accepting the work results, the remuneration paid shall not be recoverable, and the unpaid remuneration shall be paid.

If the trustee under a technical consulting contract fails to submit the consulting report on time or the submitted consulting report does not meet the contract requirements, it shall bear liability for breach of contract, such as reducing or waiving the remuneration.
Losses incurred by the commissioning party under a technical consulting contract as a result of making decisions based on the consulting report and opinions of the trustee that meet the contract requirements shall be borne by the commissioning party, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.
Article 882 The commissioning party under a technical service contract shall, in accordance with the contract, provide working conditions, complete the matters of cooperation, accept the work results, and pay the remuneration.

Article 883 The受托人 under a technical service contract shall, in accordance with the contract, complete the service items, solve the technical problems, ensure the quality of the work, and impart the knowledge for solving the technical problems.Article 884 Where the client of a technical service contract fails to perform its contractual obligations or performs them in a manner inconsistent with the agreement, thereby affecting the work progress and quality, or fails to accept or delays in accepting the work results, the remuneration already paid shall not be recovered, and the unpaid remuneration shall still be paid.
Where the trustee (service provider) of a technical service contract fails to complete the service work as agreed, it shall bear liability for breach of contract, such as waiving the remuneration.
Article 885 During the performance of a technical consulting contract or a technical service contract, new technical achievements made by the service provider using the technical materials and working conditions provided by the client shall belong to the service provider. New technical achievements made by the client using the work results of the service provider shall belong to the client. If the parties have agreed otherwise, that agreement shall prevail.
Article 886 Where a technical consulting contract or a technical service contract does not specify or clearly specify the burden of expenses necessary for the service provider to carry out its normal work, such expenses shall be borne by the service provider.
Article 887 Where laws or administrative regulations have other provisions regarding technical intermediary contracts or technical training contracts, those provisions shall apply.

#### Chapter 21: Custody Contracts

Article 888 A custody contract is a contract under which the custodian keeps the property delivered by the depositor for safekeeping and returns the property.

Where a depositor engages in activities such as shopping, dining, or lodging at the custodian's premises and deposits items in a designated place, it shall be deemed as custody, unless the parties have agreed otherwise or there is a different trade practice.

Article 889 The depositor shall pay the custodian a custody fee as agreed.

If the parties have not agreed on the custody fee or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the custody shall be deemed gratuitous.

Article 890 A custody contract is formed upon the delivery of the property for safekeeping, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

Article 891 Where the depositor delivers the property to the custodian for safekeeping, the custodian shall issue a custody certificate, unless there is a different trade practice.

Article 892 The custodian shall properly keep the property in custody.

The parties may agree on the place or method of custody. Except in an emergency or for the benefit of the depositor, the custodian shall not change the place or method of custody without authorization.

Article 893 Where the property delivered by the depositor for safekeeping has defects or requires special custody measures due to its nature, the depositor shall inform the custodian of the relevant circumstances. If the depositor fails to inform and the property is damaged as a result, the custodian shall not be liable for compensation. If the custodian suffers losses as a result, the depositor shall be liable for compensation, unless the custodian knew or should have known and failed to take remedial measures.

Article 894 The custodian shall not transfer the property in custody to a third party for safekeeping, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

If the custodian violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and transfers the property to a third party for safekeeping, causing damage to the property, the custodian shall be liable for compensation.

Article 895 The custodian shall not use or permit a third party to use the property in custody, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

Article 896 Where a third party claims rights over the property in custody, the custodian shall perform its obligation to return the property to the depositor, except where preservative measures or enforcement measures are taken against the property in accordance with the law.

If a third party files a lawsuit against the custodian or applies for seizure of the property in custody, the custodian shall promptly notify the depositor.

Article 897 During the custody period, if the property in custody is damaged or lost due to the custodian's failure to keep it properly, the custodian shall be liable for compensation. However, if a gratuitous custodian proves that it was not intentional or grossly negligent, it shall not be liable for compensation.

Article 898 Where a depositor deposits currency, securities, or other valuable items, it shall declare them to the custodian, who shall inspect or seal them. If the depositor fails to declare them, and the items are damaged or lost, the custodian may compensate them as ordinary items.

Article 899 The depositor may retrieve the property in custody at any time.

If the parties have not agreed on the custody period or the agreement is unclear, the custodian may request the depositor to retrieve the property at any time. If a custody period is agreed upon, the custodian shall not request the depositor to retrieve the property before the expiry of the period without special reasons.

Article 900 Upon the expiry of the custody period or when the depositor retrieves the property early, the custodian shall return the original property and its fruits to the depositor.

Article 901 Where the custodian keeps currency, it may return currency of the same type and amount. Where it keeps other fungible items, it may return items of the same type, quality, and quantity as agreed.

Article 902 For a paid custody contract, the depositor shall pay the custody fee to the custodian within the agreed period.

If the parties have not agreed on the payment period or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Code, the fee shall be paid at the time of retrieving the property.

Article 903 Where the depositor fails to pay the custody fee or other expenses as agreed, the custodian has a lien over the property in custody, unless the parties have agreed otherwise.

#### Chapter 22: Warehousing Contracts

Article 904 A warehousing contract is a contract under which the custodian stores the goods delivered by the depositor, and the depositor pays the storage fee.

Article 905 A warehousing contract is formed when the custodian and the depositor reach a mutual agreement.

Article 906 Where dangerous goods such as flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, or radioactive items, or perishable items are stored, the depositor shall explain the nature of the goods and provide relevant materials.

If the depositor violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the custodian may refuse to accept the goods or take corresponding measures to avoid losses, and the expenses incurred shall be borne by the depositor.

Where the custodian stores dangerous goods such as flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, or radioactive items, it shall have the corresponding storage conditions.

Article 907 The custodian shall inspect the goods delivered for storage as agreed. If the custodian finds that the goods do not conform to the agreement during inspection, it shall promptly notify the depositor. After inspection, if the type, quantity, or quality of the goods does not conform to the agreement, the custodian shall be liable for compensation.

Article 908 Where the depositor delivers the goods for storage, the custodian shall issue a warehouse receipt, a storage receipt, or other certificates.

Article 909 The custodian shall sign or affix its seal on the warehouse receipt. The warehouse receipt shall include the following items:

(1) The name or title and domicile of the depositor;
(2) The type, quantity, quality, packaging, number of pieces, and marks of the goods;
(3) The standard for loss of the goods;
(4) The storage place;
(5) The storage period;
(6) The storage fee;
(7) If the goods have been insured, the insurance amount, period, and the name of the insurer;
(8) The issuer, place of issue, and date of issue.

Article 910 A warehouse receipt is a certificate for retrieving the goods. The depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt may transfer the right to retrieve the goods by endorsing the warehouse receipt and having it signed or sealed by the custodian.

Article 911 The custodian shall, upon the request of the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt, allow them to inspect the goods or take samples.

Article 912 If the custodian discovers that the goods in storage have deteriorated or suffered other damage, it shall promptly notify the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt.

Article 913 If the custodian discovers that the goods in storage have deteriorated or suffered other damage, endangering the safety and normal storage of other goods, it shall urge the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt to take necessary measures. In an emergency, the custodian may take necessary measures; however, it shall promptly notify the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt afterwards.

Article 914 Where the parties have not agreed on the storage period or the agreement is unclear, the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt may retrieve the goods at any time, and the custodian may also request the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt to retrieve the goods at any time, but shall allow necessary preparation time.

Article 915 Upon the expiry of the storage period, the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt shall retrieve the goods by presenting the warehouse receipt, storage receipt, or other certificates. If the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt retrieves the goods late, an additional storage fee shall be charged; if they retrieve the goods early, the storage fee shall not be reduced.

Article 916 Upon the expiry of the storage period, if the depositor or the holder of the warehouse receipt does not retrieve the goods, the custodian may urge them to retrieve the goods within a reasonable period. If they still do not retrieve the goods after the expiry of that period, the custodian may deposit the goods.

Article 917 During the storage period, if the goods are damaged or lost due to improper storage, the custodian shall be liable for compensation. If the goods deteriorate or are damaged due to their natural nature, improper packaging, or exceeding the valid storage period, the custodian shall not be liable for compensation.

Article 918 Matters not provided for in this Chapter shall be governed by the relevant provisions on custody contracts.

#### Chapter 23: Mandate Contracts

Article 919 A mandate contract is a contract under which the principal and the agent agree that the agent shall handle the affairs of the principal.

Article 920 The principal may specifically entrust the agent to handle one or several affairs, or may generally entrust the agent to handle all affairs.

Article 921 The principal shall advance the expenses for handling the entrusted affairs. For necessary expenses advanced by the agent in handling the entrusted affairs, the principal shall repay them with interest.

Article 922 The agent shall handle the entrusted affairs in accordance with the instructions of the principal. If it is necessary to change the instructions of the principal, the agent shall obtain the consent of the principal. In an emergency where it is difficult to contact the principal, the agent shall properly handle the entrusted affairs, but shall promptly report the situation to the principal afterwards.

Article 923 The agent shall handle the entrusted affairs personally. With the consent of the principal, the agent may sub-entrust the affairs. If the sub-entrustment is consented to or ratified, the principal may directly give instructions to the third party of the sub-entrustment regarding the entrusted affairs, and the agent shall only be liable for the selection of the third party and the instructions given to the third party. If the sub-entrustment is not consented to or ratified, the agent shall be liable for the actions of the third party of the sub-entrustment; except where the agent needs to sub-entrust to a third party in an emergency to protect the interests of the principal.

Article 924 The agent shall report the status of handling the entrusted affairs as requested by the principal. Upon the termination of the mandate contract, the agent shall report the results of the entrusted affairs.

Article 925 Where the agent enters into a contract with a third party in its own name within the scope of authorization of the principal, and the third party knows at the time of entering into the contract about the agency relationship between the agent and the principal, the contract shall directly bind the principal and the third party; except where there is conclusive evidence that the contract only binds the agent and the third party.

Article 926 Where the agent enters into a contract with a third party in its own name, and the third party does not know about the agency relationship between the agent and the principal, if the agent fails to perform its obligations towards the principal due to the third party's reasons, the agent shall disclose the third party to the principal, and the principal may then exercise the agent's rights against the third party. However, this shall not apply if the third party would not have entered into the contract had it known the principal.

If the agent fails to perform its obligations towards the third party due to the principal's reasons, the agent shall disclose the principal to the third party, and the third party may then choose either the agent or the principal as the counterparty to assert its rights, but the third party may not change the chosen counterparty.

Where the principal exercises the agent's rights against the third party, the third party may assert against the principal any defenses it has against the agent. Where the third party chooses the principal as its counterparty, the principal may assert against the third party any defenses it has against the agent, as well as any defenses the agent has against the third party.

Article 927 The property obtained by the agent in handling the entrusted affairs shall be transferred to the principal.

Article 928 Where the agent completes the entrusted affairs, the principal shall pay the agent remuneration as agreed.

If the mandate contract is terminated or the entrusted affairs cannot be completed due to reasons not attributable to the agent, the principal shall pay the agent corresponding remuneration. If the parties have agreed otherwise, that agreement shall prevail.

Article 929 For a paid mandate contract, if the agent causes losses to the principal due to its fault, the principal may claim compensation for the losses. For a gratuitous mandate contract, if the agent causes losses to the principal due to its intentional act or gross negligence, the principal may claim compensation for the losses.

If the agent causes losses to the principal by exceeding its authority, it shall compensate for the losses.

Article 930 Where the agent suffers losses in handling the entrusted affairs due to reasons not attributable to itself, it may claim compensation for the losses from the principal.

Article 931 With the consent of the agent, the principal may entrust a third party to handle the entrusted affairs in addition to the agent. If this causes losses to the agent, the agent may claim compensation for the losses from the principal.

Article 932 Where two or more agents jointly handle the entrusted affairs, they shall bear joint and several liability towards the principal.

Article 933 The principal or the agent may terminate the mandate contract at any time. If the termination of the contract causes losses to the other party, unless the losses are due to reasons not attributable to the terminating party, the terminating party of a gratuitous mandate contract shall compensate for the direct losses caused by the improper timing of termination, and the terminating party of a paid mandate contract shall compensate for the direct losses of the other party and the benefits that could have been obtained after the performance of the contract.

Article 934 The mandate contract shall terminate upon the death or termination of the principal, or the death, loss of civil capacity, or termination of the agent; except where the parties have agreed otherwise or where termination is inappropriate due to the nature of the entrusted affairs.

Article 935 Where the termination of the mandate contract due to the death of the principal or its being declared bankrupt or dissolved would harm the interests of the principal, the agent shall continue to handle the entrusted affairs until the principal's successor, estate administrator, or liquidator takes over the entrusted affairs.

Article 936 Where the mandate contract terminates due to the death, loss of civil capacity, or being declared bankrupt or dissolved of the agent, the agent's successor, estate administrator, legal representative, or liquidator shall promptly notify the principal. If the termination of the mandate contract would harm the interests of the principal, the agent's successor, estate administrator, legal representative, or liquidator shall take necessary measures until the principal handles the aftermath.

#### Chapter 24: Property Service Contracts

Article 937 A property service contract is a contract under which the property service provider provides services such as repair and maintenance of buildings and their ancillary facilities, environmental hygiene, and management and maintenance of related order within the property service area, and the owners pay the property service fee.

Property service providers include property service enterprises and other managers.

Article 938 The content of a property service contract generally includes clauses on service items, service quality, standards and methods for collecting service fees, use of maintenance funds, management and use of service premises, service period, and service handover.

Service commitments publicly made by the property service provider that are beneficial to the owners shall be deemed part of the property service contract.

A property service contract shall be in writing.

Article 939 The preliminary property service contract concluded by the construction unit with the property service provider in accordance with the law, and the property service contract concluded by the owners' committee with the property service provider selected by the owners' assembly in accordance with the law, are legally binding on the owners.

Article 940 Where, before the expiry of the service period stipulated in the preliminary property service contract concluded by the construction unit with the property service provider in accordance with the law, the owners' committee or the owners conclude a property service contract with a new property service provider that takes effect, the preliminary property service contract shall terminate.

Article 941 Where the property service provider entrusts part of the specialized service matters within the property service area to a professional service organization or other third party, it shall be responsible to the owners for those specialized service matters.

The property service provider shall not transfer all the property services it should provide to a third party, nor shall it split all property services and transfer them separately to third parties.

Article 942 The property service provider shall, in accordance with the agreement and the nature of the property's use, properly repair, maintain, clean, landscape, and manage the common parts of the owners within the property service area, maintain basic order within the property service area, and take reasonable measures to protect the personal and property safety of the owners.

For acts within the property service area that violate laws and regulations on public security, environmental protection, fire protection, etc., the property service provider shall promptly take reasonable measures to stop them, report to the relevant administrative departments, and assist in handling them.

Article 943 The property service provider shall regularly disclose to the owners in a reasonable manner and report to the owners' assembly and the owners' committee on service matters, responsible personnel, quality requirements, fee items, fee standards, performance status, as well as the use of maintenance funds, and the operation and income of the common parts of the owners.

Article 944 The owners shall pay the property service fee to the property service provider as agreed. If the property service provider has provided services in accordance with the agreement and relevant regulations, the owners shall not refuse to pay the property service fee on the grounds that they have not accepted or do not need the relevant property services.

If an owner violates the agreement and fails to pay the property service fee after the due date, the property service provider may urge the owner to pay within a reasonable period. If the owner still fails to pay after the expiry of the reasonable period, the property service provider may file a lawsuit or apply for arbitration.

The property service provider shall not collect the property service fee by cutting off power, water, heat, or gas supply.

Article 945 Where an owner decorates or renovates its house, it shall inform the property service provider in advance, comply with the reasonable precautions reminded by the property service provider, and cooperate with necessary on-site inspections.

Where an owner transfers or leases the exclusive part of the property, establishes a right of habitation, or changes the use of the common part in accordance with the law, it shall promptly inform the property service provider of the relevant circumstances.

Article 946 Where the owners jointly decide to dismiss the property service provider in accordance with the statutory procedures, they may terminate the property service contract. If they decide to dismiss, they shall notify the property service provider in writing 60 days in advance, unless the contract has agreed on a different notice period.

If the termination of the contract in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph causes losses to the property service provider, the owners shall compensate for the losses, unless the losses are due to reasons not attributable to the owners.

Article 947 Before the expiry of the property service period, if the owners jointly decide to renew the appointment in accordance with the law, they shall renew the property service contract with the original property service provider before the expiry of the contract period.

Before the expiry of the property service period, if the property service provider does not agree to renew the appointment, it shall notify the owners or the owners' committee in writing 90 days before the expiry of the contract period, unless the contract has agreed on a different notice period.

Article 948 After the expiry of the property service period, if the owners have not made a decision in accordance with the law to renew or appoint another property service provider, and the property service provider continues to provide property services, the original property service contract shall remain valid, but the service period shall be indefinite.

The parties may terminate an indefinite property service contract at any time, but shall notify the other party in writing 60 days in advance.**Article 949** Upon termination of the property service contract, the original property service provider shall withdraw from the property service area within the agreed period or a reasonable period, and shall hand over the property service premises, relevant facilities, and all materials necessary for property services to the owners' committee, the owners who decide to self-manage, or a person designated by them, cooperate with the new property service provider in the handover, and truthfully inform them of the use and management status of the property.
If the original property service provider violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall not request the property owner to pay property fees after the termination of the property service contract; if it causes losses to the property owner, it shall compensate for the losses.
**Article 950** After the termination of the property service contract, before the new property service provider selected by the owners or the owners' assembly, or the owners who decide to self-manage, takes over, the original property service provider shall continue to handle property service matters and may request the owners to pay property fees for that period.
#### Chapter 25: Commission Contracts

**Article 951** A commission contract is a contract under which a commission agent engages in trading activities in its own name for a principal, and the principal pays remuneration.

**Article 952** Expenses incurred by the commission agent in handling the entrusted affairs shall be borne by the commission agent, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

**Article 953** If the commission agent is in possession of the entrusted item, it shall keep the entrusted item in safe custody.

**Article 954** If the entrusted item has defects or is perishable or deteriorable at the time of delivery to the commission agent, the commission agent may dispose of the item with the consent of the principal; if it cannot promptly contact the principal, the commission agent may dispose of it reasonably.

**Article 955** If the commission agent sells at a price lower than the price specified by the principal or buys at a price higher than the price specified by the principal, the consent of the principal shall be obtained; if the commission agent compensates for the difference without the principal's consent, the sale or purchase is binding on the principal.

If the commission agent sells at a price higher than the price specified by the principal or buys at a price lower than the price specified by the principal, the remuneration may be increased as agreed; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Law, the benefit belongs to the principal.

If the principal gives special instructions on the price, the commission agent shall not sell or buy in violation of such instructions.

**Article 956** When selling or buying commodities with a market price, the commission agent may itself act as the buyer or seller, unless the principal indicates otherwise.

In the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the commission agent may still request the principal to pay remuneration.

**Article 957** If the commission agent buys the entrusted item as agreed, the principal shall accept it promptly. If the principal refuses to accept it without justifiable reasons after being urged by the commission agent, the commission agent may deposit the entrusted item in accordance with the law.

If the entrusted item cannot be sold or the principal withdraws the sale, and after being urged by the commission agent, the principal fails to retrieve or dispose of the item, the commission agent may deposit the entrusted item in accordance with the law.

**Article 958** If the commission agent enters into a contract with a third party, the commission agent directly enjoys rights and assumes obligations under that contract.

If the third party's failure to perform its obligations causes damage to the principal, the commission agent shall bear liability for compensation, unless otherwise agreed between the commission agent and the principal.

**Article 959** If the commission agent completes or partially completes the entrusted affairs, the principal shall pay corresponding remuneration. If the principal fails to pay remuneration within the time limit, the commission agent has a lien over the entrusted item, unless otherwise agreed by the parties.

**Article 960** Matters not provided for in this Chapter shall be governed by the relevant provisions on mandate contracts by reference.

#### Chapter 26: Intermediation Contracts

**Article 961** An intermediation contract is a contract under which an intermediary reports to the principal opportunities for entering into a contract or provides intermediary services for the conclusion of a contract, and the principal pays remuneration.

**Article 962** The intermediary shall truthfully report to the principal matters related to the conclusion of the contract.

If the intermediary intentionally conceals important facts related to the conclusion of the contract or provides false information, thereby harming the principal's interests, it shall not request payment of remuneration and shall bear liability for compensation.

**Article 963** If the intermediary facilitates the conclusion of a contract, the principal shall pay remuneration as agreed. If there is no agreement on the intermediary's remuneration or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Law, it shall be reasonably determined based on the intermediary's services. If the intermediary facilitates the conclusion of a contract by providing intermediary services for the conclusion of the contract, the parties to that contract shall equally bear the intermediary's remuneration.

If the intermediary facilitates the conclusion of a contract, the expenses of the intermediation activities shall be borne by the intermediary.

**Article 964** If the intermediary fails to facilitate the conclusion of a contract, it shall not request payment of remuneration; however, it may request the principal to pay the necessary expenses incurred in the intermediation activities as agreed.

**Article 965** If the principal, after receiving the intermediary's services, uses the transaction opportunities or intermediary services provided by the intermediary to bypass the intermediary and directly enter into a contract, it shall pay remuneration to the intermediary.

**Article 966** Matters not provided for in this Chapter shall be governed by the relevant provisions on mandate contracts by reference.

#### Chapter 27: Partnership Contracts

**Article 967** A partnership contract is an agreement between two or more partners to share benefits and risks for a common business purpose.

**Article 968** Partners shall fulfill their capital contribution obligations in accordance with the agreed method, amount, and time limit for contribution.

**Article 969** The capital contributions of partners, income lawfully obtained from partnership affairs, and other property belong to the partnership property.

Before the termination of the partnership contract, partners shall not request the division of partnership property.

**Article 970** Decisions on partnership affairs by partners shall be made with the unanimous consent of all partners, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership contract.

Partnership affairs shall be executed jointly by all partners. One or more partners may be entrusted to execute partnership affairs in accordance with the partnership contract or the decision of all partners; other partners shall no longer execute partnership affairs but have the right to supervise the execution.

If partners execute partnership affairs separately, the executing partner may raise objections to the affairs executed by other partners; after raising objections, the other partners shall suspend the execution of such affairs.

**Article 971** Partners shall not request payment of remuneration for executing partnership affairs, unless otherwise agreed in the partnership contract.

**Article 972** The distribution of profits and sharing of losses of the partnership shall be handled in accordance with the partnership contract; if there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear in the partnership contract, it shall be decided by the partners through consultation; if consultation fails, it shall be distributed or shared by the partners in proportion to their actual capital contributions; if the proportion of capital contributions cannot be determined, it shall be distributed or shared equally by the partners.

**Article 973** Partners shall bear joint and several liability for partnership debts. A partner who has paid off partnership debts exceeding its own share has the right to seek recourse from the other partners.

**Article 974** Unless otherwise agreed in the partnership contract, a partner who transfers all or part of its property share to a person other than a partner must obtain the unanimous consent of the other partners.

**Article 975** Creditors of a partner shall not subrogate the rights enjoyed by the partner under this Chapter and the partnership contract, except for the partner's right to claim distribution of benefits.

**Article 976** If the partners have no agreement on the partnership term or the agreement is unclear, and it cannot be determined in accordance with Article 510 of this Law, the partnership shall be deemed to be a partnership at will.

If the partnership term expires and the partners continue to execute partnership affairs without objection from other partners, the original partnership contract remains valid, but the partnership term becomes at will.

A partner may terminate a partnership at will at any time, provided that it gives notice to the other partners within a reasonable period in advance.

**Article 977** If a partner dies, loses capacity for civil conduct, or is terminated, the partnership contract terminates; unless otherwise agreed in the partnership contract or it is inappropriate to terminate based on the nature of the partnership affairs.

**Article 978** After the termination of the partnership contract, if there is a surplus of partnership property after paying expenses incurred due to the termination and settling partnership debts, it shall be distributed in accordance with Article 972 of this Law.

### Part Three: Quasi-Contracts

#### Chapter 28: Negotiorum Gestio (Management of Affairs Without Mandate)

**Article 979** A manager who, without a legal or contractual obligation, manages the affairs of another person to avoid loss to that person's interests may request the beneficiary to reimburse the necessary expenses incurred in managing the affairs; if the manager suffers losses due to managing the affairs, it may request the beneficiary to provide appropriate compensation.

If the management of affairs does not conform to the true intention of the beneficiary, the manager does not enjoy the rights specified in the preceding paragraph; unless the true intention of the beneficiary violates the law or public order and good customs.

**Article 980** If the management of affairs by the manager does not fall under the circumstances specified in the preceding article, but the beneficiary enjoys the benefits of the management, the beneficiary shall, to the extent of the benefits obtained, assume the obligations specified in paragraph 1 of the preceding article towards the manager.

**Article 981** When managing the affairs of another person, the manager shall adopt methods beneficial to the beneficiary. If interrupting the management is detrimental to the beneficiary, the manager shall not interrupt it without justifiable reasons.

**Article 982** When managing the affairs of another person, if the manager can notify the beneficiary, it shall do so promptly. If the managed affairs do not require urgent handling, the manager shall await the beneficiary's instructions.

**Article 983** After the management is completed, the manager shall report to the beneficiary on the status of the managed affairs. Property obtained by the manager from managing the affairs shall be promptly transferred to the beneficiary.

**Article 984** If the management of affairs by the manager is subsequently ratified by the beneficiary, the relevant provisions on mandate contracts shall apply from the beginning of the management, unless the manager indicates otherwise.

#### Chapter 29: Unjust Enrichment

**Article 985** A person who obtains unjust enrichment without legal basis may be requested by the person who suffered the loss to return the benefits obtained, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Performance made to fulfill a moral obligation;
(2) Repayment of a debt before it falls due;
(3) Repayment of a debt knowing that there is no obligation to pay.

**Article 986** If the enriched person neither knew nor should have known that the benefits obtained had no legal basis, and the benefits no longer exist, it shall not be obligated to return such benefits.

**Article 987** If the enriched person knew or should have known that the benefits obtained had no legal basis, the person who suffered the loss may request the enriched person to return the benefits obtained and compensate for losses in accordance with the law.

**Article 988** If the enriched person has already transferred the benefits obtained to a third party gratuitously, the person who suffered the loss may request the third party to assume the obligation of return to the corresponding extent.

### Chapter 1: General Provisions

**Article 989** This Book regulates the civil relations arising from the enjoyment and protection of personality rights.

**Article 990** Personality rights are the rights enjoyed by civil subjects, including the right to life, right to bodily integrity, right to health, right to name, right to entity name, right to portrait, right to reputation, right to honor, right to privacy, and other rights.

In addition to the personality rights specified in the preceding paragraph, natural persons enjoy other personality rights and interests arising from personal freedom and personal dignity.

**Article 991** The personality rights of civil subjects are protected by law, and no organization or individual may infringe upon them.

**Article 992** Personality rights shall not be waived, transferred, or inherited.

**Article 993** Civil subjects may permit others to use their names, entity names, portraits, etc., unless otherwise provided by law or prohibited by their nature.

**Article 994** If the name, portrait, reputation, honor, privacy, or remains of a deceased person are infringed upon, their spouse, children, and parents have the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if the deceased has no spouse or children and the parents are deceased, other close relatives have the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 995** If personality rights are infringed upon, the victim has the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with this Law and other laws. The victim's right to request cessation of infringement, removal of obstacles, elimination of danger, elimination of adverse effects, restoration of reputation, and apology shall not be subject to the statute of limitations.

**Article 996** If a party's breach of contract damages the other party's personality rights and causes serious mental harm, the injured party's choice to request the breaching party to bear liability for breach of contract shall not affect the injured party's right to claim compensation for mental harm.

**Article 997** If a civil subject has evidence that an actor is committing or is about to commit an illegal act infringing upon its personality rights, and failure to stop it promptly will cause irreparable damage to its lawful rights and interests, it has the right to apply to the people's court for an order to take measures to stop the relevant act in accordance with the law.

**Article 998** When determining the civil liability of an actor for infringing upon personality rights other than the right to life, right to bodily integrity, and right to health, factors such as the occupation of the actor and the victim, the scope of impact, the degree of fault, and the purpose, method, and consequences of the act shall be considered.

**Article 999** For the public interest, acts such as news reporting and public opinion supervision may reasonably use the names, entity names, portraits, personal information, etc., of civil subjects; if the use is unreasonable and infringes upon the personality rights of civil subjects, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

**Article 1000** If an actor bears civil liability for eliminating adverse effects, restoring reputation, or apologizing due to infringement of personality rights, such liability shall be commensurate with the specific manner of the act and the scope of the impact caused.

If the actor refuses to bear the civil liability specified in the preceding paragraph, the people's court may enforce it by means such as publishing an announcement in newspapers, online media, etc., or publishing the effective judgment document, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the actor.

**Article 1001** The protection of the status rights of natural persons arising from marriage, family relationships, etc., shall be governed by the relevant provisions of Book One, Book Five, and other laws of this Code; if there are no provisions, the relevant provisions on the protection of personality rights in this Book may be applied by reference based on their nature.

### Chapter 2: Right to Life, Right to Bodily Integrity, and Right to Health

**Article 1002** Natural persons enjoy the right to life. The safety and dignity of life of natural persons are protected by law. No organization or individual may infringe upon the right to life of others.

**Article 1003** Natural persons enjoy the right to bodily integrity. The physical integrity and freedom of movement of natural persons are protected by law. No organization or individual may infringe upon the right to bodily integrity of others.

**Article 1004** Natural persons enjoy the right to health. The physical and mental health of natural persons is protected by law. No organization or individual may infringe upon the right to health of others.

**Article 1005** If the right to life, right to bodily integrity, or right to health of a natural person is infringed upon or is in other dangerous situations, organizations or individuals with a legal duty to rescue shall provide timely assistance.

**Article 1006** A person with full capacity for civil conduct has the right to voluntarily and gratuitously donate their human cells, human tissues, human organs, or remains in accordance with the law. No organization or individual may coerce, deceive, or induce them to donate.

If a person with full capacity for civil conduct agrees to donate in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall be done in writing, or a will may be made.

If a natural person did not express opposition to donation before death, after the natural person's death, their spouse, adult children, and parents may jointly decide on the donation, and the decision to donate shall be made in writing.

**Article 1007** The sale of human cells, human tissues, human organs, or remains in any form is prohibited.

Any sale in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph is void.

**Article 1008** If clinical trials are required for the development of new drugs, medical devices, or new methods of prevention and treatment, approval from the relevant competent authorities and review and approval by the ethics committee shall be obtained in accordance with the law. The subjects or their guardians shall be informed in detail of the purpose, use, and potential risks of the trial, and their written consent shall be obtained.

When conducting clinical trials, no trial fees shall be charged to the subjects.

**Article 1009** Medical and scientific research activities related to human genes, human embryos, etc., shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions, shall not harm human health, shall not violate ethics and morals, and shall not harm the public interest.

**Article 1010** If a person commits sexual harassment against another person against their will through words, text, images, physical acts, etc., the victim has the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Entities such as government agencies, enterprises, and schools shall take reasonable measures such as prevention, complaint handling, investigation, and disposal to prevent and stop sexual harassment using positions of power, subordinate relationships, etc.

**Article 1011** If a person deprives or restricts another person's freedom of movement through illegal detention or other means, or illegally searches another person's body, the victim has the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

### Chapter 3: Right to Name and Right to Entity Name

**Article 1012** Natural persons enjoy the right to name, and have the right to decide, use, change, or permit others to use their names in accordance with the law, but shall not violate public order and good customs.

**Article 1013** Legal persons and unincorporated organizations enjoy the right to entity name, and have the right to decide, use, change, transfer, or permit others to use their names in accordance with the law.

**Article 1014** No organization or individual may infringe upon another person's right to name or right to entity name by means such as interference, misappropriation, or impersonation.

**Article 1015** A natural person shall take the father's or mother's surname, but may choose a surname other than the father's or mother's surname in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Choosing the surname of another direct lineal elder relative;
(2) Being raised by a person other than the statutory supporter and choosing the supporter's surname;
(3) Having other legitimate reasons that do not violate public order and good customs.

The surname of a natural person from an ethnic minority may follow the cultural traditions and customs of that ethnic group.

**Article 1016** If a natural person decides to change their name, or a legal person or unincorporated organization decides to change or transfer its name, it shall go through registration procedures with the relevant authorities in accordance with the law, unless otherwise provided by law.

If a civil subject changes its name or entity name, the civil juristic acts performed before the change are legally binding on it.

**Article 1017** Pseudonyms, stage names, online aliases, translated names, trade names, abbreviations of names and entity names, etc., that have a certain degree of social recognition and whose use by others is likely to cause public confusion, shall be governed by the relevant provisions on the protection of the right to name and the right to entity name by reference.

### Chapter 4: Right to Portrait

**Article 1018** Natural persons enjoy the right to portrait, and have the right to make, use, disclose, or permit others to use their portraits in accordance with the law.

A portrait is an identifiable external image of a specific natural person reflected on a certain medium through images, sculptures, paintings, etc.Article 1019 No organization or individual may infringe upon another person's right to likeness by defacing, defiling, or using technical means such as forgery. Without the consent of the right holder of the likeness, no one may produce, use, or publicly disclose the portrait of the right holder, unless otherwise provided by law.

Without the consent of the right holder of the likeness, the owner of the portrait work may not use or publicly disclose the portrait of the right holder by means such as publication, reproduction, distribution, rental, or exhibition.

Article 1020 The following acts may be reasonably carried out without the consent of the right holder of the likeness:

(1) Using the portrait that has already been publicly disclosed by the right holder within the necessary scope for personal study, artistic appreciation, classroom teaching, or scientific research;

(2) Inevitably producing, using, or publicly disclosing the portrait of the right holder for the purpose of implementing news reporting;

(3) Producing, using, or publicly disclosing the portrait of the right holder within the necessary scope by state organs for the purpose of performing their duties according to law;

(4) Inevitably producing, using, or publicly disclosing the portrait of the right holder for the purpose of displaying a specific public environment;

(5) Other acts of producing, using, or publicly disclosing the portrait of the right holder for the purpose of safeguarding the public interest or the lawful rights and interests of the right holder.

Article 1021 Where there is a dispute between the parties over the understanding of the terms concerning the use of a portrait in a portrait licensing contract, the interpretation shall be made in favor of the right holder of the likeness.

Article 1022 Where the parties have not agreed upon or have not clearly agreed upon the term of the portrait licensing, either party may terminate the portrait licensing contract at any time, provided that the other party is notified within a reasonable period in advance.

Where the parties have clearly agreed upon the term of the portrait licensing and the right holder of the likeness has a legitimate reason, the right holder may terminate the portrait licensing contract, provided that the other party is notified within a reasonable period in advance. If the termination of the contract causes losses to the other party, compensation shall be paid unless the cause is not attributable to the right holder of the likeness.

Article 1023 The licensing of the use of names, etc., shall be governed by the relevant provisions on the licensing of the use of portraits.

The protection of the voice of a natural person shall be governed by the relevant provisions on the protection of the right to likeness.

### Chapter V Right to Reputation and Right to Honor

Article 1024 Civil subjects enjoy the right to reputation. No organization or individual may infringe upon another person's right to reputation by means such as insult or defamation.

Reputation is the social evaluation of a civil subject's moral character, prestige, talent, credit, etc.

Article 1025 Where an actor engages in news reporting, public opinion supervision, or other acts for the public interest, which affects another person's reputation, the actor shall not bear civil liability, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Fabricating or distorting facts;

(2) Failing to fulfill the duty of reasonable verification of seriously inaccurate content provided by others;

(3) Using insulting language or other means to disparage another person's reputation.

Article 1026 In determining whether an actor has fulfilled the duty of reasonable verification as specified in item (2) of the preceding article, the following factors shall be considered:

(1) The credibility of the source of the content;

(2) Whether necessary investigation has been conducted on content that is obviously likely to cause controversy;

(3) The timeliness of the content;

(4) The relevance of the content to public order and good customs;

(5) The likelihood of damage to the victim's reputation;

(6) The ability and cost of verification.

Article 1027 Where a literary or artistic work published by an actor describes real persons and real events or specific persons, and contains insulting or defamatory content that infringes upon another person's right to reputation, the victim has the right to request the actor to bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Where a literary or artistic work published by an actor does not describe a specific person, and only the plot is similar to the situation of that specific person, the actor shall not bear civil liability.

Article 1028 Where a civil subject has evidence proving that the content reported by newspapers, the internet, or other media is false and infringes upon his or her right to reputation, the civil subject has the right to request the media to take necessary measures such as correction or deletion in a timely manner.

Article 1029 A civil subject may, in accordance with the law, inquire about his or her own credit evaluation; if the credit evaluation is found to be improper, the civil subject has the right to raise an objection and request the adoption of necessary measures such as correction or deletion. The credit evaluator shall promptly verify the matter, and if the verification confirms the impropriety, shall promptly take necessary measures.

Article 1030 The relationship between a civil subject and a credit information processor such as a credit investigation agency shall be governed by the provisions of this Part on the protection of personal information and the relevant provisions of other laws and administrative regulations.

Article 1031 Civil subjects enjoy the right to honor. No organization or individual may unlawfully deprive another person of his or her honorary title, or defame or disparage another person's honor.

Where an honorary title obtained should have been recorded but has not been recorded, the civil subject may request the recording; where the recorded honorary title is erroneous, the civil subject may request the correction.

### Chapter VI Right to Privacy and Protection of Personal Information

Article 1032 Natural persons enjoy the right to privacy. No organization or individual may infringe upon another person's right to privacy by means such as prying, intrusion, disclosure, or publication.

Privacy is the private life of a natural person that is peaceful and the private space, private activities, and private information that the person does not want others to know.

Article 1033 Unless otherwise provided by law or with the explicit consent of the right holder, no organization or individual may commit the following acts:

(1) Intruding upon another person's peaceful private life by means such as telephone, text messages, instant messaging tools, emails, or leaflets;

(2) Entering, photographing, or peeping into another person's private space such as a residence or hotel room;

(3) Photographing, peeping, eavesdropping, or publicly disclosing another person's private activities;

(4) Photographing or peeping at the private parts of another person's body;

(5) Processing another person's private information;

(6) Infringing upon another person's right to privacy by other means.

Article 1034 The personal information of natural persons is protected by law.

Personal information refers to all kinds of information recorded electronically or by other means that can identify a specific natural person alone or in combination with other information, including the natural person's name, date of birth, identity document number, biometric information, address, telephone number, email address, health information, whereabouts information, etc.

Private information in personal information shall be governed by the provisions on the right to privacy; where there are no such provisions, the provisions on the protection of personal information shall apply.

Article 1035 The processing of personal information shall follow the principles of legality, legitimacy, and necessity, and shall not be excessive, and shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Obtaining the consent of the natural person or his or her guardian, unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations;

(2) Publicly disclosing the rules for processing information;

(3) Clearly stating the purpose, method, and scope of processing information;

(4) Not violating the provisions of laws or administrative regulations and the agreement between the parties.

The processing of personal information includes the collection, storage, use, processing, transmission, provision, and disclosure of personal information.

Article 1036 Where personal information is processed under any of the following circumstances, the actor shall not bear civil liability:

(1) Acts reasonably carried out within the scope of consent of the natural person or his or her guardian;

(2) Reasonably processing information that has been voluntarily disclosed by the natural person or other information that has been lawfully disclosed, unless the natural person expressly refuses or the processing of such information infringes upon his or her material interests;

(3) Other acts reasonably carried out for the purpose of safeguarding the public interest or the lawful rights and interests of the natural person.

Article 1037 A natural person may, in accordance with the law, access or copy his or her personal information from the information processor; if the information is found to be erroneous, the natural person has the right to raise an objection and request the adoption of necessary measures such as correction in a timely manner.

If a natural person discovers that the information processor processes his or her personal information in violation of the provisions of laws or administrative regulations or the agreement between the parties, the natural person has the right to request the information processor to delete it in a timely manner.

Article 1038 An information processor shall not disclose or tamper with the personal information it collects or stores; without the consent of the natural person, it shall not illegally provide the personal information to others, except where the information has been processed and cannot identify a specific individual and cannot be restored.

An information processor shall take technical measures and other necessary measures to ensure the security of the personal information it collects or stores and to prevent the information from being disclosed, tampered with, or lost; if a disclosure, tampering, or loss of personal information occurs or is likely to occur, it shall promptly take remedial measures, notify the natural person in accordance with the provisions, and report to the relevant competent authorities.

Article 1039 State organs, statutory institutions with administrative functions, and their staff members shall keep confidential the privacy and personal information of natural persons that they become aware of in the course of performing their duties, and shall not disclose or illegally provide such information to others.

## Book on Marriage and Family

### Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1040 This Book regulates the civil relations arising from marriage and family.

Article 1041 Marriage and family are protected by the State.

The marriage system of free choice of partners, monogamy, and equality between men and women is implemented.

The lawful rights and interests of women, minors, the elderly, and persons with disabilities are protected.

Article 1042 Arranged marriages, mercenary marriages, and other acts of interference in the freedom of marriage are prohibited. The exaction of money or property by means of marriage is prohibited.

Bigamy is prohibited. A person who has a spouse is prohibited from cohabiting with another person.

Domestic violence is prohibited. Abuse and abandonment of family members are prohibited.

Article 1043 Families shall establish good family traditions, promote family virtues, and attach importance to the construction of family civilization.

Husband and wife shall be faithful to each other, respect each other, and care for each other; family members shall respect the elderly and love the young, help each other, and maintain equal, harmonious, and civilized marriage and family relations.

Article 1044 Adoption shall follow the principle of best interests of the adoptee, and safeguard the lawful rights and interests of the adoptee and the adopter.

The sale of minors under the guise of adoption is prohibited.

Article 1045 Relatives include spouses, blood relatives, and relatives by marriage.

Spouses, parents, children, siblings, grandparents, maternal grandparents, grandchildren, and maternal grandchildren are close relatives.

Spouses, parents, children, and other close relatives living together are family members.

### Chapter II Marriage

Article 1046 Marriage shall be based on the complete willingness of both the man and the woman. Neither party may coerce the other, and no organization or individual may interfere.

Article 1047 The age of marriage shall not be earlier than 22 years for a man and 20 years for a woman.

Article 1048 Marriage between lineal relatives by blood or between collateral relatives by blood within three generations is prohibited.

Article 1049 A man and a woman who intend to marry shall apply for marriage registration in person at the marriage registration authority. If the conditions provided for in this Law are met, the marriage shall be registered and a marriage certificate shall be issued. The marriage relationship is established upon completion of the marriage registration. If the marriage registration has not been completed, the registration shall be completed retroactively.

Article 1050 After marriage registration, according to the agreement between the man and the woman, the woman may become a member of the man's family, and the man may become a member of the woman's family.

Article 1051 A marriage is void under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Bigamy;

(2) The existence of a prohibited relationship between the parties;

(3) Failure to reach the legal age for marriage.

Article 1052 Where a marriage is coerced, the coerced party may request the people's court to annul the marriage.

The request for annulment of the marriage shall be made within one year from the date on which the coercive act ceases.

Where a party whose personal freedom has been illegally restricted requests the annulment of the marriage, the request shall be made within one year from the date on which the personal freedom is restored.

Article 1053 Where one party suffers from a serious disease, the party shall truthfully inform the other party before the marriage registration; if the party fails to do so, the other party may request the people's court to annul the marriage.

The request for annulment of the marriage shall be made within one year from the date on which the other party knows or should have known the cause for annulment.

Article 1054 A void or annulled marriage has no legal effect from the outset, and the parties do not have the rights or obligations of husband and wife. The property acquired during the period of cohabitation shall be disposed of by agreement between the parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment based on the principle of favoring the innocent party. In the disposition of property in a void marriage caused by bigamy, the property rights and interests of the parties to the lawful marriage shall not be infringed upon. The children born to the parties shall be governed by the provisions of this Law on the relationship between parents and children.

Where a marriage is void or annulled, the innocent party has the right to claim damages.

### Chapter III Family Relations

#### Section 1 Husband-Wife Relations

Article 1055 Husband and wife have equal status in the marriage and family.

Article 1056 Both husband and wife have the right to use their own names.

Article 1057 Both husband and wife have the freedom to participate in production, work, study, and social activities, and neither party may restrict or interfere with the other.

Article 1058 Both husband and wife equally enjoy the right to raise, educate, and protect their minor children, and jointly bear the obligation to raise, educate, and protect their minor children.

Article 1059 Husband and wife have the obligation to support each other.

The party in need of support has the right to demand the other party to pay support fees when the other party fails to fulfill the support obligation.

Article 1060 A civil juristic act performed by one spouse for the daily needs of the family is binding on both spouses, unless otherwise agreed between the spouse and the other party.

The restriction on the scope of civil juristic acts that one spouse may perform between the spouses shall not be set up against a bona fide third party.

Article 1061 Husband and wife have the right to inherit each other's property.

Article 1062 The following property acquired by the husband and wife during the existence of the marriage relationship is the common property of the husband and wife, and shall be jointly owned by them:

(1) Wages, bonuses, and remuneration for services;

(2) Income from production, operation, and investment;

(3) Income from intellectual property rights;

(4) Property inherited or donated, except as provided for in item (3) of Article 1063 of this Law;

(5) Other property that should be jointly owned.

Husband and wife have equal rights to dispose of the common property.

Article 1063 The following property is the personal property of one spouse:

(1) Pre-marital property of one spouse;

(2) Compensation or damages received by one spouse for personal injury;

(3) Property determined by will or gift contract to belong only to one spouse;

(4) Daily necessities exclusively used by one spouse;

(5) Other property that should belong to one spouse.

Article 1064 Debts incurred by the joint signature of both spouses or by the subsequent ratification of one spouse, as well as debts incurred by one spouse in his or her own name for the daily needs of the family during the existence of the marriage relationship, are joint debts of the husband and wife.

Debts incurred by one spouse in his or her own name during the existence of the marriage relationship that exceed the daily needs of the family are not joint debts of the husband and wife, unless the creditor can prove that the debt was used for the common life or common production and operation of the husband and wife, or was based on the joint intention of both spouses.

Article 1065 A man and a woman may agree that the property acquired during the existence of the marriage relationship and the pre-marital property shall be owned separately, jointly, or partly separately and partly jointly. The agreement shall be in writing. If there is no agreement or the agreement is unclear, the provisions of Articles 1062 and 1063 of this Law shall apply.

The agreement between the husband and wife on the property acquired during the existence of the marriage relationship and the pre-marital property is legally binding on both parties.

Where the husband and wife agree that the property acquired during the existence of the marriage relationship shall be owned separately, and one spouse incurs a debt in his or her own name, if the other party knows of the agreement, the debt shall be satisfied with the personal property of that spouse.

Article 1066 During the existence of the marriage relationship, under any of the following circumstances, one spouse may request the people's court to partition the common property:

(1) One spouse conceals, transfers, sells, damages, squanders the common property of the husband and wife, or forges joint debts of the husband and wife, or commits other acts that seriously damage the interests of the common property of the husband and wife;

(2) A person for whom one spouse has a legal obligation to support suffers from a serious disease and needs medical treatment, and the other spouse refuses to pay the relevant medical expenses.

#### Section 2 Parent-Child Relations and Other Close Relative Relations

Article 1067 Where parents fail to fulfill their obligation to raise their children, minor children or adult children who cannot live independently have the right to demand their parents to pay child support.

Where adult children fail to fulfill their obligation to support their parents, parents who lack the ability to work or have financial difficulties have the right to demand their adult children to pay support fees.

Article 1068 Parents have the right and obligation to educate and protect their minor children. Where a minor child causes damage to another person, the parents shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 1069 Children shall respect their parents' right to marry, and shall not interfere with their parents' divorce, remarriage, or post-marriage life. The obligation of children to support their parents shall not terminate due to changes in the parents' marital relationship.

Article 1070 Parents and children have the right to inherit each other's property.

Article 1071 Children born out of wedlock enjoy the same rights as children born in wedlock, and no organization or individual may harm or discriminate against them.

The biological father or mother who does not directly raise the child born out of wedlock shall bear the child support for the minor child or the adult child who cannot live independently.

Article 1072 Stepparents and stepchildren shall not abuse or discriminate against each other.

The rights and obligations between a stepfather or stepmother and the stepchildren raised and educated by him or her shall be governed by the provisions of this Law on the relationship between parents and children.

Article 1073 Where there is a dispute over the parent-child relationship and there is a legitimate reason, the father or mother may file a lawsuit with the people's court to confirm or deny the parent-child relationship.

Where there is a dispute over the parent-child relationship and there is a legitimate reason, an adult child may file a lawsuit with the people's court to confirm the parent-child relationship.

Article 1074 Grandparents and maternal grandparents who have the ability to bear the burden have the obligation to raise their minor grandchildren and maternal grandchildren whose parents have died or are unable to raise them.

Grandchildren and maternal grandchildren who have the ability to bear the burden have the obligation to support their grandparents and maternal grandparents whose children have died or are unable to support them.

Article 1075 Elder brothers and sisters who have the ability to bear the burden have the obligation to support their minor younger brothers and sisters whose parents have died or are unable to raise them.

Younger brothers and sisters who have been raised by their elder brothers or sisters and have the ability to bear the burden have the obligation to support their elder brothers or sisters who lack the ability to work and have no source of income.

### Chapter IV Divorce

Article 1076 Where both husband and wife voluntarily divorce, they shall sign a written divorce agreement and apply for divorce registration in person at the marriage registration authority.

The divorce agreement shall clearly state the intention of both parties to voluntarily divorce and the consensus reached on matters such as child support, property, and debt settlement.Article 1077 Within thirty days from the date the marriage registration authority receives the divorce application, if either party is unwilling to divorce, that party may withdraw the divorce application from the marriage registration authority.

Within thirty days after the expiration of the period specified in the preceding paragraph, both parties shall personally appear before the marriage registration authority to apply for the issuance of a divorce certificate; if no application is made, the divorce application shall be deemed withdrawn.

Article 1078 Where the marriage registration authority ascertains that both parties are indeed voluntarily divorced and have reached an agreement on matters such as child support, property division, and debt settlement, it shall register the divorce and issue a divorce certificate.

Article 1079 Where one spouse requests a divorce, the relevant organization may conduct mediation, or the spouse may directly file a divorce lawsuit with the people's court.

When hearing a divorce case, the people's court shall conduct mediation; if the relationship has indeed broken down and mediation is ineffective, a divorce shall be granted.

Divorce shall be granted if mediation is ineffective in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Bigamy or cohabitation with another person;

(2) Committing domestic violence or abusing, abandoning family members;

(3) Having persistent bad habits such as gambling or drug addiction that remain uncorrected after repeated admonition;

(4) Separation for two full years due to lack of mutual affection;

(5) Other circumstances leading to the breakdown of the marital relationship.

If one party is declared missing and the other party files for divorce, a divorce shall be granted.

After a people's court has ruled against a divorce, if the parties have been separated for another full year and one party files for divorce again, a divorce shall be granted.

Article 1080 The marital relationship is dissolved upon completion of divorce registration, or upon the effective date of the divorce judgment or mediation document.

Article 1081 If the spouse of a person on active military service requests a divorce, the consent of the military person shall be obtained, unless the military person is at serious fault.

Article 1082 The husband may not file for divorce during the wife's pregnancy, within one year after childbirth, or within six months after termination of pregnancy; however, this does not apply if the wife files for divorce or if the people's court deems it necessary to accept the husband's divorce request.

Article 1083 After divorce, if both parties voluntarily wish to restore the marital relationship, they shall re-register for marriage at the marriage registration authority.

Article 1084 The relationship between parents and children is not eliminated by the parents' divorce. After divorce, regardless of whether the children are directly raised by the father or mother, they remain the children of both parents.

After divorce, parents still have the rights and obligations to raise, educate, and protect their children.

After divorce, children under the age of two shall be directly raised by the mother as a principle. For children aged two or older, if the parents fail to reach an agreement on custody, the people's court shall make a judgment based on the specific circumstances of both parties, in accordance with the principle of best interests of the minor child. For children aged eight or older, their true wishes shall be respected.

Article 1085 After divorce, if one party directly raises the children, the other party shall bear part or all of the child support. The amount and duration of support shall be agreed upon by both parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment.

The agreement or judgment specified in the preceding paragraph shall not prevent the children from making reasonable requests to either parent for amounts exceeding the originally agreed or adjudicated amount when necessary.

Article 1086 After divorce, the parent who does not directly raise the children has the right to visit the children, and the other party has the obligation to assist.

The manner and timing of exercising visitation rights shall be agreed upon by the parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment.

If visitation by a parent is detrimental to the physical or mental health of the child, the people's court shall suspend visitation in accordance with the law; after the reasons for suspension disappear, visitation shall be resumed.

Article 1087 Upon divorce, the joint property of the spouses shall be disposed of by agreement between the parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment based on the specific circumstances of the property, in accordance with the principle of protecting the interests of the children, the wife, and the innocent party.

The rights and interests enjoyed by the husband or wife in family land contractual management shall be protected in accordance with the law.

Article 1088 If one spouse bears more obligations due to raising children, caring for the elderly, or assisting the other spouse's work, that spouse has the right to request compensation from the other spouse upon divorce, and the other spouse shall provide such compensation. The specific method shall be agreed upon by both parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment.

Article 1089 Upon divorce, joint debts of the spouses shall be repaid jointly. If the joint property is insufficient to repay the debts or if the property is owned separately, the debts shall be repaid by agreement between the parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment.

Article 1090 Upon divorce, if one party is in financial difficulty, the other party who has the ability to bear the burden shall provide appropriate assistance. The specific method shall be agreed upon by both parties; if no agreement is reached, the people's court shall make a judgment.

Article 1091 In any of the following circumstances leading to divorce, the innocent party has the right to claim damages:

(1) Bigamy;

(2) Cohabitation with another person;

(3) Committing domestic violence;

(4) Abusing or abandoning family members;

(5) Having other serious faults.

Article 1092 If one spouse conceals, transfers, sells, damages, or squanders joint property, or forges joint debts in an attempt to encroach upon the other spouse's property, that spouse may receive a smaller share or no share when dividing joint property upon divorce. After divorce, if the other spouse discovers the above actions, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court to request a re-division of the joint property.

### Chapter 5 Adoption

#### Section 1 Establishment of Adoption Relationship

Article 1093 The following minors may be adopted:

(1) Orphans who have lost their parents;

(2) Minors whose biological parents cannot be found;

(3) Children whose biological parents have special difficulties and are unable to raise them.

Article 1094 The following individuals or organizations may act as the person placing the child for adoption:

(1) Guardians of orphans;

(2) Child welfare institutions;

(3) Biological parents with special difficulties who are unable to raise their children.

Article 1095 If both parents of a minor lack full capacity for civil conduct and may seriously harm the minor, the guardian of the minor may place the minor for adoption.

Article 1096 If a guardian places an orphan for adoption, the consent of the person obligated to support the orphan shall be obtained. If the person obligated to support does not consent to the adoption and the guardian is unwilling to continue performing guardianship duties, a new guardian shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of Part One of this Code.

Article 1097 When biological parents place their child for adoption, both parents shall jointly do so. If one biological parent is unknown or cannot be found, the other parent may place the child for adoption unilaterally.

Article 1098 An adoptive parent shall meet all of the following conditions:

(1) Having no children or only one child;

(2) Having the ability to raise, educate, and protect the adoptee;

(3) Not suffering from a disease that is medically considered unsuitable for adopting a child;

(4) Having no criminal record of illegal or criminal acts detrimental to the healthy growth of the adoptee;

(5) Being at least thirty years old.

Article 1099 When adopting a child of a collateral relative by blood within three generations, the restrictions in Article 1093, item (3), Article 1094, item (3), and Article 1102 of this Code may be waived.

When an overseas Chinese adopts a child of a collateral relative by blood within three generations, the restriction in Article 1098, item (1) of this Code may also be waived.

Article 1100 An adoptive parent with no children may adopt two children; an adoptive parent with children may adopt only one child.

When adopting an orphan, a disabled minor, or a minor raised by a child welfare institution whose biological parents cannot be found, the restrictions in the preceding paragraph and Article 1098, item (1) of this Code may be waived.

Article 1101 If a married person adopts a child, both spouses shall jointly adopt.

Article 1102 If an unmarried person adopts a child of the opposite sex, the age difference between the adoptive parent and the adoptee shall be at least forty years.

Article 1103 A stepfather or stepmother may adopt a stepchild with the consent of the stepchild's biological parents, and may waive the restrictions in Article 1093, item (3), Article 1094, item (3), Article 1098, and Article 1100, paragraph 1 of this Code.

Article 1104 Both the adoptive parent and the person placing the child for adoption shall act voluntarily. When adopting a minor aged eight or older, the consent of the adoptee shall be obtained.

Article 1105 Adoption shall be registered with the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level. The adoption relationship is established from the date of registration.

When adopting a minor whose biological parents cannot be found, the civil affairs department handling the registration shall make a public announcement before registration.

If the parties to the adoption relationship wish to enter into an adoption agreement, they may do so.

If any or all parties to the adoption relationship request notarization of the adoption, it shall be notarized.

The civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level shall conduct an adoption assessment in accordance with the law.

Article 1106 After the adoption relationship is established, the public security authority shall register the household registration for the adoptee in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 1107 Orphans or children whose biological parents are unable to raise them may be raised by relatives or friends of the biological parents; the relationship between the caregiver and the child is not governed by the provisions of this Chapter.

Article 1108 If one spouse dies and the surviving spouse places a minor child for adoption, the parents of the deceased spouse have a priority right to raise the child.

Article 1109 Foreigners may adopt children in the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

When a foreigner adopts a child in the People's Republic of China, the adoption shall be reviewed and approved by the competent authority of the foreigner's home country in accordance with its laws. The adoptive parent shall provide documents issued by the competent authority of the home country regarding age, marital status, occupation, property, health, and criminal record, and shall sign a written agreement with the person placing the child for adoption, and personally register with the civil affairs department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

The documents specified in the preceding paragraph shall be authenticated by the foreign affairs authority of the adoptive parent's home country or an agency authorized by it, and by the embassy or consulate of the People's Republic of China in that country, unless otherwise provided by the state.

Article 1110 If the adoptive parent or the person placing the child for adoption requests confidentiality of the adoption, others shall respect their wishes and shall not disclose it.

#### Section 2 Effect of Adoption

Article 1111 From the date the adoption relationship is established, the rights and obligations between the adoptive parents and the adopted child shall be governed by the provisions of this Code on the parent-child relationship; the rights and obligations between the adopted child and the close relatives of the adoptive parents shall be governed by the provisions of this Code on the relationship between children and the close relatives of their parents.

The rights and obligations between the adopted child and the biological parents and other close relatives are extinguished upon the establishment of the adoption relationship.

Article 1112 The adopted child may take the surname of the adoptive father or adoptive mother, or, with the agreement of the parties, may retain the original surname.

Article 1113 An adoption that falls under the circumstances of invalidity of civil legal acts as provided in Part One of this Code or that violates the provisions of this Part is invalid.

An invalid adoption has no legal effect from the outset.

#### Section 3 Dissolution of Adoption Relationship

Article 1114 The adoptive parent may not dissolve the adoption relationship before the adoptee reaches adulthood, unless the adoptive parent and the person placing the child for adoption agree to dissolve it. If the adopted child is aged eight or older, the child's consent shall be obtained.

If the adoptive parent fails to fulfill the obligation to raise the child and engages in acts such as abuse or abandonment that infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of the minor adopted child, the person placing the child for adoption has the right to request dissolution of the adoption relationship between the adoptive parents and the adopted child. If the person placing the child for adoption and the adoptive parent cannot reach an agreement on dissolution, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 1115 If the relationship between the adoptive parents and the adult adopted child deteriorates to the extent that they cannot live together, they may dissolve the adoption relationship by agreement. If no agreement can be reached, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 1116 If the parties agree to dissolve the adoption relationship, they shall register the dissolution with the civil affairs department.

Article 1117 After the adoption relationship is dissolved, the rights and obligations between the adopted child and the adoptive parents and other close relatives are extinguished, and the rights and obligations between the adopted child and the biological parents and other close relatives are automatically restored. However, whether the rights and obligations between the adult adopted child and the biological parents and other close relatives are restored may be determined by negotiation.

Article 1118 After the adoption relationship is dissolved, an adult adopted child who was raised by the adoptive parents shall provide living expenses to the adoptive parents who lack the ability to work and have no source of income. If the adoption relationship is dissolved due to the adult adopted child's abuse or abandonment of the adoptive parents, the adoptive parents may demand compensation from the adopted child for the support expenses incurred during the adoption period.

If the biological parents request dissolution of the adoption relationship, the adoptive parents may demand appropriate compensation from the biological parents for the support expenses incurred during the adoption period; however, this does not apply if the adoption relationship is dissolved due to the adoptive parents' abuse or abandonment of the adopted child.

## Part of Inheritance

Effective from January 1, 2021

### Chapter 1 General Provisions

Article 1119 This Part governs civil relationships arising from inheritance.

Article 1120 The state protects the right of inheritance of natural persons.

Article 1121 Inheritance begins upon the death of the decedent.

If several persons with mutual inheritance relationships die in the same event and it is difficult to determine the time of death, the person without other heirs is presumed to have died first. If all have other heirs and are of different generations, the elder is presumed to have died first; if of the same generation, they are presumed to have died simultaneously, and no inheritance occurs between them.

Article 1122 Estate refers to the lawful personal property left by a natural person upon death.

Estate that cannot be inherited according to law or by its nature shall not be inherited.

Article 1123 After inheritance begins, it shall be handled in accordance with statutory inheritance; if there is a will, it shall be handled in accordance with testamentary inheritance or legacy; if there is a legacy-support agreement, it shall be handled in accordance with the agreement.

Article 1124 After inheritance begins, if an heir renounces the inheritance, the heir shall make a written statement of renunciation before the estate is disposed of; if no statement is made, the heir is deemed to have accepted the inheritance.

A legatee shall make a statement of acceptance or renunciation of the legacy within sixty days after learning of the legacy; if no statement is made within the time limit, the legatee is deemed to have renounced the legacy.

Article 1125 An heir who commits any of the following acts loses the right of inheritance:

(1) Intentionally killing the decedent;

(2) Killing another heir in order to compete for the estate;

(3) Abandoning the decedent or seriously abusing the decedent;

(4) Forging, altering, concealing, or destroying the will, if the circumstances are serious;

(5) Using fraud or coercion to force or hinder the decedent from making, altering, or revoking a will, if the circumstances are serious.

If an heir commits the acts specified in items (3) to (5) of the preceding paragraph but shows genuine repentance, and the decedent expresses forgiveness or later lists the heir as an heir in the will, the heir shall not lose the right of inheritance.

If a legatee commits any of the acts specified in paragraph 1 of this Article, the legatee loses the right to the legacy.

### Chapter 2 Statutory Inheritance

Article 1126 The right of inheritance is equal for men and women.

Article 1127 Estate shall be inherited in the following order:

(1) First order: spouse, children, parents;

(2) Second order: siblings, grandparents, maternal grandparents.

After inheritance begins, the first-order heirs inherit, and the second-order heirs do not inherit; if there are no first-order heirs, the second-order heirs inherit.

For the purposes of this Part, "children" includes legitimate children, illegitimate children, adopted children, and stepchildren with a support relationship.

For the purposes of this Part, "parents" includes biological parents, adoptive parents, and stepparents with a support relationship.

For the purposes of this Part, "siblings" includes full siblings, half-siblings, adopted siblings, and stepsiblings with a support relationship.

Article 1128 If a child of the decedent dies before the decedent, the direct lineal descendants of the deceased child shall inherit by subrogation.

If a sibling of the decedent dies before the decedent, the children of the deceased sibling shall inherit by subrogation.

A subrogated heir generally may only inherit the share of the estate that the subrogated person would have been entitled to inherit.

Article 1129 A widowed daughter-in-law who has made significant contributions to supporting her parents-in-law, or a widowed son-in-law who has made significant contributions to supporting his parents-in-law, shall be treated as a first-order heir.

Article 1130 Shares of the estate inherited by heirs of the same order shall generally be equal.

When distributing the estate, special consideration shall be given to heirs who have special difficulties in life and lack the ability to work.

When distributing the estate, heirs who have made significant contributions to supporting the decedent or who lived with the decedent may receive a larger share.

Heirs who have the ability and conditions to support the decedent but fail to do so shall receive no share or a smaller share when the estate is distributed.

Heirs may also agree to unequal shares.

Article 1131 A person who is not an heir but was supported by the decedent, or a person who is not an heir but provided significant support to the decedent, may be given an appropriate share of the estate.

Article 1132 Heirs shall handle inheritance matters through negotiation in a spirit of mutual understanding, mutual accommodation, harmony, and unity. The time, method, and shares of estate division shall be determined by agreement among the heirs; if no agreement is reached, the matter may be mediated by a people's mediation committee or brought to the people's court for a lawsuit.

### Chapter 3 Testamentary Inheritance and Legacy

Article 1133 A natural person may, in accordance with the provisions of this Code, make a will to dispose of personal property and may appoint an executor of the will.

A natural person may make a will to designate personal property to be inherited by one or more statutory heirs.

A natural person may make a will to donate personal property to the state, a collective, or an organization or individual other than statutory heirs.

A natural person may establish a testamentary trust in accordance with the law.

Article 1134 A holographic will shall be written entirely by the testator in their own handwriting, signed, and dated with the year, month, and day.

Article 1135 A will made by another person on behalf of the testator shall be witnessed by two or more witnesses, written by one of them, and signed by the testator, the writer, and the other witnesses, and dated with the year, month, and day.

Article 1136 A printed will shall be witnessed by two or more witnesses. The testator and the witnesses shall sign each page of the will and date it with the year, month, and day.

Article 1137 A will made in the form of an audio or video recording shall be witnessed by two or more witnesses. The testator and the witnesses shall record their names or images, as well as the year, month, and day, in the recording.

Article 1138 In an emergency, a testator may make an oral will. An oral will shall be witnessed by two or more witnesses. After the emergency situation is resolved, if the testator is able to make a will in writing or in the form of an audio or video recording, the oral will made earlier shall be invalid.Article 1139 A notarial will shall be made by the testator through a notary agency.

Article 1140 The following persons shall not serve as witnesses to a will:

(1) Persons without capacity for civil conduct, persons with limited capacity for civil conduct, and other persons who do not have the capacity to witness;

(2) Heirs and legatees;

(3) Persons who have an interest in the heirs or legatees.

Article 1141 A will shall reserve the necessary share of the estate for an heir who lacks the ability to work and has no source of income.

Article 1142 A testator may revoke or alter a will made by him or her.

After making a will, if the testator performs a civil juristic act that is contrary to the contents of the will, it shall be deemed as a revocation of the relevant contents of the will.

Where several wills have been made and their contents conflict, the last will shall prevail.

Article 1143 A will made by a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct is void.

A will must express the true intention of the testator; a will made under fraud or duress is void.

A forged will is void.

If a will has been altered, the altered contents are void.

Article 1144 Where a testamentary succession or legacy is subject to an obligation, the heir or legatee shall perform the obligation. Without justifiable grounds for failing to perform the obligation, upon the request of an interested person or relevant organization, the people's court may cancel the right of the heir or legatee to receive the part of the estate subject to the obligation.

### Chapter IV: Administration of the Estate

Article 1145 After the commencement of succession, the executor of the will shall be the administrator of the estate; if there is no executor of the will, the heirs shall promptly elect an administrator of the estate; if the heirs fail to elect one, the heirs shall jointly act as the administrator of the estate; if there are no heirs or all heirs have renounced the succession, the civil affairs department or the villagers' committee of the place of the decedent's domicile before death shall act as the administrator of the estate.

Article 1146 If there is a dispute over the determination of the administrator of the estate, an interested person may apply to the people's court for the appointment of an administrator of the estate.

Article 1147 The administrator of the estate shall perform the following duties:

(1) Inventory the estate and prepare a list of the estate;

(2) Report the status of the estate to the heirs;

(3) Take necessary measures to prevent damage to or loss of the estate;

(4) Handle the decedent's claims and debts;

(5) Distribute the estate in accordance with the will or the provisions of the law;

(6) Perform other necessary acts related to the administration of the estate.

Article 1148 The administrator of the estate shall perform duties in accordance with the law. If losses are caused to heirs, legatees, or creditors due to intentional acts or gross negligence, the administrator shall bear civil liability.

Article 1149 The administrator of the estate may receive remuneration in accordance with the provisions of the law or as agreed upon.

Article 1150 After the commencement of succession, the heir who knows of the decedent's death shall promptly notify the other heirs and the executor of the will. If none of the heirs knows of the decedent's death or if the heir who knows is unable to give notice, the unit where the decedent worked before death or the residents' committee or villagers' committee of the decedent's domicile shall be responsible for giving notice.

Article 1151 Any person who holds property of the estate shall properly preserve the estate, and no organization or individual may misappropriate or scramble for it.

Article 1152 After the commencement of succession, if an heir dies before the distribution of the estate and has not renounced the succession, the share of the estate that the heir should inherit shall pass to the heir's own heirs, unless otherwise provided for in the will.

Article 1153 Where property is jointly owned by spouses, unless otherwise agreed, when the estate is divided, half of the jointly owned property shall first be allocated to the surviving spouse, and the remainder shall be the decedent's estate.

Where the estate is part of the property jointly owned by a family, when the estate is divided, the property of others shall first be separated.

Article 1154 Under any of the following circumstances, the relevant part of the estate shall be dealt with in accordance with statutory succession:

(1) The testamentary heir renounces the succession or the legatee renounces the legacy;

(2) The testamentary heir is disinherited or the legatee loses the right to receive the legacy;

(3) The testamentary heir or legatee predeceases the testator or terminates;

(4) The estate involved in the invalid part of the will;

(5) The estate not disposed of by the will.

Article 1155 When the estate is divided, a share shall be reserved for a fetus. If the fetus is stillborn at birth, the reserved share shall be dealt with in accordance with statutory succession.

Article 1156 The division of the estate shall be conducive to the needs of production and livelihood, and shall not impair the utility of the estate.

For estate that is not suitable for division, methods such as valuation, appropriate compensation, or co-ownership may be adopted.

Article 1157 After the death of one spouse, if the surviving spouse remarries, he or she has the right to dispose of the inherited property, and no organization or individual may interfere.

Article 1158 A natural person may enter into a legacy-support agreement with an organization or individual other than an heir. According to the agreement, the organization or individual assumes the obligation to support the natural person in life, bury him or her after death, and enjoys the right to receive the legacy.

Article 1159 When dividing the estate, the taxes and debts that the decedent should pay according to law shall be settled; however, the necessary share of the estate shall be reserved for an heir who lacks the ability to work and has no source of income.

Article 1160 An estate that is neither inherited nor bequeathed shall belong to the state and be used for public welfare undertakings; if the decedent was a member of a collective ownership organization before death, it shall belong to that collective ownership organization.

Article 1161 An heir shall be liable for the payment of taxes and debts that the decedent should pay according to law, limited to the actual value of the estate inherited. This does not apply to the portion exceeding the actual value of the estate that the heir voluntarily repays.

If an heir renounces the succession, he or she may be exempted from liability for the payment of taxes and debts that the decedent should pay according to law.

Article 1162 The execution of a legacy shall not hinder the settlement of taxes and debts that the testator should pay according to law.

Article 1163 Where there are both statutory succession and testamentary succession or legacy, the statutory heirs shall settle the taxes and debts that the decedent should pay according to law; for the portion exceeding the actual value of the estate inherited by statutory succession, the testamentary heirs and legatees shall settle them in proportion to the estate they have received.

### Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1164 This Book regulates the civil relations arising from the infringement of civil rights and interests.

Article 1165 A person who, through fault, infringes upon the civil rights and interests of another person and causes damage shall bear tort liability.

If it is presumed that a person is at fault according to the provisions of the law, and the person cannot prove that he or she is not at fault, he or she shall bear tort liability.

Article 1166 If a person causes damage to the civil rights and interests of another person, regardless of whether the person is at fault, and the law provides that the person shall bear tort liability, such provisions shall apply.

Article 1167 Where a tort endangers the personal or property safety of another person, the infringed person has the right to request the tortfeasor to bear tort liability such as cessation of the infringement, removal of the obstruction, and elimination of the danger.

Article 1168 Where two or more persons jointly commit a tort causing damage to another person, they shall bear joint and several liability.

Article 1169 A person who instigates or assists another person in committing a tort shall bear joint and several liability with the tortfeasor.

A person who instigates or assists a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct in committing a tort shall bear tort liability; if the guardian of such person without capacity for civil conduct or person with limited capacity for civil conduct fails to fulfill the guardian's duties, the guardian shall bear corresponding liability.

Article 1170 Where two or more persons engage in conduct that endangers the personal or property safety of another person, and the conduct of one or several of them causes damage to another person, if the specific tortfeasor can be identified, the tortfeasor shall bear liability; if the specific tortfeasor cannot be identified, all persons engaging in the conduct shall bear joint and several liability.

Article 1171 Where two or more persons separately commit torts causing the same damage, and each person's tort is sufficient to cause all the damage, the tortfeasors shall bear joint and several liability.

Article 1172 Where two or more persons separately commit torts causing the same damage, if the degree of liability can be determined, they shall each bear corresponding liability; if it is difficult to determine the degree of liability, they shall bear liability equally.

Article 1173 If the infringed person is also at fault for the occurrence or aggravation of the same damage, the liability of the tortfeasor may be reduced.

Article 1174 If the damage is intentionally caused by the victim, the person causing the damage shall not bear liability.

Article 1175 If the damage is caused by a third party, the third party shall bear tort liability.

Article 1176 A person who voluntarily participates in a cultural or sports activity with certain risks shall not request other participants to bear tort liability if he or she suffers damage due to the conduct of another participant, unless the other participant has intentionally or grossly negligently caused the damage.

The liability of the organizer of the activity shall be governed by the provisions of Articles 1198 to 1201 of this Law.

Article 1177 Where a legitimate right or interest is being infringed upon, the situation is urgent and protection from a state authority cannot be obtained in a timely manner, and failure to take immediate measures will cause irreparable damage to the legitimate right or interest, the victim may take reasonable measures such as detaining the property of the tortfeasor within the necessary scope to protect his or her own legitimate right or interest; however, the victim shall immediately request the relevant state authority to handle the matter.

If the victim takes improper measures causing damage to another person, the victim shall bear tort liability.

Article 1178 Where this Law or other laws provide otherwise for circumstances where liability is not borne or is reduced, such provisions shall apply.

### Chapter II: Damages

Article 1179 Where a person causes personal injury to another person, he or she shall compensate for reasonable expenses incurred for treatment and rehabilitation, such as medical expenses, nursing expenses, transportation expenses, nutrition expenses, hospital meal subsidies, as well as the income lost due to missed work. If disability is caused, compensation for assistive devices and disability compensation shall also be paid; if death is caused, compensation for funeral expenses and death compensation shall also be paid.

Article 1180 Where the same tort causes the death of multiple persons, the death compensation may be determined at the same amount.

Article 1181 If the infringed person dies, his or her close relatives have the right to request the tortfeasor to bear tort liability. If the infringed person is an organization and the organization is divided or merged, the organization that succeeds to the rights has the right to request the tortfeasor to bear tort liability.

If the infringed person dies, the person who has paid reasonable expenses such as medical expenses and funeral expenses for the infringed person has the right to request the tortfeasor to compensate for such expenses, unless the tortfeasor has already paid such expenses.

Article 1182 Where a person infringes upon the personal rights and interests of another person, causing property losses, compensation shall be made based on the losses suffered by the infringed person or the benefits gained by the tortfeasor; if the losses suffered by the infringed person and the benefits gained by the tortfeasor are difficult to determine, and the infringed person and the tortfeasor fail to reach an agreement on the amount of compensation, and a lawsuit is filed with the people's court, the people's court shall determine the amount of compensation based on the actual circumstances.

Article 1183 Where a person infringes upon the personal rights and interests of a natural person, causing serious mental distress, the infringed person has the right to request compensation for mental damages.

Where a person intentionally or through gross negligence infringes upon a specific object of personal significance to a natural person, causing serious mental distress, the infringed person has the right to request compensation for mental damages.

Article 1184 Where a person infringes upon the property of another person, the property loss shall be calculated based on the market price at the time of the loss or other reasonable methods.

Article 1185 Where a person intentionally infringes upon the intellectual property rights of another person, and the circumstances are serious, the infringed person has the right to request corresponding punitive damages.

Article 1186 If neither the victim nor the person causing the damage is at fault for the occurrence of the damage, the loss shall be shared by both parties in accordance with the provisions of the law.

Article 1187 After the damage occurs, the parties may negotiate the method of payment of compensation. If the negotiation fails, the compensation shall be paid in a lump sum; if a lump sum payment is genuinely difficult, it may be paid in installments, but the infringed person has the right to request the provision of corresponding security.

### Chapter III: Special Provisions on Subjects of Liability

Article 1188 Where a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct causes damage to another person, the guardian shall bear tort liability. If the guardian has fulfilled the guardian's duties, the guardian's tort liability may be reduced.

If a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct who has property causes damage to another person, the compensation shall be paid from his or her own property; any shortfall shall be compensated by the guardian.

Article 1189 Where a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct causes damage to another person, and the guardian entrusts the guardian's duties to another person, the guardian shall bear tort liability; if the trustee is at fault, the trustee shall bear corresponding liability.
Article 1190 If a person with full capacity for civil conduct is at fault for temporarily losing consciousness or control of his or her conduct, causing damage to another person, he or she shall bear tort liability; if he or she is not at fault, he or she shall make appropriate compensation to the victim based on his or her economic circumstances.

If a person with full capacity for civil conduct causes damage to another person due to temporarily losing consciousness or control of his or her conduct as a result of drunkenness, abuse of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances, he or she shall bear tort liability.

Article 1191 Where an employee of an employer causes damage to another person in the performance of work tasks, the employer shall bear tort liability. After the employer bears tort liability, it may seek recourse from the employee who acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

During the period of labor dispatch, if the dispatched employee causes damage to another person in the performance of work tasks, the accepting entity of the labor dispatch shall bear tort liability; if the labor dispatch unit is at fault, it shall bear corresponding liability.

Article 1192 Where a labor relationship is formed between individuals, if the party providing labor causes damage to another person in the course of providing labor, the party receiving labor shall bear tort liability. After the party receiving labor bears tort liability, it may seek recourse from the party providing labor who acted intentionally or with gross negligence. If the party providing labor suffers damage in the course of providing labor, the parties shall bear corresponding liability based on their respective faults.

During the period of providing labor, if the party providing labor suffers damage due to the conduct of a third party, the party providing labor has the right to request the third party to bear tort liability, and also has the right to request the party receiving labor to provide compensation. After the party receiving labor provides compensation, it may seek recourse from the third party.

Article 1193 If a contractor causes damage to a third party or to himself or herself in the process of completing the work, the client shall not bear tort liability. However, if the client is at fault for the ordering, instruction, or selection, the client shall bear corresponding liability.

Article 1194 A network user or network service provider who uses the network to infringe upon the civil rights and interests of another person shall bear tort liability. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall apply.

Article 1195 Where a network user uses network services to commit a tort, the right holder has the right to notify the network service provider to take necessary measures such as deletion, blocking, and disconnection of the link. The notification shall include preliminary evidence of the infringement and the true identity information of the right holder.

After receiving the notification, the network service provider shall promptly forward the notification to the relevant network user and take necessary measures based on the preliminary evidence of the infringement and the type of service; if it fails to take necessary measures in a timely manner, it shall bear joint and several liability with the network user for the expanded part of the damage.

If the right holder causes damage to the network user or network service provider due to an erroneous notification, the right holder shall bear tort liability. Where the law provides otherwise, such provisions shall apply.

Article 1196 After receiving the forwarded notification, the network user may submit a declaration to the network service provider that no infringement exists. The declaration shall include preliminary evidence that no infringement exists and the true identity information of the network user.

After receiving the declaration, the network service provider shall forward the declaration to the right holder who issued the notification and inform the right holder that he or she may file a complaint with the relevant authority or file a lawsuit with the people's court. If the network service provider does not receive a notice from the right holder that a complaint or lawsuit has been filed within a reasonable period after the declaration reaches the right holder, it shall promptly terminate the measures taken.

Article 1197 If a network service provider knows or should know that a network user is using its network services to infringe upon the civil rights and interests of another person and fails to take necessary measures, it shall bear joint and several liability with the network user.

Article 1198 Operators, managers of business premises such as hotels, shopping malls, banks, stations, airports, sports venues, entertainment venues, and other public places, or organizers of mass activities, who fail to fulfill their security obligations, causing damage to another person, shall bear tort liability.

If damage is caused to another person by the conduct of a third party, the third party shall bear tort liability; if the operator, manager, or organizer fails to fulfill their security obligations, they shall bear corresponding supplementary liability. After the operator, manager, or organizer bears supplementary liability, they may seek recourse from the third party.

Article 1199 If a person without capacity for civil conduct suffers personal injury while studying or living in a kindergarten, school, or other educational institution, the kindergarten, school, or other educational institution shall bear tort liability; however, if it can prove that it has fulfilled its educational and management duties, it shall not bear tort liability.

Article 1200 If a person with limited capacity for civil conduct suffers personal injury while studying or living in a school or other educational institution, and the school or other educational institution fails to fulfill its educational and management duties, it shall bear tort liability.

Article 1201 If a person without capacity for civil conduct or a person with limited capacity for civil conduct suffers personal injury from a third party other than the kindergarten, school, or other educational institution while studying or living in the kindergarten, school, or other educational institution, the third party shall bear tort liability; if the kindergarten, school, or other educational institution fails to fulfill its management duties, it shall bear corresponding supplementary liability. After the kindergarten, school, or other educational institution bears supplementary liability, it may seek recourse from the third party.

### Chapter IV: Product Liability

Article 1202 If a product defect causes damage to another person, the manufacturer shall bear tort liability.

Article 1203 If a product defect causes damage to another person, the infringed person may claim compensation from the manufacturer of the product or from the seller of the product.

If the product defect is caused by the manufacturer, the seller, after paying compensation, has the right to seek recourse from the manufacturer. If the product defect is caused by the seller's fault, the manufacturer, after paying compensation, has the right to seek recourse from the seller.

Article 1204 If a product defect is caused by the fault of a third party such as a carrier or warehouser, causing damage to another person, the manufacturer or seller of the product, after paying compensation, has the right to seek recourse from the third party.Article 1205 Where a product defect endangers the personal or property safety of others, the infringed person has the right to request the producer or seller to bear tort liability such as cessation of infringement, removal of obstacles, and elimination of danger.

Article 1206 Where a defect is discovered after a product has been put into circulation, the producer or seller shall promptly take remedial measures such as stopping sales, issuing warnings, and recalling the product; if remedial measures are not taken in a timely manner or are inadequate, causing the damage to expand, liability shall also be borne for the expanded damage.

Where recall measures are taken in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the producer or seller shall bear the necessary expenses incurred by the infringed person as a result.

Article 1207 Where a person knowingly produces or sells a defective product, or fails to take effective remedial measures in accordance with the provisions of the preceding article, causing death or serious damage to the health of another person, the infringed person has the right to request corresponding punitive damages.

### Chapter V Liability for Motor Vehicle Traffic Accidents

Article 1208 Where damage is caused by a motor vehicle traffic accident, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the relevant provisions of road traffic safety laws and this Law.

Article 1209 Where the owner, manager, and user of a motor vehicle are not the same person due to leasing, borrowing, or other circumstances, and damage is caused by a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the user of the motor vehicle shall bear compensation liability; if the owner or manager of the motor vehicle is at fault for the occurrence of the damage, they shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

Article 1210 Where a motor vehicle has been transferred and delivered through sale or other means between the parties but registration has not been completed, and damage is caused by a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the transferee shall bear compensation liability.

Article 1211 Where a motor vehicle engaged in road transport operations under an affiliation arrangement causes damage in a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the affiliated party and the affiliated entity shall bear joint and several liability.

Article 1212 Where a person drives another person's motor vehicle without authorization, causing damage in a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the user of the motor vehicle shall bear compensation liability; if the owner or manager of the motor vehicle is at fault for the occurrence of the damage, they shall bear corresponding compensation liability, unless otherwise provided in this Chapter.

Article 1213 Where damage is caused by a motor vehicle traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the insurer of the compulsory motor vehicle insurance shall first compensate within the liability limit of the compulsory insurance; for the shortfall, the insurer of the commercial motor vehicle insurance shall compensate in accordance with the insurance contract; if there is still a shortfall or if no commercial motor vehicle insurance has been purchased, the tortfeasor shall compensate.

Article 1214 Where a motor vehicle that is illegally assembled or has reached the scrapping standard is transferred through sale or other means, and damage is caused by a traffic accident, the transferor and the transferee shall bear joint and several liability.

Article 1215 Where a stolen, robbed, or snatched motor vehicle causes damage in a traffic accident, the thief, robber, or snatcher shall bear compensation liability. If the thief, robber, or snatcher is not the same person as the user of the motor vehicle, and damage is caused by a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the thief, robber, or snatcher and the user of the motor vehicle shall bear joint and several liability.

Where the insurer advances rescue expenses within the liability limit of the compulsory motor vehicle insurance, it has the right to seek recourse from the person liable for the traffic accident.

Article 1216 Where the driver of a motor vehicle flees after a traffic accident, and the motor vehicle is covered by compulsory insurance, the insurer shall compensate within the liability limit of the compulsory motor vehicle insurance; if the motor vehicle is unidentified, is not covered by compulsory insurance, or the rescue expenses exceed the liability limit of the compulsory motor vehicle insurance, and expenses for rescue, funeral, etc., of the infringed person's personal injury or death need to be paid, the Road Traffic Accident Social Assistance Fund shall advance the payment. After the Road Traffic Accident Social Assistance Fund advances the payment, its management authority has the right to seek recourse from the person liable for the traffic accident.

Article 1217 Where a non-commercial motor vehicle causes damage to a gratuitous passenger in a traffic accident for which the motor vehicle party is liable, the compensation liability shall be reduced, unless the user of the motor vehicle acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

### Chapter VI Liability for Medical Malpractice

Article 1218 Where a patient suffers damage during diagnosis and treatment activities, and the medical institution or its medical personnel is at fault, the medical institution shall bear compensation liability.

Article 1219 Medical personnel shall explain the patient's condition and medical measures to the patient during diagnosis and treatment activities. Where surgery, special examinations, or special treatments are required, medical personnel shall promptly explain the medical risks, alternative treatment plans, etc., to the patient in detail and obtain their explicit consent; if it is impossible or inappropriate to explain to the patient, they shall explain to the patient's close relatives and obtain their explicit consent.

If medical personnel fail to fulfill the obligations in the preceding paragraph, causing damage to the patient, the medical institution shall bear compensation liability.

Article 1220 In emergency situations such as rescuing a patient whose life is in danger, where the consent of the patient or their close relatives cannot be obtained, with the approval of the person in charge of the medical institution or an authorized person, corresponding medical measures may be implemented immediately.

Article 1221 Where medical personnel fail to fulfill the diagnosis and treatment obligations corresponding to the prevailing medical standards during diagnosis and treatment activities, causing damage to the patient, the medical institution shall bear compensation liability.

Article 1222 Where a patient suffers damage during diagnosis and treatment activities, and any of the following circumstances exists, the medical institution is presumed to be at fault:

(1) Violating laws, administrative regulations, rules, or other relevant diagnosis and treatment norms;

(2) Concealing or refusing to provide medical records related to the dispute;

(3) Losing, forging, tampering with, or illegally destroying medical records.

Article 1223 Where a patient suffers damage due to defects in drugs, disinfection products, medical devices, or the infusion of unqualified blood, the patient may claim compensation from the drug marketing authorization holder, producer, or blood supply institution, or from the medical institution. If the patient claims compensation from the medical institution, the medical institution, after compensating, has the right to seek recourse from the responsible drug marketing authorization holder, producer, or blood supply institution.

Article 1224 Where a patient suffers damage during diagnosis and treatment activities, and any of the following circumstances exists, the medical institution shall not bear compensation liability:

(1) The patient or their close relatives do not cooperate with the medical institution in diagnosis and treatment that complies with the norms;

(2) Medical personnel have fulfilled reasonable diagnosis and treatment obligations in emergency situations such as rescuing a patient whose life is in danger;

(3) It is difficult to diagnose or treat given the prevailing medical standards at the time.

In the circumstance of item (1) of the preceding paragraph, if the medical institution or its medical personnel is also at fault, they shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

Article 1225 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall fill out and properly keep medical records such as hospitalization records, medical order sheets, test reports, surgical and anesthesia records, pathological data, and nursing records in accordance with regulations.
If a patient requests to review or copy the medical records specified in the preceding paragraph, the medical institution shall provide them promptly.

Article 1226 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall keep the patient's privacy and personal information confidential. Disclosing the patient's privacy and personal information, or making public their medical records without the patient's consent, shall bear tort liability.

Article 1227 Medical institutions and their medical personnel shall not perform unnecessary examinations in violation of diagnosis and treatment norms.

Article 1228 The legitimate rights and interests of medical institutions and their medical personnel are protected by law.

Disrupting medical order, hindering the work and life of medical personnel, or infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of medical personnel shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law.

### Chapter VII Liability for Environmental Pollution and Ecological Damage

Article 1229 Where damage is caused to others by polluting the environment or damaging the ecology, the tortfeasor shall bear tort liability.

Article 1230 In disputes arising from environmental pollution or ecological damage, the actor shall bear the burden of proof regarding the circumstances exempting or mitigating liability as provided by law and the absence of a causal relationship between their act and the damage.

Article 1231 Where two or more tortfeasors pollute the environment or damage the ecology, the extent of their liability shall be determined based on factors such as the type, concentration, and discharge volume of pollutants, the method, scope, and degree of ecological damage, and the role of the act in causing the harmful consequences.

Article 1232 Where a tortfeasor intentionally pollutes the environment or damages the ecology in violation of legal provisions, causing serious consequences, the infringed person has the right to request corresponding punitive damages.

Article 1233 Where environmental pollution or ecological damage is caused by the fault of a third party, the infringed person may claim compensation from the tortfeasor or from the third party. After compensating, the tortfeasor has the right to seek recourse from the third party.

Article 1234 Where ecological and environmental damage is caused in violation of national regulations, and the ecological environment can be restored, the authorities designated by the state or organizations prescribed by law have the right to request the tortfeasor to bear restoration liability within a reasonable period. If the tortfeasor fails to restore within the period, the authorities designated by the state or organizations prescribed by law may carry out the restoration themselves or entrust others to do so, with the costs borne by the tortfeasor.

Article 1235 Where ecological and environmental damage is caused in violation of national regulations, the authorities designated by the state or organizations prescribed by law have the right to request the tortfeasor to compensate for the following losses and expenses:

(1) Losses resulting from the loss of service functions during the period from the occurrence of ecological and environmental damage to the completion of restoration;

(2) Losses caused by permanent damage to ecological and environmental functions;

(3) Expenses for investigation, appraisal, and assessment of ecological and environmental damage;

(4) Expenses for pollution cleanup and ecological and environmental restoration;

(5) Reasonable expenses incurred to prevent the occurrence and expansion of damage.

### Chapter VIII Liability for Ultra-Hazardous Activities

Article 1236 Where damage is caused to others by engaging in ultra-hazardous activities, tort liability shall be borne.

Article 1237 Where a nuclear accident occurs involving civil nuclear facilities or nuclear materials transported into or out of such facilities, causing damage to others, the operator of the civil nuclear facility shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by war, armed conflict, riot, or similar circumstances, or by the intentional act of the victim, no liability shall be borne.

Article 1238 Where a civil aircraft causes damage to others, the operator of the civil aircraft shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by the intentional act of the victim, no liability shall be borne.

Article 1239 Where possession or use of ultra-hazardous materials such as flammable, explosive, highly toxic, highly radioactive, highly corrosive, or highly pathogenic substances causes damage to others, the possessor or user shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by the intentional act of the victim or force majeure, no liability shall be borne. If the infringed person is grossly negligent in the occurrence of the damage, the liability of the possessor or user may be reduced.

Article 1240 Where activities such as high-altitude operations, high-pressure operations, underground excavation, or the use of high-speed rail transport cause damage to others, the operator shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by the intentional act of the victim or force majeure, no liability shall be borne. If the infringed person is grossly negligent in the occurrence of the damage, the liability of the operator may be reduced.

Article 1241 Where loss or abandonment of ultra-hazardous materials causes damage to others, the owner shall bear tort liability. If the owner entrusts the management of ultra-hazardous materials to another person, the manager shall bear tort liability; if the owner is at fault, they shall bear joint and several liability with the manager.

Article 1242 Where illegal possession of ultra-hazardous materials causes damage to others, the illegal possessor shall bear tort liability. If the owner or manager cannot prove that they have exercised a high degree of care to prevent illegal possession, they shall bear joint and several liability with the illegal possessor.

Article 1243 Where a person enters an ultra-hazardous activity area or an area where ultra-hazardous materials are stored without permission and suffers damage, if the manager can prove that sufficient safety measures have been taken and adequate warnings have been given, liability may be reduced or exempted.

Article 1244 Where the law provides for a limit on compensation for ultra-hazardous liability, that limit shall apply, unless the actor acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

### Chapter IX Liability for Damage Caused by Domesticated Animals

Article 1245 Where a domesticated animal causes damage to others, the animal keeper or manager shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by the intentional act or gross negligence of the infringed person, liability may be exempted or reduced.

Article 1246 Where failure to take safety measures for an animal in violation of management regulations causes damage to others, the animal keeper or manager shall bear tort liability; however, if it can be proven that the damage was caused by the intentional act of the infringed person, liability may be reduced.

Article 1247 Where a dangerous animal such as a ferocious dog that is prohibited from being kept causes damage to others, the animal keeper or manager shall bear tort liability.

Article 1248 Where an animal in a zoo causes damage to others, the zoo shall bear tort liability; however, if it can prove that it has fulfilled its management duties, no tort liability shall be borne.

Article 1249 Where an abandoned or escaped animal causes damage to others during the period of abandonment or escape, the original keeper or manager of the animal shall bear tort liability.

Article 1250 Where an animal causes damage to others due to the fault of a third party, the infringed person may claim compensation from the animal keeper or manager, or from the third party. After compensating, the animal keeper or manager has the right to seek recourse from the third party.

Article 1251 The keeping of domesticated animals shall comply with laws and regulations, respect social morality, and not hinder the lives of others.

### Chapter X Liability for Damage Caused by Buildings and Objects

Article 1252 Where the collapse or subsidence of a building, structure, or other facility causes damage to others, the construction entity and the construction unit shall bear joint and several liability, unless the construction entity and the construction unit can prove that there is no quality defect. After compensating, if there are other responsible parties, the construction entity and the construction unit have the right to seek recourse from those other responsible parties.

Where the collapse or subsidence of a building, structure, or other facility causes damage to others due to the fault of the owner, manager, user, or a third party, the owner, manager, user, or third party shall bear tort liability.

Article 1253 Where an object placed or hung on a building, structure, or other facility falls or detaches, causing damage to others, the owner, manager, or user shall bear tort liability unless they can prove that they are not at fault. After compensating, if there are other responsible parties, the owner, manager, or user has the right to seek recourse from those other responsible parties.

Article 1254 Throwing objects from buildings is prohibited. Where an object thrown from a building or falling from a building causes damage to others, the tortfeasor shall bear tort liability in accordance with the law; if it is difficult to identify the specific tortfeasor after investigation, unless they can prove that they are not the tortfeasor, the possible injuring building users shall provide compensation. After providing compensation, the possible injuring building users have the right to seek recourse from the tortfeasor.

Property service enterprises and other building managers shall take necessary safety measures to prevent the occurrence of the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph; if necessary safety measures are not taken, they shall bear tort liability for failing to fulfill safety protection obligations in accordance with the law.

Where the circumstances specified in paragraph 1 of this article occur, public security and other authorities shall promptly investigate in accordance with the law to identify the responsible person.

Article 1255 Where stacked objects collapse, roll, or slide, causing damage to others, the stacker shall bear tort liability unless they can prove that they are not at fault.

Article 1256 Where objects are stacked, dumped, or spilled on public roads, obstructing passage and causing damage to others, the actor shall bear tort liability. If the public road manager cannot prove that they have fulfilled obligations such as cleaning, protection, and warning, they shall bear corresponding liability.

Article 1257 Where the breaking, falling, or falling of fruits from trees causes damage to others, the owner or manager of the trees shall bear tort liability unless they can prove that they are not at fault.

Article 1258 Where excavation, repair, or installation of underground facilities in public places or on roads causes damage to others, the constructor shall bear tort liability unless they can prove that they have set up obvious signs and taken safety measures.

Where manholes and other underground facilities cause damage to others, the manager shall bear tort liability unless they can prove that they have fulfilled their management duties.
