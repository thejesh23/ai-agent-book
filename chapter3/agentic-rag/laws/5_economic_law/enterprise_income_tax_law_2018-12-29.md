# Enterprise Income Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Tenth National People's Congress on March 16, 2007

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Enterprise Income Tax Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 26th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on February 24, 2017

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Four Laws including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Seventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 Enterprises and other organizations that obtain income within the territory of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter collectively referred to as "enterprises") are taxpayers of enterprise income tax and shall pay enterprise income tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Sole proprietorship enterprises and partnership enterprises are not subject to this Law.

Article 2 Enterprises are divided into resident enterprises and non-resident enterprises.

For the purposes of this Law, "resident enterprise" means an enterprise that is established in China in accordance with the law, or an enterprise that is established in accordance with the laws of a foreign country (region) but whose actual management body is located in China.

For the purposes of this Law, "non-resident enterprise" means an enterprise that is established in accordance with the laws of a foreign country (region) and whose actual management body is not located in China, but which has established an institution or place in China, or an enterprise that has not established an institution or place in China but has income derived from sources within China.

Article 3 Resident enterprises shall pay enterprise income tax on their income derived from sources within and outside China.

Non-resident enterprises that have established an institution or place in China shall pay enterprise income tax on the income derived from sources within China obtained by such institution or place, and on the income derived from sources outside China that is effectively connected with such institution or place.

Non-resident enterprises that have not established an institution or place in China, or that have established an institution or place but whose income is not effectively connected with such institution or place, shall pay enterprise income tax on their income derived from sources within China.

Article 4 The enterprise income tax rate is 25%.

For the income obtained by non-resident enterprises as specified in Paragraph 3 of Article 3 of this Law, the applicable tax rate is 20%.

## Chapter II: Taxable Income

Article 5 The taxable income of an enterprise for each tax year shall be the total income for that year, reduced by the amounts of non-taxable income, tax-exempt income, various deductions, and the losses of prior years that are allowed to be offset.

Article 6 The total income of an enterprise shall include income obtained from various sources in monetary and non-monetary forms, including:

(1) income from the sale of goods;
(2) income from the provision of services;
(3) income from the transfer of property;
(4) income from equity investments such as dividends and bonuses;
(5) interest income;
(6) rental income;
(7) income from royalties;
(8) income from donations received;
(9) other income.

Article 7 The following income in the total income shall be non-taxable income:

(1) fiscal appropriations;
(2) administrative fees and government funds that are collected in accordance with the law and included in fiscal management;
(3) other non-taxable income as prescribed by the State Council.

Article 8 The actual and reasonable expenditures incurred by an enterprise in connection with the generation of income, including costs, expenses, taxes, losses, and other expenditures, shall be allowed as deductions in calculating taxable income.

Article 9 Expenditures incurred by an enterprise for public welfare donations, within 12% of the total annual profit, shall be allowed as deductions in calculating taxable income; the portion exceeding 12% of the total annual profit shall be allowed to be carried forward and deducted in the calculation of taxable income within the following three years.

Article 10 In calculating taxable income, the following expenditures shall not be deducted:

(1) payments of equity investment proceeds such as dividends and bonuses to investors;
(2) enterprise income tax payments;
(3) tax surcharges for late payment;
(4) fines, penalties, and losses from confiscated property;
(5) donation expenditures other than those specified in Article 9 of this Law;
(6) sponsorship expenditures;
(7) unverified reserve expenditures;
(8) other expenditures unrelated to the generation of income.

Article 11 In calculating taxable income, depreciation of fixed assets calculated by the enterprise in accordance with regulations shall be allowed as a deduction.

Depreciation of the following fixed assets shall not be deducted:

(1) fixed assets other than buildings and structures that are not put into use;
(2) fixed assets leased in under an operating lease;
(3) fixed assets leased out under a finance lease;
(4) fixed assets that continue to be used after full depreciation has been claimed;
(5) fixed assets unrelated to business activities;
(6) land recorded as a fixed asset at a separately appraised value;
(7) other fixed assets for which depreciation deductions are not allowed.

Article 12 In calculating taxable income, amortization expenses of intangible assets calculated by the enterprise in accordance with regulations shall be allowed as a deduction.

Amortization expenses of the following intangible assets shall not be deducted:

(1) intangible assets for which the self-developed expenditures have already been deducted in calculating taxable income;
(2) self-created goodwill;
(3) intangible assets unrelated to business activities;
(4) other intangible assets for which amortization expense deductions are not allowed.

Article 13 In calculating taxable income, the following expenditures incurred by an enterprise, treated as long-term deferred expenses and amortized in accordance with regulations, shall be allowed as deductions:

(1) renovation expenditures for fixed assets that have been fully depreciated;
(2) renovation expenditures for leased-in fixed assets;
(3) major repair expenditures for fixed assets;
(4) other expenditures that should be treated as long-term deferred expenses.

Article 14 During the period of an enterprise's external investment, the cost of investment assets shall not be deducted in calculating taxable income.

Article 15 When an enterprise uses or sells inventory, the cost of inventory calculated in accordance with regulations shall be allowed as a deduction in calculating taxable income.

Article 16 When an enterprise transfers assets, the net value of such assets shall be allowed as a deduction in calculating taxable income.

Article 17 When an enterprise calculates and pays enterprise income tax on a consolidated basis, losses from its overseas operating establishments shall not be used to offset profits from its domestic operating establishments.

Article 18 Losses incurred by an enterprise in a tax year shall be allowed to be carried forward to subsequent years and offset against income of those subsequent years, but the carryforward period shall not exceed five years at most.

Article 19 For non-resident enterprises obtaining income as specified in Paragraph 3 of Article 3 of this Law, the taxable income shall be calculated according to the following methods:

(1) for income from equity investments such as dividends and bonuses, interest, rent, and royalties, the full amount of the income shall be the taxable income;
(2) for income from the transfer of property, the balance after deducting the net value of the property from the full amount of the income shall be the taxable income;
(3) for other income, the taxable income shall be calculated by reference to the methods specified in the preceding two items.

Article 20 The specific scope and standards for income and deductions specified in this Chapter, and the specific methods for tax treatment of assets, shall be prescribed by the finance and tax authorities of the State Council.

Article 21 In calculating taxable income, where the financial and accounting treatment methods of an enterprise are inconsistent with the provisions of tax laws and administrative regulations, the calculation shall be made in accordance with the provisions of tax laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter III: Taxable Amount

Article 22 The taxable amount of an enterprise shall be the balance after multiplying the taxable income by the applicable tax rate, and deducting the amounts of tax reductions and credits in accordance with the provisions on tax incentives in this Law.

Article 23 The amount of income tax paid overseas on the following income obtained by an enterprise may be credited against its current period's tax payable, with the credit limit being the tax payable on such income calculated in accordance with the provisions of this Law; the portion exceeding the credit limit may be offset in the following five tax years against the balance after using the annual credit limit to offset the amount due for that year:

(1) taxable income derived by a resident enterprise from sources outside China;
(2) taxable income derived by a non-resident enterprise that has established an institution or place in China, from sources outside China but effectively connected with such institution or place.

Article 24 Where a resident enterprise receives dividends, bonuses, and other equity investment proceeds derived from sources outside China from a foreign enterprise that it directly or indirectly controls, the portion of the income tax actually paid overseas by the foreign enterprise that is attributable to such income may be treated as creditable overseas income tax for the resident enterprise, and may be credited within the credit limit specified in Article 23 of this Law.

## Chapter IV: Tax Incentives

Article 25 The State provides enterprise income tax incentives to industries and projects that are key supported and encouraged for development.

Article 26 The following income of an enterprise shall be tax-exempt income:

(1) interest income from treasury bonds;
(2) dividends, bonuses, and other equity investment proceeds between qualified resident enterprises;
(3) dividends, bonuses, and other equity investment proceeds obtained by a non-resident enterprise that has established an institution or place in China from a resident enterprise, which are effectively connected with such institution or place;
(4) income of qualified non-profit organizations.

Article 27 The following income of an enterprise may be exempted from or reduced in enterprise income tax:

(1) income from projects in agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, and fisheries;
(2) income from investment and operation in public infrastructure projects that are key supported by the State;
(3) income from qualified projects in environmental protection, energy conservation, and water conservation;
(4) income from qualified technology transfers;
(5) income as specified in Paragraph 3 of Article 3 of this Law.

Article 28 Qualified small low-profit enterprises shall be subject to enterprise income tax at a reduced rate of 20%.

High-tech enterprises that are key supported by the State shall be subject to enterprise income tax at a reduced rate of 15%.

Article 29 The autonomous authorities of ethnic autonomous areas may decide to reduce or exempt the portion of enterprise income tax payable by enterprises in their ethnic autonomous areas that belongs to the local share. Where an autonomous prefecture or autonomous county decides on a reduction or exemption, it must be reported to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval.

Article 30 The following expenditures of an enterprise may be additionally deducted in calculating taxable income:

(1) research and development expenses incurred in developing new technologies, new products, and new processes;
(2) wages paid for employing disabled persons and other persons whose employment is encouraged by the State.

Article 31 Venture capital enterprises engaged in venture capital investments that are key supported and encouraged by the State may deduct a certain percentage of their investment amount from their taxable income.

Article 32 Where fixed assets of an enterprise need accelerated depreciation due to technological progress or other reasons, the depreciation period may be shortened or accelerated depreciation methods may be adopted.

Article 33 For income obtained by an enterprise from comprehensively utilizing resources to produce products that meet the industrial policies prescribed by the State, the income may be reduced in calculating taxable income.

Article 34 For the investment amount of an enterprise in purchasing special equipment for environmental protection, energy and water conservation, and production safety, a certain percentage may be credited against the tax payable.

Article 35 The specific methods for tax incentives provided in this Law shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 36 Based on the needs of national economic and social development, or due to emergencies or other reasons that have a significant impact on business operations, the State Council may formulate special enterprise income tax preferential policies, which shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

## Chapter V: Withholding at Source

Article 37 For the income tax payable by non-resident enterprises on income as specified in Paragraph 3 of Article 3 of this Law, withholding at source shall be implemented, with the payer acting as the withholding agent. The tax shall be withheld by the withholding agent from the amount payable at the time of each payment or when the payment becomes due.

Article 38 For the income tax payable by non-resident enterprises on income from engineering operations and labor services obtained within China, the tax authorities may designate the payer of the project price or labor fees as the withholding agent.

Article 39 Where the income tax should have been withheld in accordance with Articles 37 and 38 of this Law, but the withholding agent fails to withhold in accordance with the law or is unable to fulfill the withholding obligation, the taxpayer shall pay the tax at the place where the income arises. If the taxpayer fails to pay in accordance with the law, the tax authorities may recover the tax payable by the taxpayer from amounts payable by the payer of other income items of the taxpayer within China.

Article 40 The tax withheld each time by the withholding agent shall be paid into the State treasury within seven days from the date of withholding, and a report on the withholding of enterprise income tax shall be submitted to the local tax authorities.

## Chapter VI: Special Tax Adjustments

Article 41 Where business transactions between an enterprise and its related parties do not comply with the arm's length principle and reduce the taxable income or income amount of the enterprise or its related parties, the tax authorities have the right to make adjustments using reasonable methods.

Costs incurred by an enterprise and its related parties in jointly developing or transferring intangible assets, or in jointly providing or receiving services, shall be allocated in accordance with the arm's length principle when calculating taxable income.

Article 42 An enterprise may propose to the tax authorities the pricing principles and calculation methods for transactions with its related parties. After consultation and confirmation between the tax authorities and the enterprise, an advance pricing arrangement may be reached.

Article 43 When an enterprise submits its annual enterprise income tax return to the tax authorities, it shall attach an annual report on related party transactions regarding its transactions with related parties.

When the tax authorities conduct investigations into related party transactions, the enterprise, its related parties, and other enterprises related to the investigation shall provide relevant information in accordance with regulations.

Article 44 If an enterprise fails to provide information on transactions with its related parties, or provides false or incomplete information, and thus fails to truthfully reflect its related party transactions, the tax authorities have the right to assess its taxable income in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Where an enterprise controlled by a resident enterprise, or by a resident enterprise and a Chinese resident, is established in a country (region) where the actual tax burden is significantly lower than the tax rate specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 4 of this Law, and does not distribute or reduces the distribution of profits without reasonable business needs, the portion of such profits attributable to the resident enterprise shall be included in the current period income of the resident enterprise.

Article 46 Interest expenses incurred by an enterprise from debt investments received from its related parties that exceed the prescribed ratio of debt investments to equity investments shall not be deducted in calculating taxable income.

Article 47 Where an enterprise implements other arrangements without reasonable commercial purposes that reduce its taxable income or income amount, the tax authorities have the right to make adjustments using reasonable methods.

Article 48 Where the tax authorities make tax adjustments in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter and need to collect additional tax, the additional tax shall be collected, and interest shall be charged in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

## Chapter VII: Tax Collection and Administration

Article 49 The collection and administration of enterprise income tax shall, in addition to the provisions of this Law, be carried out in accordance with the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection.

Article 50 Unless otherwise provided by tax laws and administrative regulations, a resident enterprise shall pay tax at the place of its registered address; however, if the registered address is overseas, the tax shall be paid at the place where the actual management body is located.

If a resident enterprise establishes operating establishments without legal personality within China, it shall calculate and pay enterprise income tax on a consolidated basis.

Article 51 For income obtained by a non-resident enterprise as specified in Paragraph 2 of Article 3 of this Law, the tax shall be paid at the location of the institution or place. If a non-resident enterprise establishes two or more institutions or places within China, and meets the conditions prescribed by the tax authorities of the State Council, it may choose to have its main institution or place calculate and pay enterprise income tax on a consolidated basis.

For income obtained by a non-resident enterprise as specified in Paragraph 3 of Article 3 of this Law, the tax shall be paid at the location of the withholding agent.

Article 52 Unless otherwise provided by the State Council, enterprises shall not file consolidated enterprise income tax returns.

Article 53 Enterprise income tax shall be calculated on a tax year basis. The tax year shall commence on January 1 and end on December 31 of the Gregorian calendar.

If an enterprise commences operations in the middle of a tax year, or terminates its business activities, resulting in an actual operating period of less than twelve months in that tax year, the actual operating period shall be treated as a tax year.

When an enterprise undergoes liquidation in accordance with the law, the liquidation period shall be treated as a tax year.

Article 54 Enterprise income tax shall be prepaid on a monthly or quarterly basis.

An enterprise shall, within fifteen days from the end of each month or quarter, submit a prepayment return for enterprise income tax to the tax authorities and prepay the tax.

An enterprise shall, within five months from the end of each year, submit an annual enterprise income tax return to the tax authorities, and settle the tax payable or refundable.

When submitting the enterprise income tax return, the enterprise shall attach financial and accounting reports and other relevant information in accordance with regulations.

Article 55 If an enterprise terminates its business activities in the middle of a tax year, it shall, within sixty days from the date of actual termination, handle the final settlement of enterprise income tax for the current period with the tax authorities.

Before completing the cancellation of registration, an enterprise shall declare its liquidation income to the tax authorities and pay enterprise income tax in accordance with the law.

Article 56 Enterprise income tax paid in accordance with this Law shall be calculated in Renminbi. If income is calculated in a currency other than Renminbi, it shall be converted into Renminbi for calculation and tax payment.

## Chapter VIII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 57 For enterprises that were approved for establishment before the promulgation of this Law and enjoyed low tax rate incentives under the tax laws and administrative regulations in effect at that time, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, they may gradually transition to the tax rates specified in this Law within five years after the implementation of this Law; for those enjoying periodic tax reduction or exemption incentives, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, they may continue to enjoy such incentives until the expiration of the period after the implementation of this Law, but for those that have not yet enjoyed the incentives due to lack of profitability, the incentive period shall be calculated from the year of implementation of this Law.

High-tech enterprises that are key supported by the State and newly established in specific areas designated by law for the development of foreign economic cooperation and technological exchanges, as well as in areas where the State Council has prescribed the implementation of special policies for the aforementioned areas, may enjoy transitional tax incentives, with specific measures to be prescribed by the State Council.

Other encouraged enterprises that have been determined by the State may enjoy tax reduction or exemption incentives in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 58 Where tax agreements concluded between the government of the People's Republic of China and foreign governments contain provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the agreements shall prevail.

Article 59 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 60 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2008. The Income Tax Law of the People's Republic of China for Enterprises with Foreign Investment and Foreign Enterprises, adopted at the Fourth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 9, 1991, and the Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise Income Tax, promulgated by the State Council on December 13, 1993, shall be simultaneously repealed.
