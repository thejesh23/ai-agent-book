# Forest Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 20, 1984

First Amendment on April 29, 1998, according to the Decision on Amending the Forest Law of the People's Republic of China at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on August 27, 2009, according to the Decision on Amending Certain Laws at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Revised at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 28, 2019

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to practice the concept that lucid waters and lush mountains are invaluable assets, to protect, cultivate, and rationally utilize forest resources, to accelerate afforestation of the country, to ensure forest ecological security, to build an ecological civilization, and to achieve harmonious coexistence between humans and nature.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities involving the protection, cultivation, and utilization of forests and trees, as well as the management and administration of forests, trees, and forest land within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The protection, cultivation, and utilization of forest resources shall respect and conform to nature, and adhere to the principles of prioritizing ecology, prioritizing protection, combining conservation with cultivation, and sustainable development.

Article 4 The State implements a responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for forest resource protection and development goals. The people's government at a higher level shall assess the performance of the people's government at a lower level in achieving forest resource protection and development goals, forest fire prevention, and the prevention and control of major forest pests and diseases, and shall make the assessment results public.

Local people's governments may establish a forest chief system based on the needs of forest resource protection and development within their respective administrative regions.

Article 5 The State adopts fiscal, tax, financial, and other measures to support the protection and development of forest resources. People's governments at all levels shall ensure investment in forest ecological protection and restoration, and promote forestry development.

Article 6 With the goal of cultivating stable, healthy, high-quality, and efficient forest ecosystems, the State implements classified management and administration of public welfare forests and commercial forests, highlighting their dominant functions while leveraging multiple functions, to achieve the sustainable use of forest resources.

Article 7 The State establishes a forest ecological benefit compensation system, increases support for the protection of public welfare forests, improves the transfer payment policy for key ecological function areas, and guides the people's governments of beneficiary areas and forest ecological protection areas to conduct ecological benefit compensation through consultation and other means.

Article 8 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with the State's provisions on the autonomy of ethnic autonomous areas, implement more preferential policies for forest protection and forestry development in ethnic autonomous areas.

Article 9 The competent forestry department of the State Council is responsible for national forestry work. The competent forestry departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for forestry work within their respective administrative regions.

Township people's governments may designate relevant institutions or assign full-time or part-time personnel to undertake forestry-related work.

Article 10 Afforestation and forest protection are duties of citizens. People's governments at all levels shall organize and carry out compulsory tree-planting activities for the whole population.

March 12 of each year is designated as Arbor Day.

Article 11 The State takes measures to encourage and support forestry scientific research, promote advanced and applicable forestry technologies, and improve the level of forestry science and technology.

Article 12 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the publicity, education, and knowledge dissemination of forest resource protection, and encourage and support grassroots self-governing organizations, news media, forestry enterprises and institutions, volunteers, etc., to carry out publicity activities for forest resource protection.

Education administrative departments and schools shall educate students on forest resource protection.

Article 13 Organizations or individuals that achieve remarkable results in afforestation, forest protection, forest management, and forestry scientific research shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II: Forest Ownership

Article 14 Forest resources belong to the State, except those that are collectively owned as provided by law.

The ownership of State-owned forest resources is exercised by the State Council on behalf of the State. The State Council may authorize the competent department of natural resources of the State Council to uniformly perform the duties of the owner of State-owned forest resources.

Article 15 The ownership and use rights of forest land and the forests and trees thereon shall be uniformly registered and certified by the real estate registration authority. The forests, trees, and forest land in key State forest areas (hereinafter referred to as key forest areas) designated by the State Council shall be registered by the competent department of natural resources of the State Council.

The legitimate rights and interests of owners and users of forests, trees, and forest land are protected by law and shall not be infringed upon by any organization or individual.

Owners and users of forests, trees, and forest land shall protect and rationally utilize forests, trees, and forest land in accordance with the law, and shall not illegally change the use of forest land or destroy forests, trees, and forest land.

Article 16 State-owned forest land and the forests and trees thereon may be determined for use by forestry operators in accordance with the law. The use rights of State-owned forest land and the forests and trees thereon legally obtained by forestry operators may, upon approval, be transferred, leased, or contributed as capital. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Forestry operators shall fulfill the obligations of protecting and cultivating forest resources, ensure the steady growth of State-owned forest resources, and enhance the ecological functions of forests.

Article 17 Where collectively owned forest land and forest land owned by the State but collectively used by farmers (hereinafter referred to as collectively owned forest land) are contracted for management, the contractor shall enjoy the contractual management right of the forest land and the ownership of the trees on the contracted forest land, unless otherwise agreed in the contract. The contractor may, in accordance with the law, transfer the management right of the forest land and the ownership and use rights of the trees through leasing (subcontracting), equity participation, transfer, etc.

Article 18 Collectively owned forest land and the trees thereon that are not contracted for management shall be uniformly managed by the rural collective economic organization. With the consent of more than two-thirds of the members of the villagers' meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the villagers' representatives, and after public announcement, the management right of the forest land and the ownership and use rights of the trees may be transferred in accordance with the law through bidding, auction, public negotiation, etc.

Article 19 A written contract shall be signed for the transfer of the management right of collectively owned forest land. The contract for the transfer of the management right of forest land generally includes the rights and obligations of both parties, the transfer period, the transfer price and payment method, the disposal of trees and fixed production facilities on the forest land upon expiration of the transfer period, and liability for breach of contract.

If the transferee causes serious damage to forests, trees, or forest land in violation of legal provisions or the contract, the party that issued the contract or the contractor has the right to recover the management right of the forest land.

Article 20 Trees planted by State-owned enterprises and institutions, government organs, organizations, and military units shall be managed and protected by the planting units, and the income from the trees shall be disposed of in accordance with State regulations.

Trees planted by rural residents in front of and behind their houses, on their private plots, and on their private hills shall belong to the individuals. Trees planted by urban residents in the courtyards of their own houses shall belong to the individuals.

Trees planted by collectives or individuals on barren mountains, wasteland, and barren beaches suitable for afforestation owned by the State or collectives under contract shall belong to the contracting collectives or individuals, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

Trees planted by other organizations or individuals shall be owned by the planters in accordance with the law, and they shall enjoy the income from the trees, unless otherwise agreed in the contract.

Article 21 Where it is truly necessary to expropriate or requisition forest land or trees for the needs of public interests such as ecological protection and infrastructure construction, the approval procedures shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations, and fair and reasonable compensation shall be given.

Article 22 Disputes over the ownership and use rights of trees and forest land between units shall be handled by the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law.

Disputes over the ownership of trees and the use rights of forest land between individuals, or between individuals and units, shall be handled by the township people's government or the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law.

If a party is dissatisfied with the handling decision of the relevant people's government, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court within thirty days from the date of receiving the notice of the handling decision.

Before the dispute over the ownership of trees and forest land is resolved, neither party may cut down the disputed trees or change the current state of the forest land, except for needs such as forest fire prevention, forest pest and disease control, and major national infrastructure construction.

## Chapter III: Development Planning

Article 23 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate forest resource protection and forestry development into national economic and social development plans.

Article 24 People's governments at or above the county level shall implement the requirements for the development and protection of territorial space, rationally plan the structure and layout of forest resource protection and utilization, formulate goals for forest resource protection and development, increase forest coverage and forest stock volume, and improve the quality and stability of forest ecosystems.

Article 25 The competent forestry departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate forestry development plans based on the goals for forest resource protection and development. Lower-level forestry development plans shall be formulated based on higher-level forestry development plans.

Article 26 The competent forestry departments of people's governments at or above the county level may, based on local conditions, formulate relevant special plans such as forest land protection and utilization, afforestation, forest management, and natural forest protection.

Article 27 The State establishes a forest resource survey and monitoring system to investigate, monitor, and evaluate the current status and changes of national forest resources, and to publish the results periodically.

## Chapter IV: Forest Protection

Article 28 The State strengthens the protection of forest resources, leveraging the multiple functions of forests such as water conservation, soil retention, climate regulation, environmental improvement, maintenance of biodiversity, and provision of forest products.

Article 29 Central and local finances shall respectively allocate funds for the afforestation, tending, protection, and management of public welfare forests, and for economic compensation to owners of non-State-owned public welfare forests. These funds shall be used for designated purposes only. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent finance department of the State Council in conjunction with the competent forestry department.

Article 30 The State supports the transformation and development of key forest areas and the protection and restoration of forest resources, improves production and living conditions, and promotes the economic and social development of the regions where they are located. Key forest areas shall enjoy policies such as transfer payments for national key ecological function areas in accordance with regulations.

Article 31 The State establishes a system of natural protected areas with national parks as the main body in typical forest ecological areas of different natural zones, forest areas where rare animals and plants grow and reproduce, natural tropical rainforest areas, and other natural forest areas with special protection value, to strengthen protection and management.

The State supports the protection and restoration of forest resources in ecologically fragile areas.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to protect wild plant resources of special value.

Article 32 The State implements a comprehensive protection system for natural forests, strictly restricts the logging of natural forests, strengthens the capacity for natural forest management and protection, protects and restores natural forest resources, and gradually improves the ecological functions of natural forests. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 33 Local people's governments at all levels shall organize relevant departments to establish forest protection organizations responsible for forest protection work; construct forest protection facilities as needed to strengthen forest resource protection; urge relevant organizations to establish forest protection covenants, organize the public to protect forests, designate forest protection responsibility areas, and assign full-time or part-time forest rangers.

People's governments at the county or township level may employ forest rangers, whose main duties are to patrol forests, detect fires, forest pests and diseases, and acts of destroying forest resources, and to promptly handle and report to the local forestry and other relevant departments.

Article 34 Local people's governments at all levels are responsible for forest fire prevention work within their respective administrative regions, leveraging the role of mass prevention; people's governments at or above the county level shall organize and lead emergency management, forestry, public security, and other departments to closely cooperate in accordance with their respective responsibilities to do a good job in the scientific prevention, suppression, and handling of forest fires:

(1) Organize and carry out forest fire prevention publicity activities, popularize forest fire prevention knowledge;
(2) Designate forest fire prevention areas and specify forest fire prevention periods;
(3) Install fire prevention facilities, equip with fire prevention and suppression equipment and materials;
(4) Establish a forest fire monitoring and early warning system, promptly eliminate hazards;
(5) Formulate forest fire emergency plans, and upon the occurrence of a forest fire, immediately organize suppression;
(6) Ensure the funds needed for the prevention and suppression of forest fires.

The national comprehensive fire rescue team undertakes the tasks of forest fire suppression and related prevention work as stipulated by the State.

Article 35 The competent forestry departments of people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for the monitoring, quarantine, and control of forest pests and diseases within their respective administrative regions.

The competent forestry departments of people's governments at or above the provincial level are responsible for determining quarantine pests of forest plants and their products, and designating epidemic areas and protected areas.

The prevention and control of major forest pest and disease disasters shall be the responsibility of local people's governments. When outbreaks of explosive, dangerous, or other major forest pest and disease disasters occur, the local people's government shall promptly organize control and elimination.

Forestry operators, under the guidance and support of the government, shall control forest pests and diseases within their management scope.

Article 36 The State protects forest land, strictly controls the conversion of forest land to non-forest land, implements total quantity control of occupied forest land, and ensures that the amount of forest land is not reduced. The occupation of forest land by various construction projects shall not exceed the total quantity control target for occupied forest land within the administrative region.

Article 37 Mineral exploration, extraction, and other types of engineering construction shall avoid or minimize the occupation of forest land; if it is truly necessary to occupy forest land, it shall be subject to the review and approval of the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level, and the approval procedures for construction land shall be handled in accordance with the law.

Units occupying forest land shall pay a forest vegetation restoration fee. Measures for the collection, use, and management of the forest vegetation restoration fee shall be formulated by the competent finance department of the State Council in conjunction with the competent forestry department.

The competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall arrange for afforestation to restore forest vegetation in accordance with regulations, and the area of afforestation shall not be less than the area of forest vegetation reduced due to the occupation of forest land. The higher-level competent forestry department shall regularly supervise the lower-level competent forestry department in organizing afforestation and restoring forest vegetation, and conduct inspections.

Article 38 If temporary use of forest land is needed, it shall be approved by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level; the term of temporary use of forest land generally shall not exceed two years, and permanent structures shall not be built on the temporarily used forest land.

Within one year after the expiration of the temporary use of forest land, the land user or individual shall restore the vegetation and forestry production conditions.

Article 39 Acts of destroying forests for reclamation, quarrying, sand mining, soil extraction, and other acts that damage trees and forest land are prohibited.

Discharging sewage or sludge containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding standards, as well as dredging sediment, tailings, slag, etc., that may cause pollution to forest land, is prohibited.

Chopping firewood, destroying seedlings, and grazing in young forest land are prohibited.

Unauthorized movement or damage to forest protection signs is prohibited.

Article 40 The State protects ancient and famous trees and precious trees. Destroying ancient and famous trees and precious trees and their natural living environment is prohibited.

Article 41 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the construction of forestry infrastructure, apply advanced and applicable scientific and technological means, and improve the capacity for forest management and protection such as forest fire prevention and forest pest and disease control.

Relevant units shall strengthen forest management and protection. State-owned forestry enterprises and institutions shall increase investment, strengthen forest fire prevention and forest pest and disease control, and prevent and stop acts of destroying forest resources.

## Chapter V: Afforestation

Article 42 The State coordinates urban and rural afforestation, carries out large-scale national land greening actions, beautifies urban and rural areas, promotes the construction of forest cities, promotes rural revitalization, and builds a beautiful homeland.

Article 43 People's governments at all levels shall organize all walks of life and urban and rural residents to carry out afforestation.

Barren mountains, wasteland, and barren beaches suitable for afforestation that are owned by the State shall be afforested by the competent forestry department and other relevant competent departments of the people's government at or above the county level; those that are collectively owned shall be afforested by the collective economic organization.

Within urban planning areas, along railways and highways, along rivers, and around lakes and reservoirs, the relevant competent departments shall organize afforestation according to local conditions in accordance with relevant regulations; in mining areas, industrial parks, land used by government organs and schools, military camps, and areas managed by farms, pastures, and fisheries, the respective units shall be responsible for afforestation. Specific measures for organizing urban afforestation shall be formulated by the State Council.

Barren mountains, wasteland, and barren beaches suitable for afforestation owned by the State or collectives may be contracted by units or individuals for afforestation.

Article 44 The State encourages citizens to participate in afforestation through tree planting, tending and management, and voluntary adoption and sponsorship.

Article 45 When organizing afforestation, people's governments at all levels shall plan scientifically, adapt to local conditions, optimize forest and tree species structures, encourage the use of native tree species and improved forest tree varieties, and plant mixed forests to improve the quality of afforestation.

Afforestation projects invested by the State or mainly funded by the State shall use improved forest tree varieties in accordance with State regulations.

Article 46 People's governments at all levels shall adopt measures combining natural restoration as the main approach with artificial restoration to scientifically protect and restore forest ecosystems. In newly planted young forest land and other areas where mountain closure for afforestation should be implemented, the local people's government shall organize mountain closure for afforestation.

People's governments at all levels shall plan and organize the conversion of cropland to forest and grassland for croplands that require ecological restoration, such as sloping cropland, severely desertified cropland, severely stony desertified cropland, and severely polluted cropland designated by the State Council.

People's governments at all levels shall implement forest ecological restoration projects according to local conditions to restore vegetation on barren and damaged mountains, degraded forest land, and barren mountains, wasteland, and barren beaches suitable for afforestation caused by natural factors, etc.

## Chapter VI: Management and Administration

Article 47 Based on the needs of ecological protection, the State designates forest land and the forests thereon that are ecologically important or ecologically fragile, with the main purpose of exerting ecological benefits, as public welfare forests. Forest land and the forests thereon that are not designated as public welfare forests belong to commercial forests.

Article 48 Public welfare forests shall be designated and announced by the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Forest land and the forests thereon in the following areas shall be designated as public welfare forests:

(1) Water catchment areas at the sources of major rivers;
(2) Banks of main streams and tributaries of major rivers, and protected areas of drinking water sources;
(3) Surroundings of important wetlands and major reservoirs;
(4) Nature reserves of forest and terrestrial wild animal types;
(5) Base shelterbelt networks for wind and sand fixation in areas with severe desertification and soil erosion;
(6) Base coastal shelterbelt networks;
(7) Undeveloped primary forest areas;
(8) Other areas that need to be designated.

If the designation of public welfare forests involves non-State-owned forest land, a written agreement shall be signed with the right holder, and reasonable compensation shall be given.

Adjustments to public welfare forests shall be approved by the original designating authority and announced.

Measures for the designation and management of national-level public welfare forests shall be formulated by the State Council; measures for the designation and management of local-level public welfare forests shall be formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 49 The State implements strict protection for public welfare forests.

The competent forestry departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall plan to organize operators of public welfare forests to take measures such as stand improvement and forest tending for low-quality and low-efficiency forests such as open forests and degraded secondary forests with low ecological functions in public welfare forests, to improve the quality and ecological protection functions of public welfare forests. Under the premise of meeting the ecological location protection requirements of public welfare forests and without affecting their ecological functions, and after scientific demonstration, the forest land resources and forest landscape resources of public welfare forests may be rationally utilized to moderately develop under-forest economy, forest tourism, etc. Such activities using public welfare forests must strictly comply with relevant national regulations.
Article 50 The State encourages the development of the following commercial forests:

(1) Forests primarily aimed at producing timber;
(2) Forests primarily aimed at producing forest products such as fruits, oils, beverages, condiments, industrial raw materials, and medicinal herbs;
(3) Forests primarily aimed at producing fuel and other biomass energy;
(4) Other forests primarily aimed at generating economic benefits.

Under the premise of ensuring ecological security, the State encourages the construction of fast-growing and high-yield forests, precious tree species forests, and large-diameter timber forests to increase forest reserves and ensure timber supply security.

Article 51 Commercial forests shall be independently managed by forest operators in accordance with the law. Without undermining the ecology, intensive management measures may be adopted to rationally utilize forests, trees, and forest land, thereby improving the economic benefits of commercial forests.

Article 52 The construction of the following engineering facilities directly serving forestry production and operation on forest land, which meet the standards prescribed by relevant state departments, shall be approved by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level, without the need to go through the approval procedures for construction land use; if the standards are exceeded and forest land occupation is required, the approval procedures for construction land use shall be handled in accordance with the law:

(1) Facilities for cultivating and producing seeds and seedlings;
(2) Facilities for storing seeds, seedlings, and timber;
(3) Skid roads, haul roads, fire patrol roads, and forest trails;
(4) Forestry scientific research and science education facilities;
(5) Facilities for wildlife protection, forest protection, forest pest control, forest fire prevention, and timber quarantine;
(6) Water supply, power supply, heat supply, gas supply, and communication infrastructure;
(7) Other engineering facilities directly serving forestry production.

Article 53 State-owned forestry enterprises and institutions shall prepare forest management plans, specifying management measures for forest cultivation and protection, and submit them to the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level for approval before implementation. Forest management plans for key forest areas shall be approved by the competent forestry department of the State Council before implementation.

The State supports and guides other forest operators in preparing forest management plans.

The specific methods for preparing forest management plans shall be formulated by the competent forestry department of the State Council.

Article 54 The State strictly controls the annual forest cutting volume. The competent forestry department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, based on the principle that consumption is lower than growth and the classified management of forests, prepare the annual cutting quota for its administrative region. After soliciting opinions from the competent forestry department of the State Council and obtaining approval from the people's government at the same level, it shall be published and implemented, and reported to the State Council for record. The annual cutting quota for key forest areas shall be prepared by the competent forestry department of the State Council and published and implemented after approval by the State Council.

Article 55 The cutting of forests and trees shall comply with the following provisions:

(1) Public welfare forests may only be cut for the purposes of tending, regeneration, and transformation of low-quality and low-efficiency forests. However, cutting is allowed when necessary for scientific research or experiments, forest pest control, construction of forest fire prevention facilities, creation of biological firebreaks, or due to natural disasters.
(2) Commercial forests shall adopt different cutting methods based on different circumstances, strictly control the area of clear-cutting, and plan and implement cutting and regeneration simultaneously.
(3) Trees in nature reserves are prohibited from being cut. However, cutting is allowed in special circumstances where it is necessary for forest pest control, forest fire prevention, maintaining the living environment of major protected objects, or due to natural disasters, as well as for bamboo forests in experimental areas.

The competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, based on the preceding paragraph and in accordance with principles such as classified management, priority on protection, and emphasis on efficiency and benefits, formulate corresponding technical regulations for tree cutting.

Article 56 The cutting of trees on forest land requires a cutting permit, and cutting shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the cutting permit; cutting of bamboo forests outside nature reserves does not require a cutting permit, but must comply with the technical regulations for tree cutting.

Rural residents cutting scattered trees on their own plots or in front of or behind their houses do not need to apply for a cutting permit.

The regeneration and cutting of farmland shelterbelts, windbreak and sand-fixation forests, road protection forests, bank and dike protection forests, and urban trees on non-forest land shall be managed by the relevant competent departments in accordance with relevant regulations.

The digging and transplanting of trees shall be managed as tree cutting. Specific methods shall be formulated by the competent forestry department of the State Council.

Forgery, alteration, sale, purchase, or leasing of cutting permits is prohibited.

Article 57 Cutting permits shall be issued by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level.

The competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall take measures to facilitate applicants in obtaining cutting permits.

For rural residents cutting trees on their own contracted hills or on collectively owned forest land contracted by individuals, the cutting permit shall be issued by the competent forestry department of the people's government at the county level or the township people's government entrusted by it.

Article 58 To apply for a cutting permit, materials concerning the location, forest type, tree species, area, volume, method, regeneration measures, and tree ownership of the cutting shall be submitted. If the area or volume exceeds the limits prescribed by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the provincial level, survey and design materials for the cutting area shall also be submitted.

Article 59 If the technical regulations for tree cutting are met, the department responsible for reviewing and issuing cutting permits shall promptly issue the cutting permit. However, the department responsible for reviewing and issuing cutting permits shall not issue cutting permits exceeding the annual cutting quota.

Article 60 A cutting permit shall not be issued under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Cutting trees in areas or periods designated for mountain closure for afforestation;
(2) Failure to complete the regeneration and afforestation tasks as required in the previous year;
(3) Occurrence of major illegal logging cases, forest fires, or forest pest disasters in the previous year without taking preventive and corrective measures;
(4) Other circumstances where cutting is prohibited by laws, regulations, or the competent forestry department of the State Council.

Article 61 Organizations and individuals cutting trees shall complete regeneration and afforestation in accordance with relevant regulations. The area of regeneration and afforestation shall not be less than the area cut, and the regeneration and afforestation shall meet the standards prescribed by relevant technical regulations.

Article 62 The State encourages and guides financial institutions to carry out forestry-related credit businesses such as forest mortgage loans and forest farmer credit loans that suit the characteristics of forestry through measures such as interest subsidies and forest rights acquisition and storage guarantee subsidies, and supports forest rights acquisition and storage institutions in conducting market-based acquisition and storage guarantees.

Article 63 The State supports the development of forest insurance. The people's government at or above the county level shall provide insurance premium subsidies for forest insurance in accordance with the law.

Article 64 Forest operators may voluntarily apply for forest certification to promote the improvement of forest management levels and sustainable management.

Article 65 Timber management and processing enterprises shall establish ledgers for the entry and exit of raw materials and products. No unit or individual may purchase, process, or transport trees known to be from illegal sources such as illegal logging or excessive logging.

## Chapter VII Supervision and Inspection

Article 66 The competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, supervise and inspect the protection, restoration, utilization, and regeneration of forest resources, and investigate and deal with illegal acts such as damaging forest resources in accordance with the law.

Article 67 When performing the duties of supervision and inspection of forest resource protection, the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level has the right to take the following measures:

(1) Entering production and operation sites for on-site inspection;
(2) Reviewing and copying relevant documents and materials, and sealing up documents and materials that may be transferred, destroyed, concealed, or altered;
(3) Seizing or impounding trees with evidence of illegal origin, as well as tools, equipment, or property used in activities that damage forest resources;
(4) Sealing up sites related to activities that damage forest resources.

The competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the provincial level may, for areas where forest resource protection and development work is ineffective, problems are prominent, and public opinion is strong, interview the main persons in charge of the local people's government at or above the county level and its relevant departments, requiring them to take measures for timely rectification. The interview and rectification situation shall be made public to society.

Article 68 If damage to forest resources causes ecological and environmental damage, the competent department of natural resources and the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level may file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law, demanding compensation for damages from the infringer.

Article 69 Audit institutions shall conduct audit supervision over state-owned forest resource assets in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 70 If the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant state organs fail to perform their duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If an administrative penalty decision should be made in accordance with the provisions of this Law but is not made, the superior competent department has the right to order the subordinate competent department to make the administrative penalty decision or directly impose the administrative penalty.

Article 71 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of the owners or users of forests, trees, or forest land shall bear tort liability in accordance with the law.

Article 72 If a state-owned forestry enterprise or institution violates the provisions of this Law by failing to fulfill the obligation to protect and cultivate forest resources, failing to prepare a forest management plan, or failing to carry out forest management activities in accordance with the approved forest management plan, the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a time limit, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 73 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, changes the use of forest land without the review and approval of the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to restore vegetation and forestry production conditions within a time limit, and may be fined not more than three times the cost required for restoring vegetation and forestry production conditions.

If forest land is occupied without going through the approval procedures for construction land use, even though it has been reviewed and approved by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China.

If permanent buildings are constructed on temporarily used forest land, or if vegetation or forestry production conditions are not restored within one year after the expiration of the temporary use of forest land, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article.

Article 74 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in activities such as reclamation, quarrying, sand mining, soil extraction, or other activities that cause damage to trees shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, and to replant trees in the same place or elsewhere within a time limit at a rate of one to three times the number of damaged trees, and may be fined not more than five times the value of the damaged trees; if forest land is damaged, the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, and to restore vegetation and forestry production conditions within a time limit, and may be fined not more than three times the cost required for restoring vegetation and forestry production conditions.

Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, causes damage to trees by cutting firewood, destroying seedlings, or grazing in young forest land shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, and to replant trees in the same place or elsewhere within a time limit at a rate of one to three times the number of damaged trees.

Discharging wastewater or sludge containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances exceeding standards, as well as dredged sediment, tailings, slag, etc., that may cause pollution to forest land, shall be penalized in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Soil Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 75 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, moves or damages forest protection signs without authorization shall have the forest protection signs restored by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level, and the costs shall be borne by the violator.

Article 76 Anyone who illegally logs trees shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to replant trees in the same place or elsewhere within a time limit at a rate of one to five times the number of illegally logged trees, and shall be fined not less than five times but not more than ten times the value of the illegally logged trees.

Anyone who excessively logs trees shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to replant trees in the same place or elsewhere within a time limit at a rate of one to three times the number of excessively logged trees, and may be fined not less than three times but not more than five times the value of the excessively logged trees.

Article 77 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, forges, alters, sells, purchases, or leases a cutting permit shall have the permit and illegal gains confiscated by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level, and shall be fined not less than one time but not more than three times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 78 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, purchases, processes, or transports trees known to be from illegal sources such as illegal logging or excessive logging shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, and the illegally purchased, processed, or transported trees or the proceeds from their sale shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than three times the price of the illegally purchased, processed, or transported trees may be imposed.

Article 79 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to complete the regeneration and afforestation tasks shall be ordered by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level to complete them within a time limit; if they fail to complete them within the time limit, a fine of not more than two times the cost required for the unfinished afforestation tasks may be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 80 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses or obstructs the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level from carrying out supervision and inspection in accordance with the law may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, they may be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification.

Article 81 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, falls under any of the following circumstances shall have the act performed on their behalf by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law, and the costs of the performance shall be borne by the violator:

(1) Refusing to restore vegetation and forestry production conditions, or the restoration of vegetation and forestry production conditions does not comply with relevant state regulations;
(2) Refusing to replant trees, or the replanting does not comply with relevant state regulations.

The standards for restoring vegetation and forestry production conditions and replanting trees shall be formulated by the competent forestry department of the people's government at or above the provincial level.

Article 82 Public security organs may, in accordance with relevant state regulations, exercise the administrative penalty powers provided for in Article 74, paragraph 1, Article 76, Article 77, and Article 78 of this Law.

Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a violation of public security administration shall be given a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

Article 83 The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) Forests include arbor forests, bamboo forests, and shrub forests specially designated by the State. They can be classified by use into shelter forests, special-purpose forests, timber forests, economic forests, and energy forests.
(2) Trees include trees and bamboos.
(3) Forest land refers to land designated by the people's government at or above the county level for forestry development. It includes arbor forest land with a canopy density of 0.2 or more, as well as bamboo forest land, shrub forest land, sparse forest land, cutting areas, burned areas, immature afforestation land, nursery land, etc.

Article 84 This Law shall come into force on July 1, 2020.
