# Railway Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 7, 1990

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Compulsory Education Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure the smooth progress of railway transport and railway construction, and to meet the needs of socialist modernization and the people's livelihood.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "railways" include national railways, local railways, dedicated railways, and railway sidings.

"National railways" refer to railways managed by the competent railway department under the State Council.

"Local railways" refer to railways managed by local people's governments.

"Dedicated railways" refer to railways managed by enterprises or other entities, which provide transport services exclusively for the internal use of such enterprises or entities.

"Railway sidings" refer to branch lines managed by enterprises or other entities that connect with national railways or other railway lines.

Article 3 The competent railway department under the State Council is responsible for the national railway work, exercising a transport management system of highly centralized and unified command over national railways, and providing guidance, coordination, supervision, and assistance to local railways, dedicated railways, and railway sidings.

National railway transport enterprises exercise the administrative functions granted by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 4 The State prioritizes the development of national railways and vigorously supports the development of local railways.

Article 5 Railway transport enterprises must adhere to the socialist business direction and the principle of serving the people, improve their management and operations, earnestly enhance their service ethos, and raise the quality of transport services.

Article 6 Citizens have the duty to protect railway facilities. It is prohibited for anyone to damage railway facilities or disrupt the normal order of railway transport.

Article 7 Local people's governments at all levels along railway lines shall assist railway transport enterprises in ensuring the safety and smooth flow of railway transport, maintaining good order at stations and on trains, preserving the integrity of railway facilities, and facilitating the smooth progress of railway construction.

Article 8 The technical management regulations for national railways shall be formulated by the competent railway department under the State Council. Technical management measures for local railways and dedicated railways shall be formulated with reference to the technical management regulations for national railways.

Article 9 The State encourages scientific and technological research in railways to enhance the level of railway science and technology. Units and individuals that achieve notable results in railway scientific and technological research shall be rewarded.

## Chapter II Railway Transport Operations

Article 10 Railway transport enterprises shall ensure the safety of passenger and freight transport and ensure that trains arrive on schedule.

Article 11 A railway transport contract is an agreement that defines the rights and obligations between a railway transport enterprise and passengers or consignors.

Passenger tickets, baggage checks, parcel checks, and consignment notes are contracts or constituent parts of contracts.

Article 12 Railway transport enterprises shall ensure that passengers travel on the date and train specified on their tickets and arrive at the destination station. If, due to the fault of the railway transport enterprise, a passenger is unable to travel on the date and train specified on the ticket, the railway transport enterprise shall, at the passenger's request, refund the full fare or arrange for the passenger to travel on another train to the same destination.

Article 13 Railway transport enterprises shall take effective measures to provide good passenger transport services, including being courteous, attentive, and thorough, maintaining cleanliness and hygiene in stations and carriages, providing drinking water, and ensuring food and beverage services on trains.

Railway transport enterprises shall take measures to prevent pollution of the environment along railway lines.

Article 14 Passengers shall travel with valid tickets. Those who travel without a ticket or with an invalid ticket shall pay the fare and an additional charge as prescribed; if they refuse to pay, the railway transport enterprise may order them to leave the train.

Article 15 National railways and local railways shall arrange freight transport plans in accordance with the principles of developing production and invigorating the circulation of goods.

Priority shall be given to the transport of materials for emergency rescue and disaster relief, and other materials that the State stipulates require priority transport.

If materials transported by local railways need to be carried by national railways, the transport plan shall be incorporated into the transport plan of the national railways.

Article 16 Railway transport enterprises shall deliver goods, parcels, and baggage to the destination station within the period agreed upon in the contract or within the period prescribed by the competent railway department under the State Council; if delivery is delayed, the railway transport enterprise shall pay liquidated damages.

If the railway transport enterprise fails to deliver goods, parcels, or baggage to the consignee or passenger within thirty days after the expiry of the delivery period, the consignor, consignee, or passenger shall have the right to claim compensation from the railway transport enterprise as for loss of the goods, parcels, or baggage.

Article 17 Railway transport enterprises shall be liable for compensation for the loss, shortage, deterioration, contamination, or damage of goods, parcels, and baggage occurring from the time of acceptance for carriage to the time of delivery:

(1) If the consignor or passenger voluntarily applies for insured transport, compensation shall be based on the actual loss, but shall not exceed the insured amount.

(2) If transport is not insured, compensation shall be based on the actual loss, but shall not exceed the compensation limit prescribed by the competent railway department under the State Council; if the loss is caused by the intentional act or gross negligence of the railway transport enterprise, the compensation limit shall not apply, and compensation shall be based on the actual loss.

Consignors or passengers may voluntarily take out cargo transport insurance with an insurance company, and the insurance company shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the insurance contract.

Consignors or passengers may voluntarily choose to apply for insured transport or take out cargo transport insurance; they may also choose neither. No one may compel them to apply for insured transport or take out cargo transport insurance by any means.

Article 18 Railway transport enterprises shall not be liable for compensation for losses of goods, parcels, or baggage caused by the following reasons:

(1) Force majeure.

(2) The inherent nature of the goods or items in parcels or baggage, or reasonable wear and tear.

(3) Fault of the consignor, consignee, or passenger.

Article 19 Consignors shall truthfully fill in the consignment note. Railway transport enterprises have the right to inspect the description, weight, and quantity of the goods and parcels declared. If the declaration is found to be inconsistent with the actual situation, the inspection costs shall be borne by the consignor; if the declaration is consistent, the inspection costs shall be borne by the railway transport enterprise, and any damage to items in the goods or parcels caused by the inspection shall be compensated by the railway transport enterprise.

If a consignor underpays freight or other charges due to a false declaration, the shortfall shall be made up, and the railway transport enterprise shall impose an additional charge for the freight and other fees in accordance with the regulations of the competent railway department under the State Council.

Article 20 If goods require packaging for transport, the consignor shall package them in accordance with national packaging standards or industry packaging standards; if no such standards exist, the goods shall be properly packaged to prevent damage during transport due to packaging issues.

Railway transport enterprises shall take effective protective measures for perishable goods and live animals accepted for carriage, in accordance with the regulations of the competent railway department under the State Council and the terms of the contract.

Article 21 After goods, parcels, or baggage arrive at the station, the consignee or passenger shall promptly collect them within the period prescribed by the competent railway department under the State Council and pay any freight or other fees not paid or underpaid by the consignor; if collection is delayed, the consignee or passenger shall pay storage fees as prescribed.

Article 22 If goods remain unclaimed for thirty days from the date the railway transport enterprise issues a notice of arrival, or if the consignee notifies the railway transport enterprise in writing of refusal to take delivery, the railway transport enterprise shall notify the consignor. If the consignor fails to respond within thirty days from receipt of the notice, the railway transport enterprise may sell the goods; after deducting storage and other expenses from the proceeds, any surplus shall be returned to the consignor. If the surplus cannot be returned or the consignor fails to claim it within one hundred eighty days from the date of sale, it shall be turned over to the State treasury.

If parcels remain unclaimed for ninety days from the date the railway transport enterprise issues a notice of arrival, or baggage remains unclaimed for ninety days after arrival at the station, the railway transport enterprise shall make a public announcement. If they remain unclaimed for ninety days after the announcement, they may be sold; after deducting storage and other expenses from the proceeds, any surplus may be claimed by the consignor, consignee, or passenger within one hundred eighty days from the date of sale. If not claimed within that period, the surplus shall be turned over to the State treasury.

Dangerous goods and items subject to transport restrictions shall be handed over to public security organs or relevant departments for handling; they shall not be sold by the railway transport enterprise itself.

For items that are not suitable for long-term storage, the processing period may be shortened in accordance with the regulations of the competent railway department under the State Council.

Article 23 If property losses are caused to a railway transport enterprise due to the fault of a passenger, consignor, or consignee, the passenger, consignor, or consignee shall be liable for compensation.

Article 24 The State encourages dedicated railways to also engage in public passenger and freight transport operations; it promotes the shared use of railway sidings by relevant entities under agreements.

If a dedicated railway engages in public passenger and freight transport operations, it must obtain approval from the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

If a dedicated railway engages in public passenger and freight transport operations, the provisions of this Law concerning railway transport enterprises shall apply.

Article 25 The passenger fare rates and freight and baggage rates for railways shall be subject to government-guided pricing or government-set pricing, while competitive areas shall be subject to market-adjusted pricing. The authority and specific scope for government-guided pricing and government-set pricing shall be based on the pricing catalogs of the central and local governments. The items and standards for incidental charges for railway passenger and freight transport, as well as the parcel rate, shall be independently determined by railway transport enterprises.

Article 26 The passenger fares, freight, parcel, and baggage rates, as well as the items and standards for incidental charges for passenger and freight transport, must be publicly announced; they shall not be implemented without such announcement.

Article 27 The forgery or alteration of passenger and freight transport tickets and documents printed and used by national railways, local railways, and dedicated railways is prohibited.

The scalping of passenger tickets and other railway transport tickets and documents is prohibited.

Article 28 The consignment and carriage of goods, parcels, and baggage must comply with State regulations on prohibited or restricted transport items.

Article 29 Domestic combined passenger and freight transport between railway transport enterprises and highway, aviation, or waterway transport enterprises shall be handled in accordance with relevant State regulations; where no State regulations exist, it shall be handled in accordance with agreements between the relevant parties.

Article 30 The participation of national railways and local railways in international combined transport must be approved by the State Council.

Article 31 Military transport by rail shall be handled in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 32 If a dispute arises over a railway transport contract, the railway transport enterprise and the consignor, consignee, or passenger may resolve it through mediation; if mediation is not desired or fails, they may apply to an arbitration institution designated by the State for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration clause in the contract or a written arbitration agreement reached afterwards.

If one party fails to comply with the arbitration award within the prescribed period, the other party may apply to a people's court for enforcement.

If the parties have not included an arbitration clause in the contract and have not reached a written arbitration agreement afterwards, they may file a lawsuit with a people's court.

## Chapter III Railway Construction

Article 33 Railway development plans shall be formulated based on the needs of national economic and social development and national defense construction, and shall be coordinated with the development plans of other modes of transport.

Article 34 Construction plans for local railways, dedicated railways, and railway sidings must conform to the national railway development plan and obtain the consent of the competent railway department under the State Council or an institution authorized by it.

Article 35 Within urban planning areas, the planning of railway lines, stations, hubs, and other related facilities shall be incorporated into the overall plan of the relevant city.

Land use plans for railway construction shall be incorporated into the overall land use plan. Land needed for future expansion or new railway construction shall be arranged by the people's government at or above the county level in the overall land use plan.

Article 36 Land for railway construction shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Relevant local people's governments shall support railway construction and assist railway transport enterprises in land acquisition for railway construction and in relocation and resettlement work.

Article 37 Land for railway construction that has been granted use rights shall be used for the approved purposes and shall not be diverted to other uses without authorization; no other unit or individual may encroach upon it.

If land for railway construction is encroached upon, the land administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the encroachment and compensation for losses.

Article 38 The standard gauge for railways is 1435 millimeters. Newly built national railways must adopt the standard gauge.

The gauge for narrow-gauge railways is 762 millimeters or 1000 millimeters.

Other technical requirements for the construction and reconstruction of railways shall comply with national standards or industry standards.

Article 39 After a railway is completed, it must be inspected and accepted in accordance with the provisions of the national capital construction procedures before it can be handed over for formal operation.

Article 40 At intersections of railways and roads, priority shall be given to the construction of grade-separated crossings; if grade-separated crossings are not constructed, level crossings or pedestrian crossings may be established in accordance with relevant State regulations. The establishment of level crossings or pedestrian crossings within urban planning areas shall be decided jointly by the railway transport enterprise or the enterprise or other entity that has built a dedicated railway or railway siding, and the urban planning authority.

The removal of existing level crossings or pedestrian crossings shall be decided through consultation between the railway transport enterprise or the enterprise or other entity that has built a dedicated railway or railway siding, and the local people's government.

Article 41 The construction of railway bridges spanning rivers shall comply with State requirements for flood control, navigation, and water flow.

## Chapter IV Railway Safety and Protection

Article 42 Railway transport enterprises must strengthen the management and protection of railways, regularly inspect and maintain railway transport facilities, ensure the integrity of railway transport facilities, and guarantee the safety of passenger and freight transport.

Article 43 Railway public security organs and local public security organs shall, with a division of responsibilities, jointly maintain public order on railways. Public order within stations and on trains shall be maintained by railway public security organs; public order along railway lines shall be jointly maintained by local public security organs and railway public security organs, with local public security organs taking the lead.

Article 44 The competent power department shall ensure the supply of electricity for railway traction and for important loads in railway operation. The scope of important loads in railway operation shall be determined through consultation between the competent railway department under the State Council and the competent power department under the State Council.

Article 45 Sloping land outside the boundaries on both sides of railway lines shall be managed by the local people's government as a priority area for soil and water conservation. Sloping land above railway tunnels shall be managed by the railway transport enterprise in coordination with the local people's government. Sloping land within railway boundaries shall be managed by the railway transport enterprise.

Article 46 If, within a certain distance on both sides of railway lines, railway bridges, or culverts, the construction of ponds, reservoirs, or dams, the excavation of river channels or main canals, quarrying, sand digging, or well drilling affects the stability of railway subgrades or endangers the safety of railway bridges or culverts, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of such construction, quarrying, sand digging, or well drilling activities, and order the restoration of the original state within a specified period or the adoption of necessary safety protection measures.

The installation of power or communication lines, the laying of cables or pipelines, or the excavation of underground passages through railway subgrades on railway lines must be approved by the railway transport enterprise, and safety protection measures must be taken.

On the inner side of railway curves, near level crossings, and pedestrian crossings, no buildings or trees that obstruct train visibility shall be constructed or planted. If buildings that obstruct train visibility are constructed, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order their demolition within a specified period. If trees that obstruct train visibility are planted, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the relevant unit or individual to relocate, prune, or cut them down within a specified period.

Units or individuals that violate the provisions of the preceding three paragraphs and cause losses to railway transport enterprises shall compensate for the losses.

Article 47 It is prohibited to lay level crossings or pedestrian crossings on railway lines without authorization.

Level crossings and pedestrian crossings must be equipped with necessary signs and protective facilities as prescribed.

Pedestrians and vehicles must comply with the relevant traffic regulations when passing through railway level crossings and pedestrian crossings.

Article 48 The transport of dangerous goods must be handled in accordance with the regulations of the competent railway department under the State Council. It is prohibited to consign dangerous goods under the name of non-dangerous goods.

It is prohibited for passengers to carry dangerous goods into stations or onto trains. Railway public security personnel and railway employees designated by the competent railway department under the State Council have the right to conduct transport safety inspections of items carried by passengers. Railway employees conducting transport safety inspections shall wear duty badges.

The list of dangerous goods shall be formulated and published by the competent railway department under the State Council.

Article 49 If railway signal devices or other train operation facilities are damaged or moved, or obstacles are placed on railway lines, railway employees have the right to stop such acts and may take the offenders to a public security organ for handling.

Article 50 It is prohibited to stow away on freight trains, cling to moving trains, or throw objects at trains. Railway employees have the right to stop such acts.

Article 51 It is prohibited to walk or sit on railway lines. Railway employees have the right to stop such acts.

Article 52 It is prohibited to graze livestock within twenty meters on both sides of railway lines or within railway protection forests. Railway employees have the right to stop such acts.

Article 53 If people gather to block trains or storm railway traffic control facilities, railway employees have the right to stop them; if they refuse to stop, the on-site public security officer in charge has the right to order them to disperse; if they refuse to disperse, the on-site public security officer in charge has the right, in accordance with relevant State regulations, to decide on necessary measures to forcibly disperse them, and to forcibly remove or detain those who refuse to comply.

Article 54 If railway transport materials are looted, railway employees have the right to stop such acts and may take the offenders to a public security organ for handling; on-site public security officers may detain the offenders.

Article 55 If, on a train, someone creates disturbances, disrupts public order, or endangers the personal safety or property of passengers, railway employees have the right to stop such acts, and railway public security officers may detain the offenders.

Article 56 If an infectious disease requiring quarantine under the law occurs at a station or on a passenger train, the railway health and quarantine institution shall carry out quarantine; upon the request of the railway health and quarantine institution, local health and quarantine institutions shall provide assistance.

Quarantine for freight transport shall be handled in accordance with State regulations.

Article 57 If a railway traffic accident occurs, the railway transport enterprise shall handle it in accordance with the regulations of the State Council and the relevant competent departments under the State Council on accident investigation and handling, and shall promptly restore normal train operations. No unit or individual may obstruct the opening of railway lines or the operation of trains.Article 58 In the event of personal injury caused by railway traffic accidents or other railway operation accidents, the railway transport enterprise shall bear the liability for compensation; if the personal injury is caused by force majeure or due to the victim's own fault, the railway transport enterprise shall not bear the liability for compensation.

Personal injury caused by crossing level crossings or pedestrian crossings in violation of regulations, or by walking, sitting, or lying down on railway tracks, shall be deemed as personal injury due to the victim's own fault.

Article 59 Important bridges and tunnels of the national railway shall be guarded by the Chinese People's Armed Police Force.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 60 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, carries dangerous goods into a station or onto a train, or consigns dangerous goods under a non-dangerous goods name, thereby causing a major accident, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law. If an enterprise, institution, state organ, or social organization commits the crime specified in this paragraph, it shall be fined, and the persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Anyone who carries explosives, detonators, or illegally carries firearms, ammunition, or controlled knives into a station or onto a train shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 61 Anyone who intentionally damages or moves railway traffic signal devices, or places obstacles on railway tracks sufficient to cause a train to overturn, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 62 Anyone who steals parts or components of railway traffic facilities or equipment on railway lines, thereby endangering traffic safety, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 63 Anyone who gathers a crowd to intercept a train or storm a railway traffic control station, and refuses to heed warnings, the ringleaders and key members shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 64 Anyone who gathers a crowd to loot railway transport materials, the ringleaders and key members shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If railway employees collude with others to commit the crime specified in the preceding paragraph, they shall be given a heavier punishment.

Article 65 Anyone who robs passengers of their property or injures passengers on a train shall be given a heavier punishment in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Anyone who engages in affrays, creates disturbances, or insults women on a train under flagrant circumstances shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; anyone who extorts or blackmails passengers for property shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 66 Anyone who scalps passenger tickets, constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law. If railway employees scalp passenger tickets or collude with others to scalp passenger tickets, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 67 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, but whose actions are not yet sufficient for criminal punishment and should be subject to public security penalties, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 68 Anyone who, without authorization, lays level crossings or pedestrian crossings on railway lines shall be ordered by the railway public security organs or local public security organs to dismantle them within a specified time limit, and may also be fined.

Article 69 If a railway transport enterprise, in violation of the provisions of this Law, overcharges freight, ticket fees, or miscellaneous charges for passenger or cargo transport, it must refund the overcharged fees to the payer; if refund is not possible, the overcharged fees shall be turned over to the state treasury. Anyone who appropriates or embezzles the overcharged fees for personal gain shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 70 If a railway employee engages in smuggling by taking advantage of their position, or colludes with others to engage in smuggling, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 71 If a railway employee, due to dereliction of duty or violation of rules and regulations, causes a railway operation accident, or abuses their power or seeks personal gain by taking advantage of their position in handling transport business, they shall be given administrative sanctions; if the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 72 The term "national railway transport enterprises" as used in this Law refers to railway bureaus and railway sub-bureaus.

Article 73 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 74 This Law shall come into effect as of May 1, 1991.
