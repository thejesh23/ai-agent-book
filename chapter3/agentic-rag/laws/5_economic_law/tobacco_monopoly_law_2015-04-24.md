# Tobacco Monopoly Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on June 29, 1991

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 28, 2013

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws including the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of implementing tobacco monopoly administration, organizing the production and operation of tobacco monopoly commodities in a planned way, improving the quality of tobacco products, protecting the interests of consumers, and ensuring state fiscal revenue.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "tobacco monopoly commodities" means cigarettes, cigars, cut tobacco, re-dried tobacco leaves, tobacco leaves, cigarette paper, filter rods, cigarette tow, and tobacco-specific machinery.

Cigarettes, cigars, cut tobacco, and re-dried tobacco leaves are collectively referred to as tobacco products.

Article 3 The State shall, in accordance with law, exercise monopoly administration over the production, sale, import and export of tobacco monopoly commodities, and shall implement a tobacco monopoly license system.

Article 4 The administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council shall be in charge of the nationwide tobacco monopoly work. The administrative departments for tobacco monopoly of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be in charge of the tobacco monopoly work within their respective jurisdictions, and shall be under the dual leadership of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council and the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, with the leadership of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council as the main one.

Article 5 The State shall strengthen scientific research and technological development of tobacco monopoly commodities, improve the quality of tobacco products, and reduce the content of tar and other harmful components.

The State and society shall strengthen publicity and education on the health hazards of smoking, prohibit or restrict smoking on public transport and in public places, discourage young people from smoking, and prohibit primary and secondary school students from smoking.

Article 6 Where the State implements tobacco monopoly administration in national autonomous areas, it shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and the Law on Regional National Autonomy, take care of the interests of the national autonomous areas and give preferential treatment to the cultivation of tobacco leaves and the production of tobacco products in those areas.

## Chapter II Cultivation, Purchase and Allocation of Tobacco Leaves

Article 7 For the purposes of this Law, "tobacco leaves" means flue-cured tobacco and sun-cured or air-cured tobacco of listed varieties required for the production of tobacco products. The list of sun-cured or air-cured tobacco of listed varieties shall be prescribed by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council.

Sun-cured or air-cured tobacco not included in the list of listed varieties may be sold at market trade fairs.

Article 8 In tobacco cultivation, fine varieties shall be cultivated and popularized in light of local conditions. Fine varieties shall be supplied by local tobacco companies.

Article 9 Plans for the purchase of tobacco leaves shall be issued by the planning departments of the people's governments at or above the county level in accordance with the plans issued by the planning department under the State Council, and no other unit or individual may alter them.

Tobacco companies or their entrusted units shall sign tobacco leaf purchase contracts with tobacco growers. The tobacco leaf purchase contracts shall stipulate the area for tobacco leaf cultivation and the purchase price of tobacco leaves.

Article 10 Tobacco leaves shall be purchased in a unified manner by tobacco companies or their entrusted units in accordance with the purchase standards prescribed by the State, and no other unit or individual may purchase them.

Tobacco companies and their entrusted units shall purchase all tobacco leaves produced by tobacco growers on the cultivation area stipulated in the tobacco leaf purchase contract, at the purchase price stipulated in the contract, and shall not depress the grade or price, and shall properly handle disputes arising from the purchase of tobacco leaves.

Article 11 Plans for the allocation of tobacco leaves and re-dried tobacco leaves between provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be issued by the planning department under the State Council; plans for the allocation of tobacco leaves and re-dried tobacco leaves within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be issued by the planning department of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and no other unit or individual may alter them.

Contracts must be signed for the allocation of tobacco leaves and re-dried tobacco leaves.

## Chapter III Production of Tobacco Products

Article 12 The establishment of an enterprise for the production of tobacco products must be approved by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council, and a tobacco monopoly production enterprise license must be obtained, and registration must be verified by the administrative department for industry and commerce; its division, merger, or dissolution must be approved by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council, and procedures for modification or cancellation of registration must be completed with the administrative department for industry and commerce. Without a tobacco monopoly production enterprise license, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall not verify registration.

Article 13 An enterprise for the production of tobacco products that undertakes capital construction or technological transformation to expand production capacity must obtain approval from the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council.

Article 14 The annual total output plan for cigarettes and cigars of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be issued by the planning department under the State Council. The annual total output plan for cigarettes and cigars of an enterprise for the production of tobacco products shall be issued by the provincial administrative department for tobacco monopoly in accordance with the plan issued by the planning department under the State Council and in light of market sales conditions; the local people's government shall not assign over-quota production tasks to the enterprise for the production of tobacco products. If an enterprise for the production of tobacco products needs to produce cigarettes or cigars in excess of the annual total output plan due to market sales conditions, it must obtain approval from the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council.

The China National Tobacco Corporation shall, in accordance with the annual total output plan issued by the planning department under the State Council, issue cigarette output targets by grade and type to provincial tobacco companies. Provincial tobacco companies shall, in accordance with the cigarette output targets by grade and type issued by the China National Tobacco Corporation and in light of market sales conditions, issue cigarette output targets by grade and type to enterprises for the production of tobacco products. An enterprise for the production of tobacco products may, in light of market sales conditions, appropriately adjust the cigarette output targets by grade and type within the scope of its annual total output plan.

## Chapter IV Sale and Transport of Tobacco Products

Article 15 An enterprise engaged in the wholesale business of tobacco products must be approved by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council or the provincial administrative department for tobacco monopoly, obtain a tobacco monopoly wholesale enterprise license, and undergo registration verification by the administrative department for industry and commerce.

Article 16 An enterprise or individual engaged in the retail business of tobacco products shall be examined and approved by the administrative department for industry and commerce of the people's government at the county level, upon the entrustment of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly at the next higher level, and shall be issued a tobacco monopoly retail license. In places where a county-level administrative department for tobacco monopoly has been established, the county-level administrative department for tobacco monopoly may also examine, approve, and issue a tobacco monopoly retail license.

Article 17 The State shall establish grade standards for the tar content of cigarettes and cigars. Cigarettes and cigars shall have their tar content grade and the warning "Smoking is harmful to health" marked on their packaging.

Article 18 It is prohibited to broadcast or publish advertisements for tobacco products on radio stations, television stations, or in newspapers and periodicals.

Article 19 Cigarettes, cigars, and packaged cut tobacco must apply for trademark registration; without approved registration, they shall not be produced or sold.

It is prohibited to produce or sell tobacco products that counterfeit the registered trademarks of others.

Article 20 Trademark labels for tobacco products must be printed by enterprises designated by the provincial administrative department for industry and commerce; non-designated enterprises shall not print trademark labels for tobacco products.

Article 21 For the consignment or self-transport of tobacco monopoly commodities, a transport permit issued by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or an institution authorized by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly must be held; without a transport permit, the carrier shall not carry them.

Article 22 The quantity of tobacco leaves or tobacco products sent by mail or carried from one place to another shall not exceed the limits prescribed by the relevant competent department under the State Council.

Article 23 The quantity of tobacco products carried by an individual entering the territory of China shall not exceed the limits prescribed by the relevant competent department under the State Council.

## Chapter V Production and Sale of Cigarette Paper, Filter Rods, Cigarette Tow, and Tobacco-Specific Machinery

Article 24 An enterprise producing cigarette paper, filter rods, cigarette tow, or tobacco-specific machinery must report to the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council for approval and obtain a tobacco monopoly production enterprise license.

For the purposes of this Law, "tobacco-specific machinery" means the complete set of tobacco-specific machinery.

Article 25 An enterprise producing cigarette paper, filter rods, cigarette tow, or tobacco-specific machinery shall organize production in accordance with the plans of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council and the order contracts signed with enterprises for the production of tobacco products.

Article 26 An enterprise producing cigarette paper, filter rods, cigarette tow, or tobacco-specific machinery may only sell its products to tobacco companies and enterprises for the production of tobacco products that hold tobacco monopoly production enterprise licenses.

## Chapter VI Import and Export Trade and Foreign Economic and Technical Cooperation

Article 27 The administrative department for tobacco monopoly under the State Council shall, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, manage the import and export trade and foreign economic and technical cooperation of the tobacco industry.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 28 Whoever, in violation of the provisions of this Law, purchases tobacco leaves without authorization shall be fined by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly, and the illegally purchased tobacco leaves shall be purchased at seventy percent of the average purchase price of tobacco leaves in the previous year as certified by the provincial administrative department for tobacco monopoly of the place where the seizure was made; if the quantity is huge, the illegally purchased tobacco leaves and illegal gains shall be confiscated.

Article 29 Whoever consigns or self-transports tobacco monopoly commodities without a transport permit or in excess of the quantity specified in the transport permit shall be fined by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly, and the illegally transported tobacco leaves may be purchased at seventy percent of the average purchase price of tobacco leaves in the previous year as certified by the provincial administrative department for tobacco monopoly of the place where the seizure was made, and other illegally transported tobacco monopoly commodities other than tobacco leaves may be purchased at seventy percent of the market wholesale price; if the circumstances are serious, the illegally transported tobacco monopoly commodities and illegal gains shall be confiscated.

If a carrier, knowing that the goods are tobacco monopoly commodities, transports them for a unit or individual without a transport permit, the administrative department for tobacco monopoly shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine.

Whoever carries tobacco leaves or tobacco products from one place to another in excess of the limits prescribed by the State, in a relatively large quantity, shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph.

Article 30 Whoever produces tobacco products without a tobacco monopoly production enterprise license shall be ordered to close down by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated and a fine imposed.

Whoever produces cigarette paper, filter rods, cigarette tow, or tobacco-specific machinery without a tobacco monopoly production enterprise license shall be ordered to stop producing the said products by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine may also be imposed.

Article 31 Whoever engages in the wholesale business of tobacco products without a tobacco monopoly wholesale enterprise license shall be ordered to close down or stop engaging in the wholesale business of tobacco products by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated and a fine imposed.

Article 32 Whoever engages in the retail business of tobacco products without a tobacco monopoly retail license shall be ordered to stop engaging in the retail business of tobacco products by the administrative department for industry and commerce, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated and a fine imposed.

Article 33 Whoever produces or sells cigarettes, cigars, or packaged cut tobacco without a registered trademark shall be ordered to stop production or sale by the administrative department for industry and commerce, and a fine imposed.

Whoever produces or sells tobacco products that counterfeit the registered trademarks of others shall be ordered to stop the infringing act by the administrative department for industry and commerce, compensate for the losses of the infringed party, and a fine may also be imposed; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with law.

Article 34 Whoever, in violation of the provisions of Article 20 of this Law, illegally prints trademark labels for tobacco products shall have the printed trademark labels destroyed by the administrative department for industry and commerce, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated and a fine imposed.

Article 35 Whoever engages in profiteering of tobacco monopoly commodities, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with law; if the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall confiscate the profiteered tobacco monopoly commodities and illegal gains, and a fine may also be imposed.

If a staff member of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or a tobacco company commits the crime mentioned in the preceding paragraph by taking advantage of their position, they shall be given a heavier punishment in accordance with law.

Article 36 Whoever forges, alters, buys, or sells the tobacco monopoly production enterprise license, tobacco monopoly business license, or other licenses or transport permits provided for in this Law shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If a staff member of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or a tobacco company commits the crime mentioned in the preceding paragraph by taking advantage of their position, they shall be given a heavier punishment in accordance with law.

Article 37 Whoever smuggles tobacco monopoly commodities, if the act constitutes the crime of smuggling, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the quantity of smuggled tobacco monopoly commodities is not large and does not constitute the crime of smuggling, the Customs shall confiscate the smuggled goods, articles, and illegal gains, and a fine may also be imposed.

If a staff member of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or a tobacco company commits the crime mentioned in the preceding paragraph by taking advantage of their position, they shall be given a heavier punishment in accordance with law.

Article 38 The administrative department for tobacco monopoly shall have the power to inspect the implementation of this Law. Whoever obstructs a tobacco monopoly inspector from performing their duties according to law by means of violence or threat shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with law; whoever refuses or obstructs a tobacco monopoly inspector from performing their duties according to law without using violence or threat shall be punished by the public security organ in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 39 If a staff member of a people's court or a relevant department handling illegal cases privately divides up confiscated tobacco products, they shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If a staff member of a people's court or a relevant department handling illegal cases purchases confiscated tobacco products, they shall be ordered to return them, and may be given administrative sanctions.

Article 40 If a staff member of the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or a tobacco company abuses their power, engages in malpractice for personal gain, or neglects their duties, they shall be given administrative sanctions; if the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, they shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with law.

Article 41 If a party is dissatisfied with an administrative penalty decision made by the administrative department for tobacco monopoly or the administrative department for industry and commerce, they may, within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the penalty notice, apply for reconsideration to the organ at the next higher level over the organ that made the penalty decision; the party may also, within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the penalty notice, directly file a lawsuit with a people's court.

The reconsideration organ shall make a reconsideration decision within sixty days from the date of receipt of the reconsideration application. If the party is dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, they may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the reconsideration decision; if the reconsideration organ fails to make a reconsideration decision within the time limit, the party may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the expiration of the reconsideration period.

If the party neither applies for reconsideration nor files a lawsuit with a people's court within the time limit, nor complies with the penalty decision, the organ that made the penalty decision may apply to a people's court for compulsory enforcement.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 42 The State Council shall, in accordance with this Law, formulate implementing regulations.

Article 43 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1992. The "Tobacco Monopoly Regulations" promulgated by the State Council on September 23, 1983 shall be simultaneously abolished.
