# Flood Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1997

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and controlling floods, defending against and mitigating flood and waterlogging disasters, protecting the safety of people's lives and property, and ensuring the smooth progress of socialist modernization.

Article 2 Flood control work shall follow the principles of comprehensive planning, overall consideration, prevention first, integrated management, and subordinating local interests to the overall interests.

Article 3 The construction of flood control engineering facilities shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plan.

Flood control funds shall be raised in accordance with the principle of combining government investment with reasonable contributions from beneficiaries.

Article 4 The development, utilization, and protection of water resources shall be subject to the overall arrangements for flood control, and the principle of combining beneficial uses with hazard mitigation shall be implemented.

The regulation of rivers and lakes, as well as the construction of flood control engineering facilities, shall conform to comprehensive basin plans and be integrated with the comprehensive development of basin water resources.

The term "comprehensive plan" as used in this Law refers to a comprehensive plan for the development and utilization of water resources and the prevention and control of water-related hazards.

Article 5 Flood control work shall adopt a system of unified planning and graded implementation based on basins or regions, combining basin management with administrative regional management.

Article 6 All units and individuals have the obligation to protect flood control engineering facilities and to participate in flood prevention and flood fighting in accordance with the law.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen unified leadership over flood control work, organize relevant departments and units, mobilize social forces, rely on scientific and technological progress, carry out planned regulation of rivers and lakes, take measures to strengthen the construction of flood control engineering facilities, and consolidate and enhance flood control capacity.

People's governments at all levels shall organize relevant departments and units, mobilize social forces, and effectively carry out flood prevention, flood fighting, and post-disaster recovery and relief efforts.

People's governments at all levels shall provide support to flood storage and detention areas; after flood storage and detention, compensation or relief shall be provided in accordance with state regulations.

Article 8 The water administrative department of the State Council, under the leadership of the State Council, shall be responsible for the daily organization, coordination, supervision, and guidance of national flood control work. The basin management agencies established by the water administrative department of the State Council for major rivers and lakes designated by the State shall, within their respective jurisdictions, exercise the flood control coordination, supervision, and management responsibilities as prescribed by laws, administrative regulations, and as authorized by the water administrative department of the State Council.

The construction administrative department of the State Council and other relevant departments, under the leadership of the State Council, shall be responsible for related flood control work in accordance with their respective functions and duties.

The water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, under the leadership of the people's governments at the same level, shall be responsible for the daily organization, coordination, supervision, and guidance of flood control work within their respective administrative regions. The construction administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments, under the leadership of the people's governments at the same level, shall be responsible for related flood control work in accordance with their respective functions and duties.

## Chapter II Flood Control Planning

Article 9 A flood control plan refers to the overall arrangement formulated for the prevention and control of flood and waterlogging disasters in a specific basin, river reach, or region, including basin flood control plans for major rivers and lakes designated by the State, flood control plans for other rivers, river reaches, and lakes, and regional flood control plans.

Flood control plans shall be subordinate to the comprehensive plans of the basin or region in which they are located; regional flood control plans shall be subordinate to the basin flood control plan of the basin in which they are located.

Flood control plans serve as the fundamental basis for the regulation of rivers and lakes and the construction of flood control engineering facilities.

Article 10 Flood control plans for major rivers and lakes designated by the State shall be formulated by the water administrative department of the State Council, based on the comprehensive basin plans for those rivers and lakes, in conjunction with relevant departments and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and submitted to the State Council for approval.

Flood control plans for other rivers, river reaches, and lakes, or regional flood control plans, shall be formulated by the water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, based on comprehensive basin plans or comprehensive regional plans respectively, in conjunction with relevant departments and relevant areas, submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval, and reported to the water administrative department of the people's government at the next higher level for recordation. Flood control plans for rivers, river reaches, and lakes crossing provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the relevant basin management agency in conjunction with the water administrative departments and relevant competent departments of the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the rivers, river reaches, and lakes are located, reviewed and commented on by the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government respectively, and then submitted to the water administrative department of the State Council for approval.

Urban flood control plans shall be formulated by the urban people's government, organizing the water administrative department, the construction administrative department, and other relevant departments based on the basin flood control plan and the regional flood control plan of the people's government at the next higher level, and shall be incorporated into the overall urban plan after approval in accordance with the approval procedures prescribed by the State Council.

Any amendment to a flood control plan shall be subject to the approval of the original approving authority.

Article 11 In formulating a flood control plan, the principles of ensuring key areas while taking into account general areas, combining flood prevention with drought relief, and integrating engineering measures with non-engineering measures shall be followed. Full consideration shall be given to the patterns of floods and waterlogging, the relationships between upstream and downstream and between left and right banks, and the requirements of the national economy for flood control. The plan shall be coordinated with the national land plan and the overall plan for land utilization.

A flood control plan shall define the objects of protection, the objectives and tasks of regulation, flood control measures and implementation plans, delineate the boundaries of floodplain areas, flood storage and detention areas, and flood protection areas, and stipulate the principles for the use of flood storage and detention areas.

Article 12 Local people's governments at or above the county level in coastal areas threatened by storm surges shall incorporate the defense against storm surges into their local flood control plans, strengthen the construction of engineering systems for storm surge defense, such as seawalls (sea dikes), tidal barriers, and coastal shelter forests, and supervise the design and construction of buildings and structures to meet the requirements for storm surge defense.

Article 13 In areas where mountain torrents may induce landslides, collapses, and debris flows, as well as other areas prone to mountain torrents, local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize the departments responsible for geological and mineral management, the water administrative departments, and other relevant departments to conduct a comprehensive survey of potential landslides, collapses, and debris flows, designate key prevention and control areas, and adopt prevention and control measures.

The layout of cities, towns, villages, and other settlements, as well as factories, mines, railways, and main highways, shall avoid areas threatened by mountain torrents. For those already built in areas threatened by mountain torrents, defensive measures shall be adopted.

Article 14 Relevant local people's governments in areas prone to waterlogging, such as plains, depressions, polder areas in water networks, valleys, and basins, shall formulate plans for waterlogging elimination and control, organize relevant departments and units to adopt corresponding control measures, improve drainage systems, develop waterlogging-tolerant crop varieties and types, and carry out comprehensive management of floods, waterlogging, droughts, and salinization.

Urban people's governments shall strengthen the construction and management of urban drainage pipe networks and pumping stations.

Article 15 The water administrative department of the State Council shall, in conjunction with relevant departments and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulate plans for the regulation of the estuaries of the Yangtze River, Yellow River, Pearl River, Liaohe River, Huaihe River, and Haihe River.

Land reclamation from the sea in the estuaries mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall conform to the estuary regulation plans.

Article 16 Land within the planned land use for river channel regulation and the planned construction of dikes as determined by a flood control plan may, after verification by the land administrative department and the water administrative department in conjunction with relevant areas and approval by the people's government at or above the county level within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council, be designated as a planned reserved area. If the land within such a planned reserved area involves land for other projects, the relevant land administrative department and water administrative department shall solicit the opinions of the relevant departments during verification.

After the planned reserved area is designated in accordance with the preceding paragraph, it shall be publicly announced.

No industrial or mining engineering facilities unrelated to flood control shall be constructed within the planned reserved area mentioned in the preceding paragraph. Under special circumstances, if a national industrial or mining construction project indeed needs to occupy land within the planned reserved area mentioned in the preceding paragraph, it shall be submitted for approval in accordance with the basic construction procedures prescribed by the State, and the opinions of the relevant water administrative department shall be solicited.

Land within the planned area for expanded or newly constructed artificial flood discharge channels determined by a flood control plan may, after verification by the land administrative department and the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level in conjunction with relevant departments and relevant areas and approval by the people's government at or above the provincial level within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council, be designated as a planned reserved area, and the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 17 The construction of flood control projects and other water projects, hydropower stations, etc., on rivers and lakes shall meet the requirements of flood control plans; reservoirs shall reserve sufficient flood control storage capacity in accordance with the requirements of flood control plans.

If the flood control projects and other water projects, hydropower stations, etc., mentioned in the preceding paragraph have not obtained a planning consent letter signed by the relevant water administrative department confirming compliance with the requirements of the flood control plan, the construction unit shall not commence construction.

## Chapter III Regulation and Protection

Article 18 In preventing and controlling river floods, both storage and discharge measures shall be employed, giving full play to the flood discharge capacity of river channels and the flood regulation and storage functions of reservoirs, depressions, and lakes. River channel protection shall be strengthened, and measures such as regular dredging and cleaning shall be adopted according to local conditions to maintain smooth flood discharge.

In preventing and controlling river floods, the protection and expansion of forest and grass vegetation in the basin shall be ensured, water sources shall be conserved, and comprehensive soil and water conservation management in the basin shall be strengthened.

Article 19 In regulating river channels and constructing projects to control and guide river flow and protect embankments, the relationships between upstream and downstream and between left and right banks shall be taken into account, and the projects shall be implemented in accordance with the planned regulation alignment, without arbitrarily changing the direction of river flow.

The planned regulation alignment for major rivers designated by the State shall be formulated by the basin management agency and submitted to the water administrative department of the State Council for approval.

The planned regulation alignment for other rivers and river reaches shall be formulated by the water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level and submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval. The planned regulation alignment for rivers and river reaches crossing provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and for provincial boundary channels between provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall be formulated by the relevant basin management agency, organizing the water administrative departments of the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the rivers and river reaches are located, reviewed and commented on by the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and then submitted to the water administrative department of the State Council for approval.

Article 20 In regulating river channels and lakes involving navigation channels, the needs of navigation shall be taken into account, and the opinions of the competent transport department shall be sought in advance. In regulating navigation channels, the safety requirements for flood control of rivers and lakes shall be met, and the opinions of the water administrative department shall be sought in advance.

In regulating river channels in rivers used for bamboo and log rafting and in fishery waters, the needs of bamboo and log water transport and fishery development shall be taken into account, and the opinions of the competent forestry and fishery administrative departments shall be sought in advance. The rafting of bamboo and logs in river channels shall not affect flood discharge or the safety of flood control engineering facilities.

Article 21 The management of river channels and lakes shall follow the principle of combining unified management by water systems with graded management, strengthening protection and ensuring unimpeded flow.

The main reaches of major rivers and lakes designated by the State, important reaches and lakes crossing provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, provincial boundary channels and lakes between provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and national (border) boundary channels and lakes shall be managed by the basin management agency and the water administrative departments of the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the rivers and lakes are located, in accordance with the delimitation by the water administrative department of the State Council. Other river channels and lakes shall be managed by the water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level in accordance with the delimitation by the water administrative department of the State Council or an institution authorized by it.

For river channels and lakes with dikes, the management scope includes the waters, sandbars, beaches, flood discharge areas between the dikes on both banks, and the dikes and their protection zones. For river channels and lakes without dikes, the management scope includes the waters, sandbars, beaches, and flood discharge areas between the historical highest flood level or the design flood level.

The management scope of river channels and lakes directly managed by a basin management agency shall be delimited by the basin management agency in conjunction with the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph. The management scope of other river channels and lakes shall be delimited by the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 22 The utilization of land and shorelines within the management scope of river channels and lakes shall meet the requirements for flood discharge and water conveyance.

It is prohibited to construct buildings or structures that obstruct flood discharge within the management scope of river channels and lakes, to dump garbage or waste soil, or to engage in activities that affect the stability of river regimes, endanger the safety of riverbanks and dikes, or otherwise obstruct flood discharge in river channels.

It is prohibited to plant trees or tall crops that obstruct flood discharge in flood discharge river channels.

In river reaches where navigation by vessels may endanger the safety of embankments, the navigation speed shall be limited. Signs indicating the speed limit shall be installed by the competent transport department in consultation with the water administrative department.

Article 23 It is prohibited to reclaim land from lakes by enclosure. Land already reclaimed shall be managed in accordance with the flood control standards prescribed by the State, and the land shall be systematically returned to the lake.

It is prohibited to reclaim land from river channels by enclosure. If reclamation is indeed necessary, a scientific demonstration shall be conducted, and after the water administrative department confirms that it does not obstruct flood discharge or water conveyance, the matter shall be submitted to the people's government at or above the provincial level for approval.

Article 24 For residents living within flood discharge river channels, the local people's government shall organize their relocation in a planned manner.

Article 25 Trees for protecting dikes and banks shall be planted and managed by the river channel and lake management agencies. Trees for protecting dikes and banks shall not be arbitrarily felled. The felling of trees for protecting dikes and banks shall be subject to a felling permit in accordance with the law, and the prescribed replanting tasks shall be completed.

Article 26 For bridges, approach roads, docks, and other river-crossing engineering facilities that seriously obstruct or impound water flow, the relevant water administrative department may, based on flood control standards, submit a request to the people's government at or above the county level to order the construction unit to make improvements or demolish them within a specified period, within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 27 The construction of engineering facilities such as bridges, docks, roads, ferries, pipelines, cables, water intakes, and drainage outlets that cross rivers, pass through rivers, pass through dikes, or are adjacent to rivers shall meet flood control standards, shoreline plans, navigation requirements, and other technical requirements, and shall not endanger dike safety, affect river regime stability, or obstruct smooth flood discharge. If the construction plan for such engineering facilities has not been reviewed and approved by the relevant water administrative department based on the aforementioned flood control requirements, the construction unit shall not commence construction.

If the engineering facilities mentioned in the preceding paragraph need to occupy land within the management scope of river channels or lakes, span the space over river channels or lakes, or pass through riverbeds, the construction unit shall obtain approval from the relevant water administrative department regarding the location and boundaries of the engineering facilities before going through the procedures for commencing construction in accordance with the law. During construction, the work shall be carried out in accordance with the location and boundaries approved by the water administrative department.

Article 28 For engineering facilities constructed within the management scope of river channels and lakes in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the water administrative department has the right to inspect them in accordance with the law. During an inspection by the water administrative department, the inspected party shall truthfully provide relevant information and materials.

When the engineering facilities mentioned in the preceding paragraph undergo completion acceptance, the water administrative department shall participate.

## Chapter IV Management of Flood Control Areas and Flood Control Engineering Facilities

Article 29 A flood control area refers to an area that may be inundated by floodwaters, and is divided into floodplain areas, flood storage and detention areas, and flood protection areas.

A floodplain area refers to an area that may be inundated by floodwaters and is not yet protected by engineering facilities.

A flood storage and detention area refers to low-lying areas and lakes outside the backside of river dikes, including flood diversion inlets, used for the temporary storage of floodwaters.

A flood protection area refers to an area protected by flood control engineering facilities within the flood control standard.

The boundaries of floodplain areas, flood storage and detention areas, and flood protection areas shall be delineated in flood control plans or flood defense plans, and shall be publicly announced after being submitted to the people's government at or above the provincial level for approval within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 30 People's governments at all levels shall implement zonal management of land use within flood control areas in accordance with flood control plans.

Article 31 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over safety construction work in flood control areas, organize relevant departments and units to conduct flood control education for units and residents within flood control areas, popularize flood control knowledge, and raise awareness of flood risks. They shall establish and improve flood control systems and hydrological, meteorological, communication, early warning, and flood and waterlogging disaster monitoring systems in accordance with flood control plans and flood defense plans to enhance flood defense capabilities. They shall organize units and residents within flood control areas to actively participate in flood control work and adopt flood control and flood avoidance measures according to local conditions.

Article 32 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where floodplain areas and flood storage and detention areas are located shall organize relevant areas and departments, in accordance with the requirements of flood control plans, to formulate safety construction plans for floodplain areas and flood storage and detention areas, control population growth in flood storage and detention areas, organize the planned relocation of residents living in frequently used flood storage and detention areas, and adopt other necessary safety protection measures.

Areas and units that directly benefit from flood storage and detention areas shall undertake the obligations of compensation and relief for the flood storage and detention areas as prescribed by the State. The State Council and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish systems for supporting, compensating, and providing relief to flood storage and detention areas.

The State Council and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate measures for the safety construction management of floodplain areas and flood storage and detention areas, as well as measures for supporting, compensating, and providing relief to flood storage and detention areas.

Article 33 For the construction of non-flood control projects within floodplain areas or flood storage and detention areas, an assessment shall be made of the potential impact of floods on the construction project and the potential impact of the construction project on flood control. A flood impact assessment report shall be prepared, and defensive measures shall be proposed. If the flood impact assessment report has not been reviewed and approved by the relevant water administrative department, the construction unit shall not commence construction.

For oil fields, railways, highways, mines, power plants, telecommunications facilities, and pipelines constructed within flood storage and detention areas, the flood impact assessment report shall include a flood control and flood avoidance plan arranged by the construction unit itself. When the construction project is put into production or use, its flood control engineering facilities shall be subject to acceptance inspection by the water administrative department.

Houses built within flood storage and detention areas shall adopt a flat-roof structure.

Article 34 Large and medium-sized cities, important railway and highway trunk lines, and large backbone enterprises shall be designated as key flood control targets to ensure their safety.

Cities, economic development zones, industrial and mining areas, and important national agricultural production bases threatened by floods shall be given priority protection, and necessary flood control engineering facilities shall be constructed.

Urban construction shall not arbitrarily fill or block original river channels, branches, water storage ponds, lakes, depressions, or abolish original flood control dikes. If filling, blocking, or abolition is indeed necessary, it shall be approved by the urban people's government.

Article 35 For flood control engineering facilities owned by the State, the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with state regulations, delineate the management and protection scope before completion acceptance, based on the approved design.

For flood control engineering facilities owned by collectives, the protection scope shall be delineated in accordance with the regulations of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Within the protection scope of flood control engineering facilities, activities such as blasting, well drilling, quarrying, and earth digging that endanger the safety of flood control engineering facilities are prohibited.**Article 36** People's governments at all levels shall organize relevant departments to strengthen regular inspections and supervision of reservoir dams. For dangerous dams that do not meet the design flood standards, seismic fortification requirements, or have serious quality defects, the competent dam department shall organize relevant units to take reinforcement and risk elimination measures, eliminate the danger or rebuild within a specified time limit, and the relevant people's government shall give priority to arranging the necessary funds. For reservoirs that may collapse, emergency rescue and temporary evacuation plans for residents shall be formulated in advance.

People's governments at all levels and relevant competent departments shall strengthen the supervision and management of tailings dams and take measures to prevent dam breaches caused by floods.

**Article 37** No unit or individual may damage, occupy, or destroy flood control projects such as reservoir dams, dikes, sluice gates, revetments, pumping stations, and drainage canal systems, as well as hydrological and communication facilities, and equipment and materials reserved for flood prevention.

## Chapter V: Flood Prevention and Fighting

**Article 38** Flood prevention and fighting work shall implement a system of responsibility of administrative heads of people's governments at all levels, with unified command and division of responsibilities among different levels and departments.

**Article 39** The State Council shall establish a national flood control headquarters responsible for leading and organizing the national flood prevention and fighting work, with its office located in the water administrative department of the State Council.

For major rivers and lakes designated by the state, flood control headquarters may be established, composed of responsible persons from the relevant provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments and the river basin management agencies, to direct flood prevention and fighting work within their jurisdiction, with their offices located in the river basin management agencies.

Local people's governments at or above the county level with flood prevention and fighting tasks shall establish flood control headquarters composed of responsible persons from relevant departments, local military garrisons, and people's armed forces departments. Under the leadership of the superior flood control headquarters and the people's government at the same level, they shall direct flood prevention and fighting work in their respective regions, with their offices located in the water administrative department at the same level; when necessary, upon the decision of the city people's government, the flood control headquarters may also establish an urban district office in the construction administrative department, responsible for the daily flood prevention and fighting work in the urban district under the unified leadership of the flood control headquarters.

**Article 40** Local people's governments at or above the county level with flood prevention and fighting tasks shall, based on the comprehensive river basin plan, the actual condition of flood control projects, and the national flood control standards, formulate flood defense plans (including measures for dealing with extraordinary floods).

The flood defense plans for the Yangtze River, Yellow River, Huaihe River, and Haihe River shall be formulated by the national flood control headquarters and submitted to the State Council for approval; the flood defense plans for other rivers crossing provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be formulated jointly by the relevant river basin management agencies and the relevant provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments, and submitted to the State Council or the relevant department authorized by the State Council for approval. Once the flood defense plans are approved, the relevant local people's governments must implement them.

All flood control headquarters and departments and units undertaking flood prevention and fighting tasks must make preparations for flood prevention and fighting based on the flood defense plans.

**Article 41** The flood control headquarters of provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments shall, based on local flood patterns, specify the start and end dates of the flood season.

When the water regime of rivers or lakes approaches the guaranteed water level or safe discharge, the reservoir water level approaches the design flood level, or a major dangerous situation occurs in flood control project facilities, the relevant flood control headquarters of people's governments at or above the county level may declare the commencement of an emergency flood season.

**Article 42** For obstacles obstructing flood discharge within the scope of river channels and lakes, the flood control headquarters shall order their removal within a specified time limit according to the principle that the party creating the obstacle is responsible for its removal; if they are not removed within the time limit, the flood control headquarters shall organize their forced removal, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the party that created the obstacle.

During the emergency flood season, the national flood control headquarters or its authorized river basin, provincial, autonomous region, or municipal flood control headquarters have the right to make emergency dispositions regarding bridges, approach roads, docks, and other cross-river engineering facilities that seriously obstruct or impede water flow.

**Article 43** During the flood season, relevant departments such as meteorology, hydrology, and oceanography shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, promptly provide relevant flood control headquarters with real-time information on weather, hydrology, and storm surge forecasts; the telecommunications department shall give priority to providing communication services for flood prevention and fighting; relevant departments such as transportation, electric power, and material supply shall give priority to serving flood prevention and fighting.

The Chinese People's Liberation Army, the Chinese People's Armed Police Force, and the militia shall carry out the flood fighting and rescue tasks assigned by the state.

**Article 44** During the flood season, the operation of reservoirs, sluice dams, and other water engineering facilities must be subject to the dispatch, command, and supervision of the relevant flood control headquarters.

During the flood season, reservoirs shall not arbitrarily store water above the flood season limited water level; the use of the flood control storage capacity above the flood season limited water level must be subject to the dispatch, command, and supervision of the flood control headquarters.

During the ice flood season, the discharge volume from upstream reservoirs on rivers with ice flood prevention tasks must obtain the consent of the relevant flood control headquarters and be subject to its supervision.

**Article 45** During the emergency flood season, the flood control headquarters, based on the needs of flood prevention and fighting, has the right to requisition materials, equipment, means of transportation, and manpower within its jurisdiction, and decide to take necessary emergency measures such as borrowing land for earth, cutting down trees, removing obstacles to water flow, and other necessary emergency measures; when necessary, relevant departments such as public security and transportation shall, according to the decisions of the flood control headquarters, implement land and water traffic control in accordance with the law.

Materials, equipment, means of transportation, etc., requisitioned in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be returned promptly after the end of the flood season; if damage is caused or they cannot be returned, appropriate compensation shall be given or other measures shall be taken in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council. For borrowing land for earth and cutting down trees, relevant procedures shall be completed with the relevant departments after the end of the flood season in accordance with the law; the relevant local people's government shall organize the reclamation of the land from which earth was taken and the replanting of the trees that were cut down.

**Article 46** When the water level or discharge of rivers or lakes reaches the national standard for flood diversion and it is necessary to activate a flood storage and detention area, the State Council, the national flood control headquarters, the river basin flood control headquarters, the provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments, and the provincial, autonomous region, and municipal flood control headquarters shall decide to activate the flood storage and detention area in accordance with the activation conditions and approval procedures specified in the legally approved flood defense plan. Once a flood storage and detention area is legally activated, no unit or individual may obstruct or delay its use; in case of obstruction or delay, the relevant local people's government at or above the county level shall enforce its implementation.

**Article 47** After a flood disaster occurs, the relevant people's government shall organize relevant departments and units to carry out disaster relief work, including providing for the livelihood of the affected population, epidemic prevention, supply of relief materials, public security management, reopening of schools, restoration of production, and rebuilding of homes, as well as the repair of various damaged water engineering facilities within its jurisdiction. The repair of flood-damaged flood control project facilities shall be given priority in the annual construction plans of the relevant departments.

The state encourages and supports the development of flood insurance.

## Chapter VI: Safeguard Measures

**Article 48** People's governments at all levels shall take measures to increase the overall level of investment in flood control.

**Article 49** The investment required for the management of rivers and lakes and the construction and maintenance of flood control project facilities shall be borne by the central and local finances in accordance with the principle of unifying administrative authority and financial authority, with responsibilities divided by level. The investment required for the construction and maintenance of urban flood control project facilities shall be borne by the city people's government.

Enterprises and institutions such as oil fields, pipelines, railways, highways, mines, electric power, and telecommunications in areas threatened by floods shall raise their own funds to build necessary self-protection flood control projects.

**Article 50** The central finance shall allocate funds for flood fighting and rescue and the repair of flood-damaged flood control projects on the dikes of major rivers and lakes designated by the state when they suffer from extraordinary flood disasters. Provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments shall allocate funds in their fiscal budgets for flood fighting and rescue and the repair of flood-damaged flood control projects in areas within their administrative regions that suffer from extraordinary flood disasters.

**Article 51** The state shall establish a water conservancy construction fund for the maintenance and construction of flood control projects and water conservancy projects. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Provincial, autonomous region, and municipal people's governments threatened by floods may, in order to strengthen the construction of flood control project facilities within their administrative regions and improve their flood defense capabilities, stipulate in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council the collection of fees for the construction, maintenance, and management of river channel projects within the flood control protection zones.

**Article 52** No unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate funds and materials for flood control and disaster relief.

Audit institutions of people's governments at all levels shall strengthen audit supervision over the use of funds for flood control and disaster relief.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

**Article 53** Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 17 of this Law by constructing flood control projects or other water projects or hydropower stations on rivers or lakes without the planning consent document signed by the water administrative department shall be ordered to cease the illegal act and go through the procedures for obtaining the planning consent document; if the construction seriously affects flood control in violation of the requirements of the planning consent document, the violator shall be ordered to demolish the construction within a specified time limit; if the construction affects flood control but remedial measures can still be taken, the violator shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified time limit and may be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 54** Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 19 of this Law by failing to regulate river channels according to the planned training line or constructing projects to control and guide river flow or protect embankments, thereby affecting flood control, shall be ordered to cease the illegal act, restore the original state, or take other remedial measures, and may be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 55** Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 or 3 of Article 22 of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered to cease the illegal act, remove the obstruction, or take other remedial measures, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) Constructing buildings or structures that obstruct flood discharge within the management scope of river channels or lakes;
(2) Dumping garbage or waste residue within the management scope of river channels or lakes, or engaging in activities that affect river regime stability, endanger the safety of riverbanks and dikes, or otherwise obstruct river flood discharge;
(3) Planting trees or tall crops that obstruct flood discharge within flood discharge river channels.

**Article 56** Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 15 or Article 23 of this Law by reclaiming land from the sea, reclaiming land from lakes, or reclaiming river channels shall be ordered to cease the illegal act, restore the original state, or take other remedial measures, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if neither the original state is restored nor remedial measures are taken, the relevant authorities shall restore the original state or take remedial measures on their behalf, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

**Article 57** Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 27 of this Law by engaging in engineering facility construction activities within the management scope of river channels or lakes without the review and approval of the construction plan by the water administrative department, or without complying with the location or boundaries reviewed and approved by the relevant water administrative department, shall be ordered to cease the illegal act and go through the procedures for review and approval or review and consent; if the engineering facility construction seriously affects flood control, the violator shall be ordered to demolish it within a specified time limit; if it is not demolished within the time limit, it shall be forcibly demolished, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the construction unit; if it affects flood discharge but remedial measures can still be taken, the violator shall be ordered to take remedial measures within a specified time limit and may be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 58** Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law by constructing a non-flood control construction project in a floodplain or flood storage and detention area without preparing a flood impact assessment report, or starting construction before the flood impact assessment report is reviewed and approved, shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified time limit; if corrections are not made within the time limit, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Anyone who violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 33 of this Law by putting a construction project into production or use before the flood control project facilities have been accepted shall be ordered to stop production or use and to have the flood control project facilities accepted within a specified time limit, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan.

**Article 59** If, in violation of the provisions of Article 34 of this Law, a city government arbitrarily fills or blocks original river channels, ditches, storage lakes, ponds, depressions, or abolishes original flood control embankments due to urban construction, the city people's government shall order the cessation of the illegal act and the restoration of the original state or the taking of other remedial measures within a specified time limit.

**Article 60** Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, damages, occupies, or destroys flood control projects such as dikes, sluice gates, revetments, pumping stations, and drainage canal systems, as well as hydrological and communication facilities, and equipment and materials reserved for flood prevention, shall be ordered to cease the illegal act, take remedial measures, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if damage is caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if public security penalties should be imposed, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

**Article 61** Anyone who obstructs or threatens the lawful performance of duties by staff members of the flood control headquarters, the water administrative department, or the river basin management agency, if a crime is constituted, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime but a public security penalty should be imposed, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

**Article 62** Anyone who intercepts or misappropriates funds and materials for flood control and disaster relief, if a crime is constituted, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed.

**Article 63** Except for the provisions of Article 59 of this Law, the administrative penalties and administrative measures stipulated in this Chapter shall be decided by the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level, or by the river basin management agency within the scope of authority prescribed by the water administrative department of the State Council. However, the decision-making authority for public security penalties stipulated in Articles 60 and 61 of this Law shall be exercised in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

**Article 64** State functionaries who commit any of the following acts, if a crime is constituted, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed:

(1) Seriously affecting flood control in violation of the provisions of Articles 17, 19, Paragraph 2 of Article 22, Paragraph 3 of Article 22, Article 27, or Article 34 of this Law;
(2) Abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain, resulting in significant losses to flood prevention and fighting work;
(3) Refusing to implement flood defense plans, flood fighting and rescue instructions, or flood storage and detention plans, measures, flood season dispatch operation plans, and other flood control dispatch plans;
(4) Violating the provisions of this Law, causing or aggravating flood damage to neighboring areas or other units.

## Chapter VIII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 65** This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 1998.
