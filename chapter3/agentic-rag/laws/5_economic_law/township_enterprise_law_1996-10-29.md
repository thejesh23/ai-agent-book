# Law of the People's Republic of China on Township Enterprises

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 29, 1996

<!-- INFO END -->

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of supporting and guiding the sustained and healthy development of township enterprises, protecting their lawful rights and interests, regulating their activities, invigorating the rural economy, and promoting the socialist modernization drive.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "township enterprises" refer to various types of enterprises that are invested primarily by rural collective economic organizations or farmers and established in townships (including villages under their jurisdiction) and that undertake the obligation to support agriculture.

"Invested primarily" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph means that the investment by rural collective economic organizations or farmers accounts for more than 50 percent, or although less than 50 percent, it is sufficient to achieve a controlling stake or actual controlling influence.

Township enterprises that meet the conditions for enterprise legal persons shall obtain the status of enterprise legal persons in accordance with the law.

**Article 3** Township enterprises are an important pillar of the rural economy and a significant component of the national economy.

The main tasks of township enterprises are to develop commodity production according to market demand, provide social services, increase effective social supply, absorb surplus rural labor, raise farmers' income, support agriculture, promote agricultural and rural modernization, and advance the development of the national economy and social undertakings.

**Article 4** In developing township enterprises, the principle of taking rural collective economy as the leading force and promoting the common development of multiple economic sectors shall be adhered to.

**Article 5** The State shall actively support, rationally plan, provide classified guidance, and exercise administration according to law with respect to township enterprises.

**Article 6** The State encourages and gives priority support to economically underdeveloped areas and ethnic minority areas in developing township enterprises, and encourages township enterprises or other economic organizations in economically developed areas to adopt various forms to support economically underdeveloped areas and ethnic minority areas in establishing township enterprises.

**Article 7** The administrative department for township enterprises under the State Council and relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, plan, coordinate, supervise, and serve township enterprises nationwide; the administrative departments for township enterprises under local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, plan, coordinate, supervise, and serve township enterprises within their respective administrative regions.

**Article 8** Township enterprises established upon registration according to law shall go through the formalities of registration and filing with the local administrative department for township enterprises.

After a township enterprise changes its name or domicile, or undergoes division, merger, suspension of business, or termination, and after going through the procedures for change registration, establishment registration, or cancellation registration according to law, it shall report to the administrative department for township enterprises for the record.

**Article 9** Branches established by township enterprises in cities, or enterprises established in cities by rural collective economic organizations that undertake the obligation to support agriculture, shall be treated as township enterprises.

**Article 10** For township enterprises invested and established by rural collective economic organizations, the property rights of the enterprise shall belong collectively to all farmers who established the enterprise.

For township enterprises jointly invested and established by rural collective economic organizations with other enterprises, organizations, or individuals, the property rights of the enterprise shall belong to the investors according to their respective investment shares.

For township enterprises invested and established by farmers in partnership or individually, the property rights of the enterprise shall belong to the investors.

**Article 11** Township enterprises shall, in accordance with the law, practice independent accounting, operate autonomously, and assume sole responsibility for their profits and losses.

Township enterprises that have the status of enterprise legal persons shall enjoy the property rights of legal persons according to law.

**Article 12** The State protects the lawful rights and interests of township enterprises; the lawful property of township enterprises shall not be infringed upon.

No organization or individual may, in violation of laws or administrative regulations, interfere with the production and operation of township enterprises, remove their responsible persons; nor may they illegally occupy or use the property of township enterprises without compensation.

**Article 13** Township enterprises shall be established in the form of enterprises prescribed by laws and administrative regulations. Investors shall, in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations, decide on major matters of the enterprise, establish operational management systems, and enjoy rights and assume obligations according to law.

**Article 14** Township enterprises shall practice democratic management according to law. When investors determine the enterprise's operational management system and responsible persons, make major operational decisions, and decide on significant issues such as employee wages, welfare, labor protection, and labor safety, they shall solicit opinions from the enterprise's trade union or employees, regularly publicize the implementation status to employees, and accept employee supervision.

**Article 15** The State encourages regions with the necessary conditions to establish and improve social insurance systems for employees of township enterprises.

**Article 16** When a township enterprise suspends business or terminates, if a social insurance system has been established, employees shall be arranged in accordance with relevant regulations; if labor contracts have been signed according to law, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the contract terms. Employees originally from rural collective economic organizations have the right to return to such organizations to engage in production, or may seek employment on their own.

**Article 17** Township enterprises shall allocate a certain proportion of funds from after-tax profits for supporting agriculture and rural social expenditures. The proportion and the methods for management and use shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations, no organ, organization, or individual may, in any manner, collect fees from or impose apportionments on township enterprises.

**Article 18** The State shall, based on the development situation of township enterprises, reduce a certain proportion of taxes for township enterprises within a certain period. The types of taxes, the period, and the proportion of tax reduction shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 19** The State shall grant tax preferences for a certain period to small and medium-sized township enterprises that meet one of the following conditions, depending on the specific circumstances:

(1) Collectively owned township enterprises experiencing genuine operational difficulties in their initial period;
(2) Those established in ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas;
(3) Those engaged in the processing, storage, transportation, and sale of grain, feed, and meat;
(4) Those requiring special support as stipulated by the State's industrial policies.

The specific measures for the tax preferences mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 20** The State uses credit instruments to encourage and support the development of township enterprises. For township enterprises that meet one of the conditions specified in the preceding article and also meet loan conditions, relevant State financial institutions may grant priority loans; for those facing difficulties in production funds but having development prospects, preferential loans may be granted.

The specific measures for the priority loans and preferential loans mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 21** People's governments at or above the county level may, in accordance with relevant State regulations, establish township enterprise development funds. Such funds shall consist of the following:

(1) Revolving funds allocated by the government for the development of township enterprises;
(2) A certain proportion of the annual increase in local taxes paid by township enterprises;
(3) Income generated from the use of the fund;
(4) Funds voluntarily provided by rural collective economic organizations, township enterprises, farmers, etc.

**Article 22** Township enterprise development funds shall be used exclusively to support the development of township enterprises, with the scope of use as follows:

(1) Supporting the development of township enterprises in ethnic minority areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas;
(2) Supporting economic and technological cooperation and joint venture projects between township enterprises in economically underdeveloped areas and ethnic minority areas and those in economically developed areas;
(3) Supporting township enterprises in adjusting their industrial structure and product structure in accordance with the State's industrial policies;
(4) Supporting township enterprises in technological transformation, developing famous, special, high-quality, and new products, and producing traditional handicraft products;
(5) Developing township enterprises that produce agricultural means of production or directly serve agricultural production;
(6) Developing township enterprises engaged in the processing, storage, transportation, and sale of grain, feed, and meat;
(7) Supporting vocational education and technical training for employees of township enterprises;
(8) Other projects requiring support.

The measures for the establishment, use, and management of township enterprise development funds shall be prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 23** The State actively cultivates talent for township enterprises and encourages scientific and technical personnel, management personnel, and graduates from universities and specialized secondary schools to work in township enterprises, serving them through various means.

Township enterprises shall train technical personnel, management personnel, and production personnel through multiple channels and forms, and adopt preferential measures to attract talent.

**Article 24** The State adopts preferential measures to encourage township enterprises to engage in various forms of economic and technological cooperation with scientific research institutions, institutions of higher learning, State-owned enterprises, and other enterprises and organizations.

**Article 25** The State encourages township enterprises to engage in foreign economic and technological cooperation and exchanges, establish export commodity production bases, and increase foreign exchange earnings through exports.

Township enterprises that meet the conditions may, upon approval according to law, obtain the right to engage in foreign trade.

**Article 26** Local people's governments at various levels shall, in accordance with the principles of unified planning and rational layout, combine the development of township enterprises with the construction of small towns, guide and promote the appropriate concentration of township enterprises, and gradually strengthen infrastructure and service facility construction to accelerate the construction of small towns.

**Article 27** Township enterprises shall, in accordance with market demand and the State's industrial policies, rationally adjust their industrial structure and product structure, strengthen technological transformation, continuously adopt advanced technology, production processes, and equipment, and improve their operational management level.

**Article 28** When establishing township enterprises, the land used for construction shall comply with the overall plan for land utilization, strictly control, rationally utilize, and economize on land use. Where wasteland or inferior land can be used, cultivated land or good land shall not be occupied.

When establishing township enterprises using rural collectively owned land, the procedures for land use approval and land registration shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.

If rural collectively owned land used by a township enterprise remains idle for two consecutive years or more, or is idle for one year or more due to the suspension of the enterprise, the original landowner shall recover the right to use the land and arrange for its reuse.

**Article 29** Township enterprises shall rationally develop and utilize natural resources according to law.

Township enterprises engaged in the exploitation of mineral resources must, in accordance with relevant legal provisions, obtain approval from relevant departments, acquire mining permits and production permits, conduct standardized operations, prevent resource waste, and strictly prohibit the destruction of resources.

**Article 30** Township enterprises shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, establish financial accounting systems, strengthen financial management, set up accounting books according to law, and truthfully record financial activities.

**Article 31** Township enterprises must, in accordance with the State's statistical system, truthfully submit statistical data. For statistical survey forms formulated in violation of State regulations, township enterprises have the right to refuse to fill them in.

**Article 32** Township enterprises shall handle tax registration according to law, file tax returns on time, and pay taxes in full.

People's governments at various levels shall strengthen the tax administration of township enterprises according to law; relevant administrative departments shall not grant tax reductions or exemptions to township enterprises beyond their administrative authority.

**Article 33** Township enterprises shall strengthen product quality management and strive to improve product quality; products produced and sold must meet national and industry standards for safeguarding human health and personal and property safety; they shall not produce or sell expired, deteriorated, or products explicitly eliminated by the State; they shall not adulterate or misrepresent products, passing off fake as genuine or inferior as superior.

**Article 34** Township enterprises shall use trademarks according to law and value corporate reputation; they shall, in accordance with State regulations, produce labels for the goods they manufacture and sell, and shall not forge the place of origin of products, or forge or fraudulently use the names and addresses of other manufacturers, or certification marks and famous-quality marks.

**Article 35** Township enterprises must comply with relevant environmental protection laws and regulations, and in accordance with the State's industrial policies and under the unified guidance of local people's governments, take measures to actively develop enterprises that are pollution-free, low-pollution, and low-resource-consuming, effectively prevent and control environmental pollution and ecological damage, and protect and improve the environment.

Local people's governments shall formulate and implement environmental protection plans for township enterprises to enhance their capacity for pollution prevention and control.

**Article 36** For projects constructed by township enterprises that may have an impact on the environment, the environmental impact assessment system must be strictly implemented.

Facilities for pollution prevention and control in township enterprise construction projects must be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project. Such facilities must pass the acceptance inspection by the competent administrative department for environmental protection before the construction project can be put into production or use.

Township enterprises shall not adopt or use production processes and equipment that are explicitly prohibited by the State due to severe environmental pollution; they shall not produce or operate products explicitly prohibited by the State due to severe environmental pollution. If pollutant emissions exceed national or local standards and cause severe environmental pollution, a deadline for treatment must be set; if the treatment task is not completed within the deadline, the enterprise shall be closed, suspended, or converted to other production in accordance with the law.

**Article 37** Township enterprises must comply with relevant laws and regulations on labor protection and labor safety, conscientiously implement the policy of putting safety first and focusing on prevention, adopt effective labor health technical measures and management measures to prevent the occurrence of production casualties and occupational diseases; for potential accidents endangering employee safety, they shall be resolved within a specified period or production shall be suspended for rectification. Managers are strictly prohibited from giving illegal commands or forcing employees to work under risky conditions. In the event of production casualties, active rescue measures shall be taken, the matter shall be properly handled according to law, and it shall be reported to relevant departments.

**Article 38** If any of the following acts is committed in violation of the provisions of this Law, the administrative department for township enterprises under the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction:

(1) Illegally changing the ownership of a township enterprise;
(2) Illegally occupying or using the property of a township enterprise without compensation;
(3) Illegally removing the responsible person of a township enterprise;
(4) Infringing upon the autonomous operation right of a township enterprise.

If the acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph cause economic losses to a township enterprise, compensation shall be made according to law.

**Article 39** Township enterprises have the right to accuse and report to the audit, supervision, finance, price, and administrative departments for township enterprises against units or individuals that illegally collect fees, impose apportionments, or levy fines on the enterprise. Relevant departments and higher authorities shall order the responsible persons to cease such acts and return the relevant property within a specified period. For the directly responsible persons, relevant departments may impose corresponding penalties depending on the severity of the circumstances.

**Article 40** If a township enterprise violates State laws and regulations on product quality, environmental protection, land management, natural resource development, labor safety, taxation, or other relevant matters, in addition to being dealt with in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, before it corrects the violation, the enjoyment of part or all of the preferences provided for in this Law shall be suspended depending on the severity of the circumstances.

**Article 41** If a township enterprise violates the provisions of this Law by failing to undertake the obligation to support agriculture, the administrative department for township enterprises shall order correction; before it corrects the violation, the enjoyment of part or all of the preferences provided for in this Law may be suspended.

**Article 42** If a party is dissatisfied with the penalty or handling decision made in accordance with Articles 38 to 41 of this Law, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate legal proceedings according to law.

**Article 43** This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1997.
