# Postal Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on December 2, 1986

Revised at the 8th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 24, 2009

First Amendment on October 26, 2012, according to the Decision on Amending the Postal Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 24, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Compulsory Education Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purposes of ensuring universal postal service, strengthening the supervision and administration of the postal market, safeguarding postal communications and information security, protecting the freedom and confidentiality of correspondence, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of users, promoting the healthy development of the postal industry, and meeting the needs of economic and social development and the people's livelihood.

**Article 2** The State guarantees universal postal service within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Postal enterprises shall, in accordance with State regulations, undertake the obligation to provide universal postal service.

The State Council and local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall take measures to support postal enterprises in providing universal postal service.

For the purposes of this Law, "universal postal service" means the postal services continuously provided to all users within the territory of the People's Republic of China at reasonable tariff rates, in accordance with the business scope and service standards prescribed by the State.

**Article 3** The freedom and confidentiality of correspondence of citizens are protected by law. Except for the need of national security or the investigation of criminal offenses, where public security organs, state security organs, or procuratorial organs may inspect correspondence in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law, no organization or individual may infringe upon the freedom and confidentiality of correspondence of citizens on any grounds.

Unless otherwise provided by law, no organization or individual may inspect or detain mail or remittances.

**Article 4** The postal administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of universal postal service and the postal market nationwide.

The postal administrative authorities of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of universal postal service and the postal market within their respective administrative regions.

Postal administrative authorities established below the provincial level in accordance with the regulations of the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of universal postal service and the postal market within their respective jurisdictions.

The postal administrative department under the State Council, the postal administrative authorities of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the postal administrative authorities below the provincial level (hereinafter collectively referred to as "postal administrative authorities") shall, in exercising supervision and administration over the postal market, adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and justice, as well as encouraging competition and promoting development.

**Article 5** The business of delivering letters within the scope prescribed by the State Council shall be exclusively operated by postal enterprises.

**Article 6** Postal enterprises shall strengthen service quality management, improve safety and security measures, and provide users with fast, accurate, safe, and convenient services.

**Article 7** Postal administrative authorities, public security organs, state security organs, and customs shall cooperate with each other, establish and improve safety and security mechanisms, and strengthen the supervision and administration of postal communications and information security to ensure the security of postal communications and information.

## Chapter II Postal Facilities

**Article 8** The layout and construction of postal facilities shall meet the needs of ensuring universal postal service.

Local people's governments at all levels shall incorporate the layout and construction of postal facilities into urban and rural planning, provide support for the construction of postal facilities for universal postal service, and give priority to supporting the construction of postal facilities in remote rural areas.

When constructing new urban areas, independent industrial and mining areas, development zones, residential areas, or renovating old urban areas, supporting postal facilities for universal postal service shall be constructed simultaneously.

The postal network composed of postal facilities for universal postal service is an important national communication infrastructure.

**Article 9** Postal facilities shall be installed in accordance with the standards prescribed by the State.

Larger railway stations, airports, ports, institutions of higher learning, and hotels shall be equipped with postal business premises for universal postal service.

Postal enterprises shall, before establishing or closing postal business premises, inform the postal administrative authorities in writing; the closure of postal business premises for universal postal service shall be subject to the approval of the postal administrative authorities and shall be publicly announced.

**Article 10** State organs, enterprises, and institutions shall install premises for receiving mail. In rural areas, village postal stations or other premises for receiving mail shall be gradually established.

When constructing urban residential buildings, mailboxes for receiving mail shall be installed and inspected in accordance with the standards prescribed by the State. If the construction unit fails to install mailboxes in accordance with the standards prescribed by the State, the postal administrative authorities shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, the postal administrative authorities shall designate another unit to install the mailboxes, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the construction unit of the residential building.

**Article 11** The design and construction of mail processing premises shall meet the requirements for state security organs and customs to perform their duties according to law.

**Article 12** Where postal business premises or mail processing premises are to be expropriated, the competent department of urban and rural planning shall, based on the requirements for ensuring universal postal service, make proper arrangements for the re-establishment of the postal business premises or mail processing premises; no expropriation shall be carried out before such proper arrangements are made.

Before the re-establishment of postal business premises or mail processing premises, postal enterprises shall take measures to ensure the normal operation of universal postal service.

**Article 13** Postal enterprises shall regularly maintain the postal facilities they have installed to ensure their normal use.

No unit or individual may damage postal facilities or affect their normal use.

## Chapter III Postal Services

**Article 14** Postal enterprises shall operate the following businesses:

(1) Mail delivery;
(2) Postal remittance and postal savings;
(3) Issuance of postage stamps and production and sale of philatelic products;
(4) Distribution of domestic newspapers, periodicals, books, and other publications;
(5) Other businesses prescribed by the State.

**Article 15** Postal enterprises shall provide universal postal service for the delivery of letters, printed matters with a single piece weight not exceeding five kilograms, parcels with a single piece weight not exceeding ten kilograms, and postal remittances.

Postal enterprises shall, in accordance with State regulations, handle special services such as confidential communications, the distribution of newspapers and periodicals prescribed by the State, and the free delivery of ordinary letters of compulsory servicemen, reading materials for the blind, and the remains of revolutionary martyrs.

Without the approval of the postal administrative authorities, postal enterprises shall not cease or restrict the handling of the businesses specified in the preceding two paragraphs; if the handling is temporarily ceased or restricted due to force majeure or other special reasons, postal enterprises shall promptly make a public announcement, take corresponding remedial measures, and report to the postal administrative authorities.

The standards for universal postal service shall be formulated by the postal administrative department under the State Council jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council; the specific measures for the supervision and administration of universal postal service shall be formulated by the postal administrative department under the State Council.

**Article 16** The State shall grant subsidies to postal enterprises for providing universal postal service and special services, and shall strengthen the supervision of the use of such subsidy funds.

**Article 17** The State shall establish a universal postal service fund. The specific measures for the collection, use, and supervision and administration of the universal postal service fund shall be formulated by the finance department under the State Council jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval before promulgation and implementation.

**Article 18** The universal postal service business and the competitive business of postal enterprises shall be operated separately.

**Article 19** Postal enterprises shall have business hours of not less than six days per week in cities and deliver mail at least once a day; in towns and townships where the people's governments are located, business hours shall be not less than five days per week, and mail shall be delivered at least five times per week.

The postal administrative department under the State Council may separately prescribe the business hours per week and the frequency of mail delivery for postal enterprises in remote areas with inconvenient transportation and other areas of towns and townships.

**Article 20** When delivering mail, postal enterprises shall comply with the delivery time limits and service standards prescribed by the postal administrative department under the State Council.

**Article 21** Postal enterprises shall display in their business premises or otherwise publish their service types, business hours, tariff standards, methods for inquiring about mail and remittances and for compensation for losses, and the methods for users to complain about their service quality.

**Article 22** Where postal enterprises use standard form contracts provided by them to determine the rights and obligations with users, such standard form contracts shall be subject to the provisions of the Contract Law of the People's Republic of China concerning standard form contracts.

**Article 23** When sending mail, users shall clearly and accurately fill in the name, address, and postal code of the recipient. Postal enterprises shall provide users with free postal code inquiry services at postal business premises.

Postal codes shall be compiled by postal enterprises in accordance with the compilation rules formulated by the postal administrative department under the State Council. The postal administrative authorities shall, according to law, supervise the compilation and use of postal codes.

**Article 24** When accepting mail from users and when users send mail, both postal enterprises and users shall comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the regulations of the State Council and the relevant departments under the State Council concerning articles prohibited or restricted from being sent by post.

**Article 25** Postal enterprises shall, according to law, establish and implement a system for inspecting mail upon acceptance.

For letters sent by users, postal enterprises may, when necessary, require users to open them for inspection, but shall not inspect the contents of the letters. If users refuse to open them, postal enterprises shall not accept them.

For mail other than letters, postal enterprises shall inspect the contents on the spot when accepting them. If users refuse inspection, postal enterprises shall not accept them.

**Article 26** If postal enterprises find articles prohibited or restricted from being sent by post enclosed in mail, they shall handle them in accordance with the relevant State regulations.

If articles prohibited or restricted from being imported or exported by the State are enclosed in inbound or outbound mail, they shall be handled by customs according to law.

**Article 27** For mail handed over for transport by postal enterprises providing universal postal service, railway, highway, waterway, and air transport enterprises shall give priority to transport, and railway stations, ports, and airports shall arrange loading and unloading premises and access passages.

**Article 28** Vehicles and vessels bearing the special postal insignia shall be given priority for passage when entering or leaving ports or crossing ferries.

If vehicles bearing the special postal insignia transporting mail need to pass through road sections where traffic is prohibited by the traffic management department of the public security organ or need to park in places where parking is prohibited, they may, with the consent of the traffic management department of the public security organ and under the premise of ensuring safety, pass or park.

Postal enterprises shall not use vehicles or vessels bearing the special postal insignia for business activities other than mail transport, nor shall they allow other units or individuals to use vehicles or vessels bearing the special postal insignia by means such as leasing.

**Article 29** When mail is transported by sea, it shall not participate in the apportionment of general average.

**Article 30** Customs shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China, supervise and control inbound and outbound international mailbags, mail containers, and international postal items.

**Article 31** The quarantine of inbound and outbound mail shall be carried out by the inbound and outbound inspection and quarantine authorities according to law.

**Article 32** Postal enterprises shall deliver mail by means of delivery to the address, collection by the user, or other methods agreed upon with the user.

State organs, enterprises, institutions, and management units of residential communities shall provide convenience for postal enterprises to deliver mail. If the address of a unit user changes, it shall promptly notify the postal enterprise.

**Article 33** Postal enterprises shall return undeliverable mail to the sender.

For letters that are undeliverable and cannot be returned, if they remain unclaimed for more than six months from the date the postal enterprise confirms that they cannot be returned, the postal enterprise shall destroy them under the supervision of the postal administrative authorities. For other mail that is undeliverable and cannot be returned, it shall be handled in accordance with the regulations of the postal administrative department under the State Council; among such mail, inbound international postal items that are undeliverable and cannot be returned shall be handled by customs in accordance with the provisions of the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 34** The payee of a postal remittance shall, within sixty days from the date of receiving the remittance notice, present a valid identification document to the postal enterprise to collect the remittance.

If the payee fails to collect the remittance within the time limit, the postal enterprise shall return it to the remitter. If the remittance cannot be returned to the remitter within one year from the expiry of the time limit for collecting the remittance, or if the remitter fails to collect the remittance within one year from the date of receiving the notice of return, the postal enterprise shall turn it over to the State treasury.

**Article 35** No unit or individual may privately open, conceal, or destroy mail belonging to others.

Unless otherwise provided by law, postal enterprises and their employees shall not disclose to any unit or individual information about users' use of postal services.

**Article 36** For the need of national security or the investigation of criminal offenses, public security organs, state security organs, or procuratorial organs may, according to law, inspect or detain relevant mail, and may require postal enterprises to provide information about relevant users' use of postal services. Postal enterprises and relevant units shall cooperate and keep the relevant information confidential.

**Article 37** No unit or individual may use mail to deliver articles containing any of the following content:

(1) Inciting the overthrow of the State political power, the subversion of the socialist system, or the splitting of the State, undermining national unity, or endangering national security;
(2) Divulging State secrets;
(3) Spreading rumors to disrupt social order or undermine social stability;
(4) Inciting ethnic hatred or ethnic discrimination, or undermining ethnic unity;
(5) Promoting cults or superstitions;
(6) Disseminating obscene, gambling, or terrorist information, or inciting crime;
(7) Other content prohibited by laws or administrative regulations.

**Article 38** No unit or individual may engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Disrupting the normal order of postal business premises;
(2) Obstructing employees of postal enterprises from delivering mail;
(3) Illegally intercepting, forcibly boarding, or clinging to vehicles bearing the special postal insignia;
(4) Fraudulently using the name of a postal enterprise or the special postal insignia;
(5) Counterfeiting special postal items or reselling counterfeit special postal items.

## Chapter IV Postal Tariffs

**Article 39** The scope of postal services subject to government-guided pricing or government-set pricing shall be based on the central government pricing catalog, and the specific tariff standards shall be formulated by the price department under the State Council jointly with the finance department under the State Council and the postal administrative department under the State Council.

Tariffs for other businesses of postal enterprises shall be subject to market-regulated pricing, and the tariff standards shall be independently determined by the postal enterprises.

**Article 40** When formulating tariff standards for postal services, the relevant departments under the State Council shall solicit opinions from postal enterprises, users, and other relevant parties.

Postal enterprises shall, in accordance with the requirements of the price department under the State Council, the finance department under the State Council, and the postal administrative department under the State Council, provide accurate and complete business cost data and other relevant information.

**Article 41** The payment of postage shall be indicated by postage certificates, stamps proving postage paid, and relevant business documents.

Postage certificates include postage stamps, postage indicia, stamped envelopes, stamped postcards, stamped aerogrammes, and stamped letter cards.

No unit or individual may counterfeit postage certificates or resell counterfeit postage certificates, nor may they without authorization imitate the printing of postage stamps or postage patterns.

**Article 42** The quantity of ordinary postage stamps to be issued shall be determined by postal enterprises according to market demand and reported to the postal administrative department under the State Council for record; the issuance plans for commemorative stamps and special stamps shall be proposed by postal enterprises according to market demand and submitted to the postal administrative department under the State Council for approval. The postal administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for the selection of themes and the examination of designs for commemorative stamps.

The postal administrative authorities shall, according to law, supervise the printing and sale of postage stamps.

**Article 43** After postage certificates are sold, the holder of the postage certificates shall not require the postal enterprise to exchange them for cash.

The discontinuation of the use of postage certificates shall be subject to the approval of the postal administrative department under the State Council, and shall be publicly announced and sales shall cease ninety days before the discontinuation. The holder of the postage certificates may, within one year from the date of the announcement, exchange them for postage certificates of equivalent value at the postal enterprise.

**Article 44** The following postage certificates shall not be used:

(1) Those whose use has been discontinued with the approval of the postal administrative department under the State Council;
(2) Those that have been cancelled or crossed out;
(3) Those that are soiled, damaged, faded, or discolored, making them difficult to identify.

Postage patterns cut from stamped envelopes, stamped postcards, stamped aerogrammes, or stamped letter cards shall not be used as postage certificates.

## Chapter V Compensation for Losses

**Article 45** The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to compensation for losses of mail and remittances within the scope of universal postal service.

Compensation for losses of mail outside the scope of universal postal service shall be subject to the provisions of relevant civil laws.

"Loss of mail" means the loss, damage, or shortage of contents of mail.

**Article 46** Postal enterprises shall not be liable for compensation for losses of ordinary mail. However, this shall not apply if the loss of ordinary mail is caused by the intentional act or gross negligence of the postal enterprise.

**Article 47** Postal enterprises shall compensate for losses of registered mail in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) If registered mail with declared value is lost or completely damaged, compensation shall be made according to the declared value; if it is partially damaged or the contents are short, compensation shall be made for the actual loss of the mail in proportion to the ratio of the declared value to the total value of the mail.
(2) If registered mail without declared value is lost, damaged, or the contents are short, compensation shall be made according to the actual loss, but the maximum compensation amount shall not exceed three times the postage collected; if registered letters are lost or damaged, compensation shall be made at three times the postage collected.

Postal enterprises shall state the provisions of the preceding paragraph in notices at their business premises and on the documents for registered mail provided to users, in a manner sufficient to draw users' attention.

If the loss of registered mail is caused by the intentional act or gross negligence of the postal enterprise, or if the postal enterprise fails to fulfill the obligations specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article to limit its liability for compensation.

**Article 48** Postal enterprises shall not be liable for compensation for losses of registered mail caused by any of the following reasons:

(1) Force majeure, except for losses of registered mail with declared value caused by force majeure;
(2) The natural nature or reasonable wear and tear of the items sent;
(3) The fault of the sender or the recipient.

**Article 49** After sending registered mail, a user may, within one year from the date of sending, present the receipt to the postal enterprise for inquiry regarding domestic mail, and within one hundred and eighty days from the date of sending, present the receipt to the postal enterprise for inquiry regarding international mail.

For inquiries regarding international mail or mail in remote areas designated by the postal administrative department under the State Council, the postal enterprise shall inform the user of the inquiry result within sixty days from the date of the user's inquiry; for inquiries regarding other mail, the postal enterprise shall inform the user of the inquiry result within thirty days from the date of the user's inquiry. If the mail is not found upon the expiry of the inquiry period, the postal enterprise shall compensate in accordance with the provisions of Article 47 of this Law.

If the user fails to make an inquiry or claim compensation within the inquiry period specified in paragraph 1 of this Article, the postal enterprise shall no longer be liable for compensation.

**Article 50** The remitter of a postal remittance may, within one year from the date of remittance, present the receipt to the postal enterprise for inquiry. The postal enterprise shall inform the remitter of the inquiry result within twenty days from the date of the user's inquiry. If the remittance is not found upon the expiry of the inquiry period, the postal enterprise shall return the remittance and the remittance fee to the remitter.## Chapter 6 Express Delivery Services

Article 51 To operate express delivery services, a business license for express delivery services shall be obtained in accordance with this Law; without such a license, no unit or individual may operate express delivery services.

Foreign investors shall not invest in or operate domestic express delivery services for letters.

Domestic express delivery services refer to express delivery services where the entire process from collection to delivery occurs within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 52 To apply for a license for express delivery services, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Meeting the conditions for being a legal person enterprise;

(2) For operations within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the registered capital shall be no less than RMB 500,000; for operations across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the registered capital shall be no less than RMB 1 million; for operating international express delivery services, the registered capital shall be no less than RMB 2 million;

(3) Having service capabilities commensurate with the geographic scope of the applied operation;

(4) Having a strict service quality management system and complete operational procedures;

(5) Having a sound security guarantee system and measures;

(6) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 53 To apply for a license for express delivery services, for operations within a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, an application shall be submitted to the postal administrative department of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; for operations across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, or for operating international express delivery services, an application shall be submitted to the postal administrative department of the State Council. The application shall be accompanied by a written application and relevant materials.

The postal administrative department that accepts the application shall review it within 45 days from the date of acceptance and make a decision to approve or disapprove. If approved, an express delivery service license shall be issued; if disapproved, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons stated.

When reviewing an application for an express delivery service license, the postal administrative department shall consider factors such as national security and solicit opinions from relevant departments.

The applicant may operate express delivery services only after registering with the administrative department for industry and commerce based on the express delivery service license.

Article 54 An enterprise other than a postal enterprise that operates express delivery services (hereinafter referred to as an express delivery enterprise) that establishes a branch, merges, or splits shall file a record with the postal administrative department.

Article 55 Express delivery enterprises shall not operate letter delivery services exclusively operated by postal enterprises, nor shall they deliver official documents of state organs.

Article 56 When an express delivery enterprise operates letter express delivery services outside the scope of business exclusively operated by postal enterprises, it shall mark the word "letter" in a prominent position on the envelope of the letter.

Express delivery enterprises shall not bundle letters together and deliver them as parcels.

Article 57 Operating international express delivery services shall be subject to supervision and inspection legally implemented by the postal administrative department and relevant departments. The postal administrative department and relevant departments may require enterprises operating international express delivery services to provide customs declaration data.

Article 58 If an express delivery enterprise ceases to operate express delivery services, it shall notify the postal administrative department in writing, return the express delivery service license, and properly handle undelivered express items in accordance with the regulations of the postal administrative department of the State Council.

Article 59 The provisions of Articles 6, 21, 22, 24, 25, the first paragraph of Article 26, the second paragraph of Article 35, and Article 36 of this Law concerning postal enterprises and their employees shall apply to express delivery enterprises and their employees; the provisions of Article 11 concerning mail processing sites shall apply to express item processing sites; the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 3, the second paragraph of Article 26, the first paragraph of Article 35, Article 36, and Article 37 concerning mail shall apply to express items; the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 45 concerning compensation for loss of mail shall apply to compensation for loss of express items.

Article 60 Industry associations lawfully established by enterprises operating express delivery services shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and their articles of association, formulate express delivery industry standards, strengthen industry self-discipline, and provide services such as information and training for enterprises, promoting the healthy development of the express delivery industry.

Enterprises operating express delivery services shall strengthen legal education, professional ethics education, and business skills training for their employees.

## Chapter 7 Supervision and Inspection

Article 61 When performing supervision and management duties in accordance with the law, the postal administrative department may take the following supervision and inspection measures:

(1) Entering postal enterprises, express delivery enterprises, or other places suspected of engaging in activities violating this Law to conduct on-site inspections;

(2) Inquiring about the situation from relevant units and individuals;

(3) Reviewing and copying relevant documents, materials, and vouchers;

(4) With the approval of the head of the postal administrative department, sealing up places related to activities violating this Law, seizing means of transport and related items used for activities violating this Law, and opening and inspecting mail and express items suspected of containing prohibited or restricted items for delivery, except for letters.

Article 62 The postal administrative department may, as needed for performing supervision and management duties, require postal enterprises and express delivery enterprises to report on their operational situations.

Article 63 When conducting supervision and inspection, the postal administrative department shall have no fewer than two inspectors, who shall present their law enforcement credentials. Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate with the lawful supervision and inspection conducted by the postal administrative department and shall not refuse or obstruct it.

Article 64 Staff members of the postal administrative department have a duty to keep confidential the trade secrets learned during supervision and inspection.

Article 65 Postal enterprises and express delivery enterprises shall promptly and properly handle user objections regarding service quality. If users are dissatisfied with the handling results, they may file a complaint with the postal administrative department, which shall promptly handle it in accordance with the law and provide a reply within 30 days from the date of receiving the complaint.

Article 66 Any unit or individual has the right to report to the postal administrative department any act violating the provisions of this Law. Upon receiving a report, the postal administrative department shall promptly handle it in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Legal Liability

Article 67 If a postal enterprise provides universal postal services that do not meet the standards for universal postal services, the postal administrative department shall order it to make corrections and may impose a fine of not more than RMB 10,000; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 50,000 shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action.

Article 68 If a postal enterprise, without approval from the postal administrative department, ceases or restricts the provision of universal postal service business and special service business, or revokes a postal business premises providing universal postal services, the postal administrative department shall order it to make corrections and may impose a fine of not more than RMB 20,000; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action.

Article 69 If a postal enterprise uses vehicles or vessels bearing the special postal logo for business activities other than mail transportation, or allows other units or individuals to use vehicles or vessels bearing the special postal logo through leasing or other means, the postal administrative department shall order it to make corrections, confiscate the illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than RMB 20,000; if the circumstances are serious, a concurrent fine of not less than RMB 20,000 but not more than RMB 100,000 shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action.

If an employee of a postal enterprise uses a vehicle or vessel bearing the special postal logo for activities other than mail transportation, the postal enterprise shall order a correction and impose disciplinary action.

Article 70 If an employee of a postal enterprise intentionally delays the delivery of mail, the postal enterprise shall impose disciplinary action.

Article 71 Anyone who fraudulently claims, privately opens, conceals, destroys, or illegally inspects another person's mail or express items, if not constituting a crime, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 72 Anyone who operates express delivery services without obtaining an express delivery service license, or a unit or individual other than a postal enterprise that operates letter delivery services exclusively operated by postal enterprises or delivers official documents of state organs, shall be ordered by the postal administrative department or the administrative department for industry and commerce to make corrections, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than RMB 50,000 but not more than RMB 100,000; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than RMB 100,000 but not more than RMB 200,000 shall be imposed; for an express delivery enterprise, it may also be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its express delivery service license revoked.

Anyone who violates the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 51 of this Law by operating domestic express delivery services for letters shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 73 If an express delivery enterprise commits any of the following acts, the postal administrative department shall order it to make corrections and may impose a fine of not more than RMB 10,000; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 50,000 shall be imposed, and it may also be ordered to suspend operations for rectification:

(1) Establishing a branch, merging, or splitting without filing a record with the postal administrative department;

(2) Failing to mark the word "letter" in a prominent position on the envelope of a letter;

(3) Bundling letters together and delivering them as parcels;

(4) Ceasing to operate express delivery services without notifying the postal administrative department in writing and returning the express delivery service license, or failing to properly handle undelivered express items in accordance with the regulations of the postal administrative department of the State Council.

Article 74 If a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise fails to clearly indicate its service charge standards to users in accordance with regulations, or commits other price-related illegal acts, the government price authority shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the Price Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 75 If a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise fails to establish or implement a system for inspecting items upon receipt, or violates laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the State Council and relevant departments of the State Council on prohibited or restricted items for delivery when collecting mail or express items, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel of the postal enterprise shall be subject to disciplinary action; for an express delivery enterprise, the postal administrative department may order it to suspend operations for rectification or have its express delivery service license revoked.

If a user inserts prohibited or restricted items for delivery into mail or express items, if not constituting a crime, the user shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law.

If the illegal acts specified in the preceding two paragraphs cause personal injury or property damage, the liable party shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

If a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise operating international delivery services, or a user sending international postal items, violates the provisions of the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China or other relevant laws and administrative regulations, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 76 If a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise illegally provides information about users' use of postal services or express delivery services, if not constituting a crime, the postal administrative department shall order it to make corrections, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 50,000; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel of the postal enterprise shall be subject to disciplinary action; for an express delivery enterprise, the postal administrative department may also order it to suspend operations for rectification or have its express delivery service license revoked.

If an employee of a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise commits the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph, if not constituting a crime, the postal administrative department shall order a correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than RMB 5,000 but not more than RMB 10,000.

Article 77 If a postal enterprise or express delivery enterprise refuses or obstructs legally implemented supervision and inspection, if not constituting a crime, it shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law; for an express delivery enterprise, the postal administrative department may also order it to suspend operations for rectification or have its express delivery service license revoked.

Article 78 If a postal enterprise and its employees, or an express delivery enterprise and its employees, engage in acts endangering national security during business operations, they shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the law; for an express delivery enterprise, the postal administrative department shall also revoke its express delivery service license.

Article 79 Anyone who fraudulently uses the name of a postal enterprise or the special postal logo, or forges postal special items or resells forged postal special items, shall be ordered by the postal administrative department to make corrections, confiscate the forged postal special items and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 but not more than RMB 50,000.

Article 80 Anyone who commits any of the following acts, if not constituting a crime, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law:

(1) Stealing, damaging, or affecting the normal use of postal facilities;

(2) Forging postage certificates or reselling forged postage certificates;

(3) Disrupting the normal order of postal business premises or express delivery enterprise business premises;

(4) Illegally intercepting, forcibly boarding, or clinging to vehicles transporting mail or express items.

Article 81 Anyone whose express delivery service license is revoked for violating the provisions of this Law shall not apply to operate express delivery services within three years from the date of revocation.

If an express delivery enterprise's express delivery service license is revoked, it shall, in accordance with the law, undergo change registration or cancellation registration with the administrative department for industry and commerce.

Article 82 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 83 If a staff member of the postal administrative department abuses power, neglects duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain during supervision and management work, if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, disciplinary action shall be taken in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 84 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

Postal enterprise refers to China Post Group Corporation and its wholly-owned enterprises and holding enterprises that provide postal services.

Delivery refers to the activity of delivering items such as letters, parcels, and printed matter to specific individuals or units according to the name and address on the packaging, including the stages of collection, sorting, transportation, and delivery.

Express delivery refers to delivery activities completed quickly within a committed time limit.

Mail refers to letters, parcels, money order notices, newspapers, periodicals, and other printed matter delivered by postal enterprises.

Express items refer to letters, parcels, printed matter, etc., delivered by express delivery enterprises.

Letter refers to correspondence and postcards. Correspondence refers to sealed information carriers delivered to specific individuals or units according to the name and address on the envelope, excluding books, newspapers, periodicals, etc.

Parcel refers to an independently packaged item delivered to a specific individual or unit according to the name and address on the packaging, with a weight not exceeding 50 kilograms, any side dimension not exceeding 150 centimeters, and the sum of length, width, and height not exceeding 300 centimeters.

Ordinary mail refers to mail for which the postal enterprise does not issue a receipt upon collection and does not require the recipient's signature upon delivery.

Registered mail refers to mail for which the postal enterprise issues a receipt to the sender upon collection and requires the recipient's signature upon delivery.

Postal facilities refer to postal business premises, mail processing sites, mailboxes, postal newsstands, letter boxes, etc., used for providing postal services.

Mail processing sites refer to sites specifically used by postal enterprises for activities such as sorting, sealing, storing, exchanging, transshipping, and delivering mail.

International postal items refer to parcels and printed matter sent between users within the territory of the People's Republic of China and users in other countries or regions.

Postal special items refer to postal date stamps, postage meters, postal business documents, postal clamps, mailbags, and other mail-specific containers.

Article 85 International freight forwarding enterprises that, before the promulgation of this Law, were approved or filed with the competent foreign trade department of the State Council in accordance with relevant state regulations, and registered with the administrative department for industry and commerce in accordance with the law to operate international express delivery services, may, based on the approval or filing documents and business license, obtain an express delivery service license from the postal administrative department of the State Council. The postal administrative department of the State Council shall notify the original administrative department for industry and commerce where the enterprise registered of the situation regarding the enterprise obtaining the express delivery service license.

Except for the enterprises specified in the preceding paragraph, enterprises that, before the promulgation of this Law, registered with the administrative department for industry and commerce in accordance with the law to operate express delivery services but do not meet the conditions for operating express delivery services stipulated in this Law shall meet the conditions stipulated in this Law within the time limit set by the postal administrative department of the State Council; if they fail to meet the conditions within the time limit, they shall not continue to operate express delivery services.

Article 86 Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on their actual local conditions, formulate specific measures to support postal enterprises in providing universal postal services.

Article 87 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2009.
