# Law of the People's Republic of China on the People's Bank of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 18, 1995

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the People's Bank of China adopted at the Sixth Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 27, 2003

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to define the status of the People's Bank of China, specify its functions and responsibilities, ensure the correct formulation and implementation of the national monetary policy, establish and improve a central bank macro-control system, and maintain financial stability.

Article 2 The People's Bank of China is the central bank of the People's Republic of China.

Under the leadership of the State Council, the People's Bank of China formulates and implements monetary policy, prevents and mitigates financial risks, and maintains financial stability.

Article 3 The objective of monetary policy is to maintain the stability of the currency's value and thereby promote economic growth.

Article 4 The People's Bank of China performs the following functions:

(1) Issuing orders and regulations related to the performance of its functions;

(2) Formulating and implementing monetary policy in accordance with the law;

(3) Issuing Renminbi and managing its circulation;

(4) Supervising and regulating the interbank lending market and the interbank bond market;

(5) Implementing foreign exchange administration and supervising and regulating the interbank foreign exchange market;

(6) Supervising and regulating the gold market;

(7) Holding, managing, and operating the state foreign exchange reserves and gold reserves;

(8) Acting as fiscal agent for the state treasury;

(9) Maintaining the normal operation of payment and settlement systems;

(10) Guiding and deploying anti-money laundering work in the financial sector and being responsible for monitoring funds related to money laundering;

(11) Being responsible for statistics, surveys, analysis, and forecasts in the financial sector;

(12) Engaging in relevant international financial activities as the central bank of the state;

(13) Other functions as specified by the State Council.

To implement monetary policy, the People's Bank of China may engage in financial business activities in accordance with the relevant provisions of Chapter IV of this Law.

Article 5 Decisions made by the People's Bank of China regarding the annual money supply, interest rates, exchange rates, and other important matters specified by the State Council shall be implemented after being reported to and approved by the State Council.

Decisions made by the People's Bank of China on other monetary policy matters not covered in the preceding paragraph shall be implemented immediately after being made and shall be reported to the State Council for the record.

Article 6 The People's Bank of China shall submit work reports to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on monetary policy and the operation of the financial sector.

Article 7 Under the leadership of the State Council, the People's Bank of China independently implements monetary policy, performs its functions, and conducts its business in accordance with the law, free from interference by local governments, government departments at all levels, social organizations, or individuals.

Article 8 All capital of the People's Bank of China is contributed by the state and belongs to the state.

Article 9 The State Council shall establish a financial supervision and regulation coordination mechanism, with specific measures to be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter II Organizational Structure

Article 10 The People's Bank of China shall have one Governor and a number of Deputy Governors.

The candidate for Governor of the People's Bank of China shall be nominated by the Premier of the State Council and decided by the National People's Congress; when the National People's Congress is not in session, it shall be decided by its Standing Committee, and the Governor shall be appointed or removed by the President of the People's Republic of China. The Deputy Governors of the People's Bank of China shall be appointed or removed by the Premier of the State Council.

Article 11 The People's Bank of China shall implement a system of Governor responsibility. The Governor leads the work of the People's Bank of China, and the Deputy Governors assist the Governor in his work.

Article 12 The People's Bank of China shall establish a Monetary Policy Committee. The functions, composition, and working procedures of the Monetary Policy Committee shall be specified by the State Council and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for the record.

The Monetary Policy Committee of the People's Bank of China shall play an important role in the state's macro-control and the formulation and adjustment of monetary policy.

Article 13 The People's Bank of China shall establish branches as its dispatched institutions based on the needs of performing its functions. The People's Bank of China exercises unified leadership and management over its branches.
The branches of the People's Bank of China, under the authorization of the People's Bank of China, maintain financial stability in their respective jurisdictions and handle relevant business.

Article 14 The Governor, Deputy Governors, and other staff members of the People's Bank of China shall diligently perform their duties, shall not abuse their power or engage in malpractice for personal gain, and shall not hold concurrent positions in any financial institution, enterprise, or foundation.

Article 15 The Governor, Deputy Governors, and other staff members of the People's Bank of China shall keep state secrets in accordance with the law and have the responsibility to keep secrets for financial institutions and parties concerned with the performance of their duties.

## Chapter III Renminbi

Article 16 The legal tender of the People's Republic of China is Renminbi. All public and private debts within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be paid in Renminbi, and no unit or individual may refuse to accept it.

Article 17 The unit of Renminbi is the yuan, and the fractional units of Renminbi are the jiao and fen.

Article 18 Renminbi shall be uniformly printed and issued by the People's Bank of China.

When issuing a new version of Renminbi, the People's Bank of China shall announce the issuance date, denomination, pattern, design, and specifications.

Article 19 It is prohibited to counterfeit or alter Renminbi. It is prohibited to sell or purchase counterfeit or altered Renminbi. It is prohibited to transport, hold, or use counterfeit or altered Renminbi. It is prohibited to intentionally damage Renminbi. It is prohibited to illegally use Renminbi designs on promotional materials, publications, or other goods.

Article 20 No unit or individual may print or sell token vouchers to replace Renminbi in circulation in the market.

Article 21 Damaged or soiled Renminbi shall be exchanged in accordance with the regulations of the People's Bank of China, and the People's Bank of China shall be responsible for recovering and destroying it.

Article 22 The People's Bank of China shall establish a Renminbi issuance vault and set up branch vaults in its branches. The allocation of Renminbi issuance funds by branch vaults shall be carried out in accordance with the allocation orders of the superior vault. No unit or individual may, in violation of regulations, use the issuance funds.

## Chapter IV Business

Article 23 To implement monetary policy, the People's Bank of China may use the following monetary policy tools:

(1) Requiring banking financial institutions to deposit reserves at a prescribed ratio;

(2) Determining the benchmark interest rate of the central bank;

(3) Handling rediscounting for banking financial institutions that have opened accounts with the People's Bank of China;

(4) Providing loans to commercial banks;

(5) Buying and selling government bonds, other government securities, financial bonds, and foreign exchange on the open market;

(6) Other monetary policy tools determined by the State Council.

When using the monetary policy tools listed in the preceding paragraph to implement monetary policy, the People's Bank of China may specify specific conditions and procedures.

Article 24 The People's Bank of China shall act as fiscal agent for the state treasury in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 25 The People's Bank of China may, on behalf of the financial department of the State Council, organize the issuance and redemption of government bonds and other government securities to various financial institutions.

Article 26 The People's Bank of China may, as needed, open accounts for banking financial institutions, but shall not overdraw the accounts of banking financial institutions.

Article 27 The People's Bank of China shall organize or assist in organizing the clearing systems among banking financial institutions, coordinate clearing matters among banking financial institutions, and provide clearing services. Specific measures shall be formulated by the People's Bank of China.

The People's Bank of China, together with the banking regulatory authority of the State Council, shall formulate payment and settlement rules.

Article 28 Based on the needs of implementing monetary policy, the People's Bank of China may decide on the amount, term, interest rate, and method of loans to commercial banks, but the term of such loans shall not exceed one year.

Article 29 The People's Bank of China shall not overdraw the government's fiscal account, and shall not directly subscribe for or underwrite government bonds or other government securities.

Article 30 The People's Bank of China shall not provide loans to local governments, government departments at all levels, non-bank financial institutions, other units, or individuals, except where the State Council decides that the People's Bank of China may provide loans to specific non-bank financial institutions.

The People's Bank of China shall not provide guarantees to any unit or individual.

## Chapter V Financial Supervision and Regulation

Article 31 The People's Bank of China monitors the operation of the financial market in accordance with the law, implements macro-control over the financial market, and promotes its coordinated development.

Article 32 The People's Bank of China has the right to inspect and supervise the following activities of financial institutions and other units and individuals:

(1) Activities related to compliance with regulations on deposit reserve management;

(2) Activities related to special loans of the People's Bank of China;

(3) Activities related to compliance with regulations on Renminbi management;

(4) Activities related to compliance with regulations on the management of the interbank lending market and the interbank bond market;

(5) Activities related to compliance with regulations on foreign exchange administration;

(6) Activities related to compliance with regulations on gold management;

(7) Activities related to acting as fiscal agent for the state treasury on behalf of the People's Bank of China;

(8) Activities related to compliance with regulations on clearing management;

(9) Activities related to compliance with regulations on anti-money laundering.

The term "special loans of the People's Bank of China" in the preceding paragraph refers to loans decided by the State Council to be provided by the People's Bank of China to financial institutions for specific purposes.

Article 33 Based on the needs of implementing monetary policy and maintaining financial stability, the People's Bank of China may recommend that the banking regulatory authority of the State Council inspect and supervise banking financial institutions. The banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall respond within thirty days from the date of receiving the recommendation.

Article 34 When a banking financial institution experiences payment difficulties that may trigger financial risks, the People's Bank of China, with the approval of the State Council, has the right to inspect and supervise the banking financial institution in order to maintain financial stability.

Article 35 Based on the needs of performing its functions, the People's Bank of China has the right to require banking financial institutions to submit necessary balance sheets, income statements, and other financial accounting and statistical reports and materials.

The People's Bank of China shall establish a mechanism for sharing supervisory information with the banking regulatory authority of the State Council and other financial regulatory authorities of the State Council.

Article 36 The People's Bank of China is responsible for uniformly compiling national financial statistics and reports and publishing them in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 37 The People's Bank of China shall establish and improve its own auditing and inspection system and strengthen internal supervision and management.

## Chapter VI Financial Accounting

Article 38 The People's Bank of China implements an independent financial budget management system.

The budget of the People's Bank of China, after being reviewed by the financial department of the State Council, shall be incorporated into the central budget and subject to the budget execution supervision of the financial department of the State Council.

Article 39 The net profit of the People's Bank of China for each fiscal year, after deducting the expenditures for that year and setting aside a general reserve at a ratio verified by the financial department of the State Council, shall be fully turned over to the central finance.

Losses of the People's Bank of China shall be made up by allocations from the central finance.

Article 40 The financial revenues and expenditures and accounting affairs of the People's Bank of China shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and the unified financial and accounting systems of the state, and shall be subject to audits and supervision conducted separately by the auditing authority and the financial department of the State Council in accordance with the law.

Article 41 The People's Bank of China shall, within three months after the end of each fiscal year, prepare a balance sheet, an income statement, and related financial accounting statements, and prepare an annual report, and publish them in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The fiscal year of the People's Bank of China begins on January 1 and ends on December 31 of the Gregorian calendar.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 42 Anyone who counterfeits or alters Renminbi, sells counterfeit or altered Renminbi, or knowingly transports counterfeit or altered Renminbi, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, the public security authority shall impose a detention of up to fifteen days and a fine of up to 10,000 yuan.

Article 43 Anyone who purchases counterfeit or altered Renminbi or knowingly holds or uses counterfeit or altered Renminbi, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, the public security authority shall impose a detention of up to fifteen days and a fine of up to 10,000 yuan.

Article 44 If Renminbi designs are illegally used on promotional materials, publications, or other goods, the People's Bank of China shall order correction, destroy the illegally used Renminbi designs, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of up to 50,000 yuan.

Article 45 If token vouchers are printed or sold to replace Renminbi in circulation in the market, the People's Bank of China shall order the cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of up to 200,000 yuan.

Article 46 For acts listed in Article 32 of this Law that violate relevant regulations, if there are penalty provisions in relevant laws or administrative regulations, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with those provisions; if there are no penalty provisions in relevant laws or administrative regulations, the People's Bank of China shall, depending on the circumstances, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and if the illegal gains are 500,000 yuan or more, impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan; for directors, senior managers, and other persons directly responsible, issue a warning and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 47 If a party is dissatisfied with an administrative penalty, it may file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the provisions of the Administrative Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 48 If the People's Bank of China commits any of the following acts, the persons in charge with direct responsibility and other persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law:

(1) Providing loans in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 30 of this Law;

(2) Providing guarantees to units or individuals;

(3) Unauthorized use of issuance funds.

If any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph causes losses, the persons in charge with direct responsibility and other persons directly responsible shall bear partial or full compensation liability.

Article 49 If local governments, government departments at all levels, social organizations, or individuals compel the People's Bank of China or its staff members to provide loans or guarantees in violation of the provisions of Article 30 of this Law, the persons in charge with direct responsibility and other persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, they shall bear partial or full compensation liability.

Article 50 If a staff member of the People's Bank of China divulges state secrets or known commercial secrets, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 51 If a staff member of the People's Bank of China commits embezzlement, bribery, malpractice for personal gain, abuse of power, or dereliction of duty, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 The term "banking financial institutions" as used in this Law refers to commercial banks, urban credit cooperatives, rural credit cooperatives, and other financial institutions that absorb public deposits, as well as policy banks, established within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Financial asset management companies, trust and investment companies, finance companies, financial leasing companies, and other financial institutions established within the territory of the People's Republic of China with the approval of the banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall be subject to the provisions of this Law applicable to banking financial institutions.

Article 53 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
