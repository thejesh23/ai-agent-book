# Yangtze River Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 26, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to strengthen the protection and restoration of the ecological environment in the Yangtze River basin, promote the rational and efficient use of resources, ensure ecological security, and achieve harmonious coexistence between humans and nature as well as the sustainable development of the Chinese nation.

**Article 2** Activities related to ecological and environmental protection and restoration in the Yangtze River basin, as well as various production, living, development, and construction activities within the basin, shall comply with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, the Yangtze River basin refers to the catchment areas formed by the main stream, tributaries, and lakes of the Yangtze River, covering Qinghai Province, Sichuan Province, Tibet Autonomous Region, Yunnan Province, Chongqing Municipality, Hubei Province, Hunan Province, Jiangxi Province, Anhui Province, Jiangsu Province, Shanghai Municipality, as well as relevant county-level administrative regions in Gansu Province, Shaanxi Province, Henan Province, Guizhou Province, Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, Guangdong Province, Zhejiang Province, and Fujian Province.

**Article 3** Economic and social development in the Yangtze River basin shall adhere to the principles of prioritizing ecological conservation and pursuing green development, jointly promoting comprehensive protection while refraining from large-scale development. Yangtze River protection shall be based on coordinated planning, scientific planning, innovation-driven approaches, and systematic governance.

**Article 4** The State shall establish a coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin to provide unified guidance and overall coordination for Yangtze River protection work, review major policies and plans for Yangtze River protection, coordinate significant cross-regional and cross-departmental matters, and supervise and inspect the implementation of key Yangtze River protection tasks.

**Article 5** Relevant departments of the State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall be responsible for implementing the decisions of the national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin and shall undertake relevant Yangtze River protection work according to their respective responsibilities.

Local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall fulfill their responsibilities for ecological and environmental protection and restoration within their administrative regions, promote the rational and efficient use of resources, optimize industrial structure and layout, and ensure the ecological security of the Yangtze River basin.

River and lake chiefs at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall be responsible for relevant Yangtze River protection work.

**Article 6** Relevant local authorities in the Yangtze River basin shall, as needed, establish coordination mechanisms in areas such as the formulation of local regulations and government rules, planning, supervision, and law enforcement to jointly promote ecological and environmental protection and restoration in the basin.

**Article 7** The competent departments of the State Council for ecological environment, natural resources, water administration, agriculture and rural affairs, and standardization shall, according to their respective responsibilities, establish and improve a standard system for the Yangtze River basin covering water environment quality and pollutant discharge, ecological environment restoration, water resource conservation and intensive use, ecological flow, biodiversity protection, aquaculture, and disaster prevention and mitigation.

**Article 8** The competent department of natural resources under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, regularly organize surveys of natural resources such as land, minerals, water flow, forests, grasslands, and wetlands in the Yangtze River basin, establish a basic resource database, conduct assessments of resource and environmental carrying capacity, and publicly disclose the status of natural resources in the Yangtze River basin.

The competent department of wildlife protection under the State Council shall organize a census of wildlife and their habitats every ten years, or conduct special surveys as needed, establish wildlife resource archives, and publicly disclose the status of wildlife resources in the Yangtze River basin.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, together with other relevant departments of the same level, conduct biodiversity surveys of important habitats such as spawning grounds, feeding grounds, wintering grounds, and migration corridors for aquatic organisms.

**Article 9** The national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate relevant departments of the State Council to improve the monitoring network system and monitoring information sharing mechanism for the ecological environment, resources, hydrology, meteorology, shipping, and natural disasters in the Yangtze River basin, based on existing stations and monitoring projects.

Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall, according to their respective responsibilities, organize the improvement of ecological and environmental risk reporting and early warning mechanisms.

**Article 10** The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, establish and improve an emergency response coordination mechanism for sudden ecological and environmental incidents in the Yangtze River basin, which shall be linked to the national emergency response system, and strengthen emergency management of sudden ecological and environmental incidents involving ships, ports, mines, chemical plants, and tailings ponds in the Yangtze River basin.

**Article 11** The State shall strengthen the construction of systems for monitoring, forecasting, early warning, prevention, emergency response, and recovery and reconstruction for disasters such as floods, droughts, forest and grassland fires, geological disasters, and earthquakes in the Yangtze River basin, to enhance the capacity for disaster prevention, mitigation, resistance, and relief.

**Article 12** The national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin shall establish an expert advisory committee to organize professional institutions and personnel to provide scientific and technical consultation on major development strategies, policies, and plans for the Yangtze River basin.

Relevant departments of the State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall, according to their respective responsibilities, organize third-party assessments, analyses, and demonstrations of the impact of construction projects, important infrastructure, and industrial layout-related plans on the Yangtze River basin ecosystem.

**Article 13** The national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate relevant departments of the State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin to establish and improve an information sharing system for the Yangtze River basin. Relevant departments of the State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall share information on the ecological environment, natural resources, and management and law enforcement in the Yangtze River basin as required.

**Article 14** Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall strengthen publicity and education on ecological and environmental protection and green development in the Yangtze River basin.

News media shall adopt various forms to conduct publicity and education on ecological and environmental protection and green development in the Yangtze River basin and shall, in accordance with the law, exercise public opinion supervision over illegal activities.

**Article 15** Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall take measures to protect historically and culturally famous cities, towns, and villages in the Yangtze River basin, strengthen the protection of cultural heritage in the basin, and inherit and promote the excellent and distinctive culture of the Yangtze River basin.

**Article 16** The State encourages and supports entities and individuals to participate in activities related to ecological and environmental protection and restoration, rational use of resources, and promotion of green development in the Yangtze River basin.

Entities and individuals that make outstanding contributions to Yangtze River protection work shall be commended and rewarded by people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments in accordance with relevant State regulations.

## Chapter II: Planning and Control

**Article 17** The State shall establish a planning system for the Yangtze River basin, with national development plans as the guide, spatial plans as the foundation, and special plans and regional plans as support, to fully leverage the guiding, directing, and constraining role of planning in promoting ecological and environmental protection and green development in the Yangtze River basin.

**Article 18** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall incorporate Yangtze River protection work into their national economic and social development plans.

The development and reform department of the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, formulate a development plan for the Yangtze River basin, scientifically coordinating ecological and environmental protection and green development in the upper, middle, and lower reaches, as well as the left and right banks and main and tributary streams of the Yangtze River, and submit it to the State Council for approval before implementation.

Water resource plans, ecological and environmental protection plans, and other plans for the Yangtze River basin shall be formulated in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 19** The competent department of natural resources under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, organize the formulation of a territorial spatial plan for the Yangtze River basin, scientifically and orderly coordinate the functional spaces for ecology, agriculture, and urban development in the basin, delineate ecological protection red lines, permanent basic farmland, and urban development boundaries, optimize the structure and layout of territorial space, and guide the tasks of territorial space utilization in the Yangtze River basin, and submit it to the State Council for approval before implementation. Special plans involving territorial space utilization in the Yangtze River basin shall be aligned with the territorial spatial plan for the basin.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall organize the formulation of territorial spatial plans for their administrative regions and submit them for approval in accordance with prescribed procedures before implementation.

**Article 20** The State implements use control over territorial space in the Yangtze River basin. The competent departments of natural resources of local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the territorial spatial plan, implement zonal and categorical use control over the territorial space in the Yangtze River basin under their jurisdiction.

Development and utilization activities of territorial space in the Yangtze River basin shall comply with the requirements of territorial space use control and obtain planning permits in accordance with the law. For activities that do not meet the requirements of territorial space use control, the competent departments of natural resources of people's governments at or above the county level shall not issue planning permits.

**Article 21** The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall coordinate the rational allocation, unified dispatch, and efficient use of water resources in the Yangtze River basin, and organize the implementation of a management system for total water withdrawal control and consumption intensity control.

The competent department of ecological environment under the State Council shall, based on water environment quality improvement targets and water pollution prevention requirements, determine the total emission control indicators for key pollutants in each provincial-level administrative region in the Yangtze River basin. For water functional areas in the Yangtze River basin where water quality exceeds standards, stricter pollutant emission reduction requirements shall be implemented. Enterprises and institutions shall take measures to control total pollutant emissions as required.

The competent department of natural resources under the State Council shall be responsible for coordinating the total control and planning arrangement of newly added construction land in the Yangtze River basin.

**Article 22** Provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, based on the ecological environment and resource utilization conditions of their administrative regions, formulate ecological environment zoning control plans and ecological environment access lists, and submit them to the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council for filing before implementation. The ecological environment zoning control plans and ecological environment access lists shall be aligned with the territorial spatial plan.

The industrial structure and layout in the Yangtze River basin shall be compatible with the carrying capacity of the ecosystem and resource environment of the basin. It is prohibited to locate industries that have a serious impact on the ecosystem in key ecological function areas of the Yangtze River basin. It is prohibited to transfer heavily polluting enterprises and projects to the middle and upper reaches of the Yangtze River.

**Article 23** The State strengthens the management of the development and utilization of hydropower resources in the Yangtze River basin. The construction of new large and medium-sized hydropower projects in the Yangtze River basin, required for national development strategies and the national economy and people's livelihood, shall undergo scientific demonstration and be approved by the State Council or a department authorized by the State Council.

For existing small hydropower projects in the Yangtze River basin that do not meet ecological protection requirements, local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize classified rectification or take measures for gradual phase-out.

**Article 24** The State implements strict protection of the sources of the main stream and important tributaries of the Yangtze River, establishes national parks and other protected natural areas, and safeguards the national ecological security barrier.

**Article 25** The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall strengthen the protection of river courses and lakes in the Yangtze River basin. Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall be responsible for delineating the management scope of river courses and lakes, publicly announce it, implement strict river and lake protection, and prohibit illegal encroachment on river and lake waters.

**Article 26** The State implements special control over the shorelines of rivers and lakes in the Yangtze River basin. The national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate the competent departments of the State Council for natural resources, water administration, ecological environment, housing and urban-rural development, agriculture and rural affairs, transportation, forestry and grassland, and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin to delineate the protection scope of river and lake shorelines, formulate protection plans for river and lake shorelines, strictly control shoreline development and construction, and promote the rational and efficient use of shorelines.

It is prohibited to build or expand chemical industrial parks and chemical projects within one kilometer of the shoreline of the main stream and tributaries of the Yangtze River.

It is prohibited to build, renovate, or expand tailings ponds within three kilometers of the shoreline of the main stream of the Yangtze River and within one kilometer of the shoreline of important tributaries; however, renovations aimed at improving safety and ecological environmental protection levels are excluded.

**Article 27** The competent department of transportation under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of the State Council for natural resources, water administration, ecological environment, agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland, scientifically designate no-navigation zones and restricted navigation zones in important habitats of aquatic organisms in the Yangtze River basin.

Ships are prohibited from navigating in designated no-navigation zones. If navigation is required in no-navigation zones within important habitats of aquatic organisms due to national development strategies and the needs of the national economy and people's livelihood, the competent department of transportation under the State Council shall consult with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council for approval, and necessary measures shall be taken to reduce interference with important aquatic organisms.

The implementation of waterway regulation projects in waters within ecological protection red lines, protected natural areas, and important habitats of aquatic organisms in the Yangtze River basin shall be strictly restricted; if regulation is indeed necessary, it shall undergo scientific demonstration and go through relevant procedures in accordance with the law.

**Article 28** The State establishes a planning and licensing system for sand mining in river courses of the Yangtze River basin. Sand mining in river courses of the Yangtze River basin shall obtain a license from the relevant basin management organization of the competent department of water administration under the State Council or the competent department of water administration of a local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the law.

The relevant basin management organization of the competent department of water administration under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the law, designate no-sand-mining zones and no-sand-mining periods, and strictly control sand mining areas, total sand mining volume, and the number of sand mining vessels within sand mining areas. Sand mining activities are prohibited in no-sand-mining zones and during no-sand-mining periods in the Yangtze River basin.

The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, organize relevant local people's governments in the Yangtze River basin and their relevant departments to carry out joint law enforcement against illegal sand mining in river courses of the Yangtze River basin.

## Chapter III: Resource Protection

**Article 29** The protection and utilization of water resources in the Yangtze River basin shall, based on comprehensive basin plans, prioritize meeting the domestic water needs of urban and rural residents, ensure basic ecological water use, and coordinate agricultural and industrial water use as well as navigation needs.

**Article 30** The relevant basin management organization of the competent department of water administration under the State Council shall, in consultation with provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, formulate water allocation plans for inter-provincial rivers in accordance with the law, and submit them to the State Council or a department authorized by the State Council for approval before implementation. The formulation of water allocation plans for inter-provincial rivers in the Yangtze River basin shall seek opinions from relevant departments of the State Council. The competent departments of water administration of provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate water allocation plans for the Yangtze River basin within their administrative regions and submit them to the people's government at the same level for approval before implementation.

The relevant basin management organization of the competent department of water administration under the State Council or the competent department of water administration of a local people's government at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, based on the approved water allocation plan, prepare annual water allocation plans and dispatch schedules, specifying flow, water volume, and water level control requirements for relevant river sections and control sections.

**Article 31** The State strengthens the guarantee of ecological water use in the Yangtze River basin. The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, propose ecological flow control indicators for control sections of the main stream, important tributaries, and important lakes of the Yangtze River. Ecological flow control indicators for other rivers and lakes shall be determined by the competent departments of water administration of local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, together with other relevant departments of the same level.

The relevant basin management organization of the competent department of water administration under the State Council shall incorporate ecological water volume into the annual water dispatch plan to ensure the basic ecological water needs of rivers and lakes, guarantee ecological flow during dry periods and fish spawning seasons, maintain the water volume and level of important lakes, and ensure the saltwater-freshwater balance at the Yangtze River estuary.

Projects such as water conservancy, hydropower, and navigation hubs on the upper reaches of the main stream, important tributaries, and important lakes of the Yangtze River shall incorporate ecological water dispatch into their daily operation dispatch procedures, establish a regular ecological dispatch mechanism, and ensure the ecological flow of rivers and lakes; if the discharged flow does not meet the ecological flow release requirements, the competent department of water administration of the people's government at or above the county level shall propose rectification measures and supervise their implementation.

**Article 32** Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall take measures to accelerate the reinforcement of dangerous reservoirs, promote the construction of embankments and flood storage and detention areas, raise the engineering standards for flood disaster prevention, strengthen the joint dispatch of water projects, conduct river sediment observation and river regime surveys, and establish flood control and disaster reduction engineering and non-engineering systems that are compatible with economic and social development, to enhance the overall capacity for flood and drought disaster prevention.

**Article 33** The State implements scientific demonstration, strengthened control, and management of inter-basin water transfers from the Yangtze River basin. The implementation of inter-basin water transfers from the Yangtze River basin shall prioritize ensuring the water use safety and ecological security of the exporting region and its downstream areas, and coordinate the water use needs of the exporting and importing regions.

**Article 34** The State strengthens the protection of drinking water sources in the Yangtze River basin. The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, formulate a directory of drinking water sources in the Yangtze River basin. The competent departments of water administration of provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, together with other relevant departments of the same level, formulate directories of other drinking water sources within their administrative regions.

Provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall organize the delineation of drinking water source protection zones, strengthen the protection of drinking water sources, and ensure the safety of drinking water.

**Article 35** Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin and their relevant departments shall rationally arrange water intake points for drinking water sources, formulate emergency plans for drinking water safety incidents, strengthen the construction of backup emergency drinking water sources, and conduct real-time monitoring of the water environment quality of drinking water sources.

**Article 36** Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Danjiangkou Reservoir area and its upstream areas shall, in accordance with the management requirements for drinking water source safety guarantee areas, water quality impact control areas, and water source conservation ecological construction areas, strengthen the overall protection of mountains, waters, forests, farmlands, lakes, and grasslands, enhance water conservation capacity, and ensure stable water quality compliance.

**Article 37** The State strengthens the protection of groundwater resources in the Yangtze River basin. Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin and their relevant departments shall regularly investigate and assess the status of groundwater resources, monitor groundwater volume, water level, and water environment quality, and take corresponding risk prevention measures to ensure the safety of groundwater resources.

**Article 38** The competent department of water administration under the State Council shall, together with other relevant departments of the State Council, determine the water use efficiency targets for agriculture and industry in the Yangtze River basin, strengthen the construction of water metering and monitoring facilities; improve the water resource demonstration system for plans and construction projects; strengthen water use quota management for high-water-consumption industries and key water users, and strictly control the construction of high-water-consumption projects.

**Article 39** The State coordinates the construction of a system of protected natural areas in the Yangtze River basin. The State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the law, establish national parks, nature reserves, natural parks, and other protected natural areas in regions such as the complete distribution areas of important and typical ecosystems, ecologically sensitive areas, natural concentration areas of precious wild animals and plants and their important habitats, and areas with important natural relics in the Yangtze River basin.

**Article 40** The State Council and provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the law, designate public welfare forests in important ecological areas and ecologically fragile areas of the Yangtze River basin and implement strict management. The State implements strict protection of natural forests in the Yangtze River basin and scientifically designates key areas for natural forest protection.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall strengthen the protection of grassland resources in the basin and implement strict management of basic grasslands that have special functions such as climate regulation, water conservation, soil retention, and wind and sand fixation.

The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council and the competent departments of forestry and grassland of provincial-level people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, together with other relevant departments of the same level, publish directories and protection scopes of national important wetlands and local important wetlands in the Yangtze River basin based on the needs of different ecological locations, ecosystem functions, and biodiversity protection, strengthen the protection and management of wetlands in the Yangtze River basin, and maintain wetland ecological functions and biodiversity.Article 41 The agricultural and rural department of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council and the provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, shall establish an evaluation system for the aquatic organism integrity index in the Yangtze River basin, organize and conduct evaluations of aquatic organism integrity, and use the results as an important basis for assessing the overall ecological status of the Yangtze River basin. The aquatic organism integrity index for the Yangtze River basin shall be aligned with the water environment quality standards of the Yangtze River basin.

Article 42 The agricultural and rural department of the State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate protection plans for precious and endangered aquatic wild animals and plants in the Yangtze River basin, and provide key protection for such species.

The State encourages qualified entities to conduct research on the habitat characteristics and population dynamics of aquatic wild animals and plants in the Yangtze River basin, including the Yangtze finless porpoise, Chinese river dolphin, Chinese paddlefish, Chinese sturgeon, Yangtze sturgeon, yellowcheek carp, Reeves shad, Sichuan white fish, Sichuan-Tibet hucho, Chinese sucker, Ussuri catfish, round-mouth copper fish, multi-scale white fish, Chinese barb, perch-like carp, and Nostoc sphaeroides, curved algae, pondweed, water cabbage, etc., to build artificial breeding and science education bases, and to organize aquatic organism rescue activities.

It is prohibited to breed or release alien species or other non-native germplasm resources in open waters of the Yangtze River basin.

## Chapter 4 Water Pollution Prevention and Control

Article 43 The ecological environment department of the State Council and the local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall take effective measures to strengthen water pollution prevention, supervision, and control in the Yangtze River basin, and to prevent, control, and reduce water environmental pollution.

Article 44 The ecological environment department of the State Council is responsible for formulating water environment quality standards for the Yangtze River basin. It may supplement provisions for items not specified in the national water environment quality standards; for items already specified in the national standards, it may set stricter requirements. The formulation of water environment quality standards for the Yangtze River basin shall seek opinions from relevant departments of the State Council and relevant provincial people's governments. Provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin may formulate local water environment quality standards that are stricter than those for the Yangtze River basin, and shall report them to the ecological environment department of the State Council for recordation.

Article 45 Provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate local water pollutant discharge standards for characteristic industries, specific pollutants without national discharge standards, or specific water pollution sources or pollutants explicitly required by the State, and shall report them to the ecological environment department of the State Council for recordation.

Under any of the following circumstances, provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate local water pollutant discharge standards stricter than the national standards, and shall report them to the ecological environment department of the State Council for recordation:

(1) Areas with dense industries and prominent water environmental issues;
(2) When existing water pollutant discharge standards cannot meet the water environment quality requirements of the Yangtze River basin under their jurisdiction;
(3) When the water environment situation in the basin or region is complex, making it impossible to apply uniform water pollutant discharge standards.

Article 46 Provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate total phosphorus pollution control plans for their administrative regions and organize their implementation. For the main and tributary rivers of the Yangtze River where phosphate rock and phosphate fertilizer production are concentrated, the relevant provincial people's governments shall establish stricter total phosphorus discharge control requirements to effectively control the total phosphorus discharge amount.

Enterprises engaged in phosphate rock mining and processing, phosphate fertilizer and phosphorus-containing pesticide manufacturing, etc., shall, in accordance with pollutant discharge permit requirements, take effective measures to control the concentration and total amount of total phosphorus discharge; they shall monitor total phosphorus at discharge outlets and the surrounding environment, and publicly disclose monitoring information in accordance with the law.

Article 47 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate the construction of centralized urban and rural sewage treatment facilities and supporting pipeline networks in the Yangtze River basin, ensure their normal operation, and improve the capacity for urban and rural sewage collection and treatment.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall organize investigations and remediation of river and lake discharge outlets within their administrative regions, clarify responsible entities, and implement classified management.

The establishment, modification, or expansion of discharge outlets in rivers and lakes of the Yangtze River basin shall be subject to approval by the ecological environment department with jurisdiction or the Yangtze River basin ecological environment supervision and management institution in accordance with relevant national regulations. For water function areas that have not met water quality targets, except for discharge outlets of centralized sewage treatment facilities, the establishment, modification, or expansion of discharge outlets shall be strictly controlled.

Article 48 The State strengthens the prevention and control of agricultural non-point source pollution in the Yangtze River basin. Agricultural production in the Yangtze River basin shall use agricultural inputs scientifically, reduce the application of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, promote the use of organic fertilizers, and properly dispose of agricultural waste such as agricultural films and crop straw.

Article 49 It is prohibited to dump, landfill, pile, discard, or dispose of solid waste within the management scope of rivers and lakes in the Yangtze River basin. Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall strengthen joint prevention and control of illegal transfer and dumping of solid waste.

Article 50 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall organize investigations and assessments of environmental risks from key groundwater pollution sources, such as landfills along rivers and lakes, gas stations, mines, tailings ponds, hazardous waste disposal sites, chemical industrial parks, and chemical projects, as well as the surrounding groundwater environment, and take corresponding risk prevention and remediation measures.

Article 51 The State establishes a mechanism combining pollution liability insurance and financial guarantees for vessels transporting dangerous goods in the Yangtze River basin. Specific measures shall be formulated by the transportation department of the State Council together with relevant departments of the State Council.

It is prohibited to transport highly toxic chemicals and other dangerous chemicals prohibited from inland waterway transport by national regulations on the waters of the Yangtze River basin. The transportation departments of local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, together with relevant departments of the same level, shall strengthen the control of dangerous chemical transport in the Yangtze River basin.

## Chapter 5 Ecological Environment Restoration

Article 52 The State implements systematic governance of the Yangtze River basin ecosystem based on natural restoration as the primary approach, combining natural restoration with artificial restoration. The natural resources department of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council, shall formulate ecological environment restoration plans for the Yangtze River basin, organize the implementation of major ecological environment restoration projects, and coordinate the advancement of various ecological environment restoration efforts in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 53 The State implements strict fishing management in key waters of the Yangtze River basin. Productive fishing is completely prohibited in aquatic organism protection areas of the Yangtze River basin; within the period specified by the State, productive fishing of natural fishery resources is completely prohibited in key waters such as the main stream and important tributaries of the Yangtze River, large lakes connected to the river, and specified areas of the Yangtze River estuary. Specific measures shall be formulated by the agricultural and rural department of the State Council together with relevant departments of the State Council.

The agricultural and rural department of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council and provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, shall strengthen law enforcement on fishing bans in the Yangtze River basin, and severely crack down on fishing activities that damage fishery resources and the ecological environment, such as electric fishing, poison fishing, and explosive fishing.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with relevant national regulations, provide compensation, job transition, and social security for fishermen who have withdrawn from fishing in key waters of the Yangtze River basin.

Management measures for fishing bans and restrictions in other waters of the Yangtze River basin shall be formulated by local people's governments at or above the county level.

Article 54 The water administrative department of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council, shall formulate and implement plans for the connectivity restoration of river and lake water systems in the main stream and important tributaries of the Yangtze River. Provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate and implement plans for the connectivity restoration of river and lake water systems within their administrative regions, gradually improving the connectivity of rivers and lakes in the Yangtze River basin, restoring ecological flow, and maintaining the ecological functions of river and lake water systems.

Article 55 The national coordination mechanism for the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate with departments of the State Council, including natural resources, water administration, ecological environment, housing and urban-rural development, agriculture and rural affairs, transportation, forestry and grassland, and provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, to formulate specifications for the restoration of river and lake shorelines in the Yangtze River basin and determine restoration indicators.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the protection plans, restoration specifications, and indicator requirements for river and lake shorelines in the Yangtze River basin, formulate and implement shoreline restoration plans, ensure the proportion of natural shorelines, and restore the ecological functions of river and lake shorelines.

It is prohibited to illegally use or occupy river and lake shorelines in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 56 Relevant departments of the State Council, together with relevant provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, shall strengthen ecological environment protection and restoration in the water-level fluctuation zones of key reservoirs such as the Three Gorges Reservoir area and the Danjiangkou Reservoir area, implement measures such as returning farmland to forest, grassland, and wetlands as appropriate to local conditions, prohibit the application of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, scientifically regulate reservoir water levels, strengthen soil and water conservation and geological disaster prevention in the reservoir areas, and ensure the good ecological functions of the water-level fluctuation zones.

Article 57 The forestry and grassland departments of local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin are responsible for organizing the implementation of plans for the restoration of forests, grasslands, and wetlands in the Yangtze River basin, scientifically advancing the restoration of forests, grasslands, and wetlands, and intensifying efforts to restore degraded natural forests, grasslands, and damaged wetlands.

Article 58 The State increases support for ecological environment restoration in key lakes such as Taihu Lake, Poyang Lake, Dongting Lake, Chaohu Lake, and Dianchi Lake.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall organize ecological environment restoration of eutrophic lakes, adopting comprehensive measures such as adjusting industrial layout and scale, implementing unified regulation of control water projects, ecological water replenishment, and river-lake connectivity, to improve and restore the quality and functions of lake ecosystems; for lakes with severely excessive nitrogen and phosphorus concentrations, measures shall be taken in the catchment areas affecting lake water quality to reduce the use of chemical fertilizers, prohibit the use of phosphorus-containing detergents, and completely clean up aquaculture involving bait and fertilizer.

Article 59 The forestry and grassland and agricultural and rural departments of the State Council shall formulate restoration plans and action plans for wild animals and plants in the Yangtze River basin that have sharply declined in number or are critically endangered, as well as severely damaged habitats, natural concentrated distribution areas, and fragmented typical ecosystems, construct ex-situ conservation facilities, establish genetic resource gene banks for wild animals and plants, and carry out rescue restoration.

Ecological environment restoration and other protection measures shall be implemented in important habitats such as spawning grounds, feeding grounds, wintering grounds, and migration channels of aquatic organisms in the Yangtze River basin. For water-related projects that obstruct the migration of fish and other aquatic organisms, multiple measures such as constructing fish passage facilities, river-lake connectivity, ecological regulation, introducing river water for fish recruitment, gene preservation, stock enhancement, and artificial breeding shall be adopted based on actual conditions to fully meet the ecological needs of aquatic organisms.

Article 60 The water administrative department of the State Council, together with relevant departments of the State Council and the local people's government at the Yangtze River estuary, shall, in accordance with the requirements of land-sea coordination and river-sea linkage, formulate and implement plans for ecological environment restoration and other protection measures at the Yangtze River estuary, strengthen comprehensive monitoring of water, sediment, salt, tidal flats, and biological populations, take effective measures to prevent seawater intrusion and backflow, and maintain the good ecological functions of the Yangtze River estuary.

Article 61 Local people's governments at or above the county level in key prevention and control areas for soil erosion in the Yangtze River basin shall take measures to prevent and control soil erosion. For soil erosion plots within the ecological protection red line, natural restoration shall be the primary approach, and measures such as returning farmland to forest, grassland, and wetlands shall be implemented in a planned manner as stipulated; permanent basic farmland designated within the core protection areas of nature reserves shall be legally and orderly withdrawn and reallocated.

It is prohibited to engage in production and construction activities that may cause soil erosion in areas with severe soil erosion and fragile ecology in the Yangtze River basin. If such activities are necessary due to national development strategies and the needs of the national economy and people's livelihoods, they shall undergo scientific demonstration and obtain approval procedures in accordance with the law.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall take comprehensive measures tailored to local conditions to restore ecosystems on rocky desertification land and prevent the spread of rocky desertification.

Article 62 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, based on local conditions, take measures such as eliminating geological hazard risks, land reclamation, vegetation restoration, and pollution prevention to accelerate the ecological environment restoration of historical legacy mines, strengthen supervision of mines under construction and in operation, and urge mining rights holders to fulfill their responsibilities for mine pollution prevention and ecological environment restoration.

Article 63 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River basin shall, based on local conditions, provide support in terms of projects, funds, talent, and management to the river source and upstream areas of the Yangtze River basin for implementing ecological environment restoration and other protection measures, thereby enhancing the capacity of ecologically fragile areas in the Yangtze River basin to implement such measures.

The State encourages social capital to invest in ecological environment restoration in the Yangtze River basin, following the principles of policy support, enterprise and social participation, and market-oriented operation.

## Chapter 6 Green Development

Article 64 Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the requirements of the Yangtze River basin development plan and territorial space planning, adjust industrial structures, optimize industrial layout, and promote green development in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 65 The State Council and local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall coordinate the implementation of the rural revitalization strategy and the new urbanization strategy, coordinate urban and rural infrastructure construction and industrial development, establish and improve a basic public service system that covers all people, is universally beneficial and shared, and integrates urban and rural areas, and promote integrated urban-rural development in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 66 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall promote the upgrading and transformation of industries such as steel, petroleum, chemicals, non-ferrous metals, building materials, and shipbuilding, and improve technical equipment levels; promote clean transformation of enterprises in industries such as papermaking, leather tanning, electroplating, printing and dyeing, non-ferrous metals, pesticides, nitrogen fertilizers, coking, and raw material drug manufacturing. Enterprises shall reduce resource consumption and pollutant emissions through technological innovation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall take measures to accelerate the relocation and transformation of hazardous chemical production enterprises in key areas.

Article 67 Relevant departments of the State Council, together with provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin, shall establish a green development evaluation mechanism for development zones, and organize regular evaluations of resource and energy conservation, intensive utilization, and ecological environment protection in various development zones.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, based on the evaluation results, optimize and adjust the industrial products, energy-saving and emission-reduction measures of the development zones.

Article 68 The State encourages and supports the implementation of water-saving technological transformation in key industries and key water users in the Yangtze River basin to improve water resource utilization efficiency.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall strengthen the construction of water-saving cities and water-saving industrial parks, promote the development of water-saving industries and enterprises, and accelerate the construction of sponge cities that naturally accumulate, infiltrate, and purify rainwater.

Article 69 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the requirements of green development, coordinate planning, construction, and management to improve the quality of urban and rural living environments, and build beautiful towns and villages.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall, following the principles of ecology, environmental protection, economy, and practicality, organize the implementation of toilet renovations based on local conditions.

Relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin, along with their relevant departments, shall strengthen the management of building materials used in new urban areas and various development zones, encourage the use of energy-saving, environmentally friendly, and high-performance building materials, and construct underground comprehensive utility tunnels and pipeline networks.

Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall establish information platforms for the comprehensive utilization of waste earth, stone, and slag, strengthen the management of collection, transportation, and centralized storage of waste earth, stone, and slag from production and construction activities, and encourage comprehensive utilization.

Article 70 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall formulate and implement plans for aquaculture waters and tidal flats, reasonably designate prohibited aquaculture areas, restricted aquaculture areas, and aquaculture areas, scientifically determine aquaculture scale and density; strengthen the management of inputs for aquaculture, and guide and regulate aquaculture and enhancement activities.

Article 71 The State strengthens the construction of a comprehensive three-dimensional transportation system in the Yangtze River basin, improves water transport infrastructure such as ports and waterways, promotes the interconnection of transportation facilities, achieves organic land-water connection and direct sea-river intermodal transport, and enhances the function of the Yangtze River golden waterway.

Article 72 Local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall coordinate the construction of facilities for receiving, transferring, and disposing of ship pollutants, and liquefied natural gas refueling stations for ships, formulate plans for the construction and renovation of shore power facilities at ports and ship power receiving facilities, and organize their implementation. Ships with shore power usage conditions shall use shore power when berthing in accordance with relevant national regulations, except for those using clean energy.

Article 73 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall provide financial support or policy incentives in accordance with regulations for the upgrading and transformation of ports, waterways, and ships in the Yangtze River basin, the construction of clean energy or new energy-powered ships such as liquefied natural gas-powered ships, and green port design.

The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall provide policy support such as financial subsidies and electricity price concessions in accordance with regulations for the renovation and use of shore power facilities at ports and ship power receiving facilities in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 74 Local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall strengthen publicity and education on green consumption for urban and rural residents, and take effective measures to support and guide residents toward green consumption.

Local people's governments at all levels in the Yangtze River basin shall, following the principles of systematic promotion, broad participation, highlighting key points, and implementing classified measures, adopt measures such as deposit refunds, restricting the use of easily polluting and non-degradable plastic products, green design, and developing public transportation, to promote a simple, moderate, green, and low-carbon lifestyle.

## Chapter 7 Guarantees and Supervision

Article 75 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River basin shall increase financial investment in ecological environment protection and restoration in the Yangtze River basin.

The State Council and provincial people's governments in the Yangtze River basin shall, in accordance with the principle of dividing fiscal powers and expenditure responsibilities between the central and local governments, allocate special funds for ecological environment protection in the Yangtze River basin, to be used for ecological environment protection and restoration. The natural resources department of the State Council, together with relevant departments such as the finance and ecological environment departments of the State Council, shall formulate policies and measures to rationally utilize social funds to promote ecological environment restoration in the Yangtze River basin.

The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research, development, and promotion of applications related to ecological environment protection and restoration in the Yangtze River basin.

The State encourages financial institutions to develop financial products such as green credit, green bonds, and green insurance to provide financial support for ecological environment protection and green development in the Yangtze River basin.

Article 76 The State establishes an ecological protection compensation system for the Yangtze River basin.

The State increases financial transfer payments to compensate for ecologically important areas such as the water source conservation areas at the sources of the main stream and important tributaries of the Yangtze River. Specific measures shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council together with relevant departments of the State Council.

The State encourages horizontal ecological protection compensation between local people's governments in the upper and lower reaches, left and right banks, and main and tributary streams of the Yangtze River basin.

The State encourages the establishment of market-oriented ecological protection compensation funds for the Yangtze River basin using social funds; encourages relevant entities to conduct ecological protection compensation through voluntary negotiation and other means.

Article 77 The State strengthens the construction of judicial guarantees for the Yangtze River basin, and encourages relevant entities to provide legal services for ecological environment protection in the Yangtze River basin.

When administrative law enforcement agencies, people's courts, and people's procuratorates at all levels in the Yangtze River basin discover suspected criminal activities during the investigation of illegal acts related to Yangtze River protection or the handling of related cases, they shall transfer the criminal leads to the authorities with investigation or inquiry powers.Article 78 The state shall implement a responsibility system and an assessment and evaluation system for ecological and environmental protection in the Yangtze River Basin. The people's government at a higher level shall assess the completion of ecological and environmental protection and restoration targets by the people's government at a lower level.

Article 79 The relevant departments of the State Council and the relevant departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level in the Yangtze River Basin shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and their respective responsibilities, supervise and inspect various protection, development, and construction activities in the Yangtze River Basin, and shall investigate and deal with illegal acts that damage natural resources, pollute the environment, or harm the ecosystem of the Yangtze River Basin in accordance with the law.
