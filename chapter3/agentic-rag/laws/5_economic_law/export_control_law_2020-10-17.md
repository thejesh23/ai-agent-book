# Export Control Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of safeguarding national security and interests, fulfilling international obligations such as non-proliferation, and strengthening and regulating export controls.

**Article 2** This Law applies to the export controls of the State over dual-use items, military products, nuclear items, and other goods, technologies, and services related to safeguarding national security and interests and fulfilling international obligations such as non-proliferation (hereinafter collectively referred to as controlled items).

The controlled items mentioned in the preceding paragraph include data such as technical materials related to the items.

Export controls as mentioned in this Law refer to the prohibitive or restrictive measures taken by the State against the transfer of controlled items from within the territory of the People's Republic of China to outside the territory, and against the provision of controlled items by citizens, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations of the People's Republic of China to foreign organizations and individuals.

Dual-use items as mentioned in this Law refer to goods, technologies, and services that have both civil and military uses, or contribute to the enhancement of military potential, especially those that can be used for the design, development, production, or use of weapons of mass destruction and their means of delivery.

Military products as mentioned in this Law refer to equipment, specialized production equipment, and other related goods, technologies, and services used for military purposes.

Nuclear items as mentioned in this Law refer to nuclear materials, nuclear equipment, non-nuclear materials for reactors, and related technologies and services.

**Article 3** Export control work shall adhere to a holistic approach to national security, maintain international peace, coordinate security and development, and improve export control management and services.

**Article 4** The State implements a unified export control system, managing through the formulation of control lists, catalogs, or directories (hereinafter collectively referred to as control lists) and the implementation of export licensing.

**Article 5** The departments of the State Council and the Central Military Commission responsible for export control functions (hereinafter collectively referred to as the national export control authorities) shall be responsible for export control work according to their respective duties. Other relevant departments of the State Council and the Central Military Commission shall be responsible for work related to export controls according to their respective duties.

The State shall establish an export control work coordination mechanism to coordinate major issues in export control work. The national export control authorities and the relevant departments of the State Council shall closely cooperate and strengthen information sharing.

The national export control authorities shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, establish an export control expert consultation mechanism to provide advisory opinions for export control work.

The national export control authorities shall, in a timely manner, issue export control guidelines for relevant industries to guide export operators in establishing and improving internal compliance systems for export controls and standardizing operations.

The relevant departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for work related to export controls in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 6** The State shall strengthen international cooperation on export controls and participate in the formulation of relevant international rules on export controls.

**Article 7** Export operators may lawfully establish and join relevant industry self-regulatory organizations such as chambers of commerce and associations.

Relevant industry self-regulatory organizations such as chambers of commerce and associations shall abide by laws and administrative regulations, provide their members with services related to export controls in accordance with their articles of association, and play a coordinating and self-regulatory role.

## Chapter II Control Policies, Control Lists, and Control Measures

### Section 1 General Provisions

**Article 8** The national export control authorities shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, formulate export control policies. Major policies shall be reported to the State Council for approval, or to the State Council and the Central Military Commission for approval.

The national export control authorities may assess countries and regions to which controlled items are exported, determine risk levels, and adopt corresponding control measures.

**Article 9** The national export control authorities shall, in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, based on export control policies, formulate and adjust the export control list of controlled items in accordance with prescribed procedures, and publish it in a timely manner.

When necessary for safeguarding national security and interests and fulfilling international obligations such as non-proliferation, with the approval of the State Council, or with the approval of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, the national export control authorities may impose temporary controls on goods, technologies, and services not included in the export control list and shall announce such controls. The duration of temporary controls shall not exceed two years. Before the expiration of the temporary control period, a timely assessment shall be conducted, and based on the assessment results, a decision shall be made to cancel the temporary controls, extend the temporary controls, or include the temporarily controlled items in the export control list.

**Article 10** When necessary for safeguarding national security and interests and fulfilling international obligations such as non-proliferation, with the approval of the State Council, or with the approval of the State Council and the Central Military Commission, the national export control authorities may, in conjunction with relevant departments, prohibit the export of relevant controlled items, or prohibit the export of relevant controlled items to specific destination countries, regions, specific organizations, and individuals.

**Article 11** Export operators engaged in the export of controlled items shall comply with the provisions of this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations; if they are required by law to obtain the relevant qualification for exporting controlled items, they shall obtain such qualification.

**Article 12** The State implements a licensing system for the export of controlled items.

For controlled items listed in the export control list or temporarily controlled items, export operators shall apply to the national export control authorities for a license.

For goods, technologies, and services other than those listed in the export control list and temporarily controlled items, if the export operator knows or should know, or has been notified by the national export control authorities, that the relevant goods, technologies, or services may pose any of the following risks, the export operator shall apply to the national export control authorities for a license:

(1) Endangering national security and interests;
(2) Being used for the design, development, production, or use of weapons of mass destruction and their means of delivery;
(3) Being used for terrorist purposes.

If an export operator is unable to determine whether the goods, technologies, or services to be exported fall within the scope of controlled items under this Law and consults the national export control authorities, the national export control authorities shall respond in a timely manner.

**Article 13** The national export control authorities shall comprehensively consider the following factors when reviewing an application from an export operator for the export of controlled items and make a decision to grant or deny the license:

(1) National security and interests;
(2) International obligations and external commitments;
(3) Type of export;
(4) Sensitivity of the controlled item;
(5) Destination country or region;
(6) End-user and end-use;
(7) Relevant credit record of the export operator;
(8) Other factors stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 14** If an export operator has established an internal compliance system for export controls and it operates effectively, the national export control authorities may grant facilitative measures such as general licenses for the export of relevant controlled items. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the national export control authorities.

**Article 15** An export operator shall submit to the national export control authorities certification documents regarding the end-user and end-use of the controlled items. Such certification documents shall be issued by the end-user or the government agency of the country or region where the end-user is located.

**Article 16** The end-user of a controlled item shall undertake not to change the end-use of the relevant controlled item without the permission of the national export control authorities, nor to transfer it to any third party.

If an export operator or importer discovers that the end-user or end-use may change, it shall immediately report to the national export control authorities in accordance with regulations.

**Article 17** The national export control authorities shall establish a risk management system for the end-users and end-uses of controlled items, assess and verify the end-users and end-uses of controlled items, and strengthen the management of end-users and end-uses.

**Article 18** The national export control authorities shall establish a control list for importers and end-users that fall under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Violating the requirements for end-user or end-use management;
(2) Potentially endangering national security and interests;
(3) Using controlled items for terrorist purposes.

For importers and end-users included in the control list, the national export control authorities may take necessary measures such as prohibiting or restricting transactions involving relevant controlled items, and ordering the suspension of exports of relevant controlled items.

Export operators shall not engage in transactions with importers or end-users included in the control list in violation of regulations. If an export operator genuinely needs to engage in a transaction with an importer or end-user included in the control list under special circumstances, it may submit an application to the national export control authorities.

If an importer or end-user included in the control list has taken measures and no longer falls under the circumstances specified in the first paragraph, it may apply to the national export control authorities for removal from the control list; the national export control authorities may, based on the actual situation, decide to remove the importer or end-user from the control list.

**Article 19** When exporting controlled goods, the consignor or the customs broker shall present to the customs the license document issued by the national export control authorities and go through the customs declaration procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations.

If the consignor of exported goods fails to present to the customs the license document issued by the national export control authorities, and the customs has evidence suggesting that the exported goods may fall within the scope of export controls, the customs may question the consignor of the exported goods; the customs may request the national export control authorities to organize an identification, and shall handle the matter according to law based on the identification conclusion made by the national export control authorities. During the identification or questioning period, the customs shall not release the exported goods.

**Article 20** No organization or individual shall provide services such as agency, freight forwarding, courier, customs declaration, third-party e-commerce trading platforms, and financial services to export operators for illegal export control activities.

### Section 2 Management of Dual-Use Item Exports

**Article 21** When applying to the national dual-use item export control authority for the export of dual-use items, an export operator shall truthfully submit relevant materials in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 22** The national dual-use item export control authority shall accept applications for the export of dual-use items, review the applications independently or jointly with relevant departments in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, and make a decision to grant or deny the license within the statutory time limit. If a decision is made to grant the license, the licensing authority shall uniformly issue the export license.

### Section 3 Management of Military Product Exports

**Article 23** The State implements a monopoly system for the export of military products. Operators engaged in the export of military products shall obtain the qualification for the monopoly of military product exports and engage in export activities within the approved scope of business.

The qualification for the monopoly of military product exports shall be examined and approved by the national military product export control authority.

**Article 24** An operator exporting military products shall, based on control policies and product attributes, apply to the national military product export control authority for the examination and approval procedures for the initiation of military product export projects, military product export projects, and military product export contracts.

Major initiations of military product export projects, major military product export projects, and major military product export contracts shall be examined by the national military product export control authority jointly with relevant departments and reported to the State Council and the Central Military Commission for approval.

**Article 25** Before exporting military products, an operator exporting military products shall apply to the national military product export control authority for a military product export license.

When exporting military products, the operator exporting military products shall present to the customs the license document issued by the national military product export control authority and go through the customs declaration procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 26** An operator exporting military products shall entrust an approved military product export transportation enterprise to handle the transportation of military product exports and related business. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the national military product export control authority jointly with relevant departments.

**Article 27** If an operator exporting military products or a scientific research or production unit participates in an international military product exhibition, it shall go through the approval procedures with the national military product export control authority in accordance with procedures.

## Chapter III Supervision and Administration

**Article 28** The national export control authorities shall supervise and inspect the export activities of controlled items according to law.

When investigating acts suspected of violating the provisions of this Law, the national export control authorities may take the following measures:

(1) Entering the business premises or other relevant premises of the person under investigation for inspection;
(2) Questioning the person under investigation, interested parties, and other relevant organizations or individuals, and requiring them to provide explanations on matters related to the incident under investigation;
(3) Consulting and copying relevant documents and materials such as documents, agreements, accounting books, and business correspondence of the person under investigation, interested parties, and other relevant organizations or individuals;
(4) Inspecting means of transport used for export, stopping the loading of suspected export items, and ordering the return of illegally exported items;
(5) Sealing or seizing relevant items involved in the case;
(6) Inquiring into the bank accounts of the person under investigation.

Taking the measures specified in items (5) and (6) of the preceding paragraph shall be subject to written approval by the person in charge of the national export control authorities.

**Article 29** When the national export control authorities perform their duties according to law, the relevant departments of the State Council, the local people's governments, and their relevant departments shall provide assistance.

When the national export control authorities conduct supervision, inspection, and investigation independently or jointly with relevant departments according to law, the relevant organizations and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct.

The relevant state organs and their staff members shall be legally obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding state secrets, trade secrets, personal privacy, and personal information learned during the investigation.

**Article 30** To strengthen the management of controlled item exports and prevent the risk of illegal controlled item exports, the national export control authorities may take measures such as regulatory interviews and issuing warning letters.

**Article 31** Any organization or individual has the right to report to the national export control authorities any act suspected of violating the provisions of this Law. Upon receiving a report, the national export control authorities shall handle it promptly according to law and maintain confidentiality for the whistleblower.

**Article 32** The national export control authorities shall, in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by China, or on the principle of equality and mutual benefit, carry out export control cooperation and exchanges with other countries or regions and international organizations.

Organizations and individuals within the territory of the People's Republic of China providing information related to export controls to foreign parties shall do so according to law; if it may endanger national security and interests, it shall not be provided.

## Chapter IV Legal Liability

**Article 33** If an export operator engages in the export of relevant controlled items without obtaining the required export qualification, a warning shall be issued, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 500,000 RMB but not more than 5 million RMB shall be imposed.

**Article 34** If an export operator commits any of the following acts, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 500,000 RMB but not more than 5 million RMB shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the operator shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, up to the revocation of the relevant export qualification for controlled items:

(1) Exporting controlled items without a license;
(2) Exporting controlled items beyond the scope permitted by the export license document;
(3) Exporting controlled items that are prohibited for export.

**Article 35** If an export license document for controlled items is obtained by improper means such as deception or bribery, or if an export license document for controlled items is illegally transferred, the license shall be revoked, the export license shall be confiscated, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 200,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 200,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 200,000 RMB but not more than 2 million RMB shall be imposed.

If an export license document for controlled items is forged, altered, or traded, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 50,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 50,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 50,000 RMB but not more than 500,000 RMB shall be imposed.

**Article 36** If an entity knowingly provides services such as agency, freight forwarding, courier, customs declaration, third-party e-commerce trading platforms, and financial services to an export operator engaged in illegal export control activities, a warning shall be issued, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 100,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 100,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 100,000 RMB but not more than 500,000 RMB shall be imposed.

**Article 37** If an export operator engages in transactions with importers or end-users included in the control list in violation of the provisions of this Law, a warning shall be issued, the illegal act shall be ordered to cease, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and if the illegal business turnover is 500,000 RMB or more, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the illegal business turnover shall be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 500,000 RMB, a fine of not less than 500,000 RMB but not more than 5 million RMB shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the operator shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, up to the revocation of the relevant export qualification for controlled items.

**Article 38** If an export operator refuses or obstructs supervision and inspection, a warning shall be issued, and a fine of not less than 100,000 RMB but not more than 300,000 RMB shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the operator shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, up to the revocation of the relevant export qualification for controlled items.

**Article 39** For an export operator penalized for violating the provisions of this Law, from the effective date of the penalty decision, the national export control authorities may refuse to accept any export license application submitted by the operator within five years; for the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons, they may be prohibited from engaging in relevant export activities within five years; if they have received criminal punishment for illegal export control activities, they shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in relevant export activities.

The national export control authorities shall, according to law, include the export operator's violation of this Law in its credit record.

**Article 40** The illegal export control activities specified in this Law shall be penalized by the national export control authorities; if laws or administrative regulations stipulate that the customs shall impose the penalty, the customs shall impose the penalty in accordance with this Law.

**Article 41** If a relevant organization or individual is dissatisfied with a decision of the national export control authorities to deny a license, it may apply for administrative reconsideration according to law. The administrative reconsideration decision shall be final.

**Article 42** State functionaries engaged in export control management who neglect their duties, engage in malpractices for personal gain, or abuse their power shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 43** If a violation of the provisions of this Law on export control management endangers national security and interests, in addition to the penalties imposed under this Law, the matter shall also be handled and penalized in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

If a violation of the provisions of this Law involves the export of controlled items prohibited by the State or the export of controlled items without a license, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.Article 44: Any organization or individual outside the territory of the People's Republic of China that violates the export control management provisions of this Law, endangers the national security and interests of the People's Republic of China, or hinders the fulfillment of international obligations such as non-proliferation, shall be dealt with according to law and held legally responsible.

## Chapter V: Supplementary Provisions

Article 45: The transit, transshipment, through-transport, re-export of controlled items, or their export from special customs supervision areas such as bonded zones and export processing zones, as well as from bonded supervision sites such as export supervision warehouses and bonded logistics centers to destinations outside the territory, shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 46: For the export of nuclear and other controlled items not covered by the provisions of this Law, the relevant laws and administrative regulations shall apply.

Article 47: The export of military products used for overseas operations of armed forces, foreign military exchanges, military assistance, etc., shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 48: If any country or region abuses export control measures to endanger the national security and interests of the People's Republic of China, the People's Republic of China may take reciprocal measures against that country or region based on the actual circumstances.

Article 49: This Law shall come into effect on December 1, 2020.
