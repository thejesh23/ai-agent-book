# Banking Regulatory Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on December 27, 2003

Amended in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Banking Regulatory Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on October 31, 2006

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen the supervision and administration of the banking industry, regulate supervisory and administrative acts, prevent and mitigate banking risks, protect the legitimate rights and interests of depositors and other customers, and promote the sound development of the banking industry.

Article 2 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of banking financial institutions and their business activities nationwide.

For the purposes of this Law, "banking financial institutions" refer to financial institutions established within the territory of the People's Republic of China that absorb public deposits, such as commercial banks, urban credit cooperatives, rural credit cooperatives, and policy banks.

The provisions of this Law governing the supervision and administration of banking financial institutions shall apply to the supervision and administration of financial asset management companies, trust and investment companies, finance companies, financial leasing companies, and other financial institutions established within the territory of the People's Republic of China with the approval of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law, exercise supervision and administration over financial institutions established overseas with its approval and the overseas business activities of the financial institutions mentioned in the preceding two paragraphs.

Article 3 The objectives of banking supervision and administration are to promote the lawful and sound operation of the banking industry and maintain public confidence in the banking industry.

Banking supervision and administration shall protect fair competition in the banking industry and enhance the competitiveness of the banking industry.

Article 4 In exercising supervision and administration over the banking industry, the banking regulatory authority shall adhere to the principles of lawfulness, openness, fairness, and efficiency.

Article 5 The banking regulatory authority and its personnel engaged in supervisory and administrative work shall perform their duties in accordance with the law and be protected by law. Local governments, government departments at all levels, social organizations, and individuals shall not interfere.

Article 6 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish a mechanism for sharing supervisory and administrative information with the People's Bank of China and other financial regulatory authorities under the State Council.

Article 7 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council may establish mechanisms for supervisory and administrative cooperation with banking regulatory authorities of other countries or regions to implement cross-border supervision and administration.

## Chapter II Supervisory and Administrative Authorities

Article 8 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish dispatched offices as needed to perform its duties. The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall exercise unified leadership and management over its dispatched offices.

The dispatched offices of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall perform supervisory and administrative duties within the scope authorized by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 9 Personnel engaged in supervisory and administrative work in the banking regulatory authority shall possess the professional knowledge and business work experience appropriate for their positions.

Article 10 Personnel of the banking regulatory authority shall be devoted to their duties, act in accordance with the law, be impartial and honest, shall not use their positions to seek improper benefits, and shall not hold concurrent positions in financial institutions or other enterprises.

Article 11 Personnel of the banking regulatory authority shall preserve state secrets in accordance with the law and have the responsibility to maintain confidentiality for the banking financial institutions under their supervision and administration and the parties involved.

Where the banking regulatory authority under the State Council exchanges supervisory and administrative information with banking regulatory authorities of other countries or regions, it shall make arrangements for the confidentiality of such information.

Article 12 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall make public its supervisory and administrative procedures and establish a system of supervisory and administrative responsibility and an internal supervision system.

Article 13 Local governments and relevant departments at all levels shall cooperate and provide assistance to the banking regulatory authority in its supervisory and administrative activities, such as handling risks in banking financial institutions and investigating related financial violations.

Article 14 The audit and supervisory authorities under the State Council shall, in accordance with legal provisions, supervise the activities of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

## Chapter III Supervisory and Administrative Responsibilities

Article 15 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, formulate and issue rules and regulations for the supervision and administration of banking financial institutions and their business activities.

Article 16 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with the conditions and procedures prescribed by laws and administrative regulations, examine and approve the establishment, change, termination, and business scope of banking financial institutions.

Article 17 When an application is made for the establishment of a banking financial institution, or when a banking financial institution changes its shareholders whose total capital or shareholding reaches a prescribed proportion, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall examine the source of funds, financial condition, capital replenishment capacity, and integrity of the shareholders.

Article 18 Business types within the business scope of a banking financial institution shall be subject to examination, approval, or filing with the banking regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with regulations. The business types requiring examination, approval, or filing shall be prescribed and published by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 19 No unit or individual may establish a banking financial institution or engage in the business activities of a banking financial institution without the approval of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 20 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall implement qualification management for directors and senior management personnel of banking financial institutions. Specific measures shall be formulated by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 21 The prudential operation rules for banking financial institutions shall be prescribed by laws and administrative regulations, or may be formulated by the banking regulatory authority under the State Council in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

The prudential operation rules referred to in the preceding paragraph include risk management, internal control, capital adequacy ratio, asset quality, loss reserves, risk concentration, related party transactions, asset liquidity, and other matters.

Banking financial institutions shall strictly comply with the prudential operation rules.

Article 22 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall, within the prescribed time limits, make a written decision to approve or disapprove the following applications; if the decision is to disapprove, reasons shall be given:

(1) For the establishment of a banking financial institution, within six months from the date of receipt of the application documents;

(2) For the change or termination of a banking financial institution, as well as the business scope and the addition of business types within the business scope, within three months from the date of receipt of the application documents;

(3) For the examination of the qualifications of directors and senior management personnel, within thirty days from the date of receipt of the application documents.

Article 23 The banking regulatory authority shall conduct off-site surveillance of the business activities and risk status of banking financial institutions, establish an information system for the supervision and administration of banking financial institutions, and analyze and evaluate the risk status of banking financial institutions.

Article 24 The banking regulatory authority shall conduct on-site inspections of the business activities and risk status of banking financial institutions.

The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall formulate on-site inspection procedures to regulate on-site inspection activities.

Article 25 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall implement consolidated supervision and administration of banking financial institutions.

Article 26 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall respond to suggestions made by the People's Bank of China regarding the inspection of banking financial institutions within thirty days from the date of receipt of such suggestions.

Article 27 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish a rating system for the supervision and administration of banking financial institutions and a risk early warning mechanism, and determine the frequency, scope, and other measures to be taken for on-site inspections based on the rating and risk status of banking financial institutions.

Article 28 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall establish a system of responsibility for the detection and reporting of banking emergencies.

Where the banking regulatory authority discovers an emergency that may trigger systemic banking risks and seriously affect social stability, it shall immediately report to the head of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council; if the head of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council deems it necessary to report to the State Council, it shall immediately report to the State Council and inform the People's Bank of China, the finance department under the State Council, and other relevant departments.

Article 29 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall, together with the People's Bank of China, the finance department under the State Council, and other relevant departments, establish a system for handling banking emergencies, formulate contingency plans for handling banking emergencies, clearly define the handling institutions and personnel and their responsibilities, handling measures, and handling procedures, and handle banking emergencies in a timely and effective manner.

Article 30 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the unified compilation of statistical data and statements of banking financial institutions nationwide and shall publish them in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 31 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall provide guidance and supervision over the activities of banking self-regulatory organizations.

The articles of association of banking self-regulatory organizations shall be filed with the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

Article 32 The banking regulatory authority under the State Council may engage in international exchanges and cooperation activities related to banking supervision and administration.

## Chapter IV Supervisory and Administrative Measures

Article 33 The banking regulatory authority, as needed to perform its duties, has the right to require banking financial institutions to submit balance sheets, income statements, and other financial and accounting statements, statistical reports, operational management materials, and audit reports issued by certified public accountants in accordance with regulations.

Article 34 The banking regulatory authority may, based on the requirements of prudential supervision, take the following measures for on-site inspections:

(1) Entering a banking financial institution for inspection;

(2) Questioning personnel of the banking financial institution and requiring them to provide explanations on matters related to the inspection;

(3) Reviewing and copying documents and materials of the banking financial institution related to the inspection, and sealing up documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed;

(4) Inspecting the systems used by the banking financial institution for managing business data through electronic computers.

On-site inspections shall be approved by the head of the banking regulatory authority. During an on-site inspection, there shall be no fewer than two inspectors, who shall present their valid credentials and an inspection notice; if there are fewer than two inspectors or they fail to present valid credentials and an inspection notice, the banking financial institution has the right to refuse the inspection.

Article 35 The banking regulatory authority, as needed to perform its duties, may hold supervisory and administrative talks with directors and senior management personnel of banking financial institutions, requiring them to provide explanations on major matters concerning the business activities and risk management of the banking financial institution.

Article 36 The banking regulatory authority shall order banking financial institutions to truthfully disclose to the public, in accordance with regulations, information such as financial and accounting reports, risk management status, changes in directors and senior management personnel, and other major matters.

Article 37 Where a banking financial institution violates prudential operation rules, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council or its provincial-level dispatched office shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, or if its conduct seriously endangers the sound operation of the banking financial institution and harms the legitimate rights and interests of depositors and other customers, with the approval of the head of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council or its provincial-level dispatched office, the following measures may be taken depending on the circumstances:

(1) Ordering the suspension of part of its business and suspending approval for the launch of new business;

(2) Restricting the distribution of dividends and other income;

(3) Restricting asset transfers;

(4) Ordering the controlling shareholder to transfer equity or restricting the rights of relevant shareholders;

(5) Ordering the adjustment of directors and senior management personnel or restricting their rights;

(6) Suspending approval for the establishment of additional branches.

After the banking financial institution has rectified the situation, it shall submit a report to the banking regulatory authority under the State Council or its provincial-level dispatched office. If the banking regulatory authority under the State Council or its provincial-level dispatched office, upon inspection, finds that the institution complies with the relevant prudential operation rules, it shall lift the measures provided for in the preceding paragraph within three days from the date of completion of the inspection.

Article 38 Where a banking financial institution has experienced or is likely to experience a credit crisis, seriously affecting the legitimate rights and interests of depositors and other customers, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council may, in accordance with the law, take over the banking financial institution or facilitate its restructuring. The takeover and restructuring shall be carried out in accordance with relevant laws and regulations of the State Council.

Article 39 Where a banking financial institution engages in illegal operations, poor management, or other circumstances, and its failure to be dissolved would seriously endanger the financial order and harm the public interest, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council has the right to dissolve it.

Article 40 Where a banking financial institution is taken over, restructured, or dissolved, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council has the right to require the directors, senior management personnel, and other staff of the banking financial institution to perform their duties in accordance with the requirements of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council.

During the period of takeover, restructuring, or dissolution and liquidation, with the approval of the head of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council, the following measures may be taken against the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons:

(1) If the departure from the country of the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons would cause significant losses to the national interest, notify the exit management authority to prevent their departure in accordance with the law;

(2) Apply to the judicial authorities to prohibit them from transferring or assigning their property or creating other rights over their property.

Article 41 With the approval of the head of the banking regulatory authority under the State Council or its provincial-level dispatched office, the banking regulatory authority has the right to inquire into the accounts of banking financial institutions suspected of financial violations, their personnel, and related actors; if illegal funds are suspected of being transferred or concealed, with the approval of the head of the banking regulatory authority, it may apply to the judicial authorities for freezing.

Article 42 When the banking regulatory authority conducts an inspection of a banking financial institution in accordance with the law, with the approval of the head of the banking regulatory authority at or above the level of a city divided into districts, it may take the following measures against units and individuals related to the suspected illegal matters:

(1) Questioning the relevant units or individuals and requiring them to provide explanations on the relevant circumstances;

(2) Reviewing and copying relevant financial and accounting documents, property rights registration documents, and other materials;

(3) Sealing up and preserving documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, destroyed, or forged on a priority basis.

When the banking regulatory authority takes the measures provided for in the preceding paragraph, there shall be no fewer than two investigators, who shall present their valid credentials and an investigation notice; if there are fewer than two investigators or they fail to present valid credentials and an investigation notice, the relevant units or individuals have the right to refuse. For measures taken in accordance with the law, the relevant units and individuals shall cooperate, truthfully explain the relevant circumstances, and provide the relevant documents and materials, and shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 43 Where personnel engaged in supervisory and administrative work in the banking regulatory authority have any of the following circumstances, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Examining and approving the establishment, change, termination, business scope, and business types within the business scope of banking financial institutions in violation of regulations;

(2) Conducting on-site inspections of banking financial institutions in violation of regulations;

(3) Failing to report emergencies in accordance with Article 28 of this Law;

(4) Inquiring into accounts or applying for freezing of funds in violation of regulations;

(5) Taking measures or imposing penalties on banking financial institutions in violation of regulations;

(6) Investigating relevant units or individuals in violation of Article 42 of this Law;

(7) Other acts of abuse of power or dereliction of duty.

Where personnel engaged in supervisory and administrative work in the banking regulatory authority engage in embezzlement or bribery, or disclose state secrets, trade secrets, or personal privacy, if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 44 Where a banking financial institution is established without authorization or business activities of a banking financial institution are illegally engaged in, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall ban it; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall confiscate the illegal gains. If the illegal gains are 500,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 45 Where a banking financial institution has any of the following circumstances, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall order it to make corrections. If there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated. If the illegal gains are 500,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 2 million yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are particularly serious or if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or its operating license may be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Establishing branches without approval;

(2) Changing or terminating without approval;

(3) Engaging in business activities that have not been approved or filed in violation of regulations;

(4) Raising or lowering deposit interest rates or loan interest rates in violation of regulations.

Article 46 Where a banking financial institution has any of the following circumstances, the banking regulatory authority under the State Council shall order it to make corrections and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are particularly serious or if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or its operating license may be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Appointing directors or senior management personnel without qualification examination;

(2) Refusing or obstructing off-site surveillance or on-site inspections;

(3) Providing false statements, reports, or other documents or materials that conceal important facts;

(4) Failing to disclose information in accordance with regulations;

(5) Seriously violating prudential operation rules;

(6) Refusing to implement the measures provided for in Article 37 of this Law.

Article 47 Where a banking financial institution fails to provide statements, reports, or other documents or materials in accordance with regulations, the banking regulatory authority shall order it to make corrections; if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 48 Where a banking financial institution violates laws, administrative regulations, or relevant state regulations on banking supervision and administration, in addition to imposing penalties in accordance with Articles 44 to 47 of this Law, the banking regulatory authority may also take the following measures depending on the circumstances:

(1) Ordering the banking financial institution to impose disciplinary sanctions on the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons;(2) If the act of a banking institution does not constitute a crime, a warning shall be issued to the directly responsible directors, senior managers, and other directly responsible personnel, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed;

(3) The qualification for holding office of the directly responsible directors and senior managers shall be revoked for a certain period or for life, and the directly responsible directors, senior managers, and other directly responsible personnel shall be prohibited from engaging in banking work for a certain period or for life.

Article 49 Anyone who obstructs the staff of the banking regulatory authority from lawfully performing their duties of inspection or investigation shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law by the public security organs; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 6 Supplementary Provisions

Article 50 The supervision and management of policy banks and financial asset management companies established within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by other laws and administrative regulations if otherwise provided.

Article 51 The supervision and management of foreign-funded banking institutions, Sino-foreign joint venture banking institutions, and branches of foreign banking institutions established within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by other laws and administrative regulations if otherwise provided.

Article 52 This Law shall come into force as of February 1, 2004.
