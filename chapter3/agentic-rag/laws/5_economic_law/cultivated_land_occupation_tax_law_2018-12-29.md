# Cultivated Land Occupation Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of rationally utilizing land resources, strengthening land management, and protecting cultivated land.

Article 2 Units and individuals that occupy cultivated land within the territory of the People's Republic of China to construct buildings, structures, or engage in non-agricultural construction shall be taxpayers of cultivated land occupation tax and shall pay the tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Those who occupy cultivated land to construct farmland water conservancy facilities shall not be subject to cultivated land occupation tax.

For the purposes of this Law, "cultivated land" means land used for growing crops.

Article 3 Cultivated land occupation tax shall be levied once based on the actual area of cultivated land occupied by the taxpayer, at the applicable tax rate prescribed. The tax payable shall be the actual area of cultivated land occupied by the taxpayer (in square meters) multiplied by the applicable tax rate.

Article 4 The tax rates for cultivated land occupation tax are as follows:

(1) In areas where the per capita cultivated land does not exceed one mu (taking counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts as units, the same below), the rate shall be 10 yuan to 50 yuan per square meter;

(2) In areas where the per capita cultivated land exceeds one mu but does not exceed two mu, the rate shall be 8 yuan to 40 yuan per square meter;

(3) In areas where the per capita cultivated land exceeds two mu but does not exceed three mu, the rate shall be 6 yuan to 30 yuan per square meter;

(4) In areas where the per capita cultivated land exceeds three mu, the rate shall be 5 yuan to 25 yuan per square meter.

The applicable tax rates for cultivated land occupation tax in various regions shall be proposed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within the range of tax rates specified in the preceding paragraph, based on factors such as per capita cultivated land area and economic development, submitted to the standing committees of the people's congresses at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation. The average level of applicable tax rates for cultivated land occupation tax in each province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall not be lower than the average tax rates specified in the "Table of Average Tax Rates for Cultivated Land Occupation Tax by Province, Autonomous Region, and Municipality Directly under the Central Government" attached to this Law.

Article 5 In areas where the per capita cultivated land is less than 0.5 mu, provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local economic development, appropriately increase the applicable tax rate for cultivated land occupation tax, provided that the increase does not exceed 50% of the applicable tax rate determined under paragraph 2 of Article 4 of this Law. The specific applicable tax rate shall be determined in accordance with the procedures specified in paragraph 2 of Article 4 of this Law.

Article 6 Where basic farmland is occupied, the tax shall be levied at 150% of the local applicable tax rate determined under paragraph 2 of Article 4 or Article 5 of this Law.

Article 7 Cultivated land occupation tax shall be exempted for the occupation of cultivated land for military facilities, schools, kindergartens, social welfare institutions, and medical institutions.

For the occupation of cultivated land for railway lines, highway lines, airport runways, aprons, ports, waterways, and water conservancy projects, the cultivated land occupation tax shall be levied at a reduced rate of 2 yuan per square meter.

Where rural residents occupy cultivated land within the prescribed land use standards to build new self-occupied houses, the cultivated land occupation tax shall be levied at half of the local applicable tax rate; where rural residents, upon approval of relocation, occupy cultivated land for new self-occupied houses, the portion not exceeding the area of the original homestead shall be exempted from cultivated land occupation tax.

Surviving family members of rural martyrs, surviving family members of military personnel who died in the line of duty, disabled military personnel, and rural residents eligible for the rural minimum subsistence allowance who build new self-occupied houses within the prescribed land use standards shall be exempted from cultivated land occupation tax.

As required by national economic and social development, the State Council may prescribe other circumstances for exemption or reduction of cultivated land occupation tax, which shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

Article 8 Where, after exemption or reduction of cultivated land occupation tax is granted in accordance with paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 7 of this Law, the taxpayer changes the original purpose of the occupied land such that it no longer falls under the circumstances for exemption or reduction, the taxpayer shall pay the supplementary cultivated land occupation tax at the local applicable tax rate.

Article 9 Cultivated land occupation tax shall be collected by the tax authorities.

Article 10 The tax liability for cultivated land occupation tax arises on the date when the taxpayer receives the written notice from the natural resources authority regarding the approval of procedures for occupying cultivated land. The taxpayer shall declare and pay the cultivated land occupation tax within 30 days from the date the tax liability arises.

The natural resources authority shall issue the construction land approval certificate based on the cultivated land occupation tax payment certificate or tax exemption certificate and other relevant documents.

Article 11 Where a taxpayer temporarily occupies cultivated land for construction project construction or geological exploration, the taxpayer shall pay cultivated land occupation tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law. If the taxpayer carries out reclamation in accordance with the law within one year from the expiration of the approved period for temporary occupation of cultivated land and restores the planting conditions, the cultivated land occupation tax already paid shall be fully refunded.

Article 12 Where garden land, forest land, grassland, farmland water use land, aquaculture water surfaces, fishery water beaches, or other agricultural land is occupied for the construction of buildings, structures, or for non-agricultural construction, cultivated land occupation tax shall be paid in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

For the occupation of agricultural land specified in the preceding paragraph, the applicable tax rate may be appropriately lower than the applicable tax rate determined for the region under paragraph 2 of Article 4 of this Law, provided that the reduction does not exceed 50%. The specific applicable tax rate shall be proposed by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

Where agricultural land specified in paragraph 1 of this Article is occupied for the construction of production facilities directly serving agricultural production, no cultivated land occupation tax shall be paid.

Article 13 Tax authorities shall establish a mechanism for sharing tax-related information on cultivated land occupation tax and a mechanism for work coordination with relevant departments. The natural resources, agriculture and rural affairs, water conservancy, and other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall regularly provide tax authorities with information on the conversion of agricultural land use and temporary land occupation, and assist tax authorities in strengthening the collection and management of cultivated land occupation tax.

Where tax authorities discover that a taxpayer's tax declaration data is abnormal or that the taxpayer has failed to declare and pay tax within the prescribed time limit, they may request relevant departments to conduct a review. The relevant departments shall issue a review opinion to the tax authorities within 30 days from the date of receiving the review request from the tax authorities.

Article 14 The collection and management of cultivated land occupation tax shall be governed by this Law and the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection."

Article 15 Taxpayers, tax authorities, and their staff who violate the provisions of this Law shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection" and relevant laws and regulations.

Article 16 This Law shall come into effect on September 1, 2019. The "Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Cultivated Land Occupation Tax" promulgated by the State Council on December 1, 2007, shall be simultaneously repealed.
