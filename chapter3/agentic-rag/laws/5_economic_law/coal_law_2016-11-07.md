# Coal Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Coal Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 22, 2011

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws Including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of rationally exploiting and protecting coal resources, regulating coal production and business operations, and promoting and ensuring the development of the coal industry.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the engagement in coal production and business operations within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 Coal resources are owned by the State. The State ownership of coal resources on or under the surface shall not change due to the difference in the ownership or right to use of the land to which the resources are attached.

Article 4 The State shall adopt the policy of unified planning, rational distribution, and comprehensive utilization for coal development.

Article 5 The State shall protect coal resources in accordance with the law and prohibit any act of indiscriminate mining or destructive excavation that damages coal resources.

Article 6 The State shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors who invest in the development of coal resources in accordance with the law.

The State shall ensure the healthy development of State-owned coal mines.

The State shall adopt the policy of supporting, reforming, rectifying, consolidating, and upgrading township coal mines, and implement standardized and rational development as well as orderly growth.

Article 7 Coal mining enterprises must adhere to the safety production policy of putting safety first and focusing on prevention, and establish and improve the responsibility system for safety production and the system of mass prevention and control.

Article 8 People's governments at all levels, their relevant departments, and coal mining enterprises must take measures to strengthen labor protection and ensure the safety and health of coal mine workers.

The State shall adopt special protective measures for workers engaged in underground operations in coal mines.

Article 9 The State encourages and supports the adoption of advanced science and technology and management methods in the process of exploiting and utilizing coal resources.

Coal mining enterprises shall strengthen and improve their management and operations to enhance labor productivity and economic benefits.

Article 10 The State shall maintain the production order and work order in coal mining areas and protect the facilities of coal mining enterprises.

Article 11 The exploitation and utilization of coal resources shall comply with relevant environmental protection laws and regulations, prevent and control pollution and other public hazards, and protect the ecological environment.

Article 12 The coal administration department under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the national coal industry in accordance with the law. The relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the coal industry within their respective functions and duties.

The coal administration departments and relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the coal industry within their respective administrative regions in accordance with the law.

Article 13 A coal mining bureau is a State-owned coal mining enterprise with independent legal personality.

Coal mining bureaus and other coal mining enterprises and coal trading enterprises with independent legal personality shall, in accordance with the law, operate independently, assume sole responsibility for their profits and losses, exercise self-restraint, and pursue self-development.

## Chapter II Coal Production and Development Planning and Coal Mine Construction

Article 14 The coal administration department under the State Council shall formulate a national coal resource exploration plan based on the national mineral resource exploration plan.

Article 15 The coal administration department under the State Council shall organize the formulation and implementation of a coal production and development plan based on the coal resources specified in the national mineral resource plan.

The coal administration departments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall organize the formulation and implementation of local coal production and development plans based on the coal resources specified in the national mineral resource plan, and report them to the coal administration department under the State Council for recordation.

Article 16 Coal production and development plans shall be formulated in accordance with the needs of national economic and social development and shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plan.

Article 17 The State shall formulate preferential policies to support the development of the coal industry and promote coal mine construction.

Coal mine construction projects shall conform to the coal production and development plan and the coal industry policy.

Article 18 The use of land for coal mine construction shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations. If land is requisitioned, land compensation fees and resettlement subsidies shall be paid in accordance with the law, and the resettlement of relocated residents shall be properly arranged.

Coal mine construction shall adhere to the principle of protecting arable land and rationally utilizing land.

Local people's governments shall provide support and assistance for coal mine construction in the lawful use of land and the relocation of residents.

Article 19 Coal mine construction shall adhere to the principle of simultaneous implementation of coal development and environmental management. The environmental protection facilities of a coal mine construction project must be designed, constructed, accepted, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

## Chapter III Coal Production and Coal Mine Safety

Article 20 Before a coal mine is put into production, the coal mining enterprise shall obtain a safety production license in accordance with the provisions of relevant safety production laws and administrative regulations. Without obtaining a safety production license, no coal production shall be conducted.

Article 21 The State shall implement protective mining for special coal types or scarce coal types that are of significant value to the national economy.

Article 22 The exploitation of coal resources must comply with coal mining regulations, follow a reasonable mining sequence, and achieve the prescribed coal resource recovery rate.

The coal resource recovery rate shall be determined by the coal administration department under the State Council based on different resources and mining conditions.

The State encourages coal mining enterprises to conduct re-mining or mine residual coal at edges and corners, as well as extremely thin coal seams.

Article 23 Coal mining enterprises shall strengthen the supervision, inspection, and management of coal product quality. Coal product quality shall be graded according to national standards or industry standards.

Article 24 Coal production shall be conducted within the approved mining scope in accordance with the law, and shall not exceed the approved mining scope by crossing boundaries or layers.

Mining operations shall not arbitrarily mine safety coal pillars, nor shall they use dangerous methods such as flooding, blasting, or tunneling through roadways that may endanger the production safety of adjacent coal mines.

Article 25 If land is occupied or surface land is subsided or excavated due to coal mining, the mining operator shall be responsible for reclamation to restore it to a usable state; if losses are caused to others, compensation shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 26 The closure of a coal mine and the decommissioning of a mine shaft shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws, regulations, and the coal administration department under the State Council.

Article 27 The State shall establish a system for coal mining enterprises to accumulate funds for the transition of aging mines.

The State encourages and supports coal mining enterprises to develop diversified operations.

Article 28 The State advocates and supports coal mining enterprises and other enterprises to develop coal-electricity integration, coking, coal chemical industry, coal building materials, etc., and to conduct deep and fine processing of coal.

The State encourages coal mining enterprises to develop coal washing and processing, and to comprehensively develop and utilize coalbed methane, coal gangue, coal slime, stone coal, and peat.

Article 29 The State shall develop and promote clean coal technology.

The State shall take measures to ban indigenous coking. The construction of new indigenous coking kilns is prohibited; existing indigenous coking kilns shall be transformed within a specified period.

Article 30 People's governments at or above the county level, their coal administration departments, and other relevant departments shall strengthen the supervision and administration of coal mine safety production.

Article 31 The safety production management of a coal mining enterprise shall implement a responsibility system under the director of the coal mining bureau or the mine manager.

Article 32 The director of the coal mining bureau, the mine manager, and other principal persons in charge of a coal mining enterprise must comply with relevant mine safety laws, regulations, and coal industry safety rules and procedures, strengthen the management of coal mine safety production, implement the safety production responsibility system, and take effective measures to prevent casualties and other safety production accidents.

Article 33 Coal mining enterprises shall provide safety production education and training to their employees; those who have not received safety production education and training shall not be allowed to work at their posts.

Employees of coal mining enterprises must comply with relevant safety production laws, regulations, coal industry rules, procedures, and enterprise rules and regulations.

Article 34 In underground coal mine operations, when an emergency situation that endangers the life safety of employees and cannot be eliminated occurs, the person in charge at the operation site or the safety management personnel shall immediately organize the employees to evacuate the dangerous site and promptly report to the relevant person in charge.

Article 35 If the trade union of a coal mining enterprise discovers that the enterprise's administrative side gives illegal commands, forces employees to work under risk, or finds obvious major accident hazards during the production process that may endanger the life safety of employees, it has the right to propose solutions, and the administrative side of the coal mining enterprise must make a handling decision promptly. If the administrative side refuses to handle it, the trade union has the right to criticize, report, and accuse.

Article 36 Coal mining enterprises must provide employees with labor protection articles necessary to ensure safety production.

Article 37 Coal mining enterprises shall, in accordance with the law, pay work-related injury insurance premiums for their employees to participate in work-related injury insurance. Enterprises are encouraged to purchase accident insurance for underground workers and pay the insurance premiums.

Article 38 The equipment, materials, explosive products, and safety instruments used by coal mining enterprises must comply with national standards or industry standards.

## Chapter IV Coal Trading

Article 39 Coal trading enterprises engaged in coal trading shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, improve services, and ensure supply. All illegal trading activities are prohibited.

Article 40 Coal trading shall reduce intermediate links and eliminate unreasonable intermediate links, and coal mining enterprises with the necessary conditions are encouraged to sell directly.

Coal users and coal trading enterprises in coal-consuming regions have the right to purchase coal directly from coal mining enterprises. In coal-producing regions, coal sales and transportation service organizations may be established to handle the sales and transportation business for small and medium-sized coal mines.

Administrative organs are prohibited from violating State regulations by arbitrarily setting up intermediate links for coal supply and charging additional fees.

Article 41 Stations, ports, and other transportation enterprises engaged in coal transportation shall not use the transport capacity they control as a means to participate in coal trading or seek improper benefits.

Article 42 The price administration department under the State Council, together with the coal administration department under the State Council and relevant departments, shall supervise and manage the sales prices of coal.

Article 43 The quality of coal supplied by coal mining enterprises and coal trading enterprises to users shall meet national standards or industry standards, with consistent quality grades and prices commensurate with quality. If users have special requirements for coal quality, the supply and demand parties shall agree in the coal purchase and sale contract.

Coal mining enterprises and coal trading enterprises shall not adulterate or misrepresent inferior coal as good quality coal.

Article 44 If the quality of coal supplied by coal mining enterprises or coal trading enterprises to users does not meet national standards or industry standards, or does not meet the contract agreement, or if the quality grade is inconsistent or the price is not commensurate with the quality, causing losses to users, compensation shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Coal mining enterprises, coal trading enterprises, transportation enterprises, and coal users shall supply, transport, and unload coal in accordance with the law, relevant State Council provisions, or contract agreements.

Transportation enterprises shall load and stack coal of different qualities separately.

Article 46 The import and export of coal shall be subject to unified management in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Large-scale coal mining enterprises that meet the conditions have the right to engage in coal export operations upon lawful permission from the foreign trade and economic cooperation department under the State Council.

Article 47 Measures for the administration of coal trading shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter V Protection of Coal Mining Areas

Article 48 No unit or individual may damage the power supply, communication, water source, transportation, or other production facilities in a coal mining area.

No unit or individual may disrupt the production order or work order in a coal mining area.

Article 49 All units and individuals have the right to report and accuse acts of stealing or destroying facilities and equipment in coal mining areas, as well as other acts that endanger the safety of coal mining areas.

Article 50 Without the consent of the coal mining enterprise, no unit or individual may plant crops, raise livestock, extract soil, or construct buildings or structures on the land for which the coal mining enterprise has lawfully obtained the right to use during the valid period of such right.

Article 51 Without the consent of the coal mining enterprise, no unit or individual may occupy the coal mining enterprise's dedicated railway lines, dedicated roads, dedicated waterways, dedicated docks, dedicated power lines, or dedicated water supply pipelines.

Article 52 If any unit or individual needs to carry out operations within the coal mining area that may endanger the safety of the coal mine, they shall obtain the consent of the coal mining enterprise, report to the coal administration department for approval, and take safety measures before proceeding with the operations.

If public works or other projects need to be constructed within the coal mining area, the relevant unit shall first negotiate and reach an agreement with the coal mining enterprise before commencing construction.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 53 The coal administration departments and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the implementation of coal laws and regulations by coal mining enterprises and coal trading enterprises.

Article 54 The supervision and inspection personnel of the coal administration departments and relevant departments shall be familiar with coal laws and regulations, possess relevant professional coal technology, be impartial and honest, and enforce the law impartially.

Article 55 When conducting supervision and inspection, the supervision and inspection personnel of the coal administration departments and relevant departments have the right to inquire about the implementation of coal laws and regulations from coal mining enterprises, coal trading enterprises, or users, inspect relevant materials, and have the right to enter the site for inspection.

Coal mining enterprises, coal trading enterprises, and users shall provide convenience to the supervision and inspection personnel of the coal administration departments and relevant departments who are lawfully performing supervision and inspection tasks.

Article 56 The supervision and inspection personnel of the coal administration departments and relevant departments have the right to require coal mining enterprises and coal trading enterprises that violate coal laws and regulations to make corrections in accordance with the law.

When conducting supervision and inspection, the supervision and inspection personnel of the coal administration departments and relevant departments shall present their credentials.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 57 If, in violation of Article 22 of this Law, the exploitation of coal resources fails to achieve the coal resource recovery rate prescribed by the coal administration department under the State Council, the coal administration department shall order rectification within a specified period; if the prescribed recovery rate is still not achieved upon expiration of the period, production shall be ordered to cease.

Article 58 If, in violation of Article 24 of this Law, safety coal pillars are arbitrarily mined or dangerous methods that may endanger the production safety of adjacent coal mines are used for mining operations, the labor administrative department, together with the coal administration department, shall order the operations to cease; the coal administration department shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if a crime is constituted, the judicial authority shall pursue criminal liability in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 59 If, in violation of Article 43 of this Law, coal products are adulterated or misrepresented as good quality, the sale shall be ordered to cease, illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, the judicial authority shall pursue criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 60 If, in violation of Article 50 of this Law, without the consent of the coal mining enterprise, buildings or structures are constructed on the land for which the coal mining enterprise has lawfully obtained the right to use during the valid period of such right, the local people's government shall mobilize for demolition; if demolition is refused, an order for demolition shall be issued.

Article 61 If, in violation of Article 51 of this Law, without the consent of the coal mining enterprise, the coal mining enterprise's dedicated railway lines, dedicated roads, dedicated waterways, dedicated docks, dedicated power lines, or dedicated water supply pipelines are occupied, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is not made upon expiration of the period, compulsory removal shall be carried out, and a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may also be imposed; if losses are caused, liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 62 If, in violation of Article 52 of this Law, without approval or without taking safety measures, operations that endanger the safety of the coal mine are carried out within the coal mining area, the coal administration department shall order the operations to cease, and may also impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; if losses are caused, liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 63 Anyone who commits one of the following acts shall be punished by the public security authority in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if a crime is constituted, the judicial authority shall pursue criminal liability in accordance with the law:

(1) Obstructing coal mine construction, causing the construction to be unable to proceed normally;

(2) Intentionally damaging the power supply, communication, water source, transportation, or other production facilities in a coal mining area;

(3) Disrupting the order of a coal mining area, causing production or work to be unable to proceed normally;

(4) Refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection personnel from lawfully performing their duties.

Article 64 If management personnel of a coal mining enterprise give illegal commands or force employees to work under risk, resulting in a major casualty accident, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 65 If management personnel of a coal mining enterprise fail to take measures to eliminate accident hazards in the coal mine, resulting in a major casualty accident, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 66 If staff members of the coal administration departments and relevant departments neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or abuse their power, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, the judicial authority shall pursue criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 67 This Law shall come into force as of December 1, 1996.
