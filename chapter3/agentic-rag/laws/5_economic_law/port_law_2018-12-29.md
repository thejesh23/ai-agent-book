# Port Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on June 28, 2003

First Amendment on April 24, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China at the Fourteenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on November 4, 2017, according to the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws Including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China at the Thirtieth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Third Amendment on December 29, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Four Laws Including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China at the Seventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of strengthening port administration, maintaining port safety and operational order, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of the parties concerned, and promoting port construction and development.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities such as port planning, construction, maintenance, operation, administration, and related activities.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, a "port" refers to an area composed of a certain range of waters and land, equipped with corresponding dock facilities, and having functions such as vessel entry and exit, berthing, mooring, passenger embarkation and disembarkation, cargo loading, unloading, lightering, and storage.

A port may consist of one or more port areas.

Article 4 The State Council and relevant local people's governments at or above the county level shall reflect the development and planning requirements of ports in their national economic and social development plans, and shall protect and rationally utilize port resources in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The State encourages domestic and foreign economic organizations and individuals to invest in port construction and operation in accordance with the law, and protects the legitimate rights and interests of investors.

Article 6 The competent transport department under the State Council shall be responsible for port work nationwide.

The administration of ports by local people's governments within their respective administrative regions shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of the State Council on the port administration system.

Under the port administration system determined in accordance with the preceding paragraph, for ports administered by the people's government of a city or county where the port is located, the people's government of that city or county shall designate a department to specifically implement the administrative management of the port; for ports administered by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the people's government of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall designate a department to specifically implement the administrative management of the port.

The department designated to specifically implement the administrative management of the port in accordance with the preceding paragraph is hereinafter collectively referred to as the "port administrative department."

## Chapter II Port Planning and Construction

Article 7 Port plans shall be formulated based on the requirements of national economic and social development and the needs of national defense construction, reflecting the principle of rational utilization of shoreline resources, conforming to the urban system plan, and being aligned and coordinated with the overall land use plan, overall urban plan, river basin plan, flood control plan, marine functional zoning plan, waterway transport development plan, and other transport mode development plans, as well as other relevant plans stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Expert argumentation shall be organized for the formulation of port plans, and environmental impact assessments shall be conducted in accordance with the law.

Article 8 Port plans include port layout plans and port master plans.

A port layout plan refers to the distribution plan of ports, including the national port layout plan and the port layout plans of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

A port master plan refers to the specific plan for a port over a certain period, including the scope of the port's waters and land, division of port areas, throughput capacity and vessel types calling at the port, the nature and functions of the port, use of waters and land, shoreline use for port facility construction, allocation of construction land, and the sequence of phased construction.

Port master plans shall conform to port layout plans.

Article 9 The national port layout plan shall be formulated by the competent transport department under the State Council after soliciting opinions from relevant departments under the State Council and relevant military authorities, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval before being promulgated and implemented.

The port layout plan of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the people's government of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government based on the national port layout plan, and shall be submitted to the competent transport department under the State Council for comments. If the competent transport department under the State Council does not propose any modification opinions within thirty days from the date of receiving the materials for comments, the port layout plan shall be promulgated and implemented by the relevant people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; if the competent transport department under the State Council considers that the plan does not conform to the national port layout plan, it shall propose modification opinions within thirty days from the date of receiving the materials for comments; if the relevant people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government disagrees with the modification opinions, the matter shall be submitted to the State Council for a decision.

Article 10 Port master plans shall be formulated by the port administrative department after soliciting opinions from relevant departments and relevant military authorities.

Article 11 The master plans of major ports with important geographical locations, large throughput capacities, and significant impacts on economic development shall be formulated by the competent transport department under the State Council after soliciting opinions from relevant departments under the State Council and relevant military authorities, and shall be approved jointly with the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and then promulgated and implemented. The list of major ports shall be determined and published by the competent transport department under the State Council after soliciting opinions from relevant departments under the State Council.

The people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall determine the important ports in its region after soliciting opinions from the competent transport department under the State Council. The master plans of important ports shall be approved by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government after soliciting opinions from the competent transport department under the State Council, and then promulgated and implemented.

The master plans of ports other than those specified in the preceding two paragraphs shall be approved by the people's government of the city or county where the port is located, promulgated and implemented, and reported to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for record.

The master plans of ports falling within the scope specified in paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, formulated by the port administrative department of the city or county people's government, shall be reviewed and approved by the people's government at the same level before being submitted for approval.

Article 12 Amendments to port plans shall be handled in accordance with the procedures for formulating port plans.

Article 13 For the construction of port facilities within a port master plan area using deep-water shoreline, approval shall be obtained from the competent transport department under the State Council jointly with the comprehensive economic macro-control department under the State Council; for the construction of port facilities using non-deep-water shoreline, approval shall be obtained from the port administrative department. However, for projects approved for construction by the State Council or the comprehensive economic macro-control department under the State Council that use port shoreline, separate approval procedures for using port shoreline shall not be required.

The standards for deep-water port shoreline shall be formulated by the competent transport department under the State Council.

Article 14 Port construction shall conform to port plans. No port facilities shall be constructed in violation of port plans.

Article 15 Port construction projects that require approval from relevant authorities in accordance with state regulations shall undergo approval procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations and shall comply with relevant state standards and technical specifications.

Environmental impact assessments shall be conducted for port construction projects in accordance with the law.

The safety facilities and environmental protection facilities of a port construction project must be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

Article 16 The use of land and waters for port construction shall be handled in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on land administration, sea area use administration, river administration, waterway administration, and military facility protection administration, as well as other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 17 Sites for dangerous cargo operations and dedicated facilities for sanitary and pest control treatment in a port shall conform to the port master plan and the requirements of the state on work safety, fire protection, inspection and quarantine, and environmental protection, and their distance from densely populated areas and port passenger transport facilities shall comply with the provisions of relevant departments under the State Council; they may only be constructed after relevant procedures have been completed in accordance with the law.

Article 18 Navigation aid facilities and other auxiliary facilities shall be constructed simultaneously with the port and shall be ensured to be put into use on schedule.

The construction of office facilities for relevant administrative agencies within the port shall conform to the port master plan, and construction costs shall not be apportioned to port operators.

Article 19 After the completion of a port facility construction project, it shall pass acceptance inspection in accordance with relevant state regulations before being put into use.

The ownership of port facilities shall be determined in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

Article 20 Relevant people's governments at or above the county level shall ensure necessary capital investment for the construction and maintenance of public infrastructure such as waterways, breakwaters, and anchorages in ports. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 21 Relevant people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to organize the construction of facilities supporting the port, such as waterways, railways, highways, water supply and drainage, power supply, and communications.

## Chapter III Port Operation

Article 22 To engage in port operations, a written application shall be submitted to the port administrative department for a port operation permit, and industrial and commercial registration shall be completed in accordance with the law.

The port administrative department shall implement the port operation permit system following the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality.

Port operations include the operation of docks and other port facilities, port passenger transport services, cargo loading, unloading, lightering, and storage operations within the port area, and port tugboat operations.

Article 23 To obtain a port operation permit, an applicant must have a fixed place of business, facilities, equipment, professional technical personnel, and management personnel suitable for the business operations, and must meet other conditions stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 24 The port administrative department shall, within thirty days from the date of receiving the written application specified in paragraph 1 of Article 22 of this Law, make a decision on whether to grant the permit in accordance with the law. If the permit is granted, a port operation permit shall be issued; if the permit is not granted, the applicant shall be notified in writing with reasons.

Article 25 The competent transport department under the State Council shall formulate service standards and specifications for port tallying.

To engage in port tallying business, it shall be reported to the port administrative department for record in accordance with regulations.

A port tallying business operator shall handle tallying business fairly and accurately; it shall not concurrently engage in the cargo loading and unloading business or storage business specified in paragraph 3 of Article 22 of this Law.

Article 26 Port operators, when engaging in business activities, must comply with relevant laws and regulations, abide by the provisions of the competent transport department under the State Council on port operation rules, fulfill contractual obligations in accordance with the law, and provide fair and good services to customers.

Operators engaged in port passenger transport services shall take effective measures to ensure passenger safety, provide fast and convenient services to passengers, and maintain a good waiting environment.

Port operators shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant environmental protection laws and regulations, take effective measures to prevent pollution and harm to the environment.

Article 27 Port operators shall give priority to operations involving emergency rescue materials, disaster relief materials, and materials urgently needed for national defense construction.

Article 28 Port operators shall publish the items and standards of charges for operational services at their business premises; charges not published shall not be implemented.

Where port operational charges are subject to government-guided prices or government-set prices in accordance with the law, port operators shall implement them accordingly.

Article 29 The State encourages and protects fair competition in port business activities.

Port operators shall not engage in monopolistic or unfair competition practices, nor shall they use any means to force others to accept their port services.

Article 30 Statistical data required by the port administrative department from port operators in accordance with the Statistics Law of the People's Republic of China and relevant administrative regulations shall be provided truthfully by the port operators.

The port administrative department shall promptly report the statistical data submitted by port operators in accordance with relevant state regulations and shall maintain the confidentiality of the port operators' trade secrets.

Article 31 The legitimate rights and interests of port operators are protected by law. No unit or individual shall apportion expenses or illegally collect fees from port operators, nor shall they illegally interfere with the operational autonomy of port operators.

## Chapter IV Port Safety and Supervision

Article 32 Port operators must, in accordance with the Work Safety Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and regulations, as well as the provisions of the competent transport department under the State Council on port safety operation rules, strengthen work safety management, establish and improve the work safety responsibility system and other rules and regulations, improve work safety conditions, take effective measures to ensure work safety, and ensure safe production.

Port operators shall, in accordance with the law, formulate emergency plans for dangerous cargo accidents, emergency evacuation and rescue plans for passengers in major production safety accidents, and plans for preventing natural disasters, and ensure their implementation.

Article 33 The port administrative department shall, in accordance with the law, formulate emergency plans for dangerous cargo accidents in ports that may endanger public interests, emergency evacuation and rescue plans for passengers in major production safety accidents, and plans for preventing natural disasters, and establish and improve the emergency rescue system for major production safety accidents in ports.

Article 34 Vessels entering or leaving a port shall report to the maritime administrative agency in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on maritime traffic safety. Upon receiving the report, the maritime administrative agency shall promptly notify the port administrative department.

Vessels carrying dangerous cargo entering or leaving a port shall report the name, characteristics, packaging of the dangerous cargo, and the time of entry or departure to the maritime administrative agency in accordance with the provisions of the competent transport department under the State Council. Upon receiving the report, the maritime administrative agency shall, within the time limit prescribed by the competent transport department under the State Council, decide whether to approve, notify the reporting party, and inform the port administrative department. However, vessels operating on fixed routes, with fixed schedules, and carrying fixed types of cargo may report periodically.

Article 35 For loading, unloading, or lightering operations of dangerous cargo within a port, the name, characteristics, packaging of the dangerous cargo, and the time and location of the operation shall be reported to the port administrative department in accordance with the provisions of the competent transport department under the State Council. Upon receiving the report, the port administrative department shall, within the time limit prescribed by the competent transport department under the State Council, decide whether to approve, notify the reporting party, and inform the maritime administrative agency.

Article 36 The port administrative department shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the work safety situation of the port, and conduct key inspections of docks with high passenger traffic, large cargo throughput, or special purposes; if potential safety hazards are found during inspections, the inspected party shall be ordered to eliminate them immediately or within a specified time limit.

The department responsible for work safety supervision and administration and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations, supervise and inspect port work safety within their respective responsibilities.

Article 37 Aquaculture and planting activities are prohibited within port waters.

Activities such as excavation and blasting that may endanger port safety shall not be carried out in the port; if such activities are necessary due to engineering construction or other reasons, corresponding safety protection measures must be taken, and approval must be obtained from the port administrative department. The port administrative department shall promptly notify the maritime administrative agency of the approval status, and the maritime administrative agency shall no longer conduct approval procedures in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on maritime traffic safety.

Dumping of soil, sand, or gravel into port waters, and discharge of toxic or hazardous substances exceeding prescribed standards in violation of relevant environmental protection laws and regulations, are prohibited.

Article 38 For engineering projects such as bridges, underwater tunnels, and hydropower stations that may affect changes in port hydrological conditions, the department responsible for approving the project shall solicit opinions from the port administrative department before approval.

Article 39 In accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on maritime traffic safety, vessels that require pilotage to enter or leave a port shall apply to the pilotage agency for pilotage. Specific measures for pilotage shall be formulated by the competent transport department under the State Council.

Article 40 In the event of passenger stranding or cargo congestion blocking the port, the port administrative department shall promptly take effective measures to clear the port; if the people's government of the city or county where the port is located deems it necessary, it may directly take measures to clear the port.

Article 41 The port administrative department shall organize the formulation of the port's charter and make it public.

The contents of the port charter shall include descriptions of the port's geographical location, waterway conditions, basin depth, mechanical facilities, and handling capacity, as well as specific measures for implementing the relevant port administration laws, regulations, and provisions of the competent transport department under the State Council.

Article 42 The port administrative department shall, within its functions, supervise and inspect the implementation of this Law.

When conducting supervision and inspection in accordance with the law, the inspectors of the port administrative department have the right to inquire about relevant information from the inspected units and relevant personnel, and may review and copy relevant materials.

Inspectors shall keep confidential the trade secrets learned during inspections.

Inspectors shall present their law enforcement credentials when conducting supervision and inspection.

Article 43 Inspectors shall make written records of the time, location, content, problems found, and handling of the supervision and inspection, and shall sign them together with the person in charge of the inspected unit; if the person in charge of the inspected unit refuses to sign, the inspectors shall record the situation and report it to the port administrative department.

Article 44 The inspected units and relevant personnel shall accept the supervision and inspection conducted by the port administrative department in accordance with the law, truthfully provide relevant information and materials, and shall not refuse inspection or conceal or falsely report relevant information and materials.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 45 If a port operator or port tallying business operator commits illegal acts as specified in this Law, it shall be included in the credit record in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations and shall be publicly disclosed.

Article 46 Any of the following acts shall be ordered by the local people's government at or above the county level or the port administrative department to make corrections within a specified time limit; if corrections are not made within the time limit, the authority that issued the order for correction shall apply to the people's court for compulsory demolition of the illegally constructed facilities; a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may also be imposed:

(1) Constructing ports, docks, or other port facilities in violation of port plans;

(2) Constructing port facilities using port shoreline without legal approval.

If the project approval department approves a construction project that violates port plans, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 47 If the distance between a dangerous cargo operation site or a dedicated facility for sanitary and pest control treatment constructed in a port and a densely populated area or port passenger transport facility does not meet the provisions of relevant departments under the State Council, the port administrative department shall order the cessation of construction or use, make corrections within a specified time limit, and may impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 48 If a dock or port loading and unloading facility or passenger transport facility is put into use without passing acceptance inspection, the port administrative department shall order the cessation of use, make corrections within a specified time limit, and may impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 49 If a person engages in port operations without legally obtaining a port operation permit, or a port tallying business operator concurrently engages in cargo loading and unloading business or storage business, the port administrative department shall order the cessation of illegal operations and confiscate the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 100,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall also be imposed; if the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed.Article 50 Where a port operator fails to prioritize operations for emergency rescue supplies, disaster relief materials, or materials urgently needed for national defense construction, the port administrative department shall order corrective action; if serious consequences result, the port operation permit shall be revoked.

Article 51 Where a port operator, in violation of relevant laws and administrative regulations, engages in monopolistic or unfair competition practices in its business activities, it shall bear legal liability in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 52 Where a port operator violates the provisions on work safety as stipulated in Article 32 of this Law, the port administrative department or other departments legally responsible for work safety supervision and management shall impose penalties in accordance with the law; if the circumstances are serious, the port administrative department shall revoke the port operation permit and impose penalties on its principal responsible persons in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Where a vessel entering or leaving a port fails to report to the maritime administrative agency in accordance with Article 34 of this Law, the maritime administrative agency shall impose penalties in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations on maritime traffic safety.

Article 54 Where loading, unloading, or transshipment of dangerous goods is carried out within a port without reporting to and obtaining approval from the port administrative department in accordance with the law, the port administrative department shall order the cessation of the operation and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 55 Where aquaculture or planting activities are conducted within port waters, the maritime administrative agency shall order corrective action within a specified period; if corrective action is not taken within the specified period, the aquaculture or planting facilities shall be forcibly removed, with the removal costs borne by the violator; a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan may also be imposed.

Article 56 Where, without lawful approval, activities such as excavation or blasting that may endanger port safety are conducted within a port, or soil or sand is dumped into port waters, the port administrative department shall order the cessation of the illegal act and the elimination of resulting safety hazards within a specified period; if elimination is not completed within the specified period, forced elimination shall be carried out, with the costs borne by the violator; a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if penalties are to be imposed by the maritime administrative agency in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations on maritime traffic safety, those provisions shall apply; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 57 Where the competent transportation department, the port administrative department, the maritime administrative agency, or other relevant authorities fail to perform their duties in accordance with the law and commit any of the following acts, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Illegally approving the construction of port facilities using port shoreline, or illegally approving vessels carrying dangerous goods to enter or leave a port, or illegally approving the loading, unloading, or transshipment of dangerous goods within a port;

(2) Granting port operation permits to applicants who do not meet the statutory conditions;

(3) Failing to promptly revoke the permit upon discovering that a port operator who has obtained an operation permit no longer meets the statutory permit conditions;

(4) Failing to perform supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law, and failing to investigate and punish in accordance with the law acts such as constructing ports, wharves, or other port facilities in violation of port planning, engaging in port operation business without lawful authorization, failing to comply with work safety management regulations, endangering port operation safety, or other violations of this Law.

Article 58 Where an administrative organ illegally interferes with the operational autonomy of a port operator, its superior administrative organ or the supervisory authority shall order corrective action; if property is apportioned to the port operator or fees are illegally collected, a refund shall be ordered; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 59 Ports open to vessels navigating international routes shall be reported by the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government to the State Council for approval after consultation with the relevant departments of the State Council and relevant military authorities in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 60 The management of fishing ports shall be the responsibility of the fishery administrative departments of the people's governments at or above the county level. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

The term "fishing ports" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to artificial ports or natural harbors specifically serving fishery production, providing berthing, shelter, loading and unloading of catches, and replenishment of fishery supplies for fishing vessels, including fishery-specific wharves, fishery-specific waters, and fishing vessel-specific anchorages within comprehensive ports.

Article 61 The construction and management measures for military ports shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 62 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2004.
