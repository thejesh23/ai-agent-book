# Cleaner Production Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 29, 2002

Amended in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Cleaner Production Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on February 29, 2012

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of promoting cleaner production, increasing the efficiency of resource utilization, reducing and preventing the generation of pollutants, protecting and improving the environment, safeguarding human health, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "cleaner production" refers to the continuous application of measures such as improving design, using clean energy and raw materials, adopting advanced process technologies and equipment, improving management, and promoting comprehensive utilization, to cut pollution at the source, increase resource utilization efficiency, reduce or prevent the generation and discharge of pollutants during production, service, and product use, thereby mitigating or eliminating harm to human health and the environment.

Article 3 Within the territory of the People's Republic of China, entities engaged in production and service activities, as well as departments involved in related management activities, shall organize and implement cleaner production in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 4 The State encourages and promotes cleaner production. The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate cleaner production promotion into national economic and social development plans, annual plans, and plans for environmental protection, resource utilization, industrial development, and regional development.

Article 5 The comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production under the State Council shall be responsible for organizing and coordinating nationwide cleaner production promotion. The environmental protection, industry, science and technology, finance, and other relevant departments under the State Council shall, within their respective functions, be responsible for related cleaner production promotion work.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall lead cleaner production promotion within their respective administrative regions. The comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing and coordinating cleaner production promotion within its administrative region. Other relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions, be responsible for related cleaner production promotion work.

Article 6 The State encourages scientific research, technological development, and international cooperation on cleaner production, organizes the dissemination and popularization of cleaner production knowledge, and promotes cleaner production technologies.

The State encourages social organizations and the public to participate in the promotion, education, dissemination, implementation, and supervision of cleaner production.

## Chapter II Promotion of Cleaner Production

Article 7 The State Council shall formulate fiscal and tax policies conducive to the implementation of cleaner production.

The State Council and its relevant departments, as well as the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall formulate industrial policies and policies for technology development and promotion that are conducive to the implementation of cleaner production.

Article 8 The comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the environmental protection, industry, science and technology, and other relevant departments under the State Council, formulate a national cleaner production promotion plan based on the national economic and social development plan and the requirements for national resource conservation, energy consumption reduction, and reduction of key pollutant emissions. The plan shall be submitted to the State Council for approval and promptly published thereafter.

The national cleaner production promotion plan shall include: objectives, main tasks, and safeguard measures for promoting cleaner production; and identification of key areas, key industries, and key projects for implementing cleaner production based on resource and energy consumption levels and pollutant emission levels.

Relevant industry authorities under the State Council shall, based on the national cleaner production promotion plan, identify key cleaner production projects in their respective industries, formulate industry-specific cleaner production promotion plans, and organize their implementation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the national cleaner production promotion plan and relevant industry-specific cleaner production promotion plans, and in accordance with local requirements for resource conservation, energy consumption reduction, and reduction of key pollutant emissions, identify key cleaner production projects in their respective regions, formulate implementation plans for promoting cleaner production, and organize their implementation.

Article 9 The central budget shall strengthen funding for cleaner production promotion, including special funds for cleaner production from the central finance and other central budget allocations for cleaner production, to support the implementation of cleaner production and technology promotion in key areas, key industries, and key projects identified in the national cleaner production promotion plan, as well as projects for implementing cleaner production in ecologically fragile areas. Specific measures for the use of central budget funds for cleaner production promotion shall be formulated by the finance department and the comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production under the State Council, in conjunction with relevant departments under the State Council.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall coordinate local fiscal funds allocated for cleaner production promotion, guide social funds, and support key cleaner production projects.

Article 10 Relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall organize and support the establishment of information systems and technical advisory service systems for promoting cleaner production, and provide the public with information and services on cleaner production methods and technologies, the supply and demand of recyclable waste, and cleaner production policies.

Article 11 The comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the environmental protection, industry, science and technology, construction, agriculture, and other relevant departments under the State Council, periodically publish a guidance catalog of cleaner production technologies, processes, equipment, and products.

The comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production and the environmental protection department under the State Council, as well as the departments responsible for comprehensive coordination of cleaner production and the environmental protection departments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall, in conjunction with relevant departments at the same level, organize the formulation of cleaner production guidelines for key industries or regions to guide the implementation of cleaner production.

Article 12 The State implements a system of phasing out outdated production technologies, processes, equipment, and products that waste resources and seriously pollute the environment within a specified time limit. Relevant departments under the State Council shall, according to their respective functions, formulate and publish lists of production technologies, processes, equipment, and products to be phased out within a specified time limit.

Article 13 Relevant departments under the State Council may, as needed, approve the establishment of product labels for environmental and resource protection, such as energy conservation, water conservation, and waste recycling, and formulate corresponding standards in accordance with national regulations.

Article 14 The science and technology departments and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall guide and support the research and development of cleaner production technologies and products conducive to environmental and resource protection, as well as the demonstration and promotion of cleaner production technologies.

Article 15 The education department under the State Council shall incorporate cleaner production technology and management courses into relevant higher education, vocational education, and technical training systems.

Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall organize the dissemination and training of cleaner production to enhance the awareness of cleaner production among state functionaries, enterprise managers, and the public, and to cultivate cleaner production management and technical personnel.

News publishing, radio, film and television, cultural units, and relevant social organizations shall leverage their respective advantages to effectively carry out cleaner production publicity.

Article 16 People's governments at all levels shall give priority to the procurement of products conducive to environmental and resource protection, such as energy-saving, water-saving, and waste recycling products.

People's governments at all levels shall, through measures such as publicity and education, encourage the public to purchase and use products conducive to environmental and resource protection, such as energy-saving, water-saving, and waste recycling products.

Article 17 The department responsible for comprehensive coordination of cleaner production and the environmental protection department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, as needed for promoting cleaner production, publish in major local media the list of enterprises that have failed to meet energy consumption control indicators or key pollutant emission control indicators, providing a basis for public supervision of enterprises' implementation of cleaner production.

Enterprises included in the list specified in the preceding paragraph shall, in accordance with the regulations of the comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production and the environmental protection department under the State Council, publish their energy consumption or the generation and discharge of key pollutants, and accept public supervision.

## Chapter III Implementation of Cleaner Production

Article 18 New construction, renovation, and expansion projects shall undergo environmental impact assessment, analyzing and demonstrating the use of raw materials, resource consumption, comprehensive resource utilization, and the generation and disposal of pollutants, and giving priority to the adoption of cleaner production technologies, processes, and equipment with high resource utilization rates and low pollutant generation.

Article 19 Enterprises shall, during the process of technological transformation, adopt the following cleaner production measures:

(1) Using non-toxic, harmless, or low-toxicity, low-hazard raw materials to replace highly toxic and hazardous raw materials;

(2) Adopting processes and equipment with high resource utilization rates and low pollutant generation to replace those with low resource utilization rates and high pollutant generation;

(3) Comprehensively utilizing or recycling waste, wastewater, and waste heat generated during the production process;

(4) Adopting pollution prevention technologies that can meet national or local pollutant discharge standards and total pollutant discharge control indicators.

Article 20 The design of products and packaging shall consider their impact on human health and the environment throughout their life cycle, and priority shall be given to solutions that are non-toxic, harmless, easily degradable, or convenient for recycling.

Enterprises shall package their products reasonably. The material, structure, and cost of the packaging shall be commensurate with the quality, specifications, and cost of the packaged products, to reduce the generation of packaging waste, and excessive packaging shall not be permitted.

Article 21 Enterprises producing large-scale mechanical and electrical equipment, motor transport vehicles, and other products designated by the industry department under the State Council shall, in accordance with the technical specifications formulated by the standardization department under the State Council or its authorized body, indicate the standard grade of material composition on the main components of the products.

Article 22 Agricultural producers shall scientifically use chemical fertilizers, pesticides, agricultural films, and feed additives, improve planting and breeding techniques, achieve high-quality, harmless agricultural products and the resource utilization of agricultural production waste, and prevent agricultural environmental pollution.

The use of toxic or hazardous waste as fertilizer or for land reclamation is prohibited.

Article 23 Service enterprises such as catering, entertainment, and hotels shall adopt energy-saving, water-saving, and other environmentally friendly technologies and equipment, and reduce or refrain from using consumer goods that waste resources and pollute the environment.

Article 24 Construction projects shall adopt building design schemes, building and decoration materials, building components, and equipment that are conducive to environmental and resource protection, such as energy-saving and water-saving features.

Building and decoration materials must comply with national standards. The production, sale, and use of building and decoration materials containing toxic or hazardous substances exceeding national standards are prohibited.

Article 25 The exploration and mining of mineral resources shall adopt exploration and mining methods and process technologies conducive to the rational utilization of resources, environmental protection, and pollution prevention, to improve the level of resource utilization.

Article 26 Enterprises shall, under economically and technically feasible conditions, recover and utilize waste, waste heat, etc., generated during production and service processes themselves, or transfer them to other qualified enterprises or individuals for utilization.

Article 27 Enterprises shall monitor resource consumption and waste generation during production and service processes, and conduct cleaner production audits on production and services as needed.

Enterprises falling under any of the following circumstances shall undergo mandatory cleaner production audits:

(1) Pollutant discharge exceeds national or local discharge standards, or, although not exceeding national or local discharge standards, exceeds the total pollutant discharge control indicators for key pollutants;

(2) Exceeding the energy consumption quota per unit of product, constituting high energy consumption;

(3) Using toxic or hazardous raw materials for production or discharging toxic or hazardous substances during production.

Enterprises whose pollutant discharge exceeds national or local discharge standards shall take remedial measures in accordance with the provisions of relevant environmental protection laws.

Enterprises undergoing mandatory cleaner production audits shall report the audit results to the local department responsible for comprehensive coordination of cleaner production and the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level, and publish them in major local media to accept public supervision, except for matters involving trade secrets.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise the implementation of mandatory cleaner production audits by enterprises, and may, when necessary, organize assessments and acceptance inspections of the effectiveness of cleaner production implementation by enterprises. The required expenses shall be included in the government budget at the same level. The departments or units undertaking the assessment and acceptance inspection work shall not charge fees to the enterprises being assessed and accepted.

Specific measures for implementing cleaner production audits shall be formulated by the comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production and the environmental protection department under the State Council, in conjunction with relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 28 Enterprises other than those specified in paragraph 2 of Article 27 of this Law may voluntarily sign agreements with the comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production and the environmental protection department to further conserve resources and reduce pollutant emissions. The said comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production and the environmental protection department shall publish the names of such enterprises and their achievements in resource conservation and pollution prevention in major local media.

Article 29 Enterprises may, on a voluntary basis, in accordance with national regulations on environmental management system certification, entrust certification bodies recognized by the certification and accreditation supervision and administration department under the State Council to conduct certification, thereby improving their cleaner production level.

## Chapter IV Incentive Measures

Article 30 The State establishes a system of commendation and rewards for cleaner production. Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions in cleaner production work shall be commended and rewarded by the people's government.

Article 31 Financial support shall be provided by the people's government at or above the county level for engaging in cleaner production research, demonstration, and training, implementing key national cleaner production technological transformation projects, and implementing technological transformation projects specified in the voluntary agreements for resource conservation and pollutant emission reduction as stipulated in Article 28 of this Law.

Article 32 In the small and medium-sized enterprise development fund established in accordance with national regulations, an appropriate amount shall be allocated as needed to support small and medium-sized enterprises in implementing cleaner production.

Article 33 Enterprises that utilize waste and recover raw materials from waste to produce products shall enjoy tax incentives in accordance with national regulations.

Article 34 Expenses incurred by enterprises for cleaner production audits and training may be included in the operating costs of the enterprise.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 35 If the comprehensive coordination department for cleaner production or other relevant departments fail to perform their duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 36 In violation of paragraph 2 of Article 17 of this Law, if an enterprise fails to publish its energy consumption or the generation and discharge of key pollutants as required, the local department responsible for comprehensive coordination of cleaner production and the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective functions, order it to publish the information, and may impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 37 In violation of Article 21 of this Law, if an enterprise fails to mark the material composition of its products or marks it untruthfully, the local quality and technical supervision department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 38 In violation of paragraph 2 of Article 24 of this Law, if an enterprise produces or sells building and decoration materials containing toxic or hazardous substances exceeding national standards, administrative, civil, and criminal legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the Product Quality Law and relevant civil and criminal laws.

Article 39 In violation of paragraphs 2 and 4 of Article 27 of this Law, if an enterprise fails to conduct a mandatory cleaner production audit, engages in fraud during the cleaner production audit, or, being an enterprise subject to mandatory cleaner production audit, fails to report or truthfully report the audit results, the local department responsible for comprehensive coordination of cleaner production and the environmental protection department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective functions, order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

In violation of paragraph 5 of Article 27 of this Law, if a department or unit undertaking the assessment and acceptance inspection work, or its staff, charges fees to the enterprises being assessed and accepted, fails to conduct truthful assessment and acceptance, engages in fraud during the assessment and acceptance, or takes advantage of their position to seek benefits, the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 40 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2003.
