# Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 28, 1995

First Amendment on August 27, 2009 according to the Decision on Amending Certain Laws at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Second Amendment on April 24, 2015 according to the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Third Amendment on December 29, 2018 according to the Decision on Amending Four Laws Including the Electric Power Law of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure and promote the development of the electric power industry, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of electric power investors, operators and users, and ensure the safe operation of electric power.

Article 2 This Law applies to electric power construction, production, supply and consumption activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 The electric power industry shall meet the needs of national economic and social development and develop appropriately ahead of demand. The State encourages and guides domestic and foreign economic organizations and individuals to invest in the development of power sources and establish electric power production enterprises in accordance with the law.

Investment in the electric power industry shall follow the principle that the investor reaps the benefits.

Article 4 Electric power facilities are protected by the State.

No unit or individual may endanger the safety of electric power facilities or illegally occupy or use electric energy.

Article 5 Electric power construction, production, supply and consumption shall protect the environment in accordance with the law, adopt new technologies, reduce the emission of harmful substances, and prevent and control pollution and other public hazards.

The State encourages and supports the use of renewable energy and clean energy for power generation.

Article 6 The electric power administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the national electric power industry. The relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the electric power industry within their respective functions and duties.

The competent economic comprehensive department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be the electric power administrative department within its administrative region, responsible for the supervision and administration of the electric power industry. The relevant departments of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the electric power industry within their respective functions and duties.

Article 7 Electric power construction enterprises, electric power production enterprises, and power grid operation enterprises shall operate independently and assume sole responsibility for their profits and losses in accordance with the law, and shall accept the supervision of the electric power administrative department.

Article 8 The State assists and supports the development of the electric power industry in ethnic minority areas, remote areas and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 9 The State encourages the adoption of advanced science and technology and management methods in the process of electric power construction, production, supply and consumption, and awards units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in researching, developing and adopting advanced science and technology and management methods.

## Chapter II Electric Power Construction

Article 10 Electric power development plans shall be formulated according to the needs of national economic and social development and shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plan.

Electric power development plans shall reflect the principles of rational utilization of energy, coordinated development of power sources and power grids, improvement of economic efficiency, and facilitation of environmental protection.

Article 11 The construction and renovation plans for urban power grids shall be incorporated into the overall urban plan. The urban people's government shall, in accordance with the plan, arrange land for substation facilities, transmission line corridors and cable channels.

No unit or individual may illegally occupy land for substation facilities, transmission line corridors or cable channels.

Article 12 The State supports and promotes electric power construction through the formulation of relevant policies.

Local people's governments shall, in accordance with electric power development plans, adopt various measures tailored to local conditions to develop power sources and promote electric power construction.

Article 13 An electric power investor shall enjoy legal rights and interests in the electric power formed by its investment. For power connected to the grid, the investor has priority in use; for self-owned power plants not connected to the grid, the investor may dispose of and use the power independently.

Article 14 Electric power construction projects shall conform to electric power development plans and comply with the national electric power industry policy.

Electric power construction projects shall not use electric power equipment and technologies that are explicitly eliminated by the State.

Article 15 Power transmission and transformation projects, dispatching communication automation projects and other power grid supporting projects, as well as environmental protection projects, shall be designed, constructed, accepted and put into use simultaneously with the power generation project.

Article 16 The use of land for electric power construction projects shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations; where land is expropriated in accordance with the law, land compensation fees and resettlement subsidies shall be paid in accordance with the law, and the resettlement of relocated residents shall be properly carried out.

Electric power construction shall adhere to the principle of effectively protecting arable land and economizing on land use.

Local people's governments shall support and assist in the lawful use of land and the relocation of residents for the electric power industry.

Article 17 Local people's governments shall support electric power enterprises in exploring water sources and lawfully obtaining and using water for the construction of power generation projects. Electric power enterprises shall economize on water use.

## Chapter III Electric Power Production and Power Grid Management

Article 18 Electric power production and power grid operation shall follow the principles of safety, quality and economy.

Power grid operation shall be continuous and stable to ensure the reliability of power supply.

Article 19 Electric power enterprises shall strengthen safety production management, adhere to the policy of safety first and prevention foremost, and establish and improve the safety production responsibility system.

Electric power enterprises shall regularly inspect and maintain electric power facilities to ensure their normal operation.

Article 20 Fuel supply enterprises, transportation enterprises and electric power production enterprises shall supply, transport and receive fuel in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council or contractual agreements.

Article 21 Power grid operation shall implement unified dispatch and hierarchical management. No unit or individual may illegally interfere with power grid dispatch.

Article 22 The State advocates the parallel operation of electric power production enterprises with power grids, and of power grids with each other. Where an electric power production enterprise with independent legal personality requests to connect its generated power to the grid for parallel operation, the power grid operation enterprise shall accept the request.

Parallel operation must comply with national standards or electric power industry standards.

The parties to parallel operation shall sign a parallel operation agreement based on the principles of unified dispatch, hierarchical management, equality, mutual benefit, and consensus through consultation, to determine their respective rights and obligations; if the parties fail to reach an agreement, the electric power administrative department at or above the provincial level shall coordinate and make a decision.

Article 23 The measures for power grid dispatch management shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

## Chapter IV Electric Power Supply and Consumption

Article 24 The State implements the management principles of safe electricity use, economical electricity use, and planned electricity use for electric power supply and consumption.

The measures for electric power supply and consumption shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 25 Power supply enterprises shall supply electricity to users within their approved power supply business areas.

The division of power supply business areas shall take into account factors such as the structure of the power grid and the rationality of power supply. Only one power supply business institution shall be established within a power supply business area.

The establishment or alteration of a power supply business area shall be applied for by the power supply enterprise, and after review and approval by the electric power administrative department in conjunction with the relevant departments at the same level according to its functions and management authority, a "Power Business License" shall be issued. The specific measures for the establishment and alteration of power supply business areas shall be formulated by the electric power administrative department under the State Council.

Article 26 The power supply business institution within a power supply business area has the obligation to supply electricity to users within that business area in accordance with state regulations; it shall not refuse to supply electricity to units or individuals applying for electricity consumption within its business area in violation of state regulations.

Applications for new electricity installation, temporary electricity use, increase of electricity capacity, change of electricity consumption, and termination of electricity consumption shall go through the formalities in accordance with the prescribed procedures.

Power supply enterprises shall publicly announce the procedures, systems and fee standards for electricity consumption at their business premises, and provide information materials necessary for users.

Article 27 Both parties to electric power supply and consumption shall, based on the principles of equality, voluntariness, and consensus through consultation, sign a power supply and consumption contract in accordance with the measures for electric power supply and consumption formulated by the State Council, to determine their respective rights and obligations.

Article 28 Power supply enterprises shall ensure that the quality of power supplied to users meets national standards. Power quality problems caused by public power supply facilities shall be dealt with promptly.

If a user has special requirements for power quality, the power supply enterprise shall provide corresponding power based on the necessity of the requirement and the capability of the power grid.

Article 29 Under normal conditions of power generation and supply systems, power supply enterprises shall supply power to users continuously without interruption. When it is necessary to interrupt power supply due to reasons such as maintenance of power supply facilities, lawful power rationing, or illegal electricity consumption by users, the power supply enterprise shall notify users in advance in accordance with relevant state regulations.

If a user disagrees with the interruption of power supply by the power supply enterprise, it may file a complaint with the electric power administrative department; the electric power administrative department receiving the complaint shall handle it in accordance with the law.

Article 30 When emergency power supply is needed for disaster relief and rescue, the power supply enterprise must arrange for power supply as quickly as possible, and the costs of the required power supply project and the electricity charges payable shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 31 Users shall install electricity metering devices. The amount of electricity consumed by a user shall be determined based on the records of the electricity metering device lawfully verified by a metrological verification institution.

The design, construction, installation and operation management of a user's power receiving device shall comply with national standards or electric power industry standards.

Article 32 Users shall not endanger the safety of power supply and consumption or disrupt the order of power supply and consumption.

Power supply enterprises have the right to stop acts that endanger the safety of power supply and consumption or disrupt the order of power supply and consumption.

Article 33 Power supply enterprises shall calculate and collect electricity charges from users based on the electricity prices approved by the State and the records of the electricity metering devices.

When personnel of a power supply enterprise enter a user's premises for electricity safety inspections or meter reading and fee collection, they shall present relevant identification.

Users shall pay electricity charges on time based on the electricity prices approved by the State and the records of the electricity metering devices; they shall provide convenience for the personnel of the power supply enterprise to lawfully perform their duties of electricity safety inspections or meter reading and fee collection.

Article 34 Power supply enterprises and users shall comply with relevant state regulations and take effective measures to ensure safe electricity use, economical electricity use, and planned electricity use.

## Chapter V Electricity Prices and Charges

Article 35 For the purposes of this Law, electricity prices refer to the on-grid prices of electric power production enterprises, the mutual supply prices between power grids, and the sales prices of power grids.

Electricity prices shall implement a unified policy, unified pricing principles, and hierarchical management.

Article 36 The formulation of electricity prices shall reasonably compensate costs, reasonably determine profits, include taxes in accordance with the law, adhere to fair burden-sharing, and promote electric power construction.

Article 37 On-grid prices shall implement the principle of same quality, same price for the same grid. The specific measures and implementation steps shall be formulated by the State Council.

Where an electric power production enterprise has special circumstances requiring separate on-grid prices, the specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 38 On-grid prices within inter-provincial, autonomous region, and municipal grids and provincial-level grids shall be proposed through consultation between the electric power production enterprise and the power grid operation enterprise, and submitted to the price administrative department under the State Council for approval.

On-grid prices within independent grids shall be proposed through consultation between the electric power production enterprise and the power grid operation enterprise, and submitted to the price administrative department with management authority for approval.

For electricity produced by locally-invested electric power production enterprises that forms an independent grid within a province or is self-generated and self-consumed, the electricity prices may be managed by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 39 Mutual supply prices between inter-provincial, autonomous region, and municipal grids and independent grids, and between provincial-level grids and independent grids, shall be proposed through consultation between the parties, and submitted to the price administrative department under the State Council or its authorized department for approval.

Mutual supply prices between independent grids shall be proposed through consultation between the parties, and submitted to the price administrative department with management authority for approval.

Article 40 Sales prices of inter-provincial, autonomous region, and municipal grids and provincial-level grids shall be proposed by the power grid operation enterprise, and submitted to the price administrative department under the State Council or its authorized department for approval.

Sales prices of independent grids shall be proposed by the power grid operation enterprise, and submitted to the price administrative department with management authority for approval.

Article 41 The State implements classified electricity prices and time-of-use electricity prices. The classification standards and time-of-use methods shall be determined by the State Council.

Users within the same grid, at the same voltage level, and of the same electricity consumption category shall be subject to the same electricity price standard.

Article 42 The fee standards for user electricity capacity increases shall be formulated by the price administrative department under the State Council jointly with the electric power administrative department under the State Council.

Article 43 No unit may set electricity prices beyond the authority of electricity price management. Power supply enterprises shall not change electricity prices without authorization.

Article 44 No unit or individual may add other charges to electricity bills; however, where laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Where local funds are raised for power construction and charges are added to electricity bills, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall formulate measures in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Power supply enterprises are prohibited from collecting other charges on behalf of others when collecting electricity bills.

Article 45 The measures for the management of electricity prices shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

## Chapter VI Rural Electric Power Construction and Agricultural Electricity Use

Article 46 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate rural electrification development plans and incorporate them into their local electric power development plans and national economic and social development plans.

Article 47 The State implements preferential policies for rural electrification and provides key support for rural electric power construction in ethnic minority areas, remote areas and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 48 The State advocates the development of hydropower resources in rural areas, the construction of medium and small hydropower stations, and the promotion of rural electrification.

The State encourages and supports the use of solar energy, wind energy, geothermal energy, biomass energy and other energy sources for rural power source construction to increase rural power supply.

Article 49 When arranging electricity consumption indicators, the local people's government at or above the county level and its competent economic comprehensive department shall ensure an appropriate proportion for agricultural and rural electricity use, and give priority to ensuring electricity for rural drainage and irrigation, drought relief, and seasonal agricultural production.

Electric power enterprises shall implement the electricity consumption arrangements mentioned in the preceding paragraph and shall not reduce the indicators for agricultural and rural electricity use.

Article 50 Agricultural electricity prices shall be determined based on the principle of covering costs and making a small profit.

The electricity prices for the daily life of farmers and local urban residents shall gradually be unified.

Article 51 The measures for the management of agricultural and rural electricity use shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

## Chapter VII Protection of Electric Power Facilities

Article 52 No unit or individual may endanger power generation facilities, substation facilities, power line facilities and their related auxiliary facilities.

Blasting operations and other operations that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities in the vicinity of such facilities shall only be carried out after obtaining approval and taking measures to ensure the safety of the electric power facilities, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council on the protection of electric power facilities.

Article 53 The electric power administrative department shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council on the protection of electric power facilities, set up signs in the protection zones of electric power facilities.

No unit or individual may construct buildings or structures that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities, plant plants that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities, or stack items that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities within the legally designated electric power facility protection zones.

Plants that were planted before the legal designation of the electric power facility protection zone and that hinder the safety of electric power facilities shall be trimmed or felled.

Article 54 Where any unit or individual needs to carry out operations that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities within the legally designated electric power facility protection zone, such operations shall only be carried out after obtaining approval from the electric power administrative department and taking safety measures.

Article 55 Where electric power facilities and public works, landscaping projects, or other projects interfere with each other during new construction, renovation or expansion, the relevant units shall negotiate in accordance with relevant state regulations and may only proceed with construction after reaching an agreement.

## Chapter VIII Supervision and Inspection

Article 56 The electric power administrative department shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the compliance of electric power enterprises and users with electric power laws and administrative regulations.

Article 57 The electric power administrative department may, as needed, appoint electric power supervision and inspection personnel.

Electric power supervision and inspection personnel shall be impartial and honest, enforce the law impartially, be familiar with electric power laws and regulations, and possess relevant professional electric power technology.

Article 58 When conducting supervision and inspection, electric power supervision and inspection personnel have the right to inquire about the compliance of electric power enterprises or users with electric power laws and administrative regulations, inspect relevant materials, and have the right to enter the site for inspection.

Electric power enterprises and users shall provide convenience for electric power supervision and inspection personnel performing supervision and inspection tasks.

Electric power supervision and inspection personnel shall present their identification when conducting supervision and inspection.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

Article 59 If an electric power enterprise or user violates a power supply and consumption contract and causes losses to the other party, it shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

If an electric power enterprise violates the provisions of Article 28 or the first paragraph of Article 29 of this Law, fails to ensure power quality or fails to notify users in advance of an interruption of power supply, and causes losses to users, it shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 60 If an electric power operation accident causes damage to a user or a third party, the electric power enterprise shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

If an electric power operation accident is caused by any of the following reasons, the electric power enterprise shall not bear liability for compensation:

(1) Force majeure;

(2) The fault of the user itself.

If damage is caused to an electric power enterprise or other users due to the fault of a user or a third party, that user or third party shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 61 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 11 of this Law, illegally occupies land for substation facilities, transmission line corridors or cable channels, shall be ordered by the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections within a specified time limit; if no correction is made within the time limit, the obstacles shall be forcibly removed.

Article 62 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 14 of this Law, constructs an electric power project that does not conform to the electric power development plan or industrial policy, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to stop construction.

Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 14 of this Law, uses electric power equipment and technologies explicitly eliminated by the State in an electric power construction project, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to stop using them, the electric power equipment explicitly eliminated by the State shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 63 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 25 of this Law, engages in power supply or alters a power supply business area without permission, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to make corrections, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than five times the illegal gains may be imposed.

Article 64 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 26 or Article 29 of this Law, refuses to supply power or interrupts power supply, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to make corrections and given a warning; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the persons directly in charge and other persons directly responsible.

Article 65 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 32 of this Law, endangers the safety of power supply and consumption or disrupts the order of power supply and consumption, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to make corrections and given a warning; if the circumstances are serious or the party refuses to make corrections, the power supply may be suspended, and a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed.Article 66 Any entity that, in violation of Articles 33, 43, and 44 of this Law, fails to charge users according to the electricity price approved by the state and the records of electricity metering devices, sets electricity prices beyond its authority, or adds other fees to the electricity charge, shall be warned by the competent administrative department of prices, ordered to refund the illegally collected fees, and may be fined up to five times the amount of the illegally collected fees; if the circumstances are serious, the persons in charge and the persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions.

Article 67 Any entity that, in violation of Paragraph 2 of Article 49 of this Law, reduces the electricity consumption targets for agriculture and rural areas, shall be ordered by the electric power management department to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, the persons in charge and the persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions; if losses are caused, the entity shall be ordered to compensate for the losses.
Article 68 Any entity that, in violation of Article 52, Paragraph 2, and Article 54 of this Law, conducts operations around electric power facilities or within the legally designated protection zones for electric power facilities without approval or without taking safety measures, thereby endangering the safety of electric power facilities, shall be ordered by the electric power administrative department to stop the operations, restore the original state, and compensate for the losses.
Article 69 Any entity that, in violation of Article 53 of this Law, constructs buildings or structures, plants vegetation, or stacks objects within the legally designated protection zones for electric power facilities, thereby endangering the safety of electric power facilities, shall be ordered by the local people's government to forcibly demolish, cut down, or remove them.

Article 70 Anyone who commits any of the following acts, if subject to public security penalties, shall be punished by the public security organs in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Obstructing electric power construction or emergency repairs of electric power facilities, thereby preventing the normal progress of such construction or repairs;

(2) Disturbing the order of electric power production enterprises, substations, electric power dispatching institutions, and power supply enterprises, thereby preventing the normal conduct of production, work, and business;

(3) Assaulting or publicly insulting electricity inspectors or meter readers and fee collectors who are performing their duties;

(4) Refusing or obstructing electric power supervision and inspection personnel from performing their duties in accordance with the law.

Article 71 Anyone who steals electric energy shall be ordered by the electric power management department to cease the illegal act, shall be required to pay the electricity charges owed, and may be fined up to five times the amount of the electricity charges payable; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.
Article 72 Anyone who steals electric power facilities or destroys them by other means, thereby endangering public safety, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 73 Staff members of the electric power management department who abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.
Article 74 Employees of electric power enterprises who violate rules and regulations, engage in illegal dispatching, or fail to comply with dispatching instructions, thereby causing a major accident, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Employees of electric power enterprises who intentionally delay emergency repairs of electric power facilities or the supply of electricity for disaster relief, thereby causing serious consequences, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Management personnel of electric power enterprises, as well as electricity inspectors and meter readers and fee collectors, who extort users or seek personal gain through electricity, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 75 This Law shall come into force on April 1, 1996.
