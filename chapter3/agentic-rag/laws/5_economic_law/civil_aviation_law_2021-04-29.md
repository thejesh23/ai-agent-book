# Civil Aviation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 30, 1995

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to safeguard the sovereignty of the airspace and the civil aviation rights of the People's Republic of China, ensure the safety and orderly conduct of civil aviation activities, protect the legitimate rights and interests of the parties involved in civil aviation activities, and promote the development of the civil aviation industry.

Article 2 The airspace over the land territory and territorial waters of the People's Republic of China constitutes the territorial airspace of the People's Republic of China. The People's Republic of China enjoys complete and exclusive sovereignty over its territorial airspace.

Article 3 The competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and administration over civil aviation activities throughout the country; it shall, within the scope of its functions and powers and in accordance with laws and decisions of the State Council, issue regulations and decisions concerning civil aviation activities.

The regional civil aviation administration authorities established by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall, pursuant to the authorization of the said authority, supervise and administer civil aviation activities within their respective regions.

Article 4 The State supports the development of the civil aviation industry, encourages and supports scientific research and education in civil aviation, and promotes the advancement of civil aviation science and technology.

The State supports the development of the civil aircraft manufacturing industry to provide safe, advanced, economical, and suitable civil aircraft for civil aviation activities.

## Chapter II Nationality of Civil Aircraft

Article 5 For the purposes of this Law, "civil aircraft" means any aircraft other than those used for military, customs, or police service.

Article 6 A civil aircraft that has been registered for nationality with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council in accordance with the law shall have the nationality of the People's Republic of China, and the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall issue a nationality registration certificate therefor.

The competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall maintain a Register of Nationality of Civil Aircraft of the People's Republic of China, in which matters concerning the nationality registration of civil aircraft shall be uniformly recorded.

Article 7 The following civil aircraft shall undergo nationality registration in the People's Republic of China:

(1) Civil aircraft owned by state organs of the People's Republic of China;

(2) Civil aircraft owned by enterprise legal persons established in accordance with the laws of the People's Republic of China; where the registered capital of an enterprise legal person includes foreign investment, its organizational structure, personnel composition, and the proportion of capital contribution by Chinese investors shall comply with the provisions of administrative regulations;

(3) Other civil aircraft approved for registration by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Where a civil aircraft is leased from abroad, if the lessee meets the requirements of the preceding paragraph and the crew of the civil aircraft is provided by the lessee, an application may be made for registration of the nationality of the People's Republic of China, provided that the original nationality registration of the civil aircraft is first cancelled.

Article 8 A civil aircraft that has acquired the nationality of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law shall display the prescribed nationality marks and registration marks.

Article 9 No civil aircraft may have dual nationality. A civil aircraft that has not cancelled its foreign nationality registration may not apply for nationality registration in the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter III Rights in Civil Aircraft

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 10 The rights in civil aircraft provided for in this Chapter include rights in the airframe, engines, propellers, radio apparatus, and all other articles intended for use on the civil aircraft, whether installed thereon or temporarily detached therefrom.

Article 11 The holder of a right in a civil aircraft shall register the following rights respectively with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council:

(1) Ownership of the civil aircraft;

(2) The right to acquire and possess a civil aircraft through purchase;

(3) The right to possess a civil aircraft under a lease agreement with a term of six months or more;

(4) A mortgage over the civil aircraft.

Article 12 The competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall maintain a Register of Rights in Civil Aircraft. Matters concerning the registration of rights in the same civil aircraft shall be recorded in the same register.

Matters recorded in the Register of Rights in Civil Aircraft shall be available for public inspection, reproduction, or extraction.

Article 13 Except where a civil aircraft is sold by compulsory auction in accordance with the law, the nationality registration or rights registration of the civil aircraft shall not be transferred abroad until the registered rights in the civil aircraft have been satisfied or the holder of the rights in the civil aircraft has consented.

### Section 2 Ownership and Mortgage of Civil Aircraft

Article 14 The acquisition, transfer, and extinction of ownership of a civil aircraft shall be registered with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council; without registration, it shall not be valid against third parties.

The transfer of ownership of a civil aircraft shall be effected by a written contract.

Article 15 Where a civil aircraft owned by the State is granted by the State to a legal person for operation or use, the provisions of this Law concerning the owner of a civil aircraft shall apply to such legal person.

Article 16 To create a mortgage over a civil aircraft, the mortgagee and the mortgagor shall jointly register the mortgage with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council; without registration, it shall not be valid against third parties.

Article 17 After a mortgage over a civil aircraft is created, the mortgagor shall not transfer the mortgaged civil aircraft to another person without the consent of the mortgagee.

### Section 3 Priority Rights in Civil Aircraft

Article 18 A priority right in a civil aircraft means the right of a creditor, in accordance with the provisions of Article 19 of this Law, to claim compensation from the owner or lessee of the civil aircraft and to be paid preferentially out of the proceeds of the civil aircraft giving rise to the claim.

Article 19 The following claims shall be secured by a priority right in a civil aircraft:

(1) Remuneration for the salvage of the civil aircraft;

(2) Necessary expenses for the preservation and maintenance of the civil aircraft.

Among the claims specified in the preceding paragraph, those arising later shall be paid first.

Article 20 A creditor holding a priority right in a civil aircraft as specified in Article 19 of this Law shall register its claim with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council within three months from the date of completion of the salvage or preservation and maintenance work.

Article 21 Expenses incurred in the execution of a judgment of a people's court and in the process of auction for the common benefit of the creditors shall be paid first from the proceeds of the auction of the civil aircraft.

Article 22 A priority right in a civil aircraft shall be satisfied before a mortgage over the civil aircraft.

Article 23 Where a claim specified in Article 19 of this Law is transferred, the priority right in the civil aircraft securing it shall be transferred accordingly.

Article 24 A priority right in a civil aircraft shall be exercised by the seizure of the civil aircraft giving rise to the priority right by a people's court.

Article 25 A priority right in a civil aircraft shall terminate upon the expiry of three months from the date of completion of the salvage or preservation and maintenance work; provided that this shall not apply where the creditor has registered its claim in accordance with Article 20 of this Law and one of the following circumstances exists:

(1) The creditor and the debtor have reached an agreement on the amount of the claim;

(2) Legal proceedings concerning the claim have been commenced.

A priority right in a civil aircraft shall not be extinguished by the transfer of ownership of the civil aircraft; provided that this shall not apply where the civil aircraft is sold by compulsory auction in accordance with the law.

### Section 4 Leasing of Civil Aircraft

Article 26 A contract for the lease of a civil aircraft, including a financial lease contract and other lease contracts, shall be concluded in writing.

Article 27 A financial lease of a civil aircraft means that the lessor, in accordance with the lessee's choice of the supplier and the civil aircraft, purchases the civil aircraft and leases it to the lessee for use, with the lessee paying rent periodically.

Article 28 During the term of a financial lease, the lessor shall enjoy ownership of the civil aircraft in accordance with the law, and the lessee shall enjoy the rights to possess, use, and benefit from the civil aircraft in accordance with the law.

Article 29 During the term of a financial lease, the lessor shall not interfere with the lessee's lawful possession and use of the civil aircraft; the lessee shall properly maintain the civil aircraft and keep it in the condition in which it was delivered, except for reasonable wear and tear and changes to the civil aircraft agreed to by the lessor.

Article 30 Upon the expiry of a financial lease, the lessee shall return the civil aircraft to the lessor in the condition specified in Article 29 of this Law; provided that this shall not apply where the lessee exercises its right to purchase the civil aircraft under the contract or retains possession of the civil aircraft for continued leasing.

Article 31 In a financial lease of a civil aircraft, the supplier shall not be liable to both the lessor and the lessee for the same damage.

Article 32 During the term of a financial lease, with the consent of the lessor and provided that the interests of third parties are not prejudiced, the lessee may transfer its right to possess the civil aircraft or other rights stipulated in the lease contract.

Article 33 In the case of a financial lease of a civil aircraft or other lease with a term of six months or more, the lessee shall register its right to possess the civil aircraft with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council; without registration, it shall not be valid against third parties.

## Chapter IV Airworthiness Management of Civil Aircraft

Article 34 The design of a civil aircraft, its engines, propellers, and equipment on board the civil aircraft shall be subject to an application to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for a type certificate. Upon examination and approval, a type certificate shall be issued.

Article 35 The manufacture and maintenance of a civil aircraft, its engines, propellers, and equipment on board the civil aircraft shall be subject to an application to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for a production certificate or a maintenance certificate. Upon examination and approval, the corresponding certificate shall be issued.

Article 36 Where a civil aircraft of any type, its engines, propellers, and equipment on board the civil aircraft manufactured by a foreign manufacturer are imported into China for the first time, the foreign manufacturer shall apply to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for a type acceptance certificate. Upon examination and approval, a type acceptance certificate shall be issued.

Where a civil aircraft, its engines, propellers, and equipment on board the civil aircraft for which a type certificate has been issued by a foreign country are manufactured in China for the first time, the holder of the type certificate shall apply to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for a type acceptance certificate. Upon examination and approval, a type acceptance certificate shall be issued.

Article 37 A civil aircraft having the nationality of the People's Republic of China shall hold an airworthiness certificate issued by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council before it may fly.

For the export of a civil aircraft, its engines, propellers, and equipment on board the civil aircraft, the manufacturer shall apply to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for an export airworthiness certificate. Upon examination and approval, an export airworthiness certificate shall be issued.

A leased foreign civil aircraft may fly only after the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council has examined and accepted the airworthiness certificate issued by the country of its original nationality registration or has issued a new airworthiness certificate.

Regulations on the administration of airworthiness of civil aircraft shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 38 The owner or lessee of a civil aircraft shall use the civil aircraft within the scope of use specified in the airworthiness certificate, carry out proper maintenance and repair of the civil aircraft, and ensure that the civil aircraft is in an airworthy condition.

## Chapter V Aviation Personnel

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 39 For the purposes of this Law, "aviation personnel" means the following flight crew and ground personnel engaged in civil aviation activities:

(1) Flight crew, including pilots, flight engineers, and cabin crew;

(2) Ground personnel, including civil aircraft maintenance personnel, air traffic controllers, flight dispatchers, and aeronautical station operators.

Article 40 Aviation personnel shall undergo specialized training and, upon passing the examination and obtaining a license issued by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, may perform the duties specified in their license.

Before obtaining a license, flight crew and air traffic controllers shall also undergo a physical examination by a medical unit recognized by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council and obtain a medical certificate issued by the said authority.

Article 41 When performing flight duties, flight crew shall carry their license and medical certificate with them and submit them for inspection by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Article 42 Aviation personnel shall undergo periodic or unscheduled checks and examinations by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council; only those who pass the checks and examinations may continue to perform the duties specified in their license.

Flight crew shall also participate in periodic emergency procedure training.

Where a flight crew member's period of absence from flying exceeds the time limit prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, they shall undergo checks and examinations; flight crew members other than cabin crew shall also undergo supervised flying. Only those who pass the checks, examinations, and supervised flying may continue to perform the duties specified in their license.

### Section 2 Crew

Article 43 The crew of a civil aircraft shall consist of the pilot-in-command and other flight crew members. The pilot-in-command shall be a pilot possessing the technical skills and experience to independently operate the type of civil aircraft.

The composition and number of the crew shall comply with the provisions of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Article 44 The operation of a civil aircraft shall be the responsibility of the pilot-in-command, who shall strictly perform their duties to ensure the safety of the civil aircraft and the persons and property on board.

All persons on board the civil aircraft shall obey the orders issued by the pilot-in-command within the scope of their authority.

Article 45 Before flight, the pilot-in-command shall carry out the necessary inspection of the civil aircraft; the aircraft shall not take off without such inspection.

If the pilot-in-command finds that the civil aircraft, airport, meteorological conditions, etc., do not comply with regulations and cannot ensure flight safety, they have the right to refuse to take off.

Article 46 During flight, the pilot-in-command has the right to take necessary and appropriate measures, while ensuring safety, against any act that damages the civil aircraft, disrupts order within the civil aircraft, endangers the safety of persons or property on board the civil aircraft, or otherwise jeopardizes flight safety.

During flight, in the event of special circumstances, the pilot-in-command has the right to take measures concerning the civil aircraft to ensure the safety of the civil aircraft and the persons on board.

Article 47 If the pilot-in-command finds that a crew member is unfit to perform flight duties, they have the right to propose adjustments to ensure flight safety.

Article 48 When a civil aircraft is in distress, the pilot-in-command has the right to take all necessary measures and to direct the crew and other persons on board the aircraft to take rescue measures. In an emergency requiring evacuation of the civil aircraft in distress, the pilot-in-command must take measures to first organize the safe evacuation of passengers from the civil aircraft; crew members shall not leave the civil aircraft without the permission of the pilot-in-command; the pilot-in-command shall be the last to leave the civil aircraft.

Article 49 In the event of an accident involving a civil aircraft, the pilot-in-command shall promptly report the circumstances of the accident truthfully to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, either directly or through the air traffic control unit.

Article 50 Upon receiving a distress signal from a vessel or other aircraft, or upon discovering a vessel, aircraft, or persons in distress, the pilot-in-command shall promptly report the distress situation to the nearest air traffic control unit and render such reasonable assistance as may be possible.

Article 51 If, during flight, the pilot-in-command is unable to perform their duties for any reason, the pilot next in rank shall act as pilot-in-command; before takeoff from the next place of landing, the owner or lessee of the civil aircraft shall appoint a new pilot-in-command to take over.

Article 52 For a civil aircraft operated by a single pilot without the need for other flight crew members, the provisions of this Section concerning the pilot-in-command shall apply to that pilot.

## Chapter VI Civil Airports

Article 53 For the purposes of this Law, "civil airport" means a defined area, including any buildings, installations, and facilities ancillary thereto, intended for use wholly or partly for the arrival, departure, taxiing, parking, and other activities of civil aircraft.

For the purposes of this Law, "civil airport" does not include temporary airports.

Separate regulations for the administration of airports used jointly by the military and civil aviation shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 54 The construction and use of civil airports shall be subject to overall planning and rational layout to enhance the utilization efficiency of airports.

The national layout and construction plan for civil airports shall be formulated by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council jointly with other relevant departments under the State Council, and shall be implemented after approval in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the State.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with the national layout and construction plan for civil airports, formulate civil airport construction plans for their respective administrative regions, and after submitting them for approval in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the State, incorporate them into their respective plans for national economic and social development.

Article 55 Civil airport construction plans shall be coordinated with urban construction plans.

Article 56 The construction, reconstruction, and expansion of civil airports shall conform to the layout and construction plan for civil airports formulated in accordance with the law, comply with civil airport standards, and be submitted for approval to the relevant competent authorities and implemented in accordance with the provisions of the State.

Civil airport construction projects that do not conform to the layout and construction plan for civil airports formulated in accordance with the law shall not be approved.

Article 57 The construction or expansion of a civil airport shall be announced by the local people's government at or above the county level in the area where the civil airport is located.

The announcement specified in the preceding paragraph shall be published in major local newspapers and posted in the areas surrounding the proposed airport to be constructed or expanded.

Article 58 The following activities are prohibited within the defined boundaries of a civil airport and within the airport obstacle limitation surfaces area designated in accordance with the provisions of the State:

(1) Constructing buildings or facilities that may emit large quantities of smoke, dust, flames, or exhaust gases into the air, thereby affecting flight safety;

(2) Constructing buildings or facilities such as shooting ranges or warehouses for highly explosive materials that affect flight safety;

(3) Constructing buildings or facilities that do not meet the obstacle limitation requirements of the airport;

(4) Installing lights, signs, or objects that affect the use of visual aids for navigation at the airport;

(5) Planting vegetation that affects flight safety or the use of airport navigation aids;

(6) Raising or releasing birds or other objects that affect flight safety;(7) Constructing buildings or facilities that affect the electromagnetic environment of an airport.

Raising livestock within the legally designated area of a civil airport is prohibited.

Article 59: Buildings, structures, trees, lights, and other obstacles that existed within the legally designated area of a civil airport and the airport clearance protection area delineated in accordance with national regulations before the announcement of the construction or expansion of a civil airport, and that may affect flight safety, shall be cleared within the prescribed period; compensation shall be made for losses caused thereby, or other remedial measures shall be taken in accordance with the law.

Article 60: After the announcement of the construction or expansion of a civil airport, if any entity or individual, in violation of this Law and relevant administrative regulations, constructs, plants, or installs buildings, structures, trees, lights, or other obstacles within the legally designated area of a civil airport and the airport clearance protection area delineated in accordance with national regulations that affect flight safety, the local people's government at or above the county level where the airport is located shall order the removal thereof; any losses caused thereby shall be borne by the person who constructed, planted, or installed such obstacles.

Article 61: For tall buildings or facilities outside a civil airport and its clearance protection area delineated in accordance with national regulations that may affect flight safety, flight obstacle lights and markers shall be installed and maintained in normal condition in accordance with relevant national regulations.

Article 62: Civil airports open to the public as prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall obtain an airport use permit before they can be opened for use. Other civil airports shall file for record in accordance with the regulations of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

To apply for an airport use permit, the following conditions must be met and acceptance checks passed in accordance with national regulations:

(1) Possession of flight areas, terminal areas, work areas, service facilities, and personnel appropriate to their operational business;
(2) Possession of air traffic control, communication navigation, meteorological, and other facilities and personnel capable of ensuring flight safety;
(3) Possession of security conditions that meet national requirements;
(4) Possession of emergency plans for handling special situations, along with corresponding facilities and personnel;
(5) Possession of other conditions prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

International airports shall also meet conditions for international navigation and establish customs and other port inspection authorities.

Article 63: The airport use permit shall be applied for by the airport management authority to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and shall be issued after examination and approval by the said authority.

Article 64: The establishment of an international airport shall be submitted by the provincial people's government where the airport is located to the State Council for examination and approval.

The opening of an international airport for use shall be publicly announced by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council; information on international airports shall be uniformly provided externally by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Article 65: Civil airports shall, in accordance with the regulations of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, take measures to ensure the safety of persons and property within the airport.

Article 66: Civil airports used by civil aircraft for transporting passengers or cargo shall, in accordance with the standards prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, install necessary facilities to provide good services to passengers, cargo consignors, and consignees.

Article 67: The management authority of a civil airport shall, in accordance with the provisions of environmental protection laws and administrative regulations, carry out airport environmental protection work.

Article 68: Civil aircraft using civil airports and their navigational aids shall pay usage fees and service fees; the charging standards for usage fees and service fees shall be formulated by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Article 69: If a civil airport is to be abandoned or used for other purposes, the airport management authority shall go through the procedures for submission and approval in accordance with national regulations.

## Chapter VII: Air Navigation

### Section 1: Airspace Management

Article 70: The State exercises unified management over airspace.

Article 71: The division of airspace shall take into account the needs of civil aviation and national defense security as well as the public interest, so that airspace is utilized reasonably, fully, and effectively.

Article 72: Specific measures for airspace management shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

### Section 2: Flight Management

Article 73: Within a designated controlled airspace, one air traffic control unit is responsible for the air traffic control of aircraft within that airspace.

Article 74: Civil aircraft conducting flight activities within controlled airspace shall obtain permission from the air traffic control unit.

Article 75: Civil aircraft shall fly along the routes and at the altitudes designated by the air traffic control unit; if it is necessary to deviate from the designated route or change the flight altitude for justifiable reasons, permission from the air traffic control unit shall be obtained.

Article 76: Aircraft flying within the territory of the People's Republic of China must comply with uniform flight rules.

Civil aircraft conducting visual flight shall comply with visual flight rules and maintain a safe distance from other aircraft and ground obstacles.

Civil aircraft conducting instrument flight shall comply with instrument flight rules.

Flight rules shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 77: The flight time and duty time of crew members of civil aircraft shall not exceed the time limits prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Crew members of civil aircraft who are impaired in their ability to work due to the influence of alcoholic beverages, narcotics, or other drugs shall not perform flight duties.

Article 78: Civil aircraft shall not fly into prohibited areas unless specially approved in accordance with national regulations; they shall not fly into restricted areas except under the prescribed restrictive conditions.

The prohibited areas and restricted areas mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be delineated in accordance with national regulations.

Article 79: Civil aircraft shall not fly over urban areas, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Necessary for takeoff, landing, or designated routes;
(2) The flight altitude is sufficient to allow the aircraft to leave the urban area in an emergency without endangering the safety of persons and property on the ground;
(3) Approved in accordance with procedures prescribed by national regulations.

Article 80: During flight, civil aircraft shall not drop objects, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Necessary for flight safety;
(2) Necessary for carrying out rescue missions or other flight missions in the public interest.

Article 81: Civil aircraft shall not fly out of the territorial airspace of the People's Republic of China without approval.

For civil aircraft that are flying out of the territorial airspace of the People's Republic of China without approval, the relevant authorities have the right to take necessary measures to stop them according to the specific circumstances.

### Section 3: Flight Support

Article 82: Air traffic control units shall provide air traffic services to civil aircraft in flight, including air traffic control services, flight information services, and alerting services.

The provision of air traffic control services aims to prevent collisions between civil aircraft and other aircraft, and between civil aircraft and obstacles, and to maintain and expedite the orderly flow of air traffic.

The provision of flight information services aims to provide information and advice useful for the safe and efficient conduct of flights.

The provision of alerting services aims to notify relevant authorities when a civil aircraft requires search and rescue, and to assist such authorities in conducting search and rescue as requested.

Article 83: When an air traffic control unit discovers that a civil aircraft has deviated from its designated route or lost its bearings, it shall promptly take all necessary measures to bring it back onto the route.

Article 84: Necessary navigation, communication, meteorological, and ground surveillance equipment shall be installed on air routes.

Article 85: Natural obstacles affecting flight safety on air routes shall be marked on aeronautical charts; artificial obstacles affecting flight safety on air routes shall be equipped with flight obstacle lights and markers, and kept in normal condition.

Article 86: Within a zone of thirty kilometers from the boundary of an air route, the construction of shooting ranges and other facilities that may affect flight safety is prohibited; however, this does not apply to flat-trajectory light weapon shooting ranges.

The construction of fixed or temporary anti-aircraft launching sites outside the zone mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be approved in accordance with national regulations; the launch direction of anti-aircraft launching sites shall not intersect with the air route.

Article 87: Any activity that may affect flight safety shall be approved in accordance with the law, and necessary measures to ensure flight safety shall be taken before it is carried out.

Article 88: The competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, manage the civil aviation radio stations and the dedicated frequencies allocated for use by the civil aviation system.

Radio stations and other instruments and devices used by any entity or individual shall not interfere with the normal use of dedicated civil aviation radio frequencies. If harmful interference is caused to dedicated civil aviation radio frequencies, the relevant entity or individual shall promptly eliminate the interference; before the interference is eliminated, the use of such radio station or other instrument or device shall be suspended.

Article 89: Postal and telecommunications enterprises shall give priority to providing services for civil aviation telecommunications transmission.

State meteorological agencies shall provide necessary meteorological data to civil aviation meteorological agencies.

### Section 4: Documents Required for Flight

Article 90: Civil aircraft engaged in flight shall carry the following documents:

(1) Certificate of nationality registration of the civil aircraft;
(2) Airworthiness certificate of the civil aircraft;
(3) Appropriate licenses of the crew members;
(4) Journey log book of the civil aircraft;
(5) For civil aircraft equipped with radio equipment, the radio station license;
(6) For civil aircraft carrying passengers, a list of the names of the passengers carried and their places of departure and destination;
(7) For civil aircraft carrying cargo, the manifest and detailed declaration of the cargo carried;
(8) Other documents that should be carried according to the flight mission.

If a civil aircraft fails to carry the documents listed in the preceding paragraph as required, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council or its authorized regional civil aviation administration may prohibit the civil aircraft from taking off.

## Chapter VIII: Public Air Transport Enterprises

Article 91: A public air transport enterprise refers to an enterprise legal person that, for profit, uses civil aircraft to transport passengers, baggage, mail, or cargo.

Article 92: An enterprise engaging in public air transport shall apply to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for an operating license.

Article 93: To obtain a public air transport operating license, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Possession of civil aircraft that meet national requirements for ensuring flight safety;
(2) Possession of necessary aviation personnel who have obtained licenses in accordance with the law;
(3) Possession of registered capital not less than the minimum amount prescribed by the State Council;
(4) Other conditions prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 94: The form of organization and organizational structure of a public air transport enterprise shall be governed by the provisions of the Company Law.

For public air transport enterprises established before the implementation of this Law, if their form of organization and organizational structure do not fully comply with the provisions of the Company Law, the original provisions may continue to apply; the date for applying the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 95: Public air transport enterprises shall take ensuring flight safety and schedule punctuality, and providing good services as their guidelines, and adopt effective measures to improve the quality of transport services.

Public air transport enterprises shall educate and require their employees to strictly perform their duties, and provide attentive and courteous service to passengers and in all aspects of cargo transportation.

In the event of delays in passenger transport flights, relevant information shall be promptly announced within the airport.

Article 96: A public air transport enterprise applying to operate routes for scheduled air transport (hereinafter referred to as scheduled air transport), or to suspend or terminate the operation of routes, shall report to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for approval.

A public air transport enterprise operating scheduled air transport shall publish flight schedules.

Article 97: The items of operating charges for public air transport enterprises shall be determined by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

The measures for the management of domestic air transport fares shall be formulated by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council in conjunction with the competent pricing authority under the State Council, and implemented after being reported to the State Council for approval.

The formulation of international air transport fares shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of agreements and treaties signed between the government of the People's Republic of China and foreign governments; in the absence of such agreements or treaties, fares shall be determined with reference to the international air transport market prices.

Article 98: A public air transport enterprise engaging in non-scheduled transport shall obtain approval from the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and shall not affect the normal operation of scheduled air transport.

Article 99: A public air transport enterprise shall, in accordance with the public air transport security regulations formulated by the State Council, formulate a security plan and report it to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for record.

Article 100: A public air transport enterprise shall not transport items prohibited by laws and administrative regulations.

A public air transport enterprise shall not transport military munitions or military supplies without the approval of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Passengers are prohibited from carrying items prohibited by laws and administrative regulations on board civil aircraft.

Article 101: A public air transport enterprise transporting dangerous goods shall comply with relevant national regulations.

It is prohibited to consign dangerous goods under the name of non-dangerous goods.

Passengers are prohibited from carrying dangerous goods on board civil aircraft. Except for carrying out official duties and with approval in accordance with national regulations, passengers are prohibited from carrying firearms or controlled cutting tools on board civil aircraft. It is prohibited to consign dangerous goods as baggage in violation of the regulations of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

The list of dangerous goods shall be prescribed and published by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Article 102: A public air transport enterprise shall not transport passengers who refuse to undergo security checks, and shall not transport baggage that has not undergone security checks in violation of national regulations.

A public air transport enterprise must, in accordance with the regulations of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, conduct security checks on the cargo it carries or take other measures to ensure safety.

Article 103: Civil aircraft engaged in international air transport by public air transport enterprises, and the persons, baggage, and cargo carried thereon, shall be subject to inspection by border control, customs, and other competent authorities; however, unnecessary delays shall be avoided during inspection.

Article 104: Public air transport enterprises shall give priority to the transport of mail in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 105: Public air transport enterprises shall insure against third-party liability on the ground.

## Chapter IX: Public Air Transport

### Section 1: General Provisions

Article 106: This Chapter applies to the transport of passengers, baggage, or cargo operated by public air transport enterprises using civil aircraft, including free transport handled by public air transport enterprises using civil aircraft.

This Chapter does not apply to the transport of mail handled using civil aircraft.

For transport by multimodal transport, the provisions of this Chapter apply to the air transport part thereof.

Article 107: For the purposes of this Law, "domestic air transport" means transport where, according to the air transport contract concluded by the parties, the place of departure, the agreed stopping places, and the place of destination are all within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "international air transport" means transport where, according to the air transport contract concluded by the parties, regardless of whether there is any interruption or transshipment, the place of departure, the place of destination, or one of the agreed stopping places is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 108: If the parties to an air transport contract consider the transport handled by several successive air carriers to be a single operation, whether concluded under a single contract or several contracts, it shall be deemed an indivisible transport.

### Section 2: Transport Documents

Article 109: A carrier transporting passengers shall issue a ticket. Passengers boarding a civil aircraft shall present a valid ticket for inspection.

Article 110: The contents to be included in a ticket shall be prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and shall at least include the following:

(1) The place of departure and the place of destination;
(2) If the place of departure and the place of destination are both within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and there are one or more agreed stopping places outside the territory, at least one stopping place shall be indicated;
(3) If the ultimate place of destination of the passenger's journey, the place of departure, or one of the agreed stopping places is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and according to the provisions of the applicable international air transport convention, the ticket should contain a statement that the transport is subject to the provisions of that convention, the ticket shall contain such a statement.

Article 111: The ticket is prima facie evidence of the conclusion of the air passenger transport contract and the conditions of transport.

The failure of a passenger to present the ticket, the ticket not complying with the regulations, or the loss of the ticket shall not affect the existence or validity of the transport contract.

In domestic air transport, if the carrier agrees to allow a passenger to board a civil aircraft without issuing a ticket, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 128 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

In international air transport, if the carrier agrees to allow a passenger to board a civil aircraft without issuing a ticket, or if the ticket does not contain the statement required by Article 110, item (3) of this Law, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 129 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

Article 112: When a carrier carries checked baggage, the baggage check may be contained in or combined with the ticket. In addition to the provisions of Article 110 of this Law, the baggage check shall also include the following:

(1) The number of pieces and weight of the checked baggage;
(2) If a declaration of interest in delivery at the place of destination is required, the declared amount shall be indicated.

The baggage check is prima facie evidence of the checking of the baggage and the conditions of transport.

The failure of a passenger to present the baggage check, the baggage check not complying with the regulations, or the loss of the baggage check shall not affect the existence or validity of the transport contract.

In domestic air transport, if the carrier carries checked baggage without issuing a baggage check, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 128 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

In international air transport, if the carrier carries checked baggage without issuing a baggage check, or if the baggage check does not contain the statement required by Article 110, item (3) of this Law, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 129 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

Article 113: The carrier has the right to require the consignor to fill out an air waybill, and the consignor has the right to require the carrier to accept the air waybill. The failure of the consignor to present the air waybill, the air waybill not complying with the regulations, or the loss of the air waybill shall not affect the existence or validity of the transport contract.

Article 114: The consignor shall fill out three original copies of the air waybill and deliver them together with the cargo to the carrier.

The first copy of the air waybill shall be marked "for the carrier" and signed and sealed by the consignor; the second copy shall be marked "for the consignee" and signed and sealed by the consignor and the carrier; the third copy shall be signed and sealed by the carrier after accepting the cargo and handed over to the consignor.If the air waybill is filled out by the carrier at the request of the shipper, it shall be deemed, in the absence of proof to the contrary, to have been filled out on behalf of the shipper.

Article 115 The contents to be included in an air waybill shall be prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and shall at least include the following:

(1) The place of departure and the place of destination;

(2) If the place of departure and the place of destination are both within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and there are one or more agreed stopping places outside that territory, at least one stopping place shall be indicated;

(3) If the final place of destination of the cargo, the place of departure, or one of the agreed stopping places is not within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and according to the provisions of the applicable international air transport convention, the air waybill must contain a statement that the transport is subject to the provisions of that convention, the air waybill shall contain such a statement.

Article 116 In domestic air transport, if the carrier agrees to carry cargo without an air waybill having been made out, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 128 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

In international air transport, if the carrier agrees to carry cargo without an air waybill having been made out, or if the air waybill does not contain the statement required by Article 115(3) of this Law, the carrier shall not be entitled to invoke the provisions of Article 129 of this Law concerning the limitation of liability.

Article 117 The shipper shall be responsible for the correctness of the particulars and statements relating to the cargo inserted by it in the air waybill.

The shipper shall be liable for damage sustained by the carrier or by any other person to whom the carrier is liable, by reason of the irregularity, incorrectness, or incompleteness of the particulars and statements inserted in the air waybill.

Article 118 The air waybill is prima facie evidence of the conclusion of the contract of carriage of cargo by air, of the conditions of carriage, and of the receipt of the cargo by the carrier.

The statements in the air waybill relating to the weight, dimensions, and packing of the cargo, as well as the number of packages, are prima facie evidence. Statements relating to the quantity, volume, and condition of the cargo do not constitute evidence against the carrier, except to the extent that they have been checked by the carrier and the shipper in the presence of each other and stated in the air waybill, or relate to the apparent condition of the cargo.

Article 119 Subject to the performance of its obligations under the contract of carriage of cargo by air, the shipper has the right to dispose of the cargo by withdrawing it at the airport of departure or destination, or by stopping it in the course of the journey on any landing, or by requesting that it be delivered at the place of destination or in the course of the journey to a consignee other than the one named in the air waybill, or by requesting its return to the airport of departure. However, the shipper shall not exercise this right in such a way as to prejudice the carrier or other shippers, and shall reimburse any expenses occasioned by the exercise of this right.

If it is impossible to carry out the instructions of the shipper, the carrier shall inform the shipper immediately.

If the carrier deals with the cargo in accordance with the instructions of the shipper, without requiring the production of the copy of the air waybill delivered to the shipper, and this causes damage to the lawful holder of the air waybill, the carrier shall be liable, without prejudice to its right of recourse against the shipper.

The right of the shipper ceases at the moment when that of the consignee begins in accordance with Article 120 of this Law. However, if the consignee declines to accept the air waybill or the cargo, or if the carrier cannot communicate with the consignee, the shipper resumes its right of disposal over the cargo.

Article 120 Except in the cases set out in Article 119 of this Law, the consignee is entitled, on arrival of the cargo at the place of destination, to require the carrier to deliver the air waybill and the cargo to it, on payment of the charges due and on complying with the conditions of carriage set out in the air waybill.

Unless otherwise agreed, the carrier shall notify the consignee as soon as the cargo arrives.

If the carrier admits the loss of the cargo, or if the cargo has not arrived within seven days after the date on which it ought to have arrived, the consignee is entitled to enforce against the carrier the rights arising from the contract of carriage of cargo by air.

Article 121 The shipper and the consignee may, subject to the performance of their obligations under the contract of carriage of cargo by air, enforce in their own names, whether for their own benefit or for the benefit of others, the rights conferred by Articles 119 and 120 of this Law respectively.

Article 122 The provisions of Articles 119, 120, and 121 of this Law shall not affect the relations between the shipper and the consignee, nor the relations between third parties whose rights are derived from the shipper or the consignee.

Any clause in the contract of carriage differing from the provisions of Articles 119, 120, and 121 of this Law shall be set out in the air waybill.

Article 123 The shipper shall furnish such information and documents as are necessary to enable the formalities required by laws and administrative regulations to be accomplished before the cargo is delivered to the consignee. The shipper shall be liable to the carrier for any damage caused by the absence, insufficiency, or irregularity of such information or documents, unless the damage is caused by the fault of the carrier, its servants, or agents.

Unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations, the carrier is under no obligation to check the information or documents referred to in the preceding paragraph.

### Section 3: Liability of the Carrier

Article 124 The carrier shall be liable for damage sustained in the event of the death or personal injury of a passenger upon condition only that the event which caused the death or injury took place on board the civil aircraft or in the course of any of the operations of embarking or disembarking. However, the carrier shall not be liable if the death or personal injury of the passenger resulted solely from the state of health of the passenger.

Article 125 The carrier shall be liable for damage sustained in the event of the destruction, loss, or damage of checked baggage or cargo, upon condition only that the event which caused the destruction, loss, or damage took place during the carriage by air. The carrier shall be liable for damage sustained in the event of the destruction, loss, or damage of articles carried by the passenger, upon condition only that the event which caused the destruction, loss, or damage took place on board the civil aircraft or in the course of any of the operations of embarking or disembarking.

The carrier shall not be liable if the destruction, loss, or damage of the articles carried by the passenger or the checked baggage resulted solely from the inherent defect, quality, or vice of the baggage.

Baggage, as used in this Chapter, includes both checked baggage and articles carried by the passenger.

The carrier shall be liable for damage sustained in the event of the destruction, loss, or damage of cargo upon condition only that the event which caused the destruction, loss, or damage took place during the carriage by air. However, the carrier shall not be liable if it proves that the destruction, loss, or damage resulted solely from one or more of the following:

(1) Inherent defect, quality, or vice of that cargo;
(2) Defective packing of that cargo performed by a person other than the carrier, its servants, or agents;
(3) An act of war or an armed conflict;
(4) An act of a public authority carried out in connection with the entry, exit, or transit of the cargo.

The period of carriage by air referred to in this Article comprises the period during which the baggage or cargo is in the charge of the carrier, whether in an airport, on board a civil aircraft, or, in the case of a landing outside an airport, in any place whatsoever.

The period of carriage by air does not include any carriage by land, by sea, or by inland waterway performed outside an airport. However, if such carriage by land, by sea, or by inland waterway takes place in the performance of a contract for carriage by air, for the purpose of loading, delivery, or transshipment, any damage is presumed, in the absence of proof to the contrary, to have been the result of an event which took place during the carriage by air.

Article 126 The carrier shall be liable for damage occasioned by delay in the carriage by air of passengers, baggage, or cargo. Nevertheless, the carrier shall not be liable for damage occasioned by delay if it proves that it and its servants and agents took all measures that could reasonably be required to avoid the damage, or that it was impossible for it or them to take such measures.

Article 127 In the carriage of passengers and baggage, if the carrier proves that the damage was caused or contributed to by the fault of the person claiming compensation, the carrier shall be wholly or partly exonerated from its liability to the claimant, to the extent that such fault caused or contributed to the damage. When a person other than the passenger claims compensation for the death or injury of the passenger, the carrier shall likewise be wholly or partly exonerated from its liability to the extent that it proves that the death or injury was caused or contributed to by the fault of the passenger.

In the carriage of cargo, if the carrier proves that the damage was caused or contributed to by the fault of the person claiming compensation or the person from whom he derives his rights, the carrier shall be wholly or partly exonerated from its liability to the extent that such fault caused or contributed to the damage.

Article 128 The limits of liability for the carrier in domestic air transport shall be prescribed by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council.

If, in the case of checked baggage or cargo, the passenger or shipper makes a special declaration of interest in delivery at destination and pays a supplementary sum if the case so requires, the carrier shall be liable to pay a sum not exceeding the declared sum, unless it proves that the sum is greater than the passenger's or shipper's actual interest in delivery at destination. The other provisions of Article 129 of this Law, except those concerning the limits of liability, shall apply to domestic air transport.

Article 129 The limits of liability for the carrier in international air transport shall be as follows:

(1) The liability of the carrier for each passenger is limited to 16,600 Special Drawing Rights. However, the passenger may, by a special contract in writing with the carrier, fix a higher limit of liability.

(2) The liability of the carrier in the case of checked baggage or cargo is limited to 17 Special Drawing Rights per kilogram. If, in the case of checked baggage or cargo, the passenger or shipper makes a special declaration of interest in delivery at destination and pays a supplementary sum if the case so requires, the carrier shall be liable to pay a sum not exceeding the declared sum, unless it proves that the sum is greater than the passenger's or shipper's actual interest in delivery at destination.

In the case of destruction, loss, damage, or delay of part of the checked baggage or cargo, or of any object contained therein, the weight to be taken into consideration in determining the amount to which the carrier's liability is limited shall be only the total weight of the package or packages concerned. However, when the destruction, loss, damage, or delay of a part of the checked baggage or cargo, or of an object contained therein, affects the value of other packages covered by the same baggage check or the same air waybill, the total weight of such package or packages shall also be taken into consideration in determining the limit of liability.

(3) The liability of the carrier in the case of objects carried by the passenger is limited to 332 Special Drawing Rights per passenger.

Article 130 Any clause contained in the contract of carriage and all special agreements entered into before the damage occurred by which the parties purport to infringe the rules laid down by this Law, either by deciding the law to be applied, or by altering the rules as to jurisdiction, shall be null and void. However, the nullity of such clause or agreement does not affect the validity of the whole contract of carriage by air.

Article 131 Any action for damages, however founded, arising out of damage sustained in the course of carriage by air can only be brought subject to the conditions and limits of liability set out in this Law, without prejudice to the question as to who are the persons who have the right to bring suit and what are their respective rights.

Article 132 If the carrier proves that the damage was caused by, or contributed to by, the intentional or reckless act or omission of the carrier, its servants, or agents, done with knowledge that damage would probably result, the carrier shall not be entitled to avail itself of the provisions of Articles 128 and 129 of this Law which exclude or limit its liability. In the case of a servant or agent of the carrier, it must also be proved that such servant or agent was acting within the scope of its employment or agency.

Article 133 If an action is brought against a servant or agent of the carrier arising out of damage sustained in the course of carriage by air, such servant or agent, if it proves that it acted within the scope of its employment or agency, shall be entitled to avail itself of the limits of liability which that carrier is entitled to invoke under Articles 128 and 129 of this Law.

In the circumstances provided for in the preceding paragraph, the aggregate of the amounts recoverable from the carrier and its servants and agents shall not exceed the said limits.

The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article shall not apply if it is proved that the damage resulted from an intentional or reckless act or omission of the servant or agent, done with knowledge that damage would probably result.

Article 134 Receipt by the person entitled to delivery of checked baggage or cargo without complaint is prima facie evidence that the same has been delivered in good condition and in accordance with the document of carriage.

In the case of damage, the person entitled to delivery must complain to the carrier forthwith after the discovery of the damage, and, at the latest, within seven days from the date of receipt in the case of checked baggage, and within fourteen days from the date of receipt in the case of cargo. In the case of delay, the complaint must be made at the latest within twenty-one days from the date on which the baggage or cargo has been placed at the disposal of the person entitled to delivery.

Every complaint must be made in writing upon the document of carriage or by separate notice in writing, within the periods aforesaid.

In the absence of fraud on the part of the carrier, no action shall lie against the carrier if no complaint has been made within the periods prescribed in paragraph 2 of this Article.

Article 135 The right to damages shall be extinguished if an action is not brought within a period of two years, reckoned from the date of arrival at the destination, or from the date on which the aircraft ought to have arrived, or from the date on which the carriage stopped.

Article 136 In the case of carriage to be performed by various successive carriers, each carrier who accepts passengers, baggage, or cargo shall be subject to the rules set out in this Law, and shall be deemed to be one of the parties to the contract of carriage, insofar as the contract deals with that part of the carriage which is performed under its supervision.

In the case of successive carriage referred to in the preceding paragraph, the passenger or his successor in title can take action only against the carrier who performed the carriage during which the accident or the delay occurred, save where, by express agreement, the first carrier has assumed liability for the whole journey.

In the case of destruction, loss, damage, or delay of checked baggage or cargo, the passenger or shipper shall have the right of action against the first carrier, and the passenger or consignee who has the right of delivery shall have the right of action against the last carrier, and, in addition, both the passenger, shipper, and consignee may take action against the carrier who performed the carriage during which the destruction, loss, damage, or delay occurred. Such carriers shall be jointly and severally liable to the passenger, shipper, or consignee.

### Section 4: Special Provisions Concerning Carriage Performed by an Actual Carrier

Article 137 The term "contracting carrier" as used in this Section means a person who as a principal makes a contract of carriage by air governed by this Chapter with a passenger or shipper, or with the agent of a passenger or shipper.

The term "actual carrier" as used in this Section means a person, other than a successive carrier as defined in this Chapter, who, by virtue of authorization from the contracting carrier, performs the whole or part of the carriage referred to in the preceding paragraph. In the absence of proof to the contrary, such authorization is presumed to exist.

Article 138 Except as otherwise provided in this Section, both the contracting carrier and the actual carrier shall be subject to the rules of this Chapter. The contracting carrier shall be responsible for the whole of the carriage contemplated in the contract. The actual carrier shall be responsible for the part of the carriage which it performs.

Article 139 The acts and omissions of the actual carrier and of its servants and agents acting within the scope of their employment or agency shall, in relation to the carriage performed by the actual carrier, be deemed to be also those of the contracting carrier.

The acts and omissions of the contracting carrier and of its servants and agents acting within the scope of their employment or agency shall, in relation to the carriage performed by the actual carrier, be deemed to be also those of the actual carrier. Nevertheless, no such act or omission shall subject the actual carrier to liability exceeding the limits of liability prescribed by law.

Any special agreement under which the contracting carrier assumes obligations not imposed by this Chapter or any waiver of rights conferred by this Chapter, or any special declaration of interest in delivery at destination made pursuant to Articles 128 and 129 of this Law, shall not affect the actual carrier unless agreed to by it.

Article 140 Any complaint to be made or instruction to be given under this Chapter to the carrier shall have the same effect whether addressed to the contracting carrier or to the actual carrier. Nevertheless, instructions referred to in Article 119 of this Law shall only be effective if addressed to the contracting carrier.

Article 141 In relation to the carriage performed by the actual carrier, any servant or agent of that carrier or of the contracting carrier who proves that he acted within the scope of his employment or agency shall be entitled to avail himself of the limits of liability which are applicable under Articles 128 and 129 of this Law to the carrier whose servant or agent he is, unless it is proved that he acted in a manner which, under this Law, prevents the limits of liability from being invoked.

Article 142 In relation to the carriage performed by the actual carrier, the aggregate of the amounts recoverable from that carrier and the contracting carrier, and from their servants and agents acting within the scope of their employment or agency, shall not exceed the highest amount which could be awarded against either the contracting carrier or the actual carrier under this Law, but none of the persons mentioned shall be liable for a sum in excess of the limit applicable to him.

Article 143 In relation to the carriage performed by the actual carrier, an action for damages may be brought, at the option of the plaintiff, against that carrier or the contracting carrier, or against both together. If the action is brought against only one of these carriers, that carrier shall have the right to require the other carrier to be joined in the proceedings.

Article 144 Except as provided in Article 143 of this Law, the provisions of this Section shall not affect the rights and obligations of the actual carrier and the contracting carrier between themselves.

## Chapter 10: General Aviation

Article 145 "General aviation" means civil aviation activities other than public air transport, using civil aircraft, including operational flights for industry, agriculture, forestry, fishery, and construction, as well as flights for medical and health services, emergency rescue and disaster relief, meteorological detection, ocean monitoring, scientific experiments, education and training, culture and sports, and other such activities.

Article 146 To engage in general aviation activities, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) Possession of civil aircraft suitable for the general aviation activities to be undertaken and meeting the requirements for ensuring flight safety;
(2) Possession of the necessary aviation personnel who have obtained licenses in accordance with the law;
(3) Compliance with other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Engaging in commercial general aviation is limited to enterprise legal persons.

Article 147 Those engaging in non-commercial general aviation shall file a record with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council.

Those engaging in commercial general aviation shall apply to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council for a general aviation business license.

Article 148 A general aviation enterprise engaging in commercial general aviation activities shall conclude a written contract with the user, except for rescue or disaster relief flights in emergency situations.

Article 149 When organizing and carrying out operational flights, effective measures shall be taken to ensure flight safety, protect the environment and ecological balance, and prevent damage to the environment, residents, crops, or livestock, etc.

Article 150 Those engaging in general aviation activities shall take out insurance for liability to third parties on the ground.

## Chapter 11: Search, Rescue, and Accident InvestigationArticle 151 When a civil aircraft encounters an emergency, it shall send signals, report to the air traffic control unit, and request rescue; the air traffic control unit shall immediately notify the search and rescue coordination center. If a civil aircraft encounters an emergency at sea, it shall also send signals to vessels and the national maritime search and rescue organization.

Article 152 Any unit or individual that discovers a civil aircraft in an emergency or receives signals indicating a civil aircraft in an emergency shall immediately notify the relevant search and rescue coordination center, maritime search and rescue organization, or local people's government.

Article 153 Upon receiving notification, the search and rescue coordination center, local people's government, and maritime search and rescue organization shall immediately organize search and rescue operations.

The search and rescue coordination center that receives the notification shall endeavor to inform the civil aircraft in emergency of the search and rescue measures already taken.

Specific measures for the search and rescue of civil aircraft shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 154 Units or individuals performing search and rescue tasks shall do their utmost to rescue persons on board the civil aircraft, take rescue measures for the aircraft in accordance with regulations, protect the scene, and preserve evidence.

Article 155 Parties involved in a civil aircraft accident and related persons shall, when undergoing investigation, truthfully provide information about the scene and the circumstances related to the accident.

Article 156 The organization and procedures for investigating civil aircraft accidents shall be prescribed by the State Council.

## Chapter 12 Liability for Damage to Third Parties on the Surface

Article 157 If a civil aircraft in flight, or a person or object falling from a civil aircraft in flight, causes personal injury or property damage on the surface (including water surfaces, hereinafter the same), the victim has the right to compensation; however, if the damage suffered is not a direct consequence of the accident causing the damage, or if the damage suffered is solely caused by the aircraft passing through the air in accordance with relevant national air traffic rules, the victim has no right to claim compensation.

For the purposes of the preceding paragraph, "in flight" means from the moment power is applied for the purpose of actual take-off until the landing run ends; for lighter-than-air aircraft, "in flight" means from the moment it leaves the surface until it again contacts the surface.

Article 158 The liability for compensation under Article 157 of this Law shall be borne by the operator of the civil aircraft.

For the purposes of the preceding paragraph, "operator" means the person using the civil aircraft at the time the damage occurs. If the right to use the civil aircraft has been directly or indirectly granted to another person, but the person retains control over the navigation of the aircraft, that person is still deemed to be the operator.

When an employee or agent of the operator uses the civil aircraft in the course of their employment or agency, whether or not acting within the scope of their employment or agency, it is deemed to be use by the operator.

The registered owner of a civil aircraft shall be deemed to be the operator and shall bear the operator's liability; unless, in the lawsuit determining their liability, the owner proves that the operator is another person and, to the extent permitted by legal procedures, takes appropriate measures to make that person a party to the lawsuit.

Article 159 If a civil aircraft is used without the consent of the person having control over its navigation, causing damage to a third party on the surface, the person having control over its navigation shall bear joint and several liability with the illegal user, unless that person proves that they have taken due care to prevent such use.

Article 160 If the damage is a direct consequence of armed conflict or civil disturbance, the person who would otherwise be liable under this Chapter shall not be liable.

If the right to use the civil aircraft of a person who would otherwise be liable under this Chapter has been lawfully deprived by a state organ, that person shall not be liable.

Article 161 If a person who would otherwise be liable under this Chapter proves that the damage was caused entirely by the fault of the victim or their employees or agents, their liability for compensation is exempted; if the liable person proves that the damage was caused partly by the fault of the victim or their employees or agents, their liability for compensation is reduced accordingly. However, if the damage is caused by the fault of the victim's employees or agents, and the victim proves that the actions of their employees or agents exceeded the scope of authorization granted to them, the liability of the person who would otherwise be liable is neither exempted nor reduced.

When one person brings a lawsuit claiming compensation for the death or injury of another person, and the damage is caused by the fault of that other person or their employees or agents, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 162 If two or more civil aircraft collide or interfere with each other in flight, causing damage compensable under Article 157 of this Law, or if two or more civil aircraft jointly cause such damage, each relevant civil aircraft shall be deemed to have caused such damage, and the operator of each relevant civil aircraft shall be liable.

Article 163 Persons specified in Article 158, paragraph 4, and Article 159 of this Law shall enjoy the rights of defense available to the operator under this Chapter.

Article 164 Unless expressly provided otherwise in this Chapter, the operator, owner, persons liable under Article 159 of this Law, and their employees and agents shall not be liable for damage on the surface caused by a civil aircraft in flight or by a person or object falling from a civil aircraft in flight, except for persons who intentionally cause such damage.

Article 165 This Chapter shall not prejudice the right of a person liable for damage under this Chapter to seek recourse from others.

Article 166 The operator of a civil aircraft shall take out insurance against liability to third parties on the surface or obtain corresponding security for liability.

Article 167 Insurers and guarantors, in addition to enjoying the same rights of defense as the operator, and the right to defend against forged documents, may only raise the following defenses against claims for compensation made under this Chapter:

(1) The damage occurred after the insurance or guarantee had terminated; however, if the insurance or guarantee expires during a flight, it shall continue in effect until the next landing specified in the flight plan, but not exceeding twenty-four hours;
(2) The damage occurred outside the geographical area specified in the insurance or guarantee, unless the flight exceeded that area due to force majeure, necessity to assist others, or errors in piloting, navigation, or flight direction.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph regarding the continuation of insurance or guarantee shall only apply when it is favorable to the victim.

Article 168 Only in the following circumstances may a victim directly initiate a lawsuit against the insurer or guarantor, without prejudice to the victim's right to initiate a direct lawsuit under the relevant insurance contract or guarantee contract laws:

(1) The insurance or guarantee continues to be effective under Article 167, items (1) and (2) of this Law;
(2) The operator is bankrupt.

Except for the right of defense provided in Article 167, paragraph 1 of this Law, the insurer or guarantor shall not, in a direct lawsuit initiated by the victim under this Chapter, defend on the grounds that the insurance or guarantee is invalid or has retroactively terminated.

Article 169 Insurance or guarantee provided in accordance with Article 166 of this Law shall be specifically designated for priority payment of compensation under this Chapter.

Article 170 Funds payable by the insurer to the operator shall not be subject to attachment or disposal by the operator's creditors until the claims of third parties under this Chapter are satisfied.

Article 171 The statute of limitations for claims for compensation for damage to third parties on the surface is two years, calculated from the date the damage occurred; however, in any case, the limitation period shall not exceed three years from the date the damage occurred.

Article 172 The provisions of this Chapter shall not apply to the following damages:

(1) Damage caused to a civil aircraft in flight or to persons or property on board such aircraft;
(2) Damage governed by a contract between the victim and the operator or the person having the right to use the civil aircraft at the time the damage occurred, or governed by the provisions on employee compensation under the law applicable to the labor contract between the two parties;
(3) Nuclear damage.

## Chapter 13 Special Provisions for Foreign Civil Aircraft

Article 173 Foreign civil aircraft operated by foreign nationals engaging in civil aviation activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by the provisions of this Chapter; matters not covered by this Chapter shall be governed by other relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 174 A foreign civil aircraft may fly into, out of, and within the airspace of the People's Republic of China, and land therein, only in accordance with the provisions of agreements or protocols signed between the government of its state of registry and the government of the People's Republic of China, or with the approval or acceptance of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

For foreign civil aircraft that fly into or out of the airspace of the People's Republic of China without authorization in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the relevant authorities of the People's Republic of China have the right to take necessary measures to order them to land at a designated airport; for aircraft that comply with the provisions of the preceding paragraph but for which there are reasonable grounds to believe an inspection is necessary, the relevant authorities have the right to order them to land at a designated airport.

Article 175 When a foreign civil aircraft flies into the airspace of the People's Republic of China, its operator shall provide relevant certificates proving that it has taken out insurance against liability to third parties on the surface or obtained corresponding security for liability; if the operator fails to provide such certificates, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China has the right to refuse its entry into the airspace of the People's Republic of China.

Article 176 An operator of a foreign civil aircraft may operate international scheduled flight transport as specified in agreements or protocols signed between the government of the People's Republic of China and the foreign government concerned only after being designated by its own government and obtaining an operating permit issued by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China; an operator of a foreign civil aircraft may operate non-scheduled air transport between a location within the territory of the People's Republic of China and a location outside the territory only after being approved by its own government and obtaining approval from the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

Operators of foreign civil aircraft as specified in the preceding paragraph shall, in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China, formulate corresponding security plans and report them to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China for record.

Article 177 Operators of foreign civil aircraft shall not operate air transport between two points within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Article 178 Foreign civil aircraft shall fly in accordance with the flight schedules or flight plans approved by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China; if changes to flight schedules or flight plans are necessary, the operator shall obtain approval from the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China; if a flight is changed or cancelled for any reason, the operator shall promptly report to the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

Article 179 Foreign civil aircraft shall take off or land at customs airports designated by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

Article 180 The competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China and other competent authorities have the right to inspect the documents specified in Article 90 of this Law when a foreign civil aircraft lands or departs.

Foreign civil aircraft and the persons, baggage, and cargo on board shall be subject to entry-exit, customs, quarantine, and other inspections carried out by the relevant competent authorities of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law.

Inspections and checks carried out under the preceding two paragraphs shall avoid unnecessary delays.

Article 181 The government of the People's Republic of China recognizes the validity of airworthiness certificates, crew member qualification certificates, and licenses issued or validated by the state of registry of a foreign civil aircraft; however, the requirements for issuing or validating such certificates or licenses shall be equal to or higher than the minimum standards established by the International Civil Aviation Organization.

Article 182 If a foreign civil aircraft is in distress within the search and rescue area of the People's Republic of China, its owner or state of registry may participate in search and rescue operations only with the approval of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China or in accordance with an agreement between the two governments.

Article 183 If an accident involving a foreign civil aircraft occurs within the territory of the People's Republic of China, its state of registry and other relevant states may appoint observers to participate in the accident investigation. The accident investigation report and findings shall be communicated by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council of the People's Republic of China to the state of registry of the foreign civil aircraft and other relevant states.

## Chapter 14 Application of Law in Foreign-Related Relations

Article 184 If an international treaty concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

If neither the laws of the People's Republic of China nor international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contain relevant provisions, international custom may be applied.

Article 185 The acquisition, transfer, and extinction of ownership of a civil aircraft shall be governed by the law of the state of registry of the civil aircraft.

Article 186 The mortgage of a civil aircraft shall be governed by the law of the state of registry of the civil aircraft.

Article 187 The priority right over a civil aircraft shall be governed by the law of the place where the court accepting the case is located.

Article 188 The parties to a civil air transport contract may choose the law applicable to the contract, unless otherwise provided by law; if the parties have not chosen the applicable law, the law of the country with which the contract is most closely connected shall apply.

Article 189 Compensation for damage caused by a civil aircraft to a third party on the surface shall be governed by the law of the place where the tortious act occurred.

Compensation for damage caused by a civil aircraft over the high seas to a third party on the water surface shall be governed by the law of the place where the court accepting the case is located.

Article 190 The application of foreign law or international custom in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter shall not violate the social public interests of the People's Republic of China.

## Chapter 15 Legal Liability

Article 191 Anyone who hijacks an aircraft by means of violence, coercion, or any other method shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 192 Anyone who uses violence against a person on board a civil aircraft in flight, endangering flight safety, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 193 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, secretly carries explosives, detonators, or other dangerous goods on board a civil aircraft, or consigns dangerous goods under the name of non-dangerous goods, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If an enterprise or institution commits the crime specified in the preceding paragraph, a fine shall be imposed, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Anyone who secretly carries firearms, ammunition, or controlled knives on board a civil aircraft shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 194 If a public air transport enterprise transports dangerous goods in violation of the provisions of Article 101 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall confiscate the illegal income and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than one times the illegal income.

If a public air transport enterprise commits the act specified in the preceding paragraph, resulting in a major accident, the illegal income shall be confiscated, and a fine shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 195 Anyone who intentionally places dangerous goods on a civil aircraft in service, or instigates others to do so, sufficient to destroy the civil aircraft and endanger flight safety, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 196 Anyone who intentionally transmits false information, disrupting normal flight order, and causing significant losses to public or private property, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 197 Anyone who steals, intentionally damages, or moves navigation facilities in service, endangering flight safety and creating a risk of the civil aircraft crashing or being destroyed, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 198 Anyone who gathers a crowd to disrupt the order of a civil airport shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 199 Any aviation personnel who neglects their duties or violates rules and regulations, resulting in a major flight accident with serious consequences, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 200 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law, where the act is not serious enough for criminal punishment but should be subject to public security penalties, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 201 If a civil aircraft flies without an airworthiness certificate in violation of Article 37 of this Law, or if a leased foreign civil aircraft flies without the airworthiness certificate issued by its original state of registry being reviewed and approved or a new airworthiness certificate being issued by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall order the flight to cease, confiscate the illegal income, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income; if there is no illegal income, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If the airworthiness certificate is invalid or the flight exceeds the scope specified in the airworthiness certificate, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 202 If a civil aircraft, its engine, propeller, or equipment on board that has not obtained a type certificate or type acceptance certificate is put into production in violation of Articles 34 and 36, paragraph 2 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall order the cessation of production, confiscate the illegal income, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than one times the illegal income; if there is no illegal income, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 203 If production or maintenance activities are engaged in without obtaining a production license certificate or maintenance license certificate in violation of Article 35 of this Law, or if public air transport or commercial general aviation is engaged in without obtaining a public air transport operating license or general aviation operating license in violation of Articles 92 and 147, paragraph 2 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council may order the cessation of production, maintenance, or business activities.

Article 204 If an enterprise that has obtained a production license certificate or maintenance license certificate as specified in Article 35 of this Law causes a serious accident due to quality problems in production or maintenance, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council may revoke its production license certificate or maintenance license certificate.

Article 205 If anyone engages in corresponding civil aviation activities without an aviation personnel license or physical examination certificate in violation of Article 40 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall order the cessation of civil aviation activities, prohibit the person from applying for relevant licenses and certificates within a period specified by the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, and impose a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan on their employing unit.

Article 206 Under any of the following illegal circumstances, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall give a warning to the pilot-in-command of the civil aircraft or suspend or revoke their license for a period of one to six months; if the circumstances are serious, the license may be revoked:

(1) The pilot-in-command violates Article 45, paragraph 1 of this Law by taking off without inspecting the civil aircraft;(2) A civil aircraft, in violation of the provisions of Article 75 of this Law, fails to fly along the route and at the altitude designated by the air traffic control unit, or, in violation of the provisions of Article 79 of this Law, flies over a city.

Article 207: Where a civil aircraft conducts a flight activity without permission from the air traffic control unit in violation of the provisions of Article 74 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall order the cessation of the flight, impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the owner or lessee of the civil aircraft, and give a warning to the pilot-in-command of the civil aircraft or suspend or revoke his license for a period of one to six months; if the circumstances are serious, the license may be revoked.

Article 208: Where the pilot-in-command or any other crew member of a civil aircraft commits any of the following acts, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall give a warning or suspend or revoke his license for a period of one to six months; if the act listed in item (2) or (3) is committed, the license may be revoked:

(1) Failing to carry the license and the physical examination certificate in accordance with the provisions of Article 41 of this Law when performing a flight mission;

(2) Leaving the civil aircraft in violation of the provisions of Article 48 of this Law when the civil aircraft is in distress;

(3) Performing a flight mission in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 77 of this Law.

Article 209: Where a civil aircraft throws objects during flight in violation of the provisions of Article 80 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall give a warning and may impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan on the person directly responsible.

Article 210: Where a civil airport is opened and used without obtaining an airport use permit in violation of the provisions of Article 62 of this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council shall order the cessation of its opening and use, confiscate any illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than the amount of the illegal gains.

Article 211: Where a public air transport enterprise or a general aviation enterprise violates the provisions of this Law and the circumstances are serious, in addition to the penalties imposed in accordance with this Law, the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council may revoke its operating license.

Where a person engaged in non-operational general aviation fails to file a record with the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council, the said authority shall order rectification; if rectification is not made within the prescribed time limit, a fine of not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 212: Where a staff member of the competent civil aviation authority under the State Council or a regional civil aviation administrative body neglects his duty, abuses his power, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

## Chapter XVI Supplementary Provisions

Article 213: The term "unit of account" as used in this Law refers to the Special Drawing Right as defined by the International Monetary Fund; the amount in Renminbi shall be calculated on the date of the court judgment, the date of the arbitral award, or the date of the agreement between the parties, in accordance with the conversion method for converting the Special Drawing Right of the International Monetary Fund into Renminbi as prescribed by the competent foreign exchange authority of the State.

Article 214: Where the State Council and the Central Military Commission have other provisions regarding the management of unmanned aircraft, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 215: This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 1996.
