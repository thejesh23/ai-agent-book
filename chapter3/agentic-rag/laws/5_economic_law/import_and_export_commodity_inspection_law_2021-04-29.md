# Law of the People's Republic of China on Import and Export Commodity Inspection

Adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on February 21, 1989

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Import and Export Commodity Inspection adopted at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 28, 2002

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Product Quality adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to strengthening the inspection of import and export commodities, standardizing the inspection activities of import and export commodities, safeguarding the public interest and the legitimate rights and interests of the parties involved in import and export trade, and promoting the smooth development of foreign economic and trade relations.

Article 2 The State Council establishes an import and export commodity inspection department (hereinafter referred to as the State Administration for Commodity Inspection), which is in charge of the inspection of import and export commodities nationwide. The import and export commodity inspection authorities (hereinafter referred to as the commodity inspection authorities) set up by the State Administration for Commodity Inspection in various localities shall administer the inspection of import and export commodities within their respective areas.

Article 3 The commodity inspection authorities and the inspection agencies established according to law (hereinafter referred to as other inspection agencies) shall, in accordance with the law, conduct inspection of import and export commodities.

Article 4 The inspection of import and export commodities shall be based on the principles of protecting human health and safety, protecting the life and health of animals or plants, protecting the environment, preventing fraudulent practices, and safeguarding national security. The State Administration for Commodity Inspection shall formulate, adjust, and publish for implementation a catalogue of import and export commodities subject to compulsory inspection (hereinafter referred to as the Catalogue).

Article 5 Import and export commodities included in the Catalogue shall be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities.

Import commodities as prescribed in the preceding paragraph that have not been inspected shall not be sold or used; export commodities as prescribed in the preceding paragraph that have not passed inspection shall not be exported.

For import and export commodities specified in the first paragraph of this Article that meet the conditions for exemption from inspection as prescribed by the state, the consignee or consignor may apply, and upon examination and approval by the State Administration for Commodity Inspection, they may be exempted from inspection.

Article 6 Compulsory inspection of import and export commodities refers to the conformity assessment activities to determine whether the import and export commodities included in the Catalogue comply with the mandatory requirements of the national technical specifications.

Conformity assessment procedures include: sampling, testing, and inspection; evaluation, verification, and assurance of conformity; registration, accreditation, and approval, as well as combinations thereof.

For the inspection of import and export commodities specified in the first paragraph of this Article, the commodity inspection authorities may accept the inspection results of inspection agencies; the State Administration for Commodity Inspection shall implement catalogue management for the aforementioned inspection agencies.

Article 7 Import and export commodities included in the Catalogue shall be inspected in accordance with the mandatory requirements of the national technical specifications; where no mandatory requirements of national technical specifications have been formulated, they shall be formulated in a timely manner in accordance with the law, and before such formulation, inspection may be conducted with reference to the relevant foreign standards designated by the State Administration for Commodity Inspection.

Article 8 Other inspection agencies may accept entrustment from parties involved in foreign trade or foreign inspection agencies to handle inspection and appraisal services for import and export commodities.

Article 9 The inspection of import and export commodities or inspection items that are required by laws or administrative regulations to be inspected by other inspection agencies shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 10 The State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities shall promptly collect and provide information on the inspection of import and export commodities to relevant parties.

Staff members of the State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities shall, in the performance of their duties in the inspection of import and export commodities, have the obligation to keep confidential any trade secrets they become aware of.

## Chapter II Inspection of Import Commodities

Article 11 The consignee or its agent of import commodities that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities as provided for in this Law shall apply for inspection to the commodity inspection authority at the place of customs declaration.

Article 12 The consignee or its agent of import commodities that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities as provided for in this Law shall, at the place and within the time limit specified by the commodity inspection authority, accept the inspection of the import commodities by the commodity inspection authority. The commodity inspection authority shall complete the inspection within the time limit uniformly prescribed by the State Administration for Commodity Inspection and issue an inspection certificate.

Article 13 If the consignee of import commodities other than those that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities as provided for in this Law discovers that the import commodities are of substandard quality or are damaged or short in quantity, and needs the commodity inspection authority to issue a certificate for claiming compensation, the consignee shall apply to the commodity inspection authority for inspection and issuance of a certificate.

Article 14 For important import commodities and large complete sets of equipment, the consignee shall, in accordance with the provisions of the foreign trade contract, conduct pre-inspection, supervision over manufacturing, or supervision over loading before shipment in the exporting country, and the competent authorities shall strengthen supervision; the commodity inspection authority may, as necessary, send inspection personnel to participate.

## Chapter III Inspection of Export Commodities

Article 15 The consignor or its agent of export commodities that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities as provided for in this Law shall apply for inspection to the commodity inspection authority at the place and within the time limit specified by the commodity inspection authority. The commodity inspection authority shall complete the inspection within the time limit uniformly prescribed by the State Administration for Commodity Inspection and issue an inspection certificate.

Article 16 Export commodities that have passed inspection and have been issued an inspection certificate by the commodity inspection authority shall be declared for export at customs within the time limit specified by the commodity inspection authority; if the time limit is exceeded, a new application for inspection shall be made.

Article 17 Enterprises manufacturing packaging containers for the export of dangerous goods must apply to the commodity inspection authority for performance appraisal of the packaging containers. Enterprises manufacturing export dangerous goods must apply to the commodity inspection authority for appraisal of the use of the packaging containers. Dangerous goods packed in containers that have not passed appraisal shall not be exported.

Article 18 For ship holds and containers used for shipping perishable foods for export, the carrier or the container loading unit must apply for inspection before loading. If they have not passed inspection, loading shall not be permitted.

## Chapter IV Supervision and Administration

Article 19 The commodity inspection authorities shall, in accordance with state regulations, carry out random inspection of import and export commodities other than those that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities as provided for in this Law.

The State Administration for Commodity Inspection may publish the results of random inspections or notify the relevant departments of the situation of random inspections.

Article 20 The commodity inspection authorities may, in accordance with state regulations and for the convenience of foreign trade, carry out quality supervision and inspection of export commodities included in the Catalogue before they leave the factory.

Article 21 When an agent handling the inspection application procedures for the consignee or consignor of import and export goods goes through the inspection application procedures, the agent shall submit a power of attorney to the commodity inspection authority.

Article 22 The State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities shall, in accordance with the law, supervise the inspection and appraisal business activities of other inspection agencies for import and export commodities, and may conduct random inspections of the commodities inspected by them.

Article 23 The certification accreditation administration department of the State Council shall, in accordance with the unified certification system of the state, implement certification management for relevant import and export commodities.

Article 24 Certification bodies may, in accordance with agreements signed between the certification accreditation administration department of the State Council and relevant foreign institutions or upon entrustment by relevant foreign institutions, carry out quality certification work for import and export commodities, and permit the use of quality certification marks on import and export commodities that have passed certification.

Article 25 The commodity inspection authorities shall, in accordance with this Law, implement verification management for import and export commodities subject to a licensing system, examine the documents, and verify whether the documents and the goods are consistent.

Article 26 The commodity inspection authorities may, as necessary, affix commodity inspection marks or seals on import and export commodities that have passed inspection.

Article 27 If the applicant for inspection of import and export commodities disagrees with the inspection results made by the commodity inspection authority, the applicant may apply for a re-inspection to the original commodity inspection authority, its superior commodity inspection authority, or even the State Administration for Commodity Inspection, and the commodity inspection authority or the State Administration for Commodity Inspection that accepts the re-inspection application shall promptly make a re-inspection conclusion.

Article 28 If a party is dissatisfied with the re-inspection conclusion made by the commodity inspection authority or the State Administration for Commodity Inspection, or is dissatisfied with the penalty decision made by the commodity inspection authority, the party may apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the law, or may file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law.

Article 29 The State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities, in performing their duties, must abide by the law, safeguard the interests of the state, strictly enforce the law in accordance with statutory functions, powers, and procedures, and accept supervision.

The State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities shall, in accordance with the needs of performing their duties according to law, strengthen team building so that the commodity inspection staff have good political and professional qualities. Commodity inspection staff shall regularly receive professional training and assessment, and may only take up their posts and perform their duties after passing the assessment.

Commodity inspection staff must be devoted to their duties, provide courteous service, abide by professional ethics, and shall not abuse their power or seek personal gain.

Article 30 The State Administration for Commodity Inspection and the commodity inspection authorities shall establish and improve internal supervision systems, and supervise and inspect the law enforcement activities of their staff.

The responsibilities and powers of the main positions within the commodity inspection authorities responsible for accepting inspection applications, conducting inspections, issuing certificates, and releasing goods shall be clearly defined, mutually separate, and mutually constraining.

Article 31 Any unit or individual has the right to report and accuse the State Administration for Commodity Inspection, the commodity inspection authorities, and their staff of illegal or disciplinary acts. The authority receiving the report or accusation shall promptly investigate and handle the matter in accordance with the law and according to the division of responsibilities, and shall keep the reporter or accuser confidential.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 32 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, sells or uses import commodities that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities without applying for inspection, or exports export commodities that must be inspected by the commodity inspection authorities without applying for inspection and passing it, shall be ordered by the commodity inspection authority to stop the illegal act, have the illegal income confiscated, and be fined not less than 5% but not more than 20% of the value of the goods; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 33 Anyone who imports or exports commodities that are adulterated, fake, of inferior quality, or passes off substandard import or export commodities as standard ones, shall be ordered by the commodity inspection authority to stop importing or exporting, have the illegal income confiscated, and be fined not less than 50% but not more than three times the value of the goods; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 34 Anyone who forges, alters, trades, or steals commodity inspection certificates, seals, marks, seals, or quality certification marks shall be pursued for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the act is not serious enough for criminal punishment, the commodity inspection authority or the certification accreditation supervision and administration department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order rectification, confiscate the illegal income, and impose a fine not exceeding the value of the goods.

Article 35 If a staff member of the State Administration for Commodity Inspection or a commodity inspection authority, in violation of the provisions of this Law, discloses trade secrets they have become aware of, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 36 If a staff member of the State Administration for Commodity Inspection or a commodity inspection authority abuses their power, deliberately creates difficulties, engages in malpractices for personal gain, forges inspection results, or neglects their duties, delaying inspection and issuance of certificates, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 37 The commodity inspection authorities and other inspection agencies, when conducting inspections and handling inspection and appraisal services in accordance with the provisions of this Law, shall charge fees in accordance with the relevant state regulations.

Article 38 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations in accordance with this Law.

Article 39 This Law shall come into force as of August 1, 1989.
