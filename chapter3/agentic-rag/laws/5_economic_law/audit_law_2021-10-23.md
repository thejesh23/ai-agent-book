# Audit Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 9th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 31, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Audit Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on February 28, 2006

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Audit Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 23, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the state's audit supervision, maintain the fiscal and economic order of the state, improve the efficiency of the use of fiscal funds, promote the building of a clean government, and ensure the healthy development of the national economy and society.

Article 2 The state practices an audit supervision system. It upholds the leadership of the Communist Party of China over audit work and establishes a centralized, unified, comprehensive, authoritative, and efficient audit supervision system.

The State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish audit institutions.

The fiscal revenues and expenditures of all departments of the State Council and of the local people's governments at all levels and their departments, the financial revenues and expenditures of state-owned financial institutions and enterprises and institutions, and other fiscal revenues and expenditures that shall be subject to audit in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall be subject to audit supervision in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Audit institutions shall, in accordance with the law, exercise audit supervision over the authenticity, legality, and efficiency of the fiscal revenues and expenditures or financial revenues and expenditures listed in the preceding paragraph.

Article 3 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision in accordance with the functions, powers, and procedures prescribed by law.

Audit institutions shall conduct audit evaluations based on laws, regulations, and other relevant state provisions concerning fiscal revenues and expenditures and financial revenues and expenditures, and shall make audit decisions within the scope of their statutory functions and powers.

Article 4 The State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level shall annually submit audit work reports to the standing committees of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels. The audit work reports shall report on the audit of budget implementation, draft final accounts, and other fiscal revenues and expenditures by the audit institutions, with a focus on reporting on the audit of budget implementation and its performance, and shall report on the audit of state-owned resources and state-owned assets in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations. When necessary, the standing committees of the people's congresses may adopt resolutions on the audit work reports.

The State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level shall report to the standing committees of the people's congresses at the corresponding levels on the rectification status and handling results of the problems pointed out in the audit work reports.

Article 5 Audit institutions shall independently exercise their power of audit supervision in accordance with the provisions of law, and shall not be interfered with by any other administrative organ, social organization, or individual.

Article 6 When handling audit matters, audit institutions and auditors shall be objective and fair, seek truth from facts, be honest and upright, and maintain confidentiality.

## Chapter II Audit Institutions and Auditors

Article 7 The State Council shall establish the National Audit Office, which shall be in charge of the audit work nationwide under the leadership of the Premier of the State Council. The Auditor General is the administrative head of the National Audit Office.

Article 8 The audit institutions of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, autonomous prefectures, counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall be responsible for the audit work within their respective administrative areas under the leadership of the governors of provinces, chairpersons of autonomous regions, mayors, prefectural chiefs, county heads, district heads, and the audit institution at the next higher level.

Article 9 Local audit institutions at all levels shall be responsible and report on their work to the people's governments at the corresponding levels and the audit institutions at the next higher level, and their audit work shall be primarily led by the audit institutions at the next higher level.

Article 10 Audit institutions may, as required by work and with the approval of the people's governments at the corresponding levels, establish representative offices within their audit jurisdiction.

Representative offices shall conduct audit work in accordance with the law based on the authorization of the audit institutions.

Article 11 The funds necessary for audit institutions to perform their duties shall be included in the budget to ensure their provision.

Article 12 Audit institutions shall build a high-quality, professional audit team that is firm in conviction, serves the people, proficient in business, pragmatic in style, courageous in taking responsibility, and honest and upright.

Audit institutions shall strengthen the supervision of auditors' compliance with the law and performance of their duties, and urge auditors to perform their duties and responsibilities in accordance with the law.

Audit institutions and auditors shall accept supervision in accordance with the law.

Article 13 Auditors shall possess the professional knowledge and business capabilities commensurate with the audit work they engage in.

Audit institutions may, as required by work, engage persons with professional knowledge related to the audit matters to participate in the audit work.

Article 14 Audit institutions and auditors shall not participate in activities that may affect their independent performance of audit supervision duties in accordance with the law, and shall not interfere with or meddle in the normal production, operation, and management activities of the auditees and their related units.

Article 15 If an auditor has an interest in the auditee or the audit matter when handling an audit matter, the auditor shall withdraw.

Article 16 Audit institutions and auditors shall keep confidential the state secrets, work secrets, trade secrets, personal privacy, and personal information learned during the performance of their duties, and shall not disclose or illegally provide them to others.

Article 17 Auditors performing their duties in accordance with the law shall be protected by law.

No organization or individual may refuse or obstruct auditors from performing their duties in accordance with the law, or retaliate against auditors.

The heads of audit institutions shall be appointed and removed in accordance with statutory procedures. The heads of audit institutions shall not be removed arbitrarily unless they are guilty of illegal acts, dereliction of duty, or other circumstances that render them unfit for the position.

The appointment and removal of the heads of local audit institutions at all levels shall be subject to prior consultation with the audit institution at the next higher level.

## Chapter III Functions and Duties of Audit Institutions

Article 18 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the implementation of budgets and final accounts of all departments at the corresponding level (including directly subordinate units) and of governments at the next lower level, as well as other fiscal revenues and expenditures.

Article 19 Under the leadership of the Premier of the State Council, the National Audit Office shall exercise audit supervision over the implementation of the central budget, draft final accounts, and other fiscal revenues and expenditures, and shall submit an audit results report to the Premier of the State Council.

Under the leadership of the governors of provinces, chairpersons of autonomous regions, mayors, prefectural chiefs, county heads, district heads, and the audit institution at the next higher level, local audit institutions at all levels shall exercise audit supervision over the implementation of budgets at the corresponding levels, draft final accounts, and other fiscal revenues and expenditures, and shall submit audit results reports to the people's governments at the corresponding levels and the audit institutions at the next higher level.

Article 20 The National Audit Office shall exercise audit supervision over the financial revenues and expenditures of the central bank.

Article 21 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the financial revenues and expenditures of state institutions and other institutions using fiscal funds.

Article 22 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the assets, liabilities, profits and losses, and other financial revenues and expenditures of state-owned enterprises, state-owned financial institutions, and enterprises and financial institutions in which state-owned capital has a controlling stake or a dominant position.

When major interests of the state's fiscal and financial sectors are involved, and to safeguard national economic security, with the approval of the State Council, the National Audit Office may conduct special audit investigations or audits of financial institutions not covered by the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 23 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the budget implementation and final accounts of government-invested and government-investment-dominated construction projects, and over the fund management, use, construction, and operation of other major public projects related to national interests and public interests.

Article 24 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over state-owned resources and state-owned assets.

Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the financial revenues and expenditures of social insurance funds managed by government departments and other units entrusted by the government, the National Social Security Fund, social donation funds, and other public funds.

Article 25 Audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over the financial revenues and expenditures of projects aided or loaned by international organizations and foreign governments.

Article 26 In accordance with the approved audit project plan, audit institutions may exercise audit supervision over the implementation of major national economic and social policies and measures by the auditees.

Article 27 In addition to the audit matters specified in this Law, audit institutions shall exercise audit supervision over other matters that shall be audited by audit institutions in accordance with other laws and administrative regulations, in accordance with this Law and the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 28 Audit institutions may conduct a comprehensive audit of matters that the auditee shall accept audit in accordance with the law, or may conduct a special audit of specific matters therein.

Article 29 Audit institutions have the power to conduct special audit investigations on specific matters related to the state's fiscal revenues and expenditures with relevant local authorities, departments, and units, and to report the audit investigation results to the people's governments at the corresponding levels and the audit institutions at the next higher level.

Article 30 When performing audit supervision duties and discovering potential risks in economic and social operations, audit institutions shall promptly report to the people's governments at the corresponding levels or notify the relevant competent authorities or units.

Article 31 Audit institutions shall determine the scope of audit jurisdiction based on the fiscal or financial subordination relationship of the auditee or the supervision and management relationship over state-owned resources and state-owned assets.

If there is a dispute over the scope of audit jurisdiction between audit institutions, it shall be determined by their common audit institution at the next higher level.

An audit institution at a higher level may authorize an audit institution at a lower level to conduct audits of audit matters within its audit jurisdiction, except for the audit matters specified in Articles 18 to 20 of this Law, which shall not be authorized; an audit institution at a higher level may directly conduct audits of major audit matters within the audit jurisdiction of an audit institution at a lower level, but unnecessary duplicate audits shall be avoided.

Article 32 The auditee shall strengthen the leadership over internal audit work and establish and improve the internal audit system in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Audit institutions shall provide professional guidance and supervision over the internal audit work of the auditee.

Article 33 If the unit audited by a social audit institution is legally an auditee, the audit institution has the power, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, to verify the relevant audit reports issued by that social audit institution.

## Chapter IV Powers of Audit Institutions

Article 34 Audit institutions have the power to require the auditee to provide financial and accounting materials, as well as business and management materials related to fiscal revenues and expenditures and financial revenues and expenditures, including electronic data and relevant documents, in accordance with the provisions of the audit institution. The auditee shall not refuse, delay, or make false reports.

The person in charge of the auditee shall be responsible for the timeliness, authenticity, and completeness of the materials provided by the unit.

If the audit institution needs to verify relevant information with the auditee after conducting a comprehensive analysis of the obtained electronic data and other materials, the auditee shall cooperate.

Article 35 National government information systems and data sharing platforms shall be opened to audit institutions in accordance with regulations.

If the electronic data and other materials obtained by the audit institution through government information systems and data sharing platforms are sufficient to meet the needs, the auditee shall not be required to provide them repeatedly.

Article 36 When conducting an audit, audit institutions have the power to inspect the financial and accounting materials of the auditee, as well as business and management materials and assets related to fiscal revenues and expenditures and financial revenues and expenditures, and have the power to inspect the security, reliability, and economy of the auditee's information systems. The auditee shall not refuse.

Article 37 When conducting an audit, audit institutions have the power to investigate relevant issues related to the audit matter with relevant units and individuals and obtain relevant evidentiary materials. Relevant units and individuals shall support and assist the audit institution's work, truthfully report the situation to the audit institution, and provide relevant evidentiary materials.

With the approval of the person in charge of the audit institution of the people's government at or above the county level, audit institutions have the power to inquire about the auditee's accounts in financial institutions.

If the audit institution has evidence proving that the auditee has transferred public funds to the accounts of other units or individuals in financial institutions in violation of state regulations, with the approval of the principal person in charge of the audit institution of the people's government at or above the county level, it has the power to inquire about the deposits of the relevant units or individuals in financial institutions related to the audit matter.

Article 38 When an audit institution is conducting an audit, the auditee shall not transfer, conceal, alter, or destroy financial and accounting materials, or business and management materials related to fiscal revenues and expenditures and financial revenues and expenditures, and shall not transfer, conceal, or intentionally damage assets held that were obtained in violation of state regulations.

The audit institution has the power to stop the auditee's actions that violate the provisions of the preceding paragraph; when necessary, with the approval of the person in charge of the audit institution of the people's government at or above the county level, it has the power to seal up relevant materials and assets obtained in violation of state regulations; if it is necessary to freeze relevant deposits in financial institutions, it shall apply to the people's court.

The audit institution has the power to stop the auditee's ongoing acts of fiscal revenues and expenditures or financial revenues and expenditures that violate state regulations; if the cessation is ineffective, with the approval of the person in charge of the audit institution of the people's government at or above the county level, it shall notify the financial department and relevant competent authorities or units to suspend the allocation of funds directly related to the acts of fiscal revenues and expenditures or financial revenues and expenditures that violate state regulations, and if already allocated, to suspend their use.

The measures taken by the audit institution in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs shall not affect the legitimate business activities and production and operation activities of the auditee.

Article 39 If the audit institution believes that the regulations of the competent authority or unit at the next higher level implemented by the auditee concerning fiscal revenues and expenditures or financial revenues and expenditures conflict with laws and administrative regulations, it shall suggest that the relevant competent authority or unit make corrections; if the relevant competent authority or unit fails to make corrections, the audit institution shall request the authority or unit with the power to handle the matter to deal with it in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Audit institutions may notify relevant government departments or publish audit results to the public.

When notifying or publishing audit results, audit institutions shall keep confidential state secrets, work secrets, trade secrets, personal privacy, and personal information, and comply with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant regulations of the State Council.

Article 41 When performing audit supervision duties, audit institutions may request assistance from public security, finance, natural resources, ecological environment, customs, taxation, market supervision and management, and other authorities. The relevant authorities shall cooperate in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Audit Procedures

Article 42 Audit institutions shall form audit teams based on the audit matters determined by the approved audit project plan, and shall deliver an audit notice to the auditee three days before the implementation of the audit; under special circumstances, with the approval of the person in charge of the audit institution of the people's government at or above the county level, the audit may be implemented directly with the audit notice.

The auditee shall cooperate with the work of the audit institution and provide necessary working conditions.

Audit institutions shall improve the efficiency of audit work.

Article 43 Auditors shall conduct audits by examining financial and accounting materials, consulting documents and materials related to the audit matter, inspecting cash, physical objects, marketable securities, and information systems, and investigating relevant units and individuals, and shall obtain evidentiary materials.

When investigating relevant units and individuals, there shall be no fewer than two auditors, and they shall present their work credentials and a copy of the audit notice.

Article 44 After implementing the audit of the audit matter, the audit team shall submit an audit report of the audit team to the audit institution. Before submitting the audit report of the audit team to the audit institution, the opinions of the auditee shall be sought. The auditee shall submit its written opinions to the audit team within ten days from the date of receiving the audit report of the audit team. The audit team shall submit the written opinions of the auditee together with its audit report to the audit institution.

Article 45 The audit institution shall review the audit report of the audit team in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the National Audit Office, and after studying the opinions submitted by the auditee on the audit report of the audit team, shall issue the audit report of the audit institution. If the acts of fiscal revenues and expenditures or financial revenues and expenditures that violate state regulations should be subject to sanctions or penalties in accordance with the law, the audit institution shall make an audit decision within the scope of its statutory functions and powers; if the matter needs to be transferred to a relevant competent authority or unit for sanctions or penalties, the audit institution shall transfer it in accordance with the law.

The audit institution shall deliver the audit report and audit decision of the audit institution to the auditee and the relevant competent authorities or units, and report them to the audit institution at the next higher level. The audit decision shall take effect from the date of delivery.

Article 46 If an audit institution at a higher level believes that an audit decision made by an audit institution at a lower level violates relevant state regulations, it may instruct the audit institution at the lower level to modify or revoke it, or may directly make a decision to modify or revoke it when necessary.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 47 If an auditee, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses or delays providing materials related to the audit matter, or provides untrue or incomplete materials, or refuses or obstructs inspection, investigation, or verification of relevant information, the audit institution shall order it to make corrections, may circulate a notice of criticism, or give a warning; if it refuses to make corrections, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 48 If an auditee, in violation of the provisions of this Law, transfers, conceals, alters, or destroys financial and accounting materials, or business and management materials related to fiscal revenues and expenditures and financial revenues and expenditures, or transfers, conceals, or intentionally damages assets held that were obtained in violation of state regulations, and the audit institution believes that the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons should be given sanctions in accordance with the law, it shall propose handling suggestions to the auditee, or transfer the matter to the supervisory authority or relevant competent authority or unit for handling; the relevant authority or unit shall inform the audit institution in writing of the handling result; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 49 For acts of all departments at the corresponding level (including directly subordinate units) and governments at the next lower level that violate the budget or other acts of fiscal revenues and expenditures that violate state regulations, the audit institution, the people's government, or the relevant competent authority or unit shall, within the scope of their statutory functions and powers and in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, take the following handling measures depending on the circumstances:

(1) Ordering the payment of amounts that should be turned over within a specified time limit;

(2) Ordering the return of encroached state-owned assets within a specified time limit;

(3) Ordering the return of illegal gains within a specified time limit;

(4) Ordering handling in accordance with the relevant provisions of the unified national financial and accounting system;

(5) Other handling measures.

Article 50 For acts of financial revenues and expenditures of the auditee that violate state regulations, the audit institution, the people's government, or the relevant competent authority or unit shall, within the scope of their statutory functions and powers and in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, take the handling measures specified in the preceding article depending on the circumstances, and may impose penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 51 The auditee shall implement the audit decisions made by the audit institution within the scope of its statutory functions and powers.

If the audit institution orders the auditee to pay amounts that should be turned over in accordance with the law, and the auditee refuses to do so, the audit institution shall notify the relevant competent authority or unit, which shall withhold the amounts or take other handling measures in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, and inform the audit institution in writing of the handling result.

Article 52 The auditee shall rectify the problems found in the audit within the specified time, report the rectification status to the audit institution, and at the same time report to the people's government at the corresponding level or the relevant competent authority or unit, and make it public to the society in accordance with regulations.

People's governments at all levels and relevant competent authorities or units shall supervise the auditee's rectification of the problems found in the audit. Audit institutions shall track and inspect the rectification status of the auditee.Audit results and rectification status shall serve as important references for the assessment, appointment, removal, rewards, and penalties of leading officials, as well as for policy formulation and system improvement; those who refuse to rectify or engage in fraud during rectification shall be held legally liable.

Article 53: If the audited entity disagrees with the audit decision made by the audit authority concerning financial revenues and expenditures, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

If the audited entity disagrees with the audit decision made by the audit authority concerning fiscal revenues and expenditures, it may request a ruling from the people's government at the same level as the audit authority, and the ruling of that people's government shall be final.

Article 54: If the fiscal or financial revenues and expenditures of the audited entity violate state regulations, and the audit authority believes that the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel should be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law, it shall propose handling suggestions to the audited entity, or transfer the matter to the supervisory authority and relevant competent authorities or units for handling. The relevant authorities or units shall inform the audit authority in writing of the handling results.

Article 55: If the fiscal or financial revenues and expenditures of the audited entity violate the provisions of laws or administrative regulations and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 56: Anyone who retaliates against or frames an auditor shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 57: Auditors who abuse their power, engage in malpractice for personal gain, neglect their duties, or disclose or illegally provide to others known state secrets, work secrets, trade secrets, personal privacy, or personal information shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7: Supplementary Provisions

Article 58: The economic responsibility audit of leading officials and the audit of natural resource assets upon departure shall be carried out in accordance with this Law and relevant state regulations.

Article 59: The regulations on audit work for the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be formulated by the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Audit authorities and military audit institutions shall establish and improve coordination and cooperation mechanisms, and conduct joint audits on economic matters involving both military and civilian sectors in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 60: This Law shall take effect on January 1, 1995. The "Audit Regulations of the People's Republic of China" promulgated by the State Council on November 30, 1988, shall be simultaneously abolished.
