# Law of the People's Republic of China on Water and Soil Conservation

Adopted at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on June 29, 1991

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Certain Laws at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revised at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 25, 2010

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and controlling soil erosion, protecting and rationally utilizing water and soil resources, mitigating disasters caused by floods, droughts, wind and sand, improving the ecological environment, and ensuring sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 Any activity related to water and soil conservation conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall comply with this Law.

For the purpose of this Law, "water and soil conservation" means the preventive and control measures taken against soil erosion caused by natural factors and human activities.

Article 3 The work of water and soil conservation shall follow the principles of giving priority to prevention, prioritizing protection, comprehensive planning, integrated management, adapting measures to local conditions, focusing on key areas, scientific management, and emphasizing effectiveness.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the unified leadership over water and soil conservation work, incorporate such work into their respective plans for national economic and social development, allocate special funds for the tasks specified in water and soil conservation plans, and organize their implementation.

The State shall implement a system of target responsibility and assessment with rewards and penalties for local people's governments at all levels in key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion.

Article 5 The water administrative department under the State Council shall be in charge of water and soil conservation work nationwide.

The river basin management organs (hereinafter referred to as river basin management organs) established by the water administrative department under the State Council for major rivers and lakes designated by the State shall, within their respective jurisdictions, perform the duties of supervision and management of water and soil conservation according to law.

The water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of water and soil conservation work within their respective administrative regions.

The relevant departments of forestry, agriculture, land and resources, etc., under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions and duties, do a good job in the prevention and control of soil erosion.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall strengthen publicity and education on water and soil conservation, disseminate scientific knowledge about water and soil conservation, and enhance the public's awareness of water and soil conservation.

Article 7 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research on water and soil conservation, improves the level of science and technology in water and soil conservation, promotes advanced technologies for water and soil conservation, and trains scientific and technological personnel in water and soil conservation.

Article 8 Any unit or individual has the obligation to protect water and soil resources and prevent and control soil erosion, and has the right to report acts that damage water and soil resources or cause soil erosion.

Article 9 The State encourages and supports social forces to participate in water and soil conservation work.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in water and soil conservation work shall be commended and rewarded by the people's governments at or above the county level.

## Chapter II: Planning

Article 10 Water and soil conservation plans shall be formulated on the basis of the results of soil erosion surveys and the delimitation of key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion, following the principles of overall coordination and classified guidance.

Article 11 The water administrative department under the State Council shall organize national soil erosion surveys at regular intervals and announce the survey results.

The water administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for soil erosion surveys within their respective administrative regions and announce the survey results, and shall report the survey results to the water administrative department under the State Council for recordation before the announcement.

Article 12 People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the results of soil erosion surveys, delimit and announce key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion.

Areas with a relatively high potential risk of soil erosion shall be delimited as key areas for prevention of soil erosion; areas with severe soil erosion shall be delimited as key areas for control of soil erosion.

Article 13 The contents of a water and soil conservation plan shall include the status of soil erosion, the classification of soil erosion types, the objectives, tasks, and measures for the prevention and control of soil erosion, etc.

A water and soil conservation plan includes the overall arrangement for preventing and controlling soil erosion and protecting and rationally utilizing water and soil resources in a river basin or region, as well as the special arrangement for special work on water and soil conservation or for preventing and controlling soil erosion in a specific area based on the overall arrangement.

A water and soil conservation plan shall be coordinated with the overall plan for land utilization, the water resources plan, the urban and rural plan, and the environmental protection plan, etc.

When formulating a water and soil conservation plan, opinions from experts and the public shall be solicited.

Article 14 The water administrative department of a people's government at or above the county level shall, together with the relevant departments of the same people's government, formulate a water and soil conservation plan, and after it is approved by the same people's government or the department authorized by it, the water administrative department shall organize its implementation.

Once a water and soil conservation plan is approved, it shall be strictly implemented; if the approved plan needs to be modified due to actual circumstances, it shall be submitted to the original approving authority for approval in accordance with the planning formulation procedures.

Article 15 For plans concerning infrastructure construction, mineral resource development, urban construction, public service facility construction, etc., which may cause soil erosion during implementation, the organ organizing the formulation of the plan shall propose countermeasures and measures for the prevention and control of soil erosion in the plan, and shall solicit the opinions of the water administrative department of the same people's government before submitting the plan for approval.

## Chapter III: Prevention

Article 16 Local people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with water and soil conservation plans, take measures such as enclosure and protection, natural restoration, etc., organize units and individuals to plant trees and grass, increase the area covered by forests and grass, conserve water sources, and prevent and mitigate soil erosion.

Article 17 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management of activities such as soil extraction, sand quarrying, and stone quarrying to prevent and mitigate soil erosion.

It is prohibited to engage in activities such as soil extraction, sand quarrying, and stone quarrying that may cause soil erosion in areas prone to collapse, landslides, and debris flows. The scope of areas prone to collapse, landslides, and debris flows shall be delimited and announced by local people's governments at or above the county level. The delimitation of areas prone to collapse, landslides, and debris flows shall be coordinated with the areas prone to geological disasters and key prevention areas determined in the geological disaster prevention plan.

Article 18 In areas with severe soil erosion and fragile ecology, production and construction activities that may cause soil erosion shall be restricted or prohibited, and plants, sand crusts, crusts, lichens, etc., shall be strictly protected.

On the slopes and banks of erosion gullies, on the banks of rivers, and around lakes and reservoirs, the owners, users, or relevant management units of the land shall create plant protection belts. It is prohibited to reclaim or develop plant protection belts.

Article 19 The owners or users of water and soil conservation facilities shall strengthen the management and maintenance of such facilities, fulfill the responsibility for their care, and ensure their normal functioning.

Article 20 It is prohibited to reclaim and plant crops on steep slopes with a gradient of 25 degrees or more. If economic forests are planted on steep slopes with a gradient of 25 degrees or more, tree species shall be scientifically selected, the scale shall be reasonably determined, and water and soil conservation measures shall be taken to prevent soil erosion.

Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions, stipulate a prohibited reclamation gradient of less than 25 degrees. The scope of steep slopes where reclamation is prohibited shall be delimited and announced by the local county people's government.

Article 21 It is prohibited to destroy forests or grasslands for reclamation and to collect facai (a type of edible lichen). It is prohibited to strip turf, dig up tree stumps, or indiscriminately dig up cordyceps, licorice, ephedra, etc., in key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion.

Article 22 Timber harvesting shall be carried out in a reasonable manner, and clear-cutting shall be strictly controlled; for protection forests such as water conservation forests, soil and water conservation forests, and windbreak and sand-fixing forests, only tending and regeneration harvesting shall be carried out; measures to prevent soil erosion shall be taken in cutting areas and on skid trails, and reforestation shall be carried out promptly after harvesting.

When harvesting timber in forest areas, the harvesting plan shall include water and soil conservation measures. After the harvesting plan is approved by the forestry authority, its implementation shall be supervised by the forestry authority and the water administrative department.
Article 23 When planting trees, tending young forests, or planting Chinese medicinal herbs on slopes with a gradient of 5 degrees or more, water and soil conservation measures shall be taken.

When reclaiming and planting crops on barren slopes with a gradient below the prohibited reclamation gradient but 5 degrees or more, water and soil conservation measures shall be taken. Specific measures shall be formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government based on the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions.

Article 24 The site selection and route selection of production and construction projects shall avoid key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion; if avoidance is impossible, the prevention and control standards shall be raised, the construction process shall be optimized, the scope of surface disturbance and vegetation damage shall be reduced, and the soil erosion that may be caused shall be effectively controlled.

Article 25 For production and construction projects that may cause soil erosion and are initiated in mountainous areas, hilly areas, wind-sand areas, and other areas prone to soil erosion as determined by water and soil conservation plans, the production and construction unit shall prepare a water and soil conservation plan, submit it to the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level for approval, and take measures to prevent and control soil erosion in accordance with the approved water and soil conservation plan. If the unit is unable to prepare the plan, it shall entrust an institution with the corresponding technical capacity to do so.

A water and soil conservation plan shall include the scope, objectives, measures, and investment for the prevention and control of soil erosion.

After the water and soil conservation plan is approved, if the location or scale of the production and construction project undergoes major changes, the water and soil conservation plan shall be supplemented or modified and submitted to the original approving authority for approval. During the implementation of the water and soil conservation plan, if major changes need to be made to the water and soil conservation measures, the approval of the original approving authority shall be obtained.

The measures for the preparation and approval of water and soil conservation plans for production and construction projects shall be formulated by the water administrative department under the State Council.

Article 26 For a production and construction project that is required by law to prepare a water and soil conservation plan, if the production and construction unit fails to prepare such a plan or the plan has not been approved by the water administrative department, the production and construction project shall not commence construction.

Article 27 For a production and construction project that is required by law to prepare a water and soil conservation plan, the water and soil conservation facilities in the project shall be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project; upon the completion and acceptance of the production and construction project, the water and soil conservation facilities shall be accepted; if the water and soil conservation facilities have not been accepted or have failed the acceptance inspection, the production and construction project shall not be put into operation.

Article 28 For a production and construction project that is required by law to prepare a water and soil conservation plan, the sand, stone, soil, gangue, tailings, waste residue, etc., discharged during its production and construction activities shall be comprehensively utilized; if they cannot be utilized and must be discarded, they shall be stacked in the specially designated sites determined in the water and soil conservation plan, and measures shall be taken to ensure that no new hazards are created.

Article 29 The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level and the river basin management organs shall track and inspect the implementation of water and soil conservation plans for production and construction projects, and promptly address any problems found.

## Chapter IV: Control

Article 30 The State shall strengthen the construction of key water and soil conservation projects such as converting sloping farmland to terraced fields and building silt dams in key areas for prevention and key areas for control of soil erosion, and intensify ecological restoration efforts.

The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction management of key water and soil conservation projects, and establish and improve the operation and maintenance systems.

Article 31 The State shall strengthen the prevention and control of soil erosion in river source areas, drinking water source protection areas, and water conservation areas, raise funds through multiple channels, and incorporate the compensation for ecological benefits of water and soil conservation into the ecological benefit compensation system established by the State.

Article 32 If a production and construction project or other production and construction activity causes soil erosion, it shall be controlled.

If a production and construction project is initiated or other production and construction activities are carried out in mountainous areas, hilly areas, wind-sand areas, and other areas prone to soil erosion as determined by water and soil conservation plans, and if water and soil conservation facilities, landforms, and vegetation are damaged and the original water and soil conservation functions cannot be restored, a water and soil conservation compensation fee shall be paid, which shall be used exclusively for the prevention and control of soil erosion. The special prevention and control of soil erosion shall be organized and implemented by the water administrative department. The measures for the collection, use, and management of the water and soil conservation compensation fee shall be formulated by the financial department under the State Council and the price department under the State Council together with the water administrative department under the State Council.

The expenses for water and soil conservation incurred during the construction and production processes of a production and construction project shall be handled in accordance with the unified national financial and accounting system.

Article 33 The State encourages units and individuals to participate in the control of soil erosion in accordance with water and soil conservation plans, and provides support in terms of funds, technology, taxation, etc.

Article 34 The State encourages and supports the contracting of barren mountains, barren gullies, barren hills, and barren beaches for control, the prevention and control of soil erosion, the protection and improvement of the ecological environment, the promotion of the rational development and sustainable use of land resources, and the protection of the legitimate rights and interests of the parties to land contracts according to law.

When contracting barren mountains, barren gullies, barren hills, and barren beaches, or contracting rural land in areas with severe soil erosion, the land contract signed according to law shall include the responsibility for the prevention and control of soil erosion.

Article 35 In areas subject to water erosion, local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall organize units and individuals, taking the small watersheds formed by natural gullies and the sloping land on both sides as units, to adopt engineering measures, plant measures, protective tillage measures, etc., according to local conditions, to carry out comprehensive control of soil erosion on sloping farmland and in gullies.

In areas subject to wind erosion, local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall organize units and individuals, according to local conditions, to adopt measures such as rotational enclosure and rotational grazing, tree planting and grass sowing, setting up artificial sand barriers and grid forest belts, etc., to establish a windbreak and sand-fixing protection system.

In areas subject to gravity erosion, local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall organize units and individuals to adopt measures such as monitoring, runoff drainage, slope cutting and load reduction, support and slope stabilization, and construction of retaining works, etc., to establish a monitoring, forecasting, and early warning system.

Article 36 In drinking water source protection areas, local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall organize units and individuals to adopt measures for preventive protection, natural restoration, and comprehensive control, construct plant filter belts as supporting facilities, actively promote biogas, carry out the construction of clean small watersheds, strictly control the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, reduce non-point source pollution caused by soil erosion, and protect drinking water sources.

Article 37 If crops have already been planted on steep slopes where reclamation is prohibited, the land shall be returned to forest or grassland according to the relevant regulations of the State; if there is a shortage of arable land and it is truly difficult to return the land, terraced fields shall be built or other water and soil conservation measures shall be taken.

If crops are planted on sloping farmland with a gradient below the prohibited reclamation gradient, measures such as building terraced fields, regulating the slope water system, adopting water-conserving and soil-conserving tillage, or returning the land shall be taken according to different circumstances.

Article 38 The topsoil of the land occupied by production and construction activities shall be stripped, preserved, and utilized in layers, so as to achieve a balance between excavation and filling of earth and rock and reduce the scope of surface disturbance; for the storage sites of discarded sand, stone, soil, gangue, tailings, waste residue, etc., measures such as retaining, slope protection, flood control and drainage shall be taken. After the production and construction activities are completed, trees and grass shall be promptly planted on the bare land of borrow pits, excavation faces, and storage sites to restore vegetation, and closed tailings ponds shall be reclaimed.

When carrying out production and construction activities in arid and water-deficient areas, measures to prevent wind erosion shall be taken, and facilities for precipitation storage and infiltration shall be installed to make full use of precipitation resources.

Article 39 The State encourages and supports the adoption of the following measures conducive to water and soil conservation in mountainous areas, hilly areas, wind-sand areas, and other areas prone to soil erosion:

(1) No-tillage, contour farming, rotational tillage and cropping, grass-crop rotation, intercropping and interplanting, etc.;

(2) Enclosure and tending, rotational enclosure and rotational grazing, stall feeding and pen feeding;

(3) Developing biogas, energy-saving stoves, utilizing solar, wind, and hydro energy, replacing firewood with coal, electricity, and gas, etc.;

(4) Relocating people from ecologically fragile areas;

(5) Other measures conducive to water and soil conservation.

## Chapter V: Monitoring and Supervision

Article 40 The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the monitoring of water and soil conservation, and give full play to the role of water and soil conservation monitoring in government decision-making, economic and social development, and public services. People's governments at or above the county level shall guarantee the funds for water and soil conservation monitoring.

The water administrative department under the State Council shall improve the national water and soil conservation monitoring network and conduct dynamic monitoring of soil erosion nationwide.

Article 41 For large and medium-sized production and construction projects that may cause severe soil erosion, the production and construction unit shall, on its own or by entrusting an institution with the qualification for water and soil conservation monitoring, monitor the soil erosion caused by the production and construction activities, and regularly report the monitoring situation to the local water administrative department.

Engaging in water and soil conservation monitoring activities shall comply with the relevant national technical standards, specifications, and procedures to ensure the quality of monitoring.

Article 42 The water administrative department under the State Council and the water administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the water and soil conservation monitoring situation, regularly announce the following matters:

(1) The types, area, intensity, distribution, and changing trends of soil erosion;

(2) The hazards caused by soil erosion;

(3) The status of prevention and control of soil erosion.

Article 43 The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for supervising and inspecting the status of water and soil conservation. River basin management organs may, within their respective jurisdictions, exercise the supervision and inspection powers of the water administrative department under the State Council.

Article 44 When performing supervision and inspection duties according to law, water administration supervision and inspection personnel shall have the right to take the following measures:

(1) Requiring the unit or individual under inspection to provide relevant documents, licenses, and materials;

(2) Requiring the unit or individual under inspection to explain the relevant situation regarding the prevention and control of soil erosion;

(3) Entering the site for investigation and evidence collection.

If the unit or individual under inspection refuses to stop the illegal act, causing severe soil erosion, with the approval of the water administrative department, the tools, construction machinery, and equipment used for the illegal act may be sealed up or seized.

Article 45 When performing supervision and inspection duties according to law, water administration supervision and inspection personnel shall present their law enforcement credentials. The unit or individual under inspection shall cooperate with the water and soil conservation supervision and inspection work, truthfully report the situation, and provide relevant documents, licenses, and materials; they shall not refuse or obstruct the water administration supervision and inspection personnel from performing their official duties according to law.

Article 46 Disputes over soil erosion between different administrative regions shall be resolved through consultation; if consultation fails, the common people's government at the next higher level shall make a ruling.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

Article 47 If a water administrative department or another department exercising supervision and management powers according to this Law fails to make an administrative licensing decision or handle an approval document according to law, fails to investigate and deal with an illegal act discovered or a report of an illegal act received, or fails to perform other duties according to the provisions of this Law, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions according to law.Article 48: In violation of this Law, engaging in activities such as soil extraction, sand digging, or stone quarrying in collapse, landslide-prone areas, or mudslide-prone areas that may cause soil erosion, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan on individuals, and not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan on entities.

Article 49: In violation of this Law, cultivating crops on steep slopes above the prohibited gradient, or cultivating or developing within protected vegetation belts where such activities are prohibited, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and require remedial measures such as returning farmland and restoring vegetation; based on the area of cultivation or development, a fine of not more than 2 yuan per square meter may be imposed on individuals, and not more than 10 yuan per square meter on entities.

Article 50: In violation of this Law, clearing forests or grasslands for cultivation, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Forest Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 51: In violation of this Law, collecting facai (a type of algae), or stripping turf, digging tree stumps, or indiscriminately digging cordyceps, licorice, ephedra, etc., in key prevention areas and key treatment areas of soil erosion, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act, require remedial measures, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed.

For violations of the preceding paragraph in grassland areas, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 52: Failing to take measures to prevent soil erosion in accordance with the law when logging in forest areas, the forestry department or water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a specified period and require remedial measures; if soil erosion has occurred, the water administrative department shall impose a fine of not less than 2 yuan but not more than 10 yuan per square meter based on the area of soil erosion.

Article 53: In violation of this Law, committing any of the following acts, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and require completion of formalities within a specified period; if formalities are not completed within the specified period, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel of the production and construction unit shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law:

(1) A production or construction project that is legally required to prepare a soil and water conservation plan fails to prepare such a plan, or the prepared plan has not been approved before construction begins;

(2) Significant changes occur in the location or scale of a production or construction project, and the soil and water conservation plan is not supplemented or modified, or the supplemented or modified plan has not been approved by the original approving authority;

(3) During the implementation of the soil and water conservation plan, major changes are made to soil and water conservation measures without approval from the original approving authority.

Article 54: In violation of this Law, putting a production or construction project into operation without the soil and water conservation facilities having been inspected and accepted, or having been inspected and found unqualified, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of production or use until the inspection is passed, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 55: In violation of this Law, dumping sand, stone, soil, gangue, tailings, waste residue, etc., in areas other than the designated storage sites specified in the soil and water conservation plan, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of the illegal act and require cleanup within a specified period, imposing a fine of not less than 10 yuan but not more than 20 yuan per cubic meter based on the amount dumped; if cleanup is not completed within the specified period, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level may designate a unit with cleanup capability to carry out the cleanup, with the costs borne by the violator.

Article 56: In violation of this Law, causing soil erosion from initiating a production or construction project or engaging in other production or construction activities without carrying out remediation, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order remediation within a specified period; if remediation is not completed within the specified period, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level may designate a unit with remediation capability to carry out the remediation, with the costs borne by the violator.

Article 57: In violation of this Law, refusing to pay soil and water conservation compensation fees, the water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order payment within a specified period; if payment is not made within the specified period, a daily late fee of 0.05% of the overdue amount shall be charged from the date of default, and a fine of not more than three times the payable soil and water conservation compensation fee may be imposed.

Article 58: In violation of this Law, causing harm from soil erosion, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose public security administrative penalties in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 59: The institution responsible for soil and water conservation work determined by the local people's government at or above the county level based on local conditions shall exercise the duties of the water administrative department as stipulated in this Law regarding soil and water conservation.

Article 60: This Law shall come into effect on March 1, 2011.
