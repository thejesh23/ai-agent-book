# Construction Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on November 1, 1997

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 20th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 22, 2011

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Eight Laws Including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to strengthening the supervision and administration of construction activities, maintaining the order of the construction market, ensuring the quality and safety of construction projects, and promoting the healthy development of the construction industry.

Article 2 All entities and individuals engaged in construction activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China, and the supervision and administration of such activities, shall comply with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "construction activities" refer to the construction of various types of buildings and their ancillary facilities, as well as the installation of supporting lines, pipelines, and equipment.

Article 3 Construction activities shall ensure the quality and safety of construction projects and comply with the national safety standards for construction projects.

Article 4 The State supports the development of the construction industry, encourages scientific and technological research in construction, improves the design level of buildings, promotes energy conservation and environmental protection, and advocates the use of advanced technologies, advanced equipment, advanced processes, new building materials, and modern management methods.

Article 5 Entities and individuals engaged in construction activities shall abide by laws and regulations and shall not harm the public interest or the legitimate rights and interests of others.

No entity or individual may obstruct or impede construction activities carried out in accordance with the law.

Article 6 The competent administrative department for construction under the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and administration over construction activities nationwide.

## Chapter II Construction Permits

### Section 1 Construction Permits for Construction Projects

Article 7 Before commencing a construction project, the project owner shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, apply to the competent administrative department for construction of the people's government at or above the county level in the project location for a construction permit; however, small-scale projects below the threshold determined by the competent administrative department for construction under the State Council are exempted.

For construction projects for which an approval report has been approved in accordance with the authority and procedures prescribed by the State Council, no construction permit is required.

Article 8 To apply for a construction permit, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) The approval procedures for the use of the land for the construction project have been completed;

(2) Where a construction project planning permit is required by law, such permit has been obtained;

(3) Where demolition is required, the progress of demolition meets the construction requirements;

(4) The construction enterprise has been determined;

(5) There are financial arrangements, construction drawings, and technical data sufficient to meet the construction needs;

(6) Specific measures to ensure project quality and safety are in place.

The competent administrative department for construction shall issue the construction permit to qualified applicants within seven days from the date of receiving the application.

Article 9 The project owner shall commence construction within three months from the date of obtaining the construction permit. If construction cannot be commenced on schedule for justified reasons, an application for extension shall be made to the issuing authority; extensions are limited to two times, each not exceeding three months. If construction is neither commenced nor an extension applied for, or if the extension period expires, the construction permit shall automatically become void.

Article 10 If a construction project under construction is suspended for justified reasons, the project owner shall, within one month from the date of suspension, report to the issuing authority and properly carry out the maintenance and management of the construction project.

When construction is resumed, the project owner shall report to the issuing authority; if construction is resumed after a suspension of one year or more, the project owner shall report to the issuing authority for verification of the construction permit.

Article 11 For a construction project whose commencement has been approved in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council, if it cannot be commenced on schedule or is suspended for justified reasons, the project owner shall promptly report the situation to the approving authority. If the commencement is delayed for more than six months for justified reasons, the approval procedures for the commencement report shall be re-applied for.

### Section 2 Qualifications for Practicing

Article 12 Construction enterprises, surveying units, design units, and project supervision units engaged in construction activities shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a registered capital that complies with the provisions of the State;

(2) Having professional and technical personnel with statutory practice qualifications commensurate with the construction activities they undertake;

(3) Having the technical equipment necessary for engaging in the relevant construction activities;

(4) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 13 Construction enterprises, surveying units, design units, and project supervision units engaged in construction activities shall be classified into different qualification grades based on their registered capital, professional and technical personnel, technical equipment, and completed construction project performance. Only after passing the qualification review and obtaining the corresponding qualification certificate may they engage in construction activities within the scope permitted by their qualification grade.

Article 14 Professional and technical personnel engaged in construction activities shall obtain the corresponding practice qualification certificates in accordance with the law and engage in construction activities within the scope permitted by their practice qualification certificates.

## Chapter III Contracting and Subcontracting of Construction Projects

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 15 The project owner and the contractor shall enter into a written contract in accordance with the law, specifying the rights and obligations of both parties.

The project owner and the contractor shall fully perform the obligations stipulated in the contract. Failure to perform obligations as agreed in the contract shall result in liability for breach of contract in accordance with the law.

Article 16 Tendering and bidding activities for the contracting and subcontracting of construction projects shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, and equal competition, and the contractor shall be selected on a merit basis.

For matters not covered by this Law regarding the tendering and bidding of construction projects, the provisions of the relevant laws on tendering and bidding shall apply.

Article 17 The project owner and its staff shall not accept bribes, kickbacks, or seek other benefits during the contracting of construction projects.

The contractor and its staff shall not use improper means such as bribing the project owner or its staff, offering kickbacks, or giving other benefits to secure projects.

Article 18 The cost of a construction project shall be agreed upon in the contract between the project owner and the contractor in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State. For projects contracted through public tender, the agreement on cost shall comply with the provisions of the tendering and bidding laws.

The project owner shall disburse project funds promptly in accordance with the contract.

### Section 2 Contracting

Article 19 Construction projects shall be contracted through tender in accordance with the law. Projects unsuitable for tender contracting may be contracted directly.

Article 20 Where a construction project is contracted through public tender, the project owner shall, in accordance with the statutory procedures and methods, issue a tender notice and provide tender documents containing the main technical requirements of the tendered project, the main contract terms, the criteria and methods for bid evaluation, and the procedures for bid opening, evaluation, and award.

Bid opening shall be conducted publicly at the time and place specified in the tender documents. After bid opening, bids shall be evaluated and compared according to the evaluation criteria and procedures specified in the tender documents, and the winning bidder shall be selected on a merit basis from among the bidders with the corresponding qualifications.

Article 21 The bid opening, evaluation, and award for construction project tenders shall be organized and implemented by the project owner in accordance with the law and shall be subject to the supervision of the relevant administrative departments.

Article 22 Where a construction project is contracted through tender, the project owner shall award the project to the contractor that has won the bid in accordance with the law. Where a construction project is contracted directly, the project owner shall award the project to a contractor with the corresponding qualifications.

Article 23 The government and its subordinate departments shall not abuse administrative power to restrict the project owner from awarding a construction project contracted through tender to a designated contractor.

Article 24 General contracting for construction projects is encouraged, and the piecemeal contracting of construction projects is prohibited.

The project owner may contract out the surveying, design, construction, and equipment procurement of a construction project together to a single general contractor, or may contract out one or more of these items to a general contractor; however, a construction project that should be completed by a single contractor shall not be divided into several parts and contracted out to multiple contractors.

Article 25 Where, according to the contract, construction materials, building components, and equipment are to be procured by the contractor, the project owner shall not designate the contractor to purchase specific construction materials, building components, or equipment for the project, nor designate specific manufacturers or suppliers.

### Section 3 Subcontracting

Article 26 A unit subcontracting a construction project shall hold a qualification certificate obtained in accordance with the law and undertake projects within the business scope permitted by its qualification grade.

Construction enterprises are prohibited from undertaking projects beyond the business scope permitted by their qualification grade or from undertaking projects in the name of other construction enterprises in any form. Construction enterprises are prohibited from allowing other units or individuals to use their qualification certificates or business licenses to undertake projects in their name in any form.

Article 27 Large-scale construction projects or structurally complex construction projects may be jointly undertaken by two or more contractors. The parties to a joint undertaking shall bear joint and several liability for the performance of the subcontract.

Where two or more units with different qualification grades jointly undertake a project, they shall undertake the project within the business scope permitted by the unit with the lower qualification grade.

Article 28 Contractors are prohibited from subcontracting the entire construction project they have contracted to others, and from dividing the entire contracted project into parts and subcontracting them separately to others in the name of subcontracting.

Article 29 A general contractor of a construction project may subcontract part of the contracted project to subcontractors with the corresponding qualifications; however, except for subcontracting stipulated in the general contract, the consent of the project owner must be obtained. For construction general contracting, the construction of the main structure of the project must be completed by the general contractor itself.

The general contractor of a construction project shall be responsible to the project owner in accordance with the general contract; the subcontractor shall be responsible to the general contractor in accordance with the subcontract. The general contractor and the subcontractor shall bear joint and several liability to the project owner for the subcontracted project.

General contractors are prohibited from subcontracting projects to units without the corresponding qualifications. Subcontractors are prohibited from re-subcontracting the projects they have contracted.

## Chapter IV Project Supervision

Article 30 The State promotes a project supervision system for construction projects.

The State Council may specify the scope of construction projects for which mandatory supervision is required.

Article 31 For construction projects subject to supervision, the project owner shall entrust a project supervision unit with the corresponding qualifications to carry out supervision. The project owner and the entrusted project supervision unit shall enter into a written entrustment contract for supervision.

Article 32 Project supervision shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, relevant technical standards, design documents, and construction project contracts, supervise the contractor's construction quality, construction period, and use of construction funds on behalf of the project owner.

If a project supervisor considers that the construction does not meet the design requirements, construction technical standards, or contract terms, they have the right to require the construction enterprise to make corrections.

If a project supervisor discovers that the project design does not meet the quality standards for construction projects or the quality requirements stipulated in the contract, they shall report to the project owner and request the design unit to make corrections.

Article 33 Before implementing project supervision, the project owner shall notify the supervised construction enterprise in writing of the entrusted project supervision unit, the content of supervision, and the scope of supervision authority.

Article 34 A project supervision unit shall undertake project supervision business within the scope permitted by its qualification grade.

A project supervision unit shall perform its supervision duties objectively and impartially based on the entrustment of the project owner.

A project supervision unit shall not have an affiliation or other interest relationship with the contractor of the supervised project or with the suppliers of construction materials, building components, and equipment.

A project supervision unit shall not transfer its project supervision business.

Article 35 If a project supervision unit fails to perform its supervision obligations as stipulated in the entrustment contract, fails to inspect items that should be inspected, or fails to inspect in accordance with regulations, causing losses to the project owner, it shall bear corresponding liability for compensation.

If a project supervision unit colludes with the contractor to seek illegal benefits for the contractor, causing losses to the project owner, it shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the contractor.

## Chapter V Safety Management of Construction Projects

Article 36 Safety management of construction projects must adhere to the principles of prioritizing safety and focusing on prevention, and establish a sound responsibility system for safety production and a system of mass prevention and mass control.

Article 37 The design of construction projects shall comply with the building safety regulations and technical specifications formulated in accordance with the provisions of the State, ensuring the safety performance of the project.

Article 38 When preparing a construction organization design, a construction enterprise shall formulate corresponding safety technical measures based on the characteristics of the construction project; for highly specialized engineering projects, a special safety construction organization design shall be prepared, and safety technical measures shall be adopted.

Article 39 A construction enterprise shall take measures at the construction site to maintain safety, prevent dangers, and prevent fires; where conditions permit, the construction site shall be enclosed for management.

If the construction site may cause damage to adjacent buildings, structures, or special working environments, the construction enterprise shall take safety protection measures.

Article 40 The project owner shall provide the construction enterprise with information on underground pipelines related to the construction site, and the construction enterprise shall take measures to protect them.

Article 41 A construction enterprise shall comply with the provisions of laws and regulations on environmental protection and safety production, and take measures to control and handle the pollution and hazards caused by various dust, exhaust gases, wastewater, solid waste, noise, and vibration at the construction site to the environment.

Article 42 Under any of the following circumstances, the project owner shall apply for approval procedures in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State:

(1) Where temporary occupation of land beyond the scope approved by the plan is required;

(2) Where public facilities such as roads, pipelines, electricity, or postal and telecommunications may be damaged;

(3) Where temporary water or power cuts or interruption of road traffic is required;

(4) Where blasting operations are required;

(5) Other circumstances where approval procedures are required by laws or regulations.

Article 43 The competent administrative department for construction shall be responsible for the management of construction safety production and shall accept the guidance and supervision of the competent administrative department for labor on construction safety production in accordance with the law.

Article 44 A construction enterprise must strengthen the management of construction safety production in accordance with the law, implement the responsibility system for safety production, and take effective measures to prevent casualties and other safety production accidents.

The legal representative of a construction enterprise shall be responsible for the safety production of the enterprise.

Article 45 Safety at the construction site shall be the responsibility of the construction enterprise. Where construction general contracting is implemented, the general contractor shall be responsible. The subcontractor shall be responsible to the general contractor and shall comply with the general contractor's safety production management at the construction site.

Article 46 A construction enterprise shall establish and improve a labor safety production education and training system, and strengthen safety production education and training for its employees; personnel who have not received safety production education and training shall not take up their posts.

Article 47 Construction enterprises and their workers shall, during the construction process, comply with relevant laws and regulations on safety production and the safety rules and regulations of the construction industry, and shall not give illegal commands or engage in illegal operations. Workers have the right to propose improvements to work procedures and conditions that affect personal health, and have the right to obtain protective equipment necessary for safety production. Workers have the right to criticize, report, and accuse acts that endanger life safety and personal health.

Article 48 A construction enterprise shall, in accordance with the law, pay work-related injury insurance premiums for its employees to participate in work-related injury insurance. Enterprises are encouraged to purchase accident insurance for employees engaged in hazardous operations and pay the insurance premiums.

Article 49 For renovation projects involving changes to the main structure or load-bearing structure of a building, the project owner shall, before construction, entrust the original design unit or a design unit with corresponding qualifications to propose a design plan; construction shall not proceed without a design plan.

Article 50 Building demolition shall be undertaken by a construction unit with the conditions to ensure safety, and the person in charge of the construction unit shall be responsible for safety.

Article 51 When an accident occurs during construction, the construction enterprise shall take emergency measures to reduce casualties and accident losses, and promptly report to the relevant departments in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

## Chapter VI Quality Management of Construction Projects

Article 52 The quality of surveying, design, and construction of construction projects must meet the requirements of the relevant national safety standards for construction projects. The specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

When national standards for construction project safety can no longer meet the requirements for ensuring building safety, they shall be revised promptly.

Article 53 The State promotes a quality system certification system for units engaged in construction activities. Units engaged in construction activities may, on a voluntary basis, apply for quality system certification from the national product quality supervision and management department under the State Council or from certification bodies recognized by the department authorized by the State Council. Upon passing the certification, the certification body shall issue a quality system certification certificate.

Article 54 The project owner shall not, for any reason, require the design unit or construction enterprise to violate laws, administrative regulations, and construction project quality and safety standards to reduce project quality during engineering design or construction operations.

Design units and construction enterprises shall refuse any requirements made by the project owner in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph to reduce project quality.

Article 55 Where a construction project is subject to general contracting, the project quality shall be the responsibility of the general contractor. If the general contractor subcontracts the construction project to other units, it shall bear joint and several liability with the subcontractor for the quality of the subcontracted project. The subcontractor shall accept the quality management of the general contractor.

Article 56 Surveying and design units of construction projects must be responsible for the quality of their surveying and design. Surveying and design documents shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, construction project quality and safety standards, technical specifications for construction project surveying and design, and the terms of the contract. The specifications, models, performance, and other technical indicators of construction materials, building components, and equipment selected in the design documents shall be indicated, and their quality requirements must meet the standards prescribed by the State.

Article 57 A design unit shall not designate manufacturers or suppliers for the construction materials, building components, and equipment selected in the design documents.

Article 58 A construction enterprise shall be responsible for the construction quality of the project.

A construction enterprise must construct in accordance with the engineering design drawings and construction technical standards, and shall not cut corners or use inferior materials. Modifications to the engineering design shall be the responsibility of the original design unit, and the construction enterprise shall not modify the engineering design without authorization.

Article 59 A construction enterprise must inspect construction materials, building components, and equipment in accordance with the engineering design requirements, construction technical standards, and the terms of the contract; those that are unqualified shall not be used.Article 60 Within the reasonable service life of a building, the quality of the foundation works and the main structure must be ensured.

Upon completion of a construction project, the roof and walls must not have quality defects such as leakage or cracking; any discovered quality defects shall be repaired by the construction enterprise.

Article 61 A construction project submitted for completion acceptance must meet the prescribed quality standards for construction projects, have complete technical and economic documentation and a signed warranty certificate, and satisfy other completion conditions stipulated by the state.

A construction project may only be delivered for use after passing completion acceptance; it shall not be delivered for use if it has not been accepted or has failed acceptance.

Article 62 A quality warranty system shall be implemented for construction projects.

The scope of warranty for a construction project shall include foundation works, main structure works, roof waterproofing works, and other civil engineering works, as well as the installation of electrical wiring, water supply and drainage pipelines, heating and cooling systems, and other items; the warranty period shall be determined based on the principle of ensuring normal use within the reasonable service life of the building and safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of users. The specific warranty scope and minimum warranty period shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 63 Any entity or individual has the right to report, accuse, or complain to the construction administrative department or other relevant departments about quality accidents or quality defects in construction projects.

## Chapter 7 Legal Liability

Article 64 Whoever, in violation of this Law, commences construction without obtaining a construction permit or without approval of the commencement report, shall be ordered to rectify; if the conditions for commencement are not met, construction shall be ordered to stop, and a fine may be imposed.

Article 65 If the contracting entity awards a project to a contractor that does not have the corresponding qualifications, or in violation of this Law, divides a construction project into parts for contracting, it shall be ordered to rectify and fined.

Whoever undertakes a project beyond its own qualification grade shall be ordered to stop the illegal act, fined, and may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification and have its qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, its qualification certificate shall be revoked; any illegal income shall be confiscated.

Whoever undertakes a project without obtaining a qualification certificate shall be banned and fined; any illegal income shall be confiscated.

Whoever obtains a qualification certificate by fraudulent means shall have its qualification certificate revoked and be fined; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 66 If a construction enterprise transfers or lends its qualification certificate or otherwise allows others to undertake projects in its name, it shall be ordered to rectify, its illegal income shall be confiscated, and it shall be fined; it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification and have its qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, its qualification certificate shall be revoked. For losses caused by the project undertaken not meeting the prescribed quality standards, the construction enterprise shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the entity or individual using its name.

Article 67 If a contractor subcontracts the project it has contracted, or sublets it in violation of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify, its illegal income shall be confiscated, and it shall be fined; it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification and have its qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, its qualification certificate shall be revoked.

If a contractor commits the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall bear joint and several liability for compensation with the entity that accepts the subcontract or sublet for losses caused by the subcontracted or illegally sublet project not meeting the prescribed quality standards.

Article 68 Soliciting, accepting, or offering bribes in project contracting and subcontracting, if constituting a crime, shall be pursued for criminal liability according to law; if not constituting a crime, a fine shall be imposed respectively, the bribery property shall be confiscated, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions.

For a contractor that offers bribes in project contracting, in addition to being punished according to the preceding paragraph, it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, have its qualification grade lowered, or have its qualification certificate revoked.

Article 69 If a project supervision entity colludes with the construction entity or construction enterprise to practice fraud and lower project quality, it shall be ordered to rectify, fined, and have its qualification grade lowered or its qualification certificate revoked; any illegal income shall be confiscated; if losses are caused, it shall bear joint and several liability for compensation; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If a project supervision entity transfers its supervision business, it shall be ordered to rectify, its illegal income shall be confiscated, and it may be ordered to suspend operations for rectification and have its qualification grade lowered; if the circumstances are serious, its qualification certificate shall be revoked.

Article 70 Whoever, in violation of this Law, commences renovation works involving changes to the main structure or load-bearing structure without authorization shall be ordered to rectify and fined; if losses are caused, it shall bear liability for compensation; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 71 If a construction enterprise, in violation of this Law, fails to take measures to eliminate hidden dangers of construction safety accidents, it shall be ordered to rectify and may be fined; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, have its qualification grade lowered, or have its qualification certificate revoked; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If management personnel of a construction enterprise give illegal commands or force workers to operate dangerously, thereby causing major casualties or other serious consequences, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 72 If a construction entity, in violation of this Law, requires the architectural design entity or construction enterprise to violate construction quality and safety standards and lower project quality, it shall be ordered to rectify and may be fined; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 73 If an architectural design entity fails to design in accordance with construction quality and safety standards, it shall be ordered to rectify and fined; if a project quality accident is caused, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, have its qualification grade lowered or its qualification certificate revoked, its illegal income shall be confiscated, and it shall be fined; if losses are caused, it shall bear liability for compensation; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 74 If a construction enterprise uses substandard materials, cuts corners in construction, uses substandard building materials, components, and equipment, or engages in other acts not in accordance with the engineering design drawings or construction technical standards, it shall be ordered to rectify and fined; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, have its qualification grade lowered, or have its qualification certificate revoked; if the construction project quality fails to meet the prescribed quality standards, it shall be responsible for rework and repair and compensate for the losses caused; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 75 If a construction enterprise, in violation of this Law, fails to fulfill its warranty obligations or delays in fulfilling them, it shall be ordered to rectify and may be fined, and it shall bear liability for compensation for losses caused by quality defects such as roof and wall leakage and cracking within the warranty period.

Article 76 The administrative penalties of ordering suspension of operations for rectification, lowering of qualification grade, and revocation of qualification certificates as provided in this Law shall be decided by the authority that issued the qualification certificate; other administrative penalties shall be decided by the construction administrative department or relevant departments within the scope of their powers according to law and the regulations of the State Council.

If a qualification certificate is revoked according to this Law, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke its business license.

Article 77 If, in violation of this Law, a qualification certificate of a corresponding grade is issued to an entity that does not meet the conditions for that grade, the superior authority shall order the recovery of the issued qualification certificate, and administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 78 If staff members of the government and its subordinate departments, in violation of this Law, restrict the contracting entity from awarding a project subject to tender to a designated contractor, the superior authority shall order rectification; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 79 If the department responsible for issuing construction project permits and its staff issue a construction permit for a project that does not meet the conditions for construction, or if the department responsible for project quality supervision and inspection or completion acceptance and its staff issue a quality compliance document for a substandard project or accept it as a qualified project, the superior authority shall order rectification and impose administrative sanctions on the responsible personnel; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if losses are caused, the department shall bear corresponding liability for compensation.

Article 80 Within the reasonable service life of a building, anyone who suffers damage due to substandard construction project quality has the right to claim compensation from the responsible party.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 81 The provisions of this Law concerning construction permits, qualification review of construction enterprises, contracting and subcontracting of construction projects, prohibition of subcontracting, project supervision, and construction safety and quality management shall apply to construction activities of other specialized construction projects, with specific measures to be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 82 The construction administrative department and other relevant departments, when exercising supervision and management over construction activities, shall not charge any fees other than those prescribed by the relevant regulations of the State Council.

Article 83 Construction activities for small-scale housing construction projects determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be carried out with reference to this Law.

The renovation of memorial buildings and ancient buildings legally designated as cultural relics protection shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant laws and regulations on cultural relics protection.

This Law shall not apply to construction activities for emergency rescue and disaster relief, other temporary housing construction, and low-rise residential buildings built by farmers themselves.

Article 84 Specific management measures for construction activities of military housing construction projects shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 85 This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 1998.
