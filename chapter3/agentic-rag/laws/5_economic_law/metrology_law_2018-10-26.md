# Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on September 6, 1985

First Amendment on August 27, 2009, according to the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Third Amendment on April 24, 2015, according to the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Fourth Amendment on December 27, 2017, according to the Decision on Amending the Tendering and Bidding Law of the People's Republic of China and the Metrology Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen metrological supervision and management, ensure the uniformity of the national system of measurement units and the accuracy and reliability of measurement values, facilitate the development of production, trade, and science and technology, meet the needs of socialist modernization, and safeguard the interests of the State and the people.

Article 2 Within the territory of the People's Republic of China, the establishment of primary measurement standards and working measurement standards, the performance of metrological verification, and the manufacture, repair, sale, and use of measuring instruments must comply with this Law.

Article 3 The State adopts a system of legal measurement units.

The International System of Units (SI) units and other measurement units selected by the State are the national legal measurement units. The names and symbols of the national legal measurement units shall be promulgated by the State Council.

The administrative measures for the use of non-legal measurement units due to special needs shall be formulated separately by the metrological administrative department of the State Council.

Article 4 The metrological administrative department of the State Council shall exercise unified supervision and management over metrological work nationwide.

The metrological administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise supervision and management over metrological work within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Primary Measurement Standards, Working Measurement Standards, and Metrological Verification

Article 5 The metrological administrative department of the State Council is responsible for establishing various primary measurement standards, which serve as the highest basis for unifying measurement values nationwide.

Article 6 The metrological administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level may, based on the needs of their respective regions, establish public working measurement standards, which shall be used only after passing the examination and approval presided over by the metrological administrative department of the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 7 The relevant competent departments under the State Council and the relevant competent departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the special needs of their departments, establish working measurement standards for use within their departments. Their highest working measurement standards shall be used only after passing the examination and approval presided over by the metrological administrative department of the people's government at the same level.

Article 8 Enterprises and institutions may, based on their needs, establish working measurement standards for use within their own units. Their highest working measurement standards shall be used only after passing the examination and approval presided over by the relevant metrological administrative department of the people's government.

Article 9 The metrological administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall implement compulsory verification for public working measurement standards, the highest working measurement standards used by departments, enterprises, and institutions, as well as working measuring instruments included in the compulsory verification catalogue used for trade settlement, safety protection, medical and health care, and environmental monitoring. Those that have not applied for verification as required or have failed verification shall not be used. The catalogue and administrative measures for working measuring instruments subject to compulsory verification shall be formulated by the State Council.

For other measurement standards and working measuring instruments not covered by the preceding paragraph, the user units shall conduct regular verification on their own or send them to other metrological verification institutions for verification.

Article 10 Metrological verification must be carried out in accordance with the national metrological verification system table. The national metrological verification system table shall be formulated by the metrological administrative department of the State Council.

Metrological verification must comply with metrological verification regulations. National metrological verification regulations shall be formulated by the metrological administrative department of the State Council. In the absence of national metrological verification regulations, the relevant competent departments under the State Council and the metrological administrative departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate departmental metrological verification regulations and local metrological verification regulations, respectively.

Article 11 Metrological verification work shall be carried out locally and nearby, following the principle of economic rationality.

## Chapter III Management of Measuring Instruments

Article 12 Enterprises and institutions that manufacture or repair measuring instruments must possess facilities, personnel, and verification instruments and equipment appropriate for the measuring instruments they manufacture or repair.

Article 13 An enterprise or institution that manufactures a new type of measuring instrument that it has not previously produced must have the metrological performance of its sample examined and approved by the metrological administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level before it can be put into production.

Article 14 No unit or individual may manufacture, sell, or import measuring instruments using non-legal measurement units in violation of regulations.

Article 15 Enterprises and institutions that manufacture or repair measuring instruments must verify the measuring instruments they manufacture or repair, ensure that the metrological performance of the products is qualified, and issue product certificates of conformity for qualified products.

Article 16 When using measuring instruments, their accuracy shall not be impaired, nor shall the interests of the State and consumers be harmed.

Article 17 Individual industrial and commercial households may manufacture and repair simple measuring instruments.

The scope and administrative measures for individual industrial and commercial households to manufacture and repair measuring instruments shall be formulated by the metrological administrative department of the State Council.

## Chapter IV Metrological Supervision

Article 18 The metrological administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, conduct supervision and inspection over the manufacture, repair, sale, import, and use of measuring instruments, as well as related metrological activities such as metrological verification. The relevant units and individuals shall not refuse or obstruct such inspections.

Article 19 The metrological administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level may, as needed, appoint metrological supervisors. The administrative measures for metrological supervisors shall be formulated by the metrological administrative department of the State Council.

Article 20 The metrological administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level may, as needed, establish metrological verification institutions or authorize the metrological verification institutions of other units to perform compulsory verification and other verification and testing tasks.

Personnel performing the verification and testing tasks specified in the preceding paragraph must pass the relevant examinations.

Article 21 Disputes arising from the accuracy of measuring instruments shall be settled based on the data verified by primary measurement standards or public working measurement standards.

Article 22 Product quality inspection institutions that provide notarized data to the public must have their metrological verification and testing capabilities and reliability examined and approved by the metrological administrative department of the people's government at or above the provincial level.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 23 Anyone who manufactures or sells new types of measuring instruments that have not passed the examination and approval shall be ordered to stop manufacturing or selling such new products, have their illegal gains confiscated, and may also be fined.

Article 24 Anyone who manufactures, repairs, or sells measuring instruments that are not qualified shall have their illegal gains confiscated and may also be fined.

Article 25 If measuring instruments subject to compulsory verification are used without applying for verification as required or after failing verification, the user shall be ordered to stop using them and may also be fined.

Article 26 If the use of unqualified measuring instruments or the impairment of the accuracy of measuring instruments causes losses to the State or consumers, the user shall be ordered to compensate for the losses, and the measuring instruments and illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine may also be imposed.

Article 27 Anyone who manufactures, sells, or uses measuring instruments with the intent to deceive consumers shall have their measuring instruments and illegal gains confiscated and be fined; if the circumstances are serious, the individual or the person directly responsible of the unit shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 28 If, in violation of this Law, the manufacture, repair, or sale of unqualified measuring instruments causes personal injury or death or significant property losses, the individual or the person directly responsible of the unit shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 29 If metrological supervisors violate the law or neglect their duties, and the circumstances are serious, they shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the circumstances are minor, they shall be subject to administrative sanctions.

Article 30 The administrative penalties provided for in this Law shall be decided by the metrological administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level.

Article 31 If a party disagrees with a decision on administrative penalties, it may file a lawsuit with a people's court within 15 days from the date of receiving the notice of penalty; if the party does not file a lawsuit or comply with the decision on administrative penalties such as fines or confiscation of illegal gains within the specified period, the organ that made the decision on administrative penalties shall apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 32 The supervision and management measures for metrological work in the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the national defense science, technology, and industry system shall be formulated separately by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 33 The metrological administrative department of the State Council shall formulate implementing regulations in accordance with this Law, which shall be submitted to the State Council for approval before implementation.

Article 34 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 1986.
