# Asset Appraisal Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purposes of regulating asset appraisal activities, protecting the lawful rights and interests of parties involved in asset appraisal and the public interest, promoting the healthy development of the asset appraisal industry, and maintaining the order of the socialist market economy.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "asset appraisal" (hereinafter referred to as "appraisal") means the professional service activity in which an appraisal institution and its appraisal professionals, based on a commission, assess and estimate immovable property, movable property, intangible assets, enterprise value, asset losses, or other economic rights and interests, and issue an appraisal report.

**Article 3** Where a natural person, legal person, or other organization needs to determine the value of the subject matter of an appraisal, it may voluntarily commission an appraisal institution to conduct the appraisal.

Where matters involve state-owned assets or the public interest, and laws or administrative regulations require an appraisal (hereinafter referred to as "statutory appraisal"), the appraisal shall be conducted by commissioning an appraisal institution in accordance with the law.

**Article 4** An appraisal institution and its appraisal professionals shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and appraisal standards when conducting business, and shall adhere to the principles of independence, objectivity, and impartiality.

An appraisal institution and its appraisal professionals conducting business in accordance with the law shall be protected by law.

**Article 5** An appraisal professional engaged in appraisal business shall join an appraisal institution and may only conduct business in one appraisal institution.

**Article 6** The appraisal industry may, in accordance with the law, establish industry associations based on professional fields, implement self-regulation, and accept supervision from relevant appraisal administrative departments and public supervision.

**Article 7** The relevant appraisal administrative departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with their respective functions and division of responsibilities, supervise and manage the appraisal industry.

The relevant appraisal administrative departments of the local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall, in accordance with their respective functions and division of responsibilities, supervise and manage the appraisal industry within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Appraisal Professionals

**Article 8** Appraisal professionals include appraisers and other appraisal practitioners with appraisal expertise and practical experience.

An appraiser refers to an appraisal professional who has passed the appraiser qualification examination. The State determines the professional categories of appraisers based on the needs of economic and social development.

**Article 9** Relevant national appraisal industry associations shall, in accordance with state regulations, organize and implement the national unified appraiser qualification examination.

Citizens with a college degree or above at the junior college level may take the national unified appraiser qualification examination.

**Article 10** Relevant national appraisal industry associations shall publish the list of appraisers on their websites and update it in real time.

**Article 11** Persons who have been subject to criminal punishment for intentional crimes, or for negligent crimes committed in the course of appraisal, finance, accounting, or auditing activities, shall not engage in appraisal business within five years from the date of completion of the execution of the punishment.

**Article 12** Appraisal professionals shall enjoy the following rights:

(1) To require the client to provide relevant ownership certificates, financial accounting information, and other materials, as well as the necessary assistance required for conducting fair appraisal procedures;

(2) To consult documents, certificates, and materials necessary for conducting business from relevant state organs or other organizations in accordance with the law;

(3) To refuse illegal interference by the client or other organizations or individuals in the appraisal conduct and appraisal results;

(4) To sign appraisal reports in accordance with the law;

(5) Other rights provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 13** Appraisal professionals shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) To act in good faith and conduct business independently, objectively, and impartially in accordance with the law;

(2) To comply with appraisal standards, perform investigation duties, conduct independent analysis and estimation, and conduct business diligently and carefully;

(3) To complete required continuing education, and maintain and improve professional competence;

(4) To verify and validate the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of relevant documents, certificates, and materials used in appraisal activities;

(5) To keep confidential any state secrets, trade secrets, and personal privacy learned during appraisal activities;

(6) To withdraw if there is a conflict of interest with the client or other relevant parties or the subject matter of the appraisal;

(7) To accept the self-regulation of the industry association and fulfill the obligations stipulated in the articles of association of the industry association;

(8) Other obligations provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 14** Appraisal professionals shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Privately accepting commissions to conduct business and collect fees;

(2) Conducting business in two or more appraisal institutions simultaneously;

(3) Soliciting business by improper means such as deception, inducement, coercion, or disparaging or defaming other appraisal professionals;

(4) Allowing others to conduct business in their own name, or conducting business under another person's name;

(5) Signing an appraisal report for business they did not undertake;

(6) Demanding, accepting, or covertly demanding or accepting remuneration, property, or other improper benefits beyond what is stipulated in the contract;

(7) Signing a false appraisal report or an appraisal report with material omissions;

(8) Other acts in violation of laws or administrative regulations.

## Chapter III Appraisal Institutions

**Article 15** An appraisal institution shall, in accordance with the law, adopt a partnership or company form and employ appraisal professionals to conduct appraisal business.

A partnership-form appraisal institution shall have two or more appraisers; two-thirds or more of its partners shall be appraisers with more than three years of practice experience who have not been subject to a penalty of suspension from practice within the last three years.

A company-form appraisal institution shall have eight or more appraisers and two or more shareholders, of which two-thirds or more of the shareholders shall be appraisers with more than three years of practice experience who have not been subject to a penalty of suspension from practice within the last three years.

If an appraisal institution has only two partners or shareholders, both partners or shareholders shall be appraisers with more than three years of practice experience who have not been subject to a penalty of suspension from practice within the last three years.

**Article 16** To establish an appraisal institution, an application for registration shall be made to the administrative department for industry and commerce. The appraisal institution shall, within thirty days from the date of obtaining the business license, file a record with the relevant appraisal administrative department. The appraisal administrative department shall promptly announce the recordation of the appraisal institution to the public.

**Article 17** An appraisal institution shall conduct business independently, objectively, and impartially in accordance with the law, establish and improve a quality control system, and ensure the objectivity, truthfulness, and reasonableness of appraisal reports.

An appraisal institution shall establish and improve an internal management system, supervise the compliance of its appraisal professionals with laws, administrative regulations, and appraisal standards, and be responsible for their practice conduct.

An appraisal institution shall accept supervision and inspection in accordance with the law, and truthfully provide appraisal files and relevant information.

**Article 18** If the client refuses to provide or fails to truthfully provide the ownership certificates, financial accounting information, and other materials required for conducting the appraisal business, the appraisal institution has the right to refuse the client's request to perform the contract in accordance with the law.

**Article 19** If the client requests the issuance of a false appraisal report or engages in other illegal interference with the appraisal results, the appraisal institution has the right to terminate the contract.

**Article 20** An appraisal institution shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Taking advantage of conducting business to seek improper benefits;

(2) Allowing other institutions to conduct business in its name, or conducting business under the name of another institution;

(3) Soliciting business by improper means such as malicious price undercutting, paying kickbacks, false advertising, or disparaging or defaming other appraisal institutions;

(4) Accepting business in which it has a conflict of interest;

(5) Accepting commissions from both parties with conflicting interests to appraise the same subject matter;

(6) Issuing a false appraisal report or an appraisal report with material omissions;

(7) Employing or appointing persons who do not meet the requirements of this Law to engage in appraisal business;

(8) Other acts in violation of laws or administrative regulations.

**Article 21** An appraisal institution shall establish a professional risk fund based on business needs, or voluntarily obtain professional liability insurance, to improve its risk prevention mechanism.

## Chapter IV Appraisal Procedures

**Article 22** The client has the right to independently choose an appraisal institution that meets the requirements of this Law, and no organization or individual may illegally restrict or interfere.

If the appraisal matter involves two or more parties, all parties shall negotiate to commission the appraisal institution.

For conducting statutory appraisal business, the appraisal institution shall be selected in accordance with the law.

**Article 23** The client shall enter into a commission contract with the appraisal institution, stipulating the rights and obligations of both parties.

The client shall pay fees to the appraisal institution in accordance with the contract and shall not demand, accept, or covertly demand or accept kickbacks.

The client shall be responsible for the authenticity, completeness, and legality of the ownership certificates, financial accounting information, and other materials it provides.

**Article 24** For an accepted appraisal business, the appraisal institution shall designate at least two appraisal professionals to handle it.

The client has the right to request the withdrawal of appraisal professionals who have a conflict of interest with the relevant parties or the subject matter of the appraisal.

**Article 25** Appraisal professionals shall, based on the specific circumstances of the appraisal business, conduct on-site investigations of the subject matter of the appraisal, collect ownership certificates, financial accounting information, and other materials, and verify, validate, analyze, and organize them as the basis for the appraisal.

**Article 26** Appraisal professionals shall appropriately select appraisal methods. Unless only one appraisal method can be selected based on appraisal practice standards, two or more appraisal methods shall be selected. After comprehensive analysis, an appraisal conclusion shall be formed, and an appraisal report shall be prepared.

The appraisal institution shall conduct an internal review of the appraisal report.

**Article 27** The appraisal report shall be signed by at least two appraisal professionals handling the business and affixed with the seal of the appraisal institution.

The appraisal institution and its appraisal professionals shall bear responsibility in accordance with the law for the appraisal reports they issue.

The client shall not collude with or instigate the appraisal institution or its appraisal professionals to issue a false appraisal report.

**Article 28** When an appraisal institution conducts statutory appraisal business, it shall designate at least two appraisers of the corresponding professional category to handle it, and the appraisal report shall be signed by at least two appraisers handling the business and affixed with the seal of the appraisal institution.

**Article 29** The retention period for appraisal files shall be no less than fifteen years; for statutory appraisal business, the retention period shall be no less than thirty years.

**Article 30** If the client has objections to the appraisal report, it may request an explanation from the appraisal institution.

**Article 31** If the client believes that the appraisal institution or its appraisal professionals are conducting business illegally, it may file a complaint or report with the relevant appraisal administrative department or industry association. The relevant appraisal administrative department or industry association shall promptly investigate and handle the matter and respond to the client.

**Article 32** The client or user of the appraisal report shall use the appraisal report in accordance with legal provisions and the scope of use specified in the appraisal report.

If the client or user of the appraisal report uses the appraisal report in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the appraisal institution and its appraisal professionals shall not bear responsibility.

## Chapter V Industry Associations

**Article 33** An appraisal industry association is a self-regulatory organization of appraisal institutions and appraisal professionals, implementing self-regulation in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and its articles of association.

The appraisal industry shall establish national appraisal industry associations based on professional fields, and establish local appraisal industry associations as needed.

**Article 34** The articles of association of an appraisal industry association shall be formulated by the members' congress, submitted to the registration authority for approval, and filed with the relevant appraisal administrative department.

**Article 35** Appraisal institutions and appraisal professionals joining a relevant appraisal industry association shall equally enjoy the rights and fulfill the obligations stipulated in the articles of association. The relevant appraisal industry association shall publish the list of appraisal institutions and appraisal professionals that have joined the association.

**Article 36** An appraisal industry association shall perform the following duties:

(1) Formulating self-regulatory management rules for members and implementing self-regulation over members;

(2) Formulating appraisal practice standards and professional ethics standards based on basic appraisal standards;

(3) Organizing and conducting continuing education for members;

(4) Establishing credit files for members, recording members' compliance with laws, administrative regulations, and appraisal standards in the credit files, and making them public;

(5) Inspecting members' establishment of risk prevention mechanisms;

(6) Accepting complaints and reports against members, accepting members' appeals, and mediating members' practice disputes;

(7) Regulating members' practice conduct, regularly inspecting appraisal reports issued by members, imposing rewards and penalties on members in accordance with the articles of association, and promptly reporting the rewards and penalties to the relevant appraisal administrative department;

(8) Ensuring members conduct business in accordance with the law and protecting members' lawful rights and interests;

(9) Other duties provided for by laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association.

**Article 37** Relevant appraisal industry associations shall establish communication, collaboration, and information sharing mechanisms, and formulate common codes of conduct as needed, to promote the healthy and orderly development of the appraisal industry.

**Article 38** The standards for membership fees collected by an appraisal industry association shall be approved by the members' congress and made public. The amount of membership fees paid shall not be used as a condition for a member to hold a position in the industry association.

The collection and use of membership fees shall be subject to the supervision of the members' congress and relevant departments, and no organization or individual may embezzle, privately distribute, or misappropriate them.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Administration

**Article 39** The relevant appraisal administrative departments of the State Council shall organize the formulation of basic appraisal standards and measures for the supervision and administration of the appraisal industry.

**Article 40** The relevant appraisal administrative departments of the local people's governments at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for supervising and managing the appraisal industry, imposing administrative penalties on illegal acts of appraisal institutions and appraisal professionals in accordance with the law, promptly notifying the relevant appraisal industry associations of the penalties, and making them public in accordance with the law.

**Article 41** Appraisal administrative departments shall conduct supervision and inspection of relevant appraisal industry associations, and shall promptly investigate and handle problems discovered during inspections and complaints or reports against the associations.

**Article 42** Appraisal administrative departments shall not, in violation of the provisions of this Law, restrict appraisal institutions from conducting business in accordance with the law.

**Article 43** Appraisal administrative departments shall not have personnel or financial connections with appraisal industry associations or appraisal institutions, and shall not use their authority to solicit business for appraisal institutions.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

**Article 44** If an appraisal professional violates the provisions of this Law and falls under any of the following circumstances, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall issue a warning, and may order a suspension from practice for a period of not less than six months but not more than one year; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, a suspension from practice for a period of not less than one year but not more than five years shall be ordered; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Privately accepting commissions to conduct business and collect fees;

(2) Conducting business in two or more appraisal institutions simultaneously;

(3) Soliciting business by improper means such as deception, inducement, coercion, or disparaging or defaming other appraisal professionals;

(4) Allowing others to conduct business in their own name, or conducting business under another person's name;

(5) Signing an appraisal report for business they did not undertake or an appraisal report with material omissions;

(6) Demanding, accepting, or covertly demanding or accepting remuneration, property, or other improper benefits beyond what is stipulated in the contract.

**Article 45** If an appraisal professional violates the provisions of this Law by signing a false appraisal report, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall order a suspension from practice for a period of not less than two years but not more than five years; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, a suspension from practice for a period of not less than five years but not more than ten years shall be ordered; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law, and the person shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in appraisal business.

**Article 46** If a person, in violation of the provisions of this Law, engages in appraisal business in the name of an appraisal institution without industrial and commercial registration, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall order the cessation of the illegal activity; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed.

**Article 47** If an appraisal institution violates the provisions of this Law and falls under any of the following circumstances, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall issue a warning, and may order a suspension of business for a period of not less than one month but not more than six months; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke the business license; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Taking advantage of conducting business to seek improper benefits;

(2) Allowing other institutions to conduct business in its name, or conducting business under the name of another institution;

(3) Soliciting business by improper means such as malicious price undercutting, paying kickbacks, false advertising, or disparaging or defaming other appraisal institutions;

(4) Accepting business in which it has a conflict of interest;

(5) Accepting commissions from both parties with conflicting interests to appraise the same subject matter;

(6) Issuing an appraisal report with material omissions;

(7) Failing to retain appraisal files for the period stipulated in this Law;

(8) Employing or appointing persons who do not meet the requirements of this Law to engage in appraisal business;

(9) Negligently managing its appraisal professionals, resulting in adverse consequences.

If an appraisal institution fails to file a record as required by this Law or does not meet the conditions stipulated in Article 15 of this Law, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, it shall be ordered to suspend business, and may be concurrently fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

**Article 48** If an appraisal institution violates the provisions of this Law by issuing a false appraisal report, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall order a suspension of business for a period of not less than six months but not more than one year; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the administrative department for industry and commerce shall revoke the business license; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

**Article 49** If an appraisal institution or appraisal professional accumulates three penalties other than suspension of business or suspension from practice within one year for violating the provisions of this Law, the relevant appraisal administrative department may order it to suspend business or suspend from practice for a period of not less than one year but not more than five years.

**Article 50** If an appraisal professional violates the provisions of this Law and causes losses to the client or other relevant parties, the appraisal institution where the professional works shall bear the compensation liability in accordance with the law. After fulfilling the compensation liability, the appraisal institution may seek recourse against the appraisal professional who acted intentionally or with gross negligence.

**Article 51** If a person, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to commission an appraisal institution for a statutory appraisal that should be commissioned, the relevant department shall order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.Article 52 Where, in violation of this Law, the principal has any of the following circumstances in statutory appraisal, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall, jointly with the relevant departments, order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed; any illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to select an appraisal institution in accordance with the law;

(2) Soliciting, accepting, or covertly soliciting or accepting kickbacks;

(3) Colluding with or instigating an appraisal institution or appraiser to issue a false appraisal report;

(4) Failing to truthfully provide the appraisal institution with ownership certificates, financial accounting information, and other materials;

(5) Failing to use the appraisal report in accordance with the law and the scope of use specified in the appraisal report.

Where a principal other than those specified in the preceding paragraph violates the provisions of this Law and causes losses to others, it shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Where an appraisal industry association violates the provisions of this Law, the relevant appraisal administrative department shall give it a warning and order it to make corrections; if it refuses to make corrections, the matter may be reported to the registration authority, which shall impose penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Where staff members of relevant administrative departments or appraisal industry associations violate the provisions of this Law by abusing their powers, neglecting their duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 55 This Law shall come into force on December 1, 2016.
