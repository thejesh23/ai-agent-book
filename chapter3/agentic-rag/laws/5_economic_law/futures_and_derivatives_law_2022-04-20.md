# Futures and Derivatives Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 34th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 20, 2022

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate futures trading and derivatives trading activities, protect the lawful rights and interests of all parties, maintain market order and social public interests, promote the futures market and derivatives market to serve the national economy, prevent and mitigate financial risks, and safeguard national economic security.

Article 2 Futures trading, derivatives trading, and related activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall be governed by this Law.

Where futures trading, derivatives trading, and related activities conducted outside the territory of the People's Republic of China disrupt market order within the territory of the People's Republic of China or harm the lawful rights and interests of domestic traders, they shall be dealt with and legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "futures trading" means trading activities where futures contracts or standardized option contracts are the subject matter of the transaction.

For the purposes of this Law, "derivatives trading" means trading activities other than futures trading, where swap contracts, forward contracts, non-standardized option contracts, and combinations thereof are the subject matter of the transaction.

For the purposes of this Law, "futures contract" means a standardized contract uniformly formulated by a futures trading venue, stipulating the delivery of a certain quantity of underlying subject matter at a specific time and place in the future.

For the purposes of this Law, "option contract" means a standardized or non-standardized contract stipulating that the buyer has the right to buy or sell a specified underlying subject matter (including futures contracts) at a specific price at a future time.

For the purposes of this Law, "swap contract" means a financial contract stipulating the mutual exchange of specific underlying subject matters within a specific future period.

For the purposes of this Law, "forward contract" means a financial contract, other than a futures contract, stipulating the delivery of a certain quantity of underlying subject matter at a specific time and place in the future.

Article 4 The State supports the healthy development of the futures market, leveraging its functions of price discovery, risk management, and resource allocation.

The State encourages the use of the futures market and derivatives market for risk management activities such as hedging.

The State takes measures to promote the development of agricultural product futures markets and derivatives markets, and to guide domestic agricultural product production and operation.

For the purposes of this Law, "hedging" means activities where a trader enters into futures trading and derivatives trading that substantially correspond to its assets, liabilities, etc., in order to manage risks arising from changes in the value of such assets, liabilities, etc.

Article 5 The futures market and derivatives market shall establish and improve mechanisms for risk monitoring, mitigation, and resolution, restrict excessive speculation in accordance with the law, and prevent systemic market risks.

Article 6 Futures trading and derivatives trading activities shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions, adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and justice, and prohibit fraud, market manipulation, and insider trading.

Article 7 All parties participating in futures trading and derivatives trading activities have equal legal status and shall abide by the principles of voluntariness, compensation, and good faith.

Article 8 The securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over the national futures market in accordance with the law. Where the State Council has other provisions on the supervision and administration of interest rate and exchange rate futures, those provisions shall apply.

The derivatives market shall be supervised and administered by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or the department authorized by the State Council according to their respective functions and duties.

Article 9 Industry associations for futures and derivatives shall exercise self-regulation in accordance with the law.

Article 10 The State audit authority shall, in accordance with the law, conduct audit supervision over futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, and the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

## Chapter II Futures Trading and Derivatives Trading

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 11 Futures trading shall be conducted on a futures exchange established in accordance with the law or other futures trading venues approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council to organize futures trading (hereinafter collectively referred to as "futures trading venues"), using open centralized trading methods or other methods approved by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Futures trading outside futures trading venues is prohibited.

Derivatives trading may be conducted using negotiated trading or other trading methods prescribed by the State Council.

Article 12 No unit or individual shall manipulate the futures market or derivatives market.

The following means of manipulating the futures market, affecting or intending to affect futures trading prices or futures trading volumes, are prohibited:

(1) Individually or in collusion, concentrating capital advantages, position advantages, or using information advantages to jointly or continuously buy or sell contracts;
(2) Colluding with others to conduct futures trading with each other at pre-arranged times, prices, and methods;
(3) Conducting futures trading between accounts under one's actual control;
(4) Using false or uncertain material information to induce traders to conduct futures trading;
(5) Frequently or in large quantities placing and canceling orders without the intention of executing a transaction;
(6) Making public evaluations, forecasts, or investment recommendations regarding relevant futures trading or trading of the underlying subject matter of the contract, and then engaging in reverse or related operations;
(7) Hoarding spot goods to influence the futures market;
(8) In the delivery month or near the delivery month, using improper means to circumvent position limits and gain a position advantage;
(9) Manipulating the futures market through activities in related markets;
(10) Other means of manipulating the futures market.

Article 13 Any person who is an insider in possession of inside information for futures trading or derivatives trading, or who has illegally obtained such inside information, shall not engage in relevant futures trading or derivatives trading, explicitly or implicitly suggest that others engage in futures trading or derivatives trading related to the inside information, or disclose the inside information before it is made public.

Article 14 For the purposes of this Law, "inside information" means undisclosed information that may have a significant impact on the trading prices of futures trading or derivatives trading.

Inside information for futures trading includes:

(1) Policies, information, or data being formulated or not yet released by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council or other relevant departments that may have a significant impact on futures trading prices;
(2) Decisions made by futures trading venues or futures clearing institutions that may have a significant impact on futures trading prices;
(3) Capital and trading trends of members and traders of futures trading venues;
(4) Material abnormal trading information in relevant markets;
(5) Other information specified by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council that has a significant impact on futures trading prices.

Article 15 For the purposes of this Law, "insider" means any unit or individual who, by virtue of their business position, management position, supervisory position, or official convenience, has access to or obtains inside information.

Insiders for futures trading include:

(1) Relevant personnel of futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, and futures service institutions;
(2) Staff members of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments;
(3) Other units and individuals specified by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council who can obtain inside information.

Article 16 No unit or individual shall fabricate or disseminate false information or misleading information to disrupt the futures market or derivatives market.

Futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and their employees; venues and institutions organizing derivatives trading and their employees; futures and derivatives industry associations; the securities regulatory authority of the State Council; and departments authorized by the State Council and their staff members are prohibited from making false statements or information misrepresentations in futures trading, derivatives trading, and related activities.

Various media disseminating information about the futures market and derivatives market shall do so truthfully and objectively, and misleading information is prohibited. Media and their staff reporting on futures market and derivatives market information shall not engage in futures trading, derivatives trading, or related activities that conflict with their professional responsibilities.

### Section 2 Futures Trading

Article 17 The listing of futures contract varieties and standardized option contract varieties shall comply with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and shall be registered by the futures trading venue with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council in accordance with the law.

The suspension of listing, resumption of listing, and termination of listing of futures contract varieties and standardized option contract varieties shall comply with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council and shall be decided by the futures trading venue and filed with the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Futures contract varieties and standardized option contract varieties shall have economic value, be not easily manipulated, and be in the public interest.

Article 18 Futures trading implements a real-name account system. A trader conducting futures trading shall hold a valid identity document and apply to open an account in their own name.

No unit or individual shall, in violation of regulations, lend their futures account or borrow another person's futures account to engage in futures trading.

Article 19 Those conducting futures trading on a futures trading venue shall be members of the futures trading venue or other participants that meet the requirements specified by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 20 A trader entrusting a futures operating institution to conduct trading may issue trading instructions in writing, by telephone, through self-service terminals, via the internet, or by other means. Trading instructions shall be clear, specific, and comprehensive.

Article 21 Programmatic trading, where trading instructions are automatically generated or issued by computer programs, shall comply with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, be reported to the futures trading venue, and shall not affect the system security or normal trading order of the futures trading venue.

Article 22 Futures trading implements a margin system. Futures clearing institutions collect margins from clearing participants, and clearing participants collect margins from traders. Margins are used for settlement and performance guarantee.

Forms of margin include cash, highly liquid securities such as government bonds, stocks, fund shares, and standard warehouse receipts, and other property specified by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council. Where securities, etc., are used as margin, this may be done through methods such as pledge that have performance guarantee functions in accordance with the law.

The form, proportion, etc., of margins collected by futures clearing institutions and clearing participants shall comply with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Where a trader conducts standardized option contract trading, the seller shall pay margin, and the buyer shall pay a premium.

The "premium" mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to the funds paid by the buyer to purchase the standardized option contract.

Article 23 Futures trading implements a position limit system to prevent risks from excessive concentration of contract positions.

Those engaged in risk management activities such as hedging may apply for exemption from position limits.

The administrative measures for position limits and hedging shall be formulated by the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 24 Futures trading implements a reporting and management system for the actual control relationship of traders. Traders shall report their actual control relationships to futures operating institutions or futures trading venues in accordance with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 25 Fees for futures trading shall be reasonable, and the fee items, fee standards, and administrative measures shall be made public.

Article 26 Transactions conducted in accordance with the business rules formulated by the futures trading venue in accordance with the law shall not have their transaction results altered, except as provided in Article 89, paragraph 2 of this Law.

Article 27 Members and traders of futures trading venues shall report material matters concerning trading, positions, margins, etc., in accordance with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 28 No unit or individual shall use credit funds or fiscal funds for futures trading in violation of regulations.

Article 29 Futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and their employees shall promptly report any prohibited trading activities they discover to the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

### Section 3 Derivatives Trading

Article 30 Venues established in accordance with the law may, upon approval by the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, organize derivatives trading.

The trading rules formulated by venues organizing derivatives trading shall fairly protect the lawful rights and interests of all parties involved in the transaction and prevent market risks, and shall be submitted to the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council for approval.

Article 31 Financial institutions conducting derivatives trading business shall obtain approval or authorization in accordance with the law, fulfill trader suitability management obligations, and comply with relevant state regulatory provisions.

Article 32 Where derivatives trading adopts a master agreement approach, the master agreement, all supplementary agreements under the master agreement, and the agreements between the trading parties on each specific transaction shall together constitute a complete single agreement between the trading parties and have legal binding force.

Article 33 The contract templates such as the master agreement referred to in Article 32 of this Law shall be submitted for filing in accordance with the provisions of the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 34 When conducting derivatives trading, performance guarantees may be provided through methods such as pledge in accordance with the law.

Article 35 Where derivatives trading is conducted using the master agreement approach in accordance with the law, upon the occurrence of agreed circumstances, the transaction may be terminated in accordance with the agreement, and the profits and losses of all transactions under the agreement shall be settled on a net basis.

Netting settlement conducted in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not be suspended, invalidated, or revoked due to any party to the transaction entering into bankruptcy proceedings in accordance with the law.

Article 36 The department authorized by the State Council and the securities regulatory authority of the State Council shall establish a derivatives trade repository for the centralized collection, storage, analysis, and management of information on derivatives trading subject matter, scale, counterparties, etc., and shall promptly disclose relevant information to the market in accordance with regulations. Specific measures shall be formulated by the department authorized by the State Council and the securities regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 37 Where derivatives trading is centrally cleared by a clearing institution approved by the department authorized by the State Council or the securities regulatory authority of the State Council acting as a central counterparty, termination netting may be conducted in accordance with the law; settlement property shall be used preferentially for settlement and delivery and shall not be sealed up, frozen, seized, or enforced; no person may use such property before settlement and delivery are completed.

Centralized clearing conducted in accordance with the law shall not be suspended, invalidated, or revoked due to any party participating in the clearing entering into bankruptcy proceedings in accordance with the law.

Article 38 Specific measures for regulating and supervising derivatives trading and related activities shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with the principles of this Law.

## Chapter III Futures Settlement and Delivery

Article 39 Futures trading implements a daily mark-to-market settlement system. Within the time specified by the futures trading venue, the futures clearing institution shall settle with clearing participants on the same day based on the settlement price; clearing participants shall settle with traders based on the settlement results of the futures clearing institution. Settlement results shall be promptly notified to clearing participants and traders on the same day.

Article 40 Margins, premiums, etc., collected by futures clearing institutions and clearing participants shall be separated from their own funds, deposited in dedicated accounts at futures margin depository institutions in accordance with the provisions of the securities regulatory authority of the State Council, managed separately, and prohibited from being misappropriated in violation of regulations.

Article 41 If a clearing participant's margin does not meet the standards specified in the business rules of the futures clearing institution, the futures clearing institution shall, in accordance with the business rules, notify the clearing participant to add margin or close positions on its own within a specified time; if the clearing participant fails to add margin or close positions on its own within the specified time, the futures trading venue shall be notified to forcibly close positions.

If a trader's margin does not meet the standards agreed upon between the clearing participant and the trader, the clearing participant shall, in accordance with the agreement, notify the trader to add margin or close positions on its own within the agreed time; if the trader fails to add margin or close positions on its own within the agreed time, positions shall be forcibly closed in accordance with the agreement.

Where securities, etc., are used as margin, and the futures clearing institution or clearing participant forcibly closes positions in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs, it may dispose of the securities, etc.

Article 42 If a clearing participant defaults during the settlement process, the futures clearing institution shall, in accordance with the business rules, use the clearing participant's margin, settlement guarantee fund, and the clearing institution's risk reserve, own funds, etc., to complete the settlement; if the futures clearing institution completes the settlement using its risk reserve, own funds, etc., it may seek recourse against the clearing participant in accordance with the law.

If a trader defaults during the settlement process, the clearing participant entrusted by the trader shall, in accordance with the contract, use the trader's margin and the clearing participant's risk reserve and own funds to complete the settlement; if the clearing participant completes the settlement using its risk reserve and own funds, it may seek recourse against the trader in accordance with the law.

For the purposes of this Law, "settlement guarantee fund" means funds paid by a clearing participant to the futures clearing institution from its own funds to guarantee performance.

Article 43 Assets such as margins, premiums, settlement guarantee funds, and risk reserves collected and withdrawn by futures clearing institutions in accordance with their business rules shall be used preferentially for settlement and delivery and shall not be sealed up, frozen, seized, or enforced.

Before settlement and delivery are completed, no person may use margins used to guarantee performance and delivery, or delivery property that has entered the delivery stage.

Settlement and delivery conducted in accordance with the law shall not be suspended, invalidated, or revoked due to any party participating in the settlement entering into bankruptcy proceedings in accordance with the law.

Article 44 When a futures contract matures, the trader shall close out open positions that have not been offset by physical delivery or cash settlement.

At the time specified in the standardized option contract, the buyer of the contract has the right to buy or sell the underlying subject matter at the agreed price, or to settle the cash difference in accordance with the agreement, and the seller of the contract shall perform the corresponding obligations in accordance with the agreement. The exercise of standardized option contracts shall be organized by the futures clearing institution.

Article 45 Where a futures contract adopts physical delivery, the futures clearing institution shall be responsible for organizing the delivery of payment and the title documents for the underlying subject matter of the contract, such as standard warehouse receipts.

Where a futures contract adopts cash settlement, the futures clearing institution shall, based on the delivery settlement price, transfer the profit and loss amounts between the parties holding positions.

For the purposes of this Law, "standard warehouse receipt" means a standardized delivery document issued by a delivery warehouse and registered with the futures trading venue.

Article 46 Physical delivery of futures trading shall be conducted at the delivery warehouse, delivery port, or other locations designated by the futures trading venue that meet the requirements of the futures trading venue. Physical delivery shall not restrict the total delivery quantity. Where physical delivery is conducted using documents other than standard warehouse receipts or other methods, the futures trading venue shall clearly stipulate the rights and obligations of each delivery party.

Article 47 If a clearing participant defaults during the delivery process, the futures clearing institution has the right to dispose of the clearing participant's title documents for the underlying subject matter of the contract, such as standard warehouse receipts.

If a trader defaults during the delivery process, the clearing participant has the right to dispose of the trader's title documents for the underlying subject matter of the contract, such as standard warehouse receipts.**Article 48** A futures operating institution that does not meet the conditions for being a clearing participant may entrust a clearing participant to conduct clearing on behalf of its clients. The rights and obligations between a futures operating institution that does not meet the conditions for being a clearing participant and the clearing participant and traders shall be implemented by reference to the provisions of this Chapter on the rights and obligations between clearing participants and traders.

## Chapter IV: Futures Traders

**Article 49** A futures trader refers to a natural person, legal person, or unincorporated organization that engages in futures trading in accordance with this Law and bears the consequences of the trading.

Unless otherwise provided by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, a futures trader engaging in futures trading shall entrust a futures operating institution to conduct the trading.

**Article 50** When providing services to a trader, a futures operating institution shall, in accordance with regulations, fully understand the trader's basic information, financial status, financial asset status, trading knowledge and experience, professional capabilities, and other relevant information; truthfully explain the important contents of the services, and fully disclose the trading risks; and provide services that match the aforementioned status of the trader.

When participating in futures trading and receiving services, a trader shall provide the truthful information listed in the preceding paragraph as explicitly required by the futures operating institution. If the trader refuses to provide information or fails to provide information as required, the futures operating institution shall inform the trader of the consequences and refuse to provide services in accordance with regulations.

If a futures operating institution violates the provisions of the first paragraph and causes losses to a trader, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

**Article 51** Based on factors such as financial status, financial asset status, trading knowledge and experience, and professional capabilities, traders can be divided into ordinary traders and professional traders. The standards for professional traders shall be stipulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

If a dispute arises between an ordinary trader and a futures operating institution, the futures operating institution shall prove that its actions comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, and that there are no circumstances such as misleading or fraud. If the futures operating institution cannot prove this, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability.

**Article 52** Legal persons and unincorporated organizations participating in futures trading shall establish internal control systems and risk control systems that are compatible with the type, scale, purpose, etc., of their trading contracts.

**Article 53** Employees of futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, and futures clearing institutions, staff members of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and the futures industry association, and other personnel prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council from participating in futures trading shall not engage in futures trading.

**Article 54** A trader has the right to inquire about their entrustment records, trading records, margin balance, and other important information related to the services they receive.

**Article 55** Futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and their employees shall keep the information of traders confidential in accordance with the law, and shall not illegally buy, sell, provide, or disclose the information of traders.

Futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and their employees shall not disclose any trade secrets they have learned.

**Article 56** If a dispute arises between a trader and a futures operating institution, etc., both parties may apply to the industry association, etc., for mediation. If an ordinary trader files a mediation request regarding a futures business dispute with a futures operating institution, the futures operating institution shall not refuse.

**Article 57** When a trader initiates a futures civil compensation lawsuit for market manipulation, insider trading, etc., if the subject matter of the lawsuit is of the same type and one party involves a large number of persons, representatives may be elected in accordance with the law to conduct the lawsuit.

**Article 58** The State establishes a futures trader protection fund. The specific measures for the raising, management, and use of the futures trader protection fund shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the finance department of the State Council.

## Chapter V: Futures Operating Institutions

**Article 59** A futures operating institution refers to a futures company established in accordance with the Company Law of the People's Republic of China and this Law, as well as other institutions approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council to engage in futures business.

**Article 60** To establish a futures company, the following conditions must be met, and approval must be obtained from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council:

(1) Having a company constitution that complies with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations;
(2) The major shareholders and actual controller have good financial status and integrity records, with net assets not lower than the standards stipulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, and no record of major violations of laws or regulations in the last three years;
(3) The registered capital shall not be less than RMB 100 million yuan, and shall be paid-in monetary capital;
(4) Personnel engaged in futures business meet the conditions stipulated in this Law, and directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel possess the corresponding qualifications for their positions;
(5) Having a good corporate governance structure, a sound risk management system, and a complete internal control system;
(6) Having qualified business premises, business facilities, and information technology systems;
(7) Other conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council may raise the minimum registered capital requirement based on the principle of prudential supervision and the risk level of various businesses.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, within six months from the date of receiving the application for establishing a futures company, conduct a review in accordance with the statutory conditions, statutory procedures, and the principle of prudential supervision, make a decision to approve or disapprove, and notify the applicant; if the application is disapproved, the reasons shall be stated.

**Article 61** A futures company shall include the word "futures" in its name, unless otherwise provided by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 62** A futures company shall obtain approval from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for the following matters:

(1) Merger, division, suspension of business, dissolution, or filing for bankruptcy;
(2) Change of major shareholders or the actual controller of the company;
(3) Change of registered capital and adjustment of the equity structure;
(4) Change of business scope;
(5) Other major matters stipulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

For the matters listed in items (3) and (5) of the preceding paragraph, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall make a decision to approve or disapprove within twenty days from the date of receiving the application; for other matters listed in the preceding paragraph, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall make a decision to approve or disapprove within sixty days from the date of receiving the application.

**Article 63** A futures company, upon approval by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, may engage in the following futures businesses:

(1) Futures brokerage;
(2) Futures trading advisory;
(3) Futures market making;
(4) Other futures businesses.

If a futures company engages in asset management business, it shall comply with the provisions of the Securities Investment Fund Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations.

Without the approval of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, no unit or individual may establish or establish a futures company in disguised form, operate or operate futures brokerage business or futures trading advisory business in disguised form, nor use the names "futures", "options", or other names that may cause confusion or misleading for business purposes.

**Article 64** Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a futures company shall be honest, trustworthy, of good conduct, familiar with futures laws and administrative regulations, and possess the operational management capabilities required to perform their duties. The appointment and removal of directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a futures company shall be reported to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for recordation.

None of the following persons may serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a futures company:

(1) Persons who are disqualified from serving as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a company under the Company Law of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a futures operating institution, or responsible persons of a futures trading venue or futures clearing institution, who have been removed from their positions due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of removal;
(3) Certified public accountants, lawyers, or other professionals of futures service institutions whose practice certificates have been revoked or whose qualifications have been cancelled due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of revocation or cancellation.

**Article 65** Futures operating institutions shall operate in accordance with the law, be diligent and responsible, and act in good faith. Futures operating institutions shall establish and improve internal control systems and adopt effective segregation measures to prevent conflicts of interest between the institution and its clients, and between different clients.

A futures operating institution shall conduct its futures brokerage business, futures market making business, asset management business, and other related businesses separately and shall not mix them.

A futures operating institution shall establish and implement an anti-money laundering system in accordance with the law.

**Article 66** When a futures operating institution accepts a trader's entrustment to conduct futures trading on their behalf, it shall sign a written entrustment contract, conduct futures trading for the trader in its own name, and the trading results shall be borne by the trader.

When engaging in brokerage business, a futures operating institution shall not accept a trader's full discretionary entrustment.

**Article 67** When a futures operating institution engages in asset management business, accepts a client's entrustment, and uses the client's assets for investment, it shall treat the different client assets under its management fairly and shall not breach its fiduciary duties.

**Article 68** A futures operating institution shall not provide financing or guarantees to its shareholders, actual controller, or related persons of its shareholders or actual controller in violation of regulations, and shall not provide external guarantees in violation of regulations.

**Article 69** Personnel engaged in futures business in a futures operating institution shall be honest, trustworthy, of good conduct, and possess the professional capabilities required for engaging in futures business.

Personnel engaged in futures business in a futures operating institution shall not privately accept client entrustments to engage in futures trading.

If personnel engaged in futures business in a futures operating institution, during the course of their futures business activities, execute instructions of the futures operating institution they belong to or use their positions to violate futures trading rules, the futures operating institution they belong to shall bear full responsibility.

**Article 70** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall formulate rules for the continuous operation of futures operating institutions, stipulating the operating conditions, risk management, internal control, margin custody, compliance management, risk supervision indicators, related party transactions, etc., of futures operating institutions and their branches. Futures operating institutions shall comply with the rules for continuous operation.

**Article 71** A futures operating institution shall, in accordance with regulations, submit business, financial, and other operational management information and materials to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. The futures regulatory authority of the State Council has the right to require the futures operating institution and its major shareholders, actual controller, and other related parties to provide relevant information and materials within a specified period.

The information and materials submitted or provided by the futures operating institution and its major shareholders, actual controller, or other related parties to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall be truthful, accurate, and complete.

**Article 72** When a futures operating institution is involved in major litigation or arbitration, its equity is frozen or used as security, or other major events occur, it shall submit a written report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council within five days from the date of the occurrence of such event.

The controlling shareholder or actual controller of the futures operating institution shall cooperate with the futures operating institution in fulfilling the obligations stipulated in the preceding paragraph.

**Article 73** If a futures operating institution fails to comply with the rules for continuous operation or faces operational risks, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall order it to rectify within a specified period; if the futures operating institution fails to rectify within the specified period, or its actions seriously endanger the sound operation of the futures operating institution and harm the legitimate rights and interests of traders, or if it is suspected of serious violations of laws or regulations and is under investigation by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may, depending on the circumstances, take the following measures against it:

(1) Restrict or suspend part of its business;
(2) Stop approving new businesses;
(3) Restrict the distribution of dividends, restrict the payment of remuneration and provision of benefits to directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel;
(4) Restrict the transfer of property or the creation of other rights over property;
(5) Order the replacement of directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or responsible persons of relevant business departments or branches, or restrict their rights;
(6) Restrict the allocation and use of the futures operating institution's own funds or risk reserves;
(7) Determine that responsible directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel are unsuitable persons;
(8) Order responsible shareholders to transfer their equity, and restrict responsible shareholders from exercising their shareholder rights.

For a futures operating institution that, after rectification, complies with the requirements of relevant laws, administrative regulations, and the rules for continuous operation, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall lift the relevant measures taken against it within three days from the date of completion of the inspection.

For a futures operating institution that, after rectification, still fails to meet the requirements of the rules for continuous operation, seriously affecting its normal operation, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council has the right to revoke part or all of its futures business permits and close part or all of its branches.

**Article 74** If a futures operating institution operates illegally or faces major risks, seriously endangering the order of the futures market and harming the interests of traders, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may take supervisory and administrative measures such as ordering a suspension of business for rectification, appointing another institution to take over, or placing it under receivership.

If a futures operating institution falls under the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph, with the approval of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, the following measures may be taken against the directly responsible directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible persons of the futures operating institution:

(1) Decide and notify the immigration and exit management authority to legally prevent them from leaving the country;
(2) Apply to the judicial authority to prohibit them from transferring, assigning, or otherwise disposing of property, or creating other rights over property.

**Article 75** If a shareholder of a futures operating institution makes false capital contributions or withdraws capital contributions, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall order it to rectify within a specified period and may order it to transfer its equity in the futures operating institution.

Before the shareholder corrects the illegal act and transfers its equity in the futures operating institution as required by the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may restrict its shareholder rights.

**Article 76** If a futures operating institution falls under any of the following circumstances, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall handle the cancellation of the relevant business license in accordance with the law:

(1) Its business license is legally revoked;
(2) After establishment, it fails to commence business for more than three months without justifiable reasons, or after commencing business, it suspends business continuously for more than three months without justifiable reasons;
(3) It voluntarily applies for cancellation;
(4) Other circumstances stipulated by the Administrative Licensing Law of the People's Republic of China and the futures regulatory authority of the State Council under which the administrative license should be cancelled.

Before canceling the relevant business license, the futures operating institution shall settle the relevant futures business and return the trader's margin and other assets in accordance with the law.

**Article 77** When the futures regulatory authority of the State Council deems it necessary, it may entrust a futures service institution to audit or evaluate the financial status, internal control status, and asset value of a futures operating institution. The specific measures shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant competent authorities.

**Article 78** Futures operating institutions are prohibited from engaging in the following acts that harm the interests of traders:

(1) Making promises to traders guaranteeing that their principal will not be lost or that they will obtain minimum returns;
(2) Agreeing with traders to share profits and bear risks jointly;
(3) Conducting futures trading in violation of the trader's entrustment;
(4) Concealing important matters or using other improper means to induce traders to trade;
(5) Providing trading advice to traders based on false or uncertain material information;
(6) Providing false transaction confirmations to traders;
(7) Failing to transmit the trader's trading orders to the futures trading venue;
(8) Misappropriating the trader's margin;
(9) Failing to open a margin account with a margin custody institution as required, or illegally transferring the trader's margin;
(10) Taking advantage of the convenience of providing services to traders to obtain improper benefits or shift risks;
(11) Other acts that harm the rights and interests of traders.

## Chapter VI: Futures Trading Venues

**Article 79** Futures trading venues shall adhere to the principle of prioritizing social public interests, provide venues and facilities for futures trading, organize and supervise futures trading, maintain a fair, orderly, and transparent market, and exercise self-regulation.

**Article 80** The establishment, modification, and dissolution of a futures exchange shall be approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A constitution shall be formulated for the establishment of a futures exchange. The formulation and modification of the constitution of a futures exchange shall be approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 81** A futures exchange shall include the words "commodity exchange" or "futures exchange" in its name. No other unit or individual may use the name "futures exchange" or other names that may cause confusion or misleading.

**Article 82** A futures exchange may adopt a membership system or a company system as its organizational form.

The organizational structure of a membership-based futures exchange shall be stipulated by its constitution.

**Article 83** A futures exchange shall formulate relevant business rules in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council; the formulation and modification of its trading rules shall be reported to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for approval.

The business rules of a futures exchange shall reflect the principle of fairly protecting the legitimate rights and interests of members, traders, and other relevant market parties.

Engaging in futures trading and related activities on a futures exchange shall comply with the business rules formulated by the futures exchange in accordance with the law. Those who violate the business rules shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions or other self-regulatory measures imposed by the futures exchange.

**Article 84** The responsible persons of a futures exchange shall be nominated or appointed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

None of the following persons may serve as responsible persons of a futures exchange:

(1) Persons who are disqualified from serving as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a company under the Company Law of the People's Republic of China;
(2) Directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a futures operating institution, or responsible persons of a futures trading venue or futures clearing institution, who have been removed from their positions due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of removal;
(3) Certified public accountants, lawyers, or other professionals of futures service institutions whose practice certificates have been revoked or whose qualifications have been cancelled due to illegal or disciplinary acts, within five years from the date of revocation or cancellation.

**Article 85** Futures trading venues shall, in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, strengthen risk control over trading activities and supervision and management over members and venue staff, and perform the following duties in accordance with the law:

(1) Provide venues, facilities, and services for trading;
(2) Design futures contracts and standardized option contract varieties, and arrange for the listing of futures contracts and standardized option contract varieties;
(3) Conduct real-time monitoring and risk surveillance of futures trading;
(4) Exercise self-regulation over members, traders, futures service institutions, etc., in accordance with the constitution and business rules;(5) Conduct trader education and market cultivation;

(6) Other duties prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures trading venue shall not directly or indirectly participate in futures trading. Without approval from the State Council, a futures trading venue shall not engage in trust investment, stock investment, non-self-use real estate investment, or other businesses unrelated to its duties.

Article 86 The income of a futures exchange shall be managed and used in accordance with relevant state regulations and shall first be used to ensure the operation and improvement of the facilities and premises for futures trading.

Article 87 A futures trading venue shall strengthen risk monitoring of futures trading. In the event of abnormal circumstances, the futures trading venue may, in accordance with its business rules, take the following emergency measures alone or jointly with the futures clearing institution, and immediately report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council:

(1) Adjust margin requirements;

(2) Adjust the price fluctuation limits;

(3) Adjust the trading or position limits for members and traders;

(4) Restrict the opening of new positions;

(5) Forcefully close positions;

(6) Temporarily suspend trading;

(7) Other emergency measures.

Once the abnormal circumstances disappear, the futures trading venue shall promptly cancel the emergency measures.

Article 88 A futures trading venue shall publish real-time futures trading quotes and shall prepare and publish a daily market report for each trading day.

The rights to futures trading quotes are vested in the futures trading venue. Without the permission of the futures trading venue, no entity or individual may publish futures trading quotes.

A futures trading venue shall not publish price forecast information.

A futures trading venue shall fulfill its information reporting obligations in accordance with the regulations of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 89 When a sudden event affects the normal conduct of futures trading, in order to maintain the normal order and market fairness of futures trading, the futures trading venue may take necessary disposal measures in accordance with this Law and its business rules, and shall promptly report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

If, due to the sudden event specified in the preceding paragraph, the results of futures trading become significantly abnormal, and settlement or delivery based on such trading results would have a material adverse impact on the normal order and market fairness of futures trading, the futures trading venue may, in accordance with its business rules, take measures such as canceling trades, and shall promptly report to and announce to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 90 A futures trading venue shall not bear civil compensation liability for losses caused by measures taken in accordance with Articles 87 and 89 of this Law, unless there is gross negligence.

## Chapter 7 Futures Clearing Institutions

Article 91 A futures clearing institution refers to a legal person established in accordance with the law to provide clearing and delivery services for futures trading and to exercise self-regulation.

Futures clearing institutions include futures trading venues with internal clearing departments, independent futures clearing institutions, and securities clearing institutions approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council to engage in futures trading clearing and delivery businesses related to securities business.

Article 92 The establishment, modification, and dissolution of an independent futures clearing institution shall be subject to the approval of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

To establish an independent futures clearing institution, the following conditions must be met:

(1) Possess sound financial conditions, with a minimum registered capital meeting the requirements of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council;

(2) Have senior management personnel with the professional knowledge and business experience required for the position;

(3) Have a sound governance structure, internal control system, and risk control system;

(4) Have business premises, information technology systems, and other facilities related to futures trading clearing that meet the requirements;

(5) Other conditions prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures trading venue that assumes the responsibilities of a futures clearing institution shall meet the conditions specified in paragraph 2 of this Article.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall conduct a review based on the principle of prudent supervision and shall make a decision on approval or disapproval within six months.

Article 93 A futures clearing institution, as a central counterparty, acts as the common counterparty to clearing participants, conducts netting settlement, and provides centralized performance guarantee for futures trading.

Article 94 A futures clearing institution performs the following duties:

(1) Organize the clearing and delivery of futures trading;

(2) Exercise self-regulation over traders, futures operating institutions, futures service institutions, and non-futures operating institution clearing participants in accordance with its articles of association and business rules;

(3) Handle information inquiry services related to the clearing and delivery of futures trading;

(4) Other duties prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 95 A futures clearing institution shall, in accordance with the regulations of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, stipulate in its business rules matters such as the clearing participant system, risk control system, information security management system, violation and default handling system, and emergency response and temporary disposal measures. The formulation and amendment of the articles of association and business rules of a futures clearing institution shall be subject to the approval of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. Participation in futures clearing shall comply with the business rules formulated by the futures clearing institution.

The formulation and implementation of business rules by a futures clearing institution shall be coordinated and aligned with the relevant systems of the futures trading venue.

Article 96 A futures clearing institution shall establish a liquidity management system to ensure the stable operation of clearing activities.

Article 97 The provisions of Article 84, paragraph 2 of Article 85, Article 86, and paragraphs 3 and 4 of Article 88 of this Law shall apply to independent futures clearing institutions and securities clearing institutions approved to engage in futures trading clearing and delivery businesses.

## Chapter 8 Futures Service Institutions

Article 98 Futures service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, asset appraisal institutions, futures margin depository institutions, delivery warehouses, and information technology service institutions shall be diligent and dutiful, provide services for futures trading and related activities in accordance with relevant business rules, and provide relevant materials as required by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 99 When futures service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, and asset appraisal institutions accept entrustment from futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, or futures clearing institutions to issue audit reports, legal opinions, or other documents, they shall verify and validate the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the content of the documents and materials on which they rely.

Article 100 Delivery warehouses include delivery warehouses and delivery factories. Delivery warehouses that provide related services for futures trading delivery shall meet the conditions prescribed by the futures trading venue. The futures trading venue shall enter into an agreement with the delivery warehouse, specifying the rights and obligations of both parties.

Delivery warehouses shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Issue false warehouse receipts;

(2) Violate the business rules of the futures trading venue by restricting the delivery or receipt of delivery commodities;

(3) Disclose trade secrets related to futures trading;

(4) Participate in futures trading in violation of relevant state regulations;

(5) Other acts prohibited by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 101 Institutions providing information technology system services for futures trading and related activities shall comply with the technical management regulations and standards related to information security of the state and the futures industry, and shall file with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council may, in accordance with the law, require information technology service institutions to provide relevant materials concerning the information technology systems specified in the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter 9 Futures Industry Association

Article 102 The Futures Industry Association is a self-regulatory organization for the futures industry and a social organization legal person.

Futures operating institutions shall join the Futures Industry Association. Futures service institutions may join the Futures Industry Association.

Article 103 The governing body of the Futures Industry Association is the General Assembly of Members.

The articles of association of the Futures Industry Association shall be formulated by the General Assembly of Members and filed with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

The Futures Industry Association shall establish a council. Members of the council shall be elected in accordance with the provisions of the articles of association.

Article 104 The Futures Industry Association performs the following duties:

(1) Formulate and implement industry self-regulatory rules, supervise and inspect the business activities of members and the professional conduct of practitioners, and impose disciplinary sanctions or implement other self-regulatory measures in accordance with the law, administrative regulations, relevant state regulations, the association's articles of association, and self-regulatory rules for violations thereof;

(2) Mediate disputes between members, and between members and traders;

(3) Protect the legitimate rights and interests of members in accordance with the law, and reflect members' suggestions and demands to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council;

(4) Organize professional training for futures practitioners and facilitate business exchanges among members;

(5) Educate members and futures practitioners on compliance with futures laws, regulations, and policies, organize the building of industry integrity, and establish an industry integrity incentive and constraint mechanism;

(6) Conduct trader education and protection, urge members to implement the trader suitability management system, and promote the futures market;

(7) Exercise self-regulation over members' information security work, and urge members to comply with relevant national and industry information security regulations and technical standards;

(8) Organize members to conduct research on the development, operation, and related content of the futures industry, collect, organize, and publish futures-related information, provide member services, organize industry exchanges, and guide industry innovation and development;

(9) Other duties prescribed by the articles of association of the Futures Industry Association.

## Chapter 10 Supervision and Administration

Article 105 The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, exercise supervision and administration over the futures market, maintain openness, fairness, and impartiality in the futures market, prevent systemic risks, protect the legitimate rights and interests of traders, and promote the healthy development of the futures market.

In exercising supervision and administration over the futures market, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, perform the following duties:

(1) Formulate rules and regulations concerning the supervision and administration of the futures market, and conduct approvals, verifications, registrations, and filings in accordance with the law;

(2) Supervise and administer the listing, trading, clearing, delivery, and other futures trading and related activities of products;

(3) Supervise and administer the futures business activities of market participants such as futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and non-futures operating institution clearing participants;

(4) Formulate codes of conduct for futures practitioners and supervise their implementation;

(5) Supervise and inspect the disclosure of information in futures trading;

(6) Protect the legitimate rights and interests of traders and conduct trader education;

(7) Investigate and punish violations of futures laws;

(8) Monitor, control, prevent, and address futures market risks;

(9) Supervise the financial technology and information security of the futures industry;

(10) Guide and supervise the self-regulatory activities of the Futures Industry Association;

(11) Other duties prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council may, as needed, establish dispatched agencies to perform supervisory and administrative duties under authorization.

Article 106 When performing its duties in accordance with the law, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council has the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, and futures clearing institutions, and require them to submit relevant financial accounting, business activity, and internal control materials;

(2) Enter the premises where suspected illegal activities occur to investigate and collect evidence;

(3) Question the parties involved and the entities and individuals related to the investigated incident, require them to provide explanations on matters related to the investigated incident, or require them to submit documents and materials related to the investigated incident in a specified manner;

(4) Inspect and copy documents and materials such as property rights registrations and communication records related to the investigated incident;

(5) Inspect and copy futures trading records, financial accounting materials, and other relevant documents and materials of the parties involved and the entities and individuals related to the investigated incident; seal or seize documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed;

(6) Inquire into the margin accounts, bank accounts, and other accounts with payment, custody, or settlement functions of the parties involved and the entities and individuals related to the investigated incident, and copy relevant documents and materials; if there is evidence that illegal funds or other involved property have been or may be transferred or concealed, or that important evidence has been or may be concealed, forged, or destroyed, freeze or seal such property with the approval of the principal or other authorized responsible person of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, for a period of six months; if an extension is necessary due to special reasons, each extension shall not exceed three months, and the maximum period shall not exceed two years;

(7) When investigating major illegal acts such as market manipulation or insider trading, with the approval of the principal or other authorized responsible person of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, restrict the trading of the parties involved in the investigated incident, but the restriction period shall not exceed three months; in complex cases, it may be extended by three months;

(8) Decide and notify the exit-entry administration authority to prevent suspected illegal persons, responsible persons of suspected illegal entities, and other directly responsible personnel from leaving the country in accordance with the law.

To prevent futures market risks and maintain market order, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may take measures such as ordering corrections, conducting supervisory interviews, and issuing warning letters.

Article 107 When performing its duties in accordance with the law to conduct supervision, inspection, or investigation, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall have no fewer than two personnel for supervision, inspection, or investigation, and shall present their law enforcement credentials and relevant enforcement documents such as inspection, investigation, and inquiry documents. If the number of personnel for supervision, inspection, or investigation is fewer than two, or if they fail to present their law enforcement credentials and relevant enforcement documents, the entity or individual being inspected or investigated has the right to refuse.

Article 108 Personnel of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall act in accordance with the law, be loyal to their duties, be fair and incorruptible, keep state secrets and the trade secrets of relevant parties, and shall not use their positions to seek improper benefits.

Article 109 When the futures regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties in accordance with the law, the entities and individuals being inspected or investigated shall cooperate, provide truthful explanations or submit relevant documents and materials, and shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal information.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments shall establish mechanisms for information sharing and coordinated supervision and administration. When the futures regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties in accordance with the law to conduct supervision, inspection, or investigation, relevant departments shall cooperate.

Article 110 Any entity or individual has the right to report suspected futures illegal or non-compliant acts to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

If a verified report of a suspected major illegal or non-compliant act is made with the real name of the reporter, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall reward the reporter in accordance with regulations.

The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall keep the identity information of the reporter confidential.

Article 111 The rules, regulations, and supervisory work systems formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall be made public in accordance with the law.

Penalty decisions made by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council based on investigation results for futures violations shall be made public in accordance with the law.

Article 112 During the investigation of an entity or individual suspected of futures violations, if the investigated party submits a written application, promising to correct the suspected violations within a period recognized by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, compensate the relevant traders for losses, and eliminate the harm or adverse effects, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may decide to suspend the investigation. If the investigated party fulfills its commitments, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may decide to terminate the investigation; if the investigated party fails to fulfill its commitments or other circumstances prescribed by the State Council occur, the investigation shall be resumed. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

If the futures regulatory authority of the State Council suspends or terminates an investigation, it shall publicly disclose relevant information in accordance with regulations.

Article 113 The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, include information on the compliance of relevant futures market entities with this Law in the futures market integrity file.

Article 114 When performing its duties in accordance with the law and discovering that futures violations are suspected of constituting a crime, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall transfer the case to the judicial authorities for handling in accordance with the law; if it discovers that public officials are suspected of duty-related violations or crimes, it shall transfer the case to the supervisory authorities for handling in accordance with the law.

Article 115 The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall establish and improve a futures market monitoring and control system, and strengthen the safe deposit monitoring and control of margin through specialized institutions.

Article 116 To prevent trading and clearing risks, futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, and non-futures operating institution clearing participants shall, in accordance with the regulations of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and the financial department of the State Council, set aside, manage, and use risk reserves from their business income.

Article 117 Futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, futures service institutions, and non-futures operating institution clearing participants shall properly retain materials and information related to their business operations in accordance with regulations, and no person shall disclose, conceal, forge, alter, or destroy them. The retention period for information and materials of futures operating institutions, futures trading venues, futures clearing institutions, and non-futures operating institution clearing participants shall not be less than twenty years; the retention period for information and materials of futures service institutions shall not be less than ten years.

## Chapter 11 Cross-Border Trading and Regulatory Cooperation

Article 118 If an overseas futures trading venue provides services for domestic entities or individuals to directly access the trading system of that venue for trading, it shall apply for registration with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and accept the supervision and administration of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, unless otherwise provided by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 119 If futures contracts, options contracts, or derivative contracts listed on an overseas futures trading venue are settled by reference to the prices of contracts listed on a domestic futures trading venue, they shall comply with the regulations of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 120 Domestic entities or individuals engaging in overseas futures trading shall entrust a domestic futures operating institution with the qualification for overseas futures brokerage business to conduct such trading, unless otherwise provided by the State Council.

If a domestic futures operating institution sub-entrusts an overseas futures operating institution to conduct overseas futures trading, the overseas futures operating institution shall apply for registration with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and accept the supervision and administration of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, unless otherwise provided by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Article 121 If an overseas futures trading venue establishes a representative office within China, it shall file with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

The representative office of an overseas futures trading venue and its staff shall not engage in or covertly engage in any business activities.

Article 122 If an overseas institution engages in futures market marketing, promotion, or solicitation activities within China, it shall obtain approval from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, and the relevant provisions of this Law shall apply.Domestic institutions must obtain approval from the State Council's futures regulatory authority to conduct futures market marketing, promotion, and solicitation activities within China on behalf of overseas institutions.

No entity or individual may engage in futures market marketing, promotion, or solicitation activities that violate the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs.

Article 123 The State Council's futures regulatory authority may establish a regulatory cooperation mechanism with overseas futures regulatory authorities, or join international organizations, to implement cross-border supervision and management.

When providing assistance at the request of an overseas futures regulatory authority, the State Council's futures regulatory authority shall comply with national laws and regulations and the principle of reciprocity, and shall not disclose state secrets or harm national interests or public interests.

Article 124 The State Council's futures regulatory authority may, in accordance with regulatory cooperation arrangements reached with overseas futures regulatory authorities, accept requests from overseas futures regulatory authorities to conduct investigations and collect evidence in accordance with the duties and procedures stipulated in this Law. The overseas futures regulatory authority shall provide relevant case materials and explain that it is investigating the party concerned for suspected violations of the local futures laws and regulations of the requesting party. Overseas futures regulatory authorities shall not directly conduct investigations, evidence collection, or other activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Without the consent of the State Council's futures regulatory authority and the relevant competent department of the State Council, no entity or individual may provide documents and materials related to futures business activities to overseas regulatory authorities without authorization.

The State Council's futures regulatory authority may, in accordance with regulatory cooperation arrangements reached with overseas futures regulatory authorities, request overseas futures regulatory authorities to conduct investigations and collect evidence.

## Chapter 12 Legal Liability

Article 125 Anyone who violates Article 12 of this Law by manipulating the futures market or derivatives market shall be ordered to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than ten million yuan shall be imposed. If an entity manipulates the market, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall also be given a warning and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan.

If market manipulation causes losses to traders, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 126 Anyone who violates Article 13 of this Law by engaging in insider trading shall be ordered to make corrections, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. If an entity engages in insider trading, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall also be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

Personnel of the State Council's futures regulatory authority, departments authorized by the State Council, futures exchanges, and futures clearing institutions who engage in insider trading shall be subject to heavier penalties.

If insider trading causes losses to traders, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 127 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 16, paragraphs 1 and 3 of this Law by fabricating or disseminating false or misleading information, thereby disrupting the futures market or derivatives market, shall have their illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Anyone who violates Article 16, paragraph 2 of this Law by making false statements or providing misleading information in futures or derivatives trading activities shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan; if the person is a state functionary, disciplinary action shall also be taken in accordance with the law.

Media outlets and their personnel engaged in reporting on futures market and derivatives market information who violate Article 16, paragraph 3 of this Law by engaging in futures or derivatives trading that conflicts with their work duties shall have their illegal gains confiscated and be fined not more than one time the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If fabricating or disseminating false information about futures or derivatives trading, or providing misleading information in such trading, causes losses to traders, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 128 Anyone who violates Article 18, paragraph 2 of this Law by lending their own futures account or borrowing another person's futures account to engage in futures trading shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 129 Anyone who violates Article 21 of this Law by using programmatic trading to affect the system security or normal trading order of a futures exchange shall be ordered to make corrections and fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 130 Anyone who violates Article 27 of this Law by failing to report major matters shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and may be fined not more than one million yuan.

Article 131 Persons prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and regulations of the State Council's futures regulatory authority from participating in futures trading who violate Article 53 of this Law by directly, under a pseudonym, or under another's name participating in futures trading shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the person is a state functionary, disciplinary action shall also be taken in accordance with the law.

Article 132 Anyone who illegally establishes a futures company or engages in related futures business without approval shall be banned, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than ten million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

Article 133 Anyone who submits false application documents or uses other fraudulent means to obtain approval for the establishment of a futures company, approval for changes in major matters, or a futures business license for a futures operating institution shall have the relevant approval revoked, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

Article 134 If a futures operating institution violates Articles 40, 62, 65, 68, 71, or 72 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its futures business license revoked. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

If a futures operating institution commits the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph and causes losses to traders, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

If the major shareholder, actual controller, or other related person of a futures operating institution violates Article 71 of this Law, they shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of this article.

Article 135 If a futures operating institution violates the trader suitability management regulations in Article 50 of this Law, or violates Article 66 by engaging in brokerage business accepting full authorization from traders, or commits acts under Article 78 that harm the interests of traders, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant business license shall be revoked. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

If a futures operating institution commits acts under Article 78 of this Law and causes losses to traders, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 136 Anyone who violates Articles 11, 80, or 92 of this Law by illegally establishing a futures exchange or futures clearing institution, or organizing futures trading in other forms, shall have their illegal gains confiscated and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than one million yuan, a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than ten million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan. If a futures exchange is illegally established, the people's government at or above the county level shall ban it.

Anyone who violates Article 30 of this Law by organizing derivatives trading without approval, or a financial institution that violates Article 31 by conducting derivatives trading without approval or authorization, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 137 If a futures exchange or futures clearing institution violates Articles 17, 40, or 85, paragraph 2 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 200,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 138 If a futures exchange or futures clearing institution violates Article 88, paragraph 3 of this Law by publishing price forecast information, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 139 If a futures service institution violates Article 98 of this Law by failing to provide relevant materials as required when engaging in futures service business, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 140 If an accounting firm, law firm, asset appraisal institution, or other futures service institution violates Article 99 of this Law by failing to exercise due diligence, resulting in false records, misleading statements, or material omissions in the documents it produces or issues, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its business income confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the business income; if there is no business income or the business income is less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

If a futures service institution commits the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph and causes losses to others, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 141 If a delivery warehouse commits any of the acts listed in Article 100 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the futures exchange shall be ordered to suspend or revoke its delivery warehouse qualification. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 142 If an information technology service institution violates Article 101 of this Law by failing to file for the record, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than 200,000 yuan.

If an information technology service institution violates Article 101 of this Law by providing services that do not comply with the national and futures industry technical management regulations and standards for information security, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its business income confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the business income; if there is no business income or the business income is less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan.

Article 143 If a futures operating institution, futures exchange, futures clearing institution, or non-futures operating institution settlement participant violates Article 116 of this Law by failing to set aside, manage, or use risk reserves in accordance with regulations, it shall be ordered to make corrections and given a warning. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 144 If a futures operating institution, futures exchange, futures clearing institution, futures service institution, or non-futures operating institution settlement participant violates Article 117 of this Law by failing to properly preserve materials and information related to business operations in accordance with regulations, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if it leaks, conceals, forges, alters, or destroys relevant documents and materials, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and fined not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than two million yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan, and its relevant business license shall be suspended or revoked, or it shall be prohibited from engaging in relevant business. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 145 If an overseas futures exchange or futures operating institution violates Articles 118 and 120 of this Law by failing to apply for registration with the State Council's futures regulatory authority, it shall be ordered to make corrections, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 146 If a domestic entity or individual violates Article 120, paragraph 1 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if the circumstances are serious, its overseas futures trading shall be suspended. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 147 If a representative office established in China by an overseas futures exchange or its personnel violates Article 121 of this Law by engaging in or engaging in any business activities in disguised form, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 148 Anyone who violates Article 122 of this Law by engaging in marketing, promotion, or solicitation activities within China shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 500,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed. The directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given a warning and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan.

Article 149 Anyone who refuses or obstructs the State Council's futures regulatory authority or the department authorized by the State Council, or their personnel, from lawfully exercising their supervision, inspection, or investigation powers shall be ordered to make corrections, fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan, and shall be subject to public security penalties by the public security organs in accordance with the law.

Article 150 If the circumstances of violating laws, administrative regulations, or relevant regulations of the State Council's futures regulatory authority are serious, the State Council's futures regulatory authority may impose a futures market ban on the responsible persons.

The term "futures market ban" in the preceding paragraph refers to a system whereby a person is prohibited from engaging in futures trading, conducting futures business, or serving as a director, supervisor, senior manager, or person in charge of a futures operating institution, futures exchange, or futures clearing institution for a certain period up to life.

Article 151 The administrative penalties stipulated in this Law shall be decided by the State Council's futures regulatory authority or the department authorized by the State Council in accordance with the division of responsibilities stipulated by the State Council; if other laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Article 152 Personnel of the State Council's futures regulatory authority or the department authorized by the State Council who fail to perform their duties stipulated in this Law, abuse their power, neglect their duties, use their positions to seek improper benefits, or disclose known commercial secrets of relevant entities or individuals shall be investigated for legal responsibility in accordance with the law.

Article 153 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal responsibility in accordance with the law.

Article 154 If a violator of this Law is liable for civil compensation and must pay fines, penalties, and illegal gains, and their property is insufficient to cover all payments, priority shall be given to satisfying the civil compensation liability.

## Chapter 13 Supplementary Provisions

Article 155 This Law shall come into force on August 1, 2022.
