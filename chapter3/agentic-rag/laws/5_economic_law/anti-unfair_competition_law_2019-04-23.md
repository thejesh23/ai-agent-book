# Anti-Unfair Competition Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on September 2, 1993

Revised at the 30th Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Construction Law of the People's Republic of China at the 10th Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 23, 2019

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy, encouraging and protecting fair competition, stopping acts of unfair competition, and protecting the lawful rights and interests of business operators and consumers.

Article 2 Business operators shall, in their production and business operations, follow the principles of voluntariness, equality, fairness, and good faith, and abide by laws and business ethics.

For the purposes of this Law, "acts of unfair competition" refer to acts of business operators that, in their production and business operations, violate the provisions of this Law, disrupt the order of market competition, and harm the lawful rights and interests of other business operators or consumers.

For the purposes of this Law, "business operators" refer to natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations that engage in the production or sale of goods or the provision of services (hereinafter "goods" includes services).

Article 3 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to stop acts of unfair competition and create a favorable environment and conditions for fair competition.

The State Council shall establish a coordination mechanism for anti-unfair competition work, study and decide on major policies regarding anti-unfair competition, and coordinate the handling of major issues concerning the maintenance of market competition order.

Article 4 The departments of the people's governments at or above the county level that perform the functions of industrial and commercial administration shall investigate and deal with acts of unfair competition; where laws or administrative regulations provide that other departments shall investigate and deal with such acts, those provisions shall apply.

Article 5 The State encourages, supports, and protects all organizations and individuals in exercising social supervision over acts of unfair competition.

State organs and their staff members shall not support or cover up acts of unfair competition.

Trade associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, guide and regulate their members to compete in accordance with the law, and maintain the order of market competition.

## Chapter II: Acts of Unfair Competition

Article 6 A business operator shall not commit any of the following confusing acts that cause others to mistake its goods for those of another business operator or to believe that there is a specific connection between its goods and another business operator:

(1) Using, without authorization, a mark identical or similar to the name, packaging, or decoration of another business operator's goods that has a certain influence;

(2) Using, without authorization, the name (including abbreviation, trade name, etc.) of another business operator's enterprise that has a certain influence, the name (including abbreviation, etc.) of a social organization, or the name (including pseudonym, stage name, translated name, etc.) of an individual;

(3) Using, without authorization, the main part of a domain name, website name, or webpage of another business operator that has a certain influence; or

(4) Other confusing acts sufficient to cause others to mistake its goods for those of another business operator or to believe that there is a specific connection between its goods and another business operator.

Article 7 A business operator shall not bribe any of the following entities or individuals with property or by other means in order to seek transaction opportunities or competitive advantages:

(1) Staff members of the counterparty to a transaction;

(2) Entities or individuals entrusted by the counterparty to a transaction to handle relevant matters; or

(3) Entities or individuals that may influence a transaction by using their authority or influence.

In a transaction, a business operator may openly pay a discount to the counterparty or pay a commission to an intermediary. Where a business operator pays a discount to the counterparty or pays a commission to an intermediary, it shall truthfully record such payment in its accounts. The business operator that accepts the discount or commission shall also truthfully record it in its accounts.

Where a staff member of a business operator engages in bribery, such act shall be deemed an act of the business operator; however, this does not apply if the business operator has evidence proving that the staff member's act is unrelated to seeking transaction opportunities or competitive advantages for the business operator.

Article 8 A business operator shall not make false or misleading commercial publicity about the performance, function, quality, sales status, user evaluation, honors received, etc. of its goods, thereby deceiving or misleading consumers.

A business operator shall not, by organizing false transactions or by other means, assist another business operator in making false or misleading commercial publicity.

Article 9 A business operator shall not commit any of the following acts of infringing trade secrets:

(1) Acquiring a right holder's trade secrets by theft, bribery, fraud, coercion, electronic intrusion, or any other illegitimate means;

(2) Disclosing, using, or allowing another person to use the right holder's trade secrets acquired by the means specified in the preceding subparagraph;

(3) Disclosing, using, or allowing another person to use the trade secrets in its possession in violation of its confidentiality obligations or the right holder's requirements for keeping the trade secrets confidential; or

(4) Abetting, inducing, or assisting another person to violate its confidentiality obligations or the right holder's requirements for keeping the trade secrets confidential, and to acquire, disclose, use, or allow another person to use the right holder's trade secrets.

Where any other natural person, legal person, or unincorporated organization commits any of the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph, it shall be deemed an infringement of trade secrets.

Where a third party, knowing or having reason to know that an employee, former employee, or other entity or individual of the trade secret right holder has committed any of the illegal acts listed in the first paragraph of this Article, still acquires, discloses, uses, or allows another person to use the trade secret, it shall be deemed an infringement of trade secrets.

For the purposes of this Law, "trade secrets" refer to technical information, operational information, and other commercial information that is not known to the public, has commercial value, and for which the right holder has taken corresponding confidentiality measures.

Article 10 A business operator shall not engage in any prize-giving sales under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The information on the prize-giving sales, such as the types of prizes, conditions for claiming prizes, amounts of prize money, or prizes, is unclear, affecting the claiming of prizes;

(2) The prize-giving sales are conducted by deceptive means, such as falsely claiming that there are prizes or intentionally having designated persons win the prizes; or

(3) In a lottery-type prize-giving sale, the amount of the top prize exceeds 50,000 yuan.

Article 11 A business operator shall not fabricate or disseminate false or misleading information to damage the business reputation or the reputation of the goods of a competitor.

Article 12 A business operator that uses the internet to engage in production and business operations shall comply with the provisions of this Law.

A business operator shall not, by using technical means or by influencing user choices or by other means, commit any of the following acts that hinder or disrupt the normal operation of network products or services lawfully provided by other business operators:

(1) Inserting links or forcing a target jump in the network products or services lawfully provided by another business operator without the consent of that business operator;

(2) Misleading, deceiving, or forcing users to modify, close, or uninstall the network products or services lawfully provided by another business operator;

(3) Maliciously implementing incompatibility with the network products or services lawfully provided by another business operator; or

(4) Other acts that hinder or disrupt the normal operation of network products or services lawfully provided by other business operators.

## Chapter III: Investigation of Suspected Acts of Unfair Competition

Article 13 When investigating a suspected act of unfair competition, the regulatory inspection department may take the following measures:

(1) Entering the business premises where the suspected act of unfair competition is taking place for inspection;

(2) Questioning the business operator under investigation, interested parties, and other relevant entities and individuals, and requiring them to explain the relevant circumstances or provide other materials related to the act under investigation;

(3) Consulting and copying agreements, account books, vouchers, documents, records, business correspondence, and other materials related to the suspected act of unfair competition;

(4) Sealing up or seizing property and articles related to the suspected act of unfair competition; and

(5) Inquiring into the bank accounts of the business operator suspected of committing the act of unfair competition.

The measures specified in the preceding paragraph shall be taken only after a written report is made to and approved by the principal responsible person of the regulatory inspection department. The measures specified in subparagraphs (4) and (5) of the preceding paragraph shall be taken only after a written report is made to and approved by the principal responsible person of the regulatory inspection department of the people's government at or above the city divided into districts.

When investigating a suspected act of unfair competition, the regulatory inspection department shall comply with the provisions of the Administrative Compulsion Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and administrative regulations, and shall promptly make the results of the investigation and handling public.

Article 14 When the regulatory inspection department investigates a suspected act of unfair competition, the business operator under investigation, interested parties, and other relevant entities and individuals shall truthfully provide relevant materials or information.

Article 15 The regulatory inspection department and its staff members shall have the obligation to keep confidential the trade secrets learned during the investigation.

Article 16 Any entity or individual has the right to report a suspected act of unfair competition to the regulatory inspection department, and the regulatory inspection department shall, upon receiving the report, handle it in a timely manner in accordance with the law.

The regulatory inspection department shall make public the telephone number, mailbox, or email address for receiving reports and shall keep the reporter confidential. Where a report is made with the real name and relevant facts and evidence are provided, the regulatory inspection department shall inform the reporter of the handling result.

## Chapter IV: Legal Liability

Article 17 A business operator that, in violation of the provisions of this Law, causes damage to another person shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

A business operator whose lawful rights and interests are harmed by an act of unfair competition may file a lawsuit with a people's court.

The amount of compensation for a business operator harmed by an act of unfair competition shall be determined based on the actual losses suffered by the business operator as a result of the infringement; where the actual losses are difficult to calculate, the amount shall be determined based on the profits gained by the infringer from the infringement. Where a business operator maliciously infringes a trade secret and the circumstances are serious, the amount of compensation may be determined at between one and five times the amount determined according to the methods mentioned above. The amount of compensation shall also include the reasonable expenses paid by the business operator to stop the infringement.

Where a business operator violates the provisions of Article 6 or Article 9 of this Law, and the actual losses suffered by the right holder as a result of the infringement or the profits gained by the infringer from the infringement are difficult to determine, the people's court shall, based on the circumstances of the infringement, award compensation of not more than five million yuan to the right holder.

Article 18 Where a business operator commits a confusing act in violation of the provisions of Article 6 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act and confiscate the illegal goods. If the illegal business turnover is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not more than five times the illegal business turnover may also be imposed; if there is no illegal business turnover or the illegal business turnover is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not more than 250,000 yuan may also be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Where the enterprise name registered by a business operator violates the provisions of Article 6 of this Law, the business operator shall promptly undergo name change registration; before the name is changed, the original enterprise registration authority shall replace the name with the unified social credit code.

Article 19 Where a business operator bribes another person in violation of the provisions of Article 7 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall confiscate the illegal gains and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than three million yuan. If the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 20 Where a business operator makes false or misleading commercial publicity about its goods in violation of the provisions of Article 8 of this Law, or assists another business operator in making false or misleading commercial publicity by organizing false transactions or by other means, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than one million yuan but not more than two million yuan shall be imposed, and the business license may be revoked.

Where a business operator, in violation of the provisions of Article 8 of this Law, publishes false advertisements, it shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 21 Where a business operator or any other natural person, legal person, or unincorporated organization infringes a trade secret in violation of the provisions of Article 9 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than one million yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than five million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 22 Where a business operator conducts prize-giving sales in violation of the provisions of Article 10 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 23 Where a business operator damages the business reputation or the reputation of the goods of a competitor in violation of the provisions of Article 11 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act, eliminate the adverse effects, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than three million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 24 Where a business operator hinders or disrupts the normal operation of network products or services lawfully provided by another business operator in violation of the provisions of Article 12 of this Law, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to stop the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than three million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 25 Where a business operator engages in unfair competition in violation of the provisions of this Law and has statutory circumstances such as voluntarily eliminating or mitigating the harmful consequences of the illegal act, the administrative penalty shall be mitigated or commuted in accordance with the law; where the illegal act is minor and is promptly corrected without causing harmful consequences, no administrative penalty shall be imposed.

Article 26 Where a business operator engages in unfair competition in violation of the provisions of this Law and is subject to an administrative penalty, the regulatory inspection department shall record it in the credit record and make it public in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 27 Where a business operator, in violation of the provisions of this Law, shall bear civil liability, administrative liability, and criminal liability, and its property is insufficient to pay all such liabilities, the property shall be first used to bear the civil liability.

Article 28 Where a person obstructs the regulatory inspection department from performing its duties in accordance with this Law, or refuses or hinders an investigation, the regulatory inspection department shall order it to make corrections; a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan may be imposed on an individual, and a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed on an entity; and the public security organ may impose a public security penalty in accordance with the law.

Article 29 Where a party is dissatisfied with a decision made by the regulatory inspection department, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

Article 30 Where a staff member of the regulatory inspection department abuses power, neglects duty, engages in malpractice for personal gain, or discloses trade secrets learned during an investigation, the staff member shall be given a sanction in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Where a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 32 In a civil trial procedure for infringement of trade secrets, where the trade secret right holder provides preliminary evidence proving that it has taken confidentiality measures for the trade secret it claims and reasonably indicates that the trade secret has been infringed, the alleged infringer shall prove that the trade secret claimed by the right holder does not fall within the scope of trade secrets as defined in this Law.

Where the trade secret right holder provides preliminary evidence reasonably indicating that the trade secret has been infringed and provides one of the following pieces of evidence, the alleged infringer shall prove that it has not infringed the trade secret:

(1) There is evidence showing that the alleged infringer had access to or an opportunity to acquire the trade secret, and the information it uses is substantially the same as the trade secret;

(2) There is evidence showing that the trade secret has been disclosed or used by the alleged infringer, or there is a risk of disclosure or use; or

(3) There is other evidence showing that the trade secret has been infringed by the alleged infringer.

## Chapter V: Supplementary Provisions

Article 33 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2018.
