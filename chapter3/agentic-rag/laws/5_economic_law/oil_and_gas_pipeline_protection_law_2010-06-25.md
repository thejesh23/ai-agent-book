# Oil and Natural Gas Pipeline Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on June 25, 2010

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting oil and natural gas pipelines, ensuring the safety of oil and natural gas transportation, and safeguarding national energy security and public safety.

**Article 2** This Law applies to the protection of pipelines used for transporting oil and natural gas within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

The protection of urban gas pipelines and pipelines within the factory areas of enterprises such as oil refineries and chemical plants is not governed by this Law.

**Article 3** For the purposes of this Law, "oil" includes crude oil and refined oil products, and "natural gas" includes natural gas, coalbed methane, and coal gas.

For the purposes of this Law, "pipelines" include pipelines and pipeline ancillary facilities.

**Article 4** The energy administration department of the State Council shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, be responsible for the national pipeline protection work, be responsible for organizing the formulation and implementation of the national pipeline development plan, coordinate the connection between the national pipeline development plan and other special plans, and coordinate major issues concerning pipeline protection across provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. Other relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, be responsible for related pipeline protection work within their respective functions and duties.

**Article 5** The energy administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the departments designated by the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties, shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, be responsible for pipeline protection work within their respective administrative regions, coordinate and handle major issues concerning pipeline protection within their administrative regions, guide and supervise relevant entities in fulfilling their pipeline protection obligations, and investigate and deal with illegal acts endangering pipeline safety in accordance with the law. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, be responsible for related pipeline protection work within their respective functions and duties.

The energy administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the departments designated by the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties, are collectively referred to as the departments responsible for pipeline protection work under local people's governments at or above the county level.

**Article 6** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen their leadership over pipeline protection work within their respective administrative regions, supervise and inspect relevant departments in fulfilling their pipeline protection duties in accordance with the law, and organize the elimination of major external safety hazards of pipelines.

**Article 7** Pipeline enterprises shall comply with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations on planning, construction, production safety, quality supervision, environmental protection, etc., implement the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications, establish and improve their own rules, regulations, and operating procedures concerning pipeline protection and organize their implementation, publicize knowledge on pipeline safety and protection, fulfill pipeline protection obligations, accept supervision implemented by the people's government and its relevant departments in accordance with the law, and ensure the safe operation of pipelines.

**Article 8** No unit or individual may commit any act that endangers pipeline safety.

Any unit or individual has the right to report acts endangering pipeline safety to the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level or other relevant departments. The department receiving the report shall handle it promptly within its functions and duties.

**Article 9** The State encourages and promotes the research, development, and application of new technologies for pipeline protection.

## Chapter II Pipeline Planning and Construction

**Article 10** The planning and construction of pipelines shall meet the requirements for pipeline protection and follow the principles of safety, environmental protection, land conservation, and economic rationality.

**Article 11** The energy administration department of the State Council shall organize the formulation of a national pipeline development plan based on the needs of national economic and social development. When organizing the formulation of the national pipeline development plan, opinions shall be solicited from relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The national pipeline development plan shall conform to the national energy plan and be coordinated with the overall land use plan, urban and rural plans, as well as plans for mineral resources, environmental protection, water conservancy, railways, highways, waterways, ports, telecommunications, etc.

**Article 12** Pipeline enterprises shall formulate pipeline construction plans based on the national pipeline development plan, and submit the pipeline route selection plan determined in the pipeline construction plan to the competent department of urban and rural planning of the local people's government at or above the county level where the proposed pipeline is to be constructed for review; if it is found to conform to the urban and rural plan after review, it shall be incorporated into the local urban and rural plan in accordance with the law.

The use of land designated for pipeline construction in the urban and rural plan shall not be changed without authorization.

**Article 13** The route selection for pipeline construction shall avoid seismically active fault zones and areas prone to floods and geological disasters, and maintain the protection distances specified in this Law, relevant laws and administrative regulations, and the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications from buildings, structures, railways, highways, waterways, ports, municipal facilities, military facilities, cables, optical cables, etc.

If the area through which a newly constructed pipeline passes is restricted by geographical conditions and cannot meet the pipeline protection requirements stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the pipeline enterprise shall propose a protection plan, which may only be constructed after being reviewed and evaluated by experts in pipeline protection and approved by the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level where the pipeline is located.

Pipeline construction projects shall undergo environmental impact assessment in accordance with the law.

**Article 14** The use of land for pipeline construction shall be governed by the provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative regulations.

If a pipeline constructed in accordance with the law passes through collectively owned land or state-owned land for which others have obtained the right to use, affecting land use, the pipeline enterprise shall provide compensation based on the use of the land at the time of pipeline construction.

**Article 15** No unit or individual may obstruct the construction of pipeline projects that have obtained administrative permits or have been filed for the record and meet the conditions for commencement in accordance with the provisions of laws and the State Council.

**Article 16** Pipeline construction shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations concerning the quality management of construction projects.

Pipeline enterprises shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, select survey, design, construction, and project supervision units with corresponding qualifications for pipeline construction.

The safety protection facilities of pipelines shall be designed, constructed, and put into use simultaneously with the main pipeline project.

The quality of pipeline products and their accessories used in pipeline construction shall meet the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications.

**Article 17** The construction of pipelines crossing or spanning water conservancy projects, flood control facilities, river courses, waterways, railways, highways, ports, power facilities, communication facilities, and municipal facilities shall comply with this Law and relevant laws and administrative regulations, and implement the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications.

**Article 18** Pipeline enterprises shall set up pipeline markers along the pipeline route in accordance with the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications. If pipeline markers are damaged or safety warnings are unclear, the pipeline enterprise shall repair or replace them promptly.

**Article 19** After a pipeline is completed, it shall undergo completion acceptance inspection in accordance with relevant state regulations. The completion acceptance inspection shall review whether the pipeline meets the pipeline protection requirements stipulated in this Law. It may only be formally delivered for use after passing the acceptance inspection.

**Article 20** Within sixty days from the date the pipeline passes the completion acceptance inspection, the pipeline enterprise shall submit the as-built survey map to the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level where the pipeline is located for filing; the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level shall distribute the as-built survey map submitted by the pipeline enterprise to the departments of planning, construction, land and resources, railways, transportation, water conservancy, public security, production safety supervision and management, and relevant military authorities at the same level.

**Article 21** If local people's governments at various levels, when formulating or adjusting the overall land use plan and urban and rural plans, require the modification, relocation, or addition of protective facilities for pipelines, they shall negotiate with the pipeline enterprise to determine a compensation plan.

## Chapter III Protection During Pipeline Operation

**Article 22** Pipeline enterprises shall establish and improve a pipeline patrol system, and assign specialized personnel for daily patrol of the pipeline route. If pipeline patrol personnel discover situations or hazards endangering pipeline safety, they shall handle and report them promptly in accordance with regulations.

**Article 23** Pipeline enterprises shall regularly inspect and maintain pipelines to ensure they are in good condition; key monitoring shall be carried out on sections and locations with higher pipeline safety risks, and effective measures shall be taken to prevent pipeline accidents.

For pipelines that do not meet safe use conditions, the pipeline enterprise shall promptly update, modify, or cease their use.

**Article 24** Pipeline enterprises shall equip themselves with the necessary personnel and technical equipment for pipeline protection, research, develop, and use advanced and applicable pipeline protection technologies, ensure the necessary funding for pipeline protection, and reward units and individuals who have made outstanding contributions to pipeline protection.

**Article 25** If a pipeline enterprise discovers a safety hazard in a pipeline, it shall promptly eliminate it. If the pipeline enterprise itself has difficulty eliminating an external safety hazard existing in the pipeline, it shall report to the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level. The department responsible for pipeline protection work that receives the report shall promptly coordinate the elimination or report to the people's government to promptly organize the elimination of the safety hazard.

**Article 26** No unit or individual may encroach upon land for which a pipeline enterprise has legally obtained the right to use.

For the rational use of land, under the condition of ensuring pipeline safety, a pipeline enterprise may agree with relevant units and individuals to allow them to plant shallow-rooted crops. However, unless otherwise agreed, the pipeline enterprise shall not be liable for compensation for crop losses caused by pipeline patrol, inspection, or maintenance.

**Article 27** When a pipeline enterprise carries out operations such as patrol, inspection, and maintenance of pipelines, relevant units and individuals along the pipeline route shall provide necessary convenience.

If losses are caused to land use right holders or other units or individuals due to operations such as pipeline patrol, inspection, and maintenance, the pipeline enterprise shall provide compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 28** The following acts endangering pipeline safety are prohibited:

(1) Unauthorized opening or closing of pipeline valves;
(2) Damaging pipelines by means such as moving, cutting, drilling, smashing, prying, or dismantling;
(3) Moving, damaging, or altering pipeline markers;
(4) Driving heavy vehicles on the inspection walkway above buried pipelines;
(5) Walking or placing heavy objects on above-ground pipeline routes, overhead pipeline routes, and pipe bridges.

**Article 29** It is prohibited to erect power lines or communication lines above the pipeline ancillary facilities listed in Item 1 of Article 58 of this Law, or to carry out engineering excavation, engineering drilling, or mining within the structural area of gas storage facilities.

**Article 30** Within a five-meter range on each side of the centerline of the pipeline route, the following acts endangering pipeline safety are prohibited:

(1) Planting trees, shrubs, vines, reeds, bamboo, or other deep-rooted plants whose roots may reach the pipeline burial depth and damage the pipeline anti-corrosion coating;
(2) Excavating soil, quarrying stone, using fire, stacking heavy objects, discharging corrosive substances, or using mechanical tools for excavation construction;
(3) Digging ponds, building canals, building drying yards, building aquaculture farms, building greenhouses, building livestock sheds, building houses, or constructing other buildings or structures.

**Article 31** When constructing the following buildings or structures near the centerline of the pipeline route and around the pipeline ancillary facilities listed in Item 1 of Article 58 of this Law, the distance between the buildings or structures and the pipeline route and pipeline ancillary facilities shall meet the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications:

(1) Densely populated buildings such as residential communities, schools, hospitals, entertainment venues, stations, and shopping malls;
(2) Places for the production, operation, or storage of flammable and explosive items such as substations, gas stations, gas filling stations, oil storage tanks, and gas storage tanks.

The mandatory requirements of national technical specifications stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be determined based on the principles of ensuring the safety of pipelines, buildings, and structures and conserving land.

**Article 32** Within a five-hundred-meter range on each side of the centerline of a pipeline crossing a river, acts such as anchoring, dragging anchors, sand dredging, mud dredging, quarrying, and underwater blasting are prohibited. However, maintenance dredging operations carried out for flood control and waterway clearance under the condition of ensuring pipeline safety are excepted.

**Article 33** Within a one-thousand-meter range on each side of the centerline of a dedicated pipeline tunnel, quarrying, mining, and blasting are prohibited, except for the circumstances specified in Paragraph 2 of this Article.

Within the area specified in the preceding paragraph, if quarrying or blasting operations are truly necessary for the construction of public projects such as railways, highways, or water conservancy projects, they may only be carried out after approval by the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the county people's government where the pipeline is located and after taking necessary safety protection measures.

**Article 34** Without the consent of the pipeline enterprise, other units shall not use pipeline ancillary facilities such as dedicated pipeline access roads, pipeline hydraulic protection facilities, and dedicated pipeline tunnels.

**Article 35** For the following construction operations, the construction unit shall apply to the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the county people's government where the pipeline is located:

(1) Construction operations crossing or spanning the pipeline;
(2) Within a range of five to fifty meters on each side of the centerline of the pipeline route and within a one-hundred-meter range around the pipeline ancillary facilities listed in Item 1 of Article 58 of this Law, constructing, modifying, or expanding railways, highways, river canals, erecting power lines, burying underground cables or optical cables, or installing safety grounding bodies or lightning grounding bodies;
(3) Within a two-hundred-meter range on each side of the centerline of the pipeline route and within a five-hundred-meter range around the pipeline ancillary facilities listed in Item 1 of Article 58 of this Law, carrying out blasting, seismic exploration, engineering excavation, engineering drilling, or mining.

After receiving the application, the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the county people's government shall organize the construction unit and the pipeline enterprise to negotiate and determine the construction operation plan and sign a safety protection agreement; if negotiation fails, the department responsible for pipeline protection work shall organize a safety review and decide whether to approve the operation.

**Article 36** Applying for construction operations specified in Paragraph 2 of Article 33 and Article 35 of this Law shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a construction operation plan that meets the requirements for pipeline safety and public safety;
(2) Having formulated an accident emergency plan;
(3) The construction operation personnel possess knowledge of pipeline protection;
(4) Having equipment and facilities to ensure safe construction operations.

**Article 37** For construction operations specified in Paragraph 2 of Article 33 and Article 35 of this Law, the pipeline enterprise shall be notified in writing seven days before the start of construction. The pipeline enterprise shall assign specialized personnel to the site to provide safety guidance for pipeline protection.

**Article 38** In emergency situations, a pipeline enterprise may use another person's land or facilities first for emergency pipeline repair operations, but shall promptly notify the owner or right holder of the land or facilities. If losses are caused to the owner or right holder of the land or facilities, the pipeline enterprise shall provide compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 39** Pipeline enterprises shall formulate their own pipeline accident emergency plans and file them with the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the county people's government where the pipeline is located; equip themselves with emergency rescue personnel and equipment, and conduct regular pipeline accident emergency rescue drills.

When a pipeline accident occurs, the pipeline enterprise shall immediately activate its own pipeline accident emergency plan, promptly notify units and residents that may be endangered by the accident in accordance with regulations, take effective measures to eliminate or mitigate the harm of the accident, and report to the department responsible for pipeline protection work, the production safety supervision and management department, and other relevant departments under the county people's government where the accident occurred, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on accident investigation and handling.

The department responsible for pipeline protection work that receives the report shall promptly report the accident situation upwards in accordance with regulations, and organize the adoption of accident handling measures based on the actual situation of the pipeline accident, or report to the people's government to promptly activate the pipeline accident emergency plan for the administrative region and organize accident emergency response and rescue.

**Article 40** If oil leaked from a pipeline or oil discharged due to pipeline emergency repair causes environmental pollution, the pipeline enterprise shall promptly remediate it. If the environmental pollution caused by the pipeline leakage is due to the act of a third party, the pipeline enterprise has the right to recover the remediation costs from the third party.

Liability for compensation for environmental pollution damage shall be governed by the Tort Liability Law of the People's Republic of China and the provisions of laws on the prevention and control of environmental pollution.

**Article 41** Oil leaked from a pipeline and oil discharged due to pipeline emergency repair shall be recovered and disposed of by the pipeline enterprise. No unit or individual may encroach upon, steal, or loot it.

**Article 42** If a pipeline is shut down, sealed, or decommissioned, the pipeline enterprise shall take necessary safety protection measures and report to the department responsible for pipeline protection work under the local people's government at or above the county level for filing.

**Article 43** The guarding of key pipeline protection parts by the Chinese People's Armed Police Force shall be carried out in accordance with the People's Armed Police Law of the People's Republic of China and the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Handling of Encounters Between Pipeline Construction Projects and Other Construction Projects

**Article 44** The encounter relationship between a pipeline construction project and other construction projects shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the law; if the law does not provide for it, it shall be handled by the two parties to the construction project through negotiation based on the following principles, and each shall provide necessary convenience for the other:

(1) The construction project that starts later shall be subject to the construction project that started earlier or is already completed;
(2) For construction projects starting simultaneously, the later-approved construction project shall be subject to the earlier-approved construction project.

In accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the construction project that starts later or is approved later shall meet the safety protection requirements of the construction project that started earlier, is already completed, or was approved earlier; if the construction project that started earlier, is already completed, or was approved earlier needs to be modified, relocated, or have protective facilities added, the party of the construction project that starts later or is approved later shall bear the costs incurred thereby.

When a pipeline construction project encounters another construction project, the two parties to the construction project shall negotiate and determine the construction operation plan and sign a safety protection agreement, and assign specialized personnel to supervise and guide the other party's construction on site.

**Article 45** If a legally approved pipeline construction project needs to pass through another construction project that is under construction, the construction unit of the other project shall reserve a pipeline passage or pre-build pipeline passage facilities according to the needs of the pipeline construction project, and the pipeline enterprise shall bear the costs incurred thereby.

If another legally approved construction project needs to pass through a pipeline construction project that is under construction, the pipeline construction unit shall reserve a passage or pre-build related facilities according to the needs of the other construction project, and the construction unit of the other project shall bear the costs incurred thereby.

**Article 46** If a pipeline construction project passes through a mineral resource mining area, the pipeline enterprise shall negotiate with the mineral resource mining enterprise to determine the safety protection plan for the pipeline. If the mineral resource mining enterprise is required to pre-build protective facilities or take other protective measures according to the pipeline safety protection requirements, the pipeline enterprise shall bear the costs incurred thereby.

If a mineral resource mining enterprise fails to pre-build protective facilities or take other protective measures as agreed, resulting in geological disasters such as ground subsidence, cracks, or settlement, necessitating the modification, relocation, or adoption of other protective measures for the pipeline, the mineral resource mining enterprise shall bear the costs incurred thereby.**Article 47** Where flood control, diversion, or other hydraulic protection facilities are constructed as part of railway, highway, or other construction projects and may affect pipeline protection, the construction entity shall notify the pipeline enterprise in advance and take care to protect the downstream hydraulic protection facilities of existing pipelines.

If the construction of hydraulic protection facilities for flood control or diversion impairs the function of downstream hydraulic protection facilities of existing pipelines, necessitating the construction, renovation, or expansion of pipeline hydraulic protection facilities, the construction entity shall bear the additional costs incurred.

**Article 48** The water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall take pipeline protection into account when formulating flood control and flood discharge plans.

When flood discharge is required in areas where pipelines are located, the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, after the flood discharge plan is finalized, promptly notify the department responsible for pipeline protection under the same level of government and the pipeline enterprise of the discharge volume and timing, or make a public announcement. The department responsible for pipeline protection and the pipeline enterprise shall take flood control protection measures for the pipelines.

**Article 49** Where a pipeline intersects a navigable waterway and it is necessary to construct pipeline protection facilities within the waterway, a technical demonstration of navigational standards shall be conducted, and approval from the competent waterway authority shall be obtained. Upon completion, the pipeline protection facilities shall be inspected and accepted by the competent waterway authority.

During the construction operations specified in the preceding paragraph, navigation marks shall be installed within the approved construction area. The costs of installing and maintaining these navigation marks shall be borne by the pipeline enterprise.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

**Article 50** If a pipeline enterprise commits any of the following acts, the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is not completed within the period, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; and the directly responsible managers and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action:

(1) Failing to patrol, inspect, and maintain pipelines in accordance with this Law;
(2) Failing to promptly update, renovate, or decommission pipelines that do not meet safety operation conditions;
(3) Failing to install, repair, or update relevant pipeline markers in accordance with this Law;
(4) Failing to submit as-built pipeline survey drawings to the department responsible for pipeline protection under the people's government for filing in accordance with this Law;
(5) Failing to formulate a pipeline accident emergency plan for the enterprise, or failing to file the enterprise's pipeline accident emergency plan with the department responsible for pipeline protection under the people's government;
(6) Failing to take effective measures to eliminate or mitigate accident hazards upon the occurrence of a pipeline accident;
(7) Failing to take necessary safety protection measures for pipelines that have been taken out of service, sealed, or decommissioned.

If the pipeline enterprise's violation of this Law also violates other laws such as those on construction project quality management, work safety, or fire protection, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with those other laws.

If a pipeline enterprise causes damage to the lawful rights and interests of others, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 51** Anyone who damages pipelines by means such as moving, cutting, drilling, smashing, prying, or dismantling, or who steals or forcibly seizes petroleum or natural gas transported, leaked, or discharged from pipelines, if the act does not constitute a crime, shall be subject to public security penalties in accordance with the law.

**Article 52** Anyone who violates the provisions of Articles 29, 30, 32, or Paragraph 1 of Article 33 of this Law by engaging in acts that endanger pipeline safety shall be ordered by the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act; if the circumstances are relatively serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the entity, and a fine of not less than 200 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed on the individual; the illegally constructed buildings, structures, or other facilities shall be ordered to be demolished within a specified period; if demolition is not completed within the period, the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level shall organize the demolition, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

**Article 53** Anyone who engages in construction operations specified in Paragraph 2 of Article 33 or Article 35 of this Law without legally required approval shall be ordered by the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act; if the circumstances are relatively serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; the illegally constructed buildings, structures, or other facilities that endanger pipeline safety shall be ordered to be demolished within a specified period; if demolition is not completed within the period, the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level shall organize the demolition, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the violator.

**Article 54** Anyone who violates this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be ordered by the department responsible for pipeline protection under the local people's government at or above the county level to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 200 yuan but not more than 1,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Opening or closing pipeline valves without authorization;
(2) Moving, damaging, or altering pipeline markers;
(3) Driving heavy vehicles on the inspection walkways above buried pipelines;
(4) Walking or placing heavy objects on above-ground pipeline routes, overhead pipeline routes, or pipeline bridges;
(5) Obstructing legally conducted pipeline construction.

**Article 55** Anyone who violates this Law by engaging in acts that endanger pipeline safety, causing damage to a pipeline enterprise, shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

**Article 56** If a local people's government at or above the county level, its department responsible for pipeline protection, or other relevant departments, in violation of this Law, fails to promptly organize the elimination of external pipeline safety hazards that should be eliminated, fails to investigate and punish acts endangering pipeline safety upon discovery or upon receiving reports of such acts in accordance with the law, or otherwise fails to perform their duties in accordance with this Law, their superior authority shall order them to make corrections, and the directly responsible managers and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law.

**Article 57** Anyone who violates this Law, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

**Article 58** For the purposes of this Law, "pipeline ancillary facilities" include:

(1) Pumping stations, heating stations, metering stations, oil gathering stations, gas gathering stations, oil transmission stations, gas transmission stations, gas distribution stations, processing yards, pigging stations, valve chambers, valve pits, venting facilities, oil depots, gas storage facilities, loading/unloading trestles, and loading/unloading yards for pipelines;
(2) Hydraulic protection facilities, wind protection facilities, lightning protection facilities, seismic protection facilities, communication facilities, safety monitoring facilities, electrical power facilities, pipeline dikes, pipeline bridges, and pipeline-specific culverts, tunnels, and other crossing facilities;
(3) Cathodic protection stations, cathodic protection test posts, anode beds, stray current drainage stations, and other anti-corrosion facilities for pipelines;
(4) Leak detection devices for pipelines crossing railways or highways;
(5) Other ancillary facilities for pipelines.

**Article 59** For densely populated areas and places for the production, operation, or storage of flammable and explosive articles that were constructed within the pipeline protection distance before this Law came into effect, the local people's government shall, based on local actual conditions, plan and implement relocation, clearance, or necessary protective measures in a phased and step-by-step manner. If existing pipelines need to be modified, relocated, or equipped with necessary protective measures, the compensation plan shall be negotiated and determined with the pipeline enterprise.

**Article 60** The State Council may formulate special provisions for the protection of offshore oil and natural gas pipelines based on their specific circumstances.

**Article 61** This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 2010.
