# Waterway Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 28, 2014

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Six Laws Including the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating and strengthening the planning, construction, maintenance and protection of waterways, ensuring unobstructed waterways and navigation safety, and promoting the development of waterway transport.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "waterway" means the channels within inland waters such as rivers and lakes within the territory of the People's Republic of China that can be used for navigation by vessels, as well as channels within internal seas and territorial waters that can be used for navigation by vessels after construction and maintenance. Waterways include navigation structures, waterway regulation structures, navigation aids and other waterway facilities.

Article 3 The planning, construction, maintenance and protection of waterways shall be carried out in accordance with the needs of economic and social development and national defense construction, follow the principles of comprehensive utilization and protection of water resources and the ecological environment, be subject to the overall arrangement of comprehensive transportation system construction and flood control, take into account the needs of water supply, irrigation, power generation, fisheries, etc., and give full play to the comprehensive benefits of water resources.

Article 4 The State Council and the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen their leadership over waterway work, organize, coordinate and urge relevant departments to take measures to maintain and improve waterway navigability, protect waterway safety, and maintain the integrity and unobstructedness of the waterway network.

The State Council and the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the level of economic and social development and the needs of waterway construction and maintenance, reasonably arrange funds for waterway construction and maintenance in their fiscal budgets.

Article 5 The competent department of transport under the State Council shall be in charge of the national waterway administration, and shall, in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, directly administer important trunk waterways crossing provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as important waterways such as international and boundary rivers.

The competent departments of transport of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government, be in charge of the administration of waterways under their jurisdiction.

The institutions responsible for waterway administration established by the competent department of transport under the State Council in accordance with the provisions of the State Council, and the departments or institutions responsible for waterway administration of local people's governments at or above the county level (hereinafter collectively referred to as the departments responsible for waterway administration) shall undertake the waterway administration work provided for in this Law.

## Chapter II Waterway Planning

Article 6 Waterway plans are divided into national waterway plans, river basin waterway plans, regional waterway plans, and waterway plans of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Waterway plans shall include the functional positioning, planning objectives, planned technical grades, implementation steps, and safeguard measures for the waterways.

Waterway plans shall conform to the river basin and regional comprehensive plans formulated according to law, comply with water resources plans, flood control plans and marine functional zoning, and be coordinated with relevant specialized plans involving comprehensive utilization of water resources, as well as other relevant plans formulated according to law such as urban and rural plans, environmental protection plans, and military facility protection zone plans.

Article 7 Waterways shall be classified into technical grades. The technical grades of waterways include current technical grades and planned technical grades. The planned technical grade of a waterway shall be assessed based on relevant natural conditions, requirements for flood control, water supply, water resources protection, ecological and environmental protection, and the needs of shipping development.

Article 8 The national waterway plan shall be formulated by the competent department of transport under the State Council jointly with the development and reform department under the State Council, the water administrative department under the State Council, and other departments, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval and promulgation. River basin waterway plans and regional waterway plans shall be formulated and promulgated by the competent department of transport under the State Council.

Waterway plans of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the competent departments of transport of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government jointly with the development and reform departments and water administrative departments at the same level, and shall be submitted to the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government jointly with the competent department of transport under the State Council for approval and promulgation.

When formulating a waterway plan, opinions of relevant departments and relevant military organs shall be solicited, and an environmental impact assessment shall be conducted according to law. Where sea areas or important fishing waters are involved, the marine department and the fishery administrative department at the same level shall participate. When formulating the national waterway plan, river basin waterway plans and regional waterway plans, opinions of the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be solicited.

River basin waterway plans, regional waterway plans and waterway plans of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall conform to the national waterway plan.

Article 9 Waterway plans formulated and promulgated according to law shall be implemented; if a waterway plan truly needs to be amended, it shall be handled in accordance with the planning formulation procedures.

## Chapter III Waterway Construction

Article 10 The construction of new waterways and waterway engineering projects carried out to improve waterway navigability shall comply with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on construction project quality management, safety management and ecological and environmental protection, conform to waterway plans, implement relevant national standards, industry standards and technical specifications, and go through relevant procedures according to law.

Article 11 The waterway construction entity shall, according to the technical requirements of the waterway construction project, select survey, design, construction and supervision units with corresponding qualifications through bidding or other means according to law, supervise and inspect the project quality and safety, and be responsible for the project quality and safety.

Survey, design, construction and supervision units engaged in waterway engineering construction shall obtain corresponding qualifications in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, engage in waterway engineering construction activities within the scope permitted by their qualification grades, and be responsible for the quality and safety of survey, design, construction and supervision according to law.

Article 12 The competent departments of transport of relevant people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the quality and safety of waterway construction projects to ensure the quality and safety of waterway construction projects.

Article 13 After the completion of a waterway construction project, a completion acceptance inspection shall be organized in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, and the project may be formally put into use only after passing the acceptance inspection.

The waterway construction entity shall, within sixty days from the date of passing the completion acceptance inspection of the waterway construction project, submit the as-built survey drawings to the department responsible for waterway administration. The as-built survey drawings of coastal waterways shall also be submitted to the naval navigation guarantee department.

Article 14 The construction of waterway engineering projects shall maintain river regime stability, meet flood control requirements, and shall not endanger the safety of other projects or facilities constructed according to law. If other projects or facilities constructed according to law are damaged due to waterway engineering construction, the waterway construction entity shall repair them or compensate according to law.

## Chapter IV Waterway Maintenance

Article 15 The competent department of transport under the State Council shall formulate technical specifications for waterway maintenance.

The departments responsible for waterway administration shall carry out waterway maintenance in accordance with the technical specifications for waterway maintenance to ensure that the waterways are in good navigable technical condition.

Article 16 The departments responsible for waterway administration shall, based on the current technical grade of the waterway or the natural conditions of the waterway, determine and publish the waterway maintenance dimensions and inland waterway charts.

Waterway maintenance dimensions refer to the technical requirements such as water depth, width, and radius of curvature that a waterway should maintain during different water level periods.

Article 17 The departments responsible for waterway administration shall, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of transport under the State Council, patrol the waterways. If it is found that the actual dimensions of a waterway are less than the maintenance dimensions or there are other circumstances that do not meet the requirements for ensuring the safety of vessel navigation, maintenance shall be carried out, a waterway notice shall be issued promptly, and the maritime administrative agency shall be notified.

Article 18 If a maritime administrative agency discovers circumstances such as waterway damage that endanger navigation safety, it shall promptly notify the department responsible for waterway administration and take necessary safety measures.

Other entities and individuals who discover circumstances such as waterway damage that endanger navigation safety shall promptly report to the department responsible for waterway administration or the maritime administrative agency.

Article 19 The departments responsible for waterway administration shall reasonably arrange waterway maintenance operations, avoiding concentrated operations that restrict navigation and operations during peak navigation periods.

When carrying out waterway maintenance activities that affect navigation, such as dredging or obstacle removal, or when maintenance operations that truly require restricting navigation are necessary, the departments responsible for waterway administration shall set up obvious operation markers, take necessary safety measures, and promptly notify the maritime administrative agency in advance to ensure the passage of passing vessels and the safety of engineering facilities constructed according to law. After the maintenance operations are completed, the operation markers and other residues that affect waterway navigability shall be promptly removed to restore normal navigation.

Article 20 If waterway maintenance operations may cause waterway blockage, the relevant departments responsible for waterway administration shall, jointly with the maritime administrative agency, notify in advance the departments responsible for waterway administration and maritime administrative agencies in the relevant areas, jointly formulate a vessel diversion plan, and announce it to the public.

Article 21 If a waterway is damaged or blocked due to emergencies such as natural disasters or accidents, the department responsible for waterway administration shall, in accordance with the emergency response plan, repair and reopen it as soon as possible; if necessary, the people's government at or above the county level shall organize the repair and reopening as soon as possible.

If a vessel, facility or other object sinks in waterway waters, affecting waterway unobstructedness and navigation safety, its owner or operator shall immediately report to the department responsible for waterway administration and the maritime administrative agency, set up markers by itself or entrust the department responsible for waterway administration or the maritime administrative agency to set up markers on its behalf in accordance with regulations, and shall salvage and remove it within the time limit set by the maritime administrative agency.

Article 22 The setting, maintenance, protection and management of navigation aids shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws, administrative regulations and national standards or industry standards.

Article 23 If military units need to use waterways for performing tasks or combat readiness training, the departments responsible for waterway administration shall provide necessary support and assistance.

## Chapter V Waterway Protection

Article 24 The construction, reconstruction, or expansion (hereinafter collectively referred to as construction) of buildings or structures such as bridges, tunnels, pipelines, and cables that span or cross waterways shall meet the requirements of the planned technical grade of the waterway regarding navigable clearance height, width, burial depth, and other waterway navigability conditions.

Article 25 When constructing a permanent river closure dam or sluice on a navigable river, the construction entity shall construct navigation structures in accordance with the planned technical grade of the waterway development. The navigation structures shall be planned, designed, constructed, accepted, and put into use simultaneously with the main project.

If it is difficult to maintain the original navigable capacity of the waterway during the construction of the dam or sluice, the construction entity shall take remedial measures such as constructing temporary waterways or arranging transshipment over the dam, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the construction entity.

If a dam or sluice is constructed on a non-navigable river and the river becomes navigable afterwards, the construction entity of the dam or sluice shall simultaneously construct navigation structures or reserve space for navigation structures. Unless otherwise provided by the State, the construction costs of the navigation structures shall be borne by the competent department of transport.

The operation of navigation structures shall meet the needs of vessel passage, and the operation plan shall be approved by the department responsible for waterway administration and published. The construction entity or management unit of the navigation structures shall maintain and service the navigation structures in accordance with regulations to keep them in normal operation.

Article 26 When constructing buildings or structures adjacent to rivers, lakes, or seas within the waterway protection zone, the requirements of the waterway navigability conditions shall be met.

The waterway protection zone shall be delineated by the competent department of transport of the local people's government at or above the county level jointly with the water administrative department or river basin management agency and the land and resources department, based on the planned technical grade of the waterway development and the actual needs of waterway protection, and shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval and promulgation. The waterway protection zone for waterways directly administered by the competent department of transport under the State Council shall be delineated and promulgated by the competent department of transport under the State Council jointly with the water administrative department under the State Council, the land and resources department under the State Council, and the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government. If the waterway protection zone involves sea areas or important fishing waters, it shall also be delineated jointly with the marine department and the fishery administrative department at the same level, respectively.

Article 27 When constructing the projects specified in Articles 24, 25(1), and 26(1) of this Law (hereinafter collectively referred to as projects related to waterways), except for special projects such as flood control and water supply carried out in accordance with laws, administrative regulations or the provisions of the State Council, the waterway navigability conditions shall not be reduced due to the project construction.

Article 28 When constructing a project related to a waterway, the construction entity shall, at the feasibility study stage of the project, evaluate the impact of the construction project on the waterway navigability conditions and submit it to the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency with review authority for review, except for the following projects:

(1) Medium and small river regulation projects adjacent to rivers or lakes;
(2) Water projects constructed on non-navigable rivers;
(3) Projects such as repair of water damage, reinforcement and risk removal of existing water projects, and renovation that do not involve navigation structures and do not change the original navigability conditions of the waterway, which do not affect the waterway navigability conditions.

If the materials submitted by the construction entity for the evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions do not comply with the provisions of this Law, they may be supplemented or modified and resubmitted to the review department for review.

If no evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions has been conducted, or if the review department considers that the construction project does not comply with the provisions of this Law, the construction entity shall not construct the project. For government-invested projects, if no evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions has been conducted, or if the review department considers that the construction project does not comply with the provisions of this Law, the department responsible for project approval shall not approve the project.

Article 29 The evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions for construction projects approved or ratified by the State Council or relevant departments under the State Council, as well as for construction projects related to waterways directly administered by the competent department of transport under the State Council, shall be reviewed by the competent department of transport under the State Council; the evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions for other construction projects shall be reviewed by the competent department of transport of the local people's government at or above the county level or the waterway administration agency in accordance with the provisions of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 30 The connection of navigable water levels between adjacent river closure dams or sluices on a waterway shall meet the national navigation standards and technical requirements. Water projects located on the waterway and its upstream tributaries shall, in the design, construction, and operation scheduling, take into account the downstream discharge required for the designed minimum navigable water level of the downstream waterway, except where hydrological conditions exceed the actual standards.

The determination of the minimum downstream discharge required to ensure downstream waterway navigation and the allowable water level changes meeting waterway navigability conditions shall be made after soliciting the opinions of the department responsible for waterway administration.

If a water project needs to significantly reduce or release a large volume of water, it shall promptly notify the department responsible for waterway administration and the maritime administrative agency in advance, allowing reasonable time for vessels to take evasive action.

Article 31 If the construction of a project related to a waterway affects the normal function of the waterway, the department responsible for waterway administration and the maritime administrative agency may, as needed, temporarily adjust the position or alignment of navigation aids or the waterway; they shall restore them promptly after the impact is eliminated. The costs incurred shall be borne by the construction entity, except for adjustments caused by flood control and emergency rescue projects.

Article 32 Before the completion acceptance of a project related to a waterway, the construction entity shall promptly remove temporary facilities and their residues that affect waterway navigability conditions.

Article 33 Construction activities of projects related to waterways shall not endanger waterway safety.

If construction activities of projects related to waterways damage the waterway, the construction entity shall repair it or compensate according to law.

Article 34 When constructing bridges or other buildings on navigable waters, the construction entity shall set up navigation aids and other facilities in accordance with relevant national regulations and technical requirements, and bear the corresponding costs.

The waterway navigation aids in the bridge area shall be managed and maintained by the department responsible for waterway administration and the maritime administrative agency.

Article 35 The following acts that endanger waterway navigation safety are prohibited:

(1) Setting up fishing gear or aquaculture facilities within the waterway;
(2) Dumping sand, gravel, soil, garbage or other waste within the waterway or the waterway protection zone;
(3) Engaging in cargo handling, refueling, vessel repair, fishing, etc., within navigation structures, their approach channels, and vessel dispatch areas, affecting the normal operation of navigation structures;
(4) Endangering the safety of waterway facilities;
(5) Other acts that endanger waterway navigation safety.

Article 36 Sand mining within river channels shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations. Illegal sand mining activities such as mining in sand mining prohibited areas legally designated within river channels, mining without a license, or mining beyond the approved scope and methods are prohibited.

Sand mining within waterways and waterway protection zones shall not impair waterway navigability conditions.

Article 37 If a river closure dam or sluice constructed before the implementation of this Law has caused the navigable river to become unnavigable, and navigation needs to be restored and conditions exist for constructing navigation structures, the development and reform department shall, jointly with the water administrative department and the competent department of transport, propose a plan for restoring navigation and submit it to the people's government at the same level for decision.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 38 If waterway construction, survey, design, construction, or supervision units violate the provisions of this Law in waterway construction activities, the competent department of transport of the people's government at or above the county level shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on bidding and project construction management.

Article 39 If a construction entity commences construction without submitting the evaluation materials on the impact on waterway navigability conditions according to law, the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency with review authority shall order it to stop construction, go through the formalities within a specified period, and impose a fine of not more than 30,000 yuan; if it fails to go through the formalities within the specified period and continues construction, the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency with review authority shall order it to restore the original state and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

If a construction entity commences construction after the submitted evaluation materials on the impact on waterway navigability conditions have not passed the review, the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency with review authority shall order it to stop construction and restore the original state, and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

If a project constructed in violation of the provisions on the evaluation of the impact on waterway navigability conditions causes a serious decline in waterway navigability conditions, the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency specified in the preceding two paragraphs shall order it to take remedial measures or demolish the project within a specified period; if remedial measures are not taken or the project is not demolished within the specified period, the competent department of transport or the waterway administration agency shall take remedial measures on its behalf or organize demolition according to law, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the construction entity.

Article 40 If the construction entity of a project related to a waterway violates the provisions of this Law by failing to promptly remove temporary facilities and their residues that affect waterway navigability conditions, the department responsible for waterway administration shall order it to remove them within a specified period and impose a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan; if they are not removed within the specified period, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the department responsible for waterway administration shall organize the removal according to law, with the costs borne by the construction entity.

Article 41 If a construction entity constructing bridges or other buildings on navigable waters fails to set up navigation aids and other facilities in accordance with regulations, the department responsible for waterway administration or the maritime administrative agency shall order it to make corrections and impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 42 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the department responsible for waterway administration to make corrections, and a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed on the entity, and a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan shall be imposed on the individual; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne according to law:(1) Setting up fishing gear or aquaculture facilities within a navigable channel;

(2) Dumping sand, gravel, soil, garbage, or other waste within a navigable channel or its protected area;

(3) Engaging in cargo handling, refueling, ship repair, fishing, or other activities within navigation structures, their approach channels, or vessel scheduling areas, thereby affecting the normal operation of navigation structures;

(4) Endangering the safety of navigation facility infrastructure;

(5) Other acts that jeopardize the navigational safety of a waterway.

Article 43: Illegal sand mining, such as mining in areas designated as sand mining prohibition zones within river channels, mining without a license, or mining beyond the approved scope or methods, shall be penalized in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Where, in violation of this Law, sand mining within a navigable channel or its protected area impairs navigational conditions, the waterway management authority shall order cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains, may impound or confiscate the illegal sand mining vessel, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if losses are caused, liability for compensation shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 44: Where environmental pollution, ecological damage, or other environmental violations occur in violation of legal provisions, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws.

Article 45: Where the competent transportation authority or other relevant departments fail to perform their duties as prescribed in this Law in accordance with the law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law.

Where the waterway management institution fails to perform its duties as prescribed in this Law in accordance with the law, its superior competent authority shall order rectification, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the law.

Article 46: Where a violation of this Law constitutes a public security violation, a public security penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 47: This Law shall not apply to specialized waterways entering military ports or fishing ports. Such specialized waterways shall be managed by the specialized departments.

Article 48: This Law shall take effect on March 1, 2015.
