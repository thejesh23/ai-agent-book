# Tourism Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 25, 2013

First Amendment on November 7, 2016, by the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China at the Twenty-Fourth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

Second Amendment on October 26, 2018, by the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the Sixth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to protect the legitimate rights and interests of tourists and tourism operators, regulate the order of the tourism market, protect and rationally utilize tourism resources, and promote the sustained and healthy development of the tourism industry.

**Article 2** This Law applies to tourism activities such as sightseeing, vacations, and leisure organized within the territory of the People's Republic of China and organized from within the territory to overseas destinations, as well as business activities providing related services for tourism activities.

**Article 3** The State shall develop the tourism industry, improve public tourism services, and protect the rights of tourists in tourism activities in accordance with the law.

**Article 4** The development of the tourism industry shall follow the principle of unifying social, economic, and ecological benefits. The State encourages various market entities to rationally utilize tourism resources in accordance with the law, provided that tourism resources are effectively protected. Tourist sites built using public resources shall reflect their public welfare nature.

**Article 5** The State advocates healthy, environmentally friendly, and civilized tourism methods, supports and encourages various social institutions to carry out public tourism promotion, and rewards units and individuals who have made outstanding contributions to the development of the tourism industry.

**Article 6** The State establishes and improves tourism service standards and market rules, prohibiting industry monopolies and regional monopolies. Tourism operators shall operate with integrity, engage in fair competition, assume social responsibilities, and provide tourists with safe, healthy, hygienic, and convenient tourism services.

**Article 7** The State Council shall establish a comprehensive tourism coordination mechanism to coordinate the development of the tourism industry.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the organization and leadership of tourism work, designate relevant departments or institutions to coordinate the development, supervision, and management of the tourism industry within their administrative regions.

**Article 8** Tourism industry organizations established according to law shall exercise self-discipline management.

## Chapter II Tourists

**Article 9** Tourists have the right to independently choose tourism products and services and the right to refuse forced transactions by tourism operators.

Tourists have the right to know the true situation of the tourism products and services they purchase.

Tourists have the right to demand that tourism operators provide products and services as agreed.

**Article 10** The personal dignity, ethnic customs, and religious beliefs of tourists shall be respected.

**Article 11** Tourists with disabilities, the elderly, minors, and others shall enjoy convenience and preferential treatment in tourism activities in accordance with laws, regulations, and relevant provisions.

**Article 12** Tourists have the right to request rescue and protection when their personal safety or property is in danger.

Tourists whose personal safety or property has been infringed upon have the right to obtain compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 13** During tourism activities, tourists shall abide by social public order and social ethics, respect local customs, cultural traditions, and religious beliefs, protect tourism resources, preserve the ecological environment, and comply with the norms of civilized tourism behavior.

**Article 14** During tourism activities or when resolving disputes, tourists shall not harm the legitimate rights and interests of local residents, interfere with the tourism activities of others, or damage the legitimate rights and interests of tourism operators and tourism practitioners.

**Article 15** When purchasing or accepting tourism services, tourists shall truthfully inform tourism operators of personal health information related to the tourism activities and comply with safety warning regulations during tourism activities.

Tourists shall cooperate with measures taken by the State to temporarily restrict tourism activities in response to major emergencies, as well as safety precautions and emergency response measures taken by relevant departments, institutions, or tourism operators.

Tourists who violate safety warning regulations or fail to cooperate with measures taken by the State to temporarily restrict tourism activities in response to major emergencies, safety precautions, and emergency response measures shall bear corresponding responsibilities in accordance with the law.

**Article 16** Outbound tourists shall not illegally stay abroad, and tourists traveling in groups abroad shall not leave the group or separate from the group without authorization.

Inbound tourists shall not illegally stay within the territory, and tourists traveling in groups inbound shall not leave the group or separate from the group without authorization.

## Chapter III Tourism Planning and Promotion

**Article 17** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the development of the tourism industry into national economic and social development plans.

The State Council, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, as well as the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties with rich tourism resources, shall organize the formulation of tourism development plans in accordance with the requirements of national economic and social development plans. When utilizing tourism resources that cross administrative regions and are suitable for overall utilization, the superior people's government shall organize the formulation of a unified tourism development plan, or the relevant local people's governments shall negotiate to formulate one.

**Article 18** Tourism development plans shall include the overall requirements and development goals of the tourism industry, requirements and measures for the protection and utilization of tourism resources, as well as requirements and promotion measures for tourism product development, improvement of tourism service quality, tourism culture construction, tourism image promotion, and construction of tourism infrastructure and public service facilities.

Based on the tourism development plan, local people's governments at or above the county level may formulate special plans for the development and utilization of key tourism resources, specifying special requirements for tourism projects, facilities, and service functions in specific areas.

**Article 19** Tourism development plans shall be coordinated with overall land use plans, urban and rural plans, environmental protection plans, and plans for the protection and utilization of natural resources and cultural resources such as cultural relics.

**Article 20** When formulating overall land use plans and urban and rural plans, people's governments at all levels shall fully consider the spatial layout and construction land requirements of relevant tourism projects and facilities. When planning and constructing infrastructure and public service facilities such as transportation, communications, water supply, power supply, and environmental protection, the needs of tourism development shall also be taken into account.

**Article 21** The tourism utilization of natural resources and cultural resources such as cultural relics must strictly comply with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, meet the requirements of resource and ecological protection and cultural relic safety, respect and maintain local traditional culture and customs, maintain the regional integrity, cultural representativeness, and geographical specificity of the resources, and consider the needs of military facility protection. Relevant competent departments shall strengthen supervision and inspection of resource protection and tourism utilization.

**Article 22** People's governments at all levels shall organize evaluations of the implementation of tourism development plans formulated by their respective governments and make the results public.

**Article 23** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate and implement industrial policies conducive to the sustained and healthy development of the tourism industry, promote the construction of a tourism and leisure system, take measures to promote regional tourism cooperation, encourage the development of cross-regional tourism routes and products, promote the integration of tourism with fields such as industry, agriculture, commerce, culture, health, sports, science, and education, and support the development of the tourism industry in ethnic minority areas, old revolutionary base areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

**Article 24** The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall allocate funds according to the actual situation to strengthen tourism infrastructure construction, public tourism services, and tourism image promotion.

**Article 25** The State formulates and implements a tourism image promotion strategy. The competent tourism department of the State Council shall coordinate the organization of the overseas promotion of the national tourism image, establish tourism image promotion institutions and networks, and carry out international tourism cooperation and exchanges.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall coordinate the organization of local tourism image promotion work.

**Article 26** The competent tourism department of the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish public tourism information and consultation platforms as needed, providing tourists with necessary information and consultation services on scenic spots, routes, transportation, weather, accommodation, safety, medical first aid, etc., free of charge. Relevant departments of the people's governments of cities divided into districts and counties shall set up tourism consultation centers at transportation hubs, commercial centers, and places where tourists gather as needed, and set up tourism directional signs at scenic spots and on roads leading to major scenic spots.

People's governments of cities divided into districts and counties with rich tourism resources may, based on local actual conditions, establish dedicated tourism passenger transport lines or tourist transfer stations to provide services for tourists traveling within and around the city.

**Article 27** The State encourages and supports the development of tourism vocational education and training to improve the quality of tourism practitioners.

## Chapter IV Tourism Operations

**Article 28** To establish a travel agency, solicit, organize, and receive tourists, and provide them with tourism services, the following conditions must be met, permission from the competent tourism department must be obtained, and industrial and commercial registration must be completed in accordance with the law:

(1) Having a fixed place of business;
(2) Having necessary business facilities;
(3) Having a registered capital that meets the requirements;
(4) Having necessary management personnel and tour guides;
(5) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 29** Travel agencies may operate the following businesses:

(1) Domestic tourism;
(2) Outbound tourism;
(3) Border tourism;
(4) Inbound tourism;
(5) Other tourism businesses.

Travel agencies operating the businesses specified in items (2) and (3) of the preceding paragraph shall obtain corresponding business operation permits, with specific conditions to be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 30** Travel agencies shall not rent, lend, or illegally transfer their travel agency business operation permits in any other form.

**Article 31** Travel agencies shall pay a tourism service quality deposit in accordance with regulations, to be used for compensating tourists for damages to their rights and interests and for advancing emergency rescue costs when tourists' personal safety is in danger.
**Article 32** Information published by travel agencies to solicit and organize tourists must be true and accurate, and shall not involve false advertising or mislead tourists.

**Article 33** When organizing and receiving tourists, travel agencies and their practitioners shall not arrange visits or participation in projects or activities that violate Chinese laws, regulations, or social ethics.

**Article 34** When organizing tourism activities, travel agencies shall order products and services from qualified suppliers.

**Article 35** Travel agencies shall not organize tourism activities at unreasonably low prices to deceive tourists and obtain improper benefits such as kickbacks through arranging shopping or additional paid tourism projects.

When organizing and receiving tourists, travel agencies shall not designate specific shopping places or arrange additional paid tourism projects, except when mutually agreed upon by both parties or requested by the tourist, and provided it does not affect the itinerary of other tourists.

If the circumstances specified in the preceding two paragraphs occur, tourists have the right, within thirty days after the end of the tourism itinerary, to request the travel agency to handle the return of goods and advance the refund of the purchase price, or to refund the fees for the additional paid tourism projects.

**Article 36** When organizing group outbound tourism or organizing/receiving group inbound tourism, travel agencies shall arrange for a tour leader or tour guide to accompany the group throughout the entire trip in accordance with regulations.

**Article 37** Persons who have passed the tour guide qualification examination and have signed a labor contract with a travel agency or are registered with a relevant tourism industry organization may apply for a tour guide certificate.

**Article 38** Travel agencies shall sign labor contracts with the tour guides they employ in accordance with the law, pay labor remuneration, and pay social insurance premiums.

If a travel agency temporarily employs a tour guide to provide services to tourists, it shall pay the tour guide in full the tour guide service fees specified in Article 60, paragraph 3 of this Law.

When a travel agency arranges a tour guide to provide services for group tours, it shall not require the tour guide to advance funds or charge any fees from the tour guide.

**Article 39** To engage in tour leader business, one must obtain a tour guide certificate, have the corresponding academic qualifications, language ability, and tourism work experience, and sign a labor contract with the travel agency that has obtained a permit for outbound tourism business and assigns the tour leader duties.

**Article 40** Tour guides and tour leaders must accept assignments from travel agencies to provide services to tourists and shall not solicit tour guide or tour leader business privately.

**Article 41** When engaging in business activities, tour guides and tour leaders shall wear their tour guide certificates, abide by professional ethics, respect the customs and religious beliefs of tourists, inform and explain the norms of civilized tourism behavior to tourists, guide tourists to travel healthily and civilly, and dissuade tourists from behaviors that violate social ethics.

Tour guides and tour leaders shall strictly implement the tourism itinerary arrangements, shall not change the tourism itinerary or suspend service activities without authorization, shall not solicit tips from tourists, and shall not induce, deceive, force, or covertly force tourists to shop or participate in additional paid tourism projects.

**Article 42** The opening of a scenic spot shall meet the following conditions and shall seek the opinions of the competent tourism department:

(1) Having necessary supporting tourism services and auxiliary facilities;
(2) Having necessary safety facilities and systems, having passed safety risk assessments, and meeting safety conditions;
(3) Having necessary environmental protection facilities and ecological protection measures;
(4) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 43** For scenic spots built using public resources, the ticket prices and fees for additional charge items such as internal tour venues and transportation shall be subject to government pricing or government-guided pricing, with strict control over price increases. If fees are to be charged or prices increased, a hearing shall be held to solicit opinions from tourists, operators, and relevant parties, and to demonstrate the necessity and feasibility.

Scenic spots built using public resources shall not increase prices covertly by adding additional charge items; if the investment cost of additional charge items has been recovered, the price shall be correspondingly reduced or the fee cancelled.

Public urban parks, museums, memorial halls, etc., except for key cultural relic protection units and units collecting precious cultural relics, shall gradually open to the public free of charge.

**Article 44** Scenic spots shall prominently display ticket prices, prices of additional charge items, and group ticket prices. If a scenic spot raises ticket prices, it shall announce the increase six months in advance.

If tickets for different scenic spots or tickets for different tour venues within the same scenic spot are sold together, the combined price shall not exceed the sum of the individual ticket prices, and tourists have the right to choose to purchase individual tickets.

If core tour projects within a scenic spot are suspended for tourists or services are stopped for any reason, this shall be announced and the fees shall be correspondingly reduced.

**Article 45** Scenic spots shall not receive tourists exceeding the maximum carrying capacity verified by the competent department of the scenic spot. Scenic spots shall announce the maximum carrying capacity verified by the competent department, formulate and implement tourist flow control plans, and may adopt methods such as ticket reservation to control the number of tourists received.

When the number of tourists may reach the maximum carrying capacity, the scenic spot shall announce this in advance and simultaneously report to the local people's government. The scenic spot and the local people's government shall promptly take measures such as guidance and diversion.

**Article 46** Urban and rural residents who engage in tourism operations using their own houses or other conditions in accordance with the law shall have their management measures formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

**Article 47** Operators of high-risk tourism projects such as high-altitude, high-speed, water, diving, and adventure activities shall obtain business operation permits in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 48** Entities operating travel agency businesses through the internet shall obtain a travel agency business operation permit in accordance with the law and prominently display their business operation permit information on the main page of their website.

Websites publishing tourism business information shall ensure the information is true and accurate.

**Article 49** Operators providing services such as transportation, accommodation, catering, and entertainment to tourists shall meet the requirements stipulated by laws and regulations and fulfill their obligations in accordance with the contract.

**Article 50** Tourism operators shall ensure that the goods and services they provide meet the requirements for ensuring personal and property safety.

If a tourism operator has obtained a relevant quality and service standard rating, its facilities and services shall not be lower than the corresponding standard; if it has not obtained a quality and service standard rating, it shall not use the titles and logos of the relevant quality and service standard.

**Article 51** Tourism operators shall not give or accept bribes when selling or purchasing goods or services.

**Article 52** Tourism operators shall keep confidential the personal information of tourists that they become aware of during their business activities.

**Article 53** Operators engaged in road tourism passenger transport shall comply with various road passenger transport safety management systems, prominently display the special road tourism passenger transport logo on the vehicle, and prominently display information about the operator and driver, the supervision telephone number of the road transport management institution, etc., inside the vehicle.

**Article 54** If scenic spot or accommodation operators entrust part of their business projects or premises to others for operations such as accommodation, catering, shopping, sightseeing, entertainment, or tourism transportation, they shall bear joint and several liability for damages caused to tourists by the actual operator's business conduct.

**Article 55** When organizing or receiving inbound or outbound tourism, if a tourism operator discovers that a tourist is engaging in illegal activities or is in a situation specified in Article 16 of this Law, it shall promptly report to the public security organ, the competent tourism department, or a Chinese overseas institution.

**Article 56** The State implements a liability insurance system for operators such as travel agencies, accommodation, tourism transportation, and the high-risk tourism projects specified in Article 47 of this Law, based on the risk level of tourism activities.

## Chapter V Tourism Service Contracts

**Article 57** When organizing and arranging tourism activities, travel agencies shall enter into contracts with tourists.

**Article 58** Package tour contracts shall be in written form and include the following contents:

(1) Basic information of the travel agency and the tourist;
(2) Tourism itinerary arrangements;
(3) The minimum number of tourists required for the tour group to form;
(4) Arrangements and standards for tourism services such as transportation, accommodation, and catering;
(5) Specific content and time for projects such as sightseeing and entertainment;
(6) Arrangements for free time;
(7) Tourism fees and the deadline and method for payment;
(8) Liability for breach of contract and methods for dispute resolution;
(9) Other matters stipulated by laws and regulations or agreed upon by both parties.

When concluding a package tour contract, the travel agency shall explain in detail to the tourist the contents specified in items (2) to (8) of the preceding paragraph.

**Article 59** Travel agencies shall provide tourists with a tourism itinerary sheet before the start of the tourism itinerary. The tourism itinerary sheet is an integral part of the package tour contract.

**Article 60** If a travel agency entrusts another travel agency to act as an agent to sell package tour products and conclude a package tour contract with a tourist, the basic information of the entrusting travel agency and the agent travel agency shall be stated in the package tour contract.

If a travel agency entrusts the reception business in a package tour contract to a local receiving travel agency for performance in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the basic information of the local receiving travel agency shall be stated in the package tour contract.

If a tour guide is arranged to provide services to tourists, the tour guide service fees shall be stated in the package tour contract.**Article 61** Travel agencies shall remind tourists participating in group tours to purchase personal accident insurance in accordance with regulations.

**Article 62** When concluding a package tour contract, a travel agency shall inform tourists of the following matters:

(1) Circumstances under which tourists are unsuitable for participating in tourism activities;

(2) Safety precautions during tourism activities;

(3) Information on circumstances where the travel agency may be exempted from or have its liability reduced according to law;

(4) Relevant laws, regulations, customs, and religious taboos of the tourist destination that tourists should be aware of, as well as activities that are inappropriate to participate in according to Chinese law;

(5) Other matters that should be informed as stipulated by laws and regulations.

During the performance of a package tour contract, if any of the matters specified in the preceding paragraph arise, the travel agency shall also inform the tourists.

**Article 63** If a travel agency solicits tourists to form a tour group but cannot dispatch the group because the agreed minimum number of participants is not reached, the organizing travel agency may rescind the contract. However, for domestic tours, tourists shall be notified at least seven days in advance; for outbound tours, tourists shall be notified at least thirty days in advance.

If the group cannot be dispatched due to failure to reach the agreed minimum number of participants, the organizing travel agency may, with the written consent of the tourists, entrust another travel agency to perform the contract. The organizing travel agency shall be liable to the tourists, and the entrusted travel agency shall be liable to the organizing travel agency. If the tourists do not consent, they may rescind the contract.

If the contract is rescinded due to failure to reach the agreed minimum number of participants for forming a group, the organizing travel agency shall refund all fees collected from the tourists.

**Article 64** Before the start of the tour itinerary, a tourist may transfer their rights and obligations under the package tour contract to a third party. The travel agency shall not refuse without justifiable reasons. Any additional costs incurred as a result shall be borne by the tourist and the third party.

**Article 65** If a tourist rescinds the contract before the end of the tour itinerary, the organizing travel agency shall refund the balance to the tourist after deducting necessary expenses.

**Article 66** A travel agency may rescind the contract under any of the following circumstances involving a tourist:

(1) The tourist suffers from an infectious disease or other illness that may endanger the health and safety of other tourists;

(2) The tourist carries items endangering public safety and refuses to hand them over to the relevant authorities for disposal;

(3) The tourist engages in illegal activities or activities contrary to social morality;

(4) The tourist engages in activities that seriously affect the rights and interests of other tourists and, despite warnings, cannot be stopped;

(5) Other circumstances stipulated by law.

If the contract is rescinded due to the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, the organizing travel agency shall refund the balance to the tourist after deducting necessary expenses; if losses are caused to the travel agency, the tourist shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 67** If the tour itinerary is affected by force majeure or events that cannot be avoided even after the travel agency or its agents have exercised reasonable care, the matter shall be handled according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the contract cannot be performed further, both the travel agency and the tourist may rescind the contract. If the contract cannot be fully performed, the travel agency may modify the contract within a reasonable scope after explaining the situation to the tourists; if the tourists do not agree to the modification, they may rescind the contract.

(2) If the contract is rescinded, the organizing travel agency shall refund the balance to the tourists after deducting fees already paid to the local ground operator or agent that are non-refundable; if the contract is modified, any additional costs incurred shall be borne by the tourists, and any reduced costs shall be refunded to the tourists.

(3) If the personal safety or property of tourists is endangered, the travel agency shall take appropriate safety measures. The expenses incurred thereby shall be shared between the travel agency and the tourists.

(4) If tourists are stranded as a result, the travel agency shall take appropriate accommodation measures. The additional accommodation and meal expenses incurred shall be borne by the tourists; the additional return travel expenses shall be shared between the travel agency and the tourists.

**Article 68** If the contract is rescinded during the tour itinerary, the travel agency shall assist the tourists in returning to the place of departure or a reasonable location designated by the tourists. If the contract is rescinded due to reasons attributable to the travel agency or its agents, the return travel expenses shall be borne by the travel agency.

**Article 69** A travel agency shall perform its obligations in accordance with the package tour contract and shall not unilaterally change the tour itinerary arrangements.

If, with the consent of the tourists, the travel agency entrusts the reception services under the package tour contract to another qualified local ground operator for performance, it shall enter into a written entrustment contract with the local ground operator, stipulating the rights and obligations of both parties, provide the local ground operator with a copy of the package tour contract concluded with the tourists, and pay the local ground operator no less than the cost of reception and services. The local ground operator shall provide services in accordance with the package tour contract and the entrustment contract.

**Article 70** If a travel agency fails to perform its obligations under the package tour contract or performs them not in conformity with the agreement, it shall bear liability for breach of contract, such as continuing performance, taking remedial measures, or compensating for losses, according to law; if personal injury or property damage is caused to tourists, it shall bear compensation liability according to law. If the travel agency has the conditions to perform but, upon the tourists' request, still refuses to perform the contract, resulting in serious consequences such as personal injury or stranding of tourists, the tourists may also demand that the travel agency pay compensation of not less than one time but not more than three times the tour cost.

If the package tour contract cannot be performed or cannot be performed as agreed, or if personal injury or property damage is caused to tourists, due to reasons attributable to the tourists themselves, the travel agency shall not be liable.

During the period when tourists are engaged in self-arranged activities, if the travel agency fails to fulfill its obligations of safety warnings and rescue assistance, it shall bear corresponding liability for personal injury or property damage to the tourists.

**Article 71** If a breach of contract occurs due to reasons attributable to the local ground operator or agent, the organizing travel agency shall bear the liability; after bearing the liability, the organizing travel agency may seek recourse from the local ground operator or agent.

If personal injury or property damage is caused to tourists due to reasons attributable to the local ground operator or agent, the tourists may demand compensation from the local ground operator or agent, or may demand compensation from the organizing travel agency; after bearing the liability, the organizing travel agency may seek recourse from the local ground operator or agent. However, if personal injury or property damage is caused to tourists due to reasons attributable to a public transport operator, the public transport operator shall bear compensation liability according to law, and the travel agency shall assist the tourists in claiming compensation from the public transport operator.

**Article 72** If a tourist, during tourism activities or in resolving disputes, damages the legitimate rights and interests of the travel agency, its agents, tourism employees, or other tourists, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

**Article 73** If a travel agency arranges a tour itinerary according to the specific requirements of a tourist and concludes a package tour contract with the tourist, and the tourist requests a change to the tour itinerary arrangement, any additional costs incurred shall be borne by the tourist, and any reduced costs shall be refunded to the tourist.

**Article 74** If a travel agency accepts a tourist's entrustment to arrange tourism services such as transportation, accommodation, meals, sightseeing, and entertainment on their behalf and collects agency fees, it shall handle the entrusted matters personally. If losses are caused to the tourist due to the travel agency's fault, the travel agency shall bear compensation liability.

If a travel agency accepts a tourist's entrustment to provide services such as tour itinerary design and tourism information consultation, it shall ensure the design is reasonable and feasible, and the information is timely and accurate.

**Article 75** Accommodation operators shall provide accommodation services to group tourists in accordance with the tourism service contract. If an accommodation operator fails to provide services in accordance with the tourism service contract, it shall provide accommodation services to the tourists that are not lower than the originally agreed standard; any additional costs incurred thereby shall be borne by the accommodation operator. However, if services cannot be provided due to force majeure or measures taken by the government for public interest reasons, the accommodation operator shall assist in arranging accommodation for the tourists.

## Chapter VI Tourism Safety

**Article 76** People's governments at or above the county level shall be uniformly responsible for tourism safety work. The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall perform their duties of tourism safety supervision and management in accordance with laws and regulations.

**Article 77** The state shall establish a system for issuing safety risk warnings for tourist destinations. The classification of safety risk warning levels for tourist destinations and the implementation procedures shall be formulated by the tourism administrative department of the State Council jointly with relevant departments.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall regard tourism safety as an important component of emergency monitoring and assessment.

**Article 78** People's governments at or above the county level shall, according to law, incorporate tourism emergency management into the government emergency management system, formulate emergency plans, and establish a mechanism for responding to tourism emergencies.

After the occurrence of an emergency, the local people's government and its relevant departments and institutions shall take measures to carry out rescue and assist tourists in returning to the place of departure or a reasonable location designated by the tourists.

**Article 79** Tourism operators shall strictly implement laws, regulations, national standards, and industry standards concerning safety production management and fire safety management, possess corresponding safety production conditions, and formulate tourist safety protection systems and emergency plans.

Tourism operators shall provide regular emergency rescue skills training to employees who directly provide services to tourists, conduct safety inspections, monitoring, and assessments of the products and services they offer, and take necessary measures to prevent hazards.

When organizing and receiving tourists such as the elderly, minors, and persons with disabilities, tourism operators shall take corresponding safety protection measures.

**Article 80** Tourism operators shall, in a clear manner, provide explanations or warnings to tourists in advance regarding the following matters during tourism activities:

(1) The correct methods for using relevant facilities and equipment;

(2) Necessary safety precautions and emergency measures;

(3) Business and service venues, facilities, and equipment not open to tourists;

(4) Groups unsuitable for participating in relevant activities;

(5) Other circumstances that may endanger the personal safety or property of tourists.

**Article 81** After the occurrence of an emergency or tourism safety accident, the tourism operator shall immediately take necessary rescue and handling measures, fulfill the obligation to report according to law, and make proper arrangements for the tourists.

**Article 82** When the personal safety or property of a tourist is in danger, the tourist has the right to request timely rescue from the tourism operator, the local government, and relevant institutions.

When a Chinese outbound tourist is in distress abroad, they have the right to request assistance and protection from the Chinese diplomatic mission or consular post in that location within the scope of its duties.

After receiving assistance from relevant organizations or institutions, tourists shall pay the expenses that should be borne by individuals.

## Chapter VII Tourism Supervision and Administration

**Article 83** The tourism administrative departments and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and regulations, supervise and administer the tourism market within their respective responsibilities.

People's governments at or above the county level shall organize the tourism administrative department, relevant competent departments, and law enforcement departments such as market regulation and transportation to conduct supervision and inspection of relevant tourism business activities.

**Article 84** When performing their supervision and administration duties, tourism administrative departments shall not charge fees from the entities under supervision in violation of laws and administrative regulations.

Tourism administrative departments and their staff shall not participate in any form of tourism business activities.

**Article 85** The tourism administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level have the right to conduct supervision and inspection of the following matters:

(1) Whether the operation of travel agency business and the provision of tour guide and tour leader services have obtained the necessary business or practice licenses;

(2) The business conduct of travel agencies;

(3) The service conduct of tourism practitioners such as tour guides and tour leaders;

(4) Other matters stipulated by laws and regulations.

When conducting supervision and inspection in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the tourism administrative department may inspect and copy contracts, bills, account books, and other materials suspected of being illegal.

**Article 86** When tourism administrative departments and relevant departments conduct supervision and inspection according to law, there shall be no fewer than two inspectors, and they shall present valid credentials. If there are fewer than two inspectors or they fail to present valid credentials, the inspected entities and individuals have the right to refuse.

Inspectors shall keep confidential the commercial secrets and personal information of the inspected entities that they become aware of during the supervision and inspection according to law.

**Article 87** Relevant entities and individuals shall cooperate with supervision and inspection conducted according to law, truthfully explain the situation, and provide documents and materials. They shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal information.

**Article 88** When the tourism administrative departments and relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level discover acts violating the provisions of this Law during the performance of their supervision and inspection duties or when handling reports or complaints, they shall handle the matter promptly according to law; for matters not within their scope of duties, they shall promptly notify in writing and transfer them to the relevant departments for investigation and punishment.

**Article 89** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a sharing mechanism for information on the investigation and punishment of tourism illegal acts. For illegal acts requiring joint investigation and punishment across departments and regions, supervision shall be exercised.

Tourism administrative departments and relevant departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, promptly disclose the status of supervision and inspection to the public.

**Article 90** Tourism industry organizations established according to law shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and their articles of association, formulate industry operation norms and service standards, conduct self-discipline management over the business conduct and service quality of their members, organize and carry out professional ethics education and business training, and improve the quality of practitioners.

## Chapter VIII Tourism Dispute Resolution

**Article 91** People's governments at or above the county level shall designate or establish a unified tourism complaint acceptance agency. Upon receiving a complaint, the acceptance agency shall handle it promptly or transfer it to the relevant department for handling, and inform the complainant.

**Article 92** Disputes between tourists and tourism operators may be resolved through the following channels:

(1) Negotiation between the parties;

(2) Applying for mediation to the Consumers' Association, the tourism complaint acceptance agency, or relevant mediation organizations;

(3) Submitting to an arbitration institution for arbitration in accordance with an arbitration agreement reached with the tourism operator;

(4) Filing a lawsuit with a people's court.

**Article 93** The Consumers' Association, tourism complaint acceptance agencies, and relevant mediation organizations shall, on a voluntary basis of both parties, mediate disputes between tourists and tourism operators according to law.

**Article 94** If a dispute arises between tourists and a tourism operator, and there are a large number of tourists on one side with common claims, they may elect representatives to participate in negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or litigation activities.

## Chapter IX Legal Liability

**Article 95** Anyone who operates travel agency business without a license in violation of the provisions of this Law shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department or the market supervision and administration department, have their illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the illegal gains are 100,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; the responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

If a travel agency, in violation of the provisions of this Law, operates the businesses specified in Items (2) and (3) of Paragraph 1 of Article 29 without a license, or leases or lends its travel agency business license, or illegally transfers the travel agency business license in other ways, in addition to the penalties specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification; if the circumstances are serious, its travel agency business license shall be revoked; the directly responsible supervisor shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan.

**Article 96** If a travel agency commits any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its travel agency business license revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to arrange for a tour leader or tour guide to accompany the entire outbound or inbound group tour as required;

(2) Arranging for personnel without a tour guide certificate to provide tour guide services or arranging for personnel without the qualifications of a tour leader to provide tour leader services;

(3) Failing to pay tour guide service fees to temporarily employed tour guides;

(4) Requiring tour guides to advance payments or collecting fees from tour guides.

**Article 97** If a travel agency commits any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department or relevant department, have its illegal gains confiscated, and be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the illegal gains are 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its travel agency business license revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan:

(1) Engaging in false advertising to mislead tourists;

(2) Ordering products and services from unqualified suppliers;

(3) Failing to purchase travel agency liability insurance as required.

**Article 98** If a travel agency violates the provisions of Article 35 of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, have its illegal gains confiscated, be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, and be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the illegal gains are 300,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, its travel agency business license shall be revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall have their illegal gains confiscated, be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan, and have their tour guide certificates temporarily seized or revoked.

**Article 99** If a travel agency fails to fulfill the reporting obligation stipulated in Article 55 of this Law, it shall be fined not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan by the tourism administrative department; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification or have its travel agency business license revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan, and have their tour guide certificates temporarily seized or revoked.

**Article 100** If a travel agency commits any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law, it shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, be fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan, and be ordered to suspend operations for rectification; if serious consequences such as tourist stranding occur, its travel agency business license shall be revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan, and have their tour guide certificates temporarily seized or revoked:

(1) Unilaterally changing the tour itinerary arrangement during the tour, seriously damaging the rights and interests of tourists;

(2) Refusing to perform the contract;

(3) Entrusting another travel agency to perform the package tour contract without obtaining the written consent of the tourists.

**Article 101** If a travel agency, in violation of the provisions of this Law, arranges for tourists to visit or participate in projects or activities that violate Chinese laws, regulations, or social morality, it shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, have its illegal gains confiscated, be ordered to suspend operations for rectification, and be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, its travel agency business license shall be revoked; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan, and have their tour guide certificates temporarily seized or revoked.

**Article 102** Anyone who engages in tour guide or tour leader activities without a tour guide certificate or without the qualifications of a tour leader, in violation of the provisions of this Law, shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, have their illegal gains confiscated, be fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan, and be publicly announced.

If a tour guide or tour leader, in violation of the provisions of this Law, privately solicits business, they shall be ordered to rectify by the tourism administrative department, have their illegal gains confiscated, be fined not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan, and have their tour guide certificate temporarily seized or revoked. If a tour guide or tour leader, in violation of this Law, solicits tips from tourists, the tourism administrative department shall order the return of such tips and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the tour guide certificate or tour leader certificate shall be temporarily seized or revoked.
Article 103: A tour guide or tour leader whose tour guide certificate has been revoked for violating this Law, or a relevant manager of a travel agency who has been penalized with the revocation of the travel agency's business license, shall not reapply for a tour guide certificate or engage in travel agency business within three years from the date of the penalty.

Article 104: If a tourism operator, in violation of this Law, gives or accepts bribes, the market supervision and administration department shall impose penalties in accordance with relevant laws and regulations; if the circumstances are serious, the competent tourism department shall revoke the travel agency's business license.
Article 105: If a scenic spot receives tourists without meeting the opening conditions stipulated in this Law, the scenic spot's competent department shall order it to suspend operations for rectification until it meets the opening conditions, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

If a scenic spot, when the number of tourists may reach its maximum carrying capacity, fails to make a public announcement as required by this Law, fails to report to the local people's government, fails to take timely measures such as guidance and diversion, or receives tourists exceeding the maximum carrying capacity, the scenic spot's competent department shall order it to make corrections; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order a suspension of operations for rectification for a period of one to six months.

Article 106: If a scenic spot, in violation of this Law, arbitrarily raises the price of tickets or additional charge items, or commits other illegal pricing acts, the relevant competent department shall impose penalties in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 107: If a tourism operator violates laws, regulations, or national or industry standards concerning safety production management and fire safety management, the relevant competent department shall impose penalties in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 108: For tourism operators and their employees who violate the provisions of this Law, the competent tourism departments and relevant departments shall record this in their credit files and make it public.
Article 109: If staff members of the tourism competent authorities and relevant departments, in the performance of their supervision and management duties, abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, and such conduct does not constitute a crime, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.
Article 110: Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 111: The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Tourism operator" refers to travel agencies, scenic spots, and operators providing services such as transportation, accommodation, catering, shopping, and entertainment to tourists.

(2) "Scenic spot" refers to a place or area with clear management boundaries that provides sightseeing services to tourists.

(3) "Package tour contract" refers to a contract in which a travel agency pre-arranges an itinerary and provides, or arranges for performance assistants to provide, two or more tourism services such as transportation, accommodation, catering, sightseeing, tour guide, or tour leader services, and the tourist pays a total price for the tourism expenses.

(4) "Organizing travel agency" refers to the travel agency that enters into a package tour contract with the tourist.

(5) "Local receiving travel agency" refers to the travel agency that accepts the entrustment of the organizing travel agency and receives tourists at the destination.

(6) "Performance assistant" refers to a legal person or natural person who has a contractual relationship with the travel agency, assists in fulfilling the obligations under the package tour contract, and actually provides the relevant services.

Article 112: This Law shall come into force on October 1, 2013.
