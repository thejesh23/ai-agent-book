# Mineral Resources Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on March 19, 1986

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Mineral Resources Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1996

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Certain Laws" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution of the People's Republic of China to develop the mining industry, strengthen the exploration, development, utilization, and protection of mineral resources, and ensure the current and long-term needs of socialist modernization.

Article 2 The exploration and extraction of mineral resources within the territory of the People's Republic of China and the sea areas under its jurisdiction must comply with this Law.

Article 3 Mineral resources are owned by the State, and the State Council exercises the State's ownership of mineral resources. The State's ownership of mineral resources, whether on the surface or underground, shall not change due to differences in the ownership or use rights of the land to which they are attached.

The State ensures the rational development and utilization of mineral resources. It prohibits any organization or individual from encroaching upon or destroying mineral resources by any means. People's governments at all levels must strengthen the protection of mineral resources.

For the exploration and extraction of mineral resources, applications must be made in accordance with the law, and exploration rights and mining rights must be obtained upon approval and registered; however, this does not apply to exploration conducted by mining enterprises that have already obtained mining rights in accordance with the law within their designated mining areas for their own production purposes. The State protects exploration rights and mining rights from infringement and ensures that the production and work order in mining areas and exploration operation areas are not affected or disrupted.

Entities engaged in the exploration and extraction of mineral resources must meet the prescribed qualification requirements.

Article 4 The State protects the lawful rights and interests of mining enterprises established in accordance with the law in extracting mineral resources.

State-owned mining enterprises are the mainstay of mineral resource extraction. The State ensures the consolidation and development of the State-owned mining economy.

Article 5 The State implements a system of paid acquisition of exploration rights and mining rights; however, the State may, depending on the circumstances, provide for reductions or exemptions of the fees for the paid acquisition of exploration rights and mining rights. Specific measures and implementation steps shall be formulated by the State Council.

For the extraction of mineral resources, resource taxes and resource compensation fees must be paid in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 6 Exploration rights and mining rights shall not be transferred except in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) The holder of an exploration right has the right to carry out prescribed exploration operations within the designated exploration area and has the priority right to obtain the mining right for mineral resources within that exploration area. After completing the prescribed minimum exploration investment, the exploration right holder may, upon approval in accordance with the law, transfer the exploration right to another person.

(2) A mining enterprise that has obtained a mining right may, due to merger, division, joint venture or cooperative operation with others, or due to the sale of enterprise assets or other changes in the ownership of enterprise assets requiring a change in the subject of the mining right, transfer the mining right to another person for extraction upon approval in accordance with the law.

The specific measures and implementation steps for the provisions in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated by the State Council.

The resale of exploration rights or mining rights for profit is prohibited.

Article 7 The State implements a policy of unified planning, rational layout, comprehensive exploration, rational extraction, and comprehensive utilization for the exploration and development of mineral resources.

Article 8 The State encourages scientific and technological research in the exploration and development of mineral resources, promotes advanced technology, and raises the scientific and technological level of mineral resource exploration and development.

Article 9 Units and individuals that achieve outstanding results in the exploration, development, and protection of mineral resources, as well as in scientific and technological research, shall be rewarded by people's governments at all levels.

Article 10 When extracting mineral resources in ethnic autonomous areas, the State shall take into account the interests of those areas, make arrangements conducive to their economic construction, and pay attention to the production and livelihood of the local ethnic minority populations.

The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas may, in accordance with legal provisions and the unified planning of the State, give priority to the rational development and utilization of mineral resources that can be developed by the locality.

Article 11 The competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction nationwide. The relevant competent departments under the State Council shall assist the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council in the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction.

The competent departments of geology and mineral resources of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction within their respective administrative regions. The relevant competent departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall assist the competent departments of geology and mineral resources at the same level in the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction.

## Chapter II Registration of Mineral Resource Exploration and Approval of Extraction

Article 12 The State implements a unified block registration management system for mineral resource exploration. The registration of mineral resource exploration shall be the responsibility of the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council; the registration of exploration for specific mineral types may be the responsibility of the relevant competent department authorized by the State Council. Measures for the administration of block registration of mineral resource exploration shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 13 The mineral reserve approval authority under the State Council or the mineral reserve approval authorities of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be responsible for reviewing and approving exploration reports used for mine construction design and shall reply to the submitting unit within the prescribed time limit. Exploration reports that have not been approved shall not be used as the basis for mine construction design.

Article 14 A unified management system shall be implemented for the archival data of mineral resource exploration results and statistical data on various mineral reserves, which shall be submitted or reported in accordance with State regulations.

Article 15 The establishment of a mining enterprise must meet the qualification requirements prescribed by the State and, in accordance with the law and relevant State regulations, the approving authority shall review its mining area scope, mine design or extraction plan, production technical conditions, safety measures, and environmental protection measures; only those that pass the review shall be approved.

Article 16 The extraction of the following mineral resources shall be approved by the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council, which shall issue a mining license:

(1) Mineral resources within State-planned mining areas and mining areas of significant value to the national economy;
(2) Mineral resources outside the areas specified in the preceding paragraph that are available for extraction and have a reserve scale of large or above;
(3) Specific mineral types designated by the State for protective extraction;
(4) Mineral resources in the territorial sea and other sea areas under China's jurisdiction;
(5) Other mineral resources as prescribed by the State Council.

The extraction of specific mineral types such as petroleum, natural gas, and radioactive minerals may be approved by the relevant competent department authorized by the State Council, which shall issue a mining license.

For the extraction of mineral resources other than those specified in paragraphs 1 and 2, where the reserve scale of the available mineral resources is medium, the competent department of geology and mineral resources of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall approve and issue a mining license.

Measures for the management of the extraction of mineral resources other than those specified in paragraphs 1, 2, and 3 shall be formulated by the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the law.

Where approvals and mining licenses are issued in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3 and 4, the competent departments of geology and mineral resources of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall summarize and report them to the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council for the record.

The classification standards for large and medium reserve scales of mineral resources shall be prescribed by the mineral reserve approval authority under the State Council.

Article 17 The State implements planned extraction for State-planned mining areas, mining areas of significant value to the national economy, and specific mineral types designated by the State for protective extraction; no unit or individual may extract them without the approval of the relevant competent department under the State Council.

Article 18 After the scope of State-planned mining areas, mining areas of significant value to the national economy, and mining areas of mining enterprises are legally delineated, the competent authority that delineated the scope shall notify the relevant county people's government to make a public announcement.

If a mining enterprise changes the scope of its mining area, it must report to the original approving authority for approval and report to the original authority that issued the mining license for reissuance of the mining license.

Article 19 Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to maintain normal order within the mining areas of State-owned mining enterprises and other mining enterprises within their administrative regions.

It is prohibited for any unit or individual to enter the legally established mining areas of State-owned mining enterprises or other mining enterprises to extract minerals.

Article 20 Without the consent of the relevant competent department authorized by the State Council, mineral resources shall not be extracted in the following areas:

(1) Within areas delineated for ports, airports, and national defense engineering facilities;
(2) Within a certain distance from important industrial areas, large-scale water conservancy engineering facilities, and urban municipal engineering facilities;
(3) Within a certain distance on both sides of railways and important highways;
(4) Within a certain distance on both sides of important rivers and dikes;
(5) Nature reserves designated by the State, important scenic spots, and locations of immovable historical relics and places of historic interest and scenic beauty under State key protection;
(6) Other areas where the extraction of mineral resources is prohibited by the State.

Article 21 To close a mine, a mine closure report must be submitted, along with data on related extraction projects, potential safety hazards, land reclamation and utilization, and environmental protection, and approval must be obtained in accordance with State regulations.

Article 22 When exploring or extracting mineral resources, if rare geological phenomena or cultural relics of significant scientific and cultural value are discovered, they shall be protected and promptly reported to the relevant departments.

## Chapter III Exploration of Mineral Resources

Article 23 Regional geological surveys shall be conducted in accordance with the unified planning of the State. Reports and maps of regional geological surveys shall be accepted in accordance with State regulations and provided to relevant departments for use.

Article 24 While completing the task of surveying major mineral types, mineral resource reconnaissance shall also conduct a preliminary comprehensive evaluation of the metallogenic geological conditions and industrial prospects of deposits, including associated or paragenetic minerals, within the work area.

Article 25 Deposit exploration must conduct a comprehensive evaluation of associated and paragenetic minerals of industrial value within the mining area and calculate their reserves. Exploration reports that do not include a comprehensive evaluation shall not be approved. However, this does not apply to deposit exploration projects for which the planning department under the State Council has otherwise provided.

Article 26 For the reconnaissance and exploration of easily damaged special non-metallic minerals, fluid minerals, flammable, explosive, and easily soluble minerals, and minerals containing radioactive elements, the reconnaissance and exploration methods prescribed by the relevant competent department of the people's government at or above the provincial level must be adopted, along with necessary technical equipment and safety measures.

Article 27 Original geological records and maps from mineral resource exploration, rock and mineral cores, test samples and other physical specimen data, and various exploration markers shall be protected and preserved in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 28 Deposit exploration reports and other valuable exploration data shall be used on a paid basis in accordance with State regulations.

## Chapter IV Extraction of Mineral Resources

Article 29 When extracting mineral resources, reasonable extraction sequences, extraction methods, and mineral processing techniques must be adopted. The extraction recovery rate, mining dilution rate, and mineral processing recovery rate of mining enterprises shall meet the design requirements.

Article 30 While extracting major minerals, associated and paragenetic minerals of industrial value shall be subject to unified planning, comprehensive extraction, and comprehensive utilization to prevent waste; for minerals that cannot be comprehensively extracted temporarily or must be extracted simultaneously but cannot be comprehensively utilized temporarily, as well as tailings containing useful components, effective protection measures shall be taken to prevent loss and damage.

Article 31 When extracting mineral resources, national labor safety and health regulations must be complied with, and the necessary conditions for ensuring safe production must be in place.

Article 32 When extracting mineral resources, relevant environmental protection laws must be complied with to prevent environmental pollution.

When extracting mineral resources, land shall be used economically. Where farmland, grassland, or forestland is damaged due to mining, the mining enterprise shall take measures such as reclamation and utilization, tree planting and grass sowing, or other utilization measures according to local conditions.

If the extraction of mineral resources causes losses to the production or livelihood of others, compensation shall be paid, and necessary remedial measures shall be taken.

Article 33 Before constructing railways, factories, reservoirs, oil pipelines, power transmission lines, and various large buildings or building complexes, the construction unit must consult with the competent department of geology and mineral resources of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government about the distribution and extraction status of mineral resources in the area where the proposed project is to be located. Without the approval of the department authorized by the State Council, important mineral deposits shall not be covered or occupied.

Article 34 For mineral products designated by the State Council for unified purchase by designated units, no other unit or individual may purchase them; extractors shall not sell them to non-designated units.

## Chapter V Collective Mining Enterprises and Individual Mining

Article 35 The State implements a policy of actively supporting, rationally planning, correctly guiding, and strengthening management for collective mining enterprises and individual mining, encourages collective mining enterprises to extract mineral resources within the scope designated by the State, and allows individuals to extract scattered and dispersed resources, sand, stone, and clay that can only be used as ordinary building materials, as well as small amounts of minerals for personal livelihood use.

Individuals shall not extract mineral resources with a reserve scale suitable for extraction by mining enterprises, specific mineral types designated by the State for protective extraction, and other mineral resources that the State prohibits individuals from extracting.

The State guides and assists collective mining enterprises and individual mining in continuously improving their technical level, resource utilization rate, and economic benefits.

The competent departments of geology and mineral resources, geological work units, and State-owned mining enterprises shall, in accordance with the principle of active support and mutual benefit on a paid basis, provide geological data and technical services to collective mining enterprises and individual mining.

Article 36 Existing collective mining enterprises within the mining areas of mining enterprises approved for establishment by the State Council or the relevant competent departments under the State Council shall be closed or moved to other designated locations for extraction, with reasonable compensation provided by the mine construction unit and proper arrangements made for the livelihood of the affected population; alternatively, joint operations may be carried out under the overall planning of the mining enterprise.

Article 37 Collective mining enterprises and individual mining shall improve their technical level and increase the recovery rate of mineral resources. Random digging and excessive extraction that destroy mineral resources are prohibited.

Collective mining enterprises must survey and produce comparison maps of above-ground and underground engineering.

Article 38 People's governments at or above the county level shall guide and assist collective mining enterprises and individual mining in carrying out technical transformation, improving management, and strengthening safety in production.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 39 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, extracts minerals without a mining license, enters a State-planned mining area or a mining area of significant value to the national economy to extract minerals without authorization, or extracts specific mineral types designated by the State for protective extraction without authorization, shall be ordered to stop extraction, compensate for losses, and have the extracted mineral products and illegal gains confiscated, and may also be fined; if the extraction is not stopped and mineral resources are destroyed, the directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If a unit or individual enters the legally established mining areas of State-owned mining enterprises or other mining enterprises to extract minerals, they shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 40 Anyone who extracts minerals beyond the approved mining area scope shall be ordered to withdraw to the approved mining area for extraction, compensate for losses, and have the mineral products extracted beyond the boundary and illegal gains confiscated, and may also be fined; if the extraction is not withdrawn to the approved mining area and mineral resources are destroyed, the mining license shall be revoked, and the directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 41 Anyone who steals or robs mineral products or other property of mining enterprises or exploration units, destroys mining or exploration facilities, or disrupts the production or work order of mining areas or exploration operation areas shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the circumstances are minor, punishment shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 42 Anyone who buys, sells, or transfers mineral resources in any other form shall have the illegal gains confiscated and be fined.

Anyone who, in violation of Article 6 of this Law, resells exploration rights or mining rights for profit shall have the exploration permit or mining license revoked, the illegal gains confiscated, and be fined.

Article 43 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, purchases or sells mineral products that are subject to unified State purchase shall have the mineral products and illegal gains confiscated, and may also be fined; if the circumstances are serious, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 44 Anyone who, in violation of this Law, uses destructive extraction methods to extract mineral resources shall be fined, and the mining license may be revoked; if serious damage is caused to mineral resources, the directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 45 The administrative penalties provided for in Articles 39, 40, and 42 of this Law shall be decided by the department responsible for geology and mineral resources management under the people's government at or above the county level within the authority prescribed by the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the State Council. The administrative penalties provided for in Article 43 shall be decided by the administrative department for industry and commerce under the people's government at or above the county level. The administrative penalties provided for in Article 44 shall be decided by the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government. The revocation of an exploration permit or mining license must be decided by the original issuing authority.

If administrative penalties should be imposed in accordance with Articles 39, 40, 42, and 44 but are not imposed, the competent department of geology and mineral resources of the higher-level people's government has the authority to order correction or impose the administrative penalty directly.

Article 46 If a party is dissatisfied with an administrative penalty decision, it may apply for reconsideration in accordance with the law or directly file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

If a party fails to apply for reconsideration or file a lawsuit within the prescribed time limit and also fails to comply with the penalty decision, the authority that made the penalty decision shall apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 47 If State functionaries responsible for the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction, or other relevant State functionaries, engage in favoritism and malpractice, abuse their power, or neglect their duties, and in violation of this Law approve the exploration and extraction of mineral resources and issue exploration permits or mining licenses, or fail to stop or punish illegal mining activities in accordance with the law, and if this constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed. The competent department of geology and mineral resources of the higher-level people's government has the authority to revoke exploration permits or mining licenses issued in violation of the law.

Article 48 Anyone who obstructs State functionaries responsible for the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction from performing their duties in accordance with the law by means of violence or threats shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; anyone who refuses or obstructs State functionaries responsible for the supervision and management of mineral resource exploration and extraction from performing their duties in accordance with the law without using violence or threats shall be punished by the public security organ in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 49 Disputes over mining area boundaries between mining enterprises shall be resolved through negotiation between the parties; if negotiation fails, the relevant local people's government at or above the county level shall handle the matter based on the legally verified mining area boundaries; for disputes over mining area boundaries that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the relevant people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall resolve them through negotiation; if negotiation fails, the State Council shall handle the matter.

## Chapter VII Supplementary ProvisionsArticle 50 Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for foreign investment in the exploration and mining of mineral resources, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 51 Prior to the implementation of this Law, those who have engaged in the mining of mineral resources without completing the approval procedures, without having the mining area delineated, or without obtaining a mining license shall apply to go through the relevant procedures retroactively in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 52 The detailed rules for the implementation of this Law shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 53 This Law shall come into force on October 1, 1986.
