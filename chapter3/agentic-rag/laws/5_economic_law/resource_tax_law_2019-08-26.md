# Resource Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 26, 2019

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 Units and individuals that develop taxable resources within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be taxpayers of resource tax and shall pay resource tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

The specific scope of taxable resources shall be determined by the "Resource Tax Items and Tax Rates Table" (hereinafter referred to as the "Tax Items and Rates Table") attached to this Law.

Article 2 The tax items and tax rates of resource tax shall be implemented in accordance with the "Tax Items and Rates Table."

Where the "Tax Items and Rates Table" provides for a range of tax rates, the specific applicable tax rate shall be proposed by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, taking into comprehensive consideration the grade of the taxable resource, mining conditions, and the impact on the ecological environment, within the tax rate range specified in the "Tax Items and Rates Table," submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation. Where the "Tax Items and Rates Table" specifies the taxable object as raw ore or beneficiated ore, specific applicable tax rates shall be determined separately.

Article 3 Resource tax shall be levied on an ad valorem basis or on a specific duty basis in accordance with the "Tax Items and Rates Table."

Where the "Tax Items and Rates Table" provides that a choice may be made between ad valorem and specific duty levy, the specific levy method shall be proposed by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

Where the tax is levied on an ad valorem basis, the tax payable shall be calculated by multiplying the sales amount of the taxable resource product (hereinafter referred to as the taxable product) by the specific applicable tax rate. Where the tax is levied on a specific duty basis, the tax payable shall be calculated by multiplying the sales volume of the taxable product by the specific applicable tax rate.

Where the taxable product is a mineral product, it includes both raw ore and beneficiated ore products.

Article 4 Where a taxpayer mines or produces taxable products under different tax items, the sales amount or sales volume of taxable products under different tax items shall be accounted for separately; if they are not accounted for separately, or the sales amount or sales volume of taxable products under different tax items cannot be accurately provided, the higher tax rate shall apply.

Article 5 Where a taxpayer mines or produces taxable products for self-use, resource tax shall be paid in accordance with the provisions of this Law; however, if they are self-used for the continuous production of taxable products, no resource tax shall be paid.

Article 6 Resource tax shall be exempted under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Crude oil and natural gas used for heating during the extraction of crude oil and during transportation within the oilfield;

(2) Coalbed (seam) gas extracted by coal mining enterprises for safety production needs.

Resource tax shall be reduced under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Crude oil and natural gas extracted from low-abundance oil and gas fields, with a 20% reduction in resource tax;

(2) High-sulfur natural gas, tertiary oil recovery, and crude oil and natural gas extracted from deep-water oil and gas fields, with a 30% reduction in resource tax;

(3) Heavy oil and high-pour-point oil, with a 40% reduction in resource tax;

(4) Mineral products extracted from mines in the depletion period, with a 30% reduction in resource tax.

Based on the needs of national economic and social development, the State Council may provide for exemptions or reductions of resource tax for circumstances that are conducive to promoting resource conservation and intensive use, environmental protection, etc., and shall report to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

Article 7 Under any of the following circumstances, provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may decide to exempt or reduce resource tax:

(1) Where a taxpayer suffers significant losses due to accidents, natural disasters, or other reasons during the process of mining or producing taxable products;

(2) Where a taxpayer mines associated ore, low-grade ore, or tailings.

The specific measures for exempting or reducing resource tax as provided in the preceding paragraph shall be proposed by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

Article 8 A taxpayer's tax-exempt or tax-reduced items shall be accounted for separately in terms of sales amount or sales volume; if they are not accounted for separately, or the sales amount or sales volume cannot be accurately provided, no tax exemption or reduction shall be granted.

Article 9 Resource tax shall be levied and managed by the tax authorities in accordance with this Law and the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection."

Tax authorities and relevant departments such as natural resources authorities shall establish a working coordination mechanism to strengthen the collection and management of resource tax.

Article 10 Where a taxpayer sells taxable products, the tax liability arises on the date of receipt of the sales proceeds or the date of obtaining a voucher for the right to collect the sales proceeds; where taxable products are used for self-use, the tax liability arises on the date of transfer of the taxable products.

Article 11 A taxpayer shall declare and pay resource tax to the tax authority at the place where the taxable products are mined or produced.

Article 12 Resource tax shall be declared and paid on a monthly or quarterly basis; if it cannot be calculated and paid on a fixed period basis, it may be declared and paid on a per-occurrence basis.

Where a taxpayer declares and pays tax on a monthly or quarterly basis, the tax return shall be filed and the tax paid to the tax authority within fifteen days from the end of the month or quarter; where tax is declared and paid on a per-occurrence basis, the tax return shall be filed and the tax paid to the tax authority within fifteen days from the date of the tax liability.

Article 13 Taxpayers, tax authorities, and their staff who violate the provisions of this Law shall be held legally liable in accordance with the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection" and relevant laws and regulations.

Article 14 The State Council, based on the needs of national economic and social development and in accordance with the principles of this Law, shall pilot the collection of a water resource tax from units and individuals that extract surface water or groundwater. Where a water resource tax is collected, the collection of water resource fees shall cease.

The water resource tax shall apply differential tax rates based on local water resource conditions, types of water extraction and use, and economic development.

The specific measures for implementing the water resource tax pilot shall be formulated by the State Council and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

The State Council shall report to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on the pilot collection of water resource tax within five years from the effective date of this Law and shall promptly propose amendments to the law.

Article 15 Enterprises engaged in Sino-foreign cooperative exploitation of onshore and offshore petroleum resources shall pay resource tax in accordance with the law.

For contracts for Sino-foreign cooperative exploitation of onshore and offshore petroleum resources lawfully concluded before November 1, 2011, during the validity period of such contracts, the enterprises shall continue to pay mining area usage fees in accordance with relevant state regulations and shall not pay resource tax; after the expiration of the contracts, resource tax shall be paid in accordance with the law.

Article 16 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) Low-abundance oil and gas fields include onshore low-abundance oil fields, onshore low-abundance gas fields, offshore low-abundance oil fields, and offshore low-abundance gas fields. An onshore low-abundance oil field refers to an oil field with a recoverable crude oil reserve abundance of less than 250,000 cubic meters per square kilometer; an onshore low-abundance gas field refers to a gas field with a recoverable natural gas reserve abundance of less than 250 million cubic meters per square kilometer; an offshore low-abundance oil field refers to an oil field with a recoverable crude oil reserve abundance of less than 600,000 cubic meters per square kilometer; an offshore low-abundance gas field refers to a gas field with a recoverable natural gas reserve abundance of less than 600 million cubic meters per square kilometer.

(2) High-sulfur natural gas refers to natural gas with a hydrogen sulfide content of 30 grams or more per cubic meter.

(3) Tertiary oil recovery refers to oil recovery carried out after secondary oil recovery using methods such as polymer flooding, compound flooding, foam flooding, alternating gas-water flooding, carbon dioxide flooding, and microbial flooding.

(4) Deep-water oil and gas fields refer to oil and gas fields with a water depth exceeding 300 meters.

(5) Heavy oil refers to crude oil with a formation oil viscosity greater than or equal to 50 millipascal-seconds or a crude oil density greater than or equal to 0.92 grams per cubic centimeter.

(6) High-pour-point oil refers to crude oil with a pour point higher than 40 degrees Celsius.

(7) Mines in the depletion period refer to mines with a designed mining life exceeding 15 years, where the remaining recoverable reserves have fallen below 20% of the originally designed recoverable reserves, or the remaining mining life does not exceed 5 years. Mines in the depletion period are determined on a per-mine basis under the mining enterprise.

Article 17 This Law shall come into effect on September 1, 2020. The "Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Resource Tax" promulgated by the State Council on December 25, 1993, shall be simultaneously repealed.
