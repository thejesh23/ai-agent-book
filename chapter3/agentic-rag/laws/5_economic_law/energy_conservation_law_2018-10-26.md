# Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on November 1, 1997

Revised at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on October 28, 2007

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of promoting energy conservation across society, improving energy utilization efficiency, protecting and improving the environment, and facilitating comprehensive, coordinated, and sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "energy" means coal, petroleum, natural gas, biomass, electric power, heat, and other resources from which useful energy is derived directly or through processing or conversion.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "energy conservation" (hereinafter referred to as "energy conservation") means strengthening energy management, adopting measures that are technically feasible, economically reasonable, and acceptable to the environment and society, reducing consumption, losses, and pollutant emissions at all stages from energy production to consumption, curbing waste, and utilizing energy efficiently and rationally.

Article 4 Resource conservation is a basic national policy of the State. The State implements an energy development strategy that emphasizes both conservation and development, with conservation given priority.

Article 5 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate energy conservation work into national economic and social development plans and annual plans, and shall organize the formulation and implementation of medium- and long-term special plans for energy conservation and annual energy conservation plans.

The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall report on energy conservation work to the people's congresses or their standing committees at the corresponding level each year.

Article 6 The State implements an energy conservation target responsibility system and an energy conservation assessment and evaluation system, incorporating the achievement of energy conservation targets into the assessment and evaluation of local people's governments and their responsible persons.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall report to the State Council each year on the fulfillment of their energy conservation target responsibilities.

Article 7 The State implements industrial policies conducive to energy conservation and environmental protection, restricts the development of industries with high energy consumption and high pollution, and promotes energy-saving and environmentally friendly industries.

The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strengthen energy conservation work, rationally adjust the industrial structure, enterprise structure, product structure, and energy consumption structure, promote enterprises to reduce energy consumption per unit of output value and per unit of product, phase out outdated production capacity, improve the development, processing, conversion, transmission, storage, and supply of energy, and enhance energy utilization efficiency.

The State encourages and supports the development and utilization of new energy and renewable energy.

Article 8 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research, development, demonstration, and promotion of energy conservation, and promotes innovation and progress in energy conservation technology.

The State conducts energy conservation publicity and education, incorporates energy conservation knowledge into the national education and training system, popularizes energy conservation science, enhances the energy conservation awareness of the entire population, and advocates a conservation-oriented consumption pattern.

Article 9 All entities and individuals shall fulfill their energy conservation obligations in accordance with the law and have the right to report acts of energy waste.

The news media shall publicize energy conservation laws, regulations, and policies, and play a role in public oversight.

Article 10 The department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and management of energy conservation nationwide. The relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for energy conservation supervision and management within their respective functions and duties, and shall accept guidance from the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council.

The departments in charge of energy conservation under local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for energy conservation supervision and management within their respective administrative regions. The relevant departments under local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for energy conservation supervision and management within their respective functions and duties, and shall accept guidance from the department in charge of energy conservation at the same level.

## Chapter II Energy Conservation Management

Article 11 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen leadership over energy conservation work, and shall deploy, coordinate, supervise, inspect, and promote energy conservation work.

Article 12 The departments in charge of energy conservation and relevant departments under people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions and duties, strengthen supervision and inspection of the implementation of energy conservation laws, regulations, and standards, and shall investigate and deal with illegal energy use activities in accordance with the law.

No fees shall be charged to the subjects of supervision and management in the performance of energy conservation supervision and management duties.

Article 13 The standardization administrative department under the State Council and the relevant departments under the State Council shall, in accordance with the law, organize the formulation and timely revision of relevant national and industrial standards for energy conservation, and establish and improve the energy conservation standard system.

The standardization administrative department under the State Council, together with the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council and the relevant departments under the State Council, shall formulate mandatory energy efficiency standards for energy-using products and equipment, and mandatory energy consumption quota standards per unit of product for products with high energy consumption in the production process.

The State encourages enterprises to formulate enterprise energy conservation standards that are stricter than national or industrial standards.

Where provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government formulate local energy conservation standards that are stricter than mandatory national or industrial standards, such standards shall be submitted by the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government to the State Council for approval, unless otherwise provided in this Law.

Article 14 National and industrial standards for building energy conservation shall be formulated by the construction administrative department under the State Council and promulgated in accordance with legal procedures.

The construction administrative departments under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local conditions, formulate local building energy conservation standards that are stricter than national or industrial standards, and shall report them to the standardization administrative department under the State Council and the construction administrative department under the State Council for recordation.

Article 15 The State implements an energy conservation assessment and review system for fixed asset investment projects. For projects that do not meet mandatory energy conservation standards, the construction entity shall not commence construction; for projects that have already been constructed, they shall not be put into production or use. For government-invested projects that do not meet mandatory energy conservation standards, the authority responsible for project approval shall not approve construction in accordance with the law. Specific measures shall be formulated by the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 16 The State implements a phase-out system for outdated energy-using products, equipment, and production processes with excessively high energy consumption. The catalog and implementation measures for phased-out energy-using products, equipment, and production processes shall be formulated and published by the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Production units of products with high energy consumption in the production process shall implement the energy consumption quota standards per unit of product. For production units that exceed the energy consumption quota standards per unit of product, the department in charge of energy conservation shall, within the authority prescribed by the State Council, order them to rectify within a specified period.

For special equipment with high energy consumption, energy conservation review and supervision shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Article 17 It is prohibited to produce, import, or sell energy-using products or equipment that have been explicitly phased out by the State or that do not meet mandatory energy efficiency standards; it is prohibited to use energy-using equipment or production processes that have been explicitly phased out by the State.

Article 18 The State implements an energy efficiency labeling management system for energy-using products such as household appliances that are widely used and consume large amounts of energy. The catalog and implementation measures for products subject to energy efficiency labeling management shall be formulated and published by the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council together with the market supervision and management department under the State Council.

Article 19 Producers and importers shall affix energy efficiency labels to energy-using products included in the national catalog of products subject to energy efficiency labeling management, provide explanations on the product packaging or in the instruction manual, and report to the institution jointly authorized by the market supervision and management department under the State Council and the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council for recordation in accordance with regulations.

Producers and importers shall be responsible for the accuracy of the energy efficiency labels and related information they affix. It is prohibited to sell products that should have been labeled with energy efficiency labels but are not.

It is prohibited to forge or fraudulently use energy efficiency labels, or to use energy efficiency labels for false advertising.

Article 20 Producers and sellers of energy-using products may, on a voluntary basis, apply for energy conservation product certification from institutions recognized by the certification and accreditation supervision and management department under the State Council for engaging in energy conservation product certification, in accordance with the State regulations on energy conservation product certification. Upon passing the certification, they may obtain an energy conservation product certification certificate and may use the energy conservation product certification mark on the energy-using products or their packaging.

It is prohibited to use forged or fraudulently used energy conservation product certification marks.

Article 21 The statistical departments under people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with the relevant departments at the same level, establish and improve the energy statistics system, improve the energy statistics indicator system, improve and standardize energy statistics methods, and ensure the authenticity and completeness of energy statistics data.

The statistical department under the State Council, together with the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council, shall regularly publish to the public information on energy consumption and energy conservation in each province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, and major energy-consuming industries.

Article 22 The State encourages the development of energy conservation service institutions and supports them in providing services such as energy conservation consulting, design, assessment, testing, auditing, and certification.

The State supports energy conservation service institutions in conducting energy conservation knowledge publicity and energy conservation technology training, and providing energy conservation information, energy conservation demonstrations, and other public welfare energy conservation services.

Article 23 The State encourages industry associations to play a role in industry energy conservation planning, the formulation and implementation of energy conservation standards, the promotion of energy conservation technologies, energy consumption statistics, energy conservation publicity and training, and information consulting.

## Chapter III Rational Use and Conservation of Energy

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 24 Energy-using units shall, in accordance with the principle of rational energy use, strengthen energy conservation management, formulate and implement energy conservation plans and energy conservation technical measures, and reduce energy consumption.

Article 25 Energy-using units shall establish an energy conservation target responsibility system and provide rewards to collectives and individuals that have achieved results in energy conservation work.

Article 26 Energy-using units shall regularly conduct energy conservation education and job-specific energy conservation training.

Article 27 Energy-using units shall strengthen energy metering management, and shall equip and use energy metering instruments that have been verified as qualified in accordance with the law and regulations.

Energy-using units shall establish an energy consumption statistics and energy utilization status analysis system, implement classified metering and statistics for the consumption of various types of energy, and ensure the authenticity and completeness of energy consumption statistics data.

Article 28 Energy production and operation units shall not provide energy to their own employees free of charge. No unit shall implement a flat-rate fee system for energy consumption.

### Section 2 Industrial Energy Conservation

Article 29 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall promote the optimal development, utilization, and rational allocation of energy resources, promote industrial restructuring conducive to energy conservation, and optimize the energy consumption structure and enterprise layout.

Article 30 The department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, shall formulate energy conservation technology policies for major energy-consuming industries such as electric power, iron and steel, non-ferrous metals, building materials, petroleum processing, chemicals, and coal, and promote energy conservation technological transformation in enterprises.

Article 31 The State encourages industrial enterprises to adopt efficient and energy-saving equipment such as electric motors, boilers, kilns, fans, and pumps, and to adopt technologies such as combined heat and power, waste heat and pressure utilization, clean coal, and advanced energy use monitoring and control.

Article 32 Grid enterprises shall, in accordance with the regulations on energy-saving power generation dispatch management formulated by the relevant departments under the State Council, arrange for clean, efficient, and compliant combined heat and power units, units utilizing waste heat and pressure for power generation, and other generating units that comply with the regulations on comprehensive resource utilization to be connected to and operate on the grid, and the on-grid electricity prices shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant State regulations.

Article 33 It is prohibited to construct new coal-fired generating units, oil-fired generating units, and coal-fired combined heat and power units that do not meet the State regulations.

### Section 3 Building Energy Conservation

Article 34 The construction administrative department under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and management of building energy conservation nationwide.

The construction administrative departments under local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and management of building energy conservation within their respective administrative regions.

The construction administrative departments under local people's governments at or above the county level, together with the departments in charge of energy conservation at the same level, shall formulate building energy conservation plans within their respective administrative regions. The building energy conservation plans shall include plans for the energy conservation renovation of existing buildings.

Article 35 The construction, design, construction, and supervision units of construction projects shall comply with building energy conservation standards.

For construction projects that do not meet building energy conservation standards, the construction administrative department shall not approve the commencement of construction; for those that have already commenced construction, it shall order the cessation of construction and rectification within a specified period; for those that have already been completed, they shall not be sold or used.

The construction administrative department shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the implementation of building energy conservation standards in ongoing construction projects.

Article 36 When selling houses, real estate development enterprises shall clearly inform buyers of information such as the energy conservation measures of the houses for sale and the warranty period of the thermal insulation works, and shall include this information in the house sales contract, quality guarantee, and user manual, and shall be responsible for its authenticity and accuracy.

Article 37 Public buildings that use air conditioning for heating and cooling shall implement an indoor temperature control system. Specific measures shall be formulated by the construction administrative department under the State Council.

Article 38 The State takes measures to implement a system of household-based metering and heat-based billing for buildings with central heating in a step-by-step manner. New buildings or existing buildings undergoing energy conservation renovation shall be equipped with heat metering devices, indoor temperature control devices, and heating system control devices in accordance with regulations. Specific measures shall be formulated by the construction administrative department under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 39 The relevant departments under local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the management of urban electricity conservation, and strictly control the energy consumption of decorative landscape lighting in public facilities and large buildings.

Article 40 The State encourages the use of new wall materials and other energy-saving building materials and energy-saving equipment, and the installation and use of renewable energy utilization systems such as solar energy in new buildings and the energy conservation renovation of existing buildings.

### Section 4 Transportation Energy Conservation

Article 41 The relevant transportation administrative departments under the State Council shall, within their respective functions and duties, be responsible for the supervision and management of energy conservation in the relevant fields of transportation nationwide.

The relevant transportation administrative departments under the State Council, together with the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council, shall formulate energy conservation plans for the relevant fields respectively.

Article 42 The State Council and its relevant departments shall guide and promote the coordinated development and effective integration of various modes of transportation, optimize the transportation structure, and build an energy-efficient comprehensive transportation system.

Article 43 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall give priority to the development of public transportation, increase investment in public transportation, improve the public transportation service system, and encourage the use of public transportation for travel; they shall also encourage the use of non-motorized vehicles for travel.

Article 44 The relevant transportation administrative departments under the State Council shall strengthen the organization and management of transportation, guide road, waterway, and air transport enterprises to improve the degree of transportation organization and intensification, and enhance energy utilization efficiency.

Article 45 The State encourages the development, production, and use of energy-saving and environmentally friendly automobiles, motorcycles, railway locomotives and rolling stock, ships, and other means of transportation, and implements a system for the scrapping and replacement of old means of transportation.

The State encourages the development and promotion of the use of clean fuels and petroleum substitute fuels for means of transportation.

Article 46 The relevant departments under the State Council shall formulate fuel consumption limit standards for commercial vehicles and vessels used in transportation; those that do not meet the standards shall not be used for commercial operations.

The relevant transportation administrative departments under the State Council shall strengthen the supervision and management of fuel consumption testing for commercial vehicles and vessels used in transportation.

### Section 5 Energy Conservation in Public Institutions

Article 47 Public institutions shall practice strict economy, eliminate waste, take the lead in using energy-saving products and equipment, and improve energy utilization efficiency.

For the purposes of this Law, "public institutions" means state organs, institutions, and organizations that use all or part of fiscal funds.

Article 48 The agencies responsible for the management of government office affairs under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level, together with the relevant departments at the same level, shall formulate and organize the implementation of energy conservation plans for public institutions at the corresponding level. The energy conservation plans for public institutions shall include plans for the energy conservation renovation of existing buildings of public institutions.

Article 49 Public institutions shall formulate annual energy conservation targets and implementation plans, strengthen the metering and monitoring management of energy consumption, and submit a report on the energy consumption status of the previous year to the agency responsible for the management of government office affairs at the corresponding level of the people's government.

The agencies responsible for the management of government office affairs under the State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level, together with the relevant departments at the same level, shall, within their management authority, formulate energy consumption quotas for public institutions at the corresponding level, and the financial departments shall formulate energy consumption expenditure standards based on such quotas.

Article 50 Public institutions shall strengthen the management of their own energy use systems to ensure that the operation of the energy use systems complies with the relevant national standards.

Public institutions shall conduct energy audits in accordance with regulations and take measures to improve energy utilization efficiency based on the results of the energy audits.

Article 51 When procuring energy-using products and equipment, public institutions shall give priority to products and equipment included in the government procurement catalog of energy-saving products and equipment. It is prohibited to procure energy-using products and equipment that have been explicitly phased out by the State.

The government procurement catalog of energy-saving products and equipment shall be formulated and published by the government procurement supervision and management department under the people's government at or above the provincial level together with the relevant departments at the same level.

### Section 6 Energy Conservation in Key Energy-Using Units

Article 52 The State strengthens energy conservation management for key energy-using units.

The following energy-using units are key energy-using units:

(1) Energy-using units with an annual comprehensive energy consumption of 10,000 tons of standard coal or more;

(2) Energy-using units with an annual comprehensive energy consumption of 5,000 tons or more but less than 10,000 tons of standard coal, as designated by the relevant departments under the State Council or the departments in charge of energy conservation under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The measures for the management of energy conservation in key energy-using units shall be formulated by the department in charge of energy conservation under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 53 Key energy-using units shall submit a report on the energy utilization status of the previous year to the department in charge of energy conservation each year. The energy utilization status includes energy consumption, energy utilization efficiency, the achievement of energy conservation targets, energy conservation benefit analysis, and energy conservation measures.

Article 54 The department in charge of energy conservation shall review the energy utilization status reports submitted by key energy-using units. For key energy-using units with an unsound energy conservation management system, unimplemented energy conservation measures, or low energy utilization efficiency, the department in charge of energy conservation shall conduct on-site investigations, organize energy efficiency testing of energy-using equipment, order the implementation of energy audits, and issue written rectification requirements with a specified period for rectification.

Article 55 Key energy-using units shall establish energy management positions, appoint energy management responsible persons from among personnel with energy conservation expertise, practical experience, and intermediate or higher technical titles, and report them to the department in charge of energy conservation and the relevant departments for recordation.The person responsible for energy management shall organize the analysis and evaluation of the unit's energy consumption status, compile the unit's energy utilization status report, propose improvement measures for the unit's energy conservation work, and organize their implementation.

The person responsible for energy management shall receive energy conservation training.

## Chapter IV: Progress in Energy Conservation Technology

Article 56: The department of the State Council responsible for energy conservation work shall, in conjunction with the science and technology competent department of the State Council, issue an outline of energy conservation technology policies to guide the research, development, and promotion of energy conservation technologies.

Article 57: People's governments at or above the county level shall prioritize energy conservation technology research and development as a key area of government science and technology investment, support research institutions and enterprises in conducting applied research on energy conservation technologies, formulate energy conservation standards, develop common and key energy conservation technologies, and promote innovation and transformation of energy conservation technologies.

Article 58: The department of the State Council responsible for energy conservation work shall, in conjunction with relevant departments of the State Council, formulate and publish a promotion catalog of energy conservation technologies and products, guiding energy-using units and individuals to use advanced energy conservation technologies and products.

The department of the State Council responsible for energy conservation work shall, in conjunction with relevant departments of the State Council, organize the implementation of major energy conservation research projects, energy conservation demonstration projects, and key energy conservation projects.

Article 59: People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the principles of adapting to local conditions, multi-energy complementarity, comprehensive utilization, and cost-effectiveness, strengthen energy conservation work in agriculture and rural areas, and increase funding for the promotion and application of energy conservation technologies and products in agriculture and rural areas.

The competent departments of agriculture, science and technology, and others shall support and promote the application of energy conservation technologies and products in agricultural production, processing, storage, and transportation, and encourage the replacement and phasing out of high-energy-consuming agricultural machinery and fishing vessels.

The State encourages and supports the vigorous development of biogas in rural areas, promotes the use of renewable energy technologies such as biomass, solar, and wind energy, develops small-scale hydropower in accordance with the principles of scientific planning and orderly development, promotes energy-efficient rural housing and stoves, encourages the use of non-arable land for planting energy plants, and vigorously develops energy forests such as fuelwood forests.

## Chapter V: Incentive Measures

Article 60: The central finance and provincial-level local finance shall allocate special funds for energy conservation to support research and development of energy conservation technologies, demonstration and promotion of energy conservation technologies and products, implementation of key energy conservation projects, energy conservation publicity and training, information services, and commendation and rewards.

Article 61: The State shall implement preferential tax policies and other supportive measures for the production and use of energy conservation technologies and products listed in the promotion catalog specified in Article 58 of this Law that require support.

The State shall support the promotion and use of energy-saving lighting fixtures and other energy-saving products through financial subsidies.

Article 62: The State shall implement tax policies conducive to energy conservation and resource conservation, improve the paid-use system for energy and mineral resources, and promote energy conservation and the improvement of mining and utilization levels.

Article 63: The State shall use tax and other policies to encourage the import of advanced energy conservation technologies and equipment, and control the export of products with high energy consumption and heavy pollution during production.

Article 64: The government procurement supervision and management department shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, formulate a government procurement catalog of energy-saving products and equipment, and shall give priority to including products and equipment that have obtained energy-saving product certification.

Article 65: The State shall guide financial institutions to increase credit support for energy conservation projects, and provide preferential loans for projects such as research and development of energy conservation technologies, production of energy-saving products, and energy conservation technological transformation that meet the conditions.

The State shall promote and guide relevant sectors of society to increase capital investment in energy conservation and accelerate energy conservation technological transformation.

Article 66: The State shall implement price policies conducive to energy conservation to guide energy-using units and individuals in saving energy.

The State shall use fiscal, tax, and price policies to support the promotion of energy conservation methods such as demand-side management, contract energy management, and voluntary energy conservation agreements.

The State shall implement time-of-use electricity pricing, seasonal electricity pricing, and interruptible load pricing systems to encourage electricity users to rationally adjust their electricity loads; for enterprises in the steel, non-ferrous metals, building materials, chemical, and other major energy-consuming industries, differential electricity pricing policies shall be implemented, categorized into elimination, restriction, permission, and encouragement classes.

Article 67: People's governments at all levels shall commend and reward units and individuals that have made significant achievements in energy conservation management, scientific research, and promotion and application, as well as those who report serious energy waste.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

Article 68: If an agency responsible for approving government-invested projects, in violation of this Law, approves the construction of a project that does not meet mandatory energy conservation standards, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If a construction unit of a fixed asset investment project starts construction of a project that does not meet mandatory energy conservation standards, or puts such a project into production or use, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall order the suspension of construction, production, or use, and require rectification within a specified period; if the production project cannot be rectified or fails to be rectified within the specified period, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall report to the people's government at the same level to order closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 69: The production, import, or sale of energy-using products or equipment explicitly eliminated by the State, or the use of counterfeit or fraudulent energy-saving product certification marks, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 70: The production, import, or sale of energy-using products or equipment that do not meet mandatory energy efficiency standards shall be ordered by the market supervision and management department to cease production, import, or sale; the illegally produced, imported, or sold energy-using products, equipment, and illegal gains shall be confiscated; and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 71: The use of energy-using equipment or production processes explicitly eliminated by the State shall be ordered by the department responsible for energy conservation work to cease use, and the energy-using equipment explicitly eliminated by the State shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the department responsible for energy conservation work may submit an opinion to the people's government at the same level to order a suspension of operations for rectification or closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 72: If a production unit uses energy exceeding the energy consumption quota standard per unit product, and the circumstances are serious, and after being ordered to rectify within a specified period, it fails to rectify or fails to meet the rectification requirements, the department responsible for energy conservation work may submit an opinion to the people's government at the same level to order a suspension of operations for rectification or closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council.

Article 73: In violation of this Law, if an energy efficiency label should be affixed but is not, the market supervision and management department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

In violation of this Law, if the energy efficiency label is not registered, or the energy efficiency label used does not comply with regulations, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If an energy efficiency label is forged or fraudulently used, or false propaganda is made using an energy efficiency label, the market supervision and management department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 74: If an energy-using unit fails to equip or use energy metering instruments in accordance with regulations, the market supervision and management department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 75: The concealment, falsification, or alteration of energy statistics data, or the fabrication of false energy statistics data, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Statistics Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 76: If an institution providing services such as energy conservation consulting, design, evaluation, testing, auditing, or certification provides false information, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall order correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 77: In violation of this Law, if energy is provided free of charge to employees of the unit or a flat-rate system for energy consumption is implemented, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 78: If a grid enterprise fails to arrange for qualified combined heat and power units and units using waste heat and waste pressure for power generation to be connected to and operate with the grid in accordance with this Law, or fails to implement the relevant national on-grid electricity price regulations, the national electric power regulatory authority shall order correction; if economic losses are caused to the power generation enterprise, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 79: If a construction unit violates building energy conservation standards, the construction competent department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

If a design unit, construction unit, or supervision unit violates building energy conservation standards, the construction competent department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the department issuing the qualification certificate shall lower the qualification level or revoke the qualification certificate; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 80: If a real estate development enterprise, in violation of this Law, fails to clearly inform buyers of the energy conservation measures, warranty period of insulation works, and other information of the house being sold at the time of sale, the construction competent department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if false propaganda is made regarding the above information, the construction competent department shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 81: If a public institution, when procuring energy-using products or equipment, fails to give priority to products or equipment included in the government procurement catalog of energy-saving products and equipment, or procures energy-using products or equipment explicitly eliminated by the State, the government procurement supervision and management department shall issue a warning and may also impose a fine; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law and shall be notified.

Article 82: If a key energy-using unit fails to submit an energy utilization status report in accordance with this Law, or the content of the report is untrue, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 83: If a key energy-using unit, without justifiable reasons, refuses to implement the rectification requirements specified in Article 54 of this Law, or the rectification fails to meet the requirements, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan.

Article 84: If a key energy-using unit fails to establish an energy management position, appoint a person responsible for energy management, and report to the department responsible for energy conservation work and relevant departments for record in accordance with this Law, the department responsible for energy conservation work shall order correction; if correction is refused, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 85: Any violation of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 86: If a state functionary abuses power, neglects duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain in energy conservation management work, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 87: This Law shall come into force on April 1, 2008.
