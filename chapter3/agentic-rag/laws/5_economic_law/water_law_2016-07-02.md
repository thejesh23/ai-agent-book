# Water Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on January 21, 1988

Revised at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on August 29, 2002

First Amendment on August 27, 2009 according to the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Second Amendment on July 2, 2016 according to the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of rationally developing, utilizing, conserving and protecting water resources, preventing and controlling water disasters, achieving sustainable utilization of water resources, and meeting the needs of national economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the development, utilization, conservation, protection and management of water resources, and the prevention and control of water disasters within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

Water resources as mentioned in this Law include surface water and groundwater.

Article 3 Water resources are owned by the State. The ownership of water resources shall be exercised by the State Council on behalf of the State. Water in ponds and reservoirs constructed and managed by rural collective economic organizations shall be used by those organizations.

Article 4 In developing, utilizing, conserving and protecting water resources and preventing and controlling water disasters, comprehensive planning shall be made, overall consideration shall be given to all relevant factors, both temporary and permanent measures shall be taken, comprehensive utilization shall be practiced, and benefits shall be sought, so as to give full play to the multiple functions of water resources and to coordinate the use of water for domestic purposes, production and business operations, and the ecological environment.

Article 5 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of water conservancy infrastructure and incorporate it into their plans for national economic and social development.

Article 6 The State encourages units and individuals to develop and utilize water resources according to law and protects their lawful rights and interests. Units and individuals that develop and utilize water resources have the obligation to protect water resources according to law.

Article 7 The State applies a permit system for water abstraction and a system for the paid use of water resources according to law, except for the use of water in ponds and reservoirs of rural collective economic organizations by such organizations and their members. The water administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for organizing the implementation of the national permit system for water abstraction and the system for the paid use of water resources.

Article 8 The State practices strict economy in water use, vigorously promotes water-saving measures, disseminates new water-saving technologies and techniques, develops water-saving industries, agriculture and service industries, and builds a water-saving society.

People's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen the management of water conservation, establish a system for the development and dissemination of water-saving technologies, and cultivate and develop water-saving industries.

Units and individuals have the obligation to conserve water.

Article 9 The State protects water resources, takes effective measures to protect vegetation, plant trees and grass, conserve water sources, prevent and control soil erosion and water pollution, and improve the ecological environment.

Article 10 The State encourages and supports research, dissemination and application of advanced science and technology for the development, utilization, conservation, protection and management of water resources and for the prevention and control of water disasters.

Article 11 Units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in the development, utilization, conservation, protection and management of water resources and in the prevention and control of water disasters shall be rewarded by the people's governments.

Article 12 The State applies a management system for water resources that combines river basin management with administrative region management.

The water administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for the unified management and supervision of water resources nationwide.

The river basin management organs (hereinafter referred to as river basin management organs) established by the water administrative department of the State Council for the important rivers and lakes designated by the State shall, within their respective jurisdictions, exercise the functions of water resources management and supervision as prescribed by laws and administrative regulations and as delegated by the water administrative department of the State Council.

The water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, within the limits of their authority as prescribed, be responsible for the unified management and supervision of water resources within their respective administrative regions.

Article 13 The relevant departments under the State Council shall, in accordance with the division of their functions and duties, be responsible for the work relating to the development, utilization, conservation and protection of water resources.

The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the division of their functions and duties, be responsible for the work relating to the development, utilization, conservation and protection of water resources within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II: Water Resources Planning

Article 14 The State formulates a national strategic plan for water resources.

For the development, utilization, conservation and protection of water resources and the prevention and control of water disasters, unified plans shall be formulated for river basins and regions. Plans are divided into river basin plans and regional plans. River basin plans include comprehensive river basin plans and specialized river basin plans; regional plans include comprehensive regional plans and specialized regional plans.

The comprehensive plans mentioned in the preceding paragraph refer to the overall arrangements for the development, utilization, conservation and protection of water resources and the prevention and control of water disasters, formulated in light of the needs of economic and social development and the current status of water resources development and utilization. The specialized plans mentioned in the preceding paragraph refer to plans for flood control, waterlogging control, irrigation, navigation, water supply, hydropower generation, bamboo and log rafting, fisheries, water resources protection, soil and water conservation, sand prevention and control, and water conservation.

Article 15 Regional plans within a river basin shall be subordinate to the river basin plan, and specialized plans shall be subordinate to the comprehensive plan.

Comprehensive river basin plans and comprehensive regional plans, as well as specialized plans closely related to land use, shall be coordinated with the national economic and social development plan, the overall land use plan, the overall urban plan, and the environmental protection plan, taking into account the needs of various regions and industries.

Article 16 In formulating plans, comprehensive scientific surveys and assessments of water resources must be conducted. Comprehensive scientific surveys and assessments of water resources shall be organized and carried out by the water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level jointly with the relevant departments at the same level.

People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of hydrological and water resources information systems. The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level and the river basin management organs shall strengthen the dynamic monitoring of water resources.

Basic hydrological data shall be made public in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 17 Comprehensive river basin plans for important rivers and lakes designated by the State shall be formulated by the water administrative department of the State Council jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council and the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval. Comprehensive river basin plans and comprehensive regional plans for other rivers and lakes that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the relevant river basin management organs jointly with the water administrative departments and relevant departments of the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the rivers and lakes are located, and after being reviewed and commented on by the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall be submitted to the water administrative department of the State Council for examination; after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments under the State Council, the water administrative department of the State Council shall submit them to the State Council or its authorized department for approval.

Comprehensive river basin plans and comprehensive regional plans for rivers and lakes other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated by the water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level jointly with the relevant departments at the same level and the relevant local people's governments, and shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level or its authorized department for approval, and shall be reported to the water administrative department at the next higher level for the record.

Specialized plans shall be formulated by the relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, and after soliciting opinions from other relevant departments at the same level, shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval. The formulation and approval of flood control plans and soil and water conservation plans shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Flood Control Law and the Soil and Water Conservation Law.

Article 18 Once approved, plans must be strictly implemented.

If an approved plan needs to be amended, it must be approved by the original approving authority in accordance with the planning procedures.

Article 19 The construction of water projects must conform to the comprehensive river basin plan. For the construction of water projects on important rivers and lakes designated by the State and on rivers and lakes that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, if the construction unit fails to obtain a planning consent letter signed by the relevant river basin management organ confirming compliance with the requirements of the comprehensive river basin plan, the construction unit shall not commence construction; for the construction of water projects on other rivers and lakes, if the construction unit fails to obtain a planning consent letter signed by the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, within its administrative authority, confirming compliance with the requirements of the comprehensive river basin plan, the construction unit shall not commence construction. If the construction of a water project involves flood control, the relevant provisions of the Flood Control Law shall apply; if it involves other regions or industries, the construction unit shall consult with the relevant regions and departments in advance.

## Chapter III: Development and Utilization of Water Resources

Article 20 The development and utilization of water resources shall adhere to the principle of combining beneficial uses with disaster prevention, taking into account the interests of upstream and downstream, left and right banks, and relevant regions, giving full play to the comprehensive benefits of water resources, and being subordinate to the overall arrangements for flood control.

Article 21 The development and utilization of water resources shall first meet the domestic water needs of urban and rural residents, while also taking into account the needs of agriculture, industry, ecological environment, and navigation.

In arid and semi-arid areas, the development and utilization of water resources shall give full consideration to the water needs of the ecological environment.

Article 22 Inter-basin water transfer shall be subject to comprehensive planning and scientific demonstration, taking into account the water needs of both the exporting and importing basins, and preventing damage to the ecological environment.

Article 23 Local people's governments at all levels shall, in light of the actual conditions of water resources in their respective regions, rationally organize the development and comprehensive utilization of water resources in accordance with the principles of unified dispatch and development of surface water and groundwater, combining the development of new sources with conservation, prioritizing conservation, and reusing treated sewage.

The formulation of national economic and social development plans and overall urban plans, as well as the layout of major construction projects, shall be compatible with local water resource conditions and flood control requirements, and shall be subject to scientific demonstration; in areas with insufficient water resources, restrictions shall be imposed on the scale of cities and on industrial, agricultural, and service projects that consume large amounts of water.

Article 24 In water-scarce areas, the State encourages the collection, development and utilization of rainwater and brackish water, and the utilization and desalination of seawater.

Article 25 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over irrigation, waterlogging drainage, and soil and water conservation work to promote the development of agricultural production; in areas prone to salinization and waterlogging, measures shall be taken to control and lower the groundwater table.

Where rural collective economic organizations or their members invest in the construction of water project facilities on collectively owned land or contracted land owned by the collective economic organization according to law, they shall manage and reasonably use the water project facilities and the water stored therein in accordance with the principle that the investor builds, manages, and benefits.

The construction of reservoirs by rural collective economic organizations shall be subject to approval by the water administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level.

Article 26 The State encourages the development and utilization of hydropower resources. On rivers rich in hydropower resources, multi-objective cascade development shall be carried out in a planned manner.

The construction of hydropower stations shall protect the ecological environment and take into account the needs of flood control, water supply, irrigation, navigation, bamboo and log rafting, and fisheries.

Article 27 The State encourages the development and utilization of water transport resources. When constructing permanent river-closing sluice gates or dams on rivers that serve as migration routes for aquatic organisms, are navigable, or are used for bamboo and log rafting, the construction unit shall simultaneously build facilities for fish passage, ship passage, and log passage, or take other remedial measures approved by the department authorized by the State Council, and shall properly arrange for the protection of aquatic organisms, navigation, and bamboo and log rafting during the construction and impoundment periods; the costs incurred shall be borne by the construction unit.

If a sluice gate or dam is constructed on a non-navigable river or artificial waterway and the waterway becomes navigable thereafter, the construction unit of the sluice gate or dam shall simultaneously build ship passage facilities or reserve space for such facilities.

Article 28 No unit or individual may divert, intercept (store), or drain water in a manner that harms the public interest or the lawful rights and interests of others.

Article 29 The State applies a development-oriented resettlement policy for migrants displaced by water projects, following the principle of combining pre-relocation compensation and subsidies with post-relocation support, properly arranging the production and livelihood of the migrants, and protecting their lawful rights and interests.

Resettlement of migrants shall be carried out simultaneously with the construction of the project. The construction unit shall, based on the environmental capacity of the resettlement area and the principle of sustainable development, formulate a resettlement plan tailored to local conditions. After the plan is approved according to law, it shall be implemented by the relevant local people's government. The funds required for resettlement shall be included in the project construction investment plan.

## Chapter IV: Protection of Water Resources, Water Areas, and Water Projects

Article 30 When formulating plans for the development and utilization of water resources and dispatching water resources, the water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level, river basin management organs, and other relevant departments shall pay attention to maintaining the reasonable flow of rivers and the reasonable water levels of lakes, reservoirs, and groundwater, and preserving the natural purification capacity of water bodies.

Article 31 Water-related activities such as the development, utilization, conservation, and protection of water resources and the prevention and control of water disasters shall comply with approved plans; if such activities violate plans and cause a reduction in the use function of river and lake waters, over-extraction of groundwater, land subsidence, or water pollution, the responsible party shall bear the responsibility for remediation.

If mining or underground engineering construction causes a drop in the groundwater table, depletion of water sources, or ground collapse due to dewatering and drainage, the mining unit or construction unit shall take remedial measures; if losses are caused to the lives or production of others, compensation shall be made according to law.

Article 32 The water administrative department of the State Council, jointly with the environmental protection administrative department of the State Council, relevant departments, and the people's governments of relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall, in accordance with comprehensive river basin plans, water resources protection plans, and the requirements of economic and social development, formulate water function zone plans for important rivers and lakes designated by the State, and submit them to the State Council for approval. Water function zone plans for other rivers and lakes that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the relevant river basin management organs jointly with the water administrative departments, environmental protection administrative departments, and other relevant departments of the people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where the rivers and lakes are located, and after being reviewed and commented on by the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, shall be examined by the water administrative department of the State Council jointly with the environmental protection administrative department of the State Council, and submitted to the State Council or its authorized department for approval.

Water function zone plans for rivers and lakes other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated by the water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level jointly with the environmental protection administrative departments and other relevant departments of the people's governments at the same level, and shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level or its authorized department for approval, and shall be reported to the water administrative department and the environmental protection administrative department at the next higher level for the record.

The water administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level or the river basin management organs shall, based on the water quality requirements of the water function zones and the natural purification capacity of the water bodies, determine the assimilative capacity of the water areas and provide opinions to the environmental protection administrative departments on the total amount of pollutant discharge limits for the water areas.

The water administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level and the river basin management organs shall monitor the water quality conditions of water function zones. If they find that the total discharge of key pollutants exceeds the control targets, or that the water quality of a water function zone fails to meet the water quality requirements for the use function of the water area, they shall promptly report to the relevant people's government for remedial measures and notify the environmental protection administrative department.

Article 33 The State establishes a system of protected areas for drinking water sources. The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall designate protected areas for drinking water sources and take measures to prevent the depletion of water sources and water pollution, ensuring the safety of drinking water for urban and rural residents.

Article 34 It is prohibited to set up sewage outfalls within protected areas for drinking water sources.

The construction, renovation, or expansion of sewage outfalls in rivers and lakes shall be subject to the consent of the water administrative department or river basin management organ with jurisdiction, and the environmental protection administrative department shall be responsible for approving the environmental impact report for the construction project.

Article 35 If an engineering construction project occupies agricultural irrigation water sources or irrigation and drainage engineering facilities, or has an adverse impact on existing irrigation water or water supply sources, the construction unit shall take corresponding remedial measures; if losses are caused, compensation shall be made according to law.

Article 36 In areas where groundwater is over-extracted, local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to strictly control the extraction of groundwater. In areas where groundwater is severely over-extracted, with the approval of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, areas where groundwater extraction is prohibited or restricted may be designated. The extraction of groundwater in coastal areas shall be subject to scientific demonstration, and measures shall be taken to prevent land subsidence and seawater intrusion.

Article 37 It is prohibited to dump or pile up objects that obstruct flood flow, or to plant trees or tall crops that obstruct flood flow, in rivers, lakes, reservoirs, canals, or channels.

It is prohibited to construct buildings or structures that obstruct flood flow within the management scope of river channels, or to engage in activities that affect river regime stability, endanger the safety of river banks and dikes, or otherwise obstruct flood flow in river channels.

Article 38 The construction of bridges, docks, and other structures or buildings that block, cross, or are adjacent to rivers, as well as the laying of pipelines or cables crossing rivers, within the management scope of river channels, shall comply with the national flood control standards and other relevant technical requirements. The engineering construction plan shall be submitted to the relevant water administrative department for review and approval in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Flood Control Law.

If the construction of the engineering facilities mentioned in the preceding paragraph requires the expansion, renovation, removal, or causes damage to existing water project facilities, the construction unit shall bear the costs of expansion, renovation, and compensation for losses, except where the existing engineering facilities are illegal.

Article 39 The State applies a permit system for sand extraction in river channels. The measures for implementing the permit system for sand extraction in river channels shall be formulated by the State Council.

If sand extraction within the management scope of river channels affects the stability of the river regime or endangers the safety of dikes, the relevant water administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall designate prohibited extraction areas and periods and make a public announcement.

Article 40 It is prohibited to reclaim land from lakes by enclosure. Land that has already been reclaimed shall be returned to the lake in a planned manner in accordance with the national flood control standards.

It is prohibited to reclaim land from river channels by enclosure. If reclamation is truly necessary, it shall be subject to scientific demonstration and, after obtaining the consent of the water administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government or the water administrative department of the State Council, shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval.

Article 41 Units and individuals have the obligation to protect water projects and shall not encroach upon or damage engineering facilities such as dikes, revetments, flood control facilities, hydrological monitoring facilities, and hydrogeological monitoring facilities.

Article 42 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to ensure the safety of water projects within their respective administrative regions, especially dams and dikes, and shall eliminate dangerous situations within a specified time limit. The water administrative departments shall strengthen the supervision and management of the safety of water projects.

Article 43 The State provides protection for water projects. For water projects owned by the State, the scope of project management and protection shall be delineated in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

For water projects managed by the water administrative department of the State Council or river basin management organs, the scope of project management and protection shall be delineated by the competent department or the river basin management organ in consultation with the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

For water projects other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, the scope of project protection and the protection responsibilities shall be delineated in accordance with the provisions of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Within the scope of water project protection, activities such as blasting, well drilling, quarrying, and earth extraction that affect the operation of water projects or endanger their safety are prohibited.

## Chapter V: Allocation and Economical Use of Water ResourcesArticle 44 The competent department of development planning under the State Council and the competent administrative department of water resources under the State Council are responsible for the macro-allocation of water resources nationwide. The national and cross-provincial, autonomous region, or municipality medium- and long-term water supply and demand plans shall be formulated by the competent administrative department of water resources under the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments, and implemented after review and approval by the competent department of development planning under the State Council. Local medium- and long-term water supply and demand plans shall be formulated by the competent administrative department of water resources under the local people's government at or above the county level in conjunction with relevant departments at the same level, based on the superior medium- and long-term water supply and demand plan and local conditions, and implemented after review and approval by the competent department of development planning under the people's government at the same level.

Medium- and long-term water supply and demand plans shall be formulated based on the current state of water supply and demand, national economic and social development plans, river basin plans, and regional plans, adhering to the principles of coordinated water supply and demand, comprehensive balance, ecological protection, strict conservation, and rational development of new sources.

Article 45 The regulation of runoff and the allocation of water volume shall be based on river basin plans and medium- and long-term water supply and demand plans, with water volume allocation plans formulated on a river basin basis.

Water volume allocation plans and drought emergency water dispatch plans that cross provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities shall be formulated by the river basin management agency in consultation with the relevant provincial, autonomous region, or municipal people's governments, and implemented after approval by the State Council or its authorized department. Other water volume allocation plans and drought emergency water dispatch plans that cross administrative regions shall be formulated by the competent administrative department of water resources under the common superior people's government in consultation with the relevant local people's governments, and implemented after approval by the people's government at that level.

Once approved, the relevant local people's governments must implement the water volume allocation plans and drought emergency water dispatch plans.

Construction of water resource development and utilization projects on boundary rivers between different administrative regions must comply with the approved water volume allocation plan for that river basin, and shall be approved by the common superior people's government's competent administrative department of water resources or the relevant river basin management agency upon submission by the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level.

Article 46 The competent administrative department of water resources under the local people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, based on the approved water volume allocation plan and the annual forecasted incoming water volume, formulate an annual water volume allocation plan and dispatch schedule, and implement unified water dispatch; the relevant local people's governments must comply.

The annual water volume allocation plan for major rivers and lakes designated by the state shall be incorporated into the national annual plan for national economic and social development.

Article 47 The state implements a system combining total water use control and quota management for water use.

The relevant competent departments of industry under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities shall formulate water use quotas for industries within their respective administrative regions, which shall be implemented after review and approval by the competent administrative department of water resources and the competent department of quality supervision and inspection at the same level, published by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality, and filed with the competent administrative department of water resources under the State Council and the competent department of quality supervision and inspection under the State Council.

The competent department of development planning under the local people's government at or above the county level shall, in conjunction with the competent administrative department of water resources at the same level, formulate an annual water use plan based on water use quotas, economic and technical conditions, and the water volume available for use in that administrative region as determined by the water volume allocation plan, and implement total control over the annual water use within that administrative region.

Article 48 Units and individuals that directly withdraw water resources from rivers, lakes, or underground shall, in accordance with the provisions of the national water permit system and the paid use system for water resources, apply to the competent administrative department of water resources or the river basin management agency for a water permit, pay water resource fees, and obtain the right to withdraw water. However, this does not apply to small-scale water withdrawals for domestic use and for drinking by scattered livestock and poultry raised in pens.

Specific measures for implementing the water permit system and collecting and managing water resource fees shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 49 Water use shall be metered and shall comply with the approved water use plan.

A system of metered water charges and progressive surcharges for exceeding quotas shall be implemented for water use.

Article 50 People's governments at all levels shall promote water-saving irrigation methods and technologies, and take necessary anti-seepage measures for agricultural water storage and conveyance projects to improve agricultural water use efficiency.

Article 51 Industrial water use shall adopt advanced technologies, processes, and equipment, increase the number of water circulation cycles, and improve the rate of water reuse.

The state shall gradually phase out backward, water-intensive processes, equipment, and products. The specific list shall be formulated and published by the competent department of economic integration under the State Council in conjunction with the competent administrative department of water resources under the State Council and relevant departments. Producers, sellers, or users in production and operation shall cease production, sale, or use of the processes, equipment, and products on the list within the specified time limit.

Article 52 City people's governments shall take effective measures tailored to local conditions, promote water-saving domestic water appliances, reduce the leakage rate of urban water supply networks, and improve domestic water use efficiency; strengthen centralized treatment of urban sewage, encourage the use of reclaimed water, and increase the rate of sewage reuse.

Article 53 New construction, expansion, or renovation projects shall formulate water-saving measures plans and construct supporting water-saving facilities. Water-saving facilities shall be designed, constructed, and put into operation simultaneously with the main project.

Water supply enterprises and units with self-built water supply facilities shall strengthen the maintenance and management of water supply facilities to reduce water leakage.

Article 54 People's governments at all levels shall actively take measures to improve the drinking water conditions of urban and rural residents.

Article 55 Users of water supplied by water projects shall pay water fees to the water supply unit in accordance with national regulations. Water supply prices shall be determined based on the principles of cost recovery, reasonable profit, high quality for high price, and fair burden. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of pricing under the people's government at or above the provincial level in conjunction with the competent administrative department of water resources or other competent water supply administrative departments at the same level, within their respective authorities.

## Chapter VI: Handling of Water Disputes and Supervision and Inspection of Law Enforcement

Article 56 Water disputes between different administrative regions shall be resolved through consultation; if consultation fails, the superior people's government shall make a ruling, which all parties concerned must comply with. Before the water dispute is resolved, without agreement between the parties or approval from the common superior people's government, no party may construct drainage, water-blocking, water intake, or water interception (storage) projects within a certain range on both sides of the administrative boundary line, nor unilaterally alter the existing water status.

Article 57 Water disputes between units, between individuals, or between units and individuals shall be resolved through consultation; if the parties are unwilling to consult or consultation fails, they may apply for mediation by the local people's government at or above the county level or its authorized department, or may directly file a civil lawsuit with the people's court. If mediation by the local people's government at or above the county level or its authorized department fails, the parties may file a civil lawsuit with the people's court.

Before the water dispute is resolved, the parties shall not unilaterally alter the existing status.

Article 58 When handling water disputes, the people's government at or above the county level or its authorized department has the right to take temporary measures, and all parties concerned or the parties must comply.

Article 59 The competent administrative departments of water resources under the people's governments at or above the county level and river basin management agencies shall strengthen supervision and inspection of violations of this Law and investigate and punish them according to law.

Water administration supervision and inspection personnel shall be dedicated to their duties and enforce the law impartially.

Article 60 When performing the supervision and inspection duties stipulated in this Law, the competent administrative departments of water resources under the people's governments at or above the county level, river basin management agencies, and their water administration supervision and inspection personnel have the right to take the following measures:

(1) Require the unit under inspection to provide relevant documents, licenses, and materials;
(2) Require the unit under inspection to provide explanations on issues related to the implementation of this Law;
(3) Enter the production premises of the unit under inspection for investigation;
(4) Order the unit under inspection to cease acts violating this Law and fulfill legal obligations.

Article 61 Relevant units or individuals shall cooperate with the supervision and inspection work of water administration supervision and inspection personnel and shall not refuse or obstruct water administration supervision and inspection personnel from performing their duties according to law.

Article 62 When performing supervision and inspection duties, water administration supervision and inspection personnel shall present their law enforcement credentials to the unit or individual under inspection.

Article 63 If the people's government at or above the county level or the superior competent administrative department of water resources discovers that the competent administrative department of water resources at the same level or a lower level has engaged in illegal or derelict acts during supervision and inspection, it shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit.

## Chapter VII: Legal Liability

Article 64 If the competent administrative department of water resources or other relevant departments, as well as water project management units and their staff, take advantage of their positions to accept property or other benefits from others, or neglect their duties, issue permits or sign review approval opinions to units or individuals that do not meet legal conditions, fail to allocate water volume according to the water volume allocation plan, fail to collect water resource fees in accordance with relevant national regulations, fail to perform supervision duties, or fail to investigate and punish illegal acts discovered, causing serious consequences, and if the act constitutes a crime, the responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the act does not constitute a criminal punishment, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 65 If buildings or structures that obstruct flood discharge are constructed within the management scope of a river channel, or activities are engaged in that affect river regime stability, endanger riverbank embankment safety, or otherwise obstruct river flood discharge, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act, dismantle the illegal buildings or structures within a specified time limit, and restore the original state; if dismantling or restoration is not completed within the time limit, forced dismantling shall be carried out, with the costs borne by the illegal unit or individual, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If a water project is constructed without the consent of the competent administrative department of water resources or the river basin management agency, or bridges, docks, or other river-blocking, river-crossing, or riverside buildings or structures are built, or river-crossing pipelines or cables are laid, and the Flood Control Law does not provide for such actions, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act and completion of the relevant procedures within a specified time limit; if the procedures are not completed within the time limit or the application is not approved, the illegal buildings or structures shall be dismantled within a specified time limit; if dismantling is not completed within the time limit, forced dismantling shall be carried out, with the costs borne by the illegal unit or individual, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If the engineering facilities listed in the preceding paragraph are constructed with the consent of the competent administrative department of water resources or the river basin management agency but not in accordance with the requirements, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order corrections within a specified time limit and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan, depending on the severity of the circumstances.

Article 66 If any of the following acts occur, and the Flood Control Law does not provide for them, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act, removal of obstacles or adoption of other remedial measures within a specified time limit, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) Dumping or piling up objects that obstruct flood discharge, or planting trees or tall crops that obstruct flood discharge, in rivers, lakes, reservoirs, canals, or channels;
(2) Reclaiming land from lakes or reclaiming river channels without approval.

Article 67 If a sewage outlet is set up within a drinking water source protection zone, the local people's government at or above the county level shall order its dismantling and restoration of the original state within a specified time limit; if dismantling or restoration is not completed within the time limit, forced dismantling and restoration shall be carried out, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If a new sewage outlet is built, or an existing one is modified or expanded, in a river or lake without review and approval by the competent administrative department of water resources or the river basin management agency, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act, restoration of the original state within a specified time limit, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 68 If backward, water-intensive processes, equipment, or products that are explicitly phased out by the state are produced, sold, or used in production or operation, the competent department of economic integration under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order cessation of production, sale, or use, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 69 If any of the following acts occur, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act, adoption of remedial measures within a specified time limit, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the water permit shall be revoked:

(1) Withdrawing water without approval;
(2) Withdrawing water not in accordance with the conditions stipulated in the approved water permit.

Article 70 If water resource fees are refused to be paid, delayed in payment, or in arrears, the competent administrative department of water resources under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order payment within a specified time limit; if payment is not made within the time limit, a late payment penalty of 0.2% of the overdue amount per day shall be imposed from the date of delay, and a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the amount of water resource fees payable or supplementary payable shall be imposed.

Article 71 If the water-saving facilities of a construction project are not built or do not meet the requirements stipulated by the state, and the project is put into use without authorization, the relevant department under the people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of use, corrections within a specified time limit, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 72 If any of the following acts occur and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the act does not constitute a criminal punishment and the Flood Control Law does not provide for it, the competent administrative department of water resources under the local people's government at or above the county level or the river basin management agency shall, within its authority, order cessation of the illegal act, adoption of remedial measures, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the act violates the Public Security Administration Punishments Law, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties according to law; if losses are caused to others, compensation liability shall be borne according to law:

(1) Encroaching upon or destroying water projects, embankments, revetments, and other related facilities, or destroying flood control, hydrological monitoring, or hydrogeological monitoring facilities;
(2) Within the protection scope of water projects, engaging in activities such as blasting, well drilling, quarrying, or earth extraction that affect the operation of water projects and endanger their safety.

Article 73 If flood control materials, flood control and drainage, farmland water conservancy, hydrological monitoring and surveying, and other water project equipment and materials are encroached upon, stolen, or robbed, or if state funds and materials for disaster relief, emergency rescue, flood control, resettlement and compensation, and other water conservancy construction are embezzled or misappropriated, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 74 If, during the occurrence and handling of a water dispute, acts such as inciting disturbances, engaging in gang fights, robbing or damaging public or private property, or illegally restricting others' personal freedom occur, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law; if the act does not constitute a criminal punishment, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties according to law.

Article 75 If a water dispute occurs between different administrative regions and any of the following acts occur, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel according to law:

(1) Refusing to implement the water volume allocation plan and water dispatch plan;
(2) Refusing to comply with unified water dispatch;
(3) Refusing to implement the ruling of the superior people's government;
(4) Before the water dispute is resolved, without agreement between the parties or approval from the superior people's government, unilaterally altering the existing water status in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Article 76 If water diversion, interception (storage), or drainage damages public interests or the legitimate rights and interests of others, civil liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 77 Administrative penalties for violations of the river sand mining permit system stipulated in Article 39 of this Law shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter VIII: Supplementary Provisions

Article 78 If international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China concerning international or border rivers and lakes contain provisions different from those of the laws of the People's Republic of China, the provisions of the international treaties or agreements shall apply, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 79 For the purposes of this Law, "water projects" refer to various types of projects for the development, utilization, control, allocation, and protection of water resources in rivers, lakes, and groundwater sources.

Article 80 The development, utilization, protection, and management of seawater shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws.

Article 81 Flood control activities shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Flood Control Law.

Water pollution prevention and control shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law.

Article 82 This Law shall come into effect on October 1, 2002.
