# Stamp Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on June 10, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 Units and individuals that execute taxable instruments or conduct securities transactions within the territory of the People's Republic of China are taxpayers of stamp tax and shall pay stamp tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Units and individuals that execute taxable instruments outside the territory of the People's Republic of China for use within the territory shall pay stamp tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "taxable instruments" refer to contracts, documents of title transfer, and business account books listed in the "Stamp Tax Item and Rate Table" attached to this Law.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "securities transactions" refer to the transfer of stocks and stock-based depositary receipts traded on legally established stock exchanges or other national securities trading venues approved by the State Council.

Stamp tax on securities transactions is levied on the transferor of the securities transaction and not on the transferee.

Article 4 The items and rates of stamp tax shall be implemented in accordance with the "Stamp Tax Item and Rate Table" attached to this Law.

Article 5 The tax basis for stamp tax is as follows:

(1) The tax basis for taxable contracts is the amount stated in the contract, excluding the stated VAT amount;

(2) The tax basis for taxable documents of title transfer is the amount stated in the document of title transfer, excluding the stated VAT amount;

(3) The tax basis for taxable business account books is the total amount of paid-in capital (share capital) and capital surplus recorded in the account books;

(4) The tax basis for securities transactions is the transaction amount.

Article 6 If the amount is not stated in a taxable contract or document of title transfer, the tax basis for stamp tax shall be determined based on the actual settlement amount.

If the tax basis still cannot be determined in accordance with the preceding paragraph, it shall be determined based on the market price at the time of executing the contract or document of title transfer; if government pricing or government-guided pricing is to be implemented in accordance with the law, it shall be determined in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

Article 7 If there is no transfer price for a securities transaction, the tax basis shall be calculated and determined based on the closing price of the securities on the previous trading day at the time of handling the transfer registration procedures; if there is no closing price, the tax basis shall be calculated and determined based on the face value of the securities.

Article 8 The tax payable for stamp tax shall be calculated by multiplying the tax basis by the applicable tax rate.

Article 9 If the same taxable instrument contains two or more tax items with amounts separately stated, the tax payable shall be calculated separately according to the applicable tax items and rates; if the amounts are not separately stated, the higher tax rate shall apply.

Article 10 If the same taxable instrument is executed by two or more parties, the tax payable shall be calculated separately based on the amounts involved for each party.

Article 11 For business account books on which stamp tax has already been paid, if the total amount of paid-in capital (share capital) and capital surplus recorded in subsequent years exceeds the total amount of paid-in capital (share capital) and capital surplus on which stamp tax has already been paid, the tax payable shall be calculated based on the increased portion.

Article 12 The following instruments are exempt from stamp tax:

(1) Duplicates or copies of taxable instruments;

(2) Taxable instruments executed by foreign embassies, consulates, and representative offices of international organizations in China that are legally entitled to tax exemption for obtaining premises;

(3) Taxable instruments executed by the Chinese People's Liberation Army and the Chinese People's Armed Police Force;

(4) Purchase and sale contracts and agricultural insurance contracts executed by farmers, family farms, farmers' specialized cooperatives, rural collective economic organizations, and villagers' committees for purchasing agricultural production materials or selling agricultural products;

(5) Interest-free or discounted loan contracts, and loan contracts executed for preferential loans provided by international financial organizations to China;

(6) Documents of title transfer executed by property owners for donating property to the government, schools, social welfare institutions, or charitable organizations;

(7) Purchase and sale contracts executed by non-profit medical and health institutions for purchasing pharmaceuticals or medical materials;

(8) Electronic orders concluded between individuals and e-commerce operators.

Based on the needs of national economic and social development, the State Council may provide for reductions or exemptions of stamp tax for circumstances such as ensuring residents' housing needs, enterprise restructuring and reorganization, bankruptcy, and supporting the development of small and micro enterprises, and shall report to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

Article 13 If the taxpayer is a unit, it shall declare and pay stamp tax to the competent tax authority at its place of establishment; if the taxpayer is an individual, he or she shall declare and pay stamp tax to the competent tax authority at the place where the taxable instrument is executed or at the taxpayer's place of residence.

If the title to immovable property is transferred, the taxpayer shall declare and pay stamp tax to the competent tax authority at the location of the immovable property.

Article 14 If the taxpayer is a unit or individual outside the territory and has an agent within the territory, the agent within the territory shall be the withholding agent; if there is no agent within the territory, the taxpayer shall declare and pay stamp tax on its own, and the specific measures shall be prescribed by the tax competent authority under the State Council.

The securities registration and settlement institution shall be the withholding agent for stamp tax on securities transactions and shall declare and pay the tax and bank settlement interest to the competent tax authority at its place of establishment.

Article 15 The time when the obligation to pay stamp tax arises is the day on which the taxpayer executes the taxable instrument or completes the securities transaction.

The time when the obligation to withhold stamp tax on securities transactions arises is the day on which the securities transaction is completed.

Article 16 Stamp tax is levied on a quarterly, annual, or per-occurrence basis. If levied on a quarterly or annual basis, the taxpayer shall declare and pay the tax within fifteen days from the end of the quarter or year; if levied on a per-occurrence basis, the taxpayer shall declare and pay the tax within fifteen days from the date the tax obligation arises.

Stamp tax on securities transactions is settled on a weekly basis. The withholding agent for stamp tax on securities transactions shall declare and pay the tax and bank settlement interest within five days from the end of each week.

Article 17 Stamp tax may be paid by affixing stamp tax stamps or by other tax payment certificates issued by the tax authority in accordance with the law.

If stamp tax stamps are affixed to a taxable instrument, the taxpayer shall cancel or obliterate them by stamping or drawing lines across the perforations of each stamp.

Stamp tax stamps are produced under the supervision of the tax competent authority under the State Council.

Article 18 Stamp tax shall be levied and managed by the tax authority in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection."

Article 19 Taxpayers, withholding agents, and tax authorities and their staff who violate the provisions of this Law shall be held legally liable in accordance with the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection" and relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 20 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2022. The "Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Stamp Tax" promulgated by the State Council on August 6, 1988 shall be simultaneously abolished.
