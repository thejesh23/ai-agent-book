# Agricultural Product Quality Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on April 29, 2006

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure the quality and safety of agricultural products, safeguard public health, and promote the development of agriculture and the rural economy.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "agricultural products" means primary products derived from agriculture, i.e., plants, animals, microorganisms, and their products obtained through agricultural activities.

For the purposes of this Law, "quality and safety of agricultural products" means that the quality of agricultural products meets the requirements for ensuring human health and safety.

Article 3 The administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the quality and safety of agricultural products. The relevant departments under the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, be responsible for the relevant work concerning the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 4 The people's government at or above the county level shall incorporate the work of ensuring the quality and safety of agricultural products into the national economic and social development plan at the corresponding level, and allocate funds for the quality and safety of agricultural products to carry out such work.

Article 5 The local people's government at or above the county level shall exercise unified leadership and coordination over the work of ensuring the quality and safety of agricultural products within its administrative region, and shall take measures to establish and improve the service system for the quality and safety of agricultural products, so as to raise the level of quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 6 The administrative department for agriculture under the State Council shall establish a committee of experts on risk assessment for the quality and safety of agricultural products, composed of experts from relevant fields, to conduct risk analysis and assessment of potential hazards that may affect the quality and safety of agricultural products.

The administrative department for agriculture under the State Council shall take corresponding management measures based on the results of the risk assessment for the quality and safety of agricultural products, and shall promptly notify the relevant departments under the State Council of such results.

Article 7 The administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and the administrative departments for agriculture under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, within the scope of their respective functions and powers, release information on the status of the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 8 The State shall guide and promote the standardized production of agricultural products, encourage and support the production of high-quality agricultural products, and prohibit the production and sale of agricultural products that do not meet the national standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 9 The State shall support scientific and technological research on the quality and safety of agricultural products, promote scientific methods of quality and safety management, and disseminate advanced and safe production technologies.

Article 10 The people's governments at all levels and the relevant departments shall strengthen the publicity of knowledge on the quality and safety of agricultural products, raise public awareness of the quality and safety of agricultural products, guide producers and sellers of agricultural products to strengthen quality and safety management, and ensure the safety of consumption of agricultural products.

## Chapter II Standards for the Quality and Safety of Agricultural Products

Article 11 The State shall establish and improve a system of standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products. The standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products are mandatory technical specifications.

The formulation and issuance of standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 12 In formulating standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products, full consideration shall be given to the results of risk assessment for the quality and safety of agricultural products, and opinions of producers, sellers, and consumers of agricultural products shall be solicited to ensure consumption safety.

Article 13 Standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall be revised in a timely manner in light of the level of scientific and technological development and the need for the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 14 The implementation of standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall be organized by the administrative department for agriculture in consultation with the relevant departments.

## Chapter III Production Areas of Agricultural Products

Article 15 If the administrative department for agriculture under the local people's government at or above the county level, in accordance with the requirements for ensuring the quality and safety of agricultural products and based on factors such as the characteristics of agricultural product varieties and the status of toxic and hazardous substances in the atmosphere, soil, and water bodies of the production area, deems an area unsuitable for the production of specific agricultural products, it shall propose the area as a prohibited production area, and submit it to the people's government at the corresponding level for approval and publication. The specific measures shall be formulated by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council in consultation with the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council.

The adjustment of prohibited production areas for agricultural products shall be handled in accordance with the procedures prescribed in the preceding paragraph.

Article 16 The people's government at or above the county level shall take measures to strengthen the construction of agricultural product bases and improve the production conditions for agricultural products.

The administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level shall take measures to promote the construction of standardized production comprehensive demonstration zones, demonstration farms, breeding communities, and areas free from specified animal and plant diseases that ensure the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 17 It is prohibited to produce, catch, or collect edible agricultural products, or to establish agricultural product production bases, in areas where toxic and hazardous substances exceed the prescribed standards.

Article 18 It is prohibited to discharge or dump wastewater, waste gas, solid waste, or other toxic and hazardous substances into agricultural product production areas in violation of the provisions of laws and regulations.

Water used for agricultural production and solid waste used as fertilizer shall meet the standards prescribed by the State.

Article 19 Producers of agricultural products shall use chemical products such as fertilizers, pesticides, veterinary drugs, and agricultural films rationally, and prevent pollution of agricultural product production areas.

## Chapter IV Production of Agricultural Products

Article 20 The administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and the administrative departments for agriculture under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate production technical requirements and operating procedures to ensure the quality and safety of agricultural products. The administrative departments for agriculture under the people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen guidance on the production of agricultural products.

Article 21 A licensing system shall be implemented for pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed and feed additives, fertilizers, and veterinary instruments that may affect the quality and safety of agricultural products, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

The administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and the administrative departments for agriculture under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall regularly conduct supervisory inspections of agricultural inputs such as pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed and feed additives, and fertilizers that may endanger the quality and safety of agricultural products, and shall publish the inspection results.

Article 22 The administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen the management and guidance of the use of agricultural inputs, and establish and improve a system for the safe use of agricultural inputs.

Article 23 Agricultural scientific research and educational institutions and agricultural technology extension institutions shall strengthen training for producers of agricultural products on knowledge and skills related to quality and safety.

Article 24 Agricultural product production enterprises and specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations shall establish production records for agricultural products, truthfully recording the following items:

(1) The name, source, method of use, dosage, and dates of use and discontinuation of agricultural inputs used;

(2) The occurrence and control of animal diseases, plant diseases, and insect and weed pests;

(3) The dates of harvesting, slaughtering, or catching.

Production records for agricultural products shall be kept for two years. It is prohibited to forge production records for agricultural products.

The State encourages other producers of agricultural products to establish production records for agricultural products.

Article 25 Producers of agricultural products shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council, use agricultural inputs rationally, strictly comply with the provisions on safety intervals or withdrawal periods for the use of agricultural inputs, and prevent endangering the quality and safety of agricultural products.

It is prohibited to use agricultural inputs that are explicitly prohibited by the State during the production of agricultural products.

Article 26 Agricultural product production enterprises and specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations shall conduct testing of the quality and safety status of agricultural products by themselves or by entrusting testing institutions; agricultural products that, upon testing, do not meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall not be sold.

Article 27 Specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations and agricultural product industry associations shall promptly provide production technical services to their members, establish management systems for the quality and safety of agricultural products, improve control systems for the quality and safety of agricultural products, and strengthen self-discipline management.

## Chapter V Packaging and Labeling of Agricultural Products

Article 28 Agricultural products sold by agricultural product production enterprises, specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations, and units or individuals engaged in the purchase of agricultural products shall, if packaging or labeling is required by regulations, be packaged or labeled before sale. The packaging or label shall, in accordance with regulations, indicate the product name, place of origin, producer, production date, shelf life, product quality grade, and other contents; if additives are used, the names of the additives shall also be indicated in accordance with regulations. The specific measures shall be formulated by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council.

Article 29 Materials such as preservatives, antiseptics, and additives used in the packaging, preservation, storage, and transportation of agricultural products shall comply with the relevant mandatory technical specifications of the State.

Article 30 Agricultural products that are agricultural genetically modified organisms shall be labeled in accordance with the relevant provisions on the safety management of agricultural genetically modified organisms.

Article 31 Animals, plants, and their products that require quarantine according to law shall be accompanied by quarantine conformity marks and quarantine conformity certificates.

Article 32 Agricultural products sold must meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products. Producers may apply to use the pollution-free agricultural product mark. If the quality of agricultural products meets the relevant national standards for high-quality agricultural products, producers may apply to use the corresponding agricultural product quality mark.

It is prohibited to fraudulently use the agricultural product quality marks prescribed in the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 33 Agricultural products in any of the following circumstances shall not be sold:

(1) Containing pesticides, veterinary drugs, or other chemical substances prohibited by the State;

(2) Having residues of chemical substances such as pesticides and veterinary drugs, or containing heavy metals or other toxic and hazardous substances that do not meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products;

(3) Containing pathogenic parasites, microorganisms, or biotoxins that do not meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products;

(4) Using materials such as preservatives, antiseptics, and additives that do not comply with the relevant mandatory technical specifications of the State;

(5) Otherwise not meeting the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 34 The State shall establish a monitoring system for the quality and safety of agricultural products. The administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the requirements for ensuring the quality and safety of agricultural products, formulate and organize the implementation of monitoring plans for the quality and safety of agricultural products, and conduct supervisory inspections of agricultural products during production or on the market. The results of supervisory inspections shall be published by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council or the administrative department for agriculture under the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within the scope of their authority.

Supervisory inspection and testing shall be entrusted to an agricultural product quality and safety testing institution that meets the conditions prescribed in Article 35 of this Law. No fees shall be charged to the inspected party, and the quantity of samples drawn shall not exceed the amount prescribed by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council. For agricultural products that have been subject to supervisory inspection by a higher-level administrative department for agriculture, the lower-level administrative department for agriculture shall not conduct a duplicate inspection.

Article 35 Testing for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall make full use of existing qualified testing institutions.

Institutions engaged in testing the quality and safety of agricultural products must possess the corresponding testing conditions and capabilities, and shall be assessed as qualified by the administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the provincial level or by a department authorized by it. The specific measures shall be formulated by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council.

Agricultural product quality and safety testing institutions shall, according to law, obtain metrological certification.

Article 36 If a producer or seller of agricultural products disagrees with the results of a supervisory inspection and testing, it may, within five days from the date of receiving the test results, apply for a re-test to the administrative department for agriculture that organized the supervisory inspection of the quality and safety of agricultural products or to its higher-level administrative department for agriculture.

If a rapid testing method recognized by the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments is used for supervisory inspection and testing of the quality and safety of agricultural products, and the inspected party disagrees with the test results, it may apply for a re-test within four hours from the time of receiving the test results. Rapid testing methods shall not be used for the re-test.

If damage is caused to a party due to erroneous test results, compensation shall be made according to law.

Article 37 Agricultural product wholesale markets shall establish or entrust agricultural product quality and safety testing institutions to conduct random testing of the quality and safety status of agricultural products sold in the market; if agricultural products are found not to meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products, the seller shall be required to immediately stop selling them, and the matter shall be reported to the administrative department for agriculture.

Agricultural product sales enterprises shall establish and improve a system for inspection and acceptance of incoming goods for the agricultural products they sell; agricultural products that, upon inspection, do not meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products shall not be sold.

Article 38 The State encourages units and individuals to exercise social supervision over the quality and safety of agricultural products. Any unit or individual has the right to report, expose, and accuse any act in violation of this Law. After receiving relevant reports, exposures, and accusations, the relevant departments shall handle them promptly.

Article 39 The administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level may, during supervision and inspection of the quality and safety of agricultural products, conduct on-site inspections of agricultural products being produced or sold, investigate and understand relevant information concerning the quality and safety of agricultural products, and consult and copy records and other materials related to the quality and safety of agricultural products; it has the power to seal up or seize agricultural products that, upon testing, do not meet the standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 40 When an accident concerning the quality and safety of agricultural products occurs, the relevant units and individuals shall take control measures and promptly report to the local township people's government and the administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at the county level; the authority receiving the report shall handle the matter promptly and report to the people's government at the next higher level and the relevant departments. When a major accident concerning the quality and safety of agricultural products occurs, the administrative department for agriculture shall promptly notify the market supervision and administration department at the same level.

Article 41 If, during supervision and administration of the quality and safety of agricultural products, the administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level discovers agricultural products falling under any of the circumstances listed in Article 33 of this Law, it shall, in accordance with the requirements of the accountability system for the quality and safety of agricultural products, identify the responsible person and handle the matter according to law or make recommendations for handling.

Article 42 Imported agricultural products must be inspected in accordance with the national standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products; if relevant standards for the quality and safety of agricultural products have not yet been formulated, they shall be formulated promptly according to law, and before their formulation, inspection may be carried out with reference to the relevant foreign standards designated by the relevant departments of the State.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 43 If a supervisor or administrator of the quality and safety of agricultural products fails to perform supervisory duties according to law, or abuses his or her power, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 44 If an agricultural product quality and safety testing institution forges test results, it shall be ordered to make corrections, its illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, its testing qualification shall be revoked; if damage is caused, compensation shall be made according to law.

If an agricultural product quality and safety testing institution issues inaccurate test results, causing damage, it shall make compensation according to law; if major damage is caused, its testing qualification shall also be revoked.

Article 45 If, in violation of the provisions of laws and regulations, wastewater, waste gas, solid waste, or other toxic and hazardous substances are discharged or dumped into agricultural product production areas, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of relevant environmental protection laws and regulations; if damage is caused, compensation shall be made according to law.

Article 46 If the use of agricultural inputs violates the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the administrative department for agriculture under the State Council, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 47 If an agricultural product production enterprise or specialized farmer cooperative economic organization fails to establish or fails to preserve production records for agricultural products in accordance with regulations, or forges production records for agricultural products, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 48 If, in violation of the provisions of Article 28 of this Law, agricultural products sold are not packaged or labeled in accordance with regulations, the seller shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 49 If, under the circumstances provided for in Item (4) of Article 33 of this Law, materials such as preservatives, antiseptics, and additives used do not comply with the relevant mandatory technical specifications of the State, the sale shall be ordered to stop, the contaminated agricultural products shall be rendered harmless, and those that cannot be rendered harmless shall be destroyed under supervision; the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 50 If agricultural products sold by an agricultural product production enterprise or specialized farmer cooperative economic organization fall under any of the circumstances listed in Items (1) to (3) or Item (5) of Article 33 of this Law, the sale shall be ordered to stop, the agricultural products already sold shall be recovered, the illegally sold agricultural products shall be rendered harmless or destroyed under supervision; the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If agricultural products sold by an agricultural product sales enterprise fall under the circumstances listed in the preceding paragraph, the matter shall be handled and penalties imposed in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

If agricultural products sold in an agricultural product wholesale market fall under the circumstances listed in the first paragraph, the illegally sold agricultural products shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph, and the seller of agricultural products shall be penalized in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph.

If an agricultural product wholesale market violates the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 37 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 51 If, in violation of the provisions of Article 32 of this Law, an agricultural product quality mark is fraudulently used, the offender shall be ordered to make corrections, its illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 52 The handling and penalties provided for in Article 44, Articles 47 to 49, the first and fourth paragraphs of Article 50, and Article 51 of this Law shall be decided by the administrative department for agriculture under the people's government at or above the county level; the handling and penalties provided for in the second and third paragraphs of Article 50 of this Law shall be decided by the market supervision and administration department.

If other provisions are made by law regarding administrative penalties and the penalizing authority, those provisions shall prevail. However, no duplicate penalty shall be imposed for the same illegal act.

Article 53 If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 54 If the production or sale of agricultural products listed in Article 33 of this Law causes damage to consumers, compensation shall be made according to law.

If agricultural products sold in an agricultural product wholesale market fall under the circumstances prescribed in the preceding paragraph, consumers may claim compensation from the agricultural product wholesale market; if the responsibility lies with the producer or seller, the agricultural product wholesale market has the right to recover the compensation. Consumers may also directly claim compensation from the producer or seller of the agricultural products.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 55 The management of pig slaughtering shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 56 This Law shall come into effect as of November 1, 2006.
