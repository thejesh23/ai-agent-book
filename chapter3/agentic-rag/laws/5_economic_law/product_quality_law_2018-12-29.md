# Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on February 22, 1993

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on July 8, 2000

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen the supervision and administration of product quality, improve product quality levels, clarify product quality responsibilities, protect the legitimate rights and interests of consumers, and maintain the social and economic order.

Article 2 Any entity or individual engaged in product production and sales activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China must comply with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "products" refer to products that are processed and manufactured for sale.

Construction projects are not subject to the provisions of this Law; however, building materials, building components and parts, and equipment used in construction projects that fall within the scope of products as defined in the preceding paragraph shall be governed by this Law.

Article 3 Producers and sellers shall establish and improve internal product quality management systems, strictly implement job quality standards, quality responsibilities, and corresponding assessment methods.

Article 4 Producers and sellers shall bear product quality responsibilities in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 5 It is prohibited to forge or fraudulently use quality marks such as certification marks; it is prohibited to forge the origin of products, or forge or fraudulently use the factory names or addresses of others; it is prohibited to adulterate or misrepresent products in production or sales, passing off fake as genuine, or shoddy as good.

Article 6 The State encourages the promotion of scientific quality management methods and the adoption of advanced science and technology, and encourages enterprises to achieve product quality that meets or exceeds industry standards, national standards, and international standards.

Awards shall be given to units and individuals that have achieved outstanding results in advanced product quality management and product quality reaching internationally advanced levels.

Article 7 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate the improvement of product quality into national economic and social development plans, strengthen overall planning, organization, and leadership of product quality work, guide and urge producers and sellers to strengthen product quality management and improve product quality, organize relevant departments to take measures in accordance with the law to stop acts violating the provisions of this Law in product production and sales, and ensure the implementation of this Law.

Article 8 The market supervision and administration department of the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision of product quality nationwide. The relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for product quality supervision within their respective functions and duties.

The market supervision and administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for product quality supervision within their respective administrative regions. The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for product quality supervision within their respective functions and duties.

Where laws provide otherwise regarding the department responsible for product quality supervision, the provisions of those laws shall prevail.

Article 9 Personnel of people's governments at all levels and other state organs shall not abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, cover up or condone acts violating the provisions of this Law in product production and sales occurring within their regions or systems, or obstruct or interfere with the investigation and punishment of such acts in accordance with the law.

Where local people's governments at all levels and other state organs cover up or condone acts violating the provisions of this Law in product production and sales, the legal responsibility of their principal responsible persons shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 10 Any unit or individual has the right to report acts violating the provisions of this Law to the market supervision and administration department or other relevant departments.

The market supervision and administration department and relevant departments shall keep the confidentiality of the whistleblower and provide rewards in accordance with the regulations of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 11 No unit or individual may exclude quality-qualified products produced by enterprises outside their region or system from entering their region or system.

## Chapter II Supervision of Product Quality

Article 12 Product quality shall be inspected and found qualified; unqualified products shall not be passed off as qualified products.

Article 13 Industrial products that may endanger human health or the safety of persons or property must comply with national standards or industry standards for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property; where no such national standards or industry standards have been formulated, they must meet the requirements for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property.

It is prohibited to produce or sell industrial products that do not meet the standards and requirements for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 14 The State promotes an enterprise quality system certification system based on internationally accepted quality management standards. Enterprises may, on a voluntary basis, apply for enterprise quality system certification to certification bodies recognized by the market supervision and administration department of the State Council or by departments authorized by it. Upon passing the certification, the certification body shall issue an enterprise quality system certification certificate.

The State promotes a product quality certification system with reference to internationally advanced product standards and technical requirements. Enterprises may, on a voluntary basis, apply for product quality certification to certification bodies recognized by the market supervision and administration department of the State Council or by departments authorized by it. Upon passing the certification, the certification body shall issue a product quality certification certificate and permit the enterprise to use the product quality certification mark on the product or its packaging.

Article 15 The State implements a supervision and inspection system for product quality, primarily through spot checks, focusing on products that may endanger human health or the safety of persons or property, important industrial products affecting the national economy and people's livelihood, and products about which consumers or relevant organizations have reported quality issues. Samples for spot checks shall be randomly selected from products on the market or from products awaiting sale in the finished product warehouses of enterprises. The supervision and spot-check work shall be planned and organized by the market supervision and administration department of the State Council. Market supervision and administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level may also organize supervision spot checks within their respective administrative regions. Where laws provide otherwise regarding the supervision and inspection of product quality, the provisions of those laws shall prevail.

For products subject to national supervision spot checks, local authorities shall not conduct duplicate spot checks; for products subject to supervision spot checks by higher-level authorities, lower-level authorities shall not conduct duplicate spot checks.

Based on the needs of supervision spot checks, products may be tested. The quantity of samples drawn for testing shall not exceed the reasonable needs of the testing, and no testing fees shall be charged to the inspected party. The testing expenses required for supervision spot checks shall be allocated in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

If producers or sellers object to the results of a spot-check test, they may, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the test results, apply for a re-test to the market supervision and administration department that conducted the supervision spot check or its superior department. The market supervision and administration department accepting the re-test application shall make a re-test conclusion.

Article 16 Producers and sellers shall not refuse product quality supervision and inspections conducted in accordance with the law.

Article 17 If the quality of products subject to supervision spot checks in accordance with this Law is found to be unqualified, the market supervision and administration department that conducted the supervision spot check shall order the producers or sellers to rectify within a specified period. If they fail to rectify within the specified period, the market supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall make a public announcement; if, after the announcement, a re-inspection still finds the products unqualified, the enterprise shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification within a specified period; if, after the rectification period, a re-inspection still finds the product quality unqualified, the business license shall be revoked.

If the products subject to supervision spot checks have serious quality problems, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of Chapter V of this Law.

Article 18 When investigating and punishing acts suspected of violating the provisions of this Law based on obtained evidence of suspected violations or reports, the market supervision and administration departments at or above the county level may exercise the following powers:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of the premises where the parties are suspected of engaging in production and sales activities violating this Law;

(2) Investigate and inquire with the legal representatives, principal responsible persons, and other relevant personnel of the parties about the circumstances related to the suspected production and sales activities violating this Law;

(3) Consult and copy the parties' relevant contracts, invoices, account books, and other relevant materials;

(4) Seal up or seize products that are reasonably believed not to meet the national standards or industry standards for ensuring human health and the safety of persons or property, or that have other serious quality problems, as well as the raw materials, auxiliary materials, packaging materials, and production tools directly used for the production and sale of such products.

Article 19 Product quality inspection institutions must possess the corresponding testing conditions and capabilities and may undertake product quality inspection work only after passing the assessment by the market supervision and administration department of the people's government at or above the provincial level or by a department authorized by it. Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise regarding product quality inspection institutions, the provisions of those laws or administrative regulations shall prevail.

Article 20 Social intermediary institutions engaged in product quality inspection and certification must be established in accordance with the law and shall not have subordinate relationships or other interests with administrative organs or other state organs.

Article 21 Product quality inspection institutions and certification institutions must, in accordance with relevant standards, objectively and impartially issue inspection results or certification certificates.

Product quality certification institutions shall, in accordance with state regulations, conduct follow-up inspections on products permitted to use certification marks; if products using certification marks do not meet the certification standards, they shall require correction; if the circumstances are serious, they shall revoke the qualification to use the certification marks.

Article 22 Consumers have the right to inquire with producers or sellers about product quality issues and to lodge complaints with the market supervision and administration department and relevant departments; the department receiving the complaint shall be responsible for handling it.

Article 23 Social organizations protecting consumer rights and interests may suggest that relevant departments handle product quality issues reported by consumers and support consumers in filing lawsuits in the People's Courts for damages caused by product quality.

Article 24 The market supervision and administration departments of the State Council and of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall regularly publish announcements on the quality status of products subject to their supervision spot checks.

Article 25 Market supervision and administration departments or other state organs, as well as product quality inspection institutions, shall not recommend producers' products to the public; nor shall they participate in product business activities through means such as supervising production or sales.

## Chapter III Product Quality Responsibilities and Obligations of Producers and Sellers

### Section 1 Product Quality Responsibilities and Obligations of Producers

Article 26 Producers shall be responsible for the quality of the products they produce.

Product quality shall meet the following requirements:

(1) There shall be no unreasonable risks endangering the safety of persons or property; where there are national standards or industry standards for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property, the products shall comply with those standards;

(2) The products shall possess the performance characteristics they ought to have, except where defects in performance have been explained;

(3) The products shall conform to the product standards indicated on the product or its packaging, and to the quality status indicated by means such as product descriptions or physical samples.

Article 27 The markings on products or their packaging must be truthful and meet the following requirements:

(1) There shall be a certificate of product quality inspection qualification;

(2) There shall be the product name, the name and address of the manufacturer, all marked in Chinese;

(3) Based on the characteristics and usage requirements of the product, if it is necessary to indicate the product specifications, grade, and the names and contents of main ingredients, these shall be correspondingly indicated in Chinese; if consumers need to be informed in advance, this shall be indicated on the outer packaging or relevant information shall be provided to consumers in advance;

(4) For products with a limited use period, the date of manufacture, safe use period, or expiry date shall be clearly marked in a prominent position;

(5) For products that are easily damaged or may endanger the safety of persons or property due to improper use, there shall be warning marks or warning instructions in Chinese.

Naked food products and other naked products that are difficult to mark due to their characteristics may be sold without product markings.

Article 28 For dangerous goods such as fragile, flammable, explosive, toxic, corrosive, or radioactive items, as well as products that cannot be inverted during storage and transportation and other products with special requirements, the packaging quality must meet the corresponding requirements, and warning marks or warning instructions in Chinese shall be provided in accordance with relevant state regulations, indicating storage and transportation precautions.

Article 29 Producers shall not produce products that have been explicitly eliminated by the State.

Article 30 Producers shall not forge the origin of products, nor forge or fraudulently use the factory names or addresses of others.

Article 31 Producers shall not forge or fraudulently use quality marks such as certification marks.

Article 32 When producing products, producers shall not adulterate or misrepresent, pass off fake as genuine, or shoddy as good, nor pass off unqualified products as qualified products.

### Section 2 Product Quality Responsibilities and Obligations of Sellers

Article 33 Sellers shall establish and implement a system for inspecting and accepting incoming goods, verifying product qualification certificates and other markings.

Article 34 Sellers shall take measures to maintain the quality of the products they sell.

Article 35 Sellers shall not sell products that have been explicitly eliminated and ordered to cease sales by the State, nor expired or deteriorated products.

Article 36 The markings on products sold by sellers shall comply with the provisions of Article 27 of this Law.

Article 37 Sellers shall not forge the origin of products, nor forge or fraudulently use the factory names or addresses of others.

Article 38 Sellers shall not forge or fraudulently use quality marks such as certification marks.

Article 39 When selling products, sellers shall not adulterate or misrepresent, pass off fake as genuine, or shoddy as good, nor pass off unqualified products as qualified products.

## Chapter IV Compensation for Damages

Article 40 If a sold product falls under any of the following circumstances, the seller shall be responsible for repair, replacement, or refund; if losses are caused to the consumer who purchased the product, the seller shall compensate for the losses:

(1) The product lacks the performance characteristics it ought to have, and this was not explained in advance;

(2) The product does not conform to the product standards indicated on the product or its packaging;

(3) The product does not conform to the quality status indicated by means such as product descriptions or physical samples.

After the seller has undertaken repair, replacement, refund, or compensation for losses in accordance with the preceding paragraph, if the responsibility lies with the producer or with another seller (hereinafter referred to as the supplier) who supplied the product to the seller, the seller has the right to recover from the producer or supplier.

If the seller fails to provide repair, replacement, refund, or compensation for losses in accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph, the market supervision and administration department shall order correction.

If the sales contracts or work contracts concluded between producers, between sellers, or between producers and sellers contain different agreements, the parties to the contract shall perform in accordance with the contract terms.

Article 41 If personal injury or damage to property other than the defective product itself (hereinafter referred to as other's property) is caused by a product defect, the producer shall bear liability for compensation.

The producer shall not bear liability for compensation if it can prove any of the following circumstances:

(1) The product was not placed into circulation;

(2) The defect causing the damage did not exist at the time the product was placed into circulation;

(3) The state of scientific and technological knowledge at the time the product was placed into circulation was not such as to enable the existence of the defect to be discovered.

Article 42 If a product defect exists due to the fault of the seller, causing personal injury or damage to other's property, the seller shall bear liability for compensation.

If the seller cannot identify the producer of the defective product nor the supplier of the defective product, the seller shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 43 If personal injury or damage to other's property is caused by a product defect, the victim may claim compensation from the producer of the product or from the seller of the product. If the responsibility lies with the producer and the seller has compensated, the seller has the right to recover from the producer. If the responsibility lies with the seller and the producer has compensated, the producer has the right to recover from the seller.

Article 44 If personal injury is caused to the victim by a product defect, the infringer shall compensate for medical expenses, nursing expenses during treatment, reduced income due to missed work, and other related expenses; if disability is caused, the infringer shall also pay for self-help devices for the disabled, living subsidies, disability compensation, and the necessary living expenses for persons supported by the disabled; if the death of the victim is caused, the infringer shall also pay for funeral expenses, death compensation, and the necessary living expenses for persons supported by the deceased during their lifetime.

If property damage is caused to the victim by a product defect, the infringer shall restore the property to its original condition or compensate for its value. If the victim suffers other significant losses as a result, the infringer shall compensate for those losses.

Article 45 The statute of limitations for claiming compensation for damage caused by a product defect is two years, calculated from the date on which the party knew or should have known that their rights and interests were infringed.

The right to claim compensation for damage caused by a product defect is extinguished ten years after the defective product causing the damage was delivered to the first consumer, unless the expressly stated safe use period has not yet expired.

Article 46 For the purposes of this Law, "defect" means an unreasonable risk existing in a product that endangers the safety of persons or other's property; where there are national standards or industry standards for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property, it means non-compliance with those standards.

Article 47 When a civil dispute arises over product quality, the parties may resolve it through negotiation or mediation. If the parties are unwilling to resolve it through negotiation or mediation, or if negotiation or mediation fails, they may apply for arbitration to an arbitration institution based on an agreement between the parties; if the parties have not reached an arbitration agreement or the arbitration agreement is invalid, they may directly file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

Article 48 An arbitration institution or the People's Court may entrust a product quality inspection institution as specified in Article 19 of this Law to conduct inspections on the relevant product quality.

## Chapter V Penalty Provisions

Article 49 For producing or selling products that do not meet the national standards or industry standards for ensuring human health and the safety of persons and property, the production and sale shall be ordered to cease, the illegally produced or sold products shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than the equivalent value but not more than three times the value of the illegally produced or sold products (including products already sold and unsold, the same below) shall be imposed; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 50 For adulterating or misrepresenting products, passing off fake as genuine, shoddy as good, or passing off unqualified products as qualified products, the production and sale shall be ordered to cease, the illegally produced or sold products shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 50% but not more than three times the value of the illegally produced or sold products shall be imposed; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.Article 51 Any person who produces products that have been eliminated by the state, or sells products that have been eliminated and whose sale has been stopped by the state, shall be ordered to stop production or sale, and the products illegally produced or sold shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than the equivalent value of the products illegally produced or sold shall be imposed; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 52 Any person who sells products that have become invalid or deteriorated shall be ordered to stop selling, and the products illegally sold shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than twice the value of the products illegally sold shall be imposed; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Any person who forges the place of origin of a product, forges or fraudulently uses the name or address of another manufacturer, or forges or fraudulently uses certification marks or other quality marks, shall be ordered to make corrections, and the products illegally produced or sold shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than the equivalent value of the products illegally produced or sold shall be imposed; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 54 If the product identification does not comply with the provisions of Article 27 of this Law, the person shall be ordered to make corrections; if the identification of packaged products does not comply with the provisions of Items (4) and (5) of Article 27 of this Law, and the circumstances are serious, the person shall be ordered to stop production or sale, and a fine of not more than 30% of the value of the products illegally produced or sold shall be imposed; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated.

Article 55 If a seller sells products whose sale is prohibited under Articles 49 to 53 of this Law, and has sufficient evidence to prove that he did not know that the products were prohibited from sale and truthfully explains the source of the products, the punishment may be mitigated or reduced.

Article 56 Any person who refuses to accept the legally conducted supervision and inspection of product quality shall be given a warning and ordered to make corrections; if he refuses to make corrections, he shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification; if the circumstances are particularly serious, the business license shall be revoked.

Article 57 If a product quality inspection institution or certification institution forges inspection results or issues false certificates, it shall be ordered to make corrections, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the unit, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, its inspection or certification qualification shall be revoked; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

If the inspection results or certificates issued by a product quality inspection institution or certification institution are untrue and cause losses, it shall bear corresponding compensation liability; if major losses are caused, its inspection or certification qualification shall be revoked.

If a product quality certification institution violates the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 21 of this Law by failing to require correction or revoking the right to use certification marks for products that do not meet certification standards but use certification marks, it shall bear joint and several liability with the product's producer and seller for losses caused to consumers due to the product's non-compliance with certification standards; if the circumstances are serious, its certification qualification shall be revoked.

Article 58 If a social organization or intermediary institution makes commitments or guarantees regarding product quality, and the product does not meet the quality requirements of such commitments or guarantees, causing losses to consumers, it shall bear joint and several liability with the product's producer and seller.

Article 59 Any person who makes false propaganda about product quality in advertisements to deceive or mislead consumers shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 60 Raw and auxiliary materials, packaging materials, and production tools specifically used by a producer to produce the products listed in Articles 49 and 51 of this Law or to produce counterfeit products shall be confiscated.

Article 61 Any person who knows or should know that a product is prohibited from production or sale under this Law and provides convenience such as transportation, storage, or warehousing for it, or provides counterfeit production technology for counterfeit products, shall have all income from transportation, storage, warehousing, or provision of counterfeit production technology confiscated, and shall be fined not less than 50% but not more than three times the illegal income; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 62 If an operator in the service industry uses products whose sale is prohibited under Articles 49 to 52 of this Law for operational services, he shall be ordered to stop using them; if he knows or should know that the products used are prohibited from sale under this Law, he shall be punished in accordance with the provisions on punishment of sellers under this Law, based on the value of the illegally used products (including both used and unused products).

Article 63 Any person who conceals, transfers, sells, or damages items sealed or seized by the market supervision and administration department shall be fined not less than the equivalent value but not more than three times the value of the concealed, transferred, sold, or damaged items; if there is any illegal income, it shall also be confiscated.

Article 64 If a person who violates the provisions of this Law should bear civil compensation liability and pay fines or penalties, and his property is insufficient to pay both, the civil compensation liability shall be borne first.

Article 65 If any staff member of a people's government at any level or any other state organ commits any of the following acts, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Covering up or indulging acts that violate the provisions of this Law in the production or sale of products;

(2) Leaking information to parties engaged in production or sales activities that violate the provisions of this Law, helping them evade investigation and punishment;

(3) Obstructing or interfering with the market supervision and administration department's lawful investigation and punishment of acts that violate the provisions of this Law in the production or sale of products, causing serious consequences.

Article 66 If the market supervision and administration department, during random inspections of product quality supervision, takes more samples than the prescribed quantity or charges inspection fees from the inspected party, the superior market supervision and administration department or the supervisory authority shall order the return of such fees; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

Article 67 If the market supervision and administration department or any other state organ, in violation of the provisions of Article 25 of this Law, recommends a producer's products to the public or participates in product business activities through methods such as supervision of production or supervision of sales, the superior organ or the supervisory authority shall order it to make corrections and eliminate the impact, and any illegal income shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

If a product quality inspection institution commits any of the illegal acts listed in the preceding paragraph, the market supervision and administration department shall order it to make corrections and eliminate the impact, and any illegal income shall be confiscated, and a fine of not more than one time the illegal income may also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, its quality inspection qualification shall be revoked.

Article 68 If a staff member of the market supervision and administration department abuses his power, neglects his duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, and the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

Article 69 Any person who obstructs a staff member of the market supervision and administration department from lawfully performing his duties by means of violence or threats shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if the obstruction or refusal does not involve violence or threats, the public security organ shall impose punishment in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

Article 70 The administrative penalties provided for in Articles 49 to 57 and Articles 60 to 63 of this Law shall be determined by the market supervision and administration department. If laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise regarding the authority to impose administrative penalties, the provisions of such laws or administrative regulations shall prevail.

Article 71 Products confiscated in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall be destroyed or otherwise disposed of in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state.

Article 72 The value of products referred to in Articles 49 to 54, Article 62, and Article 63 of this Law shall be calculated based on the marked price of the illegally produced or sold products; if there is no marked price, it shall be calculated based on the market price of similar products.

## Chapter 6 Supplementary Provisions

Article 73 Measures for the supervision and management of the quality of military products shall be separately formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

If laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise regarding liability for damages caused by nuclear facilities or nuclear products, their provisions shall prevail.

Article 74 This Law shall come into effect as of September 1, 1993.
