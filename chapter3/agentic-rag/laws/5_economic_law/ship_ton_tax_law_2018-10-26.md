# People's Republic of China Ship Tonnage Tax Law

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 27, 2017

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws Including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

Article 1 Vessels entering domestic ports from ports outside the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as taxable vessels) shall pay ship tonnage tax (hereinafter referred to as tonnage tax) in accordance with this Law.

Article 2 The tax items and tax rates of tonnage tax shall be implemented in accordance with the Tonnage Tax Items and Tax Rates Table attached to this Law.

Article 3 Tonnage tax shall have preferential tax rates and ordinary tax rates.

Taxable vessels of the nationality of the People's Republic of China, and taxable vessels whose flag state (region) has concluded a treaty or agreement with the People's Republic of China containing provisions granting each other most-favored-nation treatment in respect of ship taxes and fees, shall be subject to preferential tax rates.

Other taxable vessels shall be subject to ordinary tax rates.

Article 4 Tonnage tax shall be levied based on the net tonnage of the vessel and the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate.

When declaring and paying tax each time, the responsible person of a taxable vessel may choose to apply for a tonnage tax certificate with a validity period in accordance with the Tonnage Tax Items and Tax Rates Table.

Article 5 The amount of tonnage tax payable shall be calculated by multiplying the net tonnage of the vessel by the applicable tax rate.

Article 6 Tonnage tax shall be collected by the Customs. The Customs shall issue a payment receipt when collecting tonnage tax.

After the responsible person of a taxable vessel pays the tonnage tax or provides a guarantee, the Customs shall issue a tonnage tax certificate in accordance with the validity period applied for.

Article 7 When a taxable vessel goes through entry formalities upon entering a port, it shall declare tax to the Customs and obtain a tonnage tax certificate, or submit the tonnage tax certificate for inspection (or apply for verification of the electronic information of the tonnage tax certificate). When a taxable vessel goes through exit formalities upon leaving a port, it shall submit the tonnage tax certificate for inspection (or apply for verification of the electronic information of the tonnage tax certificate).

When applying for a tonnage tax certificate, the responsible person of a taxable vessel shall provide the following documents to the Customs:

(1) The vessel's nationality certificate or the receipt for the deposit of the vessel's nationality certificate issued by the maritime authority;

(2) The vessel's tonnage certificate.

If a taxable vessel is forced to anchor at a place without a Customs office due to force majeure, the responsible person shall immediately report to the nearest Customs office and, after the cause of force majeure is eliminated, declare tax to the Customs in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 8 The obligation to pay tonnage tax arises on the date when the taxable vessel enters the port.

If a taxable vessel has not left the port after the expiration of the tonnage tax certificate, it shall apply for a new tonnage tax certificate and pay the tonnage tax continuously from the day following the expiration of the previous certificate.

Article 9 The following vessels shall be exempted from tonnage tax:

(1) Vessels with a tax payable of less than RMB 50 yuan;

(2) Empty vessels entering a port for the first time after acquiring ownership through purchase, donation, inheritance, etc., from abroad;

(3) Vessels that do not embark or disembark passengers or load or unload cargo within 24 hours after the expiration of the tonnage tax certificate;

(4) Non-motorized vessels (excluding non-motorized barges);

(5) Fishing vessels and fishing boats;

(6) Vessels taking refuge, undergoing epidemic prevention isolation, repairs, modifications, ceasing operations, or being dismantled, and which do not embark or disembark passengers or load or unload cargo;

(7) Vessels exclusively used or requisitioned by the military or the armed police forces;

(8) Police vessels;

(9) Vessels of foreign embassies and consulates in China, representative offices of international organizations in China, and their relevant personnel, which are entitled to tax exemption according to legal provisions;

(10) Other vessels as prescribed by the State Council.

The tax exemption provisions in item (10) of the preceding paragraph shall be reported by the State Council to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

Article 10 Within the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate, if a taxable vessel falls under any of the following circumstances, the Customs shall endorse an extension of the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate based on the actual number of days:

(1) Taking refuge, undergoing epidemic prevention isolation, repairs, or modifications, and not embarking or disembarking passengers or loading or unloading cargo;

(2) Requisitioned by the military or the armed police forces.

Article 11 Vessels that meet the provisions of items (5) to (9) of paragraph 1 of Article 9 and Article 10 of this Law shall provide legally effective certification documents or documents proving the relationship of use issued by the maritime authority, the fishery administration department, or other relevant departments or institutions, stating the basis and reasons for the tax exemption or extension of the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate.

Article 12 The responsible person of a taxable vessel shall pay the tax in full within 15 days from the date the Customs issues the tonnage tax payment receipt. If the tax is not paid in full within the prescribed period, a late payment surcharge of 0.05% of the overdue tax shall be imposed daily from the date the tax becomes overdue until the date the tax is paid in full.

Article 13 If a taxable vessel, before arriving at the port, obtains Customs approval to make an advance declaration and complete entry and exit formalities, the responsible person shall provide the Customs with a guarantee commensurate with the lawful performance of the tonnage tax payment obligation; after the taxable vessel arrives at the port, it shall declare tax to the Customs in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

The following property or rights may be used as a guarantee:

(1) RMB or freely convertible currencies;

(2) Drafts, promissory notes, checks, bonds, and certificates of deposit;

(3) Letters of guarantee from banks or non-bank financial institutions;

(4) Other property or rights recognized by the Customs in accordance with the law.

Article 14 If the net tonnage of a taxable vessel changes due to repairs or modifications within the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate, the tonnage tax certificate shall remain valid. When the taxable vessel goes through entry or exit formalities, it shall provide certification documents for the repairs or modifications.

Article 15 If the applicable tax rate for a taxable vessel changes due to adjustments in tax items or tax rates or a change in flag within the validity period of the tonnage tax certificate, the tonnage tax certificate shall remain valid.

If the applicable tax rate changes due to a change in flag, the taxable vessel shall provide certification documents for the change in flag when going through entry or exit formalities.

Article 16 If a tonnage tax certificate is damaged or lost before its expiration, the responsible person shall apply in writing to the Customs office that originally issued the certificate for a duplicate of the tonnage tax certificate, and no additional tax shall be levied.

Article 17 If the Customs discovers that a tax has been under-collected or omitted, it shall collect the additional tax within one year from the date the tax was due. However, if the under-collection or omission is caused by a violation of regulations by the taxable vessel, the Customs may recover the tax within three years from the date the tax was due and shall impose a late payment surcharge of 0.05% of the under-collected or omitted tax per day from the date the tax was due.

If the Customs discovers that a tax has been over-collected, it shall notify the taxable vessel to go through the refund procedures within 24 hours and shall add interest calculated at the current demand deposit rate of the bank for the same period.

If a taxable vessel discovers that it has overpaid tax, it may, within three years from the date of payment, request in writing that the Customs refund the overpaid tax plus interest calculated at the current demand deposit rate of the bank for the same period; the Customs shall verify the facts within 30 days from the date of receiving the refund application and notify the taxable vessel to go through the refund procedures.

The taxable vessel shall go through the relevant refund procedures within three months from the date of receiving the notice specified in paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Article.

Article 18 If a taxable vessel commits any of the following acts, the Customs shall order it to rectify within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than RMB 2,000 yuan but not more than RMB 30,000 yuan; if the tax payable is not paid or is underpaid, a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of tax not paid or underpaid shall be imposed, provided that the fine shall not be less than RMB 2,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to declare tax and obtain a tonnage tax certificate in accordance with regulations;

(2) Failing to submit the tonnage tax certificate for inspection (or apply for verification of the electronic information of the tonnage tax certificate) and provide other certification documents in accordance with regulations.

Article 19 Tonnage tax, late payment surcharges, and fines shall be calculated in RMB.

Article 20 Matters concerning the collection of tonnage tax not provided for in this Law shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on tax collection and administration.

Article 21 For the purposes of this Law and the attached Tonnage Tax Items and Tax Rates Table, the following terms have the meanings specified:

Net tonnage means the net tonnage indicated on the vessel tonnage certificate issued or authorized to be issued by the government of the flag state (region).

Non-motorized vessels means vessels that have no power device of their own and are driven by external forces.

Non-motorized barges means non-motorized vessels registered as barges with the vessel registration authority.

Fishing vessels and fishing boats means vessels registered as fishing vessels or fishing boats with the fishery administration department of the People's Republic of China.

Tugboats means specialized operational vessels used exclusively for towing (or pushing) transport vessels.

Validity period of the tonnage tax certificate means the period calculated in accordance with the calendar year or day.

Article 22 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2018. The Interim Regulation of the People's Republic of China on Ship Tonnage Tax promulgated by the State Council on December 5, 2011 shall be simultaneously repealed.
