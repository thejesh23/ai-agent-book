# Environmental Protection Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 25, 2016

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting and improving the environment, reducing pollutant emissions, and promoting ecological progress.

**Article 2** Enterprises, public institutions, and other business operators that directly discharge taxable pollutants into the environment within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be taxpayers of environmental protection tax and shall pay environmental protection tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 3** For the purposes of this Law, "taxable pollutants" means the air pollutants, water pollutants, solid waste, and noise specified in the "Environmental Protection Tax Items and Tax Amount Table" and the "Table of Taxable Pollutants and Pollution Equivalent Values" attached to this Law.

**Article 4** Under any of the following circumstances, the discharge shall not be deemed as direct discharge of pollutants into the environment, and no environmental protection tax shall be paid for the corresponding pollutants:

(1) Enterprises, public institutions, and other business operators discharge taxable pollutants to centralized sewage treatment facilities or centralized domestic waste treatment facilities established according to law;

(2) Enterprises, public institutions, and other business operators store or dispose of solid waste in facilities or sites that meet the national and local environmental protection standards.

**Article 5** Centralized urban and rural sewage treatment facilities and centralized domestic waste treatment facilities established according to law that discharge taxable pollutants into the environment exceeding the national or local discharge standards shall pay environmental protection tax.

Enterprises, public institutions, and other business operators that store or dispose of solid waste in a manner not meeting the national or local environmental protection standards shall pay environmental protection tax.

**Article 6** The tax items and tax amounts of environmental protection tax shall be implemented in accordance with the "Environmental Protection Tax Items and Tax Amount Table" attached to this Law.

The determination and adjustment of the specific applicable tax amounts for taxable air pollutants and water pollutants shall be proposed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, taking into comprehensive consideration the regional environmental carrying capacity, the current status of pollutant emissions, and the requirements of economic, social, and ecological development goals, within the tax amount range specified in the "Environmental Protection Tax Items and Tax Amount Table" attached to this Law. The proposal shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

## Chapter II Tax Basis and Tax Payable

**Article 7** The tax basis for taxable pollutants shall be determined according to the following methods:

(1) For taxable air pollutants, it shall be determined based on the number of pollution equivalents converted from the pollutant discharge amount;

(2) For taxable water pollutants, it shall be determined based on the number of pollution equivalents converted from the pollutant discharge amount;

(3) For taxable solid waste, it shall be determined based on the discharge amount of solid waste;

(4) For taxable noise, it shall be determined based on the decibel level exceeding the national standard.

**Article 8** The number of pollution equivalents for taxable air pollutants and water pollutants shall be calculated by dividing the discharge amount of the pollutant by the pollution equivalent value of that pollutant. The specific pollution equivalent values for each type of taxable air pollutant and water pollutant shall be implemented in accordance with the "Table of Taxable Pollutants and Pollution Equivalent Values" attached to this Law.

**Article 9** For taxable air pollutants discharged from each discharge outlet or from a location without a discharge outlet, environmental protection tax shall be levied on the top three pollutants sorted in descending order of the number of pollution equivalents.

For taxable water pollutants discharged from each discharge outlet, in accordance with the "Table of Taxable Pollutants and Pollution Equivalent Values" attached to this Law, a distinction shall be made between Category I water pollutants and other categories of water pollutants. Sorted in descending order of the number of pollution equivalents, environmental protection tax shall be levied on the top five Category I water pollutants and on the top three other categories of water pollutants.

The people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, based on the special needs of pollutant emission reduction in its region, increase the number of taxable pollutant items for which environmental protection tax is levied at the same discharge outlet. This shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

**Article 10** The discharge amount of taxable air pollutants, water pollutants, and solid waste, and the decibel level of noise, shall be calculated according to the following methods and order of priority:

(1) If the taxpayer has installed and uses automatic pollutant monitoring equipment that complies with national regulations and monitoring specifications, the calculation shall be based on the data from the automatic pollutant monitoring equipment;

(2) If the taxpayer has not installed or used automatic pollutant monitoring equipment, the calculation shall be based on the monitoring data issued by a monitoring institution that complies with relevant national regulations and monitoring specifications;

(3) If monitoring conditions are not available due to the variety of pollutants discharged or other reasons, the calculation shall be based on the pollutant discharge coefficients or material balance calculation methods prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council;

(4) If the calculation cannot be made according to the methods specified in items (1) to (3) of this Article, the calculation shall be assessed and determined based on the sampling and estimation methods prescribed by the competent department of ecological environment under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 11** The amount of environmental protection tax payable shall be calculated according to the following methods:

(1) The tax payable for taxable air pollutants shall be the number of pollution equivalents multiplied by the specific applicable tax amount;

(2) The tax payable for taxable water pollutants shall be the number of pollution equivalents multiplied by the specific applicable tax amount;

(3) The tax payable for taxable solid waste shall be the discharge amount of solid waste multiplied by the specific applicable tax amount;

(4) The tax payable for taxable noise shall be the specific applicable tax amount corresponding to the decibel level exceeding the national standard.

## Chapter III Tax Reduction and Exemption

**Article 12** Environmental protection tax shall be temporarily exempted under the following circumstances:

(1) Discharge of taxable pollutants from agricultural production (excluding large-scale livestock and poultry farming);

(2) Discharge of taxable pollutants from mobile pollution sources such as motor vehicles, railway locomotives, non-road mobile machinery, ships, and aircraft;

(3) Discharge of corresponding taxable pollutants from centralized urban and rural sewage treatment facilities and centralized domestic waste treatment facilities established according to law, provided that the discharge does not exceed the national or local discharge standards;

(4) Comprehensive utilization of solid waste by the taxpayer that meets the national or local environmental protection standards;

(5) Other circumstances approved by the State Council for tax exemption.

The tax exemption provisions in item (5) of the preceding paragraph shall be reported by the State Council to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

**Article 13** If the concentration value of taxable air pollutants or water pollutants discharged by a taxpayer is less than 30% of the national or local pollutant discharge standard, the environmental protection tax shall be levied at a reduced rate of 75%. If the concentration value of taxable air pollutants or water pollutants discharged by a taxpayer is less than 50% of the national or local pollutant discharge standard, the environmental protection tax shall be levied at a reduced rate of 50%.

## Chapter IV Collection and Administration

**Article 14** Environmental protection tax shall be collected and administered by the tax authorities in accordance with the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection" and the relevant provisions of this Law.

The competent departments of ecological environment shall be responsible for the monitoring and management of pollutants in accordance with this Law and relevant environmental protection laws and regulations.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a mechanism for the division of labor and cooperation among tax authorities, competent departments of ecological environment, and other relevant units to strengthen the collection and administration of environmental protection tax and ensure the timely and full payment of tax into the state treasury.

**Article 15** The competent departments of ecological environment and the tax authorities shall establish a platform for sharing tax-related information and a mechanism for work coordination.

The competent departments of ecological environment shall regularly provide the tax authorities with environmental protection-related information such as pollutant discharge permits, pollutant discharge data, environmental violations, and administrative penalties of pollutant-discharging units.

The tax authorities shall regularly provide the competent departments of ecological environment with tax-related information on environmental protection tax, such as taxpayers' tax returns, tax payments, tax reductions and exemptions, tax arrears, and risk points.

**Article 16** The time when the tax liability arises shall be the day on which the taxpayer discharges the taxable pollutants.

**Article 17** The taxpayer shall declare and pay environmental protection tax to the tax authority in the place where the taxable pollutants are discharged.

**Article 18** Environmental protection tax shall be calculated on a monthly basis and declared and paid on a quarterly basis. If it cannot be calculated and paid on a fixed periodic basis, it may be declared and paid on a per-incident basis.

When declaring and paying the tax, the taxpayer shall submit to the tax authority the types and quantities of taxable pollutants discharged, the concentration values of air pollutants and water pollutants, and other tax-related materials required by the tax authority based on actual needs.

**Article 19** If a taxpayer declares and pays tax on a quarterly basis, it shall complete the tax declaration and pay the tax to the tax authority within fifteen days after the end of the quarter. If a taxpayer declares and pays tax on a per-incident basis, it shall complete the tax declaration and pay the tax to the tax authority within fifteen days after the date on which the tax liability arises.

The taxpayer shall truthfully complete the tax declaration in accordance with the law and shall bear responsibility for the authenticity and completeness of the declaration.

**Article 20** The tax authority shall compare the taxpayer's tax declaration data with the relevant data provided by the competent department of ecological environment.

If the tax authority finds that the taxpayer's tax declaration data is abnormal or that the taxpayer has failed to complete the tax declaration within the prescribed time limit, it may request the competent department of ecological environment to conduct a review. The competent department of ecological environment shall issue a review opinion to the tax authority within fifteen days after receiving the data from the tax authority. The tax authority shall adjust the taxpayer's tax payable in accordance with the data reviewed by the competent department of ecological environment.

**Article 21** Where the discharge amount of pollutants is assessed and determined in accordance with the provisions of item (4) of Article 10 of this Law, the tax authority, together with the competent department of ecological environment, shall assess and determine the types and quantities of pollutants discharged and the amount of tax payable.

**Article 22** The specific measures for taxpayers engaged in marine engineering projects to declare and pay environmental protection tax for discharging taxable air pollutants, water pollutants, or solid waste into the sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be prescribed by the competent tax department of the State Council jointly with the competent department of ecological environment of the State Council.

**Article 23** Taxpayers, tax authorities, competent departments of ecological environment, and their staff who violate the provisions of this Law shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the "Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection", the "Environmental Protection Law of the People's Republic of China", and relevant laws and regulations.

**Article 24** People's governments at all levels shall encourage taxpayers to increase investment in environmental protection construction and provide financial and policy support for taxpayers' investment in automatic pollutant monitoring equipment.

## Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

**Article 25** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Pollution equivalent" refers to a comprehensive indicator or unit of measurement used to assess the degree of environmental pollution caused by different pollutants, based on the degree of harm of the pollutant or pollution discharge activity to the environment and the technical and economic feasibility of treatment. Different pollutants with the same pollution equivalent value in the same medium have substantially equivalent pollution levels.

(2) "Pollutant discharge coefficient" refers to the statistical average of the amount of pollutants expected to be discharged per unit of product produced under normal technical, economic, and management conditions.

(3) "Material balance calculation" refers to a method of estimating the raw materials used, products produced, and waste generated during the production process based on the principle of conservation of mass.

**Article 26** Enterprises, public institutions, and other business operators that directly discharge taxable pollutants into the environment shall, in addition to paying environmental protection tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law, bear liability for the damage caused in accordance with the law.

**Article 27** From the date this Law comes into effect, environmental protection tax shall be levied in accordance with the provisions of this Law, and the collection of pollutant discharge fees shall cease.

**Article 28** This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2018.
