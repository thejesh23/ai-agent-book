# Deed Tax Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on August 11, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 Units and individuals that transfer land and housing ownership within the territory of the People's Republic of China and the recipients thereof shall be taxpayers of deed tax and shall pay deed tax in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "transfer of land and housing ownership" refers to the following acts:

(1) Grant of land use rights;

(2) Transfer of land use rights, including sale, gift, and exchange;

(3) Sale, gift, and exchange of housing.

The transfer of land use rights referred to in item (2) of the preceding paragraph does not include the transfer of land contractual management rights and land management rights.

Where land and housing ownership is transferred by means of investment (as capital contribution) with appraised value, debt repayment, transfer, or reward, deed tax shall be levied in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 3 The deed tax rate shall be three percent to five percent.

The specific applicable deed tax rate shall be proposed by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within the rate range specified in the preceding paragraph, submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, in accordance with the procedures specified in the preceding paragraph, determine differentiated tax rates for the transfer of ownership of different types of housing among different entities and in different regions.

Article 4 The tax basis for deed tax shall be:

(1) For the grant or sale of land use rights and the sale of housing, the transaction price determined in the contract for the transfer of land and housing ownership, including the monetary consideration payable and the consideration corresponding to physical objects and other economic benefits;

(2) For the exchange of land use rights or housing, the difference in price between the exchanged land use rights or housing;

(3) For the gift of land use rights or housing, and other acts of transferring land and housing ownership without a price, the price verified by the tax authority in accordance with the law by reference to the market price for the sale of land use rights or housing.

Where the transaction price or the price difference for exchange declared by the taxpayer is obviously low and without justifiable reasons, the tax authority shall verify it in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection.

Article 5 The amount of deed tax payable shall be calculated by multiplying the tax basis by the specific applicable tax rate.

Article 6 Deed tax shall be exempted under any of the following circumstances:

(1) State organs, public institutions, social organizations, and military units acquire land and housing ownership for use in offices, education, medical care, scientific research, and military facilities;

(2) Non-profit schools, medical institutions, and social welfare institutions acquire land and housing ownership for use in offices, education, medical care, scientific research, elderly care, and relief;

(3) Acquisition of land use rights of barren hills, barren land, and barren beaches for agricultural, forestry, animal husbandry, and fishery production;

(4) Changes in land and housing ownership between spouses during the existence of the marriage relationship;

(5) Legal heirs acquire land and housing ownership through inheritance;

(6) Foreign embassies, consulates, and representative offices of international organizations in China that are entitled to tax exemption in accordance with the law acquire land and housing ownership.

In light of the needs of national economic and social development, the State Council may provide for the exemption or reduction of deed tax for circumstances such as ensuring residential housing needs of residents, enterprise restructuring and reorganization, and post-disaster reconstruction, and shall report to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

Article 7 Provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may decide to exempt or reduce deed tax under the following circumstances:

(1) Re-acquisition of land and housing ownership due to the expropriation or requisition of land and housing by the people's government at or above the county level;

(2) Re-acquisition of housing ownership due to the loss of housing caused by force majeure.

The specific measures for the exemption or reduction of deed tax under the preceding paragraph shall be proposed by the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the same level for decision, and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the State Council for recordation.

Article 8 Where a taxpayer changes the use of relevant land or housing, or otherwise no longer falls under the circumstances for exemption or reduction of deed tax as specified in Article 6 of this Law, the taxpayer shall pay the tax that has been exempted or reduced.

Article 9 The time when the obligation to pay deed tax arises shall be the date on which the taxpayer signs the contract for the transfer of land and housing ownership, or the date on which the taxpayer obtains other documents of a contractual nature for the transfer of land and housing ownership.

Article 10 Taxpayers shall declare and pay deed tax before completing the registration procedures for land and housing ownership in accordance with the law.

Article 11 After the taxpayer has fulfilled the tax payment obligations, the tax authority shall issue a deed tax payment certificate. When the taxpayer handles the registration of land and housing ownership, the real estate registration authority shall examine the deed tax payment certificate, tax reduction or exemption certificate, or relevant information. If the deed tax has not been paid in accordance with the provisions, the real estate registration authority shall not handle the registration of land and housing ownership.

Article 12 Before the registration of land and housing ownership is completed in accordance with the law, if the contract for the transfer of ownership or the document of a contractual nature for the transfer of ownership becomes invalid, is void, is revoked, or is rescinded, the taxpayer may apply to the tax authority for a refund of the tax paid, and the tax authority shall handle it in accordance with the law.

Article 13 Tax authorities shall establish a mechanism for sharing deed tax-related information and cooperating with relevant departments. Departments such as natural resources, housing and urban-rural development, civil affairs, and public security shall promptly provide tax authorities with information related to the transfer of land and housing ownership and assist tax authorities in strengthening the collection and management of deed tax.

Tax authorities and their staff shall keep confidential the personal information of taxpayers learned in the course of tax collection and management in accordance with the law and shall not disclose or illegally provide it to others.

Article 14 Deed tax shall be collected and managed by the tax authority of the place where the land or housing is located in accordance with the provisions of this Law and the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection.

Article 15 Taxpayers, tax authorities, and their staff who violate the provisions of this Law shall be investigated for legal liability in accordance with the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Collection and relevant laws and regulations.

Article 16 This Law shall come into force on September 1, 2021. The Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Deed Tax promulgated by the State Council on July 7, 1997 shall be repealed simultaneously.
