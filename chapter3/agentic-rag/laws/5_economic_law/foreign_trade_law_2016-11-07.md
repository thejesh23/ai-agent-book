# Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 12, 1994

Revised at the 8th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on April 6, 2004

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to expanding opening-up, developing foreign trade, maintaining foreign trade order, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of foreign trade operators, and promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to foreign trade and the protection of intellectual property rights related to foreign trade.

For the purposes of this Law, "foreign trade" refers to the import and export of goods and technologies and international trade in services.

Article 3 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council shall be in charge of the administration of foreign trade nationwide in accordance with this Law.

Article 4 The State shall practice a unified foreign trade system, encourage the development of foreign trade, and maintain a fair and free foreign trade order.

Article 5 The People's Republic of China shall, on the principle of equality and mutual benefit, promote and develop trade relations with other countries and regions, conclude or accede to regional economic and trade agreements such as tariff union agreements and free trade area agreements, and participate in regional economic organizations.

Article 6 The People's Republic of China shall, in respect of foreign trade, grant the most-favored-nation treatment and national treatment to other parties to or participants in international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or grant the most-favored-nation treatment and national treatment to the other party on the principle of reciprocity and mutual benefit.

Article 7 Where any country or region adopts discriminatory prohibitions, restrictions or other similar measures against the People's Republic of China in respect of trade, the People's Republic of China may, in light of the actual circumstances, take corresponding measures against the said country or region.

## Chapter II Foreign Trade Operators

Article 8 For the purposes of this Law, "foreign trade operators" refer to legal persons, other organizations or individuals that have completed industrial and commercial registration or other practice procedures and engage in foreign trade operations in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 9 Foreign trade operators engaged in the import or export of goods or technologies shall register with the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or its entrusted agency, except where registration is not required as provided for by laws, administrative regulations, or the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council. The specific measures for registration shall be formulated by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council.

Where a foreign trade operator fails to complete the registration as required, the Customs shall not handle the customs declaration and release procedures for the import or export of goods.

Article 10 Those engaged in international trade in services shall comply with the provisions of this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Entities engaged in foreign labor cooperation shall possess corresponding qualifications. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 11 The State may implement state trading administration for the import and export of certain goods. The import and export of goods subject to state trading administration may only be conducted by authorized enterprises, except where the State allows a portion of the import and export of such goods to be conducted by non-authorized enterprises.

The catalogues of goods subject to state trading administration and the list of authorized enterprises shall be determined, adjusted and published by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council in conjunction with other relevant departments under the State Council.

Where goods subject to state trading administration are imported or exported without authorization in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, the Customs shall not release them.

Article 12 Foreign trade operators may, upon entrustment by others, handle foreign trade business on their behalf within their business scope.

Article 13 Foreign trade operators shall, in accordance with the provisions made by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or other relevant departments under the State Council, submit to the relevant departments documents and materials related to their foreign trade operations. The relevant departments shall keep confidential the business secrets of the providers.

## Chapter III Import and Export of Goods and Technologies

Article 14 The State permits the free import and export of goods and technologies, except where otherwise provided for by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 15 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may, based on the need to monitor the import and export situation, implement automatic import and export licensing for certain freely importable and exportable goods and publish their catalogues.

For goods subject to automatic import or export licensing, where the consignee or consignor applies for automatic licensing before going through the customs declaration procedures, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or its entrusted agency shall grant the license; where the automatic licensing procedures are not completed, the Customs shall not release the goods.

For the import or export of technologies that are freely importable or exportable, a contract registration shall be completed with the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or its entrusted agency.

Article 16 The State may restrict or prohibit the import or export of relevant goods or technologies based on the following reasons:

(1) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit import or export for the maintenance of national security, social public interests, or public morality;

(2) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit import or export for the protection of human health or safety, the life or health of animals and plants, or the environment;

(3) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit import or export for the implementation of measures related to the import or export of gold or silver;

(4) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit export due to domestic supply shortages or for the effective protection of exhaustible natural resources;

(5) It is necessary to restrict export due to the limited market capacity of the destination country or region;

(6) It is necessary to restrict export due to severe disorder in the export operation order;

(7) It is necessary to restrict import for the establishment or accelerated establishment of a specific domestic industry;

(8) It is necessary to restrict the import of any form of agricultural, pastoral, or fishery products;

(9) It is necessary to restrict import to safeguard the State's international financial position and balance of payments;

(10) Other situations where restriction or prohibition of import or export is necessary as provided for by laws and administrative regulations;

(11) Other situations where restriction or prohibition of import or export is necessary in accordance with the provisions of international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by China.

Article 17 The State may take any necessary measures in relation to the import and export of goods and technologies related to fissionable or fusionable materials or materials derived therefrom, as well as the import and export related to weapons, ammunition, or other military supplies, to safeguard national security.

In time of war or for the maintenance of international peace and security, the State may take any necessary measures in respect of the import and export of goods and technologies.

Article 18 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council, in conjunction with other relevant departments under the State Council, shall formulate, adjust and publish catalogues of goods and technologies subject to restriction or prohibition of import or export in accordance with Articles 16 and 17 of this Law.

The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council, or jointly with other relevant departments under the State Council, with the approval of the State Council, may, within the scope provided for in Articles 16 and 17 of this Law, temporarily decide to restrict or prohibit the import or export of specific goods or technologies not listed in the catalogues mentioned in the preceding paragraph.

Article 19 The State implements quota and license management for goods subject to restricted import or export, and license management for technologies subject to restricted import or export.

Goods and technologies subject to quota and license management may only be imported or exported upon permission by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or jointly with other relevant departments under the State Council in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

The State may implement tariff quota management for certain imported goods.

Article 20 Import and export goods quotas and tariff quotas shall be allocated by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or other relevant departments under the State Council within their respective responsibilities, in accordance with the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality and efficiency. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 21 The State implements a unified commodity conformity assessment system, and conducts certification, inspection and quarantine of import and export commodities in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 22 The State administers the origin of import and export goods. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 23 Where other laws and administrative regulations have provisions prohibiting or restricting the import or export of cultural relics, wild animals and plants and their products, etc., those provisions shall prevail.

## Chapter IV International Trade in Services

Article 24 The People's Republic of China shall, in respect of international trade in services, grant market access and national treatment to other parties to or participants in international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, in accordance with the commitments made therein.

Article 25 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council shall administer international trade in services in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 26 The State may restrict or prohibit relevant international trade in services based on the following reasons:

(1) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit for the maintenance of national security, social public interests, or public morality;

(2) It is necessary to restrict or prohibit for the protection of human health or safety, the life or health of animals and plants, or the environment;

(3) It is necessary to restrict for the establishment or accelerated establishment of a specific domestic service industry;

(4) It is necessary to restrict to safeguard the State's balance of foreign exchange payments;

(5) Other situations where restriction or prohibition is necessary as provided for by laws and administrative regulations;

(6) Other situations where restriction or prohibition is necessary in accordance with the provisions of international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by China.

Article 27 The State may take any necessary measures in relation to international trade in services related to military affairs, as well as international trade in services related to fissionable or fusionable materials or materials derived therefrom, to safeguard national security.

In time of war or for the maintenance of international peace and security, the State may take any necessary measures in respect of international trade in services.

Article 28 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council, in conjunction with other relevant departments under the State Council, shall formulate, adjust and publish the catalogue of market access for international trade in services in accordance with Articles 26 and 27 of this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter V Protection of Intellectual Property Rights Related to Foreign Trade

Article 29 The State shall protect intellectual property rights related to foreign trade in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on intellectual property rights.

Where imported goods infringe intellectual property rights and harm the foreign trade order, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may take measures such as prohibiting the import of relevant goods produced or sold by the infringer within a certain period.

Article 30 Where an intellectual property right holder engages in any of the following acts, such as preventing the licensee from challenging the validity of the intellectual property rights in the license contract, imposing compulsory package licensing, or stipulating exclusive grant-back conditions in the license contract, and such acts harm the fair competition order in foreign trade, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may take necessary measures to eliminate the harm.

Article 31 Where any other country or region fails to grant national treatment to legal persons, other organizations or individuals of the People's Republic of China in respect of intellectual property protection, or fails to provide adequate and effective protection of intellectual property rights for goods, technologies or services originating from the People's Republic of China, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations, and in accordance with the international treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, take necessary measures in respect of trade with the said country or region.

## Chapter VI Foreign Trade Order

Article 32 In foreign trade operations, no monopoly acts in violation of the provisions of relevant anti-monopoly laws and administrative regulations shall be committed.

Where monopoly acts are committed in foreign trade operations, harming fair market competition, they shall be dealt with in accordance with the relevant anti-monopoly laws and administrative regulations.

Where the illegal acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph also harm the foreign trade order, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may take necessary measures to eliminate the harm.

Article 33 In foreign trade operations, no unfair competition acts such as selling goods at unfairly low prices, bid rigging, publishing false advertisements, or engaging in commercial bribery shall be committed.

Where unfair competition acts are committed in foreign trade operations, they shall be dealt with in accordance with the relevant anti-unfair competition laws and administrative regulations.

Where the illegal acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph also harm the foreign trade order, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may take measures such as prohibiting the operator from importing or exporting relevant goods or technologies to eliminate the harm.

Article 34 In foreign trade activities, the following acts shall not be committed:

(1) Forging or altering the origin marks of import or export goods, forging, altering or trading in origin certificates, import or export licenses, import or export quota certificates or other import or export certification documents;

(2) Defrauding export tax rebates;

(3) Smuggling;

(4) Evading certification, inspection or quarantine required by laws and administrative regulations;

(5) Other acts in violation of the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 35 Foreign trade operators shall, in their foreign trade operations, comply with the State's regulations on foreign exchange administration.

Article 36 Where any violation of the provisions of this Law harms the foreign trade order, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may make a public announcement.

## Chapter VII Foreign Trade Investigation

Article 37 In order to maintain the foreign trade order, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may, on its own or in conjunction with other relevant departments under the State Council, investigate the following matters in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations:

(1) The impact of the import and export of goods and technologies and international trade in services on domestic industries and their competitiveness;

(2) Trade barriers of relevant countries or regions;

(3) Matters that need to be investigated to determine whether anti-dumping, countervailing or safeguard measures or other foreign trade remedy measures should be taken in accordance with the law;

(4) Acts of circumventing foreign trade remedy measures;

(5) Matters concerning national security interests in foreign trade;

(6) Matters that need to be investigated for the implementation of the provisions of Article 7, the second paragraph of Article 29, Article 30, Article 31, the third paragraph of Article 32, and the third paragraph of Article 33 of this Law;

(7) Other matters affecting the foreign trade order that need to be investigated.

Article 38 The initiation of a foreign trade investigation shall be announced by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council.

The investigation may be conducted by means of written questionnaires, hearings, on-site investigations, entrusted investigations, etc.

The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council shall, based on the investigation results, produce an investigation report or make a ruling, and publish an announcement.

Article 39 Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate and assist in foreign trade investigations.

The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council, as well as their staff members, shall be obligated to keep confidential the State secrets and business secrets learned during foreign trade investigations.

## Chapter VIII Foreign Trade Remedies

Article 40 The State may take appropriate foreign trade remedy measures based on the results of foreign trade investigations.

Article 41 Where products from other countries or regions are dumped into the Chinese market at a price lower than their normal value, causing material injury or threat of material injury to an established domestic industry, or materially retarding the establishment of a domestic industry, the State may take anti-dumping measures to eliminate or mitigate such injury, threat of injury, or retardation.

Article 42 Where products from other countries or regions are exported to a third country market at a price lower than their normal value, causing material injury or threat of material injury to an established domestic industry in China, or materially retarding the establishment of a domestic industry in China, upon application by the domestic industry, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may consult with the government of the third country and request it to take appropriate measures.

Article 43 Where imported products directly or indirectly receive any form of specific subsidy from the exporting country or region, causing material injury or threat of material injury to an established domestic industry, or materially retarding the establishment of a domestic industry, the State may take countervailing measures to eliminate or mitigate such injury, threat of injury, or retardation.

Article 44 Where a substantial increase in the quantity of imported products causes serious injury or threat of serious injury to a domestic industry producing like products or directly competitive products, the State may take necessary safeguard measures to eliminate or mitigate such injury or threat of injury, and may provide necessary support to the industry.

Article 45 Where an increase in services provided by service suppliers from other countries or regions to China causes injury or threat of injury to a domestic industry providing like services or directly competitive services, the State may take necessary remedy measures to eliminate or mitigate such injury or threat of injury.

Article 46 Where a substantial increase in the quantity of a certain product entering the Chinese market due to import restrictions by a third country causes injury or threat of injury to an established domestic industry, or retards the establishment of a domestic industry, the State may take necessary remedy measures to restrict the import of such product.

Article 47 Where a country or region that has concluded or jointly acceded to an economic and trade treaty or agreement with the People's Republic of China violates the provisions of the treaty or agreement, causing the People's Republic of China to lose or suffer impairment of the benefits it is entitled to under the treaty or agreement, or obstructing the achievement of the objectives of the treaty or agreement, the government of the People's Republic of China shall have the right to request the government of the relevant country or region to take appropriate remedial measures, and may suspend or terminate the performance of relevant obligations in accordance with the relevant treaty or agreement.

Article 48 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws, conduct bilateral or multilateral consultations, negotiations and dispute settlement in foreign trade.

Article 49 The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council and other relevant departments under the State Council shall establish early warning and emergency response mechanisms for the import and export of goods and technologies and international trade in services, to respond to sudden and abnormal situations in foreign trade and safeguard national economic security.

Article 50 The State may take necessary anti-circumvention measures against acts that circumvent the foreign trade remedy measures provided for in this Law.

## Chapter IX Promotion of Foreign Trade

Article 51 The State shall formulate foreign trade development strategies and establish and improve foreign trade promotion mechanisms.

Article 52 The State shall, based on the needs of foreign trade development, establish and improve financial institutions serving foreign trade, and set up foreign trade development funds and risk funds.

Article 53 The State shall develop foreign trade through import and export credit, export credit insurance, export tax rebates, and other means of promoting foreign trade.**Article 54** The State shall establish a public information service system for foreign trade, providing information services to foreign trade operators and the general public.

**Article 55** The State shall take measures to encourage foreign trade operators to explore international markets and develop foreign trade through various forms such as outward investment, international project contracting, and international labor service cooperation.

**Article 56** Foreign trade operators may, in accordance with the law, establish and join relevant associations and chambers of commerce.

Such associations and chambers of commerce shall abide by laws and administrative regulations, provide their members with services related to production, marketing, information, and training in foreign trade in accordance with their articles of association, play a coordinating and self-regulatory role, file applications for foreign trade remedy measures in accordance with the law, safeguard the interests of their members and the industry, reflect to relevant government departments the suggestions of their members on foreign trade, and carry out activities to promote foreign trade.

**Article 57** China's international trade promotion organizations shall, in accordance with their articles of association, conduct external liaison, organize exhibitions, provide information and consulting services, and carry out other activities to promote foreign trade.

**Article 58** The State shall support and promote small and medium-sized enterprises in conducting foreign trade.

**Article 59** The State shall support and promote the development of foreign trade in national autonomous areas and economically underdeveloped regions.

## Chapter 10: Legal Liability

**Article 60** Anyone who, in violation of Article 11 of this Law, imports or exports goods subject to state trading management without authorization shall be subject to a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan imposed by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or other relevant departments under the State Council; if the circumstances are serious, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or other relevant departments under the State Council may, within three years from the date the administrative penalty decision takes effect, refuse to accept the application of the violator for engaging in the import or export of goods subject to state trading management, or revoke the authorization already granted to them for engaging in the import or export of other goods subject to state trading management.

**Article 61** The import or export of goods that are prohibited from import or export, or the import or export of goods that are restricted from import or export without a license, shall be dealt with and punished by the Customs in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

The import or export of technologies that are prohibited from import or export, or the import or export of technologies that are restricted from import or export without a license, shall be dealt with and punished in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations; if there are no provisions in laws or administrative regulations, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council shall order rectification, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

From the date the administrative penalty decision specified in the preceding two paragraphs takes effect or the criminal penalty judgment takes effect, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council or other relevant departments under the State Council may, within three years, refuse to accept the application of the violator for import or export quotas or licenses, or prohibit the violator from engaging in the import or export of relevant goods or technologies for a period of not less than one year but not more than three years.

**Article 62** Anyone who engages in international service trade that is prohibited, or engages in international service trade that is restricted without a license, shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations; if there are no provisions in laws or administrative regulations, the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council shall order rectification, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may prohibit the violator from engaging in relevant international service trade activities for a period of not less than one year but not more than three years from the date the administrative penalty decision specified in the preceding paragraph takes effect or the criminal penalty judgment takes effect.

**Article 63** Anyone who violates Article 34 of this Law shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

The competent department of foreign trade under the State Council may prohibit the violator from engaging in relevant foreign trade activities for a period of not less than one year but not more than three years from the date the administrative penalty decision specified in the preceding paragraph takes effect or the criminal penalty judgment takes effect.

**Article 64** For anyone prohibited from engaging in relevant foreign trade activities in accordance with Articles 61 through 63 of this Law, during the period of prohibition, the Customs shall, based on the prohibition decision made by the competent department of foreign trade under the State Council in accordance with the law, refuse to handle the customs declaration and inspection procedures for the relevant import or export goods of that foreign trade operator, and the foreign exchange administration department or designated foreign exchange banks shall refuse to handle the relevant settlement or sale of foreign exchange procedures.

**Article 65** If a staff member of the department responsible for the administration of foreign trade in accordance with this Law neglects their duty, engages in malpractice for personal gain, or abuses their power, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

If a staff member of the department responsible for the administration of foreign trade in accordance with this Law, taking advantage of their position, solicits property from others, or illegally accepts property from others to seek benefits for others, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

**Article 66** If a party involved in foreign trade activities is dissatisfied with a specific administrative act made by the department responsible for the administration of foreign trade in accordance with this Law, they may, in accordance with the law, apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit with the People's Court.

## Chapter 11: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 67** For the administration of foreign trade related to military products, fissionable and fusionable materials, or materials from which such materials are derived, as well as the administration of the import and export of cultural products, if laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

**Article 68** The State shall adopt flexible measures and grant preferential treatment and convenience for trade between border areas and adjacent border areas of neighboring countries, as well as for border market trade. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

**Article 69** This Law shall not apply to the separate customs territories of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 70** This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 2004.
