# Renewable Energy Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on February 28, 2005

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Renewable Energy Law of the People's Republic of China at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 26, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of promoting the development and utilization of renewable energy, increasing energy supply, improving the energy structure, ensuring energy security, protecting the environment, and achieving sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "renewable energy" refers to non-fossil energy sources such as wind energy, solar energy, hydropower, biomass energy, geothermal energy, and ocean energy.

The application of this Law to hydropower generation shall be stipulated by the energy administration department of the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval.

The direct combustion of straw, firewood, and manure through low-efficiency stoves is not subject to this Law.

Article 3 This Law applies to the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction.

Article 4 The State prioritizes the development and utilization of renewable energy in its energy development strategy, promotes the establishment and development of the renewable energy market by setting overall targets for the development and utilization of renewable energy and adopting corresponding measures.

The State encourages economic entities of all types of ownership to participate in the development and utilization of renewable energy and protects the legitimate rights and interests of those engaged in such activities in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The energy administration department of the State Council shall exercise unified management over the development and utilization of renewable energy nationwide. The relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for the management of the development and utilization of renewable energy within their respective scopes of duties.

The energy management departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the management of the development and utilization of renewable energy within their respective administrative regions. The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the management of the development and utilization of renewable energy within their respective scopes of duties.

## Chapter II Resource Survey and Development Plan

Article 6 The energy administration department of the State Council shall be responsible for organizing and coordinating the nationwide survey of renewable energy resources and shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, organize the formulation of technical specifications for resource surveys.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for the survey of relevant renewable energy resources within their respective scopes of duties, and the survey results shall be submitted to the energy administration department of the State Council for aggregation.

The results of the renewable energy resource survey shall be made public, except for content that is required to be kept confidential by State regulations.

Article 7 The energy administration department of the State Council shall, based on the national energy demand and the actual conditions of renewable energy resources, formulate the national medium- and long-term overall target for the development and utilization of renewable energy, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the State Council for approval and shall be made public.

The energy administration department of the State Council shall, based on the overall target specified in the preceding paragraph and the economic development and actual conditions of renewable energy resources in provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, determine, together with the people's governments of these regions, the medium- and long-term targets for the development and utilization of renewable energy in each administrative region, and shall make them public.

Article 8 The energy administration department of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate the national plan for the development and utilization of renewable energy based on the national medium- and long-term overall target for the development and utilization of renewable energy and the development status of renewable energy technologies, which shall be implemented after being submitted to the State Council for approval.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall formulate relevant plans conducive to achieving the national medium- and long-term overall target for the development and utilization of renewable energy.

The energy management departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, together with the relevant departments of the people's governments at the same level, formulate plans for the development and utilization of renewable energy in their respective administrative regions based on the national plan for the development and utilization of renewable energy and the medium- and long-term targets for the development and utilization of renewable energy in their respective administrative regions. These plans shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval, filed with the energy administration department of the State Council and the national electric power regulatory institution, and then organized for implementation.

Approved plans shall be made public, except for content that is required to be kept confidential by State regulations.

Any modification to an approved plan must be approved by the original approving authority.

Article 9 The formulation of plans for the development and utilization of renewable energy shall follow the principles of adapting to local conditions, overall planning, rational layout, and orderly development, and shall make comprehensive arrangements for the development and utilization of renewable energy sources such as wind energy, solar energy, hydropower, biomass energy, geothermal energy, and ocean energy. The plan shall include development targets, main tasks, regional layout, key projects, implementation schedule, supporting power grid construction, service systems, and safeguard measures.

The organizing and formulating authority shall solicit opinions from relevant units, experts, and the public and conduct scientific demonstration.

## Chapter III Industry Guidance and Technical Support

Article 10 The energy administration department of the State Council shall, based on the national plan for the development and utilization of renewable energy, formulate and publish a guiding catalogue for the development of the renewable energy industry.

Article 11 The standardization administration department of the State Council shall formulate and publish national technical standards for grid connection of renewable energy electricity and other national standards for renewable energy technologies and products that require unified technical requirements nationwide.

For technical requirements not covered by the national standards specified in the preceding paragraph, the relevant departments of the State Council may formulate relevant industry standards and file them with the standardization administration department of the State Council.

Article 12 The State shall prioritize scientific and technological research and industrial development related to the development and utilization of renewable energy in the fields of scientific and technological development and high-tech industry development, incorporate them into the national scientific and technological development plan and the high-tech industry development plan, and allocate funds to support scientific and technological research, application demonstration, and industrial development related to the development and utilization of renewable energy, thereby promoting technological progress in the development and utilization of renewable energy, reducing production costs of renewable energy products, and improving product quality.

The education administration department of the State Council shall incorporate knowledge and technology related to renewable energy into general education and vocational education curricula.

## Chapter IV Promotion and Application

Article 13 The State encourages and supports the grid-connected generation of renewable energy electricity.

The construction of grid-connected renewable energy power generation projects shall obtain administrative permits or be filed for record in accordance with laws and regulations of the State Council.

For the construction of grid-connected renewable energy power generation projects that require administrative permits, if multiple applicants apply for the same project permit, the permit shall be granted through bidding in accordance with the law.

Article 14 The State implements a system of full guaranteed purchase of electricity generated from renewable energy.

The energy administration department of the State Council shall, together with the national electric power regulatory institution and the finance department of the State Council, determine the proportion of electricity generated from renewable energy to total electricity generation that should be achieved during the planning period in accordance with the national plan for the development and utilization of renewable energy, formulate specific measures for grid enterprises to prioritize dispatching and fully purchase electricity generated from renewable energy, and the energy administration department of the State Council shall, together with the national electric power regulatory institution, supervise the implementation during the year.

Grid enterprises shall sign grid connection agreements with renewable energy power generation enterprises that are constructed in accordance with the plan for the development and utilization of renewable energy and have obtained administrative permits or been filed for record in accordance with the law, and shall fully purchase the on-grid electricity of grid-connected renewable energy power generation projects within their grid coverage that meet the grid connection technical standards. Power generation enterprises have the obligation to cooperate with grid enterprises to ensure grid safety.

Grid enterprises shall strengthen grid construction, expand the allocation scope of renewable energy electricity, develop and apply technologies such as smart grids and energy storage, improve grid operation and management, enhance the capacity to absorb renewable energy electricity, and provide grid connection services for renewable energy power generation.

Article 15 The State supports the construction of independent renewable energy power systems in areas not covered by the grid to provide electricity services for local production and living.

Article 16 The State encourages the clean and efficient development and utilization of biomass fuels and encourages the development of energy crops.

Gas and heat produced from biomass resources that meet the technical standards for access to urban gas pipeline networks and heat pipeline networks shall be accepted by enterprises operating such networks.

The State encourages the production and utilization of bio-liquid fuels. Petroleum sales enterprises shall, in accordance with the regulations of the energy administration department of the State Council or provincial people's governments, incorporate bio-liquid fuels that meet national standards into their fuel sales systems.

Article 17 The State encourages units and individuals to install and use solar energy utilization systems such as solar water heating systems, solar heating and cooling systems, and solar photovoltaic power generation systems.

The construction administration department of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate technical and economic policies and technical specifications for the integration of solar energy utilization systems with buildings.

Real estate development enterprises shall, in accordance with the technical specifications specified in the preceding paragraph, provide necessary conditions for solar energy utilization in the design and construction of buildings.

For existing buildings, occupants may install solar energy utilization systems that meet technical specifications and product standards without affecting the quality and safety of the buildings, unless otherwise agreed by the parties concerned.

Article 18 The State encourages and supports the development and utilization of renewable energy in rural areas.

The energy management departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, formulate plans for the development of renewable energy in rural areas based on local economic and social development, ecological protection, and comprehensive health management needs, and promote the application of technologies such as biogas and other biomass resource conversion, household solar energy, small-scale wind energy, and small-scale hydropower according to local conditions.

People's governments at or above the county level shall provide financial support for renewable energy utilization projects in rural areas.

## Chapter V Price Management and Cost Compensation

Article 19 The on-grid electricity price for renewable energy power generation projects shall be determined by the price administration department of the State Council based on the characteristics of different types of renewable energy power generation and the conditions of different regions, in accordance with the principles of promoting the development and utilization of renewable energy and economic rationality, and shall be adjusted in a timely manner according to the development of renewable energy technologies. The on-grid electricity price shall be made public.

The on-grid electricity price for renewable energy power generation projects subject to bidding in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3 of Article 13 of this Law shall be implemented at the price determined by the winning bid; however, it shall not exceed the on-grid electricity price level for similar renewable energy power generation projects determined in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 20 The difference between the costs incurred by grid enterprises in purchasing renewable energy electricity at the on-grid electricity price determined in accordance with Article 19 of this Law and the costs calculated based on the average on-grid electricity price of conventional energy power generation shall be compensated by a surcharge on renewable energy electricity prices levied on electricity sales nationwide.

Article 21 Reasonable grid connection costs and other reasonable related costs paid by grid enterprises for purchasing renewable energy electricity may be included in the transmission costs of grid enterprises and recovered from electricity sales prices.

Article 22 The sales electricity price for public independent renewable energy power systems invested in or subsidized by the State shall be implemented at the classified sales electricity price for the same region, and the portion of reasonable operation and management costs exceeding the sales electricity price shall be compensated in accordance with the provisions of Article 20 of this Law.

Article 23 The prices of renewable energy heat and gas entering urban pipeline networks shall be determined in accordance with the principles of promoting the development and utilization of renewable energy and economic rationality, based on the authority of price management.

## Chapter VI Economic Incentives and Supervision Measures

Article 24 The State finance shall establish a renewable energy development fund, with funding sources including special funds arranged annually by the State finance and income from surcharges on renewable energy electricity prices levied in accordance with the law.

The renewable energy development fund shall be used to compensate the difference costs specified in Articles 20 and 22 of this Law and to support the following matters:

(1) Scientific and technological research, standard formulation, and demonstration projects for the development and utilization of renewable energy;

(2) Renewable energy utilization projects in rural and pastoral areas;

(3) Construction of independent renewable energy power systems in remote areas and islands;

(4) Exploration and evaluation of renewable energy resources and construction of related information systems;

(5) Promotion of localized production of equipment for the development and utilization of renewable energy.

If grid enterprises cannot recover the grid connection costs and other related costs specified in Article 21 of this Law through electricity sales prices, they may apply for subsidies from the renewable energy development fund.

Specific measures for the collection, use, and management of the renewable energy development fund shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council together with the energy and price administration departments of the State Council.

Article 25 For renewable energy development and utilization projects listed in the national guiding catalogue for the development of the renewable energy industry and meeting credit conditions, financial institutions may provide preferential loans with financial interest subsidies.

Article 26 The State grants tax incentives to projects listed in the guiding catalogue for the development of the renewable energy industry. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 27 Electric power enterprises shall truthfully and completely record and preserve relevant data on renewable energy power generation and shall accept inspection and supervision by electric power regulatory institutions.

When conducting inspections, electric power regulatory institutions shall follow prescribed procedures and shall keep commercial secrets and other secrets of the inspected units confidential.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 28 If the energy administration department of the State Council, the energy management departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, or other relevant departments, in the supervision and management of the development and utilization of renewable energy, violate the provisions of this Law and commit any of the following acts, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at a higher level shall order correction, and the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to make administrative permit decisions in accordance with the law;

(2) Discovering illegal acts but failing to investigate and deal with them;

(3) Other acts of failing to perform supervision and management duties in accordance with the law.

Article 29 If a grid enterprise, in violation of the provisions of Article 14 of this Law, fails to complete the purchase of renewable energy electricity as required, causing economic losses to renewable energy power generation enterprises, it shall bear compensation liability, and the national electric power regulatory institution shall order correction within a specified period; if it refuses to correct, a fine of not more than one time the amount of economic losses of the renewable energy power generation enterprise shall be imposed.

Article 30 If an enterprise operating a gas pipeline network or heat pipeline network, in violation of the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 16 of this Law, refuses to allow gas or heat that meets the technical standards for network access to enter the network, causing economic losses to gas or heat production enterprises, it shall bear compensation liability, and the energy management department of the provincial people's government shall order correction within a specified period; if it refuses to correct, a fine of not more than one time the amount of economic losses of the gas or heat production enterprise shall be imposed.

Article 31 If a petroleum sales enterprise, in violation of the provisions of paragraph 3 of Article 16 of this Law, fails to incorporate bio-liquid fuels that meet national standards into its fuel sales system as required, causing economic losses to bio-liquid fuel production enterprises, it shall bear compensation liability, and the energy administration department of the State Council or the energy management department of the provincial people's government shall order correction within a specified period; if it refuses to correct, a fine of not more than one time the amount of economic losses of the bio-liquid fuel production enterprise shall be imposed.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 32 For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Biomass energy" refers to energy converted from plants, manure, and urban and rural organic waste in nature.

(2) "Independent renewable energy power system" refers to a renewable energy power system that operates independently without being connected to the grid.

(3) "Energy crops" refer to herbaceous and woody plants specially cultivated to provide energy raw materials.

(4) "Bio-liquid fuels" refer to liquid fuels such as methanol, ethanol, and biodiesel produced from biomass resources.

Article 33 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2006.
