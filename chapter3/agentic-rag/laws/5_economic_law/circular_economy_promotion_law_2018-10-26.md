# Circular Economy Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on August 29, 2008

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of promoting the development of the circular economy, improving the efficiency of resource utilization, protecting and improving the environment, and realizing sustainable development.

Article 2 For the purpose of this Law, "circular economy" means the general term for the activities of reduction, reuse and resource recovery conducted in the processes of production, circulation and consumption.

For the purpose of this Law, "reduction" means the reduction of resource consumption and waste generation in the processes of production, circulation and consumption.

For the purpose of this Law, "reuse" means the use of waste directly as a product, or the continued use of waste as a product after repair, refurbishment or remanufacturing, or the use of all or part of the waste as components of other products.

For the purpose of this Law, "resource recovery" means the use of waste directly as raw material or the recycling of waste.

Article 3 The development of the circular economy is a major strategy for the national economic and social development, which shall follow the principles of overall planning, rational layout, adaptation to local conditions, emphasis on practical results, government promotion, market guidance, enterprise implementation and public participation.

Article 4 The development of the circular economy shall be implemented under the premise of technical feasibility, economic rationality and being conducive to resource conservation and environmental protection, following the principle of giving priority to reduction.

In the process of waste reuse and resource recovery, production safety shall be ensured, the quality of products shall meet the standards prescribed by the State, and re-pollution shall be prevented.

Article 5 The comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, supervising and administering the development of the circular economy nationwide; the competent departments of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the circular economy within their respective functions and duties.

The comprehensive administrative departments for the development of the circular economy under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, supervising and administering the development of the circular economy within their respective administrative regions; the competent departments of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of the circular economy within their respective functions and duties.

Article 6 The industrial policies formulated by the State shall comply with the requirements for the development of the circular economy.

The people's governments at or above the county level shall, when formulating plans for national economic and social development and annual plans, and the relevant departments under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, when formulating plans for environmental protection, science and technology, etc., include the content of developing the circular economy.

Article 7 The State encourages and supports the research, development and promotion of science and technology for the circular economy, and encourages the publicity, education, popularization of scientific knowledge and international cooperation concerning the circular economy.

Article 8 The people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a target responsibility system for the development of the circular economy, and adopt measures such as planning, finance, investment and government procurement to promote the development of the circular economy.

Article 9 Enterprises and public institutions shall establish and improve management systems, take measures to reduce resource consumption, reduce the amount and discharge of waste, and improve the level of waste reuse and resource recovery.

Article 10 Citizens shall enhance their awareness of resource conservation and environmental protection, consume rationally, and save resources.

The State encourages and guides citizens to use products and recycled products that are energy-saving, water-saving, material-saving and conducive to environmental protection, and to reduce the amount and discharge of waste.

Citizens have the right to report acts of wasting resources and damaging the environment, and have the right to learn about the government's information on the development of the circular economy and to put forward opinions and suggestions.

Article 11 The State encourages and supports trade associations to play a role in technical guidance and service in the development of the circular economy. The people's governments at or above the county level may entrust qualified trade associations and other social organizations to provide public services for promoting the development of the circular economy.

The State encourages and supports intermediary agencies, learned societies and other social organizations to carry out publicity, technical promotion and consulting services for the circular economy, and to promote the development of the circular economy.

## Chapter II Basic Management Systems

Article 12 The comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council, formulate a national plan for the development of the circular economy, which shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council. The comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the local people's government at or above the city divided into districts shall, together with the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the people's government at the same level, formulate a plan for the development of the circular economy within its administrative region, which shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the people's government at the same level.

The plan for the development of the circular economy shall include the planning objectives, scope of application, main content, key tasks and safeguard measures, and shall specify indicators such as resource productivity, waste reuse and resource recovery rates.

Article 13 The local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the control indicators for the total discharge of major pollutants, the total amount of land used for construction and the total amount of water used in their administrative regions issued by the people's government at the next higher level, plan and adjust the industrial structure of their administrative regions to promote the development of the circular economy.

New construction, renovation and expansion projects must meet the requirements of the control indicators for the total discharge of major pollutants, the total amount of land used for construction and the total amount of water used in their administrative regions.

Article 14 The comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of statistics, ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council, establish and improve the evaluation index system for the circular economy.

The people's government at the next higher level shall, based on the main evaluation indicators for the circular economy specified in the preceding paragraph, regularly assess the status of the development of the circular economy by the people's government at the next lower level, and shall take the completion of the main evaluation indicators as part of the assessment and evaluation of the local people's government and its responsible persons.

Article 15 Enterprises that produce products or packaging materials included in the catalog of mandatory recovery must be responsible for the recovery of discarded products or packaging materials; for those that can be utilized, the producing enterprises shall be responsible for their utilization; for those that are not suitable for utilization due to lack of technical and economic conditions, the producing enterprises shall be responsible for their harmless disposal.

For the discarded products or packaging materials specified in the preceding paragraph, if the producer entrusts a seller or other organization for recovery, or entrusts a waste utilization or disposal enterprise for utilization or disposal, the entrusted party shall be responsible for recovery, utilization or disposal in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations and the contract.

For products and packaging materials included in the catalog of mandatory recovery, consumers shall hand over the discarded products or packaging materials to the producer or the seller or other organization entrusted by the producer for recovery.

The catalog of products and packaging materials subject to mandatory recovery and the management measures thereof shall be prescribed by the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council.

Article 16 The State implements a key supervision and management system for energy and water consumption for key enterprises in industries such as iron and steel, non-ferrous metals, coal, electric power, petroleum processing, chemical industry, building materials, construction, papermaking, printing and dyeing, whose annual comprehensive energy consumption and water consumption exceed the total amount prescribed by the State.

The supervision and management of energy conservation for key energy-consuming units shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China.

The supervision and management measures for key water-using units shall be prescribed by the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council together with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 17 The State establishes and improves the statistical system for the circular economy, strengthens the statistical management of resource consumption, comprehensive utilization and waste generation, and regularly publishes the main statistical indicators to the public.

The competent department of standardization under the State Council shall, together with the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy and the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council, establish and improve the standard system for the circular economy, and formulate and improve standards for energy conservation, water conservation, material conservation, waste reuse and resource recovery.

The State establishes and improves the product resource consumption labeling system such as the energy efficiency label.

## Chapter III Reduction

Article 18 The comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council, regularly publish catalogs of technologies, processes, equipment, materials and products that are encouraged, restricted and eliminated.

It is prohibited to produce, import or sell equipment, materials and products included in the catalog of elimination, and it is prohibited to use technologies, processes, equipment and materials included in the catalog of elimination.

Article 19 When designing processes, equipment, products and packaging materials, in accordance with the requirements of reducing resource consumption and waste generation, priority shall be given to the use of materials and design schemes that are easy to recycle, disassemble, degrade, non-toxic and harmless or low-toxic and low-harm, and the mandatory requirements of relevant national standards shall be met.

For electrical and electronic products that may cause environmental pollution during disassembly and disposal, toxic and hazardous substances prohibited by the State shall not be designed for use. The catalog of toxic and hazardous substances prohibited for use in electrical and electronic products shall be formulated by the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council together with the competent department of ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council.

The design of product packaging materials shall comply with product packaging standards to prevent excessive packaging from causing resource waste and environmental pollution.

Article 20 Industrial enterprises shall adopt advanced or applicable water-saving technologies, processes and equipment, formulate and implement water-saving plans, strengthen water-saving management, and carry out whole-process control of production water use.

Industrial enterprises shall strengthen water metering management, equip and use qualified water metering instruments, and establish a system for water consumption statistics and water use status analysis.

New construction, renovation and expansion projects shall be equipped with water-saving facilities. Water-saving facilities shall be designed, constructed and put into operation simultaneously with the main project.

The State encourages and supports coastal areas to carry out seawater desalination and direct seawater utilization to save freshwater resources.

Article 21 The State encourages and supports enterprises to use high-efficiency fuel-saving products.

Enterprises in the electric power, petroleum processing, chemical, iron and steel, non-ferrous metals and building materials industries must, within the scope and time limit prescribed by the State, replace fuel oil with clean energy such as clean coal, petroleum coke and natural gas, and stop using fuel oil generator sets and fuel oil boilers that do not meet the national regulations.

Manufacturers of internal combustion engines and motor vehicles shall, in accordance with the national fuel economy standards for internal combustion engines and motor vehicles, adopt fuel-saving technologies to reduce the consumption of petroleum products.

Article 22 When exploiting mineral resources, overall planning shall be carried out, a reasonable development and utilization plan shall be formulated, and reasonable mining sequences, methods and beneficiation processes shall be adopted. The authority issuing the mining license shall review the indicators such as the mining recovery rate, mining dilution rate, beneficiation recovery rate, mine water recycling rate and land reclamation rate in the development and utilization plan submitted by the applicant in accordance with the law; if the review fails, the mining license shall not be issued. The authority issuing the mining license shall strengthen the supervision and management of the exploitation of mineral resources in accordance with the law.

While mining the main mineral species, mining enterprises shall carry out comprehensive mining and rational utilization of associated and co-existing minerals of industrial value; for minerals that must be mined simultaneously but cannot be utilized temporarily, as well as tailings containing useful components, protective measures shall be taken to prevent resource loss and ecological damage.

Article 23 Architectural design, construction and building units shall, in accordance with the relevant national regulations and standards, adopt technical processes for energy conservation, water conservation, land conservation and material conservation, as well as small-sized, lightweight and recycled products, for the buildings and structures they design, construct and build. In areas where conditions permit, renewable energy sources such as solar energy, geothermal energy and wind energy shall be fully utilized.

The State encourages the use of non-toxic and harmless solid waste to produce building materials, encourages the use of bulk cement, and promotes the use of ready-mixed concrete and ready-mixed mortar.

It is prohibited to destroy arable land for brick burning. Within the time limit and area prescribed by the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government, the production, sale and use of clay bricks are prohibited.

Article 24 The people's governments at or above the county level and their competent departments of agriculture shall promote the intensive use of land, encourage and support agricultural producers to adopt advanced planting, breeding and irrigation technologies that save water, fertilizer and pesticides, promote energy conservation in agricultural machinery, and give priority to the development of ecological agriculture.

In water-deficient areas, the planting structure shall be adjusted, priority shall be given to the development of water-saving agriculture, rainwater harvesting and utilization shall be promoted, water-saving irrigation facilities shall be constructed and maintained, water use efficiency shall be improved, and water evaporation and leakage shall be reduced.

Article 25 State organs and other organizations using financial funds shall practice strict economy, eliminate waste, take the lead in using products, equipment and facilities that are energy-saving, water-saving, land-saving, material-saving and conducive to environmental protection, and economize on the use of office supplies. The institutions for the administration of government affairs under the State Council and the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with the relevant departments under the people's governments at the same level, formulate energy and water consumption quota indicators for the state organs and other institutions at the same level, and the financial departments shall formulate expenditure standards based on such quota indicators.

The urban people's governments and the owners or users of buildings shall take measures to strengthen the maintenance and management of buildings and extend the service life of buildings. For buildings that meet the urban planning and engineering construction standards and are within their reasonable service life, the urban people's governments shall not decide to demolish them except for the needs of the public interest.

Article 26 Service enterprises such as catering, entertainment and hotels shall adopt products that are energy-saving, water-saving, material-saving and conducive to environmental protection, and reduce or refrain from using products that waste resources and pollute the environment.

Service enterprises such as catering, entertainment and hotels newly built after the implementation of this Law shall adopt technologies, equipment and facilities that are energy-saving, water-saving, material-saving and conducive to environmental protection.

Article 27 The State encourages and supports the use of reclaimed water. In areas where the use of reclaimed water is feasible, the use of tap water for urban road cleaning, urban greening and landscape water use is restricted or prohibited.

Article 28 The State restricts the production and sale of disposable consumer goods on the premise of ensuring product safety and hygiene. The specific catalog shall be formulated by the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy under the State Council together with the competent departments of finance, ecological environment and other relevant departments under the State Council.

For the production and sale of disposable consumer goods included in the catalog specified in the preceding paragraph, the competent departments of finance, taxation and foreign trade under the State Council shall formulate restrictive tax and export measures.

## Chapter IV Reuse and Resource Recovery

Article 29 The people's governments at or above the county level shall make overall plans for the regional economic layout, rationally adjust the industrial structure, and promote cooperation among enterprises in the field of comprehensive resource utilization, so as to achieve efficient utilization and recycling of resources.

Various industrial parks shall organize enterprises within the park to carry out comprehensive resource utilization and promote the development of the circular economy.

The State encourages enterprises in various industrial parks to exchange and utilize waste, cascade energy use, intensively use land, classify and recycle water, and share infrastructure and other related facilities.

New construction and renovation of various industrial parks shall undergo environmental impact assessment in accordance with the law, and ecological protection and pollution control measures shall be taken to ensure that the environmental quality of the region meets the prescribed standards.

Article 30 Enterprises shall, in accordance with national regulations, comprehensively utilize industrial wastes such as fly ash, coal gangue, tailings, waste rock, waste materials and waste gas generated during the production process.

Article 31 Enterprises shall develop series water use systems and circulating water use systems to improve the rate of water reuse.

Enterprises shall adopt advanced technologies, processes and equipment to recycle wastewater generated during the production process.

Article 32 Enterprises shall adopt advanced or applicable recovery technologies, processes and equipment to comprehensively utilize waste heat, waste pressure, etc. generated during the production process.

For grid-connected power generation projects utilizing waste heat, waste pressure, coalbed methane, and low-calorific value fuels such as coal gangue, coal slime and garbage, administrative permits shall be obtained or records shall be filed in accordance with the law and the regulations of the State Council. Power grid enterprises shall, in accordance with national regulations, sign grid connection agreements with enterprises generating electricity through comprehensive resource utilization, provide grid access services, and purchase in full the on-grid electricity of the grid-connected power generation projects.

Article 33 Construction units shall comprehensively utilize the construction waste generated during the construction of the project; if conditions for comprehensive utilization are not available, they shall entrust a producer or operator with the conditions to carry out comprehensive utilization or harmless disposal.

Article 34 The State encourages and supports agricultural producers and related enterprises to adopt advanced or applicable technologies to comprehensively utilize crop straw, livestock and poultry manure, by-products of agricultural product processing, waste agricultural film, etc., and to develop and utilize biomass energy such as biogas.

Article 35 The people's governments at or above the county level and their competent departments of forestry and grassland shall actively develop ecological forestry, encourage and support forestry producers and related enterprises to adopt wood-saving and wood-substituting technologies, carry out comprehensive utilization of forestry waste, inferior and small-diameter timber, sand shrubs, etc., and improve the comprehensive utilization rate of wood.

Article 36 The State supports producers and operators in establishing information systems for the exchange of industrial waste, and promotes the exchange of industrial waste information among enterprises.

If an enterprise does not have the conditions for comprehensive utilization of waste generated during the production process, it shall provide the waste to a producer or operator with the conditions for comprehensive utilization.

Article 37 The State encourages and promotes the establishment of a waste recovery system.

Local people's governments shall, in accordance with urban and rural planning, rationally lay out waste recovery outlets and trading markets, and support waste recovery enterprises and other organizations in the collection, storage, transportation and information exchange of waste.

Waste recovery trading markets shall comply with the national regulations on environmental protection, safety and fire protection.

Article 38 The disassembly or reuse of specific products such as waste electrical and electronic products, end-of-life motor vehicles and vessels, waste tires, and waste lead-acid batteries shall comply with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 39 Recovered electrical and electronic products, if sold after repair, must meet the standards for reused products and be prominently marked as reused products.

Recovered electrical and electronic products that need to be disassembled and recycled shall be sold to qualified disassembly enterprises.

Article 40 The State supports enterprises in carrying out remanufacturing of motor vehicle parts, construction machinery, machine tools and other products, as well as tire retreading.

The quality of remanufactured products and retreaded products sold must meet the standards prescribed by the State, and they shall be prominently marked as remanufactured products or retreaded products.

Article 41 The people's governments at or above the county level shall make overall plans for the construction of facilities for the classified collection and resource recovery of urban and rural domestic waste, establish and improve the system for classified collection and resource recovery, and improve the resource recovery rate of domestic waste.

The people's governments at or above the county level shall support enterprises in building sludge resource recovery and disposal facilities, improve the level of comprehensive sludge utilization, and prevent re-pollution.

## Chapter V Incentive Measures

Article 42 The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish special funds for the development of the circular economy to support scientific and technological research and development for the circular economy, the demonstration and promotion of circular economy technologies and products, the implementation of major circular economy projects, and information services for the development of the circular economy. The specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of finance under the State Council together with the comprehensive administrative department for the development of the circular economy and other relevant departments under the State Council.**Article 43** The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, along with their relevant departments, shall include independent innovation research, application demonstration, and industrialization development of major scientific and technological projects for the circular economy in national or provincial science and technology development plans and high-tech industry development plans, and shall arrange financial funds to provide support.

Where financial funds are used to introduce major technologies and equipment for the circular economy, plans for digestion, absorption, and innovation shall be formulated, submitted to the relevant competent department for approval, and supervised for implementation by that department. The relevant competent department shall, based on actual needs, establish a coordination mechanism to carry out overall coordination for the introduction, digestion, absorption, and innovation of major technologies and equipment, and provide financial support.

**Article 44** The State grants tax incentives for industrial activities that promote the development of the circular economy and uses tax and other measures to encourage the import of advanced technologies, equipment, and products for energy conservation, water conservation, and material conservation, while restricting the export of products with high energy consumption and heavy pollution during production. Specific measures shall be formulated by the fiscal and tax authorities under the State Council.

Enterprises that use or produce technologies, processes, equipment, or products included in the national encouraged catalogues for cleaner production and comprehensive resource utilization shall enjoy tax incentives in accordance with relevant national regulations.

**Article 45** When formulating and implementing investment plans, the comprehensive department for circular economy development under the people's government at or above the county level shall prioritize projects such as energy conservation, water conservation, land conservation, material conservation, and comprehensive resource utilization as key investment areas.

For projects that comply with national industrial policies, such as energy conservation, water conservation, land conservation, material conservation, and comprehensive resource utilization, financial institutions shall provide priority credit support such as loans and actively offer supporting financial services.

For enterprises that produce, import, sell, or use technologies, processes, equipment, materials, or products listed in the elimination catalogue, financial institutions shall not provide any form of credit support.

**Article 46** The State implements pricing policies conducive to resource conservation and rational utilization, guiding entities and individuals to conserve and rationally use resource-based products such as water, electricity, and gas.

The pricing authorities under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with national industrial policies, implement restrictive pricing policies for restricted-category projects in industries with high resource consumption.

For grid-connected power generation projects utilizing waste heat, waste pressure, coalbed methane, and low-calorific-value fuels such as coal gangue, coal slime, and garbage, the pricing authorities shall determine the on-grid electricity price based on the principle of benefiting comprehensive resource utilization.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on the economic and social development of their administrative regions, implement a garbage discharge fee system. The fees collected shall be used exclusively for garbage classification, collection, transportation, storage, utilization, and disposal, and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

The State encourages the recovery of waste through methods such as trade-in and deposits.

**Article 47** The State implements government procurement policies conducive to the development of the circular economy. When procuring with financial funds, priority shall be given to products that are energy-saving, water-saving, material-saving, and environmentally friendly, as well as recycled products.

**Article 48** The people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall commend and reward entities and individuals that have made significant achievements in circular economy management, scientific and technological research, product development, demonstration, and promotion.

Enterprises and institutions shall commend and reward collectives and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the development of the circular economy.

## Chapter 6 Legal Liability

**Article 49** If the comprehensive department for circular economy development under the people's government at or above the county level or other relevant competent departments fail to investigate violations of this Law upon discovery or after receiving reports of violations, or otherwise fail to perform their supervision and management duties according to law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant competent department of the people's government at the next higher level shall order rectification, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 50** The production or sale of products or equipment listed in the elimination catalogue shall be penalized in accordance with the provisions of the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China.

The use of technologies, processes, equipment, or materials listed in the elimination catalogue shall be ordered to cease by the comprehensive department for circular economy development under the local people's government at or above the county level; the illegally used equipment and materials shall be confiscated; and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the comprehensive department for circular economy development under the people's government at or above the county level shall submit an opinion, requesting the people's government at the same level to order a suspension of operations or closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council.

In violation of this Law, the import of equipment, materials, or products listed in the elimination catalogue shall be ordered by the Customs to be returned, and a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan may be imposed. If the importer is unknown, the carrier shall bear the responsibility for return or bear the relevant disposal costs.

**Article 51** In violation of this Law, for electrical and electronic products that may cause environmental pollution during disassembly or disposal, if toxic and hazardous substances listed in the national prohibited use catalogue are designed for use, the market supervision and administration department under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period. If rectification is not made within the period, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked according to law.

**Article 52** In violation of this Law, if enterprises in the electric power, petroleum processing, chemical, iron and steel, non-ferrous metals, and building materials industries fail to stop using fuel oil generator sets or fuel oil boilers that do not comply with national regulations within the specified scope or time limit, the comprehensive department for circular economy development under the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period. If rectification is not made within the period, the dismantling of the fuel oil generator sets or fuel oil boilers shall be ordered, and a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 53** In violation of this Law, if mining enterprises fail to meet the indicators such as mining recovery rate, ore dilution rate, mineral processing recovery rate, mine water recycling rate, and land reclamation rate as determined after legally reviewed approval, the competent department of geology and mineral resources under the people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan. If rectification is not made within the period, the mining license issuing authority shall revoke the mining license according to law.
**Article 54** In violation of this Law, if clay bricks are produced, sold, or used within the period or area where the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government have prohibited the production, sale, or use of clay bricks, the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period. If there is illegal income, the illegal income shall be confiscated. If production or sale continues after the period, the market supervision and administration department of the local people's government shall revoke the business license according to law.

**Article 55** In violation of this Law, if a grid enterprise refuses to purchase electricity generated by enterprises using waste heat, waste pressure, coalbed methane, and low-calorific-value fuels such as coal gangue, coal slime, and garbage, the national electric power regulatory authority shall order rectification within a specified period. If losses are caused to the enterprise, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 56** In violation of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the market supervision and administration department of the local people's government to rectify within a specified period, and a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed. If rectification is not made within the period, the business license shall be revoked according to law. If losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne according to law:

(1) Selling reused electrical and electronic products without a reused product label;
(2) Selling remanufactured or refurbished products without a remanufactured or refurbished product label.

**Article 57** Any violation of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 58** This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2009.
