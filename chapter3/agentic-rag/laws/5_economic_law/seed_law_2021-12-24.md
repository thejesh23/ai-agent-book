# Seed Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 16th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on July 8, 2000

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Seed Law of the People's Republic of China at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics at the Third Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Revision at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2015

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Seed Law of the People's Republic of China at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of protecting and rationally utilizing germplasm resources, regulating variety breeding, seed production, operation, and management activities, strengthening scientific and technological research in the seed industry, encouraging breeding innovation, protecting new plant variety rights, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of seed producers, operators, and users, improving seed quality, developing a modern seed industry, ensuring national food security, and promoting the development of agriculture and forestry.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to activities such as variety breeding, seed production, operation, and management conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "seeds" refer to planting materials or propagation materials of crops and forest trees, including grains, fruits, roots, stems, seedlings, buds, leaves, flowers, etc.

Article 3 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall be respectively responsible for the work concerning crop seeds and forest tree seeds nationwide. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be respectively responsible for the work concerning crop seeds and forest tree seeds within their respective administrative regions.

The people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall take measures to strengthen seed law enforcement and supervision, and punish in accordance with the law illegal seed acts that infringe upon the rights and interests of farmers.

Article 4 The State supports the protection of germplasm resources and the breeding, production, renewal, and promotion of improved varieties, encourages the integration of variety breeding with seed production and operation, and rewards units and individuals that achieve remarkable results in the protection of germplasm resources and the breeding and promotion of improved varieties.

Article 5 People's governments at or above the provincial level shall, in accordance with the policy of revitalizing agriculture through science and education and the needs of agricultural and forestry development, formulate seed industry development plans and organize their implementation.

Article 6 People's governments at or above the provincial level shall establish a seed reserve system, mainly for production needs during disasters and for adjusting surpluses and shortages, to ensure the safety of agricultural and forestry production. Reserved seeds shall be regularly inspected and renewed. Specific measures for seed reserves shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 7 The breeding, testing, approval, and promotion of genetically modified plant varieties shall undergo safety assessment and adopt strict safety control measures. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall strengthen follow-up supervision and promptly announce information on the approval and promotion of relevant genetically modified plant varieties. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter II Protection of Germplasm Resources

Article 8 The State protects germplasm resources in accordance with the law, and no unit or individual may encroach upon or destroy germplasm resources.

It is prohibited to collect or harvest natural germplasm resources under State key protection. If collection or harvesting is necessary due to special circumstances such as scientific research, approval shall be obtained from the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, or forestry and grassland under the State Council or the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 9 The State shall systematically survey, collect, sort, identify, register, preserve, exchange, and utilize germplasm resources, with emphasis on collecting rare, endangered, endemic resources and characteristic local varieties, and regularly publish a catalogue of available germplasm resources. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council.

Article 10 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall establish germplasm resource banks, germplasm resource reserves, or germplasm resource protection areas. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, as needed, establish germplasm resource banks, germplasm resource reserves, or germplasm resource protection areas. The germplasm resources in such banks, reserves, or protection areas are public resources and shall be open for utilization in accordance with the law.

Occupation of a germplasm resource bank, germplasm resource reserve, or germplasm resource protection area requires the consent of the original establishing authority.

Article 11 The State has sovereignty over germplasm resources. Any unit or individual that provides germplasm resources to foreign parties, or conducts cooperative research and utilization of germplasm resources with foreign institutions or individuals, shall report to and obtain approval from the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council, and simultaneously submit a plan for the sharing of national benefits. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council may entrust the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government to receive application materials. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall notify the competent department of ecological environment under the State Council of the approval.

The introduction of germplasm resources from abroad shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council.

## Chapter III Variety Breeding, Approval, and Registration

Article 12 The State supports research institutes and institutions of higher education in focusing on basic, cutting-edge, and applied technology research for breeding, as well as research on biological breeding technology, and supports public interest research such as the breeding of conventional crops, major afforestation tree species, and the selection of asexual propagation materials.

The State encourages seed enterprises to make full use of public interest research results to cultivate excellent varieties with independent intellectual property rights; encourages seed enterprises to establish technology research and development platforms with research institutes and institutions of higher education, to carry out breeding breakthroughs for major food crops and important economic crops, and to establish a market-oriented, benefit-sharing, risk-sharing seed industry technology innovation system combining industry, academia, and research.

The State strengthens the construction of seed industry scientific and technological innovation capabilities, promotes the transformation of seed industry scientific and technological achievements, and safeguards the legitimate rights and interests of seed industry scientific and technical personnel.

Article 13 For breeding invention patent rights and new plant variety rights formed with support from fiscal funds, except those involving national security, national interests, or major public interests, the project undertaker is authorized to obtain them in accordance with the law.

The transfer or licensing of breeding results formed mainly with support from fiscal funds shall be conducted openly in accordance with the law, and private transactions are prohibited.

Article 14 Where units or individuals suffer economic losses due to the establishment by the competent departments of forestry and grassland of measurement forests, experimental forests, plus tree collection areas, gene banks, etc., for the purpose of breeding improved forest tree varieties, the competent departments of forestry and grassland that approved the establishment shall provide economic compensation in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 15 The State implements a variety approval system for major crops and major forest trees. Major crop varieties and major forest tree varieties shall pass national or provincial approval before promotion. Major forest tree varieties determined by the competent departments of forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall undergo provincial approval.

Varieties applied for approval shall meet the requirements of distinctness, uniformity, and stability.

The approval procedures for major crop varieties and major forest tree varieties shall be formulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council. The approval procedures shall reflect the principles of fairness, openness, science, and efficiency, be conducive to the improvement and coordination of yield, quality, resistance, etc., and be conducive to the promotion of varieties that meet market and consumer needs. When formulating or revising approval procedures, opinions from breeders, seed users, producers and operators, and relevant industry representatives shall be fully solicited.

Article 16 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall respectively establish crop variety and forest tree variety approval committees composed of professionals. The variety approval committees shall undertake the approval work for major crop varieties and major forest tree varieties, establish approval archives including application documents, variety approval test data, seed samples, approval opinions, and approval conclusions, ensuring traceability. The relevant information publicly announced for approved varieties shall include the approval opinions, subject to supervision.

A recusal system shall be implemented for variety approval. Members of the variety approval committee, staff, and relevant testing and trial personnel shall be dedicated to their duties, impartial, and honest. For illegal acts of the above personnel reported by units or individuals or discovered through supervision and inspection, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments at or above the provincial level and relevant authorities shall promptly handle them in accordance with the law.

Article 17 Seed enterprises that combine breeding, production, and operation and meet the conditions prescribed by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council may independently complete tests for their self-developed major crop varieties and major forest tree varieties in accordance with the approval procedures. If they meet the approval standards, the variety approval committee shall issue an approval certificate. The seed enterprise shall be responsible for the authenticity of the test data, ensure traceability, and accept supervision from the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments at or above the provincial level and society.

Article 18 If an applicant has objections to a crop variety or forest tree variety that has failed approval, they may apply for a re-examination to the original approval committee or the national approval committee.

Article 19 Crop varieties and improved forest tree varieties that have passed national approval shall be announced by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council and may be promoted in suitable ecological regions nationwide. Crop varieties and improved forest tree varieties that have passed provincial approval shall be announced by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and may be promoted in suitable ecological regions within their respective administrative regions; if other provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government introduce crop varieties or improved forest tree varieties from regions belonging to the same suitable ecological zone, the introducer shall report the introduced varieties and regions to the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for record.

The introduction of forest tree varieties not naturally distributed in the local area shall be conducted through trials in accordance with national introduction standards.

Article 20 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall improve regional cooperation mechanisms for variety breeding and approval work, promoting the breeding and promotion of excellent varieties.

Article 21 If an approved crop variety or improved forest tree variety develops insurmountable serious defects or other circumstances making it unsuitable for continued promotion or sale, after review and confirmation by the original approval committee, the approval shall be revoked, and the original announcing department shall issue an announcement to stop promotion and sale.

Article 22 The State implements a variety registration system for certain non-major crops. Varieties included in the non-major crop registration catalogue shall be registered before promotion.

The scope of crops subject to variety registration shall be strictly controlled and determined based on the principles of protecting biodiversity, ensuring consumer safety, and ensuring seed use safety. The registration catalogue shall be formulated and adjusted by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

An applicant applying for variety registration shall submit application documents and seed samples to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and shall be responsible for their authenticity, ensure traceability, and accept supervision and inspection. The application documents shall include the type, name, origin, characteristics, breeding process, and test reports on distinctness, uniformity, and stability of the variety.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, within twenty working days from the date of accepting the variety registration application, conduct a written review of the application documents submitted by the applicant. If the requirements are met, it shall report to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council for registration and announcement.

If the application documents or seed samples of a registered variety are found to be untrue, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall revoke the registration of that variety, record the applicant's illegal information in the social credit file, and make it public; if losses are caused to seed users or other seed producers and operators, the applicant shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

If a registered variety develops insurmountable serious defects or other circumstances, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall revoke the registration and issue an announcement to stop promotion.

The registration measures for non-major crop varieties shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 23 Crop varieties subject to approval shall not be advertised, promoted, or sold without approval.

Forest tree varieties subject to approval shall not be promoted or sold as improved varieties without passing approval; however, if necessary for production, they shall be recognized by the forest tree variety approval committee.

Crop varieties subject to registration shall not be advertised or promoted, nor sold under the name of a registered variety, without registration.

Article 24 Foreign institutions or individuals without a habitual residence or place of business within the territory of China applying for variety approval or registration within the territory shall be represented by a domestic seed enterprise with legal personality.

## Chapter IV Protection of New Varieties

Article 25 The State implements a system for the protection of new plant varieties. For plant varieties that are artificially bred or discovered wild plants improved, possess novelty, distinctness, uniformity, stability, and are appropriately named, within the National Plant Variety Protection Catalogue, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council shall grant new plant variety rights to protect the legitimate rights and interests of the owners of new plant variety rights. The content and attribution of new plant variety rights, conditions for grant, application and acceptance, examination and approval, as well as duration, termination, and invalidity, shall be implemented in accordance with this Law, relevant laws, and administrative regulations.

The State encourages and supports seed industry scientific and technological innovation, the cultivation of new plant varieties, and the transformation of achievements. If a variety for which new plant variety rights have been obtained is promoted and applied, the breeder shall obtain corresponding economic benefits in accordance with the law.

Article 26 Only one new plant variety right may be granted for one new plant variety. If two or more applicants separately apply for new plant variety rights for the same variety, the new plant variety right shall be granted to the person who applied first; if applications are filed simultaneously, the right shall be granted to the person who first completed the breeding of that variety.

New plant varieties that violate the law, harm social public interests, or the ecological environment shall not be granted new plant variety rights.

Article 27 The name of a new plant variety for which new plant variety rights are granted shall be distinguishable from the names of known varieties in the same or similar plant genus or species. That name, once authorized, shall become the common name of that new plant variety.

The following names shall not be used for naming authorized varieties:

(1) Those consisting solely of numbers;
(2) Those violating social morality;
(3) Those likely to cause misunderstanding regarding the characteristics, traits, or breeder's identity of the new plant variety.

The same plant variety shall use only one name when applying for new variety protection, variety approval, variety registration, promotion, and sale. The seeds produced, promoted, and sold shall be consistent with the samples provided when applying for new plant variety protection, variety approval, and variety registration.

Article 28 The owner of a new plant variety right has an exclusive right over their authorized variety. The owner of a new plant variety right may license others to exploit the new plant variety right and collect license fees in accordance with the contract; license fees may be collected in the form of a fixed price, a percentage of promotion revenue, etc.

No unit or individual may, without the permission of the owner of the new plant variety right, produce, reproduce, or process for reproduction, offer for sale, sell, import, or export the propagation material of the authorized variety, or store such propagation material for the purpose of performing the above acts, nor may they repeatedly use the propagation material of the authorized variety for the commercial production of propagation material of another variety. This does not apply where otherwise provided by this Law, relevant laws, or administrative regulations.

If an act specified in the preceding paragraph involves harvested material obtained through the unauthorized use of propagation material of an authorized variety, permission from the owner of the new plant variety right shall be obtained; however, this does not apply if the owner of the new plant variety right has had a reasonable opportunity to exercise their right over the propagation material.

For acts specified in paragraphs 2 and 3 involving essentially derived varieties, the consent of the owner of the new plant variety right of the initial variety shall be obtained.

The implementation steps and measures for the essentially derived variety system shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 29 The use of an authorized variety under the following circumstances may be done without the permission of the owner of the new plant variety right and without payment of royalties, provided that other rights of the owner under this Law, relevant laws, and administrative regulations are not infringed:

(1) Using the authorized variety for breeding and other scientific research activities;
(2) Farmers propagating and using the propagation material of the authorized variety for their own use.

Article 30 For the sake of national interest or social public interest, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council may make a decision to grant a compulsory license for the exploitation of a new plant variety right, and shall register and announce it.

The unit or individual that obtains a compulsory license does not have an exclusive right of exploitation and has no right to authorize others to exploit it.

## Chapter V Seed Production and Operation

Article 31 The seed production and operation license for engaging in seed import and export business shall be issued by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council may entrust the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government to receive application materials.

The seed production and operation license for engaging in the production and operation of hybrid seeds of major crops and their parent seeds, propagation materials of improved forest tree varieties, and for crop seed enterprises that combine breeding, production, and operation and meet the conditions prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, shall be issued by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The seed production and operation license for seeds other than those specified in the preceding two paragraphs shall be issued by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the local people's governments at or above the county level where the producer or operator is located.

Those solely engaged in the production of non-major crop seeds and non-major forest tree seeds do not need to apply for a seed production and operation license.

Article 32 To apply for a seed production and operation license, an applicant shall have production and operation facilities, equipment, and professional technical personnel suitable for seed production and operation, as well as other conditions prescribed by laws, regulations, and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and forestry and grassland under the State Council.

Those engaged in seed production shall also have isolation and cultivation conditions for seed propagation, and a seed production site free from quarantine pests or a seed collection forest designated by the competent department of forestry and grassland under the people's government at or above the county level.

To apply for a seed production and operation license for seeds with new plant variety rights, the written consent of the owner of the new plant variety right shall be obtained.

Article 33 A seed production and operation license shall specify the name, address, legal representative of the producer or operator, the varieties and location of seed production, and the scope, validity period, valid area of seed operation, etc.

If any of the matters specified in the preceding paragraph change, an application for change of registration shall be made to the original license-issuing authority within thirty days from the date of change.

Unless otherwise provided in this Law, no unit or individual may produce or operate seeds without a seed production and operation license or in violation of the provisions of the seed production and operation license. It is prohibited to forge, alter, buy, sell, or lend seed production and operation licenses.

Article 34 Seed production shall comply with seed production technical regulations and seed inspection and quarantine regulations, ensuring that seeds meet quality requirements such as cleanliness, purity, and germination rate, as well as quarantine requirements.The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall guide and support seed producers and operators in adopting advanced seed production technologies, improving production processes, and enhancing seed quality.

Article 35: The collection of seeds within forest tree seed production bases shall be organized by the operators of such bases, and seed collection shall be carried out in accordance with relevant national standards.

It is prohibited to prematurely harvest seeds, damage mother trees, or collect seeds from inferior forests or inferior mother trees.

Article 36: Seed producers and operators shall establish and maintain production and operation records that include seed sources, origins, quantities, quality, sales destinations, sales dates, and responsible personnel, ensuring traceability. The specific items to be recorded in the production and operation records, as well as the retention periods for such records and seed samples, shall be stipulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council.

Article 37: If individual farmers have surplus conventional seeds that they have bred and used for their own purposes, they may sell or exchange them at local marketplaces without needing a seed production and operation license.

Article 38: The valid region of a seed production and operation license shall be determined by the issuing authority within its jurisdiction. Seed producers and operators who establish branches within the valid region specified in the license, who exclusively deal in packaged seeds that are not further repackaged, or who are entrusted in writing by a licensed seed producer or operator to produce or sell their seeds on a commission basis, are not required to obtain a separate seed production and operation license, but must register with the local competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland.

For seed enterprises that integrate breeding, production, and operation and meet the conditions stipulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council, the valid region of their production and operation license shall be the entire country.

Article 39: Seeds sold shall be processed, graded, and packaged, except those that cannot be processed or packaged.

Bulk or imported seeds may be repackaged; if repackaged, the repackaging unit shall be indicated on the label, and that unit shall be responsible for the seed quality.

Article 40: Seeds sold shall conform to national or industry standards and be accompanied by labels and usage instructions. The content marked on the labels and usage instructions shall be consistent with the seeds being sold. Seed producers and operators shall be responsible for the authenticity of the marked content and the seed quality.

Labels shall indicate the seed category, variety name, variety approval or registration number, suitable planting regions and seasons, producer/operator and registered location, quality indicators, quarantine certificate number, seed production and operation license number and information code, as well as other matters stipulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council.

When selling seeds of authorized varieties, the variety right number shall be indicated.

When selling imported seeds, the import approval document number and a Chinese label shall be attached.

When selling seeds of genetically modified plant varieties, clear text markings must be used, and safety control measures for use shall be indicated.

Seed producers and operators shall comply with relevant laws and regulations, act in good faith, provide seed users with information on the seed producer, the main characteristics of the seeds, main cultivation measures, adaptability, risk warnings, and related consulting services, and shall not engage in false or misleading publicity.

No unit or individual may unlawfully interfere with the production and operation autonomy of seed producers and operators.

Article 41: The content of seed advertisements shall comply with the provisions of this Law and relevant advertising laws and regulations, and descriptions of main characteristics shall be consistent with the approval and registration announcements.

Article 42: The transportation or mailing of seeds shall be subject to quarantine in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 43: Seed users have the right to purchase seeds according to their own wishes, and no unit or individual may unlawfully interfere.

Article 44: The State provides support for the promotion and use of improved forest tree seeds for afforestation. Afforestation projects funded by the State or primarily by the State, as well as afforestation by state-owned forestry units, shall use improved forest tree seeds in accordance with plans formulated by the competent departments of forestry and grassland.

Article 45: If a seed user suffers losses due to seed quality issues or because the content marked on the seed labels and usage instructions is untrue, the seed user may claim compensation from the seller of the seeds, or from the seed producer or other operators. The compensation amount shall include the purchase price of the seeds, loss of expected profits, and other losses. If the responsibility lies with the seed producer or other operators, the seller who has compensated has the right to seek recourse from the seed producer or other operators; if the responsibility lies with the seller, the seed producer or other operators who have compensated have the right to seek recourse from the seller.

## Chapter 6: Seed Supervision and Administration

Article 46: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of seed quality. The measures for seed quality management, industry standards, and inspection methods shall be formulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, may use rapid testing methods prescribed by the State to test seed varieties in production and operation, and the test results may serve as the basis for administrative penalties. If the inspected party objects to the test results, they may apply for a re-test, which shall not use the same testing method. If losses are caused to the parties due to erroneous test results, compensation shall be made in accordance with the law.

Article 47: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, may entrust seed quality inspection institutions to inspect seed quality.

Institutions undertaking seed quality inspection shall possess the necessary testing conditions and capabilities and shall have passed the assessment of the relevant competent departments of the people's governments at or above the provincial level.

Seed quality inspection institutions shall be staffed with seed inspectors. Seed inspectors shall have a secondary specialized school or higher education in a relevant field and possess the corresponding technical capability and proficiency in seed inspection.

Article 48: The production and operation of counterfeit and substandard seeds are prohibited. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, and relevant departments shall crack down on illegal activities involving the production and operation of counterfeit and substandard seeds in accordance with the law, protect the legitimate rights and interests of farmers, and maintain a fair competition market order.
The following seeds are considered counterfeit seeds:

(1) Seeds that are not seeds but are passed off as seeds, or seeds of one variety are passed off as seeds of another variety;
(2) Seeds whose type or variety does not match the content indicated on the label, or which have no label.

The following seeds are considered low-quality seeds:
(1) Seeds whose quality is below the standards prescribed by the State;
(2) Seeds whose quality is below the indicators marked on the label;
(3) Seeds carrying quarantine pests prescribed by the State.

Article 49: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, are the administrative law enforcement agencies for seeds. Seed law enforcement personnel shall present their administrative law enforcement credentials when performing official duties. When performing seed supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Enter production and operation premises for on-site inspection;
(2) Conduct sampling, testing, experiments, or inspections of seeds;
(3) Review and copy relevant contracts, invoices, account books, production and operation records, and other relevant materials;
(4) Seal or seize seeds that have evidence of being produced or operated illegally, as well as tools, equipment, and means of transport used for illegal production and operation;
(5) Seal premises where illegal seed production and operation activities are conducted.

When the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, exercise their powers in accordance with this Law, the parties involved shall assist and cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct them.

The comprehensive law enforcement agencies affiliated with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, or the seed management agencies entrusted by them, may carry out relevant seed law enforcement work.

Article 50: Seed producers and operators may voluntarily establish seed industry associations in accordance with the law to strengthen industry self-regulation, protect the legitimate rights and interests of members, and provide services such as information exchange, technical training, credit building, marketing, and consulting for members and industry development.

Article 51: Seed producers and operators may voluntarily apply for seed quality certification from qualified certification bodies. Upon passing certification, they may use the certification mark on the packaging.

Article 52: If, due to force majeure, it is necessary to use crop seeds that are below the national or local standards for production needs, approval must be obtained from the local people's government at or above the county level in the area where the seeds will be used.

Article 53: Units and individuals engaged in variety breeding, seed production and operation, and management shall comply with the provisions of relevant plant quarantine laws and administrative regulations to prevent the spread and proliferation of dangerous plant diseases, pests, weeds, and other harmful organisms.

No unit or individual is permitted to conduct inoculation experiments with quarantine pests within seed production bases.

Article 54: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the provincial level shall publish information on variety approval, variety registration, new variety protection, seed production and operation licensing, and supervision and administration on a unified government information release platform.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council shall establish a standard sample repository for plant varieties to provide a basis for seed supervision and administration.

Article 55: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, and their staff, shall not participate in or engage in seed production and operation activities.

## Chapter 7: Seed Import, Export, and Foreign Cooperation

Article 56: Imported and exported seeds must undergo quarantine to prevent the entry or exit of dangerous plant diseases, pests, weeds, and other harmful organisms. The specific quarantine work shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on plant entry and exit quarantine.

Article 57: Entities engaged in seed import and export business shall possess a seed production and operation license; among them, those engaged in the import and export of crop seeds shall also obtain a seed import and export permit in accordance with relevant national regulations.

The approval authority for introducing crop and forest tree seeds from abroad, the approval procedures for importing crop seeds, and the management measures for introducing genetically modified plant varieties shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 58: The quality of imported seeds shall meet national or industry standards. If no national or industry standards exist, the standards agreed upon in the contract may be followed.

Article 59: The import of seeds for the purpose of producing seeds for foreign entities may be exempt from the restrictions of the first paragraph of Article 57 of this Law, provided that a contract for foreign seed production exists. The imported seeds may only be used for seed production, and the resulting products shall not be sold domestically.

Seeds introduced from abroad for crop or forest tree experimentation shall be cultivated in isolation, and the harvested materials shall not be sold as seeds.

Article 60: The import or export of counterfeit or substandard seeds, as well as seeds that are prohibited from import or export by national regulations, is prohibited.
Article 61: The State establishes a national security review mechanism for the seed industry. The approval and management of investments, mergers, and acquisitions of domestic seed enterprises by foreign institutions or individuals, or technical cooperation with domestic research institutes and seed enterprises for variety research and development, seed production, and operation, shall be carried out in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter 8: Support Measures

Article 62: The State increases support for the development of the seed industry. Support shall be provided for variety breeding, production, demonstration and promotion, germplasm resource protection, seed reserves, and major seed-producing counties.

The State encourages the promotion and use of efficient, safe seed production and collection technologies and advanced, applicable seed production and collection machinery, and includes such machinery in the scope of subsidies for the purchase of agricultural machinery.

The State actively guides social capital to invest in the seed industry.

Article 63: The State strengthens the construction of public welfare infrastructure for the seed industry, ensuring reasonable land needs for breeding research facilities.

Land within advantageous seed breeding bases shall be designated as permanent basic farmland. Advantageous seed breeding bases shall be determined by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council in consultation with the people's governments of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 64: Support shall be provided to seed enterprises engaged in the breeding and production of crop and forest tree varieties in accordance with relevant national regulations.

Article 65: The State encourages and guides financial institutions to provide credit support for seed production, operation, purchase, and storage.

Article 66: The State supports insurance institutions in offering seed production insurance. People's governments at or above the provincial level may adopt measures such as insurance premium subsidies to support the development of seed production insurance.

Article 67: The State encourages research institutes and institutions of higher learning to engage in exchanges of breeding scientific and technical personnel with seed enterprises, supports their scientific and technical personnel in working at seed enterprises to transform breeding achievements, and encourages innovation and entrepreneurship among breeding research personnel.

Article 68: The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the State Council, and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government where off-site seed breeding is conducted, shall strengthen the management and coordination of off-site seed breeding work. Transportation departments shall prioritize the transport of seeds.
## Chapter 9: Legal Liability

Article 69: If the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, fail to make administrative licensing decisions in accordance with the law, fail to investigate upon discovering illegal acts or receiving reports of illegal acts, or otherwise fail to perform their duties as stipulated in this Law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department of the people's government at a higher level shall order rectification, and the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

If, in violation of Article 55 of this Law, staff of the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, engage in seed production and operation activities, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 70: If, in violation of Article 16 of this Law, members of the variety approval committee and their staff fail to perform their duties in accordance with the law, engage in fraud, or practice favoritism, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; they shall be prohibited from engaging in variety approval work for five years from the date the sanction decision is made.

Article 71: If variety testing, experimentation, or seed quality inspection institutions forge testing, experimentation, or inspection data or issue false certificates, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall order rectification, impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the institution, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if there are illegal gains, those gains shall be confiscated; if losses are caused to seed users and other seed producers and operators, joint and several liability shall be borne with the seed producers and operators; if the circumstances are serious, the relevant competent department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall revoke the seed quality inspection qualification.

Article 72: If, in violation of Article 28 of this Law, there is an act of infringing upon a new plant variety right, the parties shall resolve the matter through negotiation. If negotiation is refused or fails, the owner of the new plant variety right or an interested party may request the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's government at or above the county level to handle the matter, or may directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level may, based on the voluntary principle of the parties, mediate compensation for damages caused by infringement of new plant variety rights. If a mediation agreement is reached, the parties shall perform it; if a party fails to perform the agreement or mediation fails, the owner of the new plant variety right or an interested party may file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

The amount of compensation for infringement of a new plant variety right shall be determined based on the actual losses suffered by the right holder due to the infringement; if the actual losses are difficult to determine, it may be determined based on the profits gained by the infringer from the infringement. If the losses of the right holder or the profits gained by the infringer are difficult to determine, the amount may be reasonably determined by reference to a multiple of the licensing fee for the new plant variety right. If the infringement of the new plant variety right is intentional and the circumstances are serious, the compensation amount may be determined at between one and five times the amount determined by the above methods.

If the losses of the right holder, the profits gained by the infringer, and the licensing fee for the new plant variety right are all difficult to determine, the people's court may, based on factors such as the type of the new plant variety right and the nature and circumstances of the infringement, determine compensation of not more than 5 million yuan.

The compensation amount shall include the reasonable expenses incurred by the right holder to stop the infringement.

When handling cases of infringement of new plant variety rights, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in order to safeguard the public interest, order the infringer to cease the infringing act, confiscate the illegal gains and seeds; if the value of the goods is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 250,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the value of the goods is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the value of the goods shall also be imposed.

If an authorized variety is counterfeited, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the counterfeiting act, confiscate the illegal gains and seeds; if the value of the goods is less than 50,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 250,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the value of the goods is 50,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the value of the goods shall also be imposed.

Article 73: If a dispute arises between parties regarding the right to apply for a new plant variety or the ownership of a new plant variety right, they may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 74: If, in violation of Article 48 of this Law, counterfeit seeds are produced or operated, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall order the cessation of production and operation, confiscate the illegal gains and seeds, and revoke the seed production and operation license; if the value of the illegally produced or operated goods is less than 20,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the value of the goods is 20,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the value of the goods shall also be imposed.

If a person is sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or more for the crime of producing or operating counterfeit seeds, the legal representative or directly responsible person in charge of the seed enterprise or other unit shall not serve as the legal representative or senior manager of a seed enterprise for five years from the date the sentence is fully served.

Article 75: If, in violation of Article 48 of this Law, substandard seeds are produced or operated, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall order the cessation of production and operation, confiscate the illegal gains and seeds; if the value of the illegally produced or operated goods is less than 20,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the value of the goods is 20,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than five times but not more than ten times the value of the goods shall also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the seed production and operation license shall be revoked.
If a person is sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment or a more severe penalty for the crime of producing or dealing in substandard seeds, the legal representative or directly responsible person in charge of the seed enterprise or other entity shall not serve as the legal representative or senior manager of a seed enterprise for five years from the date the sentence is fully served.
Article 76: If, in violation of Articles 32, 33, and 34 of this Law, any of the following acts is committed, the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, as well as forestry and grassland, of the people's governments at or above the county level shall order rectification, confiscate the illegal gains and seeds; if the value of the illegally produced or operated goods is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall also be imposed; if the value of the goods is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the value of the goods shall also be imposed; the seed production and operation license may be revoked:

(1) Producing or operating seeds without a seed production and operation license;
(2) Obtaining a seed production and operation license by improper means such as deception or bribery;
(3) Producing or operating seeds not in accordance with the provisions of the seed production and operation license;
(4) Forging, altering, buying, selling, or lending a seed production and operation license;
(5) No longer having the isolation and cultivation conditions for seed propagation, or no longer having a seed production site free of quarantine pests or a seed collection forest designated by the competent department of forestry and grassland of the people's government at or above the county level, yet continuing to engage in seed production.(6) Producing seeds without following seed inspection and quarantine procedures.

For entities whose seed production and operation licenses are revoked, the legal representative and directly responsible supervisors shall not serve as the legal representative or senior management of a seed enterprise within five years from the date of the penalty decision.

Article 77: In violation of Articles 21, 22, and 23 of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, confiscate illegal gains and seeds, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan:

(1) Promoting or selling crop varieties that should have been but have not been approved;
(2) Promoting or selling as improved varieties forest tree varieties that should have been but have not been approved;
(3) Promoting or selling crop varieties or improved forest tree varieties that should have been discontinued from promotion or sale;
(4) Promoting crop varieties that should have been but have not been registered, or selling them under the name of registered varieties;
(5) Promoting crop varieties whose registration has been revoked, or selling them under the name of registered varieties.

In violation of Articles 23 and 41 of this Law, publishing advertisements for crop varieties that should have been but have not been approved or registered, or where the description of the variety's main characteristics in the advertisement is inconsistent with the approval or registration announcement, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 78: In violation of Articles 57, 59, and 60 of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, confiscate illegal gains and seeds; if the value of the illegally produced or operated goods is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the value is 10,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than three times but not more than five times the value of the goods shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the seed production and operation license shall be revoked:

(1) Importing or exporting seeds without permission;
(2) Selling seeds produced for overseas entities within China;
(3) Selling as seeds within China the harvest from trial introductions of crop or forest tree seeds imported from abroad;
(4) Importing or exporting counterfeit, inferior seeds, or seeds that are prohibited from import or export by national regulations.

Article 79: In violation of Articles 36, 38, 39, and 40 of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections, and a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Selling seeds that should be packaged but are not packaged;
(2) Selling seeds without usage instructions or with label content not conforming to regulations;
(3) Altering labels;
(4) Failing to establish or maintain seed production and operation records as required;
(5) A seed producer or operator establishing branches in other locations, specializing in selling packaged seeds that are not to be repackaged, or producing or selling seeds on commission without filing as required.

Article 80: In violation of Article 8 of this Law, encroaching upon or destroying germplasm resources, or privately collecting or cutting nationally protected natural germplasm resources, shall be ordered by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the illegal act, confiscate the germplasm resources and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 81: In violation of Article 11 of this Law, providing germplasm resources to overseas entities or introducing them from abroad, or cooperating with overseas institutions or individuals in research and utilization of germplasm resources, the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the State Council or the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall confiscate the germplasm resources and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

If germplasm resources are carried or transported out of the country without the approval documents from the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities, the customs authorities shall detain the germplasm resources and transfer them to the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for handling.

Article 82: In violation of Article 35 of this Law, prematurely harvesting, damaging mother trees, or collecting seeds from inferior forests or inferior mother trees shall be ordered by the competent forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the seed collection act, confiscate the collected seeds, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the value of the collected seeds.

Article 83: In violation of Article 17 of this Law, if a seed enterprise engages in fraudulent activities, the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall impose a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan; the enterprise shall not apply for variety approval in accordance with Article 17 of this Law; if losses are caused to seed users or other seed producers and operators, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 84: In violation of Article 44 of this Law, failing to use improved forest tree varieties according to the plan formulated by the competent forestry and grassland authorities, the competent forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at the same level shall order corrections within a specified period; if corrections are not made within the period, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 85: In violation of Article 53 of this Law, conducting inoculation tests of quarantine pests in seed production bases shall be ordered by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government at or above the county level to cease the test, and a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 86: In violation of Article 49 of this Law, refusing or obstructing the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities from lawfully carrying out supervision and inspection, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the entity may be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security authorities shall impose public security administration penalties according to law.

Article 87: In violation of Article 13 of this Law, privately trading breeding achievements, causing economic losses to the entity, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 88: In violation of Article 43 of this Law, forcing seed users to purchase or use seeds against their will, causing losses to the users, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 89: Violations of the provisions of this Law that constitute a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 90: The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Germplasm resources" refer to the basic materials for breeding new plant varieties, including the reproductive materials of cultivated and wild species of various plants, as well as the genetic materials of various plants artificially created using the aforementioned reproductive materials.

(2) "Variety" refers to a plant group that has been artificially bred or discovered and improved, with consistent morphological and biological characteristics and relatively stable genetic traits.

(3) "Major crops" refer to rice, wheat, corn, cotton, and soybeans.

(4) "Major forest trees" shall be determined and announced by the competent forestry and grassland authorities of the State Council; the competent forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may determine no more than eight other major forest trees in addition to those determined by the competent forestry and grassland authorities of the State Council.

(5) "Improved forest tree varieties" refer to approved major forest tree varieties that, within a certain region, are significantly superior to the currently main planting materials in terms of yield, adaptability, resistance, etc., in terms of reproductive and planting materials.

(6) "Novelty" means that, before the date of filing an application for a new plant variety right, the variety's seeds have been sold or promoted by the applicant or with the applicant's consent for no more than one year within China; abroad, for woody or vine plants, no more than six years, and for other plants, no more than four years.

For plant genera or species newly added to the national list of protected plant varieties after the implementation of this Law, if an application for a new plant variety right is filed within one year from the date of publication of the list, and the seeds of the variety have been sold or promoted within China for no more than four years, novelty is deemed to exist.

Except for the loss of novelty due to sales or promotion activities, the following circumstances are deemed to have lost novelty:

1. The variety has been confirmed by the competent agricultural and rural affairs or forestry and grassland authorities of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government to have actually spread based on the sown area;
2. A crop variety has been approved or registered for more than two years without applying for a new plant variety right.

(7) "Distinctness" means that a plant variety has one or more traits that are clearly different from known varieties.

(8) "Uniformity" means that, except for predictable natural variations, the relevant characteristics or traits among individuals within a plant variety population are consistent.

(9) "Stability" means that the main traits of a plant variety remain unchanged after repeated propagation or at the end of a specific propagation cycle.

(10) "Essentially derived variety" means a variety that is essentially derived from an initial variety, or from an essentially derived variety of that initial variety, is clearly distinguishable from the initial variety, and, except for the differences resulting from the derivation, conforms to the initial variety in the expression of the essential characteristics resulting from the genotype or combination of genotypes of the initial variety.

(11) "Known variety" means a plant variety that has been accepted for application, or has passed variety approval, variety registration, or new variety protection, or has been sold or promoted.

(12) "Label" refers to specific patterns and text descriptions printed, affixed, fixed, or attached to the surface of seeds or seed packaging.

Article 91: The state strengthens the protection of germplasm resources of Chinese medicinal materials and supports scientific research on the breeding technology of Chinese medicinal materials.

Activities such as germplasm resource management, breeding, production, operation, and management of grass seeds, tobacco seeds, Chinese medicinal material seeds, and edible fungus strains shall be carried out with reference to this Law.

Article 92: This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2016.
