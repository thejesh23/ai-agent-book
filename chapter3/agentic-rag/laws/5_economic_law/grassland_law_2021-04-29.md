# Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on June 18, 1985

Revised at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 28, 2002

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics adopted at the 3rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eight Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Road Traffic Safety adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on April 29, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of protecting, building and rationally utilizing grasslands, improving the ecological environment, maintaining biodiversity, developing modern animal husbandry, and promoting sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities such as grassland planning, protection, construction, utilization and management conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "grasslands" means natural grasslands and artificially cultivated grasslands.

Article 3 The State adopts the policy of scientific planning, comprehensive protection, prioritized construction and rational utilization of grasslands, so as to promote the sustainable utilization of grasslands and the coordinated development of ecology, economy and society.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the administration of the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, and incorporate the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands into the plans for national economic and social development.

People's governments at all levels shall strengthen publicity and education on the protection, construction and rational utilization of grasslands.

Article 5 Every unit and individual has the obligation to abide by grassland laws and regulations and to protect grasslands, and at the same time has the right to supervise, report and accuse any act that violates grassland laws and regulations or damages grasslands.

Article 6 The State encourages and supports scientific research on the protection, construction, utilization and monitoring of grasslands, promotes advanced technologies and achievements, and trains scientific and technical personnel.

Article 7 The State gives awards to units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in grassland administration, protection, construction, rational utilization and scientific research.

Article 8 The competent department of grassland administration under the State Council shall be in charge of the supervision and administration of grasslands nationwide.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of the supervision and administration of grasslands within their respective administrative areas.

The people's governments of townships (towns) shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands within their respective administrative areas, and may, as needed, assign full-time or part-time personnel to be responsible for specific supervision and inspection work.

## Chapter II Ownership and Use Rights of Grasslands

Article 9 Grasslands are owned by the State, except those which are owned by collectives as provided for by law. The State-owned grasslands shall be exercised by the State Council on behalf of the State.

No unit or individual may encroach upon, buy, sell or illegally transfer grasslands in any other form.

Article 10 State-owned grasslands may, in accordance with law, be designated for use by units owned by the whole people, collective economic organizations, etc.

Units that use grasslands shall fulfill the obligations of protecting, building and rationally utilizing the grasslands.

Article 11 For State-owned grasslands designated for use by units owned by the whole people, collective economic organizations, etc. in accordance with law, the people's governments at or above the county level shall register, issue certificates of use rights, and confirm the right to use the grasslands.

For State-owned grasslands for which use rights have not been designated, the people's governments at or above the county level shall register them in a register and be responsible for their protection and administration.

For grasslands owned by collectives, the people's governments at the county level shall register, issue certificates of ownership, and confirm the ownership of the grasslands.

Where the ownership or use rights of grasslands are changed in accordance with law, the procedures for changing the registration of grassland ownership or use rights shall be completed.

Article 12 The lawfully registered ownership and use rights of grasslands shall be protected by law, and no unit or individual may infringe upon them.

Article 13 Grasslands owned by collectives or State-owned grasslands designated for use by collective economic organizations in accordance with law may be contracted out for operation to households or joint households within the collective economic organization.

During the term of a grassland contract, the grasslands used by the contract operator shall not be adjusted; if individual adjustments are truly necessary, the consent of more than two-thirds of the members of the village (herdsmen) meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the village (herdsmen) representatives must be obtained, and the matter shall be reported to the people's government of the township (town) and the competent department of grassland administration under the people's government at the county level for approval.

Where grasslands owned by collectives or State-owned grasslands designated for use by collective economic organizations in accordance with law are contracted out for operation to units or individuals outside the collective economic organization, the consent of more than two-thirds of the members of the village (herdsmen) meeting of the collective economic organization or more than two-thirds of the village (herdsmen) representatives must be obtained, and the matter shall be reported to the people's government of the township (town) for approval.

Article 14 For the contracting out of grasslands for operation, the party awarding the contract and the contractor shall sign a written contract. The contents of a grassland contract shall include the rights and obligations of both parties, the boundaries, area and grade of the contracted grassland, the term of the contract and its start and end dates, the purpose of use of the contracted grassland, and liability for breach of contract, etc. Upon the expiration of the contract term, the original contractor shall have the priority right to contract under the same conditions.

Units and individuals that contract grasslands for operation shall fulfill the obligations of protecting, building and rationally utilizing the grasslands in accordance with the purposes stipulated in the contract.

Article 15 The right to contract grasslands for operation shall be protected by law and may be transferred in accordance with law on a voluntary and compensatory basis.

The transferee of the right to contract grasslands for operation must have the capability to engage in animal husbandry production and shall fulfill the obligations of protecting, building and rationally utilizing the grasslands in accordance with the purposes stipulated in the contract.

The transfer of the right to contract grasslands for operation shall be subject to the consent of the party awarding the contract. The transfer period stipulated in the transfer contract between the contractor and the transferee shall not exceed the remaining period of the original contract.

Article 16 Disputes over the ownership or use rights of grasslands shall be resolved through negotiation between the parties; if negotiation fails, the matter shall be handled by the relevant people's government.

Disputes between units shall be handled by the people's government at or above the county level; disputes between individuals, or between individuals and units, shall be handled by the people's government of the township (town) or the people's government at or above the county level.

If a party is dissatisfied with the handling decision of the relevant people's government, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with law.

Before a dispute over grassland ownership or use rights is resolved, neither party may change the current state of grassland utilization, nor damage the grasslands or facilities on the grasslands.

## Chapter III Planning

Article 17 The State implements a unified planning system for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands. The competent department of grassland administration under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, formulate a national plan for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, which shall be implemented after being approved by the State Council.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with the relevant departments at the same level, formulate plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands within their respective administrative areas based on the plans of the next higher level for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, which shall be implemented after being approved by the people's governments at the same level.

Where an approved plan for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands truly needs to be adjusted or modified, the matter must be approved by the original approving authority.

Article 18 The formulation of plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands shall be based on the plans for national economic and social development and shall follow the following principles:

(1) Improving the ecological environment, maintaining biodiversity, and promoting the sustainable utilization of grasslands;

(2) Taking existing grasslands as the basis, adapting measures to local conditions, making overall plans, and providing guidance by category;

(3) Giving priority to protection, strengthening construction, improving in batches, and utilizing rationally;

(4) Combining ecological, economic and social benefits.

Article 19 Plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands shall include: the objectives and measures for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, the functional zoning of grasslands and the overall deployment of various constructions, and various specialized plans, etc.

Article 20 Plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands shall be aligned with the overall plan for land utilization, and coordinated with the plan for environmental protection, the plan for water and soil conservation, the plan for desertification prevention and control, the plan for water resources, the long-term plan for forestry, the overall plan for cities, the plans for villages and towns, and other relevant plans.

Article 21 Once approved, plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands must be strictly implemented.

Article 22 The State establishes a grassland survey system.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with the relevant departments at the same level, conduct regular grassland surveys; grassland owners or users shall support and cooperate with the surveys and provide relevant data.

Article 23 The competent department of grassland administration under the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments under the State Council, formulate national standards for grassland grading.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the results of grassland surveys and the quality of grasslands, and in accordance with the standards for grassland grading, grade and classify the grasslands.

Article 24 The State establishes a grassland statistical system.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the people's governments at or above the county level and the statistical departments at the same level shall jointly formulate measures for grassland statistical surveys, conduct statistics on the area, grade, grass yield, and livestock carrying capacity of grasslands in accordance with law, and regularly publish grassland statistical data.

Grassland statistical data shall serve as the basis for the formulation of plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands by people's governments at all levels.

Article 25 The State establishes a monitoring and early warning system for grassland production and ecology.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct dynamic monitoring of the basic conditions of grasslands, including area, grade, vegetation composition, production capacity, natural disasters, and biological disasters, and promptly provide dynamic monitoring and early warning information services to the people's governments at the same level and relevant departments.

## Chapter IV Construction

Article 26 People's governments at or above the county level shall increase investment in grassland construction and support grassland construction.

The State encourages units and individuals to invest in grassland construction, and protects the legitimate rights and interests of grassland construction investors in accordance with the principle of "who invests, who benefits."

Article 27 The State encourages and supports the construction of artificially cultivated grasslands, the improvement of natural grasslands, and the construction of forage and feed bases, so as to stabilize and improve the production capacity of grasslands.

Article 28 People's governments at or above the county level shall support, encourage and guide farmers and herdsmen to carry out the construction of production and living facilities such as grassland fencing, forage and feed storage, livestock sheds, and herdsmen settlement sites.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall support the construction of grassland water conservancy facilities, develop water-saving irrigation on grasslands, and improve the conditions for drinking water for humans and livestock.

Article 29 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, strengthen the construction of grass seed bases, and encourage the breeding, introduction and promotion of improved grass varieties.

New grass varieties must be examined and approved by the National Grass Variety Approval Committee, and may only be promoted after being announced by the competent department of grassland administration under the State Council. The introduction of grass seeds from abroad must be approved in accordance with law.

The competent departments of grassland administration under the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with law, strengthen the supervision and administration of the production, processing, quarantine and inspection of grass seeds to ensure the quality of grass seeds.

Article 30 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in a planned manner, carry out the construction of grassland fire prevention facilities such as fire monitoring, fire prevention material reserves, and firebreaks, so as to ensure fire prevention needs.

Article 31 For grasslands that are degraded, desertified, salinized, rocky or subject to water and soil erosion, local people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, designate treatment areas and organize special treatment.

Large-scale comprehensive treatment of grasslands shall be included in the national land improvement plan.

Article 32 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands, allocate funds in their plans for national economic and social development at the same level for grassland improvement, artificial grass planting and grass seed production, and no unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate such funds; the financial departments and audit departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and administration.

## Chapter V Utilization

Article 33 Grassland contract operators shall utilize grasslands rationally and shall not exceed the livestock carrying capacity verified by the competent department of grassland administration; grassland contract operators shall take measures such as planting and storing forage and feed, increasing the supply of forage and feed, adjusting and disposing of livestock, optimizing herd structure, and increasing the slaughter rate to maintain a balance between grass and livestock.

The standards for livestock carrying capacity of grasslands and the measures for managing the balance between grass and livestock shall be formulated by the competent department of grassland administration under the State Council.

Article 34 Grassland contract operators in pastoral areas shall implement rotational grazing in designated areas, rationally allocate livestock herds, and utilize grasslands in a balanced manner.

Article 35 The State advocates the raising of livestock in pens in agricultural areas, semi-agricultural and semi-pastoral areas, and pastoral areas where conditions permit. Grassland contract operators shall, based on the types and quantities of livestock raised, adjust and store forage and feed, adopt new technologies such as silage and forage processing, and gradually change the production method of relying on natural grassland grazing.

In areas where grazing is prohibited, rested, or rotated on grasslands, the State provides grain and financial subsidies to those who implement pen feeding and stall feeding. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council or the relevant departments authorized by the State Council.

Article 36 The competent departments of grassland administration under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall stipulate reasonable mowing periods, seed collection periods, stubble heights, and mowing and collection intensities for mowing pastures and wild grass seed bases, and implement rotational mowing and rotational collection.

Article 37 In special circumstances such as natural disasters, where temporary adjustment of grassland use is necessary, the matter shall be resolved through negotiation between the parties on the principle of voluntariness and mutual benefit; where temporary cross-county adjustment of grassland use is necessary, the matter shall be resolved through negotiation organized by the relevant people's governments at the county level or the common higher-level people's government.

Article 38 For mineral exploration and engineering construction, grasslands shall not be occupied or shall be occupied as little as possible; where it is truly necessary to expropriate, requisition or use grasslands, the matter must be reviewed and approved by the competent department of grassland administration under the people's government at or above the provincial level, and then the procedures for approving construction land shall be handled in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations on land administration.

Article 39 Where grasslands owned by collectives are expropriated or requisitioned for construction, compensation shall be given in accordance with the provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China; where State-owned grasslands are used for construction, compensation shall be given to grassland contract operators in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Where grasslands are expropriated, requisitioned or used for construction, a grassland vegetation restoration fee shall be paid. The grassland vegetation restoration fee shall be used for special purposes, and the competent department of grassland administration shall use it for restoring grassland vegetation in accordance with the regulations. No unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate such fees. The measures for the collection, use and administration of the grassland vegetation restoration fee shall be formulated by the competent department of price under the State Council and the financial department under the State Council together with the competent department of grassland administration under the State Council.

Article 40 Where temporary occupation of grasslands is necessary, the matter must be reviewed and approved by the competent department of grassland administration under the local people's government at or above the county level.

The term of temporary occupation of grasslands shall not exceed two years, and no permanent buildings or structures may be constructed on the temporarily occupied grasslands; upon the expiration of the occupation period, the land-using unit must restore the grassland vegetation and promptly return the land.

Article 41 Where the construction of engineering facilities directly serving grassland protection and animal husbandry production on grasslands requires the use of grasslands, the matter shall be approved by the competent department of grassland administration under the people's government at or above the county level; where the construction of other projects requires the conversion of grasslands into land for non-animal husbandry production, the procedures for approving construction land must be handled in accordance with law.

The term "engineering facilities directly serving grassland protection and animal husbandry production" as mentioned in the preceding paragraph refers to:

(1) Facilities for producing and storing grass seeds and forage and feed;

(2) Livestock sheds, breeding stations, shearing stations, dipping baths, and drinking water facilities for humans and livestock;

(3) Scientific research, experimental and demonstration bases;

(4) Grassland fire prevention and irrigation facilities.

## Chapter VI Protection

Article 42 The State implements a basic grassland protection system. The following grasslands shall be designated as basic grasslands and subject to strict administration:

(1) Important grazing pastures;

(2) Mowing pastures;

(3) Artificially cultivated grasslands used for animal husbandry production, grasslands returned from farming, improved grasslands, and grass seed bases;

(4) Grasslands that have special functions in regulating climate, conserving water sources, maintaining water and soil, and preventing wind and fixing sand;

(5) Grasslands that serve as the living environment for key State-protected wild animals and plants;

(6) Grassland scientific research and teaching experiment bases;

(7) Other grasslands that the State Council stipulates should be designated as basic grasslands.

The measures for the protection and administration of basic grasslands shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 43 The competent department of grassland administration under the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may establish grassland nature reserves in the following areas in accordance with the relevant provisions on the administration of nature reserves:

(1) Areas with representative types of grasslands;

(2) Areas where rare and endangered wild animals and plants are distributed;

(3) Grasslands with important ecological functions and economic and scientific research value.

Article 44 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with law, strengthen the protection and administration of rare and endangered wild plants and germplasm resources on grasslands.

Article 45 The State implements a system of determining livestock numbers based on grass supply and maintaining a balance between grass and livestock on grasslands. The competent departments of grassland administration under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the standards for livestock carrying capacity of grasslands formulated by the competent department of grassland administration under the State Council and in light of local actual conditions, regularly verify the livestock carrying capacity of grasslands. People's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to prevent overgrazing.

Article 46 The reclamation of grasslands is prohibited. For reclaimed grasslands that have severe water and soil erosion, a tendency towards desertification, or require ecological environment improvement, the land shall be returned from farming to grassland in a planned and step-by-step manner; where desertification, salinization, or rocky desertification has already occurred, treatment shall be carried out within a specified time limit.

Article 47 For grasslands that are severely degraded, desertified, salinized, rocky, or located in ecologically fragile areas, a system of grazing prohibition and grazing rest shall be implemented.

Article 48 The State supports the lawful implementation of returning farmland to grassland and the prohibition and rest of grazing. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council or the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Farmers and herdsmen who implement the return of farmland to grassland within the scope of the plan approved by the State Council shall be given subsidies for grain, cash, and grass seed costs in accordance with State regulations. After the completion of the return of farmland to grassland, the competent department of grassland administration under the people's government at or above the county level shall verify and register the matter, handle the procedures for changing the land use in accordance with law, and issue certificates of grassland ownership or use rights.

Article 49 It is prohibited to dig and collect plants or engage in other activities that damage grassland vegetation on grasslands in deserts, semi-deserts, and grasslands that are severely degraded, desertified, salinized, rocky, subject to water and soil erosion, or located in ecologically fragile areas.

Article 50 Operations such as soil extraction, sand extraction, and stone extraction on grasslands shall be reported to the competent department of grassland administration under the people's government at the county level for approval; where mineral resources are exploited, the relevant procedures shall also be handled in accordance with law.

Those approved to engage in the activities listed in the first paragraph of this Article on grasslands shall operate within the specified time and area, in the approved manner of extraction, and take measures to protect grassland vegetation.

Where the activities listed in the first paragraph of this Article are to be carried out on grasslands used by others, the consent of the grassland user shall also be obtained in advance.

Article 51 The planting of pasture grasses or feed crops on grasslands shall conform to the plans for the protection, construction and utilization of grasslands; the competent departments of grassland administration under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and administration to prevent grassland desertification and water and soil erosion.**Article 52** Commercial tourism activities on grasslands shall comply with relevant plans for grassland protection, development, and utilization, and shall not infringe upon the lawful rights and interests of grassland owners, users, or contractors, nor damage grassland vegetation.

**Article 53** Grassland fire prevention shall follow the principle of putting prevention first and combining prevention with suppression.

People's governments at all levels shall establish a grassland fire prevention responsibility system, designate a grassland fire prevention period, formulate grassland fire prevention and suppression plans, and earnestly carry out the prevention and suppression of grassland fires.

**Article 54** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall properly organize and manage the prevention and control of rodent infestations, plant diseases, insect pests, and poisonous weeds on grasslands. The grassland administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to strengthen monitoring, early warning, investigation, and control of rodent infestations, plant diseases, insect pests, and poisonous weeds on grasslands, and organize research and promote comprehensive control methods.

The use of highly toxic, high-residue pesticides, and those that may cause secondary poisoning is prohibited on grasslands.

**Article 55** Except for emergency rescue, disaster relief, and motor vehicles used for herder relocation, motor vehicles are prohibited from leaving roads to drive on grasslands, thereby damaging grassland vegetation. If it is necessary to leave roads to drive on grasslands for activities such as geological exploration or scientific investigation, the driving area and route shall be reported in advance to the local grassland administrative department of the county-level people's government, and driving shall be conducted within the reported area and along the reported route.

## Chapter VII Supervision and Inspection

**Article 56** The grassland administrative department of the State Council and the grassland administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level in provinces and autonomous regions with large grassland areas shall establish grassland supervision and management institutions. These institutions are responsible for supervising and inspecting the implementation of grassland laws and regulations, and for investigating and punishing violations of grassland laws and regulations.

Grassland administrative departments and grassland supervision and management institutions shall strengthen the construction of law enforcement teams and improve the political and professional quality of grassland inspectors. Grassland inspectors shall be devoted to their duties and enforce the law impartially.

**Article 57** When performing their supervision and inspection duties, grassland inspectors have the right to take the following measures:

(1) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide documents and materials concerning grassland ownership for review or copying;
(2) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide explanations on issues such as grassland ownership;
(3) Enter the site of the violation to take photographs, record videos, and conduct surveys;
(4) Order the unit or individual under inspection to cease acts that violate grassland laws and regulations and to fulfill statutory obligations.

**Article 58** The grassland administrative department of the State Council and the grassland administrative departments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strengthen the training and assessment of grassland inspectors.

**Article 59** Relevant units and individuals shall support and cooperate with the supervision and inspection work of grassland inspectors and shall not refuse or obstruct grassland inspectors from performing their duties according to law.

When performing their supervision and inspection duties, grassland inspectors shall present their law enforcement credentials to the units and individuals under inspection.

**Article 60** For acts violating grassland laws and regulations, administrative penalties shall be imposed according to law. If the relevant grassland administrative department fails to make an administrative penalty decision, the superior grassland administrative department has the authority to order the relevant department to make a decision or to make the decision directly.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

**Article 61** If staff members of grassland administrative departments or other relevant state organs neglect their duties, abuse their power, fail to perform supervision and management duties according to law, or fail to investigate illegal acts found, causing serious consequences, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

**Article 62** If funds for grassland improvement, artificial grass planting, grass seed production, or grassland vegetation restoration fees are intercepted or misappropriated, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

**Article 63** If a unit or individual without the authority to approve the expropriation, requisition, or use of grasslands illegally approves such actions, or exceeds the approval authority, or violates the legal procedures for approval, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law. Documents for the illegal approval of expropriation, requisition, or use of grasslands are invalid. The illegally approved grasslands shall be recovered; if the party concerned refuses to return them, it shall be deemed as illegal use of grasslands.

If the illegal approval of expropriation, requisition, or use of grasslands causes losses to the party concerned, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 64** If grasslands are bought, sold, or illegally transferred in other forms, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, the grassland administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order correction within a time limit, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains.

**Article 65** If grasslands are used illegally without approval or by obtaining approval through fraudulent means, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, the grassland administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order the return of the illegally used grasslands. If grasslands are converted to construction land in violation of grassland protection, development, and utilization plans, the newly built buildings and other facilities on the illegally used grasslands shall be demolished within a time limit, grassland vegetation shall be restored, and a fine of not less than six times but not more than twelve times the average output value of the grasslands in the three years preceding the illegal use shall be imposed.

**Article 66** If grasslands are illegally reclaimed, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, the grassland administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order the cessation of the illegal act, restore vegetation within a time limit, confiscate illegal property and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if losses are caused to grassland owners or users, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 67** If plants are dug up or other activities that damage grassland vegetation are carried out on grasslands in desert, semi-desert, severely degraded, desertified, salinized, rocky, or water-eroded areas, as well as on grasslands in ecologically fragile areas, the grassland administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate illegal property and illegal gains, and may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed; if losses are caused to grassland owners or users, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 68** If activities such as soil extraction, sand extraction, or stone quarrying are carried out on grasslands without approval or not in accordance with the prescribed time, area, and method, the grassland administrative department of the county-level people's government shall order the cessation of the illegal act, restore vegetation within a time limit, confiscate illegal property and illegal gains, and may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed; if losses are caused to grassland owners or users, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 69** If, in violation of Article 52 of this Law, commercial tourism activities are carried out on grasslands, damaging grassland vegetation, the grassland administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order the cessation of the illegal act, restore vegetation within a time limit, confiscate illegal gains, and may impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than two times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not less than six times but not more than twelve times the average output value of the grasslands in the three years preceding the damage may be imposed; if losses are caused to grassland owners or users, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 70** If motor vehicles, other than those for emergency rescue, disaster relief, or herder relocation, leave roads to drive on grasslands, or if activities such as geological exploration or scientific investigation are carried out without prior reporting to the local grassland administrative department of the county-level people's government or without driving within the reported area and along the reported route, damaging grassland vegetation, the grassland administrative department of the county-level people's government shall order the cessation of the illegal act, restore vegetation within a time limit, and may impose a fine of not less than three times but not more than nine times the average output value of the grasslands in the three years preceding the damage; if losses are caused to grassland owners or users, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

**Article 71** If permanent buildings or structures are built on temporarily occupied grasslands, the grassland administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order demolition within a time limit; if demolition is not carried out within the time limit, compulsory demolition shall be carried out according to law, and the costs shall be borne by the violator.

If, upon the expiration of the temporary occupation period, the land-using unit fails to restore grassland vegetation, the grassland administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall, within its authority, order restoration within a time limit; if restoration is not carried out within the time limit, the grassland administrative department shall carry out the restoration on its behalf, and the costs shall be borne by the violator.

**Article 72** If grassland protection, development, and utilization plans are altered without approval, the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a time limit; administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel.

**Article 73** Measures for correcting or penalizing violations of the provisions of this Law on the grass-livestock balance system, where the number of livestock raised exceeds the grassland carrying capacity standard verified by the grassland administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level, shall be stipulated by the People's Congress or its Standing Committee of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

**Article 74** The term "natural grasslands" as used in the second paragraph of Article 2 of this Law includes grasslands, grassy hills, and grassy slopes. "Artificial grasslands" includes improved grasslands and grasslands converted from farmland, but does not include urban lawns.

**Article 75** This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 2003.
