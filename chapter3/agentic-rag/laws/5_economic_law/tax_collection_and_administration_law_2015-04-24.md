# Tax Collection and Administration Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 27th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 4, 1992

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Tax Collection and Administration Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on February 28, 1995

Revised at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on April 28, 2001

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Twelve Laws Including the Cultural Relics Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" at the Third Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Port Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen tax collection and administration, regulate tax collection and payment, safeguard national tax revenue, protect the legitimate rights and interests of taxpayers, and promote economic and social development.

Article 2 The collection and administration of all taxes collected by tax authorities in accordance with the law shall be governed by this Law.

Article 3 The imposition, suspension, reduction, exemption, refund, and supplementary collection of taxes shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of laws; where laws authorize the State Council to prescribe, the implementation shall be in accordance with the administrative regulations formulated by the State Council.

No organ, unit, or individual may, in violation of laws or administrative regulations, arbitrarily make decisions on the imposition, suspension, reduction, exemption, refund, or supplementary collection of taxes, or any other decisions that contravene tax laws and administrative regulations.

Article 4 Units and individuals that are obligated to pay taxes under laws and administrative regulations are taxpayers.

Units and individuals that are obligated to withhold and remit taxes, or collect and remit taxes under laws and administrative regulations are withholding agents.

Taxpayers and withholding agents must pay taxes, withhold and remit taxes, or collect and remit taxes in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 5 The tax administration department of the State Council shall be in charge of tax collection and administration nationwide. Local state taxation bureaus and local taxation bureaus shall conduct tax collection and administration respectively within the scope prescribed by the State Council.

Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership or coordination over tax collection and administration within their respective administrative regions in accordance with the law, support tax authorities in performing their duties according to law, calculate tax amounts based on statutory tax rates, and collect taxes according to law.

All relevant departments and units shall support and assist tax authorities in performing their duties according to law.

No unit or individual may obstruct tax authorities from performing their duties according to law.

Article 6 The state shall systematically equip tax authorities at all levels with modern information technology, strengthen the modernization of tax collection and administration information systems, and establish and improve a system for information sharing between tax authorities and other government administrative authorities.

Taxpayers, withholding agents, and other relevant units shall truthfully provide tax authorities with information related to tax payment, tax withholding and remittance, and tax collection and remittance in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 7 Tax authorities shall extensively publicize tax laws and administrative regulations, popularize tax knowledge, and provide free tax consultation services to taxpayers.

Article 8 Taxpayers and withholding agents have the right to obtain from tax authorities information on state tax laws and administrative regulations and matters related to tax payment procedures.

Taxpayers and withholding agents have the right to request tax authorities to keep their information confidential. Tax authorities shall keep the information of taxpayers and withholding agents confidential in accordance with the law.

Taxpayers have the right to apply for tax reductions, exemptions, and refunds in accordance with the law.

Taxpayers and withholding agents have the right to present statements and defenses regarding decisions made by tax authorities; they have the right to apply for administrative reconsideration, initiate administrative litigation, and claim state compensation in accordance with the law.

Taxpayers and withholding agents have the right to accuse and report illegal or disciplinary acts of tax authorities and tax officials.

Article 9 Tax authorities shall strengthen team building and improve the political and professional quality of tax officials.

Tax authorities and tax officials must enforce the law impartially, be devoted to their duties, be honest and upright, treat others politely, provide civilized services, respect and protect the rights of taxpayers and withholding agents, and accept supervision in accordance with the law.

Tax officials shall not solicit or accept bribes, engage in malpractice for personal gain, neglect their duties, fail to collect or under-collect taxes due; nor shall they abuse their power to over-collect taxes or deliberately make things difficult for taxpayers and withholding agents.

Article 10 Tax authorities at all levels shall establish and improve internal control and supervision management systems.

Higher-level tax authorities shall supervise the law enforcement activities of lower-level tax authorities in accordance with the law.

Tax authorities at all levels shall supervise and inspect the compliance of their staff with laws, administrative regulations, and standards of integrity and self-discipline.

Article 11 The responsibilities of tax authorities' personnel responsible for collection, administration, inspection, and administrative reconsideration shall be clearly defined, and shall be mutually separate and mutually constraining.

Article 12 Tax officials who have an interest in a taxpayer, withholding agent, or a tax violation case when collecting taxes or investigating tax violations shall recuse themselves.

Article 13 Any unit or individual has the right to report acts that violate tax laws and administrative regulations. The organ receiving the report and the organ responsible for investigation shall keep the reporter confidential. Tax authorities shall reward reporters in accordance with regulations.

Article 14 For the purposes of this Law, "tax authorities" refer to state taxation bureaus, local taxation bureaus, tax sub-bureaus, tax offices, and tax agencies established in accordance with State Council regulations and publicly announced to society.

## Chapter II Tax Administration

### Section 1 Tax Registration

Article 15 Enterprises, branches established by enterprises in other localities, places engaged in production or business operations, individual industrial and commercial households, and public institutions engaged in production or business operations (hereinafter collectively referred to as "taxpayers engaged in production or business operations") shall, within 30 days from the date of obtaining a business license, present relevant documents to the tax authority to apply for tax registration. The tax authority shall process the registration on the day of receiving the application and issue a tax registration certificate.

The administrative authority for industry and commerce shall regularly notify the tax authority of the registration and issuance of business licenses.

The scope and procedures for tax registration for taxpayers other than those specified in the first paragraph of this Article and for withholding agents to register for tax withholding shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 16 Where the content of tax registration of a taxpayer engaged in production or business operations changes, the taxpayer shall, within 30 days from the date of completing the change registration with the administrative authority for industry and commerce, or before applying to the administrative authority for industry and commerce for cancellation of registration, present relevant documents to the tax authority to apply for a change or cancellation of tax registration.

Article 17 Taxpayers engaged in production or business operations shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, hold their tax registration certificate to open basic deposit accounts and other deposit accounts with banks or other financial institutions, and report all account numbers to the tax authority.

Banks and other financial institutions shall record the tax registration certificate number in the accounts of taxpayers engaged in production or business operations, and record the account numbers of such taxpayers in the tax registration certificate.

When a tax authority lawfully inquires about the accounts opened by a taxpayer engaged in production or business operations, the relevant bank or other financial institution shall provide assistance.

Article 18 Taxpayers shall use their tax registration certificate in accordance with the regulations of the tax administration department of the State Council. The tax registration certificate shall not be lent, altered, damaged, sold, or forged.

### Section 2 Management of Accounts and Vouchers

Article 19 Taxpayers and withholding agents shall set up account books in accordance with relevant laws, administrative regulations, and the regulations of the finance and tax administration departments of the State Council, and shall keep accounts and conduct accounting based on lawful and valid vouchers.

Article 20 The financial and accounting systems or financial and accounting processing methods and accounting software of taxpayers engaged in production or business operations shall be submitted to the tax authority for recordation.

Where the financial and accounting systems or financial and accounting processing methods of taxpayers or withholding agents conflict with the tax-related provisions of the State Council or the finance and tax administration departments of the State Council, the calculation of taxes payable, taxes to be withheld and remitted, and taxes to be collected and remitted shall be in accordance with the tax-related provisions of the State Council or the finance and tax administration departments of the State Council.

Article 21 Tax authorities are the competent authorities for invoices, responsible for the management and supervision of invoice printing, procurement, issuance, acquisition, custody, and cancellation.

Units and individuals shall issue, use, and obtain invoices in accordance with regulations when purchasing or selling goods, providing or receiving business services, or engaging in other business activities.

The measures for invoice management shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 22 Special VAT invoices shall be printed by enterprises designated by the tax administration department of the State Council; other invoices shall be printed by enterprises designated by the state taxation bureaus or local taxation bureaus of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, in accordance with the regulations of the tax administration department of the State Council.

No invoices may be printed without the designation of the tax authorities specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 23 The state shall actively promote the use of tax control devices based on the needs of tax collection and administration. Taxpayers shall install and use tax control devices in accordance with regulations, and shall not damage or arbitrarily alter tax control devices.

Article 24 Taxpayers engaged in production or business operations and withholding agents must keep account books, accounting vouchers, tax payment receipts, and other relevant materials within the retention period prescribed by the finance and tax administration departments of the State Council.

Account books, accounting vouchers, tax payment receipts, and other relevant materials shall not be forged, altered, or arbitrarily damaged.

### Section 3 Tax Declaration

Article 25 Taxpayers must truthfully handle tax declaration in accordance with the declaration period and declaration content prescribed by laws or administrative regulations or determined by tax authorities in accordance with laws or administrative regulations, and submit tax declaration forms, financial accounting statements, and other tax-related materials that tax authorities require taxpayers to submit based on actual needs.

Withholding agents must truthfully submit reports on taxes withheld and remitted or collected and remitted in accordance with the declaration period and declaration content prescribed by laws or administrative regulations or determined by tax authorities in accordance with laws or administrative regulations, as well as other relevant materials that tax authorities require withholding agents to submit based on actual needs.

Article 26 Taxpayers and withholding agents may directly go to tax authorities to handle tax declaration or submit reports on taxes withheld and remitted or collected and remitted, or may adopt methods such as mail, electronic data messages, or other means to handle the above declaration and submission matters in accordance with regulations.

Article 27 Where taxpayers or withholding agents are unable to handle tax declaration or submit reports on taxes withheld and remitted or collected and remitted on time, they may apply for an extension with the approval of the tax authority.

Where an extension for handling the declaration and submission matters specified in the preceding paragraph is approved, the taxpayer or withholding agent shall prepay the tax amount based on the actual tax paid in the previous period or the tax amount assessed by the tax authority within the tax period, and settle the tax within the approved extension period.

## Chapter III Tax Collection

Article 28 Tax authorities shall collect taxes in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, and shall not impose, suspend, over-collect, under-collect, collect in advance, defer collection, or apportion taxes in violation of laws and administrative regulations.

The amount of agricultural tax payable shall be assessed in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 29 Except for tax authorities, tax officials, and units and persons entrusted by tax authorities in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, no unit or individual may engage in tax collection activities.

Article 30 Withholding agents shall fulfill their obligations to withhold and remit taxes or collect and remit taxes in accordance with laws and administrative regulations. Tax authorities shall not require units or individuals that are not obligated to withhold and remit taxes or collect and remit taxes under laws or administrative regulations to fulfill such obligations.

When a withholding agent lawfully fulfills its obligation to withhold or collect taxes, the taxpayer shall not refuse. If the taxpayer refuses, the withholding agent shall promptly report to the tax authority for handling.

Tax authorities shall pay handling fees to withholding agents for withholding and remitting or collecting and remitting taxes in accordance with regulations.

Article 31 Taxpayers and withholding agents shall pay or remit taxes within the period prescribed by laws or administrative regulations or determined by tax authorities in accordance with laws or administrative regulations.

Where a taxpayer has special difficulties and cannot pay taxes on time, an extension for tax payment may be granted with the approval of the state taxation bureau or local taxation bureau of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, but the extension shall not exceed three months at most.

Article 32 Where a taxpayer fails to pay taxes within the prescribed period, or a withholding agent fails to remit taxes within the prescribed period, the tax authority shall, in addition to ordering payment within a time limit, impose a surcharge for overdue payment at a daily rate of 0.05% of the overdue tax amount from the date the tax becomes overdue.

Article 33 Taxpayers shall handle tax reductions and exemptions in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Decisions on tax reductions or exemptions arbitrarily made by local people's governments at all levels, competent departments of people's governments at all levels, units, or individuals in violation of laws or administrative regulations shall be invalid, and tax authorities shall not implement them and shall report to higher-level tax authorities.

Article 34 When collecting taxes, tax authorities must issue tax payment receipts to taxpayers. When a withholding agent withholds or collects taxes, if the taxpayer requests the withholding agent to issue a certificate of tax withholding or collection, the withholding agent shall issue it.

Article 35 In any of the following circumstances, the tax authority has the right to assess the tax payable by the taxpayer:

(1) Where account books may be not set up in accordance with laws or administrative regulations;
(2) Where account books should be set up in accordance with laws or administrative regulations but are not set up;
(3) Where account books are arbitrarily destroyed or tax-related materials are refused to be provided;
(4) Where account books are set up but the accounts are chaotic, or cost materials, income vouchers, or expense vouchers are incomplete, making it difficult to audit;
(5) Where a tax liability arises but tax declaration is not handled within the prescribed period, and after being ordered by the tax authority to declare within a time limit, the taxpayer still fails to declare;
(6) Where the tax basis declared by the taxpayer is obviously low without justifiable reasons.

The specific procedures and methods for tax authorities to assess the tax payable shall be prescribed by the tax administration department of the State Council.

Article 36 Business transactions between an enterprise or a production or business operation establishment or place set up in China by a foreign enterprise and its affiliated enterprises shall be conducted at prices or charges as between independent enterprises. Where prices or charges are not collected or paid as between independent enterprises, thereby reducing the taxable income or profit, the tax authority has the right to make reasonable adjustments.

Article 37 For taxpayers engaged in production or business operations who have not handled tax registration in accordance with regulations, as well as taxpayers temporarily engaged in business operations, the tax authority shall assess the tax payable and order payment; if payment is not made, the tax authority may seize goods or merchandise with a value equivalent to the tax payable. If the tax payable is paid after seizure, the tax authority must immediately release the seizure and return the seized goods or merchandise; if the tax payable is still not paid after seizure, with the approval of the director of the county-level or higher tax bureau (sub-bureau), the seized goods or merchandise shall be auctioned or sold off according to law, and the proceeds from the auction or sale shall be used to offset the tax payable.

Article 38 Where the tax authority has reason to believe that a taxpayer engaged in production or business operations has engaged in acts of evading tax obligations, it may, before the prescribed tax payment period, order the taxpayer to pay the tax payable within a time limit; if during the time limit, the tax authority finds clear signs that the taxpayer is transferring or concealing taxable goods, merchandise, other property, or taxable income, the tax authority may require the taxpayer to provide tax payment guarantee. If the taxpayer cannot provide tax payment guarantee, with the approval of the director of the county-level or higher tax bureau (sub-bureau), the tax authority may take the following tax preservation measures:

(1) Issue a written notice to the taxpayer's bank of account or other financial institution to freeze the taxpayer's deposit in an amount equivalent to the tax payable;(2) Seize or seal up the taxpayer's goods, merchandise, or other property with a value equivalent to the tax payable.

If the taxpayer pays the tax within the time limit specified in the preceding paragraph, the tax authority must immediately lift the tax preservation measures; if the tax is still not paid after the expiration of the time limit, with the approval of the director of the county-level or higher tax bureau (sub-bureau), the tax authority may issue a written notice to the taxpayer's bank of account or other financial institution to deduct the tax from the frozen deposit, or auction or sell off the seized or sealed-up goods, merchandise, or other property according to law, and use the proceeds from the auction or sale to offset the tax payable.
Housing and daily necessities necessary for the subsistence of an individual and his or her dependents shall not be subject to tax preservation measures.

Article 39 If a taxpayer has paid the tax within the time limit, but the tax authority fails to immediately lift the tax preservation measures, causing losses to the taxpayer's legitimate interests, the tax authority shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 40 Where a taxpayer engaged in production or business operations or a withholding agent fails to pay or remit taxes within the prescribed period, or a tax payment guarantor fails to pay the guaranteed tax within the prescribed period, the tax authority shall order payment within a time limit; if payment is still not made after the expiration of the time limit, with the approval of the director of the county-level or higher tax bureau (sub-bureau), the tax authority may take the following compulsory enforcement measures:

(1) Issue a written notice to the taxpayer's, withholding agent's, or guarantor's bank of account or other financial institution to deduct the tax from their deposits;(2) Seize, seal up, auction, or sell off goods, merchandise, or other property with a value equivalent to the tax payable, and use the proceeds from the auction or sale to offset the tax payable.

When taking compulsory enforcement measures, the tax authority shall simultaneously enforce the collection of overdue surcharges not paid by the taxpayer, withholding agent, or tax payment guarantor specified in the preceding paragraph.

Housing and daily necessities necessary for the subsistence of an individual and his or her dependents shall not be subject to compulsory enforcement measures.

Article 41 The power to take tax preservation measures and compulsory enforcement measures as provided in Articles 37, 38, and 40 of this Law shall not be exercised by units or individuals other than the statutory tax authorities.

Article 42 Tax authorities must take tax preservation measures and compulsory enforcement measures in accordance with statutory authority and statutory procedures, and shall not seal up or seize housing and daily necessities necessary for the subsistence of an individual taxpayer and his or her dependents.

Article 43 Where tax authorities abuse their power to illegally take tax preservation measures or compulsory enforcement measures, or take such measures improperly, causing losses to the legitimate rights and interests of taxpayers, withholding agents, or tax payment guarantors, they shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

Article 44 If a taxpayer who owes taxes or his or her legal representative needs to leave the country, they shall settle the tax payable and overdue surcharges with the tax authority or provide guarantee before leaving the country. If the tax and overdue surcharges are not settled and no guarantee is provided, the tax authority may notify the exit administration authority to prevent them from leaving the country.

Article 45 When a tax authority collects taxes, the tax shall have priority over unsecured claims, unless otherwise provided by law; where the tax owed by a taxpayer arises before the taxpayer mortgages or pledges his or her property or before the taxpayer's property is retained, the tax shall be enforced prior to the mortgage right, pledge right, or retention right. If a taxpayer has outstanding taxes and is also subject to an administrative decision imposing a fine or confiscation of illegal gains, the tax claim takes priority over the fine and confiscation of illegal gains.
The tax authorities shall regularly publish information on taxpayers' outstanding taxes.

Article 46 Where a taxpayer with outstanding taxes mortgages or pledges their property, they shall inform the mortgagee or pledgee of their tax arrears. The mortgagee or pledgee may request the tax authorities to provide relevant information on the tax arrears.

Article 47 When distraining goods, commodities, or other property, the tax authorities must issue a receipt; when sealing up goods, commodities, or other property, they must issue an inventory.

Article 48 Where a taxpayer undergoes a merger or division, they shall report to the tax authorities and pay off all taxes in accordance with the law. If taxes are not paid off at the time of merger, the merged taxpayer shall continue to fulfill the outstanding tax obligations; if taxes are not paid off at the time of division, the taxpayers after division shall bear joint and several liability for the outstanding tax obligations.

Article 49 A taxpayer with a relatively large amount of outstanding taxes shall report to the tax authorities before disposing of their immovable property or large assets.

Article 50 Where a taxpayer with outstanding taxes, by failing to exercise due creditor's rights, waiving due creditor's rights, transferring property without consideration, or transferring property at a clearly unreasonable low price with the transferee's knowledge of the situation, causes damage to state tax revenue, the tax authorities may exercise the right of subrogation or the right of revocation in accordance with Articles 73 and 74 of the Contract Law.

The exercise of the right of subrogation or the right of revocation by the tax authorities in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall not relieve the taxpayer with outstanding taxes of their unfulfilled tax obligations and legal liabilities.

Article 51 Where a taxpayer has paid taxes in excess of the amount payable, the tax authorities shall refund the excess immediately upon discovery; if the taxpayer discovers the overpayment within three years from the date of tax settlement, they may request the tax authorities to refund the excess tax plus bank deposit interest for the same period, and the tax authorities shall verify and refund it immediately; if the refund involves withdrawal from the state treasury, it shall be handled in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations on treasury management.

Article 52 Where a taxpayer or withholding agent fails to pay or underpays taxes due to the fault of the tax authorities, the tax authorities may demand the taxpayer or withholding agent to pay the back taxes within three years, but no surcharge shall be imposed.

Where a taxpayer or withholding agent fails to pay or underpays taxes due to errors such as miscalculation, the tax authorities may recover the taxes and surcharges within three years; under special circumstances, the recovery period may be extended to five years.

For tax evasion, tax resistance, or tax fraud, the recovery of unpaid or underpaid taxes, surcharges, or fraudulently obtained tax refunds by the tax authorities shall not be subject to the time limits specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 53 The State Tax Bureau and Local Tax Bureau shall deposit the collected taxes into the state treasury in accordance with the tax collection and management scope and the budget level for tax revenue as prescribed by the state.

For tax violations discovered by audit or financial authorities in accordance with the law, the tax authorities shall, based on the decisions or opinions of the relevant authorities, deposit the taxes and surcharges due into the state treasury according to the budget level for tax revenue and promptly report the results to the relevant authorities.

## Chapter IV Tax Inspection

Article 54 The tax authorities have the authority to conduct the following tax inspections:

(1) Inspect the taxpayer's account books, vouchers, statements, and related materials, and inspect the withholding agent's account books, vouchers, and related materials for taxes withheld and collected;
(2) Inspect the taxpayer's taxable goods, commodities, or other property at their production and business premises and goods storage locations, and inspect the withholding agent's business operations related to taxes withheld and collected;
(3) Require the taxpayer or withholding agent to provide documents, certifications, and related materials concerning tax payment or tax withholding and collection;
(4) Inquire about issues and circumstances related to tax payment or tax withholding and collection from the taxpayer or withholding agent;
(5) Inspect relevant documents, vouchers, and materials concerning the taxpayer's consignment or mailing of taxable goods, commodities, or other property at stations, docks, airports, postal enterprises, and their branches;
(6) With the approval of the tax bureau (or its branch) at or above the county level, and based on a nationally standardized permit for inspecting deposit accounts, inquire about the deposit accounts of taxpayers and withholding agents engaged in production and business operations at banks or other financial institutions. When investigating tax violation cases, with the approval of the tax bureau (or its branch) at or above the level of a city with districts or an autonomous prefecture, the tax authorities may inquire about the savings deposits of individuals suspected of involvement. Information obtained through such inquiries shall not be used for purposes other than tax collection.

Article 55 When conducting a tax inspection on the tax payment status of a taxpayer engaged in production and business operations for a previous tax period, if the tax authorities discover that the taxpayer has engaged in tax evasion and shows clear signs of transferring or concealing taxable goods, commodities, other property, or taxable income, they may take tax preservation measures or compulsory enforcement measures in accordance with the approval authority specified in this Law.

Article 56 Taxpayers and withholding agents must accept tax inspections conducted by the tax authorities in accordance with the law, truthfully report the situation, provide relevant materials, and shall not refuse or conceal.

Article 57 When conducting tax inspections in accordance with the law, the tax authorities have the authority to investigate the circumstances related to tax payment or tax withholding and collection of taxpayers, withholding agents, and other parties from relevant units and individuals. Relevant units and individuals are obligated to truthfully provide relevant materials and evidence to the tax authorities.

Article 58 When investigating tax violation cases, the tax authorities may record, audio-record, video-record, photograph, and copy information and materials related to the case.

Article 59 Personnel dispatched by the tax authorities for tax inspections shall present their tax inspection certificates and tax inspection notices and are responsible for maintaining confidentiality for the inspected party; if they fail to present the tax inspection certificate and tax inspection notice, the inspected party has the right to refuse the inspection.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 60 Where a taxpayer commits any of the following acts, the tax authorities shall order rectification within a specified period and may impose a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to apply for, change, or cancel tax registration within the prescribed time limit;
(2) Failing to set up or maintain account books, or maintain vouchers and related materials in accordance with regulations;
(3) Failing to submit financial and accounting systems, or financial and accounting processing methods, and accounting software to the tax authorities for reference in accordance with regulations;
(4) Failing to report all bank account numbers to the tax authorities in accordance with regulations;
(5) Failing to install or use tax control devices, or damaging or altering tax control devices without authorization in accordance with regulations.

If a taxpayer fails to undergo tax registration, the tax authorities shall order rectification within a specified period; if rectification is not made within the specified period, the tax authorities shall request the administrative authority for industry and commerce to revoke the taxpayer's business license.

If a taxpayer fails to use the tax registration certificate in accordance with regulations, or lends, alters, damages, sells, or forges the tax registration certificate, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 61 Where a withholding agent fails to set up or maintain account books for taxes withheld and collected, or fails to maintain vouchers and related materials for taxes withheld and collected in accordance with regulations, the tax authorities shall order rectification within a specified period and may impose a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 62 Where a taxpayer fails to file tax returns and submit tax materials within the prescribed time limit, or a withholding agent fails to submit reports and related materials on taxes withheld and collected to the tax authorities within the prescribed time limit, the tax authorities shall order rectification within a specified period and may impose a fine of not more than 2,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan may be imposed.

Article 63 Where a taxpayer forges, alters, conceals, or destroys account books or vouchers without authorization, or overstates expenses or understates or omits income in account books, or refuses to declare or makes false tax declarations after being notified by the tax authorities, resulting in non-payment or underpayment of taxes, this constitutes tax evasion. For tax evasion by a taxpayer, the tax authorities shall recover the unpaid or underpaid taxes and surcharges and impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of unpaid or underpaid taxes; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Where a withholding agent uses the means specified in the preceding paragraph to fail to pay or underpay taxes that have been withheld or collected, the tax authorities shall recover the unpaid or underpaid taxes and surcharges and impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of unpaid or underpaid taxes; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 64 Where a taxpayer or withholding agent fabricates false tax bases, the tax authorities shall order rectification within a specified period and impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan.

Where a taxpayer fails to file tax returns, resulting in non-payment or underpayment of taxes, the tax authorities shall recover the unpaid or underpaid taxes and surcharges and impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of unpaid or underpaid taxes.

Article 65 Where a taxpayer with outstanding taxes uses means such as transferring or concealing property to obstruct the tax authorities from recovering the overdue taxes, the tax authorities shall recover the overdue taxes and surcharges and impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of overdue taxes; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 66 Where a person fraudulently obtains export tax refunds from the state by falsely declaring exports or other deceptive means, the tax authorities shall recover the fraudulently obtained tax refunds and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the amount of the fraudulently obtained tax refunds; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

For those who fraudulently obtain export tax refunds from the state, the tax authorities may suspend processing their export tax refunds for a specified period.

Article 67 Refusing to pay taxes by means of violence or threat constitutes tax resistance. In addition to the tax authorities recovering the refused taxes and surcharges, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law. If the circumstances are minor and do not constitute a crime, the tax authorities shall recover the refused taxes and surcharges and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the amount of the refused taxes.

Article 68 Where a taxpayer or withholding agent fails to pay or underpay taxes due or payable within the prescribed time limit, and after being ordered by the tax authorities to pay within a specified period, still fails to pay by the deadline, the tax authorities shall, in addition to taking compulsory enforcement measures in accordance with Article 40 of this Law to recover the unpaid or underpaid taxes, impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than five times the amount of unpaid or underpaid taxes.

Article 69 Where a withholding agent fails to withhold or collect taxes that should have been withheld or collected, the tax authorities shall recover the taxes from the taxpayer and impose a fine of not less than 50% but not more than three times the amount of taxes that should have been withheld or collected on the withholding agent.

Article 70 Where a taxpayer or withholding agent evades, refuses, or otherwise obstructs tax inspections by the tax authorities, the tax authorities shall order rectification and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 71 Where a person illegally prints invoices in violation of Article 22 of this Law, the tax authorities shall destroy the illegally printed invoices, confiscate the illegal gains and tools used, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 72 Where a taxpayer or withholding agent engaged in production and business operations commits a tax violation specified in this Law and refuses to accept the tax authorities' handling, the tax authorities may confiscate their invoices or stop issuing invoices to them.

Article 73 Where the bank or other financial institution where a taxpayer or withholding agent has an account refuses to accept the tax authorities' lawful inspection of the taxpayer's or withholding agent's deposit account, or refuses to execute the tax authorities' decision to freeze deposits or withhold taxes, or assists the taxpayer or withholding agent in transferring deposits after receiving a written notice from the tax authorities, resulting in tax loss, the tax authorities shall impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan on the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel.

Article 74 For administrative penalties specified in this Law, if the fine amount is not more than 2,000 yuan, it may be decided by the tax office.

Article 75 The tax-related penalty income of tax authorities and judicial authorities shall be turned over to the state treasury in accordance with the budget level for tax revenue.

Article 76 Where tax authorities violate regulations by arbitrarily changing the scope of tax collection and management or the budget level for tax revenue, they shall be ordered to rectify within a specified period, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions such as demotion or removal from office in accordance with the law.

Article 77 Where the acts of a taxpayer or withholding agent specified in Articles 63, 65, 66, 67, and 71 of this Law are suspected of constituting a crime, the tax authorities shall transfer the case to the judicial authorities for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

If a tax official engages in malpractice for personal gain and fails to transfer a case that should be transferred to the judicial authorities for criminal liability in accordance with the law, and the circumstances are serious, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 78 Where a person collects taxes without lawful authorization from the tax authorities, they shall be ordered to return the collected property, and shall be given administrative sanctions or administrative penalties in accordance with the law; if the lawful rights and interests of others are harmed, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 79 Where tax authorities or tax officials seal up or distrain housing or articles necessary for the daily life of a taxpayer and their dependents, they shall be ordered to return them and shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 80 Where a tax official colludes with a taxpayer or withholding agent, or instigates or assists a taxpayer or withholding agent in committing acts specified in Articles 63, 65, and 66 of this Law, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 81 Where a tax official takes advantage of their position to accept or solicit property from a taxpayer or withholding agent, or seeks other improper benefits, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 82 Where a tax official engages in malpractice for personal gain or neglects their duties, resulting in failure to collect or under-collection of taxes due, causing significant losses to state tax revenue, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Where a tax official abuses their power to deliberately make things difficult for a taxpayer or withholding agent, they shall be transferred from the tax post and given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Where a tax official retaliates against a taxpayer, withholding agent, or other whistleblower who reports or exposes tax violations or disciplinary breaches, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Where a tax official, in violation of laws and administrative regulations, deliberately overestimates or underestimates the taxable output of agricultural taxes, resulting in over-collection or under-collection of taxes, infringing on the lawful rights and interests of farmers or harming state interests, and a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, administrative sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

Article 83 Where a person collects taxes in advance, delays collection, or apportions taxes in violation of laws and administrative regulations, their superior authority or the administrative supervision authority shall order rectification, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 84 Where a person, in violation of laws and administrative regulations, arbitrarily makes decisions on the imposition, cessation, reduction, exemption, refund, or supplementary collection of taxes, or other decisions conflicting with tax laws and administrative regulations, in addition to revoking the arbitrary decision in accordance with this Law, the taxes due but not collected shall be collected, the taxes collected but not due shall be refunded, and the superior authority shall pursue the administrative responsibility of the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 85 Where a tax official fails to recuse themselves in accordance with this Law when collecting taxes or investigating tax violation cases, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 86 Where an act that should be subject to administrative penalties under tax laws and administrative regulations is not discovered within five years, no administrative penalty shall be imposed.

Article 87 Where a person fails to maintain confidentiality for a taxpayer, withholding agent, or whistleblower in accordance with this Law, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by their unit or relevant units in accordance with the law.

Article 88 Where a taxpayer, withholding agent, or tax guarantor disputes a tax matter with the tax authorities, they must first pay or remit the taxes and surcharges in accordance with the tax authorities' tax decision or provide corresponding guarantees, and may then apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the law; if dissatisfied with the reconsideration decision, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law.

If a party is dissatisfied with the tax authorities' penalty decision, compulsory enforcement measures, or tax preservation measures, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law.

If a party fails to apply for administrative reconsideration or file a lawsuit with the People's Court within the prescribed time limit regarding the tax authorities' penalty decision, and fails to comply, the tax authorities that made the penalty decision may take compulsory enforcement measures specified in Article 40 of this Law or apply to the People's Court for compulsory enforcement.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 89 Taxpayers and withholding agents may entrust tax agents to handle tax matters on their behalf.

Article 90 Specific measures for the collection and management of cultivated land occupation tax, deed tax, agricultural tax, and animal husbandry tax shall be formulated separately by the State Council.

The collection and management of customs duties and taxes collected by customs on behalf of the state shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 91 Where treaties or agreements on taxation concluded between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries contain provisions different from this Law, the provisions of the treaties or agreements shall prevail.

Article 92 Where tax laws promulgated before the implementation of this Law contain provisions different from this Law, the provisions of this Law shall apply.

Article 93 The State Council shall formulate implementing rules based on this Law.

Article 94 This Law shall take effect as of May 1, 2001.
