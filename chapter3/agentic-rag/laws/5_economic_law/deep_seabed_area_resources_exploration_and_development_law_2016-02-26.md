# Deep Seabed Area Resources Exploration and Development Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 19th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on February 26, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted for the purposes of regulating exploration and development activities of resources in the deep seabed area, promoting deep-sea scientific and technological research and resource surveys, protecting the marine environment, facilitating the sustainable use of resources in the deep seabed area, and safeguarding the common interests of mankind.

**Article 2** This Law shall apply to the exploration, development, and related environmental protection, scientific and technological research, and resource survey activities conducted in the deep seabed area by citizens, legal persons, or other organizations of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "deep seabed area" means the seabed, ocean floor, and subsoil thereof beyond the limits of national jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China and other States.

**Article 3** Exploration and development activities of resources in the deep seabed area shall adhere to the principles of peaceful use, cooperative sharing, environmental protection, and safeguarding the common interests of mankind.

The State shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of citizens, legal persons, or other organizations of the People's Republic of China engaged in exploration, development, and resource survey activities in the deep seabed area.

**Article 4** The State shall formulate plans for the exploration and development of resources in the deep seabed area, and adopt economic and technical policies and measures to encourage deep-sea scientific and technological research and resource surveys, and to enhance capabilities in resource exploration, development, and marine environmental protection.

**Article 5** The competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of exploration, development, and resource survey activities in the deep seabed area. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for related management work in accordance with their respective functions and duties as prescribed by the State Council.

**Article 6** The State encourages and supports international cooperation in the exploration and development of resources in the deep seabed area, as well as in related environmental protection, resource surveys, scientific and technological research, and education and training.

## Chapter II Exploration and Development

**Article 7** Before applying to the International Seabed Authority for engaging in exploration and development activities of resources in the deep seabed area, a citizen, legal person, or other organization of the People's Republic of China shall submit an application to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council, along with the following materials:

(1) Basic information of the applicant;
(2) A description of the location, area, and types of minerals of the proposed exploration and development area;
(3) Evidence of financial status, investment capacity, and technical capability;
(4) A work plan for exploration and development, including relevant data on the potential impact of exploration and development activities on the marine environment, and contingency plans for serious damage to the marine environment;
(5) Other materials as required by the competent oceanic authority under the State Council.

**Article 8** The competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall review the materials submitted by the applicant. If the application is in the national interest and the applicant meets the requirements for capital, technology, equipment, and other capabilities, the authority shall grant permission and issue relevant documents within sixty working days.

Only after obtaining permission and signing an exploration and development contract with the International Seabed Authority to become a contractor may the applicant engage in exploration and development activities.

The contractor shall, within thirty days from the date of signing the exploration and development contract, submit a copy of the contract to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council for recordation.

The competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall notify relevant authorities of the contractor and the location, area, and other information of the exploration and development area.

**Article 9** A contractor shall enjoy the exclusive right to explore for and develop specific resources within the contract area.

The contractor shall fulfill its obligations under the exploration and development contract, ensure the personal safety of personnel engaged in exploration and development operations, and protect the marine environment.

When conducting exploration and development operations, the contractor shall protect cultural relics, installations, and other objects within the operation area.

The contractor shall also comply with the laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China concerning work safety and labor protection.

**Article 10** Before transferring the rights and obligations under an exploration and development contract, or making significant changes to the contract, the contractor shall report to and obtain the consent of the competent oceanic authority under the State Council.

The contractor shall, within thirty days from the date of transfer, modification, or termination of the exploration and development contract, report to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council for recordation.

The competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall promptly notify relevant authorities of the transfer, modification, or termination of the exploration and development contract.

**Article 11** In the event of an accident that causes or is likely to cause serious damage to the marine environment, the contractor shall immediately activate the contingency plan and take the following measures:

(1) Issue an immediate warning;
(2) Immediately report to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council, which shall promptly notify relevant authorities;
(3) Take all practicable and reasonable measures to prevent, reduce, and control harm to persons, property, and the marine environment.

## Chapter III Environmental Protection

**Article 12** A contractor shall, to the extent reasonable and practicable, utilize available advanced technology and take necessary measures to prevent, reduce, and control pollution and other hazards to the marine environment caused by activities within the exploration and development area.

**Article 13** A contractor shall, in accordance with the provisions and requirements of the exploration and development contract and the regulations of the competent oceanic authority under the State Council, investigate and study the marine conditions of the exploration and development area, establish environmental baselines, and assess the potential impact of exploration and development activities on the marine environment; formulate and implement an environmental monitoring program to monitor the impact of exploration and development activities on the marine environment of the exploration and development area, ensure the normal operation of monitoring equipment, and preserve original monitoring records.

**Article 14** When conducting exploration and development activities, a contractor shall take necessary measures to protect and preserve rare or fragile ecosystems, as well as the habitats of depleted, threatened, or endangered species and other marine organisms, protect marine biodiversity, and maintain the sustainable use of marine resources.

## Chapter IV Scientific and Technological Research and Resource Surveys

**Article 15** The State supports deep-sea scientific and technological research and the cultivation of professional personnel, includes deep-sea science and technology as a priority area for scientific and technological development, and encourages cooperative research with related industries.

The State supports enterprises in conducting deep-sea scientific and technological research and the research and development of technical equipment.

**Article 16** The State supports the construction and operation of deep-sea public platforms, establishes a mechanism for sharing and cooperation on deep-sea public platforms, provides professional services for deep-sea scientific and technological research and resource survey activities, and promotes deep-sea scientific and technological exchange, cooperation, and the sharing of achievements.

**Article 17** The State encourages entities and individuals to carry out deep-sea science popularization activities through various means, such as opening scientific research vessels, laboratories, exhibition halls, and other venues and facilities, or by giving lectures or providing consultations.

**Article 18** Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations engaged in resource survey activities in the deep seabed area shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, submit copies of relevant data, physical samples, or catalogs to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council and other relevant departments. The departments responsible for receiving such submissions shall register and preserve the submitted data and physical samples, and make them available for public use in accordance with relevant regulations.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply to the submission of relevant data, physical samples, etc., obtained by contractors from exploration and development activities in the deep seabed area.

## Chapter V Supervision and Inspection

**Article 19** The competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall supervise and inspect the exploration and development activities of contractors.

**Article 20** A contractor shall periodically report to the competent oceanic authority under the State Council on the following matters concerning the performance of the exploration and development contract:

(1) The status of exploration and development activities;
(2) The status of environmental monitoring;
(3) The annual investment situation;
(4) Other matters as required by the competent oceanic authority under the State Council.

**Article 21** The competent oceanic authority under the State Council may inspect the vessels, facilities, and equipment used by a contractor for exploration and development activities, as well as logbooks, records, data, etc.

**Article 22** A contractor shall assist and cooperate with the supervision and inspection conducted by the competent oceanic authority under the State Council.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

**Article 23** If any of the following acts is committed in violation of Article 7, paragraph 2 of Article 9, or paragraph 1 of Article 10 of this Law, the competent oceanic authority under the State Council may revoke the permission and withdraw the relevant documents:

(1) Obtaining permission by submitting false materials;
(2) Failing to perform the obligations under the exploration and development contract, or performing the contractual obligations in a manner inconsistent with the agreement;
(3) Transferring the rights and obligations under the exploration and development contract, or making significant changes to the contract, without consent.

If a contractor commits the act specified in item (2) of the preceding paragraph, it shall also bear corresponding liability for compensation.

**Article 24** If any of the following acts is committed in violation of paragraph 3 of Article 8, paragraph 2 of Article 10, Article 18, Article 20, or Article 22 of this Law, the competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall order correction and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to submit a copy of the exploration and development contract for recordation as required;
(2) Failing to report the transfer, modification, or termination of the exploration and development contract for recordation as required;
(3) Failing to submit copies of relevant data, physical samples, or catalogs as required;
(4) Failing to report on matters concerning the performance of the exploration and development contract as required;
(5) Failing to assist or cooperate with supervision and inspection.

**Article 25** If any person engages in exploration and development activities of resources in the deep seabed area without permission or without signing an exploration and development contract, in violation of paragraph 2 of Article 8 of this Law, the competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall order cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall also be confiscated.

**Article 26** If any person causes damage to the marine environment or damage to cultural relics, installations, or other objects within the operation area, in violation of paragraph 3 of Article 9, Article 11, or Article 12 of this Law, the competent oceanic authority under the State Council shall order cessation of the illegal act and impose a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 27** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Exploration" means the searching for resources in the deep seabed area, analyzing resources, using and testing resource collection systems and equipment, processing facilities, and transportation systems, as well as the study of environmental, technical, economic, commercial, and other relevant factors to be considered during development.
(2) "Development" means the recovery and extraction of resources in the deep seabed area for commercial purposes, including the construction and operation of collection, processing, and transportation systems for the production and sale of resources.
(3) "Resource survey" means the searching for resources in the deep seabed area, including estimating the composition, quantity, distribution, and economic value of resources.

**Article 28** Tax matters related to resource development activities in the deep seabed area shall be handled in accordance with the tax laws and administrative regulations of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 29** This Law shall come into force as of May 1, 2016.
