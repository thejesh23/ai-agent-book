# Animal Epidemic Prevention Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 26th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 3, 1997

First Revision at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 30, 2007

First Amendment by the Decision on Amending Twelve Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Cultural Relics at the Third Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on June 29, 2013

Second Amendment by the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Electricity Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Second Revision at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on January 22, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen the management of animal epidemic prevention activities, prevent, control, purify, and eradicate animal diseases, promote the development of the breeding industry, prevent and control zoonotic infectious diseases, and safeguard public health security and human health.

Article 2 This Law applies to animal epidemic prevention and its supervision and management activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

The quarantine of inbound and outbound animals and animal products shall be governed by the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Quarantine of Inbound and Outbound Animals and Plants.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "animals" means livestock, poultry, and other animals artificially raised or captured.

For the purposes of this Law, "animal products" means meat, raw hides, raw wool, down, viscera, fat, blood, semen, ova, embryos, bones, hooves, heads, horns, tendons, and milk, eggs, etc., that may transmit animal diseases.

For the purposes of this Law, "animal diseases" means animal infectious diseases, including parasitic diseases.

For the purposes of this Law, "animal epidemic prevention" means the prevention, control, diagnosis and treatment, purification, and eradication of animal diseases, the quarantine of animals and animal products, and the harmless treatment of dead animals and diseased animal products.

Article 4 According to the degree of harm caused by animal diseases to the production of the breeding industry and human health, the animal diseases specified in this Law are divided into the following three categories:

(1) Category I diseases refer to foot-and-mouth disease, African swine fever, highly pathogenic avian influenza, etc., which cause particularly serious harm to humans and animals, may cause significant economic losses and social impact, and require urgent and stringent compulsory prevention and control measures;

(2) Category II diseases refer to rabies, brucellosis, grass carp hemorrhagic disease, etc., which cause serious harm to humans and animals, may cause relatively large economic losses and social impact, and require strict prevention and control measures;

(3) Category III diseases refer to colibacillosis, avian tuberculosis, soft-shelled turtle parotitis, etc., which are common and frequent, cause harm to humans and animals, may cause a certain degree of economic losses and social impact, and require timely prevention and control.

The specific list of Category I, II, and III animal diseases mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated and published by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council. The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall promptly add, reduce, or adjust the specific list of Category I, II, and III animal diseases based on the occurrence, prevalence, and degree of harm of animal diseases and publish it.

The list of zoonotic infectious diseases shall be formulated and published by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council jointly with the competent departments of health, wildlife protection, etc., under the State Council.

Article 5 Animal epidemic prevention shall implement the policy of prevention first, combining prevention with control, purification, and eradication.

Article 6 The State encourages social forces to participate in animal epidemic prevention work. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to support units and individuals in participating in publicity and education on animal epidemic prevention, epidemic reporting, volunteer services, and donations.

Article 7 Units and individuals engaged in activities such as raising, slaughtering, operating, isolating, transporting animals, and producing, operating, processing, storing animal products shall, in accordance with this Law and the regulations of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, properly carry out animal epidemic prevention work such as immunization, disinfection, testing, isolation, purification, eradication, and harmless treatment, and bear relevant responsibilities for animal epidemic prevention.

Article 8 People's governments at or above the county level shall exercise unified leadership over animal epidemic prevention work, take effective measures to stabilize grassroots institutions and teams, strengthen the construction of animal epidemic prevention teams, establish and improve the animal epidemic prevention system, and formulate and organize the implementation of animal disease prevention and control plans.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices shall organize the masses to carry out animal disease prevention and control work within their respective jurisdictions, with the assistance of villagers' committees and residents' committees.

Article 9 The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall be in charge of animal epidemic prevention work nationwide.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of animal epidemic prevention work within their respective administrative regions.

Other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall properly carry out animal epidemic prevention work within their respective responsibilities.

The military animal health supervision functional department shall be responsible for the epidemic prevention work of active-duty animals and animals raised for self-use in the military.

Article 10 The competent health departments of people's governments at or above the county level and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, wildlife protection, etc., of the people's governments at the same level shall establish a coordination mechanism for the prevention and control of zoonotic infectious diseases.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council and the General Administration of Customs shall establish a coordination mechanism to prevent the introduction of animal diseases from abroad.

Article 11 The animal health supervision institutions of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, be responsible for the quarantine of animals and animal products.

Article 12 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the regulations of the State Council and based on the principles of overall planning, rational layout, and comprehensive establishment, establish animal disease prevention and control institutions.

Animal disease prevention and control institutions shall undertake technical work such as monitoring, testing, diagnosis, epidemiological investigation, epidemic reporting, and other prevention and control of animal diseases; and undertake technical work for the purification and eradication of animal diseases.

Article 13 The State encourages and supports scientific research on animal diseases and international cooperation and exchanges, promotes advanced and applicable scientific research results, and improves the scientific and technological level of animal disease prevention and control.

People's governments at all levels, relevant departments, and news media shall strengthen the publicity of animal epidemic prevention laws, regulations, and knowledge.

Article 14 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, commend and reward units and individuals that have made contributions to animal epidemic prevention work, related scientific research, and the eradication of animal epidemics.

Relevant units shall, in accordance with the law, pay work-related injury insurance premiums for animal epidemic prevention personnel. Personnel who become ill, disabled, or die due to participation in animal epidemic prevention work shall be given subsidies or pensions in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Prevention of Animal Diseases

Article 15 The State establishes an animal disease risk assessment system.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall, based on domestic and foreign animal epidemics and the need to protect the production of the breeding industry and human health, promptly conduct risk assessments of animal diseases jointly with the competent health and other relevant departments under the State Council, and formulate and publish measures and technical specifications for the prevention, control, purification, and eradication of animal diseases.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, jointly with the competent health departments of the people's governments at the same level, carry out animal disease risk assessments within their respective administrative regions and implement measures for the prevention, control, purification, and eradication of animal diseases.

Article 16 The State implements compulsory immunization against animal diseases that seriously harm the production of the breeding industry and human health.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall determine the types of animal diseases and regions for compulsory immunization.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate compulsory immunization plans for their respective administrative regions; they may add types of animal diseases and regions for compulsory immunization based on the prevalence of animal diseases in their administrative regions, which shall be implemented after approval by the people's government at the same level and reported to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council for recordation.

Article 17 Units and individuals raising animals shall fulfill the obligation of compulsory immunization against animal diseases, vaccinate animals in accordance with the compulsory immunization plan and technical specifications, and establish immunization files and affix livestock and poultry identification marks in accordance with relevant state regulations to ensure traceability.

If animals receiving compulsory immunization do not meet the immunization quality requirements and still do not meet the immunization quality requirements after supplementary immunization, the relevant units and individuals shall handle the matter in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Vaccines used for preventive vaccination shall meet national quality standards.

Article 18 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing the implementation of compulsory immunization plans for animal diseases and supervising and inspecting the fulfillment of compulsory immunization obligations by units and individuals raising animals.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices shall organize units and individuals raising animals within their jurisdictions to properly carry out compulsory immunization and assist in supervision and inspection; villagers' committees and residents' committees shall assist in related work.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall regularly evaluate the implementation status and effectiveness of compulsory immunization plans within their respective administrative regions and publish the evaluation results to the public.

Article 19 The State implements an animal disease monitoring and epidemic early warning system.

People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve animal disease monitoring networks and strengthen animal disease monitoring.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall, jointly with relevant departments under the State Council, formulate a national animal disease monitoring plan. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the national animal disease monitoring plan, formulate animal disease monitoring plans for their respective administrative regions.

Animal disease prevention and control institutions shall monitor the occurrence and prevalence of animal diseases in accordance with the regulations of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council and the animal disease monitoring plan; units and individuals engaged in activities such as raising, slaughtering, operating, isolating, transporting animals, and producing, operating, processing, storing, and conducting harmless treatment of animal products shall not refuse or obstruct such monitoring.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall promptly issue animal epidemic warnings based on predictions of the occurrence and prevalence trends of animal diseases. After receiving animal epidemic warnings, local people's governments at all levels shall promptly take prevention and control measures.

Article 20 The people's governments of provinces and autonomous regions along land borders shall, based on the needs of animal disease prevention and control, reasonably set up animal disease monitoring stations, improve monitoring working mechanisms, and prevent the introduction of animal diseases from abroad.

Science and technology, customs, and other departments shall, in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and regulations, properly carry out animal disease monitoring and early warning work, and regularly exchange information with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, and promptly notify in case of emergencies.

People's governments at or above the county level shall improve the wildlife epidemic source and disease monitoring system and working mechanism, and reasonably deploy monitoring stations as needed; the competent departments of wildlife protection and agriculture and rural affairs shall, according to their respective responsibilities, properly carry out wildlife epidemic source and disease monitoring and other work, and regularly exchange information, and promptly notify in case of emergencies.

Article 21 The State supports local governments in establishing animal disease-free zones and encourages animal farms to build biosecurity compartments free from specified animal diseases. For animal disease-free zones and biosecurity compartments free from specified animal diseases that meet the standards prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall publish them after passing acceptance inspection and supervise and inspect their maintenance.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate and organize the implementation of construction plans for animal disease-free zones within their respective administrative regions. The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall guide the construction of animal disease-free zones across provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall implement zonal prevention and control of animal diseases based on administrative divisions, the layout of the breeding and slaughtering industry, risk assessment, etc., and may take measures such as prohibiting or restricting the cross-regional transportation of specific animals and animal products.

Article 22 The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall formulate and organize the implementation of plans for the purification and eradication of animal diseases.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the plans for the purification and eradication of animal diseases, formulate and organize the implementation of plans for the purification and eradication of animal diseases within their respective administrative regions.

Animal disease prevention and control institutions shall, in accordance with the plans for the purification and eradication of animal diseases, provide technical guidance and training for animal disease purification, and monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of animal disease purification.

The State promotes the purification of animal diseases and encourages and supports units and individuals raising animals to carry out animal disease purification. Units and individuals raising animals that meet the purification standards prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall be published by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of people's governments at or above the provincial level.

Article 23 Breeding and dairy animals shall meet the health standards prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Units and individuals raising breeding and dairy animals shall, in accordance with the requirements of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, regularly conduct animal disease testing; if the test results are unqualified, they shall handle the matter in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 24 Animal farms, isolation sites, animal slaughtering and processing sites, and sites for the harmless treatment of animals and animal products shall meet the following animal epidemic prevention conditions:

(1) The distance between the site and residential areas, drinking water sources, schools, hospitals, and other public places shall comply with the regulations of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council;

(2) The production and operation area shall be enclosed and isolated, and the engineering design and relevant processes shall meet animal epidemic prevention requirements;

(3) There shall be sewage and waste treatment facilities, facilities and equipment for the harmless treatment of dead animals and diseased animal products, or refrigeration and freezing facilities and equipment, as well as cleaning and disinfection facilities and equipment, commensurate with the scale of the site;

(4) There shall be licensed veterinarians or animal epidemic prevention technicians commensurate with the scale of the site;

(5) There shall be comprehensive animal epidemic prevention systems such as isolation and disinfection, purchase and sales ledgers, and daily inspections;
(6) Other animal epidemic prevention conditions prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall be met.

In addition to meeting the conditions specified in the preceding paragraph, sites for the harmless treatment of animals and animal products shall also have pathogen testing equipment, testing capabilities, and special transport vehicles that meet animal epidemic prevention requirements.

Article 25 The State implements an animal epidemic prevention conditions review system.

To establish an animal farm, isolation site, animal slaughtering and processing site, or a site for the harmless treatment of animals and animal products, an application shall be submitted to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level, along with relevant materials. The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs that accepts the application shall conduct a review in accordance with this Law and the Administrative Licensing Law of the People's Republic of China. If the review is passed, an animal epidemic prevention conditions certificate shall be issued; if not, the applicant shall be notified and the reasons shall be explained.

The animal epidemic prevention conditions certificate shall specify matters such as the name of the applicant, the site address, and the type of animals (animal products).

Article 26 Markets for the operation of animals and animal products shall meet the animal epidemic prevention conditions prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council and shall accept supervision and inspection by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may, based on local conditions, decide to prohibit the live trading of livestock and poultry in specific urban areas.

Article 27 The means of transport, bedding, packaging materials, containers, etc., for animals and animal products shall meet the animal epidemic prevention requirements prescribed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Diseased animals and their excrement, diseased animal products, animal excrement in means of transport, and contaminated items such as bedding, packaging materials, and containers shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations and shall not be disposed of arbitrarily.

Article 28 Activities such as collecting, preserving, transporting animal pathological materials or pathogenic microorganisms, and engaging in research, teaching, testing, and diagnosis of pathogenic microorganisms shall comply with the relevant state regulations on the management of pathogenic microorganism laboratories.

Article 29 It is prohibited to slaughter, operate, or transport the following animals and to produce, operate, process, store, or transport the following animal products:

(1) Those related to the occurring animal disease within a quarantined epidemic area;

(2) Those susceptible to infection within an epidemic area;

(3) Those that should have been quarantined according to law but have not been quarantined or have failed quarantine;

(4) Those that are diseased or suspected of being diseased;

(5) Those that are dead or of unknown cause of death;

(6) Others that do not comply with the relevant animal epidemic prevention regulations of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply to the temporary storage and transportation of animals and animal products necessary for centralized harmless treatment, provided that epidemic prevention measures are taken in accordance with regulations.

Article 30 Units and individuals raising dogs shall, in accordance with regulations, regularly vaccinate them against rabies and apply for registration with the local dog registration authority based on the immunization certificate issued by an animal diagnosis and treatment institution.

When taking a dog outdoors, a dog tag shall be worn and measures such as using a leash shall be taken to prevent the dog from injuring people and spreading diseases.

Sub-district offices and township people's governments shall organize and coordinate residents' committees and villagers' committees to properly control and dispose of stray dogs and cats within their jurisdictions to prevent the spread of diseases.

County people's governments, township people's governments, and sub-district offices shall, based on local conditions, properly carry out epidemic prevention management for dogs raised in rural areas.

Specific measures for the epidemic prevention management of dogs raised shall be formulated by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter III Reporting, Notification, and Publication of Animal Epidemics

Article 31 Units and individuals engaged in activities such as animal disease monitoring, testing, inspection and quarantine, research, diagnosis and treatment, and animal raising, slaughtering, operating, isolating, and transporting shall, upon discovering that an animal is diseased or suspected of being diseased, immediately report to the local competent department of agriculture and rural affairs or animal disease prevention and control institution and promptly take control measures such as isolation to prevent the spread of the animal epidemic. Other units and individuals discovering that an animal is diseased or suspected of being diseased shall promptly report.

The unit receiving the animal epidemic report shall promptly take necessary measures such as temporary isolation and control to prevent delays in prevention and control opportunities and shall promptly report upwards in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the State.

Article 32 Animal epidemics shall be confirmed by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of people's governments at or above the county level; major animal epidemics shall be confirmed by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and if necessary, reported to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council for confirmation.

For the purposes of this Law, "major animal epidemic" means a situation where Category I, II, or III animal diseases suddenly occur, spread rapidly, pose a serious threat or harm to the production safety of the breeding industry, and may cause harm to public health and life safety.

During the period of reporting a major animal epidemic, if necessary, the local people's government at or above the county level where the epidemic occurs may make a decision on quarantine and take measures such as culling and destruction.

Article 33 The State implements an animal epidemic notification system.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall promptly notify the competent health and other relevant departments under the State Council, relevant military departments, and the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government of the occurrence and handling of major animal epidemics.

If the customs discovers that inbound or outbound animals and animal products are diseased or suspected of being diseased, it shall promptly handle the matter and notify the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs.The competent department of wildlife protection under the local people's government at or above the county level shall, upon discovering that wild animals are infected or suspected of being infected with an animal disease, promptly handle the situation and notify the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government at the same level.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall, in accordance with treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by China, promptly notify relevant international organizations or trading partners of the occurrence and handling of major animal epidemics.

Article 34 When an epidemic of a zoonotic disease occurs, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government at or above the county level and the competent departments of health, wildlife protection, and other relevant departments under the people's government at the same level shall promptly notify each other.

When a zoonotic disease occurs, the competent department of health shall monitor populations susceptible to infection in the epidemic area, and shall promptly announce the epidemic situation and take corresponding prevention and control measures in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases.

Article 35 Persons suffering from zoonotic diseases shall not directly engage in activities such as animal disease monitoring, testing, inspection and quarantine, diagnosis and treatment, or the raising, slaughtering, operation, isolation, or transportation of susceptible animals.

Article 36 The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall promptly announce national animal epidemic situations to the public, and may also, as needed, authorize the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government to announce animal epidemic situations within their respective administrative regions. No other unit or individual may release animal epidemic information.

Article 37 No unit or individual may conceal, falsely report, delay reporting, or omit reporting animal epidemic situations, nor may they instigate others to do so, or obstruct others from reporting animal epidemic situations.

## Chapter IV Control of Animal Diseases

Article 38 When a Class I animal disease occurs, the following control measures shall be taken:

(1) The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level shall immediately dispatch personnel to the site, delineate the epidemic point, epidemic area, and threatened area, investigate the source of the epidemic, and promptly report to the people's government at the same level to impose a blockade on the epidemic area. If the epidemic area involves two or more administrative regions, the common superior people's government of the relevant administrative regions shall impose the blockade, or the respective superior people's governments of the relevant administrative regions shall jointly impose the blockade. When necessary, a higher-level people's government may instruct a lower-level people's government to impose a blockade on the epidemic area;

(2) The local people's government at or above the county level shall immediately organize relevant departments and units to take compulsory measures such as blockade, isolation, culling, destruction, disinfection, harmless treatment, and emergency vaccination;

(3) During the blockade period, it is prohibited for infected, suspected infected, and susceptible animals and animal products to leave the epidemic area, and for susceptible animals from non-epidemic areas to enter the epidemic area. Disinfection and other restrictive measures shall be taken for personnel, means of transport, and related items entering or leaving the epidemic area as needed.

Article 39 When a Class II animal disease occurs, the following control measures shall be taken:

(1) The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level shall delineate the epidemic point, epidemic area, and threatened area;

(2) The local people's government at or above the county level shall, as needed, organize relevant departments and units to take measures such as isolation, culling, destruction, disinfection, harmless treatment, emergency vaccination, and restricting the entry and exit of susceptible animals and animal products and related items.

Article 40 The revocation of epidemic points, epidemic areas, and threatened areas, and the lifting of blockades on epidemic areas, shall be decided and announced by the original decision-making authority after evaluation according to the standards and procedures stipulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 41 When a Class III animal disease occurs, the local people's government at the county or township level shall organize prevention and control in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 42 When Class II or III animal diseases break out in an epidemic form, they shall be handled as Class I animal diseases.

Article 43 Relevant units and individuals within the epidemic area shall comply with the regulations on animal disease control lawfully made by the people's government at or above the county level and its competent department of agriculture and rural affairs.

No unit or individual may conceal, transfer, or illegally dig up animals and animal products that have been lawfully isolated, sealed, or disposed of.

Article 44 When an animal epidemic occurs, air, railway, road, and waterway transport enterprises shall prioritize the transportation of epidemic prevention personnel and materials.

Article 45 The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall, based on the nature, characteristics, and potential social harm of animal diseases, formulate a national major animal epidemic emergency plan for approval by the State Council, and shall formulate implementation plans respectively according to different animal disease types, epidemic characteristics, and degrees of harm.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the superior major animal epidemic emergency plan and their local actual conditions, formulate major animal epidemic emergency plans for their respective administrative regions, report them to the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the superior people's government for filing, and send a copy to the emergency management department of the superior people's government. The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs under local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate implementation plans respectively according to different animal disease types, epidemic characteristics, and degrees of harm.

Major animal epidemic emergency plans and implementation plans shall be adjusted promptly based on the epidemic situation.

Article 46 When a major animal epidemic occurs, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council shall be responsible for delineating animal disease risk areas, prohibiting or restricting the transportation of specific animals and animal products from high-risk areas to low-risk areas.

Article 47 When a major animal epidemic occurs, emergency response measures shall be taken in accordance with laws, regulations of the State Council, and the emergency plan.

## Chapter V Quarantine of Animals and Animal Products

Article 48 Animal health supervision institutions shall implement quarantine of animals and animal products in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Official veterinarians of animal health supervision institutions shall specifically implement the quarantine of animals and animal products.

Article 49 Before slaughtering, selling, or transporting animals, or selling or transporting animal products, the owner shall declare quarantine to the local animal health supervision institution in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Upon receiving a quarantine declaration, the animal health supervision institution shall promptly assign an official veterinarian to implement quarantine of the animals and animal products; if the quarantine is qualified, a quarantine certificate shall be issued and a quarantine mark shall be affixed. The official veterinarian implementing the quarantine shall sign or seal the quarantine certificate and quarantine mark, and shall be responsible for the quarantine conclusion.

Veterinary practitioners or animal epidemic prevention technicians of animal farms and slaughter enterprises shall assist official veterinarians in implementing quarantine.

Article 50 Wild animals to be used for non-edible purposes under special circumstances such as scientific research, medicine, or exhibition shall be reported to the animal health supervision institution for quarantine in accordance with relevant state regulations, and may only be used after passing quarantine.

Captured wild animals shall be reported to the animal health supervision institution at the capture site for quarantine in accordance with relevant state regulations, and may only be raised, operated, and transported after passing quarantine.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, together with the competent department of wildlife protection under the State Council, shall formulate quarantine methods for wild animals.

Article 51 Animals slaughtered, operated, or transported, and animals used for non-edible purposes such as scientific research, exhibition, performance, and competition, shall be accompanied by a quarantine certificate; animal products operated and transported shall be accompanied by a quarantine certificate and quarantine mark.

Article 52 When transporting animals and animal products by air, railway, road, or waterway, the consignor shall provide a quarantine certificate at the time of consignment; if there is no quarantine certificate, the carrier shall not accept the consignment.

For imported and exported animals and animal products, the carrier shall transport them based on import declaration documents or quarantine documents issued by customs.

Units, individuals, and vehicles engaged in animal transportation shall register with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at the county level, and properly keep records of travel routes and information such as animal names, quarantine certificate numbers, and quantities provided by the consignor. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Conveyances shall be promptly cleaned and disinfected before loading and after unloading.

Article 53 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall determine and announce designated channels for road transport of animals entering their administrative regions, and set up guiding signs. Animals transported by road across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall enter or pass through the provincial borders via the designated channels established by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 54 For animals and animal products entering a disease-free zone, the owner shall declare quarantine to the animal health supervision institution at the location of the disease-free zone in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, and may only enter after passing quarantine.

Article 55 After breeding or dairy animals introduced across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government arrive at the destination, the owner shall conduct isolation observation of the introduced breeding or dairy animals in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 56 For animals and animal products that fail quarantine, the owner shall dispose of them under the supervision of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs in accordance with relevant state regulations, and the disposal costs shall be borne by the owner.

## Chapter VI Harmless Treatment of Dead Animals and Diseased Animal Products

Article 57 Units and individuals engaged in activities such as animal raising, slaughtering, operation, isolation, and the production, operation, processing, and storage of animal products shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, properly carry out harmless treatment of dead animals and diseased animal products, or entrust harmless treatment facilities for animals and animal products to handle them.

Units and individuals engaged in the transportation of animals and animal products shall cooperate in the proper harmless treatment of dead animals and diseased animal products, and shall not arbitrarily discard or dispose of relevant animals and animal products en route.

No unit or individual may buy, sell, process, or arbitrarily discard dead animals and diseased animal products.

Management measures for the harmless treatment of animals and animal products shall be formulated by the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs and wildlife protection under the State Council according to their respective responsibilities.

Article 58 Dead livestock found in waters such as rivers, lakes, and reservoirs shall be collected, processed, and traced by the local people's government at the county level.

Dead livestock found in urban public places and rural areas shall be collected, processed, and traced by the local sub-district office or township people's government.

Dead wild animals found in the wild shall be collected and processed by the local competent department of wildlife protection.

Article 59 The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall formulate construction plans for centralized harmless treatment facilities for animals and animal products, and establish a government-led, market-operated harmless treatment mechanism.

Article 60 Governments at all levels shall provide subsidies for the harmless treatment of dead animals. Specific subsidy standards and methods shall be formulated by the finance departments of people's governments at or above the county level together with the competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs, wildlife protection, and other relevant departments of the people's government at the same level.

## Chapter VII Animal Diagnosis and Treatment

Article 61 Institutions engaged in animal diagnosis and treatment activities shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having premises suitable for animal diagnosis and treatment activities and meeting animal epidemic prevention conditions;

(2) Having veterinary practitioners suitable for animal diagnosis and treatment activities;

(3) Having veterinary instruments and equipment suitable for animal diagnosis and treatment activities;

(4) Having a sound management system.

Animal diagnosis and treatment institutions include animal hospitals, animal clinics, and other institutions providing animal diagnosis and treatment services.

Article 62 Institutions engaged in animal diagnosis and treatment activities shall apply for an animal diagnosis and treatment license from the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level. The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs that accepts the application shall review it in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the Administrative License Law of the People's Republic of China. If the review is qualified, an animal diagnosis and treatment license shall be issued; if not, the applicant shall be notified and the reasons shall be explained.

Article 63 An animal diagnosis and treatment license shall state matters such as the name of the diagnosis and treatment institution, the scope of diagnosis and treatment activities, the practice location, and the legal representative (person in charge).

If the matters stated in the animal diagnosis and treatment license change, an application for change or renewal of the animal diagnosis and treatment license shall be made.

Article 64 Animal diagnosis and treatment institutions shall, in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, properly carry out work such as health and safety protection, disinfection, isolation, and disposal of diagnosis and treatment waste during diagnosis and treatment activities.

Article 65 Those engaged in animal diagnosis and treatment activities shall comply with relevant operational technical specifications for animal diagnosis and treatment, and use veterinary drugs and veterinary instruments that meet regulations.

Management measures for veterinary drugs and veterinary instruments shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter VIII Veterinary Management

Article 66 The state implements an official veterinarian appointment system.

Official veterinarians shall meet the conditions stipulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, be confirmed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government according to procedures, and be appointed by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level. Specific measures shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Official veterinarians of customs shall meet the prescribed conditions and be appointed by the General Administration of Customs. Specific measures shall be formulated by the General Administration of Customs together with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 67 Official veterinarians shall perform animal and animal product quarantine duties in accordance with the law, and no unit or individual may refuse or obstruct them.

Article 68 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs under people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate training plans for official veterinarians, provide training conditions, and regularly train and assess official veterinarians.

Article 69 The state implements a veterinary practitioner qualification examination system. Persons with a college degree or above in a veterinary-related major, or qualified rural veterinarians, who pass the veterinary practitioner qualification examination, shall be issued a veterinary practitioner qualification certificate by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government; those engaged in business activities such as animal diagnosis and treatment shall also register with the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at the county level.

Measures for the veterinary practitioner qualification examination shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council in consultation with the human resources department under the State Council.

Article 70 Veterinary practitioners shall personally diagnose before issuing veterinary prescriptions and shall be responsible for the diagnosis conclusions.

The state encourages veterinary practitioners to receive continuing education. The institution where a veterinary practitioner works shall support the veterinary practitioner in participating in continuing education.

Article 71 Rural veterinarians may engage in animal diagnosis and treatment activities in rural areas. Specific management measures shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

Article 72 Veterinary practitioners and rural veterinarians shall participate in activities such as animal disease prevention, control, and the eradication of animal epidemics as required by the local people's government and the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs.

Article 73 Veterinary industry associations provide services such as veterinary information, technology, and training, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of members, establish and improve industry norms and reward and punishment mechanisms in accordance with their articles of association, strengthen industry self-discipline, promote industry integrity building, and publicize animal epidemic prevention and veterinary knowledge.

## Chapter IX Supervision and Management

Article 74 The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs under local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, supervise and manage animal epidemic prevention in activities such as animal raising, slaughtering, operation, isolation, transportation, and the production, operation, processing, storage, and transportation of animal products.

Article 75 To control animal diseases, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the county people's government shall dispatch personnel to existing inspection stations lawfully established in the locality to perform supervision and inspection tasks; when necessary, with the approval of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, temporary animal epidemic prevention inspection stations may be established to perform supervision and inspection tasks.

Article 76 When performing supervision and inspection tasks, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level may take the following measures, and no relevant unit or individual may refuse or obstruct them:

(1) Sampling, retaining for inspection, and spot-checking animals and animal products in accordance with regulations;

(2) Isolating, sealing, seizing, and disposing of infected or suspected infected animals, animal products, and related items;

(3) For animals and animal products that should have been quarantined but have not been, implementing supplementary quarantine if conditions permit, or confiscating and destroying them if conditions do not permit;

(4) Inspecting quarantine certificates, quarantine marks, and livestock and poultry identifiers;

(5) Entering relevant premises to investigate and collect evidence, and inspecting and copying materials related to animal epidemic prevention.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the local people's government at or above the county level may, based on the needs of animal disease prevention and control, and with the approval of the local people's government at or above the county level, station official veterinarians or staff at relevant locations such as stations, ports, and airports.

Article 77 When performing animal epidemic prevention supervision and inspection tasks, law enforcement personnel shall present administrative law enforcement credentials and wear uniform insignia.

The competent departments of agriculture and rural affairs under people's governments at or above the county level and their staff shall not engage in for-profit activities related to animal epidemic prevention, and shall not charge any fees when conducting supervision and inspection.

Article 78 It is prohibited to transfer, forge, or alter quarantine certificates, quarantine marks, or livestock and poultry identifiers.

It is prohibited to hold or use forged or altered quarantine certificates, quarantine marks, or livestock and poultry identifiers.

Management measures for quarantine certificates and quarantine marks shall be formulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council.

## Chapter X Safeguard Measures

Article 79 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate animal epidemic prevention work into their national economic and social development plans and annual plans.

Article 80 The state encourages and supports scientific and technological research and development of new technologies, new equipment, and new products in the field of animal epidemic prevention.

Article 81 The county people's government shall equip animal health supervision institutions with official veterinarians suitable for animal and animal product quarantine work, and ensure quarantine working conditions.

The competent department of agriculture and rural affairs under the county people's government may, based on the needs of animal epidemic prevention work, station veterinary institutions or staff in townships, towns, or specific areas.

Article 82 The state encourages and supports veterinary practitioners, rural veterinarians, and animal diagnosis and treatment institutions to carry out animal epidemic prevention and disease diagnosis and treatment activities; encourages breeding enterprises, veterinary drug and feed production enterprises to form animal epidemic prevention service teams to provide epidemic prevention services. If local people's governments organize village-level epidemic prevention personnel to participate in animal disease prevention and control work, they shall ensure reasonable labor remuneration for village-level epidemic prevention personnel.

Article 83 People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective government responsibilities, incorporate into their budgets the funds needed for the monitoring, prevention, control, purification, and eradication of animal diseases, the quarantine of animals and animal products, the harmless treatment of dead animals, and supervision and management.

Article 84 People's governments at or above the county level shall stockpile epidemic prevention materials needed for emergency response to animal epidemics.

Article 85 People's governments at or above the county level shall provide compensation for animals forcibly culled, animal products destroyed, and related items during the process of animal disease prevention, control, purification, and eradication. Specific compensation standards and methods shall be formulated by the finance department under the State Council together with relevant departments.

Article 86 For personnel engaged in animal disease prevention, quarantine, supervision and inspection, on-site epidemic handling, and those who come into contact with animal disease pathogens during work, relevant units shall, in accordance with state regulations, take effective health protection and medical care measures, and provide relevant benefits such as animal husbandry and veterinary medical health subsidies.

## Chapter XI Legal Liability

Article 87 If local people's governments at all levels and their staff fail to perform their duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.**Article 88** Where the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of a people's government at or above the county level and its staff members violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the people's government at the corresponding level shall order rectification and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Failing to take timely measures for prevention, control, or eradication;

(2) Issuing an Animal Epidemic Prevention Conditions Compliance Certificate or an Animal Diagnosis and Treatment License to an entity that does not meet the requirements, or refusing to issue such a certificate or license to an entity that meets the requirements;

(3) Engaging in for-profit activities related to animal epidemic prevention, or illegally collecting fees;

(4) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 89** Where an animal health supervision institution and its staff members violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the people's government at the corresponding level or the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs shall order rectification and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Issuing a quarantine certificate or affixing a quarantine mark for animals or animal products that have not been quarantined or have failed quarantine, or refusing to issue a quarantine certificate or affix a quarantine mark for animals or animal products that have passed quarantine;

(2) Re-quarantining animals or animal products that are accompanied by a valid quarantine certificate and quarantine mark;

(3) Engaging in for-profit activities related to animal epidemic prevention, or illegally collecting fees;

(4) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 90** Where an animal epidemic prevention and control institution and its staff members violate the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the people's government at the corresponding level or the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs shall order rectification and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Failing to perform duties of animal epidemic monitoring, testing, or assessment, or falsifying monitoring, testing, or assessment results;

(2) Failing to conduct timely diagnosis or investigation upon the occurrence of an animal epidemic;

(3) Upon receiving a report of infected or suspected infected animals, failing to take measures or report in a timely manner in accordance with national regulations;

(4) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 91** Where local people's governments at various levels, relevant departments, and their staff members conceal, falsely report, delay reporting, or omit reporting an animal epidemic, or instruct others to do so, or obstruct others from reporting an animal epidemic, the people's government at the next higher level or the relevant department shall order rectification and circulate a notice of criticism; the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 92** Where any of the following acts is committed in violation of the provisions of this Law, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period and may impose a fine of not more than 1,000 yuan; if rectification is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall entrust an animal diagnosis and treatment institution, a harmless treatment site, or other entity to handle the matter on its behalf, with the costs borne by the violator:

(1) Failing to vaccinate raised animals in accordance with the compulsory animal epidemic prevention immunization plan or immunization technical specifications;

(2) Failing to conduct regular disease testing for raised breeding or dairy animals as required by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council, or failing to handle animals that have failed the test in accordance with regulations;

(3) Failing to regularly vaccinate raised dogs against rabies in accordance with regulations;

(4) Failing to clean and disinfect the means of transport for animals and animal products before loading and after unloading in a timely manner in accordance with regulations.

**Article 93** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an immunization record is not established for animals that have undergone compulsory immunization, or livestock and poultry identification marks are not affixed in accordance with regulations, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Animal Husbandry Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 94** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the means of transport, bedding, packaging materials, containers, etc., for animals and animal products do not meet the animal epidemic prevention requirements stipulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and may impose a fine of not more than 5,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 95** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, infected animals and their excreta, infected animal products, or means of transport, bedding, packaging materials, containers, etc., contaminated by infected animals or animal products are not disposed of in accordance with regulations, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order disposal within a specified period; if disposal is not made within the specified period, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall entrust a relevant entity to dispose of them on its behalf, with the costs borne by the violator, and a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If environmental pollution or ecological damage is caused, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the relevant laws and regulations on environmental protection.

**Article 96** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person suffering from a zoonotic infectious disease directly engages in activities such as animal epidemic monitoring, testing, inspection and quarantine, animal diagnosis and treatment, or the raising, slaughtering, trading, isolation, or transport of susceptible animals, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs or the competent department of wildlife protection of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification; if rectification is refused, a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 97** Where, in violation of the provisions of Article 29 of this Law, animals are slaughtered, traded, or transported, or animal products are produced, traded, processed, stored, or transported, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification, take remedial measures, confiscate the illegal gains, the animals, and the animal products, and impose a fine of not less than 15 times but not more than 30 times the value of the same type of quarantined-qualified animals or animal products; if the value of the same type of quarantined-qualified animals or animal products is less than 10,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 150,000 yuan shall be imposed; if quarantine was required by law but was not carried out, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with Article 100 of this Law.

The violator specified in the preceding paragraph, as well as its legal representative (person in charge), directly responsible persons in charge, and other directly liable persons, shall not engage in related activities for five years from the date the penalty decision is made; if a crime is constituted, they shall be permanently prohibited from engaging in related activities such as slaughtering, trading, or transporting animals, or producing, trading, processing, storing, or transporting animal products.

**Article 98** Where any of the following acts is committed in violation of the provisions of this Law, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order a suspension of business for rectification and impose a fine of not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan:

(1) Establishing an animal farm, isolation site, animal slaughtering and processing site, or harmless treatment site for animals and animal products without obtaining an Animal Epidemic Prevention Conditions Compliance Certificate;

(2) Operating a market for animals or animal products that does not meet the epidemic prevention conditions stipulated by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the State Council;

(3) Engaging in animal transport without filing a record;

(4) Failing to preserve information such as the travel route and the animal names, quarantine certificate numbers, and quantities provided by the consignor in accordance with regulations;

(5) Introducing animals or animal products into an area designated as free from specified animal diseases without passing quarantine;

(6) Introducing breeding or dairy animals across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government without conducting isolation observation upon arrival at the destination in accordance with regulations;

(7) Failing to dispose of or arbitrarily discarding dead diseased animals or diseased animal products in accordance with regulations.

**Article 99** Where an animal farm, isolation site, animal slaughtering and processing site, or harmless treatment site for animals and animal products undergoes changes in its production and operating conditions, and continues to engage in related activities without meeting the animal epidemic prevention conditions stipulated in Article 24 of this Law, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall issue a warning and order rectification within a specified period; if the conditions are still not met within the specified period, the Animal Epidemic Prevention Conditions Compliance Certificate shall be revoked, and the market supervision and administration department shall be notified for handling according to law.

**Article 100** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, animals that are slaughtered, traded, or transported are not accompanied by a quarantine certificate, or animal products that are traded or transported are not accompanied by a quarantine certificate or quarantine mark, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not more than the value of the same type of quarantined-qualified animals or animal products; for a carrier other than the owner of the goods, a fine of not less than 3 times but not more than 5 times the transport cost shall be imposed, and if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 5 times but not more than 10 times the transport cost shall be imposed.

Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, animals used for non-edible purposes such as scientific research, exhibition, performance, or competition are not accompanied by a quarantine certificate, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan.

**Article 101** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, specific animals or animal products that are prohibited or restricted from being transported are moved from a high-risk area for animal diseases to a low-risk area, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the transport costs and the illegally transported animals and animal products, and impose a fine of not less than 1 time but not more than 5 times the transport costs.

**Article 102** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, animals are transported by road across provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government without entering or passing through the province via the designated passage established by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan on the transporter; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 103** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a quarantine certificate, quarantine mark, or livestock and poultry identification mark is transferred, forged, or altered, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal gains and the quarantine certificate, quarantine mark, or livestock and poultry identification mark, and impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan.

Where a forged or altered quarantine certificate, quarantine mark, or livestock and poultry identification mark is held or used, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the quarantine certificate, quarantine mark, livestock and poultry identification mark, and the corresponding animals and animal products, and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan.

**Article 104** Where any of the following acts is committed in violation of the provisions of this Law, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan:

(1) Unauthorizedly releasing information about an animal epidemic;

(2) Failing to comply with the regulations on the control of animal diseases lawfully made by the people's government at or above the county level or its competent department of agriculture and rural affairs;

(3) Concealing, transferring, or illegally digging up animals or animal products that have been lawfully isolated, sealed, or disposed of.

**Article 105** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, animal diagnosis and treatment activities are engaged in without obtaining an Animal Diagnosis and Treatment License, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the diagnosis and treatment activities, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 1 time but not more than 3 times the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are less than 30,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Where an animal diagnosis and treatment institution violates the provisions of this Law by failing to implement health and safety protection, disinfection, isolation, and disposal of diagnosis and treatment waste in accordance with regulations, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan; if the spread of an animal epidemic is caused, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the Animal Diagnosis and Treatment License shall be revoked.

**Article 106** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person engages in for-profit animal diagnosis and treatment activities without being registered as a practicing veterinarian, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the animal diagnosis and treatment activities, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed on the animal diagnosis and treatment institution where the person works.

Where a practicing veterinarian commits any of the following acts, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall issue a warning and order a suspension of animal diagnosis and treatment activities for not less than six months but not more than one year; if the circumstances are serious, the practicing veterinarian qualification certificate shall be revoked:

(1) Violating the operational technical specifications for animal diagnosis and treatment, causing or potentially causing the spread or prevalence of an animal epidemic;

(2) Using veterinary drugs or veterinary instruments that do not meet the requirements;

(3) Failing to participate in animal epidemic prevention, control, and eradication activities as required by the local people's government or the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs.

**Article 107** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, veterinary instruments are produced or sold, and the product quality does not meet the requirements, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification within a specified period; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order a suspension of business for rectification and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

**Article 108** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, any unit or individual engaged in animal epidemic research, diagnosis and treatment, animal raising, slaughtering, trading, isolation, transport, or the production, trading, processing, storage, or harmless treatment of animal products commits any of the following acts, the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall order rectification and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan; if rectification is refused, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed, and a suspension of business for rectification may be ordered:

(1) Failing to report the discovery of infected or suspected infected animals, or failing to take control measures such as isolation;

(2) Failing to truthfully provide information related to animal epidemic prevention;

(3) Refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection by the competent department of agriculture and rural affairs;

(4) Refusing or obstructing animal epidemic prevention and control institutions from conducting animal epidemic monitoring, testing, or assessment;

(5) Refusing or obstructing official veterinarians from performing their duties according to law.

**Article 109** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the spread or prevalence of a zoonotic infectious disease is caused, heavier sanctions or penalties shall be imposed according to law.

Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an act constitutes a violation of public security administration, public security administration penalties shall be imposed according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, damage is caused to another person's person or property, civil liability shall be borne according to law.

## Chapter 12 Supplementary Provisions

**Article 110** For the purposes of this Law, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them:

(1) "Area designated as free from specified animal diseases" means an area that has a natural barrier or has adopted artificial measures, has not experienced the occurrence of one or more specified animal diseases within a certain period, and has passed acceptance inspection;

(2) "Compartment free from specified animal diseases" means a specific small area composed of several animal farms and their auxiliary production sites under the same biosafety management system, which has not experienced the occurrence of one or more specified animal diseases within a certain period, and has passed acceptance inspection;

(3) "Dead diseased animal" means a dead animal that died from infection, disease, unknown causes, or that may harm human or animal health after inspection and quarantine;

(4) "Diseased animal product" means a product derived from a dead diseased animal, or an animal product that may harm human or animal health after inspection and quarantine.

**Article 111** The assessment of the disease-free equivalence of overseas areas designated as free from specified animal diseases and compartments free from specified animal diseases shall be carried out with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

**Article 112** Where there are special requirements for the epidemic prevention of experimental animals, the relevant provisions on the management of experimental animals shall apply.

**Article 113** This Law shall come into force on May 1, 2021.
