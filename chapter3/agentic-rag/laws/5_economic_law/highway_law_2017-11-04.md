# Highway Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 26th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 3, 1997

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Highway Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 12th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 1999

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Highway Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Certain Laws" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Twelve Laws including the Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 7, 2016

Fifth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Eleven Laws including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of strengthening the construction and management of highways, promoting the development of the highway industry, and meeting the needs of socialist modernization and the people's livelihood.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the planning, construction, maintenance, operation, use, and management of highways within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "highways" include highway bridges, highway tunnels, and highway ferries.

Article 3 The development of highways shall follow the principles of comprehensive planning, rational layout, ensuring quality, guaranteeing smooth traffic, protecting the environment, and giving equal emphasis to construction, renovation, and maintenance.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to support and promote highway construction. Highway construction shall be incorporated into the national economic and social development plans.

The State encourages and guides domestic and foreign economic organizations to invest in the construction and operation of highways in accordance with the law.

Article 5 The State assists and supports minority nationality areas, remote areas, and poverty-stricken areas in developing highway construction.

Article 6 Highways are classified, according to their status in the highway network, into national highways, provincial highways, county highways, and township highways, and are classified, according to technical grades, into expressways, first-class highways, second-class highways, third-class highways, and fourth-class highways. The specific classification standards shall be prescribed by the competent department of transportation under the State Council.

Newly built highways shall meet the requirements of the technical grades. Existing highways that do not meet the minimum technical grade requirements shall be gradually upgraded to meet the technical grade requirements through appropriate measures.

Article 7 Highways are protected by the State. No unit or individual may damage, destroy, or illegally occupy highways, highway land, or highway ancillary facilities.
Every unit and individual has the duty to protect highways, highway land, and highway ancillary facilities, and has the right to report and accuse any act that damages or destroys highways, highway land, or highway ancillary facilities, or affects highway safety.
Article 8 The competent department of transportation under the State Council shall be responsible for highway work nationwide.

The competent departments of transportation of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for highway work within their respective administrative regions; however, the responsibilities of the competent departments of transportation of local people's governments at or above the county level for the management and supervision of national highways and provincial highways shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall be responsible for the construction and maintenance of township highways within their respective administrative regions.

The competent departments of transportation of local people's governments at or above the county level may decide that highway management agencies exercise the administrative management functions and powers over highways in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 9 No unit or individual may illegally set up toll stations, collect fees, impose fines, or stop vehicles on highways.

Article 10 The State encourages scientific and technological research in highway work and rewards units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in highway scientific and technological research and application.

Article 11 Where this Law contains provisions concerning special-purpose highways, such provisions shall apply to special-purpose highways.

Special-purpose highways refer to roads built, maintained, and managed by enterprises or other units, which are exclusively or mainly used to provide transportation services for the enterprise or unit itself.

## Chapter II Highway Planning

Article 12 Highway planning shall be prepared based on the needs of national economic and social development and national defense construction, and shall be coordinated with urban development plans and transportation development plans of other modes.

Article 13 The land use plan for highway construction shall conform to the overall land use plan, and the annual land used for construction shall be included in the annual land use plan for construction.

Article 14 The plan for national highways shall be prepared by the competent department of transportation under the State Council jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council and after consultation with the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government along the national highways, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

The plan for provincial highways shall be prepared by the competent department of transportation of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government jointly with the relevant departments at the same level and after consultation with the people's governments at the next lower level along the provincial highways, and shall be submitted to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for approval, and shall be reported to the competent department of transportation under the State Council for the record.

The plan for county highways shall be prepared by the competent department of transportation of the people's government of a county jointly with the relevant departments at the same level, and after examination and approval by the people's government at the same level, shall be submitted to the people's government at the next higher level for approval.

The plan for township highways shall be prepared by the competent department of transportation of the people's government of a county with the assistance of the people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns, and shall be submitted to the people's government of the county for approval.

The plans for county highways and township highways approved in accordance with the provisions of the third and fourth paragraphs shall be reported to the competent department of transportation of the people's government at the next higher level of the approving authority for the record.

The plan for provincial highways shall be coordinated with the plan for national highways. The plan for county highways shall be coordinated with the plan for provincial highways. The plan for township highways shall be coordinated with the plan for county highways.

Article 15 The plan for a special-purpose highway shall be prepared by the competent unit of the special-purpose highway, and after examination and approval by its superior competent department, shall be submitted to the competent department of transportation of the people's government at or above the county level for review.

The plan for a special-purpose highway shall be coordinated with the highway plan. If the competent department of transportation of a people's government at or above the county level finds that the plan for a special-purpose highway is not coordinated with the plans for national highways, provincial highways, county highways, or township highways, it shall propose amendments, and the competent department and unit of the special-purpose highway shall make corresponding modifications.

Article 16 Partial adjustments to the plan for national highways shall be decided by the original preparing authority. If major modifications to the plan for national highways are necessary, the original preparing authority shall propose a modification plan and submit it to the State Council for approval.

If an approved plan for provincial highways, county highways, or township highways needs to be modified, the original preparing authority shall propose a modification plan and submit it to the original approving authority for approval.

Article 17 The naming and numbering of national highways shall be determined by the competent department of transportation under the State Council; the naming and numbering of provincial highways, county highways, and township highways shall be determined by the competent departments of transportation of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the relevant provisions of the competent department of transportation under the State Council.

Article 18 The planning and construction of new towns and development zones shall maintain a prescribed distance from highways and avoid being built on both sides of highways in a corresponding manner, so as to prevent the urbanization of highways and ensure the safe and smooth operation of highways.

Article 19 The State encourages the use of special-purpose highways for public transportation. When a special-purpose highway is mainly used for public transportation, upon application by the competent unit of the special-purpose highway, or upon application by the relevant parties with the consent of the competent unit of the special-purpose highway, and with the approval of the competent department of transportation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, it may be reclassified as a provincial highway, county highway, or township highway.

## Chapter III Highway Construction

Article 20 The competent departments of transportation of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their functions and duties, maintain the order of highway construction and strengthen the supervision and management of highway construction.

Article 21 Funds for highway construction may be raised, in addition to financial allocations from people's governments at all levels, including financial allocations converted from special funds for highway construction raised through taxation in accordance with the law, through loans from domestic or foreign financial institutions or foreign governments in accordance with the law.

The State encourages domestic and foreign economic organizations to invest in highway construction. Companies engaged in the development and operation of highways may raise funds by issuing stocks and corporate bonds in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Income from the transfer of highway toll collection rights in accordance with the provisions of this Law must be used for highway construction.

Funds raised from enterprises and individuals for highway construction must be based on needs and possibilities, adhere to the principle of voluntariness, and shall not be forcibly apportioned, and shall comply with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Funds for highway construction may also be raised by other means that comply with the law or the provisions of the State Council.

Article 22 Highway construction shall be carried out in accordance with the basic construction procedures and relevant provisions prescribed by the State.

Article 23 Highway construction projects shall implement the legal person responsibility system, the bidding and tendering system, and the project supervision system in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 24 The highway construction unit shall, based on the characteristics and technical requirements of the highway construction project, select survey and design units, construction units, and project supervision units with corresponding qualifications, and shall sign contracts respectively in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws, regulations, and rules and the requirements of highway engineering technical standards, specifying the rights and obligations of both parties.

The feasibility study units, survey and design units, construction units, and project supervision units undertaking highway construction projects must hold qualification certificates prescribed by the State.

Article 25 The construction of a highway construction project shall be submitted to the competent department of transportation of the local people's government at or above the county level for approval in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of transportation under the State Council.

Article 26 Highway construction must comply with highway engineering technical standards.

The design units, construction units, and project supervision units undertaking highway construction projects shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, establish and improve a quality assurance system, implement the post responsibility system, and carry out design, construction, and supervision in accordance with the requirements of relevant laws, regulations, and rules and highway engineering technical standards and the contract, to ensure the quality of the highway project.

Article 27 The use of land for highway construction shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Highway construction shall implement the principles of effectively protecting arable land and economizing on land use.

Article 28 If highway construction requires the use of state-owned barren hills or wasteland, or requires the extraction of sand, stone, or soil from state-owned barren hills, wasteland, river beaches, or tidal flats, after handling in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, no unit or individual may obstruct or illegally collect fees.

Article 29 Local people's governments at all levels shall provide support and assistance for the lawful use of land and the relocation of residents for highway construction.

Article 30 The design and construction of highway construction projects shall meet the requirements of protecting the environment, protecting cultural relics and historic sites, and preventing soil erosion in accordance with the law.

Highway construction projects that incorporate national defense requirements in the highway plan shall be constructed strictly in accordance with the plan to meet the needs of national defense transportation.

Article 31 If the construction of a highway affects the normal use of railway, water conservancy, electric power, postal, and telecommunications facilities and other facilities, the highway construction unit shall obtain the consent of the relevant departments in advance; if the highway construction causes damage to the relevant facilities, the highway construction unit shall repair them to a level not lower than the original technical standard of the facilities, or provide corresponding economic compensation.

Article 32 When reconstructing a highway, the construction unit shall set up conspicuous construction signs and safety signs at both ends of the construction section. If vehicles need to detour, signs shall be set up at the detour intersections; if a detour is not possible, a temporary road must be built to ensure the passage of vehicles and pedestrians.

Article 33 Upon completion of a highway construction project or a highway repair project, acceptance inspection shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State; projects that have not been inspected or have failed the inspection shall not be delivered for use.

Completed highways shall be provided with conspicuous signs and markings in accordance with the provisions of the competent department of transportation under the State Council.

Article 34 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall determine a highway land area of not less than one meter from the outer edge of the side ditches (intercepting ditches, slope toe berms, the same below) on both sides of the highway.

## Chapter IV Highway Maintenance

Article 35 Highway management agencies shall maintain highways in accordance with the technical specifications and operating procedures prescribed by the competent department of transportation under the State Council, ensuring that highways are constantly in good technical condition.

Article 36 The State adopts the method of taxation in accordance with the law to raise funds for highway maintenance. The specific implementation methods and procedures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Funds raised for highway maintenance through taxation in accordance with the law must be used exclusively for the maintenance and reconstruction of highways.

Article 37 People's governments at the county and township levels shall provide support and assistance for the extraction of sand, stone, and soil, as well as water intake, needed for highway maintenance.

Article 38 People's governments at the county and township levels shall, within the scope of rural voluntary labor, organize rural residents on both sides of highways to fulfill the obligation of providing labor for highway construction and maintenance in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 39 To ensure the personal safety of highway maintenance personnel, they shall wear uniform safety warning vests when performing maintenance operations; when using vehicles for maintenance operations, conspicuous operation signs shall be set up on the highway maintenance vehicles.

When highway maintenance vehicles are operating, their driving routes and directions are not subject to the restrictions of highway signs and markings, provided that the passage of other vehicles is not affected; passing vehicles shall pay attention to avoiding highway maintenance vehicles and personnel.

When highway maintenance project construction affects the passage of vehicles and pedestrians, the construction unit shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of Article 32 of this Law.

Article 40 If traffic on national highways or provincial highways is interrupted due to severe natural disasters, the highway management agency shall promptly repair them; if the highway management agency cannot repair them promptly, the local people's government at or above the county level shall promptly organize local authorities, organizations, enterprises, institutions, and urban and rural residents to carry out emergency repairs, and may request support from local military units to restore traffic as soon as possible.

Article 41 The highway management agency shall be responsible for soil and water conservation on the slopes and wasteland within the highway land area.

Article 42 Highway greening work shall be organized and implemented by the highway management agency in accordance with highway engineering technical standards.

Trees on highway land shall not be cut down arbitrarily; if felling is necessary for renewal, the consent of the competent department of transportation of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be obtained, and the approval procedures shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the "Forest Law of the People's Republic of China", and the task of renewal and replanting shall be completed.

## Chapter V Highway Administration

Article 43 Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen the protection of highways.

The competent departments of transportation of local people's governments at or above the county level shall conscientiously perform their duties, do a good job of highway protection in accordance with the law, and strive to adopt scientific management methods and advanced technical means to improve the level of highway management, gradually improve highway service facilities, and ensure the integrity, safety, and smoothness of highways.

Article 44 No unit or individual may occupy or excavate highways without authorization.

If it is necessary to occupy or excavate a highway or to change the alignment of a highway due to the construction of railways, airports, power stations, communication facilities, water conservancy projects, or other construction projects, the construction unit shall obtain the consent of the relevant competent department of transportation in advance; if it affects traffic safety, the consent of the relevant public security organ must also be obtained. If the highway is occupied or excavated or its alignment is changed, the construction unit shall repair, reconstruct it to a level not lower than the original technical standard of that section of the highway, or provide corresponding economic compensation.

Article 45 The construction of bridges, aqueducts, or the erection or laying of pipelines and other facilities across or through highways, as well as the erection or laying of pipelines, cables, and other facilities within the highway land area, shall be subject to the prior consent of the relevant competent department of transportation; if it affects traffic safety, the consent of the relevant public security organ must also be obtained; the facilities constructed, erected, or laid shall meet the requirements of highway engineering technical standards. If damage is caused to the highway, compensation shall be made according to the extent of the damage.

Article 46 No unit or individual may engage in activities on highways or within the highway land area such as setting up stalls, piling up articles, dumping garbage, setting up obstacles, digging ditches for water diversion, discharging pollutants using highway side ditches, or other activities that damage, pollute highways, or affect highway smoothness.

Article 47 Within a radius of 200 meters around large and medium-sized highway bridges and ferries, within a range of 100 meters above highway tunnels and outside tunnel entrances, and within a certain distance on both sides of highways, no extraction of sand, stone, or soil, dumping of waste, blasting operations, or other activities endangering the safety of highways, highway bridges, highway tunnels, or highway ferries shall be permitted.

If it is necessary to build embankments, compress or widen riverbeds within the scope mentioned in the preceding paragraph due to emergency rescue or flood control needs, approval shall be obtained in advance from the competent department of transportation of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government jointly with the water administrative department, and effective measures shall be taken to protect the safety of the relevant highways, highway bridges, highway tunnels, and highway ferries.

Article 48 Iron-wheeled vehicles, tracked vehicles, and other machinery that may damage the highway pavement shall not be driven on highways.

Agricultural machinery that needs to travel short distances on highways for local field operations, or military vehicles that need to travel on highways for mission execution, may be exempted from the restriction in the preceding paragraph, but safety protection measures shall be taken. If damage is caused to the highway, compensation shall be made according to the extent of the damage.

Article 49 The axle load of vehicles traveling on highways shall meet the requirements of highway engineering technical standards.

Article 50 Vehicles exceeding the load limit, height limit, width limit, or length limit standards of highways, highway bridges, highway tunnels, or automobile ferries shall not travel on highways, highway bridges, or within highway tunnels with such limits, and shall not use automobile ferries. If it is truly necessary to travel exceeding the load limit standard of a highway or highway bridge, approval must be obtained from the competent department of transportation of the local people's government at or above the county level, and effective protective measures shall be taken as required; indivisible over-limit articles shall be transported at the designated time, along the designated route, and at the designated speed, and conspicuous signs shall be displayed.

If the transport unit cannot take protective measures in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the competent department of transportation shall assist in taking protective measures, and the costs incurred shall be borne by the transport unit.

Article 51 Motor vehicle manufacturers and other units shall not use highways as test sites for checking the braking performance of motor vehicles.

Article 52 No unit or individual may damage, move without authorization, or alter highway ancillary facilities.
The highway ancillary facilities mentioned in the preceding paragraph refer to the facilities, equipment, and special buildings and structures set up for the protection and maintenance of highways and for ensuring the safety and smoothness of highways, including those for highway protection, drainage, maintenance, management, service, traffic safety, ferry operation, monitoring, communication, and toll collection.
Article 53 If damage is caused to a highway, the responsible person shall promptly report to the highway management agency and accept the on-site investigation by the highway management agency.

Article 54 No unit or individual may set up signs other than highway signs within the highway land area without the approval of the competent department of transportation of the local people's government at or above the county level.

Article 55 The addition of level crossings on highways must be approved in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State and constructed in accordance with the technical standards prescribed by the State.

Article 56 Except for those needed for highway protection and maintenance, the construction of buildings and ground structures within the building control zones on both sides of highways is prohibited; if it is necessary to lay pipelines, cables, and other facilities within the building control zones, prior approval shall be obtained from the competent department of transportation of the local people's government at or above the county level.The scope of the building control zone as stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be delineated by the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the principles of ensuring highway operation safety and economical use of land, and in compliance with the regulations of the State Council.

Once the scope of the building control zone has been delineated by the local people's government at or above the county level in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the competent transportation department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall set up marker posts and boundary markers. No unit or individual may damage or arbitrarily move such marker posts or boundary markers.

Article 57 Except as provided in paragraph 2 of Article 47 of this Law, the highway administration duties exercised by the competent transportation department under this Chapter may be exercised by the highway management agency in accordance with paragraph 4 of Article 8 of this Law.

## Chapter 6 Toll Highways

Article 58 The State permits the establishment of toll highways in accordance with the law, while also controlling the number of such toll highways.

Except for highways that may collect vehicle tolls as specified in Article 59 of this Law, no highway is permitted to collect vehicle tolls.

Article 59 The following highways that meet the technical grades and scales prescribed by the competent transportation department of the State Council may collect vehicle tolls in accordance with the law:

(1) Highways constructed by the competent transportation department of the local people's government at or above the county level using loans or funds raised from enterprises or individuals;

(2) Highways for which the toll collection right of the highways specified in the preceding item has been legally transferred to domestic or foreign economic organizations;

(3) Highways constructed with investment from domestic or foreign economic organizations in accordance with the law.

Article 60 The toll collection period for toll highways constructed by the competent transportation department of the local people's government at or above the county level using loans or raised funds shall be determined by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government in accordance with the regulations of the competent transportation department of the State Council, based on the principle of repaying loans and raised funds through toll collection.

For highways where the toll collection right has been transferred for value, after the transfer of the toll collection right, the transferee shall operate the toll collection. The term of the transfer of the toll collection right shall be agreed upon by the transferor and the transferee, but shall not exceed the period prescribed by the State Council.

Domestic or foreign economic organizations investing in highway construction must go through the approval procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations; after the highway is completed, the investor shall operate the toll collection. The toll operation period shall be agreed upon by the relevant competent transportation department and the investor based on the principle of recovering the investment and obtaining a reasonable return, and shall go through the approval procedures in accordance with relevant state regulations, but shall not exceed the period prescribed by the State Council.

Article 61 The transfer of the toll collection right for national highways among the highways specified in item (1) of paragraph 1 of Article 59 of this Law shall be reported to the competent transportation department of the State Council for record within thirty working days from the date of signing the transfer agreement; the transfer of the toll collection right for other highways besides national highways shall be reported to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for record within thirty working days from the date of signing the transfer agreement.

The minimum transaction price for the transfer of the highway toll collection right as stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be determined based on the value assessed by a state-owned assets appraisal institution.

Article 62 Domestic or foreign economic organizations that have acquired the highway toll collection right or invested in highway construction shall establish enterprises for the development and operation of highways (hereinafter referred to as highway operation enterprises) in accordance with the law.

Article 63 The toll rate standard for vehicle tolls on toll highways shall be proposed by the highway toll collection unit and submitted to the competent transportation department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for review and approval in conjunction with the price administrative department at the same level.

Article 64 The establishment of toll stations for collecting vehicle tolls on toll highways shall be submitted to the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for review and approval. For toll highways spanning provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, the establishment of toll stations shall be determined through consultation by the relevant people's governments of the provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government; if consultation fails, the decision shall be made by the competent transportation department of the State Council. If the same toll highway is constructed by different competent transportation departments or operated by different highway operation enterprises, toll stations shall be planned comprehensively and set up reasonably based on the principle of "unified toll collection and proportional distribution."

The distance between two toll stations shall not be less than the standard prescribed by the competent transportation department of the State Council.

Article 65 For highways where the toll collection right has been transferred for value, upon the expiration of the term agreed upon in the toll collection right transfer contract, the toll collection right shall be recovered by the transferor.

For toll highways invested, constructed, and operated by domestic or foreign economic organizations in accordance with the provisions of this Law, upon the expiration of the agreed operation period, the highway shall be taken over by the State without compensation and managed by the relevant competent transportation department.

Article 66 The maintenance of highways for which the toll collection right has been acquired or which have been invested, constructed, and operated by domestic or foreign economic organizations in accordance with Article 59 of this Law shall be the responsibility of the respective highway operation enterprises. During the operation period, these highway operation enterprises shall carry out highway maintenance work in accordance with the technical specifications and operating procedures prescribed by the competent transportation department of the State Council. Upon the expiration of the toll collection right transfer period or the operation period, the highway shall be in good technical condition.

The greening of the highways specified in the preceding paragraph and the soil and water conservation work within the highway land use area shall be the responsibility of the respective highway operation enterprises.

The highway administration of the highways specified in paragraph 1 shall be governed by the provisions of Chapter 5 of this Law. The duties of highway administration for such highways shall be exercised by the dispatched agencies or personnel of the competent transportation department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the highway management agency.

Article 67 Anyone who engages in the activities listed in Article 44, paragraph 2, Article 45, Article 48, and Article 50 of this Law on a toll highway shall, in addition to handling the matter in accordance with the provisions of those respective articles, provide corresponding compensation if losses are caused to the highway operation enterprise.

Article 68 Specific management measures for toll highways shall be formulated by the State Council in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter 7 Supervision and Inspection

Article 69 The competent transportation department and the highway management agency shall supervise and inspect the implementation of laws and regulations concerning highways in accordance with the law.

Article 70 The competent transportation department and the highway management agency bear the responsibility for managing and protecting highways, and have the authority to inspect and stop any acts that encroach upon or damage highways, highway land use areas, highway ancillary facilities, or other acts that violate the provisions of this Law.

Article 71 When highway supervision and inspection personnel conduct supervision and inspection in accordance with the law at highways, building control zones, vehicle parking areas, and the units to which vehicles belong, no unit or individual may obstruct them.

Highway operators, users, and other relevant units and individuals shall accept the supervision and inspection conducted by highway supervision and inspection personnel in accordance with the law and provide convenience for them.

Highway supervision and inspection personnel, when performing official duties, shall wear identification badges and hold certificates.

Article 72 The competent transportation department and the highway management agency shall strengthen the management and education of their subordinate highway supervision and inspection personnel, requiring them to be familiar with relevant national laws and regulations, be fair and honest, provide enthusiastic service, and enforce the law impartially. They shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the law enforcement conduct of highway supervision and inspection personnel, promptly correct any illegal acts, and handle them in accordance with the law.

Article 73 Special vehicles used for highway supervision and inspection shall be equipped with uniform markings and warning lights.

## Chapter 8 Legal Liability

Article 74 Anyone who, in violation of the law or relevant regulations of the State Council, sets up checkpoints or collects tolls on highways without authorization shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to cease the illegal act, have the illegal gains confiscated, and may be fined up to three times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of up to 20,000 yuan may be imposed; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 75 Anyone who, in violation of Article 25 of this Law, commences construction without the approval of the relevant competent transportation department, the competent transportation department may order the cessation of construction and may impose a fine of up to 50,000 yuan.

Article 76 Anyone who commits one of the following illegal acts shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to cease the illegal act and may be fined up to 30,000 yuan:

(1) Violating paragraph 1 of Article 44 of this Law by occupying or excavating a highway without authorization;

(2) Violating Article 45 of this Law by constructing bridges, aqueducts, or installing or burying pipelines, cables, or other facilities without consent or without meeting the technical standards for highway engineering;

(3) Violating Article 47 of this Law by engaging in operations that endanger highway safety;

(4) Violating Article 48 of this Law by allowing iron-wheeled vehicles, tracked vehicles, and other machinery that may damage the road surface to travel on highways without authorization;

(5) Violating Article 50 of this Law by using a vehicle ferry with overloaded vehicles or traveling on highways with overloaded vehicles without authorization;

(6) Violating Articles 52 and 56 of this Law by damaging, moving, or altering highway ancillary facilities, or damaging or moving marker posts or boundary markers in building control zones, which may endanger highway safety.

Article 77 Anyone who, in violation of Article 46 of this Law, causes damage to or pollution of the highway pavement, or affects the smooth flow of traffic, or who, in violation of Article 51 of this Law, uses a highway as a test track, shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to cease the illegal act and may be fined up to 5,000 yuan.

Article 78 Anyone who, in violation of Article 53 of this Law, causes damage to a highway and fails to report it shall be fined up to 1,000 yuan by the competent transportation department.

Article 79 Anyone who, in violation of Article 54 of this Law, sets up signs other than highway signs within the highway land use area shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to remove them within a specified period and may be fined up to 20,000 yuan; if they fail to remove them within the specified period, the competent transportation department shall remove them, and the relevant costs shall be borne by the person who set them up.

Article 80 Anyone who, in violation of Article 55 of this Law, adds a level crossing on a highway without approval shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to restore the original condition and fined up to 50,000 yuan.

Article 81 Anyone who, in violation of Article 56 of this Law, constructs buildings or ground structures within a highway building control zone, or buries pipelines, cables, or other facilities without authorization, shall be ordered by the competent transportation department to remove them within a specified period and may be fined up to 50,000 yuan. If they fail to remove them within the specified period, the competent transportation department shall remove them, and the relevant costs shall be borne by the builder or constructor.

Article 82 Except as provided in Articles 74 and 75 of this Law, the administrative penalties and administrative measures exercised by the competent transportation department under this Chapter may be exercised by the highway management agency in accordance with paragraph 4 of Article 8 of this Law.

Article 83 Anyone who obstructs highway construction or emergency repair, causing the construction or repair to proceed abnormally, without causing serious losses, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China."

Anyone who damages a highway or arbitrarily moves highway signs, which may affect traffic safety, but does not constitute a criminal offense, shall be subject to the punishment provisions of Article 99 of the "Road Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China."

Anyone who refuses or obstructs highway supervision and inspection personnel from performing their duties in accordance with the law without using violence or threats shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the "Public Security Administration Punishments Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 84 Anyone who violates the relevant provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 85 Anyone who violates the relevant provisions of this Law and causes damage to a highway shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Any vehicle that causes significant damage to a highway must immediately stop, protect the scene, report to the highway management agency, and may only leave after undergoing investigation and processing by the highway management agency.

Article 86 Staff members of the competent transportation department or the highway management agency who neglect their duties, engage in malpractice for personal gain, or abuse their power, if constituting a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law; if not constituting a crime, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 87 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1998.
