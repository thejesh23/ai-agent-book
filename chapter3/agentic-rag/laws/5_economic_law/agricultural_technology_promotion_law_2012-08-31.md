# Agricultural Technology Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 2, 1993

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Agricultural Technology Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 31, 2012

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to strengthen agricultural technology promotion, facilitate the rapid application of agricultural research achievements and practical technologies to agricultural production, enhance the supporting and safeguarding capacity of science and technology, promote the sustainable development of agriculture and the rural economy, and realize agricultural modernization.

**Article 2** For the purposes of this Law, "agricultural technology" refers to research achievements and practical technologies applied to crop farming, forestry, animal husbandry, and fisheries, including:

(1) Technologies for improved seed breeding, cultivation, fertilizer application, and aquaculture;

(2) Technologies for the prevention and control of plant diseases and insect pests, animal epidemics, and other harmful organisms;

(3) Technologies for the harvesting, processing, packaging, storage, and transportation of agricultural products;

(4) Technologies for the safe use of agricultural inputs and the quality and safety of agricultural products;

(5) Technologies for farmland water conservancy, rural water supply and drainage, soil improvement, and water and soil conservation;

(6) Technologies for agricultural mechanization, agricultural aviation, agricultural meteorology, and agricultural information;

(7) Technologies for agricultural disaster prevention and mitigation, agricultural resources and ecological safety, and the development and utilization of rural energy;

(8) Other agricultural technologies.

For the purposes of this Law, "agricultural technology promotion" refers to the activities of popularizing and applying agricultural technologies throughout the entire process of pre-production, in-production, and post-production in agriculture through experimentation, demonstration, training, guidance, and consulting services.

**Article 3** The State supports the cause of agricultural technology promotion, accelerates the popularization and application of agricultural technologies, and develops agriculture that is high-yielding, high-quality, efficient, ecological, and safe.

**Article 4** Agricultural technology promotion shall follow the following principles:

(1) Be conducive to the sustainable development of agriculture and the rural economy and to increasing farmers' income;

(2) Respect the will of agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations;

(3) Be tailored to local conditions, and undergo experimentation and demonstration;

(4) Implement classified management of public-interest promotion and for-profit promotion;

(5) Balance economic and social benefits, with emphasis on ecological benefits.

**Article 5** The State encourages and supports scientific and technical personnel in developing, promoting, and applying advanced agricultural technologies, and encourages and supports agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations in applying advanced agricultural technologies.

The State encourages the use of modern information technology and other advanced means of dissemination to popularize agricultural science and technology knowledge, innovate methods and approaches for agricultural technology promotion, and improve promotion efficiency.

**Article 6** The State encourages and supports the introduction of advanced foreign agricultural technologies and promotes international cooperation and exchange in agricultural technology promotion.

**Article 7** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over agricultural technology promotion work, organize relevant departments and units to take measures to improve the service level of agricultural technology promotion, and promote the development of the agricultural technology promotion cause.

**Article 8** Units and individuals that make contributions to agricultural technology promotion work shall be rewarded.

**Article 9** The agricultural, forestry, water conservancy, and other departments of the State Council (hereinafter collectively referred to as agricultural technology promotion departments) shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for relevant agricultural technology promotion work nationwide. The agricultural technology promotion departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, under the leadership of the people's government at the same level and in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for relevant agricultural technology promotion work within their respective administrative regions. The science and technology department of the same level of people's government shall provide guidance for agricultural technology promotion work. Other relevant departments of the same level of people's government shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for relevant work in agricultural technology promotion.

## Chapter II: Agricultural Technology Promotion System

**Article 10** Agricultural technology promotion shall implement a promotion system that combines State agricultural technology promotion institutions with agricultural research units, relevant schools, specialized farmer cooperatives, agriculture-related enterprises, mass scientific and technological organizations, and farmer technicians.

The State encourages and supports supply and marketing cooperatives, other enterprises and institutions, social organizations, and scientific and technical personnel from all sectors of society in carrying out agricultural technology promotion services.

**Article 11** State agricultural technology promotion institutions at all levels are public service institutions and perform the following public-interest duties:

(1) Introduction, experimentation, and demonstration of key agricultural technologies determined by people's governments at all levels;

(2) Monitoring, forecasting, and prevention of plant diseases and insect pests, animal epidemics, and agricultural disasters;

(3) Technical services for inspection, testing, monitoring, and consultation during the production process of agricultural products;

(4) Monitoring services for agricultural resources, forest resources, agricultural ecological safety, and the use of agricultural inputs;

(5) Technical services for water resource management, flood control and drought relief, and farmland water conservancy construction;

(6) Public agricultural information and agricultural technology publicity, education, and training services;

(7) Other duties as prescribed by laws and regulations.

**Article 12** Based on the principles of scientific rationality and concentration of resources, as well as the characteristics of county-level agriculture, forest resources, and the distribution of water systems and water conservancy facilities, State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall be established at the county, township, or regional level as appropriate to local conditions.

Township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions may adopt a system where management is primarily by the agricultural technology promotion department of the county people's government, or primarily by the township people's government with professional guidance from the agricultural technology promotion department of the county people's government. The specific system shall be determined by the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 13** The staffing of State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall be reasonably determined based on the scale of planting and breeding, service scope, and work tasks in the area served, ensuring the performance of public-interest duties.

The post setup of State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall primarily consist of professional and technical posts. All posts in township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall be professional and technical posts. The proportion of professional and technical posts in county-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall not be less than 80% of the total institutional posts, and in other State agricultural technology promotion institutions, not less than 70% of the total institutional posts.

**Article 14** Professional and technical personnel of State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall possess corresponding professional and technical levels and meet the requirements of their post responsibilities.

Newly hired professional and technical personnel of State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall have a college degree or higher in a relevant specialty and pass the professional and technical level assessment organized by the relevant department of the people's government at or above the county level. In autonomous counties, ethnic townships, and contiguous destitute areas designated by the State, with the approval of the relevant department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, personnel with a secondary specialized school degree in a relevant specialty or other personnel with corresponding professional and technical levels may be hired.

The State encourages and supports college graduates and scientific and technical personnel in engaging in agricultural technology promotion work at the grassroots level. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to attract talent, strengthen, and enrich the grassroots agricultural technology promotion team.

**Article 15** The State encourages and supports village-level agricultural technology service stations and farmer technicians in carrying out agricultural technology promotion. Farmer technicians who assist in carrying out public-interest agricultural technology promotion activities shall be given subsidies in accordance with regulations.

Farmer technicians who meet the relevant conditions after assessment may be awarded corresponding technical titles and issued certificates in accordance with relevant regulations.

State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall strengthen guidance for village-level agricultural technology service stations and farmer technicians.

Villagers' committees and village collective economic organizations shall promote and assist village-level agricultural technology service stations and farmer technicians in carrying out their work.

**Article 16** Agricultural research units and relevant schools shall adapt to the needs of rural economic construction and development, carry out agricultural technology development and promotion work, and accelerate the popularization and application of advanced technologies in agricultural production.

Agricultural research units and relevant schools shall take the actual performance of their scientific and technical personnel in agricultural technology promotion work as an important part of work assessment and professional title evaluation.

**Article 17** The State encourages farms, forest farms, pastures, fisheries, and water conservancy project management units to carry out agricultural technology promotion services for the public.

**Article 18** The State encourages and supports the development of mass scientific and technological organizations such as rural professional technical associations and leverages their role in agricultural technology promotion.

## Chapter III: Promotion and Application of Agricultural Technology

**Article 19** The promotion of major agricultural technologies shall be included in relevant national and local development plans and programs. The agricultural technology promotion departments shall, in conjunction with the science and technology and other relevant departments, cooperate with each other and organize implementation in accordance with their respective responsibilities.

**Article 20** Agricultural research units and relevant schools shall take technical problems that need to be solved in agricultural production as research topics. Their research achievements may be promoted through relevant agricultural technology promotion units or directly to agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations.

The State guides agricultural research units and relevant schools in carrying out public-interest agricultural technology promotion services.

**Article 21** Agricultural technologies promoted to agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations must be proven through experimentation in the promotion area to be advanced, applicable, and safe.

**Article 22** The State encourages and supports agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations in participating in agricultural technology promotion.

When agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations apply advanced agricultural technologies in production, relevant departments and units shall provide support in terms of technical training, funds, materials, and sales.

Agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations apply agricultural technologies on a voluntary basis; no unit or individual may compel them to do so.

When promoting agricultural technologies, demonstration applications shall be carried out by selecting qualified farm households, areas, or engineering projects.

**Article 23** County and township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall organize agricultural laborers to learn agricultural science and technology knowledge and improve their ability to apply agricultural technologies.

The education, human resources and social security, agriculture, forestry, water conservancy, science and technology, and other departments shall support agricultural research units and relevant schools in carrying out vocational and technical education and technical training related to agricultural technology promotion, and improve the technical quality of agricultural technology promoters and agricultural laborers.

The State encourages social forces to carry out agricultural technology training.

**Article 24** State agricultural technology promotion institutions at all levels shall conscientiously perform the public-interest duties stipulated in Article 11 of this Law, promote agricultural technologies to agricultural laborers and agricultural production and operation organizations, and provide free services.

Units other than State agricultural technology promotion institutions and scientific and technical personnel who provide agricultural technologies in the form of technology transfer, technical services, technical contracting, technical consulting, and technology as equity may provide paid services. Their lawful income and intellectual property rights such as new plant varieties and agricultural technology patents are protected by law. When conducting technology transfer, technical services, technical contracting, technical consulting, and technology as equity, the parties involved shall enter into contracts stipulating their respective rights and obligations.

**Article 25** The State encourages and supports specialized farmer cooperatives and agriculture-related enterprises in adopting various forms to provide relevant technical services for farmers to apply advanced agricultural technologies.

**Article 26** The State encourages and supports the construction of agricultural demonstration zones focusing on the production of bulk agricultural products and advantageous and characteristic agricultural products, leverages the leading role of demonstration zones in agricultural technology promotion, and promotes the development of industrialized agriculture and modern agricultural construction.

**Article 27** People's governments at all levels may adopt methods such as purchasing services to guide social forces to participate in public-interest agricultural technology promotion services.

## Chapter IV: Safeguard Measures for Agricultural Technology Promotion

**Article 28** The State gradually increases investment in agricultural technology promotion. People's governments at all levels shall ensure funds for agricultural technology promotion within their fiscal budgets and ensure that such funds increase year by year in accordance with regulations.

People's governments at all levels raise special funds for agricultural technology promotion through fiscal allocations and by extracting a certain proportion of funds from the agricultural development fund, to be used for implementing agricultural technology promotion projects. The central finance provides subsidies for the promotion of major agricultural technologies.

The operating expenses of county and township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall be determined based on the local service scale and performance, and shall be jointly borne by fiscal authorities at all levels.

No unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate funds used for agricultural technology promotion.

**Article 29** People's governments at all levels shall take measures to ensure and improve the working conditions, living conditions, and treatment of professional and technical personnel in county and township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions, provide subsidies in accordance with State regulations, and maintain the stability of the State agricultural technology promotion team.

The evaluation of professional titles for professional and technical personnel engaged in agricultural technology promotion work in counties, townships, and villages shall primarily focus on assessing their professional and technical level and actual performance in promotion work.

**Article 30** People's governments at all levels shall take measures to ensure that State agricultural technology promotion institutions obtain necessary working conditions such as experimental demonstration sites, office spaces, and promotion and training facilities and equipment.

Local people's governments at all levels shall ensure that the experimental demonstration sites, production materials, and other property of State agricultural technology promotion institutions are not infringed upon.

**Article 31** Agricultural technology promotion departments and State agricultural technology promotion institutions at or above the county level shall systematically provide technical training and organize professional development for agricultural technology promoters, enabling them to continuously update their knowledge and improve their professional level.

**Article 32** Agricultural technology promotion departments at or above the county level and township people's governments shall supervise and evaluate the performance of public-interest duties by the State agricultural technology promotion institutions under their management.

Agricultural technology promotion departments at all levels and State agricultural technology promotion institutions shall establish a work responsibility system and an evaluation system for professional and technical personnel of State agricultural technology promotion institutions.

For personnel of township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions primarily managed by the agricultural technology promotion department of the county people's government, the opinions of the township people's government in the service area and the service recipients shall be fully solicited regarding their professional assessment, post appointment, and promotion.

For personnel of township-level State agricultural technology promotion institutions primarily managed by the township people's government with professional guidance from the agricultural technology promotion department of the county people's government, the opinions of the agricultural technology promotion department of the county people's government in the locality and the service recipients shall be fully solicited regarding their professional assessment, post appointment, and promotion.

**Article 33** Those engaged in agricultural technology promotion services may enjoy preferential treatment in taxation, credit, and other aspects as stipulated by the State.

## Chapter V: Legal Liability

**Article 34** Where relevant departments of people's governments at all levels and their staff fail to perform their duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 35** Where a State agricultural technology promotion institution or its staff fails to perform its duties in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the competent authority shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit and circulate a notice of criticism; the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 36** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, agricultural technologies that have not been proven through experimentation to be advanced, applicable, or safe are promoted to agricultural laborers or agricultural production and operation organizations, causing losses, the responsible party shall bear liability for compensation.

**Article 37** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, agricultural laborers or agricultural production and operation organizations are compelled to apply agricultural technologies, causing losses, the responsible party shall bear liability for compensation in accordance with the law.

**Article 38** Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, funds used for agricultural technology promotion are intercepted or misappropriated, the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 39** This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
