# Anti-Monopoly Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 30, 2007

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing and restraining monopolistic conduct, protecting fair competition in the market, enhancing economic efficiency, safeguarding the interests of consumers and the public interest, and promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to monopolistic conduct in economic activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China; and shall apply to monopolistic conduct outside the territory of the People's Republic of China that has the effect of eliminating or restricting competition in the domestic market.

Article 3 Monopolistic conduct as provided for in this Law includes:

(1) Monopoly agreements reached between business operators;

(2) Abuse of dominant market positions by business operators;

(3) Concentration of business operators that has or may have the effect of eliminating or restricting competition.

Article 4 The State shall formulate and implement competition rules compatible with the socialist market economy, improve macroeconomic regulation, and build a unified, open, competitive, and orderly market system.

Article 5 Business operators may, through fair competition and voluntary association, implement concentration in accordance with the law, expand their business scale, and enhance their market competitiveness.

Article 6 Business operators with a dominant market position shall not abuse that position to eliminate or restrict competition.

Article 7 With respect to industries controlled by the state-owned economy and related to the lifeline of the national economy and national security, as well as industries subject to exclusive operations and sales according to law, the State shall protect the lawful business activities of business operators therein, and shall supervise and regulate their business conduct and the prices of their goods and services in accordance with the law, in order to safeguard consumer interests and promote technological progress.

Business operators in the industries specified in the preceding paragraph shall operate according to law, be honest and trustworthy, exercise strict self-discipline, accept public supervision, and shall not use their controlling or exclusive positions to harm consumer interests.

Article 8 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to eliminate or restrict competition.

Article 9 The State Council shall establish an Anti-Monopoly Commission, which shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, and guiding anti-monopoly work, and shall perform the following duties:

(1) Studying and drafting relevant competition policies;

(2) Organizing investigations and assessments of the overall market competition situation, and issuing assessment reports;

(3) Formulating and issuing anti-monopoly guidelines;

(4) Coordinating anti-monopoly administrative enforcement work;

(5) Other duties as prescribed by the State Council.

The composition and working rules of the Anti-Monopoly Commission of the State Council shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 10 The agency designated by the State Council to undertake anti-monopoly enforcement responsibilities (hereinafter collectively referred to as the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council) shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, be responsible for anti-monopoly enforcement work.

The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council may, as needed, authorize the corresponding agencies of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government to be responsible for relevant anti-monopoly enforcement work in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 11 Industry associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, guide business operators in their industry to compete in accordance with the law, and maintain market competition order.

Article 12 For the purposes of this Law, "business operator" means a natural person, legal person, or other organization engaged in the production or operation of goods or the provision of services.

For the purposes of this Law, "relevant market" means the scope of goods and the geographic scope within which business operators compete for specific goods or services (hereinafter collectively referred to as "goods") during a certain period.

## Chapter II Monopoly Agreements

Article 13 Competing business operators are prohibited from reaching the following monopoly agreements:

(1) Fixing or changing the price of goods;

(2) Restricting the quantity of goods produced or sold;

(3) Dividing sales markets or raw material procurement markets;

(4) Restricting the purchase of new technology or new equipment, or restricting the development of new technology or new products;

(5) Engaging in concerted refusals to deal;

(6) Other monopoly agreements as determined by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council.

For the purposes of this Law, "monopoly agreement" means an agreement, decision, or other concerted practice that eliminates or restricts competition.

Article 14 Business operators are prohibited from reaching the following monopoly agreements with their trading counterparts:

(1) Fixing the price of goods for resale to a third party;

(2) Restricting the minimum price of goods for resale to a third party;

(3) Other monopoly agreements as determined by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council.

Article 15 The provisions of Articles 13 and 14 of this Law shall not apply to an agreement if the business operators can prove that it falls under any of the following circumstances:

(1) For the purpose of improving technology or researching and developing new products;

(2) For the purpose of improving product quality, reducing costs, or enhancing efficiency, by unifying product specifications or standards, or implementing specialized division of labor;

(3) For the purpose of improving the operational efficiency of small and medium-sized business operators and enhancing their competitiveness;

(4) For the purpose of achieving public interests such as energy conservation, environmental protection, disaster relief, and assistance;

(5) For the purpose of mitigating a serious decline in sales volume or obvious overproduction during an economic downturn;

(6) For the purpose of protecting legitimate interests in foreign trade and foreign economic cooperation;

(7) Other circumstances as provided by laws and the State Council.

Where an agreement falls under items (1) to (5) of the preceding paragraph and is exempted from the application of Articles 13 and 14 of this Law, the business operators shall also prove that the agreement will not seriously restrict competition in the relevant market and that consumers can share in the resulting benefits.

Article 16 Industry associations shall not organize business operators in their industry to engage in monopolistic conduct prohibited by this Chapter.

## Chapter III Abuse of Dominant Market Positions

Article 17 Business operators with a dominant market position are prohibited from engaging in the following acts of abuse of that position:

(1) Selling goods at unfairly high prices or purchasing goods at unfairly low prices;

(2) Selling goods at prices below cost without justifiable reasons;

(3) Refusing to deal with trading counterparts without justifiable reasons;

(4) Without justifiable reasons, restricting trading counterparts to deal only with themselves or with designated business operators;

(5) Without justifiable reasons, tying goods or imposing other unreasonable trading conditions in a transaction;

(6) Without justifiable reasons, applying differential treatment to trading counterparts with equivalent conditions in terms of transaction prices or other transaction terms;

(7) Other acts of abuse of a dominant market position as determined by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council.

For the purposes of this Law, "dominant market position" means a market position held by a business operator that enables it to control the price, quantity, or other trading conditions of goods in the relevant market, or to hinder or affect the ability of other business operators to enter the relevant market.

Article 18 The determination that a business operator has a dominant market position shall be based on the following factors:

(1) The market share of the business operator in the relevant market, and the competitive conditions in the relevant market;

(2) The ability of the business operator to control the sales market or the raw material procurement market;

(3) The financial resources and technical conditions of the business operator;

(4) The degree of dependence of other business operators on the business operator in transactions;

(5) The difficulty for other business operators to enter the relevant market;

(6) Other factors relevant to the determination of the business operator's dominant market position.

Article 19 A business operator may be presumed to have a dominant market position under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The market share of one business operator in the relevant market reaches one-half;

(2) The combined market share of two business operators in the relevant market reaches two-thirds;

(3) The combined market share of three business operators in the relevant market reaches three-quarters.

Where the circumstances specified in items (2) or (3) of the preceding paragraph exist, and the market share of any of the business operators is less than one-tenth, that business operator shall not be presumed to have a dominant market position.

A business operator presumed to have a dominant market position shall not be determined to have such a position if it has evidence to prove that it does not have a dominant market position.

## Chapter IV Concentration of Business Operators

Article 20 Concentration of business operators means any of the following circumstances:

(1) Merger of business operators;

(2) Acquisition by a business operator of control over other business operators through the acquisition of equity or assets;

(3) Acquisition by a business operator of control over other business operators, or the ability to exercise decisive influence over other business operators, through contracts or other means.

Article 21 Where a concentration of business operators reaches the notification threshold prescribed by the State Council, the business operators shall notify the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council in advance; the concentration shall not be implemented without such notification.

Article 22 A concentration of business operators may be notified to the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council under any of the following circumstances:

(1) One business operator participating in the concentration owns more than fifty percent of the voting shares or assets of each other business operator participating in the concentration;

(2) More than fifty percent of the voting shares or assets of each business operator participating in the concentration are owned by the same business operator not participating in the concentration.

Article 23 When notifying a concentration, business operators shall submit the following documents and materials to the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council:

(1) A notification form;

(2) An explanation of the impact of the concentration on competition in the relevant market;

(3) The concentration agreement;

(4) The financial accounting reports of the business operators participating in the concentration for the preceding fiscal year, audited by an accounting firm;

(5) Other documents and materials as prescribed by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council.

The notification form shall state the names, domiciles, business scopes of the business operators participating in the concentration, the expected date of implementation of the concentration, and other matters as prescribed by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council.

Article 24 If the documents and materials submitted by the business operators are incomplete, they shall supplement them within the time limit prescribed by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council. If the business operators fail to supplement the documents and materials within the time limit, the notification shall be deemed not to have been made.

Article 25 The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council shall, within thirty days from the date of receipt of the documents and materials submitted by the business operators that comply with the provisions of Article 23 of this Law, conduct a preliminary review of the notified concentration, make a decision on whether to conduct a further review, and notify the business operators in writing. Before the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council makes a decision, the business operators shall not implement the concentration.

If the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council decides not to conduct a further review or fails to make a decision within the time limit, the business operators may implement the concentration.

Article 26 If the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council decides to conduct a further review, it shall complete the review within ninety days from the date of the decision, make a decision on whether to prohibit the concentration, and notify the business operators in writing. A decision to prohibit the concentration shall state the reasons. During the review period, the business operators shall not implement the concentration.

Under any of the following circumstances, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council may, by written notice to the business operators, extend the review period specified in the preceding paragraph, but the extension shall not exceed sixty days:

(1) The business operators agree to the extension of the review period;

(2) The documents and materials submitted by the business operators are inaccurate and require further verification;

(3) Significant changes have occurred in the relevant circumstances after the notification.

If the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council fails to make a decision within the time limit, the business operators may implement the concentration.

Article 27 When reviewing a concentration of business operators, the following factors shall be considered:

(1) The market shares of the business operators participating in the concentration in the relevant market and their control over the market;

(2) The market concentration of the relevant market;

(3) The impact of the concentration on market entry and technological progress;

(4) The impact of the concentration on consumers and other relevant business operators;

(5) The impact of the concentration on the development of the national economy;

(6) Other factors affecting market competition that the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council considers should be considered.

Article 28 If a concentration of business operators has or may have the effect of eliminating or restricting competition, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council shall make a decision to prohibit the concentration. However, if the business operators can prove that the favorable effects of the concentration on competition clearly outweigh the adverse effects, or that the concentration is in the public interest, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council may decide not to prohibit the concentration.

Article 29 For a concentration that is not prohibited, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council may decide to attach restrictive conditions to reduce the adverse effects of the concentration on competition.

Article 30 The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency under the State Council shall promptly make public its decision to prohibit a concentration or its decision to attach restrictive conditions to a concentration.

Article 31 Where a foreign investor acquires a domestic enterprise or participates in a concentration of business operators by other means, involving national security, in addition to the review of the concentration in accordance with the provisions of this Law, a national security review shall also be conducted in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

## Chapter V Abuse of Administrative Power to Eliminate or Restrict Competition

Article 32 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to restrict or, in a disguised form, restrict units or individuals from dealing in, purchasing, or using goods provided by designated business operators.

Article 33 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to engage in the following acts that hinder the free flow of goods between regions:

(1) Imposing discriminatory fee items, discriminatory fee standards, or discriminatory prices on goods from other regions;

(2) Prescribing technical requirements or inspection standards for goods from other regions that are different from those for similar local goods, or adopting discriminatory technical measures such as repeated inspections or repeated certifications for goods from other regions, to restrict the entry of goods from other regions into the local market;

(3) Imposing administrative licenses specifically targeting goods from other regions to restrict their entry into the local market;

(4) Setting up checkpoints or taking other means to obstruct the entry of goods from other regions or the exit of local goods;

(5) Other acts that hinder the free flow of goods between regions.

Article 34 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to exclude or restrict business operators from other regions from participating in local tenders and bidding activities by setting discriminatory qualification requirements, evaluation standards, or failing to publish information in accordance with the law.

Article 35 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to exclude or restrict business operators from other regions from investing or establishing branches in the local area by adopting unequal treatment compared to local business operators.

Article 36 Administrative organs and organizations authorized by laws or regulations to perform public administrative functions shall not abuse administrative power to compel business operators to engage in monopolistic conduct as provided for in this Law.

Article 37 Administrative organs shall not abuse administrative power to formulate regulations containing content that eliminates or restricts competition.

## Chapter VI Investigation into Suspected Monopolistic Conduct

Article 38 The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall investigate suspected monopolistic conduct in accordance with the law.

Any unit or individual has the right to report suspected monopolistic conduct to the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency. The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall keep the reporter confidential.

If a report is made in writing and provides relevant facts and evidence, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall conduct a necessary investigation.

Article 39 When investigating suspected monopolistic conduct, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency may take the following measures:

(1) Entering the business premises or other relevant premises of the business operator under investigation for inspection;

(2) Questioning the business operator under investigation, interested parties, or other relevant units or individuals, and requiring them to explain the relevant circumstances;

(3) Consulting and copying relevant documents, agreements, accounting books, business correspondence, electronic data, and other documents and materials of the business operator under investigation, interested parties, or other relevant units or individuals;

(4) Sealing or seizing relevant evidence;

(5) Inquiring into the bank accounts of the business operator.

The measures specified in the preceding paragraph shall be reported in writing to the principal person in charge of the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency and shall be subject to approval.

Article 40 When investigating suspected monopolistic conduct, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall have no fewer than two enforcement officers, who shall produce their enforcement credentials.

When conducting questioning and investigations, enforcement officers shall make records, which shall be signed by the person being questioned or investigated.

Article 41 The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency and its staff members shall be obligated to keep confidential any trade secrets learned during the enforcement process.

Article 42 The business operator under investigation, interested parties, or other relevant units or individuals shall cooperate with the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency in the lawful performance of its duties, and shall not refuse or obstruct the investigation by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency.

Article 43 The business operator under investigation and interested parties have the right to state their opinions. The Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall verify the facts, reasons, and evidence presented by the business operator under investigation and interested parties.

Article 44 After investigating and verifying suspected monopolistic conduct, if the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency determines that it constitutes monopolistic conduct, it shall make a decision on handling the matter in accordance with the law and may make it public.

Article 45 With respect to suspected monopolistic conduct under investigation by the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency, if the business operator under investigation commits to taking specific measures to eliminate the consequences of the conduct within a period acceptable to the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency may decide to suspend the investigation. The decision to suspend the investigation shall specify the specific content of the commitment made by the business operator under investigation.

If the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency decides to suspend the investigation, it shall supervise the performance of the commitment by the business operator. If the business operator fulfills its commitment, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency may decide to terminate the investigation.

Under any of the following circumstances, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall resume the investigation:

(1) The business operator fails to fulfill its commitment;

(2) A significant change occurs in the facts on which the decision to suspend the investigation was based;

(3) The decision to suspend the investigation was made based on incomplete or untrue information provided by the business operator.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 46 If a business operator, in violation of the provisions of this Law, reaches and implements a monopoly agreement, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall order it to cease the illegal act, confiscate its illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one percent but not more than ten percent of its sales revenue for the preceding year; if the monopoly agreement reached has not been implemented, a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan may be imposed.

If a business operator voluntarily reports to the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency the relevant circumstances of reaching a monopoly agreement and provides important evidence, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency may, at its discretion, mitigate or exempt the penalty on that business operator.

If an industry association, in violation of the provisions of this Law, organizes business operators in its industry to reach a monopoly agreement, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency may impose a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, the social organization registration authority may revoke its registration in accordance with the law.

Article 47 If a business operator, in violation of the provisions of this Law, abuses its dominant market position, the Anti-Monopoly Enforcement Agency shall order it to cease the illegal act, confiscate its illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one percent but not more than ten percent of its sales revenue for the preceding year.Article 48 Where an operator implements a concentration in violation of the provisions of this Law, the anti-monopoly enforcement authority under the State Council shall order it to cease implementing the concentration, dispose of shares or assets within a specified time limit, transfer its business within a specified time limit, and take other necessary measures to restore the situation prior to the concentration, and may impose a fine of not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 49 In determining the specific amount of the fine provided for in Articles 46, 47, and 48 of this Law, the anti-monopoly enforcement authority shall consider factors such as the nature, severity, and duration of the illegal act.

Article 50 Where an operator causes losses to others by engaging in monopolistic conduct, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law.

Article 51 Where an administrative organ or an organization authorized by laws or regulations to manage public affairs abuses administrative power to eliminate or restrict competition, the superior authority shall order it to make corrections; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law. The anti-monopoly enforcement authority may submit a proposal to the relevant superior authority for handling in accordance with the law.

Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for the handling of acts by administrative organs or organizations authorized by laws or regulations to manage public affairs that abuse administrative power to eliminate or restrict competition, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 52 Where a party refuses to provide relevant materials or information, provides false materials or information, conceals, destroys, or transfers evidence, or engages in other acts of refusing or obstructing an investigation lawfully conducted by the anti-monopoly enforcement authority, the anti-monopoly enforcement authority shall order it to make corrections; for an individual, a fine of not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed; for a unit, a fine of not more than 200,000 yuan may be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, for an individual, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed; for a unit, a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Where a party is dissatisfied with a decision made by the anti-monopoly enforcement authority pursuant to Articles 28 and 29 of this Law, it may first apply for administrative reconsideration in accordance with the law; if it is dissatisfied with the decision on administrative reconsideration, it may initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

Where a party is dissatisfied with a decision made by the anti-monopoly enforcement authority other than those provided for in the preceding paragraph, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or initiate an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Where a staff member of the anti-monopoly enforcement authority abuses power, neglects duties, engages in favoritism and malpractice, or divulges trade secrets learned during the enforcement process, if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if a crime is not constituted, sanctions shall be given in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 8 Supplementary Provisions

Article 55 This Law shall not apply to the exercise of intellectual property rights by operators in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on intellectual property; however, this Law shall apply to acts by operators that abuse intellectual property rights to eliminate or restrict competition.

Article 56 This Law shall not apply to joint or concerted actions by agricultural producers and rural economic organizations in business activities such as the production, processing, sale, transportation, and storage of agricultural products.

Article 57 This Law shall come into force on August 1, 2008.
