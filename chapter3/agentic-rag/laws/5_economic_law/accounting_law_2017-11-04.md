# Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Ninth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on January 21, 1985

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China at the Fifth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 29, 1993

Revised at the Twelfth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 1999

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws including the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China at the Thirtieth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to regulate accounting practices, ensure the authenticity and completeness of accounting records, strengthen economic and financial management, improve economic efficiency, and maintain the order of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 State organs, social organizations, companies, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations (hereinafter collectively referred to as "units") must handle accounting affairs in accordance with this Law.

Article 3 Each unit must establish accounting books in accordance with the law and ensure their authenticity and completeness.

Article 4 The person in charge of a unit shall be responsible for the authenticity and completeness of the unit's accounting work and accounting records.

Article 5 Accounting institutions and accounting personnel shall conduct accounting and exercise accounting supervision in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

No unit or individual may, in any manner, instruct, direct, or compel an accounting institution or accounting personnel to forge or alter accounting vouchers, accounting books, or other accounting records, or to provide false financial and accounting reports.

No unit or individual may retaliate against accounting personnel who lawfully perform their duties and resist acts that violate the provisions of this Law.

Article 6 Accounting personnel who conscientiously implement this Law, are devoted to their duties, adhere to principles, and achieve outstanding results shall be given spiritual or material rewards.

Article 7 The finance department of the State Council shall be in charge of the national accounting work.

The finance departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall manage the accounting work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 8 The State shall implement a unified accounting system. The unified national accounting system shall be formulated and published by the finance department of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Relevant departments of the State Council may, in accordance with this Law and the unified national accounting system, formulate specific measures or supplementary provisions for implementing the unified national accounting system in industries with special requirements for accounting and accounting supervision, and submit them to the finance department of the State Council for review and approval.

The General Logistics Department of the People's Liberation Army may, in accordance with this Law and the unified national accounting system, formulate specific measures for the military to implement the unified national accounting system, and report them to the finance department of the State Council for record.

## Chapter II Accounting

Article 9 Each unit must conduct accounting based on actual economic transactions, fill in accounting vouchers, register accounting books, and prepare financial and accounting reports.

No unit may conduct accounting using false economic transactions or data.

Article 10 The following economic transactions shall be subject to accounting procedures and accounting:

(1) Receipt and payment of cash and securities;
(2) Receipt, dispatch, increase, decrease, and use of property;
(3) Occurrence and settlement of claims and debts;
(4) Increase and decrease of capital and funds;
(5) Calculation of income, expenditure, expenses, and costs;
(6) Calculation and processing of financial results;
(7) Other matters requiring accounting procedures and accounting.

Article 11 The fiscal year shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31 of the Gregorian calendar.

Article 12 Accounting shall be conducted with Renminbi as the functional currency.

For units whose business receipts and expenditures are primarily in currencies other than Renminbi, one such currency may be selected as the functional currency; however, the financial and accounting reports prepared shall be converted into Renminbi.

Article 13 Accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, and other accounting records must comply with the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

For units using electronic computers for accounting, the software and the accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, and other accounting records generated thereby must also comply with the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

No unit or individual may forge or alter accounting vouchers, accounting books, or other accounting records, or provide false financial and accounting reports.

Article 14 Accounting vouchers include original vouchers and recording vouchers.

For the economic transactions listed in Article 10 of this Law, original vouchers must be filled in or obtained and promptly submitted to the accounting institution.

Accounting institutions and accounting personnel must review original vouchers in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system. They have the right not to accept original vouchers that are untrue or illegal, and shall report to the person in charge of the unit; original vouchers with inaccurate or incomplete records shall be returned, and corrections or supplements shall be required in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

The contents recorded in original vouchers shall not be altered; if there are errors in an original voucher, it shall be reissued or corrected by the issuing unit, and the correction shall be stamped with the seal of the issuing unit. If there is an error in the amount of an original voucher, it shall be reissued by the issuing unit, and no correction may be made on the original voucher.

Recording vouchers shall be prepared based on reviewed original vouchers and relevant materials.

Article 15 Registration of accounting books must be based on reviewed accounting vouchers and comply with the provisions of relevant laws, administrative regulations, and the unified national accounting system. Accounting books include general ledgers, subsidiary ledgers, journals, and other auxiliary books.

Accounting books shall be registered in the order of consecutively numbered pages. If errors occur in the records of accounting books, or if there are missing pages, missing numbers, or skipped lines, corrections shall be made in accordance with the methods prescribed by the unified national accounting system, and the accounting personnel and the person in charge of the accounting institution (chief accounting officer) shall affix their seals at the correction.

For units using electronic computers for accounting, the registration and correction of accounting books shall comply with the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

Article 16 All economic transactions occurring in each unit shall be uniformly registered and accounted for in the accounting books established in accordance with the law. No unit may establish private accounting books for registration and accounting in violation of the provisions of this Law and the unified national accounting system.

Article 17 Each unit shall periodically reconcile the records in its accounting books with the physical assets, funds, and relevant materials to ensure that the accounting book records match the actual amounts of physical assets and funds, that the accounting book records correspond with the relevant contents of accounting vouchers, that corresponding records between accounting books are consistent, and that the accounting book records correspond with the relevant contents of accounting statements.

Article 18 The accounting methods adopted by each unit shall be consistent from one period to the next and shall not be changed arbitrarily; if a change is truly necessary, it shall be made in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system, and the reasons for the change, the circumstances, and the impact shall be explained in the financial and accounting report.

Article 19 Contingent matters such as guarantees provided by the unit and pending litigation shall be explained in the financial and accounting report in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

Article 20 Financial and accounting reports shall be prepared based on reviewed accounting book records and relevant materials, and shall comply with the provisions of this Law and the unified national accounting system regarding the preparation requirements, provision targets, and provision deadlines for financial and accounting reports; if other laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise, those provisions shall prevail.

Financial and accounting reports consist of accounting statements, notes to accounting statements, and financial condition explanations. The preparation basis for financial and accounting reports provided to different users of accounting records shall be consistent. If relevant laws and administrative regulations require that accounting statements, notes to accounting statements, and financial condition explanations be audited by certified public accountants, the audit report issued by the certified public accountant and their accounting firm shall be provided together with the financial and accounting report.

Article 21 Financial and accounting reports shall be signed and sealed by the person in charge of the unit, the person in charge of accounting work, and the person in charge of the accounting institution (chief accounting officer); for units with a chief accountant, the chief accountant shall also sign and seal.

The person in charge of the unit shall ensure the authenticity and completeness of the financial and accounting report.

Article 22 Accounting records shall be written in Chinese. In ethnic autonomous areas, accounting records may also be written in a commonly used local ethnic language. For foreign-invested enterprises, foreign enterprises, and other foreign organizations within the territory of the People's Republic of China, accounting records may also be written in a foreign language.

Article 23 Each unit shall establish archives for accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, and other accounting records, and keep them properly. The retention periods and destruction methods for accounting archives shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

## Chapter III Special Provisions on Accounting of Companies and Enterprises

Article 24 Companies and enterprises conducting accounting shall comply with the provisions of Chapter II of this Law and also with the provisions of this Chapter.

Article 25 Companies and enterprises must recognize, measure, and record assets, liabilities, owners' equity, income, expenses, costs, and profits in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system, based on actual economic transactions.

Article 26 Companies and enterprises shall not engage in the following acts when conducting accounting:

(1) Arbitrarily changing the recognition criteria or measurement methods for assets, liabilities, or owners' equity, or falsely listing, over-listing, under-listing, or omitting assets, liabilities, or owners' equity;
(2) Falsely listing or concealing income, or delaying or accelerating the recognition of income;
(3) Arbitrarily changing the recognition criteria or measurement methods for expenses or costs, or falsely listing, over-listing, under-listing, or omitting expenses or costs;
(4) Arbitrarily adjusting the calculation or distribution methods for profits, fabricating false profits, or concealing profits;
(5) Other acts that violate the provisions of the unified national accounting system.

## Chapter IV Accounting Supervision

Article 27 Each unit shall establish and improve its internal accounting supervision system. The internal accounting supervision system of a unit shall meet the following requirements:

(1) The duties and authority of accounting personnel, personnel approving economic transactions and accounting matters, personnel handling such matters, and personnel in charge of property custody shall be clearly defined, and they shall be mutually separate and mutually restrictive;
(2) The procedures for mutual supervision and mutual restriction in the decision-making and execution of major external investments, asset disposals, fund allocations, and other important economic transactions shall be clearly defined;
(3) The scope, period, and organizational procedures for property inspections shall be clearly defined;
(4) The methods and procedures for regular internal audits of accounting records shall be clearly defined.

Article 28 The person in charge of a unit shall ensure that the accounting institution and accounting personnel perform their duties in accordance with the law, and shall not instruct, direct, or compel the accounting institution or accounting personnel to handle accounting matters illegally.

Accounting institutions and accounting personnel have the right to refuse to handle accounting matters that violate the provisions of this Law and the unified national accounting system, or to correct them within their authority.

Article 29 If accounting institutions or accounting personnel discover that the records in accounting books do not match the physical assets, funds, or relevant materials, and if they have the authority to handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of the unified national accounting system, they shall handle it promptly; if they do not have the authority, they shall immediately report to the person in charge of the unit, requesting an investigation into the cause and appropriate action.

Article 30 Any unit or individual has the right to report acts that violate the provisions of this Law and the unified national accounting system. The department receiving the report shall, if it has the authority to handle the matter, handle it promptly in accordance with the law and its responsibilities; if it does not have the authority, it shall promptly transfer the matter to the department with authority. The department receiving the report and the department responsible for handling the matter shall keep the reporter confidential and shall not transfer the reporter's name or the reporting materials to the reported unit or individual.

Article 31 Units that are required by relevant laws and administrative regulations to be audited by certified public accountants shall truthfully provide the entrusted accounting firm with accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, other accounting records, and relevant information.

No unit or individual may, in any manner, request or suggest that a certified public accountant or their accounting firm issue an untrue or inappropriate audit report.

The finance department has the authority to supervise the procedures and content of audit reports issued by accounting firms.

Article 32 The finance department shall supervise the following matters in each unit:

(1) Whether accounting books are established in accordance with the law;
(2) Whether accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, and other accounting records are authentic and complete;
(3) Whether accounting complies with the provisions of this Law and the unified national accounting system;
(4) Whether personnel engaged in accounting work possess the necessary professional competence and abide by professional ethics.

When conducting supervision over the matters listed in item (2) of the preceding paragraph and discovering significant suspected violations, the finance department of the State Council and its dispatched agencies may inquire about relevant information from units that have economic transactions with the unit under supervision and from financial institutions where the unit under supervision has opened accounts; the relevant units and financial institutions shall provide support.

Article 33 Departments such as finance, audit, taxation, the People's Bank, securities regulatory authorities, and insurance regulatory authorities shall, in accordance with the responsibilities prescribed by relevant laws and administrative regulations, conduct supervision and inspection of the accounting records of relevant units.

After conducting supervision and inspection of the accounting records of relevant units in accordance with the law, the departments listed in the preceding paragraph shall issue inspection conclusions. If the inspection conclusions already made by a relevant supervision and inspection department can meet the needs of other supervision and inspection departments in performing their duties, the other departments shall utilize them to avoid duplicate inspections.

Article 34 Departments and their personnel that conduct supervision and inspection of the accounting records of relevant units in accordance with the law shall have the obligation to keep confidential the state secrets and commercial secrets learned during the supervision and inspection.

Article 35 Each unit must, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, accept supervision and inspection conducted by relevant supervision and inspection departments in accordance with the law, and truthfully provide accounting vouchers, accounting books, financial and accounting reports, other accounting records, and relevant information, and shall not refuse, conceal, or make false reports.

## Chapter V Accounting Institutions and Accounting Personnel

Article 36 Each unit shall, based on the needs of its accounting work, establish an accounting institution, or set up accounting personnel in a relevant institution and designate a chief accounting officer; if conditions for establishment are not met, the unit shall entrust an intermediary institution approved to engage in agency bookkeeping services.

Large and medium-sized state-owned enterprises and enterprises where state-owned assets hold a controlling or dominant position must set up a chief accountant. The qualifications, appointment and removal procedures, and duties and authority of the chief accountant shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 37 An internal audit system shall be established within the accounting institution.

Cashiers shall not concurrently hold positions in auditing, accounting archive custody, or the registration of income, expenditure, expenses, claims, and debt accounts.

Article 38 Accounting personnel shall possess the professional competence required for accounting work.

A person serving as the person in charge of a unit's accounting institution (chief accounting officer) shall have the professional technical qualification of accountant or above, or have more than three years of experience in accounting work.

The scope of accounting personnel referred to in this Law shall be prescribed by the finance department of the State Council.

Article 39 Accounting personnel shall abide by professional ethics and improve their professional quality. Education and training for accounting personnel shall be strengthened.

Article 40 Persons who have been subject to criminal liability for illegal acts related to accounting duties, such as providing false financial and accounting reports, making false accounts, concealing or intentionally destroying accounting vouchers, accounting books, or financial and accounting reports, embezzlement, misappropriation of public funds, or job-related appropriation, shall no longer be engaged in accounting work.

Article 41 When accounting personnel are transferred or leave their posts, they must complete the handover procedures with the incoming personnel.

When general accounting personnel handle handover procedures, the person in charge of the accounting institution (chief accounting officer) shall supervise the handover; when the person in charge of the accounting institution (chief accounting officer) handles handover procedures, the person in charge of the unit shall supervise the handover, and if necessary, the competent unit may send personnel to participate in the supervision.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 42 For any of the following acts in violation of the provisions of this Law, the finance department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction within a specified period, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the unit; for the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan may be imposed; if the person is a state functionary, the unit to which they belong or the relevant unit shall also impose administrative sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to establish accounting books in accordance with the law;
(2) Establishing private accounting books;
(3) Failing to fill in or obtain original vouchers in accordance with regulations, or the original vouchers filled in or obtained do not comply with regulations;
(4) Registering accounting books based on unreviewed accounting vouchers, or registering accounting books in a manner that does not comply with regulations;
(5) Arbitrarily changing accounting methods;
(6) Inconsistency in the preparation basis of financial and accounting reports provided to different users of accounting records;
(7) Failing to use the prescribed language for accounting records or functional currency;
(8) Failing to keep accounting records in accordance with regulations, resulting in damage or loss of accounting records;
(9) Failing to establish and implement an internal accounting supervision system in accordance with regulations, or refusing supervision implemented in accordance with the law, or failing to truthfully provide relevant accounting records and information;
(10) Employing accounting personnel who do not meet the requirements of this Law.

If any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

If an accounting personnel commits any of the acts listed in the first paragraph, and the circumstances are serious, they shall not be engaged in accounting work for five years.

If other laws provide for penalties for the acts listed in the first paragraph, those laws shall apply.

Article 43 Forging or altering accounting vouchers or accounting books, or preparing false financial and accounting reports, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

If the act in the preceding paragraph does not constitute a crime, the finance department of the people's government at or above the county level shall issue a notice, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the unit; for the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed; if the person is a state functionary, the unit to which they belong or the relevant unit shall also impose administrative sanctions ranging from removal from office to dismissal in accordance with the law; if the person is an accounting personnel, they shall not be engaged in accounting work for five years.

Article 44 Concealing or intentionally destroying accounting vouchers, accounting books, or financial and accounting reports that should be preserved in accordance with the law, if constituting a crime, shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

If the act in the preceding paragraph does not constitute a crime, the finance department of the people's government at or above the county level shall issue a notice, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan on the unit; for the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons, a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed; if the person is a state functionary, the unit to which they belong or the relevant unit shall also impose administrative sanctions ranging from removal from office to dismissal in accordance with the law; if the person is an accounting personnel, they shall not be engaged in accounting work for five years.Article 45 Any person who instigates, directs, or compels an accounting institution, accounting personnel, or other persons to forge or alter accounting vouchers or account books, prepare false financial and accounting reports, or conceal or intentionally destroy accounting vouchers, account books, or financial and accounting reports that should be preserved according to law, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed; if the person is a state functionary, the entity to which he belongs or the relevant entity shall also impose administrative sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or expulsion according to law.

Article 46 Where the person in charge of an entity retaliates against accounting personnel who perform their duties according to law and resist acts violating the provisions of this Law by means such as demotion, removal from office, transfer from their posts, dismissal, or expulsion, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed by the entity to which he belongs or the relevant entity according to law. The reputation and original positions and ranks of the retaliated accounting personnel shall be restored.

Article 47 Where functionaries of the financial departments and relevant administrative departments abuse their powers, neglect their duties, engage in malpractices for personal gain, or disclose state secrets or commercial secrets in the course of exercising supervision and management, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law; if the act does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed according to law.

Article 48 Any person who, in violation of the provisions of Article 30 of this Law, transfers the name of a whistleblower and the materials of the report to the reported entity or individual shall be subject to administrative sanctions imposed by the entity to which he belongs or the relevant entity according to law.

Article 49 Any person who violates the provisions of this Law and also violates other legal provisions shall be punished by the relevant departments within their respective functions and powers according to law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 50 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law:

The person in charge of an entity refers to the legal representative of the entity or the principal person responsible for exercising the functions and powers of the entity as stipulated by laws or administrative regulations.

The unified national accounting system refers to the system concerning accounting calculation, accounting supervision, accounting institutions and accounting personnel, and the management of accounting work, formulated by the financial department of the State Council in accordance with this Law.

Article 51 Specific measures for the accounting management of individual industrial and commercial households shall be separately formulated by the financial department of the State Council in accordance with the principles of this Law.

Article 52 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 2000.
