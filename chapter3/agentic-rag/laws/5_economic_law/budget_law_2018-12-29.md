# Budget Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 22, 1994

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Budget Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws including the Product Quality Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Seventh Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to regulate government revenue and expenditure, strengthen budgetary constraints, enhance budget management and oversight, establish a comprehensive, standardized, open, and transparent budget system, and ensure the healthy development of the economy and society.

Article 2 The preparation, review, approval, and oversight of budgets and final accounts, as well as the execution and adjustment of budgets, shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 3 The state implements a system of one budget for each level of government, establishing five levels of budgets: central; provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government; cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures; counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts; and townships, ethnic townships, and towns.

The national budget consists of the central budget and local budgets. Local budgets consist of the general budgets of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The general budget of a local level of government consists of the budget of that level and the aggregated general budgets of the next lower level; if the next lower level has only its own budget, the general budget of the next lower level refers to its own budget. If there is no budget at the next lower level, the general budget refers to the budget of that level itself.

Article 4 A budget consists of budgetary revenues and budgetary expenditures.

All revenues and expenditures of the government shall be included in the budget.

Article 5 Budgets include general public budgets, government fund budgets, state capital operation budgets, and social insurance fund budgets.

General public budgets, government fund budgets, state capital operation budgets, and social insurance fund budgets shall remain complete and independent. Government fund budgets, state capital operation budgets, and social insurance fund budgets shall be coordinated with general public budgets.

Article 6 A general public budget is a budget of revenues and expenditures for fiscal revenues with taxes as the main body, arranged to ensure and improve people's livelihoods, promote economic and social development, maintain national security, and ensure the normal operation of state institutions.

The central general public budget includes the budgets of all central departments (including their directly affiliated units, the same below) and the central budget for tax rebates and transfer payments to local governments.

Central general public budget revenues include central-level revenues and revenues turned over to the central government by local governments. Central general public budget expenditures include central-level expenditures, tax rebates, and transfer payments from the central government to local governments.

Article 7 Local general public budgets at various levels include the budgets of all departments at that level (including their directly affiliated units, the same below) and budgets for tax rebates and transfer payments.

Local general public budget revenues at various levels include local-level revenues, tax rebates and transfer payments from the government at the next higher level to the government at that level, and revenues turned over by governments at the next lower level. Local general public budget expenditures at various levels include local-level expenditures, expenditures turned over to the government at the next higher level, and tax rebates and transfer payments to governments at the next lower level.

Article 8 The budget of a department consists of the budgets of that department and all units subordinate to it.

Article 9 A government fund budget is a budget of revenues and expenditures for funds levied, collected, or raised by other means from specific targets within a certain period in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, which are used exclusively for the development of specific public undertakings.

Government fund budgets shall be prepared on a fund-by-fund basis based on the revenue situation of the fund items and actual expenditure needs, ensuring that expenditures are determined by revenues.

Article 10 A state capital operation budget is a budget of revenues and expenditures that arranges expenditures for the income from state capital.

State capital operation budgets shall be prepared in accordance with the principle of balancing revenues and expenditures, shall not include a deficit, and shall arrange for funds to be transferred to the general public budget.

Article 11 A social insurance fund budget is a budget of revenues and expenditures for funds raised through social insurance contributions, allocations from the general public budget, and other means, which are used exclusively for social insurance.

Social insurance fund budgets shall be prepared separately according to the pooling level and social insurance item, ensuring a balance between revenues and expenditures.

Article 12 Budgets at all levels shall adhere to the principles of overall consideration, diligence and thrift, acting within their means, emphasizing performance, and balancing revenues and expenditures.

Governments at all levels shall establish a mechanism for balancing budgets across fiscal years.

Article 13 A budget approved by the People's Congress shall not be adjusted without going through legal procedures. The expenditures of governments at all levels, all departments, and all units must be based on the approved budget; expenditures not included in the budget shall not be made.

Article 14 The reports and statements on the budget, budget adjustments, final accounts, and budget execution approved by the People's Congress at the corresponding level or its Standing Committee shall be made public by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level within twenty days after approval, along with explanations of important matters such as the arrangement and execution of fiscal transfer payments by the government at that level and the borrowing of debt.

The departmental budgets, final accounts, and statements approved by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level shall be made public by each department within twenty days after approval, along with explanations of important matters such as the arrangement and use of funds for the operation of government agencies in the departmental budgets and final accounts.

Governments at all levels, all departments, and all units shall promptly make public the status of government procurement.

The matters required to be made public as stipulated in the preceding three paragraphs of this Article shall not include those involving state secrets.

Article 15 The state implements a system of tax sharing between the central and local governments.

Article 16 The state implements a system of fiscal transfer payments. Fiscal transfer payments shall be standardized, fair, and open, with the main objective of promoting the equalization of basic public services across regions.

Fiscal transfer payments include transfer payments from the central government to local governments and from higher-level local governments to lower-level local governments, with general transfer payments as the main component, which are used to balance the basic financial resources of regions and are arranged and used comprehensively by lower-level governments.

Special transfer payments may be established in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and regulations of the State Council for handling specific matters. A mechanism for the regular evaluation and withdrawal of special transfer payments shall be established and improved. Special transfer payments shall not be established for matters that can be effectively regulated by market competition mechanisms.

When arranging special transfer payments, higher-level governments shall not require lower-level governments to provide matching funds, except for matters that should be jointly borne by higher and lower-level governments in accordance with regulations of the State Council.

Article 17 The preparation and execution of budgets at all levels shall establish and improve mechanisms of mutual checks and balances and mutual coordination.

Article 18 The fiscal year begins on January 1 and ends on December 31 of the Gregorian calendar.

Article 19 Budgetary revenues and expenditures shall be calculated in Renminbi yuan.

## Chapter II Powers and Responsibilities for Budget Management

Article 20 The National People's Congress examines the draft central and local budgets and the reports on the execution of the central and local budgets; approves the central budget and the report on the execution of the central budget; and alters or annuls inappropriate resolutions of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress concerning budgets and final accounts.

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress supervises the execution of the central and local budgets; examines and approves the adjustment plan for the central budget; examines and approves the central final accounts; annuls administrative regulations, decisions, and orders formulated by the State Council concerning budgets and final accounts that contravene the Constitution or laws; and annuls local regulations and resolutions of the People's Congresses and their Standing Committees of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government concerning budgets and final accounts that contravene the Constitution, laws, or administrative regulations.

Article 21 The People's Congresses at or above the county level examine the draft general budgets at the corresponding level and the reports on the execution of the general budgets at the corresponding level; approve the budgets at the corresponding level and the reports on the execution of the budgets at the corresponding level; alter or annul inappropriate resolutions of the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses at the corresponding level concerning budgets and final accounts; and annul inappropriate decisions and orders of the governments at the corresponding level concerning budgets and final accounts.

The Standing Committees of the People's Congresses at or above the county level supervise the execution of the general budgets at the corresponding level; examine and approve the adjustment plans for the budgets at the corresponding level; examine and approve the final accounts at the corresponding level; and annul inappropriate decisions, orders, and resolutions of the governments at the corresponding level and the People's Congresses and their Standing Committees at the next lower level concerning budgets and final accounts.

The People's Congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns examine and approve the budgets at the corresponding level and the reports on the execution of the budgets at the corresponding level; supervise the execution of the budgets at the corresponding level; examine and approve the adjustment plans for the budgets at the corresponding level; examine and approve the final accounts at the corresponding level; and annul inappropriate decisions and orders of the governments at the corresponding level concerning budgets and final accounts.

Article 22 The Financial and Economic Committee of the National People's Congress conducts preliminary examination of the preliminary draft central budget and the execution of the previous year's budget, the preliminary central budget adjustment plan, and the draft central final accounts, and provides preliminary examination opinions.

The relevant special committees of the People's Congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government conduct preliminary examination of the preliminary draft budgets at the corresponding level and the execution of the previous year's budget, the preliminary budget adjustment plans at the corresponding level, and the draft final accounts at the corresponding level, and provide preliminary examination opinions.

The relevant special committees of the People's Congresses of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures conduct preliminary examination of the preliminary draft budgets at the corresponding level and the execution of the previous year's budget, the preliminary budget adjustment plans at the corresponding level, and the draft final accounts at the corresponding level, and provide preliminary examination opinions. If no special committee is established, the relevant working body of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level shall study and provide opinions.

The Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts conduct preliminary examination of the preliminary draft budgets at the corresponding level and the execution of the previous year's budget, and provide preliminary examination opinions. The relevant working bodies of the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall study and provide opinions on the preliminary budget adjustment plans at the corresponding level and the draft final accounts at the corresponding level.

When the relevant special committees of the People's Congresses at or above the level of cities divided into districts conduct preliminary examination, and the relevant working bodies of the Standing Committees study and provide opinions, deputies to the People's Congress at the corresponding level shall be invited to participate.

Regarding the opinions provided in accordance with paragraphs one to four of this Article, the finance department of the government at the corresponding level shall promptly provide feedback on the handling status.

The opinions provided in accordance with paragraphs one to four of this Article and the reports on the handling status provided by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level shall be distributed to the deputies of the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

The relevant working bodies of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and the Standing Committees of the People's Congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures shall, in accordance with the decisions of the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level, assist the Financial and Economic Committee or the relevant special committees of the People's Congress at the corresponding level in undertaking specific tasks such as examining draft budgets, budget adjustment plans, draft final accounts, and supervising budget execution.

Article 23 The State Council prepares the draft central budget and final accounts; submits a report on the draft central and local budgets to the National People's Congress; aggregates the budgets submitted for record by the governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and reports them to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record; organizes the execution of the central and local budgets; decides on the use of the central budget reserve; prepares the central budget adjustment plan; supervises the budget execution of central departments and local governments; alters or annuls inappropriate decisions and orders of central departments and local governments concerning budgets and final accounts; and reports on the execution of the central and local budgets to the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee.

Article 24 Local governments at or above the county level prepare the draft budgets and final accounts at the corresponding level; submit a report on the draft general budget at the corresponding level to the People's Congress at the corresponding level; aggregate the budgets submitted for record by the governments at the next lower level and report them to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for record; organize the execution of the general budget at the corresponding level; decide on the use of the budget reserve at the corresponding level; prepare the budget adjustment plan at the corresponding level; supervise the budget execution of departments at the corresponding level and governments at the next lower level; alter or annul inappropriate decisions and orders of departments at the corresponding level and governments at the next lower level concerning budgets and final accounts; and report on the execution of the general budget at the corresponding level to the People's Congress at the corresponding level and its Standing Committee.

The governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns prepare the draft budgets and final accounts at the corresponding level; submit a report on the draft budget at the corresponding level to the People's Congress at the corresponding level; organize the execution of the budget at the corresponding level; decide on the use of the budget reserve at the corresponding level; prepare the budget adjustment plan at the corresponding level; and report on the execution of the budget at the corresponding level to the People's Congress at the corresponding level.

With the approval of the government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, the draft budget, budget adjustment plan, and draft final accounts of a township, ethnic township, or town at the corresponding level may be prepared on its behalf by the government at the next higher level, and shall be submitted to the People's Congress of the township, ethnic township, or town for examination and approval in accordance with Article 21 of this Law.

Article 25 The finance department of the State Council specifically prepares the draft central budget and final accounts; specifically organizes the execution of the central and local budgets; proposes a plan for the use of the central budget reserve; specifically prepares the central budget adjustment plan; and regularly reports on the execution of the central and local budgets to the State Council.

The finance departments of local governments at all levels specifically prepare the draft budgets and final accounts at the corresponding level; specifically organize the execution of the general budget at the corresponding level; propose a plan for the use of the budget reserve at the corresponding level; specifically prepare the budget adjustment plan at the corresponding level; and regularly report on the execution of the general budget at the corresponding level to the government at the corresponding level and the finance department of the government at the next higher level.

Article 26 Each department prepares the draft budget and final accounts for that department; organizes and supervises the execution of the budget of that department; and regularly reports on the execution of the budget to the finance department of the government at the corresponding level.

Each unit prepares the draft budget and final accounts for that unit; turns over budgetary revenues to the state in accordance with state regulations; arranges budgetary expenditures; and accepts supervision by relevant state departments.

## Chapter III Scope of Budgetary Revenues and Expenditures

Article 27 General public budget revenues include various tax revenues, administrative and institutional fees, income from the compensated use of state-owned resources (assets), transfer income, and other income.

General public budget expenditures, classified by function, include expenditures for general public services, diplomacy, public security, national defense, agriculture, environmental protection, education, science and technology, culture, health, sports, social security and employment, and other expenditures.

General public budget expenditures, classified by economic nature, include expenditures for wages and benefits, goods and services, capital expenditures, and other expenditures.

Article 28 The scope of revenues and expenditures for government fund budgets, state capital operation budgets, and social insurance fund budgets shall be implemented in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and regulations of the State Council.

Article 29 The specific methods for dividing revenue and expenditure items between the central budget and local budgets, the turning over of revenues from local governments to the central government, and tax rebates or transfer payments from the central government to local governments shall be prescribed by the State Council and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

Article 30 A higher-level government shall not transfer funds from the budget of a lower-level government outside the budget. A lower-level government shall not misappropriate or intercept funds belonging to the budget of a higher-level government.

## Chapter IV Budget Preparation

Article 31 The State Council shall promptly issue a notice on the preparation of the draft budget for the next year. The specific matters for preparing the draft budget shall be arranged by the finance department of the State Council.

Governments at all levels, all departments, and all units shall prepare draft budgets within the time limits prescribed by the State Council.

Article 32 Budgets at all levels shall be prepared based on the annual economic and social development goals, the overall requirements of national macroeconomic regulation, and the needs for balancing budgets across fiscal years, with reference to the execution of the previous year's budget, the results of performance evaluations of relevant expenditures, and the forecast of revenues and expenditures for the current year, and after soliciting opinions from all sides in accordance with prescribed procedures.

If a government at any level makes a decision or formulates an administrative measure within its statutory authority that involves increasing or decreasing fiscal revenues or expenditures, it shall propose such changes before the budget is approved and make corresponding arrangements in the draft budget.

All departments and units shall prepare their draft budgets in accordance with the government revenue and expenditure classification subjects, budget expenditure standards and requirements, and budget preparation regulations such as performance target management formulated by the finance department of the State Council, based on their needs for performing their functions according to law and developing their undertakings, as well as the status of their existing assets.

The government revenue and expenditure classification subjects referred to in the preceding paragraph classify revenues into classes, divisions, sections, and items; classify expenditures by function into classes, divisions, and sections; and classify expenditures by economic nature into classes and divisions.

Article 33 The governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, within the time limits prescribed by the State Council, submit their draft general budgets to the State Council for review and aggregation.

Article 34 Necessary funds in the central general public budget may be raised by borrowing domestic and foreign debts, and the scale of borrowing shall be appropriately controlled, maintaining a reasonable structure.

The debts borrowed in the central general public budget shall be managed on a balance basis, and the scale of the balance shall not exceed the limit approved by the National People's Congress.

The finance department of the State Council is specifically responsible for the unified management of central government debt.

Article 35 Local budgets at all levels shall be prepared in accordance with the principle of basing expenditures on revenues and balancing revenues and expenditures, and shall not include a deficit unless otherwise provided by this Law.

Necessary construction investment funds in the budgets of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government approved by the State Council may be raised by borrowing debts through the issuance of local government bonds within the limits determined by the State Council. The scale of borrowing shall be reported by the State Council to the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee for approval. The debts borrowed by provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government within the limits issued by the State Council shall be included in the budget adjustment plan at the corresponding level and submitted to the Standing Committee of the People's Congress at the corresponding level for approval. The borrowed debts shall have a repayment plan and stable repayment sources, and may only be used for public welfare capital expenditures, not for recurrent expenditures.

Except as provided in the preceding paragraph, local governments and their subordinate departments shall not borrow debts in any manner.

Unless otherwise provided by law, local governments and their subordinate departments shall not provide guarantees for the debts of any unit or individual in any manner.

The State Council shall establish a risk assessment and early warning mechanism, an emergency response mechanism, and an accountability system for local government debt. The finance department of the State Council shall supervise local government debt.

Article 36 The preparation of budgetary revenues at all levels shall be compatible with the level of economic and social development and coordinated with fiscal policies.

Governments at all levels, all departments, and all units shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, include all government revenues in the budget and shall not conceal or underreport them.

Article 37 Budgetary expenditures at all levels shall be prepared by classifying them according to their function and economic nature in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

The preparation of budgetary expenditures at all levels shall implement the principle of diligence and thrift, strictly controlling the operating expenses of government agencies and capital construction expenditures such as office buildings and reception halls at all departments and units.

The preparation of general public budget expenditures at all levels shall be based on overall consideration, prioritizing the arrangement of key expenditures determined by the state while ensuring the reasonable needs for basic public services.

Article 38 General transfer payments shall be prepared in accordance with the basic standards and calculation methods prescribed by the State Council. Special transfer payments shall be prepared by region and by project.Governments at or above the county level shall issue in advance the estimated amounts of transfer payments to lower-level governments to those governments.

Local governments at all levels shall incorporate the estimated amounts of transfer payments issued in advance by higher-level governments into their own budgets.

Article 39: Necessary funds shall be allocated in the central budget and relevant local budgets to support the economic and social development of old revolutionary base areas, ethnic minority areas, border areas, and poverty-stricken areas.

Article 40: A reserve fund shall be set up in each general public budget at the corresponding level, ranging from one to three percent of the total expenditure of that general public budget. This fund is used for increased expenditures arising from the handling of emergencies such as natural disasters during the budget year and other unforeseen expenditures.

Article 41: General public budgets at all levels may, in accordance with regulations of the State Council, set up a budget revolving fund to be used by the government at the corresponding level to adjust seasonal revenue and expenditure imbalances within the budget year.

General public budgets at all levels may, in accordance with regulations of the State Council, set up a budget stabilization and regulation fund to be used to cover shortfalls in budget funds in subsequent years.

Article 42: Carryover funds from the previous year's budget of a government at any level shall be used for expenditures of the carried-over projects in the following year; carryover funds that remain unused for two consecutive years shall be managed as surplus funds.

The carryover and surplus funds from the previous year's budget of all departments and units shall be handled in accordance with the regulations of the finance department of the State Council.

## Chapter 5: Budget Review and Approval

Article 43: The central budget shall be reviewed and approved by the National People's Congress.

Local budgets at all levels shall be reviewed and approved by the people's congresses at the corresponding levels.

Article 44: The finance department of the State Council shall, forty-five days before the annual session of the National People's Congress, submit the preliminary draft of the central budget to the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee of the National People's Congress for preliminary review.

The finance departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, thirty days before the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level, submit the preliminary draft of the budget at the corresponding level to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary review.

The finance departments of the people's governments of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures shall, thirty days before the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level, submit the preliminary draft of the budget at the corresponding level to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary review, or to the relevant working body of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for comments.

The people's governments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall, thirty days before the session of the people's congress at the corresponding level, submit the preliminary draft of the budget at the corresponding level to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary review.

Article 45: Before the people's congresses of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, municipal districts, townships, ethnic townships, and towns hold sessions to review the draft budget, they shall, using various forms, organize deputies to the people's congress at the corresponding level to solicit opinions from voters and all sectors of society.

Article 46: The draft budget submitted to the people's congresses at all levels for review and approval shall be detailed. Expenditures of the general public budget at the corresponding level shall be itemized by function; by economic nature, basic expenditures shall be itemized by category. Expenditures of the government-managed funds budget, the state capital management budget, and the social insurance funds budget at the corresponding level shall be itemized by function.

Article 47: When the National People's Congress is in session, the State Council shall report to the Congress on the draft central and local budgets and on the implementation of the central and local budgets.

When the people's congress at the corresponding level is in session, local governments at all levels shall report to the Congress on the draft total budget and the implementation of the total budget.

Article 48: The National People's Congress and local people's congresses at all levels shall focus their review of the draft budget, its report, and the report on budget implementation on the following:

(1) Whether the implementation of the previous year's budget complies with the requirements of the budget resolution of the people's congress at the corresponding level;
(2) Whether the budget arrangements comply with the provisions of this Law;
(3) Whether the budget arrangements implement the principles and policies for national economic and social development, and whether the revenue and expenditure policies are practical and feasible;
(4) Whether the budget arrangements for key expenditures and major investment projects are appropriate;
(5) Whether the budget compilation is complete and complies with the provisions of Article 46 of this Law;
(6) Whether the budget for transfer expenditures to lower-level governments is standardized and appropriate;
(7) Whether the debt arranged to be borrowed in the budget is legal and reasonable, and whether there is a repayment plan and stable sources of repayment funds;
(8) Whether the explanations of important matters related to the budget are clear.

Article 49: The Financial and Economic Affairs Committee of the National People's Congress shall submit to the Presidium of the National People's Congress a report on the results of the review of the draft central and local budgets and the implementation of the central and local budgets.

The relevant special committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures, and the standing committees of the people's congresses of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall submit to the presidium of the people's congress at the corresponding level a report on the results of the review of the draft total budget and the implementation of the total budget for the previous year.

The report on the results of the review shall include the following:

(1) An evaluation of the implementation of the previous year's budget and the implementation of the budget resolution of the people's congress at the corresponding level;
(2) An evaluation of whether the current year's draft budget complies with the provisions of this Law and is feasible;
(3) Recommendations to the people's congress at the corresponding level on approving the draft budget and the budget report;
(4) Opinions and suggestions on implementing the annual budget, improving budget management, enhancing budget performance, and strengthening budget oversight.

Article 50: The people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall promptly submit the budget approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level to the government at the next higher level for recordation. Local governments at or above the county level shall promptly consolidate the budget approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level and the budgets submitted for recordation by governments at the next lower level, and submit them to the government at the next higher level for recordation.

After consolidating the budgets submitted for recordation by governments at the next lower level in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, local governments at or above the county level shall submit them to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for recordation. After consolidating the budgets submitted for recordation by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the State Council shall submit them to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

Article 51: If the State Council or a local government at or above the county level considers that a budget submitted for recordation by a government at the next lower level in accordance with Article 50 of this Law conflicts with laws or administrative regulations or is otherwise inappropriate, and that the resolution approving the budget needs to be revoked, it shall submit the matter to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for deliberation and decision.

Article 52: After a budget at any level is approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level, the finance department of the government at that level shall, within twenty days, approve and issue the budget to all departments at that level. Each department shall, within fifteen days after receiving the budget approved and issued by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level, approve and issue the budget to all units under it.

General transfer payments from the central government to local governments shall be formally issued within thirty days after the National People's Congress approves the budget. Special transfer payments from the central government to local governments shall be formally issued within ninety days after the National People's Congress approves the budget.

After receiving the general transfer payments and special transfer payments from the central government, the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, within thirty days, formally issue them to governments at or above the county level within their administrative regions.

General transfer payments and special transfer payments arranged in the budgets of local governments at or above the county level for governments at the next lower level shall be formally issued within thirty days and sixty days, respectively, after the people's congress at the corresponding level approves the budget.

Transfer payments for handling emergencies such as natural disasters shall be issued promptly; transfer payments for special projects such as those settled based on actual figures may be issued in installments, or prepaid and then settled.

The finance departments of governments at or above the county level shall send copies of the budgets approved and issued to departments at the corresponding level and the budgets for transfer payments approved and issued to lower-level governments to the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee, relevant special committees, and relevant working bodies of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

## Chapter 6: Budget Implementation

Article 53: Budgets at all levels shall be implemented under the organization of the government at the corresponding level, with the specific work being the responsibility of the finance department of that government.

All departments and units are the main bodies responsible for implementing their own budgets, responsible for the budget implementation of their departments and units, and accountable for the results of implementation.

Article 54: After the start of the budget year, before the draft budget at the corresponding level is approved by the people's congress at that level, the following expenditures may be arranged:

(1) Expenditures carried over from the previous year;
(2) Basic departmental expenditures, project expenditures, and transfer expenditures to lower-level governments for the current year that must be paid, arranged by reference to the budget expenditure amounts for the same period of the previous year;
(3) Expenditures that must be paid as required by law, and expenditures for handling emergencies such as natural disasters.

The circumstances under which expenditures are arranged in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall be explained in the report on the draft budget.

After the budget is approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level, implementation shall proceed according to the approved budget.

Article 55: The departments and units responsible for collecting budget revenues must, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, collect the budget revenues due in a timely and full manner. They shall not, in violation of laws and administrative regulations, over-collect, collect in advance, reduce, exempt, or defer the collection of budget revenues due, and shall not intercept, occupy, or misappropriate budget revenues.

Governments at all levels shall not issue revenue targets to the departments and units responsible for collecting budget revenues.

Article 56: All revenues of the government shall be turned over to the State Treasury (hereinafter referred to as the Treasury). No department, unit, or individual may intercept, occupy, misappropriate, or delay the payment of such revenues.

For specific dedicated funds explicitly provided for by law or approved by the State Council, special fiscal accounts may be established in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Article 57: The finance departments of governments at all levels must, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the regulations of the finance department of the State Council, disburse budget expenditure funds in a timely and full manner, and strengthen the management and supervision of budget expenditures.

The expenditures of governments at all levels, all departments, and all units must be implemented in accordance with the budget, and no false expenditures shall be recorded.

Governments at all levels, all departments, and all units shall conduct performance evaluations on budget expenditures.

Article 58: The revenues and expenditures of budgets at all levels shall be accounted for on a cash basis.

The circumstances under which the accrual basis is applied to specific matters in accordance with the regulations of the State Council shall be reported to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 59: A treasury must be established for budgets at or above the county level; townships, ethnic townships, and towns that meet the conditions shall also establish treasuries.

The central treasury business shall be managed by the People's Bank of China, and local treasury business shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the State Council.

Treasuries at all levels shall, in accordance with the relevant regulations of the state, promptly and accurately handle the receipt, allocation, retention, refund, and disbursement of budget revenues and the allocation of budget expenditures.

The right to dispose of treasury funds at all levels belongs to the finance department of the government at the corresponding level. Unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations, no department, unit, or individual has the right to freeze, use, or otherwise dispose of treasury funds that have been deposited in the treasury without the consent of the finance department of the government at the corresponding level.

Governments at all levels shall strengthen the management and supervision of the treasury at the corresponding level, improve treasury cash management in accordance with the regulations of the State Council, and reasonably adjust the balance of treasury funds.

Article 60: If funds that have been deposited in the treasury need to be refunded in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, or decisions of the State Council, the finance department of the government at the corresponding level or its authorized agency shall handle the refund promptly. Matters that should be arranged through fiscal expenditures shall not be handled as refunds.

Article 61: The state implements a system of centralized treasury collection and centralized payment, managing all government revenues and expenditures through centralized treasury receipt and payment.

Article 62: Governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over budget implementation, support the departments responsible for collecting budget revenues, such as finance, taxation, and customs, in organizing budget revenues according to law, and support the finance departments of governments in strictly managing budget expenditures.

Departments such as finance, taxation, and customs shall, during budget implementation, strengthen the analysis of budget implementation; when problems are discovered, they shall promptly suggest that the government at the corresponding level take measures to resolve them.

Article 63: All departments and units shall strengthen the management of budget revenues and expenditures, shall not intercept or use budget revenues that should be turned over, and shall not arbitrarily change the use of budget expenditures.

Article 64: The plan for using the budget reserve fund at any level shall be proposed by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level and submitted to the government at the corresponding level for decision.

Article 65: The budget revolving fund at any level shall be managed by the finance department of the government at the corresponding level and shall not be used for other purposes.

Article 66: If there is excess revenue during the annual implementation of a general public budget at any level, it can only be used to offset deficits or supplement the budget stabilization and regulation fund.

The surplus funds of a general public budget at any level shall be used to supplement the budget stabilization and regulation fund.

If a shortfall occurs during the annual implementation of the general public budget of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and a balance between revenue and expenditure cannot be achieved through measures such as transferring in funds from the budget stabilization and regulation fund or reducing expenditures, the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government may, with the approval of the people's congress at the corresponding level or its standing committee, increase the deficit, report it to the finance department of the State Council for recordation, and shall make up for it in the budget of the following year.

## Chapter 7: Budget Adjustment

Article 67: If any of the following circumstances occur during the implementation of the central budget approved by the National People's Congress or a local budget at any level approved by the local people's congress at the corresponding level, a budget adjustment shall be made:

(1) It is necessary to increase or decrease the total budget expenditure;
(2) It is necessary to transfer in funds from the budget stabilization and regulation fund;
(3) It is necessary to reduce the amount of key expenditures arranged in the budget;
(4) It is necessary to increase the amount of debt borrowed.

Article 68: During budget implementation, governments at all levels generally shall not formulate new policies or measures that increase fiscal revenues or expenditures, nor shall they formulate policies or measures that reduce fiscal revenues; if such measures are necessary and a budget adjustment is required, they shall be arranged in the budget adjustment plan.

Article 69: During budget implementation, if a budget adjustment is necessary, the government at the corresponding level shall prepare a budget adjustment plan. The budget adjustment plan shall explain the reasons, items, and amounts for the budget adjustment.

During budget implementation, if it is necessary to promptly increase budget expenditures due to the occurrence of emergencies such as natural disasters, the reserve fund shall be used first; if the reserve fund is insufficient for the expenditures, the government at the corresponding level may arrange the expenditures first, and if it constitutes a budget adjustment, it shall be included in the budget adjustment plan.

The finance department of the State Council shall, thirty days before the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress holds a meeting to review and approve the budget adjustment plan, submit the preliminary budget adjustment plan to the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee of the National People's Congress for preliminary review.

The finance departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to review and approve the budget adjustment plan, submit the preliminary budget adjustment plan to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary review.

The finance departments of the people's governments of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to review and approve the budget adjustment plan, submit the preliminary budget adjustment plan to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary review, or to the relevant working body of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for comments.

The finance departments of the people's governments of counties, autonomous counties, cities not divided into districts, and municipal districts shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to review and approve the budget adjustment plan, submit the preliminary budget adjustment plan to the relevant working body of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for comments.

The adjustment plan for the central budget shall be submitted to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for review and approval. The adjustment plan for a local budget at or above the county level shall be submitted to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for review and approval; the adjustment plan for the budget of a township, ethnic township, or town shall be submitted to the people's congress at the corresponding level for review and approval. Without approval, the budget shall not be adjusted.

Article 70: Governments at all levels shall strictly implement the approved budget adjustment plan. Without going through the procedures stipulated in Article 69 of this Law, governments at all levels shall not make decisions on budget adjustments.

If a decision is made in violation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the people's congress at the corresponding level, the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level, or the government at the next higher level shall order its modification or revocation.

Article 71: During budget implementation, changes in budget expenditures of a local government at any level caused by an increase in special transfer payments from a higher-level government that do not require matching funds from the government at the corresponding level shall not be considered a budget adjustment.

Local governments at or above the county level that receive increased special transfer payments shall report the relevant circumstances to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level; the people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns that receive increased special transfer payments shall report the relevant circumstances to the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 72: The budget expenditures of all departments and units shall be executed in accordance with the budget accounts. The transfer of budget funds between different budget accounts, budget levels, or projects shall be strictly controlled. If a transfer is truly necessary, it shall be handled in accordance with the regulations of the finance department of the State Council.

Article 73: After the adjustment plan for a local budget at any level is approved, the government at that level shall submit it to the government at the next higher level for recordation.

## Chapter 8: Final Accounts

Article 74: Draft final accounts shall be prepared by governments at all levels, all departments, and all units after the end of each budget year within the time limit prescribed by the State Council.

The specific matters for preparing draft final accounts shall be arranged by the finance department of the State Council.

Article 75: The preparation of draft final accounts must comply with laws and administrative regulations, ensuring that revenues and expenditures are truthful, amounts are accurate, content is complete, and submission is timely.

The draft final accounts shall correspond to the budget, listing the budgeted figures, adjusted budget figures, and final account figures separately. Expenditures of the general public budget shall be itemized by function and by economic nature.

Article 76: Each department shall review the draft final accounts of the units under it, compile the draft final accounts of its own department, and submit them to the finance department of the government at the corresponding level for review within the prescribed time limit.

If, after reviewing the draft final accounts of the departments at the corresponding level, the finance department of a government at any level finds that they do not comply with the provisions of laws or administrative regulations, it has the right to correct them.

Article 77: The finance department of the State Council shall prepare the draft central final accounts. After being audited by the audit department of the State Council, it shall be submitted to the State Council for approval, and the State Council shall submit it to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for review and approval.

The finance department of a local government at or above the county level shall prepare the draft final accounts at the corresponding level. After being audited by the audit department of the government at the corresponding level, it shall be submitted to the government at the corresponding level for approval, and the government at the corresponding level shall submit it to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for review and approval.

The people's governments of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall prepare the draft final accounts at the corresponding level and submit them to the people's congress at the corresponding level for review and approval.

Article 78: The finance department of the State Council shall, thirty days before the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress holds a meeting to review and approve the draft central final accounts, submit the draft central final accounts for the previous year to the Financial and Economic Affairs Committee of the National People's Congress for preliminary review.The finance department of a provincial, autonomous region, or directly-administered municipal government shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to examine and approve the draft final accounts at the corresponding level, submit the draft final accounts of the previous year to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary examination.

The finance department of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture government shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to examine and approve the draft final accounts at the corresponding level, submit the draft final accounts of the previous year to the relevant special committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for preliminary examination, or deliver them to the relevant working body of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for comments.

The finance department of a county, autonomous county, city not divided into districts, or municipal district government shall, thirty days before the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level holds a meeting to examine and approve the draft final accounts at the corresponding level, deliver the draft final accounts of the previous year to the relevant working body of the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for comments.

The Financial and Economic Committee of the National People's Congress and the relevant special committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, directly-administered municipalities, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures shall submit a report on the examination results of the draft final accounts at the corresponding level to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level.

Article 79 The standing committees of people's congresses at and above the county level and the people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns shall focus on examining the following contents regarding the draft final accounts at the corresponding level:

(1) The status of budgetary revenue;
(2) The implementation of expenditure policies and the use and performance of funds for key expenditures and major investment projects;
(3) The use of carry-over funds;
(4) The status of fund balances;
(5) The adjustment and execution of the budget at the corresponding level;
(6) The implementation of fiscal transfer payment arrangements;
(7) The scale, structure, use, and repayment of debts approved to be borrowed;
(8) The scale and use of the budget turnover fund at the corresponding level;
(9) The use of the budget reserve fund at the corresponding level;
(10) The arrangement of excess revenue, and the scale and use of the budget stabilization and regulation fund;
(11) The implementation of the budget resolution approved by the people's congress at the corresponding level;
(12) Other important matters related to the final accounts.

The standing committees of people's congresses at and above the county level shall examine the draft final accounts at the corresponding level in conjunction with the audit work report on the implementation of the budget and other fiscal revenues and expenditures for the previous year submitted by the government at the corresponding level.

Article 80 After the final accounts at each level are approved, the finance department shall, within twenty days, approve the final accounts to each department. Each department shall, within fifteen days after receiving the approval of its final accounts from the finance department of the government at the corresponding level, approve the final accounts to its subordinate units.

Article 81 Local governments at all levels shall compile the approved final accounts and the final accounts submitted for record by the government at the next lower level and report them to the government at the next higher level for record.

Governments at and above the county level shall compile the final accounts submitted for record by the government at the next lower level and report them to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for record.

Article 82 If the State Council or a local government at or above the county level considers that the final accounts submitted for record by the government at the next lower level in accordance with Article 81 of this Law conflict with laws or administrative regulations or are otherwise inappropriate, and that the resolution approving such final accounts needs to be revoked, it shall submit the matter to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for deliberation and decision; if the deliberation decides to revoke, the standing committee of the people's congress at the next lower level shall instruct the government at the corresponding level to re-prepare a draft final accounts in accordance with this Law and submit it to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for examination and approval.

## Chapter 9 Supervision

Article 83 The National People's Congress and its Standing Committee supervise the central and local budgets and final accounts.

The local people's congresses at and above the county level and their standing committees supervise the budgets and final accounts at the corresponding level and at lower levels.

The people's congresses of townships, ethnic townships, and towns supervise the budgets and final accounts at the corresponding level.

Article 84 People's congresses at all levels and the standing committees of people's congresses at and above the county level have the authority to organize investigations into major matters or specific issues concerning budgets and final accounts. The relevant governments, departments, units, and individuals shall truthfully report the situation and provide necessary materials.

Article 85 When people's congresses at all levels and the standing committees of people's congresses at and above the county level hold meetings, deputies to the people's congresses or members of the standing committees may, in accordance with the procedures stipulated by law, raise inquiries or interpellations regarding relevant issues in budgets and final accounts. The relevant government or finance department that is inquired or interpellated must promptly provide a response.

Article 86 The State Council and local governments at and above the county level shall report the implementation of the budget to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level between June and September each year.

Article 87 Governments at all levels supervise the budget implementation of governments at lower levels; governments at lower levels shall regularly report the budget implementation to the government at the next higher level.

Article 88 The finance departments of governments at all levels are responsible for supervising the budget management work of all departments at the corresponding level and their subordinate units, and shall report the budget implementation to the government at the corresponding level and the finance department of the government at the next higher level.

Article 89 The audit departments of governments at and above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, exercise audit supervision over budget implementation and final accounts.

The audit work report on budget implementation and other fiscal revenues and expenditures shall be made public.

Article 90 Government departments at all levels are responsible for supervising and inspecting the budget implementation of their subordinate units, promptly reflecting the budget implementation of their department to the finance department of the government at the corresponding level, and correcting violations of the budget in accordance with the law.

Article 91 Citizens, legal persons, or other organizations that discover violations of this Law may report or accuse them to the relevant state organs in accordance with the law.

The state organs that receive such reports or accusations shall handle them in accordance with the law and maintain confidentiality for the whistleblowers and accusers. No unit or individual may suppress or retaliate against whistleblowers or accusers.

## Chapter 10 Legal Liability

Article 92 If a government at any level or a relevant department commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections, and administrative liability shall be pursued against the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to prepare and submit draft budgets, budget adjustment plans, draft final accounts, departmental budgets and final accounts, and to approve budgets and final accounts in accordance with the provisions of this Law;
(2) Making budget adjustments in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(3) Failing to disclose and explain relevant budget matters in accordance with the provisions of this Law;
(4) Establishing government fund projects and other fiscal revenue projects in violation of regulations;
(5) Using budget reserve funds, budget turnover funds, budget stabilization and regulation funds, or excess revenue in violation of laws and regulations;
(6) Opening fiscal accounts in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Article 93 If a government at any level, a relevant department, or a unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions of demotion, removal from office, or dismissal in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to include all government revenues and expenditures in the budget, or falsely listing revenues and expenditures;
(2) In violation of laws and administrative regulations, over-levying, collecting in advance, or reducing, exempting, or deferring the collection of budgetary revenues that should be levied;
(3) Intercepting, occupying, misappropriating, or defaulting on budgetary revenues that should be turned over to the state treasury;
(4) Changing the use of budget expenditures in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(5) Unauthorizedly changing the use of special transfer payment funds from the government at a higher level;
(6) Disbursing budget expenditure funds, handling the collection, division, retention, and refund of budgetary revenues in violation of the provisions of this Law, or freezing, using treasury funds, or otherwise disposing of funds already in the state treasury in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Article 94 If a government at any level, a department, or a unit borrows debts or provides guarantees for others' debts in violation of the provisions of this Law, misappropriates funds for key expenditures, or constructs office buildings and guesthouses outside the budget or beyond budget standards, it shall be ordered to make corrections, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions of removal from office or dismissal.

Article 95 If a relevant department, unit, or its staff member of a government at any level commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections, the defrauded or used funds shall be recovered, any illegal gains shall be confiscated, and the unit shall be given a warning or a notice of criticism; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Changing the method of turning over budgetary revenues in violation of laws and regulations;
(2) Defrauding budgetary funds through false reporting, fraudulent claims, or other means;
(3) Expanding the scope of expenditures or raising expenditure standards in violation of regulations;
(4) Other acts violating fiscal management regulations.

Article 96 If other laws provide otherwise for the handling and punishment of the illegal acts listed in Articles 92, 93, 94, and 95 of this Law, those provisions shall prevail.

If a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 11 Supplementary Provisions

Article 97 The finance departments of governments at all levels shall prepare annual comprehensive government financial reports on an accrual basis, reporting the overall financial condition, operational status, and medium-to-long-term fiscal sustainability of the government, and submit them to the standing committee of the people's congress at the corresponding level for record.

Article 98 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 99 The budget management of ethnic autonomous areas shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Law on Regional Ethnic Autonomy; if the Law on Regional Ethnic Autonomy has no provisions, it shall be implemented in accordance with this Law and the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 100 The people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and directly-administered municipalities, or their standing committees, may, based on this Law, formulate decisions or local regulations concerning budget examination and supervision.

Article 101 This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 1995. The "State Budget Management Regulations" promulgated by the State Council on October 21, 1991, shall be simultaneously abolished.
