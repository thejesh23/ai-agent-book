# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Promotion of Small and Medium-sized Enterprises

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 29, 2002

Revised at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 1, 2017

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of improving the business environment for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), ensuring their fair participation in market competition, safeguarding their legitimate rights and interests, supporting their entrepreneurship and innovation, promoting their healthy development, expanding urban and rural employment, and giving full play to their important role in the national economy and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "small and medium-sized enterprises" (SMEs) refer to enterprises established within the territory of the People's Republic of China in accordance with the law, which are relatively small in terms of personnel size and business scale, including medium-sized enterprises, small enterprises, and micro enterprises.

The criteria for classifying medium-sized, small, and micro enterprises shall be formulated by the department of the State Council responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion, jointly with other relevant departments of the State Council, based on indicators such as the number of employees, operating revenue, and total assets, taking into account the characteristics of different industries, and shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

Article 3 The State shall regard the promotion of SME development as a long-term development strategy, adhere to the principles of equal rights, equal opportunities, and equal rules for all types of enterprises, and implement a policy of actively supporting, strengthening guidance, improving services, regulating in accordance with the law, and protecting the rights and interests of SMEs, especially small and micro enterprises, thereby creating a favorable environment for the establishment and development of SMEs.

Article 4 SMEs shall operate in accordance with the law, comply with national laws and regulations concerning labor and employment, work safety, occupational health, social security, resources and the environment, quality standards, intellectual property, fiscal taxation, etc., adhere to the principle of good faith, standardize internal management, and improve their management and operational capabilities; they shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of workers, nor shall they harm the public interest.

Article 5 The State Council shall formulate policies for promoting SME development, establish a coordination mechanism for SME promotion, and coordinate SME promotion work nationwide.

The department of the State Council responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion shall organize the implementation of policies for promoting SME development, and shall provide macro-level guidance, comprehensive coordination, and supervision and inspection of SME promotion work.

Other relevant departments of the State Council shall, within their respective responsibilities, be responsible for SME promotion work in accordance with the national policies for promoting SME development.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual conditions, establish coordination mechanisms for SME promotion work, designate corresponding departments responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion, and be responsible for SME promotion work within their respective administrative regions.

Article 6 The State shall establish a statistical monitoring system for SMEs. Statistical departments shall strengthen statistical surveys, monitoring, and analysis of SMEs, and regularly publish relevant information.

Article 7 The State shall promote the establishment of a credit system for SMEs, establish a socialized credit information collection and evaluation system, and achieve the socialization of credit information inquiry, exchange, and sharing for SMEs.

## Chapter II: Fiscal and Tax Support

Article 8 The central finance shall set up an item for SMEs in its budget and allocate special funds for SME development.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual conditions, allocate special funds for SME development in their respective fiscal budgets.

Article 9 Special funds for SME development shall be used primarily to support the construction of public service systems and financing service systems for SMEs through means such as grants, purchase of services, and rewards.

Special funds for SME development shall be tilted towards small and micro enterprises. The management and use of funds shall adhere to the principles of openness and transparency, and shall implement budget performance management.

Article 10 The State shall establish an SME development fund. The national SME development fund shall adhere to the principles of policy orientation and market-oriented operation, and shall be mainly used to guide and drive social funds to support SMEs in their start-up phase, promoting entrepreneurship and innovation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may establish SME development funds.

The measures for the establishment, use, and management of SME development funds shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 11 The State shall implement tax policies favorable to the development of small and micro enterprises. For eligible small and micro enterprises, measures such as deferred payment, reduction, or exemption of corporate income tax and value-added tax shall be implemented in accordance with regulations, tax collection and management procedures shall be simplified, and the tax burden on small and micro enterprises shall be reduced.

Article 12 The State shall implement preferential policies such as reductions and exemptions of administrative fees for small and micro enterprises, thereby reducing their burden.

## Chapter III: Financing Promotion

Article 13 Financial institutions shall perform the function of serving the real economy and serve SMEs efficiently and fairly.

Article 14 The People's Bank of China shall comprehensively utilize monetary policy tools to encourage and guide financial institutions to increase credit support for small and micro enterprises and improve their financing environment.

Article 15 The banking regulatory authority of the State Council shall formulate differentiated regulatory policies for financial institutions providing financial services to small and micro enterprises, adopt measures such as reasonably increasing the tolerance for non-performing loans of small and micro enterprises, and guide financial institutions to increase the scale and proportion of financing for small and micro enterprises, thereby improving the level of financial services.

Article 16 The State shall encourage various types of financial institutions to develop and provide financial products and services suitable for the characteristics of SMEs.

State policy-based financial institutions shall, within their business scope, provide financial services to SMEs in various forms.

Article 17 The State shall promote and support the construction of an inclusive financial system, promote the orderly and healthy development of small and medium-sized banks, non-deposit-taking lending institutions, and internet finance, and guide banking financial institutions to extend their outlets and businesses to areas with weak financial services for small and micro enterprises, such as counties and townships.

Large state-owned commercial banks shall establish inclusive financial institutions to provide financial services for small and micro enterprises. The State shall promote other banking financial institutions to establish specialized institutions for financial services for small and micro enterprises.

Regional small and medium-sized banks shall actively provide financial services to small and micro enterprises in their localities, promoting the development of the real economy.

Article 18 The State shall improve the multi-level capital market system, promote equity financing through multiple channels, develop and regulate the bond market, and facilitate SMEs in using various methods for direct financing.

Article 19 The State shall improve the secured financing system and support financial institutions in providing secured financing to SMEs using accounts receivable, intellectual property, inventory, machinery and equipment, etc., as collateral.

Article 20 When an SME applies for secured financing using accounts receivable, the payer of the accounts receivable shall promptly confirm the creditor-debtor relationship to support the SME's financing.

The State shall encourage SMEs and their payers to confirm the creditor-debtor relationship through the accounts receivable financing service platform, thereby improving financing efficiency and reducing financing costs.

Article 21 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish a policy-based credit guarantee system for SMEs and encourage various guarantee institutions to provide credit guarantees for SME financing.

Article 22 The State shall promote insurance institutions to carry out loan guarantee insurance and credit insurance businesses for SMEs, and develop insurance products that meet the needs of SMEs for risk diversification and loss compensation.

Article 23 The State shall support credit investigation agencies in developing credit investigation products and services for SME financing, and collect information from relevant government departments, public institutions, and commercial enterprises in accordance with the law.

The State shall encourage third-party rating agencies to carry out rating services for SMEs.

## Chapter IV: Entrepreneurship Support

Article 24 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, through government websites, promotional materials, etc., provide free legal and policy consultation and public information services to entrepreneurs regarding industry and commerce, fiscal taxation, finance, environmental protection, work safety, labor and employment, social security, etc.

Article 25 College graduates, demobilized military personnel, unemployed persons, and disabled persons who establish small and micro enterprises shall enjoy tax incentives and fee reductions or exemptions in accordance with state regulations.

Article 26 The State shall take measures to support the participation of social funds in investing in SMEs. Venture capital enterprises and individual investors who invest in start-up technology-based innovation enterprises shall enjoy tax incentives in accordance with state regulations.

Article 27 The State shall improve the entrepreneurial environment for enterprises, optimize the approval process, facilitate administrative licensing for SMEs, and reduce the cost of establishing SMEs.

Article 28 The State shall encourage the construction and establishment of business bases and incubation bases for small and micro enterprises, providing them with production and operation sites and services.

Article 29 Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on the needs of SME development, arrange for necessary land and facilities in urban and rural planning to facilitate SMEs in obtaining production and operation premises.

The State shall support the use of idle commercial premises, industrial plants, enterprise warehouses, and logistics facilities to provide low-cost production and operation premises for entrepreneurs.

Article 30 The State shall encourage internet platforms to open up resources such as technology, development, marketing, and promotion to SMEs, strengthen resource sharing and cooperation, and provide services for SME entrepreneurship.

Article 31 The State shall simplify the deregistration procedures for SMEs, facilitating their market exit.

## Chapter V: Innovation Support

Article 32 The State shall encourage SMEs to promote innovation in technology, products, management models, and business models in accordance with market demand.

If the fixed assets of an SME need accelerated depreciation due to technological progress or other reasons, the depreciation period may be shortened or accelerated depreciation methods may be adopted in accordance with the law.

The State shall improve the policy of additional deduction of research and development expenses for SMEs and support their technological innovation.

Article 33 The State shall support SMEs in applying modern technical means such as the Internet, cloud computing, big data, and artificial intelligence in areas such as R&D and design, production and manufacturing, and operation management, innovating production methods and improving production and operational efficiency.

Article 34 The State shall encourage SMEs to participate in the R&D of key generic technologies for industries and in the implementation of scientific research projects funded by fiscal funds.

The State shall promote the deep development of military-civilian integration and support SMEs in participating in national defense research and production activities.

The State shall support SMEs and relevant industry organizations of SMEs in participating in the formulation of standards.

Article 35 The State shall encourage SMEs to research and develop technologies and products with independent intellectual property rights, standardize internal intellectual property management, and enhance their ability to protect and utilize intellectual property; encourage SMEs to take out intellectual property insurance; and reduce the burden of costs such as application and maintenance of intellectual property rights for SMEs.

Article 36 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide support in terms of planning, land use, and finance to promote the establishment and development of various types of innovation service institutions.

The State shall encourage various types of innovation service institutions to provide SMEs with services such as technical information, R&D design and application, quality standards, experiments and testing, inspection and testing, technology transfer, and technical training, promote the transformation of scientific and technological achievements, and facilitate the upgrading of enterprise technology and products.

Article 37 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall broaden channels, adopt measures such as subsidies and training, guide college graduates to work in SMEs, and help SMEs introduce innovative talents.

The State shall encourage scientific research institutions, institutions of higher education, and large enterprises to create conditions to open up their experimental facilities to SMEs, carry out technology R&D and cooperation, help SMEs develop new products, and cultivate professional talents.

The State shall encourage scientific research institutions and institutions of higher education to support their scientific and technical personnel in engaging in industry-university-research cooperation and the transformation of scientific and technological achievements in SMEs through forms such as part-time work, temporary posts, and participation in project cooperation, and to receive corresponding remuneration in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter VI: Market Development

Article 38 The State shall improve the market system, implement a unified market access and market supervision system, oppose monopoly and unfair competition, and create a market environment for SMEs to participate in competition fairly.

Article 39 The State shall support the establishment of stable collaborative relationships between large enterprises and SMEs based on market allocation of resources in areas such as raw material supply, production, sales, service outsourcing, technology development, and technological transformation, thereby driving and promoting the development of SMEs.

Article 40 Relevant departments of the State Council shall formulate relevant preferential policies for government procurement by SMEs, and increase the share of SMEs in government procurement through measures such as formulating procurement demand standards, reserving procurement shares, offering price evaluation preferences, and implementing priority procurement.

The procurement share reserved for SMEs shall account for no less than 30% of the total budget for the department's annual government procurement projects; among this, the proportion reserved for small and micro enterprises shall be no less than 60%. This does not apply to goods and services that SMEs are unable to provide.

Government procurement shall not impose differential treatment or discriminatory treatment on SMEs in terms of enterprise equity structure, operating history, business scale, or financial indicators.

Government procurement departments shall promptly publish procurement information to the public on media designated by the government procurement supervision and administration department, and provide guidance and services for SMEs to obtain government procurement contracts.

Article 41 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide guidance and assistance to SMEs in the export of their products and services in areas such as legal consultation, intellectual property protection, technical trade measures, and product certification, and promote foreign economic and technological cooperation and exchanges.

Relevant state policy-based financial institutions shall support SMEs in exploring overseas markets by conducting businesses such as import and export credit and export credit insurance.

Article 42 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide convenience for SMEs in areas such as foreign exchange use and personnel entry and exit, and support SMEs in investing abroad and exploring international markets.

## Chapter VII: Service Measures

Article 43 The State shall establish and improve a socialized public service system for SMEs, providing services to SMEs.

Article 44 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual needs, establish and improve public service institutions for SMEs, providing them with public welfare services.

Article 45 The department of the people's government at or above the county level responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion shall establish a cross-departmental policy information internet publishing platform, promptly collect various government service information related to SMEs, such as laws and regulations, entrepreneurship, innovation, finance, markets, and rights protection, and provide convenient and free services to SMEs.

Article 46 The State shall encourage various types of service institutions to provide SMEs with services such as entrepreneurship training and guidance, intellectual property protection, management consulting, information consulting, credit services, marketing, project development, investment and financing, accounting and taxation, property rights trading, technical support, talent introduction, foreign cooperation, exhibitions and trade fairs, and legal consultation.

Article 47 The department of the people's government at or above the county level responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion shall allocate funds and plan to organize training for the management personnel of SMEs.

Article 48 The State shall support relevant institutions and institutions of higher education in conducting personnel training for SMEs in areas such as operation management and production technology, thereby improving their marketing, management, and technical levels.

The State shall support institutions of higher education, vocational education institutions, and various types of vocational skills training institutions in cooperating with SMEs to jointly build internship and practice bases, support two-way exchanges between teachers of vocational education institutions and technical personnel of SMEs, and innovate the talent cultivation model for SMEs.

Article 49 Relevant industry organizations of SMEs shall, in accordance with the law, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of their members, reflect the demands of their members, strengthen self-discipline management, and provide services for SME entrepreneurship, innovation, and market development.

## Chapter VIII: Rights and Interests Protection

Article 50 The State shall protect the property rights and other legitimate rights and interests of SMEs and their investors. No unit or individual shall infringe upon the property or legitimate income of SMEs.

Article 51 The department of the people's government at or above the county level responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion shall establish dedicated channels to solicit opinions and suggestions from SMEs on relevant government management work, promptly provide feedback to relevant departments, and urge improvements.

Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant industry organizations shall publish their contact information, accept complaints and reports from SMEs, and investigate and handle them within a specified time limit.

Article 52 Local people's governments at all levels shall implement administrative licensing and carry out management work in accordance with the law, shall not conduct inspections without legal or regulatory basis, and shall not force or indirectly force SMEs to participate in activities such as assessments, evaluations, commendations, or training.

Article 53 State organs, public institutions, and large enterprises shall not default on payments for goods, projects, or services owed to SMEs in breach of contract.

SMEs have the right to demand payment of overdue amounts from the defaulting party and to claim compensation for losses caused by the default.

Article 54 No unit shall charge fees from SMEs in violation of laws or regulations, impose fines without legal or regulatory basis, or apportion property to SMEs. SMEs have the right to refuse, report, and accuse any act that violates the above provisions.

Article 55 The State shall establish and implement a list system for administrative fees related to enterprises. The list of fees and their implementation status shall be made public to society and subject to social supervision.

No unit shall charge SMEs administrative fees not included in the list, or arbitrarily increase the fee standard or expand the scope of fees for SMEs; it is strictly prohibited to force SMEs to make donations or sponsorships, subscribe to newspapers and periodicals, join associations, or accept designated services through various means; it is strictly prohibited for industry organizations to rely on delegated government functions or utilize administrative resources to arbitrarily establish fee items or increase fee standards.

Article 56 Relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct supervision and inspection of SMEs in accordance with the law and establish a random inspection mechanism. If multiple supervision and inspection items conducted by the same department on an SME can be merged, they shall be merged; if multiple supervision and inspection items conducted by different departments on an SME can be completed jointly, the people's government at the same level shall organize the relevant departments to conduct a combined or joint inspection.

## Chapter IX: Supervision and Inspection

Article 57 People's governments at or above the county level shall regularly organize supervision and inspection of SME promotion work; promptly correct any violations of this Law, and impose administrative sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 58 The department of the State Council responsible for the comprehensive management of SME promotion shall entrust a third-party institution to regularly conduct an assessment of the SME development environment and make the results public.

Local people's governments at all levels may, based on actual conditions, entrust third-party institutions to conduct assessments of the SME development environment.

Article 59 People's governments at or above the county level shall regularly organize enterprise evaluations, social evaluations, and dynamic assessments of the use effects of special funds for SME development and SME development funds, and promptly make the evaluation and assessment results public to society, subject to social supervision.The relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, supervise the management and use of special funds for the development of small and medium-sized enterprises and the development fund for small and medium-sized enterprises, investigate and deal with acts such as intercepting, misappropriating, diverting, embezzling, or corrupting such funds or fund in accordance with the law, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 60 The relevant departments of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, investigate and deal with acts of forcing or covertly forcing small and medium-sized enterprises to participate in assessments, evaluations, commendations, training, and other activities, illegally charging fees, imposing fines, or apportioning property to small and medium-sized enterprises, as well as other acts infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of small and medium-sized enterprises, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 10 Supplementary Provisions

Article 61 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2018.
