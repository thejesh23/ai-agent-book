# Anti-Food Waste Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on April 29, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to prevent food waste, ensure national food security, promote the traditional virtues of the Chinese nation, practice the core socialist values, conserve resources, protect the environment, and promote sustainable economic and social development.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "food" refers to food as defined in the Food Safety Law of the People's Republic of China, including all kinds of food intended for human consumption or drinking.

For the purposes of this Law, "food waste" refers to the failure to reasonably utilize food that is safe for human consumption or drinking in accordance with its functional purpose, including discarding, or causing a reduction in quantity or a decline in quality due to unreasonable utilization.

Article 3 The State practices thrift and opposes waste.

The State adheres to the principles of taking multiple measures simultaneously, implementing targeted policies, conducting scientific management, and promoting social co-governance, and adopts technically feasible and economically reasonable measures to prevent and reduce food waste.

The State advocates civilized, healthy, resource-conserving, and environmentally friendly consumption patterns, and promotes a simple, moderate, green, and low-carbon lifestyle.

Article 4 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over anti-food waste work, determine the objectives and tasks for anti-food waste, establish and improve the working mechanism for anti-food waste, organize monitoring, investigation, analysis, and assessment of food waste, strengthen supervision and management, and advance anti-food waste work.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall annually report to the public on the status of anti-food waste, propose measures to strengthen anti-food waste efforts, and continuously promote anti-food waste across the whole society.

Article 5 The development and reform department of the State Council shall strengthen the organization and coordination of national anti-food waste work; together with the relevant departments of the State Council, it shall annually analyze and assess the situation of food waste, make overall arrangements for anti-food waste work, and propose relevant work measures and opinions for implementation by the relevant departments.

The competent commerce department of the State Council shall strengthen the management of the catering industry, establish and improve industry standards and service norms; together with the market supervision and management department of the State Council, it shall establish anti-food waste system norms for the catering industry, and take measures to encourage catering service providers to offer individual meal services and publicly disclose their anti-food waste status.

The market supervision and management department of the State Council shall strengthen supervision over the anti-food waste efforts of food producers and traders, and urge them to implement anti-food waste measures.

The national grain and material reserve department shall strengthen the management of grain conservation and loss reduction during grain storage and circulation, and together with the relevant departments of the State Council, organize the implementation of standards for grain storage, transportation, and processing.

The relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and the responsibilities stipulated by the State Council, take measures to carry out anti-food waste work.

Article 6 State organs, people's organizations, and state-owned enterprises and institutions shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, refine and improve meal norms for official activities such as official receptions, meetings, and training, strengthen management, take the lead in practicing thrift, and oppose waste.

When meals are required for official activities, the quantity and form of meals shall be arranged frugally according to actual circumstances, and shall not exceed the prescribed standards.

Article 7 Catering service providers shall take the following measures to prevent food waste:

(1) Establish and improve management systems for food procurement, storage, and processing, strengthen professional training for service personnel, and incorporate the value of cherishing food and opposing waste into training content;

(2) Proactively remind consumers to prevent food waste, post or place anti-food waste signs in conspicuous locations, or have service personnel provide prompts and explanations, guiding consumers to order food according to their needs and in appropriate quantities;

(3) Improve the quality of food supply, prepare food in accordance with standards and specifications, reasonably determine quantities and portions, and offer choices of different sizes such as small portions;

(4) When providing group meal services, incorporate the concept of preventing food waste into menu design, and reasonably configure dishes and staple foods according to the number of diners;

(5) When providing buffet services, proactively inform consumers of the consumption rules and requirements for preventing food waste, provide tableware of different sizes, and remind consumers to take food in appropriate amounts.

Catering service providers shall not induce or mislead consumers into ordering excessive amounts of food.

Catering service providers may enrich menu information by indicating food portions, specifications, and recommended number of diners on the menu, provide ordering prompts to consumers, and provide serving spoons, serving chopsticks, and packaging services as needed by consumers.

Catering service providers may offer rewards to consumers who participate in the "Clean Plate Campaign"; they may also charge consumers who cause obvious waste a corresponding fee for the disposal of kitchen waste, and the fee standard shall be clearly disclosed.

Catering service providers may use information technology to analyze dining needs, and through measures such as building central kitchens and distribution centers, scientifically manage food procurement, transportation, storage, and processing.

Article 8 Units with canteens shall establish and improve canteen meal management systems, formulate and implement measures to prevent food waste, strengthen publicity and education, and enhance awareness of anti-food waste.

Unit canteens shall strengthen dynamic management of food procurement, storage, and processing, purchase, prepare, and serve food according to the number of diners, improve the utilization rate of raw materials and cooking standards, provide meals based on the principles of health, economy, and standardization, and pay attention to dietary balance.

Unit canteens shall improve meal service methods, post or place anti-food waste signs in conspicuous locations, and guide diners to order and take food in appropriate amounts; for wasteful behavior, they shall promptly remind and correct it.

Article 9 Schools shall monitor, analyze, and assess the number and structure of diners, and strengthen the management of school canteen catering services; if they choose external meal suppliers, they shall establish and improve mechanisms for introduction and withdrawal, and select the best.

School canteens and external meal suppliers shall strengthen refined management, provide meals according to demand, improve meal service methods, scientifically prepare nutritious meals, offer a variety of portion sizes and flavor choices, regularly solicit opinions from diners, and ensure the quality of dishes and staple foods.

Article 10 Online food delivery platforms shall prominently remind consumers to order food in appropriate amounts. Catering service providers offering services through online food delivery platforms shall provide consumers with information such as food portions, specifications, or recommended number of diners on the platform page.

Article 11 Tourism operators shall guide tourists to dine in a civilized and healthy manner. Travel agencies and tour guides shall reasonably arrange group meals and remind tourists to order and take food in appropriate amounts. Relevant industries shall incorporate the anti-food waste work of tourism operators into relevant quality standard rating indicators.

Article 12 Food operators such as supermarkets and shopping malls shall conduct daily inspections of the food they sell, manage food approaching its expiration date by category, and mark it specially or display it for sale in a centralized manner.

Article 13 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall take measures to oppose extravagance and waste, encourage and promote civilized and frugal event organization, and foster an atmosphere where waste is shameful and thrift is honorable.

For meals required for weddings, funerals, gatherings of friends and family, business activities, etc., the organizers and participants shall prepare and order food in moderation, and dine in a civilized and healthy manner.

Article 14 Individuals shall establish civilized, healthy, rational, and green consumption concepts, and when dining out, reasonably order and take food based on their personal health conditions, dietary habits, and dining needs.

Families and their members shall, in family life, cultivate and form scientific, healthy, and waste-preventing good habits of making the best use of everything, and purchase, store, and prepare food according to actual daily needs.

Article 15 The State shall improve standards for the production, storage, transportation, and processing of grain and other edible agricultural products, promote the use of new technologies, new processes, and new equipment, guide moderate processing and comprehensive utilization, and reduce losses.

Food producers and traders shall take measures to improve conditions for food storage, transportation, and processing, prevent food spoilage, and reduce losses during storage and transportation; increase the utilization rate of food processing, and avoid excessive processing and overuse of raw materials.

Article 16 When formulating and revising relevant national standards, industry standards, and local standards, preventing food waste shall be an important consideration, and under the premise of ensuring food safety, waste shall be prevented to the greatest extent possible.

Food expiration dates shall be set scientifically and reasonably, prominently marked, and easily identifiable.

Article 17 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall establish a supervision and inspection mechanism for anti-food waste, and promptly urge rectification of any food waste problems discovered.

If food producers or traders seriously waste food during the production and operation process, the market supervision and management, commerce, and other departments of the local people's government at or above the county level may conduct an interview with their legal representative or principal responsible person. The interviewed food producer or trader shall immediately rectify the situation.

Article 18 The government offices administration department, together with relevant departments, shall establish an effectiveness evaluation and notification system for anti-food waste work in government canteens, and incorporate anti-food waste into the assessment of energy and resource conservation in public institutions and the creation of conservation-oriented government offices.

Article 19 Food and catering industry associations shall strengthen industry self-discipline, formulate and implement relevant group standards and industry self-discipline norms for anti-food waste in accordance with the law, publicize and disseminate knowledge on preventing food waste, promote advanced models, guide members to consciously carry out anti-food waste activities, and take necessary self-discipline measures against members with wasteful behavior.

Food and catering industry associations shall conduct food waste monitoring, strengthen analysis and assessment, annually report to the public on the status of anti-food waste and the results of monitoring and assessment, provide support for state organs in formulating laws, regulations, policies, standards, and conducting related research, and accept social supervision.

Consumers' associations and other consumer organizations shall strengthen dietary consumption education for consumers, guiding them to form consumption habits that consciously resist waste.

Article 20 State organs, people's organizations, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and grassroots self-governing mass organizations shall incorporate the practice of thrift and opposition to waste into mass spiritual civilization creation activities, include them in relevant creation evaluation systems and local civic conventions, village rules and regulations, industry norms, etc., strengthen anti-food waste publicity, education, and scientific popularization, promote the "Clean Plate Campaign," advocate civilized, healthy, and scientific dietary culture, and enhance public awareness of anti-food waste.

People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall continuously organize and carry out anti-food waste publicity and education, and include anti-food waste as an important part of the National Food Security Publicity Week.

Article 21 The education administrative department shall guide and supervise schools to strengthen anti-food waste education and management.

Schools shall carry out national conditions education in accordance with regulations, incorporate the practice of thrift and opposition to waste into educational and teaching content, carry out special anti-food waste educational activities through learning practices, experiential labor, and other forms, and cultivate students' habits of diligence, frugality, and cherishing food.

Schools shall establish a supervision and inspection mechanism for preventing food waste, and formulate and implement corresponding reward and punishment measures.

Article 22 News media shall conduct public interest publicity on anti-food waste laws, regulations, and related standards and knowledge, report on advanced models, expose waste phenomena, guide the public to establish correct dietary consumption concepts, and exercise public opinion supervision over food waste behaviors. Relevant anti-food waste publicity reports shall be truthful and fair.

It is prohibited to produce, publish, or disseminate programs or audio-video information that promotes eating large quantities, binge eating, or other food waste behaviors.

If an online audio-video service provider discovers that a user has violated the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall immediately stop transmitting the relevant information; if the circumstances are serious, it shall stop providing information services.

Article 23 The civil affairs, market supervision and management, and other departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a mechanism to match donation needs, guide food producers and traders, etc., to donate food to relevant social organizations, welfare institutions, relief institutions, or individuals under the premise of ensuring food safety. Relevant organizations shall promptly receive and distribute food as needed.

The State encourages social forces to participate in food donation activities. Online information service providers may build platforms to provide services for food donation, etc.

Article 24 Units, families, and individuals that generate kitchen waste shall fulfill their obligation to reduce kitchen waste at the source in accordance with the law.

Article 25 The State organizes nutritional status monitoring and nutrition knowledge popularization, guides citizens to form scientific dietary habits, and reduces the risk of diseases caused by unhealthy diets.

Article 26 People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to support scientific research, technological development, and other activities for preventing food waste.

Government procurement of related goods and services shall be conducive to preventing food waste.

The State implements tax policies that are conducive to preventing food waste.

Article 27 Any unit or individual that discovers food waste behaviors by food producers or traders, etc., has the right to report to the relevant departments and organs. The departments and organs that receive the report shall promptly handle it in accordance with the law.

Article 28 If a catering service provider, in violation of this Law, fails to proactively remind consumers to prevent food waste, the market supervision and management department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning.

If a catering service provider, in violation of this Law, induces or misleads consumers into ordering excessive amounts of food, causing obvious waste, the market supervision and management department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning; if it refuses to correct, a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed.

If a food producer or trader, in violation of this Law, causes serious food waste during the production and operation process, the market supervision and management department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction; if it refuses to correct, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 29 If a unit with a canteen, in violation of this Law, fails to formulate or implement measures to prevent food waste, the department designated by the local people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and give a warning.

Article 30 If a radio station, television station, or online audio-video service provider, in violation of this Law, produces, publishes, or disseminates programs or audio-video information that promotes eating large quantities, binge eating, or other food waste behaviors, the radio and television, cyberspace administration, and other departments shall, within their respective responsibilities, order correction and give a warning; if it refuses to correct or the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed, and it may be ordered to suspend relevant business or undergo rectification, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be held legally responsible in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, or cities divided into districts and autonomous prefectures may, based on specific circumstances and actual needs, formulate specific local measures for anti-food waste.

Article 32 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
