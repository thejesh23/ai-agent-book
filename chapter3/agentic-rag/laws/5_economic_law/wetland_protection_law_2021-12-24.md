# Wetland Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 32nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Law is enacted to strengthen wetland protection, maintain wetland ecological functions and biodiversity, ensure ecological security, promote ecological civilization, and achieve harmonious coexistence between humans and nature.

**Article 2** This Law applies to activities involving wetland protection, utilization, restoration, and related management within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction.

For the purposes of this Law, "wetlands" refer to natural or artificial, perennial or seasonal waterlogged areas and water bodies with significant ecological functions, including sea areas with a water depth of no more than six meters at low tide, excluding paddy fields and artificially constructed water bodies and tidal flats used for aquaculture. The state implements a tiered management system and a directory system for wetlands.

The protection, utilization, and related management activities of rivers, lakes, and sea areas shall also comply with the provisions of the Water Law of the People's Republic of China, the Flood Control Law of the People's Republic of China, the Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China, the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, the Yangtze River Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, the Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China, the Sea Area Use Management Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws.

**Article 3** Wetland protection shall adhere to the principles of prioritizing protection, strict management, systematic governance, scientific restoration, and rational utilization, giving full play to the multiple ecological functions of wetlands, such as water conservation, climate regulation, environmental improvement, and biodiversity maintenance.

**Article 4** People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate wetland protection into national economic and social development plans and include the funds required for wetland protection work in their budgets according to the principle of division of powers.

Local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for wetland protection within their administrative regions and shall take measures to maintain stable wetland areas and enhance wetland ecological functions.

Township people's governments shall organize the public to carry out wetland protection work, with village committees providing assistance.

**Article 5** The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council is responsible for the supervision and management of wetland resources, the formulation of wetland protection plans and relevant national standards, the supervision and management of wetland development and utilization, and the ecological protection and restoration of wetlands. Other relevant departments under the State Council, such as those for natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural development, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs, shall undertake wetland protection, restoration, and management work according to their respective responsibilities.

The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural development, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, establish a mechanism for wetland protection coordination and information reporting.

**Article 6** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the coordination of wetland protection work. The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for wetland protection, restoration, and management according to their respective responsibilities.

**Article 7** People's governments at all levels shall strengthen publicity, education, and popularization of scientific knowledge on wetland protection, carry out publicity and education activities through Wetland Protection Day, Wetland Protection Publicity Week, etc., to enhance public awareness of wetland protection; encourage grassroots self-governing organizations, social organizations, and volunteers to conduct publicity activities on wetland protection laws, regulations, and knowledge, creating a favorable atmosphere for wetland protection.

Education authorities and schools shall focus on cultivating students' awareness of wetland protection in educational and teaching activities.

News media shall conduct public welfare publicity on wetland protection laws, regulations, and knowledge, and exercise public opinion supervision over acts that damage wetlands.

**Article 8** The state encourages units and individuals to participate in wetland protection activities through donations, funding, volunteer services, etc., in accordance with the law.

Units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in wetland protection shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

**Article 9** The state supports the research, development, and application of science and technology for wetland protection, strengthens the training of professional and technical personnel in wetland protection, and improves the scientific and technological level of wetland protection.

**Article 10** The state supports international cooperation and exchanges in wetland protection science and technology, biodiversity, and migratory bird migration.

**Article 11** All units and individuals have the obligation to protect wetlands and have the right to report or accuse acts that damage wetlands. The authorities receiving such reports or accusations shall handle them promptly and protect the legitimate rights and interests of the reporters and accusers in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Wetland Resource Management

**Article 12** The state establishes a wetland resource survey and evaluation system.

The competent department of natural resources under the State Council shall, together with the competent department of forestry and grassland and other relevant departments under the State Council, regularly conduct national wetland resource surveys and evaluations, investigating wetland types, distribution, area, biodiversity, protection and utilization status, and establishing a unified information release and sharing mechanism.

**Article 13** The state implements a total wetland area control system, incorporating the total wetland area control target into the wetland protection target responsibility system.

The competent departments of forestry and grassland and natural resources under the State Council, together with relevant departments under the State Council, shall determine the total wetland area control targets for the whole country and for each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government based on the national wetland resource status, natural changes, and the requirements of total wetland area control, and submit them to the State Council for approval. Local people's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to implement the requirements of the total wetland area control targets.

**Article 14** The state implements tiered management of wetlands, dividing them into important wetlands and general wetlands according to their ecological location, area, and importance in maintaining ecological functions and biodiversity. Important wetlands include nationally important wetlands and provincially important wetlands; wetlands other than important wetlands are general wetlands. Important wetlands shall be designated as ecological protection red lines in accordance with the law.

The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural development, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, publish the list and scope of nationally important wetlands and set up protection signs. Internationally important wetlands shall be included in the list of nationally important wetlands.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, or their authorized departments, are responsible for publishing the list and scope of provincially important wetlands and reporting them to the competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council for record.

The list and scope of general wetlands shall be published by local people's governments at or above the county level or their authorized departments.

**Article 15** The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall, together with relevant departments under the State Council, formulate a national wetland protection plan based on the national economic and social development plan, the territorial space plan, and the ecological environment protection plan, and organize its implementation after approval by the State Council or its authorized department.

The competent departments of forestry and grassland of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with relevant departments, formulate wetland protection plans for their administrative regions based on the territorial space plan at the same level and the wetland protection plan at the next higher level, and organize their implementation after approval by the people's government at the same level.

Wetland protection plans shall specify the objectives, tasks, overall layout, key protection and restoration points, and safeguard measures. If an approved wetland protection plan needs to be adjusted, it shall be handled in accordance with the original approval procedures.

The formulation of wetland protection plans shall be coordinated with comprehensive river basin plans, flood control plans, and other plans.

**Article 16** The competent departments of forestry and grassland and standardization under the State Council shall, together with the competent departments of natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural development, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs under the State Council, organize the formulation of national standards for wetland classification, monitoring and early warning, and ecological restoration; where national standards are not provided, local standards may be formulated and filed in accordance with the law.

**Article 17** The competent departments of forestry and grassland of people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a wetland protection expert consultation mechanism to provide evaluation and demonstration services for formulating wetland protection plans, developing wetland directories, and formulating relevant standards.

**Article 18** When handling the registration of natural resource ownership involving wetlands, information such as the geographic coordinates, spatial scope, type, and area of the wetland shall be recorded in accordance with regulations.

**Article 19** The state strictly controls the occupation of wetlands.

Occupation of nationally important wetlands is prohibited, except for national major projects, disaster prevention and mitigation projects, important water conservancy and protection facility projects, and wetland protection projects.

The site selection and route selection of construction projects shall avoid wetlands; where avoidance is impossible, occupation shall be minimized, and necessary measures shall be taken to mitigate adverse effects on wetland ecological functions.

When approving or authorizing the site selection or route selection of a construction project involving a nationally important wetland, the opinion of the competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall be sought; when involving a provincially important wetland or a general wetland, the opinion of the department authorized by the local people's government at or above the county level shall be sought according to the management authority.

**Article 20** If a construction project indeed needs to temporarily occupy a wetland, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China, the Water Law of the People's Republic of China, the Forest Law of the People's Republic of China, the Grassland Law of the People's Republic of China, the Sea Area Use Management Law of the People's Republic of China, and other relevant laws and regulations. The period of temporary wetland occupation shall generally not exceed two years, and no permanent structures shall be built on the temporarily occupied wetland.

Within one year after the expiration of the temporary wetland occupation period, the land user or individual shall restore the wetland area and ecological conditions.

**Article 21** Except for wetlands within the management scope of river channels and flood storage and detention areas occupied due to flood control, navigation, ports, or other water projects, units that have been approved to occupy important wetlands shall, in accordance with the law, restore or reconstruct wetlands equivalent in area and quality to the occupied wetlands based on local natural conditions; if restoration or reconstruction is not feasible, they shall pay a wetland restoration fee. Those who pay the wetland restoration fee shall not be required to pay other restoration fees of a similar nature.

The measures for the collection and use of wetland restoration fees shall be formulated by the competent department of finance under the State Council together with the competent department of forestry and grassland and other relevant departments under the State Council.

**Article 22** The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall conduct dynamic monitoring of nationally important wetlands in accordance with monitoring technical specifications, promptly obtaining information on changes in wetland distribution, area, water volume, biodiversity, and threat status.

The competent department of forestry and grassland under the State Council shall, based on monitoring data, assess the ecological status of nationally important wetlands and issue early warning information in accordance with regulations.

The competent departments of forestry and grassland of people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall conduct dynamic monitoring, assessment, and early warning of provincially important wetlands in accordance with monitoring technical specifications.

The competent departments of forestry and grassland of local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen dynamic monitoring of general wetlands.

## Chapter III Wetland Protection and Utilization

**Article 23** The state adheres to ecological priority and green development, improves the wetland protection system, strengthens policy support and scientific and technological support mechanisms for wetland protection, ensures wetland ecological functions and sustainable use, and achieves the unity of ecological, social, and economic benefits.

**Article 24** People's governments at or above the provincial level and their relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, incorporate wetlands into national parks, nature reserves, or natural parks based on wetland protection plans and protection needs.

**Article 25** Local people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall take measures to prevent and control the adverse effects of human activities on wetlands and their biodiversity, strengthen wetland pollution prevention, slow down wetland degradation caused by human and natural factors, and maintain the stability of wetland ecological functions.

Utilization activities such as tourism, planting, animal husbandry, aquaculture, and shipping conducted within wetland areas shall avoid changing the natural state of the wetlands and shall take measures to mitigate adverse effects on wetland ecological functions.

When handling relevant administrative permits such as environmental impact assessments, territorial space plans, sea area use, aquaculture, and flood control, the relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the review of the necessity, rationality, and wetland protection measures of related wetland utilization activities.

**Article 26** Local people's governments at all levels shall provide classified guidance for the utilization activities of provincially important wetlands and general wetlands, encouraging units and individuals to carry out ecological tourism, ecological agriculture, ecological education, and nature experiences that meet wetland protection requirements, and moderately controlling the scale of wetland utilization such as planting and aquaculture.

Local people's governments at all levels shall encourage relevant units to prioritize the employment of local residents in wetland management and protection.

**Article 27** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall fully consider the need to ensure the ecological functions of important wetlands and optimize the industrial layout around important wetlands.

Local people's governments at or above the county level may adopt measures such as targeted support, industrial transfer, attracting social funds, and community co-construction to promote green development in areas surrounding wetlands and coordinate economic development with wetland protection.

**Article 28** The following acts that damage wetlands and their ecological functions are prohibited:

(1) Reclaiming, draining natural wetlands, or permanently cutting off water sources for natural wetlands;
(2) Unauthorized filling of natural wetlands, unauthorized sand mining, mineral extraction, or soil extraction;
(3) Discharging industrial wastewater, domestic sewage, or other wastewater and sewage that do not meet water pollutant discharge standards and pollute wetlands, or dumping, piling, discarding, or scattering solid waste;
(4) Overgrazing or indiscriminate harvesting of wild plants, overfishing or destructive fishing, excessive fertilization, application of chemicals, or bait casting that pollutes wetlands in planting and aquaculture activities;
(5) Other acts that damage wetlands and their ecological functions.

**Article 29** Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, conduct monitoring of harmful organisms in wetlands and promptly take effective measures to prevent, control, and eliminate the harm of harmful organisms to wetland ecosystems.

**Article 30** People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the protection of wetlands that are concentrated distribution areas of nationally key protected wild animals and plants. No unit or individual shall damage the living environment of birds and aquatic organisms.

It is prohibited to engage in activities that endanger the survival and reproduction of waterbirds, such as fishing, digging for benthic organisms, picking up bird eggs, or destroying bird nests, in nature reserves and other important habitats where waterbirds are the protected targets. Activities such as birdwatching, scientific research, and science popularization shall maintain a safe distance to avoid affecting the normal feeding and breeding of birds.

Protection measures shall be implemented in important habitats such as spawning grounds, feeding grounds, wintering grounds, and migration channels of important aquatic organisms. If the construction of sluice gates or dams in migration channels, approved in accordance with the law, may affect the migration of aquatic organisms, the construction unit shall build fish passage facilities or take other remedial measures.

It is prohibited to introduce or release alien species into wetlands; if introduction is indeed necessary, a scientific assessment shall be conducted, and approval shall be obtained in accordance with the law.

**Article 31** The competent department of water administration under the State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management and protection of wetlands within rivers and lakes, take measures such as water system connectivity, dredging, water conservation, and soil and water conservation for governance and restoration according to local conditions, strictly control wetland development and utilization activities in areas such as river sources, flood storage and detention areas, and areas with severe soil erosion, and mitigate adverse effects on wetlands and their biodiversity.

**Article 32** The competent department of natural resources under the State Council and coastal local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management and protection of coastal wetlands, strictly controlling the reclamation and filling of coastal wetlands. Projects approved in accordance with the law shall simultaneously implement ecological protection and restoration to mitigate adverse effects on the ecological functions of coastal wetlands.

**Article 33** The competent department of housing and urban-rural development under the State Council and local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen the management and protection of urban wetlands, take measures such as urban water system governance and ecological restoration to improve the ecological quality of urban wetlands, and give full play to the functions of urban wetlands in rainwater and flood regulation, water purification, leisure and recreation, and science education.

**Article 34** Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where mangrove wetlands are located shall organize the formulation of special plans for mangrove wetland protection and take effective measures to protect mangrove wetlands.

Mangrove wetlands shall be included in the list of important wetlands; those meeting the criteria for nationally important wetlands shall be given priority for inclusion in the list of nationally important wetlands.

Occupation of mangrove wetlands is prohibited. If occupation is indeed necessary due to national major projects, disaster prevention and mitigation, etc., after evaluation by the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the provincial level, it shall be handled in accordance with relevant legal provisions, and protection and restoration work shall be carried out. If related construction projects change the hydrological regime of the estuary where mangroves are located and have a significant impact on mangrove growth, effective measures shall be taken to mitigate adverse effects.

It is prohibited to dig ponds in mangrove wetlands, to cut, dig, transplant mangroves, or excessively pick mangrove seeds, and to release or plant species that harm the growth of mangroves. If cutting, digging, transplanting, or picking is necessary for scientific research, medicine, or mangrove wetland protection, it shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

**Article 35** Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where peat bog wetlands are located shall formulate special plans for peat bog wetland protection and take effective measures to protect peat bog wetlands.

Peat bog wetlands that meet the criteria for important wetlands shall be included in the list of important wetlands.

It is prohibited to mine peat or extract groundwater without authorization in peat bog wetlands; it is prohibited to drain water stored in peat bog wetlands, except when necessary for disaster prevention and mitigation.

**Article 36** The state establishes a wetland ecological protection compensation system.

The State Council and provincial people's governments shall increase financial investment in the protection of important wetlands and increase fiscal transfer payments to areas where important wetlands are located, in accordance with the principle of division of powers.

The state encourages people's governments in wetland ecological protection areas and wetland ecological benefit areas to conduct inter-regional ecological protection compensation through consultation or market mechanisms.

If the legitimate rights and interests of wetland owners or users are damaged due to public interests such as ecological protection, the people's government at or above the county level shall provide compensation.

## Chapter IV Wetland Restoration

**Article 37** People's governments at or above the county level shall adhere to the principle of giving priority to natural restoration and combining natural restoration with artificial restoration, strengthen wetland restoration work, restore wetland areas, and improve the quality of wetland ecosystems.

People's governments at or above the county level shall carry out comprehensive remediation and restoration of severely fragmented or functionally degraded natural wetlands, giving priority to restoring important wetlands with severely degraded ecological functions.

**Article 38** When organizing wetland protection and restoration, people's governments at or above the county level shall fully consider water resource endowment conditions and carrying capacity, rationally allocate water resources, ensure the basic ecological water demand of wetlands, and maintain wetland ecological functions.

**Article 39** Local people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct scientific demonstrations and take measures according to local conditions to restore the ecological functions of original wetlands, degraded wetlands, and salinized wetlands that have restoration potential.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with wetland protection plans, take measures such as water body treatment, land remediation, vegetation restoration, and animal protection according to local conditions to enhance the ecological and carbon sink functions of wetlands.

It is prohibited to illegally occupy cultivated land to construct artificial wetlands.

**Article 40** Local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where mangrove wetlands are located shall give priority to the restoration of mangrove wetlands in areas with important ecological functions, areas with high marine disaster risk levels, areas for the protection of endangered species, or areas with favorable afforestation conditions, and carry out rescue restoration of severely degraded mangrove wetlands, using local tree species as much as possible.**Article 41** The local people's government at or above the county level in the area where a peat swamp wetland is located shall, based on local conditions, organize the restoration of degraded peat swamp wetlands and adopt corresponding restoration measures according to the type, development status, and degree of degradation of the peat swamp wetland.

**Article 42** A wetland restoration plan shall be prepared for the restoration of important wetlands.

The restoration plan for an important wetland shall be submitted to the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the provincial level for approval. Before approving the restoration plan, the competent forestry and grassland department shall seek opinions from the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level, such as those for natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural construction, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs.

**Article 43** The restoration of an important wetland shall be carried out in accordance with the approved wetland restoration plan.

After the restoration of an important wetland is completed, it shall be subject to acceptance inspection by the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the provincial level, and the restoration status shall be made public in accordance with the law. The competent forestry and grassland department at or above the provincial level shall strengthen the post-restoration management and dynamic monitoring of the restored wetland, and conduct post-restoration effectiveness assessments as needed.

**Article 44** Where wetland damage is caused by illegal activities such as occupation, mining, reclamation, landfill, or discharge of pollutants, the violator shall be responsible for the restoration. If the violator changes, the entity that succeeds to its claims and debts shall be responsible for the restoration.

Where wetland damage is caused by major natural disasters, or where the entity responsible for wetland restoration has ceased to exist or cannot be identified, the people's government at or above the county level shall organize the implementation of the restoration.

## Chapter V: Supervision and Inspection

**Article 45** The competent departments of forestry and grassland, natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural construction, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and their respective responsibilities, supervise and inspect activities such as the protection, restoration, and utilization of wetlands, and investigate and deal with illegal acts that damage wetlands in accordance with the law.

**Article 46** When conducting supervision and inspection, the competent departments of forestry and grassland, natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural construction, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level shall have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Inquire of the entity or individual under inspection and require them to provide explanations regarding matters related to the supervision and inspection;
(2) Conduct on-site inspections;
(3) Review and copy relevant documents and materials, and seal up documents and materials that may be transferred, destroyed, concealed, or altered;
(4) Seal up or seize premises, facilities, or property suspected of being involved in illegal activities.

**Article 47** When the competent departments of forestry and grassland, natural resources, water administration, housing and urban-rural construction, ecological environment, and agriculture and rural affairs of the people's government at or above the county level perform their supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law, the relevant entities and individuals shall cooperate and shall not refuse or obstruct them.

**Article 48** The competent forestry and grassland department of the State Council shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the protection of nationally important wetlands. The competent forestry and grassland departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the protection of provincially important wetlands.

The competent forestry and grassland department and relevant departments of the people's government at the county level shall make full use of information technology to supervise and inspect the protection of wetlands.

People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall disclose information related to wetland protection in accordance with the law and accept social supervision.

**Article 49** The state implements a target responsibility system for wetland protection, incorporating wetland protection into the comprehensive performance evaluation of local people's governments.

For regions where problems of wetland damage are prominent, protection efforts are weak, and public complaints are strong, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall, together with relevant departments, interview the principal responsible persons of the local people's government.

**Article 50** The protection, restoration, and management of wetlands shall be included in the audit of natural resource assets of leading cadres upon their departure from office.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 51** If the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level discover illegal acts that damage wetlands or receive reports of such illegal acts but fail to investigate or fail to investigate in accordance with the law, or engage in other acts of dereliction of duty, abuse of power, or favoritism, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 52** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a construction project occupies a nationally important wetland without authorization, the competent forestry and grassland and other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for demolishing the buildings, structures, and other facilities newly constructed on the illegally occupied wetland, restore the wetland or take other remedial measures, and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan per square meter based on the area of the illegally occupied wetland; if the violator does not cease construction or fails to demolish within the deadline, the department that made the administrative penalty decision shall apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement in accordance with the law.

**Article 53** If a construction project occupies an important wetland but fails to restore or rebuild the wetland in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order restoration or rebuilding of the wetland within a specified period; if the violator fails to rectify within the specified period, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall entrust another party to carry out the restoration, with the costs borne by the violator, and impose a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan per square meter based on the area of the occupied wetland.

**Article 54** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if natural wetlands are reclaimed (enclosed) or filled, the competent forestry and grassland and other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for restoring the wetland or taking other remedial measures, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 5,000 yuan per square meter based on the area of the damaged wetland; if a nationally important wetland is damaged, a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan per square meter shall be imposed based on the area of the damaged wetland.

In violation of the provisions of this Law, if natural wetlands are drained or the water source of a natural wetland is permanently cut off, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for restoring the wetland or taking other remedial measures, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 55** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if alien species are introduced or released into a wetland, the matter shall be handled and punished in accordance with the provisions of the Biosecurity Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and regulations.

**Article 56** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if ponds are dug within a mangrove wetland, the competent forestry and grassland and other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for restoring the wetland or taking other remedial measures, and impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan per square meter based on the area of the damaged wetland; if trees are damaged, the violator shall be ordered to replant and ensure the survival of a number of trees not less than one time but not more than three times the number of damaged trees within a specified period; if the number of damaged trees cannot be determined, the number shall be calculated based on the growth density of the same tree species in the same area.

In violation of the provisions of this Law, if species that hinder the growth of mangroves are released or planted within a mangrove wetland, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for cleaning up, and impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if serious consequences are caused, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 57** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if peat is mined, the competent forestry and grassland and other relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for restoring the wetland or taking other remedial measures, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan per cubic meter based on the volume of peat mined.

In violation of the provisions of this Law, if water is drained from a peat swamp wetland, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for restoring the wetland or taking other remedial measures, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan shall be imposed.

**Article 58** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if a wetland is restored without preparing a restoration plan or not in accordance with the restoration plan, causing damage to the wetland, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall order rectification and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan.

**Article 59** If the violator who damages a wetland fails to restore the wetland within the specified period or in accordance with the restoration plan, the competent forestry and grassland department of the people's government at or above the county level shall entrust another party to carry out the restoration, with the costs borne by the violator; if the violator loses the ability to restore due to reasons such as being declared bankrupt, the people's government at or above the county level shall organize the implementation of the restoration.

**Article 60** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if supervision and inspection conducted in accordance with the law by the relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level are refused or obstructed, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the violator may be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification.

**Article 61** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if damage is caused to the ecological environment, the organs designated by the state or the organizations stipulated by law shall have the right to request the violator to bear the responsibility for restoration, compensate for losses, and pay related expenses in accordance with the law.

**Article 62** In violation of the provisions of this Law, if an act constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose a public security administration penalty in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 63** The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Mangrove wetland" refers to coastal and intertidal wetlands dominated by mangrove plants;
(2) "Peat swamp wetland" refers to swamp wetlands where peat has developed.

**Article 64** Provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, cities divided into districts, and autonomous prefectures may formulate specific measures for wetland protection based on local conditions.

**Article 65** This Law shall come into force on June 1, 2022.
