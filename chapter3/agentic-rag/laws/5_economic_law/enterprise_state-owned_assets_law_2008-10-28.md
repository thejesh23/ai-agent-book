# Law of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise State-Owned Assets

Adopted at the 5th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on October 28, 2008

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of maintaining the basic economic system of the state, consolidating and developing the state-owned economy, strengthening the protection of state-owned assets, giving play to the leading role of the state-owned economy in the national economy, and promoting the development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, enterprise state-owned assets (hereinafter referred to as state-owned assets) refer to the rights and interests formed by the state through various forms of capital contribution to enterprises.

Article 3 State-owned assets belong to the state, i.e., to the whole people. The State Council exercises the ownership of state-owned assets on behalf of the state.

Article 4 The State Council and local people's governments shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the state with respect to state-invested enterprises and enjoy the rights and interests of capital contributors.

For large state-invested enterprises that are determined by the State Council to be vital to the lifeline of the national economy and national security, and state-invested enterprises in important infrastructure, important natural resources, and other fields, the State Council shall perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the state. For other state-invested enterprises, local people's governments shall perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the state.

Article 5 For the purposes of this Law, state-invested enterprises refer to wholly state-owned enterprises, wholly state-owned companies, and state-owned capital holding companies and state-owned capital participating companies in which the state has made capital contributions.

Article 6 The State Council and local people's governments shall perform the duties of capital contributors in accordance with the law, adhering to the principles of separating government functions from enterprise management, separating social public management functions from the functions of state-owned asset capital contributors, and not interfering with the lawful autonomous operation of enterprises.

Article 7 The state shall take measures to promote the concentration of state-owned capital in important industries and key fields vital to the lifeline of the national economy and national security, optimize the layout and structure of the state-owned economy, promote the reform and development of state-owned enterprises, improve the overall quality of the state-owned economy, and enhance the control and influence of the state-owned economy.

Article 8 The state shall establish and improve a state-owned asset management and supervision system that is compatible with the requirements of the development of the socialist market economy, establish and improve an assessment system for the preservation and appreciation of state-owned assets and a responsibility investigation system, and implement the responsibility for the preservation and appreciation of state-owned assets.

Article 9 The state shall establish and improve a basic management system for state-owned assets. Specific measures shall be formulated in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Article 10 State-owned assets are protected by law, and no unit or individual may infringe upon them.

## Chapter II Institutions Performing the Duties of Capital Contributors

Article 11 The state-owned assets supervision and administration institution of the State Council and the state-owned assets supervision and administration institutions established by local people's governments in accordance with the provisions of the State Council shall, under the authorization of the people's government at the corresponding level, perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the people's government at the corresponding level with respect to state-invested enterprises.

The State Council and local people's governments may, as needed, authorize other departments or institutions to perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the people's government at the corresponding level with respect to state-invested enterprises.

The institutions and departments that perform the duties of capital contributors on behalf of the people's government at the corresponding level are hereinafter collectively referred to as institutions performing the duties of capital contributors.

Article 12 An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall, on behalf of the people's government at the corresponding level, enjoy the rights of capital contributors with respect to state-invested enterprises in accordance with the law, including the right to asset returns, participation in major decisions, and selection of managers.

An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, formulate or participate in the formulation of the articles of association of state-invested enterprises.

An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall report to the people's government at the corresponding level for approval any major matter in performing the duties of capital contributors that, as stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the people's government at the corresponding level, requires approval by the people's government at the corresponding level.

Article 13 A shareholder representative appointed by an institution performing the duties of capital contributors to attend a shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of a state-owned capital holding company or a state-owned capital participating company shall, in accordance with the instructions of the appointing institution, submit proposals, express opinions, and exercise voting rights, and shall promptly report to the appointing institution on the performance of his or her duties and the results thereof.

Article 14 An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall perform the duties of capital contributors in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, protect the rights and interests of the capital contributor, and prevent losses of state-owned assets.

An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall safeguard the rights lawfully enjoyed by the enterprise as a market entity and shall not interfere in the business activities of the enterprise except when performing the duties of capital contributors in accordance with the law.

Article 15 An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall be responsible to the people's government at the corresponding level, report to the people's government at the corresponding level on the performance of its duties as a capital contributor, accept the supervision and assessment of the people's government at the corresponding level, and be responsible for the preservation and appreciation of state-owned assets.

An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, regularly report to the people's government at the corresponding level on the aggregate, structure, changes, returns, and other summary analysis of state-owned assets.

## Chapter III State-Invested Enterprises

Article 16 A state-invested enterprise shall enjoy the rights to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of its movable property, immovable property, and other property in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise.

The operational autonomy and other lawful rights and interests lawfully enjoyed by a state-invested enterprise shall be protected by law.

Article 17 A state-invested enterprise, when engaging in business activities, shall comply with laws and administrative regulations, strengthen business management, improve economic efficiency, accept the management and supervision lawfully exercised by the people's government and its relevant departments and institutions, accept the supervision of the public, assume social responsibilities, and be responsible to the capital contributor.

A state-invested enterprise shall establish and improve a corporate governance structure in accordance with the law, and establish and improve internal supervision, management, and risk control systems.

Article 18 A state-invested enterprise shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the financial department of the State Council, establish and improve financial and accounting systems, set up accounting books, conduct accounting, and provide true and complete financial and accounting information to the capital contributor in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise.

A state-invested enterprise shall distribute profits to the capital contributor in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise.

Article 19 A wholly state-owned company, a state-owned capital holding company, and a state-owned capital participating company shall establish a board of supervisors in accordance with the provisions of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China. For a wholly state-owned enterprise, the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall appoint supervisors to form a board of supervisors in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

The board of supervisors of a state-invested enterprise shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, supervise the performance of duties by directors and senior management personnel, and supervise and inspect the enterprise's finances.

Article 20 A state-invested enterprise shall, in accordance with the provisions of the law, implement democratic management through the staff and workers' congress or other forms.
Article 21 A state-invested enterprise shall, in accordance with the law, enjoy the rights of a capital contributor with respect to the enterprises in which it has invested, including the right to asset returns, participation in major decisions, and selection of managers.

A state-invested enterprise shall, with respect to the enterprises in which it has invested, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, establish an internal supervision, management, and risk control system with clear rights and responsibilities and effective checks and balances by formulating or participating in the formulation of the articles of association of the invested enterprise, and safeguard its rights and interests as a capital contributor.

## Chapter IV Selection and Assessment of Managers of State-Invested Enterprises

Article 22 An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall, in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, appoint or recommend the appointment of the following personnel of a state-invested enterprise:

(1) Appoint the manager, deputy managers, financial officer, and other senior management personnel of a wholly state-owned enterprise;
(2) Appoint the chairman, vice-chairman, directors, chairman of the board of supervisors, and supervisors of a wholly state-owned company;
(3) Propose candidates for directors and supervisors to the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of a state-owned capital holding company or a state-owned capital participating company.

Directors and supervisors who are to be held by employee representatives in a state-invested enterprise shall be democratically elected by the employees in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 23 Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel appointed or recommended for appointment by an institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Have good conduct;
(2) Have the professional knowledge and work ability required for the position;
(3) Have the physical condition to perform duties normally;
(4) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

If a director, supervisor, or senior management personnel, during their term of office, fails to meet the conditions specified in the preceding paragraph or falls under the circumstances stipulated in the Company Law of the People's Republic of China that disqualify a person from serving as a director, supervisor, or senior management personnel of a company, the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall dismiss them or propose their dismissal in accordance with the law.

Article 24 An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall investigate the candidates for directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel to be appointed or recommended for appointment in accordance with the prescribed conditions and procedures. Those who pass the investigation shall be appointed or recommended for appointment in accordance with the prescribed authority and procedures.

Article 25 Without the consent of the institution performing the duties of capital contributors, directors and senior management personnel of a wholly state-owned enterprise or a wholly state-owned company shall not hold concurrent positions in other enterprises. Without the consent of the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders, directors and senior management personnel of a state-owned capital holding company or a state-owned capital participating company shall not hold concurrent positions in other enterprises engaged in the same type of business.

Without the consent of the institution performing the duties of capital contributors, the chairman of a wholly state-owned company shall not concurrently serve as the manager. Without the consent of the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders, the chairman of a state-owned capital holding company shall not concurrently serve as the manager.

Directors and senior management personnel shall not concurrently serve as supervisors.

Article 26 Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel of a state-invested enterprise shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, owe a duty of loyalty and a duty of diligence to the enterprise, shall not use their positions to accept bribes or obtain other illegal income or improper benefits, shall not embezzle or misappropriate enterprise assets, shall not decide on major matters of the enterprise beyond their authority or in violation of procedures, and shall not engage in any other acts that infringe upon the rights and interests of the capital contributor of state-owned assets.

Article 27 The state shall establish a performance assessment system for the managers of state-invested enterprises. An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall conduct annual and term assessments of the enterprise managers it appoints, and shall decide on rewards and penalties for the enterprise managers based on the assessment results.

An institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the state, determine the compensation standards for the managers of state-invested enterprises it appoints.

Article 28 The principal responsible persons of a wholly state-owned enterprise, a wholly state-owned company, and a state-owned capital holding company shall undergo a term economic responsibility audit conducted in accordance with the law.

Article 29 Where the State Council or a local people's government stipulates that the enterprise managers specified in items (1) and (2) of the first paragraph of Article 22 of this Law shall be appointed or removed by the people's government at the corresponding level, such provisions shall prevail. The institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall conduct assessments, rewards, and penalties for the aforementioned enterprise managers and determine their compensation standards in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.

## Chapter V Major Matters Concerning the Rights and Interests of Capital Contributors of State-Owned Assets

### Section 1 General Provisions

Article 30 Major matters of a state-invested enterprise, such as merger, division, restructuring, listing, increase or decrease of registered capital, issuance of bonds, making significant investments, providing large guarantees for others, transferring major property, making large donations, distributing profits, dissolution, and applying for bankruptcy, shall comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, and shall not harm the rights and interests of the capital contributor and creditors.

Article 31 The merger, division, increase or decrease of registered capital, issuance of bonds, distribution of profits, dissolution, and application for bankruptcy of a wholly state-owned enterprise or a wholly state-owned company shall be decided by the institution performing the duties of capital contributors.

Article 32 For matters listed in Article 30 of this Law concerning a wholly state-owned enterprise or a wholly state-owned company, except for those decided by the institution performing the duties of capital contributors in accordance with Article 31 of this Law and relevant laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, a wholly state-owned enterprise shall decide by collective discussion of its responsible persons, and a wholly state-owned company shall decide by its board of directors.

Article 33 For matters listed in Article 30 of this Law concerning a state-owned capital holding company or a state-owned capital participating company, the shareholders' meeting, general meeting of shareholders, or board of directors of the company shall decide in accordance with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and the company's articles of association. If the decision is made by the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders, the shareholder representative appointed by the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall exercise rights in accordance with the provisions of Article 13 of this Law.

Article 34 For the merger, division, dissolution, application for bankruptcy of an important wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company, and other major matters that, as stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the people's government at the corresponding level, require the institution performing the duties of capital contributors to report to the people's government at the corresponding level for approval, the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall report to the people's government at the corresponding level for approval before making a decision or issuing instructions to the shareholder representative it appoints to attend the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of the state-owned capital holding company.

The important wholly state-owned enterprises, wholly state-owned companies, and state-owned capital holding companies referred to in this Law shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

Article 35 Where relevant laws and administrative regulations stipulate that matters such as the issuance of bonds or investments by a state-invested enterprise require approval, verification, or filing by the people's government or relevant departments or institutions of the people's government, such provisions shall prevail.

Article 36 Investments by a state-invested enterprise shall comply with the national industrial policy and shall be subject to feasibility studies in accordance with national regulations; transactions with others shall be fair and for consideration, obtaining reasonable consideration.

Article 37 For major matters such as merger, division, restructuring, dissolution, and application for bankruptcy of a state-invested enterprise, the opinions of the enterprise's trade union shall be solicited, and the opinions and suggestions of the employees shall be solicited through the staff and workers' congress or other forms.
Article 38 A wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company shall, with respect to major matters of the enterprises in which it has invested, perform the duties of a capital contributor by reference to the provisions of this Chapter. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

### Section 2 Enterprise Restructuring

Article 39 For the purposes of this Law, enterprise restructuring refers to:

(1) Changing a wholly state-owned enterprise into a wholly state-owned company;
(2) Changing a wholly state-owned enterprise or a wholly state-owned company into a state-owned capital holding company or a non-state-owned capital holding company;
(3) Changing a state-owned capital holding company into a non-state-owned capital holding company.

Article 40 Enterprise restructuring shall be decided by the institution performing the duties of capital contributors or by the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of the company in accordance with legal procedures.

For the restructuring of an important wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company, the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall submit the restructuring plan to the people's government at the corresponding level for approval before making a decision or issuing instructions to the shareholder representative it appoints to attend the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of the state-owned capital holding company.

Article 41 An enterprise restructuring plan shall be formulated, specifying the organizational form of the enterprise after restructuring, the plan for handling enterprise assets and creditor's rights and debts, the plan for equity changes, the operating procedures for restructuring, and the selection of intermediary institutions such as those for asset appraisal and financial audit.

If the enterprise restructuring involves the re-placement of enterprise employees, an employee placement plan shall also be formulated and submitted to the staff and workers' congress or the general meeting of employees for review and approval.
Article 42 Enterprise restructuring shall, in accordance with regulations, conduct asset verification, financial audit, and asset appraisal, accurately define and verify assets, and objectively and fairly determine the value of assets.

If the enterprise restructuring involves converting non-monetary property such as physical assets, intellectual property rights, or land use rights of the enterprise into state-owned capital contributions or shares, the property to be converted shall be appraised in accordance with regulations, and the appraised price shall serve as the basis for determining the amount of state-owned capital contributions or the number of shares. No property shall be converted into shares at a low price, and no other acts that harm the rights and interests of the capital contributor shall be carried out.

### Section 3 Transactions with Related Parties

Article 43 A related party of a state-invested enterprise shall not use transactions with the state-invested enterprise to seek improper benefits or harm the interests of the state-invested enterprise.

For the purposes of this Law, related parties refer to the directors, supervisors, senior management personnel of the enterprise and their close relatives, as well as enterprises owned or actually controlled by these persons.

Article 44 A wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company shall not provide funds, goods, services, or other assets to related parties free of charge, and shall not conduct transactions with related parties at unfair prices.

Article 45 Without the consent of the institution performing the duties of capital contributors, a wholly state-owned enterprise or a wholly state-owned company shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Conclude agreements on property transfer or loans with related parties;
(2) Provide guarantees for related parties;
(3) Establish enterprises jointly with related parties, or invest in enterprises owned or actually controlled by directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or their close relatives.

Article 46 Transactions between a state-owned capital holding company or a state-owned capital participating company and its related parties shall be decided by the shareholders' meeting, general meeting of shareholders, or board of directors of the company in accordance with the provisions of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China, relevant administrative regulations, and the company's articles of association. If the decision is made by the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders, the shareholder representative appointed by the institution performing the duties of capital contributors shall exercise rights in accordance with the provisions of Article 13 of this Law.

When the board of directors of a company makes a resolution on a transaction between the company and a related party, the director involved in the transaction shall not exercise voting rights, nor shall they act as a proxy for other directors to exercise voting rights.

### Section 4 Asset Appraisal

Article 47 When a wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company merges, divides, restructures, transfers major property, makes external investments with non-monetary property, goes into liquidation, or has other circumstances requiring asset appraisal as stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the articles of association of the enterprise, the relevant assets shall be appraised in accordance with regulations.

Article 48 A wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company shall entrust a qualified asset appraisal institution established in accordance with the law to conduct the asset appraisal; if the matter involves reporting to the institution performing the duties of capital contributors for a decision, the situation of entrusting the asset appraisal institution shall be reported to the institution performing the duties of capital contributors.

Article 49 A wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-owned capital holding company, and its directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel shall truthfully provide relevant information and materials to the asset appraisal institution, and shall not collude with the asset appraisal institution in appraising and pricing.

Article 50 An asset appraisal institution and its staff, when entrusted to appraise relevant assets, shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and appraisal practice standards, and conduct the appraisal of the entrusted assets independently, objectively, and fairly. The asset appraisal institution shall be responsible for the appraisal report it issues.

### Section 5 Transfer of State-Owned Assets

Article 51 For the purposes of this Law, transfer of state-owned assets refers to the act of transferring the rights and interests formed by the state's capital contribution to an enterprise to other units or individuals in accordance with the law; excluding the gratuitous transfer of state-owned assets in accordance with national regulations.

Article 52 The transfer of state-owned assets shall be conducive to the strategic adjustment of the layout and structure of the state-owned economy, prevent losses of state-owned assets, and shall not harm the lawful rights and interests of the parties to the transaction.**Article 53** The transfer of state-owned assets shall be decided by the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor. Where the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor decides to transfer all state-owned assets, or to transfer part of the state-owned assets so that the state no longer holds a controlling stake in the enterprise, the matter shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval.

**Article 54** The transfer of state-owned assets shall follow the principles of equivalent compensation, openness, fairness, and justice.

Unless otherwise provided by the state for direct negotiation transfer, the transfer of state-owned assets shall be conducted publicly on lawfully established property rights trading venues. The transferor shall truthfully disclose relevant information and solicit transferees; where there are two or more transferees resulting from the solicitation, the transfer shall adopt an open bidding method.

The transfer of shares listed for trading shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China.

**Article 55** The transfer price of state-owned assets shall be reasonably determined based on a lawfully assessed value, which is recognized by the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor or approved by the people's government at the same level upon the submission of the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor, to establish a reasonable minimum transfer price.

**Article 56** Where laws, administrative regulations, or regulations of the state-owned assets supervision and administration authority of the State Council permit the transfer of state-owned assets to directors, supervisors, senior management personnel of the enterprise, or their close relatives, or to enterprises owned or actually controlled by such persons, if such persons or enterprises participate in the transfer as transferees, they shall compete on an equal footing with other transferee participants; the transferor shall truthfully disclose relevant information in accordance with relevant state regulations; and the relevant directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel shall not participate in the formulation of the transfer plan or in any work related to its organization and implementation.

**Article 57** The transfer of state-owned assets to foreign investors shall comply with relevant state regulations and shall not harm national security or public interests.

## Chapter VI: Budget for State Capital Operations

**Article 58** The state shall establish and improve a budget system for state capital operations, implementing budgetary management over the income derived from state capital and its expenditures.

**Article 59** The following state capital income obtained by the state, as well as the expenditures from such income, shall be compiled into a budget for state capital operations:

(1) Profits distributed from state-invested enterprises;
(2) Income from the transfer of state-owned assets;
(3) Liquidation income obtained from state-invested enterprises;
(4) Other state capital income.

**Article 60** The budget for state capital operations shall be compiled separately on an annual basis, incorporated into the budget of the people's government at the same level, and submitted to the people's congress at the same level for approval.

Expenditures under the budget for state capital operations shall be arranged according to the scale of budget revenue for the current year, and no deficit shall be incurred.

**Article 61** The finance departments of the State Council and relevant local people's governments shall be responsible for drafting the budget for state capital operations. The institution performing the responsibilities of an investor shall submit a proposed draft budget for the state capital operations under its responsibility to the finance department.

**Article 62** Specific measures and implementation steps for the management of the budget for state capital operations shall be formulated by the State Council and reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for recordation.

## Chapter VII: Supervision of State-Owned Assets

**Article 63** The standing committees of the people's congresses at various levels shall exercise their supervisory powers in accordance with the law by hearing and deliberating special work reports from the people's governments at the same level on the performance of investor responsibilities and the supervision and management of state-owned assets, and by organizing law enforcement inspections on the implementation of this Law.

**Article 64** The State Council and local people's governments shall supervise the performance of duties by the institutions they have authorized to perform the responsibilities of an investor.

**Article 65** Audit authorities of the State Council and local people's governments shall, in accordance with the Audit Law of the People's Republic of China, conduct audit supervision over the implementation of the budget for state capital operations and over state-invested enterprises that are subject to audit supervision.

**Article 66** The State Council and local people's governments shall, in accordance with the law, publicly disclose the status of state-owned assets and the situation of state-owned assets supervision and management work to society, accepting public supervision.

Any entity or individual has the right to report and accuse acts that cause losses to state-owned assets.

**Article 67** The institution performing the responsibilities of an investor may, as needed, entrust an accounting firm to audit the annual financial and accounting reports of wholly state-owned enterprises and wholly state-owned companies, or, through a resolution of the shareholders' meeting or general meeting of shareholders of a state-controlled company, have the state-controlled company engage an accounting firm to audit its annual financial and accounting reports, in order to protect the rights and interests of the investor.

## Chapter VIII: Legal Liability

**Article 68** Where an institution performing the responsibilities of an investor commits any of the following acts, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Appointing or recommending the appointment of managers of state-invested enterprises not in accordance with the statutory qualifications;
(2) Embezzling, intercepting, or misappropriating funds of state-invested enterprises or state capital income that should be turned over;
(3) Deciding on major matters of state-invested enterprises in violation of statutory authority or procedures, causing losses to state-owned assets;
(4) Other acts of failing to perform investor responsibilities in accordance with the law, causing losses to state-owned assets.

**Article 69** Where staff members of the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor are derelict in their duties, abuse their power, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, if the act does not constitute a crime, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 70** Where a shareholder representative appointed by the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor fails to perform their duties in accordance with the instructions of the appointing institution, causing losses to state-owned assets, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law; if they are a state functionary, they shall also be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

**Article 71** Where directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a state-invested enterprise commit any of the following acts, causing losses to state-owned assets, they shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law; if they are state functionaries, they shall also be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Using their position to accept bribes or obtain other illegal income or improper benefits;
(2) Embezzling or misappropriating enterprise assets;
(3) During processes such as enterprise restructuring or property transfer, violating laws, administrative regulations, and fair trading rules by transferring enterprise property at a low price or converting it into shares at a low price;
(4) Conducting transactions with the enterprise in violation of the provisions of this Law;
(5) Failing to truthfully provide relevant information and materials to asset appraisal institutions or accounting firms, or colluding with asset appraisal institutions or accounting firms to issue false asset appraisal reports or audit reports;
(6) Deciding on major matters of the enterprise in violation of the decision-making procedures stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, or the enterprise's articles of association;
(7) Other acts of performing duties in violation of laws, administrative regulations, or the enterprise's articles of association.

Any income obtained by directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a state-invested enterprise as a result of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph shall be recovered in accordance with the law or shall belong to the state-invested enterprise.

Where directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel appointed or recommended for appointment by the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor commit any of the acts listed in the first paragraph of this Article, causing significant losses to state-owned assets, the institution performing the responsibilities of an investor shall remove them from their positions or propose their removal in accordance with the law.

**Article 72** In transaction activities involving related-party transactions or the transfer of state-owned assets, if the parties maliciously collude to harm the rights and interests of state-owned assets, the transaction shall be void.

**Article 73** Where directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-controlled company violate the provisions of this Law, causing significant losses to state-owned assets, and are removed from their positions, they shall not serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of any wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-controlled company for five years from the date of removal; where they cause particularly significant losses to state-owned assets, or are sentenced to criminal punishment for embezzlement, bribery, misappropriation of property, or disrupting the socialist market economic order, they shall be permanently disqualified from serving as directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of any wholly state-owned enterprise, wholly state-owned company, or state-controlled company.

**Article 74** Where an asset appraisal institution or accounting firm entrusted to conduct asset appraisal or financial auditing of a state-invested enterprise violates the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, and professional standards by issuing a false asset appraisal report or audit report, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 75** Whoever violates the provisions of this Law, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 76** Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for the management and supervision of state-owned assets of financial enterprises, those provisions shall prevail.

**Article 77** This Law shall come into effect as of May 1, 2009.
