# Agricultural Mechanization Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on June 25, 2004

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws Including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of encouraging and supporting farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to use advanced and applicable agricultural machinery, promoting agricultural mechanization, and building modern agriculture.

Article 2 For the purpose of this Law, agricultural mechanization means the process of using advanced and applicable agricultural machinery to equip agriculture, improve agricultural production and operation conditions, and continuously raise the technical level of agricultural production as well as economic and ecological benefits.

For the purpose of this Law, agricultural machinery means the machinery and equipment used in agricultural production, primary processing of agricultural products, and related agricultural activities.

Article 3 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the promotion of agricultural mechanization into their plans for national economic and social development, adopt measures such as financial support, implementation of tax preferential policies prescribed by the State, and financial support, gradually increase funding for agricultural mechanization, give full play to the role of the market mechanism, and promote the development of agricultural mechanization in accordance with the principles of adapting measures to local conditions, being economically effective, ensuring safety, and protecting the environment.

Article 4 The State guides and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in independently selecting advanced and applicable agricultural machinery. No unit or individual may compel farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations to purchase designated agricultural machinery products.

Article 5 The State takes measures to carry out publicity and education on agricultural mechanization science and technology, train professionals in agricultural mechanization, promote information services for agricultural mechanization, and raise the level of agricultural mechanization.

Article 6 The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and other departments responsible for work related to agricultural mechanization shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, work closely together to jointly promote agricultural mechanization.

The departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the local people's governments at or above the county level and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, work closely together to jointly promote agricultural mechanization within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Scientific Research and Development

Article 7 People's governments at or above the provincial level and their relevant departments shall organize relevant units to take measures such as technological breakthroughs, experiments, and demonstrations to promote basic, key, and public-interest scientific research on agricultural machinery and the popularization and application of advanced and applicable agricultural machinery.

Article 8 The State supports relevant scientific research institutions and colleges and universities in strengthening scientific and technological research on agricultural mechanization, researching and developing advanced and applicable agricultural machinery based on different agricultural production conditions and farmers' needs; supports the integration of agricultural machinery research, teaching, production, and promotion, and promotes the adaptation of agricultural machinery to the development requirements of agricultural production technology.

Article 9 The State supports agricultural machinery producers in developing advanced and applicable agricultural machinery, adopting advanced technologies, processes, and materials, improving the quality and technical level of agricultural machinery products, reducing production costs, and providing agricultural machinery products that are serialized, standardized, multifunctional, of excellent quality, energy-saving, and reasonably priced.

Article 10 The State supports the introduction and utilization of advanced agricultural machinery, key parts and components, and technologies, and encourages the introduction of foreign investment in the research, development, production, and operation of agricultural machinery.

## Chapter III Quality Assurance

Article 11 The State strengthens the construction of the agricultural mechanization standard system, formulating and improving standards for the quality of agricultural machinery products, repair quality, and operation quality. Technical requirements for agricultural machinery products involving personal safety, the quality and safety of agricultural products, and environmental protection shall be formulated as mandatory technical specifications in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 12 The market supervision and administration department shall, in accordance with the law, organize supervision and spot checks on the quality of agricultural machinery products and strengthen the supervision and management of the agricultural machinery product market.

The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and the departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the provincial people's governments may, based on complaints from agricultural machinery users and the actual needs of agricultural production, organize investigations into the applicability, safety, reliability, and after-sales service status of specific types of agricultural machinery products in use, and publish the investigation results.

Article 13 Agricultural machinery producers and sellers shall be responsible for the quality of the agricultural machinery products they produce or sell, and shall bear the responsibility for after-sales services such as the supply of spare parts and training in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Agricultural machinery producers shall, in accordance with national standards, industry standards, and requirements for ensuring personal safety, install necessary safety protection devices, warning signs, and Chinese warning instructions on the agricultural machinery products they produce.

Article 14 If agricultural machinery products do not meet quality requirements, the agricultural machinery producer or seller shall be responsible for repair, replacement, or return; if losses are caused to agricultural production or other losses to the agricultural machinery user, compensation shall be made in accordance with the law. The agricultural machinery user has the right to request the agricultural machinery seller to provide compensation first. After the agricultural machinery seller has compensated, if the liability lies with the agricultural machinery producer, the agricultural machinery seller has the right to seek recourse from the agricultural machinery producer.

If personal injury or property damage is caused by defects in agricultural machinery products, the agricultural machinery producer or seller shall compensate for the losses in accordance with the law.

Article 15 Agricultural machinery products included in the product catalog subject to mandatory certification according to law shall not be shipped from the factory, sold, or imported without certification and the marking of the certification mark.

It is prohibited to produce or sell agricultural machinery products that do not meet the mandatory requirements of national technical specifications.

It is prohibited to assemble agricultural machinery products using substandard spare parts or components from scrapped machinery.

## Chapter IV Popularization and Use

Article 16 The State supports the popularization of advanced and applicable agricultural machinery products to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations. The popularization of agricultural machinery products shall be adapted to the needs of local agricultural development and, in accordance with the provisions of the Law on the Popularization of Agricultural Technology, shall be proven to be advanced and applicable through experiments in the popularization area.

Agricultural machinery producers or sellers may entrust agricultural machinery testing and appraisal institutions to conduct tests on the applicability, safety, and reliability of their finalized or sold agricultural machinery products and to make technical evaluations. Agricultural machinery testing and appraisal institutions shall publish the test results of agricultural machinery products with proven applicability, safety, and reliability, providing information for farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to select and purchase advanced and applicable agricultural machinery.

Article 17 People's governments at or above the county level may, based on actual conditions, establish agricultural mechanization demonstration bases in different agricultural regions, and encourage agricultural machinery producers, operators, etc., to establish agricultural machinery demonstration sites to guide farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in using advanced and applicable agricultural machinery.

Article 18 The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the finance department under the State Council and the comprehensive economic macro-control department, determine and publish a catalog of advanced and applicable agricultural machinery products supported for popularization by the State, based on the principles of promoting agricultural structural adjustment, protecting natural resources and the ecological environment, popularizing new agricultural technologies, and accelerating the renewal of agricultural machinery, and shall adjust it periodically. The departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the provincial people's governments shall, in conjunction with the finance departments at the same level and the comprehensive economic macro-control departments, determine and publish a catalog of advanced and applicable agricultural machinery products supported for popularization by the provincial people's governments based on the above principles, and shall adjust it periodically.

Products included in the catalog mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be voluntarily applied for by the agricultural machinery producer and shall pass the appraisal of advancement, applicability, safety, and reliability conducted by an agricultural machinery testing and appraisal institution.

Article 19 The State encourages and supports farmers in cooperatively using agricultural machinery to increase the utilization rate and operational efficiency of agricultural machinery and reduce operational costs.

The State supports and protects farmers, on the basis of adhering to the household contract management system, in voluntarily organizing regionalized and standardized planting to improve the operational level of agricultural machinery. No unit or individual may, under the pretext of regionalized or standardized planting, infringe upon farmers' land contract management rights.

Article 20 The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council and the departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the policy of putting safety first and focusing on prevention, strengthen the publicity, education, and management of the safe use of agricultural machinery.

When operating agricultural machinery, users shall operate the machinery in accordance with safe operating procedures and install protective devices or warning signs in dangerous parts and at the operation site.

## Chapter V Socialized Services

Article 21 Farmers and agricultural machinery operation organizations may, in accordance with the principles of mutual voluntariness and equal consultation, provide various paid agricultural machinery operation services to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations locally or in other areas. Paid agricultural machinery operations shall meet the national or local quality standards for agricultural machinery operations.

The State encourages the provision of agricultural machinery operation services across administrative regions. People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall support cross-administrative-region agricultural machinery operations, maintain operational order, provide convenience and services, and implement safety supervision and management in accordance with the law.

Article 22 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to encourage and support the development of various forms of agricultural machinery service organizations, promote the construction of agricultural mechanization information networks, and improve the agricultural mechanization service system. Agricultural machinery service organizations shall, based on the needs of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, provide socialized services such as agricultural machinery demonstration and promotion, practical technical training, repair, information, and intermediation.

Article 23 The State-established grassroots agricultural machinery technology promotion institutions shall, relying on experimental demonstration bases, provide farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations with free public-interest services for the promotion and training of agricultural machinery technology.

Article 24 Those engaged in agricultural machinery repair shall possess instruments and equipment appropriate for the repair business and technical personnel with vocational skills in agricultural machinery repair, and shall ensure repair quality. If the repair quality is unqualified, the repairer shall perform the repair again free of charge; if personal injury or property damage is caused, the repairer shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 25 Agricultural machinery producers, operators, and repairers may, in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations, voluntarily establish industry associations to practice industry self-discipline, provide services to members, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of members.

## Chapter VI Support Measures

Article 26 The State takes measures to encourage and support agricultural machinery producers in increasing investment in the research and development of new products, new technologies, and new processes, and implements tax preferential policies for the scientific research, development, and manufacturing of agricultural machinery.

Science and technology development funds arranged in the central and local fiscal budgets shall provide support for technological innovation in the agricultural machinery industry.

Article 27 The central finance and provincial finance shall respectively arrange special funds to provide subsidies to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations for purchasing advanced and applicable agricultural machinery supported for popularization by the State. The use of subsidy funds shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, timeliness, and effectiveness. The subsidies may be paid directly to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, or may be used in the form of interest subsidies to support financial institutions in providing loans to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations for purchasing advanced and applicable agricultural machinery. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 28 Income derived from providing agricultural machinery production operation services shall be granted tax preferences in accordance with State regulations.

The State, based on the needs of agricultural and rural economic development, arranges fiscal subsidies for fuel used in agricultural machinery for agricultural production operations. Fuel subsidies shall be paid to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations directly engaged in agricultural machinery operations. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 29 Local people's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen the construction and maintenance of agricultural mechanization infrastructure such as rural tractor roads, creating conditions for agricultural mechanization.

The departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a system for the collection, collation, and publication of agricultural mechanization information, providing free information services to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 30 Anyone who violates the provisions of Article 15 of this Law shall be punished in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Product Quality Law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Agricultural machinery drivers or operators who violate the safe operating procedures prescribed by the State and operate in violation of regulations shall be ordered to make corrections and punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 32 If an agricultural machinery testing and appraisal institution fails to conduct appraisals for agricultural machinery producers or sellers in accordance with regulations during the appraisal work, or forges appraisal results or issues false certificates, causing losses to agricultural machinery users, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 33 If the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council or the departments in charge of agricultural mechanization work under the local people's governments at or above the county level, in violation of the provisions of this Law, compel or covertly compel agricultural machinery producers or sellers to have their produced or sold agricultural machinery products appraised, the superior competent authority or the supervisory authority shall order them to make corrections within a specified time limit, and impose administrative sanctions on the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel.

Article 34 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Articles 27 and 28 of this Law, intercepts or misappropriates relevant subsidy funds shall be ordered by the superior competent authority to return the intercepted or misappropriated funds within a specified time limit, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the superior competent authority, the supervisory authority, or the unit to which the person belongs shall impose administrative sanctions on the directly responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 35 This Law shall come into force as of November 1, 2004.
