# Statistics Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Third Session of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on December 8, 1983

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Statistics Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Nineteenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on May 15, 1996

Revised at the Ninth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on June 27, 2009

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of organizing statistical work in a scientific and effective manner, ensuring the authenticity, accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of statistical data, giving full play to the important role of statistics in understanding national conditions and national strength, serving economic and social development, and promoting the development of the socialist modernization drive.

Article 2 This Law applies to statistical activities organized and implemented by the people's governments at all levels, the statistical institutions of the people's governments at or above the county level, and relevant departments.

The basic tasks of statistics are to conduct statistical surveys and statistical analyses of economic and social development, provide statistical data and statistical consulting opinions, and exercise statistical supervision.

Article 3 The State establishes a centralized and unified statistical system, implementing a statistical management system of unified leadership and graded responsibility.

Article 4 The State Council, local people's governments at all levels, and relevant departments shall strengthen the organizational leadership of statistical work and provide necessary guarantees for statistical work.

Article 5 The State strengthens statistical scientific research, improves the scientific statistical indicator system, continuously refines statistical survey methods, and enhances the scientific nature of statistics.

The State systematically strengthens the construction of statistical informatization, promoting the modernization of statistical information collection, processing, transmission, sharing, storage technologies, and the statistical database system.

Article 6 Statistical institutions and statisticians shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, independently exercise their functions and powers of statistical survey, statistical reporting, and statistical supervision, which shall not be infringed upon.

Leaders of local people's governments at all levels, government statistical institutions, relevant departments, and units shall not arbitrarily modify statistical data lawfully collected and compiled by statistical institutions and statisticians, shall not in any way require statistical institutions, statisticians, or other institutions or personnel to fabricate or tamper with statistical data, and shall not retaliate against statisticians who lawfully perform their duties or who refuse or resist statistical violations.

Article 7 State organs, enterprises, public institutions, other organizations, and individual business households, individuals, and other statistical survey respondents must, in accordance with this Law and relevant State regulations, provide the data required for statistical surveys truthfully, accurately, completely, and promptly. They shall not provide untrue or incomplete statistical data, and shall not delay reporting or refuse to report statistical data.

Article 8 Statistical work shall be subject to public supervision. Any unit or individual has the right to report violations of the law such as fraud in statistics. Units and individuals that have rendered meritorious service in reporting shall be commended and rewarded.

Article 9 Statistical institutions and statisticians shall keep confidential any State secrets, trade secrets, and personal information that come to their knowledge in the course of statistical work.

Article 10 No unit or individual may use false statistical data to obtain honorary titles, material benefits, or job promotions.

## Chapter II: Management of Statistical Surveys

Article 11 Statistical survey projects include national statistical survey projects, departmental statistical survey projects, and local statistical survey projects.

National statistical survey projects refer to statistical survey projects concerning basic national conditions. Departmental statistical survey projects refer to specialized statistical survey projects of relevant departments under the State Council. Local statistical survey projects refer to local statistical survey projects of local people's governments at or above the county level and their departments.

National statistical survey projects, departmental statistical survey projects, and local statistical survey projects shall have a clear division of labor, be interconnected, and shall not overlap.

Article 12 National statistical survey projects shall be formulated by the National Bureau of Statistics, or jointly formulated by the National Bureau of Statistics and relevant departments under the State Council, and reported to the State Council for record; major national statistical survey projects shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

Departmental statistical survey projects shall be formulated by relevant departments under the State Council. If the statistical survey respondents fall within the management system of the department, the project shall be reported to the National Bureau of Statistics for record; if the statistical survey respondents fall outside the management system of the department, the project shall be reported to the National Bureau of Statistics for approval.

Local statistical survey projects shall be formulated separately or jointly by the statistical institutions of local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments. Among them, those formulated solely by the statistical institution of the provincial people's government or jointly with relevant departments shall be reported to the National Bureau of Statistics for approval; those formulated solely by the statistical institution of a people's government below the provincial level or jointly with relevant departments shall be reported to the statistical institution of the provincial people's government for approval; those formulated by relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be reported to the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level for approval.

Article 13 The approving authority for a statistical survey project shall review the necessity, feasibility, and scientific nature of the survey project. If the statutory conditions are met, it shall make a written decision of approval and make it public; if the statutory conditions are not met, it shall make a written decision of disapproval and state the reasons.

Article 14 When formulating a statistical survey project, the statistical survey system for that project shall also be formulated, and shall be submitted for approval or record together in accordance with Article 12 of this Law.

The statistical survey system shall stipulate the survey purpose, survey content, survey method, survey respondents, survey organization method, survey form, and the submission and publication of statistical data.

Statistical surveys shall be organized and implemented in accordance with the statistical survey system. Any change to the content of the statistical survey system shall be reported to the original approving authority for approval or to the original filing authority for record.

Article 15 Statistical survey forms shall clearly indicate the form number, the formulating authority, the approval or filing document number, the period of validity, and other markings.

For statistical survey forms that do not bear the markings specified in the preceding paragraph or have exceeded their period of validity, statistical survey respondents have the right to refuse to fill them in; the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, order the cessation of the relevant statistical survey activities.

Article 16 The collection and compilation of statistical data shall be based on periodic censuses, with regular sample surveys as the main method, comprehensively utilizing methods such as comprehensive surveys and key surveys, and making full use of administrative records and other data.

Major censuses of national conditions and national strength shall be uniformly led by the State Council, and the State Council and local people's governments shall organize statistical institutions and relevant departments to implement them jointly.

Article 17 The State formulates unified statistical standards to ensure the standardization of indicator meanings, calculation methods, classification catalogs, survey forms, and statistical codes used in statistical surveys.

National statistical standards shall be formulated by the National Bureau of Statistics, or jointly formulated by the National Bureau of Statistics and the competent department of standardization under the State Council.

Relevant departments under the State Council may formulate supplementary departmental statistical standards, which shall be reported to the National Bureau of Statistics for approval. Departmental statistical standards shall not conflict with national statistical standards.

Article 18 The statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level may, based on the needs of statistical tasks, promote the use of computer networks for submitting statistical data among statistical survey respondents.

Article 19 The people's government at or above the county level shall include the necessary funds for statistical work in the fiscal budget.

The funds required for major censuses of national conditions and national strength shall be borne jointly by the State Council and local people's governments, included in the fiscal budgets of the corresponding years, allocated on time, and ensured to be in place.

## Chapter III: Management and Publication of Statistical Data

Article 20 The statistical institutions of the people's governments at or above the county level, relevant departments, and township and town people's governments shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, establish systems for the preservation and management of statistical data, and establish and improve mechanisms for sharing statistical information.

Article 21 State organs, enterprises, public institutions, other organizations, and other statistical survey respondents shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, set up original records and statistical ledgers, and establish and improve management systems for the review, signing, handover, and archiving of statistical data.

Personnel responsible for the review and signing of statistical data shall be responsible for the authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the statistical data they review and sign.

Article 22 Relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall promptly provide the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level with administrative record data required for statistics and financial data, fiscal data, and other data required for national economic accounting, and shall promptly submit to the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level the relevant data obtained from the statistical surveys they organize and implement, in accordance with the provisions of the statistical survey system.

The statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall promptly provide relevant statistical data to the relevant departments of the people's government at the same level.

Article 23 The statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall regularly publish statistical data in accordance with relevant State regulations.

National statistical data shall be based on the data published by the National Bureau of Statistics.

Article 24 Statistical data obtained from statistical surveys conducted by relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall be published by the department in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 25 No unit or individual may provide or disclose to the outside, or use for purposes other than statistics, any data obtained in statistical surveys that can identify or infer the identity of an individual statistical survey respondent.

Article 26 Statistical data obtained from statistical surveys conducted by the statistical institutions of the people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall be made public promptly for public inquiry, except for data that should be kept confidential in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IV: Statistical Institutions and Statisticians

Article 27 The State Council establishes the National Bureau of Statistics, which shall, in accordance with the law, organize, lead, and coordinate statistical work nationwide.

The survey agencies dispatched by the National Bureau of Statistics as needed shall undertake tasks such as statistical surveys assigned by the National Bureau of Statistics.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish independent statistical institutions. Township and town people's governments shall set up statistical work positions, equipped with full-time or part-time statisticians, to manage and carry out statistical work and conduct statistical surveys in accordance with the law.

Article 28 Relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of statistical tasks, establish statistical institutions, or set up statisticians in relevant institutions, and designate a person in charge of statistics, to organize and manage statistical work within their scope of duties in accordance with the law, conduct statistical surveys, and be subject to the guidance of the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level in statistical operations.

Article 29 Statistical institutions and statisticians shall perform their duties in accordance with the law, truthfully collect and submit statistical data, shall not fabricate or tamper with statistical data, shall not in any way require any unit or individual to provide untrue statistical data, and shall not engage in any other acts violating the provisions of this Law.

Statisticians shall adhere to seeking truth from facts, abide by professional ethics, and be responsible for the consistency between the statistical data they are responsible for collecting, reviewing, and entering and the statistical data submitted by statistical survey respondents.

Article 30 When conducting statistical surveys, statisticians have the right to question relevant personnel on matters related to statistics, require them to truthfully provide relevant information and data, and correct untrue or inaccurate data.

When conducting statistical surveys, statisticians shall present their work credentials issued by the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level or relevant departments; if they fail to do so, the statistical survey respondents have the right to refuse the survey.

Article 31 The State implements an examination, evaluation, and appointment system for professional technical qualifications in statistics to improve the professional quality of statisticians and ensure the stability of the statistical workforce.

Statisticians shall possess the professional knowledge and business capabilities commensurate with the statistical work they engage in.

The statistical institutions of the people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall strengthen professional training and professional ethics education for statisticians.

## Chapter V: Supervision and Inspection

Article 32 The people's governments at or above the county level and their supervisory organs shall supervise the implementation of this Law by lower-level people's governments, the statistical institutions of the people's governments at the same level, and relevant departments.

Article 33 The National Bureau of Statistics organizes and manages the supervision and inspection of statistical work nationwide, investigating and dealing with major statistical violations.

The statistical institutions of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, investigate and deal with statistical violations occurring within their respective administrative regions. However, statistical violations occurring in statistical survey activities organized and implemented by survey agencies dispatched by the National Bureau of Statistics shall be investigated and dealt with by the survey agency that organized and implemented the statistical survey.

Where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise for relevant departments to investigate and deal with statistical violations, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 34 Relevant departments of the people's government at or above the county level shall actively assist the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level in investigating and dealing with statistical violations, and promptly transfer relevant materials on statistical violation cases to the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level.

Article 35 When investigating statistical violations or verifying statistical data, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level has the right to take the following measures:

(1) Issue a statistical inspection inquiry letter to inquire about relevant matters from the entity under inspection;

(2) Require the entity under inspection to provide relevant original records and vouchers, statistical ledgers, statistical survey forms, accounting data, and other relevant certifications and materials;

(3) Inquire with relevant personnel regarding matters related to the inspection;

(4) Enter the business premises and statistical data processing information systems of the entity under inspection for inspection and verification;

(5) With the approval of the head of the institution, register and preserve the relevant original records and vouchers, statistical ledgers, statistical survey forms, accounting data, and other relevant certifications and materials of the entity under inspection;

(6) Record, audio record, video record, photograph, and copy the information and materials related to the inspection matter.

When the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level conducts supervision and inspection, there shall be no fewer than two inspectors, and they shall present their law enforcement credentials; if they fail to do so, the relevant units and individuals have the right to refuse the inspection.

Article 36 When the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level performs its supervision and inspection duties, the relevant units and individuals shall truthfully reflect the situation, provide relevant certifications and materials, shall not refuse or obstruct the inspection, and shall not transfer, conceal, tamper with, or destroy original records and vouchers, statistical ledgers, statistical survey forms, accounting data, and other relevant certifications and materials.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

Article 37 Where leaders of local people's governments, government statistical institutions, or relevant departments or units commit any of the following acts, they shall be given sanctions by the appointing and removing authority or the supervisory organ in accordance with the law, and shall be notified by the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level:

(1) Arbitrarily modifying statistical data or fabricating false statistical data;

(2) Requiring statistical institutions, statisticians, or other institutions or personnel to fabricate or tamper with statistical data;

(3) Retaliating against statisticians who lawfully perform their duties or who refuse or resist statistical violations;

(4) Failing to detect serious statistical violations occurring in their locality, department, or unit.

Article 38 Where the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level or a relevant department commits any of the following acts in organizing and implementing statistical survey activities, it shall be ordered to make corrections and be notified by the people's government at the same level, the statistical institution of the higher-level people's government, or the statistical institution of the people's government at the same level; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions by the appointing and removing authority or the supervisory organ in accordance with the law:

(1) Organizing and implementing a statistical survey without approval;

(2) Arbitrarily changing the content of a statistical survey system without approval;

(3) Fabricating or tampering with statistical data;

(4) Requiring statistical survey respondents or other institutions or personnel to provide untrue statistical data;

(5) Failing to submit relevant data in accordance with the provisions of the statistical survey system.

Where a statistician commits any of the acts listed in items (3) to (5) of the preceding paragraph, they shall be ordered to make corrections and given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 39 Where the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level or a relevant department commits any of the following acts, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions by the appointing and removing authority or the supervisory organ in accordance with the law:

(1) Illegally publishing statistical data;

(2) Disclosing trade secrets or personal information of statistical survey respondents, or providing or disclosing data obtained in statistical surveys that can identify or infer the identity of an individual statistical survey respondent;

(3) Violating relevant State regulations, causing damage or loss of statistical data.

Where a statistician commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Where a statistical institution or statistician discloses State secrets, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 41 Where a State organ, enterprise, public institution, or other organization that is a statistical survey respondent commits any of the following acts, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections, issue a warning, and may notify it; if its directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel are public officials, they shall be given sanctions by the appointing and removing authority or the supervisory organ in accordance with the law:

(1) Refusing to provide statistical data or failing to provide statistical data on time after a reminder;

(2) Providing untrue or incomplete statistical data;

(3) Refusing to answer or not truthfully answering a statistical inspection inquiry letter;

(4) Refusing or obstructing statistical surveys or statistical inspections;

(5) Transferring, concealing, tampering with, destroying, or refusing to provide original records and vouchers, statistical ledgers, statistical survey forms, and other relevant certifications and materials.

Where an enterprise, public institution, or other organization commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph, it may also be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Where an individual business household commits any of the acts listed in paragraph 1 of this Article, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections, issue a warning, and may also impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 42 Where a State organ, enterprise, public institution, or other organization that is a statistical survey respondent delays reporting statistical data, or fails to set up original records and statistical ledgers in accordance with relevant State regulations, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections and issue a warning.

Where an enterprise, public institution, or other organization commits any of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph, it may also be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Where an individual business household delays reporting statistical data, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall order it to make corrections, issue a warning, and may also impose a fine of not more than 1,000 yuan.

Article 43 When investigating and dealing with a statistical violation, if the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level believes that a relevant public official should be given a sanction in accordance with the law, it shall propose a suggestion for imposing a sanction; the appointing and removing authority or the supervisory organ of the public official shall make a decision promptly in accordance with the law and notify the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level in writing of the result.

Article 44 Where an individual who is a statistical survey respondent refuses or obstructs a statistical survey in a major census of national conditions and national strength, or provides untrue or incomplete census data, the statistical institution of the people's government at or above the county level shall order them to make corrections and give them criticism and education.

Article 45 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, false statistical data are used to obtain honorary titles, material benefits, or job promotions, in addition to pursuing legal liability in accordance with the law for the act of fabricating false statistical data or requiring others to fabricate false statistical data, the unit that made the relevant decision, its superior unit, or the supervisory organ shall cancel the honorary titles, recover the material benefits obtained, and revoke the promoted position.Article 46 If a party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision made by a statistical institution of a people's government at or above the county level, it may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law. Specifically, if a party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision made by a survey institution dispatched by the National Bureau of Statistics in a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, it shall apply for administrative reconsideration to the National Bureau of Statistics; if a party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision made by any other survey institution dispatched by the National Bureau of Statistics, it shall apply for administrative reconsideration to the survey institution dispatched by the National Bureau of Statistics in the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the dispatched institution is located.

Article 47 Any violation of this Law that constitutes a crime shall be subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 48 For the purposes of this Law, "statistical institutions of people's governments at or above the county level" refers to the National Bureau of Statistics and its dispatched survey institutions, as well as statistical institutions of local people's governments at or above the county level.

Article 49 Measures for the administration of non-governmental statistical survey activities shall be formulated by the State Council.

If an organization or individual outside the People's Republic of China needs to conduct statistical survey activities within the territory of the People's Republic of China, it shall submit an application for approval in accordance with the regulations of the State Council.

Any use of statistical surveys to endanger national security, harm public interests, or engage in fraudulent activities shall be subject to legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 50 This Law shall take effect as of January 1, 2010.
