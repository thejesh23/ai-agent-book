# Government Procurement Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 29, 2002

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Five Laws Including the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating government procurement activities, improving the efficiency of the use of government procurement funds, safeguarding national interests and the public interest, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of parties involved in government procurement, and promoting integrity.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to government procurement conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "government procurement" refers to the conduct of state organs, public institutions, and social organizations at all levels in acquiring, using fiscal funds, goods, works, and services that are included in the centralized procurement catalog formulated in accordance with the law or whose value exceeds the procurement threshold.

The centralized procurement catalog and procurement thresholds shall be formulated in accordance with the authority prescribed by this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "procurement" refers to the conduct of acquiring goods, works, and services for consideration by means of contract, including purchase, lease, entrustment, and hiring.

For the purposes of this Law, "goods" refers to articles of various forms and types, including raw materials, fuel, equipment, and products.

For the purposes of this Law, "works" refers to construction works, including the construction, reconstruction, expansion, renovation, demolition, and repair of buildings and structures.

For the purposes of this Law, "services" refers to objects of government procurement other than goods and works.

Article 3 Government procurement shall follow the principles of openness and transparency, fair competition, impartiality, and good faith.

Article 4 Where government procurement of works is conducted through tendering and bidding, the Tendering and Bidding Law shall apply.

Article 5 No entity or individual may, by any means, obstruct or restrict the free access of suppliers to the government procurement market of their region or industry.

Article 6 Government procurement shall be carried out strictly in accordance with the approved budget.

Article 7 Government procurement shall combine centralized procurement and decentralized procurement. The scope of centralized procurement shall be determined by the centralized procurement catalog published by the people's governments at or above the provincial level.

For government procurement projects under the central budget, the centralized procurement catalog shall be determined and published by the State Council; for government procurement projects under local budgets, the centralized procurement catalog shall be determined and published by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, or by their authorized agencies.

Government procurement projects included in the centralized procurement catalog shall be subject to centralized procurement.

Article 8 For government procurement projects under the central budget, the procurement thresholds shall be determined and published by the State Council; for government procurement projects under local budgets, the procurement thresholds shall be determined and published by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, or by their authorized agencies.

Article 9 Government procurement shall contribute to the achievement of the state's economic and social development policy objectives, including environmental protection, support for underdeveloped areas and ethnic minority regions, and promotion of the development of small and medium-sized enterprises.

Article 10 Government procurement shall procure domestic goods, works, and services, except in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The required goods, works, or services are not available within the territory of China, or are not available on reasonable commercial terms;

(2) The procurement is for use outside the territory of China;

(3) Other laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise.

The definition of domestic goods, works, and services referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be determined in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 11 Information on government procurement shall be promptly disclosed to the public through the media designated by the government procurement supervision and administration department, except for information involving trade secrets.

Article 12 In government procurement activities, procurement personnel and relevant persons who have an interest in a supplier must recuse themselves. If a supplier believes that procurement personnel or relevant persons have an interest in another supplier, it may apply for their recusal.

The "relevant persons" referred to in the preceding paragraph include members of the bid evaluation committee in tendering procurement, members of the negotiation team in competitive negotiation procurement, and members of the inquiry team in inquiry procurement.

Article 13 The finance departments of the people's governments at all levels are the departments responsible for the supervision and administration of government procurement and shall perform their duties of supervision and administration over government procurement activities in accordance with the law.

Other relevant departments of the people's governments at all levels shall perform their duties of supervision and administration related to government procurement activities in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Parties to Government Procurement

Article 14 The parties to government procurement refer to various entities that enjoy rights and assume obligations in government procurement activities, including procuring entities, suppliers, and procurement agencies.

Article 15 A procuring entity refers to a state organ, public institution, or social organization that conducts government procurement in accordance with the law.

Article 16 Centralized procurement agencies are procurement agencies. The people's governments at the level of cities divided into districts or autonomous prefectures and above shall establish centralized procurement agencies as needed for organizing centralized procurement of their government procurement projects.

Centralized procurement agencies are non-profit legal persons that handle procurement matters on behalf of procuring entities.

Article 17 In conducting government procurement activities, centralized procurement agencies shall meet the requirements of procurement prices lower than the average market price, higher procurement efficiency, excellent procurement quality, and good service.

Article 18 Where a procuring entity procures a government procurement project included in the centralized procurement catalog, it must entrust a centralized procurement agency to act as its agent for the procurement; for projects not included in the centralized procurement catalog, it may conduct the procurement itself or entrust a centralized procurement agency to act as its agent within the scope of the entrustment.

For government procurement projects included in the centralized procurement catalog that are of a general nature, they shall be entrusted to a centralized procurement agency for procurement; for projects with special requirements of the department or system, departmental centralized procurement shall be implemented; for projects with special requirements of the entity itself, with the approval of the people's government at or above the provincial level, the entity may conduct the procurement itself.

Article 19 A procuring entity may entrust a procurement agency other than a centralized procurement agency to handle government procurement matters within the scope of the entrustment.

A procuring entity has the right to choose its own procurement agency, and no entity or individual may, by any means, designate a procurement agency for the procuring entity.

Article 20 Where a procuring entity entrusts a procurement agency to handle procurement matters in accordance with the law, the procuring entity and the procurement agency shall enter into an entrustment agency agreement, which shall specify the matters entrusted and the rights and obligations of both parties in accordance with the law.

Article 21 A supplier refers to a legal person, other organization, or individual that provides goods, works, or services to a procuring entity.

Article 22 A supplier participating in government procurement activities shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having the capacity to independently bear civil liability;

(2) Having a good business reputation and a sound financial and accounting system;

(3) Having the equipment and professional technical capabilities necessary for performing the contract;

(4) Having a good record of paying taxes and social security contributions in accordance with the law;

(5) Having no major illegal record in its business activities in the three years prior to participating in the government procurement activity;

(6) Other conditions prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

A procuring entity may, based on the special requirements of the procurement project, specify specific conditions for suppliers, but may not impose differential or discriminatory treatment on suppliers with unreasonable conditions.

Article 23 A procuring entity may require suppliers participating in government procurement to provide relevant qualification documents and performance records, and shall examine the qualifications of suppliers in accordance with the conditions for suppliers prescribed by this Law and the specific requirements of the procurement project for suppliers.

Article 24 Two or more individuals, legal persons, or other organizations may form a consortium to participate in government procurement jointly as a single supplier.

Where government procurement is conducted in the form of a consortium, each supplier in the consortium shall meet the conditions prescribed in Article 22 of this Law, and shall submit a consortium agreement to the procuring entity, specifying the work and obligations undertaken by each party to the consortium. All parties to the consortium shall jointly sign the procurement contract with the procuring entity and shall be jointly and severally liable to the procuring entity for the matters agreed upon in the procurement contract.

Article 25 Parties to government procurement shall not collude with each other to harm national interests, the public interest, or the legitimate rights and interests of other parties; nor shall they use any means to exclude other suppliers from competition.

Suppliers shall not seek to win the bid or secure the transaction by bribing the procuring entity, the procurement agency, members of the bid evaluation committee, members of the competitive negotiation team, or members of the inquiry team, or by resorting to other improper means.

Procurement agencies shall not seek illegal gains by bribing the procuring entity or by resorting to other improper means.

## Chapter III Methods of Government Procurement

Article 26 Government procurement shall be conducted using the following methods:

(1) Open tendering;

(2) Invited tendering;

(3) Competitive negotiation;

(4) Single-source procurement;

(5) Inquiry;

(6) Other procurement methods recognized by the government procurement supervision and administration department of the State Council.

Open tendering shall be the primary method of government procurement.

Article 27 Where a procuring entity is required to use open tendering for the procurement of goods or services, the specific monetary thresholds for central budget government procurement projects shall be prescribed by the State Council; for local budget government procurement projects, they shall be prescribed by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. If, due to special circumstances, a procurement method other than open tendering is necessary, approval must be obtained from the government procurement supervision and administration department of the people's government at the level of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture or above before the procurement activity begins.

Article 28 A procuring entity shall not break down a procurement of goods or services that should be procured by open tendering into smaller parts or use any other means to circumvent open tendering.

Article 29 The following goods or services may be procured by invited tendering in accordance with this Law:

(1) Those of a special nature that can only be procured from a limited number of suppliers;

(2) The cost of using open tendering accounts for an excessively large proportion of the total value of the government procurement project.

Article 30 The following goods or services may be procured by competitive negotiation in accordance with this Law:

(1) After tendering, no supplier submits a bid, no qualified bid is received, or a re-tendering fails;

(2) The technology is complex or the nature is special, making it impossible to determine detailed specifications or specific requirements;

(3) The time required for tendering cannot meet the urgent needs of the user;

(4) The total price cannot be calculated in advance.

Article 31 The following goods or services may be procured by single-source procurement in accordance with this Law:

(1) They can only be procured from a single supplier;

(2) An unforeseeable emergency occurs and procurement cannot be made from other suppliers;

(3) To ensure consistency with the original procurement project or requirements for service compatibility, additional procurement must be continued from the original supplier, and the total amount of the additional procurement does not exceed ten percent of the original contract procurement amount.

Article 32 Government procurement projects where the goods have uniform specifications and standards, sufficient spot supply, and small price fluctuations may be procured by inquiry in accordance with this Law.

## Chapter IV Government Procurement Procedures

Article 33 Departments responsible for preparing departmental budgets shall, when preparing the departmental budget for the next fiscal year, list the government procurement projects and their funding budgets for that fiscal year and submit them to the finance department at the same level for consolidation. The approval of departmental budgets shall be carried out in accordance with budget management authority and procedures.

Article 34 Where goods or services are procured by invited tendering, the procuring entity shall select three or more suppliers from among those meeting the corresponding qualification conditions by random means and issue invitations to bid to them.

Article 35 Where goods or services are procured by tendering, the period from the date of issuance of the tender documents to the deadline for submission of bids by bidders shall be no less than twenty days.

Article 36 In tendering procurement, the tender shall be rejected in any of the following circumstances:

(1) Fewer than three suppliers meet the professional conditions or respond substantively to the tender documents;

(2) Illegal or irregular conduct affecting the fairness of the procurement occurs;

(3) All bids exceed the procurement budget and the procuring entity cannot afford them;

(4) The procurement task is cancelled due to a major change.

After the tender is rejected, the procuring entity shall notify all bidders of the reasons for rejection.

Article 37 After the tender is rejected, unless the procurement task is cancelled, a new tender shall be organized; if another procurement method is necessary, approval must be obtained from the government procurement supervision and administration department of the people's government at the level of a city divided into districts or an autonomous prefecture or above, or from the relevant government department, before the procurement activity begins.

Article 38 Where procurement is conducted by competitive negotiation, the following procedures shall be followed:

(1) Establish a negotiation team. The negotiation team shall consist of representatives of the procuring entity and relevant experts, totaling three or more persons in an odd number, with the number of experts being no less than two-thirds of the total members.

(2) Prepare negotiation documents. The negotiation documents shall clearly specify the negotiation procedures, negotiation content, terms of the draft contract, and criteria for determining the successful transaction.

(3) Determine the list of suppliers invited to participate in the negotiation. The negotiation team shall select no fewer than three suppliers from the list of suppliers meeting the corresponding qualification conditions to participate in the negotiation and provide them with the negotiation documents.

(4) Negotiate. All members of the negotiation team shall negotiate with each supplier individually and collectively. During the negotiation, neither party shall disclose the technical data, prices, or other information of other suppliers related to the negotiation. If there are substantive changes to the negotiation documents, the negotiation team shall notify all participating suppliers in writing.

(5) Determine the successful supplier. After the negotiation concludes, the negotiation team shall require all participating suppliers to submit their final quotations within a specified time. The procuring entity shall determine the successful supplier from among the candidates proposed by the negotiation team based on the principle of meeting procurement needs, equal quality and service, and the lowest quotation, and shall notify all unsuccessful participating suppliers of the result.

Article 39 Where procurement is conducted by single-source procurement, the procuring entity and the supplier shall conduct the procurement in accordance with the principles prescribed by this Law, ensuring the quality of the procurement project and agreeing on a reasonable price.

Article 40 Where procurement is conducted by inquiry, the following procedures shall be followed:

(1) Establish an inquiry team. The inquiry team shall consist of representatives of the procuring entity and relevant experts, totaling three or more persons in an odd number, with the number of experts being no less than two-thirds of the total members. The inquiry team shall specify matters such as the price composition of the procurement project and the criteria for determining the successful transaction.

(2) Determine the list of suppliers to be inquired. The inquiry team shall, based on the procurement needs, select no fewer than three suppliers from the list of suppliers meeting the corresponding qualification conditions and issue inquiry notices to them requesting quotations.

(3) Conduct the inquiry. The inquiry team shall require the suppliers being inquired to submit a single quotation that cannot be changed.

(4) Determine the successful supplier. The procuring entity shall determine the successful supplier based on the principle of meeting procurement needs, equal quality and service, and the lowest quotation, and shall notify all unsuccessful suppliers that were inquired of the result.

Article 41 The procuring entity or its entrusted procurement agency shall organize the acceptance inspection of the supplier's contract performance. For large or complex government procurement projects, nationally recognized quality inspection agencies shall be invited to participate in the acceptance inspection. Members of the acceptance inspection team shall sign the acceptance certificate and bear corresponding legal liability.

Article 42 The procuring entity and the procurement agency shall properly preserve the procurement documents for each procurement activity of a government procurement project and shall not forge, alter, conceal, or destroy them. The retention period for procurement documents shall be at least fifteen years from the date of completion of the procurement.

Procurement documents include procurement activity records, procurement budgets, tender documents, bid documents, bid evaluation criteria, evaluation reports, bid award documents, contract texts, acceptance certificates, responses to inquiries, decisions on complaints, and other relevant documents and materials.

Procurement activity records shall at least include the following:

(1) The category and name of the procurement project;

(2) The budget for the procurement project, the composition of funds, and the contract price;

(3) The procurement method, and if a method other than open tendering is used, the reasons shall be stated;

(4) The conditions and reasons for inviting and selecting suppliers;

(5) The bid evaluation criteria and the reasons for determining the winning bidder;

(6) The reasons for rejecting the tender;

(7) Corresponding records for procurement methods other than tendering.

## Chapter V Government Procurement Contracts

Article 43 Government procurement contracts shall be governed by the Contract Law. The rights and obligations between the procuring entity and the supplier shall be agreed upon by contract in accordance with the principles of equality and voluntariness.

The procuring entity may entrust a procurement agency to sign the government procurement contract on its behalf with the supplier. If the procurement agency signs the contract in the name of the procuring entity, it shall submit a power of attorney from the procuring entity, which shall be attached to the contract.

Article 44 Government procurement contracts shall be in writing.

Article 45 The government procurement supervision and administration department of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council, prescribe the clauses that must be included in government procurement contracts.

Article 46 The procuring entity and the successful bidder or supplier shall sign the government procurement contract within thirty days from the date of issuance of the notice of award or successful transaction, in accordance with the matters determined in the procurement documents.

The notice of award or successful transaction is legally binding on both the procuring entity and the successful bidder or supplier. After the notice of award or successful transaction is issued, if the procuring entity changes the result of the award or successful transaction, or if the successful bidder or supplier abandons the awarded or successful transaction project, they shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 47 Within seven working days from the date of signing the procurement contract for a government procurement project, the procuring entity shall submit a copy of the contract to the government procurement supervision and administration department at the same level and the relevant departments for record.

Article 48 With the consent of the procuring entity, the successful bidder or supplier may perform the contract by subcontracting in accordance with the law.

Where a government procurement contract is performed by subcontracting, the successful bidder or supplier shall be liable to the procuring entity for the procurement project and the subcontracted project, and the subcontractor shall be liable for the subcontracted project.

Article 49 During the performance of a government procurement contract, if the procuring entity needs to procure additional goods, works, or services of the same type as the subject matter of the contract, it may negotiate with the supplier to sign a supplementary contract without changing other terms of the contract, provided that the total procurement amount of all supplementary contracts does not exceed ten percent of the original contract procurement amount.

Article 50 Neither party to a government procurement contract may unilaterally modify, suspend, or terminate the contract.

If continued performance of the government procurement contract would harm national interests or the public interest, both parties shall modify, suspend, or terminate the contract. The party at fault shall bear liability for compensation; if both parties are at fault, they shall each bear corresponding liability.

## Chapter VI Inquiries and Complaints**Article 51** If a supplier has a question regarding a government procurement activity, it may raise an inquiry with the procuring entity, which shall respond promptly, provided that the response does not involve trade secrets.

**Article 52** If a supplier believes that its rights or interests have been harmed by the procurement documents, the procurement process, or the award or contract award result, it may, within seven working days from the date it knew or should have known of such harm, submit a written challenge to the procuring entity.

**Article 53** The procuring entity shall respond to the supplier's written challenge within seven working days of receipt, and shall notify the challenging supplier and other relevant suppliers in writing, provided that the response does not involve trade secrets.

**Article 54** Where the procuring entity entrusts a procurement agency with the procurement, the supplier may raise an inquiry or challenge with the procurement agency. The procurement agency shall respond, in accordance with Articles 51 and 53 of this Law, on matters within the scope of authority delegated by the procuring entity.

**Article 55** If a challenging supplier is dissatisfied with the response from the procuring entity or the procurement agency, or if the procuring entity or procurement agency fails to respond within the prescribed time limit, the supplier may, within fifteen working days after the expiration of the response period, file a complaint with the government procurement supervisory authority at the same level.

**Article 56** The government procurement supervisory authority shall, within thirty working days of receiving a complaint, make a decision on the matter and notify the complainant and the parties concerned with the complaint in writing.

**Article 57** During the handling of a complaint, the government procurement supervisory authority may, depending on the specific circumstances, notify the procuring entity in writing to suspend the procurement activity, but the suspension period shall not exceed thirty days.

**Article 58** If a complainant is dissatisfied with the complaint handling decision of the government procurement supervisory authority, or if the authority fails to handle the matter within the prescribed time limit, the complainant may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7: Supervision and Inspection

**Article 59** The government procurement supervisory authority shall strengthen its supervision and inspection of government procurement activities and centralized procurement agencies.

The main contents of supervision and inspection are:

(1) The implementation of laws, administrative regulations, and rules concerning government procurement;
(2) The implementation of procurement scope, procurement methods, and procurement procedures;
(3) The professional competence and technical skills of government procurement personnel.

**Article 60** The government procurement supervisory authority shall not establish centralized procurement agencies, nor shall it participate in the procurement activities of government procurement projects.

Procurement agencies shall not have a subordinate relationship or other interest relationship with administrative organs.

**Article 61** Centralized procurement agencies shall establish and improve internal supervision and management systems. The decision-making and execution procedures for procurement activities shall be clear, mutually supervised, and mutually constrained. The responsibilities and authority of personnel handling procurement and those responsible for reviewing procurement contracts and acceptance shall be clear and mutually separated.

**Article 62** Procurement personnel of centralized procurement agencies shall possess relevant professional competence and technical skills, and meet the professional post requirements stipulated by the government procurement supervisory authority.

Centralized procurement agencies shall strengthen education and training for their staff; they shall regularly assess the professional level, work performance, and professional ethics of procurement personnel. Procurement personnel who fail the assessment shall not continue in their positions.

**Article 63** The procurement standards for government procurement projects shall be made public.

Where the procurement methods stipulated in this Law are adopted, the procuring entity shall publish the procurement results after the procurement activity is completed.

**Article 64** The procuring entity must conduct procurement in accordance with the procurement methods and procedures stipulated in this Law.

No unit or individual may, in violation of the provisions of this Law, require the procuring entity or its procurement staff to procure from a supplier designated by them.

**Article 65** The government procurement supervisory authority shall inspect the procurement activities of government procurement projects. The parties involved in government procurement shall truthfully report the situation and provide relevant materials.

**Article 66** The government procurement supervisory authority shall assess the procurement prices, cost-saving effects, service quality, reputation status, and any illegal activities of centralized procurement agencies, and shall regularly and truthfully publish the assessment results.

**Article 67** Relevant government departments that have administrative supervision responsibilities over government procurement in accordance with laws and administrative regulations shall strengthen supervision over government procurement activities according to their respective responsibilities.

**Article 68** Audit authorities shall conduct audit supervision over government procurement. The government procurement supervisory authority and all parties involved in government procurement activities shall accept the audit supervision of the audit authorities.

**Article 69** Supervisory authorities shall strengthen supervision over state organs, state civil servants, and other personnel appointed by state administrative organs participating in government procurement activities.

**Article 70** Any unit or individual has the right to report and accuse illegal activities in government procurement activities. Relevant departments and authorities shall handle them promptly according to their respective responsibilities.

## Chapter 8: Legal Liability

**Article 71** Where a procuring entity or procurement agency commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to correct within a time limit, given a warning, and may be concurrently fined; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions by their administrative department or relevant authority, and shall be notified:

(1) Adopting other procurement methods without authorization when an open tender method should have been used;
(2) Raising procurement standards without authorization;
(3) Applying differential or discriminatory treatment to suppliers based on unreasonable conditions;
(4) Engaging in negotiations with bidders during the tender procurement process;
(5) Failing to sign a procurement contract with the winning or successful supplier after issuing the award notice;
(6) Refusing lawful supervision and inspection by relevant authorities.

**Article 72** Where a procuring entity, procurement agency, or their staff commit any of the following acts, if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if not constituting a crime, a fine shall be imposed, any illegal gains shall be confiscated, and if they are state organ staff, administrative sanctions shall be given according to law:

(1) Maliciously colluding with suppliers or procurement agencies;
(2) Accepting bribes or obtaining other improper benefits during the procurement process;
(3) Providing false information during lawful supervision and inspection by relevant authorities;
(4) Disclosing the bid bottom price before bid opening.

**Article 73** Where any illegal act mentioned in the preceding two articles affects or may affect the award or contract award result, it shall be handled according to the following circumstances:

(1) If the winning or successful supplier has not been determined, the procurement activity shall be terminated;
(2) If the winning or successful supplier has been determined but the procurement contract has not yet been performed, the contract shall be revoked, and a new winning or successful supplier shall be determined from among the qualified winning or successful candidates;
(3) If the procurement contract has already been performed, causing losses to the procuring entity or supplier, the responsible person shall bear the liability for compensation.

**Article 74** Where a procuring entity fails to entrust a centralized procurement agency to conduct centralized procurement for a government procurement project that should be subject to centralized procurement, the government procurement supervisory authority shall order correction; if correction is refused, payment of funds according to the budget shall be stopped, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions by their superior administrative department or relevant authority according to law.

**Article 75** Where a procuring entity fails to publish the procurement standards and procurement results of a government procurement project according to law, it shall be ordered to correct, and the directly responsible person in charge shall be given sanctions according to law.

**Article 76** Where a procuring entity or procurement agency, in violation of the provisions of this Law, conceals, destroys, forges, or alters procurement documents that should be preserved, the government procurement supervisory authority shall impose a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 77** Where a supplier commits any of the following acts, it shall be fined not less than five per thousand but not more than ten per thousand of the procurement amount, listed in the record of bad conduct, prohibited from participating in government procurement activities for one to three years, any illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the circumstances are serious, the business license shall be revoked by the administrative authority for industry and commerce; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law:

(1) Providing false materials to seek winning or successful bid;
(2) Using improper means to slander or exclude other suppliers;
(3) Maliciously colluding with the procuring entity, other suppliers, or procurement agencies;
(4) Offering bribes or providing other improper benefits to the procuring entity or procurement agency;
(5) Engaging in negotiations with the procuring entity during the tender procurement process;
(6) Refusing supervision and inspection by relevant authorities or providing false information.

Where a supplier commits any of the acts specified in items (1) to (5) of the preceding paragraph, the winning or successful bid shall be invalid.

**Article 78** Where a procurement agency commits illegal acts in acting as an agent for government procurement business, it shall be fined according to relevant legal provisions, and may be prohibited from acting as an agent for government procurement business for one to three years; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 79** Where a party involved in government procurement commits any of the illegal acts specified in Articles 71, 72, or 77 of this Law, causing losses to others, it shall also bear civil liability in accordance with relevant civil laws.

**Article 80** Where staff of the government procurement supervisory authority, in violation of the provisions of this Law, abuse their power, neglect their duty, or engage in malpractice for personal gain during supervision and inspection, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 81** Where the government procurement supervisory authority fails to handle a supplier's complaint within the prescribed time limit, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions.

**Article 82** Where the government procurement supervisory authority makes false statements, conceals the true situation, or fails to conduct regular assessments and publish assessment results regarding the performance assessment of centralized procurement agencies, it shall promptly correct the situation; its superior authority or supervisory authority shall circulate a notice of criticism against its responsible person, and the directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions according to law.

Where a centralized procurement agency makes false reports on performance or conceals the true situation during the assessment by the government procurement supervisory authority, it shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and shall be notified; if the circumstances are serious, its qualification to act as an agent for procurement shall be revoked.

**Article 83** Where any unit or individual obstructs or restricts suppliers from entering the local or industry government procurement market, it shall be ordered to correct within a time limit; if correction is refused, the superior administrative department or relevant authority of the unit or individual shall give sanctions to the person in charge of the unit or the individual.

## Chapter 9: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 84** For government procurement using loans from international organizations or foreign governments, if the agreements reached between the lender, fund provider, and the Chinese side have different provisions on the specific conditions of procurement, those provisions may apply, provided that they do not harm national interests or social public interests.

**Article 85** This Law shall not apply to emergency procurement implemented due to severe natural disasters and other force majeure events, nor to procurement involving national security and secrets.

**Article 86** Military procurement regulations shall be formulated separately by the Central Military Commission.

**Article 87** The specific steps and measures for implementing this Law shall be stipulated by the State Council.

**Article 88** This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2003.
