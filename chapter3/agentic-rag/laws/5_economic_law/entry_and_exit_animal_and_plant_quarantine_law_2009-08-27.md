# Animal and Plant Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on October 30, 1991

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to prevent the introduction or spread of animal infectious diseases, parasitic diseases, and plant dangerous diseases, pests, weeds, and other harmful organisms (hereinafter referred to as pests and diseases) into or out of the country, to protect agricultural, forestry, animal husbandry, and fishery production as well as human health, and to promote the development of foreign economic trade.

Article 2 Quarantine shall be implemented in accordance with the provisions of this Law for animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects entering or leaving the country, as well as for the containers and packaging materials used to load such items, and for means of transport originating from animal or plant epidemic areas.

Article 3 The State Council shall establish an animal and plant quarantine authority (hereinafter referred to as the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority) to uniformly administer the national animal and plant quarantine work for entry and exit. Port animal and plant quarantine authorities established by the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority at open ports and locations where entry-exit animal and plant quarantine operations are concentrated shall implement entry-exit animal and plant quarantine in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

The quarantine authority for the export of animal products of a commercial nature shall be designated by the State Council based on the circumstances.

The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council shall be responsible for the administration of national entry-exit animal and plant quarantine work.

Article 4 Port animal and plant quarantine authorities may exercise the following powers when implementing quarantine:

(1) Boarding ships, vehicles, and aircraft to implement quarantine in accordance with the provisions of this Law;
(2) Entering ports, airports, railway stations, post offices, and places where quarantine objects are stored, processed, raised, or planted to implement quarantine, and collecting samples in accordance with regulations;
(3) Entering relevant production sites, warehouses, and other premises as needed for quarantine to conduct epidemic monitoring, investigation, and quarantine supervision and management;
(4) Inspecting, copying, and extracting operation logs, cargo manifests, contracts, invoices, and other documents related to quarantine objects.

Article 5 The following items are prohibited from entering the country:

(1) Animal and plant pathogens (including bacterial strains, virus strains, etc.), pests, and other harmful organisms;
(2) Relevant animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects from countries or regions where animal or plant epidemics are prevalent;
(3) Animal carcasses;
(4) Soil.

If a port animal and plant quarantine authority discovers any of the items prohibited from entry as specified in the preceding paragraph, it shall order their return or destruction.

If the items prohibited from entry as specified in paragraph 1 of this Article are required for special purposes such as scientific research, an application must be submitted in advance and approved by the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority.

The list of items prohibited from entry as specified in item (2) of paragraph 1 of this Article shall be formulated and published by the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council.

Article 6 When a major animal or plant epidemic occurs abroad and may spread into China, the State Council shall take emergency preventive measures, and may, if necessary, order the prohibition of entry of means of transport from animal or plant epidemic areas or seal off relevant ports; the local people's government of the area threatened by the animal or plant epidemic and the relevant port animal and plant quarantine authority shall immediately take emergency measures and simultaneously report to the higher-level people's government and the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority.

Postal and transportation departments shall give priority to the transmission of reports on major animal or plant epidemics and materials submitted for inspection.

Article 7 The National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority and port animal and plant quarantine authorities shall implement a quarantine supervision system over the production, processing, and storage processes of animals, plants, and animal and plant products entering and leaving the country.

Article 8 When port animal and plant quarantine authorities perform quarantine duties at ports, airports, railway stations, and post offices, relevant departments such as customs, transportation, civil aviation, railways, and postal services shall cooperate.

Article 9 Quarantine personnel of animal and plant quarantine authorities must be devoted to their duties and enforce the law impartially.

No unit or individual may obstruct quarantine personnel of animal and plant quarantine authorities from performing their official duties in accordance with the law.

## Chapter II Entry Quarantine

Article 10 For the import of animals, animal products, plant seeds, seedlings, and other propagating materials, an application must be submitted in advance, and quarantine approval procedures must be completed.

Article 11 When importing animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects through trade, scientific and technological cooperation, exchange, donation, or aid, the contract or agreement shall specify the statutory quarantine requirements of China and shall stipulate that a quarantine certificate issued by the government animal and plant quarantine authority of the exporting country or region must be attached.

Article 12 The owner or their agent shall, before or at the time of entry of animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects, present the quarantine certificate from the exporting country or region, the trade contract, and other documents to the port animal and plant quarantine authority at the entry port for quarantine declaration.

Article 13 When a means of transport carrying animals arrives at the port, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall take on-site preventive measures and carry out epidemic prevention and disinfection treatment for personnel boarding or alighting from the means of transport or approaching the animals, the means of transport carrying the animals, and contaminated sites.

Article 14 Imported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects shall be quarantined at the entry port. Without the consent of the port animal and plant quarantine authority, they shall not be unloaded from the means of transport.

Imported animals requiring isolation quarantine shall be quarantined at a designated isolation site specified by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Due to port conditions or other limitations, the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority may decide to transport animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects to a designated location for quarantine. During transportation and loading/unloading, the owner or their agent shall take epidemic prevention measures. The designated storage, processing, isolation feeding, or isolation planting sites shall comply with animal and plant quarantine and epidemic prevention regulations.

Article 15 Imported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects that pass quarantine inspection shall be permitted entry; customs shall release them upon presentation of the quarantine documents issued by the port animal and plant quarantine authority or the seal affixed to the customs declaration form.

If imported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects need to be moved out of the customs supervision zone for quarantine, customs shall release them upon presentation of the "Quarantine Transfer Notice" issued by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Article 16 If imported animals fail quarantine inspection, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall issue a "Quarantine Treatment Notice" to inform the owner or their agent to take the following actions:

(1) Animals found to have a Category I infectious disease or parasitic disease shall be returned along with all animals in the same group, or all animals in the same group shall be slaughtered and their carcasses destroyed;
(2) Animals found to have a Category II infectious disease or parasitic disease shall be returned or slaughtered, and other animals in the same group shall be placed under isolation observation at an isolation site or other designated location.

If imported animal products and other quarantine objects fail quarantine inspection, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall issue a "Quarantine Treatment Notice" to inform the owner or their agent to carry out disinfestation, return, or destruction treatment. Those that pass quarantine inspection after disinfestation treatment shall be permitted entry.

Article 17 If imported plants, plant products, and other quarantine objects are found to have plant dangerous diseases, pests, or weeds upon quarantine inspection, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall issue a "Quarantine Treatment Notice" to inform the owner or their agent to carry out disinfestation, return, or destruction treatment. Those that pass quarantine inspection after disinfestation treatment shall be permitted entry.

Article 18 The lists of Category I and Category II animal infectious diseases and parasitic diseases referred to in items (1) and (2) of paragraph 1 of Article 16 of this Law, and the list of plant dangerous diseases, pests, and weeds referred to in Article 17 of this Law, shall be formulated and published by the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council.

Article 19 If imported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects are found upon quarantine inspection to have other pests or diseases not included in the lists specified in Article 18 of this Law but which cause serious harm to agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, or fishery, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall, in accordance with the regulations of the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council, notify the owner or their agent to carry out disinfestation, return, or destruction treatment. Those that pass quarantine inspection after disinfestation treatment shall be permitted entry.

## Chapter III Export Quarantine

Article 20 The owner or their agent shall, before the export of animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects, make a quarantine declaration to the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Animals requiring isolation quarantine before export shall be quarantined at a designated isolation site specified by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Article 21 Exported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects shall be quarantined by the port animal and plant quarantine authority. Those that pass quarantine inspection or pass after disinfestation treatment shall be permitted export; customs shall release them upon presentation of the quarantine certificate issued by the port animal and plant quarantine authority or the seal affixed to the customs declaration form. Those that fail quarantine inspection and for which there is no effective method of disinfestation treatment shall not be permitted export.

Article 22 If animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects that have passed quarantine inspection fall under any of the following circumstances, the owner or their agent shall make a new quarantine declaration:

(1) The importing country or region is changed, and the new importing country or region has different quarantine requirements;
(2) The packaging is changed, or items originally not consolidated are later consolidated;
(3) The validity period specified for quarantine has expired.

## Chapter IV Transit Quarantine

Article 23 To transport animals in transit, prior consent must be obtained from the National Animal and Plant Quarantine Authority of China, and the transit shall be conducted via the designated ports and routes.

The means of transport, containers, feed, and bedding materials used for transporting animals in transit must comply with China's animal and plant quarantine regulations.

Article 24 For the transit of animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects, the carrier or escort shall, at the time of entry, present the cargo manifest and the quarantine certificate issued by the government animal and plant quarantine authority of the exporting country or region to the port animal and plant quarantine authority for quarantine declaration; no further quarantine shall be conducted at the exit port.

Article 25 Animals in transit that pass quarantine inspection shall be permitted transit; if any animal infectious disease or parasitic disease listed in the list specified in Article 18 of this Law is found, all animals in the group shall not be permitted transit.

If the feed for animals in transit is contaminated by pests or diseases, it shall be subject to disinfestation treatment, or the feed shall not be permitted transit or shall be destroyed.

Animal carcasses, excrement, bedding materials, and other waste from animals in transit must be disposed of in accordance with the regulations of the animal and plant quarantine authority and shall not be discarded without authorization.

Article 26 For plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects in transit, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall inspect the means of transport or packaging. Those that pass quarantine inspection shall be permitted transit; if any pest or disease listed in the list specified in Article 18 of this Law is found, disinfestation treatment shall be carried out, or transit shall not be permitted.

Article 27 During the transit of animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects, without the approval of the animal and plant quarantine authority, the packaging shall not be opened, nor shall the items be unloaded from the means of transport.

## Chapter V Quarantine of Carried and Mailed Items

Article 28 For carrying or mailing plant seeds, seedlings, and other propagating materials into the country, an application must be submitted in advance, and quarantine approval procedures must be completed.

Article 29 The list of animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects prohibited from being carried or mailed into the country shall be formulated and published by the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council.

If animals, plants, animal products, plant products, or other quarantine objects listed in the list specified in the preceding paragraph are carried or mailed into the country, they shall be returned or destroyed.

Article 30 For carrying animals, plants, animal products, plant products, or other quarantine objects not listed in the list specified in Article 29 of this Law into the country, a declaration shall be made to customs at the time of entry, and quarantine by the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall be accepted.

For carrying animals into the country, a quarantine certificate and other documents from the exporting country or region must be held.

Article 31 For mailing animals, plants, animal products, plant products, or other quarantine objects not listed in the list specified in Article 29 of this Law into the country, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall implement quarantine at the international mail exchange office, and may, if necessary, retrieve the items to the port animal and plant quarantine authority for quarantine; the items shall not be transported or delivered without quarantine.

Article 32 Mailed animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects entering the country shall be released after passing quarantine inspection or disinfestation treatment; those that fail quarantine inspection and for which there is no effective method of disinfestation treatment shall be returned or destroyed, and a "Quarantine Treatment Notice" shall be issued.

Article 33 For animals, plants, animal products, plant products, and other quarantine objects carried or mailed out of the country, if the owner has quarantine requirements, quarantine shall be implemented by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

## Chapter VI Quarantine of Means of Transport

Article 34 When ships, aircraft, or trains from animal or plant epidemic areas arrive at the port, quarantine shall be implemented by the port animal and plant quarantine authority. If any pest or disease listed in the list specified in Article 18 of this Law is found, measures such as prohibiting removal from the means of transport, disinfestation, sealing, or destruction shall be taken.

Article 35 Vehicles entering the country shall be subject to epidemic prevention and disinfection treatment by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Article 36 Swill and animal or plant waste on means of transport entering or leaving the country shall be disposed of in accordance with the regulations of the port animal and plant quarantine authority and shall not be discarded without authorization.

Article 37 Means of transport used for loading exported animals, plants, animal products, and other quarantine objects shall comply with animal and plant quarantine and epidemic prevention regulations.

Article 38 Old or used ships entering the country for scrapping shall be quarantined by the port animal and plant quarantine authority; if any pest or disease listed in the list specified in Article 18 of this Law is found, disinfestation treatment shall be carried out.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 39 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts shall be fined by the port animal and plant quarantine authority:

(1) Failing to make a quarantine declaration or failing to complete quarantine approval procedures in accordance with the law;
(2) Without the permission of the port animal and plant quarantine authority, unloading imported animals, plants, animal products, or other quarantine objects from the means of transport or transporting or delivering them;
(3) Without authorization, transferring or disposing of animals or plants under isolation quarantine at a designated isolation site specified by the port animal and plant quarantine authority.

Article 40 If the animals, plants, animal products, or other quarantine objects declared for quarantine do not match the actual items, the port animal and plant quarantine authority shall impose a fine; if quarantine documents have already been obtained, they shall be revoked.

Article 41 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, without authorization, opens the packaging of animals, plants, animal products, or other quarantine objects in transit, unloads such items from the means of transport in transit, or discards animal carcasses, excrement, bedding materials, or other waste from animals in transit, shall be fined by the animal and plant quarantine authority.

Article 42 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, causes a major animal or plant epidemic shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 43 Anyone who forges or alters quarantine documents, seals, marks, or seals shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 44 If a party disagrees with a penalty decision made by an animal and plant quarantine authority, they may, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the penalty notice, apply for reconsideration to the authority at the next higher level than the one that made the penalty decision; the party may also, within fifteen days from the date of receiving the penalty notice, directly file a lawsuit with a people's court.

The reconsideration authority shall make a reconsideration decision within sixty days from the date of receiving the reconsideration application. If the party disagrees with the reconsideration decision, they may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the reconsideration decision. If the reconsideration authority fails to make a reconsideration decision within the time limit, the party may file a lawsuit with a people's court within fifteen days from the expiration of the reconsideration period.

If the party neither applies for reconsideration nor files a lawsuit with a people's court within the time limit, nor complies with the penalty decision, the authority that made the penalty decision may apply to a people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 45 If quarantine personnel of animal and plant quarantine authorities abuse their power, engage in malpractice for personal gain, forge quarantine results, or neglect their duty, delaying quarantine certification, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 46 The meanings of the following terms used in this Law are:

(1) "Animals" refers to live animals, whether domesticated or wild, such as livestock, poultry, wild animals, snakes, turtles, fish, shrimp, crabs, shellfish, silkworms, bees, etc.;
(2) "Animal products" refers to products derived from animals that are unprocessed or processed but may still transmit diseases, such as raw hides, furs, meats, internal organs, fats, aquatic animal products, dairy products, eggs, blood, semen, embryos, bones, hooves, horns, etc.;
(3) "Plants" refers to cultivated plants, wild plants, and their seeds, seedlings, and other propagating materials;
(4) "Plant products" refers to products derived from plants that are unprocessed or processed but may still transmit pests or diseases, such as grains, beans, cotton, oil, hemp, tobacco, kernels, dried fruits, fresh fruits, vegetables, raw medicinal materials, timber, feed, etc.;
(5) "Other quarantine objects" refers to animal vaccines, sera, diagnostic fluids, animal or plant waste, etc.

Article 47 If an international treaty concerning animal and plant quarantine concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China contains provisions different from those of this Law, the provisions of the international treaty shall apply, except for clauses on which the People's Republic of China has declared reservations.

Article 48 Port animal and plant quarantine authorities shall charge fees for implementing quarantine in accordance with regulations. The fee collection measures shall be formulated by the competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council jointly with the competent department for prices and other relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 49 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 50 This Law shall come into effect as of April 1, 1992. The "Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Quarantine of Import and Export Animals and Plants" promulgated by the State Council on June 4, 1982, shall be simultaneously abolished.
