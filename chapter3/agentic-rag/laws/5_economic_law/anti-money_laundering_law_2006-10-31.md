# Anti-Money Laundering Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on October 31, 2006

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of preventing money laundering activities, maintaining financial order, and curbing money laundering crimes and related crimes.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "anti-money laundering" refers to the adoption of relevant measures in accordance with the provisions of this Law to prevent money laundering activities that, through various means, conceal or disguise the source and nature of proceeds derived from drug crimes, organized crimes of a mafia nature, terrorist activities crimes, smuggling crimes, corruption and bribery crimes, crimes of disrupting financial management order, financial fraud crimes, etc., and the income generated from such proceeds.

Article 3 Financial institutions established within the territory of the People's Republic of China and specific non-financial institutions that are required to fulfill anti-money laundering obligations according to regulations shall, in accordance with the law, adopt preventive and monitoring measures, establish and improve a customer identification system, a system for preserving customer identity materials and transaction records, and a system for reporting large-value transactions and suspicious transactions, thereby fulfilling their anti-money laundering obligations.

Article 4 The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and administration of anti-money laundering nationwide. The relevant departments and institutions under the State Council shall perform their respective anti-money laundering supervision and administration duties within the scope of their functions.

The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council, the relevant departments and institutions under the State Council, and the judicial authorities shall cooperate with each other in anti-money laundering work.

Article 5 Customer identity materials and transaction information obtained through the lawful performance of anti-money laundering duties or obligations shall be kept confidential; they shall not be provided to any entity or individual unless otherwise provided by law.

Customer identity materials and transaction information obtained by the anti-money laundering administrative authority and other departments and institutions legally responsible for anti-money laundering supervision and administration in the course of performing their anti-money laundering duties may only be used for anti-money laundering administrative investigations.

Customer identity materials and transaction information obtained by judicial authorities in accordance with this Law may only be used for criminal proceedings related to money laundering.

Article 6 Institutions fulfilling anti-money laundering obligations and their staff members are protected by law when submitting reports on large-value transactions and suspicious transactions in accordance with the law.

Article 7 Any entity or individual that discovers money laundering activities has the right to report them to the anti-money laundering administrative authority or the public security authority. The authority receiving the report shall keep the identity of the whistleblower and the content of the report confidential.

## Chapter II Anti-Money Laundering Supervision and Administration

Article 8 The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall organize and coordinate nationwide anti-money laundering efforts, be responsible for anti-money laundering fund monitoring, formulate or jointly formulate with the relevant financial regulatory authorities under the State Council rules for anti-money laundering by financial institutions, supervise and inspect the fulfillment of anti-money laundering obligations by financial institutions, investigate suspicious transaction activities within the scope of its duties, and perform other anti-money laundering duties as prescribed by law and the State Council.

The local offices of the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall, within the scope of authorization granted by the said authority, supervise and inspect the fulfillment of anti-money laundering obligations by financial institutions.

Article 9 The relevant financial regulatory authorities under the State Council shall participate in the formulation of anti-money laundering rules for the financial institutions under their supervision, require the financial institutions under their supervision to establish and improve internal control systems for anti-money laundering in accordance with regulations, and perform other anti-money laundering duties as prescribed by law and the State Council.

Article 10 The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall establish an Anti-Money Laundering Information Center, which shall be responsible for receiving and analyzing reports on large-value transactions and suspicious transactions, reporting the analysis results to the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council in accordance with regulations, and performing other duties as prescribed by the said authority.

Article 11 To fulfill its duty of monitoring anti-money laundering funds, the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council may obtain necessary information from the relevant departments and institutions under the State Council, and such departments and institutions shall provide it.

The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall regularly brief the relevant departments and institutions under the State Council on the status of anti-money laundering work.

Article 12 Where customs discovers that an individual entering or leaving the country is carrying cash or bearer negotiable securities exceeding the prescribed amount, it shall promptly notify the anti-money laundering administrative authority.

The amount standard for notification as mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be prescribed by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council jointly with the General Administration of Customs.

Article 13 Where the anti-money laundering administrative authority and other departments and institutions legally responsible for anti-money laundering supervision and administration discover transaction activities suspected of money laundering crimes, they shall promptly report them to the investigative authorities.

Article 14 When examining applications for the establishment of new financial institutions or the addition of branches by financial institutions, the relevant financial regulatory authorities under the State Council shall review the new institution's plan for internal control systems for anti-money laundering; applications for establishment that do not comply with the provisions of this Law shall not be approved.

## Chapter III Anti-Money Laundering Obligations of Financial Institutions

Article 15 Financial institutions shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, establish and improve internal control systems for anti-money laundering. The persons in charge of financial institutions shall be responsible for the effective implementation of the internal control systems for anti-money laundering.

Financial institutions shall establish specialized anti-money laundering units or designate internal departments to be responsible for anti-money laundering work.

Article 16 Financial institutions shall, in accordance with regulations, establish a customer identification system.

When establishing a business relationship with a customer or providing a one-time financial service such as cash remittance, currency exchange, or bill payment exceeding a prescribed amount, a financial institution shall require the customer to present valid and authentic identity documents or other identity certification documents, verify them, and register the information.

Where a customer is represented by another person in conducting business, the financial institution shall simultaneously verify and register the identity documents or other identity certification documents of both the agent and the principal.

When establishing a business relationship such as life insurance or trust, where the beneficiary of the contract is not the customer himself/herself, the financial institution shall also verify and register the identity documents or other identity certification documents of the beneficiary.

Financial institutions shall not provide services to or conduct transactions with customers whose identity is unclear, nor shall they open anonymous accounts or fictitious-name accounts for customers.

If a financial institution has doubts about the authenticity, validity, or completeness of previously obtained customer identity materials, it shall re-identify the customer.

Any entity or individual, when establishing a business relationship with a financial institution or requesting a one-time financial service from a financial institution, shall provide valid and authentic identity documents or other identity certification documents.

Article 17 Where a financial institution identifies a customer through a third party, it shall ensure that the third party has adopted customer identification measures that meet the requirements of this Law; if the third party has not adopted such measures, the financial institution shall bear the responsibility for failing to fulfill the customer identification obligation.

Article 18 When conducting customer identification, a financial institution may, if it deems necessary, verify the relevant identity information of the customer with public security, industrial and commercial administration, and other relevant departments.

Article 19 Financial institutions shall, in accordance with regulations, establish a system for preserving customer identity materials and transaction records.

During the duration of a business relationship, if customer identity materials change, they shall be updated promptly.

Customer identity materials shall be preserved for at least five years after the termination of the business relationship, and customer transaction information shall be preserved for at least five years after the completion of the transaction.

When a financial institution goes bankrupt or is dissolved, it shall transfer the customer identity materials and customer transaction information to an institution designated by the relevant department under the State Council.

Article 20 Financial institutions shall, in accordance with regulations, implement the system for reporting large-value transactions and suspicious transactions.

Where a single transaction handled by a financial institution, or cumulative transactions within a prescribed period, exceed the prescribed amount, or where a suspicious transaction is discovered, the financial institution shall promptly report to the Anti-Money Laundering Information Center.

Article 21 The specific measures for financial institutions to establish the customer identification system and the system for preserving customer identity materials and transaction records shall be formulated by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council jointly with the relevant financial regulatory authorities under the State Council. The specific measures for financial institutions to report large-value transactions and suspicious transactions shall be formulated by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council.

Article 22 Financial institutions shall carry out anti-money laundering training and publicity in accordance with the requirements of the anti-money laundering prevention and monitoring system.

## Chapter IV Anti-Money Laundering Investigations

Article 23 Where the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council or its provincial-level local office discovers a suspicious transaction activity that requires investigation and verification, it may conduct an investigation into the financial institution, and the financial institution shall cooperate and truthfully provide relevant documents and materials.

When investigating a suspicious transaction activity, there shall be no fewer than two investigators, who shall present their valid credentials and the investigation notice issued by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council or its provincial-level local office. If there are fewer than two investigators or if they fail to present valid credentials and the investigation notice, the financial institution has the right to refuse the investigation.

Article 24 When investigating a suspicious transaction activity, investigators may question relevant personnel of the financial institution and require them to explain the situation.

Questioning shall be recorded in written interrogation records. The interrogation records shall be presented to the person being questioned for verification. If there are omissions or errors in the records, the person being questioned may request supplements or corrections. After the person being questioned confirms that the records are accurate, they shall sign or affix their seal; the investigators shall also sign the records.

Article 25 Where further verification is needed during an investigation, with the approval of the person in charge of the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council or its provincial-level local office, investigators may consult and copy the account information, transaction records, and other relevant materials of the subject under investigation; documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, altered, or destroyed may be sealed up.

When sealing up documents and materials, investigators shall, together with the on-site staff of the financial institution, check and count them clearly, make an inventory in duplicate on the spot, which shall be signed or sealed by the investigators and the on-site staff of the financial institution, with one copy handed over to the financial institution and the other attached to the file for future reference.

Article 26 If, after investigation, the suspicion of money laundering cannot be ruled out, the matter shall be immediately reported to the investigative authority with jurisdiction. If the customer requests the transfer of funds in the accounts involved in the investigation abroad, with the approval of the person in charge of the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council, temporary freezing measures may be taken.

After receiving the report, the investigative authority shall promptly decide whether to continue freezing the funds that have been temporarily frozen in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph. If the investigative authority deems it necessary to continue freezing, it shall take freezing measures in accordance with the provisions of the Criminal Procedure Law; if it deems it unnecessary to continue freezing, it shall immediately notify the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council, which shall immediately notify the financial institution to lift the freeze.

Temporary freezing shall not exceed forty-eight hours. If, within forty-eight hours after taking temporary freezing measures at the request of the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council, the financial institution does not receive a notice from the investigative authority to continue the freeze, it shall immediately lift the freeze.

## Chapter V International Cooperation in Anti-Money Laundering

Article 27 The People's Republic of China shall carry out international cooperation in anti-money laundering in accordance with the international treaties it has concluded or acceded to, or on the principle of equality and mutual benefit.

Article 28 The anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council shall, as authorized by the State Council, represent the Chinese government in conducting anti-money laundering cooperation with foreign governments and relevant international organizations, and shall exchange information and materials related to anti-money laundering with overseas anti-money laundering institutions in accordance with the law.

Article 29 Judicial assistance involving the investigation of money laundering crimes shall be handled by the judicial authorities in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 30 Where personnel of the anti-money laundering administrative authority and other departments and institutions legally responsible for anti-money laundering supervision and administration who are engaged in anti-money laundering work commit any of the following acts, they shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Conducting inspections, investigations, or taking temporary freezing measures in violation of regulations;
(2) Disclosing state secrets, commercial secrets, or personal privacy learned of due to anti-money laundering work;
(3) Imposing administrative penalties on relevant institutions and personnel in violation of regulations;
(4) Other acts of failing to perform duties in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Where a financial institution commits any of the following acts, the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council or its authorized local office at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if the circumstances are serious, it shall recommend that the relevant financial regulatory authority order the financial institution to impose disciplinary sanctions on the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to establish an internal control system for anti-money laundering in accordance with regulations;
(2) Failing to establish a specialized anti-money laundering unit or designate an internal department to be responsible for anti-money laundering work in accordance with regulations;
(3) Failing to provide anti-money laundering training to employees in accordance with regulations.

Article 32 Where a financial institution commits any of the following acts, the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council or its authorized local office at or above the level of a city divided into districts shall order it to make corrections within a specified time limit; if the circumstances are serious, it shall impose a fine of not less than 200,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan on the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to fulfill the customer identification obligation in accordance with regulations;
(2) Failing to preserve customer identity materials and transaction records in accordance with regulations;
(3) Failing to submit reports on large-value transactions or suspicious transactions in accordance with regulations;
(4) Conducting transactions with customers whose identity is unclear or opening anonymous accounts or fictitious-name accounts for customers;
(5) Violating confidentiality provisions and disclosing relevant information;
(6) Refusing or obstructing anti-money laundering inspections or investigations;
(7) Refusing to provide investigation materials or intentionally providing false materials.

If a financial institution commits any of the acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph, resulting in the occurrence of money laundering consequences, it shall be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 5,000,000 yuan, and the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are particularly serious, the anti-money laundering administrative authority may recommend that the relevant financial regulatory authority order the suspension of business for rectification or revoke its business license.

For the directly responsible directors, senior management personnel, and other directly responsible personnel of a financial institution that has the circumstances specified in the preceding two paragraphs, the anti-money laundering administrative authority may recommend that the relevant financial regulatory authority order the financial institution to impose disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the law, or recommend the cancellation of their qualifications for holding office or prohibit them from engaging in work in the relevant financial industry in accordance with the law.

Article 33 Whoever violates the provisions of this Law, if the act constitutes a crime, shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 34 For the purposes of this Law, "financial institutions" refers to policy banks, commercial banks, credit cooperatives, postal savings and remittance institutions, trust and investment companies, securities companies, futures brokerage companies, insurance companies, and other institutions engaged in financial business as determined and announced by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council, which are established in accordance with the law.

Article 35 The scope of specific non-financial institutions that shall fulfill anti-money laundering obligations, and the specific measures for them to fulfill anti-money laundering obligations and for their supervision and administration, shall be formulated by the anti-money laundering administrative authority under the State Council jointly with the relevant departments under the State Council.

Article 36 The monitoring of funds suspected of being related to terrorist activities shall be governed by this Law; where other laws provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Article 37 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 2007.
