# Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Sixth National People's Congress on January 20, 1986

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 2000

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Third Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Certain Laws" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on December 28, 2013

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated to strengthen the protection, enhancement, development, and rational utilization of fishery resources, develop artificial aquaculture, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of fishery producers, promote the development of fishery production, and meet the needs of socialist construction and the people's livelihood.

Article 2 All fishery production activities, including the cultivation and harvesting of aquatic animals and plants in the inland waters, tidal flats, territorial seas, exclusive economic zones, and all other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China, must comply with this Law.

Article 3 The State implements a policy for fishery production that prioritizes aquaculture, while simultaneously developing aquaculture, fishing, and processing, adapting measures to local conditions, and placing different emphases.

People's governments at all levels shall incorporate fishery production into national economic development plans, take measures to strengthen the unified planning and comprehensive utilization of water areas.

Article 4 The State encourages scientific research in fisheries, promotes advanced technology, and raises the level of fishery science and technology.

Article 5 Units and individuals that achieve outstanding results in enhancing and protecting fishery resources, developing fishery production, or conducting fishery scientific research shall be given spiritual or material rewards by people's governments at all levels.

Article 6 The competent fisheries administration department under the State Council shall be responsible for the nationwide fisheries work. The competent fisheries administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for fisheries work within their respective administrative regions. The competent fisheries administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level may establish fishery superintendency agencies in important fishing waters and fishing ports.

The competent fisheries administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level and their subordinate fishery superintendency agencies may appoint fishery inspectors. Fishery inspectors shall carry out tasks assigned by the competent fisheries administration departments and their subordinate fishery superintendency agencies.

Article 7 The State implements unified leadership and graded management for the supervision and administration of fisheries.

For marine fisheries, except for sea areas and specific fishery resource fishing grounds designated by the State Council to be supervised and administered by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council and its subordinate fishery superintendency agencies, supervision and administration shall be carried out by the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of the adjacent provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

For fisheries in rivers, lakes, and other waters, supervision and administration shall be carried out by the competent fisheries administration departments of the relevant people's governments at or above the county level according to administrative divisions; for waters crossing administrative regions, the relevant local people's governments at or above the county level shall negotiate and formulate management measures, or the competent fisheries administration department of the people's government at the next higher level and its subordinate fishery superintendency agency shall carry out supervision and administration.

Article 8 Foreigners and foreign fishing vessels entering the waters under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China to engage in fishery production or fishery resource investigation activities must obtain approval from the relevant competent department under the State Council and comply with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations of the People's Republic of China; if there are treaties or agreements concluded with the People's Republic of China, the provisions of such treaties or agreements shall prevail.

The national fishery administration and fishing port superintendency agency shall exercise the power of fishery administration and fishing port superintendency externally.

Article 9 The competent fisheries administration departments, their subordinate fishery superintendency agencies, and their staff members shall not participate in or engage in fishery production and business operations.

## Chapter II Aquaculture

Article 10 The State encourages units under ownership by the whole people, units under collective ownership, and individuals to make full use of water areas and tidal flats suitable for aquaculture to develop the aquaculture industry.

Article 11 The State conducts unified planning for the utilization of water areas and determines water areas and tidal flats that can be used for aquaculture. Units and individuals using water areas and tidal flats owned by the whole people and designated by the State plan for aquaculture shall apply to the competent fisheries administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level, and the people's government at the same level shall issue an aquaculture permit, authorizing the use of such water areas and tidal flats for aquaculture production. The specific measures for issuing aquaculture permits shall be formulated by the State Council.

Water areas and tidal flats owned by collectives or owned by the whole people but used by agricultural collective economic organizations may be contracted by individuals or collectives for aquaculture production.

Article 12 When issuing aquaculture permits, local people's governments at or above the county level shall give priority to local fishery producers.

Article 13 Disputes arising between parties over the use of water areas and tidal flats designated by the State plan for aquaculture for production shall be handled in accordance with the procedures stipulated by relevant laws. Before the dispute is resolved, neither party shall disrupt aquaculture production.

Article 14 The requisition of water areas and tidal flats owned by collectives for State construction shall be handled in accordance with the provisions on land requisition in the "Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 15 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to strengthen the protection of commercial fish production bases and important aquaculture waters in urban suburbs.

Article 16 The State encourages and supports the selection, breeding, and promotion of improved aquatic varieties. New aquatic varieties must be examined and approved by the National Committee for the Examination of Original and Improved Aquatic Varieties and promoted after being announced by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council.

The import and export of aquatic fry and fingerlings shall be subject to approval by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council or the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The production of aquatic fry and fingerlings shall be subject to approval by the competent fisheries administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, except for aquatic fry and fingerlings self-bred and self-used by fishery producers.

Article 17 The import and export of aquatic fry and fingerlings must undergo quarantine to prevent the introduction and spread of diseases. The specific quarantine work shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations on the entry and exit quarantine of animals and plants.

The introduction of genetically modified aquatic fry and fingerlings must undergo safety assessment, and the specific management work shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Article 18 The competent fisheries administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen technical guidance for aquaculture production and disease prevention and control.

Article 19 Those engaged in aquaculture production shall not use bait or feed containing toxic or harmful substances.

Article 20 Those engaged in aquaculture production shall protect the ecological environment of water areas, scientifically determine stocking density, and rationally apply bait, fertilizer, and drugs, and shall not cause environmental pollution of water areas.

## Chapter III Fishing Industry

Article 21 The State takes measures in terms of finance, credit, and taxation to encourage and support the development of distant-water fishing, and arranges inland and inshore fishing capacity based on the allowable catch of fishery resources.

Article 22 The State determines the total allowable catch of fishery resources based on the principle that the catch volume shall be lower than the growth volume of fishery resources, and implements a catch quota system. The competent fisheries administration department under the State Council is responsible for organizing the investigation and assessment of fishery resources to provide a scientific basis for implementing the catch quota system. The total catch quota for the inland seas, territorial seas, exclusive economic zones, and other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China shall be determined by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council and submitted to the State Council for approval before being broken down and assigned level by level; the total catch quota for important rivers and lakes designated by the State shall be determined or negotiated by the relevant people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and broken down and assigned level by level. The allocation of the total catch quota shall reflect the principles of fairness and justice, and the allocation methods and results must be disclosed to the public and subject to supervision.

The competent fisheries administration department under the State Council and the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the implementation of the catch quota system, and for those exceeding the catch quota targets assigned by the higher level, the quota shall be reduced in the following year's catch quota targets.

Article 23 The State implements a fishing license system for the fishing industry.

Fishing licenses for fishing operations in common fishing areas determined by agreements concluded between the People's Republic of China and relevant countries or on the high seas shall be approved and issued by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council. Fishing licenses for large-scale marine trawl and purse seine operations shall be approved and issued by the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government. Fishing licenses for other operations shall be approved and issued by the competent fisheries administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level; however, the issuance of fishing licenses for marine operations shall not exceed the vessel and gear control targets assigned by the State, and the specific measures shall be formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Fishing licenses shall not be bought, sold, leased, or transferred in any other form, and shall not be altered, forged, or counterfeited.

Those engaging in fishing operations in sea areas under the jurisdiction of other countries must obtain approval from the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council and comply with the relevant treaties or agreements concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China and the laws of the relevant countries.

Article 24 A fishing license may be issued only if the following conditions are met:

(1) Possession of a fishery vessel inspection certificate;
(2) Possession of a fishery vessel registration certificate;
(3) Compliance with other conditions stipulated by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council.

Fishing licenses approved and issued by the competent fisheries administration departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be compatible with the catch quota targets assigned by the competent fisheries administration department of the people's government at the next higher level.

Article 25 Units and individuals engaged in fishing operations must conduct operations in accordance with the provisions of the fishing license regarding the type of operation, location, time limit, quantity of fishing gear, and catch quota, and comply with the State's regulations on the protection of fishery resources. Large and medium-sized fishing vessels shall fill in fishing logs.

Article 26 Vessels constructed, renovated, purchased, or imported for fishing operations must pass inspection by the fishery vessel inspection department before being put into operation. The specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 27 The construction of fishing ports shall comply with the unified planning of the State and implement the principle of whoever invests benefits. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and administration of fishing ports located within their administrative regions and maintain the normal order of fishing ports.

## Chapter IV Enhancement and Protection of Fishery Resources

Article 28 The competent fisheries administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall conduct unified planning for the fishing waters under their management and take measures to enhance fishery resources. The competent fisheries administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level may collect fees for the enhancement and protection of fishery resources from benefiting units and individuals, which shall be used exclusively for the enhancement and protection of fishery resources. The measures for collecting fees for the enhancement and protection of fishery resources shall be formulated by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council jointly with the financial department and submitted to the State Council for approval before implementation.

Article 29 The State protects aquatic germplasm resources and their living environment, and establishes aquatic germplasm resource conservation areas in the main growth and breeding areas of aquatic germplasm resources with high economic value and genetic breeding value. Without approval from the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council, no unit or individual may engage in fishing activities within aquatic germplasm resource conservation areas.

Article 30 It is prohibited to use methods such as explosive fishing, poison fishing, or electric fishing that destroy fishery resources. It is prohibited to manufacture, sell, or use prohibited fishing gear. It is prohibited to fish in no-fishing zones or during closed seasons. It is prohibited to use nets with a mesh size smaller than the minimum mesh size. The proportion of juvenile fish in the catch shall not exceed the prescribed limit. It is prohibited to sell illegally caught fish in no-fishing zones or during closed seasons.

The varieties of key protected fishery resources and their catchable standards, no-fishing zones and closed seasons, prohibited or restricted fishing gear and methods, minimum mesh sizes, and other measures for protecting fishery resources shall be stipulated by the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council or the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 31 It is prohibited to catch fry and fingerlings of aquatic animals with significant economic value. If catching fry and fingerlings of significant economic value or prohibited gravid broodstock is necessary for aquaculture or other special needs, approval must be obtained from the competent fisheries administration department under the State Council or the competent fisheries administration departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and fishing shall be conducted within designated areas and time limits and according to quotas.

When diverting water for use in key production areas of aquatic animal fry and fingerlings, measures shall be taken to protect the fry and fingerlings.

Article 32 When constructing sluice gates or dams on the migration routes of fish, shrimp, or crabs that have a serious impact on fishery resources, the construction unit shall build fish passage facilities or take other remedial measures.

Article 33 For water bodies used for fisheries and also serving functions such as regulation, storage, and irrigation, the relevant competent departments shall determine the minimum water level line required for fishery production.

Article 34 It is prohibited to reclaim land from lakes by enclosure. Coastal tidal flats shall not be enclosed or reclaimed without approval from the people's government at or above the county level; important fry and fingerling bases and aquaculture sites shall not be enclosed or reclaimed.

Article 35 When conducting underwater blasting, exploration, or construction operations that have a serious impact on fishery resources, the operating unit shall consult in advance with the relevant competent fisheries administration department of the people's government at or above the county level and take measures to prevent or reduce damage to fishery resources; if losses to fishery resources are caused, the relevant people's government at or above the county level shall order compensation.

Article 36 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to protect and improve the ecological environment of fishing waters and prevent pollution.

The supervision and management of the ecological environment of fishing waters and the investigation and handling of fishery pollution accidents shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 37 The State provides key protection for precious and endangered aquatic wild animals such as the Chinese river dolphin (baiji) to prevent their extinction. It is prohibited to kill or harm aquatic wild animals under key State protection. If catching aquatic wild animals under key State protection is necessary for scientific research, domestication and breeding, exhibition, or other special circumstances, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the "Wild Animal Protection Law of the People's Republic of China."

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 38 Those who fish using methods such as explosive fishing, poison fishing, or electric fishing that destroy fishery resources, fish in violation of regulations on no-fishing zones or closed seasons, or use prohibited fishing gear, fishing methods, or nets with a mesh size smaller than the minimum mesh size, or whose catch contains juvenile fish exceeding the prescribed proportion, shall have their catch and illegal gains confiscated and be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their fishing gear shall be confiscated and their fishing license revoked; if the circumstances are especially serious, their fishing vessel may be confiscated; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

If illegally caught fish are sold in no-fishing zones or during closed seasons, the competent fisheries administration department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall promptly investigate and handle the matter.

Those who manufacture or sell prohibited fishing gear shall have the illegally manufactured or sold fishing gear and illegal gains confiscated and be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 39 Those who steal or rob aquatic products cultivated by others, or damage the aquaculture water bodies or facilities of others, shall be ordered to make corrections and may be fined not more than 20,000 yuan; if losses are caused to others, they shall bear compensation liability according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 40 Those who use water areas or tidal flats owned by the whole people for aquaculture production and, without justifiable reasons, allow the water areas or tidal flats to lie idle for a full year, shall be ordered by the authority that issued the aquaculture permit to develop and utilize them within a specified time limit; if they fail to develop and utilize them within the time limit, their aquaculture permit shall be revoked, and they may be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Those who engage in aquaculture production in water areas owned by the whole people without lawfully obtaining an aquaculture permit shall be ordered to make corrections, obtain an aquaculture permit retroactively, or dismantle the aquaculture facilities within a specified time limit.

Those who engage in aquaculture production in water areas owned by the whole people without lawfully obtaining an aquaculture permit or beyond the scope permitted by the aquaculture permit, thereby obstructing navigation or flood discharge, shall be ordered to dismantle the aquaculture facilities within a specified time limit and may be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 41 Those who engage in fishing without lawfully obtaining a fishing license shall have their catch and illegal gains confiscated and be fined not more than 100,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their fishing gear and fishing vessel may also be confiscated.

Article 42 Those who fish in violation of the provisions of the fishing license regarding the type of operation, location, time limit, and quantity of fishing gear shall have their catch and illegal gains confiscated and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their fishing gear may also be confiscated and their fishing license revoked.

Article 43 Those who alter, buy, sell, lease, or transfer a fishing license in any other form shall have their illegal gains confiscated, their fishing license revoked, and may be fined not more than 10,000 yuan; those who forge, alter, or buy or sell a fishing license, if a crime is constituted, shall be pursued for criminal liability according to law.

Article 44 Those who illegally produce, import, or export aquatic fry and fingerlings shall have the fry and fingerlings and illegal gains confiscated and be fined not more than 50,000 yuan.

Those who deal in unexamined aquatic fry and fingerlings shall be ordered to immediately cease operations, have their illegal gains confiscated, and may be fined not more than 50,000 yuan.

Article 45 Those who engage in fishing activities within aquatic germplasm resource conservation areas without approval shall be ordered to immediately cease fishing, have their catch and fishing gear confiscated, and may be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 46 Foreigners or foreign fishing vessels that, in violation of this Law, enter the waters under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China without authorization to engage in fishery production or fishery resource investigation activities shall be ordered to leave or be expelled, and their catch and fishing gear may be confiscated, and they may be fined not more than 500,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, their fishing vessel may be confiscated; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

Article 47 Those who cause damage to the ecological environment of fishing waters or fishery pollution accidents shall be pursued for legal liability in accordance with the provisions of the "Marine Environment Protection Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Water Pollution Prevention and Control Law of the People's Republic of China."

Article 48 The administrative penalties stipulated in this Law shall be decided by the competent fisheries administration department of the people's government at or above the county level or its subordinate fishery superintendency agency, except where this Law has already stipulated the penalizing authority.

When enforcing the law at sea, for violations of regulations on no-fishing zones or closed seasons, or the use of prohibited fishing gear or fishing methods for fishing, as well as fishing without obtaining a fishing license, if the facts are clear and the evidence is sufficient, but the administrative penalty decision cannot be made and executed on the spot in accordance with legal procedures, the fishing license, fishing gear, or fishing vessel may be temporarily detained, and the administrative penalty decision shall be made and executed according to law after returning to port.

Article 49 If the competent fisheries administration department, its subordinate fishery superintendency agency, or their staff members violate the provisions of this Law by issuing licenses, allocating catch quotas, or engaging in fishery production and business operations, or commit other acts of dereliction of duty, abuse of power, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 50 This Law shall come into force as of July 1, 1986.
