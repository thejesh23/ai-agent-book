# Agriculture Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Second Session of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 2, 1993

Revised at the Thirty-first Session of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on December 28, 2002

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the Tenth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Agriculture Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the Thirtieth Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 28, 2012

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of consolidating and strengthening the foundational role of agriculture in the national economy, deepening rural reform, developing agricultural productivity, advancing agricultural modernization, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, increasing farmers' income, improving farmers' scientific and cultural literacy, promoting the sustained, stable and healthy development of agriculture and the rural economy, and achieving the goal of building a moderately prosperous society in all respects.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "agriculture" refers to industries such as crop cultivation, forestry, animal husbandry and fishery, including services directly related to pre-production, in-production and post-production.

For the purposes of this Law, "agricultural production and operation organizations" refer to rural collective economic organizations, specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations, agricultural enterprises and other organizations engaged in agricultural production and operation.

Article 3 The State places agriculture in the primary position in the development of the national economy.

The basic objectives for the development of agriculture and the rural economy are: to establish a rural economic system that meets the requirements of the socialist market economy, to continuously emancipate and develop rural productive forces, to improve the overall quality and efficiency of agriculture, to ensure the supply and quality of agricultural products to meet the needs of national economic development, population growth and improvement of living standards, to raise farmers' income and living standards, to promote the transfer of surplus rural labor to non-agricultural industries and towns and cities, to narrow the gap between urban and rural areas and between regions, to build a prosperous, democratic and civilized new socialist countryside, and to gradually realize agricultural and rural modernization.

Article 4 The State takes measures to ensure that agriculture better plays its role in providing food, industrial raw materials and other agricultural products, maintaining and improving the ecological environment, and promoting rural economic and social development.

Article 5 The State upholds and improves the basic economic system under which public ownership is dominant and economic entities under diverse forms of ownership develop side by side, and revitalizes the rural economy.

The State maintains for a long time to come the two-tier management system that combines centralized and decentralized operations on the basis of household contract management in rural areas, develops the socialized service system, strengthens the collective economic strength, and guides farmers onto the path of common prosperity.

The State upholds and improves in rural areas the distribution system under which distribution according to work is dominant and multiple modes of distribution coexist.

Article 6 The State adheres to the policy of revitalizing agriculture through science and education and promoting sustainable agricultural development.

The State takes measures to strengthen agricultural and rural infrastructure construction, adjust and optimize the agricultural and rural economic structure, promote industrialized agricultural operation, develop agricultural science, technology and education, protect the agricultural ecological environment, promote agricultural mechanization and informatization, and improve comprehensive agricultural production capacity.

Article 7 The State protects the property and other legitimate rights and interests of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations from infringement.

People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall take measures to increase farmers' income and effectively reduce their burdens.

Article 8 The whole society shall attach great importance to agriculture and support its development.

The State awards units and individuals that have made remarkable achievements in developing agriculture and the rural economy.

Article 9 People's governments at all levels shall be uniformly responsible for the work of agricultural and rural economic development, and organize relevant departments and the whole society to do a good job in developing agriculture and providing services for agricultural development.

The competent administrative department for agriculture under the State Council shall be in charge of the work of agricultural and rural economic development nationwide, and the competent administrative department for forestry under the State Council and other relevant departments shall be responsible for the relevant work of agricultural and rural economic development within their respective functions and duties.

The competent administrative departments for agriculture of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the work of agricultural and rural economic development, including crop cultivation, animal husbandry and fishery, within their respective administrative regions, and the competent administrative departments for forestry shall be responsible for forestry work within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the work of providing services for agricultural production and operation within their respective administrative regions and within their respective functions and duties.

## Chapter II Agricultural Production and Operation System

Article 10 The State implements the system of rural land contract operation, guarantees the long-term stability of rural land contractual relationships according to law, and protects farmers' right to use contracted land.

The methods and duration of rural land contract operation, the rights and obligations of the party awarding the contract and the contractor, and the protection and transfer of the right to land contract operation shall be governed by the Land Administration Law of the People's Republic of China and the Law of the People's Republic of China on Rural Land Contracts.

On the basis of household contract operation, rural collective economic organizations shall manage collective assets according to law, provide production, technology, information and other services for their members, organize the rational development and utilization of collective resources, and strengthen their economic strength.

Article 11 The State encourages farmers to voluntarily form various types of specialized cooperative economic organizations on the basis of household contract operation.

Specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations shall adhere to the purpose of serving their members, and carry out agricultural production, operation and service activities within the scope stipulated in their articles of association according to law, following the principles of voluntary membership, freedom of withdrawal, democratic management, and return of surplus.

Specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations may take various forms and shall be established and registered according to law. No organization or individual may infringe upon the property and operational autonomy of specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations.

Article 12 Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations may voluntarily invest capital, technology, physical objects, etc., as shares, and establish various types of enterprises according to law, following the principles of democratic management, distribution according to work and distribution according to shares.

Article 13 The State takes measures to develop various forms of industrialized agricultural operation, and encourages and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to develop integrated operations of production, processing and sales.

The State guides and supports enterprises, research institutions and other organizations engaged in the production, processing, circulation and service of agricultural products to form interest communities that share benefits and risks by concluding contracts with farmers or specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations or establishing various types of enterprises, thereby promoting industrialized agricultural operation and driving agricultural development.

Article 14 Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations may establish various agricultural product industry associations in accordance with laws and administrative regulations, to provide production, marketing, information, technology, training and other services for their members, play a coordinating and self-regulatory role, file applications for agricultural trade remedy measures, and safeguard the interests of their members and the industry.

## Chapter III Agricultural Production

Article 15 People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate agricultural development plans based on the medium and long-term plans for national economic and social development, the basic objectives of agricultural and rural economic development, and the regional planning of agricultural resources.

The competent administrative departments for agriculture under people's governments at or above the provincial level shall, in accordance with the agricultural development plans, take measures to leverage regional advantages, promote the formation of a rational regional distribution of agricultural production, and guide and coordinate the adjustment of the agricultural and rural economic structure.

Article 16 The State guides and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to adjust and optimize the agricultural production structure in light of local conditions and market demand, to coordinate the development of crop cultivation, forestry, animal husbandry and fishery, to develop high-quality, high-yield and high-efficiency agriculture, and to enhance the international competitiveness of agricultural products.

Crop cultivation shall focus on optimizing varieties, improving quality and increasing efficiency, and adjust the crop structure, variety structure and quality structure.

Strengthen the construction of forestry ecology, implement projects for the protection of natural forests, the conversion of cropland to forest and the control of desertification, strengthen the construction of shelterbelt systems, and accelerate the cultivation of fast-growing and high-yielding forests, industrial raw material forests and fuelwood forests.

Strengthen grassland protection and construction, accelerate the development of animal husbandry, promote pen feeding and stall feeding, improve livestock and poultry breeds, and actively develop the feed industry and livestock and poultry product processing industry.

Fishery production shall protect and rationally utilize fishery resources, adjust the fishing structure, and actively develop aquaculture, deep-sea fisheries and aquatic product processing industry.

People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate policies, allocate funds, and guide and support the adjustment of the agricultural structure.

Article 17 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen comprehensive agricultural development and the construction of agricultural and rural infrastructure, including farmland water conservancy, agricultural ecological environment protection, rural roads, rural energy and power grids, agricultural product storage and circulation, fishing ports, grassland fencing, and animal and plant germplasm and improved variety bases, improve agricultural production conditions, and protect and enhance comprehensive agricultural production capacity.

Article 18 The State supports the breeding, production, renewal and popularization of improved animal and plant varieties, encourages the combination of variety breeding with production and operation, and implements seed projects and improved livestock and poultry variety projects. The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall set up special funds to support the breeding and popularization of improved animal and plant varieties.

Article 19 People's governments at all levels and agricultural production and operation organizations shall strengthen the construction of farmland water conservancy facilities, establish and improve the management system for farmland water conservancy facilities, conserve water, develop water-saving agriculture, strictly control the occupation of irrigation water sources for non-agricultural construction according to law, and prohibit any organization or individual from illegally occupying or damaging farmland water conservancy facilities.

The State gives priority support to water-deficient areas in developing water-saving agriculture.

Article 20 The State encourages and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to use advanced and applicable agricultural machinery, strengthen the safety management of agricultural machinery, and improve the level of agricultural mechanization.

The State provides support to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations for the purchase of advanced agricultural machinery.

Article 21 People's governments at all levels shall support the development of the meteorological industry serving agriculture, and improve the level of monitoring and forecasting of meteorological disasters.

Article 22 The State takes measures to improve the quality of agricultural products, establish and improve the agricultural product quality standard system and the quality inspection, testing and supervision system, organize the production and operation of agricultural products in accordance with relevant technical specifications, operating procedures and quality, hygiene and safety standards, and ensure the quality and safety of agricultural products.

Article 23 The State supports the establishment of a system for the certification and labeling of high-quality agricultural products according to law.

The State encourages and supports the production of high-quality agricultural products. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in light of local conditions and in accordance with relevant State regulations, take measures to develop the production of high-quality agricultural products.

High-quality agricultural products that meet the standards prescribed by the State may apply for the use of relevant labels in accordance with the provisions of laws or administrative regulations. Agricultural products that meet the requirements of the prescribed origin and production specifications may apply for the use of geographical indications for agricultural products in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws or administrative regulations.

Article 24 The State implements a system for animal and plant epidemic prevention and quarantine, improves the animal and plant epidemic prevention and quarantine system, strengthens the monitoring, early warning and control of animal diseases and plant diseases, pests, weeds and rodents, establishes a rapid eradication mechanism for major animal epidemics and plant diseases and insect pests, builds animal disease-free zones, and implements plant protection projects.

Article 25 The production and operation of agricultural means of production that may endanger human and animal safety, such as pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed and feed additives, fertilizers, seeds and agricultural machinery, shall be subject to a registration or licensing system in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations.

People's governments at all levels shall establish and improve a safe use system for agricultural means of production. Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations shall not use agricultural means of production, such as pesticides, veterinary drugs and feed additives, that are explicitly eliminated or prohibited by the State, or other prohibited products.

Producers and sellers of agricultural means of production shall be responsible for the quality of the products they produce or sell, and are prohibited from passing off inferior products as high-quality ones, passing off fake products as genuine ones, or passing off unqualified products as qualified ones; they are also prohibited from producing or selling agricultural means of production, such as pesticides, veterinary drugs, feed additives and agricultural machinery, that are explicitly eliminated by the State.

## Chapter IV Circulation and Processing of Agricultural Products

Article 26 The purchase and sale of agricultural products shall be regulated by the market. The State shall exercise necessary macro-control over the purchase and sale of major agricultural products that are vital to the national economy and the people's livelihood, establish a system of graded reserve and regulation at the central and local levels, and improve the warehousing and transportation system to ensure supply and stabilize the market.

Article 27 The State shall gradually establish a unified, open, competitive and orderly agricultural product market system, and formulate development plans for agricultural product wholesale markets. The State provides support to rural collective economic organizations and specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations in establishing agricultural product wholesale markets and agricultural product trade fairs.

The administrative departments for industry and commerce and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, manage agricultural product wholesale markets according to law, regulate transaction order, and prevent local protectionism and unfair competition.

Article 28 The State encourages and supports the development of various forms of agricultural product circulation activities. It supports farmers and specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations in engaging in the purchase, wholesale, storage, transportation, retail and intermediary activities of agricultural products in accordance with relevant State regulations. It encourages supply and marketing cooperatives and other agricultural production and operation organizations engaged in the purchase and sale of agricultural products to provide market information, open up agricultural product circulation channels, and serve the sale of agricultural products.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to urge relevant departments to ensure smooth transportation of agricultural products and reduce the cost of agricultural product circulation. Relevant administrative departments shall simplify procedures to facilitate the transportation of fresh and live agricultural products, and shall not impound the means of transport for fresh and live agricultural products unless otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 29 The State supports the development of the agricultural product processing industry and the food industry to increase the added value of agricultural products. People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate development plans for the agricultural product processing industry and the food industry, guide agricultural product processing enterprises to form a rational regional layout and scale structure, and support specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations and township enterprises in engaging in agricultural product processing and comprehensive development and utilization.

The State establishes and improves quality standards for processed agricultural products, improves testing methods, and strengthens quality safety management and supervision during the processing of agricultural products to ensure food safety.

Article 30 The State encourages the development of import and export trade in agricultural products.

The State takes measures such as strengthening international market research and providing information and marketing services to promote the export of agricultural products.

In order to maintain the order of production and sale of agricultural products and fair trade, an early warning system for agricultural product imports shall be established. When certain imported agricultural products have caused or may cause a significant adverse impact on the production of relevant domestic agricultural products, the State may take necessary measures.

## Chapter V Food Security

Article 31 The State takes measures to protect and improve comprehensive grain production capacity, steadily raise the level of grain production, and ensure food security.

The State establishes a system for the protection of cultivated land and provides special protection for basic farmland according to law.

Article 32 The State gives priority support to major grain-producing areas in terms of policies, funds and technology, establishes stable commodity grain production bases, improves grain storage and processing facilities, and enhances the level of grain production, processing and economic efficiency in major grain-producing areas.

The State supports the establishment of stable purchase and sale cooperation relationships between major grain-producing areas and major grain-consuming areas.

Article 33 When the market price of grain is too low, the State Council may decide to implement a protective price system for certain grain varieties. The protective price shall be determined based on the principle of protecting farmers' interests and stabilizing grain production.

When farmers sell grain under the protective price system, the purchasing units entrusted by the State shall not refuse to purchase.

People's governments at or above the county level shall organize finance, banking and other departments, as well as the purchasing units entrusted by the State, to raise grain purchase funds in a timely manner. No department, unit or individual may withhold or misappropriate such funds.

Article 34 The State establishes an early warning system for food security and takes measures to ensure grain supply. The State Council shall formulate food security guarantee targets and grain reserve quantity indicators, and organize relevant competent departments to conduct inspections of cultivated land and grain stockpiles as needed.

The State implements a system of graded reserve and regulation of grain at the central and local levels, and builds a warehousing and transportation system. Enterprises undertaking the task of national grain reserves shall ensure the quantity and quality of the reserved grain in accordance with State regulations.

Article 35 The State establishes a grain risk fund to support grain reserves, stabilize the grain market and protect farmers' interests.

Article 36 The State advocates the cherishing and conservation of grain, and takes measures to improve the nutritional structure of the people's diet.

## Chapter VI Agricultural Inputs and Support and Protection

Article 37 The State establishes and improves the agricultural support and protection system, and adopts measures such as financial input, tax incentives and financial support to support farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in developing agricultural production and increasing farmers' income levels, in terms of capital investment, scientific research and technology promotion, education and training, supply of agricultural means of production, market information, quality standards, inspection and quarantine, socialized services, and disaster relief.

Without prejudice to the relevant international treaties concluded or acceded to by China, the State implements income support policies for farmers. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 38 The State gradually increases the overall level of agricultural input. The growth rate of the total annual agricultural input by the central and local finances at or above the county level shall be higher than the growth rate of their regular fiscal revenue.

The various funds allocated for agriculture in the fiscal budgets of people's governments at all levels shall be mainly used for: strengthening agricultural infrastructure construction; supporting agricultural restructuring and promoting industrialized agricultural operation; protecting comprehensive grain production capacity and ensuring national food security; improving the animal and plant quarantine and epidemic prevention system and strengthening the control of animal diseases and plant diseases, pests, weeds and rodents; establishing and improving the agricultural product quality standard and inspection, testing and supervision system, and the agricultural product market and information service system; supporting agricultural scientific research and education, agricultural technology extension and farmer training; strengthening agricultural ecological environment protection and construction; supporting the development of poverty-stricken areas; and ensuring farmers' income levels.

The investment in agricultural capital construction for crop cultivation, forestry, animal husbandry, fishery and farmland water conservancy by finances at or above the county level shall be coordinated and increase in a balanced manner.

In order to accelerate the development of the western region, the State increases input for agricultural development and ecological environment protection in the western region.

Article 39 The various funds allocated for agriculture in the annual fiscal budgets of people's governments at or above the county level shall be disbursed in full and on time. People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the supervision and management of the allocation and use of various State agricultural funds to ensure the safety of funds and improve the efficiency of fund use.

No unit or individual may withhold or misappropriate fiscal funds and credit funds used for agriculture. Audit authorities shall strengthen the audit and supervision of fiscal and credit funds used for agriculture according to law.

Article 40 The State uses tax, price, credit and other means to encourage and guide farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to increase investment in agricultural production and operation and in capital construction such as small-scale farmland water conservancy.

The State encourages and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to raise agricultural funds in various forms on a voluntary basis and according to law.

Article 41 The State encourages social funds to be invested in agriculture, and encourages enterprises, institutions, social organizations and individuals to donate funds to establish various agricultural construction and agricultural science, technology and education funds.The State shall take measures to promote the expansion of foreign investment utilization in agriculture.

Article 42 People's governments at all levels shall encourage and support enterprises, public institutions, and other types of economic organizations in providing agricultural information services.

The competent administrative departments for agriculture under people's governments at or above the county level, along with other relevant departments, shall establish a system for the collection, organization, and dissemination of agricultural information, and provide market information and other services to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in a timely manner.

Article 43 The State encourages and supports the development of the agricultural production means industry.

The State adopts tax, credit, and other measures to encourage and support the production and trade of agricultural production means, providing material guarantees for the stable growth of agricultural production.

The State employs macroeconomic regulation measures to maintain reasonable price ratios between major agricultural production means such as chemical fertilizers, pesticides, agricultural films, agricultural machinery, and agricultural diesel, and agricultural products.

Article 44 The State encourages supply and marketing cooperatives, rural collective economic organizations, specialized farmer cooperative economic organizations, other organizations, and individuals to develop various forms of socialized services for agricultural production before, during, and after the production process. People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall take measures to support agricultural socialized service undertakings.

For those engaged in cross-regional agricultural socialized services, departments such as agriculture, industry and commerce administration, transportation, and public security shall take measures to provide support.

Article 45 The State establishes and improves the rural financial system, strengthens the construction of the rural credit system, and enhances rural financial supervision.

Relevant financial institutions shall take measures to increase credit input, improve rural financial services, and provide credit support for the agricultural production and operation activities of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations.

Rural credit cooperatives shall adhere to the purpose of serving agriculture, farmers, and rural economic development, and give priority to providing credit services for the production and operation activities of local farmers.

The State encourages financial institutions to provide loans to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations for their agricultural production and operation activities through measures such as interest subsidies.

Article 46 The State establishes and improves the agricultural insurance system.

The State gradually establishes and improves a policy-based agricultural insurance system. It encourages and supports farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in establishing mutual aid cooperative insurance organizations serving agricultural production and operation activities, and encourages commercial insurance companies to carry out agricultural insurance business.

Agricultural insurance operates on a voluntary principle. No organization or individual may force farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations to participate in agricultural insurance.

Article 47 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to enhance the ability of agriculture to defend against natural disasters, do a good job in disaster prevention, resistance, and relief, help disaster victims resume production, organize production self-help, and carry out social mutual assistance and relief; provide relief and support to disaster victims lacking basic living guarantees.

## Chapter VII Agricultural Science and Technology and Agricultural Education

Article 48 The State Council and provincial people's governments shall formulate development plans for agricultural science and technology and agricultural education, and develop agricultural science and technology and education undertakings.

People's governments at or above the county level shall gradually increase funding for agricultural science and technology and agricultural education in accordance with relevant state regulations.

The State encourages and attracts enterprises and other social forces to increase investment in agricultural science and technology, and encourages farmers, agricultural production and operation organizations, enterprises, and public institutions to establish agricultural science and technology and education undertakings in accordance with the law.

Article 49 The State protects intellectual property rights such as new plant varieties and geographical indications of agricultural products, encourages and guides agricultural scientific research and education units to strengthen basic and applied research in agricultural science and technology, disseminate and popularize agricultural science and technology knowledge, accelerate the transformation and industrialization of scientific and technological achievements, and promote progress in agricultural science and technology.

Relevant departments of the State Council shall organize scientific and technological research on major key agricultural technologies. The State takes measures to promote international cooperation and exchange in agricultural science and technology and education, and encourages the introduction of advanced foreign technologies.

Article 50 The State supports the cause of agricultural technology extension, and establishes an agricultural technology extension system that combines government support with market guidance, paid services with free services, and national agricultural technology extension institutions with social forces, to promote the rapid application of advanced agricultural technologies in agricultural production.

Article 51 Agricultural technology extension institutions established by the State shall rely on agricultural technology experiment and demonstration bases, undertake public welfare responsibilities such as the extension and demonstration of key technologies needed by the public, and provide free agricultural technology services to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations.

People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the needs of agricultural production development, stabilize and strengthen the agricultural technology extension team, and ensure the working funds of agricultural technology extension institutions.

People's governments at all levels shall take measures, in accordance with state regulations, to ensure and improve the working conditions, salaries, and living conditions of professional scientific and technical personnel engaged in agricultural technology extension work, and encourage them to serve agriculture.

Article 52 Agricultural scientific research units, relevant schools, specialized farmer cooperatives, agriculture-related enterprises, mass scientific and technological organizations, and relevant scientific and technical personnel may, based on the needs of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, provide free services, or provide paid services and obtain legitimate income through forms such as technology transfer, technical services, technical contracting, technical consulting, and technology investment. Agricultural scientific research units, relevant schools, specialized farmer cooperatives, agriculture-related enterprises, mass scientific and technological organizations, and relevant scientific and technical personnel shall improve service levels and ensure service quality.

The State provides tax, credit, and other preferences to enterprises established by agricultural scientific research units, relevant schools, and agricultural technology extension institutions that serve agriculture.

The State encourages and supports farmers, supply and marketing cooperatives, other enterprises and public institutions, etc., to participate in agricultural technology extension work.

Article 53 The State establishes a continuing education system for agricultural professional and technical personnel. The competent administrative departments for agriculture under people's governments at or above the county level, together with relevant departments such as education and personnel, shall formulate continuing education plans for agricultural professional and technical personnel and organize their implementation.

Article 54 The State implements compulsory education in rural areas in accordance with the law and guarantees funding for compulsory education. The salaries of teaching and administrative staff in ordinary primary and secondary schools established by the State in rural areas shall be uniformly disbursed by the people's governments at the county level in accordance with state regulations, and the funds for the construction and maintenance of school buildings and other teaching facilities shall be uniformly arranged by the people's governments at the county level in accordance with state regulations.

Article 55 The State develops vocational education in agriculture. Relevant departments of the State Council, in accordance with the unified provisions of the national vocational qualification certificate system, carry out work on occupational classification and vocational skill appraisal in the agricultural industry, and manage vocational qualification certificates in the agricultural industry.

Article 56 The State takes measures to encourage farmers to adopt advanced agricultural technologies, supports farmers in establishing various scientific and technological organizations, and carries out training in practical agricultural techniques, farmer green certificate training, and other employment training to improve the cultural and technical quality of farmers.

## Chapter VIII Agricultural Resources and Agricultural Environmental Protection

Article 57 The development of agriculture and the rural economy must rationally utilize and protect natural resources such as land, water, forests, grasslands, and wild animals and plants, rationally develop and utilize renewable energy sources and clean energy such as hydropower, biogas, solar energy, and wind energy, develop ecological agriculture, and protect and improve the ecological environment.

People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate agricultural resource zoning or zoning for the rational utilization and protection of agricultural resources, and establish an agricultural resource monitoring system.

Article 58 Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations shall conserve cultivated land, rationally use chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and agricultural films, increase the use of organic fertilizers, adopt advanced technologies, protect and improve soil fertility, and prevent the pollution, destruction, and fertility decline of agricultural land.

The competent administrative departments for agriculture under people's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to support farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in strengthening the quality construction of cultivated land, and conduct regular monitoring of cultivated land quality.

Article 59 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to strengthen comprehensive management of small watersheds, and prevent and control soil erosion. Units and individuals engaged in production and construction activities that may cause soil erosion must take preventive measures and be responsible for controlling the soil erosion caused by their production and construction activities.

People's governments at all levels shall take measures to prevent land desertification and control desertified land. The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level in areas where desertified land is located shall formulate plans for preventing and controlling desertification in accordance with legal provisions and organize their implementation.

Article 60 The State implements a system of compulsory tree planting for all citizens. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to organize the masses to plant trees, protect forest land and trees, prevent forest fires, prevent and control forest pests and diseases, stop indiscriminate logging and illegal logging of trees, and increase the forest coverage rate.

The State implements a system of banning or restricting logging in natural forest protection areas, and strengthens afforestation and forest protection.

Article 61 Relevant local people's governments shall strengthen the protection, construction, and management of grasslands, guide and organize farmers (herders) and agricultural (pastoral) production and operation organizations to build artificial grasslands, forage and feed bases, and improve natural grasslands, implement a system of determining livestock numbers based on grass yield, control stocking rates, promote rotational grazing, rest grazing, and grazing prohibition systems, protect grassland vegetation, and prevent grassland degradation, desertification, and salinization.

Article 62 It is prohibited to destroy forests or grasslands for reclamation, burn mountains for reclamation, or reclaim steep slopes that the State prohibits from reclamation. Land already reclaimed shall be gradually returned to forests or grasslands.

It is prohibited to reclaim land from lakes or reclaim wetlands that the State prohibits from reclamation. Land already reclaimed shall be gradually returned to lakes or wetlands.

Farmers who implement returning farmland in areas within the scope of plans approved by the State Council shall receive subsidies in accordance with state regulations.

Article 63 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to implement the fishing quota system and the fishing ban and fishing moratorium system in accordance with the law, increase fishery resources, and protect the ecological environment of fishing waters.

The State guides and supports farmers (fishermen) and agricultural (fishery) production and operation organizations engaged in the fishing industry to engage in aquaculture or other occupations, and provides subsidies in accordance with state regulations to farmers (fishermen) who change their production and occupation according to the unified planning of the local people's government.

Article 64 The State establishes a system for the protection of biological species resources related to agricultural production, protects biodiversity, and provides key protection for rare, endangered, and precious biological resources and their original habitats. The introduction of biological species resources from abroad must be registered or approved in accordance with the law, and corresponding safety control measures must be taken.

Research, experimentation, production, processing, operation, and other applications of agricultural genetically modified organisms must strictly implement various safety control measures in accordance with state regulations.

Article 65 Competent administrative departments for agriculture at all levels shall guide farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to adopt biological measures or use highly effective, low-toxicity, low-residue pesticides and veterinary drugs to prevent and control diseases, pests, weeds, and rodents in animals and plants.

Straw and other residues after the harvest of agricultural products shall be comprehensively utilized and properly disposed of to prevent environmental pollution and ecological damage.

Units and individuals engaged in large-scale breeding of livestock, poultry, and other animals shall conduct harmless treatment or comprehensive utilization of manure, wastewater, and other wastes. Units and individuals engaged in aquaculture shall rationally feed, fertilize, and use drugs to prevent environmental pollution and ecological damage.

Article 66 People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to supervise relevant units in carrying out treatment and prevent pollution of the agricultural ecological environment by wastewater, waste gas, and solid waste. In the event of pollution accidents to the agricultural ecological environment caused by the discharge of wastewater, waste gas, and solid waste, the competent administrative department for environmental protection or the competent administrative department for agriculture shall investigate and handle the matter in accordance with the law; if losses are caused to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, the responsible parties shall compensate in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IX Protection of Farmers' Rights and Interests

Article 67 Any organ or unit collecting administrative or institutional fees from farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations must be based on the provisions of laws and regulations. The items, scope, and standards of fees shall be made public. Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations have the right to refuse fees that have no basis in laws or regulations.

Any organ or unit imposing fines or penalties on farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations must be based on the provisions of laws, regulations, or rules. Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations have the right to refuse fines that have no basis in laws, regulations, or rules.

No organ or unit may, in any manner, apportion expenses to farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations. Unless otherwise provided by laws or regulations, any request by any organ or unit, in any manner, for farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations to provide human, financial, or material resources constitutes apportionment. Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations have the right to refuse any form of apportionment.

Article 68 People's governments at all levels, their relevant departments, and their subordinate units shall not, in any manner, raise funds from farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations.

Without a basis in laws or regulations or without the approval of the State Council, no organ or unit may conduct any form of standard-reaching, upgrading, or acceptance inspection activities in rural areas.

Article 69 Farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations bear the obligation to pay taxes in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations. Tax authorities and units responsible for withholding and collecting taxes shall levy taxes in accordance with the law, and shall not illegally apportion taxes or levy taxes by other illegal methods.

Article 70 Except for fees collected in accordance with the provisions of the State Council for rural compulsory education, no other fees may be charged to farmers or students. No organ or unit is allowed to collect fees from farmers through rural primary and secondary schools.

Article 71 When the State expropriates land collectively owned by farmers in accordance with the law, it shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of farmers and rural collective economic organizations, provide compensation for land expropriation to farmers and rural collective economic organizations in accordance with the law, and no unit or individual may intercept or misappropriate the compensation fees for land expropriation.

Article 72 People's governments at all levels, rural collective economic organizations, or villagers' committees shall not, during processes such as agricultural and rural economic structural adjustment, industrialized agricultural operation, and the transfer of land contractual management rights, infringe upon farmers' land contractual management rights, interfere with farmers' independently arranged production and operation projects, or force farmers to purchase designated means of production or sell agricultural products through designated channels.

Article 73 If a rural collective economic organization or villagers' committee needs to raise funds or labor from its members (villagers) for developing production or running public welfare undertakings, it may do so only after the matter is approved by a majority vote of the members (villagers) meeting or the members (villagers) representative meeting.

When a rural collective economic organization or villagers' committee raises funds or labor in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, it shall not exceed the upper limit control standards stipulated by the people's government at or above the provincial level, and forcing the substitution of labor with funds is prohibited.

Rural collective economic organizations and villagers' committees shall disclose important matters concerning the interests of farmers to the public, regularly publish financial accounts, and accept the supervision of farmers.

Article 74 Any unit or individual providing paid services such as production, technology, information, culture, or insurance to farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations must adhere to the principle of voluntariness and shall not force farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations to accept the services.

Article 75 When purchasing agricultural products, purchasing units shall not downgrade the grade or reduce the price, nor deduct any fees from the payment amount. Where laws or administrative regulations provide for the withholding or collection of taxes, the matter shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the laws or administrative regulations.

If a dispute arises between the purchaser of agricultural products and the seller of agricultural products regarding the quality grade of the agricultural products, they may entrust a legally qualified agricultural product quality inspection institution for inspection.

Article 76 If a user of agricultural production means suffers losses due to quality problems of the production means, the operator who sold the production means shall compensate for the losses, and the compensation amount shall include the purchase price, relevant expenses, and lost profits.

Article 77 Farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations have the right to report situations and make legitimate requests to people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments to safeguard their own legitimate rights and interests. People's governments and their relevant departments shall promptly respond to the reasonable requests made by farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations in accordance with state regulations.

Article 78 If farmers' rights and interests are violated in violation of legal provisions, farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations may apply for administrative reconsideration or file a lawsuit with the People's Court in accordance with the law, and the relevant people's government and its relevant departments or the People's Court shall accept the case in accordance with the law.

The People's Court and the competent administrative department of justice shall provide legal aid to farmers in accordance with relevant regulations.

## Chapter X Rural Economic Development

Article 79 The State adheres to the policy of coordinated urban and rural development, supports the development of secondary and tertiary industries in rural areas, adjusts and optimizes the rural economic structure, increases farmers' income, promotes the comprehensive development of the rural economy, and gradually narrows the gap between urban and rural areas.

Article 80 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to develop township enterprises, support the development of agriculture, and transfer surplus agricultural labor.

The State improves support measures for the development of township enterprises, guides them to optimize their structure, update their technology, and improve their quality.

Article 81 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the local economic development level, locational advantages, and resource conditions, and in accordance with the principles of rational layout, scientific planning, and land conservation, promote the construction of small towns in rural areas with a focus.

Local people's governments at all levels shall focus on utilizing market mechanisms, improving corresponding policies, attracting farmers and social funds to invest in the development and construction of small towns, developing secondary and tertiary industries, and guiding the relatively concentrated development of township enterprises.

Article 82 The State takes measures to guide the rational and orderly flow of surplus rural labor between urban and rural areas and regions. Local people's governments at all levels shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of rural laborers who enter cities and towns for employment in accordance with the law, and shall not impose unreasonable restrictions; those already imposed shall be abolished.

Article 83 The State gradually improves the rural social relief system, ensuring the basic living standards of rural households enjoying the five guarantees (food, clothing, medical care, housing, and burial expenses), impoverished disabled farmers, impoverished elderly farmers, and other farmers who have lost their ability to work.

Article 84 The State encourages and supports farmers in consolidating and developing rural cooperative medical care and other forms of medical security to improve the health level of farmers.

Article 85 The State supports poverty-stricken areas in improving their economic development conditions and assists in economic development. Provincial people's governments, based on the national overall goals and requirements for supporting poverty-stricken areas, formulate poverty alleviation and development plans and organize their implementation.

People's governments at all levels shall adhere to the development-oriented poverty alleviation policy, organize farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations in poverty-stricken areas to use poverty alleviation funds rationally, rely on their own strength to change their poverty and backwardness, and guide farmers in poverty-stricken areas to adjust their economic structure and develop local resources. Poverty alleviation and development shall adhere to the combination of resource protection and ecological construction, promoting the coordinated development and comprehensive progress of the economy and society in poverty-stricken areas.

Article 86 Central and provincial finance departments shall include poverty alleviation and development investment in the annual fiscal budget, increase it year by year, and increase fiscal transfer payments and construction fund investment in poverty-stricken areas.

The State encourages and supports financial institutions, other enterprises and public institutions, and individuals to invest funds to support the development and construction of poverty-stricken areas.

Any unit or individual is prohibited from intercepting or misappropriating poverty alleviation funds. Audit institutions shall strengthen the audit and supervision of poverty alleviation funds.

## Chapter XI Law Enforcement Supervision

Article 87 People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to gradually improve the agricultural administrative management system that meets the requirements of the socialist market economy development.The competent administrative departments for agriculture and other relevant administrative departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen their responsibilities in planning, guidance, management, coordination, supervision, and service, and shall exercise their powers according to law and enforce the law impartially.

The competent administrative departments for agriculture of the local people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their scope of duties, improve the administrative law enforcement teams, implement comprehensive law enforcement, and enhance the efficiency and level of law enforcement.

Article 88 When performing their duties of supervision and inspection over law enforcement, the competent administrative departments for agriculture of the people's governments at or above the county level and their law enforcement personnel shall have the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Requiring the unit or individual under inspection to explain the situation and provide relevant documents, licenses, and materials;

(2) Ordering the unit or individual under inspection to cease acts violating this Law and to perform statutory obligations.

When performing their duties of supervision and inspection, agricultural administrative law enforcement personnel shall present their administrative law enforcement credentials to the unit or individual under inspection and comply with law enforcement procedures. The relevant unit or individual shall cooperate with agricultural administrative law enforcement personnel in lawfully performing their duties and shall not refuse or obstruct them.

Article 89 The competent administrative departments for agriculture and agricultural production and operation units must be completely separated in terms of institutions, personnel, and finances. The competent administrative departments for agriculture and their staff shall not participate in or engage in agricultural production and operation activities.

## Chapter XII Legal Liability

Article 90 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, infringes upon the property rights, such as the right to land contractual management, or other lawful rights and interests of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations shall cease the infringement and restore the original state; if losses or damages are caused, they shall bear compensation liability according to law.

If a state functionary, by taking advantage of his position or under any other pretext, infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, he shall compensate for the losses, and shall be given administrative sanctions by the unit to which he belongs or by the competent authority at a higher level.

Article 91 Anyone who violates the provisions of Articles 19, 25, 62, and 71 of this Law shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws or administrative regulations.

Article 92 Anyone who commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent authority at a higher level to return the intercepted or misappropriated funds within a specified time limit, and the illegal gains shall be confiscated; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by the competent authority at a higher level or by the unit to which they belong; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law:

(1) Intercepting or misappropriating funds for grain purchase in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 3 of Article 33 of this Law;

(2) Intercepting or misappropriating financial funds and credit funds for agriculture in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 39 of this Law;

(3) Intercepting or misappropriating poverty alleviation funds in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 3 of Article 86 of this Law.

Article 93 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 67 of this Law, illegally collects fees, imposes fines, or apportions expenses on farmers or agricultural production and operation organizations shall be stopped by the competent authority at a higher level, and a public announcement shall be made; if money has already been collected or manpower or material resources have already been used, the competent authority at a higher level shall order the return of the collected money within a specified time limit or compensate for the manpower and material resources used at a discounted price; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by the competent authority at a higher level or by the unit to which they belong; if the circumstances are serious and constitute a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

Article 94 Anyone who commits one of the following acts shall be ordered by the competent authority at a higher level to cease the illegal act, and the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions, and the illegally collected pooled funds, taxes, or fees shall be ordered to be returned:

(1) Illegally conducting fundraising, reaching standards, upgrading, or acceptance inspection activities in rural areas in violation of the provisions of Article 68 of this Law;

(2) Levying taxes on farmers by illegal methods in violation of the provisions of Article 69 of this Law;

(3) Charging farmers fees in excess of the prescribed amounts or items through rural primary and secondary schools in violation of the provisions of Article 70 of this Law.

Article 95 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 73 of this Law, forces farmers to contribute labor in lieu of funds shall be ordered by the people's government of the township (town) to make corrections and return the illegally collected funds.

Article 96 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of Article 74 of this Law, forces farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations to accept paid services shall be ordered by the relevant people's government to make corrections and return the illegally collected fees; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions; if losses are caused to farmers and agricultural production and operation organizations, compensation liability shall be borne according to law.

Article 97 If a staff member of the competent administrative department for agriculture of a people's government at or above the county level participates in or engages in agricultural production and operation activities in violation of the provisions of this Law, he shall be given administrative sanctions according to law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law.

## Chapter XIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 98 The provisions of this Law concerning farmers shall apply to employees of state-owned farms, pastures, forests, fisheries, and other enterprises and institutions who have contracted for operation.

Article 99 This Law shall come into force as of March 1, 2003.
