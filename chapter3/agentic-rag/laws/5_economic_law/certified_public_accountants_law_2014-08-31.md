# Certified Public Accountants Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 31, 1993

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Five Laws Including the Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China at the Tenth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted with a view to giving play to the role of certified public accountants in attestation and service in social and economic activities, strengthening the administration of certified public accountants, safeguarding public interests and the legitimate rights and interests of investors, and promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 A certified public accountant is a practitioner who has obtained a certified public accountant certificate in accordance with the law and is engaged in auditing, accounting consultancy, and accounting services upon engagement.

Article 3 An accounting firm is an institution established in accordance with the law to undertake the business of certified public accountants.

A certified public accountant shall join an accounting firm to practice.

Article 4 The Institute of Certified Public Accountants is a social organization composed of certified public accountants. The Chinese Institute of Certified Public Accountants is the national organization of certified public accountants, and the institutes of certified public accountants of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government are the local organizations of certified public accountants.

Article 5 The finance department of the State Council and the finance departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and guide certified public accountants, accounting firms, and the institutes of certified public accountants.

Article 6 Certified public accountants and accounting firms must abide by laws and administrative regulations when practicing.

Certified public accountants and accounting firms shall practice independently and impartially in accordance with the law and are protected by law.

## Chapter II Examination and Registration

Article 7 The State implements a national unified examination system for certified public accountants. The measures for the national unified examination for certified public accountants shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council and organized and implemented by the Chinese Institute of Certified Public Accountants.

Article 8 Chinese citizens who have graduated from junior college or above, or who have intermediate or higher technical titles in accounting or related disciplines, may apply to take the national unified examination for certified public accountants; persons with senior technical titles in accounting or related disciplines may be exempted from part of the examination subjects.

Article 9 Persons who have passed the national unified examination for certified public accountants and have been engaged in auditing for more than two years may apply for registration with the institute of certified public accountants of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Unless the circumstances listed in Article 10 of this Law exist, the institute of certified public accountants that accepts the application shall grant registration.

Article 10 The institute of certified public accountants that accepts the application shall not grant registration under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Lacking full capacity for civil conduct;
(2) Having been subjected to criminal punishment, and less than five years have elapsed from the date of completion of the execution of the punishment to the date of application for registration;
(3) Having committed serious errors in financial, accounting, auditing, enterprise management, or other economic management work, and having been subjected to administrative sanctions or sanctions of removal from office or above, and less than two years have elapsed from the date of the decision on the sanction to the date of application for registration;
(4) Having been subjected to the penalty of revocation of the certified public accountant certificate, and less than five years have elapsed from the date of the decision on the penalty to the date of application for registration;
(5) Other circumstances where registration is not granted as prescribed by the finance department of the State Council.

Article 11 The institute of certified public accountants shall report the list of persons granted registration to the finance department of the State Council for the record. If the finance department of the State Council finds that the registration by the institute of certified public accountants does not conform to the provisions of this Law, it shall notify the relevant institute of certified public accountants to revoke the registration.

If the institute of certified public accountants does not grant registration in accordance with Article 10 of this Law, it shall notify the applicant in writing within fifteen days from the date of the decision. If the applicant disagrees, it may apply for reconsideration to the finance department of the State Council or the finance department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the notice.

Article 12 For an applicant granted registration, the institute of certified public accountants shall issue a certified public accountant certificate uniformly formulated by the finance department of the State Council.

Article 13 For a person who has obtained a certified public accountant certificate, except for the circumstances specified in the first paragraph of Article 11 of this Law, if any of the following circumstances occurs after registration, the institute of certified public accountants that granted the registration shall revoke the registration and withdraw the certified public accountant certificate:

(1) Completely losing capacity for civil conduct;
(2) Being subjected to criminal punishment;
(3) Having committed serious errors in financial, accounting, auditing, enterprise management, or other economic management work, and having been subjected to administrative sanctions or sanctions of removal from office or above;
(4) Voluntarily ceasing to practice as a certified public accountant for a full year.

If the party whose registration has been revoked disagrees, it may apply for reconsideration to the finance department of the State Council or the finance department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the notice of revocation of registration and withdrawal of the certified public accountant certificate.

A person whose registration has been revoked in accordance with the first paragraph may reapply for registration, but must meet the requirements of Articles 9 and 10 of this Law.

## Chapter III Scope of Business and Rules

Article 14 Certified public accountants undertake the following auditing business:

(1) Examining the financial statements of an enterprise and issuing audit reports;
(2) Verifying the capital of an enterprise and issuing capital verification reports;
(3) Handling auditing business in matters of merger, division, and liquidation of an enterprise and issuing relevant reports;
(4) Other auditing business prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

Reports issued by certified public accountants in accordance with the law when performing auditing business have evidentiary effect.

Article 15 Certified public accountants may undertake accounting consultancy and accounting services.

Article 16 The business undertaken by a certified public accountant shall be uniformly accepted by the accounting firm to which it belongs, and the accounting firm shall sign an engagement contract with the client.

The accounting firm shall bear civil liability for the business undertaken by its certified public accountants in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 17 When practicing, a certified public accountant may, as necessary, review the relevant accounting materials and documents of the client, inspect the client's business premises and facilities, and require the client to provide other necessary assistance.

Article 18 A certified public accountant who has an interest in the client shall withdraw; the client has the right to request its withdrawal.

Article 19 A certified public accountant has a duty of confidentiality regarding commercial secrets learned during practice.

Article 20 When performing auditing business, a certified public accountant shall refuse to issue relevant reports under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The client suggests making an untrue or improper certification;
(2) The client intentionally fails to provide relevant accounting materials and documents;
(3) Due to other unreasonable requirements of the client, the report issued by the certified public accountant cannot correctly state important matters of financial accounting.

Article 21 When performing auditing business, a certified public accountant must issue reports in accordance with the working procedures determined by the professional standards and rules.

When issuing a report after performing auditing business, a certified public accountant shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) Knowingly failing to point out that the client's financial accounting treatment of important matters conflicts with relevant state regulations;
(2) Knowingly concealing or making an untrue report regarding the client's financial accounting treatment that would directly harm the interests of report users or other interested parties;
(3) Knowingly failing to point out that the client's financial accounting treatment would cause significant misunderstanding among report users or other interested parties;
(4) Knowingly failing to point out other untrue content in important matters of the client's financial statements.

If the certified public accountant should have known of the acts listed in the preceding paragraph by the client in accordance with professional standards and rules, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 22 A certified public accountant shall not engage in any of the following acts:

(1) During the period of performing auditing business, within the period prescribed by laws and administrative regulations during which it is prohibited to buy or sell the stocks or bonds of the audited entity or to purchase other property of the audited entity or individual, buying or selling the stocks or bonds of the audited entity or purchasing other property owned by the audited entity or individual;
(2) Soliciting or accepting remuneration or other property beyond the engagement contract, or seeking other improper benefits by taking advantage of the practice;
(3) Accepting an engagement to collect debts;
(4) Allowing others to practice in their own name;
(5) Practicing in two or more accounting firms simultaneously;
(6) Advertising their abilities to solicit business;
(7) Other acts that violate laws and administrative regulations.

## Chapter IV Accounting Firms

Article 23 An accounting firm may be established as a partnership by certified public accountants.

The debts of an accounting firm established as a partnership shall be borne by the partners with their respective properties in proportion to their capital contributions or as agreed in the agreement. The partners shall bear joint and several liability for the debts of the accounting firm.

Article 24 An accounting firm that meets the following conditions may be a legal person with limited liability:

(1) Having a registered capital of not less than 300,000 yuan;
(2) Having a certain number of full-time practitioners, including at least five certified public accountants;
(3) Other conditions regarding the scope of business and other matters prescribed by the finance department of the State Council.

An accounting firm with limited liability shall bear liability for its debts with all its assets.

Article 25 The establishment of an accounting firm shall be approved by the finance department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

To apply for the establishment of an accounting firm, the applicant shall submit the following documents to the approving authority:

(1) A written application;
(2) The name, organizational structure, and business premises of the accounting firm;
(3) The articles of association of the accounting firm, and if there is a partnership agreement, the partnership agreement shall also be submitted;
(4) A list of certified public accountants, their resumes, and relevant certifying documents;
(5) The names and resumes of the principal responsible persons and partners of the accounting firm, and relevant certifying documents;
(6) The capital contribution certificate of the accounting firm with limited liability;
(7) Other documents required by the approving authority.

Article 26 The approving authority shall decide whether to approve or disapprove within thirty days from the date of receipt of the application documents.

An accounting firm approved by the finance department of the people's government of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be reported to the finance department of the State Council for the record. If the finance department of the State Council finds the approval inappropriate, it shall notify the original approving authority to re-examine within thirty days from the date of receipt of the record report.

Article 27 The establishment of a branch by an accounting firm shall be approved by the finance department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government where the branch is located.

Article 28 Accounting firms shall pay taxes in accordance with the law.

Accounting firms shall establish a professional risk fund and handle professional insurance in accordance with the provisions of the finance department of the State Council.

Article 29 The acceptance of business by an accounting firm is not limited by administrative region or industry; however, except as otherwise provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 30 No unit or individual may interfere when a client engages an accounting firm to handle business.

Article 31 The provisions of Articles 18 to 21 of this Law apply to accounting firms.

Article 32 An accounting firm shall not engage in the acts listed in items (1) to (4), (6), and (7) of Article 22 of this Law.

## Chapter V Institute of Certified Public Accountants

Article 33 Certified public accountants shall join the Institute of Certified Public Accountants.

Article 34 The articles of association of the Chinese Institute of Certified Public Accountants shall be formulated by the National Congress of Members and reported to the finance department of the State Council for the record; the articles of association of the institute of certified public accountants of a province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall be formulated by the congress of members of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government and reported to the finance department of the people's government of that province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government for the record.

Article 35 The Chinese Institute of Certified Public Accountants shall, in accordance with the law, draft professional standards and rules for certified public accountants, which shall be implemented after approval by the finance department of the State Council.

Article 36 The Institute of Certified Public Accountants shall support certified public accountants in practicing in accordance with the law, protect their legitimate rights and interests, and reflect their opinions and suggestions to relevant parties.

Article 37 The Institute of Certified Public Accountants shall conduct annual inspections on the qualifications and practice of certified public accountants.

Article 38 The Institute of Certified Public Accountants shall obtain the status of a social organization legal person in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 39 If an accounting firm violates the provisions of Articles 20 and 21 of this Law, the finance department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall give a warning, confiscate illegal income, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income; if the circumstances are serious, the finance department of the people's government at or above the provincial level may suspend its business operations or revoke it.

If a certified public accountant violates the provisions of Articles 20 and 21 of this Law, the finance department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall give a warning; if the circumstances are serious, the finance department of the people's government at or above the provincial level may suspend its practice or revoke its certified public accountant certificate.

If an accounting firm or certified public accountant violates the provisions of Articles 20 and 21 of this Law and intentionally issues false audit reports or capital verification reports, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 40 If a unit undertakes the certified public accountant business prescribed in Article 14 of this Law without approval, the finance department of the people's government at or above the provincial level shall order it to cease its illegal activities, confiscate illegal income, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal income.

Article 41 If a party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision, it may apply for reconsideration to the authority at the next higher level of the authority that made the penalty decision within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the penalty notice; the party may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the penalty decision notice.

The reconsideration authority shall make a reconsideration decision within sixty days from the date of receipt of the reconsideration application. If the party disagrees with the reconsideration decision, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the reconsideration decision. If the reconsideration authority fails to make a reconsideration decision within the time limit, the party may file a lawsuit with the people's court within fifteen days from the expiration of the reconsideration period.

If the party fails to apply for reconsideration within the time limit, nor file a lawsuit with the people's court, nor comply with the penalty decision, the authority that made the penalty decision may apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 42 If an accounting firm violates the provisions of this Law and causes losses to the client or other interested parties, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 43 Registered auditors working in audit firms who are recognized as having the qualifications of certified public accountants may perform the business prescribed in this Law. The measures for the recognition of their qualifications and for their supervision, guidance, and management shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Article 44 Foreigners applying to take the national unified examination for certified public accountants of China and for registration shall be handled in accordance with the principle of reciprocity.

If a foreign accounting firm needs to temporarily handle relevant business within the territory of China, it must obtain approval from the relevant finance department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 45 The State Council may formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

Article 46 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1994. The "Regulations on Certified Public Accountants of the People's Republic of China" promulgated by the State Council on July 3, 1986 shall be simultaneously abolished.
