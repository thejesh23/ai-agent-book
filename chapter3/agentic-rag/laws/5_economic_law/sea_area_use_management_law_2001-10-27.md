# Sea Area Use Management Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 27, 2001

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to strengthen the management of sea area use, safeguard the state ownership of sea areas and the legitimate rights and interests of sea area use right holders, and promote the rational development and sustainable utilization of sea areas.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "sea areas" refer to the water surface, water body, seabed and subsoil of the internal waters and territorial sea of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "internal waters" refer to the sea areas on the landward side of the baseline of the territorial sea of the People's Republic of China up to the coastline.

This Law applies to exclusive sea area use activities that continuously use specific sea areas in the internal waters and territorial sea of the People's Republic of China for more than three months.

Article 3 Sea areas are owned by the state, and the State Council exercises the ownership of sea areas on behalf of the state. No unit or individual may encroach upon, buy, sell or illegally transfer sea areas in any other form.

Units and individuals using sea areas must obtain the right to use sea areas in accordance with the law.

Article 4 The state implements a marine functional zoning system. Sea area use must conform to marine functional zoning.

The state strictly manages sea area use activities that alter the natural attributes of sea areas, such as land reclamation and enclosure of the sea.

Article 5 The state establishes a sea area use management information system to monitor and survey the status of sea area use.

Article 6 The state establishes a registration system for sea area use rights. Sea area use rights registered in accordance with the law are protected by law.

The state establishes a statistical system for sea area use and regularly publishes statistical data on sea area use.

Article 7 The marine administrative department of the State Council is responsible for the supervision and management of sea area use nationwide. The marine administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level along the coast are responsible, within the scope of their authorization, for the supervision and management of sea area use in the adjacent sea areas under their respective administrative divisions.

The fishery administrative department shall, in accordance with the "Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China", exercise supervision and management over marine fisheries.

The maritime administration agency shall, in accordance with the "Maritime Traffic Safety Law of the People's Republic of China", exercise supervision and management over maritime traffic safety.

Article 8 All units and individuals have the obligation to abide by the laws and regulations on sea area use management and have the right to report and accuse any act violating these laws and regulations.

Article 9 Units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in the protection and rational utilization of sea areas and in conducting related scientific research shall be rewarded by the people's government.

## Chapter II Marine Functional Zoning

Article 10 The marine administrative department of the State Council shall, together with the relevant departments of the State Council and the people's governments of the coastal provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulate the national marine functional zoning.

The marine administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level along the coast shall, together with the relevant departments of the people's governments at the same level and based on the marine functional zoning at the next higher level, formulate local marine functional zoning.

Article 11 Marine functional zoning shall be formulated in accordance with the following principles:

(1) Scientifically determine the functions of sea areas based on their natural attributes such as location, natural resources, and natural environment;

(2) Make overall arrangements for sea area use by various relevant industries based on the needs of economic and social development;

(3) Protect and improve the ecological environment, ensure the sustainable utilization of sea areas, and promote the development of the marine economy;

(4) Ensure maritime traffic safety;

(5) Ensure national defense security and meet the needs of military sea area use.

Article 12 Marine functional zoning is subject to approval at different levels.

The national marine functional zoning shall be submitted to the State Council for approval.

The marine functional zoning of coastal provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, after being reviewed and approved by the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality, be submitted to the State Council for approval.

The marine functional zoning of coastal cities and counties shall, after being reviewed and approved by the people's government of the relevant city or county, be submitted to the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality for approval, and shall be reported to the marine administrative department of the State Council for record.

Article 13 Any modification to marine functional zoning shall be proposed by the original formulating authority together with the relevant departments at the same level and submitted to the original approving authority for approval; without approval, the functions of sea areas determined by the marine functional zoning shall not be changed.

If, with the approval of the State Council, it is necessary to change the marine functional zoning due to public interest, national defense security, or the construction of large-scale energy, transportation, or other infrastructure projects, the marine functional zoning shall be modified in accordance with the approval document of the State Council.

Article 14 Once approved, marine functional zoning shall be made public to society; however, the parts involving state secrets are excluded.

Article 15 Industry plans involving sea area use, such as those for aquaculture, salt industry, transportation, and tourism, shall conform to marine functional zoning.

Overall land use plans, urban plans, and port plans involving sea area use shall be coordinated with marine functional zoning.

## Chapter III Application and Approval for Sea Area Use

Article 16 Units and individuals may apply to the marine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level for the use of sea areas.

An applicant for sea area use shall submit the following written materials:

(1) An application form for sea area use;

(2) Demonstration materials for sea area use;

(3) Relevant creditworthiness certification materials;

(4) Other written materials as stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 17 The marine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall, based on marine functional zoning, review the application for sea area use and, in accordance with this Law and the provisions of the people's government of the relevant province, autonomous region, or municipality, submit it to the people's government with the authority to approve for approval.

When reviewing an application for sea area use, the marine administrative department shall seek the opinions of the relevant departments at the same level.

Article 18 The following projects involving sea area use shall be submitted to the State Council for approval:

(1) Projects involving land reclamation of fifty hectares or more;

(2) Projects involving enclosure of the sea of one hundred hectares or more;

(3) Projects involving sea area use of seven hundred hectares or more that do not alter the natural attributes of the sea area;

(4) Major national construction projects involving sea area use;

(5) Other projects involving sea area use as stipulated by the State Council.

The approval authority for projects involving sea area use other than those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be stipulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government as authorized by the State Council.

## Chapter IV Sea Area Use Rights

Article 19 After an application for sea area use is approved in accordance with the law, if the sea area use is approved by the State Council, the marine administrative department of the State Council shall register it in the register and issue a certificate of sea area use right to the applicant; if the sea area use is approved by a local people's government, the local people's government shall register it in the register and issue a certificate of sea area use right to the applicant. The applicant for sea area use shall obtain the sea area use right from the date of receiving the certificate of sea area use right.

Article 20 In addition to obtaining sea area use rights in the manner specified in Article 19 of this Law, they may also be obtained through bidding or auction. The bidding or auction plan shall be formulated by the marine administrative department and, after being approved by the people's government with the approval authority, shall be organized and implemented. When formulating the bidding or auction plan, the marine administrative department shall seek the opinions of the relevant departments at the same level.

After the bidding or auction work is completed, a certificate of sea area use right shall be issued to the winning bidder or buyer in accordance with the law. The winning bidder or buyer shall obtain the sea area use right from the date of receiving the certificate of sea area use right.

Article 21 The issuance of certificates of sea area use right shall be announced to the public.

No fees other than the sea area use fee collected in accordance with the law may be charged for the issuance of certificates of sea area use right.

The measures for the issuance and management of certificates of sea area use right shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Article 22 Before the implementation of this Law, if aquaculture sea areas operated and managed by rural collective economic organizations or villagers' committees conform to marine functional zoning, and with the verification and approval of the local people's government at the county level, the sea area use right may be determined for the said rural collective economic organization or villagers' committee, and may be contracted to members of the collective economic organization for aquaculture production.

Article 23 The right of a sea area use right holder to use the sea area in accordance with the law and to obtain benefits therefrom is protected by law, and no unit or individual may infringe upon it.

A sea area use right holder has the obligation to protect and rationally use the sea area in accordance with the law; a sea area use right holder shall not obstruct non-exclusive sea area use activities that do not hinder its lawful use of the sea area.

Article 24 During the period of using the sea area, a sea area use right holder shall not engage in basic marine surveying and mapping without approval in accordance with the law.

If a sea area use right holder discovers significant changes in the natural resources and natural conditions of the sea area being used, it shall promptly report to the marine administrative department.

Article 25 The maximum term of a sea area use right shall be determined according to the following purposes:

(1) Fifteen years for aquaculture sea area use;

(2) Twenty years for shipbreaking sea area use;

(3) Twenty-five years for tourism and entertainment sea area use;

(4) Thirty years for salt industry and mining sea area use;

(5) Forty years for public welfare undertakings sea area use;

(6) Fifty years for construction projects such as ports and shipyards.

Article 26 When the term of a sea area use right expires, if the sea area use right holder needs to continue using the sea area, it shall apply for an extension to the people's government that originally approved the sea area use no later than two months before the expiry of the term. Unless the sea area use right needs to be recovered due to public interest or national security, the people's government that originally approved the sea area use shall approve the extension. If the extension is granted, the sea area use right holder shall pay the sea area use fee for the extended period in accordance with the law.

Article 27 If the sea area use right holder changes due to enterprise merger, division, or joint venture or cooperative operation with others, the approval of the people's government that originally approved the sea area use is required.

Sea area use rights may be transferred in accordance with the law. The specific measures for the transfer of sea area use rights shall be stipulated by the State Council.

Sea area use rights may be inherited in accordance with the law.

Article 28 A sea area use right holder shall not change the approved purpose of sea area use without authorization; if a change is truly necessary, it shall, on the premise of conforming to marine functional zoning, be reported to the people's government that originally approved the sea area use for approval.

Article 29 When the term of a sea area use right expires, if no application for extension is made or the application for extension is not approved, the sea area use right shall terminate.

After the termination of a sea area use right, the original sea area use right holder shall dismantle the sea area use facilities and structures that may cause marine environmental pollution or affect other sea area use projects.

Article 30 If required by public interest or national security, the people's government that originally approved the sea area use may recover the sea area use right in accordance with the law.

If the sea area use right is recovered before the expiry of its term in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, corresponding compensation shall be given to the sea area use right holder.

Article 31 If a dispute arises over a sea area use right and the parties cannot resolve it through negotiation, the marine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall mediate; the parties may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Before the dispute over the sea area use right is resolved, neither party shall change the status quo of the sea area use.

Article 32 The land formed after the completion of a land reclamation project belongs to the state.

Within three months from the date of completion of the land reclamation project, the sea area use right holder shall, with the certificate of sea area use right, apply to the land administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level for land registration. The people's government at or above the county level shall register it in the register, issue a certificate of state-owned land use right, and confirm the land use right.

## Chapter V Sea Area Use Fees

Article 33 The state implements a paid-use system for sea areas.

Units and individuals using sea areas shall pay sea area use fees in accordance with the provisions of the State Council. Sea area use fees shall be turned over to the state treasury in accordance with the provisions of the State Council.

The specific implementation steps and measures for collecting sea area use fees from fishermen using sea areas for aquaculture activities shall be separately stipulated by the State Council.

Article 34 Depending on the nature or circumstances of the sea area use, the sea area use fee may be paid in a lump sum or annually on a yearly basis as stipulated.

Article 35 The following sea area uses are exempt from sea area use fees:

(1) Military sea area use;

(2) Sea area use for exclusive piers of official vessels;

(3) Sea area use for non-operational transportation infrastructure such as navigation channels and anchorages;

(4) Sea area use for non-operational public welfare undertakings such as teaching, scientific research, disaster prevention and mitigation, and maritime search and rescue.

Article 36 The following sea area uses may, in accordance with the provisions of the financial department of the State Council and the marine administrative department of the State Council, and upon examination and approval by the financial department and the marine administrative department of the people's government with the approval authority, have their sea area use fees reduced or exempted:

(1) Sea area use for public facilities;

(2) Sea area use for major national construction projects;

(3) Sea area use for aquaculture.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 37 The marine administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of sea area use.

The financial departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the payment of sea area use fees.

Article 38 The marine administrative departments shall strengthen team building and improve the political and professional quality of the personnel responsible for the supervision and inspection of sea area use management. The personnel responsible for the supervision and inspection of sea area use management must enforce the law impartially, be devoted to their duties, be honest and upright, provide civilized services, and accept supervision in accordance with the law.

The marine administrative departments and their staff shall not participate in or engage in production and business activities related to sea area use.

Article 39 When performing their supervision and inspection duties, the marine administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level have the right to take the following measures:

(1) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide relevant documents and materials concerning sea area use;

(2) Require the unit or individual under inspection to provide explanations on relevant issues concerning sea area use;

(3) Enter the sea area site occupied by the unit or individual under inspection for survey;

(4) Order the parties concerned to cease ongoing illegal acts.

Article 40 When performing their supervision and inspection duties, the personnel responsible for the supervision and inspection of sea area use management shall present valid law enforcement credentials.

Relevant units and individuals shall cooperate with the supervision and inspection conducted by the marine administrative departments and shall not refuse or obstruct the law enforcement personnel from performing their official duties in accordance with the law.

Article 41 The relevant departments exercising the power of marine supervision and management in accordance with the law shall closely cooperate and support each other when enforcing the law at sea, jointly safeguarding the state ownership of sea areas and the legitimate rights and interests of sea area use right holders.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 42 Anyone who illegally occupies a sea area without approval or by obtaining approval through deception shall be ordered to return the illegally occupied sea area, restore the sea area to its original state, confiscate the illegal gains, and be fined not less than five times but not more than fifteen times the sea area use fee payable for the area of the sea area during the period of illegal occupation; if the illegal occupation involves enclosure of the sea or land reclamation activities without approval or by obtaining approval through deception, a fine of not less than ten times but not more than twenty times the sea area use fee payable for the area of the sea area during the period of illegal occupation shall also be imposed.

Article 43 If a unit without the authority to approve sea area use illegally approves sea area use, exceeds its approval authority to illegally approve sea area use, or approves sea area use not in accordance with marine functional zoning, the approval document shall be invalid, and the illegally used sea area shall be recovered; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel for the illegal approval of sea area use shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 44 Anyone who, in violation of Article 23 of this Law, obstructs or hinders a sea area use right holder from lawfully using the sea area, the sea area use right holder may request the marine administrative department to remove the obstruction, or may file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law; if losses are caused, compensation for damages may be claimed in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Anyone who, in violation of Article 26 of this Law, continues to use the sea area after the expiry of the sea area use right term without going through the relevant procedures shall be ordered to go through the procedures within a time limit and may be fined not more than 10,000 yuan; if the order is refused, the act shall be treated as illegal occupation of the sea area.

Article 46 Anyone who, in violation of Article 28 of this Law, changes the purpose of sea area use without authorization shall be ordered to make corrections within a time limit, confiscate the illegal gains, and be fined not less than five times but not more than fifteen times the sea area use fee payable for the area of the sea area during the period of the unauthorized change of purpose; if the order to make corrections is refused, the people's government that issued the certificate of sea area use right shall cancel the certificate and recover the sea area use right.

Article 47 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 29 of this Law, fails to dismantle the sea area use facilities and structures as required after the termination of the sea area use right shall be ordered to dismantle them within a time limit; if the order is refused beyond the time limit, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed, and the marine administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall entrust a relevant unit to dismantle them on its behalf, with the costs borne by the original sea area use right holder.

Article 48 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to pay the sea area use fee on time when it is payable annually on a yearly basis shall be ordered to pay within a time limit; if the payment is still refused within the time limit, the people's government that issued the certificate of sea area use right shall cancel the certificate and recover the sea area use right.

Article 49 Anyone who, in violation of the provisions of this Law, refuses to accept the supervision and inspection of the marine administrative department, fails to truthfully report the situation, or fails to provide relevant materials shall be ordered to make corrections within a time limit, given a warning, and may be fined not more than 20,000 yuan.

Article 50 The administrative penalties stipulated in this Law shall be decided by the marine administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level within their authority, except where this Law has otherwise provided for the punishing authority.

Article 51 If the marine administrative department of the State Council or a local people's government at or above the county level issues a certificate of sea area use right in violation of the provisions of this Law, or fails to conduct supervision and management after issuing the certificate, or fails to investigate and deal with illegal acts discovered, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime due to engaging in malpractices for personal gain, abusing power, or neglecting duties, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 For exclusive sea area use activities in the internal waters and territorial sea of the People's Republic of China that last less than three months but may have a significant impact on national defense security, maritime traffic safety, and other sea area use activities, a temporary sea area use certificate shall be handled with reference to the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 53 The management measures for military sea area use shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 54 This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2002.
