# Price Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on December 29, 1997

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of regulating pricing activities, giving play to the role of prices in rationally allocating resources, stabilizing the overall price level, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers and business operators, and promoting the healthy development of the socialist market economy.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to pricing activities that take place within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purpose of this Law, prices include prices of commodities and prices of services.

Prices of commodities refer to prices of tangible products and intangible assets.

Prices of services refer to charges for various types of paid services.

Article 3 The State implements and gradually improves a mechanism under which prices are mainly determined by the market under macroeconomic control. The formulation of prices shall conform to the law of value. For most commodities and services, prices shall be determined by market regulation; for a very small number of commodities and services, prices shall be subject to government guidance or government pricing.

Market-regulated prices refer to prices independently determined by business operators through market competition.

For the purpose of this Law, business operators refer to legal persons, other organizations, and individuals engaged in the production or sale of commodities or the provision of paid services.

Government-guided prices refer to prices formulated by business operators under the guidance of the competent pricing departments of the government or other relevant departments, which, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, set base prices and their floating ranges within their pricing authority and scope.

Government-set prices refer to prices formulated by the competent pricing departments of the government or other relevant departments in accordance with the provisions of this Law, within their pricing authority and scope.

Article 4 The State supports and promotes fair, open, and lawful market competition, maintains normal pricing order, and exercises management, supervision, and necessary regulation over pricing activities.

Article 5 The competent pricing department under the State Council shall be responsible for pricing work nationwide. Other relevant departments under the State Council shall be responsible for pricing work within their respective functions and duties.

The competent pricing departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for pricing work within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for pricing work within their respective functions and duties.

## Chapter II Pricing Activities of Business Operators

Article 6 Prices of commodities and services shall, except for those subject to government guidance or government pricing under Article 18 of this Law, be determined by market regulation and independently formulated by business operators in accordance with this Law.

Article 7 Business operators shall adhere to the principles of fairness, legality, honesty, and trustworthiness when setting prices.

Article 8 The basic basis for business operators to set prices shall be the production and operation costs and the market supply and demand situation.

Article 9 Business operators shall strive to improve production and operation management, reduce production and operation costs, provide consumers with reasonably priced commodities and services, and obtain lawful profits in market competition.

Article 10 Business operators shall, based on their operating conditions, establish and improve internal price management systems, accurately record and verify the production and operation costs of commodities and services, and shall not engage in fraud.

Article 11 Business operators shall enjoy the following rights in pricing activities:

(1) To independently set prices that fall under market regulation;

(2) To set prices within the range specified by government-guided prices;

(3) To set trial sale prices for new products within the scope of products subject to government-guided or government-set prices, except for specific products;

(4) To report or accuse any act that infringes upon their right to independently set prices in accordance with the law.

Article 12 Business operators shall, in their pricing activities, comply with laws and regulations, implement government-guided prices and government-set prices formulated in accordance with the law, and statutory price intervention measures and emergency measures.

Article 13 When selling or purchasing commodities or providing services, business operators shall clearly mark prices in accordance with the provisions of the competent pricing departments of the government, indicating the name, place of origin, specifications, grade, unit of measurement, and price of the commodity, or the items and charging standards of the service, and other relevant information.

Business operators shall not sell commodities at prices higher than those marked, nor charge any fees not clearly indicated.

Article 14 Business operators shall not engage in the following unfair pricing practices:

(1) Colluding to manipulate market prices, harming the legitimate rights and interests of other business operators or consumers;

(2) Dumping commodities at prices below cost, except for the lawful reduction of prices for fresh goods, seasonal goods, overstocked goods, etc., in order to exclude competitors or monopolize the market, disrupting normal production and operation order, and harming the interests of the State or the legitimate rights and interests of other business operators;

(3) Fabricating or spreading price increase information, driving up prices, and causing excessive price increases of commodities;

(4) Using false or misleading pricing methods to induce consumers or other business operators to transact with them;

(5) Applying price discrimination to other business operators with the same transaction conditions when providing the same commodities or services;

(6) Raising or lowering grades to purchase or sell commodities or provide services, thereby covertly increasing or decreasing prices;

(7) Seeking exorbitant profits in violation of laws and regulations;

(8) Other unfair pricing practices prohibited by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 15 All types of intermediary institutions charging fees for providing paid services shall comply with the provisions of this Law. Where other laws provide otherwise, those provisions shall apply.

Article 16 Business operators selling imported commodities or purchasing export commodities shall comply with the relevant provisions of this Chapter to maintain domestic market order.

Article 17 Trade organizations shall comply with pricing laws and regulations, strengthen price self-discipline, and accept the work guidance of the competent pricing departments of the government.

## Chapter III Government Pricing Activities

Article 18 The government may, when necessary, implement government-guided prices or government-set prices for the following commodities and services:

(1) Prices of a very small number of commodities that are of significant importance to the development of the national economy and the people's livelihood;

(2) Prices of a small number of scarce resources;

(3) Prices of commodities subject to natural monopoly operations;

(4) Prices of important public utilities;

(5) Prices of important public welfare services.

Article 19 The pricing authority and specific scope of application for government-guided prices and government-set prices shall be based on the central and local pricing catalogs.

The central pricing catalog shall be formulated and revised by the competent pricing department under the State Council, and shall be published after approval by the State Council.

Local pricing catalogs shall be formulated by the competent pricing departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, based on the pricing authority and specific scope of application specified in the central pricing catalog, and shall be published after review and approval by the people's government at the same level and examination by the competent pricing department under the State Council.

Local people's governments below the level of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall not formulate pricing catalogs.

Article 20 The competent pricing department under the State Council and other relevant departments shall formulate government-guided prices and government-set prices in accordance with the pricing authority and specific scope of application specified in the central pricing catalog; among these, government-guided prices and government-set prices for important commodities and services shall be approved by the State Council in accordance with regulations.

The competent pricing departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and other relevant departments shall formulate government-guided prices and government-set prices to be implemented in their respective regions in accordance with the pricing authority and specific scope of application specified in the local pricing catalog.

People's governments of cities and counties may, upon authorization by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, formulate government-guided prices and government-set prices to be implemented in their respective regions in accordance with the pricing authority and specific scope of application specified in the local pricing catalog.

Article 21 When formulating government-guided prices and government-set prices, the social average cost of the relevant commodities or services, market supply and demand, the requirements of national economic and social development, and the social affordability shall be taken into account, and reasonable purchase-sale price differentials, wholesale-retail price differentials, regional price differentials, and seasonal price differentials shall be implemented.

Article 22 When formulating government-guided prices and government-set prices, the competent pricing departments of the government and other relevant departments shall conduct price and cost investigations and solicit opinions from consumers, business operators, and relevant parties.

When the competent pricing departments of the government conduct price and cost investigations for government-guided prices and government-set prices, the relevant units shall truthfully provide information and furnish the necessary account books, documents, and other materials.

Article 23 When formulating government-guided prices and government-set prices for public utility prices, public welfare service prices, and prices of commodities subject to natural monopoly operations that affect the vital interests of the people, a hearing system shall be established. The competent pricing departments of the government shall preside over the hearings to solicit opinions from consumers, business operators, and relevant parties, and to demonstrate the necessity and feasibility of such prices.

Article 24 After government-guided prices and government-set prices are formulated, the department that formulated them shall announce them to consumers and business operators.

Article 25 The specific scope of application and price levels of government-guided prices and government-set prices shall be adjusted in a timely manner based on economic operation conditions, in accordance with the prescribed pricing authority and procedures.

Consumers and business operators may propose adjustments to government-guided prices and government-set prices.

## Chapter IV Regulation of the Overall Price Level

Article 26 Stabilizing the overall market price level is an important macroeconomic policy objective of the State. The State shall, based on the needs of national economic development and social affordability, determine the regulatory target for the overall market price level, incorporate it into the national economic and social development plan, and comprehensively apply monetary, fiscal, investment, import and export policies and measures to achieve it.

Article 27 The government may establish a reserve system for important commodities and set up price adjustment funds to regulate prices and stabilize the market.

Article 28 To meet the needs of price regulation and management, the competent pricing departments of the government shall establish a price monitoring system to monitor changes in the prices of important commodities and services.

Article 29 When the market purchase prices of important agricultural products such as grain are too low, the government may implement protective prices in procurement and adopt corresponding economic measures to ensure their realization.

Article 30 When prices of important commodities and services rise significantly or are likely to rise significantly, the State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may adopt intervention measures such as limiting the price differential rate or profit rate, setting price caps, and implementing a price increase reporting system and a price adjustment filing system for some prices.

When the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government adopt the intervention measures specified in the preceding paragraph, they shall report to the State Council for the record.

Article 31 When the overall market price level experiences abnormal conditions such as drastic fluctuations, the State Council may adopt emergency measures such as temporarily centralizing pricing authority or partially or fully freezing prices nationwide or in certain regions.

Article 32 After the circumstances that necessitated the adoption of intervention measures or emergency measures under Articles 30 and 31 of this Law have been eliminated, such intervention measures or emergency measures shall be promptly lifted.

## Chapter V Supervision and Inspection of Prices

Article 33 The competent pricing departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect pricing activities and impose administrative penalties for illegal pricing acts in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 34 When conducting price supervision and inspection, the competent pricing departments of the government may exercise the following powers:

(1) To question the parties concerned or relevant personnel and require them to provide evidentiary materials and other information related to the illegal pricing acts;

(2) To examine and copy account books, receipts, vouchers, documents, and other materials related to the illegal pricing acts, and to verify bank information related to the illegal pricing acts;

(3) To inspect property and goods related to the illegal pricing acts, and when necessary, order the parties concerned to suspend relevant business operations;

(4) When evidence may be destroyed or difficult to obtain later, to register and preserve it in advance in accordance with the law, and the parties concerned or relevant personnel shall not transfer, conceal, or destroy it.

Article 35 When business operators accept supervision and inspection by the competent pricing departments of the government, they shall truthfully provide the account books, receipts, vouchers, documents, and other materials necessary for the price supervision and inspection.

Article 36 Pricing personnel of government departments shall not use the materials or information obtained in accordance with the law for any purpose other than price management in accordance with the law, and shall not disclose the business secrets of the parties concerned.

Article 37 Consumer organizations, employee price supervision organizations, residents' committees, villagers' committees, and other organizations, as well as consumers, have the right to exercise social supervision over pricing activities. The competent pricing departments of the government shall give full play to the role of the masses in price supervision.

News units have the right to exercise public opinion supervision over prices.

Article 38 The competent pricing departments of the government shall establish a reporting system for illegal pricing acts.

Any unit or individual has the right to report illegal pricing acts. The competent pricing departments of the government shall encourage reporters and be responsible for keeping their identities confidential.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 39 If a business operator fails to implement government-guided prices, government-set prices, or statutory price intervention measures or emergency measures, it shall be ordered to make corrections, its illegal gains shall be confiscated, and it may be concurrently fined not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine may be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification.

Article 40 If a business operator commits any of the acts listed in Article 14 of this Law, it shall be ordered to make corrections, its illegal gains shall be confiscated, and it may be concurrently fined not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a warning shall be given, and a fine may also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification, or its business license shall be revoked by the administrative authority for industry and commerce. If other laws provide otherwise for the punishment of the acts listed in Article 14 and the punishing authorities, those laws may be followed.

If the acts listed in Items (1) and (2) of Article 14 of this Law are nationwide in nature, they shall be determined by the competent pricing department under the State Council; if they are regional in nature at the provincial level or below, they shall be determined by the competent pricing departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 41 If a business operator causes consumers or other business operators to overpay due to illegal pricing acts, it shall refund the overpaid amount; if damage is caused, it shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 42 If a business operator violates the provisions on clearly marking prices, it shall be ordered to make corrections, its illegal gains shall be confiscated, and it may be concurrently fined not more than 5,000 yuan.

Article 43 If a business operator fails to suspend relevant business operations when ordered to do so, or transfers, conceals, or destroys property and goods that have been registered and preserved in accordance with the law, it shall be fined not less than one time but not more than three times the income from the relevant business operations or the value of the property and goods transferred, concealed, or destroyed.

Article 44 If a party refuses to provide the materials required for supervision and inspection in accordance with regulations or provides false materials, it shall be ordered to make corrections and given a warning; if it fails to make corrections within the time limit, a fine may be imposed.

Article 45 If a local people's government at any level or a relevant department of a people's government at any level violates the provisions of this Law by exceeding its pricing authority and scope to formulate or adjust prices without authorization, or fails to implement statutory price intervention measures or emergency measures, it shall be ordered to make corrections and may be criticized in a circular; the persons directly in charge and other persons directly responsible shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 46 If pricing personnel divulge State secrets or business secrets, abuse their power, engage in malpractice for personal gain, neglect their duties, or solicit or accept bribes, and if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 47 Charges by State administrative organs shall be conducted in accordance with the law, with strict control over the items of charges and limitation of the scope and standards of charges. Specific measures for the management of charges shall be separately formulated by the State Council.

Interest rates, exchange rates, insurance premium rates, and securities and futures prices shall be governed by the provisions of relevant laws and administrative regulations, and this Law shall not apply to them.

Article 48 This Law shall come into force as of May 1, 1998.
