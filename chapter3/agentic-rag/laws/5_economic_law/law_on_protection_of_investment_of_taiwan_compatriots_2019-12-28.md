# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Investments by Taiwan Compatriots

Adopted at the Sixth Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on March 5, 1994

First amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Four Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprises at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on September 3, 2016

Second amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Investments by Taiwan Compatriots at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 28, 2019

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect and encourage investments by Taiwan compatriots and to promote economic development across the Taiwan Strait.

Article 2 Investments by Taiwan compatriots shall be governed by this Law. Where matters are not covered by this Law, and other relevant laws or administrative regulations of the State contain provisions on investments by Taiwan compatriots, those provisions shall apply.

For the purposes of this Law, "investments by Taiwan compatriots" refers to investments made by companies, enterprises, other economic organizations, or individuals from the Taiwan region as investors in other provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government.

Article 3 The State shall, in accordance with the law, protect the investments, investment returns, and other lawful rights and interests of Taiwan compatriot investors.

Taiwan compatriot investors must comply with the laws and regulations of the State.

Article 4 The State shall not nationalize or expropriate the investments of Taiwan compatriot investors. Under special circumstances, where required by the public interest, investments by Taiwan compatriot investors may be expropriated in accordance with legal procedures, and appropriate compensation shall be provided.

Article 5 The property, industrial property rights, investment returns, and other lawful rights and interests of Taiwan compatriot investors may be transferred or inherited in accordance with the law.

Article 6 Taiwan compatriot investors may invest using freely convertible currencies, machinery and equipment or other tangible assets, industrial property rights, non-patented technology, and the like.

Taiwan compatriot investors may reinvest the returns obtained from their investments.

Article 7 Investments by Taiwan compatriots may take the form of establishing enterprises wholly or partially invested by Taiwan compatriot investors (hereinafter referred to as "Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises"), or may adopt other forms of investment prescribed by laws, administrative regulations, or the State Council.

The establishment of Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises shall conform to the industrial policies of the State and be conducive to the development of the national economy.

Article 8 Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises shall conduct their business management activities in accordance with the law, and their autonomy in business management shall not be interfered with.

Article 9 In areas where Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises are concentrated, associations of Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises may be established in accordance with the law, and their lawful rights and interests shall be protected by law.

Article 10 The investment returns, other lawful income, and funds after liquidation lawfully obtained by Taiwan compatriot investors may be remitted back to Taiwan or abroad in accordance with the law.

Article 11 Taiwan compatriot investors may entrust relatives or friends as agents for their investments.

Article 12 Taiwan compatriot investment enterprises shall enjoy preferential treatment in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council on encouraging investments by Taiwan compatriots.

Article 13 Disputes related to investments arising between Taiwan compatriot investors and companies, enterprises, other economic organizations, or individuals from other provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government may be resolved by the parties through consultation or mediation.

If the parties are unwilling to engage in consultation or mediation, or if consultation or mediation fails, the dispute may be submitted to an arbitration institution for arbitration in accordance with the arbitration clause in the contract or a written arbitration agreement reached afterwards.

If the parties have not included an arbitration clause in the contract and have not reached a written arbitration agreement afterwards, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

Article 14 This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
