# Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on November 8, 1988

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 11th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2004

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revision adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of protecting wildlife, saving rare and endangered wildlife, maintaining biodiversity and ecological balance, and promoting the construction of an ecological civilization.

Article 2 This Law applies to the protection of wildlife and related activities conducted within the territory of the People's Republic of China and other sea areas under its jurisdiction.

The wildlife protected under this Law refers to rare and endangered terrestrial and aquatic wildlife, and terrestrial wildlife of significant ecological, scientific, or social value.

The wildlife and their products as referred to in this Law mean the whole (including eggs and spawn), parts, and derivatives of wildlife.

The protection of aquatic wildlife other than rare and endangered aquatic wildlife shall be governed by the Fisheries Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws.

Article 3 Wildlife resources are owned by the State.

The State protects the lawful rights and interests of organizations and individuals that engage in wildlife scientific research, artificial breeding, and other protection and related activities in accordance with the law.

Article 4 The State applies the principles of prioritizing protection, regulating utilization, and strictly supervising wildlife, encourages scientific research on wildlife, fosters citizens' awareness of wildlife protection, and promotes the harmonious development of humans and nature.

Article 5 The State protects wildlife and their habitats. People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate relevant protection plans and measures for wildlife and their habitats, and incorporate wildlife protection funds into their budgets.

The State encourages citizens, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in wildlife protection activities through donations, funding, volunteer services, and other means in accordance with the law, and supports public welfare undertakings for wildlife protection.

The wildlife habitats as referred to in this Law mean important areas where wild populations of wildlife inhabit and reproduce.

Article 6 All organizations and individuals have the duty to protect wildlife and their habitats. It is prohibited to illegally hunt wildlife or destroy wildlife habitats.

All organizations and individuals have the right to report or accuse any violation of this Law to the relevant departments and authorities. The competent departments for wildlife protection and other relevant departments and authorities shall handle such reports or accusations promptly in accordance with the law.

Article 7 The competent departments for forestry, grassland, and fisheries under the State Council shall respectively be in charge of the protection of terrestrial and aquatic wildlife nationwide.

The competent departments for forestry, grassland, and fisheries under local people's governments at or above the county level shall respectively be in charge of the protection of terrestrial and aquatic wildlife within their respective administrative regions.

Article 8 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the publicity, education, and popularization of scientific knowledge on wildlife protection, and encourage and support grassroots self-governing organizations, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and volunteers to carry out publicity activities on wildlife protection laws, regulations, and knowledge.

Education administrative departments and schools shall educate students on wildlife protection knowledge.

News media shall conduct publicity on wildlife protection laws, regulations, and knowledge, and exercise public opinion supervision over illegal acts.

Article 9 Organizations and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in wildlife protection and scientific research shall be rewarded by people's governments at or above the county level.

## Chapter II Protection of Wildlife and Their Habitats

Article 10 The State implements classified and graded protection of wildlife.

The State provides key protection to rare and endangered wildlife. Wildlife under State key protection is divided into Class I protected wildlife and Class II protected wildlife. The list of wildlife under State key protection shall be formulated by the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council after organizing scientific assessments, and shall be adjusted every five years based on the assessment results. The list of wildlife under State key protection shall be submitted to the State Council for approval and publication.

Local key protected wildlife refers to wildlife that is under key protection by provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, other than wildlife under State key protection. The list of local key protected wildlife shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government after organizing scientific assessments.

The list of terrestrial wildlife of significant ecological, scientific, or social value shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council after organizing scientific assessments.

Article 11 The competent departments for wildlife protection under people's governments at or above the county level shall regularly organize or entrust relevant scientific research institutions to conduct surveys, monitoring, and assessments of the status of wildlife and their habitats, and establish and improve archives for wildlife and their habitats.

Surveys, monitoring, and assessments of the status of wildlife and their habitats shall include the following:

(1) The distribution areas, population numbers, and structures of wild wildlife;

(2) The area and ecological status of wildlife habitats;

(3) The main threat factors to wildlife and their habitats;

(4) The status of artificial breeding of wildlife and other content requiring surveys, monitoring, and assessments.

Article 12 The competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the relevant departments under the State Council, determine and publish a list of important wildlife habitats based on the results of surveys, monitoring, and assessments of the status of wildlife and their habitats.

People's governments at or above the provincial level shall, in accordance with the law, designate relevant natural protection areas to protect wildlife and their important habitats, and to protect, restore, and improve the living environment of wildlife. Where conditions for designating relevant natural protection areas are not met, people's governments at or above the county level may adopt other forms of protection, such as designating hunting (fishing) prohibited areas or specifying hunting (fishing) prohibited periods.

It is prohibited or restricted to introduce alien species, create single-species forests, excessively apply pesticides, or engage in other human disturbances that threaten the inhabitation and reproduction of wildlife within relevant natural protection areas.

Relevant natural protection areas shall be designated and managed in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations.

Article 13 When formulating relevant development and utilization plans, people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall fully consider the needs for protecting wildlife and their habitats, analyze, predict, and assess the overall impact that the implementation of the plans may have on the protection of wildlife and their habitats, and avoid or reduce any adverse consequences that may result from the implementation of the plans.

It is prohibited to construct projects that are not allowed by laws and regulations within relevant natural protection areas. The site selection and route planning of construction projects such as airports, railways, highways, water conservancy and hydropower projects, cofferdams, and sea reclamation shall avoid relevant natural protection areas and wildlife migration and passage routes; where avoidance is not possible, measures such as building wildlife passages and fish passage facilities shall be taken to eliminate or reduce adverse impacts on wildlife.

Where a construction project may affect relevant natural protection areas or wildlife migration and passage routes, the department approving the environmental impact assessment document shall, when examining and approving the environmental impact assessment document, seek the opinion of the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council if the project involves wildlife under State key protection; and seek the opinion of the competent department for wildlife protection under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government if the project involves local key protected wildlife.

Article 14 Competent departments for wildlife protection at all levels shall monitor and survey the impact of the environment on wildlife. Where environmental impacts cause harm to wildlife, the competent departments for wildlife protection shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, investigate and handle the matter.

Article 15 Where wildlife under State or local key protection is threatened by emergencies such as natural disasters or major environmental pollution accidents, the local people's government shall promptly take emergency rescue measures.

The competent departments for wildlife protection under people's governments at or above the county level shall organize wildlife shelter and rescue work in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

It is prohibited to buy or sell wildlife or their products under the guise of wildlife shelter and rescue.

Article 16 The competent departments for wildlife protection and veterinary authorities under people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, monitor wildlife epidemic sources and diseases, organize forecasting and reporting work, and formulate wildlife epidemic emergency plans in accordance with the provisions, which shall be submitted to the people's government at the same level for approval or filing.

The competent departments for wildlife protection, veterinary authorities, and health authorities under people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for the prevention, control, and management of animal infectious diseases related to zoonotic infectious diseases.

Article 17 The State strengthens the protection of wildlife genetic resources and implements rescue protection for endangered wildlife.

The competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council shall, in conjunction with the relevant departments under the State Council, formulate plans for the protection and utilization of wildlife genetic resources, establish a national wildlife genetic resource gene bank, and provide key protection for the genetic resources of rare and endangered wildlife native to China.

Article 18 Relevant local people's governments shall take measures to prevent and control potential harm caused by wildlife, and ensure the safety of humans and livestock as well as agricultural and forestry production.

Article 19 Where personal injury, death, or damage to crops or other property is caused by the protection of wildlife protected under this Law, the local people's government shall provide compensation. Specific measures shall be formulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government. Relevant local people's governments may promote insurance institutions to develop wildlife damage compensation insurance.

The expenses required for relevant local people's governments to take measures to prevent and control harm caused by wildlife under State key protection and to provide compensation shall be subsidized by the central finance in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

## Chapter III Wildlife Management

Article 20 Within relevant natural protection areas and hunting (fishing) prohibited areas or during hunting (fishing) prohibited periods, hunting and other activities that hinder the inhabitation and reproduction of wildlife are prohibited, except as otherwise provided by laws and regulations.

During wildlife migration periods, within migration and passage routes outside the areas specified in the preceding paragraph, hunting is prohibited and other activities that hinder the inhabitation and reproduction of wildlife are strictly restricted. The scope of migration and passage routes and the content of activities that hinder the inhabitation and reproduction of wildlife shall be specified and published by the people's governments at or above the county level or their competent departments for wildlife protection.

Article 21 It is prohibited to hunt or kill wildlife under State key protection.

Where it is necessary to hunt Class I protected wildlife for scientific research, population regulation, epidemic source and disease monitoring, or other special circumstances, an application for a special hunting permit shall be submitted to the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council; where it is necessary to hunt Class II protected wildlife, an application for a special hunting permit shall be submitted to the competent department for wildlife protection under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 22 To hunt wildlife not under State key protection, a hunting license issued by the competent department for wildlife protection under the local people's government at or above the county level shall be obtained in accordance with the law, and the hunting quota management shall be complied with.

Article 23 Hunters shall hunt in accordance with the species, quantity, location, tools, methods, and time limits specified in the special hunting permit or hunting license.

Those who hunt with firearms shall obtain a firearm license issued by the public security authority in accordance with the law.

Article 24 It is prohibited to use poisons, explosives, electric shocks or electronic trapping devices, as well as snares, traps, ground guns, row guns, and other tools for hunting; it is prohibited to use methods such as night lighting hunting, extermination encirclement hunting, destroying nests, fire attacks, smoking, and netting for hunting, except where netting or electronic trapping is truly necessary for scientific research.

Hunting tools and methods prohibited in addition to those specified in the preceding paragraph shall be specified and published by local people's governments at or above the county level.

Article 25 The State supports relevant scientific research institutions in artificially breeding wildlife under State key protection for species conservation purposes.

A licensing system is implemented for the artificial breeding of wildlife under State key protection other than that specified in the preceding paragraph. To artificially breed wildlife under State key protection, approval shall be obtained from the competent department for wildlife protection under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and an artificial breeding license shall be obtained, except where the State Council has other provisions on the approving authority.

Artificial breeding of wildlife under State key protection shall use offspring from artificial breeding as source stock, and establish species pedigrees, breeding archives, and individual data. Where it is truly necessary to use wild source stock for species conservation purposes, the provisions of Articles 21 and 23 of this Law shall apply.

The term "offspring from artificial breeding" as used in this Law refers to offspring individuals born under artificially controlled conditions, whose parents were also born under artificially controlled conditions.

Article 26 Artificial breeding of wildlife under State key protection shall be conducive to species conservation and scientific research, shall not destroy wild population resources, and shall, based on the habits of the wildlife, ensure they have the necessary living space and conditions for inhabitation, reproduction, health, and hygiene, and be equipped with facilities, equipment, and technology appropriate to the purpose, species, and development scale of the breeding, comply with relevant technical standards and epidemic prevention requirements, and shall not abuse wildlife.

The competent departments for wildlife protection under people's governments at or above the provincial level may, based on the needs of protecting wildlife under State key protection, organize the release of wildlife under State key protection into the wild.

Article 27 It is prohibited to sell, purchase, or utilize wildlife under State key protection or their products.

Where it is necessary to sell, purchase, or utilize wildlife under State key protection or their products for scientific research, artificial breeding, public display and exhibition, cultural relic protection, or other special circumstances, approval shall be obtained from the competent department for wildlife protection under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, and special identification marks shall be obtained and used in accordance with the provisions to ensure traceability, except where the State Council has other provisions on the approving authority.

The scope and management measures for special identification marks for wildlife under State key protection and their products shall be specified by the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council.

To sell or utilize wildlife not under State key protection, a certificate of lawful source such as a hunting or import/export certificate shall be provided.

To sell the wildlife specified in the second and fourth paragraphs of this Article, a quarantine certificate shall also be attached in accordance with the law.

Article 28 For wildlife under State key protection with mature and stable artificial breeding techniques, after scientific demonstration, they shall be included in the list of artificially bred wildlife under State key protection formulated by the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council. For wildlife and their products included in the list, special identification marks may be directly obtained based on the annual production quantity verified by the competent department for wildlife protection under the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, using the artificial breeding license, and such wildlife and products may be sold and utilized with the special identification marks to ensure traceability.

When adjusting the list of wildlife under State key protection as specified in Article 10 of this Law, based on the protection status of relevant wild populations, the artificially bred populations of wildlife with mature and stable artificial breeding techniques as specified in the preceding paragraph may no longer be included in the list of wildlife under State key protection, and different management measures from those for wild populations may be applied, provided that an artificial breeding license and special identification marks are obtained in accordance with the provisions of Article 25, paragraph 2, and Article 28, paragraph 1 of this Law.

Article 29 The utilization of wildlife and their products shall primarily rely on artificially bred populations, be conducive to the conservation of wild populations, comply with the requirements of ecological civilization construction, respect social ethics, and abide by laws, regulations, and relevant provisions of the State.

Where wildlife and their products are operated and utilized as pharmaceuticals, the laws and regulations on pharmaceutical management shall also be complied with.

Article 30 It is prohibited to produce or operate food made from wildlife under State key protection or their products, or food made from wildlife not under State key protection or their products without a certificate of lawful source.

It is prohibited to illegally purchase wildlife under State key protection or their products for consumption.

Article 31 It is prohibited to publish advertisements for the sale, purchase, or utilization of wildlife or prohibited hunting tools. It is prohibited to publish advertisements for the illegal sale, purchase, or utilization of wildlife products.

Article 32 It is prohibited for online trading platforms, commodity trading markets, and other trading venues to provide trading services for the illegal sale, purchase, or utilization of wildlife or their products, or for prohibited hunting tools.

Article 33 To transport, carry, or send by post wildlife under State key protection or their products, or wildlife or their products as specified in Article 28, paragraph 2 of this Law, out of a county, a copy of the license, approval document, or special identification mark as specified in Articles 21, 25, 27, or 28 of this Law, as well as a quarantine certificate, shall be held or attached.

To transport wildlife not under State key protection out of a county, a certificate of lawful source such as a hunting or import/export certificate, as well as a quarantine certificate, shall be held.

Article 34 The competent departments for wildlife protection under people's governments at or above the county level shall supervise and manage activities utilizing wildlife and their products, such as scientific research, artificial breeding, and public display and exhibition.

Other relevant departments under people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, conduct supervision and inspection over activities such as the sale, purchase, utilization, transportation, and sending by post of wildlife and their products.

Article 35 The list of wildlife or their products prohibited or restricted from trade by international conventions concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the national authority for the management of import and export of endangered species.

To import or export wildlife or their products included in the list specified in the preceding paragraph, or to export wildlife under State key protection or their products, approval shall be obtained from the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council or the State Council, and an import/export permit certificate issued by the national authority for the management of import and export of endangered species shall be obtained. Customs shall implement entry and exit quarantine in accordance with the law, and handle customs clearance procedures in accordance with the provisions based on the import/export permit certificate and quarantine certificate.

The export of wildlife species involving scientific and technological secrets shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council.

Wildlife included in the list specified in paragraph 1 of this Article may, upon approval by the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council, be managed as wildlife under State key protection within the scope of application of this Law.

Article 36 The State organizes international cooperation and exchanges in wildlife protection and related law enforcement activities; establishes a departmental coordination mechanism for preventing and combating the smuggling and illegal trade of wildlife and their products; and carries out actions to prevent and combat smuggling and illegal trade.

Article 37 To introduce wildlife species from abroad, approval shall be obtained from the competent department for wildlife protection under the State Council. To introduce wildlife included in the list specified in Article 35, paragraph 1 of this Law from abroad, an import/export permit certificate shall also be obtained in accordance with the law. Customs shall implement entry quarantine in accordance with the law, and handle customs clearance procedures in accordance with the provisions based on the import approval document or import/export permit certificate and quarantine certificate.When introducing wildlife species from abroad, safe and reliable preventive measures shall be taken to prevent them from entering the wild environment and to avoid causing harm to the ecosystem. If it is truly necessary to release them into the wild, the matter shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 38 Any organization or individual releasing wildlife into the wild environment shall select local species suitable for survival in the release area, shall not disrupt the normal life and production of local residents, and shall avoid causing harm to the ecosystem. Those who release wildlife arbitrarily, causing personal injury, property damage, or harm to the ecosystem, shall bear legal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 39 It is prohibited to forge, alter, buy, sell, transfer, or lend special hunting permits, hunting licenses, artificial breeding permits and special identifiers, approval documents for the sale, purchase, or use of nationally key protected wildlife and their products, or permits for import and export, import and export approval documents, etc.

The issuance of relevant licenses, special identifiers, and approval documents as specified in the preceding paragraph shall be made public in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Foreigners conducting field research on nationally key protected wildlife or filming movies or videos in the wild in China shall obtain approval from the wildlife protection authority of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government, or from an authorized entity, and shall comply with relevant laws and regulations.
Article 41 The management measures for locally key protected wildlife and other non-nationally key protected wildlife shall be formulated by the people's congress or its standing committee of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

## Chapter 4 Legal Liability

Article 42 If the wildlife protection competent department or other relevant departments or agencies fail to make administrative licensing decisions in accordance with the law, fail to investigate or handle illegal acts upon discovery or upon receiving reports of illegal acts, or abuse their power or otherwise fail to perform their duties in accordance with the law, the people's government at the same level or the relevant department or agency of the people's government at a higher level shall order corrections, and impose demerit, major demerit, or demotion sanctions on the responsible persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel; if serious consequences are caused, removal from office or expulsion shall be imposed, and the principal responsible person shall resign; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 43 Violations of the provisions of Article 12, Paragraph 3, or Article 13, Paragraph 2 of this Law shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 44 Violations of the provisions of Article 15, Paragraph 3 of this Law, where wildlife and their products are bought and sold under the guise of shelter and rescue, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the wildlife and their products and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the value of the wildlife and their products; the relevant illegal information shall be recorded in the social credit file and made public; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 45 Violations of the provisions of Article 20, Article 21, Article 23, Paragraph 1, or Article 24, Paragraph 1 of this Law, where nationally key protected wildlife are hunted in relevant nature reserves, no-hunting (fishing) areas, or during no-hunting (fishing) periods, without obtaining a special hunting permit, or hunting or killing nationally key protected wildlife not in accordance with the special hunting permit, or using prohibited tools or methods to hunt nationally key protected wildlife, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level, the marine law enforcement department, or the relevant protected area management agency shall, according to their respective responsibilities, confiscate the hunted items, hunting tools, and illegal gains, revoke the special hunting permit, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the value of the hunted items; if there are no hunted items, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 46 Violations of the provisions of Article 20, Article 22, Article 23, Paragraph 1, or Article 24, Paragraph 1 of this Law, where non-nationally key protected wildlife are hunted in relevant nature reserves, no-hunting (fishing) areas, or during no-hunting (fishing) periods, without obtaining a hunting license, or hunting non-nationally key protected wildlife not in accordance with the hunting license, or using prohibited tools or methods to hunt non-nationally key protected wildlife, the wildlife protection competent department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the relevant protected area management agency shall, according to their respective responsibilities, confiscate the hunted items, hunting tools, and illegal gains, revoke the hunting license, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the value of the hunted items; if there are no hunted items, a fine of not less than 2,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Violations of the provisions of Article 23, Paragraph 2 of this Law, where wildlife is hunted with a firearm without obtaining a firearm license, constituting a violation of public security administration, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 47 Violations of the provisions of Article 25, Paragraph 2 of this Law, where nationally key protected wildlife or wildlife specified in Article 28, Paragraph 2 of this Law are bred without obtaining an artificial breeding permit, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the wildlife and their products, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the value of the wildlife and their products.

Article 48 Violations of the provisions of Article 27, Paragraphs 1 and 2, Article 28, Paragraph 1, or Article 33, Paragraph 1 of this Law, where without approval, without obtaining or using special identifiers in accordance with regulations, or without holding or attaching copies of artificial breeding permits, approval documents, or special identifiers, nationally key protected wildlife and their products or wildlife and their products specified in Article 28, Paragraph 2 of this Law are sold, purchased, used, transported, carried, or sent by post, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level or the market supervision and management department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, confiscate the wildlife and their products and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the value of the wildlife and their products; if the circumstances are serious, the artificial breeding permit shall be revoked, the approval documents shall be withdrawn, and the special identifiers shall be recovered; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Violations of the provisions of Article 27, Paragraph 4, or Article 33, Paragraph 2 of this Law, where non-nationally key protected wildlife are sold, used, or transported without holding a legal source certificate, the wildlife protection competent department of the local people's government at or above the county level or the market supervision and management department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, confiscate the wildlife, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the value of the wildlife.

Violations of the provisions of Article 27, Paragraph 5, or Article 33 of this Law, where relevant wildlife and their products are sold, transported, carried, or sent by post without holding or attaching a quarantine certificate, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Animal Epidemic Prevention Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 49 Violations of the provisions of Article 30 of this Law, where food is produced or operated using nationally key protected wildlife and their products or non-nationally key protected wildlife and their products without a legal source certificate, or where nationally key protected wildlife and their products are illegally purchased for consumption, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level or the market supervision and management department shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate the wildlife and their products and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the value of the wildlife and their products; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 50 Violations of the provisions of Article 31 of this Law, where advertisements are published for the sale, purchase, or use of wildlife and their products or prohibited hunting tools, shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the Advertising Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 51 Violations of the provisions of Article 32 of this Law, where transaction services are provided for the illegal sale, purchase, or use of wildlife and their products or prohibited hunting tools, the market supervision and management department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the cessation of the illegal act, set a deadline for correction, confiscate illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 52 Violations of the provisions of Article 35 of this Law, where wildlife or their products are imported or exported, the customs, public security organs, or marine law enforcement departments shall impose penalties in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and relevant state provisions; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Violations of the provisions of Article 37, Paragraph 1 of this Law, where wildlife species are introduced from abroad, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the introduced wildlife, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 250,000 yuan; if entry quarantine is not implemented in accordance with the law, punishment shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Entry and Exit Animal and Plant Quarantine; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 54 Violations of the provisions of Article 37, Paragraph 2 of this Law, where wildlife introduced from abroad are released into the wild environment, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order recapture within a time limit, and impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if recapture is not completed within the time limit, the relevant wildlife protection competent department shall carry out recapture or take measures to reduce the impact, and the costs shall be borne by the party ordered to recapture.

Article 55 Violations of the provisions of Article 39, Paragraph 1 of this Law, where relevant documents, special identifiers, or relevant approval documents are forged, altered, bought, sold, transferred, or lent, the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level shall confiscate the illegal documents, special identifiers, relevant approval documents, and illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 250,000 yuan; if a violation of public security administration is constituted, the public security organ shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 56 The physical items confiscated in accordance with this Law shall be handled by the wildlife protection competent department of the people's government at or above the county level or its authorized entity in accordance with regulations.

Article 57 The evaluation standards and methods for the value of hunted items and wildlife and their products as specified in this Law shall be formulated by the wildlife protection competent department of the State Council.

## Chapter 5 Supplementary Provisions

Article 58 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2017.
