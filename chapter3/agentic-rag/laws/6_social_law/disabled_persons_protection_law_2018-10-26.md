# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Persons with Disabilities

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on December 28, 1990

Revised at the 2nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on April 24, 2008

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Wildlife Protection Law of the People's Republic of China at the 6th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution to safeguard the lawful rights and interests of persons with disabilities, develop the cause of persons with disabilities, and ensure their equal and full participation in social life and their share of the material and cultural achievements of society.

Article 2 A person with a disability refers to a person who has lost or is abnormal in the structure or function of a certain organ, psychologically, physiologically, or in human body structure, and who has lost, wholly or in part, the ability to engage in certain activities in a normal manner.

Persons with disabilities include those with visual disabilities, hearing disabilities, speech disabilities, physical disabilities, intellectual disabilities, mental disabilities, multiple disabilities, and other disabilities.

The criteria for disability shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 3 Persons with disabilities shall enjoy equal rights with other citizens in political, economic, cultural, social, and family life.

The civil rights and personal dignity of persons with disabilities shall be protected by law.

Discrimination on the basis of disability is prohibited. Insulting or infringing upon persons with disabilities is prohibited. Disparaging or harming the dignity of persons with disabilities through mass media or other means is prohibited.

Article 4 The State shall adopt supplementary measures and supportive policies to provide special assistance to persons with disabilities, to reduce or eliminate the effects of disabilities and external barriers, and to ensure the realization of the rights of persons with disabilities.

Article 5 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the cause of persons with disabilities into national economic and social development plans, strengthen leadership, coordinate comprehensively, include the funds for the cause of persons with disabilities in the fiscal budget, and establish a stable funding guarantee mechanism.

The State Council shall formulate the Outline of the Development of the Cause of Persons with Disabilities in China. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the Outline, formulate development plans and annual plans for the cause of persons with disabilities within their respective administrative regions, so as to ensure the coordinated development of the cause of persons with disabilities with the economy and society.

The institutions responsible for the work concerning persons with disabilities under the people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, guiding, and supervising the relevant departments in carrying out the work concerning persons with disabilities.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall maintain close ties with persons with disabilities, listen to their opinions, and perform their duties well in the work concerning persons with disabilities.

Article 6 The State shall take measures to ensure that persons with disabilities, in accordance with the law and through various channels and forms, manage state affairs, economic and cultural undertakings, and social affairs.

When formulating laws, regulations, rules, and public policies on major issues involving the rights and interests of persons with disabilities and the cause of persons with disabilities, the opinions of persons with disabilities and organizations of persons with disabilities shall be solicited.

Persons with disabilities and organizations of persons with disabilities shall have the right to submit opinions and suggestions to state organs at all levels regarding the protection of the rights and interests of persons with disabilities and the development of the cause of persons with disabilities.

Article 7 The whole society shall promote the spirit of humanitarianism, understand, respect, care for, and assist persons with disabilities, and support the cause of persons with disabilities.

The State encourages social organizations and individuals to provide donations and services to persons with disabilities.

State organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and grassroots mass autonomous organizations in urban and rural areas shall do a good job in the work concerning persons with disabilities within their respective scopes.

State functionaries and other personnel engaged in the work concerning persons with disabilities shall perform their duties in accordance with the law and strive to serve persons with disabilities.

Article 8 The China Disabled Persons' Federation and its local organizations represent the common interests of persons with disabilities, safeguard their lawful rights and interests, unite and educate persons with disabilities, and serve them.

The China Disabled Persons' Federation and its local organizations shall, in accordance with laws, regulations, and their constitutions, or upon entrustment by the government, carry out work concerning persons with disabilities, mobilize social forces, and develop the cause of persons with disabilities.

Article 9 The supporters of a person with a disability must fulfill their obligation to support the person.

The guardians of a person with a disability must fulfill their guardianship duties, respect the wishes of the ward, and safeguard the lawful rights and interests of the ward.

The relatives and guardians of a person with a disability shall encourage and help the person enhance their ability to be self-reliant.

Domestic violence against persons with disabilities is prohibited. Abusing or abandoning persons with disabilities is prohibited.

Article 10 The State encourages persons with disabilities to be self-respecting, self-confident, self-reliant, and self-empowered, and to contribute to the socialist construction.

Persons with disabilities shall abide by laws and regulations, fulfill their due obligations, observe public order, and respect social morality.

Article 11 The State shall carry out disability prevention work in a planned manner, strengthen leadership over disability prevention, publicize and popularize knowledge on maternal and child health care and disability prevention, establish and improve mechanisms for the prevention, early detection, and early treatment of birth defects, and, targeting genetic, disease, drug, accident, disaster, environmental pollution, and other disability-causing factors, organize and mobilize social forces to take measures to prevent the occurrence of disabilities and reduce the severity of disabilities.

The State shall establish and improve a statistical survey system for persons with disabilities, and carry out statistical surveys and analyses on the situation of persons with disabilities.

Article 12 The State and society shall provide special guarantees, pensions, and preferential treatment to disabled military personnel, persons disabled in the line of duty, and other persons disabled in safeguarding the interests of the State and the people.

Article 13 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall commend and reward persons with disabilities who have made outstanding achievements in socialist construction, and units and individuals who have made outstanding achievements in safeguarding the lawful rights and interests of persons with disabilities, developing the cause of persons with disabilities, and serving persons with disabilities.

Article 14 The third Sunday of May each year is the National Day of Assistance for Persons with Disabilities.

## Chapter II Rehabilitation

Article 15 The State guarantees the right of persons with disabilities to enjoy rehabilitation services.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall take measures to create conditions for the rehabilitation of persons with disabilities, establish and improve the rehabilitation service system for persons with disabilities, implement key rehabilitation projects in stages, help persons with disabilities restore or compensate for their functions, and enhance their ability to participate in social life.

Article 16 Rehabilitation work shall proceed from reality, combining modern rehabilitation techniques with traditional Chinese rehabilitation techniques; based on community rehabilitation, with rehabilitation institutions as the backbone and families of persons with disabilities as the support; focusing on practical, easy-to-implement, and widely beneficial rehabilitation content, prioritizing the provision of life-saving treatment and rehabilitation for children with disabilities; developing science and technology that meets rehabilitation requirements, encouraging independent innovation, strengthening the research, development, and application of new rehabilitation techniques, and providing effective rehabilitation services for persons with disabilities.

Article 17 People's governments at all levels encourage and support social forces to establish rehabilitation institutions for persons with disabilities.

Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall organize and guide urban and rural community service organizations, medical and health care institutions, organizations of persons with disabilities, families of persons with disabilities, and other social forces to carry out community rehabilitation work.

Educational institutions for persons with disabilities, welfare units, and other institutions serving persons with disabilities shall create conditions to carry out rehabilitation training activities.

Persons with disabilities shall, under the guidance of professionals and with the help of relevant staff, volunteers, and relatives, strive to undergo training in functions, self-care abilities, and labor skills.

Article 18 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, as needed and in a planned manner, establish rehabilitation medical departments in medical institutions, establish rehabilitation institutions for persons with disabilities, and carry out rehabilitation medical treatment and training, personnel training, technical guidance, scientific research, and other work.

Article 19 Medical colleges and other relevant institutions shall, in a planned manner, offer rehabilitation courses, establish relevant majors, and train various types of rehabilitation professionals.

The government and society shall, through various forms, provide technical training to personnel engaged in rehabilitation work; popularize rehabilitation knowledge and teach rehabilitation methods to persons with disabilities, their relatives, relevant staff, and volunteers.

Article 20 Relevant government departments shall organize and support the research, development, production, supply, and maintenance services of rehabilitation equipment and assistive devices for persons with disabilities.

## Chapter III Education

Article 21 The State guarantees the right of persons with disabilities to equal access to education.

People's governments at all levels shall regard education for persons with disabilities as an integral part of the national education undertaking, plan it uniformly, strengthen leadership, and create conditions for persons with disabilities to receive education.

The government, society, and schools shall take effective measures to address the practical difficulties faced by children and adolescents with disabilities in attending school and help them complete compulsory education.

People's governments at all levels shall provide free textbooks to students with disabilities and students from poor families with disabilities receiving compulsory education, and grant subsidies for boarding living expenses; and shall provide financial assistance to students with disabilities and students from poor families with disabilities receiving education other than compulsory education in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 22 Education for persons with disabilities shall implement the policy of combining universal access with improvement, with universal access as the focus, ensuring compulsory education, focusing on the development of vocational education, actively developing preschool education, and gradually developing education at the upper secondary level and above.

Article 23 Education for persons with disabilities shall be implemented in accordance with the following requirements, based on their physical and mental characteristics and needs:

(1) While conducting ideological and cultural education, strengthen physical and mental compensation and vocational education;

(2) Adopt regular education methods or special education methods based on the type of disability and the ability to receive education;

(3) The curriculum, teaching materials, teaching methods, and age for enrollment and schooling in special education may have appropriate flexibility.

Article 24 People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on factors such as the number, distribution, and types of disabilities of persons with disabilities, reasonably establish educational institutions for persons with disabilities, and encourage social forces to run schools and donate to support education.

Article 25 Regular educational institutions shall provide education to persons with disabilities who have the ability to receive regular education, and provide convenience and assistance for their learning.

Regular primary schools and junior secondary schools must enroll children and adolescents with disabilities who can adapt to their learning and life; regular senior secondary schools, secondary vocational schools, and institutions of higher learning must enroll candidates with disabilities who meet the admission requirements stipulated by the State, and shall not refuse admission on the grounds of their disability; if admission is refused, the person concerned or their relatives or guardians may request the relevant department to handle the matter, and the relevant department shall order the school to admit the student.

Regular preschool education institutions shall admit young children with disabilities who can adapt to their life.

Article 26 Preschool education institutions for children with disabilities, classes for children with disabilities attached to regular preschool education institutions, preschool classes in special education institutions, welfare institutions for children with disabilities, and families of children with disabilities shall provide preschool education to children with disabilities.

Special education institutions at the junior secondary level and below, and special education classes attached to regular educational institutions, shall provide compulsory education to children and adolescents with disabilities who do not have the ability to receive regular education.

Special education institutions at the upper secondary level and above, special education classes attached to regular educational institutions, and vocational education institutions for persons with disabilities shall provide cultural education at the upper secondary level and above and vocational education to eligible persons with disabilities.

Institutions providing special education shall have venues and facilities suitable for the learning, rehabilitation, and living characteristics of persons with disabilities.

Article 27 Relevant government departments, the units where persons with disabilities work, and relevant social organizations shall carry out literacy education, vocational training, entrepreneurship training, and other adult education for persons with disabilities, and encourage persons with disabilities to become educated through self-study.

Article 28 The State shall, in a planned manner, establish special education normal colleges and universities and majors at various levels and types, attach special education classes to regular normal colleges and universities, and train and cultivate special education teachers. Regular normal colleges and universities shall offer special education courses or teach relevant content, so that regular teachers acquire necessary special education knowledge.

Special education teachers and sign language interpreters shall enjoy special education subsidies.

Article 29 Relevant government departments shall organize and support the research and application of Braille and sign language, the compilation and publication of special education teaching materials, and the research, development, production, and supply of special education teaching aids and other auxiliary items.

## Chapter IV Employment

Article 30 The State guarantees the right of persons with disabilities to work.

People's governments at all levels shall make overall plans for the employment of persons with disabilities and create conditions for their employment.

Article 31 The employment of persons with disabilities shall adopt the policy of combining centralized and decentralized arrangements, adopt preferential policies and supportive and protective measures, and through multiple channels, levels, and forms, gradually make the employment of persons with disabilities universal, stable, and reasonable.

Article 32 The government and society shall establish welfare enterprises for persons with disabilities, massage institutions for the blind, and other welfare units to centrally arrange employment for persons with disabilities.

Article 33 The State implements a system of employing persons with disabilities in proportion.

State organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and private non-enterprise units shall employ persons with disabilities in the prescribed proportion and select appropriate types of work and positions for them. If the prescribed proportion is not met, they shall fulfill their obligations to ensure the employment of persons with disabilities in accordance with relevant state regulations. The State encourages employers to employ persons with disabilities in excess of the prescribed proportion.

The specific measures for the employment of persons with disabilities shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 34 The State encourages and supports persons with disabilities to choose their own jobs and start their own businesses.

Article 35 Local people's governments at all levels and rural grassroots organizations shall organize and support rural persons with disabilities to engage in planting, breeding, handicrafts, and other forms of productive labor.

Article 36 The State shall grant tax preferences in accordance with the law to employers that meet or exceed the prescribed proportion for employing persons with disabilities or that centrally arrange employment for persons with disabilities, and to persons with disabilities engaged in self-employment, and shall provide support in production, operation, technology, funds, materials, and venues. The State shall exempt administrative and institutional fees for persons with disabilities engaged in self-employment.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall determine products and projects suitable for production and operation by persons with disabilities, give priority to the production or operation of welfare units for persons with disabilities, and determine certain products to be exclusively produced by such units based on their production characteristics.

Government procurement shall, under equal conditions, give priority to purchasing products or services from welfare units for persons with disabilities.

Local people's governments at all levels shall develop public welfare positions suitable for the employment of persons with disabilities.

For persons with disabilities applying for self-employment, the relevant departments shall give priority to issuing business licenses.

For rural persons with disabilities engaged in various types of productive labor, the relevant departments shall provide assistance in production services, technical guidance, supply of agricultural materials, purchase and sale of agricultural and sideline products, and credit.

Article 37 Public employment service institutions established by relevant government departments shall provide free employment services to persons with disabilities.

Employment service institutions for persons with disabilities established by the Disabled Persons' Federation shall organize and provide free vocational guidance, job placement, and vocational training, and provide services and assistance to persons with disabilities for employment and to employers for recruiting persons with disabilities.

Article 38 The State protects the property ownership and operational autonomy of welfare units for persons with disabilities, and their lawful rights and interests shall not be infringed upon.

Discrimination against persons with disabilities is prohibited in the recruitment, regularization, promotion, professional title evaluation, labor remuneration, welfare, rest and leave, social insurance, and other aspects of employees.

The unit employing a worker with a disability shall, based on the characteristics of the worker, provide appropriate working conditions and labor protection, and, as needed, modify the workplace, labor equipment, and living facilities.

The State shall take measures to protect the lawful rights and interests of blind health care and medical massage practitioners.

Article 39 The unit employing a worker with a disability shall provide the worker with job skills training to improve their labor skills and technical level.

Article 40 No unit or individual may force a person with a disability to work by means of violence, threats, or illegal restriction of personal freedom.

## Chapter V Cultural Life

Article 41 The State guarantees the right of persons with disabilities to equal participation in cultural life.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments encourage and help persons with disabilities to participate in various cultural, sports, and recreational activities, actively create conditions, and enrich the spiritual and cultural life of persons with disabilities.

Article 42 Cultural, sports, and recreational activities for persons with disabilities shall be oriented towards the grassroots, integrated into the public cultural life of society, adapted to the different characteristics and needs of various types of persons with disabilities, and enable their broad participation.

Article 43 The government and society shall take the following measures to enrich the spiritual and cultural life of persons with disabilities:

(1) Timely publicize and report on the work and life of persons with disabilities through radio, film, television, newspapers, periodicals, books, the internet, and other forms, and serve persons with disabilities;

(2) Organize and support the compilation and publication of Braille books, audio books for the blind, and other books for persons with disabilities, and, based on the actual needs of the blind, establish Braille and audio book rooms in public libraries;

(3) Broadcast sign language programs on television, launch special radio programs for persons with disabilities, and promote the addition of subtitles and commentary to television programs and film and television works;

(4) Organize and support persons with disabilities to carry out mass cultural, sports, and recreational activities, hold special art performances and sports meets for persons with disabilities, and participate in international competitions and exchanges;

(5) Provide convenience and care for persons with disabilities in cultural, sports, recreational, and other public activity venues. Establish activity venues for persons with disabilities in a planned manner.

Article 44 The government and society encourage and help persons with disabilities to engage in creative work in literature, art, education, science, technology, and other fields beneficial to the people.

Article 45 The government and society promote mutual understanding and exchange between persons with disabilities and other citizens, publicize the cause of persons with disabilities and the deeds of assisting persons with disabilities, promote the spirit of self-improvement among persons with disabilities, and advocate a social atmosphere of unity, friendship, and mutual assistance.

## Chapter VI Social Security

Article 46 The State guarantees the right of persons with disabilities to enjoy various social security benefits.

The government and society shall take measures to improve social security for persons with disabilities and ensure and improve their lives.

Article 47 Persons with disabilities and their units shall participate in social insurance in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Grassroots mass autonomous organizations in urban and rural areas where persons with disabilities reside, and families of persons with disabilities, shall encourage and help persons with disabilities to participate in social insurance.

For persons with disabilities who have genuine difficulties in life, social insurance subsidies shall be provided in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 48 People's governments at all levels shall provide life, education, housing, and other social assistance to persons with disabilities who have genuine difficulties in life through multiple channels.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall take other measures to ensure the basic living of families of persons with disabilities who still have particular difficulties after receiving the minimum subsistence allowance.

People's governments at all levels shall provide assistance in accordance with regulations for the basic medical care, rehabilitation services, and necessary fitting and replacement of assistive devices for poor persons with disabilities.

For persons with disabilities who are unable to care for themselves, local people's governments at all levels shall provide nursing subsidies based on the circumstances.

Article 49 Local people's governments at all levels shall provide support in accordance with regulations to persons with disabilities who have no labor capacity, no supporters or whose supporters lack the ability to support them, and no source of income.

The State encourages and supports social forces to establish support and care institutions for persons with disabilities.

Support and care institutions for persons with disabilities and their staff shall not insult, abuse, or abandon persons with disabilities.Article 50 The people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on actual circumstances, provide convenience and preferential treatment for persons with disabilities when they use public transportation. Persons with disabilities may carry essential assistive devices free of charge.

Blind persons may, with valid identification, ride on urban public buses, trolleybuses, subways, ferries, and other public transportation free of charge. Mail items containing reading materials for the blind shall be delivered free of charge.

The state encourages and supports entities providing telecommunications and radio and television services to offer preferential treatment to blind persons, persons with hearing disabilities, and persons with speech disabilities.

People's governments at all levels shall gradually increase other care and assistance for persons with disabilities.

Article 51 Relevant government departments and organizations for persons with disabilities shall establish and improve channels for donations and services from all sectors of society to persons with disabilities, encourage and support the development of charitable undertakings for persons with disabilities, and carry out public welfare activities such as volunteer assistance for persons with disabilities.

## Chapter 7 Barrier-Free Environment

Article 52 The state and society shall take measures to gradually improve barrier-free facilities, promote barrier-free information exchange, and create a barrier-free environment for persons with disabilities to participate equally in social life.

People's governments at all levels shall carry out overall planning, comprehensive coordination, and strengthen supervision and management for the construction of barrier-free environments.

Article 53 The construction and renovation of barrier-free facilities shall meet the actual needs of persons with disabilities.

The construction, renovation, and expansion of buildings, roads, and transportation facilities shall comply with the national standards for barrier-free facility engineering.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with national regulations on barrier-free facility engineering, gradually promote the renovation of existing facilities, prioritizing the renovation of public service facilities closely related to the daily work and life of persons with disabilities.

Barrier-free facilities shall be promptly repaired and maintained.

Article 54 The state takes measures to create conditions for barrier-free information exchange for persons with disabilities.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall take measures to facilitate access to public information for persons with disabilities.

The state and society shall research and develop information exchange technologies and products suitable for persons with disabilities.

In various national entrance examinations, professional qualification examinations, and job appointment examinations involving blind persons, Braille test papers, electronic test papers, or assistance from specialized staff shall be provided.

Article 55 Public service institutions and public places shall create conditions to provide information exchange services such as voice and text prompts, sign language, and Braille for persons with disabilities, and offer priority services and auxiliary services.

Public transportation shall gradually meet the requirements for barrier-free facilities. Conditional public parking lots shall set up dedicated parking spaces for persons with disabilities.

Article 56 Departments organizing elections shall provide convenience for persons with disabilities to participate in elections; where conditions permit, Braille ballots shall be provided for blind persons.

Article 57 The state encourages and supports the research and development of barrier-free assistive devices and barrier-free transportation.

Article 58 Blind persons entering public places with guide dogs shall comply with relevant national regulations.

## Chapter 8 Legal Liability

Article 59 If the lawful rights and interests of a person with a disability are infringed upon, they may file a complaint with an organization for persons with disabilities, which shall protect their lawful rights and interests and have the right to request the relevant department or entity to investigate and handle the matter. The relevant department or entity shall investigate and handle it in accordance with the law and provide a response.

Organizations for persons with disabilities shall provide support to persons with disabilities who need assistance in safeguarding their lawful rights and interests through litigation.

Organizations for persons with disabilities have the right to request relevant departments to investigate and handle acts that infringe upon the interests of specific groups of persons with disabilities in accordance with the law.

Article 60 If the lawful rights and interests of a person with a disability are infringed upon, they have the right to request the relevant department to handle the matter in accordance with the law, or apply for arbitration to an arbitration institution in accordance with the law, or file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with the law.

For persons with disabilities who have financial difficulties or other reasons genuinely requiring legal aid or judicial assistance, the local legal aid institution or people's court shall provide assistance and offer legal aid or judicial assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 61 In violation of this Law, if any entity or individual shirks, delays, suppresses, or fails to investigate complaints, accusations, or reports of acts infringing upon the rights of persons with disabilities, or retaliates against those who have made such complaints, accusations, or reports, the entity, its competent department, or the superior authority shall order correction, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

If a state functionary fails to perform their duties in accordance with the law, fails to promptly stop acts infringing upon the rights of persons with disabilities, or fails to provide necessary assistance to the victimized person with a disability, resulting in serious consequences, the entity or the superior authority shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 62 In violation of this Law, if any entity or individual belittles or damages the dignity of persons with disabilities through mass media or other means, the competent departments of culture, radio and television, film, press and publication, or other relevant departments shall, within their respective functions, order correction and impose administrative penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 63 In violation of this Law, if an educational institution refuses to admit students with disabilities or imposes additional conditions beyond the admission requirements stipulated by the state to restrict the enrollment of students with disabilities, the relevant competent department shall order correction and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 64 In violation of this Law, if any entity discriminates against persons with disabilities in matters such as employee recruitment, the relevant competent department shall order correction; the worker with a disability may file a lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with the law.

Article 65 In violation of this Law, if a support or care institution or its staff insults, abuses, or abandons persons with disabilities, sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, administrative penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

Article 66 In violation of this Law, if the construction, renovation, or expansion of buildings, roads, or transportation facilities fails to meet the national standards for barrier-free facility engineering, or if barrier-free facilities are not promptly repaired or maintained, resulting in consequences, the relevant competent department shall handle the matter in accordance with the law.

Article 67 In violation of this Law, if the lawful rights and interests of a person with a disability are infringed upon, and other laws or regulations provide for administrative penalties, those provisions shall apply; if property loss or other damage is caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 68 This Law shall come into effect on July 1, 2008.
