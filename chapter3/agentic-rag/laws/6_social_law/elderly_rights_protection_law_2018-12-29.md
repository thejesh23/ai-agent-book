# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of the Rights and Interests of the Elderly

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on August 29, 1996

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revised at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 28, 2012

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws including the Electricity Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to protect the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, develop undertakings for the elderly, and promote the virtues of respecting, providing for, and assisting the elderly in the Chinese nation.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, the term "elderly" refers to citizens aged sixty or above.

Article 3 The State guarantees the rights and interests enjoyed by the elderly in accordance with the law.

The elderly have the right to obtain material assistance from the State and society, the right to enjoy social services and social preferences, and the right to participate in social development and share the fruits of development.

Discrimination, insult, abuse, or abandonment of the elderly is prohibited.

Article 4 Actively responding to population aging is a long-term strategic task of the State.

The State and society shall take measures to improve various systems for protecting the rights and interests of the elderly, gradually enhance the conditions for ensuring the livelihood, health, safety, and participation in social development of the elderly, and achieve the goals of providing for the elderly, ensuring their medical care, enabling their continued contribution, supporting their learning, and promoting their well-being.

Article 5 The State establishes a multi-level social security system and gradually raises the level of protection for the elderly.

The State establishes and improves a social elderly care service system based on home care, supported by community services, and backed by institutional care.

The whole society is encouraged to provide preferential treatment to the elderly.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate undertakings for the elderly into national economic and social development plans, include expenditures for such undertakings in fiscal budgets, establish a stable funding guarantee mechanism, and encourage investment from all sectors of society, so that undertakings for the elderly develop in coordination with the economy and society.

The State Council formulates a national development plan for undertakings for the elderly. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the national development plan, formulate development plans and annual plans for undertakings for the elderly within their respective administrative regions.

The working body for undertakings for the elderly under the people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, guiding, and supervising relevant departments in protecting the rights and interests of the elderly.

Article 7 Protecting the lawful rights and interests of the elderly is the common responsibility of the whole society.

State organs, social organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and other organizations shall, in accordance with their respective duties, properly protect the rights and interests of the elderly.

Grassroots self-governing organizations and lawfully established organizations for the elderly shall reflect the demands of the elderly, safeguard their lawful rights and interests, and provide services for them.

Voluntary services for the elderly are advocated and encouraged.

Article 8 The State conducts national education on population aging to enhance the whole society's awareness of actively responding to population aging.

The whole society shall extensively carry out publicity and education activities on respecting, providing for, and assisting the elderly, and foster a social atmosphere of respecting, caring for, and helping the elderly.

Youth organizations, schools, and kindergartens shall conduct moral education on respecting, providing for, and assisting the elderly, as well as legal education on protecting the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, for young people and children.

Radio, film, television, newspapers, the internet, and other media shall reflect the lives of the elderly, carry out publicity on protecting the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, and serve the elderly.

Article 9 The State supports scientific research on aging and establishes a system for statistical surveys and publication of the status of the elderly.

Article 10 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, commend or reward organizations, families, or individuals that have achieved outstanding results in protecting the lawful rights and interests of the elderly and in respecting, providing for, and assisting the elderly, as well as elderly persons who have made outstanding contributions to participating in social development.

Article 11 The elderly shall abide by laws and regulations and fulfill the obligations stipulated by law.

Article 12 The ninth day of the ninth lunar month each year is designated as the Elderly Day.

## Chapter II Family Support and Care

Article 13 Elderly care is based on home care, and family members shall respect, care for, and attend to the elderly.

Article 14 Supporters shall fulfill the obligations of providing financial support, daily care, and spiritual comfort to the elderly, and attend to their special needs.

Supporters refer to the children of the elderly and other persons who are legally obligated to provide support.

The spouse of a supporter shall assist the supporter in fulfilling the support obligations.

Article 15 Supporters shall ensure that ill elderly persons receive timely treatment and nursing care; for elderly persons with financial difficulties, supporters shall provide medical expenses.

For elderly persons who are unable to care for themselves, supporters shall bear the responsibility of care; if supporters cannot personally provide care, they may, in accordance with the wishes of the elderly, entrust the care to other persons or elderly care institutions.

Article 16 Supporters shall properly arrange housing for the elderly and shall not force the elderly to live in or move to substandard housing.

Children or other relatives shall not encroach upon or arbitrarily change the property rights or leasehold rights of housing owned or rented by the elderly.

Supporters have the obligation to maintain housing owned by the elderly.

Article 17 Supporters have the obligation to cultivate, or entrust others to cultivate, the land contracted by the elderly, and to tend, or entrust others to tend, the trees and livestock of the elderly, with the proceeds belonging to the elderly.

Article 18 Family members shall care about the spiritual needs of the elderly and shall not neglect or snub them.

Family members who live separately from the elderly shall frequently visit or inquire after them.

Employers shall, in accordance with relevant State regulations, guarantee the right of supporters to take leave for family visits.

Article 19 Supporters shall not refuse to fulfill their support obligations on the grounds of renouncing inheritance rights or any other reasons.

If a supporter fails to fulfill support obligations, the elderly person has the right to demand payment of support costs and other rights.

Supporters shall not require the elderly to perform labor beyond their capacity.

Article 20 With the consent of the elderly, supporters may enter into agreements on fulfilling support obligations. The content of such support agreements shall not violate legal provisions or the wishes of the elderly.

Grassroots self-governing organizations, organizations for the elderly, or the employer of the supporter shall supervise the performance of the agreement.

Article 21 The freedom of marriage of the elderly is protected by law. Children or other relatives shall not interfere with the divorce, remarriage, or post-marriage life of the elderly.

The support obligations of supporters shall not be eliminated due to changes in the marital status of the elderly.

Article 22 The elderly have the right to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of their personal property in accordance with the law. Children or other relatives shall not interfere with or infringe upon the property rights and interests of the elderly through theft, fraud, coercion, or other means.

The elderly have the right to inherit the estate of their parents, spouses, children, or other relatives in accordance with the law, and the right to accept gifts. Children or other relatives shall not encroach upon, seize, transfer, conceal, or destroy property that should be inherited or accepted as gifts by the elderly.

When disposing of property by will, the elderly shall reserve the necessary share for their elderly spouse in accordance with the law.

Article 23 The elderly and their spouses have the mutual obligation to support each other.

Younger siblings raised by elder brothers or sisters, upon reaching adulthood and having the ability to do so, have the obligation to support their elder brothers or sisters who are aged and lack supporters.

Article 24 If supporters or caregivers fail to fulfill their support or care obligations, grassroots self-governing organizations, organizations for the elderly, or the employer of the supporter or caregiver shall urge them to fulfill such obligations.

Article 25 Domestic violence against the elderly is prohibited.

Article 26 Elderly persons with full capacity for civil conduct may, through consultation, designate their guardian among close relatives or other individuals or organizations that have a close relationship with them and are willing to assume guardianship responsibilities. The guardian shall assume guardianship responsibilities in accordance with the law when the elderly person loses or partially loses capacity for civil conduct.

If an elderly person has not designated a guardian in advance, the guardian shall be determined in accordance with relevant legal provisions when the elderly person loses or partially loses capacity for civil conduct.

Article 27 The State establishes and improves policies to support family-based elderly care, encourages family members to live with or near the elderly, provides conditions for the elderly to relocate with their spouses or supporters, and assists family members in caring for the elderly.

## Chapter III Social Security

Article 28 The State guarantees the basic livelihood of the elderly through the basic old-age insurance system.

Article 29 The State guarantees the basic medical needs of the elderly through the basic medical insurance system. The government shall provide subsidies for the individual contributions required for elderly persons receiving minimum subsistence allowances and eligible elderly persons from low-income families to participate in the new rural cooperative medical care system and the basic medical insurance for urban residents.

Relevant departments shall give consideration to the elderly when formulating medical insurance measures.

Article 30 The State gradually develops long-term care security to meet the care needs of the elderly.

For elderly persons who are long-term unable to care for themselves and have financial difficulties, local people's governments at all levels shall provide care subsidies based on their degree of disability and other circumstances.

Article 31 The State provides basic living, medical, housing, or other assistance to elderly persons with financial difficulties.

For elderly persons who are unable to work, have no source of income, and have no supporters or caregivers, or whose supporters or caregivers are genuinely unable to provide support or care, local people's governments at all levels shall provide support or assistance in accordance with relevant regulations.

For elderly persons who are homeless, wandering, begging, or abandoned, local people's governments at all levels shall provide assistance in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 32 When implementing housing security systems such as low-rent housing and public rental housing, or carrying out renovation of dilapidated and old houses, local people's governments at all levels shall give priority to eligible elderly persons.

Article 33 The State establishes and improves the welfare system for the elderly, and increases social welfare for the elderly based on the level of economic and social development and the actual needs of the elderly.

The State encourages local governments to establish an old-age allowance system for low-income elderly persons aged eighty or above.

The State establishes and improves the support system for elderly persons from families that have complied with family planning policies.

In rural areas, part of the un-contracted collectively-owned land, mountain forests, water surfaces, and tidal flats may be used as bases for elderly care, with the proceeds used for the care of the elderly.

Article 34 The pensions, medical benefits, and other benefits lawfully enjoyed by the elderly shall be guaranteed. Relevant institutions must pay them in full and on time, and shall not withhold, delay, or misappropriate them.

The State shall, based on economic development, increases in average wages of employees, and price increases, raise the level of old-age security in a timely manner.

Article 35 The State encourages charitable organizations and other organizations and individuals to provide material assistance to the elderly.

Article 36 The elderly may enter into legacy-support agreements or other support agreements with collective economic organizations, grassroots self-governing organizations, elderly care institutions, or individuals.

Organizations or individuals with support obligations under a legacy-support agreement shall assume the responsibility of caring for the elderly person during their lifetime and arranging their funeral after death, and shall have the right to receive the legacy.

## Chapter IV Social Services

Article 37 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall take measures to develop urban and rural community-based elderly care services, and encourage and support professional service institutions and other organizations and individuals to provide home-based elderly persons with various forms of services such as daily care, emergency rescue, medical care, spiritual comfort, and psychological counseling.

For elderly persons with financial difficulties, local people's governments at all levels shall gradually provide elderly care service subsidies.

Article 38 Local people's governments at all levels, relevant departments, and grassroots self-governing organizations shall incorporate elderly care service facilities into the planning of supporting facilities for urban and rural communities, establish service facilities and outlets that meet the needs of the elderly, such as those for daily living services, cultural and sports activities, day care, disease care and rehabilitation, and provide services to the elderly nearby.

The tradition of mutual assistance among neighbors shall be promoted, and neighbors are encouraged to care for and help elderly persons in need.

Charitable organizations and volunteers are encouraged to serve the elderly. Mutual assistance services among the elderly are advocated.

Article 39 People's governments at all levels shall gradually increase investment in elderly care services based on the level of economic development and the service needs of the elderly.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall take measures in terms of finance, taxation, land, and financing to encourage and support enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, or individuals to establish and operate facilities such as elderly care institutions, day care centers for the elderly, and cultural and sports activity venues for the elderly.

Article 40 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the proportion and distribution of the elderly population, incorporate the construction of elderly care service facilities into urban and rural planning and overall land use planning, and make overall arrangements for the land and materials needed for the construction of elderly care service facilities.

Land for public welfare elderly care service facilities may be allocated through State-owned allocated land or collectively-owned land of farmers in accordance with the law.

The use of land for elderly care service facilities shall not be changed without legal procedures.

Article 41 Elderly care institutions invested in and operated by the government shall give priority to meeting the service needs of elderly persons who are financially disadvantaged, such as those who are childless, disabled, or of advanced age.

Article 42 Relevant departments of the State Council shall formulate standards for the construction of elderly care service facilities, the quality of elderly care services, and elderly care service professions, and establish and improve a classified management system for elderly care institutions and an evaluation system for elderly care services.

People's governments at all levels shall regulate the items and standards of fees for elderly care services and strengthen supervision and management.

Article 43 The establishment of a public welfare elderly care institution shall be registered in accordance with the law.

The establishment of a for-profit elderly care institution shall be registered with the market supervision and administration department.

An elderly care institution may commence service activities after registration and shall file with the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level.

Article 44 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over the management of elderly care institutions within their respective administrative regions and establish a comprehensive supervision system for elderly care institutions.

The civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the guidance, supervision, and management of elderly care institutions, and other relevant departments shall supervise elderly care institutions in accordance with their respective duties.

Article 45 The civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level shall perform supervision and inspection duties in accordance with the law and may take the following measures:

(1) Inquire about the situation from elderly care institutions and individuals;
(2) Enter elderly care institutions suspected of illegal activities for on-site inspection;
(3) Review or copy relevant contracts, bills, account books, and other relevant materials;
(4) If an elderly care institution is found to have risks that may endanger personal health or life and property safety, order it to rectify within a specified period; if it fails to rectify within the period, order it to suspend operations for rectification.

When investigating suspected illegal activities of elderly care institutions, the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level shall comply with the provisions of the Administrative Compulsion Law of the People's Republic of China and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 46 If an elderly care institution is changed or terminated, it shall properly arrange for the elderly persons in its care and go through the relevant procedures with the relevant departments in accordance with regulations. Relevant departments shall provide assistance to elderly care institutions in properly arranging for the elderly.

Article 47 The State establishes and improves a system for the training, utilization, evaluation, and incentive of elderly care service personnel, standardizes employment in accordance with the law, promotes reasonable growth of labor remuneration for practitioners, and develops a service team for the elderly combining full-time, part-time, and volunteer workers.

The State encourages institutions of higher learning, secondary vocational schools, and vocational training institutions to set up relevant majors or training programs to cultivate professional talents for elderly care services.

Article 48 Elderly care institutions shall sign service agreements with the elderly persons they serve or their agents, specifying the rights and obligations of both parties.

Elderly care institutions and their staff shall not infringe upon the rights and interests of the elderly in any way.

Article 49 The State encourages elderly care institutions to take out liability insurance and encourages insurance companies to underwrite liability insurance.

Article 50 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall incorporate medical and health services for the elderly into urban and rural medical and health service plans, and incorporate health management and common disease prevention for the elderly into the national basic public health service projects. The provision of services such as health care, nursing, and hospice care for the elderly is encouraged.

The State encourages medical institutions to set up specialized departments or outpatient clinics for geriatric diseases.

Medical and health institutions shall carry out health services and disease prevention and treatment work for the elderly.

Article 51 The State takes measures to strengthen research on geriatric medicine and personnel training, improve the level of prevention, treatment, and scientific research of geriatric diseases, and promote the early detection, diagnosis, and treatment of geriatric diseases.

The State and society shall take measures to carry out various forms of health education, popularize knowledge of geriatric health care, and enhance the self-health awareness of the elderly.

Article 52 The State takes measures to develop the silver economy and includes the silver economy in the national catalog of supported industries. It supports and guides enterprises in developing, producing, and operating products suitable for the needs of the elderly and providing related services.

## Chapter V Social Preferences

Article 53 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall, based on the economic and social development situation and the special needs of the elderly, formulate measures for providing preferential treatment to the elderly and gradually raise the level of such treatment.

Equal preferential treatment shall be given to elderly persons from other places who are permanently resident within the administrative region.

Article 54 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall provide conditions for the elderly to receive pensions, settle medical expenses, and enjoy other material assistance in a timely and convenient manner.

Article 55 When handling major matters involving the rights and interests of the elderly, such as changes in housing property rights or household registration transfers, people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall inquire whether the matter reflects the true intention of the elderly and handle it with priority in accordance with the law.

Article 56 If an elderly person has difficulty paying litigation fees when filing a lawsuit due to infringement of their lawful rights and interests, the fees may be deferred, reduced, or waived; if the elderly person needs the assistance of a lawyer but cannot afford the legal fees, they may receive legal aid.

Law firms, notary offices, basic-level legal service offices, and other legal service institutions are encouraged to provide free or preferential services to elderly persons with financial difficulties.

Article 57 Medical institutions shall provide convenience for the elderly to seek medical treatment and give priority to the elderly in medical services. Where conditions permit, family beds may be set up for the elderly, and services such as mobile medical visits, nursing, rehabilitation, and free physical examinations may be provided.

Free medical consultations for the elderly are encouraged.

Article 58 Service industries closely related to the daily lives of the elderly are encouraged to provide priority and preferential services to the elderly.

Urban public transportation, as well as highway, railway, waterway, and air passenger transport, shall provide preferential treatment and care for the elderly.

Article 59 Venues such as museums, art galleries, science and technology museums, memorial halls, public libraries, cultural centers, theaters, gymnasiums, parks, and tourist attractions shall be open to the elderly free of charge or at a reduced price.

Article 60 Rural elderly persons are exempt from the obligation to contribute labor for public welfare undertakings.## Chapter 6 Livable Environment

Article 61 The State shall take measures to promote the construction of a livable environment, providing a safe, convenient, and comfortable environment for the elderly.

Article 62 When formulating urban and rural plans, people's governments at all levels shall, based on the development trend of population aging, the distribution of the elderly population, and the characteristics of the elderly, make overall plans for the construction of public infrastructure, living service facilities, medical and health facilities, and cultural and sports facilities suitable for the elderly.

Article 63 The State shall formulate and improve the engineering construction standard system involving the elderly, and strengthen the implementation and supervision of relevant standards in planning, design, construction, supervision, acceptance, operation, maintenance, management, and other aspects.

Article 64 The State shall formulate engineering construction standards for barrier-free facilities. The construction, renovation, and expansion of roads, public transportation facilities, buildings, and residential areas shall comply with the national engineering construction standards for barrier-free facilities.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the national engineering construction standards for barrier-free facilities, give priority to promoting the renovation of public service facilities closely related to the daily lives of the elderly.

The owners and managers of barrier-free facilities shall ensure the normal use of such facilities.

Article 65 The State shall promote the construction of age-friendly communities, guide and support the development of age-friendly housing, promote and support the renovation of barrier-free facilities in the homes of the elderly, and create a barrier-free living environment for the elderly.

## Chapter 7 Participation in Social Development

Article 66 The State and society shall value and cherish the knowledge, skills, experience, and fine virtues of the elderly, give full play to their expertise and roles, and ensure their participation in economic, political, cultural, and social life.

Article 67 The elderly may, through organizations for the elderly, carry out activities beneficial to physical and mental health.

Article 68 When formulating laws, regulations, rules, and public policies involving major issues concerning the rights and interests of the elderly, the opinions of the elderly and organizations for the elderly shall be solicited.

The elderly and organizations for the elderly have the right to submit opinions and suggestions to state organs regarding the protection of the rights and interests of the elderly and the development of undertakings for the elderly.

Article 69 The State shall create conditions for the elderly to participate in social development. According to social needs and possibilities, the elderly are encouraged, on a voluntary basis and within their capabilities, to engage in the following activities:

(1) Educating adolescents and children in socialist, patriotic, collectivist, and hard-working fine traditions;
(2) Imparting cultural, scientific, and technological knowledge;
(3) Providing consulting services;
(4) Participating in scientific and technological development and application in accordance with the law;
(5) Engaging in business and production activities in accordance with the law;
(6) Participating in volunteer services and establishing social welfare undertakings;
(7) Participating in maintaining social order and assisting in mediating civil disputes;
(8) Participating in other social activities.

Article 70 The lawful income of the elderly from their labor shall be protected by law.

No unit or individual may assign the elderly to engage in labor or dangerous operations that harm their physical and mental health.

Article 71 The elderly have the right to continue their education.

The State shall develop education for the elderly, incorporate it into the lifelong education system, and encourage society to run various types of schools for the elderly well.

People's governments at all levels shall strengthen leadership over education for the elderly, make unified plans, and increase investment.

Article 72 The State and society shall take measures to carry out mass cultural, sports, and recreational activities suitable for the elderly, enriching their spiritual and cultural life.

## Chapter 8 Legal Liability

Article 73 Where the lawful rights and interests of the elderly are infringed upon, the infringed party or their agent has the right to request the relevant department to handle the matter, or to file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

The people's courts and relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, promptly accept complaints, accusations, and reports concerning the infringement of the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, and shall not prevaricate or delay.

Article 74 Where a department or organization fails to fulfill its duty to protect the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, its superior competent department shall give criticism and education, and order it to make corrections.

Where a state functionary, through illegal conduct or dereliction of duty, causes damage to the lawful rights and interests of the elderly, the unit to which they belong or the superior organ shall order them to make corrections, or impose sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 75 Where disputes arise between the elderly and family members over support, maintenance, housing, property, or other matters, they may apply to the people's mediation committee or other relevant organizations for mediation, or directly file a lawsuit with the people's court.

When mediating the disputes mentioned in the preceding paragraph, the people's mediation committee or other relevant organizations shall resolve conflicts and disputes through persuasion, guidance, and other means; family members at fault shall be given criticism and education.

When the elderly apply for advance execution of support or maintenance payments, the people's court may, in accordance with the law, rule for advance execution.

Article 76 Where a person interferes with the freedom of marriage of the elderly, refuses to fulfill the obligation to support or maintain the elderly, abuses the elderly, or commits domestic violence against the elderly, the relevant unit shall give criticism and education; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 77 Where a family member steals, defrauds, forcibly takes, embezzles, extorts, or intentionally damages the property of the elderly, if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 78 Where a person insults or slanders the elderly, if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 79 Where a nursing home for the elderly or its staff infringes upon the personal or property rights and interests of the elderly, or fails to provide services as agreed, they shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; the relevant competent department shall impose administrative penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 80 Where a department responsible for the management and supervision of nursing homes for the elderly or its staff abuses power, neglects duty, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 81 Where a person fails to fulfill the obligation to provide preferential treatment to the elderly as prescribed, the relevant competent department shall order them to make corrections.

Article 82 Where a project involving the elderly does not meet the standards prescribed by the State, or the owner or manager of barrier-free facilities fails to fulfill their maintenance and management duties, the relevant competent department shall order them to make corrections; if damage is caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; administrative penalties shall be imposed on the relevant unit or individual in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 83 The people's congresses of national autonomous areas may, in accordance with the principles of this Law and in light of the specific conditions of local ethnic customs, formulate adaptive or supplementary provisions in accordance with legal procedures.

Article 84 Nursing homes for the elderly established before the implementation of this Law that do not meet the conditions prescribed by this Law shall make corrections within a specified period. Specific measures shall be formulated by the civil affairs department of the State Council.

Article 85 This Law shall come into force on July 1, 2013.
