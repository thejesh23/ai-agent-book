# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Women's Rights and Interests

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 3, 1992

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Women's Rights and Interests adopted at the 17th Session of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 28, 2005

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Fifteen Laws including the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Wildlife adopted at the Sixth Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 26, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution and the actual conditions of our country, with a view to protecting the legitimate rights and interests of women, promoting equality between men and women, and giving full play to women's role in socialist modernization.

Article 2 Women shall enjoy equal rights with men in all aspects of political, economic, cultural, social and family life.

The realization of equality between men and women is a basic national policy. The State shall take necessary measures to gradually improve the systems for protecting women's rights and interests and eliminate all forms of discrimination against women.

The State shall protect the special rights and interests enjoyed by women in accordance with law.

Discrimination against, maltreatment of, abandonment of, or physical harm to women shall be prohibited.

Article 3 The State Council shall formulate the Outline for the Development of Chinese Women and incorporate it into the national economic and social development plan.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the Outline for the Development of Chinese Women, formulate plans for the development of women within their respective administrative regions and incorporate them into the national economic and social development plans.

Article 4 The protection of the legitimate rights and interests of women is the common responsibility of the whole society. State organs, social organizations, enterprises, institutions, and grassroots mass self-governing organizations in urban and rural areas shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and relevant laws, protect the rights and interests of women.

The State shall take effective measures to provide necessary conditions for women to exercise their rights in accordance with law.

Article 5 The State shall encourage women to cultivate self-respect, self-confidence, self-reliance, and self-strength, and to use the law to protect their own legitimate rights and interests.

Women shall abide by the laws of the State, respect social morality, and fulfill the obligations stipulated by law.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall attach importance to and strengthen the work of protecting women's rights and interests.

The institutions responsible for women's and children's work under people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating, guiding, and supervising relevant departments in the work of protecting women's rights and interests.

Relevant departments under people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective functions and duties, do a good job in protecting women's rights and interests.

Article 7 The All-China Women's Federation and local women's federations at all levels shall, in accordance with the law and the Constitution of the All-China Women's Federation, represent and safeguard the interests of women of all ethnic groups and from all walks of life, and do a good job in protecting women's rights and interests.

Trade unions and the Communist Youth League shall, within their respective scopes of work, do a good job in protecting women's rights and interests.

Article 8 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have achieved outstanding results in protecting the legitimate rights and interests of women.

## Chapter II Political Rights

Article 9 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal political rights with men.

Article 10 Women shall have the right to manage state affairs, economic and cultural undertakings, and social affairs through various channels and in various ways.

When formulating laws, regulations, rules, and public policies, opinions of women's federations shall be heard on major issues involving women's rights and interests.

Women and women's organizations shall have the right to submit opinions and suggestions to state organs at all levels regarding the protection of women's rights and interests.

Article 11 Women shall enjoy the equal right to vote and stand for election with men.

There shall be an appropriate number of women deputies in the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at all levels. The State shall take measures to gradually increase the proportion of women deputies in the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at all levels.

There shall be an appropriate number of women members in residents' committees and villagers' committees.

Article 12 The State shall actively train and select women cadres.

State organs, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions must adhere to the principle of equality between men and women when training, selecting, and appointing cadres, and there shall be an appropriate number of women holding leadership positions.

The State attaches importance to the training and selection of women cadres from ethnic minorities.

Article 13 The All-China Women's Federation and local women's federations at all levels shall represent women in actively participating in the democratic decision-making, democratic management, and democratic supervision of state and social affairs.

Women's federations at all levels and their group members may recommend women cadres to state organs, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions.

Article 14 Relevant departments shall listen to and adopt criticisms or reasonable suggestions concerning the protection of women's rights and interests; regarding complaints, accusations, and reports concerning the infringement of women's rights and interests, relevant departments must ascertain the facts and handle them responsibly, and no organization or individual may suppress them or retaliate.

## Chapter III Cultural and Educational Rights and Interests

Article 15 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal cultural and educational rights with men.

Article 16 Schools and relevant departments shall implement relevant state regulations to ensure that women enjoy equal rights with men in terms of enrollment, admission to higher-level schools, job assignment upon graduation, conferment of academic degrees, and dispatch for study abroad.

When admitting students, schools shall not refuse to admit women or raise the admission standards for women on the grounds of gender, except for special majors.

Article 17 Schools shall, based on the characteristics of female adolescents, take measures in education, management, facilities, etc., to ensure the healthy physical and mental development of female adolescents.

Article 18 Parents or other guardians must fulfill the obligation to ensure that school-age female children and adolescents receive compulsory education.

Except for reasons of illness or other special circumstances approved by the local people's government, parents or other guardians who fail to send school-age female children and adolescents to school shall be criticized and educated by the local people's government, which shall take effective measures to order them to send the school-age female children and adolescents to school.

The government, society, and schools shall take effective measures to address the practical difficulties faced by school-age female children and adolescents in attending school, and create conditions to ensure that school-age female children and adolescents from impoverished, disabled, and migrant populations complete compulsory education.

Article 19 People's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with regulations, incorporate the work of eliminating illiteracy and semi-illiteracy among women into the plan for literacy education and post-literacy continuing education, adopt organizational forms and working methods suitable for women's characteristics, and organize and supervise relevant departments for specific implementation.

Article 20 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall take measures, based on the needs of women in urban and rural areas, to organize women to receive vocational education and practical technical training.

Article 21 State organs, social organizations, enterprises, and institutions shall implement relevant state regulations to ensure that women enjoy equal rights with men in engaging in scientific, technological, literary, artistic, and other cultural activities.

## Chapter IV Labor and Social Security Rights and Interests

Article 22 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal labor rights and social security rights with men.

Article 23 When recruiting employees, all units shall not refuse to recruit women or raise the recruitment standards for women on the grounds of gender, except for types of work or positions unsuitable for women.

When recruiting female employees, all units shall sign labor (employment) contracts or service agreements with them in accordance with law, and such contracts or agreements shall not contain provisions restricting female employees from getting married or having children.

The recruitment of female minors under the age of 16 is prohibited, except as otherwise provided by the State.

Article 24 Equal pay for equal work shall be implemented for men and women. Women shall enjoy equal rights with men in terms of receiving welfare benefits.

Article 25 In matters such as promotion, advancement in rank, and evaluation of professional technical titles, the principle of equality between men and women shall be adhered to, and women shall not be discriminated against.

Article 26 All units shall, based on women's characteristics, protect women's safety and health during work and labor in accordance with law, and shall not assign work or labor unsuitable for women.

Women shall receive special protection during menstruation, pregnancy, childbirth, and lactation.

Article 27 No unit may reduce the wages of female employees, dismiss female employees, or unilaterally terminate labor (employment) contracts or service agreements due to marriage, pregnancy, maternity leave, breastfeeding, or other such circumstances. However, this does not apply if the female employee requests termination of the labor (employment) contract or service agreement.

When implementing the state retirement system, all units shall not discriminate against women on the grounds of gender.

Article 28 The State shall develop social insurance, social assistance, social welfare, and medical and health undertakings to ensure that women enjoy rights and interests such as social insurance, social assistance, social welfare, and health care.

The State advocates and encourages social welfare activities aimed at helping women.

Article 29 The State shall promote the maternity insurance system and establish and improve other security systems related to childbirth.

Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall, in accordance with relevant regulations, provide necessary maternity assistance to impoverished women.

## Chapter V Property Rights and Interests

Article 30 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal property rights with men.

Article 31 In the relationship of marital or family jointly owned property, the rights and interests enjoyed by women in accordance with law shall not be infringed upon.

Article 32 Women shall enjoy equal rights with men in terms of rural land contractual management, distribution of income from collective economic organizations, use of compensation for land expropriation or requisition, and use of homestead land.

Article 33 No organization or individual may infringe upon women's various rights and interests in rural collective economic organizations on the grounds of being unmarried, married, divorced, or widowed.

Where a man settles in the woman's household due to marriage, the man and his children shall enjoy equal rights and interests with members of the local rural collective economic organization.

Article 34 Women's equal right of inheritance with men shall be protected by law. Among statutory heirs in the same order, women shall not be discriminated against.

A widowed woman has the right to dispose of the inherited property, and no one may interfere.

Article 35 Where a widowed woman has fulfilled the main obligation of supporting her parents-in-law, she shall be regarded as a first-order statutory heir of her parents-in-law, and her inheritance right shall not be affected by the subrogation inheritance of her children.

## Chapter VI Personal Rights

Article 36 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal personal rights with men.

Article 37 Women's personal freedom shall be inviolable. Unlawful detention or deprivation or restriction of women's personal freedom by other unlawful means is prohibited; unlawful search of women's bodies is prohibited.

Article 38 Women's right to life and health shall be inviolable. Drowning, abandoning, or physically harming female infants is prohibited; discrimination against or maltreatment of women who give birth to female infants or women who are infertile is prohibited; harming women by superstitious or violent means is prohibited; maltreatment of or abandonment of sick, disabled, or elderly women is prohibited.

Article 39 Trafficking in or kidnapping of women is prohibited; buying trafficked or kidnapped women is prohibited; obstructing the rescue of trafficked or kidnapped women is prohibited.

People's governments at all levels and departments such as public security, civil affairs, labor and social security, and health shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, take timely measures to rescue trafficked or kidnapped women and carry out follow-up work properly. Women's federations shall assist and cooperate in the relevant work. No one may discriminate against trafficked or kidnapped women.

Article 40 Sexual harassment against women is prohibited. The victimized woman has the right to file a complaint with the unit or relevant authorities.

Article 41 Prostitution and patronizing prostitutes are prohibited.

Organizing, forcing, enticing, harboring, or procuring women for prostitution or engaging in obscene activities with women is prohibited.

Organizing, forcing, or enticing women to engage in pornographic performances is prohibited.

Article 42 Women's personality rights such as the right to reputation, right to honor, right to privacy, and right to portrait shall be protected by law.

Damaging women's personal dignity by means of insult, defamation, etc., is prohibited. Belittling or damaging women's personality through mass media or other means is prohibited. Without the consent of the woman concerned, her portrait shall not be used for profit-making purposes through advertisements, trademarks, display windows, newspapers, periodicals, books, audio-visual products, electronic publications, the internet, or other forms.

## Chapter VII Rights and Interests in Marriage and Family

Article 43 The State shall guarantee that women enjoy equal rights with men in marriage and family.

Article 44 The State shall protect women's right to freedom of marriage. Interference with women's freedom to marry or divorce is prohibited.

Article 45 During a woman's pregnancy, within one year after childbirth, or within six months after termination of pregnancy, the husband may not file for divorce. This restriction does not apply if the wife files for divorce, or if the people's court deems it necessary to accept the husband's divorce petition.

Article 46 Domestic violence against women is prohibited.

The State shall take measures to prevent and stop domestic violence.

Departments such as public security, civil affairs, and judicial administration, as well as urban and rural grassroots mass self-governing organizations and social organizations, shall, within their respective functions and duties, prevent and stop domestic violence and provide assistance to victimized women in accordance with law.

Article 47 Women shall enjoy equal rights with their spouses to possess, use, benefit from, and dispose of the property jointly owned by the husband and wife as stipulated by law, regardless of the income status of both parties.

Where the husband and wife have agreed in writing that property acquired during the marriage shall be owned separately, and the wife has undertaken more obligations such as raising children, caring for the elderly, or assisting the husband in his work, she has the right to request compensation from the husband at the time of divorce.

Article 48 Where a house is jointly owned by the husband and wife, the division of housing at the time of divorce shall be resolved by agreement between the parties; if an agreement cannot be reached, the people's court shall make a judgment based on the specific circumstances of both parties, in accordance with the principle of favoring the children and the rights of the wife. This does not apply if the husband and wife have otherwise agreed.

Where a house is jointly rented by the husband and wife, the housing issue for the wife at the time of divorce shall be resolved in accordance with the principle of favoring the children and the rights of the wife.

Article 49 Both parents shall have equal guardianship rights over their minor children.

If the father dies, loses capacity for civil conduct, or is otherwise unable to serve as the guardian of the minor child, no one may interfere with the mother's guardianship rights.

Article 50 At the time of divorce, if the wife has lost the ability to bear children due to sterilization surgery or other reasons, when handling the issue of child custody, the wife's reasonable requests shall be accommodated in a manner favorable to the interests of the child.

Article 51 Women shall have the right to bear children in accordance with relevant state regulations, as well as the freedom not to bear children.

Both spouses of childbearing age shall practice family planning in accordance with relevant state regulations. Relevant departments shall provide safe and effective contraceptives and techniques, and ensure the health and safety of women undergoing sterilization surgery.

The State shall implement a system of pre-marital health care and maternal health care, and develop maternal and infant health care services. People's governments at all levels shall take measures to ensure that women have access to family planning technical services and improve women's reproductive health levels.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 52 Where a woman's legitimate rights and interests are infringed upon, she has the right to request the relevant department to handle the matter in accordance with law, or to apply for arbitration to an arbitration institution in accordance with law, or to file a lawsuit with a people's court.

For women in financial difficulty who need legal aid or judicial assistance, the local legal aid institution or people's court shall provide assistance and provide them with legal aid or judicial assistance in accordance with law.

Article 53 Where a woman's legitimate rights and interests are infringed upon, she may file a complaint with a women's organization. The women's organization shall safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of the infringed woman and have the right to request and assist the relevant department or unit to investigate and deal with the matter. The relevant department or unit shall investigate and deal with the matter in accordance with law and provide a response.

Article 54 Women's organizations shall provide support to victimized women who need help in filing lawsuits.

The Women's Federation or relevant women's organizations may expose and criticize acts that infringe upon the interests of specific groups of women through mass media, and have the right to request relevant departments to investigate and deal with the matter in accordance with law.

Article 55 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a woman's various rights and interests in a rural collective economic organization are infringed upon on the grounds of being unmarried, married, divorced, or widowed, or where, due to a man settling in the woman's household because of marriage, the equal rights and interests of the man and his children with members of the local rural collective economic organization are infringed upon, the township people's government shall mediate in accordance with law; the victim may also apply for arbitration to a rural land contract arbitration institution in accordance with law, or file a lawsuit with a people's court, and the people's court shall accept the case in accordance with law.

Article 56 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the legitimate rights and interests of women are infringed upon, and other laws or regulations provide for administrative penalties, those provisions shall apply; where property losses or other damages are caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with law; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with law.

Article 57 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a complaint, accusation, or report concerning the infringement of women's rights and interests is shirked, delayed, suppressed, or not investigated, or where retaliation is taken against the person who made the complaint, accusation, or report, the unit to which the person belongs, the competent department, or the superior organ shall order correction, and shall impose administrative sanctions on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with law.

Where a state organ and its staff fail to perform their duties in accordance with law, fail to promptly stop acts infringing upon women's rights and interests, or fail to provide necessary assistance to victimized women, resulting in serious consequences, the unit to which they belong or the superior organ shall impose administrative sanctions on the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with law.

Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, women's cultural and educational rights and interests, labor and social security rights and interests, personal and property rights and interests, and rights and interests in marriage and family are infringed upon, the unit to which the person belongs, the competent department, or the superior organ shall order correction; if the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible persons are state functionaries, the unit to which they belong or the superior organ shall impose administrative sanctions on them in accordance with law.

Article 58 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, sexual harassment or domestic violence is committed against a woman, constituting a violation of public security administration, the victim may request the public security organ to impose administrative penalties on the violator in accordance with law, or may file a civil lawsuit with a people's court in accordance with law.

Article 59 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, women's personality is belittled or damaged through mass media or other means, the departments of culture, radio, television, film, press and publication, or other relevant departments shall, within their respective functions and powers, order correction and impose administrative penalties in accordance with law.

## Chapter IX Supplementary Provisions

Article 60 The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate measures for implementation based on this Law.

The people's congresses of national autonomous areas may, in accordance with the principles stipulated in this Law and in light of the specific conditions of local ethnic women, formulate adaptive or supplementary provisions. The provisions of autonomous regions shall come into effect after being submitted to and approved by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress; the provisions of autonomous prefectures and autonomous counties shall come into effect after being submitted to and approved by the standing committees of the people's congresses of the relevant provinces, autonomous regions, or municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall be reported to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress for record.

Article 61 This Law shall come into effect as of October 1, 1992.
