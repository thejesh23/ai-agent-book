# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of the Rights and Interests of Returned Overseas Chinese and the Family Members of Overseas Chinese

Adopted at the 15th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 7, 1990

First Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of the Rights and Interests of Returned Overseas Chinese and the Family Members of Overseas Chinese" at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 31, 2000

Second Amendment in accordance with the "Decision on Amending Certain Laws" at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to protect the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese.

Article 2 "Returned overseas Chinese" refers to overseas Chinese who have returned to and settled in China. "Overseas Chinese" refers to Chinese citizens residing abroad.

"Family members of overseas Chinese" refers to the relatives of overseas Chinese or returned overseas Chinese who are living in China.

The term "family members of overseas Chinese" as used in this Law includes: the spouses, parents, children and their spouses, siblings, grandparents, maternal grandparents, grandchildren, maternal grandchildren, and other relatives of overseas Chinese or returned overseas Chinese who have a long-term relationship of support with them.

Article 3 Returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese enjoy the rights of citizens as stipulated by the Constitution and laws, and shall fulfill the duties of citizens as stipulated by the Constitution and laws. No organization or individual may discriminate against them.

The State shall, based on actual circumstances and the characteristics of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese, provide appropriate preferential treatment. Specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council or the competent departments under the State Council.

Article 4 The people's governments at or above the county level and their agencies responsible for overseas Chinese affairs shall organize and coordinate relevant departments to protect the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese.

Article 5 The State shall make arrangements for overseas Chinese who return to and settle in China.

Article 6 The National People's Congress and the local people's congresses in areas with a relatively large number of returned overseas Chinese shall have an appropriate number of deputies who are returned overseas Chinese.

Article 7 Returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese have the right to apply, in accordance with the law, to establish social organizations and to engage in lawful social activities that meet their needs.

The property of social organizations lawfully established by returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese shall be protected by law. No organization or individual may infringe upon it.

Article 8 The All-China Federation of Returned Overseas Chinese and local federations of returned overseas Chinese shall represent the interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese and safeguard their legitimate rights and interests in accordance with the law.

Article 9 The State shall support enterprises such as farms and forestry centers established to resettle returned overseas Chinese. No organization or individual may encroach upon the land lawfully used by such enterprises or infringe upon their legitimate rights and interests.

In areas where enterprises such as farms and forestry centers for resettling returned overseas Chinese are located, schools and medical and health care institutions may be reasonably established as needed. The State shall provide assistance in terms of personnel, equipment, and funding.

Article 10 The State shall safeguard the social security rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese who are employees, in accordance with the law. Employers and employees who are returned overseas Chinese or family members of overseas Chinese shall participate in local social insurance and pay social insurance premiums in accordance with the law.

For returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese who have lost the ability to work and have no source of income, or who are genuinely living in difficulty, the local people's government shall provide relief.

Article 11 The State encourages and guides returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese to invest in and establish industries in accordance with the law, especially high-tech enterprises. People's governments at all levels shall provide support, and their legitimate rights and interests shall be protected by law.

Article 12 When returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese establish public welfare undertakings in China, people's governments at all levels shall provide support, and their legitimate rights and interests shall be protected by law.

For materials donated by relatives and friends of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese residing abroad for use in domestic public welfare undertakings, customs duties and import value-added tax shall be reduced or exempted in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 13 The State shall protect the ownership of private houses owned by returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese in China, in accordance with the law.

If private houses owned by returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese are lawfully requisitioned, expropriated, or demolished, the construction unit shall provide reasonable compensation and proper resettlement in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 14 People's governments at all levels shall give preferential treatment to returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese in terms of employment and provide necessary guidance and services.

For returned overseas Chinese students, children of returned overseas Chinese, and children of overseas Chinese in China who are pursuing higher education, preferential treatment shall be given in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 15 The State shall protect the remittance income of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese.

Article 16 Returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese have the right to accept bequests or gifts from relatives and friends residing abroad.

The rights of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese to inherit property abroad shall be protected by law.

Returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese have the right to dispose of their property abroad.

Article 17 The correspondence and communication between returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese and their relatives and friends abroad shall be protected by law.

Article 18 When returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese apply to leave the country, the relevant competent departments shall process the procedures within the prescribed time limit.

If returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese urgently need to leave the country due to special circumstances such as the critical illness or death of a direct relative abroad, or the need to dispose of property abroad within a time limit, the relevant competent departments shall give priority to processing the procedures based on valid certificates provided by the applicant.

Article 19 The State guarantees the right of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese to leave the country for family visits.

Employees who are returned overseas Chinese or family members of overseas Chinese shall enjoy the benefits of leaving the country for family visits in accordance with relevant State regulations.

Article 20 Returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese may apply to leave the country for settlement in accordance with relevant State regulations. If approval is granted for leaving the country for settlement, no organization or individual may harm their legitimate rights and interests.

For employees who are returned overseas Chinese or family members of overseas Chinese who have retired, resigned, or are on leave, and who leave the country for settlement, their retirement pensions, resignation allowances, and old-age pensions shall continue to be paid.

Article 21 When returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese apply to go abroad for self-funded study or lectures, or for business purposes, their work units and relevant departments shall provide convenience.

Article 22 The State shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese abroad, in accordance with international treaties concluded or acceded to by the People's Republic of China, or international practices.

Article 23 When the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese are infringed upon, the infringed party has the right to request the relevant competent department to handle the matter in accordance with the law, or to file a lawsuit with a people's court. The Federation of Returned Overseas Chinese shall provide support and assistance.

Article 24 If a State functionary, through neglect of duty or abuse of power, causes damage to the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese, the unit to which the functionary belongs or the competent authority at a higher level shall order rectification or impose administrative sanctions; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 25 Any organization or individual that infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese, causing property loss or other damage, shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 26 If, in violation of the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 9 of this Law, land lawfully used by farms or forestry centers for resettling returned overseas Chinese is illegally occupied, the relevant competent department shall order its return; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 27 If, in violation of the provisions of Article 13 of this Law, private houses owned by returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese in China are illegally encroached upon, the relevant competent department shall order their return; if losses are caused, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 28 If, in violation of the provisions of the second paragraph of Article 20 of this Law, the retirement pensions, resignation allowances, or old-age pensions of returned overseas Chinese and the family members of overseas Chinese who have left the country for settlement are suspended, withheld, misappropriated, or embezzled, the relevant unit or competent department shall order their retroactive payment and provide compensation in accordance with the law; administrative sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 29 The State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law.

The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate implementing regulations based on this Law and the implementing regulations of the State Council.

Article 30 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1991.
