# Social Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 17th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on October 28, 2010

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Social Insurance Law of the People's Republic of China at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution with a view to regulating social insurance relationships, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of citizens to participate in social insurance and enjoy social insurance benefits, enabling citizens to share the fruits of development, and promoting social harmony and stability.

Article 2 The State establishes social insurance systems such as basic old-age insurance, basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, unemployment insurance, and maternity insurance to guarantee citizens' right to obtain material assistance from the State and society in accordance with the law under circumstances such as old age, illness, work-related injury, unemployment, and childbirth.

Article 3 The social insurance system adheres to the principles of broad coverage, basic protection, multi-level structure, and sustainability. The level of social insurance shall be commensurate with the level of economic and social development.

Article 4 Employers and individuals within the territory of the People's Republic of China shall pay social insurance premiums in accordance with the law, and have the right to inquire about contribution records and individual rights records, and to request social insurance agencies to provide social insurance consultation and related services.

Individuals enjoy social insurance benefits in accordance with the law and have the right to supervise their employer's contribution on their behalf.

Article 5 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate social insurance undertakings into their national economic and social development plans.

The State raises social insurance funds through multiple channels. People's governments at or above the county level shall provide necessary financial support for social insurance undertakings.

The State supports social insurance undertakings through tax preferential policies.

Article 6 The State exercises strict supervision over social insurance funds.

The State Council and the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall establish and improve a supervision and management system for social insurance funds to ensure the safety and effective operation of social insurance funds.

People's governments at or above the county level shall take measures to encourage and support the participation of all sectors of society in the supervision of social insurance funds.

Article 7 The social insurance administrative department of the State Council is responsible for the national social insurance management work. Other relevant departments of the State Council are responsible for related social insurance work within their respective scopes of duties.

The social insurance administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for the social insurance management work within their respective administrative regions. Other relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level are responsible for related social insurance work within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 8 Social insurance agencies provide social insurance services and are responsible for social insurance registration, recording of individual rights, payment of social insurance benefits, and other tasks.

Article 9 Trade unions shall safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of employees in accordance with the law, have the right to participate in the study of major social insurance matters, participate in social insurance supervision committees, and supervise matters related to employees' social insurance rights and interests.

## Chapter II Basic Old-Age Insurance

Article 10 Employees shall participate in basic old-age insurance, with the employer and the employee jointly paying the basic old-age insurance premiums.

Individual industrial and commercial households without employees, part-time employees who do not participate in basic old-age insurance through an employer, and other flexible employment personnel may participate in basic old-age insurance, with the individual paying the basic old-age insurance premiums.

The measures for old-age insurance for civil servants and personnel managed in accordance with the Civil Servant Law shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 11 Basic old-age insurance combines social pooling with individual accounts.

The basic old-age insurance fund consists of contributions from employers and individuals, as well as government subsidies.

Article 12 Employers shall pay basic old-age insurance premiums at a proportion of the total wages of their employees as prescribed by the State, which shall be credited to the basic old-age insurance pooling fund.

Employees shall pay basic old-age insurance premiums at a proportion of their own wages as prescribed by the State, which shall be credited to their individual accounts.

Individual industrial and commercial households without employees, part-time employees who do not participate in basic old-age insurance through an employer, and other flexible employment personnel who participate in basic old-age insurance shall pay basic old-age insurance premiums in accordance with State regulations, which shall be credited respectively to the basic old-age insurance pooling fund and individual accounts.

Article 13 For employees of State-owned enterprises and public institutions who participated in basic old-age insurance before its implementation, the basic old-age insurance premiums payable during the deemed contribution period shall be borne by the government.

When the basic old-age insurance fund has insufficient funds to pay benefits, the government shall provide subsidies.

Article 14 Individual accounts shall not be withdrawn in advance. The crediting interest rate shall not be lower than the bank fixed deposit interest rate for the same period, and interest income is exempt from tax. If an individual dies, the balance of the individual account may be inherited.

Article 15 Basic pension consists of a pooled pension and an individual account pension.

The basic pension is determined based on factors such as the individual's cumulative contribution years, contribution wages, the average wage of local employees, the amount in the individual account, and the average life expectancy of the urban population.

Article 16 Individuals who have participated in basic old-age insurance and have made cumulative contributions for fifteen years upon reaching the statutory retirement age shall receive a basic pension on a monthly basis.

Individuals who have participated in basic old-age insurance but have made cumulative contributions for less than fifteen years upon reaching the statutory retirement age may continue contributing until they reach fifteen years to receive a monthly basic pension, or they may transfer to the new rural social old-age insurance or the urban resident social old-age insurance to enjoy corresponding old-age insurance benefits in accordance with State Council regulations.

Article 17 If an individual who has participated in basic old-age insurance dies due to illness or non-work-related reasons, their survivors may receive funeral subsidies and pensions. If an individual becomes completely incapacitated due to illness or non-work-related disability before reaching the statutory retirement age, they may receive sickness and disability allowances. The required funds shall be paid from the basic old-age insurance fund.

Article 18 The State establishes a normal adjustment mechanism for basic pensions. The level of basic old-age insurance benefits shall be raised in a timely manner based on the increase in the average wage of employees and changes in consumer prices.

Article 19 When an individual works across different pooling regions, their basic old-age insurance relationship shall be transferred with them, and their contribution years shall be calculated cumulatively. When the individual reaches the statutory retirement age, the basic pension shall be calculated in segments and paid uniformly. Specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 20 The State establishes and improves the new rural social old-age insurance system.

The new rural social old-age insurance combines individual contributions, collective subsidies, and government subsidies.

Article 21 New rural social old-age insurance benefits consist of a basic pension and an individual account pension.

Rural residents who participate in the new rural social old-age insurance and meet the conditions prescribed by the State shall receive new rural social old-age insurance benefits on a monthly basis.

Article 22 The State establishes and improves the urban resident social old-age insurance system.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on actual conditions, implement the urban resident social old-age insurance and the new rural social old-age insurance in a consolidated manner.

## Chapter III Basic Medical Insurance

Article 23 Employees shall participate in the basic medical insurance for employees, with the employer and the employee jointly paying the basic medical insurance premiums in accordance with State regulations.

Individual industrial and commercial households without employees, part-time employees who do not participate in the basic medical insurance for employees through an employer, and other flexible employment personnel may participate in the basic medical insurance for employees, with the individual paying the basic medical insurance premiums in accordance with State regulations.

Article 24 The State establishes and improves the new rural cooperative medical system.

The administrative measures for the new rural cooperative medical system shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 25 The State establishes and improves the basic medical insurance system for urban residents.

The basic medical insurance for urban residents combines individual contributions with government subsidies.

For individuals receiving minimum subsistence allowances, disabled persons who have lost the ability to work, elderly persons aged 60 or above from low-income families, and minors, the portion of individual contributions required shall be subsidized by the government.

Article 26 The benefit standards for the basic medical insurance for employees, the new rural cooperative medical system, and the basic medical insurance for urban residents shall be implemented in accordance with State regulations.

Article 27 Individuals who have participated in the basic medical insurance for employees and have made cumulative contributions reaching the number of years prescribed by the State upon reaching the statutory retirement age shall no longer pay basic medical insurance premiums after retirement and shall enjoy basic medical insurance benefits in accordance with State regulations. If the number of years prescribed by the State has not been reached, they may continue contributing until they reach that number of years.

Article 28 Medical expenses that comply with the basic medical insurance drug catalog, diagnostic and treatment items, and medical service facility standards, as well as expenses for emergency and rescue treatment, shall be paid from the basic medical insurance fund in accordance with State regulations.

Article 29 The portion of medical expenses of insured persons that should be paid by the basic medical insurance fund shall be settled directly between the social insurance agency and the medical institution or pharmaceutical business.

The social insurance administrative department and the health administrative department shall establish a settlement system for medical expenses incurred outside the insured person's place of residence to facilitate insured persons' access to basic medical insurance benefits.

Article 30 The following medical expenses shall not be covered by the basic medical insurance fund:

(1) Expenses that should be paid from the work-related injury insurance fund;
(2) Expenses that should be borne by a third party;
(3) Expenses that should be borne by public health;
(4) Expenses for medical treatment abroad.

If medical expenses should legally be borne by a third party, but the third party fails to pay or cannot be identified, the basic medical insurance fund shall make the payment in advance. After making the advance payment, the basic medical insurance fund has the right to recover the amount from the third party.

Article 31 Social insurance agencies may, based on the needs of management and services, sign service agreements with medical institutions and pharmaceutical businesses to regulate medical service behavior.

Medical institutions shall provide reasonable and necessary medical services to insured persons.

Article 32 When an individual works across different pooling regions, their basic medical insurance relationship shall be transferred with them, and their contribution years shall be calculated cumulatively.

## Chapter IV Work-Related Injury Insurance

Article 33 Employees shall participate in work-related injury insurance. The employer shall pay the work-related injury insurance premiums, and employees shall not pay work-related injury insurance premiums.

Article 34 The State determines differentiated industry rates based on the degree of work-related injury risk in different industries, and determines rate levels within each industry based on factors such as the use of work-related injury insurance funds and the incidence of work-related injuries. The differentiated industry rates and intra-industry rate levels shall be formulated by the social insurance administrative department of the State Council and promulgated for implementation after approval by the State Council.

Social insurance agencies shall determine the contribution rate for an employer based on factors such as the employer's use of work-related injury insurance funds, the incidence of work-related injuries, and the rate level of the industry to which it belongs.

Article 35 Employers shall pay work-related injury insurance premiums based on the total wages of their employees and the rate determined by the social insurance agency.

Article 36 Employees who suffer accidental injuries or contract occupational diseases due to work-related reasons and are recognized as having a work-related injury shall enjoy work-related injury insurance benefits. If they lose the ability to work as determined by a labor ability appraisal, they shall enjoy disability benefits.

The recognition of work-related injuries and labor ability appraisals shall be simple and convenient.

Article 37 Injuries or deaths of employees at work caused by any of the following circumstances shall not be recognized as work-related injuries:

(1) Intentional crime;
(2) Drunkenness or drug use;
(3) Self-harm or suicide;
(4) Other circumstances as provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 38 The following expenses incurred due to work-related injuries shall be paid from the work-related injury insurance fund in accordance with State regulations:

(1) Medical expenses and rehabilitation expenses for treating the work-related injury;
(2) Hospital meal subsidies;
(3) Transportation, accommodation, and meal expenses for seeking medical treatment outside the pooling region;
(4) Expenses for fitting and configuring assistive devices for the disability;
(5) Living care expenses confirmed by the labor ability appraisal committee for those who cannot take care of themselves;
(6) One-time disability subsidies and monthly disability allowances for employees with Grade 1 to 4 disabilities;
(7) One-time medical subsidy payable upon termination or dissolution of the labor contract;
(8) Funeral subsidies, pensions for dependents, and death benefits for work-related deaths payable to the survivors;
(9) Labor ability appraisal fees.

Article 39 The following expenses incurred due to work-related injuries shall be paid by the employer in accordance with State regulations:

(1) Wages and benefits during the period of treatment for the work-related injury;
(2) Monthly disability allowances for employees with Grade 5 or 6 disabilities;
(3) One-time disability employment subsidy payable upon termination or dissolution of the labor contract.

Article 40 When an employee with a work-related injury meets the conditions for receiving a basic pension, the disability allowance shall cease, and the employee shall enjoy basic old-age insurance benefits. If the basic old-age insurance benefits are lower than the disability allowance, the difference shall be made up from the work-related injury insurance fund.

Article 41 If the employer of an employee fails to pay work-related injury insurance premiums in accordance with the law and a work-related injury accident occurs, the employer shall pay the work-related injury insurance benefits. If the employer fails to pay, the benefits shall be paid in advance from the work-related injury insurance fund.

The work-related injury insurance benefits paid in advance from the work-related injury insurance fund shall be reimbursed by the employer. If the employer fails to reimburse, the social insurance agency may recover the amount in accordance with Article 63 of this Law.

Article 42 If a work-related injury is caused by a third party, and the third party fails to pay the medical expenses for the work-related injury or cannot be identified, the work-related injury insurance fund shall make the payment in advance. After making the advance payment, the work-related injury insurance fund has the right to recover the amount from the third party.

Article 43 An employee with a work-related injury shall cease to enjoy work-related injury insurance benefits under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Losing the conditions for enjoying the benefits;
(2) Refusing to undergo a labor ability appraisal;
(3) Refusing treatment.

## Chapter V Unemployment Insurance

Article 44 Employees shall participate in unemployment insurance, with the employer and the employee jointly paying the unemployment insurance premiums in accordance with State regulations.

Article 45 Unemployed persons who meet the following conditions may receive unemployment insurance benefits from the unemployment insurance fund:

(1) The employer and the individual have paid unemployment insurance premiums for at least one year before unemployment;
(2) The interruption of employment is not due to the individual's own will;
(3) Have registered as unemployed and are seeking employment.

Article 46 If the cumulative contribution period of the employer and the individual before unemployment is at least one year but less than five years, the maximum period for receiving unemployment insurance benefits is twelve months; if the cumulative contribution period is at least five years but less than ten years, the maximum period is eighteen months; if the cumulative contribution period is ten years or more, the maximum period is twenty-four months. Upon re-employment and subsequent re-unemployment, the contribution period shall be recalculated, and the period for receiving unemployment insurance benefits shall be combined with the period of benefits that were due but not yet received from the previous unemployment, with the total not exceeding twenty-four months.

Article 47 The standard for unemployment insurance benefits shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and shall not be lower than the minimum subsistence standard for urban residents.

Article 48 During the period of receiving unemployment insurance benefits, unemployed persons shall participate in the basic medical insurance for employees and enjoy basic medical insurance benefits.

The basic medical insurance premiums payable by unemployed persons shall be paid from the unemployment insurance fund, and individuals shall not pay basic medical insurance premiums.

Article 49 If an unemployed person dies during the period of receiving unemployment insurance benefits, a one-time funeral subsidy and pension shall be paid to their survivors with reference to the regulations on the death of in-service employees in the locality. The required funds shall be paid from the unemployment insurance fund.

If an individual's death simultaneously meets the conditions for receiving funeral subsidies from basic old-age insurance, work-related injury insurance, and unemployment insurance, their survivors may only choose to receive one of them.

Article 50 The employer shall promptly issue a certificate of termination or dissolution of the labor relationship to the unemployed person and inform the social insurance agency of the list of unemployed persons within fifteen days from the date of termination or dissolution of the labor relationship.

The unemployed person shall promptly go to the designated public employment service institution to register as unemployed with the certificate of termination or dissolution of the labor relationship issued by their employer.

The unemployed person shall go through the procedures for receiving unemployment insurance benefits at the social insurance agency with their unemployment registration certificate and personal identification document. The period for receiving unemployment insurance benefits shall be calculated from the date of unemployment registration.

Article 51 An unemployed person shall cease to receive unemployment insurance benefits and simultaneously cease to enjoy other unemployment insurance benefits under any of the following circumstances during the period of receiving unemployment insurance benefits:

(1) Re-employment;
(2) Conscription for military service;
(3) Emigration;
(4) Enjoyment of basic old-age insurance benefits;
(5) Without justifiable reason, refusing to accept suitable work introduced or training provided by the designated department or agency of the local people's government.

Article 52 When an employee works across different pooling regions, their unemployment insurance relationship shall be transferred with them, and their contribution years shall be calculated cumulatively.

## Chapter VI Maternity Insurance

Article 53 Employees shall participate in maternity insurance. The employer shall pay the maternity insurance premiums in accordance with State regulations, and employees shall not pay maternity insurance premiums.

Article 54 If an employer has paid maternity insurance premiums, its employees shall enjoy maternity insurance benefits. The unemployed spouse of an employee shall enjoy maternity medical expense benefits in accordance with State regulations. The required funds shall be paid from the maternity insurance fund.

Maternity insurance benefits include maternity medical expenses and maternity allowances.

Article 55 Maternity medical expenses include the following items:

(1) Medical expenses for childbirth;
(2) Medical expenses for family planning;
(3) Other project expenses as provided by laws and regulations.

Article 56 Employees may enjoy maternity allowances in accordance with State regulations under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Female employees taking maternity leave for childbirth;
(2) Taking leave for family planning surgery;
(3) Other circumstances as provided by laws and regulations.

Maternity allowances shall be calculated and paid based on the average monthly wage of the employees of the employer in the previous year.

## Chapter VII Collection and Payment of Social Insurance Premiums

Article 57 An employer shall, within thirty days from the date of its establishment, apply to the local social insurance agency for social insurance registration with its business license, registration certificate, or company seal. The social insurance agency shall review the application within fifteen days from the date of receipt and issue a social insurance registration certificate.

If the registered items of an employer's social insurance registration change or the employer is legally terminated, the employer shall, within thirty days from the date of change or termination, go to the social insurance agency to handle the modification or cancellation of the social insurance registration.

Market supervision and administration departments, civil affairs departments, and institutional organization management authorities shall promptly inform social insurance agencies of the establishment and termination of employers. Public security organs shall promptly inform social insurance agencies of the birth, death, household registration, migration, and cancellation of individuals.

Article 58 An employer shall, within thirty days from the date of employment, apply to the social insurance agency for social insurance registration for its employees. If social insurance registration is not handled, the social insurance agency shall determine the social insurance premiums payable by the employer.Self-employed individuals without employees, part-time workers not enrolled in social insurance by their employers, and other flexible workers who voluntarily participate in social insurance shall apply to the social insurance agency for social insurance registration.

The state establishes a unified national personal social security number. The personal social security number is the citizen's identity number.

Article 59: People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the collection of social insurance premiums.

Social insurance premiums shall be collected uniformly, and the implementation steps and specific measures shall be prescribed by the State Council.

Article 60: Employers shall declare on their own and pay social insurance premiums in full and on time, and shall not defer or reduce payment except for force majeure or other statutory reasons. Social insurance premiums payable by employees shall be withheld and paid by the employer, and the employer shall inform the employee monthly of the details of the social insurance premiums paid.

Self-employed individuals without employees, part-time workers not enrolled in social insurance by their employers, and other flexible workers may directly pay social insurance premiums to the social insurance premium collection agency.

Article 61: The social insurance premium collection agency shall collect social insurance premiums in full and on time in accordance with the law, and regularly inform employers and individuals of the payment status.

Article 62: If an employer fails to declare the amount of social insurance premiums payable as required, the amount payable shall be determined as 110% of the employer's previous month's payment; after the employer completes the declaration procedures, the social insurance premium collection agency shall settle the amount in accordance with regulations.

Article 63: If an employer fails to pay social insurance premiums in full and on time, the social insurance premium collection agency shall order it to pay or make up the amount within a specified period.

If the employer still fails to pay or make up the social insurance premiums after the specified period, the social insurance premium collection agency may inquire about its deposit accounts with banks and other financial institutions; and may apply to the relevant administrative department at or above the county level for a decision to transfer the social insurance premiums, and notify its account-opening bank or other financial institution in writing to transfer the social insurance premiums. If the employer's account balance is less than the social insurance premiums payable, the social insurance premium collection agency may require the employer to provide a guarantee and sign a deferred payment agreement.

If the employer fails to pay social insurance premiums in full and does not provide a guarantee, the social insurance premium collection agency may apply to the People's Court for seizure, attachment, and auction of property equivalent to the value of the social insurance premiums payable, and use the auction proceeds to offset the social insurance premiums.

## Chapter 8: Social Insurance Funds

Article 64: Social insurance funds include the basic pension insurance fund, basic medical insurance fund, work-related injury insurance fund, unemployment insurance fund, and maternity insurance fund. Except for the basic medical insurance fund and the maternity insurance fund, which are combined in account establishment and accounting, other social insurance funds are established and accounted for separately according to the type of social insurance. Social insurance funds shall implement the unified national accounting system.

Social insurance funds are earmarked for specific purposes and shall not be encroached upon or misappropriated by any organization or individual.

The basic pension insurance fund shall gradually achieve national pooling, while other social insurance funds shall gradually achieve provincial pooling, with the specific timing and steps prescribed by the State Council.

Article 65: Social insurance funds shall achieve a balance of revenue and expenditure through budgeting.

People's governments at or above the county level shall provide subsidies when social insurance funds are insufficient for payment.

Article 66: Social insurance funds shall have budgets established according to the pooling level. Except for the combined budget preparation of the basic medical insurance fund and the maternity insurance fund, budgets for other social insurance funds shall be prepared separately according to the social insurance item.

Article 67: The preparation, review, and approval of draft budgets and final accounts for social insurance funds shall be carried out in accordance with the law and regulations of the State Council.

Article 68: Social insurance funds shall be deposited into fiscal accounts, with specific management measures prescribed by the State Council.

Article 69: On the premise of ensuring safety, social insurance funds shall be invested and operated in accordance with State Council regulations to achieve preservation and appreciation.

Social insurance funds shall not be invested or operated in violation of regulations, used to balance other government budgets, used for constructing or renovating office premises, paying personnel expenses, operating costs, management fees, or misappropriated for other purposes in violation of laws and administrative regulations.

Article 70: Social insurance agencies shall regularly disclose to the public information on social insurance participation and the income, expenditure, balance, and earnings of social insurance funds.

Article 71: The state establishes a National Social Security Fund, composed of allocations from the central fiscal budget and funds raised through other methods approved by the State Council, to supplement and adjust social security expenditures. The National Social Security Fund shall be managed and operated by the National Social Security Fund management and operation institution, achieving preservation and appreciation on the premise of ensuring safety.

The National Social Security Fund shall regularly disclose to the public its income, expenditure, management, and investment operations. The financial department of the State Council, the social insurance administrative department, and the audit authority shall supervise the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of the National Social Security Fund.

## Chapter 9: Social Insurance Administration

Article 72: Social insurance agencies shall be established in pooling areas. Social insurance agencies may, with the approval of the local social insurance administrative department and the institutional establishment management authority, set up branches and service outlets within their pooling area as needed.

Personnel expenses, basic operating costs, and management fees incurred by social insurance agencies in carrying out social insurance work shall be guaranteed by the government at the same level in accordance with state regulations.

Article 73: Social insurance agencies shall establish and improve business, financial, security, and risk management systems.

Social insurance agencies shall pay social insurance benefits in full and on time.

Article 74: Social insurance agencies shall obtain data required for social insurance work through business operations, statistics, and surveys, and relevant entities and individuals shall provide it promptly and truthfully.

Social insurance agencies shall promptly establish files for employers, completely and accurately record social insurance data such as participants and contributions, and properly preserve original vouchers for registration and declaration, as well as accounting vouchers for payment and settlement.

Social insurance agencies shall promptly, completely, and accurately record personal rights records, including individual contributions and employer contributions, as well as social insurance benefits received, and regularly send personal rights record statements to individuals free of charge.

Employers and individuals may inquire and verify their contribution and social insurance benefit records free of charge from social insurance agencies, and request social insurance agencies to provide social insurance consultation and related services.

Article 75: The national social insurance information system shall be jointly built by people's governments at or above the county level in accordance with the unified national plan and the principle of graded responsibility.

## Chapter 10: Social Insurance Supervision

Article 76: The standing committees of people's congresses at all levels shall hear and deliberate special work reports from the people's government at the same level on the income, expenditure, management, investment operations, and supervision of social insurance funds, organize law enforcement inspections on the implementation of this law, and exercise supervisory powers in accordance with the law.

Article 77: The social insurance administrative department of the people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and inspection of compliance with social insurance laws and regulations by employers and individuals.

When the social insurance administrative department conducts supervision and inspection, the inspected employer and individual shall truthfully provide materials related to social insurance and shall not refuse inspection or make false reports or concealments.

Article 78: The financial department and the audit authority shall, within their respective responsibilities, supervise the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds.

Article 79: The social insurance administrative department shall supervise and inspect the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds. If problems are found, it shall propose corrective suggestions, make a handling decision in accordance with the law, or propose handling suggestions to the relevant administrative department. The results of social insurance fund inspections shall be regularly disclosed to the public.

When the social insurance administrative department conducts supervision and inspection of social insurance funds, it has the authority to take the following measures:

(1) Inspect, record, and copy materials related to the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds, and seal up materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed;

(2) Inquire about entities and individuals related to the investigation, requiring them to explain issues related to the investigation and provide relevant evidentiary materials;

(3) Stop and order the correction of acts of concealing, transferring, encroaching upon, or misappropriating social insurance funds.

Article 80: The people's government of a pooling area shall establish a social insurance supervision committee composed of employer representatives, insured representatives, trade union representatives, experts, etc., to understand and analyze the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds, provide consulting opinions and suggestions on social insurance work, and implement social supervision.

Social insurance agencies shall regularly report to the social insurance supervision committee on the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds. The social insurance supervision committee may engage accounting firms to conduct annual audits and special audits of the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds. The audit results shall be disclosed to the public.

If the social insurance supervision committee finds problems in the income, expenditure, management, and investment operations of social insurance funds, it has the right to propose corrective suggestions; for illegal acts by social insurance agencies and their staff, it has the right to propose handling suggestions to the relevant department in accordance with the law.

Article 81: The social insurance administrative department and other relevant administrative departments, social insurance agencies, social insurance premium collection agencies, and their staff shall keep confidential the information of employers and individuals in accordance with the law and shall not disclose it in any form.

Article 82: Any organization or individual has the right to report or complain about acts that violate social insurance laws and regulations.

The social insurance administrative department, health administrative department, social insurance agency, social insurance premium collection agency, financial department, and audit authority shall handle reports and complaints within their respective responsibilities in accordance with the law; for those not within their responsibilities, they shall notify in writing and transfer them to the department or agency with authority to handle them. The department or agency with authority shall handle them promptly and shall not shirk responsibility.

Article 83: If an employer or individual believes that an act of the social insurance premium collection agency infringes upon their legitimate rights and interests, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

If an employer or individual believes that a social insurance agency fails to handle social insurance registration, assess social insurance premiums, pay social insurance benefits, handle social insurance transfer and continuation procedures in accordance with the law, or infringes upon other social insurance rights and interests, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit in accordance with the law.

If an individual has a social insurance dispute with their employer, they may apply for mediation, arbitration, or file a lawsuit in accordance with the law. If the employer infringes upon the individual's social insurance rights and interests, the individual may also request the social insurance administrative department or the social insurance premium collection agency to handle it in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 11: Legal Liability

Article 84: If an employer fails to handle social insurance registration, the social insurance administrative department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, the employer shall be fined an amount between one and three times the social insurance premiums payable, and the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined between 500 and 3,000 yuan.

Article 85: If an employer refuses to issue a certificate of termination or dissolution of the labor relationship, it shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of the Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 86: If an employer fails to pay social insurance premiums in full and on time, the social insurance premium collection agency shall order payment or make-up within a specified period, and a late payment fee of 0.05% per day shall be charged from the date of arrears; if payment is still not made within the specified period, the relevant administrative department shall impose a fine of between one and three times the amount in arrears.

Article 87: If a social insurance agency, medical institution, pharmaceutical business unit, or other social insurance service institution fraudulently obtains social insurance fund expenditures through fraud, falsification of evidentiary materials, or other means, the social insurance administrative department shall order the return of the fraudulently obtained social insurance funds and impose a fine of between two and five times the amount fraudulently obtained; if it is a social insurance service institution, the service agreement shall be terminated; if the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel have professional qualifications, their professional qualifications shall be revoked in accordance with the law.

Article 88: If social insurance benefits are fraudulently obtained through fraud, falsification of evidentiary materials, or other means, the social insurance administrative department shall order the return of the fraudulently obtained social insurance funds and impose a fine of between two and five times the amount fraudulently obtained.

Article 89: If a social insurance agency or its staff commits any of the following acts, the social insurance administrative department shall order correction; if losses are caused to the social insurance fund, employer, or individual, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law:

(1) Failing to perform statutory social insurance duties;

(2) Failing to deposit social insurance funds into fiscal accounts;

(3) Withholding or refusing to pay social insurance benefits on time;

(4) Losing or altering contribution records, social insurance benefit records, or other social insurance data or personal rights records;

(5) Other acts violating social insurance laws and regulations.

Article 90: If a social insurance premium collection agency arbitrarily changes the contribution base or rate of social insurance premiums, resulting in under-collection or over-collection of social insurance premiums, the relevant administrative department shall order it to recover the social insurance premiums that should have been paid or refund the social insurance premiums that should not have been paid; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 91: If, in violation of this law, social insurance funds are concealed, transferred, encroached upon, misappropriated, or invested or operated in violation of regulations, the social insurance administrative department, financial department, or audit authority shall order recovery; if there is illegal income, it shall be confiscated; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 92: If the social insurance administrative department and other relevant administrative departments, social insurance agencies, social insurance premium collection agencies, and their staff disclose employer or individual information, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if losses are caused to the employer or individual, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 93: If state functionaries abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain in social insurance management or supervision, they shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 94: If a violation of this law constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 12: Supplementary Provisions

Article 95: Rural residents who work in cities shall participate in social insurance in accordance with the provisions of this law.

Article 96: When expropriating land collectively owned by rural areas, social insurance premiums for the farmers whose land is expropriated shall be arranged in full, and they shall be included in the corresponding social insurance system in accordance with State Council regulations.

Article 97: Foreigners employed within the territory of China shall participate in social insurance by reference to the provisions of this law.

Article 98: This law shall take effect on July 1, 2011.
