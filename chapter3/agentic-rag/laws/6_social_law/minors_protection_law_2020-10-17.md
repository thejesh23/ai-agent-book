# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Minors

Adopted at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on September 4, 1991

First Revision at the 25th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2006

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Minors at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

Second Revision at the 22nd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on October 17, 2020

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to protect the physical and mental health of minors, safeguard their lawful rights and interests, promote their comprehensive development in morality, intelligence, physical fitness, aesthetics, and labor, cultivate socialist builders and successors with ideals, moral integrity, knowledge, and a sense of discipline, and nurture a new generation capable of undertaking the great task of national rejuvenation.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "minors" refer to citizens under the age of eighteen.

Article 3 The State guarantees the rights of minors to survival, development, protection, and participation.

Minors equally enjoy all rights in accordance with the law and shall not be discriminated against on the grounds of their own or their parents' or other guardians' ethnicity, race, gender, household registration, occupation, religious belief, education level, family status, or physical or mental health condition.

Article 4 The protection of minors shall adhere to the principle of acting in the best interests of the minor. Matters involving minors shall be handled in accordance with the following requirements:

(1) Granting special and priority protection to minors;
(2) Respecting the personal dignity of minors;
(3) Protecting the privacy and personal information of minors;
(4) Adapting to the laws and characteristics of the physical and mental development of minors;
(5) Listening to the opinions of minors;
(6) Combining protection with education.

Article 5 The State, society, schools, and families shall provide minors with education on ideals, morality, science, culture, the rule of law, national security, health, and labor; strengthen education on patriotism, collectivism, and socialism with Chinese characteristics; cultivate public virtues of loving the motherland, the people, labor, science, and socialism; resist the erosion of capitalist, feudal, and other decadent ideologies; and guide minors to establish and practice the core socialist values.

Article 6 The protection of minors is a shared responsibility of state organs, armed forces, political parties, people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, urban and rural grassroots self-governing organizations, guardians of minors, and other adults.

The State, society, schools, and families shall educate and assist minors in safeguarding their own lawful rights and interests and in enhancing their awareness and ability of self-protection.

Article 7 The parents or other guardians of minors shall assume guardianship responsibilities for minors in accordance with the law.

The State shall take measures to guide, support, assist, and supervise the parents or other guardians of minors in fulfilling their guardianship duties.

Article 8 People's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate the protection of minors into their national economic and social development plans, with relevant funds included in their fiscal budgets.

Article 9 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish coordination mechanisms for the protection of minors to coordinate, harmonize, supervise, and guide relevant departments in fulfilling their duties within their respective scopes. The specific work of the coordination mechanism shall be undertaken by the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level; provincial people's governments may, based on local conditions, designate other departments to undertake this work.

Article 10 The Communist Youth League, Women's Federation, Trade Unions, Disabled Persons' Federation, Committee for the Care of the Next Generation, Youth Federation, Student Federation, Young Pioneers, and other people's organizations and relevant social organizations shall assist people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, people's procuratorates, and people's courts in the protection of minors and in safeguarding their lawful rights and interests.

Article 11 Any organization or individual that discovers circumstances detrimental to the physical or mental health of minors or infringing upon their lawful rights and interests has the right to dissuade, stop, or report and accuse such acts to the public security, civil affairs, education, or other relevant departments.

State organs, residents' committees, villagers' committees, units in close contact with minors, and their staff members, upon discovering in the course of their work that a minor's physical or mental health is being harmed, is suspected of being harmed, or is facing other dangerous situations, shall immediately report to the public security, civil affairs, education, or other relevant departments.

Upon receiving reports, accusations, or complaints involving minors, the relevant departments shall promptly accept and handle them in accordance with the law and inform the relevant units and individuals of the outcome in an appropriate manner.

Article 12 The State encourages and supports scientific research on the protection of minors, establishes relevant disciplines and specialties, and strengthens personnel training.

Article 13 The State shall establish and improve a statistical survey system for minors, conduct statistics, surveys, and analyses on the health, education, and other conditions of minors, and release relevant information on the protection of minors.

Article 14 The State shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have achieved remarkable results in the protection of minors.

## Chapter II: Family Protection

Article 15 The parents or other guardians of minors shall learn family education knowledge, accept family education guidance, and create a good, harmonious, and civilized family environment.

Other adult family members living together shall assist the parents or other guardians of minors in raising, educating, and protecting the minors.

Article 16 The parents or other guardians of minors shall fulfill the following guardianship duties:

(1) Providing for the living, health, and safety of minors;
(2) Paying attention to the physical and psychological conditions and emotional needs of minors;
(3) Educating and guiding minors to abide by laws and regulations, be diligent and thrifty, and develop good moral character and behavioral habits;
(4) Providing safety education to minors to enhance their self-protection awareness and ability;
(5) Respecting the right of minors to education and ensuring that school-age minors receive and complete compulsory education in accordance with the law;
(6) Ensuring that minors have time for rest, recreation, and physical exercise, and guiding them to engage in activities beneficial to their physical and mental health;
(7) Properly managing and protecting the property of minors;
(8) Legally representing minors in civil legal acts;
(9) Preventing and stopping minors' bad behavior and illegal and criminal acts, and providing reasonable discipline;
(10) Other guardianship duties that should be fulfilled.

Article 17 The parents or other guardians of minors shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Abusing, abandoning, or illegally placing minors for adoption, or committing domestic violence against minors;
(2) Indulging, instigating, or using minors to commit illegal or criminal acts;
(3) Indulging or inciting minors to participate in cults, superstitious activities, or to be subjected to terrorism, separatism, extremism, or other harmful influences;
(4) Indulging or inciting minors to smoke (including e-cigarettes, the same below), drink alcohol, gamble, beg, or bully others;
(5) Indulging or forcing minors who should receive compulsory education to be absent from school or drop out;
(6) Indulging minors in internet addiction or exposing them to books, newspapers, films, radio and television programs, audio-visual products, electronic publications, online information, etc., that are harmful or may affect their physical and mental health;
(7) Indulging minors in entering commercial entertainment venues, bars, internet service venues, or other places unsuitable for minors;
(8) Allowing or forcing minors to engage in labor beyond what is stipulated by the State;
(9) Allowing or forcing minors to marry or arranging engagements for them;
(10) Illegally disposing of or embezzling the property of minors or using minors to seek improper benefits;
(11) Other acts that infringe upon the physical and mental health or property rights of minors or fail to fulfill the obligation to protect minors in accordance with the law.

Article 18 The parents or other guardians of minors shall provide a safe family living environment for minors, promptly eliminate safety hazards that may cause electric shock, burns, falls, and other injuries; take measures such as equipping child safety seats and educating minors to obey traffic rules to prevent minors from being injured in traffic accidents; and enhance awareness of outdoor safety protection to prevent minors from drowning, animal attacks, and other accidents.

Article 19 The parents or other guardians of minors shall, based on the age and intellectual development of the minor, listen to the minor's opinions and fully consider their true wishes before making decisions related to the minor's rights and interests.

Article 20 If the parents or other guardians of minors discover that a minor's physical or mental health is being harmed, is suspected of being harmed, or other lawful rights and interests are being infringed upon, they shall promptly ascertain the situation and take protective measures; if the situation is serious, they shall immediately report to the public security, civil affairs, education, or other departments.

Article 21 The parents or other guardians of minors shall not leave minors under the age of eight, or minors who require special care due to physical or psychological reasons, unattended, or entrust them to the temporary care of persons without civil capacity, with limited civil capacity, suffering from serious infectious diseases, or otherwise unsuitable.

The parents or other guardians of minors shall not allow minors under the age of sixteen to live apart from their guardians.

Article 22 If the parents or other guardians of minors are unable to fully fulfill their guardianship duties for a certain period due to reasons such as working away from home, they shall entrust a person with full civil capacity who has the ability to provide care to act as a caregiver; without justifiable reasons, they shall not entrust others to act as caregivers.

When determining the entrusted person, the parents or other guardians of minors shall comprehensively consider factors such as the person's moral character, family situation, physical and mental health condition, and emotional connection with the minor, and shall listen to the opinions of minors who have the ability to express their wishes.

The following persons shall not be entrusted as caregivers:

(1) Those who have committed illegal or criminal acts such as sexual assault, abuse, abandonment, trafficking, or violent injury;
(2) Those with bad habits such as drug use, alcohol abuse, or gambling;
(3) Those who have refused to fulfill or have long been negligent in fulfilling guardianship or care duties;
(4) Other persons unsuitable to serve as entrusted caregivers.

Article 23 The parents or other guardians of minors shall promptly inform the minor's school, kindergarten, and the residents' committee or villagers' committee of the actual place of residence in writing about the entrusted care arrangement, strengthen communication with the minor's school and kindergarten; and contact and communicate with the minor and the entrusted caregiver at least once a week to understand the minor's life, study, and psychological conditions, and provide the minor with family affection and care.

Upon receiving notifications from the entrusted caregiver, residents' committee, villagers' committee, school, kindergarten, or other entities regarding abnormal psychological or behavioral conditions of the minor, the parents or other guardians shall promptly take intervention measures.

Article 24 When the parents of a minor divorce, they shall properly handle matters concerning the custody, education, visitation, and property of the minor child, and listen to the opinions of minors who have the ability to express their wishes. They shall not compete for custody by snatching or hiding the minor child.

After the parents of a minor divorce, the parent who does not directly raise the minor child shall visit the minor child in accordance with the time and method determined by the agreement, the people's court judgment, or mediation, without affecting the minor's study and life; the parent who directly raises the child shall cooperate, except where the visitation right has been suspended by the people's court in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III: School Protection

Article 25 Schools shall fully implement the State's educational policy, adhere to the principle of fostering virtue through education, implement quality education, improve the quality of education, focus on cultivating the cognitive, cooperative, innovative, and practical abilities of minor students, and promote their comprehensive development.

Schools shall establish a work system for the protection of minor students, improve student behavioral norms, and cultivate good behavioral habits of minor students in abiding by laws and regulations.

Article 26 Kindergartens shall provide good care and education, follow the laws of physical and mental development of young children, implement enlightenment education, and promote the harmonious development of young children in terms of physical fitness, intelligence, and moral character.

Article 27 The faculty and staff of schools and kindergartens shall respect the personal dignity of minors and shall not impose corporal punishment, disguised corporal punishment, or other acts that insult the personal dignity of minors.

Article 28 Schools shall guarantee the right of minor students to education and shall not expel or disguisedly expel minor students in violation of State regulations.

Schools shall register minor students who have dropped out before completing compulsory education and persuade them to return to school; if persuasion is ineffective, they shall promptly report in writing to the education administrative department.

Article 29 Schools shall care for and cherish minor students and shall not discriminate against them based on family, physical, psychological, or learning ability conditions. For students with family difficulties or physical or mental disabilities, schools shall provide care; for students with abnormal behavior or learning difficulties, schools shall provide patient assistance.

Schools shall cooperate with relevant government departments in establishing information files for left-behind minor students and minor students in difficult circumstances, and carry out care and assistance work.

Article 30 Schools shall, based on the characteristics of the physical and mental development of minor students, provide guidance on social life, psychological health counseling, puberty education, and life education.

Article 31 Schools shall organize minor students to participate in daily life labor, productive labor, and service labor appropriate to their age, help minor students acquire necessary labor knowledge and skills, and develop good labor habits.

Article 32 Schools and kindergartens shall carry out publicity and education activities on diligence and thrift, opposing waste, cherishing food, and civilized dining, to help minors develop an awareness that waste is shameful and thrift is honorable, and cultivate civilized, healthy, green, and environmentally friendly living habits.

Article 33 Schools shall cooperate with the parents or other guardians of minor students to reasonably arrange the study time of minor students and ensure their time for rest, recreation, and physical exercise.

Schools shall not occupy national statutory holidays, rest days, or winter and summer vacations to organize collective make-up classes for minor students in the compulsory education stage, thereby increasing their study burden.

Kindergartens and off-campus training institutions shall not provide primary school curriculum education to preschool minors.

Article 34 Schools and kindergartens shall provide necessary health care conditions and assist health departments in carrying out health care work for minors at school or in kindergarten.

Article 35 Schools and kindergartens shall establish safety management systems, provide safety education to minors, improve security facilities, equip security personnel, and ensure the personal and property safety of minors during their time at school or in kindergarten.

Schools and kindergartens shall not conduct educational and teaching activities in school buildings, other facilities, or places that endanger the personal safety or physical and mental health of minors.

When arranging for minors to participate in collective activities such as cultural entertainment or social practice, schools and kindergartens shall protect the physical and mental health of minors and prevent personal injury accidents.

Article 36 Schools and kindergartens that use school buses shall establish and improve school bus safety management systems, equip safety management personnel, conduct regular safety inspections of school buses, provide safety education to school bus drivers, and teach minors about safe school bus riding knowledge and cultivate their emergency response skills for school bus safety accidents.

Article 37 Schools and kindergartens shall, as needed, formulate plans for responding to emergencies such as natural disasters, accidents, public health incidents, and accidental injuries, equip corresponding facilities, and conduct necessary drills regularly.

If a personal injury accident occurs to a minor on campus, in kindergarten, or during off-campus activities organized by the school or kindergarten, the school or kindergarten shall immediately provide first aid, handle the matter properly, promptly notify the minor's parents or other guardians, and report to the relevant departments.

Article 38 Schools and kindergartens shall not arrange for minors to participate in commercial activities, nor shall they promote or require minors or their parents or other guardians to purchase designated goods or services.

Schools and kindergartens shall not cooperate with off-campus training institutions to provide paid course tutoring to minors.

Article 39 Schools shall establish a work system for the prevention and control of student bullying, and conduct education and training on the prevention and control of student bullying for faculty, staff, and students.

Schools shall immediately stop student bullying, notify the parents or other guardians of the minor students who committed the bullying and those who were bullied to participate in the identification and handling of the bullying; provide timely psychological counseling, education, and guidance to the relevant minor students; and provide necessary family education guidance to the parents or other guardians of the relevant minor students.

For minor students who commit bullying, schools shall strengthen discipline in accordance with the law based on the nature and severity of the bullying. For serious bullying, schools shall not conceal it and shall promptly report to the public security organs and education administrative departments, and cooperate with relevant departments in handling it in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Schools and kindergartens shall establish a work system for the prevention of sexual assault and sexual harassment of minors. For illegal and criminal acts such as sexual assault and sexual harassment of minors, schools and kindergartens shall not conceal them and shall promptly report to the public security organs and education administrative departments, and cooperate with relevant departments in handling them in accordance with the law.

Schools and kindergartens shall provide age-appropriate sex education to minors to enhance their self-protection awareness and ability to prevent sexual assault and sexual harassment. For minors who have suffered sexual assault or sexual harassment, schools and kindergartens shall promptly take relevant protective measures.

Article 41 Infant and toddler care service institutions, early education service institutions, off-campus training institutions, off-campus care institutions, etc., shall, with reference to the relevant provisions of this Chapter, carry out the protection of minors based on the growth characteristics and laws of minors of different ages.

## Chapter IV: Social Protection

Article 42 The whole society shall foster a good atmosphere of caring for and cherishing minors.

The State encourages, supports, and guides people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and other organizations and individuals to carry out social activities and services conducive to the healthy growth of minors.

Article 43 Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall designate dedicated personnel to be responsible for the protection of minors, assist relevant government departments in publicizing laws and regulations on the protection of minors, guide, help, and supervise the parents or other guardians of minors in fulfilling their guardianship duties in accordance with the law, establish information files for left-behind minors and minors in difficult circumstances, and provide care and assistance.Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall assist relevant government departments in supervising the entrustment of minors' care. If they discover that the entrusted person lacks the capacity to provide care or neglects their caregiving duties, they shall promptly report to the relevant government departments, inform the minor's parents or other guardians, and help urge the entrusted person to fulfill their caregiving responsibilities.

Article 44 Patriotic education bases, libraries, youth palaces, children's activity centers, and children's homes shall be open to minors free of charge. Museums, memorial halls, science and technology museums, exhibition halls, art galleries, cultural centers, community public internet service venues, as well as cinemas, theaters, sports venues, zoos, botanical gardens, parks, and other venues, shall provide free or discounted access to minors in accordance with relevant regulations.

The State encourages public venues such as patriotic education bases, museums, science and technology museums, and art galleries to hold special sessions for minors, providing targeted services for them.

The State encourages state organs, enterprises, institutions, and military units to develop their own educational resources, establish open days for minors, and provide support for thematic education, social practice, and career experience for minors.

The State encourages scientific research institutions and science and technology social organizations to carry out science popularization activities for minors.

Article 45 Urban public transportation, as well as road, railway, waterway, and air passenger transport, shall implement free or discounted fares for minors in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 46 The State encourages large public places, public transport vehicles, tourist attractions, and scenic spots to set up nursing rooms, baby changing tables, and sanitary facilities such as toilets and washbasins convenient for young children, to provide convenience for minors.

Article 47 No organization or individual may, in violation of relevant regulations, restrict the care or preferential treatment that minors are entitled to.

Article 48 The State encourages the creation, publication, production, and dissemination of books, newspapers, films, radio and television programs, stage artworks, audio-visual products, electronic publications, and online information that are conducive to the healthy development of minors.

Article 49 News media shall strengthen publicity on the protection of minors and conduct public opinion supervision over acts that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of minors. When interviewing and reporting on incidents involving minors, news media shall be objective, prudent, and moderate, and shall not infringe upon the reputation, privacy, or other legitimate rights and interests of minors.

Article 50 It is prohibited to produce, reproduce, publish, distribute, or disseminate books, newspapers, films, radio and television programs, stage artworks, audio-visual products, electronic publications, and online information that contain content promoting obscenity, pornography, violence, cults, superstition, gambling, incitement to suicide, terrorism, separatism, extremism, or other content harmful to the physical and mental health of minors.

Article 51 Any organization or individual that publishes, distributes, or disseminates books, newspapers, films, radio and television programs, stage artworks, audio-visual products, electronic publications, or online information containing content that may affect the physical and mental health of minors shall prominently display a warning.

Article 52 It is prohibited to produce, reproduce, publish, distribute, or disseminate, or possess, any pornographic or obscene materials or online information concerning minors.

Article 53 No organization or individual may publish, broadcast, post, or distribute advertisements containing content harmful to the physical and mental health of minors. No commercial advertisements may be broadcast, posted, or distributed in schools or kindergartens. No commercial advertisements may be published or disguised as published on school uniforms, textbooks, or other items.

Article 54 It is prohibited to abduct, traffic, kidnap, abuse, or illegally adopt minors. It is prohibited to commit sexual assault or sexual harassment against minors.

It is prohibited to coerce, entice, or instigate minors to join mafia-style organizations or engage in illegal or criminal activities.

It is prohibited to coerce, deceive, or exploit minors for begging.

Article 55 Food, drugs, toys, utensils, game and amusement equipment, and amusement facilities produced and sold for use by minors shall comply with national or industry standards and shall not endanger the personal safety or physical and mental health of minors. Producers of the above products shall prominently indicate precautions; products without such indications shall not be sold.

Article 56 Public places where minors gather in groups shall comply with national or industry safety standards and adopt corresponding safety protection measures. Facilities that may pose safety risks shall be regularly maintained, with safety warning signs prominently displayed, along with age suitability and precautions. When necessary, dedicated personnel shall be assigned for supervision.

Operators of large shopping malls, supermarkets, hospitals, libraries, museums, science and technology museums, amusement parks, stations, docks, airports, tourist attractions, and scenic spots shall install safety alarm systems for searching for lost minors. Upon receiving a request for help, the operator shall immediately activate the safety alarm system, organize personnel to search, and report to the public security authorities.

In the event of an emergency in a public place, minors shall be given priority in rescue.

Article 57 When accommodating minors, or minors and adults together, in hotels, inns, hostels, and other lodging establishments, the operator shall inquire about the contact information of the parents or other guardians, the identity relationship of the occupants, and other relevant information. If any suspected illegal or criminal activity is discovered, the operator shall immediately report to the public security authorities and promptly contact the minor's parents or other guardians.

Article 58 No commercial entertainment venues, bars, internet service venues, or other places unsuitable for minors' activities may be established near schools or kindergartens. Operators of commercial song and dance entertainment venues, bars, internet service venues, and other places unsuitable for minors' activities shall not allow minors to enter. Electronic game equipment set up in amusement entertainment venues shall not be provided to minors except during national legal holidays. Operators shall prominently display signs indicating that minors are prohibited or restricted from entering. If it is difficult to determine whether a person is a minor, the operator shall request the presentation of identification documents.

Article 59 No cigarette, alcohol, or lottery sales outlets may be established near schools or kindergartens. It is prohibited to sell cigarettes, alcohol, or lottery tickets to minors, or to cash lottery winnings for minors. Operators of cigarettes, alcohol, and lottery tickets shall prominently display signs indicating that cigarettes, alcohol, or lottery tickets are not sold to minors. If it is difficult to determine whether a person is a minor, the operator shall request the presentation of identification documents.

No one may smoke or drink alcohol in schools, kindergartens, or other public places where minors gather.

Article 60 It is prohibited to provide or sell controlled knives or other instruments that may cause serious injury to minors. If an operator finds it difficult to determine whether a buyer is a minor, the operator shall request the presentation of identification documents.

Article 61 No organization or individual may employ minors under the age of 16, except as otherwise provided by the State.

Commercial entertainment venues, bars, internet service venues, and other places unsuitable for minors' activities shall not employ minors who have reached the age of 16.

Units and individuals employing minors who have reached the age of 16 shall comply with state regulations on types of work, working hours, labor intensity, and protective measures, and shall not assign them to heavy, toxic, hazardous, or other labor or dangerous operations harmful to their physical and mental health.

No organization or individual may organize performances or other activities that harm the physical and mental health of minors. With the consent of the minor's parents or other guardians, if a minor participates in performances, program production, or other activities, the organizer shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, protect the minor's legitimate rights and interests.

Article 62 When recruiting staff, units that have close contact with minors shall inquire with public security authorities and people's procuratorates whether the applicant has a record of sexual assault, abuse, trafficking, violent injury, or other illegal or criminal acts. If such a record is found, the applicant shall not be hired.

Units that have close contact with minors shall conduct annual regular inquiries into whether their staff have the above-mentioned illegal or criminal records. If, through inquiries or other means, it is discovered that a staff member has engaged in the above-mentioned acts, the unit shall promptly dismiss that staff member.

Article 63 No organization or individual may conceal, destroy, or illegally delete a minor's letters, diaries, emails, or other online communication content.

Except in the following circumstances, no organization or individual may open or inspect a minor's letters, diaries, emails, or other online communication content:

(1) The parents or other guardians of a minor who lacks civil capacity open or inspect them on behalf of the minor;

(2) Inspection is conducted in accordance with the law for reasons of national security or the investigation of criminal offenses;

(3) In an emergency, to protect the personal safety of the minor.

## Chapter 5 Online Protection

Article 64 The State, society, schools, and families shall strengthen the education and promotion of minors' online literacy, cultivate and improve minors' online literacy, enhance minors' awareness and ability to use the internet in a scientific, civilized, safe, and reasonable manner, and protect minors' legitimate rights and interests in cyberspace.

Article 65 The State encourages and supports the creation and dissemination of online content conducive to the healthy development of minors, and encourages and supports the research, development, production, and use of online technologies, products, and services specifically designed for minors and suitable for their physical and mental health characteristics.

Article 66 The cyberspace administration department and other relevant departments shall strengthen supervision and inspection of the online protection of minors, punish activities that use the internet to harm the physical and mental health of minors in accordance with the law, and provide a safe and healthy online environment for minors.

Article 67 The cyberspace administration department, in conjunction with public security, culture and tourism, press and publication, film, radio and television, and other departments, shall, based on the needs of protecting minors of different age groups, determine the types, scope, and criteria for judging online information that may affect the physical and mental health of minors.

Article 68 Departments of press and publication, education, health, culture and tourism, and cyberspace administration shall regularly carry out publicity and education on preventing minors from becoming addicted to the internet, supervise the fulfillment of obligations by online product and service providers to prevent minors from becoming addicted to the internet, and guide families, schools, and social organizations to cooperate and adopt scientific and reasonable methods to prevent and intervene in minors' internet addiction.

No organization or individual may intervene in a minor's internet addiction in a manner that harms the minor's physical and mental health.

Article 69 Internet service facilities provided by schools, communities, libraries, cultural centers, youth palaces, and other venues for minors shall be equipped with minors' online protection software or other safety protection technical measures.

Manufacturers and sellers of smart terminal products shall install minors' online protection software on the products, or prominently inform users of the installation channels and methods for such software.

Article 70 Schools shall use the internet reasonably for teaching activities. Without school permission, minor students shall not bring mobile phones or other smart terminal products into the classroom. Those brought into the school shall be managed uniformly.

If a school discovers that a minor student is addicted to the internet, it shall promptly inform the student's parents or other guardians, and together educate and guide the minor student to help them return to normal study and life.

Article 71 Parents or other guardians of minors shall improve their own online literacy, regulate their own use of the internet, and strengthen the guidance and supervision of minors' use of the internet.

Parents or other guardians of minors shall, by installing minors' online protection software on smart terminal products, selecting service models and management functions suitable for minors, and other means, prevent minors from coming into contact with online information that is harmful or may affect their physical and mental health, reasonably arrange the time minors spend using the internet, and effectively prevent minors from becoming addicted to the internet.

Article 72 When processing the personal information of minors through the internet, information processors shall follow the principles of legality, legitimacy, and necessity. When processing the personal information of minors under the age of 14, the consent of the minor's parents or other guardians shall be obtained, except as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

If a minor, parent, or other guardian requests the information processor to correct or delete the minor's personal information, the information processor shall promptly take measures to correct or delete it, except as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 73 If an online service provider discovers that a minor has published private information through the internet, it shall promptly issue a warning and take necessary protective measures.

Article 74 Online product and service providers shall not provide minors with products or services that induce addiction.

Online service providers such as online games, live streaming, audio and video, and social networking shall set up corresponding time management, permission management, consumption management, and other functions for minors using their services.

Online education products and services targeting minors shall not insert links to online games or push advertisements or other information unrelated to teaching.

Article 75 Online games may only be operated after approval in accordance with the law.

The State establishes a unified electronic identity authentication system for minors in online games. Online game service providers shall require minors to register and log in to online games using their real identity information.

Online game service providers shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations and standards, classify game products, provide age-appropriate tips, and adopt technical measures to prevent minors from accessing inappropriate games or game functions.

Online game service providers shall not provide online game services to minors between 10:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m. the next day.

Article 76 Online live streaming service providers shall not provide account registration services for live streaming broadcasters to minors under the age of 16. When providing account registration services for live streaming broadcasters to minors who have reached the age of 16, they shall authenticate their identity information and obtain the consent of their parents or other guardians.

Article 77 No organization or individual may engage in online bullying against minors through text, images, audio, video, or other forms, including insults, defamation, threats, or malicious damage to their image.

Minors who have suffered online bullying, as well as their parents or other guardians, have the right to notify the online service provider to take measures such as deletion, blocking, or disconnection of links. Upon receiving the notification, the online service provider shall promptly take necessary measures to stop the online bullying and prevent the spread of information.

Article 78 Online product and service providers shall establish convenient, reasonable, and effective complaint and reporting channels, publicly disclose information such as complaint and reporting methods, and promptly accept and handle complaints and reports involving minors.

Article 79 Any organization or individual that discovers that an online product or service contains information harmful to the physical and mental health of minors has the right to file a complaint or report with the online product or service provider or with the cyberspace administration, public security, or other departments.

Article 80 If an online service provider discovers that a user has published or disseminated information that may affect the physical and mental health of minors without prominently displaying a warning, it shall issue a warning or notify the user to do so. If no warning is issued, the relevant information shall not be transmitted.

If an online service provider discovers that a user has published or disseminated information containing content harmful to the physical and mental health of minors, it shall immediately stop transmitting the relevant information, take measures such as deletion, blocking, or disconnection of links, retain relevant records, and report to the cyberspace administration, public security, or other departments.

If an online service provider discovers that a user is using its online service to commit illegal or criminal acts against a minor, it shall immediately stop providing online services to that user, retain relevant records, and report to the public security authorities.

## Chapter 6 Government Protection

Article 81 The functional departments of people's governments at or above the county level that undertake the specific work of the coordination mechanism for the protection of minors shall designate relevant internal institutions or specialized personnel to be responsible for the protection of minors.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices shall establish workstations for the protection of minors or designate specialized personnel to promptly handle matters related to minors. They shall support and guide residents' committees and villagers' committees in designating dedicated personnel and posts to effectively carry out the protection of minors.

Article 82 People's governments at all levels shall incorporate family education guidance services into the urban and rural public service system, carry out publicity on family education knowledge, and encourage and support relevant people's organizations, enterprises, institutions, and social organizations to provide family education guidance services.

Article 83 People's governments at all levels shall guarantee the right of minors to receive education and take measures to ensure that left-behind minors, minors in distress, and disabled minors receive compulsory education.

For minor students who have dropped out of school without completing compulsory education, the education administrative department shall order their parents or other guardians to send them to school to receive compulsory education.

Article 84 People's governments at all levels shall develop childcare and preschool education, properly run infant and toddler care service institutions and kindergartens, and support social forces in establishing nursing rooms, infant and toddler care service institutions, and kindergartens in accordance with the law.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall train and educate childcare workers in infant and toddler care service institutions and kindergartens to improve their professional ethics and competence.

Article 85 People's governments at all levels shall develop vocational education, guarantee that minors receive vocational education or vocational skills training, and encourage and support people's organizations, enterprises, institutions, and social organizations to provide vocational skills training services for minors.

Article 86 People's governments at all levels shall ensure that disabled minors who have the ability to receive regular education and can adapt to campus life receive education in regular schools and kindergartens nearby. They shall ensure that disabled minors who do not have the ability to receive regular education receive preschool education, compulsory education, and vocational education in special education schools and kindergartens.

People's governments at all levels shall guarantee the conditions for running special education schools and kindergartens, and encourage and support social forces to establish special education schools and kindergartens.

Article 87 Local people's governments and their relevant departments shall ensure campus safety, supervise and guide schools, kindergartens, and other units to implement campus safety responsibilities, and establish mechanisms for reporting, handling, and coordinating emergencies.

Article 88 Public security authorities and other relevant departments shall, in accordance with the law, maintain public order and traffic order around campuses, install monitoring equipment and traffic safety facilities, and prevent and stop illegal and criminal acts against minors.

Article 89 Local people's governments shall establish and improve activity venues and facilities suitable for minors, support the construction and operation of public welfare activity venues and facilities for minors, encourage social forces to establish activity venues and facilities suitable for minors, and strengthen management.

Local people's governments shall take measures to encourage and support schools to open cultural and sports facilities to minors free of charge or at a discount during national legal holidays, rest days, and winter and summer vacations.Local people's governments shall take measures to prevent any organization or individual from encroaching upon, damaging, or occupying the premises, buildings, and facilities of venues for minors' activities, such as schools, kindergartens, and infant and toddler care service institutions.

Article 90 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall provide health care and nutrition guidance to minors and deliver health care services.

Health departments shall, in accordance with the law, standardize vaccination for minors, prevent and treat common and frequently occurring diseases among minors, strengthen the prevention, control, and supervision of infectious diseases, carry out injury prevention and intervention, and guide and supervise schools, kindergartens, and infant and toddler care service institutions in conducting health care work.

Education administrative departments shall strengthen mental health education for minors and establish mechanisms for the early detection and timely intervention of minors' psychological problems. Health departments shall carry out psychological treatment, psychological crisis intervention, and early identification, diagnosis, and treatment of mental disorders for minors.

Article 91 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall implement categorized safeguards for minors in distress and take measures to meet their basic needs in areas such as livelihood, education, safety, medical rehabilitation, and housing.

Article 92 In any of the following circumstances, civil affairs departments shall, in accordance with the law, provide temporary guardianship for minors:

(1) The minor is a vagrant or beggar, or their identity is unknown, and their parents or other guardians cannot be temporarily located;

(2) The guardian is missing and no other person can serve as guardian;

(3) The guardian is unable to fulfill guardianship duties due to objective reasons or emergencies such as natural disasters, accidents, or public health incidents, resulting in a lack of guardianship for the minor;

(4) The guardian refuses or neglects to fulfill guardianship duties, leaving the minor unattended;

(5) The guardian instigates or uses the minor to engage in illegal or criminal acts, requiring the minor to be removed and placed;

(6) The minor suffers serious harm from the guardian or faces threats to personal safety, requiring emergency placement;

(7) Other circumstances as provided by law.

Article 93 For minors under temporary guardianship, civil affairs departments may arrange placement through methods such as entrusting relatives to raise them or foster care, or may hand them over to minor rescue and protection institutions or child welfare institutions for shelter and care.

During the period of temporary guardianship, if, upon assessment by the civil affairs department, the guardian is deemed capable of fulfilling guardianship duties again, the civil affairs department may return the minor to the guardian for care.

Article 94 In any of the following circumstances, civil affairs departments shall, in accordance with the law, provide long-term guardianship for minors:

(1) The minor's parents or other guardians cannot be located;

(2) The guardian has died or has been declared dead, and no other person can serve as guardian;

(3) The guardian has lost the capacity to provide guardianship, and no other person can serve as guardian;

(4) A people's court has revoked the guardian's qualifications and designated the civil affairs department as the guardian;

(5) Other circumstances as provided by law.

Article 95 After conducting an adoption assessment, civil affairs departments may, in accordance with the law, place minors under their long-term guardianship for adoption by qualified applicants. Upon the establishment of the adoption relationship, the guardianship relationship between the civil affairs department and the minor shall terminate.

Article 96 When civil affairs departments assume temporary or long-term guardianship duties, finance, education, health, public security, and other departments shall cooperate according to their respective responsibilities.

People's governments at or above the county level and their civil affairs departments shall, as needed, establish minor rescue and protection institutions and child welfare institutions to shelter and care for minors under the guardianship of civil affairs departments.

Article 97 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish a unified national hotline for the protection of minors to promptly receive and refer complaints and reports of violations of the legitimate rights and interests of minors; they shall encourage and support people's organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations in participating in the construction of minor protection service platforms, service hotlines, and service stations, providing consultation and assistance on minor protection matters.

Article 98 The state shall establish an information query system for persons with records of crimes such as sexual assault, abuse, abduction, and violent injury, providing free query services to units that have close contact with minors.

Article 99 Local people's governments shall cultivate, guide, and regulate relevant social organizations and social workers to participate in minor protection work, provide family education guidance services, and offer professional services such as psychological counseling, rehabilitation assistance, and guardianship and adoption assessments for minors.

## Chapter 7 Judicial Protection

Article 100 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall perform their duties in accordance with the law to protect the legitimate rights and interests of minors.

Article 101 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall establish specialized agencies or designate specialized personnel to handle cases involving minors. Personnel handling cases involving minors shall undergo specialized training and be familiar with the physical and psychological characteristics of minors. Such specialized agencies or personnel shall include female staff members.

Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall implement evaluation and assessment standards for the above-mentioned agencies and personnel that are compatible with the work of protecting minors.

Article 102 When handling cases involving minors, public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall consider the physical and psychological characteristics and the needs of healthy growth of minors, use language and expressions that minors can understand, and listen to the opinions of minors.

Article 103 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, judicial administrative departments, and other organizations and individuals shall not disclose the names, images, addresses, schools attended, or other information that could identify minors involved in cases, except in cases such as searching for missing or abducted minors.

Article 104 For minors in need of legal aid or judicial assistance, legal aid institutions or public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall provide assistance and, in accordance with the law, provide them with legal aid or judicial assistance.

Legal aid institutions shall assign lawyers familiar with the physical and psychological characteristics of minors to provide legal aid services to minors.

Legal aid institutions and bar associations shall provide guidance and training to lawyers handling legal aid cases involving minors.

Article 105 People's procuratorates shall, through the exercise of their procuratorial powers, supervise litigation activities involving minors in accordance with the law.

Article 106 Where the legitimate rights and interests of a minor are infringed upon, and the relevant organization or individual fails to initiate a lawsuit on their behalf, the people's procuratorate may urge or support the initiation of a lawsuit; if the public interest is involved, the people's procuratorate has the right to initiate a public interest lawsuit.

Article 107 When hearing inheritance cases, people's courts shall protect the inheritance rights and rights to receive bequests of minors in accordance with the law.

When hearing divorce cases involving the custody of minor children, people's courts shall respect the true wishes of minor children who have reached the age of eight and handle the matter in accordance with the principle of best interests of the minor child, based on the specific circumstances of both parties.

Article 108 Where the parents or other guardians of a minor fail to fulfill their guardianship duties in accordance with the law or seriously infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of the minor under their guardianship, the people's court may, upon application by relevant persons or units, issue a personal safety protection order or revoke the guardian's qualifications in accordance with the law.

Parents or other guardians whose guardian qualifications have been revoked shall continue to bear the costs of support in accordance with the law.

Article 109 When hearing cases involving minors, such as divorce, custody, adoption, guardianship, and visitation, people's courts may conduct or entrust social organizations to conduct social investigations into the relevant circumstances of the minor.

Article 110 When interrogating minor criminal suspects or defendants, or questioning minor victims or witnesses, public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall, in accordance with the law, notify their legal representatives, adult relatives, representatives of their schools, or other suitable adults to be present, and shall conduct the proceedings in an appropriate manner and in an appropriate place, protecting the reputation, privacy, and other legitimate rights and interests of the minor.

When a people's court holds a hearing in a case involving a minor, minor victims or witnesses generally shall not testify in court; if they must appear in court, protective measures such as technical means to protect their privacy and psychological intervention shall be adopted.

Article 111 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall cooperate with other relevant government departments, people's organizations, and social organizations to implement necessary protective measures such as psychological intervention, financial assistance, legal aid, and school transfer for minor victims of sexual assault or violent injury and their families.

Article 112 When handling cases involving minors who have suffered sexual assault or violent injury, public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall, when questioning minor victims or witnesses, adopt measures such as synchronous audio and video recording, and strive to complete the process in one session; if the minor victim or witness is female, the questioning shall be conducted by female staff.

Article 113 For minors who have committed illegal or criminal acts, the policy of education, persuasion, and rescue shall be implemented, adhering to the principle of focusing on education and supplementing with punishment.

After minors who have committed illegal or criminal acts are punished in accordance with the law, they shall not be discriminated against in terms of school enrollment or employment.

Article 114 Where public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments discover that a relevant unit has failed to fulfill its protection duties such as education, management, rescue, and care for minors, they shall make suggestions to that unit. The unit receiving the suggestion shall provide a written reply within one month.

Article 115 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall, based on actual conditions and the characteristics of cases involving minors, carry out legal education and publicity work for minors.

Article 116 The state encourages and supports social organizations and social workers to participate in work such as psychological intervention, legal aid, social investigation, social observation, educational correction, and community correction for minors involved in cases.

## Chapter 8 Legal Liability

Article 117 Where a violation of the provisions of Article 11, paragraph 2, results in serious consequences due to failure to fulfill the reporting obligation, the competent superior department or the unit where the person works shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 118 Where the parents or other guardians of a minor fail to fulfill their guardianship duties in accordance with the law or infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of the minor, the residents' committee or villagers' committee of their place of residence shall admonish and stop the behavior; if the circumstances are serious, the residents' committee or villagers' committee shall promptly report to the public security organ.

When a public security organ receives a report, or when a public security organ, people's procuratorate, or people's court discovers during the handling of a case that the parents or other guardians of a minor are in the above-mentioned situation, they shall reprimand them and may order them to receive family education guidance.

Article 119 Where schools, kindergartens, infant and toddler care service institutions, and their faculty and staff violate the provisions of Articles 27, 28, and 39 of this Law, the public security, education, health, and market supervision and management departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections; if corrections are refused or the circumstances are serious, sanctions shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 120 Where a violation of the provisions of Articles 44, 45, and 47 results in failure to provide free or preferential treatment to minors, the market supervision and management, culture and tourism, and transportation departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period and issue a warning; if corrections are refused, a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed.

Article 121 Where a violation of the provisions of Articles 50 and 51 occurs, the press and publication, radio and television, film, and cyberspace administration departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan; if corrections are refused or the circumstances are serious, they shall order the suspension of relevant business, production suspension, or revocation of the business license or relevant permit; if the illegal gains are 1 million yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 1 million yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan shall be imposed.

Article 122 Where a venue operator violates the provisions of Article 56, paragraph 2, or an accommodation operator violates the provisions of Article 57, the market supervision and management, emergency management, and public security departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period and issue a warning; if corrections are refused or serious consequences result, they shall order business suspension for rectification or revocation of the business license or relevant permit, and concurrently impose a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 123 Where a relevant operator violates the provisions of Articles 58, 59, paragraph 1, and 60, the culture and tourism, market supervision and management, tobacco monopoly, and public security departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused or the circumstances are serious, they shall order business suspension for rectification or revocation of the business license or relevant permit, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 124 Where a violation of the provisions of Article 59, paragraph 2, involves smoking or drinking alcohol in schools, kindergartens, or other public places where minors gather, the health, education, and market supervision and management departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections, issue a warning, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 500 yuan; if the venue manager fails to stop the behavior in a timely manner, the health, education, and market supervision and management departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, issue a warning and impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan.

Article 125 Where a violation of the provisions of Article 61 occurs, the culture and tourism, human resources and social security, and market supervision and management departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains, and may concurrently impose a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan; if corrections are refused or the circumstances are serious, they shall order production suspension or revocation of the business license or relevant permit, and concurrently impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan.

Article 126 Where a unit that has close contact with minors violates the provisions of Article 62 by failing to fulfill the inquiry obligation, or by hiring or continuing to employ persons with relevant criminal records, the education, human resources and social security, and market supervision and management departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections within a specified period, issue a warning, and impose a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan; if corrections are refused or serious consequences result, they shall order business suspension for rectification or revocation of the business license or relevant permit, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 127 Where an information processor violates the provisions of Article 72, or a network product or service provider violates the provisions of Articles 73, 74, 75, 76, 77, and 80, the public security, cyberspace administration, telecommunications, press and publication, radio and television, and culture and tourism departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, order corrections, issue a warning, confiscate illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 1 million yuan or more, a fine of not less than one time but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 1 million yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 1 million yuan shall be imposed; and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed on the directly responsible supervisors and other responsible personnel; if corrections are refused or the circumstances are serious, they may also order the suspension of relevant business, business suspension for rectification, closure of the website, or revocation of the business license or relevant permit.

Article 128 Where a state functionary neglects their duties, abuses their power, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, harming the legitimate rights and interests of minors, sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with the law.

Article 129 Where a violation of the provisions of this Law infringes upon the legitimate rights and interests of a minor, causing personal, property, or other damage, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Where a violation of the provisions of this Law constitutes a violation of public security administration, a public security administration penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 130 The meanings of the following terms in this Law are:

(1) "Units that have close contact with minors" refers to educational institutions such as schools and kindergartens; off-campus training institutions; minor rescue and protection institutions, child welfare institutions, and other minor placement and rescue institutions; infant and toddler care service institutions and early education service institutions; off-campus care and temporary care institutions; domestic service agencies; medical institutions providing medical services to minors; and other enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations that bear responsibilities for the education, training, guardianship, rescue, care, and medical care of minors.

(2) "Schools" refers to ordinary primary and secondary schools, special education schools, secondary vocational schools, and specialized schools.

(3) "Student bullying" refers to behavior that occurs among students where one party intentionally or maliciously uses physical, verbal, or online means to bully or insult another party, causing personal injury, property damage, or mental harm to the other party.

Article 131 Foreigners and stateless persons under the age of 18 within the territory of China shall be protected in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 132 This Law shall come into effect on June 1, 2021.
