# Family Education Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 31st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on October 23, 2021

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of carrying forward the fine tradition of the Chinese nation in attaching importance to family education, guiding the whole society to value family, family education, and family traditions, enhancing family happiness and social harmony, and cultivating socialist builders and successors who are well developed morally, intellectually, physically, aesthetically, and labor-wise.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "family education" refers to the cultivation, guidance, and influence exerted by parents or other guardians on minors for the purpose of promoting their comprehensive and healthy growth, in aspects such as moral character, physical fitness, life skills, cultural cultivation, and behavioral habits.

Article 3 The fundamental task of family education is to foster virtue and nurture people, cultivate and practice core socialist values, carry forward the excellent traditional Chinese culture, revolutionary culture, and advanced socialist culture, and promote the healthy growth of minors.

Article 4 The parents or other guardians of minors are responsible for implementing family education.

The State and society provide guidance, support, and services for family education.

State functionaries shall take the lead in cultivating good family traditions and fulfilling their family education responsibilities.

Article 5 Family education shall meet the following requirements:

(1) Respect the laws of physical and mental development and individual differences of minors;

(2) Respect the personal dignity of minors, protect their right to privacy and personal information, and safeguard their legitimate rights and interests;

(3) Follow the characteristics of family education and implement scientific concepts and methods of family education;

(4) Closely integrate and coordinate family education, school education, and social education;

(5) Adopt flexible and diverse measures based on actual circumstances.

Article 6 People's governments at all levels shall guide family education work and establish and improve a mechanism for coordinated education by families, schools, and society. The agencies responsible for women's and children's work of people's governments at or above the county level shall organize, coordinate, guide, and supervise relevant departments to carry out family education work well.

Education administrative departments and women's federations shall coordinate social resources, jointly promote the construction of a family education guidance service system covering urban and rural areas, and undertake the daily affairs of family education work according to their respective responsibilities.

The spiritual civilization development departments at or above the county level and the relevant departments of public security, civil affairs, judicial administration, human resources and social security, culture and tourism, health, market supervision and administration, radio and television, sports, press and publication, and cyberspace administration of people's governments at or above the county level shall do a good job in family education work within their respective duties.

Article 7 People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate special plans for family education work, incorporate family education guidance services into the urban and rural public service system and the government procurement service catalog, include relevant funds in the fiscal budget, and encourage and support the provision of family education guidance through government procurement of services.

Article 8 People's courts and people's procuratorates shall perform their functions, cooperate with the people's governments at the same level and their relevant departments to establish a linkage mechanism for family education work, and jointly do a good job in family education work.

Article 9 Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, disabled persons' federations, science and technology associations, committees for the care of the next generation, as well as residents' committees and villagers' committees, shall actively carry out family education work in conjunction with their own work and provide social support for family education.

Article 10 The State encourages and supports enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individuals to lawfully carry out public welfare family education service activities.

Article 11 The State encourages the conduct of research on family education, encourages institutions of higher learning to offer professional courses in family education, supports normal universities and qualified institutions of higher learning to strengthen the disciplinary construction of family education, cultivate professional talents for family education services, and carry out training for family education service personnel.

Article 12 The State encourages and supports natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations to make donations or provide volunteer services for the cause of family education, and grants tax incentives to those who meet the conditions according to law.

The State commends and rewards organizations and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to family education work in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 13 The week of May 15 each year, International Day of Families, is designated as the National Family Education Publicity Week.

## Chapter II Family Responsibilities

Article 14 Parents or other guardians shall establish the awareness that the family is the first classroom and parents are the first teachers, assume the primary responsibility for implementing family education for minors, and educate minors to develop good thoughts, conduct, and habits with correct ideas, methods, and behaviors.

Other family members living together who have full capacity for civil conduct shall assist and cooperate with the parents or other guardians of minors in implementing family education.

Article 15 Parents or other guardians of minors and other family members shall focus on family building, cultivate a positive and healthy family culture, establish and pass on good family traditions, carry forward the family virtues of the Chinese nation, jointly build civilized and harmonious family relationships, and create a good family environment for the healthy growth of minors.

Article 16 Parents or other guardians of minors shall, based on the characteristics of the physical and mental development of minors of different ages, carry out family education guided by the following content:

(1) Educate minors to love the Party, the country, the people, the collective, and socialism, establish the concept of safeguarding national unity, consolidate the sense of community of the Chinese nation, and cultivate feelings for the family and the country;

(2) Educate minors to advocate virtue and goodness, respect the elderly and love the young, love the family, be diligent and thrifty, unite and help each other, be honest and friendly, abide by laws and regulations, and cultivate their awareness of good social morality, family virtues, personal moral character, and the rule of law;

(3) Help minors establish a correct view of success, guide them to cultivate broad interests, healthy aesthetic pursuits, and good study habits, and enhance their spirit of scientific exploration, innovative awareness, and ability;

(4) Ensure minors have balanced nutrition, scientific exercise, adequate sleep, and physical and mental pleasure, guide them to develop good living and behavioral habits, and promote their healthy physical and mental development;

(5) Pay attention to the mental health of minors, teach them to cherish life, provide them with safety knowledge education on traffic travel, healthy internet use, and prevention of bullying, drowning, fraud, abduction, and sexual assault, help them master safety knowledge and skills, and enhance their awareness and ability of self-protection;

(6) Help minors establish a correct view of labor, participate in力所能及 labor, improve their self-care ability and independent living ability, and cultivate the excellent character of enduring hardship and a good habit of loving labor.
Article 17 When implementing family education, parents or other guardians of minors shall pay attention to the physical, psychological, and intellectual development status of minors, respect their right to participate in relevant family affairs and express opinions, and reasonably use the following methods:

(1) Personally nurture and strengthen parent-child companionship;

(2) Participate jointly and give full play to the role of both parents;

(3) Educate opportunely and integrate education into daily life;

(4) Influence subtly and combine words with deeds;

(5) Combine strictness with kindness, and balance care and love with strict requirements;

(6) Respect differences and provide scientific guidance based on age and personality characteristics;

(7) Communicate equally and provide respect, understanding, and encouragement;

(8) Promote mutual growth, with parents and children growing together;

(9) Other methods beneficial to the comprehensive development and healthy growth of minors.

Article 18 Parents or other guardians of minors shall establish correct concepts of family education, consciously learn family education knowledge, conduct targeted learning during pregnancy and at important stages when minors enter infant and toddler care service institutions, kindergartens, primary and secondary schools, master scientific family education methods, and improve their family education capabilities.

Article 19 Parents or other guardians of minors shall closely cooperate with primary and secondary schools, kindergartens, infant and toddler care service institutions, and communities, actively participate in the public welfare family education guidance and practical activities provided by them, and jointly promote the healthy growth of minors.

Article 20 If the parents of a minor live separately or are divorced, they shall cooperate with each other to fulfill their family education responsibilities, and neither party may refuse or neglect to fulfill them; unless otherwise provided by law, neither party may obstruct the other from implementing family education.

Article 21 If parents or other guardians of a minor lawfully entrust another person to care for the minor, they shall maintain contact with the entrusted person and the minor, regularly understand the minor's learning, living conditions, and psychological state, and jointly fulfill family education responsibilities with the entrusted person.

Article 22 Parents or other guardians of minors shall reasonably arrange the time for minors to study, rest, entertain, and engage in physical exercise, avoid increasing the learning burden on minors, and prevent minors from becoming addicted to the internet.

Article 23 Parents or other guardians of minors shall not discriminate against minors based on gender, physical condition, intelligence, etc., shall not commit domestic violence, and shall not coerce, lure, instigate, condone, or use minors to engage in activities that violate laws, regulations, and social morality.

## Chapter III State Support

Article 24 The State Council shall organize relevant departments to formulate, revise, and promptly promulgate the national family education guidance outline.

People's governments at the provincial level or qualified people's governments at the city level divided into districts shall organize relevant departments to compile or adopt family education guidance reading materials suitable for local conditions, and formulate corresponding work norms and evaluation norms for family education guidance services.

Article 25 People's governments at or above the provincial level shall organize relevant departments to coordinate the construction of a family education information sharing service platform, open public welfare online parent schools and online courses, set up service hotlines, and provide online family education guidance services.

Article 26 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen supervision and management, reduce the homework burden and off-campus training burden of students in compulsory education, smooth the communication channels between schools and families, and promote the mutual cooperation of school education and family education.

Article 27 Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall organize the establishment of professional teams for family education guidance services, strengthen the training of professionals, and encourage social workers and volunteers to participate in family education guidance services.

Article 28 Local people's governments at or above the county level may, based on local actual conditions and needs, determine family education guidance institutions through various channels and methods.

Family education guidance institutions shall guide community parent schools, school parent schools, and other family education guidance service sites within their jurisdiction, and simultaneously carry out family education research, team building and training of service personnel, and research and development of public service products.

Article 29 Family education guidance institutions shall promptly provide services to families in need.

For families where parents or other guardians have certain difficulties in fulfilling their family education responsibilities, family education guidance institutions shall, based on specific circumstances, cooperate with relevant departments to provide targeted services.

Article 30 People's governments at the city level divided into districts, county level, and township level shall, based on local conditions, take measures to establish files for families of left-behind minors and minors in distress, provide care services such as life assistance, entrepreneurship and employment support, and create conditions for the parents or other guardians of left-behind minors and minors in distress to implement family education.

Education administrative departments and women's federations shall take targeted measures to provide services for the parents or other guardians of left-behind minors and minors in distress to implement family education, guide them to actively pay attention to the physical and mental health status of minors, and strengthen family affection and care.

Article 31 When carrying out family education guidance service activities, family education guidance institutions shall not organize or covertly organize for-profit education and training.

Article 32 Marriage registration agencies and adoption registration agencies shall, through on-site consultation and guidance, playing educational videos, etc., publicize family education knowledge to parties handling marriage registration or adoption registration, and provide family education guidance.

Article 33 Child welfare institutions and minor rescue and protection institutions shall provide family education guidance to the foster families arranged by the institution and the parents or other guardians of minors receiving rescue and protection.
Article 34 When hearing divorce cases, people's courts shall provide family education guidance to both spouses who have minor children.

Article 35 Women's federations shall play the unique role of women in carrying forward the family virtues of the Chinese nation and establishing good family traditions, publicize and popularize family education knowledge, organize and carry out family education practice activities through multiple channels such as family education guidance institutions, community parent schools, and civilized family construction, and provide family education guidance services.

Article 36 Natural persons, legal persons, and unincorporated organizations may lawfully establish non-profit family education service institutions.

Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments may adopt support measures such as government subsidies, rewards and incentives, and service procurement to cultivate family education service institutions.

Relevant departments such as education, civil affairs, health, and market supervision and administration shall, within their respective duties, guide and supervise family education service institutions and their practitioners according to law.

Article 37 State organs, enterprises, public institutions, mass organizations, and social organizations shall incorporate family tradition building into their organizational culture construction and support employees in participating in relevant family education service activities.

In the creation activities of civilized cities, civilized villages and towns, civilized units, civilized communities, civilized campuses, and civilized families, the situation of family education shall be taken as an important content.

## Chapter IV Social Coordination

Article 38 Residents' committees and villagers' committees may, relying on urban and rural community public service facilities, set up family education guidance service sites such as community parent schools, cooperate with family education guidance institutions to organize family education knowledge publicity for residents and villagers, and provide family education guidance services for parents or other guardians of minors.

Article 39 Primary and secondary schools and kindergartens shall incorporate family education guidance services into their work plans and as part of teacher professional training.

Article 40 Primary and secondary schools and kindergartens may, by establishing parent schools or other means, regularly organize public welfare family education guidance services and practical activities based on the characteristics of minors of different ages, and promptly contact and urge parents or other guardians of minors to participate.

Article 41 Primary and secondary schools and kindergartens shall, based on the needs of parents, invite relevant personnel to teach family education concepts, knowledge, and methods, organize and carry out family education guidance services and practical activities, and promote joint education by families and schools.

Article 42 Qualified primary and secondary schools and kindergartens shall, under the guidance of education administrative departments, provide support for family education guidance service sites to carry out public welfare family education guidance service activities.

Article 43 If a primary or secondary school discovers that a minor student has seriously violated school rules and discipline, it shall promptly stop and discipline the student, inform his or her parents or other guardians, and provide targeted family education guidance services to his or her parents or other guardians; if a minor student is found to have misbehavior or serious misbehavior, it shall be handled in accordance with relevant legal provisions.
Article 44 Infant and toddler care service institutions and early education service institutions shall provide family education guidance services such as scientific parenting guidance to parents or other guardians of minors.

Article 45 When providing services such as premarital health care, maternal health care, child health care, and vaccination, medical and health care institutions shall provide publicity and guidance on scientific parenting knowledge and early infant and toddler development to relevant adults, parents or other guardians of minors.

Article 46 Public cultural service institutions such as libraries, museums, cultural centers, memorial halls, art galleries, science and technology museums, sports venues, youth palaces, and children's activity centers, as well as patriotic education bases, shall regularly carry out public welfare family education publicity, family education guidance services, and practical activities each year, and develop public cultural service products related to family education.

News media such as radio, television, newspapers, and the internet shall publicize correct family education knowledge, disseminate scientific family education concepts and methods, and create a good social atmosphere that values family education.

Article 47 Family education service institutions shall strengthen self-discipline management, formulate family education service norms, organize training for practitioners, and improve the professional quality and ability of practitioners.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 48 If residents' committees, villagers' committees, women's federations in the place where a minor resides, the work unit of the minor's parents or other guardians, as well as primary and secondary schools, kindergartens, and other units that have close contact with minors, discover that parents or other guardians refuse or neglect to fulfill their family education responsibilities, or illegally obstruct other guardians from implementing family education, they shall criticize, educate, admonish, and stop such behavior, and if necessary, urge them to accept family education guidance.

If parents or other guardians of a minor lawfully entrust another person to care for the minor, and a relevant unit discovers that the entrusted person fails to fulfill family education responsibilities according to law, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply.

Article 49 If, in the process of handling cases, public security organs, people's procuratorates, or people's courts discover that a minor has serious misconduct or has committed a criminal act, or that the parents or other guardians of a minor have not correctly implemented family education, infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of the minor, they shall, depending on the circumstances, admonish the parents or other guardians and may order them to accept family education guidance.
Article 50 If a government department or institution responsible for family education work falls under any of the following circumstances, its superior organ or competent unit shall order it to make corrections within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished according to law:

(1) Failing to perform family education work duties;

(2) Intercepting, misappropriating, diverting, or falsely reporting or claiming family education work funds;

(3) Other circumstances of abusing power, neglecting duty, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

Article 51 If a family education guidance institution, primary or secondary school, kindergarten, infant and toddler care service institution, or early education service institution violates the provisions of this Law by failing to perform or correctly performing its family education guidance service duties, the competent department shall order it to make corrections within a time limit; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be punished according to law.

Article 52 If a family education service institution falls under any of the following circumstances, the competent department shall order it to make corrections within a time limit; if it refuses to make corrections or the circumstances are serious, the competent department shall order it to suspend operations for rectification, revoke its business license, or cancel its registration:

(1) Failing to go through the establishment procedures according to law;

(2) Engaging in activities beyond the scope of its licensed business or making false or misleading publicity, resulting in adverse consequences;

(3) Infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of minors and their parents or other guardians.Article 53 If parents or other guardians of a minor commit domestic violence against the minor during the process of family education, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Minors, the Law of the People's Republic of China on Domestic Violence, and other laws.

Article 54 Any violation of the provisions of this Law that constitutes a violation of public security administration shall be subject to public security administration penalties by the public security organs in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 55 This Law shall come into force on January 1, 2022.
