# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Red Cross Society

Adopted at the 4th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on October 31, 1993

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Revised at the 26th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on February 24, 2017

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to protect human life and health, uphold human dignity, promote the spirit of humanitarianism, advance the cause of peace and progress, and ensure and regulate the Red Cross Society's performance of its duties in accordance with the law.

Article 2 The Red Cross Society of China is the unified Red Cross organization of the People's Republic of China and a social relief organization engaged in humanitarian work.

Article 3 Citizens of the People's Republic of China, regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, religious belief, or level of education, may voluntarily join the Red Cross Society of China if they recognize its Constitution and pay membership dues.

Enterprises, public institutions, and relevant organizations may become group members of the Red Cross Society upon application.

The State encourages natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations to participate in Red Cross voluntary services.

The State supports the development of Red Cross youth work in schools.

Article 4 The Red Cross Society of China shall abide by the Constitution and laws, follow the fundamental principles established by the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, and carry out its work independently in accordance with the Geneva Conventions and their Additional Protocols ratified or acceded to by China, as well as the Constitution of the Red Cross Society of China.

The National Congress of the Red Cross Society of China formulates or amends the Constitution of the Red Cross Society of China in accordance with the law, and the Constitution shall not contravene the Constitution or laws.

Article 5 People's governments at all levels shall provide support and funding to the Red Cross Society, ensure that it performs its duties in accordance with the law, and supervise its activities.

Article 6 The Red Cross Society of China shall develop friendly relations and cooperation with Red Cross and Red Crescent societies of other countries based on the principles of independence, equality, and mutual respect.

## Chapter II Organization

Article 7 The China Red Cross National Headquarters shall be established nationwide. The China Red Cross National Headquarters represents the Red Cross Society of China externally.

Local Red Cross societies at or above the county level shall be established according to administrative divisions, with full-time staff allocated based on actual work needs.

National industries may establish industry Red Cross societies as needed.

Higher-level Red Cross societies shall guide the work of lower-level Red Cross societies.

Article 8 Red Cross societies at all levels shall establish councils and supervisory boards. The councils and supervisory boards shall be elected by the congress of members, be accountable to and report to the congress of members, and accept its supervision.

The council shall democratically elect a president and vice presidents. The council shall implement the resolutions of the congress of members.

The executive committee is the standing executive body of the council, with its membership determined by the council, and it shall be accountable to and report to the council.

The supervisory board shall democratically elect a chairperson and vice chairpersons. The work of the council and the executive committee shall be subject to the supervision of the supervisory board.

Article 9 The China Red Cross National Headquarters may appoint honorary presidents and honorary vice presidents. The honorary presidents and honorary vice presidents shall be invited by the council of the China Red Cross National Headquarters.

Article 10 The China Red Cross National Headquarters has the status of a social organization legal person; local Red Cross societies at all levels and industry Red Cross societies shall obtain the status of a social organization legal person in accordance with the law.

## Chapter III Duties

Article 11 The Red Cross Society shall perform the following duties:

(1) Carry out work related to rescue and disaster relief, and establish a Red Cross emergency rescue system. Provide emergency rescue and humanitarian assistance to the injured, sick, and other victims during emergencies such as war, armed conflict, natural disasters, accidents, public health incidents, etc.;

(2) Conduct emergency rescue training, disseminate knowledge on emergency rescue, disaster prevention and avoidance, and health, and organize volunteers to participate in on-site rescue;

(3) Participate in and promote unpaid blood donation, body and human organ donation, and participate in work related to hematopoietic stem cell donation;

(4) Organize and carry out Red Cross voluntary services and Red Cross youth work;

(5) Participate in international humanitarian rescue operations;

(6) Publicize the fundamental principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement and the Geneva Conventions and their Additional Protocols;

(7) Complete tasks entrusted by the people's government in accordance with the fundamental principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement;

(8) Carry out work in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Geneva Conventions and their Additional Protocols;

(9) Assist the people's government in carrying out other humanitarian service activities related to its duties.

Article 12 During emergencies such as war, armed conflict, natural disasters, accidents, and public health incidents, personnel, materials, and means of transport performing rescue and relief tasks and bearing the Red Cross emblem shall have the right of priority passage.

Article 13 No organization or individual may obstruct Red Cross staff from performing their duties of rescue, relief, and first aid in accordance with the law.

## Chapter IV Emblem and Name

Article 14 The Red Cross Society of China uses the red cross emblem on a white background.

The Red Cross emblem has a protective function and an indicative function.

The protective use of the Red Cross emblem indicates personnel, equipment, and facilities that must be respected and protected during war and armed conflict. The methods for its use shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Geneva Conventions and their Additional Protocols.

The indicative use of the Red Cross emblem indicates persons or objects related to Red Cross activities. The methods for its use shall be prescribed by the State Council and the Central Military Commission in accordance with this Law.

Article 15 Medical and health institutions of the State armed forces using the Red Cross emblem shall comply with the relevant provisions of the Geneva Conventions and their Additional Protocols.

Article 16 The Red Cross emblem and name are protected by law. It is prohibited to use the Red Cross emblem and name for profit, and it is prohibited to impersonate, misuse, or alter the Red Cross emblem and name in any form.

## Chapter V Property and Supervision

Article 17 The main sources of property for the Red Cross Society are:

(1) Membership dues paid by Red Cross members;

(2) Donations of money and goods from domestic and foreign organizations and individuals;

(3) Income from movable and immovable property;

(4) Allocations from the people's government;

(5) Other lawful income.

Article 18 The State provides support for public welfare undertakings established by the Red Cross Society that are consistent with its purposes.

Article 19 The Red Cross Society may conduct fundraising activities in accordance with the law. Fundraising activities shall comply with the relevant provisions of the Charity Law of the People's Republic of China.

Article 20 When the Red Cross Society accepts donations of money and goods from natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations in accordance with the law, it shall issue to the donors a receipt for public welfare donations uniformly supervised (printed) by the financial department. If the donor is anonymous or waives the right to receive the donation receipt, the Red Cross Society shall keep relevant records.

Donors shall enjoy tax benefits in accordance with the law.

Article 21 The Red Cross Society shall dispose of the donated money and goods it accepts in accordance with the fundraising plan, the donor's wishes, or the donation agreement.

Donors have the right to inquire about and copy relevant materials concerning the management and use of their donated property, and the Red Cross Society shall promptly and proactively provide feedback to donors on the relevant situation.

If the Red Cross Society violates the fundraising plan, the donor's wishes, or the purposes agreed upon in the donation agreement, and abuses the donated property, the donor has the right to demand correction; if the Red Cross Society refuses to correct, the donor may file a complaint or report to the civil affairs department of the people's government, or file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 22 The Red Cross Society shall establish systems for financial management, internal control, audit disclosure, and supervision and inspection.

The use of the Red Cross Society's property shall be consistent with its purposes.

The Red Cross Society shall establish a special review and supervision system for donations of money and goods accepted from abroad.

The Red Cross Society shall promptly engage an independent third-party institution established in accordance with the law to audit the income and use of donated money and goods, report the audit results to the council and supervisory board of the Red Cross Society, and make them public.

Article 23 The Red Cross Society shall establish a sound information disclosure system, standardize information release, and promptly disclose to the public the income and use of donated money and goods on a unified information platform, subject to public supervision.

Article 24 The income and use of the Red Cross Society's property shall be subject to supervision by the audit and other departments of the people's government in accordance with the law.

The acceptance of social donations by the Red Cross Society and their use shall be subject to supervision by the civil affairs department of the people's government in accordance with the law.

Article 25 No organization or individual may privately distribute, misappropriate, intercept, or embezzle the property of the Red Cross Society.

## Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 26 If the Red Cross Society or its staff members commit any of the following acts, the audit, civil affairs, and other departments of the people's government at the same level shall order correction; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if damage is caused, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Violating the fundraising plan, the donor's wishes, or the donation agreement by disposing of the accepted donated money and goods without authorization;

(2) Privately distributing, misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling property;

(3) Failing to provide feedback to donors or issue donation receipts in accordance with the law;

(4) Failing to audit the income and use of donated money and goods in accordance with the law;

(5) Failing to disclose information in accordance with the law;

(6) Other circumstances provided for by laws and regulations.

Article 27 If a natural person, legal person, or other organization commits any of the following acts, causing damage, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a violation of public security administration, public security administration penalties shall be imposed in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Impersonating, misusing, or altering the Red Cross emblem and name;

(2) Using the Red Cross emblem and name for profit;

(3) Fabricating, publishing, or disseminating false information, damaging the reputation of the Red Cross Society;

(4) Stealing, damaging, or otherwise infringing upon the property of the Red Cross Society;

(5) Obstructing Red Cross staff from performing their duties of rescue, relief, and first aid in accordance with the law;

(6) Other circumstances provided for by laws and regulations.

If the Red Cross Society or its staff members commit the acts listed in items (1) and (2) of the preceding paragraph, they shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 28 If the relevant departments of the people's government at all levels or their staff members abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain during the implementation of supervision and management, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 29 The term "fundamental principles established by the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement" as used in this Law refers to the seven fundamental principles of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity, and universality established in the "Statutes of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement" adopted at the 25th International Conference of the Red Cross in Geneva in October 1986.

The term "Geneva Conventions" as used in this Law refers to the four Geneva Conventions concluded on August 12, 1949, ratified by China, namely: the "Geneva Convention for the Amelioration of the Condition of the Wounded and Sick in Armed Forces in the Field," the "Geneva Convention for the Amelioration of the Condition of Wounded, Sick and Shipwrecked Members of Armed Forces at Sea," the "Geneva Convention Relative to the Treatment of Prisoners of War," and the "Geneva Convention Relative to the Protection of Civilian Persons in Time of War."

The term "Additional Protocols" to the Geneva Conventions as used in this Law refers to the "Protocol Additional to the Geneva Conventions of 12 August 1949, and relating to the Protection of Victims of International Armed Conflicts (Protocol I)" and the "Protocol Additional to the Geneva Conventions of 12 August 1949, and relating to the Protection of Victims of Non-International Armed Conflicts (Protocol II)," concluded on June 8, 1977, acceded to by China.

Article 30 This Law shall come into force on May 8, 2017.
