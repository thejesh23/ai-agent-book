# Law of the People's Republic of China on Prevention and Control of Occupational Diseases

Adopted at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 27, 2001

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on Prevention and Control of Occupational Diseases at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on December 31, 2011

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Six Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Energy Conservation at the 21st Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on July 2, 2016

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Eleven Laws Including the Law of the People's Republic of China on Accounting at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on November 4, 2017

Fourth Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China at the Seventh Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 29, 2018

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution for the purposes of preventing, controlling and eliminating occupational disease hazards, preventing and treating occupational diseases, protecting the health and related rights and interests of workers, and promoting economic and social development.

Article 2 This Law applies to activities for the prevention and control of occupational diseases within the territory of the People's Republic of China.

For the purposes of this Law, "occupational diseases" refer to diseases caused by workers of employers such as enterprises, public institutions, and individual economic organizations, during their occupational activities, due to exposure to dust, radioactive substances, and other toxic or harmful factors.

The classification and catalogue of occupational diseases shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the health administrative department of the State Council jointly with the labor and social security administrative department of the State Council.

Article 3 The work on prevention and control of occupational diseases shall adhere to the principles of prevention first and combining prevention with treatment, establish a mechanism featuring employer responsibility, administrative supervision, industry self-discipline, worker participation, and social oversight, and implement classified management and comprehensive treatment.

Article 4 Workers shall enjoy the right to occupational health protection in accordance with the law.

Employers shall create working environments and conditions that meet national occupational health standards and hygiene requirements for their workers, and take measures to ensure that workers receive occupational health protection.

Trade union organizations shall, in accordance with the law, supervise the work on prevention and control of occupational diseases and protect the legitimate rights and interests of workers. When formulating or amending rules and regulations concerning the prevention and control of occupational diseases, employers shall solicit the opinions of trade union organizations.

Article 5 Employers shall establish and improve the responsibility system for the prevention and control of occupational diseases, strengthen the management of such prevention and control, enhance the level of prevention and control, and bear responsibility for the occupational disease hazards arising from their units.

Article 6 The principal responsible persons of employers shall be fully responsible for the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their units.

Article 7 Employers must participate in work-related injury insurance in accordance with the law.

The labor and social security administrative departments of the State Council and of local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the supervision and management of work-related injury insurance to ensure that workers enjoy work-related injury insurance benefits in accordance with the law.

Article 8 The State encourages and supports the research, development, promotion, and application of new technologies, new processes, new equipment, and new materials that are conducive to the prevention and control of occupational diseases and the protection of workers' health; strengthens basic research on the mechanisms and occurrence patterns of occupational diseases; improves the scientific and technological level of occupational disease prevention and control; actively adopts effective technologies, processes, equipment, and materials for occupational disease prevention and control; and restricts the use of or phases out technologies, processes, equipment, and materials that cause serious occupational disease hazards.

The State encourages and supports the construction of medical and rehabilitation institutions for occupational diseases.

Article 9 The State implements a system of occupational health supervision.

The health administrative department and the labor and social security administrative department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law and the responsibilities determined by the State Council, be responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of occupational diseases nationwide. The relevant departments of the State Council shall be responsible for the relevant supervision and management of the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their respective scopes of duties.

The health administrative departments and the labor and social security administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective responsibilities, be responsible for the supervision and management of the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their respective administrative regions. The relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the relevant supervision and management of the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their respective scopes of duties.

The health administrative departments and the labor and social security administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level (hereinafter collectively referred to as the occupational health supervision and administration departments) shall strengthen communication, cooperate closely, exercise their powers and assume responsibilities in accordance with the law according to their respective division of duties.

Article 10 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate plans for the prevention and control of occupational diseases, incorporate them into the national economic and social development plans, and organize their implementation.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall be uniformly responsible for, lead, organize, and coordinate the work on the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their respective administrative regions; establish and improve the systems and mechanisms for the prevention and control of occupational diseases; uniformly lead and command the response to occupational health emergencies; strengthen the capacity building and service system construction for the prevention and control of occupational diseases; and improve and implement the responsibility system for the prevention and control of occupational diseases.

The people's governments of townships, nationality townships, and towns shall conscientiously implement this Law and support the occupational health supervision and administration departments in performing their duties in accordance with the law.

Article 11 The occupational health supervision and administration departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen publicity and education on the prevention and control of occupational diseases, popularize knowledge about the prevention and control of occupational diseases, enhance employers' awareness of occupational disease prevention and control, and improve workers' awareness of occupational health, self-protection, and their ability to exercise their rights to occupational health protection.

Article 12 The national occupational health standards concerning the prevention and control of occupational diseases shall be formulated and published by the health administrative department of the State Council.

The health administrative department of the State Council shall organize and carry out monitoring of key occupational diseases and special investigations, assess occupational health risks, and provide a scientific basis for formulating occupational health standards and policies for the prevention and control of occupational diseases.

The health administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall regularly conduct statistics and investigation and analysis of the situation regarding the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their respective administrative regions.

Article 13 Any unit or individual has the right to report and accuse any violation of this Law. The relevant departments shall promptly handle such reports and accusations after receiving them.

Units and individuals that achieve outstanding results in the prevention and control of occupational diseases shall be rewarded.

## Chapter II Preliminary Prevention

Article 14 Employers shall, in accordance with the requirements of laws and regulations, strictly abide by national occupational health standards, implement measures for the prevention of occupational diseases, and control and eliminate occupational disease hazards at the source.

Article 15 In addition to meeting the establishment conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations, the workplaces of employers that generate occupational disease hazards shall also meet the following occupational health requirements:

(1) The intensity or concentration of occupational disease hazard factors complies with national occupational health standards;
(2) There are facilities corresponding to the protection against occupational disease hazards;
(3) The production layout is reasonable, complying with the principle of separating hazardous from non-hazardous operations;
(4) There are supporting sanitary facilities such as changing rooms, shower rooms, and rest rooms for pregnant workers;
(5) Facilities such as equipment, tools, and appliances meet the requirements for protecting the physical and mental health of workers;
(6) Other requirements stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and the health administrative department of the State Council regarding the protection of workers' health.

Article 16 The State establishes a declaration system for projects involving occupational disease hazards.

If the workplaces of employers contain hazard factors listed in the catalogue of occupational diseases, they shall promptly and truthfully declare the hazardous projects to the local health administrative department and accept supervision.

The classified catalogue of occupational disease hazard factors shall be formulated, adjusted, and published by the health administrative department of the State Council. The specific measures for declaring projects involving occupational disease hazards shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 17 For new construction, expansion, or renovation projects, as well as technological transformation or technology introduction projects (hereinafter collectively referred to as construction projects) that may generate occupational disease hazards, the construction entity shall conduct a pre-assessment of the occupational disease hazards during the feasibility study stage.

For medical institution construction projects that may generate radioactive occupational disease hazards, the construction entity shall submit a pre-assessment report on radioactive occupational disease hazards to the health administrative department. The health administrative department shall, within thirty days of receiving the pre-assessment report, make a review decision and notify the construction entity in writing. If no pre-assessment report is submitted, or if the pre-assessment report has not been reviewed and approved by the health administrative department, construction shall not commence.

The pre-assessment report on occupational disease hazards shall evaluate the occupational disease hazard factors that may be generated by the construction project and their impact on the workplace and workers' health, determine the hazard category, and specify the occupational disease protection measures.

The measures for the classified management of occupational disease hazards in construction projects shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 18 The costs required for occupational disease protection facilities in construction projects shall be included in the project budget and shall be designed, constructed, and put into production and use simultaneously with the main project.

The design of occupational disease protection facilities for construction projects shall comply with national occupational health standards and hygiene requirements; for construction projects of medical institutions with severe radioactive occupational disease hazards, the design of protection facilities shall be subject to review and approval by the health administrative department before construction can proceed.

Before the completion and acceptance of a construction project, the construction entity shall conduct an evaluation of the effectiveness of occupational disease hazard control.

When a medical institution construction project that may generate radioactive occupational disease hazards is completed and accepted, its radioactive occupational disease protection facilities may only be put into use after passing the acceptance inspection by the health administrative department; for other construction projects, the occupational disease protection facilities shall be organized for acceptance by the construction entity in accordance with the law, and may only be put into production and use after passing the acceptance inspection. The health administrative department shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the acceptance activities organized by the construction entity and the acceptance results.

Article 19 The State implements special management for operations involving radioactive substances, highly toxic substances, high-hazard dust, etc. The specific management measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter III Protection and Management During the Labor Process

Article 20 Employers shall adopt the following management measures for the prevention and control of occupational diseases:

(1) Establish or designate an occupational health management institution or organization, and equip it with full-time or part-time occupational health management personnel responsible for the prevention and control of occupational diseases within the unit;
(2) Formulate plans and implementation schemes for the prevention and control of occupational diseases;
(3) Establish and improve occupational health management systems and operating procedures;
(4) Establish and maintain occupational health archives and workers' health surveillance records;
(5) Establish and improve a system for monitoring and evaluating occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace;
(6) Establish and improve emergency rescue plans for occupational disease hazard accidents.

Article 21 Employers shall ensure the necessary funding for the prevention and control of occupational diseases, shall not misappropriate or divert such funds, and shall bear responsibility for consequences resulting from insufficient funding.

Article 22 Employers must use effective occupational disease protection facilities and provide workers with personal protective equipment for occupational diseases.

The personal protective equipment for occupational diseases provided by employers to workers must meet the requirements for preventing and controlling occupational diseases; those that do not meet the requirements shall not be used.

Article 23 Employers shall prioritize the adoption of new technologies, new processes, new equipment, and new materials that are conducive to the prevention and control of occupational diseases and the protection of workers' health, gradually replacing technologies, processes, equipment, and materials that cause serious occupational disease hazards.

Article 24 Employers that generate occupational disease hazards shall set up bulletin boards in conspicuous locations to publicize the rules and regulations on the prevention and control of occupational diseases, operating procedures, emergency rescue measures for occupational disease hazard accidents, and the test results of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace.

For workstations that generate severe occupational disease hazards, warning signs and warning notices in Chinese shall be placed in conspicuous locations. The warning notices shall specify the types of occupational disease hazards generated, their consequences, prevention measures, and emergency treatment measures.

Article 25 For toxic or harmful workplaces where acute occupational injuries may occur, employers shall install alarm devices, equip them with on-site first-aid supplies, washing equipment, emergency evacuation passages, and necessary decontamination areas.

For radioactive workplaces and the transportation and storage of radioactive isotopes, employers must equip protection devices and alarm devices, and ensure that workers exposed to radiation wear personal dosimeters.

Employers shall regularly maintain, inspect, and periodically test the performance and effectiveness of occupational disease protection equipment, emergency rescue facilities, and personal protective equipment for occupational diseases, ensuring they are in normal working condition, and shall not dismantle or stop using them without authorization.

Article 26 Employers shall implement daily monitoring of occupational disease hazard factors by designated personnel and ensure that the monitoring system is in normal operating condition.

Employers shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administrative department of the State Council, periodically test and evaluate occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace. The test and evaluation results shall be kept in the employer's occupational health archives, reported periodically to the local health administrative department, and disclosed to workers.

The testing and evaluation of occupational disease hazard factors shall be carried out by occupational health technical service institutions that are legally established and have obtained qualification recognition from the health administrative department of the State Council or the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the city divided into districts, according to their respective division of duties. The tests and evaluations conducted by occupational health technical service institutions shall be objective and truthful.

When it is found that occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace do not comply with national occupational health standards and hygiene requirements, the employer shall immediately take corresponding control measures; if the standards and requirements still cannot be met, the operations involving the occupational disease hazard factors must be stopped; operations may only resume after the hazard factors have been controlled and comply with national occupational health standards and hygiene requirements.

Article 27 Occupational health technical service institutions shall engage in the testing and evaluation of occupational disease hazard factors in accordance with the law and accept supervision and inspection by health administrative departments. Health administrative departments shall perform their supervisory duties in accordance with the law.

Article 28 Entities that supply equipment that may generate occupational disease hazards to employers shall provide instructions in Chinese and place warning signs and warning notices in Chinese in conspicuous locations on the equipment. The warning notices shall specify the equipment's performance, the occupational disease hazards that may be generated, precautions for safe operation and maintenance, occupational disease protection measures, and emergency treatment measures.

Article 29 Entities that supply chemicals, radioactive isotopes, or materials containing radioactive substances that may generate occupational disease hazards to employers shall provide instructions in Chinese. The instructions shall specify the product's characteristics, main ingredients, existing harmful factors, potential harmful consequences, precautions for safe use, occupational disease protection measures, and emergency treatment measures. The product packaging shall bear conspicuous warning signs and warning notices in Chinese. Storage areas for the aforementioned materials shall have dangerous goods signs or radioactive warning signs placed in designated locations.

For chemical materials related to occupational disease hazards that are used or imported for the first time in China, the user or importer, after obtaining approval from the relevant department of the State Council in accordance with state regulations, shall submit to the health administrative department of the State Council the toxicity identification of the chemical material and documents such as registration or import approval from the relevant departments.

The import of radioactive isotopes, radiation devices, and articles containing radioactive substances shall be handled in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 30 No unit or individual may produce, operate, import, or use equipment or materials that are explicitly prohibited by the State and may generate occupational disease hazards.

Article 31 No unit or individual may transfer operations that generate occupational disease hazards to units or individuals that do not have the conditions for occupational disease protection. Units or individuals without the conditions for occupational disease protection shall not accept operations that generate occupational disease hazards.

Article 32 Employers shall be aware of the occupational disease hazards generated by the technologies, processes, equipment, and materials they adopt. If they adopt technologies, processes, equipment, or materials with occupational disease hazards while concealing such hazards, they shall bear responsibility for the resulting consequences of occupational disease hazards.

Article 33 When concluding a labor contract (including an employment contract, hereinafter the same) with a worker, the employer shall truthfully inform the worker of the occupational disease hazards that may arise during the work process, their consequences, occupational disease protection measures, and benefits, and shall specify these in the labor contract, without concealment or deception.

If, during the term of an existing labor contract, a worker's job position or work content changes, leading them to engage in operations involving occupational disease hazards that were not disclosed in the labor contract, the employer shall fulfill the obligation of truthful disclosure to the worker in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph and negotiate amendments to the relevant terms of the original labor contract.

If an employer violates the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs, the worker has the right to refuse to engage in operations involving occupational disease hazards, and the employer shall not terminate the labor contract concluded with the worker on this ground.

Article 34 The principal responsible persons and occupational health management personnel of employers shall receive occupational health training, comply with laws and regulations on the prevention and control of occupational diseases, and organize the prevention and control of occupational diseases within their units in accordance with the law.

Employers shall provide workers with pre-job occupational health training and regular on-the-job occupational health training, popularize occupational health knowledge, urge workers to comply with laws, regulations, rules, and operating procedures on the prevention and control of occupational diseases, and guide workers in the correct use of occupational disease protection equipment and personal protective equipment.

Workers shall learn and master relevant occupational health knowledge, enhance their awareness of occupational disease prevention, comply with laws, regulations, rules, and operating procedures on the prevention and control of occupational diseases, correctly use and maintain occupational disease protection equipment and personal protective equipment, and promptly report any potential hazards of occupational disease accidents.

If a worker fails to fulfill the obligations stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the employer shall provide education to the worker.

Article 35 For workers engaged in operations involving exposure to occupational disease hazards, employers shall, in accordance with the regulations of the health administrative department of the State Council, organize pre-job, periodic on-the-job, and off-job occupational health examinations, and inform the workers of the examination results in writing. The costs of occupational health examinations shall be borne by the employer.

Employers shall not assign workers who have not undergone pre-job occupational health examinations to engage in operations involving exposure to occupational disease hazards; shall not assign workers with occupational contraindications to engage in the operations they are contraindicated for; shall transfer workers found during occupational health examinations to have health damage related to their occupation from their original positions and make appropriate arrangements; and shall not terminate or dissolve the labor contract concluded with workers who have not undergone off-job occupational health examinations.

Occupational health examinations shall be conducted by medical and health institutions that have obtained a "Medical Institution Practice License." The health administrative department shall strengthen the standardized management of occupational health examination work, and the specific management measures shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 36 Employers shall establish occupational health surveillance records for workers and properly preserve them for the prescribed period.

Occupational health surveillance records shall include the worker's occupational history, history of exposure to occupational disease hazards, results of occupational health examinations, and diagnosis and treatment of occupational diseases, as well as other relevant personal health information.

When a worker leaves the employer, the worker has the right to obtain a copy of their personal occupational health surveillance records. The employer shall provide them truthfully and free of charge, and affix its seal to the copy provided.**Article 37** When an acute occupational disease hazard accident occurs or is likely to occur, the employer shall immediately take emergency rescue and control measures and promptly report to the local health administrative department and relevant authorities. Upon receiving the report, the health administrative department shall promptly organize an investigation and handling in conjunction with the relevant departments; when necessary, it may take temporary control measures. The health administrative department shall organize medical treatment efforts.

For workers who have suffered or are likely to suffer from acute occupational disease hazards, the employer shall promptly organize treatment, conduct health checks, and arrange medical observation, with all costs borne by the employer.

**Article 38** Employers shall not assign minor workers to jobs involving exposure to occupational disease hazards; nor shall they assign female workers during pregnancy or lactation to jobs that are harmful to themselves, their fetuses, or their infants.

**Article 39** Workers shall enjoy the following rights to occupational health protection:

(1) To receive occupational health education and training;

(2) To obtain occupational health examinations, occupational disease diagnosis and treatment, rehabilitation, and other occupational disease prevention services;

(3) To be informed of the occupational disease hazard factors that exist or may exist in the workplace, their consequences, and the protective measures to be taken;

(4) To request the employer to provide occupational disease prevention facilities and personal protective equipment that meet the requirements for preventing occupational diseases, and to improve working conditions;

(5) To criticize, report, and file charges against acts that violate laws and regulations on occupational disease prevention and that endanger life and health;

(6) To refuse illegal commands and being forced to perform work without occupational disease protective measures;

(7) To participate in the democratic management of the employer's occupational health work and to offer opinions and suggestions on occupational disease prevention.

Employers shall guarantee the exercise of the rights listed in the preceding paragraph by workers. Any act that reduces a worker's wages, benefits, or other treatment, or terminates or rescinds the labor contract with the worker, due to the worker's lawful exercise of legitimate rights, shall be invalid.

**Article 40** Trade union organizations shall urge and assist employers in carrying out occupational health publicity, education, and training; have the right to offer opinions and suggestions on the employer's occupational disease prevention work; sign special collective contracts on labor safety and health with the employer on behalf of workers according to law; and coordinate with and urge the employer to resolve issues concerning occupational disease prevention raised by workers.

Trade union organizations have the right to demand correction of acts by employers that violate laws and regulations on occupational disease prevention and infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of workers. When serious occupational disease hazards occur, they have the right to demand the adoption of protective measures or to suggest that relevant government departments take compulsory measures. When an occupational disease hazard accident occurs, they have the right to participate in the investigation and handling of the accident. When situations endangering the life and health of workers are discovered, they have the right to suggest that the employer organize the evacuation of workers from the dangerous site, and the employer shall handle the matter immediately.

**Article 41** Expenses incurred by the employer for the prevention and control of occupational disease hazards, workplace hygiene testing, health surveillance, and occupational health training, as required for occupational disease prevention, shall be itemized and included in production costs according to relevant state regulations.

**Article 42** The occupational health supervision and administration departments shall, according to their respective responsibilities, strengthen supervision and inspection of the employer's implementation of occupational disease prevention management measures, exercise their powers according to law, and assume corresponding responsibilities.

## Chapter IV: Diagnosis of Occupational Diseases and Protection of Occupational Disease Patients

**Article 43** The diagnosis of occupational diseases shall be undertaken by medical and health institutions that have obtained the "Medical Institution Practice License." The health administrative department shall strengthen the standardized management of occupational disease diagnosis work, with specific management measures formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council.

Medical and health institutions undertaking occupational disease diagnosis shall also meet the following conditions:

(1) Having medical and health technicians suitable for carrying out occupational disease diagnosis;

(2) Having instruments and equipment suitable for carrying out occupational disease diagnosis;

(3) Having a sound quality management system for occupational disease diagnosis.

Medical and health institutions undertaking occupational disease diagnosis shall not refuse a worker's request for occupational disease diagnosis.

**Article 44** Workers may undergo occupational disease diagnosis at a medical and health institution legally authorized to undertake such diagnosis, located in the place of the employer's business, the worker's registered residence, or the worker's habitual residence.

**Article 45** The diagnostic criteria for occupational diseases and the methods for diagnosis and appraisal of occupational diseases shall be formulated by the health administrative department of the State Council. The methods for appraising the grade of occupational disease disability shall be formulated by the labor and social security administrative department of the State Council jointly with the health administrative department of the State Council.

**Article 46** The diagnosis of an occupational disease shall comprehensively analyze the following factors:

(1) The patient's occupational history;

(2) The history of exposure to occupational disease hazards and the situation of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace;

(3) Clinical manifestations and results of auxiliary examinations, etc.

If there is no evidence to negate the inevitable link between the occupational disease hazard factors and the patient's clinical manifestations, the condition shall be diagnosed as an occupational disease.

The certificate of occupational disease diagnosis shall be signed by the practicing physician who participated in the diagnosis and is qualified for occupational disease diagnosis, and shall be reviewed and stamped by the medical and health institution undertaking the diagnosis.

**Article 47** The employer shall truthfully provide materials required for occupational disease diagnosis and appraisal, such as the worker's occupational history, history of exposure to occupational disease hazards, and test results of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace. The health administrative department shall supervise, inspect, and urge the employer to provide the aforementioned materials. Workers and relevant institutions shall also provide materials related to occupational disease diagnosis and appraisal.

When occupational disease diagnosis or appraisal institutions need to understand the situation of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace, they may conduct on-site investigations of the workplace or request the health administrative department to do so. The health administrative department shall organize an on-site investigation within ten days. The employer shall not refuse or obstruct.

**Article 48** During the process of occupational disease diagnosis or appraisal, if the employer fails to provide materials such as test results of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace, the diagnosis or appraisal institution shall make a diagnosis or appraisal conclusion based on the worker's clinical manifestations, auxiliary examination results, occupational history, history of exposure to occupational disease hazards, and with reference to the worker's own account and daily supervision and inspection information provided by the health administrative department.

If the worker disputes the materials provided by the employer, such as the test results of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace, or if the employer has been dissolved or declared bankrupt and no employer provides the aforementioned materials, the diagnosis or appraisal institution shall request the health administrative department to conduct an investigation. The health administrative department shall make a determination on the disputed materials or the situation of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace within thirty days from the date of receiving the application. Relevant departments shall cooperate.

**Article 49** During the process of occupational disease diagnosis or appraisal, when confirming the worker's occupational history and history of exposure to occupational disease hazards, if the parties dispute the labor relationship, type of work, job position, or duration of employment, they may apply for arbitration to the local labor and personnel dispute arbitration committee. The labor and personnel dispute arbitration committee that receives the application shall accept it and make an award within thirty days.

The parties have the responsibility to provide evidence for their claims during the arbitration process. If the worker is unable to provide evidence related to the arbitration claim that is under the control and management of the employer, the arbitration tribunal shall require the employer to provide it within a specified period. If the employer fails to provide it within the specified period, it shall bear adverse consequences.

If the worker is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court according to law.

If the employer is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, it may file a lawsuit with the People's Court according to law within fifteen days from the date of the conclusion of the occupational disease diagnosis or appraisal procedure. During the litigation period, the worker's treatment costs shall be paid through the channels stipulated for occupational disease treatment benefits.

**Article 50** When employers and medical and health institutions discover a worker with an occupational disease or a suspected occupational disease patient, they shall promptly report to the local health administrative department. If the disease is confirmed as an occupational disease, the employer shall also report to the local labor and social security administrative department. The department receiving the report shall handle the matter according to law.

**Article 51** The health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall be responsible for the management of occupational disease statistical reports within its administrative region and shall report them upwards according to regulations.

**Article 52** If a party disputes an occupational disease diagnosis, they may apply for an appraisal to the health administrative department of the local people's government at the place where the medical and health institution that made the diagnosis is located.

Disputes over occupational disease diagnosis shall be appraised by an occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee organized by the health administrative department of the local people's government at or above the city level divided into districts, upon the application of the parties.

If a party is dissatisfied with the appraisal conclusion of the occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee at the city level divided into districts, they may apply for a re-appraisal to the health administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 53** The occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee shall be composed of experts in relevant fields.

The health administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government shall establish a relevant expert database. When an appraisal of an occupational disease dispute is needed, the experts to serve on the diagnosis appraisal committee shall be determined by random selection from the expert database by the parties or by the relevant health administrative department entrusted by the parties.

The occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee shall conduct the diagnosis and appraisal in accordance with the occupational disease diagnostic criteria and the methods for diagnosis and appraisal of occupational diseases promulgated by the health administrative department of the State Council, and shall issue a certificate of occupational disease diagnosis and appraisal to the parties. The costs of occupational disease diagnosis and appraisal shall be borne by the employer.

**Article 54** Members of the occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee shall abide by professional ethics, conduct diagnosis and appraisal objectively and impartially, and bear corresponding responsibilities. Members of the occupational disease diagnosis appraisal committee shall not have private contact with the parties, accept property or other benefits from the parties, and shall withdraw if they have an interest in the parties.

When a People's Court accepts a relevant case and needs to conduct an occupational disease appraisal, it shall select experts for the appraisal from the relevant expert database legally established by the health administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

**Article 55** When a medical and health institution discovers a suspected occupational disease patient, it shall inform the worker personally and promptly notify the employer.

The employer shall promptly arrange for the diagnosis of the suspected occupational disease patient. During the period of diagnosis or medical observation of the suspected occupational disease patient, the employer shall not terminate or rescind the labor contract concluded with the patient.

The costs incurred by the suspected occupational disease patient during the period of diagnosis and medical observation shall be borne by the employer.

**Article 56** The employer shall guarantee that occupational disease patients enjoy the occupational disease treatment benefits stipulated by the state according to law.

The employer shall, according to relevant state regulations, arrange for the treatment, rehabilitation, and regular check-ups of occupational disease patients.

The employer shall transfer occupational disease patients who are no longer suitable for their original jobs to other positions and make proper arrangements.

The employer shall provide appropriate job subsidies to workers engaged in jobs involving exposure to occupational disease hazards.

**Article 57** The costs of diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation for occupational disease patients, as well as the social security for occupational disease patients who are disabled or have lost their ability to work, shall be handled in accordance with the state's regulations on work-related injury insurance.

**Article 58** In addition to enjoying work-related injury insurance according to law, if occupational disease patients still have the right to compensation under relevant civil laws, they have the right to claim compensation from the employer.

**Article 59** If a worker is diagnosed with an occupational disease but the employer has not participated in work-related injury insurance according to law, the medical and living expenses of the worker shall be borne by the employer.

**Article 60** If an occupational disease patient changes work units, the benefits they are entitled to according to law shall remain unchanged.

When an employer undergoes division, merger, dissolution, bankruptcy, or other situations, it shall conduct health checks on workers engaged in jobs involving exposure to occupational disease hazards and properly arrange for occupational disease patients according to relevant state regulations.

**Article 61** Occupational disease patients whose employer no longer exists or whose labor relationship cannot be confirmed may apply to the local people's government's medical security and civil affairs departments for medical assistance and living assistance.

Local people's governments at all levels shall, based on the actual conditions of their regions, take other measures to ensure that the occupational disease patients mentioned in the preceding paragraph receive medical treatment.

## Chapter V: Supervision and Inspection

**Article 62** The occupational health supervision and administration departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with laws and regulations on occupational disease prevention, national occupational health standards, and health requirements, and based on their respective responsibilities, conduct supervision and inspection of occupational disease prevention work.

**Article 63** When performing their supervision and inspection duties, the health administrative departments have the right to take the following measures:

(1) To enter the unit under inspection and the site of occupational disease hazards to understand the situation, investigate, and collect evidence;

(2) To review or copy materials related to acts violating laws and regulations on occupational disease prevention and to collect samples;

(3) To order units and individuals violating laws and regulations on occupational disease prevention to cease their illegal acts.

**Article 64** When an occupational disease hazard accident occurs or there is evidence proving that a hazardous state may lead to an occupational disease hazard accident, the health administrative department may take the following temporary control measures:

(1) To order the suspension of operations that caused the occupational disease hazard accident;

(2) To seal up materials and equipment that caused or may cause the occupational disease hazard accident;

(3) To organize the control of the site of the occupational disease hazard accident.

After the occupational disease hazard accident or hazardous state has been effectively controlled, the health administrative department shall promptly lift the control measures.

**Article 65** When occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel perform their duties according to law, they shall present their supervision and law enforcement credentials.

Occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel shall be loyal to their duties, enforce the law impartially, and strictly abide by law enforcement norms. If the secrets of the employer are involved, they shall keep them confidential.

**Article 66** When occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel perform their duties according to law, the unit under inspection shall accept the inspection and provide support and cooperation, and shall not refuse or obstruct.

**Article 67** When performing their duties, the health administrative departments and their occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Issuing certification documents or qualification certificates for construction projects, or granting approvals, to those that do not meet the statutory conditions;

(2) Failing to perform supervision and inspection duties for those who have already obtained relevant certification documents;

(3) Failing to promptly take control measures according to law upon discovering that an employer has occupational disease hazards that may cause an occupational disease hazard accident;

(4) Other acts violating this Law.

**Article 68** Occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel shall undergo qualification recognition according to law.

Occupational health supervision and administration departments shall strengthen team building, improve the political and professional quality of occupational health supervision and law enforcement personnel, establish and improve internal supervision systems in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations, and conduct supervision and inspection on the implementation of laws and regulations and compliance with discipline by their staff.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability

**Article 69** If a construction entity violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the health administrative department shall issue a warning and order correction within a specified period. If correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the entity shall be ordered to cease operations that generate occupational disease hazards, or the relevant people's government shall be requested to order the suspension of construction or closure within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council:

(1) Failing to conduct a pre-assessment of occupational disease hazards as required;

(2) For a medical institution's construction project that may generate radioactive occupational disease hazards, failing to submit a pre-assessment report on radioactive occupational disease hazards as required, or starting construction before the pre-assessment report has been reviewed and approved by the health administrative department;

(3) Failing to design, construct, and put into production and use the occupational disease prevention facilities simultaneously with the main project as required;

(4) The design of occupational disease prevention facilities for a construction project does not meet national occupational health standards and health requirements, or for a medical institution's construction project with severe radioactive occupational disease hazards, the design of prevention facilities is constructed without prior review and approval by the health administrative department;

(5) Failing to conduct a post-assessment of the effectiveness of occupational disease hazard control for the occupational disease prevention facilities as required;

(6) Before the construction project is completed and put into production and use, the occupational disease prevention facilities have not passed the acceptance check as required.

**Article 70** If any of the following acts are committed in violation of the provisions of this Law, the health administrative department shall issue a warning and order correction within a specified period. If correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not more than 100,000 yuan shall be imposed:

(1) Failing to file, report, or publish the test and evaluation results of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace;

(2) Failing to adopt the occupational disease prevention management measures stipulated in Article 20 of this Law;

(3) Failing to publicize the rules and regulations, operating procedures, and emergency rescue measures for occupational disease hazard accidents as required;

(4) Failing to organize occupational health training for workers as required, or failing to provide guidance and supervision on workers' personal occupational disease protection;

(5) For chemical materials related to occupational disease hazards used or imported for the first time domestically, failing to submit toxicity identification materials and documents registered or approved for import by relevant departments as required.

**Article 71** If an employer violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the health administrative department shall order correction within a specified period, issue a warning, and may concurrently impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to promptly and truthfully report to the health administrative department the projects that generate occupational disease hazards as required;

(2) Failing to implement daily monitoring of occupational disease hazard factors by a designated person, or the monitoring system cannot function normally;

(3) When concluding or modifying a labor contract, failing to inform the worker of the true situation of occupational disease hazards;

(4) Failing to organize occupational health examinations as required, establish occupational health surveillance files, or inform workers of the examination results in writing;

(5) Failing to provide copies of occupational health surveillance files when a worker leaves the employer, as required by this Law.

**Article 72** If an employer violates the provisions of this Law by committing any of the following acts, the health administrative department shall issue a warning and order correction within a specified period. If correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed. If the circumstances are serious, the employer shall be ordered to cease operations that generate occupational disease hazards, or the relevant people's government shall be requested to order closure within the limits of authority prescribed by the State Council:

(1) The intensity or concentration of occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace exceeds national occupational health standards;

(2) Failing to provide occupational disease prevention facilities and personal protective equipment, or the provided facilities and equipment do not meet national occupational health standards and health requirements;

(3) Failing to maintain, repair, test, or keep in normal operation and use the occupational disease prevention equipment, emergency rescue facilities, and personal protective equipment as required;

(4) Failing to test and evaluate occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace as required;

(5) When occupational disease hazard factors in the workplace still fail to meet national occupational health standards and health requirements after treatment, failing to cease operations involving those hazard factors;

(6) Failing to arrange for the diagnosis and treatment of occupational disease patients and suspected occupational disease patients as required;(7) Failing to immediately take emergency rescue and control measures or failing to report in a timely manner as required when an acute occupational disease hazard accident occurs or is likely to occur;

(8) Failing to set up warning signs and Chinese warning instructions in conspicuous positions at workstations where serious occupational disease hazards are generated, as required;

(9) Refusing inspections by the occupational health supervision and administration department;

(10) Concealing, falsifying, altering, or destroying occupational health monitoring files, workplace occupational disease hazard factor testing and evaluation results, or other related materials, or refusing to provide materials required for occupational disease diagnosis and identification;

(11) Failing to bear the costs of occupational disease diagnosis, identification, and the medical and living expenses of occupational disease patients as required.

Article 73: If equipment or materials that may cause occupational disease hazards are provided to an employer without providing Chinese instructions or setting up warning signs and Chinese warning instructions as required, the health administrative department shall order correction within a specified period, issue a warning, and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 74: If an employer or medical and health institution fails to report occupational diseases or suspected occupational diseases as required, the relevant competent authority shall, based on its division of responsibilities, order correction within a specified period, issue a warning, and may impose a fine of not more than 10,000 yuan; if fraud is involved, a fine of not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel may be given demotion or dismissal sanctions according to law.

Article 75: In violation of this Law, under any of the following circumstances, the health administrative department shall order remediation within a specified period and impose a fine of not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 300,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall order the cessation of operations that generate occupational disease hazards, or request the relevant people's government to order closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council:

(1) Concealing occupational disease hazards generated by technologies, processes, equipment, or materials and still adopting them;

(2) Concealing the true occupational health situation of the unit;

(3) Toxic or harmful workplaces, radiation workplaces, or the transport or storage of radioactive isotopes that may cause acute occupational injury do not comply with the provisions of Article 25 of this Law;

(4) Using equipment or materials that are explicitly prohibited by the state and may cause occupational disease hazards;

(5) Transferring operations that generate occupational disease hazards to units or individuals without occupational disease protection conditions, or units or individuals without occupational disease protection conditions accepting such operations;

(6) Unauthorized removal or cessation of use of occupational disease protection equipment or emergency rescue facilities;

(7) Arranging workers who have not undergone occupational health examinations, workers with occupational contraindications, minor workers, or female workers during pregnancy or lactation to engage in operations involving occupational disease hazards or prohibited operations;

(8) Giving illegal commands or forcing workers to perform operations without occupational disease protection measures.

Article 76: If equipment or materials that are explicitly prohibited by the state and may cause occupational disease hazards are produced, operated, or imported, penalties shall be imposed in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 77: If an employer violates this Law and has caused serious harm to the life and health of workers, the health administrative department shall order the cessation of operations that generate occupational disease hazards, or request the relevant people's government to order closure in accordance with the authority prescribed by the State Council, and impose a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan.

Article 78: If an employer violates this Law, causing a major occupational disease hazard accident or other serious consequences, and constitutes a crime, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

Article 79: If occupational health technical services are provided without obtaining qualification recognition, the health administrative department shall order the immediate cessation of the illegal act and confiscate the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 5,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than ten times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 5,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, dismissal, or expulsion sanctions according to law.

Article 80: If an institution providing occupational health technical services or a medical and health institution undertaking occupational disease diagnosis violates this Law by committing any of the following acts, the health administrative department shall order the immediate cessation of the illegal act, issue a warning, and confiscate the illegal gains; if the illegal gains are 5,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 5,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the original recognition or registration authority shall cancel its corresponding qualifications; the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, dismissal, or expulsion sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated according to law:

(1) Engaging in occupational health technical services or occupational disease diagnosis beyond the scope of qualification recognition or diagnosis item registration;

(2) Failing to perform statutory duties in accordance with this Law;

(3) Issuing false certification documents.

Article 81: If a member of the occupational disease diagnosis and identification committee accepts property or other benefits from a party to an occupational disease diagnosis dispute, a warning shall be issued, the accepted property shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 3,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed; their qualification as a member of the occupational disease diagnosis and identification committee shall be revoked, and they shall be removed from the expert database established by the health administrative department of the people's government of the province, autonomous region, or municipality directly under the Central Government.

Article 82: If the health administrative department fails to report occupational diseases and occupational disease hazard accidents as required, the higher-level administrative department shall order correction, circulate a notice of criticism, and issue a warning; if false reports or concealments are made, the person in charge of the unit, the directly responsible supervisor, and other directly responsible personnel shall be given demotion, dismissal, or expulsion sanctions according to law.

Article 83: If a local people's government at or above the county level fails to perform its duties under this Law in the prevention and control of occupational diseases, resulting in a major occupational disease hazard accident in its administrative region with serious social impact, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions ranging from a major demerit to expulsion according to law.

If the occupational health supervision and administration department of a people's government at or above the county level fails to perform its duties under this Law, abuses power, neglects its duties, or engages in malpractice for personal gain, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions of a major demerit or demotion according to law; if an occupational disease hazard accident or other serious consequences are caused, sanctions of dismissal or expulsion shall be given according to law.

Article 84: Violations of this Law that constitute a crime shall be investigated for criminal liability according to law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 85: The meanings of the following terms in this Law:

Occupational disease hazards refer to various hazards that may cause occupational diseases to workers engaged in occupational activities. Occupational disease hazard factors include: various harmful chemical, physical, and biological factors present in occupational activities, as well as other occupational harmful factors generated during the work process.

Occupational contraindications refer to a worker's special physiological or pathological state that makes them more susceptible to occupational disease hazards and contracting occupational diseases than the general occupational population when engaged in a specific occupation or exposed to specific occupational disease hazard factors, or that may lead to aggravation of their own existing diseases, or that may induce diseases that pose a danger to the life and health of others during the work process.

Article 86: Units other than employers specified in Article 2 of this Law that generate occupational disease hazards may refer to this Law for their occupational disease prevention and control activities.

Labor dispatch employing units shall fulfill the obligations of employers under this Law.

The measures for the People's Liberation Army to implement this Law by reference shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 87: The supervision and management of the control of radioactive occupational disease hazards in medical institutions shall be implemented by the health administrative department in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 88: This Law shall come into effect on May 1, 2002.
