# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention of Juvenile Delinquency

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 28, 1999

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Prevention of Juvenile Delinquency at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on October 26, 2012

Revised at the 24th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 26, 2020

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to safeguard the physical and mental health of juveniles, cultivate good conduct among juveniles, and effectively prevent juvenile delinquency.

Article 2 The prevention of juvenile delinquency shall be based on the combination of education and protection of juveniles, adhere to the principles of prevention first and early intervention, and carry out graded prevention, intervention, and correction of minor misconduct and serious misconduct of juveniles in a timely manner.

Article 3 In carrying out the work of preventing juvenile delinquency, the personal dignity of juveniles shall be respected, and their legitimate rights and interests such as the right to reputation, the right to privacy, and personal information shall be protected.

Article 4 The prevention of juvenile delinquency shall be subject to comprehensive governance under the organization of people's governments at all levels.

State organs, people's organizations, social organizations, enterprises and institutions, residents' committees, villagers' committees, schools, families, etc., shall each assume their own responsibilities and cooperate with each other to jointly carry out the work of preventing juvenile delinquency, promptly eliminate various negative factors that breed juvenile delinquency, and create a good social environment for the healthy physical and mental development of juveniles.

Article 5 The work responsibilities of people's governments at all levels in preventing juvenile delinquency are:

(1) Formulating work plans for the prevention of juvenile delinquency;

(2) Organizing relevant departments such as public security, education, civil affairs, culture and tourism, market supervision and administration, cyberspace administration, health, press and publication, film, radio and television, and judicial administration to carry out the work of preventing juvenile delinquency;

(3) Providing policy support and funding guarantees for the work of preventing juvenile delinquency;

(4) Inspecting the implementation of this Law and the execution of work plans;

(5) Organizing and carrying out publicity and education on the prevention of juvenile delinquency;

(6) Other work responsibilities for the prevention of juvenile delinquency.

Article 6 The State shall strengthen the construction of specialized schools to provide specialized education for juveniles with serious misconduct. Specialized education is an integral part of the national education system and an important protective disciplinary measure for educating and correcting juveniles with serious misconduct.

Provincial people's governments shall incorporate the development of specialized education and the construction of specialized schools into economic and social development plans. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall establish specialized education steering committees and set up specialized schools reasonably as needed.

The specialized education steering committee shall be composed of units such as education, civil affairs, finance, human resources and social security, public security, judicial administration, people's procuratorates, people's courts, the Communist Youth League, the Women's Federation, the Committee for the Care of the Next Generation, specialized schools, as well as personnel such as lawyers and social workers, to study and determine matters related to the teaching and management of specialized schools.

Specific measures for the construction of specialized schools and specialized education shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 7 Public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall be responsible for the work of preventing juvenile delinquency through specialized agencies or specialized personnel who have received professional training and are familiar with the physical and mental characteristics of juveniles.

Article 8 The Communist Youth League, the Women's Federation, trade unions, the Disabled Persons' Federation, the Committee for the Care of the Next Generation, the All-China Youth Federation, the All-China Students' Federation, the Young Pioneers, and relevant social organizations shall assist people's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, people's procuratorates, and people's courts in carrying out the work of preventing juvenile delinquency, cultivate social forces for the prevention of juvenile delinquency, and provide support services.

Article 9 The State encourages, supports, and guides social work service institutions and other social organizations to participate in work related to the prevention of juvenile delinquency, and strengthens supervision over them.

Article 10 No organization or individual may instigate, coerce, or entice a juvenile to commit minor misconduct or serious misconduct, or provide conditions for a juvenile to commit the above-mentioned acts.

Article 11 Juveniles shall abide by laws, regulations, and social public moral standards, foster a sense of self-respect, self-discipline, and self-improvement, enhance their ability to distinguish right from wrong and protect themselves, and consciously resist the temptation and infringement of various misconduct and illegal and criminal acts.

Article 12 In preventing juvenile delinquency, research on adolescent education, psychological care, psychological correction, and crime prevention strategies shall be strengthened in light of the physiological and psychological characteristics of juveniles at different ages.

Article 13 The State encourages and supports the construction of disciplines related to the prevention of juvenile delinquency, the establishment of specialties, personnel training, and scientific research, and carries out international exchanges and cooperation.

Article 14 The State shall commend and reward organizations and individuals that have achieved remarkable results in the work of preventing juvenile delinquency.

## Chapter II Education on Crime Prevention

Article 15 The State, society, schools, and families shall strengthen education on socialist core values for juveniles, carry out education on crime prevention, enhance juveniles' awareness of the rule of law, enable juveniles to develop a sense of abiding by laws and disciplines and guarding against illegal and criminal acts, and improve their self-control ability.

Article 16 The parents or other guardians of a juvenile bear direct responsibility for the juvenile's education on crime prevention. They shall perform their guardianship duties in accordance with the law, cultivate a good family tradition, and foster good conduct in the juvenile; if they discover any abnormal psychological or behavioral changes in the juvenile, they shall promptly understand the situation and provide education, guidance, and admonition, and shall not refuse or neglect to perform their guardianship duties.

Article 17 Education administrative departments and schools shall incorporate education on crime prevention into school teaching plans, and guide teaching staff to combine the characteristics of juveniles and adopt various methods to provide targeted education on crime prevention for juvenile students.

Article 18 Schools shall employ full-time or part-time teachers engaged in legal education, and may appoint vice principals for legal affairs and off-campus legal counselors from judicial and law enforcement organs, legal education and legal service institutions, and other units.

Article 19 Schools shall be equipped with full-time or part-time mental health education teachers to carry out mental health education. Schools may, based on actual conditions, cooperate with professional mental health institutions to establish mental health screening and early intervention mechanisms to prevent and solve students' psychological and behavioral abnormalities.

Schools shall strengthen communication with the parents or other guardians of juvenile students to jointly carry out mental health education for juvenile students; if they discover that a juvenile student may be suffering from a mental disorder, they shall immediately inform their parents or other guardians to take the student to a relevant professional institution for diagnosis and treatment.

Article 20 Education administrative departments shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, establish a system for the prevention and control of student bullying. Schools shall strengthen daily safety management, improve the workflow for detecting and handling student bullying, and strictly investigate and promptly eliminate various hidden dangers that may lead to student bullying.

Article 21 Education administrative departments encourage and support schools to hire social workers to be stationed in schools on a long-term or regular basis to assist in carrying out moral education, legal education, life education, and mental health education, and to participate in the prevention and handling of student bullying and other behaviors.

Article 22 Education administrative departments and schools shall, by organizing lectures, seminars, training, and other activities, introduce scientific and reasonable educational methods, and guide teaching staff, parents or other guardians of juvenile students to effectively prevent juvenile delinquency.

Schools shall inform the parents or other guardians of juvenile students of the education plan for crime prevention. The parents or other guardians of juvenile students shall cooperate with the school in providing targeted education on crime prevention for juvenile students.

Article 23 Education administrative departments shall incorporate the effectiveness of education on crime prevention into the annual assessment of schools.

Article 24 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments, people's procuratorates, people's courts, the Communist Youth League, the Young Pioneers, the Women's Federation, the Disabled Persons' Federation, the Committee for the Care of the Next Generation, etc., shall, based on actual conditions, organize and hold various forms of publicity and education activities on the prevention of juvenile delinquency. Where conditions permit, juvenile legal education bases may be established to carry out legal education for juveniles.

Article 25 Residents' committees and villagers' committees shall actively carry out targeted publicity activities on the prevention of juvenile delinquency, assist public security organs in maintaining public order around schools, promptly keep abreast of the guardianship, schooling, and employment status of juveniles within their jurisdiction, and organize and guide community social organizations to participate in the work of preventing juvenile delinquency.

Article 26 Off-campus activity venues such as youth palaces and children's activity centers shall take education on crime prevention as an important work content and carry out various forms of publicity and education activities.

Article 27 Vocational training institutions and employers, when providing vocational training to juveniles who have reached the age of sixteen and are preparing for employment, shall incorporate education on crime prevention into the training content.

## Chapter III Intervention in Minor Misconduct

Article 28 For the purposes of this Law, "minor misconduct" refers to the following acts committed by a juvenile that are detrimental to their healthy growth:

(1) Smoking or drinking alcohol;

(2) Repeatedly skipping classes or playing truant;

(3) Staying out all night without reason or running away from home;

(4) Being addicted to the internet;

(5) Associating with persons with bad habits in society, organizing or participating in gangs that commit minor misconduct;

(6) Entering places where juveniles are prohibited by laws and regulations from entering;

(7) Participating in gambling or disguised gambling, or participating in feudal superstitious or cult activities;

(8) Reading, watching, or listening to materials, audio-visual products, or online information that promote obscenity, pornography, violence, terror, extremism, etc.;

(9) Other minor misconduct detrimental to the healthy physical and mental development of juveniles.

Article 29 If parents or other guardians of a juvenile discover that the juvenile has committed minor misconduct, they shall promptly stop it and strengthen discipline.

Article 30 If public security organs, residents' committees, or villagers' committees discover that a juvenile within their jurisdiction has committed minor misconduct, they shall promptly stop it and urge their parents or other guardians to perform their guardianship duties in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Schools shall strengthen management and education for juvenile students who have committed minor misconduct and shall not discriminate against them; if the student refuses to correct the behavior or the circumstances are serious, the school may impose a punishment or take the following management and education measures depending on the situation:

(1) Giving a reprimand;

(2) Requiring compliance with specific codes of conduct;

(3) Requiring participation in specific thematic education;

(4) Requiring participation in on-campus service activities;

(5) Requiring acceptance of psychological counseling and behavioral intervention from social workers or other professionals;

(6) Other appropriate management and education measures.

Article 32 Schools and families shall strengthen communication and establish a home-school cooperation mechanism. If a school decides to take management and education measures against a juvenile student, it shall promptly inform their parents or other guardians; the parents or other guardians of the juvenile student shall support and cooperate with the school in carrying out management and education.

Article 33 If a juvenile student steals a small amount of property, or engages in student bullying such as beating, insulting, intimidating, or forcibly demanding property, and the circumstances are minor, the school may take corresponding management and education measures in accordance with Article 31 of this Law.

Article 34 If a juvenile student skips class or plays truant, the school shall promptly contact their parents or other guardians to understand the relevant situation; if there is no justifiable reason, the school and the parents or other guardians of the juvenile student shall urge them to return to school for study.

Article 35 If a juvenile stays out all night without reason or runs away from home, their parents or other guardians, or the boarding school they attend, shall promptly search for them and, if necessary, report to the public security organ.

Anyone who takes in a juvenile who stays out all night without reason or runs away from home shall promptly contact their parents or other guardians or the school they attend; if contact cannot be made, they shall promptly report to the public security organ.

Article 36 If a juvenile who stays out all night without reason, runs away from home, or wanders the streets is discovered by public security organs, public place management agencies, etc., or upon receiving a report, they shall promptly take effective protective measures, notify their parents or other guardians or the boarding school they attend, and if necessary, escort them back to their residence or school; if contact cannot be made with their parents or other guardians or the school, they shall escort the juvenile to a rescue and protection institution for assistance.

Article 37 If parents or other guardians of a juvenile, or a school, discover that a juvenile is organizing or participating in a gang that commits minor misconduct, they shall promptly stop it; if they find that the gang is suspected of illegal or criminal activities, they shall immediately report to the public security organ.

## Chapter IV Correction of Serious Misconduct

Article 38 For the purposes of this Law, "serious misconduct" refers to acts committed by a juvenile that are stipulated in the criminal law but are not subject to criminal punishment due to being under the statutory age of criminal responsibility, as well as the following acts that seriously endanger society:

(1) Gang fighting, chasing or intercepting others, forcibly taking or demanding property, or willfully damaging or occupying public or private property, constituting affray;

(2) Illegally carrying firearms, ammunition, crossbows, daggers, or other controlled instruments stipulated by the State;

(3) Beating, insulting, intimidating, or intentionally injuring another person's body;

(4) Stealing, looting, snatching, or intentionally damaging public or private property;

(5) Disseminating obscene materials, audio-visual products, or information;

(6) Engaging in prostitution or patronizing prostitutes, or performing obscene acts;

(7) Inhaling or injecting drugs, or providing drugs to others;

(8) Participating in gambling with relatively large stakes;

(9) Other acts that seriously endanger society.

Article 39 If parents or other guardians of a juvenile, schools, residents' committees, or villagers' committees discover that someone is instigating, coercing, or enticing a juvenile to commit serious misconduct, they shall immediately report to the public security organ. Upon receiving a report or discovering the above-mentioned circumstances, the public security organ shall promptly investigate and handle the matter in accordance with the law; if a juvenile's personal safety is threatened, it shall immediately take effective protective measures.

Article 40 Upon receiving a report or discovering that a juvenile has committed serious misconduct, the public security organ shall promptly stop it, investigate and handle it in accordance with the law, and may order their parents or other guardians to eliminate or mitigate the consequences of the illegal act and take strict disciplinary measures.

Article 41 For juveniles with serious misconduct, the public security organ may, based on the specific circumstances, take the following corrective education measures:

(1) Giving a reprimand;

(2) Ordering an apology and compensation for losses;

(3) Ordering a written statement of repentance;

(4) Ordering regular reports on activities;

(5) Ordering compliance with specific codes of conduct, and prohibiting the commission of specific acts, contact with specific persons, or entry into specific places;

(6) Ordering acceptance of psychological counseling and behavioral correction;

(7) Ordering participation in social service activities;

(8) Ordering acceptance of community supervision, where social organizations or relevant institutions educate, supervise, and restrain the juvenile in appropriate places;

(9) Other appropriate corrective education measures.

Article 42 When carrying out corrective education for a juvenile, the public security organ may, as needed, invite schools, residents' committees, villagers' committees, and social organizations such as social work service institutions to participate.

The parents or other guardians of the juvenile shall actively cooperate with the implementation of corrective education measures and shall not obstruct, hinder, or neglect them.

Article 43 If the parents or other guardians of a juvenile with serious misconduct, or the school the juvenile attends, are unable to discipline the juvenile or discipline is ineffective, they may apply to the education administrative department. After evaluation and approval by the specialized education steering committee, the education administrative department shall decide to send the juvenile to a specialized school for specialized education.

Article 44 If a juvenile falls under any of the following circumstances, after evaluation and approval by the specialized education steering committee, the education administrative department, in conjunction with the public security organ, may decide to send the juvenile to a specialized school for specialized education:

(1) Committing acts that seriously endanger society, with vile circumstances or causing serious consequences;

(2) Repeatedly committing acts that seriously endanger society;

(3) Refusing to accept or cooperate with the corrective education measures stipulated in Article 41 of this Law;

(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 45 If a juvenile commits an act stipulated in the criminal law but is not subject to criminal punishment due to being under the statutory age of criminal responsibility, after evaluation and approval by the specialized education steering committee, the education administrative department, in conjunction with the public security organ, may decide to carry out specialized corrective education for the juvenile.

Provincial people's governments shall, based on local actual conditions, designate at least one specialized school to set up specialized venues in the form of separate campuses or separate classes to carry out specialized corrective education for the juveniles mentioned in the preceding paragraph.

The specialized venues mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall implement closed-loop management. Public security organs and judicial administrative departments shall be responsible for the correction work of the juveniles, and education administrative departments shall undertake the education work for the juveniles.

Article 46 Specialized schools shall, at appropriate times each semester, promptly request the specialized education steering committee to evaluate the situation of juvenile students receiving specialized education. For those who, after evaluation, are deemed suitable to return to regular schools for study, the specialized education steering committee shall submit a written recommendation to the original decision-making authority, which shall decide whether to transfer the juvenile student back to a regular school for study.

If the original decision-making authority decides to transfer the juvenile student back to a regular school, the student's original school shall not refuse to accept them; if, due to special circumstances, it is not suitable to return to the original school, the education administrative department shall arrange for a school transfer.

Article 47 Specialized schools shall provide education and correction to juveniles receiving specialized education in a graded and classified manner, carry out targeted moral education, legal education, and mental health education, and provide vocational education based on actual conditions; for juveniles who have not completed compulsory education, they shall ensure that they continue to receive compulsory education.

The student status of juvenile students in specialized schools shall be retained in their original schools. If they meet the graduation requirements, the original schools shall issue graduation certificates.

Article 48 Specialized schools shall strengthen contact with the parents or other guardians of juveniles receiving specialized education, regularly provide feedback to them on the correction and education status of the juveniles, and facilitate visits by parents, other guardians, relatives, etc., to see the juveniles.

Article 49 If juveniles or their parents or other guardians are dissatisfied with the administrative decisions stipulated in this Chapter, they may initiate administrative reconsideration or administrative litigation in accordance with the law.

## Chapter V Prevention of Recidivism

Article 50 When handling juvenile criminal cases, public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall, based on the physiological and psychological characteristics of the juvenile and the circumstances of the crime, provide targeted legal education.When educating minors involved in criminal cases, if the participation of adult relatives other than their legal representatives, or teachers, counselors, etc., is conducive to rehabilitating and saving the minor, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall invite them to participate in relevant activities.

Article 51 When handling criminal cases involving minors, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts may conduct or entrust relevant social organizations or institutions to conduct social investigations into the minor suspect's or defendant's growth experience, causes of the crime, guardianship, education, and other circumstances; with the consent of the minor suspect or defendant and their legal representative, and based on actual needs, they may conduct psychological assessments of the minor suspect or defendant.

Reports from social investigations and psychological assessments may serve as references for handling cases and educating minors.

Article 52 When applying bail pending trial to a minor who has no fixed residence and cannot provide a guarantor, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, and people's courts shall designate a suitable adult as the guarantor, and may, when necessary, arrange for the minor on bail pending trial to receive social observation and protection.

Article 53 Minors who are detained, arrested, or serving sentences in juvenile correctional facilities shall be held, managed, and educated separately from adults. Community corrections for minors shall be conducted separately from those for adults.

For minors in the above situations who have not completed compulsory education, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall coordinate with the education administrative departments to ensure they continue to receive compulsory education.

Article 54 Juvenile correctional facilities and community correction institutions shall strengthen legal education for juvenile offenders and juvenile community correction recipients, and provide them with vocational education based on actual circumstances.

Article 55 Community correction institutions shall inform juvenile community correction recipients of the relevant provisions on placement and assistance, and cooperate with the departments responsible for placement and assistance to implement or resolve issues such as school enrollment and employment for juvenile community correction recipients.

Article 56 For minors released after serving their sentences, juvenile correctional facilities shall notify their parents or other guardians in advance to pick them up on time and assist in implementing placement and assistance measures. If there are no parents or other guardians, or if they cannot be identified, the juvenile correctional facility shall notify the judicial administrative department of the minor's original registered residence or place of residence in advance to arrange for someone to pick them up on time, and the civil affairs department or residents' committee or villagers' committee shall legally provide guardianship.

Article 57 The parents or other guardians of minors, as well as schools, residents' committees, and villagers' committees, shall take effective assistance and education measures for minors receiving community corrections or released after serving sentences, and assist judicial organs and relevant departments in carrying out placement and assistance work.

Residents' committees and villagers' committees may engage retired personnel, volunteers, or other individuals with excellent ideological and moral character, upright conduct, and enthusiasm for minor-related work to assist in the placement and assistance work specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 58 Minors released after serving sentences or receiving community corrections shall enjoy the same rights as other minors in terms of resuming studies, advancing in education, and employment, in accordance with the law, and no unit or individual shall discriminate against them.

Article 59 If a minor's criminal record is legally sealed, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, people's courts, and judicial administrative departments shall not provide it to any unit or individual, except when judicial organs need it for case handling or when relevant units conduct inquiries in accordance with national regulations. Units and individuals conducting inquiries in accordance with the law shall keep the relevant record information confidential.

Records of minors receiving specialized correctional education, specialized education, as well as records of administrative penalties, criminal compulsory measures, and non-prosecution, shall be subject to the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 60 The people's procuratorates shall supervise the prevention of re-offending among minors through the lawful exercise of their procuratorial powers.

## Chapter 6 Legal Liability

Article 61 If, during the handling of a case, the public security organs, people's procuratorates, or people's courts discover that the parents or other guardians of a minor who has engaged in serious misconduct fail to fulfill their guardianship duties in accordance with the law, they shall issue a reprimand and may order them to receive family education guidance.

Article 62 If a school or its faculty and staff violate the provisions of this Law by failing to fulfill their duties in preventing juvenile delinquency, or by abusing or discriminating against relevant minors, the education administration and other departments shall order corrections and issue a notice of criticism; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law. If the conduct constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organs shall impose public security administration penalties in accordance with the law.

If faculty or staff incite, coerce, or entice minors to engage in misconduct or serious misconduct, or if they have poor conduct with adverse effects, the education administrative department or school shall legally dismiss or discharge them.

Article 63 If anyone violates the provisions of this Law by discriminating against relevant minors in terms of resuming studies, advancing in education, or employment, the unit to which they belong or the education, human resources, and social security departments shall order corrections; if they refuse to correct, the directly responsible supervisors or other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 64 If relevant social organizations, institutions, or their staff abuse or discriminate against minors receiving social observation and protection, or issue false social investigation or psychological assessment reports, the civil affairs, judicial administration, and other departments shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors or other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law; if the conduct constitutes a violation of public security administration, the public security organs shall impose public security administration penalties.

Article 65 Anyone who incites, coerces, or entices a minor to engage in misconduct or serious misconduct, constituting a violation of public security administration, shall be subject to public security administration penalties by the public security organs in accordance with the law.

Article 66 If state organs or their staff abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in favoritism and fraud in the work of preventing juvenile delinquency, the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 67 Anyone who violates the provisions of this Law and constitutes a crime shall be investigated for criminal responsibility in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 68 This Law shall take effect on June 1, 2021.
