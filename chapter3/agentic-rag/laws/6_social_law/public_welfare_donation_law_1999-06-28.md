# Law of the People's Republic of China on Donations for Public Welfare

Adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 28, 1999

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to encourage donations, regulate donation and receipt activities, protect the legitimate rights and interests of donors, recipients, and beneficiaries, and promote the development of public welfare undertakings.

Article 2 This Law applies to natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations that voluntarily and gratuitously donate property to lawfully established public welfare social organizations and public welfare non-profit institutions for public welfare purposes.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "public welfare undertakings" refer to the following non-profit activities:

(1) Activities to provide disaster relief, alleviate poverty, and assist disabled persons and other disadvantaged social groups and individuals;

(2) Education, science, culture, public health, and sports undertakings;

(3) Environmental protection and construction of public facilities;

(4) Other social public and welfare undertakings that promote social development and progress.

Article 4 Donations shall be voluntary and gratuitous. Forced apportionment or disguised apportionment is prohibited, and no profit-making activities shall be conducted under the guise of donations.

Article 5 The use of donated property shall respect the donor's wishes, conform to public welfare purposes, and shall not be diverted to other uses.

Article 6 Donations shall comply with laws and regulations, shall not violate social ethics, and shall not harm the public interest or the legitimate rights and interests of other citizens.

Article 7 Property received by public welfare social organizations and its appreciation shall be social public property, protected by state law. No unit or individual may encroach upon, misappropriate, or damage it.

Article 8 The State encourages the development of public welfare undertakings and provides support and preferential treatment to public welfare social organizations and public welfare non-profit institutions.

The State encourages natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations to make donations to public welfare undertakings.

Natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations that make outstanding contributions to public welfare donations shall be commended by the people's government or relevant departments. Public commendation of donors shall be subject to prior consultation with the donors.

## Chapter II Donations and Receipt of Donations

Article 9 Natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations may choose public welfare social organizations and public welfare non-profit institutions that align with their donation intentions to make donations. The donated property shall be lawful property over which the donor has the right of disposal.

Article 10 Public welfare social organizations and public welfare non-profit institutions may accept donations in accordance with this Law.

For the purposes of this Law, "public welfare social organizations" refer to social organizations such as foundations and charitable organizations that are lawfully established with the purpose of developing public welfare undertakings.

For the purposes of this Law, "public welfare non-profit institutions" refer to lawfully established educational institutions, scientific research institutions, medical and health institutions, social public cultural institutions, social public sports institutions, and social welfare institutions that engage in public welfare undertakings without profit-making purposes.

Article 11 In the event of natural disasters or when overseas donors require the people's government at or above the county level or its departments to act as recipients, the people's government at or above the county level or its departments may accept donations and manage the donated property in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

The people's government at or above the county level or its departments may transfer the donated property to public welfare social organizations or public welfare non-profit institutions, or distribute it or use it to establish public welfare undertakings in accordance with the donor's wishes, provided that the government organ itself shall not be the beneficiary.

Article 12 Donors may enter into donation agreements with recipients regarding the type, quality, quantity, and purpose of the donated property. Donors have the right to decide the amount, purpose, and method of donation.

Donors shall fulfill the donation agreement in accordance with the law and transfer the donated property to the recipient within the time limit and in the manner agreed upon in the donation agreement.

Article 13 Where a donor donates property to construct a public welfare project, the donor shall enter into a donation agreement with the recipient, specifying the funding, construction, management, and use of the project.

The donated public welfare project shall be subject to project approval procedures by the recipient unit in accordance with relevant state regulations, and construction shall be organized by the recipient unit or jointly by the recipient and the donor. The project quality shall meet national quality standards.

Upon completion of the donated public welfare project, the recipient unit shall report the project construction, use of construction funds, and quality acceptance to the donor.

Article 14 Donors may have their names commemorated on donated public welfare projects; for projects donated solely by a donor or primarily funded by a donor, the donor may propose the project name, subject to approval by the people's government at or above the county level.

Article 15 Property donated by overseas donors shall go through entry procedures by the recipient in accordance with relevant state regulations; for items subject to license management, the recipient shall apply for the necessary licenses in accordance with relevant state regulations, and customs shall inspect and supervise based on the licenses.

For donations made by overseas Chinese to the mainland, the overseas Chinese affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level may assist in handling relevant entry procedures and provide assistance to donors in implementing donation projects.

## Chapter III Use and Management of Donated Property

Article 16 Upon accepting a donation, the recipient shall issue a lawful and valid receipt to the donor, register the donated property, and keep it properly.

Article 17 Public welfare social organizations shall use donated property to fund activities and undertakings consistent with their purposes. Donated property received for disaster relief shall be used promptly for relief activities. The annual amount of funds used by foundations to fund public welfare undertakings shall not be less than the proportion prescribed by the State.

Public welfare social organizations shall strictly comply with relevant state regulations and, following the principles of legality, safety, and effectiveness, actively seek to preserve and increase the value of donated property.

Public welfare non-profit institutions shall use donated property to develop their own public welfare undertakings and shall not divert it to other uses.

For donated property that is difficult to store, transport, or exceeds actual needs, the recipient may sell it, and all proceeds shall be used for the donation purpose.

Article 18 Where a recipient has entered into a donation agreement with a donor, the donated property shall be used in accordance with the purposes agreed upon in the agreement, and the purpose shall not be changed without authorization. If a change is truly necessary, the donor's consent shall be obtained.

Article 19 Recipients shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, establish and improve financial accounting systems and systems for the use of donated property, and strengthen the management of donated property.

Article 20 Recipients shall report to the relevant government departments each year on the use and management of donated property and accept supervision. When necessary, the relevant government departments may audit their finances.

Customs shall supervise and manage donated items that are exempted from or have reduced customs duties in accordance with the law.

The overseas Chinese affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level may participate in supervising the use and management of property donated by overseas Chinese to the mainland.

Article 21 Donors have the right to inquire with recipients about the use and management of donated property and to make suggestions and comments. Recipients shall truthfully respond to such inquiries.

Article 22 Recipients shall disclose information on the acceptance of donations and the use and management of donated property to the public and accept social supervision.

Article 23 Public welfare social organizations shall practice economy, reduce management costs, and pay staff salaries and office expenses from income such as interest, in accordance with standards prescribed by the State.

## Chapter IV Preferential Measures

Article 24 Companies and other enterprises that donate property for public welfare undertakings in accordance with this Law shall enjoy preferential treatment in terms of corporate income tax in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 25 Natural persons and individual industrial and commercial households that donate property for public welfare undertakings in accordance with this Law shall enjoy preferential treatment in terms of personal income tax in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 26 Materials donated from overseas to public welfare social organizations and public welfare non-profit institutions for public welfare purposes shall be subject to reduced or exempted import customs duties and import value-added tax in accordance with laws and administrative regulations.

Article 27 For donated projects, the local people's government shall provide support and preferential treatment.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 28 Where a recipient, without the donor's permission, changes the nature or purpose of donated property, the relevant department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order correction and issue a warning. If correction is refused, after consulting the donor, the people's government at or above the county level shall transfer the donated property to a public welfare social organization or public welfare non-profit institution with the same or similar purpose.

Article 29 Where donated funds or materials are misappropriated, embezzled, or stolen, the relevant department of the people's government at or above the county level shall order the return of the misappropriated or obtained funds or materials and impose a fine; the directly responsible person shall be dealt with by their unit in accordance with relevant regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Donated funds or materials recovered or recovered in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall be used for the original donation purpose and use.

Article 30 Any of the following acts in donation activities shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws and regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law:

(1) Evasion of foreign exchange or fraudulent purchase of foreign exchange;

(2) Tax evasion or avoidance;

(3) Smuggling activities;

(4) Selling, transferring, or using for other purposes donated materials imported with reduced or exempted duties within the territory without customs permission and without paying the due tax amount.

Article 31 Where staff members of a recipient unit abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, causing significant losses to donated property, they shall be dealt with by their unit in accordance with relevant regulations; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 32 This Law shall come into effect as of September 1, 1999.
