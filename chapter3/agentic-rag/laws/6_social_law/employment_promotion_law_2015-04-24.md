# Employment Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Tenth National People's Congress on August 30, 2007

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Revising Six Laws Including the Electricity Law of the People's Republic of China at the 14th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on April 24, 2015

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of promoting employment, coordinating economic development with the expansion of employment, and promoting social harmony and stability.

Article 2 The State gives high priority to the expansion of employment in economic and social development, implements a proactive employment policy, adheres to the principle of workers choosing jobs on their own, employment regulated by the market, and employment promoted by the government, and expands employment through multiple channels.

Article 3 Workers shall have the right to equal employment and independent choice of jobs in accordance with the law.

When seeking employment, workers shall not be subject to discrimination based on ethnicity, race, gender, religious belief, etc.

Article 4 People's governments at or above the county level shall take the expansion of employment as an important goal of economic and social development, incorporate it into national economic and social development plans, and formulate medium- and long-term plans and annual work plans for promoting employment.

Article 5 People's governments at or above the county level shall create employment conditions and expand employment by developing the economy and adjusting the industrial structure, regulating the human resources market, improving employment services, strengthening vocational education and training, and providing employment assistance.

Article 6 The State Council shall establish a national coordination mechanism for promoting employment, study major issues in employment work, and coordinate and promote the nationwide employment promotion efforts. The labor administrative department of the State Council shall be specifically responsible for the nationwide employment promotion work.

People's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on the needs of employment promotion work, establish coordination mechanisms for promoting employment to coordinate and resolve major issues in employment work within their respective administrative regions.

The relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with their respective functions and duties, work together to promote employment.

Article 7 The State advocates that workers establish a correct concept of career choice, improve their employability and entrepreneurial ability; and encourages workers to start their own businesses and seek jobs on their own.

People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall simplify procedures, improve efficiency, and provide convenience for workers to start their own businesses and seek jobs on their own.

Article 8 Employers shall have the right to independently employ personnel in accordance with the law.

Employers shall protect the legitimate rights and interests of workers in accordance with the provisions of this Law and other laws and regulations.

Article 9 Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, disabled persons' federations, and other social organizations shall assist the people's governments in carrying out employment promotion work and safeguard the labor rights of workers in accordance with the law.

Article 10 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall commend and reward units and individuals that have made outstanding achievements in employment promotion work.

## Chapter II Policy Support

Article 11 People's governments at or above the county level shall take the expansion of employment as an important duty and coordinate industrial policies with employment policies.

Article 12 The State encourages various types of enterprises to increase job positions by establishing industries or expanding operations within the scope prescribed by laws and regulations.

The State encourages the development of labor-intensive industries and the service industry, supports small and medium-sized enterprises, and increases job positions through multiple channels and methods.

The State encourages, supports, and guides the development of the non-public sector of the economy to expand employment and increase job positions.

Article 13 The State develops domestic and foreign trade and international economic cooperation to broaden employment channels.

Article 14 When arranging government investment and determining major construction projects, people's governments at or above the county level shall leverage the role of investment and major construction projects in driving employment and increase job positions.

Article 15 The State implements fiscal policies conducive to promoting employment, increases capital investment, improves the employment environment, and expands employment.

People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the employment situation and employment work objectives, allocate special employment funds in the fiscal budget for promoting employment.

Special employment funds shall be used for subsidies for job placement, vocational training, public welfare positions, vocational skill appraisal, specific employment policies and social insurance, small loan guarantee funds and interest subsidies for small guaranteed loans for micro-profit projects, and support for public employment services. The measures for the management and use of special employment funds shall be formulated by the fiscal department and the labor administrative department of the State Council.

Article 16 The State establishes and improves the unemployment insurance system to ensure the basic livelihood of unemployed persons in accordance with the law and promote their re-employment.

Article 17 The State encourages enterprises to increase job positions and support the employment of unemployed persons and persons with disabilities, and grants tax preferences to the following enterprises and individuals:

(1) Enterprises that have absorbed unemployed persons meeting the conditions prescribed by the State to the required extent;
(2) Small and medium-sized enterprises established by unemployed persons;
(3) Enterprises that have placed persons with disabilities to the prescribed proportion or that intensively employ persons with disabilities;
(4) Unemployed persons meeting the conditions prescribed by the State who engage in individual businesses;
(5) Persons with disabilities who engage in individual businesses;
(6) Other enterprises and individuals to which the State Council stipulates tax preferences shall be granted.

Article 18 For the persons specified in items (4) and (5) of Article 17 of this Law, the relevant departments shall provide care in terms of business premises, etc., and exempt administrative fees.

Article 19 The State implements financial policies conducive to promoting employment, increasing financing channels for small and medium-sized enterprises; encourages financial institutions to improve financial services, increase credit support for small and medium-sized enterprises, and provide support such as small loans to self-employed persons within a certain period.

Article 20 The State implements an employment policy that coordinates urban and rural areas, establishes and improves a system of equal employment for urban and rural workers, and guides the orderly transfer of surplus agricultural labor for employment.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall promote the construction of small towns and accelerate the development of the county economy, guiding the local transfer of surplus agricultural labor for employment; when formulating small town plans, they shall take the transfer of surplus agricultural labor in the region for employment as an important element.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall guide the orderly transfer of surplus agricultural labor to cities in other places for employment; the people's governments of labor-exporting areas and labor-importing areas shall cooperate with each other to improve the environment and conditions for rural workers seeking employment in cities.

Article 21 The State supports regional economic development, encourages regional cooperation, and coordinates the balanced growth of employment in different regions.

The State supports the development of the economy in ethnic minority areas to expand employment.

Article 22 People's governments at all levels shall coordinate the employment of new urban labor, the transfer of surplus agricultural labor for employment, and the employment of unemployed persons.

Article 23 People's governments at all levels shall take measures to gradually improve and implement labor and social insurance policies that are compatible with flexible employment forms such as part-time work, and provide assistance and services to flexible workers.

Article 24 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall strengthen guidance for unemployed persons engaged in individual businesses, and provide services such as policy consultation, employment training, and business start-up guidance.

## Chapter III Fair Employment

Article 25 People's governments at all levels shall create an environment for fair employment, eliminate employment discrimination, formulate policies and take measures to provide support and assistance to persons with difficulties in employment.

Article 26 When recruiting personnel, employers and employment agencies engaged in intermediary activities shall provide workers with equal employment opportunities and fair employment conditions, and shall not engage in employment discrimination.

Article 27 The State guarantees women the equal labor rights as men.

When recruiting personnel, employers shall not refuse to hire women on the grounds of gender or raise the recruitment standards for women, except for types of work or positions that are not suitable for women as prescribed by the State.

When employing female workers, employers shall not stipulate in the labor contract restrictions on marriage or childbirth of female workers.

Article 28 Workers of all ethnic groups enjoy equal labor rights.

When recruiting personnel, employers shall give appropriate consideration to workers of ethnic minorities in accordance with the law.

Article 29 The State guarantees the labor rights of persons with disabilities.

People's governments at all levels shall make overall plans for the employment of persons with disabilities and create employment conditions for them.

When recruiting personnel, employers shall not discriminate against persons with disabilities.

Article 30 When recruiting personnel, employers shall not refuse to hire on the grounds that the person is a carrier of a pathogen of an infectious disease. However, before being cured or ruled out of infectiousness through medical assessment, carriers of pathogens of infectious diseases shall not engage in work that is prone to the spread of infectious diseases and is prohibited by laws, administrative regulations, and the health administrative department of the State Council.

Article 31 Rural workers seeking employment in cities enjoy equal labor rights as urban workers, and no discriminatory restrictions shall be imposed on rural workers seeking employment in cities.

## Chapter IV Employment Services and Management

Article 32 People's governments at or above the county level shall cultivate and improve a unified, open, competitive, and orderly human resources market, and provide services for workers' employment.

Article 33 People's governments at or above the county level shall encourage all sectors of society to carry out employment service activities in accordance with the law, strengthen guidance and supervision over public employment services and vocational intermediary services, and gradually improve the employment service system covering urban and rural areas.

Article 34 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of human resources market information networks and related facilities, establish and improve the human resources market information service system, and improve the market information release system.

Article 35 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve the public employment service system, set up public employment service institutions, and provide the following services free of charge to workers:

(1) Consultation on employment policies, laws, and regulations;
(2) Release of information on job supply and demand, market wage guidance prices, and vocational training information;
(3) Vocational guidance and job placement;
(4) Implementation of employment assistance for persons with difficulties in employment;
(5) Handling of matters such as employment registration and unemployment registration;
(6) Other public employment services.

Public employment service institutions shall continuously improve the quality and efficiency of their services and shall not engage in for-profit activities.

Funds for public employment services shall be included in the fiscal budget at the same level.

Article 36 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide subsidies to vocational intermediary institutions that provide public welfare employment services in accordance with regulations.

The State encourages all sectors of society to provide donations and funding for public welfare employment services.

Article 37 Local people's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall not establish or jointly establish for-profit vocational intermediary institutions with others.

Job fairs organized by local people's governments at all levels, relevant departments, and public employment service institutions shall not charge fees from workers.

Article 38 People's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall strengthen the management of vocational intermediary institutions, encourage them to improve service quality, and play their role in promoting employment.

Article 39 Engaging in vocational intermediary activities shall follow the principles of legality, honesty and trustworthiness, fairness, and openness.

When recruiting personnel through vocational intermediary institutions, employers shall truthfully provide job requirement information to the vocational intermediary institutions.

No organization or individual is allowed to use vocational intermediary activities to infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of workers.

Article 40 The establishment of a vocational intermediary institution shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having a clear charter and management system;
(2) Having the necessary fixed premises, office facilities, and a certain amount of start-up capital for conducting business;
(3) Having a certain number of full-time staff with corresponding professional qualifications;
(4) Other conditions prescribed by laws and regulations.

A vocational intermediary institution shall, after registering with the administrative department for industry and commerce, apply to the labor administrative department for administrative permission.

Institutions that have not obtained permission and registration in accordance with the law shall not engage in vocational intermediary activities.

Where the State has other provisions on foreign-invested vocational intermediary institutions and vocational intermediary institutions providing overseas employment services to workers, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 41 Vocational intermediary institutions shall not engage in the following acts:

(1) Providing false employment information;
(2) Providing vocational intermediary services to employers without legal licenses or certificates;
(3) Forging, altering, or transferring vocational intermediary permits;
(4) Detaining workers' resident identity cards or other certificates, or collecting deposits from workers;
(5) Other acts that violate the provisions of laws and regulations.

Article 42 People's governments at or above the county level shall establish an unemployment early warning system to prevent, regulate, and control possible large-scale unemployment.

Article 43 The State shall establish a labor force survey and statistics system and an employment registration and unemployment registration system, conduct surveys and statistics on labor resources and employment and unemployment conditions, and publish the survey and statistics results.

When the statistics department and the labor administrative department conduct labor force surveys and statistics and employment and unemployment registration, employers and individuals shall truthfully provide the information required for the surveys, statistics, and registration.

## Chapter V Vocational Education and Training

Article 44 The State develops vocational education in accordance with the law, encourages the conduct of vocational training, promotes the improvement of workers' vocational skills, and enhances their employability and entrepreneurial ability.

Article 45 People's governments at or above the county level shall, based on economic and social development and market demand, formulate and implement vocational ability development plans.

Article 46 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen overall coordination, encourage and support various types of vocational colleges, vocational skills training institutions, and employers to carry out pre-employment training, on-the-job training, re-employment training, and entrepreneurship training in accordance with the law; and encourage workers to participate in various forms of training.

Article 47 Local people's governments at or above the county level and relevant departments shall, based on market demand and industrial development direction, encourage and guide enterprises to strengthen vocational education and training.

Vocational colleges, vocational skills training institutions, and enterprises shall maintain close ties, implement the integration of production and education, serve economic construction, and cultivate practical talents and skilled workers.

Enterprises shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, allocate funds for employee education and conduct vocational skills training and continuing education training for workers.

Article 48 The State shall take measures to establish and improve the labor preparation system. Local people's governments at or above the county level shall provide a certain period of vocational education and training to junior and senior high school graduates who have employment requirements, enabling them to obtain corresponding vocational qualifications or master certain vocational skills.

Article 49 Local people's governments at all levels shall encourage and support the conduct of employment training, help unemployed persons improve their vocational skills, and enhance their employability and entrepreneurial ability. Unemployed persons participating in employment training shall enjoy government training subsidies in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 50 Local people's governments at all levels shall take effective measures to organize and guide rural workers seeking employment in cities to participate in skills training, encourage various types of training institutions to provide skills training for rural workers seeking employment in cities, and enhance their employability and entrepreneurial ability.

Article 51 The State implements a vocational qualification certificate system for workers engaged in special types of work involving public safety, personal health, and the safety of life and property. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter VI Employment Assistance

Article 52 People's governments at all levels shall establish and improve the employment assistance system, adopt measures such as tax and fee reductions, loan interest subsidies, social insurance subsidies, and job subsidies, and through means such as placement in public welfare positions, provide priority support and key assistance to persons with difficulties in employment.

Persons with difficulties in employment refer to those who have difficulty finding employment due to reasons such as physical condition, skill level, family factors, loss of land, or who have been continuously unemployed for a certain period and still cannot find employment. The specific scope of persons with difficulties in employment shall be determined by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government based on the actual conditions of their respective administrative regions.

Article 53 Public welfare positions developed with government investment shall be given priority to persons with difficulties in employment who meet the job requirements. Those placed in public welfare positions shall receive job subsidies in accordance with national regulations.

Article 54 Local people's governments at all levels shall strengthen grassroots employment assistance services, provide key assistance to persons with difficulties in employment, and provide targeted employment services and public welfare position assistance.

Local people's governments at all levels shall encourage and support all sectors of society to provide services such as skills training and job information to persons with difficulties in employment.

Article 55 People's governments at all levels shall take special support measures to promote the employment of persons with disabilities.

Employers shall arrange for the employment of persons with disabilities in accordance with national regulations. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

Article 56 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall adopt multiple forms of employment, broaden the scope of public welfare positions, develop job positions, and ensure that at least one person in each urban household with employment needs is employed.

Urban resident households where all family members within the statutory working age are unemployed may apply for employment assistance to the public employment service institutions of the sub-district or community where they reside. If the sub-district or community public employment service institution confirms the situation, it shall provide appropriate job positions for at least one person in the household.

Article 57 The State encourages resource-based cities and independent industrial and mining areas to develop industries that are compatible with market demand and guide workers to transfer employment.

For areas where the concentration of persons with difficulties in employment is caused by resource depletion or economic restructuring, the people's government at the higher level shall provide necessary support and assistance.

## Chapter VII Supervision and Inspection

Article 58 People's governments at all levels and relevant departments shall establish a target responsibility system for promoting employment. People's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the requirements of the target responsibility system for promoting employment, assess and supervise the relevant departments under them and the people's governments at the next lower level.

Article 59 Audit institutions and fiscal departments shall supervise and inspect the management and use of special employment funds in accordance with the law.

Article 60 The labor administrative department shall supervise and inspect the implementation of this Law, establish a reporting system, accept reports of violations of this Law, and promptly verify and handle them.

## Chapter VIII Legal Liability

Article 61 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, the labor administrative department or other relevant departments and their staff abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law.

Article 62 Where employment discrimination is practiced in violation of the provisions of this Law, workers may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 63 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, local people's governments at all levels, relevant departments, or public employment service institutions establish for-profit vocational intermediary institutions, engage in for-profit vocational intermediary activities, or charge fees from workers, the competent authority at the higher level shall order them to correct within a time limit, refund the illegally collected fees to the workers, and impose sanctions on the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible persons in accordance with the law.

Article 64 Where, in violation of the provisions of this Law, an institution engages in vocational intermediary activities without permission and registration, the labor administrative department or other competent authority shall close it down in accordance with the law; if there are illegal gains, the illegal gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan shall be imposed concurrently.Article 65: Where, in violation of this Law, a job placement agency provides false employment information, provides job placement services to an employer without lawful licenses or certificates, or forges, alters, or transfers a job placement permit, the labor administrative department or other competent authority shall order it to make corrections; if there are illegal gains, such gains shall be confiscated, and a fine of not less than RMB 10,000 yuan but not more than RMB 50,000 yuan shall be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the job placement permit shall be revoked.

Article 66: Where, in violation of this Law, a job placement agency detains a worker's resident identity card or other documents, the labor administrative department shall order it to return such documents to the worker within a specified time limit and impose penalties in accordance with relevant legal provisions.

Where, in violation of this Law, a job placement agency collects a deposit from a worker, the labor administrative department shall order it to return the deposit to the worker within a specified time limit and impose a fine of not less than RMB 500 yuan but not more than RMB 2,000 yuan per person.

Article 67: Where, in violation of this Law, an enterprise fails to allocate funds for employee education and training as required by state regulations, or misappropriates such funds, the labor administrative department shall order it to make corrections and impose penalties in accordance with the law.

Article 68: Where, in violation of this Law, a party infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of a worker, causing property loss or other damage, it shall bear civil liability in accordance with the law; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter 9 Supplementary Provisions

Article 69: This Law shall come into force as of January 1, 2008.
