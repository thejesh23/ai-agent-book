# Trade Union Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fifth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress on April 3, 1992

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Trade Union Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 24th Session of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on October 27, 2001

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Session of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Trade Union Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 32nd Session of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on December 24, 2021

## Chapter I: General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to safeguard the status of trade unions in the political, economic, and social life of the state, define their rights and obligations, and enable them to play their role in the cause of socialist modernization.

Article 2 Trade unions are mass organizations of the working class formed voluntarily by workers under the leadership of the Communist Party of China, serving as a bridge and bond linking the Party with the masses of workers.

The All-China Federation of Trade Unions and its affiliated trade union organizations represent the interests of workers and lawfully protect their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 3 All workers within the territory of China who rely primarily on wage income for their livelihood, whether employed in enterprises, public institutions, state organs, or social organizations (hereinafter collectively referred to as "employing units"), regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, occupation, religious belief, or educational level, have the right to join or form trade unions in accordance with the law. No organization or individual may obstruct or restrict this right.

Trade unions shall adapt to developments and changes in enterprise organizational forms, workforce structure, labor relations, and employment patterns, and shall lawfully protect workers' right to join or form trade unions.

Article 4 Trade unions must abide by and uphold the Constitution, take it as the fundamental guiding principle for their activities, center on economic development, adhere to the socialist path, uphold the people's democratic dictatorship, uphold the leadership of the Communist Party of China, uphold Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, Deng Xiaoping Theory, the important thought of the "Three Represents," the Scientific Outlook on Development, and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, uphold reform and opening up, maintain and enhance their political nature, advanced nature, and mass character, and carry out their work independently in accordance with the trade union constitution.

The National Congress of Trade Union Members formulates or amends the Constitution of the Chinese Trade Unions, which shall not contravene the Constitution or laws.

The state protects the legitimate rights and interests of trade unions from infringement.

Article 5 Trade unions shall organize and educate workers to exercise their democratic rights in accordance with the Constitution and laws, play their role as masters of the state, participate through various channels and forms in the management of state affairs, economic and cultural undertakings, and social affairs; assist the people's governments in their work, and safeguard the socialist state power of the people's democratic dictatorship led by the working class and based on the alliance of workers and peasants.

Article 6 Safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of workers and wholeheartedly serving the masses of workers are the basic duties of trade unions. While safeguarding the overall interests of the people nationwide, trade unions shall represent and protect the legitimate rights and interests of workers.

Trade unions shall, through systems such as equal consultation and collective contracts, promote the improvement of labor relations coordination mechanisms, protect workers' labor rights and interests, and build harmonious labor relations.

Trade unions shall, in accordance with legal provisions, organize workers to participate in democratic elections, democratic consultations, democratic decision-making, democratic management, and democratic supervision of their employing units through workers' congresses or other forms.

Trade unions shall establish a broad-reaching work system that serves workers, maintain close ties with workers, listen to and reflect their opinions and demands, care about their lives, help them solve difficulties, and serve them wholeheartedly.

Article 7 Trade unions shall mobilize and organize workers to actively participate in economic construction and strive to fulfill production and work tasks. They shall educate workers to continuously improve their ideological and moral standards, technical and professional skills, and scientific and cultural literacy, building a workforce with ideals, morality, culture, and discipline.

Article 8 Trade unions shall promote the reform of the industrial workforce, improve the overall quality of the industrial workforce, give play to the backbone role of industrial workers, protect their legitimate rights and interests, ensure their status as masters of the state, and cultivate a large industrial workforce with ideals, convictions, technical skills, innovation ability, a sense of responsibility, and a spirit of dedication.

Article 9 The All-China Federation of Trade Unions shall strengthen friendly and cooperative relations with trade union organizations of other countries on the principles of independence, equality, mutual respect, and non-interference in each other's internal affairs.

## Chapter II: Trade Union Organization

Article 10 Trade union organizations at all levels shall be established on the principle of democratic centralism.

Trade union committees at all levels shall be democratically elected by the general assembly of members or the congress of members' representatives. Close relatives of the principal responsible persons of an enterprise shall not be candidates for membership in the grassroots trade union committee of that enterprise.

Trade union committees at all levels shall be responsible to and report on their work to the general assembly of members or the congress of members' representatives at the same level, and shall accept their supervision.

The general assembly of members or the congress of members' representatives has the power to recall or remove the representatives it has elected or the members of the trade union committee.

Higher-level trade union organizations shall lead lower-level trade union organizations.

Article 11 An employing unit with 25 or more members shall establish a grassroots trade union committee; if there are fewer than 25 members, a grassroots trade union committee may be established separately, or a joint grassroots trade union committee may be established by members from two or more units, or one organizer may be elected to organize members' activities. Where there is a relatively large number of female workers, a trade union women workers' committee may be established to carry out work under the leadership of the trade union at the same level; where there are relatively few female workers, a women workers' committee member may be appointed within the trade union committee.

In townships and urban subdistricts with a large number of enterprise workers, a federation of grassroots trade unions may be established.

General trade unions at various levels shall be established at or above the county level.

National or local industrial trade unions may be established for the same industry or several similar industries as needed.

A unified All-China Federation of Trade Unions shall be established nationwide.

Article 12 The establishment of grassroots trade unions, general trade unions at various levels, and national or local industrial trade union organizations must be reported to the next higher-level trade union for approval.

Higher-level trade unions may send personnel to assist and guide enterprise workers in forming trade unions, and no unit or individual may obstruct this.

Article 13 No organization or individual may arbitrarily dissolve or merge trade union organizations.

If the employing unit where a grassroots trade union is located is terminated or dissolved, the corresponding trade union organization shall be dissolved accordingly, and this shall be reported to the next higher-level trade union.

For trade unions dissolved in accordance with the preceding paragraph, the membership of their members may continue to be retained, and specific management measures shall be formulated by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.

Article 14 Trade unions in enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations with 200 or more workers may have full-time trade union chairpersons. The number of full-time trade union staff shall be determined through consultation between the trade union and the enterprise, public institution, or social organization.

Article 15 The All-China Federation of Trade Unions, local general trade unions, and industrial trade unions have the status of social organization legal persons.

Grassroots trade union organizations that meet the conditions for legal persons as stipulated in the Civil Code shall obtain the status of social organization legal persons in accordance with the law.

Article 16 The term of office of a grassroots trade union committee shall be three or five years. The term of office of local general trade union committees and industrial trade union committees at all levels shall be five years.

Article 17 Grassroots trade union committees shall regularly convene general assemblies of members or congresses of members' representatives to discuss and decide on major issues concerning trade union work. Upon the proposal of the grassroots trade union committee or more than one-third of the trade union members, a general assembly of members or a congress of members' representatives may be convened temporarily.

Article 18 The work of trade union chairpersons and vice-chairpersons shall not be arbitrarily transferred before the expiration of their terms. If a transfer is necessary due to work requirements, the consent of the trade union committee at the same level and the next higher-level trade union must be obtained.

The recall of a trade union chairperson or vice-chairperson must be discussed at a general assembly of members or a congress of members' representatives, and shall not be carried out unless approved by a majority of all members of the general assembly or all representatives of the congress.

Article 19 From the date a full-time grassroots trade union chairperson, vice-chairperson, or committee member assumes office, their labor contract term shall be automatically extended for a period equivalent to their term of office; for a part-time chairperson, vice-chairperson, or committee member, if the remaining term of their labor contract is shorter than their term of office, the labor contract term shall be automatically extended until the expiration of their term of office. However, this shall not apply in cases of serious personal fault during the term of office or upon reaching the statutory retirement age.

## Chapter III: Rights and Obligations of Trade Unions

Article 20 If an enterprise, public institution, or social organization violates the system of workers' congresses or other democratic management systems, the trade union has the right to demand correction and to ensure that workers exercise their right to democratic management in accordance with the law.

Matters that laws and regulations require to be submitted to the workers' general assembly or workers' congress for deliberation, approval, or decision shall be handled accordingly by the enterprise, public institution, or social organization.

Article 21 Trade unions shall assist and guide workers in signing labor contracts with enterprises, enterprise-managed public institutions, and social organizations.

Trade unions shall conduct equal consultations on behalf of workers with enterprises, enterprise-managed public institutions, and social organizations, and sign collective contracts in accordance with the law. The draft collective contract shall be submitted to the workers' congress or all workers for discussion and approval.

Higher-level trade unions shall provide support and assistance when a trade union signs a collective contract.

If an enterprise, public institution, or social organization violates a collective contract and infringes upon workers' labor rights and interests, the trade union may lawfully demand that the enterprise, public institution, or social organization make corrections and assume responsibility; if a dispute arises over the performance of a collective contract and cannot be resolved through consultation, the trade union may apply to a labor dispute arbitration institution for arbitration; if the arbitration institution refuses to accept the case or the trade union disagrees with the arbitration award, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 22 If an enterprise, public institution, or social organization imposes a sanction on a worker and the trade union considers it inappropriate, the trade union has the right to express its opinion.

When an employing unit unilaterally terminates a worker's labor contract, it shall notify the trade union of the reasons in advance. If the trade union considers that the employing unit has violated laws, regulations, or relevant contracts and requests reconsideration, the employing unit shall study the trade union's opinion and notify the trade union in writing of the outcome.

If a worker believes that the employing unit has infringed upon their labor rights and interests and applies for labor dispute arbitration or files a lawsuit with the people's court, the trade union shall provide support and assistance.

Article 23 If an enterprise, public institution, or social organization violates labor laws and regulations in any of the following ways, infringing upon workers' labor rights and interests, the trade union shall represent the workers in negotiating with the enterprise, public institution, or social organization, demanding that it take measures to make corrections; the enterprise, public institution, or social organization shall study and handle the matter and give a reply to the trade union; if the enterprise, public institution, or social organization refuses to make corrections, the trade union may request the local people's government to handle the matter in accordance with the law:

(1) Withholding or delaying payment of workers' wages;

(2) Failing to provide labor safety and health conditions;

(3) Arbitrarily extending working hours;

(4) Infringing upon the special rights and interests of female workers and minor workers;

(5) Other serious infringements upon workers' labor rights and interests.

Article 24 Trade unions shall, in accordance with state regulations, supervise the simultaneous design, construction, and commissioning of labor conditions and safety and health facilities with the main projects in new construction, expansion, and technological renovation projects. If the trade union raises opinions, the enterprise or competent department shall seriously handle them and notify the trade union in writing of the outcome.

Article 25 If a trade union discovers that an enterprise is giving illegal commands, forcing workers to work under dangerous conditions, or finds obvious major accident hazards or occupational hazards during production, it has the right to propose solutions, and the enterprise shall promptly study and reply; if it discovers situations that endanger workers' lives, the trade union has the right to suggest that the enterprise organize workers to evacuate the dangerous site, and the enterprise must promptly make a decision on handling the matter.

Article 26 Trade unions have the right to investigate issues concerning enterprises, public institutions, or social organizations infringing upon workers' legitimate rights and interests, and the relevant units shall provide assistance.

Article 27 Trade unions must participate in the investigation and handling of work-related injury and death accidents and other serious health hazards to workers. Trade unions shall submit opinions to the relevant departments and have the right to demand that the directly responsible supervisors and other responsible persons be held accountable. The opinions raised by the trade union shall be promptly studied and replied to.

Article 28 In the event of a work stoppage or slowdown in an enterprise, public institution, or social organization, the trade union shall represent the workers in consulting with the enterprise, public institution, social organization, or relevant parties, reflect the workers' opinions and demands, and propose solutions. For reasonable demands of the workers, the enterprise, public institution, or social organization shall address them. The trade union shall assist the enterprise, public institution, or social organization in doing a good job and restoring production and work order as soon as possible.

Article 29 Trade unions shall participate in the labor dispute mediation work of enterprises.

Local labor dispute arbitration organizations shall include representatives from trade unions at the same level.

Article 30 General trade unions at or above the county level shall provide legal aid and other legal services to their affiliated trade unions and workers in accordance with the law.

Article 31 Trade unions shall assist employing units in running collective welfare undertakings for workers and in doing a good job with wages, labor safety and health, and social insurance.

Article 32 Trade unions shall, together with employing units, strengthen ideological and political guidance for workers, educate workers to treat labor with the attitude of masters of the state and to cherish the property of the state and the unit; organize workers to carry out mass activities such as rationalization suggestions, technological innovation, labor and skills competitions, conduct spare-time cultural and technical studies and worker training, participate in vocational education and cultural and sports activities, and promote occupational safety and health education and labor protection work.

Article 33 Upon entrustment by the government, trade unions shall work with relevant departments to jointly carry out the selection, commendation, cultivation, and management of model workers and advanced producers (workers).

Article 34 When state organs organize the drafting or amendment of laws, regulations, or rules that directly involve the vital interests of workers, they shall solicit the opinions of trade unions.

When people's governments at or above the county level formulate plans for national economic and social development, they shall solicit the opinions of trade unions at the same level on major issues involving workers' interests.

When people's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments study and formulate policies and measures concerning labor employment, wages, labor safety and health, social insurance, and other matters involving workers' vital interests, they shall involve trade unions at the same level in the study and solicit their opinions.

Article 35 Local people's governments at or above the county level may convene meetings or adopt appropriate means to inform trade unions at the same level of important government work deployments and administrative measures related to trade union work, and study and resolve the opinions and demands of the masses of workers reflected by trade unions.

The labor administrative departments of people's governments at all levels shall, together with trade unions at the same level and representatives of enterprises, establish a tripartite consultation mechanism for labor relations to jointly study and resolve major issues concerning labor relations.

## Chapter IV: Grassroots Trade Union Organizations

Article 36 The workers' congress of a state-owned enterprise is the basic form of democratic management in the enterprise and the body through which workers exercise their power of democratic management, exercising its functions and powers in accordance with legal provisions.

The trade union committee of a state-owned enterprise is the working body of the workers' congress, responsible for the daily work of the workers' congress and for inspecting and supervising the implementation of the resolutions of the workers' congress.

Article 37 The trade union committee of a collectively owned enterprise shall support and organize workers to participate in democratic management and democratic supervision, and safeguard workers' power to elect and dismiss management personnel and to decide on major issues of operation and management.

Article 38 Trade union committees of enterprises and public institutions other than those specified in Articles 36 and 37 of this Law shall, in accordance with legal provisions, organize workers to participate in the democratic management of the enterprise or public institution in forms appropriate to the enterprise or public institution.

Article 39 When an enterprise, public institution, or social organization studies major issues of operation, management, and development, it shall listen to the opinions of the trade union; when convening meetings to discuss issues involving workers' vital interests such as wages, welfare, labor safety and health, working hours, rest and leave, protection of female workers, and social insurance, trade union representatives must participate.

Enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations shall support trade unions in carrying out their work in accordance with the law, and trade unions shall support enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations in exercising their rights of operation and management in accordance with the law.

Article 40 The election of worker representatives to the board of directors and board of supervisors of a company shall be carried out in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Company Law.

Article 41 When a grassroots trade union committee convenes meetings or organizes worker activities, it shall do so outside of production or working hours; if it needs to occupy production or working hours, it shall obtain the prior consent of the enterprise, public institution, or social organization.

Part-time committee members of a grassroots trade union may occupy production or working time to attend meetings or engage in trade union work for no more than three working days per month, during which their wages shall be paid as usual and other benefits shall not be affected.

Article 42 The wages, bonuses, and subsidies of full-time staff of an employing unit's trade union committee shall be paid by the employing unit. They shall enjoy the same social insurance and other welfare benefits as other workers of the unit.

## Chapter V: Trade Union Funds and Property

Article 43 The sources of trade union funds are:

(1) Membership dues paid by trade union members;

(2) Funds allocated by employing units that have established trade union organizations, at a rate of two percent of the total monthly wages of all workers, to the trade union;

(3) Income turned over by enterprises and public institutions owned by the trade union;

(4) Subsidies from the people's government;

(5) Other income.

The funds allocated by enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations as specified in item (2) of the preceding paragraph shall be listed as expenses before tax.

Trade union funds shall be mainly used for serving workers and for trade union activities. Specific measures for the use of funds shall be formulated by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.

Article 44 If an enterprise, public institution, or social organization delays or refuses to allocate trade union funds without justifiable reasons, the grassroots trade union or the higher-level trade union may apply to the local people's court for a payment order; if the payment order is not complied with, the trade union may lawfully apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 45 Trade unions shall, based on the principle of financial independence, establish systems for budgeting, final accounting, and financial review and supervision.

Trade unions at all levels shall establish financial review committees.

The revenue and expenditure of trade union funds at all levels shall be reviewed by the trade union financial review committee at the same level and shall be reported regularly to the general assembly of members or the congress of members' representatives for supervision. The general assembly of members or the congress of members' representatives has the right to express opinions on the use of funds.

The use of trade union funds shall be subject to state supervision in accordance with the law.

Article 46 People's governments at all levels and employing units shall provide necessary facilities and venues for trade union offices and activities.

Article 47 No organization or individual may encroach upon, misappropriate, or arbitrarily transfer the property, funds, or real estate allocated by the state for use by trade unions.

Article 48 The affiliation of enterprises and public institutions owned by trade unions that serve workers shall not be arbitrarily changed.

Article 49 The treatment of retired and resigned personnel of trade unions at or above the county level shall be the same as that of state organ staff.

## Chapter VI: Legal Liability**Article 50** If a trade union's lawful rights and interests are violated in violation of this Law, it has the right to request the people's government or relevant departments to handle the matter, or to file a lawsuit with the people's court.

**Article 51** Anyone who, in violation of Articles 3 and 12 of this Law, obstructs workers from lawfully participating in and organizing a trade union, or obstructs a higher-level trade union from helping or guiding workers in preparing to establish a trade union, shall be ordered to make corrections by the labor administrative department; if corrections are refused, the labor administrative department shall refer the matter to the people's government at or above the county level for handling; if the obstruction involves violence, threats, or other means, causing serious consequences and constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 52** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, without justifiable reasons, transfers a trade union staff member who is lawfully performing their duties to another position, or retaliates against them, shall be ordered by the labor administrative department to make corrections and restore the original position; if losses are caused, compensation shall be paid.

Anyone who insults, slanders, or physically injures a trade union staff member lawfully performing their duties, if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law; if it does not constitute a crime, the public security organ shall impose penalties in accordance with the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishments Law.

**Article 53** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the labor administrative department to reinstate the worker's employment and retroactively pay the remuneration due during the period of the unlawfully terminated labor contract, or be ordered to pay compensation equal to twice the worker's annual income:

(1) A worker's labor contract is terminated because they participated in trade union activities;
(2) A trade union staff member's labor contract is terminated because they performed duties stipulated in this Law.

**Article 54** Anyone who, in violation of this Law, commits any of the following acts shall be ordered by the people's government at or above the county level to make corrections and be dealt with according to law:

(1) Obstructing a trade union from organizing workers to lawfully exercise their democratic rights through the workers' congress or other forms;
(2) Illegally dissolving or merging a trade union organization;
(3) Obstructing a trade union from participating in the investigation and handling of work-related injury or death accidents or other matters infringing upon the lawful rights and interests of workers;
(4) Refusing, without justifiable reasons, to engage in equal consultation.

**Article 55** Anyone who, in violation of Article 47 of this Law, misappropriates trade union funds or property and refuses to return them, the trade union may file a lawsuit with the people's court to demand their return and compensation for losses.

**Article 56** If a trade union staff member, in violation of this Law, harms the rights and interests of workers or the trade union, the trade union at the same level or a higher-level trade union shall order them to make corrections or impose sanctions; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be removed from their position in accordance with the *Constitution of the Chinese Trade Unions*; if losses are caused, they shall bear liability for compensation; if the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 57** The All-China Federation of Trade Unions shall, together with the relevant state organs, formulate specific measures for the implementation of this Law by trade unions in state organs.

**Article 58** This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation. The *Trade Union Law of the People's Republic of China* promulgated by the Central People's Government on June 29, 1950, shall be repealed simultaneously.
