# Work Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Ninth National People's Congress on June 29, 2002

First Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

Second Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Work Safety Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Twelfth National People's Congress on August 31, 2014

Third Amendment in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Work Safety Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 29th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress on June 10, 2021

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purpose of strengthening work safety, preventing and reducing production safety accidents, ensuring the safety of people's lives and property, and promoting the sustained and healthy development of the economy and society.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to the work safety of entities engaged in production and business activities (hereinafter collectively referred to as production and business entities) within the territory of the People's Republic of China; where other laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise for fire safety, road traffic safety, railway traffic safety, water traffic safety, civil aviation safety, nuclear and radiation safety, and special equipment safety, those provisions shall apply.

Article 3 Work safety shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China.

Work safety shall be people-oriented, prioritize people and life above all else, place the protection of people's life safety first, firmly establish the concept of safe development, adhere to the principles of safety first, prevention foremost, and comprehensive management, and prevent and resolve major safety risks at the source.

Work safety shall implement the principle that those in charge of an industry must be responsible for its safety, those in charge of business operations must be responsible for safety, and those in charge of production and business activities must be responsible for safety. It shall strengthen and implement the primary responsibility of production and business entities and the regulatory responsibility of the government, and establish a mechanism featuring the responsibility of production and business entities, participation of employees, government supervision, industry self-discipline, and social oversight.

Article 4 Production and business entities must comply with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations on work safety, strengthen work safety management, establish and improve a full-staff work safety responsibility system and work safety rules and regulations, increase investment in work safety funds, materials, technology, and personnel, improve work safety conditions, strengthen the standardization and informatization of work safety, build a dual prevention mechanism for hierarchical safety risk management and control and hidden danger investigation and treatment, improve risk prevention and resolution mechanisms, enhance the level of work safety, and ensure safe production.

Production and business entities in emerging industries and fields such as the platform economy shall, based on the characteristics of their respective industries and fields, establish, improve, and implement a full-staff work safety responsibility system, strengthen work safety education and training for employees, and fulfill the work safety obligations stipulated in this Law and other laws and regulations.

Article 5 The principal person in charge of a production and business entity is the primary person responsible for work safety in that entity and shall be fully responsible for the work safety of the entity. Other persons in charge shall be responsible for work safety within their respective scopes of duties.

Article 6 Employees of production and business entities have the right to obtain work safety protection in accordance with the law and shall fulfill their obligations in work safety in accordance with the law.

Article 7 Trade unions shall supervise work safety in accordance with the law.

Trade unions of production and business entities shall, in accordance with the law, organize employees to participate in the democratic management and democratic supervision of the entity's work safety, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of employees in work safety. When a production and business entity formulates or revises rules and regulations related to work safety, it shall solicit the opinions of the trade union.

Article 8 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall formulate work safety plans based on national economic and social development plans and organize their implementation. Work safety plans shall be coordinated with relevant plans such as territorial space plans.

People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the construction of work safety infrastructure and work safety supervision capacity, with the necessary funds included in their respective budgets.

Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to establish and improve a safety risk assessment and verification mechanism, carry out industrial planning and spatial layout in accordance with safety risk management and control requirements, and implement joint prevention and control of major safety risks for production and business entities that are adjacent in location, similar in industry, or similar in business type.

Article 9 The State Council and local people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen leadership over work safety, establish and improve coordination mechanisms for work safety, support and urge relevant departments to perform their work safety supervision and management duties in accordance with the law, and promptly coordinate and resolve major problems existing in work safety supervision and management.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices, as well as development zones, industrial parks, port areas, scenic areas, etc., shall clearly define the work institutions responsible for work safety supervision and management and their duties, strengthen the construction of work safety supervision forces, conduct supervision and inspection of the work safety status of production and business entities within their respective administrative or management areas in accordance with their duties, and assist the relevant departments of the people's government or perform work safety supervision and management duties in accordance with the law as authorized.

Article 10 The emergency management department of the State Council shall, in accordance with this Law, implement comprehensive supervision and management of work safety nationwide; the emergency management departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law, implement comprehensive supervision and management of work safety within their respective administrative areas.

The relevant departments of the State Council, such as those for transport, housing and urban-rural development, water resources, and civil aviation, shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations, implement supervision and management of work safety in relevant industries and fields within their respective scopes of duties; the relevant departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations, implement supervision and management of work safety in relevant industries and fields within their respective scopes of duties. Where the responsibility for work safety supervision and management of emerging industries and fields is unclear, the local people's government at or above the county level shall determine the supervisory department based on the principle of business similarity.

The emergency management department and the departments responsible for implementing supervision and management of work safety in relevant industries and fields are collectively referred to as departments with work safety supervision and management responsibilities. Departments with work safety supervision and management responsibilities shall cooperate with each other, work together, share information, and share resources to strengthen work safety supervision and management in accordance with the law.

Article 11 The relevant departments of the State Council shall, in accordance with the requirements for ensuring work safety, promptly formulate relevant national standards or industry standards in accordance with the law, and revise them in a timely manner based on technological progress and economic development.

Production and business entities must implement the national standards or industry standards for ensuring work safety formulated in accordance with the law.

Article 12 The relevant departments of the State Council shall, according to their division of responsibilities, be responsible for the proposal, drafting, solicitation of opinions, and technical review of mandatory national standards for work safety. The emergency management department of the State Council shall coordinate the proposal of project plans for mandatory national standards for work safety. The standardization administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for the project approval, numbering, external notification, and authorized approval and publication of mandatory national standards for work safety. The standardization administrative department of the State Council and relevant departments shall, in accordance with their statutory duties, supervise and inspect the implementation of mandatory national standards for work safety.

Article 13 People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments shall adopt various forms to strengthen the publicity of laws, regulations, and knowledge related to work safety, and enhance the work safety awareness of the whole society.

Article 14 Relevant trade associations shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and their charters, provide services such as information and training on work safety to production and business entities, play a self-regulatory role, and promote production and business entities to strengthen work safety management.

Article 15 Institutions legally established to provide technical and management services for work safety shall, in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, and professional standards, accept the entrustment of production and business entities to provide technical and management services for their work safety.

Where a production and business entity entrusts an institution specified in the preceding paragraph to provide work safety technical and management services, the responsibility for ensuring work safety shall still be borne by the entity itself.

Article 16 The State implements a system of accountability for production safety accidents, and shall pursue the legal liability of the units and individuals responsible for the accident in accordance with this Law and relevant laws and regulations.

Article 17 People's governments at or above the county level shall organize departments with work safety supervision and management responsibilities to compile lists of work safety powers and responsibilities in accordance with the law, make them public, and accept social supervision.

Article 18 The State encourages and supports scientific and technological research on work safety and the promotion and application of advanced work safety technologies to improve the level of work safety.

Article 19 The State shall reward units and individuals that have achieved remarkable results in improving work safety conditions, preventing production safety accidents, and participating in rescue and emergency response.

## Chapter II Work Safety Assurance of Production and Business Entities

Article 20 A production and business entity shall meet the work safety conditions stipulated by this Law, relevant laws, administrative regulations, and national standards or industry standards; if it does not meet the work safety conditions, it shall not engage in production and business activities.

Article 21 The principal person in charge of a production and business entity shall have the following responsibilities for the work safety of the entity:

(1) Establish, improve, and implement the full-staff work safety responsibility system of the entity, and strengthen the standardization of work safety;

(2) Organize the formulation and implementation of the entity's work safety rules and regulations and operating procedures;

(3) Organize the formulation and implementation of the entity's work safety education and training plans;

(4) Ensure the effective implementation of the entity's work safety investment;

(5) Organize the establishment and implementation of a dual prevention working mechanism for hierarchical safety risk management and control and hidden danger investigation and treatment, supervise and inspect the entity's work safety, and promptly eliminate hidden dangers of production safety accidents;

(6) Organize the formulation and implementation of the entity's emergency rescue plan for production safety accidents;

(7) Report production safety accidents promptly and truthfully.

Article 22 The full-staff work safety responsibility system of a production and business entity shall clearly define the responsible persons for each position, the scope of responsibility, and the assessment standards.

Production and business entities shall establish corresponding mechanisms to strengthen the supervision and assessment of the implementation of the full-staff work safety responsibility system, ensuring the implementation of the full-staff work safety responsibility system.

Article 23 The necessary capital investment for the work safety conditions that a production and business entity should possess shall be guaranteed by the entity's decision-making body, principal person in charge, or individual business investor, who shall bear responsibility for the consequences caused by insufficient capital investment necessary for work safety.

Relevant production and business entities shall, in accordance with regulations, set aside and use work safety expenses specifically for improving work safety conditions. Work safety expenses shall be itemized and included in the cost. The specific measures for the setting aside, use, and supervision and management of work safety expenses shall be formulated by the finance department of the State Council together with the emergency management department of the State Council after soliciting opinions from the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 24 Entities engaged in mining, metal smelting, construction, transportation, and the production, operation, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials shall establish work safety management institutions or be equipped with full-time work safety management personnel.

For other production and business entities not specified in the preceding paragraph, if the number of employees exceeds one hundred, they shall establish work safety management institutions or be equipped with full-time work safety management personnel; if the number of employees is one hundred or less, they shall be equipped with full-time or part-time work safety management personnel.

Article 25 The work safety management institutions and work safety management personnel of a production and business entity shall perform the following duties:

(1) Organize or participate in the drafting of the entity's work safety rules and regulations, operating procedures, and emergency rescue plans for production safety accidents;

(2) Organize or participate in the entity's work safety education and training, and truthfully record the work safety education and training;

(3) Organize the identification and assessment of hazard sources, and supervise the implementation of the entity's safety management measures for major hazard sources;

(4) Organize or participate in the entity's emergency rescue drills;

(5) Inspect the entity's work safety status, promptly investigate hidden dangers of production safety accidents, and propose suggestions for improving work safety management;

(6) Stop and correct actions such as giving illegal commands, forcing risky operations, and violating operating procedures;

(7) Supervise the implementation of the entity's work safety rectification measures.

Production and business entities may appoint a dedicated deputy person in charge of work safety to assist the principal person in charge in performing work safety management duties.

Article 26 The work safety management institutions and work safety management personnel of a production and business entity shall be diligent and perform their duties in accordance with the law.

When a production and business entity makes business decisions involving work safety, it shall listen to the opinions of the work safety management institutions and work safety management personnel.

A production and business entity shall not reduce the wages, benefits, or other treatment of work safety management personnel, or terminate their labor contracts, because they perform their duties in accordance with the law.

The appointment and removal of work safety management personnel in entities producing and storing hazardous materials, as well as mining and metal smelting entities, shall be reported to the competent department with work safety supervision and management responsibilities.

Article 27 The principal person in charge and work safety management personnel of a production and business entity must possess the work safety knowledge and management capabilities corresponding to the production and business activities of the entity.

The principal person in charge and work safety management personnel of entities engaged in the production, operation, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials, as well as mining, metal smelting, construction, and transportation entities, shall be assessed by the competent department with work safety supervision and management responsibilities to have passed the examination on work safety knowledge and management capabilities. No fees shall be charged for the assessment.

Entities producing, storing, and loading/unloading hazardous materials, as well as mining and metal smelting entities, shall have registered safety engineers engaged in work safety management. Other production and business entities are encouraged to employ registered safety engineers for work safety management. Registered safety engineers shall be managed by specialty classification, and the specific measures shall be formulated by the human resources and social security department of the State Council and the emergency management department of the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 28 Production and business entities shall provide work safety education and training to employees to ensure that employees possess the necessary work safety knowledge, are familiar with the relevant work safety rules and regulations and safe operating procedures, master the safe operation skills for their positions, understand accident emergency response measures, and are aware of their rights and obligations in work safety. Employees who have not passed the work safety education and training shall not start work.

Where a production and business entity uses dispatched workers, it shall incorporate the dispatched workers into the unified management of the entity's employees and provide them with education and training on job safety operating procedures and safe operation skills. The labor dispatch unit shall provide the dispatched workers with necessary work safety education and training.

Where a production and business entity accepts students from secondary vocational schools or higher education institutions for internships, it shall provide the intern students with corresponding work safety education and training and necessary labor protection supplies. The school shall assist the production and business entity in providing work safety education and training to the intern students.

Production and business entities shall establish archives for work safety education and training, truthfully recording the time, content, participants, and assessment results of the work safety education and training.

Article 29 When a production and business entity adopts new processes, new technologies, new materials, or uses new equipment, it must understand and master their safety technical characteristics, adopt effective safety protection measures, and provide specialized work safety education and training to employees.

Article 30 Special operations personnel of production and business entities must, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, undergo specialized safety operation training and obtain corresponding qualifications before they can start work.

The scope of special operations personnel shall be determined by the emergency management department of the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council.

Article 31 The safety facilities of new construction, renovation, and expansion projects (hereinafter collectively referred to as construction projects) of production and business entities must be designed, constructed, and put into production and use simultaneously with the main project. The investment in safety facilities shall be included in the budget estimate of the construction project.

Article 32 Construction projects for mining, metal smelting, and those for the production, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials shall undergo safety assessment in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 33 The designer and design unit of safety facilities for a construction project shall be responsible for the design of the safety facilities.

The design of safety facilities for construction projects for mining, metal smelting, and those for the production, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials shall be submitted for review by the relevant departments in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State. The reviewing department and the personnel responsible for the review shall be responsible for the review results.

Article 34 The construction unit of construction projects for mining, metal smelting, and those for the production, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials must construct in accordance with the approved design of the safety facilities and be responsible for the engineering quality of the safety facilities.

Before a construction project for mining, metal smelting, or for the production, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous materials is completed and put into production or use, the construction entity shall be responsible for organizing the acceptance inspection of the safety facilities; only after passing the acceptance inspection can it be put into production and use. Departments with work safety supervision and management responsibilities shall strengthen the supervision and inspection of the acceptance inspection activities and results of the construction entity.

Article 35 Production and business entities shall set up obvious safety warning signs at production and business sites and on relevant facilities and equipment that have relatively high risk factors.

Article 36 The design, manufacture, installation, use, testing, maintenance, modification, and scrapping of safety equipment shall comply with national standards or industry standards.

Production and business entities must regularly maintain and service safety equipment and conduct periodic testing to ensure normal operation. Records of maintenance, servicing, and testing shall be made and signed by the relevant personnel.

Production and business entities shall not shut down or damage monitoring, alarm, protection, and lifesaving equipment and facilities directly related to production safety, or tamper with, conceal, or destroy their related data and information.

Production and business entities in industries such as catering that use gas shall install combustible gas alarm devices and ensure their normal use.

Article 37 Containers and transport vehicles for hazardous materials used by production and business entities, as well as special equipment for offshore oil extraction and special equipment for underground mines that involve personal safety and are highly dangerous, must be manufactured by specialized production units in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State, and after passing inspection and testing by testing and inspection institutions with professional qualifications, obtain a safety use certificate or safety mark before being put into use. The testing and inspection institutions shall be responsible for the testing and inspection results.

Article 38 The State implements a phase-out system for processes and equipment that seriously endanger production safety. The specific catalog shall be formulated and published by the emergency management department of the State Council together with the relevant departments of the State Council. Where laws and administrative regulations provide otherwise for the formulation of the catalog, those provisions shall apply.

The people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may, based on local actual conditions, formulate and publish specific catalogs to phase out processes and equipment that endanger production safety but are not covered by the preceding paragraph.Production and business units shall not use technologies or equipment that are eliminated as endangering production safety.

Article 39 The production, operation, transportation, storage, use of hazardous items, or disposal of hazardous waste shall be subject to examination and supervision by the relevant competent departments in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws, regulations, and national or industry standards.

Production and business units that produce, operate, transport, store, use hazardous items, or dispose of hazardous waste must comply with relevant laws, regulations, and national or industry standards, establish dedicated safety management systems, adopt reliable safety measures, and accept supervision and management implemented by the relevant competent departments in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Production and business units shall register and establish files for major hazard sources, conduct regular testing, assessment, and monitoring, formulate emergency response plans, and inform employees and relevant personnel of the emergency measures to be taken in an emergency.

Production and business units shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, report their major hazard sources and related safety measures and emergency measures to the emergency management department of the local people's government and relevant departments for record. The emergency management department of the local people's government and relevant departments shall achieve information sharing through relevant information systems.

Article 41 Production and business units shall establish a hierarchical safety risk management and control system, and adopt corresponding control measures according to the level of safety risk.

Production and business units shall establish, improve, and implement a system for investigating and controlling hidden dangers of production safety accidents, adopt technical and management measures to promptly detect and eliminate accident hazards. The investigation and control of accident hazards shall be truthfully recorded and communicated to employees through employee congresses, employee representative meetings, information bulletin boards, or other means. Among them, the investigation and control of major accident hazards shall be promptly reported to the departments responsible for production safety supervision and management and to the employee congress or employee representative meeting.

The departments responsible for production safety supervision and management of local people's governments at or above the county level shall incorporate major accident hazards into relevant information systems, establish and improve a supervision system for the control of major accident hazards, and urge production and business units to eliminate major accident hazards.

Article 42 Workshops, stores, and warehouses that produce, operate, store, or use hazardous items shall not be located in the same building as employee dormitories, and shall maintain a safe distance from employee dormitories.

Production and business premises and employee dormitories shall be equipped with exits and evacuation routes that meet emergency evacuation requirements, are clearly marked, and remain unobstructed. It is prohibited to occupy, lock, or block the exits or evacuation routes of production and business premises or employee dormitories.

Article 43 When production and business units carry out blasting, hoisting, hot work, temporary electricity use, and other hazardous operations as specified by the emergency management department of the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments of the State Council, they shall arrange for dedicated personnel to conduct on-site safety management to ensure compliance with operating procedures and the implementation of safety measures.

Article 44 Production and business units shall educate and urge employees to strictly implement the unit's production safety rules and regulations and safe operating procedures; and shall truthfully inform employees of the hazardous factors, preventive measures, and emergency measures in the workplace and work positions.

Production and business units shall pay attention to the physical and psychological conditions and behavioral habits of employees, strengthen psychological counseling and spiritual comfort for employees, strictly implement post safety responsibilities, and prevent accidents caused by abnormal employee behavior.

Article 45 Production and business units must provide employees with labor protection equipment that meets national or industry standards, and supervise and educate employees to wear and use it in accordance with usage rules.

Article 46 Safety management personnel of production and business units shall conduct regular inspections of the production safety status based on the characteristics of the unit's production and operation; safety issues found during inspections shall be dealt with immediately; if they cannot be handled, they shall promptly report to the relevant person in charge of the unit, who shall promptly address them. The inspection and handling status shall be truthfully recorded.

If safety management personnel of a production and business unit discover a major accident hazard during an inspection and report it to the relevant person in charge of the unit in accordance with the preceding paragraph, and the relevant person in charge fails to handle it promptly, the safety management personnel may report to the competent department responsible for production safety supervision and management, and the department receiving the report shall handle it promptly in accordance with the law.

Article 47 Production and business units shall allocate funds for equipping labor protection supplies and conducting production safety training.

Article 48 When two or more production and business units operate in the same work area and their activities may endanger each other's production safety, they shall sign a production safety management agreement, clarify their respective production safety management responsibilities and safety measures to be taken, and designate dedicated production safety management personnel for safety inspections and coordination.

Article 49 Production and business units shall not contract or lease production and business projects, premises, or equipment to units or individuals that do not have the conditions for safe production or corresponding qualifications.

If production and business projects or premises are contracted or leased to other units, the production and business unit shall sign a dedicated production safety management agreement with the contractor or lessee, or stipulate their respective production safety management responsibilities in the contract or lease agreement; the production and business unit shall coordinate and manage the production safety work of the contractor and lessee, conduct regular safety inspections, and promptly urge rectification if safety issues are found.

Construction units for mining, metal smelting, and construction projects for the production, storage, and loading/unloading of hazardous items shall strengthen the safety management of construction projects, shall not resell, rent, lend, affiliate, or illegally transfer construction qualifications in other forms, shall not subcontract the entire construction project to a third party or divide the entire contracted construction project into parts and subcontract them separately to a third party in the name of subcontracting, and shall not subcontract the project to units that do not have the corresponding qualifications.

Article 50 When a production safety accident occurs in a production and business unit, the principal responsible person of the unit shall immediately organize rescue and shall not leave their post without authorization during the accident investigation and handling period.

Article 51 Production and business units must participate in work-related injury insurance in accordance with the law and pay insurance premiums for employees.

The state encourages production and business units to insure production safety liability insurance; production and business units in high-risk industries and fields as stipulated by the state shall insure production safety liability insurance. The specific scope and implementation measures shall be formulated by the emergency management department of the State Council in conjunction with the finance department of the State Council, the insurance regulatory authority of the State Council, and relevant industry competent departments.

## Chapter III Rights and Obligations of Employees in Production Safety

Article 52 The labor contract signed between a production and business unit and an employee shall include matters concerning ensuring the employee's labor safety, preventing occupational hazards, and handling work-related injury insurance for the employee in accordance with the law.

Production and business units shall not, in any form, enter into agreements with employees to exempt or reduce their liability for casualties of employees caused by production safety accidents that they should bear in accordance with the law.

Article 53 Employees of a production and business unit have the right to understand the hazardous factors, preventive measures, and emergency measures in their workplace and work positions, and have the right to make suggestions on the unit's production safety work.

Article 54 Employees have the right to criticize, report, and accuse problems existing in the unit's production safety work; and have the right to refuse illegal commands and forced risky operations.

Production and business units shall not reduce the wages, benefits, or other treatment of employees, or terminate their labor contracts, because employees criticize, report, or accuse problems in the unit's production safety work, or refuse illegal commands or forced risky operations.

Article 55 When employees discover an emergency situation that directly endangers personal safety, they have the right to stop work or evacuate the workplace after taking possible emergency measures.

Production and business units shall not reduce the wages, benefits, or other treatment of employees, or terminate their labor contracts, because employees stop work or take emergency evacuation measures in the emergency situation described in the preceding paragraph.

Article 56 After a production safety accident occurs in a production and business unit, measures shall be taken promptly to treat the relevant personnel.

Employees who suffer damage due to a production safety accident, in addition to enjoying work-related injury insurance in accordance with the law, have the right to claim compensation if they are entitled to compensation under relevant civil laws.

Article 57 During the work process, employees shall strictly implement post safety responsibilities, comply with the unit's production safety rules and regulations and operating procedures, obey management, and correctly wear and use labor protection equipment.

Article 58 Employees shall receive production safety education and training, master the production safety knowledge required for their work, improve production safety skills, and enhance accident prevention and emergency response capabilities.

Article 59 When employees discover accident hazards or other unsafe factors, they shall immediately report to the on-site production safety management personnel or the person in charge of the unit; the person receiving the report shall handle it promptly.

Article 60 Trade unions have the right to supervise the simultaneous design, construction, and commissioning of safety facilities with the main project for construction projects, and to provide opinions.

Trade unions have the right to demand correction of violations of production safety laws and regulations by production and business units that infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of employees; when discovering illegal commands, forced risky operations, or accident hazards, trade unions have the right to propose solutions, and the production and business unit shall promptly study and respond; when discovering situations that endanger the safety of employees' lives, trade unions have the right to suggest that the production and business unit organize employees to evacuate the dangerous area, and the production and business unit must immediately take action.

Trade unions have the right to participate in accident investigations in accordance with the law, submit handling opinions to relevant departments, and request that the responsible persons be held accountable.

Article 61 When a production and business unit uses dispatched workers, the dispatched workers shall enjoy the rights of employees stipulated in this Law and shall fulfill the obligations of employees stipulated in this Law.

## Chapter IV Supervision and Management of Production Safety

Article 62 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall, based on the production safety situation within their administrative regions, organize relevant departments to conduct strict inspections of production and business units within their administrative regions that are prone to major production safety accidents, according to their respective responsibilities.

The emergency management department shall, in accordance with the requirements of classified and graded supervision and management, formulate an annual production safety supervision and inspection plan, and conduct supervision and inspection in accordance with the annual supervision and inspection plan. If accident hazards are found, they shall be dealt with promptly.

Article 63 Departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall, in accordance with the provisions of relevant laws and regulations, strictly review matters involving production safety that require examination and approval (including approval, verification, licensing, registration, certification, issuance of licenses, etc., hereinafter the same) or acceptance, in accordance with the production safety conditions and procedures stipulated by relevant laws, regulations, and national or industry standards; if the production safety conditions stipulated by relevant laws, regulations, and national or industry standards are not met, approval or acceptance shall not be granted. For units that engage in relevant activities without obtaining approval or passing acceptance in accordance with the law, the department responsible for administrative approval shall immediately ban them upon discovery or receipt of a report, and handle them in accordance with the law. For units that have obtained approval in accordance with the law, if the department responsible for administrative approval finds that they no longer meet the production safety conditions, it shall revoke the original approval.

Article 64 Departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall not charge fees when examining or accepting matters involving production safety; they shall not require units undergoing examination or acceptance to purchase safety equipment, devices, or other products of designated brands or from designated production or sales units.

Article 65 The emergency management department and other departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall carry out administrative law enforcement work for production safety in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the implementation of relevant production safety laws, regulations, and national or industry standards by production and business units, and exercise the following powers:

(1) Enter production and business units for inspection, review relevant materials, and inquire about the situation from relevant units and personnel;

(2) Immediately correct or order rectification within a time limit for production safety violations found during inspections; make administrative penalty decisions for acts that should be subject to administrative penalties in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and administrative regulations;

(3) Order the immediate elimination of accident hazards found during inspections; if safety cannot be guaranteed before or during the elimination of major accident hazards, order the evacuation of workers from the hazardous area, order temporary suspension of production and business or use of relevant facilities and equipment; after the major accident hazard is eliminated, production, business, and use may resume only after review and approval;

(4) Seal or seize facilities, equipment, and devices that are deemed to not meet the national or industry standards for ensuring production safety, as well as illegally produced, stored, used, operated, or transported hazardous items; seal workplaces where hazardous items are illegally produced, stored, used, or operated; and make handling decisions in accordance with the law.

Supervision and inspection shall not affect the normal production and business activities of the inspected unit.

Article 66 Production and business units shall cooperate with the inspection personnel of departments responsible for production safety supervision and management (hereinafter collectively referred to as production safety inspection personnel) in the lawful performance of their supervision and inspection duties, and shall not refuse or obstruct them.

Article 67 Production safety inspection personnel shall be loyal to their duties, adhere to principles, and enforce the law impartially.

When performing supervision and inspection tasks, production safety inspection personnel must present valid administrative law enforcement credentials; they shall keep confidential any technical secrets and business secrets of the inspected unit that come to their knowledge.

Article 68 Production safety inspection personnel shall make written records of the time, location, content, problems found, and their handling during inspections, which shall be signed by the inspection personnel and the person in charge of the inspected unit; if the person in charge of the inspected unit refuses to sign, the inspection personnel shall record the situation in the records and report it to the department responsible for production safety supervision and management.

Article 69 Departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall cooperate with each other and conduct joint inspections during supervision and inspection; if separate inspections are necessary, they shall exchange information; if safety issues are found that should be handled by other relevant departments, they shall promptly transfer them to the other relevant departments and keep records for reference, and the receiving department shall handle them promptly.

Article 70 When a department responsible for production safety supervision and management makes a decision to suspend production and business, stop construction, or stop using relevant facilities or equipment for a production and business unit with a major accident hazard in accordance with the law, the production and business unit shall implement it in accordance with the law and promptly eliminate the accident hazard. If the production and business unit refuses to implement it and there is a real risk of a production safety accident, with the approval of the principal responsible person of the department, and under the premise of ensuring safety, the department responsible for production safety supervision and management may take measures such as notifying relevant units to cut off power supply or stop supplying civil explosives to force the production and business unit to implement the decision. The notice shall be in writing, and the relevant units shall cooperate.

If the department responsible for production safety supervision and management takes measures to cut off power supply in accordance with the preceding paragraph, except in emergency situations that endanger production safety, it shall notify the production and business unit twenty-four hours in advance. If the production and business unit implements the administrative decision in accordance with the law and takes corresponding measures to eliminate the accident hazard, the department responsible for production safety supervision and management shall promptly lift the measures specified in the preceding paragraph.

Article 71 Supervisory authorities shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Supervision Law, supervise the performance of production safety supervision and management duties by departments responsible for production safety supervision and management and their staff.

Article 72 Institutions responsible for safety assessment, certification, testing, and inspection shall meet the qualification conditions stipulated by the state and be responsible for the legality and authenticity of the safety assessment, certification, testing, and inspection results they produce. The qualification conditions shall be formulated by the emergency management department of the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments of the State Council.

Institutions responsible for safety assessment, certification, testing, and inspection shall establish and implement systems for service disclosure and report disclosure, and shall not rent out qualifications, affiliate, or issue false reports.

Article 73 Departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall establish a reporting system, make public the reporting telephone number, mailbox, or email address and other online reporting platforms, and accept reports on production safety; after investigation and verification of accepted reports, written materials shall be formed; if rectification measures need to be implemented, they shall be reported to the relevant person in charge for signature and supervision of implementation. For matters that are not within the department's responsibilities and need to be investigated and handled by other relevant departments, they shall be transferred to the other relevant departments for handling.

Reports involving the death of personnel shall be investigated and handled by the people's government at or above the county level.

Article 74 Any unit or individual has the right to report or report accident hazards or production safety violations to the departments responsible for production safety supervision and management.

If a major accident hazard or a major accident is caused by a production safety violation, resulting in damage to national interests or social public interests, the People's Procuratorate may file a public interest lawsuit in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Civil Procedure Law and the Administrative Procedure Law.

Article 75 When residents' committees and villagers' committees discover accident hazards or production safety violations in production and business units within their areas, they shall report to the local people's government or relevant departments.

Article 76 People's governments at or above the county level and their relevant departments shall reward individuals who have made meritorious contributions by reporting major accident hazards or reporting production safety violations. The specific reward measures shall be formulated by the emergency management department of the State Council in conjunction with the finance department of the State Council.

Article 77 News, publishing, broadcasting, film, television, and other units have the obligation to conduct public welfare publicity and education on production safety, and have the right to exercise public opinion supervision over acts that violate production safety laws and regulations.

Article 78 Departments responsible for production safety supervision and management shall establish a database of production safety violation information, truthfully record the production safety violation information of production and business units and their relevant employees; for production and business units and their relevant employees with serious violations, they shall promptly announce them to the public and notify the industry competent departments, investment competent departments, natural resources competent departments, ecological environment competent departments, securities regulatory authorities, and relevant financial institutions. Relevant departments and institutions shall take joint disciplinary measures such as increasing the frequency of law enforcement inspections, suspending project approvals, raising relevant insurance rates, and imposing industry or occupational bans on production and business units and their relevant employees with dishonest behavior, and shall make them public.The department responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall strengthen the timely collection, sharing, application, and disclosure of administrative penalty information on production and business units. Within seven working days after making a penalty decision, it shall publicly disclose the information on the supervision and administration department's public disclosure system, thereby enhancing social supervision over illegal and untrustworthy production and business units and their relevant employees, and improving the level of integrity in production safety across society.

## Chapter 5: Emergency Rescue and Investigation and Handling of Production Safety Accidents

Article 79 The State shall strengthen the construction of emergency response capabilities for production safety accidents, establish emergency rescue bases and teams in key industries and fields, and coordinate and command them uniformly through the national production safety emergency rescue agency; it shall encourage production and business units and other social forces to establish emergency rescue teams, equip them with corresponding emergency rescue equipment and materials, and improve the professional level of emergency rescue.

The emergency management department of the State Council shall take the lead in establishing a unified national production safety accident emergency rescue information system. Relevant departments of the State Council, such as transportation, housing and urban-rural construction, water resources, and civil aviation, as well as local people's governments at or above the county level, shall establish and improve production safety accident emergency rescue information systems for relevant industries, fields, and regions, achieving interconnection and information sharing. By promoting online safety information collection, safety supervision, and monitoring and early warning, the precision and intelligence of supervision shall be enhanced.

Article 80 Local people's governments at or above the county level shall organize relevant departments to formulate emergency rescue plans for production safety accidents within their administrative regions and establish an emergency rescue system.

Township people's governments and sub-district offices, as well as development zones, industrial parks, port areas, scenic spots, etc., shall formulate corresponding emergency rescue plans for production safety accidents and assist the relevant departments of the people's government or perform the duties of production safety accident emergency rescue in accordance with authorization and the law.

Article 81 Production and business units shall formulate their own emergency rescue plans for production safety accidents, which shall be aligned with the emergency rescue plans formulated by the local people's government at or above the county level, and shall organize regular drills.

Article 82 Units that produce, store, or transport hazardous materials, as well as mining, metal smelting, urban rail transit operation, and construction units, shall establish emergency rescue organizations; if the scale of production and business operations is small, an emergency rescue organization may not be established, but part-time emergency rescue personnel shall be designated.

Units that produce, store, transport, or use hazardous materials, as well as mining, metal smelting, urban rail transit operation, and construction units, shall be equipped with necessary emergency rescue equipment, devices, and materials, and shall perform regular maintenance and upkeep to ensure normal operation.

Article 83 After a production safety accident occurs in a production and business unit, the relevant personnel at the accident site shall immediately report to the person in charge of the unit.

Upon receiving the accident report, the person in charge of the unit shall promptly take effective measures to organize rescue, prevent the accident from escalating, reduce casualties and property losses, and immediately and truthfully report to the local department responsible for production safety supervision and administration in accordance with relevant state regulations. They shall not conceal, falsely report, or delay the report, nor shall they intentionally damage the accident scene or destroy relevant evidence.

Article 84 Upon receiving an accident report, the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall immediately report the accident situation to higher authorities in accordance with relevant state regulations. The department responsible for production safety supervision and administration and the relevant local people's government shall not conceal, falsely report, or delay the report of the accident situation.

Article 85 After receiving a report of a production safety accident, the responsible persons of the relevant local people's government and the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall immediately rush to the accident scene in accordance with the requirements of the emergency rescue plan for production safety accidents and organize the accident rescue.

Departments and units participating in the accident rescue shall obey unified command, strengthen coordination and linkage, take effective emergency rescue measures, and take measures such as cordoning off and evacuating as required by the rescue needs, to prevent the accident from escalating and the occurrence of secondary disasters, and to reduce casualties and property losses.

During the accident rescue process, necessary measures shall be taken to avoid or reduce harm to the environment.

Any unit or individual shall support and cooperate with the accident rescue and provide all convenient conditions.

Article 86 Accident investigation and handling shall follow the principles of scientific rigor, compliance with laws and regulations, seeking truth from facts, and focusing on effectiveness. The cause of the accident shall be promptly and accurately identified, the nature and responsibility of the accident shall be determined, the emergency response work shall be evaluated, lessons from the accident shall be summarized, corrective measures shall be proposed, and handling suggestions for the responsible units and personnel shall be made. The accident investigation report shall be promptly disclosed to the public in accordance with the law. The specific measures for accident investigation and handling shall be formulated by the State Council.

The unit where the accident occurred shall promptly and comprehensively implement corrective measures, and the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall strengthen supervision and inspection.

The relevant departments of the State Council and local people's governments responsible for accident investigation and handling shall, within one year after approving the accident investigation report, organize relevant departments to evaluate the implementation of accident rectification and preventive measures, and promptly disclose the evaluation results to the public; for units and personnel who fail to perform their duties, leading to the non-implementation of accident rectification and preventive measures, responsibility shall be pursued in accordance with relevant regulations.

Article 87 If a production safety accident occurs in a production and business unit and is determined to be a liability accident after investigation, in addition to identifying the responsibility of the accident unit and pursuing it according to law, the responsibility of the administrative department that has the duty of reviewing, approving, and supervising matters related to production safety shall also be identified. For acts of dereliction of duty or malfeasance, legal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the provisions of Article 90 of this Law.

Article 88 No unit or individual may obstruct or interfere with the lawful investigation and handling of accidents.

Article 89 The emergency management department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall regularly conduct statistical analysis of the production safety accidents occurring within its administrative region and regularly disclose the information to the public.

## Chapter 6: Legal Liability

Article 90 If a staff member of the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration commits any of the following acts, they shall be given a demotion or dismissal sanction; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law:

(1) Approving or passing the acceptance of matters involving production safety that do not meet the statutory production safety conditions;

(2) Failing to ban or handle according to law units that engage in relevant activities without obtaining approval or acceptance according to law, or failing to handle after receiving a report;

(3) Failing to perform supervision and management duties for units that have obtained approval according to law, failing to revoke the original approval upon discovering that they no longer meet production safety conditions, or failing to investigate and deal with production safety violations discovered;

(4) Failing to promptly handle major accident hazards discovered during supervision and inspection according to law.

If a staff member of the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration commits acts of abuse of power, dereliction of duty, or engaging in malpractices for personal gain other than those specified in the preceding paragraph, they shall be given sanctions according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 91 If a department responsible for production safety supervision and administration requires the unit under review or acceptance to purchase its designated safety equipment, devices, or other products, or collects fees during the review or acceptance of production safety matters, its superior authority or supervisory authority shall order it to make corrections and order the return of the fees collected; if the circumstances are serious, the directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 92 If an institution responsible for safety assessment, certification, testing, or inspection issues a false report, it shall be ordered to suspend operations for rectification and fined not less than 30,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if it causes damage to others, it shall bear compensation liability according to law.

If an institution responsible for safety assessment, certification, testing, or inspection lends its qualifications, engages in affiliation, or issues a false report, its illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the illegal gains are more than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed singly or concurrently; its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if it causes damage to others, it shall bear joint and several compensation liability with the production and business unit; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

For an institution and its directly responsible personnel that commit the illegal acts specified in the preceding paragraph, their corresponding qualifications and credentials shall be revoked, and they shall not engage in safety assessment, certification, testing, inspection, or other related work for five years; if the circumstances are serious, a lifetime industry and occupational ban shall be imposed.

Article 93 If the decision-making body, principal responsible person, or individual business investor of a production and business unit fails to ensure the necessary capital investment for production safety in accordance with the provisions of this Law, resulting in the production and business unit not meeting production safety conditions, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and provide the necessary funds; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, the production and business unit shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification.

If the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph leads to a production safety accident, the principal responsible person of the production and business unit shall be given a dismissal sanction, and the individual business investor shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 94 If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit fails to perform the production safety management duties stipulated in this Law, they shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if they fail to make corrections within the specified period, they shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan, and the production and business unit shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification.

If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit commits the illegal act specified in the preceding paragraph, leading to a production safety accident, they shall be given a dismissal sanction; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit receives criminal punishment or a dismissal sanction in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, they shall not serve as the principal responsible person of any production and business unit for five years from the date of completion of the sentence or the date of the sanction; if they are responsible for a major or particularly major production safety accident, they shall be permanently prohibited from serving as the principal responsible person of a production and business unit in that industry.

Article 95 If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit fails to perform the production safety management duties stipulated in this Law, leading to a production safety accident, the emergency management department shall impose a fine in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For a general accident, a fine of 40% of the previous year's annual income;

(2) For a relatively large accident, a fine of 60% of the previous year's annual income;

(3) For a major accident, a fine of 80% of the previous year's annual income;

(4) For a particularly major accident, a fine of 100% of the previous year's annual income.

Article 96 If other responsible persons and production safety management personnel of a production and business unit fail to perform the production safety management duties stipulated in this Law, they shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 30,000 yuan; if this leads to a production safety accident, their qualifications related to production safety shall be suspended or revoked, and they shall be fined not less than 20% but not more than 50% of the previous year's annual income; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 97 If a production and business unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not more than 100,000 yuan; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan:

(1) Failing to establish a production safety management organization or to equip production safety management personnel or registered safety engineers as required;

(2) The principal responsible person and production safety management personnel of units producing, storing, transporting, or loading and unloading hazardous materials, as well as mining, metal smelting, construction, and transportation units, failing to pass the required assessment as required;

(3) Failing to provide production safety education and training to employees, dispatched workers, or intern students as required, or failing to truthfully inform them of relevant production safety matters as required;

(4) Failing to truthfully record production safety education and training situations;

(5) Failing to truthfully record the investigation and management of accident hazards or failing to notify employees;

(6) Failing to formulate emergency rescue plans for production safety accidents as required or failing to organize regular drills;

(7) Special operations personnel failing to undergo specialized safety operation training and obtain corresponding qualifications as required before taking up their posts.

Article 98 If a production and business unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to stop construction or suspend production or business for rectification, make corrections within a specified period, and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 500,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be fined not less than 500,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law:

(1) Failing to conduct a safety assessment for mining or metal smelting construction projects, or for construction projects used for producing, storing, or loading and unloading hazardous materials, as required;

(2) Mining or metal smelting construction projects, or construction projects used for producing, storing, or loading and unloading hazardous materials, lacking safety facility design, or the safety facility design not being submitted to the relevant department for review and approval as required;

(3) The construction unit of mining or metal smelting construction projects, or construction projects used for producing, storing, or loading and unloading hazardous materials, failing to construct according to the approved safety facility design;

(4) Before mining or metal smelting construction projects, or construction projects used for producing, storing, or loading and unloading hazardous materials, are completed and put into production or use, the safety facilities have not passed acceptance inspection.

Article 99 If a production and business unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; if the circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law:

(1) Failing to set up obvious safety warning signs in production and business premises and on relevant facilities and equipment with relatively large risk factors;

(2) The installation, use, testing, modification, and scrapping of safety equipment not conforming to national standards or industry standards;

(3) Failing to perform regular maintenance, upkeep, and periodic testing of safety equipment;

(4) Shutting down or destroying monitoring, alarm, protection, or life-saving equipment and facilities directly related to production safety, or tampering with, concealing, or destroying their related data or information;

(5) Failing to provide employees with labor protection articles that meet national standards or industry standards;

(6) Containers and transport vehicles for hazardous materials, as well as special equipment for offshore oil extraction and special equipment for underground mines that involve personal safety and are relatively dangerous, being put into use without passing inspection and testing by a professionally qualified institution and obtaining a safety use certificate or safety mark;

(7) Using processes or equipment that should be eliminated and endanger production safety;

(8) Production and business units in the catering and other industries using gas without installing combustible gas alarm devices.

Article 100 If anyone produces, operates, transports, stores, or uses hazardous materials, or disposes of waste hazardous materials, without approval according to law, they shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of laws and administrative regulations on the safety management of hazardous materials; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 101 If a production and business unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not more than 100,000 yuan; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification and fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 50,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law:

(1) When producing, operating, transporting, storing, or using hazardous materials, or disposing of waste hazardous materials, failing to establish a special safety management system or take reliable safety measures;

(2) Failing to register and file major hazard sources, failing to conduct periodic testing, assessment, and monitoring, failing to formulate emergency plans, or failing to inform of emergency measures;

(3) When conducting blasting, hoisting, hot work, temporary electricity use, and other dangerous operations specified by the emergency management department of the State Council in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, failing to arrange for specialized personnel to conduct on-site safety management;

(4) Failing to establish a safety risk classification and control system or failing to take corresponding control measures according to the safety risk level;
(5) Failing to establish an accident hazard investigation and management system, or failing to report the investigation and management of major accident hazards as required.

Article 102 If a production and business unit fails to take measures to eliminate accident hazards, it shall be ordered to eliminate them immediately or within a specified period and fined not more than 50,000 yuan; if the production and business unit refuses to comply, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 103 If a production and business unit contracts or leases its production and business projects, premises, or equipment to a unit or individual that does not meet production safety conditions or corresponding qualifications, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and its illegal gains shall be confiscated; if the illegal gains are more than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed singly or concurrently; its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan; if this leads to a production safety accident causing damage to others, it shall bear joint and several compensation liability with the contractor or lessee.

If a production and business unit fails to sign a special production safety management agreement with the contractor or lessee, or fails to clarify their respective production safety management responsibilities in the contract or lease agreement, or fails to coordinate and manage the production safety of the contractor or lessee in a unified manner, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and fined not more than 50,000 yuan, and its directly responsible supervisor and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined not more than 10,000 yuan; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production or business for rectification.If the construction unit of a mine, a metal smelting project, or a construction project for the production, storage, or loading and unloading of hazardous materials fails to conduct safety management of the construction project as required, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and be fined not more than 100,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not more than 20,000 yuan. If corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production for rectification. If the above construction unit resells, leases, lends, affiliates, or illegally transfers construction qualifications in any other form, it shall be ordered to suspend production for rectification, its qualification certificate shall be revoked, and illegal gains shall be confiscated. If the illegal gains are 100,000 yuan or more, a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the illegal gains shall be imposed; if there are no illegal gains or the illegal gains are less than 100,000 yuan, a fine of not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan shall be imposed alone or concurrently. The directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 104 If two or more production and business units operate in the same work area and may endanger each other's production safety, and they fail to sign a production safety management agreement or designate a dedicated production safety manager for safety inspections and coordination, they shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not more than 10,000 yuan. If corrections are not made within the specified period, they shall be ordered to suspend production.

Article 105 If a production and business unit commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and be fined not more than 50,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not more than 10,000 yuan. If corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend production for rectification. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law:

(1) The workshop, store, or warehouse for producing, operating, storing, or using hazardous materials is located in the same building as the employee dormitory, or the distance from the employee dormitory does not meet safety requirements;

(2) The production and business premises and employee dormitories are not equipped with exits and evacuation passages that meet emergency evacuation needs, have clear signs, and remain unobstructed, or the exits and evacuation passages of the production and business premises or employee dormitories are occupied, locked, or blocked.

Article 106 If a production and business unit enters into an agreement with an employee to waive or reduce its liability for compensation for the employee's casualties caused by a production safety accident, the agreement shall be void; the principal responsible person of the production and business unit or the individual business investor shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan.

Article 107 If an employee of a production and business unit fails to fulfill post safety responsibilities, disobeys management, or violates production safety rules and regulations or operating procedures, the production and business unit shall give criticism and education, and impose sanctions in accordance with relevant rules and regulations. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 108 If, in violation of this Law, a production and business unit refuses or obstructs the lawful supervision and inspection by the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration, it shall be ordered to make corrections. If it refuses to make corrections, it shall be fined not less than 20,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be fined not less than 10,000 yuan but not more than 20,000 yuan. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 109 If a production and business unit in a high-risk industry or field fails to insure production safety liability insurance as required by the state, it shall be ordered to make corrections within a specified period and be fined not less than 50,000 yuan but not more than 100,000 yuan. If corrections are not made within the specified period, it shall be fined not less than 100,000 yuan but not more than 200,000 yuan.

Article 110 If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit fails to immediately organize rescue when a production safety accident occurs in the unit, or leaves the post without authorization or absconds during the accident investigation and handling, he or she shall be given a demotion or dismissal, and the emergency management department shall impose a fine of 60% to 100% of the previous year's annual income. If he or she absconds, he or she shall be detained for not more than 15 days. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

If the principal responsible person of a production and business unit conceals, falsely reports, or delays reporting a production safety accident, he or she shall be punished in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 111 If the relevant local people's government or the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration conceals, falsely reports, or delays reporting a production safety accident, the directly responsible person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be given sanctions in accordance with the law. If the act constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 112 If a production and business unit violates this Law, is ordered to make corrections and fined, but refuses to make corrections, the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration may impose a daily fine based on the original penalty amount starting from the day after the date of the order to make corrections.

Article 113 If a production and business unit falls under any of the following circumstances, the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall request the local people's government to close it down, and the relevant departments shall revoke its relevant licenses and certificates in accordance with the law. The principal responsible person of the production and business unit shall not serve as the principal responsible person of any production and business unit for five years; if the circumstances are serious, he or she shall be permanently prohibited from serving as the principal responsible person of a production and business unit in the industry:

(1) There is a major accident hazard, and it has been subject to administrative penalties under this Law three times within 180 days or four times within one year;

(2) After suspension of production for rectification, it still does not meet the production safety conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and national standards or industry standards;

(3) It does not meet the production safety conditions stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, and national standards or industry standards, resulting in a major or particularly major production safety accident;

(4) It refuses to implement the decision to suspend production for rectification made by the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration.

Article 114 If a production safety accident occurs, in addition to requiring the responsible production and business unit to bear corresponding compensation and other liabilities in accordance with the law, the emergency management department shall impose a fine in accordance with the following provisions:

(1) For a general accident, a fine of not less than 300,000 yuan but not more than 1,000,000 yuan;

(2) For a larger accident, a fine of not less than 1,000,000 yuan but not more than 2,000,000 yuan;

(3) For a major accident, a fine of not less than 2,000,000 yuan but not more than 10,000,000 yuan;

(4) For a particularly major accident, a fine of not less than 10,000,000 yuan but not more than 20,000,000 yuan.

If a production safety accident occurs with particularly serious circumstances and particularly adverse effects, the emergency management department may impose a fine of not less than two times but not more than five times the penalty amount specified in the preceding paragraph on the responsible production and business unit.

Article 115 The administrative penalties stipulated in this Law shall be decided by the emergency management department and other departments responsible for production safety supervision and administration according to their respective responsibilities. Among them, administrative penalties that should be imposed on production and business units in the civil aviation, railway, and electric power industries and their principal responsible persons in accordance with Articles 95, 110, and 114 of this Law may also be imposed by the competent department responsible for production safety supervision and administration. The administrative penalty of closure shall be reported by the department responsible for production safety supervision and administration to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision in accordance with the authority stipulated by the State Council. The administrative penalty of detention shall be decided by the public security organ in accordance with the provisions on public security administration penalties.

Article 116 If a production safety accident causes casualties or property damage to others, the production and business unit shall bear compensation liability in accordance with the law. If it refuses to bear the liability or its responsible person absconds, the people's court shall enforce the law.

If the person responsible for a production safety accident fails to bear compensation liability in accordance with the law, and after the people's court takes enforcement measures in accordance with the law, is still unable to provide full compensation to the victim, he or she shall continue to fulfill the compensation obligation. If the victim discovers that the responsible person has other property, he or she may request the people's court to enforce it at any time.

## Chapter 7 Supplementary Provisions

Article 117 The meanings of the following terms in this Law:

Hazardous materials refer to flammable and explosive items, dangerous chemicals, radioactive items, and other items that can endanger personal safety and property safety.

Major hazard sources refer to units (including sites and facilities) where hazardous materials are produced, handled, used, or stored on a long-term or temporary basis, and the quantity of hazardous materials equals or exceeds the critical quantity.

Article 118 The classification standards for general, larger, major, and particularly major production safety accidents stipulated in this Law shall be stipulated by the State Council.

The emergency management department of the State Council and other departments responsible for production safety supervision and administration shall, according to their respective responsibilities, formulate identification standards for major hazard sources and judgment standards for major accident hazards in relevant industries and fields.

Article 119 This Law shall come into force on November 1, 2002.
