# Anti-Domestic Violence Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 18th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 12th National People's Congress on December 27, 2015

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to prevent and stop domestic violence, protect the lawful rights and interests of family members, maintain equal, harmonious, and civilized family relationships, and promote family harmony and social stability.

Article 2 For the purpose of this Law, domestic violence refers to physical, psychological, or other forms of harm inflicted by family members on each other through acts such as beating, binding, mutilation, restriction of personal freedom, frequent verbal abuse, and intimidation.

Article 3 Family members shall help each other, care for each other, live in harmony, and fulfill family obligations.

Opposing domestic violence is a shared responsibility of the state, society, and every family.

The state prohibits any form of domestic violence.

Article 4 The institutions responsible for women's and children's affairs under people's governments at or above the county level shall organize, coordinate, guide, and supervise relevant departments in carrying out anti-domestic violence work.

Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, judicial organs, people's organizations, social organizations, residents' committees, villagers' committees, enterprises, and public institutions shall, in accordance with this Law and relevant legal provisions, carry out anti-domestic violence work.

People's governments at all levels shall provide necessary financial guarantees for anti-domestic violence work.

Article 5 Anti-domestic violence work shall follow the principle of prevention first, combined with education, correction, and punishment.

Anti-domestic violence work shall respect the true wishes of victims and protect the privacy of the parties involved.

Special protection shall be given to minors, the elderly, persons with disabilities, pregnant and breastfeeding women, and persons with serious illnesses who suffer domestic violence.

## Chapter II Prevention of Domestic Violence

Article 6 The state shall carry out publicity and education on family virtues, disseminate knowledge about anti-domestic violence, and enhance citizens' awareness of opposing domestic violence.

Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, and federations of persons with disabilities shall, within their respective scopes of work, organize and carry out publicity and education on family virtues and anti-domestic violence.

Radio, television, newspapers, and the internet shall conduct publicity on family virtues and anti-domestic violence.

Schools and kindergartens shall carry out education on family virtues and anti-domestic violence.

Article 7 Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, judicial organs, and women's federations shall incorporate the prevention and cessation of domestic violence into professional training and statistical work.

Medical institutions shall keep diagnosis and treatment records for victims of domestic violence.

Article 8 Township people's governments and sub-district offices shall organize and carry out domestic violence prevention work, and residents' committees, villagers' committees, and social work service institutions shall cooperate and assist.

Article 9 People's governments at all levels shall support social organizations such as social work service institutions in providing services including psychological counseling, family relationship guidance, and education on domestic violence prevention knowledge.

Article 10 People's mediation organizations shall mediate family disputes in accordance with the law to prevent and reduce the occurrence of domestic violence.

Article 11 If an employer discovers that a member of its staff is involved in domestic violence, it shall provide criticism and education and properly mediate and resolve the family conflict.

Article 12 Guardians of minors shall conduct family education in a civilized manner, fulfill their guardianship and educational duties in accordance with the law, and shall not commit domestic violence.

## Chapter III Handling of Domestic Violence

Article 13 Victims of domestic violence, their legal representatives, and close relatives may file complaints, reports, or seek help from the unit where the perpetrator or victim works, residents' committees, villagers' committees, women's federations, or other relevant units. Upon receiving such complaints, reports, or requests for help, the relevant units shall provide assistance and handle the matter.

Victims of domestic violence, their legal representatives, and close relatives may also report the case to the public security organs or file a lawsuit with the people's court in accordance with the law.

Any unit or individual has the right to promptly dissuade ongoing domestic violence.

Article 14 Schools, kindergartens, medical institutions, residents' committees, villagers' committees, social work service institutions, relief management agencies, welfare institutions, and their staff, upon discovering in the course of their work that a person with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct is suffering or is suspected of suffering domestic violence, shall promptly report the case to the public security organs. The public security organs shall keep the information of the reporter confidential.

Article 15 Upon receiving a report of domestic violence, the public security organs shall promptly dispatch police to stop the domestic violence, investigate and collect evidence in accordance with relevant regulations, and assist the victim in seeking medical treatment and having injuries assessed.

If a person with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct suffers serious physical injury from domestic violence, faces a threat to personal safety, or is in a dangerous state such as being unattended, the public security organs shall notify and assist the civil affairs department in placing them in temporary shelters, relief management agencies, or welfare institutions.

Article 16 If the circumstances of domestic violence are relatively minor and do not warrant public security administration penalties according to law, the public security organs shall give the perpetrator criticism and education or issue a warning letter.

The warning letter shall include the perpetrator's identity information, a statement of the facts of the domestic violence, and a prohibition against the perpetrator committing domestic violence.

Article 17 The public security organs shall deliver the warning letter to the perpetrator and the victim and notify the residents' committee and villagers' committee.

Residents' committees, villagers' committees, and police stations shall conduct follow-up visits to the perpetrator and victim who received the warning letter and supervise the perpetrator to ensure no further domestic violence occurs.

Article 18 People's governments at the county level or at the level of a city divided into districts may establish temporary shelters independently or rely on relief management agencies to provide temporary living assistance to victims of domestic violence.

Article 19 Legal aid agencies shall provide legal aid to victims of domestic violence in accordance with the law.

People's courts shall, in accordance with the law, defer, reduce, or waive litigation costs for victims of domestic violence.

Article 20 When hearing cases involving domestic violence, people's courts may determine the facts of domestic violence based on evidence such as police dispatch records, warning letters, and injury assessment opinions from public security organs.

Article 21 If a guardian commits domestic violence that seriously infringes upon the lawful rights and interests of the ward, the people's court may, upon application by the ward's close relatives, residents' committee, villagers' committee, civil affairs department of the people's government at the county level, or other relevant persons or units, revoke the guardian's qualification in accordance with the law and appoint another guardian.

The perpetrator whose guardian qualification has been revoked shall continue to bear the corresponding expenses for support, maintenance, and upbringing.

Article 22 Trade unions, the Communist Youth League, women's federations, federations of persons with disabilities, residents' committees, villagers' committees, etc., shall conduct legal education for perpetrators of domestic violence and, when necessary, may provide psychological counseling to both the perpetrator and the victim.

## Chapter IV Personal Safety Protection Orders

Article 23 If a party suffers domestic violence or faces a real risk of domestic violence and applies to the people's court for a personal safety protection order, the people's court shall accept the application.

If the party is a person with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct, or is unable to apply for a personal safety protection order due to coercion, intimidation, or other reasons, their close relatives, public security organs, women's federations, residents' committees, villagers' committees, or relief management agencies may apply on their behalf.

Article 24 An application for a personal safety protection order shall be made in writing; if it is truly difficult to make a written application, an oral application may be made, and the people's court shall record it in the case file.

Article 25 Cases involving personal safety protection orders shall be under the jurisdiction of the basic people's court at the place of residence of the applicant or the respondent, or at the place where the domestic violence occurred.

Article 26 Personal safety protection orders shall be issued by the people's court in the form of a ruling.

Article 27 To issue a personal safety protection order, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) There is a clearly identified respondent;

(2) There are specific requests;

(3) There are circumstances of suffering domestic violence or facing a real risk of domestic violence.

Article 28 After accepting an application, the people's court shall issue a personal safety protection order or reject the application within seventy-two hours; in urgent circumstances, it shall do so within twenty-four hours.

Article 29 A personal safety protection order may include the following measures:

(1) Prohibiting the respondent from committing domestic violence;

(2) Prohibiting the respondent from harassing, stalking, or contacting the applicant or their close relatives;

(3) Ordering the respondent to move out of the applicant's residence;

(4) Other measures to protect the personal safety of the applicant.

Article 30 The validity period of a personal safety protection order shall not exceed six months and shall take effect from the date of issuance. Before the personal safety protection order becomes invalid, the people's court may, upon the applicant's application, revoke, modify, or extend it.

Article 31 If the applicant disagrees with the rejection of the application or the respondent disagrees with the personal safety protection order, they may apply for one reconsideration to the people's court that issued the ruling within five days from the date the ruling takes effect. If the people's court issues a personal safety protection order in accordance with the law, the execution of the personal safety protection order shall not be suspended during the reconsideration period.

Article 32 After issuing a personal safety protection order, the people's court shall serve it on the applicant, the respondent, the public security organs, and relevant organizations such as residents' committees and villagers' committees. The personal safety protection order shall be executed by the people's court, and the public security organs, residents' committees, villagers' committees, etc., shall assist in its execution.

## Chapter V Legal Liability

Article 33 If a perpetrator commits domestic violence constituting a violation of public security administration, they shall be subject to public security administration penalties in accordance with the law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

Article 34 If a respondent violates a personal safety protection order and it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, the people's court shall give a reprimand and may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose a fine of not more than 1,000 yuan or detention for not more than 15 days.

Article 35 If schools, kindergartens, medical institutions, residents' committees, villagers' committees, social work service institutions, relief management agencies, welfare institutions, or their staff fail to report to the public security organs in accordance with Article 14 of this Law, resulting in serious consequences, the higher competent authority or the unit itself shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel in accordance with the law.

Article 36 If state functionaries responsible for anti-domestic violence work neglect their duties, abuse their power, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, they shall be subject to sanctions in accordance with the law; if it constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 37 Violent acts committed between persons who live together but are not family members shall be handled with reference to the provisions of this Law.

Article 38 This Law shall come into effect on March 1, 2016.
