# Labor Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 8th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eighth National People's Congress on July 5, 1994

First Amendment on August 27, 2009, according to the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress

Second Amendment on December 29, 2018, according to the Decision on Amending Seven Laws Including the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 7th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Thirteenth National People's Congress

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is formulated in accordance with the Constitution to protect the legitimate rights and interests of laborers, regulate labor relations, establish and maintain a labor system suited to the socialist market economy, and promote economic development and social progress.

Article 2 This Law applies to enterprises and individual economic organizations (hereinafter collectively referred to as employing units) within the territory of the People's Republic of China and the laborers who form labor relations with them.

State organs, public institutions, social organizations, and the laborers who establish contractual labor relations with them shall implement this Law.

Article 3 Laborers have the right to equal employment and choice of occupation, the right to obtain remuneration for labor, the right to rest and leave, the right to protection of labor safety and health, the right to receive vocational skills training, the right to enjoy social insurance and welfare, the right to submit labor disputes for handling, and other labor rights stipulated by law.

Laborers shall complete their labor tasks, improve their vocational skills, implement labor safety and health regulations, and observe labor discipline and professional ethics.

Article 4 Employing units shall establish and improve rules and regulations in accordance with the law to ensure that laborers enjoy their labor rights and fulfill their labor obligations.

Article 5 The State shall adopt various measures to promote employment, develop vocational education, formulate labor standards, regulate social income, improve social insurance, coordinate labor relations, and gradually raise the living standards of laborers.

Article 6 The State advocates that laborers participate in voluntary social labor, carry out labor competitions and rationalization suggestion activities, encourage and protect laborers in scientific research, technological innovation, and invention creation, and commend and reward model workers and advanced workers.

Article 7 Laborers have the right to participate in and organize trade unions in accordance with the law.

Trade unions represent and safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of laborers and independently carry out activities in accordance with the law.

Article 8 Laborers, in accordance with the provisions of the law, participate in democratic management or conduct equal negotiations with employing units on the protection of the legitimate rights and interests of laborers through the general meeting of employees, the congress of employees' representatives, or other forms.

Article 9 The labor administrative department of the State Council shall be in charge of labor work nationwide.

The labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of labor work within their respective administrative regions.

## Chapter II Promotion of Employment

Article 10 The State shall create employment conditions and expand employment opportunities by promoting economic and social development.

The State encourages enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations to establish industries or expand operations within the scope stipulated by laws and administrative regulations to increase employment.

The State supports laborers in voluntarily organizing for employment and engaging in self-employment to achieve employment.

Article 11 Local people's governments at various levels shall take measures to develop various types of employment service agencies and provide employment services.

Article 12 Laborers shall not be discriminated against in employment on the grounds of ethnicity, race, gender, or religious belief.

Article 13 Women enjoy equal employment rights with men. When recruiting employees, no employing unit may refuse to recruit women or raise recruitment standards for women on the grounds of gender, except for types of work or positions that are not suitable for women as stipulated by the State.

Article 14 Where laws and regulations have special provisions on the employment of persons with disabilities, persons from ethnic minorities, and demobilized military personnel, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 15 Employing units are prohibited from recruiting minors under the age of sixteen.

Units in the arts, sports, and special crafts that recruit minors under the age of sixteen must comply with the relevant provisions of the State and guarantee their right to compulsory education.

## Chapter III Labor Contracts and Collective Contracts

Article 16 A labor contract is an agreement between a laborer and an employing unit to establish a labor relationship and clarify the rights and obligations of both parties.

A labor contract shall be concluded when establishing a labor relationship.

Article 17 The conclusion and modification of a labor contract shall follow the principles of equality, voluntariness, and consensus through consultation, and shall not violate the provisions of laws and administrative regulations.

A labor contract concluded in accordance with the law shall have legal binding force from the date of its conclusion. The parties must fulfill the obligations stipulated in the labor contract.

Article 18 The following labor contracts are invalid:

(1) Labor contracts that violate the provisions of laws and administrative regulations;

(2) Labor contracts concluded through means such as fraud or coercion.

An invalid labor contract shall have no legal binding force from the time of its conclusion. If the invalidity of part of a labor contract does not affect the validity of the remaining parts, the remaining parts shall remain valid.

The invalidity of a labor contract shall be confirmed by a labor dispute arbitration committee or a people's court.

Article 19 A labor contract shall be concluded in writing and shall contain the following clauses:

(1) The term of the labor contract;

(2) The content of the work;

(3) Labor protection and working conditions;

(4) Labor remuneration;

(5) Labor discipline;

(6) Conditions for the termination of the labor contract;

(7) Liability for breach of the labor contract.

In addition to the essential clauses specified in the preceding paragraph, the parties may negotiate and agree on other contents.

Article 20 The term of a labor contract is divided into fixed-term, open-ended, and term based on the completion of a specific task.

If a laborer has worked continuously for the same employing unit for ten years or more and both parties agree to renew the labor contract, if the laborer proposes to conclude an open-ended labor contract, an open-ended labor contract shall be concluded.

Article 21 A probation period may be stipulated in a labor contract. The probation period shall not exceed six months at most.

Article 22 The parties to a labor contract may stipulate in the labor contract matters concerning the confidentiality of the employing unit's trade secrets.

Article 23 A labor contract shall terminate upon the expiration of its term or upon the occurrence of the conditions for termination agreed upon by the parties.

Article 24 A labor contract may be rescinded upon the consensus of the parties to the labor contract through consultation.

Article 25 An employing unit may rescind a labor contract if the laborer falls under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Being proven during the probation period not to meet the recruitment conditions;

(2) Seriously violating labor discipline or the rules and regulations of the employing unit;

(3) Being seriously derelict in duty, engaging in malpractice for personal gain, and causing major damage to the interests of the employing unit;

(4) Being investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 26 An employing unit may rescind a labor contract under any of the following circumstances, but shall notify the laborer in writing thirty days in advance:

(1) The laborer is ill or injured not related to work, and after the period of medical treatment expires, is unable to engage in the original work or any other work arranged by the employing unit;

(2) The laborer is incompetent for the job and, after training or adjustment of the work post, remains incompetent;

(3) The objective circumstances based on which the labor contract was concluded have undergone major changes, rendering the original labor contract unperformable, and the parties fail to reach an agreement on modifying the labor contract through consultation.

Article 27 If an employing unit is on the verge of bankruptcy and undergoing a statutory reorganization period, or if its production and operation are experiencing serious difficulties, and it truly needs to reduce its workforce, it shall explain the situation to the trade union or all employees thirty days in advance, listen to the opinions of the trade union or the employees, and may reduce its workforce after reporting to the labor administrative department.

If an employing unit reduces its workforce in accordance with the provisions of this Article and recruits personnel within six months, it shall give priority to recruiting the personnel who were laid off.

Article 28 If an employing unit rescinds a labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Articles 24, 26, and 27 of this Law, it shall provide economic compensation in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State.

Article 29 An employing unit shall not rescind a labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Articles 26 and 27 of this Law if the laborer falls under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Suffering from an occupational disease or being injured at work and being confirmed as having lost or partially lost the ability to work;

(2) Being ill or injured and within the prescribed period of medical treatment;

(3) A female employee being during pregnancy, childbirth, or the nursing period;

(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 30 If an employing unit rescinds a labor contract, and the trade union considers it inappropriate, the trade union has the right to express its opinion. If the employing unit violates laws, regulations, or the labor contract, the trade union has the right to request a re-handling; if the laborer applies for arbitration or initiates a lawsuit, the trade union shall provide support and assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 31 A laborer who rescinds a labor contract shall notify the employing unit in writing thirty days in advance.

Article 32 A laborer may notify the employing unit at any time to rescind the labor contract under any of the following circumstances:

(1) During the probation period;

(2) The employing unit forces the laborer to work by means of violence, threats, or illegal restriction of personal freedom;

(3) The employing unit fails to pay labor remuneration or provide working conditions as agreed in the labor contract.

Article 33 The employees of an enterprise may conclude a collective contract with the enterprise on matters such as labor remuneration, working hours, rest and leave, labor safety and health, and insurance and welfare. The draft collective contract shall be submitted to the congress of employees' representatives or all employees for discussion and approval.

The collective contract shall be concluded between the trade union, representing the employees, and the enterprise; in an enterprise where no trade union has been established, the contract shall be concluded between representatives elected by the employees and the enterprise.

Article 34 After a collective contract is concluded, it shall be submitted to the labor administrative department; if the labor administrative department does not raise any objection within fifteen days from the date of receiving the text of the collective contract, the collective contract shall take effect.

Article 35 A collective contract concluded in accordance with the law shall be binding on both the enterprise and all its employees. The standards for working conditions and labor remuneration stipulated in individual labor contracts concluded between employees and the enterprise shall not be lower than those stipulated in the collective contract.

## Chapter IV Working Hours, Rest, and Leave

Article 36 The State implements a working hour system in which laborers work no more than eight hours per day and an average of no more than forty-four hours per week.

Article 37 For laborers engaged in piecework, the employing unit shall reasonably determine their labor quotas and piecework remuneration standards based on the working hour system stipulated in Article 36 of this Law.

Article 38 The employing unit shall ensure that laborers have at least one day off per week.

Article 39 If an enterprise cannot implement the provisions of Articles 36 and 38 of this Law due to the characteristics of its production, it may implement other working and rest methods with the approval of the labor administrative department.

Article 40 The employing unit shall arrange for laborers to take leave during the following holidays in accordance with the law:

(1) New Year's Day;

(2) Spring Festival;

(3) International Labor Day;

(4) National Day;

(5) Other holidays stipulated by laws and regulations.

Article 41 If an employing unit needs to extend working hours due to production or operational needs, it may do so after consultation with the trade union and the laborers. Generally, the extension shall not exceed one hour per day; if it needs to be extended for special reasons, under the condition of ensuring the health of the laborers, the extension shall not exceed three hours per day, but shall not exceed thirty-six hours per month.

Article 42 The extension of working hours shall not be subject to the restrictions of Article 41 of this Law under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Occurrence of natural disasters, accidents, or other reasons that threaten the life, health, and property safety of laborers and require urgent handling;

(2) Breakdown of production equipment, transportation lines, or public facilities that affects production and public interests and requires urgent repair;

(3) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 43 Employing units shall not extend the working hours of laborers in violation of the provisions of this Law.

Article 44 Under any of the following circumstances, the employing unit shall pay labor remuneration higher than the normal working hours' wages according to the following standards:

(1) Arranging for laborers to work extended hours, pay no less than 150 percent of the wages;

(2) Arranging for laborers to work on rest days without being able to arrange compensatory leave, pay no less than 200 percent of the wages;

(3) Arranging for laborers to work on statutory holidays, pay no less than 300 percent of the wages.

Article 45 The State implements a system of paid annual leave.

Laborers who have worked continuously for one year or more shall enjoy paid annual leave. The specific measures shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter V Wages

Article 46 The distribution of wages shall follow the principle of distribution according to work and implement equal pay for equal work.

The level of wages shall be gradually increased on the basis of economic development. The State exercises macro-control over the total amount of wages.

Article 47 Employing units shall independently determine their own wage distribution methods and wage levels in accordance with the law, based on their production and operational characteristics and economic benefits.

Article 48 The State implements a minimum wage guarantee system. The specific standards for minimum wages shall be stipulated by the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government and reported to the State Council for record.

The wages paid by employing units to laborers shall not be lower than the local minimum wage standard.

Article 49 The following factors shall be comprehensively considered when determining and adjusting the minimum wage standard:

(1) The minimum living expenses of the laborer and the average number of dependents;

(2) The average social wage level;

(3) Labor productivity;

(4) Employment conditions;

(5) Differences in the level of economic development between regions.

Article 50 Wages shall be paid in monetary form to the laborer personally on a monthly basis. Wages shall not be deducted or delayed without justification.

Article 51 The employing unit shall pay wages in accordance with the law during statutory holidays, marriage and funeral leave, and when laborers participate in social activities in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Labor Safety and Health

Article 52 Employing units must establish and improve labor safety and health systems, strictly implement national labor safety and health regulations and standards, provide laborers with education on labor safety and health, prevent accidents during labor, and reduce occupational hazards.

Article 53 Labor safety and health facilities must meet the standards stipulated by the State.

The labor safety and health facilities of new construction, renovation, and expansion projects must be designed, constructed, and put into production and use simultaneously with the main project.

Article 54 Employing units must provide laborers with labor safety and health conditions that meet the standards stipulated by the State and necessary labor protection supplies, and conduct regular health checks for laborers engaged in operations with occupational hazards.

Article 55 Laborers engaged in special types of work must undergo specialized training and obtain qualifications for special types of work.

Article 56 Laborers must strictly observe safety operation procedures during labor.

Laborers have the right to refuse to carry out orders from the management personnel of the employing unit that violate regulations and force risky operations; they have the right to criticize, report, and accuse behaviors that endanger life safety and health.

Article 57 The State establishes a system for the statistical reporting and handling of casualty accidents and occupational diseases. The labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level, relevant departments, and employing units shall, in accordance with the law, conduct statistics, reports, and handling of casualty accidents occurring to laborers during labor and the occupational disease conditions of laborers.

## Chapter VII Special Protection for Female Employees and Juvenile Workers

Article 58 The State provides special labor protection for female employees and juvenile workers.

Juvenile workers refer to laborers who have reached the age of sixteen but are under the age of eighteen.

Article 59 It is prohibited to arrange for female employees to engage in work in mines and pits, work at the fourth level of physical labor intensity as stipulated by the State, and other work that is prohibited for them.

Article 60 Female employees shall not be arranged to engage in work at heights, in low temperatures, in cold water, or work at the third level of physical labor intensity as stipulated by the State during their menstrual period.

Article 61 Female employees shall not be arranged to engage in work at the third level of physical labor intensity as stipulated by the State or work prohibited during pregnancy during their pregnancy. Female employees who are seven months or more pregnant shall not be arranged to work extended hours or night shifts.

Article 62 Female employees shall enjoy no less than ninety days of maternity leave for childbirth.

Article 63 Female employees shall not be arranged to engage in work at the third level of physical labor intensity as stipulated by the State or other work prohibited during the nursing period while nursing an infant under one year old, and shall not be arranged to work extended hours or night shifts.

Article 64 Juvenile workers shall not be arranged to engage in work in mines and pits, work involving toxic or harmful substances, work at the fourth level of physical labor intensity as stipulated by the State, or other work prohibited for them.

Article 65 Employing units shall conduct regular health checks for juvenile workers.

## Chapter VIII Vocational Training

Article 66 The State shall develop vocational training undertakings through various channels and by adopting various measures, develop the vocational skills of laborers, improve the quality of laborers, and enhance their employability and work ability.

Article 67 People's governments at various levels shall incorporate the development of vocational training into their social and economic development plans, encourage and support qualified enterprises, public institutions, social organizations, and individuals to conduct various forms of vocational training.

Article 68 Employing units shall establish vocational training systems, allocate and use vocational training funds in accordance with the provisions of the State, and conduct vocational training for laborers in a planned manner based on their actual conditions.

Laborers engaged in technical types of work must undergo training before taking up their posts.

Article 69 The State determines occupational classifications, formulates vocational skill standards for specified occupations, implements a vocational qualification certificate system, and employs registered assessment and certification bodies to conduct vocational skill assessments and certifications for laborers.

## Chapter IX Social Insurance and Welfare

Article 70 The State develops social insurance undertakings, establishes a social insurance system, and sets up social insurance funds to enable laborers to receive assistance and compensation in cases of old age, illness, work-related injury, unemployment, childbirth, etc.

Article 71 The level of social insurance shall be compatible with the level of social and economic development and the social affordability.

Article 72 The sources of funds for social insurance funds shall be determined according to the type of insurance, and social pooling shall be gradually implemented. Employing units and laborers must participate in social insurance and pay social insurance premiums in accordance with the law.

Article 73 Laborers shall enjoy social insurance benefits in accordance with the law under the following circumstances:

(1) Retirement;

(2) Illness or injury;

(3) Work-related disability or occupational disease;

(4) Unemployment;

(5) Childbirth.

After the death of a laborer, their surviving family members shall enjoy survivor benefits in accordance with the law.

The conditions and standards for laborers to enjoy social insurance benefits shall be stipulated by laws and regulations.

The social insurance benefits enjoyed by laborers must be paid in full and on time.Article 74 Social insurance fund management institutions shall, in accordance with the law, collect, manage, and operate social insurance funds, and bear the responsibility of preserving and increasing the value of such funds.

Social insurance fund supervision institutions shall, in accordance with the law, supervise the collection, management, and operation of social insurance funds.

The establishment and functions of social insurance fund management institutions and social insurance fund supervision institutions shall be prescribed by law.

No organization or individual may misappropriate social insurance funds.

Article 75 The State encourages employing units to establish supplementary insurance for workers based on their actual circumstances.

The State advocates that workers engage in personal savings insurance.

Article 76 The State develops social welfare undertakings, constructs public welfare facilities, and provides conditions for workers' rest, recuperation, and convalescence.

Employing units shall create conditions to improve collective welfare and enhance the welfare benefits of workers.

## Chapter X Labor Disputes

Article 77 In the event of a labor dispute between an employing unit and a worker, the parties may apply for mediation, arbitration, or initiate legal proceedings in accordance with the law, or may resolve the matter through negotiation.

The principle of mediation applies to arbitration and litigation procedures.

Article 78 Labor disputes shall be resolved in accordance with the principles of legality, fairness, and timely handling, safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of the parties involved in the labor dispute according to law.

Article 79 After a labor dispute arises, the parties may apply for mediation to the labor dispute mediation committee of their employing unit; if mediation fails and one party requests arbitration, they may apply for arbitration to the labor dispute arbitration committee. A party may also directly apply for arbitration to the labor dispute arbitration committee. If dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court.

Article 80 A labor dispute mediation committee may be established within an employing unit. The labor dispute mediation committee shall be composed of representatives of workers, representatives of the employing unit, and representatives of the trade union. The chairperson of the labor dispute mediation committee shall be held by a representative of the trade union.

If an agreement is reached through mediation of a labor dispute, the parties shall fulfill it.

Article 81 The labor dispute arbitration committee shall be composed of representatives of the labor administrative department, representatives of the trade union at the same level, and representatives from the employing unit side. The chairperson of the labor dispute arbitration committee shall be held by a representative of the labor administrative department.

Article 82 A party requesting arbitration shall submit a written application to the labor dispute arbitration committee within sixty days from the date the labor dispute arises. The arbitration award shall generally be made within sixty days from the date of receiving the arbitration application. If no objection is raised to the arbitration award, the parties must comply with it.

Article 83 If a party to a labor dispute is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the arbitration award. If one party neither files a lawsuit within the statutory period nor complies with the arbitration award, the other party may apply to the People's Court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 84 If a dispute arises over the conclusion of a collective contract and the parties fail to resolve it through negotiation, the labor administrative department of the local People's Government may organize relevant parties for coordinated handling.

If a dispute arises over the performance of a collective contract and the parties fail to resolve it through negotiation, they may apply for arbitration to the labor dispute arbitration committee; if dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within fifteen days from the date of receiving the arbitration award.

## Chapter XI Supervision and Inspection

Article 85 The labor administrative departments of People's Governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the compliance of employing units with labor laws and regulations, have the authority to stop acts violating labor laws and regulations, and order corrections.

Article 86 When performing official duties, inspectors from the labor administrative departments of People's Governments at or above the county level have the authority to enter employing units to understand the implementation of labor laws and regulations, inspect necessary materials, and examine labor sites.

When performing official duties, inspectors from the labor administrative departments of People's Governments at or above the county level must present their credentials, enforce the law impartially, and comply with relevant regulations.

Article 87 Relevant departments of People's Governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, supervise the compliance of employing units with labor laws and regulations.

Article 88 Trade unions at all levels shall, in accordance with the law, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of workers and supervise the compliance of employing units with labor laws and regulations.

Any organization or individual has the right to report and accuse acts violating labor laws and regulations.

## Chapter XII Legal Liability

Article 89 If the labor rules and regulations formulated by an employing unit violate the provisions of laws or regulations, the labor administrative department shall issue a warning and order corrections;

If damage is caused to workers, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 90 If an employing unit violates the provisions of this Law by extending workers' working hours, the labor administrative department shall issue a warning, order corrections, and may impose a fine.

Article 91 If an employing unit commits any of the following acts infringing upon the legitimate rights and interests of workers, the labor administrative department shall order the payment of wages, economic compensation, and may order the payment of compensation:

(1) Deducting or delaying payment of workers' wages without reason;

(2) Refusing to pay workers' wages for extended working hours;

(3) Paying workers' wages below the local minimum wage standard;

(4) Failing to provide workers with economic compensation in accordance with the provisions of this Law after terminating a labor contract.

Article 92 If the labor safety facilities and labor hygiene conditions of an employing unit do not meet national standards or if the employing unit fails to provide workers with necessary labor protection supplies and facilities, the labor administrative department or relevant departments shall order corrections and may impose a fine; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be submitted to the People's Government at or above the county level for a decision to order suspension of operations for rectification; if measures are not taken against accident hazards, resulting in a major accident causing loss of life or property to workers, the responsible persons shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 93 If an employing unit forces workers to operate in violation of regulations and take risks, resulting in a major casualty accident with serious consequences, the responsible persons shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 94 If an employing unit illegally employs minors under the age of sixteen, the labor administrative department shall order corrections and impose a fine; if the circumstances are serious, the market supervision and management department shall revoke the business license.

Article 95 If an employing unit violates the provisions of this Law on the protection of female workers and minor workers, infringing upon their legitimate rights and interests, the labor administrative department shall order corrections and impose a fine; if damage is caused to female workers or minor workers, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 96 If an employing unit commits any of the following acts, the public security organ shall impose a detention of up to fifteen days, a fine, or a warning on the responsible persons; if a crime is constituted, the responsible persons shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law:

(1) Forcing workers to work by means of violence, threats, or illegal restriction of personal freedom;

(2) Insulting, physically punishing, beating, illegally searching, or detaining workers.

Article 97 If an invalid contract is concluded due to the fault of the employing unit, causing damage to workers, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 98 If an employing unit terminates a labor contract in violation of the conditions stipulated in this Law or deliberately delays in concluding a labor contract, the labor administrative department shall order corrections; if damage is caused to workers, compensation liability shall be borne.

Article 99 If an employing unit hires a worker who has not yet terminated their labor contract with the original employing unit, causing economic losses to the original employing unit, the employing unit shall bear joint and several compensation liability in accordance with the law.

Article 100 If an employing unit fails to pay social insurance premiums without reason, the labor administrative department shall order payment within a specified period; if payment is not made within the specified period, a late fee may be imposed.

Article 101 If an employing unit unreasonably obstructs the labor administrative department, relevant departments, or their staff from exercising supervision and inspection powers, or retaliates against whistleblowers, the labor administrative department or relevant departments shall impose a fine; if a crime is constituted, the responsible persons shall be investigated for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 102 If a worker terminates a labor contract in violation of the conditions stipulated in this Law or violates confidentiality obligations agreed upon in the labor contract, causing economic losses to the employing unit, compensation liability shall be borne in accordance with the law.

Article 103 If staff members of the labor administrative department or relevant departments abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, and if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if no crime is constituted, administrative sanctions shall be imposed.

Article 104 If state functionaries or staff members of social insurance fund management institutions misappropriate social insurance funds, and if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

Article 105 If acts violating the provisions of this Law infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of workers, and other laws or administrative regulations have already stipulated penalties, those laws or administrative regulations shall apply.

## Chapter XIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 106 The People's Governments of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, based on this Law and the actual circumstances of their regions, formulate implementation steps for the labor contract system and report them to the State Council for record.

Article 107 This Law shall come into effect as of January 1, 1995.
