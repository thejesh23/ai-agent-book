# Mine Safety Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Seventh National People's Congress on November 7, 1992

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending Certain Laws adopted at the 10th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the Eleventh National People's Congress on August 27, 2009

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted to ensure safety in mine production, prevent mine accidents, protect the personal safety of mine workers, and promote the development of the mining industry.

Article 2 This Law must be observed in the extraction of mineral resources within the territory of the People's Republic of China and in other sea areas under the jurisdiction of the People's Republic of China.

Article 3 Mining enterprises must have facilities to ensure safe production, establish and improve safety management systems, take effective measures to improve workers' working conditions, strengthen mine safety management, and ensure safe production.

Article 4 The labor administrative department of the State Council shall exercise unified supervision over mine safety work nationwide.

The labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise unified supervision over mine safety work within their respective administrative regions.

The departments in charge of mining enterprises under the people's governments at or above the county level shall manage mine safety work.

Article 5 The State encourages scientific and technological research on mine safety, promotes advanced technologies, improves safety facilities, and enhances the level of mine safety production.

Article 6 Units and individuals that achieve notable results in adhering to mine safety production, preventing mine accidents, participating in mine rescue and emergency response, and conducting scientific and technological research on mine safety shall be rewarded.

## Chapter II Safety Assurance in Mine Construction

Article 7 The safety facilities of a mine construction project must be designed, constructed, and put into production and use simultaneously with the main project.

Article 8 The design documents for a mine construction project must comply with mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications, and must be approved by the department in charge of mining enterprises in accordance with state regulations; if they do not comply with mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications, they shall not be approved.

The design of safety facilities for a mine construction project must be reviewed with the participation of the labor administrative department.

Mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications shall be formulated by the department in charge of mining enterprises under the State Council.

Article 9 The following items in mine design must comply with mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications:

(1) The ventilation system of the mine, including air supply volume, air quality, and air velocity;
(2) The slope angle of open-pit mines and the width and height of benches;
(3) The power supply system;
(4) The hoisting and transportation systems;
(5) The waterproofing and drainage systems, and the fire prevention and extinguishing systems;
(6) The gas prevention and dust prevention systems;
(7) Other items related to mine safety.

Article 10 Every mine must have at least two safe exits accessible to personnel, and the straight-line horizontal distance between the exits must comply with mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications.

Article 11 Mines must have transportation and communication facilities that are connected to the outside world and meet safety requirements.

Article 12 Mine construction projects must be constructed in accordance with the design documents approved by the department in charge of mining enterprises.

Upon completion of the safety facilities of a mine construction project, they must be inspected and accepted by the department in charge of mining enterprises, with the participation of the labor administrative department; if they do not comply with mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications, they shall not be accepted or put into production.

## Chapter III Safety Assurance in Mine Extraction

Article 13 Mine extraction must meet the conditions for ensuring safe production and comply with the mine safety regulations and industry technical specifications applicable to the extraction of different minerals.

Article 14 Ore pillars and rock pillars designated for retention in mine design shall be protected within the specified period and shall not be extracted or destroyed.

Article 15 Equipment, materials, protective gear, and safety testing instruments with special safety requirements used in mines must comply with national safety standards or industry safety standards; if they do not comply, they shall not be used.

Article 16 Mining enterprises must regularly inspect and maintain electromechanical equipment, their protective devices, and safety testing instruments to ensure safe use.

Article 17 Mining enterprises must test for toxic and hazardous substances in the workplace and the oxygen content in underground air to ensure compliance with safety requirements.

Article 18 Mining enterprises must take preventive measures against the following hazards that endanger safety:

(1) Roof falls, rib spalling, slope sliding, and surface subsidence;
(2) Gas explosions and coal dust explosions;
(3) Rock bursts, gas outbursts, and blowouts;
(4) Fires and water hazards on the surface and underground;
(5) Hazards caused by blasting materials and blasting operations;
(6) Hazards caused by dust, toxic and hazardous gases, radioactive substances, and other harmful substances;
(7) Other hazards.

Article 19 Mining enterprises shall take preventive measures against hazards that may arise from the use of machinery and electrical equipment, waste dumps, spoil heaps, tailings ponds, and after mine closure.

## Chapter IV Safety Management of Mining Enterprises

Article 20 Mining enterprises must establish and improve a safety production responsibility system.

The mine manager shall be responsible for the safety production work of the enterprise.

Article 21 The mine manager shall regularly report on safety production work to the workers' congress or the general meeting of workers, and shall give play to the supervisory role of the workers' congress.

Article 22 Workers of mining enterprises must comply with relevant mine safety laws, regulations, and enterprise rules and regulations.

Workers of mining enterprises have the right to criticize, report, and accuse acts that endanger safety.

Article 23 The trade union of a mining enterprise shall, in accordance with the law, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of workers in production safety and organize workers to supervise mine safety work.

Article 24 If a mining enterprise violates relevant safety laws and regulations, the trade union has the right to request the enterprise administration or relevant departments to handle the matter seriously.

When a mining enterprise holds meetings to discuss matters related to safety production, trade union representatives shall participate, and the trade union has the right to put forward opinions and suggestions.

Article 25 If the trade union of a mining enterprise discovers that the enterprise administration gives illegal commands, forces workers to work under hazardous conditions, or finds obvious major accident hazards and occupational hazards during the production process, it has the right to propose solutions; if it finds situations that endanger the life safety of workers, it has the right to suggest to the enterprise administration that workers be organized to evacuate the dangerous site, and the enterprise administration must promptly make a decision on handling the matter.

Article 26 Mining enterprises must provide safety education and training to workers; those who have not received safety education and training shall not be assigned to work.

Special operations personnel for safety production in mining enterprises must receive specialized training and may only work after passing an assessment and obtaining an operation qualification certificate.

Article 27 The mine manager must pass an assessment, possess specialized safety knowledge, and have the ability to lead safety production and handle mine accidents.

Safety work personnel of mining enterprises must possess the necessary specialized safety knowledge and experience in mine safety work.

Article 28 Mining enterprises must provide workers with labor protection supplies necessary to ensure safe production.

Article 29 Mining enterprises shall not employ minors to work underground in mines.

Mining enterprises shall implement special labor protection for female workers in accordance with state regulations and shall not assign female workers to work underground in mines.

Article 30 Mining enterprises must formulate measures to prevent mine accidents and organize their implementation.

Article 31 Mining enterprises shall establish rescue and medical emergency organizations composed of full-time or part-time personnel, and equip them with necessary equipment, materials, and medicines.

Article 32 Mining enterprises must, in accordance with state regulations, set aside special funds for safety technical measures from the sales revenue of mineral products. The special funds for safety technical measures must be fully used to improve mine safety production conditions and shall not be misappropriated for other purposes.

## Chapter V Supervision and Management of Mine Safety

Article 33 The labor administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise the following supervisory duties over mine safety work:

(1) Inspect the implementation of mine safety laws and regulations by mining enterprises and the departments in charge of mining enterprises;
(2) Participate in the design review and completion acceptance of safety facilities for mine construction projects;
(3) Inspect mine labor conditions and safety status;
(4) Inspect the safety education and training work for workers in mining enterprises;
(5) Supervise the allocation and use of special funds for safety technical measures by mining enterprises;
(6) Participate in and supervise the investigation and handling of mine accidents;
(7) Other supervisory duties as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 34 The departments in charge of mining enterprises under the people's governments at or above the county level shall exercise the following management duties over mine safety work:

(1) Inspect the implementation of mine safety laws and regulations by mining enterprises;
(2) Review and approve the design of safety facilities for mine construction projects;
(3) Be responsible for the completion acceptance of safety facilities for mine construction projects;
(4) Organize the training of mine managers and safety work personnel of mining enterprises;
(5) Investigate and handle major mine accidents;
(6) Other management duties as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 35 Mine safety supervisors of the labor administrative department have the right to enter mining enterprises to inspect safety conditions on site; if they find emergency dangers that endanger worker safety, they shall require the mining enterprise to deal with them immediately.

## Chapter VI Handling of Mine Accidents

Article 36 When a mine accident occurs, the mining enterprise must immediately organize rescue efforts to prevent the accident from escalating, reduce casualties and property losses, and must promptly and truthfully report the casualty accident to the labor administrative department and the department in charge of mining enterprises.

Article 37 For ordinary mine accidents, the mining enterprise shall be responsible for investigation and handling.

For major mine accidents, the government, relevant departments, trade unions, and the mining enterprise shall conduct investigation and handling in accordance with the provisions of administrative regulations.

Article 38 Mining enterprises shall provide pensions or compensation to workers injured or killed in mine accidents in accordance with state regulations.

Article 39 After a mine accident occurs, the danger at the site shall be eliminated as soon as possible, the cause of the accident shall be identified, and preventive measures shall be proposed. Production may resume only after the danger at the site has been eliminated.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 40 In violation of the provisions of this Law, any of the following acts shall be ordered to make corrections by the labor administrative department, and a fine may also be imposed; if the circumstances are serious, the matter shall be submitted to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision to order suspension of production for rectification; the person in charge and the directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions by their unit or the competent authority at a higher level:

(1) Failing to provide safety education and training to workers and assigning them to work;
(2) Using equipment, materials, protective gear, or safety testing instruments that do not comply with national safety standards or industry safety standards;
(3) Failing to allocate or use special funds for safety technical measures in accordance with regulations;
(4) Refusing on-site inspection by mine safety supervisors or concealing accident hazards or failing to truthfully report the situation during inspection;
(5) Failing to report mine accidents promptly and truthfully in accordance with regulations.

Article 41 If the mine manager lacks specialized safety knowledge, or if special operations personnel for safety production work without obtaining an operation qualification certificate, the labor administrative department shall order corrections within a specified time limit; if corrections are not made within the time limit, the matter shall be submitted to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision to order suspension of production, and production may resume only after qualified personnel are assigned.

Article 42 If the design of safety facilities for a mine construction project is constructed without approval, the department in charge of mining enterprises shall order the suspension of construction; if the order is not complied with, the department in charge of mining enterprises shall submit the matter to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision to have the relevant department revoke its mining license and business license.

Article 43 If the safety facilities of a mine construction project are put into production without acceptance or fail acceptance, the labor administrative department, together with the department in charge of mining enterprises, shall order the suspension of production, and the labor administrative department shall impose a fine; if production is not stopped, the labor administrative department shall submit the matter to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision to have the relevant department revoke its mining license and business license.

Article 44 If a mining enterprise already in production forcibly extracts minerals without meeting safety production conditions, the labor administrative department, together with the department in charge of mining enterprises, shall order improvements within a specified time limit; if safety production conditions are still not met after the time limit, the labor administrative department shall submit the matter to the people's government at or above the county level for a decision to order suspension of production for rectification or to have the relevant department revoke its mining license and business license.

Article 45 If a party disagrees with an administrative penalty decision, it may apply for reconsideration to the organ at the next higher level of the organ that made the penalty decision within 15 days from the date of receiving the notice of the penalty decision; the party may also directly file a lawsuit with the people's court within 15 days from the date of receiving the notice of the penalty decision.

The reconsideration organ shall make a reconsideration decision within 60 days from the date of receiving the reconsideration application. If the party disagrees with the reconsideration decision, it may file a lawsuit with the people's court within 15 days from the date of receiving the reconsideration decision. If the reconsideration organ fails to make a reconsideration decision within the time limit, the party may file a lawsuit with the people's court within 15 days from the expiration of the reconsideration period.

If the party neither applies for reconsideration nor files a lawsuit with the people's court within the time limit, nor complies with the penalty decision, the organ that made the penalty decision may apply to the people's court for compulsory enforcement.

Article 46 If a person in charge of a mining enterprise gives illegal commands or forces workers to work under hazardous conditions, thereby causing a major casualty accident, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 47 If a person in charge of a mining enterprise fails to take measures against mine accident hazards, thereby causing a major casualty accident, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Criminal Law.

Article 48 If mine safety supervisors or safety management personnel abuse their power, neglect their duties, or engage in malpractice for personal gain, constituting a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with the law; if it does not constitute a crime, administrative sanctions shall be imposed.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 49 The labor administrative department of the State Council shall formulate implementing regulations based on this Law, which shall be submitted to the State Council for approval before implementation.

The standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government may formulate implementation measures based on this Law and the actual conditions of their respective regions.

Article 50 This Law shall come into effect as of May 1, 1993.
