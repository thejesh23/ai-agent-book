# Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the 28th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress on June 29, 2007

Amended in accordance with the Decision on Amending the Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China adopted at the 30th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 11th National People's Congress on December 28, 2012

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of improving the labor contract system, clarifying the rights and obligations of the parties to labor contracts, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of workers, and building and developing harmonious and stable labor relations.

Article 2 This Law applies to the establishment of labor relations between, and the conclusion, performance, modification, rescission or termination of labor contracts by, organizations such as enterprises, individually-owned businesses, and private non-enterprise units within the territory of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as employing units) and workers.

State organs, public institutions, social organizations, and workers with whom they establish labor relations shall conclude, perform, modify, rescind or terminate labor contracts in accordance with this Law.

Article 3 The conclusion of a labor contract shall follow the principles of lawfulness, fairness, equality, voluntariness, mutual agreement, and good faith.

A lawfully concluded labor contract is binding. The employing unit and the worker shall perform the obligations stipulated in the labor contract.

Article 4 An employing unit shall establish and improve labor rules and regulations in accordance with the law, ensuring that workers enjoy their labor rights and perform their labor obligations.

When an employing unit formulates, modifies, or decides on rules and regulations or major matters that directly involve the vital interests of workers, such as labor remuneration, working hours, rest and leave, occupational safety and health, insurance and welfare, worker training, labor discipline, and labor quota management, it shall submit a draft plan and opinions to the workers' congress or all workers for discussion, and then determine them through equal consultation with the trade union or worker representatives.

During the implementation of rules and regulations or decisions on major matters, if the trade union or workers deem them inappropriate, they have the right to raise the issue with the employing unit and seek modification and improvement through consultation.

The employing unit shall publicize or inform workers of rules and regulations and decisions on major matters that directly involve their vital interests.

Article 5 The labor administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, together with trade unions and representatives of enterprises, establish and improve a tripartite mechanism for coordinating labor relations to jointly study and resolve major issues concerning labor relations.

Article 6 Trade unions shall assist and guide workers in lawfully concluding and performing labor contracts with employing units, and establish collective consultation mechanisms with employing units to safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of workers.

## Chapter II Conclusion of Labor Contracts

Article 7 An employing unit establishes a labor relationship with a worker from the date it starts using the worker. The employing unit shall establish a roster of workers for reference.

Article 8 When recruiting a worker, the employing unit shall truthfully inform the worker of the job content, working conditions, work location, occupational hazards, safety production conditions, labor remuneration, and other information that the worker requests to know. The employing unit has the right to know basic information about the worker that is directly related to the labor contract, and the worker shall truthfully provide such information.

Article 9 When recruiting a worker, the employing unit shall not detain the worker's resident identity card or other documents, nor shall it require the worker to provide a guarantee or collect property from the worker in any other name.

Article 10 To establish a labor relationship, a written labor contract shall be concluded.

If a labor relationship has been established but a written labor contract has not been concluded at the same time, a written labor contract shall be concluded within one month from the date the employing unit starts using the worker.

If the employing unit and the worker conclude a labor contract before the worker starts working, the labor relationship is established from the date the employing unit starts using the worker.

Article 11 If the employing unit does not conclude a written labor contract at the same time as it starts using the worker, and the labor remuneration agreed upon with the worker is unclear, the labor remuneration for the newly recruited worker shall be determined according to the standards stipulated in the collective contract. If there is no collective contract or the collective contract does not stipulate such standards, equal pay for equal work shall be implemented.

Article 12 Labor contracts are divided into fixed-term labor contracts, open-ended labor contracts, and labor contracts that expire upon the completion of a specific task.

Article 13 A fixed-term labor contract is a labor contract in which the employing unit and the worker agree on a termination time for the contract.

The employing unit and the worker may conclude a fixed-term labor contract through mutual agreement.

Article 14 An open-ended labor contract is a labor contract in which the employing unit and the worker agree on no fixed termination time.

The employing unit and the worker may conclude an open-ended labor contract through mutual agreement. Under any of the following circumstances, if the worker proposes or agrees to renew or conclude a labor contract, an open-ended labor contract shall be concluded, unless the worker proposes to conclude a fixed-term labor contract:

(1) The worker has worked continuously for the employing unit for ten years or more;
(2) When the employing unit initially implements the labor contract system or when a state-owned enterprise re-concludes labor contracts due to restructuring, the worker has worked continuously for the employing unit for ten years or more and is less than ten years away from the statutory retirement age;
(3) The worker has concluded two consecutive fixed-term labor contracts and does not fall under the circumstances specified in Article 39 or Items (1) and (2) of Article 40 of this Law, and the labor contract is to be renewed.

If the employing unit fails to conclude a written labor contract with the worker within one year from the date it starts using the worker, it shall be deemed that the employing unit and the worker have concluded an open-ended labor contract.

Article 15 A labor contract that expires upon the completion of a specific task is a labor contract in which the employing unit and the worker agree that the contract term is based on the completion of a specific job.

The employing unit and the worker may conclude a labor contract that expires upon the completion of a specific task through mutual agreement.

Article 16 A labor contract becomes effective upon the mutual agreement of the employing unit and the worker, and after both parties sign or affix their seals to the text of the labor contract.

The employing unit and the worker shall each hold one copy of the labor contract text.

Article 17 A labor contract shall contain the following clauses:

(1) The name, domicile, and legal representative or principal responsible person of the employing unit;
(2) The name, address, and resident identity card number or other valid identification document number of the worker;
(3) The term of the labor contract;
(4) The job content and work location;
(5) Working hours, rest and leave;
(6) Labor remuneration;
(7) Social insurance;
(8) Labor protection, working conditions, and protection against occupational hazards;
(9) Other matters that laws and regulations require to be included in a labor contract.

In addition to the essential clauses specified in the preceding paragraph, the employing unit and the worker may agree on other matters such as probation period, training, confidentiality, supplementary insurance, and welfare benefits.

Article 18 If the standards for labor remuneration, working conditions, etc., stipulated in the labor contract are unclear, leading to a dispute, the employing unit and the worker may renegotiate. If negotiation fails, the provisions of the collective contract shall apply. If there is no collective contract or the collective contract does not stipulate labor remuneration, equal pay for equal work shall be implemented. If there is no collective contract or the collective contract does not stipulate standards for working conditions, etc., the relevant state regulations shall apply.

Article 19 For a labor contract with a term of three months or more but less than one year, the probation period shall not exceed one month. For a labor contract with a term of one year or more but less than three years, the probation period shall not exceed two months. For a fixed-term labor contract with a term of three years or more or an open-ended labor contract, the probation period shall not exceed six months.

The same employing unit and the same worker may agree on a probation period only once.

No probation period may be stipulated for a labor contract that expires upon the completion of a specific task or for a labor contract with a term of less than three months.

The probation period shall be included in the term of the labor contract. If the labor contract only stipulates a probation period, the probation period is invalid, and that period shall be deemed the term of the labor contract.

Article 20 The wages of a worker during the probation period shall not be less than the lowest wage for the same position in the employing unit or 80% of the wage agreed upon in the labor contract, and shall not be less than the minimum wage standard in the location of the employing unit.

Article 21 During the probation period, the employing unit shall not rescind the labor contract unless the worker falls under the circumstances specified in Article 39 or Items (1) and (2) of Article 40 of this Law. If the employing unit rescinds the labor contract during the probation period, it shall explain the reasons to the worker.

Article 22 If the employing unit provides special training expenses for a worker and provides the worker with professional technical training, it may enter into an agreement with the worker stipulating a service period.

If the worker violates the service period agreement, the worker shall pay liquidated damages to the employing unit as agreed. The amount of liquidated damages shall not exceed the training expenses provided by the employing unit. The liquidated damages demanded by the employing unit from the worker shall not exceed the portion of the training expenses attributable to the unfulfilled part of the service period.

If the employing unit and the worker agree on a service period, it shall not affect the increase of the worker's labor remuneration during the service period in accordance with the normal wage adjustment mechanism.

Article 23 The employing unit and the worker may stipulate in the labor contract matters concerning the confidentiality of the employing unit's trade secrets and intellectual property-related confidential information.

For a worker who has a confidentiality obligation, the employing unit may stipulate non-competition clauses in the labor contract or a confidentiality agreement, and agree to provide the worker with monthly economic compensation during the non-competition period after the rescission or termination of the labor contract. If the worker violates the non-competition agreement, the worker shall pay liquidated damages to the employing unit as agreed.

Article 24 The persons subject to non-competition restrictions are limited to senior management personnel, senior technical personnel, and other personnel with confidentiality obligations of the employing unit. The scope, geographical area, and duration of non-competition restrictions shall be agreed upon by the employing unit and the worker, and such agreements shall not violate laws and regulations.

After the rescission or termination of the labor contract, the non-competition period for the persons specified in the preceding paragraph, who work for other employing units that produce or operate the same type of products or engage in the same type of business as the original employing unit and are in competition with it, or who start their own business to produce or operate the same type of products or engage in the same type of business, shall not exceed two years.

Article 25 Except for the circumstances specified in Articles 22 and 23 of this Law, the employing unit shall not agree with the worker that the worker shall bear liquidated damages.

Article 26 The following labor contracts are invalid or partially invalid:

(1) A labor contract concluded or modified by means of fraud, coercion, or taking advantage of the other party's difficulties, causing the other party to act against its true intentions;
(2) An employing unit exempts itself from legal responsibilities or excludes the rights of the worker;
(3) Violations of mandatory provisions of laws or administrative regulations.

Disputes over the invalidity or partial invalidity of a labor contract shall be confirmed by a labor dispute arbitration institution or a people's court.

Article 27 If part of a labor contract is invalid, and the validity of the remaining parts is not affected, the remaining parts shall remain valid.

Article 28 If a labor contract is confirmed as invalid, and the worker has already performed labor, the employing unit shall pay labor remuneration to the worker. The amount of labor remuneration shall be determined by reference to the labor remuneration of workers in the same or similar positions in the employing unit.

## Chapter III Performance and Modification of Labor Contracts

Article 29 The employing unit and the worker shall fully perform their respective obligations in accordance with the provisions of the labor contract.

Article 30 The employing unit shall pay labor remuneration to the worker in full and on time in accordance with the provisions of the labor contract and state regulations.

If the employing unit defaults on or fails to pay the full amount of labor remuneration, the worker may apply to the local people's court for a payment order in accordance with the law, and the people's court shall issue a payment order in accordance with the law.

Article 31 The employing unit shall strictly implement labor quota standards and shall not force or covertly force workers to work overtime. If the employing unit arranges overtime work, it shall pay overtime pay to the worker in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 32 If a worker refuses to follow the illegal directions or orders for risky operations given by the management personnel of the employing unit, it shall not be deemed a violation of the labor contract.

The worker has the right to criticize, report, and accuse the employing unit regarding working conditions that endanger life safety and physical health.

Article 33 Changes in matters such as the name, legal representative, principal responsible person, or investors of the employing unit shall not affect the performance of the labor contract.

Article 34 In the event of a merger or division of the employing unit, the original labor contract remains valid, and the employing unit that succeeds to its rights and obligations shall continue to perform the labor contract.

Article 35 The employing unit and the worker may modify the contents of the labor contract through mutual agreement. The modification of a labor contract shall be in writing.

The employing unit and the worker shall each hold one copy of the modified labor contract text.

## Chapter IV Rescission and Termination of Labor Contracts

Article 36 The employing unit and the worker may rescind the labor contract through mutual agreement.

Article 37 The worker may rescind the labor contract by giving written notice to the employing unit thirty days in advance. During the probation period, the worker may rescind the labor contract by giving notice to the employing unit three days in advance.

Article 38 The worker may rescind the labor contract under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The employing unit fails to provide labor protection or working conditions as stipulated in the labor contract;
(2) The employing unit fails to pay labor remuneration in full and on time;
(3) The employing unit fails to pay social insurance premiums for the worker in accordance with the law;
(4) The rules and regulations of the employing unit violate laws or regulations, harming the rights and interests of the worker;
(5) The labor contract is invalid due to the circumstances specified in Item (1) of Article 26 of this Law;
(6) Other circumstances under which laws or administrative regulations allow the worker to rescind the labor contract.

If the employing unit forces the worker to work by means of violence, threats, or illegal restriction of personal freedom, or if the employing unit gives illegal directions or orders for risky operations that endanger the personal safety of the worker, the worker may immediately rescind the labor contract without giving prior notice to the employing unit.

Article 39 The employing unit may rescind the labor contract under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The worker is proven during the probation period not to meet the recruitment conditions;
(2) The worker seriously violates the rules and regulations of the employing unit;
(3) The worker commits serious dereliction of duty or engages in graft, causing significant damage to the employing unit;
(4) The worker simultaneously establishes labor relations with another employing unit, which seriously affects the completion of the work tasks of the employing unit, or the worker refuses to correct the situation after the employing unit raises the issue;
(5) The labor contract is invalid due to the circumstances specified in Item (1) of Article 26, Paragraph 1 of this Law;
(6) The worker is subject to criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 40 Under any of the following circumstances, the employing unit may rescind the labor contract by giving the worker written notice thirty days in advance or by paying the worker an additional month's wages:

(1) The worker is ill or injured for non-work-related reasons, and after the prescribed medical treatment period expires, is unable to engage in the original work or any other work arranged by the employing unit;
(2) The worker is incompetent and remains incompetent after training or adjustment of the work position;
(3) A major change in the objective circumstances relied upon at the time of concluding the labor contract renders the labor contract unperformable, and the employing unit and the worker fail to reach an agreement on modifying the contents of the labor contract after negotiation.

Article 41 Under any of the following circumstances, if the employing unit needs to reduce its workforce by twenty persons or more, or by less than twenty persons but accounting for 10% or more of the total number of enterprise workers, the employing unit may reduce the workforce after explaining the situation to the trade union or all workers thirty days in advance, listening to the opinions of the trade union or the workers, and reporting the workforce reduction plan to the labor administrative department:

(1) Restructuring in accordance with the Enterprise Bankruptcy Law;
(2) Serious difficulties in production and operation;
(3) The enterprise changes its production, undergoes major technological innovation, or adjusts its business model, and still needs to reduce its workforce after modifying the labor contracts;
(4) Other major changes in the objective economic circumstances relied upon at the time of concluding the labor contract render the labor contract unperformable.

When reducing the workforce, priority shall be given to retaining the following personnel:

(1) Those who have concluded fixed-term labor contracts of longer duration with the employing unit;
(2) Those who have concluded open-ended labor contracts with the employing unit;
(3) Those who are the only employed persons in their families and have elderly persons or minors to support.

If the employing unit reduces its workforce in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 1 of this Article and rehires personnel within six months, it shall notify the laid-off personnel and give them priority in hiring under equal conditions.

Article 42 The employing unit shall not rescind the labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Articles 40 and 41 of this Law under any of the following circumstances:

(1) Workers engaged in operations exposed to occupational hazards have not undergone pre-departure occupational health checks, or suspected occupational disease patients are during the diagnosis or medical observation period;
(2) Workers who have contracted an occupational disease or suffered a work-related injury at the employing unit and have been confirmed to have lost or partially lost their ability to work;
(3) Workers who are ill or injured for non-work-related reasons and are within the prescribed medical treatment period;
(4) Female workers during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding;
(5) Workers who have worked continuously for the employing unit for fifteen years or more and are less than five years away from the statutory retirement age;
(6) Other circumstances specified by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 43 When the employing unit unilaterally rescinds a labor contract, it shall notify the trade union of the reasons in advance. If the employing unit violates laws, administrative regulations, or the provisions of the labor contract, the trade union has the right to demand that the employing unit correct the situation. The employing unit shall study the opinions of the trade union and notify the trade union in writing of the outcome.

Article 44 A labor contract is terminated under any of the following circumstances:

(1) The term of the labor contract expires;
(2) The worker begins to receive basic old-age insurance benefits in accordance with the law;
(3) The worker dies, or is declared dead or missing by a people's court;
(4) The employing unit is declared bankrupt in accordance with the law;
(5) The employing unit's business license is revoked, it is ordered to close down or is dissolved, or the employing unit decides to dissolve early;
(6) Other circumstances specified by laws or administrative regulations.

Article 45 If a labor contract expires and any of the circumstances specified in Article 42 of this Law exists, the term of the labor contract shall be extended until the corresponding circumstance disappears. However, the termination of the labor contract of a worker who has lost or partially lost their ability to work as specified in Item (2) of Article 42 of this Law shall be handled in accordance with the relevant state regulations on work-related injury insurance.

Article 46 Under any of the following circumstances, the employing unit shall pay economic compensation to the worker:

(1) The worker rescinds the labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Article 38 of this Law;
(2) The employing unit proposes to rescind the labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Article 36 of this Law and rescinds the labor contract through mutual agreement with the worker;
(3) The employing unit rescinds the labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Article 40 of this Law;
(4) The employing unit rescinds the labor contract in accordance with the provisions of Article 41, Paragraph 1 of this Law;(5) Except where the worker does not agree to renew the labor contract under conditions equal to or better than those stipulated in the original contract, termination of a fixed-term labor contract in accordance with Item (1) of Article 44 of this Law;

(6) Termination of a labor contract in accordance with Items (4) and (5) of Article 44 of this Law;

(7) Other circumstances as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 47 Economic compensation shall be paid to the worker based on the number of years he has worked for the employer, at the rate of one month's wages for each full year of service. For a period of six months or more but less than one year, it shall be counted as one year; for a period of less than six months, the worker shall be paid half a month's wages as economic compensation.

If the worker's monthly wage is higher than three times the average monthly wage of employees in the previous year in the region, as published by the municipal people's government of the directly-administered municipality or the city divided into districts where the employer is located, the standard for paying economic compensation shall be three times the average monthly wage of employees, and the number of years for which economic compensation is paid shall not exceed twelve years.

The term "monthly wage" as used in this Article refers to the worker's average wage for the twelve months preceding the termination or dissolution of the labor contract.

Article 48 If the employer dissolves or terminates a labor contract in violation of the provisions of this Law, and the worker demands continued performance of the labor contract, the employer shall continue to perform it; if the worker does not demand continued performance of the labor contract or the labor contract can no longer be performed, the employer shall pay compensation in accordance with Article 87 of this Law.

Article 49 The State shall take measures to establish and improve a system for the transfer and continuation of social insurance relations for workers across regions.

Article 50 The employer shall, at the time of dissolving or terminating the labor contract, issue a certificate of dissolution or termination of the labor contract, and shall, within fifteen days, handle the procedures for the transfer of the worker's personnel file and social insurance relations.

The worker shall, in accordance with the agreement between the parties, handle the handover of work. If the employer is required to pay economic compensation to the worker in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law, it shall pay it upon the completion of the work handover.

The employer shall retain the text of the dissolved or terminated labor contract for at least two years for reference.

## Chapter V Special Provisions

### Section 1 Collective Contracts

Article 51 The employees of an enterprise, through equal negotiation with the employer, may conclude a collective contract on matters such as labor remuneration, working hours, rest and vacation, occupational safety and health, insurance and welfare. The draft collective contract shall be submitted to the employees' congress or all employees for discussion and approval.

The collective contract shall be concluded between the trade union, on behalf of the employees, and the employer; in an employer that has not yet established a trade union, it shall be concluded between the representatives elected by the workers under the guidance of the higher-level trade union and the employer.

Article 52 The employees of an enterprise and the employer may conclude special-purpose collective contracts on matters such as occupational safety and health, protection of female employees' rights and interests, and wage adjustment mechanisms.

Article 53 Within areas below the county level, industries such as construction, mining, catering and service industries may have trade unions and representatives of the enterprises conclude industry-wide collective contracts or regional collective contracts.

Article 54 After a collective contract is concluded, it shall be submitted to the labor administrative department; if the labor administrative department raises no objection within fifteen days from the date of receiving the text of the collective contract, the collective contract shall take effect.

A collective contract concluded in accordance with the law is binding on the employer and the workers. Industry-wide and regional collective contracts are binding on the employers and workers in the local industry or region.

Article 55 The standards for labor remuneration and working conditions in a collective contract shall not be lower than the minimum standards prescribed by the local people's government; the standards for labor remuneration and working conditions in a labor contract concluded between the employer and a worker shall not be lower than the standards stipulated in the collective contract.

Article 56 If an employer violates a collective contract and infringes upon the labor rights and interests of the workers, the trade union may, in accordance with the law, demand that the employer assume liability; if a dispute arises over the performance of a collective contract and cannot be resolved through consultation, the trade union may apply for arbitration or initiate litigation in accordance with the law.

### Section 2 Labor Dispatch

Article 57 To engage in the business of labor dispatch, the following conditions shall be met:

(1) The registered capital shall not be less than RMB two million yuan;

(2) There shall be fixed business premises and facilities suitable for carrying out the business;

(3) There shall be a labor dispatch management system that complies with laws and administrative regulations;

(4) Other conditions as provided by laws and administrative regulations.

To engage in the business of labor dispatch, an administrative license shall be applied for from the labor administrative department in accordance with the law; upon obtaining the license, the corresponding company registration shall be handled in accordance with the law. Without a license, no unit or individual may engage in the business of labor dispatch.

Article 58 A labor dispatch unit is the employer as referred to in this Law and shall fulfill the obligations of an employer towards the workers. The labor contract concluded between the labor dispatch unit and the dispatched worker shall, in addition to the matters specified in Article 17 of this Law, also specify the user unit of the dispatched worker, the dispatch period, and the job position.

The labor dispatch unit shall conclude with the dispatched worker a fixed-term labor contract for a period of not less than two years and pay labor remuneration on a monthly basis; during periods when the dispatched worker has no work, the labor dispatch unit shall pay remuneration on a monthly basis in accordance with the minimum wage standard prescribed by the local people's government.

Article 59 When dispatching workers, the labor dispatch unit shall conclude a labor dispatch agreement with the unit that accepts the workers in the form of labor dispatch (hereinafter referred to as the user unit). The labor dispatch agreement shall stipulate the dispatch positions and the number of personnel, the dispatch period, the amount and method of payment of labor remuneration and social insurance premiums, and the liability for breach of the agreement.

The user unit shall determine the dispatch period with the labor dispatch unit based on the actual needs of the job positions and shall not split a continuous employment period into several short-term labor dispatch agreements.

Article 60 The labor dispatch unit shall inform the dispatched worker of the contents of the labor dispatch agreement.

The labor dispatch unit shall not deduct from the labor remuneration paid by the user unit to the dispatched worker in accordance with the labor dispatch agreement.

Neither the labor dispatch unit nor the user unit shall charge any fees to the dispatched worker.

Article 61 If a labor dispatch unit dispatches workers across regions, the labor remuneration and working conditions enjoyed by the dispatched workers shall be implemented in accordance with the standards of the location of the user unit.

Article 62 The user unit shall fulfill the following obligations:

(1) Implement national labor standards and provide corresponding working conditions and labor protection;

(2) Inform the dispatched worker of the job requirements and labor remuneration;

(3) Pay overtime pay and performance bonuses, and provide welfare benefits related to the job position;

(4) Provide necessary training for the dispatched worker on the job position;

(5) In the case of continuous employment, implement a normal wage adjustment mechanism.

The user unit shall not re-dispatch the dispatched worker to another employer.

Article 63 Dispatched workers have the right to equal pay for equal work with the workers of the user unit. The user unit shall, in accordance with the principle of equal pay for equal work, apply the same labor remuneration distribution method to dispatched workers and its own workers in similar positions. If the user unit has no workers in similar positions, the labor remuneration shall be determined by reference to the labor remuneration of workers in the same or similar positions in the location of the user unit.

The labor contract concluded between the labor dispatch unit and the dispatched worker and the labor dispatch agreement concluded with the user unit shall specify or stipulate the labor remuneration to be paid to the dispatched worker in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

Article 64 Dispatched workers have the right to join or organize trade unions in the labor dispatch unit or the user unit in accordance with the law to protect their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 65 A dispatched worker may dissolve the labor contract with the labor dispatch unit in accordance with the provisions of Articles 36 and 38 of this Law.

If a dispatched worker falls under the circumstances specified in Article 39 and Items (1) and (2) of Article 40 of this Law, the user unit may return the worker to the labor dispatch unit, and the labor dispatch unit may dissolve the labor contract with the worker in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law.

Article 66 Labor contract employment is the basic form of employment for enterprises in China. Labor dispatch employment is a supplementary form and may only be implemented in temporary, auxiliary, or substitute job positions.

The temporary job positions referred to in the preceding paragraph refer to positions with a duration of not more than six months; auxiliary job positions refer to non-core business positions that provide services to core business positions; substitute job positions refer to positions where, during a certain period when a worker of the user unit is unable to work due to off-the-job study, vacation, or other reasons, another worker may be substituted to perform the work.

The user unit shall strictly control the number of dispatched workers, which shall not exceed a certain proportion of its total workforce; the specific proportion shall be stipulated by the labor administrative department of the State Council.

Article 67 An employer shall not establish a labor dispatch unit to dispatch workers to itself or its subordinate units.

### Section 3 Part-time Employment

Article 68 Part-time employment refers to a form of employment where remuneration is mainly calculated on an hourly basis, and the worker generally works an average of no more than four hours per day for the same employer, with cumulative weekly working hours not exceeding twenty-four hours.

Article 69 The parties to part-time employment may conclude an oral agreement.

A worker engaged in part-time employment may conclude labor contracts with one or more employers; however, a subsequently concluded labor contract shall not affect the performance of a previously concluded labor contract.

Article 70 The parties to part-time employment shall not agree on a probation period.

Article 71 Either party to part-time employment may terminate the employment at any time by notifying the other party. Upon termination of the employment, the employer shall not pay economic compensation to the worker.

Article 72 The hourly remuneration standard for part-time employment shall not be lower than the minimum hourly wage standard prescribed by the local people's government where the employer is located.

The settlement and payment cycle for labor remuneration in part-time employment shall not exceed fifteen days at most.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Inspection

Article 73 The labor administrative department of the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision and management of the implementation of the labor contract system nationwide.

The labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the supervision and management of the implementation of the labor contract system within their respective administrative regions.

In the supervision and management of the implementation of the labor contract system, the labor administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall listen to the opinions of trade unions, representatives of enterprises, and relevant industry authorities.

Article 74 The labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with the law, supervise and inspect the following situations regarding the implementation of the labor contract system:

(1) The formulation and implementation of internal rules and regulations by the employer that directly involve the vital interests of the workers;

(2) The conclusion and dissolution of labor contracts between the employer and the workers;

(3) The compliance of labor dispatch units and user units with the relevant provisions on labor dispatch;

(4) The compliance of the employer with the state regulations on workers' working hours and rest and vacation;

(5) The payment of labor remuneration as stipulated in the labor contract and the implementation of the minimum wage standard by the employer;

(6) The participation of the employer in various social insurances and the payment of social insurance premiums;

(7) Other labor inspection matters as provided by laws and regulations.

Article 75 When conducting supervision and inspection, the labor administrative departments of local people's governments at or above the county level have the right to consult materials related to labor contracts and collective contracts, and have the right to conduct on-site inspections of workplaces; both the employer and the workers shall truthfully provide relevant information and materials.

Staff members of the labor administrative department conducting supervision and inspection shall present their credentials, exercise their powers in accordance with the law, and enforce the law in a civilized manner.

Article 76 The relevant competent departments of people's governments at or above the county level, such as construction, health, and work safety supervision and administration, shall, within their respective responsibilities, supervise and manage the implementation of the labor contract system by employers.

Article 77 If a worker's legitimate rights and interests are infringed upon, he has the right to request the relevant department to handle the matter in accordance with the law, or to apply for arbitration or initiate litigation in accordance with the law.

Article 78 Trade unions shall, in accordance with the law, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of workers and supervise the performance of labor contracts and collective contracts by employers. If an employer violates labor laws, regulations, labor contracts, or collective contracts, the trade union has the right to raise opinions or demand correction; if a worker applies for arbitration or initiates litigation, the trade union shall provide support and assistance in accordance with the law.

Article 79 Any organization or individual has the right to report violations of this Law. The labor administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly verify and handle the reports, and shall reward those who have rendered meritorious service in reporting.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

Article 80 If the internal rules and regulations of an employer that directly involve the vital interests of the workers violate the provisions of laws or regulations, the labor administrative department shall order correction and give a warning; if damage is caused to the workers, the employer shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 81 If the text of a labor contract provided by an employer does not contain the mandatory clauses required by this Law, or if the employer fails to deliver the text of the labor contract to the worker, the labor administrative department shall order correction; if damage is caused to the worker, the employer shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 82 If an employer fails to conclude a written labor contract with a worker for a period of more than one month but less than one year from the date of employment, it shall pay the worker twice the monthly wage each month.

If an employer, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to conclude an open-ended labor contract with a worker, it shall pay the worker twice the monthly wage each month from the date on which the open-ended labor contract should have been concluded.

Article 83 If an employer agrees on a probation period with a worker in violation of the provisions of this Law, the labor administrative department shall order correction; if the illegally agreed probation period has already been performed, the employer shall pay compensation to the worker based on the worker's monthly wage after the probation period, for the period exceeding the statutory probation period.

Article 84 If an employer, in violation of the provisions of this Law, detains a worker's resident identity card or other certificates, the labor administrative department shall order the return of the certificates to the worker within a specified period, and impose a penalty in accordance with the relevant legal provisions.

If an employer, in violation of the provisions of this Law, collects property from a worker in the name of a guarantee or other means, the labor administrative department shall order the return of the property to the worker within a specified period, and impose a fine of not less than 500 yuan but not more than 2,000 yuan per person; if damage is caused to the worker, the employer shall bear liability for compensation.

If a worker dissolves or terminates a labor contract in accordance with the law, and the employer detains the worker's personnel file or other items, the penalty shall be imposed in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

Article 85 If an employer falls under any of the following circumstances, the labor administrative department shall order it to pay labor remuneration, overtime pay, or economic compensation within a specified period; if the labor remuneration is lower than the local minimum wage standard, the difference shall be paid; if payment is not made within the specified period, the employer shall be ordered to pay additional compensation to the worker at a rate of not less than 50% but not more than 100% of the amount payable:

(1) Failing to pay labor remuneration to the worker in full and on time in accordance with the labor contract or state regulations;

(2) Paying wages to the worker below the local minimum wage standard;

(3) Arranging overtime work without paying overtime pay;

(4) Dissolving or terminating a labor contract without paying economic compensation to the worker in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 86 If a labor contract is confirmed invalid in accordance with Article 26 of this Law, and damage is caused to the other party, the party at fault shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 87 If an employer dissolves or terminates a labor contract in violation of the provisions of this Law, it shall pay compensation to the worker at twice the standard for economic compensation specified in Article 47 of this Law.

Article 88 If an employer falls under any of the following circumstances, it shall be given administrative penalties in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if damage is caused to the worker, the employer shall bear liability for compensation:

(1) Forcing labor by means of violence, threats, or illegal restriction of personal freedom;

(2) Giving illegal commands or forcing risky operations that endanger the personal safety of the worker;

(3) Insulting, physically punishing, beating, illegally searching, or detaining the worker;

(4) Providing poor working conditions or causing serious environmental pollution, resulting in serious damage to the physical and mental health of the worker.

Article 89 If an employer, in violation of the provisions of this Law, fails to issue a written certificate of dissolution or termination of the labor contract to the worker, the labor administrative department shall order correction; if damage is caused to the worker, the employer shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 90 If a worker dissolves a labor contract in violation of the provisions of this Law, or violates the confidentiality obligations or non-competition restrictions agreed upon in the labor contract, causing losses to the employer, the worker shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 91 If an employer recruits a worker who has not yet dissolved or terminated a labor contract with another employer, causing losses to the other employer, the employer shall bear joint and several liability for compensation.

Article 92 If, in violation of the provisions of this Law, a person engages in the business of labor dispatch without a license, the labor administrative department shall order the cessation of the illegal act, confiscate the illegal gains, and impose a fine of not less than one time but not more than five times the illegal gains; if there are no illegal gains, a fine of not more than 50,000 yuan may be imposed.

If a labor dispatch unit or user unit violates the provisions of this Law on labor dispatch, the labor administrative department shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the specified period, a fine of not less than 5,000 yuan but not more than 10,000 yuan per person shall be imposed, and for the labor dispatch unit, its labor dispatch business operation permit shall be revoked. If the user unit causes damage to the dispatched worker, the labor dispatch unit and the user unit shall bear joint and several liability for compensation.

Article 93 For the illegal or criminal acts of an employer that does not have legitimate business qualifications, legal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law; if the worker has already performed labor, the unit or its investors shall pay the worker labor remuneration, economic compensation, and compensation in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Law; if damage is caused to the worker, the employer shall bear liability for compensation.

Article 94 If an individual contractor employs workers in violation of the provisions of this Law, causing damage to the workers, the contracting organization and the individual contractor shall bear joint and several liability for compensation.

Article 95 If the labor administrative department and other relevant competent authorities and their staff members neglect their duties, fail to perform their statutory duties, or illegally exercise their powers, causing damage to workers or employers, they shall bear liability for compensation; the directly responsible person in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given administrative sanctions in accordance with the law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be investigated in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 96 For the conclusion, performance, modification, dissolution, or termination of labor contracts between public institutions and their staff members under the employment system, if there are separate provisions in laws, administrative regulations, or by the State Council, those provisions shall apply; if there are no such provisions, the relevant provisions of this Law shall apply.

Article 97 Labor contracts that were legally concluded before the implementation of this Law and continue to exist on the date of its implementation shall continue to be performed; the number of consecutive fixed-term labor contracts concluded as specified in Item (3) of Paragraph 2 of Article 14 of this Law shall be calculated from the date of the renewal of the fixed-term labor contract after the implementation of this Law.For an employment relationship established before this Law takes effect, where a written labor contract has not yet been concluded, the contract shall be concluded within one month from the date this Law takes effect.

For a labor contract that is in existence on the date this Law takes effect and is dissolved or terminated after this Law takes effect, if economic compensation is payable in accordance with Article 46 of this Law, the period for calculating economic compensation shall commence from the date this Law takes effect. If, prior to this Law taking effect, the employer was required to pay economic compensation to the worker in accordance with the relevant provisions in effect at that time, such compensation shall be paid in accordance with those provisions.

Article 98 This Law shall take effect as of January 1, 2008.
