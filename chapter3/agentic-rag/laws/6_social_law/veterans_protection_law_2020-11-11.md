# Law of the People's Republic of China on the Protection of Veterans

Adopted at the 23rd Meeting of the Standing Committee of the 13th National People's Congress on November 11, 2020

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted in accordance with the Constitution to strengthen the protection of veterans, safeguard their lawful rights and interests, and make military service a profession respected by the whole society.

Article 2 For the purposes of this Law, "veterans" refer to officers, non-commissioned officers, and conscripts who have been lawfully discharged from active service in the People's Liberation Army.

Article 3 Veterans have made significant contributions to national defense and military construction and are an important force in socialist modernization.

Respecting and caring for veterans is the common responsibility of the whole society. The State cares for and gives preferential treatment to veterans, strengthens the construction of the veteran protection system, and ensures that veterans enjoy corresponding rights and interests in accordance with the law.

Article 4 The protection of veterans shall adhere to the leadership of the Communist Party of China, follow the policy of serving economic and social development as well as national defense and military construction, and abide by the principles of people-orientation, classified protection, service priority, and law-based management.

Article 5 The protection of veterans shall be coordinated with economic development and compatible with social progress.

The placement of veterans shall be open, fair, and just.

The political and living treatment of veterans shall be linked to their contributions during active service.

The State establishes a special preferential treatment mechanism for combat veterans.

Article 6 Veterans shall continue to carry forward the fine traditions of the People's Army, faithfully abide by the Constitution and laws and regulations, protect military secrets, practice the core socialist values, and actively participate in socialist modernization.

Article 7 The competent department for veteran affairs under the State Council shall be responsible for the protection of veterans nationwide. The competent departments for veteran affairs of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the protection of veterans within their respective administrative regions.

Relevant central and state organs, relevant departments of the Central Military Commission, and relevant local organs at all levels shall perform their duties well in the protection of veterans within their respective responsibilities.

Departments of the armed forces responsible for veteran-related work and the competent departments for veteran affairs of people's governments at or above the county level shall closely cooperate to ensure the protection of veterans.

Article 8 The State strengthens the informatization of veteran protection, establishes files for veterans, facilitates information sharing among relevant departments, and provides support for improving the capacity to protect veterans.

The competent department for veteran affairs under the State Council shall closely cooperate with relevant central and state organs and relevant departments of the Central Military Commission to coordinate the construction, maintenance, application, and information security management of information data systems.

Article 9 The funds required for the protection of veterans shall be jointly borne by the central and local finances. Funds for veteran placement, education and training, and pensions and preferential treatment shall be mainly borne by the central finance.

Article 10 The State encourages and guides enterprises, social organizations, individuals, and other social forces to provide support and assistance to veterans through donations, establishment of funds, volunteer services, and other means in accordance with the law.

Article 11 Units and individuals that have made outstanding contributions to the protection of veterans shall be commended and rewarded in accordance with relevant state regulations.

## Chapter II Transfer and Reception

Article 12 The competent department for veteran affairs under the State Council, the political work department of the Central Military Commission, and relevant central and state organs shall formulate annual plans for the transfer and reception of veterans nationwide.

Article 13 The original unit of a veteran shall transfer the veteran to the competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement, and the competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement shall be responsible for receiving the veteran.

The place of placement for a veteran shall be determined in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 14 A veteran shall, within the prescribed time limit, report to the competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement with the discharge certificate issued by the armed forces.

Article 15 When receiving a veteran, the competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement shall issue a veteran preferential treatment certificate to the veteran.

The veteran preferential treatment certificate shall be uniformly produced and numbered nationwide, and the measures for its management and use shall be formulated by the competent department for veteran affairs under the State Council in conjunction with relevant departments.

Article 16 When a service member is discharged, the unit to which the service member belongs shall promptly transfer the service member's personnel file to the competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement.

The competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations on personnel file management, receive, keep, and transfer the veteran's personnel file to relevant units.

Article 17 The public security organ of the local people's government at the place of placement shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, promptly register the household registration for the veteran, and the competent department for veteran affairs at the same level shall provide assistance.

Article 18 The original unit of a veteran shall, in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, promptly transfer the social insurance relationships and corresponding funds for old-age, medical, and other insurance of the veteran and the veteran's unemployed spouse who accompanied the service member to the social insurance agency at the place of placement.

The competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at the place of placement shall closely cooperate with the social insurance agency and relevant military departments to handle the transfer and continuation of relevant social insurance relationships and corresponding funds in accordance with the law.

Article 19 During the transfer and reception of a veteran, problems related to the veteran's active service shall be handled by the original unit; problems related to the veteran's placement shall be handled by the local people's government at the place of placement; and other problems related to transfer and reception shall be handled by the local people's government at the place of placement, with the cooperation of the original unit.

If the original unit of a veteran is dissolved, transferred, or merged, the superior unit of the original unit or the unit after transfer or merger shall handle the matter in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.

## Chapter III Veteran Placement

Article 20 Local people's governments at all levels shall, in accordance with the transfer and reception plans, ensure the placement of veterans and fulfill the tasks of veteran placement.

State organs, mass organizations, enterprises, public institutions, and social organizations shall receive and place veterans in accordance with the law, and veterans shall accept placement.

Article 21 For discharged officers, the State shall adopt methods such as retirement, transfer to civilian work, monthly pension, and demobilization for proper placement.

For those placed through retirement and transferred to the local people's government, the local people's government at the place of placement shall provide service management and ensure their treatment through a combination of state guarantees and socialized services.

For those placed through transfer to civilian work, the local people's government at the place of placement shall arrange work positions and determine corresponding positions and ranks based on their moral integrity and ability, as well as their positions, ranks, contributions, expertise, and work needs during active service.

For those who have served for the prescribed number of years and are placed through monthly pension, they shall receive a monthly pension in accordance with relevant state regulations.

For those placed through demobilization, they shall receive a demobilization fee in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 22 For discharged non-commissioned officers, the State shall adopt methods such as monthly pension, self-employment, job placement, retirement, and state support for proper placement.

For those who have served for the prescribed number of years and are placed through monthly pension, they shall receive a monthly pension in accordance with relevant state regulations.

For those who have served less than the prescribed number of years and are placed through self-employment, they shall receive a one-time severance payment.

For those placed through job placement, the local people's government at the place of placement shall arrange work positions based on their contributions and expertise during active service.

For those placed through retirement, the local people's government at the place of placement shall provide service management and ensure their treatment through a combination of state guarantees and socialized services.

For those placed through state support, they shall be supported by the State for life.

Article 23 For discharged conscripts, the State shall adopt methods such as self-employment, job placement, and state support for proper placement.

For those placed through self-employment, they shall receive a one-time severance payment.

For those placed through job placement, the local people's government at the place of placement shall arrange work positions based on their contributions and expertise during active service.

For those placed through state support, they shall be supported by the State for life.

Article 24 The applicable conditions for placement methods such as retirement, transfer to civilian work, monthly pension, demobilization, self-employment, job placement, and state support shall be implemented in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 25 Officers transferred to civilian work and non-commissioned officers and conscripts placed through job arrangement shall be received and placed by state organs, mass organizations, public institutions, and state-owned enterprises. Priority in placement shall be given to the following veterans:

(1) Combat veterans;

(2) Officers transferred to civilian work who served as principal leaders of divisions, brigades, regiments, or battalions of combat units;

(3) Veterans who are children of martyrs or model heroes;

(4) Veterans who have served for a long time in remote and difficult areas or in special positions.

Article 26 State organs, mass organizations, and public institutions that receive and place officers transferred to civilian work and non-commissioned officers and conscripts placed through job arrangement shall provide staffing guarantees in accordance with relevant state regulations.

State-owned enterprises that receive and place officers transferred to civilian work and non-commissioned officers and conscripts placed through job arrangement shall sign labor contracts with them and ensure corresponding treatment in accordance with state regulations.

When the employing units specified in the preceding two paragraphs lawfully reduce their workforce, they shall give priority to retaining the veterans received and placed through transfer to civilian work or job arrangement.

Article 27 For discharged officers and non-commissioned officers placed through monthly pension, if they are recruited as civil servants or employed as staff of public institutions, their pension shall be suspended from the month following their recruitment or employment, and their treatment shall be implemented in accordance with relevant laws and regulations on the management of civil servants and public institution staff.

Article 28 The State establishes a system for the mandatory transfer, placement, and medical treatment and recuperation of sick, wounded, or disabled veterans. Relevant military departments shall promptly transfer sick, wounded, or disabled veterans to the local people's government at the place of placement for placement. The local people's government at the place of placement shall properly address the housing, medical care, rehabilitation, nursing, and living difficulties of sick, wounded, or disabled veterans.

Article 29 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen the work of supporting the military and giving preferential treatment to their families, and help solve difficulties for service members and their families.

When eligible officers and non-commissioned officers are discharged from active service, their spouses and children may be transferred or relocated accordingly in accordance with relevant state regulations.

If the spouse to be transferred works in a state organ or public institution and meets the requirements of relevant laws and regulations, the local people's government at the place of placement shall arrange for the spouse to work in a corresponding unit; if the spouse to be transferred works in another unit or is unemployed, the local people's government at the place of placement shall provide employment guidance and assist in achieving employment.

If the children to be relocated need to transfer schools or enroll in school, the education administrative department of the local people's government at the place of placement shall handle the matter promptly. Priority shall be given to the relocated children of the following veterans:

(1) Combat veterans;

(2) Veterans who are children of martyrs or model heroes;

(3) Veterans who have served for a long time in remote and difficult areas or in special positions;

(4) Other eligible veterans.

Article 30 Specific measures for the placement of discharged service members shall be formulated by the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

## Chapter IV Education and Training

Article 31 Education and training for veterans shall be oriented towards improving employment quality, closely focus on social needs, and provide veterans with distinctive, refined, and targeted training services.

The State shall take measures to strengthen education and training for veterans, help them improve their knowledge structure, enhance their political and ideological level, vocational skills, and overall professional competence, and boost their employability and entrepreneurial ability.

Article 32 The State shall establish an education and training system for veterans that combines academic education and vocational skills training, establish a coordination mechanism for veteran education and training, and comprehensively plan veteran education and training work.

Article 33 Before a service member is discharged, the unit to which the service member belongs may, on the premise of ensuring the completion of military tasks, provide reserve vocational skills training based on the characteristics and conditions of the unit, organize participation in higher education self-study examinations and higher academic continuing education offered by various higher education institutions, as well as non-academic continuing education such as knowledge expansion and skills training.

The competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level where the unit is located shall provide support and assistance to the unit of the active service member in carrying out education and training.

Article 34 When receiving academic education, veterans shall enjoy state education assistance policies such as tuition and stipend subsidies in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Higher education institutions may, in accordance with the overall arrangements of the State, recruit veterans through separate plans, separate enrollment, and other methods.

Article 35 If an active service member was admitted to a regular higher education institution or was a student at a regular higher education institution before enlistment, the service member's admission qualification or student status shall be retained during active service, and the service member shall be allowed to enroll or resume studies within two years after discharge, and may transfer to another major of the same institution in accordance with relevant state regulations. If the service member meets the conditions for applying for graduate studies, the service member shall enjoy preferential policies in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 36 The State shall rely on and support regular higher education institutions, vocational colleges (including technical colleges), professional training institutions, and other educational resources to provide vocational skills training for veterans. If a veteran needs employment or entrepreneurship before reaching the statutory retirement age, the veteran may enjoy corresponding support policies such as vocational skills training subsidies.

After a service member is discharged from active service, the local people's government at the place of placement shall organize the service member to participate in free vocational education and skills training based on employment needs. Those who pass the examinations and assessments shall be issued corresponding academic certificates, vocational qualification certificates, or vocational skill level certificates and recommended for employment.

Article 37 The competent department for veteran affairs of the provincial people's government shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, strengthen dynamic management, regularly inspect and assess the training quality of regular higher education institutions, vocational colleges (including technical colleges), and professional training institutions that provide vocational skills training for veterans, so as to improve the quality and level of vocational skills training.

## Chapter V Employment and Entrepreneurship

Article 38 The State shall adopt a combination of government promotion, market guidance, and social support to encourage and support veteran employment and entrepreneurship.

Article 39 People's governments at all levels shall strengthen guidance and services for veteran employment and entrepreneurship.

The competent department for veteran affairs of the local people's government at or above the county level shall strengthen the publicity, organization, and coordination of veteran employment and entrepreneurship, and, in conjunction with relevant departments, adopt methods such as special job fairs for veterans to carry out job recommendations and career guidance to help veterans find employment.

Article 40 Disabled veterans who were assessed as having a disability level due to war, duty, or illness during active service, or who are assessed or reassessed after discharge, and who have the ability to work and the willingness to work, shall have priority in enjoying the preferential employment policies for persons with disabilities stipulated by the State.

Article 41 Public human resources service agencies shall provide free services such as job placement and entrepreneurship guidance to veterans.

The State encourages for-profit human resources service agencies and social organizations to provide free or discounted services for veteran employment and entrepreneurship.

If a veteran fails to find employment in a timely manner, the veteran may, after registering for job seeking with the human resources and social security department, enjoy unemployment insurance benefits in accordance with regulations.

Article 42 When state organs, mass organizations, public institutions, and state-owned enterprises recruit or hire personnel, they may appropriately relax the age and education requirements for veterans and give priority to recruiting and hiring veterans under equal conditions. The active service experience of discharged non-commissioned officers and conscripts shall be regarded as grassroots work experience.

If discharged non-commissioned officers and conscripts were personnel of state organs, mass organizations, public institutions, or state-owned enterprises before enlistment, they may choose to return to their original positions or resume work after discharge.

Article 43 Localities shall set a certain number of grassroots civil servant positions for the recruitment of college graduate veterans who have served for five years or more.

College graduate veterans who have served for five years or more may apply for positions specifically targeted at personnel serving grassroots projects and share the targeted civil servant recruitment plans with such personnel.

Localities shall pay attention to selecting outstanding veterans for appointment as full-time staff of Party grassroots organizations, communities, and villages.

Positions such as civilian personnel in the armed forces and positions in national defense education institutions shall give priority to employing eligible veterans.

The State encourages veterans to participate in border stability and consolidation and other border construction work.

Article 44 The number of years a veteran has served in active service shall be counted as length of service and shall be accumulated with the number of years worked at the employing unit after discharge.

Article 45 Entrepreneurship incubation bases and entrepreneurship parks invested in or jointly built with society by local people's governments at or above the county level shall give priority to providing services for veteran entrepreneurship. Where conditions permit, veteran entrepreneurship incubation bases and entrepreneurship parks may be established to provide veterans with preferential services such as business premises and investment and financing.

Article 46 If a veteran establishes a small or micro enterprise, the veteran may apply for entrepreneurship guarantee loans in accordance with relevant state regulations and enjoy preferential financing policies such as loan interest subsidies.

If a veteran engages in self-employment, the veteran shall enjoy tax preferential policies in accordance with the law.

Article 47 If an employing unit recruits veterans in accordance with state regulations, the employing unit shall enjoy tax preferential policies and other policies in accordance with the law.

## Chapter VI Pensions and Preferential Treatment

Article 48 People's governments at all levels shall adhere to the principle of combining universal benefits with preferential treatment, and on the basis of ensuring that veterans enjoy universal policies and public services, provide preferential treatment based on their contributions during active service and local actual conditions.

For combat veterans, the standard of preferential treatment shall be raised.

Article 49 The State shall gradually eliminate urban-rural differences in the pension and preferential treatment system for veterans, narrow regional differences, and establish a coordinated and balanced quantitative standard system for pensions and preferential treatment.

Article 50 Veterans shall participate in social insurance such as old-age, medical, work-related injury, unemployment, and maternity insurance in accordance with the law and enjoy corresponding benefits.

The number of years a veteran has served in active service shall be merged with the contribution years for basic old-age insurance for employees, basic medical insurance for employees, and unemployment insurance before enlistment and after discharge in accordance with the law.

Article 51 If a veteran meets the conditions for preferential treatment in housing placement, a combination of market purchase and centralized construction by the military and local authorities shall be adopted, and the local people's government at the place of placement shall make overall plans and implement them scientifically.

Article 52 Military medical institutions and public medical institutions shall provide preferential treatment services for veterans seeking medical treatment and give preferential treatment to combat veterans and disabled veterans.

Article 53 Veterans shall enjoy preferential treatment in public transportation, culture, and tourism by presenting valid documents such as the veteran preferential treatment certificate, and specific measures shall be formulated by the provincial people's government.

Article 54 People's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the construction of preferential treatment hospitals and glory homes, make full use of existing medical and elderly care service resources, and admit or centrally support elderly veterans who are alone or unable to care for themselves.

All types of social welfare institutions shall give priority to receiving elderly veterans and disabled veterans.

Article 55 The State establishes a mechanism for providing assistance and support to veterans, and shall provide assistance and support to veterans in financial difficulties in areas such as elderly care, medical care, and housing in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 56 Disabled veterans shall enjoy pensions in accordance with the law.Disabled veterans receive disability pensions according to their disability grade. The standards are determined by the veterans affairs department of the State Council, in conjunction with the finance department of the State Council, taking into account factors such as the national economic and social development level, consumer price levels, average wages of employees in urban units nationwide, and national financial capacity. Disability pensions are distributed by the veterans affairs department of the county-level people's government.

## Chapter 7: Commendation and Incentives

Article 57: The state establishes an honor and incentive mechanism for veterans, commending and rewarding those who have made outstanding contributions to socialist modernization. Veterans who received commendations or rewards during active service shall, after retirement, enjoy corresponding benefits in accordance with relevant state regulations.

Article 58: When receiving veterans, the people's government at the place of resettlement shall hold a welcoming ceremony. The welcoming ceremony shall be organized by the veterans affairs department of the people's government at the place of resettlement.

Article 59: Local people's governments shall display honorary plaques for the families of veterans and regularly conduct visits and condolence activities.
Article 60: When the state, local governments, or the military hold major celebrations, representatives of veterans shall be invited to participate.

Invited veterans may wear their standard uniforms from the time of their retirement when attending major celebrations, and may wear medals, decorations, and commemorative badges awarded during and after their active service.

Article 61: The state emphasizes the positive role of veterans in patriotic education and national defense education activities. Government agencies, mass organizations, enterprises, institutions, and social organizations may invite veterans to assist in conducting patriotic education and national defense education. Education administrative departments of people's governments at or above the county level may invite veterans to participate in school national defense education training, and schools may hire veterans to assist in student military training.

Article 62: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen the promotion of veterans' exemplary deeds, promoting patriotism, revolutionary heroism, and the dedication of veterans through means such as producing public service advertisements and creating thematic literary and artistic works.

Article 63: Local chronicles institutions of local people's governments at or above the county level shall compile and record the names and deeds of the following veterans within their administrative regions into local chronicles:

(1) Veterans who participated in combat;
(2) Veterans who have received second-class merit or higher awards;
(3) Veterans who have received commendations at the provincial/ministerial level or theater command level or above;
(4) Other eligible veterans.

Article 64: The state coordinates the planning of martyrs' memorial facilities, promoting the spirit of heroes and martyrs through various forms such as organizing commemorative activities for heroes and martyrs. The veterans affairs department is responsible for the repair, protection, and management of martyrs' memorial facilities.

The state promotes the construction of military cemeteries. Eligible veterans may be buried in military cemeteries after death.

## Chapter 8: Service and Management

Article 65: The state strengthens the construction of veteran service institutions and establishes a sound veteran service system. People's governments at or above the county level shall establish veteran service centers, and townships, sub-districts, rural, and urban communities shall establish veteran service stations to enhance the capacity for veteran service and support.

Article 66: Veteran service institutions such as veteran service centers and stations shall strengthen communication with veterans, providing services and support in areas such as employment and entrepreneurship assistance, preferential treatment and support, visits and condolences, and rights protection.
Article 67: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall strengthen ideological and political education for veterans, promptly understand their ideological status and work/living conditions, and guide receiving and resettlement units and other organizations in conducting ideological and political work and related support for veterans.

Receiving and resettlement units and other organizations shall, in conjunction with veterans' work and living conditions, conduct ideological and political work and related support for veterans.

Article 68: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level, receiving and resettlement units, and other organizations shall strengthen confidentiality education and management for veterans.

Article 69: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall publicize laws, regulations, and policies related to veterans through various channels such as radio, television, newspapers, and the internet.

Article 70: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a sound mechanism for protecting veterans' rights and interests, unblock channels for expressing demands, and provide support and assistance for veterans to safeguard their legitimate rights and interests. If veterans' legitimate rights and interests are infringed upon, the matter shall be resolved according to law. Relevant public legal service institutions shall provide necessary assistance such as legal aid to veterans according to law.

Article 71: Veterans affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, according to law, guide and supervise relevant departments and units in carrying out work such as resettlement, education and training, employment and entrepreneurship, pensions and preferential treatment, commendation and incentives, and supporting the military and their families. They shall supervise and inspect the implementation of laws, regulations, and policy measures related to veteran support, and promote the resolution of problems in veteran support work.

Article 72: The state implements a responsibility system and assessment and evaluation system for veteran support work. People's governments at or above the county level shall include the completion of veteran support work in the assessment and evaluation content for departments responsible for veteran work under the same level of government and their leaders, as well as lower-level people's governments and their leaders.

For regions and units where veteran support policies are not implemented properly or work is not progressing effectively, the veterans affairs department of the provincial-level people's government or above shall, in conjunction with relevant departments, interview the main leader of the people's government of that region or the main leader of that unit.

Article 73: Veterans affairs departments and their staff shall consciously accept social supervision when performing their duties.

Article 74: Reports and accusations of violations of this Law in veteran support work shall be promptly handled by relevant authorities and departments according to law, and the handling results shall be informed to the whistleblower or accuser.

## Chapter 9: Legal Liability

Article 75: If a veterans affairs department or its staff commits any of the following acts, the superior competent department shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law:

(1) Failing to determine veteran resettlement benefits as stipulated;
(2) Issuing false documents in veteran resettlement work;
(3) Issuing veteran preferential treatment certificates to ineligible persons;
(4) Misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling funds for veteran support work;
(5) Violating regulations in determining the recipients, standards, or amounts of pensions and preferential treatment, or granting related benefits to veterans;
(6) Using their position for personal gain or for the benefit of others in veteran support work;
(7) Dereliction of duty in veteran support work;
(8) Other violations of laws and regulations.

Article 76: If other departments responsible for veteran-related work or their staff violate the relevant provisions of this Law, the superior competent department shall order correction, and the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be given sanctions according to law.

Article 77: If a unit violates the provisions of this Law by refusing or unreasonably delaying the execution of veteran resettlement tasks, the veterans affairs department of the people's government at the place of resettlement shall order correction within a specified period; if correction is not made within the period, a notice of criticism shall be issued. The main leader and directly responsible personnel of the unit shall be given sanctions by relevant departments according to law.

Article 78: If a veteran fraudulently obtains retirement-related benefits through deception, the veterans affairs department of the local people's government at or above the county level shall cancel the relevant benefits, recover the illegal gains, and the unit to which the veteran belongs or relevant departments shall give sanctions according to law.

Article 79: If a veteran commits a crime, the veterans affairs department of the provincial-level people's government shall, in accordance with relevant state regulations, suspend, reduce, or cancel their retirement-related benefits, and report to the veterans affairs department of the State Council for record.

If a veteran disagrees with the decision of the provincial-level people's government's veterans affairs department to suspend, reduce, or cancel their retirement-related benefits, they may apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit according to law.

Article 80: Violations of the provisions of this Law that constitute a violation of public security administration shall be given public security administration penalties according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter 10: Supplementary Provisions

Article 81: This Law applies to officers, sergeants, and conscripts of the Chinese People's Armed Police Force who are legally retired from active service.

Article 82: The provisions of this Law concerning officers apply to civilian cadres.

Cadets of military academies who legally retire from active service shall refer to the relevant provisions of this Law for implementation.

Article 83: Veterans who participated in nuclear tests shall refer to the provisions of this Law concerning veterans who participated in combat for implementation.

The scope, identification standards, and identification procedures for veterans who participated in combat and veterans who participated in nuclear tests shall be stipulated by relevant departments of the Central Military Commission in conjunction with the veterans affairs department of the State Council and other departments.

Article 84: Officers who retire from active service for rest and officers at the military level or above who retire shall be resettled and managed in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

The benefits of veterans who have been resettled under the voluntary job selection method before the implementation of this Law shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Council and the Central Military Commission.

Article 85: This Law shall come into effect on January 1, 2021.
