# Charity Law of the People's Republic of China

Adopted at the Fourth Session of the Twelfth National People's Congress on March 16, 2016

<!-- INFO END -->

## Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 This Law is enacted for the purposes of developing charity undertakings, promoting charity culture, regulating charity activities, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of participants in charity activities such as charitable organizations, donors, volunteers, and beneficiaries, promoting social progress, and sharing the fruits of development.

Article 2 This Law shall apply to natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations that carry out charity activities and activities related to charity. Where other laws have special provisions, those provisions shall prevail.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Law, "charity activities" refer to the following public welfare activities voluntarily carried out by natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations by means of donating property or providing services:

(1) Poverty alleviation and relief;
(2) Assisting the elderly, orphans, the sick, the disabled, and providing special care to entitled groups;
(3) Relieving damage caused by emergencies such as natural disasters, accident disasters, and public health incidents;
(4) Promoting the development of education, science, culture, health, sports, and other undertakings;
(5) Preventing and controlling pollution and other public hazards, protecting and improving the ecological environment; and
(6) Other public welfare activities that comply with the provisions of this Law.

Article 4 Charity activities shall follow the principles of legality, voluntariness, good faith, and non-profit, shall not violate social morality, and shall not endanger national security, harm the public interest, or the legitimate rights and interests of others.

Article 5 The State encourages and supports natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations to practice socialist core values, promote the traditional virtues of the Chinese nation, and carry out charity activities in accordance with the law.

Article 6 The civil affairs department of the State Council shall be in charge of charity work nationwide. The civil affairs departments of local people's governments at or above the county level shall be in charge of charity work within their respective administrative regions. Relevant departments of the people's governments at or above the county level shall, in accordance with this Law and other relevant laws and regulations, perform relevant duties within their respective responsibilities.

Article 7 September 5 of each year shall be the "Chinese Charity Day."

## Chapter II Charitable Organizations

Article 8 For the purposes of this Law, "charitable organization" refers to a non-profit organization established in accordance with the law, meeting the requirements of this Law, and aiming to carry out charity activities for the public.

Charitable organizations may take the organizational forms of foundations, social groups, social service institutions, etc.

Article 9 A charitable organization shall meet the following conditions:

(1) Having the purpose of carrying out charity activities;
(2) Not being for profit;
(3) Having its own name and domicile;
(4) Having articles of association;
(5) Having necessary property;
(6) Having qualified organizational bodies and responsible persons; and
(7) Other conditions stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 10 To establish a charitable organization, an application for registration shall be submitted to the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level. The civil affairs department shall make a decision within thirty days from the date of accepting the application. If the conditions stipulated in this Law are met, registration shall be granted and publicly announced; if not, registration shall be denied with a written explanation of the reasons.

Non-profit organizations such as foundations, social groups, and social service institutions established before the promulgation of this Law may apply to the civil affairs department where they are registered for recognition as charitable organizations. The civil affairs department shall make a decision within twenty days from the date of accepting the application. If the conditions for charitable organizations are met, recognition shall be granted and publicly announced; if not, recognition shall be denied with a written explanation of the reasons.

If special circumstances require an extension of the time limit for registration or recognition, the time limit may be appropriately extended with the approval of the civil affairs department of the State Council, but the extension shall not exceed sixty days.

Article 11 The articles of association of a charitable organization shall comply with the provisions of laws and regulations and shall specify the following matters:

(1) Name and domicile;
(2) Organizational form;
(3) Purpose and scope of activities;
(4) Sources and composition of property;
(5) Composition and responsibilities of decision-making and executive bodies;
(6) Internal supervision mechanisms;
(7) Property management and use systems;
(8) Project management systems;
(9) Circumstances of termination and liquidation methods after termination; and
(10) Other important matters.

Article 12 A charitable organization shall, in accordance with laws, regulations, and its articles of association, establish and improve its internal governance structure, clearly define the responsibilities and authority of decision-making, execution, and supervision, and carry out charity activities.

A charitable organization shall implement the unified national accounting system, conduct accounting in accordance with the law, establish and improve accounting supervision systems, and accept supervision and management by relevant government departments.

Article 13 A charitable organization shall submit an annual work report and financial accounting report to the civil affairs department where it is registered each year. The report shall include the annual fundraising and donation acceptance situation, the management and use of charitable property, the implementation of charitable projects, and the wages and benefits of the staff of the charitable organization.

Article 14 The founders, major donors, and management personnel of a charitable organization shall not use their affiliated relationships to harm the interests of the charitable organization, beneficiaries, or the public interest.

If the founders, major donors, or management personnel of a charitable organization engage in transactions with the charitable organization, they shall not participate in the decision-making of the charitable organization regarding such transactions, and the relevant transaction information shall be disclosed to the public.

Article 15 A charitable organization shall not engage in or fund activities that endanger national security or the public interest, shall not accept donations with conditions that violate laws, regulations, or social morality, and shall not impose conditions on beneficiaries that violate laws, regulations, or social morality.

Article 16 Persons in any of the following circumstances shall not serve as responsible persons of a charitable organization:

(1) Persons with no capacity for civil conduct or with limited capacity for civil conduct;
(2) Persons who have been sentenced to criminal punishment for intentional crimes, where less than five years have elapsed since the completion of the sentence;
(3) Persons who served as responsible persons of an organization whose registration certificate was revoked or which was banned, where less than five years have elapsed since the revocation or banning; or
(4) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

Article 17 A charitable organization shall terminate in any of the following circumstances:

(1) The circumstances for termination specified in its articles of association occur;
(2) Termination is necessary due to division or merger;
(3) It has not engaged in charity activities for two consecutive years;
(4) Its registration is revoked or its registration certificate is revoked in accordance with the law; or
(5) Other circumstances stipulated by laws and administrative regulations where termination is required.

Article 18 When a charitable organization terminates, it shall undergo liquidation.

The decision-making body of the charitable organization shall establish a liquidation group within thirty days from the date the termination circumstances specified in Article 17 of this Law occur, conduct liquidation, and make a public announcement. If no liquidation group is established or the liquidation group fails to perform its duties, the civil affairs department may apply to the people's court to designate relevant personnel to form a liquidation group for liquidation.

The remaining property after liquidation of a charitable organization shall be transferred to a charitable organization with the same or similar purpose in accordance with the provisions of its articles of association; if the articles of association do not provide for this, the civil affairs department shall arrange for its transfer to a charitable organization with the same or similar purpose and make a public announcement.

After the liquidation of a charitable organization is completed, it shall go through the cancellation of registration with the civil affairs department where it is registered, and the civil affairs department shall make a public announcement.

Article 19 Charitable organizations may establish industry organizations in accordance with the law.

Charitable industry organizations shall reflect industry demands, promote industry exchanges, enhance the credibility of the charity industry, and promote the development of charity undertakings.

Article 20 The specific measures for the organizational forms and registration management of charitable organizations shall be formulated by the State Council.

## Chapter III Charitable Fundraising

Article 21 For the purposes of this Law, "charitable fundraising" refers to the activity of raising property by a charitable organization based on charitable purposes.

Charitable fundraising includes public fundraising directed at the general public and targeted fundraising directed at specific objects.

Article 22 A charitable organization conducting public fundraising must obtain the qualification for public fundraising. A charitable organization that has been registered for at least two years may apply to the civil affairs department where it is registered for the qualification for public fundraising. The civil affairs department shall make a decision within twenty days from the date of accepting the application. If the charitable organization meets the conditions of having a sound internal governance structure and standardized operations, it shall be issued a certificate of qualification for public fundraising; if not, the certificate shall not be issued with a written explanation of the reasons.

For foundations and social groups that are allowed by laws and administrative regulations to conduct public fundraising from the date of registration, the civil affairs department shall directly issue a certificate of qualification for public fundraising.

Article 23 Public fundraising may be conducted by the following methods:

(1) Setting up donation boxes in public places;
(2) Holding charity performances, competitions, sales, exhibitions, auctions, charity galas, etc., directed at the general public;
(3) Publishing fundraising information through media such as radio, television, newspapers, and the internet; and
(4) Other methods of public fundraising.

If a charitable organization conducts public fundraising using the methods specified in items (1) and (2) of the preceding paragraph, it shall do so within the jurisdiction of the civil affairs department where it is registered. If it is truly necessary to conduct such activities outside that jurisdiction, it shall report to the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level where the fundraising activity is to be conducted for filing. The donation behavior of donors is not subject to geographical restrictions.

If a charitable organization conducts public fundraising through the internet, it shall publish the fundraising information on the unified or designated charity information platform of the civil affairs department of the State Council, and may simultaneously publish the fundraising information on its own website.

Article 24 When conducting public fundraising, a fundraising plan shall be formulated. The fundraising plan includes the purpose of the fundraising, start and end times and geographical scope, name and office address of the person in charge, methods of accepting donations, bank accounts, beneficiaries, intended use of the funds and goods raised, fundraising costs, and the handling of remaining property.

The fundraising plan shall be submitted to the civil affairs department where the charitable organization is registered for filing before the fundraising activity begins.

Article 25 When conducting public fundraising, the name of the fundraising organization, the certificate of qualification for public fundraising, the fundraising plan, contact information, and methods for querying fundraising information shall be published in a prominent location at the fundraising activity site or on the carrier of the fundraising activity.

Article 26 Organizations or individuals without the qualification for public fundraising may, for charitable purposes, cooperate with a charitable organization that has the qualification for public fundraising, which shall conduct the public fundraising and manage the funds and goods raised.

Article 27 Radio, television, newspapers, internet service providers, and telecommunications operators shall verify the registration certificate and the certificate of qualification for public fundraising of charitable organizations using their platforms to conduct public fundraising.

Article 28 A charitable organization may conduct targeted fundraising from the date of its registration.

When a charitable organization conducts targeted fundraising, it shall do so within the scope of specific objects such as its founders, council members, and members, and shall explain to the fundraising objects matters such as the purpose of the fundraising and the intended use of the funds and goods raised.

Article 29 When conducting targeted fundraising, the methods specified in Article 23 of this Law shall not be used, either directly or in disguised form.

Article 30 When major natural disasters, accident disasters, public health incidents, or other emergencies occur and rapid relief is needed, the relevant people's government shall establish a coordination mechanism, provide demand information, and guide fundraising and relief activities in a timely and orderly manner.

Article 31 When conducting fundraising activities, the legitimate rights and interests of the fundraising objects shall be respected and protected, their right to know shall be guaranteed, and they shall not be deceived or induced into making donations through fabricating facts or other means.

Article 32 When conducting fundraising activities, apportionment or disguised apportionment shall not be allowed, and public order, enterprise production and operation, and residents' daily life shall not be disrupted.

Article 33 No organization or individual is allowed to conduct fundraising activities under the guise of charity or by impersonating a charitable organization to defraud property.

## Chapter IV Charitable Donations

Article 34 For the purposes of this Law, "charitable donation" refers to the activity of voluntarily and gratuitously donating property by natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations for charitable purposes.

Article 35 Donors may donate through charitable organizations or directly to beneficiaries.

Article 36 The property donated by a donor shall be legal property over which the donor has the right of disposal. Donated property includes tangible and intangible property such as currency, physical goods, houses, marketable securities, equity, and intellectual property.

Physical goods donated by a donor shall have use value and meet standards for safety, hygiene, and environmental protection.

If a donor donates products from their own enterprise, they shall bear product quality responsibility and obligations in accordance with the law.

Article 37 If natural persons, legal persons, or other organizations conduct commercial activities such as performances, competitions, sales, or auctions, and promise to use all or part of the proceeds for charitable purposes, they shall sign a donation agreement with the charitable organization or other recipient of the donation before the activity, fulfill the donation obligations according to the donation agreement after the activity, and disclose the donation situation to the public.

Article 38 When a charitable organization accepts a donation, it shall issue a donation receipt uniformly supervised (printed) by the financial department to the donor. The donation receipt shall specify the donor, the type and quantity of the donated property, the name of the charitable organization and the name of the handler, and the date of the receipt. If the donor is anonymous or waives the right to receive the donation receipt, the charitable organization shall keep relevant records.

Article 39 When a charitable organization accepts a donation, if the donor requests a written donation agreement, the charitable organization shall sign a written donation agreement with the donor.

The written donation agreement shall include the names of the donor and the charitable organization, the type, quantity, quality, purpose, and delivery time of the donated property.

Article 40 When a donor agrees with a charitable organization on the purpose of the donated property and the beneficiary, the donor shall not designate a person with an interest relationship to the donor as the beneficiary.

No organization or individual may use charitable donations to promote tobacco products in violation of legal provisions, or to promote products and matters prohibited by law in any way.

Article 41 A donor shall fulfill their donation obligations in accordance with the donation agreement. If a donor violates the donation agreement and fails to deliver the donated property after the deadline, and falls under any of the following circumstances, the charitable organization or other recipient of the donation may demand delivery; if the donor refuses to deliver, the charitable organization or other recipient may apply to the people's court for a payment order or file a lawsuit in accordance with the law:

(1) The donor publicly promised the donation through media such as radio, television, newspapers, or the internet; or
(2) The donated property is used for the charity activities specified in items (1) to (3) of Article 3 of this Law, and a written donation agreement has been signed.

If a donor's economic situation significantly deteriorates after making a public promise to donate or signing a written donation agreement, seriously affecting their production, operation, or family life, they may be exempted from fulfilling the donation obligation after reporting to the civil affairs department of the place where the public promise was made or the written donation agreement was signed and publicly explaining the situation.

Article 42 A donor has the right to inquire about and copy relevant materials concerning the management and use of their donated property, and the charitable organization shall promptly and proactively provide feedback to the donor.

If a charitable organization violates the agreed purpose of the donation agreement and misuses the donated property, the donor has the right to demand correction; if the charitable organization refuses to correct, the donor may lodge a complaint or report with the civil affairs department or file a lawsuit with the people's court.

Article 43 State-owned enterprises implementing charitable donations shall comply with relevant regulations on the management of state-owned assets and go through approval and filing procedures.

## Chapter V Charitable Trusts

Article 44 For the purposes of this Law, a "charitable trust" belongs to the category of public interest trusts and refers to the act where a settlor, for charitable purposes, entrusts their property to a trustee in accordance with the law, and the trustee manages and disposes of the property in the trustee's own name according to the settlor's wishes, carrying out charity activities.

Article 45 The establishment of a charitable trust and the appointment of a trustee and a supervisor shall be in writing. The trustee shall, within seven days from the date of signing the charitable trust documents, file the relevant documents with the civil affairs department of the people's government at or above the county level where the trustee is located.

If the relevant documents are not filed with the civil affairs department as stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the trust shall not enjoy tax benefits.

Article 46 The trustee of a charitable trust may be a charitable organization or trust company trusted by the settlor.

Article 47 If the trustee of a charitable trust violates the trust obligations or finds it difficult to perform their duties, the settlor may change the trustee. The changed trustee shall, within seven days from the date of the change, report the change to the original civil affairs department that filed the trust for re-filing.

Article 48 The trustee of a charitable trust shall manage and dispose of the trust property in accordance with the trust purpose, perform their duties diligently, and fulfill the obligations of good faith and prudent management.

The trustee of a charitable trust shall, in accordance with the trust documents and the requirements of the settlor, promptly report to the settlor on the handling of trust affairs and the management and use of trust property. The trustee of a charitable trust shall report the handling of trust affairs and financial status to the civil affairs department where it is filed at least once a year and make it public.

Article 49 The settlor of a charitable trust may, as needed, appoint a trust supervisor.

The trust supervisor shall supervise the actions of the trustee and safeguard the rights and interests of the settlor and beneficiaries in accordance with the law. If the trust supervisor discovers that the trustee violates the trust obligations or finds it difficult to perform their duties, they shall report to the settlor and have the right to file a lawsuit with the people's court in their own name.

Article 50 Matters concerning the establishment of charitable trusts, management of trust property, trust parties, termination and liquidation of trusts, etc., not provided for in this Chapter, shall be governed by other relevant provisions of this Law; if not provided for in this Law, the relevant provisions of the Trust Law of the People's Republic of China shall apply.

## Chapter VI Charitable Property

Article 51 The property of a charitable organization includes:

(1) Initial property donated and funded by the founders;
(2) Property raised; and
(3) Other lawful property.

Article 52 The property of a charitable organization shall be used entirely for charitable purposes in accordance with the provisions of its articles of association and donation agreements, and shall not be distributed among the founders, donors, or members of the charitable organization.

No organization or individual may privately divide, misappropriate, intercept, or embezzle charitable property.

Article 53 A charitable organization shall register, strictly manage, and use the raised property exclusively for its designated purposes.

If physical goods donated by a donor are not easy to store, transport, or difficult to use directly for charitable purposes, the charitable organization may auction or sell them in accordance with the law, and the proceeds, after deducting necessary expenses, shall be used entirely for charitable purposes.

Article 54 If a charitable organization invests to preserve or increase the value of its property, it shall follow the principles of legality, safety, and effectiveness, and the income from the investment shall be used entirely for charitable purposes. Major investment plans of a charitable organization shall be approved by more than two-thirds of the members of its decision-making body. Property funded by government grants and property agreed in donation agreements not to be invested shall not be used for investment. The responsible persons and staff of a charitable organization shall not hold concurrent positions in or receive remuneration from enterprises in which the charitable organization invests.

The specific measures for the matters stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall be formulated by the civil affairs department of the State Council.**Article 55** When conducting charitable activities, charitable organizations shall use donated property in accordance with laws, regulations, and their articles of association, as well as in compliance with fundraising plans or donation agreements. If a charitable organization truly needs to change the use of donated property as stipulated in the fundraising plan, it shall file a record with the civil affairs department; if it truly needs to change the use of donated property as agreed in the donation agreement, it shall obtain the consent of the donor.

**Article 56** Charitable organizations shall reasonably design charitable projects, optimize implementation procedures, reduce operating costs, and improve the efficiency of the use of charitable property.

Charitable organizations shall establish project management systems to track and supervise the implementation of projects.

**Article 57** If there is surplus donated property after the termination of a charitable project, it shall be handled in accordance with the fundraising plan or donation agreement; if the fundraising plan does not specify or the donation agreement does not stipulate, the charitable organization shall use the remaining property for other charitable projects with the same or similar purposes and make it known to the public.

**Article 58** When determining charitable beneficiaries, charitable organizations shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality, and shall not designate persons with an interest in the management personnel of the charitable organization as beneficiaries.

**Article 59** Charitable organizations may, as needed, enter into agreements with beneficiaries to clarify the rights and obligations of both parties, specifying the purpose, amount, and method of use of the charitable property.

Beneficiaries shall cherish the charitable assistance and use the charitable property in accordance with the agreement. If a beneficiary fails to use the charitable property according to the agreement or has other serious violations of the agreement, the charitable organization has the right to demand correction; if the beneficiary refuses to correct, the charitable organization has the right to terminate the agreement and demand the return of the property.

**Article 60** Charitable organizations shall actively conduct charitable activities, fully and efficiently utilize charitable property, adhere to the principle of minimizing administrative expenses, practice economy, and reduce unnecessary expenditures. For foundations with public fundraising qualifications among charitable organizations, the annual expenditure on charitable activities shall not be less than 70% of the total income of the previous year or 70% of the average income of the previous three years; the annual administrative expenses shall not exceed 10% of the total annual expenditure. Under special circumstances, if the annual administrative expenses are difficult to meet the aforementioned provisions, the organization shall report to the civil affairs department with which it is registered and explain the situation to the public.

The standards for annual expenditure on charitable activities and administrative expenses for charitable organizations other than foundations with public fundraising qualifications shall be formulated by the civil affairs department of the State Council in conjunction with the finance and taxation departments of the State Council in accordance with the principles set forth in the preceding paragraph.

If a donation agreement stipulates the expenditure on charitable activities and administrative expenses for a single donation of property, such stipulations shall prevail.

## Chapter VII: Charitable Services

**Article 61** For the purposes of this Law, "charitable services" refer to voluntary, unpaid services and other non-profit services provided by charitable organizations, other organizations, and individuals to society or others for charitable purposes.

Charitable organizations may provide charitable services themselves, recruit volunteers to provide them, or entrust other organizations with specialized expertise to provide them.

**Article 62** When providing charitable services, the personal dignity of beneficiaries and volunteers shall be respected, and the privacy of beneficiaries and volunteers shall not be infringed upon.

**Article 63** When providing charitable services such as medical rehabilitation, education, and training that require specialized skills, the standards and procedures formulated by the state or industry organizations shall be followed.

If a charitable organization recruits volunteers to participate in charitable services that require specialized skills, it shall provide relevant training to the volunteers.

**Article 64** When a charitable organization recruits volunteers to participate in charitable services, it shall disclose all information related to the charitable services and inform them of the risks that may occur during the service process.

Charitable organizations may, as needed, enter into agreements with volunteers to clarify the rights and obligations of both parties, specifying the content, method, and duration of the service.

**Article 65** Charitable organizations shall register volunteers with their real names and record information such as the volunteers' service hours, content, and evaluations. Upon request by a volunteer, the charitable organization shall provide a certificate of volunteer service records free of charge and truthfully.

**Article 66** When arranging for volunteers to participate in charitable services, charitable organizations shall ensure compatibility with the volunteers' age, educational level, skills, and physical condition.

**Article 67** Volunteers who accept arrangements by charitable organizations to participate in charitable services shall comply with management and undergo necessary training.

**Article 68** Charitable organizations shall provide necessary conditions for volunteers to participate in charitable services and protect the legitimate rights and interests of volunteers.

Before arranging for volunteers to participate in charitable services that may involve personal danger, charitable organizations shall purchase corresponding personal accident insurance for the volunteers.

## Chapter VIII: Information Disclosure

**Article 69** People's governments at or above the county level shall establish and improve systems for the statistics and publication of charitable information.

The civil affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly disclose charitable information to the public on a unified information platform and provide free information publication services.

Charitable organizations and trustees of charitable trusts shall publish charitable information on the platform specified in the preceding paragraph and be responsible for the authenticity of the information.

**Article 70** The civil affairs departments and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall promptly disclose the following charitable information to the public:

(1) Registration matters of charitable organizations;
(2) Filing matters of charitable trusts;
(3) List of charitable organizations with public fundraising qualifications;
(4) List of charitable organizations qualified to issue receipts for pre-tax deduction of public welfare donations;
(5) Promotion measures such as tax incentives, subsidies, and grants for charitable activities;
(6) Information on purchasing services from charitable organizations;
(7) Results of inspections and evaluations of charitable organizations and charitable trusts;
(8) Results of commendations and penalties for charitable organizations, other organizations, and individuals;
(9) Other information that should be disclosed according to laws and regulations.

**Article 71** Charitable organizations and trustees of charitable trusts shall fulfill their obligations of information disclosure according to law. Information disclosure shall be truthful, complete, and timely.

**Article 72** Charitable organizations shall disclose to the public their articles of association, information on members of decision-making, executive, and supervisory bodies, and other information required to be disclosed by the civil affairs department of the State Council. If there are significant changes to the above information, the charitable organization shall promptly disclose them to the public.

Charitable organizations shall disclose their annual work reports and financial accounting reports to the public each year. The financial accounting reports of charitable organizations with public fundraising qualifications must be audited.

**Article 73** Charitable organizations with public fundraising qualifications shall regularly disclose their fundraising status and the implementation status of charitable projects to the public.

If the period of a public fundraising campaign exceeds six months, the fundraising status shall be disclosed at least once every three months, and the full fundraising status shall be disclosed within three months after the end of the public fundraising campaign.

If the implementation period of a charitable project exceeds six months, the project implementation status shall be disclosed at least once every three months, and the full project implementation status and the use of funds and materials raised shall be disclosed within three months after the project ends.

**Article 74** When a charitable organization conducts targeted fundraising, it shall promptly inform donors of the fundraising status and the management and use of the funds and materials raised.

**Article 75** Charitable organizations and trustees of charitable trusts shall inform beneficiaries of information such as their funding standards, work procedures, and work norms.

**Article 76** Information involving state secrets, commercial secrets, personal privacy, and the names, titles, addresses, and communication methods of donors or settlors of charitable trusts who do not consent to disclosure shall not be disclosed.

## Chapter IX: Promotion Measures

**Article 77** People's governments at or above the county level shall formulate policies and measures to promote the development of charitable undertakings based on economic and social development conditions.

Relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall, within their respective responsibilities, provide charitable organizations, trustees of charitable trusts, etc., with information on charitable needs and provide guidance and assistance for charitable activities.

**Article 78** The civil affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall establish mechanisms for sharing charitable information with other departments.

**Article 79** Charitable organizations and their income shall enjoy tax incentives according to law.

**Article 80** Natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations that donate property for charitable activities shall enjoy tax incentives according to law. If the charitable donation expenditure of an enterprise exceeds the portion allowed to be deducted in the current year when calculating the taxable income for enterprise income tax as stipulated by law, the excess may be carried forward for deduction within the following three years when calculating the taxable income.

Materials donated from abroad for charitable activities shall be subject to reduced or exempted import tariffs and import value-added tax according to law.

**Article 81** Beneficiaries who accept charitable donations shall enjoy tax incentives according to law.

**Article 82** If charitable organizations, donors, or beneficiaries are entitled to tax incentives according to law, the relevant departments shall promptly handle the relevant procedures.

**Article 83** If a donor donates physical goods, securities, equity, or intellectual property to a charitable organization, the relevant administrative and institutional fees for the transfer of rights shall be exempted according to law.

**Article 84** The state implements special preferential policies for charitable activities aimed at poverty alleviation and relief.

**Article 85** If a charitable organization needs land for charitable service facilities to conduct charitable activities specified in Items 1 and 2 of Article 3 of this Law, it may apply for the use of state-allocated land or rural collective construction land according to law. The use of land for charitable service facilities shall not be changed without legal procedures.

**Article 86** The state provides financial policy support for charitable undertakings and encourages financial institutions to provide financial services such as financing and settlement for charitable organizations and charitable trusts.

**Article 87** People's governments at all levels and their relevant departments may, according to law, support qualified charitable organizations in providing services to society through methods such as purchasing services, and shall disclose the relevant circumstances to the public in accordance with relevant government procurement laws and regulations.

**Article 88** The state takes measures to promote a culture of charity and cultivate citizens' awareness of charity.

Educational institutions such as schools shall incorporate charity culture into their educational and teaching content. The state encourages higher education institutions to cultivate professional talents in charity and supports higher education institutions and research institutions in conducting theoretical research on charity.

Media such as radio, television, newspapers, and the internet shall actively conduct public welfare publicity activities for charity, popularize charity knowledge, and spread charity culture.

**Article 89** The state encourages enterprises, institutions, and other organizations to provide venues and other conveniences for conducting charitable activities.

**Article 90** With the consent of the beneficiary, a donor may name a charitable project they have donated to as a memorial. If approval is required by laws and regulations, such provisions shall prevail.

**Article 91** The state establishes a commendation system for charity, and natural persons, legal persons, and other organizations that have made outstanding contributions to the development of charitable undertakings shall be commended by people's governments at or above the county level or relevant departments.

## Chapter X: Supervision and Administration

**Article 92** The civil affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall perform their duties according to law, supervise and inspect charitable activities, and provide guidance to charitable industry organizations.

**Article 93** The civil affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level have the authority to take the following measures against charitable organizations suspected of violating the provisions of this Law:

(1) Conduct on-site inspections of the domicile of the charitable organization and the place where the charitable activity occurs;
(2) Require the charitable organization to provide explanations, and inspect and copy relevant materials;
(3) Investigate and gather information related to supervision and management from units and individuals involved in the charitable activity;
(4) With the approval of the people's government at the same level, inquire into the financial accounts of the charitable organization;
(5) Other measures stipulated by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 94** When the civil affairs department of a people's government at or above the county level conducts inspections or investigations of charitable organizations, relevant units, or individuals, there shall be no fewer than two inspectors or investigators, and they shall present valid credentials and inspection/investigation notices.

**Article 95** The civil affairs departments of people's governments at or above the county level shall establish a credit record system for charitable organizations and their responsible persons and make it public.

The civil affairs department shall establish an evaluation system for charitable organizations, encourage and support third-party institutions in evaluating charitable organizations, and make the evaluation results public.

**Article 96** Charitable industry organizations shall establish and improve industry norms and strengthen industry self-discipline.

**Article 97** Any unit or individual that discovers illegal activities by a charitable organization or charitable trust may file a complaint or report with the civil affairs department, other relevant departments, or a charitable industry organization. Upon receiving a complaint or report, the civil affairs department, other relevant departments, or the charitable industry organization shall promptly investigate and handle the matter.

The state encourages the public and the media to supervise charitable activities, expose acts of defrauding property under the guise of charity or by impersonating charitable organizations, as well as illegal or non-compliant acts by charitable organizations and charitable trusts, thereby leveraging the role of public opinion and social supervision.

## Chapter XI: Legal Liability

**Article 98** If a charitable organization falls under any of the following circumstances, the civil affairs department shall order it to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, its registration certificate shall be revoked and a public announcement shall be made:

(1) Failing to conduct activities in accordance with the charitable purpose;
(2) Illegally distributing, misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling charitable property;
(3) Accepting donations with conditions that violate laws, regulations, or social ethics, or imposing conditions on beneficiaries that violate laws, regulations, or social ethics.

**Article 99** If a charitable organization falls under any of the following circumstances, the civil affairs department shall issue a warning and order it to make corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend activities for rectification:

(1) Violating the provisions of Article 14 of this Law, causing losses to charitable property;
(2) Using property that should not be used for investment for investment;
(3) Unauthorizedly changing the use of donated property;
(4) Violating the provisions of Article 60 of this Law regarding the standards for annual expenditure on charitable activities or administrative expenses;
(5) Failing to fulfill the obligation of information disclosure according to law;
(6) Failing to submit annual work reports, financial accounting reports, or file fundraising plans according to law;
(7) Disclosing the personal privacy of donors, volunteers, or beneficiaries, or disclosing the names, titles, addresses, or communication methods of donors or settlors of charitable trusts who do not consent to disclosure.

If a charitable organization violates the provisions of this Law by disclosing state secrets or commercial secrets, it shall be punished in accordance with relevant laws.

If a charitable organization has the circumstances specified in the preceding two paragraphs and, within one year after being dealt with according to law, again has the circumstances specified in the preceding paragraph, or if there are other serious circumstances, the civil affairs department shall revoke its registration certificate and make a public announcement.

**Article 100** If a charitable organization has the circumstances specified in Articles 98 and 99 of this Law and has illegal gains, the civil affairs department shall confiscate them; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined between RMB 20,000 and RMB 200,000.

**Article 101** If a fundraising activity falls under any of the following circumstances, the civil affairs department shall issue a warning and order the cessation of the fundraising activity; for property raised illegally, it shall order its return to the donors; if return is difficult, the civil affairs department shall confiscate it and transfer it to other charitable organizations for charitable purposes; the relevant organization or individual shall be fined between RMB 20,000 and RMB 200,000:

(1) An organization or individual without public fundraising qualifications conducts public fundraising;
(2) Deceiving or inducing fundraising targets to make donations through fabricating facts or other means;
(3) Apportioning or disguisedly apportioning donations to units or individuals;
(4) Obstructing public order, enterprise production and operation, or residents' lives.

If radio, television, newspapers, internet service providers, or telecommunications operators fail to fulfill the verification obligations specified in Article 27 of this Law, their competent authorities shall issue a warning and order corrections within a specified period; if they fail to make corrections within the specified period, they shall be subject to criticism by circular.

**Article 102** If a charitable organization fails to issue donation receipts to donors according to law, fails to issue volunteer service record certificates to volunteers according to law, or fails to promptly and proactively provide feedback to donors on relevant situations, the civil affairs department shall issue a warning and order corrections within a specified period; if it fails to make corrections within the specified period, it shall be ordered to suspend activities for a specified period.

**Article 103** If a charitable organization obtains tax incentives through fraud or deception, the tax authorities shall investigate and punish it according to law; if the circumstances are serious, the civil affairs department shall revoke its registration certificate and make a public announcement.

**Article 104** If a charitable organization engages in or funds activities that endanger national security or public interests, the relevant authorities shall investigate and punish it according to law, and the civil affairs department shall revoke its registration certificate and make a public announcement.

**Article 105** If a trustee of a charitable trust falls under any of the following circumstances, the civil affairs department shall issue a warning and order corrections within a specified period; if there are illegal gains, the civil affairs department shall confiscate them; the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined between RMB 20,000 and RMB 200,000:

(1) Using trust property and its income for non-charitable purposes;
(2) Failing to report the handling of trust affairs and financial status to the civil affairs department or disclose them to the public as required.

**Article 106** During the provision of charitable services, if damage is caused to beneficiaries or third parties due to the fault of the charitable organization or volunteers, the charitable organization shall bear compensation liability according to law; if the damage is caused by the volunteer's intentional act or gross negligence, the charitable organization may seek recourse from the volunteer.

If a volunteer suffers damage during participation in charitable services due to the fault of the charitable organization, the charitable organization shall bear compensation liability according to law; if the damage is caused by force majeure, the charitable organization shall provide appropriate compensation.

**Article 107** If a natural person, legal person, or other organization defrauds property under the guise of charity or by impersonating a charitable organization, the public security organs shall investigate and punish it according to law.

**Article 108** If the civil affairs departments and other relevant departments of people's governments at or above the county level, or their staff members, fall under any of the following circumstances, the superior organ or the supervisory organ shall order corrections; if disciplinary action should be taken according to law, the appointing organ or the supervisory organ shall impose sanctions on the directly responsible supervisors and other directly responsible personnel:

(1) Failing to fulfill the obligation of information disclosure according to law;
(2) Apportioning or disguisedly apportioning donation tasks, or forcibly designating volunteers or charitable organizations to provide services;
(3) Failing to perform supervision and management duties according to law;
(4) Illegally implementing administrative coercive measures and administrative penalties;
(5) Illegally distributing, misappropriating, intercepting, or embezzling charitable property;
(6) Other acts of abusing power, neglecting duties, or engaging in malpractice for personal gain.

**Article 109** Violations of the provisions of this Law that constitute a violation of public security administration shall be subject to public security administration penalties by the public security organs according to law; if a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

## Chapter XII: Supplementary Provisions

**Article 110** Urban and rural community organizations and work units may conduct mutual aid activities within their own communities or units.

**Article 111** Organizations other than charitable organizations may conduct charitable activities within their capabilities.

**Article 112** This Law shall come into effect on September 1, 2016.
