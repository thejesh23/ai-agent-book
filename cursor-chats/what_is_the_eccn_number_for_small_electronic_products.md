**What is an ECCN (Export Control Classification Number)?**

An ECCN (Export Control Classification Number) is a coding system used by the U.S. Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) to classify items for export control purposes.

**Common ECCN Classifications for Small Electronic Products:**

The ECCN number for a small electronic product depends on its specific technical characteristics and functions. Common classifications include:

- **3A991** - Electronic components
- **4A994** - Computer-related products
- **5A992** - Telecommunications and information security equipment
- **EAR99** - Items not listed on the Commerce Control List (CCL), typically not subject to specific export controls

**ECCN Code Structure:**

An ECCN consists of 5 characters:
- 1st digit: Item category
- 2nd character (letter): Item group
- Last 3 digits: Specific reason and details for export control

**How to Determine Your Product's ECCN Number:**

1. **Consult the manufacturer or supplier** - They can usually provide the product's ECCN number

2. **Use the BIS Export Control Classification Tool** - Helps identify the appropriate ECCN number

3. **Submit an official classification request** - Submit a product classification request online through the BIS SNAP-R system to obtain an official classification

**Important Note:**

Determining the correct ECCN number is crucial for complying with export control regulations. An incorrect classification could lead to violations of export control laws. It is recommended to accurately identify your product's ECCN number before exporting.

**Detailed Explanation of EAR99:**

EAR99 is a special classification code within the U.S. export control system, standing for "Export Administration Regulations 99."

## Core Concept

**EAR99** = **Items not on the Commerce Control List (CCL)**

Simply put, if a product:
- ✅ Does not contain sensitive technology
- ✅ Is not a military or dual-use item
- ✅ Is not explicitly listed on the CCL (Commerce Control List)
- ✅ Is a common commercial/consumer-grade product

Then it is classified as **EAR99**.

## Characteristics of EAR99

### 1. **Relatively Lenient**
- An export license is **not required** in most cases
- Applicable to ordinary commercial products

### 2. **But Not Completely Free**
The following restrictions still apply:
- ❌ Cannot be exported to sanctioned countries (e.g., Iran, North Korea, Syria, etc.)
- ❌ Cannot be exported to companies or individuals on the "Entity List"
- ❌ Cannot be used for sensitive purposes such as military or nuclear proliferation

### 3. **Common Examples of EAR99 Products**
- Ordinary consumer electronics (phone cases, headphones, keyboards)
- Basic office supplies
- General commercial software
- Conventional tools and equipment
- **Consumer-grade AI voice recorders like the Plaud Note**

## Difference Between EAR99 and ECCN

| Classification | Description | Export Restrictions | Example |
|------|------|----------|------|
| **EAR99** | Not on the control list | Relatively lenient | Ordinary voice recorder, mouse |
| **ECCN** | On the control list | Requires assessment or license | Encryption device (5A992), high-performance computer |

## Practical Application

If the Plaud Note is classified as EAR99, this means:

✅ **Good News:**
- No license is required for export to most countries
- Compliance process is relatively simple
- Suitable for global sales in the consumer market

⚠️ **Still Need to Note:**

- Understand the end use

## How to Confirm a Product is EAR99?

1. **Manufacturer's Declaration** - Most authoritative source
2. **Feature Assessment** - If it is an ordinary consumer product, it is likely EAR99
3. **Process of Elimination** - If not on the CCL list, it is EAR99

**Summary: EAR99 is the most "friendly" classification in the U.S. export control system, indicating that the product is an ordinary commercial item with the fewest export restrictions.**
